### Remittances from Silver and Money Exchanges Must Include Real Names

The Ministry of Finance has Formulated Five Measures

[Our Newspaper, Nanjing, 18th Dispatch] Various silver and money exchanges must use the real names of their customers. The Ministry of Finance has repeatedly formulated measures and ordered them to be followed. Recently, to ensure strict implementation, the ministry has further stipulated that the names and addresses of remitters should also be strictly investigated when handling remittance business. Five new measures have been formulated and circulated for compliance: (1) When conducting domestic remittances, whether by money order or telegraphic transfer, silver and money exchanges must have the remitter fill in the remitter's and payee's real names (or the full name of the organization, company, or factory) and detailed addresses on the remittance application form, and the remitting exchanges must record them in the remittance account at all times, without any omissions. (2) If the payee of a telegraphic transfer is replaced by a telegraphic registration number or the remitting exchange's current account number, the remitter should still be asked to provide the payee's original name and address so that it can be recorded. If the remitter requests that a blank-headed remittance order be issued, the remitter should also be asked to fill in the payee's name and address as a basis for accounting. (3) For all remittances handled by the paying exchange (including remittances left for the payee to collect, remittances entrusted to the paying exchange to collect, and remittances in the nature of traveler's checks, etc.), the remitter should be asked to explain the reason why the address of the person leaving the remittance and the payee is not yet determined, and the remitting exchange should record the reason in the ledger. (4) With the exception of interbank transfers, government fund transfers from military and political agencies, and transfers of funds from production, public utility, and transportation enterprises, which should be handled in the name of the agency or enterprise, the paying exchange should require the payee to provide a reliable guarantee before releasing ordinary customer remittances of 200 million yuan or more per transaction. If the guarantee is not provided and the remittance is released, the remitting exchange will be held responsible if it is found that the payee's name is false or the address does not match. (5) Silver and money exchanges that violate the above provisions will be subject to disciplinary action by the Ministry of Finance, depending on the severity of the circumstances, in accordance with the provisions of Articles 13 and 14 of the Measures for Strengthening the Control of Financial Business.

### After President Truman's Two Speeches

US President Truman's speech to a joint session of both houses of Congress the day before yesterday is undoubtedly the most important historical document since the end of World War II. From now on, we will have to see how the American people and all countries that love democracy and peace react to President Truman's speech.

In the past half month, the situation in Europe has taken a sharp turn for the worse, almost reaching the final stage of the "Cold War," causing US President Truman, standing on the other side of the Atlantic, to begin to show that his confidence in world peace was wavering. However, the United States after World War II bears a special responsibility in terms of morality and history for maintaining world peace, and its words and actions are related to the safety of the world. Therefore, the people of the whole world, especially those who are threatened by the communist forces, are looking forward to the movements in Washington. At the same time, President Truman himself is deeply aware of the current global crisis and the great responsibility of the United States, and is eager to awaken the vigilance of the American government and the public. Therefore, a few days ago, he decided to deliver a speech to a joint session of both houses of Congress, clarifying the world situation and requesting Congress to take necessary steps to save the situation before it is too late. This diplomatic speech is not only valued by the American government and the public, but also by the people of all countries in the world; in the moment before President Truman delivered his speech, the people of the whole world were probably holding their breath and listening to President Truman's roar.

Now, we have finally heard President Truman's speech on the radio, and at the same time read the full text of this important speech in the telegram. The main points of the speech are summarized as follows: (1) The survival of democracy has been threatened, and it is time for the free men and women of the world to bravely confront the threats to their freedom. (2) The United States must maintain its strong strength to support European countries that are still free and feel threatened by communism and the rule of police states. (3) We must be prepared to pay the price of peace, otherwise we will inevitably pay the price of war. (4) He called for the temporary restoration of the draft and the implementation of universal military training to cope with the serious situation caused by the continuous aggression of the Soviet Union. (5) He requested that Congress act quickly on the $5.3 billion European recovery plan. (6) Our ability to quickly assemble and train a large number of well-trained troops in times of crisis can prevent future conflicts from occurring, and other national policies can restore world stability. (7) Implementing universal military training is enough to declare to the world that we are a support for peace, not only with great determination, but also with the power to achieve peace. (8) We should, regardless of party affiliation, organize a united front and unite with all countries in the world to make every possible effort to maintain world peace.

President Truman's speech was drafted by the President himself in consultation with political, diplomatic, military, and economic experts, and every sentence has a purpose and every word has weight, but because the speech time was only twenty minutes, it could not be explained in detail. However, after President Truman attended the joint session of Congress, he immediately flew to New York to attend the St. Peter's Dinner and gave a speech, more frankly criticizing the Soviet Union's attempt to establish authoritarian rule in the still free countries of Western Europe, and pointed out that communism denies God and does not recognize the right of mankind to self-government, and is in fact a tyranny. He also said that the existence of democracy is currently threatened by communism, and the United States has become the only protector of the free world. At this time, we should unite our strength with the strength of other free people to fight against communism. Therefore, we should use military, economic, and moral strength to support these countries. Although the contents of the two speeches are different, in our opinion, the latter is more powerful and more frank than the former. However, if President Truman only roars and condemns the Soviet Union's aggression and criticizes the communist threat to freedom verbally, and the United States still cannot immediately take effective actions in all aspects to stop the flood of communist forces, then we cannot be sure whether the world's chaos can be immediately reversed.

Looking at Europe in the past half month, the situation is crystallizing. On the one hand, there was first the coup in Czechoslovakia. After the Czech communists seized power, the tragedy of democracy has been repeated. Later, the Soviet Union forced Finland to conclude a mutual assistance treaty. Although it is still in the negotiation stage, Finland, forced by the general trend, is afraid that it will only obey orders. Recently, Europe has been filled with alarming rumors, either that the Soviet Union will take action against Turkey, or that the Soviet Union will require the Nordic countries of Norway and Sweden to sign alliances. The upcoming general election in Italy and the activities of the Communist Party are even more shocking. On the other hand, diplomatic activities are also quite lively. The London Conference decided to merge the three occupied areas of West Germany, and the Bejing Five-Nation Conference has also completed the drafting of the Western European Five-Nation Alliance Treaty, which was signed and established in Paris yesterday, and there is a possibility of expansion. In addition, the European Sixteen-Nation Conference is also being held in Paris. Regardless of the attitude of the representatives of various countries or the course of the meeting, it is enough to show that the countries of Western Europe are united in good faith, unanimously dealing with the threat of communism. Although the United States did not directly participate, the United States is in fact the "guardian" of democratic and free countries, which is an undeniable fact. After reading President Truman's speech, we can especially believe that the US aid policy to Europe will gradually evolve from economic aid to military aid. From this point of view, in addition to the unity of Western European countries themselves, coupled with the full support of the United States, it is logically hoped that the European situation will gradually become clear. However, why hasn't President Truman's speech stabilized the hearts of the world? We believe there are at least two reasons;

First, today's bipolarization of Europe is even more serious than the situation after the Munich Conference in 1938. The aggressor in the past was Nazi Germany. Although Hitler owned Austria and part of Czechoslovakia at that time, the nine countries in Eastern Europe had allied relations with Britain and France. In addition, France was the most powerful country in Europe at that time, and further east, there was a third force - the Soviet Union - restraining it. Looking back at today's Europe, because totalitarian communist countries are always taking the lead, and democratic countries are lagging behind in everything, these two major groups have almost reached a point where their strengths are evenly matched and cannot be compared, both in terms of population and area. Moreover, from the point of view of geographical location or military strategy, the Eastern European group has an advantage, which seems to be beyond the reach of the Western European group. Therefore, the establishment of the Western European Alliance is important today. The five countries of Western Europe not only need to help each other to stop the Soviet Union's military aggression, but also need to work together to prevent the activities of the fifth column within the country. However, according to official observations, its role may not be as great as the world imagines, and looking forward to its future, it is full of thorns, and it is unlikely to be smooth sailing, so we cannot be too optimistic.

Second, although President Truman has frankly expressed his anti-communist stance and frankly announced his support for democratic and free countries, his focus is still on Europe. In particular, General Marshall is still clinging to his belief that "Europe is more important than Asia" and openly stated that he is unwilling to engage in a "cold war" on two fronts, as if the Asian Chinese side is not worthy of attention. This is where a major crisis is lurking. Our newspaper's editorial will again point out the national nature of communism, which cannot be divided into important and unimportant. The Soviet Union is currently expanding its power simultaneously in Europe and Asia. If the US authorities only know one and not the other, only know the crisis in Europe and not the seriousness of the situation in Asia, then no matter what steps the United States takes, it may not be enough to stop the expansion of communist forces. It must be known that if China falls into the hands of communist rulers, with the entire red China supporting the communist camp, the map of all of Asia will change color, and the United States on the other side of the Pacific may not be able to sleep soundly. Therefore, President Truman's two speeches did not mention China, which is on the front line of anti-communism, and we really cannot help but express our regrets. We would like to reiterate to the American government and the public that the policy of Europe first and Asia later will indeed lead to irreparable disasters. If we do not quickly change course, we will inevitably repeat the mistakes and make a historical mistake!

### Burma Since Independence

â€” Our Reporter Yuan Wenzhong

On January 4, 1948, the Blue Sky White Sun banner flew high over the land of Burma. The Union of Burma, with an area of over 260,000 square miles and a population of over 14 million, was thus freed from the 61-year rule of the British Empire, beginning her new life of independence and autonomy.

Independence Ceremony

On March 11th, Lord Mountbatten and his wife flew from Calcutta to Rangoon. On the 12th, they presided over the ceremony of returning the Burmese throne, completing the final ritual of Burmese independence. The press corps took the first flight of the China National Aviation Corporation (CNAC) between China and Burma to visit Burma, and it coincided with this event, so we were able to participate in this most precious and exciting moment in Burmese history. The Burmese throne was built in 1875 by King Mindon in Mandalay. It is 30 feet and 10 inches high, gilded, and shaped much like a Chinese paifang. There are steps at the back to go up. In 1885, the British captured Mandalay, and the Burmese king was exiled to Bombay. In 1902, Lord Curzon dismantled the throne, packed it into 36 boxes, and transported it to India, where it was displayed in the Indian Museum until now. After Burma became independent, the throne was shipped back to Rangoon from India in 60 sections on February 6th and reassembled in the hall of what used to be the Governor's Office and is now the Presidential Palace. The ceremony presided over by Mountbatten took place in this city hall. On that day, there were hundreds of people including foreign envoys, guests, and Burmese commissioners participating. The ceremony was very simple, with only Mountbatten and the Burmese President each reading a moving speech. The curtain covering the seats of honor was unveiled by Mountbatten, and at the same time the Burmese national anthem was played, thus the form of Burmese independence was successfully completed. Although Mountbatten was still so handsome, he seemed to carry a tired and indifferent expression throughout the ceremony. It is not difficult for people to understand how this lord, who was performing the task of removing a jewel from the British Empire's crown, felt.

Britain Holds the Trump Card

Thus, Britain officially withdrew from the political stage in Burma, but in military and economic terms, she still holds the invincible trump card. The Anglo-Burmese Defense Agreement signed on August 29, 1947, stipulated that British troops should withdraw as soon as possible and that all military equipment should be transferred to the Burmese government. In addition, both sides agreed that Britain should send a military advisory group of the navy, army, and air force to train the Burmese army. The Burmese government must not accept military advisors from other countries other than Britain. "Both sides'" naval vessels may enter each other's territory with notification, and "both sides'" air force planes may fly over each other's territory with notification. "Both sides'" armies may also enter each other's territory when necessary. This agreement is provisionally valid for three years. In other words, British military power in Burma can be controlled for at least three years. Economically, Burma's largest industries and businesses are still firmly in the hands of the British. Several large companies, such as the Burma Oil Company, the Irrawaddy Flotilla Company, and Steel Brothers & Co., control Burma's industry, shipping, and commerce. Their deep-rooted power cannot be shaken in a short period of time.

Government Personnel

Even so, Burma is finally independent. The current Burmese government claims to be an anti-fascist, left-leaning government. It was originally composed of three political parties: the Socialist Party, the People's Volunteer Organization, and the Communist Party, and was named the Anti-Fascist People's Freedom League (AFPFL). However, after coming to power, the Communist Party withdrew due to disagreements over its political program and split into the Red Flag Communist Party and the White Flag Communist Party. The Red Flag is the most leftist radical party and has now been declared an illegal party by the government. The White Flag is more moderate, but it also refuses to cooperate with the government. In addition, the right-wing opposition parties include the Dobama Asiayone, the Patriotic Party, and the People's Party. In last year's world-shaking Burmese coup, Aung San, chairman of the AFPFL, was assassinated by U Saw, leader of the Patriotic Party. U Saw was recently sentenced to death by the court. Prime Minister Thakin Nu, the vice-chairman of the AFPFL, holds the actual power.

Clarifying Policies

Regarding the current government's policies, Thakin Nu elaborated in detail at the Constituent Assembly. He said: "Why should Burma go to the left? Because Burma's wealth is completely concentrated in the hands of the first British, second Indian, and third Chinese capitalists. The Burmese common people live in the lowest level of poverty, gnawing on the bones left by others. This kind of unfair situation must be eliminated, but its meaning is not to create a small number of Burmese capitalists to replace the British, Indian, and Chinese capitalists, while the majority of the people remain poor and suffering. Therefore, the government's policy is to establish a system that benefits all the people, so that all the people can enjoy its benefits. This policy is not radical, and it cannot be successful overnight. This policy is not completely Soviet-style either, because Burma's geographical environment is not as close to the Soviet Union as Czechoslovakia or Bulgaria. The most important task of the Burmese government today is to do its utmost to make the country strong. Only when the country is stronger can we move further forward!"

Economic Status Quo

Indeed, until now, Burma's wealth is completely in the hands of the British, Indians, and Chinese. The British are far above, while the Indians and Overseas Chinese hold the middle and lower levels of industry and commerce. In the downtown area of Rangoon, it is simply the world of the British, Indians, and Overseas Chinese. It is rare to see shops opened by Burmese people. The Burmese government is now striving to move towards national economic socialism. It announced a real estate law prohibiting the sale or mortgage of real estate in Burma to foreigners, but this only affects the Overseas Chinese, while British and Indian residents can still be an exception. It also controls import and export trade, stipulating that 50 percent of the quota should be given to Burmese merchants, but because the Burmese merchants themselves do not have this ability, they either transfer the license or cooperate with foreigners, so the results are still obtained by foreign merchants. It also wants to nationalize the mining industry, but because of financial difficulties, it cannot afford to pay the purchase price, so it is unlikely to be realized in the near future. In 1774, King Louis XIV of France even accepted this experiment himself and did not experience any adverse reactions. The result confirmed that vaccination could prevent smallpox. Based on these experimental results, Jenner completed a masterpiece in 1798, using a rigorous and upright attitude, insightful and decisive intelligence, and wrote a report entitled "For the investigation of the cause and effect of cowpox as it is called by the people of West England." Not only did it awaken people at that time to pay attention to preventive medicine, but it is also a model for us public health officials to strive for today.

Not to mention antiques, even houses, fields, and large mansions change owners and surnames. The emperors always say that they will be boundless for generations, but it is just a good-sounding word, a good-looking pattern. Not only is it impossible in fact, but it is also unnecessary in principle. Good things can be appreciated by everyone. If they are permanently locked in one family and one surname, it is not truly cherishing antiques.

The seals of gentlemen with clearer insights are not like that. Furthermore, "Once collected in a certain place," and the certain place is the name of the current owner. The prefix "Once" means that sooner or later it will belong to others, and "Once in my hands" is also optimistic. When others get it in the future, they will definitely laugh when they see the seal. He has already said it in advance.

Because of this, in many rubbings, both before and after, and even in the heaven and earth margins, various kinds of inscriptions such as "Someone's collection," "Someone's guarding," "Someone's seen," "Someone's," and "Someone's family" are stamped, which is very lively, and people who buy rubbings are even more interested in this, because it has been proven by many predecessors.
