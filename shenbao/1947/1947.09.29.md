### The Ministry of Economic Affairs Re-drafts the Commercial Registration Law Draft

[Our Nanjing Office, 28th Dispatch] The Commercial Registration Law Draft,

was re-drafted by the Ministry of Economic Affairs because it believed that the implementation of the current Commercial Registration Law

was not entirely suitable for the current situation. It was

reviewed by the Ministers of Social Affairs, Economic Affairs, Finance, and Judicial Administration, as well as Political Committee Member Ju Zhen, and then submitted to the Executive Yuan for discussion. Now,

it is learned that they all agreed that this new registration law draft is very

essential, and all expressed their approval. The key points and advantages of the new

law are summarized as follows: The new law combines the current Commercial

Registration Law and its Implementation Rules into one law, adding two chapters on Commercial Names and Businesses Operated by Foreigners.

The articles are quite appropriate and not excessive. Combining the law and the

implementation rules into one makes it easier for the people to understand and comply with, and the implementation is more convenient (as is the case with the new Company Law and Banking Law).

The new law does not adopt a listing method for the scope of commerce,

but instead, like the Company Law, uses a definition-based provision. For small-scale

businesses, the central competent department is given the discretion to approve it at any time, making it more flexible in practice. The new law changes the free registration principle

to a mandatory registration principle, so that the government can use the registration system to

manage commerce reasonably and promote its normal development.

The new law requires that registrations other than the establishment or alteration of a business, such as partnership registration, manager registration,

agent registration, registration of persons with limited capacity for action, and registration of agents, etc., should be

registered simultaneously at the time of establishment or alteration, to simplify the process. The new law adds a section on Commercial Names,

with clear provisions for the protection and exercise of exclusive rights to trade names,

and still adopts the usage principle in registration to conform to the facts.

The new law adds a chapter on Foreign Businesses,

hoping to use registration methods to properly manage foreign businesses, and to echo the chapter on Foreign Companies in the new Company Law, thereby aligning with the purpose of utilizing

foreign investment to develop post-war industries.

[Our Nanjing Office, 28th Dispatch] The Commercial Registration Law Draft

was submitted to a provisional meeting of the Executive Yuan for discussion on the 27th. It was resolved to have the Ministry of Economic Affairs publish the draft, solicit opinions from public and private legal entities,

and then submit it to the Political Affairs Conference for revision. The key points of the draft are recorded as follows:

Chapter One, "General Provisions," consisting of eight articles, explains various

terms, defining a merchant as an enterprise engaged in business for profit,

excluding technical and specialized professions. Unless otherwise provided by law, commercial registration shall be governed by the provisions of this law. Small-scale businesses may not be subject to the provisions of this law.

The scope of small-scale businesses shall be determined by the central competent authority, taking into account the economic conditions within the provinces and directly administered municipalities, and shall be separately proposed and

approved by the Executive Yuan for promulgation. If the business operated by a commercial entity requires prior approval from the relevant professional competent authorities in accordance with laws and regulations, it shall

apply for registration in accordance with this law after obtaining the approval documents.

Chapter Two, "Registered Items and Their Alteration and Cancellation,"

consisting of eleven articles, explains various procedures after registration, etc. Article 9 states that a business shall, within fifteen days of its establishment, report the following

items and apply to the competent authority of the city or county where its head office is located for establishment registration:

Business name, business operations, location of the head office, sole proprietorship or partnership,

total capital, name and address of the business owner or partners,

type and amount of contribution of each partner in a partnership,

whether the business owner or partners have persons with capacity for action or persons with limited capacity for action, the reasons therefor, the limitations on their business, and the name and address of their legal representative,

if a partnership has partners who execute business, their names, if there is a manager, their name, address, and limitations on their management authority,

if there is an agent, their name, address, and limitations on their agency, if there is a special seal for business use, the seal. If a partnership has been registered under the preceding paragraph, those who contribute or agree to contribute but are not registered as partners shall be deemed to be

nominal partners. Article 10 states that in the case of a branch office established by a business, the same items as above shall be reported for registration within fifteen days of establishment. Article 11

states that a person with limited capacity for action who, with the permission of their legal representative, independently operates a business, or is a partner, or a shareholder with unlimited liability, shall have their legal representative apply to the competent authority

of the city or county where the business is located for registration. If the legal representative discovers that the person with limited capacity for action is incompetent in the aforementioned act, and revokes their permission or increases their restrictions, they shall apply to the said authority

for registration of the reasons therefor. Article 13 explains that when there is an alteration or termination after registration, an application for alteration registration shall be made to the original registration authority within fifteen days of the alteration or termination. Also, if the registered business owner or partner

experiences situations such as inheritance, transfer, cessation of business, or dissolution, the alteration registration shall also be made within fifteen days.

Chapter Three, "Commercial Names," consisting of twelve articles, stipulates

the following as names that may not be used as commercial names: Names that are the same as the name of the Republic of China, the name or pseudonym of the Father of the Nation, the name of a government agency, or the name of a legal entity; The use of the words "China",

or something that is harmful to public order and morals or could deceive the public.

Chapter Four, "Businesses Operated by Foreigners," consisting of five articles, stipulates that in the case of a business operated by a foreigner whose head office is located

within China, if it is a sole proprietorship, the business owner shall have a residence within China; if it is a partnership, at least one of the partners shall have a residence within China. If the head office of a foreign business is not

located within China, but it establishes a branch office within China, and the business owner or partners all do not have a residence within China, they shall designate a person with a residence within China

as their agent for litigation and non-litigation matters. The aforementioned agent shall report their

name, nationality, and address and attach their letter of authorization when the branch office applies for establishment registration. This chapter also stipulates that it applies to businesses operated jointly by foreigners and Chinese in the following circumstances: If the contribution of foreign partners exceeds half of the total partnership capital, If the number of foreign partners exceeds the number of Chinese partners, If the Chinese partners do not have a residence within China.

Chapter Five, "Registration Procedures and Fees," consisting of twenty articles.

Article 37 stipulates that the establishment, transfer, and cancellation of registration of the head office of a business shall be applied for by the business owner or all partners. The alteration registration of the head office, except as otherwise provided in Chapter Two regarding transfer and the circumstances of persons with limited capacity for action, shall be applied for by the business owner, partners, or partners who execute business. Article 38 states that in the case of a business operated by a foreigner within China, the establishment, transfer, or cancellation registration of its head office shall be applied for by the business owner or all partners with a residence within China; the alteration registration of its head office, or the establishment, alteration, or cancellation registration of its branch office, shall be applied for by the business owner, the partners who execute business, or the partners with a residence within China. Article 39 states that commercial registration may be applied for by an agent entrusted by the business owner or the partners with the obligation to apply. The application of the agent shall be accompanied by

a letter of authorization from the business owner or the partners with the obligation to apply. Article 41 states that if a business operated by a foreigner within China requires a special permit, the original or a video copy of the special permit shall be attached when applying for establishment registration. Article 44 states that if the documents submitted by a foreigner are in a foreign language, a Chinese translation shall be attached. Article 45 states that if the applicant for registration discovers that there is an error or omission in the registered items after registration, they may apply to the city or county competent authority for correction within one month. Article 47 states that the city or county competent authority shall issue a registration certificate for the approved registration of the head office or branch office. Article 48 states that if a business applies for alteration registration, causing the contents of the original registration certificate to be altered, it shall surrender its original registration certificate and apply for the issuance of a new registration certificate. Article 49 states that when a business applies for cancellation registration, its registration certificate shall be cancelled. Article 52 A business applying for registration shall pay the registration fee in accordance with the following provisions: The establishment registration of the head office, one-thousandth of its total capital; The establishment registration of a branch office, A. If the capital is divided, one-thousandth of its capital; B. If the capital is not divided, 10,000 national currency for each branch office. Alteration registration, A. If the capital is increased, one-thousandth of the increase in capital; B. If the capital is not increased. 5,000 national currency each time; Cancellation registration, 5,000 national currency; Other registrations, 5,000 national currency each time. Article 54 The city and county competent authorities shall prepare the following registration books and record the registered items one by one: Head office registration book, Branch office registration book, Registration book for business rights of persons with limited capacity for action. The format of the aforementioned registration book shall be prescribed by the central competent authority, and each city and county competent authority shall make and use it themselves in accordance with the format. Article 53 Stakeholders of a business may request the city or county competent authority to inspect the registration items concerning the business in the registration book and its attached documents, and shall be entitled to apply for the issuance of a certificate of whether there have been alterations or whether a certain item has not been registered, and a copy or excerpt of the attached documents. The registration authority shall not refuse the aforementioned request except for justifiable reasons. ### Refuting Stryker's Argument Against Reparations

Mr. Stryker, advisor on reparations issues for the U.S. Department of the Army, recently published an article in "America Magazine" entitled "Retaliation is Waste," fundamentally opposing the United Nations' demands for reparations from Germany and Japan. Regarding the issue of Japanese reparations, he even advocated abandoning the Pauley mission's reparations plan and overturning the Far Eastern Commission's resolutions on this issue, so that Japan could retain a higher level of industry and embark on a path of self-sufficiency. When we saw Mr. Stryker's argument against reparations, we initially thought that this was merely Mr. Stryker's personal opinion and could not represent the attitude of the American diplomatic authorities towards the issue of Japanese reparations, and therefore did not deserve much attention. However, based on Mr. Stryker's brief biography, he has served as an advisor on reparations issues for the U.S. Department of the Army since November 1945, worked in Germany for a year, and then returned to the United States, where he served as chairman of the Special Committee on Japanese Reparations and represented the Department of the Army in investigating the situation in Japan. Now, he has published such a strange argument against reparations, which is indeed worthy of our attention. Moreover, this strange argument can be mutually confirmed with the delayed attitude of the Allied Headquarters in Tokyo towards the issue of reparations. Especially in the United States, after an individual's views are published in newspapers and magazines, if they can gain the approval of the majority of the people, then they can influence American policy towards Japan. Therefore, Mr. Stryker's publication of the argument against reparations will have an adverse impact on the Allies' demands for reparations, which deserves the attention of our country's government and public.

We have repeatedly read Mr. Stryker's article published in "America Magazine" and summarized the main points as follows: The United States does not need the industrial equipment of Germany and Japan, so the United States not only does not want reparations from Germany and Japan, but should also encourage or help Germany and Japan to be self-sufficient. Only in this way can the United States stop its relief efforts. Regarding the issue of reparations from Germany, the opinions of Britain and the United States are relatively close, both advocating giving Germany a better opportunity to produce more food and more goods that can be exported. However, the Soviet Union demanded 10 billion U.S. dollars in reparations from Germany, and France also wanted to collect as much as possible from Germany, which were both opposed by the United States. The Pauley plan has been approved by the Far Eastern Commission, and the U.S. representative has also expressed agreement, but the United States should abandon or revise this plan. China and the Soviet Union can both exercise veto power within the Far Eastern Commission and will inevitably express objections to the United States' revision of the Pauley plan. In order to implement its demands, the United States has no choice but to set aside the Far Eastern Commission. Even if the eventual outcome is the United States making peace with Japan alone, it is worth doing.

If we stand in the narrow, selfish position of the American people to read Mr. Stryker's grand argument, we may also think that it is reasonable. But from our perspective, Mr. Stryker's fallacies, if adopted by the American authorities, will not only mislead people and have an adverse impact on other countries, but will also inevitably lead to the United States suffering the consequences and making a major diplomatic mistake.

First, during the occupation, the United States certainly spent a large number of U.S. dollars in Germany and Japan, which the American taxpayers had to bear. However, the purpose of occupying Germany and Japan was to control them, to disarm them militarily, to make them increasingly democratic politically, and to reduce their industrial level economically, thereby fundamentally eliminating their aggressive ideology and depriving them of their aggressive strength, so as not to launch another world war. Therefore, the burden on American taxpayers today can be said to be the price of permanent peace, and certainly not a waste. The meaning of reparations is not only to punish the defeated countries, but also to thoroughly destroy their war industries so that their aggressive train cannot start again. Reparations are not about retaliation, let alone waste. It is true that the United States does not need the industrial equipment of Germany and Japan, but this certainly cannot be used as a reason to oppose reparations, nor can it be used as a reason to prevent other victorious countries from dismantling German and Japanese factories, let alone encourage and help the defeated countries maintain their industrial level, so that their aggressive ambitions will not be rekindled one day.

Second, in recent years, the Allied Headquarters has consistently adopted a lenient attitude towards Japan. This newspaper has repeatedly written articles believing that leniency is sufficient to breed evil, and special vigilance should be raised regarding Japan's rapid recovery. Mr. Stryker's views are completely the same as what the Allied Headquarters has done. Therefore, we cannot help but suspect that some people in the United States today have gradually forgotten the painful lessons of the Pearl Harbor incident, and in the minds of these people, the enemy of the United States in the Far East is no longer Japan, and in Europe it is no longer Germany. The formation of this kind of thinking is really too dangerous! Not only is it enough to promote the division of the world at present, but it is also enough to lead to another war in the future. Judging from the diplomatic offensive that the United States has adopted at the Second General Assembly of the United Nations, it can be seen that the United States is moving along this road.

Third, the Far Eastern Commission was created in accordance with the Moscow Three-Power Foreign Ministers Conference Declaration, and its sole task is to deal with the Japanese problem and draft a peace treaty with Japan. Because China, Britain, the United States, and the Soviet Union are the countries most closely related to the Far Eastern situation, they all have the special privilege of veto power. The existence of veto power is certainly sufficient to hinder the progress of the Far Eastern Commission, but for the sake of a fair and lasting peace in the Far East, the veto power should be retained, and at most it can only be restricted. The United States wants to set aside the Far Eastern Commission, and there is ultimately no legal basis for it in the process of drafting a peace treaty with Japan. Moreover, once the steps are disrupted, drafting a peace treaty with Japan will become more difficult.

Fourth, the idea of the United States making peace with Germany and Japan alone was first advocated by former President Hoover. Although American public opinion was interested in it, the American government did not pay much attention to it. Today, seeing Mr. Stryker's strange argument, he also advocates making peace alone, which shows that Hoover's advocacy has gradually become more common and powerful in the United States today. However, it must be known that peace is indivisible. If the United States truly wants to make peace with Japan alone, it is meaningless and will only cause international disputes.

In short, American policy towards Japan seems to need to be changed. We want to advise the American diplomatic authorities not to haggle over their own immediate interests, but to plan for the entire Far East.

### Improving Agricultural Production and Increasing Exports to Overcome Current Economic Difficulties; Ge Jingzhong Speaks on "How China Can Achieve Economic Self-Reliance"

Mr. Ge Jingzhong During his Broadcast (Photo by Our Newspaper)

[Report from Our Newspaper] Yesterday, this newspaper held its 26th Sunday special broadcast at the Gospel Radio Station. Mr. Ge Jingzhong, General Manager of the China Silk Company, spoke on "How China Can Achieve Economic Self-Reliance." The main points of his speech are recorded below:

Recently, the Chinese economy has encountered many difficult

problems. Not only are we ourselves anxious, but our allied countries

are also extremely concerned. This can be seen from Wedemeyer's

report. At this difficult

time, we are putting forward the slogan of "self-reliance." The

so-called "self-reliance" is not intended to be isolationist.

International relations are now very close. We need to import

materials that we lack from other countries, and other countries also need

to export materials that they lack from our country. An isolationist policy can no longer adapt to

the current situation.

Self-Reliance to Regulate Trade

For example,

when the exchange rate

is high, prices also rise. When the exchange rate is low,

prices also fall. Also, China is an agricultural country.

To build it into an industrial country, we must purchase foreign

machinery. Therefore, when we talk about self-reliance now, it is about how to regulate our foreign trade,

which is about how to adjust exports and imports.

In short, it is about how to expand and increase our country's

exports in order to exchange for foreign imports, so that they can

be balanced. China is an agricultural country, and 80-90% of its products are agricultural products. Therefore, most of our exports

are tea, silk, tung oil, and pig bristles. Before the "September 18th Incident," soybeans were the top export. In 1937,

China's exports and imports were not very different. Unfortunately, the war broke out and lasted for eight years.

Export Quality Must Be Excellent

After

victory, we

also encountered domestic unrest. Therefore, there has been a huge trade deficit recently. The most tragic month last year,

the trade deficit reached 90%, meaning that exports were only worth 10% and imports were worth

100%. According to the latest customs announcement, the trade deficit is approximately 45%. On the surface, it seems that the situation has improved, but in reality,

because the use of foreign exchange is restricted, many luxurious or excessively valuable

items have been temporarily stopped from being imported. Some may ask, "Can we reduce imports and

balance them with exports?" This is impossible. When it comes to construction, it is essential to import machinery.

When there is a disaster, it is essential to import rice from Southeast Asia and cotton from India. Since reducing imports is impossible, we can only

try our best to expand exports. This raises the issue of quality and quantity.

The quality of exports must be excellent and scientific. For example, the tea leaves from Taiwan,

after scientific roasting, are very popular when shipped to the United States and Canada. If the Chinese mainland

were to use scientific methods for roasting, would the export of tea leaves not increase significantly?

Purchasing Agricultural Products while Considering Costs

Expanding

foreign trade

by improving quality is a fundamental solution,

but it is too slow. To solve the urgent problem, the quantity

must be increased. There is a simple method for this.

That is, the prices of products purchased from farmers must take their costs into account. For each agricultural product,

the labor, fertilizer, capital, and interest involved in the production process must be accurately calculated.

If we purchase materials from farmers based on these costs, they will naturally be happy

to sell, and without encouraging them to increase production, they will naturally increase production. If the purchase price does not cover the cost,

and we force them to sell, then the farmers would rather cut down the tea trees and mulberry trees to burn as firewood than do business at a loss.

Not Incurring Foreign Debt for Construction

Therefore,

if the

government wants to increase production and expand foreign trade, it must conduct a detailed investigation into the costs

of agricultural products. This is the most appropriate method and also a method for increasing quantity.

When foreign trade expands, we can exchange our

agricultural products for foreign industrial products. This way,

we can carry out construction without incurring foreign debt. This

is the true meaning of economic self-reliance. To realize

this true meaning, we must find ways to improve

the quality and quantity of our agricultural products, increase exports, and

overcome the current economic difficulties.

, so the osmanthus tree was intertwined with the autumn examination, simply calling passing the examination

### Old Customs of the Mid-Autumn Festival

Mid-Autumn, commonly known as the Eighth Month Half, on this evening, families each have

banquets to celebrate the festival. People also use the weather of this night to predict

On the evening of Mid-Autumn, every household has flower arrangements and fragrant candles, worshipping towards the sky:

Children kowtow under the moon, playing in front of the lanterns, called worshiping the moon palace. Lu Qi's Beijing Annual Customs records: "On the evening of Mid-Autumn,

families each set up an image of the moon palace talisman, with a rabbit standing like a human on the talisman.
