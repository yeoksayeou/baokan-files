### The Conscription Problem in Shanghai

 Written by Fang Qiu

Shanghai is about to implement conscription, which is still a pioneering effort in Shanghai. As a result, the conscription process has been prepared many times and delayed many times, causing anxiety among the people and social unrest. The municipal authorities and military service agencies also lack publicity and explanation of government orders. Originally, it was an ordinary administration, but it was misunderstood by a few people as harming the people, which is an unfortunate event for the country's military service administration. The household survey of eligible young men began at the beginning of this month. According to the slight conscription process, after the household survey, there will be a physical examination, lottery, and conscription. The completion of the entire conscription process is cumbersome and time-consuming and cannot be delayed any longer. If delayed, it may reduce the efficiency of military service administration; it may also cause confusion due to incomplete procedures and the discovery of errors, which will only increase social strife and public criticism. Therefore, we hope that Shanghai's first conscription should be legal and reasonable, completed on time and in the required numbers, and strive for its ideal, striving to be a demonstration for the national military service!

Military service is an important policy of the state, the foundation of building an army, and the potential strength of the country. An independent country should have its national defense, and should have its army and its military service. The constitution of any country stipulates that the people have the obligation to perform military service. Our country's constitution also has special articles. The Military Service Law is a substantial law that promotes military service and stipulates the obligation of the people to perform military service. During the eight years of the War of Resistance against Japan, the frontline urgently needed more soldiers, and the Military Service Law provided a constant stream of soldiers. The Military Service Law brought us victory and protected our nation's survival and national security. The merits of the Military Service Law will last forever and will never decay. The Military Service Law was partially amended in light of the war experience and national defense goals. The revised Military Service Law was promulgated and implemented by the National Government on October 10th last year. At the same time, the National Government issued a proclamation to the people: "Since October 10th of the thirty-fifth year, the revised military service regulations will continue to be implemented. All our citizens must know that military service is the natural duty of everyone, and must encourage each other to respond enthusiastically so that the foundation of army building can be laid and national defense forces can be enriched." To assist in the implementation of the Military Service Law, the National Government promulgated and implemented the "Implementing Regulations of the Military Service Law" on February 19th of this year. From the perspective of the military service legal system, the military service legal system is largely complete. Local governments at all levels and military service agencies at all levels should proceed in an orderly manner, implement conscription according to law and procedures, train citizens, promote martial virtue, and enrich their patriotic feelings to enhance the survival potential of an independent country.

Since the promulgation and implementation of the Military Service Law, all localities in the country, except for the pacification zones and the Yellow River disaster areas, have implemented conscription in accordance with the law. Only Shanghai has been delayed. In terms of responsibilities, the Shanghai Municipal Government has a Department of Civil Affairs, and the Department of Civil Affairs has a Military Affairs Section dedicated to this matter; the Ministry of National Defense has also established a Division and Regiment Garrison Command in Shanghai, all of which are military service administrative agencies. In terms of expenses, the military service expenses in the Shanghai Municipal Budget for this year are 184,881,140 yuan, in addition to the defense affairs expenditure of the Division and Regiment Garrison Command. In terms of population, the population of Shanghai is more than 4 million. According to the survey conducted by the Department of Civil Affairs in October last year: there are 991,3178 men aged 18 to 20; there are 231,538 men aged 20 to 25; there are 113,550 men aged 25 to 30; there are 194,996 men aged 30 to 35; there are 179,045 men aged 35 to 40; and there are 156,791 men aged 40 to 45. Logically speaking, the conditions for implementing military service in Shanghai are very sufficient, and there are many enthusiastic and righteous people in Shanghai. If conscription is implemented, raising preferential expenses and collecting comfort supplies is also better than in other places. With these subjective and objective conditions, conscription in Shanghai should be promoted smoothly, handled in accordance with the law, and will surely be successful!

Because Shanghai's conscription has been delayed for a long time, people in neighboring provinces and counties believe that Shanghai has special circumstances and can evade military service. As a result, Shanghai's population has increased sharply in the past six months, the household registration has become chaotic, and those who evade military service are trying to obtain positions. Due to the increase in population, the lack of housing, the expansion of consumption, and the soaring prices of food and goods, the stability and order of society have been affected, affecting the control of the public security organs. If this phenomenon continues to develop, it will not be the happiness of the Shanghai society, but a hidden danger in the Shanghai society. Today's Shanghai, solving the unemployment problem and the population problem is one thing, and solving the entire society is another, and conscription must be promoted as soon as possible, and those who evade military service and concentrate in cities must be punished. This can not only assist in solving social problems, but also eliminate social chaos and anxiety. In short, in today's Shanghai, social problems and military service problems have become linked, so solving the military service problem is solving the social problem. This point, I hope the administrative authorities and social figures, must recognize, must pay attention to.

Now the household survey of eligible young men is underway, which is only the beginning of the military service process. The survey is passive, and the physical examination and lottery conscription involve legal departments and the determination of people's identity. The handling must be cautious, and the review must be in accordance with the law. Therefore, Article 33 of the Military Service Implementing Regulations stipulates that the organized Military Service Association should also be advocated by social dignitaries, from assisting military farmers to supervising military service, whether it is inspection and lottery, or review of exemption from military service, all must be fair, just, reasonable, and lawful. Shanghai is an area where Chinese and foreign people are concentrated. Everyone is paying attention to the handling of conscription. It is hoped that the evil practices of handling military service in the interior and the malpractices of promoting military service in remote areas will not occur in Shanghai.

In short, Shanghai has superior conditions for handling military service. The administrative authorities should have the responsible courage and determination to handle Shanghai's military service well, set an example for the country, and change the national atmosphere, so as to achieve the effect of moving the wind and bending the grass. The time is now. May the Shanghai Municipal Authorities and social figures work hard to achieve it!

### Preface to the Thousand Character Classic in Standard Cursive Script with Corrections

 Yu Youren

In the twenty-first year of the Republic, I established the Standard Cursive Script Society in Shanghai, extensively seeking the legacy of cursive saints from past generations, as well as Han and Jin bamboo slips and stone tablets, engaging in the organization of cursive script. The reasons for its dedication and the ultimate hope are to make the national cultural tool of more than two thousand years simpler, easier, more enjoyable, unified, and more practical. Therefore, the selection of characters is based on being easy to recognize, easy to write, beautiful, and accurate. The materials are also sought from the authors of the Zhang, Jin, and Kuang schools, so that each can make the most of their strengths and have the opportunity for balanced development. For this reason, what can be comforting to the world at this time is that in the selection of materials and characters, we have discovered the ancestral legacy accumulated over generations, a sharp weapon for progress in the new era, so that order can be found in chaos and rebellion. What is regrettable is that the era is distant, so the materials are insufficient, and the carvings are often distorted. The era is near, so the practicality is narrow, and there are few famous works passed down. It is very difficult to create a systematic ordinance! In the past, Xu Shuozhong wrote "Shuowen", and he did not determine the characters throughout his life. Chong began to present the book to Emperor An, showing the difficulty of urgently organizing characters like burning heart. I have been governing this for only ten years, relying on the help of my colleagues, and I have achieved initial success, which is also fortunate!

In the twenty-fifth year, the Hanwen Zhengkai Publishing House printed 500 copies to rush out the book, and the explanations were incomplete. In the twenty-sixth year, the second revised edition was about to be printed, but at the same time that the War of Resistance and construction were advancing, there were things that should be done, and I should rush there. Moreover, writing is the crystallization of human culture, the connection of human life, and also the total driving force of world civilization. It is also important for hundreds of industries and tens of thousands of products, which are closely related to the survival and life of the country and the nation. The construction of a new country is even more dependent on the progress of writing to promote it and speed up its success. The strong countries of Europe and America have made scientific progress, and their writing is also simple. Printing uses regular script, and writing uses chapter script, and those who learn it all know its convenience. Our country's writing is difficult, and if we want to stand on our own in the fiercely competitive world, the result must change, or it will all fall behind. From the perspective of time, Dayu cherished every moment, and Tao Kan cherished every minute, "Time, time," is what the sages valued so much! Now we know that what we should cherish and fight for today, calculated in minutes and inches, has been miscalculated! Therefore, the foundation of the country and nation for tens of thousands of generations should start from one point, one stroke, one flicker, and one second; the strength or weakness, success or failure of people and countries is determined by the length and amount of time they have gained. Although writing reform is a matter of saving time, it is a small matter, but with its wide use, it adds up to the infinite days and months of our entire nation in the future! For several years, I have not dared to slacken, and after two consultations, I have re-edited it. In the twenty-seventh year, I handed it over to the China Printing Bureau to print 300 copies, and in the twenty-ninth year, I printed 500 copies. I planned to sell them after a review, but in the meantime, only a few copies were distributed to friends, and the rest were stranded in Hong Kong, and could not be transported back. This made those who care about cultural undertakings and know that I have devoted myself to this wait endlessly, and I am deeply uneasy.

In recent years, the national construction has been advancing by leaps and bounds, but the work of writing reform that we are responsible for cannot catch up. How regrettable! This spring, my colleagues in the club gradually gathered, and I re-selected and reviewed it once. The book is divided into two volumes, the upper volume is the Thousand Character Classic of Cursive Saints, the characters are all selected from the cursive saints of ancient dynasties, carefully making the shapes, and listing the sources; the lower volume is the Explanations of the Standard Cursive Script, which summarizes the changes in cursive script from ancient to modern times, and explains them with examples. The standard symbols are made into tables, marked "Sheng Qian", "Shi Li", mutually annotating each other. The former provides the essence of cursive writing, and the latter clarifies the secrets of calligraphy history that are not passed down.

I am deeply afraid that the principles will be correct but the use will be wrong, or that the words that the ancients created occasionally will be forced on later generations who are difficult to learn. During the selection and review, I often use pens and paper to test whether they are in line. Comrade Liu Yantao has accumulated what he has written before and after, selected and hooked them into a book, and also requested that they be printed together with the Standard Cursive Script, as a reference for those who learn the Thousand Character Classic of Cursive Saints, and to increase the interest of calligraphy learners! He has worked diligently, and his contributions to me are the most. It is extremely important that my colleagues are thinking of publicizing cursive script and liberating writing, and they are doing everything they can. In the past, a certain master practiced the cursive script of a former sage, and he did not achieve success all day long, and said with joy: "The truth is also in front of me, making people discouraged!" I feel the same way now when I write this! Fortunately, the Standard Cursive Script can be printed and completed, and the principles of symbol organization and the changes in the rules are explained in detail in a list. Today's scholars will surely be encouraged and work hard, and there will be even more amazing inventions. July in the thirty-first year, Yu Youren wrote in the mountain cave.

Wishing Mr. Yu Youren a Happy Birthday - Zhu Jingxin

Today is the sixty-ninth birthday of Mr. Yu Youren, the president of the Control Yuan. I remember that when Mr. Yu celebrated his sixty-seventh birthday in Chongqing in the thirty-fourth year, he wrote two poems (already seen in this publication) which can be regarded as his biography. When Mr. Yu was born, the Qing Dynasty was, the government was corrupt, and there were foreign troubles. Therefore, he had the ambition of the rivers and mountains since childhood. In these sixty-nine years, from the revolution to the Northern Expedition to the War of Resistance, he has repeatedly fought against the evil forces of violence, writing the most glorious page in the history of the Chinese nation. Mr. Yu can just represent the Chinese people of the firecrackers, shouting to mankind.

Mr. Yu's ancestral home is Jingyang, Shaanxi, and he lives in Sanyuan, so he was born in Xinzhuang, Sanyuan. His fluttering silver beard is the demeanor of a man from Guanzhong. He lost his mother at the age of three, and was raised by his aunt, Madam Fang. At the age of seven, he entered the school, and at the age of eleven, he studied with Mr. Mao Banxiang in Sanyuan. At the age of fourteen, he entered the county school, at the age of twenty, he supplemented the , and at the age of twenty-five, he passed the imperial examination. Mr. Yu was extremely dissatisfied with the corruption of the Qing Dynasty, and once printed "The Half-Crying, Half-Laughing Building Poems" to satirize the current government. The magistrate of Sanyuan County pointed him out as a revolutionary party and reported him to the governor of Shaanxi. The governor then requested to dismiss him as a juren and strictly arrest him. At that time, Mr. Yu was in Bianliang for the Ministry of Rites examination. Mr. Li Yutian, a fellow villager, sent someone to secretly inform him. Mr. Yu then went south in secret to Shanghai and entered Aurora University, studying under Mr. Ma Xiangbo.

In the thirty-first year of the Guangxu reign of the Qing Dynasty, Aurora University was dissolved due to the interference of foreign teachers. Mr. Yu was ordered by Mr. Ma to establish Fudan Public School in Wusong. At this time, students studying in Japan returned to China due to the ban on the tide, and they also founded China Public School in Paotai Bay. Mr. Yu was also listed as a founder and served as the national language lecturer. Soon, he and his colleagues from the two schools initiated the Shenzhou Daily. After the agreement, Mr. Yu and Mr. Shao Lizi went to Tokyo to raise shares and met with President Sun Yat-sen in Tokyo. It was at this time that he joined the Tongmenghui. Therefore, the publication of Shenzhou Daily was welcomed by the intellectual class for promoting the revolution. In less than a year, Shenzhou Daily suffered a fire, and Mr. Yu resigned and reorganized Yu Lun Daily.

In the beginning, revolutionaries founded newspapers one after another to promote nationalism, and they fell one after another, which was a brilliant era in the history of newspapers. At that time, there were newspapers advocating revolution, which were known as the three people of Jian Tian and the three people of Heng. The so-called three people of Jian were the three newspapers of Min Hu, Min Yu, and Min Li, all of which were founded by Mr. Yu. After Shenzhou Daily was burned, Mr. Yu had already left, but the idea of advocating revolution always lingered in his mind. Not long after serving as the editor of Yu Lun Daily, he founded Chang Hu Daily. In the publication notice, there was a statement for the people, which was noted by the Qing court. After the publication, the advocacy was intense, and it was even more disliked by the Qing officials. He was trapped in prison for the Gansu account incident. Although the prison was later released, the publication of the newspaper was still a condition for his release. Mr. Yu then went to Japan. The following year, he returned to Shanghai and founded Min Li Daily again. At that time, the Qing court's defense against the revolution was more serious, but the editor made progress more quickly than before. On the eve of the Wuchang Uprising, Min Li Daily became the general organ of the revolutionary army. The news of the war was announced by Min Li Daily every day, and everyone wanted to see it first. The printing machine did not stop all night, but there were still people who were unable to obtain it.

In the seventh year of the Republic, the enemy in Guanzhong was in great distress. Mr. Yu responded to the invitation of the generals of the Jingguo Army and went north to preside over the Shaanxi Jingguo Army affairs. Although the number of people was very different and the food and salary were not enough, he was still able to deal with the strong enemy for several years. This was all due to Mr. Yu's sincerity, so they all died without yielding. In particular, in the Jingguo Army District, the promotion of the doctrine, the implementation of education, and the promotion of cultural affairs were able to cooperate with military affairs and promote it, so that the people of the Northwest were able to be nurtured by the spirit of the times and become the pioneers of the revolutionary forces in the North. Until the eleventh year of the Republic, the military situation became increasingly difficult, and Mr. Yu left the army and went east, passing through Wuhan to Shanghai. The Jingguo Army affairs came to an end.

In the fifteenth year of the Republic, Liu Zhenhua led his troops into Shaanxi and surrounded the city of Chang'an. Mr. Yu wanted to help Shaanxi but the road was blocked, so he took the road to Soviet Russia, crossed Mongolia south, and arrived in Shaanxi. At that time, General Tong Kuai Xiang also returned from Soviet Russia. Wan jointly established the National Army Allied Forces and swore an oath in Wuyuan. Mr. Yu then went to Sanyuan and joined the local militias of the 2nd and 3rd armies to relieve the siege of Chang'an. At that time, Chang'an had been besieged for eight or nine months, and tens of thousands of soldiers and civilians had been killed. Mr. Yu, as the commander-in-chief and acting provincial government official, comforted the soldiers and civilians and planned to deploy troops. In less than two months, he wanted to meet the Northern Expedition army in Zhengzhou.

When Mr. Yu first went to Tokyo to meet President Sun Yat-sen, President Sun Yat-sen appointed Mr. Yu as the Grand Governor of the Yangtze River. After the founding of the Republic, President Sun Yat-sen took the position of the first President in Nanjing and appointed Mr. Yu as the Minister of Transportation. In the seventeenth year of the Republic, the National Government established its capital in Nanjing, and Mr. Yu served as a Standing Committee Member of the Party, Government, and Military Affairs. The following year, he served as the President of the Audit Yuan. In the twentieth year of the Republic, Mr. Yu took office as the President of the Control Yuan, and has been in office for sixteen years. His reputation for being upright and upright is universally recognized.

Mr. Yu's calligraphy and his silver beard are both famous throughout the country. His characters are like dragons and snakes dancing, and at first glance, they seem extremely easy, but upon closer inspection, every stroke is divine. This is due to his deep skill. Mr. Yu first followed the Northern Wei Dynasty, then studied running script in middle age, and in recent years has focused on Zhangcao. He does not adhere to one school of calligraphy, and he often imitates the famous works of ancient and modern times, one character at a time, one stroke at a time, which has reached a magical state. He often imitates the two characters of Sun Wen, which were personally signed by President Sun Yat-sen, and the character  written by the martyr Huang . Not only are they powerful, but they are also exquisitely superb. Mr. Yu's calligraphy has reached a magical realm, but he does not cherish ink like gold. Whether it is a comrade, a friend, or even a peddler, even if he has never met him, he will gladly respond. Therefore, Mr. Yu's calligraphy can be seen everywhere on the market. If you go to Lan Yuan, then all the winning plaques and signboards of the organizations, schools, temples, and shops are almost all written by Mr. Yu alone.

Mr. Yu also believes that our country's characters are anti-corrosive, and writing is time-consuming, and simplified characters violate the principles of philology. Therefore, he advocates cursive script, believing that it is convenient for writing, quite beautiful, and in line with the purpose of the ancients in creating characters. In the twenty-first year of the Republic, the Standard Cursive Script Society was founded in Shanghai, collecting cursive script steles and rubbings, and engaging in organization, from divergent to unified, from spiritual to practical. It is truly a "sharp weapon of the new era."

Mr. Yu's achievements and articles do not need to be repeated. Although he has reached the high age of sixty-nine, his spirit is still extremely strong, making young people see him and their emotions become even more excited, making old people see him and relive their youthful feelings, and making the sick and weak see him and encourage their courage to fight. His spirit is stronger than most people.
