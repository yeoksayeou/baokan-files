Japanese Provisional Cotton Yarn and Cloth Management Bureau Formulates and Implements Regulations for Purchasing Cotton Yarn and Cloth

After the National Government decided to implement the purchase of cotton yarn and cloth, the Japanese side also stipulated that all cotton yarn and cloth owned by Japanese merchants and civilians should be purchased by the Japanese Provisional Cotton Yarn and Cloth Management Bureau of the China Trade Federation at the same price as China. The payment price will be paid in Japanese yen-denominated registered bonds, and other matters will be handled in accordance with the regulations of our country. It is learned that the Japanese Provisional Cotton Yarn and Cloth Management Bureau has formulated regulations for the purchase of cotton yarn and cloth, which were submitted to the Shanghai Office of the Japanese Embassy on the 14th for approval and implementation. The full text of the regulations is as follows:

Increase or Decrease Value Based on Packaging

Article 1: The purchase of yarn and cloth (including other cloth, the same below) in accordance with the instructions of the Shanghai Office of the Japanese Embassy and the Japanese Consulate General shall be carried out in accordance with these regulations, unless otherwise stipulated.

Article 2: The purchase price of yarn and cloth, regardless of the other party, shall be based on 10,000 yuan per bale of 20-count Blue Phoenix, including transportation and tax. The purchase price mentioned above refers to large bales packed in linen bags with copper skins, or packaged with cotton cloth instead of packaging. In the case of processed cotton cloth, it refers to boxed packaging. If the packaging is different or packaged in original kraft paper, the price will be increased or decreased, which will be determined separately.

The reduction in price for inferior goods and those with defective packaging will also be determined separately.

Purchase Price Paid with Bonds

Article 3: The payment of the purchase price shall be made in accordance with separate regulations, with Grade A registered public funds of the Japanese Imperial Government. Article 4: Holders of yarn and cloth in the Shanghai area as of August 9, Showa 18 (hereinafter referred to as the seller) shall fill out four copies each of the summary table of goods for sale application (Form No. 1) and the application for sale (Form No. 2) prescribed by the municipal association, classifying them into cotton yarn, cotton cloth raw materials, and processed cotton cloth. These documents shall be submitted to the Provisional Yarn and Cloth Management Bureau (hereinafter referred to as the Secretariat) through its affiliated trade association or trade union and the China Japanese Cotton Products Trade Federation (hereinafter referred to as the Federation) before August 25, Showa 18.

For cotton cloth raw materials and processed cotton cloth other than textile products, two samples of cloth shall be attached to the application.

Retailers of yarn and cloth or manufacturers or processors who use yarn and cloth as raw materials must, at the same time as the sales application in paragraph 1, submit to this association four copies of the performance report (Form No. 3) prescribed by this association, stating the monthly sales results of yarn and cloth or the monthly actual usage for the first half of Showa 18.

Apply for Various Procedures for Goods Prices

Article 5: When this association accepts the application mentioned above, it shall add a blue acceptance number to the summary table of goods for sale application and the sales application, and return it to the seller through the Federation and the affiliated trade association or trade union.

Article 6: This association may send inspectors to inspect the items, quantities, storage conditions, etc. declared by the seller.

Article 7: When this association deems it necessary, it may move the goods for sale application before the purchase.

Article 8: After this association inspects the goods, it shall decide on the items, quantities, and purchase price for purchase, and affix the purchase certificate seal to the sales application. One copy shall be used as the purchase agreement certificate, which shall be returned to the seller through the Federation and the affiliated trade association or trade union.

Article 9: When this association inspects, moves, or inspects the goods, it may request the seller or their agent to cooperate.

Article 10: The seller shall not raise any objections to the matters decided by this association after inspecting the goods.

Article 11: After this association has completed the inspection and acceptance, it shall deliver the payment certificate to the seller within five days after receiving the goods, or when exchanging warehouse receipts, insurance policies, and tax receipts.

In the case of the preceding paragraph, the delivery of the payment certificate shall be made at the same time as the delivery of the purchase contract certificate in Article 8 using the same procedure.

Article 12: In the case of the preceding paragraph, the storage fee (including warehouse rent and insurance fee) for the goods shall be borne by the seller within one week of the delivery of the payment certificate. The seller shall complete the procedures with the storage-related parties in advance regarding the payment of the storage fee that they are responsible for.

Article 13: If the seller is not a member or member of a trade association or trade union affiliated with the Federation, they shall entrust their affiliated organization or apply directly to this association for sale.

Measures for Dealing with Illegal Forgery

Article 14: When this association discovers yarn and cloth with an unknown owner, it may request the possessor of the yarn and cloth to hand it over.

When this association hands over the yarn and cloth mentioned in the preceding paragraph, it shall deliver a temporary purchase contract certificate and a temporary payment certificate to the possessor mentioned in the preceding paragraph, and retain a corresponding amount when paying the price.

If there is a legitimate owner of the yarn and cloth in the first paragraph, they may receive the temporary purchase contract certificate and the temporary payment certificate from the possessor in the first paragraph, and submit a document proving the right relationship to this association to request payment of the purchase price.

Article 15: In the event of failure to submit forms and applications without legitimate reason, or false statements, refusal to inspect, move, or inspect goods, or improper interference, and other acts that impede the purchase, this association shall report to the relevant authorities.

Article 16: Matters not specified in these regulations shall be determined separately by the president of the association.

### Supplementary Provisions

These regulations shall be implemented from the date of approval by the Japanese Consulate General in Shanghai.

The Extreme Evils of Individual Freedom

Someone may ask, "Since we are adopting an open-minded approach, can't we adopt any ideology? Why must it be the Three Principles of the People?" My answer is as follows:

Dr. Sun Yat-sen's will states, "I have devoted myself to the National Revolution for forty years." Dr. Sun passed away in 1925, which is now fifty-eight years ago. Dr. Sun said in his preface, "In the years of the Sino-French War, I first conceived the ambition to overthrow the Qing Dynasty and establish the Republic of China." He launched the first revolution in the year of Yiwu. During that time, from the Revive China Society to the Chinese Revolutionary Alliance, to the Chinese Revolutionary Party, to the Chinese Nationalist Party, the names and organizations changed, but the ideology remained consistent. I ask, is there a second ideology or a second party in China with such a long history? Moreover, the Three Principles of the People and the Chinese Nationalist Party having such a long history is by no means accidental. Some people think that the Three Principles of the People emphasizes the unity of the nation in nationalism, the freedom of the state in democracy, and the livelihood of society in people's livelihood, which is almost based on the theory of collectivism, and cannot escape the mold of Italian Fascism and German National Socialism. This is completely wrong. As mentioned above, the Three Principles of the People originated fifty years ago. Its form as a theory, and its complexities, began forty years ago. The lectures on the Three Principles of the People were completed twenty years ago, long before the establishment of Italian Fascism and the German National Socialist Party. The so-called "mold" is nothing but nonsense. Some people also think that the Principle of People's Livelihood was actually influenced by Marxism, so the Principle of People's Livelihood is a byproduct of communism. This is even more ridiculous. "Min Bao" was the first to criticize Marxism (then translated as "Mai Si"). It made a strict and fair criticism of its class struggle theory early on. Later, regarding class struggle, the Principle of People's Livelihood also gave an earnest warning. The so-called Principle of People's Livelihood is communism is just a way to say that if you follow the Principle of People's Livelihood, you can achieve the Great Harmony and don't need to seek communism separately. The meaning is very clear. How could there be a misunderstanding? Some people also believe that the Principle of People's Rights in the Three Principles of the People is not actually the democracy advocated by Europe and America. The so-called democracy in Europe and America is based on individual liberalism, while the Principle of People's Rights in the Three Principles of the People is not based on individual liberalism, so it cannot be said to be democracy. This kind of argument is just the ingrained habit of people who call themselves intellectuals to regard British and American theories as divine truth. It must be understood that individual liberalism has had its glorious achievements in Europe, but it also has its extreme evils. During the Middle Ages in Europe, thought was suppressed by religion, and politics and economy were extremely oppressed by feudalism, which is still called the Dark Ages. What broke this Dark Ages was individual liberalism, seeking liberation from religious constraints in thought, and seeking to overthrow feudal extremes in politics and economy. Rousseau's "Social Contract" has a weak historical basis, but his ideas of freedom have spread among the general public, thus awakening the 18th-century revolution. From the "Declaration of the Rights of Man" of the French Revolution to the constitutions of various countries, the rights and obligations of the people are based on individual liberalism. Individual liberalism truly had its glorious history in the 17th and 18th centuries. However, by the 19th century, the evils of individual liberalism had already been exposed. The culmination of individual liberalism is the denial of the state. Why would it deny the state? Because the theory of individual liberalism believes that the so-called state is only a tool to protect individual freedom. According to this reasoning, if the state hinders individual freedom, it should naturally be abandoned.

Collectivism Arises as the Times Demand

With the passage of time, the capitalist system has replaced the feudal system. In the feudal era, the landlords controlled the economy. In the capitalist era, the capitalists control the economy. Relying on the power of their capital, they enslaved laborers, turning them into slaves of machines, and attributed their gains to themselves. Their tyranny and willfulness are hundreds of times worse than those of landlords. The general public hates these capitalists and therefore hates the state that protects these capitalists. Therefore, they deny the state more vehemently, attacking it in every way, leaving no room for it. Anarchism arises from this. In fact, it is utterly confused. How do capitalists arise? Because of private capitalism. How does private capitalism arise? Because of individual liberalism. Instead of correcting the evils of individual liberalism, they attack the state. Isn't this putting the cart before the horse? For this reason, collectivism arises as the times demand. Collectivism has been foreshadowed in ancient philosophy. It was re-proposed at the beginning of the 19th century. Although there are different schools and different theories, there is one key point in common: Individual liberalism recognizes that the state is only a tool to protect individual freedom, while collectivism recognizes that the state is an organism, and the individual is only a cell in this organism. Cells are indeed important. If the cells are strong and powerful, the whole can be strong and powerful. If the cells are sick, the whole will also be sick. Collectivism never denies the importance of cells, but cells cannot operate independently of the whole. Cells within the whole must engage in organized and systematic activities. This key point is neglected by individual liberalism. The evil of individual liberalism is the denial of the state. Collectivism, on the other hand, puts the state first. Fifty years ago, when individual liberalism was in its heyday, everyone praised it as the mother of modern civilization, but Dr. Sun Yat-sen was uniquely able to see through its weaknesses and adopt the essence of collectivist theory as the basis for the theory of the Three Principles of the People. This truly makes us admire him. At the same time, he saw through the pernicious influence of communism and corrected and prevented it early on. This truly makes us admire him.

Methods for Implementing the Three Principles of the People

The Three Principles of the People already has a theoretical foundation, which is to seek the happiness of the entire people, to seek the freedom and equality of the entire people. The so-called entire people can only be represented by the state. Therefore, the first sentence of the will is: "I have devoted myself to the National Revolution for forty years." The second sentence is: "To seek freedom and equality for China." The Three Principles of the People are originally a whole. In terms of national relations, it is nationalism. In terms of its political system, it is democracy. In terms of its economic organization, it is the Principle of People's Livelihood. Like a glass, in terms of its material, it is glass. In terms of its name, it is glass. In terms of its use, it is for holding drinks. I have never agreed with Mr. Hu Hanmin's book on the operation of the Three Principles of the People, because three can be used in a linked way, but one cannot. The Three Principles of the People are clearly one, not three. However, I am not willing to argue too much, because all believers who know difficulty in easy practice should understand the principle of knowing after acting. Knowing without acting is just not knowing. Now the Three Principles of the People has not yet been implemented, so how can one say that they know it? Therefore, in the New National Movement, after highlighting our original Three Principles of the People, we then put forward methods for implementation.

It is Necessary to Implement Pan-Asianism

The first point is that to implement nationalism, we must implement Pan-Asianism. Do you students think this is a forced connection? In fact, there is no forced connection at all. The purpose of nationalism is to liberate China from its semi-colonial status. How did China fall into a semi-colonial status? Because it is bound by unequal treaties. What are unequal treaties? They are the Treaty of Nanjing after the Opium War and the Treaty of Tianjin that arose from it. These were all imposed on China by Britain and the United States. The purpose of Pan-Asianism is to awaken the nations of Asia and jointly break free from the shackles of Britain and the United States. In November of 1924, Dr. Sun Yat-sen delivered a speech on Pan-Asianism in Kobe, hoping that Japan would assist China in abolishing unequal treaties. This is a consistent argument, an unwavering belief. Speaking of this, there is something to congratulate you students on. In January of this year, Japan and China both participated in the Greater East Asia War. On the same day, Japan and China signed a mutual agreement to return concessions ahead of schedule and abolish extraterritoriality. At the end of March, it first returned the exclusive concessions in various places. Subsequently, due to Japanese mediation, the French exclusive concessions and the Shanghai International Settlement were completely returned on August 1. In the abolition of extraterritoriality, the right to taxation was also given priority. We must follow Dr. Sun Yat-sen's instructions to abolish unequal treaties, and strive to love our country, love our neighbors, and move forward on the bright path of common love for East Asia.

Implement a Democratic Centralized System

The second point is that to realize democracy, we must implement a democratic centralized system. As mentioned above, the Principle of People's Rights emphasizes the freedom of the state, so the false democracy based on individual liberalism is not the Principle of People's Rights. The individual liberalism adopted creates a private capitalist system economically. As mentioned above, in politics, it creates so-called democratic elections, which are completely manipulated by capitalists. The people elected are just tools of the capitalists, representing the capitalists, speaking in the parliament. Capitalists come in all shapes and sizes, so their representatives also come in all shapes and sizes, creating a chaotic political phenomenon. So it is false, and it is not only false, but also corrupt. Even if there are so-called representatives of the proletariat, they are just like capitalist representatives, only representing the interests of one class, not the interests of the entire people, only making the chaotic phenomenon even more chaotic. This is what the Principle of People's Rights rejects. The Principle of People's Rights emphasizes the principle of ability. This is in line with the "selection of the virtuous and capable" in the Li Yun. Only the people selected in this way can work for the country, not for one class or one individual. This is how it is with talent. In terms of organization, the focus is on centralization. The separation of the five powers seems like a separation of powers, but having someone in charge becomes centralization. However, this centralization is democratic centralization, not individual dictatorship. Individual dictatorship is effective in exerting talent and responding quickly to opportunities, but the disadvantages outweigh the advantages. The biggest disadvantage is not hearing criticism. All individual dictators consider themselves sacred and do not like others to say they are wrong. Over time, they become deaf to advice. When the general public sees that they are wrong, they dare not speak up, but they dare to laugh in their hearts. Over time, they rebel and care about nothing, and they overthrow without any consideration. All individual dictatorships have no good ending, partly because of this. Democracy without centralization is false, and centralization without democracy is a dark individual dictatorship. These are what the Principle of People's Rights does not adopt. The essence of democratic centralization is "full research before a major event is decided, and unified implementation after it is decided." And the most important method for implementing this is to "always think of knowing difficulty in easy practice, and implement it bravely," which is to "seek knowledge earnestly."

Implement the Development of State Capital

The third point is that to realize the Principle of People's Livelihood, we must implement the development of state capital. Regarding this point, it is most important to explain that the Chinese Nationalist Party repeatedly stated between 1924 and 1925 that what China needs now is the development of industry, not communism, because there is no property yet, so what is there to share? The communists could not deny this explanation, but they said sharply and bitterly, "Developing industry requires capital. Then why does the Nationalist Party talk about controlling private capital? Isn't this like telling a starving person to diet?" Therefore, they went around saying, "So we must first develop private capital in order to develop industry. When industry is developed, then we will share it." Thus, class struggle becomes useful. Little do they know that what the Principle of People's Livelihood advocates is both the control of private capital and the development of state capital. The Principle of People's Livelihood does not forget to develop capital in order to develop industry. However, the purpose of the Principle of People's Livelihood is to seek the happiness of the entire people, not to seek the happiness of one class or one person. Therefore, capital is operated by the state, and industry is run by the state. Only in this way can we consider the interests of the country and distinguish the priorities. This is absolutely different from private capitalism. The purpose of private capitalism is purely to make money. It will do whatever industry can make more money. Although the result of industrial development may be beneficial to the country, it will never consider the interests of the country and distinguish the priorities. Therefore, it is necessary to develop state capital in order to run industries that the country considers the most important and urgent, which is so-called planned economy, also called controlled economy. The state operates the most important and urgent industries itself, and the rest, which are relatively less important and less urgent, can be left to private individuals to manage. In this way, the state holds the largest and most important part of industry. The state belongs to the entire people, and the state's industry also belongs to the entire people. Then what is there to share? So with state property, there is no need to talk about communism anymore. This kind of planned economy is actually common in various countries today. The Soviet Union did implement communism when it first started its revolution, but later it realized that it would not work and hurriedly changed to the New Economic Policy, which is a planned economy. The subsequent two five-year economic plans were even more planned economies and had nothing to do with communism. What Italy and Germany implemented was also a planned economy. Other countries also implement the most important and urgent industries in the spirit of planned economy. Planned economy has no room for doubt today. Even if there are concerns that state management often becomes bureaucratic, the benefits outweigh the disadvantages, and this disadvantage is not impossible to prevent. In short, the private capitalist system that comes from individual liberalism only knows how to make money for individuals. The communism that comes from the theory of class struggle has no way to share when there is no property. When there is state industry, there is no need to talk about sharing anymore. This is an extremely clear and simple way of saying it. We can only strive towards developing state capital as indicated by the Principle of People's Livelihood.

The main points of the first to third points of the New National Movement are summarized as such. We don't know much about the Three Principles of the People. Now the one thing we are putting forward is implementation, the second thing is implementation, and the third thing is still implementation. (End of Lecture 2)
