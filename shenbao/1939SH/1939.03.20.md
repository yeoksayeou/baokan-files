(I) The Key to Victory or Defeat in Modern Warfare

In the World War of 1914, Germany single-handedly resisted and fought for four years, ultimately suffering defeat. This was not due to unfavorable conditions on the front lines, but rather to the inadequacy of the home front. General Ludendorff, based on his experience in the Great War, advocated the theory of total war, believing that in modern warfare there is no distinction between the front and the rear. Victory or defeat is determined not by the quantity of armaments but by the strength of the nation's resources. This is no longer limited to the clash of the two armies, but a struggle between two great nations.

As the ancient saying goes, "Victory or defeat is a common occurrence for military strategists." This is especially true today. Temporary gains or losses are not enough to be a key to victory. The endurance of the war, leading to final victory, depends on the resilience of the entire nation and the strength of the entire national economy, especially the latter as a prerequisite for the material basis of a long-term war of resistance.

Furthermore, modern international relations are intricate and complex. Disputes between two countries are often sufficient to trigger conflicts of interest involving related third countries or even the entire world. China, often referred to as a semi-colonial country, has always been a battleground for great powers. Japan's war against China is undoubtedly aimed at excluding the influence of Europe and America in the Far East, blockading China's door, and allowing Japan to enjoy it exclusively. Therefore, since the September 18th Incident, the Sino-Japanese conflict has long been a major performance on the international political stage. Since the July 7th Incident developed into the August 13th Battle, although the great powers have not actively performed due to various reasons, their spiritual and material sympathy and assistance to China have played a considerable role. Especially today, after the world has become aware of Japan's ambition to swallow China alone, they can no longer sit idly by. Our country is fighting for independent survival and has national resources hundreds of times greater than Japan as its backing. Whether or not we can obtain foreign resources is not enough to sway our determination and real strength to win the war. However, obtaining foreign resources during wartime can indeed make the strength of the war of resistance more substantial. Moreover, our country's industry is backward, and modern military-industrial industry has no foundation to speak of. Therefore, striving for overseas resources is an indispensable step in the great cause of the war of resistance. The European War ended with the participation of the United States. On the surface, it was because the Allied Powers had this new force to join, and they were able to defeat the exhausted German army. In essence, it was because Germany was poor in resources, and therefore could not use overseas resources to make up for it, which led to its rapid collapse.

Our government, aware of this, ordered the stabilization of finance at the beginning of the war, engaged in the relocation of industries, promoted the industrial cooperative movement, and built the Southwest. (II) The International Development of Resisting Aggression

Resisting aggression lies in our resolute resistance to the war until the final victory. (1) Prohibit the supply of military or industrial raw materials to Japan. Because Japan's military and industrial raw materials are largely dependent on foreign countries, if various countries stop supplying them, even if it is limited to a small number of major items, it will inevitably be severely hit.

(2) Boycott Japanese goods and Japanese ships. Japan's international balance of payments depends on the export of industrial products and its shipping income to achieve balance. (6) Revival of the Cash Market Shanghai's gold market was originally a place for speculators to operate. In the past, monthly transactions often reached three to four million taels, but after the currency reform, it became deserted, with monthly transactions of only four to five thousand taels. After the outbreak of the Shanghai war, it stopped completely. China's monetary system was originally based on silver, but since the implementation of the legal tender policy, it has become a managed currency. The exchange rate with foreign currencies has long lost its connection with the fluctuations of world gold and silver prices. However, since the government implemented foreign exchange controls last March, speculators have once again taken advantage of the fluctuations in the world's gold and silver ratios to manipulate them. As a result, the cash trading in Shanghai's unofficial market has suddenly become active. After May last year, the Shanghai cash price was affected by the London market, and a surge began, rising to 2,000 yuan in mid-June (the standard price for ten taels of gold). It once softened, but then rose again, reaching 2,068 yuan in November. At the beginning of March this year, due to a shortage of cash chips, coupled with purchases from North China, it once created a high price of 2,200 yuan, and is currently hovering around 2,100 yuan. Compared with before the war, it has risen by about 1,000 yuan. Although the cash market can affect the movement of a portion of gold, because China's foreign exchange base has become stable and the Sino-American Silver Agreement was also extended last year, it does not seem to have any impact on the overall financial situation.

(7) Changes in Clearinghouse Transactions Shanghai's clearinghouse transactions are divided into three groups: Chinese banks and money shops, and foreign banks. The Chinese Banks Clearing House was established in January 1933, and is sponsored by the Bankers' Joint Reserve Association. Its clearing procedures are similar to those of European and American countries. Money shops also have an organization called the Exchange Remittance Association, but their clearing methods are very outdated. As for the clearing of instruments between Bund banks (including foreign banks and the Bank of China, Bank of Communications, and Farmers Bank of China), there is only a reconciliation method and no clearinghouse organization or system.

Before the war, the bank clearinghouse held two clearing sessions every day at 1:00 PM and 3:30 PM. After the war, due to a decrease in instruments, it was changed to once a day at 3:00 PM. From January to July 1937, the average daily clearing amount of the clearinghouse was more than 29 million yuan. From August to December, the daily average was only 7 million yuan, a ratio of about four to one. From January to July 1938, the daily average was only more than 6 million yuan. From August to December, there was a slight improvement, with a daily average of about 9 million yuan. Among the clearing amounts in the months after the war, December 1937 and December 1938 were the lowest, with a daily average of only 3.6 million yuan. However, the clearing amount has gradually increased recently. In January of this year, the daily average clearing amount has reached more than 24 million yuan, almost the same as the daily average before the war, which shows that Shanghai's commercial transactions have gradually become active under special circumstances.

The money shops' clearinghouse transactions are commonly known as "Zha Gong Che." The daily clearing amount includes the amount of interbank lending and transfer accounts, which is different in nature from the bank clearing figures and is difficult to compare. However, general observations show that the trend of the rise and fall of the money shops' clearing amount after the war is the same as that of the bank clearing figures. As for the clearing methods of the money industry, there have been slight improvements after the war. In the past, each money shop sent old cashiers to collect bills separately, but now each money shop sends two clearing staff to the Exchange Remittance Association from 1:00 PM to 2:00 PM every day to handle the clearing centrally. In addition, the money shops' interbank clearing bills were originally stipulated that those over 500 yuan should be marked with large public bills, and those under 500 yuan and odd amounts should be marked with small public bills, which were cleared daily at the money industry reserve. In November last year, the Money Industry Association decided to cancel the names of large and small, and all amounts, regardless of the amount, should be marked with one public bill, which should be cleared daily, simplifying the procedures.

As for the clearing procedures of the Bund banks, after verifying the instruments with each other, the Bank of China used large transfer slips for the overall remittance, and the difference was settled daily by means of depositing or withdrawing cash. There was no clearinghouse organization. Since the outbreak of the war, the large transfer slip method adopted in the past has been abandoned, and each bank now sends personnel to the HSBC bank at 11:30 AM every day to conduct centralized verification. In addition, foreign banks have never had deposit and loan relationships with Chinese banks. After the war, for the convenience of clearing, foreign banks exceptionally opened current accounts with our Central Bank, and the Central Bank also opened accounts with foreign banks. As a result, the daily receivables and payables of Chinese and foreign banks are entrusted to each other for collection, and transferred within the current accounts, which is much more convenient than before.

The Situation of the Financial Industry Since the outbreak of the war, the environment in which the Shanghai financial industry is located has been very unusual. Leaving aside foreign banks for the time being, as far as Chinese banks and money shops are concerned, since the July 7th Incident two years ago, there has been a wave of withdrawals. In the month before the outbreak of the Shanghai war, the deposits of various banks and money shops clearly decreased. After the outbreak of the Shanghai war, the banking and money industry closed for four days. After the government promulgated measures to stabilize the financial situation, people's hearts began to stabilize slightly, and deposits did not decrease significantly. As the war zone expanded, wealthy people from various places came to Shanghai to seek refuge with their assets. As a result, the deposits of various banks and money shops gradually increased. However, most of these increased deposits were current deposits. On the banking and money industry side, it was necessary to accumulate sufficient reserves to respond to the situation.

After the war, the business policies of various banks and money shops strived for stability, and they were very cautious about various loans, mostly reducing the scope of business, only accepting deposits and not making loans. Fortunately, a portion of the pawnshop customers of the banking and money industry who suffered losses in the war, recently benefited from the high prices in Shanghai. In addition to making up for the losses of the war, they also had surpluses. Therefore, the loans made by the banking and money industry before the war, except for customers who suspended business during the war, could often be recovered on time. In addition, the business of various banks, except for a portion of loans, foreign exchange and other businesses, inevitably stagnated after the war, while other businesses such as warehousing, real estate trusts, etc., have increased compared with before the war. This kind of abnormal development is the only phenomenon in the current special environment in Shanghai, and it is self-evident that it cannot last long.

The loss of assets of the banking and money industry during the war was inevitable. Fortunately, most banks and money shops usually operate stably and have sufficient reserves. The previous surpluses are more than enough to offset the current losses. Only a very small number of smaller banks suffered from the impact of the war, and there were rumors of financial difficulties. As for those who went bankrupt as a result, there was only one bank. At the same time, among the four banks that suspended business in the month before the outbreak of the war, two had resumed business one after another last year.

The four banks of the Central Bank, Bank of China, Bank of Communications, and Farmers Bank of China are the largest government banks in the country and have outstanding influence in the financial industry. Their head offices were originally located in Shanghai. After the outbreak of the Shanghai war, these four government banks moved their head offices to the inland and established branches in various provinces in the southwest and Hong Kong. Shanghai's financial power has become dispersed. Currently, Shanghai's banks feel that the channels and targets for their usual use of funds have been blocked and reduced after the war. In addition, the government is actively promoting and encouraging investment in the development of agriculture, mining, industry, and commerce in the inland. Therefore, general commercial banks in Shanghai have a tendency to develop towards the inland. Recently, many banks have established branches in the provinces of Sichuan, Yunnan, Guizhou, and Guangxi. The center of gravity of finance has shifted, and the future development is worthy of attention.

For example, draw a birdcage on the front of the paper and a bird on the back, and then tie a string to each side of the round paper. Now use your fingers to twist the string to make the round paper rotate rapidly, and the bird will be seen in the cage (see figure). This experiment is very interesting. Isn't it a strange thing? However, if you study its reason, it is not difficult to discover its scientific mystery, because when we usually look at things with our eyes, the image of the object is reflected on the retina of our eyeballs. At this time, if you suddenly move the object away, the image does not disappear immediately, but can be temporarily retained on the retina. This phenomenon is called "afterimage" in science. When the paper is rotating, the cage image that remains on our retina has not had time to disappear, and the bird image is immediately reflected on the retina, so the result is that the two are superimposed, and we see it as if the bird is in the cage.

Readers can now know the general idea of the above experiment. This is originally a very ordinary thing. An ordinary card is drawn according to the method and then turned to persuade it to work. A Brief History of the International Settlement Volunteer Corps: In the 95 years since Shanghai was opened as a port, the International Settlement Volunteer Corps has a long history of 85 years. The establishment of the International Settlement Volunteer Corps in Shanghai dates back to 1853. The motivation for its organization was the influence of the Taiping Heavenly Kingdom revolutionary movement. At that time, the concessions did not have the right to station troops for self-defense. Foreign merchants, aware of the booming power of the Hong Yang army and the precarious state of security within the concessions, gathered together with the consuls of various countries in Shanghai to jointly devise defensive measures. They decided to organize volunteer corps from various countries, recruit merchants living in Shanghai to enlist, and appoint Captain Tulangsun, an officer of the British Indian Army, as commander. At that time, American expatriates also formed their own team, commanded by Captain Kyle. This was the beginning of the International Settlement Volunteer Corps. After the Battle of Muddy Creek, it was disbanded for a time. In 1860, the Taiping Army launched a second uprising, and its power was restored. It was re-recruited, and two teams were formed, each with only 80 men. Two years later, under the guidance of Captain Bowlans, it was gradually expanded and began to take shape. In 1870, there were three infantry teams and one light cavalry team, with 200 members. Two mountain guns were transported from Hong Kong, and a field artillery team was established. In 1878, the entire corps was reorganized, the light cavalry team was withdrawn, and two infantry teams were reorganized. At that time, the commander was Mr. Doulitai, and his brother took over his position until Mr. Dou returned to his country in 1882. In 1883, when the entire corps held its first review, there were already 176 officers. Three years later, the artillery team was donated by the British government and obtained four artillery pieces. By 1895, the Portuguese team had joined, and the light cavalry team was also restored. In 1900, after the Boxer Rebellion, the entire corps underwent major changes. At that time, the commander was Major Gracock, and there were 350 members. Later, the Customs team, the American team, the Japanese team, and the armored car team were established and joined one after another, reaching 1,000 people, and the strength was greatly increased. At the same time, the Public Works Department also launched the organization of a machine gun team and purchased many light machine guns, which were distributed to various teams for application. Since then, it has gradually evolved and become more perfect! The powers of the Commander-in-Chief (Corps Commander) have also become more onerous. The first corps commander was Major Watson, who was succeeded by Captain McGinquan, in 1905, and in 1908 by Major Pence. In 1913, it was Major Whiteley. When the European War broke out, all the soldiers were called back to their countries to participate in the war, and there was no way to send them for a while, so the British military appointed the leader of the corps, Major Truman, as commander to be responsible for the corps affairs.
