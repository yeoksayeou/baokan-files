### Japan's Economic Warfare Against China

Since Japan's invasion of China over two years ago, it has been exhausting its troops abroad and mobilizing its people at home, wavering at the crossroads of peace and war. Now, the news of the landing at Beihai has arrived. Beihai is a coastal port in the south of Hepu County. It was opened as a commercial port in the second year of the Guangxu reign of the Qing Dynasty. Initially, it served as the entry and exit point for products from Guangxi, Yunnan, and Guizhou provinces. However, with the subsequent opening of Wuzhou, Nanning, Mengzi and others, it lost its commercial importance. Today, the Japanese army suddenly landed at Beihai, with the sole intention of cutting off the highway between Guangxi and Vietnam and attempting to attack Nanning (now called Yongning)! According to what the people of the Japanese Naval Ministry have declared; "The goal is to tighten the blockade of the South China coast. After the fall of Beihai, all Chinese seaports will be in Japanese hands," etc. It can be seen that their intention lies, in addition to military considerations, placing particular emphasis on economic ones. Nanning is the distribution center for goods in the Xijiang River basin, known as the "Guangxi Warehouse." It was opened as a commercial port in the thirty-second year of the Guangxu reign of the Qing Dynasty. Its commercial prosperity rivaled Wuzhou. Goods transported by sea mostly took the route through Beihai, and now there is a highway connecting Annam's Dong Dang and Lang Son. But even if the Japanese occupy the port of Beihai, it will be difficult to succeed in advancing north to spy on Nanning. Even if they want to cut off the Guangxi-Vietnam highway, the shortest distance is still 140 kilometers, which cannot be hoped to be achieved without preparing to sacrifice a large part of human and material resources. However, what is noteworthy is that the Japanese desire to end the war against China is like a child in urgent need of color, with a great sense of impatience. Now, in addition to military matters, politics and economy are being pursued simultaneously. Politics aside for now. Regarding the economic aspect, before occupying a place, they fear not destroying it to the fullest extent. Therefore, the bombing of airplanes often chooses undefended cities, destroying large commercial and industrial areas. After they have occupied it, they fear not encompassing everything thoroughly. Therefore, they take over all agriculture, industry, commerce, mining, shipping under the name of "control" and practice "monopoly." Knowing that a snake swallowing an elephant is difficult to digest, they seem not to care until they want to vomit and cannot.

Their offensive in finance involves issuing "United Reserve Bank" banknotes in North China, thinking to seize the position of legal tender. Having difficulty succeeding, they turned to using banknotes from North China to exchange for foreign exchange in Shanghai, also without success. Then they thought to meddle in Central China, but the so-called "China Development" banknotes have difficulty gaining popularity in the occupied areas. They then shifted their fierce flames to customs duties, thinking that they could promote it without obstruction. They also wanted to blockade the British concession in Tianjin and seize China's silver reserves. Yunnan Opera is a unique local opera in Yunnan and is similar to Peking Opera in appearance.

Various local operas can be seen in Shanghai; only Yunnan Opera has not yet had a "chance to meet" the people of Shanghai.

Old Actors Rely on Skill to Make a Living, New Performers Rely on Building the Crowd to Make Money

The first day's opening play, "Wang Baochuan," sold out completely. The grand scenes of the second and third days were not much diminished. The people who came to listen to the opera, because they saw in the golden advertisement the news that Wang Yaoqing, the old court musician, was "personally supervising the scene," so all those who sat in the lower arena (connoisseurs who watched the opera liked to find seats in front of the lower arena door) invariably craned their necks before Yurong was about to appear on stage, looking straight into the upper arena door. Sure enough, they saw an old man wearing dark glasses, extending two fingers towards the musicians and drummers on stage, using hand signals to make a greeting. Thus, Yurong stepped onto the stage amid thunderous applause. According to the rules of opera performance in the past, there was no such thing as a private huqin. Famous actors among the court musicians, such as Gui Fen, Tan Xinpei, and Sun Juxian, etc., such famous figures, never used exclusive musicians and drummers. Among those famous actors, there were only Mei Yutian and Sun Zuochen who played the huqin. As for when each person was about to go on stage, they always extended two or three fingers towards the stage, which was an indication to the person playing the instrument to use the flute to tune the strings to the correct palace tune or B character tune. Only Gui Fen's voice was the best, and the tune was particularly high. Every time he appeared on stage, he simply flicked his sleeves, implying that no matter what three limits you set, I don't care if you want me to sing the upper word tune. However, at that time, it was very common to be able to sing the B character tune. Like Old Tan, who could only eat the tune to the correct palace, he was still ridiculed at the time as being softly decadent. As for those who sang the six-character tune, they simply had no qualifications to perform on stage. (143)
