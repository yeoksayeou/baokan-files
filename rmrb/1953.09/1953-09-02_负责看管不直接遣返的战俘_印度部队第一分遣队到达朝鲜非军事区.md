Title: First Detachment of Indian Troops Arrives at Korean Demilitarized Zone to Oversee POWs Not Directly Repatriated
Author:
Date: 1953-09-02
Page: 4

    Overseeing POWs Not Directly Repatriated
    First Detachment of Indian Troops Arrives at Korean Demilitarized Zone
    [Xinhua News Agency, Kaesong, 1st] Lieutenant Colonel Er. Striniwasin, Chief Liaison Officer of the Neutral Nations Repatriation Commission (NNRC), announced at a press conference in Panmunjom on the 1st that the first detachment of Indian troops to oversee POWs not directly repatriated had arrived in the Demilitarized Zone this afternoon. Lieutenant Colonel Striniwasin also said that the Indian representative to the Neutral Nations Repatriation Commission, the Chairman of the Commission, Lieutenant General K.S. Thimayya, and his chief alternate representative, Mr. B.N. Chakravarti, will leave New Delhi for Korea on September 5th.
    The first detachment of Indian troops consists of 1,700 soldiers and 77 officers. Also arriving were personnel from the Indian Red Cross and a medical team. According to Lieutenant Colonel Striniwasin, another ship carrying Indian troops is scheduled to arrive in Korea on September 4th, another ship will arrive around September 12th, and yet another ship will set sail from India on the 1st or 2nd. According to the mandate of the Neutral Nations Repatriation Commission, the Indian troops will oversee POWs from both sides who are not directly repatriated to ensure that all POWs have the opportunity to exercise their right to be repatriated.

### 负责看管不直接遣返的战俘  印度部队第一分遣队到达朝鲜非军事区

1953-09-02
第4版()
专栏：

    负责看管不直接遣返的战俘
    印度部队第一分遣队到达朝鲜非军事区
    【新华社开城一日电】中立国遣返委员会印度首席对外联络官尔·斯特里尼瓦生中校，一日在板门店举行的记者招待会上宣布，看管不直接遣返的战俘的印度部队第一分遣队已在今天下午到达非军事区。斯特里尼瓦生中校并说，中立国遣返委员会的印度代表、委员会的主席克·斯·蒂迈雅中将和他的首席候补代表布·纳·查克拉瓦蒂先生，将在九月五日离新德里赴朝鲜。
    印度部队第一分遣队有一千七百名士兵和七十七名军官。同时到达的还有印度红十字会人员和医疗队。据斯特里尼瓦生中校说，另一艘载运印度部队的船预定在九月四日驶抵朝鲜，还有一艘船将在九月十二日左右驶抵朝鲜，再一艘船将在一日或二日自印度启碇。按照中立国遣返委员会的职权范围，印度部队将看管不直接遣返的双方战俘，以保证所有战俘有机会行使其被遣返的权利。
