Title: Demanding Wage Increases and Opposing Layoffs: Japanese Coal Miners Regularly Hold Strikes
Author:
Date: 1953-09-27
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, Tokyo, 26th: The 240,000 workers belonging to the unions of the Japan Coal Miners' Union began an indefinite production reduction movement simultaneously in mines across the country on the 24th, to oppose the monopoly capitalists' measures to lay off workers on a large scale. According to Kyodo News, production in major coal mines in Kyushu and Hokkaido has been reduced by 20 to 50 percent.

Prior to this, the Japan Coal Miners' Union convened a Central Struggle Committee on the 22nd, deciding to demand wage increases and improvements to the working hours system. In order to implement these demands, which are linked to the opposition to layoffs, the Japan Coal Miners' Union, in addition to implementing the production reduction struggle, decided to coordinate with the General Council of Trade Unions of Japan's autumn struggle plan to hold a 24-hour strike on September 28th.

### 要求增加工资、反对解雇  日本煤矿工人定期举行罢工

1953-09-27
第4版()
专栏：

    要求增加工资、反对解雇
    日本煤矿工人定期举行罢工
    【新华社二十六日讯】东京消息：日本煤矿工会所属各会员工会的二十四万工人从二十四日开始，在全国各矿山同时举行无一定期限的减少生产运动，以反对垄断资本家大规模解雇工人的措施。据共同社报道：九州、北海道各大煤矿的生产已减少百分之二十到五十。
    在这以前，日本煤矿工会在二十二日召开了中央斗争委员会，决定要求增加工资和改善工作时间制度。为了贯彻这些和反对解雇结合在一起的要求，日本煤矿工会除了实行减产斗争以外，决定配合日本工会总评议会的秋季斗争计划，在九月二十八日举行二十四小时的罢工。
