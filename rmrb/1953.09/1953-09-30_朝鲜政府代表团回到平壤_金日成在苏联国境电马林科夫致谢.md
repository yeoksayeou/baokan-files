Title: Korean Government Delegation Returns to Pyongyang; Kim Il-sung Thanks Malenkov in Telegram from Soviet Border
Author:
Date: 1953-09-30
Page: 1

Xinhua News Agency, Pyongyang, September 29th: The government delegation of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea, headed by Premier Kim Il-sung, returned to Pyongyang at 1:30 PM on the 29th.

Those who went to the station to welcome the delegation included: Kim Tu-bong, Chairman of the Standing Committee of the Supreme People's Assembly of Korea; Park Chang-ok, Vice Chairman of the Central Committee of the Korean Workers' Party; Park Yong-bin, Director of the Organization and Guidance Department; Kim Chang-man, Director of the Propaganda and Agitation Department; Vice Premiers of the Cabinet Choi Chang-ik and Choi Yong-kun; Hong Myong-hui; Cabinet Ministers; representatives of various democratic parties and people's organizations; and senior generals of the Korean People's Army.

Also present at the station to welcome the delegation were: the ambassadors and embassy staff of the Soviet Union, the People's Republic of China, and various people's democratic countries to Korea.

The flags of Korea, the Soviet Union, and China were hung at the station.

When the delegation's special train arrived at the station, the band played solemn welcoming music. Premier Kim Il-sung and members of the delegation reviewed the honor guard.

Kim Il, Vice Chairman of the Central Committee of the Korean Workers' Party, and Park Ui-wan, Vice Premier of the Cabinet, met the delegation at Sinuiju on the Korean border and accompanied them back to Pyongyang.

Xinhua News Agency, September 29th: TASS, Moscow, September 28th: Kim Il-sung, Premier of the Cabinet of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea, sent a telegram to G.M. Malenkov, Chairman of the Council of Ministers of the Soviet Union. The text of the telegram is as follows:

To Comrade G.M. Malenkov, Chairman of the Council of Ministers of the Soviet Union, Moscow Kremlin:

Respected Chairman Comrade!

As I leave the territory of the great Soviet Union, I would like to express my deep gratitude to the Soviet government and to you personally for the care and attention given to our delegation.

There is no doubt that the negotiations between our two governments in Moscow will be a new contribution to further strengthening the friendly relations between our two peoples, to the early restoration and development of our national economy, and to the peaceful settlement of the Korean question.

I wish you good health and greater achievements.

Kim Il-sung
Premier of the Cabinet of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea
September 28, 1953

### 朝鲜政府代表团回到平壤  金日成在苏联国境电马林科夫致谢

1953-09-30
第1版()
专栏：

    朝鲜政府代表团回到平壤
    金日成在苏联国境电马林科夫致谢
    【新华社平壤二十九日电】以金日成首相为首的朝鲜民主主义人民共和国政府代表团在二十九日下午一时半返国到达平壤。
    前往车站欢迎的有：朝鲜最高人民会议常任委员会委员长金枓奉，朝鲜劳动党中央委员会副委员长朴昌玉、组织指导部部长朴永彬、宣传鼓动部部长金昌满，内阁副首相崔昌益、崔庸健、洪命熹，内阁各相，各民主党派、人民团体代表，朝鲜人民军高级将领等。
    前往车站欢迎的，还有：苏联、中华人民共和国及各人民民主国家驻朝鲜的使节和使馆人员等。
    车站上悬挂着朝、苏、中三国国旗。
    代表团专车抵达车站后，乐队奏起庄严的欢迎曲。金日成首相和代表团团员们检阅了仪仗队。
    朝鲜劳动党中央委员会副委员长金一，内阁副首相朴义琓等曾往朝鲜国境新义州迎接代表团，并陪同代表团回到平壤。
    【新华社二十九日讯】塔斯社莫斯科二十八日讯：朝鲜民主主义人民共和国内阁首相金日成致电苏联部长会议主席格·马·马林科夫，电文如下：莫斯科克里姆林苏联部长会议主席格·马·马林科夫同志敬爱的主席同志！
    在离开伟大的苏联的国土的时候，我对于我国代表团所受到的关怀与照顾，谨向苏联政府和您个人表示深切的感激。
    毫无疑问，我们两国政府在莫斯科举行的谈判，对于进一步加强我们两国人民间的友好关系，对于尽早地恢复和发展我国的国民经济，以及对于朝鲜问题的和平解决，将是一个新的贡献。
    祝您身体健康，并获得更多的成就。
    朝鲜民主主义人民共和国内阁首相　金日成
              一九五三年九月二十八日
