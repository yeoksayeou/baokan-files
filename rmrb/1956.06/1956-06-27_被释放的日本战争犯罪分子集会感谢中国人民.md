Title: Released Japanese War Criminals Gather to Thank the Chinese People
Author:
Date: 1956-06-27
Page: 1

    Xinhua News Agency, Tianjin, June 26th: Released Japanese war criminals, on the eve of their departure from China to return to Japan, gathered tonight to express their gratitude to the Chinese people for their lenient treatment and to the Chinese Red Cross for assisting them in their return home.

    At the meeting, Kunitomo Shuntaro, a representative of the released Japanese war criminals, spoke. He said that the path of aggressive war we took in the past was a dark path full of sin. Now that we have human conscience and feelings, we will absolutely not repeat the mistakes of the past. We will, as upright people, oppose aggressive war, defend peace, and do our utmost for the friendship and unity between the Chinese and Japanese people.

    Wu Shifa, Secretary-General of the Tianjin Branch of the Chinese Red Cross, also spoke, expressing his hope that their wishes would be realized.

    Subsequently, representatives of the released Japanese war criminals presented two silk banners to the Supreme People's Procuratorate of the People's Republic of China and the Tianjin Branch of the Chinese Red Cross, embroidered with "We will never forget the Chinese people's policy of leniency and will dedicate all our remaining years to fighting against aggressive war and safeguarding lasting peace" and "Thank you for the Chinese people's spirit of internationalism and humanitarianism, and we will strive for the friendship and unity of the Chinese and Japanese people."

    At the meeting, all the released Japanese war criminals sang in unison "The Song of Defending Peace," "People of the World are of One Heart," and "Tokyo-Beijing."

    Xinhua News Agency, June 26th: Nagano Shigeuemon, Abe Yukizo, Okada Yoshiji, Matsui Matsuji, and Nakahara Junji, representatives of the Japanese Red Cross Society, the Japan-China Friendship Association, and the Japan Peace Liaison Council, accompanied by Ni Feijun and other representatives of the Chinese Red Cross, arrived in Beijing from Tianjin at 11:30 tonight. They will visit Beijing. Twelve Japanese journalists also accompanied them.

### 被释放的日本战争犯罪分子集会感谢中国人民

1956-06-27
第1版()
专栏：

    被释放的日本战争犯罪分子集会感谢中国人民
    新华社天津26日电　被释放的日本战争犯罪分子在离开中国回日本的前夕——今晚集会，向给予他们宽大处理的中国人民和协助他们回国的中国红十字会表示感谢。
    会上，被释放的日本战争犯罪分子的代表国友俊太郎讲话。他说，过去我们所走的侵略战争的道路是充满罪恶的黑暗道路，现在我们有了人类的良心和感情，绝对不再重犯过去的错误，我们将作为正直的人反对侵略战争、保卫和平，为中日两国人民的友谊和团结尽我们最大的努力。
    中国红十字会天津市分会总干事吴士法也讲了话，他预祝他们愿望能够实现。
    随后，被释放的日本战争犯罪分子的代表把绣着“永远不忘中国人民的宽大政策，将贡献余生的一切为反对侵略战争、维护持久和平而奋斗”和“感谢中国人民的国际主义精神和人道主义的道义，为中日两国人民的友好与团结而努力”的两面锦旗分别献给中华人民共和国最高人民检察院和中国红十字会天津市分会。
    在会上，全体被释放的日本战争犯罪分子齐声高唱“保卫和平之歌”、“全世界人民心一条”和“东京——北京”。
    新华社26日讯　日本红十字会、日中友好协会、日本和平联络会三团体代表长野重右卫门、阿部行藏、冈田好治、松井松次、中原淳吉，今夜十一时三十分在中国红十字会代表倪斐君等陪同下，从天津到达北京。他们将在北京进行参观访问。随同前来的还有日本记者十二人。
