Title: Sukarno Says in Canadian Parliament: No One Can Resist the Torrent of Asian-African Nationalism
Author:
Date: 1956-06-07
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, June 6th, Ottawa Dispatch: Indonesian President Sukarno said yesterday at a joint session of the Canadian Parliament's Senate and House of Commons that no one should underestimate the power of "the torrent of nationalism surging in Asia and Africa today."

    Sukarno said: "This is a powerful torrent," and "Any attempt to resist this torrent will be futile, just as every attempt to resist the course of history in the past has been futile."

    Sukarno continued: "Colonialism, no matter what form it takes, no matter what mask it wears, is a hateful and detestable thing." He said: "Colonialism has left Indonesia with a legacy of illiteracy, human disease, human ignorance, and a human degradation that is a shame and a threat to the twentieth century."

    Sukarno said that part of his country is still not free. He said that until West Irian is incorporated into Indonesia, his country will feel "incomplete and insecure."

    Sukarno said that Indonesia's aim is to maintain and safeguard its national status. His people cherish their national status "as much as they cherish life." He said: "Nothing can take it from us. No hope or promise of quick compensation will induce us to sell a single iota of our independence."

    Speaking of Indonesia's foreign policy, Sukarno said: "We do not intend to join any military organization. We intend to maintain our own character." He said that Indonesia adopts an independent approach "consistent with what we believe to be in the best interests of the world and our own country," and "we act according to this approach."

    Sukarno concluded his speech by expressing his hope to develop friendship between Indonesia and Canada.

### 苏加诺在加拿大议会说  谁也不能抗拒亚非民族主义的洪流

1956-06-07
第4版()
专栏：

    苏加诺在加拿大议会说  
    谁也不能抗拒亚非民族主义的洪流
    新华社6日讯　渥太华消息：印度尼西亚总统苏加诺昨天在加拿大议会参众两院联席会议上说：谁也不应该过低地估计
  “今天在亚非两洲奔腾的民族主义洪流”的力量。
    苏加诺说：“这是一股强大的洪流”，“抗拒这股洪流的任何企图，都将是枉费心机的，正如过去每一次对历史的进程进行抗拒的企图都是枉费心机一样。”
    苏加诺接着说：“殖民主义，不管它采取什么样的形式，不管它戴上什么样的面具，都是一种可恨的令人讨厌的东西。”他说：“殖民主义给印度尼西亚留下了这样的遗产：文盲、人类疾病、人类愚昧和一种对二十世纪说来是耻辱和威胁的人类堕落。”
    苏加诺说，他的国家的一部分现在仍然没有获得自由。他说，在西伊里安归并于印度尼西亚以前，他的国家将感觉“不完整和不安全。”
    苏加诺说，印度尼西亚的目的在于维持和维护它的民族地位。他的人民珍视他们的民族地位，“就像珍视生命一样”。他说：“任何事物都不能把它从我们手中夺走。提出迅速付给补偿的任何希望或者任何诺言，也都不能诱使我们出卖我们的独立的一分一毫”。
    苏加诺在谈到印度尼西亚的外交政策时，他说：“我们并不打算参加任何军事组织。我们打算保持我们的本色。”他说，印度尼西亚采取一种“符合我们认为是全世界和我们自己国家的最高利益的”独立方针，“我们根据这种方针行动”。
    苏加诺在演说中最后表示希望发展印度尼西亚和加拿大之间的友谊。
