Title: Prime Minister Sastroamidjojo Says the Bandung Spirit is Increasingly Becoming a Living Reality; Prospects for Holding a Second Afro-Asian Conference are Good
Author:
Date: 1956-06-15
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, Jakarta, June 14th: Indonesian Prime Minister Ali Sastroamidjojo said yesterday that the Bandung spirit is becoming more and more of a living reality.

In response to questions from Marvin Stone, Director of the Far East Division of the U.S. International News Service, he said that one of the basic principles of the Bandung spirit is to seek points of agreement and not to excessively reveal points of disagreement. He said that we hope that this new attitude in international affairs may prompt major powers to resolve their differences, thereby easing international tensions.

He also said: "The prospects for holding a second Afro-Asian conference are good, and we are studying the possibility of holding such a conference."

In response to questions about accepting aid, Sastroamidjojo said that Indonesia is willing to accept any assistance from any source, as long as it does not mean assuming any political or military obligations.

Sastroamidjojo said that as a result of the Asian-African Conference, Indonesia's relations with the People's Republic of China have generally improved in the past year.

### 沙斯特罗阿米佐约总理说  万隆精神日益成为活生生的现实  举行第二次亚非会议的前景良好

1956-06-15
第4版()
专栏：

    沙斯特罗阿米佐约总理说
    万隆精神日益成为活生生的现实
    举行第二次亚非会议的前景良好
    据新华社雅加达14日电　印度尼西亚总理阿里·沙斯特罗阿米佐约昨天说，万隆精神正在愈来愈加成为活生生的现实。
    他在答复美国国际新闻社远东分社社长马文·斯通提出的问题的时候说，万隆精神的基本原则之一就是要寻求意见一致之点，而不要过分揭示意见不同之点。他说，我们希望国际事务中的这种新态度可能会促使大国解决它们之间的分歧，从而缓和国际紧张局势。
    他还说：“举行第二次亚非会议的前景是良好的，我们正在研究举行这样一次会议的可能性。”
    沙斯特罗阿米佐约在答复关于接受援助问题的时候说，印度尼西亚愿意接受来自任何方面的任何援助，只要这不意味着承担政治上或军事上的任何义务。
    沙斯特罗阿米佐约说，由于亚非会议的结果，印度尼西亚同中华人民共和国的关系在过去一年中一般地有了改善。
