Title: President Tito Arrives in Bucharest; Romanian and Yugoslav National Leaders Begin Talks
Author:
Date: 1956-06-26
Page: 4
Xinhua News Agency, according to a report by the Romanian News Agency: On the afternoon of June 24th, the day Yugoslav President Tito arrived in Bucharest, talks began between Romania and Yugoslavia.

The talks exchanged opinions on international issues of common concern and mutual relations. The talks were conducted in a harmonious, sincere, and mutually understanding atmosphere.

That same evening, the Presidium of the Romanian National Assembly and the Council of Ministers held a grand reception for Tito and others.

Xinhua News Agency, according to a TASS report: Yugoslav President Tito and his entourage arrived in Bucharest by train on the morning of June 24th.

At 8:58 a.m., the special train entered the station. Tito and others stepped onto the platform amidst the enthusiastic applause of the welcomers. They warmly shook hands with the leaders of the Romanian Workers' Party and the government who had come to welcome them.

Subsequently, the Yugoslav guests participated in a mass rally held in the square near the station.

Stoica, representing the Presidium of the Romanian National Assembly and the Council of Ministers, delivered a welcoming speech at the rally, saying, "We believe that the negotiations between the Yugoslav leaders and the leaders of the Romanian government and the Romanian Workers' Party will reflect the principles that form the basis of relations between our two countries, their common aspirations and interests, mutual respect, and loyalty to the common cause of peace and socialism. Therefore, these negotiations will achieve success and will contribute to the further development of Romanian-Yugoslav relations."

Tito then delivered a reply. He said, "We have come to your country with sincere wishes, and we hope that for the benefit of our two peoples and the cause of peace, we can completely eliminate and forget the damage that our two countries' relations suffered not long ago. Our two countries' relations are now expanding and continuously developing."

Stoica's and Tito's speeches were constantly interrupted by stormy applause.

### 铁托总统到达布加勒斯特  罗南两国国家领导人开始会谈

1956-06-26
第4版()
专栏：

    铁托总统到达布加勒斯特 
    罗南两国国家领导人开始会谈
    新华社据罗马尼亚通讯社报道　在二十四日南斯拉夫总统铁托到达布加勒斯特的当天下午，罗马尼亚和南斯拉夫之间开始举行会谈。
    这次会谈就共同关心的国际问题和相互关系交换了意见。会谈是在融洽、诚挚和相互谅解的气氛中进行的。
    同一天晚上，罗马尼亚国民议会主席团和部长会议为铁托等举行了盛大的招待会。
    新华社据塔斯社报道　南斯拉夫总统铁托和他的随行人员，在六月二十四日上午乘火车到达布加勒斯特。
    上午八时五十八分专车进入车站。铁托等在欢迎者的热烈掌声中走下月台。他们同前来欢迎的罗马尼亚工人党和政府的领导人员亲切握手。
    随后，南斯拉夫客人们参加了在车站附近广场上举行的群众集会。
    斯托伊卡代表罗马尼亚国民议会主席团和部长会议在会上致欢迎词说，我们相信，南斯拉夫领导者同罗马尼亚政府和罗马尼亚工人党的领导者的谈判，将反映作为两国关系的基础的原则、它们的共同愿望和利益、相互尊重和对和平和社会主义的共同事业的忠诚，因而这些谈判将取得成就，并且将对罗南两国关系的进一步发展作出贡献。
    铁托接着致答词。他说，我们怀着真诚的愿望来到了你们的国家，我们希望：为了我们两国人民的利益和和平的利益，我们能够完全消除和忘记我们两国关系在不久以前所遭到的破坏。我们两国关系现在正在扩大和不断发展中。
    斯托伊卡和铁托的讲话不断为暴风雨般的掌声所打断。
