Title: China Red Cross and Japanese Three Organizations End Talks; Agreed on Matters Concerning the Transfer of Released Japanese War Criminals, etc.
Author: 
Date: 1956-06-29
Page: 1

Xinhua News Agency, Tianjin, June 28th. The talks between the Chinese Red Cross and the three Japanese organizations, namely the Japanese Red Cross, the Japan-China Friendship Association, and the Japan Peace Liaison Council, have concluded successfully. This morning, representatives from both sides issued a joint communiqué and held a signing ceremony in Tianjin.

The signatories to the communiqué were Zhao Anbo, Peng Yan, Ni Feijun, and Ji Feng on the Chinese side, representing the Chinese Red Cross; and Ioue Masutaro, Nagano Shigeemon, and Abe Yukizo on the Japanese side, representing the Japanese Red Cross, the Japan-China Friendship Association, and the Japan Peace Liaison Council, respectively.

The full text of the communiqué is as follows:

The representatives of the Chinese Red Cross and the representatives of the Japanese Red Cross, the Japan-China Friendship Association, and the Japan Peace Liaison Council held a meeting in Tianjin from June 24th to June 28th, 1956, and agreed on the following:

	I. The Chinese Red Cross has handed over 335 Japanese war criminals who were exempted from prosecution by the Supreme People's Procuratorate of the People's Republic of China, seven sets of remains, and their belongings to the representatives of the Japanese Red Cross, the Japan-China Friendship Association, and the Japan Peace Liaison Council.

	Regarding the transfer of Japanese war criminals who will be released in the future, the Japanese Red Cross, the Japan-China Friendship Association, and the Japan Peace Liaison Council will be notified each time. It is expected that the second batch of Japanese war criminals to be released will be handed over in late July, at which time the "Xing'an Maru" will be dispatched to Tianjin.

	II. The Chinese Red Cross will provide necessary convenience and assistance to the families of Japanese war criminals who come to China to visit their relatives.

	The first group of family members to visit China can come to China on the "Xing'an Maru" in late July, which will transport the second batch of war criminals who have been exempted from prosecution.

	III. The Chinese Red Cross, the Japan-China Friendship Association, and the Japan Peace Liaison Council have agreed to work towards opening up channels for free mutual visits between overseas Chinese in Japan and Japanese nationals in China.

	The Japanese Red Cross reserves its opinion on this issue.

	IV. The Chinese Red Cross, the Japanese Red Cross, the Japan-China Friendship Association, and the Japan Peace Liaison Council, based on humanitarian grounds, believe that Japanese women who have married Chinese citizens in China, if they wish, can go to Japan to visit relatives through formal procedures and then return to China. If their families in Japan wish, they can also go to China through formal procedures to visit Japanese women who have married Chinese citizens and then return to Japan.

	V. The repatriation of overseas Chinese in Japan will be handled in the same manner as before.

	VI. The repatriation of the remains of Chinese martyrs in Japan will continue in an appropriate manner.

	VII. Regarding the remains of Japanese people in China, as long as the Japanese side can provide materials and the Chinese Red Cross can find them, the Chinese Red Cross can assist in returning them to Japan.

	VIII. The Chinese Red Cross will continue to help Japanese nationals applying to return to their country.

	IX. The Chinese Red Cross has handed over the list and information of Japanese deceased persons, which have been investigated and verified, to the representatives of the Japanese Red Cross, the Japan-China Friendship Association, and the Japan Peace Liaison Council.

### 中国红十字会和日本三团体会谈结束  议定关于移交被释放的日本战争犯罪分子等事项

1956-06-29
第1版()
专栏：

    中国红十字会和日本三团体会谈结束  
    议定关于移交被释放的日本战争犯罪分子等事项
    新华社天津28日电　中国红十字会和日本红十字会、日中友好协会、日本和平联络会三团体的会谈已经圆满结束。今天上午，双方代表在天津发表了联合公报并举行了签字仪式。
    在公报上签字的，中国方面是中国红十字会代表赵安博、彭炎、倪斐君、纪锋；日本方面是日本红十字会代表井上益太郎、日中友好协会代表长野重右卫门、日本和平联络会代表阿部行藏。
    公报全文如下：
    中国红十字会代表和日本红十字会、日中友好协会、日本和平联络会的代表于1956年6月24日到6月28日在天津举行会议，议定如下：
    一、中国红十字会已将中华人民共和国最高人民检察院免予起诉的日本战争犯罪分子三百三十五名、遗骨七具和遗物交给日本红十字会、日中友好协会和日本和平联络会的代表。
    关于移交今后被释放的日本战争犯罪分子问题，每次都将随时通知日本红十字会、日中友好协会和日本和平联络会。其中第二批将被释放的日本战争犯罪分子预定在七月下旬移交，届时将派遣“兴安丸”来天津。
    二、中国红十字会对于前往中国探望亲人的日本战争犯罪分子家属，将给予以必要的便利和协助。
    首批来华家属可乘七月下旬接运将被释放的第二批免予起诉的战争犯罪分子的“兴安丸”前来中国。
    三、中国红十字会和日中友好协会、日本和平联络会约定为开辟日本和中国的侨民相互自由往来的道路而努力。
    日本红十字会对此问题保留意见。
    四、中国红十字会和日本红十字会、日中友好协会、日本和平联络会根据人道的立场认为：在中国和中国人结婚的日本妇女如果愿意，可以经过正式手续去日本访问亲属，然后再回到中国。她们在日本的家属如果愿意，也可以经过正式手续到中国访问和中国人结婚的日本妇女，然后再回日本。
    五、关于送回在日华侨的问题照以前同样办理。六、关于送回在日本的中国烈士遗骨问题，继续按适当方式进行。
    七、在中国的日本人的遗骨，只要日本方面能够提供材料而中国红十字会能够发现的，中国红十字会可以协助送回日本。
    八、中国红十字会继续帮助申请回国的日本侨民回国。
    九、中国红十字会已将经调查后查明的日本人死亡者名单和资料交给日本红十字会、日中友好协会和日本和平联络会代表。
