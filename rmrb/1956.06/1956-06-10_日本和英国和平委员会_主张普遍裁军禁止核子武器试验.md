Title: Japan and British Peace Committees Advocate for Universal Disarmament and a Ban on Nuclear Weapons Testing
Author:
Date: 1956-06-10
Page: 4

    Japan and British Peace Committees
    Advocate for Universal Disarmament and a Ban on Nuclear Weapons Testing

    Xinhua News Agency: The Japan Council for the Protection of Peace decided at the closing session of its extraordinary conference on the 7th that the focus of the committee's activities from now on would be to launch a movement demanding arms reduction.

    The conference discussed the current domestic and international situation and its characteristics, and adopted the current movement guidelines and organizational guidelines. The conference decided to strengthen the committee's ties with various relevant local organizations and decided to rename the Japan Council for the Protection of Peace as the Japan Peace Committee.

    Xinhua News Agency: Secretary-General of the Japan Council for the Protection of Peace, Giichiro Hirano, and Secretary-General of the Japan Council Against Atomic and Hydrogen Bombs, Iku Yasui, and others met with Foreign Minister Mamoru Shigemitsu yesterday, requesting the Japanese government to dispatch a special envoy to the United States to urge a ban on American hydrogen bomb testing in the Pacific.

    Representatives of these peace organizations also requested the government to facilitate the entry of foreign representatives to participate in the Second World Conference Against Atomic and Hydrogen Bombs, to be held in Japan this August.

    Foreign Minister Mamoru Shigemitsu did not respond to the issue of dispatching a special envoy to the United States; he expressed willingness to consider the issue of foreign representatives' entry.

    Xinhua News Agency: The British Peace Committee held a meeting in London. The meeting unanimously passed a resolution calling for shortening the term of military service in Britain, stopping nuclear weapons testing, and ending colonial wars.

    President of the British Peace Committee, D.N. Pritt, spoke at the meeting. He said that the peace movement has achieved great successes, but peace defenders should continue to fight for universal disarmament.

### 日本和英国和平委员会  主张普遍裁军禁止核子武器试验

1956-06-10
第4版()
专栏：

    日本和英国和平委员会
    主张普遍裁军禁止核子武器试验
    据新华社讯　日本拥护和平委员会七日在它的临时大会闭幕会上决定，今后委员会的活动中心是展开要求裁减军备的运动。
    大会讨论了当前的内外形势和特点，并且通过了当前的运动方针和组织方针。大会决定要加强委员会同各种有关的地方团体的联系，并且决定把日本拥护和平委员会改名为日本和平委员会。
    据新华社讯　日本拥护和平委员会总书记平野义太郎和日本禁止原子弹和氢弹协议会事务总长安井郁等人昨天会见外务相重光葵，要求日本政府派遣特使到美国去促使禁止美国在太平洋试验氢弹。
    这些和平团体的代表并且要求政府使外国代表顺利入境，参加今年八月在日本举行的第二届禁止原子弹和氢弹世界大会。
    外务相重光葵对于派遣特使到美国去的问题没有给予答复；对于外国代表入境问题表示愿意考虑。
    据新华社讯　英国和平委员会在伦敦举行了一次会议。会议一致通过决议，要求在英国缩短服役期限，要求停止核子武器试验和停止殖民战争。
    英国和平委员会会长丹·诺·普里特在会上讲话。他说，和平运动已经取得了巨大的成就，但是和平保卫者应当继续奋斗，争取普遍裁军。
