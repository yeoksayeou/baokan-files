Title: The Stalin International Peace Prize Committee Announces That Renowned Peace Fighters Guo Moruo, Nenni, and Others Have Won This Year's Stalin International Peace Prize

Author: 
Date: 1951-12-22
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, December 21st dispatch from Moscow: The Stalin International Peace Prize Committee for "Strengthening International Peace" held meetings in Moscow on the 18th and 20th. The meetings were presided over by the committee chairman, Academician Skobeltsyn of the Soviet Academy of Sciences. Attending the meetings were committee vice-chairman, French writer Aragon, committee members Professor Bernal of the University of London, British Poet Neruda, Professor Tymowski of the University of Lodz in Poland, Academician Sadoveanu of the Romanian Academy, and Soviet writers Fadeyev and Ehrenburg.

The meetings discussed suggestions for awarding the 1951 Stalin International Peace Prize and decided to award the prize to the following representatives of democratic forces from various countries who have made outstanding contributions to the cause of defending and strengthening peace: (1) Guo Moruo, President of the Chinese Academy of Sciences; (2) Pietro Nenni, Italian Parliamentarian; (3) Professor Ikuo Oyama, Japanese Parliamentarian; (4) Monica Felton, British Public Figure; (5) Anna Seghers, German Writer; (6) Jorge Amado, Brazilian Writer.

Note: The Stalin International Peace Prize for "Strengthening International Peace" was established in accordance with a decree issued by the Presidium of the Supreme Soviet of the Soviet Union on December 20, 1949, to celebrate Stalin's 70th birthday. The prize is awarded once a year, with a quota of five to ten recipients. Citizens of all countries around the world, regardless of their race, political views, or religious beliefs, who have made outstanding contributions to the cause of defending and strengthening peace can receive this prize. Each winner will receive a certificate, a gold medal engraved with Stalin's image, and a prize of 100,000 rubles.

The Stalin International Peace Prize is selected and awarded by the Stalin International Peace Prize Committee for "Strengthening International Peace." The committee is composed of representatives of democratic forces from various countries appointed by the Presidium of the Supreme Soviet of the Soviet Union. In addition to the above-mentioned individuals, the committee members also include: Vice Chairman, Guo Moruo, President of the Chinese Academy of Sciences, and members Martin Andersen Nexø, Danish writer and Bernhard Kellermann, German writer.

### “加强国际和平”斯大林国际奖金委员会宣布 著名的和平战士郭沫若南尼等荣获今年斯大林国际和平奖金

1951-12-22
第4版()
专栏：

      “加强国际和平”斯大林国际奖金委员会宣布
      著名的和平战士郭沫若南尼等荣获今年斯大林国际和平奖金
     【新华社二十一日讯】据塔斯社莫斯科讯：“加强国际和平”斯大林国际奖金委员会于十八日和二十日在莫斯科举行会议。会议由委员会主席、苏联科学院院士斯科贝尔琴主持。出席会议的有委员会副主席、法国作家阿拉贡，委员、英国伦敦大学教授贝尔纳、智利诗人聂鲁达、波兰罗兹大学教授第姆鲍夫斯基，罗马尼亚科学院院士沙杜维亚努、苏联作家法捷耶夫和爱伦堡。
    会议讨论了颁发一九五一年斯大林国际和平奖金的建议，并决定以奖金授予下列在保卫和加强和平事业方面有卓越功绩的各国民主力量的代表：（一）郭沫若，中国科学院院长；（二）彼特罗·南尼，意大利议员；（三）大山郁夫教授，日本议员；
    （四）蒙尼卡·费尔顿，英国公众领袖；（五）安娜·西格斯，德国女作家；（六）乔治·亚马多，巴西作家。
    按：“加强国际和平”斯大林国际奖金，是遵照苏联最高苏维埃主席团在一九四九年十二月二十日为庆祝斯大林七十寿辰而颁布的法令设立的。此项奖金每年颁发一次，名额为五名到十名。全世界各国的公民，凡在保卫和加强和平事业方面有卓越贡献者，不论其种族、政治见解和宗教信仰如何，都可以得到此项奖金。得奖人将各得到奖状一份、刻有斯大林像的金质奖章一枚和奖金十万卢布。
    斯大林国际和平奖金由“加强国际和平”斯大林国际奖金委员会评选颁发。该委员会由苏联最高苏维埃主席团延聘世界各国民主力量的代表人物组成。委员会人员除上述诸人外，还有：副主席、中国科学院院长郭沫若，委员、丹麦作家尼克索、德国作家凯勒曼。
