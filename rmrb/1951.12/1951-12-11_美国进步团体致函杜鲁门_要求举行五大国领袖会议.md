Title: US Progressive Groups Send Letter to Truman Demanding a Five-Power Leaders' Conference
Author:
Date: 1951-12-11
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, Prague, December 4th: According to a report from TASS, the National Council of Arts, Sciences and Professions in the United States published an open letter to Truman and Acheson in the New York "Daily Worker," demanding a five-power leaders' conference.

The letter stated: "The National Council of Arts, Sciences and Professions believes that now is the time to make the greatest effort to break the deadlock of the Cold War and begin to pave the way for peace. The lessons of history and the events of our time make it impossible for us to avoid the conclusion that another world war must be avoided. Due to the different social systems of the world's countries, a method must be found to allow the world's countries to coexist peacefully side by side. This method is only through great power negotiations."

The letter emphasized: "We believe that the continued exclusion of the People's Republic of China from various international conferences will inevitably delay the creation of lasting peace, especially the peace of the Far East; the representatives of more than one billion people in the Far East disagree with this decision. We demand an immediate reconsideration of this decision."

The letter concluded by requesting that the leaders of the five major powers, the Soviet Union, the United States, Britain, France, and China, immediately hold negotiations to resolve international disputes through peaceful agreements.

### 美国进步团体致函杜鲁门  要求举行五大国领袖会议

1951-12-11
第4版()
专栏：

    美国进步团体致函杜鲁门
    要求举行五大国领袖会议
    【新华社七日讯】据电通社布拉格四日讯：美国艺术、科学、自由职业全国委员会在纽约“工人日报”发表了一封致杜鲁门和艾奇逊的公开信，要求举行五大国领袖会议。
    这封信说：“艺术、科学、自由职业全国委员会认为目前是作最大努力来打破冷战的僵局并着手开辟和平道路的时机。历史的教训和我们时代所发生的事件，使我们不能不得出这样的结论：一定要避免发生另一次世界战争。由于世界各国社会制度的不同，必须找到一个方法，使世界各国肩并肩地和平共存。这个方法就只有举行大国谈判。”
    这封信着重指出：“我们认为迟迟不让中华人民共和国参加各种国际会议，将不可避免地耽搁持久和平的缔造，特别是远东和平的缔造；远东有十亿以上人民的代表不同意这种决定。我们要求立即重新考虑这种决定。”
    这封信最后要求苏、美、英、法、中五大国的领袖们立即举行谈判，以便用和平协议的方式来解决国际间的各种纠纷。
