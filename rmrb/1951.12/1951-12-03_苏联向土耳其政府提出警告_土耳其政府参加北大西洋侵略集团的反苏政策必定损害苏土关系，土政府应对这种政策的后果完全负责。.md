Title: Soviet Union Warns Turkish Government
Author:
Date: 1951-12-03
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, Moscow, 2nd: TASS reports: On November 30th, the Soviet government sent a note to the Turkish government regarding Turkey's preparations to join the anti-Soviet North Atlantic military aggressive bloc, refuting the Turkish government's sophistry and issuing a solemn warning. Prior to this, on November 3rd, the Soviet government issued a statement to the Turkish government, pointing out that the Soviet government could not ignore Turkey's preparations to join the North Atlantic aggressive bloc and its permission to establish foreign military bases for aggression on Turkish territory bordering the Soviet Union. On November 12th, the Turkish government submitted a reply to the Soviet government's statement. The Soviet government's note of November 30th pointed out that in its reply, the Turkish government attempted to deny the aggressive nature of the North Atlantic Group, evaded the issue of the construction of military bases by the United States in Turkey, and attempted to portray Turkey's participation in the North Atlantic aggressive bloc and the adoption of military measures on Turkish territory as guaranteeing Turkey's security. The Turkish government intends to justify its participation in the North Atlantic Group while simultaneously attempting to slander the Soviet Union's peaceful foreign policy. This intention is to conceal the true nature of the Turkish government's policy from public opinion, which is to drive Turkey to realize the North Atlantic Group's aggressive plans against the Soviet Union and other peace-loving countries. The Soviet government's note stated that the Turkish government's desire to join the North Atlantic Group and the intensified construction of American military bases on Turkish territory—including areas bordering the Soviet Union—completely refutes the Turkish government's claims about the peaceful purposes of its policies. It can be seen from the Turkish government's reply that the Turkish government has not taken any measures to prevent Turkish territory from being used to establish military bases for aggression on the Soviet border. The Soviet government resolutely opposes any attempts by the Turkish government to justify its actions with slanderous statements about Soviet threats. The Soviet government's note concluded by saying that the Soviet government considers it necessary to draw the Turkish government's attention to the following: its policy of driving Turkey to join the North Atlantic Group's aggressive plans against the Soviet Union will inevitably cause serious damage to Turkish-Soviet relations, and the Turkish government will be fully responsible for the consequences of this policy.

### 苏联向土耳其政府提出警告  土耳其政府参加北大西洋侵略集团的反苏政策必定损害苏土关系，土政府应对这种政策的后果完全负责。

1951-12-03
第4版()
专栏：

    苏联向土耳其政府提出警告
    土耳其政府参加北大西洋侵略集团的反苏政策必定损害苏土关系，土政府应对这种政策的后果完全负责。
    【新华社二日讯】据塔斯社莫斯科讯：苏联政府在十一月三十日就土耳其准备参加反苏的北大西洋军事侵略集团事照会土耳其政府，驳斥土耳其政府的狡辩，并提出郑重警告。在这以前，苏联政府曾于十一月三日向土耳其政府提出声明，指出苏联政府对于土耳其政府准备加入北大西洋侵略集团和允许利用与苏联接壤的土耳其领土建立供侵略之用的外国军事基地一事不能置之不理。十一月十二日，土耳其政府对苏联政府的声明提出覆文。苏联政府十一月三十日的照会指出，土耳其政府在它的覆文中打算否认北大西洋集团的侵略本质，对于美国在土耳其建筑军事基地问题避而不答，而企图把土耳其参加北大西洋侵略集团和在土耳其领土上采取军事措施说成是为了保证土耳其的安全。土耳其政府打算为参加北大西洋集团一事辩解，同时又打算诽谤苏联的和平外交政策。这种打算是为了在舆论面前掩饰土耳其政府政策的真正本质，即驱使土耳其来实现北大西洋集团针对苏联和其他爱好和平国家的侵略计划。苏联政府的照会说：土耳其政府想要加入北大西洋集团的愿望以及在土耳其领土上——包括与苏联接壤的区域——加紧建筑美国军事基地的情形，都完全推翻了土耳其政府的关于它的政策的和平宗旨的说法。从土耳其政府的答复中可以看出，土耳其政府并没有采取任何措施，使土耳其领土不致被利用来在苏联边境建立军事基地以供侵略之用。苏联政府坚决反对土耳其政府想用苏联威胁之类的诽谤谰言来辩解它的行动的一切打算。苏联政府的照会最后说：苏联政府认为有必要提起土耳其政府注意以下的情形：它的驱使土耳其加入北大西洋集团侵略计划以反对苏联的政策，必定会使土耳其与苏联的关系受到严重损害，这种政策的后果完全要由土耳其政府负责。
