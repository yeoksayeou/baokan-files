Title: U.S. Imperialism's Escalation of the Vietnam War Sparks Outrage; People Around the World Protest Against Murderer Johnson
Author:
Date: 1966-07-16
Edition: 6

People around the world have continued to hold demonstrations and rallies in recent days, angrily protesting against U.S. imperialism's bombing of Hanoi and Haiphong, and expressing support for the Vietnamese people's just struggle against American aggression.

On the evening of the 9th, over 300 representatives from various ethnic groups and sectors in the Khammouane Province of Laos held a rally. A resolution passed at the meeting stated, "The U.S. imperialists' new 'escalation' has further exposed the hypocritical and war-mongering faces of the Johnson, McNamara, and Rusk clique in front of the world's people."

The resolution expressed that the patriotic forces and people of Laos are determined to unite with their Vietnamese brothers and sisters to actively eliminate more American aggressors and their lackeys on their own soil.

On the 13th, people from the textile industry center of Lyallpur in Pakistan held a demonstration. The crowd chanted slogans condemning the U.S. aggression in Vietnam and demanding the immediate withdrawal of U.S. troops from South Vietnam.

On the 9th, workers and students in Karachi, Pakistan's largest city, held a march. Protesters carried placards with slogans in Urdu and English such as, "Johnson, the murderer of the Vietnamese people," and "The struggle of the Vietnamese people is our struggle."

During the march through the city center, the protesters burned an effigy of U.S. President Johnson and an American flag. They then went to the Chinese Consulate General in Karachi to express their solidarity with China and all other countries truly supporting the Vietnamese people's struggle against U.S. imperialism.

On the 11th, about 10,000 workers, employees, and teachers in Kolkata, India, held a demonstration. They chanted "Down with U.S. imperialism!" in protest against the U.S. aggression in Vietnam and in support of the Vietnamese people's struggle against American aggression. The protesters also chanted "Long live Mao Zedong!" and "Long live Ho Chi Minh!" They marched to the heavily guarded U.S. Consulate to submit a memorandum condemning the U.S. government's escalation of the war in Vietnam and demanding the withdrawal of U.S. troops from Vietnam.

The protesters condemned the Indian government's policy towards the U.S. and called on the Indian people to "launch a nationwide movement against U.S. imperialism."

On the 10th, over 200 young students in Kuala Lumpur held a demonstration. They carried banners and placards with slogans such as, "Yankees, go home!" and "Murderers, stop your actions!"

The angry protesters chanted slogans in front of the U.S. Information Service library, opposing the U.S. escalation of the war in Vietnam. They also smashed the library's windows with bottles and stones.

On the 13th, when British Prime Minister Wilson and former Australian Prime Minister Menzies went to Brighton to receive honorary degrees from the University of Sussex, students there held a demonstration protesting against the U.S. aggression in Vietnam and the British Labour government's support for it.

About 200 students at the University of Sussex booed and jeered as Wilson arrived. They chanted slogans such as, "Yankees, get out of Vietnam!" and "Wilson is a traitor!"

On the evening of the 13th, about 700 young people in Amsterdam held a protest rally and then marched to the U.S. Consulate General to submit a protest letter. They chanted, "Johnson is a murderer!" outside the U.S. Consulate General.

On the evening of the 10th, several hundred young people in Naples, Italy, held a demonstration in front of the U.S. Consulate. They chanted, "Kill Johnson!" and burned an effigy of Johnson made from the American flag. Later, they threw rotten eggs and paint cans at the U.S. Consulate.

Previously, citizens of Rome had held anti-U.S. demonstrations in front of the U.S. Embassy and the Italian Ministry of Foreign Affairs on the 6th and 8th.

On July 5th, over 20,000 Japanese people held a rally in Meiji Park, Tokyo, strongly protesting against U.S. imperialism's bombing of Hanoi and Haiphong.

### 美帝侵越战争“升级”罪行激起公愤　各国人民示威集会声讨杀人犯约翰逊

1966-07-16
第6版()
专栏：

　　美帝侵越战争“升级”罪行激起公愤
　　各国人民示威集会声讨杀人犯约翰逊
    据新华社讯　世界各国人民连日来继续示威游行和集会，愤怒抗议美帝国主义轰炸河内海防的侵略罪行，并声援越南人民反对美国侵略的正义斗争。
    老挝川圹省各族各界代表三百多人九日晚上举行集会，会上通过的一项决议指出，“美帝新的‘升级’行动，在世界人民面前进一步暴露了约翰逊、麦克纳马拉和腊斯克集团的假和平面目和好战面目。”
    决议表示，老挝爱国力量和老挝人民决心同兄弟的越南人民团结一致，在自己的国土上积极消灭更多的美国侵略者及其走狗。
    巴基斯坦纺织工业中心莱雅普尔人民，十三日举行示威游行。群众高呼口号，谴责美国侵略越南，要求美军立即撤出南越。
    巴基斯坦最大城市卡拉奇的工人和学生九日举行游行示威，示威者高举标语牌，上面用乌尔都文和英文写着：“约翰逊，杀害越南（人民）的凶手”，“越南人民的斗争就是我们的斗争”等口号。
    示威者在市中心游行示威时，焚毁了美国总统约翰逊的模拟象和一面美国国旗。示威者然后来到中国驻卡拉奇总领事馆，表示他们坚决站在中国以及所有其它真正支持越南人民反对美帝国主义斗争的国家的一边。
    印度加尔各答市一万名工人、职员和教员十一日举行示威游行，高呼“打倒美帝国主义！”的口号，抗议美国侵略越南，支持越南人民抗美救国的斗争。示威群众还高呼“毛泽东万岁！”“胡志明万岁！”示威者游行到戒备森严的美国领事馆递交了一份备忘录，谴责美国政府扩大侵略越南战争，并且要求美国军队撤出越南。
    示威群众谴责印度政府对美国的政策，并号召印度人民“发动一次全国范围的反对美帝国主义的群众运动”。
    吉隆坡二百多名青年学生十日举行示威游行，他们高举着旗帜和标语牌，上面写着：“美国佬，滚回去！”“杀人犯，停止你们的行动！”等口号。
    愤怒的示威者在美国新闻处的图书馆门前高呼口号，反对美国扩大侵略越南的战争，并且用瓶子、石块等砸碎了图书馆的玻璃窗。
    英国首相威尔逊和前澳大利亚总理孟席斯十三日到布赖顿去接受苏塞克斯大学授予的“名誉学位”的时候，那里的学生们举行了示威，抗议美国侵略越南和英国工党政府对美国侵略的支持。
    苏塞克斯大学的大约二百名学生在威尔逊等到来的时候，发出了嘘声和嘲笑声。他们高呼：“美国佬必须离开越南！”“威尔逊是一个卖国贼！”等口号。
    荷兰七百名青年十三日晚上在阿姆斯特丹市举行抗议大会后上街游行，并且到美国总领事馆去递交了抗议信。他们在美国总领事馆外面高呼：“约翰逊是杀人犯！”
    意大利那不勒斯的几百名青年十日晚上在美国领事馆外面举行示威。他们高呼“打死约翰逊！”并且焚烧了用美国国旗扎成的约翰逊的模拟象。后来，他们向美国领事馆扔臭鸡蛋和油漆罐。
    在这以前，罗马市民也曾在六日和八日分别在美国大使馆外面和意大利外交部前面举行了反美示威。（附图片）
    七月五日，二万多名日本人民在东京明治公园举行集会，强烈抗议美帝国主义轰炸河内和海防的罪行。　东京传真照片（新华社发）
