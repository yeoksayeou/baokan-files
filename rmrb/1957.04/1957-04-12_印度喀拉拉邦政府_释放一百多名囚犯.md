Title: Kerala Government in India Releases Over One Hundred Prisoners
Author:
Date: 1957-04-12
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, New Delhi, April 11th: The newly formed Kerala government in India announced on April 4th the reduction or remission of sentences for prisoners. On April 10th, one hundred and thirty-one prisoners were released from Kerala prisons. It is reported that many of the prisoners were arrested during peasant and worker struggles. The remission order applies to six hundred prisoners.

A press release from the Kerala government stated that the state government has full power to reduce or remit sentences for prisoners, including those sentenced to death.

An Indian central government spokesperson said on April 9th that, according to the Indian constitution, the Kerala government has the right to release political prisoners and commute death sentences.

### 印度喀拉拉邦政府  释放一百多名囚犯

1957-04-12
第4版()
专栏：

    印度喀拉拉邦政府
    释放一百多名囚犯
    新华社新德里11日电　印度喀拉拉邦新任政府4日宣布对囚犯减刑或免刑以后，在4月10日从喀拉拉的监狱中释放了一百三十一名囚犯。据报道，囚犯中间有许多在农民和劳工斗争中被捕的人。减免令适用于六百名囚犯。
    喀拉拉邦政府发表的新闻公报说，邦政府完全有权力对囚犯包括被判处死刑的犯人在内，实行减刑或免刑。
    印度中央政府发言人4月9日说，根据印度宪法，喀拉拉邦政府有权释放政治犯和对死刑犯减刑。
