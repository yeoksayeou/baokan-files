Title: Dulles Intimidates Egypt, Clamoring for Support of Israel's Forcible Passage Through the Canal and Gulf
Author: 
Date: 1957-04-04
Page: 6
Xinhua News Agency, 3rd: Washington dispatch: U.S. Secretary of State Dulles expressed dissatisfaction at a press conference on April 2nd with Egypt's recent memorandum regarding the use of the Suez Canal, believing that the memorandum did not satisfy the six principles for resolving the Suez Canal issue adopted by the United Nations Security Council last October. He said that the United States' goal for the canal is to establish the rights of canal-using countries and to establish some kind of organization of users to negotiate with Egypt, and that these aspects are required by the six principles.

Dulles said that the U.S. government has pointed out to Egypt the aspects of the Egyptian memorandum that the United States believes do not satisfy the six principles, as well as methods to "remedy these deficiencies." On the canal issue, Egypt is currently facing a choice that will largely determine Egypt's future. Dulles also expressed full support for Israel's right to send ships through the Suez Canal and the Gulf of Aqaba.

A reporter asked Dulles what the United States would do if Egypt refused to change its position. Dulles replied that the United States has no pressure to exert in terms of military threats or boycotting the canal. But this does not mean that the United States would never participate in economic sanctions against Egypt. As for boycotting the canal, this is a matter that should be decided primarily by countries that are economically dependent on the canal. The U.S. economy is not dependent on the canal to any appreciable extent.

Dulles mentioned that one of the reasons the United States refused to help Egypt build the Aswan Dam was that at the time "Egypt was developing increasingly close relations with Soviet bloc countries" and "Egypt recognized Communist China."

Speaking about Asian issues, Dulles reiterated the U.S. government's policy of continuing to occupy Taiwan and the coastal islands of China. In this regard, he denied that the United States had given Chiang Kai-shek assurances of assisting in the defense of Jinmen and Mazu, and believed that the U.S. government's "only obligation" was the power granted by the Congressional act. According to the provisions of this act, the United States will "defend the coastal islands if the defense of these islands appears to be related to the defense of Taiwan and Penghu."

### 杜勒斯对埃及进行恫吓  叫嚣支持以色列强行通过运河和海湾

1957-04-04
第6版()
专栏：

    杜勒斯对埃及进行恫吓
    叫嚣支持以色列强行通过运河和海湾
    新华社3日讯　华盛顿消息：美国国务卿杜勒斯4月2日在记者招待会上对埃及最近提出的关于使用苏伊士运河的备忘录表示不满，认为这个备忘录没有满足联合国安全理事会在去年10月通过的关于解决苏伊士运河问题的六项原则。他说，美国对运河的目标是：确立运河使用国的权利，并且建立使用国的某种组织来和埃及办交涉，而这些方面是六项原则所要求的。
    杜勒斯说，美国政府已经向埃及指出了美国认为埃及备忘录没有满足六项原则的方面以及“补救这些欠缺”的方法。在运河的问题上，埃及目前正在面临一个抉择，这种抉择会在很大程度上决定埃及的未来。杜勒斯还表示全力支持以色列享有派遣船只在苏伊士运河和亚喀巴湾通航的权利。
    有记者问杜勒斯，如果埃及拒绝改变它的立场，美国将怎样办？杜勒斯回答说，美国在军事威胁或者抵制运河方面是没有什么压力可加的。但是这并不等于说美国决不会参加对埃及的经济制裁。至于抵制运河，这是一个主要应该由经济上依赖运河的国家来决定的事情。而美国的经济是不在任何可观的程度上依赖运河的。
    杜勒斯谈到，美国所以拒绝援助埃及建立阿斯旺水坝，原因之一是当时“埃及正在同苏联集团国家发展愈益亲密的关系”，同时“埃及承认了共产党中国”。
    谈到亚洲问题，杜勒斯重申美国政府要继续霸占台湾和中国沿海岛屿的政策。在这方面，他否认美国曾经给予蒋介石以协助防守金门和马祖的保证，而认为美国政府“唯一的义务”是国会法案中所授予的权力。根据这个法案的规定，美国将“防守沿海岛屿，如果这些岛屿的防守看来同台湾和澎湖的防守有关的话”。
