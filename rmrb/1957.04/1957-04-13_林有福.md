Title: Lim Yew Hock
Author:
Date: 1957-04-13
Page: 6

    Lim Yew Hock
    Lim Yew Hock is forty-three years old this year. He succeeded Marshall as the Chief Minister of Singapore on June 8, 1956, and concurrently held the post of Minister of Labor and Welfare, which he originally held.
    He was born into a working-class family in Singapore. In his early years, he worked as a clerk in a trading company, and later specialized in trade union work. In 1947, he was elected as the first Permanent Secretary of the Singapore Clerical and Administrative Workers' Union, and later served as the Chairman of the Singapore Trades Union Congress. Before taking office as Chief Minister, he was the Chairman of the Labor Front of Singapore.
    In 1951, when he ran for election as a popularly elected member of the Singapore Legislative Assembly, he was elected with the largest majority.
    When he took office as Chief Minister last year, he stated that he would continue to strive for Singapore's independence. In December of the same year, he went to London to negotiate with the United Kingdom on the resumption of negotiations on the issue of Singapore's autonomy. (Omitted) (Attached picture)

### 林有福

1957-04-13
第6版()
专栏：人物介绍

    林有福
    林有福今年四十三岁。他于1956年6月8日继马歇尔之后出任新加坡首席部长，并兼任原来担任的劳工和福利部部长职务。
    他出生在新加坡一个工人家庭，早年曾在贸易企业中任职员，后来专门从事工会工作。1947年，他当选为新加坡职员工会的第一位常任书记，以后又担任新加坡职工总会主席。在就任首席部长之前，他是新加坡劳工阵线的主席。
    1951年他竞选新加坡立法议会的民选议员时，曾以最大的多数票当选。
    在去年就任首席部长时，他表示将继续为争取新加坡的独立而努力。同年12月，他便前往伦敦同英国商谈恢复关于新加坡自治问题的谈判。（恕）（附图片）
