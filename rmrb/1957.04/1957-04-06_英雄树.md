Title: Hero Tree
Author: Hu Mingshu
Date: 1957-04-06
Page: 8

    Hero tree, hero among trees,
    Blooms with ten thousand red flowers in March.
    In the South, in the remote regions of our motherland, on riverbanks, in forests, the kapok tree—the hero tree—is in bloom, blooming extraordinarily. On its thick branches, devoid of even a single green leaf, it bears ten thousand red flowers, like ten thousand burning torches held aloft towards the sky.
    The longevity tree is five zhang tall, perhaps, while the hero tree beside it is six zhang tall; the banyan tree next to the hero tree is ten zhang tall, maybe, but the hero tree reaches eleven zhang.
    It's not that the hero tree has any competitive spirit, but simply because it loves the sun, admires the sun, and cannot allow any branches or leaves to obscure the sunlight coming from the southeast, northwest, and every direction. It draws heat from the sun, borrows redness from the sun, and thus extraordinarily blooms with large red flowers that burn like torches.
    A gust of rain, a gust of wind,
    Red flowers blanket the ground beneath the hero tree.
    Not individual petals, but entire flowers, the petals and body forever inseparable, each red bloom the size of a palm, detaching from the branches.
    These complete flowers, having borrowed their redness from the sun, burn fiercely on the ground, burning, burning.
    The rain passes, the wind subsides—a clear sky after the rain. On the riverbanks, in the forests, appear people working, people playing.
    Beneath the hero tree appear shepherd boys, elementary school students, Young Pioneers.
    Beneath the hero tree also appear the people's heroes defending the territory. —They play together with the children.
    A PLA uncle picks up a hero flower and tucks it into a little girl's braid; the little girl returns the favor, tucking one onto his chest. ...Two Young Pioneers, holding a large wreath made of hero flowers, suddenly slip it over the neck of an unsuspecting PLA uncle, startling and delighting him. Then, he lifts the two Young Pioneers up with both hands.
    A burst of applause, another burst of applause...
    The hero tree's branches shed their fading red,
    Suddenly donning a new green attire.
    Having bloomed, it bears fruit—the hero tree bears kapok pods. The kapok pods gradually ripen, the kapok pods gradually open. The kapok pods contain something extraordinary—snow-white cotton.
    The east wind rises, the floss flies; the south wind rises, the floss flies; the May wind blows, the floss flies all over the sky...
    The extraordinary floss floats with the wind, floats... Each piece of floss carries a seed of the hero (tree).
    On the riverbanks, in the fields, on the broad roads of the cities and countryside, children, shepherd boys, elementary school students, Young Pioneers, run, jump, and cheer in the direction of the flying floss...
    The floss follows the May wind,
    The hero tree sows its seeds again...

### 英雄树
胡明树
1957-04-06
第8版()
专栏：

    英雄树
    胡明树
    英雄树，树中英雄，
    三月开花万朵红。
    在南方，在祖国的边远地方，在河岸上，在树林里，木棉树——英雄树开花了，出奇地开花了，在连一片绿叶也没有的粗枝上开着万朵红花，像是万枝燃烧着的火把擎向高空。
    万寿树高五丈吧，旁边的英雄树高六丈；英雄树边邻的大榕树高十丈吧，英雄树就高十一丈。
    不是英雄树有什么争强好胜的心理，而只是因为它爱太阳，仰慕太阳，不能让任何枝叶遮掩来自东南西北方的阳光。它从太阳吸取热，从太阳借来红色，它就出奇地开出了火把似地燃烧着的大红花。
    一阵雨，一场风，
    英雄树下落花遍地红。
    不是一片片的花瓣，而是完整的花瓣花身永不分离的一朵巴掌大的红花，从枝头脱落。
    这些完整的花朵，从太阳借来了红色，在地面上火一般燃烧着，燃烧着。
    雨过了，风息了，——雨后晴天。河岸上，树林里，出现了干活的人们，游玩的人们。
    英雄树下出现了牧童们，小学生们，红领巾们。
    英雄树下也出现了保卫疆土的人民英雄们。——他们和儿童们一起玩耍。
    一位解放军叔叔拾起了一朵英雄花插在一位小姑娘的发辫上；那位小姑娘也回敬了他一朵，把它插在他的胸前。……两位红领巾捧着一个用英雄花串成的大花圈，猛可地套在一位冷不提防的解放军叔叔的颈项上，使得对方又惊又喜。接着，他用左右两手把两个红领巾举了起来。
    一阵鼓掌声，又一阵鼓掌声……。
    英雄树枝头谢了残红，
    忽又换上新装绿葱葱。
    开了花，结了果，——英雄树上结了木棉蕉。木棉蕉渐渐成熟了，木棉蕉渐渐开了口。木棉蕉口里含着出奇的东西——雪白的棉絮。
    东风起，飞絮飞；南风起，飞絮飞；五月的风吹着，飞絮满天飞……。
    出奇的飞絮随风飘着，飘着……。每朵飞絮都带着一粒英雄（树）的种子。
    在河岸上，在田野上，在城乡的大路上，儿童们、牧童们、小学生们、红领巾们，随着飞絮的方向跑着、跳着、欢呼着……。
    飞絮随着五月的风，
    英雄树又播种……。
