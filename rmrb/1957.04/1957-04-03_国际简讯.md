International Briefs

    A Batch of Enterprises in Democratic Germany Shortens Working Hours
    The construction, textile, pharmaceutical, food, paper, timber, glass, electricity, gas, and waterworks industries in Democratic Germany have also implemented a 45-hour work week starting from April 1. This increases the number of employees in Democratic Germany who have had their work week reduced from 48 to 45 hours, according to the law on reducing working hours, to 2.5 million.
    Employees in some heavy industries in Democratic Germany, such as mining, steel, machinery manufacturing, chemical industries, and railway repair plants, had already begun implementing the 45-hour work week on March 1.

    Bulgarian Parliamentary Delegation to Democratic Germany
    A Bulgarian National Assembly delegation headed by Kozovski, Chairman of the Executive Committee of the Bulgarian National Assembly, left Sofia for Berlin on April 2 by special plane for a friendly visit at the invitation of the People's Chamber of the German Democratic Republic. The delegation members include Bulgarian Minister of Finance and State Supervision Lazarov, and member of the Central Committee of the Bulgarian Communist Party, Kostov, among fourteen people.

### 国际简讯

第5版()
专栏：国际简讯

    国际简讯
    民主德国一批企业缩短工作日
    民主德国的建筑、纺织、制药、食品、造纸、木材、玻璃、电力、煤气等工业部门以及自来水工厂从4月1日开始也实行了四十五小时工作周。这就使民主德国按照减少工作时间的法律把工作周从四十八小时缩短到四十五小时的职工增加到二百五十万人。
    民主德国的采矿、钢铁、机器制造、化学等工业以及铁路修理厂等一些重工业部门的职工，早在3月1日就已经开始实行了四十五小时工作周。
    保议会代表团去民主德国
    以保加利亚国民议会执行委员会主席科佐夫斯基为首的保加利亚国民议会代表团应德意志民主共和国人民议院的邀请，在2日乘专机离索非亚到柏林进行友好访问。代表团团员有保加利亚财政和国家监察部长拉扎罗夫、保加利亚共产党中央委员科斯托夫等十四人。
