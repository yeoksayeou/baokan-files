Date: 1957-04-22

    Soviet Research on Peaceful Uses of Thermonuclear Reactions
    The year 1956 brought greater hope than ever before for controlling thermonuclear reactions to serve mankind. The experiments conducted by Soviet scientists on using deuterium (heavy hydrogen) found in seawater to convert it into useful energy, and the problem of strictly controlling temperatures of millions of degrees, have aroused widespread attention in scientific circles around the world.
    Regarding artificially controlled thermonuclear reactions, the following is already the case: First, the equipment for generating thermonuclear energy will be extremely safe because at operating temperatures, there is little fuel inside the machine, and it will not produce dangerous fission fragments like when using uranium or plutonium as fuel. Second, the fusion of deuterium with itself, or with heavier elements, will be an inexhaustible source of energy because deuterium exists in large quantities in seawater. Third, in order to fuse deuterium under control, temperatures of 100 million degrees Celsius or higher will be required, equivalent to the high temperatures inside the sun and stars. Fourth, the main problem is how to create an electric or magnetic field to isolate the inner wall of the reaction chamber from the extremely high temperature at its center. At such high temperatures, any existing material would be completely vaporized in an instant. Fifth, a controlled thermonuclear reactor using Helium-3 as fuel may be established. This reactor will not produce any neutrons, and therefore will not require the neutron shielding necessary for hydrogen isotope fusion reactors. But the establishment of a helium fusion furnace is further off than that of a hydrogen fusion furnace because it requires a much higher temperature than the latter.
    Obtaining energy through fusion will completely solve mankind's growing need for energy. Among the several available light elements, deuterium, which exists in the world's oceans, alone can provide four times the output of existing energy sources and sustain it for one hundred million years. Therefore, establishing a controllable thermonuclear reactor is one of the great scientific tasks of this century. Applying the energy generated by fusion will help improve human health and living standards. (Science)

### 苏联和平利用热核反应的研究

1957-04-22
第3版()
专栏：学术动态

    苏联和平利用热核反应的研究
    控制热核反应以服务于人类，1956年带来了比以往更大的希望。苏联科学家进行利用存在于海水中的氘（重氢）使它转换为有用能量的实验，并将几百万度的高温加以严密控制的问题，在各国科学界引起了广泛的注意。
    关于人工控制下的热核反应，已经有如下的情况：一、产生热核能的设备将是极其安全的，因为在操作温度下，机器内的燃料少，而且不致像在用铀或钚作燃料时产生危险的裂变碎片。二、氘的互相熔合，或同更重的元素的熔合，将是一种取之不尽的能源，因为氘大量地存在于海水中。三、为了在控制下将氘加以熔合，将需要摄氏一亿度或更高的温度，相当于太阳和星体内部的高温。四、主要问题是如何产生一种电场或磁场，以便将反应室的内壁和它中心的极度高温加以隔离。在这样高的温度下，任何现有的物料一瞬间就会全部气化。五、一种以氦-3为燃料的控制下的热核反应器，可能建立起来。这种反应器将不产生任何中子，因而也不需要氢同位素熔合反应器所必需的中子屏蔽。但氦熔合炉比氢熔合炉的建立为期更远，因为它所需要的温度比后者要高出很多。
    通过熔合作用以取得能量，将可以彻底解决人类对能源的日益增长的需要。在几种可用的轻元素中，仅以存在于全世界海洋中的氘来计算，就可提供四倍于现有能源的产量，并维持一亿年。因此建立可控制的热核反应器是本世纪内伟大的科学任务之一。应用由熔合而产生的能量，将有助于改善人类的健康和生活水平。　（科）
