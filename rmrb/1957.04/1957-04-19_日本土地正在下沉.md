Title: Japan's Land is Sinking
Author:
Date: 1957-04-19
Page: 6

    Japan's Land is Sinking
    (By our newspaper) The Earthquake Research Institute of Tokyo University in Japan released a report stating that Japan's fertile land is sinking. This research report lists three reasons for Japan's "sinking feeling": natural changes in the earth's crust, earthquakes, and the increasing depletion of underground water sources.
    In industrialized areas such as Tokyo and Osaka, tall and heavy buildings are causing the loose strata to subside. One of Tokyo's largest office buildings has sunk twelve inches since its completion, to the point where steps had to be added to enter the front door.

### 日本土地正在下沉

1957-04-19
第6版()
专栏：

    日本土地正在下沉
    本报讯　日本东京大学地震研究所发表的报告说，日本的富饶的土地正在下沉。这个研究报告对于日本的“下沉感”列举出三点原因：地壳的自然变化，地震和地下水源的日益减少。
    在东京和大阪这些工业化地区中的高大的和沉重的建筑物使得松弛的地层下陷了。东京的一所最大的办公楼自从建成以来已经下陷了十二英寸，以至于必须增加台阶才能够走进前门。
