Title: Syrian Premier Accuses Western Countries of Aggressive Policies, Emphasizes Syria and the Soviet Union are Friends
Author:
Date: 1957-04-30
Page: 6
Xinhua News Agency, Damascus, April 29th: Syrian Premier al-Asali emphasized on April 28th that Syria's relationship with the Soviet Union is entirely "like the relationship between friends."

Al-Asali said, "The Soviet Union has not made any demands of us that would harm our sovereignty or in any way affect our independence."

Al-Asali said that Western countries have done the complete opposite, "They make new suggestions to us every day and want to hinder our unity and paralyze our independence."

Al-Asali said that Syria "has no hatred for the West or the East."

Al-Asali made these remarks when answering questions from former British Minister of State Anton Nutting, who was posing as a reporter for the "New York Herald Tribune." Damascus Radio broadcast Al-Asali's reply.

### 叙总理指责西方国家侵略政策  强调叙利亚和苏联是朋友

1957-04-30
第6版()
专栏：

    叙总理指责西方国家侵略政策
    强调叙利亚和苏联是朋友
    新华社29日讯　大马士革消息：叙利亚总理阿萨利4月28日强调说，叙利亚同苏联的关系完全是跟“朋友同朋友相处的关系一样。”
    阿萨利说，“苏联没有对我们提出任何损害我们主权或者在任何方面影响我们独立的要求。”
    阿萨利说，西方国家的所作所为则完全相反，它们“每天向我们提出新的建议，并且想妨碍我们的团结、使我们的独立陷于瘫痪。”
    阿萨利说，叙利亚“对西方和东方都没有仇恨。”
    阿萨利是在回答英国前国务大臣安东·纳丁以“纽约先驱论坛报”记者身份提出的问题的时候说这番话的。大马士革电台广播了阿萨利的答复。
