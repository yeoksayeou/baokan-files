Gandhi's Letter to Tolstoy Discovered

    A previously unpublished letter written by Gandhi to the Russian novelist Tolstoy forty-seven years ago has been discovered in a book in the Tolstoy Library in the Soviet Union.

    The Soviet journal "Soviet Oriental Studies" published the contents of this letter on April 8. In the letter, Gandhi asked Tolstoy to use his influence to promote the passive resistance movement in any way he saw fit.

    Gandhi said in the letter, "If we persevere to the end—and I think we will persevere to the end, I have no doubt that we will achieve final victory, and your encouragement to us in your own way can only strengthen our resolve."

    This letter is the second letter Gandhi wrote to Tolstoy, and it was written in London on November 10, 1909.

### 甘地给托尔斯泰的信被发现

1957-04-14
第5版()
专栏：国际文化消息

    甘地给托尔斯泰的信被发现
    四十七年前甘地写给俄国小说家托尔斯泰的一封没有发表过的信已经在苏联托尔斯泰图书馆的一本书内发现。
    苏联“苏维埃东方学”杂志4月8日发表了这封信的内容。甘地在信中要求托尔斯泰以他的影响用他认为适合的任何方式来宣传消极抵抗运动。
    甘地在信中说，“如果我们坚持到底——我想我们是会坚持到底的，我毫不怀疑我们将取得最后的胜利，你以你的方式对我们的鼓励，只能加强我们的决心。”
    这封信是甘地写给托尔斯泰的第二封信，它是1909年11月10日在伦敦写的。
