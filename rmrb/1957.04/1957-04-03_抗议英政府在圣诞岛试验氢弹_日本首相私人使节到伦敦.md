Title: Protests Against British Government's Hydrogen Bomb Tests on Christmas Island: Japanese Prime Minister's Special Envoy Arrives in London
Author:
Date: 1957-04-03
Page: 6
Xinhua News Agency, London, April 2nd: A Japanese delegation demanding that Britain cancel its plan to test hydrogen bombs on Christmas Island arrived in London on April 1st.

The delegation consists of four people, including Masashige Matsushita, President of Rikkyo University, who is the personal envoy of Japanese Prime Minister Nobusuke Kishi. Upon arriving at London Airport, Matsushita told reporters that the main task of his delegation was to deliver a letter from Japanese Prime Minister Nobusuke Kishi to British Prime Minister Macmillan, protesting the British government's decision to test hydrogen bombs on Christmas Island.

He said he would convey to Macmillan the "tragic experience and painful feelings" of Japan's opposition to atomic weapons and nuclear explosions. He said: "I will request a stop to nuclear explosions, and I will make this request not only as the personal envoy of the (Japanese) Prime Minister, but also as a Japanese, an educator, and a human being."

Matsushita said that in addition to meeting with Macmillan, he also hoped to meet with people from British religious, political, and academic circles.

A Japanese student demonstration protesting against Britain's hydrogen bomb tests on Christmas Island sat in front of the British Embassy, listening to a representative read a letter of protest to the British Ambassador.

### 抗议英政府在圣诞岛试验氢弹  日本首相私人使节到伦敦

1957-04-03
第6版()
专栏：

    抗议英政府在圣诞岛试验氢弹
    日本首相私人使节到伦敦
    新华社2日讯　伦敦消息：一个要求英国取消在圣诞岛试验氢弹计划的日本代表团4月1日到达伦敦。
    这个代表团是由日本首相岸信介的私人使节、立教大学校长松下正寿等四人组成的。代表团团长松下正寿在到达伦敦机场的时候向记者们说，他的代表团的主要任务是递交一封日本首相岸信介给英国首相麦克米伦的信，抗议英国政府决定在圣诞岛试验氢弹。
    他说，他将向麦克米伦转达日本反对原子武器和原子核爆炸的“悲惨的经验和痛苦的感情”。他说：“我将请求停止原子核爆炸，我不仅将以（日本）首相的私人使节的身份这样要求，而且将以一个日本人、一个教育工作者和一个人的身份这样要求。”
    松下正寿表示除了要会见麦克米伦以外还希望会见英国的宗教界、政界和学术界人士等。
    抗议英国将在圣诞岛试验氢弹的日本学生示威队伍，坐在英国大使馆门前，听着一个代表向英国大使宣读抗议书。
