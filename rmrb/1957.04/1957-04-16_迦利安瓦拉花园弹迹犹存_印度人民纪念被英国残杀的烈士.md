Title: Jallianwala Bagh Still Bears Bullet Marks: Indian People Commemorate Martyrs Massacred by the British
Author:
Date: 1957-04-16
Page: 7
    Xinhua News Agency, New Delhi, April 15th: People across India paid tribute to the "Martyrs of Jallianwala Bagh" on April 13th.

    In 1919, to maintain its special power to suppress the masses, the British rulers enacted the Rowlatt Act. This provoked widespread anger, and demonstrations, strikes, or riots occurred in many places. The British authorities carried out brutal suppression. On April 13th of the same year, the masses of Amritsar gathered in Jallianwala Bagh to protest British atrocities. British General Dyer ordered the shooting, killing 400 people and injuring 1,000 to 2,000. The marks of machine gun bullets on the walls around the garden are still visible today. Kitchlew, Chairman of the All-India Peace Council, was one of the leaders of this movement.

### 迦利安瓦拉花园弹迹犹存  印度人民纪念被英国残杀的烈士

1957-04-16
第7版()
专栏：

    迦利安瓦拉花园弹迹犹存
    印度人民纪念被英国残杀的烈士
    据新华社新德里15日电　印度全国各地人民在4月13日向“迦利安瓦拉花园烈士”致敬。
    1919年，英国统治者为了维持它镇压群众的特殊权力，制定了罗拉特法案。这激起了普遍的愤怒，许多地方群众发生示威、罢工或暴动。英国当局进行了残暴的镇压。同年4月13日，阿姆利则市的群众聚集在迦利安瓦拉花园里抗议英国的暴行。英国将军戴尔下令开枪，有四百人被杀，有一千到两千人受伤。在花园周围的墙上机关枪子弹的痕迹至今犹存。全印和平理事会主席克其鲁是这个运动的领导人之一。
