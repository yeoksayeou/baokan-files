Title: The World's Most: Which Country is the Largest? Which Country is the Smallest? ... What is the Hottest Place? Which River is the Longest? ...
Author:
Date: 1957-04-19
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, New York, 18th: "What is the biggest, brightest, and oldest..." The American "Science Digest" published some statistics called "The World's Most" in its February issue this year.

The magazine pointed out that the largest country in the world is the Soviet Union (almost three times the size of the United States). The smallest country is the Vatican, only one hundred and nine acres (0.44 square kilometers).

The hottest place in the world is in western Libya, with the highest temperature in the shade being 136 degrees Fahrenheit; the coldest place is in northern Siberia, reaching a low of minus 108 degrees.

Jefferson County, Iowa, USA, holds the record for the highest rainfall - 0.69 inches per minute on July 10, 1955.

Yuma County, Arizona, USA, is a destination for sun lovers, with 96% of the year being sunny, at least it was in 1924.

"How deep is the deepest part of the ocean?" The answer is seven miles, southwest of Guam.

The longest river in the world is the Nile, 4,145 miles, the Amazon River 3,900 miles, and the Missouri-Mississippi River is eight miles shorter than the Amazon. The most spectacular waterfall is in Venezuela, Angel Falls, with a total height of 3,312 feet.

The brightest star in the universe is Sirius, commonly known as the Dog Star, said to be twenty-six times brighter than the sun.

The largest animal in the world is the blue whale, weighing 131 tons (each ton equals 2,000 catties); while an African elephant weighs only seven tons.

The magazine says that the Dutch are the longest-lived people in the world, with an average life expectancy of 71 years for men and 73 years for women.

The magazine believes that the shortest reign in history was that of King John I of France in the fourteenth century, who reigned for only 120 hours.

The longest strike occurred in Dublin (Ireland), where the bar waiters' union launched a strike against a hotel that lasted nearly fifteen years.

The 1923 earthquake in Japan was the most severe, killing more than 142,000 people.

"Science Digest" said that these unrelated figures were excerpted from a recently published British book called "The Guinness Book of Records," which includes thousands of interesting figures.

### 世界之最  那个国家最大？那个国家最小？……  什么地方最热？什么河流最长？……

1957-04-19
第6版()
专栏：

    世界之最
    那个国家最大？那个国家最小？……
    什么地方最热？什么河流最长？……
    新华社18日讯　纽约消息：“什么是最大的最亮的和最老的……”美国“科学文摘”今年2月号以这样的标题发表了一些号称“世界之最”的统计数字。
    这家杂志指出，世界上最大的国家是苏联（有将近美国的三倍大）。最小的国家是梵蒂冈，只有一百零九英亩（零点四四平方公里）。
    世界最热的地方是利比亚西部，阴暗处最热为华氏一百三十六度；最冷的地方是西伯利亚北部，最冷达零下一百零八度。
    美国衣阿华州杰斐逊县保持一项雨量的最高纪录——1955年7月10日那天，每分钟的雨量为0.69英寸。
    美国阿利桑那州育马县是阳光爱好者的去处，一年中可能有96％都是晴天，至少在1924年是如此。
    “海洋最深究竟有多深？”答案是七英里，在关岛的西南。
    世界上最长的河，尼罗河四千一百四十五英里，亚马孙河三千九百英里，密苏里—密士失必河比亚马孙河短八英里。委内瑞拉的瀑布最为壮观，安琪儿瀑布全高三千三百一十二英尺。
    宇宙间最亮的星是天狼星，俗名天狗星，据说比太阳还亮二十六倍。
    世界上最大的动物是蓝鲸鱼，重一百三十一吨（每吨合二千市斤）；而一只非洲象只有七吨。
    这家杂志说，荷兰人是世界上最长寿的，男人平均寿命为七十一岁，女人为七十三岁。
    杂志认为，历史上最短的政权是十四世纪的法国国王让第一世，他在位只有一百二十小时。
    最长的罢工发生在都柏林（爱尔兰），那里的酒吧侍者工会对一个酒店发动的罢工延续将近十五年。
    1923年日本的地震记录是最严重的，死亡十四万二千多人。
    “科学文摘”说，这些不相关的数字是从最近英国出版的一本叫“吉纳斯汇编世界第一”的书中摘引出来的，这本书包括了成千个有趣的数字。
