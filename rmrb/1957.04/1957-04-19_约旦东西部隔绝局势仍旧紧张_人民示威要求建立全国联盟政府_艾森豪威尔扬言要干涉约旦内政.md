Title: Jordan's East-West Isolation Remains Tense; People Demonstrate Demanding the Establishment of a National Coalition Government; Eisenhower Threatens to Interfere in Jordan's Internal Affairs
Author:
Date: 1957-04-19
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, Beirut, April 17th: Amman news, the situation in Jordan remains tense.

    Western Jordan has been isolated from the eastern part of the country. In the cities of Nablus, Jerusalem, Ramallah, and Hebron, the masses continue to hold demonstrations. The demonstrators demand the establishment of a national coalition government with the participation of all political parties and formally reject the "Eisenhower Doctrine." It is reported that the Arab Ba'ath Party, the Syrian Social Nationalist Party, the Communist Party, and other political parties have established a united front.

    Xinhua News Agency, April 18th: Amman news: Jordan's new Prime Minister Khalidi said on the 17th that despite the changes in Jordan's political situation, steps are still being taken to establish a federation of Syria, Egypt, and Jordan. Khalidi said: "Establishing an economic and cultural union is the first step towards unification. This is what we are doing now."

    In his first meeting with reporters since becoming Prime Minister, Khalidi said that he believes it would be a serious mistake if the United States opposed the establishment of such an Arab federation. He said: "Nothing is more beneficial to the United States than a stable and prosperous Arab world, and the establishment of a federation of Arab states is the best way to achieve this."

    Xinhua News Agency, April 18th: Washington news: US President Eisenhower stated at a press conference on the 17th that the United States could provide "assistance" to Jordan under the "Eisenhower Doctrine" and the 1950 Tripartite Declaration of the United States, Britain, and France on intervention in Middle Eastern affairs. He said that if Jordan is subjected to so-called "communist aggression" or attacked by Israel, the United States could come out and "assist" according to the aforementioned statements. Jordanian masses rally against the Eisenhower Doctrine.

### 约旦东西部隔绝局势仍旧紧张  人民示威要求建立全国联盟政府  艾森豪威尔扬言要干涉约旦内政

1957-04-19
第6版()
专栏：

    约旦东西部隔绝局势仍旧紧张
    人民示威要求建立全国联盟政府
    艾森豪威尔扬言要干涉约旦内政
    新华社18日讯　据塔斯社贝鲁特17日讯：安曼消息，约旦局势仍旧紧张。
    约旦西部已经同这个国家的东部隔绝。在纳布鲁斯、耶路撒冷、拉马拉和哈利尔各城市，群众继续举行示威游行。示威游行的人要求建立有各政治党派参加的全国联盟的政府，并且正式拒绝“艾森豪威尔主义”。据报道，阿拉伯复兴党、国家社会党、共产党等政党已经建立了统一战线。
    新华社18日讯　安曼消息：约旦新首相哈利迪在17日说，尽管约旦的政局发生了变化，仍然在继续采取步骤，以便使叙利亚、埃及和约旦三国成立一个联邦。哈利迪说：“成立一个经济和文化联合组织是走向统一的第一步。这就是我们现在正在进行的事情。”
    哈利迪在担任首相以来第一次接见记者时说，他认为，要是美国反对建立这样一个阿拉伯联邦，这将是严重的错误。他说：“什么也不如一个稳定、繁荣的阿拉伯世界对美国有利，而建立阿拉伯国家联邦是实现这一点的最好途径。”
    据新华社18日讯　华盛顿消息：美国总统艾森豪威尔17日在记者招待会上表示，美国根据“艾森豪威尔主义”和1950年的美英法三国干预中东事务的宣言，都可以给予约旦以“援助”。他说，如果约旦受到所谓“共产党侵略”或者受到以色列攻击时，美国根据上述那些声明就可以出而“援助”。约旦群众集会反对艾森豪威尔主义
