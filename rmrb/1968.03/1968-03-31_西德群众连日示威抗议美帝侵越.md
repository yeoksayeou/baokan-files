Title: West German Masses Protest U.S. Aggression in Vietnam
Author:
Date: 1968-03-31
Edition: 5

West German masses have been holding continuous demonstrations to protest U.S. aggression in Vietnam. On the 29th, 2,000 people in the city of Kiel, carrying red flags and banners, held a demonstration march in the city. Before the march, the demonstrators held a protest rally in the city hall square, where speakers called on the West German people to support the Vietnamese people and oppose U.S. imperialism. On the 26th, 600 young people in Nuremberg also held a demonstration march. The demonstrators gathered in front of the hotel where the U.S. occupying troops were staying and shouted slogans such as "Yankees go home" and "Johnson—murderer."

### 西德群众连日示威抗议美帝侵越

1968-03-31
第5版()
专栏：简明新闻

    西德群众连日示威抗议美帝侵越
    西德基尔市的二千名群众，打着红旗和标语牌，二十九日在市内举行示威游行，反对美帝侵略越南。游行前，示威者在市政厅广场举行了抗议集会，发言者号召西德人民支持越南人民、反对美帝国主义。纽伦堡的六百名青年二十六日晚也举行了示威游行。示威青年集合到美国占领军的军人旅馆前，高呼“美国佬滚回去”和“约翰逊——杀人犯”等口号。
