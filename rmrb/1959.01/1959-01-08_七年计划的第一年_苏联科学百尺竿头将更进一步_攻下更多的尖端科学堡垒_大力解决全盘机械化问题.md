Title: The First Year of the Seven-Year Plan: Soviet Science Will Reach New Heights, Capturing More Cutting-Edge Scientific Fortresses, and Vigorously Solving the Problem of Overall Mechanization

Author: 
Date: 1959-01-08
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, Moscow, 7th - In 1959, the first year of the Soviet Union's Seven-Year Plan, Soviet scientists will launch an offensive in many cutting-edge scientific departments, making significant contributions to the scientific research stipulated in the Seven-Year Plan.  Toptchiev, Vice President of the Soviet Academy of Sciences, published an article in "Pravda," describing the broad scope of scientific research that the Soviet Union will undertake this year and the task of the scientific community to criticize bourgeois ideology.

Toptchiev said that Soviet science has achieved world-leading status in a series of important knowledge sectors in a relatively short period.  In mentioning this, he cited the Soviet Union's great achievements in the peaceful use of atomic energy, controlled thermonuclear reactions, intercontinental missiles, and artificial satellites.

Regarding theoretical research, Toptchiev pointed out that Soviet scientists will continue to conduct arduous and complex scientific research on the following issues: controlled thermonuclear reaction processes, nuclear physics, especially further research on "elementary particles," solid-state physics, especially semiconductor problems, the theory of fast computers, and a series of the most significant problems in modern mathematics. Regarding technological progress, Toptchiev said that Soviet science is facing important problems.  Therefore, this year, research related to formulating unified scientific principles for fuel and power supply and demand and the Soviet Union's power zoning will be greatly expanded, as will research related to solving a series of important physical and technical tasks in long-distance power transmission and unified power system management.

Toptchiev also talked about overall mechanization and the use of electronic technology for automatic control.  Toptchiev wrote that the main forces should be used to design new machines. The Institute of Automation and Remote Control is carrying out a lot of valuable work, using automatic machinery in oil extraction and blast furnace smelting processes, etc.

Toptchiev emphasized that during the Seven-Year Plan, the output of chemical products will almost double, and the output of nickel, titanium, germanium, and silicon will greatly increase, and other non-ferrous metals and rare elements will also be produced.  The Soviet Union's raw material base for rare elements is fully capable of supplying industry with the necessary quantities of rare metals, alloys, and compounds.

Toptchiev pointed out that it is necessary to criticize modern revisionism and bourgeois ideology.  He said that this criticism should be carried out and strengthened in various scientific fields, such as natural sciences, philosophy, history, political economy, and legal theory.

### 七年计划的第一年  苏联科学百尺竿头将更进一步  攻下更多的尖端科学堡垒  大力解决全盘机械化问题

1959-01-08
第4版()
专栏：

    七年计划的第一年
    苏联科学百尺竿头将更进一步
    攻下更多的尖端科学堡垒
    大力解决全盘机械化问题
    新华社莫斯科7日电　在1959年——苏联七年计划的第一年，苏联科学家将向许多尖端科学部门展开进攻，在七年计划所规定的科学研究方面做出重大贡献。苏联科学院副院长托普切夫在“真理报”上发表文章，叙述了苏联在今年将展开的科学研究的广泛规模，以及科学界批判资产阶级思想的任务。
    托普切夫说，苏联科学在比较短的时期内已经在一系列重要的知识部门取得了世界第一的地位。在谈到这一点时，他列举了苏联在和平利用原子能、可控制的热核反应、洲际导弹、人造卫星等方面的巨大成就。
    托普切夫在谈到理论研究时指出，苏联科学家将继续对下列问题展开艰巨复杂的科学研究：可控制热核反应过程，原子核物理学，特别是对“基本粒子”的进一步研究，固体物理学，特别是半导体问题，快速计算机的理论和一系列现代数学上最重大的问题。在技术进步方面，托普切夫说，苏联科学面临着重要的问题。因此在这一年中将大大扩大同制订统一的燃料——动力供求和苏联动力分区的科学原则有关的研究工作，扩大同解决远距离输电和统一电力系统管理方面一系列重要的物理和技术任务有关的研究工作。
    托普切夫还谈到全盘机械化和用电子技术自动操纵的问题。托普切夫写道，应该拿主要力量来设计新机器。自动化和远距离操纵研究所正在进行很多有价值的工作，在石油开采和高炉冶炼过程等等方面使用自动机械。
    托普切夫强调指出，在七年计划中，化学品产量差不多要增加两倍，镍、钛、锗、硅的产量将大大增加，还要生产其他有色金属和稀有元素。苏联稀有元素的原料基地完全能够为工业供应必要数量的稀有金属、合金和化合物。
    托普切夫指出，必须批判现代修正主义和资产阶级思想意识。他说，应该在自然科学、哲学、历史学、政治经济学和法学理论等等各个科学领域中展开和加强这种批判。
