Title: US Public Opinion a Mixture of Admiration and Mortification, Many Newspapers Forced to Applaud Soviet Union's Great Achievements
Author:
Date: 1959-01-06
Page: 4
Xinhua News Agency, January 5th, New York Dispatch: The news of the Soviet cosmic rocket soaring in interstellar space has become the most important news for all American news agencies and newspapers.

　　Yesterday, New York newspapers published many articles and news from their commentators and reporters stationed in Moscow, Washington, London and other places under large headlines. The "New York Times" published an editorial saying, "We echo the world's applause for the new scientific victory of Soviet scientists. It is a historically significant achievement to send such a heavy behemoth from Earth into interstellar space hundreds of thousands of kilometers away." The editorial also believes that the flight of the Soviet cosmic rocket "is the beginning of mankind's direct study of the solar system." This newspaper acknowledged in its weekly review of major events that "the Russians are still better than the Americans in developing interstellar rockets, and maintain the championship." "New York Times" reporter Finney reported that "the Soviet Union has succeeded in areas where the United States has failed four times, which emphatically demonstrates the Soviet Union's technological and spiritual superiority." The Associated Press published an article by science commentator Alton Blakeslee. He wrote: "Now, Soviet scientists have added a little brother planet to Earth." Blakeslee went on to write, "The inscription of 'Union of Soviet Socialist Republics, January 1959' on the planet brings great prestige to Soviet scientists."

　　An Associated Press reporter wrote: "In the struggle for prestige in conquering the universe," "the Russian rocket with the hammer and sickle symbol" is a "new and serious challenge" to the United States.

　　American space experts treat the Soviet Union's world-shocking achievements with a mixture of admiration and mortification.

　　Glennan, director of the U.S. National Aeronautics and Space Administration, said that this achievement is another significant result of the Soviet Union's active efforts in space research. He said, "Soviet scientists and engineers who have contributed to expanding human knowledge of the solar system for peaceful purposes should be congratulated by the world's scientific community."

　　American space expert Eric said that the United States is probably eighteen months to two years behind the Soviet Union in terms of specific plans for launching rockets to the moon and exploring Venus, as well as rocket technology.

　　Lee Haworth, a member of the U.S. Atomic Energy Commission, expressed "a feeling full of admiration" for the Soviet Union's achievements.

### 美国舆论钦佩与懊丧交集 许多报纸也不得不为苏联伟大成就喝采

1959-01-06
第4版()
专栏：

   美国舆论钦佩与懊丧交集
   许多报纸也不得不为苏联伟大成就喝采
    新华社5日讯　纽约消息：苏联宇宙火箭飞翔在星际空间的消息成为美国所有通讯社和报纸的最重要的新闻。
    昨天纽约各报以大字标题刊登了它们的评论员和驻莫斯科、华盛顿、伦敦等地记者的很多文章和消息。“纽约时报”发表社论说，“我们响应全世界对苏联科学家新的科学胜利的喝彩。从地球上把这样重的庞然大物送到数十万公里以外的星际空间是有历史意义的功绩”。社论并且认为，苏联宇宙火箭的飞行“是人类直接研究太阳系的开始”。这家报纸在一周大事评述中承认，“俄国人在发展星际火箭方面仍然胜于美国，而保持着冠军”。“纽约时报”记者芬尼的一篇报道说，“苏联在美国曾经四次遭到失败的方面取得成功，这种情况，着重表明苏联在技术上和精神上的优势”。美联社发表了科学评论员艾尔顿·布来克斯利的文章。他写道：“现在，苏联科学家给地球增添了一个小兄弟行星”。布来克斯利接着写道，“行星上的‘苏维埃社会主义共和国联盟·1959年1月’的标帜给苏联科学家带来极大的威望。”
    美联社记者写道：“在征服宇宙方面为树立威信而进行的斗争中”，“带有镰刀和锤子标帜的俄国火箭”是对美国的“新的严重的挑战”。
    美国的宇宙空间专家怀着既钦佩又懊丧的心情对待苏联这个震惊世界的成就。
    美国全国星际和空间航行局局长格伦南说，这个成就是苏联在空间研究方面所作的积极努力的另一个显著的结果。他说，“在扩大人类对太阳系的知识用于和平目的努力中作出贡献的苏联科学家和工程师们，应该得到全世界科学界的祝贺”。
    美国宇宙空间专家埃里克说，美国在向月球发射火箭和探测金星的具体计划以及火箭技术方面大概比苏联落后十八个月到两年。
    美国原子能委员会委员李毕表示对苏联的这一成就“充满了敬佩之感”。
