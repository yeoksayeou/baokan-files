Venus's Carbon Dioxide Layer

Venus is particularly rich in carbon dioxide, which has become the main component of the Venusian atmosphere. This is due to the differences in physical conditions and evolutionary processes between Venus and Earth. Other planets also have compositions very different from Earth's. For example, Jupiter and Saturn have large amounts of hydrogen, as well as methane and ammonia, and the main component of the Martian atmosphere is likely nitrogen. As for a thorough explanation of why these situations formed, this is part of the origin and evolution of the solar system, and space rockets will provide us with a wealth of valuable information to pave the way for solving these major problems.

### 金星的二氧化碳层

1959-01-07
第7版()
专栏：名词解释

    金星的二氧化碳层
    金星上二氧化碳特别丰富，成为金星大气的最主要成分，这是由于金星的物理条件以及演化过程和地球有所不同而造成的。其他行星也有这种在组成上和地球很不同的情况，例如木星和土星上有大量氢气，还有甲烷和氨，火星大气的主要成分很可能是氮等等。至于要彻底解释这些情况为什么形成，这就是太阳系起源和演化问题的一部分，宇宙火箭将为我们取得大量珍贵的资料，为解决这些重大问题铺平道路。
