Title: Panama Decides to Extend Territorial Waters; U.S. Government Actually Prepares to Protest
Author: 
Date: 1959-01-12
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, 11th Dispatch from Washington: The U.S. government is unreasonably opposed to the bill passed by the Panamanian National Assembly concerning the extension of territorial waters to twelve nautical miles.

The Panamanian National Assembly passed a bill on December 12th of last year stipulating that the territorial waters of the Panamanian mainland and islands be extended from three nautical miles to twelve nautical miles. Prior to this, Panama had been forced to grant the United States special judicial rights over the sea surface of the Panama Canal. The United States has consistently opposed Panama's demand to extend its territorial waters, because this would make the coastal waters of the Panama Canal under U.S. control Panama's territorial waters.

According to United Press International reports, the U.S. government issued a note to the Panamanian government on the 10th, stating that the U.S. government has "no obligation" to recognize Panama's extended territorial waters. The report said that the United States is also prepared to file a "protest" with the Panamanian Foreign Ministry on this matter.

### 巴拿马决定扩展领海范围  美国政府竟准备提出抗议

1959-01-12
第5版()
专栏：

    巴拿马决定扩展领海范围
    美国政府竟准备提出抗议
    新华社11日讯　华盛顿消息：美国政府无理反对巴拿马国民议会通过的关于延伸领海十二海里的法案。
    巴拿马国民议会在去年12月12日通过一项法案，规定把巴拿马大陆和岛屿的领海范围从三海里扩展到十二海里。在这以前，巴拿马曾被迫给予美国享有巴拿马运河海面特有的司法权。美国一直反对巴拿马延伸领海的要求，因为这将使美国控制下的巴拿马运河的沿海海面成为巴拿马的领海。
    据合众国际社报道，美国政府10日向巴拿马政府发出了照会，表示美国政府没有承认巴拿马延伸领海范围的“任何义务”。消息说，美国还准备就此向巴拿马外交部提出“抗议”。
