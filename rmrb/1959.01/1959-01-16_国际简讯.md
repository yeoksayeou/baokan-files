International Briefs

	The Soviet Union will supply Afghanistan with a batch of wheat
	Kabul News: According to the Eastern News Agency, the Soviet government has agreed to supply Afghanistan with 40,000 tons of wheat at the request of the Afghan government.
	It is reported that the United States has also agreed to supply Afghanistan with a batch of wheat.
	The Brazilian Congress will raise disaster relief funds
	Rio de Janeiro News: The Brazilian Congress will raise new funds to be used as emergency relief for disaster victims. According to newspapers, in the drought-stricken areas of northeastern Brazil, thousands of people have nothing to eat, and the situation has become very serious. In Ceará State, more than half of the infants under the age of one have died in the most severely affected areas.
	Lebanon recognizes the Algerian Provisional Government
	Beirut News: A communiqué issued by the Lebanese government on the evening of the 14th stated that the cabinet decided to recognize the Provisional Government of the Republic of Algeria after holding a meeting today. The communiqué stated that Lebanon's recognition of the Provisional Government of the Republic of Algeria "does not affect the sincere and friendly relations between Lebanon and France."
		Dechin Kyaw Dun arrested
	Yangon News: Dechin Kyaw Dun, the General Secretary of the Anti-Fascist People's Freedom League (Clean) of Burma and the General Secretary of the All Burma Peasants Association, was arrested at his residence on the morning of the 14th. He was formerly the Minister of Agriculture and Forestry in the former U Nu-Dechin Din government.
	The Central Executive Committee of the Anti-Fascist People's Freedom League (Clean) decided on the same day that former Minister of Interior Bo Min Gaung would succeed him as General Secretary.
	According to local newspaper reports, Dechin Din Myat, the Vice Chairman of the Anti-Fascist People's Freedom League (Clean) of the Yong Sheng County branch, and U Soe, a member of the National House of Representatives, were also arrested.

### 国际简讯

1959-01-16
第5版()
专栏：国际简讯

    国际简讯
    苏联将供给阿富汗一批小麦
    喀布尔讯：据东方通讯社报道，苏联政府应阿富汗政府的请求，已经同意供给阿富汗四万吨小麦。
    据报道，美国也同意向阿富汗供给一批小麦。
  巴西国会将筹措救灾基金
    里约热内卢讯：巴西国会将筹措新的基金用来作为紧急救济灾民的费用。据报纸报道：在巴西东北部的旱灾地区，成千上万的人没有东西吃，情况已经到了非常严重的地步。在西阿拉州，在最严重的受灾地区有一半以上未满周岁的婴孩死亡。
    黎巴嫩承认阿临时政府
    贝鲁特讯：黎巴嫩政府14日晚间发表的公报说，内阁在今天举行了会议以后，决定承认阿尔及利亚共和国临时政府。公报说，黎巴嫩承认阿尔及利亚共和国临时政府，并“不影响黎巴嫩同法国之间真挚和友好的关系”。
        德钦觉顿被捕
    仰光讯：缅甸（廉洁的）反法西斯人民自由同盟总书记、全缅农民协会总书记德钦觉顿14日上午在他的寓所中被捕。他曾经是前吴努—德钦丁政府中的农林部长。
      （廉洁的）反法西斯人民自由同盟中央执行委员会在当天决定由前内政部长波敏贡继任总书记。
    据此间报纸消息，（廉洁的）反法西斯人民自由同盟永生县分部副主席德钦丁妙、民族院议员吴苏等也被逮捕。
