International Briefs

    South African Coloreds Oppose Apartheid
    Johannesburg: The African National Congress of South Africa and four other organizations are preparing to implement an economic boycott to protest the apartheid policies of the federal authorities.
    Iraq Bans Import of Certain Foreign Goods
    Baghdad: Iraqi Minister of Economy Ibrahim Kubba has requested the Supreme Supply Committee to ban the import of certain foreign goods in order to protect the development of Iraq's national industry.
    Tunisia Trying Treason Case
    Tunis: The Tunisian Supreme Court began on the 24th to try former Prime Minister Mohammed Salah Moussali and seven of his cabinet members, who were accused of treason. Moussali was a puppet prime minister during the French rule of Tunisia before its independence in 1956.
    Morocco Protests French Rocket Weapon Testing
    Rabat: The Moroccan government recently protested to the French government against France's plan to test rocket weapons in the Colomb-Béchar area (within Algeria) adjacent to Morocco.
    Cuba Refuses to Establish Religious Classes in Public Schools
    Havana: Cuban Minister of Education Armando Hart refused on the 22nd a suggestion from the Roman Catholic Church to establish religious courses in Cuban public schools.

### 国际简讯

1959-02-26
第5版()
专栏：国际简讯

    国际简讯
    南非有色人反对种族隔离政策
    约翰内斯堡讯：南非的非洲人国民大会和另外四个组织准备实行经济抵制，以抗议联邦当局的种族隔离政策。伊拉克禁止某些外国货入口
    巴格达讯：伊拉克经济部长易卜拉欣·库巴已经要求最高供应委员会禁止某些外国商品进口，以保护伊拉克民族工业的发展。
    突尼斯审讯叛国案
    突尼斯讯：突尼斯最高法院24日开始审讯被控犯有叛国罪的前首相穆罕默德·萨拉姆·穆萨利和他的七名阁员。穆萨利是突尼斯在1956年独立以前、法国统治时期的一名傀儡首相。
    摩洛哥抗议法国试验火箭武器
    拉巴特讯：摩洛哥政府最近向法国政府提出抗议，抗议法国计划在邻近摩洛哥的科隆培沙地区（阿尔及利亚境内）试验火箭武器。
    古巴拒绝在公立学校设宗教课
    哈瓦那讯：古巴教育部长阿曼多·哈特曾在22日拒绝了罗马天主教会提出要在古巴的公立学校里开设宗教课程的建议。
