Title: Geneva Conference Co-Chairmen Send Letters to Indian Government and International Commission for Laos Hoping to Continue Efforts for Peace in Indochina
Author:
Date: 1959-02-04
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, February 4th. The two chairmen of the 1954 Geneva Conference, Soviet Foreign Minister A. Gromyko and British Foreign Secretary Selwyn Lloyd, wrote to the Indian government and the International Commission for Supervision and Control in Laos on January 31, responding to questions regarding the Commission's future activities. The full text of the two letters is as follows:

Letter from the Two Chairmen of the Geneva Conference on Indochina to the Indian Government

Under the authorization of the British and Soviet governments, British Minister of State for Foreign Affairs, A. Noble, and Soviet Ambassador Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary to Britain, Ya. A. Malik, on behalf of the two chairmen of the Geneva Conference on Indochina, met in London and exchanged views on issues related to the further activities of the International Commission for Laos.

They discussed the Indian government's memoranda of May 3 and 13, 1958, and considered that the Indian government consistently cares about the implementation of the Geneva Agreements, which are the foundation of peace and security in Indochina.

In the meantime, the two chairmen received a notification from the chairman of the International Commission for Laos, stating that the commission had passed a resolution on July 19, 1958, by a majority vote, stipulating that "the Lao Commission should suspend its activities indefinitely, but may reconvene according to normal procedures."

The two chairmen noted the Indian government's explanation that this resolution does not affect the legal provisions of the commission, nor does it diminish the commission's authority in performing the tasks and responsibilities stipulated in the Geneva Agreements. The two chairmen agreed that the commission's resolution of July 19, 1958, was a procedural decision regarding the indefinite suspension of work, and has nothing to do with the dissolution of the commission. They agreed that there was no mention in any way of repealing any provisions concerning the International Commission in the Agreement on the Cessation of Hostilities in Laos, especially Article 39.

The two chairmen wish to express their gratitude to the Indian government (whose representative serves as chairman of the International Supervisory Commission in Indochina) for its valuable contribution to the smooth work of the aforementioned commission, and hope that it will continue to actively promote the commission's activities for the benefit of peace in Indochina.

		January 31, 1959
			A. Gromyko
			Soviet Foreign Minister
			Selwyn Lloyd
			British Foreign Secretary

Letter from the Two Chairmen of the Geneva Conference on Indochina to the Chairman of the International Commission for Laos

The two chairmen of the Geneva Conference on Indochina have the honor to acknowledge that they have received, through the assistance of the Indian government, the letter of July 22, 1958, from the chairman of the International Commission for Laos. The two chairmen noted that the commission passed a resolution on July 19 by a majority vote: "The Lao Commission should suspend its activities indefinitely, but may reconvene according to normal procedures."

The two chairmen wish to take this opportunity to highly value the positive role played by the International Supervisory Commission for Laos in implementing the agreements reached at the Geneva Conference, and in particular to express gratitude for the valuable contribution of the Indian government (whose representative has served as chairman since the commission's establishment).

The two chairmen unanimously agreed to distribute copies of the Indian chairman's letter of July 22 and this reply to the other participating countries of the Geneva Conference and the governments of Canada and the People's Republic of Poland.

		January 31, 1959
			A. Gromyko
			Soviet Foreign Minister
			Selwyn Lloyd
			British Foreign Secretary

### 日内瓦会议两位主席致函印度政府和老挝国际委员会  希望继续致力印度支那和平

1959-02-04
第6版()
专栏：

    日内瓦会议两位主席致函印度政府和老挝国际委员会
    希望继续致力印度支那和平
    新华社4日讯  1954年日内瓦会议的两位主席，苏联外交部长阿·葛罗米柯和英国外交大臣赛·劳埃德1月31日写信给印度政府和老挝国际监察和监督委员会，答复了关于老挝国际委员会今后继续活动的问题。这两封信的全文如下：
    关于印度支那的日内瓦会议两位主席给印度政府的信
    按照英国政府和苏联政府的授权，英国外交国务大臣阿·诺贝尔和苏联驻英国特命全权大使雅·阿·马立克代表关于印度支那的日内瓦会议两位主席，在伦敦进行了会晤并就同老挝国际监察委员会进一步活动有关的问题交换了意见。
    他们讨论了1958年5月3日和13日印度政府的备忘录，认为印度政府一贯关心日内瓦协议的履行，日内瓦协议是印度支那和平和安全的基础。
    在此期间，两位主席接到老挝国际监察委员会主席的通知，说委员会于1958年7月19日以多数票通过了决议，规定“老挝委员会应无限期地停止活动，但可根据正常的程序重新复会。”
    两位主席注意到印度政府的解释，此一决议并不影响委员会的法律规定，也不减少委员会在执行日内瓦协议所规定的任务和职责方面的权限。两位主席一致认为，委员会1958年7月19日的决议是关于无限期停止工作的程序性的决定，同解散委员会的问题没有任何关系。他们一致认为，没有用任何方式提到过关于废止老挝停止敌对行动协定中有关国际委员会的任何条款，特别是第三十九条。
    两位主席愿对印度政府（它的代表在印度支那的国际监察委员会中担任主席）对上述委员会的顺利工作所做出的宝贵贡献表示感谢，并且希望它今后也将积极促进委员会的活动，以利于印度支那的和平。
                1959年1月31日
                  苏联外交部长阿·葛罗米柯
                  英国外交大臣赛·劳埃德
    关于印度支那日内瓦会议两位主席给老挝国际监察委员会主席的信
    关于印度支那的日内瓦会议两位主席荣幸地确认，他们经由印度政府的协助收到了老挝国际监察委员会主席1958年7月22日的来信。两位主席指出：委员会于7月19日以多数票通过了决议：“老挝委员会应无限期地停止活动，但可根据正常的程序重新复会”。
    两位主席愿借此机会对老挝国际监察委员会在执行日内瓦会议上达成协议的协定方面所起的良好作用给予崇高的估价，特别是对印度政府（它的代表自委员会成立之日起即担任主席）的宝贵贡献表示感谢。
    两位主席一致同意将印度主席7月22日的来信及这封复信的副本分送给日内瓦会议的其他参加国以及加拿大和波兰人民共和国的政府。
                        1959年1月31日
                苏联外交部长阿·葛罗米柯
                  英国外交大臣赛·劳埃德
