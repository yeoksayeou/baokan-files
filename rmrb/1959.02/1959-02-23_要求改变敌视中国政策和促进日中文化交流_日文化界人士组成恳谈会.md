Title: Demanding a Change in the Hostile Policy Toward China and Promotion of Sino-Japanese Cultural Exchange: Japanese Cultural Figures Form Consultative Council
Author:
Date: 1959-02-23
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, Tokyo, February 21st: Approximately seventy prominent figures from the Japanese cultural world gathered in Tokyo today and established the "Sino-Japanese Cultural Relations Consultative Council" to promote a movement to break the deadlock in Sino-Japanese relations and advance cultural exchange between the two countries.

The meeting unanimously passed a statement calling on the Japanese people to compel the Nobusuke Kishi government to abandon its policy of non-recognition of China, and to change its policy of militarily opposing China by revising the U.S.-Japan "Security Treaty."

The initiators of the Consultative Council included Kenzo Nakajima, Kiyoshi Aono, Zenye Hotta, Koreya Senda, Yoko Matsuoka, Katsuiro Kamei, and Shigeharu Nakano. Currently, approximately one hundred and thirty people have joined the "Sino-Japanese Cultural Relations Consultative Council."

### 要求改变敌视中国政策和促进日中文化交流  日文化界人士组成恳谈会

1959-02-23
第5版()
专栏：

    要求改变敌视中国政策和促进日中文化交流
    日文化界人士组成恳谈会
    新华社21日讯　东京消息：日本文化界约七十位著名人士今天在东京集会，成立了“日中文化关系恳谈会”，以推进打开日中关系僵局，促进日中两国文化交流的运动。
    会议一致通过一项声明，呼吁日本人民迫使岸信介政府放弃不承认中国的政策，并且改变通过修改美日“安全条约”，从而在军事上同中国敌对的政策。
  恳谈会的发起人有中岛健藏、青野季吉、堀田善卫、千田是也、松冈洋子、龟井胜一郎和中野重治等。目前参加“日中文化关系恳谈会”的大约已经有一百三十人。
