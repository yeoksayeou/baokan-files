Title: Natural Soda
Author:
Date: 1959-02-20
Page: 2

    Natural soda is a crystalline substance naturally deposited in soda lakes or salt lakes. Soda lakes or salt lakes exist in many places in China, such as Qinghai, Xinjiang, Tibet, Inner Mongolia, and Ningxia. These lakes are affected by the harsh continental climate of severe cold and heat, coupled with sparse rainfall and high water evaporation. Under appropriate temperature conditions, they naturally deposit and crystallize into soda. Some crystallize several meters deep at the bottom of the lake, forming layered accumulations; others, on the dried-up lake surface, become scattered crystals or fine powder.
    Natural soda contains chlorides, sulfates, carbonates, and silt. If the precipitated crystals are mostly chlorides, then the lake is not a soda lake but a salt lake.

### 天然碱

1959-02-20
第2版()
专栏：

    天然碱
    天然碱是碱湖或盐湖中自然沉积出的结晶状物质。碱湖或盐湖在我国的青海、新疆、西藏、内蒙古、宁夏等很多地方都有。这些湖由于受大陆性气候严寒酷热的影响，再加上雨量稀少，水分蒸发很高，在适当温度条件下就自然沉积结晶成碱。有的结晶在湖底下几公尺深，成层积结；有的在干涸的湖表面，成为零散晶体或者是细粉。
    天然碱中含氯化物、硫酸盐、炭酸盐和泥砂等。如果析出的结晶大部为氯化物，这样的湖就不是碱湖而是盐湖了。
