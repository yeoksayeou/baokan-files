Title: U.S. Intelligence Agencies Commit Countless Evildoing Deeds; Harvard University Publishes Book Exposing the Truth
Author:
Date: 1959-02-12
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, New York, 10th. TASS reported on the 7th: Harvard University's "Harvard University Press" recently published a book by Ransome, an academic worker at the university. This book is specifically dedicated to researching "a vast, but little-known department within the U.S. government – the intelligence system."

The author points out that the Central Intelligence Agency (CIA), which is directly under the National Security Council, the President's military office, leads "a large-scale, powerful intelligence system." The CIA's secret personnel are equivalent to the number of State Department personnel. The CIA's annual budget is confidential, but in recent years, its budget has likely exceeded the State Department's budget by a factor of one. In addition, the U.S. government spends hundreds of millions of dollars each year on other government reconnaissance agencies, which are also under the CIA's leadership.

The author states, "Worldwide espionage and secret political conspiracies have become part of the U.S. plan for waging the Cold War. U.S. spies operate openly or under various 'covers' all over the world, collecting facts, or conducting secret political activities." He points out that the current U.S. intelligence system's work is characterized by: "war in peacetime."

The author says: "In addition to U.S. spies in foreign countries conducting dangerous activities openly or using various disguises, scientific achievements in electronics, photography, and various communication links have also begun to play a role in the reconnaissance process. For example, balloons sent by the United States over the Soviet Union carry specially made cameras for photographing Russian industrial targets..." He said: "The purpose of these provocative actions is to obtain reconnaissance materials regarding the characteristics of Russian military defenses, their tactics, transportation, and codes. This provocation is carried out on land, sea, and air."

The author points out, "From this, it can be seen that many incidents often reported in the American press, such as so-called 'unprovoked' attacks by Russia on Western forces, 'accidental' intrusions into Soviet territory, and so on, are deliberately incited for reconnaissance purposes."

### 美国情报机关作恶多端  哈佛大学出版一本书暴露真相

1959-02-12
第5版()
专栏：

    美国情报机关作恶多端
    哈佛大学出版一本书暴露真相
    新华社10日讯　据塔斯社纽约7日讯：美国哈佛大学“哈佛大学学报”出版局不久前出版了该校学术工作者兰索姆的一本书，这本书专门研究“美国政府工作中一个庞大的，但不为人们知道的部门——情报系统”。
    作者指出，直属总统的军事办公室——国家安全委员会的中央情报局，领导着“一个规模庞大的、强有力的情报系统”。中央情报局的秘密人员相等于国务院的人数。中央情报局的年度预算是保密的，最近几年来，它的预算大概超过国务院的预算一倍。此外，美国政府每年还要花费数亿美元在政府其他侦察机构上，这些机构也属中央情报局领导。
    作者说，“世界规模的间谍事件和秘密政治阴谋成了美国进行冷战的计划中的一部分。美国间谍公开地或在各种‘掩护’下在全世界各地进行活动，收集事实，或进行秘密政治活动”。他指出，美国当前情报系统的工作特点是：“和平时期的战争”。
    作者说：“除了美国在外国的间谍公开或利用各种伪装在外国进行危险活动以外，在电子学、摄影术和各种通讯联系方面的科学成就也开始在侦察过程中起作用。例如，美国放到苏联上空的气球中带有拍摄俄国工业目标的特制照相机……。”他说：“这些挑衅行动的目的是要获得关于俄国军事防御的特点、他们的策略、交通和密码方面的侦察材料。这种挑衅在陆、海、空都在进行”。
    作者指出，“由此可见，美国报刊上常常报道的所谓俄国‘无端’进攻西方力量，（西方）‘偶然’侵入苏联领土等等许多这样的事件，都是为了侦察目的而故意煽动的。”
