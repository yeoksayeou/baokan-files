Title: Cuban Economy Minister Issues Statement Accusing U.S. Trade Policies of Harming Cuba
Author:
Date: 1959-03-28
Page: 5

New York dispatch: The Havana-published "Hoy" newspaper published a statement by Economy Minister Bonilla. He pointed out that the trade relationship between Cuba and the United States harms the Cuban economy because this relationship is not built on a mutually beneficial foundation, but is one-sided.

Bonilla mentioned that U.S. congressmen often threaten to reduce the quota of Cuban sugar imports. He said: "If the U.S. Congress reduces the quota of Cuban sugar imports, this will inevitably lead to a shrinking of the market for U.S. products in Cuba, because if our existing foreign exchange reserves do not increase, it will be difficult for our export volume to maintain free trade."

The minister continued, "When the U.S. Congress passes corresponding laws to reduce imports from Cuba, Cuba cannot but adopt internal measures to deal with imports from the United States. Cuban trade is not currently conducted on a reciprocal basis, although trade agreements stipulate this." He believes that trade contracts that cause a considerable deficit for one party each year cannot be called mutually beneficial trade.

### 古巴经济部长发表声明  指责美国贸易政策损害古巴

1959-03-28
第5版()
专栏：

    古巴经济部长发表声明
    指责美国贸易政策损害古巴
    纽约讯：哈瓦那出版的“今日报”发表了经济部长博尼利亚的声明。他指出，古巴和美国的贸易关系损害着古巴经济，因为这种关系不是建筑在互利的基础上面，而是带有片面性的。
    博尼利亚谈到美国国会议员经常威胁说，美国要降低古巴糖的输入限额。他说：“如果美国国会降低古巴糖的输入限额，那么这将不可避免地导致美国产品在古巴的市场的缩小，因为我们现有的外汇储备如果不增加，我们的出口额就很难维持自由贸易。”
    这位部长接着说，“当美国国会通过相应法律来减少从古巴的进口时，古巴也不能不采用内部手段来对付来自美国的进口货。古巴贸易现在并不是在互惠基础上进行的，尽管贸易协定规定了这点。”他认为每年给一方造成相当大赤字的这种贸易合同，不能称为是互利的贸易。
