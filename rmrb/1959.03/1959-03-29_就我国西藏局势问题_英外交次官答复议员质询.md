Title: British Under-Secretary for Foreign Affairs Answers Parliamentary Questions on the Situation in Tibet, China
Author:
Date: 1959-03-29
Page: 3

Xinhua News Agency, London, 28th. British Under-Secretary for Foreign Affairs Robert Allan, answering questions in the House of Commons on the situation in Tibet on the 25th, said that in November 1950, the Labour government's Under-Secretary for Foreign Affairs, Ernest Davies, had stated: "We have for a long time recognized Chinese suzerainty over Tibet, but only on the understanding that Tibet is considered autonomous." Allan said, "This remains the position of Her Majesty's Government. The information we have received so far does not suggest that the disturbances in Tibet pose any threat to world peace. However, we will continue to carefully study any reliable information that may come from that region."

### 就我国西藏局势问题  英外交次官答复议员质询

1959-03-29
第3版()
专栏：

    就我国西藏局势问题
    英外交次官答复议员质询
    新华社28日讯　伦敦消息，英国外交次官罗伯特·阿兰25日在下院回答关于西藏局势的质询时说，1950年11月工党政府外交次官顾内斯特·戴维斯曾经声明：“我们很长时期以来一直承认中国对西藏的宗主权，但是只是根据这样的谅解，即：西藏被认为是自治的”。阿兰说，“这仍然是女皇政府的态度。我们目前收到的消息并不说明西藏的骚动对世界和平有了任何威胁。但是，我们将继续仔细研究从那个地区可能发出的任何可靠的消息”。
