    Extracting Metals from Seawater
    Seawater contains varying amounts of gold, copper, nickel, cobalt, and other chemical elements. In the future, the ocean will become an inexhaustible source for obtaining various metals.
    The Microelement Topic Laboratory of the Leningrad Technological Institute in the Soviet Union, under the leadership of Professor Alekskovsky, is conducting research on extracting chemical elements from seawater. A series of experiments were conducted by an expedition working on the Bering Sea. They used a specialized absorber manufactured by the laboratory to extract many of the aforementioned metals from the seawater.
    Many workers in the laboratory have also created special absorbers for extracting trace particles of precious metals from industrial wastewater.

### 从海水中提炼金属

1960-08-04
第6版()
专栏：

    从海水中提炼金属
    海水里含有不同数量的金、铜、镍、钴以及其他各种化学元素。将来，海洋将成为获得各种金属的取之不尽的源泉。
    苏联列宁格勒工艺研究所微量元素专题实验室，在阿列克斯科夫斯基教授领导下，正在进行从海水中提取化学元素的研究。在白令海上工作的考察队进行了一系列的实验。他们用该实验室制造的专门吸收器从海水里提出了上述金属中的许多种金属。
    实验室的许多工作者还制成了由工业废水中提取珍贵金属微粒的特殊吸收器。
