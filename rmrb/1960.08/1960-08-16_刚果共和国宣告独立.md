Title: Republic of the Congo Declares Independence
Author:
Date: 1960-08-16
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, Brazzaville, August 15th: The Republic of the Congo (formerly French Middle Congo), located in Equatorial Africa, formally declared its independence today.

At midnight this morning, President Fulbert Youlou of the Republic announced the birth of this new African nation at an independence ceremony held in the capital, Brazzaville, with a 101-gun salute.

On the afternoon of the 14th, French Minister of State André Malraux, representing the French government, exchanged documents with Youlou regarding the transfer of power by France. It is reported that the independent Republic of the Congo will remain within the adjusted "French Community."

The Republic of the Congo, led by Youlou, is the seventh African country to declare independence since August of this year. It covers an area of more than 340,000 square kilometers and has a population of more than 760,000. Since French colonialists invaded the country in 1880, the local people have been constantly fighting heroically against the colonialists for national independence.

### 刚果共和国宣告独立

1960-08-16
第6版()
专栏：

    刚果共和国宣告独立
    新华社15日讯　布拉柴维尔消息：位于赤道非洲的刚果共和国（前法属中央刚果）今天正式宣告独立。
    今晨零时，共和国总统富耳贝·尤卢在首都布拉柴维尔举行的独立仪式上宣布这个新的非洲国家诞生，并鸣放礼炮一百零一响。
    在14日下午，法国国务部长安德烈·马尔罗代表法国政府同尤卢交换了关于法国移交权力的文件。据报道，独立后的刚果共和国将留在调整后的“法兰西共同体”内。
    尤卢领导的刚果共和国是今年8月份以来第七个宣布独立的非洲国家，面积三十四万多平方公里，人口七十六万多人。自法国殖民者于1880年侵入这个国家以来，当地人民就不断同殖民者进行英勇斗争，争取祖国独立。
