International Briefs

    Eisenhower Appoints New Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff
    Washington dispatch: U.S. President Eisenhower appointed U.S. Army Chief of Staff Lemnitzer on the 15th as the Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff, succeeding Twining. Lemnitzer commanded the U.S. invading army's 7th Infantry Division during the Korean War.
    Malaysia-Japan Trade Agreement Comes into Effect
    Kuala Lumpur dispatch: The Malaysia-Japan trade agreement, which creates favorable conditions for the large-scale investment of Japanese monopoly capital in Malaya, came into effect on August 16. This agreement was signed on May 10 this year in Kuala Lumpur by representatives of the governments of the Federation of Malaya and Japan. The agreement stipulates that the contracting parties grant each other "most-favored-nation treatment" and stipulates that the Federation of Malaya abolish the restrictive provisions of the "General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade" concerning trade with Japan.

### 国际简讯

1960-08-22

专栏：国际简讯

    国际简讯
    艾森豪威尔任命新参谋长联席会议主席
    华盛顿讯　美国总统艾森豪威尔15日任命美国陆军参谋长兰尼兹尔继特文宁为美国参谋长联席会议主席。兰尼兹尔曾在朝鲜战争中指挥美国侵略军第七步兵师。
    马、日贸易协定生效
    吉隆坡讯　为日本垄断资本大量投入马来亚创造有利条件的马、日贸易协定已于8月16日开始生效。这个协定是今年5月10日由马来亚联合邦和日本两国政府的代表在吉隆坡签订的。协定规定缔约双方互相给予“最惠国待遇”，并规定马来亚联合邦取消“关税及贸易总协定”有关对日本贸易的限制条文。
