Title: Premier Zhou and Foreign Minister Chen Send Congratulatory Telegrams on the Independence of the Central African Republic; China Recognizes the Central African Republic
Author:
Date: 1960-08-13
Page: 1

    Xinhua News Agency, 12th: Premier Zhou Enlai of the People's Republic of China today sent a telegram to Mr. David Dacko, Prime Minister of the Central African Republic, congratulating the Central African Republic on its declaration of independence. The text of the telegram is as follows:
    Bangui, Central African Republic
    His Excellency Mr. David Dacko, Prime Minister of the Central African Republic:

    On the occasion of the declaration of independence of the Central African Republic, I would like to extend my warm congratulations to the government and people of the Central African Republic on behalf of the government and people of the People's Republic of China. I wish the people of the Central African Republic further achievements in their struggle to uphold national independence and development, and to oppose imperialism and colonialism. I wish your country prosperity and your people happiness.
            Zhou Enlai
            Premier of the State Council of the People's Republic of China
                        August 12, 1960, Beijing

    Xinhua News Agency, 12th: Chen Yi, Minister of Foreign Affairs of the People's Republic of China, today sent a telegram to Mr. David Dacko, Prime Minister of the Central African Republic, congratulating the Central African Republic on its declaration of independence and informing him that the Chinese government has decided to recognize the Central African Republic. The text of the telegram is as follows:
    Bangui, Central African Republic
    His Excellency Mr. David Dacko, Prime Minister of the Central African Republic:

    I am delighted to learn of the declaration of independence of the Central African Republic, and I would like to extend my sincere congratulations to the government of the Central African Republic on behalf of the government of the People's Republic of China. I have the honor to inform you that the government of the People's Republic of China has decided to recognize the Central African Republic. It is my hope that our government's recognition of your country will lead to the further development of relations between our two countries.
              Chen Yi
              Minister of Foreign Affairs of the People's Republic of China
                        August 12, 1960, Beijing

### 周总理陈外长电贺中非共和国独立  我国承认中非共和国

1960-08-13
第1版()
专栏：

    周总理陈外长电贺中非共和国独立
    我国承认中非共和国
    新华社12日讯　中华人民共和国总理周恩来今天打电报给中非共和国总理戴维·达科，祝贺中非共和国宣告独立。电文如下：中非共和国　班吉中非共和国总理戴维·达科先生阁下：
    值此中非共和国宣告独立之际，我谨代表中华人民共和国政府和人民向中非共和国政府和人民致以热烈的祝贺。祝中非共和国人民在坚持民族独立发展的道路、反对帝国主义和殖民主义的斗争中取得进一步的成就，祝贵国国家繁荣，人民幸福。
            中华人民共和国国务院总理　周恩来
                        1960年8月12日于北京
    新华社12日讯　中华人民共和国外交部长陈毅今天打电报给中非共和国总理戴维·达科，祝贺中非共和国宣告独立，并通知他中国政府决定承认中非共和国。电文如下：中非共和国　班吉中非共和国总理戴维·达科先生阁下：
    欣闻中非共和国宣告独立，我谨代表中华人民共和国政府向中非共和国政府致以诚挚的祝贺。我荣幸地通知阁下，中华人民共和国政府已经决定承认中非共和国。我希望，我国政府对贵国的承认将导致我们两国关系的进一步发展。
              中华人民共和国外交部长　陈毅
                        1960年8月12日于北京
