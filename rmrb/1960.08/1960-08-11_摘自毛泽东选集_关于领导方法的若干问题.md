Title: Some Questions Concerning Methods of Leadership
Author:
Date: 1960-08-11
Page: 4

Any work task, if it lacks a general and universal appeal, cannot mobilize the broad masses to take action. However, if we are limited to a general appeal, and leading personnel do not specifically and directly implement the appealed work deeply from several organizations, break through at one point, gain experience, and then use this experience to guide other units, we will not be able to test whether the general appeal we put forward is correct, nor will we be able to enrich the content of the general appeal, and there is a risk of the general appeal falling through.
        ——Excerpt from Mao Zedong’s Selected Works “Some Questions Concerning Methods of Leadership”

### 摘自毛泽东选集“关于领导方法的若干问题”

1960-08-11
第4版()
专栏：

    任何工作任务，如果没有一般的普遍的号召，就不能动员广大群众行动起来。但如果只限于一般号召，而领导人员没有具体地直接地从若干组织将所号召的工作深入实施，突破一点，取得经验，然后利用这种经验去指导其他单位，就无法考验自己提出的一般号召是否正确，也无法充实一般号召的内容，就有使一般号召归于落空的危险。
        ——摘自毛泽东选集“关于领导方法的若干问题”
