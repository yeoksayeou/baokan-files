Title: China-Afghanistan Joint Communiqué
Author:
Date: 1960-08-28
Page: 1

Xinhua News Agency, August 27th: China-Afghanistan Joint Communiqué

At the invitation of Sardar Mohammad Naim, Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Foreign Affairs of the Kingdom of Afghanistan. Chen yi, Vice Premier of The state council of the People's Republic of China and Minister of Foreign Affairs, paid a friendly visit to the kingdom of Afghanistan from August 21 to 27, 1961, and attended the celebrations of the forty-second anniversary of the independence of the kingdom of Afghanistan. Accompanying him were Kong Yuan, Deputy Director of the Foreign Affairs Office of the State Council, Geng Biao, Vice Minister of Foreign Affairs, Xiong Xianghui, leader of the group of the Foreign Affairs Office of the State Council, and other officials.

During his visit, Vice Premier Chen Yi was received by His Majesty King Mohammad Zahir of Afghanistan, and paid visits to Prime Minister Sardar Mohammad Daoud, Deputy Prime Minister Ali Mohammad, and Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Foreign Affairs Sardar Mohammad Naim.

Vice Premier Chen Yi, Prime Minister Sardar Mohammad Daoud, Deputy Prime Minister Ali Mohammad, and Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Foreign Affairs Sardar Mohammad Naim held sincere talks in a friendly and harmonious atmosphere on issues of common concern to both countries, as well as international issues. On the Chinese side, the participants in the talks included Kong Yuan, Deputy Director of the Foreign Affairs Office of the State Council, Geng Biao, Vice Minister of Foreign Affairs, Xiong Xianghui, leader of the group of the Foreign Affairs Office of the State Council, and Hao Ting, Chinese Ambassador to Afghanistan; on the Afghan side, the participants included Ghulam Mohammad Suleiman, Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs, Noor Ahmad Etemadi, Director of the Political Department of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, and Abdul Samad, Afghan Ambassador to China.

Both sides expressed satisfaction with the development of friendly relations between the two countries, and agreed that further strengthening economic and cultural relations and friendly cooperation between the two countries not only serves the fundamental interests of the two peoples, but also helps to consolidate peace in Asia and the world. After friendly consultations, the two sides signed the "Treaty of Friendship and Non-Aggression between the People's Republic of China and the Kingdom of Afghanistan". The signing of this treaty marks a new stage in the development of friendly relations between China and Afghanistan. At the same time, the two sides renewed the "Agreement on Exchange of Goods and Payments between the Government of the People's Republic of China and the Government of the Kingdom of Afghanistan".

Both sides reaffirmed their firm belief in the principles formulated and unanimously agreed upon at the Bandung Asian-African Conference; and agreed that continuing to follow these principles is of great significance for promoting the unity of Asian and African countries and maintaining peace in Asia and the world. Both sides believe that all disputes between Asian and African countries can be resolved fairly and reasonably in the spirit of friendly cooperation, mutual understanding, and mutual respect.

Both sides reaffirmed their support for the peoples and nations who have not yet achieved independence in their struggle for complete independence; and are convinced that the struggle of these peoples and nations against colonialism, for freedom, and for the right to determine their future in accordance with their freely expressed will is just and will eventually be victorious.

Both sides expressed the hope that the leaders of all countries in the world will dedicate themselves to eliminating international tensions and conducting peaceful consultations in order to maintain world peace. Both sides also expressed their hope and willingness to see achievements in the upcoming discussions on disarmament.

Both sides believe that the above-mentioned positions expressed by both sides in this meeting are fully in line with the policies of the two governments, the basic principles of the United Nations Charter, and the spirit of the Bandung Conference.

Vice Premier Chen Yi, entrusted by Liu Shaoqi, Chairman of the People's Republic of China, and on behalf of the Government of the People's Republic of China, invited His Majesty King Mohammad Zahir to pay a friendly visit to China at his convenience, which was gladly accepted.

    Done on August 26, 1960 in Kabul.
    Chen Yi
    (Signed)
    Vice Premier of the State Council and Minister of Foreign Affairs of the People's Republic of China

    Sardar Mohammad Naim
    (Signed)
    Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Foreign Affairs of the Kingdom of Afghanistan

### 中国阿富汗联合公报

1960-08-28
第1版()
专栏：

    中国阿富汗联合公报
    新华社27日讯　中华人民共和国和阿富汗王国联合公报
    中华人民共和国国务院副总理兼外交部长陈毅，应阿富汗王国副首相兼外交大臣萨达尔·穆罕默德·纳伊姆的邀请，在1960年8月21日至27日在阿富汗王国进行了友好访问，并且参加了阿富汗王国独立第四十二周年的庆典。陪同访问的还有国务院外事办公室副主任孔原、外交部副部长耿飚、国务院外事办公室组长熊向辉和其他官员。
    陈毅副总理在访问期间受到了阿富汗国王穆罕默德·查希尔陛下的接见，并且拜会了首相萨达尔·穆罕默德·达乌德、副首相阿里·穆罕默德和副首相兼外交大臣萨达尔·穆罕默德·纳伊姆。
    陈毅副总理同萨达尔·穆罕默德·达乌德首相、阿里·穆罕默德副首相和萨达尔·穆罕默德·纳伊姆副首相兼外交大臣在友好、和谐的气氛中就两国共同关心的各项问题以及国际问题进行了诚挚的会谈。中国方面参加会谈的还有国务院外事办公室副主任孔原、外交部副部长耿飚、国务院外事办公室组长熊向辉和中国驻阿富汗大使郝汀；阿富汗方面参加会谈的还有外交副大臣吴拉姆·穆罕默德·苏莱曼、外交部政治司司长努尔·艾哈迈德·埃特马迪和阿富汗驻中国大使阿卜杜勒·萨马德。
    双方对两国友好关系的发展表示满意，并且一致认为，继续增进两国间的经济文化关系和友好合作不仅符合两国人民的根本利益，并且有利于巩固亚洲和世界的和平。经过友好商谈，双方签订了“中华人民共和国和阿富汗王国友好和互不侵犯条约”。这项条约的签订，标志着中阿两国友好关系发展的一个新阶段。同时双方续订了“中华人民共和国政府和阿富汗王国政府交换货物和支付协定”。
    双方重申对万隆亚非会议所制定和一致同意的原则的坚定信念；并且一致认为，继续遵循这些原则对于促进亚非国家的团结和维护亚洲和世界的和平有着重大的意义。双方相信，亚非各国之间的一切争端，是完全有可能本着友好合作和互相谅解、互相尊重的精神求得公平合理的解决的。
    双方重申，支持那些尚未取得独立的人民和国家争取完全的独立；并且深信，这些人民和国家反对殖民主义、争取自由和按照他们自由表达的意愿决定他们的未来的权利的斗争是正义的，终将获得胜利。
    双方表示希望世界各国的领导人将致力于消除国际紧张局势，进行和平协商，以维护世界和平。双方并且表示希望和愿意看到即将举行的关于裁军问题的讨论获得成就。
    双方相信，在这次会谈中双方所表示的上述立场完全符合两国政府的政策、联合国宪章的基本原则和万隆会议的精神。
    陈毅副总理受中华人民共和国主席刘少奇的委托并且代表中华人民共和国政府邀请穆罕默德·查希尔国王陛下在对他方便的时候到中国进行友好访问，这一邀请已被高兴地接受。
       1960年8月26日于
    喀布尔中华人民共和国国务院副总理兼外交部长
              陈　毅
              （签字）
    阿富汗王国副首相兼外交大臣
    萨达尔·穆罕默德·纳伊姆
                （签字）
