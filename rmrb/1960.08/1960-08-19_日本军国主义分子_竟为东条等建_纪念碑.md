Title: Japanese Militarists Actually Build "Monument" for Tojo and Others
Author:
Date: 1960-08-19
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, Tokyo, 17th: Japanese militarists have brazenly erected a "monument" for Hideki Tojo and the other seven executed war criminals who invaded China and launched the Pacific War, and held an unveiling ceremony on the 16th.

This "monument" was built in Hazu County, Aichi Prefecture, in central Japan. Japanese militarists actually referred to Tojo Hideki and others as "martyrs," and the current Speaker of the House of Representatives, Ichiro Kiyose, wrote the words "Monument to the Seven Martyrs" on the monument.

According to Japan's Kyodo News Agency, the Japanese public strongly opposed the construction of this "monument."

### 日本军国主义分子  竟为东条等建“纪念碑”

1960-08-19
第5版()
专栏：

    日本军国主义分子
    竟为东条等建“纪念碑”
    新华社17日讯　东京消息：日本军国主义分子公然为侵略中国和发动太平洋战争的东条英机等七个被处决的大战犯建立了“纪念碑”，并在16日举行了揭幕式。
    这座“纪念碑”建立在日本中部爱知县的幡豆郡。日本军国主义分子们竟然把东条英机等称为“烈士”，并由现任众议院议长清濑一郎在这个碑上写了“七烈士碑”的字样。
    据日本共同社报道，日本公众曾经强烈反对建立这个“纪念碑”。
