Title: Our Descendants Will Forever Sing Your Names – A Visit to Volunteer Army Wounded and Sick Soldiers in a Hospital in the Capital
Author: Shi Guangxi
Date: 1952-01-30
Page: 4

    Our Descendants Will Forever Sing Your Names
    —A Visit to Volunteer Army Wounded and Sick Soldiers in a Hospital in the Capital
    Representative of the Korean People's Delegation to China, Shi Guangxi
The sun shone through the bright glass windows, and with激动心情, we entered the ward filled with the smell of medicine. At a glance, we saw the smiles on your faces. How could we not feel pain when we saw the wounds on your chests, arms, and legs? To express the gratitude of the 30 million Korean people to you, we came here from the fighting Korea. "Do you still have a fever?" I asked with concern, touching the head of a wounded soldier. "It's okay, it's nothing!" The soldier's eyes revealed an indomitable heroic spirit. "Although my body is lying in bed and cannot move freely, my heart is fighting the enemy in Korea..." said young wounded soldier Li Yi, holding my hand tightly. Remembering your resolute spirit on the front lines, the blood rushes through my young heart. When I return to my country, I will tell the people of my country everything about you. Words cannot express the deep affection in my heart, and my eyes were filled with tears. Comrade, I want to turn my tears into guns and knives to avenge you and eliminate the enemy! Under the great banner of internationalism – "Resist America, Aid Korea" – on the Korean battlefield, how many comrades bravely struggled and sacrificed themselves! You shed your blood on the nameless mountains of Korea, dedicated your beautiful youth, and established great feats. These will forever remain in the legends of the Korean people, and our descendants will forever sing your names. At this moment, when our feelings are completely融洽在一起, on the distant front lines – on the snow-covered highlands and in the trenches, our soldiers are fighting the frantically invading American bandits, singing songs of victory. Beloved name – Chinese People's Volunteer Army, our dear comrades-in-arms, your kindness will shine like the sun forever in the hearts of 30 million people.
    
    January 23, 1952, Night
    (Translated by Bing Wei)

### 我们的子孙将永远歌颂着你们的名字——慰问首都某医院志愿军伤病员
石光熙
1952-01-30
第4版()
专栏：

    我们的子孙将永远歌颂着你们的名字
    ——慰问首都某医院志愿军伤病员
    朝鲜人民访华代表团代表　石光熙太阳照进了明亮的玻璃窗，我们怀着激动的心情，走进充满药味的病房，一眼便看到脸上挂着笑容的你们。看见你们胸口、胳膊和腿上的伤，我们怎么会不感到疼痛？为把三千万朝鲜人民感激的心情献给你们，我们从战斗的朝鲜来到这里。“还发烧么？”抚摸着伤员的头亲切地问。“不要紧，没有什么！”伤员的眼睛里流露出不屈的英雄气概。——我的身体虽然躺在床上不能自由活动，但是我的心却在朝鲜与敌人搏斗……。年青的伤病员李毅同志，用力握紧我的手说。想起你们在火线上的坚决的精神，热血冲动着我年青的心，我要在回到祖国的时候，把你们的一切告诉给祖国的人民。言语也难以表达心底深处的亲热，我的眼眶被泪水润湿了，同志，我要把眼泪变成枪刀，替你报仇，消灭敌人！在“抗美援朝”——伟大的国际主义旗帜下，朝鲜的战场上，有多少同志英勇斗争牺牲了自己！你们把自己的鲜血流在朝鲜无名的山上，献出了自己美丽的青春，树立了伟大的功勋，这些，将会永远留在朝鲜人民的传说中，我们的子子孙孙将会永远歌颂着你们的名字。和你们的感情完全融洽在一起的现在，遥远的前线上——覆盖着白雪的高地上和战壕
    中，我们的战士正在打击着疯狂进犯的美国强盗，高唱着胜利的战歌战斗着。可爱的名字——中国人民志愿军亲密的战友，你们的恩情，在三千万人民的心中将会像太阳似地永远放射着光芒。
    一九五二·一·二三·夜
    　（冰蔚译）
