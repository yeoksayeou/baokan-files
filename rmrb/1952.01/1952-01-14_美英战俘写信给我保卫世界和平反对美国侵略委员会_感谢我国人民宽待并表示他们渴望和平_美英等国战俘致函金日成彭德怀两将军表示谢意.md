Title: American and British POWs Write to the China Peace Committee in Defense of World Peace and Against American Aggression, Thanking the Chinese People for Their Generous Treatment and Expressing Their Desire for Peace; American, British and Other POWs Send Letters to Generals Kim Il-sung and Peng Dehuai Expressing Their Gratitude
Author:
Date: 1952-01-14
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, January 12th: Seven American and British prisoners of war, representing the Peace Committee of the Third POW Camp, wrote to the Chinese People's Committee for World Peace and Against American Aggression, expressing their gratitude. The original letter is as follows:

    We, on behalf of the Peace Committee of the Third POW Camp in North Korea and the many POWs who support this committee, express our sincere gratitude for the work you have done for us. First of all, we thank you for doing your best to mail our letters, which has brought great comfort and joy to our loved ones at home. We know you must have encountered many difficulties, but we are confident that you have done your best to solve them and mail our letters quickly. This humanitarian work you have done is of great significance to us and our loved ones, and we will always be grateful to you.

    Today, the forces of peace are growing stronger, and we know that you have played and are playing a great role in the great struggle of people all over the world for peace. We prisoners of war would like to express to you and to the outside world that we also firmly stand on the side of peace. Only when all ordinary people unite and work for this noble cause can we hope to achieve lasting peace throughout the world.

    We would like to take this opportunity to wish you every success in the New Year on behalf of all the peace-loving prisoners of war in this camp. We hope to achieve peace and consolidate peace in 1952.

    Long live New China! Long live peace!

    Signed: Leonard Allen (UK)
    Edward Spencer (UK)
    John Green (UK)
    Earl Smith (US)
    Raymond McDonnell (US)
    Thomas Bess (US)
    Smith (UK)

Xinhua News Agency, Pyongyang, January 12th: American, British, and other POWs in POW camps in North Korea wrote letters on New Year's Eve 1951, thanking the Korean People's Army and the Chinese People's Volunteers for their humanitarian and generous treatment, and expressing their desire for peace. More than 70 American prisoners from F Company, 7th Regiment, 1st Marine Division, including Pvt. Baldorf, in a certain POW camp, wrote to General Kim Il-sung, Supreme Commander of the Korean People's Army, on New Year's Eve 1951, saying: "Today is the last day of 1951. As we look back, we see American imperialist planes destroying Korean villages and towns, and massacring defenseless Korean women and children. We feel very wrong about this. Now we know that it was wrong for us to come to Korea and fight the Korean people, and to destroy the property of the Korean people. We also realize that the Korean people are our friends, not our enemies. The Korean people gave us houses to live in and firewood to burn, for which we are very grateful. We hope that in 1952, all the Korean people will be able to rebuild their homes and industries, and live a life of lasting peace."

    In their letter to General Peng Dehuai, Commander of the Chinese People's Volunteers, they said: "We sincerely thank you and the Chinese People's Volunteers for the generous treatment you have given us. The volunteer soldiers under your command gave us good food and protected us from American bombing. They are very kind and patient to us. With their help, we received good medical treatment, food, clothing and shelter. Our gratitude to you and the Chinese People's Volunteers cannot be expressed in words."

### 美英战俘写信给我保卫世界和平反对美国侵略委员会   感谢我国人民宽待并表示他们渴望和平  美英等国战俘致函金日成彭德怀两将军表示谢意

1952-01-14
第4版()
专栏：

    美英战俘写信给我保卫世界和平反对美国侵略委员会
     感谢我国人民宽待并表示他们渴望和平
    美英等国战俘致函金日成彭德怀两将军表示谢意
    【新华社十二日讯】七个美英战俘，代表第三战俘营和平委员会写信给中国人民保卫世界和平反对美国侵略委员会，表示感谢。原信如下：
    我们代表朝鲜北部第三战俘营和平委员会以及支持这个委员会的许多战俘，对你们为我们做的工作表示诚挚的谢忱。首先我们感谢你们尽你们最大的努力把我们的信件付邮，使我们家里的亲人得到很大的安慰和快乐。我们知道你们一定碰到许多困难，但是我们确信你们已尽了最大的努力去解决困难，把我们的信件迅速邮去。你们所做的这种人道的工作，对我们以及我们家里的亲人都有很大的意义，我们永远感谢你们。
    今天，和平的力量日益壮大，我们知道你们在全世界人民争取和平的伟大斗争中，已经起了、而且正在起着伟大的作用。我们战俘们愿意向你们以及外界表示，我们也坚决站在和平这一边。只有一切普通人民团结一致为这种崇高的事业而努力，我们才能希望在全世界得到持久的和平。
    我们愿意利用这个机会代表本战俘营所有渴望和平的战俘，祝你们在新的一年中一切成功。我们希望能够在一九五二年中取得和平并巩固和平。
    新中国万岁！和平万岁！
    签字人：伦纳德·阿伦（英）
    爱德华·斯潘塞（英）
    约翰·葛林（英）
    额尔·斯密斯（美）
    雷蒙德·麦克唐奈尔（美）
    汤马斯·贝斯（美）
    斯密斯（英）
    【新华社平壤十二日电】在朝鲜北部战俘营中的美英等国战俘，在一九五一年除夕时，写信感谢朝鲜人民军和中国人民志愿军给予他们以人道主义的宽待，和表示他们对和平的愿望。某战俘营里的美俘海军陆战队第一师第七团F连士兵拔多夫等七十多人，在一九五一年除夕写给朝鲜人民军最高司令官金日成将军的感谢信上说：“今天是一九五一年的最后一天，当我们回顾过去，我们看到美帝国主义飞机毁坏朝鲜村庄和城镇，屠杀手无寸铁的朝鲜妇女和儿童，我们感到非常不应该。现在，我们已知道我们来到朝鲜和朝鲜人民打仗，毁灭朝鲜人民的财产是错误的。我们也认识到朝鲜人民是我们的朋友，不是我们的敌人。朝鲜人民让房子给我们住，给我们柴烧，使我们非常感谢。我们希望在一九五二年，全体朝鲜人民将得以重建他们家园和工业，过持久和平的生活。”
    他们在写给中国人民志愿军司令员彭德怀将军的信上说：“我们衷心感谢你和中国人民志愿军所给予我们的宽大待遇。在你指挥下的志愿军战士，给予我们很好的食物，保护我们不受美国飞机的轰炸。他们对我们非常和善，而且耐心。在他们的帮助下，我们得到很好的医药治疗、食物、衣服和住处。我们对你和中国人民志愿军的感谢不是言词所能形容的。”
