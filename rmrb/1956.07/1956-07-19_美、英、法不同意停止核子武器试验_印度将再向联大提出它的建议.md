Title: U.S., Britain, and France Disagree on Halting Nuclear Weapons Tests; India Will Re-Submit Its Proposal to the UN General Assembly
Author:
Date: 1956-07-19
Page: 6
Xinhua News Agency, July 18th, New York: Indian representative to the United Nations, Menon, left New York on July 17th.

Menon came to New York to present India's proposal for banning nuclear weapons testing to the United Nations Disarmament Commission. He delivered a speech lasting one hour and twenty minutes at the meeting, but the representatives of the United States, Britain, and France stated that they could not agree to Menon's proposal.

Reports indicate that Menon's proposal has been referred to a subcommittee, which will convene this autumn but is not expected to discuss his proposal. Menon intends to re-submit this appeal at the United Nations General Assembly convening on November 12th.

After leaving New York, Menon will return to his country via London and Paris. The Associated Press reports that he may hold talks with British Foreign Secretary Lloyd and French Foreign Minister Pineau.

### 美、英、法不同意停止核子武器试验  印度将再向联大提出它的建议

1956-07-19
第6版()
专栏：

    美、英、法不同意停止核子武器试验
    印度将再向联大提出它的建议
    新华社18日讯　纽约消息：印度驻联合国代表梅农在七月十七日离开纽约。
    梅农是为了向联合国裁军委员会会议陈述印度关于禁止试验核子武器的主张而来纽约的。他在会议上发表了一小时又二十分钟的演说，但是美、英、法代表说他们不能够同意梅农的主张。
    消息说，梅农的建议被推给小组委员会去处理，这个单位将在今年秋季举行会议，但估计不会讨论他的建议。梅农打算在十一月十二日召开的联合国大会上重新提出这个呼吁。
    梅农离纽约后取道伦敦、巴黎回国。美联社说，他可能同英国外交大臣劳埃德和法国外交部长比诺举行会谈。
