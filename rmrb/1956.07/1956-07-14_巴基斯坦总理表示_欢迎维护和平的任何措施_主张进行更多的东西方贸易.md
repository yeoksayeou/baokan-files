Title: Pakistani Prime Minister Expresses Welcome for Any Measures to Maintain Peace; Advocates for More East-West Trade
Author:
Date: 1956-07-14
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, July 13th, Paris dispatch: Pakistani Prime Minister Ali held a press conference in Paris on July 12th. When discussing the issue of East-West trade, he said: "We Pakistanis welcome any measures that ease tensions, bring people closer, eliminate the danger of war, and maintain peace and stability. We should contribute all our strength to all these aspects."

Ali said: "For example, there should be more East-West trade. We recently concluded a trade agreement with the Soviet Union… I don’t think there will be any danger in trade relations."

He confirmed that he will visit Beijing later this year. When asked by reporters whether he favored China's entry into the United Nations, he replied: "We have recognized China, and the inevitable result is that we favor the admission of Communist China to the United Nations."

Ali spoke in detail about the Algerian issue. He believes an immediate ceasefire is "imperative." He said, "The bloodshed in Algeria must not continue. Our opinion is to give the Algerian people the right to self-determination, but as I have said, the current issue is to achieve a ceasefire. This can be arranged through consultations between the French government and Algerian leaders."

When a reporter asked him to comment on US Vice President Nixon's statement that accepting aid from Eastern European countries is like putting a rope around one's own neck, Ali said that he was not talking about economic aid but trade. He said that this issue has not arisen for Pakistan. He advocated for more trade, saying it is "a good thing."

The Pakistani Prime Minister still believes that the Manila Treaty Organization and the Baghdad Pact currently "help achieve the purposes of peace and stability." But at the same time, he said: "As the world situation changes, policies will also change." Ali said: "I would like to emphasize that Pakistan will never participate in any aggressive actions."

### 巴基斯坦总理表示  欢迎维护和平的任何措施  主张进行更多的东西方贸易

1956-07-14
第6版()
专栏：

    巴基斯坦总理表示
    欢迎维护和平的任何措施
    主张进行更多的东西方贸易
    新华社13日讯　据法新社巴黎消息：巴基斯坦总理阿里七月十二日在巴黎举行记者招待会。他在谈到东西方贸易的问题时说：“我们巴基斯坦人欢迎缓和紧张局势、使人民接近、消除战争的危险和维护和平和稳定的任何措施。我们应该在所有这些方面贡献我们的一切力量。”
    阿里说：“譬如，应该进行更多的东西方贸易，我们最近和苏联缔结了贸易协定……我并不认为在贸易关系方面会有任何危险。”
    他证实他将在今年晚些时候访问北京。记者问，他是否赞成中国进入联合国，他回答说：“我们已经承认了中国，其必然的结果是我们赞成接纳共产党中国加入联合国。”
    阿里详尽地谈论了阿尔及利亚问题。他认为立即停火是“迫不及待的”，他说，“阿尔及利亚的流血决不能再继续下去。我们的意见是要给阿尔及利亚人民以自决权，但是像我已经说过的，当前的问题就是要实现停火。这可以通过法国政府同阿尔及利亚领袖进行的协商来安排。”
    有记者要求他评论一下美国副总统尼克松所说的从东欧国家接受援助就是把绳子套在自己脖子上的话，阿里说，他并没有谈论经济援助而是贸易。他说，对巴基斯坦说来并没有产生这种问题。他主张进行更多的贸易，说这是“一件好事”。
    这位巴基斯坦总理仍然认为马尼拉条约组织和巴格达条约在目前“有助于实现和平和稳定的目的”。但是他同时说：“随着世界形势的改变，政策也会改变。”阿里说：“我愿意着重地说，巴基斯坦绝不会参加任何侵略性的行动”。
