Title: Yugoslav, Egyptian Presidents and Indian Prime Minister Meet; Tito and Nasser Issue Communiqué Reaffirming Policy of Active Coexistence
Author:
Date: 1956-07-20
Page: 6
Xinhua News Agency, July 19th – According to a report by the Yugoslav News Agency: Egyptian President Gamal Abdel Nasser, Yugoslav President Josip Broz Tito, and Indian Prime Minister Jawaharlal Nehru began their talks on the island of Vanga, near Brioni, on the afternoon of July 18th.

The Yugoslav side also included Aleksandar Ranković and Edvard Kardelj, Vice-Presidents of the Federal Executive Council, and Koča Popović, Secretary of State for Foreign Affairs; the Egyptian side included Abdel Latif Boghdadi, Minister of Municipal and Rural Affairs and Minister of State in charge of economic development plans, and Mahmoud Fawzi, Minister of Foreign Affairs; the Indian side included N.R. Pillai, Secretary-General of the Ministry of External Affairs, and Rajeshwar Dayal, Indian Ambassador to Yugoslavia.

The representatives of the three countries exchanged views on current international issues during the meeting.

Xinhua News Agency, July 19th – According to a report by the Yugoslav News Agency, a communiqué issued on Brioni on the evening of July 18th regarding the visit of Egyptian President Gamal Abdel Nasser to Yugoslavia stated that the two statesmen reached a complete agreement in their talks on further promoting cooperation between Egypt and Yugoslavia. The two sides share many views on the major problems of the contemporary world, and the policies of both countries towards these problems are guided by the principles of peace-loving, active coexistence, and non-alignment.

The communiqué said that President Abdel Nasser and President Tito unanimously estimated that the process of positive development towards relaxation in the international situation is continuing, which is due to the universal intensification of efforts by all countries to develop international cooperation and strengthen trust among the peoples of all countries, and as a result of the increasingly successful application of consultative methods in resolving disputed issues.

The two presidents discussed the situation in the Near and Middle East, and they reiterated their support for the resolutions of the Bandung Conference concerning these regions.

They noted with satisfaction that relations between their two countries are developing smoothly along the principles put forward in the joint declaration of January 5, 1956.

The two presidents believe that direct exchange of views and personal contact at the highest level have proven to be of great benefit to each other. Therefore, they have decided to continue to take this beneficial measure and to conduct regular consultations with each other in the future.

The communiqué said that the talks proved that there are broad opportunities for expanding economic cooperation and exchanges between them. To achieve this goal, it has been decided to begin more concrete negotiations between experts and relevant institutions of the two countries in the coming months.

In addition, the two presidents also decided to continue contacts in the form of exchanging experiences and information on economic policies, plans, etc. To achieve this goal, visits by relevant personnel will be organized from time to time.

The two presidents have decided to take measures to promote and expand existing cultural cooperation. The two countries will conclude a cultural agreement in the near future.

The two presidents paid particular attention to the issue of mutual cooperation and assistance in the technical field and in the peaceful uses of atomic energy. To this end, appropriate measures will be taken.

Xinhua News Agency, July 18th – According to a report by the Yugoslav News Agency, Egyptian President Gamal Abdel Nasser arrived on Brioni Island on the evening of July 17th.

The Egyptian President was welcomed on Brioni by Yugoslav President Josip Broz Tito, Vice-Presidents of the Federal Executive Council Aleksandar Ranković and Edvard Kardelj, and Secretary of State for Foreign Affairs Koča Popović.

Xinhua News Agency, Prague, July 19th – Belgrade dispatch: Indian Prime Minister Jawaharlal Nehru arrived on Brioni Island in Yugoslavia at noon on July 18th. He arrived at the port of Pula, near Brioni, by plane from Paris.

At the Brioni pier, the Indian Prime Minister was welcomed by Yugoslav President Josip Broz Tito and other Yugoslav leaders.

### 南斯拉夫、埃及总统和印度总理会谈  铁托和纳赛尔会谈公报重申积极共处的政策

1956-07-20
第6版()
专栏：

    南斯拉夫、埃及总统和印度总理会谈
    铁托和纳赛尔会谈公报重申积极共处的政策
    新华社19日讯　据南斯拉夫通讯社报道：埃及总统阿卜杜勒·纳赛尔、南斯拉夫总统铁托和印度总理尼赫鲁，七月十八日下午在布里俄尼岛附近的万加岛上开始举行会谈。
    参加这次会谈的南斯拉夫方面还有联邦执行委员会副主席卡德尔和兰科维奇、外交国务秘书长波波维奇；埃及方面还有城乡事务部长兼负责经济发展计划的国务部长阿卜杜勒·拉蒂夫·波格达迪、外交部长马茂德·法齐；印度方面还有外交部秘书长纳·拉·皮莱和印度驻南斯拉夫大使拉杰希瓦尔·达雅尔。
    三国代表们在会谈中就目前国际问题发表了意见。
    新华社19日讯　据南斯拉夫通讯社报道，七月十八日晚上在布里俄尼发表的关于埃及总统阿卜杜勒·纳赛尔访问南斯拉夫的公报说，两国政治家在会谈中就进一步促进埃及和南斯拉夫合作问题达成了完全的协议。双方对于当代世界的主要问题的看法有很多相同之处，两国对待这些问题的政策都是以爱好和平、积极共处和不参加集团的原则为指针的。
    公报说，阿卜杜勒·纳赛尔总统和铁托总统一致地估计：国际局势向着缓和方面积极发展的过程正在继续进行，这是由于各国普遍加紧努力来发展国际合作和加强各国人民之间的信任，由于在解决争执的问题时越来越成功地应用协商方法的结果。
    两位总统讨论了中近东的局势，他们重申他们支持万隆会议有关这些地区的决议。
    他们满意地指出，他们两国之间的关系正在循着1956年1月5日的联合宣言中所提出的原则顺利地发展。
    两位总统认为，直接交换意见和最高级的个人接触证明对彼此是有很大的益处的。因此，他们已经决定继续采取这种有益的措施，在将来定期地互相进行磋商。
    公报说，这次会谈证明，存在着扩大彼此之间的经济合作和交流的广泛机会。为了达到这个目的，已经决定在今后几个月中，在两国的专家和有关机构之间开始进行比较具体的谈判。
  另外，两位总统还决定要采取交流有关经济政策、计划等方面的经验和情报的形式来继续进行接触。为了达到这样的目的，将不时组织有关人员的访问。
    两位总统已经决定采取措施来促进和扩大现有的文化合作。两国将在最近的将来缔结一个文化协定。
    两位总统特别考虑了在技术方面和在把原子能用于和平目的方面彼此进行合作和援助的问题。为了这个目的，将采取相应的措施。
    据新华社18日讯　据南斯拉夫通讯社报道，埃及总统阿卜杜勒·纳赛尔在七月十七日晚间到达布里俄尼岛。
    埃及总统在布里俄尼受到了南斯拉夫总统铁托，联邦执行委员会副主席卡德尔、兰科维奇和外交国务秘书长波波维奇的欢迎。
    据新华社布拉格19日电　贝尔格莱德消息：印度共和国总理尼赫鲁七月十八日中午到达南斯拉夫的布里俄尼岛。他是从巴黎乘飞机到达布里俄尼附近的普拉港的。
    在布里俄尼码头上，印度总理受到南斯拉夫总统铁托和南斯拉夫其他领导人的欢迎。
