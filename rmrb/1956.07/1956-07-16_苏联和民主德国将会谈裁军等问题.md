Title: Soviet Union and East Germany to Discuss Disarmament and Other Issues
Author:
Date: 1956-07-16
Page: 5
Xinhua News Agency, Berlin, July 15th: According to an announcement from the Press Office of the Prime Minister of the German Democratic Republic, a government delegation from the German Democratic Republic will depart from Berlin for Moscow on July 16th for talks. The delegation will be led by Prime Minister Grotewohl, and will include First Deputy Prime Minister Ulbricht, Deputy Prime Ministers Stoph, Selbmann, Nuschke, Loch, Scholz, Bolz, and the Ambassador of the German Democratic Republic to the Soviet Union, Koenig.

The talks scheduled to take place in Moscow will discuss the issues of disarmament, the establishment of European security, and contributions to be made towards achieving the unification of a peace-loving, independent, and democratic Germany. In addition, they will discuss matters that will help achieve the goals of the German Democratic Republic's second Five-Year Plan, particularly in further improving the people's living standards and implementing the social welfare measures stipulated in the second Five-Year Plan.

### 苏联和民主德国将会谈裁军等问题

1956-07-16
第5版()
专栏：

    苏联和民主德国将会谈裁军等问题
    据新华社柏林15日电　据德意志民主共和国总理新闻处宣布，德意志民主共和国政府代表团将在七月十六日从柏林启程去莫斯科举行会谈。代表团将由总理格罗提渥率领，团员包括第一副总理乌布利希，副总理斯多夫、赛尔布曼、努舍克、洛赫、绍尔茨、博尔茨和德意志民主共和国驻苏大使柯尼希。
    定在莫斯科举行的会谈将讨论裁军问题，建立欧洲安全和为达成一个爱好和平的、独立的和民主的德国的统一将作出的贡献的问题。此外还要讨论有助于达到民主德国的第二个五年计划的目标、特别是在进一步提高人民的生活水平和实行第二个五年计划中规定的社会福利措施方面的目标等问题。
