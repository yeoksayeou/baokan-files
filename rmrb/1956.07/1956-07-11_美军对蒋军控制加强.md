Title: U.S. Military Tightens Control Over Chiang's Troops
Author: 
Date: 1956-07-11
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, 9th. A Taipei dispatch published by the Kuomintang newspaper "Hong Kong Times" on July 1st stated that the number of U.S. military assistance advisory group personnel stationed in Taiwan has "increased significantly" in the past year. These American soldiers have penetrated into the "grassroots combat units" of Chiang's army. Smith, the head of the U.S. military assistance advisory group stationed in Taiwan, also frequently inspects Chiang's army bases and training centers, urging his subordinates to train Chiang's army diligently. In the past year, Smith spent at least four months inspecting Kinmen, Matsu, and various military bases in Taiwan.

The report also stated that the United States has handed over a considerable amount of weapons to Chiang's army in the past year. American soldiers are responsible for training Chiang Kai-shek's troops in the use of these weapons.

### 美军对蒋军控制加强

1956-07-11
第4版()
专栏：

    美军对蒋军控制加强
    新华社9日讯　国民党报纸“香港时报”七月一日发表台北专电说，美国驻台湾军事援助顾问团人员在过去一年中“数量大增”，这些美国军人已经深入到蒋军“基层的战斗单位”。美国驻台湾军事援助顾问团团长史密斯也经常到蒋军各基地和训练中心视察，督促其部下认真训练蒋军。在过去一年中，史密斯最少有四个月的时间花在视察金门、马祖和台湾各军事基地上面。
    消息还说，一年来，美国又把数量相当庞大的武器交给蒋军。美国军人负责训练蒋介石军队使用这些武器。
