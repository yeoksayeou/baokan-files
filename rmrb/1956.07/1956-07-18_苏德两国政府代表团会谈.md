Title: Soviet-German Government Delegations Hold Talks
Author:
Date: 1956-07-18
Page: 5
Xinhua News Agency, Moscow, July 17th: The Soviet government delegation and the German Democratic Republic government delegation began talks on July 16th in the Kremlin.

    During the talks, the two sides extensively exchanged views on issues of further expanding political and economic cooperation between the two countries, as well as certain international issues of mutual concern.

    At the beginning of the talks, the Prime Minister of the German Democratic Republic, Grotewohl, delivered a speech. He outlined the international situation, explained the political and economic situation of the German Democratic Republic, and the issue of restoring German unity on the principles of peace and democracy.

    Subsequently, Bulganin and Khrushchev spoke. They expressed their conviction that the talks between the government delegations of the two countries would surely promote the further consolidation of the fraternal relations between the two countries.

    During the talks, Deputy Prime Ministers of the German Democratic Republic, Nuschke, Loch, Selbmann, Scholz, and Ulbricht also spoke.

    The talks will continue on the 17th.

### 苏德两国政府代表团会谈

1956-07-18
第5版()
专栏：

    苏德两国政府代表团会谈
    新华社17日讯　据塔斯社莫斯科讯：苏联政府代表团和德意志民主共和国政府代表团七月十六日在克里姆林宫开始会谈。
    在会谈中，双方就进一步扩大两国的政治和经济合作问题以及双方关心的某些国际问题广泛地交换了意见。
    在会谈开始的时候，德意志民主共和国总理格罗提渥讲了话。他概述了国际局势，说明了德意志民主共和国的政治、经济情况以及在和平、民主的原则上恢复德国统一的问题。
    随后，布尔加宁和赫鲁晓夫讲了话。他们确信，两国政府代表团的会谈必将促进两国兄弟关系的进一步巩固。
    会谈的时候，德意志民主共和国副总理努舍克、洛赫、赛尔布曼、绍尔茨和乌布利希也讲了话。
    会谈将在十七日继续进行。
