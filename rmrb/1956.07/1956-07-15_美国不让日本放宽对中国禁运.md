Title: The United States Does Not Allow Japan to Relax the Embargo Against China
Author:
Date: 1956-07-15
Page: 6

    Xinhua News Agency, Washington, July 14th: According to Kyodo News Agency, Washington, U.S. government authorities issued a statement on the 12th, expressing their rejection of Japan's request to relax the embargo restrictions against China.
    U.S. authorities said that Japan and the United States had negotiated in Washington two months ago on the issue of relaxing the embargo restrictions against China. The United States is also considering the opinions of Japanese manufacturers requesting permission to expand trade with China. It also said that the United States does not believe that Japan will abandon its close cooperation with the Coordinating Committee for Multilateral Export Controls (COCOM) in the past.
    Regarding this statement by the U.S. government authorities, authoritative Japanese sources said that the U.S. attitude remains tough. In the short term, the United States will not change its attitude.

### 美国不让日本放宽对中国禁运

1956-07-15
第6版()
专栏：

    美国不让日本放宽对中国禁运
    新华社14日讯　据共同社华盛顿消息，美国政府当局在十二日发表谈话，表示拒绝日本放宽对中国禁运限制的要求。
    美国当局说，在两个月以前，日本和美国就在华盛顿谈判放宽对中国的禁运限制问题。美国也在考虑日本厂商要求准许扩大对中国贸易的意见。它又说，但是，美国不认为日本会毁弃过去它同巴黎统筹委员会的密切合作。
    对于美国政府当局的这个谈话，日本方面权威人士说，美国的态度依然是强硬的。在短期内，美国不会改变态度。
