Title: Nixon Publicly Admits the United States Faces a Dilemma in the Face of Soviet Peaceful Initiatives
Author:
Date: 1956-07-03
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, New York, July 2nd -- U.S. Vice President Nixon said at a gathering in Miami on June 30 that the United States faces a "painful dilemma" in dealing with the Soviet Union's "peaceful actions."

Nixon said, "If we reject these actions, many countries will see us as warmongers; but if we accept them, we may weaken our alliance."

Nixon said the United States should accept the "challenge of sincere exchanges of views" with the Communists, and the United States cannot ignore "any opportunity to increase personal contacts between countries behind the Iron Curtain."

The United States, in doing so, "should not have the idea that everything has changed."

The Vice President also said that if Americans take advantage of the opportunity that the Soviet Union allows more Westerners to visit the Soviet Union, Americans "can plant ideas of freedom and justice."

### 尼克松公开承认  美国在苏联和平行动面前进退两难

1956-07-03
第6版()
专栏：

    尼克松公开承认
    美国在苏联和平行动面前进退两难
    新华社2日讯　纽约消息：美国副总统尼克松六月三十日在迈阿密城的一次集会上说，美国在对待苏联的“和平行动”上面临着“痛苦的进退两难局面”。
    尼克松说：“如果拒绝这些行动，许多国家就会把我们看成是战争贩子；而如果加以接受，我们就可能削弱我们的联盟。”
    尼克松说，美国应当接受同共产党人进行“诚恳的意见交换的挑战”，美国不能忽视“增加同铁幕以后的国家间的个人来往的任何机会”。
    美国在这样做的时候“不应当抱有认为一切都改变了的想法”。
    这位副总统还表示，如果美国人利用苏联允许较多的西方人到苏联去的机会，美国人“就可以种植自由和正义的思想。”
