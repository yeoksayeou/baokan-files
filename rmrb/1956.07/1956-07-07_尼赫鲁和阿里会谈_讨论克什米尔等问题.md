Title: Nehru and Ali Meet to Discuss Kashmir and Other Issues
Author:
Date: 1956-07-07
Page: 6

    Xinhua News Agency, 6th: London dispatch: The prime ministers of the Commonwealth countries held a plenary meeting on the afternoon of July 5 to discuss drafting the final communiqué of the meeting. The meeting is scheduled to close on July 6.
    On the morning of July 5, Indian Prime Minister Nehru and Pakistani Prime Minister Ali held talks to discuss the Kashmir issue and other issues related to India and Pakistan. According to Agence France-Presse, when Nehru was asked after the talks whether the meeting had found a solution to the Kashmir issue, Nehru replied, "Any friendly contact is a good thing," but "it is not easy to solve difficult problems quickly."
    Pakistani Prime Minister Ali told a United Press reporter that the talks were "inconclusive." He added: "No further talks are scheduled. I cannot say that I am disappointed, because I never had any real hope for this meeting."

### 尼赫鲁和阿里会谈  讨论克什米尔等问题

1956-07-07
第6版()
专栏：

    尼赫鲁和阿里会谈
    讨论克什米尔等问题
    新华社6日讯　伦敦消息：英联邦国家的总理们在七月五日下午举行了全体会议，讨论起草会议最后公报事宜。会议预定在七月六日闭幕。
    七月五日上午，印度总理尼赫鲁和巴基斯坦总理阿里举行了会谈，讨论克什米尔问题以及同印度和巴基斯坦有关的其他问题。据法新社消息说，会谈后有人问尼赫鲁，这次会谈是不是已经找出了解决克什米尔问题的办法。尼赫鲁回答说：“任何友好接触都是好事情”，但是“要很快解决困难问题并不容易”。
    巴基斯坦总理阿里对合众社记者说，会谈“没有结果”。他又说：“没有安排进一步的会谈。我不能说我感到失望，因为我从来没有对这次会谈抱有任何真正的希望。”
