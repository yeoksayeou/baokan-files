Title: Soviet Scientist Khlebnikov Says Humans Can Uncover the Secrets of Mars
Author: 
Date: 1956-07-30
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, Moscow, July 29th. Soviet Deputy Doctor of Technical Sciences Khlebnikov recently published an article in "Literary Gazette" stating that modern science and technology allows us to anticipate that by 1971 – when Mars and Earth are once again at their closest distance – humans will be able to unlock the secrets of Mars.

He said that whether there is life on Mars is a question of the greatest interest to people. The materials scientists have accumulated through observations over many centuries still cannot provide a definitive conclusion to this question.

At first glance, the only way to uncover the secrets of Mars seems to be to send interstellar astronauts in rocket ships to Mars. However, exploring Mars is not an easy task. According to scientists' calculations, a round trip would take five hundred and twenty days. The departure and return times are not arbitrarily chosen by the explorers, because the positions of Earth and Mars are not fixed; they move along their orbits at tremendous speeds. Therefore, the launch time, flight path, and flight duration of the rocket ship must be precisely calculated in conjunction with the relative positions of Earth and Mars so that when the spacecraft enters the Martian orbit, Mars is exactly where we need it to be. To return to Earth from Mars, the appropriate relative positions of Earth and Mars must also be selected, and this only occurs once every four hundred and fifty days.

If there is even a slight error in the calculation of time and route, the rocket ship will never land on Earth or Mars, but will be permanently lost in the universe.

Since sending people to Mars for the first few explorations would involve many difficulties, it is best to first launch radio-controlled automatic rockets from Earth to study Mars with specialized instruments.

This plan for exploration using automatic rockets can be implemented in three stages. The purpose of the first two stages is to determine whether there is life on Mars. The rocket does not need to land on the surface of Mars; instead, it can photograph Mars from above using electronic telescopes and then transmit the images back to Earth via radio. The third stage is to send a small tank-shaped mobile laboratory controlled by radio to land on Mars in order to directly examine the surface of Mars.

Khlebnikov said that after conducting such explorations and carefully synthesizing the results, it will be possible to send people on rocket ships to explore Mars.

### 苏联科学家赫列勃切维奇说  人类能揭开火星的秘密

1956-07-30
第5版()
专栏：

    苏联科学家赫列勃切维奇说
    人类能揭开火星的秘密
    新华社莫斯科29日电　苏联技术科学副博士赫列勃切维奇最近在“文学报”上发表文章说，现代的科学技术使我们可以预料，到1971年——即火星和地球再一次相距最近的时候，人类将可以揭开火星的秘密。
    他说，火星上是否有生物是一个使人最感兴趣的问题。许多世纪以来科学家在观察中所掌握的材料到现在还不能对这个问题作出最后的结论。
    乍一看来，揭开火星秘密的唯一办法似乎就是派星际飞行家乘火箭船到火星那里去。但是，到火星上去考察不是一件容易的事情。据科学家的统计，作一个往返飞行需要五百二十天的时间。出发和返回的时间不是考察家们可以任意选择的，因为地球和火星的位置不是固定不变的，它们按着自己的轨道以极大的速度运行着。因此，有关火箭飞船的起飞时间、飞行路线和飞行时间，都必须结合地球和火星的相互位置来加以精密的计算，以便在飞船跨上火星轨道的时候，火星恰好处在我们所需要的地方。而要从火星回到地球，也要选择好地球和火星相互之间所处的恰当的位置，而这每隔四百五十天才有一次。
    如果在时间和路线的计算上稍有差错，火箭飞船就永远不会落到地球上，也不会落到火星上，而将永久迷失在宇宙之间。
    既然派人到火星去进行头几次考察会有很多的困难，那么，最好先从地球上放射出用无线电操纵的自动火箭，用专门的仪器对火星进行研究。
    这种利用自动火箭考察的计划可以分三个阶段来实现。头两个阶段的目的是弄清楚火星上有没有生命，火箭可以不在火星的表面着陆，而在火星的上空用电子望远镜对着火星摄影，然后用无线电把形象传到地球上来。第三个阶段就是派出一个用无线电操纵的小坦克型的流动实验室在火星上着陆，以便直接考察火星的表面。
    赫列勃切维奇说，在进行了这种考察并且把考察的结果加以仔细的综合以后，就可以派人乘火箭飞船到火星上去考察了。
