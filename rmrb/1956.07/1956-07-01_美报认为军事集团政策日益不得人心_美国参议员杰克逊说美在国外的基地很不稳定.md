Title: US Newspapers Believe Military Bloc Policy Increasingly Unpopular; US Senator Jackson Says US Bases Abroad Are Very Unstable
Author:
Date: 1956-07-01
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, June 30th dispatch from New York: The "Wall Street Journal" recently published an editorial saying: The current environment is forcing people to re-evaluate the value of the United States organizing military alliances with other countries. For many years, organizing such alliances has been a major task of American foreign policy. But now this American approach is "becoming increasingly unpopular and increasingly ineffective in the world."

The newspaper analyzed the reasons for this trend. It said that at the Geneva Four-Power Summit, "everyone has tacitly agreed that the hydrogen bomb and other new weapons have made resorting to war unimaginable," coupled with the influence of the Soviet Union's peaceful foreign policy, which makes "any theory of war will not be widely accepted by the world."

The US government has become confused on the issue of its attitude towards neutral countries. Washington is trying to formulate a policy that "will not offend countries that have risked allying with the United States, while at the same time demonstrating that there is plenty of room in the world for genuine neutral countries to step in." However, Washington cannot help but feel "distressed" in this regard.

Xinhua News Agency, June 30th dispatch: According to an Associated Press report from New York: US Senator Henry M. Jackson recently raised the question: How long can US military bases on foreign soil be maintained?

Jackson believes that US bases abroad can be divided into two major categories, one is jointly managed with "allies" or established according to mutually agreed military treaties, and the other is "somewhat imposed...on the people." He admitted that due to the development of national forces around the world, US military bases abroad are very unstable.

He cited bases in North Africa, Okinawa, and Iceland as examples. He said: "The growth of neutralism and anti-Western sentiment in North Africa threatens the future use of bases there. Japanese nationalists oppose the continued occupation of Okinawa."

After reviewing these facts, Jackson expressed doubt: "Can the United States continue to maintain military bases in any unwanted places?"

### 美报认为军事集团政策日益不得人心  美国参议员杰克逊说美在国外的基地很不稳定

1956-07-01
第6版()
专栏：

    美报认为军事集团政策日益不得人心
    美国参议员杰克逊说美在国外的基地很不稳定
    据新华社30日讯　纽约消息：“华尔街日报”最近发表一篇社论说：现在环境正在迫使人们重新估计一下美国同其他国家组织军事联盟的价值。许多年来，组织这种联盟一直是美国外交政策的一个主要任务。而现在美国的这种做法“在世界上越来越不得人心，越来越无效”。
    这家报纸分析了发生这种倾向的原因。它说，在日内瓦四国政府首脑会议上，“大家已经默默同意氢弹和其他新武器已经使诉诸战争成为不可想像的事情”，再加上苏联和平外交政策的影响，这就使得“任何战争理论都不会得到世界的广泛接受”。
    美国政府在对中立国家的态度问题上已经发生混乱。华盛顿正在设法制定一个“既不会触怒已经冒险同美国联盟的国家，同时又将表现世界上是有很大余地让真正的中立国家插足”的政策。但是，在这方面，华盛顿却不能不感到“苦恼”。
    新华社30日讯　据美联社纽约消息：美国参议员亨利·麦·杰克逊最近提出了这样一个问题：美国在外国土地上的军事基地能够保持多久？
    杰克逊认为美国在国外的基地可以分为两大类，一类是同“盟国”共同管理或者按双方同意的军事条约建立的，另一类是“多少强加……在人民头上的”。他承认，由于世界各地民族力量的发展，美国在外国的军事基地是很不稳定的。
    他举出在北非、冲绳岛和冰岛的基地为例子。他说：“北非的中立主义和反对西方情绪的增长威胁着将来在那里的基地的使用。日本民族主义者要反对继续占领冲绳岛。”
    杰克逊在环顾了这些事实之后表示怀疑：“美国是不是能够在任何不受欢迎的地方继续保持军事基地”？
