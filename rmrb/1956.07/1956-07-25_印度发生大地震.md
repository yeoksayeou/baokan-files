Title: Earthquake Occurs in India
Author: 
Date: 1956-07-25
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, New Delhi, July 23rd: An earthquake occurred in the Angar area, twenty-four miles southeast of Bhuj, the capital of the Kutch region on the west coast of India, on the evening of July 21st, resulting in 87 deaths and 250 injuries. Military police are currently continuing rescue efforts in the disaster area.

This earthquake is the most severe since the major earthquake of 1950, which changed the topography of the Assam province of India.

According to the Press Trust of India, about 800 houses were destroyed in Angar, a town of 20,000 residents, and about 2,000 houses were toppled throughout the Kutch region during the ten-second earthquake.

### 印度发生大地震

1956-07-25
第6版()
专栏：

    印度发生大地震
    据新华社新德里23日电　印度西海岸喀齐省首府卜契东南二十四哩的安加尔一带在七月二十一日晚上发生地震，有八十七人死亡，二百五十人受伤。军警目前仍在灾区继续进行抢救工作。
    这次地震是1950年使印度阿萨姆省的地形发生改变的大地震之后最严重的一次。
    据印度报业托辣斯报道，在持续十秒钟的地震中，有两万居民的安加尔约有八百所房屋被毁，喀齐全省约有二千所房子被震倒。
