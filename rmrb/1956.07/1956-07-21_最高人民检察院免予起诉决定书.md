The investigation has confirmed that the above-mentioned 328 Japanese war criminals who committed war crimes during the Japanese invasion of China flagrantly violated the norms of international law and humanitarian principles during the Japanese imperialist war of aggression against our country, and committed crimes of varying degrees against our people. Among them, Ko-Bane Kenji, Komiya Masaka and other 32 Japanese war criminals who committed war crimes during the Japanese invasion of China, after Japan's surrender, participated in the Yan Xishan counter-revolutionary group to revive Japanese militarism and attempted to establish a base in Shanxi to re-invade our country. According to the crimes committed by these criminals, they should have been prosecuted, tried, and given the punishment they deserved. However, in view of the changes in the situation and the current situation in the ten years since Japan's surrender, and in view of the development of friendly relations between the Chinese and Japanese people in recent years, and at the same time, considering that the criminals have shown better remorse during their detention, or are minor war criminals, therefore, in accordance with the "Decision of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress of the People's Republic of China on the Handling of Japanese War Criminals Detained in the War of Aggression Against China" regarding the lenient treatment of Japanese war criminals who committed war crimes during the Japanese invasion of China and the provisions of Article 1, Paragraph 1, this Court decides: to leniently treat the second batch of 328 Japanese war criminals, including Ko-Bane Kenji, who are detained, exempt them from prosecution, and release them immediately.
                      Procurator-General Zhang Dingcheng
                          July 15, 1956
