Title: Fifty-One Nobel Laureates Issue Appeal, Demanding All Nations Refrain from Using Force in Foreign Policy
Author:
Date: 1956-07-02
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, Bonn, 30th: An appeal, read at the International Conference of Nobel Laureates that closed on June 29th in Lindau, West Germany, demanded that all nations renounce the use of force in foreign policy.

This appeal was signed last year by fifty-one Nobel laureates from various countries.

The appeal pointed out: "Today, world peace can be maintained through people's fear of these deadly weapons. But we also feel that if governments were to think that a long period of fear of these deadly weapons would prevent the outbreak of war, it would be self-deception."

The appeal concluded by demanding that "all nations must voluntarily decide to abandon the use of force as a last resort in foreign policy."

### 五十一位诺贝尔奖金得奖人发出呼吁书  要求各国在外交政策中不使用武力

1956-07-02
第5版()
专栏：

    五十一位诺贝尔奖金得奖人发出呼吁书
    要求各国在外交政策中不使用武力
    新华社30日讯　波恩消息：六月二十九日在西德林道闭幕的诺贝尔奖金得奖人国际会议上宣读的一份呼吁书，要求一切国家在外交政策中放弃使用武力。
    这份呼吁书是在去年由五十一位诺贝尔奖金各国得奖人签署的。
    呼吁书指出：“今天，可以借着人们对于这种致命的武器的恐惧而维持世界和平。但是，我们也感觉到，假如各国政府竟然认为，经过一个长时期的对于这种致命武器的恐惧就会防止战争的爆发，那将是自欺。”
    呼吁书最后要求“一切国家都必须自愿地决定放弃以武力作为外交政策中的最后手段”。
