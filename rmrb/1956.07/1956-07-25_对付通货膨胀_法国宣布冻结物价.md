Title: To Combat Inflation, France Announces Price Freeze
Author:
Date: 1956-07-25
Page: 6

    To Combat Inflation
    France Announces Price Freeze
    Xinhua News Agency, July 23rd, Paris dispatch: The French government announced a price freeze on July 21st. A government decree stipulates that all prices must not exceed the price levels of June 15th of this year.
    The prices of agricultural and fishery products are not frozen, nor are the prices of imported goods and steel and iron whose prices are stipulated by the "European Coal and Steel Community."
    French Finance Minister Ramadier said that the purpose of freezing prices is to combat inflation. News has it that the number of banknotes issued in France has reached an unprecedented peak, while the price of the franc in the free market is the lowest in three years.

### 对付通货膨胀  法国宣布冻结物价

1956-07-25
第6版()
专栏：

    对付通货膨胀
    法国宣布冻结物价
    新华社23日讯　巴黎消息：法国政府七月二十一日宣布冻结物价，政府法令规定，各种物价不得超过今年六月十五日的物价水平。
    农产品和渔产品的价格不冻结，进口货和由“欧洲煤钢联营”规定价格的钢和铁的价格也不冻结。
    法国财政部长拉马第说，冻结物价目的在于对付通货膨胀。有消息说，法国现在纸币发行数量已经到了前所未有的高峰，而法郎在自由市场的价格是三年来的最低价格。
