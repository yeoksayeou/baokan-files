Title: US Senate Allocates Additional Funds for Espionage to Intensify Sabotage Against Socialist Countries
Author:
Date: 1956-07-12
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, 11th: Washington dispatch: The United States Senate recently decided to allocate an additional $25 million to intensify espionage and sabotage activities in socialist countries.

The Senate's decision was adopted during a discussion on foreign "aid" issues. This money, used to strengthen espionage activities, is called a "special fund" to "encourage freedom."

During the discussion on this appropriation to strengthen espionage activities, Democratic Senator Douglas suggested establishing a "Freedom Agency" to fund espionage activities. This so-called "Freedom Agency" would provide "financial assistance" to any private or semi-private organization in socialist countries that is "actively engaged in maintaining the spirit of freedom." Douglas's suggestion was later revised to establish a "special fund" managed by the United States Treasury Department.

### 美参院追加间谍活动经费  加强对社会主义国家破坏

1956-07-12
第6版()
专栏：

    美参院追加间谍活动经费
    加强对社会主义国家破坏
    新华社11日讯　华盛顿消息：美国参议院在不久前决定追加拨款两千五百万美元，以加强在社会主义阵营国家进行间谍破坏活动。
    参议院这个决定是在讨论对外“援助”问题的过程中通过的。这笔用来加强间谍活动的钱被叫做是“鼓励自由”的“特别基金”。
    在讨论这笔加强间谍活动的拨款的时候，民主党参议员道格拉斯建议设立一个资助间谍活动的“自由署”，由这个所谓“自由署”给予社会主义阵营国家中“积极从事保持自由精神”的任何私人或者半私人性质的组织以“财政援助”。道格拉斯的建议后来被修改为设立一个由美国财政部管理的“特别基金”。
