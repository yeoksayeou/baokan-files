Title: India Announces New Import Policy: Restricting Consumer Goods, Relaxing Import of Machine Parts and Industrial Raw Materials
Author:
Date: 1956-07-04
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, New Delhi, July 3rd: The Indian Ministry of Commerce and Industry announced a new import policy on June 30th. From July to December this year, the import of machine parts and industrial raw materials will be further relaxed, while the import of various commodities, mainly consumer goods, will be restricted. This policy is formulated to cooperate with the country's industrialization under the Second Five-Year Plan.

Recently, India has been importing a large number of capital goods from the Soviet Union and other Eastern European countries for its industrial projects, such as steel plants, oil exploration and drilling, sugar factories, etc. The main Western countries have been the main exporters of consumer goods to India.

The relaxation of imports of capital goods, parts, and raw materials needed by domestic manufacturers in expanding their production or improving the quality of their products indicates that the domestic machine industry is growing so rapidly that domestic production cannot meet the demand for machine tools.

A comparison of imports over the past few years shows that not only has there been a continuous increase in the quantity of imported goods, but also the number and value of imported goods needed for the country's rapid industrialization have increased. In 1952-53, the value of imported machinery was 890 million rupees. In 1955-56, this number increased to 1.2 billion rupees. In 1952-53, imports of metals reached 230 million rupees, while in 1955-56, this figure increased to 660 million rupees.

The restricted imported goods include a total of 141 items such as wool, wool fabrics, glass, and rayon.

### 印度宣布新的进口政策  限制输入消费品放宽机器零件和工业原料进口

1956-07-04
第6版()
专栏：

    印度宣布新的进口政策
    限制输入消费品放宽机器零件和工业原料进口
    据新华社新德里3日电　印度工商部在六月三十日宣布一项新的进口政策，从今年七月到十二月，机器零件和工业原料的进口将进一步放宽，而对主要是消费品的各种商品的进口则加以限制。这项政策是为了配合第二个五年计划的国家工业化而制定的。
    最近以来，印度一直从苏联和其他东欧国家进口大量的资本货物以供它的工业工程使用，这些工程有钢铁厂、石油勘测和钻探、制糖厂等等。向印度输出消费品的主要国家一直是西方国家。
    对印度国内的制造商在扩大他们的生产或者改进他们的产品质量方面所需的资本货物、零件和原料的进口的放宽表明，国内的机器工业是这样迅速地增长，以致国内的生产不能满足对工作母机的需求。
    对过去几年的进口作一比较就可以看出，不仅在进口货的数量方面有继续不断的增长，而且为国家迅速工业化所需要的进口物品的数目及其价值也增加了。在1952—53年，进口的机器的价值是八亿九千万卢比。1955—56年，这个数目增加到十二亿卢比。在1952—53年，金属的进口达到二亿三千万卢比，而在1955—56年，这个数字增加到六亿六千万卢比。
    限制进口的货物有羊毛、毛织品、玻璃和人造丝等共计一百四十一项。
