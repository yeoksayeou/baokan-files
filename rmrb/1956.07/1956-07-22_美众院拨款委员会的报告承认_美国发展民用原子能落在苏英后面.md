Title: U.S. House Appropriations Committee Report Acknowledges U.S. Lags Behind USSR and UK in Developing Civilian Nuclear Energy
Author: 
Date: 1956-07-22
Page: 6
Xinhua News Agency, Washington, July 21st: The U.S. House Appropriations Committee said in a majority report on the 20th that the United States has fallen behind the Soviet Union and the United Kingdom in the race to develop civilian nuclear energy and is "stuck in an inexcusable state of stagnation."
    The report, attached to the bill granting funds to the Atomic Energy Commission, sharply criticized Strauss, chairman of the Atomic Energy Commission, and called for the reorganization of the commission. The report stated that if the United States continues in this state, "the rest of the world will inevitably regard Britain and Russia as suppliers of atomic power plants."
    The report, drafted by the Democratic majority, called for accelerating the development of civilian nuclear energy and recommended allocating $400 million to the U.S. government for the implementation of a plan to build atomic power plants.

### 美众院拨款委员会的报告承认  美国发展民用原子能落在苏英后面

1956-07-22
第6版()
专栏：

    美众院拨款委员会的报告承认 
    美国发展民用原子能落在苏英后面
    新华社21日讯　华盛顿消息：美国众议院拨款委员会二十日在一项多数报告中说，美国在发展民用原子能的竞赛中落在苏联和英国的后面，“陷于不可原谅的停滞状态”。
    这份附在给予原子能委员会拨款的法案中的报告对原子能委员会主席斯特劳斯提出尖锐的批评，并且要求改组这个委员会。报告说，如果美国继续处于这种状态，“世界的其他国家将不可避免地把英国和俄国当作原子电力工厂的供应者”。
    由民主党多数起草的这项报告要求加速发展民用原子能，报告建议拨出四亿美元给美国政府执行建造原子动力工厂的计划之用。
