Title: The Rise of Socialism and the Decline of Capitalism
Author: Fú Yīngjié
Date: 1950-03-03
Page: 5

　　The contrast between socialist Soviet Union and the capitalist world, whether in terms of economic conditions or social welfare, becomes more and more obvious with the passage of time, month by month.
　　In 1949, the Soviet people had already repaired the damage of the war, and now they continue to raise production and living standards at an unprecedented rate. They have created a fully independent modern industry and technology, some sectors of which are far ahead of the rest of the world, and are able to meet all requirements – even atomic energy. Now there is nothing that can stop the Soviet Union from moving forward steadily and in a planned way towards abundance and complete communism.
　　In the Soviet Union, no one makes a fortune from war, and there is no economic depression or unemployment that requires war or arms production to "cure." Therefore, the Soviet people and the Soviet government view rearmament and war as waste and suffering.
　　But in the capitalist world, economic panic deepened in 1949. Production and trade declined because people did not have the power to buy what was produced. In all capitalist countries, the living standards of workers have been encroached upon—either through currency devaluation, unemployment, or cuts in social services—making the panic even worse. American and British monopoly capital want to use rearmament and war to increase their profits, keep their steel mills running, and seize new markets for their surplus goods and capital.
　　Today, the achievements of the Soviet Union point the people of the capitalist world to the path of escaping capitalist panic and imperialist war. Capitalism has no answer to this; capitalism can only try to prevent its own people from seeing these achievements of the Soviet Union.
　　In the first nine months of 1949, Soviet industrial production was 20 percent higher than in the same period in 1948. This means that, despite the damage suffered in the war, industrial production is now two-fifths higher than in 1940, and is growing rapidly. 　　In Britain, industrial production was only 6 to 7 percent higher than in 1948, and 19 percent higher than in 1938 (a pre-war depression year), and has recently been declining due to falling exports.
　　In the United States, industrial production fell by 15 percent by July 1949 due to the growth of capitalist panic and difficulties in sales.
　　Soviet agriculture had a better harvest this year than in 1948 (which was already a bumper harvest), exceeding 1940.
　　In the capitalist world, agriculture has been shrouded in the shadow of "overproduction." The US government has told farmers to reduce the area planted with cotton by 24 percent in 1950 to prevent excessive inventories.

　　In the United States, retail trade in July 1949 was 3 percent lower than in July 1948.
　　Living standards are rapidly increasing in the Soviet Union, but decreasing in the United States.

　　Compare it to Britain. Currency devaluation will further reduce the living standards of the British people. Since June 1947, workers' real wages have decreased by 4 percent.
　　In the Soviet Union, housing construction in the first nine months of 1949 increased by 29 percent compared to the same period in 1948.
　　In Britain, the rate of construction decreased from 112,000 units in the first half of 1948 to 101,000 units in the first half of 1949.
　　In the Soviet Union, the construction of new hospitals and the expansion of existing ones increased by 22,000 beds in 1949.
　　In Britain, there is almost no new construction or expansion of hospitals. There are still 50,000 beds that do not have enough people to care for them due to a shortage of nurses.
　　In the Soviet Union, schools, kindergartens, and nurseries have first priority. In Moscow alone, 398 schools, 94 nurseries, and kindergartens will be built in 1949 to accommodate 60,000 children.
　　In Britain, only 61 new schools have been built since the war. Many five-year-old children have no schools to attend, so there is talk of changing the school entry age from five to six.
　　In 1949, the speed of capital construction in the Soviet Union was 22 percent higher than in 1948. Compare it to Britain, where Attlee plans to cut capital construction by 140 million pounds, about 7 percent.
　　In the Soviet Union, rural electrification is progressing rapidly. In 1949, the construction of power stations was 40 percent greater than in 1948. In Britain, Attlee cut capital for fuel and electricity, power plant production was reduced, and rural electrification made no progress.
　　Under the leadership of the Communist Party, the Soviet people are moving from socialism to communism, and they can face the future with confidence. They know that each year will improve their living standards, improve social services, and increase cultural achievements.
　　In the capitalist world, they are moving towards economic depression, and the ruling classes everywhere are trying to push the burden of capitalist panic onto the shoulders of the working people. What the working people encounter is not a higher standard of living, but cuts—cuts in real wages, cuts in education and healthcare, cuts in housing.
　　The progress of the Soviet Union, the achievements of the Soviet Union, point out the way forward for all people.

### 社会主义的上升与资本主义的下降
福英节
1950-03-03
第5版()
专栏：国际问题

　　社会主义的上升与资本主义的下降
    福英节译
    社会主义的苏联与资本主义的世界之间的对照，不论是经济情况方面，或社会福利方面，都随着时期的推移，一月比一月明显。
    苏联人民于一九四九年已把战争的破坏修复了，现在继续不断地以空前的速度在提高着生产与生活标准。他们已创造一充分独立的现代工业与技术，某些部门，已远超乎世界其他各处，而且能够应付一切的要求——甚至原子能。现在已没有什么能够阻挡苏联走向充裕与完全共产主义的有计划的稳步前进了。
    在苏联没有人发战争财，也没有经济萧条或失业要战争或军火生产来“救治”。所以，苏联人民与苏联政府视重新武装与战争为浪费与苦难。
    但资本主义世界在一九四九年却经济恐慌加深。生产与贸易衰落，因为人民没有力量购买所生产的东西。在一切资本主义国度中，工人的生活标准都被侵害——或由于货币贬值、或由于失业、或由于削减社会服务，——使得恐慌更厉害。美英垄断资本要借重新武装与战争来提高其利润，来维持其钢厂开工，并为其过剩的货物与资本夺取新的市场。
    今天，苏联的成就，向资本主义世界的人民指出了逃脱资本主义的恐慌与帝国主义的战争之道路。资本主义对此是没有答复；资本主义只能想法不让其本国人民看见苏联的这些成就。
    在一九四九年头九个月中苏联工业生产比一九四八年同一时期多百分之二十。这即是说，虽遭受了战争的破坏，现在的工业生产比一九四○年高五分之二，而且在迅速增长着。苏联工业生产（按一九二六——二七年价格计算）

    在英国，工业生产只比一九四八年高百分之六至七，比一九三八年（战前萧条的一年）高百分之十九，而最近则因输出衰落而在下降着。
    在美国，一九四九年至七月为止，工业生产因资本主义恐慌之增长与销售困难而下跌百分之十五。
    苏联农业，今年收获比一九四八年（已是丰收）还要好，超过了一九四○年。

    比一比英国吧。货币贬值将使英国人民生活标准更降低。自一九四七年六月以来，工人实际工资降低了百分之四。
    在苏联，房屋建筑，在一九四九年头九个月，比一九四八年同一时期，增加了百分之二十九。
    在英国，建筑速度从一九四八年上半年的十一万二千座减为一九四九年上半年的十万零一千座。
    在苏联，医院建筑新的房屋与扩充旧的，在一九四九年便增加了二万二千床位。
    在英国，医院差不多没有新的建筑或扩充。现在还有五万个床位因护士缺乏而没有足够的人照管。
    在苏联，学校、幼稚园、托儿所，是有第一位的优先权。单在莫斯科一地，在一九四九年就要建造三九八所学校，九四所托儿所和幼稚园以容纳六万儿童。
    在英国，自战争以来只建造六十一所新的学校。有许多五岁的儿童没有学校可入，因此正在谈论要将入学年龄从五岁改为六岁。
    在一九四九年苏联资本建设之速度比一九四八年高百分之二十二。比一比英国吧，艾德礼计划把资本建设削减一亿四千万镑，约为百分之七。
    在苏联，农村电气化进行得很快。在一九四九年，发电站的建造，比一九四八年大百分之四十。在英国，艾德礼削减用于燃料及电力上的资本，发电厂之生产被减少，而农村电气化则毫无进步。
    苏联人民在共产党领导之下，由社会主义向共产主义前进，他们能够正视将来。他们知道每年都将使他们的生活标准提高，社会服务改善，文化的成就更大。
    在资本主义世界，正朝着经济萧条前进，各地的统治阶级都企图把资本主义的恐慌的重担推在劳动人民肩上。劳动人民所碰到的，不是提高的生活标准，而是削减——实际工资削减、教育与保健削减、住宅削减。
    苏联的前进，苏联的成就，对一切人民指出了前进之路。
