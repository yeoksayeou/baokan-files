Title: All-China Federation of Trade Unions Issues Notice: National Trade Union Production Department Heads Conference Scheduled for May 20th in Beijing; Will Discuss Issues Such as How to Implement Democratic Factory Management
Author:
Date: 1950-03-29
Page: 1

Xinhua News Agency report:

The All-China Federation of Trade Unions has recently issued a notice regarding the convening of the National Trade Union Production Department Heads Conference. The full text is as follows:

(1) The National Trade Union Production Department Heads Conference is scheduled to be held in Beijing on May 20, 1950, for a period of ten days.

(2) The National Trade Union Production Department Heads Conference is prepared to study the following issues:

    I. How to implement democratic factory management and the role of trade unions in transforming old enterprises.

    II. How trade unions can lead the masses in production work (1. The tasks and scope of work of production departments at all levels of trade unions; 2. How to organize production competitions; 3. Labor agreements; 4. Collective contracts; 5. The organization and work of the production wage committees and invention and technological improvement, rationalization proposal committees of trade union grassroots organizations).

    III. Other matters.

(3) This conference will focus on larger state-owned enterprises in industries such as hardware (steel, machinery, military industry), coal mining, electricity, railways, and textiles, and relevant administrative departments will be invited to participate.

Number of Representatives from Each Region:

Based on the needs of the conference, the number of representatives from each region attending this conference is determined as follows:

    I. Each National Committee of industrial trade unions and the All-China Federations of Trade Unions (or offices) in the Northeast, East China, Central South, Northwest, and Southwest Greater Administrative Regions will send one production department head to participate.

    II. Production department heads from the All-China Federations of Trade Unions in Hebei, Shanxi, Chahar, Pingyuan, Shandong, Zhejiang, Fujian, Henan, Hubei, Hunan, Jiangxi, Guangdong, and Guangxi provinces, as well as the General Trade Unions of Northern Jiangsu and Southern Jiangsu regions.

    III. Production department heads from the General Trade Unions in Beijing, Tianjin, Shanghai, Harbin, Jilin, Changchun, Shenyang, Andong, Fushun, Benxi, Anshan, Lüda, Tangshan, Taiyuan, Shijiazhuang, Jinan, Qingdao, Xuzhou, Nanjing, Wuxi, Hangzhou, Fuzhou, Wuhan, Changsha, Guangzhou, Xi'an, and Chongqing.

    IV. Production department heads from the industrial trade unions of hardware (steel, machinery, military industry), electricity, railways, and textiles under the General Trade Unions of Northeast China, Beijing, Tianjin, Shanghai, and Shenyang.

    V. Production committee members from grassroots trade union organizations will be selected according to industrial systems such as hardware (steel, machinery, military industry), coal mining, railways, textiles, and electricity. Six factories in Northeast China and three factories in Shanghai (including one private factory) will be decided by the local General Trade Unions or industrial trade unions in their respective regions. In the North China region, one person each will be sent from Beijing Shijingshan Iron and Steel Plant, Power Plant, Tianjin Zhongfang Third Factory, Hengyuan Yarn Factory, Tangshan Kailuan, Taiyuan Northwest Iron and Steel Company, Datong Coal Mine, Longyan Iron Mine, and Xiahuayuan Power Plant.

    VI. Each industrial department under the State Council of the Central People's Government will be invited to send one representative, three from administrative departments in the Northeast Region, two from East China, and one each from Beijing and Tianjin. The candidates for administrative department representatives in each region will be decided through consultations between the General Trade Unions and administrative departments in each region.

(4) Each unit participating in the conference should submit its work summary and typical materials to the Production Department of the All-China Federation of Trade Unions before April 30th.

(5) Representatives participating in the conference should report to Beijing on May 18th.

(6) Representatives should not bring attendants, should provide their own travel expenses, and should bring food expenses for fifteen days based on the original supply standard.

### 中华全国总工会发出通知  全国工会生产部长会议定五月二十日在京召开  将讨论如何贯彻工厂管理民主化等问题

1950-03-29
第1版()
专栏：

　　中华全国总工会发出通知
    全国工会生产部长会议定五月二十日在京召开
    将讨论如何贯彻工厂管理民主化等问题
    【新华社讯】中华全国总工会顷发出关于召开全国工会生产部长会议的通知。全文如下：
    （一）订于一九五○年五月二十日在北京召开全国工会生产部长会议，会期十天。
    （二）全国工会生产部长会议准备研究下列问题：
    一、如何贯彻工厂管理民主化，工会在改造旧企业中的作用。
    二、工会如何领导群众进行生产工作（１、各级工会生产部的任务与工作范围问题。２、如何组织生产竞赛。３、劳动公约问题。４、集体合同问题。５、工会基层组织的生产工资委员会与发明创造技术改进，合理化建议委员会的组织与工作问题）。
    三、其他。
    （三）此次会议，拟以公营企业中五金（钢铁、机器、军工）、煤矿、电业、铁路、纺织等较大产业为重点，并邀请有关行政部门参加。
  各地代表名额：
    根据会议需要决定各地出席此次会议之代表名额如下：
    一、各产业工会全国委员会及东北、华东、中南、西北、西南各大行政区总工会（或办事处）派生产部长一人参加。
    二、河北、山西、察哈尔、平原、山东、浙江、福建、河南、湖北、湖南、江西、广东、广西各省总工会及苏北苏南地区总工会生产部长。
    三、北京、天津、上海、哈尔滨、吉林、长春、沈阳、安东、抚顺、本溪、鞍山、旅大、唐山、太原、石家庄、济南、青岛、徐州、南京、无锡、杭州、福州、武汉、长沙、广州、西安、重庆各市总工会生产部长。
    四、东北及北京、天津、上海、沈阳各总工会所属五金（钢铁、机器、军工）、电业、铁路、纺织等产业总工会生产部长。
    五、按五金（钢铁、机器、军工）、煤矿、铁路、纺织、电业等产业系统选派工会基层组织生产委员。东北六个厂、上海三个厂（包括一个私营工厂）由各该地区地方总工会或产业工会决定。华北区北京石景山钢铁厂、发电厂、天津中纺三厂、恒源纱厂、唐山开滦、太原西北钢铁公司、大同煤矿、龙烟铁矿、下花园发电厂各派一人。
    六、邀请中央人民政府政务院所属各工业部门各派代表一人，东北区行政部门三人，华东二人，北京、天津各一人，各地区行政部门代表人选由各地区总工会与行政部门磋商决定。
    （四）参加会议的各单位，应将工作总结及典型材料于四月三十日前，送以中华全国总工会生产部。
    （五）参加会议之代表，应于五月十八日来京报到。
    （六）代表请勿携带随员，自备旅费，并带原供给标准伙食费十五天。
