Date: 1950-03-29

　　Tibetan People Sing Praises of Chairman Mao
　　--Northwest Travelogue, Part 7
　　By our correspondent Li Wen

Young Tibetan men and women are mostly skilled in singing and dancing. During my travels in Tibetan areas, I often encountered three or five horsemen singing loudly with their heads held high. Their singing echoed in the valleys, particularly melodious and resonant. When these young men and women became engrossed in their singing, they often spurred their horses onward, displaying a very majestic and robust air. Tibetan folk songs can be roughly divided into two types: one is a hymn praising gods or congratulating someone, mostly sung during religious gatherings or when relatives and friends have joyous occasions; the other is a love song sung by young men and women in the valleys and open fields. There are many kinds of tunes, and the lyrics are mostly improvised by the singers. The two sides sing back and forth, passionately and unrestrained. I once recorded a popular folk song in the Labrang Tibetan area. If translated into Chinese, the lyrics would be something like this:

　　“Lovely little cuckoo,
　　Without you,
　　I wouldn't know spring, summer, autumn, and winter,
　　It's not that there are no other birds,
　　But I don't want to listen to other birds sing.
　　× × × ×
　　Lovely little white horse,
　　Without you,
　　I can't climb the big slope,
　　It's not that there are no other horses,
　　But I don't want to ride other horses.
　　× × × ×
　　Lovely girl,
　　Without you, the days are so long,
　　It's not that there are no other girls,
　　But I don't love other girls."

Labrang was liberated in September of last year. The policies of the Communist Party and the People's Government, the hard-working style of Party and government workers and commanders and fighters of the People's Liberation Army, and their sincere attitude of respecting the customs and habits of ethnic minorities quickly won widespread and deep love and respect from the broad masses of Tibetan people. Tibetan people, skilled in singing, quickly composed new songs to express their love for the Communist Party and Chairman Mao. I heard a Tibetan song in the Tibetan area praising Chairman Mao:

　　From the Eastern Sea,
　　A joyful sun rises,
　　You—Chairman Mao,
　　Your brilliance fills Jambudvipa (Note 1)
　　Joyful, shu la ya shu (Note 2)
　　× × × ×

　　A multi-ethnic country,
　　Equality, peace and happiness,
　　You—Chairman Mao,
　　A person loved by everyone,
　　Joyful, shu la ya shu.
　　× × × ×

　　With an army for the people's liberation,
　　With you, Chairman Mao,
　　People of all classes and nationalities,
　　Have gained freedom,
　　Joyful, shu la ya shu.
　　× × × ×

　　The sun and moon in the sky,
　　—You, Chairman Mao
　　The shining stars,
　　—The generals and soldiers and people you lead,
　　Joyful, shu la ya shu.
　　× × ×

　　The regime is as solid as Mount Sumeru (Note 3),
　　—Led by you, Chairman Mao;
　　Wisdom and power are as bright as the sun,
　　—You, Chairman Mao;
　　Joyful, shu la ya shu.
　　× × ×

　　Longevity like the Vajra mountains,
　　—You, Chairman Mao;
　　A heart like a wish-fulfilling jewel,
　　—You, Chairman Mao;
　　Joyful, shu la ya shu.
　　× × ×

　　Good stars in the sky,
　　Good times on earth,
　　Auspicious sun!
　　Good opportunities come together!
　　Joyful, shu la ya shu.
　　× × × ×

　　Dancing and singing,
　　Unrestrained joy,
　　This is nothing strange,
　　The Communist Party is like a key,
　　We enter a beautiful paradise.
　　Joyful, shu la ya shu.
　　× × ×

　　The sun rises in the east,
　　Beautiful lotus flowers bloom,
　　Chairman Mao has come,
　　The Tibetan people are safe and sound.
　　Joyful, shu la ya shu.

　　Note 1: Jambudvipa is a geographical term in Buddhist scriptures, referring to Asia here.
　　Note 2: "Shu la ya shu" is a kind of tune.
　　Note 3: There is a legend in Tibetan scriptures that Mount Sumeru is the most solid mountain.

### 藏民歌唱毛主席——西北旅行通讯之七

第3版()

　　藏民歌唱毛主席
    ——西北旅行通讯之七
    本报记者  力文
    藏区青年男女大都擅长歌舞。记者旅行藏区途中，常常遇见三五骑士，在马上昂首高歌。歌声与山谷的回响应和，分外悠扬嘹亮。这些青年男女唱到兴浓时，往往拍马奔驰，显出十分雄壮豪健。藏民歌曲大致可分两种，一种是赞美神佛或恭祝某人的祝颂歌，多在宗教的集会中或亲朋有喜庆时歌唱；一种是青年男女在山谷旷野中欢唱的情歌。曲调种类极多，歌词多是歌唱者临时编造的。双方一唱一和，热情奔放。记者在拉卜楞藏区曾经记下一首流行的民歌，如果译成汉话，歌辞是这样的：
    “可爱的小布谷，
    没有你，
    我不知道春夏秋冬，

    别的鸟唱我不愿听。×      ×        ×    ×

    拉卜楞于去年九月解放了。共产党和人民政府的政策，党政工作人员和人民解放军指战员艰苦奋斗的作风，以及他们尊重少数民族风俗习惯的诚恳态度，迅速获得广大藏民普遍而深厚的爱戴。擅长歌唱的藏民很快地编出了新歌，表达他们爱戴共产党、毛主席的情意。记者在藏区听到了一首赞颂毛主席的藏歌：
