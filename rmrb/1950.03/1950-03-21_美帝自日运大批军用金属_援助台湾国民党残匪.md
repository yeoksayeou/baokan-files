Title: US Imperialism Transports Large Quantities of Military Metal from Japan to Aid the Remnant Chiang Kai-shek Bandits in Taiwan
Author:
Date: 1950-03-21
Page: 1

　　US Imperialism Transports Large Quantities of Military Metal from Japan
　　Aiding the Remnant Chiang Kai-shek Bandits in Taiwan
　　[Xinhua News Agency, 19th] The American occupation authorities in Japan have ordered the transportation of a large quantity of military metal to aid the remnant Chiang Kai-shek bandit gang in Taiwan. According to a Moscow broadcast on the 15th, citing a New York dispatch from an Associated Press reporter: MacArthur's headquarters issued an order to transport a large quantity of non-ferrous metals worth $200,000 to Taiwan. The metals to be transported include 461,125 kilograms of aluminum, 123,808 kilograms of tin, and 42,533 kilograms of zinc. The Moscow broadcast stated: Japanese democratic groups pointed out that this is part of the American military aid program for the remnant Chiang Kai-shek bandits. The United States has stipulated that military equipment and sea vessels be transported from Japan to Taiwan.

### 美帝自日运大批军用金属  援助台湾国民党残匪

1950-03-21
第1版()
专栏：

　　美帝自日运大批军用金属
    援助台湾国民党残匪
    【新华社十九日讯】日本的美占领当局命令运送大批军用金属援助台湾国民党残余匪帮。据莫斯科广播十五日引美联社记者纽约消息称：麦克阿瑟总部发布命令，将价值二十万美元的大批有色金属输往台湾。确定输送的金属中包括有四十六万一千一百二十五公斤铝，十二万三千八百零八公斤锡，四万二千五百三十三公斤锌。莫斯科广播称：日本民主团体指出，这是美国对国民党残匪军事援助计划的一部分，美国曾规定将军用装备和海船由日本运往台湾。
