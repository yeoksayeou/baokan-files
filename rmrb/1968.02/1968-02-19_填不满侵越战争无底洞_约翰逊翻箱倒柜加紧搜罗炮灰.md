Title: Unable to Fill the Bottomless Pit of the Vietnam War, Johnson Rummages for Cannon Fodder
Author:
Date: 1968-02-19
Edition: 5

	To fill the ever-growing casualties of U.S. aggressor troops in South Vietnam, the Johnson administration is rummaging through every corner at home to hurriedly gather cannon fodder.

	The U.S. draft board announced on the 16th that it would abolish the previous deferment policy for certain specialized professions and university graduates, thereby making some of these people possibly subject to immediate conscription.

	Chairman of the U.S. Education Committee, Logan Wilson, said that the Johnson administration's "decision means that in the near future, most of the 1968 university graduates and 1968 first-year graduate students will be conscripted." He lamented that the Johnson administration's decision "is shocking" and "will inevitably cause a serious shortage of trained manpower in the United States."

	The Johnson administration's desperate struggle will inevitably provoke even stronger opposition from the American people to the Vietnam War. The U.S. imperialists' attempt to increase cannon fodder to salvage the failing Vietnam War is doomed to failure.

### 填不满侵越战争无底洞  约翰逊翻箱倒柜加紧搜罗炮灰

1968-02-19
第5版()
专栏：

    填不满侵越战争无底洞
    约翰逊翻箱倒柜加紧搜罗炮灰
    新华社十八日讯　华盛顿消息：为了填补美国侵略军在南越战场上越来越惨重的伤亡，约翰逊政府在国内翻箱倒柜，加紧搜罗炮灰。
    美国征兵局十六日宣布，它将废除过去对从事某些专门职业的人和大学毕业生实行的缓征办法，从而使这些人中的某些人可能被立即征召入伍。
    美国教育委员会主席洛根·威尔逊说，约翰逊政府的“这个决定意味着，不久的将来将征召一九六八年的大部分大学毕业生和一九六八年读完了一年级的研究生。”他哀叹，约翰逊政府的这一决定“使人震惊”，“将不可避免地造成美国……受过训练的人力严重缺乏”。
    约翰逊政府的这种垂死挣扎，势必将激起美国人民更强烈的反对侵越战争运动，美帝国主义妄图通过增加炮灰来挽救侵越战争的败局是绝办不到的。
