Title: Kenyan African Legislators Demand an African as Chief Minister
Author:
Date: 1959-12-22
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, Cairo, December 17th: According to news from Nairobi received here today, the thirteen elected African members of the Kenyan Legislative Council have demanded that Britain establish a democratic autonomous government in Kenya within the next year, with an African as Chief Minister.

These African leaders, headed by Oginga Odinga, Chairman of the Kenya Independence Movement, issued a statement after meeting with British Colonial Secretary Macleod, demanding that the British government accept this request in principle before the Kenya Constitutional Round Table Conference to be held in London in January next year.

These thirteen African legislators originally belonged to the Kenya Independence Movement and the British-backed Kenya National Party. At the end of November this year, they formed a united front on the condition that the African legislators belonging to the Kenya National Party abandon multiracialism (an ideology aimed at permanently maintaining white rule).

### 怯尼亚非洲议员  要求由非洲人任首席部长

1959-12-22
第6版()
专栏：

　　怯尼亚非洲议员
    要求由非洲人任首席部长
    据新华社开罗17日电　据今天这里收到的来自内罗毕的消息说，怯尼亚立法会议的十三名由选举产生的非洲议员要求英国在明年内建立以非洲人担任首席部长的怯尼亚民主自治政府。
    以怯尼亚独立运动党主席奥金加·奥廷加为首的这些非洲人领袖，在同英国殖民地大臣麦克劳德举行会谈以后发表的一项声明中，要求英国政府在明年1月在伦敦举行怯尼亚制宪圆桌会议以前原则上接受这个要求。
    这十三名非洲议员本来属于怯尼亚独立运动党和英国支持的怯尼亚民族党。他们在今年11月底在属于怯尼亚民族党的非洲议员表示放弃多种族主义（旨在永久保持白人统治的思想）的条件下，组成了统一阵线。
