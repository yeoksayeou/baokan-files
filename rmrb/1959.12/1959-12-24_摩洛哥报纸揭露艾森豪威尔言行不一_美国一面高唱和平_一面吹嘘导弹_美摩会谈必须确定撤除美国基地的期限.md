Title: Moroccan Newspapers Expose Eisenhower's Hypocrisy: U.S. Chants Peace While Bragging About Missiles; U.S.-Morocco Talks Must Set Deadline for Removal of American Bases
Author:
Date: 1959-12-24
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, Rabat, December 20th: Moroccan labor union newspaper "Avant-Garde" published two articles exposing the hypocrisy of Eisenhower's statements before leaving for his overseas visit, and criticizing the U.S.'s foreign policy.

One article in the newspaper recounted Eisenhower's statement that the United States desires to live in a world where all countries are free from oppression and fear. The newspaper pointed out that, at the same time, the United States stood with countries that violate freedom, disregard justice, and undermine peace in the United Nations, refusing to support the cause of Algeria. The newspaper said that the Maghreb people feel very sorry and indignant about the U.S.'s stance, because they themselves and all African people hope to achieve unity and independence.

Under the title "What Will We Say to Eisenhower?", another article published by the newspaper pointed out that Eisenhower "is full of contradictions." The newspaper recalled that when Eisenhower left the United States, he said that in two or three years, the United States must rely not only on intercontinental ballistic missiles, but also on faster and more powerful bombers. Commenting on Eisenhower's statement, the newspaper said, "This is a bizarre statement for a 'peace visit'." The newspaper concluded, "We must know how to speak to Eisenhower; the people of underdeveloped countries cannot express friendship to him unless they are completely liberated. Our people and our leaders must assume the important responsibility of withdrawing foreign troops from our country and from the rest of the Maghreb."

"Opinion Newspaper" published an editorial today, expressing hope that the upcoming meeting between Moroccan King Mohammed V and U.S. President Eisenhower will set a definite date for the removal of U.S. bases in Morocco. The newspaper said: We hope that our friendship with the United States will not affect our support for African countries striving for liberation.

### 摩洛哥报纸揭露艾森豪威尔言行不一  美国一面高唱和平　一面吹嘘导弹  美摩会谈必须确定撤除美国基地的期限

1959-12-24
第6版()
专栏：

　　摩洛哥报纸揭露艾森豪威尔言行不一
    美国一面高唱和平　一面吹嘘导弹
    美摩会谈必须确定撤除美国基地的期限
    据新华社拉巴特20日电　摩洛哥劳工联盟机关报“前卫报”发表了两篇文章，揭露艾森豪威尔出国访问前发表的声明的虚伪性质，并且抨击了美国的外交政策。
    这家报纸的一篇文章追述了艾森豪威尔说过的所谓美国渴望生活在所有国家不受任何压迫和恐惧的世界中这句话。这家报纸指出，就在这个时候，美国在联合国中站在侵犯自由、无视正义和破坏和平的国家那一边，不肯支持阿尔及利亚的事业。这家报纸说，马格里布人民对于美国采取这种立场感到非常遗憾，并且十分愤慨，因为他们自己和所有非洲人民都希望实现统一和获得独立。
    在“我们将对艾森豪威尔说些什么？”的标题下，这家报纸发表的另一篇文章指出，艾森豪威尔“充满了矛盾”。这家报纸追述说，艾森豪威尔在动身离开美国时曾说，在二、三年内，美国必须不仅依靠洲际弹道导弹，还要依靠更快速、更有力的轰炸机。报纸评论艾森豪威尔这句话时说，“对于一次‘和平访问’来说，这是一次离奇的声明”。报纸最后说，“我们必须懂得怎样同艾森豪威尔说话，不发达国家的人民不能向他表示友谊，除非他们完全获得解放。我国人民和我国领导人必须把如下的重大责任担当起来，即：使外国军队撤离我们国家，撤离马格里布的其余地方。”
    “舆论报”今天发表社论，表示希望在摩洛哥国王穆罕默德五世同美国总统艾森豪威尔即将举行的会谈中，将定出撤销美国在摩洛哥的基地的肯定日期。这家报纸说：我们希望我们同美国的友谊不影响我们对争取解放的非洲国家的支持。
