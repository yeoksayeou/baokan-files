　　Little Knowledge

　　The Higher You Climb, the Farther You See
    An ancient saying goes: "Stand high, see far." This is a metaphor for people who should have great ambition and a broad vision. So, is this really the case? It is indeed. How many meters high do you stand, and how many kilometers can you see? Please see the table below:
    ○·Five meters high, visible for two·59 kilometers.
    One·7 meters (human height) high, visible for four·66 kilometers.
    Five meters high, visible for seven·97 kilometers.
    Ten meters high, visible for eleven·3 kilometers.
    One hundred meters high, visible for thirty-five·7 kilometers.
    One kilometer high, visible for one hundred and fifteen kilometers.
    Ten kilometers high, visible for three hundred and fifty-five kilometers.
    One hundred kilometers high, visible for one thousand one hundred and ten kilometers.
    Five ○○ kilometers high, visible for two thousand four hundred and forty-two kilometers.
    The straight-line distance from Beijing to Guangzhou is about one thousand nine hundred and twenty kilometers, which means that only by standing at an altitude of three hundred and one kilometers above the capital can you see this large southern city.
          (Guang)

### 登多高，望多远

1959-12-24
第8版()
专栏：小常识

　　登多高，望多远
    古语说：站得高，看得远。这是比喻人应该树雄心，立大志，高瞻远瞩。那么，事实是不是这样呢？确是这样。究竟站几米高，看几公里远呢？请看下表：
    ○·五米高，可见二·五九公里远。
    一·七米（人的高度）高，可见四·六六公里远。
    五米高，可见七·九七公里远。
    十米高，可见十一·三公里远。
    一百米高，可见三十五·七公里远。
    一公里高，可见一百一十五公里远。
    十公里高，可见三百五十五公里远。
    一百公里高，可见一千一百一十公里远。
    五○○公里高，可见二千四百四十二公里远。
    北京到广州的直线距离约有一千九百二十公里，就是说，只有站在首都三百○一公里的高空，才有可能望见这座南方的大城市。
          （光）
