Title: Indonesian, Japanese Oil Companies Agree to Jointly Develop North Sumatra Oil Resources
Author:
Date: 1959-12-14
Page: 4
Xinhua News Agency, Jakarta, December 4th: Antara News Agency reported that in an agreement reached with the Japanese Petroleum Company to jointly exploit oil resources in North Sumatra, the Indonesian state-owned oil company announced that it would receive a loan of $50 million from Japan in the form of money, labor and goods.

The Indonesian state-owned oil company will repay the loan with crude oil it produces within ten years, and the interest rate on the loan has not yet been determined. It was announced that this agreement does not mean that "the Indonesian state-owned oil company and Japan operate a joint venture." Further talks with the Japanese side have not yet concluded. Earlier reports indicated that a formal agreement would be signed by the end of this year.

### 印度尼西亚、日本两国石油公司协议  联合开采北苏门答腊石油资源

1959-12-14
第4版()
专栏：

    印度尼西亚、日本两国石油公司协议
    联合开采北苏门答腊石油资源
    据新华社雅加达4日电　据安塔拉通讯社报道，印度尼西亚国营石油公司在同日本石油公司达成的联合开采北苏门答腊石油资源的协定中宣布，印度尼西亚国营石油公司将从日本得到一笔以金钱、劳役和货物的形式提供的五千万美元的贷款。
    印度尼西亚国营石油公司将用它生产的原油在十年内归还这笔贷款，贷款的利息还没有决定。据宣布，这项协定并不意味着“印度尼西亚国营石油公司同日本经营联营企业”。同日本方面的进一步会谈还没有结束。据早些时候的消息说，正式协定将在今年年底签订。
