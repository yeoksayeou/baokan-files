Title: Kishi Nobusuke Decides to Go to the United States Personally to Sign the Japan-U.S. Military Treaty
Author:
Date: 1959-12-10
Page: 6

    Xinhua News Agency, Tokyo, December 9th: Japanese Prime Minister Nobusuke Kishi decided yesterday to go to the United States personally to sign the new Japan-U.S. military alliance treaty.

    On December 8th, Nobusuke Kishi consulted with Vice President of the Liberal Democratic Party, Banboku Ohno, Secretary-General Masajiro Kawashima, and Chief Cabinet Secretary Etsusaburo Shiina regarding the composition of the delegation to Washington to sign the new treaty, and decided that Kishi himself would serve as the "chief plenipotentiary" of the delegation. They also agreed that the delegation would depart on January 16th of the following year and sign the treaty on the 19th. The news said that in this delegation, which is expected to be composed of four to five people, the only confirmed members so far are Nobusuke Kishi and Foreign Minister Aiichiro Fujiyama.

### 岸信介决定亲自出马到美国  签订日美军事条约

1959-12-10
第6版()
专栏：

    岸信介决定亲自出马到美国
    签订日美军事条约
    新华社9日讯  东京消息：日本首相岸信介昨天决定亲自出马到美国去签订新的日美军事同盟条约。
    岸信介8日同自由民主党副总裁大野伴睦、干事长川岛正次郎和内阁官房长官椎名悦三郎磋商了到华盛顿签订新条约的代表团组成问题，决定由岸信介自己担任代表团的“首席全权代表”。他们并且商定代表团在明年1月16日启程，19日签字。消息说，在这个预定由四人到五人组成的代表团中，目前已经确定的只有岸信介和外务相藤山爱一郎两人。
