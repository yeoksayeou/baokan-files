Title: Soviet Union Creates World's First Electronic Typesetting Machine Capable of Typesetting Articles and Complex Charts, Also Capable of Long-Distance Typesetting
Author:
Date: 1959-12-11
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, Moscow, December 10th: According to today's "Izvestia" report: The Soviet Union has created the world's first program-controlled electronic typesetting machine. Its basic principle is similar to that of an electronic computer.

Employees of the Mechanization and Automation Laboratory of the Soviet Scientific and Technical Information Research Institute, under the leadership of scientist Rakov, after a period of research, successfully created this unique new machine. Its working procedure is as follows: first, the article to be typeset is perforated on paper tape by an electromagnetic device, and then the processed paper tape is used to control the electronic machine, which automatically operates the typesetting machine. In addition to typesetting ordinary text, this machine can also typeset complex charts. Another great advantage of this machine is that it can be used for long-distance typesetting. For example, people in Moscow can typeset in any place far from Moscow through telecommunications links.

### 苏联制成世界上第一架电子排版机  能排文章和复杂图表也能远距离排版

1959-12-11
第5版()
专栏：

    苏联制成世界上第一架电子排版机
    能排文章和复杂图表也能远距离排版
    新华社莫斯科10日电　据“消息报”今天报道：苏联制成了世界上第一架用程序控制的电子排版机。它的基本原理同电子计算机相仿。
    苏联科学技术情报研究所的资料工作机械化和自动化实验室的职工们，在科学家拉科夫的领导下，经过一个时期的钻研后，创制成功了这种独一无二的新机器。它的工作程序是这样的：先将要排版的文章经过电磁装置在纸带上凿孔，然后利用这种经过处理后的纸带来操纵电子机器，通过电子机器使排字机自动进行工作。这种机器除了排一般文字外，还能够排复杂的图表。这种机器还有另一个很大的优点，就是可以利用它来进行远距离排版工作。例如，人们在莫斯科可以通过电讯联系，在远离莫斯科的任何地方进行排版。
