　　关税及贸易总协定组织
　　关税及贸易总协定组织是美国用来扩张市场、打击其他资本主义国家对外贸易的工具。
　　1947年联合国国际贸易组织筹备委员会举行第二次会议时，参加会议的二十三国订立了一百二十三条双边减低关税协定。同年10月30日，在日内瓦签订了关税及贸易总协定，参加的有美、英、法、印度、印度尼西亚、巴基斯坦、缅甸、西德、加拿大、巴西、智利等三十七个国家。瑞士为临时会员国。该组织的最高权力机构是会员国大会。从1947年以来，共举行了十五次大会和四次关税会议。
　　这个协定名义上规定参与国互相减低关税，并在彼此间进行贸易时应用“最惠国”原则，但实际上美国一直向各缔约国的关税壁垒、尤其是向英国的帝国优惠制实行进攻。它一面实行高关税政策，百般限制别国货物的进口，一面则强迫各国减低关税，取消美货进口限制。因此，十余年来，该组织虽在降低关税方面取得一些妥协，但并未能缓和资本主义国家间争夺市场的矛盾，也未能消除各国的贸易壁垒。1953年9月，这个组织召开的第八次大会，就曾通过一项决议，要求美国“注意”它的“限制措施对国际贸易关系造成的有害影响”，并停止其限制入口的措施。1954年第九次大会讨论进口限额问题时，由于美国坚持它根据农业调整法案所采取的农产品进口限制，结果大会准许美国继续施行这些无理限制，而对其他国家的农产品限额则加紧控制。但在最近召开的第十五届大会上，美国同其他资本主义国家在贸易上的矛盾更加尖锐了，许多国家要求美国放弃美国的贸易歧视政策。　（静）

General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade Organization

The General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade Organization is a tool used by the United States to expand its market and打击 [dǎ jī: strike; hit; attack; crack down on] the foreign trade of other capitalist countries.

When the Preparatory Committee of the United Nations International Trade Organization held its second meeting in 1947, the twenty-three countries participating in the meeting concluded one hundred and twenty-three bilateral tariff reduction agreements. On October 30 of the same year, the General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade was signed in Geneva, with the participation of thirty-seven countries, including the United States, Britain, France, India, Indonesia, Pakistan, Burma, West Germany, Canada, Brazil, and Chile. Switzerland was a temporary member. The highest authority of the organization is the General Assembly of member states. Since 1947, a total of fifteen general assemblies and four tariff conferences have been held.

This agreement nominally stipulates that participating countries should mutually reduce tariffs and apply the "most-favored-nation" principle in trade with each other, but in reality, the United States has always launched attacks on the tariff barriers of various contracting countries, especially the British Imperial Preference system. On the one hand, it implements high tariff policies, doing everything possible to restrict the import of goods from other countries, and on the other hand, it forces countries to reduce tariffs and abolish restrictions on the import of American goods. Therefore, although the organization has achieved some compromises in reducing tariffs over the past ten years, it has not been able to缓和 [huǎn hé: to ease; to alleviate; to moderate; to allay; to reconcile] the contradictions between capitalist countries in the competition for markets, nor has it been able to eliminate the trade barriers of various countries. In September 1953, the eighth General Assembly of the organization adopted a resolution requiring the United States to "pay attention" to the "harmful effects of its restrictive measures on international trade relations" and to stop its import restrictions. When the ninth General Assembly discussed the issue of import quotas in 1954, because the United States insisted on the agricultural product import restrictions it adopted based on the Agricultural Adjustment Act, the General Assembly allowed the United States to continue to implement these unreasonable restrictions, while tightening control over agricultural product quotas of other countries. However, at the recently held fifteenth General Assembly, the contradictions between the United States and other capitalist countries in trade have become more acute, and many countries have demanded that the United States abandon its trade discrimination policies. (Jing)
