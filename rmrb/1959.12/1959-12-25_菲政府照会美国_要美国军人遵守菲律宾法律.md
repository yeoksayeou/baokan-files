Title: Philippine Government Sends Note to the U.S., Demanding U.S. Military Personnel Obey Philippine Laws
Author:
Date: 1959-12-25
Page: 5

According to a report in the "Manila Chronicle" on the 22nd, the Philippine Foreign Ministry, in a note to the U.S. Embassy in the Philippines on the 21st, pointed out that U.S. authorities have an obligation to ensure that U.S. military personnel and civilians stationed in the Philippines comply with Philippine laws and regulations within military bases.

According to reports, the Philippine authorities issued this note because the U.S. Thirteenth Air Force at Clark Air Base refused to directly transfer to the Philippine authorities smuggled diamonds worth 63,000 pesos that the U.S. authorities at Clark Air Base confiscated from an American pilot in April of this year. The U.S. Air Force authorities attempted to first hand these diamonds back to the American, thereby turning the transfer into a private matter between the smuggler and the Philippine government. Philippine Foreign Minister Serrano believes that this violates the principles of the Philippine government.

According to reports, the Philippine authorities stated in the note that the Philippine government intends to enforce all applicable laws and regulations within U.S. military bases.

### 菲政府照会美国  要美国军人遵守菲律宾法律

1959-12-25
第5版()
专栏：

　　菲政府照会美国
    要美国军人遵守菲律宾法律
    据“马尼拉纪事报”22日报道，菲律宾外交部在21日给美国驻菲律宾大使馆的照会中指出，美国当局有义务保证美国驻菲律宾的军事人员和平民在军事基地范围内遵守菲律宾的法律和条例。
    据报道，菲律宾当局提出这一照会，是由于在克拉克基地的美国第十三航空队拒绝把克拉克基地美国当局今年4月从一名美国飞行员那里没收的价值六万三千比索的走私钻石直接移交给菲律宾当局。美国空军当局企图首先把这些钻石交给这个美国人，从而把移交的事变为走私者和菲律宾政府之间的私事。菲律宾外交部长塞兰诺认为，这是违反菲律宾政府的原则的。
    据报道，菲律宾当局在照会中表示，菲律宾政府打算在美国军事基地内执行所有适用的法律和条例。
