Title: Soviet Union Makes First Batch of Artificial Plastic Semiconductors; Abundant Raw Materials, Easy to Process, Will Supply Industry Infinitely; The Party and Government of the Soviet Union Congratulate the Builders of the "Lenin" Atomic Icebreaker

Author: 
Date: 1959-12-22
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, Moscow, December 21st -- TASS reported: The world's first semiconductors made from organic polymer materials have recently been successfully tested at the Institute of Petrochemical Synthesis of the Soviet Academy of Sciences.
    This is one of the most significant achievements of Soviet science this year.
    Manufacturing artificial plastic semiconductors with the same performance as germanium and silicon semiconductors, which are already widely used in technology, has always been the ideal of scientists. Academician Semyonov, a Nobel Prize winner, once compared this problem to the problem of manufacturing artificial meat. Scientists in the United States, Britain, and other countries have long been trying to create such semiconductors, but have never succeeded.
    According to expert comments, semiconductors made of polymers are more stable and easier to process than ordinary semiconductors. Since these semiconductors can be manufactured from natural gas and petroleum cracking products, the use of semiconductors in industry will be supplied in unlimited quantities.
    The first batch of polymer semiconductors manufactured by Soviet scientists is polyacrylonitrile. It is a well-known wool substitute. It produces the same ability to conduct current as germanium and silicon under low radiation of four and a half million roentgens.
    Combining polyacrylonitrile with a silicon-containing organic polymer can create another type of organic semiconductor.
    Currently, Soviet scientists are continuing further research on new theoretical foundations.

    Xinhua News Agency, Moscow, December 21st -- TASS reported: The "Lenin," the world's first atomic icebreaker, has successfully completed its trial voyage and has officially joined the fleet for Arctic navigation. The Central Committee of the Communist Party of the Soviet Union and the Council of Ministers of the Soviet Union congratulated the scientists, designers, engineers, technicians, and workers who participated in the construction of this icebreaker on the 19th.
    The congratulatory message stated that the successful construction of the atomic icebreaker created new conditions for the Soviet Union to develop Arctic resources and further develop the national economy of the northern regions of the Soviet Union.
    The relevant Soviet government departments also issued a communique on the results of the "Lenin" trial voyage.
    The communique pointed out that the "Lenin" atomic icebreaker has high navigation performance.

### 苏联制成第一批人造塑料半导体  原料丰富、处理容易、将能无限地供应工业使用  苏联党和政府祝贺“列宁号”原子破冰船建造者

1959-12-22
第5版()
专栏：

　　苏联制成第一批人造塑料半导体
    原料丰富、处理容易、将能无限地供应工业使用
    苏联党和政府祝贺“列宁号”原子破冰船建造者
    新华社莫斯科21日电　据塔斯社报道：世界上第一批用有机聚合物质制造的半导体，最近已经在苏联科学院石油化学合成物研究所试验成功。
   这是苏联科学今年最重大的成就之一。
    制造能够同在技术上已经广泛使用的锗和硅的半导体有同样性能的人造塑料半导体，一直是科学家的理想。诺贝尔奖金获得者谢苗诺夫院士曾经把这个问题同制造人造肉的问题并列。美、英等国的科学家长期来都在企图创造这种半导体，但始终没有成功。
    根据专家的评论，用聚合物制造的半导体，比一般的半导体更稳定，处理起来更容易。由于这种半导体可以用天然气和石油裂化产品制造，因此，在工业中半导体的使用，将得到无限量的供应。
    苏联科学家制造的第一批聚合物半导体是聚丙烯睛。它是一种著名的羊毛代用品。它在四百五十万伦琴低辐射作用的情况下，便产生了同锗和硅一样的能导引电流的性能。
    把聚丙烯睛同一种含硅的有机聚合物相结合，又可以制造另一种有机半导体。
    目前，苏联科学家正在新的理论基础上，继续作进一步的研究。
    据新华社莫斯科21日电　据塔斯社报道：世界上第一艘原子破冰船“列宁号”试航胜利结束，已经正式参加船队在北极开航。苏联共产党中央委员会和苏联部长会议19日向参加建造这艘破冰船的科学家、设计师、工程师、技术员和工人表示祝贺。
    贺词中说，原子破冰船的建造成功为苏联开发北极资源和进一步发展苏联北部地区的国民经济创造了新的条件。
    苏联政府有关部门还发表了关于“列宁号”试航结果的公报。
    公报指出，“列宁号”原子破冰船具有高度的航行性能。
