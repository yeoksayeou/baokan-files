Title: Soviet and U.S. Foreign Ministers Agree to Resume Strategic Nuclear Weapons Talks in June
Author: Xǔ Hóngzhì
Date: 1989-05-11
Page: 3

Xinhua News Agency, Berlin, May 11th.

Soviet Foreign Minister Eduard Shevardnadze and U.S. Secretary of State James Baker agreed on May 10th in Moscow to resume nuclear weapons and space weapons talks in Geneva in mid-June.

Soviet Foreign Ministry spokesman Gennady Gerasimov announced this news to the press yesterday evening. He said that Shevardnadze believed his talks with Baker were "complex, important, and responsible."

Local news agencies reported that the Soviet Union insists that both sides should adhere to the 1972 Anti-Ballistic Missile Treaty, but there are still differences in the interpretation of this treaty. The Soviet Union believes this is a matter of principle and that the treaty can only be narrowly interpreted. The U.S. side, however, believes that the treaty can be interpreted in various ways, including broadly.

This morning, Soviet Communist Party General Secretary Mikhail Gorbachev met with Baker, but as of noon today, the details of the talks have not been disclosed.

### 苏美外长商定  六月重开战略核武器谈判
许宏治
1989-05-11
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

　　苏美外长商定    
    六月重开战略核武器谈判
    本报柏林５月１１日电  记者许宏治报道：苏联外长谢瓦尔德纳泽和美国国务卿贝克５月１０日在莫斯科就今年６月中旬在日内瓦恢复核武器和太空武器谈判达成一致意见。
    苏联外交部发言人格拉西莫夫昨天晚上对新闻界宣布了这一消息。他说，谢瓦尔德纳泽认为，他同贝克的会谈是“复杂、重要和负责的。”
    此间新闻机构报道说，苏联坚持双方应遵守１９７２年签订的限制反弹道导弹条约，但双方在对这一条约的解释上仍有分歧。苏联认为这是原则问题，对条约只能作狭义解释。美国方面则认为可对条约作各种不同的解释，包括广义的解释。
    今天上午，苏共中央总书记戈尔巴乔夫会见了贝克，但到今天中午为止，会谈内容细节尚未透露。（附图片）
