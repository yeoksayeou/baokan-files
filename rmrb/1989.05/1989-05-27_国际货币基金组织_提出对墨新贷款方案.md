Title: The International Monetary Fund Proposes a New Loan Plan for Mexico
Author: Guo Weicheng
Date: 1989-05-27
Edition: 3

The International Monetary Fund has proposed a new loan plan for Mexico.

	According to a report by Mexico City Television, the International Monetary Fund will approve a new package of loans for Mexico tomorrow, totaling $420 million in financial aid. Officials from the organization explained that $360 million of this amount is a three-year loan, with 30% of it used to reduce debt. The remaining $60 million in loans will be used to offset Mexico's trade deficit.

	It is reported that the U.S. government today reminded international private banks that if they do not voluntarily forgive part of the debt of developing countries, they will face more embarrassing solutions.

	Next Monday, Mexico will negotiate with the Paris Club on the payment of a significant portion of its public debt.

	It is understood that the total amount proposed for negotiation is approximately $3.7 billion. Financial experts in the United States revealed today that Mexico's negotiations will have a relatively favorable condition for reaching an agreement.

### 国际货币基金组织  提出对墨新贷款方案
郭伟成
1989-05-27
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

　　国际货币基金组织
    提出对墨新贷款方案
    本报墨西哥城５月２５日电  记者郭伟成报道：国际货币基金组织再次提出对墨西哥的贷款方案。
    据墨西哥电视台报道，国际货币基金组织将于明天通过的对墨西哥新的一揽子贷款方案中给予墨西哥总数为４２亿美元的财政帮助。该组织的官员解释说，３６亿美元是３年期限的贷款，其中３０％用作缩减债务。其它６亿美元贷款将用作弥补墨西哥的出口逆差。
    据报道，美国政府今天提醒国际私人银行，如果不是自愿地免除发展中国家的部分债务，那将会面临更加难堪的解决办法。
    下星期一，墨西哥将与巴黎俱乐部谈判公共债务中的相当部分支付问题。
    据悉，提出谈判的总额约为３７０亿美元。美国金融界人士今天透露说，墨西哥的这一谈判会有一个较可能达成协议的条件。
