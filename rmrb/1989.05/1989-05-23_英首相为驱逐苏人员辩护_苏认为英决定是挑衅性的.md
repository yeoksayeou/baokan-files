Title: British Prime Minister Defends Expulsion of Soviet Personnel
Author:
Date: 1989-05-23
Page: 3

Xinhua News Agency, London, May 22nd.

British Prime Minister Margaret Thatcher today defended the decision to expel 11 Soviet diplomats and journalists from the UK.

Thatcher said the actions of these Soviet individuals in Britain were "completely unacceptable."

British Foreign Secretary Geoffrey Howe told reporters on the 21st that he had "concrete evidence" that these Soviet individuals had engaged in activities inconsistent with their diplomatic status. According to a BBC report on the 22nd, these activities included espionage related to projects crucial to the British economy.

The UK issued the order to expel the 11 Soviet diplomats and journalists on the 19th.

A spokesperson for the Soviet Embassy in the UK stated that the British decision was "unfriendly and provocative."

### 英首相为驱逐苏人员辩护  苏认为英决定是挑衅性的

1989-05-23
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

　　英首相为驱逐苏人员辩护
    苏认为英决定是挑衅性的
    新华社伦敦５月２２日电  英国首相撒切尔夫人今天为英国驱逐１１名苏联外交官和记者的决定进行辩护。
    撒切尔夫人说，这些苏联人在英国的所作所为是“完全不能接受的”。
    英国外交大臣杰弗里·豪２１日对记者说，他掌握有这些苏联人参与了与他们的身分不相符的活动的“确凿证据”。据英国广播公司２２日报道，这些苏联人参与的活动包括对与英国经济有关的项目从事间谍活动。
    英国是在１９日下令驱逐１１名苏联外交官和记者的。
    苏联驻英国大使馆的一位发言人说，英国的决定是“不友好的和挑衅性的”。
