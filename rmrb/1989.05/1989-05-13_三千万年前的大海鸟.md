Title: A Giant Seabird from 30 Million Years Ago
Author: Zhū Qǐ
Date: 1989-05-13
Page: 7

Recently, during the construction of an airport in Charles City, USA, workers discovered a batch of seabird fossils from 30 million years ago in the sandstone layers. These fossils are remarkably well-preserved. This seabird is similar to modern pelicans and cormorants. Scientists estimate that it weighed about 40 kilograms and had a wingspan of approximately 5.5 meters. In comparison, the largest flying bird in the world today, the albatross, has a wingspan of only 3.6 meters. Therefore, this giant seabird fossil is of great value for further research into the development and evolution of birds.

### 三千万年前的大海鸟
朱启
1989-05-13
第7版(国际副刊)
专栏：

　　三千万年前的大海鸟
    最近，美国查尔斯市在修建一座飞机场时，施工人员在沙岩层中发现了一批３０００万年前的大海鸟化石，保存得十分完整。这种海鸟与现在的鹈鹕和鸬鹚等鸟类十分相似。科学家们推测，它重约４０公斤，翼展约５．５米，而现在世界上最大的飞行鸟——信天翁的翼展只有３．６米，因此，这种大海鸟化石对进一步研究鸟类的发展和进化具有很大的价值。　　　　　　　　　·朱启·
