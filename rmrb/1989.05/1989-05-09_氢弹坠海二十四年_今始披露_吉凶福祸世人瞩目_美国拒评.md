Title: Hydrogen Bomb Lost at Sea for 24 Years, Now Revealed
Author:
Date: 1989-05-09
Page: 3

Xinhua News Agency, Washington, May 8th.

The U.S. government today refused to comment on a report that an American hydrogen bomb fell into the Pacific Ocean near Japan's Okinawa Island.

According to the latest issue of the U.S. magazine "Newsweek," on December 5, 1965, an A-4 Skyhawk attack aircraft carrying a hydrogen bomb fell from the U.S. aircraft carrier "Ticonderoga" into international waters 80 miles off Okinawa Island, sinking to a depth of 16,200 feet.

The magazine reported based on a report from the American Policy Research Institute. A U.S. military spokesman in Tokyo has confirmed the incident.

Japanese newspapers and American television stations reported the news today. Japanese people are outraged by the incident.

At a press conference, a reporter asked about the U.S. government's response to the report. White House spokesman Fitzwater said, "I haven't heard of this news."

However, the U.S. Greenpeace organization revealed at a press conference today that the U.S. Department of Defense had acknowledged such an incident in a 1981 report.

### 氢弹坠海二十四年　今始披露  吉凶福祸世人瞩目　美国拒评

1989-05-09
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

　　氢弹坠海二十四年　今始披露    
    吉凶福祸世人瞩目　美国拒评
    新华社华盛顿５月８日电  美国政府今天拒绝对一则关于有一颗美国氢弹掉在日本冲绳岛附近太平洋海域的报道作出评论。
    据最新一期美国《新闻周刊》报道，１９６５年１２月５日，一架Ａ—４型天鹰式攻击机连同一枚氢弹从美国“提康德罗加”号航空母舰上坠入距日本冲绳岛８０英里的国际海域，沉入离海面１６２００英尺的深度。
    该杂志是根据美国政策研究所的一份报告报道的。美国驻东京的一位军事发言人已证实确有此事。
    日本的报纸和美国电视台今天都对这一消息都作了报道。日本人对此事件表示愤慨。
    有记者在新闻发布会上问，美国政府对此报道有何评论？白宫发言人菲茨沃特说，“我没有听到这一消息”。
    但据美国绿色和平组织今天在记者招待会上透露，美国防部曾于１９８１年在一份报告中承认有这样的事件。
