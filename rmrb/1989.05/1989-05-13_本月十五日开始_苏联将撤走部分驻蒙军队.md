Title: Soviet Union to Begin Withdrawal of Part of Its Troops from Mongolia
Author:
Date: 1989-05-13
Edition: 1

Starting from the 15th of this month, the Soviet Union will begin withdrawing part of its troops from Mongolia.

According to the New China News Agency in Moscow on May 12, the Soviet Union today announced that it will begin withdrawing part of its troops from the Mongolian People's Republic starting from the 15th of this month.

According to TASS, the first units to be withdrawn will be tank and anti-aircraft missile troops.

Soviet Army Chief of Staff General Grigory Grinko said that during the period from 1989 to 1990, nearly 50,000 personnel, over 850 tanks, approximately 1,100 infantry fighting vehicles and armored personnel carriers, over 820 pieces of various artillery, around 190 aircraft, and 130 helicopters will be withdrawn from Mongolia. He hopes that this step taken by the Soviet Union will help to strengthen trust, good-neighborly relations, and peaceful cooperation in this region.

### 本月十五日开始  苏联将撤走部分驻蒙军队

1989-05-13
第1版(要闻)
专栏：

　　本月十五日开始    
    苏联将撤走部分驻蒙军队
    据新华社莫斯科５月１２日电  苏联今天宣布，从本月１５日开始从蒙古人民共和国撤走其部分军队。
    据塔斯社报道，首批撤出的是坦克和高射火箭部队。
    苏联陆军参谋长格林克维奇上将说，１９８９至１９９０年期间，从蒙古将共撤出近５万人，８５０多辆坦克，约１１００辆步兵战车和装甲车，８２０多门各种火炮，约１９０架飞机和１３０架直升机。他希望苏联采取的这一步骤有利于加强这个地区的信任、睦邻关系与和平合作。
