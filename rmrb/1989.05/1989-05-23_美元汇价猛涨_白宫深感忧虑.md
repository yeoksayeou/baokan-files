Title: Dollar Exchange Rate Surges, White House Expresses Concern
Author:
Date: 1989-05-23
Page: 1

The dollar exchange rate surged today, breaking through the 140 yen or 2 marks per dollar barrier. This rate has reportedly exceeded the "alert line," causing serious concern in the White House.

In the New York foreign exchange market, the dollar's exchange rate against the yen, mark, pound, and franc—the four major Western currencies—rose to 1:142.15, 1:2.012, 1:0.6342, and 1:6.81, respectively.

The White House issued a statement saying that this is a worrying issue. If the dollar continues to appreciate, it may undermine the efforts of various countries to correct global trade imbalances. The statement reaffirmed that the United States will uphold the agreement on coordinated economic policies reached by the Group of Seven.

### 美元汇价猛涨  白宫深感忧虑

1989-05-23
第1版(要闻)
专栏：

　　美元汇价猛涨
    白宫深感忧虑
    本报华盛顿５月２２日电  美元汇价今天猛涨，已突破１美元兑换１４０日元或２马克大关。据说这一汇率已超过“警戒线”，因而引起了白宫的严重不安。
    在纽约外汇市场，美元对日元、马克、英镑和法郎四种西方主要货币的比价分别升至１∶１４２．１５、１∶２．０１２、１∶０．６３４２和１∶６．８１。
    白宫就此发表声明说，这是一个令人忧虑的问题，如果美元继续升值，可能会损害世界各国为纠正全球贸易失衡状况所进行的努力。声明重申，美国将信守西方七国集团达成的协调经济政策的协议。
