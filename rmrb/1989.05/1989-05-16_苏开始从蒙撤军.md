Title: The Soviet Union Begins Withdrawing Troops from Mongolia
Author:
Date: 1989-05-16
Page: 3

Xinhua News Agency, Moscow, May 15th.

The Soviet Union has begun withdrawing part of its troops from Mongolia. Soviet military spokesman Marklov announced this on the 15th. He said that from 1989 to 1990, the Soviet Union will withdraw 50,000 troops, over 850 tanks, and all of its air force from Mongolia, which accounts for 75% of the total Soviet troops stationed in Mongolia.

He added that in the future, at the request of Mongolia and based on the agreement between the Soviet Union and Mongolia, Soviet troops will completely withdraw from Mongolia.

Fateyevich, Deputy Director of the Asian Socialist Countries Department of the Soviet Foreign Ministry, told reporters that the improvement in Sino-Soviet relations and Sino-Mongolian relations has led to this change in the international and political situation in the region.

It is reported that on the 15th at noon, a gathering was held in Bulgan Province, Mongolia, to see off the first batch of personnel from the Soviet tank division that is withdrawing.

### 苏开始从蒙撤军

1989-05-16
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

　　苏开始从蒙撤军
    新华社莫斯科５月１５日电  苏军总参谋部新闻发言人马尔克洛夫１５日下午宣布，苏已于当天开始从蒙古撤回部分军队。
    马尔克洛夫在新闻发布会上说，１９８９至１９９０年期间苏联将从蒙古撤回５万人，８５０多辆坦克和全部空军力量，占苏驻蒙部队总人数的７５％。
    他说，将来有一天应蒙古的请求，在苏联和蒙古协定的基础上，苏联军队将全部撤出蒙古。
    苏联外交部亚洲社会主义国家局副局长法捷耶维奇对记者们说，现在苏中关系、蒙中关系都有了改善，苏联撤军是这一地区国际和政治形势变化的结果。
    据悉，１５日中午，在蒙古的布尔干省举行了集会，欢送即将撤回的苏军坦克师首批人员。
