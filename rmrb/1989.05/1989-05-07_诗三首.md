Three Poems
Hu Yaobang

I
The frost moon shines brightly in the courtyard,
A weak woman applies thick makeup and tunes her white zither.
A sudden sound at the window,
It must be a lone swan landing on the cold shore.

II
Worldly affairs rush by, each rising and falling,
The winds and clouds roar, many heroines emerge.
In the face of death, still writing with vigor,
You are a rare woman of extraordinary talent.

III
Changes in the world are commonplace,
Human joys and sorrows tug at the heart.
Who can steal the peaches of immortality?
Take pity on the fine writings of your loved one.

### 诗三首
胡耀邦
1989-05-07
第8版(副刊)
专栏：

　　诗三首
    胡耀邦
    一
    霜月皎皎到中庭，
    弱女浓妆理素琴。
    窗前嘎然一声响，
    料是孤鸿落寒汀。
    　　　　二
    世事匆匆各浮沉，
    风云叱咤多女英。
    死神面前犹奋笔，
    君是巾帼罕见人。
    　　　　三
    沧桑变化寻常事，
    人间悲欢最牵魂。
    谁能偷得蟠桃果，
    怜取卿卿锦绣文。
    　　一九八七年九月二十五日
