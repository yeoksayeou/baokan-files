Title: The United States Focuses on India's Medium-Range Missile Test
Author:
Date: 1989-05-23
Page: 3

Xinhua News Agency, Washington, May 22nd.

The United States government has expressed concern over India's test launch of its first medium-range missile today, according to White House Deputy Spokesperson Popaduk.

Popaduk stated that the U.S. government has conveyed to the Indian government that the further proliferation of intercontinental ballistic missiles in South Asia will exacerbate instability in the region.

India successfully launched its first ground-to-ground medium-range missile today. The missile weighs 7.5 tons and has a range of 2,500 kilometers.

		May 22nd, India's first "Agni" ground-to-ground medium-range missile was launched from the Chandipur launch site in Orissa State (left) and fell into the Bay of Bengal. This missile has a range of 2,500 kilometers. (Photo courtesy of Xinhua News Agency)

### 美国关注印度试射中导

1989-05-23
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

　　美国关注印度试射中导
    新华社华盛顿５月２２日电  美国白宫副发言人波帕杜克今天说，美国政府对印度今天试验发射第一枚中程导弹一事表示关注。
    波帕杜克说，美国政府已向印度政府表明，洲际导弹在南亚的进一步扩散意味着该地区不稳定因素的进一步发展。
    印度今天成功地发射了第一枚地对地中程导弹。这枚导弹重７．５吨，射程为２５００公里。
    （附图片）
    五月二十二日，印度第一枚“火”式地对地中程导弹在奥里萨邦的昌迪普尔发射场（左）发射后，落入孟加拉湾。这枚导弹射程为二千五百公里。新华社发（传真照片）
