Title: The Old Man on the Seawall
Author: Sūn Xīn Chén
Date: 1989-05-22
Edition: 8

The winter sun
Is like your affectionate gaze
Sagebrush, wormwood, reed grass
And the fluttering reed flowers
The vast mudflats of the western Yellow Sea
A patch of withered yellow
Spread out beneath your feet
The seawall is a height
That lets you gaze into the distance
Cranes, little swans, egrets
Or perhaps the silver sails
White spirits
Remind you of your hometown in June
The scorching sun like fire
Remind you of the magpie nest in the dense shade
That patch of green chirping and chattering
The breeze between you and the saplings
Between the shovel and your arms
Carries the scent of new earth
(What a pleasant scent it is!)
You smooth back the white hair on your forehead
While sweat flows over your wrinkles
Like tree rings
Behind you is the silent land
In front of you is the excited sea
The winding seawall
Will find the green spring
Guided by the trees

### 海堤上的老人
孙昕晨
1989-05-22
第8版(副刊)
专栏：

　　海堤上的老人
    孙昕晨
    冬日的阳光
    如你温情的眸子
    盐蒿、苦艾、茅草
    还有翻飞的芦花
    西黄海广阔的滩涂
    一片枯黄
    铺在你的脚下
    海堤是一种高度
    使你极目远眺
    丹顶鹤、小天鹅、鹭鸶
    或者是点点银帆
    白色的精灵
    让你想起家乡六月
    炽热如火的骄阳
    想起浓荫里的喜鹊窝
    那一片叽叽喳喳的绿色
    微风在你和树苗之间
    在铁锹和手臂之间
    传送着新土的气息
    （一种很好闻的气息啊）
    你捋了捋额上的白发
    而汗水正流过你的皱纹
    仿佛年轮
    身后是沉默的陆地
    前方是激动的大海
    蜿蜒的海堤啊
    会在树的指引下
    找到碧绿的春天
