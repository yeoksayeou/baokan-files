Title: Escalation of the Anglo-Soviet Expulsion War
Author: Chén Tè'ān
Date: 1989-05-24
Page: 3

The escalation of the "spy" dispute between Britain and the Soviet Union is leading to a "war of expulsions."

After the Soviet Union retaliated on the 19th by expelling 8 British diplomats and 3 journalists, yesterday it further demanded that the number of employees of the British Embassy and media outlets in the Soviet Union be halved, equalizing it with the number of Soviet personnel allowed to work in British institutions. This could mean that approximately 170 British citizens will have to leave the Soviet Union, and the number of Soviet employees at the British Embassy will be reduced from over 200 to 65.

The British Foreign Office today rejected the Soviet Union's aforementioned demand, calling it absurd.

### 英苏驱逐战升级
陈特安
1989-05-24
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

　　英苏驱逐战升级
    本报伦敦５月２３日电  记者陈特安报道：英苏间由“间谍”问题争吵引起的“驱逐”战正在逐步升级。
    苏联继１９日“以牙还牙”宣布驱逐英８名外交官和３名新闻记者后，昨天又要求限制英驻苏使馆和传播媒介雇员的人数，使其削减一半，同苏联被允许在其驻英机构工作的人数相等。这可能意味着将有约１７０个英国人不得不离开苏联，意味着英驻苏使馆的苏联雇员将从２００多人减至６５人。
    英国外交部今天拒绝了苏联的上述要求，并称苏联的这一要求是荒谬的。
