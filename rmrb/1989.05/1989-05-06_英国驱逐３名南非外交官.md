Title: Britain Expels 3 South African Diplomats
Author: Chen Tiezhen
Date: 1989-05-06
Page: 3

British Foreign Office orders the expulsion of 3 South African diplomats involved in the theft of British missile secrets. They must leave the country within a week.

Yesterday, the British Foreign Office notified the South African ambassador to the UK that the British government is seriously concerned about the involvement of South African diplomats in a Northern Ireland arms deal.

According to informed sources within the Foreign Office, the British government believes that the South African authorities were involved in this arms deal, which aimed to steal British missile secrets, under extremely secretive conditions. They said it is inconceivable that South African diplomats would get involved in the incident without notifying Pretoria.

Despite South African President P.W. Botha sending a letter of apology to British Prime Minister Margaret Thatcher, the British government still decided to take this action, expelling a first secretary, a counselor, and a technical officer from the South African embassy in the UK.

### 英国驱逐３名南非外交官
陈特安
1989-05-06
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

　　英国驱逐３名南非外交官
    本报伦敦５月６日电  记者陈特安报道：英国外交部昨天下令驱逐３名与窃取英国导弹机密有关的南非外交官，他们必须在一周内离开英国。
    昨天，英外交部通知南非驻英大使，英政府对南非驻英使馆外交官卷入一起北爱尔兰军备交易事件表示严重关切。
    据外交部消息灵通人士说，英国政府认为南非当局是在一种非常秘密的情况下卷入这起旨在窃取英国导弹机密的军备交易事件的。他们说，南非外交代表没有通知比勒陀利亚就卷入事件是不可思议的。
    尽管南非总统博塔致信英国首相撒切尔夫人，对此表示道歉，但英国政府还是决定采取这一行动，把南非驻英使馆一名一等秘书、一名参赞和一名技术官员驱逐出境。
