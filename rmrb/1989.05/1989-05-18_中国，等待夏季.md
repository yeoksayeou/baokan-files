Title: China, Awaiting Summer
Author: Lan Kuang
Date: 1989-05-18
Page: 8

The sun bears witness,
The world bears witness.
We sow the seeds of our aspirations,
The pursuit of our nation,
Ideals and grand beliefs.
China, awaiting summer.

The young shoots,
With an unprecedented major chord,
Compose a new season.
Even if the wind and snow in the sky
Strangle the land and the green theme,
The lonely roots sing unceasingly.
China, awaiting summer.

A great sun,
Rises from the river in the east,
The spring of national belief
Breaks through the shattered ice,
Illuminating the persistent ridges
And the thoughts we sow on the earth.
China, awaiting summer.

Awaiting summer,
To arrive at the feet of the sowers,
Holding the golden sickles,
Sweating profusely in the fields,
Harvesting a bountiful yield,
Writing the century of China on the earth.
China, awaiting summer.
