Title: Gene Transplantation Successful in Cancer Treatment Trials
Author: Liú Tiānchén
Date: 1989-05-29
Page: 3

In a cancer treatment trial, American scientists successfully transplanted non-human genes into a human body.

The People's Daily, New York, May 23rd - Reporter Liu Kai Chen reports: Scientists from the U.S. National Institutes of Health announced on the 22nd that they had successfully transplanted non-human genes into a human body for the first time in a cancer treatment trial.

In a medical center in Bethesda, Maryland, Dr. Rosenberg's team injected a patient with cancer with half a liter of milky liquid over 30 minutes. The liquid contained approximately 100 billion cancer-fighting cells with specially marked genes. These genes are used to monitor the penetration and effectiveness of the cancer-fighting cells in reaching the tumor.

Dr. Rosenberg stated that the purpose of their experiment is to design cells with greater anti-cancer capabilities.

### 在治疗癌症试验中  美国移植基因成功
刘天宸
1989-05-29
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

　　在治疗癌症试验中
    美国移植基因成功
    本报纽约５月２３日电  记者刘开宸报道：美国国家卫生研究所科学家们２２日首次成功地把非人的基因移植到人体内进行治癌试验。
    在马里兰州贝塞斯达城的一个医疗中心内，罗森伯格博士的小组向一位癌症病人，用３０分钟时间注射半公升的乳白色溶液，其中含有约１０００亿个带有专门标志基因的抗癌细胞，这种基因用于监测抗癌细胞渗透进肿瘤的途径和能力。
    罗森伯格博士说，他们的实验目的是为了设计出更大抗癌能力的细胞。
