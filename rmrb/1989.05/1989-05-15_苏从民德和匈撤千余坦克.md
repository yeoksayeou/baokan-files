Title: Soviet Union Withdraws Over a Thousand Tanks from East Germany and Hungary
Author:
Date: 1989-05-15
Edition: 3

Soviet Union Withdraws Over a Thousand Tanks from East Germany and Hungary

	New China News Agency, Moscow, May 14th: The first deputy chief of the Soviet General Staff, General Omelichev, announced in the Red Star newspaper on the 14th that over a thousand tanks have been withdrawn from East Germany back to the Soviet Union so far.

	He also mentioned that approximately 200 tanks have been withdrawn from Hungary.

	He stated that three airborne assault battalions will be withdrawn from the Soviet troops stationed in East Germany and the Central Group of Forces.

	In February of this year, Soviet Defense Minister Yazov announced that by August 15th of this year, the Soviet Union will reduce three tank divisions, two of which are stationed in East Germany and one in Hungary.

### 苏从民德和匈撤千余坦克

1989-05-15
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

　　苏从民德和匈撤千余坦克
    新华社莫斯科５月１４日电  苏军第一副总参谋长奥梅利切夫上将１４日在《红星报》上宣布，至今已有１０００多辆坦克从民主德国撤回苏联国内。
    他还说，大约２００辆坦克已从匈牙利撤回。
    他说，从苏军驻民主德国集群和中部集群中将撤出３个空降强击营。
    今年２月，苏联国防部长亚佐夫曾宣布，在今年８月１５日前，苏联将裁减３个坦克师，其中２个驻在民主德国，１个驻在匈牙利。
