Title: Former Leader of the Polish United Workers' Party, Edward Ochab, Passes Away
Author:
Date: 1989-05-03
Edition: 3

Former leader of the Polish United Workers' Party, Edward Ochab, passed away.

	Xinhua News Agency, Warsaw, May 2 — According to a report by the Polish News Agency on May 2, Edward Ochab, former First Secretary of the Central Committee of the Polish United Workers' Party, passed away in Warsaw on May 1 due to long-term illness and ineffective treatment. He was 83 years old.

	Ochab was involved in revolutionary activities at an early age and was imprisoned multiple times. He joined the Polish Communist Party in 1929 and held leadership positions within the party for many years. On March 20, 1956, after the death of Bolesław Bierut, the leader of the Polish United Workers' Party, in Moscow, Ochab was elected as the First Secretary of the Central Committee of the Polish United Workers' Party. In 1961, he took on the roles of Vice Chairman and Chairman of the Polish Council of State, retiring in 1968.

### 波党前领导人奥哈布逝世

1989-05-03
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

　　波党前领导人奥哈布逝世
    新华社华沙５月２日电  据波通社２日报道，波兰统一工人党中央前第一书记爱·奥哈布因长期患病，医治无效，于５月１日在华沙逝世，享年８３岁。
    奥哈布早年投身革命活动，曾多次被捕入狱。他于１９２９年加入波兰共产党，在党内长期担任领导工作。１９５６年３月２０日，波兰统一工人党领导人博·贝鲁特在莫斯科逝世后，奥哈布被选为波兰统一工人党中央第一书记。１９６１年，他改任波兰国务委员会副主席、主席等职务，于１９６８年退休。
