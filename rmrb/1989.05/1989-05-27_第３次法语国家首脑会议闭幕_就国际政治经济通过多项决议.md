Title: The Third Summit of French-Speaking Countries Concludes
Author:
Date: 1989-05-27
Page: 3

Xinhua News Agency, Dakar, May 26th

The third summit of French-speaking countries concluded today after three days of discussions. Senegalese President Diouf, speaking at a press conference following the closing of the summit, described the meeting as one that strengthened, expanded, and matured, deeply resonating with the people of Africa. The attendees extensively discussed international political and economic situations, agriculture, environment, energy, culture, communication, language, scientific information, technological development, education, training, and enhancing cooperation among French-speaking countries.

The summit adopted 15 resolutions on issues such as Southern Africa, Namibia, Angola, the Middle East, Lebanon, and the environment, as well as the "Dakar Declaration."

The participants decided that the next summit will be held in 1991 in Kinshasa, the capital of Zaire.

### 第３次法语国家首脑会议闭幕　就国际政治经济通过多项决议

1989-05-27
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

　　第３次法语国家首脑会议闭幕
  　就国际政治经济通过多项决议
    新华社达喀尔５月２６日电  历时３天的第３次法语国家首脑会议今天在这里闭幕。
    塞内加尔总统迪乌夫在会议闭幕后举行的记者招待会上说，这次首脑会议是一次巩固、扩大、成熟和在非洲土地上深入人心的首脑会议，与会者广泛地讨论了国际政治和经济形势、农业、环境、能源、文化、通讯、语言、科学情报、工艺发展、教育、培训以及加强法语国家间的合作等问题。
    会议通过了关于南部非洲、纳米比亚、安哥拉、中东、黎巴嫩和环境等１５项决议以及一项《达喀尔宣言》。
    与会者决定下次首脑会议将于１９９１年在扎伊尔首都金沙萨召开。
