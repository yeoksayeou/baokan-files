Title: Two Trains Collide in Portugal, 10 Dead, 50 Injured
Author:
Date: 1989-05-08
Edition: 3

Two trains collided in Portugal on May 7 in the central region of Sacavém, resulting in 10 deaths and 50 injuries.

According to local newspapers, the incident involved a passenger train and a freight train carrying explosives and chemicals. After the collision, several passenger carriages caught fire.

Following the accident, hundreds of firefighters, Red Cross workers, local military personnel, and police from Sacavém rushed to the scene. They quickly extinguished the fire in the carriages and provided appropriate care for the deceased and the injured. Fortunately, the fire did not cause the explosives on the freight train to detonate, thus preventing a larger disaster. The cause of the accident is still under investigation.

### 葡萄牙两列火车相撞  十人死亡　五十人受伤

1989-05-08
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

　　葡萄牙两列火车相撞     
    十人死亡　五十人受伤
    新华社里斯本５月８日电  葡萄牙两列火车７日在葡中部的萨卡文相撞，造成１０人死亡，５０人受伤。
    据当地报纸报道，出事的是一列载有旅客的客车和一列满载爆炸品与化学品的货车。两车相撞后，部分客车车厢起火燃烧。
    事故发生后，萨卡文地方消防队、红十字会及当地驻军和警察数百人迅速赶往事故现场，及时扑灭了车厢大火，并把死难者和伤员进行了妥善安置。由于大火未引起货车上的易爆品爆炸，因而避免了一次更大的灾难。事故原因还在调查之中。
