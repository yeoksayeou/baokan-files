Title: India Successfully Launches First Medium-Range Missile
Author:
Date: 1989-05-22
Page: 3

Xinhua News Agency, New Delhi, May 22nd.

India successfully tested its first medium-range missile today. The missile, named "Agni," has a maximum range of 2,500 kilometers.

According to the Indian News Agency, the medium-range missile was launched at 7:15 AM local time from a test site in Orissa State in eastern India and successfully landed in the waters of the Bay of Bengal between the Andaman Islands and Sri Lanka.

Prior to this, India had postponed the medium-range missile test twice, on April 20th and May 1st this year, due to technical issues.

This successful test makes India the seventh country in the world to possess medium-range missiles. The other six countries are the United States, the Soviet Union, the United Kingdom, France, China, and Israel.

### 印度成功发射首枚中导

1989-05-22
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

　　印度成功发射首枚中导
    新华社新德里５月２２日电  印度今天成功地试验了它的第一枚中程导弹。这枚称为“火”式的中程弹道导弹最大射程为２５００公里。
    据印度报业托拉斯报道，这枚中程导弹于当地时间７时１５分在印度东部的奥里萨邦的一个试验场发射后，成功地降落在印度安达曼群岛和斯里兰卡之间的孟加拉湾水域。
    在此之前，印度曾于今年４月２０日和５月１日两次因技术故障而推延了中程导弹发射试验。
    这次试验成功使得印度成为世界上第７个拥有中程导弹的国家。其它６个国家是美国、苏联、英国、法国、中国和以色列。
