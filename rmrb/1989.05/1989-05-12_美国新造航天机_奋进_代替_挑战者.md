Title: U.S. Builds New Spacecraft "Endeavour" to Replace "Challenger"
Author:
Date: 1989-05-12
Edition: 3

	The U.S. National Aeronautics and Space Administration announced on May 10 that U.S. President Bush has decided to name the new spacecraft under construction as "Endeavour."

	The "Endeavour" spacecraft will replace the "Challenger" spacecraft, which exploded after launch on January 28, 1986.

	The name "Endeavour" is taken from the name of the exploration ship that British explorer James Cook sailed on during his first Pacific expedition in the 18th century. During this voyage, James Cook mapped the eastern coast of Australia and discovered New Zealand.

	The new spacecraft, valued at $1.3 billion, is being built by the Rockwell International Corporation in the United States. If all goes well, the new spacecraft is scheduled to make its first flight in 1992.

### 美国新造航天机  “奋进”代替“挑战者”

1989-05-12
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

　　美国新造航天机  “奋进”代替“挑战者”
    新华社华盛顿５月１１日电  美国宇航局５月１０日宣布，美国总统布什已经决定，将正在建造的新的航天飞机命名为“奋进”号。
    “奋进”号航天飞机将替代１９８６年１月２８日升空后爆炸的“挑战者”号航天飞机。
    “奋进”号是取自１８世纪英国探险家詹姆斯·库克在他的第一次太平洋探险中乘坐的探险船的名字。在这次远征中，詹姆斯·库克绘制了澳大利亚东海岸的地图，并且发现了新西兰。
    这架价值１３亿美元的新航天飞机正在由美国的罗克韦尔国际公司建造，如果一切顺利的话，新航天飞机将于１９９２年进行首次飞行。
