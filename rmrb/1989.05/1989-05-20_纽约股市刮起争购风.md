Title: A Buying Frenzy Sweeps the New York Stock Market
Author: Zhang Liang
Date: 1989-05-20
Edition: 3

A buying frenzy swept the New York Stock Exchange today. The Dow Jones Industrial Average soared by 30.98 points, reaching 2501.10 points, setting a new record since the "Black Monday" of October 19, 1987. The trading volume was 242.2 million shares. Statistics show that over the past eight days, the index has risen by approximately 130 points, an increase of 5.5%.

Wall Street analysts believe that the continuous rise in stock prices is due to the slowing down of the U.S. economic growth rate. Investors generally expect the Federal Reserve to ease monetary policy and lower interest rates to achieve a "soft landing" for the U.S. economy, avoiding a recession in the near term.

### 纽约股市刮起争购风
张亮
1989-05-20
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

　　纽约股市刮起争购风
    本报华盛顿５月２０日电  本报记者张亮报道：今天纽约证券交易所再次出现争购风，道·琼斯３０种工业股票平均价格指数猛升３０．９８点，达２５０１．１０点，又创１９８７年１０月１９日“黑色星期一”以来的最高纪录，成交额为２．４２２亿股。据统计，在过去的８天中，指数已上涨大约１３０点，增长幅度为５．５％。
    华尔街的股市分析家认为，连日来股票价格扶摇直上的原因是，由于美国经济的增长速度放慢，投资者们普遍估计联邦储备委员会将会放松银根、降低利率，以使美国经济“软着陆”，而不会在近期内发生衰退。
