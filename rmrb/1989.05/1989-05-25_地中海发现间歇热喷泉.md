Title: Intermittent Hot Springs Discovered in the Mediterranean
Author:
Date: 1989-05-25
Edition: 3

	American scientists have discovered an area of intermittent hot springs on the seabed of the Mediterranean Sea, based on photographs taken by an unmanned submarine. The hot springs are located 177 kilometers southwest of Naples.

	According to the New York Times, scientists from the Woods Hole Oceanographic Institution in Massachusetts found that the intermittent hot spring area is situated on the top of an underwater volcano. The volcano is 1,524 meters high, with its peak 610 meters below the sea surface. The intermittent hot spring area is at least 91.4 meters long and 27 meters wide. Despite a fissure on the seabed, the arrangement of the intermittent hot springs is quite orderly.

### 地中海发现间歇热喷泉

1989-05-25
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

　　地中海发现间歇热喷泉
    新华社华盛顿电  美国科学家根据一艘机器人潜艇在地中海海底拍摄的照片发现，距离那不勒斯西南１７７公里的地中海海底有一处间歇性热喷泉区。
    据《纽约时报》报道，马萨诸塞的伍兹·霍尔海洋地理学研究所的科学家发现，这个间歇热喷泉区位于一个海底火山顶部。该火山高１５２４米，顶部距离海面６１０米。间歇热喷泉区至少有９１．４米长，２７米宽。尽管海底有一裂缝，间歇热喷泉的排列还是很整齐的。
