Title: Conditions for Nobel Prize Winners
Author:
Date: 1989-05-27
Edition: 8

Nobel Prize winners have both subjective and objective conditions, according to Lars Jorell, chairman of the Nobel Foundation. He identifies three key factors:

First, in the field of the winner's discipline, there must be an exceptionally favorable academic environment.

Second, there must be a social environment that highly values science and literature, where scientists and writers can enjoy economic support and the appropriate social status.

Third, there must be a dedication to one's career, a rich imagination, and a high degree of diligence.

(Source: Issue 4, 1989, People's Pictorial)

### 诺贝尔奖获得者的主客观条件

1989-05-27
第8版(每周文摘)
专栏：

　　诺贝尔奖
    获得者的主客观条件
    诺贝尔基金会董事长拉斯·杰伦斯坦，谈到诺贝尔奖获奖者的主观与客观条件有三：
    第一，在获奖者的学科领域内，需要有一个得天独厚的学术环境；
    第二，需有一个高度重视科学与文学的社会条件，科学家与作家能享有经济上的资助及应有的社会地位；
    第三，对自己事业的献身精神，富有创造性的想象力及高度的勤奋。
    　　　　（１９８９年第４期《人民画报》）
