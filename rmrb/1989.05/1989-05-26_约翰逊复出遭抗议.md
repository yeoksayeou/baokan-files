Title: Johnson's Return Met with Protest
Author: Yang Yaowu
Date: 1989-05-26
Edition: 4

Canadian sprinter Ben Johnson, who was disgraced at the Seoul Olympics, recently appeared at a cycling event in San Sebastian, Spain. The organizers invited him to serve as the honorary starter and to present awards to the winning athletes. For this, Johnson received $48,000 and a gift—a Basque beret.

Johnson told reporters, "I have resumed training and hope to compete with Carl Lewis in future international events to defeat him. I will participate in the 1992 Olympics."

Johnson's actions immediately drew protests from international doping control expert Professor Manfred Donike. Donike wrote to the president of the International Cycling Union, the Spaniard Luis Puig, stating: "Allowing Johnson to start the race is truly unpleasant. This will harm the reputation of cycling."

### 约翰逊复出遭抗议
杨要武
1989-05-26
第4版(文化生活·体育)
专栏：国际体坛

　　约翰逊复出遭抗议
    汉城奥运会上出丑的加拿大短跑运动员本·约翰逊，最近在西班牙圣巴斯蒂安举行的赛车场自行车比赛上公开露面，组织者让他作为名誉发令员，并为获胜运动员颁奖。为此，约翰逊得到４．８万美元的酬劳和礼品——一顶巴斯克贝蕾帽。
    约翰逊对记者说，我已经恢复训练，希望能在以后的国际比赛上与卡尔·刘易斯同场较量，以打败他。我要参加１９９２年奥运会。
    约翰逊此举立即遭到国际兴奋剂检查专家曼弗雷特·多尼克教授的抗议。多尼克致函国际自行车运动联合会主席、西班牙人路易斯·普伊格，指出：让约翰逊为比赛发令，实在令人不快，这会不利于自行车运动的声誉。
        　杨要武
