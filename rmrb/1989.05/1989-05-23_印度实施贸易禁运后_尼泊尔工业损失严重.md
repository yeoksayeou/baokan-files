Title: Nepal's Industry Suffers Heavy Losses After India Imposes Trade Embargo
Author:
Date: 1989-05-23
Page: 3

Xinhua News Agency, Kathmandu, May 23rd.

Since India imposed a trade embargo on Nepal on March 23, Nepal's industrial production has suffered severe losses.

According to a report by the *Rising Nepal* today, Nepal's industrial output in April this year has dropped from the usual monthly figure of 1.7 billion rupees (63.4 million US dollars) to 850 million rupees (31.7 million US dollars). It is estimated that the output in May will drop to 680 million rupees (25.4 million US dollars) and in June to 510 million rupees (19 million US dollars). Therefore, it is unlikely that the target of 20.39 billion rupees (760.8 million US dollars) for the fiscal year (ending July 15 this year) will be met.

The report says that the industries that rely heavily on imports of petroleum products, coal, raw materials, and auxiliary materials from India have suffered the most severe losses.

### 印度实施贸易禁运后  尼泊尔工业损失严重

1989-05-23
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

　　印度实施贸易禁运后
    尼泊尔工业损失严重
    新华社加德满都５月２３日电  自３月２３日印度对尼泊尔实行贸易禁运以来，已给尼泊尔的工业生产造成严重损失。
    据《新兴尼泊尔报》今天报道，今年４月份，尼泊尔工业产值已由正常月份的１７亿卢比（６３４０万美元）下降到８．５亿卢比（３１７０万美元）。预计，５月份将下降到６．８亿卢比（２５４０万美元），６月份将下降到５．１亿卢比（１９００万美元）。因此，要完成本财政年度（今年７月１５日结束）２０３．９亿卢比（７．６０８亿美元）的预定指标希望很小。
    报道说，特别是那些主要依靠从印度进口的石油产品、煤、原材料和辅助原材料的工业部门遭受的损失最严重。
