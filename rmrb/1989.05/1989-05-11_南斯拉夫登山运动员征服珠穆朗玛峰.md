Title: Yugoslav Mountaineers Conquer Mount Everest
Author:
Date: 1989-05-11
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, Belgrade, May 10th.

Yugoslav mountaineers have successfully conquered the world's highest peak, Mount Everest, which stands at 8,848 meters above sea level.

According to a report by Tanjug, three Yugoslav mountaineers—36-year-old V. Grošelj, 37-year-old S. Božič, and 35-year-old D. Livjevski—reached the summit today. They ascended from the south side of the mountain, guided by two Sherpas.

The trio set off from the fourth camp at 7,985 meters at 4:30 AM local time and reached the summit after 15 hours of climbing.

### 南斯拉夫登山运动员征服珠穆朗玛峰

1989-05-11
第4版(文化生活·体育)
专栏：体育

　　南斯拉夫登山运动员征服珠穆朗玛峰
    新华社贝尔格莱德５月１０日电  据南通社报道，南斯拉夫３名登山运动员今天成功地登上世界最高峰——海拔８８４８米的珠穆朗玛峰。
    这３名登山运动员是３６岁的维·格罗舍利、３７岁的斯·博日奇和３５岁的迪·利维埃夫斯基。他们是在两名向导的带领下从南坡登上峰顶的。
    他们于今晨当地时间四点半从海拔７９８５米处的四号营地出发，用了整整１５个小时才登顶成功。
