Title: "Delhi Times" Weekly Indicates India Should Not Support Tibetan Reactionaries
Author:
Date: 1959-05-21
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, New Delhi, 19th May. The "Delhi Times" weekly published an article by its special correspondent in its May 17th issue.

	The author begins by pointing out, "The current struggle in Tibet is a struggle between progress and reaction."

	It states, "India's feudal reactionaries do not like reforms here, in Tibet, or anywhere else. Reform means their death. It is mainly them who have been clamoring about the Tibetan issue without wanting to understand the real reasons for the current turmoil."

	The article analyzes the social structure of Tibet and points out that the Chinese government is on the side of progress, "which actually means that 90% of the people support its position, and about 5% of the reactionaries oppose reform. Without outside agitation of the rebels, the reform would have been completed smoothly, bringing progress and happiness to the masses."

	The article continues, "As far as India is concerned, we must clearly state our position: Are we in favor of progress? Or are we in favor of maintaining the status quo, which means supporting a reactionary system? This reactionary system is extremely tyrannical for the common people. If we give moral support to those reactionaries who oppose reform, then we will put ourselves in a very difficult position from which it will be difficult to extricate ourselves."

	The article also says, "Merely criticizing the actions of the Chinese will only expose the emptiness of our position. In the current Tibetan dispute, what basic principles are we trying to maintain? Are we going to oppose the reforms necessary to free people from suffering? If we do so, it means that we will drastically lose our lofty moral standing in the world and stand on the side of reaction."

	The article states that China, India, and other sister countries in Asia are breaking the long-standing shackles that hinder their development and are in the process of national rebirth. The article says, "Since we do not help backward Tibet break the social and economic chains that oppress and suffocate it, let us not create new chains for the very poor and very backward people because of our misused sympathy."

### “德里时代”周报指出  印度不应支持西藏反动分子

1959-05-21
第5版()
专栏：

    “德里时代”周报指出
    印度不应支持西藏反动分子
    新华社新德里19日电　“德里时代”周报在5月17日出版的一期上刊载了这家报纸的特派记者写的一篇文章。
    作者一开始就指出，“目前在西藏的斗争是进步和反动之间的斗争”。
    它说，“印度的封建反动分子不喜欢在这里、西藏或者其它任何地方进行改革。改革意味着他们的死亡。一直在西藏事件上叫嚣不休而不想了解造成目前动乱的真正原因的，主要是他们。”
    文章分析了西藏的社会结构，并且指出，中国政府是站在进步的一边的，“这实际上意味着90％的人民支持它的立场，大约占5％的反动分子反对改革。如果没有外界对叛乱分子的鼓动，改革原会顺利地完成，而使人民大众获得进步和幸福”。
    文章接着说，“就印度而论，我们必须清楚无误地表明我们的立场：我们是赞成进步呢？还是赞成维持意味着支持反动制度的现状。这种反动制度对于老百姓来说是极端暴虐的。如果我们给反对改革的那些反动分子以道义上的支持，那么，我们将使自己陷入难以自拔的非常困难的境地。”
    文章又说，“单单指责中国人的行动，只会暴露我们立场的空虚。在目前西藏的纠纷中，什么是我们试图维护的基本原则？我们是否要反对为使人脱离苦难所必要的改革呢？如果我们这样做的话，就意味着我们将急剧地失去我们在世界上的崇高的道义地位，而站在反动的一边。”
    文章说，中国、印度和亚洲的其它姐妹国家正在打破阻碍它们发展壮大的长期存在的枷锁，并且正处在民族新生的过程中。文章说，“我们既然不帮助落后的西藏打破那些压迫和窒息它的社会和经济的锁链，那么，让我们也不要由于我们错用同情心而给十分贫困和十分落后的人民制造新的锁链。”
