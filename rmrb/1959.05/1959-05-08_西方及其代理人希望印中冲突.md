Title: The West and Its Agents Hope for Sino-Indian Conflict
Author: Kǎlǎngjíyà
Date: 1959-05-08
Page: 5
Outer Commentary Abstract

　　The West and Its Agents Hope for Sino-Indian Conflict
　　India Karangia
    Our Western benefactors are frantically dragging India into the Cold War by exploiting the Tibetan tragedy, using methods that are cruel and shameless.

    When I visited the Arab East, I encountered some Pakistanis and Americans in several bars and receptions, where, under the influence of alcohol, they unconsciously revealed many hidden thoughts.

    They almost unanimously stated that they anticipated a large-scale conflict between India and China might erupt if the Tibet issue could be dragged out until the end of the year.

    According to their subjective desires (or perhaps what is being planned?), this conflict would force Delhi to open a new front against China in addition to the existing front against Pakistan, increasing India's defense expenditure, which already accounts for half of the national budget, to an absurd extent.

    But how would this benefit the West or Pakistan? Here is a preliminary answer.

    The proposed second front against China would first weaken the existing front against Pakistan.

    This might help Washington engineer a Munich in the Kashmir issue between Delhi and Karachi, compromising on the basis of partitioning Kashmir or even establishing an independent Kashmir siding with Pakistan.

    The Americans do not even rule out the possibility that they could exploit the fear of Chinese aggression in the strategically significant Indian Ocean region to piece together India, Pakistan, Burma, Ceylon, and Nepal, as well as other Himalayan countries, into a NATO-CENTO-SEATO-style war bloc.

    Of course, Western countries and their Asian agents are considering this plan according to their subjective desires without consulting the main parties involved—Jawaharlal Nehru and the Indian people—who, due to historical necessity, must implement a policy of active neutrality and friendship with China, despite the fact that the developments in Tibet have greatly affected India's position.

    Western countries and their Asian agents only rely on their lackeys and servants, such as Tata-Masani and India's "socialists," who now find their natural allies among India's fascists and Hindu Mahasabha members.

    However, all of this should make those idiots who attacked the Chinese embassy and consulates in India reconsider, as they desperately try to force Nehru to break with China, but Nehru will not agree to this.

    Pakistan should award these gentlemen the Ayub Khan (Editor's note: President of Pakistan) Peace Prize.

    (Excerpt from the April 25th issue of the Indian weekly "Blitz," originally titled "Award the Ayub Khan Peace Prize to Our Tibetan Patriots")

### 西方及其代理人希望印中冲突
卡朗吉亚
1959-05-08
第5版()
专栏：外论摘要

　　西方及其代理人希望印中冲突
　　印度  卡朗吉亚
    我们西方的恩人们正在利用西藏悲剧疯狂地把印度拖进冷战中去，他们使用的方法是残酷的和无耻的。
    当我访问阿拉伯东方的时候，我在好些酒吧间和招待会上遇到了一些巴基斯坦人和美国人，在那里，由于酒后吐真言，他们下意识地道出了许多隐蔽的想法。
    他们几乎是异口同声地谈到，他们预料，如果西藏问题能够拖到年底的话，印度和中国之间可能爆发一次大规模的冲突。
    按照他们的主观愿望（或者说是正在策划中？），这种冲突将迫使德里在现有的对付巴基斯坦的战线以外再开辟一条反对中国的新战线，而使已占国家预算一半的印度国防开支增加到大得荒唐的程度。
    但是这件事将怎样使西方或巴基斯坦得到好处呢？下面是初步的答案。
    拟议中的对付中国的第二战线首先将削弱现有的对付巴基斯坦的战线。
    这可能帮助华盛顿在克什米尔问题上在德里和卡拉奇之间制造一个慕尼黑，在分治克什米尔或甚至在建立一个站在巴基斯坦一边的独立的克什米尔的基础上妥协。
    美国人甚至也不排斥这样的可能性：它能够利用人们担心中国侵略具有战略意义的印度洋地区的恐惧心理，把印度、巴基斯坦、缅甸、锡兰以及尼泊尔和其它喜马拉雅山国家拼凑起来成为一个北大西洋公约组织——中东防御组织——东南亚条约组织式样的战争集团。
    当然，西方国家和它们的亚洲代理人根据主观愿望考虑这种计划的时候是没有同主要有关方面——贾瓦哈拉尔·尼赫鲁和印度人民——商量的，他们由于历史的需要，必须执行一种积极的中立和同中国友好的政策，尽管西藏的事态发展使印度的立场受到很大的影响。
    西方国家和它们的亚洲代理人只依靠它们的走狗和仆从，如塔塔—马萨尼和印度的“社会主义者”，他们现在在印度的法西斯分子和印度教大会分子里找到他们的天然同盟者。
    然而，这一切应该使那些袭击中国在印度的大使馆和领事馆的白痴们再三考虑，他们拚命想迫使尼赫鲁同中国决裂，但是尼赫鲁是不会同意这种做法的。
    巴基斯坦应当给这些先生们颁发阿尤布汗（编者注：巴基斯坦总统）和平奖金。
    （摘自4月25日印度“闪电”周刊，原题为“赠给我国的西藏爱国者以阿尤布汗和平奖金”）
