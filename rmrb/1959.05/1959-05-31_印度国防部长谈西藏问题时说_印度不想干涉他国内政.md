Title: Indian Defense Minister Speaks on Tibet Issue: India Does Not Want to Interfere in Other Countries' Internal Affairs
Author:
Date: 1959-05-31
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, New Delhi, May 30th. According to a report in the "Statesman," Indian Defense Minister Menon spoke to students at the Chail Study Academy near Simla on May 29th. When discussing the Tibet issue, he said that India does not want to interfere in the internal affairs of any country.

When talking about Kashmir, he emphasized that Kashmir is an inseparable part of Indian territory.

On the issue of Goa, Menon said that no country would be allowed to establish military bases there.

Subsequently, Menon, speaking at a mass rally organized by the Simla City Committee of the Indian National Congress, called on the Indian people to unite and work diligently for India's social security and economic stability.

### 印度国防部长谈西藏问题时说  印度不想干涉他国内政

1959-05-31
第4版()
专栏：

    印度国防部长谈西藏问题时说
    印度不想干涉他国内政
    新华社新德里30日电　据“政治家报”报道，印度国防部长梅农29日在西姆拉附近的查伊尔自修学院对学员讲话，谈到西藏问题时他说，印度不想干涉任何一个国家的内政。
    谈到克什米尔时，他强调说，克什米尔是印度领土不可分割的一部分。
    在谈到果阿问题时，梅农说，不允许任何一个国家在那里建立军事基地。
    随后，梅农在印度国大党西姆拉城委员会组织的群众集会上讲话时，呼吁印度人民团结一致地、认真地为印度的社会安全和经济稳定而努力。
