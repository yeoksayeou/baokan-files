Title: Indian National Congress President Says at National Committee Meeting That Maintaining Mutual Friendship Between China and India is Necessary; Meeting Did Not Discuss or Vote on Proposal Regarding the Tibet Issue
Author:
Date: 1959-05-12
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, New Delhi, May 10th: The Indian National Congress National Committee began its meeting this morning.

The President of the National Congress, Mrs. Indira Gandhi, read out the resolution on the Tibet issue passed by the National Congress Working Committee yesterday. Mrs. Indira Gandhi said that she was informing the National Congress National Committee of the Working Committee's resolution on the Tibet issue because "there is a strong feeling in the country on that issue."

Mrs. Indira Gandhi said that what was mentioned in this resolution had already been discussed by Prime Minister Nehru in Parliament. The National Congress fully supports the government's policy on the Tibet issue.

Mrs. Indira Gandhi also said, "We are saddened by the developments in Tibet and sympathize with the people there." She emphasized that India "has a special relationship with Tibet." She said, "But this does not mean that we want to interfere in Tibet's affairs. We simply want to express our sympathy to the Tibetan people."

The President of the National Congress said, "For the sake of Asian peace and world peace, it is necessary for China and India to maintain mutual friendship."

She also said, "If someone criticizes us, we do not intend to refute them, because that is very naive. We should not do this. We should not be overwhelmed and angry just because another country criticizes us. We should be firm in implementing our policies and maintain dignity."

The meeting did not discuss the Tibet issue, nor did it vote on proposals related to the Tibet issue. Two informal proposals on the Tibet issue had been put forward. Mrs. Indira Gandhi said at the meeting that if the members agreed, no informal proposals would be discussed.

Among these two undiscussed proposals, one was a proposal from Mr. Patel, a member from Madhya Pradesh, to "express sympathy for Tibet." This proposal slandered that "the recent events in Tibet violate the ideals of Panchsheel and are tantamount to forcibly forcing a great nation to accept China's control over its internal affairs."

The second proposal was a proposal from Mr. Sanyal, a member from West Bengal, to "study the international situation in connection with India's peace and security." This proposal incited that "recent events in Tibet have caused serious concern in India, Nepal, Sikkim, and Bhutan." According to this proposal, the reason for this is "because the scope of military totalitarianism backed by strong forces is about to expand to their borders, thereby undermining the balance of power in Asia."

The Indian National Congress National Committee today discussed the issue of Indian cooperative farms.

### 印度国大党主席在全国委员会会议上说  中印保持相互友好是必要的  会议对有关西藏问题的提案没有进行讨论和表决

1959-05-12
第4版()
专栏：

    印度国大党主席在全国委员会会议上说
    中印保持相互友好是必要的
    会议对有关西藏问题的提案没有进行讨论和表决
    新华社新德里10日电　印度国大党全国委员会今天上午开始举行会议。
    国大党主席英迪拉·甘地夫人在会上宣读了国大党工作委员会昨天通过的关于西藏问题的决议。英迪拉·甘地夫人说，她所以要把工作委员会关于西藏问题的决议通知国大党全国委员会，是由于“在那个问题上，全国有着强烈的感觉”。
    英迪拉·甘地夫人说，这项决议中谈到的东西，尼赫鲁总理在议会中已经谈过了。国大党完全支持政府对西藏问题的政策。
    英迪拉·甘地夫人还说，“我们对西藏的事态发展感到难过，并且同情那里的人民。”她强调印度“同西藏有着特殊的关系”。她说，“但是，这并不是说我们想干涉西藏的事务。我们只不过是想对西藏人民表示同情。”
    这位国大党主席说，“为了亚洲和平和世界和平，中国同印度保持相互友好是必要的。”
    她又说，“如果有人批评我们，我们不打算反驳他们，因为那是很幼稚的。我们不应该这样做。我们不应该仅仅因为另一个国家批评我们，我们就不知所措和发脾气。我们在执行我们的政策时应该坚定，并且保持尊严”。
    会议没有就西藏问题进行讨论，也没有就有关西藏问题的提案进行表决。有人曾经提出两项有关西藏问题的非正式提案。英迪拉·甘地夫人在会上说，如果委员们同意的话，将不讨论任何非正式的提案。
    在这两项没有讨论的提案中，有一项是由中央邦的委员巴特尔提出的“对西藏表示同情”的提案。这项议案诬蔑说，“最近在西藏发生的事件违背了潘查希拉的理想，就等于用武力迫使一个伟大的民族接受中国对其内部事务的控制。”
    第二个提案是西孟加拉的委员萨尼雅尔提出的“联系印度的和平与安全来研究国际局势”的提案。这项提案挑拨地说，“西藏最近发生的事件在印度、尼泊尔、锡金和不丹引起了严重的关切”。据这项议案说，其所以如此，是“因为以强大的力量作后盾的军事极权主义的范围眼看就要扩张到它们的边境，从而破坏亚洲的力量均衡。”
    印度国大党全国委员会今天讨论了印度合作农场问题。
