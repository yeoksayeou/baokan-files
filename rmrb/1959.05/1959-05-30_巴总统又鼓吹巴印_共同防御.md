Title: Pakistani President Again Advocates Pakistan-India "Joint Defense"
Author:
Date: 1959-05-30
Page: 4
Xinhua News Agency, Karachi, May 26th: Pakistani President Ayub Khan today once again advocated the so-called "joint defense" between Pakistan and India.

    Speaking to reporters in Rawalpindi today, he said that Pakistan hopes for "peace and friendship" with India. He repeated what he has said many times before, "We should not be wary of each other, we should look outward and stand together to deal with any threat from the outside."

    In view of India's clear rejection of the so-called Indo-Pakistani "joint defense" proposal, Ayub Khan specifically stated: "Joint defense" does not mean that Pakistan and India will conclude any form of treaty. It simply means working together to deal with any "threat" from the outside.

### 巴总统又鼓吹巴印“共同防御”

1959-05-30
第4版()
专栏：

    巴总统又鼓吹巴印“共同防御”
    新华社卡拉奇26日电　巴基斯坦总统阿尤布汗今天再一次鼓吹所谓巴基斯坦同印度的“共同防御”。
    他在拉瓦尔品第同新闻记者谈话时说，巴基斯坦希望同印度“和平和友好”。他重复已经多次讲过的话说，“我们不应当彼此提防，我们应当向外看，站在一起对付来自外面的任何威胁。”
    鉴于印度已经明白拒绝了所谓印、巴“共同防御”的建议，阿尤布汗便特别说明：“共同防御”并不意味着巴基斯坦和印度将缔结任何形式的条约。这只是意味着共同努力来对付来自外界的任何“威胁”。
