	International Briefs
	World Population Increase
	New York Dispatch: According to the latest United Nations Demographic Yearbook, the world's population continues to increase at a rate of 45 million people per year (equivalent to the total population of France). The current world birth rate averages 34 per thousand per year, while the death rate is only 18 per thousand. Therefore, the world's population increases by 85 people per minute, and more than 5,000 people per hour. Currently, more than half of the world's 2.8 billion people live in Asia, and 14% live in Europe. If the current population growth trend remains unchanged, by 2000, the Asian population may account for 60% of the world's population, while the European population will be relatively reduced to 10%.
	Burma to Ban Import of Foreign Books in Burmese
	Rangoon Dispatch: The Burmese Ministry of Trade Development recently ordered a ban on the import of all foreign books, maps, and magazines published in Burmese, and foreign films dubbed in Burmese.

    国际简讯
    世界人口增加情况
    纽约讯　据最近出版的联合国人口统计年鉴说，世界人口继续以每年四千五百万人（相当于法国人口总数）的速度增加。目前世界人口出生率平均每年千分之三十四，而死亡率只有千分之十八。因此世界人口每分钟增加八十五人，每小时增加五千多人。目前全世界二十八亿人口中有一半以上居住在亚洲，有14％住在欧洲。假如目前的人口增加的趋势不变，到2000年亚洲人口可能占全世界人口60％，而欧洲人口相对地减为10％。
    缅将禁止进口缅文版外国书籍
    仰光讯：缅甸贸易发展部最近下令禁止进口所有用缅文出版的外国书籍、地图、杂志和以缅语配音的外国影片。
