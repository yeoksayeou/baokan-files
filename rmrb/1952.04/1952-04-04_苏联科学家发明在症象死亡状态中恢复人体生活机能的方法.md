Title: Soviet Scientists Invent Method to Restore Human Life Functions in Apparent Death State
Author:
Date: 1952-04-04
Page: 3

Xinhua News Agency, Moscow, March 23rd (TASS dispatch) - Soviet scientists are successfully researching one of the most important problems in modern biology: prolonging life and reviving human tissue. The Soviet government attaches great importance to the achievements of this research. Recently, Professor Negovsky and his assistants Smirenskaya and Gaevskaya-Sokolova, as well as Professor Andreyev, were awarded the Stalin Prize for inventing a method to restore human life functions in the face of impending death or in a state of apparent death.

Andreyev, a Meritorious Scientist of Russia, was the pioneer of research in this area. About forty years ago, he published his first work on restoring the functions of the heart, respiratory organs, and central nervous system. Currently, the young scientist Negovsky is persistently continuing this research.

Medical science has proven that death often does not mean the sudden cessation of life. Death can be divided into two stages: apparent (relative) death and biological (true) death. Within five to six minutes after breathing and heart function stop, metabolism continues in the human body. At this time, the human body is not actually dead because its tissues and organs have not had time to undergo irreversible changes. This period is called apparent death.

The research of these Soviet scientists shows that even after apparent death, life can still be saved, and apparent death can be reversed.

To achieve this goal, Negovsky uses the method of arterial blood transfusion, transfusing blood in the opposite direction of normal arterial blood flow. The heart, which has stopped beating, thus receives fresh blood mixed with adrenaline. At the same time, a special instrument is used to perform artificial respiration on the patient. All these procedures must be done extremely quickly, because even a slight delay will lead to biological death.

When the heart starts beating again, the doctor performs intravenous blood transfusions. Once blood circulation and respiratory functions are restored, the patient's consciousness returns. Of course, not all cases of apparent death can be saved. If certain vital organs in the human body have been irreparably affected by some disease or trauma, the treatment of apparent death may not be effective.

These Soviet scientists have revived several people who were in a state of apparent death due to severe injuries, shock, or excessive blood loss. These people are still alive today.

### 苏联科学家发明在症象死亡状态中恢复人体生活机能的方法

1952-04-04
第3版()
专栏：

    苏联科学家发明在症象死亡状态中恢复人体生活机能的方法
    苏联的科学家们正在顺利地研究现代生物学中一个最重要的问题——延续生命和使人体组织复活的问题。苏联政府非常重视这方面研究工作的成果。最近，涅果夫斯基教授和他的助手斯米伦斯卡雅和加耶夫斯卡雅—索科洛娃以及安德列耶夫教授就因为发明了在弥留之际或在症象死亡状态中恢复人体生活机能的方法，而荣获了斯大林奖金。
    俄罗斯功勋科学家安德列耶夫是这一方面研究的首创者。大约在四十年前，他就发表了第一部关于恢复心脏、呼吸器官和中央神经系统作用的著作。目前，青年科学家涅果夫斯基在坚持不渝地继续这种研究。
    医学上已经证明：死亡往往并不意味着生命的骤然停止。死亡可以分为两个阶段——症象（相对）死亡和生命（真正）死亡。呼吸和心脏作用停止后的五、六分钟以内，代谢作用依然在人体中继续，这时人体实际上没有死，因为它的组织和器官还没有来得及发生不可挽回的变化。这个时期就叫做症象死亡。
    这几位苏联科学家的研究表明：即使在症象死亡以后，生命还是可以挽救的，症象死亡是可以救得活的。
    为了达到这个目的，涅果夫斯基采用动脉输血的方法，输血的方向恰恰与平常动脉血流的方向相反。已经停止搏动的心脏因此得到了混和着副肾素的新鲜血液。同时，借用一种特殊的器械对病人行人工呼吸。所有这些手术必须做得极快，因为稍微缓慢一点，生命死亡就会跟着来了。
    在心脏再度开始搏动的时候，医师就行静脉输血。一当血液循环和呼吸器官恢复作用之后，病人的知觉就恢复了。当然，症象死亡并不是都可以救得活的。人体中有许多非常重要的器官，如果这些器官受到某种疾病或创伤的不可挽救的影响，症象死亡的救治就可能不会有什么效果了。
    这几位苏联科学家曾经救活了几个因为受了重伤、震荡或是失血过多而陷于症象死亡状态的人。这些人一直活到现在。
    （新华社据塔斯社莫斯科三月二十三日电）
