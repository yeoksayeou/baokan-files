Title: Japanese People's Peace Congress Opens in Tokyo; Oyama Ikuo Reports on International Situation and Peace Movement Issues
Author:
Date: 1953-12-18
Page: 4

    Xinhua News Agency, Tokyo, December 17th: Under the slogan "Let's Join Hands to Strive for Peace," the Japanese People's Peace Congress opened in Tokyo on the 14th. This congress was convened in response to the World Peace Council's call to launch a movement for negotiation and to further strengthen the Japanese people's peace movement.
    Participants in the congress included prominent Japanese peace advocates such as Oyama Ikuo, Matsumoto Jiichiro, Awa Tokusaburo, Hani Goro, and representatives from all walks of life across Japan, including workers, farmers, women, and students.
    After the opening of the congress, Oyama Ikuo delivered a report on the international situation and the movement to defend peace. The first day of the meeting featured heated discussions on turning Japan from a U.S. military base into a bastion of peace, and on further strengthening and expanding Japan's peace forces.

### 日本人民和平大会在东京开幕  大山郁夫报告国际形势与和平运动问题

1953-12-18
第4版()
专栏：

    日本人民和平大会在东京开幕
    大山郁夫报告国际形势与和平运动问题
   【新华社十七日讯】东京消息：在“大家携起手来争取和平”的口号下，日本人民和平大会已于十四日在东京开幕。这次大会是为响应世界和平理事会关于展开争取协商运动的号召，并进一步加强日本人民和平运动而召开的。
    参加大会的有：日本著名和平人士大山郁夫、松本治一郎、淡德三郎、羽仁五郎等人，以及来自日本全国各地的许多工人、农民、妇女、学生等各阶层人民的代表。
    大会开幕后，由大山郁夫作了关于国际形势与保卫和平运动的报告。第一天的会议热烈地讨论了把日本从美国的军事基地变为和平堡垒，以及进一步加强和扩大日本和平力量的问题。
