Title: Voters in Xidan District, Beijing Enthusiastically Participate in Voting
Author:
Date: 1953-12-09
Page: 1

Xinhua News Agency dispatch: On December 6th, most electoral districts in the Xidan District of Beijing held elections. Voters cast their ballots to elect representatives to the District People's Congress with immense joy.

The election day was a festive occasion throughout the district. Over seventy polling stations were decorated with colorful displays. Many polling stations displayed inspiring slogans such as "Implement the People's Congress system of universal suffrage, consolidate the people's democratic dictatorship, and strive to realize the general line of the state during the transition period!" Encouraged by the bright future of the motherland, voters flocked to the polling stations at dawn, forming long queues at the entrance of each station. The people of the capital cherish the democratic government. Many voters wore red flowers on their chests to celebrate this joyous day.

On this day, all the leading officials and staff members of central and Beijing municipal government organs located in the Xidan District actively participated in the voting. Chen Shutong, Vice Chairman of the National Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference, Shen Junru, President of the Supreme People's Court of the Central People's Government, and Xie Juezai, Minister of the Ministry of Internal Affairs of the Central People's Government, personally went to the polling stations to cast their votes. Li Weihan, Head of the United Front Work Department of the CPC Central Committee, An Ziwen, Deputy Head of the Organization Department of the CPC Central Committee, Lai Ruoyu, Chairman of the All-China Federation of Trade Unions, and Zhang Youyu, Deputy Mayor of the Beijing Municipal People's Government, also participated in the voting. The famous traditional Chinese painter, 93-year-old Qi Baishi, also cast his ballot in a mobile ballot box.

The workers in various factories who are studying the general line of the state during the transition period deeply understand the great significance of implementing the People's Congress system of universal suffrage during the transition period. Workers from many factories lined up early in the morning to vote at the polling stations. Among these worker voters were many night shift workers who had just left their machines. The workers of the No. 4 printing machine in the offset printing workshop of the Fine Arts Printing Factory successfully completed the production plans for September, October, and November. On this election day, they received glorious award flags. The honor of labor filled each of them with boundless pride as they participated in the voting. Some employees of state-owned stores exceeded their business plans for the day to celebrate the election day. The workers said with confidence that the general line of the state during the transition period has pointed out the direction for us to move forward, and the universal suffrage has elected good leaders, so our lives will become happier and happier.

Compatriots of various ethnic minorities, such as Hui and Mongolian, returned overseas Chinese, and followers of Catholicism and Christianity living in the Xidan District also enthusiastically went to the polling stations to vote. Students of various ethnic minorities from all over the motherland who are studying at the Central Institute for Nationalities Preparatory School wore their bright ethnic costumes to participate in the voting. In front of the polling stations, they sang and danced, expressing the great unity of all ethnic groups and singing praises of the happiness that Chairman Mao has brought them. On this day, Luo Wang, the acting director of the Beijing Office of the Panchen Lama of Tibet, also participated in the voting in the Xidan District. More than 900 voters from the Beijing Overseas Chinese Students' Supplementary School, carrying a banner with the slogan "Casting the first vote in our lives," came to the polling station to cast their ballots. Many religious figures, such as Ju Zan, Deputy Secretary-General of the Buddhist Association of China and representative of the People's Representatives Conference of all circles in Beijing, and Zhi Da, Abbot of Guangji Temple in Beijing, also participated in the voting on this grand election day.

### 北京市西单区选民热烈参加投票

1953-12-09
第1版()
专栏：

    北京市西单区选民热烈参加投票
    据新华社讯：十二月六日，北京市西单区大多数选区举行了投票，选民们以万分欢欣的心情投票选举区人民代表大会的代表。
    选举的一天，全区一片节日景象，七十余个选举站被装饰得五彩缤纷。许多投票站的门前贴着“实行普选的人民代表大会制度，巩固人民民主专政，为实现国家过渡时期的总路线而奋斗！”的振奋人心的标语。为祖国的美好未来所鼓舞着的选民们，黎明就涌向选举站，每个选举站的门口都排成了长长的行列。首都人民热爱民主政权，许多选民胸前戴上了红花，表示对这个喜庆日子的祝贺。
    这一天，所有驻在北京市西单区的中央机关和北京市机关的首长和工作人员们，都积极参加了投票。中国人民政治协商会议全国委员会副主席陈叔通、中央人民政府最高人民法院院长沈钧儒、中央人民政府内务部部长谢觉哉都亲自到投票站投了票。参加投票的还有中共中央统一战线工作部部长李维汉、中共中央组织部副部长安子文、中华全国总工会主席赖若愚以及北京市人民政府副市长张友渔等。著名国画家、九十三岁的齐白石老人也在流动票箱投了自己的选票。
    正在进行国家过渡时期总路线学习的各工厂的工人们，他们深刻地体会到在过渡时期实行普选的人民代表大会制度的重大意义。许多工厂的工人们很早就排成整齐的队伍来到选举站投票。在这些工人选民的行列中，有很多刚刚离开机器的夜班工人。美术印刷厂胶印车间四号印刷机的工人，圆满地完成九、十、十一三个月份的生产计划，正在选举这一天，他们得到了光荣的奖旗，劳动的荣誉使他们每一个人都怀着无限的自豪感参加了投票。有些国营商店的职工们，为了庆祝选举日，这一天超额完成了营业计划。工人们都满怀信心地说，国家在过渡时期的总路线给我们指出了前进的方向，普选又选出了好的带头人，我们日子将越过越幸福。
    住在西单区的回、蒙等各少数民族的同胞，归国的华侨和天主教、基督教的教徒们，也都热烈的到选举站投了票。来自祖国四面八方的中央民族学院附中的各少数民族的学生，都穿上了鲜艳的自己民族的服装参加投票。在选举站的门前，他们载歌载舞，尽情地歌唱各民族的大团结，歌唱毛主席带给他们的幸福。在这一天，参加西单区投票的还有西藏班禅驻京办事处代理处长罗旺。北京归国华侨学生中等补习学校的九百余选民，打着“投下有生以来的第一张选票”的横幅标语，来到选举站投下了自己的票。在这隆重的选举日参加投票的还有中国佛教协会副秘书长、北京市各界人民代表会议代表巨赞法师，北京广济寺主持志达法师等许多宗教界的人士。
