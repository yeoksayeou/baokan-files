Title: New Economic Crisis Looms: British Industrialist Boothby Expresses Concern
Author:
Date: 1958-01-28
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, London, January 27th: According to TASS, Robert Boothby, a well-known British industrialist and Conservative Party Member of Parliament, expressed concern in a letter published in "The Times" on the 25th about the increasingly imminent signs of a new economic crisis in capitalist countries.

Boothby wrote that if a profound crisis occurs in Western countries, "it would be tantamount to saying that we do not know how to manage the economy, or that the inherent serious shortcomings of (our) economy preclude the possibility of matching supply and demand."

Boothby then pointed out, "When most countries have a serious lack of industrial equipment, American ships are sitting idle, steel production is reduced, and steel mills are only utilizing 60% of their production capacity."

### 新的经济危机咄咄迫人  英国著名工业家布斯比感到忧虑

1958-01-28
第5版()
专栏：

    新的经济危机咄咄迫人
    英国著名工业家布斯比感到忧虑
    据新华社27日讯　据塔斯社伦敦讯：英国著名的工业家、保守党议员罗伯特·布斯比在25日发表在“泰晤士报”上的一封信中对资本主义国家中新的经济危机日益临近的迹象表示忧虑。
    布斯比写道，如果西方国家发生深刻的危机，“那就等于说，我们不知道如何管理经济，或者说，（我们的）经济固有的严重缺点排除了供求之间相适应的可能性。”
    布斯比接着指出，“当大多数国家严重缺乏工业装备时，美国的船只却停泊不动，钢的生产缩减，炼钢厂的生产能力只利用了60％。”
