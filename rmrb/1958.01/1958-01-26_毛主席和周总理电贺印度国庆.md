Title: Chairman Mao and Premier Zhou Send Congratulatory Messages on Indian National Day
Author:
Date: 1958-01-26
Page: 1
Xinhua News Agency, January 25th: Chairman Mao Zedong sent a telegram to Indian President Rajendra Prasad on the 24th, congratulating him on the eighth anniversary of the Republic of India. The telegram reads as follows:

    To His Excellency Rajendra Prasad, President of the Republic of India:
    On the occasion of the eighth anniversary of the Republic of India, I would like to extend my sincere congratulations to Your Excellency and the great people of India on behalf of the Chinese people and in my own name. May the traditional friendship between the Chinese and Indian peoples be as enduring as the green mountains and as long-flowing as the clear waters, constantly consolidated and developed, and I wish Your Excellency good health.

                                         Chairman of the People's Republic of China, Mao Zedong
                                                            January 24, 1958

Xinhua News Agency, January 25th: Premier Zhou Enlai sent a telegram to Indian Prime Minister Jawaharlal Nehru on the 24th, congratulating him on the eighth anniversary of the Republic of India. The telegram reads as follows:

    To His Excellency Jawaharlal Nehru, Prime Minister of the Republic of India:
    On the joyous occasion of the National Day of the Republic of India, I would like to extend my warm congratulations to Your Excellency and the government of your country on behalf of the government of the People's Republic of China and in my own name.
    The Chinese people are pleased to see that the Republic of India is playing a significant role in international affairs. Your Excellency's positive attitude towards disarmament and the prohibition of nuclear weapons, as well as Your Excellency's recent support for convening a summit meeting and concluding a treaty of non-aggression between East and West, have been widely praised by peace-loving people all over the world. I sincerely hope that the close friendship between China and India will continue to grow on the basis of the Five Principles of Peaceful Coexistence, and that the two countries will cooperate more closely in maintaining world peace, promoting the relaxation of the international situation, and strengthening the friendly unity of the people of Asian and African countries.

                                      Premier of the State Council of the People's Republic of China, Zhou Enlai
                                                            January 24, 1958

### 毛主席和周总理电贺印度国庆

1958-01-26
第1版()
专栏：

    毛主席和周总理电贺印度国庆
    新华社25日讯　毛泽东主席二十四日致电印度总统拉金德拉·普拉沙德，祝贺印度共和国国庆八周年。电文如下：印度共和国总统拉金德拉·普拉沙德阁下：
    值此印度共和国国庆八周年之际，我谨代表中国人民，并且以我个人的名义向阁下和伟大的印度人民表示热诚的祝贺。祝中印两国人民间的传统友谊青山不老，绿水长流，不断巩固和发展，并祝阁下身体健康。		          中华人民共和国主席毛泽东　				一九五八年一月二十四日
    新华社25日讯　周恩来总理二十四日致电印度总理贾瓦哈拉尔·尼赫鲁，祝贺印度共和国国庆八周年。电文如下：印度共和国总理贾瓦哈拉尔·尼赫鲁阁下：
    欣逢印度共和国国庆之际，我谨代表中华人民共和国政府、并以我个人名义向阁下和贵国政府致热烈的祝贺。
    中国人民高兴地看到：印度共和国在国际事务中起着重大的作用。阁下对于裁减军备、禁止核武器所采取的积极态度以及最近阁下对召开最高级会议和缔结东西方互不侵犯条约的倡议所给予的支持获得全世界爱好和平的人民的普遍赞扬。我热诚地希望：中印两国间的亲密友谊在和平共处五项原则的基础上不断增进，两国在维护世界和平、促进国际形势缓和以及加强亚非各国人民友好团结的工作中更紧密地合作。		    中华人民共和国国务院总理周恩来　				一九五八年一月二十四日
