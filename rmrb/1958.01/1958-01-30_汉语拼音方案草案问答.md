Author: Ye Laishi

    Questions and Answers on the Draft of the Hanyu Pinyin Scheme
    Ye Laishi
    1. What is the Hanyu Pinyin Scheme?
    Any language can have its phonetics analyzed into several phonemes, and then a set of letters is created based on these phonemes to serve as symbols for recording the language. The situation is the same for the language of the Han people—Chinese. We can analyze the phonetics of Mandarin Chinese (Beijing pronunciation) into several phonemes, and then create a set of pinyin letters for these phonemes, to be used to spell Mandarin and annotate Han characters. In order to meet the needs of annotating Han characters and spelling Mandarin, a set of pinyin letters and spelling rules are formulated based on the phonetic analysis of Mandarin Chinese. This is called the Hanyu Pinyin Scheme. Once you learn the Hanyu Pinyin Scheme, any sentence in Mandarin can be correctly spelled out using this set of letters, and the standard pronunciation of any Han character can be correctly annotated using this set of letters.
    2. What is the basis for the pronunciation of these letters?
    The pronunciation of the Hanyu Pinyin letters is based on the standard pronunciation of Mandarin Chinese, namely, Beijing pronunciation. The pronunciation of each letter is authentic Beijing pronunciation. Therefore, this set of letters is called Hanyu Pinyin letters.
    The Hanyu Pinyin Scheme uses Latin letters (also called Roman letters by some). Latin letters are the most widely used letters internationally: more than sixty countries around the world use them as symbols for writing languages, and they are also commonly used symbols in modern science and technology. Latin letters have been widely used by many ethnic groups for a long time, so the pronunciation and usage of its letters are not exactly the same in all countries. The scheme we are formulating now makes the Latin letters adapt to the needs of Chinese, so that they can completely and correctly express the phonetics of Chinese, while also taking into account the general international habits of Latin letters. Therefore, the pronunciation of the letters specified in this draft has similarities with English, French, and German, but also has differences. This is a very natural thing. We cannot pronounce Hanyu Pinyin letters according to the pronunciation of English letters, just as we cannot pronounce German according to the pronunciation of French letters.
    3. What are the uses of the alphabet?
    The alphabet has three functions: First, it stipulates the order of arrangement of Hanyu Pinyin letters. Second, it stipulates the style of Hanyu Pinyin letters. Third, it stipulates the name of each Hanyu Pinyin letter. These three regulations are necessary for teaching and using Hanyu Pinyin letters.
    4. In what order are the Hanyu Pinyin letters arranged?
    There are a total of twenty-six Hanyu Pinyin letters, the same as English, French, and German letters. The first draft published in February 1956 had five new letters, which have now been cancelled. These twenty-six letters are arranged in the order of the alphabet, namely a, b, c, d, e, f, g, h, i, j, k, l, m, n, o, p, q, r, s, t, u, v, w, x, y, z. This is the traditional arrangement order of Latin letters, and countries around the world that use Latin letters adopt this arrangement order. For example, Vitamin A, Vitamin B, Vitamin C, and a+b+c, x+y+z in mathematics... are all based on this customary order. In the future, we can use this internationally accepted A, B, C order to compile various indexes, arrange lists, archives, materials... etc., which is simple, rigorous, and has many conveniences.
    Why not use the arrangement order of Zhuyin letters? When describing Chinese phonology, it is convenient to take the order of b, p, m, f, Zhuyin letters, but as the general order of letters, it is not as good as the ABC order. For example, ba, bo, bai, bei, bao... such an arrangement is incomprehensible to those who are not familiar with Zhuyin letters.
    The letter V in the alphabet does not have this sound in Beijing pronunciation, but it is useful when spelling foreign words, minority languages, and Chinese dialects, so it is also included in the alphabet. (To be continued)

### 汉语拼音方案草案问答
叶籁士
1958-01-30
第7版()
专栏：

    汉语拼音方案草案问答
    叶籁士
    1、什么叫做汉语拼音方案？
    任何一种语言，都可以把它的语音分析成若干音素，然后根据这些音素制定一套字母，作为记录这种语言的符号。对于汉民族的语言——汉语来说，情况也是一样。我们可以把汉语普通话的语音（北京语音）分析成若干音素，然后为这些音素制定一套拼音字母，用来拼写普通话并给汉字注音。为了满足给汉字注音和拼写普通话的需要，以汉语普通话的语音分析为基础，而制订一套拼音字母和拼写法式，叫汉语拼音方案。学会了汉语拼音方案，普通话里的任何一句话都可以用这套字母正确地拼写出来，任何一个汉字的标准读音都可以用这套字母正确地注出来。
    2、这套字母的读音是根据什么规定的？
    汉语拼音字母的读音，是根据汉语普通话的标准音，即北京语音规定的。每个字母的读音都是地道的北京音。因此这套字母叫做汉语拼音字母。
    汉语拼音方案采用的是拉丁字母（也有人叫它罗马字）。拉丁字母是国际上使用得最广的字母：全世界有六十几个国家用它作为书写语言的符号，也是现代科学技术上普遍使用的符号。拉丁字母长时期为许多民族广泛使用，因此它的字母的读音和用法在各国不尽相同。现在我们制订的这个方案，做到了使拉丁字母适应汉语的需要，因而能够完全正确地表达汉语的语音，同时又照顾了拉丁字母的一般国际习惯。因此这个草案中规定的字母的读音，跟英文、法文、德文固然有相同的地方，但也有不同的地方。这是很自然的事情。我们不能根据英文字母的读音来念汉语拼音字母，正如不能根据法文字母的读音来念德文一样。
    3、字母表有些什么用处？
    字母表有三个作用：第一、规定了汉语拼音字母的排列顺序。第二、规定了汉语拼音字母的体式。第三、规定了每个汉语拼音字母的名称。这三项规定，对于教学和使用汉语拼音字母，都是必要的。
    4、汉语拼音字母是按照什么顺序排列的呢？
    汉语拼音字母一共有二十六个，跟英文字母、法文字母、德文字母一样。1956年2月发表的第一个草案中有五个新字母，现在都取消了。这二十六个字母按照字母表的顺序排列，即a，b，c，d，e，f，g，h，i，j，k，l，m，n，o，p，q，r，s，t，u，v，w，x，y，z。这是拉丁字母的传统的排列顺序，世界上应用拉丁字母的国家都采用这个排列顺序。如维他命A，维他命B，维他命C，以及数学中a＋b＋c，x＋y＋z……都是根据这个习惯的顺序来的。今后我们编各种索引排列名单、档案、资料……等等都可以采用这个国际通用的A，B，C顺序，既简单严密，又有很多便利。
    为什么不采用注音字母的排列顺序？在叙述汉语音韵的时候，采取b，p，m，f，注音字母的顺序是有便利的，但是作为字母的一般的顺序，那就不如ABC顺序。比方ba，bo，bai，bei，bao……这样的排列，对于不熟悉注音字母的人，是没有办法理解的。
    字母表中的V，在北京语音中没有这个音，但是在拼写外来语、少数民族语言和汉语方言的时候有用，所以也列入了字母表。（未完）
