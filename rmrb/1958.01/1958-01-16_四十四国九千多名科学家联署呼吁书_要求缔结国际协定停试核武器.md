Title: More Than 9,000 Scientists From Forty-Four Countries Jointly Sign Appeal, Demanding International Agreement to Halt Nuclear Weapons Testing
Author:
Date: 1958-01-16
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, New York, January 15th: American renowned scientist and Nobel Prize winner, Linus Pauling, handed over an appeal to UN Secretary-General Hammarskjöld on the 13th, signed by more than 9,000 scientists from various countries, calling for immediate action by all countries to conclude an international agreement to halt atomic weapons testing.

A total of 9,200 scientists from forty-four countries signed the appeal, including 101 members of the U.S. National Academy of Sciences, 216 academicians and corresponding members of the Soviet Academy of Sciences, 35 fellows of the British Royal Society, more than 250 Czechoslovak scientists, more than 80 Polish scientists, more than 2,000 Romanian scientists, more than 1,000 Japanese scientists, and more than 500 Indian scientists. British philosopher Bertrand Russell, French atomic scientist Frédéric Joliot-Curie, and physician Albert Schweitzer, as well as more than 30 other Nobel Prize winners from Western countries, signed the appeal.

Chinese scientists Wu Yuzhang and Wu Youxun wrote to Pauling, expressing their support for the call for an immediate cessation of nuclear weapons testing. Chinese scientists in the United States, Li Zhengdao and Yang Zhenning, signed the letter.

The appeal points out the harm to human health caused by atomic explosion tests, stating, "Each atomic bomb test increases the amount of radioactive elements in all parts of the world. And each increase in radioactivity damages the health of all mankind."

The appeal also emphasizes that if atomic explosion tests continue and more countries possess atomic weapons, this will increase the risk of a catastrophic atomic war. The appeal calls on all countries to immediately "conclude an international agreement to stop nuclear weapons testing as the first step towards broader disarmament and the ultimate effective destruction of atomic weapons, in order to avoid the possibility of a nuclear war that could be catastrophic for all mankind."

Pauling also held a press conference on the 13th. At the meeting, he said that he believes this appeal "represents the opinion of the majority of scientists around the world." He said that some other American scientists have also expressed that they believe it is necessary to stop conducting atomic weapons tests, but because they "have connections" with the government, they did not sign the appeal. Pauling also pointed out that the signature campaign is still ongoing.

This is Pauling's second large-scale effort to stop nuclear testing. In June 1957, Pauling handed over an appeal signed by 2,000 American scientists to U.S. President Eisenhower, demanding an end to nuclear weapons testing.

### 四十四国九千多名科学家联署呼吁书  要求缔结国际协定停试核武器

1958-01-16
第5版()
专栏：

    四十四国九千多名科学家联署呼吁书  要求缔结国际协定停试核武器
    新华社15日讯　纽约消息：美国著名科学家、诺贝尔奖金得奖人保林13日把一份有各国九千多名科学家签名的呼吁书交给联合国秘书长哈马舍尔德，呼吁各国立即采取行动缔结国际协定来停止试验原子武器。
    在呼吁书上签名的一共有四十四个国家的九千二百多名科学家，其中包括美国国立科学院一百○一名院士、苏联科学院二百一十六名院士和通讯院士、英国皇家学会三十五名会员、二百五十多名捷克斯洛伐克科学家、八十多名波兰科学家、两千多名罗马尼亚科学家、一千多名日本科学家和五百多名印度科学家。英国哲学家罗素、法国原子科学家约里奥—居里和医学家施维泽尔以及西方国家另外三十多名诺贝尔奖金得奖人在呼吁书上签了名。
    中国科学家吴玉章和吴有训曾经写信给保林，对于立即停止核武器试验的呼吁表示支持。在美国的中国科学家李政道、杨振宁在这封信上签了名。
    这份呼吁书中指出原子爆炸试验对人类健康的危害，它说，“每一次原子核炸弹试验都使全世界各地的放射性元素的数量增加。而每增加一些放射性，都使全世界人类的健康遭受损害”。
    呼吁书并且着重指出，如果继续进行原子爆炸试验以及有更多的国家拥有原子武器，这就会增加爆发灾难性的原子战争的危险。呼吁书要求各国立即“缔结停止试验核武器的国际协定，作为更广泛的裁军和最后有效地销毁原子武器的第一步，以避免发生一次可能对全人类说来都是灾难的核战争的可能性”。
    保林在13日还举行了记者招待会。他在会上说，他认为，这份呼吁书“代表着全世界大多数科学家的意见”。他说，另外有一些美国科学家也曾经表示他们认为有必要停止进行试验原子武器，但是由于他们同政府“有联系”，没有在呼吁书上签名。保林还指出，目前，签名运动仍在继续中。
    这是保林为停止核试验进行的第二次规模巨大的努力。1957年6月，保林曾经把一份有两千名美国科学家签名的呼吁书交给美国总统艾森豪威尔，要求停止进行核武器试验。
