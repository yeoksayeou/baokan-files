Title: Danish Prime Minister Advocates Negotiated Settlement of Disputes, US Congressman Calls for Non-Aggression Pact with Soviet Union
Author:
Date: 1958-01-09
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, January 8th: Copenhagen Dispatch: Danish Prime Minister Hansen advocates resolving international disputes through negotiation. At a meeting in Vordingborg, he said: Armaments alone cannot solve problems. Now that new assessments have emerged in many aspects of international politics, I hope that negotiations can begin.

In this speech, Hansen also mentioned the proposal originally talked about by British Prime Minister Macmillan regarding the possibility of concluding a non-aggression treaty with the Soviet Union. He said that this proposal "should be seriously studied."

Xinhua News Agency, January 8th: According to a United Press reporter from Washington, Wisconsin Democratic Congressman Reuss said on the 6th that the Eisenhower administration should declare its willingness to conclude a non-aggression treaty with the Soviet Union. It must also take action to establish the attitude that the North Atlantic Treaty Organization should have in order to conduct "effective negotiations."

### 丹麦首相主张谈判解决争端  美国一议员要求同苏联缔结互不侵犯条约

1958-01-09
第5版()
专栏：

    丹麦首相主张谈判解决争端
    美国一议员要求同苏联缔结互不侵犯条约
    据新华社8日讯　哥本哈根消息：丹麦首相汉森主张通过谈判来解决国际争端。他在佛季勒的一次会议上说：军备本身不能解决问题。在国际政治中已经有许多方面出现了新的估计的今天，我希望谈判能够开始。
    汉森在这次讲话中还提到英国首相麦克米伦原先谈过的关于可以同苏联缔结互不侵犯条约的主张。他说，“应该认真研究”这个主张。
    据新华社8日讯　据合众社记者自华盛顿报道，威斯康星州民主党众议员劳斯6日说，艾森豪威尔政府应当宣布它愿意同苏联缔结互不侵犯条约。它还必须采取行动，确立北大西洋公约组织为了进行“有效果的谈判”所应有的态度。
