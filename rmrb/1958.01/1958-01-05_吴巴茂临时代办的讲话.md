Title: Speech by Chargé d'Affaires U Ba Maung

Author:

Date: 1958-01-05
Page: 4

Vice Chairman, friends:

First, allow me on behalf of all the staff of the Burmese Embassy in Beijing to express our heartfelt gratitude to you for joining us tonight to celebrate the tenth anniversary of Burmese Independence Day.

Ten years is not a long time, but these ten years have been filled with so many different problems that we feel we have gained a lot of experience in solving them during this short period.

When we gained independence, we had to accomplish a lot of work if we wanted to maintain the fruits of our freedom. But when we started, we encountered serious internal problems that weakened a lot of our strength and resources. Nevertheless, we have made progress and achieved success in strengthening our defense forces, replacing the old government system with a new democratic administrative system, and abolishing the old economic measures and implementing new social reforms. In this way, Burma is moving forward from a country impoverished by war and hampered by its own internal problems. Today, our independence is secure and consolidated.

In our international relations, we have successfully promoted the historic Five Principles of Peaceful Coexistence with the People's Republic of China, and we are pleased to see the growing influence of these principles. We now see no fewer than eighty countries that have accepted these principles of coexistence, and we are confident that their influence will grow.

We also maintain the closest and best relations with a number of friendly countries. And, because we believe that no one country can single-handedly defend peace or work for peace and the progress of mankind, our representatives have played a more active role in international conferences.

Now, let's talk about our own relationship, the relationship between the peoples of China and Burma, who are like relatives. We can say that this relationship is closer than ever before. With the frequent and increasing mutual visits between leaders and people at all levels of the two countries, the close understanding that currently exists will be greatly enhanced and built on a more solid foundation.

Today, at this very moment, our Deputy Prime Minister U Kyaw Nyein is also celebrating this anniversary in Kunming. In my opinion, nothing could be more meaningful than his presence in Kunming.

As the leaders and people of all walks of life in our two countries are intensifying their efforts to build a more solid edifice of friendship, allow me to invite you to join us in a toast to:

The health of Chairman Mao Zedong of the People's Republic of China,

To the eternal friendship between China and Burma,

To peace in Asia and the world,

Cheers!

### 吴巴茂临时代办的讲话

1958-01-05
第4版()
专栏：

    吴巴茂临时代办的讲话副主席阁下，朋友们：
    首先，允许我代表北京缅甸大使馆的全体人员向你们表达我们衷心的谢意，感谢你们今天晚上同我们一起来庆祝缅甸独立日十周年纪念。
    十年的时间并不长，但是这十年内充满了那么多的、各种不同的问题，以致我们感到，在这个短促的时期内，我们从解决这些问题的经验里有很多的收获。
    在我们取得独立时，如果我们要保持我们自由的果实，我们就必须完成很多工作。但是当我们一开始的时候，就碰到严重的内部问题，这些问题削弱了我们很多的力量和资源。尽管如此，我们在加强国防力量方面，在以新的民主管理制度代替了旧的政府制度以及在废除旧的经济措施和推行新的社会改革方面，都有了进步和成功。这样，缅甸从一个由于战争而变穷的国家，由于她自己的内部问题而受到不小的阻力的国家，在往前迈进。今天，我们的独立是安全的和巩固的。
    在我们的国际关系中，我们成功地和中华人民共和国一起倡导了历史性的五项原则，我们高兴地看到受这些原则所影响的地区在日益增大。我们现在看到不少于八十个国家已经接受了这些共处的原则，我们确信它们的影响将日益增长。
    我们还和一些友好的国家维持最紧密和良好的关系。并且，由于我们相信，没有一个国家能单独地保卫和平或为和平以及人类的进步而努力，因此，我们的代表在国际会议上已在发挥更为积极的作用。
    现在，我们来谈谈我们自己的关系，谈谈中缅两国有亲戚关系的人民之间的关系。我们可以说，这种关系比过去更加紧密了。随着两国各级领导人和人民之间相互访问的频繁和增多，目前存在的这种亲密的了解将大大地增强并将建筑在更巩固的基础上。
    今天，就在此刻，我们的吴觉迎副总理正同时在昆明庆祝这个周年纪念日，在我看来，目前没有别的事比他能在昆明这件事更为意味深长。
    当我们两国的领导人和各种地位的人民正在为建立一个更加坚固的友谊的大厦而加强努力的时候，允许我请你们和我们一起来为：
    中华人民共和国主席毛泽东先生的健康，
    为中缅两国之间永恒的友谊，
    为亚洲和世界和平，
    干杯！
