　　May Be a Negative Number
　　The U.S. House Government Operations Committee recently submitted a report accusing the United States of wasting military "aid". The report believes that the reason for this waste is that the allocation of military aid is often based on factors other than purely military ones, so that those countries receiving "aid" may not be able to provide a single soldier or jet pilot when actually needed. The report says regretfully that, as a result, the strength of the common security plan is merely on paper.
　　The fact that military "aid" only obtains strength on paper is, of course, very sad for the U.S. policy of aggression. However, if we see the condemnation and opposition to the U.S. policy of aggression caused by U.S. military "aid" in recipient countries, then its effect will not only be on paper, but will also be a negative number. This is probably something the U.S. House Government Operations Committee did not anticipate.

### 可能是负数

1958-01-19
第5版()
专栏：札记

    可能是负数
    美国众议院政府工作委员会最近提出报告，指责美国的军事“援助”被浪费了。报告认为，造成这种浪费的原因是由于分配军火援助往往是基于纯粹军事因素以外的其他东西，以致那些接受“援助”的国家，在实际需要时可能不能够提供一兵或一个喷气机驾驶员。报告以惋惜的口吻说，结果是，共同安全计划上的力量仅仅是纸上空文。
    军事“援助”所得到的仅仅是纸上空文的力量，这对于美国侵略政策来讲当然是十分可悲的。但是，如果看到美国的军事“援助”在受援国家所引起的对美国侵略政策的斥责和反对，那么，其效果就不只是纸上空文，而且会是负数了。这恐怕是美国众议院政府工作委员会所没有设想到的吧。
