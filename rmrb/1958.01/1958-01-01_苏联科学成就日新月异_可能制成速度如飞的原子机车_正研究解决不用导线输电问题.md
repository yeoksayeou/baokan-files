Title: Soviet Scientific Achievements are Advancing Rapidly; Atomic Locomotives with Lightning Speed May Be Made; Research on Solving the Problem of Wireless Power Transmission is Underway
Author:
Date: 1958-01-01
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, Moscow, 31st - TASS reported: Soviet Academician Blagonravov stated in an article published in the "Soviet Air Force News" on the 29th that the cermet alloy made by Soviet scientists using carbides of titanium and boron can withstand temperatures up to two thousand degrees.

In this article introducing Soviet scientific achievements, he also pointed out that the current level of technological development makes it possible to use nuclear fuel in transportation. Therefore, the navigable areas in the far north will be expanded, and scientific investigations can be carried out in the Arctic and Antarctic throughout the year. In railway transportation, atomic locomotives with the same speed as postal airplanes can be made, with a speed of 250 to 300 kilometers per hour. Small mobile nuclear power stations, either tracked or wheeled, are being designed, which can be used in the energy-deficient far north and mountainous areas.

Blagonravov wrote that atomic batteries will be made in the near future, which can supply power to complex radio instruments and various automatic instruments on artificial satellites.

Speaking of the Soviet Union's largest charged particle accelerator in the world—the synchrophasotron—Blagonravov said that these particles can travel a distance longer than a round trip from Earth to the Moon in just three seconds, with a speed almost equal to the speed of light. And now Soviet scientists are designing even more powerful synchrophasotrons. The academician believes that Soviet science is facing the problem of using solar energy and converting it into electrical energy.

He pointed out that research has already begun to solve the problem of wireless power transmission. Soviet scientists are studying the use of specialized transmitting antennas with strictly directed radio radiation to concentrate electricity into a thin beam for transmission.

Blagonravov finally said that some new artificial satellites will appear soon, and then the first rockets (initially unmanned) will open up the road to the Moon and other planets.

### 苏联科学成就日新月异  可能制成速度如飞的原子机车  正研究解决不用导线输电问题

1958-01-01
第5版()
专栏：

    苏联科学成就日新月异
    可能制成速度如飞的原子机车
    正研究解决不用导线输电问题
    新华社31日讯　据塔斯社莫斯科讯：苏联著名科学家布拉冈拉沃夫院士29日在“苏联空军报”发表文章说，苏联科学家用钛、硼的碳化物所制造的金属陶瓷合金能够经受的温度已经达到了两千度。
    他在这篇介绍苏联科学成就的文章中还指出，目前技术发展的水平使得在运输上使用核燃料已经成为可能。因而极北地带的通航地区将会扩大，北极和南极将终年都可以进行科学调查。在铁路运输方面可能制成速度与邮政飞机相同的原子机车，这种机车每小时可走二百五十至三百公里。履带式或轮式活动小型原子发电站正在设计中，这种发电站可用于缺乏能源的极北地区，山岳地区。
    布拉冈拉沃夫写道，原子电池在不久的将来即将制成，这种电池可以供电给人造卫星上的复杂的无线电仪表和各种各样的自动化仪器。
    在谈到苏联拥有的、世界最大的带电粒子加速器——同步稳相加速器的时候，布拉冈拉沃夫说，这些粒子只要三秒钟就可以走完比由地球到月球来回一趟还要远的距离，其速度几乎同光速相等。而现在苏联科学家还在设计更强大的同步稳相加速器。这位院士认为，苏联科学已经面临着运用太阳能和把太阳能变为电能的问题。
    他指出，现在已经开始研究解决不用导线输电的问题。苏联科学家正在研究用具有严格的无线电辐射方向的专门传导天线把电力集中成一个细小的射线输送出去。
    布拉冈拉沃夫最后说，很快就会出现一些新的人造卫星，而后第一批火箭（起先是无人的）就会开辟出通往月球和其他行星的道路。
