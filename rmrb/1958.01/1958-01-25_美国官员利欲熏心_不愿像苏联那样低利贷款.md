Title: U.S. Officials Are Greedy and Unwilling to Offer Low-Interest Loans Like the Soviet Union
Author:
Date: 1958-01-25
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, Washington, January 23rd: A statement released by the U.S. International Cooperation Administration on the 18th indicates that the United States does not intend to lower the interest rates on its loans to underdeveloped countries.

According to McIntosh, the head of the newly established U.S. "Development Loan Fund," the current interest rates on U.S. foreign "aid" long-term loans range from 3.5% to 5.7%; the interest rates on loans from the U.S. Export-Import Bank are 5.25% to 5.75%. In contrast, the interest rates on Soviet loans are only around 2%.

### 美国官员利欲熏心  不愿像苏联那样低利贷款

1958-01-25
第6版()
专栏：

    美国官员利欲熏心
    不愿像苏联那样低利贷款
    据新华社23日讯　华盛顿消息：美国国际合作署18日发表的公告表明，美国不打算降低它给经济不发达国家的贷款的利率。
    据美国新成立的“开发贷款基金”负责人麦金托什说，美国目前的对外“援助”长期贷款利率在3.5％到5.7％之间；美国进出口银行的贷款利率为5.25％到5.75％。而苏联贷款的利率只有2％左右。
