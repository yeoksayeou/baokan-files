Title: Sent from Death Row
Author: Yīsī'ěr Luósēnbó
Date: 1953-03-06
Page: 4

    Sent from Death Row
    Israel Rosenberg
    As far as the eye can see,
    Near and far, everywhere are bricks and steel,
    Above our heads, dark clouds loom,
    The winter sky is vast and cold.
    We are prisoners in death row,
    Waiting for doom—if the enemy succeeds.
    We two are forever fearless and calm,
    Even if surrounded by the shadows of death and terror,
    The courage of the people inspires us,
    Our strength comes from millions of kind hearts.
    "Oh, prisoners in death row, we have arrived!
    To rescue you from doom—we will!"
    We clearly hear them advancing, advancing,
    Forever with us, millions of people.
    The living, and those who have been framed,
    Will no longer be discriminated against—never again.
    At that time, the prisoners in death row
    Will break through the electric chair's death sentence.
    January 11, 1953, Sing Sing Prison.
    (Translated by Yuan Shuipai)

### 寄自死牢
伊斯尔·罗森堡
1953-03-06
第4版()
专栏：

    寄自死牢
    伊斯尔·罗森堡
    只要眼睛所及到的地方，
    不论远近，到处是砖块和钢，
    在我们头顶，笼罩着乌云，
    冬季的天空苍茫而寒冷。
    我们是死牢里的囚人，
    等候着厄运——如果敌人得逞。
    我们两人永远坦然无畏，
    那怕四周包围着死亡和恐怖的阴影，
    人民的勇气使我们振奋，
    我们的力量来自千万颗善良的心。
    “呵，死牢里的囚人，我们已经来临！
    来援救你们挣脱厄运——我们一定！”
    我们清楚地听到他们在前进，前进，
    永远和我们在一起，千百万人民。
    活着的，和那些被陷害的，
    再也不容受到歧视——再也不能。
    到那时候，死牢里的囚人
    将冲破电椅的死刑。
    一九五三年一月十一日，辛辛监狱。
    （袁水拍译）
