Title: China and the Soviet Union Further Expand Trade: Signing of this Year's Goods Turnover Protocol and More; The Soviet Union Helps China Build Power Stations and Supplies Industrial Equipment

Author: 
Date: 1953-03-27
Page: 1

    Xinhua News Agency, Moscow, March 26th. Negotiations between the Trade Delegation of the People's Republic of China and the Soviet Ministry of Domestic and Foreign Trade have concluded in an atmosphere of mutual understanding and friendship. Agreements were signed recently between China and the Soviet Union regarding the 1953 Goods Turnover Protocol, the Protocol based on the "Agreement on Granting Loans to the People's Republic of China of February 14, 1950," and the Agreement on Soviet assistance to China in expanding existing power stations and building new power stations. The aforementioned protocols stipulate the further expansion of trade between China and the Soviet Union. In 1953, the Soviet Union will supply China with equipment needed for metallurgy, mining, machine manufacturing, chemical, power and other industrial sectors, as well as materials needed for industry and transportation, new agricultural machinery, breeding livestock, seeds, and many other goods. China will supply the Soviet Union with non-ferrous metals, rice, vegetable oil, oilseeds, meat, tobacco leaves, tea, fruit, wool, jute, raw silk, silk fabrics, leather, and other goods.
    The signatories to the protocols and agreements were, on the Soviet side, Soviet Minister of Domestic and Foreign Trade, Mikoyan, and on the Chinese side, Vice-Chairman of the Financial and Economic Committee of the Central People's Government and Minister of Foreign Trade, Ye Jizhuang, and Vice-Chairman of the Financial and Economic Committee, Li Fuchun.
    Those present at the signing ceremony included, on the Chinese side, Premier of the State Administration Council and Minister of Foreign Affairs of the People's Republic of China, Zhou Enlai, Ambassador Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary of the People's Republic of China to the Soviet Union, Zhang Wentian, Commercial Counselor of the Embassy, Li Qiang, all members of the Trade Delegation of the People's Republic of China, and responsible personnel of the Chinese Embassy in the Soviet Union; on the Soviet side, First Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of the Soviet Union, Malik, Deputy Ministers of Domestic and Foreign Trade of the Soviet Union, Kumykin and Borisov, Ambassador Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary of the Soviet Union to the People's Republic of China, Kuznetsov, as well as responsible personnel of the Soviet Ministry of Foreign Affairs and the Soviet Ministry of Domestic and Foreign Trade. ### 中苏两国进一步扩大贸易  签订今年度货物周转议定书等  苏联帮助我建设电站和供给我工业设备

1953-03-27
第1版()
专栏：

    中苏两国进一步扩大贸易
    签订今年度货物周转议定书等
    苏联帮助我建设电站和供给我工业设备
    【新华社莫斯科二十六日电】中华人民共和国商务代表团与苏联国内和对外贸易部间的谈判，已在相互了解的友好气氛中结束，并于日前签订了中苏间关于一九五三年度货物周转的议定书、根据“一九五零年二月十四日关于贷款给中华人民共和国的协定”的议定书以及关于苏联帮助中国扩大现有电力站和建设新电力站的协定。上述各议定书规定了中苏间贸易的进一步扩大。苏联在一九五三年内将供给中国以冶金、采掘、机器制造、化工、电力和其他工业部门所需要的设备，以及工业和运输所需要的材料，新式的农业机械、种畜、种子和许多其他货物。中国则以有色金属、大米、植物油、油籽、肉、烟叶、茶叶、水果、羢毛、黄麻、生丝、绸缎、皮革及其他货物供给苏联。
    在议定书和协定上签字者，苏联方面为苏联国内和对外贸易部部长米高扬，中国方面为中央人民政府政务院财政经济委员会副主任兼对外贸易部部长叶季壮和财政经济委员会副主任李富春。出席签字仪式者，中国方面为中华人民共和国中央人民政府政务院总理兼外交部部长周恩来，中华人民共和国驻苏联特命全权大使张闻天、大使馆商务参赞李强，中华人民共和国商务代表团全体团员和中国驻苏联大使馆负责人员；苏联方面为苏联外交部第一副部长马立克，苏联国内和对外贸易部副部长库米金、波里索夫，苏联驻中华人民共和国特命全权大使库兹涅佐夫，以及苏联外交部、苏联国内和对外贸易部的负责人员。（附图片）
