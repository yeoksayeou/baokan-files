Title: British Writers Publish Open Letter Demanding Major Powers Hold Meeting to Achieve Peace
Author:
Date: 1953-03-30
Page: 4
    Xinhua News Agency, London, March 29th: The London "Times" published a letter from a group of British writers on the 25th, calling on the world's major powers to hold a meeting to seek peace in the Far East.

    The letter stated that the British people know that if a direct or indirect attack is launched against China, the danger of world war will become more serious. Few people believe that anything other than this will result.

    The letter said: "Even fewer people believe that the Indochina War can be resolved by military means, or that the Japanese rearmament, which is about to be implemented, will produce any result other than hindering peace negotiations."

    The letter said: "We call on the major powers of Europe, Asia, and the Americas to open up the negotiation channels that are now deadlocked, and for high-level authorities to hold meetings to find solutions to the Far East problem."

    The letter was signed by Akase, Cecil Day-Lewis, Compton MacKenzie, Herbert Read, Christopher, and others.

### 英国一批作家发表公开信  要求大国举行会议实现和平

1953-03-30
第4版()
专栏：

    英国一批作家发表公开信
    要求大国举行会议实现和平
    【新华社二十九日讯】伦敦消息：伦敦“泰晤士报”二十五日发表一批英国作家的来信，信中要求世界各大国举行会议，以求得实现远东的和平。
    这封信说，英国人都知道，如果向中国发动直接或间接的攻击，世界大战的危险就更加严重。很少有人以为除此之外会产生其他的结果。
    这封信说：“更少有人以为印度支那战争可以用军事方法解决，或者马上就要实行的日本重新武装会产生除了妨碍和平谈判以外的其他结果。”
    这封信说：“我们要求欧洲、亚洲和美洲的主要大国敞开现在已陷于僵局的谈判途径，而由高级当局举行会议，以找出解决远东问题的办法。”
    在信上签名的有阿卡塞、西西尔·戴·刘易斯、坎普顿·麦康西、赫伯特·李德、克里斯托夫等人。
