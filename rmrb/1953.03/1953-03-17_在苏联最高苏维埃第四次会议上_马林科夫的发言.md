Title: Malenkov's Speech at the Fourth Session of the Supreme Soviet of the Soviet Union
Author:
Date: 1953-03-17
Page: 1

Comrade Deputies!

You have instructed me to submit the list of the government—the Council of Ministers of the Soviet Union—to the Supreme Soviet of the Union of Soviet Socialist Republics for consideration.

Comrades, I thank you for the great trust and lofty honor you have given me. (Stormy applause)

Comrades, you already know that the joint plenary session of the Central Committee of the Communist Party of the Soviet Union, the Council of Ministers of the Soviet Union, and the Presidium of the Supreme Soviet of the Soviet Union has adopted a number of important resolutions aimed at ensuring the uninterrupted and correct leadership of the country's life.

At this meeting, we must review these resolutions and, in accordance with the Constitution, give legal effect to some measures that our highest state power organ—the Supreme Soviet of the Soviet Union—deems possible and necessary to accept and approve.

It should be stated that the timing for taking measures to merge existing ministries and to merge the leadership of related departments of the national economy, culture, and administration is not only ripe today. Even when Comrade Stalin was alive, our party and government had been considering these measures with him for a long time. Now, at this time when our country has suffered this heavy loss, we have merely accelerated the adoption of organizational measures that have matured for further improvement of the leadership of state and economic activities.

In suggesting these measures, we took into account that we must intensify our efforts to implement the plans for further development of the Soviet Union formulated by the party and government, and these plans must be implemented more actively and smoothly.

We also took into account that the implementation of the organizational measures to improve state and economic leadership submitted to the Supreme Soviet of the Soviet Union for consideration will undoubtedly create better conditions for the successful completion of the historical tasks facing our country, which are: to continuously and comprehensively consolidate our great multi-ethnic socialist country, to strengthen the powerful Soviet armed forces in order to ensure the defense and security of our motherland; to do our best to develop socialist industry, consolidate the collective farm system, develop culture, improve the material welfare of workers, collective farmers, intellectuals and all Soviet people; and to guarantee the further development of the cause of building a communist society in our country.

We have the possibility of successfully carrying out the reorganization of the merger of ministries and obtaining all the benefits of this reorganization because we have cadres who have grown greatly, have rich experience, and have the ability to lead the expanded ministries.

The following suggestions are submitted to the Supreme Soviet of the Soviet Union for consideration.

To merge the Soviet Ministry of State Security and the Soviet Ministry of Internal Affairs into one ministry, namely the Soviet Ministry of Internal Affairs;

To merge the Soviet Ministry of Military Affairs and the Ministry of the Navy into one ministry, namely the Soviet Ministry of Defense;

To merge the Ministry of Foreign Trade and the Soviet Ministry of Commerce into one ministry, namely the Soviet Ministry of Domestic and Foreign Trade;

To merge the Ministry of Agriculture , the Ministry of Cotton Growing, the Ministry of State Farms, the Ministry of Agricultural Procurement, and the Ministry of Forestry into one ministry, namely the Soviet Ministry of Agriculture and Agricultural Procurement;

To merge the Ministry of Higher Education, the Ministry of Film Industry, the Arts Committee, the All-Union Radio Broadcasting Committee, the Central Printing Bureau, and the Labor Reserve Department into one ministry, namely the Soviet Ministry of Culture;

To merge the Ministry of Light Industry, the Ministry of Food Industry, the Ministry of Meat and Dairy Industry, and the Ministry of Fisheries into one ministry, namely the Soviet Ministry of Light Industry and Food Industry;

To merge the Ministry of Iron and Steel Industry and the Ministry of Nonferrous Metals Industry into one ministry, namely the Ministry of Metallurgical Industry;

To merge the Ministry of Automobile and Tractor Industry, the Ministry of Machinery and Instrument Manufacturing, the Ministry of Agricultural Machinery Manufacturing, and the Ministry of Machine Tool Manufacturing into one ministry, namely the Ministry of Machine Building Industry;

To merge the Ministry of Transport Machine Building, the Ministry of Shipbuilding Industry, the Ministry of Heavy Machine Building, and the Ministry of Construction and Road Construction Machinery into one ministry, namely the Ministry of Transport and Heavy Machine Building Industry;

To merge the Ministry of Power Stations, the Ministry of Electrical Industry, and Ministry of Communication Equipment Industry into one ministry, namely the Ministry of Power Stations and Electrical Engineering Industry;

To merge the Ministry of Armaments and the Ministry of Aviation Industry into one ministry, namely the Ministry of Defense Industry;

To merge the Soviet Ministry of Timber Industry and the Ministry of Paper and Woodworking Industry into one ministry, namely the Soviet Ministry of Timber and Paper Industry;

To merge the Ministry of Construction of Heavy Industry Enterprises and the Ministry of Construction of Machine Building Enterprises into one ministry, namely the Ministry of Construction;

To merge the Ministry of Maritime Shipping, the Ministry of River Shipping, and the Central Administration of the Northern Sea Route into one ministry, namely the Ministry of Maritime and River Shipping.

To abolish the Ministry of Geological Survey and transfer its work separately the Ministry of Metallurgical Industry, the Ministry of Coal Industry, the Ministry of Petroleum Industry, the ministry Chemical Industry, the Ministry of Building Materials Industry and other ministries governing the mining industry;

To abolish the Soviet Ministry of Automobile Transportation and transfer its work to the Ministry of Transportation;

To merge the State Committee for Material and Technical Supply of the National Economy under the Council of Ministers of the Soviet Union and the State Committee for the Supply of Food and Industrial Products under the Council of Ministers of the Soviet Union with the Soviet State Planning Commission.

Comrade Deputies!

Comrade Deputies!

The joint plenary session of the Central Committee of the Communist Party of the Soviet Union, the Council of Ministers of the Soviet Union, and the Presidium of the Supreme Soviet of the Soviet Union, when submitting the proposal on the government list to the Supreme Soviet of the Soviet Union for consideration, started from the fact that the strength of our leadership lies in its collectivity, unity, and consolidation. We believe that strict adherence to this supreme principle is a guarantee of correct leadership of the country and a major prerequisite for the victorious progress of communist construction in our country.

In submitting the government list to the Supreme Soviet of the Soviet Union for approval, I believe it is necessary to state that the government, in all its activities, will strictly follow the foreign and domestic policies formulated by the Party. We have declared this position of the Soviet government. I remember the speeches made by myself, Comrade L.P. Beria, and Comrade V.M. Molotov at the memorial service on March 9th.

Regarding domestic policy, it has been made very clear in our speeches that the Soviet government will continue to strengthen the unbreakable alliance between the working class and the collective farmers, strengthen the fraternal friendship of the people of all nationalities in our country, and strive to consolidate the defense capabilities of the socialist country. The guideline of the Soviet government is to unremittingly strive for the welfare of the people, for the maximum satisfaction of their material and cultural needs, and for the further prosperity of the socialist motherland. (Stormy applause)

Regarding foreign policy, it was also fully explained in our speeches that the Soviet government will always consistently pursue the long-tested policy of safeguarding and consolidating peace, the policy of ensuring the defense and security of the Soviet Union, and the policy of cooperation and development of affairs with all countries on the basis of mutual benefit, the Soviet government will continue to cooperate closely with the great Chinese people and the people of various people's democracies in politics and economy, and strengthen their fraternal friendship and unity.
(Stormy applause)

The Soviet Union's peace policy is based on respect for the rights of the people of other countries, large and small, and on adherence to generally accepted international norms; the Soviet Union's foreign policy is based on strict and unwavering adherence to all treaties concluded by the Soviet Union with other countries. Now, there are no disputes or outstanding issues that cannot be peacefully resolved on the basis of mutual agreement among the countries concerned. This refers to our relations with all countries, including the United States.

Countries that care about maintaining peace can now and in the future trust the Soviet Union's lasting peace policy. (Stormy applause)

Comrade Deputies!

The Soviet people are deeply convinced of their own strength. The strength of the Soviet state is unprecedentedly strong, and the spiritual and political unity of the Soviet people is unprecedentedly consolidated. The Soviet government will strive with all its strength to build a communist society in our country and to improve the free and happy life of the Soviet people. (Stormy applause)

Our big family of harmonious friendship among all nationalities of the Soviet Union will advance along the road of communist construction in the close unity of the Party, the government, and the Soviet people. (Everyone stands, stormy and prolonged applause)

### 在苏联最高苏维埃第四次会议上  马林科夫的发言

1953-03-17

    在苏联最高苏维埃第四次会议上
    马林科夫的发言代表同志们！
    你们指令我把政府——苏联部长会议的名单提交苏维埃社会主义共和国联盟最高苏维埃审议。
    同志们，我感谢你们给我的巨大信任和崇高荣誉。（暴风雨般的掌声）
    同志们，你们已经知道苏联共产党中央委员会、苏联部长会议、苏联最高苏维埃主席团联席全体会议已通过了旨在保证国家生活得到不间断的和正确的领导的若干重要决议。
    在这次会议上，我们必须审查这些决议，并且依据宪法，使我国最高国家权力机关——苏联最高苏维埃认为可以并且必须接受和批准的一些措施具有法律效力。
    应该说明，采取措施，合并现有各部，把国民经济、文化和行政各个有关联的部门的领导合并在一起，其时机并不是今天才成熟的。在斯大林同志在世的时候，我们的党和政府就已经和他一道把这些措施考虑了很久。现在，在我们国家遭受到这个沉重的损失的时候，我们只不过是加速采取了已经成熟的进一步改进国家和经济活动的领导的组织措施而已。
    在建议这些措施的时候，我们考虑到：我们必须加紧努力执行党和政府所制订的苏联进一步发展的计划，必须更加积极地更加顺利地执行这些计划。
    我们还考虑到，提交苏联最高苏维埃审议的改进国家和经济领导的组织措施的实行无疑地将为顺利完成我国面临的历史性任务创造更好的条件，这些任务是：不断地、全面地巩固我们伟大的多民族的社会主义国家，加强强大的苏联武装部队，以便确保我们祖国的防务和安全；尽力发展社会主义工业，巩固集体农庄制度，发展文化，增进工人、集体农民、知识分子和全体苏联人民的物质福利；保证进一步发展在我国建设共产主义社会的事业。

    把高等教育部、电影事业部、艺术委员会、全苏无线电广播委员会、中央印刷出版局和劳动后备部合并成一个部，即苏联文化部；
    把轻工业部、食品工业部、肉乳工业部和渔业部合并成一个部，即苏联轻工业和食品工业部；
    把钢铁工业部和有色金属工业部合并成一个部，即冶金工业部；
    把汽车与拖拉机工业部、机器与器械制造部、农业机器制造部和母机制造部合并成一个部，即机器制造工业部；
