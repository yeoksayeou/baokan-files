Title: The Thriving Democratic World Market: Soviet Union, Hungary, Poland-Romania, Poland-Albania, and Poland-Hungary Sign Commodity Exchange and Payment Agreements
Author:
Date: 1953-03-27
Page: 4

    Democratic World Market is Thriving
    Soviet Union, Hungary, Poland-Romania, Poland-Albania, and Poland-Hungary Sign Commodity Exchange and Payment Agreements
    [Xinhua News Agency, Moscow, 26th] According to TASS, after negotiations conducted in a friendly atmosphere of mutual understanding between the Ministry of Domestic and Foreign Trade of the Soviet Union and the trade delegation of the Hungarian People's Republic, a Soviet-Hungarian commodity exchange agreement for 1953 was signed on the 24th. The agreement stipulates that the trade volume between the Soviet Union and Hungary in 1953 will greatly exceed the trade volume in 1952 and the trade volume in 1953 stipulated in the long-term Soviet-Hungarian trade agreement signed on January 23, 1952. The Soviet Union will supply Hungary with the metallurgical, mining equipment and other industrial equipment it needs, as well as cotton, iron ore, coking coal and agricultural machinery. Hungary will supply the Soviet Union with seagoing and inland river vessels, cranes, transportation equipment and other equipment, as well as locomotives and general consumer goods.
    The agreement was signed on behalf of the Soviet Union by A. I. Mikoyan, Minister of Domestic and Foreign Trade of the Soviet Union, and on behalf of Hungary by An. Sobek, Minister of Foreign Trade of Hungary.
     [Xinhua News Agency, 25th] Poland and Romania, Albania, and Hungary have recently signed new barter and payment agreements respectively. The 1953 barter and payment agreement between Poland and Romania stipulates that Poland will export rolled metal, zinc, coking coal, machines and machine tools to Romania, and Romania will export petroleum products, agricultural products, drilling equipment and metal products to Poland. The 1953-1955 barter and payment agreement between Poland and Albania stipulates that Poland will export locomotives, vehicles, coal mining industrial equipment and textiles to Albania, and Albania will export asphalt, petroleum, tobacco, etc. to Poland. Poland has also signed a 1953 barter and payment agreement with Hungary.

### 民主世界市场欣欣向荣  苏匈、波罗、波阿、波匈分别签订了换货和付款协定

1953-03-27
第4版()
专栏：

    民主世界市场欣欣向荣
    苏匈、波罗、波阿、波匈分别签订了换货和付款协定
    【新华社二十六日讯】据塔斯社莫斯科讯：苏联国内和对外贸易部与匈牙利人民共和国贸易代表团在友好和互相谅解的气氛中进行谈判后，已在二十四日签订了苏匈一九五三年换货协定。协定规定，苏匈一九五三年的贸易额将大大超过一九五二年的贸易额，和一九五二年一月二十三日签订的苏匈长期贸易协定所规定的一九五三年的贸易额。苏联将供给匈牙利所需的冶金、采矿设备和其他工业设备，以及棉花、铁矿、焦煤和农业机器等。匈牙利将供给苏联航海和内河轮船、起重机、运输设备和其他设备，以及机车和一般消费品等。
    代表苏联方面签字的是苏联国内和对外贸易部部长阿·伊·米高扬，代表匈牙利方面签字的是匈牙利对外贸易部部长安·索贝克。
     【新华社二十五日讯】波兰和罗马尼亚、阿尔巴尼亚、匈牙利最近分别签订了新的易货和付款协定。波兰和罗马尼亚一九五三年易货和付款协定规定：波兰向罗马尼亚输出压延金属、锌、焦煤、机器和工作母机等，罗马尼亚向波兰输出石油产品、农产品、钻探设备和金属品等。波兰和阿尔巴尼亚一九五三—一九五五年易货和付款协定规定：波兰向阿尔巴尼亚输出机车、车辆、采煤工业设备和纺织品等，阿尔巴尼亚向波兰输出沥青、石油、烟草等。波兰也和匈牙利签订了一九五三年的易货和付款协定。
