Title: All-China Journalists Association Protests Dutch Government's Oppression of Overseas Chinese Journalists
Author:
Date: 1950-06-18
Page: 1

　　All-China Journalists Association
    Protests Dutch Government's Oppression of Overseas Chinese Journalists
    [Xinhua News Agency, 17th] According to news recently received here: Chen Changdai, editor-in-chief of the Dutch Overseas Chinese publication "People's Century," received a notice from the Dutch government on April 22, ordering him to leave the country with his family within sixty days. Upon hearing this news, the Beijing press circles were deeply concerned. The All-China Journalists Association sent a telegram to Dutch Prime Minister Drees today to protest. The telegram stated: "We protest the unreasonable action of the Dutch government in ordering Chen Changdai, editor-in-chief of the Overseas Chinese publication "People's Century," to leave the Netherlands within sixty days, and demand that the Dutch government immediately withdraw the departure notice against Mr. Chen."

### 全国新闻工作者协会  抗议荷政府压迫华侨新闻工作者

1950-06-18
第1版()
专栏：

　　全国新闻工作者协会
    抗议荷政府压迫华侨新闻工作者
    【新华社十七日讯】据最近传抵此间的消息称：荷兰华侨刊物《人民世纪》主编陈昌岱，于四月二十二日接到了荷兰政府限其在六十天内携眷离境的通知。北京新闻界闻讯后，对此深为关切。中华全国新闻工作者协会特于本日致电荷兰总理特里斯提出抗议。电称：“我们抗议荷兰政府限令华侨刊物《人民世纪》主编陈昌岱在六十天内离荷的无理举措，并要求荷政府立即撤回对陈君的离境通知。”
