Title: Beijing Municipal Federation of Literary and Art Circles Established; Literary and Art Workers Congress Concludes, Clarifying the "Popularization First" Policy
Author:
Date: 1950-06-01
Page: 1

　　Beijing Municipal Federation of Literary and Art Circles Established
    Literary and Art Workers Congress Concludes
    Clarifying the "Popularization First" Policy
    [Report from this newspaper] According to the Beijing Municipal News Agency, the Beijing Municipal Congress of Literary and Art Workers concluded successfully on May 31st after four days of meetings. Premier Zhou Enlai of the Central People's Government's State Council took time out of his busy schedule to attend and provide guidance. The congress discussed and approved the "Charter of the Beijing Municipal Federation of Literary and Art Circles" and 75 proposals from representatives, plus 7 supplementary proposals; it elected 45 directors of the federation, including Lao She, and 5 alternate directors (list below). The "Beijing Municipal Federation of Literary and Art Circles" was thus formally established. The congress also passed a telegram of respect to Chairman Mao and a telegram to the World Congress of Supporters of Peace expressing support for peace. The congress was filled with a warm and harmonious atmosphere from beginning to end, demonstrating the great unity of the capital's literary and art workers. In her summary report, Li Bozhao pointed out that the biggest gain of this congress was that the literary and art workers of Beijing had ideologically clarified the "popularization first" policy for future literary and art work. The congress representatives expressed their firm support for and implementation of the three reports from the meeting. After Zhao Shuli delivered the closing remarks, the congress concluded victoriously with all the representatives shouting slogans such as "Long live Chairman Mao."
    The directors of the Beijing Municipal Federation of Literary and Art Circles elected by the congress are: Lao She, Wang Yaping, Mei Lanfang, Li Bozhao, Zhao Shuli, Ouyang Yuqian, Lian Kuoru, Zhang Menggeng, Feng Zi, Duanmu Hongliang, Xin Fengxia, Lao Zhicheng, Miao Peishi, Tian Han, Li Guangtian, Hu Man, Cao Baolu, Hou Baolin, Dai Ailian, Xu Beihong, Shang Xiaoyun, Cheng Yanqiu, Jiao Juyin, Tian Jian, Wang Songsheng, Luo Changpei, Ye Shengzhang, Wang Yaoqing, Yang Zhensheng, Hong Shen, Zhou Weizhi, Ma Ke, Ma Shaobo, Jin Ziguang, Lu Su, Ye Qianyu, She Shiguang, Ding Li, Zhang Shirong, Liang Xiaoluan, Ruan Zhangjing, Cai Ruohong, Han Shichang, Zu Tiangong, and Li Bokang. The alternate directors are: Lin Geng, Tian Fang, Cai Jinbo, Jin Shan, and Ma Feng.

### 京市文联成  文艺工作者代表大会闭幕  明确了“普及第一”的方针

1950-06-01
第1版()
专栏：

　　京市文联成
    文艺工作者代表大会闭幕
    明确了“普及第一”的方针
    【本报讯】据京市新闻处讯：北京市文艺工作者代表大会经过四天会议，已于五月三十一日圆满闭幕，中央人民政府政务院周恩来总理并于百忙中抽暇莅会指导。大会讨论通过了“北京市文学艺术工作者联合会章程”及代表提案七十五件，补充提案七件；选出联合会理事老舍等四十五人及候补理事五人（名单见后）。“北京市文学艺术工作者联合会”于此宣告正式成立。大会并通过向毛主席致敬电和致世界拥护和平大会表示拥护和平电。大会自始至终充满着热烈、和谐的气氛，表现了首都文艺工作者的大团结。李伯钊在总结报告时指出这次大会最大收获是北京市的文艺工作者在思想上明确了今后文艺工作“普及第一”的方针。大会代表纷纷表示坚决拥护及执行此次会议的三个报告。大会由赵树理致闭会词后，在全体代表高呼“毛主席万岁”等口号声中胜利闭幕。
    大会选出的北京市文学艺术工作者联合会理事为：老舍、王亚平、梅兰芳、李伯钊、赵树理、欧阳予倩、连阔如、张梦庚、凤子、端木蕻良、新凤霞、老志诚、苗培时、田汉、李广田、胡蛮、曹宝禄、侯宝林、戴爱莲、徐悲鸿、尚小云、程砚秋、焦菊隐、田间、王松声、罗常培、叶盛章、王瑶卿、杨振声、洪深、周巍峙、马可、马少波、金紫光、芦肃、叶浅予、佘世光、丁里、张世荣、梁小鸾、阮章竞、蔡若虹、韩世昌、祖田工、李伯康。候补理事为：林庚、田方、蔡金波、金山、马烽。
