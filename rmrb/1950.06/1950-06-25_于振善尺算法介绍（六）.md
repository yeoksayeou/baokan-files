　　Introduction to Yu Zhanshan's Slide Rule Algorithm (6)
    Six. Consecutive Subtraction Algorithm
    Number A - Number B - Number C = Total Difference
    Rule: The same rule as used in the consecutive addition algorithm in Section 5.
    Method:
    (1) Align Number B on the right side of 0 on the upper scale with Number A on the lower scale.
    (2) The number on the lower scale that aligns with Number C on the left side of 0 on the upper scale is the total difference sought.
    [Note] Numbers B and C can be interchanged, and different values of C will yield different total differences.
    (Example) A comrade has 150 yuan, spends 70 yuan, and then spends another 30 yuan. How much money is left?
    Method:
    (Attached Figure [Figure])
    (1) Align 7 on the upper scale (representing 70 yuan) to the right of 0 with 15 on the lower scale (representing 150 yuan).
    (2) The number 5 on the lower scale that aligns with 3 on the upper scale to the left of 0 (representing 30 yuan) is the total difference sought.
    Therefore, we find: 50 yuan remains.
    Seven. Multiplication
    Multiplicand × Multiplier = Product
    Rule: Square slide rule calculator (including one positive upper scale and one lower scale each) or circular slide rule calculator, or rectangular slide rule calculator.
    When using a square slide rule calculator, fix the 0 point of the upper scale on the 0 line of the lower scale.
    Method:
    (1) Align 1 or 10 on the upper scale with the multiplier (or multiplicand) on the lower scale;
    (2) The number on the lower scale that aligns with the multiplicand (or multiplier) on the upper scale is the product sought.
    Positioning Method (determining the number of digits in the product):
    (1) Several is called a one-digit number, tens are called a two-digit number, hundreds are called a three-digit number, thousands are called a four-digit number, ten thousands are called a five-digit number... and so on.
    (2) Positioning is divided into two situations:
    ① If the first digit of the product is smaller than the first digit of the multiplier or multiplicand, the number of digits in the product is equal to the number of digits in the multiplier plus the number of digits in the multiplicand.
    ② If the first digit of the product is equal to or greater than the first digit of the multiplier or multiplicand, the number of digits in the product is equal to the number of digits in the multiplier plus the number of digits in the multiplicand minus one.
    (Note) When using a square slide rule calculator, the inclination of the upper scale should not be too small for ease of reading. Sometimes it is necessary to interchange the upper and lower scales. This usage is explained in "Example 2."

### 于振善尺算法介绍（六）

1950-06-25
第4版()
专栏：

　　于振善尺算法介绍（六）
    六、连减算法
    甲数—乙数—丙数＝总差
    用尺：与第五节连加算法所用的尺相同。
    求法：
    （１）把天尺上０右边的乙数对准地尺上的甲数。
    （２）天尺上的０左边的丙数所对着的地尺上的数即是所求的总差。
    〔注〕乙、丙二数可以互相调换，不同的丙数可得出不同的总差。
    （例题）某同志有１５０元，用去７０元，又用去３０元，问还剩多少元？
    求法：
    （附图  [图]）
    （１）把天尺上的０右边的７（代表７０元）对准地尺上的１５（代表１５０元）。
    （２）天尺上０左边的３（代表３０元）对着的地尺上的５，即是所求的总差。
    所以求得：还剩５０元。
    七、乘法
    被乘数×乘数＝乘积
    用尺：方形尺算器（包括正数天尺及地尺各一个）或圆形尺算器，或长形尺算器。
    用方形尺算器时将天尺的０点固定在地尺的０线上。
    求法：
    （１）把天尺的１或１０对准地尺上的乘数（或被乘数）；
    （２）天尺上的被乘数（或乘数）对着的地尺上的数即是所求的乘积。
    定位法（决定乘积的位数）：
    （１）几个叫一位数，几十叫二位数，几百叫三位数，几千叫四位数，几万叫五位数……其余依此类推。
    （２）定位法分两种情形：
    ①如乘积的第一位数，小于乘数或被乘数的第一位时，乘积的位数等于乘数的位数加被乘数的位数。
    ②如乘积的第一位数，等于或大于乘数或被乘数的第一位数时，乘积的位数等于乘数的位数加被乘数的位数减一。
    （注）用方形尺算器时，为了读数方便起见，天尺的倾斜度不能太小。有时需要把天尺和地尺交换来用。这种用法在“例题２”中说明。
