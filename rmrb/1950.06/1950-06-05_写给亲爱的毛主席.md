Title: To Our Dear Chairman Mao
Author: Ma Lianyu, Zhang Shenglin
Date: 1950-06-05
Page: 4

To Our Dear Chairman Mao,

Respected Chairman Mao:

Today is Children's Day. We are all very happy to be celebrating this day. Especially we, the students of the Half-Day Primary School, feel even more joyful. Because it is the first time for us to celebrate such a happy holiday. Before liberation, most of us were poor children selling newspapers, selling candy, and picking coal cinders.

The People's Liberation Army liberated Beijing, and we, the children of the laboring people, were finally emancipated and had the opportunity to receive education and enter the Half-Day Primary School. This makes us deeply feel that the laboring people are indeed valued and cared for in the new society. The Communist Party and you are truly our saviors.

Among our classmates, there are still many who sell newspapers, sell candy, or are older sisters who have no mothers and must do chores and cook at home. They all participate in labor besides studying every day. We can all help and love each other. Under the guidance of our volunteer teachers, we are all working hard to study.

Although we have not yet established a Young Pioneers organization in our school, we must cultivate the good style of the Young Pioneers, and develop good habits of loving the motherland, loving the people, loving science, loving labor, and cherishing public property.

Respectfully wishing you eternal health!

　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Ma Lianyu, Beijing Seventh District Half-Day Primary School
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　June 1

Dear Chairman Mao:

In the past, under the rule of the Kuomintang, the people of Guangdong suffered exploitation and could not live a good life. Fortunately, on the evening of October 14, 1949, the heroic People's Liberation Army under your leadership liberated Guangzhou and saved the people of the whole city. Now it is dawn, and the people have been emancipated. We children must study hard, and when we grow up, we will join the People's Liberation Army under your leadership.

Wishing you good health!

　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Respectfully submitted by Zhang Shenglin, Third Grade, Guangzhou Municipal Third Primary School
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　May 9

### 写给亲爱的毛主席
马连玉　张盛林
1950-06-05
第4版()
专栏：

　　写给亲爱的毛主席敬爱的毛主席：
    今天是儿童节。我们都很快乐的过着这个日子。尤其是我们半日小学的同学们，更感到高兴。因为我们过这样快乐的节日还是第一次。在解放以前，我们大部分是卖报的、卖糖的、捡煤核的贫苦孩子。
    解放军解放了北京，我们这群劳动人民的子女才翻了身，得到了受教育的机会，进了半日小学。这使我们深切地感到：劳动人民在新社会里的确受到了重视和爱护。共产党和您真是我们的救星。
    我们的同学中现在还有许多是卖报的、卖糖的，或者是没有母亲而必须在家做活做饭的姐姐妹妹们。他们每天在学习以外，都参加劳动。我们都能做到互爱互助。我们在义务老师的教导下，都在努力学习。
    目前我们学校里虽还没有建立起少年儿童队的组织；但是我们一定也要养成少年儿童队的好作风，养成爱祖国、爱人民、爱科学、爱劳动，爱护公共财产的好习惯。
    敬祝永远健康！
                  北京市七区半日小学 
                              马连玉
                          六月一日亲爱的毛主席：
    在过去国民党统治下广东的人民受尽剥削，不能过好的日子。幸亏在一九四九年十月十四日晚上，你领导下的英勇的人民解放军解放了广州，救了全市的老百姓。现在天亮了，人民翻身了。我们小朋友们一定要努力学习，待长大成人后，参加你领导的人民解放军。
    祝你健康！
                    广州市立三小三年级  张盛林敬上
                           五月九日
