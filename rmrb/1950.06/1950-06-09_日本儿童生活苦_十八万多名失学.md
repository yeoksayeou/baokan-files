Title: Japanese Children's Hard Lives: More Than 180,000 Out of School
Author:
Date: 1950-06-09
Page: 1

　　Xinhua News Agency, Tokyo, June 2nd (TASS): The Communications News Agency reported: The All-Japan Teachers Union recently conducted a survey of 706 middle school students and 1,406 elementary school students in Tokyo.

　　The survey results prove that the living environment of children is mostly very poor, which greatly affects their academic progress. Approximately 3% of elementary school students and 7.2% of middle school students are forced to work. There are 182,620 school-age children throughout Japan who do not have the opportunity to attend school.

### 日本儿童生活苦  十八万多名失学

1950-06-09
第1版()
专栏：

　　日本儿童生活苦
    十八万多名失学
    【新华社讯】塔斯社东京二日电：通讯新闻社报道：日本全国教师联合会最近进行调查东京七百零六个中学生与一千四百零六个小学生的情况。
    调查的结果证明儿童的生活环境大多是很坏的，因此大大影响他们的学业进步。约有百分之三的小学生与百分之七点二的中学生被迫去工作。日本全国有十八万二千六百二十名学龄儿童没有入学的机会。
