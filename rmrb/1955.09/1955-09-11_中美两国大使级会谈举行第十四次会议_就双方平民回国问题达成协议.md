Title: 14th Ambassadorial Meeting Between China and the United States Held; Agreement Reached on the Issue of Civilian Repatriation
Author:
Date: 1955-09-11
Page: 1

Xinhua News Agency, Geneva, September 10th: The 14th ambassadorial meeting between China and the United States in Geneva was held on September 10th. At the meeting, the ambassadorial representatives of China and the United States reached the following agreement on the issue of civilian repatriation:

Statement of Agreement Between the Ambassadors of the People's Republic of China and the United States of America:

The Ambassadors of the People's Republic of China and the United States of America agree to announce the measures taken by their respective governments regarding the repatriation of civilians to their home countries.

Regarding the issue of Americans in the People's Republic of China, Ambassador Wang Bingnan, on behalf of the People's Republic of China, notified Ambassador U. Alexis Johnson as follows:

　　(1) The People's Republic of China recognizes that Americans in the People's Republic of China who wish to return to the United States of America enjoy the right to return, and declares that it has taken and will continue to take appropriate measures to enable them to exercise their right to return as quickly as possible.

　　(2) The Government of the United Kingdom will be entrusted to provide assistance to Americans who wish to return to the United States of America, as follows:

　　　　(1) If any American believes that their departure has been obstructed contrary to the policies announced by the People's Republic of China, they may notify the Chargé d'Affaires of the United Kingdom in the People's Republic of China and request them to negotiate with the Government of the People's Republic of China on their behalf. If the United States of America is willing, the Government of the United Kingdom may also investigate the facts of any such incident.

　　　　(2) If any American in the People's Republic of China wishes to return to the United States of America but has difficulty raising the cost of repatriation, the Government of the United Kingdom may provide the necessary financial assistance to enable them to return home.

　　(3) The Government of the People's Republic of China will widely publicize the above arrangements, and the Chargé d'Affaires of the United Kingdom in the People's Republic of China may do the same.

Regarding the issue of Chinese in the United States of America, Ambassador U. Alexis Johnson, on behalf of the United States of America, notified Ambassador Wang Bingnan as follows:

　　(1) The United States of America recognizes that Chinese in the United States of America who wish to return to the People's Republic of China enjoy the right to return, and declares that it has taken and will continue to take appropriate measures to enable them to exercise their right to return as quickly as possible.

　　(2) The Government of the Republic of India will be entrusted to provide assistance to Chinese who wish to return to the People's Republic of China, as follows:

　　　　(1) If any Chinese believes that their departure has been obstructed contrary to the policies announced by the United States of America, they may notify the Embassy of the Republic of India in the United States of America and request them to negotiate with the Government of the United States of America on their behalf. If the People's Republic of China is willing, the Government of the Republic of India may also investigate the facts of any such incident.

　　　　(2) If any Chinese in the United States of America wishes to return to the People's Republic of China but has difficulty raising the cost of repatriation, the Government of the Republic of India may provide the necessary financial assistance to enable them to return home.

　　(3) The Government of the United States of America will widely publicize the above arrangements, and the Embassy of the Republic of India in the United States of America may do the same.
						September 10, 1955

Xinhua News Agency, Geneva, September 10th: The 14th ambassadorial meeting between China and the United States was held today. The two sides reached an agreement on the first item on the agenda, the issue of civilian repatriation. After the agreement was reached, Ambassador Wang Bingnan informed the other party of the results of the review of cases involving Americans who violated the law in China, as follows: 1. Rigoni, Lake, and Ren Fugen will be released early before the expiration of their sentences. 2. Ganaitai, Bauer, Medesu, Pan Ailian, Golden, An Mingxin, and Xu Ousu will be deported. 3. The above ten Americans who violated the law will be deported to Hong Kong within a few days.

### 中美两国大使级会谈举行第十四次会议  就双方平民回国问题达成协议

1955-09-11
第1版()
专栏：

    中美两国大使级会谈举行第十四次会议
    就双方平民回国问题达成协议
    新华社日内瓦十日电　中美两国在日内瓦举行的大使级会谈在九月十日举行第十四次会议。在会议上中美两国大使级代表就双方平民回国问题达成协议如下。
    中华人民共和国和美利坚合众国两国大使协议的声明：
    中华人民共和国和美利坚合众国两国大使协议公布各自政府就平民各回其本国问题所采取的措施。
    关于在中华人民共和国的美国人问题，王炳南大使代表中华人民共和国通知尤·阿·约翰逊大使如下：
      （一）中华人民共和国承认，在中华人民共和国的美国人愿意返回美利坚合众国者，享有返回的权利，并宣布已经采取、且将继续采取适当措施，使他们能够尽速行使其返回的权利。
    （二）联合王国政府将被委托对愿意回国的美国人返回美利坚合众国提供协助如下：
    （1）如果任何美国人认为同中华人民共和国所公布的政策相反，其离境受到了阻碍，他可以通知联合王国驻中华人民共和国代办处，要求代为向中华人民共和国政府交涉。如果美利坚合众国愿意的话，联合王国政府并可对任何此种事件的事实进行调查。
    （2）如果任何在中华人民共和国的美国人愿意返回美利坚合众国而筹措回国旅费有困难，联合王国政府可给予所需的财政援助，使其回国。
    （三）中华人民共和国政府将对以上安排广为公布，联合王国驻中华人民共和国代办处亦可照做。
    关于在美利坚合众国的中国人问题，尤·阿·约翰逊大使代表美利坚合众国通知王炳南大使如下：
    （一）美利坚合众国承认，在美利坚合众国的中国人愿意返回中华人民共和国者，享有返回的权利，并宣布已经采取、且将继续采取适当措施，使他们能够尽速行使其返回的权利。
    （二）印度共和国政府将被委托对愿意回国的中国人返回中华人民共和国提供协助如下：
    （1）如果任何中国人认为同美利坚合众国所公布的政策相反，其离境受到了阻碍，他可以通知印度共和国驻美利坚合众国大使馆，要求代为向美利坚合众国政府交涉。如果中华人民共和国愿意的话，印度共和国政府并可对任何此种事件的事实进行调查。
    （2）如果任何在美利坚合众国的中国人愿意返回中华人民共和国而筹措回国旅费有困难，印度共和国政府可给予所需的财政援助，使其回国。
    （三）美利坚合众国政府将对以上安排广为公布，印度共和国驻美利坚合众国大使馆亦可照做。
                      一九五五年九月十日
    新华社日内瓦十日电　中美两国大使级会谈今日举行第十四次会议。双方就双方平民回国问题的第一项议程达成了协议。在协议达成后，王炳南大使就在中国犯法的美国人的案件中已经审查完毕的结果向对方作了如下的通知：一、芮歌尼、李克、任福根将在刑期期满前被提前释放。二、改乃台、鲍尔、梅德素、潘爱莲、高登、安明新、徐偶苏等七人将被遣送出境。三、以上十名犯法的美国人将在几天以内被遣送到香港。
