Title: Nasser Announces Egypt and Czechoslovakia Will Exchange Cotton, Rice, and Arms
Author: 
Date: 1955-09-30
Page: 4
Xinhua News Agency, September 28th, Cairo Dispatch: Egyptian Prime Minister Nasser announced at the opening ceremony of the Armed Forces Exhibition on the 27th that Czechoslovakia has agreed to supply various types of armaments to the Egyptian army in exchange for Egyptian cotton and rice.

	Nasser said that the arms deal with Czechoslovakia was "purely a commercial transaction." He said that Egypt and Czechoslovakia signed a barter agreement last week. He also said, "Czechoslovakia informed Egypt that it is prepared to supply the Egyptian army with everything it needs on a commercial basis."

	Nasser revealed that shortly after the military coup in July 1952, Egypt requested the United States, Britain, and France to supply arms, "but their replies came with conditions that infringed on our independence and freedom." Nasser said that Egypt "cannot buy a penny's worth of American weapons." "The United States wanted us to sign a mutual security treaty or some kind of alliance, and we refused."

	He said that France demanded that Egypt stop supporting North African nationalists. He also said, we rejected France's conditions because "it meant we had to abandon our Arab and humanitarian feelings."

	Nasser said that foreign countries hoped to impose their conditions on arms supplies in order to maintain their past influence in Egypt. The Prime Minister added: "We will resist such influence, and we will strengthen our own army."

	Nasser concluded his speech by saying: "Egypt has its own independent foreign policy. This policy is neither in line with the Eastern bloc nor with the Western bloc."

Xinhua News Agency, September 29th, New York Dispatch: Egyptian Foreign Minister Fawzi, who is attending the United Nations General Assembly, told the press on the 28th that whether Egypt buys weapons from the Soviet Union or elsewhere is purely Egypt's business and is of no concern to anyone.

	Fawzi said: "This is a matter that is directly related to our security, our sovereignty, and our honor. We will not accept any instruction from anyone. We will vigorously oppose all attempts to revive the old system of power politics and spheres of influence."

	Fawzi said, "It is our undeniable right and our inescapable responsibility to obtain the equipment necessary for our defense from anywhere we can get it. We have no intention of attacking anyone, but we are fully determined to defend our sovereignty and safeguard our way of life."

### 纳赛尔宣布埃捷将互换棉花大米和军火  法齐说从任何地方得到防御装备是埃及不可争辩的权利

1955-09-30
第4版()
专栏：

    纳赛尔宣布埃捷将互换棉花大米和军火
    法齐说从任何地方得到防御装备是埃及不可争辩的权利
    新华社二十八日讯　开罗消息：埃及总理纳赛尔二十七日在武装部队展览会的开幕典礼上发表演说时宣布说，捷克斯洛伐克已经同意把各种类型的军火供应给埃及军队，以换取埃及的棉花和大米。
    纳赛尔说，同捷克斯洛伐克的军火交易“纯粹是一种商业性的交易”。他说，埃及和捷克斯洛伐克上周签订了换货协定。他又说，“捷克斯洛伐克通知埃及说，它准备在商业基础上供应埃及军队一切需要的东西。”
    纳赛尔透露说，在一九五二年七月军事政变之后不久，埃及曾要求美国、英国和法国供应军火，“但是它们的答复却是附带着侵犯我们的独立和自由的条件”。纳赛尔说，埃及“不能买进一个辨士的美国武器”。“美国要我们签订一个共同安全条约或是某种联盟，而我们拒绝了。”
    他说，法国要求埃及停止支持北非民族主义者。他又说，我们拒绝了法国的条件，因为“这意味着我们必须抛弃我们的阿拉伯人和人道主义感情”。
    纳赛尔说，外国希望在武器供应上强加上它们的条件以维持它们过去在埃及的势力。这位总理又说：“我们将抵抗这样一种势力，我们将加强我们自己的军队。”
    纳赛尔最后结束他的演说道：“埃及有它自己独立的外交政策。这种政策既不附合东方阵营，也不附和西方阵营。”
    新华社二十九日讯　纽约消息：出席联合国大会的埃及外交部长法齐二十八日向报界发表谈话说，埃及是在苏联购买武器还是在其他地方购买武器，这只是埃及的事情，不与任何人相干。
    法齐说：“这是一个对我们的安全、我们的主权和我们的荣誉有直接关系的问题。我们将不接受任何人的任何指教。我们将大力反对一切复活强权政治和势力范围的旧制度的企图。”
    法齐说，“从任何我们能够得到的地方得到我们的防御所必需的装备，是我们的不可争辩的权利，也是我们不能逃避的责任。我们没有进攻任何人的任何打算，但是，我们完全有决心来保卫我们的主权和保障我们的生活方式。”
