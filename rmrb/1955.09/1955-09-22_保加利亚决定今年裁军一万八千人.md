Title: Bulgaria Decides to Demobilize 18,000 Troops This Year
Author:
Date: 1955-09-22
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, Prague, September 21st: The Bulgarian Telegraph Agency published the following communiqué from the Council of Ministers of the People's Republic of Bulgaria:

    In view of the certain degree of relaxation of tensions in international relations, especially after the Geneva Conference, and in order to promote the further improvement of the international situation, and to consolidate peace and trust between countries, including peace and trust among the countries of the Balkan Peninsula; the Government of the People's Republic of Bulgaria has decided to reduce the armed forces of the People's Republic of Bulgaria by 18,000 men before December 31, 1955.

    The funds saved by reducing the armed forces will be used for housing construction, cultural measures, and welfare facilities.

    Demobilized soldiers and officers will work in industrial enterprises, government agencies, and agriculture.

### 保加利亚决定今年裁军一万八千人

1955-09-22
第4版()
专栏：

    保加利亚决定今年裁军一万八千人
    新华社布拉格二十一日电　保加利亚通讯社发表保加利亚人民共和国部长会议的公报如下：
    鉴于国际关系中特别是日内瓦会议以后，紧张局势有了一定程度的缓和，为了促进国际局势的进一步改善，巩固国与国之间的和平和信任、其中包括巴尔干半岛各国之间的和平和信任；保加利亚人民共和国政府决定在一九五五年十二月三十一日以前把保加利亚人民共和国的武装部队裁减一万八千人。
    由于裁减武装部队而节省下来的资金将用在住宅建设、文化措施和生活福利设施方面。
    复员后的士兵和军官将到工业企业、机关和农业中去工作。
