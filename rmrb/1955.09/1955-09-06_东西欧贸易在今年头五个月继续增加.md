Title: East-West European Trade Continues to Increase in the First Five Months of This Year
Author:
Date: 1955-09-06
Page: 4
Xinhua News Agency, Geneva, September 4th: A bulletin published by the United Nations Economic Commission for Europe on the 2nd reported that trade between Eastern and Western Europe has increased considerably in the first five months of this year, and the trend is continuing to expand.

The bulletin stated that compared to the same period in 1954, Western European countries' imports from Eastern European countries increased by 35% in the first five months of this year, and exports to Eastern European countries increased by 9%. The United Kingdom and Turkey saw the largest increases in imports from Eastern European countries. The United Kingdom's imports from Eastern European countries increased from $63.8 million to $94.9 million, and exports to Eastern European countries increased from $27 million to $33.9 million. Austria, France, Italy, Sweden, and West Germany also saw considerable increases in imports from Eastern European countries.

### 东西欧贸易在今年头五个月继续增加

1955-09-06
第4版()
专栏：

    东西欧贸易在今年头五个月继续增加
    新华社四日讯　日内瓦消息：联合国欧洲经济委员会二日出版的公报报道，东西欧贸易在今年头五个月里有相当的增加，并且有继续扩大的趋势。
    公报说，同一九五四年同时期比较，西欧国家在今年头五个月中从东欧国家的输入增加了百分之三十五，对东欧国家的输出增加了百分之九。英国和土耳其从东欧国家的输入增加得最多。英国从东欧国家的输入从六千三百八十万美元增加到九千四百九十万美元，对东欧国家的输出从二千七百万美元增加到三千三百九十万美元。奥地利、法国、意大利、瑞典和西德从东欧国家的输入也有相当的增加。
