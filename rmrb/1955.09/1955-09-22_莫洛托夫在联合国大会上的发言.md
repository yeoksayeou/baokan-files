Title: Molotov's Speech at the United Nations General Assembly
Author: 
Date: 1955-09-22
Page: 1

Mr. President, Gentlemen!

    The Soviet delegation deems it necessary to draw everyone's attention to a problem that has been repeatedly submitted for discussion at previous sessions of the United Nations General Assembly, but has not yet been satisfactorily resolved. We are referring to the issue of restoring the legitimate rights of the People's Republic of China in the United Nations. There is no reason to delay resolving this issue, especially in the current international environment.

    This session of the General Assembly is opening in a more favorable international environment. The results of the Geneva meeting of the four heads of government first proved this point. We see that relations between countries have improved somewhat, trust between them has been strengthened, and international tensions have eased.

    Now, after the Geneva meeting, resolving pressing outstanding issues already has more favorable prerequisites. Among these issues, the issue of restoring the legitimate rights of the People's Republic of China in the United Nations occupies a particularly important position. Due to certain circumstances and artificial obstacles, this issue has not been resolved for several years. This is a clear injustice to the People's Republic of China, and the interests of further developing international cooperation and the interests of the United Nations itself require the elimination of this practice.

    This situation cannot but affect the prestige of the United Nations, causing damage to this prestige, and cannot but hinder the United Nations from carrying out the tasks stipulated in the Charter – maintaining international peace and security, developing friendly relations between countries, realizing international cooperation in all aspects, and becoming a center for coordinating the actions of various countries.

    Under the current international conditions, restoring the legitimate rights of the People's Republic of China in the United Nations will be a new and important step on the road to further easing international tensions. On the other hand, if the People's Republic of China, one of the great powers, is artificially excluded and cannot participate in the work of the United Nations, while the seat of the People's Republic of China in the United Nations is illegally occupied by those who, as everyone knows, do not represent anyone, then the United Nations cannot smoothly complete its many tasks.

    The People's Republic of China is the largest country in Asia, and it has actually been recognized by other countries as a great power. The Chinese people have never been as united as they are today for many centuries.

    Six hundred million Chinese people wholeheartedly support their people's democratic government and are building their political and social life with great enthusiasm and in accordance with the true interests of the nation and the true interests of the working people, based on new principles. In China, national strength is rising unprecedentedly, the economy is continuously developing, and great democratic and social reforms are being carried out. No one can stop this historic turn of events, which, together with other great events in Asia, shows that the people of Asian countries have entered a new and glorious historical era – an era of great change.

    The Bandung Conference held this spring proved that the People's Republic of China, as well as India, Indonesia, and other Asian and African countries, are important factors in international development and progress. Twenty-nine Asian and African countries, with different national systems and social systems, participated in the Bandung Conference, but they found a common language and opened a new page in the history of the development of international relations. Any attempt to diminish the significance of this fact and underestimate the role of the People's Republic of China under such international conditions is tantamount to regression and adopting a position that is condemned by history and hopeless.

### 莫洛托夫在联合国大会上的发言

1955-09-22
第1版()
专栏：

    莫洛托夫在联合国大会上的发言主席先生，各位先生！
    苏联代表团认为有必要提请大家注意一个问题，这个问题曾经一再提交联合国大会以前历届会议讨论，但是至今还没有得到圆满的解决。我们所指的是恢复中华人民共和国在联合国中的合法权利的问题。拖延解决这个问题是没有任何理由的，特别是在目前的国际环境下。
    本届大会会议是在比较有利的国际环境中开幕的。四国政府首脑日内瓦会议的结果首先证明了这一点。我们看到，各国关系有了一些改善，它们之间的信任有了加强，国际紧张局势有了缓和。
    现在，在日内瓦会议之后，解决迫切的悬而未决的问题已经具有比较有利的前提。在这些问题当中，恢复中华人民共和国在联合国中的合法权利的问题占有特殊重要的地位。由于某些情况和人为的障碍，这个问题在几年来一直没有得到解决。这是对中华人民共和国显然不公正的做法，进一步发展国际合作的利益和联合国本身的利益要求消除这种做法。
    这种情况不能不影响联合国的威信，使这种威信遭到损害，不能不妨碍联合国执行宪章所规定的任务——维护国际和平和安全，发展各国的友好关系，实现各方面的国际合作和成为使各国行动取得协调的中心。
    在当前形成的国际条件下，恢复中华人民共和国在联合国的合法权利将是在进一步缓和国际紧张局势道路上的新的重要的一步。另一方面，如果大国之一的中华人民共和国被人为地排斥，不能参加联合国组织的工作，而中华人民共和国在联合国的席位被那些大家都知道不代表任何人的人非法占据，那么联合国组织就不能顺利地完成它的许多任务。
    中华人民共和国是亚洲最大的国家，它实际上已经被其他国家承认为一个大国。中国人民许多世纪以来从来没有像今天这样团结一致。
    六亿中国人民衷心拥护自己的人民民主政府，并且按照民族的真正利益和劳动者的真正利益极其热情地本着新的原则建设自己的政治生活和社会生活。在中国，民族力量正在空前高涨，经济不断发展，正在进行伟大的民主和社会改革。无论谁都无法阻止这一历史性的事态转变，这一转变同亚洲的其他的伟大事件表明了，亚洲各国人民已经进入了新的、光荣的历史时代——伟大变革的时代。
    今年春天举行的万隆会议证明：中华人民共和国以及印度、印度尼西亚和其他亚非国家是国际发展和进步的重要因素。参加万隆会议的有二十九个亚非国家，它们具有不同的国家制度和社会制度，但是它们找到了共同的语言并且在国际关系发展史上揭开了新的一页。如果企图缩小这种事实的意义和低估中华人民共和国在这样的国际条件下的作用，就等于是倒退和采取受到历史谴责和没有希望的立场。
    这里无须乎把中华人民共和国曾经或者是正在其中起卓越的积极作用的其他国际事件一一列举。联合国许多会员国已经同中华人民共和国建立了正常的政治关系和经济关系。其他一些国家也在想沿着这条道路走。
    无论是为了巩固国际和平和安全，或者是为了保持联合国的国际威信，中华人民共和国作为联合国的一个平等权利的会员国参加大会、安全理事会和联合国其他机构都是必要的。继续无视中华人民共和国在联合国组织中的不可被剥夺的权利，就是继续破坏联合国宪章，破坏公正的原则和国际法的原则。
      苏联代表团认为，没有任何理由继续拖延解决关于恢复中华人民共和国的无可争辩的权利的问题，为此，苏联代表团提出下列提案交大会批准：
    “大会决定，中华人民共和国中央人民政府任命的代表是中国驻联合国大会和联合国其他机构中的代表。”
    苏联代表团希望，这个建议将得到其他代表团方面的支持；并且认为，这个建议的通过将有助于完成联合国组织所担负的基本任务——维护和巩固和平以及发展国际合作的任务。
