Title: Canadian Minister of Health and Welfare Martin Uneasy About U.S. International Trade Policy
Author:
Date: 1955-09-06
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, New York, September 3rd (Tass dispatch): United Press International reported that the Canadian representative to the United Nations Disarmament Commission Subcommittee, Canadian Minister of Health and Welfare Martin, said in a speech in Rochester, New York, that Canada firmly requests the United States to consult with the Canadian government in advance when making major political decisions.

Martin said: Since Canada relies on exports, I believe that the Canadian government "is rightly uneasy" about the direction of U.S. foreign policy and the U.S. approach to international trade policy.

Martin said that safeguarding Canada's interests abroad would not conflict with U.S. interests, but this does not mean agreeing with U.S. policies, nor does it mean that Canada can be satisfied with silently accepting major decisions on various issues that have not been consulted with Canada.

### 加拿大卫生福利部长马丁  对美国的国际贸易政策感到不安

1955-09-06
第4版()
专栏：

    加拿大卫生福利部长马丁
    对美国的国际贸易政策感到不安
    新华社四日讯　据塔斯社纽约三日讯：合众社报道，出席联合国裁军委员会小组委员会的加拿大代表、加拿大卫生福利部长马丁在纽约州罗彻斯特发表谈话说，加拿大坚决要求美国在采取重大的政治决定时事先同加拿大政府商议。
    马丁说道：由于加拿大依靠出口，我认为，加拿大政府对美国对外政策的方针和美国在国际贸易政策的方针“感到应有的不安”。
    马丁说道，维护加拿大在国外的利益不会同美国利益相抵触，但是这并不意味着同意美国的政策，也不等于说，加拿大能够满足于默默地接受那些没有同加拿大商议过的关于各项问题的重大决定。
