Title: Final Communiqué of Negotiations Between the Soviet Union and the German Democratic Republic
Author:
Date: 1955-09-22
Page: 1

Xinhua News Agency, Moscow, September 20th, TASS: Final Communiqué of Negotiations between the Soviet Union and the German Democratic Republic:

From September 17th to 20th, a delegation from the Soviet government and a delegation from the government of the German Democratic Republic held negotiations in Moscow.

The Soviet side participating in the talks included: Chairman of the Council of Ministers of the USSR, N.A. Bulganin; Member of the Presidium of the Supreme Soviet of the USSR, N.S. Khrushchev; Minister of Foreign Trade of the USSR, I.G. Kabanov; as well as Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of the USSR, V.S. Semyonov; Ambassador Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary of the USSR to the German Democratic Republic, G.M. Pushkin; and Director of the Third European Department of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of the USSR, S.G. Lapin.

The German Democratic Republic side participating in the talks included: Prime Minister of the German Democratic Republic, Otto Grotewohl; Deputy Prime Minister of the Geman Democratic Republic, Walter Ulbricht; Deputy Prime Minister of the German Democratic Republic Willi Stoph, Deputy Prime Minister of the German Democratic Republic Otto Nuschke, Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Foreign Affairs of the German Democratic Republic, Lothar Bolz; Chairman of the State Planning Commission of the German Democratic Republic, Bruno Leuschner; Ambassador Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary of the German Democratic Republic to the Soviet Union, Johannes König; Chairman of the Foreign Affairs Committee of the People's Chamber of the German Democratic Republic, Peter Florin; Chairman of the Presidium of the National Council of the National Front of Democratic Germany, Professor Erich Correns; General Secretary of the Democratic Farmers' Party, Berthold Rose; General Secretary of the Free Democratic Party, Manfred Gerlach; and Secretary of the delegation, Ambassador Fritz Große.

During the negotiations, the most important issues related to strengthening and further developing the friendly relations between the Soviet Union and the German Democratic Republic were discussed.

The negotiations took place in a friendly and sincere atmosphere, and as a result, the "Treaty on Relations Between the Union of Soviet Socialist Republics and the German Democratic Republic" was signed. Both sides expressed their firm belief that this treaty, signed on the principles of equality, mutual respect for sovereignty, and non-interference in internal affairs, will promote the strengthening and further development of the already existing friendly relations and close cooperation between the Soviet Union and the German Democratic Republic. Both sides also believe that this treaty is a significant factor in strengthening peace and security in Europe, and that it will thereby contribute to the reunification of Germany as a peace-loving and democratic state.

During the negotiations, the two sides exchanged views on international issues of mutual concern, particularly on issues related to the upcoming Geneva Conference of Foreign Ministers of the Four Powers. Both sides confirmed that in order to have a fruitful discussion on issues concerning Germany at the aforementioned conference, the governments of the German Democratic Republic and the Federal Republic of Germany must participate in the discussion of these issues.

The two sides exchanged views on the issue of some former German prisoners of war who are currently still serving sentences in the Soviet Union for crimes they committed. The Soviet government, taking into consideration the request made by the President and government of the German Democratic Republic on this issue, and also taking into consideration the request of the government of the Federal Republic of Germany, declared that it would study this issue leniently and would submit a proposal to the Presidium of the Supreme Soviet of the USSR.
The texts of the "Treaty on Relations Between the Union of Soviet Socialist Republics and the German Democractic Republic" and the letters exchanged between the Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of the Soviet Union V.A. Zorin and the Minister of Foreign Affairs of the German Democratic Republic Lothar Bolz, signed in Moscow on September 20th, will be published separately.

### 苏联和民主德国谈判最后公报

1955-09-22
第1版()
专栏：

    苏联和民主德国谈判最后公报
    新华社二十一日讯　塔斯社莫斯科二十日讯：苏联和德意志民主共和国谈判的最后公报：
    苏联政府代表团和德意志民主共和国政府代表团从九月十七日到二十日在莫斯科举行了谈判。
    苏联方面参加谈判的有：苏联部长会议主席尼·亚·布尔加宁、苏联最高苏维埃主席团委员尼·谢·赫鲁晓夫、苏联对外贸易部部长伊·格·卡巴诺夫以及苏联外交部副部长弗·斯·谢苗诺夫、苏联驻德意志民主共和国特命全权大使格·马·普希金、苏联外交部第三欧洲司司长斯·格·拉宾。
    德意志民主共和国方面参加谈判的有：德意志民主共和国总理奥托·格罗提渥、德意志民主共和国副总理瓦尔特·乌布利希、德意志民主共和国副总理威利·斯多夫、德意志民主共和国副总理奥托·努舍克、德意志民主共和国副总理兼外交部长洛塔·博尔茨、德意志民主共和国国家计划委员会主席布伦诺·劳施纳、德意志民主共和国驻苏联特命全权大使约翰尼斯·柯尼希、德意志民主共和国人民议院外交委员会主席彼得·弗罗林、民主德国全国阵线全国委员会主席团主席埃里希·柯伦斯教授、民主农民党总书记贝托尔特·罗斯、自由民主党总书记曼弗雷德·吉尔拉赫、代表团秘书弗里茨·格罗斯大使。
    在谈判过程中讨论了有关加强和进一步发展苏联和德意志民主共和国两国之间的友好关系的最重要的问题。
    谈判是在友好和真诚的气氛中进行的，谈判结果签订了“苏维埃社会主义共和国联盟和德意志民主共和国关系条约”。双方表示坚决相信，这一根据平等、互相尊重主权和互不干涉内政的原则签订的条约将促进苏联和德意志民主共和国两国之间已经存在的友好关系和紧密合作的加强和进一步发展。双方还相信，这个条约是加强欧洲的和平和安全的重大因素，它将从而促使德国重新统一成为爱好和平和民主的国家。
    在谈判过程中，双方对彼此所关心的国际问题，特别是有关即将举行的四国外交部长日内瓦会议的问题交换了意见。双方确认，为了在上述会议上富有成效地讨论有关德国的问题，在讨论这些问题时，必须有德意志民主共和国政府和德意志联邦共和国政府参加。
    双方对由于犯有罪行目前还在苏联服刑的一部分前德国战俘的问题交换了意见。苏联政府考虑到德意志民主共和国总统和政府就这个问题提出的请求，并且考虑到德意志联邦共和国政府的请求，声明它将从宽研究这一个问题，并且将向苏联最高苏维埃主席团提出建议。
    九月二十日在莫斯科签订的“苏维埃社会主义共和国联盟和德意志民主共和国关系条约”以及苏联外交部副部长瓦·亚·佐林和德意志民主共和国外交部长洛塔·博尔茨所交换的信件的文本另行发表。
