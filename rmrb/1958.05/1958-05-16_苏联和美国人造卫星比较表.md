　　(Note) To launch satellites of the same size, the rocket thrust required for an orbital inclination of 35 degrees to the equator is smaller than that of 65 degrees, and the required rocket technology level is lower.
    Satellites with an orbital inclination of 65 degrees to the equator can be seen in all continents except for the central regions of the two poles, while satellites below 35 degrees can only reach near Zhengzhou in China in the north and near Montevideo in South America in the south.
