Title: Digging Dry Wells
Author: Han Qixiang
Date: 1958-05-12
Page: 8

　　Digging Dry Wells
    Han Qixiang
　　When the rooster crows three times, the east turns bright,
　　A young couple leaves the village to dig a dry well.
　　You carry the hoe, I take the spade,
　　Earthen baskets and hemp ropes, we bring them all.
　　Brother digs the soil, sister hoists,
　　Singing in harmony, humming the tamping song.
　　Brother digs the soil like a diamond drill,
　　Sister, you hoist the soil faster than an electric fan.
　　Brother's heart is harder than steel,
　　Unafraid of the three-foot thick snow and frozen ground.
　　Sister's enthusiasm is like a raging fire,
　　Melting the ice and snow into a river.
　　Flowing into a river, irrigating the dry well,
　　No longer needing to pray for rain and wait for the heavens in drought.
　　The dry well is wide at the bottom and narrow at the top,
　　Rainwater that comes down won't escape.
　　This dry well is eight feet wide and twelve feet deep,
　　Can you calculate how many catties of rainwater it can store?
　　As the saying goes: seawater cannot be measured by the dou,
　　Look, rows of dry wells cover the mountains.
　　Row upon row, string upon string,
　　Like a broken string of pearls.
　　From now on, no more drought or flood,
　　A happy life will come.
　　Cross the Yellow River, cross the Yangtze River,
　　Strive for a thousand catties of grain per mu.
　　  (Originally published in "Street Ballads" Second Issue)

### 打旱井
韩起祥
1958-05-12
第8版()
专栏：

　　打旱井
    韩起祥
　　鸡叫三遍东方亮，
　　小俩口要打旱井走出庄。
　　你扛镢头我拿锨，
　　土筐麻绳都带全。
　　哥哥掏土妹妹吊，
　　一唱一和还哼着夯歌调。
　　哥哥挑土真像金刚钻，
　　妹妹呀！你吊土赛过电风扇。
　　哥哥的心情比钢铁硬，
　　不怕那雪厚三尺土地冻。
　　妹妹的热情像热火，
　　能烧化冰雪流成河。
　　流成河，灌旱井，
　　再不用旱天求雨把天等。
　　旱井肚大口口小，
　　雨水下来走不了。
　　这旱井，八尺宽来丈二深，
　　你算算能蓄雨水多少斤？
　　常言说：海水不能用斗量，
　　你看看，遍山的旱井一行行。
　　一行行、一串串，
　　好像珍珠断了线。
　　从今后，无旱涝，
　　幸福的生活就来到。
　　跨黄河，过长江，
　　争取每亩千斤粮。
　　  （原载“街头说唱”  第二期）
