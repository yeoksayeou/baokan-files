Title: Illiteracy Eradication Hero Appears in Vietnam: Young Pioneer Teaches Over Two Hundred People to Read and Write in Four Years
Author:
Date: 1958-05-22
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, Hanoi Dispatch: According to the Vietnam News Agency, a fifteen-year-old Young Pioneer member, Nguyen Van Tong, has taught two hundred and fifteen illiterate people to read and write in the past four years.

Nguyen Van Tong is a primary school student from a village near Hanoi. He has been actively involved in literacy work since he was twelve years old. He first taught his mother and relatives to read, and then taught others in the village. Sometimes he went fishing and used the money from selling the fish to buy chalk, ink, and textbooks for his literacy class.

Nguyen Van Tong has been selected as a delegate to the Third National Congress of Labor Models and Advanced Workers currently being held in Hanoi, and he is the youngest delegate.

### 越南出现扫盲小英雄  一个红领巾四年教会二百多人读写

1958-05-22
第6版()
专栏：

    越南出现扫盲小英雄
    一个红领巾四年教会二百多人读写
    新华社河内电　据越南通讯社报道，一个十五岁的越南少年先锋队队员阮文宗在过去四年内教会了二百一十五名文盲读书和写字。
    阮文宗是河内附近乡下的一名小学生。他从十二岁起就积极参加扫盲工作。他首先教他的母亲和亲戚认字，然后教村里其他的人。他有时出去钓鱼，把卖鱼得来的钱替他的文化班购买粉笔、墨水和教科书。
    阮文宗被选为目前正在河内召开的越南劳动模范和先进工作者第三次代表大会的代表，他是代表中年纪最小的一个。
