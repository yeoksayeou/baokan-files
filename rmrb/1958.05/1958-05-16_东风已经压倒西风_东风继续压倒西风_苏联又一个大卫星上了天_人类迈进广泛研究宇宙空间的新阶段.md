Title: The East Wind Prevails Over the West Wind; The East Wind Continues to Prevail Over the West Wind. The Soviet Union Launches Another Great Satellite into Space. Humanity Enters a New Stage of Extensive Research into Outer Space.

Author:

Date: 1958-05-16
Page: 1

Xinhua News Agency, Moscow, May 15th. TASS Moscow, May 15th Dispatch: TASS's communiqué regarding the Soviet Union's launch of its third artificial Earth satellite:

In accordance with the plan for the International Geophysical Year, the Soviet Union launched its third artificial Earth satellite on May 15, 1958.

The purpose of launching the artificial satellite is to conduct scientific research on the upper atmosphere and outer space.

The satellite entered an orbit that intersects the equatorial plane at an angle of sixty-five degrees.

According to preliminary materials, the maximum height of the orbit from the ground is 1,880 kilometers, and the satellite's orbital period is 106 minutes.

The satellite has separated from the carrier rocket, and the rocket is moving along a closer-to-earth orbit.

At 13:41 Moscow time on May 15, the third satellite will fly over the Moscow city area, from southwest to northeast.

The Soviet Union's third artificial Earth satellite is conical in shape, with a base diameter of one meter and seventy-three centimeters, and a height of three meters and fifty-seven centimeters, excluding the exposed antenna.

The satellite weighs 1,327 kilograms, of which 968 kilograms are scientific research instruments, radio measuring instruments, and energy equipment.

The instruments installed on the satellite can conduct the following studies throughout the orbit:

	Pressure and composition of the upper atmosphere,
	Concentration of positive ions,
	Magnitude of the satellite's charge and the intensity of the Earth's electrostatic field,
	Earth's magnetic field intensity,
	Intensity of solar corpuscular radiation,
	Composition and changes in primary cosmic rays, distribution of photons and heavy nuclei in cosmic rays,
	Micro-meteorites,
	Temperatures inside and on the surface of the satellite.

According to the scheduled plan, a series of geophysical and physical problems will be studied using the instruments carried by the satellite to high altitudes.

In order to transmit scientific observation data to ground recording stations, a multi-channel telemetry system with high resolution capability is installed on the satellite. The satellite is equipped with a special transmitting device to measure the coordinates of its trajectory.

In order to attract a wide range of scientists from around the world to participate in the observation of the Soviet Union's third artificial Earth satellite, the satellite is equipped with a radio transmitter that continuously transmits telegraph signals at a frequency of 20.005 MHz. The signal duration is 150-300 milliseconds, and the transmission power is enormous.

The operation of the scientific instruments and radio technology instruments on the satellite is controlled by program devices. In addition to electrochemical power sources, the satellite is also equipped with solar cells.

The temperature conditions necessary for the normal operation of the instruments on the satellite are guaranteed by a temperature control system, which uses special devices to change the radiation and surface reflection coefficients.

Specially established scientific stations, equipped with a large number of radio technology instruments and optical instruments, are observing the satellite, receiving scientific information from the satellite, and measuring the coordinates of its trajectory. The data on the satellite's coordinates received from the radar station can be automatically converted, summarized into a unified astronomical time, and then collected to the adjustment calculation center via telecommunications lines. The measurement information sent from the scientific stations to the calculation center automatically enters a high-speed electronic computer, which can quickly calculate the basic parameters of the satellite's orbit and calculate its ephemeris. Many optical observation stations, observatories, radio clubs, and radio enthusiasts are participating in the observation of the satellite.

The satellite and carrier rocket can be seen under the illumination of the rising and setting sun.

The Soviet Union's third artificial Earth satellite is a new stage for conducting extensive scientific research on the upper atmosphere and research into outer space, and is a huge contribution by Soviet scientists to world science.

### 东风已经压倒西风　东风继续压倒西风  苏联又一个大卫星上了天　人类迈进广泛研究宇宙空间的新阶段

1958-05-16
第1版()
专栏：

　　东风已经压倒西风　东风继续压倒西风
　　苏联又一个大卫星上了天
　　人类迈进广泛研究宇宙空间的新阶段
　　新卫星重1327公斤，比第一个重15倍，比第二个重1.6倍
    新华社15日讯　塔斯社莫斯科15日电：塔斯社关于苏联发射第三个人造地球卫星的公报：
    根据国际地球物理年的计划，苏联在1958年5月15日发射了第三个人造地球卫星。
    发射人造卫星的目的是对大气高层和宇宙空间进行科学研究。
    卫星进入了同赤道平面成六十五度交角的轨道。
    根据初步材料，轨道距地面的最大高度是一千八百八十公里，卫星绕地球一圈的时间是一百零六分钟。
    卫星已经脱离运载火箭，火箭正沿着离地面较近的轨道运行。
    5月15日莫斯科时间十三点四十一分，第三个卫星将飞过莫斯科市区上空，方向是从西南往东北。
    苏联第三个人造地球卫星呈圆锥形，底的直径为一公尺七十三公分，高三公尺五十七公分，露出的天线不计算在内。
    卫星重一千三百二十七公斤，其中科学研究仪器、无线电测量仪器和能源设备的重量为九百六十八公斤。
    卫星上装置的仪器，可以在整个轨道上进行以下研究：
    高层大气的压力和组成，
    正离子的浓度，
    卫星电荷的大小和地球静电场的强度，
    地球磁场强度，
    太阳微粒辐射强度，
    原始宇宙线的组成和变化，宇宙线中光子和重核的分布，
    微流星，
    卫星内部和表面温度。
    根据预定计划，将利用由卫星带到高空去的仪器，研究一系列地球物理问题和物理问题。
    为了把科学观测数据发到地面纪录站，在卫星上安装了具有很高分辨能力的多路遥测系统。卫星装备有专门的发射装置，可以测量它的轨迹的座标。
    为吸引世界广大科学界人士参加对苏联第三个人造地球卫星的观测，在卫星上装有无线电发射机，不停地以二○·○○五兆周的频率发射电报讯号，讯号持续时间为一五○——三○○毫秒，发射功率巨大。
    卫星上安装的科学仪器和无线电技术仪器的工作，通过程序装置来操纵。除电化学电源装置外，卫星上还装有太阳能电池。
    卫星上的仪器进行正常工作所必须的温度状态，是由温度调节系统保证的，这个系统用特别的装置来改变辐射和表面反射的系数。
    装备着大量无线电技术仪器和光学仪器的专门建立的科学站，在对卫星进行观测，接收卫星发来的科学情报和测量它的轨迹的座标。从雷达站收到的关于卫星座标的数据，可以自动变换，归纳到一个统一的天文时中，并通过电讯线路汇集到调整计算中心。各地科学站发到计算中心的测量情报自动地进入快速电子计算机，快速电子计算机可以算出卫星轨道基本参数和计算它的星历表。许多光学观测站、天文台、无线电俱乐部和无线电爱好者都在参加对卫星的观测。
    卫星和运载火箭在旭日和落日的照耀下可以看到。
    苏联第三个人造地球卫星是对大气高层进行广泛科学研究和对宇宙空间进行研究的新阶段，是苏联科学家对世界科学的巨大贡献。
