Title: West Sumatra Rebel Leaders Scatter in Disarray; Government Troops Recapture Batu Sangkar
Author: 
Date: 1958-05-10
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, Jakarta, 9th: Indonesian Army authorities announced on the evening of the 8th that government troops had recaptured Batu Sangkar in West Sumatra on the 5th. Batu Sangkar is located more than twenty kilometers southeast of Bukittinggi. After government troops recaptured Bukittinggi, the stronghold of the West Sumatra rebel group, Batu Sangkar had been one of the main havens for the rebels.

When government troops recaptured Batu Sangkar, the city was already in a semi-vacant state, with some residents having fled elsewhere under the coercion of the rebels.

Reports say that when government troops attacked Bukittinggi, Mohammad Natsir, chairman of the Masyumi Party, fled in panic. Currently, the rebel group's leaders have scattered in disarray: Sjafruddin, Simbolon, and Hussein fled to Kerinci in southern West Sumatra; Djambek and Natsir fled to Lake Maninjau, west of Bukittinggi.

### 西苏门答腊叛乱头子狼狈逃散  政府军收复巴株山卡

1958-05-10
第6版()
专栏：

    西苏门答腊叛乱头子狼狈逃散
    政府军收复巴株山卡
    据新华社雅加达9日电　印度尼西亚陆军当局在8日晚间宣布，政府军在5日收复了西苏门答腊的巴株山卡，巴株山卡在武吉丁宜东南二十多公里。在政府军收复西苏门答腊叛乱集团的老巢武吉丁宜以后，巴株山卡曾经是叛乱分子的主要避难所之一。
    当政府军收复巴株山卡的时候，这个城市已经处于半真空状态，那里的部分居民已经在叛乱分子的胁迫下逃往他处。
    有消息说，当政府军进攻武吉丁宜的时候，马斯友美党主席穆罕默德·纳席尔仓皇逃走。目前，叛乱集团的头子们已经狼狈逃散：沙弗鲁丁、西姆波朗和侯赛尼逃往西苏门答腊南部的葛林芝；贾姆贝克和纳席尔逃往武吉丁宜以西的马临辛湖。
