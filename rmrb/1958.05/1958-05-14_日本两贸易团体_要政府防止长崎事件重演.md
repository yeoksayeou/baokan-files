Title: Two Japanese Trade Groups Want Government to Prevent Nagasaki Incident from Recurring
Author:
Date: 1958-05-14
Page: 5

　　Two Japanese Trade Groups
　　Want Government to Prevent Nagasaki Incident from Recurring
　　Xinhua News Agency, Tokyo, 12th. TASS, Tokyo, 12th: According to Japanese radio broadcasts, Japanese business figures are seriously concerned about the current Japanese government's hostile stance towards the People's Republic of China, which has led to abnormal situations in Sino-Japanese trade relations.
　　According to the "Hokkaido Shimbun" newspaper, representatives of the Japan International Trade Promotion Association and the Japan-China Import and Export Association held a joint meeting on the 12th to discuss measures to normalize Sino-Japanese trade relations.
　　The leaders of these two organizations decided to advise the government to take all necessary measures to prevent a recurrence of incidents such as the insult to the national flag of the People's Republic of China that occurred in Nagasaki.

### 日本两贸易团体  要政府防止长崎事件重演

1958-05-14
第5版()
专栏：

　　日本两贸易团体
    要政府防止长崎事件重演
    新华社13日讯  塔斯社东京12日讯：据日本电台广播，日本工商界人士对于日本现政府采取敌视中华人民共和国的立场、因而在日中贸易关系中出现了不正常的局面这件事感到严重的不安。
    据“北海道新闻”报道，日本国际贸易促进协会和日中输出入协会的代表在12日举行联席会议，讨论采取措施使日中贸易关系正常化的问题。
    这两个机构的领导人决定建议政府采取一切必要的措施，防止类似在长崎发生的侮辱中华人民共和国国旗的事件重演。
