Title: This Autumn's Mars "Great Opposition": Chinese and Soviet Scientists to Collaborate on Observations
Author:
Date: 1958-05-27
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, Kharkov, 26th: Professor Zhang Yuzhe, Director of the Purple Mountain Observatory of China, who is currently visiting the Soviet Union, told a TASS reporter that during this autumn's Mars "Great Opposition" (i.e., when Mars is at its closest point to Earth), "we will work with our friends—Soviet astronomers—to study the mysteries of Mars."

The renowned Chinese astrophysicist participated in a recent meeting of the Planetary Committee of the Astronomical Council of the Soviet Academy of Sciences held in Kharkov. During this time, Zhang Yuzhe visited the Kharkov University Observatory led by Academician Barabashov and reached an agreement with the observatory stipulating that astronomers in Kharkov and Nanjing will simultaneously cooperate in observing Mars during this autumn's Mars "Great Opposition".

During this year's Mars "Great Opposition," the distance from Earth will be approximately 73 million kilometers. At this time, if the Kharkov and Nanjing observatories, which are far apart, can collaborate on visual and photographic observations, more complete data on Mars can be obtained.

### 今秋火星“大冲”  中苏科学家将协同观测

1958-05-27
第6版()
专栏：

    今秋火星“大冲”
    中苏科学家将协同观测
    据新华社26日讯　塔斯社哈尔科夫讯：目前正在苏联访问的中国紫金山天文台台长张钰哲教授向塔斯社记者说，在今年秋天火星“大冲”（即火星运行到离地球最近位置）的时候，“我们将和我们的朋友——苏联天文学家一道来研究火星之谜”。
    这位著名的中国天体物理学家参加了不久前在哈尔科夫召开的苏联科学院天文学委员会行星委员会的一次会议。这期间，张钰哲访问了由巴拉巴绍夫院士领导的哈尔科夫大学天文台，并且同这个天文台达成协议，规定当今年秋天火星“大冲”的时候，哈尔科夫和南京的天文学家将同时配合起来观察火星。
    今年火星“大冲”时，与地球的距离大约只有七千三百万公里。这时，如果哈尔科夫和南京这两个相距很远的天文台能够协作进行目测和摄影观察，就可以获得关于火星的更完整的资料。
