    Skin Develops "Goosebumps" When Stimulated
    The hairs on the skin are not erect, but rather slanted. At the base of each hair, beneath the epidermis, a small muscle is connected, called the arrector pili muscle.
    When a person is suddenly subjected to stimuli such as cold or fright, the arrector pili muscles all over the body contract through the action of nerves, tightening and straightening each hair, which forms "goosebumps."
    The occurrence of "goosebumps" has a certain physiological significance: 1. Muscle contraction can generate heat. 2. When the arrector pili muscles contract, the hairs stand erect, blocking the openings of nearby sweat glands, preventing perspiration and the evaporation of sweat, thus allowing the body to conserve heat and resist the cold. ### 皮肤受到刺激会起“鸡皮疙瘩”

1958-05-09
第8版()
专栏：为什么？

    皮肤受到刺激会起“鸡皮疙瘩”
    皮肤上的汗毛，并不是直立、而是歪歪斜斜的。每一根汗毛的根部，在表皮下面同一条很小的肌肉连在一块儿，这条小肌肉叫竖毛肌。
    当人突然受到寒冷、惊恐等刺激时，通过神经的作用，全身的竖毛肌就会收缩，把每一根汗毛拉紧竖直，这就形成了“鸡皮疙瘩”。
    “鸡皮疙瘩”的发生在生理上有一定的意义：一、肌肉的收缩，可以发生热量。二、竖毛肌一收缩，汗毛竖直，把邻近的汗腺口子堵住，防止排汗和汗的挥发，使身体保存热量，抵御寒冷。　（富）
