　　Ancient Pictographic Writing on Cliff Walls

　　The five "Mo Ya" (cliff inscriptions) on the cliffs of Tainei Township, Hua'an County, Fujian Province, have always been regarded as writings by immortals, impossible to decipher. Recently, the Fujian Provincial Cultural Relics Management Committee sent personnel to conduct on-site investigations, and after identification by relevant departments, it has been proven to be a type of ancient pictographic writing, possibly relics of the indigenous people before the Han Chinese entered Fujian.

　　These "Mo Ya" are carved on a cliff wall beside an ancient pool. They are all pictographic characters in the shape of human bodies, varying in size, the largest being more than one meter, and the most numerous location having more than twenty figures.

### 崖壁上的古代图象文字
赵肃芳
1958-05-13
第7版()
专栏：

　　崖壁上的古代图象文字
    福建省华安县汰内乡崖壁上的五处“摩崖”（即岩壁题字），一向被人们认为是神仙写的字，无法辨认。最近经福建省文物管理委员会派员实地考察，并经有关部门鉴定，已证明是一种古代图象文字，可能是汉族进入福建以前当地土著居民的遗迹。
    这些“摩崖”刻在一个古潭边的崖壁上，都是人体形状的象形图文，形状大小不一，最大的有一公尺多，最多的一处有二十多个图形。
