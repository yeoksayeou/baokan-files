Title: Spitting Blood

Author:

Date: 1958-05-14
Page: 6

A blaze of fury ignited by the Lebanese populace consumed the USIS library in Beirut, while across Latin America, the American Vice President was met with a chorus of boos and a barrage of stones. The root cause of this indignation sweeping through the Middle East and Latin America lies in America's own imperialist policies.

Yet, Dulles resorts to slanderous accusations, brazenly stating that the "anti-American sentiment in both regions is being fanned by elements we can well imagine – elements I prefer not to name here." He further asserts, "With sufficient propaganda and ample funds, anti-American demonstrations can be incited anywhere..." In reality, the "anti-American sentiment" is fueled by none other than America itself; and it is America that has squandered vast sums on "propaganda." Though Dulles is adept at distorting the truth, he dares not openly answer questions such as the one posed by the Colombians: "Who is stealing our coffee?" His sophistry, therefore, amounts to nothing more than shameless denial.

### 血口喷人

1958-05-14
第6版()
专栏：札记

    血口喷人
    黎巴嫩愤怒的人民一把火烧毁了贝鲁特的美新处图书馆，拉丁美洲各国人民到处用嘘声和石子追赶着美国副总统。中近东和拉丁美洲各国人民的这种愤慨，不是为了什么别的，从美国的帝国主义政策里就能找到根源。
    但是，杜勒斯却在血口喷人了，他居然说，这两处的“反美情绪是由我们很可以想像到的方面煽起的——这里我不愿说明哪些方面”。他还说，“有充分的宣传和足够的金钱可以在任何地方都煽起任何反美的示威……”。其实，煽起“反美情绪”的不是别的什么“方面”，而恰恰是美国自己；花了巨款“宣传”的，也是美国自己。杜勒斯虽然是颠倒黑白的能手，但是他却不敢公开回答例如哥伦比亚人提出的问题：“谁窃取了我们的咖啡？”因之，他的这种狡辩只是耍赖而已。
