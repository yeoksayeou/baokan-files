Title: Yugoslavia Builds Grand Canal
Author:
Date: 1957-07-24
Page: 5

Yugoslavia has recently begun construction of a canal connecting the Danube and Tisza rivers. It is said that this will be the largest irrigation and drainage project in Europe. The construction of the canal is currently underway near Vršac, northeast of Belgrade, on the southern Romanian border. The first phase of the project is 85 kilometers long, from Bela Crkva to Bezdan. Once completed, it will drain 500,000 hectares of flooded land. According to the overall project plan, this canal connecting the Danube and Tisza rivers will drain more than one million hectares of land and irrigate 360,000 hectares of farmland in the southern region of Vojvodina, Yugoslavia. Once the entire canal is completed, the drainage and irrigation problems of this major agricultural area of Yugoslavia can be solved. (Attached picture)

### 南斯拉夫兴建大运河

1957-07-24
第5版()
专栏：

    南斯拉夫兴建大运河
    南斯拉夫最近已经开始兴建连接多瑙河和蒂萨河的运河的工程。据说，这将是欧洲最大的灌溉和排水工程。现在开凿运河的工程正在贝尔格莱德东北部南罗边境的伏尔沙茨附近进行。运河的第一段工程全长八十五公里，即从贝拉·茨尔克伐到贝兹旦，开凿成功以后将可排干面积达五十万公顷的被淹土地。按照整个工程的计划，这条连接多瑙河和蒂萨河的运河将要排干面积一百万公顷以上的土地，并灌溉南斯拉夫伏伊伏丁那省南部地区的三十六万公顷的农田。在运河全部建成以后，南斯拉夫这个主要农业区的排水和灌溉问题就可以获得解决了。（附图片）
