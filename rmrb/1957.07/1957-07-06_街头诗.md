Street Poems
(Selected from Peking University's "666" Wall Poster)

    If, if someone sets a fire beside you, if someone wants to overthrow our regime, if someone attacks our fortress, if someone conspires to change the world... My dear friend, what will you use to answer?

    Suddenly realizing that ahead is an abyss of sin, ghost fires shuttle back and forth on patrol, the right foot is raised, where will it fall? I want to shout loudly to you: Wake up!

    What a Tan Tianrong's rightist banner, raised high, Kautsky's needle and thread, sewing up Engels' phrases. Tan Tianrong's trickery caters to Chiang Kai-shek's tastes.

### 街头诗

1957-07-06
第8版()
专栏：

    街头诗
    （选自北京大学“666”墙报）
      假如假如有人在你身边放火，假如有人要把我们的政权搞垮，假如有人向我们的堡垒进攻，假如有人阴谋改变天下……我亲爱的朋友，你用什么来回答？
      猛省前面是罪恶的深渊，鬼火穿梭来往巡行，右脚抬起了，落向哪里？我要向你大喝一声：猛省！
    好个谭天荣右字大旗，高高挑起，考茨基的针线，缝起恩格斯的词句。谭天荣的把戏，投了蒋介石的脾气。
