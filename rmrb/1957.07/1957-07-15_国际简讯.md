International Briefs

    Polish Parliament Session Concludes
    Warsaw Dispatch: The Polish Parliament session, lasting nearly five months, concluded on the 13th. At the plenary session held on the 13th, several bills and resolutions were passed.
    This parliamentary session was the first since the election of the new Polish Parliament; since its opening on February 20th, a total of eight plenary sessions have been held, and the standing committees of the parliament have met more than two hundred times. In addition to approving this year's economic plan and the 1956-1960 Five-Year Plan, this parliamentary session passed more than twenty bills and reviewed and approved a number of decrees.
    Albania Trains First Batch of Doctors
    Tirana Dispatch: The Albanian Medical College held its first graduation ceremony in Tirana on the 12th. Sixty male and female students received their diplomas.
    These graduates are the first batch of doctors trained domestically in Albania since liberation. They are equivalent to more than half of the total number of doctors in the country on the eve of liberation.
    West German Farmers Demand Land Reform
    According to a report by the German News Agency, farmer representatives from Bavaria, Schleswig-Holstein, Rhineland-Palatinate, Baden-Württemberg, North Rhine-Westphalia, and Hesse in West Germany are demanding democratic land reform in West Germany.
    British Government Building About to Collapse
    London News: The British Prime Minister's residence, 10 Downing Street, is in danger of collapsing. Prime Minister Macmillan requested the appointment of a committee on the 14th to conduct an inspection to determine how to save it.
    People who have resided at 10 Downing Street have long complained about the problems with the house. Former Prime Minister Churchill believed that this was the result of "profiteering builders" cutting corners.
    Influenza Spreads to the Middle East
    Influenza has spread to countries in the Middle East such as Iran, Syria, Lebanon, and Saudi Arabia. A spokesman for the Iranian Ministry of Health announced on July 13 that in Tehran alone, more than 15,000 people have contracted influenza.
                  (Xinhua News Agency dispatch)

### 国际简讯

1957-07-15
第3版()
专栏：国际简讯

    国际简讯
    波兰议会会议闭幕
    华沙讯：历时将近五个月的波兰议会会议已经在13日闭幕。在13日举行的全体会议上，通过了一些法案和决议。
    这次议会会议是波兰新议会选出后的第一次会议；从2月20日开幕以来，全体会议共举行八次，议会各常设委员会共开会二百多次。这次议会会议除通过今年的经济计划和1956—1960年五年计划外，共通过了二十多项法案和审查批准了一些法令。
    阿培养出第一批医生
    地拉那讯：阿尔巴尼亚医学院12日在地拉那举行了第一届毕业生典礼。有六十名男女学生得到了毕业证书。
    这批毕业生是阿尔巴尼亚解放以后在国内培养出来的第一批医生。他们相当于解放前夕全国医生的半数以上。
    西德农民要求土地改革
    据德意志通讯社报道，西德巴伐利亚、石勒苏益格—荷尔斯泰因、莱因兰—法尔茨、巴登—维尔次堡、北莱因威斯特伐利亚和黑森州等地的农民代表要求在西德实行民主的土地改革。
    英国政府大厦将要倒塌
    伦敦消息：英国首相官邸——唐宁街十号有倒塌的危险，麦克米伦首相已经在14日要求委任一个委员会进行检查，以便决定挽救的办法。
    在唐宁街十号居留过的人早就埋怨过这所房子有毛病了。前首相丘吉尔认为，这是“唯利是图的营造商”偷工减料的结果。
    流行性感冒蔓延到中东
    流行性感冒已经蔓延到伊朗、叙利亚、黎巴嫩、沙特阿拉伯等中东国家境内。伊朗卫生部发言人7月13日宣布，仅德黑兰一处，已经有一万五千余人患流行性感冒。
                  （据新华社讯）
