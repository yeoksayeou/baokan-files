Title: Soviet Scientists Plan to Uncover the Mysteries of Venus
Author:
Date: 1957-07-27
Page: 5

    Xinhua News Agency, Moscow, July 26th. Soviet Deputy Doctor of Technical Sciences, Khlebtsevich, recently published a plan in the Soviet magazine "Knowledge is Power" to use rockets to probe the mysteries of Venus between 1962 and 1967.

    He wrote that a five-stage rocket weighing two hundred and fifty tons could reach the skies above Venus in one hundred and forty-six days.

    When the rocket has traveled for one hundred and forty-five days and reaches an altitude of 300,000 kilometers above Venus, the electronic telescope photography equipment on the rocket will begin taking pictures and transmitting the captured images to Earth via facsimile. The final photograph will be taken at an altitude of several tens of kilometers above Venus. If any life forms exist on Venus, the photography equipment will be able to capture them clearly and transmit them to Earth.

### 苏联科学家制定计划准备揭开金星奥秘

1957-07-27
第5版()
专栏：

    苏联科学家制定计划准备揭开金星奥秘
    新华社莫斯科26日电　苏联技术科学副博士赫列勃采维奇，最近在苏联杂志“知识就是力量”上发表了在1962年到1967年之间，用火箭来窥探金星奥秘的计划。
    他写道，一枚二百五十吨重的五段火箭，在一百四十六天的时间内，可以飞到金星上空。
    当火箭飞行了一百四十五天，到达离金星三十万公里的上空的时候，火箭上的电子望远镜摄影装置就开始摄影，并且把摄得的照片向地球传真。最后一张照片将在离金星数十公里高的上空拍摄，如果金星上有任何生物存在，摄影装置就能够把它们拍摄清楚传送到地球上来。
