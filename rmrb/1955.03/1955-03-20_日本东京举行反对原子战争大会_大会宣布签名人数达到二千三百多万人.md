Title: Tokyo Rally Against Atomic War; Rally Announces Signature Count Reaches Over 23 Million
Author:
Date: 1955-03-20
Page: 1
Xinhua News Agency, Tokyo, 19th: A "Rally Against Atomic War" was held in Tokyo on the 17th, under the auspices of the Japan National Council for the Prohibition of Atomic and Hydrogen Bomb Signature Campaign. This rally was held to commemorate the first anniversary of the incident in which Japanese fishermen were injured by the U.S. hydrogen bomb test on Bikini Atoll.

At the rally, the Secretary-General of the Council, Ikuo Yasui, announced that as of March 17th, the number of signatures collected nationwide in Japan demanding the prohibition of atomic and hydrogen bombs had reached 23,184,393. The audience of over two thousand people applauded enthusiastically at this announcement.

The twenty-two fishermen injured by the U.S. hydrogen bomb test also attended the rally. They offered a solemn silent tribute to Kuboyama Aikichi, their compatriot who lost his life to the U.S. hydrogen bomb, and expressed their determination to work to strengthen the movement to ban atomic and hydrogen bombs.

### 日本东京举行反对原子战争大会  大会宣布签名人数达到二千三百多万人

1955-03-20
第1版()
专栏：

    日本东京举行反对原子战争大会
    大会宣布签名人数达到二千三百多万人
    【新华社十九日讯】东京消息：在日本禁止原子弹和氢弹签名运动全国协议会的主持下，东京在十七日举行了“反对原子战争大会”。这次大会是为纪念美国在比基尼岛试验氢弹伤害日本渔民事件的一周年而召开的。
    协议会事务局局长安井郁在大会上宣布：到三月十七日为止，日本全国签名要求禁止原子弹和氢弹的人数已经达到二千三百一十八万四千三百九十三人。全场二千多人热烈鼓掌欢迎这一宣布。
    被美国氢弹试验所伤的二十二个渔民也出席了大会。他们为被美国氢弹夺去了生命的同胞久保山爱吉致沉痛的默哀，并且表示决心为加强禁止原子弹和氢弹的运动而努力。
