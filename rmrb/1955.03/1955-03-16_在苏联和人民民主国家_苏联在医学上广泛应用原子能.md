In the Soviet Union and People's Democracies
    The Soviet Union Widely Applies Atomic Energy in Medicine
    I. Kochetkov, Deputy Minister of Health of the Soviet Union, published an article in "Pravda" on the 13th, stating that atomic energy is being used more and more widely in various sectors of the national economy and in science and technology in the Soviet Union.
    Kochetkov pointed out that the Soviet Union is widely using artificial radioactive elements, especially radioactive cobalt, phosphorus, iodine, and sodium, to treat and diagnose various diseases. A wide range of medical and preventive institutions can obtain these artificial radioactive elements, which have great advantages over natural radioactive substances.
    The scientific research institutes of the Soviet Academy of Medical Sciences and clinics in Moscow, Leningrad, Kiev, and other cities have achieved good results in using radioactive phosphorus to treat polycythemia vera for five or six years. This treatment can improve blood composition, enhance people's overall health, and restore their ability to work.
    Recently, radioactive iodine can be used not only to diagnose thyroid inflammation but also to treat it. Radioactive iodine can also be used to diagnose brain enlargement.
    Radioactive sodium can be used to study the rate of blood circulation in patients with various heart and vascular diseases.
    Currently, oncological science research institutes and tumor clinics are using radioactive cobalt to study and treat tumors. This radioactive element can completely replace radium in the treatment of malignant tumors. Placing radioactive cobalt, with the equivalent effect of 400 grams of radium, in a powerful deep irradiation device can treat malignant tumors that grow deep within the body. In addition, radioactive cobalt can also be used to treat serious diseases such as lung cancer.
    Kochetkov said that the use of radioactive isotopes in biochemistry allows researchers to understand the complex metabolic processes of "tracer" substances in the body with extreme precision. This method is far superior to all previous precise research methods (including spectroscopic analysis).
    Kochetkov finally pointed out that the above are just some of the situations in which radioactive isotopes are used in the Soviet health sector. This work is still expanding, and it can be believed that, in the near future, there will be no medical department that does not use radioactive isotopes.

### 在苏联和人民民主国家  苏联在医学上广泛应用原子能

1955-03-16
第4版()
专栏：

    在苏联和人民民主国家
    苏联在医学上广泛应用原子能
    苏联卫生部副部长伊·科切尔金十三日在“真理报”发表文章说，在苏联国民经济各部门和科学与技术中，正在日益广泛地使用原子能。
    科切尔金指出，苏联正在广泛地使用人工放射性元素，特别是放射性钴、磷、碘和钠来治疗和诊断各种疾病。广大的医疗预防机构都可以得到这些人工放射性元素，它们比天然放射性物质具有很大的优点。
    苏联医学科学院各科学研究所和莫斯科、列宁格勒、基辅等城市的诊疗所，五、六年来在使用放射性磷医治红血球增加的病症方面获得良好效果。这种治疗可以改善血液的成分，增进人们的总的健康情况和恢复人们的劳动能力。
    近来，不仅可以使用放射性碘来诊断甲状腺发炎，而且可以用它来治疗这种疾病。放射性碘还可以用来诊断脑肿大。
    放射性钠可以用来研究各种心脏和血管病患者的血液循环速度。
    现在，肿瘤学科学研究所和肿瘤诊疗所都在使用放射性钴来研究和治疗肿瘤。这种放射性元素可以完全代替镭来治疗恶性肿瘤。在强力的深部照射器中置入相当于四百公分镭的作用的放射性钴，可以治疗在机体深处长成的恶性肿瘤。此外，放射性钴还可以用来治疗像肺癌这样的严重疾病。
    科切尔金说，在生物化学上使用放射性同位素可以使研究工作者极精确地了解复杂的“示踪”物质在机体中的新陈代谢过程。这种方法比以前使用的一切精确的研究方法（包括光谱分析在内）都好得多。
    科切尔金最后指出，上面只是苏联卫生事业方面采用放射性同位素的一些情形，这一工作现在还在不断扩大，可以相信，在不久的将来，没有一个医学部门不使用放射性同位素。
