Title: De Gaulle Says the United States Commands French Policy
Author:
Date: 1955-03-27
Page: 4

De Gaulle Says
The United States Commands French Policy
(Xinhua News Agency, Paris, 26th) According to Agence France-Presse, Paris, General de Gaulle said in a letter published on the 24th that French policy is commanded by the United States.

In the letter, General de Gaulle wrote: "Under the current regime, there can be no French policy. The closest and most influential foreign country commands French policy. Currently, it is the United States that is doing this."

This letter was published the day after the French Senate began debating the bill to ratify the Paris Agreements. It was written by General de Gaulle on March 2nd in response to a letter from a secondary school teacher in Paris regarding the current political situation.

### 戴高乐说  美国指挥着法国的政策

1955-03-27
第4版()
专栏：

    戴高乐说
    美国指挥着法国的政策
    【新华社二十六日讯】据法新社巴黎消息，戴高乐将军在二十四日发表的一封信中说：法国的政策是由美国指挥的。
    戴高乐将军在信中写道：“在目前的政权下，不能有法国的政策。关系最密切的、最有影响的外国指挥着法国的政策。在目前，是由美国这样做的。”
    这封信在法国参议院开始辩论关于批准巴黎协定法案的第二天发表，是戴高乐将军在三月二日写来答复巴黎的一个中学教师写给他的关于目前政治局势的信件的。
