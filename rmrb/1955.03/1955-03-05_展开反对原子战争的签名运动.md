Title: Launch a Signature Campaign Against Atomic War

Author:

Date: 1955-03-05
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, Berlin, March 3rd: The members of the Executive Committee of the World Federation of Democratic Youth held a press conference in Berlin on March 1st.

    Jacques Denis, Secretary-General of the World Federation of Democratic Youth, briefed reporters on the situation of the Executive Committee meeting of the World Federation of Democratic Youth held in Berlin from February 25th to 28th. He said that the Executive Committee of the World Federation of Democratic Youth called on young people around the world to actively participate in the World Peace Council's movement against the use of atomic weapons. The Executive Committee approved the plan for the 5th World Youth and Student Festival for Peace and Friendship to be held in Warsaw. The Second World Student Sports Games will be held during this festival.

    Jacques Denis said that the meeting of the Executive Committee of the World Federation of Democratic Youth showed that young people from all over the world actively support the struggle of German youth against the revival of German militarism and for the reunification of Germany based on peace and democracy.

    Czechoslovakia Starts Signature Campaign

    Xinhua News Agency, Prague, March 2nd: Czechoslovakia began a signature campaign on February 28th in support of the World Peace Council's appeal to the people of the world.

    That morning, thousands of propagandists from the Czechoslovak National Front visited factories, mines, government offices, schools, and rural areas to collect signatures from residents of all walks of life. Mass rallies to collect signatures were successively held in enterprises and unified agricultural cooperatives across the country.

    By noon, 7,000 workers at the famous Dawn Shoe Factory had signed the appeal to the people of the world. They also put forward many production guarantees to support their signatures.

    Li. Sudorowski, a welder at the Vitkovice Iron and Steel Plant, said when signing: "Every sensible person must sign the appeal to the people of the world. We ordinary people do not want war." He and his fellow workers were determined to make more steel for peace.

    I. Ilka, a welder at the Skode-Dukla Machine Manufacturing Factory, said: "Our peace efforts are not just signing the appeal to the people of the world. We will work in our workplaces to continuously improve the quality of our products."

    Romania's Signatures Reach Over Two Million

    Xinhua News Agency, March 2nd: According to the Romanian News Agency, the signature campaign against the use of atomic weapons continues throughout Romania. According to incomplete statistics from six states, including Krosch, Petezdi, Baco, Arad, Iasi, and Krajova, more than two million people have signed. The number of signatures in Bucharest, the capital of Romania, has reached 700,000.

    Hungary's Signatures Reach Over 3.7 Million

    Xinhua News Agency, Prague, March 3rd: According to the Hungarian News Agency, Hungary's signature campaign against the use of atomic weapons has achieved remarkable results. As of February 26th, 3,746,800 people across the country had signed the World Peace Council Standing Committee's appeal to the people of the world.

    Mongolia's Signatures Reach Over 300,000

    Xinhua News Agency, March 2nd: TASS, Ulan Bator, March 1st: The Mongolian People's Republic is enthusiastically launching a campaign to sign the World Peace Council Standing Committee's "Appeal to the People of the World." As of now, more than 300,000 people have signed the "Appeal to the People of the World."

    The signature campaign is continuing.

    Indonesian Public Leaders Sign One After Another

    Xinhua News Agency, Jakarta, March 4th: The Indonesian "People's Daily" reported on March 3rd that many Indonesian public leaders have signed the appeal against preparing for atomic war. These include Indonesian Political Party Group Consultation Committee Chairman Huseini Kata Sasimita, Mrs. Aruqi Katawenata, Labor Party Chairman Ahmed Natakusuma, and the wife of Jakarta Mayor Sudiro.

    Huseini Kata Sasimita said when signing: "We must prove that we have the power to oppose atomic war preparations." University student Ahmed Ivan said: "I believe everyone is against atomic war."

### 展开反对原子战争的签名运动

1955-03-05
第4版()
专栏：

    展开反对原子战争的签名运动
    世界青联号召青年参加签名运动
    【新华社三日讯】据塔斯社柏林讯：世界民主青年联盟执行委员会的委员们三月一日在柏林举行了记者招待会。
    世界民主青年联盟总书记雅克·德尼向记者们介绍了二月二十五日到二十八日在柏林举行的世界民主青年联盟执行委员会会议的情况。他说，世界民主青年联盟执行委员会号召全世界青年积极参加世界和平理事会的反对使用原子武器的运动。执行委员会批准了关于将在华沙举行的第五届世界青年与学生和平友谊联欢节的计划。在这次联欢节中将举行第二次世界学生体育比赛。
    雅克·德尼说，世界民主青年联盟执行委员会的会议表明，世界各国青年积极支持德国青年反对复活德国军国主义、争取根据和平和民主的方针重新统一德国的斗争。
    捷克斯洛伐克开始签名运动
    【新华社布拉格二日电】　捷克斯洛伐克在二月二十八日开始了拥护世界和平理事会告全世界人民书的签名运动。
    这一天的清晨，捷克斯洛伐克民族阵线的数千个宣传员访问了工厂、矿山、机关、学校和农村，向各阶层的居民征集签名。各地的企业和统一农业合作社相继举行了征集签名的群众大会。
    到中午的时候，著名的曙光制鞋工厂已经有七千工人在告全世界人民书上签了名。他们还提出了许多生产保证来支持他们的签名。
    维特科维泽钢铁厂的焊工利·苏多罗夫斯基在签名时说：“每个有常识的人必须在告全世界人民书上签名。我们老百姓都不希望战争。”他和同他在一起的工人们决心要为和平炼出更多的钢。
    斯克德—杜克拉机器制造工厂的焊工伊·伊尔卡说：“我们的和平努力不仅仅是在告全世界人民书上签名。我们将要在我们的工作场所为不断改善我们产品的质量而努力。”
    罗马尼亚签名人数达二百多万
      【新华社二日讯】　据罗马尼亚通讯社讯：罗马尼亚全国各地正在继续开展反对使用原子武器的签名运动。据克罗什、彼特兹迪、巴科、阿拉德、雅西和克拉约发等六个州的不完全统计，已有二百多万人签了名。罗马尼亚首都布加勒斯特的签名人数已经达到了七十万。
    匈牙利签名人数达三百七十多万
    【新华社布拉格三日电】　据匈牙利通讯社报道，匈牙利反对使用原子武器的签名运动已经取得了显著成就。到二月二十六日，全国已经有三百七十四万六千八百人在世界和平理事会常务委员会告全世界人民书上签了名。
    蒙古签名人数达三十多万
    【新华社二日讯】　塔斯社乌兰巴托一日讯：蒙古人民共和国正在热烈展开在世界和平理事会常务委员会“告全世界人民书”上签名的运动。截至目前为止，在“告全世界人民书”上签名的已有三十多万人。
    签名运动正在继续进行。
    印度尼西亚公众领袖纷纷签名
    【新华社雅加达四日电】　印度尼西亚“人民日报”三日报道，印度尼西亚的许多公众领袖已经在反对准备原子战争的呼吁书上签了名。这些人当中包括印度尼西亚政党团体协商委员会主席侯赛尼·卡塔萨斯米塔、阿鲁齐·卡塔韦纳塔夫人、工党主席艾哈迈德·纳塔库苏马和雅加达市长苏迪罗的夫人等。
    侯赛尼·卡塔萨斯米塔在签名时说：“我们必须证明，我们是有力量反对原子战争准备的”。大学生艾哈迈德·伊凡说：“我相信每一个人都反对原子战争”。
