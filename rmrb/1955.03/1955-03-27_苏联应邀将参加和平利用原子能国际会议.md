Title: The Soviet Union Accepts Invitation to Participate in International Conference on Peaceful Uses of Atomic Energy
Author:
Date: 1955-03-27
Page: 1

Xinhua News Agency, New York, March 25th: TASS reports that on February 1st, the United Nations Secretary-General sent letters to all United Nations member states and member states of the United Nations specialized agencies, inviting them to participate in the International Conference on the Peaceful Uses of Atomic Energy scheduled to be held in Geneva in August 1955.

It is understood that the Soviet Union's Permanent Representative to the United Nations, Sobolev, wrote to United Nations Secretary-General Hammarskjöld on March 22nd, stating: "Regarding your letter to the Minister of Foreign Affairs of the Soviet Union on February 1st of this year, which included a formal invitation to participate in the International Conference on the Peaceful Uses of Atomic Energy, I am authorized to declare that the Government of the Union of Soviet Socialist Republics accepts the invitation to participate in the International Conference on the Peaceful Uses of Atomic Energy scheduled to be held in Geneva in August 1955. I have the honor to inform you that Academician D.V. Skobeltsyn has been nominated by the Union of Soviet Socialist Republics as Vice-President of the International Conference."

### 苏联应邀将参加和平利用原子能国际会议

1955-03-27
第1版()
专栏：

    苏联应邀将参加和平利用原子能国际会议
    【新华社二十五日讯】塔斯社纽约讯：二月一日，联合国秘书长致函所有联合国会员国和联合国各专门机构的成员国，邀请它们参加定于一九五五年八月在日内瓦举行的和平利用原子能国际会议。
    据悉，苏联常驻联合国代表索波列夫在三月二十二日写信给联合国秘书长哈马舍尔德，信中说：“关于你在今年二月一日致苏联外交部长的附有一份邀请参加和平利用原子能国际会议的正式请柬的信件，我受权声明，苏维埃社会主义共和国联盟政府接受参加定于一九五五年八月在日内瓦举行的和平利用原子能国际会议的邀请。我荣幸地通知你，德·维·斯科贝尔琴院士被苏维埃社会主义共和国联盟提名为国际会议的副主席。”
