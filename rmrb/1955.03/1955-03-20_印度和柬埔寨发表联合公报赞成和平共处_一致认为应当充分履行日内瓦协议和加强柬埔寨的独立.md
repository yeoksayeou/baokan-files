Title: India and Cambodia Issue Joint Communiqué Advocating Peaceful Coexistence, Agreeing on Full Implementation of the Geneva Accords and Strengthening Cambodian Independence
Author:
Date: 1955-03-20
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, New Delhi, March 18th: The Cambodian friendship delegation, led by former Cambodian King Sihanouk, who recently abdicated, arrived in New Delhi on the 16th for a visit. The delegation included Cambodian Prime Minister Lan Te, among others.

Xinhua News Agency, New Delhi, March 19th: According to the Indian Information Service, Indian Prime Minister Nehru and the delegation of the Royal Government of Cambodia, led by Prince Sihanouk, who were invited by the Indian government to visit India, issued a joint communiqué on March 18th after holding talks in New Delhi.

The joint communiqué stated that the delegation of the Royal Government of Cambodia and the Indian Prime Minister "discussed many issues of common concern to both countries, as well as certain aspects of world issues related to both countries."

The joint communiqué stated, "The head of the Cambodian delegation expressed respect for India's general attitude towards world affairs and expressed the hope to maintain peace. The delegation of the Royal Government of Cambodia and the Indian Prime Minister agreed that the most reliable guarantee of world peace and friendship between countries is adherence to the following principles: recognition of each other's sovereignty, independence, and integrity; non-aggression; equality and mutual respect; non-interference in internal affairs and non-intervention in the internal affairs of other countries; and the promotion of conditions for peaceful coexistence."

The joint communiqué stated, "The two governments also agreed that the Geneva Accords should be fully implemented, and that every effort should be made to maintain and strengthen Cambodia's independence and improve the conditions of its people."

The joint communiqué also stated that the Indian government assured the delegation of the Royal Government of Cambodia that they hoped to assist Cambodia to the best of their ability. The two governments also agreed to exchange diplomatic missions at the level of embassies as soon as possible.

### 印度和柬埔寨发表联合公报赞成和平共处  一致认为应当充分履行日内瓦协议和加强柬埔寨的独立

1955-03-20
第4版()
专栏：

    印度和柬埔寨发表联合公报赞成和平共处
    一致认为应当充分履行日内瓦协议和加强柬埔寨的独立
    【新华社新德里十八日电】由最近退位的前柬埔寨国王西哈努克率领的柬埔寨友好代表团在十六日到达新德里进行访问。代表团团员包括柬埔寨首相兰?特等人。
    【新华社新德里十九日电】据印度新闻处消息，印度总理尼赫鲁和应印度政府邀请到印度访问的由西哈努克太子率领的柬埔寨王国政府代表团在新德里举行会谈以后在三月十八日发表了联合公报。
    联合公报说，柬埔寨王国政府代表团和印度总理“讨论了许多同两国共同有关的问题，也讨论了同两国有关的世界问题的某些方面。”
    联合公报说，“柬埔寨代表团团长表示尊重印度对世界问题的一般态度，并且表示希望维持和平。柬埔寨王国政府代表团和印度总理一致认为，世界和平和各国友好的最可靠的保证是遵守下述原则：承认彼此主权、独立和完整，互不侵犯，平等及互相尊重，互不干涉内政和不干涉其他国家内政，以及促进和平共处的条件。”
    联合公报说，“两国政府并且一致认为，应当充分履行日内瓦协议，应当尽一切努力以维护和加强柬埔寨的独立，并且改善它的人民的境况。”
    联合公报还说，印度政府向柬埔寨王国政府代表团保证，它们希望尽力之所及援助柬埔寨。两国政府并同意尽早交换公使馆级的外交使团。
