Title: U.S. Price Increases Break Records
Author:
Date: 1947-08-28
Page: 3

　　United States
  　Price Increases Break Records
    (Xinhua News Agency, Northern Shaanxi, 26th) New York dispatch: "High prices have broken the records since 1920," this is the description of the recent soaring prices in the United States in a report by the U.S. Department of Labor on the 21st. According to the department, the prices of 900 basic commodities have soared in the past five weeks. The average price in August increased by 2% compared to July, and surged by 19% compared to the same period last year. A shirt that cost $1.50 before the war now costs $4, and butter that cost 40 cents a pound before the war now costs 80 cents. It is reported that the value of the U.S. dollar has depreciated by 45% compared to before the war. Families with general incomes of $5,000 are now unable to make ends meet, greatly reducing their living standards.

### 美国　物价上涨创新纪录

1947-08-28
第3版()
专栏：

　　美国
  　物价上涨创新纪录
    【新华社陕北二十六日电】纽约讯：“物价高涨创一九二○年以来的新纪录”，这是美劳工部二十一日报告中对最近美国物价飞涨之描绘。据该部公布，五个星期来九百种基本商品的价格飞涨不已。八月份物价较七月份平均涨了百分之二，较去年同期猛增百分之十九。战前值一元五角的衬衫，现在索价四元，战前四角一磅的牛油，现在要八角。据悉：美元价值已较战前贬值百分之四十五。一般收入五千元的家庭现均入不敷出，大大降低了他们的生活水准。
