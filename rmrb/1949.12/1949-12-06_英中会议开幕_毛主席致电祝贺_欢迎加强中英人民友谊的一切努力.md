Title: Anglo-Chinese Conference Opens; Chairman Mao Sends Congratulatory Telegram; Welcomes All Efforts to Strengthen Friendship Between the Chinese and British People
Author:
Date: 1949-12-06
Page: 1

Xinhua News Agency, Prague, December 5th: London dispatch: The Anglo-Chinese Conference, initiated by British progressives, opened on December 3rd at Beaufoy Hall in London. Professor Farrington of Goosehay University, a professor of classical literature, served as chairman of the conference. According to a statistic taken on the 1st, 334 delegates elected by 198 mass organizations attended the conference. These included representatives from the British National Peace Committee, ten of the largest industrial unions in Britain, and forty local unions, as well as prominent figures from all walks of life. When the national flag of the People's Republic of China was hung alongside the British flag on the rostrum for the first time, the delegates erupted in prolonged and enthusiastic cheers. When Farrington read out the congratulatory telegram from Chairman Mao Zedong of the People's Republic of China to the conference, the delegates unanimously stood up, and the venue resounded with lasting applause. Farrington announced that the Anglo-Chinese Conference was supported by the broad masses of British workers, intellectuals, scientists, educators, and some members of parliament. The purpose of the conference is to cultivate and develop friendship and business relations with the People's Republic of China.

Xinhua News Agency, Beijing, December 5th: Chairman Mao Zedong of the Central People's Government of the People's Republic of China sent a telegram on November 30th to congratulate the Anglo-Chinese Conference held in London on the 3rd. The original telegram is as follows:

Mr. Jack Dribben, please forward to all the sponsors and representatives of the Anglo-Chinese Conference:

I extend my warm congratulations on the convening of the Anglo-Chinese Conference. The Chinese people welcome all efforts to strengthen the friendship between the Chinese and British people and hope that such efforts will be successful.

Mao Zedong
November 30, 1949

### 英中会议开幕　毛主席致电祝贺　欢迎加强中英人民友谊的一切努力

1949-12-06
第1版()
专栏：

　　英中会议开幕
  　毛主席致电祝贺
  　欢迎加强中英人民友谊的一切努力
    【新华社布拉格五日电】伦敦消息：由英国进步人士所发起的英中会议，已于十二月三日在伦敦贝佛尔大厅开幕。会议由鹅海大学古典文学教授法林顿任主席，出席大会的代表，据一日统计，有一百九十八个群众团体推选出来的三百三十四人，其中包括英国全国和平委员会、十个英国最大的产业工会和四十个地方工会的代表，以及各界名流等。当中华人民共和国的国旗第一次与英国国旗并悬于主席台上时，代表们发出了长久而热情的欢呼。当法林顿向会议宣读中华人民共和国毛泽东主席的贺电时，代表们一致起立，会场响澈历久不息的掌声。法林顿宣布：英中会议受到英国广大工人、知识分子、科学家、教育家和若干国会议员的支持，会议的目的，在于培养并发展对中华人民共和国的友谊与商务关系。
    【新华社北京五日电】中华人民共和国中央人民政府主席毛泽东，于十一月三日致电祝贺三日在伦敦举行的英中会议，原电如下：
    杰克·德里本先生请转英中会议的全体赞助人先生们和代表先生们：
    对于英中会议的召开，谨致热烈的祝贺。中国人民欢迎一切加强中英人民友谊的努力，并希望这种努力获得成功。
    　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　毛泽东
    　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　一九四九年十一月三十日
