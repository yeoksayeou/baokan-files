Title: The Whole Nation Exuberantly Rejoices and Cheers for Chairman Mao's Visit to the Soviet Union
Author:
Date: 1949-12-20
Page: 1

Xinhua News Agency, Beijing, December 19th: After learning that Chairman Mao Zedong of the Central People's Government had arrived in Moscow, people across the country were very excited and encouraged, and expressed their feelings about this great event. Students from Peking University, Normal University, and Jiaotong University, after receiving this important news on the evening of the 17th, immediately broadcast it to the entire school using loudspeakers. Everyone applauded warmly, chanting "Long live Chairman Mao!", "Long live Stalin!", and "Long live Sino-Soviet friendship!". The Philosophy Department group at Peking University stated: "Chairman Mao is bringing the fervent feelings of our people to the great leader of the world's people, Stalin. This is a sublime expression of internationalism and patriotism."

Huang Huoqing, Deputy Director of the Tianjin Sino-Soviet Friendship Association Branch and Director of the Preparatory Committee of the Municipal Federation of Trade Unions, said: "Chairman Mao's visit to Moscow symbolizes the further consolidation of the relations between China and the Soviet Union, and the friendship between the peoples of the two countries will achieve new development. Therefore, the peace-loving people of the world and the workers of Tianjin will be happy and cheer for this!" Labor models from various units under the Municipal Federation of Trade Unions—Wang Liankai from the Waterworks, Zhao Xi'en from the Automotive Transportation Company, Sun Detang from the North China Electric Power Company Branch, and Yu Zhaozhang from the First Power Plant—unanimously stated: The meeting of the leaders of the two great nations of the world will bring more light and happiness to the working class of the world and the working people of China. Zhang Huaisan, Director of the Tianjin Youth League Preparatory Committee, Peng Qing, Secretary-General of the Municipal Women's Federation, Wu Gongjun, Secretary-General of the Municipal Student Federation, Xiao Caiyu, Chairman of the University Faculty and Staff Union, Dong Jinqiu, Chairman of the Primary and Secondary School Faculty and Staff Union, A Ying, Director of the Literature and Art Department of the Municipal Military Control Commission's Culture and Education Department, and figures from the industrial and commercial sectors such as Li Zhuchen, Zhou Shutao, Zi Yaohua, and Bi Mingqi also expressed their feelings, believing that Chairman Mao's visit to the Soviet Union further enhanced Sino-Soviet friendship and strengthened the international democratic and peaceful forces.

When the Sino-Soviet Friendship Association of Shenyang and the Municipal Federation of Literary and Art Circles were holding a celebration for Stalin's 70th birthday, they heard the news of Chairman Mao's meeting with Marshal Stalin. The entire venue immediately erupted in thunderous applause, with cheers lasting for a long time. Li Junfu, Secretary-General of the Northeast General Branch of the Sino-Soviet Friendship Association, believed that this great event would encourage millions of people to redouble their efforts to develop Sino-Soviet friendship and expressed the need to more widely introduce the Soviet Union's construction experience and expand the organization of the Sino-Soviet Friendship Association. Workers from the Shenyang Chemical Factory and the Glass Factory expressed their intention to commemorate this great event with practical actions of creating new records. The representatives attending the Northeast Student Work Conference, which was in session, were extremely excited upon hearing the news. Lu Guangji, Chairman of the Shenyang Municipal Federation of Industry and Commerce, issued a written statement expressing support for the close cooperation between the two great nations of China and the Soviet Union.

After hearing the news of Chairman Mao's arrival in the Soviet capital, leaders of various democratic parties, organizations, and groups in Nanjing, as well as professors and scholars, were extremely happy. Wang Qizhong of the Democratic League and Zhang Shiming of the Chinese Peasants' and Workers' Democratic Party both made statements. Zhang Shiming stated: The meeting of the two giants will inspire peace-loving and democratic people around the world and make domestic and foreign reactionaries led by American imperialism tremble even more. Wang Mingyuan, Secretary of the Municipal Youth League Committee and Director of the Democratic Youth Federation Preparatory Committee, Zhu Chengxue, Chairman of the Municipal Student Federation, Liu Yulin, Director of the Municipal Farmers' Association Preparatory Committee, Pan Shu, Chairman of the Nanjing University School Committee, scientist Yin Zanxun, and economist Wu Baoshan also expressed their views. Wang Mingyuan and Zhu Chengxue pointed out: Chairman Mao's visit to Moscow is another major event in the nation's political life following the founding ceremony.

The people of Changsha felt boundless excitement after hearing the news of Chairman Mao's arrival in the Soviet Union. Cheng Qian, Vice Chairman of the Central South Military and Political Committee, Tang Shengzhi, Member of the Central South Military and Political Committee, and Chen Mingren, Commander of the 21st Army Corps of the People's Liberation Army, all made statements, stating that the relations between China and the Soviet Union would become more consolidated, the peace and democracy camp would become stronger, and it would be a strong guarantee for the success of New China's production and construction. Cao Yong, President of the Changsha Branch of the Sino-Soviet Friendship Association, Huang Guoliang, a railway worker, Yu Yongquan, a postal worker, and Wu Yixing, a telecommunications worker, all expressed their opinions in excited and joyful tones.

After scrambling to buy extra editions from newsboys, the citizens of Xi'an were all beaming with joy and spreading the news. Representatives from industry, agriculture, youth, women, students, and commerce all made statements, and the Masses Daily received many communications and reports from all walks of life celebrating the meeting of the two great people's leaders.

### 全国人民兴奋鼓舞为毛主席访苏欢呼

1949-12-20
第1版()
专栏：

　　全国人民兴奋鼓舞为毛主席访苏欢呼
    【新华社北京十九日电】全国各地闻悉中央人民政府毛泽东主席抵达莫斯科后，都很兴奋鼓舞，并纷纷发表对于这一伟大事件的感想。北京大学、师范大学及交通大学等校同学，在十七日晚获知这一重大消息后，立即用扩大器向全校广播，大家热烈鼓掌，欢呼“毛主席万岁！”、“斯大林万岁！”、“中苏友好万岁！”。北京大学哲学系小组表示：“毛主席把我们人民火热的心情带给全世界人民伟大的领袖斯大林，这就是国际主义与爱国主义的崇高表现。”
    天津中苏友好协会分会副主任、市总工会筹委会主任黄火青说：“毛主席赴莫斯科，象征着中苏两大国家的邦交更加巩固，中苏两国人民的友谊将获得新的发展。因此，爱好世界和平的人民和天津市的工人，都将为此高兴和欢呼！”市政工会所属各单位的劳动模范——自来水厂王连凯、汽车运输公司赵锡恩、华北电力公司分公司孙德棠、第一发电所于兆璋等一致表示：世界两大民族领袖的会晤，将带给世界各国工人阶级和中国的劳动人民以更多的光明和幸福。青年团天津筹委会主任张淮三、市妇女联合会秘书长彭青、市学生联合会秘书长吴功俊、大学教职员联合会主任委员肖采瑜、中小学教职员联合会主席董劲秋、市军管会文教部文艺处处长阿英及工商业界人士李烛尘、周叔韬、资耀华、毕鸣岐等都纷纷发表感想，认为毛主席的访问苏联，进一步增进了中苏的友谊，使国际民主和平势力更加壮大。
    沈阳市中苏友好协会和市文联在举行庆祝斯大林七十寿辰大会时听到毛主席和斯大林大元帅会晤的消息，全场立即响起雷鸣般的掌声，欢呼历久不息。中苏友好协会东北总分会秘书长李俊夫认为这一伟大事件将鼓舞千百万群众为开展中苏友好事业而加倍努力，并表示要更广泛地介绍苏联的建设经验，扩大中苏友好协会的组织。沈阳化学工厂、玻璃厂工人纷纷表示要以创新纪录的实际行动来纪念这一伟大事件。正在开会的东北学生工作会议各代表听到消息后都异常兴奋。沈阳市工商联合会主席卢广绩发表书面谈话，表示拥护中苏两大民族的亲密合作。
    南京市各民主党派、机关、团体等负责人及教授、学者等闻悉毛主席抵苏京消息后，都欢欣异常，民主同盟王气钟、农工民主党章师明都发表谈话，章师明表示：两大巨人的会晤，将使全世界爱好和平民主的人民鼓舞；使以美国帝国主义为首的国内外反动派更加战栗。青年团市委书记、民主青联筹委会主任王明远、市学联主席朱成学、市农民协会筹委会主任刘玉林、南京大学校委会主席潘菽、科学家尹赞勋、经济学家巫宝山等分别发表意见。王明远与朱成学指出：毛主席访问莫斯科，是全国政治生活中继开国盛典后的又一件大事。
    长沙市人民听到毛主席抵苏的消息后，感到无限的兴奋。中南军政委员会副主席程潜、中南军政委员会委员唐生智、人民解放军第廿一兵团司令员陈明仁均发表谈话，表示中苏两国邦交将益臻巩固，和平民主阵营将更形强大，而且对新中国生产建设的成功是一个有力的保证。中苏友协长沙支会会长曹甬、铁路工人黄国梁、邮政局工人于涌泉、电讯局工人伍翼兴等都以兴奋欢快的语调发表了意见。
    西安市民从报童手中争购号外后，莫不喜形于色，纷纷传诵。工、农、青、妇、学、商各界代表都发表了谈话，群众日报收到各界欢庆两大人民领袖会晤的许多通讯和报道。
