　　Talking About Dates
    Wang Huaizhou
    Dates ripen in August and September, and can be eaten in both rural and urban areas, making them one of the most popular fruits. The discovery that dates contain a large amount of Vitamin C has been circulating since last year, but has not yet been formally published. Now that fresh dates are on the market again, let me briefly introduce their nutritional value.
    The main component of dates is carbohydrates, followed by small amounts of protein, fat, and minerals. The most special and important thing is that they contain extremely rich Vitamin C (referred to as Vitamin C). Vitamin C is one of the essential food components for humans. A lack of it in the human diet can lead to conditions such as sores in the stomach and intestines, subcutaneous bleeding, brittle bones, unsound teeth, and loose tooth roots. In ancient times, many sailors died from scurvy because they could not eat fresh vegetables and fruits. This was due to that reason.

   An adult needs to consume at least 75 mg of Vitamin C daily. If relying on only one type of food to provide this amount, one would need to eat 3 jin of peaches, 10 jin of sand pears, 13 jin of apples, or 6 liang of mandarin oranges. However, eating just 8 fresh small sour dates or 2-7 ordinary fresh dates is sufficient.
    There are three things to note when eating dates:
    First, eat them fresh. Dates that have been stored for too long and become soft have significantly reduced Vitamin C content, and dried dates contain almost no Vitamin C.
    Second, wash them thoroughly, preferably peel them. Dates easily attract flies, and if not washed properly, eating them can easily lead to gastrointestinal infections. Date skin is not easy to digest, so eating too many dates can easily cause diarrhea. Peeling them before eating not only ensures cleanliness and hygiene but also avoids indigestion, which is a win-win situation.
    Third, fresh dates should not be cooked, as cooking significantly reduces the Vitamin C content.
    Vitamin C is almost completely lost in ripe, soft, or dried dates. How to preserve this large amount of Vitamin C is a question worth studying. We have tried the method of soaking them in alcohol, but it was unsuccessful. However, selecting whole, intact dates while they are fresh and storing them in a refrigerator near or below 0 degrees Celsius, or preserving them in a solution with a sufficient acidity (using citric acid) and adding sugar to keep them fresh, may prevent severe loss of Vitamin C. We are planning to experiment further. Readers who are interested in this experiment may also try it themselves and are encouraged to share their experiences and results with us. (Nutrition Research Office, Xian Nong Tan Hygiene Experimental Institute, Beiping)
    One more thing worth noting is that, taking Gaga dates as an example, we can see that almost half of them have worms inside when we eat them. To improve the quality of dates, we should make every effort to eliminate the worms on the date trees.

　　谈枣
    王淮洲
    枣在八九月间成熟，无论乡村、城市，都可吃到，堪称大众化果品之一。枣含有大量丙种维生素，这个研究的结果，从去年起就传出了，但一直还没有正式发表。现在又到鲜枣上市的时候，让我来简单介绍一下它的营养价值。
    枣的主要成分是糖类物质，其次还含有少量的蛋白质、脂肪和矿物质，最特殊重要的是它含有极丰富的丙种维生素（简称丙素）。丙素是人类所不可缺少的食物成分之一，人的膳食中缺少了它，就发生肠胃生疮、皮下出血、骨节脆薄、牙齿不健全、牙根动摇等病状。古代航海的人，因为吃不到新鲜蔬菜水果，得坏血病而致死亡的很多。就是这个缘故。

   一个成人每日至少需吃丙素七十五毫克，如果单靠吃一种东西来供给这个数量的丙素，那么要吃桃三斤，沙果十斤，苹果十三斤，橘子也要吃六两，但若吃枣只要新鲜小酸枣八个或普通鲜枣二至七个就够了。
    吃枣时应当注意三件事：
    第一、要吃新鲜的，放得过久软了的枣所含丙素大为减少，晒干了的枣几乎不含丙素。
    第二、要用水洗净，最好是削皮——枣是易招苍蝇的，若不把它洗净，吃了就容易生胃肠传染病，枣皮不易消化，所以吃枣吃多了容易泻肚，削了皮再吃，不但清洁卫生，而且也免得不易消化，是一举两得。
    第三、鲜枣不可煮着吃，因为煮过的枣，丙素是会损失很多的。
    熟软或晒干以后的枣，丙素几乎完全没有了。怎样可以把这大量的丙素保存起来，这是一个值得研究的问题。用酒醉的方法我们已经试过，是不成功的。但是趁着新鲜的时候择出整齐不破的，放在近于摄氏零度或零度以下的冰箱中，把它冷藏起来；或是放在相当的酸度溶液里（用柠檬酸），并加糖把它罐藏起来，使枣保持新鲜，可能使丙素不受严重损失，我们还预备试验。读者对于这个试验有兴趣的，无妨自己也试一试，并希望将试验的情形和结果告诉我们。（北平先农坛卫生实验院营养研究室）
    还有一件值得注意的事，以嘎嘎枣来说，我们吃的时候，可以看见，差不多有一半里边有虫子，为了提高枣的品质，枣树上的虫子应该尽力设法消灭。
