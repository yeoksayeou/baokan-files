  　Twelve, Statistical Chart and Explanation of the Post-War Five-Year Plan

    "We must enable our industry to produce 50 million tons of pig iron, 60 million tons of steel, 500 million tons of coal, and 60 million tons of oil annually. Only under this condition can we guarantee that our motherland can cope with all contingencies. For this, maybe, not much, but three new five-year plans. This cause can be done, and we must accomplish it."

    "The basic task of the new five-year plan is to rehabilitate the areas of our country affected by the war, restore industry and agriculture to pre-war levels, and then, to a greater or lesser extent, surpass pre-war levels."
    　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　——Stalin

    The Soviet Union's five-year plan for post-war economic recovery and development (1946-1950) stipulated that the total value of the Soviet Union's total industrial output in 1950 should be 250 billion rubles, an increase of 48 percent over the 138.5 billion rubles in 1940.

  　十二、战后五年计划统计图解
    “我们一定要使我们的工业能够每年生产五千万吨的铣铁，六千万吨的钢，五亿吨的煤，六千万吨的石油。只有这个条件才能保证我们祖国能够应付一切事变。为了这个，可能，不多的话，再订出三个新的五年计划。这个事业是能够做得到的，而且我们必须要完成它。”
    “新五年计划的基本任务是要复兴我国受战祸的区域，恢复工业和农业到战前的水准，然后，或多或少地还要超过战前的水准。”
