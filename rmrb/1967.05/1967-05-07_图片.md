Title: International Friends Discuss the Great Proletarian Cultural Revolution with Red Guards of Peking University
Author:
Date: 1967-05-07
Edition: 5

	International friends recently visited Peking University to engage in a lively discussion with the Red Guards about the Great Proletarian Cultural Revolution. The atmosphere was filled with warmth and camaraderie as they exchanged views on the significance and impact of the Cultural Revolution.

	The international friends expressed their admiration for the revolutionary spirit and determination of the Chinese people. They praised the Red Guards for their courage in standing up against the revisionist elements and for their commitment to safeguarding the achievements of the revolution. The Red Guards, in turn, shared their experiences and insights gained from participating in the Cultural Revolution.

	The discussion covered a wide range of topics, including the role of the proletariat in leading the revolution, the importance of class struggle, and the need to eliminate bourgeois ideology. The international friends emphasized the global significance of the Cultural Revolution, noting that it was not just a Chinese affair but a struggle that had implications for the entire world.

	The Red Guards highlighted the importance of studying Mao Zedong Thought and the need to apply it in practice. They stressed that the Cultural Revolution was a continuous process that required constant vigilance and effort to ensure its success.

	The meeting concluded with a call for greater international solidarity and cooperation in the struggle against imperialism, revisionism, and all reactionary forces. The international friends and the Red Guards pledged to support each other in their respective struggles and to learn from each other's experiences.

	The event was a testament to the growing international support for the Great Proletarian Cultural Revolution and the unity of the world's revolutionary forces. It underscored the importance of internationalism in the struggle against oppression and exploitation.
