Title: African Workers Celebrate Their Own Festival
Author:
Date: 1967-05-03
Edition: 6

Thousands of workers in Tanzania held a demonstration and rally in the capital, Dar es Salaam, on May 1 to celebrate International Labor Day.

The workers participating in the parade wore work clothes and red flowers on their chests, holding up banners with slogans such as: "All reactionaries are paper tigers!" "We condemn the atrocities of American imperialism in Vietnam!" "Yankee, stop interfering in Africa!" "Workers must be vigilant against the spy activities of the U.S. Information Service!" and so on.

In his speech at the rally, President Nyerere called on all workers in Tanzania to contribute their efforts to realize the Arusha Declaration and build a self-reliant Tanzania.

Attendees at the mass rally included the second vice president, Kawawa, government ministers, and leaders of the Tanganyika Federation of Labor.

In Zanzibar, a morning rally and mass meeting were also held to celebrate International Labor Day, with about 10,000 participants. First Vice President Karume, in his speech, called on the people of Tanzania to defend the country's independence and unity. He said, "We must not forget the sufferings we endured before the revolution."

In the Congo (Brazzaville), tens of thousands of workers and youth held a rally and parade in Brazzaville on May 1 to enthusiastically celebrate International Labor Day.

President Massemba-Debat of the Congo (Brazzaville), Speaker of the National Assembly Mouyabi Andre, members of the Political Bureau of the National Revolutionary Movement, and government ministers reviewed the parade.

Thousands of workers in Mauritania held a parade and rally in the capital, Nouakchott, on May 1 to celebrate International Labor Day.

Fahal Marik, General Secretary of the Mauritania Workers' Union, said at the rally, "As long as colonialism and imperialism continue to exist, our independence will always be threatened." Marik, in his speech, condemned imperialist aggression against Vietnam and expressed firm support for the struggles of the African, Palestinian, and occupied South Arabian peoples for independence.

In Algeria, thousands of workers, peasants, youth, and students held a rally and parade in Algiers on May 1 to celebrate International Labor Day.

The participants in the rally and parade carried banners with slogans such as "Down with American imperialism!" "Get out of Vietnam, American imperialism!" "Oppose the infiltration of Zionism!" and chanted slogans such as "Johnson is a murderer!"

### 非洲劳动人民欢庆自己的节日  坦桑尼亚、刚果（布）、毛里塔尼亚等国人民示威游行和集会

1967-05-03
第6版()
专栏：

    非洲劳动人民欢庆自己的节日
    坦桑尼亚、刚果（布）、毛里塔尼亚等国人民示威游行和集会
    坦桑尼亚几千名工人一日在首都达累斯萨拉姆举行了示威游行和集会，庆祝“五一”国际劳动节。
    参加游行的工人们穿着工作服，胸前戴着红花，高举的标语牌上写着：“一切反动派都是纸老虎！”“我们谴责美帝国主义在越南的暴行！”“美国佬，停止你们对非洲的干涉！”“工人必须对美国新闻处的间谍活动保持警惕！”等口号。
    尼雷尔总统在大会上讲话中号召坦桑尼亚的全体工人为实现《阿鲁沙宣言》，建设一个自力更生的坦桑尼亚而尽自己的一份力量。
    出席群众大会的有第二副总统卡瓦瓦、政府部长们和坦噶尼喀全国工人联合会的领导人。
    在桑给巴尔，一日上午也举行了庆祝“五一”国际劳动节的游行和群众大会。参加的大约有一万人。第一副总统卡鲁姆在大会上讲话号召坦桑尼亚人民捍卫国家的独立和团结。他说：“我们不要忘记我们在革命前所遭受的苦难。”
    刚果（布）上万名工人和青年一日在布拉柴维尔举行集会和游行，热烈庆祝“五一”国际劳动节。
    刚果（布）总统马桑巴—代巴，国民议会议长穆亚比·安德烈，全国革命运动政治局委员和政府部长们检阅了游行队伍。
    毛里塔尼亚几千名劳动群众五月一日在首都努瓦克肖特举行游行和集会，庆祝“五一”国际劳动节。
    毛里塔尼亚工人联合会总书记法尔·马立克在集会上说：“只要殖民主义和帝国主义还继续存在，我们的独立就将始终受到威胁。”马立克在讲话中，谴责帝国主义对越南的侵略，并表示坚决支持非洲、巴勒斯坦以及被占领的南阿拉伯人民争取独立的斗争。
    阿尔及利亚几千名工人、农民、青年和学生一日在阿尔及尔举行集会和游行，庆祝“五一”国际劳动节。
    参加集会和游行的队伍高举写着“打倒美帝国主义！”“美帝国主义从越南滚出去！”“反对犹太复国主义的渗透！”等横幅标语，并高呼：“约翰逊是杀人犯！”等口号。
                                      （据新华社讯）
