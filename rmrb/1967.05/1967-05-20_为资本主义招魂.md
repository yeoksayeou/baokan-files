Title: Summoning the Spirit of Capitalism
Author:
Date: 1967-05-20
Edition: 6

To meet the political needs of the Soviet revisionist leadership's restoration of capitalism, Soviet literary works have spread many ideas advocating the development of capitalism. The second volume of Stadnyuk's novel "Man is Not an Angel" is a prominent example.

The protagonist of "Man is Not an Angel" Part 2, the collective farm chairman Pavel, sees many farm members cultivating large private plots, renting out houses, and engaging in commerce. Instead of trying to stop them, he stands on the platform and loudly proclaims to the people: "Do not be afraid of these contradictions in our lives! ... Since the state cares about material abundance and the peasants care about private plots and livestock, how can one avoid such contradictions?"

### 为资本主义招魂

1967-05-20
第6版()
专栏：看苏修怎样在国内复辟资本主义

    为资本主义招魂
    适应苏修领导集团实行资本主义复辟的政治需要，苏联文学作品中散布了许多鼓吹发展资本主义的东西。斯塔德纽克的长篇小说《人不是天使》第二部就是一个突出的例子。
    《人不是天使》第二部的主人公集体农庄主席巴维尔，见到很多庄员大种自留地，出租房屋，上集做买卖，他不但不想去制止，反而要站到讲台上向人们大声疾呼：“不要害怕我们生活中的这些矛盾！……既然国家关心物质丰富和农民关心自留地、私养牲口是完全一致的，怎么可能免除这种矛盾现象呢？……”
