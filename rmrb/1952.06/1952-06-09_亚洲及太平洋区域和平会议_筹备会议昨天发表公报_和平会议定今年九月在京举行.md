Title: Asian and Pacific Regions Peace Conference Preparatory Meeting Yesterday Released Communiqué, Peace Conference Scheduled to be Held in Beijing This September
Author:
Date: 1952-06-09
Page: 1

Xinhua News Agency, 8th: The Preparatory Meeting for the Asian and Pacific Regions Peace Conference held a press conference in Beijing on the 8th and released the following communiqué:

Communiqué of the Preparatory Meeting for the Asian and Pacific Regions Peace Conference

The Preparatory Meeting for the Asian and Pacific Regions Peace Conference, which opened in Beijing on June 3rd, successfully concluded on June 6th.

This meeting was initiated by eleven prominent Chinese figures dedicated to the peace movement, representing the will of the Chinese people and accepting suggestions from India and other peace-loving individuals. Attending the meeting were forty-seven representatives from twenty countries, including China, India, Pakistan, the Soviet Union, Japan, Korea, the United States, Australia, Mexico, Vietnam, Indonesia, Canada, Burma, Ceylon, Mongolia, Chile, New Zealand, the Philippines, Thailand, and Malaya, as well as two secretaries from the Executive Bureau of the World Peace Council and one member of the World Federation of Trade Unions Asian-Australian Liaison Bureau. During the meeting, representatives from Colombia, Guatemala, El Salvador, Nicaragua, and Costa Rica were en route to Beijing. Peru, Ecuador, Laos, and Nepal agreed to convene this meeting, but due to various difficulties, were unable to send representatives this time. Those attending the meeting as observers included: two from Australia, two from Burma, five from China, one from India, and one from Indonesia. At this meeting, representatives from various countries reported on the ardent desire for peace among their people, the status of peace movements in their countries, and various opinions on convening the Asian and Pacific Regions Peace Conference to expand and strengthen the peace movement. The main tasks of this meeting were to discuss and pass the preparatory meeting's declaration and proposals regarding the various preparatory works for the Asian and Pacific Regions Peace Conference. To draft these two documents, the meeting organized a Declaration Drafting Committee and an Organizing Committee. These two committees, through significant efforts, completed their tasks. After extensive and in-depth discussions, the meeting unanimously passed the meeting's declaration and proposals regarding the preparatory work for the Asian and Pacific Regions Peace Conference. In addition to Guo Moruo signing the declaration as an initiator, representatives from the following countries also signed:

China: Chen Shutong, Liu Ningyi, Li Dequan, Nan Hanchen, Wu Yaozong.

Korea: Park Chung-ae, Jeon Dong-hyuk.

United States: John Kingsbury.

Australia: Jean Bolton, Vern Ilde, Stephen Magindo, Ada Bromham, A. Gietzelt.

Mexico: Elí Gordari, Rafael Méndez.

Vietnam: Duong Bach Lien, Le Chan Phuong.

Indonesia: Suwati, Surusho, Suryonikoro.

Canada: Mary Jennison.

Burma: U Hla, U Myo Myint, Tekin Lwin, U Tun Ba Maung, U Khun Ti, U Aye Kyi.

Ceylon: Saralankara.

Mongolia: Bazar Shirindib.

Chile: José Venturelli.

New Zealand: Rewi Alley, Courtney Archer.

Philippines: Manuel Cruz.

Thailand: ? Sawan Dulalak, ? Base Sassuntorn.

Malaya: Zeng Xuehong.

World Federation of Trade Unions Asian-Australian Liaison Bureau Member: Thornton.

Executive Bureau Secretary of the World Peace Council: Guryayev and John Darr.

In addition, Yusuf, an Indian observer representative, was also entrusted by the All-India Trade Union Congress to sign the declaration on behalf of the All-India Trade Union Congress.

According to the resolution of this meeting, the Asian and Pacific Regions Peace Conference will be held in Beijing in the last week of September. In order to specifically carry out the preparatory work for the Asian and Pacific Regions Peace Conference, the meeting has decided to establish one Secretary-General and two Deputy Secretaries-General under the Preparatory Committee. The Secretary-General will be from China, and the Deputy Secretaries-General will be from India and Japan. When necessary, the Secretary-General may invite representatives from relevant countries to serve as assistants to the Secretary-General. The Chinese side has decided to appoint Liu Ningyi as Secretary-General to immediately carry out various preparatory works.

June 8, 1952, Beijing

### 亚洲及太平洋区域和平会议  筹备会议昨天发表公报  和平会议定今年九月在京举行

1952-06-09
第1版()
专栏：

    亚洲及太平洋区域和平会议
    筹备会议昨天发表公报
    和平会议定今年九月在京举行
    【新华社八日讯】亚洲及太平洋区域和平会议筹备会议八日在北京举行招待记者会，发表公报如下：
    亚洲及太平洋区域和平会议筹备会议公报
    六月三日在北京开幕的亚洲及太平洋区域和平会议筹备会议已于六月六日圆满闭幕。
    这个会议是由中国十一位著名的努力和平运动的人士代表中国人民的意志并接受印度及其他爱好和平的人士的建议发起的。出席这次会议的，有中国、印度、巴基斯坦、苏联、日本、朝鲜、美国、澳大利亚、墨西哥、越南、印尼、加拿大、缅甸、锡兰、蒙古、智利、新西兰、菲律宾、泰国、马来亚二十个国家的四十七个代表，还有世界和平理事会执行局秘书二人，世界工联亚澳联络局委员一人。在会议期间，已在来北京途中的，还有哥仑比亚、危地马拉、萨尔瓦多、尼加拉瓜、哥斯达黎加五个国家的代表。同意召开这一会议，但因种种困难，这一次不能派代表出席的，还有秘鲁、厄瓜多尔、寮国、尼泊尔四个国家。列席这次会议的有：澳大利亚二人，缅甸二人，中国五人，印度一人，印尼
    一人。在这次会议中，各国代表都报告了各国人民对于和平的殷切愿望，各国和平运动的情况以及对于召开亚洲及太平洋区域和平会议，以扩大和加强和平运动的各种意见。这次会议的主要任务，在于讨论并通过筹备会议宣言和关于亚洲及太平洋区域和平会议各项筹备工作的提议。为了起草这两个文件，会议曾组织宣言起草委员会和组织委员会。这两个委员会经过重大的努力，完成了它的任务。会议经过广泛地深入地讨论之后，全体一致地通过了会议宣言和关于亚洲及太平洋区域和平会议筹备工作的提议。在这个宣言上签字的除郭沫若以发起人身分参加签名外，有下列各国家的代表：
    中国：陈叔通、刘宁一、李德全、南汉宸、吴耀宗。

    苏联：瓦丁姆·科热夫尼科夫、贺捷耶夫、伊凡·格鲁森科。
    日本：高良富、帆足计、宫腰喜助。

    墨西哥：艾里·哥尔达利、拉斐尔·门德斯。
    越南：杨白莲、黎真芳。
    印尼：苏瓦蒂、苏鲁梭、苏约尼哥罗。
