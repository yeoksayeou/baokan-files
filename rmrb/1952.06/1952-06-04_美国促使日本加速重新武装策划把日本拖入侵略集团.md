Title: US Urges Japan to Accelerate Rearmament, Plotting to Drag Japan into an Aggressive Bloc
Author:
Date: 1952-06-04
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, June 3rd, Tokyo dispatch: After the separate peace treaty with Japan came into effect, the United States is repeatedly urging Japan to accelerate its rearmament and is plotting to pull Japan into a Pacific aggressive bloc.

According to a May 29th dispatch from Tokyo by Agence France-Presse, citing "well-informed sources," U.S. occupation forces commander in Japan, Clark, sent a secret letter to the traitorous Japanese Prime Minister Yoshida. In the letter, Clark proposed: First, Japan must "accelerate" its rearmament efforts and can rely on U.S. "aid." Second, "The United States believes that Japan is strategically very important." The U.S. military headquarters is actually "re-evaluating" the "strategic plan" to use Japan for aggression, and the results of this work will be notified to Japan. Third, the United States "requires Japan to provide intelligence obtained on the Asian continent and to cooperate with the United States in collecting intelligence on the mainland."

On May 27th, U.S. Ambassador to Japan Murphy revealed during a meeting with Yoshida that the United States will disregard the opposition of the people of Far Eastern countries, organize a Pacific aggressive bloc similar to the notorious Atlantic aggressive bloc, and take Japan as the core force of the Pacific aggressive bloc. The aforementioned "well-informed sources" revealed that Murphy told Yoshida: "The United States will not abandon its plan to establish a Pacific Pact like the Atlantic Treaty until it combines the various security treaties it has concluded with Pacific countries into a Pacific Pact." According to the U.S. plan, "The United States first intends to give the U.S., New Zealand, and Australia trilateral security treaty a practical method and finally make certain modifications so that this treaty will eventually be merged into a larger security system." In order to realize this conspiracy, the United States is preparing to hold negotiations with the Australian and New Zealand governments this summer on this issue. After persuading them to dispel their concerns about the rearmament of Japan, "the United States will invite Japan and the Philippines to participate in the 'Pacific Defense Group'." Therefore, Japan must "rearm and establish many regular divisions and build arsenals to help this Pacific Defense Group." In this way, the United States' conspiracy to force Japan to provide the cannon fodder needed by the United States to carry out aggression in the Pacific region is even more exposed.

### 美国促使日本加速重新武装策划把日本拖入侵略集团

1952-06-04
第4版()
专栏：

    美国促使日本加速重新武装策划把日本拖入侵略集团
    【新华社三日讯】东京消息：在单独对日和约生效之后，美国正在再三催促日本加速重新武装，并策划把日本拉到太平洋侵略集团中去。
    据法新社东京五月二十九日电引“消息灵通人士”透露，美国驻日占领军司令克拉克曾经给日本卖国首相吉田一封秘密信。克拉克在信中提出：第一，日本必须“加速”重新武装工作，并且可以依靠美“援”。第二，“美国认为日本在战略上是很重要的”。美军总部实际上正在“重新估计”利用日本进行侵略的“战略计划”，这项工作的结果将通知日本。第三，美国“要求日本把它在亚洲大陆方面所得的情报提供出来，并且还要和美国合作搜集大陆上的情报”。
    五月二十七日，美国驻日大使墨菲与吉田会谈时透露，美国将不顾远东各国人民的反对，组织与臭名远扬的大西洋侵略集团相类似的太平洋侵略集团，并以日本为太平洋侵略集团的核心力量。上述“消息灵通人士”透露，墨菲告吉田说：“美国非到把它和太平洋国家所缔结的各种安全条约结合成大西洋公约那样的太平洋公约时，是不会放弃成立太平洋公约的计划的。”根据美国的计划，“美国首先打算给美国、新西兰、澳大利亚三国安全条约定一个切实可行的办法，最后予以某些修改，以便使得这种条约终于合并到一个较大的安全系统中去”。为了实现这一阴谋，美国准备在今年夏天与澳、新两国政府就这个问题举行谈判。在说服它们打消对重新武装日本的顾虑之后，“美国就要邀请日本和菲律宾参加‘太平洋防御集团’”。因此，日本必须“重新武装起来，并成立许多正规师和建立兵工厂来帮助这个太平洋防御集团”。这样，美国强制日本供给美国在太平洋区域进行侵略所需要的炮灰的阴谋，就更加暴露出来了。
