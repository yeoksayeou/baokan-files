Title: Two Questions Concerning Commodity Production
Author: Yin Huige
Date: 1949-08-18
Page: 6

Learning Mailbox

To the Editors Regarding Two Questions on Commodity Production:
    We encountered two problems when studying the section on "Capitalist Society" in the "A Brief History of Social Development":
    (1) Why does the study of capitalist production begin with commodity production?
    (2) What is the most fundamental difference between simple commodity production and capitalist commodity production? What is the significance of distinguishing them?
    With best regards,
  　　　　　　　　　　Chinese People's Liberation Army Navy
    　　　　　　　　　Yin Huige
  　　　　　　　　　　School Research Office Study Group
    Answer: First, why does the study of capitalist production begin with commodity production? This is a question of methodology in political economy.
    Science tells us that to truly and deeply understand things, we must understand them from the basic aspects and basic factors.
    In physics, to fundamentally understand matter, we must understand its atoms and even electrons; in biology, we must understand the cells of organisms; in social sciences, to understand society, we must understand the material production process at the foundation of society. Political economy, as one of the social sciences, aims to understand society fundamentally, so it must study the material production process of society.
    In capitalist society, the fundamental aspect of capitalist production is commodity production. Therefore, starting the study of the capitalist economy from commodity production aligns with the requirements of scientific method.
    Studying the capitalist economy from commodity production is precisely to understand the capitalist economy concretely. Bourgeois economists believe that capitalist production is the production of wealth. This view is an empty and abstract one that fails to distinguish capitalist production from various non-capitalist forms of production.
    Marxist-Leninist economics differs from bourgeois economics. It does not regard capitalist commodity production as the production of general products but as commodity production, as capitalist commodity production. In this way, we can distinguish the characteristics of different types of production, and we can understand capitalist production concretely and profoundly.
    Second, what is the most fundamental difference between simple commodity production and capitalist commodity production? What is the significance of distinguishing them?
    So-called simple commodity production refers to commodity production before capitalism. Capitalist commodity production refers to commodity production during the capitalist period (from the perspective of economic development).
    Simple commodity production, such as in the feudal era, is the commodity production of small producers (farmers and artisans). Capitalist commodity production is commodity production under the control of capitalists (from the perspective of the mode of production).
    Simple commodity production is production for exchange, for sale. Capitalist commodity production is production for making money (for profit, which comes from exploiting hired workers) (from the perspective of the purpose of production).
    Distinguishing simple commodity production from capitalist commodity production clarifies the boundaries between two different types of commodity production in history and allows us to recognize their fundamental differences in terms of the mode of production. This is not only theoretically necessary but also has practical significance. For example, in the Soviet Union, the Trotskyites (who had long become Trotskyite bandits) did not distinguish between simple commodity production and capitalist commodity production, but treated them the same. This became the theoretical basis for their argument that the working class should not unite with the peasants (because they considered the small commodity producers to be capitalist elements).
    In China, the Trotskyites did not distinguish between these two types of commodity production, considering them both to be capitalist production. This also became one of the theoretical bases for their claim that the Chinese economy was dominated by capitalist economics (therefore, they advocated that the Chinese revolution was a proletarian revolution).
    Conversely, in the Soviet Union, correctly distinguishing between simple commodity production and capitalist commodity production led to the establishment of an alliance between the working class and the peasantry, increased the strength of the revolution, and prevented the working class from becoming isolated. In New China, because we correctly distinguish between simple commodity production and capitalist commodity production, we can correctly understand the Chinese economy and classes in this regard, making it the starting point of our policies, and we can correctly understand and implement policies in this area. Otherwise, we will make mistakes in this area. - Editor -

### 关于商品生产两问题
尹挥戈
1949-08-18
第6版()
专栏：学习信箱

　　关于商品生产两问题编辑同志：
    我们在学习“社会发展简史”关于“资本主义社会”一节的时候，有两个问题不能解决：
    （一）研究资本主义生产为什么从商品生产开始？
    （二）单纯的商品生产与资本主义商品生产最基本的区别是什么？区别它有何意义？
    此致敬礼
  　　　　　　　　　　中国人民解放军海军
    　　　　　　　　　尹挥戈
  　　　　　　　　　　学校研究室学习小组
    答：第一、为什么研究资本主义生产从商品生产开始？这是政治经济学方法论上的一个问题。
    科学告诉我们，要从基本的方面，基本的因素了解，才能真正地深入地了解事物。
    在物理学上基本地了解物，要了解物的原子以至电子；在生物学上要了解生物的细胞，在社会科学上要了解社会，要了解到社会底物质的生产过程，社会科学之一的政治经济学为的是从基本上了解社会，就要研究社会的物质的生产过程。
    在资本主义社会里资本主义的生产，基本的是商品生产。所以从商品生产开始研究资本主义经济，正合乎科学方法的要求。
    从商品生产开始研究资本主义经济，正是具体地了解资本主义经济。资产阶级经济学者认为资本主义的生产是财富的生产，这种看法，是空洞的抽象的看法，看不出资本主义生产与各种非资本主义的生产的区别。
    马列主义经济学和资产阶级经济学不同，它不把资本主义商品生产当作一般生产物的生产来看，而当作商品生产，当作资本主义商品生产来看；这样一来，就能区别不同生产的特点，就对资本主义的生产，可以了解得具体深入了。
    第二、关于单纯商品生产与资本主义商品生产最基本的区别是什么？区别它有何意义？
    所谓单纯商品生产，是资本主义以前的商品生产；资本主义的商品生产是资本主义时期的商品生产（从经济发展的时期说）。
    单纯商品生产，例如在封建时代，是小生产者（农民、手工业者）的商品生产。资本主义的商品生产，是资本家支配下的商品生产（从生产方式说）。
    单纯商品生产是为交换为出卖而生产；资本主义的商品生产是为赚钱（为利润，其利润由剥削被雇佣的工人而来）而生产（从生产的目的说）。
    区别单纯商品生产与资本主义的商品生产，一方面在历史上两种不同的商品生产，可以划分明确的界限，并从生产方式上认识其基本的区别。这不仅是理论上的必要，同时也有其实际的意义，例如在苏联托洛斯基派（早已成为托匪）不区别单纯商品生产与资本主义的商品生产，而把单纯商品生产与资本主义的商品生产一样看待，这就成为他们所说工人阶级不要联合农民的理论根据（因为他们认为农民小商品生产者是资本主义成份）。
    在中国，托派不区别这两种商品生产，认为都是资本主义的生产；这也就成为他们所说中国经济是资本主义经济占支配地位的理论根据之一（因此他们主张中国革命是无产阶级革命）。
    相反地，在苏联，对单纯商品生产与资本主义的商品生产，正确地加以区别，就建立了工人阶级与农民的联盟，就增加了革命力量，工人阶级就不孤立。在新中国由于我们对单纯商品生产与资本主义的商品生产，正确地加以区别，就能在这方面正确地认识中国经济与阶级，成为我们政策的出发点，就能在这一方面正确地了解政策，执行政策。不然的话，在这一方面就要犯错误。——编者——
