The Reality of Sino-American Relations (Continued II)
    Wang Cheng
　　Under the guise of "Open Door Policy" and "maintaining China's territorial sovereignty," how much sovereignty did American imperialism plunder from China?
    American imperialism, under the guise of "Open Door Policy" and "maintaining China's territorial sovereignty and integrity," gently opened China's door and plundered China's sovereignty. Later, during the Anti-Japanese War and after the Anti-Japanese War, it transformed from solely dominating China to becoming the supreme overlord of the Kuomintang reactionary government in China, thereby carrying out even greater plunder of China.
    From the end of the 19th century to before the First World War, the total amount of American trade with China almost doubled. In 1899, the total amount of American trade with China was 43.7 million taels, and in 1913 it was 73 million taels. The number of commercial firms in China also increased from 70 to 131. By 1919, due to the Chinese people's opposition to Japan and their boycott of Japanese goods, American exports to China soared to 110 million taels (a three-fold increase compared to before the war).
    Thereafter, American trade with China rose sharply. We can understand this by looking at the following statistics:
　　　　　　　　1934　　　　　　1936
　　American goods imported to China　174,930,000 yuan　185,512,000 yuan
　　Chinese goods exported to America　136,410,000 yuan　186,221,000 yuan
　　Import surplus　　　　　　　　38,520,000 yuan　 809,000 yuan
    Comparing the imports of various countries in January 1937, the United States accounted for 19.6%, Japan 16.7%, Germany 15.9%, and the United Kingdom 11.7%. The United States surpassed all imperialist countries and firmly sat in the first place. Among these commodities, American imports were mainly metal hardware and other manufactured goods, while Chinese exports were mainly raw materials such as tung oil, leather, and pig bristles. This is the characteristic of trade between capitalist countries and semi-colonial countries.
    In addition, American investment in China also expanded daily. The situation at the end of the 19th century and the beginning of the 20th century was as follows:

    From these statistics, we can see that American investment in China, especially after the war, increased rapidly, and was invested in political loans, aviation, shipping, railways, and other government transportation industries, which are not productive but have the highest profits.
    Now, the industrial and commercial investment, the U.S. loans and securities to the Chinese government, and the assets of U.S. churches and charitable organizations in China will be described separately:
    (A) Industrial and Commercial Investment
    According to the 1929 statistics, the total amount of American industrial and commercial investment in China was US$155,778,000. After investigating 353 companies and shops, he learned that: 17 large companies, accounting for 4.8%, accounted for 8.2% of industrial and commercial investment, that is, about 12.5 million US dollars; 196 medium-sized companies and shops, accounting for 55.5%, invested 16.6%, that is, about 25 million US dollars, with an average investment of about 129,000 US dollars; and 140 small-scale ones, accounting for 39.7%, only invested about 2 million US dollars, that is, 1.4%, with an average investment of 15,000 US dollars. From here, we can see how powerful the few monopolistic organizations are in China.

Most of these companies and foreign firms were established after the war, and 64.9% are concentrated in Shanghai. Shanghai's reputation as a paradise for adventurers is well-deserved.

    These loans are mostly guaranteed by consolidated taxes or customs duties, and are either attached with political clauses or articles of management rights.
    (3) American Churches and Charitable Organizations' Investments in China
    According to Remer's statistics, the total amount of such investment is US$41,904,889. This number is quite large, it is even slightly more than the amount of loans the Chinese government borrowed from the United States! According to the statistics of Christian church assets, medicine accounts for 14.7%, education 38.2%, and religious activities 47.1%. Many famous universities in our country, such as Yenching, Union, Huiwen, St. John's, Jinling, Xiangya, Soochow, and West China, are all schools established by the United States.
    In addition, the investments of churches and charitable undertakings are mostly dispersed in the inland:
　　Shanghai　　　　22.3%

    This is exactly the opposite of the commercial investment area, one is concentrated along the coast and the other is dispersed in the inland. This is worthy of attention. American imperialism spent such a large sum of money to anesthetize the Chinese people, open up the market, and act as a vanguard for capitalists, which is not without reason.     王城
　　美国帝国主义在“门户开放”，“维护中国领土主权”的糖衣下掠夺了中国多少主权？
    美国帝国主义就在“门户开放”，“维护中国领土主权完整”的糖衣下，轻轻地打开了中国的国门，掠夺了中国的主权。以后到抗日战争期间及抗日战争以后，一变独霸了中国，而为国民党反动政府中国的太上皇了，从而也就对中国进行更大的掠夺。
    从十九世纪末到第一次世界大战前，美国对中国贸易的总额，几乎增加了一倍。一八九九年时，美国对中国贸易的总额是四三·七百万两，一九一三年是七三百万两。在中国的商号也从七十家增至一百三十一家。迄一九一九年因中国人民反对日本，抵制日货，美国对中国的输出，激增至一一○百万两（比战前增加三倍）
    此后，美国对中国的贸易，扶摇直上，我们看一看下面这个统计，就可以明白了：一九三四年　一九三六年美货输华　一七四、九三○、○○○元　一八五、五一二、○○○元华货输美　一三六、四一○、○○○元　一八六、二二一、○○○元入超  三八、五二○、○○○元  八○九、○○○元
