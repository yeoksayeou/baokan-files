　　The Truth About Sino-US Relations (Continued 4)
　　Wang Cheng
　　Quite a Bit of Capital Spent
　　In January 1947, after Marshall slipped out of China, sure enough, within five months, the State Department announced the sale of 30 million rounds of surplus U.S. military rifle bullets to the Chinese reactionaries at one-tenth of the original price, for a total of $656,658, with freight borne by the Kuomintang reactionary government. At the same time, it announced that the United States could freely sell various surplus materials to the Chinese reactionaries, and the government could issue export permits for military supplies, which would be handled by commercial entities. In addition to this, the Export-Import Bank also announced on the same day that after the expiration of the $500 million loan to China, it would consider granting the Kuomintang reactionary government another loan for special purposes or projects in order to "assist the National Government in restoring its country, improving its agricultural and industrial economy, and establishing its military system, so that it can fulfill China's responsibilities for maintaining peace and order at home and abroad"; in reality, it was to help the Kuomintang reactionary government carry out the anti-people civil war.

　　中美关系实况（四续）
    王城
　　本钱花得不算少
    一九四七年一月，马歇尔溜出中国之后，果然，不出五个月，国务院宣布以美军剩余步枪子弹三千万发，按原价十分之一，作价六十五万六千六百五十八元售予中国反动派，运费由国民党反动政府负担。同时宣布美国可以自由以各种剩余物资售予中国反动派，并且可以由政府签发军用品出国证，由商业方面经营此项买卖。除此以外，进出口银行同日也宣布五亿元对华贷款期满之后，还可以考虑给国民党反动政府另批特种目的或计划之用的贷款，以便“协助国民政府复兴其国家，改善其农工经济，并建立其军事体制，俾能尽中国对国内对国际所负维护和平秩序之职责”；其实，就是帮助国民党反动政府进行反人民的大内战而已。
