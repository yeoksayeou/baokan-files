Title: Voyager 2 Discovers Aurora on Neptune
Author:
Date: 1989-08-31
Edition: 3

The U.S. National Aeronautics and Space Administration said that photos sent back by Voyager 2 on August 28 show that Neptune and Triton do indeed have auroras.

Roger Yelle, a researcher from the University of Arizona, said that although Neptune's magnetic poles are tilted 50 degrees from Neptune's rotational axis, the auroras are not just above the tilted poles but show signs of being all around Neptune. Moreover, when Triton orbits Neptune, which is surrounded by a magnetic layer, Triton also produces faint auroras.

### “旅行者二”号发现海王星极光

1989-08-31
第3版(国际新闻·体育)
专栏：

    “旅行者二”号发现海王星极光
    新华社北京8 月30日电　美国宇航局说，从“旅行者2”号28日发回的照片中发现，海王星和海卫一上确实有极光。
    亚利桑那大学的研究人员罗杰·耶利说，尽管海王星的磁极偏斜海王星的旋转轴50度，但极光不只是倾斜磁极上方有，而在海王星周围都有极光的迹象。不仅如此，当海卫一飞绕被磁性层包围的海王星时，海卫一也产生微弱的极光。
