Leaders of Non-Aligned Countries Emphasize Adherence to Principles of Non-Aligned Movement
Oppose Imperialism, Hegemonism, and Support Liberation Movements

BEIJING, Sept. 4 (Xinhua) -- At the opening of the ninth summit of non-aligned countries today, many leaders stressed that while adapting to new situations, the non-aligned movement must adhere to its established principles of opposing imperialism, colonialism, and hegemonism, and supporting liberation movements.

The executive chairman of the ninth summit of non-aligned countries and the chairman of the Federal Presidency of Yugoslavia, Dejan Dervosevic, said in his opening speech that changes in the world require non-aligned countries to quickly adapt to new situations. However, changes in contemporary international relations confirm that the goals and policies of the non-aligned movement remain the aspirations of humanity. The basic strategic guidelines of the non-aligned movement have not changed. Maintaining the independence and non-aligned status of the non-aligned movement is the primary task of this summit.

The executive chairman of the previous summit of non-aligned countries and President of Zimbabwe, Robert Mugabe, emphasized in his speech that the reform of the non-aligned movement should not mean abandoning principles, but rather to better achieve the goals of the non-aligned movement and strengthen its actions.

Egyptian President Hosni Mubarak stressed that while engaging in dialogue with major powers, non-aligned countries must remain true to the basic principles of the non-aligned movement.

The President of Bangladesh, Hussain Muhammad Ershad, pointed out that in today's changing world, it is necessary to maintain the basic values of the non-aligned movement.

He said that as long as there is any form of external political and economic domination and hegemonism, the non-aligned movement will provide small and weak countries with the key to security and unity.

The chairman of the Southern Commission of the non-aligned movement, Julius Nyerere, emphasized that if non-aligned countries want to play an effective role in international economic and political negotiations, they must strengthen joint actions.

### 不结盟国家首脑强调坚持不结盟运动原则  反帝反霸支持解放运动

1989-09-05
第3版(国际新闻·体育)
专栏：

    不结盟国家首脑强调坚持不结盟运动原则
    反帝反霸支持解放运动
    新华社贝尔格莱德9月4日电　在今天开幕的第九次不结盟国家首脑会议第一天会议上，许多不结盟国家首脑在发言中强调，不结盟运动在适应新形势的同时，必须坚持既定的反帝反殖反霸和支持解放运动的基本原则。
    第九次不结盟国家首脑会议执行主席、南斯拉夫联邦主席团主席德尔诺夫舍克在开幕式上说，世界上发生的变化要求不结盟国家必须尽快适应新的形势。而当代国际关系中发生的变化证实不结盟运动的目的和方针仍然是人类的愿望。不结盟运动的基本战略方针没有改变。继续保持不结盟运动的独立和不结盟地位，是这次会议的首要任务。
    上届不结盟国家首脑会议执行主席、津巴布韦总统穆加贝在讲话中强调，不结盟运动的改革不应意味着放弃原则，而是为了更好地实现不结盟运动的目的和加强行动能力。
    埃及总统穆巴拉克强调，不结盟国家在同大国进行对话的同时，必须忠于不结盟运动的基本原则。
    孟加拉国总统艾尔沙德指出，在今天这个正在变化的世界上，必须保持不结盟运动的基本价值。
    他说，只要存在着任何政治和经济的外来统治和霸权主义，不结盟就会向小国和弱国提供保障安全和联合的钥匙。
    不结盟运动南方委员会主席尼雷尔强调，如果不结盟国家想在国际经济和政治谈判中起到有效作用，就必须加强共同行动。
