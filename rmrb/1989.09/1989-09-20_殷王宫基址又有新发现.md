Title: New Discoveries at the Yin Dynasty Palace Site
Author: Yang Yinglan
Date: 1989-09-20
Edition: 2

New discoveries have been made at the site of the Yin Dynasty palace. The Xinhua News Agency reported from Zhengzhou on September 19 that since the founding of the People's Republic, China has made its first discovery of the palace building foundations of the Yin Dynasty at the Yin Ruins. Excavation is currently underway.

The Yin Ruins are located in the outskirts of Xiaotun Village, Anyang City, Henan Province, and were the capital during the late Shang Dynasty. The excavation of the Yin Ruins marked the birth of modern archaeology in China. The palace building foundations discovered in the 1930s. The newly discovered foundation covers an area of 5,000 square meters, making it the largest discovered to date. The discovery of this foundation provides new evidence for the architectural style of the Yin Ruins.

### 殷王宫基址又有新发现
杨英兰
1989-09-20
第2版(国内新闻)
专栏：四面八方

　　殷王宫基址又有新发现
    新华社郑州九月十九日电　（记者杨英兰）建国以来我国首次在殷墟发现了殷王宫殿建筑基址，目前发掘正在进行中。
    殷墟位于河南安阳市郊小屯村，是商代晚期都城所在地。殷墟的发掘，是我国近代考古学诞生的标志，其王宫建筑基址都是在三十年代发现的。这座新发现的基址，占地面积为五千平方米，是到目前为止所发现的面积最大的一座。这座基址的发现提供了殷墟建筑基址新的形制。
