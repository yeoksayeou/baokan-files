Title: Ethnic Education Thrives
Author: Yang Wen
Date: 1989-09-17
Page: 4

Ethnic education thrives

In the early 1950s, Tibet had virtually no modern education. After the democratic reforms of 1959, Tibet's educational sector flourished. Today, the region boasts three institutions of higher learning with 2,543 students; 15 vocational secondary schools with 3,465 students; 67 middle schools with 23,439 students; and 2,453 primary schools with over 144,800 students. The enrollment rate for school-age children in the region has reached 55.7%. In 1985, the autonomous region began implementing the "three guarantees" policy (guaranteeing food, clothing, and housing) for minority students in key middle and primary schools within the region. Currently, minority students make up 50% and 70% of the student population in the region's universities and vocational schools, respectively. From 1985 to 1988, the autonomous region sent over 6,450 Tibetan and other minority children to study in Tibetan classes and Tibetan schools in 17 provinces (municipalities) across the country.

### 民族教育蓬勃发展
杨文
1989-09-17
第4版(国庆专版)
专栏：

　　民族教育蓬勃发展
    五十年代初，西藏根本没有近代教育。1959年实行民主改革后，西藏的教育事业蓬勃发展。如今，全区已有高等学校3所，在校学生2543人；中等专业技术学校15所，在校学生3465人；中学67所，在校学生23439人；小学2453所，在校学生144800余人。全区适龄儿童入学率达到55.7％。1985年，自治区开始对区内重点中、小学少数民族学生实行“三包”（包吃、包穿、包住）。现在，区内高校、中专在校学生中，少数民族学生分别占50％和70％。从1985年到1988年，自治区共选派6450余名藏族和其他少数民族儿童到内地17个省（市）西藏班和西藏中学学习。　（杨　文）
