Title: Funeral for Mr. Okazaki Held in Tokyo
Author: Yu Qing
Date: 1989-09-27
Edition: 3

	Funeral for Mr. Okazaki Held in Tokyo
	Our reporter Yu Qing reports from Tokyo: Over 3,000 people from various sectors attended the farewell ceremony for Mr. Okazaki Kaitaro, a permanent advisor to the Japan-China Economic Association and an advisor to All Nippon Airways, at Honmon-ji Temple in Tokyo this afternoon. Attendees included Mr. Ito Masayoshi, the chairman of the Japan-China Friendship Parliamentarians Union, Mr. Yang Zhenya, the Chinese Ambassador to Japan, and friends of Mr. Okazaki.

	The funeral ceremony was held in a solemn atmosphere. Mr. Okazaki's coffin was placed in the main hall of the temple, surrounded by flowers and wreaths from various organizations and individuals. The ceremony began with a moment of silence in memory of Mr. Okazaki, followed by speeches from his friends and colleagues.

	Mr. Ito Masayoshi, in his speech, praised Mr. Okazaki's contributions to the development of Japan-China relations and his dedication to promoting economic cooperation between the two countries. He described Mr. Okazaki as a true friend of the Chinese people and a pioneer in the field of Japan-China economic exchanges.

	Mr. Yang Zhenya, on behalf of the Chinese government and people, expressed deep condolences to Mr. Okazaki's family and friends. He highlighted Mr. Okazaki's significant role in fostering mutual understanding and friendship between Japan and China, and his efforts in promoting cultural and economic exchanges between the two nations.

	The ceremony concluded with a final farewell to Mr. Okazaki, as attendees paid their last respects and offered flowers and wreaths. The event underscored the deep respect and admiration that Mr. Okazaki had earned from people in both Japan and China, and his enduring legacy in promoting bilateral relations.

### 冈崎先生遗体告别仪式在东京举行
于青
1989-09-27
第3版(国际新闻·体育)
专栏：

    冈崎先生遗体告别仪式在东京举行
    本报东京9月26日电　记者于青报道：各界人士3000余人，今天下午在东京本门寺内向日中经济协会常任顾问、全日空公司顾问冈崎嘉平太的遗体告别。参加告别仪式的有日中友好议员联盟会长伊东正义、中国驻日本大使杨振亚以及冈崎先生生前友好等。
