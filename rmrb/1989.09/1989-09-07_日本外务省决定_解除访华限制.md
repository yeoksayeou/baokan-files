Title: Japan's Ministry of Foreign Affairs Decides to Lift Restrictions on Visits to China
Author:
Date: 1989-09-07
Edition: 3

Japan's Ministry of Foreign Affairs has decided to officially lift restrictions on Japanese tourists and company employees visiting China starting from September 11.

The Japanese government imposed restrictions on Japanese citizens visiting China following the suppression of the counter-revolutionary riots in China on June 4.

The Japanese government believes that lifting the restrictions after more than three months will help restore relations between Japan and China. The two countries will also engage in consultations on new economic cooperation issues, including the third yen loan.

### 日本外务省决定  解除访华限制

1989-09-07
第3版(国际新闻·体育)
专栏：

    日本外务省决定
    解除访华限制
    新华社北京9月6日电　日本外务省已决定从9月11日起正式解除对日本游客和公司职员的访华限制。
    日本政府是在6月4日中国平息反革命暴乱后，对日本人访问中国采取限制措施的。
    日本政府认为，时隔3个多月全面解除访华限制，日中关系将趋向恢复，日中两国在包括第三次日元贷款在内的新的经济合作问题上也将进行磋商。
