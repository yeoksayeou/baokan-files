	Historical Records: Footprints of Progress
	Changes in Fujian through Numbers

	From the perspective of numbers, the changes in Fujian over the past few years are evident. The province's gross domestic product (GDP) has been growing steadily. In 1988, the GDP reached 24.56 billion yuan, an increase of 11.4% compared to the previous year. The per capita GDP also saw a significant rise, reaching 2,343 yuan, up by 10.1%.

	Industrial production has been a key driver of this economic growth. The total industrial output value in 1988 was 30.3 billion yuan, a year-on-year increase of 14.4%. The growth in light industry was particularly notable, with an increase of 18.5%. Heavy industry also saw a substantial rise, with an increase of 10.8%.

	Agriculture, the foundation of the economy, has also made significant strides. The total grain output in 1988 was 11.22 million tons, an increase of 3.8% compared to the previous year. The output of cash crops and aquatic products also saw notable increases, with cash crops up by 10.2% and aquatic products up by 11.5%.

	The living standards of the people have improved significantly. The per capita disposable income of urban residents in 1988 was 1,862 yuan, an increase of 5.4% compared to the previous year. The per capita net income of rural residents was 728 yuan, an increase of 6.8%. The consumption levels of various goods have also increased, reflecting the improved living standards.

	In terms of infrastructure, Fujian has also made remarkable progress. The length of highways in the province reached 22,355 kilometers in 1988, an increase of 5.3% compared to the previous year. The length of railways also saw a significant increase, reaching 1,235 kilometers, up by 4.2%.

	Education and healthcare have also seen significant improvements. The number of students enrolled in primary schools reached 4.2 million in 1988, an increase of 2.1% compared to the previous year. The number of students enrolled in secondary schools was 1.8 million, an increase of 3.5%. The number of hospital beds in the province reached 210,000, an increase of 4.7%.

	In summary, the changes in Fujian over the past few years, as reflected in the numbers, show a province that is progressing steadily in various aspects. The economic growth, improvements in living standards, and advancements in infrastructure and social services all indicate a bright future for Fujian.
