Title: A Soldier Statue Erected at Tanggula Pass
Author: Zhāng Zhènpú  Wáng Zhìléi
Date: 1989-09-26
Edition: 2

A soldier statue, approximately 5 meters high and 3 meters wide, was recently unveiled at the highest point of the Qinghai-Tibet Highway—the Tanggula Pass, which is at an altitude of 5,270 meters.

This statue, erected by the Qinghai Provincial Government, commemorates the achievements of the soldiers on the Qinghai-Tibet Line. The inscription praises their efforts in "building golden bridges, flying long arcs, dancing oil dragons, securing the borders, spreading civilization, and benefiting the people." The 4,000-kilometer Qinghai-Tibet Line was opened in the summer of 1954. Over the past 35 years, the "plateau iron cavalry" of the motorized troops have overcome numerous difficulties, traveled 1.9 billion kilometers, and transported over 400 million tons of goods in and out of Tibet.

### 唐古拉山口落成一军人石雕
郅振璞　王志雷
1989-09-26
第2版(国内新闻)
专栏：四面八方

    唐古拉山口落成一军人石雕
    本报西宁9月25日电　一座高约5米、宽3米的军人石雕，日前在青藏公路最高点——海拔5270米的唐古拉山口落成。
    这尊由青海省人民政府树立的石雕，其碑文颂扬青藏线军人“架金桥，飞长虹，舞油龙，固边陲，播文明，惠万民”的功绩。四千里青藏线于1954年夏开通，35年来，“高原铁骑”汽车兵克服严重困难，行驶19亿车公里，运送进出藏物资400多万吨。　		（郅振璞　王志雷）
