Title: The British and Their Letters
Author: Xīnxīn
Date: 1989-09-09
Edition: 7

The British write an average of 41 letters per person per year, making them the world's leading letter writers.

Despite the increasing use of telephones, telegrams, and postcards, the British tradition of writing letters remains undiminished. In 1985, the total number of letters mailed in Britain was 16 million more than in 1984, and in 1988, it was 12 million more than in 1987. According to a report from the British Post Office, no other Western European country has seen such an increase.

Among these letters, more than half are personal correspondence, with individuals under 25 years old writing the most. Approximately 10% of these are love letters.

### 英国人与信
心心
1989-09-09
第7版(国际副刊)
专栏：

    英国人与信
    英国人一年每人平均写信41封，这在世界上是“首屈一指”的。
    尽管电话、电报和明信片的使用量越来越大，英国人传统的“写信热”却丝毫未减——1985年英国人邮寄的信件总数比1984年多了1600万件，而1988年又比1987年多了1200万件！据英国邮政总局的有关报告说，在西欧其他国家都未出现此类情况。
    在这些信件中，有一多半是私人信件（25岁以下的青年人写信最多），其中约10％是情书。　（心　心译）
