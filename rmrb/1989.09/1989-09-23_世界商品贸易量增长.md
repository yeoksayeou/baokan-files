Title: Growth in World Commodity Trade Volume
Author:
Date: 1989-09-23
Edition: 7

According to the annual report released by the General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade (GATT) on September 15, the volume of world commodity trade in 1988 is estimated to have increased by 8.5 percent. The statistical figures provided in the report show that the total value of global exports last year reached a record high of $2.88 trillion, an increase of 14 percent over the previous year.

West Germany remains the world's largest exporter, with an export value of $323 billion in 1988, accounting for 11.2 percent of the world's total exports. The United States had an export value of $322 billion, accounting for 11.1 percent of the total, placing it in second position. Japan ranks third, with an export value of $265 billion, accounting for 9.2 percent.

Among the top 25 exporting countries and regions, seven are developing countries and regions, with five from Asia and two from Latin America. According to the ranking by GATT based on export value, China's position has risen from 33rd in 1978 to 16th in 1988.

### 世界商品贸易量增长

1989-09-23
第7版(国际专页)
专栏：

    世界商品贸易量增长
    据关税及贸易总协定十五日发表的年度报告，一九八八年世界商品贸易量估计增长了百分之八点五。报告提供的统计数字显示，以金额计算，去年全世界的出口总额达到创纪录的二万八千八百亿美元，比上一年增加百分之十四。
    西德仍为世界第一大出口国，去年的出口额为三千二百三十亿美元，占全世界出口总额的百分之十一点二。美国去年的出口额为三千二百二十亿美元，占总额的百分之十一点一，屈居第二。位居第三的是日本，去年的出口额为二千六百五十亿美元，占百分之九点二。
    在出口居世界前二十五名的国家和地区中，有七个属发展中国家和地区，其中亚洲五个，拉丁美洲两个。据关贸总协定排列的名次，按出口额计算，去年中国的名次已由一九七八年的第三十三位上升到第十六位。　（新华社）
