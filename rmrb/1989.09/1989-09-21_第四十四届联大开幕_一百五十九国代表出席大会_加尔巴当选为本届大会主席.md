Title: The 44th Session of the UN General Assembly Opens
Author: Liú Kāichēn
Date: 1989-09-21
Page: 3

Xinhua News Agency, United Nations, September 19th.

The 44th session of the United Nations General Assembly opened this afternoon at the UN headquarters. Representatives from 159 countries filled the meeting hall. The Assembly unanimously elected Joseph Garba, nominated by African countries, as the president of this session through a warm round of applause. Garba, who previously served as Nigeria's Foreign Minister, is currently the country's permanent representative to the UN.

Garba delivered a lengthy speech. In discussing the improvement of US-Soviet relations, he urged the two superpowers to match their words with actions and continue making progress in arms reduction, using the resources saved from arms reduction to improve the economies of developing countries.

He called for strengthened international efforts to help Africa and other developing countries in similar situations to restore and develop their economies.

The Assembly appointed a committee of representatives from China and 10 other countries to examine the credentials of representatives attending this session of the General Assembly and the special session on South African apartheid to be held in December.

This session's provisional agenda includes 149 items, among which are the debt and economic development issues of the Third World, science and technology for development, and environmental problems. This session also added a new agenda item, "Preparations for the 1990 Special Session." The situation in Cambodia and other regional conflicts remain on the provisional agenda.

The General Assembly will begin a three-week general debate starting from September 25th. As of today, 156 countries have registered to speak during the general debate, including 17 heads of state, 7 prime ministers, 8 deputy prime ministers, and 48 foreign ministers. Chinese Foreign Minister Qian Qichen will attend the meeting and deliver a speech.

### 第四十四届联大开幕  一百五十九国代表出席大会  加尔巴当选为本届大会主席
刘开宸
1989-09-21
第3版(国际新闻·体育)
专栏：

    第四十四届联大开幕
    一百五十九国代表出席大会
    加尔巴当选为本届大会主席
    本报联合国9月19日电　记者刘开宸报道：联合国第44届大会今天下午在联合国总部开幕。159个国家的代表坐满会议大厅。大会以热烈鼓掌方式通过了非洲国家推选的约瑟夫·加尔巴为本届联大主席。加尔巴曾任尼日利亚外长，现为该国常驻联合国代表。
    加尔巴发表了长篇讲话。他在谈到美苏关系改善时，要求这两个超级大国言行一致，在裁军方面继续取得进展，把裁军节省下来的资源用于改善发展中国家的经济。
    他要求加强国际努力，帮助非洲和类似情况的其他发展中国家恢复和发展经济。
    大会任命了中国等10国代表组成的“全权证书委员会”，以审查本届联大以及将于12月召开的关于南非种族主义特别联大的代表们的证书。
    本届联大临时议程共有149项，其中包括第三世界债务和经济与发展问题和科技与发展问题、环境问题。本届联大还增加了“1990年特别联大的筹备工作”新议程。“柬埔寨局势”及其它一系列地区冲突问题仍为本届联大临时议程。
    本届大会从本月25日开始将进行为期3周的一般性辩论。到今天为止，已有156个国家的代表报名要求在一般性辩论中发言，其中有17位国家元首、7位政府总理、8位副总理、48位外长。中国外长钱其琛将出席会议并发言。
