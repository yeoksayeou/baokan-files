Title: Experts Predicting at the World Energy Conference: Oil Prices Will Rise Gradually
Author: Yuan Rongsheng
Date: 1989-09-26
Page: 3

Xinhua News Agency, Montreal, September 24th.

Experts attending the World Energy Conference predict that oil prices will inevitably rise in the future, but the increase will be gradual and limited. However, the possibility of significant fluctuations cannot be ruled out.

Kenneth Davis, chairman of the World Energy Conference Planning Committee, said that oil prices, which have a decisive influence on the prices of other conventional energy sources, may gradually rise over the next 10 years but are not expected to surge sharply.

He said that the attendees unanimously agreed that oil will remain the most important energy resource throughout the first half of the next century. The supply and use of oil will have a significant social and economic impact and will be an important factor in addressing environmental issues.

After being hit by two oil crises, the Western world has comprehensively adjusted its energy strategies and industrial structures, reducing the proportion of oil in energy consumption. The industrial structure has shifted from high energy consumption to high value-added types, and this trend will continue into the 1990s. Despite this, the absolute value of oil demand will continue to rise. According to energy experts, the oil demand in the Western world will increase from the current 50.2 million barrels per day to 52 million barrels per day in 1990 and 58 million barrels per day in 2000.

### 出席世界能源大会专家预测  石油价格将缓慢上升
袁荣生
1989-09-26
第3版(国际新闻·体育)
专栏：

    出席世界能源大会专家预测
    石油价格将缓慢上升
    新华社蒙特利尔9月24日电　（记者袁荣生）出席世界能源大会的专家们预测，将来石油涨价是不可避免的，然而又是缓慢的、有限的，但大幅度波动的可能性不能排除。
    世界能源大会计划委员会主席肯尼思·戴维斯说，对其它常规能源价格具有决定性影响的石油价格，在今后10年可能会逐渐上涨，但预计不会猛涨。
    他说，与会者一致认为，到下个世纪整个上半叶，石油仍将是最重要的能源资源。石油的供应和利用将具有巨大的社会和经济影响，并且是影响整个环境问题的重要因素。
    在两次石油危机冲击下，西方世界全面调整了能源战略和产业结构。降低石油在能源消费中的比重。产业结构由高耗能型向高增值型转变，这一趋势进入90年代以后仍将继续发展。尽管如此，石油需求量的绝对值仍在上升。据能源专家们预测，西方世界的石油需求量将由目前的每天5020万桶上升到1990年的每天5200万桶和2000年的每天5800万桶。
