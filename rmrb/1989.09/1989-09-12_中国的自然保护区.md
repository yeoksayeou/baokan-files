Title: China's Nature Reserves
Author: Chang Zhongnong
Date: 1989-09-12
Edition: 5

Nature reserves are specially designated areas aimed at protecting endangered species and preserving natural historical heritage. The establishment of nature reserves has become an important part of protecting natural resources and the environment. According to statistics from the end of 1987, China has established a total of 482 nature reserves of various types, covering an area of 237,492 square kilometers, which accounts for 2.47% of the country's total land area. Among these, there are 31 national-level reserves covering 63,684 square kilometers; 314 provincial-level reserves covering 139,817 square kilometers; 21 city-level reserves covering 5,975 square kilometers; and 116 county-level reserves covering 28,016 square kilometers. The Changbai Mountains, Dinghu Mountains, Wolong, Fanjing Mountains, Wuyi Mountains, and Xilingol nature reserves have been included in the international biosphere reserve network.

### 中国的自然保护区
常仲农
1989-09-12
第5版(国内专页（教科文）)
专栏：社会主义事业在前进

　　中国的自然保护区
    自然保护区是保护濒于灭绝的物种、保护自然历史遗产而划定的特殊地域。建设自然保护区已成为保护自然资源与环境的重要组成部分。据一九八七年底统计，我国共建各类自然保护区四百八十二处，面积为二十三万七千四百九十二平方公里，占国土面积的百分之二点四七。其中，国家级的三十一处，面积六万三千六百八十四平方公里；省级的三百一十四处，面积一十三万九千八百一十七平方公里；地市级的二十一处，面积五千九百七十五平方公里；县级的一百一十六处，面积二万八千零一十六平方公里。长白山、鼎湖山、卧龙、梵净山、武夷山和锡林郭勒六个自然保护区，已加入国际生物圈保护区网。
