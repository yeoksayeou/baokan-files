Title: Bronze Chariot Unearthed in Shanxi from the Western Zhou Dynasty
Author: Chi Maohua
Date: 1989-09-18
Edition: 2

The Shanxi Provincial Institute of Archaeology recently unearthed a batch of precious artifacts from an ancient tomb of the Western Zhou Dynasty in the southern part of Shanxi Province. Among these artifacts, the most striking is a six-wheeled bronze chariot. Experts say that this type of artifact is the first of its kind to be discovered in China.

The bronze chariot is 9.1 centimeters high, 13 centimeters long, and 11 centimeters wide. It has a unique design and exquisite craftsmanship. The sides of the chariot are adorned with intricate engravings of pots, birds, monkeys, bears, and other patterns.

### 晋出土西周青铜马车
池茂花
1989-09-18
第2版(国内新闻)
专栏：四面八方

　　晋出土西周青铜马车
    新华社太原九月十七日电　（记者池茂花）山西省考古研究所最近在山西南部地区一座西周古墓葬中，发掘出一批珍贵文物，其中最引人注目的是一辆六轮青铜马车。据专家们说，这种文物在我国是首次发现。
    这辆青铜马车高九点一厘米，长十三厘米，宽十一厘米。造型奇特，工艺精美。车的四周装饰着镂空的壶、鸟、猴、熊罴等浮雕和花纹。
