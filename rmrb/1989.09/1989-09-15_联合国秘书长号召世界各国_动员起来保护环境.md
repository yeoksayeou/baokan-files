UN Secretary-General Calls on World Nations to Mobilize to Protect the Environment

	BEIJING, Sept. 13 (Xinhua) -- UN Secretary-General Javier Perez de Cuellar today called on all countries to mobilize to protect the global environment.

	This call was made by de Cuellar at the opening ceremony of the Non-Governmental Organizations Conference on Environment and Development.

	He said that the world must turn its concern for the environment into concrete policies and then into concrete actions.

	De Cuellar said that the rapid population growth and technological development in the world are exerting tremendous pressure on the ecological balance of the earth, thereby threatening the survival of the planet. If these environmental problems are not quickly and effectively solved, they will hinder the growth of the world economy, harm people's health, and cause serious social disorder.

	De Cuellar pointed out that the environmental and development problems in developing countries are particularly severe. He said that the excessive population growth and widespread poverty in developing countries are damaging the natural resources necessary for economic development. Deserts are expanding, forests are decreasing, biological diversity is declining, and water pollution is threatening the health of millions of people. At the same time, external pressures also hinder their effective response to the environmental crisis.

	De Cuellar noted that as the 1990s are approaching, people are increasingly aware of the seriousness of environmental issues. This is a very welcome progress and may mark a new loyalty—globalism.

### 联合国秘书长号召世界各国　动员起来保护环境
刘开宸
1989-09-15
第3版(国际新闻·体育)
专栏：

　　联合国秘书长号召世界各国
　　动员起来保护环境
    本报联合国9月13日电　记者刘开宸报道：联合国秘书长佩雷斯·德奎利亚尔今天号召世界各国动员起来保护全球环境。
    这个号召是德奎利亚尔在关于环境和发展的非政府组织会议开幕式上发出的。
    他说，要求全世界把对环境情况的关心化为具体政策，再把政策化为具体行动。
    德奎利亚尔说，世界上人口的迅速增长和技术的发展正对地球的生态平衡造成巨大压力，从而威胁地球的生存。这些环境问题如不能迅速、有效地解决，将妨碍世界经济增长，危害人们健康，并造成严重社会混乱。
    德奎利亚尔谈到发展中国家环境和发展问题尤为严重。他说，发展中国家人口增长过多和普遍贫穷正在损害经济发展所必需的自然资源。沙漠在扩大，森林在减少，生物的种类在减少，水污染在威胁着千百万人的健康。同时，外部的压力也妨碍它们有效地应付环境危机。
    德奎利亚尔指出，当90年代快要来临时，人们日益认识到环境问题的严重性。这是一种最可喜的进步，很可能标志着一种新的忠诚——热爱地球主义。
