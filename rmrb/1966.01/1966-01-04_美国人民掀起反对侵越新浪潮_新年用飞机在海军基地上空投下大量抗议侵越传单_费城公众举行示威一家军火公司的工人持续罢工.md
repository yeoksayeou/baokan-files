Title: American People Launch a New Wave of Opposition to the Invasion of Vietnam. New Year's Day: Plane Drops Large Numbers of Anti-Vietnam War Leaflets Over Naval Base. Philadelphia Public Holds Demonstrations. Workers at a Munitions Company Continue Strike.
Author:
Date: 1966-01-04
Edition: 4

    Xinhua News Agency, Washington, January 3rd: On New Year's Eve, a light aircraft dropped a large number of leaflets over the Long Beach Naval Base near Los Angeles, protesting the American war of aggression against Vietnam. According to the United Press International, the Long Beach Naval authorities were very annoyed by this action of the American people against the Johnson administration's policy of aggression.
    This is the first anti-war action taken by the American people this year, and it is also a new form of protest for them.
    The plane also dropped hundreds of leaflets over Disneyland amusement park near Los Angeles, condemning the United States' barbaric crimes in Vietnam.
    It is reported that in Des Moines, Iowa, about seventy demonstrators (mainly students from universities and middle schools) have been holding an "indefinite hunger strike" since New Year's Eve to protest the American aggression against Vietnam. According to the leader of the hunger strike, announced on the 2nd, this struggle will continue indefinitely.
    In Wisconsin, the "National Coordinating Committee to End the War in Vietnam" issued a press release on New Year's Day, calling on the public to launch another "March on Washington" after Congress reconvenes on the 10th of this month, and hold a camping demonstration next to Congress, to express strong opposition to the "escalation" of the American war of aggression against Vietnam. The communiqué requires the public to pre-organize convoys and raise enough travel expenses so that they can reach Washington as quickly as possible once they receive the "marching" call.
    This committee was the organizer of the unprecedented "National Protest Day" demonstration against American aggression against Vietnam in October last year. The communiqué emphasized that if the United States further expands the Vietnam War, people should be prepared to hold protests and demonstrations of the same scale.
    Xinhua News Agency, Philadelphia: 150 American citizens demonstrated in front of a helicopter factory in Morton, a suburb of Philadelphia, on December 29th, protesting the American war of aggression against Vietnam, and demanding that the factory stop producing military helicopters.
    This factory produces the CH-47A "Chinook" helicopters used by the American aggressor troops on the South Vietnamese battlefield.
    The authorities dispatched nearly two hundred policemen to suppress the demonstrators, and hired a group of hooligans to disrupt the demonstration. However, many demonstrators still tried to break into the factory to demonstrate. At least sixteen demonstrators were arrested by the police, including Thomas Rodd, a 19-year-old who had been sentenced for refusing to register for the draft, and Edward Gottlieb, the principal of a New York public school.
    Xinhua News Agency, New York: The strike by more than 4,000 workers at the Olin Mathieson Chemical Corporation entered its twenty-eighth day on December 28th of last year.
    It is reported that the gunpowder and bullets used by the American government for the Vietnam War mainly come from a Mathieson company factory in Alton, Illinois. This factory is also the main supplier of gunpowder to other ammunition manufacturing companies in the United States.
    The American government is creating excuses to persecute the strikers. One government official said that the Department of Defense considers the strike to be "extremely dangerous." The military also issued a statement saying that unless the factory resumes production immediately, the United States will face "serious ammunition supply problems" in Vietnam.
    The strike was mainly triggered by wage issues. The workers also protested against poor working conditions and unreasonable labor regulations stipulated by the capitalists. As soon as the strike began, the American government sent "federal mediators" to interfere in the strike. (Attached picture)
    The picture shows demonstrators in Berkeley, USA, bravely blocking a train full of American troops invading Vietnam, despite police suppression. (Published by Xinhua News Agency)

### 美国人民掀起反对侵越新浪潮  新年用飞机在海军基地上空投下大量抗议侵越传单  费城公众举行示威一家军火公司的工人持续罢工

1966-01-04
第4版()
专栏：

    美国人民掀起反对侵越新浪潮
    新年用飞机在海军基地上空投下大量抗议侵越传单
    费城公众举行示威一家军火公司的工人持续罢工
    新华社三日讯华盛顿消息：元旦晚上，一架轻型飞机在洛杉矶附近的长滩海军基地上空投下了大批传单，抗议美国侵略越南战争。据合众国际社报道，长滩海军当局对美国人民反对约翰逊政府侵略政策的这个行动感到非常恼火。
    这是美国人民今年采取的第一个反战行动，也是他们的一个新的抗议形式。
    这架飞机还向洛杉矶近郊的迪斯尼兰娱乐公园投了数以百计的传单，谴责美国在越南的野蛮罪行。
    据报道，在衣阿华州的得梅因，大约七十名示威者（其中主要是大中学校的学生）自去年除夕起开始举行“不定期绝食”斗争，抗议美国侵略越南。据绝食运动的领导人二日宣布，这一斗争将不定期地继续下去。
    在威斯康星州，“结束越南战争全国协调委员会”在元旦发表一项新闻公报，号召公众在本月十日国会复会后，再次发动“向华盛顿进军”并在国会旁边举行宿营示威，以表示强烈反对美国侵略越南战争“不断升级”。公报要求公众预先组织好车队和筹足路费，以便一旦接到“进军”号召时，就可以最迅速地到达华盛顿。
    这个委员会是去年十月间空前规模的反对美国侵略越南“全国抗议日”示威活动的组织者。公报强调，如果美国进一步扩大越南战争，人们应当准备举行同样规模的抗议示威。
    新华社讯　费城消息：美国公众一百五十人去年十二月二十九日在费城近郊区摩顿的一家制造直升飞机工厂的门口举行示威，反对美国侵略越南战争，并且要求这家工厂停止生产军事直升飞机。
    这家工厂是生产美国侵略军在南越战场上使用的CH—47A“支奴克式”直升飞机的。
    当局出动了近两百名警察进行镇压，并且雇用了一批流氓进行捣乱。但是，许多示威者仍然试图冲进工厂去进行示威。至少有十六名示威群众被警察逮捕，其中包括曾因拒绝征兵登记而被判过刑的十九岁青年托马斯·罗德和纽约公立学校的校长爱德华·戈德里布。
    新华社讯　纽约消息：美国奥林·马锡森化学公司四千多名工人的罢工，在去年十二月二十八日进入了第二十八天。
    据报道，美国政府用于侵越战争的火药和子弹，主要来自马锡森公司在伊利诺斯州阿尔敦的一个工厂。这家工厂还是美国其它制造弹药公司的火药的主要供应者。
    美国政府正在为了对罢工者进行迫害制造借口。一个政府官员说，国防部认为这次罢工是“极其危险的”。军方也发表声明说：除非这个厂立即恢复生产，否则美国会在越南出现“严重的弹药供应问题”。
    这次罢工主要是由于工资问题而爆发的。工人们还抗议劳动条件不好和资方规定的劳动规章不合理。罢工一开始，美国政府就派出“联邦调解人员”对罢工进行干涉。（附图片）
