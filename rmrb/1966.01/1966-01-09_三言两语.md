    Brief Comments
    U.S. Republican Senator Dirksen said that the United States must "win" on the South Vietnam battlefield because "there is nothing to replace victory."
    To "win" on the South Vietnam battlefield, of course, is not just Dirksen's wish among the American ruling class. From Kennedy to Johnson, they have always fantasized about this. However, this kind of "victory" is not only unattainable but is becoming increasingly elusive. Now, the likes of Dirksen should discover that in South Vietnam, it is not that "there is nothing to replace victory," but that only failure can replace "victory."

    British Foreign Secretary Stewart, while visiting Peru, vigorously expressed Britain's desire to break into Latin America. He hoped that Latin American countries would not think that their "only connection to the world is through the United States."
    However, this gentleman from Downing Street hurriedly changed his tune, saying that Britain had no intention of "trying to weaken the United States' influence in Latin America."
    Wanting to get a piece of the pie in the United States' "backyard" while also being afraid of offending the United States, he truly reveals the pitiful appearance of a lackey.

### 三言两语

1966-01-09
第3版()
专栏：三言两语

    三言两语
    美国共和党参议员德克森说，美国在南越战场上必须“胜利”，因为“没有什么东西可以替代胜利”。
    在南越战场上“胜利”，这在美国统治集团中间，当然不仅是德克森一个人的想望而已。从肯尼迪到约翰逊，一直是这样幻想的。可是，这种“胜利”不但到不了手，反而越加渺茫了。现在，德克森之流应该发现的倒是，在南越不是“没有什么东西可以替代胜利”，而是只有用失败来替代“胜利”了。

    英国外交大臣斯图尔特在秘鲁访问时，竭力表示英国想要打入拉丁美洲。他希望拉丁美洲各国不要认为，它们“同世界的唯一联系是通过美国”。
    但是，这个唐宁街的绅士又连忙心虚地改口说，英国并没有“努力削弱美国在拉丁美洲的影响”的打算。
    既想插足美国“后院”分一杯羹，又怕得罪了美国，真是活现出一副小伙计的可怜相。
