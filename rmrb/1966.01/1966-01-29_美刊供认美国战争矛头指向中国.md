Title: U.S. Magazine Admits U.S. War's Spearhead Aimed at China
Author:
Date: 1966-01-29
Edition: 4

    American Magazine Admits U.S. War's Spearhead Aimed at China
    The American magazine *Newsweek*, in its January 31st issue (published in advance), carried an article titled "Encirclement Around Asia: Making Beijing Take Notice." The following is an excerpt:
    Today, the United States has more troops stationed in the Pacific than in Europe. The eastern edge of Asia is currently the site of the largest U.S. military buildup since World War II. From Thailand to Korea, bulldozers and gas drills, rollers and steam rollers, earthmovers and stone crushers are rumbling late into the night, restoring old, overgrown small airfields, opening up new ones, and establishing ports along the jungle-covered coast.
    This year alone, the Pentagon plans to spend $1.4 billion to build and improve bases in Asia. Of course, this massive effort is a direct result of the Vietnam War, but its impact extends far beyond Vietnam.
    In the eyes of the United States, the focus of world tension has shifted from Europe to Asia in the past few years. The United States' declared policy is to expand the U.S. base circle in Asia, making Beijing aware that the United States is prepared to defend what it considers its interests in the region, no matter where or how those interests are threatened.
    The key area for the U.S. buildup in the Pacific is Okinawa. In recent months, personnel, machines, and supplies have been pouring into this 70-mile-long island at an astonishing rate. As a result of this activity, Okinawa has become an important intermediate stop for the U.S. deployment of forces in the Pacific. There are ship anchorages, three modern runways crammed with jet aircraft, underground nuclear storage facilities, and at least four Mace-B missile launch sites.
    Japan is no less important than Okinawa. The United States has more than 150 military facilities there, including five first-class airfields and the massive Yokosuka Naval Base. With the best maintenance equipment, Yokosuka is almost indispensable to the Seventh Fleet.
    The U.S. base in Guam, with more than 30 long-range B-52 bombers and four Polaris submarines loaded with missiles, is undoubtedly aimed at Red China. The Seventh Fleet, composed of 150 ships of all types, can provide approximately 75 long-range jet bombers for any showdown in Asia. U.S. military facilities in Korea, the Philippines (where a new jet aircraft airfield has been built), and Taiwan (where an emergency program is underway to repair the 12,000-foot runway at Kungkuan Airport) are also under construction.

### 美刊供认美国战争矛头指向中国

1966-01-29
第4版()
专栏：外论摘要

    美刊供认美国战争矛头指向中国
    一月三十一日（提前出版）的美国《新闻周刊》刊登一篇题为《在亚洲周围的包围圈：促使北京注意》的文章，摘要如下：
    今天美国驻在太平洋的军队比驻在欧洲的军队还要多，目前亚洲东部边缘是美国进行第二次世界大战以来最大一次军事集结的场所。从泰国到朝鲜，挖泥机和气体钻孔机、压路机和蒸气碾路机、运土机和碎石机轧轧地响到深夜，在恢复旧的长满了草的小型机场、开辟出新的机场以及在丛林覆盖的海岸沿线建立起港口。
    仅仅今年一年，五角大楼就计划花十四亿美元来修建和改进亚洲基地。当然，这个巨大的努力是越南战争所引起的一个直接的结果，但是它的影响却远远超出了越南的境界。
    在美国看来，世界紧张的焦点在过去几年内已从欧洲转移到亚洲，美国公开的政策是要扩大美国在亚洲的基地圈，使北京注意到，美国准备保卫它认为是美国在这个地区的利益，不管这种利益在什么地方受到威胁以及如何受到威胁。
    美国在太平洋进行集结的关键地区是冲绳。最近几个月来，人员、机器和供应品以惊人的速度倾注入这个七十英里长的岛屿。由于这种活动，冲绳已经成为美国部署太平洋力量的重要的中间停留地区。那里有舰只停泊处，三条挤满了喷气式飞机的现代化跑道，地下核储存设备和至少有四个马斯—B导弹发射场。
    日本的重要性不下于冲绳，在那里美国设有一百五十多个军事设施，其中包括五个一级机场和庞大的横须贺海军基地。具有最优良的维修设备的横须贺对第七舰队来说几乎是必不可少的。
    美国在关岛的基地三十多架远程B—52轰炸机和四艘装满导弹的北极星潜艇，毫无疑问它们的矛头都是指向赤色中国的。由一百五十艘各式舰只组成的第七舰队可以为在亚洲的任何摊牌提供大约七十五架远程喷气轰炸机。美国在朝鲜、菲律宾（那里新建了一个喷气式飞机的机场）和台湾（那里正在进行一项紧急计划以修复公馆机场长达一万二千英尺的跑道）的军事设施也都在建设中。
