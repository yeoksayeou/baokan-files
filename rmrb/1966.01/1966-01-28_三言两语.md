    Brief Comments
    Why Study It?
    The *Washington Evening Star* revealed that the U.S. government is studying a problem: Why was the "gradual escalation strategy" "very effective during the Cuban Missile Crisis" but has "consistently been disappointing" when applied to Vietnam?
    The U.S.'s war blackmail can only scare the Khrushchev revisionists suffering from osteomalacia. Of course, it cannot work against the Vietnamese people with backbones of steel. The answer to the question is so simple, why do the likes of Johnson need to study it further!
    The Law of Aggression
    U.S. Secretary of State Rusk said in Congress: "We know from painful experience that the appetite for aggression grows with aggression." Rusk said this to attack others and cover up for the American aggressors themselves, but it serves perfectly as an admission by U.S. imperialism.
    For the U.S. ruling group, such a law of aggression does not require any "painful experience" to understand. What they should understand from "painful experience" is another law: the bigger the appetite for aggression, the more miserable the failure.

### 三言两语

1966-01-28
第3版()
专栏：三言两语

    三言两语
    何需研究
    《华盛顿明星晚报》透露，美国政府正在研究一个问题：为什么“逐步升级战略”“在古巴导弹危机时非常灵验”，而运用到越南后却“一直令人失望”呢？
    美国的战争讹诈，只能吓倒患软骨病的赫鲁晓夫修正主义者，用来对付硬骨头的越南人民当然不能奏效。问题的答案就是这样简单，约翰逊之流何需再去研究呢！
    侵略规律
    美国国务卿腊斯克在国会中说：“我们根据痛苦的经验知道，侵略的胃口是越侵略越大”。腊斯克说这样的话，是要攻击别人而为美国侵略者自己掩饰的，但是却正好作为美帝国主义的自供。
    对于美国统治集团来说，这样一条侵略的规律，是根本用不着什么“痛苦的经验”才知道的。他们根据“痛苦的经验”应当懂得的倒是另外一个规律：侵略的胃越大就失败得越惨。
