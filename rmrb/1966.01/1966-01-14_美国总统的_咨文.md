Title: The U.S. President's "Message"
Author:
Date: 1966-01-14
Edition: 4

    American Constitution stipulates that the American President should provide the Congress with information on the state of the country at any time, and put forward relevant reports and recommendations. Such reports or recommendations, whether the President reads them in person in Congress or sends them to Congress in writing, are called "Messages."
    The U.S. President's "Messages" are of many kinds, such as the "State of the Union Message," the "Economic Message," the "Budget Message," and special messages on many specific issues.
    The "State of the Union Message" is usually presented at the beginning of each year when Congress opens, and its content includes domestic and foreign situations and measures that the government considers should be taken. It is an important document reflecting U.S. policy guidelines. Although the "State of the Union Message" cannot produce any legal consequences in form, it is, in fact, a tool for the U.S. President to manipulate or carry out legislative activities.

### 美国总统的“咨文”

1966-01-14
第4版()
专栏：名词解释

    美国总统的“咨文”
    根据美国宪法的规定，美国总统应随时向国会提供国家的情况，提出有关的报告和建议。这种报告或建议，不论是总统亲自在国会宣读或用书面的方式送到国会，都叫作“咨文”。
    美国总统的“咨文”有许多种，如“国情咨文”、“经济咨文”、“预算咨文”，以及就许多具体问题提出的特别咨文。
    “国情咨文”通常在每年年初国会开幕时提出，内容包括国内外情况和政府认为应当采取的措施。它是反映美国政策方针的重要文件。“国情咨文”在形式上虽然不能产生法律上的任何后果，但是，实际上是美国总统操纵或进行立法活动的工具。
