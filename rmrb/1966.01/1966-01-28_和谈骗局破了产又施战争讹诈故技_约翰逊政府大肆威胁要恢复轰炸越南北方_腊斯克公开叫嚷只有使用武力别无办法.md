Title: The Peace Talk Swindle Has Gone Bankrupt, and the Old Trick of War Blackmail is Being Used Again: The Johnson Government is Threatening to Resume Bombing North Vietnam on a Large Scale; Rusk Publicly Cries That There is No Other Way Than to Use Force
Author: 
Date: 1966-01-28
Edition: 3

　　Xinhua News Agency, Washington, January 27th - News: With the "peace talks" swindle going bankrupt, the Johnson government and the American propaganda machine have recently attempted to use the resumption of bombing North Vietnam to carry out war blackmail against the Vietnamese people.
　　U.S. Secretary of State Rusk, while testifying before the House Foreign Affairs Committee on the 26th, blatantly threatened that the United States' "peace efforts" have "gone from A to Z (note: A and Z are the first and last letters in the English alphabet), and have almost completed Z."
　　He said that the United States, while helping to repel so-called "Communist aggression," is "constantly striving for a peaceful settlement," but the United States' "peace initiatives" have not received "positive or encouraging responses." He clamored, "There is no other way than to meet force with force."
　　On the same day, White House spokesman Moyers, in response to a question about whether the United States was about to resume bombing, claimed that Johnson "is considering all factors related to our position in Southeast Asia and is assessing and calculating these factors."
　　Former U.S. "Ambassador" to South Vietnam, now Special Advisor to the U.S. President, Taylor, publicly clamored at a press conference on the 26th that there was no reason to continue the bombing halt. He said: "We have demonstrated the sincerity of our peaceful intentions to friends and enemies alike. But there is no reason to continue this way." He clamored that the United States "must maintain this (bombing) pressure."
　　The day before, Johnson convened leaders of both parties in Congress to discuss the Vietnam issue. According to United Press International's chief Washington correspondent Hansley, Johnson presented "a gloomy report on the failure of his peace offensive and the increasing dangers faced by U.S. troops." Associated Press's chief Washington correspondent Hightower reported that "the President, Rusk, and other government leaders gave Congressional leaders the impression at a White House meeting on Tuesday that, given military considerations, they see no other option than to resume bombing."
　　Hightower also said that "Johnson, by sending messages to foreign governments and holding discussions with Congressional leaders, is apparently seeking to obtain the broadest possible support and to form the broadest possible consensus to resume the aerial operations that have been suspended." The U.S. State Department announced on the 25th that the United States had sent messages to "many" countries, informing them of the "situation in Vietnam." It was reported that the United States notified these countries that "it cannot continue the halt in bombing North Vietnam indefinitely."

### 和谈骗局破了产又施战争讹诈故技  约翰逊政府大肆威胁要恢复轰炸越南北方  腊斯克公开叫嚷只有使用武力别无办法

1966-01-28
第3版()
专栏：

    和谈骗局破了产又施战争讹诈故技
    约翰逊政府大肆威胁要恢复轰炸越南北方
    腊斯克公开叫嚷只有使用武力别无办法
    新华社二十七日讯　华盛顿消息：在“和谈”骗局破产的情况下，约翰逊政府和美国宣传机器最近又妄图以恢复对越南北方的轰炸来对越南人民进行战争讹诈。
    美国国务卿腊斯克二十六日在众议院外交委员会作证时，露骨地威胁说，美国的“和平努力”已经“从A进行到了Z（按：A和Z是英文字母中的第一个字和最末一个字），而且几乎已经走完了Z。”
    他说，美国一面在帮助击退所谓“共产党侵略”，一面在“不断努力寻求和平解决”，但是，美国的“和平主动行动”并未得到“积极的或令人鼓舞的答复”。他叫嚷，“只有以武力对付武力，此外没有别的办法”。
    同一天，白宫发言人莫耶斯在答复有关美国是否即将恢复轰炸的问题时声称：约翰逊“正在斟酌同我们在东南亚的立场有关的一切因素，正在估计和盘算这些因素”。
    前美国驻南越“大使”、现任美国总统特别顾问泰勒二十六日在记者招待会上公开叫嚣，继续停止轰炸的理由没有了。他说：“我们已经向朋友和敌人表明了对我们的和平目的的诚意。但是，继续这样作的理由没有了。”他叫嚷美国“必须保持这种（轰炸）压力”。
    在这前一天，约翰逊召集两党国会领袖讨论了越南问题。据合众国际社驻华盛顿首席记者汉斯莱报道，约翰逊在这次会上提出了“一个阴郁的报告，谈到他的和平攻势的失败，以及美军面临的越来越大的危险。”美联社驻华盛顿首席记者海托华报道说，“总统、腊斯克和政府其他领导人在星期二白宫一次会议上给国会领袖们这样一种印象：鉴于军事上的考虑，除了恢复轰炸以外，他们看不出有什么别的办法。”
    海托华还说，“约翰逊利用向外国政府发出信息和同国会领袖进行讨论的办法，显然是想要取得尽可能广泛的支持，并形成尽可能广泛的一致意见，来恢复已经停下来的空中行动。”美国国务院在二十五日宣布，美国已经向“许多”国家发出信息，把“越南局势”通知它们。据报道，美国通知这些国家，“它不能无限期地继续停止轰炸北越”。
