Title: Soviet Union and India Sign New Agreement to Expand Trade
Author: 
Date: 1966-01-13
Edition: 4

    Soviet Union and India Sign New Agreement to Expand Trade
    Xinhua News Agency, New Delhi, 10th - India and the Soviet Union signed a long-term trade agreement for the period 1966-1970 in New Delhi on the 7th. According to this agreement, Indo-Soviet trade will increase to 3 billion rupees by 1970, nearly doubling the 1964 figure. Indian Commerce Minister Shah said with endless gratitude at the signing ceremony: "No country has increased its trade volume with India to this extent in such a short period as the Soviet Union." According to a TASS dispatch from New Delhi, twelve years ago, the trade volume between the Soviet Union and India was 10 million rupees; in 1964 it reached 1.5 billion rupees; this new trade agreement will again multiply the Indo-Soviet trade volume in a few years.

### 苏联印度签订扩大贸易新协定

1966-01-13
第4版()
专栏：

    苏联印度签订扩大贸易新协定
    新华社十日讯　新德里消息：印度和苏联七日在新德里签订了一项一九六六——一九七○年的长期贸易协定。根据这项协定，印苏贸易到一九七○年将增加到三十亿卢比，比一九六四年增加将近一倍。印度商业部长沙阿在签字仪式上感激不尽地说：“没有一个国家象苏联那样，在这么短的时期内把同印度的贸易额增加到这个程度”。据塔斯社从新德里发出的消息说，在十二年以前，苏联和印度的贸易额为一千万卢比，在一九六四年达到了十五亿卢比，这个新的贸易协定又将使印苏贸易额在几年当中有成倍的增长。
