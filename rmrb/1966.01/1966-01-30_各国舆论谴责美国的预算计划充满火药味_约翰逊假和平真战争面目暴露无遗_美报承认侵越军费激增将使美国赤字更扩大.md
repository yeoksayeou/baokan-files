Title: International Opinion Condemns US Budget Plan as Full of Gunpowder: Johnson's False Peace, True War Face Fully Exposed; US Media Admits Increased Vietnam War Spending Will Further Expand US Deficit
Author:
Date: 1966-01-30
Edition: 4

    According to Xinhua News Agency, Tirana, 27th - The Albanian newspaper "Bashkimi" published a commentary on the 26th, stating that the US government's 1967 budget plan is full of gunpowder and is a budget for war and aggression.
    The commentary pointed out that from the US government's budget message, it can be seen that US imperialism is plotting to expand the war of aggression to the entire Indochina. At the same time, US imperialism is also preparing to strengthen its aggression against the whole world.
    The commentary said that countless facts clearly show that US imperialism is the main stronghold of new and old colonialism, the main force of war and aggression, and the most vicious and cunning aggressor and war criminal of mankind.
    Xinhua News Agency, Rawalpindi, 28th - The "Pakistan Times" published a dispatch from its correspondent in New York on the 27th, stating: "President Johnson's unprecedentedly huge 1966-1967 budget is essentially a war budget."
    According to President Johnson's budget proposal for the next fiscal year, out of a total expenditure of $112.8 billion, $60.5 billion is directly used for military spending. The dispatch said: "In addition to providing substantial military and economic assistance to the tottering Saigon regime, Washington will spend $10.3 billion on its own troops fighting in Vietnam – nearly one-fifth of the total defense spending."
    Xinhua News Agency, Mexico City, 27th - The Mexican newspaper "El Día" published a commentary on the 27th on President Johnson's budget message on the 24th, pointing out that the budget message exposed the US government's conspiracy of playing false peace and waging true war.
    The commentary said, "It is difficult to talk about peace when presenting a war budget as high as $60.5 billion." The commentary said, "This huge amount, accounting for 53 percent of the US budget for this year, greatly stimulates the US war industry." The commentary asked, "Can the Johnson administration still talk about peace when it spends such a sum of money on the military?"
    Xinhua News Agency, 29th - Washington News: American newspapers and members of Congress criticized President Johnson's proposed 1967 fiscal year budget as being deceptive and misleading to the public.
    The "Wall Street Journal" wrote in an editorial titled "Illusory Figures" on the 25th, "The manipulation is particularly evident in the revenue estimates. Even with an increase of as much as $6.4 billion in estimated expenditures for the 1967 fiscal year, the projected revenue is so large that the deficit is only $1.8 billion. To prevent the deficit from being even larger, taxes will increase by $11 billion. How is this achieved? In part, by canceling new cuts in excise taxes, implementing a progressive withholding system, and accelerating the collection of corporate income taxes. Relying on this approach indicates that budget makers must be in a desperate mood. But the bulk of the increase in revenue will depend on further economic growth, and if the economy remains at its current... level, the result will be a huge deficit, and if the economy declines slightly, the deficit will be catastrophic. Therefore, the budget leaves no room for maneuver for what is entirely possible to happen in the next eighteen months." The "Washington Evening Star" editorial on the same day also pointed out that in Johnson's new budget, "the biggest unknown is the Vietnam issue"; "all indications suggest that (the Vietnam War) spending will far exceed his budget blueprint."
    The editorial continued, "In the past five months, the government has already asked Congress for an additional $14 billion to conduct this war. If the war intensifies further, and if military spending accelerates, tax increases or expanded deficits will be inevitable."
    The "New York Times" pointed out in an editorial on the 26th, "The estimates in the government's new budget appear to contain a greater-than-usual amount of wishful thinking, particularly in estimating Vietnam spending and the threat of inflation." (Attached picture)

### 各国舆论谴责美国的预算计划充满火药味  约翰逊假和平真战争面目暴露无遗  美报承认侵越军费激增将使美国赤字更扩大

1966-01-30
第4版()
专栏：

    各国舆论谴责美国的预算计划充满火药味
    约翰逊假和平真战争面目暴露无遗
    美报承认侵越军费激增将使美国赤字更扩大
    据新华社地拉那二十七日电  阿尔巴尼亚《团结报》二十六日发表评论说，美国政府一九六七年度的预算计划充满了火药味，是战争和侵略的预算。
    评论指出，从美国政府的预算咨文中可以看出，美帝国主义阴谋把侵略战争扩大到整个印度支那。同时，美帝国主义也在为加强对全世界的侵略进行准备。
    评论说，无数的事实清楚表明，美帝国主义是新老殖民主义的主要堡垒，是战争和侵略的主要力量，是人类最凶恶、最狡猾的侵略者和战争罪犯。
    新华社拉瓦尔品第二十八日电　《巴基斯坦时报》二十七日登载了这家报纸的记者从纽约发出的一条消息指出：“约翰逊总统空前巨大的一九六六——一九六七年预算实质上是一个战争预算。”
    根据美国总统约翰逊关于下一财政年度的预算的建议，在一千一百二十八亿美元的总开支中，有六百零五亿美元直接用于军费开支。消息说：“除了给摇摇欲坠的西贡政权大量军事和经济援助外，华盛顿将为它自己在越南作战的部队支出一百零三亿美元——这将近占防务开支总数的五分之一。”
    据新华社墨西哥城二十七日电　墨西哥《日报》二十七日就美国总统约翰逊二十四日提出的预算咨文发表评论指出，这个预算咨文暴露了美国政府玩弄的假和平、真战争的阴谋。
    评论说，“当提出一个高达六百零五亿美元的战争预算的时候，就很难再谈论和平。”评论说，“这笔占美国本年度预算总额百分之五十三的庞大的款项大大刺激了美国的战争工业。”评论问道，约翰逊政府“把这样一笔钱用在军事上，还能谈论和平吗？”
    据新华社二十九日讯　华盛顿消息：美国报刊和国会议员抨击美国总统约翰逊提出的一九六七财政年度预算是在弄虚作假、欺骗公众。
    《华尔街日报》二十五日在一篇题为《幻想的数字》的社论中写道，“弄虚作假的手法在收入估计方面特别明显。即使一九六七财政年度的开支估计增加六十四亿美元之多，预计的收入竟大得使赤字只有十八亿美元。为了使赤字不致更大，税收将增加一百十亿美元。这怎么实现呢？一部分是靠取消在消费税方面新的削减，实行递增预扣制度，并且加速征收公司所得税。依靠这种办法就表明预算制订者必然处于绝望气氛中。但是收入增加的大部分要靠经济进一步增长，如果经济保持在目前……水平上，结果将是巨大的赤字，如果经济稍为下降，赤字将会泛滥成灾。因此，预算对于在今后十八个月内完全可能发生的情况丝毫没有留下转圜余地。”同一天《华盛顿明星晚报》的社论也指出，在约翰逊的新预算中，“最大的未知数是越南问题”；“种种迹象表明，（越南战争的）开支将远远超出他的预算蓝图”。
    社论接着写道，“在过去五个月里，政府已经要求国会追加一百四十亿美元来进行这场战争。假若战争进一步激化，假若军费的开支加快的话，捐税增加或是赤字扩大将是不可避免的。”
    《纽约时报》在二十六日的社论中指出，“政府新预算中的估计数字看来包含着超出通常数量的大量一厢情愿的想法，特别在估计越南开支和通货膨胀威胁时是如此”。（附图片）
