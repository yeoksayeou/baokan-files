Title: Cambodian Foreign Ministry Issues Statement Opposing Expansion of Powers of International Commission; International Commission Personnel Invited to Inspect Sites of Thai Military Invasion of Cambodia, Witnessing Aggression Relics Firsthand
Author: 
Date: 1966-01-31
Edition: 5

    Xinhua News Agency, Phnom Penh, January 29th (Electric Dispatch) - According to the Cambodian News Agency, the Cambodian Royal Government's Foreign Ministry issued a statement on January 25th, expressing opposition to expanding the powers of the International Commission for Supervision and Control in Cambodia. The statement said that the International Commission should under no circumstances possess the mission of "protecting" the borders of the Kingdom of Cambodia.
    The Cambodian Foreign Ministry's statement was issued in response to remarks made by Canadian Foreign Minister Paul Martin at a press conference held in Ottawa on January 12th.
    The statement pointed out that Martin stated at the meeting that it seemed regrettable that the International Commission had no means to guarantee the "protection" of the Cambodian border.
    The statement pointed out that the Cambodian Royal Government hopes to see the role played by the International Commission in Cambodia, but the methods the commission can take are only those stipulated in the supplementary documents to the 1954 Geneva Agreements. Therefore, it seems that it is sufficient as long as the International Commission strictly implements the already stipulated supervision methods.
  Xinhua News Agency, Phnom Penh, January 30th (Electric Dispatch) The personnel of the International Commission for Supervision and Control in Cambodia, military attachés from various countries' embassies in Cambodia and news commissioners, foreign journalists, and some local journalists, on the 29th, at the Chineasa local border outpost and Bai Nangchangyang Village in Koh Kong Province, Cambodia, personally witnessed the traces left by the Thai military's invasion of Cambodia.
    They were invited by the Cambodian Ministry of Information to inspect these places. They saw the places where Thai military shells and bullets had struck, and saw part of the Chineasa outpost damaged.

### 柬埔寨外交部发表声明  反对扩大柬埔寨国际委员会权力  国际委员会人员等应邀视察泰军侵柬现场亲眼目睹侵略遗迹

1966-01-31
第5版()
专栏：

    柬埔寨外交部发表声明
    反对扩大柬埔寨国际委员会权力
    国际委员会人员等应邀视察泰军侵柬现场亲眼目睹侵略遗迹
    新华社金边二十九日电  据柬埔寨新闻社报道：柬埔寨王国政府外交部二十五日发表声明，表示反对扩大柬埔寨国际监察和监督委员会的权力。声明说，国际委员会无论如何不能拥有“保护”柬埔寨王国边界的使命。
    柬埔寨外交部的这项声明是针对加拿大外交部长保罗·马丁一月十二日在渥太华举行的一次记者招待会上的讲话而发表的。
    声明指出，马丁在会上表示，国际委员会一点也没有能够保证“保护”柬埔寨边界的手段，似乎是令人遗憾的。
    声明指出，柬埔寨王国政府希望看到国际委员会在柬埔寨所起的作用，但是委员会能够采取的方法只能是一九五四年日内瓦协议附属文件规定的方法。因此，看来只要国际委员会严格执行已经规定的监督办法，这就足够了。
  据新华社金边三十日电　柬埔寨国际监察和监督委员会的人员、各国驻柬埔寨大使馆武官和新闻专员、外国记者以及部分当地记者，二十九日在柬埔寨戈公省奇内萨地方边防哨所和百囊昌扬村亲眼看到了泰国军队侵犯柬埔寨留下的痕迹。
    他们是应柬埔寨新闻部的邀请去这些地方视察的。他们看到了泰国军队的炮弹和枪弹打过的地方，看到奇内萨哨所一部分被打坏了。
