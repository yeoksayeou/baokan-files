Title: The United States is Eager to Drag Western European Allies into the Water; Cleveland Demands They Undertake Obligations in the War of Aggression Against Vietnam

Author:

Date: 1966-01-22
Edition: 3

    The United States is Eager to Drag Western European Allies into the Water
    Cleveland Demands They Undertake Obligations in the War of Aggression Against Vietnam
    (Xinhua News Agency, Paris, 20th) United States Representative to NATO, Cleveland, pressured the U.S.'s European allies on the 20th, demanding they "participate to the maximum extent" in the Vietnam War.

    At a luncheon held here by the Anglo-American Press Association, Cleveland complained: "The United States feels a little lonely in those foreign regions (Vietnam). The United States is increasingly perplexed as to why Europe, which is now so strong and prosperous, has not participated in an important, fiercely fought front in our global confrontation with the Communists."

    Cleveland wanted the U.S.'s European allies to clearly express their attitude on the Vietnam issue. He said: "We hope that Europeans will recognize that a new strategy of drawing a line and preventing the Communists from crossing this line to carry out their indirect infiltration is very important to all of us."

    As for the "form and extent of assuming obligations," Cleveland had to say that this was a matter to be decided by the allied governments. On the other hand, he made no secret of the fact that the United States welcomes any assistance there (according to guides, South Vietnam), military or otherwise.

    In his speech, Cleveland also hoped to develop NATO from "a credible military deterrent force into a credible (political reconciliation with the Soviet Union)." He also called on the Western world to "continue to strive to reach a disarmament agreement with the Soviet Union." He said the United States would "patiently wait" for possible "changes" when the seventeen-nation disarmament conference in Geneva resumes on January 27.

### 美国急于拖西欧盟国下水  克利夫兰又要它们在侵越战争中承担义务

1966-01-22
第3版()
专栏：

    美国急于拖西欧盟国下水
    克利夫兰又要它们在侵越战争中承担义务
    新华社巴黎二十日电  美国驻北大西洋公约组织代表克利夫兰二十日向美国的欧洲盟国施加压力，要它们“最大限度地参预”越南战争。
    克利夫兰在这里的英美记者协会举办的一次午餐会上抱怨说：“美国在那些国外地区（越南）感到有点孤单。美国感到越来越不解的是，如今很强大和繁荣的欧洲怎么没有参加在我们同共产党人的全球大对峙中的一条重要的、斗争激烈的战线。”
    克利夫兰要美国的欧洲盟国在越南问题上明确表示态度。他说：“我们希望欧洲人将会认识到，划一条线、不让共产党人超越这一条线来实行他们的间接渗透的新战略，这对我们大家来说是非常重要的。”
    至于“承担义务的形式和程度”，克利夫兰一方面不得不说这是应该由盟国政府来决定的事情，另一方面，他却毫不讳言，美国欢迎对那里（按指南越）的任何援助，军事的或其他方面的援助。
    克利夫兰在讲话中还希望把北大西洋公约组织从“一个真实可信的军事威慑力量发展成为真实可信的（同苏联的）政治和解”。他还要求西方世界
  “继续努力同苏联达成裁军协议”。他说，美国将“耐心地等待”一月二十七日日内瓦十七国裁军会议复会时可能发生的“变化”。
