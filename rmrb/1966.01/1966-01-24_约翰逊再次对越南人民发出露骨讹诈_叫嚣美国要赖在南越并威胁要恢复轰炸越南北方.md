Title: Johnson Again Issues Blatant Blackmail Against the Vietnamese People, Clamoring that the U.S. Will Remain in South Vietnam and Threatening to Resume Bombing North Vietnam
Author:
Date: 1966-01-24
Edition: 3

    According to Xinhua News Agency, Washington, January 23rd: U.S. President Johnson openly stated again on the 22nd that the U.S. will insist on remaining in South Vietnam, and threatened to resume bombing North Vietnam, engaging in blatant blackmail against the Vietnamese people.

    This is the second time in three days that Johnson has issued such blackmailing rhetoric. Similar to his speech on the 20th in Independence, Missouri, U.S., Johnson, in a letter to a group of congressmen on the 22nd, continued to feign a peaceful posture, saying: "Our current cessation of bombing North Vietnam has only received hostile reactions, but you can be assured that we will continue to relentlessly pursue efforts for peace." On the other hand, he emphasized that the U.S. is "determined to maintain and support our combat troops in South Vietnam," and blatantly threatened: "For a month, we have not taken action in an important area of military operations (referring to the bombing of North Vietnam). But the aggressor's troops continue to infiltrate, and they continue to attack our allies and our own troops. I believe you will agree that we have a grave obligation not to lightly increase the dangers that our troops must face."

    Seventy-six members of Johnson's own Democratic Party had jointly written to Johnson on the 21st, expressing their doubts and unease about the government's intensified preparations to expand its war adventure. Johnson's letter was a response to these congressmen.

    Senator Russell, Chairman of the Senate Armed Services Committee and a Democrat, told reporters after hearing McNamara testify on the Vietnam issue on the 21st that McNamara's testimony convinced him that Washington would soon order the resumption of air strikes if no significant progress was made towards a "peaceful solution."

    Another Democratic Senator "revealed" after the meeting that, according to the testimony of McNamara and Wheeler, the U.S. military in South Vietnam may increase to 400,000 by October or November. (Attached picture)

    On January 18, the 25th Infantry Division of the invading U.S. forces landed in Vung Tau.

### 约翰逊再次对越南人民发出露骨讹诈  叫嚣美国要赖在南越并威胁要恢复轰炸越南北方

1966-01-24
第3版()
专栏：

    约翰逊再次对越南人民发出露骨讹诈
    叫嚣美国要赖在南越并威胁要恢复轰炸越南北方
    据新华社二十三日讯  华盛顿消息：美国总统约翰逊二十二日再次公开表示，美国要坚持赖在南越，并且威胁要恢复对越南北方的轰炸，对越南人民进行露骨的讹诈。
    这是约翰逊在三天内的第二次发出这种讹诈论调。同他二十日在美国密苏里州独立城发表的那篇演说一样，约翰逊在二十二日给一批议员的复信中，一方面继续装出一副和平姿态，说什么：“我们目前停止轰炸北越只得到了敌对的反应，但是你们可以相信，我们将继续进行不屈不挠的追求和平的努力。”另方面，他又强调说，美国“决心要维持和支持我们在南越的战斗部队”，并且露骨地威胁说：“一个月以来，我们在一个重要的军事行动领域内（按指对越南北方的轰炸）没有动手。但是侵略者的部队继续渗入，而且他们继续向我们的盟友和我们自己的部队进攻。我相信你们会同意，我们负有沉重的义务不要轻率地增加我们的部队所必须面对的危险。”
    约翰逊自己的民主党内的七十六名议员曾在二十一日联名写信给约翰逊，对政府加紧准备扩大战争冒险的作法表示疑虑不安。约翰逊的信就是答复这批议员的。
    参议院军事委员会主席、民主党人鲁塞尔二十一日在听取麦克纳马拉就越南问题作证后，对记者扬言，麦克纳马拉的作证使他相信，华盛顿不久将下令恢复空袭，如果在“和平解决”方面没有取得显著进展的话。
    另一个民主党参议员扬会后“透露”，根据麦克纳马拉和惠勒的证词，美国在南越的军队可能在十月或十一月增加到四十万人。（附图片）
    一月十八日，侵越美军第二十五步兵师在头顿登陆。
                                    新华社发
