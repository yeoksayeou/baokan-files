Title: The American People's New Year "Gift" to Humphrey: Honolulu Public Demonstrations Protest American Aggression Against Vietnam
Author:
Date: 1966-01-05
Edition: 3

    The American People's New Year "Gift" to Humphrey
    Honolulu Public Demonstrations Protest American Aggression Against Vietnam
    Xinhua News Agency, 4th Dispatch from Honolulu: Approximately 150 members of the Honolulu public held demonstrations on the 2nd, protesting American aggression against Vietnam, when U.S. Vice President Humphrey passed through Honolulu on his way back from a visit to the Far East.
    Humphrey returned to Honolulu on the 2nd after plotting to expand the war of aggression against Vietnam in Japan, the Philippines, Taiwan, and South Korea. That evening, as he delivered a speech at the University of Hawaii, extravagantly praising Johnson for "making peace," approximately 150 members of the public set up a picket line outside the auditorium as a 1966 New Year's "gift"; some demonstrators also marched with signs reading "Stop the Vietnam War." ### 美国人民给汉弗莱的新年“见面礼”  檀香山公众示威抗议美国侵越

1966-01-05
第3版()
专栏：

    美国人民给汉弗莱的新年“见面礼”
    檀香山公众示威抗议美国侵越
    新华社四日讯檀香山消息：大约一百五十名檀香山公众二日在美国副总统汉弗莱访问远东回国途中经过檀香山时，举行了示威，抗议美国侵略越南。
    汉弗莱是在日本、菲律宾、台湾和南朝鲜等地策划扩大侵越战争的阴谋后于二日回到檀香山的。当天晚上当他在夏威夷大学发表讲话、大肆吹捧约翰逊“在缔造和平”时，大约一百五十名公众在大礼堂外边布置了示威纠察线，作为一九六六年的“见面礼”；一部分示威者还举着“停止越南战争”的标语牌游行。（附图片）
