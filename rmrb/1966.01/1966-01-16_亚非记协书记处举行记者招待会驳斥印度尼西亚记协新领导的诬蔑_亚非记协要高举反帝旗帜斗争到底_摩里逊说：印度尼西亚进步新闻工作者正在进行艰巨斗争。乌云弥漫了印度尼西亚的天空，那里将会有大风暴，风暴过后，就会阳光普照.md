Title: Afro-Asian Journalists' Association Secretariat Holds Press Conference to Refute Slanders by New Leaders of Indonesian Journalists' Association
Afro-Asian Journalists' Association to Hold High the Anti-Imperialist Banner and Fight to the End
Morrison Says: Indonesian Progressive Journalists are Engaged in an Arduous Struggle. Clouds Fill the Indonesian Sky; There Will Be a Great Storm There, and After the Storm, the Sun Will Shine

Author:
Date: 1966-01-16
Edition: 5

    Xinhua News Agency, January 15th: The Secretariat of the Afro-Asian Journalists' Association held a press conference in Beijing this afternoon to brief Chinese and foreign journalists on the circumstances of the Secretariat's withdrawal from Jakarta. The Secretariat announced at the conference that any interference in the affairs of the Afro-Asian Journalists' Association by the new leadership of the Indonesian Journalists' Association is illegal and invalid.
    A.R. Abukus (Arab Syrian Republic), Chen Quanbi (China), Ri Morrison (South Africa), and Sugiura Ichiei (Japan), resident secretaries of the Afro-Asian Journalists' Association Secretariat, who came to Beijing for a temporary meeting, presided over the press conference and answered questions raised by journalists.
    More than 100 journalists from major newspapers, news agencies, radio stations, television stations, and magazines in the capital, as well as journalists from Asian, African, and other countries in Beijing, attended the press conference.
    Ri Morrison, at the beginning of the press conference, introduced the history of the establishment of the Afro-Asian Journalists' Association and its aims. He said that for the past two and a half years since its establishment, the Afro-Asian Journalists' Association has unremittingly fought for the independence and peace of Asian and African countries, and it supports the struggles of the people of Congo, Vietnam, and other Asian and African countries. The Afro-Asian Journalists' Association has never been separated from the anti-imperialist struggle of the people of the world.
    He said that journalistic work and politics cannot be separated. We journalists must devote ourselves to the anti-imperialist struggle. The struggle of the Afro-Asian Journalists' Association is part of the struggle of the Asian and African people against imperialism, colonialism, and neo-colonialism. We also resolutely oppose those who call themselves friends of the Afro-Asian Journalists' Association, namely, those fake revolutionaries. For us, it is important to recognize who our friends are and who our enemies are.
    He said that imperialism has not died. American imperialism is the head of imperialism and the enemy of the people of the world. We must never compromise with imperialism; there is no compromise between the oppressors and the oppressed. The Afro-Asian Journalists' Association will hold high the banner of anti-imperialism and fight to the end.
    Morrison concluded by saying that now, progressive journalists in Indonesia are engaged in an arduous struggle. Clouds fill the Indonesian sky; there will be a great storm there, and after the storm, the sun will shine on Indonesia.
    Sugiura Ichiei then spoke at the press conference. He introduced the contributions made by the Afro-Asian Journalists' Association to opposing imperialism, old and new colonialism, and to strengthening the unity of the Asian and African people and journalists. He talked about the interference, obstruction, and sabotage encountered by the Secretariat since the changes in the situation in Indonesia in October 1965, and the circumstances of the Secretariat's withdrawal from Jakarta. In his speech, he also refuted the various slanders of the Afro-Asian Journalists' Association Secretariat by the new leadership of the Indonesian Journalists' Association.
    At the press conference, Ri Morrison also read out a telegram from the Afro-Asian Journalists' Association Secretariat to the Indonesian Ministry of Foreign Affairs. The telegram pointed out that the Secretariat protested with great anger against the arbitrary arrest of Yusuf, Acting Secretary-General of the Afro-Asian Journalists' Association, by the Indonesian armed forces, and demanded the immediate release of Yusuf and other persecuted Indonesian journalists.

### 亚非记协书记处举行记者招待会驳斥印度尼西亚记协新领导的诬蔑  亚非记协要高举反帝旗帜斗争到底  摩里逊说：印度尼西亚进步新闻工作者正在进行艰巨斗争。乌云弥漫了印度尼西亚的天空，那里将会有大风暴，风暴过后，就会阳光普照

1966-01-16
第5版()
专栏：

    亚非记协书记处举行记者招待会驳斥印度尼西亚记协新领导的诬蔑
    亚非记协要高举反帝旗帜斗争到底
    摩里逊说：印度尼西亚进步新闻工作者正在进行艰巨斗争。乌云弥漫了印度尼西亚的天空，那里将会有大风暴，风暴过后，就会阳光普照
    新华社十五日讯　亚非新闻工作者协会书记处今天下午在北京举行记者招待会，向中外记者介绍书记处撤离雅加达的经过。书记处在会上宣布：印度尼西亚新闻工作者协会的新领导所做的任何干涉亚非新闻工作者协会事务的事，都是非法的和无效的。
    来北京暂时会合的亚非新闻工作者协会书记处驻会书记阿·勒·阿布库斯（阿拉伯叙利亚共和国）、陈泉璧（中国）、里·摩里逊（南非）和杉山市平（日本）主持记者招待会，分别回答了记者提出的问题。
    首都各报社、通讯社、电台、电视台和杂志社的记者，以及亚非各国和其它国家在北京的记者，共一百多人出席了招待会。
    里·摩里逊在招待会开始的时候，介绍了亚非新闻工作者协会成立的历史和它的宗旨。他说，亚非新闻工作者协会成立两年半以来，曾不懈地为亚非各国的独立和和平而斗争，它支持刚果、越南以及亚非各国人民的斗争。亚非新闻工作者协会从来没有和全世界人民的反帝斗争分开。
    他说，新闻业务和政治不能分开。我们新闻工作者必须投身于反帝斗争。亚非新闻工作者协会的斗争是亚非人民反对帝国主义、殖民主义、新殖民主义斗争的一部分。我们也坚决反对那些自称为亚非新闻工作者协会的朋友的人，就是那些假的革命者。对我们来说，重要的是要认清谁是我们的朋友，谁是我们的敌人。
    他说，帝国主义没有死亡。美帝国主义是帝国主义的头子，是全世界人民的敌人。我们决不能和帝国主义妥协，压迫者和被压迫者没有妥协可言。亚非新闻工作者协会要高举反对帝国主义的旗帜，斗争到底。
    摩里逊最后说，现在，印度尼西亚进步的新闻工作者正在进行艰巨的斗争。乌云弥漫了印度尼西亚的天空，那里将会有大风暴，风暴之后，阳光会普照印度尼西亚。
    杉山市平接着在招待会上讲话。他介绍了亚非新闻工作者协会为反对帝国主义和新老殖民主义、为加强亚非人民以及新闻工作者的团结所做出的贡献，谈到书记处自一九六五年十月印度尼西亚局势发生变化以来所遇到的干涉、阻难和破坏，以及书记处撤离雅加达的经过。他在讲话中还驳斥了印度尼西亚新闻工作者协会新领导对亚非新闻工作者协会书记处的种种诬蔑。
    在招待会上，里·摩里逊还宣读了亚非新闻工作者协会书记处致印度尼西亚外交部的一份电报。电报指出，书记处以极大愤怒的心情，抗议印度尼西亚武装部队专横逮捕亚非新闻工作者协会代总书记优素福，要求立即释放优素福和其他被迫害的印度尼西亚新闻工作者。
