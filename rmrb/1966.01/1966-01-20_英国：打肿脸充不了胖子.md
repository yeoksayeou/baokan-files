Title: Britain: Trying to Look Fat by Swelling One's Face

Author: 
Date: 1966-01-20
Edition: 5

    As far as Britain is concerned, a lot happened in 1965, but nothing was solved – more so in the economic field (the root cause of all our difficulties) than in any other. But it was also the year our international standing reached its lowest point, and Britain has reached the point of beginning to lose confidence due to being strapped for cash and heavily in debt.

    Our current situation is the result of a period in which we have increasingly mistaken trying to look fat internationally by swelling one’s face for actual world power. The past year will only be valuable to Britain if it has made us understand this point, if we have learned from it what kind of world role we can abandon, and indeed must abandon.
                        Financial Times (UK)
                              December 29, 1965
    Looking ahead to 1966, it is difficult to suppress a sense of powerlessness; Britain's role is certainly a subordinate one. Our voices are no more than whispers, or at least they seem to be.
                        The Observer (UK)
                                  January 2, 1966

### 英国：打肿脸充不了胖子

1966-01-20
第5版()
专栏：

    英国：打肿脸充不了胖子
    就英国来说，一九六五年发生了许多事情，但是没有解决任何事情——经济方面（我们全部困难的根本原因）比其他方面更其如此。但是对我们来说，也是我们的国际地位降到最低点的一年，英国已经到了因捉襟见肘和负债累累而开始失去自信的地步。
    我们目前所处的状况，是一个时期的结果，在这个时期里，我们越来越把在国际上打肿脸充胖子误以为是实际的世界权力。过去的一年只有如果是使我们明白了这一点，如果我们从中懂得了我们能够抛弃，而且是必须抛弃的那种世界角色，这一年对英国来说才是有价值的。
                        英国《金融时报》
                              一九六五年十二月二十九日
    在展望一九六六年的时候，很难抑制一种无能为力的情绪，英国的作用肯定是从属的作用。我们的声音都不过象耳语一样，或者说至少看来是这样。
                        英国《观察家报》
                                  一九六六年一月二日
