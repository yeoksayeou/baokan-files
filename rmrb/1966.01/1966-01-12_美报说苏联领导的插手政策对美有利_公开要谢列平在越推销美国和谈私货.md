Title: U.S. Media Says the Soviet Leadership's Intervention Policy Benefits the U.S., Openly Asks Shelepin to Peddle U.S. Private Goods on Vietnam Negotiations
Author:
Date: 1966-01-12
Edition: 4

　　Xinhua News Agency, Washington, January 11th: An article in the U.S. newspaper *The Christian Science Monitor* on January 8th commenting on Shelepin's visit to Hanoi stated that Johnson's closest advisors believe that "the Soviet Union's re-entry into the Southeast Asian stage to a considerable extent" is "beneficial and harmless" to the United States. Therefore, "Washington extended the Christmas bombing halt to include the current trip by a group of Soviets led by Shelepin."

　　The article pointed out that "the Soviet Union cannot appear to be helping the United States achieve a solution (to the Vietnam problem)." "It cannot do this while it still bears the obligation (to support) 'wars of liberation' in front of the communist world." The article said that whatever the Soviet Union intends to do in this regard, it "will have to do it under the cover of constant public attacks on the United States."

　　The article then quoted a report from *The Washington Post* saying, "The Soviet Union had told the United States that if the bombing was suspended, it would send a Soviet mission to Hanoi."

　　The newspaper also said: "There are indications of something that could be called consistent signals between the United States and the Soviet Union… Both capitals have avoided revealing anything that could be seized upon by the Chinese to reiterate their accusations of collusion between the two countries."

　　The article revealed that "according to senior White House officials, President Johnson has been doing more behind the scenes than he has been doing publicly while continuing the bombing halt in North Vietnam in order to find out whether it is possible to hold peace talks now."

　　An editorial in *The New York Times* on the 7th stated that there is reason to "hope that the Soviet delegation to Hanoi may be conducive to promoting negotiations on the Vietnam issue."

　　The editorial said, "Judging from Moscow's leadership of the world communist movement and its relations with the United States and the West, helping to achieve peace in Vietnam is clearly in the Soviet Union's interest. Their official attitude on the Vietnam issue is belligerent, but behind this attitude, Soviet officials have long given Western diplomats the impression that they are very eager for negotiations, and a cessation of bombing of North Vietnam would make it possible to hold peace talks"; while accelerating the war "would prevent the development of East-West detente, and Moscow's plans for peaceful economic development and a 'consumer goods revolution' all depend on East-West detente."

　　An article published in *The New York Herald Tribune* on the same day bluntly stated: "The best thing one can hope for at present is that the Russians will exert influence on their allies in Hanoi by increasing military and economic aid to North Vietnam, and may soon demand that they engage in the kind of negotiations Washington is pursuing to end the war."

### 美报说苏联领导的插手政策对美有利  公开要谢列平在越推销美国和谈私货

1966-01-12
第4版()
专栏：

    美报说苏联领导的插手政策对美有利
    公开要谢列平在越推销美国和谈私货
    新华社十一日讯　华盛顿消息：美国《基督教科学箴言报》八日发表的一篇评论谢列平访问河内的文章写道：约翰逊的最亲密的顾问们认为，“苏联在相当大的程度上重新进入东南亚舞台”，对美国来说，“是有利而无害的”。因此，“华盛顿把圣诞节停炸延长到把以谢列平为首的一伙苏联人目前的旅行（期间）包括在内”。
    文章指出，“苏联不能显得是在帮助美国取得一项（越南问题的）解决办法”。“当它在共产主义世界面前仍然承担着
  （支持）‘解放战争’的义务时，它不能这样作。”文章说，不论苏联在这方面打算作些什么，它都“将不得不在经常公开攻击美国的掩盖下来作”。
    文章接着引《华盛顿邮报》的报道说，“苏联曾经告诉美国，如果暂停轰炸，就会派一个苏联使团到河内去”。
    这家报纸还说：“有迹象表明，在美国和苏联之间存在着可以称为一致的信号的东西……两国首都都避免透露任何可能被中国人抓住来重新发出他们的关于两国勾结的指责的东西。”
    文章透露说，“据白宫高级官员说，在继续暂停轰炸北越的时候，约翰逊总统在幕后进行的活动比他公开进行的更多，以便了解，现在进行和谈是否可能。”
    美国《纽约时报》七日的社论说，人们有理由“希望苏联去河内的代表团可能会有利于促使在越南问题上进行谈判。”
    社论说，“从莫斯科对世界共产主义运动的领导以及它和美国与西方的关系来看，帮助在越南实现和平显然是符合苏联利益的。他们在越南问题上的官方态度是好战的，但在这种态度背后，苏联的官员们早就使西方外交官员得到这样一种印象：他们非常希望谈判，停止轰炸北越将使得有可能举行和平谈判”；而加速战争“将会阻止发展中的东西方和解，而莫斯科的和平经济发展和
  ‘消费品革命’的计划全都寄托在东西方和解上面”。
    《纽约先驱论坛报》同一天发表的文章露骨地说：“目前人们所能希望的最好事情是：俄国人将通过给北越增加军事和经济援助，对他们在河内的盟友施加影响，并且可以在不久后要求他们进行那种华盛顿正在追求的结束战争的谈判。”
