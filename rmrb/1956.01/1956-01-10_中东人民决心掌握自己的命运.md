Title: Middle Eastern People Determined to Control Their Own Destiny
Author:
Date: 1956-01-10
Page: 4

　　Middle Eastern People Determined to Control Their Own Destiny
    Jordanian People Continue to Demonstrate
    Xinhua News Agency, 9th dispatch: Amman news: Jordan's King Hussein designated Samir al-Rifai on January 8th to form a new government to replace the Hashim cabinet, which resigned on January 7th. Rifai was originally the Deputy Prime Minister and Foreign Minister of the Hashim cabinet.
    At the same time, the situation in Jordan remains tense. Following demonstrations and strikes in many cities, including the capital Amman, on the 7th, demonstrations against the Baghdad Pact continued on the 8th. According to the Associated Press, during the demonstrations on the 7th, demonstrators in Amman set fire to the building of the agency responsible for American "technical assistance," and demonstrators in the Jordanian district of Jerusalem tore up the American flag in front of the American consulate.
    In the Jordanian district of Jerusalem, the Jordanian people continued to demonstrate on the 8th. Demonstrators shouted slogans such as "Down with colonialism." Soldiers of the Arab Legion fired on demonstrators. Streets were filled with barbed wire, and soldiers wearing steel helmets patrolled the streets.
    Leaders of Syria and Lebanon Hold Meeting
    Xinhua News Agency, 8th dispatch: TASS Beirut, 7th dispatch: Under the chairmanship of Lebanese President Chamoun, Syrian Prime Minister Said Ghazi, Interior Minister Ali Bozo, Lebanese Prime Minister Rashid Karami, and Foreign Minister Salim Lahoud held a meeting in Beirut on January 6th. The participants discussed issues related to the relations between the two countries, such as the signing of a bilateral military agreement, Lebanon's extradition of members of the Syrian National Socialist Party to the Syrian authorities (because they opposed Syria), and the economic relations between the two countries.
    Rashid Karami told reporters that the exchange of views took place in a friendly atmosphere, but no decisions were made. Lebanese Foreign Minister Salim Lahoud pointed out that the Lebanese side had informed the Syrian representatives that Lebanon was prepared to sign a bilateral military agreement. Syrian Interior Minister Ali Bozo told reporters that the two sides had reached agreements on some very important issues at the meeting on January 6th.
    Syrian Students Support Jordanian People
    Xinhua News Agency, 9th dispatch: Damascus news: Thousands of Syrian students held demonstrations on January 8th to support the Jordanian people's struggle against the Baghdad Pact.

### 中东人民决心掌握自己的命运

1956-01-10
第4版()
专栏：

　　中东人民决心掌握自己的命运
    约旦人民继续举行示威
    据新华社9日讯　安曼消息：约旦国王侯赛尼1月8日指定萨米尔·里法伊组织新政府，以接替在1月7日辞职的哈希姆内阁。里法伊原来是哈希姆内阁的副首相兼外交大臣。
    同时，约旦局势继续紧张。包括首都安曼在内的许多城市继7日发生示威游行和罢工之后，在8日继续举行了反对巴格达条约的示威游行。据美联社报道，在7日的示威中，安曼示威群众曾经放火烧了负责美国“技术援助”的机构的大厦，耶路撒冷的约旦区示威群众在美国领事馆门前撕毁了美国国旗。
    在耶路撒冷的约旦区，约旦人民在8日也继续举行示威游行。示威者高呼“打倒殖民主义”等口号。阿拉伯军团的士兵曾向示威者开枪。街道上布满了铁丝网，戴着钢盔的士兵在路上巡逻着。
    叙利亚和黎巴嫩两国政府领袖举行会议
    新华社8日讯　塔斯社贝鲁特7日讯：在黎巴嫩总统夏蒙主持下，叙利亚总理赛义德·加齐、内政部长阿里·波佐同黎巴嫩总理拉希德·卡拉米、外交部长萨利姆·拉霍德1月6日在贝鲁特举行了会议。会议参加者讨论了关于两国关系的一些问题，如签订双边军事协定，黎巴嫩把叙利亚国家社会党党员引渡给叙利亚当局（因为他们反对叙利亚），以及两国经济关系的问题。
    拉希德·卡拉米向记者说，交换意见是在友好气氛中进行的，不过没有作出任何的决定。黎巴嫩外交部长萨利姆·拉霍德指出，黎巴嫩方面已通知叙利亚代表：黎巴嫩准备签订双边军事协定。叙利亚内政部长阿里·波佐向记者说，在1月6日的会议上，双方在一些十分重要的问题上达成了协议。
    叙利亚学生声援约旦人民
    据新华社9日讯　大马士革消息：数以千计的叙利亚学生在1月8日举行示威游行，声援约旦人民反对巴格达条约的斗争。
