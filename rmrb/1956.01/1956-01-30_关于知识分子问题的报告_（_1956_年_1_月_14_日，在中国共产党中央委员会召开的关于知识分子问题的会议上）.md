Title: Report on the Issue of Intellectuals
Author: Zhou Enlai
Date: 1956-01-30
Page: 1

To strengthen the Party's leadership over intellectuals and over the entire scientific and cultural work, the Central Committee has decided to convene a meeting to discuss the issue of intellectuals. This decision by the Central Committee is part of the Party's overall struggle against conservative thinking and to fulfill the tasks of the transitional period.

Everyone knows that in 1955, based on the initiative of Comrade Mao Zedong, the Party Central Committee conducted a series of struggles against right-wing conservative thinking within the Party. The most important result of these struggles is that the campaign to suppress counter-revolutionaries has been systematically and thoroughly carried out within institutions and society, and it is expected to be basically completed nationwide within the next two years. The agricultural cooperativization movement, based on the rapid growth of mass awareness, has made rapid progress, and this year it is expected to be basically completed nationwide with semi-socialist cooperativization and is moving towards full socialist cooperativization. The full industry-public joint management of capitalist industry and commerce is rapidly developing, and it is also very likely to be basically completed nationwide this year. The first five-year plan for the development of the national economy will be completed ahead of schedule and in excess, with some production sectors expected to complete the 1957 production plan in 1956, and other sectors also having the possibility of completing the five-year plan ahead of schedule and in excess.

All these great and inspiring achievements were unimaginable just a year ago; if we had not launched the struggle against right-wing conservative thinking, these achievements would still be impossible to achieve now. This shows how serious the harm of right-wing conservative thinking within our Party is.

The basic requirement of the struggle against right-wing conservative thinking is to strengthen and develop the people's democratic dictatorship, to complete the socialist transformation ahead of schedule, to exceed the state industrial development plan, and to accelerate the technological transformation of the national economy. This struggle has significant global implications. In our country with a population of six hundred million, if we can complete and accelerate these tasks ahead of schedule, and complete and carry out these tasks more, faster, better, and more economically, it will greatly strengthen the entire socialist camp, making it more conducive to our efforts to prevent the outbreak of new wars. If the mad invaders dare to launch a new war, we will also be in a more powerful position. Therefore, the Party Central Committee has decided to make the struggle against right-wing conservative thinking the central issue of the Eighth National Congress of the Party, requiring the entire Party to launch this struggle in all work departments.

The issue of intellectuals is raised on this basis.

What is the relationship between the issue of intellectuals and our current task of accelerating socialist construction?

The reason we want to build a socialist economy is ultimately to meet the ever-increasing material and cultural needs of society to the greatest extent possible. To achieve this goal, we must continuously develop the productive forces and continuously improve labor productivity, and we must continuously increase and improve socialist production on a highly technological basis. Therefore, in the socialist era, more than in any other era, it is necessary to fully improve production technology and to fully develop science and utilize scientific knowledge. Therefore, to develop socialist construction in a way that is more, faster, better, and more economical, in addition to relying on the active labor of the working class and the broad masses of peasants, we must also rely on the active labor of intellectuals, that is, we must rely on the close cooperation of physical labor and mental labor, and the alliance of workers, peasants, and intellectuals. The various construction projects we are currently undertaking are increasingly in need of the participation of intellectuals. For example, if we want to find minerals, we need a group of geologists to lead a large number of university and high school graduates to various remote and desolate areas to conduct surveys, general investigations, detailed investigations, and drilling. If we want to build mines, factories, railways, and water conservancy projects, we need a group of engineers and a large number of technicians to conduct surveys, design, construction, and installation. The production of factories, from product design to final product inspection, requires a certain number and level of technical personnel. The management of industry and commerce increasingly requires various specialized knowledge. To build a modern national defense, various scientific experts are needed. Without teachers and doctors, we cannot have schools and hospitals. Without cultural and artistic workers, we cannot have cultural life. In rural areas, after achieving agricultural mechanization and electrification, a large number of agricultural machinery engineers, power station engineers, agronomists, accountants, etc., will certainly be needed; even at present, to implement the projects that need to be started immediately in the draft outline for agricultural development from 1956 to 1967, such as the production of new-style animal-drawn agricultural tools, chemical fertilizers, and water pumps, the elimination of major pests and diseases, the elimination of serious human diseases and livestock epidemics, it is necessary to rely on the active participation of scientific and technological workers, plant protection experts, medical workers, and veterinarians. Therefore, intellectuals have become an important factor in all aspects of our country's life. And correctly solving the issue of intellectuals, fully mobilizing and giving full play to their strengths, and serving the great cause of socialism, has also become an important condition for us to fulfill the tasks of the transitional period. The various departments of our Party and the various levels of Party organizations should attach importance to this issue.

What is the current issue of intellectuals? The fundamental issue at present is that the strength of our intellectuals, whether in terms of quantity, professional level, or political awareness, is not sufficient to meet the rapidly developing needs of socialist construction; and some irrational phenomena in our current use and treatment of intellectuals, especially the sectarian sentiments of some comrades towards non-Party intellectuals, to a considerable extent hinder the full play of the existing strengths of intellectuals. We must strengthen our leadership, overcome shortcomings, and take a series of effective measures to fully mobilize and give full play to the existing strengths of intellectuals, continuously improve their political awareness, cultivate new forces on a large scale to expand their ranks, and as quickly as possible improve their professional level to meet the country's growing needs for intellectuals. This is the fundamental task of our Party in the issue of intellectuals at present.

To discuss the task of the Party in the issue of intellectuals, we must first examine the current situation of intellectuals.

Our Party has always attached importance to the issue of intellectuals. As early as 1939, the Party Central Committee, under the leadership of Comrade Mao Zedong, adopted a decision to absorb a large number of intellectuals and effectively implemented it in various anti-Japanese base areas. After the liberation of the whole country, the Party implemented a policy of uniting, educating, and transforming intellectuals nationwide. The Party Central Committee believed: the revolution needs to absorb intellectuals, and construction especially needs to absorb intellectuals. In particular, because pre-liberation China was a culturally and scientifically backward country, we must be good at fully utilizing the historical heritage left over from the old society, making them serve the socialist construction of our country. The Party Central Committee also believed: the intellectuals of the old era, although influenced by imperialism and the reactionary classes in the past, were also subjected to the oppression of imperialism and the Kuomintang, so a part of them participated in the revolution, a part sympathized with the revolution, and the majority took a neutral and wait-and-see attitude towards the revolution, with only a small number being counter-revolutionaries. Facts increasingly proved to Chinese intellectuals that they had no other way out except to join the working class and the Communist Party. Therefore, uniting with intellectuals is necessary and completely possible. Based on this understanding, the Party Central Committee adopted the policy of "taking them all in" for the intellectuals of the old era, continuing to give appropriate work to the vast majority, and even assigning responsible work to a part of them; for the originally unemployed intellectuals, efforts were also made to help them find jobs or make other appropriate arrangements. In the political sphere, the Party gave many representatives of intellectuals their due positions. The Party Central Committee believed: the intellectuals of the old era must be helped to undergo self-transformation, to abandon the ideas of the landlord class and the bourgeoisie, and to accept the ideas of the working class. For this purpose, the Party took a series of steps. The Party organized them to participate in land reform, suppression of counter-revolutionaries, aid to Korea, the "Three Antis" and "Five Antis" campaigns, visits to factories and villages, visits to the Soviet Union, participation in various international activities, leading them to study the basic knowledge of Marxism-Leninism, criticize bourgeois idealist viewpoints, and conduct struggles against the counter-revolutionary Hu Feng clique and other counter-revolutionaries, and on this basis, carry out criticism and self-criticism. In terms of professional work, the Party also took many steps to help them master the principle of combining theory with practice, learn from the advanced experience of the Soviet Union, improve work methods, and enhance professional abilities. The vast majority of them have already become state cadres, already serving socialism, and already a part of the working class. While uniting, educating, and transforming old intellectuals, the Party also devoted considerable effort to cultivating a large number of new intellectuals, among whom there is already a considerable number of intellectuals of labor class origin. Due to all this, the face of China's intellectual community has undergone fundamental changes in the past six years.

Regarding the current political situation of intellectuals, many units have conducted statistics. These statistics show: among senior intellectuals, those who actively support the Communist Party and the people's government, actively support socialism, and actively serve the people, account for about 40%; those who support the Communist Party and the people's government, generally can complete tasks, but are not politically active, also account for about 40%. The two parts together account for about 80%. Outside this 80%, those who lack political awareness or oppose socialism in their thoughts account for about 10%, and counter-revolutionaries and other bad elements account for a few percent.

Compared with the early days of liberation, this change is very rapid. For example, according to statistics from four universities in Beijing, Tianjin, and Qingdao on 141 teachers, in the past six years, the number of progressive elements has increased from 18% to 41%, and the number of backward elements has decreased from 28% to 15%. Many intellectuals, in the face of China's great cause of socialist transformation and construction, cannot but be increasingly influenced and see the fate of the entire nation and their own closely connected in the new birth of China.

It should be noted here that the ideological state of intellectuals is not completely consistent with their changes in political and social status. Many progressive elements still have varying degrees of bourgeois idealism and individualism in their thinking, let alone the intermediate elements. In addition, many units have slow changes among their intellectuals, especially among the more backward parts, which also reflects that we have done little work among them.

The ranks of intellectuals have also expanded rapidly in the past six years. Currently, the estimated number of senior intellectuals in scientific research, education, engineering technology, health, culture and arts, and other fields nationwide is about 100,000, of which the number increased after liberation, according to some statistical materials, accounts for about one-third. Some sectors have increased particularly rapidly. For example, the number of geologists at the beginning of liberation was less than 200, but in 1955, according to statistics from the four departments of geology, heavy industry, petroleum industry, and coal industry, the number of engineers alone has already increased to 497, and the number of technical personnel graduated from colleges and universities has reached 3,440.

In the six years since liberation, the number of graduates from colleges and universities nationwide has reached 217,900. Although they do not all meet the standard of senior intellectuals we mentioned, they are the new forces in the intellectual community and the reserve army of experts. Moreover, it should be pointed out that many young people, although not yet experts in terms of rank, have already undertaken the work of experts and generally performed well. Among the 42,000 teaching staff in colleges and universities, professors and associate professors account for 17.8%, lecturers account for 24%, and assistant lecturers account for 58.2%, and some assistant lecturers are now participating in teaching. The situation in the engineering field is the same. There are only 31,000 engineers of all levels nationwide, but there are 63,600 technical personnel graduated from colleges and universities, and many of them are actually undertaking the work of engineers, and some of them should have been promoted to engineers long ago. In addition to this, as the reserve army of senior intellectuals, there is also a broad army of other intellectuals, who are constantly improving their knowledge level in the process of actual work and self-study in their spare time.

There is no strict boundary between senior intellectuals and general intellectuals. Now, just looking at the statistics of the five aspects of scientific research, education, engineering technology, health, and culture and arts, there are 3.84 million intellectuals in total. They are a great force in the cause of socialist construction. Correctly estimating and using these intellectuals, and helping them to make continuous progress in politics and professional skills in a planned way, is an extremely important task for the Party and the state. In my report, although the discussion focuses on issues related to senior intellectuals, most of the principles are also applicable to general intellectuals.

As mentioned above, China's intellectuals have now become a very large force. However, our country is so large, and our construction cause is developing so fast, and it will develop even faster in the future, so we must expand the ranks of intellectuals, especially senior intellectuals, faster to meet the urgent needs of socialist construction. It should be admitted that there are still many shortcomings in our work of cultivating and promoting new forces, and these shortcomings hinder the faster expansion of the ranks of intellectuals.

The professional level of China's intellectuals has also been significantly improved in the past six years. Colleges and universities nationwide have carried out teaching reforms, established many new departments and majors that did not exist before nationwide, compiled and translated a large number of textbooks, and improved the quality of teaching. The scientific and technological community nationwide has done a great deal of work in geological exploration, basic construction design and construction, new product design and trial production, and has achieved remarkable results. As a result of efforts to learn from the Soviet Union, the engineering community in China has now learned many modern designs and constructions of factories, mines, bridges, and water conservancy projects, and the ability to design large-scale machinery, locomotives, and ships has also been greatly improved. From 1952 to 1955, the number of new mechanical products successfully trial-produced has reached about 3,500, and a few have reached the world level. In metallurgy, China can now smelt 240 kinds of high-quality steel and alloy steel; the utilization coefficient of China's blast furnaces and open-hearth furnaces has reached the level of the Soviet Union in 1952. In theoretical science, China's achievements in some fields of mathematics, physics, organic chemistry, and biology have also been recognized by the world scientific community, and some of them have already contributed to production practice.

However, overall, China's science and technology are still very backward. Not only have we not been able to master and utilize many of the latest achievements of world science, but even many of the complex technical problems in China's current construction, we still cannot solve independently without the help of Soviet experts. However, until recently, we have not made a comprehensive plan for improving China's scientific and technological level; even the existing forces have not been fully and effectively utilized. The backwardness of technical science is inseparable from the weakness of the theoretical science foundation, and it is precisely in the field of scientific research that we have invested the least effort.

From the above simple description, it can be seen: our achievements are great, but there are still many shortcomings.

So, what policy should we adopt in the issue of intellectuals? What tendencies should we prevent and correct?

The main tendency in the issue of intellectuals at present is sectarianism, but at the same time, there is also a tendency of complacency and indulgence. The former tendency is: underestimating the great progress of the intellectual community in politics and professional skills, underestimating their important role in China's socialist cause, not recognizing them as a part of the working class, thinking that after all, production relies on workers, and technology relies on Soviet experts, and therefore not seriously implementing the Party's policy on intellectuals, not seriously studying and solving problems related to intellectuals; indifferent to how to fully mobilize and give full play to the strengths of intellectuals, how to further transform intellectuals, expand the ranks of intellectuals, and improve the professional level of intellectuals, etc. The latter tendency is: only seeing the progress of the intellectual community and not seeing their shortcomings, overestimating them, blindly trusting them without distinction, and even not being vigilant against bad elements, and therefore not carrying out educational and transformational work on them, or although seeing their shortcomings, but due to various inappropriate concerns, not daring to carry out educational and transformational work on them. These two tendencies are opposite in form, but the actual result is the same kind of right-wing conservatism, both of which are abandoning leadership, lacking a spirit of active struggle, and both hinder us from correctly solving the issue of intellectuals and scientific and cultural issues, and both hinder the development of China's socialist cause.

We must oppose these two incorrect tendencies at the same time. We should neither ignore the existing strengths of the intellectual community nor be satisfied with them; we should neither rely on Soviet experts indefinitely nor relax learning the most advanced science and technology from the Soviet Union and other countries. The only correct policy we should adopt is: in order to make China's socialist construction cause proceed in a way that is more, faster, better, and more economical, we must make every effort to fully mobilize and give full play to the existing strengths of intellectuals, and at the same time, make every effort to transform, expand, and improve them as quickly as possible, so that the speed and scale of this transformation, expansion, and improvement can truly match the giant steps of the great development of all aspects of our country.

To fully mobilize and give full play to the existing strengths of intellectuals, it is not only necessary for the current tense construction cause, but also a prerequisite for further transforming, expanding, and improving the intellectual community.

Generally speaking, intellectuals have been mobilized on a large scale under the leadership of our Party. Otherwise, the great progress of intellectuals we mentioned earlier, and their great contributions to the country, would be unimaginable. We must first affirm this, which is the main aspect of the matter.

However, there are indeed many shortcomings in our work, including some serious ones. In the current high tide of socialist construction and transformation, we need to strengthen our leadership and quickly overcome these shortcomings to more fully mobilize and give full play to the strengths of intellectuals.

To fully mobilize and give full play to the strengths of intellectuals, first, we should improve their use and arrangement, so that they can give full play to their expertise beneficial to the country.

In the use and arrangement of intellectuals, we have done well in most cases. Many intellectuals have undertaken many important tasks in national construction, and their abilities have also been continuously improved in practice.

However, we cannot say that we have used and arranged intellectuals appropriately and have not wasted any strength. For example, in many agencies, due to inappropriate work distribution or poor work organization, there are still cases where a few intellectuals are "bored to death," and these intellectuals are often precisely because they have some specialized knowledge that they are assigned to these agencies. This phenomenon of wasting the country's most precious resources must be eliminated. Again, in colleges and universities nationwide, there are still a few teachers who have not been assigned courses. Some of these people are not necessarily unable to teach, or just need to organize them to study a little and they can teach, so they should be assigned courses; if they are really unable to teach, they should be assigned work that they can undertake, such as editing and translation work, publishing work, library work, etc., and not let them be idle. Again, in society, there are still a few unemployed intellectuals with considerable labor power, and they should be assigned appropriate work according to the situation, either by local authorities or by the central authorities.

In some places, there is still the phenomenon of using intellectuals inappropriately in their arrangement and use. Some scientists, who are willing to engage in scientific research work and believe that it is most beneficial to the country, are assigned to administrative work in agencies or administrative work in schools. There are also some experts who, due to errors in work distribution, are completely unreasonably assigned to work that they have not studied, sometimes today they are asked to do this, and tomorrow they are asked to do that, but they are not allowed to return to their original field. According to statistics from the Fourth Office of the State Council on five units under the Ministry of Light Industry, this situation of using non-specialized skills in senior intellectuals accounts for about 10%, which is a serious loss! We must take decisive steps to correct this bureaucratic, sectarian, and departmentalist error in the treatment of talents, so as to use specialized talents in the most needed places.

To fully mobilize and give full play to the strengths of intellectuals, second, we should have a full understanding of the intellectuals we use, give them the trust and support they deserve, so that they can actively carry out their work.

Intellectuals are generally satisfied with the trust and support we have given them, but we should still see the shortcomings in our work. In the issue of trusting intellectuals, as mentioned earlier, one tendency is to trust them too much without distinction in politics and professional skills, to the extent of telling some unrelated people or unreliable people some state secrets without necessity, or to overuse some completely unqualified people, causing work to suffer losses. This situation exists and must be corrected. Another tendency is not to give them the trust they deserve, for example, not allowing them to go to factories they can go to, or not allowing them to see materials they can see. This situation also exists and must be corrected. State secrets must be unconditionally kept, and any relaxation is not allowed; the issue is to correctly draw the boundary of secrets and not arbitrarily expand the scope of secrets, causing work to suffer losses and workers to encounter difficulties. At the same time, we should have a correct estimate and understanding of the history of intellectuals to avoid some people being suspected for a long time due to "complex history." Now, many senior intellectuals have a relatively complex history, which is not surprising. However, only a few people have political problems, and only a very few people have current political problems. Many intellectuals with historical problems that have not been concluded are due to the lack of serious and responsible efforts by leading comrades to clarify their problems, resulting in the long-term delay of the problems. We should concentrate relatively strong forces, distinguish between light and heavy, urgent and not urgent, and clear up their pending cases as quickly as possible to facilitate their use in the future.

Non-Party intellectuals, in addition to deserving trust, also deserve support. This means that they should have positions and power, their opinions should be respected, their professional research and work results should be valued, academic discussions in socialist construction should be encouraged and promoted, and their inventions and creations should be given the opportunity to be tested and promoted. There are a few Party members who do not respect their superior non-Party intellectuals, and this situation we should be responsible for correcting.

The lack of trust and support for some intellectuals is the main manifestation of sectarianism in the issue of intellectuals among some of our comrades. Many comrades are still not used to consulting with non-Party intellectuals on matters and giving timely guidance and help before the matter. Some comrades even adopt an attitude of respect but keeping a distance towards non-Party intellectuals. In this way, both sides lack understanding and easily form a rift. However, they are important personnel of our country, and their work performance directly affects national construction, so we have the responsibility to learn to approach them with a comradely attitude, correctly understand them, and thus give them guidance and help, so that they can play an active role in their work.

To fully mobilize and give full play to the strengths of intellectuals, third, we should provide intellectuals with necessary working conditions and appropriate treatment.

The working conditions and treatment of intellectuals have been greatly improved compared to before liberation. However, as mentioned earlier, there are still some urgent problems to be solved in this regard.

In the working conditions of intellectuals, a current important issue is that many of them cannot effectively control their working time. Many intellectuals feel that they spend too much time on non-professional meetings and administrative affairs, and these meetings and many affairs can actually be done without them. Almost the more famous scientists, writers, and artists, the more time they are occupied by various meetings, affairs, and social activities, which is a serious phenomenon on China's cultural front. The Central Committee believes that it is necessary to ensure that they spend at least six-tenths of their working days (i.e., 40 hours per week) on their own professional work, and the remaining time can be used for political study, necessary meetings, and social activities. This requirement should be firmly implemented. Intellectuals participating in social activities is beneficial, but the current shortcoming is that these activities are often concentrated on a few people. In the future, we should make adjustments to enable as many people as possible to participate, and these activities should not be a burden on everyone. Many experts have too many concurrent jobs, and this should be adjusted as soon as possible.

Some intellectuals feel a lack of necessary books and materials and working equipment in their work, or a lack of appropriate assistants, resulting in low work efficiency. This situation indeed exists. For example, many units with a large number of books and materials have not fully valued these precious properties, have not assigned appropriate personnel to organize them, and thus some experts cannot use these books and materials for research. The main reason for this phenomenon is that we are not familiar with their needs, or although we have heard about it many times, we have not taken responsibility to solve it. Some workers do not want to bother with these "small things," which is wrong. This is not a "small thing," we should quickly and seriously solve these problems.

The living treatment of intellectuals, generally speaking, has been improved compared to before liberation, but to enable senior intellectuals to devote more energy to work, their living treatment should be appropriately improved. Some senior intellectuals spend too much unnecessary time on daily trivial matters, which should be regarded as a loss of national labor. The living conditions of some senior intellectuals are too poor, and in Beijing and other cities with particularly rapid population growth, there is a situation where a family of several people live in a small room. Their rest and entertainment life is also poorly organized. All these problems should be seriously solved by the competent departments.

To better solve the living treatment problem of intellectuals, we mainly need to start from three aspects: First, we should educate the administrative management personnel of all relevant units to attach importance to the living conditions of intellectuals from a ideological perspective, especially to break the erroneous idea of only caring about the living of administrative leaders while feeling that intellectuals are "not worth caring for, why should I serve you." Once this is done, half of the problem can be solved. Second, we should educate the trade union organizations and consumer cooperative organizations of all relevant units to strive to expand services for the intellectuals of their units. The union dues should be used to a large extent for the cultural activities and material welfare of the unit. The union workers should go deep into the masses and use a tenacious spirit to solve various living difficulties for the members of the unit, which should be an important task for various intellectual unions. Third, we should adjust the wages of intellectuals appropriately according to the principle of remuneration according to work, so that the wages they receive are commensurate with the size of their contributions to the country, and eliminate the egalitarian tendency and other irrational phenomena in the wage system. In addition, some intellectuals who are not within the scope of state personnel, such as some actors, painters, and traditional Chinese medicine practitioners, currently have relatively low incomes, and this problem should also be solved by the competent departments.

Another important issue related to the living treatment of intellectuals is the promotion system. Our current promotion system has many irrational aspects: there are many grades but small intervals; the grades of college graduates are too low; there are no definite promotion methods and promotion standards; many units do not promote for several years, so some people have not been promoted. This irrational promotion system greatly hinders the enthusiasm of intellectuals in their professional work, especially the cultivation of new forces and the promotion of general intellectuals. This system must be quickly modified. In addition, regarding the systems of academic degrees, academic titles, honorary titles in the intellectual community, and rewards for inventions and excellent works, these are also important methods to encourage intellectuals to make progress and stimulate the progress of science and culture. These systems are being drafted by relevant departments and should be determined and published as soon as possible.

Regarding the political treatment of intellectuals, there are also some places that need to be improved. The main issue here is to eliminate the indifference of many units to the political life of intellectuals. Some intellectuals complain that we make them listen to a long report several times, but more intellectuals complain that we do not let them listen to a report for a year. Similarly, there are many social activities, some people think they participate too much, but more people think that if they can have the opportunity to participate once, they will be greatly encouraged. These aspects, we should pay attention to and make appropriate adjustments. In addition, we should also conduct education among the staff to let them understand how to correctly treat intellectuals and not inadvertently hurt their legitimate self-respect, which is something that any honest laborer should have.

The above-mentioned points are some necessary conditions for fully mobilizing and giving full play to the strengths of intellectuals. Of course, for this purpose, we still need to give them education and transformation, and give them political and professional leadership, which we will discuss later. However, in any case, the above-mentioned conditions are indispensable. With these conditions, it will be more convenient to mobilize intellectuals to give full play to their strengths in the great cause of national construction, and it will also be more convenient to promote their progress in politics and professional skills.

Title: Continuing the Self-Transformation of Intellectuals is an Important Political Task in the Transitional Period
Author:
Date: 1956-04-26
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, Beijing, April 26th.

Continuing the self-transformation of intellectuals is one of the important political tasks in the transitional period for the Party. Our country is now in a transitional period, which is the most profound period of social transformation. The private ownership of the means of production, which has lasted for thousands of years, is to be transformed into socialist public ownership; the exploitative system, which has lasted for thousands of years, is to be permanently abolished; all people are to become laborers of different types. Such a radical change cannot but cause intense reactions in various aspects of our social life and thought. Therefore, all social problems in this period, including the problem of intellectuals, cannot be observed without considering class struggle. The political divisions and changes among intellectuals are precisely the reflection of the progress of class struggle within the ranks of intellectuals. We have already seen that among the current intellectuals, there are still a few percent of counter-revolutionaries and other bad elements (such as swindlers and hooligans), and these people need to be purged from the ranks of intellectuals. Apart from this, there are also a few people who oppose socialism in their thoughts and are completely ignorant of socialism. We should criticize their erroneous thoughts and strive to win them over to the side of socialism.

The work of purging counter-revolutionaries hidden in the intellectual community has achieved great success in the past year. We should continue to strive to achieve the goal of basically purging counter-revolutionaries in the next two years.

In the struggle against counter-revolutionaries, it is important not to implicate those who have only had ordinary social relations with counter-revolutionaries, because there are many such people among intellectuals. If they were previously incorrectly mixed with counter-revolutionaries, we should explain it to them clearly. For those who have only had historical relations with counter-revolutionary organizations and have genuinely changed their stance since liberation, after they have made appropriate explanations, they should be treated the same as ordinary people.

As mentioned earlier, there is still some estrangement between a part of the intellectuals and our Party. We must actively strive to eliminate this estrangement. However, this estrangement often comes from both sides: on the one hand, it is because our comrades have not approached them and understood them; on the other hand, it is because a part of the intellectuals have taken a reserved or even opposing attitude towards socialism. In our enterprises, schools, and institutions, and in society, there are still such intellectuals: they do not distinguish between the Communist Party and the Kuomintang, between the Chinese people and imperialism; they are dissatisfied with the policies and measures of the Party and the people's government, longing for capitalism and even feudalism; they oppose the Soviet Union and are unwilling to learn from the Soviet Union; they refuse to learn Marxism-Leninism and even defame Marxism-Leninism; they look down on labor, look down on laboring people, look down on cadres of laboring people's origin, and are unwilling to associate with workers, peasants, and cadres of worker-peasant origin; they do not want to see the growth of new forces, thinking that progressives are opportunists; they not only often create disputes and antagonism between intellectuals and the Party but also create disputes and antagonism among intellectuals; they are arrogant, thinking they are the best in the world, unable to accept the leadership and criticism of anyone; they deny the interests of the people and society, looking at everything from the perspective of personal interests, supporting what is in their own interests and opposing what is not in their own interests. Of course, all these errors are not common among current intellectuals; however, those who have one or several of the above errors are not few. Not only among backward elements but also among a part of the intermediate elements, there are often some of the above erroneous viewpoints. Narrow-mindedness, arrogance, and looking at problems from the perspective of personal interests are also not uncommon among progressives. Such intellectuals, if they do not change their stance, even if we strive to approach them, there will still be estrangement between them and us.

Therefore, we should not only transform backward elements but also educate intermediate elements as much as possible to help them get out of the intermediate state and become progressives; for progressives, we must help them continue to progress, help them strive to learn Marxism-Leninism, and eliminate the influences of capitalism, individualism, and idealism in their thoughts. We should cultivate a large number of red experts who are determined to struggle for socialism among high-level intellectuals. Currently, some high-level intellectuals have already become red experts, and many people have such aspirations. We should enthusiastically help them in their self-transformation and realize their aspirations. Any attitude of exclusion and discrimination towards them is wrong.

In summary, thoroughly purging counter-revolutionaries hidden in the intellectual community, reducing backward elements to the minimum, helping intermediate elements to become progressives as much as possible, and making progressives into completely socialist intellectualsâ€”this is our program for continuing the transformation of intellectuals in the current stage.

Regarding the transformation of intellectuals, we have already accumulated rich experience and achieved great success. Therefore, the question of whether intellectuals can be transformed no longer needs detailed discussion. Our current task is to summarize past experience, overcome some shortcomings in past work, and make future work more planned. Since we see various political divisions among intellectuals, we should roughly formulate plans according to this situation to educate progressives, intermediate elements, and backward elements, and implement various effective measures that have been proven.

The transformation of intellectuals usually goes through three paths: one is through the observation and practice of social life; one is through their own professional practice; one is through general theoretical learning. These three aspects are interrelated, and a person's change in thought is often influenced by all three aspects. However, generally speaking, the educational role of social life is the most extensive and direct. Everyone knows that the transformation of the thoughts of many intellectuals began with participating in land reform and the War to Resist U.S. Aggression and Aid Korea. In recent years, visits to factories and villages have also greatly helped intellectuals gain confidence in socialism. However, we have not systematically organized this work, especially many intermediate elements and backward elements are often not included in our plans. In the future, we should treat organizing intellectuals to visit socialist construction as an important task and make comprehensive arrangements so that everyone who has not visited can have the opportunity in the next few years.

Professional practice also has a significant impact on the ideological transformation of intellectuals. In the past few years, the reform of teaching methods and the adoption of Soviet textbooks have made many teachers who previously did not believe in the Soviet Union and Marxism-Leninism change their minds. Similarly, the creative achievements of the Chinese working people and peasants in production technology, the promotion of Soviet scientific and technological achievements in China, and the practical demonstrations of Soviet experts have also convinced many scientific and technological personnel of the superiority of the socialist system. In the future, we should continue to develop this aspect of experience. However, in the issue of learning from the Soviet Union, there have been shortcomings of being overly hasty, rigid, and mechanical in the past, and some comrades even arbitrarily denied the scientific and technological achievements of capitalist countries. These shortcomings should be avoided in the future.

The learning of Marxism-Leninism has a decisive significance for establishing the revolutionary worldview and scientific worldview of intellectuals. However, in some places, this learning is not well organized, or the level of the guiding personnel is too low, or the learning plans and methods do not meet the needs of high-level intellectuals. In the future, we should overcome these shortcomings, formulate some compulsory basic courses of Marxism-Leninism according to the principles of voluntariness and relevance to professional work, and focus on adopting self-study, night schools, correspondence schools, and scientific discussion meetings to help intellectuals learn theory.

Since the transformation of intellectuals is a reflection of class struggle, this transformation process itself cannot be without considerable struggle. First, we must require all intellectuals to stand on the patriotic position, abide by the constitution, and distinguish between enemies and us. If a person violates the patriotic position and the constitution, and in speech and action confuses enemies and us, then it is unimaginable that people would not struggle against such a person. Second, between socialist thought and capitalist thought, between materialist thought and idealist thought, there cannot be a sharp struggle. The process of ideological transformation of intellectuals is inseparable from the development of ideological struggle in the intellectual community. Since liberation, the Party-led ideological transformation movement and the criticism of idealist thought have had a great effect on the progress of intellectuals. In ideological struggle, it should be noted that a person's change in thought must go through his own self-awareness. Using rough methods to carry out ideological transformation cannot solve the problem. However, the phenomenon of using rough methods to deal with scientists and scientific issues has recently been discovered, and all places must pay attention to correcting it. In addition, some people, although they insist on their erroneous thoughts, as long as they do not oppose the people in speech and action and are even willing to use their knowledge and energy to work for the people, then, while criticizing their erroneous thoughts, we should still be patient and help them gradually awaken.

To help intellectuals achieve progress, it is important for Party leaders to have direct contact with them. Many intellectuals hope that we can give them more help and criticism in thought and politics, and they feel that we give them too little of this kind of help. Many intellectuals, not only have difficulty contacting the leading comrades of local Party committees, but even living with the committee members of their own units, they also have few opportunities to talk. They say that we "use them more, help them less," or "only use them, not help them." Some people say that our cadres only find them for three things: (1) transferring work; (2) explaining history; (3) making mistakes. These criticisms are sharp and should be noted. We should include having thought and political talks with them, giving them comrade-like criticism, in the plan for transforming intellectuals. Organizing some symposiums to exchange views with them is something they welcome, and this should be held regularly in the future. In addition, according to specific circumstances, some non-Party intellectuals should be invited to attend certain Party group meetings and branch meetings, so that they can further understand the Party's intentions and accept the Party's education.

To help the progress of intellectuals, in addition to relying on Communist Party members, we must also organize the approximately 40% of progressives in the ranks of intellectuals to participate in work. The Youth League, trade unions, and various democratic parties have done a lot of work in this regard in the past few years, and in the future, they should use their strength more systematically.

Because we have more experience in transforming intellectuals and have the support of progressive forces, and because general intellectuals are increasingly deeply influenced by socialist education in the rapid development of the motherland, their progress will certainly be faster than in the past few years. As long as every unit in the country formulates plans for the transformation of intellectuals, including the seven-year plan from 1956 to 1962 and the annual plan, and implements them persistently, then we will definitely be able to achieve the goal that by the end of the second five-year plan, progressive elements who actively struggle for socialism and accept the basic views of Marxism-Leninism will account for more than three-quarters of high-level intellectuals, and backward elements will be reduced to about 5%.

Regarding the work of recruiting Party members from intellectuals, plans should also be formulated. Now there are many progressive intellectuals who want to join the Party. For example, the Engineering and Technical Personnel of the Non-Ferrous Metallurgy Design Institute of the Ministry of Heavy Industry have a total of 1,920 people, with 605 applying to join the Party, accounting for 31.5%. There are 291 lecturers and above in six colleges and universities in Tianjin, with 106 applying to join the Party, accounting for 36.4%. There are 131 researchers in the North China Agricultural Science Research Institute, with 53 applying to join the Party, accounting for 40%. However, in the past few years, we have rarely recruited Party members from them, which is a tendency of exclusivism. This tendency must be corrected. Of course, we must pay attention to strictly recruiting Party members according to Party member conditions, but we can believe that among these applicants, there are many who meet the conditions of Party members. Considering the increase of progressive forces among high-level intellectuals and the continuous influx of new forces into the ranks of high-level intellectuals, we believe that planning to make Party members account for about one-third of the total number of high-level intellectuals by 1962 is appropriate.

By implementing the above plans, the ideological and political situation of the intellectual community in our country will have a fundamental change. We will be able to basically complete the specific historical task of transforming intellectuals in the transitional period of our country. At that time, intellectuals, like all people, will still need to continuously transform themselves in learning and practice and strive to advance to higher progressive standards on a new level. However, that will be a task of a permanent nature.

To adapt to the rapid development of national construction, our intellectual community must be expanded in quantity and improved in professional level.

The scientific and cultural strength of our country is currently much smaller and much lower in quality than that of the Soviet Union and other major countries, which is very inconsistent with the needs of our socialist country with a population of six hundred million. We must catch up quickly and strive to expand and improve our scientific and cultural strength as soon as possible, and catch up with the world's advanced level in a not too long time. This is a great combat task for our Party and the entire intellectual community and the people of the country.

We often say that our science and culture are backward, but we do not often study where exactly we are backward. Comrades! I want to talk a little more about scientific issues here, not only because science is a decisive factor in our national defense, economy, and culture, but also because world science has made particularly great and rapid progress in the past twenty or thirty years, and these progress have left us far behind in scientific development.

Modern science and technology are advancing by leaps and bounds. The production process is gradually realizing comprehensive mechanization, comprehensive automation, and remote control, thereby raising labor productivity to an unprecedented level. Various high-temperature, high-pressure, high-speed, and ultra-high-temperature, ultra-high-pressure, ultra-high-speed machines are being designed and produced. Land, water, and air transportation machines are improving in range and speed, and high-speed aircraft have already exceeded the speed of sound. The progress in technology requires various materials with new special properties, so various new metal and alloy materials, as well as materials artificially synthesized by chemical methods, are being continuously produced to meet these new needs. The production technology and process specifications of various industries are changing day by day, ensuring the further acceleration and intensification of the production process, the most thorough utilization of the useful components of resources, the maximum saving of raw materials, and the continuous improvement of product quality.

The highest peak of the new developments in science and technology is the utilization of atomic energy. Atomic energy provides mankind with an incredibly powerful new source of energy, opening up a vast prospect for the renewal of various fields of science. At the same time, due to the progress of electronics and other sciences, the emergence of electronic automatic control machines (such as electronic computers that can perform 15,000 mathematical calculations per second, etc.) has begun to be able to conditionally replace a part of specific mental labor, just as other machines replace physical labor, thereby greatly improving the level of automation technology. These latest achievements make mankind face the dawn of a new scientific and technological and industrial revolution. This revolution, as Comrade Bulganin has said, "in terms of its significance, far exceeds the industrial revolution brought about by the appearance of steam and electricity."

We must catch up with this world's advanced scientific level. We must remember that when we catch up, others are also advancing rapidly. Therefore, we must work hard in this area. Only by mastering the most advanced science can we have a solid national defense, a powerful and advanced economic force, and sufficient conditions to defeat imperialist countries together with the Soviet Union and other people's democratic countries, whether in peaceful competition or in wars of aggression launched by the enemy.

It is currently difficult to accurately estimate how long it will take for our science to catch up with the world's advanced level. However, we must now propose such a task, that is, to make our most urgently needed scientific fields approach the world's advanced level by the end of the third five-year plan, and make the latest achievements of foreign countries quickly reach us through our own efforts. With this foundation, we can further solve the problem of catching up with the world's level.

To complete this great task, we must first break away from the lack of national self-confidence and dependence. "China's backwardness in science cannot be changed immediately, and it must rely on Soviet aid." It is true that we need to rely on Soviet aid to change our backwardness. However, how should we use this aid? One way is to have no overall plan, treating each problem as it arises, and asking the Soviet Union for help when encountering a problem. Those sent to study in the Soviet Union are not scientists, but mostly high school graduates. The result is that they can never get rid of the state of dependence and imitation, and the burden on the Soviet scientific community is indefinitely increased, hindering the planned and rapid development of Chinese science, which affects the speed of growth of the scientific strength, economic strength, and defensive strength of the entire socialist camp. Another way is to make an overall plan, distinguish between urgent and non-urgent, and systematically use the latest achievements of Soviet science to catch up with the Soviet level as soon as possible. This means that, in addition to asking for Soviet aid for urgent tasks and learning technology from Soviet aid, for all the fields in which Chinese science needs to learn from the Soviet Union, we should systematically send relatively mature scientific talents to study in the Soviet Union, or invite Soviet experts to help us establish the foundation of scientific research in China, so that we can quickly start continuous research and cadre training at the level that the Soviet Union has already reached. In this way, we can most effectively and reasonably use Soviet aid to promote the planned development of Chinese science, making it possible for the two countries' scientific undertakings to establish a cooperative relationship as soon as possible, which is to promote the growth of the scientific strength, economic strength, and defensive strength of the entire socialist camp. The latter method is the only correct method that has been repeatedly suggested by scientists of the two countries and that we should adopt.

To systematically improve the scientific level of our country, we must also break away from the short-sighted tendency. In theoretical work and technical work, in long-term needs and current needs, the allocation of forces should maintain an appropriate proportion and form a correct division of labor and cooperation to avoid neglecting any aspect. In the past few years, various work in our country has just begun, and we have invested more strength in current needs and technical work, while paying less attention to long-term needs and theoretical work, which is understandable. However, if we do not pay timely attention to strengthening long-term needs and theoretical work, we will make a big mistake. Without a certain amount of theoretical scientific research as a foundation, it is impossible to have fundamental progress and innovation in technology. However, the growth of theoretical strength is always slower than the growth of technical strength, and the effect of theoretical work is generally indirect and not easy to see immediately. Precisely because of this, many comrades still have a short-sighted tendency, they are not willing to invest the necessary strength in scientific research, and often require scientists to solve relatively simple technical application and production operation problems. Of course, theory should not be separated from practice, and any "theoretical research" that is separated from practice is what we must oppose, but the current main tendency is the neglect of theoretical research. This situation not only exists in natural sciences but also in social sciences. Currently, the strength we allocate in social sciences is even weaker than that in natural sciences, which is very inconsistent with the needs of our country. For example, the members of the Chinese Academy of Sciences in the field of natural sciences are 172 people, and currently, more than half of them can actually use most of their time for scientific work; in the field of social sciences, there are 51 people, but currently, only a few of them can actually use most of their time for scientific research work!

The above two tendencies are interrelated and mutually reinforcing. We must thoroughly correct these incorrect tendencies and change these situations that are not conducive to the development of science.

The State Council has now entrusted the State Planning Commission to be responsible, together with relevant departments, to formulate a long-term plan for scientific development from 1956 to 1967 within three months. When formulating this long-term plan, we must, according to what is possible and necessary, introduce the most advanced achievements of world science to the scientific departments, national defense departments, production departments, and educational departments of our country as soon as possible, and make up for the scientific and technological fields that are most lacking in our scientific community and most urgently needed for national construction as soon as possible, so that in 12 years, the scientific and technological level of these fields in our country can approach that of the Soviet Union and other major countries.

What is the fastest and most effective way to achieve this goal?

Such a path is: first, according to the fields we most urgently need, quickly dispatch several groups of experts, outstanding scientific workers, and outstanding university graduates to the Soviet Union and other countries for one to two years of internship or as graduate students, and upon their return, immediately establish the foundation for the development of these sciences and technologies in the Academy of Sciences and various government departments, and train a large number of new cadres. At the same time, according to the needs, send people to intern and study every year. Second, for a part of the disciplines, invite several groups of experts from the Soviet Union and other relevant countries to be responsible for helping us establish scientific research institutions in the Academy of Sciences and relevant departments in the shortest time, train cadres, or cooperate with the Chinese scientific community. Third, systematically organize a large number of scientific workers and technicians to learn from the Soviet experts currently in China, treating them as mentors rather than ordinary workers. In the process of construction and production of the 156 projects aided by the Soviet Union in China, systematically organize a large number of technicians to study and master the new technical principles, and quickly pass them on. Fourth, concentrate the most outstanding scientific strength and the most outstanding university graduates in scientific research. Use great efforts to strengthen the Chinese Academy of Sciences, making it the locomotive for leading the improvement of the scientific level and cultivating new forces nationwide. Fifth, the scientific strength in various colleges and universities accounts for the vast majority of the national scientific strength, and must, under the guidance of the national scientific development plan, vigorously develop scientific research work and train a large number of new scientific and technological forces that meet modern standards. Sixth, the various departments of the government, especially the departments of geology, industry, agriculture, water conservancy, transportation, national defense, and health, should quickly establish and strengthen the necessary research institutions, reasonably divide labor and cooperate with the Academy of Sciences, jointly expand the scientific community, and be responsible for introducing the latest achievements of world science into practical applications in a planned and systematic way, so as to equip our country's various aspects with the world's latest technology as soon as possible.

To seriously, rather than just talk about, march towards modern science, we must seize the time. A year's time is very easy to waste in empty talk and indecision. Therefore, the Central Committee requires: by the end of April this year, we must determine the long-term plan for scientific development, the specific plans for this year and next year that are suitable for this long-term plan, and the first batch of scientific strength that needs to be immediately mobilized to implement this long-term plan and the plans for this year and next year (including the list of scientific personnel to be dispatched abroad, the number of Soviet experts to be hired, the number of people to be transferred from other positions to scientific research positions, and the main personnel list, etc.), and strive to implement the dispatch and transfer plans by the end of June this year as much as possible. Regarding the plan to expand scientific research work and expand the cultivation of scientific strength in colleges and universities nationwide, it must be implemented after this summer vacation.

To implement the plan to march towards science, we must prepare all necessary conditions for the development of scientific research. Here, the most important thing is to ensure that scientists have the necessary books, archives, technical materials, and other working conditions. We must increase the book fees of various research institutions and colleges and universities and use them reasonably, strengthen the work of libraries, archives, and museums, greatly improve the work of importing foreign books and periodicals, and rationally allocate existing books and periodicals. We must expand foreign language teaching and expand the translation of important foreign books.

The above-mentioned principles generally also apply to other departments of culture and education. All cultural and educational departments should formulate development plans from 1956 to 1967 and adopt the most effective measures to implement them.

Our plan to develop scientific and cultural strength must be a plan that is both improved and expanded, because not only is our country a large country, which requires a certain number to meet the needs of various aspects, but quality generally can only be produced on the basis of a certain number.

To expand the scientific and cultural strength of our country, we must first increase the number of students in colleges and universities according to the plan, and we must also pay attention to cultivating existing general intellectuals and continuously improving their professional level. They are not only an extremely important reserve force and collaborator for high-level intellectuals but also, as mentioned earlier, are much more numerous than high-level intellectuals; they are distributed in various regions and departments of the country, shouldering a huge responsibility for national construction. The central departments and various provinces and municipalities must formulate special plans to help their further studies, so that their professional abilities can be rapidly improved, and the outstanding elements among them can be promoted to the ranks of high-level intellectuals.

Our Party is successfully solving the problems of agricultural cooperation and handicraft cooperation, and is successfully solving the problem of transforming capitalist industry and commerce. The whole Party and the working people of the whole country are striving to complete the five-year plan ahead of schedule and over-fulfill it. The people of the whole country are full of confidence in victory and the spirit of struggle. With the surging tide of socialist economic construction, a high tide of cultural construction will surely appear. At this time, we comprehensively solve a series of problems about intellectuals, which is undoubtedly very necessary for our socialist transformation and socialist construction.

In the tasks we have proposed regarding intellectuals, it cannot be said that there are no difficulties. However, these difficulties will not be more difficult than transforming five hundred million peasants and transforming the capitalist industry and commerce of the whole country, and will not be more difficult than completing the first five-year plan. In the past six years, our Party has achieved great success in leading intellectuals and leading scientific and cultural undertakings. Now we are more experienced and have more ways to do it, and on the basis of past experience, our Party will definitely be able to lead intellectuals to achieve new and greater victories in scientific and cultural undertakings. The idea that the Party cannot lead intellectuals to engage in scientific and cultural construction is groundless.

The key is to learn well. Whether it is the central departments or various regions, they must learn to better lead intellectuals and lead scientific and cultural undertakings. We should not think that since we are Communist Party members, we will naturally be able to lead intellectuals to engage in cultural construction, and we will naturally not make mistakes. This is a very dangerous idea. In some places, some comrades are precisely because of holding such an arrogant attitude that the Party's work has suffered losses. We must adopt an honest attitude of "knowing is knowing, not knowing is not knowing" towards any problem, and we must not pretend to know when we do not know, but we must change from not knowing to knowing. Our Party must cultivate a large number of cadres who are proficient in the knowledge of various departments of science and culture. As long as we seriously delve into it, we will definitely be able to learn it.

In the report, many tasks need to be solved by the central government in a unified manner. Regarding these problems, we propose the following division of labor:

Regarding various administrative issues about intellectuals, because they need to be uniformly regulated and managed, the State Council plans to set up a China Expert Bureau to be responsible for handling them. However, before this institution is established, relevant departments should immediately start solving various problems about intellectuals according to the instructions of the central government. After this institution is established, various departments should not reduce their direct responsibility for handling them, because the Expert Bureau generally only handles problems that are not convenient for a single department to handle alone. The Expert Bureau is responsible for unified planning, unified adjustment, and unified supervision and promotion of various administrative issues about high-level intellectuals. For the places where various departments handle high-level intellectual issues improperly, the Expert Bureau has the right to correct them according to certain procedures.

Regarding various political issues about intellectuals, regarding continuing ideological transformation and handling related counter-revolutionary elements in intellectuals, they are directly responsible by various relevant departments, and the Central Propaganda Department is responsible for unified supervision. The Central Propaganda Department should regularly check the implementation of the central policies on intellectuals by various departments and regions, check their work plans and the implementation of plans, overcome the shortcomings in their work, promote the advanced experience in their work, and timely report problems and suggestions to the central government.

The work of recruiting Party members from intellectuals is the responsibility of the Central Organization Department. The work of democratic parties among intellectuals is the responsibility of the Central United Front Work Department. The trade union work among intellectuals is the responsibility of the All-China Federation of Trade Unions. However, when solving important problems in these works, they should be coordinated with the Central Propaganda Department.

To strengthen leadership, handle, and supervise issues related to intellectuals, party committees and various departments at all levels should respectively designate appropriate institutions to take on the responsibility of regular work, and regularly hold some special meetings to continuously exchange experiences and improve the work situation.

Comrades! We believe that through our work, intellectuals will be more united around the Party and will be more active in contributing their strength to the great cause of socialism. The alliance formed by workers, peasants, and intellectuals in the cause of socialism will be more solid and powerful with the development of our work. Relying on this alliance, we will definitely be able to build our country into a completely modernized, prosperous, and powerful socialist industrial power in a not too long time, and definitely be able to realize Comrade Mao Zedong's great callâ€”"We will appear before the world as a nation with a high level of culture."
