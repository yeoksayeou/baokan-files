Title: The Underground Sea of Karaganda, Soviet Union
Author:
Date: 1956-01-13
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, Moscow, December 12th: Soviet geologists recently discovered a vast underground water source in the Karaganda coal region of Kazakhstan. Its area covers 1,200 square kilometers, and its water volume is approximately equivalent to the Rybinsk Reservoir on the Volga River, and is called the "Karaganda Sea." The discovery of this underground sea provides the water-scarce northeastern and central Kazakhstan with a unique but huge freshwater reservoir, providing excellent conditions for solving the local water supply problem.

Currently, ten water-spraying wells have been successfully drilled, each of which can spray 20 liters of water per second. During the Sixth Five-Year Plan (1956-1960), the water from this "Karaganda Sea" will be able to fully guarantee the industrial and drinking water needs of this coal region.

According to research results, this "sea" was formed approximately 175 million years ago.

### 苏联卡拉干达的地下海

1956-01-13
第4版()
专栏：

    苏联卡拉干达的地下海
    据新华社莫斯科12日电  苏联地质学家最近在哈萨克卡拉干达煤区发现了一个巨大的地下水源，它的面积有1，200平方公里，水量约和伏尔加河上的律平斯克人工海相当，被称为“卡拉干达海”。这个地下海的发现为缺水的哈萨克东北部和中部找到了唯一的，然而是巨大的淡水库，给解决当地的供水问题提供了良好的条件。
    现在，已经钻凿成功了十个喷水孔，每个喷水孔每秒钟能喷出20公升的水。在第六个五年计划（1956—1960年）里，这个“卡拉干达海”的水将能完全保证这个煤区的工业用水和饮用水的需要。
    根据研究结果，这个“海”是在大约17，500万年以前形成的。
