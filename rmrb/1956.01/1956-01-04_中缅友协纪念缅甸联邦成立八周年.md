Title: Sino-Burmese Friendship Association Commemorates the Eighth Anniversary of the Founding of the Union of Burma
Author:
Date: 1956-01-04
Page: 4
　　Xinhua News Agency report: The Sino-Burmese Friendship Association held a memorial meeting on the evening of the 3rd to celebrate the eighth anniversary of the founding of the Union of Burma.

　　Zheng Zhenduo, President of the Sino-Burmese Friendship Association, delivered a speech at the meeting, extending warm congratulations to the Burmese people and the Government of the Union of Burma on behalf of the Sino-Burmese Friendship Association and the Chinese people. Zheng Zhenduo said: China and Burma have been close neighbors since ancient times. After Burma and the People's Republic of China were successively established, the friendly relations between our two countries entered a new historical stage. The mutual visits of the Prime Ministers of China and Burma in 1954 further deepened the mutual understanding and friendship between the people of China and Burma. Zheng Zhenduo said: In the past two years, economic and cultural exchanges between China and Burma have achieved good development. Zheng Zhenduo pointed out that the friendly exchanges and economic and cultural ties between China and Burma are becoming wider and deeper, which is a vivid manifestation of the continuous development and consolidation of the friendly relations between the two countries, and it encourages the people of the two countries to move towards the common goal of friendship, peace and happiness.

　　U Maung Maung Toe, Chargé d'Affaires ad interim of the Embassy of the Union of Burma in China, was invited to attend the memorial meeting and delivered a speech expressing his gratitude. He pointed out that the Prime Ministers of China and Burma, together with the Prime Ministers and Foreign Ministers of other countries, participated in the Bandung Conference, made contributions to the understanding between Asian and African countries and to the easing of international tensions, and expanded and strengthened the basis of the Five Principles of Peaceful Coexistence in the Joint Statement of the Prime Ministers of China and Burma. The development of relations between China and Burma in religion, culture, trade and politics has further developed and consolidated Sino-Burmese friendship. U Maung Maung Toe wished Sino-Burmese friendship a growing development and the Chinese people prosperity and strength.

### 中缅友协纪念缅甸联邦成立八周年

1956-01-04
第4版()
专栏：

　　中缅友协纪念缅甸联邦成立八周年
    新华社讯　中缅友好协会3日晚举行纪念会，庆祝缅甸联邦成立八周年。
    中缅友好协会会长郑振铎在会上致词，他代表中缅友好协会和中国人民，向缅甸人民和缅甸联邦政府表示热烈的祝贺。郑振铎说：中国和缅甸自古以来就是亲密的邻邦。在缅甸联邦和中华人民共和国相继成立以后，我们两国的友好关系进入了一个新的历史阶段。1954年中缅两国总理的互相访问，进一步加深了中缅两国人民的相互了解和友谊。郑振铎说：这两年来，中缅两国间的经济、文化交流获得了很好的发展。郑振铎指出，中缅两国之间的友好往来和经济、文化联系正在愈来愈广泛，愈来愈深入，这是两国友好关系在不断地发展和巩固的生动表现，它鼓舞着两国人民朝着友谊、和平与幸福的共同目标前进。
    缅甸联邦驻华大使馆临时代办吴茂茂铎应邀出席了纪念会，他在会上致词表示感谢。他指出，中缅两国总理同其他国家的总理和外交部长一同参加了万隆会议，对亚非国家间的谅解、对于国际紧张局势的缓和作出了贡献，并且使中缅两国总理联合声明中的和平共处五项原则的基础得到扩大和加强。中缅两国在宗教、文化、贸易和政治方面的关系的发展，使中缅友好得到进一步发展和巩固。吴茂茂铎祝中缅友好日益发展，祝中国人民康乐富强。
