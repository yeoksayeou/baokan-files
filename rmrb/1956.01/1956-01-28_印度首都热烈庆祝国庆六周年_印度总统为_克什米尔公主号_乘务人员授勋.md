Title: Indian Capital Celebrates Sixth Anniversary of Republic; Indian President Awards Crew of "Kashmir Princess"
Author:
Date: 1956-01-28
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, New Delhi, January 27th: Celebrations marking the sixth anniversary of the founding of the Republic of India were held enthusiastically in many parts of the country on January 26th.

    A parade was held in Delhi, the Indian capital, on this day. People flocked to Rajpath, the venue for the parade, early in the morning. It is estimated that over 500,000 people came here.

    Indian Prime Minister Nehru, senior Indian government officials, high-ranking military officers, members of the Indian Parliament, many foreign dignitaries, and diplomatic envoys from various countries to India also arrived early in the morning to wait in the specially designated enclosure on Rajpath to watch the parade.

    Indian President Prasad ascended the reviewing stand at 9:30 a.m.

    Then a special award ceremony was held. President Prasad presented the Ashoka Chakra Class I medal, awarded posthumously to Captain Jatar, the late pilot of the Indian aircraft "Kashmir Princess" which was sabotaged during the Asian-African Conference, to Captain Jatar's wife.

    After the award ceremony, the military parade began. Following the troops was a literary and artistic contingent led by an elephant team adorned in gorgeous golden elephant costumes, about 4,000 children dressed in various costumes, and youth song and dance troupes from all over India wearing their own national costumes. Many workers from the capital and farmers from nearby rural areas also participated in the parade. In the evening, colorful fireworks were set off at 11 different locations in Old and New Delhi.

    In the evening, Indian President Prasad held a National Day banquet at the Presidential Palace.

Xinhua News Agency, New Delhi, January 26th: At a special award ceremony on January 26th, the President of the Republic of India, Prasad, posthumously awarded and conferred Ashoka Chakra Class I, II, and III medals to Captain D. K. Jatar, the pilot of the "Kashmir Princess" aircraft, and other crew members who were sabotaged en route to Jakarta carrying staff members and journalists from the Chinese delegation attending the Asian-African Conference.

    On the same day, the families of Captain Jatar and other Indian victims, as well as Captain Gac Khsit, Kanik, and Pathak, who survived the "Kashmir Princess" plane crash, received telegrams from Premier Zhou Enlai of the State Council of the People's Republic of China, in which Premier Zhou Enlai expressed his respect for the victims and extended his condolences to the survivors.

### 印度首都热烈庆祝国庆六周年  印度总统为“克什米尔公主号”乘务人员授勋

1956-01-28
第4版()
专栏：

    印度首都热烈庆祝国庆六周年
    印度总统为“克什米尔公主号”乘务人员授勋
    新华社新德里27日电　印度全国许多地方在1月26日热烈地庆祝了印度共和国成立六周年国庆。
    这天在印度首都德里举行了检阅。人们一清早就涌向进行检阅的地方拉吉路。涌向这里来的估计约有50多万人。
    印度总理尼赫鲁、印度政府高级官员、高级军官、印度国会议员，和许多外国贵宾、各国驻印外交使节也从早晨就到在拉吉路特设的围圈里等候观看检阅。
    印度总统普拉沙德在9点30分登上检阅台。
    然后举行了特别授勋仪式。普拉沙德总统把授予在亚非国家会议时因遭到破坏而失事的印度飞机“克什米尔公主号”的已故机长贾塔尔上尉的一级阿输迦宝轮勋章给予贾塔尔上尉的妻子。
    授勋仪式结束后，开始了军事检阅。跟在军队后面的是在披着华丽的金色象服的象队为前导的文艺大队和4，000名左右穿着各色服装的儿童，以及来自印度各地的、穿着自己的民族服装的青年歌舞团。首都的许多工人和附近农村中的农民也参加了游行。在晚上，在旧德里和新德里的11个不同地点放射了五彩缤纷的焰火。
    晚间，印度总统普拉沙德在总统府举行了国庆宴会。
    据新华社新德里26日电　印度共和国总统普拉沙德1月26日在一次特别授勋典礼上，把一级、二级和三级阿输迦宝轮勋章分别追赠和授给在载送出席亚非会议的中国代表团工作人员和记者前往雅加达途中遭到破坏的“克什米尔公主号”飞机机长德·克·贾塔尔上尉和其他乘务人员。
    同一天，贾塔尔上尉和其他印度死难人员的家属以及在“克什米尔公主号”飞机失事事件中得救生还的加克西特上尉、卡尼克和帕塔克都收到了中华人民共和国国务院总理周恩来的来电，周恩来总理在电报中对死难者表示敬意，对生还者表示嘉慰。
