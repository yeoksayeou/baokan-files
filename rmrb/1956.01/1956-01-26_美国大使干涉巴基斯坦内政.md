Title: U.S. Ambassador Interferes in Pakistan's Internal Affairs
Author:
Date: 1956-01-26
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, Delhi, January 25th: TASS reports that U.S. Ambassador to Pakistan, Mr. Hildreth, delivered a radio address in Dacca (East Pakistan) on January 23rd that essentially constituted direct interference in Pakistan's internal affairs.

Hildreth, clearly uneasy about the movement in Pakistan demanding a neutral policy, said: "Now in Pakistan, the question is often raised whether a neutral policy would not be wiser for Asian countries..." The U.S. ambassador attempted to give Pakistanis a lesson in foreign policy, saying that raising such questions "is inconsistent with the patriotic citizens of Pakistan, a country that is both proud and free."

Hildreth openly stated that if Pakistan adopted a neutral policy, it could not expect to receive "assistance" from the United States.

### 美国大使干涉巴基斯坦内政

1956-01-26
第4版()
专栏：

    美国大使干涉巴基斯坦内政
    据新华社25日讯　塔斯社德里讯：美国驻巴基斯坦大使希尔德莱斯1月23日在达卡（东巴基斯坦）发表了实质是直接干涉巴基斯坦内政的广播演说。
    希尔德莱斯显然对在巴基斯坦进行的要求实行中立政策的运动感到不安，他说：“现在在巴基斯坦常常提出实行中立政策对亚洲国家不是更明智些吗这个问题……。”这个美国大使企图给巴基斯坦人上一堂外交政策课，他说，提出这类问题“是同巴基斯坦这种又自豪又自由的国家的爱国公民不相称的”。
    希尔德莱斯公然表示，如果巴基斯坦实行中立政策，那时它就不能指望获得美国的“援助”。
