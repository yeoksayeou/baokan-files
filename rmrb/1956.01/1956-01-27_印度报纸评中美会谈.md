Title: Indian Newspapers Comment on Sino-U.S. Talks
Author:
Date: 1956-01-27
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, New Delhi, January 24th: The progress of the Sino-U.S. ambassadorial talks in Geneva is attracting increasing attention from Indian newspapers.

In comparing the attitudes of China and the United States towards the talks, the "Amrita Bazar Patrika" published an editorial on January 22nd, saying: "Compared with the United States, China can convincingly prove its sincere desire for peace, because all proposals advocating peaceful resolution of the issue through direct negotiations at the Foreign Minister level have come from Beijing and have not yet been responded to by the other party."

The "National Herald" editorial on January 21st mocked the contradiction between the United States demanding that China abandon the right to use force to liberate Taiwan and the U.S. government's policy of military aggression. The newspaper said: "The United States cannot talk about abandoning the use of force when Dulles threatens to launch an atomic attack on China and sends more weapons and personnel to Taiwan and South Korea." The newspaper also said: "The international aspect of this issue is that the island is now under U.S. military occupation, and the purpose of the Sino-U.S. talks should be how to end this illegal occupation."

### 印度报纸评中美会谈

1956-01-27
第4版()
专栏：

    印度报纸评中美会谈
    新华社新德里24日电　日内瓦中美大使级会谈的进展情况受到印度报纸越来越多的注意。
    在对照中美两国对会谈的态度的时候，1月22日的“甘露市场报”发表社论说：“同美国相比，中国能够令人信服地证明它希望和平的真诚愿望，因为一切主张通过外交部长级的直接谈判来和平解决问题的建议都是从北京来的，而且到现在为止还没有得到另一方的响应。”
    “国民先驱报”1月21日的社论嘲笑了美国要中国放弃为了解放台湾而使用武力的权利同美国政府执行的军事侵略政策之间的矛盾。这家报纸说：“当杜勒斯扬言要对中国发动原子攻击并且把更多的武器和兵员源源送到台湾和南朝鲜的时候，美国是不能谈论放弃武力的。”这家报纸还说：“这个问题的国际方面是这个岛现在处在美国的军事占领下，中美会谈的目的应当是如何结束这种非法的占领。”
