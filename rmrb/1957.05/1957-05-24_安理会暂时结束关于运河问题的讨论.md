Title: Security Council Temporarily Ends Discussion on Canal Issue
Author:
Date: 1957-05-24
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, May 22nd dispatch from New York: The United Nations Security Council temporarily ended its discussion on the Suez Canal issue on May 21st. The French representative, who requested this discussion, did not present any proposals at the meeting. It is reported that this was because French Foreign Minister Pineau had previously met with U.S. Secretary of State Dulles, and Dulles opposed submitting proposals, advocating instead for seeking a so-called "solution" to the Suez Canal issue through other channels.

The focus of the day's discussion remained France's demand for renewed negotiations on the Suez Canal issue. Representatives from Australia, Colombia, and Chiang Kai-shek's faction in Taiwan supported France's demand. Iraq and Egypt expressed opposition.

### 安理会暂时结束关于运河问题的讨论

1957-05-24
第6版()
专栏：

    安理会暂时结束关于运河问题的讨论
    据新华社22日讯　纽约消息：联合国安全理事会5月21日暂时结束了关于苏伊士运河问题的讨论。要求进行这次讨论的法国代表在会上没有提出任何提案，据报道，这是因为法国外交部长比诺在会前曾经同美国国务卿杜勒斯进行会谈，杜勒斯反对提出提案，而主张用其他途径来寻求苏伊士运河问题的所谓“解决”。
    这一天的讨论中心仍然是法国关于重开苏伊士运河问题谈判的要求。澳大利亚、哥伦比亚和台湾蒋介石分子的代表支持法国的要求。伊拉克和埃及表示反对。
