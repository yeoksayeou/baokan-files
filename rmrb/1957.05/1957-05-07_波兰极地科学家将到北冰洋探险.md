    Polish Polar Scientists to Explore the Arctic Ocean

    (Our Dispatch) During the International Geophysical Year of 1957 to 1958, Poland will send a polar expedition to Spitsbergen Island (belonging to Norway) in the Arctic Ocean to conduct scientific observations. Last August, Polish scientists risked great danger and overcame floating ice and seals to establish a scientific observation base there in advance.

    Spitsbergen Island is located at 80 degrees north latitude and 10 degrees east longitude, which is one of the areas where precise observations will be carried out during this International Geophysical Year. Spitsbergen Island is the largest island in the Svalbard archipelago in the Arctic Ocean. The entire area of the Svalbard archipelago is approximately 25,000 square miles. Spitsbergen Island has always been a place for navigators to hunt foxes, seals, and polar bears. Since the seventeenth century, people have tried to spend the Arctic night here, but their plans ultimately failed. Even now, it cannot be said to be a safe place. Observers living in tents need to be vigilant at all times and need to have great physical and mental resilience.

    Conducting scientific research here also has another important significance for Poland. Because three-quarters of Poland's entire territory is composed of strata from the pre-glacial period. In order to further study the geological composition of Poland, it is necessary to correspondingly study the situation in the Arctic region. Polish geologists had already formed expeditions several times from 1932 to 1938 to conduct geological research in the Arctic region. (Attached picture)

### 波兰极地科学家将到北冰洋探险

1957-05-07
第5版()
专栏：

    波兰极地科学家将到北冰洋探险
    本报讯　在1957年到1958年国际地球物理年中，波兰将派出一个极地探险队到北冰洋中的斯匹次培根岛（属挪威）去进行科学观测工作。去年8月间，波兰科学家们曾经冒着极大的危险，战胜浮冰和海豹，在这里预先建立了一个科学观察基地。
    斯匹次培根岛位于北纬八十度和东经十度的地方，这里是本届国际地球物理年中要进行精确观察的地区之一。斯匹次培根岛是北冰洋中斯瓦尔巴特群岛中最大的一个岛。斯瓦尔巴特群岛的整个面积约为二万五千平方英里。其中的斯匹次培根岛一向是航海家们猎取野狐、海豹和北极熊的地方。从十七世纪起，就曾经有人在这里试图度过北极夜，但是他们的打算最后都失败了。即使是现在，这里也绝不能说是一个安全的地方。居住在帐篷里的观测人员需要时刻警惕着，并且需要具有身体上和精神上的极大坚韧力。
    在这里进行科学考察工作，对于波兰来说还有着另外一种重要的意义。因为波兰现在整个领土的四分之三，都是由前冰河时期的岩层所组成的。为了对波兰本土的地质构成作出进一步的研究，就有必要相应地研究北极地区的情况。波兰的地质学家们早在1932年至1938年，就曾数次组成探险队深入北极地区进行了地质研究工作。（附图片）
