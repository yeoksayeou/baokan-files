Title: Demanding Withdrawal from Military Blocs: Pakistani Hunger Strikers Persist in Struggle; Some Political Parties Decide to Launch Support Movement
Author:
Date: 1957-05-24
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, Karachi, 23rd -- According to the "Achievement Report," representatives of some Pakistani parties (including the Karachi Muslim League, the National Party, and the Islamic People's Party) have decided to launch a movement to support the hunger strikers' demands for Pakistan to withdraw from the Baghdad Pact and the Southeast Asia Treaty Organization.

According to reports, these representatives recently held a meeting on the sidewalk in front of the Presidential Palace and decided to establish a committee to launch this movement.

The hunger strike by five staff members of the Karachi Civil Liberties Committee began on May 7th. Later, the number of hunger strikers increased. They are continuing their hunger strike in front of the Presidential Palace.

According to newspaper reports, two hunger strikers were arrested by the police on May 21st while going to a printing factory to print leaflets and slogans.

### 要求退出军事集团  巴基斯坦绝食者坚持斗争  一些政党决发动支援运动

1957-05-24
第6版()
专栏：

    要求退出军事集团
    巴基斯坦绝食者坚持斗争
    一些政党决发动支援运动
    新华社卡拉奇23日电　据“成就报”报道，巴基斯坦一些党派（包括卡拉奇穆斯林联盟、国民党和伊斯兰人民党）的代表们决定发动一次运动，支持绝食者们要巴基斯坦退出巴格达条约和东南亚条约组织的要求。
    据报道，这些代表们最近在总统府前面的人行道上举行了一次会议，决定成立一个委员会来发动这个运动。
    卡拉奇公民自由委员会的五个工作人员的绝食是从5月7日开始的。后来绝食者的人数有增加。他们到目前为止还继续在总统府前面绝食。
    据报纸消息，有两位绝食者在5月21日去印刷厂印传单和标语的时候被警察逮捕了。
