Title: U.S. Senate Establishes Foreign Commerce Body to Study Trade with China
Author:
Date: 1957-05-25
Page: 5
Xinhua News Agency, May 23rd, Washington dispatch: At a time when the United States has repeatedly rejected its allies' demands to relax trade restrictions with China, Senator Magnuson, chairman of the Senate Commerce Committee, announced on May 22nd that the U.S. Senate recently established a Foreign Commerce Subcommittee to study the possibility of opening trade between the United States and China.

Magnuson himself is the chairman of this subcommittee. He said that the first task of the subcommittee is to understand "why the United States is out of step with the rest of the world" in prohibiting all trade with China.

Magnuson believes that the United States "cannot forever refrain from economic dealings with a government of several hundred million people simply because it dislikes its policies." He said: "Everyone else is going there, only we are not. In March, there were sixty-nine ships from all countries in the world except ours docked in Shanghai alone."

### 研究对华贸易问题  美参院成立对外商务机构

1957-05-25
第5版()
专栏：

    研究对华贸易问题
    美参院成立对外商务机构
    新华社23日讯　华盛顿消息：在美国一再拒绝它的盟国关于放宽对中国贸易要求的消息传来的时候，美国参议院商务委员会主席麦格纳逊5月22日宣布，美国参议院最近成立了一个对外商务小组委员会来研究打开美国和中国之间的贸易的可能性。
    麦格纳逊本人就是这个小组委员会的主任。他说，这个小组委员会的第一个任务是：了解美国在禁止同中国进行一切贸易方面“为什么步调不同世界其他国家一致”。
    麦格纳逊认为，美国“不能仅仅因为不喜欢这个有着好几亿人民的政府的政策”，就永远在经济上不和它往来。他说：“谁都到那里去，只有我们不去。在3月份，在上海一地就有除我国以外的世界各国的六十九艘船停泊过。”
