Title: Soviet Government Note to West German Government Suggests Negotiations Next Month to Improve Relations
Author:
Date: 1957-05-28
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, Moscow: The Soviet government sent a note to the West German government on May 23, inviting it to send a delegation to Moscow to begin negotiations on the development and improvement of relations between the two countries on June 15.

The Soviet government's note is a reply to the West German government's note of April 16, in which West Germany expressed its agreement with the Soviet government's proposal to hold negotiations on the development and improvement of relations between the two countries.

The Soviet government's reply pointed out that the Soviet government's starting point is that the negotiations will discuss the issues of bilateral relations mentioned in the correspondence between Soviet Premier Bulganin and West German Chancellor Adenauer, in particular, the conclusion of relevant agreements that can lay a solid foundation for expanding and fully developing trade, scientific and technological, and cultural ties between the two countries, as well as the conclusion of a consular agreement.

The Soviet government expressed its hope that the negotiations would result in agreements conducive to peaceful cooperation and mutual understanding between the two countries.

### 苏联政府照会西德政府  建议下月谈判改善关系

1957-05-28
第5版()
专栏：

    苏联政府照会西德政府
    建议下月谈判改善关系
    据新华社讯　据塔斯社莫斯科讯：苏联政府5月23日照会西德政府，邀请它派代表团到莫斯科来，以便在6月15日开始就发展和改善两国关系问题进行谈判。
    苏联政府的这个照会是对西德政府4月16日的照会的复照，西德的照会曾经表示同意苏联政府关于就发展和改善两国关系举行谈判的建议。
    苏联政府的复照指出，苏联政府的出发点是：在谈判时将讨论由苏联部长会议主席布尔加宁和西德总理阿登纳的来往信件中谈到的两国关系问题，特别是指缔结一些能为扩大和全面发展两国之间的贸易、科学技术和文化联系奠定巩固基础的有关协定以及缔结领事协定的问题。
    苏联政府表示希望这次谈判后能够缔结有助于两国之间和平合作和互相谅解的协定。
