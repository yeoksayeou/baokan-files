Title: Rejecting the Japanese Government's Demands, US Vows to Intensify Nuclear Weapons Testing; Norwegian Prime Minister Calls for an End to Atomic Warmongering
Author:
Date: 1957-05-03
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency dispatch: The US State Department submitted a note to the Japanese Embassy in the United States on April 30th, refusing to cease nuclear explosion tests.

    This note was a response to the Japanese government's request, made in mid-March, for the cessation of such tests. The US note stated that "the United States' nuclear explosion tests must continue in order to defend the free world."

    Kyodo News reported that the content of the note was similar to the views informally expressed by Howard P. Jones, Special Assistant to the Secretary of State, when the Japanese Chargé d'Affaires ad interim to the United States, Takeso Shimoda, submitted a memorandum on April 29th requesting the United States to stop testing nuclear weapons in Nevada. At that time, Jones told Shimoda that the United States, for the defense of Western countries including Japan, not only could not stop the tests, but would strengthen nuclear weapons testing.

    The US Atomic Energy Commission also announced on the same day that it would hold nine public nuclear weapons tests in Nevada, USA, between mid-May and September, allowing American journalists to observe.

    Although the United States and Britain have successively threatened to test nuclear weapons, the voices opposing this warmongering action are growing. Norwegian Prime Minister Gerhardsen delivered a speech at a mass rally in Oslo celebrating "May Day", calling on all countries to cease atomic and hydrogen bomb testing under full mutual supervision. He said that the terrible substances produced by these explosions are not restricted by national boundaries, and will harm capitalists and communists alike, regardless of race or religious belief.

    American chemist and Nobel Prize winner Linus Pauling gave a speech in Chicago on April 30th, opposing the continued nuclear explosion tests.

    Pauling believes that the impact of nuclear explosion tests may cause leukemia and may increase the number of infants born with deformities and mental retardation. Pauling said: "For political and humanitarian reasons, he strongly supports the United States, the Soviet Union, and Britain concluding an international agreement to stop nuclear explosion tests at the present time."

### 拒绝日本政府的要求  美国扬言要加紧试验核武器  挪威首相呼吁停止原子黩武行动

1957-05-03
第6版()
专栏：

    拒绝日本政府的要求
    美国扬言要加紧试验核武器
    挪威首相呼吁停止原子黩武行动
    本报综合报道  据新华社消息：美国国务院4月30日向日本驻美大使馆提出一个照会，拒绝停止进行原子核爆炸试验。
    这个照会是对日本政府在3月中旬向美国提出的要求停止进行这种试验的照会的答复。美国的照会里说，“为了保卫自由世界起见，美国的原子核爆炸试验必须继续进行下去”。
    共同社说，这项照会的内容和日本驻美国大使馆代办下田武三在4月29日把要求美国停止在内华达试验核武器的备忘录送交国务院时，国务卿特别助理霍华德·普·琼斯非正式表示的意见相似，当时琼斯告诉下田说，美国为了包括日本在内的西方国家的防务，不但不能停止试验，而且要加强核武器的试验。
    美国原子能委员会当天也宣布，它将在5月中旬到9月间在美国内华达州举行九次公开的核武器试验，允许美国记者去观看。
    尽管美国和英国先后扬言要试验核武器；但是反对这种黩武行动的呼声更日益扩大。挪威首相基哈德森在奥斯陆市庆祝“五一”节的群众大会上发表演说，要求各国在充分互相监督之下停止原子弹和氢弹的试验。他说，这种爆炸所产生的可怕的物质是不受国界限制的，不论是资本家和共产党人，也不论种族和宗教信仰的不同，同样都会受到危害。
    美国著名化学家、诺贝尔奖金得奖人保林4月30日在芝加哥发表演说时，反对继续进行原子核爆炸试验。
    保林认为，原子核爆炸试验的影响可能使人患白血病，可能使出生的婴孩患畸形和低能的人数增加。保林说：“为了政治和人道的理由，他强烈地赞成美国、苏联和英国缔结国际协定，在目前停止进行原子核爆炸试验。”
