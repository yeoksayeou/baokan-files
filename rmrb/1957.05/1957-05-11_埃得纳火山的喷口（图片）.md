Title: The Crater of Mount Etna (Image)
Author:
Date: 1957-05-11
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency Draft:
    The crater of Mount Etna (altitude 3,274 meters) in Sicily, southern Italy, is one of the most frequently erupting active volcanoes in history. In the past 2,500 years, it has ejected 100 to 150 cubic kilometers of lava, ash, and so on. Its largest eruption occurred in 1669, lasting four months and destroying many nearby villages. Mount Etna's central crater is 500 meters in diameter and 400 meters deep. This crater and the southeast crater began another eruption on April 19th of this year, spewing lava as high as 2,700 meters, and the eruption continues. This is Mount Etna during this eruption.

### 埃得纳火山的喷口（图片）

1957-05-11
第6版()
专栏：

    埃得纳火山的喷口
    意大利南部西西里岛的埃得纳火山（海拔三千二百七十四公尺）是历史上爆发次数最多的一个活火山，过去两千五百年之间所喷射出来的熔岩、灰尘等达一百至一百五十立方公里之多。它的最大一次爆发是在公元1669年，爆发继续了四个月，破坏了附近的许多乡村。埃得纳火山的中央喷火口直径五百公尺，深四百公尺，这个喷火口和东南的喷火口从今年4月19日又开始的一次爆发，喷射熔岩飞到二千七百多公尺高，现在还继续爆发。这是这次爆发的埃得纳火山。
                                    （新华社稿）
