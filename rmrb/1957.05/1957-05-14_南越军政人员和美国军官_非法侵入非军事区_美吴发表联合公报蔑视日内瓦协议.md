Title: South Vietnamese Military and Government Personnel and American Officers Illegally Invade the Demilitarized Zone; US-Ngo Publish Joint Communiqué Disregarding the Geneva Accords
Author:
Date: 1957-05-14
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, Hanoi, 13th: Vietnam News Agency reports: On May 9th, the delegation of the General Command of the Vietnam People's Army wrote to the International Commission of Vietnam, asking them to pay attention to the South Vietnamese military and government personnel’s violations of the agreement and illegal invasions of the Demilitarized Zone.

According to incomplete statistics, in April alone, 637 South Vietnamese military and government personnel, in 135 vehicles of various types, illegally invaded the southern part of the Demilitarized Zone. They inspected the situation in northern Vietnam along the Ben Hai River.

On April 11th and 27th alone, 327 South Vietnamese military and government personnel and American officers, in about 70 vehicles, illegally entered the southern part of the Demilitarized Zone, going all the way to the Hien Luong Bridge.

Xinhua News Agency, 12th: Washington dispatch: On May 11th, US President Dwight D. Eisenhower and South Vietnamese "President" Ngo Dinh Diem issued a joint communiqué stating that Eisenhower had assured Ngo Dinh Diem that the United States was willing to continue to "provide effective assistance" to South Vietnam.

The communiqué deliberately ignored the 1954 Geneva Accords and did not propose any concrete methods for the peaceful reunification of Vietnam.

The joint communiqué wantonly slandered the Democratic Republic of Vietnam and the People's Republic of China, claiming that China "constantly threatens" the "security of all free countries in Asia."

Ignoring the provisions of the Geneva Accords, Eisenhower and Ngo Dinh Diem reaffirmed in the joint communiqué that Article IV of the Southeast Asia Treaty regarding military action applied to South Vietnam.

According to United Press International, although the United States promised to continue to aid Ngo Dinh Diem, it "did not promise to increase aid."

### 南越军政人员和美国军官  非法侵入非军事区  美吴发表联合公报蔑视日内瓦协议

1957-05-14
第6版()
专栏：

    南越军政人员和美国军官
    非法侵入非军事区
    美吴发表联合公报蔑视日内瓦协议
    新华社河内13日电　据越南通讯社报道：5月9日，越南人民军总司令部代表团已经写信给越南国际委员会，要求他们注意南越军政人员破坏协议，非法侵入非军事区的事件。
    据不完全的统计，今年4月内，有六百三十七名南越军政人员乘坐一百三十五辆各式车辆非法侵入非军事区的南部，他们沿边海河视察了越南北方的情况。
    仅在4月11日和27日两天内，即有三百二十七名南越军政人员和美国军官，乘坐七十辆左右的车辆，非法进入非军事区的南部，一直走到贤良桥。
    据新华社12日讯　华盛顿消息：美国总统艾森豪威尔和南越“总统”吴庭艳5月11日，发表联合公报说，艾森豪威尔已经向吴庭艳保证，美国愿意继续向南越“提供有效的援助”。
    公报故意忽视1954年的日内瓦协议，没有提出和平统一越南的具体办法。
    联合公报对越南民主共和国和中华人民共和国滥肆诽谤，硬说中国“不断威胁”“亚洲所有自由国家的安全”。
    艾森豪威尔和吴庭艳不顾日内瓦协议的规定，在联合公报中重申东南亚条约关于采取军事行动的第四条适用于南越。
    据合众社透露，美国虽然答应继续援助吴庭艳，“但是没有答应增加援助”。
