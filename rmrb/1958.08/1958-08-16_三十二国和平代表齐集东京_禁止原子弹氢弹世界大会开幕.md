Title: Peace Representatives from Thirty-Two Countries Gather in Tokyo: World Conference Against Atomic and Hydrogen Bombs Opens
Author:
Date: 1958-08-16
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, Tokyo, August 15th: The Fourth World Conference Against Atomic and Hydrogen Bombs and for Disarmament International Conference officially opened at 3:30 PM today at the Waseda University Memorial Hall in Tokyo. Nomaya Hatsue, a representative of the Japan Christian Peace Conference, read the opening declaration.

Ninety-eight delegates from thirty-two countries and nine international organizations, along with one hundred and thirteen Japanese delegates, are participating in the conference.

Nagao Shimizu, Speaker of the Tokyo Metropolitan Assembly, delivered a welcoming speech to the foreign delegates and wished the conference success.

Subsequently, the conference elected Kioan, the Algerian representative, and thirty-eight others to the presidium, and also elected the agenda committee.

Garcia Rendon, Secretary-General of the International Preparatory Committee of the World Conference, gave a report on the committee's work.

The conference also introduced the progress of the 1,000-kilometer Peace March by the Japanese people. Takeshi Nishimoto spoke on behalf of all the marchers. He said that Japan had taken the wrong path in the past and committed great crimes against the people of China and Asia. During the march, we walked and thought that we must atone for past mistakes with the actions of each individual and strive for peace.

John Hartley, the representative from Australia, gave a speech praising the march. He said that the march greatly encouraged the world peace movement.

Next, the marchers presented the foreign delegates with scorched tiles from Hiroshima and scorched earth from Nagasaki, as well as paper cranes symbolizing peace.

The conference read congratulatory telegrams and letters from British philosopher Bertrand Russell, West German Social Democratic leader Erich Ollenhauer, Mrs. Ali, the Mayor of Delhi, and Ichiro Hatoyama, Chairman of the Japan-Soviet Association.

Subsequently, representatives Rachidov from the Secretariat of the Asian-African People's Solidarity Council, George Grace from Australia, Harder from West Germany, André Trocmé from France, and others delivered speeches.

Meanwhile, the Japan Conference of the World Conference Against Atomic and Hydrogen Bombs and for Disarmament also closed on the 15th. The conference passed a resolution demanding that the Japanese government and the Japanese Diet issue a declaration banning the nuclear armament of Japan, formulate laws banning the transportation of nuclear weapons into Japan, formulate laws banning the nuclear armament of the "Self-Defense Forces," abolish the US military's atomic warfare bases, normalize relations between Japan and China, abolish various policies that oppress Japanese democracy, demand that the US and Britain immediately stop testing nuclear weapons, and propose to the US, Britain, and the Soviet Union that they conclude an agreement not to use nuclear weapons.

### 三十二国和平代表齐集东京　禁止原子弹氢弹世界大会开幕

1958-08-16
第5版()
专栏：

　　三十二国和平代表齐集东京
　　禁止原子弹氢弹世界大会开幕
    新华社15日讯　东京消息：第四届禁止原子弹氢弹与争取裁军世界大会国际大会今天下午三时半在东京早稻田大学纪念堂正式开幕。日本基督教和平会议代表野宫初枝朗读了开会宣言。
    参加大会的有世界三十二个国家和九个国际组织的九十八名代表和一百一十三名日本代表。
    东京都议会议长清水长雄向外国代表致了欢迎词，并且祝大会成功。
    接着，大会选出了阿尔及利亚代表基乌安等三十九人为议长团，同时还选出了议程委员。
    世界大会国际筹备委员会秘书长加西亚·伦登作了这个委员会的工作报告。
    大会还介绍了日本人民一千公里和平大游行的经过。西本笃代表全体游行队员讲话。他说，日本曾经走错了路，对中国以至亚洲人民犯了很大的罪行。在游行的途中，我们一面走一面想，我们必须以每个人的行动对过去的错误赎罪，并且争取和平。
    澳大利亚的代表约翰·哈特莱致词对大游行表示赞扬。他说，大游行非常地鼓励了世界的和平运动。
    接着，大游行队员们把广岛烧焦的瓦砾和长崎的焦土以及象征和平的纸鹤赠送给外国代表。
    大会宣读了英国哲学家罗素、西德社会民主党领袖奥伦豪尔、德里市长阿里夫人以及日苏协会会长鸠山一郎的贺电和贺信。
    接着亚非人民团结理事会秘书处代表拉奇多夫、澳大利亚代表乔治·格雷斯、西德代表哈德、法国代表安德烈·特罗克姆等先后发表演说。
    同时，禁止原子弹氢弹与争取裁军世界大会日本大会，也在15日闭幕。大会通过决议，要求日本政府和日本国会发表禁止核武装日本的宣言，制定禁止把核武器运进日本的法律，制定禁止核武装“自卫队”的法律，撤销美军的原子战争基地，使日中两国邦交正常化，取销压迫日本民主的各项政策，要求美英立即停止试验核武器，向美、英、苏三国建议缔结不使用核武器的协定。
