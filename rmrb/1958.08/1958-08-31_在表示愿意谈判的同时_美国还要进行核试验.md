Title: While Expressing Willingness to Negotiate, the United States Continues Nuclear Testing
Author:
Date: 1958-08-31
Page: 3

　　While Expressing Willingness to Negotiate, the United States Continues Nuclear Testing
    Xinhua News Agency, August 30th: Washington dispatch: After U.S. President Eisenhower issued a statement on August 22nd expressing the United States' willingness to discuss a halt to nuclear weapons testing, the U.S. government announced on August 29th that it would conduct another ten atomic explosions at the Nevada Test Site in the next two months.
    In announcing this statement, the U.S. Atomic Energy Commission admitted that this series of atomic explosions is the final part of the United States' entire nuclear weapons testing program for 1958. They are a continuation of the nuclear explosions that the United States recently conducted on Eniwetok Island and Johnston Island in the Pacific Ocean.
    The commission said that some of this series of nuclear weapons explosion tests would be conducted in tunnels constructed underground, and some would be conducted on balloons and test towers.

### 在表示愿意谈判的同时  美国还要进行核试验

1958-08-31
第3版()
专栏：

　　在表示愿意谈判的同时
    美国还要进行核试验
    新华社30日讯　华盛顿消息：在美国总统艾森豪威尔22日发表声明表示美国愿意讨论停止试验核武器以后，美国政府又在29日宣布：在未来的两个月中美国将在内华达州的原子试验基地再进行十次原子爆炸。
    美国“原子能委员会”在宣布这一声明时承认，这一系列的原子爆炸已经是美国1958年整个核武器试验计划的最后一部分。它们是美国不久以前在太平洋上的恩尼威托克岛和约翰斯顿岛上所进行的核爆炸的继续。
    委员会说，这一系列的核武器爆炸试验有些将在地下构筑的隧道里进行，有些在汽球和试验塔上进行。
