Title: Chinese Academy of Sciences Purple Mountain Observatory Forecasts Time When Soviet Rocket Passes Over China
Author:
Date: 1958-08-16
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, Nanjing, August 15th. According to a forecast by the Purple Mountain Observatory of the Chinese Academy of Sciences, the Soviet Union's third artificial satellite and its carrier rocket will fly over China from northwest to southeast every evening in the middle and late part of this month. The vast areas of our country can see this carrier rocket with the naked eye, while the artificial satellite appears darker and is more difficult to see with the naked eye.

By 11:00 AM Beijing time on the 15th of this month, the Soviet Union's third artificial satellite and its carrier rocket had orbited the Earth for three months. During this period, the artificial satellite completed a total of 1,252 orbits, and the carrier rocket completed 17 more orbits, taking the lead. Due to the influence of atmospheric resistance during space flight, their orbital period has been slowly decreasing. The rocket has a larger cross-sectional area and is subject to greater resistance, so its period decreases faster. Currently, the time for the rocket to complete one orbit is 102.02 minutes, and this period is changing at a rate of approximately 3.1 seconds shorter each day.

To facilitate public observation, the observatory has created a route map (see figure) showing the carrier rocket's path over China from the 16th to the 25th. The map marks three routes and two hypothetical locations, A and B, on each route. During this period, the rocket will pass through these same three routes every day, but at different times. It takes about six minutes to fly from point A to point B, and the time it takes to pass through other areas on the route can be estimated proportionally.

The schedule (Beijing time) for the rocket to fly over point A each day is now published below:

Month	Day	Route 1	Route 2	Route 3	Latitude Region Where the Rocket Is Visible in China
August	16	20:44	22:26	24:08	North of 38 degrees North latitude
August	17	20:31	22:13	23:55	North of 36 degrees North latitude
August	18	20:16	21:58	23:40	North of 34 degrees North latitude
August	19	20:01	21:43	23:25	North of 31 degrees North latitude
August	20	19:46	21:28	23:10	North of 29 degrees North latitude
August	21	19:29	21:11	22:53	Between 26 and 43 degrees North latitude
August	22	19:12	20:54	22:36	Between 23 and 41 degrees North latitude
August	23	18:54	20:36	22:18	Between 20 and 38 degrees North latitude
August	24	18:36	20:18	22:00	South of 36 degrees North latitude
August	25	18:16	19:58	21:40	South of 33 degrees North latitude

The last column in the table indicates the latitude region where the rocket is visible each day. This means that although the rocket passes by every day, it is not visible from all locations on the route, but rather within a certain latitude (north-south) range. Outside this range, it is difficult to see due to unfavorable sunlight conditions.

For example, on August 20th, the time for the rocket to fly over point A on Route 2 is 21:28 Beijing time, and the visible region for the rocket on this day is north of 29 degrees North latitude. From the route map, it can be seen that the rocket will pass east of Lanzhou at 21:31, Xi'an at 21:32, west of Wuhan and north of Changsha at 21:34, and then, further south, the rocket will move out of the visible latitude range.

When the rocket appears, it moves slowly across the sky, and because it is constantly tumbling in the air, its brightness is constantly changing, sometimes bright, sometimes dark, sometimes visible, sometimes invisible. The brightness fluctuates approximately every few seconds.

If the sky is clear and cloudless, within the latitude region where the rocket is visible each day, people in areas approximately 700 kilometers to the east and west of the above routes can clearly see it with the naked eye in different directions. When observing, they should pay attention to the appearance of the rocket in the northern sky in advance according to the time in the table. (Attached picture)

### 中国科学院紫金山天文台预报　苏联火箭经过中国的时间

1958-08-16
第6版()
专栏：

　　中国科学院紫金山天文台预报
　　苏联火箭经过中国的时间
    新华社南京15日电　据中国科学院紫金山天文台预报，本月中下旬，苏联第三个人造卫星及其运载火箭都将于每天晚上从西北向东南飞过我国上空。我国广大地区可用肉眼看到这个运载火箭，人造卫星则显得暗些，较难用肉眼看到。
    到本月15日北京时间十一点正为止，苏联第三个人造卫星及其运载火箭已经围绕地球运转了三个月。这段期间，人造卫星总共运行了一千二百五十二圈，运载火箭则领先多走了十七圈。由于在空中运行时受到大气阻力的影响，它们的运行周期一直在缓慢地减小。火箭的截面积大，所受阻力大，周期减小得比较快。现在，火箭运行一圈的时间是一○二·○二分钟，这个周期正在以每天约缩短三·一秒的速度变化着。
    为便于群众观察，天文台制定了一张运载火箭从16日到25日经过我国上空的路线图（见图），图中标出了三条路线和每条路线上甲、乙两个假想地点。这段期间，火箭每天都经过这同样的三条路线，只是时间不同。它从甲点飞到乙点需时约六分钟，经过路线上其他地区的时间则可按比例估计出来。
    现在将火箭每天飞过甲点的时间表（北京时间）公布如下：
  月　日　路线一　路线二　路线三　我国可见火箭的纬度区域
  8月16日　20.44分　22.26分　24.08分　北纬38度以北
  8月17日　20.31分　22.13分　23.55分　北纬36度以北
  8月18日　20.16分　21.58分　23.40分　北纬34度以北
  8月19日　20.01分　21.43分　23.25分　北纬31度以北
  8月20日　19.46分　21.28分　23.10分　北纬29度以北
  8月21日　19.29分　21.11分　22.53分　北纬26—43度
  8月22日　19.12分　20.54分　22.36分　北纬23—41度
  8月23日　18.54分　20.36分　22.18分　北纬20—38度
  8月24日　18.36分　20.18分　22.00分　北纬36度以南
  8月25日　18.16分　19.58分　21.40分　北纬33度以南
    上表中最后一栏表明了每天可见火箭的纬度区域，这就是说，虽然火箭每天经过，但并不是路线上所有的地方都能看到，而是有一定的纬度（南北）范围。在这范围以外，由于日光照射条件不利，难以看到。
    例如，8月20日火箭飞过第二条路线甲点的时间是北京时间二十一点二十八分，这一天火箭的可见区域是北纬二十九度以北。从路线图可以看出来，火箭将于二十一点三十一分经过兰州以东，二十一点三十二分过西安，二十一点三十四分过武汉以西和长沙以北，再往南，火箭就走出可见的纬度范围。
    火箭出现时在天空缓缓移动，并且由于它本身在空中不断翻转，亮度就时时在变化，忽明忽暗，忽隐忽现，大约几秒钟内，亮度就要波动一次。
    如果天空晴朗无云，在每天可见火箭的纬度区域里，沿上述路线东西两侧各约七百公里的地区，都可以在不同方向用肉眼清楚看到它。观察的时候，应该按照表中时间提早在偏北方天空注意火箭的出现。（附图片）
