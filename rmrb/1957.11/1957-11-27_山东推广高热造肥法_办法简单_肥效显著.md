Title: Shandong Promotes High-Heat Fertilizer Production Method: Simple Method, Remarkable Fertilizer Effect
Author:
Date: 1957-11-27
Page: 2

    山东推广高热造肥法
    办法简单　肥效显著
    本报讯　山东省今冬在大力开展养猪积肥运动的同时，广泛推广寿张县农民创造的人工造肥法。
    人工造肥是由寿张县永丰农业社生产小队长白喜然在1955年冬季苦心钻研试制成功的一种肥料。经过两年来的多次试验，证明这种人工造肥简便易行、成本低、质量好、肥效大，是开展群众性积肥运动中的一种好办法。聊城专区已确定今冬要大力推广。初步计划全区人工造肥六百万车，加上其他肥料，小麦的追肥量和明年春播的基肥比今年增加半倍到一倍。在省最近召开的农业积极分子会议上，经过研究认为群众这种创造是科学的，应该大力推广。
    人工造肥的方法很简单，用少量人、畜粪和大量脏土、杂草经过速效高温发酵沤制而成。10％的圈肥，再灌10％的人粪尿汤（羊、鸡粪也行），作酵母，坑底铺一寸厚左右的圈肥，再铺一尺厚左右的杂草或脏土，然后灌透水成稠浆状。这样逐层把坑填满，用木棍扎上眼，再用10％的鲜大粪汤灌入眼内，促使发酵，最后用土密封。经过高热发酵沤成的肥料又黑又臭，肥效很高。沤制时间，一般冬季一月，春季二十天，夏季半月左右。坑的深度，冬季深些，夏季可浅些。
    永丰社创造了人工造肥，使全社农作物施肥量大增，因而大大提高了产量。1955年每亩施肥一车（约一千五百斤），全年每亩平均产量二百五十斤，1956年每亩施肥两车半，亩产三百零三斤，比1955年增产五十三斤。1957年仅人造肥一项使全社增产九十一万七千多斤粮食，两年共计增产一百三十八万斤。为了争取农业生产上更大的丰收，这个社除了大量养猪积肥外，还计划从今年9月起到明年5月止，人工造肥三万四千多车，1958年每亩施肥量达到六车，争取每亩平均产量四百五十斤。
Shandong Promotes High-Heat Fertilizer Production Method
Simple Method, Remarkable Fertilizer Effect

This newspaper reports that Shandong Province is vigorously promoting the artificial fertilizer production method created by peasants in Shouzhang County, while simultaneously vigorously launching a pig raising and manure accumulation campaign this winter.

The artificial fertilizer production method is a type of fertilizer painstakingly researched and successfully developed by Bai Xiran, production team leader of the Yongfeng Agricultural Cooperative in Shouzhang County, in the winter of 1955. After repeated experiments over the past two years, it has been proven that this artificial fertilizer production method is simple and easy to implement, low in cost, good in quality, and highly effective, and is a good method for carrying out a mass manure accumulation campaign. The Liaocheng Special District has decided to vigorously promote it this winter. The preliminary plan is for the entire district to produce six million cartloads of artificial fertilizer. Combined with other fertilizers, the amount of topdressing fertilizer for wheat and the base fertilizer for next year's spring sowing will increase by half to one times compared to this year. At the recent provincial conference of agricultural activists, after research, it was considered that this creation by the masses is scientific and should be vigorously promoted.

The method of artificial fertilizer production is very simple. It involves using a small amount of human and animal manure and a large amount of dirty soil and weeds, which are fermented and composted using a rapid-acting, high-temperature method. 10% of stable manure is used, then 10% of human urine broth (sheep and chicken manure are also acceptable) is poured in, acting as yeast. A one-inch thick layer of stable manure is spread at the bottom of the pit, then a one-foot thick layer of weeds or dirty soil is spread, and then thoroughly watered to form a thick slurry. The pit is filled layer by layer in this way, holes are poked with wooden sticks, and then 10% of fresh manure broth is poured into the holes to promote fermentation. Finally, it is sealed with soil. The fertilizer, fermented and composted with high heat, is black and smelly, and has very high fertilizer effectiveness. The composting time is generally one month in winter, twenty days in spring, and about half a month in summer. The depth of the pit should be deeper in winter and shallower in summer.

The Yongfeng Cooperative's creation of artificial fertilizer production has greatly increased the amount of fertilizer applied to crops throughout the cooperative, thereby greatly increasing yields. In 1955, one cartload of fertilizer was applied per mu (approximately 1,500 jin), and the average yield per mu for the whole year was 250 jin. In 1956, two and a half cartloads of fertilizer were applied per mu, and the yield per mu was 303 jin, an increase of 53 jin compared to 1955. In 1957, artificial fertilizer alone increased the cooperative's grain production by more than 917,000 jin, for a total increase of 1.38 million jin over two years. In order to strive for even greater harvests in agricultural production, in addition to raising pigs and accumulating manure on a large scale, this cooperative plans to produce more than 34,000 cartloads of artificial fertilizer from September of this year to May of next year. In 1958, the amount of fertilizer applied per mu will reach six cartloads, striving for an average yield of 450 jin per mu.
