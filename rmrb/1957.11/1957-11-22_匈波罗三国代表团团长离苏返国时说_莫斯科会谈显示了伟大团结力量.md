Title: Heads of Delegations from Hungary, Poland and Romania Say Upon Leaving Soviet Union That Moscow Talks Demonstrated Great Strength of Unity
Author:
Date: 1957-11-22
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, November 21st: TASS reports that the Hungarian, Polish, and Romanian delegations that participated in the 40th anniversary celebrations of the October Revolution left Moscow on the 20th to return to their countries. The Bulgarian delegation also left Moscow on the 21st.

The heads of these delegations made statements upon leaving Moscow, expressing gratitude for the warm reception from the Soviet Union and satisfaction with the talks held in Moscow.

The head of the Hungarian delegation, János Kádár, said that during the Hungarian delegation's stay in the Soviet capital, they felt the deep and sincere friendship of the Soviet people towards the Hungarian people at every moment. He said that for the sake of the happy future of our people, we will do everything possible to make the friendship between the great Soviet Union and the Hungarian people an eternal friendship and to further strengthen it. We will strive to make the Hungarian People's Republic a worthy member of the powerful socialist camp of 950 million people led by the Soviet Union.

The head of the Polish delegation, Władysław Gomułka, said, "We believe that the results of the talks in Moscow are very good. The unity between our two parties, between the Communist Party of the Soviet Union and other fraternal parties, on the issues of socialist construction and the struggle for peace and socialism, on the issues of our mutual relations, and on the most pressing international political issues, is our common great achievement."

Gomułka expressed his belief that the fraternal friendship between the Polish and Soviet peoples, the socialist internationalist ties and solidarity between the Polish United Workers' Party and the Communist Party of the Soviet Union, have been strengthened by the visit of the Polish delegation and will continue to be strengthened in the future.

The head of the Romanian delegation, Chivu Stoica, said that the grand military parade and festive procession in Moscow demonstrated the invincible strength of the Soviet Union and the unbreakable unity of the Soviet people.

Stoica pointed out that the Romanian people, who have received enormous assistance from the Soviet Union in all aspects, believe that Romanian-Soviet friendship and the fraternal alliance with the Soviet people are one of their priceless assets. The Romanian people are determined to further develop their friendship with the Soviet Union and other socialist countries.

### 匈波罗三国代表团团长离苏返国时说  莫斯科会谈显示了伟大团结力量

1957-11-22
第6版()
专栏：

    匈波罗三国代表团团长离苏返国时说
    莫斯科会谈显示了伟大团结力量
    新华社21日讯　据塔斯社报道，参加十月革命四十周年庆祝典礼的匈牙利、波兰和罗马尼亚代表团在20日离开莫斯科回国，保加利亚代表团也在21日离开莫斯科回国。
    这些代表团的团长在离开莫斯科时发表了谈话，他们对苏联的热情接待表示感谢，对在莫斯科举行的会谈表示满意。
    匈牙利代表团团长卡达尔说，匈牙利代表团人员在苏联首都逗留期间每时每刻都感受到了苏联人民对匈牙利人民的深厚的真诚友谊。他说，为了我国人民的幸福未来，我们将尽一切努力，使伟大的苏联和匈牙利两国人民的友谊成为永恒的友谊，并且更加加强。我们将力求使匈牙利人民共和国成为苏联领导下的拥有九亿五千万人口的强大的社会主义国家阵营中的毫无愧色的一员。
    波兰代表团团长哥穆尔卡说，我们认为在莫斯科举行的会谈的结果是非常良好的。我们两党之间、苏联共产党和其他兄弟党之间在社会主义建设和为争取和平和社会主义的斗争的问题上，在我们相互关系的问题上以及最迫切的国际政治问题方面的团结一致，是我们共同的巨大成就。
    哥穆尔卡表示相信，波兰人民和苏联人民之间的兄弟友谊、波兰统一工人党和苏联共产党的社会主义国际主义的联系和团结由于波兰代表团的访问而加强了，并且在今后将继续不断地加强。
    罗马尼亚代表团团长斯托伊卡说，莫斯科的盛大的阅兵和节日游行表现了苏联的不可战胜的力量，表现了苏联人民的牢不可破的团结。
    斯托伊卡指出，在各方面受到苏联巨大援助的罗马尼亚人民认为，罗苏友谊和同苏联人民的兄弟联盟是无价的财富之一。罗马尼亚人民决心进一步发展他们同苏联和其他各社会主义国家的友谊。
