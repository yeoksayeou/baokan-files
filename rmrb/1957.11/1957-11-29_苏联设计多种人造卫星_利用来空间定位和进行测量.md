Title: Soviet Union Designs Various Artificial Satellites for Space Positioning and Measurement
Author:
Date: 1957-11-29
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, November 28th: TASS reports: Soviet Professor Pobedonostsev published an article in "Air Force Communications" magazine introducing various design schemes for current artificial satellites.

    When discussing a space positioning satellite scheme, Pobedonostsev pointed out that a positioning satellite is a satellite equipped with specialized instruments that ensure the satellite's relative position to the Earth or the Sun remains stable. On positioning satellites, battery packs can be continuously recharged by the current generated by thermoelectric couples constantly exposed to sunlight. Another scheme for this type of satellite involves fixing an empty aluminum tube with a fixed axis of rotation in space. The two ends of the aluminum tube are dipole antennas, and various instruments are installed inside the tube: gamma-ray counters, and instruments for recording cosmic rays and particles that cause auroras. A small window on the satellite's outer shell is made of specially refined glass that only allows sunlight and ultraviolet rays to pass through, while another small window is made of beryllium, which only allows solar X-rays to pass through. The data collected by the counters is automatically, or according to commands from Earth, transmitted via radio to ground stations.

    Experts believe that this type of satellite can exist at an altitude of about 300 kilometers for about twelve days, revolving around the Earth approximately two hundred times.

    Professor Pobedonostsev also pointed out that other, simpler "beacon" or "lighthouse" type Earth satellites, without instruments, can orbit the Earth at an altitude of 300 kilometers for several weeks, or even several months.

    He said that the surface of these satellites can reflect sunlight or radar pulses emitted by ground-based devices. "Beacon" and "lighthouse" satellites can be effectively used in measurement. For example, determining the density of the Earth's atmosphere at high altitudes, studying the Earth's gravitational constant, and experimentally verifying the general theory of relativity.

### 苏联设计多种人造卫星  利用来空间定位和进行测量

1957-11-29
第5版()
专栏：

    苏联设计多种人造卫星
    利用来空间定位和进行测量
    据新华社28日讯　据塔斯社讯：苏联波别多诺斯采夫教授在“空军通讯”杂志上发表文章，介绍了目前人造卫星的各种设计方案。
    波别多诺斯采夫在谈到一种空间定位卫星的方案时指出，定位卫星是一种装有专门仪器的卫星，这种仪器能保证卫星与地球或太阳相对的位置稳定不变。在定位卫星上，可以靠不断被阳光照射的温差电偶发出的电流进行蓄电池组的补充充电。这种卫星的另一个方案是顺着空间方向固定的自转轴安一个空铝管，铝管的两端是偶极天线，而在铝管的内部装设各种仪器：丙种射线计数器、记录引起极光的宇宙射线和粒子的仪器。卫星外壳的一个小窗是用专门精制的玻璃制成的，它只让太阳光线和紫外线通过，另一个小窗是用铍制成的，它只让太阳的X射线通过。计数器所收集的资料自动地或按照地球上的指挥讯号，用无线电发给地面的台站。
    专家们认为，这种卫星可以在三百公里的高空存在十二天左右，环绕地球约旋转二百圈。
    波别多诺斯采夫教授还指出另一些不装仪器的最简单的“标石”式或“灯塔”式地球卫星可以在三百公里高空绕地球转上几个星期，甚至几个月。
    他说，这种卫星的表面能够反射阳光或地面装置发出的雷达脉冲。“标石”式和“灯塔”式卫星可以有效地应用于测量方面。如在高空测定地球大气层的密度，研究地球引力的常数，用实验验证广义相对论等。
