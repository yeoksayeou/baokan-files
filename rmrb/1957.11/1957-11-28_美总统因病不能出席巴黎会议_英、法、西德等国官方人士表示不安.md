Title: US President Unable to Attend Paris Conference Due to Illness; Officials from Britain, France, West Germany and Other Countries Express Unease
Author:
Date: 1957-11-28
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, November 27th: U.S. President Eisenhower's heart condition prevents him from attending the North Atlantic Treaty Organization (NATO) meeting scheduled for December, causing reactions in the United States and Western countries.

    The U.S. State Department announced that it has informed NATO Secretary General Spaak to consult with NATO countries on how to proceed with the summit scheduled for next month, given Eisenhower's inability to attend.

    Eisenhower's illness has caused great unease in Western countries. Spaak said on the evening of the 26th that the NATO Permanent Representatives Council will meet on the 28th to consider the impact of Eisenhower's illness on the NATO summit originally scheduled for next month. Meanwhile, officials from Britain, France, West Germany, and Italy have also expressed concern about Eisenhower's illness, fearing that Eisenhower's inability to attend the NATO summit will greatly weaken the role of the meeting.

### 美总统因病不能出席巴黎会议  英、法、西德等国官方人士表示不安

1957-11-28
第6版()
专栏：

    美总统因病不能出席巴黎会议
    英、法、西德等国官方人士表示不安
    据新华社27日讯　美国总统艾森豪威尔患心脏病，不能参加在12月间举行的北大西洋公约组织会议，在美国和西方国家引起了反应。
    美国国务院宣布，它已经通知北大西洋公约组织秘书长斯巴克，请他征求北大西洋公约组织国家在艾森豪威尔不能参加的情况下如何处理定在下月举行的最高级会议。
    艾森豪威尔的患病在西方国家里引起了很大的不安。斯巴克26日晚上说，北大西洋公约组织常任代表理事会将在28日举行会议，考虑艾森豪威尔患病对于原订下月举行的北大西洋公约组织最高级会议的影响。同时，英国、法国、西德和意大利等国的官方人士也对艾森豪威尔的患病表示忧虑，他们担心艾森豪威尔不能出席北大西洋公约组织最高级会议，将会大大削弱这个会议的作用。
