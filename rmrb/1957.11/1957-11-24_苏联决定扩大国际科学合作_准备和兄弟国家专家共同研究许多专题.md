Title: The Soviet Union Decides to Expand International Scientific Cooperation, Preparing to Jointly Study Many Topics with Experts from Brotherly Countries
Author:
Date: 1957-11-24
Page: 5

    Xinhua News Agency, November 23rd dispatch from Moscow: The Presidium of the Soviet Academy of Sciences held a meeting on the 22nd to discuss the implementation of the scientific cooperation agreements signed by the Soviet Academy of Sciences with the Academies of Sciences of the German Democratic Republic, Poland, Hungary, and Czechoslovakia over the past two years.

    Academician Sikakiyan, head of the International Scientific Liaison Committee of the Academy Presidium, made a report at the meeting. He said that the Soviet Union has accumulated some experience in conducting joint research with the Academies of Sciences of these countries. Currently, Soviet experts are working with Polish experts to jointly study ultrasonics and its application in technology. Soviet scientists have also reached agreements with Polish scientists on studying polymer compounds and using isotope methods in chemical experiments. Staff from the Soviet Geophysical Institute and the Institute of Plant Physiology are working with their counterparts in Czechoslovakia. Regular periodicals from many Soviet research institutes publish works by Polish, German, and Hungarian scientists. The Soviet Institute of Economics is preparing to jointly compile two important collective works with economists from various People's Democratic countries: "The Emergence and Development of the World Socialist Economic System" and "The Problems of Socialist Industrialization in the People's Democratic Countries."

    Academician Sikakiyan continued by saying that more than 500 scientific workers from various People's Democratic countries have come to the Soviet Union this year to participate in various conferences and to work in Soviet scientific institutions. Soviet scientists have also participated in many foreign scientific gatherings.

    Nessmeyanov, Topchiev, Semenov, Vinogradov, and others who spoke at the meeting put forward many suggestions for expanding and improving international scientific cooperation. (Attached Picture)

### 苏联决定扩大国际科学合作  准备和兄弟国家专家共同研究许多专题

1957-11-24
第5版()
专栏：

    苏联决定扩大国际科学合作
    准备和兄弟国家专家共同研究许多专题
    新华社23日讯　据塔斯社莫斯科讯：苏联科学院主席团22日举行会议，讨论了最近两年来苏联科学院同民主德国、波兰、匈牙利和捷克斯洛伐克各国的科学院所签定的科学合作协定的执行情况。
    科学院主席团国际科学联系委员会负责人西卡基扬院士在会上作了报告。他说，苏联在同这些国家的科学院进行共同研究当中已经积累了一定的经验。现在，苏联专家正和波兰专家一起共同研究超声波学以及它在技术中应用的问题。苏联科学家还同波兰科学家就研究高分子化合物和在化学试验等方面采用同位素方法的问题达成了协议。苏联地球物理学研究所、植物生理学研究所的工作人员正在同捷克斯洛伐克的同行们一起工作。苏联许多研究所的定期刊物，都刊载了波兰、德国和匈牙利科学家的著作。苏联经济研究所准备同各人民民主国家的经济学家联合编写两部重要的集体著作：“世界社会主义经济体系的产生和发展”和“人民民主国家社会主义工业化的问题”。
    西卡基扬院士接着说，今年有五百多位科学工作者从各个人民民主国家来到苏联参加各种会议和参加苏联科学机关的工作。苏联科学家也参加了许多外国的科学集会。
    在这次会议上发言的涅斯米扬诺夫、托普切夫、谢苗诺夫、维诺格拉多夫等人，提出了许多扩大和改进国际科学合作的建议。（附图片）
