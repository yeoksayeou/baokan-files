Title: More Than 100,000 Japanese Steel Workers Hold Ninth Strike
Author:
Date: 1957-11-20
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, Tokyo, November 19th: More than 100,000 steel workers from Japan's five major steel companies (Yawata Iron & Steel, Fuji Iron & Steel, Nippon Kokan, Sumitomo Metal Industries, Kobe Steel) began their ninth strike this morning, demanding higher wages.

Japanese steel workers have been fighting for more than a month since launching their first strike on October 8. According to the Japan Iron and Steel Industry Workers' Union Federation, which is leading the strike, the first eight strikes held by the workers have caused the capitalists a total loss of 18.5 billion yen.

### 日本十多万钢铁工人举行第九次罢工

1957-11-20
第6版()
专栏：

    日本十多万钢铁工人举行第九次罢工
    据新华社19日讯　东京消息：日本五大钢铁公司（八幡制铁、富士制铁、日本钢管、住友金属工业、神户制钢）的十多万名钢铁工人，从今天早晨开始举行第九次罢工，要求提高工资。
    日本钢铁工人从10月8日展开第一次罢工以来，已经坚持斗争一个多月。据领导罢工的日本钢铁产业工会联合会宣布，工人们举行的前八次罢工已经使资本家们遭受了共达一百八十五亿日元的损失。
