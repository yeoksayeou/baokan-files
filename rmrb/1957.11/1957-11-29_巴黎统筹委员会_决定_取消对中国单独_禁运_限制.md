Title: "Paris Coordinating Committee" Decides to Lift Separate "Embargo" Restrictions on China
Author: 
Date: 1957-11-29
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, Paris, 28th: Sources close to the "Paris Coordinating Committee," which manages so-called "embargo" affairs, revealed that the committee recently decided that from January 1st next year, trade restrictions on China and all socialist camp countries will be unified, and there will no longer be separate lists of restricted goods specifically for China. Observers point out that this decision will end the work of the "China Committee" of the "Paris Coordinating Committee."

The "Paris Coordinating Committee" includes fourteen North Atlantic group countries and Japan, excluding Iceland. Since April this year, the committee has repeatedly discussed the issue of relaxing the "embargo" on China. Most countries, including Britain, France, and West Germany, advocated changing the "embargo" on China to be equivalent to the "embargo" on the Soviet Union due to their own trade requirements. However, due to opposition from the United States, the meeting failed to reach an agreement on this issue for a long time.

### “巴黎统筹委员会”决定  取消对中国单独“禁运”限制

1957-11-29
第5版()
专栏：

    “巴黎统筹委员会”决定
    取消对中国单独“禁运”限制
    新华社28日讯　巴黎消息：据接近掌管所谓“禁运”事务的“巴黎统筹委员会”的人士透露，委员会最近作出决定，从明年1月1日起，将把对中国和一切社会主义阵营国家的贸易限制统一起来，不再另订单独对中国的不同的限额货单。观察家指出，这个决定将结束“巴黎统筹委员会”的“中国委员会”的工作。
    “巴黎统筹委员会”包括除了冰岛以外的北大西洋集团十四个国家和日本。委员会自从今年4月以来曾多次讨论放宽对中国的“禁运”问题。英国、法国、西德等大多数国家由于本身的贸易要求都主张把对中国的“禁运”改为同对苏联的“禁运”相等，但是，由于美国的反对，会议在很长时期中未能在这个问题上达成协议。
