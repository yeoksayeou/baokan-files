Title: In Missiles and Satellites, the United States is Far Behind the Soviet Union: A Famous American Rocket Expert Tells the Truth
Author:
Date: 1957-11-12
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, November 11th, New York dispatch: American rocket expert Braus admitted on the 10th, in response to questions from the Associated Press, that the Soviet Union is ahead of the United States in satellites and missiles, and that the United States "will have to spend at least five more years to catch up with the Soviet Union, even without any obstacles."

He said that the successful launch of the Soviet Union's second artificial satellite shows that the Soviet Union already possesses a powerful multi-stage rocket. Using this rocket, the Soviets will soon be able to send objects weighing perhaps over a hundred pounds to the moon on a one-way trip. With just a slight increase in speed, their final-stage rocket could permanently escape Earth's gravitational field and enter an orbit around the sun. He believes that the Soviets can undoubtedly achieve many results in this area now.

Xinhua News Agency, November 11th, Tokyo dispatch: Director of the Tokyo Observatory, Masaji Miyaji, said yesterday in the Japanese Diet that the Soviet Union is clearly "far ahead of the United States" in its missile and satellite tests.

Miyaji Masaji testified before a science committee of the Japanese House of Representatives regarding the achievements made by the Soviet Union in launching two artificial earth satellites, saying that the Soviet Union's successful launching of artificial satellites "reached the pinnacle of advanced science." He said that they are "an achievement that Japan cannot currently reach." Miyaji Masaji called for improved treatment of Japanese scientists. (Attached picture)

First satellite: "How do you do! You finally arrived!"

Second satellite: "Здравствуйте! Please speak Russian, I am also from the Soviet Union!"

### 在导弹和卫星方面  美国远远落后于苏联  一个美国著名火箭专家说了实话

1957-11-12
第5版()
专栏：

    在导弹和卫星方面
    美国远远落后于苏联
    一个美国著名火箭专家说了实话
    新华社11日讯　纽约消息：美国著名火箭专家布劳思10日在答复美联社的问题时承认，在卫星和导弹方面，苏联走在美国前面，美国“即使不受任何阻碍，也必须再过五年以上才能够赶上苏联”。
    他说，苏联第二个人造卫星的发射成功，表明苏联已经拥有一种强力的多级火箭。利用这种火箭，苏联人不久就一定能把重量可能在一百磅以上的东西单程地送到月球上。只要速度再增加一点，他们的最后一级火箭就可能永久脱离地球的引力场，而进入围绕太阳旋转的轨道了。他认为，苏联人现在毫无疑问能够在这方面收到许多成果。
    新华社11日讯　东京消息：东京天文台台长宫地政司昨天在日本国会里说，苏联显然在它的导弹和卫星试验方面“远远跑在美国前面”。
    宫地政司就苏联在发射两颗人造地球卫星方面取得的成就在日本众议院的一个科学委员会上作证时说，苏联发射人造卫星成功，“达到了先进科学的顶峰”。他说，它们是“日本在目前不可能达到的一种成就”。宫地政司要求改善日本科学家的待遇。（附图片）
