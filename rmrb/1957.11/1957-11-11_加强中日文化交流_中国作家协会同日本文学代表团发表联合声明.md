Title: Strengthening Sino-Japanese Cultural Exchange: Joint Statement Issued by Chinese Writers Association and Japanese Literary Delegation
Author:
Date: 1957-11-11
Page: 1
Xinhua News Agency, 10th:

    Strengthening Sino-Japanese Cultural Exchange
    Joint Statement Issued by Chinese Writers Association and Japanese Literary Delegation
    Xinhua News Agency, 10th—During the visit of the Japanese Literary Delegation to Beijing, discussions were held with the Chinese Writers Association on issues of cultural exchange between the two countries. They issued a joint statement with the Chinese Writers Association on behalf of the Japan PEN Club. The signing ceremony for this joint statement was held in Beijing this morning.
    The full text of the joint statement is as follows:
    We, the literary workers of China and Japan, feel deeply pleased to be able to gather and talk happily during this second visit of the Japanese Literary Delegation to China, and to exchange views on various cultural issues of the two countries.
    The cultures of China and Japan not only have a long history of relations, but also have achieved today's prosperity due to close mutual ties. The current special political situation makes us feel more deeply the necessity of strengthening cultural exchange.
    During the visit of the Japanese Literary Delegation to China, the literary workers of the two countries reached complete consensus on the need to further strengthen the exchange of literary workers and works in order to promote the development of culture in the two countries, and are willing to do everything possible to promote its realization.
    Signing the joint statement on behalf of the Chinese side were Zhou Yang, Vice Chairman of the Chinese Writers Association, Shao Quanlin, Secretariat Secretaries Liu Baiyu, Zang Kejia, Wu Zuxiang, Chen Baichen, Yan Wenjing, and Director Xiao San.
    Signing on behalf of the Japanese side were the head of the delegation and Director of the Japan PEN Club, Kenkichi Yamamoto, members of the delegation and Director of the Japan PEN Club, Yasushi Inoue, novelist Yukei Tada, literary critic Hajime Tohan, writer Shigeharu Nakano, Japan PEN Club member Yoshie Hotta, and literary critic Akio Honda.
    Kenzo Nakajima, Chairman of the Japan-China Cultural Exchange Association and Director of the Japan PEN Club, who is also visiting Beijing, also attended today's ceremony and signed the joint statement.
    After the signing, Zhou Yang and Kenkichi Yamamoto both spoke, and the writers of the two countries held friendly talks. Nakajima Kenzo said that the Japan-China Cultural Exchange Association has decided to invite Chinese writers to visit Japan in the spring of next year. Zhou Yang, on behalf of the Chinese writers, expressed his pleasure in accepting this invitation. The Japanese Literary Delegation has now concluded its visit here. The delegation will leave Beijing by train this afternoon for Shanghai, and will visit Shanghai, Chengdu, Chongqing, Wuhan, Guangzhou, and other places before returning home.

### 加强中日文化交流  中国作家协会同日本文学代表团发表联合声明

1957-11-11
第1版()
专栏：

    加强中日文化交流
    中国作家协会同日本文学代表团发表联合声明
    新华社10日讯　日本文学代表团访问北京期间同中国作家协会就两国文化交流问题进行了商谈，他们代表日本文艺家协会同中国作家协会发表了联合声明。这项联合声明的签字仪式今天上午在北京举行。
    联合声明全文如下：
    我们中国和日本两国的文学工作者，在此第二次日本文学代表团访问中国期间能够聚首欢谈，就两国文化上各种问题交换意见，深感愉快。
    中日两国文化，不仅有悠久的历史关系，而且由于相互间的密切联系，才获得了今天的繁荣，目前特殊的政治形势，更使我们深切地感觉有加强文化交流的必要。
    在日本文学代表团访问中国期间，两国文学工作者对于为了促进两国文化的发展必须进一步加强文学工作者与作品的交流，取得了完全一致的意见，并愿意尽一切力量促其实现。
    代表中国方面在联合声明上签字的是中国作家协会副主席周扬、邵荃麟，书记处书记刘白羽、臧克家、吴组湘、陈白尘、严文井和理事萧三。
    代表日本方面签字的是代表团团长、日本文艺家协会理事山本健吉，团员、日本文艺家协会理事井上靖，小说家多田裕计，文艺评论家十返肇，作家中野重治，日本笔会会员堀田善卫，文艺评论家本多秋五。
    正在北京访问的日中文化交流协会理事长、日本文艺家协会理事中岛健藏也出席了今天的仪式并且在联合声明上签了字。
    在签字以后，周扬和山本健吉都讲了话，两国作家们进行了友好的交谈。中岛健藏说，日中文化交流协会决定邀请中国作家在明年春天访问日本，周扬代表中国作家表示欣然接受这一邀请。日本文学代表团现已结束在这里的访问。代表团在今天下午离开北京坐火车去上海，将在上海、成都、重庆、武汉、广州等地访问以后回国。
