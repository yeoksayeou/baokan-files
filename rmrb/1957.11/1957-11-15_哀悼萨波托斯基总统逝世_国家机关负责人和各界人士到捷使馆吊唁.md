Title: Mourning President Zapotocky's Death: State Organ Leaders and People from All Walks of Life Visit the Czechoslovak Embassy to Pay Condolences
Author:
Date: 1957-11-15
Page: 4

Mourning President Zapotocky's Death
State Organ Leaders and People from All Walks of Life Visit the Czechoslovak Embassy to Pay Condolences

Nearly ten thousand people, including heads of various departments of our national state organs and people from all walks of life in the capital, visited the Czechoslovak Embassy in China on the 14th to pay condolences on the death of President Zapotocky.

The mourners, wearing black gauze and carrying wreaths, solemnly entered the hall of the embassy and stood in silent tribute before the portrait of President Zapotocky.

Dong Biwu, member of the Political Bureau of the Central Committee of the Communist Party of China and President of the Supreme People's Court, went to pay condolences.

Also paying condolences were members of the Central Committee of the Communist Party of China and heads of various departments of the Central Committee of the Communist Party of China, members and deputy secretaries-general of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress, the Secretary-General and Deputy Secretaries-General of the State Council, ministers and deputy ministers of various ministries, directors and deputy directors of various commissions, and heads of various directly affiliated agencies, the Secretary-General, Deputy Secretaries-General and Standing Committee members of the National Committee of the CPPCC, the Vice President of the Supreme People's Court, the Deputy Procurator-General of the Supreme People's Procuratorate, members of the National Defense Council, senior generals of the Chinese People's Liberation Army, chairmen, vice-chairmen and central standing committee members of various democratic parties, heads of various people's organizations, Beijing municipal party and government leaders, and representatives from the capital's science, education, culture, arts, religion, and news circles, government cadres, and officers and soldiers of the People's Liberation Army.

The Sino-Czech Friendship Agricultural Production Cooperative also sent representatives from Zhangguozhuang to the embassy to mourn the death of President Zapotocky.

More than 9,000 people in Shanghai visit the Czech Consulate General to pay their respects
On the 14th, more than 9,500 people in Shanghai went to the Czechoslovak Consulate General in Shanghai to express their deep condolences on the death of President Zapotocky.

Among those who went to pay condolences were Liao Chengzhi, member of the Central Committee of the Communist Party of China, Wei Wenbo and Ma Tianshui, secretaries of the Shanghai Municipal Committee of the Communist Party of China, Xu Jianguo, Niu Shucai, Song Richang, Zhao Zukang, Sheng Pihua, and Rong Yiren, vice mayors of Shanghai, Major General Xiong Yingtang, deputy commander of the Shanghai Garrison of the People's Liberation Army, Rear Admiral Peng Deqing, deputy commander of the East China Sea Fleet of the People's Liberation Army, as well as heads and representatives of various democratic parties, mass organizations, and government agencies in Shanghai.

Żukrowski, head of the Polish Higher Education Delegation and Minister of Higher Education of Poland, who is visiting Shanghai, Soviet Consul General in Shanghai, Yelisavigin, and consuls from other countries in Shanghai also went to pay condolences.

Around the Czechoslovak Consulate General, there were long lines of workers, students, government cadres, housewives, and industrialists and businessmen. Wearing black gauze or yellow flowers and carrying wreaths, they slowly walked to the portrait of President Zapotocky in the mourning hall and stood in silent tribute. Among the mourners were the heads of the Yangshupu Power Plant and the Zhabei Power Plant in Shanghai, which received huge assistance from the Czechoslovak Republic. Representatives of the leaders and employees of the Shanghai Turbine Plant, which was built with the assistance of the Czechoslovak Republic, also traveled more than sixty miles to pay their condolences.

More than 7,000 people in Guangzhou visit the Czech Consulate to pay their respects
On the 14th, more than 7,000 people in Guangzhou visited the Czechoslovak Consulate in Guangzhou to pay condolences on the death of President Antonín Zápotocký.

Among those who went to pay condolences were cadres from the Guangdong Provincial Committee and the Guangzhou Municipal Committee of the Communist Party of China, the Guangdong Provincial People's Committee and the Guangzhou Municipal People's Committee, officers and soldiers of the Chinese People's Liberation Army stationed in Guangzhou, as well as cadres and workers from government agencies and organizations, young students, and women.

Foreign Envoys to China Visit the Czech Embassy to Pay Condolences
On the 14th, foreign envoys and diplomatic officials to China visited the Czechoslovak Embassy in China to pay condolences on the death of President Zapotocky.

Among those who went to pay condolences were: Polish Ambassador Kiryluk, Finnish Ambassador Sundström, Pakistani Ambassador Ahmed, Indian Ambassador R.K. Nehru, Ambassador Gipner of the German Democratic Republic, Norwegian Ambassador Kløgt-Hansen, Albanian Ambassador Ballili, Swiss Ambassador Béguin, Bulgarian Ambassador Negalkov, Vietnamese Ambassador Nguyen Khang, Mongolian Ambassador Luvsan, Korean Ambassador Lee Yong-ho, Soviet Chargé d'Affaires Gutikov, Indonesian Chargé d'Affaires Suwanto, Swedish Chargé d'Affaires Beelding, Yugoslav Chargé d'Affaires Mirićević, Burmese Chargé d'Affaires U Ba Maw, Afghan Chargé d'Affaires Latifi, Romanian Chargé d'Affaires Iesan, Syrian Chargé d'Affaires Shalan, Hungarian Chargé d'Affaires Sár, British Chargé d'Affaires Văn Úc Sinh, Dutch Chargé d'Affaires Meyer, and diplomatic officials from the Egyptian and Danish embassies.

Soviet Delegation Visits the Czech Consulate General to Pay Condolences
The Soviet delegation to China, headed by Andreyev, Chairman of the Soviet-Chinese Friendship Association, which is participating in the Chinese people's celebration of the 40th anniversary of the October Revolution, visited the Czechoslovak Consulate General in Shanghai on the afternoon of the 14th to pay condolences on the death of President Antonín Zápotocký and presented a wreath.

Go Baoquan, Deputy Secretary-General of the Sino-Soviet Friendship Association, accompanied the delegation to the consulate to pay condolences.

Ambassador Cao Ying Visits the Czech Premier to Express Condolences
On the morning of the 14th, Cao Ying, the Chinese Ambassador to Czechoslovakia, visited Czechoslovak Premier Široký to express his deepest condolences to Premier Široký on the death of President Zapotocky.

### 哀悼萨波托斯基总统逝世  国家机关负责人和各界人士到捷使馆吊唁

1957-11-15
第4版()
专栏：

    哀悼萨波托斯基总统逝世
    国家机关负责人和各界人士到捷使馆吊唁
    我国国家机关各部门的负责人和首都各界人士近万人，14日到捷克斯洛伐克驻中国大使馆吊唁萨波托斯基总统逝世。
    吊唁的人们戴着黑纱，抬着花圈，肃穆地走进大使馆的大厅，在萨波托斯基总统的遗像前默哀悼念。
    前往吊唁的有中共中央政治局委员、最高人民法院院长董必武。
    前往吊唁的还有中共中央委员和中共中央各部门的负责人，全国人民代表大会常务委员会的委员和副秘书长，国务院秘书长、副秘书长，各部部长、副部长，各委员会主任、副主任以及各直属机关的负责人，政协全国委员会秘书长、副秘书长和常务委员，最高人民法院副院长，最高人民检察院副检察长，国防委员会委员，中国人民解放军的高级将领，各民主党派的主席、副主席和中央常务委员，各人民团体的负责人，北京市党政领导人，首都科学、教育、文化艺术、宗教、新闻界人士，机关干部，解放军的军官和士兵。
    中捷友好农业生产合作社也派代表从张郭庄来大使馆，悼念萨波托斯基总统逝世。上海九千多人到捷总领事馆吊唁
    上海14日有九千五百多人前往捷克斯洛伐克驻上海总领事馆，对萨波托斯基总统逝世表示沉痛的哀悼。
    前往吊唁的人中有中共中央委员廖承志，中共上海市委书记处书记魏文伯、马天水，上海市副市长许建国、牛树才、宋日昌、赵祖康、盛丕华、荣毅仁，人民解放军上海警备区副司令员熊应堂少将、人民解放军海军东海舰队副司令员彭德清海军少将，以及上海市各民主党派、各群众团体、各机关的负责人和各界代表。
    正在上海访问的波兰高等教育代表团团长、波兰高等教育部部长茹凯夫斯基，苏联驻上海总领事叶里沙维金以及其他国家驻上海的领事也前往吊唁。
    捷克斯洛伐克总领事馆周围排着工人、学生、机关干部、家庭主妇、工商业者的长长的队伍，他们佩着黑纱或黄花，抬着花圈，缓步走到灵堂里的萨波托斯基总统遗像前默哀。吊唁的人群中有得到捷克斯洛伐克共和国巨大援助的上海杨树浦发电厂和闸北发电厂的负责人。在捷克斯洛伐克共和国帮助下建设起来的上海汽轮机厂的负责人和职工代表，也从六十多里外赶来吊唁。广州市七千多人到捷领事馆吊唁
    广州市14日有七千多人到捷克斯洛伐克驻广州领事馆吊唁安托宁·萨波托斯基总统逝世。
    前往吊唁的人中有中共广东省委员会和广州市委员会、广东省人民委员会和广州市人民委员会的干部，中国人民解放军驻广州部队官兵以及机关、团体的干部和工人、青年学生、妇女等。各国驻华使节到捷大使馆吊唁
    各国驻中国的使节和外交官员14日到捷克斯洛伐克驻中国大使馆，吊唁萨波托斯基总统逝世。

    正在上海访问的以苏中友好协会主席安德烈耶夫为首的苏联各界参加中国人民庆祝十月革命四十周年纪念代表团，14日下午到捷克斯洛伐克驻上海总领事馆吊唁安托宁·萨波托斯基总统的逝世，并献花圈。
