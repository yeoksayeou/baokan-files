Title: Satellites Can Transmit Various Intelligence - Soviet Scientists Introduce Radio Telemetering Devices
Author:
Date: 1957-11-22
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, Moscow, November 21st: The Soviet Union already possesses extremely comprehensive radio telemetry devices capable of studying a wide variety of very different issues in areas such as using artificial satellites to study cosmic conditions and animal physiological functions. This was stated by Soviet scientists Kogozik and Kubansky in a recent article in the "Soviet Russia" newspaper.

The authors wrote in their article, titled "How Satellites Send Intelligence to Scientists": Materials on biological functions in satellites must be obtained using radio waves. Radio waves can not only be used to transmit sound and images, but also to transmit various indices over long distances, especially physiological indices. For this purpose, radio telemetry stations must be established.

The article introduced the method of transmitting animal pulse, breathing, body temperature, and other conditions to the ground. When introducing the method of recording animal breathing, the article stated that a variable resistor is tied to the animal's chest with a special band. When the animal breathes, the change in chest circumference causes a change in resistance, creating a specific electrical signal. The animal's breathing process constantly affects the radio frequency of the radio transmitter through a special modulation device. After the receiving station receives this radio wave, it extracts the code. The code extraction work is done by a detection device. The electrical code signal, after preliminary amplification, reaches the recording instrument from the detection device. This instrument can then record the signal in the form of a curve.

The article stated that the study of the upper atmosphere, rocket and artificial satellite operations, cosmic rays, etc., also uses this telemetry principle. Each index can be expressed in different curves.

The authors pointed out that the data that usually needs to be sent back to Earth is large and diverse, so multi-channel radio telemetry systems must be used.

### 卫星能发各种情报  苏联科学家介绍无线电遥测装置

1957-11-22
第6版()
专栏：

    卫星能发各种情报
    苏联科学家介绍无线电遥测装置
    据新华社莫斯科21日电　在利用人造卫星研究宇宙情况及动物生理机能等方面，苏联已拥有极完善的、能够研究各种各样极不相同的问题的无线电遥测装置，这是苏联科学家科戈吉克和库班斯基最近在“苏维埃俄罗斯报”上的一篇文章中说的。
    作者在这篇题为“卫星如何将情报发给科学家”的文章中写道：卫星上生物机能的材料必须利用无线电波来取得，无线电波不但可用来转发声音和形象，而且可用来远距离播送各种指数，特别是生理机能的指数。为此就要建立无线电遥测台。
    这篇文章介绍了把动物的脉搏、呼吸、体温等等情况传送到地面上的方法。文章在介绍纪录动物呼吸的方法时说，把可变电阻用一条特制的带子拴在动物胸部，动物呼吸时，胸围的变化引起了电阻的变化，造成特定的电讯号。动物的呼吸过程通过一种特制的调变装置而不断地影响着无线电发报机的射频。接收站收到这种无线电波后，析出其中的电码。析电码工作是由检波装置来做的。经过初步放大的电码讯号从检波装置到达记录仪器。这个仪器就可以把讯号用曲线形式记录下来。
    文章说，大气高层、火箭和人造卫星运行情况、宇宙线等的研究也是运用这种遥测原理。每一种指数都可以用不同曲线表现出来。
    作者指出，通常需要发回地球的数据是大量的和多种多样的，因此必须采用多波道无线电遥测系统。
