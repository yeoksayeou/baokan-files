Title: Molotov Replies to UNESCO on the Development of Cultural Exchange
Author:
Date: 1956-02-22
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, Moscow, 20th - TASS reported: In view of the discussions on the development of East-West contacts at the Geneva Conference of the Four Foreign Ministers of the Soviet Union, the United States, Britain, and France in 1955, the Acting Director-General of UNESCO, Mr. Mathew, wrote to the four foreign ministers, raising the question of the role that UNESCO could play in developing East-West contacts. Mr. Mathew's letter suggested establishing a fund controlled by UNESCO to implement a number of educational and cultural measures conducive to closer cooperation between countries.

On December 30, 1955, Soviet Foreign Minister Molotov replied to UNESCO Director-General Luther Evans. The reply pointed out that the Soviet government supports broad international cooperation in cultural exchange and strengthening cultural ties. It believes that this cooperation is an important means of consolidating peace and creating an atmosphere of international trust.

Molotov's letter pointed out that UNESCO, established specifically to promote international peace, universal welfare, and understanding among peoples through cooperation in education, science, and culture, must play an important role in developing international cultural exchange. Molotov pointed out that there are still many untapped potentials and opportunities in developing East-West contacts, especially in providing technical and other assistance to economically underdeveloped countries and regions.

Molotov generally agreed with the practical suggestions made in UNESCO's letter, and he expressed that the Soviet Union would join other countries in establishing the aforementioned fund. He also put forward several suggestions on carrying out the work of UNESCO.

Molotov drew attention to the fact that while promoting the study of Russian, English, French, and German, more attention should be paid to the study of other languages, especially Chinese, Indian, and Arabic. This will promote a deeper understanding between East and West.

Molotov requested the heads of UNESCO to pay attention to the abnormal situation in which the People's Republic of China is not represented in the organization. He pointed out the People's Republic of China's enormous contribution to world culture and the importance of its participation in international organizations that develop worldwide cultural ties.

UNESCO Director-General Evans recently wrote to Soviet Foreign Minister Molotov, expressing satisfaction with Molotov's reply. He said that he is prepared to consider, together with Soviet representatives, the measures that should be taken to establish a UNESCO fund.

Evans pointed out that the issue of the People's Republic of China's participation in UNESCO is within the purview of the UNESCO General Conference.

Evans finally expressed his readiness to work with the recently established Soviet Union on UNESCO.

### 莫洛托夫就发展文化交流问题复信给联合国教育科学及文化组织

1956-02-22
第4版()
专栏：

    莫洛托夫就发展文化交流问题复信给联合国教育科学及文化组织
    新华社21日讯　据塔斯社莫斯科20日讯：联合国教育、科学及文化组织的代理总干事马休鉴于1955年苏、美、英、法四国外交部长日内瓦会议上对发展东西方接触问题进行的讨论，写信给四国外交部长，信里提出了联合国教育、科学及文化组织在发展东西方接触方面能够起的作用问题。马休的信建议设立一笔由联合国教育、科学及文化组织控制的基金，用来执行有助于建立各国间更密切合作的若干教育和文化措施。
    1955年12月30日，苏联外交部长莫洛托夫复信给联合国教育、科学及文化组织的总干事路·伊凡斯。复信指出，苏联政府赞成在文化交流方面进行广泛的国际合作，并且赞成加强文化联系。它认为这种合作是巩固和平和创造国际信任气氛的重要手段。
    莫洛托夫的信指出，联合国教育、科学及文化组织是特别为通过发展教育、科学和文化方面的合作来促使达到国际和平、普遍福利和各国人民之间的谅解等目的而成立的，它必须在发展国际文化交流方面起重要的作用。莫洛托夫指出，在发展东西方接触、特别是在给经济落后的国家和地区以技术和其他援助方面还有许多没有利用的潜力和机会。
    莫洛托夫大体上同意联合国教育、科学及文化组织的信中提出的切实可行的建议，他表示苏联将同其他国家一道参加建立上述基金。他还提出了若干关于开展联合国教育、科学及文化组织的工作的建议。
    莫洛托夫提请注意，在促进对俄文、英文、法文和德文这些文字的学习的同时，应该更多地注意学习其他文字，特别是像中国、印度和阿拉伯文字。这将会促进东西方更深的了解。
    莫洛托夫要求联合国教育、科学及文化组织的负责人注意这个组织里还没有中华人民共和国的代表的不正常情况。他指出中华人民共和国对于世界文化的巨大贡献和它参加发展全世界文化联系的国际组织的重要性。
    联合国教育—科学及文化组织总干事伊凡斯日前写信给苏联外交部长莫洛托夫，对莫洛托夫的复信表示满意。他说，他准备同苏联代表们在一起考虑在建立联合国教育、科学及文化组织基金方面应该采取的措施。
    伊凡斯指出，关于中华人民共和国参加联合国教育、科学及文化组织问题，是在联合国教育、科学及文化组织的大会的权限之内的。
