Title: The Soviet Union to Build a Modern Institute of Technology for Burma

Author:
Date: 1956-02-26
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, Rangoon, February 24th. The Burmese government's information bureau announced on February 23rd preliminary data from the Soviet Ministry of Higher Education regarding the construction of a modern engineering college for Burma. This engineering college was gifted to Burma by the Soviet government during the visit of Bulganin and Khrushchev to Burma.

    The preliminary data indicates that the college will initially accommodate 1,000 students and 100 graduate students. It is planned to construct 60,000 cubic meters of classrooms and laboratory buildings, and 20,000 to 25,000 cubic meters of practical training factories.

    The college will have two departments: a process engineering department and a mechanical engineering department. Engineers graduating from this college will be qualified as experts in designing and managing various enterprises, as well as experts in scientific institutions and laboratory work.

    The Soviet Ministry of Higher Education will soon send four experts to Rangoon to be responsible for the organization, construction, and other preparatory work of the engineering college.

### 苏联为缅甸建设一所现代化工学院

1956-02-26
第4版()
专栏：

    苏联为缅甸建设一所现代化工学院
    新华社仰光24日电　缅甸政府新闻局在2月23日公布了苏联高等教育部向缅甸提出的关于建设一所现代化工程学院的初步资料。这所工程学院是苏联政府在布尔加宁和赫鲁晓夫访问缅甸的时候作为礼物赠送给缅甸的。
    初步资料说明，这所学院开始时期可以容纳学生1，000人和研究生100人。计划建筑6万立方公尺的教室和实验室大厦，两万到两万五千立方公尺的实习工厂。
    这所学院将分两个系：工艺系和机械系。从这所学院毕业出来的工程师，将能够达到设计和管理不同企业的专家的条件，以及科学机构和实验室工作方面的专家的条件。
    苏联高等教育部不久将派遣4位专家前来仰光，负责进行工程学院的组织、建设和其他准备工作。
