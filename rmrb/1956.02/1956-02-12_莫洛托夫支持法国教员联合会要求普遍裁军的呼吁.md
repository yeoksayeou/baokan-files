Title: Molotov Supports the French Teachers' Union's Call for Universal Disarmament
Author:
Date: 1956-02-12
Page: 4

    Xinhua News Agency, Moscow, February 11th: According to TASS, Moscow, February 10th, Soviet Foreign Minister Molotov wrote a letter on February 8th to Lavvergne, the General Secretary of the French National Education Federation, in response to the appeal made to major powers and the United Nations by this federation, which has 220,000 primary and secondary school teacher members, on the issue of universal disarmament and the use of nuclear energy purely for peaceful purposes.

    The appeal called on major powers and the United Nations to begin unconditionally following an effective disarmament policy that guarantees peace, and called on working people of all countries to urge their respective governments to adopt universal, simultaneous, and supervised disarmament, and to use nuclear energy purely for peaceful purposes.

    In his letter to Lavvergne, Molotov said, "I, along with the entire Soviet people, fully share the same views expressed by your colleagues in the appeal. Your initiative will undoubtedly receive warm support from all peace-loving people. I wish you success."

### 莫洛托夫支持法国教员联合会要求普遍裁军的呼吁

1956-02-12
第4版()
专栏：

    莫洛托夫支持法国教员联合会要求普遍裁军的呼吁
    新华社11日讯　据塔斯社莫斯科10日讯：苏联外交部长莫洛托夫2月8日写回信给法国国民教育联合会总书记拉维尔涅，答复这个拥有22万小学和中学教员会员的联合会就普遍裁军和把核子能纯粹用于和平目的问题向大国和联合国发出的呼吁书。
    呼吁书里要求大国和联合国开始无条件地遵循一项保障和平的有效的裁军政策，呼吁一切国家的劳动人民促使他们各自的政府采取普遍的、同时的和有监督的裁军，并且把核子能纯粹用于和平目的。
    莫洛托夫给拉维尔涅的信中说，“我同全体苏联人民一起，完全具有你的同事们在呼吁书中表示的同样的意见。你们的倡议无疑地将会得到一切爱好和平的人民的热烈支持。祝你成功。”
