Title: Cambodian Prime Minister Sihanouk's Speech at the Beijing Municipal Celebration Welcoming the Cambodian Delegation
Author: 
Date: 1956-02-18
Page: 2

Mr. Prime Minister, Mr. Mayor, Ladies and Gentlemen, Dear Friends:
    I am deeply grateful to the city of Beijing and to the numerous citizens who are participating in this grand and warm welcome meeting.

    Upon our arrival in Beijing, our generous hosts asked us Khmers to feel as if we were at home in China.

    We can assure you with all the gratitude of our brotherhood: coming among you – people who treat us so kindly, and coming to your great land, we feel completely at home.

    We, the indigenous Khmer people, live on the land where we have lived since prehistoric times, but our queen married an Indian prince in the early years of the Common Era, and their descendants deeply influenced our civilization and culture.

    According to the records of the Chinese envoys Kang Tai and Zhu Ying (245-250 AD), from the time the Khmer people began to establish an organized state, that is, from the first century AD, our country – Cambodia, has maintained diplomatic, commercial, and cultural relations with your great country and the great Chinese people; at that time, Chinese historians called the northern and southern parts of Cambodia "Funan" and "Zhenla" respectively.

    Today, there are more than 300,000 Chinese people in Cambodia, living in harmony with the Khmer people. Because Chinese and Cambodian people live together in daily life, many Cambodians have Chinese ancestry; taking the members of the Cambodian national delegation sitting here as an example, few do not have Chinese ancestors.

    Our capital, Phnom Penh, has a population of 500,000, of which at least one-third are Chinese. You can easily see that because we are so accustomed to the Chinese way of life and because of your perfect hospitality towards us, we feel at home here.

    Therefore, having come here at your kind invitation, we regard the people, things, and brilliant achievements of New China with the eyes of brothers, not with the eyes of outsiders.

    I am sure that you are undertaking a very great cause, a cause that places your country at a stage far more advanced than many independent countries.

    The Chinese people and the Chinese government, under the leadership of the great man of our time, your revered Chairman Mao Zedong, and under the outstanding and capable leadership of the great statesman of Asia, Mr. Zhou Enlai, are devoting themselves fully to this cause. We firmly believe that the purpose of this cause is to promote the happiness of the Chinese people and social tranquility. Therefore, this is a cause of peace. We express our unreserved admiration for your cause and extend our warmest congratulations.

    As the mayor of this great city emphasized, the Cambodian nation, people, and government are also doing our best, which cannot be compared with your strength, to strive for peace in Asia and the world and for brotherly friendship among the peoples of all countries.

    The great Chinese people can do much for peace and respect for the Five Principles of Peaceful Coexistence, which China, India, and Cambodia have declared and which we voluntarily declared our adherence to in New Delhi, and which were later triumphantly endorsed in Bandung.

    The friendship between India, China, and Cambodia, as the mayor rightly pointed out, marks a new page in history.

    Since the place where this friendship is embodied today is Beijing – a city that, in our view, has as much historical importance as our ancient capital of Angkor, please allow me to express our infinite gratitude to all of you, dear friends and brothers, and to the people of all China, and to warmly and sincerely wish you happiness and prosperity, glory to China, and lasting peace and friendship between our two countries and the peoples of Asia and the world.

### 在北京各界欢迎柬埔寨代表团大会上  柬埔寨首相西哈努克的讲话

1956-02-18
第2版()
专栏：

    在北京各界欢迎柬埔寨代表团大会上
    柬埔寨首相西哈努克的讲话总理先生，市长先生，先生们，亲爱的朋友们：
    我深深感激北京市，感激参加这个盛大、热烈的欢迎会的众多的市民。
    我们一到北京，慷慨的主人就请我们高棉人在中国要像在自己家里一样。
    我们可以用我们兄弟般的全部感谢心情来向你们保证说：来到你们——这样亲切对待我们的人们中间，来到你们的伟大国土上，我们感到完全像在家里一样。
    我们在种族上是土著的柬埔寨民族，居住在从史前时期以来我们一直居住着的土地上，可是我们的女王在公元的初年同一位印度王子结了婚，他们的后代深深地影响了我们的文明和文化。
    根据中国的使节康泰和朱应的记载（公元245—250年），从高棉人开始建立有组织的国家，也就是从公元一世纪起，我国——柬埔寨就同你们的伟大国家，同伟大的中国人民一直保持着外交、商务和文化上的关系；当时中国历史家把柬埔寨的南、北两部分别叫作“扶南”和“真腊”。
    现在柬埔寨有30多万中国人，同高棉人很和睦地生活在一起。由于中国人和柬埔寨人日常生活在一起，许多柬埔寨人都有着中国人的血统；就拿坐在这里的柬埔寨国家代表团的成员来说，很少人没有中国的祖先。
    我国首都金边有50万人口，其中至少有三分之一是中国人。你们很容易看出：由于我们很习惯于中国的生活方式，和由于你们对我们的完美招待，因此我们感到在这里像在家里一样。
    因此，我们应你们的好意邀请来到这里以后，我们是用兄弟的眼光而不是用外人的眼光来看待新中国的人民、事物和辉煌成就的。
    我敢肯定说，你们进行着的是一件极为伟大的事业，这件事业使你们的国家处在比许多独立国家更先进得多的阶段。
    中国人民和中国政府正在当代的伟人、你们的敬爱的主席毛泽东先生的领导下，在亚洲的伟大政治家周恩来先生的出色的和干练的主持下全力进行着这项事业。我们深信，这个事业的目的是为了促进中国人民的幸福和社会的安宁。因此这是一项和平的事业。我们对你们的事业表示无保留的钦佩和致以最热烈的祝贺。
    承蒙这个伟大城市的市长强调指出的，柬埔寨国家、人民和政府，也正在尽着我们微薄得不能同你们相比的力量，为争取亚洲及全世界的和平和各国人民间的兄弟友好而努力。
    伟大的中国人民能够为和平及尊重和平共处的五项基本原则做很多工作，这五项原则是中国、印度和柬埔寨曾经宣布并且我们曾经在新德里自愿地声明遵守的，后来这些原则在万隆又得到了胜利。
    印度、中国和柬埔寨三国间的友谊，正像市长很正确地指出的，标志着历史上新的一页。
    由于今天体现这种友谊的地方是北京——在我们看来这个城市同我们的古都吴哥一样地具有伟大历史重要性的城市，请允许我向你们大家，亲爱的朋友们和兄弟们，以及向全中国的人民表示我们无限的感激，并热烈而诚恳地祝你们幸福和繁荣，祝中国光荣，祝我们两国以及亚洲和世界的人民之间的和平和友谊持久不懈。
