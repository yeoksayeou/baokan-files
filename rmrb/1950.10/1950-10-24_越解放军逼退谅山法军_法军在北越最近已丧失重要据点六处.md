Title: Vietnamese Liberation Army Forces French Troops to Retreat from Lang Son; French Troops Lose Six Important Strongholds in North Vietnam Recently
Author:
Date: 1950-10-24
Page: 1

　　Vietnamese Liberation Army Forces French Troops to Retreat from Lang Son
    French Troops Lose Six Important Strongholds in North Vietnam Recently
    [Xinhua News Agency, October 23rd] Saigon dispatch by Agence France-Presse: The French military headquarters in Vietnam issued a communique on the 21st, announcing that French troops had withdrawn from Lang Son, an important strategic stronghold in North Vietnam, 80 miles north of Hanoi. After the Vietnamese People's Liberation Army launched a powerful offensive and annihilated more than 6,000 French troops, the French invading forces have lost six important strongholds in North Vietnam. In addition to Lang Son, the other five are Dong Khe, Cao Bang, That Khe, Na Cham, and Dong Dang.

### 越解放军逼退谅山法军  法军在北越最近已丧失重要据点六处

1950-10-24
第1版()
专栏：

　　越解放军逼退谅山法军
    法军在北越最近已丧失重要据点六处
    【新华社二十三日讯】据法新社西贡消息：驻越法军总部二十一日发表公报，宣布法军已撤出北越重要战略据点、河内以北八十英里的谅山。在越南人民解放军发动强大攻势，先后歼灭法军六千余人以后，法军侵略军已在北越丧失了重要据点六处。除谅山外，其他五处为东溪、高平、七溪、那岑和同登。
