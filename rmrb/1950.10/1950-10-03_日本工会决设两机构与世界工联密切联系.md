Title: Japanese Unions Decide to Establish Two Organizations for Close Contact with the World Federation of Trade Unions

Author:

Date: 1950-10-03
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, 29th. According to a United Press dispatch: Progressive Japanese unions convened a meeting in Tokyo on September 8th to discuss how Japanese unions could maintain contact with the World Federation of Trade Unions after the illegal dissolution of the All-Japan Union Liaison Council, a member of the World Federation of Trade Unions. Representatives from numerous unions, including those in the metal, postal (postal and telecommunications), shipbuilding, film, theater, and journalism industries, attended the meeting. The meeting decided to establish two organizations: the World Federation of Trade Unions Liaison Office and the World Trade Union Movement Research Council. The Liaison Office will be composed of representatives from Japanese unions that support the World Federation of Trade Unions, and its work will be to liaise with the World Federation of Trade Unions and make decisions on all related matters. The work of the World Trade Union Movement Research Council is to scientifically study the trends of the world trade union movement and political economy to benefit the development of the Japanese trade union movement.

### 日本工会决设两机构与世界工联密切联系

1950-10-03
第4版()
专栏：

　　日本工会决设两机构与世界工联密切联系
    【新华社二十九日讯】据联合通讯社讯：日本各进步工会于九月八日在东京召开会议，商讨世界工会联合会会员全日工会联络协议会被非法解散后日本工会与世界工联如何联系的问题。出席会议者有金属、递运（邮电）、造船、电影、演剧、新闻等许多工会的代表。会议决定设立世界工联联络事务局及世界工运研究协议会两个机构。联络事务局将由拥护世界工会联合会的日本各工会的代表组成，其工作为与世界工联联系及决议有关一切事项。世界工运研究协议会的工作是科学地研究世界工运及政治经济的动向，以利日本工运的发展。
