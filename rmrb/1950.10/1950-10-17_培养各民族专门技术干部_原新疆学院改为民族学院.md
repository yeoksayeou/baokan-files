Title: Cultivating Specialized Technical Cadres from All Ethnic Groups: Former Xinjiang College Reorganized as Nationalities Institute
Author:
Date: 1950-10-17
Page: 1

　　Cultivating Specialized Technical Cadres from All Ethnic Groups
    Former Xinjiang College Reorganized as Nationalities Institute
    [Xinhua News Agency, Xi'an, 16th] Dihua Dispatch: In order to systematically cultivate specialized technical cadres from all ethnic groups, according to the decision of the first meeting of the Northwest Nationalities Affairs Commission, and with the approval of the Central People's Government, the former Xinjiang College was officially reorganized into the Nationalities Institute in late September. Its educational policy will be closely coordinated with Xinjiang's economic and cultural construction, cultivating cadres for the current construction tasks to meet the needs of practical work. The current class is set at 500 students. In addition to the original 179 students of Xinjiang College, it will recruit 321 students from Uyghur, Kazakh, Kyrgyz, Uzbek, Hui, Mongolian, Tatar, and Tajik ethnic groups.

### 培养各民族专门技术干部  原新疆学院改为民族学院

1950-10-17
第1版()
专栏：

　　培养各民族专门技术干部
    原新疆学院改为民族学院
    【新华社西安十六日电】迪化讯：为了有计划地培养各民族专门技术干部，根据西北民族事务委员会首次会议决定，并经中央人民政府批准，原新疆学院于九月下旬正式改为民族学院。其教育方针将与新疆经济、文化建设密切配合，为当前建设任务培养干部，以适应实际工作的需要。本期学生定为五百名，除原新疆学院学生一百七十九名外，将招收维吾尔、哈萨克、柯尔克孜、乌兹别克、回、蒙、塔塔尔、塔吉克等学生三百二十一名。
