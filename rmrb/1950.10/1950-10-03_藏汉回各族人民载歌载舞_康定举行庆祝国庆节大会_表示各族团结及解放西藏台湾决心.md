　　Tibetan, Han, and Hui People Sing and Dance
    Kangding Holds National Day Celebration Rally
    Expressing Determination for Ethnic Unity and Liberating Tibet and Taiwan
    [Kangding Dispatch, 1st of this month] The Tibetan, Han, and Hui people of Kangding warmly celebrated the first anniversary of the founding of the People's Republic of China. In the days leading up to National Day, workers at the Kangding People's Enterprise Company power plant repaired a generator, Tibetan youths diligently rehearsed Tibetan operas and dances, Tibetan women made red and green lanterns, and lamas from the seven major monasteries in Kangding, including Anjue Monastery, practiced the dance of the gods. Hui youths hurriedly rehearsed national unity dances to welcome the festival of their own people. On this day, red flags fluttered throughout the city, and slogans written in Tibetan, Han, and Hui languages, such as "Long Live the People's Republic of China" and "Long Live Chairman Mao," were plastered all over the streets. Women and children of all ethnic groups wore brand-new clothes, and the entire mountain city was filled with the sounds of gongs and drums, Yangko dances, shawms, conch shells (Tibetan musical instruments), and cheers. At noon, more than 5,000 people from all ethnic groups and sectors of society held a celebration rally. After Liu Geping, head of the Central Delegation, conveyed Premier Zhou's report "Striving to Consolidate and Develop the People's Victory," the entire audience shouted slogans, expressing the determination of all ethnic groups to support the Central People's Government. Miao Fengshu, Director of the Kangding Military Control Commission, called on the Tibetan and Hui people to strengthen unity and strive to liberate Tibet and Taiwan as soon as possible. Danba Jiangyang Danzeng Living Buddha, Hui representative Sha Xiaozhou, and Tibetan gentry Jia Liansheng warmly praised the ethnic policies of the Common Program of the CPPCC and expressed the determination of the Tibetan, Hui, and Han people, under the leadership of the Central People's Government and Chairman Mao, to actively support the People's Liberation Army and strive to plant the five-star red flag on the Himalayas as soon as possible. In the evening, a torch parade was held throughout the city.

### 藏汉回各族人民载歌载舞  康定举行庆祝国庆节大会  表示各族团结及解放西藏台湾决心

1950-10-03
第3版()
专栏：

　　藏汉回各族人民载歌载舞
    康定举行庆祝国庆节大会
    表示各族团结及解放西藏台湾决心
    【本报康定一日专电】康定藏、汉、回族人民热烈庆祝中华人民共和国诞生一周年伟大的节日。国庆日前几天，康定人民企业公司发电所的工人修好发电机一部，藏族青年加紧排演藏戏和舞蹈，藏族妇女扎好红绿灯笼，康定安觉等七大喇嘛寺的喇嘛练着跳神舞。回族青年赶排民族团结舞，来迎接各族人民自己的节日。这一天全市红旗飘扬，藏、汉、回文字写的“中华人民共和国万岁”，“毛主席万岁”等标语遍贴街头。各族妇女小孩穿出了崭新的衣服，整个山城荡漾在锣鼓秧歌沙喇海螺（藏族乐器）和欢呼声中。正午，全市各族各界五千余人举行庆祝大会。中央访问团刘格平团长传达周总理“为巩固和发展人民的胜利而奋斗”的报告后，全场高呼口号，表达各族人民拥护中央人民政府的决心。康定军管会苗逢澍主任号召藏、回各族人民加强团结，争取早日解放西藏、台湾。丹巴降央丹增活佛，回族沙筱舟，藏族绅耆甲联升等热烈颂扬政协共同纲领的民族政策，并表示了藏、回、汉各族人民在中央人民政府和毛主席领导下积极支援人民解放军，争取早日将五星红旗插在喜马拉雅山上的决心。晚上全市举行火炬大游行。
