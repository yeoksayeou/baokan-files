Title: American Celebrities Issue Declaration Calling for Peace Conference, Demanding Korean Armistice and Five-Power Conference
Author:
Date: 1952-02-28
Page: 4
　
Xinhua News Agency, New York, February 22nd: According to TASS, a large group of American celebrities—including scientists, educators, union leaders, writers, and others—have issued a declaration calling for a National Peace Conference to be held in Washington on March 20th, demanding an immediate Five-Power Conference, the conclusion of a Five-Power Peace Pact, and full recognition of the right to self-government for colonial peoples.

The declaration states: “The peace aspirations of our people have recently reached an unprecedented peak. People everywhere are demanding an end to the slaughter in Korea and the establishment of genuine peace. According to a Gallup poll, 70 percent of the American people believe that talks between Truman, Churchill, and Stalin could resolve the outstanding disputes between the major powers. An increasing number of national labor leaders are already calling for a negotiated peace in Korea and elsewhere. Hundreds of thousands of working men and women are signing petitions calling for an immediate armistice in Korea and the conclusion of a Five-Power Peace Pact."

The declaration points out: "The struggle of colonial peoples for independence is raging like wildfire, particularly the struggles in Egypt, Tunisia, Iran, and Burma, which are the most intense. It must be recognized that the United States' siding with Britain and France in military or other intervention to suppress these peoples' movements for self-government is a threat to world peace and risks creating new Korean wars. The rearmament and re-Nazification of Germany foreshadows dangerous moves toward war.”

The declaration continues: “At home in America, every family and every wage earner knows that 'emergency mobilization' means the unbearable exploitation of the American people through taxes and skyrocketing prices. At the same time, wages are frozen, unemployment is increasing, and war industries are reaping exorbitant profits from death. ... Our war atmosphere provokes racial and other discrimination. ... This war scene demonstrates internal decay and moral bankruptcy.”

The declaration states: “The representatives of our government in Washington must hear directly the opinions of the men and women they represent, and act on those opinions to take peaceful action. Therefore, we call on all American people, people of all colors, all religious beliefs, all classes, and all political views, to join us in holding a grand National Peace Conference in Washington on March 20th.

“This conference will demand: An immediate armistice in Korea, and the conclusion of a lasting peace agreement after the armistice. An immediate conference of the five major powers—the United States, the Soviet Union, Britain, France, and New China—and the conclusion of a Five-Power Peace Pact. Full recognition of the right to self-government for colonial peoples. Elimination of war hysteria, and swift punishment for those who incite persecution, racial discrimination, and prejudice against blacks, Jews, and other minorities. Rejection of the $650 billion war budget. Not cannons, tanks, and bombs, but hospitals, housing, schools, roads, and electrification construction projects. A halt to the expansion of the arms race, and the implementation of global economic cooperation for security and peace. Rejection of the universal military training law. A halt to the rearmament and re-Nazification of West Germany. No ratification of the Japanese Peace Treaty."

Signatories to the declaration include: Forbes, Secretary of the Episcopal Church Social Action Alliance; Du Bois, black historian; Cronbach, retired honorary president of Hebrew Union College-Jewish Institute of Religion; Baorong, Chairman of the American Women's Peace Committee; Stouffer, President of the Iowa Farmers Union; Paul Robeson; Mrs. Simpkins, head of the National Association for the Advancement of Colored People; Morrison, nuclear physicist; Danning, responsible person of the United Electrical Workers Union; Brison, Chairman of the Marine Cooks and Stewards Union; Professor Brust, writer; Gojack, responsible person of the Electrical Workers Union; Richardson, director of the American Peace Crusade; Uphaus, director of the American Peace Crusade; and Travis, secretary of the Mine, Mill and Smelter Workers Union, among others.

### 美国名流多人发表宣言  号召举行和平大会  要求朝鲜停战举行五大国会议

1952-02-28
第4版()
专栏：

　　美国名流多人发表宣言
    号召举行和平大会
    要求朝鲜停战举行五大国会议
    【新华社二十三日讯】据塔斯社纽约二十二日讯：一大批美国名流——包括科学家、教育家、工会领袖、作家及其他人士——顷发表宣言，号召于三月二十日在华盛顿举行全国和平大会，要求立即举行五大国会议、缔结五大国和平公约，并完全承认殖民地人民的自治权利。
    宣言说：“我国人民的和平愿望，最近已经达到空前的高峰。各地人民纷纷要求停止在朝鲜的屠杀，建立真正的和平。据盖洛普民意测验表明，百分之七十的美国人民都认为：杜鲁门、邱吉尔、斯大林三人之间的会谈，可以解决大国之间的悬而未决的纠纷。越来越多的全国劳工领袖们已经在要求以谈判的方法谋求朝鲜和其他地方的和平。数十万的劳动男女，正在签名呼吁立即在朝鲜停战并缔结五大国和平公约。”
    宣言指出：“殖民地人民争取独立的斗争，正在如火如荼地进行，特别是埃及、突尼斯、伊朗和缅甸的斗争，最为激烈。必须承认，美国站在英国和法国一边进行军事的或其他的干涉，镇压这些人民争取自治的运动，是对世界和平的威胁，并有造成新的朝鲜战争的危险。德国的重新武装和重新纳粹化，预示着走向战争的危险的行动。”
    宣言接着说：“在美国国内，每一个家庭和每一个靠工资生活的人都知道：‘紧急动员’就是以赋税和扶摇直上的物价来对美国人民进行不能忍受的榨取。同时，工资被冻结，失业人数在增加，战争工业从死亡中获得暴利。……我国的战争气氛激起了种族歧视和其他歧视。……这种战争景象表明了内部的腐朽和道德的破产。”
    宣言说：“必须使在华盛顿的我国政府的代表人物们直接听到他们所代表的男女人民的意见，使他们根据这种意见，采取和平行动。因此，我们号召美国全体人民，各种肤色、各种宗教信仰、各阶层和各种政治见解的人民，和我们一起在三月二十日在华盛顿举行盛大的全国和平大会。
    “这个大会将要求：立即在朝鲜停战，并在停战以后缔结持久的和平协定。立即举行五大国——美国、苏联、英国、法国和新中国——会议并缔结五大国和平公约。完全承认殖民地人民的自治权利。消除战争歇斯底里，迅速惩办那些煽动对黑人、犹太人和其他少数民族的迫害、种族歧视和偏见的人。否决六百五十亿美元的战争预算。不要大炮、坦克和炸弹，而要医院、住宅、学校、公路和电气化的建设工程。停止扩张军备竞赛，为了安全与和平而实行世界经济合作。否决普遍军训法。制止西德的重新武装和重新纳粹化。不要批准对日和约。”
    在宣言上签名的人有：圣公会社会活动联盟秘书福贝斯，黑人历史学家杜波依斯，辛辛那提希伯来联合大学退职名誉校长克龙巴哈，美国妇女和平委员会主席巴奥朗，衣阿华州农民联盟主席史图佛，保罗·罗伯逊，全国有色人种协进会负责人辛普金斯夫人，原子核物理学家摩里逊，联合电气工人工会负责人员丹明，海船厨师侍者工会主席勃里逊，作家勃陆斯特教授，电气工人工会负责人员高杰克，美国和平十字军干事李查逊，美国和平十字军干事厄普豪斯，开采、冶炼工人工会书记特拉维斯等多人。
