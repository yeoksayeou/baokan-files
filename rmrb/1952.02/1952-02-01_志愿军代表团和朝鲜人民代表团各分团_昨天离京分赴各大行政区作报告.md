Title: Volunteer Army Delegation and Korean People's Delegation Depart Beijing Yesterday to Report to Major Administrative Regions
Author:
Date: 1952-02-01
Page: 1
Xinhua News Agency, 31st — The Chinese People's Volunteer Army Returning Delegation and the Korean People's Delegation to China have been divided into six groups: Northeast, North China, East China, Central South, Southwest, and Northwest, to report to the broad masses of people in major administrative regions on their heroic deeds in fighting the American aggressors. Except for the North China group, which will remain in Beijing for a few more days, the other five groups left Beijing successively on the 31st.

The heads of the Chinese People's Volunteer Army Returning Delegation groups are: Feng Cun (Northeast Group), Tian Bo (North China Group), Tao Yong (East China Group), Zhang Lie (Central South Group), Pei Guang (Southwest Group), and Qu Jingji (Northwest Group). The heads of the Korean People's Delegation to China groups are: Kim Kyu-beom (Northeast Group), Shen Guozhen (North China Group), Zhu Changjun (East China Group), Huang Guihan (Central South Group), Zhu Jinjing (Southwest Group), and Cai Hengmo (Northwest Group). Li Xuesan, head of the Chinese People's Volunteer Army Returning Delegation, and Hong Chunzhe, head of the Korean People's Delegation to China, went to the East China region, and Kim Chao-gyu, deputy head of the Korean People's Delegation to China, went to the Southwest region.

During their stay in Beijing, the Chinese People's Volunteer Army Returning Delegation and the Korean People's Delegation to China gave approximately 90 reports to workers, students, and citizens, with a total audience of over 122,000 people. Li Xuesan, head of the Chinese People's Volunteer Army Returning Delegation, Hong Chunzhe, head of the Korean People's Delegation to China, and some combat heroes and model workers also gave radio speeches on the Central People's Broadcasting Station.

They were warmly welcomed by people from all walks of life in Beijing and received many books, newspapers, magazines, and letters of sympathy from various organizations and people's groups. Tsinghua University sent them five boxes of gifts, with each gift inscribed with "To the Most Beloved People" or specifying it was for a particular combat hero.

### 志愿军代表团和朝鲜人民代表团各分团  昨天离京分赴各大行政区作报告

1952-02-01
第1版()
专栏：

     志愿军代表团和朝鲜人民代表团各分团
     昨天离京分赴各大行政区作报告
    【新华社三十一日讯】中国人民志愿军归国代表团和朝鲜人民访华代表团各分为东北、华北、华东、中南、西南、西北六个分团，到各大行政区向广大人民报告他们跟美国侵略军作战的英勇事迹。除华北分团仍在北京逗留几天外，其余五个分团在三十一日先后离开北京。
    中国人民志愿军归国代表团各分团团长是：东北分团团长冯村，华北分团团长田波，华东分团团长陶庸，中南分团团长张烈，西南分团团长裴光，西北分团团长曲竟济。朝鲜人民访华代表团各分团团长是：东北分团团长金圭范，华北分团团长沈国镇，华东分团团长朱昌骏，中南分团团长黄龟汉，西南分团团长朱镇璟，西北分团团长蔡亨默。中国人民志愿军归国代表团团长李雪三和朝鲜人民访华代表团团长洪淳哲赴华东区，朝鲜人民访华代表团副团长金朝奎赴西南区。
    中国人民志愿军归国代表团和朝鲜人民访华代表团在北京期间，向工人、学生、市民作了约九十次报告，听众有十二万二千多人。中国人民志愿军归国代表团团长李雪三，朝鲜人民访华代表团团长洪淳哲和一部分战斗英雄、工作模范，并在中央人民广播电台作了广播演讲。
    他们在北京受到了各界人民的热烈欢迎，收到了各机关、各人民团体赠送给他们的许多书报、刊物和慰问信。清华大学送给他们五箱礼品，每件礼品上都写着送给“最可爱的人”，或注明送给某战斗英雄。
