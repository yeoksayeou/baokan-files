Title: Marshal Choibalsan's Funeral Held Solemnly; Our Delegation Leader Nie Rongzhen Expressed Condolences at the Bier

Author:

Date: 1952-02-12
Page: 4

(Xinhua News Agency, Ulaanbaatar, February 11th) According to TASS, Ulaanbaatar: On February 9th, the Mongolian people paid their last respects to their loyal son and outstanding leader, Chairman of the Council of Ministers of the Mongolian People's Republic, and member of the Political Bureau of the Mongolian People's Revolutionary Party, Marshal Choibalsan.

　　For three days, approximately 100,000 workers, staff, herdsmen, writers, scientists, artists, students, and members of the Mongolian army walked past Marshal Choibalsan's coffin, which was placed in the hall of the Government Building.

　　On February 9th, over 30,000 working people from the capital and representatives from all sectors of the country held a memorial service in Sukhbaatar Square. At 2 p.m., the General Secretary of the Central Committee of the Mongolian People's Revolutionary Party and Deputy Chairman of the Council of Ministers of the Mongolian People's Republic, Tsedenbal, Chairman of the Presidium of the Great Hural, Bumtsend, members of the Political Bureau of the Central Committee of the Mongolian People's Revolutionary Party, Suranjab and Damba, members of the Soviet government delegation Tarasov, Marshal Budyonny, Leonov, and Prikhodov, and members of the Chinese government delegation carried Marshal Choibalsan's coffin to the venue. Funeral music filled the air.

　　As the funeral procession reached Sukhbaatar Square, Marshal Choibalsan's coffin was placed on a high platform in front of the government stand.

　　Bumtsend announced the opening of the memorial service. Tsedenbal delivered a speech, saying, "The unfortunate death of Comrade Choibalsan is a serious and irreplaceable loss for our party and our people. Comrade Choibalsan dedicated his glorious life to striving for and consolidating the freedom and independence of our people, building our people's democratic state, and building a happy life for the working people."

　　Tsedenbal added, "Comrade Choibalsan was a loyal friend of the great Soviet Union—our faithful friend and reliable supporter—and a tireless fighter for consolidating international friendship and cooperation. Therefore, the great Soviet people share our deep sorrow over the passing of our beloved leader. The people of the people's democratic countries and the hundreds of millions of people of our great neighbor, the People's Republic of China, also mourn with us. The Mongolian people express their infinite gratitude to the heroic Soviet people, the great Chinese people, the people of the people's democratic countries, and all our friends for their sincere sympathy and mourning for our loss, and pledge to continue to strive in every possible way to consolidate international friendship and cooperation, and to always be loyal to the great banner of internationalism."

　　Tsedenbal concluded with grief, "Farewell, our beloved leader, dear friend, and comrade. May the glorious life of Comrade Choibalsan become a source of courage, strength, and vigor for our people in building socialism in the motherland."

　　Next to speak was the head of the Soviet government delegation and Deputy Chairman of the Presidium of the Supreme Soviet of the Soviet Union, Tarasov. Tarasov spoke of Marshal Choibalsan's great contributions to the Mongolian people, saying, "The death of Comrade Choibalsan is a serious loss for the Mongolian people. The Soviet people also feel deeply saddened by this loss along with the Mongolian people. They will always remember this great friend of the Soviet Union."

　　Following him was the head of the government delegation of the People's Republic of China, member of the Central People's Government Committee of the People's Republic of China, and Acting Chief of General Staff of the People's Revolutionary Military Commission, Nie Rongzhen.

　　After him, a member of the Soviet government delegation, Marshal Budyonny, spoke.

　　After speeches by Sabu, a worker from the Choibalsan factory, Sonodasa, a representative of former guerrillas, and Lieutenant General Janchiv, Minister of Defense of the Mongolian People's Republic, the memorial service concluded.

　　Subsequently, members of the Political Bureau of the Central Committee of the Mongolian People's Revolutionary Party Tsedenbal, Bumtsend, Suranjab, and Damba, and other leaders of the Mongolian People's Revolutionary Party and the government, members of the Soviet government delegation Tarasov and Budyonny, and all members of the Chinese government delegation carried Marshal Choibalsan's coffin towards the cemetery—a mausoleum specially built in front of the government reviewing stand in front of the Republic Government Building. Behind the coffin followed the deceased's wife, relatives, and friends, Mongolian People's Republic government officials, and the ambassadors of the Soviet Union, the People's Republic of China, and the Democratic People's Republic of Korea.

　　The funeral began, and the band played funeral music. Three volleys of artillery fire were fired, and all factories and locomotives in the city sounded their whistles simultaneously. After the funeral, wreaths were laid on the tomb.

　　Leaders of the Mongolian People's Revolutionary Party and the Republic's government, members of the Soviet and Chinese government delegations ascended the reviewing stand, and the Ulaanbaatar garrison troops marched past the stand in formation, paying the final military salute to Marshal Choibalsan, the beloved leader of the Mongolian people.

### 乔巴山元帅葬礼隆重举行  我代表团团长聂荣臻在灵前讲话表示哀悼

1952-02-12
第4版()
专栏：

    乔巴山元帅葬礼隆重举行
    我代表团团长聂荣臻在灵前讲话表示哀悼
    【新华社十一日讯】据塔斯社乌兰巴托讯：二月九日，蒙古人民向他们忠实的儿子和杰出的领袖、蒙古人民共和国部长会议主席、蒙古人民革命党政治局委员乔巴山元帅致最后的告别。
    在三天之中，有十万左右的工人、职员、牧民、作家、科学家、艺术家、学生、蒙古军队人员在停放在政府大厦的大厅里的乔巴山元帅灵柩前面走过。
    首都三万多名劳动人民和全国各界人民的代表，于二月九日在苏赫——巴托尔广场举行追悼会。下午二时，蒙古人民革命党中央委员会总书记、蒙古人民共和国部长会议副主席泽登巴尔，大呼拉尔主席团主席布曼增迪，蒙古人民革命党中央委员会政治局委员苏兰扎布、达姆巴，苏联政府代表团团员塔拉索夫、布琼尼元帅、里昂诺夫、普里霍多夫以及中国政府代表团团员把乔巴山元帅灵柩抬到会场。会场响起了哀乐。
    葬仪行列走到苏赫——巴托尔广场，乔巴山元帅的灵柩停放在政府席前面的高台上。
    追悼会由布曼增迪宣布开始。泽登巴尔发表演说，他说：“乔巴山同志的不幸逝世，对我们党和我国人民都是严重的不可补偿的损失。乔巴山同志贡献他辉煌的一生来争取和巩固我国人民的自由和独立，建设我们的人民民主国家，建设劳动人民的幸福生活。”
    泽登巴尔又说：“乔巴山同志是伟大苏联——我们忠实的友人和可靠的支持者——的忠诚的友人，是争取巩固国际友好和合作的不懈的战士。因此，伟大的苏联人民对于我们敬爱的领袖的逝世，与我们共同深感哀痛。人民民主国家的人民、我们伟大的邻邦——中华人民共和国的亿万人民也与我们同声哀悼。蒙古人民对于英勇的苏联人民、伟大的中国人民、人民民主国家的人民以及我们的所有友人对我们的损失所表示的热诚的同情和哀悼，致以无限的感谢，并保证继续以一切可能的方法来争取巩固国际友好和合作，并始终忠诚于伟大的国际主义的旗帜。”
    泽登巴尔最后沉痛地说：“永别了，我们敬爱的领袖、亲爱的朋友和同志。愿乔巴山同志光辉的一生，成为我国人民在祖国建设社会主义当中获得勇敢、力量和蓬勃生气的源泉。”
    接着是苏联政府代表团团长、苏联最高苏维埃主席团副主席塔拉索夫发言。塔拉索夫谈到乔巴山元帅对蒙古人民的伟大功绩说：“乔巴山同志的逝世，是蒙古人民的严重损失。苏联人民同样和蒙古人民对这损失感到沉痛。他们将永远记住这位苏联的伟大朋友。”
    接着发言的是中华人民共和国政府代表团团长、中华人民共和国中央人民政府委员会委员、人民革命军事委员会代总参谋长聂荣臻。
    继他以后，是苏联政府代表团团员、苏联元帅布琼尼发言。
    在乔巴山工厂的工人萨布、前游击队代表索诺达萨、蒙古人民共和国国防部长詹契夫中将等人相继发言之后，追悼会宣告结束。
    接着，蒙古人民革命党中央委员会政治局委员泽登巴尔、布曼增迪、苏兰扎布、达姆巴和蒙古人民革命党及政府的其他领袖，苏联政府代表团团员塔拉索夫和布琼尼以及中国政府代表团各团员抬起乔巴山元帅的灵柩，走向墓地——在共和国政府大厦前的政府检阅台前特别建立的陵墓。灵柩后面跟着死者的夫人、亲属和好友，蒙古人民共和国政府官员，苏联、中华人民共和国和朝鲜民主主义人民共和国的大使。
    葬礼开始，乐队奏起哀乐。三门礼炮齐放，全市的工厂和火车头也同时鸣汽笛。葬礼完毕之后，向墓上献花圈。
    蒙古人民革命党和共和国政府领袖、苏联和中国政府代表团团员登上检阅台，乌兰巴托卫戍部队在台前列队走过，向蒙古人民敬爱的领袖乔巴山元帅致最后的军事敬礼。
