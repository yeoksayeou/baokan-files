Title: Ten Thousand People Rally in Madras, India, to Welcome Film Workers from China, the Soviet Union, Czechoslovakia, and Hungary
Author:
Date: 1952-02-19
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, Madras, February 17th: TASS, Madras, February 12th: Film workers from the Soviet Union, China, Czechoslovakia, and Hungary, participating in the International Film Exhibition, held a rally with the citizens of Madras on the Triplicane beach of the Bay of Bengal. 100,000 people participated.

The chairman of the conference, Indian film actor Krishnan, presented flowers to the members of the film delegations amidst the cheers of the crowd. Poet Katobadaya read congratulatory messages from the Madras Peace Committee, the Student Federation, the Andhra People's Drama Association, and the Tamil Nadu Progressive Writers Association. The heads of the film delegations delivered speeches at the meeting. Their speeches were repeatedly interrupted by cheers from the crowd, who continuously shouted: "Long live world peace!" "Long live the unity of the people of India, the Soviet Union, China, and the People's Democracies!"

The conference concluded with a musical performance.

### 印度马德拉斯十万人集会欢迎中苏捷匈电影工作者

1952-02-19
第4版()
专栏：

    印度马德拉斯十万人集会欢迎中苏捷匈电影工作者
    【新华社十七日讯】塔斯社马德拉斯十二日讯：参加国际电影展览会的苏联、中国、捷克斯洛伐克与匈牙利的电影工作者，和马德拉斯市民在孟加拉湾海岸的垂普里坎海滨举行集会。参加者有十万人。
    大会主席、印度电影演员克里施南在群众的欢呼声中向各电影代表团团员献花。诗人卡托巴达雅宣读了马德拉斯和平委员会、学生联合会、安德拉人民戏剧协会、泰米尔纳德进步作家协会的贺电。各电影代表团的团长在会上发表演说。他们的演说不时被群众的欢呼声所打断。群众不断高呼：“世界和平万岁！”“印度、苏联、中国和人民民主国家的人民团结万岁！”
    大会最后举行了音乐演奏。
