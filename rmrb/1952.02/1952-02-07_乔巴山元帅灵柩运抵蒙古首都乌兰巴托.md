Title: Marshal Choibalsan's Coffin Arrives in Ulaanbaatar, Mongolian Capital
Author:
Date: 1952-02-07
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, February 6th, Ulaanbaatar dispatch: According to TASS Ulaanbaatar, the working people of the Mongolian People's Republic welcomed the train carrying the remains of the great son of the Mongolian people, Marshal Choibalsan, in the capital, Ulaanbaatar, on February 5th. Early in the morning, tens of thousands of people gathered at the train station square and nearby streets, holding flags with black borders and portraits of Marshal Choibalsan.

Gathered on the platform to welcome the train were Buman-Ochir and Damba, members of the Political Bureau of the Central Committee of the Mongolian People's Revolutionary Party, government officials, members of the Presidium of the Great People's Hural, representatives of the Great People's Hural, representatives of the Soviet Embassy, Ji Yatai, Ambassador Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary of the People's Republic of China, Kim Yong-jin (transliteration), Ambassador Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea, and officials from the Chinese and Korean embassies.

At 9:00 AM, the train slowly entered the station, and mournful music filled the fresh air. The guard of honor paid military respects. Flags with black borders were lowered.

Members of the Mongolian government delegation, led by Tsendenbal, General Secretary of the Central Committee of the Mongolian People's Revolutionary Party, who had gone halfway to meet the train, and members of the Soviet government delegation attending Marshal Choibalsan's funeral, Tarasov, Marshal Budyonny, Leonov, Tsirempilon, Prikhodov, and Ivanikov, Ambassador Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary of the Soviet Union to the Mongolian People's Republic, stepped out of the train and proceeded to the carriage carrying Marshal Choibalsan's remains.

The numerous wreaths placed on the coffin in Moscow and along the way were taken out of the train carriages. Buman-Ochir, Damba, and other members of the Political Bureau of the Central Committee of the Mongolian People's Revolutionary Party, along with members of the Soviet government delegation, carefully carried the coffin from the carriage to the hearse. A solemn silence fell. The flags with black borders were lowered further, and the guard of honor paid military respects.

The funeral procession then slowly proceeded along the main street of the train station area towards the city center, passing through Sukhbaatar Square and heading towards the Government House of the Mongolian People's Republic. There, members of the Political Bureau of the Central Committee of the Mongolian People's Revolutionary Party and members of the Soviet government delegation carried the coffin into the hall of the Government House and placed it on a high platform. The hall was filled with numerous wreaths.

Tens of thousands of citizens of the capital gathered in front of the Government House. They stood in silence near the building to express their grief – Marshal Choibalsan had worked tirelessly in this building for the happiness of the Mongolian people.

The train carrying Marshal Choibalsan's remains departed from Moscow on January 29th. Numerous people gathered at the train stations along the route from Moscow to the Mongolian capital to bid a final farewell to Marshal Choibalsan. Memorial services were held at each train station, and wreaths were laid on Marshal Choibalsan's coffin.

Xinhua News Agency, February 6th, Ulaanbaatar dispatch: The Mongolian people, deeply mourning the death of their beloved leader Marshal Choibalsan, vow to steadfastly follow his instructions—to carry out socialist construction in their country and to strengthen and develop the lasting and unbreakable friendship with the Soviet Union in all aspects. Many participants at the memorial services said: The best way to commemorate our beloved leader is for each of us to work sincerely and selflessly. Herders, workers, and enterprise personnel have all pledged to work more unremittingly for the benefit of their motherland.

### 乔巴山元帅灵柩运抵蒙古首都乌兰巴托

1952-02-07
第4版()
专栏：

    乔巴山元帅灵柩运抵蒙古首都乌兰巴托
    【新华社六日讯】据塔斯社乌兰巴托讯：蒙古人民共和国首都乌兰巴托的劳动人民于二月五日迎接载运蒙古人民的伟大儿子乔巴山元帅的遗体的火车。大清早，成千成万的人拿着带有黑边的旗子和乔巴山元帅的画像，在火车站广场和附近的街道上集合。
    聚集在月台上迎接火车的，有蒙古人民革命党中央委员会政治局委员布曼增迪和达姆巴、政府官员、大人民呼拉尔主席团委员、大人民呼拉尔代表、苏联大使馆代表、中华人民共和国特命全权大使吉雅泰、朝鲜民主主义人民共和国特命全权大使金容镇（译音）、中国和朝鲜大使馆官员。
    上午九时，火车缓缓进站，清新的空气中响起哀乐。仪仗队致军人哀礼。带有黑边的旗子下降。
    先到半路上去迎接火车的以蒙古人民革命党中央委员会总书记泽登巴尔为首的蒙古政府代表团团员，随车前来参加乔巴山元帅丧礼的苏联政府代表团团员塔拉索夫、布琼尼元帅、里昂诺夫、齐伦比隆、普里霍多夫和苏联驻蒙古人民共和国特命全权大使伊凡尼科夫步出火车，前往载运乔巴山元帅的遗体的车厢。
    在莫斯科和沿路放在灵柩上的许许多多花圈被拿出火车车厢。蒙古人民革命党中央委员会政治局委员布曼增迪、达姆巴和其他委员以及苏联政府代表团团员小心地把灵柩从车厢抬到灵车上。这时出现
    一片严肃的静默。带有黑边的旗子降得更低，仪仗队致军人哀礼。
    送葬的队伍接着沿着火车站区大街向市中心缓缓前进，并越过苏赫—巴托尔广场，前往蒙古人民共和国政府大厦。在那里，蒙古人民革命党中央委员会政治局委员和苏联政府代表团团员扶着灵柩进入政府大厦的大厅，把灵柩放在高高的柩台上。灵堂里陈列着许许多多花圈。
    首都的成千上万的市民聚集在政府大厦前面。他们在大厦附近静立志哀——乔巴山元帅就在这座大厦里始终不倦地为蒙古人民的幸福而工作。
    载运乔巴山元帅灵柩的火车是在一月二十九日从莫斯科出发的。许许多多人聚集在火车经过的从莫斯科到蒙古首都的沿途各火车站上，向乔巴山元帅作最后的告别。民众在各火车站举行追悼会，向乔巴山元帅的灵柩献花圈。
    【新华社六日讯】塔斯社乌兰巴托讯：深切哀悼他们的亲爱的领袖乔巴山元帅逝世的蒙古人民，发誓要坚定不移地遵从他的训示——在他们的国家内进行社会主义建设，在各方面加强并发展和苏联间持久的牢不可破的友谊。许多追悼会上的参加者都说：纪念我们亲爱的领袖的最好的办法便是我们每个人的诚恳的忘我的劳动。牧人、工人和企业人员都保证更加不屈不挠地为他们祖国的利益而工作。
