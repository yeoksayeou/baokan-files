Title: Marshal Choibalsan's Coffin Returns to the Mongolian People's Republic; Soviet People Hold Solemn Funeral Ceremony; Tarasov and Others Form Soviet Government Delegation to Attend Funeral in Mongolia
Author:
Date: 1952-02-02
Page: 4

    Marshal Choibalsan's Coffin Returns to the Mongolian People's Republic
    Soviet People Hold Solemn Funeral Ceremony
    Tarasov and Others Form Soviet Government Delegation to Attend Funeral in Mongolia
    [Xinhua News Agency, dated 1st] According to TASS, Moscow, January 30th: The working people of the Soviet Union, together with the Mongolian people, deeply mourn the passing of Marshal Choibalsan, the glorious leader of the Mongolian people and a loyal friend of the Soviet Union.
    More than 100,000 citizens of the Soviet capital paid their respects to Marshal Choibalsan's remains, which lay in state in the Hall of Columns of the Trade Union Building.
    At seven o'clock on the morning of January 29th, mournful music was played in the hall. Present were Soviet ministers, representatives of the Supreme Soviet of the USSR and the Supreme Soviet of the Russian Soviet Federative Socialist Republic, and Party and Soviet activists from the capital.
    Standing beside the coffin were Tarasov, Vice-President of the Presidium of the Supreme Soviet of the USSR; Gorkin, Secretary of the Presidium of the Supreme Soviet of the USSR; Gromyko and Bogomolov, Deputy Ministers of Foreign Affairs of the USSR; Marshal Budyonny of the Soviet Union; and Yasnov, Chairman of the Executive Committee of the Moscow City Soviet of Working People's Deputies.
    Also standing beside the coffin were Sharav, Deputy Prime Minister of the Mongolian People's Republic; Idamjab, Ambassador Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary of the Mongolian People's Republic to the Soviet Union; embassy officials; and relatives and friends of the deceased.
    To the strains of mournful music, Marshal Choibalsan's coffin was carefully carried out of the Hall of Columns. The funeral procession slowly moved towards the Yaroslavsky Railway Station. Representatives of the working people from all twenty-five districts of Moscow had gathered there to wait.
    A guard of honor was lined up on the platform of the railway station; the Soviet and Mongolian People's Republic flags, draped with black crepe, were flown at half-mast to express grief.
    The funeral procession, led by wreaths, moved towards the special train to the sound of mournful music. Those who came to the station to see off the procession included: Bulganin, Vice-Chairman of the Council of Ministers of the USSR; Malyshev, Vice-Chairman of the Council of Ministers of the USSR; Vyshinsky, Minister of Foreign Affairs of the USSR; Marshal Vasilevsky of the Soviet Union, Minister of the Army of the USSR; Beshchev, Minister of Railways; Kumykin, Minister of Foreign Trade of the USSR; Shabanov, Deputy Minister of Health of the USSR; Marshals of the Soviet Union Sokolovsky and Govorov; General Artemyev; General Kuznetsov; and several heads and officials of embassies and diplomatic missions in Moscow.
    At eight o'clock, the funeral train began to move smoothly away from the station, heading for Ulaanbaatar, the capital of Mongolia. The Soviet government delegation accompanying the train to the capital of the Mongolian People's Republic to attend Marshal Choibalsan's funeral was composed of the following individuals: Tarasov, Vice-President of the Presidium of the Supreme Soviet of the USSR; Marshal Budyonny of the Soviet Union; Leonov, Secretary of the Executive Committee of the Moscow City Soviet of Working People's Deputies; Tsirembilon, Chairman of the Council of Ministers of the Buryat-Mongol Autonomous Republic; and Prikhodov, a responsible official of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of the USSR. The government delegation also included Ivannikov, the current Ambassador Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary of the Soviet Union to the Mongolian People's Republic in Ulaanbaatar.
    Sharav, Deputy Prime Minister of the Mongolian People's Republic, Idamjab, Ambassador Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary of the Mongolian People's Republic to the Soviet Union, and relatives and friends of the deceased also traveled on the same train.

### 乔巴山元帅灵柩运回蒙古人民共和国  苏联人民举行隆重送葬仪式  塔拉索夫等组成苏联政府代表团赴蒙古参加葬礼

1952-02-02
第4版()
专栏：

    乔巴山元帅灵柩运回蒙古人民共和国  
    苏联人民举行隆重送葬仪式  
    塔拉索夫等组成苏联政府代表团赴蒙古参加葬礼
    【新华社一日讯】据塔斯社莫斯科一月三十日讯：苏联劳动人民和蒙古人民一同深深地哀悼蒙古人民的光荣领袖、苏联的忠实友人乔巴山元帅的逝世。
    十万多名苏联首都的公民瞻仰停放在工会大厦圆柱大厅中的乔巴山元帅的遗容。
    一月二十九日晨七点，大厅中奏起哀乐。在场的有苏联各部长，苏联最高苏维埃和俄罗斯苏维埃联邦社会主义共和国最高苏维埃的代表，党和首都苏维埃活动分子。
    站在灵榇旁边的有苏联最高苏维埃主席团副主席塔拉索夫、苏联最高苏维埃主席团秘书高尔金、苏联外交部副部长葛罗米柯和波格莫洛夫、苏联元帅布琼尼、莫斯科市劳动人民代表苏维埃执行委员会主席雅斯诺夫。
    站在灵榇旁边的还有蒙古人民共和国副总理沙拉普、蒙古人民共和国驻苏特命全权大使伊丹甲布、大使馆官员和死者的亲属与朋友。
    在哀乐声中，乔巴山元帅的灵柩被谨慎地抬出圆柱大厅。送葬行列缓缓地走向雅罗斯拉夫斯基火车站。莫斯科所有的二十五个区的劳动人民的代表都已集合在那里等候。
    火车站的月台上排列了仪仗队；带有黑绸子的苏联国旗和蒙古人民共和国国旗都下半旗志哀。
    以花圈为先导的送葬行列在哀乐声中走向专车。到车站送葬的有：苏联部长会议副主席布尔加宁、苏联部长会议副主席马利雪夫、苏联外交部长维辛斯基、苏联陆军部长苏联元帅华西列夫斯基、铁道部长别什切夫、苏联对外贸易部长库米金、苏联卫生部副部长夏巴诺夫、苏联元帅索科洛夫斯基和戈沃罗夫、阿尔杰米也夫上将、库兹湼佐夫上将、若干驻莫斯科大使馆和外交代表团的首长和官员。
    八点钟，丧礼火车开始平稳地离开车站向蒙古首都乌兰巴托进发。随车到蒙古人民共和国首都去参加乔巴山元帅葬礼的苏联政府代表团由下列诸人组成：苏联最高苏维埃主席团副主席塔拉索夫、苏联元帅布琼尼、莫斯科市劳动人民代表苏维埃执行委员会书记里昂诺夫、布利亚蒙古自治共和国部长会议主席齐伦比隆和苏联外交部负责官员普里霍多夫。政府代表团还包括现在乌兰巴托的苏联驻蒙古人民共和国特命全权大使伊凡尼科夫。
    蒙古人民共和国副总理沙拉普、蒙古人民共和国驻苏联特命全权大使伊丹甲布及死者的亲属朋友也同车回去。
