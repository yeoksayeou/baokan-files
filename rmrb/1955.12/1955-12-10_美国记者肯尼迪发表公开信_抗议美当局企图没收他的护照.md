Title: American Journalist Kennedy Publishes Open Letter Protesting U.S. Authorities' Attempt to Confiscate His Passport
Author:
Date: 1955-12-10
Page: 4
Xinhua News Agency, Geneva, 8th - Tass, Geneva, 8th: The Tass correspondent in Geneva has received a letter from American journalist Stetson Kennedy. Kennedy hopes to have this letter published to the world public.

The letter points out that although U.S. Secretary of State Dulles, on the eve of the Four Foreign Ministers Conference, issued a statement saying that U.S. citizens could visit any country except China with a U.S. passport, U.S. authorities are attempting to confiscate Kennedy's passport simply because he has visited many countries in Eastern Europe.

Kennedy said: "I was the first American journalist to visit China, and I expressed my belief that China does not want war, but it has the right to liberate Taiwan Island. An American expressing such an opinion made Mr. Dulles and the expansionists on Wall Street unhappy. Obviously, the people at the U.S. State Department believe that by confiscating my passport, they can force me to return to the United States immediately, and make me fall into the hands of violent and unreasonable judges."

Kennedy pointed out: "Wherever I am, when I write articles, I always follow the good principles of my hometown of Florida: 'Do not lie to anyone, do not slander anyone.' But obviously, an objective attitude is not in line with Mr. Dulles's purposes."

Kennedy concluded: "Based on the State Department's attempt to confiscate my passport, public opinion can correctly assess whether Mr. Dulles's so-called new travel freedom is just a propaganda trick, because those who are allowed to travel to socialist countries can only be Mr. Dulles's messengers, not objective observers who criticize certain aspects of American policy."

### 美国记者肯尼迪发表公开信  抗议美当局企图没收他的护照

1955-12-10
第4版()
专栏：

    美国记者肯尼迪发表公开信
    抗议美当局企图没收他的护照
    新华社八日讯　塔斯社日内瓦八日讯：塔斯社驻日内瓦记者收到美国新闻记者斯蒂逊·肯尼迪的一封信。肯尼迪希望能把这封信向世界公众发表。
    信中指出，尽管美国国务卿杜勒斯在四国外交部长会议前夕发表声明说，美国公民可以持美国护照访问中国以外的任何国家，美国当局却企图没收肯尼迪的护照，只是因为他到过东欧的许多国家。
    肯尼迪说：“我是第一个访问中国的美国记者，我曾表示相信，中国不要战争，但是它有权解放台湾岛。一个美国人表示了这种意见，使杜勒斯先生和华尔街的扩张主义分子感到不愉快。显然，美国国务院的人认为，没收了我的护照，他们就可以迫使我立刻回美国，而使我落入凶暴无理的裁判者手中”。
    肯尼迪指出：“无论在那里，我写文章的时候，始终遵循着我的故乡佛罗里达的良好的原则：‘不要对任何人撒谎，不要诽谤任何人’。但是，显然客观的态度是同杜勒斯先生的目的不相符合的”。
    肯尼迪最后写道：“根据国务院企图没收我的护照这件事，舆论可以正确地估价杜勒斯先生所说的新的旅行自由是不是只是宣传花招，因为获准到社会主义国家去旅行的只能是杜勒斯先生的信使，而不是对美国政策某些方面加以批评的客观的观察家”。
