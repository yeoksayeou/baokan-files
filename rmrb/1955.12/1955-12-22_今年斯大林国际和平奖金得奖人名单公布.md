Title: List of Winners of the Stalin International Peace Prize for This Year Announced
Author:
Date: 1955-12-22
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, Moscow, December 21st: TASS reported from Moscow on the 20th that the Committee for International Stalin Prizes "For Strengthening Peace Among Nations" held a meeting from December 7th to 9th.

The meeting discussed the awarding of the 1955 Stalin International Prizes "For Strengthening Peace Among Nations" and adopted a resolution on the matter. The resolution decided to award the 1955 Stalin International Peace Prize to the following six individuals, in recognition of their outstanding contributions to the struggle for maintaining and consolidating peace:

    Lázaro Cárdenas (Former President of Mexico),
    Muhammad al-Ashmar (Syrian Public Figure),
    Josef Wirth (Former Chancellor of Germany, Federal Republic of Germany),
    Tôn Đức Thắng (Chairman of the National Committee of the Vietnam Fatherland Front),
    Kanjitsu Guan (Japanese Artist),
    Pastor Ragnar Forbech (Pastor of a Church in Oslo, Norway).

Xinhua News Agency, Moscow, December 21st: Moscow newspapers published an article by Academician Skobeltsyn, Chairman of the Committee for International Stalin Prizes "For Strengthening Peace Among Nations," introducing the winners of the 1955 Stalin International Peace Prize.

In the article, Academician Skobeltsyn pointed out that the Committee for International Stalin Prizes "For Strengthening Peace Among Nations" commends the outstanding achievements of a new group of representatives of peace-loving democratic forces from all countries around the world. These outstanding peace fighters represent different countries and continents, and different political tendencies and religious beliefs. However, they are united by a common thread: hatred of war, and loyalty to the cause of peace and friendship among all peoples, and to the cause of the social progress of all mankind.

    Lázaro Cárdenas, former President of Mexico and winner of the 1955 Stalin International Peace Prize, is an outstanding Mexican politician and social activist. He dedicated his entire life to the struggle for the independence, freedom, and happiness of the Mexican people. He advocates peaceful coexistence between countries with different socio-political systems and opposes the preparation and initiation of new world wars.

    Muhammad al-Ashmar, a progressive figure in Syria, actively participated in the struggle against foreign colonial oppression and for the freedom of the Syrian people and other peoples of the Near East. Since 1950, he has been an active peace fighter, actively participating in successive World Peace Congresses and World Peace Council meetings. He considers improving the people's welfare as the basic goal of his social and religious activities, and he believes that this goal is inseparable from defending peace.

    Josef Wirth, the first person from the Federal Republic of Germany to receive the Stalin International Peace Prize, is a veteran German politician and social activist. Since 1948, he has been dedicated to the cause of reunifying his motherland into a unified, democratic, and peaceful country. He opposes the Bonn and Paris Treaties, which lead to the revival of German militarism, and supports the idea of European collective security. He is one of the chairmen of the German League for Unity, Peace, and Freedom, and is a member of the World Peace Council.

    Tôn Đức Thắng, Chairman of the National Committee of the Vietnam Fatherland Front, has fought for the freedom of the peoples of Indochina for fifty years. After the victory of the August Revolution in 1945, he actively participated in the struggle against foreign colonialists. He is an active participant in the construction of a new life in the Democratic Republic of Vietnam and an active peace fighter.

    Kanjitsu Guan, a famous Japanese female social activist, is a Japanese patriot who has made outstanding contributions to the struggle to defend and consolidate peace.

    Pastor Forbech of the Oslo City Church is an active participant in the Norwegian peace movement. He has consistently opposed Norway's participation in aggressive military blocs and advocates Norway's maintaining neutrality.

### 今年斯大林国际和平奖金得奖人名单公布

1955-12-22
第4版()
专栏：

    今年斯大林国际和平奖金得奖人名单公布
    新华社二十一日讯　据塔斯社莫斯科二十日讯：“加强国际和平”斯大林国际奖金委员会从十二月七日到九日举行了会议。
    会议讨论了关于授予一九五五年“加强国际和平”斯大林国际奖金的问题，并且就这个问题通过了决议。决议决定授予下列六人以一九五五年“加强国际和平”斯大林国际奖金，以表扬他们在维护和巩固和平的斗争中所作的卓越贡献：
    拉·卡德纳斯（墨西哥前总统），
    穆·阿契玛尔阿訇（叙利亚公众领袖），
    约·维尔特（德国前总理，德意志联邦共和国），
    孙德胜（越南祖国战线全国委员会主席），
    关鉴子（日本艺术界人士），
    拉·福贝克牧师（挪威奥斯陆市教堂牧师）。
    新华社据塔斯社莫斯科二十一日讯　莫斯科的报纸发表了“加强国际和平”斯大林国际奖金委员会主席斯科贝尔琴院士的文章，介绍一九五五年斯大林国际和平奖金得奖人。
    斯科贝尔琴院士在文章中指出，“加强国际和平”斯大林国际奖金委员会表扬世界各国爱好和平的民主力量的一批新代表的卓越功绩。这些杰出的和平战士代表着不同的国家和大陆，代表着不同的政治倾向和宗教信仰。但是，有一个共同点把他们联合在一起，这就是对战争的憎恨、对和平和各国人民之间的友好事业和全人类社会进步事业的忠诚。
      一九五五年斯大林国际和平奖金得奖人、前墨西哥总统拉·卡德纳斯，是墨西哥杰出的政治家和社会活动家。他把全部的生活贡献给了争取墨西哥人民的独立、自由和幸福的斗争。他主张不同社会政治制度的国家和平共处，反对准备和发动新的世界战争。
    叙利亚的进步人士穆·阿契玛尔阿訇曾经积极地参加了反对外国殖民压迫、争取叙利亚人民和近东其他各国人民的自由的斗争。他从一九五○年开始成为积极的和平战士，积极参加了历届世界和平大会和世界和平理事会会议。他把提高人民的生活福利作为自己的社会和宗教活动的基本目标，他相信这一目标是同保卫和平分不开的。
    德意志联邦共和国第一个获得斯大林国际和平奖金的约·维尔特，是德国的一位老政治家和社会活动家。从一九四八年起，他致力于把祖国重新统一成为一个统一、民主和和平的国家的事业。他反对导致德国军国主义复活的波恩和巴黎条约，拥护欧洲集体安全的主张。他是德国人争取统一、和平和自由联盟的主席之一，并且是世界和平理事会的理事。
    越南祖国战线全国委员会主席孙德胜为印度支那各族人民的自由进行了五十年的斗争。一九四五年八月革命胜利后，他积极地参加了反抗外国殖民者的斗争。他是越南民主共和国建设新生活的积极参加者，也是积极的和平战士。
    著名的日本女社会活动家关鉴子是日本的爱国者，她在争取保卫和巩固和平的斗争中有着卓越的功绩。
    奥斯陆市教堂的牧师福贝克是挪威和平运动的积极参加者。他一贯反对挪威参加侵略性军事集团，主张挪威保守中立。
