Title: Nehru Dissatisfied with Anti-India Propaganda in British Newspapers
Author:
Date: 1955-12-21
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, New Delhi, December 20th. According to the Indian Press Trust, Indian Prime Minister Nehru said in the Council of States (Rajya Sabha) of the Indian Parliament on the 19th that "some British newspapers have almost made it a habit to publish unfounded and fabricated news about India." He said that he must express his "deep regret" about this situation.

Prime Minister Nehru made these remarks when a member of the Council of States raised the issue of anti-India propaganda by British newspapers.

When asked whether certain foreign journalists in India should be held responsible for this propaganda, Nehru said: "Probably so."

### 尼赫鲁不满英报的反印宣传

1955-12-21
第4版()
专栏：

    尼赫鲁不满英报的反印宣传
    新华社新德里二十日电　据印度报业托辣斯报道，印度总理尼赫鲁十九日在印度国会联邦院说，“英国有一些报纸几乎已经形成发表有关印度的毫无根据和凭空捏造的消息的习惯”。他说，他对这种情况必须表示“感到深为遗憾”。
    尼赫鲁总理是在联邦院中有议员提出关于英国报刊进行反印宣传的问题的时候说这番话的。
    有人问到是不是在印度的某些外国记者应该对这种宣传负责。尼赫鲁说：“大概是的。”
