Title: Mourning the Passing of Prominent Peace Fighter Ooyama Ikuo
Author:
Date: 1955-12-02
Page: 1

Zhou Enlai's Condolence Message to Mrs. Ooyama Yaneko:
	I was deeply saddened to hear of the unfortunate passing of Mr. Ooyama Ikuo. Please accept my sincere condolences.

	Mr. Ooyama Ikuo made outstanding contributions to promoting friendship between the Chinese and Japanese people, striving for peace, independence, democracy, and freedom in Japan, and safeguarding peace in Asia and the world. His passing has not only deprived the Japanese people of a great peace fighter but has also deprived the Chinese people of a close friend. However, we believe that as the Chinese and Japanese people mourn the passing of Mr. Ooyama Ikuo, they will draw closer together and work harder to promote friendly cooperation between the two countries and safeguard peace in Asia and the world.
                     Zhou Enlai
                      November 30, 1955

China Peace Committee's Condolence Message to the Japan Peace Committee and to Mrs. Ooyama Yaneko:

	Upon learning of the passing of Mr. Ooyama Ikuo, the China Peace Committee offers its deepest condolences to the Japan Peace Committee and to Mrs. Ooyama Yaneko.

	Mr. Ooyama Ikuo dedicated his life to the peace, independence, and freedom of Japan, and to peace in Asia and the world. Mr. Ooyama's name will live forever in our hearts. We firmly believe that although the Japanese people have lost Mr. Ooyama, they will unite more closely with peace-loving people around the world and strive tirelessly to safeguard peace in Asia and the world.
              China Peace Committee
                    November 30, 1955

All-China Federation of Trade Unions and Two Other Organizations' Condolence Message to the Japan Peace Committee:

	Upon learning of the passing of the great peace fighter and close friend of the Chinese people, Mr. Ooyama Ikuo, we offer our deepest condolences.
            All-China Federation of Trade Unions
               All-China Democratic Women's Federation
                All-China Youth Federation
                  December 1, 1955

China Relief Association's Condolence Message to the Japan National Relief Association at 7-12 Shiba Shimbashi, Minato-ku, Tokyo:

	We are deeply saddened to learn of the passing of Mr. Ooyama Ikuo, advisor to your association and chairman of the Japan Peace Committee. Mr. Ooyama dedicated his life to the cause of peace and made many contributions to promoting the Japanese National Relief Movement and Sino-Japanese friendship. We extend our deepest sympathy to your association.
               China Relief Association
         December 1, 1955

Liu Ningyi, Secretary-General of the Asian and Pacific Regions Peace Liaison Committee's Condolence Message to the Japan Peace Committee and to Mrs. Ooyama Yaneko:

	I was deeply saddened to hear of the passing of Mr. Ooyama Ikuo. I extend my deepest condolences and sympathy to the Japan Peace Committee and to Mrs. Ooyama Yaneko.

	The passing of Mr. Ooyama Ikuo is a great loss to the peace movement in Japan and the Asian and Pacific regions. However, we firmly believe that the peace-loving people of Japan and the people of all countries in the Asian and Pacific regions will continue to strive for peace in Asia and the Pacific region, inspired by Mr. Ooyama Ikuo's spirit of dedicating his life to the cause of peace.
        Liu Ningyi, Secretary-General of the Asian and Pacific Regions Peace Liaison Committee
         November 30, 1955

Comrade Liao Chengzhi and Others' Condolence Message to the Japan Peace Committee and to Mrs. Ooyama Yaneko:

	We were deeply saddened to hear of the passing of Mr. Ooyama Ikuo. Please allow us to extend our deepest condolences and heartfelt sympathy to you.

	Mr. Ooyama Ikuo fought for the independence and freedom of Japan and for peace in Asia and the world, day after day, for decades. He is a shining example for every one of us working for peace. Let us turn our grief into strength, unite closely, and achieve greater success in the cause of defending peace in Asia and the world, in memory of our beloved friend, Mr. Ooyama Ikuo.
         Liao Chengzhi Liu Guanyi Tang Mingzhao Zheng Senyu Zhao Anbo
             November 30, 1955

### 哀悼著名和平战士大山郁夫逝世

1955-12-02
第1版()
专栏：

    哀悼著名和平战士大山郁夫逝世
    周恩来同志唁电大山柳子夫人：
    惊闻大山郁夫先生不幸逝世，无任哀悼。我谨向你致以诚挚的慰问。
    大山郁夫先生对于促进中日两国人民的友好、争取日本的和平、独立、民主、自由和保卫亚洲及世界的和平有着卓越的贡献。他的逝世不但使日本人民失去了一位伟大的和平战士，而且也使中国人民失去了一位亲密的朋友。但是我们相信，中日两国人民在哀悼大山郁夫先生逝世的时候，将会更加接近，共同为推进中日两国人民的友好合作和保卫亚洲及世界的和平作出更大的努力。
                     周恩来
                      一九五五年十一月三十日
    我保卫世界和平委员会唁电日本拥护和平委员会并转大山柳子夫人：
    惊悉大山郁夫先生病逝，中国人民保卫世界和平委员会谨向日本拥护和平委员会和大山柳子夫人致以沉痛的哀悼。
    大山郁夫先生为了日本的和平、独立、自由和亚洲以及世界的和平，献出了自己的一生。大山先生的名字将永远活在我们的心里。我们坚信，日本人民虽然丧失了大山先生，但是他们将同全世界爱好和平的人民更加紧密地团结起来，为保卫亚洲和世界的和平而奋斗不懈。
              中国人民保卫世界和平委员会
                    一九五五年十一月三十日
    我全国总工会等三团体唁电日本拥护和平委员会：
    惊闻伟大的和平战士、中国人民亲密的朋友大山郁夫先生逝世，谨致深切的悼念。
            中华全国总工会
               中华全国民主妇女联合会
                中华全国民主青年联合会
                  一九五五年十二月一日
    中国人民救济总会唁电日本东京都港区芝新桥七之十二文化工业会馆日本国民救援会：
    惊闻贵会顾问、日本拥护和平委员会主席大山郁夫先生逝世，不胜悲痛。大山先生生平致力于和平事业，并在促进日本的国民救援运动和中日友好方面有很多的贡献。我们谨向贵会表示深切慰问。
               中国人民救济总会
         一九五五年十二月一日
    亚洲及太平洋区域和平联络委员会秘书长刘宁一唁电日本拥护和平委员会并转大山柳子夫人：
    惊悉大山郁夫先生逝世的消息，不胜沉痛，我谨向日本拥护和平委员会和大山柳子夫人表示深切的哀悼和慰问。
    大山郁夫先生的逝世，对于日本和平运动和亚洲及太平洋区域的和平运动来说是一个巨大的损失。但是我们坚信，爱好和平的日本人民和亚洲及太平洋区域各国人民将在大山郁夫先生终身为和平事业而奋斗的精神的鼓舞下，继续为争取亚洲和太平洋区域的和平而奋斗。
        亚洲及太平洋区域和平联络委员会秘书长  刘宁一
         一九五五年十一月三十日
    廖承志同志等唁电日本拥护和平委员会并转大山柳子夫人：
    听到大山郁夫先生逝世的消息，我们感到万分悲痛，请允许我们向你们致以沉痛的哀悼和亲切的慰问。
    大山郁夫先生为日本的独立、自由和亚洲及世界的和平而奋斗，数十年如一日，他是我们每个和平工作者的光辉榜样。让我们化悲痛为力量，紧密地团结起来，在保卫亚洲和世界和平的事业中取得更大的成就，以纪念我们敬爱的朋友大山郁夫先生不幸的逝世。
         廖承志　刘贯一　唐明照  郑森禹　赵安博
             一九五五年十一月三十日
