Title: More Than 2.5 Million People in North Vietnam Accuse Ngo Dinh Diem of Atrocities
Author:
Date: 1955-12-06
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, Hanoi, December 5th: According to the Vietnam News Agency on the 4th, from September to the end of November, more than 2.5 million people in North Vietnam submitted nearly 7,000 petitions to the International Commission in Vietnam, condemning the Ngo Dinh Diem regime for violating the armistice agreement and persecuting former resistance personnel and civilians. They also demanded that the International Commission conduct an investigation as soon as possible into the terrorist incidents such as the massacre of 92 residents in Huong Dien commune, Quang Tri province, and the killing of female teacher Nguyen Thi Dieu in Saigon by the Ngo Dinh Diem regime.

At the same time, people in various parts of North Vietnam held rallies one after another during this period to protest the above-mentioned violations of the armistice agreement by the Ngo Dinh Diem regime.

### 越南北方二百五十多万人控诉吴庭艳暴行

1955-12-06
第4版()
专栏：

    越南北方二百五十多万人控诉吴庭艳暴行
    新华社河内五日电　据越南通讯社四日讯：从九月到十一月底，越南北方已经有二百五十多万人向越南国际委员会提出了将近七千封请愿书，谴责吴庭艳政权违反停战协定迫害前抗战人员和平民的行为。他们并且要求国际委员会早日就吴庭艳政权在广治省香田乡屠杀九十二个居民和在西贡残杀女教员阮氏妙等恐怖事件进行调查。
    同时，越南北方各地人民在这一期间，还纷纷举行集会，抗议吴庭艳政权上述违反停战协定的行为。
