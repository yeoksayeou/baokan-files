Date: 1950-05-14

　　Reports from the Peace Front

　　On April 14th, a train loaded with American military supplies, on its way to Cherbourg, encountered demonstrations and protest strikes by the French people everywhere. At every station, supporters of peace sounded sirens, announcing the arrival of the arms train and preventing it from moving forward. Thus, for a full seven hours, the train traveled only ninety kilometers.
　　　　　　　×　　　　　　　　　　×　　　　　　　　　　×
　　In Cartres, France, peace supporters lay on the tracks to prevent a train carrying Republican Security Corps guards from going to the port of Saint-Nazaire, because American military supplies in the freighters there had not yet been unloaded due to the workers' persistent refusal to transport them. As a result, the train was stopped for several hours.
　　　　　　　×　　　　　　　　　　×　　　　　　　　　　×
　　Archdeacon Johnson of Canterbury stated in a telegram: the signatures of one million peace petitioners are enough to trouble the warmongers; ten million signatures can make them hesitate; one hundred million signatures can destroy their evil plans and save the world.
　　　　　　　×　　　　　　　　　　×　　　　　　　　　　×
　　　　　×
　　In Tampere, Finland, an elderly woman of seventy, Anna Hortunainen, has already collected a thousand signatures calling for peace and is continuing her sacred work.
　　　　　×　　　　　　　　　　×　　　　　　　　　　×
　　Romanian female writer Rossandra Perante declared at the conference of the Romanian Writers' Union: "I guarantee that every page I write will be dedicated to the cause of peace."
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　(Compiled and translated by this newspaper)

### 来自和平战线的报道

1950-05-14
第4版()
专栏：

　　来自和平战线的报道
    四月十四日，一辆满载着美国军火的火车，在驶向瑟堡的途中，到处遇到法国人民的示威与抗议罢工。在每一个车站上，和平的拥护者们响起警笛，宣布军火列车的到来，并阻止它再向前进。这样，整整七小时，那辆列车只行驶了九十公里。

    在法国卡尔特尔斯，和平拥护者卧在铁轨上，为了要阻止一辆运送共和国保安团的卫队的火车到圣那最尔港口去，由于那里货船中的美国军火在工人坚持拒运下还未卸下。结果使火车停顿了几个钟点。
