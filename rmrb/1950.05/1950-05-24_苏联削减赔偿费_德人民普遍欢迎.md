Title: Soviet Union Reduces Reparations Fees, German People Universally Welcome
Author:
Date: 1950-05-24
Page: 4

　　Soviet Union Reduces Reparations Fees
    German People Universally Welcome
    [This Newspaper] News: Xinhua News Agency, Berlin, May 21st dispatch: The working people of the German Democratic Republic are very satisfied with the Soviet government's decision to reduce reparations fees. According to the "German News," five outstanding workers at the former Krupp factory in Magdeburg decided to write a letter to the German people's great friend, Marshal Stalin, expressing their gratitude, after hearing the decision.
    Erich Wilde, a famous worker from Saxony, said: "This decision is a testament to the great trust that the Soviet Union has placed in the German people. We must move forward impartially along the path of seeking a better life through our own efforts, in order to gloriously live up to this trust. This step by the Soviet government will undoubtedly be of great help to the most rapid economic leap forward in our republic."

### 苏联削减赔偿费  德人民普遍欢迎

1950-05-24
第4版()
专栏：

　　苏联削减赔偿费
    德人民普遍欢迎
    【本报讯】据新华社讯：塔斯社柏林二十一日电：德意志民主共和国劳动人民对苏联政府关于削减赔偿费的决定，非常满意。据《德意志新闻报》称，马德堡的前克虏伯工厂中的五位卓越的工人，听到了这个决定之后，就决定写信给德国人民伟大的朋友斯大林大元帅，表示感激。
    萨克森的著名工人埃里奇·威尔特说：“这个决定乃是苏联对德国人民所表示的极大信任的证明。我们必须不偏不倚地沿着以我们自己的力量求得较好的生活的道路前进，借以光荣地不负此种信任。苏联政府的这一步骤，对于我们共和国最迅速的经济跃进，无疑地将有重大的帮助。”
