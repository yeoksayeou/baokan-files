    “Now this man can be released! He is no longer a danger.”
    This political satire, “Now this man can be released! He is no longer a danger,” by Daumier, was created in 1834. The painting depicts the July Monarchy tyrant Louis-Philippe and a prosecutor standing beside the deathbed of a participant in an uprising. This participant, emaciated and reduced to skin and bones from torture, lies forlornly on a wooden bed in a prison cell, one limp hand dangling to the floor, yet still bound by iron chains. The king is feeling the faint pulse of his other hand. There is no struggle here; the struggle is already over. But this desolate moment is all the more tragic, a powerful condemnation of the rulers! This work is full of the author's sharp satire on the rulers' brutality and hypocrisy, and can teach people to be indignant and rise up to continue the struggle.

### “现在这个人可以释放了！他再没有危险了。”（图片）

1958-12-23
第8版()
专栏：

    “现在这个人可以释放了！他再没有危险了。”
    杜米埃的这幅政治讽刺画“现在这个人可以释放了！他再没有危险了。”是在1834年创作的。画中描绘了法国七月王朝的暴君路易—菲力浦和检察官站在将要死去的一位起义参加者的病床旁边。这位参加过起义的人，被折磨得皮包着骨头，凄凉地躺在囚室的木床上，一只无力的手垂在地板上，但仍然被铁锁链子束缚着。国王按着他的另一只手的微弱的脉搏。这里没有斗争，斗争已经过去了。但是这一凄凉的时刻却更富于悲剧性，对于统治者真是一个强有力的谴责！这一作品充满了作者对于统治者残暴、虚假的尖锐讽刺，能教导人们愤恨和起来继续斗争。
