Title: Representatives from All Sectors of Japan Petition the Diet, Demanding a Ban on Japanese Nuclear Armament
Author:
Date: 1958-12-17
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, Tokyo, December 15th: Representatives from the Japan Council Against Atomic and Hydrogen Bombs and other organizations petitioned the Japanese Diet today, demanding a ban on Japanese nuclear armament.

    Prior to the petition, a rally was held, passing a resolution containing five demands. The five demands are: 1. The government is requested to urge countries possessing nuclear weapons to cease nuclear weapons testing and to strive for the conclusion of an agreement to immediately, unconditionally, and permanently ban the testing of nuclear weapons. 2. The government is requested to issue a declaration that Japan will not engage in nuclear armament, and to announce a ban on the transportation of nuclear weapons into Japanese territory and a ban on the nuclear armament of the Japanese "Self-Defense Forces." 3. The government is requested to abolish the Japan-U.S. "Security Treaty." 4. The government is requested to halt the conspiracy to revise the "Police Duties Execution Law" and formulate an "Anti-Espionage Law." 5. The government is requested to formulate laws to treat atomic bomb victims and provide relief to their surviving families, and to take concrete measures to promote investigation and research for the treatment of atomic diseases.

    During the petition, the representatives submitted the rally's resolution and resolutions brought from various regions by the representatives to the Speakers of the House of Representatives and the House of Councillors, the Chief Cabinet Secretary, and the Minister of Health and Welfare. They also met with representatives of Diet members from various political parties, requesting their assistance.

    The more than one hundred representatives who petitioned the Diet included representatives from the central council and twenty-eight prefectural councils of the Japan Council Against Atomic and Hydrogen Bombs, as well as representatives from the General Council of Trade Unions of Japan (Sohyo), the Japan Coal Miners' Union, the Federation of Japanese Women's Organizations, the Christian Peace Association, Buddhist monks from Myohoji Temple, representatives from the Japan Confederation of A- and H-Bomb Sufferers Organizations, and Diet members from the Liberal Democratic Party and the Socialist Party.

    The international joint action month to prevent nuclear armament, launched by the Japan Council Against Atomic and Hydrogen Bombs in October, officially concluded.

### 日本各界代表向国会请愿  要求禁止日本进行核武装

1958-12-17
第6版()
专栏：

    日本各界代表向国会请愿
    要求禁止日本进行核武装
    新华社15日讯  东京消息：日本禁止原子弹和氢弹协议会等的代表今天到日本国会请愿，要求禁止日本进行核武装。
    在请愿之前举行了大会，通过了包含五项要求的决议。五项要求是：一、要求政府促使持有核武器的国家停止核武器试验，并且努力促使缔结立即无条件永久禁止试验核武器的协定。二、要求政府发表日本不进行核武装的宣言，并且宣布禁止把核武器运进日本境内，禁止日本“自卫队”进行核武装。三、要求政府废除日美“安全条约”。四、要求政府停止修改“警察官职务执行法”、制订“防谍法”的阴谋。五、要求政府制订为治疗原子弹受害者和救济他们的遗属的生活的法律，并且采取具体措施推进为治疗原子病的调查和研究。
    代表们在请愿时把大会决议和代表们分别从各地带来的决议递交给众议院和参议院议长、内阁官房长官以及厚生相。他们还会见了各党派的国会议员代表，要求他们给予协助。
    到国会请愿的一百多名代表，除了协议会中央和二十八个府、县单位协议会的代表之外，还有日本工会总评议会、日本煤矿工会、全日本妇女团体联合会、基督教徒和平会、妙法寺和尚、日本原子弹氢弹受害者团体协议会的代表以及自由民主党、社会党的国会议员等。
    日本禁止原子弹和氢弹协议会从10月开始展开的阻止核武装国际共同行动月，至此正式结束。
