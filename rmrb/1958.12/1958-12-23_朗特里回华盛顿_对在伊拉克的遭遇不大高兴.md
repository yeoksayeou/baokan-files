Title: Rountree Returns to Washington: Not Happy with His Experience in Iraq
Author:
Date: 1958-12-23
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, Washington, 22nd: U.S. Assistant Secretary of State for Near Eastern Affairs, Rountree, said on the 21st that he was "not happy" about the demonstrations and protests by the Iraqi people during his recent visit to Baghdad.

Rountree made these remarks upon returning to Washington after engaging in political conspiracies in the Middle East. He said that the demonstrations by the Iraqi people against his visit left him with a "very unpleasant impression."

Rountree declined to disclose the content of his talks with leaders of some Arab countries during this visit. Sources say that Rountree has already reported to the State Department on his visits to various capitals and will draft a memorandum on the Middle East in the next few days.

### 朗特里回华盛顿  对在伊拉克的遭遇不大高兴

1958-12-23
第5版()
专栏：

    朗特里回华盛顿
    对在伊拉克的遭遇不大高兴
    新华社22日讯  华盛顿消息：美国负责中东事务的助理国务卿朗特里，21日说，他对于最近在访问巴格达期间遭到伊拉克人民的示威抗议“不大高兴”。
    朗特里是在前往中东搞政治阴谋后返回华盛顿时说这些话的。他说，伊拉克人民反对他的访问的示威给他留下“很不愉快的印象”。
    朗特里拒绝透露他在这次访问中同一些阿拉伯国家领导人会谈的内容。有消息说，朗特里已经向国务院作了他访问各国首都的报告，并且将在最近几天中拟定一项关于中东的备忘录。
