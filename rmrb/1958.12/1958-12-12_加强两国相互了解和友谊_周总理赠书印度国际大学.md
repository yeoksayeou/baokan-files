Title: Strengthening Mutual Understanding and Friendship Between the Two Countries: Premier Zhou Presents Books to Visva-Bharati University, India
Author:
Date: 1958-12-12
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, New Delhi, 10th. Chinese Ambassador to India, Pan Zili, on the morning of this day, at a presentation ceremony held in Santiniketan, presented 12,000 Chinese books to Visva-Bharati University on behalf of Premier Zhou Enlai.

Premier Zhou Enlai had promised to donate Chinese books to the university during his visit there in January 1957. The presentation ceremony was held at the university's Cheena-Bhavana (China Institute).

The Vice-Chancellor of Visva-Bharati University, renowned scientist Dr. Bose, accepted the books on behalf of the Chancellor, Prime Minister Nehru. He said that these books would be very beneficial for the study of Chinese culture. This gift will further strengthen mutual understanding and friendship between the two countries.

Dr. Bose asked Ambassador Pan Zili to convey his gratitude to Premier Zhou Enlai.

Ambassador Pan Zili said at the ceremony: "We are very pleased that the Five Principles of Peaceful Coexistence, jointly initiated by our two countries, have generated new developments in the political, economic, and cultural relations between our two countries."

### 加强两国相互了解和友谊  周总理赠书印度国际大学

1958-12-12
第4版()
专栏：

    加强两国相互了解和友谊
    周总理赠书印度国际大学
    新华社新德里10日电  中国驻印度大使潘自力，今天上午在山提尼克坦举行的一个赠礼仪式上代表周恩来总理把一万两千册中国书籍赠送给设在那里的国际大学。
    周恩来总理在1957年1月访问这个大学时曾经答应赠送中国书籍给这家大学。赠书仪式是在这家大学的中国学院举行的。
    国际大学副校长、著名科学家鲍斯博士代表校长尼赫鲁总理接受赠书。他说，这些书籍对于研究中国文化工作将是非常有益的。这一礼物将进一步加强两国的相互了解和友谊。
    鲍斯博士要求潘自力大使转达他对周恩来总理的谢意。
    潘自力大使在仪式上说：“我们非常高兴，我们两国联合创议的五项原则已经在两国的政治、经济和文化关系方面产生了新的发展。”
