Title: Thai and Philippine Foreign Ministers Express Possible Intention to Send Observers to the Asian-African Conference
Author:
Date: 1955-01-10
Page: 1
Xinhua News Agency, January 9th: Bangkok News: Thai Foreign Minister Naradhip stated on the 8th that Thailand may still be considering sending an observer to the Asian-African Conference.

	On the eve of the South Asian Five Nations Conference, Thai Acting Foreign Minister Bunchai had told a reporter from the American-edited "Bangkok Post" that Thailand would definitely not participate in the Asian-African Conference.
Xinhua News Agency, January 9th: Manila News: Philippine Vice President and Foreign Minister Garcia said on the 7th that the Philippines currently "has no preconceived notions" about the Asian-African Conference and may send an observer to participate.

	Prior to this, Garcia stated at a press conference held on the 3rd that the Philippines would not participate in the Asian-African Conference.

### 泰国和菲律宾外长分别表示  可能派观察员参加亚非会议

1955-01-10
第1版()
专栏：

    泰国和菲律宾外长分别表示
    可能派观察员参加亚非会议
    【新华社九日讯】曼谷消息：泰国外交部长那拉底八日表示，泰国可能仍在考虑派遣一名观察员参加亚非会议。
    在南亚五国会议的前夕，泰国代理外交部长班差曾经对美国人主编的“曼谷邮报”的记者说，泰国肯定地将不参加亚非会议。
    【新华社九日讯】马尼拉消息：菲律宾副总统兼外交部长加西亚在七日说，菲律宾目前对亚非会议“不抱成见”，可能派观察员参加。
    在此以前，加西亚曾在三日举行的记者招待会上表示，菲律宾将不参加亚非会议。
