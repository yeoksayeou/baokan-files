Title: Prominent Representatives of Japanese Business Circles Advocate Holding Japan-Soviet Economic Conference
Author:
Date: 1955-01-15
Page: 1
Xinhua News Agency, January 14th, dispatch from Tokyo: According to reports from Tokyo, prominent representatives of Japanese business circles advocate holding a Japan-Soviet economic conference to allow "the two countries to conduct trade on a broad basis." The report points out that these business representatives intend to request the government to hold negotiations with the Soviet Union to discuss expanding trade relations.
    These individuals also pointed out that if the government refuses to hold such negotiations, prominent industrialists and businessmen will continue to insist on the importance of establishing "official contact" with Soviet commercial representatives. They will suggest establishing contact with the Soviet delegation at the meeting of the United Nations Economic Commission for Asia and the Far East, which will be held in Tokyo in March of this year.

### 日本工商界的著名代表  主张召开日苏经济会议

1955-01-15
第1版()
专栏：

    日本工商界的著名代表
    主张召开日苏经济会议
    【新华社十四日讯】塔斯社十三日讯：据东京消息，日本工商界的著名代表主张召开日苏经济会议，以便“两国在广泛的基础上进行贸易”。消息中指出，这些工商界的代表打算请求政府同苏联举行谈判以讨论扩大贸易关系问题。
    这些人士还指出，如果政府拒绝举行这种谈判，著名的工商业家将继续坚持同苏联商务代表建立“官方接触”的重要性。他们将建议在联合国亚洲及远东经济委员会会议上和苏联代表团建立接触，这次会议将于今年三月间在东京举行。
