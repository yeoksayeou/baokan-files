Title: Leaders of the Government of the German Democratic Republic Receive Soviet Ambassador to Discuss Future Relations Between the Two Countries
Author:
Date: 1955-01-19
Page: 1
Xinhua News Agency, Berlin, 17th: According to TASS, after the conclusion of the Moscow Conference of European countries, the Soviet government exchanged views with the government of the German Democratic Republic on the issue of future relations between the Soviet Union and the German Democratic Republic. At that time, both sides took into consideration the situation: West Germany and France were both subjected to increasing pressure from certain foreign countries to accelerate the ratification of the Paris Agreements. Because of this, on January 14th, Prime Minister Otto Grotewohl of the German Democratic Republic, Deputy Prime Minister Walter Ulbricht, and Acting Foreign Minister Georg Dertinger received the Soviet Ambassador Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary to the German Democratic Republic, G.M. Pushkin, and held discussions on these issues.
    Ambassador Pushkin flew to Moscow on January 15th to report to the Soviet government.

### 德意志民主共和国政府领导人员  接见苏联大使商谈两国今后关系的问题

1955-01-19
第1版()
专栏：

    德意志民主共和国政府领导人员
    接见苏联大使商谈两国今后关系的问题
    【新华社十八日讯】据塔斯社柏林十七日讯：在欧洲国家莫斯科会议结束以后，苏联政府同德意志民主共和国政府就苏联和德意志民主共和国今后关系的问题交换了意见。当时，双方考虑到这种情况：西德和法国都受到某些外国迫使它们加速批准巴黎协定的日益加强的压力。由于这个缘故，德意志民主共和国总理奥托·格罗提渥、副总理瓦尔特·乌布利希和代理外交部长格奥克·韩克在一月十四日接见了苏联驻德意志民主共和国特命全权大使格·马·普希金，并且就这些问题进行了商谈。
    普希金大使已经在一月十五日飞往莫斯科向苏联政府作报告。
