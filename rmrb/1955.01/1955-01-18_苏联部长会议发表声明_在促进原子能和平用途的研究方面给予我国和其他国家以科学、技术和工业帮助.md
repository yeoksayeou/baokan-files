Title: Announcement by the Council of Ministers of the Soviet Union on Providing Scientific, Technical, and Industrial Assistance to Our Country and Other Countries in Promoting Research on the Peaceful Uses of Atomic Energy
Author:
Date: 1955-01-18
Page: 1

    Announcement by the Council of Ministers of the Soviet Union
    Providing Scientific, Technical, and Industrial Assistance to Our Country and Other Countries in Promoting Research on the Peaceful Uses of Atomic Energy

    (Xinhua News Agency, Moscow, January 17th) TASS, Moscow, January 17th: The following is the Announcement by the Council of Ministers of the Soviet Union regarding the Soviet Union's provision of scientific, technical, and industrial assistance to other countries in promoting research on the peaceful uses of atomic energy:

    The Soviet government has always attached great importance to the peaceful uses of atomic energy and has decided to provide other countries with scientific, technical, and industrial assistance to establish scientific experimental centers to develop research in nuclear physics and the peaceful uses of atomic energy.

    The Soviet government has already provided extensive assistance to the People's Republic of China, the People's Republic of Poland, the Czechoslovak Republic, the Romanian People's Republic, and the German Democratic Republic, including designing, supplying equipment, and constructing experimental atomic piles with a thermal power of up to 5,000 kilowatts, and atomic particle accelerators. Attention will also be paid to supplying these countries with the necessary quantity of atomic piles and fissionable materials for scientific research. An understanding has been reached that the aforementioned countries will supply the Soviet Union with the necessary raw materials. Scientists and engineers from these countries will have the opportunity to understand the scientific research conducted by the Soviet Union in the peaceful use of atomic energy and to understand the operation of experimental atomic piles.

    The establishment of the aforementioned scientific experimental centers will enable these countries to develop large-scale nuclear research, use experimental atomic piles to produce a sufficient number of radioactive isotopes to meet the needs of medicine, biology, and other sciences and engineering, and to train the necessary scientists and engineers for the development of research on the peaceful uses of atomic energy.

    The Soviet Union is considering the question of expanding the scope of countries it can assist in scientific research and experimentation in the development of the peaceful uses of atomic energy.

### 苏联部长会议发表声明  在促进原子能和平用途的研究方面给予我国和其他国家以科学、技术和工业帮助

1955-01-18
第1版()
专栏：

    苏联部长会议发表声明
    在促进原子能和平用途的研究方面给予我国和其他国家以科学、技术和工业帮助
    【新华社十七日讯】塔斯社莫斯科十七日电：苏联部长会议关于苏联在促进原子能和平用途的研究方面给予其他国家以科学、技术和工业上的帮助的声明：
    苏联政府向来极为重视原子能的和平用途，已经决定给予其他国家以科学、技术和工业上的帮助来建立科学实验中心，以发展对核子物理学和原子能的和平用途方面的研究。
    苏联政府已经向中华人民共和国、波兰人民共和国、捷克斯洛伐克共和国、罗马尼亚人民共和国和德意志民主共和国提供了广泛的帮助，包括设计、供给设备及建设具有达五千瓩热能的实验性原子堆，和原子微粒加速器。还注意将供给这些国家必要数量的原子堆和科学研究用的可分裂物质。并已取得谅解，上述国家将供给苏联以必需的原料。这些国家的科学家和工程师们可以有机会来了解苏联在和平利用原子能方面所进行的科学研究和了解实验性原子堆的工作情况。
    建立上述科学实验中心将能使这些国家发展大规模的核子研究，借助实验性原子堆生产足够数量的放射性同位素，来满足医学、生物学和其他各门科学和工程学的需要，并为发展原子能和平用途的研究而训练必要的科学家和工程师干部。
    苏联正在考虑在发展原子能和平用途的科学研究和实验方面扩大它所能够援助的国家的范围的问题。
