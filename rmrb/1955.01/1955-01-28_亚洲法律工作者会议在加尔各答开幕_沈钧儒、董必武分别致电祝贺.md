Title: Asian Legal Workers Conference Opens in Calcutta; Shen Junru and Dong Biwu Send Congratulatory Telegrams Respectively
Author: Dong Biwu
Date: 1955-01-28
Page: 4
Xinhua News Agency, Calcutta, January 27th. The Asian Legal Workers Conference opened in Calcutta on the 25th. The conference was convened under the auspices of the All-India Democratic Lawyers Association.

    Representatives and observers from Australia, Burma, China , Ceylon, Egypt, Indonesia, Japan, the Democratic People's Republic of Korea, ​​ Pakistan, the Soviet Union, Thailand, and the Democratic Republic of Vietnam are participating in the conference.  Dan N. Pritt, Chairman of the International Association of Democratic Lawyers, Secretary-General Joe Nordmann, Jacques Denis, Secretary-General of the World Federation of Democratic Youth, and Renu Chakravartty, representative of the Women's International Democratic Federation, attended the meeting.  The Governor of West Bengal, India, Mukherjee, also attended the meeting.

    The conference unanimously adopted the resolution proposed by Zhang Zhiran, head of the delegation of the China Political Science and Law Society, to send a telegram to Indian President Prasad and the Indian people congratulating them on the fifth anniversary of the founding of the Republic of India. The meeting also read congratulatory telegrams from the World Peace Council, Shen Junru, Vice Chairman of the International Association of Democratic Lawyers, A.C. Gupta, a well-known Indian legal worker, Dong Biwu, Chairman of the Council of the China Political Science and Law Society, and Giichiro Hirano, Secretary-General of the Japan Peace Protection Committee.

    Dr. R.B. Pal, a member of the Presidium of the conference and a member of the United Nations International Law Commission, emphasized the awakening of the Asian people in his opening speech and pointed out Asia's important position in the world political situation.

    Pritt, Chairman of the International Association of Democratic Lawyers, said in his speech that the danger of war is greater now than at any time since 1945. He said: "Under such circumstances, we can indeed be glad about the convening of this conference, because it proves that the conscience of Asian legal workers has awakened more widely." Pritt then pointed out the serious threat to world peace posed by the rearmament of West Germany and the establishment of the Manila Pact.

    Yasui Iku, a member of the Japanese delegation and professor of international law at Hosei University and Kanagawa University, made a report at the meeting on the prohibition of all atomic weapons, hydrogen weapons, and other weapons of mass destruction. Sudanese representative Ibrahim gave a report on civil liberties at the meeting.

    [Our Newspaper] With the opening of the Asian Legal Workers Conference, Shen Junru, Vice Chairman of the International Association of Democratic Lawyers, and Dong Biwu, Chairman of the Council of the China Political Science and Law Society, sent congratulatory telegrams a few days ago. The telegrams are as follows:
    To the Asian Legal Workers Conference:

    The convening of the Asian Legal Workers Conference is the result of the long-term efforts of legal workers in Asian countries, and it is also the beginning of universal unity and cooperation among legal workers in Asian countries. On the occasion of the opening of this conference, please accept my heartfelt congratulations. I wish the conference further achievements in promoting the common cause of legal workers in various countries in defending world peace, national independence, and people's freedom!

    Shen Junru, Vice Chairman of the International Association of Democratic Lawyers
    To the Asian Legal Workers Conference:

    I am delighted to hear of the opening of the conference, and I would like to extend warm congratulations on behalf of Chinese legal workers.

    The convening of the Asian Legal Workers Conference embodies the common aspiration of legal workers in Asian and neighboring countries for friendly cooperation. Chinese legal workers believe that on the basis of this unity and cooperation, beneficial contributions will certainly be made to the just cause of peaceful coexistence among Asian and world countries, striving for and defending the sovereignty and national independence of all countries, and maintaining and developing the democratic and free rights of the people.

    I wish the conference success.

    Dong Biwu, Chairman of the Council of the China Political Science and Law Society

### 亚洲法律工作者会议在加尔各答开幕  沈钧儒、董必武分别致电祝贺
董必武
1955-01-28
第4版()
专栏：

    亚洲法律工作者会议在加尔各答开幕
    沈钧儒、董必武分别致电祝贺
    【新华社加尔各答二十七日电】亚洲法律工作者会议二十五日在加尔各答开幕。这次会议是在全印民主法律工作者协会的主持下召开的。
    参加会议的有澳大利亚、缅甸、中国、锡兰、埃及、印度尼西亚、日本、朝鲜民主主义人民共和国、巴基斯坦、苏联、泰国、越南民主共和国等国的代表和观察员。国际民主法律工作者协会主席丹·诺·普里特、总书记乔·诺尔曼、世界民主青年联盟总书记雅克·德尼及国际民主妇女联合会代表雷努·查克拉瓦蒂都出席了会议。印度西孟加拉省省长慕克吉也出席了会议。
    会议一致通过了中国政治法律学会代表团团长张志让提出的致电印度总统普拉沙德和印度人民祝贺印度共和国成立五周年的决议。会议还宣读了世界和平理事会、国际民主法律工作者协会副主席沈钧儒、著名的印度法律工作者阿·钱·古普塔、中国政治法律学会理事会主席董必武及日本拥护和平委员会总书记平野义太郎等的贺电。
    会议主席团成员、联合国国际法委员会委员拉·比·帕尔博士在开幕词中强调亚洲人民的觉醒，并指出亚洲在世界政治局势中的重要地位。
    国际民主法律工作者协会主席普里特在会上发言说：现在的战争危险比一九四五年以来任何时候都要大些。他说：在这种情况下，我们的确能够因为这个会议的召开而感到欢欣，因为它证明亚洲法律工作者的良心更加广泛地觉醒了。普里特接着指出武装西德和建立马尼拉集团对世界和平的严重威胁。
    日本代表团团员、法政大学和神奈川大学国际法教授安井郁在会上做了关于禁止所有原子武器、氢武器以及其他大规模毁灭性武器的报告。苏丹代表易卜拉欣在会上作了关于公民自由问题的报告。
    【本报讯】亚洲法律工作者会议开幕，国际民主法律工作者协会副主席沈钧儒，中国政治法律学会理事会主席董必武，特于日前分别致电祝贺，电文如下：亚洲法律工作者会议：
    亚洲法律工作者会议的召开，是亚洲各国法律工作者长时期以来努力争取的成果，也是亚洲各国法律工作者普遍团结合作的开端。值此会议开幕之际，谨电致衷心的祝贺。祝会议对促进各国法律工作者保卫世界和平、民族独立和人民自由的共同事业，获得进一步的成就！
    国际民主法律工作者协会副主席沈钧儒亚洲法律工作者会议：
    欣闻会议开幕，特代表中国法律工作者致以热烈的祝贺。
    亚洲法律工作者会议的召开，体现了亚洲及其邻近国家的法律工作者们友好合作的共同愿望。中国法律工作者相信：在这个团结合作的基础上，对于亚洲及世界各国的和平共处，争取和保卫各个国家的主权与民族独立，维护和发展人民的民主自由权利的正义事业，必将作出有益的贡献。
    谨祝会议的成功。
    中国政治法律学会理事会主席  董必武
