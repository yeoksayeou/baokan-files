     Organization Regulations for Urban Sub-district Offices
    (Adopted at the Fourth Session of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress on December 31, 1954)
    Article 1: In order to strengthen urban residents' work and to strengthen the relationship between the government and the residents, the people's committees of districts under the jurisdiction of cities and cities not divided into districts may establish sub-district offices as their dispatched agencies, according to work needs.
    Article 2: Districts under the jurisdiction of cities and cities not divided into districts with a population of over 100,000 should establish sub-district offices; districts under the jurisdiction of cities and cities not divided into districts with a population between 50,000 and 100,000 may establish sub-district offices if the work truly requires it; districts under the jurisdiction of cities and cities not divided into districts with a population under 50,000 shall generally not establish sub-district offices.
    The establishment of sub-district offices must be approved by the people's committee at the next higher level.
    Article 3: The area under the jurisdiction of a sub-district office should generally be the same as the area under the jurisdiction of the public security police station.
    Article 4: The tasks of the sub-district offices are as follows:
    (1) To handle matters related to residents' work assigned by the people's committees of the city and districts under the jurisdiction of the city;
    (2) To guide the work of the residents' committees;
    (3) To reflect the opinions and demands of the residents.
    Article 5: Each sub-district office shall have one director, and a number of staff members according to the complexity of the work and the size of the area under its jurisdiction; when necessary, one deputy director may be appointed.
    Each sub-district office shall have three to seven full-time cadres, including one cadre responsible for women's work in the sub-district.
    The director, deputy director, and staff members of the sub-district office shall be appointed by the people's committee of the city or district under the jurisdiction of the city.
    Article 6: The various working departments of the people's committees of the city and districts under the jurisdiction of the city shall not directly assign tasks to the sub-district offices without the approval of the people's committees of the city and districts under the jurisdiction of the city.
    Article 7: The office expenses and staff salaries of the sub-district offices shall be allocated uniformly by the people's committees of the provinces and municipalities directly under the Central Government.

### 城市街道办事处组织条例  （一九五四年十二月三十一日全国人民代表大会常务委员会第四次会议通过）

1955-01-01
第2版()
专栏：

     城市街道办事处组织条例
    （一九五四年十二月三十一日全国人民代表大会常务委员会第四次会议通过）
    第一条　为了加强城市的居民工作，密切政府和居民的联系，市辖区、不设区的市的人民委员会可  以按照工作需要设立街道办事处，作为它的派出机关。
    第二条　十万人口以上的市辖区和不设区的市，应当设立街道办事处；十万人口以下五万人口以上的市辖区和不设区的市，如果工作确实需要，也可以设立街道办事处；五万人口以下的市辖区和不设区的市，一般地不设立街道办事处。
    街道办事处的设立，须经上一级人民委员会批准。
    第三条　街道办事处的管辖区域，一般地应当同公安派出所的管辖区域相同。
    第四条　街道办事处的任务如下：
    （一）办理市、市辖区的人民委员会有关居民工作的交办事项；
    （二）指导居民委员会的工作；
    （三）反映居民的意见和要求。
    第五条　街道办事处设主任一人，按照工作的繁简和管辖区域的大小，设干事若干人，在必要的时候，可以设副主任一人。
    街道办事处共设专职干部三人至七人，内有作街道妇女工作的干部一人。
    街道办事处主任、副主任、干事都由市辖区、不设区的市的人民委员会委派。
    第六条　市、市辖区的人民委员会的各工作部门，非经市、市辖区的人民委员会批准，不得直接向街道办事处布置任务。
    第七条　街道办事处的办公费及工作人员的工资，由省、直辖市的人民委员会统一拨发。
