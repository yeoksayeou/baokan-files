Title: American Businessmen Demand Expansion of International Trade
Author:
Date: 1955-01-12
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency dispatch: A United Press International correspondent in New York reported that the "International Economic Alliance Citizens' Conference," attended by prominent American business figures, wrote to U.S. President Eisenhower and Congress, requesting the U.S. government to lift restrictions on trade with the Soviet Union and the People's Democracies (except for a small number of goods of strategic significance).

The "International Economic Alliance Citizens' Conference" requested that a global international trade conference be convened under the auspices of the United Nations to study measures to expand trade and establish a world trade organization. The letter stated that now is the time to implement a policy of promoting international cooperation through international trade.

### 美国商界人士  要求扩大国际贸易

1955-01-12
第4版()
专栏：

    美国商界人士
    要求扩大国际贸易
    据新华社讯：合众社驻纽约记者报道，美国著名商界人士参加的“国际经济联盟民间会议”写信给美国总统艾森豪威尔和国会，要求美国政府取消同苏联和人民民主国家进行贸易的限制（对少数有战略意义的商品除外）。
    “国际经济联盟民间会议”要求在联合国主持下召开世界性的国际贸易会议，来研究扩大贸易和建立世界贸易机构的措施。信中说，现在是执行以国际贸易来促进国际合作的政策的时候了。
