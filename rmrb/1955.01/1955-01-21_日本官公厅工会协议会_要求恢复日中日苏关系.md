Title: Japanese Government and Public Workers Union Council Demands Restoration of Japan-China, Japan-Soviet Relations
Author:
Date: 1955-01-21
Page: 4

    Japanese Government and Public Workers Union Council
    Demands Restoration of Japan-China, Japan-Soviet Relations
    [Xinhua News Agency, Tokyo, 20th] The Japanese Government and Public Workers Union Council, with over 1.9 million members, demanded on the 19th that the Japanese government restore diplomatic relations between Japan and China, and Japan and the Soviet Union.
    This demand was made in a written statement submitted by the council to Japanese Minister of State, Hideji Miyoshi. The statement also called for an immediate halt to the manufacture, use, and testing of atomic and hydrogen bombs, and for international control. The council further stated in the declaration its opposition to Japanese rearmament, and opposed conscription for the "Self-Defense Forces" and the system of recruiting "volunteer soldiers." The statement also expressed the attitude of Japanese civil servants and public utility workers against government restrictions on workers' personal rights.

### 日本官公厅工会协议会  要求恢复日中日苏关系

1955-01-21
第4版()
专栏：

    日本官公厅工会协议会
    要求恢复日中日苏关系
    【新华社二十日讯】东京消息：拥有会员一百九十多万人的日本官公厅工会协议会在十九日要求日本政府恢复日本同中国、苏联的外交关系。
    这个要求是在协议会交给日本政府国务相三好英之的一个书面声明中提出的。声明也要求立即停止制造、使用、试验原子弹和氢弹，并且进行国际管制。协议会在声明中又表示反对日本重整军备，反对“自卫队”的征兵和招募“志愿兵”制度。声明还表示了日本公务人员和公用事业工人反对政府限制工人切身权利的态度。
