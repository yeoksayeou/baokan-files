Title: Korean Foreign Minister Sends Note to Soviet Foreign Minister Supporting the Soviet Stance on the Japanese Peace Treaty, Arguing that the Chinese and Korean People Must Participate in the Conference
Author:
Date: 1951-07-03
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, Pyongyang, 29th – According to the Korean Central News Agency, the Foreign Minister of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea, Pak Hon-yong, sent a note to Soviet Foreign Minister Vyshinsky on the 26th, through the Korean Ambassador to the Soviet Union, Zhu Ninghe, regarding the issue of the Japanese peace treaty. The note stated: The government of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea has carefully studied both the "Soviet Government's Note to the United States Government Regarding the Japanese Peace Treaty" and the "Soviet Government's Opinions on the U.S. Draft for the Japanese Peace Treaty" that it received. The note declares: The government of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea fully supports the attitude taken by the Soviet government regarding the purpose, essence, and preparation procedures of the Japanese peace treaty.

The note further stated: First, the preparation of the Japanese peace treaty must be carried out in accordance with the procedures stipulated in the Potsdam Agreement, and the government of the People's Republic of China must participate. Second, the Korean people must be granted the right to participate in the Japanese peace treaty. The government of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea hopes that its representatives will be invited to participate in the conference discussing the Japanese peace treaty, just like the representatives of other relevant countries. Third, the government of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea warmly supports the Soviet government's proposal to make the purpose and essence of the Japanese peace treaty to guarantee lasting and genuine peace in the Far East. It demands that the Japanese peace treaty provide full guarantees against the resurgence of Japanese militarism, and that the special interests of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea be taken into consideration.

The note denounced the U.S. conspiracy to conclude a separate peace with Japan and the crime of using Japan to participate in the war against the Korean people. The note also declares that the government of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea supports and agrees with the Soviet government's proposal to stipulate the following matters in the Japanese peace treaty: Japan shall not be allowed to join alliances against countries that have an interest in signing the treaty, occupying forces shall withdraw from Japan within a limited time, and foreign countries shall not maintain troops or military bases in Japan, and the provisions of international agreements such as the Cairo Declaration regarding territorial issues shall be fulfilled.

### 朝外相照会苏外长  支持苏联关于对日和约态度  认为中朝人民必须参加和会

1951-07-03
第4版()
专栏：

　　朝外相照会苏外长
    支持苏联关于对日和约态度     
    认为中朝人民必须参加和会
    【新华社平壤二十九日电】据朝鲜中央通讯社消息：朝鲜民主主义人民共和国外务相朴宪永于二十六日通过朝鲜驻苏联大使朱宁河，就对日和约问题向苏联外交部长维辛斯基致送照会一件。照会说：朝鲜民主主义人民共和国政府已将所收到的“苏联政府致美国政府关于对日和约的照会”和“苏联政府关于美国对日和约草案的意见书”的副本，一起加以慎重的研究。照会声明：朝鲜民主主义人民共和国政府完全支持苏联政府关于对日和约的目的、本质及其准备程序所采取的态度。
    照会进一步说：一、对日和约必须按照波茨坦协定中所规定的程序来进行准备，并且必须有中华人民共和国政府参加。二、必须赋予朝鲜人民参加对日和约的权利。朝鲜民主主义人民共和国政府希望自己的代表将和其他有关国家的代表一样地被邀请参加讨论对日和约的会议。三、朝鲜民主主义人民共和国政府热烈地支持苏联政府关于以保障远东持久与真正的和平为对日和约的目的与本质的提案。要求对日和约对于防止日本军国主义再起有充分的保证，并要求考虑朝鲜民主主义人民共和国的特殊利害关系。
    照会痛斥美国单独对日媾和的阴谋和利用日本参加反对朝鲜人民的战争的罪行。照会并声明朝鲜民主主义人民共和国政府支持和同意苏联政府关于在对日和约中规定下列诸事项的提案，即：不允许日本参加反对那些与签订和约有利害关系之国家的联盟，占领军限期撤离日本和外国不得在日本保有军队或军事基地，履行开罗宣言等国际协议关于领土问题的规定。
