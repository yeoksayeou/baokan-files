Title: General Administration of Customs Announces Measures for Registering Gold and Silver Carried by Passengers Entering the Country
Author:
Date: 1951-07-05
Page: 2

　　General Administration of Customs Announces
    Measures for Registering Gold and Silver Carried by Passengers Entering the Country
    After obtaining the approval of the Head Office of the People's Bank of China, the General Administration of Customs of the Central People's Government announced on June 27th the Measures for Registering Gold and Silver Carried by Passengers Entering the Country. The measures stipulate that passengers entering the country carrying gold and silver should declare it to customs for full registration. Customs will provide a certificate allowing them to keep it without having to exchange it at the People's Bank. Each passenger may carry up to two taels (市两) of gold jewelry, fifteen taels of silver jewelry, and forty taels of silver utensils without registration. Foreign passengers entering the country may carry up to one tael of gold jewelry, four taels of silver jewelry, and twenty taels of silver utensils without registration. All other gold and silver carried should be fully registered.
                            (Xinhua News Agency)

### 海关总署公布  旅客携带金银入境登记办法

1951-07-05
第2版()
专栏：

　　海关总署公布
    旅客携带金银入境登记办法
    中央人民政府海关总署于函准中国人民银行总行同意后，于六月二十七日公布旅客携带金银入境登记办法，规定旅客由国外携带金银入境，应向海关报明，全部登记，由海关给予证明，准其持有，毋须向人民银行兑换。每一旅客携带金饰二市两，银饰十五市两，银质器皿四十市两以内者，得免予登记。外籍旅客入境所携金银，除每人携带金饰一市两，银饰四市两，银质器皿二十市两以内者得免予登记外，概应全部登记。
                            （新华社）
