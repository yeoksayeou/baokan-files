Title: Introduction to the Seventh Issue of "World Peace" Magazine
Author:
Date: 1952-03-23
Page: 4

This issue's editorial, "Two Major International Gatherings," provides a clear comparison between the Sixth Session of the United Nations General Assembly and the Vienna World Peace Council. It points out the increasing internal contradictions within the Atlantic bloc and the growing isolation of the United States within the United Nations. It also highlights the achievements of the peace movement in mobilizing the masses and organizing leadership structures. Morgun's "The Vienna Conference" vividly depicts the scenes outside the conference: the suffering and love of peace of the Austrian people.

Regarding the future working guidelines of the peace movement, Italian Socialist Party leader Nenni, in discussing "How to Save International Tensions," provides a profound analysis of the balance of power between peace and war forces over the past year. French Priest Boulier wrote an article discussing "The Content of the Five-Power Peace Pact," advocating for the promotion of disarmament agreements.

One of the main weaknesses of the Atlantic Alliance is the delayed strategic deployment in the Mediterranean. The "Talks of Four Arab Celebrities" and "Egypt Launches Silent Resistance" illustrate how the national liberation movement in the Middle East has thwarted the Atlantic Alliance in the Mediterranean, thus disrupting the war-mongers' plans.

In response to the World Peace Council's resolution on international cultural exchange, this issue publishes essays by famous Soviet, Vietnamese, British, French, and German scientists and writers, exposing many facts about the US imperialists' deployment of a cultural iron curtain and pointing out the role of cultural exchange in defending peace.

"The Melting Pot of French People's Opinions" and "The Peace Forum of the French Ministry of Finance" are both vivid depictions of the French peace movement. "Silent Shouts" uses a fictional format to point out the anti-war sentiment of the West German people. Hu Yuzhi's "Relying on Yellow Mud Feet" describes the power demonstrated by Chinese farmers in the land reform, reflecting China's position in the peace camp.

Finally, this issue also publishes the full text of the various resolutions of the World Peace Council's Vienna Conference.

### 介绍第七期保卫和平杂志

1952-03-23
第4版()
专栏：

    介绍第七期保卫和平杂志
    本期社论“两大国际集会”对联合国第六届大会及维也纳世界和平理事会，作了一番明显的对照。指出了大西洋集团内部矛盾的日益加深，美国在联合国开始感到孤立；也指出了保卫和平运动在发动群众和组织领导机构方面的成绩。摩尔岗的“维也纳会议”一文，用生动的笔法描写会外的情景：奥国人民的困苦与热爱和平。
    关于今后和平运动的工作方针，意大利社会党领袖南尼，在讨论“如何挽救国际的紧张局势”中，对过去一年和平与战争力量的消长做了深刻的分析。法国布利埃神甫写了一篇文章讨论“五大国和平公约的内容”，主张促成裁军的协议。
    大西洋同盟主要弱点之一，是地中海的战略部署迟迟不能实现。“四位阿拉伯名人的谈话”和“埃及发动了沉默的抵抗”两文，说明中东的民族解放运动如何使大西洋同盟在地中海方面受到挫折，因而打乱了战争贩子的如意算盘。
    为了响应世界和平理事会关于国际文化交流的决议，本期登载了苏联、越南、英国、法国、德国有名的科学家和作家的论文，揭发美帝国主义者布置文化铁幕的许多事实，并指出文化交流对于保卫和平的作用。
    “法国人民意见的熔炉”和“法国财政部的和平座谈会”都是关于法国和平运动的生动描写。“无声的呐喊”用小说体裁，指出西德人民厌战的情绪。胡愈之的“依靠黄泥巴脚杆”写出中国农民在土地改革中所表现的力量，反映出中国在和平阵营中所处的地位。
    最后，本期还刊载了世界和平理事会维也纳会议各项决议的全文。
