Title: British "Daily Worker" Reporter Condemns Continued US Air Intrusion into Neutral Zone
Author:
Date: 1951-09-16
Page: 1
Xinhua News Agency, 15th: Alan Winnington, a reporter for the British "Daily Worker" in London, reported from Kaesong on the 13th: After Joy blandly acknowledged the "accidental" strafing incident on the 10th, although the American propaganda drums beat out an optimistic tune, American planes continued to arrogantly demonstrate over the neutral zone, disregarding the Kaesong neutralization agreement. This would not happen if the U.S. side ordered all American pilots not to enter the neutral zone. However, while acknowledging the strafing incident on the 10th, Ridgway made it clear that he had no intention of issuing such an order. Thus, Joy's "acknowledgment" can only be regarded as a declaration that there will be more, even more serious, "flying errors" in the future.

### 英工人日报记者  斥美机继续侵犯中立区

1951-09-16
第1版()
专栏：

    英工人日报记者
    斥美机继续侵犯中立区
    【新华社十五日讯】英国伦敦“工人日报”记者阿兰·魏宁顿十三日自开城报道：乔埃轻描淡写地承认十日“偶然”扫射事件之后，美国宣传的锣鼓虽然敲出乐观的调子，但美国飞机仍然蔑视开城中立化协议，继续骄横地在中立区上空示威。如果美方命令所有的美国飞行员不得闯入中立区，这是不会发生的。但是李奇微在承认十日的扫射事件的同时，明白表示他无意发出这样的命令。这样看来，乔埃的“承认”就只能被认为是宣告今后还会有更多甚至更严重的“飞航差错”。
