Title: A Peace Appeal to Musicians Worldwide from Participants of the 1951 "Prague Spring"

Author:

Date: 1951-09-29
Page: 4

Editor's Note: This is an appeal to musicians worldwide from the participants of the "Prague Spring" music festival held in Czechoslovakia in May 1951. Chinese musicians also participated in performances at this music festival.

We, composers, music theorists, performers, and critics from sixteen countries participating in the "1951 Prague Spring" International Music Festival, extend our warm greetings to musicians worldwide from this atmosphere filled with friendship and internationalism, from this grand gathering of peace and unity.

In Prague, in the spring of 1951, during such a tense international situation, we recognized the urgent need for peace more clearly than ever before. Every artist must create for the happiness of mankind – for the happiness of the broad masses of people. We believe that our primary task is to do everything in our power and with our firm conviction to defend peace.

Therefore, we appeal to you:

Do not forget for a moment our most urgent and important task at present. Work as a citizen of your country, as a member of the masses, and as an artist. Use all your strength to promote international cooperation and support the signing of a Five-Power Peace Pact.

Join the millions of honest people who have risen to defend peace! Through your art, do everything possible to express their most urgent desires, strengthen their growing power to fight for peace, and through your work, use your art to win more people to join this struggle to win the survival and happiness of mankind. You must act immediately, because we are convinced that this is the only way we can save the great achievements of centuries of musical creation for our descendants. This is also the only way to ensure the free development of musical art and guide it to a glorious peak that has never been dreamed of in the past.

Let the primary slogan of the 1951 Prague Spring – "Unite the masses with music and fight for lasting peace" – be your slogan as well. Musicians, join the camp defending world peace and fight together for the glorious victory of a free humanity!

### 一九五一年“布拉格之春”参加者  致全世界音乐工作者的和平呼吁书

1951-09-29
第4版()
专栏：

    一九五一年“布拉格之春”参加者
    致全世界音乐工作者的和平呼吁书
    编者按：这是一九五一年五月在捷克斯洛伐克举行的“布拉格之春”音乐节的参加者所发给全世界音乐工作者的一封呼吁书。在这一次音乐节上，中国的音乐工作者也曾参加表演。我们，来自十六个国家参加“一九五一年布拉格之春”国际音乐节的作曲家、音乐理论家、演奏家和批评家，从这个充满友好和国际主义精神的气氛里，从这个和平与团结的盛会里，向全世界的音乐工作者致以亲切的慰问。
    在布拉格，在一九五一年春天这样国际局势紧张的时候，我们比过去任何时候，更清楚地认识了和平的迫切需要，每一个艺术家必须为人类的幸福——为广大人民群众的幸福而创造。我们认为，我们的首要任务是要尽我们一切的力量，并以我们的坚定的信心，来保卫和平。
    因此，我们向你们呼吁：
    请一分钟也不要忘记我们目前最紧迫最重大的任务。要作为祖国公民的一分子、广大群众的一分子，作为一个艺术家而工作。用你们的一切力量来促进国际间的合作，拥护签订五大国和平公约。
    参加到起来护卫和平的千百万诚实的人民群众中来！通过你们的艺术，尽一切力量来表现他们最迫切的愿望，增强他们的与日俱增的为和平而奋斗的力量，通过你们的工作，运用你们的艺术，争取更多的人来参加这个斗争，以赢得人类的生存和幸福。你们必须马上行动起来，因为我们深信，这是我们能够为我们后代拯救几百年来音乐创造的伟大成绩的唯一道路。这也是能够保证音乐艺术自由发展，并引导它走向过去从未梦见过的、辉煌的高峰的唯一道路。
    让一九五一年布拉格之春的首要的口号——“用音乐来团结人民群众，为永久的和平而斗争”——同样地成为你们的口号。音乐工作者们，参加到保卫世界和平的阵营中来，共同为自由人类的光荣胜利而斗争！
