Title: India Does Not Participate in San Francisco Conference; Burmese Government Expresses Full Support
Author:
Date: 1951-09-02
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, 1st dispatch: According to the Indian News Service in New Delhi on August 30th: The Burmese government supports the Indian government's views against the draft peace treaty and agrees with India's decision not to attend the San Francisco Conference. A Burmese official spokesman said in Yangon on August 29th: "Burma insists that Japan pay reparations. India does not insist on this, but the two countries are in complete agreement on all other issues."

The Burmese public also supports the decision of the two countries not to attend the peace conference. "The Nation" said: "This decision shows the opinion of Southeast Asia." The newspaper also said: "Neither Japan nor China will be satisfied with the current peace treaty, because it will inevitably disrupt peace in Asia."

### 印度不参加旧金山会议  缅政府表示完全支持

1951-09-02
第4版()
专栏：

    印度不参加旧金山会议
    缅政府表示完全支持
    【新华社一日讯】据印度新闻处新德里八月三十日讯：缅甸政府支持印度政府反对和约草案的意见，同意印度不参加旧金山会议的决定。缅甸的官方发言人于八月二十九日在仰光说：“缅甸坚持日本支付赔偿，印度并不坚持，但是两国在其他所有问题上意见是完全一致的。”
    缅甸的舆论界也支持两国不参加和会的决定。“利都报”说：“这个决定表明了东南亚的舆论。”该报并说：“不论是日本还是中国都不会对目前这样的和约感到满意，因为它必然会扰乱亚洲的和平。”
