Title: U.S. Communist Leader Foster Calls on Workers to Launch Movement Against U.S. Government's War Policy of Separate Peace with Japan
Author:
Date: 1951-09-15
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, New York, 13th (via telegraph) – U.S. Communist leader William Z. Foster published an article in the *Daily Worker*, calling on workers to launch a movement to force the Senate to reject the peace treaty with Japan signed at the San Francisco conference. He said that the reason this treaty could pass was essentially because all the signatory countries "were either dependent on U.S. financial aid or desperately wanted to obtain U.S. financial aid." Foster wrote: Turning Japan into a U.S. dependency and military base "is not beneficial to our national interests. The American people need peace, but this treaty is a step towards war." He pointed out that if the masses do not force the government to change its current suicidal war policy, they will suffer the disasters of fascism and a war with no hope of victory.

### 美共领袖福斯特号召工人展开运动  反对美政府单独对日媾和的战争政策

1951-09-15
第4版()
专栏：

    美共领袖福斯特号召工人展开运动
    反对美政府单独对日媾和的战争政策
    【新华社十四日讯】电通社纽约十三日讯：美共领袖福斯特在《工人日报》上发表文章，号召工人展开运动，迫使参院拒绝在旧金山会议签订的对日和约。他说：这个和约之所以能通过，实际上是由于所有的签约国“不是依靠美国的财政援助，就是拚命地想取得美国的财政援助”。福斯特写道：把日本变成美国的附庸国与军事基地“对我们的民族利益并没有好处。美国人民需要和平，但这个条约却是走向战争的一个步骤”。他指出：如果人民群众不迫使政府改变目前自杀的战争政策，他们将遭受法西斯主义与没有胜利希望的战争的灾难。
