Title: To Promote Friendship and Cultural Exchange Between the Chinese and Indian/Burmese People: Our Cultural Delegation to India and Burma Departed Beijing Yesterday
Author:
Date: 1951-09-21
Page: 1

    Xinhua News Agency, 20th. At 6:40 PM on the 20th, the Cultural Delegation of the People's Republic of China, dispatched by the Central People's Government of China at the invitation of the Government of the Republic of India and the Government of the Union of Burma, departed Beijing by train for a friendly visit to India and Burma. Those who saw them off at the station included: Shen Yanbing, Vice-Chairman of the Government Administration Council's Committee of Cultural and Educational Affairs and Minister of Culture; Hu Qiaomu, Secretary-General of the Committee of Cultural and Educational Affairs and Director of the Press Administration; Hu Yuzhi, Director of the Publications Administration; Zhang Hanfu, Vice Minister of Foreign Affairs; Zhou Yang, Vice Minister of Culture; Wei Que, Vice Minister of Education; Sa Kongliao, Deputy Director of the Press Administration; Hong Shen, Director of the Bureau of Foreign Cultural Relations of the Government Administration Council's Committee of Cultural and Educational Affairs; Chen Yi, Director of the Cultural Department of the General Political Department of the People's Revolutionary Military Commission; Wu Maosun, Deputy Secretary-General of the Chinese People's Committee for World Peace and Against American Aggression; Zhou Gengsheng, Vice President of the Chinese People's Institute of Foreign Affairs; Wu Yunfu, Secretary-General of the China People's Relief Administration; and Sha Kefu, Secretary-General of the National Committee of the All-China Federation of Literary and Art Circles, along with over a hundred other government leaders and representatives of people's organizations.
    Indian Ambassador Panikkar, Burmese Ambassador U Law Maung and his wife, as well as personnel from the Indian and Burmese embassies, were also at the station to see them off.
    To promote the friendship and cultural exchange between the Chinese, Indian and Burmese people, the delegation is taking with them various pictures introducing New China, replicas of ancient Chinese art from the Dunhuang murals, Chinese arts and crafts, and seven New China films.
    Prior to this, Indian Ambassador Panikkar hosted a banquet on September 13th to bid farewell to the delegation. U Pe Kin, Chargé d'Affaires of the Burmese Embassy, also hosted a banquet on September 19th to bid farewell to the delegation. U Law Maung, the newly appointed Burmese Ambassador, also attended the banquet.

### 促进中印、中缅人民的友谊和文化交流  我赴印缅文化代表团昨离京

1951-09-21
第1版()
专栏：

    促进中印、中缅人民的友谊和文化交流
    我赴印缅文化代表团昨离京
    【新华社二十日讯】我国中央人民政府应印度共和国政府和缅甸联邦政府邀请而派遣的中华人民共和国文化代表团，于二十日晚六时四十分乘车离京，前往印度和缅甸两国进行友谊的访问。到车站欢送的有政务院文化教育委员会副主任兼文化部部长沈雁冰，文化教育委员会秘书长兼新闻总署署长胡乔木，出版总署署长胡愈之，外交部副部长章汉夫，文化部副部长周扬，教育部副部长韦悫，新闻总署副署长萨空了，政务院文化教育委员会对外文化联络事务局局长洪深，人民革命军事委员会总政治部文化部部长陈沂，中国人民保卫世界和平反对美国侵略委员会副秘书长吴茂荪，中国人民外交学会副会长周鲠生，中国人民救济总会秘书长伍云甫，中华全国文学艺术界联合会全国委员会秘书长沙可夫等政府首长和人民团体的代表共一百余人。
    印度大使潘尼迦，缅甸大使吴拉茂与夫人以及印度、缅甸两国大使馆人员均到车站欢送。
    为促进中印与中缅人民的友谊和文化交流、代表团带去介绍新中国的各种图片、中国古代艺术敦煌壁画的摹本、中国的美术工艺品及新中国电影片七部。
    在此以前，印度大使潘尼迦曾于九月十三日设宴欢送代表团。缅甸大使馆代办吴辟也在九月十九日设宴欢送代表团，缅甸新任大使吴拉茂亦出席了这次宴会。
