Title: Japanese Peace Committee Writes to Chairman Mao, Submits Appeal to Heads of Five Major Powers
Author:
Date: 1951-09-24
Page: 4

    Japanese Peace Committee Writes to Chairman Mao
    Submits Appeal to Heads of Five Major Powers
    [By Our Own Correspondent] Delayed news: Mr. Ikuo Oyama, representative of the Japanese Peace Committee, wrote to Chairman Mao on September 5th. The translation is as follows:

    To His Excellency Chairman Mao Zedong of the People's Republic of China:

    The All-Japan Peace Conference passed an appeal to the heads of the five major powers on August 14th and 15th, 1951. Representatives from forty prefectures [Note] attended this conference.

    We hereby submit this appeal to Your Excellency and sincerely hope that Your Excellency will give due consideration to the wishes of hundreds of millions of Japanese men and women.

    Ikuo Oyama, Representative of the Japanese Peace Committee
    Tokyo, September 5, 1951

    [Note] Japan's "prefectures" are equivalent to our country's provinces.

### 日本和平委员会致书毛主席  递交致五大国元首的呼吁书

1951-09-24
第4版()
专栏：

    日本和平委员会致书毛主席
    递交致五大国元首的呼吁书
    【本报讯】迟到消息：日本和平委员会代表大山郁夫于九月五日致书毛主席，译文如下：中华人民共和国主席毛泽东先生阁下：
    日本全国和平会议于一九五一年八月十四、十五日通过致五大国元首呼吁书一件，出席这次会议的计有四十县〔注〕的代表。
    我们谨将这呼吁书交给阁下，并诚恳地希望阁下对亿万的日本男女的愿望加以妥善的考虑。
    日本和平委员会代表大山郁夫
    一九五一年九月五日于东京
    〔注〕日本的“县”相当于我国的省。
