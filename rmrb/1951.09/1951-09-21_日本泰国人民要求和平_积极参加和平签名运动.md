Title: Japanese and Thai People Demand Peace; Actively Participate in Peace Signature Campaign
Author:
Date: 1951-09-21
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, September 20th:

    Japanese and Thai People Demand Peace
    Actively Participate in Peace Signature Campaign
    [Xinhua News Agency, 20th] TASS, 19th: Tokyo dispatch: According to the Japanese newspaper "World Peace," as of September 10th, the Japanese people had collected approximately 5.5 million signatures for the World Peace Council's declaration calling for the conclusion of a Five-Power Peace Pact.
    [Xinhua News Agency, 20th] Bangkok dispatch: The Thai Peace Committee announced that since the launch of the signature campaign in support of the Five-Power Peace Pact declaration on June 2nd, the number of signatures had reached 120,049 in the twelve weeks up to August 25th. This number is close to the number of signatures obtained in the twenty weeks of the previous Stockholm Peace Appeal signature campaign. The Thai people are now demanding peace even more actively. The number of signatures has been increasing weekly since the first anniversary of the Korean War.

### 日本泰国人民要求和平  积极参加和平签名运动

1951-09-21
第4版()
专栏：

    日本泰国人民要求和平
    积极参加和平签名运动
    【新华社二十日讯】塔斯社十九日讯：东京消息：据日本《世界和平》报报道，截至九月十日为止，日本人民已为世界和平理事会要求缔结五大国和平公约的宣言征集了约五百五十万人的签名。
    【新华社二十日讯】曼谷消息：泰国和平委员会宣布：自六月二日展开拥护五大国缔结和平公约宣言签名运动起，至八月二十五日为止的十二个星期中，签名的人数已达十二万零四十九名。这个数目已接近上次斯德哥尔摩和平宣言签名运动的二十个星期中所得到的签名人数。泰国人民已经更加积极地要求和平。从朝鲜战争一周年以后，签名人数每周都有增加。
