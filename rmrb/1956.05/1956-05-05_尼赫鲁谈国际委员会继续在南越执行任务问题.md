Title: Nehru on the Issue of the International Commission Continuing its Mission in South Vietnam
Author:
Date: 1956-05-05
Page: 4

　　Xinhua News Agency, New Delhi, May 4th. According to the Indian Press Trust, Indian Prime Minister Nehru said in the People's House on May 4th that the International Supervisory and Monitoring Commission in Indochina had agreed to the request of the two chairmen of the Geneva Conference to "temporarily" continue its mission in South Vietnam. Nehru also said that the commission had pointed out that unless certain satisfactory steps were taken in accordance with the Geneva Accords, it would not be possible for it to carry out its mission normally there.
　　In response to a question from a member of parliament about whether the safety of the commission was being compromised, Minister without Portfolio and leader of the Indian delegation to the United Nations, Menon, said: The South Vietnamese government has announced that they will guarantee the safety of the commission's personnel.

### 尼赫鲁谈国际委员会继续在南越执行任务问题

1956-05-05
第4版()
专栏：

　　尼赫鲁谈国际委员会继续在南越执行任务问题
    新华社新德里4日电　据印度报业托辣斯消息：印度总理尼赫鲁五月四日在人民院说，在印度支那的国际监督和监察委员会已经同意日内瓦会议的两位主席的要求，“暂时”继续在南越执行任务。尼赫鲁又说，但是委员会曾经指出，除非根据日内瓦协定采取某些令人满意的步骤，否则它就将不可能在那里正常地执行任务。
    在回答一位议员所提关于委员会安全是否受到危害的问题时，不管部长兼印度驻联合国代表团领袖梅农说：南越政府已经宣布，他们将保证保护委员会人员的安全。
