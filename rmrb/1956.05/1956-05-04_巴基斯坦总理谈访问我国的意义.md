Title: Pakistani Prime Minister Discusses Significance of Visit to China
Author:
Date: 1956-05-04
Page: 1

Xinhua News Agency, New Delhi, 3rd May: According to a report from the Indian Press Trust, Pakistani Prime Minister Muhammad Ali stated at a press conference in Karachi on May 2nd: "We have repeatedly stated that our policy is to promote friendly relations with all countries. The visit to China is in accordance with this policy of promoting goodwill and friendship."

Ali said: China is experiencing significant developments in industry and culture, "and this trip will enable us to study these developments."

He said that his upcoming visit to China does not signify any change in Pakistan's foreign policy, nor does it indicate any change in "our attitude towards our friends and allies, especially the United States."

### 巴基斯坦总理谈访问我国的意义

1956-05-04
第1版()
专栏：

　　巴基斯坦总理谈访问我国的意义
    新华社新德里3日电　据印度报业托辣斯消息，巴基斯坦总理穆罕默德·阿里五月二日在喀喇蚩的记者招待会上说：“我们已经反复地说过，我们的政策是要促进同一切国家的友好关系。访问中国就是遵循着这种促进亲善和友谊的政策。”
    阿里说：中国正在工业和文化方面有重要的发展，“这次旅行将使我们能够研究这些发展情况。”
    他说，他即将到中国的访问并不意味着巴基斯坦的外交政策有任何改变，也不表示“我们对我们的朋友和盟国，特别是美国的态度”有任何改变。
