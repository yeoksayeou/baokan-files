Title: Pakistani President Says the U.S. Should Have Diplomatic Relations with China
Author:
Date: 1956-05-18
Page: 4
Xinhua News Agency, Karachi, May 16th: Pakistani President Mirza said on May 15th that the United States should recognize the People's Republic of China to ease world tension.

According to a United Press report on May 15th: The first president of Pakistan said in an interview with a United Press reporter that the United States should face the facts and recognize the government of the People's Republic of China.

When talking about Asian issues and tensions, Mirza said that the United States should "have diplomatic relations" with China.

He pointed out that the United States doing so "will help ease tensions in Asia."

Mirza emphatically pointed out that Pakistan's actions and attitudes toward China do not indicate that its friendship with the United States has weakened.

He said that Pakistani Prime Minister Muhammad Ali's visit to China on June 2nd should not cause such anxiety: Pakistan is violating its obligations to the United States and other Western countries. Mirza then reiterated Pakistan's support for the Baghdad Pact and the Manila Treaty Organization.

Mirza said, "People should not worry just because someone in our government goes somewhere. There is no danger in our going (to China)."

### 巴基斯坦总统说美国应该和中国有外交关系

1956-05-18
第4版()
专栏：

    巴基斯坦总统说美国应该和中国有外交关系
    新华社16日讯　喀喇蚩消息：巴基斯坦总统米尔扎五月十五日说，美国应当承认中华人民共和国以缓和世界紧张局势。
    据合众社十五日报道：这位巴基斯坦的首任总统在接见合众社记者的时候说，美国应当正视事实，承认中华人民共和国政府。
    米尔扎在谈到亚洲的问题和紧张局势的时候说，美国应当和中国“有外交关系”。
    他指出，美国这样做“将会有助于缓和亚洲的紧张局势”。
    米尔扎着重地指出，巴基斯坦对中国的行动和态度并不表示它同美国的友谊已经削弱。
    他说，巴基斯坦总理穆罕默德·阿里六月二日对中国的访问不应该会引起这种不安：巴基斯坦正在违背它对美国和其他西方国家所承担的义务。米尔扎接着重申巴基斯坦支持巴格达条约和马尼拉条约组织。
    米尔扎说，“人们不应该仅仅因为我们政府中的什么人到什么地方去了就感到担忧。我们去（中国）是没有危险的。”
