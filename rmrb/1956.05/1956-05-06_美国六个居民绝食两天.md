Title: Six Residents in the U.S. Fast for Two Days
Author:
Date: 1956-05-06
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, May 5th. According to a report by the United Press, six residents of Madison, Wisconsin began a hunger strike on May 4th to protest the U.S. government's hydrogen bomb tests in the Pacific. Five of the six people are members of the Religious Society of Friends (Quakers). They sent a telegram to Eisenhower saying: "To remind you that we are seriously opposed to the hydrogen bomb tests that have begun in the Pacific, we, the undersigned, will fast for forty-eight hours."

### 美国六个居民绝食两天

1956-05-06
第4版()
专栏：

    美国六个居民绝食两天
    新华社5日讯　据合众社报道，威斯康星州麦迪逊六个居民从五月四日起开始绝食，抗议美国政府在太平洋举行氢弹试验。这六个人中有五个是公谊会会员。他们拍了一份电报给艾森豪威尔说：“为了使你记住我们是在认真地反对在太平洋开始的氢弹试验，我们这些签名的人将绝食四十八小时。”
