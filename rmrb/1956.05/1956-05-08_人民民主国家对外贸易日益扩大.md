Date: 1956-05-08

　　People's Democratic Countries' Foreign Trade Increasingly Expanding
    Romania Trades with More Than Sixty Countries

    Xinhua News Agency, Prague, May 5th (dispatch): Bucharest dispatch: Currently, Romania is trading with more than sixty countries. During the first Five-Year Plan, which ended last year, Romania's total foreign trade volume nearly doubled.

    In addition to trading with the Soviet Union and other People's Democratic countries, Romania's trade with other countries has also expanded, increasing by one and a half times in the past five years.

    Recently, Romania's trade relations with Asian and African countries have been particularly strengthened. Not long ago, Romania concluded trade and payment agreements with Syria, Lebanon, Burma, and Ceylon.

    Romania and India signed a trade agreement in Delhi this March for a total amount of 4.55 million rupees. According to this agreement, Romania will supply India with drilling equipment and send experts to train personnel for India's petroleum industry.

    Romania's trade with Egypt has increased fourfold compared to 1953. The long-term Romanian economic exhibition, which will be held in Cairo this May, will help further develop trade relations between the two countries.

　　Poland and Sweden Sign Trade and Payment Protocol

    Xinhua News Agency, Warsaw, May 7th (dispatch): Poland and Sweden signed a trade and payment protocol in Stockholm on May 4th. This protocol, which is valid for one year, stipulates the further expansion of trade between Poland and Sweden. Poland will export coal, chemicals, textiles, metal products, and other goods in exchange for Swedish iron ore and machinery, etc.

    It is reported that the trade and payment protocol between Sweden and Bulgaria has been agreed to be extended for another year.

    Hungary and Burma Sign Trade Protocol

    Xinhua News Agency, Rangoon, May 6th (dispatch): Representatives of the Government of the Union of Burma and representatives of the Government of the Hungarian People's Republic signed a trade protocol in Rangoon on May 5th.

    The protocol stipulates that Hungary will purchase 50,000 to 70,000 English tons of rice and rice products from Burma, and Burma will purchase machinery, equipment, and other goods from Hungary equivalent to the value of the aforementioned rice and rice products.

    Bulgaria and Greece Sign This Year's Barter Agreement

    Xinhua News Agency, May 7th (dispatch): According to a TASS dispatch from Sofia: Bulgaria and Greece signed a barter agreement for 1956 in Athens.

    The agreement stipulates that the total value of goods exchanged by both parties will increase to $3.5 million for each party, which is double that of the old agreement.

### 人民民主国家对外贸易日益扩大

1956-05-08
第4版()
专栏：

　　人民民主国家对外贸易日益扩大
    罗同六十多个国家进行贸易
    新华社布拉格5日电  布加勒斯特消息：目前，罗马尼亚在同六十多个国家进行贸易。在去年结束的第一个五年计划期间，罗马尼亚的对外贸易总额增加了将近一倍。
    除了同苏联和其他人民民主国家进行贸易外，罗马尼亚同其他国家的贸易也扩大了，在过去五年中，这方面的贸易额增加了一倍半。
    最近，罗马尼亚同亚洲和非洲国家的贸易关系特别加强了。不久以前，罗马尼亚同叙利亚、黎巴嫩、缅甸和锡兰缔结了贸易和支付协定。
    罗马尼亚和印度今年三月在德里签订了总额达四百五十五万卢比的贸易协定。根据这个协定，罗马尼亚将供给印度钻探设备和派专家为印度培养石油工业人才。
    罗马尼亚同埃及的贸易已经增加到1953年的四倍。今年五月在开罗举办的长期的罗马尼亚经济展览会将有助于两国之间的贸易关系的进一步发展。
　　波兰瑞典签订贸易和支付议定书
    新华社华沙7日电  波兰和瑞典五月四日在斯德哥尔摩签订了贸易和支付议定书。这个议定书的有效期限为一年，它规定进一步扩大波兰和瑞典的贸易。波兰将输出煤、化学品、纺织品、金属制品和其他货物以换取瑞典的铁矿石和机器等。
    据报道，瑞典和保加利亚的贸易和支付议定书经商定再延长一年。
    匈缅签订贸易议定书
    新华社仰光6日电  缅甸联邦政府代表和匈牙利人民共和国政府代表五月五日在仰光签订了一项贸易议定书。
    议定书规定，匈牙利将从缅甸购买五万到七万英吨的大米和大米产品，缅甸将从匈牙利购买价值同上述大米和大米产品相等的机器、设备和其他货物。
    保希签订今年换货协定
    新华社7日讯  据塔斯社索非亚讯：保加利亚同希腊在雅典签订了1956年换货协定。
    协定规定，双方换货总值将增加到每方各为三百五十万美元，即为旧协定的两倍。
