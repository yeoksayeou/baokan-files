Title: Sukarno Speaks About Indonesia's Foreign Policy in the United States: Establishing a Peaceful World, Eliminating Colonialism, Condemning War

Author:
Date: 1956-05-26
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, May 25th: Indonesian President Sukarno, who is currently visiting the United States, delivered a speech to a gathering of the Foreign Policy Association in New York on the 24th. He described Indonesia's foreign policy and condemned colonialism.

Sukarno first emphasized that Asian and African countries that "do not lean to either side" are playing an important role in mobilizing for peace. Sukarno said that Indonesia's foreign policy is "independent and active" and will continue "not to participate in the world's major power blocs." He said: "Our policy is not a neutral policy. We do not intend to withdraw from the world's struggles. We believe that we should play a role in these struggles. Our interest lies in a peaceful world, and we are taking action and will continue to take action to make that situation a reality." He then said that as long as there is oppression of man by man anywhere in the world, Indonesia's foreign policy will never be neutral. Sukarno said that the Asian-African Conference was "the practical application of independent and active foreign policy."

In his speech, Sukarno condemned colonialism as "the fundamental source of world instability." He said: "In this era of Asian-African nationalism, final peace or security cannot be achieved until the last vestiges of colonialism are thrown into the ashes of history along with fascism, feudalism, slavery, and other long-standing garbage."

Sukarno called for greater understanding between countries and advocated that countries with different social and economic systems around the world learn to coexist.

Sukarno condemned the use of war. He believes that war should not be used for any self-proclaimed noble purpose, and he believes that the practice of preparing to drop hydrogen bombs on city residents is a "despicable means." He said at the end of his speech: "Our main goal is to maintain world peace."

Sukarno spoke about the issue of accepting foreign aid at a banquet held by the Mayor of New York on May 23, attended by hundreds of American entrepreneurs. He asked "America's industrial and commercial giants" to give Indonesia unconditional assistance. He said: Indonesia's doors are open, and we welcome assistance because we are extremely short of the funds and technology needed to exploit our own resources. But assistance cannot be at the cost of our freedom and cannot be carried out on the basis of a colonial economy.

### 苏加诺在美谈印度尼西亚的外交政策  建立和平世界，扫除殖民主义，谴责战争手段

1956-05-26
第4版()
专栏：

    苏加诺在美谈印度尼西亚的外交政策
    建立和平世界，扫除殖民主义，谴责战争手段
    据新华社25日讯　正在访问美国的印度尼西亚总统苏加诺二十四日在纽约向美国外交政策协会的一次集会发表了演说。他叙述了印度尼西亚的外交政策，并且谴责了殖民主义。
    苏加诺首先强调：亚洲和非洲的“不倒向任何一方”的国家正在动员起来争取和平的运动中发挥重要的作用。苏加诺说，印度尼西亚的外交政策是“独立的和积极的”，并且将继续“不参加世界上的各大国家集团”。他说：“我们的政策并不是中立政策。我们不打算从世界的斗争中脱身出来。我们认为我们应当在这些斗争中发挥作用。我们的利益在于一个和平的世界，我们在采取行动而且将继续采取行动，以便使那种局面得以实现。”他接着说：只要世界上任何地方存在着人压迫人的现象，印度尼西亚的外交政策就永远不会是中立的。苏加诺说，亚非国家会议就是“独立和积极的外交政策的实际应用”。
    苏加诺在他的演说里谴责殖民主义是
  “世界不安定的基本根源”。他说：“在这个亚非民族主义的时代，在把殖民主义的最后残余同法西斯主义、封建主义、奴役和其他由来已久的垃圾一起扔到历史的灰烬中去以前，决不能得到最后的和平或安全。”
    苏加诺呼吁国与国之间加强谅解，并且主张全世界具有不同社会制度和经济制度的国家学会共处。
    苏加诺谴责了战争的手段。他认为不管是为了什么样自称的崇高目的，都不能采用战争手段，他认为准备向城市居民投掷氢弹的做法是一种“卑鄙的手段”。他在演说的结尾里说：“我们的主要目的是维持世界和平。”
    苏加诺在五月二十三日纽约市长举行的有几百个美国企业家参加的宴会上谈到接受外国援助的问题。他要求“美国的工商业大王”给印度尼西亚以不附带条件的援助。他说：印度尼西亚的大门是敞开着的，我们欢迎援助，因为我们极为缺乏开采自己的富源所需要的资金和技术。但是援助不能以我们的自由为代价，不能在殖民经济基础上进行。
