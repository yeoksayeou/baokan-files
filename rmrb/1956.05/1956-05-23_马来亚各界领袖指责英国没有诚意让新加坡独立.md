Title: Malayan Leaders Accuse Britain of Lacking Sincerity in Granting Singapore Independence
Author:
Date: 1956-05-23
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, Singapore, 22nd: Senu, the Secretary-General of the United Malays National Organisation, said on May 18th that the British government did not want to give Singapore independence, which is the reason for the breakdown of the negotiations on Singapore's political status in London.

Other political figures in the Federation of Malaya also accused the British government of not being sincere in granting Singapore independence. Chen Shu-xin, a member of the Legislative Council of the Federation of Malaya, said, "I don't believe the British government is truly sincere in reaching a solution for Singapore's independence."

Diwa, the Secretary-General of the Malayan Trade Union Council, said that the British government should be held responsible for the breakdown of the London negotiations.

### 马来亚各界领袖指责英国没有诚意让新加坡独立

1956-05-23
第4版()
专栏：

    马来亚各界领袖指责英国没有诚意让新加坡独立
    新华社22日讯　新加坡消息：马来民族联合机构总书记西奴五月十八日发表谈话说，英国政府并不想给新加坡独立，这就是在伦敦举行的关于新加坡政治地位谈判破裂的原因。
    马来亚联合邦其他政界人士也指责英国政府没有诚意让新加坡独立。马来亚联合邦立法议会议员陈修信说，我不相信英国政府真正有诚意来达成一个解决新加坡独立的办法。
    马来亚工会理事会总书记狄哇说，英国政府应该对伦敦谈判破裂负责。
