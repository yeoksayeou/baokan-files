Title: "New York Times" Reporter Reston Says: The United States Does Not Support Indonesia's Demands
Author: 
Date: 1956-05-26
Page: 4

    Xinhua News Agency dispatch: "New York Times" Washington correspondent Reston said in an article published on May 20th that the United States has its "vested interests" in the West Irian issue. The "security structure" of the United States in Asia "relies on naval and air forces based on the island chain composed of the Aleutian Islands, Japan, Okinawa, Taiwan, the Philippines, and West Irian." For political and strategic reasons, Washington cannot be expected to "abandon its allies and its defense" in order to express its "understanding" of Indonesia's aspirations. Reston pointed out that the United States obviously cannot agree to Sukarno's demands on this issue.

### “纽约时报”记者赖斯顿说： 美国不支持印度尼西亚的要求

1956-05-26
第4版()
专栏：

    “纽约时报”记者赖斯顿说：
    美国不支持印度尼西亚的要求
    据新华社讯　“纽约时报”驻华盛顿记者赖斯顿在五月二十日发表的一篇文章里说，美国在西伊里安问题上有着它的
  “切身利益”。美国在亚洲的“安全结构”“依靠以阿留申群岛、日本、冲绳、台湾、菲律宾和西伊里安组成的岛屿链环为基地的海空军力量”。根据政治和战略各方面的理由，不能期望华盛顿为了表示它对印度尼西亚愿望的“了解”而“抛弃它的盟国和它的防御”。赖斯顿指出，美国显然不能答应苏加诺在这个问题上的要求。
