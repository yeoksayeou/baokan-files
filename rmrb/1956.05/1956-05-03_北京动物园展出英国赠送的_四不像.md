Beijing Zoo Exhibits "Milu" (Pere David's Deer) Gifted by Britain

Xinhua News Agency

Beijing Zoo exhibited four Milu deer ("four-not-alike") on April 30th. These four rare animals were a gift from the Zoological Society of London in Britain to the Chinese Zoological Society. They arrived in Beijing on the 29th, escorted by a specialist.

This type of Milu deer is of considerable academic research value in zoology. According to scientific researchers, this animal no longer exists in the wild anywhere in the world. It originated in the Yellow River basin of China and became extinct domestically after being introduced to foreign countries in 1900. This animal has a long tail like a donkey, large hooves like a cow, and a body and antlers like a deer, hence the name "four-not-alike."

The Chinese Zoological Society will reciprocate by gifting a batch of special native animals to the Zoological Society of London in Britain, including Yangtze alligators, giant salamanders, and mandarin ducks. These animals will be taken back to Britain by the person who escorted the Milu deer.

### 北京动物园展出英国赠送的“四不像”

1956-05-03
第3版()
专栏：

    北京动物园展出英国赠送的“四不像”
    北京动物园四月三十日展出了四只麋鹿“四不像”。这四只珍奇的动物是英国伦敦动物学会赠送中国动物学会的礼物，它们是在二十九日由专人护送到达北京的。
    这种麋鹿在动物学上颇有学术研究的价值。据科学工作者的调查，这种动物目前在世界上已经没有野生的。它原产我国黄河流域一带，从1900年传到外国后，国内已经绝种。这种动物的尾巴很长像驴，蹄很大像牛，而身体和角又像鹿，因此也称“四不像”。
    中国动物学会将回赠给英国伦敦动物学会一批特产动物，其中有扬子鳄、鲵鱼、鸳鸯等。这批动物将由英国护送麋鹿的人带回英国去。
                        （新华社）
