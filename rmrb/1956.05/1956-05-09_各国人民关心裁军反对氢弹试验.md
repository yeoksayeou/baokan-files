Title: People of All Countries Concerned About Disarmament and Oppose Hydrogen Bomb Tests
Author:
Date: 1956-05-09
Page: 4

　　People of All Countries Concerned About Disarmament and Oppose Hydrogen Bomb Tests
　　Public Representatives from Paris and Berlin Meet
　　Discussing Issues of Maintaining Peace and Cooperation Between the French and German Peoples
Xinhua News Agency, Berlin, 8th. A total of two hundred public representatives from Paris and Berlin held a two-day meeting in West Berlin starting on May 5th to discuss issues such as maintaining peace, cooperation between the French and German peoples, and the future of Germany, and reached a unanimous conclusion.
    A statement passed by the meeting said: The path to peace is not through re-militarization and an arms race, but through universal disarmament and the security of all countries. This will make it possible to peacefully resolve the German question. We will do our utmost to consolidate the cause of peace and friendship.
    Those who spoke at the meeting unanimously pointed out that a unified Germany in the future must become a peaceful and democratic country.
    The delegation of thirty people from Paris was composed of French scientists, cultural workers, and representatives of various political parties, including Paris University professor Lavallée and Paris Peace Council secretary Langignon. Langignon stated at the meeting that he hoped a delegation composed of representatives of the Berlin public would visit Paris in the near future. He emphasized that the French people desire universal disarmament and the prohibition of atomic weapons, and advocate a peaceful resolution of the German question.
    Japanese "Yomiuri Shimbun" says
　　Hydrogen Bomb Tests Will Cause Great Losses to Japan
Xinhua News Agency, 7th. Tokyo dispatch: The major Japanese newspaper "Yomiuri Shimbun" expressed concern on the 6th about the nuclear weapons tests being conducted by the United States in the Pacific. It pointed out in an editorial that these tests will hinder the activities of Japanese fishermen catching tuna in the area and may cause huge losses to Japan.
    "Yomiuri Shimbun" said, "We hope that the day when there is no longer a need to conduct atomic bomb and hydrogen bomb tests will arrive as soon as possible, even if it is one day sooner."
    American Women Oppose American Hydrogen Bomb Tests
Xinhua News Agency, 8th. New York dispatch: The "New York Times" published on the 7th an open letter from the Women's International League for Peace and Freedom in the United States to Eisenhower, requesting a halt to the American hydrogen bomb tests in the Pacific.
    The letter said that these tests are likely to be seen as a provocation by millions of people around the world.
    The letter, which was signed by prominent American social figures, said: "We oppose not only the current series of tests, but also all future tests."

### 各国人民关心裁军反对氢弹试验

1956-05-09
第4版()
专栏：

　　各国人民关心裁军反对氢弹试验
    巴黎和柏林的公众代表集会
　　讨论维护和平和法德两国人民合作问题
    新华社柏林8日电  来自巴黎和柏林两部分的公众代表共二百人五月五日起在西柏林举行了两天会议，讨论了关于维护和平和法、德两国人民合作以及德国前途等问题，并且得出了一致的结论。
    会议通过的一项声明说：通向和平的道路不是通过重新军国主义化和军备竞赛，而是通过普遍裁军和各国的安全。这将使德国问题有可能得到和平解决。我们将尽我们最大的努力来巩固和平事业和友谊。
    在会上发言的人一致指出，将来统一的德国必须成为和平的民主国家。
    从巴黎来的三十人的代表团是由法国科学家、文化工作者和各政党代表组成的，其中包括巴黎大学教授拉维尔涅和巴黎和平理事会书记兰日尼翁。兰日尼翁在会上表示，希望柏林公众的代表们组成的代表团在最近将来访问巴黎。他强调说，法国人希望普遍裁军和禁止原子武器。并且主张和平解决德国问题。
    日本“读卖新闻”说
　　氢弹试验将对日本造成大损失
    新华社7日讯  东京消息：日本的大报“读卖新闻”六日表示对美国在太平洋开始举行的核子武器试验感到忧虑。它在社论中指出，这种试验将阻碍捕捉金枪鱼的日本渔民在这个地区的活动，并且可能对日本造成巨大的损失。
    “读卖新闻”说，“我们希望不再需要举行原子弹和氢弹试验的时候早日到来，即使早到一天也是好的。”
    美国妇女反对美国氢弹试验
    新华社8日讯  纽约消息：“纽约时报”七日刊载了美国妇女争取和平自由国际联盟写给艾森豪威尔的一封公开信，信里要求停止美国在太平洋举行的氢弹试验。
    这封信说，这些试验很可能被全世界千百万人民认为是一种挑衅。
    在这封上面有美国著名的社会人士签名的信中说：“我们不只反对目前的一系列试验，而且反对所有将来的试验。”
