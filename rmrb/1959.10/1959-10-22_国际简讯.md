International Briefs

　　Abbas Leaves Morocco for Tunisia

　　Rabat Dispatch: Abbas, Prime Minister of the Provisional Government of the Republic of Algeria, left here on the afternoon of the 20th for Tunisia. He was accompanied by Algerian Minister of North African Affairs, Mehri.

　　Venezuelan Journalists' Congress Opens

　　Maracaibo Dispatch: The Third National Journalists' Congress, convened by the Venezuelan Journalists Association, opened on the evening of the 19th in Maracaibo, the oil region of Venezuela. This congress will study the plan proposed by journalists from Cuba, Chile, Peru, Argentina, and Mexico to establish a Federation of Latin American Journalists.

　　Invited to participate in the congress are: Germain Bookhardt, leader of the International Federation of Journalists, as well as journalists' representatives from Peru, Haiti, Nicaragua, and other Latin American countries.

　　Brazil Announces Trade Delegation to Visit Soviet Union

　　Rio de Janeiro Dispatch: The Brazilian Foreign Ministry issued an official announcement on the 19th, stating that the government will send a trade delegation to Moscow in the second half of November to discuss issues related to the development of trade between the two countries with the Soviet government.

### 国际简讯

1959-10-22
第6版()
专栏：国际简讯

    国际简讯
    阿巴斯离摩去突尼斯
    拉巴特讯　阿尔及利亚共和国临时政府总理阿巴斯于20日下午离开这里前往突尼斯。同他一起前往的有阿尔及利亚北非事务部长迈赫里。
    委内瑞拉记者代表大会开幕
    马拉开波讯　由委内瑞拉新闻工作者协会召开的第三次全国记者代表大会，19日晚在委内瑞拉石油区的马拉开波城开幕。这次大会上将研究由古巴、智利、秘鲁、阿根廷和墨西哥记者所倡议的建立拉丁美洲新闻工作者联合会的计划。
    应邀参加大会的有：国际新闻工作者协会领导人哲曼·伯克哈特以及秘鲁、海地和尼加拉瓜等拉丁美洲国家的新闻工作者代表。
    巴西宣布将派贸易代表团访苏
    里约热内卢讯　巴西外交部19日发表一项正式公告，宣布政府将在11月下半月派一个贸易代表团前往莫斯科，同苏联政府讨论关于开展两国间贸易的问题。
