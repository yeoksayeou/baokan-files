Title: Zhang Xiruo and Shen Yanbing Hold Banquet to See Off Burmese Cultural Delegation; Burmese Pagan Murals Exhibited in Beijing
Author:
Date: 1959-10-17
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, October 16th: Zhang Xiruo, Director of the Commission for Cultural Relations with Foreign Countries, and Shen Yanbing, Minister of Culture, held a banquet tonight to see off U Chit Thoung, the Burmese government representative and Minister of Culture, and the Burmese cultural goodwill delegation he led. The delegation will leave Beijing in the coming days.

Zhang Xiruo and U Chit Thoung spoke at the banquet successively. Zhang Xiruo said that the long-standing traditional friendship between China and Burma has greatly developed on the basis of the Five Principles of Peaceful Coexistence. This has not only brought many benefits to the construction and the people's happiness in China and Burma, but also contributed to maintaining peace in Asia and the world. The visit of Minister U Chit Thoung and the delegation to China will further strengthen cultural cooperation and friendship between the two countries. U Chit Thoung said in his speech that their visit to China was like visiting relatives they had been separated from for a long time. He said that after returning home, they would tell the Burmese people about the rapid development of China in various fields. He finally wished that the achievements of New China in the next ten years would be many times greater than in the past ten years.

Minister Shen Yanbing and Burmese Ambassador to China Kyaw Winn also spoke at the banquet, congratulating the continuous development of cultural exchanges and friendship between the two countries.

Attending the banquet were Zhang Hanfu, Vice Minister of Foreign Affairs; Ding Xilin, Deputy Director of the Commission for Cultural Relations with Foreign Countries; Xia Yan, Vice Minister of Culture; historian Jian Bozan; Zhao Puchu, Vice President of the Buddhist Association of China; heads of other relevant departments; and people from cultural circles in the capital.

Xinhua News Agency, October 16th: The exhibition of Burmese Pagan murals, sponsored by the Ministry of Culture and the Commission for Cultural Relations with Foreign Countries, opened this morning at the Qiwang Tower in Jingshan Park. The exhibition is scheduled to run from the 16th to the 26th of this month.

Pagan was the cultural, religious, and administrative center of Burma from the 9th to the 14th centuries, and it remains a religious holy site. It houses various types and sizes of temples built during different historical periods of Burma. On display today are reproductions of some of the murals from these temples.

Minister of Culture Shen Yanbing spoke at the opening ceremony. He praised the Pagan murals as precious Burmese art and warmly congratulated the success of the exhibition. He said that the exhibition would help the Chinese people better understand Burmese culture and art and enhance the friendship between the people of China and Burma.

U Chit Thoung, the Burmese Minister of Culture, and all members of the Burmese cultural goodwill delegation he led, as well as Kyaw Winn, the Burmese Ambassador to China, attended the opening ceremony. In his speech, U Chit Thoung mentioned that he had presided over the opening ceremony of the Chinese painting exhibition in Yangon three months ago. He said that by holding painting exhibitions reciprocally, it could promote the friendly relations between Burma and China. He also presented a book of photographs related to Burmese music and dance to the Chinese Ministry of Culture on behalf of the Burmese Ministry of Culture.

Also attending the opening ceremony were Ding Xilin, Deputy Director of the Commission for Cultural Relations with Foreign Countries; Zhao Puchu, Vice President of the Buddhist Association of China; the famous traditional painter Chen Banding, and other figures from the literary and art circles in the capital.

### 张奚若和沈雁冰举行宴会  欢送缅甸文化代表团  缅甸蒲甘壁画在京展出

1959-10-17
第5版()
专栏：

　　张奚若和沈雁冰举行宴会
    欢送缅甸文化代表团
    缅甸蒲甘壁画在京展出
    新华社16日讯　对外文化联络委员会主任张奚若和文化部部长沈雁冰今晚举行宴会，欢送缅甸政府代表、缅甸文化部部长吴漆东和他率领的缅甸文化友好代表团。代表团日内即将离开北京。
    张奚若和吴漆东先后在宴会上讲话。张奚若说，具有悠久历史传统的中缅友谊，在和平共处五项原则的基础上大大地向前发展了。这不仅给中缅两国的建设和人民幸福带来了许多好处，并且对维护亚洲和世界和平也有它的贡献。吴漆东部长和代表团来中国的访问，必将进一步加强两国的文化合作和两国人民的友谊。吴漆东在讲话中说，他们这次来中国访问就好像到久别的亲戚家里作客一样。他说，他们回国以后，要把中国各方面飞跃发展的情况告诉缅甸人民。他最后预祝新中国在未来十年中取得的成就要比过去的十年还要大许多倍。
    沈雁冰部长和缅甸驻华大使叫温也在宴会上致词，祝贺两国文化交流和人民友谊的不断发展。
    出席宴会作陪的，有外交部副部长章汉夫，对外文化联络委员会副主任丁西林，文化部副部长夏衍，历史学家翦伯赞，中国佛教协会副会长赵朴初，以及其他有关部门的负责人和首都文化界人士。
    新华社16日讯　文化部和对外文化联络委员会主办的缅甸蒲甘壁画展览，今天上午在景山公园绮望楼开幕。展览会预定展出日期是本月16日到26日。
    蒲甘是九世纪到十四世纪缅甸文化、宗教和行政中心，至今仍是宗教圣地，那里有着缅甸各个历史时期建筑的各种类型和大小不同的寺庙。今天展出的就是这些寺庙壁画中的一部分复制品。
    文化部部长沈雁冰在开幕仪式上讲话。他称赞蒲甘壁画是缅甸的珍贵艺术，热烈祝贺展出成功。他说，展览会将帮助中国人民更好地了解缅甸的文化艺术，增进中缅两国人民的友谊。
    缅甸文化部部长吴漆东和他率领的缅甸文化友好代表团全体人员，以及缅甸驻华大使叫温，都出席了开幕式。吴漆东在讲话中提到三个月前他曾经在仰光主持过中国国画展览的开幕式，他说，通过相互举行画展，可以促进缅中两国的友好关系。他并且代表缅甸文化部，把一部有关缅甸音乐舞蹈的照片赠送给中国文化部。
    出席开幕式的，还有对外文化联络委员会副主任丁西林，中国佛教协会副会长赵朴初，著名国画家陈半丁等首都文艺界人士。
