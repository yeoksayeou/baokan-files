Title: Japanese People Continue to Rise Up Against the Japan-U.S. "Security Treaty"; People from All Walks of Life Prepare to Hold the Seventh National Unified Action
Author:
Date: 1959-10-10
Page: 6
Xinhua News Agency, Tokyo, 9th Dispatch: The Secretariat of the National Conference to Prevent Revision of the Japan-U.S. "Security Treaty" in Japan today called on joint struggle organizations in each prefecture and all mass organizations to launch a nationwide protest movement during the seventh national unified action on the 20th, demanding that the Nobusuke Kishi government immediately stop negotiations with the United States to revise the "Security Treaty."

    The appeal condemned the Nobusuke Kishi government's attempt to forcibly conclude a new Japan-U.S. military alliance treaty, despite the opposition of the Japanese people, and pointed out that this puts the struggle to prevent revision of the "Security Treaty" in a critical situation.

    The appeal called on people from all walks of life in Japan to coordinate with the working class's strike on the 20th and hold rallies and demonstrations in various places, demanding that the Japanese Diet and local assemblies urge the Nobusuke Kishi government to immediately stop negotiations to revise the "Security Treaty."

    The appeal called on Japanese people's organizations to continue their efforts, consolidate their organizations, and prepare to launch a larger-scale eighth national unified action.

    The secretariat meeting also decided to hold a "General Mobilization Rally for Autumn Struggle to Prevent Revision of the Security Treaty and Oppose Coal Mine Enterprise Rationalization" on October 26, the day the extraordinary session of the Japanese Diet opens. 30,000 people will be mobilized to participate in rallies and demonstrations on that day.

    The National Movement Implementation Committee for Realizing the Restoration of Japan-China Diplomatic Relations and Celebrating the National Day of the People's Republic of China, composed of sixty-one parties and organizations, decided on the 8th to request that local assemblies pass resolutions on opening up Japan-China relations, promoting the restoration of Japan-China diplomatic relations, and opposing the revision of the "Security Treaty." At the same time, the Implementation Committee decided to launch a movement to protest the Nobusuke Kishi government's forced revision of the "Security Treaty."

    According to a report by Japan Shortwave Broadcasting Corporation, Akira Iwai, Secretary-General of the General Council of Trade Unions of Japan (Sohyo), said today in Hakata City that Sohyo will hold a general strike in late November to prevent the revision of the Japan-U.S. "Security Treaty."

    Xinhua News Agency, 8th Dispatch: Tokyo News: The Japan Council Against Atomic and Hydrogen Bombs held a board meeting on the 7th and decided on a specific plan to launch a movement to prevent the revision of the Japan-U.S. "Security Treaty" and nuclear armament before the end of this year.

    The meeting decided to designate October and November as National Movement Months to prevent nuclear armament and demand peace. Participate in the seventh national unified action to prevent revision of the Japan-U.S. "Security Treaty" on October 20; On October 30, mobilize victims of the U.S. atomic bombings of Hiroshima and Nagasaki to petition the Japanese Diet, demanding that the Diet issue a declaration on Japan's non-nuclear armament; On November 10, hold central rallies and mass rallies in Tokyo and various places, and launch a signature campaign, demanding that the Nobusuke Kishi government and local assemblies issue declarations on stopping the revision of the Japan-U.S. "Security Treaty" and Japan's non-nuclear armament.

    The meeting also decided to launch a movement to provide relief to atomic bomb victims.

    On the 7th, thirty-three scientists from the Physical Society of Japan held a special committee meeting on atomic nuclei in Hiroshima, deciding to fully support the appeal of the Fifth World Conference Against Atomic and Hydrogen Bombs held in Hiroshima in August this year, regarding scientists not cooperating in preparations for war.

    Xinhua News Agency, 4th Dispatch: Tokyo News: The General Council of Trade Unions of Japan (Sohyo) decided on the 3rd to hold the seventh national unified action on October 20 to oppose the revision of the Japan-U.S. "Security Treaty."

    In the seventh unified action, workers will also oppose unemployment and support the struggle of coal miners against "enterprise rationalization."

    According to the decision, coal miners will hold a 24-hour strike; steel, chemical, metal, and dock workers will hold timed strikes; railway workers, train drivers, telecommunications workers, etc., will hold mass rallies; and school teachers and staff will stop teaching and hold rallies.

    The General Council of Trade Unions of Japan (Sohyo) also decided that Hokkaido and Kyushu will hold autumn struggle mass rallies on October 23, and Tokyo will hold autumn struggle mass rallies on October 24, persisting in striving to realize the workers' demands.

    Xinhua News Agency, 7th Dispatch: Tokyo News: The three-day 23rd Extraordinary Convention of the Japan Coal Miners' Union successfully closed today. The convention decided that on October 20, the day of the seventh national unified action to prevent the revision of the Japan-U.S. "Security Treaty," workers from major coal companies will hold a 24-hour strike together.

    The action plan adopted by the convention stipulates that coal miners will hold strikes, rallies, and demonstrations to oppose "enterprise rationalization," unemployment, and revision of the Japan-U.S. "Security Treaty." The convention resolutely demanded that the authorities revoke the so-called "enterprise rationalization plan" that shifts the coal mining industry crisis onto the workers.

    Xinhua News Agency, 8th Dispatch: Tokyo News: Representatives from ten prefectural branches of the National Federation of Farmers' Associations of Japan in the Tohoku and Hokuriku regions, together with leaders from the General Council of Trade Unions of Japan (Sohyo) and the All-Japan Federation of Agriculture and Forestry Ministry Workers' Unions, held a meeting in Niigata on the 4th and 5th, deciding that farmers and workers from the ten prefectures will unite to carry out a joint struggle against the Nobusuke Kishi government's revision of the Japan-U.S. "Security Treaty" and demanding an increase in the government's purchase price of rice.

### 日本人民继续奋起反对日美“安全条约”  各阶层人士作好举行第七次全国统一行动的部署

1959-10-10
第6版()
专栏：

    日本人民继续奋起反对日美“安全条约”
    各阶层人士作好举行第七次全国统一行动的部署
    新华社9日讯　东京消息：日本阻止修改日美“安全条约”国民会议干事会今天呼吁各县共同斗争组织和所有群众团体在20日举行第七次全国统一行动的时候，发动一个全民性的抗议运动，要求岸信介政府立即停止同美国进行修改“安全条约”的谈判。
    呼吁书谴责岸信介政府不顾日本人民的反对企图强行缔结新的日美军事同盟条约的行为，并且指出，这使阻止修改“安全条约”的斗争面临重大的局面。
    呼吁书号召日本各阶层人民在20日那天配合工人阶级的罢工，在各地举行集会和示威游行，要求日本国会和地方议会促使岸信介政府立即停止修改“安全条约”的谈判。
    呼吁书号召日本各人民团体再接再厉，巩固团体，准备展开更大规模的第八次全国统一行动。
    干事会会议还决定，在日本国会临时会议开幕的一天——10月26日，举行“阻止修改安全条约、反对煤矿企业合理化秋季斗争总奋起大会”，当天将动员三万群众参加集会和示威游行。
    由六十一个党派和团体组成的实现恢复日中邦交和庆祝中华人民共和国国庆国民运动实行委员会在8日决定，将要求各个地方议会通过关于打开日中关系、促进恢复日中邦交的决议和反对修改“安全条约”的决议。实行委员会同时决定展开抗议岸信介政府强行修改“安全条约”的运动。
    据日本短波广播公司电台报道，日本工会总评议会事务局长岩井章今天在博多市说，“总评”将在11月下旬举行总罢工，以阻止修改日美“安全条约”。
    新华社8日讯　东京消息：日本禁止原子弹和氢弹协议会7日举行理事会会议，决定今年年底以前展开阻止修改日美“安全条约”和核武装运动的具体计划。
    会议决定把10、11两个月作为阻止核武装和要求和平的国民运动月。10月20日参加阻止修改日美“安全条约”第七次全国统一行动；10月30日，发动广岛、长崎两地遭受美国原子弹轰炸的受害者向日本国会请愿，要求国会发表关于日本非核武装的宣言；11月10日，在东京和各地方举行中央集会和群众集会，并且展开签名运动，要求岸信介政府和地方议会发表关于停止修改日美“安全条约”和日本非核武装的宣言。
    会议还决定展开救济原子弹受害者的运动。
    7日，三十三位日本物理学会的科学家在广岛举行原子核特别委员会会议，决定全面支持今年8月在广岛召开的第五届禁止原子弹氢弹世界大会关于科学家不要在准备战争方面实行合作的呼吁书。
    新华社4日讯　东京消息：日本工会总评议会3日决定在10月20日举行第七次全国统一行动，反对修改日美“安全条约”。
    在第七次统一行动中，工人还将反对失业，支持煤矿工人反对“企业合理化”的斗争。
    根据决定，煤矿工会将举行二十四小时罢工；钢铁、化学、金属、码头工人将举行定时罢工；铁路工人、火车司机、电信工人等将举行群众大会；学校的教职员将停止授课和举行大会。
    总评议会还决定，北海道和九州将在10月23日，东京将在10月24日举行秋季斗争群众大会，坚持争取实现工人的要求。
    据新华社7日讯　东京消息：为期三天的日本煤矿工会第二十三次非常代表大会今天胜利闭幕。大会决定在阻止修改日美“安全条约”第七次全国统一行动的10月20日那天，各大煤矿公司的工人一齐举行二十四小时罢工。
    大会通过的运动方针规定，煤矿工人将举行罢工、集会和示威游行来反对“企业合理化”、失业和修改日美“安全条约”。大会坚决要求当局撤销把煤矿业危机转嫁给工人的所谓“企业合理化计划”。
    新华社8日讯　东京消息：全日本农民协会联合会东北地方和北陆地方的十个县分会的代表同日本工会总评议会及全农林省工会的领导人4、5两日在新澙举行会议，决定十个县的农民和工人联合起来进行共同斗争，反对岸信介政府修改日美“安全条约”和要求提高政府收购大米的价格。
