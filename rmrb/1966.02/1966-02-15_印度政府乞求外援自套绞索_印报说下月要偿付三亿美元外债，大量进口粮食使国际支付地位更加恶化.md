Title: Indian Government Begs for Foreign Aid, Tightening the Noose
Author:
Date: 1966-02-15
Edition: 4

    Indian Government Begs for Foreign Aid, Tightening the Noose
    Indian Newspaper Says $300 Million in Foreign Debt Due Next Month, Massive Grain Imports Further Worsen International Payment Position
    (Xinhua News Agency, New Delhi, 13th) India, relying increasingly on foreign loans and "aid" to survive, is sinking deeper and deeper into the quagmire of foreign debt.
    The *Indian Express* reported on the 12th: "India is obligated to repay approximately $300 million to creditor countries and institutions by the end of next month, which will add a heavy burden to India's meager foreign exchange resources."
    The newspaper said that India's international payment position will further deteriorate due to the need to spend large amounts of foreign exchange to purchase grain from abroad. The newspaper said: "Every million tons of grain requires $12 million in freight. This year's grain imports will hardly be less than 12 million tons, and freight is estimated to increase to $144 million."

### 印度政府乞求外援自套绞索  印报说下月要偿付三亿美元外债，大量进口粮食使国际支付地位更加恶化

1966-02-15
第4版()
专栏：

    印度政府乞求外援自套绞索
    印报说下月要偿付三亿美元外债，大量进口粮食使国际支付地位更加恶化
    据新华社十三日讯　新德里消息：印度由于越来越依赖外国贷款和“援助”过日子，它也就越来越深地陷在外债的泥坑中。
    《印度快报》十二日报道：“印度有义务在下月底以前向债权国和债权机构偿付大约三亿美元，这将给印度的微少的外汇资源加上沉重的负担。”
    这家报纸说，由于必须花大笔外汇向国外购买粮食，印度的国际支付地位还会更加恶化。这家报纸说：“每一百万吨粮食要花一千二百万美元运费。今年的粮食进口很难少于一千二百万吨，运费估计将增加到一亿四千四百万美元。”
