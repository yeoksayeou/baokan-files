　　Indian People's Anger Against Hunger Intensifies
    Calcutta's Starving People Demonstrate in the Streets, Open Granaries, and Burn Down Police Barracks
    Xinhua News Agency, February 23rd Dispatch from Calcutta:  Despite the increasingly brutal suppression by the Indian authorities, the anti-hunger struggle that began in Calcutta on the 16th continued on the 20th.

    According to a report in the *Hindustan Standard* on the 21st, the masses in 24-Parganas, a suburb of Calcutta, held anti-hunger demonstrations throughout the entire day on the 20th. In places like Barasat, Habra, Maslandapur, and Baduria, the masses have been holding gatherings in recent days to protest the Indian government's food policy.

    This sustained and large-scale struggle by the masses in Calcutta has greatly alarmed the reactionary Indian authorities, who continue to mobilize police and indiscriminately arrest demonstrating masses, attempting to extinguish the flames of popular resistance. According to a report in *The Patriot* on the 21st, "The West Bengal government has deployed two battalions of special police from Orissa, Bihar, Madhya Pradesh, and the Central Reserve Police." The Press Trust of India reported that the Calcutta authorities have ordered all public schools closed for a week starting on the 21st.

    Indian newspapers also reported that since the 16th, the number of people arrested in Calcutta and its suburbs has reached 3,449.

    These brutal repressions have further aroused the anger of the demonstrating masses in various suburbs of Calcutta. According to a report in *The Indian Express*, in the Basirhat subdivision, hungry and angry masses opened two large rice warehouses in Bagjola and Ramchandrapur. In Nadia, a large number of students surrounded the rampaging police forces and set fire to police barracks. In Basirhat and Bangaon, many people held hunger strikes to protest the Indian government's food policy.

    Similar mass struggles are also taking place in other parts of India. According to a report in the *Hindustan Standard* on the 21st, in Madhya Pradesh, people held a demonstration in front of the Bhopal State Assembly on February 16th. The demonstrators shouted slogans demanding immediate relief for the impoverished people in the drought-stricken areas of the state and condemning the government's food policy.

    Echoing the anti-hunger struggle, more than 5,000 government employees in New Delhi held demonstrations demanding increased cost-of-living allowances. In Maharashtra, approximately 1,000 workers from six cotton ginning mills and 350 hotel service personnel in Akola have been on strike since February 16th, demanding higher wages.

### 印度人民反饥饿的怒火愈燃愈烈  加尔各答饥民上街示威打开粮仓烧毁警营

1966-02-26
第4版()
专栏：

　　印度人民反饥饿的怒火愈燃愈烈
    加尔各答饥民上街示威打开粮仓烧毁警营
    新华社二十三日讯　加尔各答消息：不顾印度当局越来越蛮横的镇压，加尔各答群众十六日开始的反饥饿斗争，二十日仍在坚持。
    据《印度斯坦旗报》二十一日报道，加尔各答郊区24——帕加纳斯的群众，二十日一整天都在举行反饥饿的示威游行。在巴拉萨特、哈布拉、马斯兰普尔和巴杜里亚等地群众连日来纷纷举行集会，抗议印度政府的粮食政策。
    这次持续而规模巨大的加尔各答群众斗争使印度反动当局十分恐慌，他们继续调集警察和滥施逮捕示威群众，企图扑灭人民的反抗怒火。据《爱国者报》二十一日报道，“西孟加拉邦政府已从奥里萨、比哈尔、中央邦和从中央后备警察中调来了两营特别警察”。印度报业托辣斯报道，加尔各答当局已下令从二十一日起封闭所有公立学校一个星期。
    印度报纸还报道，从十六日以来，在加尔各答和郊区各地被逮捕的群众已达三千四百四十九名。
    这些残暴的镇压更加激起了加尔各答郊区各地示威群众的愤怒。据《印度快报》报道，在巴塞哈特分区，饥饿愤怒的群众打开了巴格乔拉和拉姆钱德拉普的两个大米仓库。在纳迪亚，大批学生包围了正在逞凶的警察部队，放火烧掉了警察营帐。在巴塞哈特和班冈，许多人举行绝食斗争，抗议印度政府的粮食政策。
    印度的其他地方也发生同样的群众斗争。据《印度斯坦旗报》二十一日报道，在中央邦，人民在二月十六日在博帕尔邦议会的大门前举行示威。示威者高呼口号，要求立即救济这个邦受旱灾地区的贫困人民，并且谴责政府的粮食政策。
    同反饥饿的斗争相呼应，新德里的五千多名政府职员举行示威，要求增加物价津贴。在马哈拉施特拉邦，六家轧棉厂的约一千名工人和三百五十多名旅馆服务人员自二月十六日以来在阿科拉举行罢工，要求增加工资。
