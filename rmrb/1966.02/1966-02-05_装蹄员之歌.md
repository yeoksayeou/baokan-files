Title: Song of the Farrier
Author: Zhang Chenbang
Date: 1966-02-05
Edition: 6

    Song of the Farrier
    Soldier Zhang Chenbang
  I am a farrier,
  Crossing rivers and ditches, traversing mountains;
  The hammer rings out, ding-ding-dang-dang,
  I put "shoes" on the army horses.
  The "iron shoes" are made by my hands,
  Once the horses wear them, they gallop like arrows;
  Iron hooves cut through wind and snow,
  High mountains and steep ridges are nothing to them.
  Wearing "iron shoes" to patrol,
  Trampling every inch of the motherland's border defense line;
  Wearing "iron shoes" to fight,
  Unafraid of guns, cannons, and bombs.
  I am a farrier,
  Bearing the heavy responsibility of revolution on my shoulders,
  Carrying the red sun on the end of my carrying pole,
  Mao Zedong Thought shines in my heart.

### 装蹄员之歌
张辰邦
1966-02-05
第6版()
专栏：

    装蹄员之歌
    战士　张辰邦
  我是一个装蹄员，
  跨河越沟走山川；
  叮叮当当锤声响，
  我给军马把“鞋”穿。
  “铁鞋”是俺双手造，
  马儿穿上驰如箭；
  铁蹄斩断风和雪，
  高山峻岭只等闲。
  穿上“铁鞋”去巡逻，
  踏遍祖国边防线；
  穿上“铁鞋”去作战，
  不怕枪炮和炸弹。
  我是一个装蹄员，
  革命重任担双肩，
  扁担梢上挑红日，
  毛主席思想照心间。
