Title: What Power is So Strong?
Author: Zhang Yongmei, Wei Qiu, Li Shifei, Yi Zheng, Ye Man, Sima Yushang, Guan Zhendong, Shen Renkang, Ouyang Ling
Date: 1966-02-27
Edition: 6

　　What Power is So Strong?
　　An Overture
　　What power is so strong?
　　It has stirred the hearts of 650 million people;
　　What power is so strong?
    It has filled the land of China with songs of triumph!
　　Rivers and lakes,
　　Surging with heroic spirit,
　　Mountains,
　　With spines made even stronger!
　　The horns of cars echo through the towns,
　　The silver wings of airplanes carry back the red clouds!
　　What power is so strong?
　　It has scared the plunderers out of their souls!
　　What power is so strong?
　　It has made the traitors gnash their teeth!
　　Ah!
　　The spiritual atomic bomb—
　　The people have boundless self-confidence:
　　This power will build a paradise of ideals,
　　This power will completely eradicate the roots of poverty!
　　This power comes from the invincible Mao Zedong Thought,
　　Invincible in attack,
　　Victorious in every battle;
　　This power comes from the glorious Mao Zedong Thought,
　　Striving for strength,
　　Relying on our own efforts!
　　Ah,
　　The Daqing spirit, the people of Daqing!
　　Lead the charge on our great road!
　　You are a symphony of revolutionary heroes,
　　Resounding through the heavens and shaking the earth!
　　First Notice
　　The Oilfield Party Committee issued the first notice: Study "On Practice" and "On Contradiction"...
　　How to awaken,
　　The land that has been sleeping for thousands of years?
　　How to seize,
　　The victory of a great battle?
　　The Oilfield Party Committee,
　　Issued the first notice:
　　Ask for guidance from the revolutionary commander—
　　Our beloved Chairman Mao!
　　So by the bonfire,
　　In the tents,
　　Thousands of hands open "Selected Works of Mao Zedong,"
　　As if Chairman Mao, through the swirling snow,
　　Personally came to the oilfield to distribute weapons...
　　Looking back from Daqing,
　　Looking back on the journey of thousands of miles,
　　Along the road opened by these weapons,
　　The sun can rise from the East!
　　Now the people of Daqing have powerful arms,
　　With their own hands they create miracles that shock the world:
　　Let the earth obey the commands of the drilling rig,
　　Supply the people with endless heat!
　　Looking into the distance from Daqing,
　　Is even more inspiring:
　　How many similar "First Notices,"
　　Are being passed among hundreds of millions of people...
　　Revolutionaries around the world arm themselves,
　　Will create an eternal century:
　　The bright red flag will be planted all over the globe,
　　The earth will be as magnificent as the sun!
　　Ode to the "Dry-built Wall"
　　At the beginning of the oilfield development, the people of Daqing worked and lived in cattle sheds and stables that had been abandoned for who knows how long. Now, in the modern oilfield area, the offices and dormitories are still "dry-built walls".
　　The gale scours the vast oilfield,
　　Attacking the dwellings of the builders:
　　"Dry-built Wall"—
　　A glorious little earthen house,
　　Filled with heroism in the rainstorm!
　　The soldiers laugh and march toward the formation,
　　Impacting the land that has been slumbering deeply.
　　Wake up!
　　Wake up!
　　Wake up!
　　Everywhere are fighters wielding wind and thunder!
　　In the cowshed,
　　Issue commands for one attack after another,
　　"Dry-built Wall,"
　　Flying out of magnificent blueprints;
　　The battle-hardened veterans,
　　See before their eyes the earliest construction sites of the Republic—
　　The thatched huts on Jinggang Mountain,
　　The grasslands on the Long March,
　　The bright lights of the Yan'an cave dwellings,
　　The Republic was born here!
　　From Jinggang Mountain to Daqing,
　　Storms and rain all over the sky;
　　Revolutionaries eat the wind and drink the snow, but their hearts are even more heroic,
　　Wherever their footsteps pass, red flags flutter!
　　Youth shines in the storm,
　　Life races in the lightning;
　　The people of Daqing set out from here on the Long March,
　　To seize one victory after another!
　　Yan'an - Daqing
　　Only a few years have passed in wind, frost, rain and snow,
　　The remote countryside has long become a landscape of flowers;
　　Now so many people are paying tribute to you:
　　You are so much like the ancient city of Yan'an on the Northwest Plateau!
　　Drilling rig clusters—
　　So much like the myriad incarnations of the Yan'an Pagoda!
　　Today,
　　Your tall and upright drilling rig,
　　On the land of the motherland
　　Has taken deep roots!
　　Here there are no painted boats or pleasure boats,
　　High-rise buildings reaching into the clouds,
　　Rows of small earthen houses are particularly handsome and charming!
　　The new city born in the cold wind and flying snow,
　t sparkle with the spirit of hard struggle?
　　The lights of the oil city are brilliant and the songs are surging,
　　Isn't this clearly the night scene of Yan'an in those years:
　　In the thousands of cave dwellings, it seems that there is still the sound of spinning wheels,
　　In front of the myriad lights, it seems that the winds and clouds of the world are gathering!
　　Yan'an,
　　The soldiers call on you to charge and fight,
　　Daqing,
　　The builders gaze at you and advance rapidly.
　　Daqing - Yan'an,
　　Yan'an - Daqing,
　　You are all incarnations of revolution!
　　To Wang Tieren
　　Good iron is put into the furnace,
　　The more it is smelted, the brighter it becomes;
　　The soldiers are wholeheartedly devoted to the Party,
　　The more they struggle, the stronger they become!
　　That year you marched to Daqing,
　　Greeting the sun in the midst of wind and snow;
　　You and your comrades jointly drilled the first well,
　　Raising the red flag high on the wilderness!
　　Step by step measuring the oilfield road,
　　Iron shoulders support the derrick beam;
　　Sheepskin coats emit the fragrance of petroleum,
　　Covered in dust, but with a bright heart!
　　With a hundred battles under your belt, your bones are hard,
　　Even if your flesh is scorched, you won't leave your post,
　　Revolutionaries are full of courage,
　　Determined to make the earth offer up its treasures!
　　Listen,
　　The whistle blows long and the locomotive moves,
　　That is you and your comrades singing;
　　Look,
　　The motors are spinning and the earth is shaking,
　　The red hearts of the people of Daqing are throbbing!
　　Five Millimeters
　　Ten huge beams are placed in the center of the meeting place. Just because they are five millimeters wider than the quality standard, an on-site inspection meeting of hundreds of people is underway...
　　Five millimeters—a whip mark,
　　Branded on the chest of the warrior;
　　Five millimeters—a thunderclap,
　　Resounding in the ears of the warrior.
　　Hundreds of heavy hearts,
　　Are throbbing uneasily;
　　Hundreds of roaring fighters,
　　Why are they silent?
　　Think,
　　Think!
　　Gaze,
　　Gaze!
　　Crude oil,
　　Rolling!
　　Heart surge,
　　Setting off huge waves!
　　Five millimeters,
　　Must not let it grow!
　　Five millimeters,
　　Eradicate it from the heart!
　　Let the torrent of petroleum surge and surge,
　　Let the footsteps of heroes be as strong as steel!
　　The soaring derricks rise from here,
　　The surging oil mines open from here,
　　The speeding chariots set off from here,
　　The mighty warships set sail from here!
　　People of Daqing,
　　Brought thousands of miles of oil fragrance to the motherland;
　　Five millimeters,
　　The inspiration you give us will never be forgotten!
　　More Sublime Than a Drilling Tower
　　The people of Daqing carried the entire set of drilling equipment on their shoulders,
　　And erected the first magnificent drilling rig in the wilderness.
　　Is the weight on the shoulders just sixty tons?
　　No!
　　They want to put soaring wings on the motherland!
　　What is drilled out is not just one straight oil well after another,
　　But opening up one road after another to victory;
　　The gushing petroleum does not only make the motor turn,
　　It fans the revolutionary motivation in people's hearts!
　　The people of Daqing are so close to us!
　　Seeing the machines flying, we see the spirit of Daqing.
　　The people of Daqing are more sublime than the blue sky drilling tower,
　　The spirit of Daqing nourishes our hearts!
　　Daqing Lullaby
　　Remember, child,
　　Your place of origin is—Daqing!
　　In the vast wilderness,
　　Your fathers and brothers drilled the first well;
　　In the leaky tent,
　　You were born together with the new city!
　　Your cradle,
　　Is woven with the red thread of class struggle;
　　Your mother,
　　Is a revolutionary big family!
　　Accompanying your childhood years,
　　One arduous struggle after another;
　　When you grow up,
　　This place will already be an oil city ten times more glorious!
　　At that time, listen to grandpa tell stories,
　　Child,
　　Don't be surprised,
　　The people of Daqing are so heroic,
　　Their tradition is—revolution!
　　Remember, child,
　　Your place of origin is—Daqing!
　　Red Flame
　　Looking at Daqing in the vast universe,
　　It's like a red flame rising from the ground!
　　Wind and thunder travel thousands of miles, clouds surge,
　　She heralds the early spring for the world.
　　Spring flowers bloom in the vast snowfield,
　　How many drilling rigs rise up;
　　Heaven is alarmed,
　　The earth shakes,
　　Everywhere you look are revolutionaries!
　　Unveiling thousands of layers of the formation,
　　The people of Daqing take out a heart,
　　A heart that is hot,
　　Sweat sprinkles and connects with the colorful clouds!
　　Willing to use light and heat,
　　To decorate the motherland and make it more youthful,
　　Ten thousand red flames rise into the sky,
　　Flying to the five continents to splash sparks!
