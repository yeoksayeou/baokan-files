Title: Intensifying Collusion with the United States to Suppress Revolutionary Struggles of the Asian and African Peoples: Britain Announces Shifting Strategic Focus to "East of Suez"; British First Lord of the Admiralty Resigns, Opposing the Labour Government's Military Subservience to the U.S.

Author:

Date: 1966-02-24
Edition: 4

　　According to a dispatch from Xinhua News Agency, London, February 22nd: The British Labour government on February 22nd released a "Defense" policy statement (i.e., the Defense White Paper) in the House of Commons, indicating Britain's determination to focus its military strategy on "East of Suez," particularly the Far East region, and to intensify its collusion with the United States to suppress the revolutionary struggles of the Asian and African countries.
    The statement uneasily acknowledges that "large parts of Africa, the Middle East, and Asia are undergoing a period of revolutionary change."
    The statement specifically emphasizes: "In the coming decade, the greatest danger to peace is likely to be in the Far East and South Asia, where some of our Commonwealth partners may be directly threatened."
    The statement, following imperialist logic, repeatedly slanders the revolutionary struggles of the Asian and African peoples as "threatening peace." It states, "In recent years, the threat to peace has been much greater outside Europe than within Europe, and when this instability leads to open warfare, it may threaten not only the economic interests of the region but even world peace."
    The Labour government states that Britain must resolutely fulfill its "obligations" in the region, saying, "Some of our obligations cannot be unilaterally or easily abandoned, and some will continue to exist in the 1970s."
    The statement emphasizes that Britain will "continue to maintain military forces" in the Far East region, particularly maintain "military facilities in Malaysia and Singapore." At the same time, Britain has begun negotiations with the Australian government to study the "practical possibilities" of establishing "military facilities" in Australia. The statement also says that it is "necessary" for Britain to maintain "substantial forces" in Hong Kong.
    The statement indicates that Britain "intends" to withdraw from Aden in 1968 and reduce its "responsibilities" in the Mediterranean region but will increase its forces in the Persian Gulf to fulfill its so-called "remaining obligations" in the Middle East region.
    In addressing European issues, the statement says, "The continued existence of the North Atlantic Treaty Organization is of extreme importance to our survival." While promoting "disarmament" and "prevention of nuclear proliferation," the statement insists on establishing an "Atlantic nuclear force" within the North Atlantic group. It says that to stop the proliferation of nuclear weapons, "we intend to internationalize our nuclear strategic forces, dissuade further proliferation, and strengthen the [North Atlantic] alliance."
    The Labour government vigorously promotes "strengthening the role of the United Nations as the main tool for maintaining peace." It reiterates Britain's willingness to provide "logistical support" for establishing a United Nations force to suppress people's revolutionary movements.
    The Labour government's statement demonstrates that Britain will further rely on the United States in its "defense" policy in the future. The statement announces that Britain has decided to purchase fifty F-111 jet bombers from the United States and to continue having the United States manufacture Polaris missiles and certain guided missiles for it. At the same time, it has decided to cancel its own plan to manufacture aircraft carriers in order to use the money to buy American aircraft.
    Western media commented on this, pointing out: "The White Paper shows that Britain's strategic plans for the next decade will increasingly rely on the United States. In this sense, this White Paper is a new 'Nassau' Agreement."
    The Labour government's "defense" statement caused fierce disputes in the House of Commons. Mayhew, who resigned from his post as First Lord of the Admiralty on the 19th due to serious disagreements with the government's "defense" policy, issued a statement immediately after Healey's speech, pointing out that the "East of Suez" policy "contains a fundamental error" and "entails enormous risks both militarily and politically." He also criticized the Labour government for relying on the United States in its "defense" policy and acting as "America's appendage." The British First Sea Lord Luce also announced his resignation before the House of Commons began debating the government's statement, expressing opposition to the government's "defense" policy. ### 加紧勾结美国镇压亚非人民革命斗争  英国宣布把战略重点放在“苏伊士以东”  英海军大臣辞职表示反对工党政府在军事上作美附庸

1966-02-24
第4版()
专栏：

　　加紧勾结美国镇压亚非人民革命斗争
    英国宣布把战略重点放在“苏伊士以东”
    英海军大臣辞职表示反对工党政府在军事上作美附庸
    据新华社伦敦二十二日电  英国工党政府二十二日在下院公布“防务”政策声明（即国防白皮书），表示英国决心把军事战略重点放在“苏伊士以东”特别是远东地区，加紧同美国的勾结，镇压亚非各国人民的革命斗争。
    声明不安地承认，“非洲、中东、亚洲的大部分地区正在经历一个革命性变化的时期”。
    声明特别强调：“在今后十年内，和平遭到的最大危险可能是在远东和南亚，我们的某些英联邦伙伴可能直接受到威胁。”
    声明按照帝国主义的逻辑一再诬蔑亚非人民的革命斗争“威胁和平”。它说，“最近一些年来，对和平的威胁在欧洲以外比在欧洲以内大得多，当这种不稳定导致公开战争的时候，它可能不仅威胁到这个地区的经济利益，而且甚至威胁到世界和平”。
    工党政府表示英国要坚决履行它在这个地区的“义务”，它说，“我们有些义务是不能片面或轻易放弃的，有些义务在七十年代将继续存在”。
    声明强调说，英国要在远东地区“继续保持军事力量”，特别是保持“在马来西亚和新加坡的军事设施”，同时，英国已经开始同澳大利亚政府举行谈判，研究在澳大利亚建立“军事设施的实际可能性”。声明还说，英国在香港保持“大量部队”是“必要的”。
    声明表示，英国“打算”在一九六八年撤出亚丁，并削减在地中海地区的“责任”，但是将增加它在波斯湾的部队以完成所谓在中东地区“剩下的义务”。
    在谈到欧洲问题时，声明说，“北大西洋公约组织的继续存在对我们的生存是极端重要的”。声明在鼓吹“裁军”和“防止核扩散”的同时，坚持要在北大西洋集团内建立“大西洋核力量”。它说，为了制止核武器的扩散，“我们打算把我们的核战略力量国际化，劝阻进一步的扩散，并加强（北大西洋）联盟”。
    工党政府竭力鼓吹“加强联合国作为维护和平主要工具的作用”。它再次表示英国愿意为建立一支镇压人民革命运动的联合国部队提供“后勤支援”。
    工党政府的声明表明，英国今后在“防务”政策上将进一步依靠美国。声明宣布，英国决定向美国购买五十架F—111喷气式轰炸机，继续由美国给它制造北极星导弹和某些定向导弹，同时，决定取消英国自己制造航空母舰的计划，以便把这笔钱用来买美国飞机。
    西方报界就此发表评论指出：“白皮书表明英国今后十年的战略计划越来越依靠美国，从这种意义上来说，这份白皮书是一项新的‘拿骚’协定”。
    工党政府的“防务”声明在下院引起了激烈的争吵。因为同政府的“防务”政策有严重分歧而在十九日辞去海军大臣职务的梅休在希利发言后立即发表声明，指出“苏伊士以东”政策“有原则错误”，“在军事上和政治上都包含着巨大的风险”。他还批评工党政府在“防务”政策上依靠美国，作“美国的附庸”。英国海军第一副大臣卢斯在下院开始辩论政府声明之前也宣布辞职，表示反对政府的“防务”政策。（附图片）
