    U.S. Strategic Thinking Has Shifted From U.S.-Soviet Confrontation to U.S.-China Confrontation
    The Japanese newspaper *Asahi Shimbun* published a report on December 17 of last year by its Washington correspondent, stating:
    The reason why the United States emphasizes China's nuclear threat at the North Atlantic Treaty Council, in addition to the background of the Vietnam War, has two other backgrounds. First, in the military aspect, as the focus of American strategic thinking shifts from a U.S.-Soviet confrontation to a U.S.-China confrontation, there will be a significant change in American nuclear strategic thinking. Second, in the diplomatic aspect, the United States wants to pave the way for realizing the proposals existing within the U.S. government to expand the North Atlantic Treaty Organization to Asia or to extend the "nuclear umbrella" to the Indian Ocean or the Far East. The first point, namely the shift in strategic thinking, is brought to the agenda as a more urgent and realistic issue.

### 美国战略思想已由美苏对抗转向美中对抗

1966-02-01
第4版()
专栏：外论摘要

    美国战略思想已由美苏对抗转向美中对抗
    日本《朝日新闻》去年十二月十七日发表驻华盛顿记者写的一篇报道说：
    美国之所以在北大西洋公约理事会上强调中国的核威胁，除了因为有越南战争这个背景以外，还有两个背景。第一、军事方面，随着美国战略思想由以美苏对抗为中心转向以美中对抗为中心，美国核战略思想也要来一个相当重大的转变。第二、外交方面，美国想为实现美国政府内部所存在的把北大西洋公约组织扩大到亚洲的主张或者把“核保护伞”扩大到印度洋或远东的主张铺平道路。作为更紧急的现实问题提到日程上的，还是第一点，即战略思想的转变。
