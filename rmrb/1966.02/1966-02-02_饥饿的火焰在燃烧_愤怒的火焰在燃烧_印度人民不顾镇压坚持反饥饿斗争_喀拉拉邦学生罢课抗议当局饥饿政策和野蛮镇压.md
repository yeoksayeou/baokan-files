Title: The Flames of Hunger are Burning, The Flames of Anger are Burning; The Indian People Persist in Anti-Hunger Struggle Despite Suppression; Kerala Students Strike to Protest Authorities' Hunger Policies and Brutal Suppression
Author:
Date: 1966-02-02
Edition: 5

    Xinhua News Agency, 1st dispatch, New Delhi: Following mass arrests on January 29th, authorities in Kerala, India, continue to suppress student demonstrations against the reduction of rice rations.

    On January 31st, police used tear gas to brutally suppress demonstrating students in Thiruvananthapuram, the capital of Kerala. Simultaneously, Thiruvananthapuram authorities suspended bus transportation in the city and suburbs during the student demonstrations.

    Students are currently holding a strike to protest the Indian authorities' food policies and suppression. In the city of Kottayam in central Kerala, police charged demonstrating students with batons. In the city of Thrissur in central Kerala, police also adopted brutal measures to suppress demonstrating students.

    According to Western news agencies, Kerala Governor Jain blatantly refused on January 31st to investigate the incident in which police opened fire on demonstrators on January 28th.

### 饥饿的火焰在燃烧　愤怒的火焰在燃烧  印度人民不顾镇压坚持反饥饿斗争  喀拉拉邦学生罢课抗议当局饥饿政策和野蛮镇压

1966-02-02
第5版()
专栏：

    饥饿的火焰在燃烧　愤怒的火焰在燃烧
    印度人民不顾镇压坚持反饥饿斗争
    喀拉拉邦学生罢课抗议当局饥饿政策和野蛮镇压
    新华社一日讯　新德里消息：印度喀拉拉邦当局在一月二十九日进行大规模逮捕后，继续镇压学生反对削减大米配给额的示威。
    警察三十一日使用催泪弹残酷镇压喀拉拉邦首府特里凡得琅的示威学生。同时，特里凡得琅当局在学生示威时停止市区和郊区的公共汽车交通。
    学生们正在举行罢课，抗议印度当局的粮食政策和镇压。在喀拉拉邦中部的科塔延城，警察用警棍向示威学生冲击。在喀拉拉邦中部的特里丘尔城，警察也采取野蛮手段镇压示威学生。
    据西方通讯社报道，喀拉拉邦邦长贾殷三十一日悍然拒绝对警察在一月二十八日开枪射击示威者的事件进行调查。
