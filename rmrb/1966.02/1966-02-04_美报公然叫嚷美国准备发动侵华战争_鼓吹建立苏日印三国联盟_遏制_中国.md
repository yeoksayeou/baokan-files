Title: US Newspaper Publicly Clamors for US to Launch a War of Aggression Against China, Advocates Establishing a Soviet-Japanese-Indian Alliance to "Contain" China
Author:
Date: 1966-02-04
Edition: 4

    (Dispatch from this newspaper) The American newspaper *Chicago Sun-Times* published a report on January 30 entitled "Will the Future Bring War with Red China?" openly clamoring for the United States to prepare for "war" with China and advocating the establishment of a Soviet-Japanese-Indian alliance to "contain" China. The following is an abstract of the report:

    Will the United States go to war with Red China? This ominous question has been behind most public and private diplomatic activities over the past week.

    This was the main attitude in Washington as the British and American foreign ministers and defense ministers sat down to draft a long-term plan to contain China.

    This is also the concern publicly expressed by Defense Secretary McNamara, who told the House Military Subcommittee that in the mid- to late-1970s, China's nuclear weapons and intercontinental missiles could become a small but significant threat to the United States.

    Since the explosion of the first Chinese atomic device in 1964, a crucial question in the diplomatic and military considerations of Western governments has been when, where, how, and by whom to contain the Yellow Dragon. This is likely to remain a key issue in these considerations for the next decade or two.

    Some in the Pentagon favor expanding the Vietnam War into a preventive war against China. This proposal was rejected, partly because the United States would become severely isolated in this conflict.

    What is currently needed is an alliance that can successfully contain China without waging a major war. A neutral alliance would include Russia, India, and Japan, aided by the United States, Britain, and countries such as Korea (referring to South Korea), Thailand, Laos, the Philippines, Australia, New Zealand, and Formosa (referring to China's territory of Taiwan).

    However, there is no consensus among these countries that containment is necessary.

    Perhaps the biggest obstacle is disagreement over where to draw the line to prevent Chinese expansion. The United States is trying to draw this line at the 17th parallel in Vietnam.

    It might be much easier to form an alliance if peace in Vietnam could be achieved, or if a major effort at reconciliation—including admitting Beijing to the United Nations—failed due to China's continued hostility.

    But for better or worse, this line has already been drawn across Vietnam, and the United States has guaranteed to stay there.

### 美报公然叫嚷美国准备发动侵华战争  鼓吹建立苏日印三国联盟“遏制”中国

1966-02-04
第4版()
专栏：

    美报公然叫嚷美国准备发动侵华战争
    鼓吹建立苏日印三国联盟“遏制”中国
    本报讯　美国《芝加哥太阳时报》一月三十日刊载一篇题为《前途是否会同赤色中国交战？》的报道，公然叫嚷美国准备同中国“交战”，并且鼓吹建立苏日印三国联盟来“遏制”中国。下面是这篇报道的摘要：
    美国是否将同赤色中国交战？过去一星期在大部分公开的和私下的外交活动背后都存在这个不祥的问题。
    当英美两国外交部长和国防部长坐下来起草遏制中国的长期计划时，这是华盛顿方面主要的态度。
    这也是国防部长麦克纳马拉公开表示的担心，他对众院军事小组委员会说，在七十年代中期或后期，中国的核武器和洲际导弹可以成为对美国的一个小小的、但是很重要的威胁。
    自从一九六四年爆炸第一枚中国原子装置以来，西方政府的外交和军事考虑中很重要的一个问题是，在什么时候，在什么地方，用什么办法，由谁来遏制这条黄龙。在今后一二十年内这很可能一直是在这种考虑中的主要问题。
    五角大楼中的一些人赞成把越南战争扩大为对中国的预防性战争。这个主张遭到拒绝，部分原因是美国在这场冲突中将陷于严重孤立。
    目前所需要的是结成一个在不进行大战的情况下可以成功地遏制中国的联盟。一个中立联盟将包括俄国、印度和日本，由美国、英国以及象朝鲜（指南朝鲜）、泰国、老挝、菲律宾、澳大利亚、新西兰、福摩萨（指中国领土台湾）提供帮助。
    但是在这批国家中，并不一致认为遏制是必要的。
    也许最大的障碍是，究竟把防止中国扩张的界线划在哪里，对这一点有分歧意见。美国努力要把这条线划在越南的十七度。
    如果能够实现越南和平，要结成一个联盟可能容易得多，如果在和解方面作出的一项重大努力——包括接纳北京参加联合国——由于中国继续持敌对态度而失败，这项工作也会容易些。
    但是不管是好是坏，这条线已在越南拦腰划定，美国已保证要留在那里。
