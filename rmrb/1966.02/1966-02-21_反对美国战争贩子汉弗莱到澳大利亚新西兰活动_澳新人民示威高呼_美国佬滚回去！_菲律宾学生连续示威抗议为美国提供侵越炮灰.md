Title: Oppose War-Monger Humphrey's Activities in Australia and New Zealand; Australian and New Zealand People Demonstrate, Shouting "Yankees Go Home!"; Philippine Students Continuously Demonstrate, Protesting the Provision of Cannon Fodder for U.S. Aggression in Vietnam
Author:
Date: 1966-02-21
Edition: 3

    Xinhua News Agency, Melbourne, 19th. This afternoon, as visiting U.S. Vice President Humphrey emerged from Parliament House after holding talks with Australian Prime Minister Holt in Canberra, about 200 Australian demonstrators rushed forward, broke through the police cordon, and surrounded Humphrey's car. They shouted, "Yankees go home!"
    The demonstrators included students and union members, as well as those opposed to conscription and women. They had gathered in front of the building from noon, holding signs and banners with slogans such as: "Humphrey Go Home!" "Stop the Vietnam War!" "Yankees Get Out of Vietnam!" "No Australian Troops to Vietnam!"
    When Humphrey appeared at the door and headed towards his car, the demonstrators rushed up, surrounded his car, booed and jeered at him, called him a war-monger, and told him to go back home.
    Humphrey scrambled into the car in a panic and slipped away under heavy police protection.
    Many demonstrators came from Sydney and Melbourne, and many were local residents.
    Xinhua News Agency, Wellington, 18th. Masses in Auckland, New Zealand, held a demonstration on the 18th, strongly opposing U.S. Vice President Humphrey's upcoming visit to New Zealand to gather cannon fodder for the U.S. war of aggression in Vietnam.
    Carrying signs such as "Humphrey Go Home!" and "Don't Exchange New Zealand Troops for Dollars!", they demonstrated in front of the U.S. Consulate. Reid, chairman of the Auckland Committee for Peace in Vietnam, and a group of women strongly protested to the U.S. Consul against U.S. aggression in Vietnam and against the U.S. dragging New Zealanders into this dirty war.
    Xinhua News Agency, 18th. Manila News: Hundreds of Philippine students held continuous demonstrations in front of the U.S. Embassy and the Philippine Congress in Manila on the 18th, protesting against the U.S. enticing the Philippine authorities to send troops to South Vietnam to participate in the U.S. war of aggression, and opposing the Philippine authorities' yielding to U.S. pressure to provide cannon fodder for the U.S.
    The demonstrators demanded the cancellation of a bill submitted to Congress by Philippine President Marcos on the 17th. This bill calls for an allocation of 35 million pesos (approximately $9 million) to send 2,000 Philippine troops to South Vietnam.
    The demonstrators held up signs with slogans such as: "To hell with the Vietnam Bill (referring to the bill allocating funds for sending troops to South Vietnam)!" "What's 35 million pesos for? Aggression?" "Don't be manipulated by dollars!"

### 反对美国战争贩子汉弗莱到澳大利亚新西兰活动　澳新人民示威高呼“美国佬滚回去！”　菲律宾学生连续示威抗议为美国提供侵越炮灰

1966-02-21
第3版()
专栏：

　　反对美国战争贩子汉弗莱到澳大利亚新西兰活动
　　澳新人民示威高呼“美国佬滚回去！”
　　菲律宾学生连续示威抗议为美国提供侵越炮灰
    新华社墨尔本十九日电  十九日下午，当前来访问的美国副总统汉弗莱在堪培拉同澳大利亚总理霍尔特举行会谈后走出议会大厦时，约二百名澳大利亚示威者涌上前去，突破警察封锁线，包围了汉弗莱的汽车，他们高呼：“美国佬滚回去！”
    示威的人有学生和工会会员，还有反对征兵的人和妇女，他们从中午起就聚集在大厦前面，举着标语牌和横幅标语，上面写着：“汉弗莱滚回去！”“停止越南战争！”“美国佬滚出越南！”“不许派澳大利亚军队到越南去！”
    当汉弗莱在门口出现，朝着他的汽车走去的时候，示威者涌上去把他的汽车团团围住向他发出嘘声和嘲笑声，管他叫战争贩子，要他滚回老家去。
    汉弗莱狼狈地钻进汽车，在警察严密的保护下溜走了。
    许多示威者是从悉尼和墨尔本赶来的，还有许多是当地居民。
    据新华社惠灵顿十八日电新西兰奥克兰市的群众十八日举行示威，强烈反对美国副总统汉弗莱即将来新西兰为美国侵略越南的战争搜罗炮灰。
    他们带着“汉弗莱滚回去！”“不拿新西兰军队去换取美元！”等标语牌，到美国领事馆前示威。奥克兰争取越南和平委员会主席里德和一群妇女向美国领事强烈抗议美国侵略越南，并且抗议美国把新西兰人拉进这场肮脏的战争。
    据新华社十八日讯　马尼拉消息：数百名菲律宾学生十八日连续在马尼拉美国大使馆和菲律宾国会前面举行示威，抗议美国诱迫菲律宾当局派遣军队到南越参加美国的侵略战争，反对菲律宾当局屈从美国的压力给美国提供炮灰。
    示威者要求取消菲律宾总统马科斯十七日向国会提出的一项法案。这项法案要求拨款三千五百万比索（约合九百万美元），以便派遣菲律宾军队二千人到南越。
    示威者高举起标语牌，上面的一些标语写道：“越南法案（指拨款作派兵到南越用的法案）见鬼去吧！”“三千五百万比索干啥？侵略吗？”“不要受美元操纵吧！”
