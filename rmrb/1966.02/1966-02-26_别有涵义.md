Author: 
Date: 1966-02-26
Edition: 3

    Another Meaning
    “Ranch Hand,” as the name implies, refers to a laborer in the livestock industry.
    No, in the dictionary of American imperialism, it has another meaning. Recently, the United States used C-123 aircraft in Laos to widely spray chemical toxins to defoliate trees and wither crops. The personnel piloting these aircraft are all merciless demons, but the Pentagon gave these executioners a code name – "Ranch Hand."
    Such things may seem absurd and ridiculous, but in fact, American imperialism has long been doing things this way. Isn't Johnson's so-called "seeking peace" synonymous with expanding the war?
    Everything that the Johnson administration says with pompous officialdom must be understood from its opposite. In this way, one will grasp its true meaning.

### 别有涵义

1966-02-26
第3版()
专栏：三言两语

    别有涵义
    “牧场工人”，顾名思义，是畜牧业的劳动者。
    不，在美帝国主义的辞典里，它是另一种涵义。最近，美国在老挝使用Ｃ—123型飞机，对丛林地带广泛喷射化学毒剂，以使树叶脱落，庄稼枯萎。驾驶这种飞机的人员，都是些杀人不眨眼的魔鬼，但是，五角大楼却给这些刽子手起了一个代号——“牧场工人”。
    这类事情看来似乎荒唐可笑，其实，美帝国主义早已行之若素。约翰逊口中的所谓“谋求和平”，不正是扩大战争的同义词吗？
    对于约翰逊政府的一切堂而皇之的官腔，必须从它的反面去理解。这样，就会掌握到它的真实涵义。
