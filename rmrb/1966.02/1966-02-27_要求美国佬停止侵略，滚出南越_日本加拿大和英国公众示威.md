Title: Demanding the Yankee Stop Aggression, Get Out of South Vietnam: Public Demonstrations in Japan, Canada, and Britain
Author:
Date: 1966-02-27
Edition: 4

　　Demanding the Yankee Stop Aggression, Get Out of South Vietnam
　　Public Demonstrations in Japan, Canada, and Britain
    This newspaper provides comprehensive coverage　According to Xinhua News Agency, the public in Japan, Canada, and Britain held demonstrations against American aggression in Vietnam.
    On the 20th, the Japanese people held a massive demonstration, constantly shouting slogans such as "Yankees go home!" and "Stop the aggression against Vietnam!"
    In Montreal, Canada's largest city, more than two thousand people demonstrated outside the American consulate on the afternoon of the 18th, shouting, "Vietnam for the Vietnamese!"
    In Birmingham, the center of British steel production, more than two hundred people demonstrated on the 18th against the Labor government's policy of supporting American aggression in Vietnam. At that time, British Prime Minister Wilson was staying in the city.

### 要求美国佬停止侵略，滚出南越　日本加拿大和英国公众示威

1966-02-27
第4版()
专栏：

　　要求美国佬停止侵略，滚出南越
　　日本加拿大和英国公众示威
    本报综合报道　据新华社讯，日本、加拿大和英国公众举行示威，反对美国侵略越南。
    日本人民二十日举行声势浩大的示威游行，不断高呼：“美国佬滚回去！”“停止侵略越南！”等口号。
    加拿大第一大城市蒙特利尔的群众两千多人，十八日下午在美国领事馆外面示威，高呼：“越南是越南人的越南！”
    在英国钢铁生产中心伯明翰，十八日有两百多人示威反对工党政府支持美国侵略越南的政策。这时，英国首相威尔逊正在这个城市逗留。
