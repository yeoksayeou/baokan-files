Title: French Government Declares Opposition to US Resumption of Bombing of Vietnam; Points out that the UN is not Qualified to Interfere in the Vietnam Issue
Author: 
Date: 1966-02-03
Edition: 4

    Xinhua News Agency, Paris, February 2nd -- The French government issued a statement today openly opposing the U.S. government's resumption of bombing of the Democratic Republic of Vietnam and attempts to use the United Nations to interfere in the Vietnam issue.
    The statement, issued after a meeting of the French cabinet, said that "the French government can only express regret and disapproval at the resumption of bombing."
    The statement said that the French government also believes that the United Nations "is not qualified to interfere" in the Vietnam issue. The statement pointed out that neither North Vietnam nor South Vietnam, nor China, are represented in the United Nations. The statement said that the French government believes that "re-establishing peace in Vietnam means: in accordance with the Geneva Accords, in fact and above all, renouncing any foreign interference."

### 法国政府声明反对美国恢复轰炸越南  指出联合国没有资格干涉越南问题

1966-02-03
第4版()
专栏：

    法国政府声明反对美国恢复轰炸越南
    指出联合国没有资格干涉越南问题
    新华社巴黎二日电　法国政府今天发表声明，公开反对美国政府恢复对越南民主共和国的轰炸和企图利用联合国来干涉越南问题。
    这个在法国内阁举行会议后发表的声明说，“法国政府对恢复轰炸只能表示遗憾和不赞同”。
    声明说，法国政府还认为，联合国“没有资格干涉”越南问题。声明指出，无论越南北方和越南南方以及中国，都在联合国中没有代表。声明说，法国政府认为，“重建越南的和平意味着：根据日内瓦协议，事实上和首先摒弃任何外来干涉。”
