Title: Making Something Out of Nothing
Author: 
Date: 1966-02-04
Edition: 3

    Johnson and his ilk are always clamoring about the United States fulfilling its "obligation to defend South Vietnam." But who exactly gave the United States this "obligation"?
    U.S. Senator Fulbright put it nicely. He said: It is an "obligation undertaken by restating that we have an obligation. It is an obligation manufactured by oneself."
    Fulbright's words not only reveal the gangster logic of U.S. imperialism, but also explain why Johnson and his ilk keep harping on the so-called "obligation" of the United States to South Vietnam.

### 无中生有

1966-02-04
第3版()
专栏：三言两语

    无中生有
    约翰逊之流总是叫嚷什么美国要履行“捍卫南越的义务”。但是，究竟是谁给予美国这种“义务”呢？
    美国参议员富布赖特说得妙。他说：这是一种“通过重申我们负有义务这句话而承担的义务。这是一种自己制造出来的义务。”
    富布赖特的这番话，不仅道出了美帝国主义的强盗逻辑，而且也说明了约翰逊之流为什么老是要反反复复叨念所谓美国对南越“负有义务”的鬼话。
