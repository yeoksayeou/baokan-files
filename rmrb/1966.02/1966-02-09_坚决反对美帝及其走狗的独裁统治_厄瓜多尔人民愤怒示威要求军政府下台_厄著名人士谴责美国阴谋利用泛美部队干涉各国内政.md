Title: Resolutely Oppose the Dictatorial Rule of U.S. Imperialism and its Running Dogs; Ecuadorian People Angrily Demonstrate, Demanding the Military Government Step Down; Prominent Ecuadorian Figures Condemn U.S. Conspiracy to Use Pan-American Forces to Interfere in Other Countries' Internal Affairs
Author:
Date: 1966-02-09
Edition: 5

    Xinhua News Agency, 6th dispatch from Quito: The anti-U.S. and anti-dictatorship struggle that began in Ecuador on January 27th has spread from the capital, Quito, to Guayaquil, the country's largest city, on the 4th. On this day, residents of both major cities held fierce anti-U.S. and anti-dictatorship demonstrations.
    In Guayaquil, students and citizens held multiple anti-U.S. and anti-dictatorship gatherings and marches throughout the day on the 4th. Speakers at the meetings strongly condemned the military government's dictatorial rule, which was fostered by U.S. imperialism, and demanded the restoration of the constitution. The reactionary authorities dispatched large numbers of military police to suppress the demonstrators. The angry demonstrators, unafraid of brute force, bravely fought back with stones. After being dispersed by the police, the demonstrators regrouped and persisted in their demonstrations. This struggle quickly spread throughout the urban area of Guayaquil.
    On the same day, students and citizens in the capital, Quito, also took to the streets and held demonstrations and gatherings in several parts of the city. Ignoring police suppression, the demonstrators surrounded the Presidential Palace, strongly demanding the restoration of the constitution and protesting the reactionary military government's refusal to relinquish power. Since January 27th, the people of Quito have held many fierce anti-U.S. and anti-dictatorship demonstrations and have attacked the "U.S.-Ecuadorian Cultural Center," an American cultural aggression institution.
    Xinhua News Agency, 4th dispatch from Quito: Jorge Zavala Baquerizo, former president of the University of Guayaquil in Ecuador, recently issued a statement condemning the U.S. attempt to establish a "Permanent Pan-American Armed Force" through the Organization of American States to trample on the Latin American continent and protect the interests of the U.S. ruling clique.
    In the statement, he revealed that the U.S. ruling clique, due to its shameless aggression against the Dominican Republic, has been exposed as practitioners of a despicable policy of interventionism. To escape the condemnation of world opinion, they have decided to establish a "Permanent Pan-American Armed Force" to use this force to intervene wherever the U.S. feels unstable.
    The statement pointed out that if the U.S. attempt succeeds, it will not only be the U.S. Marines that trample on the Latin American continent, but also the "Permanent Pan-American Armed Force" armed by the U.S.
    The statement said that the establishment of this aggressive army, controlled by the U.S. Pentagon, poses an extremely serious danger to the independence, self-determination, and security of the people of Latin American countries and a genuine threat to world peace.

### 坚决反对美帝及其走狗的独裁统治  厄瓜多尔人民愤怒示威要求军政府下台  厄著名人士谴责美国阴谋利用泛美部队干涉各国内政

1966-02-09
第5版()
专栏：

    坚决反对美帝及其走狗的独裁统治
    厄瓜多尔人民愤怒示威要求军政府下台
    厄著名人士谴责美国阴谋利用泛美部队干涉各国内政
    新华社六日讯　基多消息：厄瓜多尔人民一月二十七日开始的反美反独裁斗争，在四日从首都基多扩展到全国最大的城市瓜亚基尔。这一天两大城市居民都举行了激烈的反美反独裁示威。
    瓜亚基尔的学生和市民在四日一天内举行了多次反美反独裁的集会和游行。会上的发言者强烈谴责由美帝国主义一手扶植的军政府独裁统治，要求恢复宪法。反动当局出动大批军警对示威者进行镇压，愤怒的示威群众不畏强暴，用石块英勇回击。示威者在被警察冲散后，重新集结起来，坚持示威游行。这一斗争迅速扩展到瓜亚基尔整个市区。
    同一天，首都基多的学生和市民也涌上街头，在市内好几个地区举行示威和集会。示威者不顾警察的镇压，包围了总统府，强烈要求恢复宪制和抗议反动的军政府拒不交出政权。基多人民从一月二十七日以来，连续多次举行了激烈的反美反独裁示威，并且袭击了美国的文化侵略机构“美国—厄瓜多尔文化中心”。
    新华社四日讯　基多消息：厄瓜多尔瓜亚基尔大学前校长豪尔赫·萨瓦拉·巴克里索最近发表声明谴责美国企图通过美洲国家组织建立一支“常设泛美武装部队”，来蹂躏拉丁美洲大陆和保卫美国统治集团的利益。
    他在声明中揭露说，美国统治集团由于对多米尼加进行无耻地侵略而原形毕露，他们是卑鄙的干涉主义政策的奉行者。他们为了逃避世界舆论的谴责，决定建立一支“常设泛美武装部队”，以便用这支军队在一切美国感到不稳的地方进行干涉。
    声明指出，如果美国的企图一旦得逞，蹂躏拉丁美洲大陆的将不光光是美国海军陆战队，而且还有由美国武装起来的“常设泛美武装部队”。
    声明说，建立这支由美国五角大楼控制的侵略军，对拉丁美洲各国人民的独立、自决和安全是极为严重的危险，对世界和平是真正的威胁。
