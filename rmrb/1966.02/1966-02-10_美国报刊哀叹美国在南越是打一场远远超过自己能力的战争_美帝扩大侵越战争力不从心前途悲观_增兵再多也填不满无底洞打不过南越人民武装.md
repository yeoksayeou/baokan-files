Title: American Newspapers Lament That the U.S. is Fighting a War in South Vietnam Far Beyond Its Capabilities; U.S. Imperialism Strains Under the Expansion of the War of Aggression Against Vietnam, With Pessimistic Prospects; No Amount of Increased Troops Can Fill the Bottomless Pit or Defeat the South Vietnamese People's Armed Forces
Author:
Date: 1966-02-10
Edition: 4

　　新华社七日讯　美国许多资产阶级记者和专栏评论家认为，约翰逊政府扩大侵越战争势在必行，但是他们看到美国的实力同侵略野心之间的差距越来越大，战争越升级，对美国的后果越不堪设想。
　　二月七日的《美国新闻与世界报道》的一篇文章说，“随着停战（指暂停轰炸）的结束，越南战争大概将走向一个更有决定意义的阶段。比过去更大规模的战争被认为是不可避免的。它不排除（战争）扩大到邻近的老挝或泰国。在未来的战斗中，北越——作为麻烦的根源——是不会被饶恕的。”
　　文章说，“恢复轰炸北方的军事目标——曾经停止数周——是新战略的一部分。被选定的轰炸目标的重要性必将不断逐步升级。”
　　文章说，“在这同时，今后几个月里地面战斗将会大大加强，在这些战斗中，美国军队将负担主要任务。美国目前在越南有十九万人，夏天将迅速地上升为四十万而在秋末将增加到五十万。”
　　文章透露，“美国准备战争的港口和供应基地已经接近完成。空军基地正在扩建，兵营正在修建。大量的战争物资正在南越和泰国大量地屯积起来。成千的直升飞机、飞机和坦克、价值数亿美元的军火和炸弹正在源源不断地运往战场。美国未来一年的战争费用将大大超过一百亿美元。”
　　文章说，“结果，战争在规模上和开支上将很快地接近朝鲜战争，迹象表明，其激烈程度在其未结束之前将远远超过那场战争。”
　　《纽约时报》驻华盛顿首席记者赖斯顿二日在这家报纸上发表文章说，美国政府恢复轰炸越南北方的决定“预示着战场上美国兵力和火力将不断加强”。
　　他说，华盛顿的“一切迹象”表明，约翰逊目前“正在进行将比恢复轰炸的决定更为重要的增加兵力和火力的准备”。他还透露，美国侵略军还计划对越南南方解放区加紧轰炸。
　　据赖斯顿透露，美国政府过去一个多月对越南北方“暂停轰炸”的做法，除了它的政治目的以外，还有军事上的目的。他说：“实际上，停止轰炸是在盟国方面存在着严重的供应问题时进行的，那时有数十艘油船、军火船以及其它货船呆滞在南越海外。”美国政府企图利用暂停轰炸的机会来缓和供应紧张的问题。他指出，美国在暂停轰炸期间已经向南越运进了大量的供应物资。
　　美国一些大报对美国扩大战争的前途，表示悲观。美国专栏作家李普曼二日在《纽约先驱论坛报》上发表文章指出，“只要我们被束缚在国务卿腊斯克的目标上面，即打败和消灭越共……，那么我们就是在打一场远远超过我们的或是任何其他人的军事和政治能力的战争。”
　　李普曼说：“有些人会说，只要轰炸得多一些，大一些，并且大量增兵，我们将能够使军事现状发生有利于我方的变化。但是经验和这场可恶战争的历史是和这种希望相反的。因为反对我们的力量能够无限地增加，而要想对亚洲的陆上国家取得决定性的军事优势是危险的狂想。”
　　《基督教科学裁箴报》一日发表文章承认，“各种迹象表明，这场战争在全世界不得人心”。这家报纸指出：“现在的局势（对美国来说比一年前）更加严重”。
　　文章特别提到现在在美国的国会里，对越南问题的不安情绪是“公开化”了。国会议员们的“情绪越来越阴郁”。“访问过前线的曼斯菲尔德五人小组认为这场战争是‘没有底的’；那就是说，共产党人对美国的步步增兵，是敌得过的。”
　　《纽约时报》七日的社论承认，“当美国在扩大军事行动的时候，南越在政治、经济、社会和心理方面的情况却在不断恶化。在那里的二十万美国驻军使得问题更加严重，这引起了通货膨胀，并且把责任从越南人手中移到了美国人手中，使冲突逐渐变成一场美国人对亚洲人的战争”。这家报纸表示担心，对于许多亚洲人，不结盟国家甚至美国的盟国说来，美国殖民主义的性质将更加暴露。

Xinhua News Agency, 7th -- Many bourgeois journalists and columnists in the United States believe that the Johnson administration's expansion of the war of aggression against Vietnam is inevitable, but they see that the gap between the United States' strength and its aggressive ambitions is widening, and the more the war escalates, the more unimaginable the consequences for the United States will be.
An article in the February 7th issue of *U.S. News & World Report* stated, "With the end of the truce (referring to the suspension of bombing), the Vietnam War will probably move towards a more decisive stage. A war on a larger scale than in the past is considered inevitable. It does not rule out the expansion of the war into neighboring Laos or Thailand. In future battles, North Vietnam -- as the root of the trouble -- will not be spared."
The article stated, "Resuming the bombing of military targets in the North -- which had been suspended for several weeks -- is part of a new strategy. The importance of the selected bombing targets will gradually escalate."
The article stated, "At the same time, ground battles will be greatly intensified in the coming months, in which the U.S. military will bear the main task. The United States currently has 190,000 troops in Vietnam, which will rapidly rise to 400,000 in the summer and increase to 500,000 by the end of autumn."
The article revealed, "The ports and supply bases that the United States is preparing for war are nearing completion. Air bases are being expanded and barracks are being built. Large quantities of war materials are being stockpiled in large quantities in South Vietnam and Thailand. Thousands of helicopters, airplanes, and tanks, and hundreds of millions of dollars worth of munitions and bombs are constantly being shipped to the battlefield. The United States' war expenses for the coming year will greatly exceed 10 billion U.S. dollars."
The article stated, "As a result, the war will soon approach the Korean War in scale and expenditure, and indications are that its intensity will far exceed that war before it ends."
Reston, chief Washington correspondent for *The New York Times*, published an article in the newspaper on the 2nd, stating that the U.S. government's decision to resume bombing North Vietnam "foreshadows a continuous increase in American troop strength and firepower on the battlefield."
He said that "all indications" in Washington show that Johnson is currently "preparing to increase troop strength and firepower, which is more important than the decision to resume bombing." He also revealed that the U.S. aggressor troops also plan to intensify bombing of the liberation areas of South Vietnam.
According to Reston, the U.S. government's "suspension of bombing" of North Vietnam for the past month or more has had military purposes in addition to its political objectives. He said: "In fact, the cessation of bombing took place when there were serious supply problems on the Allied side, when dozens of oil tankers, munitions ships and other cargo ships were stagnant overseas in South Vietnam." The U.S. government attempted to use the opportunity of the suspension of bombing to alleviate the problem of supply tensions. He pointed out that the United States has shipped large quantities of supplies to South Vietnam during the suspension of bombing.
Some major U.S. newspapers expressed pessimism about the prospects for the U.S. expanding the war. U.S. columnist Lippmann pointed out in an article in the *New York Herald Tribune* on the 2nd, "As long as we are bound by Secretary of State Rusk's goal of defeating and eliminating the Viet Cong..., then we are fighting a war that is far beyond our or anyone else's military and political capabilities."
Lippmann said: "Some people will say that as long as we bomb more, bigger, and increase troops on a large scale, we will be able to change the military situation in our favor. But experience and the history of this odious war are contrary to this hope. Because the forces opposing us can increase indefinitely, and it is a dangerous fantasy to want to gain a decisive military advantage over Asian land countries."
*The Christian Science Monitor* published an article on the 1st acknowledging that "all indications show that this war is unpopular throughout the world." The newspaper pointed out: "The current situation is (more serious for the United States than a year ago)."
The article specifically mentioned that in the U.S. Congress, the anxiety about the Vietnam issue has become "public." The "mood of the congressmen is becoming more and more gloomy." "The Mansfield five-member group, which visited the front lines, believes that the war is 'bottomless'; that is to say, the communists can defeat the United States' step-by-step increase in troops."
*The New York Times* editorial on the 7th acknowledged that "while the United States is expanding military operations, the political, economic, social and psychological situation in South Vietnam is constantly deteriorating. The 200,000 American troops stationed there have made the problem even more serious, which has caused inflation and shifted responsibility from the hands of the Vietnamese to the hands of the Americans, gradually turning the conflict into a war of Americans against Asians." The newspaper expressed concern that for many Asians, non-aligned countries, and even allies of the United States, the nature of American colonialism will be more exposed.
