Title: The All-Out Brawl in a Civilized Play
Author: Chi Bei'ou, Jiang Fan
Date: 1966-02-26
Edition: 4

　　The All-Out Brawl in a Civilized Play
　　Chi Bei'ou　Poem　Jiang Fan　Drawing
　　Parliamentary democracy,
　　Strange and bizarre.
　　One moment giggling and laughing,
　　The next moment arguing and shouting,
　　When it comes to falling out,
　　A full-blown fight is also acceptable.
　　Please look,
　　India, this "showcase,"
　　The parliament turns into a battlefield.
　　Big officials beat small officials,
　　The "Sharp-End Party" smashes the "Bald-End Party."*
　　Ink bottles and leather shoes fly together,
　　Microphones clash with tables.
　　Bloody struggle in parliamentary politics,
　　The all-out brawl in a civilized play.
　　It seems,
　　There is a little less "Tashkent spirit,"
　　And a little more Chicago style.
　　*In the world-famous book "Gulliver's Travels," it is written that in the land of Lilliput there are two opposing parties. Supporters of one party crack eggs from the sharper end, called the "Sharp-End Party"; supporters of the other crack eggs from the balder end, called the "Bald-End Party." (Attached image)

### 文明戏里的全武行
池北偶　江帆
1966-02-26
第4版()
专栏：

　　文明戏里的全武行
　　池北偶　诗　江帆　画
　　议会民主，
　　奇形怪状。
　　一会儿嘻嘻哈哈，
　　一会儿吵吵嚷嚷，
　　逢到翻脸那时节，
　　大打出手也无妨。
　　请看，
　　印度这“橱窗”，
　　议会变战场。
　　大老爷揍二老爷，
　　“尖端党”砸“秃头党”。＊
　　墨水瓶与皮鞋齐飞，
　　麦克风同桌子对抗。
　　议会政治中的流血斗争，
　　文明戏里的全武行。
　　看来，
　　少了一点“塔什干精神”，
　　多了一点芝加哥风尚。
    ＊世界名著《格列佛游记》中写到，在小人国里有两个敌对的政党。一个政党的支持者从较尖的一端打鸡蛋，叫“尖端党”；另一个的支持者却从较秃一端打鸡蛋，叫“秃头党”。（附图片）
