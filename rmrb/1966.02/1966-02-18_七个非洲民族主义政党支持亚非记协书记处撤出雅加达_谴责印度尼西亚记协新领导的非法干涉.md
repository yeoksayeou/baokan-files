Title: Seven African Nationalist Parties Support the Withdrawal of the AAJA Secretariat from Jakarta, Condemn the Illegal Interference of the New Leadership of the Indonesian Journalists Association
Author: 
Date: 1966-02-18
Edition: 4

    Xinhua News Agency, Cairo, February 16th - Seven African nationalist parties issued a statement here on the 16th, supporting the withdrawal of the Secretariat of the Afro-Asian Journalists Association (AAJA) from Jakarta and condemning the serious interference of the new leadership of the Indonesian Journalists Association in the affairs of the AAJA.
    The representatives in Cairo of the Pan-Africanist Congress of Azania (PAC), the Mozambique Revolutionary Committee (COREMO), the Bechuanaland People's Party (BPP), the Basutoland Congress Party (BCP), and the Swaziland Progressive Party (SPP) issued statements with identical content. The statements said that their parties were seriously disturbed by the many difficulties created by the new leadership of the Indonesian Journalists Association for the Secretariat of the AAJA. They "fully support the decision of the Secretariat of the AAJA to withdraw from Jakarta and relocate to a place where it can continue its struggle against imperialism and old and new colonialism unhindered."
    The statements expressed support for the Secretariat of the AAJA in implementing the principled line against imperialism and old and new colonialism in accordance with the Jakarta Declaration.
    The statements said, "The activities of the Secretariat of the AAJA have a profound impact on our struggle against imperialism and old and new colonialism. Therefore, we firmly believe that hindering the activities of the Secretariat of the AAJA is hindering the oppressed peoples of Asia and Africa."
    The representatives in Cairo of the South West Africa National Union (SWANU) and the Zimbabwe African National Union (ZANU) also issued statements with identical content. The statements pointed out: "The AAJA is an independent, anti-imperialist association of revolutionary journalists, and any interference with its activities, or any attempt, such as that by the new leadership of the Indonesian Journalists Association, to transform it into a tool serving the interests of any particular country, cannot and should not be tolerated."
    The statements said, "We firmly support the Secretariat's efforts to find a new and favorable location for its activities."
    The statements said, "The anti-imperialist Jakarta Declaration is the guiding principle of the AAJA, and it is the solemn responsibility of all members of the AAJA to resolutely oppose any conspiracy to deviate from this principle and to strive to strengthen the AAJA."
    The statements concluded: "The AAJA has a historical mission to fulfill and must be given all support to facilitate the smooth completion of these tasks: opposing imperialism, led by the United States, and old and new colonialism."

### 七个非洲民族主义政党支持亚非记协书记处撤出雅加达  谴责印度尼西亚记协新领导的非法干涉

1966-02-18
第4版()
专栏：

    七个非洲民族主义政党支持亚非记协书记处撤出雅加达
    谴责印度尼西亚记协新领导的非法干涉
    新华社开罗十六日电　七个非洲民族主义政党十六日在这里发表声明，支持亚非新闻工作者协会书记处撤出雅加达，并且谴责印度尼西亚新闻工作者协会新领导严重干涉亚非新闻工作者协会的事务。
    南非泛非主义者大会、莫三鼻给革命委员会、贝专纳人民党、巴苏陀兰大会党和斯威士兰进步党驻开罗代表分别发表了内容相同的声明。声明说，他们的政党对印度尼西亚新闻工作者协会新领导给亚非新闻工作者协会书记处制造许多困难，感到严重不安。他们“完全支持亚非新闻工作者协会书记处关于撤出雅加达，迁到它能够不受妨碍地继续进行反对帝国主义和新老殖民主义的斗争的地方去的决定”。
    声明表示支持亚非新闻工作者协会书记处本着雅加达宣言，执行反对帝国主义和新老殖民主义的原则性路线。
    声明说，“亚非新闻工作者协会书记处的活动对我们反对帝国主义和新老殖民主义的斗争具有深刻的影响。因此我们深信，妨碍亚非新闻工作者协会书记处的活动就是妨碍受压迫的亚非人民。”
    西南非洲民族联盟和津巴布韦非洲民族联盟驻开罗代表也发表了内容相同的声明。声明指出：“亚非新闻工作者协会是革命新闻工作者的一个独立、反帝的协会，对它活动的任何干预，或者象印度尼西亚新闻工作者协会的新领导那样，企图把它变成为任何一个特定国家的利益效劳的一种工具的任何尝试，都是不能也不应该容忍的。”
    声明说：“我们坚决支持书记处为寻找一个新的有利的活动地点而作的努力。”
    声明说：“反帝的雅加达宣言是亚非新闻工作者协会的指导方针，亚非新闻工作者协会全体会员的重任是坚决反对任何背离方针的阴谋，努力加强亚非新闻工作者协会。”
    声明最后说：“亚非新闻工作者协会有着历史任务需要完成，必须给予它一切支持以便于它顺利完成这些任务：反对以美国为首的帝国主义和新老殖民主义。”
