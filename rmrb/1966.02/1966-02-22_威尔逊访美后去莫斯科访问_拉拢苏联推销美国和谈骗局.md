　　Wilson Visits Moscow After Visiting the U.S.
    拉拢 Soviet Union to Peddle U.S. Peace Talk Swindle
    Xinhua News Agency, London, February 21st - British Prime Minister Wilson left for Moscow today for a four-day visit at the invitation of the Soviet government.
    Before his departure, Wilson revealed to reporters that he would "discuss major international issues such as easing East-West tensions, Vietnam, and preventing the proliferation of nuclear weapons in a very frank manner" with Soviet leaders. Regarding the Vietnam issue, Wilson made no secret of the fact that the purpose of his visit to the Soviet Union was to discuss with Soviet leaders using their status as co-chairmen of the 1954 Geneva Conference to propose an international conference on the Vietnam issue, in order to realize Johnson's "unconditional discussions" conspiracy. He said, "We have taken various initiatives, and we will continue to do so in order to bring all parties to the negotiating table."
    Wilson announced his acceptance of the Soviet invitation after visiting Washington last December. Minister of Technology Cousins and Minister of State for Disarmament Affairs Chalfont are accompanying Wilson.

### 威尔逊访美后去莫斯科访问  拉拢苏联推销美国和谈骗局

1966-02-22
第6版()
专栏：

　　威尔逊访美后去莫斯科访问
    拉拢苏联推销美国和谈骗局
    新华社伦敦二十一日电  英国首相威尔逊应苏联政府邀请今天动身去莫斯科进行四天访问。
    在动身前，威尔逊向记者透露，他将同苏联领袖们“十分坦率地讨论缓和东西方紧张关系、越南以及防止核武器扩散等重大国际问题”。关于越南问题，威尔逊毫不掩饰他这次访苏的目的是和苏联领导人商谈利用一九五四年日内瓦会议两主席的身份倡议召开关于越南问题的国际会议，来实现约翰逊的“无条件讨论”阴谋。他说，“我们曾经采取了种种主动行动，我们还要继续这样做以便使各个方面到会议桌旁来”。
    威尔逊是在去年十二月访问华盛顿以后宣布接受苏联邀请的。和威尔逊同行的有工艺大臣卡曾斯和负责裁军事务的国务大臣查尔方特。
