Title: Tanganyika Journalists' Association Chairman Sends Letter to Indonesian Foreign Minister Condemning Indonesian Rightists' Interference in Affairs of Afro-Asian Journalists' Association

Author: 
Date: 1966-02-13
Edition: 4

    Xinhua News Agency, Dar es Salaam, February 11th: B.S. Karonji Mair, Chairman of the Tanganyika Journalists' Association, recently wrote to the Minister of Foreign Affairs of the Indonesian government, condemning the Indonesian rightists' interference in the work of the Secretariat of the Afro-Asian Journalists' Association and the persecution of progressive Indonesian journalists.
    In his letter, Karonji Mair said: "We are deeply shocked by the situation that forced the Secretariat of the Afro-Asian Journalists' Association to temporarily withdraw from Jakarta." He pointed out that the various acts of interference by Indonesian right-wing forces made it impossible for the Secretariat of the Afro-Asian Journalists' Association and all progressive newspapers in Indonesia to carry out their work. He said: The spirit of solidarity, the Jakarta Declaration, and the Bandung Spirit compel us to condemn all these barbaric acts without compromise.
    He said that the members of the Afro-Asian Journalists' Association express solidarity with their colleagues in Indonesia and demand "an end to all reactionary actions persecuting progressive journalists."

### 坦噶尼喀记协主席致函印度尼西亚外长  谴责印度尼西亚右派干涉亚非记协事务

1966-02-13
第4版()
专栏：

    坦噶尼喀记协主席致函印度尼西亚外长
    谴责印度尼西亚右派干涉亚非记协事务
    新华社达累斯萨拉姆十一日电　坦噶尼喀新闻工作者协会主席贝·萨·卡荣朱迈勒最近写信给印度尼西亚政府外交部长，谴责印度尼西亚右派对亚非新闻工作者协会书记处工作的干涉和对印度尼西亚进步新闻工作者的迫害。
    卡荣朱迈勒在信中说：“我们对于迫使亚非新闻工作者协会书记处暂时撤离雅加达的事态感到十分震惊。”他指出，印度尼西亚右派势力采取的各种干涉活动，使亚非新闻工作者协会书记处和印度尼西亚一切进步报纸不可能进行工作。他说：团结的精神、雅加达宣言和万隆精神使我们不能不毫不妥协地谴责所有这一切野蛮行动。
    他说，亚非新闻工作者协会的会员都表示声援他们在印度尼西亚的同行，要求“停止一切迫害进步新闻工作者的反动行动。”
