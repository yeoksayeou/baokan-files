Title: Western Newspapers Say Johnson's Bombs Solve Nothing; U.S. Use of the United Nations is Doomed to Fail
Author:
Date: 1966-02-03
Edition: 4

    According to Xinhua News Agency, February 2nd, the U.S. decision to resume bombing the Democratic Republic of Vietnam has made Western public opinion doubt the U.S.'s "peaceful sincerity." They fear that the U.S. action will further escalate the war. Some newspapers also pointed out that the U.S.'s false peace appeal to the United Nations Security Council contradicts the decision to resume bombing, believing it is a smokescreen to deceive public opinion and cover up its expansion of the war of aggression against Vietnam.
    The French newspaper *Le Monde* published an article on the 1st, saying: "The U.S. decision to resume attacks on North Vietnam and to hold discussions in the Security Council has aroused suspicion and opposition almost everywhere."
    *Le Figaro* believes that resuming bombing on the one hand, while appealing to the United Nations on the other, are "two contradictory aspects."
    Commenting on the letter from U.S. representative to the United Nations Goldberg to the Security Council, the newspaper said: "No one believes these high-sounding words." The newspaper believes that the real motive for the U.S. to request a meeting of the Security Council is either "to defend the adverse effects of resuming bombing in the face of world opinion, or to continue to launch the peace offensive that was launched a few weeks ago in another broader context."
    An editorial in the British *Times* on the same day expressed concern that "whether the increased bombing means that the war is more likely to escalate." The editorial said, "Bombing cancels out any appeal to the Security Council." It also pointed out that resuming bombing "cannot win the war for South Vietnam."
    An editorial in the *Yorkshire Post* on January 31st, when talking about the U.S.'s actions to expand the war, pointed out: "This conflict is weakening the U.S. economy and tormenting its foreign relations. The more deeply the U.S. is trapped in the Far East, the more difficult it will be for it to maintain its interests and influence in Europe and elsewhere."
    A commentary in the Swedish *Stockholm News* on the 1st pointed out that the U.S. will not succeed in picking up the banner of the United Nations and continuing to peddle the "peace talk fraud" after the failure of the "peace offensive." Johnson's request to convene the Security Council to hear a report on the Vietnam issue, while proposing motions such as "opening the road to the conference table," is destined to fail.
    Canada's *Toronto Star* published an editorial on the 1st, saying: "Bombs solve nothing." The editorial criticized the U.S. for suggesting "unconditional negotiations" while refusing to negotiate with the South Vietnam National Liberation Front, which controls most of South Vietnam and is actually fighting the U.S. military.

### 西方报纸说约翰逊的炸弹解决不了任何问题  美国利用联合国注定要失败

1966-02-03
第4版()
专栏：

    西方报纸说约翰逊的炸弹解决不了任何问题
    美国利用联合国注定要失败
    据新华社二日讯　美国恢复轰炸越南民主共和国的决定使西方舆论怀疑美国的“和平诚意”，它们担心美国的行动会使战争进一步升级。有的报纸还指出，美国向联合国安理会发出的假和平呼吁同恢复轰炸的决定自相矛盾，认为这是蒙骗舆论、掩盖它扩大侵越战争的烟幕。
    法国《世界报》一日发表文章说：“美国恢复袭击越南北方和在安全理事会进行讨论的决定几乎到处引起怀疑和反对。”
    《费加罗报》认为，一方面恢复轰炸，同时向联合国呼吁是“两个相互矛盾的方面”。
    这家报纸在评论美国驻联合国代表戈德堡给安理会的信件时说：“谁都不相信这种冠冕堂皇的言词。”报纸认为，美国要求召开安理会的真实动机要么就是“想在世界舆论面前为恢复轰炸所产生的恶劣影响申辩，要么就是想在另一场合更广泛地继续展开几周前所发动的和平攻势”。
    英国《泰晤士报》同一天的社论担心“加强轰炸是否意味着战争更加可能扩大”。社论说，“轰炸勾消了向安理会提出的任何呼吁”。它还指出，恢复轰炸“也不能使南越赢得战争的胜利”。
    《约克郡邮报》一月三十一日的社论在谈到美国扩大战争的行径时指出：“这次冲突正在削弱美国的经济和折磨着它的对外关系。美国越是严重地陷在远东，它要维持在欧洲和其他地方的利益和影响就会越加困难。”
    瑞典《斯德哥尔摩新闻》一日的评论指出，美国在“和平攻势”失败后又拣起联合国的招牌继续兜售“和谈骗局”是不会得逞的。约翰逊要求召集安理会以听取一项关于越南问题的报告，同时提出诸如“打开通向会议桌的道路”的动议，其结果是注定要失败的。
    加拿大的《多伦多明星报》一日发表社论说：“炸弹解决不了任何问题”。社论批评美国一方面建议“无条件谈判”，同时又拒绝同控制南越大部分地区、并且实际在同美军作战的南越民族解放阵线谈判。
