Title: World Opinion Condemns Honolulu Conference for Plotting to Expand War of Aggression Against Vietnam; Albanian and Asian-African Opinion Point Out Vietnamese People Will Surely Defeat the Enemy; French Newspapers Mock the U.S. for Not Finding an Exit from the Vietnam Quagmire
Author: 
Date: 1966-02-10
Edition: 4

    Comprehensive Report by Our Newspaper According to Xinhua News Agency, world opinion has widely condemned the Honolulu Conference for plotting the conspiracy to expand the war of aggression against Vietnam.
    The Albanian newspaper *Zëri i Popullit* published a commentary on the 8th, stating that various news surrounding the Honolulu Conference indicate that the U.S. government is sparing no effort to expand the aggressive war in Vietnam. U.S. President Johnson, in Honolulu, explicitly stated that the U.S. must firmly remain in Vietnam, frankly admitting this purpose.
    The commentary stated that the glorious history of the Vietnamese people's anti-American national salvation struggle convincingly demonstrates that no matter what conspiracies and tricks the U.S. imperialists and their servants use, they are destined for shameful bankruptcy, and the Vietnamese people will undoubtedly defeat their enemies.
    The Basutoland Congress Party office in Accra, Ghana's capital, issued a statement on the 8th, saying that the U.S. resumption of bombing the Democratic Republic of Vietnam and Johnson's recent war cries in Honolulu "further prove that the U.S. intends to formulate new plans to continue this war of aggression on a larger scale."
    The statement pointed out that the UN Security Council has no right to discuss the Vietnam issue. The U.S.'s attempt to discuss the Vietnam issue in the UN Security Council is an attempt to escape the world's condemnation of its imperialist acts of aggression.
    The Syrian newspaper *Al-Ba'ath* published a commentary on the 9th, condemning the Honolulu Conference as a meeting where the U.S. aimed to continue its aggression against Vietnam and expand the war of aggression, and a serious threat to world peace.
    The newspaper called on people all over the world to condemn the conference and support the Vietnamese people's anti-aggression struggle.
    The Algerian Press Service published a commentary on the 7th regarding the Honolulu Conference, pointing out that the U.S. government, after its "peace offensive" suffered a defeat, "intends to continue and intensify the Vietnam War, and even expand the war to other countries in Southeast Asia."
    The French newspaper *Nation* published an article on the 8th, stating that the Honolulu Conference was both a conference to plan war actions and a conference to study "ways to manage South Vietnam," and "Washington has decided to pursue a two-pronged approach." However, in any case, the Vietnam issue has become a veritable labyrinth, and the Americans have lost the map that would enable them to find the exit from this labyrinth."
    The newspaper quoted U.S. journalist James Reston, mocking Johnson's decision to submit the Vietnam issue to the United Nations, reflecting that "they (referring to the Americans) are at a loss."
    The Swedish newspaper *Stockholm News* said on the 7th that the Honolulu Conference "symbolizes President Johnson's failure in Vietnam." The newspaper pointed out that the purpose of the conference "is, of course, to formulate military guidelines and political goals for the U.S. to continue its actions in the Vietnam War." However, the U.S. has no future in Vietnam, and "the U.S. policy in Vietnam has gone bankrupt."
    The newspaper also pointed out that the South Vietnam National Liberation Front and its armed forces have the support of the broad masses of the people, and they have already achieved success both militarily and politically; while the Americans "support all kinds of dictators, and whenever they think any of them have become useless horses, they participate in formulating conspiracies to help the (South Vietnamese puppet) army to seize power."

### 各国舆论谴责檀香山会议策划扩大侵越战争  阿尔巴尼亚和亚非舆论指出越南人民一定能战胜敌人  法国报纸嘲笑美国在越南问题上找不到迷魂阵的出口

1966-02-10
第4版()
专栏：

    各国舆论谴责檀香山会议策划扩大侵越战争
    阿尔巴尼亚和亚非舆论指出越南人民一定能战胜敌人
    法国报纸嘲笑美国在越南问题上找不到迷魂阵的出口
    本报综合报道　据新华社讯，各国舆论纷纷谴责檀香山会议策划扩大侵越战争的阴谋。
    阿尔巴尼亚《人民之声报》八日发表评论说，围绕檀香山会议的各种消息表明，美国政府正不惜一切努力扩大越南的侵略战争。美国总统约翰逊在檀香山声称美国必须坚决地留在越南，直言不讳地承认了这一目的。
    评论说，越南人民抗美救国斗争的辉煌历史令人信服地表明，不管美帝国主义者及其仆从使用什么阴谋和诡计，都注定要可耻地破产，而越南人民毫无疑问必将战胜自己的敌人。
    巴苏陀兰大会党驻加纳首都阿克拉的办事处八日发表声明说，美国恢复轰炸越南民主共和国和约翰逊最近在檀香山的战争叫嚣“进一步证明，美国打算制定新的计划，在更大规模上继续进行这场侵略战争”。
    声明指出，联合国安理会没有权利讨论越南问题。美国要在联合国安理会上讨论越南问题，是企图逃避全世界对它的帝国主义侵略行动的谴责。
    叙利亚《复兴报》九日发表评论谴责檀香山会议是美国要继续侵略越南和扩大侵略战争的一次会议，是对世界和平的严重威胁。
    报纸号召全世界人民谴责这次会议，并且支持越南人民的反侵略斗争。
    阿尔及利亚新闻社七日就檀香山会议发表评论指出，美国政府在它的“和平攻势”遭到失败后，“打算继续进行和强化越南战争，甚至把战争扩大到东南亚其他国家”。
    法国《民族报》八日发表文章说，檀香山会议既是策划战争行动的会议，又是研究“管理南越的办法”的会议，“华盛顿已经决定双管齐下”。但是，不管怎样，越南问题已经成为一个名副其实的迷魂阵，美国人已经失掉了使他们能够找到这个迷魂阵的出口的那张路线图。”
    这家报纸引用美国记者詹姆斯·赖斯顿的话，嘲笑约翰逊决定把越南问题提交联合国，反映出“他们（指美国人）手足无措”。
    瑞典《斯德哥尔摩新闻》七日说，檀香山会议“象征着约翰逊总统在越南的失败”。报纸指出，会议的目的“当然是为美国在越南战争中继续采取行动制订军事方针和政治目标”。但是，美国在越南是没有前途的，“美国在越南的政策已经破产”。
    报纸还指出，南越民族解放阵线及其武装部队得到广大人民的支持，他们已经在军事和政治上都取得了成就；而美国人却“支持各式各样的独裁者，而且每当他们认为其中的任何一个已经成了不中用的马时，他们就参与制订阴谋来帮助（南越伪）陆军来夺取政权”。
