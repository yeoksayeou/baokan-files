Title: America's Decadence as Seen Through Crime

Author: 
Date: 1966-02-17
Edition: 4

    In the United States, the prevalence of crime has reached an alarming level. Not only is the American government helpless to deal with it, but even Washington, where the American government is located, and even within government agencies, has become a breeding ground for crime. "U.S. News & World Report" magazine, citing official statistics, stated that from July 1964 to June 1965, hundreds of ordinary criminal cases, sixty-nine major criminal cases, and ten gambling cases occurred inside and outside federal government office buildings. In the new State Department office building, purse snatching, rape, and theft cases occurred one after another. The weekly magazine said that Washington is the fastest-growing city in the United States for criminal cases, and it also cited police statistics, which are obviously greatly reduced, saying that in July 1965 alone, Washington had two thousand eight hundred and forty-four criminal cases, an average of more than ninety cases per day.
    In addition to Washington, other important cities such as New York, Chicago, Philadelphia, Detroit, Cleveland, Boston, New Orleans, Pittsburgh, and Kansas City are all centers of criminal activity. According to materials released by the U.S. Federal Bureau of Investigation, the number of criminal cases that occurred in the United States in the first nine months of 1965 increased by 5 percent compared to the same period in 1964. The head of the FBI's Crime Records Division said in a speech that since 1958, criminal cases in American society have increased by 58 percent. In 1964, police departments across the United States received reports of more than two million six hundred thousand serious violations, higher than previous records. In 1964, in the United States, a serious crime occurred on average every twelve minutes, a murder, assault, or rape occurred every two and a half minutes, a robbery occurred every five minutes, and a theft occurred every twenty-eight seconds. In 1964, 43 percent of all property crimes in the United States were committed by teenagers aged 10 to 17; teenagers only account for about 15 percent of the national population.

### 从犯罪看腐朽的美国

1966-02-17
第4版()
专栏：

    从犯罪看腐朽的美国
    在美国，犯罪的盛行，已经达到了惊人的程度。不但美国政府对此束手无策，而且连美国政府所在地华盛顿、甚至政府机关里也已经成了犯罪的渊薮。《美国新闻与世界报道》杂志引用官方的统计数字说，从一九六四年七月到一九六五年六月，联邦政府办公楼内外曾经发生了好几百起一般犯罪案件，六十九起较大的犯罪案件和十起赌博案件。在国务院的新办公楼内，接二连三地发生了抢钱包、强奸和偷盗案件。这家周刊说，华盛顿是美国各大城市中犯罪案件增加得最快的，它还引用警察方面公布的显然是大大缩小的数字说，仅在一九六五年七月，华盛顿就发生二千八百四十四起犯罪案件，平均每天发生九十多起。
    除华盛顿以外，其他一些重要城市如纽约、芝加哥、费城、底特律、克利夫兰、波士顿、新奥尔良、匹兹堡、堪萨斯市等等都是犯罪活动中心。据美国联邦调查局公布的材料，一九六五年头九个月在美国各地发生的犯罪案件，比一九六四年同期增加了百分之五。联邦调查局犯罪档案司负责人在一次讲话中说，自从一九五八年以来，美国社会的犯罪案件增加了百分之五十八。在一九六四年，美国各地的警察局一共收到二百六十余万件严重犯法行为的报告，比往年的记录都高。在一九六四年，在美国，平均每十二分钟发生一起严重犯罪案件，每两分半钟发生一起谋杀、行凶或强奸案，每五分钟发生一起抢劫案，每二十八秒钟发生一起偷窃案。在一九六四年美国全部侵犯财产的犯罪案件中，有百分之四十三是十岁到十七岁的青少年干的；青少年在全国人口中只占百分之十五左右。
