Title: Johnson Cries for Reinforcements and Escalation of the War of Aggression Against Vietnam; Admits All Tricks Have Failed, Tries to Conceal Divisions Within the U.S. Ruling Clique
Author:
Date: 1966-02-13
Edition: 3

    According to Xinhua News Agency, Washington, February 12th: U.S. President Johnson announced at a press conference yesterday that the United States will further increase its troops in South Vietnam and expand the war of aggression against Vietnam.

    When a reporter asked if he thought it necessary to greatly increase the U.S. invasion force in Vietnam, Johnson emphasized in his answer, "It is necessary to increase manpower, and manpower will be provided according to the limits and requirements used by General Westmoreland (commander of the U.S. invasion forces in Vietnam)." According to reports, during the Honolulu Conference, Westmoreland requested that the U.S. invasion forces in Vietnam be increased from the current 200,000 to 400,000.

    Johnson revealed a state of panic and exhaustion in dealing with the Vietnam issue, saying, "I have tested all the suggestions that people have made—stopping bombing for twenty or thirty days, proposing economic development proposals, various suggestions from the United Nations and non-aligned countries, the International Control Commission, and Geneva—I have done all of these."

    But, unfortunately, none of these tricks worked.

    Johnson also tried to conceal the U.S. government's plan to expand the war of aggression against Vietnam and the sharp divisions within the U.S. ruling clique on this issue. He said that there was "no major difference of opinion" between what Gavin and Kennan said and what the government was doing.

    Johnson expressed anxiety about the outbreak of another strong anti-American struggle in the Dominican Republic. He said that he expressed "deep regret" over the development of the situation in the Dominican Republic, "We are closely watching the situation," "This is a very difficult situation," "I hope... peace can be restored as soon as possible."

    Johnson also announced that Indian Prime Minister Mrs. Gandhi will visit the United States in March or April.

### 约翰逊叫嚷增兵扩大侵越战争  承认一切诡计都不能得逞，力图掩饰美国统治集团内部分歧

1966-02-13
第3版()
专栏：

    约翰逊叫嚷增兵扩大侵越战争
    承认一切诡计都不能得逞，力图掩饰美国统治集团内部分歧
    据新华社十二日讯　华盛顿消息：美国总统约翰逊昨天在记者招待会上宣布，美国将进一步向南越增兵，扩大侵越战争。
    当有记者问，他是否认为需要大大增加美国的侵越兵力，约翰逊在回答时强调，“需要增加兵员，而且将按照威斯特摩兰将军（侵越美军司令）使用的限度和要求提供兵员”。据报道，在檀香山会议期间，威斯特摩兰要求把侵越美军从目前的二十万人，增加到四十万人。
    约翰逊透露了他在越南问题上手忙脚乱、黔驴技穷的一副窘态，他说，“我已经把人们提出的各种建议作了试验——停炸二十或三十天，提出经济开发建议，联合国和不结盟国家的各种建议，国际监督委员会以及日内瓦——这一切我都照办了”。
    但是，无奈这些诡计都不能得逞。
    约翰逊还力图掩饰美国政府扩大侵越战争的计划和美国统治集团内部在这个问题上的尖锐分歧。他说，加文和凯南所说的跟政府所做的之间“没有什么很大的意见分歧”。
    约翰逊对于多米尼加又爆发强大的反美斗争表示焦虑不安。他说，他对多米尼加局势的发展表示“深为遗憾”，“我们正在密切注视着局势”，“这是一个非常困难的局势”，“我希望……尽快能恢复和平”。
    约翰逊还宣布印度新总理甘地夫人将在三、四月间访问美国。
