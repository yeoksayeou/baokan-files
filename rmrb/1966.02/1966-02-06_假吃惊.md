Title: Feigned Surprise
Author:
Date: 1966-02-06
Edition: 4

	The French Cabinet’s public statement expressing disagreement with UN intervention in the Vietnam issue surprised Washington. However, the French newspaper *Le Monde* believes that France's attitude "should not surprise American diplomacy" because Paris had already made its stance clear long ago.
	Indeed, Washington has nothing to be surprised about regarding France's attitude. The United States wants to use the UN as a tool for aggression in Vietnam, which no country with even a slight understanding of the advantages and disadvantages would agree to. Especially when the United States is having such a hard time, why would France pull chestnuts out of the fire for the United States? If France supported the United States in letting the UN interfere in the Vietnam issue, that would be beyond Washington's expectations.

### 假吃惊

1966-02-06
第4版()
专栏：三言两语

    假吃惊
    法国内阁公报表示不同意联合国干预越南问题，这使华盛顿感到吃惊。但是法国《世界报》认为法国的态度“不应该使美国外交感到吃惊”，因为巴黎早就表明这种态度了。
    的确，对于法国的这种态度，华盛顿是没有什么可吃惊的。美国要利用联合国作为侵略越南的工具，这是任何一个稍识利弊的国家都不会同意的。特别是在美国大倒其霉的时候，法国更怎么会为美国火中取栗呢？如果法国支持美国让联合国插手越南问题，那才是出乎华盛顿意料之外的哩。
