Title: Hatoyama Says He Will Send Representatives to Negotiate with the Soviet Union Before the National Election
Author:
Date: 1955-02-19
Page: 1

Xinhua News Agency, February 18th, Dateline Moscow: Tokyo Radio reported that Japanese Prime Minister Ichiro Hatoyama told reporters on February 16th that he had received a "reply from the Soviet side expressing willingness to fulfill Japan's wishes" through Domnitsky, head of the Soviet delegation in Japan. Hatoyama said: "I believe this is a formal reply from the Soviet side." He pointed out that before the national election, that is, before February 27th, the Japanese government "will probably decide on the members of the Japanese delegation. It is very likely that the delegation will be headed by Sugihara Arata, Vice Chairman of the Policy Research Council of the Democratic Party."
Hatoyama also said that he would discuss the location of the Soviet-Japanese negotiations with Foreign Minister Mamoru Shigemitsu.

### 鸠山说将在国会选举前派代表和苏联谈判

1955-02-19
第1版()
专栏：

    鸠山说将在国会选举前派代表和苏联谈判
    【新华社十八日讯】塔斯社十七日讯：东京电台报道，日本首相鸠山一郎二月十六日向记者们说，他通过苏联驻日本代表团团长多穆尼茨基接到了“苏联方面的答复，表示愿意满足日本的愿望”。鸠山说：“我认为这是苏联方面的正式答复。”他指出：在国会选举以前，也就是在二月二十七日以前，日本政府“大概会决定日本代表团的人选。代表团极有可能将由民主党的政务调查会副会长杉原荒太任团长”。
    鸠山又说：关于苏日谈判的地点问题，他要和外务相重光葵商谈。
