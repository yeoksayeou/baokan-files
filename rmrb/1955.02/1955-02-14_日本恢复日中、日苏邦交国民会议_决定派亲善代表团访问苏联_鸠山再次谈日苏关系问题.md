Title: National Conference for the Restoration of Japan-China and Japan-Soviet Diplomatic Relations Decides to Send Goodwill Delegation to Visit the Soviet Union; Hatoyama Again Discusses Japan-Soviet Relations

Author:

Date: 1955-02-14
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, Tokyo, February 12th: The National Conference for the Restoration of Japan-China and Japan-Soviet Diplomatic Relations, composed of prominent figures from all walks of life in Japan, held an enlarged cadre meeting on the 11th and decided to send a goodwill delegation to the Soviet Union in April to enhance friendly relations between Japan and the Soviet Union. A national conference will be held in late March to elect representatives for the delegation.

    The meeting decided to request Prime Minister Ichiro Hatoyama and Foreign Minister Mamoru Shigemitsu to actively promote a diplomatic policy of establishing normal relations with China and the Soviet Union, and decided to welcome the Chinese trade delegation that will visit Japan in March.

    The meeting elected Fusanosuke Kuhara as the chairman of the National Conference.

Xinhua News Agency, February 12th: TASS reported on the 11th: Radio Tokyo broadcast that Japanese Prime Minister Ichiro Hatoyama held talks with Arata Sugihara, a diplomatic policy advisor to the Democratic Party, on the 10th to discuss Molotov's report on the international situation and the foreign policy of the Soviet government, which he delivered at the Soviet Supreme Soviet meeting.

    After the meeting, Ichiro Hatoyama issued a statement to reporters, saying: "It is clear from the recent speech by Soviet Foreign Minister Molotov that the Soviet Union's policy of adjusting diplomatic relations with Japan has not changed. Therefore, the Japanese government intends to strive to establish normal diplomatic relations with the Soviet Union as soon as possible in the upcoming negotiations."

    Ichiro Hatoyama said that Japan will send an official delegation to negotiate with the Soviet Union before the Japanese Diet election (February 27th).

    Ichiro Hatoyama said in a speech in Nagasaki on the 9th, "Soviet Foreign Minister Molotov's speech was delivered in the spirit we expected." He said, "I believe that when negotiating with the Soviet Union on normalizing diplomatic relations, we should first discuss economic issues and then political issues." Hatoyama said, "After improving economic relations with North Korea and China, we also hope to hold negotiations."

### 日本恢复日中、日苏邦交国民会议  决定派亲善代表团访问苏联  鸠山再次谈日苏关系问题

1955-02-14
第4版()
专栏：

    日本恢复日中、日苏邦交国民会议
    决定派亲善代表团访问苏联
    鸠山再次谈日苏关系问题
   【新华社十二日讯】东京消息：日本各界知名人士组成的恢复日中、日苏邦交国民会议，十一日举行干部扩大会议，决定要在四月份派遣一个亲善代表团到苏联访问，以增进日苏两国的友好关系，并将在三月下旬举行全国性大会，推选代表团代表。
    会议决定要求首相鸠山一郎和外相重光葵积极推行同中国、苏联建立正常关系的外交政策，并决定欢迎三月将到日本访问的中国贸易代表团。
    会议选出久原房之助为国民会议会长。
   【新华社十二日讯】据塔斯社十一日消息：东京电台广播，日本首相鸠山一郎十日和民主党外交政策顾问杉原荒太举行会谈，讨论莫洛托夫在苏联最高苏维埃会议上所作的关于国际局势和苏联政府外交政策的报告。
    会谈以后，鸠山一郎对记者发表声明说：从苏联外交部长莫洛托夫最近的演说可以清楚看出，苏联调整和日本的外交关系的政策没有改变。因此，日本政府打算在即将举行的谈判中努力尽速和苏联建立正常的外交关系。
    鸠山一郎说，日本将在日本国会选举（二月二十七日）以前派遣一个官方代表团和苏联进行谈判。
    鸠山一郎九日在长崎发表演说时说，“苏联外交部长莫洛托夫的演说是在我们所预期的精神中发表的。”他说，“我认为，在和苏联进行关于使外交关系正常化的谈判时，首先应该讨论经济问题，然后再讨论政治问题。”鸠山说，“在和北朝鲜及中国改善经济关系以后，也希望进行谈判。”
