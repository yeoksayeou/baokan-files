Title: Chinese Arts and Crafts Exhibition Opens in Delhi, India
Author:
Date: 1955-02-09
Page: 4

    Xinhua News Agency, New Delhi, February 7th: The People's Republic of China Arts and Crafts Exhibition, jointly sponsored by the Chinese People's Association for Friendship with Foreign Countries and the All-India Fine Arts and Crafts Society, opened in Delhi on the afternoon of the 5th.
    Those attending the opening ceremony included Indian Vice President Radhakrishnan, Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs Chanda, Foreign Secretary of the Ministry of External Affairs R.K. Nehru, All-India Fine Arts and Crafts Society President Sain, Vice President Chakravarti, All-India Peace Council Vice Chairman Major General Sok, as well as people from Delhi's cultural and artistic circles and leaders of various social organizations.
    Chinese Ambassador to India Yuan Zhongxian and embassy staff also attended the opening ceremony. Foreign envoys and diplomatic officials stationed in India also attended the opening ceremony.
    Vice President Radhakrishnan delivered the opening speech, saying that he was very pleased to see the development of ancient Chinese fine arts and crafts. He added: "China and India have a long history of economic and cultural relations. China has made great contributions to human civilization. This exhibition will further strengthen the cultural ties between China and India."
    Ambassador Yuan Zhongxian said in his speech: The Chinese people attach great importance to their own excellent traditional art, and are also happy to learn from the artistic achievements of other nations. China and India have had a very deep relationship in the field of art in the past. Today, mutual learning and mutual assistance in this regard will undoubtedly be beneficial to the development of traditional art in both countries.
    All-India Fine Arts and Crafts Society Vice President Chakravarti read out Indian Prime Minister Nehru's congratulatory telegram at the meeting. The telegram said: "China has a great artistic tradition. It is a pleasure to see many Chinese works of art. We can learn a lot from them. It is particularly interesting to see that the Chinese government has been encouraging folk art and handicrafts in recent years." The telegram also said, "In addition to its artistic value, this exhibition will be welcomed because it brings about a greater understanding between the people of India and the people of China."
    After Vice President Radhakrishnan cut the ribbon, the guests visited the exhibition. The exhibition is divided into four rooms. More than 1,400 items are on display, including porcelain from Jingdezhen, Jiangxi Province, pottery from Shiwan, Guangdong Province, metalware, carvings, lacquerware, and various woven products, dyed fabrics, and embroidery.
    The exhibition is scheduled to be held in Delhi for ten days. It will then tour Calcutta, Madras, Bombay and other places.

### 我国工艺美术展览会在印度德里开幕

1955-02-09
第4版()
专栏：

    我国工艺美术展览会在印度德里开幕
    【新华社新德里七日电】中国人民对外文化协会和全印美术工艺协会联合举办的中华人民共和国工艺美术展览会于五日下午在德里开幕。
    参加开幕典礼的有印度共和国副总统拉德哈克里希南、外交部副部长钱达、外交部外事秘书拉·库·尼赫鲁、全印美术工艺协会主席塞恩、副主席查克拉瓦蒂、全印和平理事会副主席索克少将以及德里文化艺术界人士和各社会团体负责人。
    中国驻印度大使袁仲贤及大使馆人员也出席了开幕典礼。出席开幕典礼的还有各国驻印度使节和外交官员。
    拉德哈克里希南副总统致开幕词说，他很高兴看到中国古老的美术工艺得到了发展。他又说：“中印两国经济文化关系有着悠久的历史。中国对人类文明有着伟大贡献。通过这次展览会将更加强中印之间的文化联系。”
    袁仲贤大使致词说：中国人民极为重视本国优秀的传统艺术，同时也乐于学习别的民族的艺术成就。中印两国在艺术方面过去有很深的渊源关系。今天在这方面的互相学习、互相帮助，对于两国传统艺术的发展，无疑是有益的。
    全印美术工艺协会副主席查克拉瓦蒂在会上宣读了印度总理尼赫鲁的贺电。贺电说：“中国具有伟大的艺术传统。观看许多中国艺术品是一件乐事。我们可以从当中学习许多东西。特别感到有兴趣的是看到中国政府近年来在鼓励民间艺术和手工艺。”贺电又说，“除了它的艺术价值以外，这个展览会之所以将会受到欢迎，还因为它带来了印度人民和中国人民之间的更大的了解。”
    在拉德哈克里希南副总统剪彩后，来宾们参观了展览会。展览会共分四个室。展出的物品有一千四百多件，其中包括江西省景德镇的瓷器、广东省石湾的陶器、金属器、雕刻、漆器以及各种编织品、染织品和刺绣等。
    展览会预定在德里展出十天。然后将到加尔各答、马德拉斯、孟买等地巡回展出。
