Title: The American People Oppose the Eisenhower Government's Policy of Aggression; Some University Professors Criticize the U.S. Government's Crude Interference in China's Internal Affairs
Author:
Date: 1955-02-03
Page: 1

Xinhua News Agency, New York, February 2nd: TASS, New York, February 1st: The January 28th issue of the U.S. Congressional Record devoted twenty pages to publishing letters and telegrams received by Senator Morse (Oregon), an independent who spoke out against U.S. aggressive policies in the Far East and voted against the Congressional resolution authorizing U.S. military intervention in China's internal affairs.

　　Briskey of Van Nuys, California, demanded: "Let the Chinese solve their own problems."

　　Stevenson and Ellison of New Milford, Connecticut, wrote: "We hope you can stop the continuous hysteria and attempts to lead our country into adventures, which will have serious consequences for us."

　　Richard of Allentown, Pennsylvania, said, "These islands (Chinese islands) are not ours. The more we treat them as our own, the more friends we lose in the world."

　　Conner of St. Paul, Minnesota, said, "We should leave the Chinese people alone and let them solve their own problems. China will not succumb to our pressure."

　　A telegram from two people named Davis in Newark, New Jersey, said: "Please continue to represent us in opposing the war policies pursued by our government."

Xinhua News Agency, New York, February 2nd: TASS, New York, February 1st: According to United Press, some American university professors wrote to the "New York Times" criticizing the United States' crude interference in the internal affairs of the People's Republic of China, and pointed out the dangerous consequences of such interference.

　　For example, Gange, the president of the Woodrow Wilson School of International Relations affiliated with the University of Virginia, warned in a letter that the U.S. policy toward the Taiwan region could trigger a war with China. He also said that this war would make the people of Asia and all other people in "colored regions" around the world disgusted with the United States.

　　Professor Chandler said in a letter that he believes that U.S. armed intervention in Chinese affairs is an unrealistic policy.

　　Professor Goodrich of Columbia University pointed out in his letter that the U.S. policy toward Taiwan exceeds "the scope of rights stipulated in the United Nations Charter."

Xinhua News Agency, New York, February 2nd: New York dispatch: The "Time" magazine published on January 31st revealed that the U.S. "ceasefire" proposal is just a disguise for its policy of armed intervention in China.

　　The American weekly said that senior U.S. government officials said: The United States has not changed its policy, and Eisenhower and Dulles do not really expect the "ceasefire" to be realized. "They merely want to maintain the position of the United States as a peace advocate in the eyes of the world through the United Nations."

### 美国人民反对艾森豪威尔政府的侵略政策  一些大学教授抨击美国政府对中国内政的粗暴干涉

1955-02-03
第1版()
专栏：

    美国人民反对艾森豪威尔政府的侵略政策
    一些大学教授抨击美国政府对中国内政的粗暴干涉
   【新华社二日讯】塔斯社纽约一日讯：一月二十八日的美国国会官方公报“国会记录”用二十页的篇幅刊载了发言反对美国在远东的侵略政策、并且投票反对允许美国对中国内政实行军事干涉的国会决议的无党派参议员莫尔斯（俄勒冈州）所收到的信件和电报。
    加利福尼亚州范纳斯的布里斯凯要求说：“让中国人自己解决自己的问题吧。”
    康涅狄格州新密尔福的史蒂文森和埃利森写道：“我们希望你能制止连续不断的歇斯底里和使我国走向冒险的企图，这种冒险会使我们遭到严重后果。”
    宾夕法尼亚州阿兰敦的李查德说，“这些岛屿
  （中国的岛屿）不是我们的，我们愈加把它们当作我们的岛屿看待，我们就在世界上失掉愈多的朋友。
    明尼苏达州圣保罗的康诺说，“我们应当让中国人民安生，让他们自己解决自己的事情。中国是不会向我们的压力屈服的。”
    新泽西州纽瓦克两个姓戴维斯的人打来的电报说：“请您继续代表我们反对我国政府执行的战争政策。”
   【新华社二日讯】塔斯社纽约一日讯：据合众社消息，某些美国大学的教授写信给“纽约时报”，批评美国对中华人民共和国内政的粗暴干涉，并且指出了这种干涉的危险后果。
    例如，弗吉尼亚州大学附设伍德罗·威尔逊国际关系学校校长根奇在信中警告说，美国对台湾地区的政策可能引起同中国的战争。他又说，这个战争将使亚洲人民和全世界所有其他“有色人种地区”的人民厌恶美国。
    昌德勒教授在信中说，他认为，美国对中国事务的武装干涉是不现实的政策。
    哥伦比亚大学教授古德里奇在自己信中指出，美国对台湾的政策超过“联合国宪章所规定的权利范围”。
   【新华社二日讯】纽约消息：一月三十一日出版的“时代”周刊透露，美国的“停火”建议不过是掩盖它武装干涉中国的政策的外衣而已。
    这份美国周刊说，美国政府的高级官员说：美国并没有改变它的政策，艾森豪威尔和杜勒斯并不真正期望“停火”会实现。“他们仅仅想通过联合国来在世界面前维持美国作为和平倡导者的地位。”
