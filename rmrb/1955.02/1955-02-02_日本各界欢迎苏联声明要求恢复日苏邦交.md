Title: All Circles in Japan Welcome Soviet Statement Requesting Restoration of Japanese-Soviet Diplomatic Relations
Author:
Date: 1955-02-02
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, Tokyo, February 1st: Following the presentation by Soviet delegation head to Japan, Domnitsky, of a statement from the Soviet government to Hatoyama concerning Japanese-Soviet relations, it has sparked widespread reaction in Japan. People from all walks of life in Japan are eagerly requesting the early restoration of diplomatic relations between Japan and the Soviet Union.

    The National Council for the Restoration of Japanese-Chinese and Japanese-Soviet Diplomatic Relations, composed of prominent figures from Japanese political, economic, and cultural circles, submitted a petition and request to Japanese Prime Minister Hatoyama Ichiro on January 31, urging the government to take concrete steps to normalize Japanese-Soviet relations as soon as possible.
    Representatives of the Governors' Conference of the Tohoku and Hokkaido regions of Japan also submitted a request to the government and the Diet on January 29, requesting that the government and the Diet hold a Japanese-Soviet economic conference, conclude a Japanese-Soviet fisheries agreement, and invite a Soviet fisheries delegation to visit Japan as soon as possible.
    Suzuki Mosaburo, chairman of the Japan Socialist Party (Left), said after the publication of the Soviet government's statement: "We do not need to speculate on the intention of the Soviet proposal. Since the Soviet Union has extended its hand, we should grasp it." Suzuki also pointed out that the Japanese Foreign Ministry should eliminate foreign oppression.
    Tadatsugu Shimazu, president of the Japanese Red Cross Society, called the report of the Soviet Union's proposal to normalize relations with Japan "certainly bright news." Ito Imasato, chairman of the Japan-Soviet Trade Promotion Conference, said that the restoration of diplomatic relations between Japan and the Soviet Union would bring many benefits to Japan, especially in terms of trade.

### 日本各界欢迎苏联声明要求恢复日苏邦交

1955-02-02
第4版()
专栏：

    日本各界欢迎苏联声明要求恢复日苏邦交
    【新华社一日讯】东京消息：苏联驻日代表团团长多穆尼茨基代表苏联政府交给鸠山关于日苏关系问题的声明发表后，在日本引起广泛的反应，日本各界人士热烈要求早日恢复日苏两国间的邦交。
    由日本政治、经济和文化界知名人士组成的恢复日中日苏邦交国民会议，在一月三十一日交给日本首相鸠山一郎一份恳请书和要求书，要求政府积极采取具体步骤，使日苏关系早日正常化。
    日本东北和北海道地区知事会议的代表，一月二十九日也向政府和国会提交了一份要求书，要求政府和国会早日举行日苏经济会议、缔结日苏渔业协定和邀请苏联渔业代表团访问日本。
    日本左派社会党主席铃木茂三郎在苏联政府的声明发表后说：“我们对苏联的建议没有揣度其意图的必要。苏联既然伸过手来，我们就应该握住这只手。”铃木并指出日本外务省应排除外国的压迫。
    日本红十字会会长岛津忠承把苏联提出的同日本关系正常化的报道，称为“肯定地是一个光明的消息”。日苏贸易促进会议主席伊藤今朝市说：日本同苏联恢复外交关系将给日本带来很多好处，特别是在贸易方面。
