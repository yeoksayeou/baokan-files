Title: New Zealand Communist Party General Secretary Wilcock Represents the Central Committee in Issuing a Statement: The Devaluation of the Pound Reflects the Deep Crisis of the Imperialist Camp
Author:
Date: 1967-12-14
Edition: 5

New Zealand Communist Party General Secretary Wilcock recently issued a statement on behalf of the New Zealand Communist Party Central Committee. The statement says: "Within the imperialist camp, the devaluation of the pound not only indicates the rapid decline of Britain's economic status, but it also reflects the deep and increasingly severe crises in various aspects of this camp." The various maneuvers surrounding currency within the imperialist world "are an external manifestation of the deepening total crisis within the imperialist camp."

This statement, published in the weekly *Voice of the People*, points out that the position of the U.S. dollar is shaky. The U.S. gold reserves have dropped to an unprecedented low. The U.S. share in international trade has decreased by fifty percent since 1949.

The statement discusses the New Zealand government's decision to follow Britain in devaluing the New Zealand currency. It says that this is not a solution to the increasingly severe economic problems facing New Zealand. It merely proves that New Zealand is part of the imperialist world and is heading towards a deeper crisis.

The statement points out that the New Zealand ruling class is increasingly submitting to the interests of U.S. monopolies, providing more troops and other "aid" to Vietnam. Their entire policy has increased economic difficulties within New Zealand. The statement points out that the devaluation of the New Zealand currency will make the distribution of "national income" even more unfavorable to workers and other low-income groups, and more favorable to big monopolists. The statement calls on the New Zealand people to fight against the New Zealand government's policy of protecting and increasing the profits of big enterprises while lowering the living standards of the broad masses of the people.

In the final part of the statement, Wilcock says: "From the perspective of historical development, capitalism has become outdated. Humanity has reached a stage where only the socialist system can meet its social needs."

The statement then quotes the words of the great leader of the world's people, Chairman Mao: "In the world today, there are two kinds of winds: the East Wind and the West Wind. There is a Chinese saying: 'It is either the East Wind prevailing over the West Wind, or the West Wind prevailing over the East Wind.' I think the characteristic of the current situation is that the East Wind prevails over the West Wind, that is, the forces of socialism have the upper hand over the forces of imperialism."

The statement points out that under this overall situation, for the broad masses of the people in New Zealand and other capitalist countries, the fundamental solution is to establish a socialist society.

### 新共总书记威尔科克斯代表全国书记处发表声明指出　英镑贬值反映帝国主义阵营的深刻危机　号召新西兰人民进行斗争反对政府屈从美帝国主义的反动政策

1967-12-14
第5版()
专栏：

　　新共总书记威尔科克斯代表全国书记处发表声明指出
　　英镑贬值反映帝国主义阵营的深刻危机
　　号召新西兰人民进行斗争反对政府屈从美帝国主义的反动政策
    据新华社十一日讯  新西兰共产党总书记威尔科克斯最近代表新西兰共产党全国书记处发表声明说：“在帝国主义阵营里，英镑贬值不仅表明了英国经济地位的急速下降”，而且“它也反映了这个阵营各方面的深刻和日益发展的危机。”帝国主义世界围绕着货币而玩弄的一切手法，“是帝国主义阵营内部总危机急速加深的一种外部反映”。
    这项刊载在《人民之声》周刊上的声明在谈到美元摇摇欲坠的地位时指出：美国的黄金储备已经下降到前所未有的最低点。美国在国际贸易中的份额从一九四九年以来下降了百分之五十。
    声明在谈到新西兰政府追随英国宣布新西兰货币贬值时说，这根本不是解决新西兰面临的日益严重的经济问题的办法。它仅仅证明新西兰是帝国主义世界的一部分，并且正在日益走向更加深刻的危机。
    声明指出，新西兰统治集团越来越屈从于美国垄断资本的利益，向越南提供更多的军队和其它“援助”，他们的整个政策增加了新西兰国内的经济困难。声明指出，新西兰货币贬值将使新西兰“国民收入”的分配更加不利于工人和其他收入低微的阶层，而更加有利于大垄断资本家。声明号召新西兰人民进行斗争，来反对新西兰政府保护和增加大企业的利润而降低广大人民群众生活水平的政策。
    威尔科克斯在声明的最后一部分说：“从历史发展看，资本主义已经过时了，人类已经发展到只有社会主义制度才能适应它的社会需要的阶段。”
    声明接着引用了世界人民的伟大领袖毛主席的话：“世界上现在有两股风：东风，西风。中国有句成语：‘不是东风压倒西风，就是西风压倒东风。’我认为目前形势的特点是东风压倒西风，也就是说，社会主义的力量对于帝国主义的力量占了压倒的优势。”
    声明指出，在这样的总形势下，对新西兰和其它资本主义国家的广大人民群众来说，只有建立社会主义社会才是根本的解决办法。
