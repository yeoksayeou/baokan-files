Title: Premier Zhou Enlai Sends Congratulations on Tunisian Independence
Author:
Date: 1956-04-05
Page: 2
Xinhua News Agency, April 4th: Premier Zhou Enlai sent a telegram to Tunisian Prime Minister Tahar Ammar on the 4th, congratulating Tunisia on its independence. The text of the telegram is as follows:
His Excellency Prime Minister Tahar Ammar:
    On the occasion of Tunisia's independence, I am honored to represent the Government of the People's Republic of China and the Chinese people in extending warm congratulations to Your Excellency and the people of your country.
    The Chinese people have always deeply sympathized with Tunisia's struggle for freedom and independence and are delighted with the victory achieved by your people's long-term struggle.
    We sincerely hope that the Tunisian people will achieve new successes in safeguarding national sovereignty and building a happy life, and we are willing to see the development of friendly and cooperative relations between our two countries.
                      Zhou Enlai, Premier of the State Council of the People's Republic of China
                                          April 4, 1956, Beijing

### 周恩来总理电贺突尼斯独立

1956-04-05
第2版()
专栏：

    周恩来总理电贺突尼斯独立
    新华社4日讯　周恩来总理四日打电报给突尼斯首相塔哈尔·阿玛尔，祝贺突尼斯的独立。电文如下：塔哈尔·阿玛尔首相阁下：
    值此突尼斯获得独立之际，我荣幸地代表中华人民共和国政府和中国人民向阁下和贵国人民表示热烈的祝贺。
    中国人民对突尼斯为争取自由独立而进行的斗争一向寄予深切的同情，对贵国人民长期斗争所取得的胜利感到高兴。
    我们诚挚地希望，突尼斯人民在维护国家主权和建设幸福生活的事业中将获得新的成就，并且愿意看到我们两国之间的友好和合作关系的发展。
                      中华人民共和国国务院总理　周恩来
                                          1956年4月4日于北京
