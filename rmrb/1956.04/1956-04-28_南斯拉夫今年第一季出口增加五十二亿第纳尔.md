Title: Yugoslavia's First Quarter Exports Increase by 5.2 Billion Dinars
Author: 
Date: 1956-04-28
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency: Yugoslavia's exports in the first quarter of this year totaled 19.2 billion dinars (Yugoslav currency), an increase of 5.2 billion dinars compared to the same period last year. Imports totaled nearly 44 billion dinars, slightly lower than the level of the same period last year.

	During this period, Yugoslavia increased its exports of agricultural products, machinery and equipment, and other industrial products. Of the exported goods, 74.7% were shipped to European countries. Italy ranked first in Yugoslavia's exports, followed by the United States, West Germany, and the Soviet Union.

	Yugoslavia's imports of transportation equipment and machinery decreased in the first quarter, while food and consumer goods increased. During the first quarter, Yugoslavia imported the most goods from the United States, followed by Italy, West Germany, and the Soviet Union. Yugoslavia also increased its purchases of goods from countries such as Egypt and Burma.

### 南斯拉夫今年第一季出口增加五十二亿第纳尔

1956-04-28
第4版()
专栏：

    南斯拉夫今年第一季出口增加五十二亿第纳尔
    据新华社讯  南斯拉夫今年第一季度的出口总额达到了一百九十二亿第纳尔（南币名），比去年同期增加了五十二亿第纳尔。进口总额将近达到四百四十亿第纳尔，略低于去年同期的水平。
    在这个期间，南斯拉夫输出的农产品、机器设备等和其他工业品增加了。在出口货物中，有74.7％输往欧洲国家。意大利在南斯拉夫出口中，占第一位，其次是美国、西德和苏联。
    南斯拉夫今年第一季进口的运输工具和机器设备等减少了，食品和消费品增加了。在第一季度中，南斯拉夫从美国进口的货物最多，其次是意大利、西德和苏联。南斯拉夫从埃及、缅甸等国购买的货物也有增加。
