Title: Thai Deputy Minister of Interior Says Thai Citizens Can Trade Directly with China
Author:
Date: 1956-04-28
Page: 4

    Xinhua News Agency, April 27th: According to a report by Agence France-Presse, the Thai Deputy Minister of Interior, Nai Pote Sarasin, said on April 26th that direct trade between Thai citizens and China is now considered legal, as long as the transactions do not include strategic materials.
    Nai Pote said in a statement to the press that if Thailand had enough ships, the government would even allow Thai ships to go to Shanghai and Shantou for trade.
    Xinhua News Agency, April 27th, Bangkok News: A recent issue of the Thai-language "Haid Weekly" published a commentary strongly advocating that Thailand should take India and Burma as examples and trade with China.
    The commentary also advocated that the Thai authorities should expel the potential forces of imperialism in Thailand, stop accepting "U.S. aid," and reduce military and police expenditures in the budget.

### 泰国内政部副部长说  泰公民可直接和中国进行贸易

1956-04-28
第4版()
专栏：

    泰国内政部副部长说
    泰公民可直接和中国进行贸易
    新华社27日讯  据法新社报道，泰国内政部副部长乃炮·是耶暖四月二十六日说，泰国公民同中国进行直接贸易现在被认为是合法的，只要交易里不包括战略物资。
    乃炮在向报界发表谈话的时候说，如果泰国有足够的船只的话，政府甚至会允许泰国船只到上海和汕头去进行贸易。
    新华社27日讯  曼谷消息：最近一期泰文“海德周报”发表一篇评论，竭力主张泰国应以印度和缅甸为榜样，同中国进行贸易。
    评论还主张，泰国当局应驱逐帝国主义在泰国的潜在势力，停止接受“美援”，减少预算中的军警开支。
