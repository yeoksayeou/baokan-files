Title: Pakistani Ambassador to China Says in Hong Kong: China-Pakistan Relations are Very Friendly
Author:
Date: 1956-04-28
Page: 4
Xinhua News Agency, Hong Kong, April 27th. The Ambassador of the Islamic Republic of Pakistan to China, Mr. A.H.B. Ahamed, passed through Hong Kong on the 26th on his way back to his country. He told reporters from Hong Kong newspapers that the relations between China and Pakistan are "very friendly."
    Ahamed spoke of his impressions of China. He said: "In the year that I have served as Ambassador to China, the material conditions in Beijing and other parts of China have greatly improved. This is not only my personal opinion, but also a fact generally acknowledged by everyone." He added: "In China, not only do Muslims have freedom of religious belief, but all religious believers enjoy freedom of religious belief. As I have personally seen, the mosques in Beijing are well maintained."
    Ahamed told reporters that he was returning to his country to accompany Pakistani Prime Minister Ali on his visit to China in mid-May.

### 巴基斯坦驻中国大使在香港说  中巴关系十分友好

1956-04-28
第4版()
专栏：

    巴基斯坦驻中国大使在香港说
    中巴关系十分友好
    新华社香港27日电  巴基斯坦伊斯兰共和国驻中国大使阿哈默德在返国途中于二十六日经过香港。他对香港报纸的记者说，中国和巴基斯坦“两国的关系十分友好”。
    阿哈默德谈到他对中国的印象。他说： “在我任驻中国大使的这一年来，北京和中国其他地方的物质状况又有了很大的改进。这不单是我个人的意见，也是一般人一致公认的事实。”他又说：“在中国，不但回教徒有宗教信仰的自由，所有的宗教徒都享受着宗教信仰的自由。据我亲自看见的，北京的清真寺都保持得很好。”
    阿哈默德对记者表示，他这次回国准备陪同巴基斯坦总理阿里在五月中旬到中国访问。
