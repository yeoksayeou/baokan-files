Title: France and South Vietnam Sign Agreement on Withdrawal of French Troops
Author:
Date: 1956-04-01
Page: 4

　　Xinhua News Agency, Hanoi, March 31st: Western news agencies report that France and the South Vietnamese authorities signed an agreement in Saigon on March 30th, stipulating that France will withdraw its remaining 20,000 expeditionary troops from South Vietnam before June 30th. At the end of the Indochina War, the French expeditionary force numbered 120,000, and troops have been gradually withdrawn since then.
　　According to the agreement, before the withdrawal, the French troops will hand over all military facilities to the South Vietnamese authorities, and all the equipment that the United States "loaned" to France during the Indochina War will also be left behind.
　　The French Federation troops stationed in Indochina were one of the signatories to the Geneva Accords. According to the provisions of the Geneva Accords, the signatories and their successors are responsible for complying with and implementing all the terms and provisions of the agreement, and are responsible for protecting the safety of the International Commission.

### 法国和南越签订撤退法军协定

1956-04-01
第4版()
专栏：

    法国和南越签订撤退法军协定
    新华社河内31日电　据西方通讯社消息：法国和南越当局三月三十日在西贡签订了一项协定，规定法国将在六月三十日以前从南越撤出它的最后两万名远征军。在印度支那战争刚结束的时候，法国远征军曾经有十二万人，后来陆续撤出。
    根据这个协定，法军在撤出前将把全部军事设施移交给南越当局，美国在印度支那战争期间“借给”法国的一切装备也要留下。
    驻印度支那法兰西联邦部队是日内瓦协定的签字一方。按照日内瓦协定的规定，协定的签署人及其职务的继任人都有遵守并实施协定的各项条款和规定的责任，并且负责保护国际委员会的安全。
