Title: Soviet and Mongolian Negotiation Communiqué
Author:
Date: 1956-04-10
Page: 4
Xinhua News Agency report: The Soviet government delegation, headed by Soviet First Deputy Chairman of the Council of Ministers, Mikoyan, arrived in Ulaanbaatar at 10:00 AM on the 8th at the invitation of the government of the Mongolian People's Republic. The Chairman of the Council of Ministers of the Mongolian People's Republic, Tsedenbal, received Mikoyan on the same day and held a banquet in the evening to entertain the Soviet government delegation.
  Government representatives of the Mongolian People's Republic and the Soviet Union held negotiations in Ulaanbaatar on further consolidating economic relations between the two countries. Chairman of the Council of Ministers of the Mongolian People's Republic, Tsedenbal, and Soviet First Deputy Chairman of the Council of Ministers, Mikoyan, signed the Mongolian-Soviet Negotiation Communiqué in Ulaanbaatar on the 8th. The Soviet government delegation left Ulaanbaatar on the 9th to return home.
  The full text of the Mongolian-Soviet Negotiation Communiqué is as follows:
  In accordance with the agreements previously reached between the governments of the Mongolian People's Republic and the Soviet Union, negotiations were recently held in Ulaanbaatar on further developing and consolidating economic relations between the Mongolian People's Republic and the Soviet Union. These negotiations were successfully completed during the visit of Soviet First Deputy Chairman of the Council of Ministers, A.I. Mikoyan, to the Mongolian People's Republic.
  Following the aforementioned negotiations, an agreement has been reached to assist the Mongolian People's Republic in constructing 40,000 square meters of housing in the city of Ulaanbaatar during the period of 1956-1960. The method will involve Soviet organizations completing the design work, providing semi-finished products and materials, sanitary equipment and other equipment, construction machinery and transportation vehicles, and dispatching Soviet experts to assist.
  An agreement was also reached to transfer the Solovyovsk-Bayan-Tumen and Choibalsan-Tamtsag-Bulak railways, along with all service projects, buildings, and narrow-gauge locomotives and vehicles along the lines, to the Soviet-Mongolian "Ulaanbaatar Railway" joint-stock company free of charge. In addition, an agreement was reached to build a milk factory and four flour mills in the Ulaanbaatar area, supply equipment for a new confectionery factory, assist in the construction of Ulaanbaatar's water pipes, sewers, and heating equipment, build six livestock machinery stations and provide technical equipment, and jointly conduct geological exploration work on the territory of the Mongolian People's Republic. Agreements were also reached on a number of other issues related to the further development and strengthening of economic relations between the Mongolian People's Republic and the Soviet Union.
  In order to complete the aforementioned construction projects and purchase equipment, construction machinery, and other materials, the Soviet government is providing the government of the Mongolian People's Republic with long-term loans on favorable terms.

### 苏联和蒙古的谈判公报

1956-04-10
第4版()
专栏：

    苏联和蒙古的谈判公报
    据新华社讯　以苏联部长会议第一副主席米高扬为首的苏联政府代表团，应蒙古人民共和国政府邀请，于八日上午十时到达乌兰巴托。蒙古人民共和国部长会议主席泽登巴尔当日接见了米高扬，并在晚上举行宴会招待苏联政府代表团。
  蒙古人民共和国和苏联两国政府代表在乌兰巴托就进一步巩固两国经济关系问题举行了谈判。蒙古人民共和国部长会议主席泽登巴尔和苏联部长会议第一副主席米高扬八日在乌兰巴托签署了蒙苏谈判公报。苏联政府代表团已于九日离开乌兰巴托返国。
    蒙苏谈判公报全文如下：
    根据蒙古人民共和国政府和苏联政府以前所达成的协议，最近在乌兰巴托就进一步发展和巩固蒙古人民共和国和苏联之间的经济关系问题举行了谈判。这次谈判是在苏联部长会议第一副主席阿·伊·米高扬访问蒙古人民共和国期间顺利地完成的。
    经过上述谈判后已经达成协议，决定在1956—1960年期间帮助蒙古人民共和国在乌兰巴托市建筑面积四万平方公尺的住宅，办法是由苏联机构完成设计工作，提供半制成品和材料、卫生设备和其他设备、建筑机械和运输工具，并且派遣苏联专家协助。
    还达成协议，决定把索洛维约夫斯克—巴扬—第乌门、乔巴山—塔姆查克—布拉克两条铁路连同沿线的全部服务工程、建筑物和窄轨机车车辆无偿地转让给苏蒙“乌兰巴托铁路”股份公司。此外还达成协议，决定在乌兰巴托区建筑一所牛奶厂和四个面粉厂，供应一所新糖果厂的设备，帮助建设乌兰巴托的自来水管、下水道和暖气设备，建筑六个畜牧业机器站并供给技术装备，以及在蒙古人民共和国领土上共同进行地质勘察工作；在有关进一步发展和加强蒙古人民共和国和苏联的经济关系的其他一些问题上也达成了协议。
    为完成上述建筑工程以及购买设备、建筑机械和其他材料，苏联政府以优惠条件向蒙古人民共和国政府提供长期贷款。
