Title: Singapore and Britain Continue Negotiations
Author:
Date: 1956-04-27
Page: 4
Xinhua News Agency, London, April 26th: Negotiations on the future political status of Singapore continued in London on April 25th. British Colonial Secretary Lennox-Boyd suggested at the meeting the establishment of a special committee to handle defense and domestic security issues after Singapore's independence. The meeting adjourned temporarily at the request of the Singapore delegation. Negotiations will resume on April 27th.

According to Reuters, quoting people who attended the meeting, the British proposal "apparently aims to resolve the main issue in the talks -- who should control Singapore's internal security after its independence." These people said that this proposal will prolong the negotiations because the negotiations appear to be on the verge of a crisis over the issue of internal security.

### 新加坡和英国继续举行谈判

1956-04-27
第4版()
专栏：

    新加坡和英国继续举行谈判
    据新华社26日讯  伦敦消息：关于新加坡未来政治地位的谈判四月二十五日继续在伦敦举行。英国殖民大臣伦诺克斯—波伊德在会上建议成立一个特别委员会来处理新加坡独立以后的防务和国内安全问题。会议在新加坡代表团的要求下暂时休会。谈判将在四月二十七日继续举行。
    据路透社引参加会议的人士的话说，英国的建议的“目的显然是要解决会谈中的主要问题——在新加坡独立以后，它的内部安全应由谁控制”。这些人士说，这个建议将使谈判拖长，因为谈判看来有在内部安全问题上陷入危机之势。
