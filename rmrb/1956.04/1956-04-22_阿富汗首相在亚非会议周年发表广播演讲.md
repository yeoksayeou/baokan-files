Title: Afghan Prime Minister Delivers Radio Address on Anniversary of Asian-African Conference
Author:
Date: 1956-04-22
Page: 4
Xinhua News Agency, Kabul, April 21st: Afghan Prime Minister Mohammed Daoud Khan delivered a radio address on April 18th commemorating the anniversary of the Asian-African Conference. In his broadcast, he pointed out: "This historic gathering is an unprecedented and memorable political, social, and cultural event for Asia and Africa." He then reiterated Afghanistan's stance of pursuing a policy of peaceful neutrality and friendly cooperation with all Asian and African nations.

When discussing the significant impact of the Asian-African Conference in opposing and condemning colonialism, Daoud Khan cited the independence achieved by Sudan, Morocco, and Tunisia over the past year, saying: "The Afghan government welcomes these events with sincere joy." He expressed regret over France's current policy in Algeria.

Finally, he hoped that the next conference of Asian-African countries would take practical steps to safeguard their common goals and interests, eliminate differences between countries, create a harmonious atmosphere, and enable cooperation between Asian and African nations.

### 阿富汗首相在亚非会议周年发表广播演讲

1956-04-22
第4版()
专栏：

    阿富汗首相在亚非会议周年发表广播演讲
    新华社21日讯  喀布尔消息：阿富汗首相穆罕默德·达乌德汗四月十八日发表了纪念亚非会议周年的广播演讲。他在广播词中指出：“这次历史性的集会是亚非两洲空前未有的值得纪念的政治、社会和文化上的事件”。接着，他重申阿富汗执行和平中立政策和一切亚非国家友好合作的立场。
    在谈到亚非会议反对和谴责殖民主义的重大影响的时候，达乌德汗列举一年来苏丹、摩洛哥和突尼斯取得独立的事实说：“阿富汗政府以真诚的喜悦欢迎这些事件”，他对法国目前的阿尔及利亚政策表示遗憾。
    最后他希望亚非国家的下一次会议将能采取实际步骤来保障他们的共同目标和利益，将能消除各国之间的分歧创造一种和谐的气氛，使亚非两洲各国进行合作。
