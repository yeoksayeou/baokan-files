Title: U.S. Simplifies Export Procedures to Soviet Union and Eastern European Countries
Author:
Date: 1956-04-28
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, Washington, April 27th: U.S. Secretary of Commerce Sinclair Weeks announced on April 26th the simplification of export procedures for 700 items of "non-strategic goods" to the Soviet Union and Eastern European People's Democratic countries.

According to a Reuters report, under Weeks' announcement, U.S. exporters can now export 700 items of "non-strategic goods" in over 57 commodity categories to the Soviet Union and Eastern European People's Democratic countries based on a new general license, without having to apply to the U.S. government for a separate license for each shipment. Weeks said that this simplification of export procedures "expands opportunities to increase trade."

At the same time, Weeks revealed that in the first quarter of this year, trade between the United States and the Soviet Union and Eastern European countries had a "substantial increase." He said that during this period, the total value of U.S. exports to the Soviet Union was more than $8.78 million, while it was only more than $1.62 million in the fourth quarter of 1955 and only more than $4.96 million in the first quarter of 1955.

### 美国简化对苏联和东欧国家的出口手续

1956-04-28
第4版()
专栏：

    美国简化对苏联和东欧国家的出口手续
    新华社27日讯  华盛顿消息：美国商务部长威克斯四月二十六日宣布简化向苏联和东欧人民民主国家输出的七百项“非战略货物”的出口手续。
    据路透社报道，根据威克斯的宣布，美国出口商现在可以不需要向美国政府申请每一批货物的单独的特许执照，而仅仅根据一项新的一般的执照向苏联和东欧人民民主国家输出五十七种以上的商品类别中的七百项“非战略货物”。威克斯说，这种出口手续的简化，“扩大了增加贸易的机会”。
    同时，据威克斯透露，在今年第一季度，美国同苏联和东欧国家的贸易有了“大量的增加”。他说，在这期间内，美国向苏联出口的货物总值为八百七十八万多美元，而1955年第四季度只有一百六十二万多美元，1955年第一季度也只有四百九十六万多美元。
