Title: Icelandic Foreign Minister Opposes Continued Stationing of US Troops
Author:
Date: 1956-04-06
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, Reykjavik, April 5th: Icelandic Foreign Minister Gudmundsson said in a radio broadcast in Reykjavik on the evening of April 4th that Iceland cannot tolerate foreign troops stationed on its territory when the Atlantic nations are not threatened by aggression. Gudmundsson said that when Iceland joined the Atlantic Pact in 1949, it did not consider the issue of foreign troops being stationed on its soil. He stated that although Iceland later agreed to the stationing of American troops, now in 1956, the threat of war does not exist, and it is not necessary for foreign troops to be stationed in the country.

Gudmundsson added: Trade competition among North Atlantic countries is very fierce, and Iceland has suffered losses as a result of this competition.

### 冰岛外长反对美军继续留驻

1956-04-06
第4版()
专栏：

    冰岛外长反对美军继续留驻
    新华社5日讯　雷克雅未克消息：冰岛外交部长格德门森四月四日晚间在雷克雅未克电台发表广播说，冰岛不能容忍在大西洋国家没有受到侵略威胁的时候让外国军队驻在它的领土上。格德门森说，当冰岛在1949年参加大西洋公约的时候，它并没有考虑外国军队驻在它的国土上的问题。他表示，冰岛虽然在后来同意美国军队驻扎，但是，现在在1956年，战争的威胁并不存在，外国军队没有必要驻在这个国家。
    格德门森又说：北大西洋各国之间的贸易竞争是很残酷的，冰岛由于这种竞争而受到损失。
