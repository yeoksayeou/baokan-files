Title: Burmese Elephant and Peacocks Gifted to China on Display at Beijing Zoo
Author:
Date: 1956-04-27
Page: 1

Xinhua News Agency, April 26th: The elephant and two peacocks presented to China by the Burmese government were officially put on display at the Beijing Zoo this afternoon. This gift of friendship was presented by the Burmese Minister of Agriculture and Forestry to the Chinese agricultural delegation that visited Burma last year.

At a simple ceremony held before the exhibition, Wang Kunlun, Deputy Mayor of Beijing, expressed sincere gratitude to the Burmese government. In his speech, the Burmese Ambassador to China, U Hla Maung, said that the Burmese people believe that the peacock is the most beautiful of birds, and the elephant is the strongest and noblest of beasts. The exhibition of these three animals in this zoo symbolizes the strong friendly relations between the two beautiful countries of Burma and China, and the lofty goal of the two peoples in striving for peace.

### 缅甸赠送给我国的象和孔雀在北京动物园展出

1956-04-27
第1版()
专栏：

    缅甸赠送给我国的象和孔雀在北京动物园展出
    新华社26日讯  缅甸政府赠送给中国的一只象和两只孔雀，今天下午在北京动物园正式展出。这项友谊的礼物是由缅甸农林部部长赠送给去年访问缅甸的中国农业访问团的。
    北京市副市长王昆仑在展出前举行的一个简单的仪式上，向缅甸政府表示真诚的感谢。缅甸驻中国大使吴拉茂在讲话中说，缅甸人民相信鸟类中最美丽的是孔雀，兽类中最强大、高尚的是象。在这个动物园中展出这三个动物，象征着缅甸和中国两个美丽的国家之间坚强的友好关系和两国人民争取和平的崇高目标。
