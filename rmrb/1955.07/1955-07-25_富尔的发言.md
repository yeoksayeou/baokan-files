Title: Faure's Speech
Author:
Date: 1955-07-25
Page: 4

    Faure's Speech
    Our meeting is coming to an end. But we must not separate here. What I mean is that if our four bodies are no longer in this hall, we must remain united in spirit under a single will.
    Please allow me to think that, in addition to the agreement we have reached among ourselves on certain issues, certain documents, and certain instructions, the very fact that we have met, the spirit that runs through our debates, and the mutual understanding that has arisen from these debates, will leave a profound mark on international relations and will have a positive impact on their development.
    We have shown here our common determination. Now we must find a way. The first step has been taken along this path, but obstacles will still occur. We have not tried to hide them, because all progress can only be achieved through truth.
    If life is based on tension, if life is often propelled by a force, if this is true, then let this tension and this force be understanding and friendship, rather than hostility and distrust. To those who are watching us, and not only to those who trust us, we must be able to suggest gradually replacing the still needed security tasks with constructive and beneficial peaceful tasks. (Xinhua News Agency)

### 富尔的发言

1955-07-25
第4版()
专栏：

    富尔的发言
    我们的会议就要结束了。但是我们一定不要就此分开。我的意思是说，如果我们四个人的身体不再在这个大厅里的话，我们必须在精神上仍然团结在一个单一的意志下。
    请允许我这样认为，除了我们之间就某些问题、某些文件以及某些指示达成了协议以外，我们开会这一事实本身，贯串着我们的辩论的精神和从这些辩论所产生的相互谅解，将在国际关系上留下深刻的标志并且将对它们的发展起良好的影响。
    我们在这里显示了我们共同的决心。现在我们必须找出办法。已经沿着这条道路采取了第一个步骤，但是障碍仍然会发生。我们并没有企图掩盖它们，因为一切进展是通过真理才能取得的。
    如果说生活以紧张为基础，如果说生活常常是由一种力量来推进的，如果真是这样的话，那末让这种紧张和这种力量是谅解和友谊，而不再是敌对和不信任吧。对于那些正在注视着我们的人们，而不仅是对于那些信托我们的人们，我们必须能够建议逐步以建设性的和有益的和平任务来代替仍然需要的安全任务。　（新华社）
