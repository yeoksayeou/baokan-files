Title: Academician Dubinin of the Presidium of the USSR Academy of Sciences Speaks to Reporters: The Soviet Union Has Made Great Achievements in the Peaceful Use of Atomic Energy; Foreign Scientists Visit Scientific Institutions in Moscow and Talk with Soviet Experts
Author:
Date: 1955-07-11
Page: 4
Xinhua News Agency, Moscow, July 9th: TASS reports that Academician Dubinin of the Presidium of the USSR Academy of Sciences spoke to a "Red Star" reporter on the issue of the peaceful use of atomic energy. He said: Among the scientific and technical problems solved to date, none has had such a great impact on human progress as the liberation of the energy of the atomic nucleus and the use of this energy to benefit mankind. He pointed out that the recently concluded conference of the USSR Academy of Sciences on the peaceful use of atomic energy demonstrated the great achievements of scientific thought in this regard.

Soviet science and technology have accumulated experience in the use of atomic fuel. The world's first atomic power plant has been supplying electricity for industrial use for a year. Scientists and engineers are now working on building more economical atomic power plants. Industrial methods for the production of heavy water and methods for the fine selection of uranium have been discovered. It is therefore possible to manufacture uranium-water reactors. Other atomic boiler systems are also being studied. The nuclear energy produced by Soviet reactors opens up unprecedented prospects for science, technology, and the prosperity and continuous development of the entire national economy.

Dubinin said that Soviet science has studied particularly carefully the changes that occur when elements such as thorium, uranium, bismuth, silver, and tungsten are bombarded by various particles at energies below 700 million electron volts. Hundreds of radioactive isotopes have been discovered in this situation; Soviet scientists have found many new isotopes among them that have never been discovered scientifically. Soviet scientists have discovered many compounds of hafnium with other metals in their work using stable and radioactive isotopes to study the structure of compounds.

Dubinin said that the Soviet Technical Science Department is successfully solving the problem of using radiation and isotopes in industry. The study of metallurgical processes and the study of metallography and metal physics have shown that there are wide possibilities for the industrial use of radioactive isotopes. As a result of the research on the utilization of gamma rays, an electrolytic flaw detection method has been invented, which can be used directly in factories to find flaws in metal products. The atomic energy electromagnetic wave method used by the Soviet oil industry in the exploration of oil wells and natural gas wells also has great prospects in the work of prospecting for mineral deposits.

The use of radioactive isotopes in medicine, biology, and agriculture has great practical and theoretical significance.

Dubinin concluded by saying that Soviet scientists are very happy to introduce the achievements in the research work on the peaceful use of atomic energy to the foreign guests who participated in the conference of the USSR Academy of Sciences on the peaceful use of atomic energy and to absorb the experience of scientists from other countries. He pointed out that this will lay the foundation for international cooperation among scientists in order to jointly study the ways in which atomic energy will be used to serve all mankind in the future.

Xinhua News Agency reports that scientists from China, Czechoslovakia, Bulgaria, India, Burma, Yugoslavia, Japan, Norway, the Netherlands, and other countries who participated in the conference of the USSR Academy of Sciences on the peaceful use of atomic energy are currently visiting scientific institutions in Moscow that are engaged in research on the peaceful use of atomic energy and are talking with Soviet experts in this field.

Foreign scientists visited many institutes of the USSR Academy of Sciences in groups according to their respective specialties. One group visited the Institute of Geochemistry and Analytical Chemistry. The institute's staff demonstrated to them the methods of using the latest Soviet instruments for highly accurate radiochemical research. Professor Baranov also introduced to them the methods and techniques for determining the half-life of radioactive isotopes. One of the world's largest synchrocyclotrons of the USSR Academy of Sciences was used in conducting this research.

Foreign scientists also visited one of Moscow's largest biological research institutions, the Institute of Microbiology. Soviet scientist Professor Messel introduced to the guests the work being done using radioactive isotopes and other new methods in research and conducted experiments on the spot.

### 苏联科学院主席团委员杜比宁院士向记者发表谈话  苏联在和平利用原子能方面有巨大成就  外国科学家在莫斯科参观科学机关并同苏联专家谈话

1955-07-11
第4版()
专栏：

    苏联科学院主席团委员杜比宁院士向记者发表谈话
    苏联在和平利用原子能方面有巨大成就
    外国科学家在莫斯科参观科学机关并同苏联专家谈话
    新华社十日讯　据塔斯社莫斯科九日讯：苏联科学院主席团委员杜比宁院士就和平利用原子能问题对“红星报”记者发表了谈话。他说：到现在为止已经解决的科学和技术问题之中，没有一个问题能像解放原子核的能量和把这种能量用来造福人群那样对人类的进步产生巨大的影响。他指出，苏联科学院最近结束的讨论和平使用原子能的会议展示了科学思想在这方面的巨大成就。
    苏联科学和技术已经累积了使用原子燃料的经验。世界上第一座原子能电力站供给工业用电已经一年了。科学家和工程师现在正在从事建设更为经济的原子能发电站。生产重水的工业方法和精选铀的方法已经发现了。因而就有可能制造出铀—水反应器。其他原子锅炉系统也正研究中。苏联的反应器生产的核子能为科学、技术和整个国民经济的繁荣和不断发展打开了空前的远景。
    杜比宁说：苏联科学特别仔细地研究了钍、铀、铋、银、钨等元素在各种粒子以七亿电子伏特以下的能量冲击时所发生的变化。在这种情况下发现了几百种放射性同位素；苏联科学家在其中找到了科学上从没有发现过的许多新的同位素。苏联科学家在使用稳定的和放射性的同位素来研究化合物的结构工作中，发现了鉟同其他金属的许多化合物。
    杜比宁说，苏联技术科学部成功地解决着在工业中使用放射线和同位素的问题。冶金过程的研究以及金相学和金属物理学的研究表明，在工业方面使用放射性同位素的方法是有广泛可能性的。对丙种射线的利用的研究工作发展的结果，发明了一种电解疵病检查法，利用这种方法，在工厂中就可直接找出金属制品中的疵病。苏联石油工业在探测石油井和天然煤气井时利用的原子能电磁波法，在勘探矿藏的工作中也具有很大的前途。
    在医学、生物学和农业中使用放射性同位素，有着巨大的实践和理论意义。
    杜比宁最后说，苏联科学家很乐意地向参加苏联科学院和平利用原子能问题会议的外国来宾介绍在和平利用原子能的研究工作方面的成绩，并吸收其他国家科学家的经验。他指出，这将为科学家们的国际合作奠定基础，以便共同研究今后将原子能用来为全人类服务的道路。
    据新华社讯　参加苏联科学院和平利用原子能问题会议的中国、捷克斯洛伐克、保加利亚、印度、缅甸、南斯拉夫、日本、挪威、荷兰等国科学家，现正在莫斯科参观苏联从事和平利用原子能的研究的科学机关，并且和苏联这方面的专家谈话。
    外国科学家们按照各自的专业，分组访问了苏联科学院的许多研究所。其中一组访问了地质化学和分析化学研究所。研究所工作人员向他们演习了使用苏联最新仪器进行极精确的放射化学研究工作的方法。巴拉诺夫教授还向他们介绍了测定放射性同位素半衰期时间的方法和测定技术等。在进行这种研究时，使用了苏联科学院的一座世界上最大的同步回旋加速器。
    外国科学家们又参观了莫斯科最大的生物学研究机构之一——微生物学研究所。苏联科学家梅塞尔教授向来宾们介绍了在研究中使用放射性同位素和其他新方法的工作情况，并且当场作了试验。
