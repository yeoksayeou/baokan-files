Title: People in Vietnam, Burma and other countries enthusiastically sign against atomic weapons. Many Nobel Prize winners call for abandoning the use of weapons of mass destruction.
Author:
Date: 1955-07-19
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, Hanoi, news: As of July 7, 6,354,048 people in North Vietnam had signed the World Peace Council's "Appeal to the People of the World" against atomic weapons. At present, the signature campaign has basically ended, and individual regions are still in progress.

Xinhua News Agency, July 17th, news: Rangoon dispatch: The Burmese Committee for the Defense of World Peace issued a statement on the 12th, calling on the Burmese people to actively participate in the signature campaign to ban atomic weapons. The statement pointed out that the preparation for atomic war poses a great threat to Southeast Asian countries, including Burma.

According to Burmese newspapers, the peace committees in various parts of Burma have recently been going deep into the masses to carry out a signature campaign to ban atomic weapons. The Mandalay Peace Committee launched a signature campaign on the 5th and collected 3,700 signatures that day. The signature campaign in Wundwin, Myitkyina and other places had also started at the end of June.

Xinhua News Agency, news: Melbourne dispatch: According to a recent report in the Australian weekly "Guardian", the Australian National Peace Council announced on June 29 that more than 150,000 people in Australia had signed the World Peace Council Standing Committee's "Appeal to the People of the World."

Xinhua News Agency, July 16th, news: According to TASS, Berlin, July 16th: Nobel Prize winners attending the fifth annual meeting on Mainau Island, West Germany, issued a peace appeal to all countries. They demanded that all governments abandon the use of weapons of mass destruction.

Eighteen people, including Kurt Alder (West Germany), Arthur Compton (USA), Hans von Euler-Chelpin (Sweden), Paul Müller (Switzerland), Frederick Soddy (UK), and Hideki Yukawa (Japan), signed this appeal.

### 越南缅甸等国人民踊跃签名反对原子武器  诺贝尔奖金的许多得奖人呼吁放弃使用大规模毁灭性武器

1955-07-19
第4版()
专栏：

    越南缅甸等国人民踊跃签名反对原子武器
    诺贝尔奖金的许多得奖人呼吁放弃使用大规模毁灭性武器
    新华社河内电　到七月七日为止，越南北方已经有六百三十五万四千零四十八人在世界和平理事会的“告全世界人民书”上签名，反对原子武器。目前，签名运动基本上已经结束，有个别地区还在进行中。
    新华社十七日讯　仰光消息：缅甸保卫世界和平委员会十二日发表声明，呼吁缅甸人民踊跃参加禁止原子武器的签名运动。声明指出，原子战争的准备给东南亚国家，包括缅甸在内，带来了很大的威胁。
    据缅甸报纸报道，缅甸各地的和平委员会最近正深入群众，开展禁止原子武器的签名运动。曼德勒和平委员会在五日发动了签名运动，并且在当天就征集了三千七百个人的签名。温都、密艺等地也已经在六月底展开了签名运动。
    新华社讯　墨尔本消息：据澳大利亚周报“卫报”最近报道，澳大利亚全国和平理事会六月二十九日宣布，澳大利亚在世界和平理事会常务委员会的“告全世界人民书”上签名的已经超过了十五万人。
    新华社十六日讯　据塔斯社柏林十六日讯：在西德梅诺岛举行第五次年会的诺贝尔奖金得奖人们发表了一项致所有国家的和平呼吁书。他们要求各国政府放弃使用大规模毁灭性武器。
    在这项呼吁书上签名的有库尔特·艾德尔（西德）、阿瑟·康普敦（美国）、汉斯·冯·艾勒·谢尔平（瑞典）、普·缪勒（瑞士）、弗雷德里希·索迪（英国）、汤川秀树（日本）等十八人。
