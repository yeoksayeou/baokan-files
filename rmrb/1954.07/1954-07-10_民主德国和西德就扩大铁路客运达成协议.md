Title: East Germany and West Germany Reach Agreement on Expanding Railway Passenger Transport
Author: 
Date: 1954-07-10
Page: 4

    East Germany and West Germany Reach Agreement on Expanding Railway Passenger Transport
    [Xinhua News Agency, Berlin, July 9] TASS Berlin, July 8: Berlin's democratic newspapers published a communique from the Railway Transport Department of the German Democratic Republic, which stated: Recently, representatives of the railway transport departments of the German Democratic Republic and West Germany held negotiations. As a result of the negotiations, an agreement was reached to further expand railway passenger transport between the German Democratic Republic and West Germany. According to this agreement, starting from July 15, 1954, one to two passenger trains will operate daily on the following lines: Berlin-Hamburg, Berlin-Cologne, Berlin-Frankfurt, Berlin-Munich, Leipzig-Cologne, and Leipzig-Munich.

### 民主德国和西德就扩大铁路客运达成协议

1954-07-10
第4版()
专栏：

    民主德国和西德就扩大铁路客运达成协议
    【新华社九日讯】塔斯社柏林八日讯：柏林各民主报刊刊登了德意志民主共和国铁路运输部的公报，公报说道：最近德意志民主共和国和西德的铁路运输代表之间举行了谈判。谈判的结果达成了一项协议，决定进一步扩大德意志民主共和国和西德之间的铁路客运。按照这项协议，从一九五四年七月十五日起，柏林——汉堡、柏林——科隆、柏林——法兰克福、柏林——慕尼黑、来比锡——科隆、来比锡——慕尼黑等线每天开客车一班到两班。
