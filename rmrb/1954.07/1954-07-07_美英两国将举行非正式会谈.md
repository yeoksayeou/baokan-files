Title: US and Britain to Hold Informal Talks

Author: 
Date: 1954-07-07
Page: 4

    US and Britain to Hold Informal Talks
    To Study Plans for Establishing a Southeast Asia Military Alliance
    [Xinhua News Agency, New York, 5th] TASS, New York, 5th dispatch: The "New York Herald Tribune" correspondent in Washington, Russell, reported that the United States and Britain intend to hold informal talks this week to study the plan for establishing a Southeast Asia military alliance based on the agreement reached by Eisenhower and Churchill last week. The date of the start of the Anglo-American talks has not yet been determined.
    Russell wrote that the talks will discuss the following issues: 1. Which countries should participate in this convention; 2. How to select these countries and who should invite them to participate in the convention; 3. What obligations should the countries participating in the alliance bear.

### 美英两国将举行非正式会谈

1954-07-07
第4版()
专栏：

    美英两国将举行非正式会谈
    研究建立东南亚军事同盟计划
    【新华社六日讯】塔斯社纽约五日讯：“纽约先驱论坛报”驻华盛顿记者鲁塞尔报道，美国和英国打算在本周内做非正式的会谈，研究根据艾森豪威尔与邱吉尔上周所达成的协议建立东南亚军事同盟的计划。英美会谈开始的日期目前还没有确定。
    鲁塞尔写道，会谈将讨论下列问题：一、哪些国家应当参加这个公约；二、如何选择这些国家并且应当由谁去邀请它们参加公约；三、参加同盟的国家应负担何种义务。
