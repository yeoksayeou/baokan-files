Title: Outstanding Indian Public Leader Sokhey Receives Stalin International Peace Prize in Moscow
Author:
Date: 1954-07-16
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, Moscow, July 15th: According to TASS, Professor Sahib Singh Sokhey, an outstanding Indian public leader, received the "Strengthening International Peace" Stalin International Prize at the Sverdlov Hall of the Kremlin on July 14th. Many Soviet public representatives attended the award ceremony. Also attending the ceremony were K.P.S. Menon, Ambassador Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary of the Republic of India to the Soviet Union, staff members of the embassy, a delegation of Indian scientists currently visiting the Soviet Union, and writers from China, Romania, and the German Democratic Republic who came to Moscow to participate in the 50th anniversary of the death of the great Russian writer Anton Chekhov.

Academician Dmitri Skobeltsyn, Chairman of the Stalin International Prize "For Strengthening International Peace" Committee, awarded Professor Sokhey the gold medal and certificate, and warmly congratulated him on receiving this lofty award.

Academician Skobeltsyn said that India's contribution to seeking peace in Asia and the world, and its efforts to peacefully resolve the Korean War and the Indochina War, have been highly praised by the world's public. The participation of outstanding Indian men and women in various international peace conferences is very beneficial. Professor Sahib Singh Sokhey, a world-renowned scientist, is one of these distinguished representatives of the Indian people.

Academician Skobeltsyn said, we pay tribute to the great Indian people through Professor Sokhey. May today's award ceremony become another link between the great people of India and the Soviet Union, whose friendship is developing and growing stronger.

Professor Sokhey said in his reply that it is a great honor for Indian citizens to be awarded the "Strengthening International Peace" Stalin International Prize for two consecutive years. Giving him this lofty award is an award to India. He said that India, under the leadership of Prime Minister Nehru, has consistently strived for peace. The people of all countries must have solid and lasting peace so that they can freely develop their resources to improve their living and cultural conditions.

Finally, he expressed his desire to work even more diligently for this noble cause of peace, which is so precious to all mankind.

### 杰出的印度公众领袖  索克在莫斯科接受斯大林国际和平奖金

1954-07-16
第4版()
专栏：

    杰出的印度公众领袖
    索克在莫斯科接受斯大林国际和平奖金
   【新华社十五日讯】据塔斯社莫斯科十四日讯：杰出的印度公众领袖萨希布·辛格·索克教授七月十四日在克里姆林宫斯维德洛夫大厅接受“加强国际和平”斯大林国际奖金。参加授奖典礼的有许多苏联公众代表。参加典礼的还有印度共和国驻苏联特命全权大使克·普·斯·梅农和大使馆的工作人员、目前正在苏联访问的印度科学家代表团和来到莫斯科参加伟大的俄国作家安东·契诃夫逝世五十周年纪念会的中国、罗马尼亚和德意志民主共和国的作家。
    “加强国际和平”斯大林国际奖金委员会主席德米特里·斯科贝尔琴院士以金质奖章和奖状授予索克教授并热烈地祝贺他荣获这个崇高的奖励。
    斯科贝尔琴院士说，印度对谋求亚洲和世界和平的贡献，它为和平解决朝鲜战争和印度支那战争而作的努力得到了全世界公众很高的评价。杰出的印度男女参加各种国际和平会议是非常有益的。世界闻名的科学家萨希布·辛格·索克教授就是这些著名的印度人民代表中的一个。
    斯科贝尔琴院士说，我们通过索克教授向伟大的印度人民致敬。愿今天的授奖典礼能成为友谊正在发展和壮大中的印苏两国伟大人民之间的又一个联系。
    索克教授致答词说，印度公民接连两年被授予
  “加强国际和平”斯大林国际奖金，这是很大的光荣。给他以这个崇高的奖励，就是对印度的奖励。他说，印度在尼赫鲁总理的领导下一贯为争取和平而努力。各国人民必须有巩固和持久的和平，使他们能自由地开发他们的资源，来改善他们的生活和文化条件。
    最后他表示要更加孜孜不倦地为全人类如此珍贵的这个崇高的和平事业而努力。
