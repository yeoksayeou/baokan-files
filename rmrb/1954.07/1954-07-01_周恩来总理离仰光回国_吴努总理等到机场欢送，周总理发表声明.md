Title: Premier Zhou Enlai Departs Rangoon for Home; Premier U Nu and Others See Him Off at the Airport; Premier Zhou Issues Statement
Author:
Date: 1954-07-01
Page: 1

Xinhua News Agency, Rangoon, June 30th: Zhou Enlai, Premier of the State Council and Minister of Foreign Affairs of the Central People's Government of the People's Republic of China, left Rangoon by plane at 8:30 PM on the 29th to return home.

Premier U Nu of Burma and Acting Minister of Foreign Affairs U Khaw Gyaing were at the airport to see him off.

Also at the airport to see him off were Burmese Minister of Commerce Thakin Tha Khin and other high-ranking officials from various Burmese government ministries.

Diplomatic envoys from various countries to Burma were also at the airport to see him off: Pakistani Ambassador Sultanuddin Ahmed, Indian Ambassador K.K. Chettur, Ceylon Minister V. Comaraswamy, Israeli Minister David Hacohen, Soviet Chargé d'affaires Semyon P. Dukaryev, Indonesian Chargé d'affaires T.M. Habsjah, Dutch Chargé d'affaires Joseph Welsing, Nepalese Acting Consul D.B. Basnyat, and Representative of the Democratic Republic of Vietnam Tran Van Luan.

Yao Zhongming, Ambassador of the People's Republic of China to Burma, and embassy staff were also at the airport to see him off.

Premier Zhou Enlai reviewed the Burmese honor guard at the airport. During the review, the band played the national anthems of the People's Republic of China and Burma.

Premier Zhou Enlai issued the following statement upon leaving Rangoon:

"As I am about to leave Rangoon, please allow me to express my sincere gratitude to the Burmese government and people for their warm welcome and hospitality. The Sino-Burmese talks over the past two days have been fruitful. The Joint Statement of the Prime Ministers of China and Burma, which is about to be published, is a concrete manifestation of this achievement. I am deeply convinced that the mutual understanding and friendly spirit shown by both sides in this meeting will help strengthen the relations between China and Burma and consolidate peace in Asia and the world."

As Premier Zhou Enlai boarded the plane and took off, he was warmly cheered by thousands of Burmese people and overseas Chinese who were seeing him off outside the airport.

### 周恩来总理离仰光回国  吴努总理等到机场欢送，周总理发表声明

1954-07-01
第1版()
专栏：

    周恩来总理离仰光回国
    吴努总理等到机场欢送，周总理发表声明
    【新华社仰光三十日电】中华人民共和国中央人民政府政务院总理兼外交部部长周恩来在二十九日下午八时三十分乘飞机离仰光回国。
    缅甸总理吴努和外交部代理部长吴觉迎到机场欢送。
    到机场欢送的还有缅甸商业部部长德钦达庆和缅甸政府各部部长等高级官员。
    到机场欢送的还有各国驻缅甸的外交使节：巴基斯坦大使苏丹努丁·艾哈迈德，印度大使克·克·契图尔，锡兰公使维卢皮莱·科马拉斯瓦米，以色列公使戴维·哈科汉，苏联代办谢米昂·普·久卡列夫，印尼代办特·麦·哈布斯雅，荷兰代办约瑟夫·韦尔辛，尼泊尔代理领事德·布·巴斯尼雅特，越南民主共和国代表陈文伦。
    到机场欢送的还有中华人民共和国驻缅甸大使姚仲明和使馆人员。
    周恩来总理在机场检阅了缅甸仪仗队。检阅时，乐队奏起了中华人民共和国国歌和缅甸国歌。
    周恩来总理在离开仰光时发表声明如下：
    “在我行将离开仰光之际，请允许我对缅甸政府和人民的热烈欢迎和款待表示衷心的感谢。这两天来的中缅会谈是有成就的。即将公布的中缅两国总理联合声明就是这一成就的具体表现。我深信，在这次会谈中双方所表现的相互谅解和友好精神，必将有助于加强中缅两国的邦交并巩固亚洲及世界的和平。”
    周恩来总理登机起飞时，受到在机场外面欢送的成千缅甸人民和华侨的热烈欢呼。
