Title: Meeting of the Council of Ministers of the Democratic Republic of Vietnam Held: New Tasks Formulated for Achieving Ceasefire and Consolidating Peace; Eight Policies for Newly Liberated Cities Announced
Author:
Date: 1954-07-24
Page: 1

Xinhua News Agency, July 23rd: Vietnam News Agency, North Vietnam, July 23rd: The full text of the communique of the meeting of the Council of Ministers of the Democratic Republic of Vietnam on July 22nd is as follows:

The Council of Ministers of the Democratic Republic of Vietnam, having learned that the Geneva Conference had concluded with the signing of agreements on the cessation of hostilities in Vietnam, Laos, and Cambodia, held a meeting on July 22nd, 1954, under the chairmanship of President Ho Chi Minh.

The Council of Ministers heard a report from the delegation of the Democratic Republic of Vietnam attending the Geneva Conference on the results of the conference.

The Council of Ministers studied this new situation and formulated new tasks for all aspects of activity in order to achieve a ceasefire throughout the country, win and consolidate peace, and achieve unification, independence, and democracy.

President Ho Chi Minh, on behalf of the Government of the Democratic Republic of Vietnam, issued a message to the people, army, and cadres of Vietnam.

Xinhua News Agency, July 22nd: Vietnam News Agency, North Vietnam, July 22nd: The eight policies of the Government of the Democratic Republic of Vietnam for newly liberated cities:

	I. The Government of the Democratic Republic of Vietnam protects the lives and property of all residents of newly liberated cities.

	II. The government protects industry and commerce. All private factories, companies, banks, and shops are protected. Individuals are allowed to engage in private production and trade activities.

	III. The government will take over and manage industries and public institutions formerly managed by the colonial regime and the puppet regime.

	IV. The government protects schools, hospitals, cultural and educational institutions, stadiums, cinemas, theaters, and other public buildings.

	V. All civil servants who originally served in the puppet regime, including police and civil servants in various departments of these cities, will continue to hold their original positions and continue to work.

	VI. In order to ensure urban security and restore social order, all remnants of the French army and puppet army still hidden in these cities must automatically report to the People's Government.

	VII. The Government of the Democratic Republic of Vietnam protects the lives and property of foreigners (including French nationals).

	VIII. The government protects freedom of belief for all religious denominations. Churches, temples, pagodas, and properties of all religious denominations are protected, and the safety of religious believers is guaranteed.

### 越南民主共和国部长会议举行会议  拟定实现停战巩固和平的新任务  公布对新解放城市的八项政策

1954-07-24
第1版()
专栏：

    越南民主共和国部长会议举行会议
    拟定实现停战巩固和平的新任务
    公布对新解放城市的八项政策
    【新华社二十三日讯】越南通讯社北越二十三日讯：越南民主共和国部长会议七月二十二日会议的公报全文如下：
    越南民主共和国部长会议在得悉日内瓦会议已在签订了关于在越南、老挝和柬埔寨停止敌对行动的协定而结束后，一九五四年七月二十二日在胡志明主席主持下举行了会议。
    部长会议听取了来自出席日内瓦会议的越南民主共和国代表团的关于会议结果的报告。
    部长会议研究了这种新的局势，并拟定了各方面活动的新任务，以便在全国各地实现停战，赢取与巩固和平，并获得统一、独立和民主。
    胡志明主席代表越南民主共和国政府向越南全国人民、军队和干部发表了一篇文告。
    【新华社二十二日讯】越南通讯社北越二十二日讯：越南民主共和国政府对新解放城市的八项政策：
    一、越南民主共和国政府保护新解放城市所有居民的生命和财产。
    二、政府保护工商业。所有私人的工厂、公司、银行和商店都受保护。允许个人进行私人生产和贸易活动。
    三、政府将接收并管理原来由殖民政权和傀儡政权经管的产业和公共机关。
    四、政府保护学校、医院、文化教育机关、体育场、电影院、剧院和其它公共建筑物。
    五、所有原在傀儡政权中服务的文职人员，包括警察和这些城市各部门的文职人员在内，继续保持他们原来的职位，并继续工作。
    六、为保证城市安全并恢复社会秩序，一切仍然隐藏在这些城市中的法军和傀儡军残余必须自动向人民政府报到。
    七、越南民主共和国政府保护外侨（包括法国侨民在内）的生命和财产。
    八、政府保护各种宗教派别的信仰自由。一切宗教派别的教堂、庙宇、宝塔和财产受到保护，宗教信徒的安全得到保障。
