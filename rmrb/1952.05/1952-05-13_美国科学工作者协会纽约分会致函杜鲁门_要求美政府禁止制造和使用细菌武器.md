Title: New York Branch of the American Federation of Scientists Sends Letter to Truman Requesting U.S. Government Ban the Manufacture and Use of Bacteriological Weapons
Author:
Date: 1952-05-13
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, New York, 11th Dispatch from TASS: The New York Branch of the American Federation of Scientists recently sent a copy of its letter to Truman to the Soviet Representative to the United Nations, Malik. The letter is dated May 6th of this year and is signed by Grundfest, Chairman of the New York Branch of the American Federation of Scientists.

The letter states: "Dear Mr. President: I am writing to you on behalf of the Executive Committee of the New York Branch of the American Federation of Scientists to express the association's long-standing concern over the issue of bacteriological warfare. Our concern stems from the fact that between 1940 and 1941, the American Federation of Scientists, in order to counter the threat of Nazi and fascist barbarism, mobilized the then-underutilized skills of bacteriologists to contribute to our national defense. Thus, a national defense research group consisting of approximately 300 bacteriologists was established in New York. After discussions, this group produced an important report on the possibility of bacteriological warfare, entitled the 'Rosebury-Kabat Report.' This report, aimed at defending against possible bacteriological warfare by Germany and Japan, enumerated the principles and possibilities of bacteriological weapons in a highly creative and rational manner, perhaps the single most important contribution on this issue.

After the war, the contents of the Rosebury-Kabat Report were made public. At that time, the American Federation of Scientists informed the public of the seriousness of biological weapons of mass extermination and emphasized that, since it is impossible to control and supervise such weapons, it is all the more important to prohibit them and punish the use of such weapons with collective measures."

The letter goes on to point out that the American Federation of Scientists had "submitted a memorandum on the issue of bacteriological warfare to the United Nations General Assembly. In this memorandum, we presented the reasons for declaring bacteriological weapons illegal and attached a bibliography of books on bacteriological warfare, which was the most complete at the time. The memorandum concluded that 'for the sake of world peace, this major weapon of mass destruction should be eliminated.'

On November 16, 1949, the New York Branch of the American Federation of Scientists once again petitioned the United Nations General Assembly to declare biological weapons and other weapons of mass human extermination illegal. The arguments in these two memoranda are now generally recognized as well-founded."

The letter continued: "However, the issue is even more urgent today. Accusations have been made and widely publicized that the American armed forces have used bacteriological weapons in Korea and China."

The letter concludes by pointing out that the United States has not acceded to the 1925 Geneva Protocol and therefore urges Truman to "instruct the U.S. delegation to the United Nations to recommend an unconditional ban on the manufacture, storage, and use of bacteriological weapons, followed by the adoption of measures for collective action against violators."

### 美国科学工作者协会纽约分会致函杜鲁门  要求美政府禁止制造和使用细菌武器

1952-05-13
第4版()
专栏：

    美国科学工作者协会纽约分会致函杜鲁门
    要求美政府禁止制造和使用细菌武器
    【新华社十二日讯】塔斯社纽约十一日讯：美国科学工作者协会纽约分会近将该会致杜鲁门函的副本一份，送给苏联驻联合国代表马立克。这封信的日期是本年五月六日，署名的是美国科学工作者协会纽约分会主席格伦德法斯特。
    这封信说：“亲爱的总统先生：我奉美国科学工作者协会纽约分会执行委员会之命，写这封信给你，表示本会长期以来一直对细菌战问题感到焦虑。我们之所以感到焦虑，是因为在一九四○年和一九四一年间，美国科学工作者协会曾为了对付纳粹和法西斯野蛮主义的威胁，使科学家都为我们的国防尽最大的贡献，而动员了当时还没有发挥力量的细菌学家的技术为国防事业尽力。这样，一个包括了大约有三百多名细菌学家的国防研究小组便在纽约成立了。这个小组经过讨论之后，曾就细菌战的可能性制订了一个重要的报告，名为鲁斯贝利—卡巴特报告。这个报告以防御德国和日本可能进行细菌战为目的，以极具创造性和合理的形式，一一举出了细菌武器的原则和可能，这也许是在这个问题上独一无二的最重要的贡献。
    战后，鲁斯贝利—卡巴特报告的内容公诸于世，当时美国科学工作者协会便向大众说明大规模绝灭人类的生物武器的严重性，并着重指出，由于对这种武器不可能实行管制和监督，禁止这种武器并以集体措施来惩罚使用这种武器的行为，就愈加重要了。”
    这封信接着指出，美国科学工作者协会曾“就细菌战问题向联合国大会提出过一个备忘录。我们在这个备忘录里提出了宣布细菌武器为非法的理由，并附有一个关于细菌战的书籍文件索引，在当时这算是最完备的了。备忘录的结论认为，‘为了世界和平，应该消灭这种大规模毁灭的主要武器’。
    一九四九年十一月十六日，美国科学工作者协会纽约分会再一次向联合国大会请愿，要求将生物武器和其他大规模绝灭人类的武器宣布为非法。这两个备忘录的论据的理由之充足，现在是人所公认的。”
    该信继续说：“然而，这问题在今天更为迫切了。别人已经指责美国武装部队在朝鲜和中国使用了细菌武器，并且广泛地予以广播宣传。”
    这封信最后指出美国没有参加一九二五年的日内瓦议定书，因此促请杜鲁门“训令美国驻联合国代表团建议无条件地禁止细菌武器的制造、存储和使用，并随后通过对违约者采取集体行动的措施。”
