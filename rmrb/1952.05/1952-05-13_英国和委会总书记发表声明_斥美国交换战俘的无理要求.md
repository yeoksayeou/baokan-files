Title: British Peace Committee Secretary-General Issues Statement Condemning Unreasonable U.S. Demand for POW Exchange
Author:
Date: 1952-05-13
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, London, May 10th: TASS, London, May 9th: Jones, Secretary-General of the British Peace Committee, issued a statement emphasizing that for world peace, the stalemate in the Korean armistice negotiations must be broken to find a solution.

Jones said: Peace has never been achieved through ultimatums.

Koreans and Chinese are being asked to agree to conditions for the exchange of prisoners of war that violate international law and are unacceptable to any government. What would Churchill and Truman say if the Koreans made such a demand?

In making and supporting such a demand, the U.S. and British governments are attempting to establish a principle that contains the most dangerous elements. The U.S. military command claims that, regardless of international law, humanitarianism requires saving these people from being killed upon their return. Strangely, this concern for humanity comes from a command responsible for the massive bombing of civilians, the continuous use of napalm bombs, and the launching of germ warfare.

### 英国和委会总书记发表声明  斥美国交换战俘的无理要求

1952-05-13
第4版()
专栏：

    英国和委会总书记发表声明
    斥美国交换战俘的无理要求
    【新华社十日讯】塔斯社伦敦九日讯：英国和平委员会总书记琼斯发表一项声明，强调说：为了世界和平，必须打破朝鲜停战谈判的僵局，求得一个解决办法。
    琼斯说：和平从来没有用最后通牒来获得的。
    朝鲜人与中国人正被要求同意违反国际法的而且是任何政府所不能接受的交换俘虏条件。如果朝鲜人提出这样的要求，邱吉尔和杜鲁门将说些什么话呢？
    在提出并且支持这样一个要求时，美国和英国政府是企图建立一个包含着最危险内容的原则。美军司令部声称：不管国际法如何，人道主义要求拯救这些人，使之免于在回去后遭到杀害。说来奇怪，这种对于人道的关心，居然是来自一个负责进行大规模轰炸平民、连续不断地使用汽油弹并发动细菌战的司令部。
