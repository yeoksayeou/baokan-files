Title: Peace Advocates in Asian and Pacific Rim Countries Warmly Support the Convening of a Peace Conference; Twenty Countries Including Australia are Actively Making Preparations
Author:
Date: 1952-05-17
Page: 1
Xinhua News Agency, 16th Dispatch:

    Since Song Qingling, Guo Moruo, and nine other prominent figures in China's peace efforts jointly issued an initiation letter on March 21st, inviting peace-loving and justice-minded individuals from Asian, Australian, and American Pacific Rim countries to jointly initiate the "Asian and Pacific Region Peace Conference," it has received enthusiastic responses and support from a broad range of peace-loving individuals in these countries. As of May 15th, in chronological order based on the dates of received telegrams, peace advocates from twenty countries, including Australia, Mongolia, Nicaragua, Guatemala, Burma, Indonesia, Colombia, North Korea, India, Chile, Mexico, El Salvador, Ceylon, Canada, Pakistan, the United States, Japan, Vietnam, Laos, and the Soviet Union, have unanimously expressed their support for holding the peace conference in Beijing. They have either appointed initiators, established preparatory committees in their respective countries to participate in the conference, or designated representatives to come to China to participate in the preparatory meetings, actively carrying out preparatory work.

    On April 3rd, peace advocates from Australia telegraphed their enthusiastic endorsement of the suggestions in the initiation letter and decided to send representatives to Beijing to participate in the preparatory meeting and the peace conference. On May 13th, they appointed eight initiators: Senator Clive Evatt, Labor Youth Committee member Morrow Lambeni, Labor Executive Committee member Albert Thomson, Methodist Church Social Department Director Reverend Hebin, former missionary doctor in China Dr. Owen Chapman, scientist Stephen Machindo, Writers' Association Secretary Jo Hughes, and Major Brobin.

    On April 4th, the Mongolian Peace Protection Committee telegraphed, stating that the committee had unanimously resolved at its plenary meeting to support the opinions in the initiation letter.

    On April 8th, peace advocates from Nicaragua in Latin America decided to send representatives to Beijing to participate in the peace conference.

    On April 9th, peace advocates from Guatemala in Latin America appointed initiators for the peace conference: Honorary Chairman of the Guatemalan National Peace Committee and writer Miguel Ángel Asturias, Chairman of the Guatemalan National Peace Committee and writer Luis Cardoza y Aragón, former Speaker of the Guatemalan Republic's Congress Roberto Alvarado Fuentes, General Secretary of the Workers' Union Victor Manuel Gutiérrez, Vice-Chairman of the Guatemalan National Peace Committee and lawyer Antonio Cruz Franco, and leader of the Farmers' Association Leonardo Castillo Flores.

    On April 9th, peace advocates from Burma formed an initiators' committee, including writer Tekin Kodumai, Rangoon University Registrar Dr. Dingwang, Medical College Dean Dr. Baden, Teachers' College Dean Wu Ba, Rangoon University Translation Department Director Wu Wen, Burma-Soviet Cultural Association Chairman Professor Wang La, Burma-China Friendship Association Chairman Dr. Xinbo, Labor Union Joint Action Committee Chairman Wu Tunwangjue, All-Burma Students' Union Chairman Ge Lawin, Member of Parliament Man Tunyin, Pastor Wu Suodika, People's Daily editor Sui Wu Dong, artist Wu Weng Lun, writer Degong Dayer, and former Mayor Wu Basuo. They have dispatched representatives to attend the preparatory meeting for the peace conference in Beijing.

    On April 10th, Indonesian peace advocates decided to send representatives to Beijing to participate in the preparatory meeting, and on April 14th and 18th, they appointed eleven initiators: Chairman of the Indonesian World Peace Defense Committee Setiadi, World Peace Council member Tsai Xiyin, Member of Parliament and Indonesian General Labor Union representative Wodoyo, Secretary-General of the People's Culture Association Dada, educator Gotot Sukardi, Antara News Agency Editor-in-Chief Zha Heduo, Member of Parliament and Indonesian Women's Awakening Association Chairman Suwati, Member of Parliament Xiao Yuchan, Chairman of the Indonesian Farmers' United Front Sidi Geda Badi, Pastor of the North Sumatra Christian Church and Vice-Chairman of the North Sumatra Peace Committee Okto Sia, and businessman Albin Dongga.

    On April 12th, the Peace Committee of Colombia in Latin America decided to send representatives to Beijing to participate in the preparatory meeting.

    According to a letter forwarded on April 14th, peace advocates in North Korea had organized a preparatory committee composed of: Vice-Chairman of the Korean National Committee for the Defense of Peace and Chairman of the Korean Democratic Women's League Park Jung-ae, Chairman of the Korean National Committee for the Defense of Peace, Chairman of the Korean Federation of Literary and Art Organizations, and writer Han Seol-ya, Vice-Chairman of the Korean National Committee for the Defense of Peace, Chairman of the Korean Cultural Association, and writer Lee Ki-young, Vice-Chairman of the Korean National Committee for the Defense of Peace, Vice-Chairman of the Korean Federation of Literary and Art Organizations, and writer Lee Tae-joon, Member of the Korean National Committee for the Defense of Peace and Vice-Chairman of the Korean Democratic Party Jeong Seong-yeon, Vice-Chairman of the Central Committee of the Korean Democratic Youth League Lee Hwan-gi, Chairman of the Korean Federation of Trade Unions Hyun Heon, Zhu Huangxie, Vice-Chairman of the Korea-Soviet Cultural Association and poet Lim Hwa, Vice-Chairman of the Korean Democratic Women's League Zhao Fu-li, Vice-Chairman of the Central Committee of the Korean Farmers' League Hyun Chil-jong, Deputy Director of the Central Propaganda and Agitation Department of the Korean Workers' Party Choi Cheol-hwan, Qi Xifu, Chairman of the Music League of the Korean Federation of Literary and Art Organizations and composer Lee Myeon-sang, Chairman of the Central Committee of the Korean Democratic Youth League Kim So-jin, Yoon Jeong-ha, farmer representative Hong Young-jae, Korean language expert Lee Keu-lu, Kim Il-sung University professor and archaeologist Han Heung-soo, pharmacologist Kim Ryang-ha, and chemist Lee Seung-gi. They have decided to send representatives to Beijing to participate in the preparatory meeting.

    On April 14th, the All-India Peace Council in India telegraphed, expressing full agreement with the preparatory meeting for the peace conference to be held in Beijing on May 28th. The list of Indian initiators is as follows: Chairman of the All-India Peace Council Saifuddin Kitchlew, Vice-Chairmen of the All-India Peace Council Edward, Kapoor, Gosambi, Anand, and Singh, and Secretary-General of the All-India Peace Council Chanda. On April 26th, they appointed representatives to participate in the preparatory meeting in Beijing.

    On April 15th, Chilean Peace Committee member and painter José Venturelli and Canadian Peace Congress Chairman James Endicott jointly issued a declaration supporting the convening of the peace conference. The declaration stated: "We – peace advocates from two Pacific Rim countries – welcome with immense excitement the initiation letter for the Asian People's Peace Conference, including countries of the American Pacific Rim. We call on all peace-loving people in the countries of the American Pacific Rim to unite more broadly to establish preparatory committees in order to actively participate in this Asian and Pacific Region Peace Conference and to widely publicize the significant meaning of this conference."

    On April 15th, peace advocates in Mexico in Latin America appointed painter Diego Rivera, Dr. Yllarleya Guzman, scientist Cosio Villegais, first Stalin International Peace Prize winner General Eriberto Jara, and Supreme Court President Franco Careno as initiators, and on May 10th, they appointed representatives to participate in the preparatory meeting in Beijing.

    On April 16th, peace advocates from El Salvador in Latin America wrote, stating that the people of El Salvador are constantly conducting peace advocacy, and people from all walks of life recognize that the purpose of this peace conference is to safeguard the principles of defending peace. International conferences are the best driving force to encourage the struggle to defend peace. Peace advocates from El Salvador have dispatched representatives to participate in the preparatory meeting in Beijing and will begin extensive publicity regarding the peace conference.

    On April 19th, peace advocates in Ceylon appointed the first group of initiators: Pastor Naravira Damarattana, Member of Parliament Azez, former Mayor of Colombo Ratnam, Member of Parliament and World Peace Council member Cuneman, and artist Kasman. They expressed full support for the convening of the peace conference and hoped that the peace conference would achieve success.

    On April 19th, peace advocates in Canada telegraphed, saying that they had widely publicized the initiation letter for the Asian and Pacific Region Conference throughout the country and had appointed the first group of initiators: Canadian Peace Congress Chairman Dr. James Endicott and Vancouver Fisheries Union and Labor Federation Secretary He Mai Stevens. They have also dispatched representatives to China to attend the preparatory meeting.

    On April 27th, preparatory committees were established in Karachi and Lahore, Pakistan. The Karachi preparatory committee consists of fifteen members, and the Lahore preparatory committee consists of eleven members. They have appointed representatives to attend the preparatory meeting in Beijing.

    During April, peace-loving people in the United States expressed their support for the convening of the peace conference and decided to dispatch representatives to Beijing to participate in the preparatory meeting.

    Japanese peace advocates appointed initiators for the peace conference in April: Senator and World Peace Council member Ikuo Oyama, Ritsumeikan University President and Chairman of the Democratic Scientists Association Hiroshi Suekawa, Hiroshima University Professor and Chairman of the Japan Educational Society Arata Osada, Director of the China Research Institute and World Peace Council member Yoshitaro Hirano, World Peace Council member and Vice-Chairman of the Japan Cultural Persons Conference Sanzaburo Tange, Vice-Chairman of the Japan Cultural Persons Conference Yoichi Fukushima, Director of the War Dead Students Memorial Association and Doctor of Literature Kenjiro Yanagida, General Secretary of the Journalists' Alliance Masaharu Hatanaka, Gakushuin University Professor Ikutaro Shimizu, World Peace Council member and Aichi University Professor Sumio Takaku, artist and Peace Roundtable's Tokusaburo Masamune, Chairman of the Japan Farmers' Association Yutaka Kubota, Chairman of the Central Executive Committee of the National Federation of Student Self-Government Associations Jin Tamai, World Peace Council member and Chairman of the Central Committee of the Democratic Women's Club Fuki Kushida, Chairman of the Japan World Government Association Jooichi Sakurazawa, Chairman of the Korean Residents Democratic United Front Conference Kim Hoon, and Chairman of the Overseas Chinese Democratic Promotion Association Gan Wenfang. Japanese peace advocates also established a preparatory committee for the peace conference, held its first preparatory meeting on April 21st, and officially began working. They have also issued an appeal urging peace-loving people throughout Japan to discuss the peace conference issue and elect representatives to participate in the conference.

    During April, Vietnam established a preparatory committee for the peace conference, consisting of seven members: Chairman of the Vietnam Committee for the Defense of World Peace Le Dinh Tham, Executive Committee member of the Vietnam Committee for the Defense of World Peace Nguyen Xuan Thuy, Secretary-General of the Vietnam Committee for the Defense of World Peace Duong Bach Mai, Central Committee member of the Vietnam Socialist Party and agricultural technician Hoang Van Duc, Secretary of the Vietnam Committee for the Defense of World Peace and Central Committee member of the Vietnam Youth League Nguyen Xuan Chim, Central Executive Committee member of the Vietnam General Labor Union Duong Bach Lien, and Committee member of the Vietnam Women's Federation Le Tran Phuong. The Chairman of the preparatory committee is Nguyen Xuan Thuy. Vietnamese peace advocates will send a delegation to participate in the peace conference, including representatives from people's organizations, parties, labor heroes, and religious figures. After receiving the initiation letter from Song Qingling, Guo Moruo, et al., the Vietnam Committee for the Defense of World Peace and the Executive Committee of the Vietnam National United Front organized a national movement committee to actively promote the significance of the Asian and Pacific Region Peace Conference to the people.

    During April, the Central Committee Executive Committee of the Lao National United Front wrote to the Chinese People's Committee for the Defense of World Peace, expressing a warm welcome to the initiation letter and fully agreeing that Beijing be the meeting place. The letter stated: "The Lao National United Front, representing the will of all peace-loving people from all strata, religions, and ethnic groups in Laos, is willing to widely publicize the great significance of convening the peace conference to the Lao people and to send a delegation to attend the conference. We believe that with the sincere support of the Chinese people, who have incomparable enthusiasm and faith in the achievements of the peace movement, and the people of the Asian and Pacific region, the conference will surely achieve the expected success."

    On May 14th, Chairman of the Soviet Peace Protection Committee Tikhonov telegraphed, saying that the Soviet Union had dispatched three representatives to Beijing to participate in the preparatory meeting for the peace conference.

### 亚洲及太平洋沿岸各国和平人士  热烈拥护召开和平会议  澳大利亚等二十个国家积极进行筹备工作

1952-05-17
第1版()
专栏：

    亚洲及太平洋沿岸各国和平人士
    热烈拥护召开和平会议
    澳大利亚等二十个国家积极进行筹备工作
    【新华社十六日讯】我国努力和平事业的著名人士宋庆龄、郭沫若等十一人，在三月二十一日联名发出发起书，邀请亚洲、澳洲及美洲太平洋沿岸各国爱好和平与正义的人士，共同发起“亚洲及太平洋区域和平会议”之后，业已得到亚洲、澳洲及美洲太平洋沿岸各国广大爱好和平人士的热烈响应和支持。截至五月十五日止，按照来电日期的先后，计有澳大利亚、蒙古、尼加拉瓜、危地马拉、缅甸、印度尼西亚、哥伦比亚、朝鲜、印度、智利、墨西哥、萨尔瓦多、锡兰、加拿大、巴基斯坦、美国、日本、越南、寮国、苏联等二十个国家的和平人士一致表示拥护在北京召开和平会议，他们或者推定了发起人，成立了本国参加会议的筹备委员会，或者推定了来中国参加筹备会议的代表，积极进行着筹备工作。

    日本和平人士已在四月间推定了和平会议发起人，他们是：参议员、世界和平理事会理事大山郁夫，立命馆大学校长、民主主义科学者协会主席末川博，广岛大学教授、日本教育学会主席长田新，中国研究所所长、世界和平理事会理事平野义太郎，世界和平理事会理事、日本文化人会议副主席淡德三郎，日本文化人会议副主席福岛要一，战殁学生纪念会理事长、文学博士柳田谦十郎，记者联盟总书记畑中政春，学习院大学教授清水几太郎，世界和平理事会理事、爱知大学教授高桑纯夫，美术家、和平恳谈会的正宗得三郎，日本农民协会主席久保田丰，全国学生自治会总联合会中央执行委员会主席玉井仁，世界和平理事会理事、民主妇女俱乐部中央委员会主席栉田蕗，日本世界政府协会主席樱泽如一，朝鲜侨民民主统一战线会议主席金薰和华侨民主促进会主席甘文芳。日本和平人士并成立了和平会议筹备委员会，在四月二十一日举行了第一次筹备会会议，正式开始办公。他们同时已发出了号召，敦促日本各地爱好和平的人民讨论和平会议问题，并举出参加会议的代表。
    四月间，越南已成立了和平会议的筹备委员会，委员七人为：越南保卫世界和平委员会主席黎廷探，越南保卫世界和平委员会执行委员阮春水，越南保卫世界和平委员会总书记杨白梅，越南社会党中央委员、农业技师黄文德，越南保卫世界和平委员会书记、越南青年团中央委员阮春针，越南总工会中央执行委员杨白莲和越南妇女联合会委员黎真芳。筹备委员会主席为阮春水。越南和平人士将派代表团参加和平会议，其中包括人民团体、各党派人士、劳动英雄、宗教界人士等。在收到宋庆龄、郭沫若等人的发起书后，越南保卫世界和平委员会和越南国民联合战线的执行委员会组织了一个全国运动委员会，积极向人民宣传亚洲及太平洋区域和平会议的意义。
    四月间，寮国民族统一战线中央委员会执行委员会给中国人民保卫世界和平委员会来信，表示热烈欢迎发起书并完全同意以北京为开会地点。信里说：“寮国民族统一战线代表寮国全体爱好和平的各阶层、各宗教、各种族人民的意志，愿把召开和平会议的重大意义向寮国人民进行广泛的宣传，同时派代表团出席会议。我们相信有了对和平运动的成就具有无比热诚与信心的中国人民与亚洲和太平洋区域人民的真诚拥护，大会一定能获得预期的成功。”
    五月十四日，苏联拥护和平委员会主席吉洪诺夫来电说，苏联已推派代表三人来北京参加和平会议筹备会。
