Title: Developing Sino-Burmese Friendship, Promoting Cultural Exchange Between the Two Countries: The Sino-Burmese Friendship Association Formally Established in Beijing; Celebration Meeting for the Establishment of the Sino-Burmese Friendship Association Held Yesterday
Author: 
Date: 1952-05-12
Page: 1

    Xinhua News Agency, 11th. In order to develop the friendship between China and Burma and strengthen the cultural exchange between the two countries, Chinese cultural and academic figures have initiated the organization of the Sino-Burmese Friendship Association. After four months of preparation, the association held its inaugural meeting in Beijing on the 11th. More than one hundred members of the association attended the meeting, including scholars, experts, professors, writers, and artists from various fields.
    Zheng Zhenduo, representing the preparatory committee of the Sino-Burmese Friendship Association, reported on the association's preparation process and its purpose at the meeting. The meeting adopted the charter and elected Zheng Zhenduo as president, Qian Weichang as vice president, and Di Chaobai, Jin Kemu, Shao Zonghan, Ni Feijun, Cao Yu, Chen Zhongjing, Chen Ying, Huang Caoliang, Feng Binfu, Dong Yueqian, Zhang Junxiang, Ye Dingyi, Jian Bozan, Zheng Senyu, and Luo Changpei as directors (in order of the number of strokes in their surnames).
    The meeting adopted a telegram to the Burma-China Friendship Association. The text of the telegram is as follows: To Dr. Htin Aung, Chairman of the Burma-China Friendship Association, Rangoon:
    The Sino-Burmese Friendship Association was established in Beijing on May 11, 1952. We hope to establish regular contact in the future and work together to develop friendly relations between China and Burma and promote cultural exchange between the two countries.
    Inaugural Meeting of the Sino-Burmese Friendship Association
    May 11, Beijing
    Xinhua News Agency, 11th. The Sino-Burmese Friendship Association held a celebration meeting in Beijing on the 11th to celebrate the association's establishment.
    Attendees included: Guo Moruo, Vice Premier of the State Council of the Central People's Government, Shen Junru, President of the Supreme People's Court, Shen Yanbing, Minister of Culture, Liang Xi, Minister of Forestry, Zhang Hanfu, Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs, Xing Xiping, Deputy Minister of Personnel, Chen Shutong, Zhang Xiruo, Zhu Xuefan, Liao Chengzhi, Deng Yingchao, heads of various people's organizations, as well as the Burmese Overseas Chinese returning to China for sightseeing, and members of the Sino-Burmese Friendship Association.
    Also attending were: the Burmese Cultural Delegation headed by U Tun Pe, and the Burmese Ambassador to China, U Hla Maung, and diplomatic personnel from the embassy.
    After the meeting began, Zheng Zhenduo, President of the Sino-Burmese Friendship Association, delivered a speech. He said: China and Burma are both located on the Asian continent and share a border. There is a long and deep friendship between the Chinese and Burmese people. The cultures of China and Burma have many things in common. Moreover, both China and Burma have suffered from the brutal aggression of imperialism. Therefore, the people of both countries share a deep resentment towards the aggressive actions of the imperialists. He said: In order to defend Asian peace and world peace, and for the development of the peaceful construction of China and Burma, the people of China and Burma should stand on the same front in resisting imperialist aggression, and must be closely united and connected. He pointed out that the establishment of the Sino-Burmese Friendship Association will certainly enhance the understanding and friendship between China and Burma.
    After President Zheng Zhenduo's speech, Vice Premier Guo Moruo, U Tun Pe, head of the Burmese Cultural Delegation, Burmese Ambassador U Hla Maung, Zhang Xiruo, President of the Chinese People's Institute of Foreign Affairs, and Daw Ohn, a member of the Burmese Cultural Delegation, spoke successively.
    In his speech, Vice Premier Guo Moruo pointed out the close cultural and economic relations between China and Burma throughout history. He said: In the past hundred years, both China and Burma have suffered from the oppression of imperialists. The people of both countries have a common responsibility in the cause of opposing imperialism and defending Asian and world peace. He said: Since the founding of the People's Republic of China, the friendly relations between China and Burma have been increasing day by day. The friendship between the people of the two countries is also growing stronger over time. He congratulated the establishment of the Sino-Burmese Friendship Association and wished the friendship between the people of China and Burma a long life.
    U Tun Pe, head of the delegation, pointed out that the people of China and Burma have had close relations and friendly feelings since the dawn of history, and this relationship still exists today. We hope that it will continue to develop permanently. He finally wished the Chinese people greater achievements under the wise leadership of Chairman Mao Zedong.
    Burmese Ambassador to China U Hla Maung said: The establishment of the Sino-Burmese Friendship Association will surely further develop the cultural, trade, and friendly relations that the people of China and Burma have had for a long time.
    Zhang Xiruo, President of the Chinese People's Institute of Foreign Affairs, said: Friendly relations and cultural exchanges between the people of all countries are the foundation of world peace. The establishment of the Sino-Burmese Friendship Association will surely contribute to the peace of Asia and the world.
    Daw Ohn, a member of the Burmese Cultural Delegation, pointed out in her speech the contributions of Chinese women to the cause of building a new China. She said: Women all over the world can create a more pleasant environment for mankind by actively opposing war and seeking peace together.
    After the speeches, the Sino-Burmese Friendship Association presented gifts to the Burmese Cultural Delegation. The celebration ended in applause.

### 发展中缅友好关系、促进两国文化交流  中缅友好协会在北京正式成立  昨天召开庆祝中缅友好协会成立大会

1952-05-12
第1版()
专栏：

    发展中缅友好关系、促进两国文化交流
    中缅友好协会在北京正式成立
    昨天召开庆祝中缅友好协会成立大会
    【新华社十一日讯】为发展中缅两国的友谊、加强两国的文化交流，我国文化学术界人士特发起组织中缅友好协会。经过四个月的筹备，该会于十一日在北京举行了成立大会。参加大会的有该会会员一百多人，其中包括各方面的学者、专家、教授、作家和艺术家。
    郑振铎代表中缅友好协会筹备会在大会上报告了该会筹备经过及该会的宗旨。大会通过了会章，并选出会长郑振铎，副会长钱伟长，理事狄超白、金克木、邵宗汉、倪斐君、曹禺、陈忠经、陈应、黄操良、冯宾符、董越千、张骏祥、叶丁易、翦伯赞、郑森禹和罗常培（按姓氏笔划为序）。
    大会通过致缅中友好协会电。电文如下：仰光缅中友好协会主席信波博士：
    中缅友好协会已于一九五二年五月十一日在北京成立，希望今后建立经常联系，为发展中缅两国友好关系、促进两国文化交流而共同努力。
    中缅友好协会成立大会
    五月十一日于北京
    【新华社十一日讯】中缅友好协会于十一日在北京举行庆祝该会成立大会。
    参加者有：中央人民政府政务院副总理郭沫若，最高人民法院院长沈钧儒，文化部部长沈雁冰，林业部部长梁希，外交部副部长章汉夫，人事部副部长邢西萍，各人民团体负责人陈叔通、张奚若、朱学范、廖承志、邓颖超以及缅甸华侨归国观光团和中缅友好协会的会员们。
    参加者还有：以宇吞帕为首的缅甸文化代表团以及缅甸驻华大使吴拉茂和大使馆外交人员。
    大会开始后，中缅友好协会会长郑振铎首先致词。他说：中国和缅甸同处于亚洲大陆，国境相邻。中国人民和缅甸人民之间，有悠久而深厚的友谊。中缅两国的文化有很多的共同点。更有进者，中缅两国都受到帝国主义的强暴的侵略，因之，两国人民对于帝国主义者的侵略行为，同样地怀着深切的愤恨。他说：为了保卫亚洲和平与世界和平，为了中缅两国的和平建设事业的发展，中缅两国人民在反抗帝国主义者的侵略方面，是应该站在同一战线上的，而且必须密切地团结着、联系着的。他指出：中缅友好协会成立，一定更会增进中缅两国之间的了解与友好。
    郑振铎会长致词后，郭沫若副总理、缅甸文化代表团团长宇吞帕、缅甸大使吴拉茂、中国人民外交学会会长张奚若、缅甸文化代表团团员都翁相继发言。
    郭沫若副总理在讲话中指出中缅两国历来在文化上、经济上的密切关系。他说：近百年来，中缅两国都遭受帝国主义者的压迫。两国人民在反对帝国主义、保卫亚洲和世界和平的事业中，有共同的责任。他说：自从中华人民共和国成立以来，中缅两国之间的友好关系正日益增进。两国人民之间的友谊也历久益新。他祝贺中缅友好协会的成立，并祝中缅两国人民友好万岁。
    宇吞帕团长指出中缅两国人民在历史的黎明时期就有着密切的关系和友好的感情，这种关系今天仍存在着，我们更盼望它永久地发展下去。他最后祝中国人民在贤明的领袖毛泽东主席领导下，获得更大的成就。
    缅甸驻华大使吴拉茂说：中缅友好协会的成立，必定会使中缅两国人民很久以来就有的文化上贸易上的交往和友好关系得到进一步的发展。
    中国人民外交学会会长张奚若说：各国人民之间的友好关系与文化交流是世界和平的基础。中缅友好协会的成立，对于亚洲与世界的和平一定会有所贡献。
    缅甸文化代表团团员都翁在讲话中指出中国妇女在建设新中国的事业上的贡献。她说：世界各地的妇女共同积极地反对战争，寻求和平，就可以为人类造成更愉快的环境。
    讲话完毕后，由中缅友好协会向缅甸文化代表团献礼。庆祝会在掌声中结束。
