Title: Chinese and Japanese Buddhists Hold Sino-Japanese Friendship World Peace Dharma Assembly in Beijing
Author:
Date: 1957-09-18
Page: 4
　　
　　Chinese and Japanese Buddhists Hold
　　Sino-Japanese Friendship World Peace Dharma Assembly in Beijing
    Xinhua News Agency, September 16th: The Japanese Buddhist Goodwill Mission to China, invited by the Buddhist Association of China, held a Sino-Japanese Friendship World Peace Dharma Assembly with Chinese Buddhists at Guangji Temple in Beijing today.
    At the Dharma Assembly, the Japanese Buddhist Goodwill Mission returned a bronze Buddha statue to the Buddhist Association of China that had been taken from Qingdao by Japanese soldiers during the War of Resistance Against Japan.
    Takakai Senkyo, the head of the mission and chairman of the Japan Buddhist Association, said at the Dharma Assembly: "Today's Buddhists should follow the teachings of the Buddhist scriptures and sincerely pursue world peace." He thanked the Buddhist Association of China for the invitation, which gave the mission the opportunity to commemorate the 2,500th anniversary of the Nirvana of Sakyamuni with Chinese Buddhists and pray for Sino-Japanese friendship and world peace.
    Zhao Puchu, vice president of the Buddhist Association of China, said that the spread of Buddhist doctrines in the East for 1,500 years has given Buddhists in China and Japan a close relationship in religion and culture.

### 中日佛教徒在京举行  中日友好世界和平法会

1957-09-18
第4版()
专栏：

　　中日佛教徒在京举行
　　中日友好世界和平法会
    据新华社16日讯　应中国佛教协会邀请来中国访问的日本佛教访华亲善使节团，今天和中国佛教徒在北京广济寺举行中日友好世界和平法会。
    在法会上，日本佛教访华亲善使节团把在抗日战争期间日本军人从青岛搬走的一尊铜佛像归还给中国佛教协会。
    使节团团长、日本佛教协会主席高阶仙垅在法会上说：“今日的佛教徒应遵循佛经教义真诚地追求世界和平”。他感谢中国佛教协会的邀请，使使节团有机会和中国佛教徒一起纪念释迦牟尼涅槃二千五百周年，并为中日友好世界和平祈祷。
    中国佛教协会副会长赵朴初说，一千五百年以来佛教教义在东方的传播，使中日两国的佛教徒在宗教和文化上有了密切的关系。
