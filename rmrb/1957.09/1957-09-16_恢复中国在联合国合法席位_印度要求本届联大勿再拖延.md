Title: India Demands the Current UN General Assembly No Longer Delay Restoration of China's Legitimate Seat in the United Nations
Author:
Date: 1957-09-16
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, New York, September 15th: Indian representative to the United Nations, Arthur Lall, submitted a memorandum on the 14th, requesting the 12th session of the United Nations General Assembly, opening on September 17th, to discuss the issue of restoring China's legitimate seat in the United Nations.

Lall presented this memorandum in a letter to United Nations Secretary-General Dag Hammarskjöld.

India's memorandum stated that the United Nations should consider and remedy the significant deficiency that continues to exist due to the absence of an effective Chinese delegation; doing so is appropriate and urgent. Obviously, without the participation of the government representing approximately one-quarter of the world's population, the various agencies of the United Nations cannot smoothly carry out the tasks of their real charter purposes. This is especially true given China's special charter obligations as a permanent member of the Security Council. Furthermore, it can no longer be denied or ignored that the Central People's Government of China is the only government representing China capable and willing to fulfill the obligations of a United Nations member state in accordance with the Charter.

The memorandum continued, stating that without genuine Chinese representation, the United Nations lacks a spokesperson for a large population and a spokesperson for an important government. If this government participates in the United Nations, it will help resolve all issues, especially those directly related to the Far East, as well as disarmament, the peaceful use of atomic energy, and economic improvement in underdeveloped countries. In addition, due to the absence of effective Chinese representation, there is an imbalance that prevents the opinions in the United Nations from fully valuing the voice of a large part of Asia.

The memorandum also stated that this year, due to the continued deadlock on the issue of disarmament and the need for progress on a broad basis in all other aspects of United Nations activities, it is particularly necessary and urgent for the United Nations to quickly consider ending the current situation of non-participation of the Chinese people and the Chinese government.

Therefore, the Indian delegation once again recommends that the item entitled "The Question of Chinese Representation in the United Nations" be included on the agenda of the twelfth session of the United Nations General Assembly as an urgent and important issue.

### 恢复中国在联合国合法席位  印度要求本届联大勿再拖延

1957-09-16
第6版()
专栏：

　　恢复中国在联合国合法席位
　　印度要求本届联大勿再拖延
    新华社15日讯  纽约消息：印度驻联合国代表阿瑟·拉尔14日提出一项备忘录，要求9月17日开幕的联合国大会第十二届会议讨论恢复中国在联合国的合法席位的问题。
    拉尔是在给联合国秘书长哈马舍尔德的一封信中提出这项备忘录的。
    印度的备忘录说，联合国应当考虑和弥补由于没有中国的有效代表团而继续存在的这个重大缺陷；这样作是适宜的和迫切的。显然，由于没有世界的大约四分之一的人口的政府参加，联合国的各个机构不能顺利地执行它们的现实宪章的目的的任务。鉴于中国作为安全理事会的一个常任理事国所负担的特别宪章义务，情况尤其是这样。此外，这是不能够再加以否认或忽视的：中国的中央人民政府是代表中国能够和愿意根据宪章来履行联合国会员国的义务的唯一政府。
    备忘录继续说，没有真正的中国代表参加，联合国就没有了一大批人民的发言人，也没有了一个重要政府的发言人。如果这个政府参加联合国，将有助于解决一切问题，特别是那些直接有关远东的问题，以及裁军、原子能用于和平目的和不发达国家改善经济等问题。此外，由于没有有效的中国代表参加，因此存在一种不均衡状态，它使得在联合国的意见中不能充分重视亚洲一大部分地区的声音。
    备忘录还说，今年，由于在裁军问题上继续陷于僵局和需要在联合国活动的其他一切方面在广泛的基础上获得进展，因此格外必要和迫切由联合国迅速考虑结束目前没有中国人民和中国政府参加的情况。
    因此，印度代表团再度建议，作为一个紧急和重要的问题，把名为“中国在联合国中的代表权问题”的项目列入联合国大会第十二届会议议程。
