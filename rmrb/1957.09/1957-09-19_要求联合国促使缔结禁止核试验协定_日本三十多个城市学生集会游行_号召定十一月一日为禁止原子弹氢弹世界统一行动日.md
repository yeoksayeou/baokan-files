Title: Demanding the United Nations to Urge the Conclusion of a Nuclear Test Ban Treaty: Students in More Than Thirty Japanese Cities Rally and March, Calling for Setting November 1st as the World Unified Action Day for the Prohibition of Atomic and Hydrogen Bombs
Author:
Date: 1957-09-19
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, Tokyo, 18th. The All-Japan Federation of Student Self-Government Associations simultaneously launched rallies and marches in more than thirty cities including Tokyo, Osaka, Kyoto, Hiroshima, Fukuoka, and Sapporo on the 17th. A total of 70,000 students participated, demanding that the United Nations urge the United States, Britain, and the Soviet Union to immediately and unconditionally conclude a treaty banning nuclear weapons testing.

In Tokyo, more than 1,000 students participated in the assembly despite the rain. The assembly passed a resolution demanding that the United Nations General Assembly, convened on the 17th, urge the United States, Britain, and the Soviet Union to immediately and unconditionally conclude a treaty banning nuclear weapons testing; and that the Japanese government respect the "Tokyo Declaration" passed by the Third World Conference Against Atomic and Hydrogen Bombs and for Disarmament, and immediately submit to the United Nations a proposal for concluding an unconditional nuclear weapons test ban treaty. The assembly also protested against Britain's recent nuclear weapons testing in Australia, and demanded that the United States stop the nuclear weapons tests scheduled to be held in the Pacific Ocean next April.

The assembly also passed a declaration calling on the Japanese people and the people of the world to designate November 1st as the "World Unified Action Day for the Prohibition of Atomic and Hydrogen Bombs" in order to launch a wide and powerful movement on this day.

After the assembly, representatives were sent to the US and British embassies in Japan to deliver the assembly's resolution, and a demonstration was held in the city.

The Japan Council Against Atomic and Hydrogen Bombs is scheduled to hold an assembly with 2,000 participants on the evening of the 17th, demanding that the United Nations urge the United States, Britain, and the Soviet Union to immediately and unconditionally conclude a treaty banning atomic and hydrogen bomb testing.

### 要求联合国促使缔结禁止核试验协定　日本三十多个城市学生集会游行　号召定十一月一日为禁止原子弹氢弹世界统一行动日

1957-09-19
第5版()
专栏：

　　要求联合国促使缔结禁止核试验协定
　　日本三十多个城市学生集会游行
　　号召定十一月一日为禁止原子弹氢弹世界统一行动日
    新华社18日讯　东京消息：全日本学生自治会总联合会17日在东京、大阪、京都、广岛、福冈、扎幌等三十多个城市中同时发动了共有七万学生参加的集会和游行，要求联合国促使美、英、苏三国立即无条件缔结禁止核武器试验的协定。
    在东京，一千多名学生冒雨参加了大会。大会通过决议，要求17日召开的联合国大会促使美、英、苏三国立即无条件缔结禁止核武器试验协定；要求日本政府尊重第三届禁止原子弹氢弹和争取裁军世界大会通过的“东京宣言”，并且立即向联合国提出缔结无条件禁止核武器试验协定的提案。大会同时抗议英国最近在澳大利亚进行核武器试验，并且要求美国停止预定在明年4月在太平洋上举行的核武器试验。
    大会还通过一项宣言，呼吁日本人民同全世界人民把11月1日定为“禁止原子弹氢弹世界统一行动日”，以便在这一天展开广泛而有力的运动。
    大会在结束后，派代表到美国和英国驻日本大使馆递交了大会决议，并且在市内举行了示威游行。
    日本禁止原子弹和氢弹协议会预定在17日晚间举行有二千人参加的大会，要求联合国促使美、英、苏三国立即无条件地缔结禁止原子弹和氢弹试验的协定。
