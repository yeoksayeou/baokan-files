Title: Further Advance Towards the Center of the Antarctic Continent: The Third Group of Soviet Scientific Workers Set Off

Author:

Date: 1957-09-30
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, September 29th: TASS reports: The Soviet Antarctic Expedition's diesel-electric ship "Ob" left the port of Kaliningrad on the 27th, commencing its third Antarctic voyage.

    This research vessel carries more than four thousand tons of equipment for the Soviet research bases in Antarctica—the "Mirny" Observatory, the "Vostok" Station, and the "Sovetskaya" Station. The "Ob" is transporting six aircraft, fifteen tractors, ten towing vehicles, and five new high-powered transportation vehicles to the Antarctic land expedition, for their use in penetrating deep into the Antarctic interior by land.

    The ship, along with the "Kooperatsiya" motor vessel, will transport the third group of people going to work in Antarctica. The one hundred and eighty-nine staff members (including American meteorologist Katlat) currently working at the four Soviet scientific stations in Antarctica are preparing to leave Antarctica aboard the "Kooperatsiya" in early 1958.

    This new group of people coming to the continent will be led by Ye. Tolstikov, who led the "North Pole-4" drifting ice station in 1954-1955.

    The new expedition will expand the scientific work begun by Soviet polar workers on the continent in 1956. Scientists will continue to advance towards the high plateau in the center of the continent. Preparations are now underway to move the "Vostok-1" Station, located six hundred and thirty-five kilometers from the Indian Ocean coast, to the South Magnetic Pole. A new station, "Sovetskaya" Station, will be established in the Antarctic interior.

    After unloading the cargo, the "Ob" will continue the high-latitude oceanographic research in the Southern Hemisphere that it began in 1956. Participants in the oceanographic expedition will travel 45,000 nautical miles in the Southern Ocean to conduct a comprehensive survey of the ocean surrounding Antarctica.

### 再向南极大陆中心挺进  苏联第三批科学工作者启程

1957-09-30
第6版()
专栏：

    再向南极大陆中心挺进
    苏联第三批科学工作者启程
    据新华社29日讯　据塔斯社讯：苏联南极考察队的“鄂毕号”柴油电动船27日离开加里宁格勒港，开始了第三次南极航行。
    在这艘考察轮船上载有四千多吨供苏联在南极的考察基地——“和平”观测站、“东方”站和“苏维埃”站使用的装备。“鄂毕号”把六架飞机、十五台拖拉机、十台牵引机和五辆新式的大马力运输机械运给南极陆地考察队，供他们从地面深入南极内陆之用。
    这只船和“合作号”内燃机船将把第三批去南极工作的人送往南极。目前正在南极四个苏联科学站上工作的一百八十九位工作人员（包括美国气象学家卡特拉特在内），准备在1958年初乘“合作号”离开南极。
    这批新来大陆的人将由叶·托尔斯季科夫领导，他在1954—1955年曾经领导过“北极—四号”浮冰科学站的工作。
    新考察队将扩大苏联极地工作者于1956年在大陆开始进行的科学工作。科学家们将继续向大陆中心的高原地带前进。现在准备把离印度洋海岸六百三十五公里的“东方一号”站迁往南磁极，在南极内陆，将建立一个新的站——“苏维埃”站。
    在货卸下以后，“鄂毕号”将继续进行它在1956年开始的南半球高纬度海洋考察工作。海洋考察队的参加者将到南极作四万五千浬的远洋航行，对南极洲附近的海洋进行综合考察。
