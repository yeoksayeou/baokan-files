Title: Shells and Art—Australian Travel Poems
Author: Xin Qiu
Date: 1957-09-09
Page: 8

    Shells and Art
    —Australian Travel Poems
    Xin Qiu
    As soon as we entered the welcoming hall,
        Thunderous applause erupted in the hall.
        "Hello, friends!"
        "Hello, friends!"…
        Ah! So many blue eyes,
            Shining with the light of friendship;
        So many white arms,
            Embracing our shoulders.
        Ah! Friends, why are you welcoming us like this?
        Why are you so affectionate towards us?
        A worker-like man shouted: "Good, brothers!"
        I vaguely felt my hand being squeezed painfully by him.
        "Brothers, we have been waiting for so long,
        Today, you finally descend like white cranes (note),
        Come, brothers, let us embrace even tighter,
        Listen to the sound of my heartbeat!"
        "Why wouldn't we welcome you?!
        Some people are constantly shipping shells over here;
        But you, my best, best brothers,
        You bring us the most precious gift—art."
        "Shells, art, these two are so incompatible,
        Humph, to hell with it—shells!
        Here, we only have art—
        Only art will receive our greatest welcome!"
        Oh, I saw his expression very clearly when he spoke,
        One moment it was joy; the next it showed anger.
    (Note) New Zealanders rarely see white cranes, they regard white cranes as the most auspicious birds in the world.

### 炮弹和艺术——澳洲纪行诗抄
欣秋
1957-09-09
第8版()
专栏：

    炮弹和艺术
    ——澳洲纪行诗抄
    欣秋
    当我们一进入欢迎会的大厅，
    大厅内便立即响起春雷似的掌声。
    “你好呵，朋友！”
    “你好呵，朋友！”……
    呵！有多少双蓝色的眼睛，
    向我们闪着友谊的光；
    有多少双白色的手臂，
    搂抱着我们的肩膀。
    呵！朋友，为什么这样欢迎我们？
    为什么对我们这样深情？
    一个工人模样的人喊着：“好呵，兄弟！”
    我恍惚地觉得我的手被他攥得发疼。
    “兄弟呀，我们盼望多久了，
    今天，你们才像白鹤一样降临，（注）
    来吧，兄弟，让我们拥抱得更紧，
    你听一听我心跳的声音！”
    “为什么不欢迎你们？！
    有些人时刻都在把炮弹向这儿输送；
    而你们，我的最好最好的兄弟，
    你们却把最珍贵的礼物——艺术送给我们。”
    “炮弹、艺术，这两者多不相称，
    哼，滚它的吧——炮弹！
    在我们这儿只有艺术——
    只有艺术才会受到我们最大的欢迎！”
    噢，他说话时的脸色我看得很清，
    一会儿是欢乐；一会儿又现出怒容。
    （注）新西兰人很不容易看到白鹤，他们把白鹤看成是世界上最吉祥的鸟。
