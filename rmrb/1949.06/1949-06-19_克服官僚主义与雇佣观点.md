　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　华北邮政总局副局长  成安玉
    According to the "1949 North China Postal Construction Plan," to examine the work of the past six months, although there has been some progress in postal routes, offices, and services, and some even exceeded the original plan, it is still far from meeting the development of the revolutionary situation and the demands of the broad masses of the people.
    This is primarily reflected in speed. For example, on the Pinghan Line, due to a sudden increase in mail and insufficient transportation capacity, approximately 20,000 kilograms of mail were recently backlogged in Zhuoxian and Baoding. On the Beining Line, Shanhaiguan, at the end of April, due to a shortage of trains and space, also had a backlog of over 3,400 bags of parcels and heavy items, approximately 100,000 kilograms. In Qinhuangdao, at the end of April, due to the Chiang Kai-shek bandit gang's prohibition of navigation and postal services, there was a backlog of 948 bags, totaling 27,323 kilograms. Although the Chiang family bandit gang should be held responsible for this, we also failed to take timely measures, and it was not until May 15th that the items were transported back to Tianjin and shipped to Hong Kong for forwarding. Furthermore, due to financial difficulties, rural postal routes (i.e., postal routes from districts to villages) were transferred to local authorities, which not only caused great difficulties for local governments but also led to a relaxation in our thinking regarding rural postal work, affecting postal speed. Now that the rainy season is approaching, if we do not make efforts to prevent and prepare adequately, the problem will be extremely serious.
    Secondly, due to a lack of business experience, low cultural and technical levels, and the uneven historical development and inconsistent systems and procedures in various liberated areas, many new services cannot be quickly and universally launched. We must select key areas or large offices for pilot programs, and even now, we do not have remittance services with the Northeast, Northwest, or Central China regions (electric remittances only recently began with Hankou). Therefore, this has affected the smooth development of services and our ability to adapt to the objective situation and the needs of industry, commerce, and the broad masses. Although last year, during the Sixth Labor Congress, representatives of workers from the Northeast whose families were in Guannei unanimously requested the opening of remittance services to postal workers present, for various reasons, this has not yet been realized.
    Thirdly, according to this year's plan and last year's experience, the postal economy of the entire North China region, under the principle of strict economy and diligent production, should have been 60 percent self-sufficient. However, based on the past six months, it is less than 30 percent. Although this is due to: (1) overly low postage rates and high transportation costs (a surge in transit mail); (2) large staffing and high salaries in newly liberated cities; and (3) price fluctuations and increased expenditures, if we were adept at management, actively and extensively developing existing services, launching new services, and striving for concurrent and agency services, we could certainly greatly reduce costs, increase income, reduce deficits, and alleviate the state's expenditures and the people's burdens.
    Originally, the characteristics of the postal service are points, lines, and networks, which can be described as reaching far and wide, existing everywhere. Therefore, whether in capitalist, new democratic, or socialist countries, all know to utilize the postal service's universality and mass character, entrusting it with various services to increase income, benefit the country, and benefit the people, and to build the postal service with the postal service. For example: paying pensions on behalf of others, concurrently operating savings and remittances, handling insurance, collecting donations and taxes on behalf of others, and so on.
    The above is only a portion of our shortcomings. The main reasons for these shortcomings are the bureaucratic work style of our old liberated area postal organs, the lack of dedication to work, the lack of study of business, and the lack of diligent learning. In the newly liberated cities, in general, political awareness and work enthusiasm have indeed improved—such as a large number of employees formulating production plans, soliciting parcels, promoting the sale of stamps, and reducing salaries—but there is still a strong employee mentality, and there is still a lot of waste. Furthermore, because the inland bureaus are spread throughout the region, far from the leadership organs, the leadership truly feels a sense of being out of reach. If local governments do not exercise strict supervision, it is even easier for them to become bunkers for bureaucracy.
    As the entire country is about to achieve victory and the entire country is about to be liberated, the tasks have become even more serious. If we do not actively overcome bureaucracy and the employee mentality, we will not be able to overcome the shortcomings mentioned above, complete this year's plan, and shoulder the task of building a new democratic people's postal service.

### 克服官僚主义与雇佣观点
苏幼农　成安玉
1949-06-19
第2版()
专栏：专论

　　克服官僚主义与雇佣观点
    华北邮政总局局长  苏幼农
              副局长  成安玉
    根据“一九四九年华北邮政建设计划”来检查这半年的工作，虽然在邮线、局所、业务各方面有了一些进步，甚至有的超过了原来计划，但比起革命形势的发展，和人民大众的要求来，却还差得很远。
    这首先表现在速度上，例如平汉线，由于邮件突增，运输力量不足，不久前在涿县和保定，均曾积压邮件至二万公斤左右。北宁线山海关，四月底因火车及容间缺乏，也积压包裹及重件三千四百多袋，约十万余公斤。秦皇岛，四月底因蒋介石匪帮禁止通航通邮。也积压了九百四十八袋，二万七千三百二十三公斤，这虽然应由蒋家匪帮负责，但我们也未能及时设法，直至五月十五始运回天津，由轮航运往香港转发。又由于财政困难，乡邮（即由区到村之邮线）划归地方，既使地方政府感到极大困难，也在我们思想上放松了广大农村的乡邮工作，并使邮速受到影响。现在雨季快要到了，如不努力预防，充分准备，问题会是十分严重的。
    其次，由于缺乏业务经验，文化技术水平也低，而各解放区的历史发展又不平衡，制度手续也不一致，致许多新的业务不能迅速地普遍地开办，必须选择重点或大局试办，甚至现在和东北、西北、华中尚不通汇兑（最近始和汉口通电汇），因此，影响了业务的顺利发展，不能适应客观形势、工商业及广大群众的需要。虽然去年六次劳大开会时，家在关内的东北工人代表即曾一致向出席的邮政职工代表提出要求通汇，但因种种原因，至今尚未实现。
    第三，根据今年的计划，和去年的经验，全华北的邮政经济，在厉行节约、与努力生产的原则下，本应自给百分之六十。但从这六个月看来，尚不到百分之三十。这虽然是由于：（一）邮资过低，运费高（过境邮件猛增）。（二）新解放城市编制大，待遇高。（三）物价波动，支出增加。但如果我们善于经营管理，能主动地积极地大量地开展原有业务，创办新的业务，争取兼营和代办业务，那我们一定可以大大减少成本，增加收入，减少赤字，减轻国家的开支与人民的负担。
    本来邮政的特点是点、线、网，可谓无远弗届，无处弗有，故无论资本主义、新民主主义，及社会主义国家，均莫不知利用邮政的普遍性与群众性，委托其办理各种业务，以增加收入，而裕国利民，以邮建邮。例如：代付恤金、兼营储汇、办理保险、代收捐税等等。
    以上只是我们缺点的一部分，产生这些缺点的主要原因是由于我们老解放区邮政的机关作风，官僚主义，不安心工作，不钻研业务，不努力学习，在新解放城市中，一般地说，政治觉悟与工作情绪确已提高——如大批员工制订生产计划、揽收包裹、推销印花及降低待遇等，但还存在着浓厚的雇佣观点，还有很多的浪费。又由于内地各局遍布全区，距离领导机关遥远，领导上实有鞭长莫及之感，如果地方政府监督不严，更容易成为官僚主义的防空洞。
    际此全国即将胜利，全国即将解放，任务已更加严重，如不积极克服官僚主义与雇佣观点，我们就不可能克服上面那些缺点，完成今年的计划，担负起建设新民主主义的人民邮政的任务。
    　　　　　　　　　　　　　　一九四九、六、十七日。
