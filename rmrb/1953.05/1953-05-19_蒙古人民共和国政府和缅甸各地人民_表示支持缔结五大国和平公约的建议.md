Title: The Government of the Mongolian People's Republic and People from All Parts of Burma Express Support for the Proposal to Conclude a Five-Power Peace Pact
Author:
Date: 1953-05-19
Page: 4

    Xinhua News Agency, Ulan Bator, 17th Dispatch from TASS: The Mongolian "Truth Daily" published the reply from the Government of the Mongolian People's Republic to the letter from the International Committee of the World People's Peace Congress requesting the Mongolian government to support the proposal to conclude a Five-Power Peace Pact. The reply states: The Government of the Mongolian People's Republic, loyal to the cause of international peace and cooperation, in order to represent the Mongolian people's desire for peace, warmly welcomes and vigorously supports the lofty proposal of the Vienna World People's Peace Congress calling for the conclusion of a Five-Power Peace Pact. The Government and people of the Mongolian People's Republic are deeply convinced that as long as the governments of all countries are sincere, the various problems that create international tension can be resolved through consultation and cooperation. The conclusion of a peace pact by the five major powers will greatly contribute to the resolution of all international problems. Our people, together with all peace-loving people, will continue to resolutely struggle to safeguard universal peace among nations.
    Xinhua News Agency, Rangoon, 18th: The Burmese people are eagerly hoping that the proposal of the World People's Peace Congress calling for the five major powers to conclude a peace pact can be realized soon. In the past month or more, mass meetings with thousands of people participating have been held in twenty-five cities including Rangoon, Mandalay, Moulmein, Prome, Pegu, and Henzada. At all these meetings, the masses unanimously and enthusiastically passed resolutions supporting the proposal for the five major powers to conclude a peace pact, and demanding that the Government of the Union of Burma promptly support it.
    The Burma Peace Committee in Defence of World Peace wrote a letter to the Prime Minister of the Union of Burma, U Nu, on May 13, requesting the government to respond to the demands of the Burmese people and the appeal of the World Peace Organization. The committee requested the Burmese government to appoint representatives to consult with the committee on ways to support the conclusion of a Five-Power Peace Pact.

### 蒙古人民共和国政府和缅甸各地人民  表示支持缔结五大国和平公约的建议

1953-05-19
第4版()
专栏：

    蒙古人民共和国政府和缅甸各地人民
    表示支持缔结五大国和平公约的建议
    【新华社十八日讯】据塔斯社乌兰巴托十七日讯：蒙古“真理报”发表蒙古人民共和国政府对于世界人民和平大会国际委员会要求蒙古政府支持缔结五大国和平公约建议来信的答复。复信说：忠于国际和平与合作事业的蒙古人民共和国政府，为了代表蒙古人民表示他们对和平的愿望，热烈欢迎并竭力支持维也纳世界人民和平大会要求缔结五大国和平公约的崇高的建议。蒙古人民共和国政府与人民深信：只要各国政府具有诚意，造成国际紧张局势的种种问题是能够通过协商和合作获得解决的。五大国缔结和平公约，将大大地有助于所有国际问题的解决。我国人民和所有爱好和平的人民一道，将继续为维护国际间普遍和平而坚决斗争。
    【新华社十八日讯】仰光消息：缅甸人民热烈希望世界人民和平大会要求五大国缔结和平公约的建议能够早日得到实现。最近一个多月来，在仰光、曼德勒、毛淡棉、卑谬、勃固、兴实达等二十五个城市中，曾分别举行了有数千人参加的群众大会。在所有这些大会上，群众都一致热烈地通过决议，拥护五大国缔结和平公约的主张，并要求缅甸联邦政府迅速予以支持。
    缅甸保卫世界和平委员会在五月十三日写信给缅甸联邦总理吴努，要求政府回答缅甸人民的要求和世界和平组织的呼吁。该会要求缅甸政府指派代表与该会协商关于支持五大国缔结和平公约的办法。
