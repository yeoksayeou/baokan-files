Title: Sixth International Peace Bicycle Race Concludes in Warsaw
Author:
Date: 1953-05-18
Page: 4

(Xinhua News Agency, May 17th, Warsaw) The Sixth Prague-Berlin-Warsaw International Peace Bicycle Race, jointly sponsored by the Polish "People's Tribune," the Czechoslovak "Red Rights," and the German Democratic Republic's "New Germany," concluded in Warsaw on the 14th.

The race began on May 1st in Bratislava, Czechoslovakia, passing through Czechoslovakia, the German Democratic Republic, and Poland, covering a total distance of over 2,200 kilometers.  Ninety-three cyclists from Poland, Czechoslovakia, Hungary, Romania, Bulgaria, the German Democratic Republic, Great Britain, France, Austria, Belgium, Denmark, Finland, and Trieste participated in the race. The German Democratic Republic team won first place overall, and Petersen of Denmark won first place individually.

This race was a major event attracting attention in European life.  In the towns and cities of Czechoslovakia, the German Democratic Republic, and Poland through which the bicycle race passed, tens of thousands of residents held rallies to warmly welcome the participating athletes. These rallies became powerful demonstrations of peace and friendship among the peoples of various countries.

When the athletes passed through Prague, the residents of Prague held a rally at the Sparta Stadium to express their welcome. Czechoslovak President Zapotocky and Premier Široký, among others, attended the rally.

When the bicycle race passed through Berlin, tens of thousands of Berlin residents gathered on Stalin Avenue to welcome it. Deputy Premier Ulbricht of the German Democratic Republic and Madame Blum, a member of the World Peace Council, congratulated the athletes on their smooth journey. Ulbricht asked the athletes to convey the warm greetings of the peace-loving people of Germany to the Polish people and assured them that the German people would do everything in their power to consolidate peace. In the German city of Görlitz, located on the Oder-Neisse border, German and Polish youth held a rally as the athletes were about to arrive. Honecker, Chairman of the Free German Youth League, and Szczawkowski, Secretary of the Central Council of the Polish Youth League, both spoke at the rally, stating that the friendship between the German and Polish peoples was unbreakable.

The athletes arrived at the finish line, the Polish Army Stadium in Warsaw, on the 14th.  Polish State Council Chairman Zawadzki, members of the Polish United Workers' Party Politburo, heads of the Polish government, representatives of diplomatic envoys from various countries to Poland, and 50,000 residents of the Polish capital welcomed them at the stadium.  Polish Central Sports Committee Chairman Łejczak spoke at the stadium, pointing out that this race strengthened the friendly unity of the peoples of European countries in the struggle for peace.

### 第六届国际和平自行车比赛在华沙结束

1953-05-18
第4版()
专栏：

    第六届国际和平自行车比赛在华沙结束
    【新华社十七日讯】华沙消息：由波兰“人民论坛报”、捷克斯洛伐克“红色权利报”和德意志民主共和国“新德意志报”联合主持的第六届布拉格—柏林—华沙国际和平自行车比赛，已于十四日在华沙结束。
    比赛是五月一日在捷克斯洛伐克的布拉迪斯拉发开始的，道经捷克斯洛伐克、德意志民主共和国、波兰三国，全程共二千二百多公里。参加比赛的有波兰、捷克斯洛伐克、匈牙利、罗马尼亚、保加利亚、德意志民主共和国、英国、法国、奥地利、比利时、丹麦、芬兰和的里雅斯特的选手共九十三人。比赛结果：德意志民主共和国选手队得团体第一名，丹麦的彼德森得个人第一名。
    这次比赛是欧洲生活中吸引人们注意的一件大事。在自行车竞赛所经过的地方，捷克斯洛伐克、德意志民主共和国和波兰各城镇成千上万的居民，纷纷举行集会，热烈欢迎参加比赛的运动员们。这些集会成为各国人民和平与友好的有力示威。
    当运动员们经过布拉格时，布拉格居民在斯巴达运动场举行大会表示欢迎，捷克斯洛伐克总统萨波托斯基和总理西罗基等人都出席了大会。
    在自行车经过柏林时，几万柏林居民齐集斯大林大街欢迎。德意志民主共和国副总理乌布利希和世界和平理事会理事布伦姆夫人祝贺运动员们一路顺风，乌布利希要求运动员们把德国爱好和平的人民的热烈问候带给波兰人民，并向他们保证德国人民要尽一切力量来巩固和平。在德国的位于奥得—尼斯河边境的哥利兹城，德国和波兰青年在运动员们将要到达时举行了大会，自由德国青年联盟主席昂纳克和波兰青年联盟中央理事会书记斯查沃科夫斯基都在会上讲话，表示德国和波兰两国人民间的友谊是牢不可破的。
    运动员们在十四日到达了终点——华沙的波兰陆军运动场。波兰国务委员会主席萨瓦茨基、波兰统
    一工人党政治局委员们、波兰政府首长和各国驻波兰外交使节代表、以及五万波兰首都的居民，在运动场欢迎。波兰中央体育委员会主席莱柴克在运动场上讲话，他指出，这次比赛加强了欧洲各国人民在争取和平斗争中的友好团结。
