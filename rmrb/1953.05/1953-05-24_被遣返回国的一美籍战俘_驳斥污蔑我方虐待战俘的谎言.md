Title: American POW Repatriated Home Refutes Lies of Torture Against Our Side

Author: 
Date: 1953-05-24
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, New York, May 22nd -- According to a TASS report from New York: An Associated Press reporter from Winston-Salem, North Carolina, reported that Pinkston (from Jacksonville, North Carolina), a recently released POW returning to the United States, said in a meeting with reporters that the rumors of abuse of American POWs by the Korean and Chinese sides were "all lies." Pinkston said that those managing the prisoners "did everything possible to make our lives pleasant."

Pinkston said that the Chinese gave them books to read and allowed them to play ball and swim. "They listened to our opinions, and they did what they could. No, I have not seen anyone being abused."

### 被遣返回国的一美籍战俘  驳斥污蔑我方虐待战俘的谎言

1953-05-24
第4版()
专栏：

    被遣返回国的一美籍战俘
    驳斥污蔑我方虐待战俘的谎言
    【新华社二十三日讯】据塔斯社纽约二十二日讯：美联社记者自北卡罗来纳州温斯顿·萨勒姆报道说：最近被释放回到美国的战俘平克斯顿（北卡罗来纳州杰克逊维尔人）在会见记者时说：所谓朝鲜和中国方面虐待美国战俘的传说“全都是谎话”。平克斯顿说：管理俘虏的人“尽一切可能使我们生活过得愉快”。
    平克斯顿说：中国人给书看并准许玩球和游泳。“他们倾听我们的意见，只要是能办的他们就办到。不，我没有看见过任何人受虐待”。
