Title: Six African Countries to Form Observation Team to Monitor Namibia's Independence Process
Author:
Date: 1989-04-21
Edition: 3

Xinhua News Agency, Gaborone, April 20th.

A joint observation team composed of six frontline states in Southern Africa will depart from Botswana on April 23rd to monitor Namibia's independence process.

According to reliable sources, three officials from each of the five frontline states—Zambia, Zimbabwe, Angola, Mozambique, and Tanzania—will arrive in Botswana on April 21st. They will then join representatives from Botswana before heading to Namibia.

The observation team will remain in Namibia until the elections scheduled for November.

### 非洲六国组成观察团将赴纳监督独立进程

1989-04-21
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

　　非洲六国组成观察团将赴纳监督独立进程
    新华社哈博罗内４月２０日电  南部非洲前线６国组成的联合观察团将于本月２３日离开博茨瓦纳前往纳米比亚，监督纳米比亚的独立进程。
    据此间可靠人士透露，赞比亚、津巴布韦、安哥拉、莫桑比克和坦桑尼亚等５个前线国家的各３名官员将在２１日抵达博茨瓦纳同博茨瓦纳派出的代表会合后赴纳米比亚。
    这个观察团将一直呆在纳米比亚直到１１月举行大选。
