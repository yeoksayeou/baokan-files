Title: The Camel Bells of the Silk Road
Author: Fù Jiànshēng  Lǐ Chūnhuá
Date: 1989-04-06
Edition: 8

    The Camel Bells of the Silk Road
    Photography by Fù Jiànshēng  Poetry by Lǐ Chūnhuá

    Farewell to
    The lonely little station
    With tears of cold dew
    I yearn for the moist blue wind
    Once
    I curled up alone
    On the cold yellow earth
    In dreams, I stepped on
    A series of desolate echoes
    Once
    I raised my head
    To the sun, begging for that giant hand that holds the sky
    Leaving green, taking away sorrow
    Hope is a dream with no light in the heart
    Today
    Finally, I set off
    The Great Wall is my spine
    The sun is the bell under my neck
    Lifting my heavy legs
    Walking towards the homeland of the west wind
    Walking towards the blue sea

### 丝路驼铃
傅建升　李春华
1989-04-06
第8版(副刊)
专栏：诗画配

    丝路驼铃
    傅建升摄影　李春华配诗
    告别了
    寂寞的小站
    噙着清冷的泪滴
    我渴望那湿润的蓝色风
    曾经
    孤独地蜷缩在
    冰凉的黄土地上
    睡梦中踩响
    一串串荒凉
    曾经
    仰起头
    向太阳乞求那一只托天巨掌
    留下绿色带走凄凉
    希望是梦心无光
    今天
    终于启程了
    长城是我的脊梁
    太阳是我颈下的铃当
    抬起沉重的腿
    走向西风的故乡
    走向蓝色的海洋
