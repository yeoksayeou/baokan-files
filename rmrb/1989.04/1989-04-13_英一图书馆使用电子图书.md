Title: An Oxford Library Adopts Electronic Books
Author:
Date: 1989-04-13
Edition: 3

	An Oxford library in Britain has started using electronic books.

	Electronic books are liquid crystal displays the size of a book. They display information from laser disc read-only memory. Each laser disc is 4.75 inches in diameter and can store the contents of 350 books.

	At present, there are about 350 kinds of laser disc read-only memory publications in the world. Most of them are scientific and professional articles and charts.

	Electronic books that have text, images, sound, and music are currently being developed. The library's manager believes that within 5 to 10 years, the market for laser disc read-only memory could be as large as the personal computer market.

	The advantage of laser disc read-only memory is that it is compatible with personal computers. Therefore, information on laser discs can be easily transferred to personal computer programs.

### 英一图书馆使用电子图书

1989-04-13
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

　　英一图书馆使用电子图书
    新华社伦敦4月11日电　据《金融时报》今天报道，英国牛津一家图书馆开始使用电子图书。
    电子图书是像书一样大小的液晶显示器，它显示来自激光盘只读存储器的信息。每个激光盘直径4.75英寸，储存350本书的内容。
    全世界现在大约有350种激光盘只读存储器出版物。大部分是科学和专业性的文章和图表。
    现在正在研制既有文字和图像又有声音和音乐的电子图书。这家图书馆的经理认为，在5—10年内，激光盘只读存储器的市场可能会同个人计算机的市场一样大。
    激光盘只读存储器的优点是，它同个人计算机兼容。因此，激光盘只读存储器上的信息，能够很容易地转到个人计算机的程序中去。
