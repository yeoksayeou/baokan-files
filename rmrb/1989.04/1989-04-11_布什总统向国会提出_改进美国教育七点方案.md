Title: President Bush Proposes Seven-Point Plan to Improve American Education
Author:
Date: 1989-04-11
Edition: 3

President Bush submitted a seven-point plan to improve American education to Congress on April 5.

Bush said that the core of this plan is based on four fundamental principles: rewarding excellence, assisting the impoverished, promoting choice and flexibility, and emphasizing responsibility.

Bush's plan proposes an allocation of $2.23 billion for educational improvements this year, which is $440 million more than the previous fiscal budget proposed by President Reagan.

Bush's seven-point plan includes: awarding bonuses to schools with outstanding educational achievements; providing new incentives to the best teachers in each state; offering scholarships to 570 of the nation's top students in natural sciences, mathematics, and engineering to attend college; supporting flexible handling of teacher certification requirements to allow talented individuals outside academia to teach in public schools; providing emergency funds to some urban schools to help eliminate drugs from classrooms; and allocating funds to some historically black colleges to help establish endowments. These schools have traditionally been the social foundation of the black middle class.

Bush said, "What you sow today, you reap for life. We cannot wait to cultivate the next generation."

### 布什总统向国会提出　改进美国教育七点方案

1989-04-11
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

　　布什总统向国会提出
　　改进美国教育七点方案
    本报讯　布什总统４月５日向国会提出了一项改进美国教育的“七点方案”。
    布什说，这个方案的核心是４项基本原则：即奖励优秀、帮助清贫、选择与灵活性和责任性。
    布什的方案建议今年为改进教育拨款２２３亿美元，比里根总统准备的上一个财政预算多４．４亿美元。
    布什的七点方案包括：授予教育成就卓越的优秀学校以奖金；授予每个州的最优秀教师以新的奖励；向全国５７０名最优秀的自然科学、数学、工程学科的学生提供高等学校的奖学金；支持对教师资格的要求灵活处理，以便使一些学术界以外有才能的人能在公立学校任教；为帮助市区学校扫除教室里的毒品而向一些市区学校提供紧急经费支援；向一些历史性的黑人大学提供款项，以便帮助建立基金会。这些学校传统上是黑人中产阶级的社会基础。
    布什说，今天播种，终生结果，为培养下一代时不可待。
　　　　　（文）
