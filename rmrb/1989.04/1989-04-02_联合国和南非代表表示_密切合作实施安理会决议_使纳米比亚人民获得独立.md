Title: UN and South African Representatives Express Close Cooperation to Implement Security Council Resolution, Granting Independence to the People of Namibia
Author: Xiong Zhongqi
Date: 1989-04-02
Edition: 4

Xinhua News Agency, Windhoek, March 31st. The United Nations Secretary-General's Special Representative, Martti Ahtisaari, arrived here today to lead the United Nations Transition Assistance Group in supervising the implementation of the Security Council's Resolution 435 on Namibian independence.

Large crowds of Namibians lined the approximately 45-kilometer road from the airport to the city center of Windhoek to welcome Ahtisaari and his entourage.

The UN assistance group consists of 4,650 military personnel, 500 police officers, and 2,000 civilian staff from 23 countries. They will begin taking over Namibia tonight to ensure the country's independence following the November elections.

Xinhua News Agency, Windhoek, March 31st. (Reporter Xiong Zhongqi) The United Nations Secretary-General's Special Representative for Namibia, Martti Ahtisaari, and the South African Administrator for Namibia, Louis Pienaar, today expressed their commitment to close cooperation for the smooth implementation of Security Council Resolution 435, which begins on April 1st.

Ahtisaari said at a joint press conference held upon his arrival at the airport that before free and fair elections can be held in Namibia, all political prisoners must be released, and all exiled individuals must be given the opportunity to return to the country.

He stated that laws hindering free and fair elections must be abolished, and all political parties must be able to freely and fairly present their platforms to the people. He emphasized that the Namibian people must freely choose their leaders, and these elected leaders should be accepted and respected by all Namibians.

Ahtisaari stressed that the United Nations and South Africa can and will work together sincerely to grant independence to the Namibian people.

Before Ahtisaari's speech, Pienaar assured that he would closely cooperate with the UN representative to address any issues that arise during the transition period.

Pienaar also welcomed the return of refugees and members of the South West Africa People's Organization to participate in the elections.

### 联合国和南非代表表示  密切合作实施安理会决议  使纳米比亚人民获得独立
熊仲琪
1989-04-02
第4版(国际新闻)
专栏：

　　联合国和南非代表表示
    密切合作实施安理会决议  使纳米比亚人民获得独立
    新华社温得和克３月３１日电  联合国秘书长特别代表马蒂·阿蒂萨利今天抵达这里，以领导联合国纳米比亚过渡时期援助团监督实施安理会关于纳米比亚独立的４３５号决议。
    大批纳米比亚人等候在机场到温得和克市中心约４５公里长的公路两旁，欢迎阿蒂萨利一行的到来。
    联合国援助团由来自２３个国家的４６５０名军事人员、５００名警察和２０００名文职人员组成。他们将从今夜开始接管纳米比亚，以确保这个国家今年１１月大选后实现独立。
    新华社温得和克３月３１日电　（记者熊仲琪）驻纳米比亚的联合国秘书长特别代表马蒂·阿蒂萨利和南非驻纳米比亚的行政长官路易斯·皮纳尔今天表示，他们将为４月１日开始的联合国安理会４３５号决议的顺利实施而密切合作。
    阿蒂萨利在抵达这里时在机场举行的联合记者招待会上说，在纳米比亚进行自由和公正的选举之前，所有被监禁和拘留的政治犯必须释放，所有被驱逐的人必须有机会回国。
    他说，那些有碍于自由、公正选举的法律必须废除，各政党都能自由和公平地向人民阐述自己的主张。他说，纳米比亚人民必须自由选择他们的领导人，这些被选出的领导人应当被全体纳米比亚人民所接受和尊重。
    阿蒂萨利强调，联合国和南非能够并将精诚合作，使纳米比亚人民获得独立。
    在阿蒂萨利讲话之前，皮纳尔已保证，他将同联合国代表密切合作，处理过渡时期出现的任何问题。
    皮纳尔还对难民和西南非洲人民组织人员回国参加选举表示欢迎。
