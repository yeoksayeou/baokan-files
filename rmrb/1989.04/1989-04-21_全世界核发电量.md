Title: Global Nuclear Power Generation
Author:
Date: 1989-04-21
Edition: 7

Xinhua News Agency, April 14th.

Global nuclear power generation reached 17.9 billion kilowatt-hours in 1988, accounting for 1/6 of the world's total electricity generation. Among the 26 countries with nuclear reactors, 11 countries have nuclear power accounting for more than 1/3 of their total electricity generation.

The report states that there were 429 operational nuclear reactors worldwide in 1988, with a total installed capacity of 310,800 megawatts.

The United States has the most nuclear reactors, with 108, followed by the Soviet Union (56), France (55), the United Kingdom (40), Japan (38), West Germany (23), and Canada (18).

France's nuclear power accounted for 69.9% of its total electricity generation in 1988, the highest in the world.

Among developing countries, Argentina (2 reactors), India (6 reactors), Brazil, and Pakistan each have 1 reactor.

The report also mentions that there are 105 nuclear reactors under construction around the world.

### 全世界核发电量

1989-04-21
第7版(国际专页（经济）)
专栏：

　　全世界核发电量
    据国际原子能机构１４日发表的报告，去年核发电量达１７．９亿兆瓦小时，占全世界发电总量的１／６。在２６个拥有核反应堆的国家中，有１１个国家的核电占其全部发电量的１／３以上。
    报告说，去年全世界运营的核反应堆为４２９座，装机容量３１．０８万兆瓦。
    美国有１０８座核反应堆，居世界首位。以下依次为苏联（５６座），法国（５５座），英国（４０座），日本（３８座）西德（２３座），加拿大（１８座）。
    法国去年核电占其总发电量的６９．９％，居世界首位。
    在发展中国家中，拥有核反应堆的国家有阿根廷（２座）、印度（６座），巴西和巴基斯坦（各１座）。
    报告说，世界各地还有１０５座核反应堆正在建设中。
　　　（新华社）
