Title: Zambian President Kaunda Reveals: If the South African Authorities Create Conditions for Dialogue, the ANC is Prepared to Halt Armed Struggle
Author:
Date: 1989-04-16
Edition: 3

	Zambian President Kenneth Kaunda revealed on April 13 that if the South African authorities create conditions for dialogue, the African National Congress (ANC) is prepared to halt its armed struggle.

	New China News Agency, Lusaka, April 13th: Zambian President Kenneth Kaunda said in an interview with South African radio on April 13th that all countries and liberation movements in Southern Africa need peace, therefore South Africa should create conditions for dialogue.

	He said that if South Africa does this, the leadership of the African National Congress will lay down their arms without needing persuasion. He also said that freedom fighters are not terrorists.

	Kaunda also welcomed the initiative by South Africa and the Soviet Union to arrange dialogues to resolve issues in Southern Africa.

	Kaunda said that if South Africa resolves its domestic apartheid issue, he would be ready to fly to South Africa at any time.

### 赞比亚总统卡翁达透露　如南非当局创造对话条件　非国大准备停止武装斗争

1989-04-16
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

　　赞比亚总统卡翁达透露
　　如南非当局创造对话条件
　　非国大准备停止武装斗争
    新华社卢萨卡４月１３日电　赞比亚总统卡翁达１３日在这里接受南非电台记者电话采访时透露，如果南非创造对话条件，南非非洲人国民大会准备停止武装斗争。
    卡翁达说，南部非洲所有的国家和解放运动都需要和平，因此南非应当为对话创造条件。
    他说，如果南非这样做，南非非洲人国民大会领导人无需说服即会放下武器。他又说，自由战士不是恐怖主义分子。
    卡翁达还对南非和苏联倡议安排解决南部非洲问题的对话表示欢迎。
    卡翁达说，如果南非解决了国内的种族隔离问题，他将随时准备飞往南非。
