Title: British Prime Minister Visits Namibia
Author:
Date: 1989-04-03
Edition: 4

Xinhua News Agency, Windhoek, April 1st

British Prime Minister Margaret Thatcher arrived in Namibia's Grootfontein on April 1st for a visit.

Upon arriving in this city in northern Namibia, Thatcher inspected British troops within the United Nations Transition Assistance Group for Namibia and urged them to play a role in implementing the UN Security Council's Resolution 435 on Namibian independence.

It is estimated that Thatcher will hold talks with the United Nations Secretary-General's Special Representative for Namibia, Martti Ahtisaari, and South African Foreign Minister Pik Botha on issues related to Namibia and the region during her stay in Namibia.

Thatcher's original itinerary for her African tour did not include a visit to Namibia.

		April 1st, British Prime Minister Margaret Thatcher, who is visiting Namibia, visits British troops participating in the United Nations Assistance Group in Windhoek. The United Nations Assistance Group, composed of more than 7,000 military personnel, police, and civilian staff from 23 countries, will ensure that Namibia achieves independence after the November elections.

### 英国首相访问纳米比亚

1989-04-03
第4版(国际新闻)
专栏：

　　英国首相访问纳米比亚
    新华社温得和克４月１日电  英国首相撒切尔夫人４月１日飞抵纳米比亚的格鲁特方丹进行访问。
    撒切尔夫人在抵达纳米比亚北部的这座城市后，视察了联合国纳米比亚过渡时期援助团中的英国部队，并要求他们在实施联合国安理会关于纳米比亚独立的４３５号决议中发挥作用。
    据估计，撒切尔夫人在纳米比亚将同联合国秘书长负责纳米比亚问题的特别代表马尔蒂·阿赫蒂萨里和南非外长皮克·博塔就纳米比亚和地区问题进行会谈。
    撒切尔夫人此次非洲之行中原没有访问纳米比亚的计划。
    （附图片）
    4月1日，正在纳米比亚访问的英国首相撒切尔夫人在温得和克看望参加联合国援助团的英国部队，由来自23个国家的7000多名军事人员、警察和文职人员组成的联合国援助团，将确保纳米比亚在今年11月大选后实现独立。
                              新华社发（传真照片）
