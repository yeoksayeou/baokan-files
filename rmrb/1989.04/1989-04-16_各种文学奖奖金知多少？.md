Title: How Much Prize Money Do Various Literary Awards Offer?
Author: Zhou Sai Chun
Date: 1989-04-16
Edition: 8

How Much Prize Money Do Various Literary Awards Offer?

    Nobel Prize in Literature (Sweden) — $186,000.
    Hemingway Prize (France) — $150,000.
    Lenin Literature Prize (Soviet Union) — $86,000.
    Feltrinelli Prize (Italy) — $56,000.
    Planeta Prize (Spain) — $46,000.
    Paul Morand Prize (France) — $32,000.
    Neustadt Prize (United States) — $25,000.
    National Book Award (United States) — $25,000.
    Sinone Del Duca Prize (France) — $21,500.
    Booker Prize (United Kingdom) — $19,000.
    Sanning Prize (Denmark) — $17,000.
    Feltrinelli Prize (Italy) — $16,500.
    Goethe Prize (West Germany) — $16,000.
    Arno Schmidt Prize (West Germany) — $16,000.
    Betty Trask Prize (United Kingdom) — $15,800.
    Finlandia Prize (Finland) — $15,400.

    (March 25, 1989, Financial Times, Zhou Sai Chun, Wang Min Cai)
