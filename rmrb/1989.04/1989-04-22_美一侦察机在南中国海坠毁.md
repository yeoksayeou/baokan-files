Edition: 4

A U.S. Reconnaissance Plane Crashes in the South China Sea

	NEW YORK, April 21 (Xinhua) -- The United States announced today that a U.S. SR-71 Blackbird reconnaissance plane crashed in the South China Sea.

	An Air Force spokesman said the reconnaissance plane, which had been in service for 22 years, crashed while on a reconnaissance mission on the evening of April 20. However, both pilots are safe.

	He said the plane belonged to the reconnaissance squadron of the U.S. Kadena Air Base in Japan, but he did not disclose the specific mission or the target country.

	He said the pilots reported engine trouble shortly after takeoff. The two pilots were rescued by a U.S. helicopter and taken to Clark Air Base in the Philippines.

### 美一侦察机在南中国海坠毁

1989-04-22
第4版(国际新闻)
专栏：

　　美一侦察机在南中国海坠毁
    新华社华盛顿４月２１日电  据美国官方今天宣布，一架美国ＳＲ—７１黑鸟式间谍飞机在南中国海坠毁。
    一位空军发言人说，这架已服役２２年的侦察机是在２０日晚执行侦察任务时坠毁的，但两名飞行员安然无恙。
    他说，这架飞机属于美国在日本的嘉手纳空军基地侦察机分队，但他未透露该机执行的具体任务和针对的具体国家。
    他说，飞行员在飞机起飞后便报告引擎出现故障。这两名飞行员被美国直升机救起，送往菲律宾克拉克空军基地。（附图片）
