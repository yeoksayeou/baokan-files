Title: Over 300 Million People Live in Iodine-Deficient Areas in China
Author: Xiao Di
Date: 1989-04-25
Edition: 2

Xinhua News Agency, Tianjin, April 25th.

Over 300 million people live in iodine-deficient areas in China

    Xinhua News Agency, Tianjin, April 25th. Reporter Xiao Di reports: Health Minister Chen Minzhang said yesterday at the Asian-Pacific Thyroid Association's iodine deficiency disorder symposium in Tianjin, "There are 800 million people living in iodine-deficient areas worldwide, and China has 370 million people living in known iodine-deficient areas, accounting for 47% of the world's population threatened by iodine deficiency."

    More than 200 experts and scholars from 20 countries and regions will engage in four days of academic exchanges here. They will also specifically study China's experience in the prevention and treatment of iodine deficiency disorders and help plan future strategies. Professor Spray, chairman of the International Council for the Control of Iodine Deficiency Disorders, and Professor Chōji Nagatake, chairman of the Asian-Pacific Thyroid Association, delivered speeches at the meeting.

### 我国缺碘地区三亿多人居住
肖荻
1989-04-25
第2版(国内新闻)
专栏：

　　我国缺碘地区三亿多人居住
    本报天津４月２５日电  记者肖荻报道：卫生部长陈敏章昨天在天津召开的亚大甲状腺学会碘缺乏病专题会议上说：“全世界有８亿人居住在缺碘地区，而中国有３．７亿人口生活在已知的碘缺乏地区，占世界受碘缺乏威胁人口的４７％”。
    来自世界２０个国家和地区的２００余位碘缺乏病专家、学者将在这里进行４天的学术交流，还将专门研究中国在碘缺乏病防治方面的经验，并帮助规划今后对策。国际控制碘缺乏病理事会主席斯伯雷教授、亚大甲状腺学会主席长泷重信教授等在会上发表了讲话。
