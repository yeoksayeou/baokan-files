Title: U.S. Congress Passes Bill to Aid Nicaraguan Anti-Government Forces
Author: Zhang Qixun Guo Weicheng
Date: 1989-04-16
Edition: 3

	The U.S. Congress has passed a bill to provide aid to Nicaraguan anti-government forces, allocating a total of $66.6 million.

	Our reporters Zhang Qixun and Guo Weicheng report from Washington and Mexico City on April 13: The U.S. House and Senate today formally passed a bill to provide $49.75 million in aid to Nicaraguan anti-government forces.

	The aid will start this April and continue until February 1990. The funds will be used to provide food, clothing, housing, and medical supplies to the anti-government forces, and can also be used to assist personnel of the anti-government forces in returning to their homes. Additionally, over $16 million in aid funds will be used to cover transportation, refugee medical care, and other related expenses.

	The total amount of aid approved by the bill is $66.6 million.

	According to reports from Mexican news media, the new aid bill passed by the U.S. Congress stipulates that as long as the Nicaraguan government keeps its promise to carry out democratic reforms, the U.S. will not provide military aid to the Nicaraguan anti-government armed forces.

### 美国国会通过法案援助尼反政府力量　拨款总额为６６６０万美元
张启昕　郭伟成
1989-04-16
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

　　美国国会通过法案援助尼反政府力量
　　拨款总额为６６６０万美元
    综合本报华盛顿、墨西哥城４月１３日电　记者张启昕、郭伟成报道：美国国会众、参两院今天正式通过向尼加拉瓜反政府力量提供４９７５万美元援助的法案。
    援助从今年４月开始直到１９９０年２月，援款将用于向尼反政府力量提供食品、衣服、住房和医疗用品，也可用于协助反政府力量的人员返回家园。另有１６００多万美元的援款用于支付运输、难民医疗和其他有关费用。
    法案同意的援款总数为６６６０万美元。
    据墨西哥城新闻媒介报道，美国国会通过的这次新的援助法案规定，只要尼加拉瓜政府遵守进行民主改革的诺言，美国就不得向尼加拉瓜反政府武装提供军事援助。
