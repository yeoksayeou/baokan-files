Title: Western Europe, North America, and Japan Coordinate Relations with the Soviet Union
Author: Zhang Qihua
Date: 1989-04-12
Edition: 3

People's Daily, Paris, April 11th. Correspondent Zhang Qihua reports: The 20th plenary session of the Trilateral Commission of Western Europe, North America, and Japan concluded yesterday in Paris. The main topic of this meeting was to discuss the report on "The Prospects of East-West Relations" jointly drafted by Giscard d'Estaing, Henry Kissinger, and Yasuhiro Nakasone.

The report points out that the competitive relationship between the East and the West remains a long-term reality. Therefore, the West must have a common strategy when developing cooperative relations with the Soviet Union. In military terms, NATO must remain vigilant on arms control issues and the reality of the imbalance in military forces between the two sides. In economic terms, the same vigilance must be maintained against potential threats from the Soviet Union and to prevent strategic materials from flowing to the Soviet Union. Additionally, the West should not forget to continue pressuring the Soviet Union on human rights issues. Based on the above analysis, it is required that Western support for the Soviet Union should be conditional.

### 西欧北美日本协调对苏关系　强调有条件地支持苏联改革
张启华
1989-04-12
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

　　西欧北美日本协调对苏关系
　　强调有条件地支持苏联改革
    本报巴黎4月11日电　记者张启华报道：西欧、北美、日本三边委员会第20届全体会议昨天在巴黎结束。这次会议的主要议题是讨论德斯坦、基辛格和中曾根三人联合起草的“东西方关系的前景”报告。
    报告指出，东西方之间的竞争关系依然是一个长期的现实。因此，西方在发展同苏联的合作关系时必须有共同的战略。在军事上，北约在裁军问题上，以及对双方军事力量不平衡的现实要特别保持警惕性；在经济上，同样要看到苏联潜在的威胁，防止战略物资流向苏联。还有，西方不要忘记在人权问题上，继续向苏联施加压力。基于上述分析，要求西方对苏联支持应该是有条件的。
