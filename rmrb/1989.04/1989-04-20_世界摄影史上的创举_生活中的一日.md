Title: A Milestone in World Photography History – A Day in Life
Author: Zhang Yunwen
Date: 1989-04-20
Edition: 7

On April 26, 1986, 200 renowned photographers from 33 countries departed from Denver, Colorado, and headed to various corners of the 50 states. On May 2, they captured a day in the life of Americans on film.

The organizers, American amateur photographer Rick Smolan and editor David Cohen, briefed these internationally acclaimed photographers: avoid preconceptions, steer clear of clichés, and capture the most extraordinary moments from ordinary events. Pulitzer Prize winner and American photographer Dickerman traveled to Maine, capturing the first sunrise of the day atop Mount Katahdin, and then rushed to Maui, Hawaii, to photograph the same day's sunset. Australian photographer Forkner bravely infiltrated a Ku Klux Klan gathering to capture the scene. Pulitzer Prize winner Price spent the day with illegal immigrants, documenting their experiences. Each photographer shot 30 rolls of film, resulting in 245,000 high-quality photographs. A beautifully crafted book, "A Day in the Life of America," featuring 850 selected photos, was published.

The book is filled with the essence of daily life, offering an honest portrayal of "typical American life." The camera lenses were focused on ordinary people: high school students at a dance, innocent children fresh out of the bath, commuters waiting at a New York subway station, prostitutes in Las Vegas, a punk on the streets of San Francisco, medical staff rushing to save a critically ill patient in an emergency room, cowboys in the western wilderness, and brokers in the New York Stock Exchange. The entire book includes only one photo of then-Vice President Bush congratulating a centenarian and one photo of a Supreme Court justice at work.

The photographs almost cover all aspects of ordinary Americans' daily activities, but surprisingly, all 200 photographers overlooked fast-food restaurants.

In 1978, while interviewing a woman who rode a camel 4,600 kilometers across Australia, Rick Smolan had an idea: invite 100 renowned photographers to Australia to document a day in this unique country. After many twists and turns, this idea came to fruition. "A Day in the Life of Australia" began shooting on March 6, 1981, and the book was published soon after, setting a sales record and causing a sensation. This was followed by "A Day in the Life of Hawaii," "A Day in the Life of Canada," "A Day in the Life of Japan," "A Day in the Life of America," "A Day in the Life of Spain," and "A Day in the Life of the Soviet Union." This year, "A Day in the Life of China" began shooting on April 15.

Following this year's "A Day in the Life of China," there will be more "A Day" projects. One "crazy idea" has opened a new chapter in the history of world photography.

### 世界摄影史上的创举——生活中的一日
张允文
1989-04-20
第7版(国际专页（文教）)
专栏：

　　世界摄影史上的创举——    
    生活中的一日
    张允文
    １９８６年４月２６日，３３个国家的２００名颇有名气的摄影记者从美国丹佛市出发，分赴全国５０个州的各个角落，在５月２日那一天，把美国人生活中的一天“凝结”在胶卷上。
    这项活动的组织者、美国业余摄影师里克·斯摩兰和编辑戴维·柯恩向这些蜚声国际摄影界的人物作了简要的交代：不抱成见，不落窠臼，从平凡的事件中捕捉最不平凡的镜头。普利策奖获得者、美国摄影师迪克曼朝抵缅因州，在策战顿山之巅拍摄美国的第一个东升旭日，又夕至夏威夷的毛依岛追赶同一天的落日。澳大利亚摄影师福克玛冒险潜入“三Ｋ党”群拍摄了党徒的集会。普利策奖获得者普赖斯同非法移民整日共处，纪录了他们的种种遭遇。摄影记者各显神通，每人拍了３０个胶卷。成果是２４５０００张高质量的照片。一本精选了８５０幅照片的大型精美画册——《美国一日》（《美国生活中的一天》）问世了。
    画册充满了生活气息，是对“典型的美国生活”“诚实观察”，相机的焦距都对准普通人：舞会上的中学生、刚洗完澡的天真活泼的孩子、纽约地铁站上候车的职员、赌城拉斯维加斯妓院中的神女、旧金山街头的崩克、急诊室内抢救危急病人的医务人员、西部旷野的牛仔、纽约证券交易所大厅中的经纪人……整本画册中只收入了一幅布什任副总统时祝贺一位百岁老人的照片和一幅最高法院大法官的工作照。
    画片几乎包括了普通美国人的各种日常活动、各个生活侧面，但使组织者感到吃惊的是，２００人都忽略了快餐店。
    １９７８年，里克·斯摩兰采访一名妇女骑骆驼行程４６００公里横越澳洲的活动时，突然头脑里冒出了一个“怪念头”：请世界上１００位名摄影师来澳大利亚纪录这个富有特色的国土的一天。几经周折，这个“怪念头”居然得以实现：《澳大利亚生活的一日》于１９８１年３月６日开拍，不久画册问世，创发行纪录，轰动一时。接着是《夏威夷一日》、《加拿大一日》、《日本一日》、《美国一日》、《西班牙一日》和《苏联一日》。今年４月１５日开拍《中国一日》。
    继今年的《中国一日》之后，将会有更多的《一日》。一个“怪念头”开创了世界摄影史上的新的一页。
