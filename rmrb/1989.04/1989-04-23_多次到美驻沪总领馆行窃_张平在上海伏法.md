Title: Repeatedly Burglarizing the U.S. Consulate in Shanghai
Author: YE TING CHEN MAO DI
Date: 1989-04-23
Edition: 2

Repeatedly Burglarizing the U.S. Consulate in Shanghai
Zhang Ping Executed in Shanghai

Xinhua News Agency, Shanghai, April 22 (Correspondent Ye Ting, Reporter Chen Mao Di) Zhang Ping, who repeatedly burglarized the U.S. Consulate in Shanghai, was sentenced to death and deprivation of political rights for life by the Shanghai Intermediate People's Court today.

Zhang Ping was originally a private taxi driver in Shanghai. In 1981, he was sentenced to three years in prison for theft. From December 1986 to February 1988, Zhang Ping broke into the U.S. Consulate in Shanghai four times, stealing a large amount of Chinese currency, foreign exchange certificates, U.S. dollars, Hong Kong dollars, jewelry, cameras, and other items, totaling over 33,000 yuan. Additionally, from March 1985 to December 1986, Zhang Ping traveled to Jiashan in Zhejiang and Liyang in Jiangsu, stealing various items worth over 6,000 yuan.

### 多次到美驻沪总领馆行窃  张平在上海伏法
叶亭　陈毛弟
1989-04-23
第2版(国内新闻)
专栏：

　　多次到美驻沪总领馆行窃    
    张平在上海伏法
    新华社上海４月２２日电（通讯员叶亭、记者陈毛弟）多次盗窃美国驻上海总领事馆财物的张平，今天被上海市中级人民法院判处死刑，剥夺政治权利终身。
    张平原为上海市出租汽车个体户。１９８１年，他曾因犯盗窃罪被判处有期徒刑３年。自１９８６年１２月至１９８８年２月，张平４次潜入美国驻上海总领事馆进行盗窃，共窃得人民币、外汇兑换券、美元、港币、首饰、照相机等大量财物，计价值人民币３．３万余元。另外，张平还于１９８５年３月至１９８６年１２月，流窜到浙江嘉善、江苏溧阳等地盗窃，窃得各种财物达６０００余元。
