Advanced Science and Technology of Fraternal Countries

    Electronic Welding
    Staff members of the Paton Electric Welding Institute in the Soviet Union recently produced a trial production device for electronic welding. It can weld the most refractory metals (molybdenum, tungsten, etc.) and alloys to produce large parts with consistent and strong welds.
    During welding, a powerful electron beam emitted from a cathode electron source is used to heat the metal weld. The simplest cathode is a spiral tungsten wire heated to a high temperature above two thousand degrees. Electrons fly out from the cathode, accelerate under the action of a voltage of up to tens of thousands of volts, and are then compressed into an electron beam as thin as a line by a focusing device. When this electron beam hits the metal being welded, it can melt the metal. This welding is carried out in a vacuum chamber with a pressure not exceeding one hundred millionth of an atmosphere.
      Liquefaction of Helium
    The Institute of Nuclear Physics in Czechoslovakia recently achieved the first helium liquefaction in Czechoslovakia, mastering and developing this important method of experimental physics.
    During the helium liquefaction process, a low temperature of minus 269 degrees Celsius was reached. This low temperature will be used to conduct research work that will help to further understand the structure of the atomic nucleus. The liquefaction device was manufactured in a mechanical factory in Czechoslovakia.
      Artificial Gems
    Polish scientists have recently trial-produced the first batch of artificial rubies and light-colored sapphires in Poland. Artificial gems are made of special alumina with pure chemical texture. Polish engineer Łopata has been researching the synthesis of gems for two years. Artificial gems have extreme hardness, can withstand a pressure of 300,000 kilograms per cubic centimeter, and will not melt at temperatures below 2,000 degrees Celsius. It has very important value in technology. A jet aircraft must use 250 gem bearings, and a 10,000-ton displacement ship needs 2,500 gem bearings.

### 兄弟国家的先进科学技术

1960-09-28
第6版()
专栏：兄弟国家的先进科学技术

    兄弟国家的先进科学技术
    电子焊接
    苏联帕顿电焊研究所工作人员最近制成了一台电子焊接试验生产装置，可以焊接最难熔的金属（钼、钨、?等）和合金，制出大部件，而且焊缝质地一致而坚固。
    在焊接时利用阴极电子源发出强大的电子束来加热金属焊缝。最简单的阴极就是加热到两千度以上高温的螺旋线形钨丝。电子从阴极飞出来，在高达几万伏特的电压作用下加速，再由聚焦装置压缩成像线一样细的电子束。这种电子束打到被焊接金属上，就可以使金属熔化。这种焊接是在不超过一亿分之一大气压的高度真空容器中进行的。
      氦的液化
    捷克斯洛伐克原子核物理学研究所最近实现了捷克斯洛伐克的第一次氦液化，掌握和发展了这个试验物理学的重要方法。
    在氦液化的过程中，达到了摄氏负二百六十九度的低温。这种低温将用来进行有助于更深刻地了解原子核结构的研究工作。液化装置是在捷克斯洛伐克一个机械工厂里制造的。
      人造宝石
    波兰科学家最近试制成了波兰第一批人造红宝石和淡色蓝宝石。人造宝石是利用化学质地纯净的特种氧化铝制成的。波兰工程师洛帕塔为合成宝石进行了两年研究。人造宝石具有极大的硬度，可以经受住每立方厘米三十万公斤的压力，并且在摄氏二千度以下的温度中不会融化。它在技术方面具有十分重要的价值。一架喷气式飞机必须使用二百五十个宝石轴承，一艘排水量一万吨的轮船需要二千五百个宝石轴承。
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　  （据新华社讯）
