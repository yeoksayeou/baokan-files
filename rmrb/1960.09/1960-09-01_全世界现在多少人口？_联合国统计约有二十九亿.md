Title: What is the World's Current Population? The United Nations Estimates About 2.9 Billion
Author:
Date: 1960-09-01
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, 30th - New York: The United Nations published a world population statistics yearbook on the 28th, stating that the current global population is approximately 2.9 billion, increasing by about 48 million people each year.

The countries with the largest populations in the world are China, India (403 million), the Soviet Union (209 million), and the United States (178 million). These four countries account for more than half of the world's population. Ranked in descending order after the United States are Japan, Pakistan, Indonesia, Brazil, West Germany, and the United Kingdom.

By region, Asia has the largest population, accounting for half of the world's total. Africa's population is growing the fastest, with a birth rate of 45 per thousand; Europe's population is growing the slowest, with a birth rate of only 19 per thousand.

The world's population increases by about 1.7% annually, which is equivalent to an increase of 85 people per minute, or 48 million people per year.

### 全世界现在多少人口？  联合国统计约有二十九亿

1960-09-01
第6版()
专栏：

    全世界现在多少人口？
    联合国统计约有二十九亿
    新华社30日讯
  纽约消息：联合国28日发表的世界人口统计年鉴说，目前全世界大约有二十九亿人口，每年大约增加四千八百万人。
    世界上人口最多的几个国家是中国、印度（四亿零三百万人）、苏联（二亿零九百万人）和美国（一亿七千八百万人）。这四个国家共占了全世界人口的一半以上。美国以下按人口多寡排列依次为日本、巴基斯坦、印度尼西亚、巴西、西德和英国。
    按地区说，亚洲人口最多，占全世界人口的一半。非洲人口增长最快，它的出生率是千分之四十五；欧洲人口增长最慢，它的出生率只有千分之十九。
    世界人口每年约增加1.7％，这等于每分钟增加八十五人，每年增加四千八百万人。
