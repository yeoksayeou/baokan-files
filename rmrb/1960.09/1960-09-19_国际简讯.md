International Briefs

    U.S. Grants Another Loan to Yugoslavia
    Belgrade Dispatch: According to a report by the Yugoslav News Agency, the U.S. Development Loan Fund signed an agreement with Yugoslavia stipulating that the United States will provide Yugoslavia with a loan of $23 million for the import of equipment for eight newly built chemical factories in Zagreb.

    UAR Imposes Curfew on Roads to Jordan
    Damascus Dispatch: According to a report by Damascus Radio, a spokesman for the UAR's First Army announced here on the evening of the 16th that all roads leading to Jordan will be closed from 7:00 p.m. to 6:00 a.m. the following morning, effective from the 16th until further notice.
    The spokesman also said that during permitted hours of passage, entry from Jordan into the Syrian region of the UAR, or travel from the Syrian region to Jordan, is not allowed except via officially designated routes.

### 国际简讯

1960-09-19
第5版()
专栏：国际简讯

    国际简讯
    美国又给南斯拉夫一笔贷款
    贝尔格莱德讯　据南斯拉夫通讯社报道，美国开发贷款基金会同南斯拉夫签订了一项协定，规定美国向南斯拉夫提供二千三百万美元贷款，作为萨格勒布八个新建化学工厂进口设备之用。
    阿联通约旦道路实行宵禁
    大马士革讯　据大马士革电台报道，阿联第一军发言人16日晚上在这里宣布，从16日起到另行通知时为止，通向约旦的一切道路从晚上七时到次晨六时将予以封锁。
    发言人还说，在允许通行的时间内，除非通过官方指定的道路，否则不得从约旦进入阿联叙利亚地区，也不得从叙利亚地区前往约旦。
