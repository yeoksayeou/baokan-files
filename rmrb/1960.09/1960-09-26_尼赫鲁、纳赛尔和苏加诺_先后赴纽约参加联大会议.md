Title: Nehru, Nasser, and Sukarno Travel to New York to Attend UN General Assembly

Author:

Date: 1960-09-26
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, September 25th: According to the Press Trust of India, Indian Prime Minister Nehru left Bombay by plane this morning to attend the United Nations General Assembly in New York.

The President of the United Arab Republic, Nasser, arrived in New York on the 23rd. Indonesian President Sukarno will also leave for the United Nations tomorrow. On his way to New York, he will stop in Tokyo for a day to meet with Japanese Prime Minister Hayato Ikeda.

According to Reuters, Nehru spoke to reporters at New Delhi airport yesterday before his departure. When talking about the United Nations' intervention in the internal affairs of the Congo, he said that he "generally supports the United Nations' actions in the Congo," although he does "not understand or approve of" "certain actions" of the United Nations there. He also expressed his opposition to the suggestion of removing Hammarskjöld from the post of United Nations Secretary-General.

### 尼赫鲁、纳赛尔和苏加诺　先后赴纽约参加联大会议

1960-09-26
第5版()
专栏：

　　尼赫鲁、纳赛尔和苏加诺
　　先后赴纽约参加联大会议
    新华社25日讯  据印度报业托辣斯报道，印度总理尼赫鲁在今天早晨乘飞机离开孟买前往纽约出席联合国大会。
    阿拉伯联合共和国总统纳赛尔已经在23日到达纽约。印度尼西亚总统苏加诺也将在明天动身前往联合国，他在前往纽约的途中，将在东京停留一天，会见日本首相池田勇人。
    据路透社报道，尼赫鲁在昨天离开新德里机场时，曾对记者发表谈话。他在谈到联合国干涉刚果内政的活动时说，他“总的看来，支持联合国在刚果的行动”，虽然他并“不了解或者不赞成”联合国在那里的“某些行动”。他还表示反对撤消哈马舍尔德的联合国秘书长职务的建议。
