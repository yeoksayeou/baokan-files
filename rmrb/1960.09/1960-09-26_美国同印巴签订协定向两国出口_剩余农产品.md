Title: US Signs Agreements with India and Pakistan to Export "Surplus Agricultural Products"
Author: 
Date: 1960-09-26
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, September 24th: According to a report from the Indian Information Office today, the Indian government and the US government signed an agreement in Washington regarding the US selling a batch of "surplus agricultural products" to India.

　　This batch of "surplus agricultural products" is worth more than 17 million US dollars, including tobacco, sorghum, and soybeans.

　　Xinhua News Agency, September 25th, Karachi: The United States and Pakistan signed an agreement in Karachi on September 23rd, according to the agricultural product agreement signed in April this year, for the United States to provide East Pakistan with 75,000 tons of rice. For several years, East Pakistan has relied on imports to solve the rice shortage.

　　Until 1952, Pakistan had grain exports. However, by 1958, it had to import 750,000 tons of wheat from the United States.

### 美国同印巴签订协定向两国出口“剩余农产品”

1960-09-26
第6版()
专栏：

　　美国同印巴签订协定向两国出口“剩余农产品”
    新华社24日讯　据印度新闻处今天报道，印度政府和美国政府在华盛顿签订了一项关于美国向印度出售一批“剩余农产品”的协定。
    这批“剩余农产品”价值一千七百多万美元，包括烟草、高粱和大豆。
    据新华社25日讯卡拉奇消息：美国和巴基斯坦23日在卡拉奇根据今年4月签订的农产品协定，签订了一项由美国向东巴基斯坦提供七万五千吨大米的协定。几年来，东巴基斯坦一直依靠进口来解决大米的不足。
    到1952年为止，巴基斯坦还有粮食出口。但是，到1958年它就不得不从美国进口七十五万吨小麦。
