Title: Khrushchev Holds Talks with Castro
Author:
Date: 1960-09-22
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, New York, September 21st:  Nikita Khrushchev, head of the Soviet delegation to the Fifteenth Session of the United Nations General Assembly and Chairman of the Soviet Council of Ministers, visited Cuban Prime Minister Fidel Castro, who is leading the Cuban delegation to the United Nations General Assembly, at the Theresa Hotel in Harlem, a black residential area of New York, on the 20th. The two sides held friendly talks.

After the meeting, Khrushchev and Castro walked out of the hotel together. About five thousand New York citizens, mostly black, gathered at the entrance of the hotel and cheered them enthusiastically. Hundreds of New York police officers used batons to disperse the crowd. The crowd responded to the police with jeers and angry boos.

Xinhua News Agency, New York, September 21st: Despite various obstacles created by the U.S. government, Cuban Prime Minister Castro is still welcomed by the general public in New York.

When Prime Minister Castro and his entourage left the United Nations headquarters and returned to the hotel yesterday, thousands of people stood on the street in front of the hotel, shouting "Long live Castro" and "We want Castro" loudly.

At the same time, several hundred other people held demonstrations near the United Nations headquarters, expressing their support for Cuba.

### 赫鲁晓夫同卡斯特罗会谈

1960-09-22
第6版()
专栏：

　  赫鲁晓夫同卡斯特罗会谈
    新华社21日讯　纽约消息：出席联合国大会第十五届会议的苏联代表团团长、苏联部长会议主席赫鲁晓夫20日到纽约黑人居住区哈林姆区的赛里萨旅馆访问了率领古巴代表团出席联合国大会的古巴总理卡斯特罗，双方进行了友好的会谈。
    赫鲁晓夫和卡斯特罗会谈后一起步出旅馆大门。约有五千纽约市民，其中大部分是黑人，聚集在旅馆门口向他们热烈欢呼。几百名纽约警察用警棍驱散群众。群众对警察报以讥笑声和愤怒的嘘声。
    新华社21日讯　纽约消息：尽管美国政府多方刁难，古巴总理卡斯特罗在纽约仍然受到广大公众的欢迎。
    卡斯特罗总理和他的随行人员在昨天离开联合国总部回到旅馆时，数以千计的人站在旅馆前面的街上，用力地向他高呼“卡斯特罗万岁”和“我们要卡斯特罗”。
    同时，另外有好几百人在联合国总部附近举行示威，表示支持古巴。
