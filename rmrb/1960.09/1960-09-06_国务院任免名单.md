Title: State Council Appointment and Removal List
Author:
Date: 1960-09-06
Page: 4
Xinhua News Agency

    State Council Appointment and Removal List
    Xinhua News Agency News: At its 102nd plenary meeting held on August 15th, the State Council approved the appointment and removal of a number of personnel from state organs, higher education institutions, and embassies abroad. The list is as follows:
    Appointments:
    Wang Jinshan as Vice Minister of Public Security;
    Liang Yao, Guo Xianrui, and Wang Wenbo as Vice Ministers of Commerce;
    Bai Xiangyin, Fu Shenglin, Jia Shi, and Zhou Huamin as Assistant Ministers of Foreign Trade;
    Li Su as Vice Minister of Chemical Industry;
    Li Tingzan as Director of the National Bureau of Surveying and Mapping;
    Lin Langtian as President of Zhengzhou Grain College, and Zhang Jing as Vice President;
    Liu Gang as Vice President of Northwestern Polytechnical University;
    Gu Xi as President of Tangshan Railway Institute;
    Huang Jiasi as President of China Medical University;
    Zhang Dequn as Counselor of the Embassy of the People's Republic of China in the Soviet Union;
    Zhu Ze as Cultural Counselor of the Embassy of the People's Republic of China in the People's Republic of Poland;
    Zhao Jin as Counselor of the Embassy of the People's Republic of China in the Mongolian People's Republic;
    Wang Yantang as Counselor of the Embassy of the People's Republic of China in the United Arab Republic;
    Qin Shenzhi as Counselor of the Embassy of the People's Republic of China in the Republic of Iraq, and Yang Keyi as Cultural Counselor;
    Gong Dafei as Counselor of the Embassy of the People's Republic of China in the Kingdom of Morocco;
    Wang Yimu as Counselor of the Embassy of the People's Republic of China in the Republic of Ghana.
    Removals:
    Li Jing's position as Vice President of Changchun Geological College;
    Yan Duan's position as Vice President of Beijing Textile Institute;
    Li Tingzan's position as Deputy Director of the National Bureau of Surveying and Mapping;
    Zhang Weilie's position as Counselor of the Embassy of the People's Republic of China in the Soviet Union;
    Wang Yu's position as Counselor of the Embassy of the People's Republic of China in the People's Republic of Bulgaria;
    Ge Buhai's position as Counselor of the Embassy of the People's Republic of China in the Czechoslovak Socialist Republic;
    Xu Ming's position as Counselor of the Embassy of the People's Republic of China in the German Democratic Republic;
    Liu Yufeng's position as Counselor of the Embassy of the People's Republic of China in the Republic of Indonesia;
    Guo Dakai's position as Counselor of the Embassy of the People's Republic of China in the Republic of Iraq;
    Xu Ying's position as Counselor of the Embassy of the People's Republic of China in the Kingdom of Morocco.
