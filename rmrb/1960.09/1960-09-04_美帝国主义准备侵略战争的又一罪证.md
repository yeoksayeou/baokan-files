Another Proof of U.S. Imperialism's Preparation for Wars of Aggression

    The American magazine "Time" published a lengthy news column on August 1, extravagantly touting the underwater test launch of the "Polaris" missile by the American nuclear submarine "George Washington." At the same time, the magazine published a map titled "Deterrent in Deep Water" (with the accompanying explanation: "The planned fleet of forty-five nuclear submarines will regularly have thirty on standby"), depicting American nuclear submarines using missiles to attack the Soviet Union, China, and other socialist countries from all directions. This map is another confession of the U.S. imperialism's ambition for aggression. It shows that U.S. imperialism will never give up its plot to launch armed attacks against socialist countries and start new wars.

    According to the legend, the submarines on the map represent the submarines of the "missile fleet" equipped with "Polaris" missiles with a range of 1,200 nautical miles; the anchors represent overseas supply bases; and the ships represent tankers that can supply fuel for nine submarines.

    The curves extending from each submarine on the map represent the missiles launched from the submarines. The arc at the head of the missile represents the range of the missile.

    The submarine near the center of the map, located in the Kara Sea, emits the longest curve. The explanation at the other end of the curve is: 2,500 nautical mile range of the "Polaris" missile in 1965. The arc representing the missile's range encompasses the entire Soviet Union, Eastern European socialist countries, Mongolia, half of mainland China, and part of Korea.

    The locations of the remaining ten submarines, in clockwise order, are: Laptev Sea, Sea of Okhotsk, Yellow Sea, Bay of Bengal, Arabian Sea, Persian Gulf, eastern Mediterranean Sea, western Mediterranean Sea, Baltic Sea, and Barents Sea. The "Polaris" missiles with a range of 1,200 nautical miles launched from these submarines are also directed at the territories of the Soviet Union and China respectively.

    The three tankers (represented by ships) on the map that can supply fuel for nine submarines each are berthed in the Barents Sea (top) near the Arctic Ocean, the front line of the Indian Ocean (bottom), and the Pacific Ocean (right). The three overseas supply bases (represented by anchors) are Japan (upper right), the Philippines (lower right), and the United Kingdom (left).

    The place names marked on Soviet territory are Moscow (with the Soviet flag) in the center of the image, and the rest are Leningrad, Kiev, Kharkov, Rostov, Stalingrad, Batumi, Baku, Kuibyshev, Arkhangelsk, Magnitogorsk, Chelyabinsk, Tashkent, Omsk, Novosibirsk, Irkutsk, Chita, Nikolaevsk, Khabarovsk, and Vladivostok.

    The place names marked on Chinese territory are Beijing (with the Chinese flag), and from top to bottom are Harbin, Shanghai, Hankou, Chongqing, and Guangzhou.

    Some of the Chinese descriptions on the map were added by this newspaper to make it easier for readers to understand the map. (Attached image)

### 美帝国主义准备侵略战争的又一罪证

1960-09-04
第4版()
专栏：

    美帝国主义准备侵略战争的又一罪证
    美国“时代”周刊在8月1日发表了长篇的专栏新闻，为美国核潜艇“乔治·华盛顿号”由水底试射“北极星”式导弹大肆吹嘘。同时，这家周刊又用“深水里的威慑力量”为题（题下附有说明：“计划中的由四十五艘核潜艇组成的舰队将经常有三十艘在备战状态中”）发表了一幅地图，图上画着美国核潜艇从四面八方用导弹袭击苏联、中国和其它社会主义国家。这幅地图是美帝国主义侵略野心的又一次招供。它表明美帝国主义决不会放弃武装袭击社会主义国家和发动新战争的阴谋。
    根据图例说明，地图上的潜艇代表装备有一千二百浬射程的“北极星”式导弹的“导弹舰队”的潜艇，铁锚代表海外供应基地，舰艇代表可以供应九艘潜艇用油的油船。
    图中从各艘潜艇发出的曲线，表示从潜艇发出的导弹。导弹头部的弧线，表示导弹的射程范围。
    接近图正中的潜艇，位在喀拉海，发出了最长的曲线。在曲线另端的说明是：1965年“北极星”导弹的二千五百浬射程。在代表导弹射程范围的弧线内，包括了整个苏联、东欧社会主义国家、蒙古、中国大陆的一半和朝鲜的一部分。
    其余十艘潜艇的所在地点，按顺时针方向是：拉比帖夫海、鄂霍次克海、黄海、孟加拉湾、阿拉伯海、波斯湾、地中海东部、地中海西部、波罗的海和巴伦支海。从这些潜艇发出的射程一千二百浬的“北极星”导弹，也都分别射向苏联、中国的领土。
    图上可供应九艘潜艇用油的三艘油船（即用舰艇表示者），分别停泊在北冰洋附近的巴伦支海（上）、印度洋前线（下）和太平洋（右）。三个海外供应基地（即用铁锚表示者）分别为日本（右上）、菲律宾（右下）和英国（左）。
    图中在苏联领土上所标的地名，上绘苏联国旗者为莫斯科，其余为列宁格勒、基辅、哈尔科夫、罗斯托夫、斯大林格勒、巴统、巴库、古比雪夫、阿尔汉格尔斯克、马格尼托哥尔斯克、丘拉塔、塔什干、鄂木斯克、新西伯利亚、伊尔库次克、赤塔、尼古拉也夫斯克、哈巴罗夫斯克和海参崴。
    在中国领土上所标地名，绘有中国国旗的是北京，其余的自上而下是哈尔滨、上海、汉口、重庆和广州。
    图上的一些中文说明，是本报为了便于读者看图而加上的。（附图片）
