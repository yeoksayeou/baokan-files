Title: Hundreds of Thousands of Japanese Workers Strike Against U.S. Imperialism's Aggression in Vietnam
Author:
Date: 1966-10-25
Edition: 6

Hundreds of thousands of workers from the General Council of Japanese Trade Unions and the Neutral Federation of Trade Unions went on strike on October 21 to protest against U.S. imperialism's aggression in Vietnam and to demand improved living conditions.

Workers from the state-owned railway union held a two-hour strike at seven locations across the country.

Forty thousand workers from the All-Japan Federation of Local Government Employees' Unions went on strike in 38 prefectures and six major cities.

Port workers struck at major ports nationwide. Seventy thousand coal miners held a 24-hour strike. Workers in the metal, printing, and taxi industries also held short strikes.

On the same day, about 20,000 workers and students gathered in Hibiya Park in Tokyo for a rally, expressing their opposition to U.S. imperialism's aggression in Vietnam and their determination to fight against the Sato cabinet.

On the same day, in Naha, the capital of Okinawa, which is occupied by U.S. forces, 3,000 people held a demonstration march to protest against U.S. imperialism's aggression in Vietnam.

### 日本数十万工人罢工反对美帝侵越

1966-10-25
第6版()
专栏：

    日本数十万工人罢工反对美帝侵越
    据新华社二十三日讯　东京消息：日本工会总评议会和中立工会联合会的数十万工人二十一日举行罢工，抗议美帝国主义侵略越南，要求改善生活条件。
    国营铁路工会的工人早晨在全国七个地方的调车场举行了两小时的罢工。
    四十万全日本自治团体工会工人二十一日早晨在三十八个都道府县和六大城市举行了罢工斗争。
    港湾工人在全国主要港湾举行了罢工。七万名煤矿工人举行了二十四小时罢工斗争。金属、印刷、出租汽车等工人也举行了短时间罢工。
    同一天晚间，大约两万名工人、学生在东京日比谷公园举行大会，表示了反对美帝国主义侵略越南，坚决同佐藤内阁进行斗争的决心。
    同一天，为美军占领的冲绳的那霸市，也有三千人举行示威游行，反对美帝国主义侵略越南。
