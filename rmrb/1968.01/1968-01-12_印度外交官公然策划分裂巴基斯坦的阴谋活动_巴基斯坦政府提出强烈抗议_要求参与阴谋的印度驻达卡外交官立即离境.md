Title: Indian Diplomat Openly Plots to Split Pakistan
Author:
Date: 1968-01-12
Edition: 6

Indian Diplomat Openly Plots to Split Pakistan
Pakistan Government Lodges Strong Protest
Demands Indian Diplomat Involved in Plot to Leave Immediately

According to Xinhua News Agency in Rawalpindi on the 9th, the Pakistani government lodged a strong protest with the Indian government on the 7th regarding an Indian senior diplomat's involvement in a plot to separate East Pakistan.

After uncovering a plot to separate East Pakistan from Pakistan, the Pakistani government arrested 28 individuals and demanded that the First Secretary of the Indian Deputy High Commission in Dhaka, Ojha, leave Pakistan immediately.

The Pakistani government's protest note stated: "The evidence in the possession of the Pakistani government indicates that the First Secretary of the Indian Deputy High Commission in Dhaka, Ojha, has consistently supported those who harbor ill intentions against the Pakistani state, and these individuals were recently arrested in Pakistan." "The Pakistani government strongly protests against this blatant violation of recognized diplomatic customs and international legal norms, and the shameless abuse of the good treatment and diplomatic privileges granted to Ojha." The note also pointed out that after the Indian government learned of the demand for Ojha to leave Pakistan, it hastily expelled the Counselor of the Pakistani High Commission in Delhi, Ahmad, which was obviously used as a retaliatory measure.

Pakistani public opinion expressed great indignation over India's attempt to separate East Pakistan. National Assembly member Syed Asghar Ali Shah told the press that after India's failure to invade Pakistan in 1965, it is now attempting to create trouble to split Pakistan. The Pakistani government's discovery of Indian officials involved in the plot to separate East Pakistan from Pakistan clearly proves this. The patriotic people of Pakistan will never allow such plots to succeed and will not tolerate any plotters.

Provincial Assembly member Khan Mughal Ram Khan also condemned India's plotting. He pointed out that just as India failed to achieve any military victory in 1965, this plot will also not succeed.

### 印度外交官公然策划分裂巴基斯坦的阴谋活动  巴基斯坦政府提出强烈抗议   要求参与阴谋的印度驻达卡外交官立即离境

1968-01-12
第6版()
专栏：

    印度外交官公然策划分裂巴基斯坦的阴谋活动
    巴基斯坦政府提出强烈抗议
    要求参与阴谋的印度驻达卡外交官立即离境
    据新华社拉瓦尔品第九日电　巴基斯坦政府就印度的一名高级外交官参与策划使东巴基斯坦分裂出去的阴谋，在七日向印度政府提出强烈抗议。
    巴基斯坦政府在破获一项使东巴基斯坦从巴基斯坦分裂出去的阴谋以后，逮捕了二十八人，并要求参与策划这一阴谋的印度驻达卡副高级专员公署一等秘书奥杰哈立即离开巴基斯坦。
    巴基斯坦政府的抗议照会说：“巴基斯坦政府掌握的证据表明，印度驻达卡副高级专员公署一等秘书奥杰哈一贯积极支持那些心怀恶意反对巴基斯坦国家的人，这些人最近在巴基斯坦被逮捕。”“巴基斯坦政府对这种粗暴破坏公认的外交惯例和国际法准则，滥用给予奥杰哈的良好待遇和外交特权的无耻行径，提出强烈的抗议。”照会并且指出印度政府在得知要求奥杰哈离开巴基斯坦以后不久，就急急忙忙地驱逐巴基斯坦驻德里高级专员公署参赞艾哈迈德，这显然是用来作为一种报复的手段。
    巴基斯坦舆论对印度企图把东巴基斯坦分裂出去的阴谋表示极大愤慨。国民议会议员赛义德·阿斯加尔·阿里·沙阿向报界发表谈话说，印度在一九六五年侵略巴基斯坦失败以后，现在又企图制造事端来分裂巴基斯坦。巴基斯坦政府发现印度官员参与了策划使东巴基斯坦从巴基斯坦分裂出去的阴谋，就清楚地证明了这一点。巴基斯坦爱国人民决不允许任何这样的阴谋得逞，也决不宽容任何这样的阴谋家。
    省议会议员汗·穆加尔拉姆汗向报界发表谈话，谴责印度策划这一阴谋。他指出，正如印度一九六五年在军事上没有能够取得任何胜利一样，它的这种阴谋也决不会得逞。
