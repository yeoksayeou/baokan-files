    Yemen's Foreign Ministry Denounces British Military Provocation
    The Yemeni government's Foreign Ministry issued a statement in Sana'a on the 8th, denouncing the British troops stationed in Dali and Sana'a for opening fire on the city of Qataba and its surrounding areas on the 5th, provoking a confrontation.
    The statement said that the Yemeni government condemns this provocative act to the world public opinion and reaffirms its legitimate right to defend the integrity of Yemen's territory.

    Tanzania Decides to Nationalize All Banks
    The Tanzanian government has decided to nationalize all banks, including foreign banks operating in Tanzania, starting from the 6th. The people of Tanzania held large-scale demonstrations in the capital, Dar es Salaam, and Zanzibar, firmly supporting the government's decision.

    Indian Prime Minister Assaulted During Election Campaign
    Indian Prime Minister Indira Gandhi was attacked by angry young students during an election rally in Bhubaneswar, the capital of Orissa state, on the 8th. She was struck with stones, sustaining injuries to her nose and lips, causing blood to flow freely. The youths shouted slogans such as "Down with the Congress Party!" and "Indira Gandhi, go back!" Indira Gandhi was in a pathetic state after the assault and was rushed to the emergency room.

    Tunisian People Demand U.S. "Peace Corps" to Leave
    The "African Freedom Movement" organization in Tunisia distributed leaflets in the capital on the 9th, demanding that the U.S. "Peace Corps" immediately leave Africa. The leaflets stated: "Today, every African must declare to these gentlemen from the American CIA: Leave Africa's soil immediately and return to your boastful America. Civilize your own country!"

    Japanese House of Representatives Election Ends in Chaos
    The 31st election for the Japanese House of Representatives concluded in a chaotic manner on January 29th. In the 486 seats of the House of Representatives, the Liberal Democratic Party secured 277 seats, the Socialist Party 140 seats, the Democratic Socialist Party 30 seats, the Komeito Party 25 seats, the Communist Party 5 seats, and independents 9 seats.
    The Liberal Democratic Party, led by Eisaku Sato, once again took power, winning the applause of its American imperialist masters, but faced strong opposition from the broad masses of the Japanese people.

### 简明新闻

    简明新闻
    也门外交部谴责英国的军事挑衅
    也门政府外交部八日在萨那发表声明，谴责英国驻达利和萨那赫的军队五日向也门卡塔巴市及其附近地区开火进行挑衅。
    声明说，也门政府向全世界公众舆论谴责英国这一挑衅行动，并重申它保卫也门领土完整的合法权利。
    坦桑尼亚决定把所有银行国有化
    坦桑尼亚政府决定从六日起把所有银行，包括开设在坦桑尼亚的外国银行，全部收归国有。坦桑尼亚人民在首都达累斯萨拉姆和桑给巴尔连日举行大规模示威游行，坚决支持政府这个决定。
    印度总理在竞选时挨揍狼狈不堪
    印度总理英迪拉·甘地八日在奥里萨邦的首府布巴内斯瓦尔的竞选集会上，当场被愤怒的青年学生用石块打得鼻破唇肿，鲜血直流。青年们还高呼“打倒国大党！”“英迪拉·甘地滚回去！”的口号。英迪拉·甘地挨揍后狼狈不堪，被送去“急救”。
    突尼斯人民要美“和平队”滚蛋
    突尼斯“非洲自由运动”组织九日在突尼斯首都散发传单，要美国“和平队”立即滚出非洲。传单说：“今天每个非洲人必须向这些美国情报局的先生们宣布：立即离开非洲的土地，回到你们吹得天花乱坠的美洲去，开化你们自己的国家吧！”
    日众院竞选丑剧在吵闹声中收场
    日本第三十一次众议院竞选丑剧，一月二十九日在一片喧嚣声中收场。在众议院的四百八十六个席位中，自由民主党占二百七十七席，社会党一百四十席，民主社会党三十席，公明党二十五席，共产党五席，无党派九席。
    以佐藤荣作为首的自由民主党再次执政，博得了它的美帝国主义主子的喝采，遭到了日本广大人民的强烈反对。　（据新华社讯）
