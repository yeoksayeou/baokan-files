Title: Chaos and Change
Author: Sun Youchun
Date: 1967-02-06
Edition: 6

The essence of Marxism is critical and revolutionary. Its fundamental point is criticism, struggle, rebellion, and revolution.

To criticize, to struggle, to rebel, and to revolutionize, one must create "chaos." "Chaos" is a necessary law of the movement of things. Revolutionary "chaos" means smashing the old order, destroying the old world, and removing all obstacles in the path of the revolution. The result of revolutionary "chaos" will definitely push society forward. Without revolutionary "chaos," there would be no revolutionary "change."

Without the eight years of "chaos" in the War of Resistance Against Japan, it would have been impossible to defeat Japanese imperialism's invasion of China.

Without the more than twenty years of "chaos" in the domestic revolutionary wars, it would have been impossible to overthrow the Chiang Kai-shek regime and establish the People's Republic of China, where the people are masters.

Similarly, without the "chaos" of the current Cultural Revolution of the proletariat, it would be impossible to expose those in power within the Party who are taking the capitalist road, those ghosts and monsters in society, and those who stubbornly adhere to the bourgeois reactionary line. Without the "chaos" of the Cultural Revolution of the proletariat, it would be impossible to eliminate all the old ideas, old culture, old customs, and old habits of the exploiting classes and establish new ideas, new culture, new customs, and new habits of the proletariat.

The process of "chaos" is the process of criticism, struggle, rebellion, and "change."

"Only heroes can drive away tigers and leopards, and there are no heroes who fear bears and tigers." True proletarian revolutionary rebels are not afraid of "chaos." To revolutionize and struggle means not to fear "chaos." Those who fear "chaos" will not revolutionize, will not struggle, and will take the path of conciliationism, reformism, opportunism, and revisionism. Those who fear "chaos" are not true proletarian revolutionary rebels.

Some people accuse the revolutionary rebels of "fearing that the world is not chaotic enough." Yes! We proletarian revolutionary rebels want the world to be in great "chaos," want the storm of revolution to roar, want the giant waves of revolution to shake the heavens, and want those in power within the Party who are taking the capitalist road, those ghosts and monsters, and those who stubbornly adhere to the bourgeois reactionary line to be in a state of terror, soul-shaking, falling over, and scattered like fallen flowers.

Revolutionary great "chaos" is excellent. The result of revolutionary great "chaos" is the loss of all rotten old things, and in the "chaos," we will create a brand-new order of the proletariat and "chaos" out a red communist new world!

Long live the revolutionary great "chaos"!

### “乱”与“变”
孙有纯
1967-02-06
第6版()
专栏：

    “乱”与“变”
    工人　孙有纯
    马克思主义的本质是批判的，革命的。它的基本点就是批判，就是斗争，就是造反，就是革命。
    要批判，要斗争，要造反，要革命，就要“乱”。“乱”，是事物运动的必然规律。革命的“乱”，就是要打碎旧秩序，砸烂旧世界，清除革命前进道路上的一切障碍。革命的“乱”的结果，一定会推动社会向前发展。如果没有革命的“乱”，也就不会有革命的“变”。
    没有八年抗日战争的“乱”，就不可能打败日本帝国主义对中国的侵略。
    没有二十多年国内革命战争的“乱”，就不可能打倒蒋家王朝，建立起人民当家作主的中华人民共和国。
    同样，没有这次无产阶级文化大革命的“乱”，就不可能把那些党内走资本主义道路的当权派，把那些社会上的牛鬼蛇神，把那些顽固地坚持资产阶级反动路线的家伙们揪出来，斗倒、斗垮、斗臭。没有无产阶级文化大革命的“乱”，就不可能清除一切剥削阶级的旧思想，旧文化，旧风俗，旧习惯，而建立起无产阶级的新思想，新文化，新风俗，新习惯。
    “乱”的过程，就是批判的过程，就是斗争的过程，就是造反的过程，就是“变”的过程。
    “独有英雄驱虎豹，更无豪杰怕熊罴。”真正的无产阶级革命造反派是不怕“乱”的。要革命，要斗争就不怕“乱”。怕“乱”，就会不革命，不斗争，而走上调和主义、改良主义、机会主义、修正主义的道路。怕“乱”的人就不是真正的无产阶级革命造反派。
    有些人指责革命造反派“唯恐天下不乱”。对！我们无产阶级革命造反派，就是要让天下大“乱”一场，让革命的风暴怒吼，让革命的巨浪掀天，把那些党内走资本主义道路的当权派，把那些牛鬼蛇神，把那些顽固地坚持资产阶级反动路线的家伙们，“乱”个胆颤心惊，魂不附体，人仰马翻，落花流水。
    革命的大“乱”好得很。革命的大“乱”的结果，失去的只是一切腐朽的旧东西，而在“乱”中我们将创立一个崭新的无产阶级的新秩序，“乱”出一个红彤彤的共产主义的新世界！
    革命的大“乱”万岁！
