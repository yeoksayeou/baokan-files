Title: Implementing the Japan-U.S. Military Alliance Treaty: U.S. Department of Defense Announces Troop Increase in Okinawa
Author:
Date: 1960-06-06
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, Washington, June 4th: The U.S. Department of Defense announced that on June 5th, another batch of U.S. armed forces will depart from the U.S. mainland for Okinawa to strengthen U.S. war deployments in the Western Pacific region.

The news said that this force has 1,900 people and is part of the 82nd Airborne Division.

According to the U.S. Department of Defense, sending this force to Okinawa is to enable the U.S. Armed Forces Commander in the Pacific region to "make a more flexible response in the Western Pacific." This force will become part of the U.S. Army's Pacific strategic reserve force.

According to previous reports in American newspapers, the U.S. troop increase in Okinawa is the initial step in specifically implementing the new U.S.-Japan "Security Treaty."

### 执行日美军事同盟条约  美国防部宣布增兵冲绳

1960-06-06
第5版()
专栏：

    执行日美军事同盟条约
    美国防部宣布增兵冲绳
    据新华社4日讯　华盛顿消息：美国国防部宣布，6月5日将又有一批美国武装部队从美国本土开往冲绳，加强美国在西太平洋地区的战争部署。
    消息说，这支部队拥有一千九百人，是第八十二空运师的一部分。
    据美国国防部说，把这支部队派往冲绳是要使太平洋地区美国武装部队总司令能“在西太平洋作出更灵活的回答”。这支部队将成为美国陆军太平洋战略后备部队的一部分。
    据美国报纸前此报道，美国增兵冲绳是作为具体执行美日新“安全条约”的最初步骤。
