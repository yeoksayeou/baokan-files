Title: U.S. "Aid" is a Tool for Promoting Colonialism - "Pakistan Times" Publishes Article Exposing U.S. and Britain Using "Aid" to Hinder Industrialization of "Recipient" Countries - Demanding the Pakistani Government Refuse U.S. and British "Aid" and Seek True Economic Independence
Author:
Date: 1960-06-08
Page: 7
Xinhua News Agency, Karachi, June 4th - The "Pakistan Times" published an article by Al-Khunja on May 31st, calling on the Pakistani government to reject the "aid" of the United States and Britain and to advance "along the path of true economic independence."

The newspaper pointed out that the United States and Britain are doing everything possible to prevent countries that provide them with agricultural products from industrializing. Their "aid agencies in small countries attempt to place the entire focus on agriculture and similar non-basic industries, thus further leading these small countries down the road of relying on basically industrialized countries."

The newspaper said, "When we seek economic independence, we invoke the words of British economic authorities to support the economic policies we have formulated with the help of British experts and their American allies, thus we fall into a logical error."

The article said, "The drafters of our first five-year plan seemed to confirm that basic industries such as steel and heavy machinery must continue to exist outside our borders indefinitely. When we need a truck, refrigerator, or bicycle, we must beg, borrow, or earn (foreign exchange). When we learn that the price of machinery has doubled in the past few years, while the price of our cotton and jute is almost the same as before, we consult foreign economic experts, and they tell us that, from all perspectives, the best way for us is to try to double the planting volume of cotton, jute, and tea." The article said, "We formulated another five-year plan completely according to the same principle." It seems that we intend to repeat this mistake from the beginning.

The newspaper repeatedly emphasized in this article that what Pakistan urgently needs is to establish an industrial base for electricity, steel, machine tools, heavy machinery, and other industries. The newspaper pointed out, "According to the opinions of some foreign economists, Pakistan must continue to be an 'agricultural country'. If we agree with this view, it will mean that we will forever continue to grow cotton, jute, tea, and oilseeds."

### 美“援”是推销殖民主义的工具  “巴基斯坦时报”发表文章揭露  美英利用“援助”阻挠受“援”国工业化  要求巴政府拒绝接受美英“援助”谋求真正的经济独立

1960-06-08
第7版()
专栏：

    美“援”是推销殖民主义的工具
    “巴基斯坦时报”发表文章揭露
    美英利用“援助”阻挠受“援”国工业化
    要求巴政府拒绝接受美英“援助”谋求真正的经济独立
    新华社卡拉奇4日电　“巴基斯坦时报”5月31日刊载阿尔·罕加写的一篇文章，要求巴基斯坦政府拒绝美国和英国的“援助”，而“沿着真正经济独立的道路”前进。
    这家报纸指出，美国和英国正千方百计地不让那些为它们提供农业产品的国家实行工业化。它们“在小国设立的援助机构企图把整个重点放在农业和类似的非基本工业上，这样就使这些小国进一步走上依靠基本上工业化的国家的道路。”
    这家报纸说，“当我们寻求经济独立的时候，我们援引英国经济学权威的话来支持我们在英国专家和他们的盟友美国人的帮助下所制订的经济政策，这样我们就陷入了一种逻辑上的错误。”
    文章说，“我们第一个五年计划的制订者看来确认，像钢铁和重型机器这样一些基础工业必须无限期地继续存在于我们国境之外，当我们需要一辆卡车、冰箱或自行车的时候，我们必须乞求、借贷、或者赚取（外汇）。当我们获悉机器的价格在过去几年内上涨了一倍，而我们棉花和黄麻的价格几乎还是原来水平的时候，我们请教外国经济专家，而他们告诉我们说，从各方面考虑，对我们来说最好的办法就是设法把棉花、黄麻和茶叶的种植量增加一倍。”文章说，“我们完全按照同样的方针制订了另一个五年计划”。看来又打算从头重复这种错误。
    这家报纸在这篇文章中反复强调，巴基斯坦迫切需要的是建立电力、钢铁、工作母机、重型机器等工业基础。这家报纸指出，“根据某些外国经济学家的意见，巴基斯坦必须继续是一个‘农业国家’。如果我们同意这种看法，这将意味着，我们将永远继续种植棉花、黄麻、茶叶和油籽。”
