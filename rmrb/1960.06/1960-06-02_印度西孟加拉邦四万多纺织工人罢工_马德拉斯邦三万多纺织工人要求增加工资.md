Title: More Than 40,000 Textile Workers in West Bengal, India, Go on Strike; More Than 30,000 Textile Workers in Madras State Demand Wage Increases
Author:
Date: 1960-06-02
Page: 7

    Xinhua News Agency, New Delhi, June 1st - According to a report from Calcutta by the Indian News Service, more than 45,000 textile workers in West Bengal held a one-day strike on May 30th, demanding wage increases.

    It is reported that these workers belong to twenty-six cotton textile factories in six districts of West Bengal. Due to the impact of this one-day strike, more than 800,000 yards of cloth were lost from a total of 10,000 looms.

    According to an earlier report by the same news agency, 8,500 textile workers in Rajasthan held a one-day strike on May 20th for the same reason.

    According to the Press Trust of India report from Coimbatore, 32,000 textile workers in Madras State notified one hundred employers on May 31st that if the workers' demands for the implementation of the Textile Industry Wage Board's recommendations are not met by June 14th, they will begin an indefinite strike on that day.

### 印度西孟加拉邦四万多纺织工人罢工  马德拉斯邦三万多纺织工人要求增加工资

1960-06-02
第7版()
专栏：

    印度西孟加拉邦四万多纺织工人罢工
    马德拉斯邦三万多纺织工人要求增加工资
    新华社新德里1日电　据印度新闻社从加尔各答报道，西孟加拉邦的四万五千多名纺织工人在5月30日举行了一天的罢工，要求增加工资。
    据报道，这些工人属于西孟加拉邦六个县的二十六家棉纺织厂。由于这一天的罢工的影响，一万台织布机共少生产了八十多万码布。
    据这家通讯社早些时候报道，拉贾斯坦邦八千五百名纺织工人在5月20日由于同样原因举行了一天的罢工。
    据印度报业托辣斯自哥印拜陀报道，马德拉斯邦三万二千名纺织工人在5月31日通知一百家雇主说，如果在6月14日以前不满足工人们提出的实行纺织业工资委员会的建议的要求，就将在那一天开始举行无限期的罢工。
