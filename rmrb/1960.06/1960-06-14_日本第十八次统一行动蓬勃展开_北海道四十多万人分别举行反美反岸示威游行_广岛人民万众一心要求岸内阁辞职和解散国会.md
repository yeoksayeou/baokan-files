Title: Japan's 18th Unified Action Vigorously Unfolds; Over 400,000 People in Hokkaido Hold Anti-American, Anti-Kishi Demonstrations; Hiroshima People United in Demanding Kishi Cabinet Resignation and Dissolution of the Diet
Author:
Date: 1960-06-14
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, Tokyo, June 13th: A deafening anti-American, anti-Kishi wave is sweeping across Japan.

    On June 11th, the first day of the 18th National Unified Action, over 400,000 people in Hokkaido held rallies and demonstrations in 150 locations, demanding the resignation of the Kishi Nobusuke cabinet, immediate dissolution of the Diet, and strongly opposing Eisenhower's "visit" to Japan.

    In Sapporo, over 30,000 workers, professors, students, businessmen, religious figures, and citizens held a "Crush the Security Treaty Rally," unanimously passing a resolution firmly opposing the American president's "visit" to Japan and supporting the workers' planned second strike on June 15th. The resolution demanded the cessation of ratification of the new "Security Treaty" and demanded the resignation of the Kishi Nobusuke cabinet and immediate dissolution of the Diet.

    In Kyushu, southern Japan, over 10,000 workers and citizens in Fukuoka City held a general mobilization rally, unanimously passing a resolution protesting against the activities of the American president's press secretary, Hagerty, in Japan, opposing Eisenhower's visit to Japan, and protesting against Kishi Nobusuke's tyrannical act of forcibly "passing" the new "Security Treaty." That evening, eighteen cultural organizations jointly held a citizens' rally demanding the dissolution of the Diet. The 4,000 intellectuals who attended the rally marched through the streets with lanterns after the meeting. Prior to this, many university professors braved strong winds and heavy rain to distribute leaflets on the streets to citizens, opposing the Japan-U.S. military alliance treaty, opposing Eisenhower's "visit" to Japan, and demanding the resignation of the Kishi Nobusuke government.

    The people of Nagoya began the 18th Unified Action a day early, on the 10th. On this day, over 60,000 workers and citizens held the Aichi Prefecture Central Rally in Tsuruma Park. As the rally was in progress, the radio broadcast the real-time situation of thousands of angry people in Tokyo surrounding Hagerty at Haneda International Airport and staging strong protests, and the venue resounded with thunderous and lasting applause and cheers. After the rally, all participants marched through the streets with lanterns.

    The people of Hiroshima City held a massive general mobilization rally in Peace Park on the afternoon of the 11th. Over 10,000 attendees unanimously passed a declaration protesting against the Kishi Nobusuke cabinet's trampling on democracy and forcibly voting on the new "Security Treaty," demanding the collective resignation of the Kishi Nobusuke cabinet and the immediate dissolution of the Diet, and opposing Eisenhower's "visit" to Japan. After the rally, the participants held a demonstration march through the streets.

    In Kochi Prefecture, the High School Student Association Federation, composed of students from twenty-nine senior high schools, decided at its regular meeting held on the 10th and 11th that Kochi Prefecture high school students would launch a movement to overthrow the Kishi Nobusuke cabinet and oppose the "Security Treaty." The movement policy adopted by the conference stipulated that students would hold a strike on the 15th to support the workers' second strike and participate in the 18th Unified Action.

    According to the "Asahi Shimbun" report, fifty Christian pastors and followers in Tokyo held an all-night prayer meeting on the 12th to oppose the ratification of the new "Security Treaty" and defend peace.

    One hundred professors and lecturers from eleven universities in the Kansai region held a "Kansai Scholars and Researchers' Rally to Defend Democracy" in Osaka on the 11th, unanimously passing a statement demanding the resignation of the Kishi Nobusuke cabinet, the dissolution of the Diet, and opposing Eisenhower's "visit" to Japan.

### 日本第十八次统一行动蓬勃展开  北海道四十多万人分别举行反美反岸示威游行  广岛人民万众一心要求岸内阁辞职和解散国会

1960-06-14
第5版()
专栏：

    日本第十八次统一行动蓬勃展开
    北海道四十多万人分别举行反美反岸示威游行
    广岛人民万众一心要求岸内阁辞职和解散国会
    新华社13日讯　东京消息：日本全国掀起震天动地的反美反岸浪潮。
    在第十八次全国统一行动的第一天——11日，北海道的四十多万人民在一百五十个地方举行大会和示威游行，要求岸信介内阁辞职、立即解散国会，强烈反对艾森豪威尔“访问”日本。
    在札幌市，三万多名工人、教授、学生、商人、宗教家和市民举行了“粉碎安全条约大会”，一致通过决议，坚决表示反对美国总统“访问”日本，支持工人预定在6月15日举行的第二次罢工。决议要求停止批准新“安全条约”，并要求岸信介内阁辞职和立即解散国会。
    在日本南部的九州，福冈市的一万多工人和市民举行了总奋起大会，一致通过决议抗议美国总统新闻秘书哈格蒂到日本活动，反对艾森豪威尔访日，抗议岸信介强行“通过”新“安全条约”的暴行。当天傍晚，文化界的十八个团体联合举行了要求解散国会市民大会。与会的四千名知识分子在会后提着灯笼到街上举行了游行。在这以前，许多大学教授们冒着强风大雨到街上向市民散发了反对日美军事同盟条约、反对艾森豪威尔“访问”日本、要求岸信介政府下台的传单。
    名古屋人民提前一天在10日开始展开第十八次统一行动。这一天，六万多名工人和市民在鹤舞公园举行了爱知县中央大会。当大会进行时，广播电台广播了东京成千上万的愤怒群众在羽田国际机场把哈格蒂包围起来进行强烈抗议的实况，会场响起了春雷般的经久不息的掌声和欢呼声。大会结束后，全体与会者提着灯笼到街上举行游行。
    广岛市人民11日下午在和平公园举行了声势浩大的总奋起大会。一万多名与会者一致通过大会宣言，抗议岸信介内阁践踏民主强行表决新“安全条约”的暴行，要求岸信介内阁全体辞职和立即解散国会，反对艾森豪威尔“访问”日本。大会结束后与会者到街上举行了示威游行。
    在高知县，由二十九个高级中学学生组成的高中学生会联合会，在10日和11日举行的定期大会决定，高知县高中学生将展开运动，打倒岸信介内阁，反对“安全条约”。大会通过的运动方针规定，学生们将在15日举行罢课，支援工人的第二次罢工和参加第十八次统一行动。
    据“朝日新闻”报道，东京的五十名基督教牧师和教徒12日举行通宵的祷告，反对批准新“安全条约”、保卫和平。
    关西地区的十一个大学的一百名教授和讲师，11日在大阪举行“关西学者研究家保卫民主大会”，一致通过一项声明，要求岸信介内阁下台，解散国会，反对艾森豪威尔“访问”日本。
