Title: The crimes of the Kishi Nobusuke group are intolerable; the Japanese cultural and educational circles utter a roar; Eisenhower's visit to Japan at this time will arouse even greater anger among the people

Author: 
Date: 1960-06-07
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, Tokyo, June 5th: More and more people in Japanese cultural and educational circles are demanding the resignation of the Kishi Nobusuke cabinet and the dissolution of the Diet.

　　On the 4th, people from cultural and journalistic circles submitted 70,000 petitions to the Diet, opposing the ratification of the Japan-U.S. military alliance treaty and demanding the dissolution of the Diet and the resignation of the Kishi Nobusuke cabinet.

　　Fifty professors from ten universities, including Osaka University of Economics, Osaka City University, and Osaka Gakugei University, issued a joint statement on the 4th, demanding the resignation of the Kishi Nobusuke cabinet and the dissolution of the Diet. The statement pointed out that the measures taken by the Kishi government in the Diet on May 20 were an intolerable atrocity that trampled on democracy.

　　The "Japan French Literature Society," composed of scholars who study French literature, held a conference in Tokyo on the 4th and issued a statement in the name of all 400 members attending the meeting, opposing the new "Security Treaty" "passed" by the House of Representatives on May 20, and demanding the immediate dissolution of the Diet.

　　More than 300 physicians attending the 33rd meeting of the Japan Association of Industrial Health in Matsumoto City issued a joint statement on the 4th, demanding the overall resignation of the Kishi Cabinet and the immediate dissolution of the Diet.

Xinhua News Agency, Tokyo, June 4th: Japanese cultural and educational figures have successively issued statements demanding the overall resignation of the Kishi Nobusuke cabinet and the immediate dissolution of the Diet.

　　Shin-izumi Ohama, the president of Waseda University, and 286 faculty members jointly issued a statement today saying: "Extending the Diet session and forcibly voting on the new (Japan-U.S.) Security Treaty is a disregard for Diet work conventions and democratic principles, and we cannot agree to this kind of vote. In order to defend democracy, (we) demand the overall resignation of the Kishi cabinet and the immediate dissolution of the Diet."

　　This statement was announced at the founding meeting of the "Waseda University Defend Democracy Association" held this morning.

　　After the meeting, the faculty members of Waseda University went to the Diet to demonstrate and participated in the national rally of the 17th National Unified Action.

　　Hideki Yukawa, a famous Japanese scientist, issued a statement on the 3rd saying, "Like the majority of the people, I believe that the Diet must be dissolved immediately to listen to the voices of all the people; there is no other way to save today's crisis except this method."

　　Masatoshi Matsushita, the president of Rikkyo University, and 20 professors also jointly issued a statement on the 3rd, demanding the immediate dissolution of the Diet and holding general elections.

　　The 106 professors and lecturers of Ochanomizu Women's University jointly issued a statement on the 3rd, strongly demanding the overall resignation of the Kishi Nobusuke cabinet and the immediate dissolution of the Diet.

　　At the same time, the voices of Japanese cultural and educational circles opposing Eisenhower's visit to Japan are becoming louder and louder. The organization of 1,600 teachers from 65 elementary schools, middle schools, and kindergartens in Kumamoto City yesterday and today directly sent letters and telegrams to Eisenhower, opposing his visit to Japan. The letters explained the current situation of the Japanese people's opposition to the new "Security Treaty" and protests against the Kishi Nobusuke cabinet, and wrote that Eisenhower's "visit to Japan under these circumstances will make the people even more angry."

Xinhua News Agency, Tokyo, June 4th: More and more university professors across Japan are joining the anti-American and anti-Kishi patriotic struggle.

　　Approximately 700 professors, associate professors, and lecturers at the University of Tokyo jointly issued a statement on the 3rd, resolutely demanding the immediate dissolution of the Diet and declaring the separate vote on the new Japan-U.S. "Security Treaty" invalid.

　　The statement said that the Kishi Nobusuke group's forced "passage" of the new Japan-U.S. "Security Treaty" is a disregard for parliamentary principles.

　　The number of faculty members who signed the statement is equal to 80% of the university's entire faculty.

　　The faculty group of Miyazaki University issued a statement on the 3rd opposing the "Security Treaty," demanding the resignation of the Kishi Nobusuke cabinet and the dissolution of the Diet, and sent the statement to Nobusuke Kishi, the speakers of both houses of the Diet, and the U.S. Embassy. Among the professors at Miyazaki University, a signature campaign against the "Security Treaty" has been underway since the end of last month. Of the school's 172 professors and associate professors, 143 have signed.

　　On the 2nd, professors from nine universities in Japan issued statements strongly demanding the resignation of the Kishi Nobusuke cabinet and the dissolution of the Diet, opposing the ratification of the Japan-U.S. military alliance treaty. These universities are: Hokkaido University, Tohoku University, Nagoya University, Kanagawa University, Nara Women's University, Kagoshima University, Chiba University, Kochi University, Ritsumeikan University.

　　In addition, eight cultural organizations in Nagasaki City today also issued a statement demanding the overall resignation of the Kishi Cabinet and the dissolution of the Diet.

### 对岸信介集团的罪行忍  无可忍日本文化教育界发出吼声  艾森豪威尔在目前访日将会激起人民更大愤怒

1960-06-07
第6版()
专栏：

    对岸信介集团的罪行忍
    无可忍日本文化教育界发出吼声
    艾森豪威尔在目前访日将会激起人民更大愤怒
    新华社5日讯　东京消息：日本越来越多的文化教育界人士要求岸信介内阁辞职和解散国会。
    4日，文化界、新闻界人士向国会递交了七万份请愿书，反对批准日美军事同盟条约、要求解散国会和岸信介内阁下台。
    大阪经济大学、大阪市立大学和大阪学艺大学等十个大学的五十位教授4日发表联合声明，要求岸信介内阁总辞职和解散国会。声明指出，岸政府5月20日在国会所采取的措施是蹂躏民主的不可容许的暴行。
    由研究法国文学的学者组成的“日本法国文学会”4日在东京举行大会，并以四百名全体与会会员名义发表声明，反对众议院在5月20日“通过”的新“安全条约”，并要求立即解散国会。
    正在松本市参加日本产业医学会第三十三次会议的三百多名医师4日发表联合声明，要求岸内阁全体辞职，立即解散国会。
    新华社4日讯　东京消息：日本文教界人士纷纷发表声明，要求岸信介内阁全体辞职和立即解散国会。
    早稻田大学校长大滨信泉和教员等二百八十六人今天联名发表声明说：“延长国会会期和强行表决新（日美）安全条约，是无视国会工作贯例和民主原则的，不能同意这种表决。为了保卫民主，（我们）要求岸内阁全体辞职和立即解散国会。”
    这项声明是在今天上午举行的“早稻田大学保卫民主会”成立大会上宣布的。
    早稻田大学的教员们在会后前往国会示威，并且参加了第十七次全国统一行动的国民集会。
    日本著名科学家汤川秀树3日发表声明说，“同多数人民一样，我认为必须立即解散国会，听取全体人民的声音；除了这种方法以外，没有别的方法能够拯救今天的危机。”
    立教大学的校长松下正寿和二十名教授也在3日联名发表声明，要求立即解散国会，举行大选。
    御茶水女子大学的一百零六位教授和讲师3日联合发表的声明，强烈要求岸信介内阁全体辞职，立即解散国会。
    同时，日本文教界反对艾森豪威尔访问日本的呼声也越来越响亮。熊本市内小学、中学、幼儿园等六十五所学校的一千六百位教师的组织昨天和今天直接寄信和打电报给艾森豪威尔，反对他访问日本。信里说明了目前日本人民反对新“安全条约”和抗议岸信介内阁的斗争情况，并且写道，艾森豪威尔“在这种情况下访问日本，将会使国民更加愤怒。”
    新华社4日讯　东京消息：日本全国各地越来越多的大学教授投入了反美反岸的爱国斗争。
    东京大学约有七百名教授、副教授、讲师3日联合发表声明，坚决要求立即解散国会，宣布单独表决新日美“安全条约”无效。
    声明说，岸信介集团强行“通过”新日美“安全条约”，这是忽视议会原则的。
    在声明上签名的教员人数等于这个大学的全体教员的80％。
    宫崎大学的教授团3日发表了反对“安全条约”、要求岸信介内阁下台和解散国会的声明，并且将这份声明分别寄给岸信介、国会众参两院议长和美国大使馆。在宫崎大学的教授中，从上月底来一直在展开反对“安全条约”的签名运动。在全校的一百七十二位教授和副教授中，签名的共有一百四十三人。
    在2日这一天，日本共有九个大学的教授发表声明，强烈要求岸信介内阁下台和解散国会，反对批准日美军事同盟条约。这些大学是：北海道大学、东北大学、名古屋大学、神奈川大学、奈良女子大学、鹿儿岛大学、千叶大学、高知大学、立命馆大学。
    另外，长崎市的八个文化团体，今天也发表了要求岸内阁总辞职和解散国会的声明。
