Title: Eisenhower's Forced Postponement of Visit to Japan Stuns Britain and France; London Believes American Policy Suffered Setback; Paris Feels Japanese Political Situation is Serious
Author:
Date: 1960-06-17
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, June 16th - According to Agence France-Presse, London: The news of Eisenhower's forced postponement of his visit to Japan "caused a stir" in London. British evening newspapers today reported the news on the front page with large headlines. The British Foreign Office avoided making any official comment on the incident. However, Foreign Office sources privately expressed concern about the possible impact of the situation in Japan on other regions of the Far East. John Earl, the chief diplomatic correspondent for Reuters, which is close to the British government, said in a report: London regards Eisenhower's postponement of his visit to Japan as a setback for American policy.

According to Reuters, Paris, French government officials believe that the Japanese government's request for Eisenhower to postpone his visit to Japan "reflects the severity of the internal political tensions in Japan." French Foreign Ministry spokesmen declined to comment publicly on the event, but they did not conceal privately that they believed this would "have a far-reaching impact on current American defense plans in the Pacific region."

### 艾森豪威尔被迫延期访日轰动英法两国  伦敦认为美国政策遭受挫折  巴黎感到日本政治局势严重

1960-06-17
第5版()
专栏：

    艾森豪威尔被迫延期访日轰动英法两国
    伦敦认为美国政策遭受挫折
    巴黎感到日本政治局势严重
    新华社16日讯　据法新社伦敦消息：艾森豪威尔被迫推迟访日的消息在伦敦“引起了一阵轰动”，英国的晚报今天都以大字标题在头版报道了这一消息。英国外交部避免就这一事件发表正式评论。但是，外交部人士私下对于日本的形势对远东其他地区可能产生的影响表示不安。接近英国官方的路透社首席外交记者约翰·厄尔在报道中说：伦敦把艾森豪威尔推迟访问日本看作是美国政策所遭受的一次挫折。
    另据路透社巴黎的消息说，法国政府人士认为，日本政府要求艾森豪威尔延期访问日本“反映了日本内部政治上的紧张的严重程度”。法国外交部发言人不愿公开评论这一事件，但是他们在私下里却并不掩饰，他们认为，这将“对美国目前在太平洋地区的防御计划产生深远的影响”。
