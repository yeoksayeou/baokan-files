Title: Further Discussion on "Mutual Criticism"
Author: Zheng Zhiqiang
Date: 1949-10-20
Page: 6

　　Further Discussion on "Mutual Criticism"
　　Worker at Chaoyang Station Zheng Zhiqiang

　　Although everyone inevitably has shortcomings and errors, some people often exaggerate their own small strengths or small achievements, becoming complacent and self-satisfied. They know about shortcomings and errors but pretend not to know, or use various conditions and difficulties to cover up errors and excuse themselves. Such people will not move forward, but only fall behind. A strong revolutionary youth who serves the people must always discover problems in work and study, see their own shortcomings and errors, and only when they have this sense of responsibility towards others can they conduct mutual criticism.
    　　　　　※　　　　　　　　　　※　　　　　　　　　　　※
    Neither in criticism nor self-criticism should major shortcomings or errors be deliberately portrayed as unimportant minor matters, nor should minor errors be deliberately exaggerated into major problems. Sometimes, the same thing, under certain conditions, is a major error, while under other conditions it may be relatively small. We need to give it an appropriate analysis during criticism. Some people do not distinguish between these, and when criticizing themselves, they minimize major issues, while when criticizing others, they exaggerate minor issues. This is not a correct attitude.
    　　　　　※　　　　　　　　　　※　　　　　　　　　　　※
    After criticism, solutions to the problems should be proposed. For various shortcomings and errors, the causes should be found. Is it conscious? Unconscious? Is it consistent? Or accidental? Is there a problem with the method? Is the effort insufficient? It is necessary to find the main reason, and then everyone should study and come up with ways to overcome shortcomings and correct errors, that is, to make oneself or others understand where the shortcomings and errors come from, know what things are wrong, and know how to do them correctly.
    　　　　　※　　　　　　　　　　※　　　　　　　　　　　※
    When criticizing others, it is necessary to distinguish the different objects, so that the criticism is appropriate. If the other party has not the slightest awareness of their own mistakes, they should be gradually guided to have some awareness of this mistake before criticizing them head-on. Do not launch a major criticism all at once, which will make it difficult for them to accept your opinion. When you criticize, you should have a sincere, frank, and well-intentioned attitude in order to be effective.

### 再谈“互相批评”
郑志强
1949-10-20
第6版()
专栏：

　　再谈“互相批评”
    朝阳车站工人  郑志强
    虽然任何人都难免有缺点和错误，但是有些人常常把自己某些小的长处或一点小的成就夸大起来，自满自足，对于缺点和错误他知道而装作不知，或是用各种的条件困难来掩饰错误，原谅自己，象这样人不会前进的，只有落伍的，一个坚强为人民服务的革命青年要经常在工作、学习中发现问题，看出自己的缺点和错误，究竟在那里，而对于别人也如此有责任感才能进行互相批评。
    　　　　　※　　　　　　　　　　※　　　　　　　　　　　※
    批评和自我批评都不要把大的缺点或错误，故意说成不重要的小事，也不要把一点小错误，故意夸成重大的问题，有时同样一件事情，在这种条件下是重大的错误，而在另一种条件下却可能比较小一点，这都须要我们在批评的，来给他一个适当的分析，有的人不区别这些，而且在批评自己时，把大事化小，在批评别人时又把小事夸大，这是不正确的态度。
    　　　　　※　　　　　　　　　　※　　　　　　　　　　　※
    在批评以后，要提出解决问题的办法，对于各种缺点与错误要找出产生的原因，是自觉的？不自觉的？是一贯性的？抑是偶然的？方法上有问题？是努力不够？必需找出主要的原因，然后要大家研究出来克服缺点，和改正错误的办法，也就是说，好使自己或别人要晓得缺点和错误，是由那里产生的，晓得那些事情是不对的，而且知道怎样作才是对的。
    　　　　　※　　　　　　　　　　※　　　　　　　　　　　※
    在对别人批评的时候，要分清他不同的对象，使批评恰到好处，如果对方对他自己的错误还没有丝毫的认识，就应该慢慢的引他对这错误有些自觉以后，再正面的批评他，切不要一下就大发批评，反而使他不易接受你的意见。你在批评的时候，要有诚恳、坦白、与人为善的态度，才会有效果。
