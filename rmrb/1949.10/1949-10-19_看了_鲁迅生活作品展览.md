Title: Visited the "Lu Xun Life and Works Exhibition"
Author: Zhujun
Date: 1949-10-19
Page: 6

Visited the "Lu Xun Life and Works Exhibition"
    Zhujun

    In welcoming the thirteenth anniversary of Mr. Lu Xun's death, the National Beijing Library held the "Lu Xun Life and Works Exhibition," which should be a very meaningful exhibition today.

    Outside the exhibition hall, it is clearly written: A passage about Mr. Lu Xun from "Chairman Mao's Speech at the Yan’an Forum on Literature and Art":

    "All Communists, all revolutionaries, all revolutionary literary and art workers should learn from Lu Xun's example and be 'oxen' for the proletariat and the masses, dedicating themselves to the utmost until their dying day."

    Upon entering the exhibition hall, what is most likely to catch one's attention should be the large portraits of Mr. Lu Xun and Gorky hanging on the wall, as well as a photograph of Mr. Lu Xun and Gorky together.

    The exhibits can be roughly divided into two parts: one part belongs to texts, and the other part belongs to photographs.

    In terms of text displays, they include: Mr. Lu Xun's manuscripts, letters, literary works, translations, contributions to art, edited publications, as well as various Russian, English, and Japanese translations and critical biographies of Mr. Lu Xun's works. In addition, to give the younger generation a clearer understanding of Mr. Lu Xun, his fighting life is divided into two stages, namely: Mr. Lu Xun in the old democratic period (1881-1917), and Mr. Lu Xun in the new democratic period (1918-1936). At the same time, there are also "Mr. Lu Xun's Remarks during the March 18th Incident" and various records of "Mr. Lu Xun joining the Three Leagues (Left-Wing Writers League, League for Civil Rights, League for Freedom)."

    In terms of photographs: it includes almost all of Mr. Lu Xun's important activities from when he was studying in Japan until his funeral. In particular, several photos of Mr. Lu Xun's former residence make people feel as if Mr. Lu Xun is still among us, and one cannot help but feel a sense of attachment.

    In addition, the wall also displays: a list of Mr. Lu Xun's translations and a list of his pen names, so that we can gain a very systematic understanding of Mr. Lu Xun's life's translations and pen names.

    This exhibition greatly helps us to understand the various aspects of this generation's giant – Mr. Lu Xun. From morning to night, there is a constant stream of visitors, and everyone looks so carefully, not letting go of even the smallest character.

    In the comment book, everyone hopes that after this exhibition, the Beijing Library will be able to open a Lu Xun special room or research room, which is naturally a good idea, but it reminds me of what Mr. Li Guangtian said in "Commemorating Mr. Lu Xun" (see the "Zhongjian" semi-monthly, Volume 1, Issue 7, published on October 20th last year):

    "To commemorate Mr. Lu Xun, we must learn from Mr. Lu Xun in practice. Research work is important, but Mr. Lu Xun is not an antique; it is not enough for us to 'research him once.' We must transform his fighting spirit into our own fighting spirit. We must not only accept it but also promote it. We must make Mr. Lu Xun continue to grow among us in the practice of life and the practice of art."

    This is right. We must not only know and study Mr. Lu Xun, but also learn from Mr. Lu Xun, and the main thing is to be able to "transform his fighting spirit into our own fighting spirit." After returning from seeing the "Lu Xun Life and Works Exhibition," I hastily wrote these things, and I am willing to encourage and learn together with young friends. In this way, this exhibition will truly have enlightenment and meaning for us.

    　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　1949, October, 13.

### 看了“鲁迅生活作品展览”
竹均
1949-10-19
第6版()
专栏：

　　看了“鲁迅生活作品展览”
    竹  均
    迎接着鲁迅先生逝世十三周年纪念，国立北京图书馆举办了“鲁迅生活作品展览”，这在今日，应该是一个很有意义的展览。
    在展览室的外面，很明显的，写着：“毛主席在延安文艺座谈会上的讲话”中关于鲁迅先生的一段：
    “一切共产党员，一切革命家，一切革命的文艺工作者，都应该学鲁迅的榜样，做无产阶级和人民大众的‘牛’，鞠躬尽粹，死而后已。”
    走进了展览室，最容易引人注意的，应该是墙上悬挂着的鲁迅先生和高尔基的大画像，还有鲁迅先生和高尔基的合影。
    展览品，大致可以分为两个部分：一部分是属于文字的，另一部分则是属于照片的。
    在文字展出方面，包括有：鲁迅先生的手稿、手札、书简、文学作品、译品、对艺术的供献、编辑的刊物，以及鲁迅先生作品的俄文、英文、日文各种译本和评传。此外，为了使年青的一代，对于鲁迅先生更清楚的认识，将先生战斗的一生，划分了两个阶段，那就是：旧民主主义时期的先生（一八八一——一九一七），和新民主主义时期的先生（一九一八—一九三六），同时，还有就是“三一八时候鲁迅先生的言论”，和“鲁迅先生加入三盟（左冀联盟，自由同盟，民权同盟）”时候的各种记述。
    在照片展出方面：包括了从鲁迅先生在日本读书时起，直到死后大殓止，一生重要的活动，差不多都汇集一起了，尤其是先生故居的几幅照片，使人看了，仿佛觉得先生仍在人间，而不胜依恋之情。
    另外，墙上还悬挂着有：先生的译著书目表，和笔名表，使我们对于先生一生的译著和笔名，可以得到一个很有系统的认识。
    这一个展览，帮助我们对于这一代巨人——鲁迅先生各方面的认识，都是很多的。参观的人，从早到晚，始终不断，而且每一个人，都是那样仔细的看，就是很小的一个字，也不肯轻轻放过。
    在批评簿上，大家都希望北京图书馆在这一次展览以后，能够开辟鲁迅专室或研究室，这意思自然是很好的，但因此，我却记起了李广田先生在“纪念鲁迅先生”（见去年十月二十日出版的“中建”半月刊一卷七期）一文中的话来，他说：
    “纪念鲁迅先生，我们必须在实践中学习鲁迅先生。研究工作是重要的，然而鲁迅先生并非古董，不是只要我们“研究一番就算事的，我必须把他的战斗精神，变成我们自己的战斗精神，我们不但要接受它，而且要发扬它，必须在生活的实践和艺术的实践中，使鲁迅先生在我们中间继续生长。”
    这话是对的，我们不仅要认识先生，研究先生，更要学习先生，主要的是能够“把他的战斗精神，变成我们自己的战斗精神”。看完了“鲁迅生活作品展览”归来，拉杂的写了这些，愿意和青年朋友们共同勉励，共同学习。这样，这一次的展览，对于我们，才真的有了启示，也才真的有了意义。
    　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　一九四九，十，十三。
