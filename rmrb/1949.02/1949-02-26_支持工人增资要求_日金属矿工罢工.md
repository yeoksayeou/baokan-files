Title: Supporting Workers' Wage Increase Demands: Japanese Metal Miners Strike
Author:
Date: 1949-02-26
Page: 3

　　Supporting Workers' Wage Increase Demands
  　Japanese Metal Miners Strike
    [Xinhua News Agency, Northern Shaanxi, 24th] According to a AFP dispatch from Tokyo: The All-Japan Metal Mine Workers' Union launched a five-day strike on the 21st to support workers' demands for wage increases and to oppose the government's failure to fulfill its promise last December to issue subsidies totaling 430 million yen. On the first day, 20,000 miners participated in the strike, which will be held successively in five regions: Tokyo, northern Honshu, Osaka, Hokkaido, and Kyushu, with each strike lasting 24 hours.
    Furthermore, according to a bulletin released by the Japanese government's Ministry of Labor, in December of last year, a total of 257 strikes occurred nationwide in Japan (an increase of 28 compared to November), with more than 1,236,000 workers participating in the strikes.

### 支持工人增资要求　日金属矿工罢工

1949-02-26
第3版()
专栏：

　　支持工人增资要求
  　日金属矿工罢工
    【新华社陕北二十四日电】据法新社东京讯：全日本金属矿工工会已于二十一日发动五天罢工，以支持工人增加工资的要求，并反对政府迄今仍不履行其在去年十二月所约许的发给津贴共四亿三千万日元的诺言。参加罢工的矿工，第一天已达二万人，罢工将在东京、本州北部、大坂、北海道及九州五个地区相继举行，每一罢工将历时二十四小时。
    又讯：据日本政府劳工省公报透露，去年十二月份，日本全国共发生罢工二百五十七次（较十一月份增加二十八次），参加罢工的工人共一百二十三万六千余人。
