Title: Regulations on Preferential Treatment for Revolutionary Servicemen Sacrificed in the North China Region
Author:
Date: 1949-02-10
Page: 2

　　Regulations on Preferential Treatment for Revolutionary Servicemen Sacrificed in the North China Region
    Formulated by the North China People's Government
    Promulgated on January 25th, 38th Year of the Republic of China

    Article 1　All commanders and fighters of the People's Liberation Army (including field armies, local armies, detached guerrilla forces, rear military organs, and other personnel in the military system who have obtained military status), who are sacrificed in combat, due to participation in war, or due to public service, or killed by the enemy (such as those who are captured and unyielding and die with righteousness, or assassinated by special agents), shall be called martyrs. Their family members shall be called families of martyrs (referred to as martyr families). All shall enjoy the treatment stipulated in these regulations.

    Article 2　The martyr families referred to in these regulations refer to the direct relatives of the martyr who live in the same household and live together, such as parents (some parents who have separated may also be considered martyr families), wife (or husband), children, and siblings under the age of sixteen, as well as other relatives who raised the martyr and now must rely on the martyr for their livelihood.

    Article 3　After a revolutionary serviceman is sacrificed, the unit to which he belonged shall properly bury him; and the name, native place, age, and position of the martyr shall be engraved with bricks and stones (or written with ink on wooden tablets when impossible) and erected in front of the tomb as a marker; and the list of names shall be submitted to the village government near the burial site for registration and preservation. The coffin and burial expenses shall be within the range of 500 to 600 catties of millet in the plains and 400 to 500 catties of millet in the mountains, with actual expenses reimbursed.

    Article 4　The martyr families shall be issued the "Glorious Certificate for Families of Revolutionary Sacrificed Servicemen" by the government, and a one-time consolation payment shall be issued according to the following regulations:

    (1) Squad leader and soldier level: 600 catties of millet.
    (2) Company, platoon, and battalion level: 800 catties of millet.
    (3) Regiment level and above: 1,000 catties of millet.

    The above-mentioned martyr family consolation payment shall be received in order by one of the following family members: (1) Wife (or husband) residing at home; (2) Children residing at home; (3) Parents; (4) Siblings under the age of sixteen; (5) Other relatives who raised the martyr and now need to rely on the martyr for their livelihood; (6) Direct relatives who participate in work. If there are no such martyr families, no payment will be made.

    Article 5　Martyr families shall enjoy the treatment of general military families, and shall be given priority over military families under the same conditions when enjoying preferential treatment.

    Article 6　In order to commend the deeds of martyrs, various places may establish martyr monuments, towers, pavilions, ancestral halls, forests, martyr tombs, etc. And the Qingming Festival is designated as the Martyr's Tomb Sweeping Festival. Governments at all levels may organize martyr deeds compilation committees to be responsible for collecting and compiling heroic deeds of martyrs. Governments above the administrative office level may also set up special personnel to be responsible for this investigation and collection work.

    Article 7　Martyr consolation procedures: After a martyr is sacrificed, the political organ above the regiment level of the unit to which he belonged shall fill out a "Certificate of Sacrifice of Revolutionary Serviceman" and send it to the county government of the martyr's place of origin for transfer to the martyr's family. After receiving the above-mentioned certificate, the martyr's family may hold the certificate to the county government to receive the consolation payment and the "Glorious Certificate for Families of Revolutionary Sacrificed Servicemen" (the glorious certificate will not be issued for those who died of illness). The county government shall be responsible for review, and only relatives who meet the requirements of Article 2 of these regulations may be issued the certificate.

    Article 8　Those who die from overwork and illness in revolutionary work shall not be called martyrs; however, they shall be properly buried in accordance with the provisions of Article 3 of these regulations, and the political organ above the regiment level of the unit to which they belonged shall fill out a "Certificate of Deceased Serviceman" with reference to the provisions of Article 7, and the county government of the place of origin shall issue a one-time consolation payment to their families according to the following regulations:

    (1) Regiment level and above: 750 catties of millet.
    (2) Company, platoon, and battalion level: 600 catties of millet.
    (3) Squad leader and soldier level: 450 catties of millet.

    The order of family members receiving the payment shall be in accordance with the provisions of Article 4 of these regulations.

    Article 9　These regulations shall come into effect from the date of promulgation. The Regulations on Preferential Treatment for Families of Martyrs promulgated by the former Shanxi-Hebei-Shandong-Henan Border Region Government, the Shanxi-Chahar-Hebei Border Region Administrative Committee, and governments at all levels under their jurisdiction shall be repealed.

### 华北区革命军人牺牲褒恤条例

1949-02-10
第2版()
专栏：

　　华北区革命军人牺牲褒恤条例
    华北人民政府制定
    中华民国三十八年一月廿五日公布
    第一条　凡人民解放军的指战员（包括野战军、地方军、脱离生产的游击队，后方各军事机关及军事系统的其他取得军籍的人员），因作战参战牺牲，或因公牺牲，或被敌杀害（如被俘不屈慷慨就义，被特务暗杀等）者均称烈士。其家属称烈士家属（简称烈属）。均得享受本条例规定之待遇。
    第二条　本条例所称烈属系指与烈士同居一家过活之父母（有的父母虽已分居亦得以烈属论）、妻（或夫）、子女等直系亲属与十六岁以下之弟妹、及抚养烈士长大而现在又必须依靠烈士生活之其他亲属而言。
    第三条　革命军人牺牲后，应由所在部队妥为安葬；并用砖石镌刻（不可能时用木牌墨写）烈士姓名、籍贯年岁职务等，竖在墓前以作标志；并将其名单交由埋葬就近村政府登记保存。棺葬费在平原地得在小米五百市斤至六百市斤，在山地得在四百市斤至五百市斤范围内，实报实销。
    第四条　烈属应由政府发给“革命牺牲军人家属光荣纪念证”，并按下列规定发给一次抚恤：
    （一）班长战士级：小米六百市斤。
    （二）营连排级：小米八百市斤。
    （三）团级以上：小米一千市斤。
    上述烈士家属抚恤费由下列家属之一，依次承领之：（一）在家居住之妻（或夫）；（二）在家居住之子女；（三）父母；（四）十六岁以下之弟妹；（五）抚养烈士长大而现在又需依靠烈士生活之其他亲属；（六）参加工作之直系亲属。无上述烈属者即不发。
    第五条　烈属得享受一般军属待遇，且在享受优待时与军属同样条件下应先尽烈属。
    第六条　为表扬烈士事迹，各地得建立烈士纪念碑、塔、亭、祠、林、烈士墓等。并定清明节为烈士扫幕节。各级政府得组织烈士事迹编纂委员会，负责收集编纂烈士英勇事迹，行署以上政府并得设专人负责此项调查收集工作。
    第七条　烈士抚恤手续：烈士牺牲后应由所在部队团以上政治机关填具“革命军人牺牲证明书”，发至原籍县政府转送烈属。该烈属接到上述证明书后，可持证到县政府领取抚恤费及“革命牺牲军人家属光荣纪念证”（病故的不发光荣纪念证）。县政府应负责审查，合于本条例第二条所规定之亲属始得发给。
    第八条　凡因在革命工作中积劳成疾而致死亡者，不得称烈士；但得按本条例第三条之规定妥为安葬，参照第七条之规定由所在部队团以上政治机关填具“病故军人证明书”，并由原籍县政府按下列规定发给其家属一次抚恤费：
    （一）团级以上：小米七百五十市斤。
    （二）营连排级：小米六百市斤。
    （三）班长战士级：小米四百五十市斤。
    其家属领取顺序适用本条例第四条之规定。
    第九条　本条例自公布之日施行。前晋冀鲁豫边区政府、晋察冀边区行政委员会及所属各级政府所颁烈士遗族抚恤条例均即作废。
