Title: Nehru's Reactionary Government Continues to Persecute Communists; Postal, Telegraph, and Railway Workers Decide on General Strike
Author:
Date: 1949-02-26
Page: 3

　　Nehru's Reactionary Government
  　Continues to Persecute Communists
  　Postal, Telegraph, and Railway Workers Decide on General Strike
    Xinhua News Agency, Northern Shaanxi, February 24th: According to a TASS dispatch from Delhi on the 21st, the reactionary Indian government is continuing to persecute communists and members of other democratic groups throughout the country. Within two days, 500 communists have been arrested, and the Hyderabad Communist Party has been declared illegal. The reactionary Indian bourgeois newspapers are stepping up propaganda against the general strike of railway workers, scheduled for March 9th, initiated by the All India Railwaymen's Federation. However, the Postal and Telegraph Workers' Union and the Central Government Employees' Union have passed resolutions deciding to persist in the strike regardless of government suppression.

### 尼赫鲁反动政府　继续迫害共产党人　邮电铁路职工决定总罢工

1949-02-26
第3版()
专栏：

　　尼赫鲁反动政府
  　继续迫害共产党人
  　邮电铁路职工决定总罢工
    【新华社陕北二十四日电】据塔斯社德里二十一日讯：印度反动政府在全国各地继续迫害共产党人及其他民主团体的成员。两天之内，已有五百名共产党人被逮捕，海德拉巴共产党已被宣布为非法。印度资产阶级反动报纸正在加紧宣传，反对印度铁路职工大会所发动的定于三月九日举行的铁路职工总罢工。但是，邮电职工会及中央政府机关职工会已通过决议，决定将不顾政府的镇压坚持罢工。
