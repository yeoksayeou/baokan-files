Title: Indian Ambassador to the Soviet Union Holds Reception for Nehru's Visit to the Soviet Union
Author:
Date: 1955-06-12
Page: 4

    Xinhua News Agency, Moscow, June 11th: According to TASS, Moscow, the Ambassador of the Republic of India to the Soviet Union, K.P.S. Menon, held a reception on the 10th for the visit of the Prime Minister of the Republic of India, Nehru, to the Soviet Union.
    Nehru and his entourage attended the reception.
    The Soviet side present at the reception included: Bulganin, Voroshilov, Kaganovich, Malenkov, Mikoyan, Pervukhin, Saburov, Khrushchev, Chairman of the Presidium of the Supreme Soviet of the Russian Federation Tarasov, Chairman of the Council of Ministers of the Russian Federation Puzanov, Ministers of the Soviet Union Benediktov, Zhukov, Kabanov, Mikhailov, Soviet Ambassador to India Menshikov, President of the Soviet Academy of Sciences Nesmeyanov, Soviet Army and Navy generals, responsible personnel from the Soviet Council of Ministers, the Soviet Ministry of Foreign Affairs, the Soviet Ministry of Culture, the Ministry of Foreign Trade, the Soviet Ministry of Defense, representatives of Soviet mass organizations, as well as Soviet scientists, artists, and literary figures who had visited India.
    Diplomatic envoys of various countries to the Soviet Union also attended the reception.
    Xinhua News Agency, Moscow, June 11th: According to TASS, Moscow, the Prime Minister of the Republic of India, Nehru, and his entourage continued their tour in Moscow on the 10th.
    On the morning of that day, Prime Minister Nehru and his entourage visited Moscow Middle School No. 545 and were warmly welcomed by the teachers and students of the school. The students presented Prime Minister Nehru with flowers and, amidst thunderous applause, put a Young Pioneer red scarf on him. Prime Minister Nehru was very touched by the children's enthusiastic welcome, and he immediately gave a small sandalwood cane, which he cherished and almost never parted with, to the young hosts. In thanking the children for their welcome, he said, "I am very happy to be able to visit your school, and I will convey your warm hospitality to the students in India."
    Afterwards, the distinguished guests went to visit the Moscow Metro. At the Arbat Metro station, where they began their tour, thousands of Muscovites applauded enthusiastically and cheered Prime Minister Nehru. During the tour, the distinguished guests admired the palace-like architecture of the various stations, learned about the electrical equipment of the Metro, and visited the Metro driver's cabin, riding the Metro.
    After coming out of the Metro, Prime Minister Nehru and others took a car to visit the new buildings of Moscow University on Lenin Hills. During the visit, Academician Petrovsky, the President of Moscow University, introduced the university's teaching situation to the distinguished guests. The distinguished guests then visited the university's Geography Museum, Student Culture House, and student dormitories, and ascended the twenty-fourth floor of the building to overlook the scenery of Moscow. Finally, Prime Minister Nehru also spoke to the teachers and students of the university in the auditorium of Moscow University. He expressed his belief that Moscow University would not only be able to complete the task of consolidating the friendship of all nationalities in the Soviet Union, but also would complete the task of consolidating the friendship between people of all countries in the world. During the visit, Prime Minister Nehru received many gifts from the students and professors of Moscow University.
    Later, the distinguished guests went to the Soviet Academy of Arts to visit the "Exhibition of Indian Culture and Art."
    That evening, Prime Minister Nehru and his entourage watched Tchaikovsky's ballet "Swan Lake" at the Bolshoi Theatre. Soviet government leaders Bulganin, Voroshilov, Kaganovich, Malenkov, Mikoyan, Pervukhin, Saburov, Khrushchev, Pospelov, and Suslov also went to the theater to watch the performance.
    Xinhua News Agency, Moscow, June 11th: According to TASS, Moscow, June 11th: The Prime Minister and Minister of Foreign Affairs of the Republic of India, Nehru, and his entourage, who are on an official visit to the Soviet Union, left Moscow on the 11th to visit other areas.

### 印度驻苏大使为尼赫鲁访苏举行招待会

1955-06-12
第4版()
专栏：

    印度驻苏大使为尼赫鲁访苏举行招待会
    新华社十一日讯　据塔斯社莫斯科讯：印度共和国驻苏联大使克·普·西·梅农十日为印度共和国总理尼赫鲁访问苏联举行了招待会。
    尼赫鲁和他的随行人员出席了招待会。
    苏联方面出席招待会的有：布尔加宁、伏罗希洛夫、卡冈诺维奇、马林科夫、米高扬、别尔乌辛、萨布罗夫、赫鲁晓夫，俄罗斯联邦最高苏维埃主席团主席塔拉索夫，俄罗斯联邦部长会议主席普扎诺夫，苏联各部部长别涅吉克托夫、朱可夫、卡巴诺夫、米哈伊洛夫，苏联驻印度大使曼希科夫，苏联科学院院长涅斯米扬诺夫，苏联陆海军将领，苏联部长会议、苏联外交部、苏联文化部、对外贸易部、苏联国防部的负责人员，苏联各群众团体的代表，以及曾经到印度访问过的苏联科学、艺术和文学工作者等。
    出席招待会的还有各国驻苏联的外交使节。
    新华社十一日讯　据塔斯社莫斯科讯：印度共和国总理尼赫鲁和他的随行人员十日继续在莫斯科参观。
    这一天上午，尼赫鲁总理和他的随行人员参观了莫斯科第五四五中学，受到这个学校的师生的热烈欢迎。学生们向尼赫鲁总理献了花，并且在暴风雨般的掌声中给他戴上了少年先锋队的红领巾。孩子们的热情欢迎使尼赫鲁总理很感动，他当场把一根他心爱的几乎从不离手的白檀木小棍送给年轻的主人。他在答谢孩子们的欢迎时说：“我非常高兴能访问你们的学校，我将把你们的盛情转达给印度学生们。”
    随后，贵宾们去莫斯科地下电车道参观。在他们开始参观的阿尔巴特地下电车站上，有几千名莫斯科人热烈鼓掌向尼赫鲁总理欢呼。参观时，贵宾们欣赏了各个车站的宫殿似的建筑，了解了地下电车道的电机设备，并且参观了地下电车司机室，乘坐了地下电车。
    从地下电车道出来，尼赫鲁总理等又乘汽车前往参观了列宁山上的莫斯科大学的新校舍。参观时，莫斯科大学校长彼得罗夫斯基院士向贵宾们介绍了这个大学的教学情况。贵宾们接着参观了这个大学的地理学博物馆、学生文化之家和学生宿舍，并登上二十四层楼的大厦，俯瞰莫斯科的景色。尼赫鲁总理最后还到莫斯科大学的礼堂向大学的师生们讲了话。他表示相信，莫斯科大学不仅能够完成巩固苏联各族人民友谊的任务，而且将完成巩固全世界各国人民之间的友谊的任务。参观期间，尼赫鲁总理收到了莫斯科大学的学生和教授赠送的许多礼物。
    后来，贵宾们又前往苏联艺术研究院参观了“印度文化和艺术展览会。”
    这天晚上，尼赫鲁总理和他的随行人员在莫斯科大剧院观看了柴可夫斯基的巴蕾舞剧“天鹅湖”。到剧院观剧的还有苏联政府的领导人布尔加宁、伏罗希洛夫、卡冈诺维奇、马林科夫、米高扬、别尔乌辛、萨布罗夫、赫鲁晓夫、波斯伯洛夫和苏斯洛夫等。
    新华社十一日讯　据塔斯社莫斯科十一日讯：正在苏联作正式访问的印度共和国总理兼外交部长尼赫鲁和他的随行人员，十一日离莫斯科去外地访问。
