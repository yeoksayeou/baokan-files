Title: U Nu and Tito Conclude Talks and Issue Communiqué
Author: 
Date: 1955-06-18
Page: 4
    Xinhua News Agency, Belgrade, June 17th: The talks between Burmese Prime Minister U Nu, who is visiting Yugoslavia, and Yugoslav President Tito have concluded. The communiqué issued on the 16th at the end of the talks stated that the two governments agreed to make every effort to "fully adopt the policy of peaceful coexistence" and to "establish peaceful and equal relations between all countries." Both countries also expressed their hope to "resolve all international problems through negotiations and agreements based on the principles of the United Nations." The communiqué indicated that Burma and Yugoslavia had achieved "full mutual understanding" during the talks.
    On the same day, the protocol on economic cooperation signed by the Burmese and Yugoslav governments on the 14th was also published.

### 吴努和铁托会谈结束并发表公报

1955-06-18
第4版()
专栏：

    吴努和铁托会谈结束并发表公报
    新华社十七日讯　贝尔格莱德消息：访问南斯拉夫的缅甸总理吴努和南斯拉夫总统铁托之间的会谈已经结束。十六日发表的会谈结束的公报说，两国政府同意尽一切努力争取“充分采取共处政策”，并且在“各国之间建立和平的和平等的关系”。两国还表示希望“以根据联合国原则举行谈判和达成协议的方法来解决一切国际问题”。公报表明，在这次会谈中，缅甸和南斯拉夫取得了“充分的互相谅解”。
    同一天还发表了缅甸和南斯拉夫政府在十四日签订的关于经济合作的议定书。
