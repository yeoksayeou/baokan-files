Adult Radio Gymnastics Illustrated Instructions (Third Set)
Compiled by Gymnastics Section, Sports Department, State Physical Culture and Sports Commission

First Section: Stretching Exercises (Four Eight-Beat Movements) Explanation:
    Preparatory Position: Standing upright.
    1.  Arms extended laterally (palms down), while stepping the left foot to the left side (feet approximately shoulder-width apart). (Inhale)
    2.  Lower the arms, vibrate them forward and upward (palms forward), while turning the body 90° to the left, shifting weight to the left foot, raising the right heel, chest out, upper body slightly arched back. (Inhale)
    3.  Lower the arms forward, returning to the position in step 1. (Exhale)
    4.  Return to standing upright. (Exhale)
    5, 6, 7, 8. Same as 1, 2, 3, 4, but with left and right reversed.
    Repeat the above movements for three more eight-beat cycles. Key points and precautions:
    1.  In the second beat, stretch all parts of the body as much as possible.
    2.  When turning the body, lower the arms and then vibrate them upwards immediately.
    3.  The commands should be relaxed and gentle.

Second Section: Squatting Exercises (Four Eight-Beat Movements) Explanation:
    Preparatory Position: Standing upright.
    1.  Arms forward, half-fisted (palms down), while stepping the left foot to the left side (feet approximately shoulder-width apart).
    2.  Arms flexed in front of the chest and then vibrate backwards (palms down), chest out, while squatting fully.
    3.  Arms vibrate forward and open laterally, turning the knuckles upward while opening, and straightening both knees.
    4.  Arms swing back naturally via the front, while retracting the left foot.
    5, 6, 7, 8. Same as 1, 2, 3, 4, but switching to the right foot. (Breathe naturally during this section)
    Repeat the above movements for three more eight-beat cycles, returning to standing upright with palms open on the last beat. Key points and precautions:
    1.  When flexing the arms, vibrate both elbows back as far as possible, do not let them droop; look straight ahead, keep the upper body upright, and do not lean forward.
    2.  When squatting fully, do not raise the heels, keep the knees pointing in the same direction as the toes.
    3.  The commands should be short and forceful.

Third Section: Leg Kicking Exercises (Four Eight-Beat Movements) Explanation:
    Preparatory Position: Standing upright.
    1.  Arms raise forward and then open laterally to a lateral elevation (turning palms upwards while opening), while stepping the left foot forward, shifting weight to the front foot, and raising the heel of the back foot. (Inhale)
    2.  Kick the right leg forward and upward, while raising both arms overhead, forward, and then vibrate backward. (Exhale)
    3.  Arms raise forward and then open laterally to a lateral elevation, returning to the position in step 1. (Inhale)
    4.  Arms raise overhead, forward and then swing back naturally, while retracting the left foot. (Exhale)
    5, 6, 7, 8. Same as 1, 2, 3, 4, but with left and right reversed.
    Repeat the above movements for three more eight-beat cycles, returning to standing upright on the last beat. Key points and precautions:
    1.  When stepping one foot forward, shift weight to the front foot, raise the heel of the back foot, stretch the body as much as possible, chest out, abdomen tucked in.
    2.  When kicking, do not bend the knee; keep the upper body upright, and vibrate both arms back forcefully to help the leg kick upward with power.
    3.  Do not move the position while doing this section.
    4.  The arm routes in steps 1 and 3 are the same as steps 2 and 4.
    5.  The commands should be forceful.

Fourth Section: Body Forward and Backward Bending Exercises (Four Eight-Beat Movements) Explanation:
    Preparatory Position: Standing upright.
    1.  Arms raise forward to the oblique upward lateral elevation (palms facing each other), while stepping the left foot to the left side (feet slightly wider than shoulder-width apart), upper body slightly arched back, chest out, head up. (Inhale)
    2.  Upper body bends forward and downward, arms vibrate forcefully backward between the legs, bringing hands together. (Exhale)
    3.  Return to the position in step 1. (Inhale)
    4.  Arms lower via the front, returning to standing upright. (Exhale)
    5, 6, 7, 8. Same as 1, 2, 3, 4, but switching to the right foot.
    Repeat the above movements for three more eight-beat cycles. Key points and precautions:
    1.  Arms should be straight when raised to the oblique upward lateral elevation, and should maintain the correct position; keep the head aligned with the upper body when slightly arched back.
    2.  Keep both legs straight when bending the upper body forward and downward; vibrate both arms backward forcefully to increase the power of the body's forward bend.
    3.  The commands should be slightly longer and forceful.

Fifth Section: Body Side Bending Exercises (Four Eight-Beat Movements) Explanation:
    Preparatory Position: Standing upright.
    1.  Arms swing to the left, raising them overhead.
    2.  Arms continue to circle until the right hand is on the hip, left arm diagonally upward to the left side (palm inward), looking at the left hand, while stepping the left foot a large step to the left side.
    3.  Bend the left knee, upper body bends to the right side, left arm close to the ear.
    4.  Bend to the right again using the rebound force.
    5.  Upper body straightens, left foot retracts, while both arms diagonally upward to the left side, circle via in front of the abdomen to the right, raising them overhead.
    6, 7, 8. Same as 2, 3, 4, but with left and right reversed. (Breathe naturally during this section)
    Repeat the above movements for three more eight-beat cycles, with steps 1 and 5 being the same for each eight-beat cycle, and returning to standing upright on the last beat of the section. Key points and precautions:
    1.  The arm circling movements in steps 1, 2, 5, and 6 should be done continuously, without pausing in the overhead position.
    2.  Elbow joints must be straight as much as possible when circling the arms.
    3.  Do not lean forward or backward when bending the upper body to the side; keep the raised arm straight, and when placing the hand on the hip (four fingers in front, thumb in back) the elbow joint should point directly to the side.
    4.  The arm circling movement (the raised arm) should help with the body's side bending power.
    5.  The commands should be slightly longer, and steps 3, 4, 7, and 8 should be emphasized.

Sixth Section: Body Circling Exercises (Four Eight-Beat Movements) Explanation:
    Preparatory Position: Standing upright.
    1-2. Upper body turns to the left and back, arms swing diagonally upward to the left and back from below, half-fisted (knuckles upward), looking at both hands, while stepping the left foot to the left side (feet approximately shoulder-width apart), heel of the right foot raised.
    3-4. Upper body turns to the right and back, while both arms swing diagonally upward to the right and back from below via in front of the abdomen, heel of the left foot raised.
    5-8. Upper body circles from right, forward bend, to left, back again, then right, and back to the starting position of 1-2, arms swing naturally with the body. (Breathe naturally during this section)
    Repeat the above movements for three more eight-beat cycles, with the feet remaining open and stationary during the 1-2 beats of each eight-beat cycle, and returning to standing upright on the last beat. The second and fourth eight-beat cycles are reversed left and right. Key points and precautions:
    1.  Raise the right (left) heel when turning the body and swinging the arms to the left (right). Keep both feet stationary when circling the body.
    2.  Circle the body one and a half times, using the waist as the axis; keep the arms consistent with the upper body.
    3.  Don't make the upper body movements too large when circling the body, relax the waist.
    4.  The commands for 1-4 should be forceful, and the commands for 5-8 should be slightly longer.

Seventh Section: Whole Body Exercises (Four Eight-Beat Movements) Explanation:
    Preparatory Position: Standing upright.
    1.  Left arm diagonally upward to the left front (palm upward), right arm diagonally downward to the right back (palm upward), looking at the left hand, while stepping the left foot forward in the direction of the toes, bending the left knee, straightening the right leg (forward lunge), with weight on the front foot. (Inhale)
    2.  Left leg straightens, right knee bends (backward lunge), shifting weight to the right leg, while the upper body leans diagonally downward to the right front, left arm presses down from above (palm downward), right arm bent in front of the head (palm forward), looking at the hand pressing down. (Exhale)
    3.  Return to the position in step 1. (Inhale)
    4.  Return to standing upright. (Exhale)
    5, 6, 7, 8. Same as 1, 2, 3, 4, but with left and right reversed.
    Repeat the above movements for three more eight-beat cycles. Key points and precautions:
    1.  Keep both arms as straight as possible in steps 1 and 3, forming a diagonal line.
    2.  Turn both hands simultaneously when the upper body leans diagonally downward to the right front in step 2; follow the hand pressing down with your eyes, and the hand pressing down needs to press forcefully to help the body lean forward.
    3.  Do not raise the heels in forward and backward lunges.
    4.  The body's center of gravity also shifts when switching between bending and straightening the legs.
    5.  The commands should be slightly longer and forceful.

Eighth Section: Jumping Exercises (Two Eight-Beat Movements) Explanation:
    Preparatory Position: Standing upright.
    1.  Jump with feet open front and back (left foot in front, right foot in back), hands on hips.
    2.  Jump back to the original position.
    3.  Same as 1, but with the right foot in front.
    4.  Jump back to the original position.
    5.  Jump with feet open to the sides.
    6.  Jump back to the original position.
    7.  Same as 5.
    8.  Same as 6. (Breathe naturally during this section) The second eight-beat cycle is the same as the first, returning to standing upright on the last beat. Key points and precautions:
    1.  Do not make the distance between the feet too large when jumping open; jump as high as possible, and the movements should be relaxed and natural.
    2.  Land on the balls of the feet when jumping.
    3.  Keep both hands on hips and stationary throughout this section, and only lower them at the end.
    4.  Breathe naturally.
    5.  The commands should be slightly faster and shorter.

After completing the jumping exercises, do spot marching for half a minute.

### 成人广播体操图解说明（第三套）  中华人民共和国体育运动委员会运动司体操科编制

1957-10-07
第8版()
专栏：

    成人广播体操图解说明（第三套）
    中华人民共和国体育运动委员会运动司体操科编制
    第一节　伸展运动　四个八拍动作说明：
    预备姿势：直立。
    1、两臂侧平举（掌心向下），同时左脚向左侧出一步（两脚距离约与肩同宽）。（吸气）
    2、两臂放下、经前向上振（掌心向前），同时向左转体90°，体重移至左脚，右脚脚跟提起，挺胸，上体稍后屈。（吸气）
    3、两臂经前放下还原成1的姿势。（呼气）
    4、还原直立。（呼气）
    5、6、7、8同1、2、3、4，惟左右相反。
    以上动作再做三个八拍。要领及注意事项：
    1、第2拍身体各部尽量伸展。
    2、转体时，两臂放下、随即上振。
    3、口令要轻松缓和。
    第二节　下蹲运动　四个八拍动作说明：
    预备姿势：直立。
    1、两臂前平举、半握拳（拳心向下），同时左脚向左侧出一步（两脚距离约与肩同宽）。
    2、两臂在胸前平屈后振（拳心向下），挺胸，同时屈膝全蹲。
    3、两臂经前侧开平振，侧开时拳眼转向上，同时两膝伸直。
    4、两臂经前自然后摆，同时左脚收回。
    5、6、7、8同1、2、3、4，惟换右脚侧出。（此节自然呼吸）
    以上动作再做三个八拍，最后一拍还原直立，拳变掌。要领及注意事项：
    1、两臂平屈时，两肘尽量向后振，不要下垂；眼向前平视，上体保持正直，不要前倾。
    2、屈膝全蹲时，脚跟不要提起，两膝的方向与脚尖相同。
    3、口令短促而有力。第三节　踢腿运动（四个八拍）动作说明：
    预备姿势：直立。
    1、两臂经前举侧开至侧平举部位（侧开时手心转向上），同时左脚前出一步，体重移至前脚上，后脚脚跟提起。（吸气）
    2、右腿向前上踢起，同时两臂经上举、前举、向后振。（呼气）
    3、两臂经前举侧开至侧平举部位，还原成1、的姿势。（吸气）
    4、两臂经上举、前举部位自然后摆，同时左脚收回。（呼气）
    5、6、7、8同1、2、3、4，惟左右相反。
    以上动作再做三个八拍，最后一拍还原直立。要领及注意事项：
    1、一脚前出时，体重移到前脚上，后脚脚跟提起，身体尽量伸展，挺胸、收腹。
    2、踢腿时，膝不要弯屈；上体保持正直，同时两臂用力后振，以助腿上踢的力量。
    3、做此节时，位置不要移动。
    4、第1、3拍，2、4拍臂的路线相同。
    5、口令要有力。
    第四节　体前后屈运动　（四个八拍）动作说明：
    预备姿势：直立。
    1、两臂经前至侧斜上举部位（掌心相对），同时左脚向左侧出一步（两脚距离比肩稍宽），上体稍后屈，挺胸、抬头。（吸气）
    2、上体前下屈、两臂在两腿间用力后振，两手靠拢。（呼气）
    3、还原成1的姿势。（吸气）
    4、两臂经前放下还原直立。（呼气）
    5、6、7、8同1、2、3、4，惟换右脚侧出。
    以上动作再做三个八拍。要领及注意事项：
    1、两臂侧斜上举时应伸直，并应保持正确的部位；上体稍后屈时，头与上体保持一致。
    2、上体前下屈时，两腿伸直；两臂用力向后以增加体前屈的力量。
    3、口令稍长而有力。
    第五节　体侧屈运动　四个八拍动作说明：
    预备姿势：直立。
    1、两臂向左摆至上举部位。
    2、两臂继续绕环至右手叉腰、左臂左侧斜上举（掌心向内），眼看左手同时左脚向左侧出一大步。3、屈左膝，上体向右侧屈，左臂靠近耳部。
    4、利用反弹力再向右屈一次。
    5、上体直立，左脚收回，同时两臂左侧斜上举、经腹前向右绕环至上举部位。
    6、7、8同2、3、4，惟左右相反。（此节自然呼吸）
    以上动作再做三个八拍，惟每个八拍的第1拍与5同，本节最后一拍还原直立。要领及注意事项：
    1、1、2拍及5、6、拍两臂绕环的动作要连续做，不要在上举的部位停顿。
    2、两臂绕环时，肘关节须尽量伸直。
    3、上体侧屈时，不要前倾或后仰；上举之臂伸直，叉腰时（四指在前、姆指在后）肘关节正指侧方。
    4、臂的绕环动作（上举之臂）应帮助身体侧屈的力量。
    5、口令稍长，3、4、7、8拍应加强。
    第六节　体回环运动　四个八拍动作说明：
    预备姿势：直立。
    1—2、上体向左后转，两臂由下向左后斜上摆、半握拳（拳眼向上），眼看两手，同时左脚向左侧出一步（两脚距离约与肩同宽），右脚脚跟提起。
    3—4、上体向右后转，同时两臂由下经腹前向右后斜上摆，左脚脚跟提起。
    5—8、上体经右、前屈、向左向后再向右、前回环一周半至1、2的姿势，两臂随身体自然摆动。（此节自然呼吸）
    以上动作再作三个八拍，惟每个八拍的1—2拍两脚开立不动，最后一拍还原直立。第二个八拍和第四个八拍左右相反。要领及注意事项：
    1、向左（右）转体摆臂时，右（左）脚跟提起。体回环时，两脚不动。
    2、体回环时，以腰为轴转动一周半；两臂与上体保持一致。
    3、体回环时，上体动作不用太大，腰部放松。
    4、1—4拍口令有力，5—8拍口令稍长。
    第七节　全身运动　四个八拍动作说明：
    预备姿势：直立。
    1、左臂左前斜上举（掌心向上），右臂右后斜下举（掌心向上），眼看左手，同时左脚顺脚尖方向向左前出一大步，左膝弯屈、右腿伸直（前弓箭步），体重放在前脚上。（吸气）
    2、左腿伸直、右膝弯屈（后弓箭步），体重移至右腿，同时上体向右前斜下倾，左臂由上向下按压（掌心向下），右臂弯屈于头上前方（掌心向前），眼看下压的手。（呼气）
    3、还原1的姿势。（吸气）
    4、还原直立。（呼气）
    5、6、7、8同1、2、3、4，惟左右相反。
    以上动作再做三个八拍。要领及注意事项：
    1、第1、3拍时，两臂尽量伸直，要成一条斜的直线。
    2、第2拍上体向右前斜下倾时，两手要同时翻转；眼跟着下压的手移动，下压的手需用力按压，以助身体前倾的力量。
    3、前、后弓箭步时，两脚脚跟不要提起。
    4、两腿交换屈伸时，身体重心也随着移动。
    5、口令稍长而有力。
    第八节　跳跃运动　二个八拍动作说明：
    预备姿势：直立。
    1、两脚前后开跳（左脚在前、右脚在后），两手叉腰。
    2、两脚跳还原。
    3、同1，惟右脚在前。
    4、两脚跳还原。
    5、两脚向两侧开跳。
    6、两脚跳还原。
    7、同5、8、同6、（此节自然呼吸）后八拍和前八拍相同，惟最后一拍还原直立。要领及注意事项：
    1、开跳时，两脚距离不要太大；跳跃时，尽量往上跳起，动作要轻松自然。
    2、跳跃时要以前脚掌着地。
    3、此节两手始终叉腰不动，最后才放下。
    4、呼吸要自然。
    5、口令应稍快而短促。
    跳跃做完以后，作原地踏步半分钟。
