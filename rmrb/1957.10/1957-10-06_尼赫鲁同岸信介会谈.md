Title: Nehru and Kishi Nobusuke Hold Talks
Author:
Date: 1957-10-06
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, Tokyo, 5th -- It is reported that Indian Prime Minister Nehru held his first two-hour meeting with Japanese Prime Minister Nobusuke Kishi and Foreign Minister Aiichiro Fujiyama this morning.

Aiichiro Fujiyama said after the meeting that the meeting was preliminary and did not discuss specific issues. Aiichi Kiichi, Chief Cabinet Secretary of Japan, who attended the meeting, said that Nehru and Kishi Nobusuke agreed during the meeting that Japan and India should first strive to stop nuclear weapons testing in order to eliminate the tension between the United States and the Soviet Union. In order to maintain world peace, Japan and India should mediate the differences between the United States and the Soviet Union.

### 尼赫鲁同岸信介会谈

1957-10-06
第6版()
专栏：

    尼赫鲁同岸信介会谈
    据新华社5日讯　东京消息：印度总理尼赫鲁今天上午同日本首相岸信介和外务相藤山爱一郎举行了为时两小时的第一次会谈。
    藤山爱一郎在会谈后说，这次会谈是预备性的，并未讨论具体问题。参加会谈的日本内阁官房长官爱知揆一说，尼赫鲁和岸信介在会谈中同意，印日两国首先应该努力促使停止核武器试验，以便消除美国同苏联之间的紧张关系。为了维护世界和平，日本和印度应当调解美国和苏联之间的分歧。
