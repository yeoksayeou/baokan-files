Title: Soviet Foreign Minister Warns Western Countries That Any Aggression Against Syria Threatens World Peace; U.S. Publicly Encourages Turkey to Intervene in Syria
Author:
Date: 1957-10-03
Page: 3

    Xinhua News Agency, Cairo, October 1st: According to a report in "Al-Gomhouria" on September 30th, Soviet Foreign Minister Andrei Gromyko, in an interview with the newspaper's New York correspondent, said that any aggression against Syria would threaten world peace.
    Gromyko said he hoped that Western countries would realize that "world peace is indivisible. If anyone attacks Syria, world peace will be threatened, and peace-loving people will have no choice but to defend Syria's independence."
    Gromyko reiterated the Soviet Union's proposal for major powers to issue a declaration guaranteeing non-interference in Middle Eastern affairs. He said, "If the Western countries' current provocative policies are not stopped, serious obstacles will arise and threaten world peace."
    Gromyko said that the Soviet Union has always supported the idea of direct negotiations. He said that the outcome of direct negotiations between Syria and the United States would depend on whether the United States is prepared to understand the current issues without prejudice.
    Xinhua News Agency, October 2nd: Washington Dispatch: The United States is openly encouraging Turkey to intervene in Syria. U.S. Presidential Press Secretary James Hagerty publicly denied on October 1st rumors that the United States does not support such intervention by Turkey.
    According to "The New York Times," U.S. President Dwight Eisenhower recently wrote a letter to the King of Saudi Arabia. The revealed content of the letter indicates that the United States intends to continue interfering in the internal affairs of Syria and other Arab countries.
    Xinhua News Agency, October 2nd: TASS, Beirut, October 1st: The Lebanese newspaper "Al-Telegraph" reported that according to people returning from Turkey, they saw American and Turkish troops deployed on the roads between Alexandretta and the border outposts, aimed at the Syrian city of Latakia. The newspaper pointed out that, according to eyewitnesses, the Turkish and American troops possess artillery and other weapons.

### 苏联外长警告西方国家  对叙任何侵略都威胁世界和平  美国公开鼓动土耳其对叙进行干涉

1957-10-03
第3版()
专栏：

    苏联外长警告西方国家
    对叙任何侵略都威胁世界和平
    美国公开鼓动土耳其对叙进行干涉
    新华社开罗1日电  据“共和国报”9月30日报道，苏联外交部长葛罗米柯在接见这家报纸驻纽约记者时说，对叙利亚的任何侵略都会威胁世界和平。
    葛罗米柯说，他希望西方国家认识到，“世界和平是不可分割的。如果有任何人进攻叙利亚，世界和平就会受到威胁，爱好和平的人民也就不得不保卫叙利亚的独立。”
    葛罗米柯重申苏联关于大国发表宣言保证不干涉中东事务的建议。他说，“如果不停止西方国家目前的挑衅性政策，就会产生严重的障碍而形成对世界和平的威胁。”
    葛罗米柯说，苏联一向是赞成直接谈判的主张的。他说，叙利亚同美国之间直接谈判的结果如何要看美国是否准备毫无偏见地来了解目前的问题。
    新华社2日讯  华盛顿消息：美国正在公开鼓动土耳其对叙利亚进行干涉。美国总统新闻秘书哈格蒂10月1日公开否认了关于美国不赞成土耳其进行这种干涉的传闻。
    据“纽约时报”报道，美国总统艾森豪威尔最近写信给沙特阿拉伯国王。透露出来的信件内容表明，美国打算继续干涉叙利亚和其他阿拉伯国家的内政。
    新华社2日讯  塔斯社贝鲁特1日讯：黎巴嫩“电讯报”报道，据从土耳其回来的人说，他们看到美国和土耳其的军队布置在阿历山大勒达和边境据点之间的道路上，矛头指向叙利亚的拉塔基亚市。报纸指出，据目击者说，土耳其和美国的军队拥有大炮和其他武器。
