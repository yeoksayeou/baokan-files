Title: Celebrating the Eighth Anniversary of the Founding of the People's Republic of China
Author:
Date: 1957-10-02
Page: 2

    Telegrams of Congratulations from Indian Prime Minister Nehru
    To His Excellency Mr. Zhou Enlai, Premier and Minister of Foreign Affairs of the Government of the People's Republic of China:
    On the occasion of your National Day celebrations, I am very pleased to convey to Your Excellency, on behalf of my colleagues in the government and in my own name, and through Your Excellency to the Chinese people, my sincere congratulations. I take this opportunity to express my firm hope and confidence that the good relations existing between our two countries, and strengthened by your visit to India last year, will be further consolidated, thereby benefiting the highest interests of both our countries and world peace. Jawaharlal Nehru

      Telegram of Congratulations from Yugoslav President Tito
    To Comrade Mao Zedong, Chairman of the People's Republic of China:
    On the occasion of the anniversary of the founding of the People's Republic of China, on behalf of the people of all nationalities of Yugoslavia and in my own name, I express to you, Comrade Chairman, and to the fraternal Chinese people, my heartfelt congratulations and sincere wishes, wishing the Chinese people progress and prosperity, and wishing the friendly relations and cooperation between Yugoslavia and China to develop and strengthen day by day, and wishing the People's Republic of China further achievements in building socialism and defending peace.
    Josip Broz Tito

    Telegram of Congratulations from King Zahir of the Kingdom of Afghanistan
    To His Excellency Chairman Mao Zedong of the People's Republic of China:
    On the occasion of the eighth anniversary of the founding of the People's Republic of China, I am very pleased to extend to Your Excellency and the Chinese people my warmest congratulations and most sincere wishes for Your Excellency's happiness and for the prosperity of the great Chinese people. Mohammad Zahir Shah
                                  Kabul, September 28, 1957

    Telegram of Congratulations from President U Win Maung of the Union of Burma
    To His Excellency Mr. Mao Zedong, Chairman of the People's Republic of China:
    On this auspicious occasion of the National Day celebration of the People's Republic of China, I am very pleased to extend to Your Excellency and, through Your Excellency, to the people of the Union of Burma my warm congratulations. Please allow me to express my sincere good wishes for Your Excellency's health and for the continued progress, prosperity, and happiness of the people of the People's Republic of China.
                                  U Win Maung, President of the Union of Burma
                                      Rangoon, September 27, 1957

    Telegram of Congratulations from Prime Minister U Nu of the Union of Burma
    To His Excellency Mr. Zhou Enlai, Premier of the State Council of the People's Republic of China:
    On this auspicious occasion of the National Day celebration of the People's Republic of China, I am very pleased to extend to Your Excellency and the Government of the People's Republic of China my sincere congratulations and best wishes, wishing the Chinese government continued success in building a new China—a China dedicated to the happiness of the people and the cause of world peace.  I believe that the friendly relations and cooperation between our two countries, based on the Five Principles of Peaceful coexistence, will continue to expand and develop, and will benefit the peoples of our two countries. U Nu, Prime Minister of the Union of Burma
                                      Rangoon, September 27, 1957

    Telegram of Congratulations from Prime Minister Bandaranaike of Ceylon

    Telegram of Congratulations from King Mahendra of the Kingdom of Nepal

    Telegram of Congratulations from Hungarian Foreign Minister Imre Horváth
    To Comrade Zhou Enlai, Minister of Foreign Affairs of the People's Republic of China:
    On the occasion of the great National Day of the People's Republic of China, please accept my sincere and good wishes, Comrade Minister. Our people follow with joy the brilliant achievements of the fraternal Chinese people in building socialism, and regard these achievements as their own, and at the same time wish the Chinese people further achievements in the great work of building their motherland.
    The peaceful foreign policy of the People's Republic of China has so far effectively prevented the imperialists' conspiracy to turn East Asia into a permanent source of war from being realized, and I sincerely wish this foreign policy further achievements for the benefit of the peace-loving peoples of all countries and for the benefit of maintaining peace in the Far East and the world.
            Imre Horváth, Minister of Foreign Affairs of the Hungarian People's Republic
                              Budapest, September 29, 1957

### 庆贺中华人民共和国成立八周年

1957-10-02
第2版()
专栏：

    庆贺中华人民共和国成立八周年
    印度总理尼赫鲁的贺电中华人民共和国政府总理兼外交部长周恩来先生阁下：
    在贵国国庆纪念的时候，我很愉快地代表我政府中的同事并且以我个人的名义向阁下，并通过阁下向中国人民，致以诚挚的祝贺。我借此机会表达我的坚定的希望和信心，即存在于我们两国之间的，并由于你去年访问印度而加强了的良好关系将会进一步巩固，从而有利于我们两国本身以及世界和平的最高利益。  贾瓦哈拉尔·尼赫鲁
      南斯拉夫总统铁托的贺电中华人民共和国主席毛泽东同志：
    在中华人民共和国成立纪念的时候，我以南斯拉夫各族人民和我个人的名义，特别愉快地向您，主席同志，和兄弟的中国人民，表达衷心的祝贺和诚挚的愿望，祝中国人民进步和繁荣，祝南斯拉夫和中国之间的友好关系和合作日益发展和加强，并祝中华人民共和国在建设社会主义和保卫和平方面取得进一步的成就。
    约瑟夫·布罗兹·铁托
    阿富汗王国国王查希尔的贺电中华人民共和国毛泽东主席阁下：
    当中华人民共和国成立八周年的时候，我很愉快地向阁下和中国人民致以最热烈的祝贺并最诚挚地祝阁下幸福，祝伟大的中国人民昌盛。  穆罕默德·查希尔·沙阿
                                  1957年9月28日喀布尔
    缅甸联邦总统吴温貌的贺电中华人民共和国主席毛泽东先生阁下：
    值此中华人民共和国国庆纪念的佳节，我很愉快地向阁下并通过阁下向中国人民表达我个人和缅甸联邦人民的热烈祝贺。请允许我为阁下的健康和中华人民共和国人民的继续进步、繁荣和幸福，表示我的诚挚的良好愿望。
                                  缅甸联邦总统  吴温貌
                                      1957年9月27日仰光
    缅甸联邦总理吴努的贺电中华人民共和国国务院总理周恩来先生阁下：
    值此中华人民共和国国庆纪念的佳节，我很愉快地向阁下和中华人民共和国政府致以诚挚的祝贺和最好的愿望，祝中国政府在创建新中国——一个致力于人民的幸福和世界和平事业的中国方面，继续获得成就。我相信，我们两国之间以和平共处的五项原则为基础的友好关系和合作将继续扩大和发展，而有益于我们两国的人民。  缅甸联邦总理  吴努
                                      1957年9月27日仰光
