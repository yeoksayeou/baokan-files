　　Brave Mexicans

　　The Soviet color film "The Mexican" is adapted from the novel of the same name by Jack London, one of the pioneers of American revolutionary literature. Through the struggle of a young Mexican revolutionary, it reflects the noble qualities of the Mexican revolutionary heroes in the 19th century—bravery and resourcefulness—and foretells the brilliant prospect of the people's inevitable victory.

　　Soviet young actor Strizhnev (the male lead in the film "The Gadfly") successfully portrays the image of this young Mexican in this film, and very delicately conveys the inner emotions of the character. The image of the revolutionary leader General Villa, created by the famous Soviet actor Andreyev (the male lead in the film "The Fall of Berlin") in this film, is also very moving.

　　The film has beautiful composition and bright colors. Many of the shots with regional characteristics, such as the early morning in the Americas, the glittering dewdrops clinging to cacti and agaves, and Mexicans singing aloud while playing the guitar, greatly enhance the realism of the film. (Yuè Gǔ) (Attached picture)

### 勇敢的墨西哥人

专栏：舞台和银幕

　　勇敢的墨西哥人
    苏联彩色影片“墨西哥人”是根据美国革命文学的先驱者之一——杰克·伦敦的同名小说改编的。它通过一个墨西哥革命青年的斗争事迹，反映了十九世纪墨西哥革命志士勇敢机智的高尚品质，预示出了人民必定胜利的辉煌远景。
    苏联青年演员斯特里席诺夫（影片“牛虻”的男主角）在这部影片中成功地塑造了这个青年墨西哥人的形象，并且非常细腻地把角色的内心情感传达了出来。苏联著名演员安德列也夫（影片“攻克柏林”的男主角）在这部影片中创造的革命领袖维拉将军的形象，也是十分动人的。
    影片构图优美、色彩鲜艳，其中许多富有地域特点的镜头，如美洲的初晨，依附在仙人掌、龙舌兰上的金光闪闪的露珠，弹着吉他高歌的墨西哥人等，也大大加强了影片的真实感。  （岳谷）（附图片）
