Title: Striving for Peace in Algeria: French People Hold National Day of Struggle
Author:
Date: 1957-10-20
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, Paris, 19th — The French working people, responding to the call of the French Communist Party, held a national day of struggle for peace in Algeria on October 17th.

Despite the "caretaker" government of Félix Gaillard using the excuse of "endangering national security" to prohibit mass rallies and demonstrations for a peaceful resolution to the Algerian question, and deploying large numbers of police to deal with the participants in the demonstrations, French workers, peasants, small business people, students, and intellectuals actively participated in the activities of the day of struggle. The working people of many enterprises stopped work and held rallies and mass meetings.

According to "l'Humanité", the working people in many places actively collected signatures on petitions to parliamentarians, demanding an end to the bloodshed in Algeria. In some places, police clashed with demonstrators.

The Political Bureau of the Central Committee of the French Communist Party issued a statement saying that October 17th was an important stage in the struggle for a peaceful resolution to the Algerian question, moving towards unity.

### 争取阿尔及利亚和平  法国人民举行全国斗争日

1957-10-20
第5版()
专栏：

　　争取阿尔及利亚和平
    法国人民举行全国斗争日
    据新华社巴黎19日电  法国劳动人民响应法国共产党的号召，在10月17日举行了争取阿尔及利亚和平的全国斗争日。
    尽管布歇—莫努里的“看管”政府借口“危害国家安全”禁止举行争取和平解决阿尔及利亚问题的群众大会和示威，并且调集大量警察来对付示威游行的参加者，法国工人、农民、小商业者、学生和知识界人士还是积极地参加了斗争日的活动。许多企业的劳动人民都停止工作，举行集会和群众大会。
    据“人道报”报道，许多地方的劳动人民纷纷在给议员的请愿书上征集签名，要求结束阿尔及利亚的流血事件。有些地方警察和示威的人发生了冲突。
    法国共产党中央委员会政治局发表了一项声明说，10月17日这一天是在争取和平解决阿尔及利亚问题的斗争中走向团结一致的一个重要阶段。
