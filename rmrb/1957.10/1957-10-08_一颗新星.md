Title: A New Star
Author: Zāng Kèjiā
Date: 1957-10-08
Page: 8

A New Star
Zang Kejia

(I)
A new star appears in the high sky,
Stirring the eyes of all mankind in the world,
Let us clap to it, let us cheer for it,
It flies, it flies, carrying our excited hearts.

Da da da, like a friendly whisper,
The radio emits rhythmic sounds,
A scientific giant is striding forward quickly,
Da da, knocking on the door to the secrets of the universe.

(II)
In the blink of an eye, one hundred and eight thousand li,
It waves to mankind from nine hundred kilometers in the sky:
Fly up, fly up,
Let us go to the stars for a carefree journey.

Passing over the Western Hemisphere,
Its heart is especially excited:
"I am a guest from the Soviet Union,"
High, high it swept over the skies of New York.

### 一颗新星
臧克家
1957-10-08
第8版()
专栏：

    一颗新星
    臧克家
    （一）
    一颗新星出现在高空，
    牵动着全世界人类的眼睛，
    向他拍手吧，向他欢呼吧，
    他飞，他飞，带着我们兴奋的心。
    哒哒哒，像亲切的耳语，
    收音机里发出节奏的声音，
    一个科学巨人在迈着快步，
    哒哒地扣着宇宙秘密的大门。
    （二）
    一转眼十万八千里，
    他在九百公里的天上向人类招手：
    飞起来呀，飞起来呀，
    我们到星际去作逍遥游。
    路过西半球的时候，
    他的心特别激动：
    “我是来自苏联的客人呀”，
    高高地，高高地他掠过了纽约的上空。
