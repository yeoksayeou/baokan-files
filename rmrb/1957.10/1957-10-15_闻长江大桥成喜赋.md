Title: An Ode of Joy on Hearing of the Completion of the Yangtze River Bridge
Author: Dong Biwu
Date: 1957-10-15
Page: 8

　　An Ode of Joy on Hearing of the Completion of the Yangtze River Bridge
　　Dong Biwu
　　The natural chasm divides North and South, the three cities stand as tripods.
　　I dreamt of a fairy staff transforming, joyfully witnessing an iron rainbow suspended.
　　Wuhan is connected as one breath, Tortoise and Snake Hills at the two ends.
　　The surging river flows endlessly, the broad road deviates not.
　　The Yellow Crane Tower is no longer as it was, the Qingchuan Pavilion remains complete.
　　Visiting and viewing at sunset, what could possibly cause sorrow and worry?

　　　　　　　　　　June 1957
　　　　　　　　　　　　(Originally published in "Poetry Magazine")

### 闻长江大桥成喜赋
董必武
1957-10-15
第8版()
专栏：

　　闻长江大桥成喜赋
　　董必武
　　天堑分南北，三城鼎足然。
　　梦思仙杖化，喜见铁虹悬。
　　武汉连一气，龟蛇在两端。
　　滔滔流不尽，荡荡路无偏。
　　黄鹤楼非旧，晴川阁尚全。
　　游观当日暮，何物惹愁牵。
　　　　　　　　　　一九五七年六月
　　　　　　　　  　　    （原载“诗刊”）
