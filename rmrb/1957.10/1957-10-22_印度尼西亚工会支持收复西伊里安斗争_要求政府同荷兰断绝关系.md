Title: Indonesian Unions Support Struggle to Recover West Irian, Demand Government Sever Relations with Netherlands
Author:
Date: 1957-10-22
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, Jakarta, October 21st: The Central Organization of All-Indonesian Trade Unions yesterday issued a statement welcoming the Indonesian Cabinet's decision to hold a West Irian Struggle Week. The statement said that the struggle to recover West Irian is a struggle to liberate the people economically, politically and militarily from Dutch colonial rule.

The statement pointed out that the Netherlands is using West Irian as a base for aggression against Indonesia in order to maintain its economic and political position in Indonesia.

The statement suggested that the Indonesian government take the following actions in the political, financial and economic fields to deal with Dutch imperialism: diplomatically sever relations with the Netherlands, end the privileges of Dutch oil companies, restrict Dutch companies from remitting money out of Indonesia, control Dutch banks and docks, and abolish the Dutch monopoly on inter-island navigation in Indonesia.

### 印度尼西亚工会支持收复西伊里安斗争  要求政府同荷兰断绝关系

1957-10-22
第6版()
专栏：

    印度尼西亚工会支持收复西伊里安斗争
    要求政府同荷兰断绝关系
    据新华社雅加达21日电　全印度尼西亚工会中央组织昨天发表声明，欢迎印度尼西亚内阁作出关于举行西伊里安斗争周的决定。声明说，收复西伊里安的斗争是从荷兰殖民主义统治下在经济、政治和军事上解放人民的斗争。
    声明指出，荷兰正在利用西伊里安作为侵略印度尼西亚的基地，以维护它在印度尼西亚的经济和政治上的地位。
    声明建议印度尼西亚政府在政治和财政经济方面采取下列行动对付荷兰帝国主义：在外交上同荷兰断绝关系，结束荷兰石油企业的特权，限制荷兰企业汇款到印度尼西亚境外，控制荷兰的银行和码头，取消荷兰在印度尼西亚岛际航行方面的垄断权。
