Title: Foundation of Western Deterrence Policy Crumbles; American Technological Superiority Myth Shattered: British and French Press Comment on the Successful Launch of Soviet Artificial Satellite

Author:

Date: 1957-10-14
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency report: London dispatch: British newspapers "The Scotsman" and "The Manchester Guardian" believe that the Soviet Union's launch of an artificial satellite is a serious blow to American foreign policy.

"The Scotsman" published an article by its foreign affairs commentator on October 10, saying that American experts admit that the satellite was indeed launched into its orbit by a rocket that could be used as an intercontinental ballistic missile, and it is far ahead of anything the United States currently possesses. This rocket is an accurate tool with excellent guidance properties.

American scientists tend to believe that the Soviet Union's lead will become increasingly larger in the future, because the Soviet Union trains far more scientists each year than the United States, and it has a complete educational system to support the training of scientists. The famous Professor Teller said that the Soviet Union's advantage in science over the next ten years is now irreversible.

"The Manchester Guardian" published an article by its foreign affairs reporter on October 11, pointing out that the Soviet Union's Earth satellite shows that the American "massive retaliation" policy is "becoming obsolete." The reporter said that no matter how the United States interprets and tries to downplay the significance of this event, it is impossible to diminish the enormous significance of the Soviet achievement in the minds of ordinary people. And "anything that increases the Soviet Union's power or prestige or achievement will inevitably have a tendency to attract non-aligned countries to become closer to the Soviet bloc."

The reporter pointed out that the Soviet Union's successful launch of the satellite may cause the main basis of the West's so-called "massive retaliation" and the military idea of using nuclear bombs as a "deterrent" to "crumble."

Paris dispatch: The weekly "International Literary World" pointed out in an article entitled "The Tragedy of America": The successful launch of the Soviet artificial Earth satellite shattered the myth of American technological superiority.

The article said: The American press and public are demanding that all forces be mobilized to overcome America's backwardness in this area. The article pointed out: However, the American social system does not possess the possibility of engaging in large-scale scientific work in the field of interplanetary rockets.

### 西方威慑政策根据破灭  美国技术优越神话粉碎  英法报刊评论苏联发射人造卫星成功

1957-10-14
第5版()
专栏：

　　西方威慑政策根据破灭  美国技术优越神话粉碎
    英法报刊评论苏联发射人造卫星成功
    本报综合报道　据新华社讯：伦敦消息：英国报纸“苏格兰人报”和“曼彻斯特卫报”认为，苏联发射人造卫星是给美国外交政策的严重打击。
    “苏格兰人报”10月10日刊载了该报外交评论员的一篇文章说，美国专家们承认，卫星确实是由能够用来作为洲际弹道导弹的火箭发射到它的轨道上去的，并且比较美国现有的这类东西大大领先了。这种火箭是有优良导向性质的准确工具。
    美国科学家倾向于认为，俄国领先的程度今后将愈来愈大，因为苏联每年培养出来的科学家的人数要比美国多得多，它有配合培养科学家的一整套教育制度。有名的泰勒教授说，今后十年苏联在科学方面的优势现在已不可扭转。
    “曼彻斯特卫报”10月11日发表了该报外交记者的一篇文章，指出苏联的地球卫星表明美国的“大模规报复”政策“即将过时”。这个记者说，不管美国怎样进行解释，企图贬低这件事的意义，也不可能缩小苏联的成就在普通人的心目中的巨大意义。而“任何足以增加苏联的力量或声望或成就的东西都不可避免地必然会有把未参加集团国家吸引到比较接近苏联集团的趋势。”
    这个记者指出，苏联顺利地发射了卫星一事可能使得西方的所谓“大规模报复”和使用核炸弹作为“威慑力量”的军事思想的主要根据“归于破灭”。
    巴黎消息：“国际文坛”周刊在一篇题为“美国的悲剧”的文章中指出：苏联人造地球卫星的发射成功，粉碎了美国技术优越的神话。
    文章说：美国报界和公众要求动员一切力量来克服美国在这方面的落后状态。文章指出：然而美国的社会制度不具备在星际火箭方面从事大规模科学工作的可能性。
