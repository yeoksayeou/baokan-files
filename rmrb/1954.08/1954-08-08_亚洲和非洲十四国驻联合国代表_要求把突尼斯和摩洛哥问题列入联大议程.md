Title: Asian and African Fourteen Nations' Representatives to the UN Request to Include the Tunisia and Morocco Issues on the UN General Assembly Agenda
Author:
Date: 1954-08-08
Page: 4

    Asian and African Fourteen Nations' Representatives to the UN
    Request to Include the Tunisia and Morocco Issues on the UN General Assembly Agenda
    [Xinhua News Agency, New York, 7th] News: The representatives of fourteen Asian and African countries to the United Nations formally requested the United Nations Secretary-General, Dag Hammarskjöld, on July 29th to include the Tunisia and Morocco issues on the agenda of the ninth session of the United Nations General Assembly, which will convene in September.
    The two memorandums simultaneously submitted to the United Nations Secretary-General by the representatives of the fourteen Asian and African countries reviewed the situation in Tunisia and Morocco since the seventh session of the United Nations General Assembly in 1952, and accused the French authorities of implementing policies of "force" and "cruel abuse" in these two regions.

### 亚洲和非洲十四国驻联合国代表  要求把突尼斯和摩洛哥问题列入联大议程

1954-08-08
第4版()
专栏：

    亚洲和非洲十四国驻联合国代表
    要求把突尼斯和摩洛哥问题列入联大议程
    【新华社七日讯】纽约消息：亚洲和非洲十四国驻联合国的代表已经在七月二十九日正式要求联合国秘书长哈马舍尔德把突尼斯和摩洛哥问题列入九月间开会的联合国大会第九届会议议程。
    亚洲和非洲十四国代表同时提交联合国秘书长的两个备忘录回顾了一九五二年联合国大会第七届会议以来突尼斯和摩洛哥的局势，并且指责法国当局在这两个地区实行“武力”和“残酷的虐待”政策。
