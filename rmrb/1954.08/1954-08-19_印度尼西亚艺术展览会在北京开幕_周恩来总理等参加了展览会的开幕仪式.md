Title: Indonesian Art Exhibition Opens in Beijing Premier Zhou Enlai and Others Attend Opening Ceremony

Author: 
Date: 1954-08-19
Page: 1

Xinhua News Agency, August 18th: The “Indonesian Art Exhibition,” sponsored by the Ministry of Culture of the Central People's Government, opened on the morning of the 18th at the Water Pavilion in Zhongshan Park, Beijing.

    The exhibition hall was decorated solemnly and beautifully. On the front curtain of the exhibition hall hung portraits of Chairman Mao Zedong and President Sukarno. The national flags of China and the Republic of Indonesia were hung on either side.

    Attending the opening ceremony were: Zhou Enlai, Premier of the State Council of the Central People's Government; Xi Zhongxun, Secretary-General; Shen Yanbing, Minister of Culture; Zhang Bojun, Minister of Communications; Zhang Hanfu, Vice Minister of Foreign Affairs; Lei Renmin, Vice Minister of Foreign Trade; Zheng Zhenduo, Vice Minister of Culture; Sa Kongliao, Deputy Director of the General Administration of Publications; Qian Junrui, Secretary-General of the Cultural and Educational Committee of the State Council; Fan Changjiang, Deputy Secretary-General; Hong Shen, Director of the Bureau of Foreign Cultural Relations; Wang Bingnan, Director of the General Office of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs; Chen Jiakang, Director of the Asian Department; Wu Han, Deputy Mayor of the Beijing Municipal People's Government, and others.

    Also attending the opening ceremony were: Chu Tunan, President of the Chinese People's Association for Cultural Relations with Foreign Countries; and prominent figures from the capital's literary and artistic circles, including Jiang Feng, Chen Huangmei, Zhang Geng, Zhang Guangnian, Wang Manshuo, Wang Renshu, Yuan Shuipai, Hua Junwu, Shao Yu, and others.

    Attending the opening ceremony were: Mr. Mengoadas Nasoetion, head of the Indonesian Art Troupe, some members of the troupe, and Zeng Zusen, Dean of the Chinese Department of the University of Indonesia.

    Also attending the opening ceremony was Mr. Mononutu, Indonesian Ambassador to China.

    Foreign guests invited to attend the opening ceremony included diplomatic envoys and embassy staff from various countries to China.

    The opening ceremony was presided over by Shen Yanbing, Minister of Culture of the Central People's Government, who delivered the opening speech. He first thanked the Indonesian Art Troupe for bringing these exquisite works of art, giving the Chinese people the opportunity to fully understand the rich Indonesian art and appreciate the fruits of the labor and wisdom of the Indonesian people. He said: "This visit by the delegation is a tremendous achievement in the cultural exchange between China and Indonesia, and it will greatly help to enhance mutual understanding and friendly unity between the two peoples."

    Next, Mr. Mengoadas Nasoetion, head of the Indonesian Art Troupe, who was invited to visit and perform in China, spoke. He hoped that through this exhibition, more information about Indonesia could be introduced to the Chinese people, so that the Chinese people could better understand the Indonesian people, thereby generating deep friendship and consolidating the foundation of friendship that has existed between the Chinese and Indonesian peoples for a long time in history. He said: "Our bringing artworks to China is only a beginning. It is hoped that the cultural exchanges between the two peoples, who have the same situation, the same goals, and ideals in many aspects, will develop even more than in the past."

    Mr. Mononutu, Ambassador of the Republic of Indonesia to China, was invited to speak at the meeting. He first thanked the Chinese government for providing all assistance in arranging the exhibition. Then, he briefly introduced the development of modern Indonesian art. He said: "The three most important centers of the modern Indonesian art movement are Jakarta, Bandung, and Yogyakarta. Formal education in art by the government now includes two aspects. In Yogyakarta, it is based on the continuation of the early twentieth-century art development in a collective lifestyle, and Bandung is based on the Western academy method. In addition to formal education, there are organizations with various ideologies that play an important role in various art lives."

    Ambassador Mononutu then cut the ribbon for the opening of the exhibition.

    After the ceremony, the distinguished guests visited the exhibits.

    The exhibition is divided into four rooms and includes ninety-six beautiful oil paintings and a selection of exquisite handicrafts.

### 印度尼西亚艺术展览会在北京开幕  周恩来总理等参加了展览会的开幕仪式

1954-08-19
第1版()
专栏：

    印度尼西亚艺术展览会在北京开幕
    周恩来总理等参加了展览会的开幕仪式
    【新华社十八日讯】中央人民政府文化部主办的“印度尼西亚艺术展览会”十八日上午在首都中山公园水榭开幕。
    展览厅布置得庄严、美丽。展览厅的正面序幕上挂着毛泽东主席和苏加诺总统的画像。中国和印度尼西亚共和国的国旗分悬在两边。
    参加展览会开幕仪式的有：中央人民政府政务院总理周恩来，秘书长习仲勋，文化部部长沈雁冰，交通部部长章伯钧，外交部副部长章汉夫，对外贸易部副部长雷任民，文化部副部长郑振铎，出版总署副署长萨空了，政务院文化教育委员会秘书长钱俊瑞、副秘书长范长江，对外文化联络事务局局长洪深，外交部办公厅主任王炳南、亚洲司司长陈家康，北京市人民政府副市长吴晗等。
    参加开幕仪式的，还有：中国人民对外文化协会会长楚图南，首都文学、艺术界著名人士江丰、陈荒煤、张庚、张光年、王曼硕、王任叔、袁水拍、华君武、邵宇等。
    参加开幕仪式的有：印度尼西亚艺术团团长孟阿达斯·纳苏申和部分团员以及印尼大学汉学院院长曾祖森。
    参加开幕仪式的还有印尼驻我国大使莫诺努图。
    应邀参加开幕仪式的外宾还有各国驻华使节和使馆人员。
    开幕仪式由中央人民政府文化部部长沈雁冰主持并致开幕词。他首先感谢印尼艺术团带来这些精美的艺术品，使中国人民有机会全面地了解丰富的印尼艺术，欣赏印尼人民的劳动和智慧的果实。他说：“这次代表团的访问是中国和印度尼西亚两国文化交流中一个巨大的收获，它对于增进两国人民的相互了解和友好团结，将有很大的帮助。”
    接着，应邀来我国访问演出的印度尼西亚艺术团团长孟阿达斯·纳苏申先生讲话。他希望能够通过这次展览会向中国人民介绍更多的印尼情况，使中国人民能够更多地了解印尼人民，从而产生深厚的友谊，并将巩固中国人民和印尼人民之间在历史上很久以来就已经存在着的友谊基础。他说：“我们带艺术品来中国只是一个开始，希望在许多方面有着同样的情况、同样的目的和理想的两国人民的文化交流会比过去更加发展起来。”
    印度尼西亚共和国驻我国大使莫诺努图应邀在会上讲话。他首先感谢中国政府对安排展览会给予一切帮助。接着，他简单地介绍了现代印尼艺术的发展。他说：“现代印尼艺术运动最主要的三个中心是雅加达、万隆和日惹，政府在艺术方面的正式教育现在包括两个方面。在日惹是基于集体生活方式继续二十世纪初的艺术发展，万隆是西方学院的方式。除了正式的教育之外，还有具备各种思潮对各种艺术生活有重要作用的组织。”
    展览会继由莫诺努图大使剪彩。
    仪式进行毕，贵宾们参观了展览品。
    展览会共分四个室，展览品包括九十六件优美的油画和一部分精致的手工艺品。
