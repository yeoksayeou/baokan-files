Title: Five Nations Participating in Colombo Conference Issue Joint Statement; Believe Indochina Armistice Agreements Contribute to Southeast Asian Peace
Author:
Date: 1954-08-06
Page: 4

    Xinhua News Agency, Colombo, 5th—The governments of Burma, Ceylon, Indonesia, India, and Pakistan, the five nations participating in the Colombo South Asian Prime Ministers' Conference, issued the following joint statement on the 3rd regarding the Agreements on the Cessation of Hostilities in Vietnam, Laos, and Cambodia reached at the Geneva Conference:
    "The governments of the five nations participating in the Colombo South Asian Prime Ministers' Conference, namely Burma, Ceylon, Indonesia, India, and Pakistan, express their deep satisfaction with the agreements that have been reached regarding the cessation of hostilities in Vietnam, Cambodia, and Laos.
    "They believe that these agreements are a significant contribution to the consolidation of peace in Southeast Asia, and they give their firm support to these agreements.
    "They hope that, for the sake of international peace and security, these agreements will be fully respected by all countries participating in the Geneva Conference."

### 参加科伦坡会议的五个国家发表联合声明  认为印度支那停战协定有助于东南亚和平

1954-08-06
第4版()
专栏：

    参加科伦坡会议的五个国家发表联合声明
    认为印度支那停战协定有助于东南亚和平
    【新华社五日讯】科伦坡消息：参加科伦坡南亚总理会议的缅甸、锡兰、印度尼西亚、印度和巴基斯坦五国政府，在三日就日内瓦会议达成的越南、老挝、柬埔寨停止敌对行动协定发表联合声明如下：
    “参加科伦坡南亚总理会议的各国政府，即缅甸、锡兰、印度尼西亚、印度和巴基斯坦五国政府，对于已经达成的关于在越南、柬埔寨和老挝停止敌对行动的协定，表示深感满意。
    “它们认为这些协定是对巩固东南亚和平的一个重大的贡献，它们对这些协定给予坚决的支持。
    “它们希望，为了国际和平和安全起见，这些协定将得到参加日内瓦会议的各个国家的充分尊重。”
