Title: Eisenhower Signs Bill Outlawing U.S. Communist Party Activities; U.S. Communist Party Expresses Determination to Defend the Rights of the Party and the People; Organizations Such as the U.S. Progressive Party Denounce U.S. Congress for Passing Anti-Communist Bill
Author:
Date: 1954-08-26
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, August 25th: Washington Dispatch: U.S. President Eisenhower, disregarding the opposition of upright public opinion in the United States and around the world, brazenly signed the bill passed by the U.S. Congress outlawing the activities of the U.S. Communist Party on the 24th. Thus, this fascist bill, which deprives the U.S. Communist Party of its legitimate rights, has officially become a tool for the reactionary U.S. authorities to persecute the U.S. Communist Party and all upright people in the United States.
    
    Xinhua News Agency, August 24th: According to a TASS dispatch from New York on the 23rd: New York's "Daily Worker" reported that the U.S. Communist Party wrote to President Eisenhower, requesting him to veto the bill prohibiting Communist Party activities. The letter was signed by William Z. Foster, Chairman of the National Committee of the U.S. Communist Party, Elizabeth Gurley Flynn, and Pettis Perry.
    
    The letter to Eisenhower stated: "The Communist Party and many other groups unanimously request that the bill regulating Communists be vetoed. This bill is a shocking violation of the Constitution and a major step towards fascism. This bill is an anti-union tool. By passing this bill, a precedent is set for stifling the freedom of speech, freedom of the press, and freedom of association of all Americans in a Nazi manner. Americans holding various political views feel deeply uneasy about this measure. A study of the reaction of national newspapers proves that newspaper editors, legal scholars, constitutional experts, historians, and millions of ordinary Americans believe that this bill is unconstitutional. Trade union organizations, including the AFL and CIO, oppose many provisions of the bill. They believe that this bill infringes upon the right to collective bargaining and is one of the worst anti-union measures passed by the legislature."
    
    The letter pointed out: "This is the first time in American history that the activities of a political party have been banned, a measure that sets a precedent for threatening all political parties." The letter further stated: "World opinion believes that this bill is very similar to the laws once passed by the Hitler's Reichstag… After taking action to ban the activities of the Communist Party and independent trade unions, Hitler's Germany and Mussolini's Italy immediately suppressed all freedoms of citizens."
    
    The letter emphasized: "We are determined to defend the right of a political party to exist legally. We believe that in doing so, we are not only defending the rights of a political party, but also defending the freedom of all Americans."

    Xinhua News Agency, Berlin: According to a report in New York's "Daily Worker," Baldwin, the secretary of the National Committee of the U.S. Progressive Party, sent a telegram to Eisenhower on August 19th, requesting him to veto the bill prohibiting Communist Party activities. The telegram said: This is a "bill brewed in hatred and violating the Constitution, passed by a cowardly Congress in a hysterical atmosphere." The telegram said that this bill "destroys human rights, and signing this bill will greatly destroy our precious freedom." The telegram said that the vast majority of Americans will support vetoing this bill.
    
    Another report: Fitzgerald, president of the United Electrical, Radio and Machine Workers of America (UE), issued a statement to the press, denouncing the bill prohibiting Communist Party activities and restricting trade union activities. He said: This is "the most hateful" measure in our country's history. He asked Eisenhower to veto this bill.

### 艾森豪威尔签署禁止美国共产党活动的法案  美国共产党表示决心捍卫党和人民的权利  美国进步党等组织斥责美国国会通过反共法案

1954-08-26
第4版()
专栏：

    艾森豪威尔签署禁止美国共产党活动的法案
    美国共产党表示决心捍卫党和人民的权利
    美国进步党等组织斥责美国国会通过反共法案
    【新华社二十五日讯】华盛顿消息：美国总统艾森豪威尔不顾美国的和全世界的正直舆论的反对，在二十四日悍然签署了美国国会通过的禁止美国共产党活动的法案。这样，这个剥夺了美国共产党合法权利的法西斯法案，就正式变成为美国反动当局迫害美国共产党和美国一切正直人士的工具。
     【新华社二十四日讯】据塔斯社纽约二十三日讯：纽约“工人日报”报道，美国共产党写信给艾森豪威尔总统，要求否决禁止共产党活动的法案，在信上签名的有美国共产党全国委员会主席威廉·福斯特，伊丽莎白·弗林和佩蒂斯·佩里。
    给艾森豪威尔的信说：“共产党和其他许多团体一致要求否决管制共产党员的法案。这一个法案是对宪法的惊人的破坏，是走向法西斯主义的一大步。这一个法案是反工会的工具。通过这一法案，就为用纳粹方式扼杀全体美国人的言论自由、出版自由和结社自由开了先例。抱有各种不同政治观点的美国人，都对这一措施感到深刻的不安。研究一下全国报纸的反应就可证明，报纸编辑、法律学家、宪法问题专家、历史学家，以及数百万普通美国人都认为这一法案是违背宪法的。工会组织，包括劳联和产联在内，都反对法案中的许多条文。它们认为这个法案侵害了订立集体合同的权利，认为是立法机关通过的最恶劣的反工会措施之一。”
    信中指出：“在美国历史上，这还是第一次禁止政党活动，这一个措施为威胁一切政党开了先例。”信中又说：“世界舆论认为，这一个法案和希特勒国会曾经通过的法律十分相似……希特勒德国和墨索里尼意大利在采取行动禁止共产党和独立工会活动之后，紧接着就扼制公民的一切自由。”
    信中强调说：“我们决心捍卫一个政党的合法存在的权利。我们认为，我们这样做不仅是捍卫一个政党的权利，而且是捍卫全体美国人的自由。”
   【新华社柏林讯】据纽约“工人日报”报道，美国进步党全国委员会书记鲍德温八月十九日打电报给艾森豪威尔，要求他否决禁止共产党活动的法案。电报说：这是一个“在仇恨中酝酿出来的，违反宪法的法案，它是由懦怯的国会在歇斯底里的气氛中通过的”。电报说，这个法案“是破坏人权的，签署这个法案，就将大大地摧毁我们宝贵的自由”。电报说，极大多数的美国人将支持否决这个法案。
    另讯：美国独立的联合电气及无线电工人工会主席费兹吉拉德向报界发表声明，斥责禁止共产党活动和限制工会活动的法案。他说：这是“我国历史上最令人痛恨的”措施。他要求艾森豪威尔否决这个法案。
