Title: British People Oppose U.S. Troops Stationed in Britain
Author:
Date: 1954-08-08
Page: 4

    Xinhua News Agency dispatch: An article published in the British "Manchester Guardian" on July 31 stated: "The British people's aversion to American 'guests' has reached such a point that Americans are unwilling to walk alone on the streets of Manchester, Liverpool, or London." "Sometimes, an American soldier might be dragged into an alley off Oxford Street... More commonly, groups of jeering youths harass Americans."
    The article stated that Americans in Britain are completely isolated because the British people do not associate with them and even avoid talking to them. When people occasionally talk to them, it is often to complain about American foreign policy or attack McCarthy. The article pointed out that the people who talk to them in this way are not always intellectuals or communists, but ordinary working people.

### 英国人民反对美军驻在英国

1954-08-08
第4版()
专栏：

    英国人民反对美军驻在英国
    据新华社讯：英国“曼彻斯特卫报”七月三十一日刊载的一篇文章写道：“英国人对美国‘客人’的厌恶竟到了这种地步，使得美国人不愿意单独在曼彻斯特、利物浦或者伦敦的街上行走。”“有时候，一个美国士兵可能被拖入牛津街上的一条小巷……。更常见的情况是：一些美国人被一群群大叫大骂的青年弄得狼狈不堪。”
    文章写道，在英国的美国人是完全孤立的，因为英国人民不和他们交往，甚至避免和他们谈话。人们偶尔和他们谈话时也往往是抱怨美国的外交政策或者攻击麦卡锡。文章指出，和他们谈这种话的人并不总是知识分子或共产党人，而是普通的劳动人民。
