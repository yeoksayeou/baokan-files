Title: People's Democratic Countries Celebrate the October Revolution Anniversary
Author:
Date: 1953-11-10
Page: 4

    People's Democratic Countries Celebrate the October Revolution Anniversary
    [Xinhua News Agency, 9th] The working people of the Eastern European People's Democratic countries and the Mongolian People's Republic warmly celebrated the 36th anniversary of the great October Socialist Revolution.
    On the eve of the October Revolution anniversary, the working people of Warsaw, the capital of Poland, held a celebration meeting. Those present included: Aleksander Zawadzki, Chairman of the State Council of the Polish People's Republic; Bolesław Bierut, Chairman of the Council of Ministers of Poland and Chairman of the Central Committee of the United Workers' Party; P.T. Popkov, the Soviet Ambassador to Poland; and diplomatic envoys from various People's Democratic countries. Stolyarov, the head of the Soviet Foreign Cultural Association delegation currently in Poland for the Soviet-Polish Friendship Month and Deputy Minister of Culture of the Soviet Union, also attended the celebration meeting. After Zawadzki presided over the opening of the meeting, Henryk Jóźwiakowski, member of the Central Committee of the Polish United Workers' Party and Vice-Chairman of the Council of Ministers, delivered a lengthy report commemorating the October Revolution anniversary. Soviet Deputy Minister of Culture Stolyarov also spoke at the meeting. On the anniversary day, representatives of the Central Committee of the Polish United Workers' Party, the State Council of the Republic, and government organs laid wreaths at the Soviet Army Fallen Soldiers Memorial in Warsaw. Other cities in Poland also held gatherings to celebrate the October Revolution anniversary.
    In Prague, the capital of Czechoslovakia, a grand celebration meeting attended by over 100,000 people was held on the afternoon of the 6th. Republic Premier Viliam Široký, Communist Party Central Committee First Secretary Antonín Novotný, and diplomatic envoys from the Soviet Union and other countries attended the celebration meeting. At the meeting, Premier Široký delivered a speech, emphasizing that Czechoslovak-Soviet friendship is the guarantee of Czechoslovakia's independence and progress. That evening, Prague also held a mass carnival. On the 7th, at the Radbuza River bank in Pilsen City, over 80,000 people and representatives from various districts of the Pilsen region held the unveiling ceremony of the Stalin memorial statue. Those attending the ceremony included Republic President Antonín Zápotocký, a government delegation headed by First Deputy Premier and Minister of National Defense Alexej Čepička, members of the Communist Party Central Committee, and Krekochin, the Counselor of the Soviet Embassy in Czechoslovakia. In addition, the central cities of other states and regions in Czechoslovakia also held gatherings of tens of thousands of people to celebrate the October Revolution anniversary and the Czechoslovak-Soviet Friendship Month.
    The working people of Bucharest, the capital of Romania, also held a grand gathering and parade on the 7th. The leaders of the Romanian Workers' Party and the government, headed by Gheorghe Gheorghiu-Dej, Chairman of the Presidium of the Grand National Assembly, Constantin Parhon, Soviet Ambassador to Romania Melnikov, and representatives of the Soviet delegation participating in the Romanian-Soviet Friendship Month, attended this gathering and reviewed the parade of the working people. The working people of Iași, Stalin City, and many other places also held parades to celebrate the anniversary.
    The working people of various cities in Bulgaria held grand parades on the 7th. Two hundred and fifty thousand people participated in the parade in Sofia, the capital. The leaders of the Bulgarian Communist Party and the government, headed by Valko Chervenkov, Soviet Ambassador to Bulgaria Podurov, and others, reviewed the ranks of the working people from the mausoleum platform of Georgi Dimitrov.
    People across Hungary held gatherings to celebrate the October Revolution anniversary on the 6th. On the morning of the 7th, the working people of Budapest, the capital of Hungary, held a wreath-laying ceremony at the Soviet Army Martyrs Memorial in Freedom Square. Mátyás Rákosi, Secretary of the Central Committee of the Hungarian Working People's Party, laid a wreath on behalf of the Party Central Committee, Imre Nagy, Chairman of the Council of Ministers, laid a wreath on behalf of the Council of Ministers, and József Révai, Vice-Chairman of the Presidential Council of the Republic, laid a wreath on behalf of the Presidential Council of the Republic. Many other cities and villages in Hungary also held wreath-laying ceremonies at the Soviet Army Martyrs Memorials.
    The people of Tirana, the capital of Albania, also held a gathering on the 6th to celebrate the October Revolution anniversary. Those attending the celebration included Enver Hoxha, Chairman of the Council of Ministers of Albania and First Secretary of the Central Committee of the Labor Party, Haxhi Lleshi, Chairman of the Presidium of the People's Assembly, as well as the heads of various mass organizations and representatives of the working people. Koleka, a member of the Political Bureau of the Central Committee of the Labor Party, delivered a report celebrating the anniversary at the meeting.
    Various cities and villages of the Mongolian People's Republic held parades to celebrate the October Revolution anniversary. In Sukhbaatar Square in Ulaanbaatar, thousands of residents held a celebration meeting. Damba, Secretary of the Central Committee of the Mongolian People's Revolutionary Party, spoke at the meeting. After reviewing the Ulaanbaatar garrison troops, the meeting was followed by a mass parade.

### 各人民民主国家庆祝十月革命节

1953-11-10
第4版()
专栏：

    各人民民主国家庆祝十月革命节
    【新华社九日讯】东欧人民民主国家和蒙古人民共和国的劳动人民，热烈庆祝伟大的十月社会主义革命三十六周年。
    波兰首都华沙的劳动人民在十月革命节的前夕举行了庆祝会。到会的有：波兰人民共和国国务委员会主席团主席萨瓦茨基，波兰部长会议主席、统一工人党中央委员会主席贝鲁特，苏联驻波兰大使波波夫和各人民民主国家的外交使节。现在波兰参加波苏友好月的苏联对外文化协会代表团团长、苏联文化部副部长斯托列托夫也参加了庆祝会。大会由萨瓦茨基主持开幕后，波兰统一工人党中央委员、部长会议副主席英德里霍夫斯基作了纪念十月革命节的长篇报告。苏联文化部副部长斯托列托夫也在会上讲了话。在节日那一天，波兰统一工人党中央委员会、共和国国务委员会和政府各机关的代表，都到华沙的苏军阵亡将士纪念碑前献了花圈。波兰其他各城市也举行了庆祝十月革命节的集会。
    在捷克斯洛伐克首都布拉格，六日下午举行了有十余万人参加的盛大的庆祝会。共和国总理西罗基，共产党中央委员会第一书记诺沃提尼和苏联及其他国家的外交使节等参加了庆祝会。会上，西罗基总理发表了演说，他强调指出捷苏友好是捷克斯洛伐克独立和进步的保证。当晚，布拉格并举行了群众游艺会。七日，在比尔森城拉德布查河岸边，八万多人民和比尔森州各区的代表举行了斯大林纪念像的揭幕典礼。参加典礼的有共和国总统萨波托斯基、以第一副总理兼国防部长契皮茨卡为首的政府代表团、共产党中央委员会委员，以及苏联驻捷大使馆参赞克雷科钦。此外，捷克斯洛伐克其他各州区的中心城市，也都举行了上万人的集会，庆祝十月革命节和捷苏友好月。
    罗马尼亚首都布加勒斯特的劳动人民也在七日举行了盛大的集会和游行。以乔治乌—德治为首的罗马尼亚工人党和政府的领袖们，国民议会主席团主席格罗查，苏联驻罗大使麦尔尼科夫和参加罗苏友好月的苏联代表团代表等，都出席了这个集会，并检阅了劳动人民的游行队伍。雅西、斯大林城和其他许多地方的劳动人民也都举行了庆祝节日的游行。
    保加利亚各城市的劳动人民在七日举行了盛大的游行。参加首都索非亚游行的有二十五万人。以契尔文科夫为首的保加利亚共产党和政府的领袖们，苏联驻保加利亚大使波德罗夫等，在季米特洛夫陵墓的墓台上检阅了劳动人民的队伍。
    匈牙利全国各地人民在六日举行了庆祝十月革命节的集会。七日晨，匈牙利首都布达佩斯的劳动人民在自由广场上的苏军烈士纪念碑前举行了献花圈的典礼。匈牙利劳动人民党中央委员会书记拉科西代表党中央委员会，部长会议主席纳吉代表部长会议，共和国主席团副主席里瓦伊代表共和国主席团献了花圈。匈牙利其他许多城市和乡村也举行了向苏军烈士纪念碑献花圈的典礼。
    阿尔巴尼亚首都地拉那的人民也在六日举行了庆祝十月革命节的集会。参加庆祝会的有阿尔巴尼亚部长会议主席、劳动党中央委员会总书记霍查，人民议会主席团主席列希，以及各群众团体负责人和劳动人民的代表等。劳动党中央委员会政治局委员科列加在会上作了庆祝节日的报告。
    蒙古人民共和国的各城市和乡村都举行了庆祝十月革命节的游行。在乌兰巴托的苏赫—巴托尔广场上。数千居民举行了庆祝大会。蒙古人民革命党中央委员会书记达姆巴在大会上讲了话。大会在检阅了乌兰巴托卫戍部队之后，接着举行了群众游行。
