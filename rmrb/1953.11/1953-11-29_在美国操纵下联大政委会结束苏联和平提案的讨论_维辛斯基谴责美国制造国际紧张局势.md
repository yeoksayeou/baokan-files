Title: Under US Manipulation, UN Political Committee Ends Discussion of Soviet Peace Proposal; Vyshinsky Condemns US for Creating International Tension
Author:
Date: 1953-11-29
Page: 4
Xinhua News Agency, November 28th Dispatch from New York: The United Nations General Assembly's Political Committee concluded its discussion on the Soviet Union's proposal regarding "Measures to Eliminate the Threat of Another World War and Ease International Tension" on November 26th.

    Soviet representative Vyshinsky spoke at the meeting. He refuted the arguments of the US, British representatives, and representatives of other countries following them against the Soviet proposal. Vyshinsky said that despite the attempts of certain national delegations, primarily the US delegation, to obliterate the importance of the Soviet proposal, it still displayed great strength before everyone. The Soviet delegation firmly believed that the Soviet proposal pointed out the only correct path for eliminating the threat of another world war and easing international tension. The United Nations must concentrate enough strength to eliminate the obstacles placed on this path by the reactionary groups of major Western powers. The reactionary groups of major Western powers do not want to ease international tension but intend to further worsen the international situation; they do not want to eliminate the threat of a new war but intend to launch a new world war. This situation must be stopped, and these obstacles must be eliminated.

    US representative Lodge spoke next, once again futilely defending the US's aggressive actions.

    Subsequently, the Political Committee voted on the Soviet proposal section by section.

    The preamble of the Soviet proposal, namely the section regarding the belief that the Korean armistice was a significant step towards easing international tension, received twenty-one votes in favor. Thirty abstained, and not a single representative dared to vote against this section.

    The voting on the main body of the Soviet proposal demonstrated that the US-British bloc was not interested in adopting measures to eliminate the threat of another world war and ease international tension. The mechanical majority following the United States, by a majority of thirty-two votes, rejected the section of the Soviet proposal regarding the declaration of an unconditional ban on atomic weapons, hydrogen weapons, and other weapons of mass destruction, and the establishment of strict international supervision. Fourteen countries (India, Burma, Pakistan, Arab countries, and Argentina, Mexico, Israel) abstained. The Soviet Union and the People's Democracies voted in favor.

    The US-British bloc rejected the section of the Soviet proposal regarding the suggestion that the five major powers reduce their armed forces by one-third within one year with the same number of votes. Fourteen countries including Arab countries, India, Burma, and Argentina abstained.

    The section of the Soviet proposal condemning the establishment of military bases on foreign territories received twelve votes in favor (the Soviet Union and the People's Democracies, Syria, Yemen, Egypt, Saudi Arabia, India, Lebanon, Iraq). Nine delegations (Yugoslavia, Iran, Afghanistan, Argentina, Bolivia, Burma, Pakistan, Indonesia, Mexico) abstained, and twenty-nine delegations voted against it.

    The recommendation to abolish military bases established on foreign territories received seven votes in favor (the Soviet Union and the People's Democracies, Syria, Egypt). Ten Asian and Arab countries and Yugoslavia, Argentina abstained, and thirty-two voted against.

    The section of the Soviet proposal condemning war propaganda received six votes in favor (the Soviet Union and the People's Democracies and Iran), ten Asian and Arab countries and Argentina, Mexico, Venezuela abstained, and thirty-two voted against.

    Because the US-British bloc rejected the various sections of the main body of the Soviet proposal, the entire Soviet proposal was not put to a vote.

### 在美国操纵下联大政委会结束苏联和平提案的讨论  维辛斯基谴责美国制造国际紧张局势

1953-11-29
第4版()
专栏：

    在美国操纵下联大政委会结束苏联和平提案的讨论
    维辛斯基谴责美国制造国际紧张局势
    【新华社二十八日讯】据塔斯社纽约讯：联合国大会政治委员会，于十一月二十六日结束了苏联提出的“关于消除另一次世界大战的威胁和缓和国际紧张局势的措施”的讨论。
    苏联代表维辛斯基在会上发言。他驳斥了美国、英国代表和跟随他们的其他国家代表反对苏联提案的言论。维辛斯基说，尽管某些国家代表团，首先是美国代表团千方百计地抹煞苏联提案的重要性，但是它仍然在大家面前显示出极大的力量。苏联代表团深信，苏联提案为消除另一次世界大战的威胁和缓和国际紧张局势指出了唯一正确的道路。联合国必须集中足够的力量，来消除西方各大国反动集团在这条道路上所安置的障碍。西方各大国的反动集团不想缓和国际紧张局势，却打算进一步使国际局势恶化；不想消除新战争的威胁，却打算发动一次新的世界大战。这种情况必须制止，这些障碍必须消除。
    美国代表洛奇接着发言，他再一次徒劳地为美国的侵略行动辩护。
    接着，政治委员会对苏联提案进行逐节表决。
    苏联提案中的序言，即关于认为朝鲜停战是导向国际紧张局势缓和的重大步骤的一节，得到二十一票赞成。弃权的三十票，没有一个代表敢投票反对这一节。
    对苏联提案主文的表决，说明美英集团对于通过消除另一次世界大战的威胁和缓和国际紧张局势的措施是不感兴趣的。追随美国的机械多数，以三十二票多数否决了苏联提案中关于宣布无条件禁止原子武器、氢武器和其它大规模毁灭性武器并建立严格国际监督的一节。十四个国家（印度、缅甸、巴基斯坦、阿拉伯国家及阿根廷、墨西哥、以色列）弃权。苏联及人民民主国家投票赞成。
    美英集团以同样的票数否决了苏联提案关于建议五大国在一年之内把武装部队裁减三分之一的一节。阿拉伯国家、印度、缅甸与阿根廷等十四国弃权。
    苏联提案中关于谴责在外国领土上建立军事基地的一节，得到十二票赞成（苏联及人民民主国家、叙利亚、也门、埃及、沙特阿拉伯、印度、黎巴嫩、伊拉克）。九个代表团（南斯拉夫、伊朗、阿富汗、阿根廷、玻利维亚、缅甸、巴基斯坦、印度尼西亚、墨西哥）弃权，二十九个代表团投反对票。
    要求取消在外国领土上建立的军事基地的建议，得到七票（苏联及人民民主国家、叙利亚、埃及）赞成。十个亚洲及阿拉伯国家和南斯拉夫、阿根廷弃权，三十二票反对。
    苏联提案关于谴责战争宣传的一节，得到六票（苏联及人民民主国家和伊朗）赞成，十个亚洲及阿拉伯国家和阿根廷、墨西哥、委内瑞拉弃权，三十二票反对。
    由于美英集团否决了苏联提案主文各节，苏联整个提案没有交付表决。
