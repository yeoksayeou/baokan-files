Title: Deputy Prime Minister and Foreign Minister Bolz of the German Democratic Republic Issues Statement Welcoming the Soviet Government's Note to the Governments of France, Britain and the United States
Author:
Date: 1953-11-30
Page: 4
Xinhua News Agency, Berlin, November 28th: Lothar Bolz, Deputy Prime Minister and Foreign Minister of the German Democratic Republic, made a statement on the Soviet government's note of November 26, saying: "I warmly welcome the Soviet government's note to the governments of the United States, Britain, and France proposing a four-power conference in Berlin. The political viewpoint expressed in this note makes the statement made by Acting Prime Minister Walter Ulbricht of the German Democratic Republic on November 25 even more irrefutable. Ulbricht said: The implementation of the 'European Defense Community' treaty will deepen the division of Germany, make the conclusion of a peace treaty impossible, and its purpose is to launch a new world war, which will also be a German civil war."

Bolz emphasized: At such a four-power conference, agreements can be reached through consultation with the aim of peacefully resolving the German question, as mentioned in the statement of the Government of the German Democratic Republic. He said that the speedy conclusion of a peace treaty with Germany with the participation of an all-German government would eliminate the harmful division of Germany and the danger of a new war, and would help establish an independent, unified, peace-loving, and democratic Germany.

### 民主德国副总理兼外长博尔茨发表谈话  欢迎苏联政府给法英美三国政府的照会

1953-11-30
第4版()
专栏：

    民主德国副总理兼外长博尔茨发表谈话
    欢迎苏联政府给法英美三国政府的照会
    【新华社二十九日讯】据德意志通讯社柏林二十八日讯：德意志民主共和国副总理兼外交部长洛·博尔茨就苏联政府十一月二十六日的照会发表谈话说：“我热烈地欢迎苏联政府给美、英、法三国政府建议在柏林召开四国会议的照会。这个照会所表达的政治观点使得德意志民主共和国代理总理瓦尔特·乌布利希在十一月二十五日发表的政府声明中所说的话成为更加不可辩驳的了。乌布利希说：实现‘欧洲防务集团’条约将要加深德国的分裂，将要使得缔结和约成为不可能，其目的是发动一次新的世界战争，这次战争同时也就将是一次德国人的内战。”
    博尔茨强调：在四国的这样一次会议上，可以通过协商达成目的在和平解决德国问题的协议，这也是德意志民主共和国政府的声明中所提到的。他说，在一个全德政府的参加下，迅速缔结对德和约，将消除对德国有害的分裂和一次新战争的危险，并将帮助建立起一个独立、统一、爱好和平与民主的德国。
