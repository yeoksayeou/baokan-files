Title: Premier Kim Il-sung Telegraphed Chairman Mao to Express Gratitude Before Returning Home; Korean Government Delegation Returned to Pyongyang Yesterday
Author:
Date: 1953-11-28
Page: 1
    Xinhua News Agency, November 27th: Chairman Mao Zedong of the Central People's Government of China received a telegram from Kim Il-sung, Premier of the Cabinet of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea, upon leaving Chinese territory in Andong. The text of the telegram is as follows:

    Beijing, Chairman Mao Zedong of the Central People's Government of the People's Republic of China:

    As we leave the border of great China, on behalf of the delegation of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea, I once again express our deep gratitude to you and the Central People's Government of the People's Republic of China for your care and hospitality.

    I believe that the achievements of the Beijing talks will further consolidate the friendship and solidarity between the peoples of Korea and China, and ensure the restoration and development of the Korean people's economy after the war, which will be conducive to our country's peaceful reunification and independent struggle. At the same time, it will also make a great contribution to the cause of peace and security in the Far East.

    I hope that the fraternal Chinese people will achieve greater success in the construction of a new life, and sincerely wish you good health.

      Kim Il-sung, Premier of the Cabinet of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea
              November 26, 1953, in Andong

    Xinhua News Agency, Pyongyang, November 27th: Marshal Kim Il-sung, the leader of the Korean people, and the government delegation of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea, which he led, returned to Pyongyang by special train at 12:00 noon on the 27th. Gan Yetao, Chargé d'Affaires ad interim of our country's embassy in the Democratic People's Republic of Korea, accompanied them.

    Those who welcomed the delegation at Pyongyang Station included: Vice Premier of the Cabinet and Minister of National Defense Choi Yong-kun, Vice Premiers Choi Chang-ik and Jung Il-ryong, Vice Chairman of the Standing Committee of the Supreme People's Assembly Hong Ki-ju, Secretary-General Kang Ryang-uk, Vice Chairmen of the Supreme People's Assembly Kim Dal-hyun and Lee Young, Director of the Organization and Guidance Department of the Central Committee of the Workers' Party of Korea Park Young-bin, Director of the Propaganda and Agitation Department Kim Chang-man, Chief of the General Staff of the Korean People's Army Kim Kwang-hyup, Director of the General Political Bureau Choi Jong-hak, ministers of the Cabinet, senior generals of the People's Army, Chairman of the Presidium of the Central Committee of the Democratic Front for the Unification of the Fatherland, heads of democratic parties and people's organizations, Choi Il, Ambassador of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea to our country, Ko Bong-ki, Chairman of the Pyongyang Municipal Committee of the Workers' Party of Korea, Jung Yeon-pyo, Chairman of the Pyongyang Municipal People's Committee, and people from all walks of life in Pyongyang.

    Also present at the station to welcome the delegation were Soviet Ambassador to Korea Suzdalev, First Secretary of the Chinese Embassy in Korea Li Shi, diplomatic envoys from various countries stationed in Korea, and embassy staff.

    The national flags of Korea and China were hung at the station. As the special train entered the station, the military band played a solemn welcome song. After Marshal Kim Il-sung and the representatives of the delegation got off the train, they shook hands with the welcomers one by one and reviewed the guard of honor.

    Kim Il, Vice Chairman of the Central Committee of the Workers' Party of Korea, Vice Premier of the Cabinet Park Ui-wan, Minister of Internal Affairs Fang Hak-se, and Director of the Bureau of Affairs of the Cabinet Kim Chang-hyup, etc., went to Sinuiju on the border to welcome the government delegation and returned to Pyongyang on the same train with the delegation.

### 金日成首相返国前电毛主席致谢  朝鲜政府代表团昨天回到平壤

1953-11-28
第1版()
专栏：

    金日成首相返国前电毛主席致谢
    朝鲜政府代表团昨天回到平壤
    【新华社二十七日讯】我中央人民政府毛泽东主席收到了朝鲜民主主义人民共和国内阁首相金日成在离开我国国境安东时的来电。电文如下：北京市中华人民共和国中央人民政府毛泽东主席：
    兹值离开伟大中国的边境之际，我以朝鲜民主主义人民共和国代表团的名义，再次对于尊敬的您和中华人民共和国中央人民政府给予我们的关心和款待，表示深切的感谢。
    我相信此次北京会谈的成就，将更加巩固朝中两国人民间的友谊团结，并保障战后朝鲜人民经济的恢复发展，从而有利于我国的和平统一、独立的斗争。同时，对于远东的和平安全事业，亦将有巨大的贡献。
    我希望兄弟的中国人民在新生活的建设中获得更大的成就，并衷心祝贺您身体健康。
      朝鲜民主主义人民共和国内阁首相金日成
              一九五三年十一月二十六日于安东
    【新华社平壤二十七日电】朝鲜人民领袖金日成元帅和他所率领的朝鲜民主主义人民共和国政府访问中国代表团，乘专车于二十七日上午十二时返抵平壤。我国驻朝鲜民主主义人民共和国大使馆临时代办甘野陶陪同到达。
    到平壤车站欢迎代表团的有：内阁副首相兼民族保卫相崔庸健，副首相崔昌益、郑一龙，最高人民会议常任委员会副委员长洪箕畴、书记长康良煜，最高人民会议副议长金达铉、李英，朝鲜劳动党中央组织指导部部长朴永彬、宣传鼓动部部长金昌满，朝鲜人民军总参谋长金光侠、总政治局局长崔宗学，内阁各相，人民军高级将领，祖国统一民主主义战线中央委员会议长团议长，各民主政党、人民团体负责人，朝鲜民主主义人民共和国驻我国大使崔一，朝鲜劳动党平壤市委员会委员长高凤起，平壤市人民委员会委员长郑然彪和平壤各界人士等。
    到车站欢迎的还有苏联驻朝鲜大使苏兹达列夫，我国驻朝鲜大使馆一等秘书李石和各国驻朝鲜外交使节及使馆人员等。
    车站上悬挂朝、中两国国旗。当专车进站时，军乐队奏起庄严的欢迎曲。金日成元帅和代表团代表们下车后，与欢迎者一一握手，并检阅了仪仗队。
    朝鲜劳动党中央委员会副委员长金一，内阁副首相朴义琓，内务相方学世，内阁事务局局长金昌洽等曾到国境新义州欢迎政府代表团，并与代表团同车回到平壤。
