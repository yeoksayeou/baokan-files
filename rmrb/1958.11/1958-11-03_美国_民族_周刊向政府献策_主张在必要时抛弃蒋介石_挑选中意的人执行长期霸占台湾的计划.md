Title: U.S. "Nation" Weekly Offers Advice to Government, Advocating Abandoning Chiang Kai-shek When Necessary, Selecting a Favored Person to Implement a Plan for Long-Term Occupation of Taiwan

Author:

Date: 1958-11-03
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, 31st dispatch from Washington: The American "Nation" weekly, reflecting the opinions of many Democrats, published an article by Alexander DeConde on the 25th, openly stating that Chiang Kai-shek has become a burden to the United States today, and advocating the cancellation of the US-Chiang "treaty." The article used a very impolite sentence as its title: "Mr. Chiang, the Entanglement."

The article said that the current crisis in the Taiwan Strait "has caused confusion and division in American public opinion regarding foreign policy, a situation that has not existed since Eisenhower entered the White House."

The article said: "The nature of the events and the alliance itself (referring to the US-Chiang military treaty) seems to indicate that the Formosa Treaty is an unnecessary entanglement, which is the root of our current dilemma."

The article said, "The United States does not want to obligate itself to a war in which most of the fighting is to be borne by the United States but only serves Chiang's interests."

The article said that American critics of the government pointed out that "Chiang (Kai-shek) is a dangerous debt (for the United States)." The article said mercilessly, "The time has come to realize that Chiang (Kai-shek) is a debt we cannot afford. One of the initial steps we can take to reduce this debt will be to clarify our alliance with him, or, if necessary, completely abandon this alliance (we are legally entitled to do so)."

The article continued, "This does not necessarily mean abandoning Chiang, because we can still defend Formosa." But this sentence clearly indicates that the author of the article has not completely ruled out such a practice: kicking Chiang Kai-shek aside and directly implementing a plan for long-term occupation of Taiwan and creating "two Chinas" by the United States itself or by someone it favors more.

The article believes that "such a step would be a retreat in the face of Communist forces, but we have no choice." The article also pointed out that the United States does not lack precedents for abandoning "allies," "We cancelled our first alliance with France when it was no longer useful."

### 美国“民族”周刊向政府献策  主张在必要时抛弃蒋介石  挑选中意的人执行长期霸占台湾的计划

1958-11-03
第4版()
专栏：

    美国“民族”周刊向政府献策
    主张在必要时抛弃蒋介石
    挑选中意的人执行长期霸占台湾的计划
    新华社31日讯　华盛顿消息：反映许多民主党人意见的美国“民族”周刊，25日刊载了亚历山大·德康德的一篇文章，公开表示蒋介石今天成了美国的累赘，鼓吹取消美蒋“条约”。文章以十分不客气的一句话作为它的标题：“牵连人的蒋先生”。
    这篇文章说，目前台湾海峡的危机“已经使美国舆论对外交政策的看法发生混乱和分歧，这种情况是自从艾森豪威尔进入白宫以来从未有过的。”
    文章说：“事件和（美蒋）联盟本身的性质似乎表明，福摩萨条约（指美蒋军事条约）是一种不必要的牵连，它是我们目前的进退两难的局面的根源。”
    文章说，“美国并不希望使自己承担义务进行一场大部分战斗要由美国来担负却只是为蒋的利益服务的战争”。
    文章说，美国批评政府的人指出，“蒋（介石）是（美国）一笔危险的债务”。文章毫不留情地说，“现在是时候了，应当认识到蒋
  （介石）是一笔我们没有力量担负的债务。我们减少这笔债务所能采取的最初的步骤之一将是澄清我们与他的联盟，或者如有必要的话，完全抛弃这个联盟（我们在法律上是有权这样做的）。”
    文章接着说，“这不一定意味着抛弃蒋，因为我们仍然能够保卫福摩萨。”但是这句话显然表明，文章的作者完全没有排除这样的作法：一脚踢开蒋介石，由美国自己或者它更中意的人来直接执行长期霸占台湾，制造“两个中国”的计划。
    文章认为，“这样一个步骤将是在共产党力量的面前退却，但是我们没有什么选择”。文章还指出，美国抛弃“盟友”并不缺乏先例，“当我们同法国的第一个联盟不再有用时，我们就取消了这个联盟。”
