Title: American Newspapers Lament: Americans' Common Sense is Extremely Lacking; Know Nothing of the High-Speed Development of China and the Soviet Union
Author:
Date: 1958-11-16
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, 14th: Boston dispatch: An article by Sterlingger, the Washington bureau chief of the American "Christian Science Monitor," complains that the level of common sense of the average American is shockingly low.

    He said, "The (American) news media faces a permanent challenge. A large part of the public often really seems not to need to know anything." The author pointed out that, according to national opinion polls in the United States, before the Soviet Union's first artificial satellite went into space, only 6% of those surveyed knew about how space satellites worked, which was not surprising. However, after the first artificial satellite went into space, only 12% still knew anything about satellite issues.

    The author gave another example: on the day the Soviet Union launched its first artificial satellite, a newspaper in Milwaukee, Wisconsin, ran the headline: "Today We Make History," but this headline referred to the World Series of softball; the news of the Soviet satellite was published on an inside page.

    The author complained that today millions of Americans "know nothing" about such "urgent issues" as the high-speed development of China and the Soviet Union, the construction of India, and the movements of Arab countries to strive for independence.

    He said that an opinion poll conducted in the United States a few years ago showed that even "only a relatively small number of Americans really know who Chiang Kai-shek is."

### 美国报纸感叹  美国人的常识十分贫乏  对中苏高速度发展一无所知

1958-11-16
第4版()
专栏：

    美国报纸感叹
    美国人的常识十分贫乏
    对中苏高速度发展一无所知
    新华社14日讯　波士顿消息：美国“基督教科学箴言报”刊载的该报驻华盛顿办事处主任斯特林格的一篇文章，抱怨一般美国人的常识水平低得惊人。
    他说，“（美国）报纸的新闻媒介面对着一种永久性的挑战。很大一部分公众常常真的好像不需要知道什么似的。”作者指出，根据美国全国性的民意测验，在苏联的第一个人造卫星上了天之前，在被调查的人中只有6％的人知道关于空间卫星如何运转的问题，这是没有什么奇怪的。但是在第一颗人造卫星上了天之后，知道一点卫星问题的仍然只有12％。
    作者又举了另一个例子：在苏联发射了第一颗人造卫星的那一天，美国密尔窝基州的一家报纸标着这样的大字标题：“今天我们创造历史”，但是这个标题是指垒球的世界锦标赛的；苏联人造卫星的消息却登载在里页。
    作者抱怨说，今天数以百万计的美国人对关于中国和苏联高速度的发展，印度的建设以及阿拉伯国家争取独立的运动等这样一些“迫切的问题”“并无所知”。
    他说，几年前在美国举行的一次民意测验表明，甚至“真正知道蒋介石是谁的只有比较少数的美国人”。
