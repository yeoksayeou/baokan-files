Title: Mars Experiences Storms: Increased Sunspots May Be the Cause
Author:
Date: 1958-11-24
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, 23rd dispatch: Mars is currently experiencing a period of great storms. The storms have stirred up dust and sand from the surface of Mars, and the Martian atmosphere is filled with yellowish-brown dust clouds, making it impossible to see the terrain that is usually easily distinguishable on the surface of Mars. This was stated by Professor Sharonov, a famous Soviet astronomer and director of the Leningrad University Observatory, to a TASS reporter on the 22nd.

When asked about the cause of these large dust clouds, Sharonov said that there is a hypothesis that the increase in sunspots and other processes occurring on the Sun's intensely burning surface can affect the Martian atmosphere, just as it affects the Earth's atmosphere.

### 火星起风暴  原因可能是太阳黑子增多

1958-11-24
第5版()
专栏：

    火星起风暴
    原因可能是太阳黑子增多
    据新华社23日讯　火星目前正在经历着大风暴时期，风暴从火星表面掀起了尘沙，火星大气层中充满了发黄的尘雾，以致火星表面通常很容易分辨的地形都看不清了。这是苏联著名天文学家、列宁格勒大学天文台台长夏罗诺夫教授22日对塔斯社记者说的。
    夏罗诺夫在回答这种大尘雾产生的原因的时候说，有一种设想，即太阳的大火熊熊的表面上所发生的黑子增多和其它过程，可以影响火星大气层，就像它对地球大气层的影响一样。
