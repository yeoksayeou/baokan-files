Title: Scientific Books and Translated Books: Soviet Union Ranked First in 1956
Author:
Date: 1959-06-03
Page: 7

Scientific Books and Translated Books
In 1956, the Soviet Union Ranked First in Both

According to the report on the publication of books and newspapers in various countries contained in the 1958 United Nations Statistical Yearbook published not long ago, the total number of books published in the Soviet Union in 1956 was 59,530. In that year, the Soviet Union published the most theoretical and applied science books in the world. In 1956, the Soviet Union also ranked first in the publication of translated books. In that year, thirty countries published a total of 27,617 translated books, of which the Soviet Union accounted for 4,641. According to reports on the publication situation in 1957 (the yearbook does not contain figures related to the Soviet Union after 1956): in terms of the content of published books, Japan published the most philosophical books, India published the most religious books, and the United Kingdom published the most theoretical science books. In that year, the United States consumed the most newsprint, accounting for 50% of the world's newsprint consumption (but 3% less than in 1956), followed by the United Kingdom, accounting for 8% (6% more than in 1956). The United Kingdom had the highest sales volume of daily newspapers, with 573 copies per thousand people, followed by Sweden, with 462 copies per thousand people. Following were: Luxembourg, Finland, Japan, New Zealand, Australia, Denmark, Norway, and the United States (337 copies per thousand people).

### 科学书籍和翻译书籍的出版一九五六年苏联均占第一

1959-06-03
第7版()
专栏：学术动态

    科学书籍和翻译书籍的出版
    一九五六年苏联均占第一
    根据不久前发表的1958年联合国统计年鉴所载关于各国书报出版情况的报道，1956年苏联出书总数五万九千五百三十种。这一年苏联出版的理论科学和实用科学书籍占世界第一位。1956年在翻译书籍出版方面苏联也占第一位。这一年三十个国家共出版二万七千六百十七种翻译书籍，苏联占了四千六百四十一种。据报道1957年的出版情况是：（年鉴中没有1956年以后的有关苏联的数字）按出版书籍的内容来说，这一年出版哲学书籍最多的是日本，出版宗教书籍最多的是印度，出版理论科学书籍最多的是英国。这一年新闻纸消耗量最大的是美国，占全世界新闻纸消耗量的50％（但较1956年减少3％），其次是英国，占8％（较1956年增6％）。各国日报的销售量最多的是英国，每千人有五百七十三份，其次是瑞典，每千人有四百六十二份。随后是：卢森堡、芬兰、日本、新西兰、澳大利亚、丹麦、挪威、美国（每千人三百三十七份）。
