Title: US Attempt to Assemble a Central American "International Police Force" Thwarted; Meeting of Interior Ministers from Five Central American Countries Opposes US Plan
Author:
Date: 1959-06-23
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, June 22nd, Guatemala City: The United States' plot to assemble a "international police force" in Central America has suffered another setback.

The United States Senate recently passed a resolution to allocate $29.5 million to establish a so-called "police force" for American countries. At a meeting of the interior ministers from five Central American countries—Guatemala, Honduras, El Salvador, Nicaragua, and Costa Rica—held on June 4th and 5th in Guatemala, the United States instructed Guatemalan Interior Minister Salazar to put forward this proposal.

Although the meeting passed some "anti-communist" resolutions, according to an Associated Press report on June 21st, the proposal to establish an "international police force" was opposed by the participating countries. Even Guatemala, which initially put forward the proposal, ultimately voted against it.

### 美国拼凑中美“国际警察部队”受挫  中美五个国家内政部长会议反对美国计划

1959-06-23
第5版()
专栏：

    美国拼凑中美“国际警察部队”受挫
    中美五个国家内政部长会议反对美国计划
    新华社22日讯　危地马拉城消息：美国要求中美洲国家建立一支“国际警察部队”的阴谋又一次遭到失败。
    美国参议院不久前曾通过一项决议，决定拨款二千九百五十万美元来建立一支所谓美洲国家的“警察部队”。在6月4、5两日举行的危地马拉、洪都拉斯、萨尔瓦多、尼加拉瓜和哥斯达黎加中美五个国家的内政部长会议上，美国指使危地马拉内政部长萨拉萨尔提出了这一建议。
    这个会议虽然通过了一些“反共”决议，但是，据美联社21日透露，关于成立一支“国际警察部队”的建议却遭到与会国家的反对，甚至到最后，连提出这一建议的危地马拉也投了反对票。
