International Briefs

    French Government Refuses Visas to Soviet, Chinese, and Other Countries' Trade Union Representatives
    Paris Dispatch: The French government has refused to grant entry visas to trade union representatives from the Soviet Union, China, Vietnam, Albania, the German Democratic Republic, Bulgaria, Hungary, Poland, Romania, and Czechoslovakia. They were invited by the French General Confederation of Labor to attend its national congress scheduled to be held in Paris from June 14 to 19.

    Kassem Congratulates the People on "Eid al-Adha"
    Baghdad Dispatch: According to a Baghdad radio broadcast on the evening of the 13th, Prime Minister Kassem extended congratulations to the Iraqi people, the armed forces, and the police on the occasion of the upcoming "Eid al-Adha" (June 16), a major Muslim holiday, expressing his hope for the prosperity and peace of all the people and the republic.

    Algerian National Liberation Army Launches Attacks
    Cairo Dispatch: A war communique issued by the Provisional Government of the Republic of Algeria on the 12th stated that the Algerian National Liberation Army engaged in eight battles with the French army on June 8 and 9, attacked fifteen French military outposts, and burned down some farms belonging to French colonists.
    It was announced that 183 French soldiers were killed and 134 wounded in these two days, while the Algerian National Liberation Army suffered nine deaths and seven injuries.

    Abbas Concludes Visit to Yugoslavia
    Belgrade Dispatch: Abbas, Prime Minister of the Algerian Provisional Government, left here for Cairo on the 12th after a seven-day visit to Yugoslavia at the invitation of the Yugoslav government.
    During his visit, Abbas held talks with Tito and other Yugoslav representatives, and a joint communique was issued by both sides.

    Italian Seamen Strike
    Rome Dispatch: The Italian seamen's strike, which began on the 9th, has entered its fifth day. Approximately seventy ships are now forced to remain idle in Italian and foreign ports.
    The seamen are demanding a 30% wage increase, and their strike has completely halted maritime traffic between Italy and Sicily and Sardinia.
    A spokesman for the Italian Shipowners' Association admitted that the strike is costing shipowners between 70 and 100 million lire per day and is greatly damaging Italy's tourism industry.

### 国际简讯

1959-06-15
第5版()
专栏：国际简讯

    国际简讯
    法政府拒发苏、中等国
    工会代表入境签证
    巴黎讯　法国政府拒绝给予苏联、中国、越南、阿尔巴尼亚、德意志民主共和国、保加利亚、匈牙利、波兰、罗马尼亚、捷克斯洛伐克的工会代表入境签证，他们应法国总工会邀请，参加定于6月14到19日在巴黎举行的法国总工会全国代表大会。
    卡塞姆向人民祝贺“古尔邦”节
    巴格达讯　据巴格达电台13日晚广播，在穆斯林的一个主要节日“古尔邦”节（6月16日）即将到来的时候，卡塞姆总理向伊拉克人民、部队、警察致贺词，希望全体人民和共和国繁荣与和平。
    阿尔及利亚民族解放军出击
    开罗讯　阿尔及利亚共和国临时政府12日发表的战报说，阿尔及利亚民族解放军在6月8日和9日同法国军队进行了八次战斗，攻击了十五个法军岗哨，烧毁了法国殖民者的一些农庄。
    据宣布，在这两天中，法军有一百八十三人被击毙，一百三十四人受伤，而在阿尔及利亚民族解放军方面有九人牺牲，七人受伤。
    阿巴斯结束在南斯拉夫访问
    贝尔格莱德讯　阿尔及利亚临时政府总理阿巴斯应邀在南斯拉夫进行了为时七天的访问以后，在12日离开这里前往开罗。
    在访问期间，阿巴斯先后同铁托以及南斯拉夫的其他代表进行了会谈，并且发表了双方的会谈公报。
    意大利海员罢工
    罗马讯　从9日开始的意大利海员罢工已经进入第五天。在意大利本国和外国的港口里，现在约有七十艘船只被迫停驶。
    海员们要求增加工资30％，他们的罢工已经使意大利同西西里岛和撒丁岛的海上交通完全停顿。
    意大利船主协会的发言人承认，这次罢工使船主们每天损失七千万到一亿里拉，并且大大损害了意大利的游览事业。
