Title: Monk Commits Suicide in Front of Japanese Prime Minister's Residence to Oppose Revision of "Security Treaty"
Author:
Date: 1959-06-05
Page: 4

    Opposing the Revision of the "Security Treaty"
    Monk Commits Suicide in Front of Japanese Prime Minister's Residence
    Xinhua News Agency, Tokyo, June 3rd: A monk from Hiroshima City, Japan, with the Dharma name释蕴彻 (Shi Yunche), committed suicide with a knife in front of the Tokyo residence of Nobusuke Kishi today and died.
    Before committing suicide, the monk handed a public letter to the guards in front of Kishi Nobusuke's residence. In the letter, he wrote that he wanted to speak for the victims of the war. He expressed his opposition to the revision of the U.S.-Japan "Security Treaty" and the rearmament of Japan.

### 反对修改“安全条约”  日首相府前和尚自杀

1959-06-05
第4版()
专栏：

    反对修改“安全条约”
    日首相府前和尚自杀
    新华社3日讯  东京消息：日本广岛市的一个法号叫做释蕴彻的和尚今天在东京岸信介的官邸前用刀自杀身死。
    这个和尚在自杀前递交给岸信介官邸门前的警卫一封公开信。他在信中写道，他要代替战争中的牺牲者讲话。他表示反对修改日美“安全条约”和重新武装日本。
