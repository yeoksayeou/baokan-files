Title: Ugandan Women Demonstrate, Demanding Colonial Authorities Release National Movement Leaders
Author:
Date: 1959-06-24
Page: 5

    Demanding Colonial Authorities Release National Movement Leaders
    Ugandan Women Demonstrate

    Xinhua News Agency, June 23rd, Kampala Dispatch: One hundred African women in Uganda held a demonstration on June 22nd, demanding that the British colonial authorities release six leaders of the "Uganda National Movement."
    Dressed in black, they rode in two trucks to Entebbe, the capital of Uganda, intending to petition the British Governor Crawford to release the detainees. However, on the way, they were intercepted by colonial police armed with batons and shields, who prevented them from entering Entebbe.
    On May 23rd, the British colonial authorities declared the "Uganda National Movement" "illegal" on the grounds of "engaging in terrorist activities" and then arrested six leaders of the organization.

### 要求殖民当局释放民族运动领袖  乌干达妇女举行示威

1959-06-24
第5版()
专栏：

    要求殖民当局释放民族运动领袖
    乌干达妇女举行示威
    新华社23日讯　坎帕拉消息：乌干达的一百名非洲妇女22日举行示威，要求英国殖民当局释放“乌干达民族运动”的六名领袖。
    她们身穿黑衣，乘坐两辆卡车前往乌干达首府恩德培准备向英国总督克劳福德提出要求释放这些被捕者。但是她们在途中，就受到手持棍棒和盾牌的殖民警察的阻拦，不让进入恩德培。
    英国殖民当局在5月23日以“进行恐怖活动”的罪名，宣布“乌干达民族运动”为“非法”，并且接着逮捕了这个组织的六名领袖。
