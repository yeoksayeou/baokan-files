International Briefs

    Pakistan Decides to Move Capital
    Karachi Dispatch: The Pakistan President's Secretariat issued a press release on the evening of the 12th, stating that Pakistan has decided to move its capital from Karachi to an area near Rawalpindi in West Pakistan. According to the press release, the government has also appointed a committee to consider establishing a co-capital in East Pakistan.

    Nehru Visits Nepal
    Kathmandu Dispatch: Indian Prime Minister Nehru has been conducting visits in Nepal since arriving in the capital, Kathmandu, on the 11th. On the 11th, he attended a royal banquet hosted by King Mahendra of Nepal in his honor, met with Nepalese Prime Minister Koirala, and delivered speeches at several public gatherings.

    Subandrio Visits Belgium
    Brussels Dispatch: Indonesian Foreign Minister Subandrio said at a press conference on the 10th that the purpose of his visit to Belgium was to study the possibility of conducting trade and economic cooperation.

    Burma's Internal Peace Committee Dissolved
    Rangoon Dispatch: According to news from local newspapers on the 13th, the Burmese Internal Peace Committee, headed by Tekin Kodaw Hmaing, announced on the 12th that it had decided to dissolve itself on June 9th.
    In a letter to the Rangoon Abbot Association, the committee said that it had completed its task of informing the government and underground forces about their respective attitudes towards achieving internal peace. Therefore, the committee feels that there is nothing else to do now.

    Yugoslavia Receives Another Loan from the United States
    Washington Dispatch: The United States and Yugoslavia signed an agreement in Washington on the 12th, under which Yugoslavia can obtain a loan of five million U.S. dollars from the U.S. "Development Loan Fund."

    Turkey's Arms Expansion and War Preparations Again Cause Price Increases
    Istanbul Dispatch: The arms race and huge military expenditures have once again caused price increases in Turkey.
    In recent days, the price of coal and postal and telecommunications fees have increased by 50% and more than 50%, respectively. Due to the increase in wheat prices, bread prices have also risen.

### 国际简讯

1959-06-14
第4版()
专栏：国际简讯

    国际简讯
    巴基斯坦决定迁都
    卡拉奇讯　巴基斯坦总统秘书处12日晚发表新闻公报说，巴基斯坦已经决定把它的首都从卡拉奇迁往西巴基斯坦的拉瓦尔品第附近的地区。据新闻公报说，政府还任命了一个委员会来考虑在东巴基斯坦建立一个陪都的问题。
    尼赫鲁在尼泊尔访问
    加德满都讯　印度总理尼赫鲁在11日到达尼泊尔首都加德满都以后，连日进行访问活动。他在11日出席了尼泊尔国王马亨德拉为他而设的皇家宴会，同尼泊尔首相柯伊拉腊会见，并且在几次公众集会上发表了讲话。
    苏班德里约访问比利时
    布鲁塞尔讯　印度尼西亚外交部长苏班德里约10日在记者招待会上说，他这次访问比利时的目的是为了研究进行贸易和经济合作的可能性。
    缅甸国内和平委员会解散
    仰光讯　据此间报纸13日消息，以德钦哥都迈为首的缅甸国内和平委员会在12日宣布，它已经在6月9日决定解散。
    委员会在一封给仰光方丈协会的信中说，它已经完成了这样的任务，即转告政府和地下力量关于各自对实现国内和平的态度。因此，委员会感到现在没有其他的事情可做了。
    南斯拉夫又从美国得到贷款
    华盛顿讯　美国和南斯拉夫12日在华盛顿签订一项协定，南斯拉夫根据这个协定可以从美国的“开发贷款基金”中得到五百万美元贷款。
    土扩军备战再次引起物价上涨
    伊斯坦布尔讯　军备竞赛和庞大的军事开支又一次引起土耳其物价的上涨。
    近几天以来，煤的价格和邮电费上涨了50％和50％以上。由于小麦价格提高，面包价格也跟着上涨了。
