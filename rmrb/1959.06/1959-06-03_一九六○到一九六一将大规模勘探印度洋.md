    Large-Scale Exploration of the Indian Ocean to Take Place from 1960 to 1961
    Anderson, head of the Ocean Science Division of the National Physical Research Laboratory of the Union of South Africa, recently announced that a large-scale international exploration of the Indian Ocean will be conducted in 1960 and 1961. It is estimated that fifteen countries will participate, using approximately twenty ships from the Soviet Union, the United States, the United Kingdom, France, Japan, and other countries. The purpose of the exploration is to obtain information on water currents, seabed temperatures, and geological structures, and it is hoped that it will help solve the problem of dumping atomic waste into the deep sea. Cape Town or Durban will serve as the exploration base. The South African National Committee for the International Geophysical Year is preparing for this exploration.

### 一九六○到一九六一将大规模勘探印度洋

1959-06-03
第7版()
专栏：学术动态

    一九六○到一九六一
    将大规模勘探印度洋
    南非联邦国家物理研究实验室海洋科学处处长安德生不久前宣布，在1960年和1961年将对印度洋进行大规模的国际勘探，估计有十五个国家参加，并将使用苏、美、英、法、日及其他国家的约二十艘船只。勘探的目的是得到关于水流、海底温度和地质结构等方面的情报，并希能对解决将原子废料倾入深海的问题有所帮助。开普顿或德班将作为勘探基地。南非联邦国际地球物理年委员会正在为这次勘探进行准备工作。
