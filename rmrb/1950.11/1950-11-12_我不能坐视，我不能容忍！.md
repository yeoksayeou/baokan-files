Title: I Cannot Sit Idly By, I Cannot Tolerate!
Author: Chao Liu
Date: 1950-11-12
Page: 7

　　I Cannot Sit Idly By, I Cannot Tolerate!
　　(Honorary Soldier Chao Liu)
　　Editor's Note: This is a poem written by a disabled soldier, and the deeds described in it are deeply touching. This is one of countless facts about the brotherly relationship between the Chinese and Korean people who share weal and woe. We hope that literary and art workers will pay more attention to this type of subject matter and use various artistic forms to give it a vivid and glorious expression.

　　I am a soldier in the People's Liberation Army,
　　　　The bullets of the Japanese devils, the shells of the Americans,
　　Wounded my waist, broke my right leg,
　　　　I have an irreconcilable hatred for imperialism!
　　I will never forget the blood debt it owes me,
　　　　I will also never forget how I lived to this day.
　　In the spring of 1945,
　　　　Fighting the Japanese devils in Yangzhuang,
　　I was seriously wounded and could not leave the line of fire,
　　A Korean comrade in the squad, Liu Qing,
　　　　For the liberation of our motherland, fought side by side for six years,
　　He braved fierce artillery fire, rain-like bullets,
　　　　Crawled to my side,
　　Used leggings to tie my waist, and dragged me off the line of fire.
　　　　When he went to save another comrade,
　　Six or seven enemies rushed to him with bayonets,
　　He fought desperately, killing three enemies,
　　　　He himself fell in a pool of blood, sacrificing himself under the enemy's bayonets—
　　　　To save me, save comrades, save our motherland,
　　He shed his last drop of blood.
　　　　Three years of the Liberation War, eight years of the War of Resistance,
　　There were thousands upon thousands of Liu Qings,
　　　　I will always remember them, always keep them in my heart.

　　Now American imperialism is invading Liu Qing's country,
　　　　Seizing Seoul and Pyongyang,
　　And burning the flames of war to the border of our country.
　　American imperialism is like a mad beast,
　　American imperialism is like a plague germ,
　　　　Killing, arson, and rape everywhere.
　　Liu Qing sacrificed his life for the liberation of our motherland,
　　　　Liu Qing shed his blood to save me,
　　Liu Qing's country is my country,
　　　　Liu Qing's parents, brothers and sisters are my relatives.
　　I cannot sit idly by, I cannot tolerate,
　　　　I cannot let the beasts ravage them!
　　I cannot let the germs contaminate their bodies;
　　　　Like Comrade Liu Qing treated us,
　　I want to go to help the Korean people.
　　　　Extinguish the flames of aggression of American imperialism,
　　　　Defend my own country, defend world peace!

### 我不能坐视，我不能容忍！
潮流
1950-11-12
第7版()
专栏：

　　我不能坐视，我不能容忍！
    （荣誉军人　潮流）
    编者按：这是一位荣军写的诗，其中所叙述的事迹感人甚深。这是中朝两国人民休戚相共的兄弟般的关系的无数事实之一。我们希望文艺工作者们更多注意这类题材，用种种文艺形式给以生动的光辉的表现。
    我是人民解放军里的一个士兵，
      日本鬼子的子弹，美国的炮弹，
    打伤了我的腰，打断了我的右腿，
      我与帝国主义有不共戴天的仇恨！
    我永远忘不了它欠我的血债，
      我也永远忘不了我怎么活到今天。
    一九四五年的春天，
      跟日本鬼子在杨庄作战，
    我负了重伤，不能走下火线，
    班里的朝鲜同志刘青，
      为了我们祖国解放，并肩作战了六年，
    他冒着猛烈的炮火，雨一般的子弹，
      爬到我的身边，
    绑腿捆住腰，把我拖下了火线。
      当他去救另一个同志时，
    六七个敌人端着刺刀冲到他的面前，
    他拚死了三个敌人，
      自己也倒在血泊中，牺牲在敌人刺刀下面——
      为了救我，救同志，救我们的祖国，
    他流尽了他最后的热血。
      三年解放战争，八年抗战，
    有千千万万的刘青，
      我永远记着他们，永远铭记在心。
　
    如今美帝国主义侵略刘青的祖国，
      抢占了汉城、平壤，
    把战火燃烧到我国的边境。
      美帝国主义像一只疯狂的野兽，
    美帝国主义像瘟疫的病菌，
      到处杀人、放火、奸淫。
    刘青为了我们祖国的解放牺牲了生命，
      刘青为了救我把热血流尽，
    刘青的祖国就是我的祖国，
      刘青的父母兄弟姊妹就是我的亲人。
    我不能坐视，我不能容忍，
      我不能让野兽来摧残他们！
    我不能让病菌沾他们的身；
      像刘青同志对待我们一样，
    我要去援助朝鲜人民。
      扑灭美帝国主义的侵略火焰，
      保卫我自己的祖国，保卫世界和平！
