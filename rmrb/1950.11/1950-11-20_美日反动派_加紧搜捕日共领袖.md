Title: U.S.-Japanese Reactionaries Intensify Search for Japanese Communist Party Leaders
Author:
Date: 1950-11-20
Page: 4
Xinhua News Agency, 19th: TASS reported on the 17th, citing Japanese newspapers, that the Special Screening Bureau of the Yoshida government's Ministry of Justice had received 100 million yen from MacArthur's staff as funds to search for Japanese Communist Party leaders who had gone underground. Currently, one-third of Tokyo's police force is conducting this search. The Mainichi Shimbun reported that the National Police Headquarters has formulated special instructions for the search of Japanese Communist Party leaders. These instructions are accompanied by photographs, fingerprints, signatures, and physical characteristics of eight Japanese Communist Party leaders. These instructions have been distributed to police agencies throughout the country and have also been notified to railway police agencies and the Maritime Safety Agency.

### 美日反动派  加紧搜捕日共领袖

1950-11-20
第4版()
专栏：

　　美日反动派
    加紧搜捕日共领袖
    【新华社十九日讯】塔斯社十七日电引日本报纸消息：吉田政府法务府特别审查局曾从麦克阿瑟的参谋人员方面领得一亿日元，作为搜捕进入地下的日共领袖的经费。目前有三分之一的东京警察正在进行这种搜捕。每日新闻报道：国家警察本部已制订搜捕日共领袖的特别指令，该指令附有日共八个领袖的相片、指印、签字和外貌特征。这一指令已分发给全国各地的警察机关，同时也已通告铁路警察机关和海上保安厅。
