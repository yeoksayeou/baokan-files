Title: Second World Peace Congress Delegates Unjustly Denied Entry to Britain; British Government Refuses Visas to Most Delegates and Sends Agents to Conduct Activities in Sheffield
Author:
Date: 1950-11-11
Page: 1

　　Xinhua News Agency, 10th. Reuter reports: The Organizing Committee of the World Peace Congress announced on the 9th that, to date, only 140 of the 900 foreign delegates attending the Second World Peace Congress have received entry visas from British authorities. According to recent news from London, all delegates from Mexico and Argentina, 15 delegates from Czechoslovakia, 10 from Romania, 3 from Albania, 10 from Finland, 10 from Austria, and 2 from Brazil have been unreasonably denied visas. Ms. Hodinova-Spurna, Vice-Chairman of the Czechoslovak National Assembly, Professor Nadrezhkov of Sofia University, and the famous Chilean poet Neruda have also been denied entry visas.

　　A Reuter correspondent in Havana, the capital of Cuba, reported that Cuban police, at the instigation of the American ambassador, have arrested and imprisoned five Mexican delegates, one Brazilian delegate, and one Cuban delegate who were passing through the city on their way to the meeting in Britain. Among them are: Toledano (Mexico), General Secretary of the Latin American Confederation of Workers, and Posali (Brazil), Secretary of the World Peace Prize Judging Committee.

　　Xinhua News Agency, 10th. A Reuter dispatch from London on the 9th quoted the Worker's Daily as saying that the British government has sent Sir Cyril, Director of Military Intelligence, to Sheffield to organize espionage activities and persecute delegates attending the Second World Peace Congress. The agents under Sir Cyril's command include American secret police thugs and secret police from various Western European countries.

　　Xinhua News Agency, 10th. TASS reports: British authorities have unreasonably refused entry visas to some delegates from Bulgaria and Finland who are attending the Second World Peace Congress. Dragoycheva, Chairman of the Bulgarian Committee for the Defense of Peace, Pavlov, President of the Academy of Sciences, and the famous writer Karaslavov, as well as Mairti, head of the Finnish delegation and Chairman of the Standing Committee of the Finnish Peace Congress, Vice-Chairman Professor Iversen, Secretary-General Tominen, and Secretary Genny, among others, have been denied entry.

　　Members of the Finnish delegation who have obtained British entry visas departed for Britain on the 9th.

### 二届和大代表赴英遭无理拒绝 英政府拒发大部分签证  并派特务往设菲尔德进行活动

1950-11-11
第1版()
专栏：

　　二届和大代表赴英遭无理拒绝
    英政府拒发大部分签证         
    并派特务往设菲尔德进行活动
    【新华社十日讯】综合电通社讯：世界保卫和平大会组织委员会九日宣布；出席第二届世界和大的九百名外国代表，迄今只有一百四十人获得英国当局的入境签证。据伦敦最近消息，遭无理拒绝的已有墨西哥及阿根廷的全体代表，捷克斯洛伐克代表十五人，罗马尼亚十人，阿尔巴尼亚三人，芬兰十人，奥地利十人，巴西两人。捷克斯洛伐克国民议会副主席荷第诺瓦——斯普尔纳、索非亚大学教授那德热科夫、智利名诗人尼鲁达等人，亦被拒发入境签证。
    电通社驻古巴京城哈瓦那记者报道，古巴警察在美国大使的授意下，已逮捕并监禁了道经该城赴英开会的墨西哥代表五人，巴西及古巴代表各一人，其中有：拉丁美洲工联总书记托列达诺（墨西哥），世界和平奖金评议委员会书记波萨里（巴西）。
    【新华社十日讯】电通社伦敦九日电引工人日报消息：英国政府已派遣军事情报局局长西里托前往设菲尔德组织特务活动，迫害出席第二届世界保卫和平大会的代表。西里托所指挥的特务人员，包括美国的秘密警察打手和西欧各国的秘密警察在内。
    【新华社十日讯】综合塔斯社消息：英国当局无理拒发入境签证给出席第二届世界保卫和平大会的保加利亚和芬兰的一部分代表。保加利亚保卫和平委员会主席德拉哥伊切娃，科学院院长巴夫洛夫和名作家卡拉斯拉渥夫，芬兰代表团团长、芬兰保卫和平大会常设委员会主席麦尔提，副主席伊维尔森教授，秘书长托米宁，秘书根尼等多人，均被拒绝入境。
    获得英国入境签证的芬兰代表团团员已于九日启程赴英。
