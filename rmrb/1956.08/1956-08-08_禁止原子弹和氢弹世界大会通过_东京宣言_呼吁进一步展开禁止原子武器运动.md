Title: World Conference Against Atomic and Hydrogen Bombs Passes "Tokyo Declaration" Calling for Further Development of the Movement to Ban Atomic Weapons
Author:
Date: 1956-08-08
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, Tokyo, 7th – The Second World Conference Against Atomic and Hydrogen Bombs opened in Tokyo on the evening of the 6th. The conference unanimously passed the "Tokyo Declaration" with the support of more than 30,000 representatives from various parts of Japan and foreign countries, appealing to the people of the world to further develop the movement to ban the manufacture, testing, and use of atomic and hydrogen bombs, and to achieve disarmament at the same time.

The declaration solemnly stated: The movement to ban atomic and hydrogen bombs is to protect human life and happiness from the terror of weapons of mass destruction. Now, nearly 700 million people around the world have signed in favor of banning atomic and hydrogen bombs, and this force has gradually pushed international politics towards the direction of banning atomic and hydrogen bombs. A bright road for the future has been opened.

The opening meeting of the World Conference was held at the International Stadium in Tokyo. Paintings of white doves of peace and national flags of various countries were hung in the venue. Representatives from various parts of Japan and various mass organizations, as well as tens of thousands of people, filled the hall. On the rostrum sat peace representatives from various international organizations and more than a dozen countries.

The conference was declared open amidst the singing of "No Atomic Bombs Allowed" by all participants and the band playing "Let's Connect the Whole World into a Garland". Yasui Iku, Secretary-General of the Japan Council Against Atomic and Hydrogen Bombs, reported to the conference on the development of the movement to ban atomic and hydrogen bombs. He said that as of the 6th, 35,556,308 people in Japan had signed in favor of banning atomic and hydrogen bombs. Yasui said that our movement to ban atomic and hydrogen bombs has now entered a new stage and can rely on public opinion and strength that supports the ban on atomic and hydrogen bombs to lead the world towards the prohibition of nuclear weapons, hydrogen weapons, and disarmament.

Representatives of atomic bomb victims from Hiroshima and Nagasaki accused the conference of the disasters and sufferings brought to them by American atomic bombs. They cried out in grief: The tragedy of Hiroshima and Nagasaki must never be repeated! Every word they spoke was interrupted by thunderous applause from the crowd.

The conference received many congratulatory telegrams and letters from foreign countries and various Japanese organizations. Japanese Prime Minister Ichiro Hatoyama also wrote a letter congratulating the conference on its success. He expressed his hope that atomic energy could be applied to the cause of promoting world peace and human happiness.

Many foreign representatives addressed the conference. Xu Guangping, head of the Chinese delegation, said in her speech: We have come to participate in the Second World Conference Against Atomic and Hydrogen Bombs with the peaceful will of the entire Chinese people. Please allow us to first express here the heartfelt support of the entire Chinese people for this conference and extend our sincere condolences to the Japanese people who have suffered from atomic and hydrogen bomb disasters. We are prepared to present 50,000 yuan in Renminbi as a token of sympathy from six Chinese people's organizations at the Nagasaki conference as a small amount of assistance from the Chinese people to the Japanese atomic bomb victims.

Xu Guangping called on peace-loving people to join hands and not allow the Hiroshima and Nagasaki events to be repeated!

Soviet representative Sofronov addressed the meeting, reiterating the Soviet Union's proposal for a universal ban on atomic and hydrogen bombs.

American representative Wilson spoke about the disasters that atomic weapons would bring to mankind. He said that many Americans are fighting to ban further testing of nuclear weapons.

British famous peace fighter Mrs. Felton said: Dropping atomic bombs on Hiroshima and Nagasaki was a terrible crime against the Japanese people. She said: We must work hard to prevent such a tragedy from happening again.

Others who addressed the conference included: Mrs. Pulmón, representative of the World Peace Council, Mr. Ribelini, representative of the International Organization of Journalists, Mrs. Hoyer, representative of the Women's International League for Peace and Freedom, Professor Higa, representative of the World Federation of Scientific Workers, Mrs. Vaillant-Couturier, representative of the Women's International Democratic Federation, Kesadi, representative of the World Federation of Trade Unions, and representatives from Poland, Czechoslovakia, Romania, and other countries. The representatives unanimously called for a ban on atomic and hydrogen weapons in their speeches.

After the conference in the evening, the famous Matsuyama Tree Seedling Ballet Company and Guyue Dance Company performed wonderful programs.

This morning, representatives from various countries have flown from Tokyo to Nagasaki, and the World Conference will continue from the 9th in that second Japanese city that was bombed by American atomic bombs.

### 禁止原子弹和氢弹世界大会通过“东京宣言”  呼吁进一步展开禁止原子武器运动

1956-08-08
第6版()
专栏：

    禁止原子弹和氢弹世界大会通过“东京宣言”
    呼吁进一步展开禁止原子武器运动
    新华社7日讯　东京消息：第二届禁止原子弹和氢弹世界大会六日晚上在东京开幕。大会在日本各地代表和外国代表三万多人的一致赞成下通过了“东京宣言”，向世界人民呼吁进一步展开运动，禁止制造、试验、使用原子弹和氢弹，同时实现裁减军备。
    宣言庄严地说：禁止原子弹和氢弹运动是要保护人类的生命和幸福，免受大规模毁灭性武器的恐怖。现在全世界已经有近七万万人签名赞成禁止原子弹和氢弹，这种力量已经逐渐把国际政治推动走向禁止原子弹和氢弹的方向。未来的光明的道路已经给打开了。
    世界大会开幕会议是在东京的国际体育场举行的。会场内悬挂着白色和平鸽的画和各国国旗。来自日本各地和各群众团体的代表以及成万名的群众挤满了大厅。在主席台上，坐着各国际组织和十多个国家的和平代表。
    大会在全体与会者合唱“不许使用原子弹”和乐队吹奏“把全世界联结成一个花环”的歌声中宣布开幕。日本禁止原子弹和氢弹协议会事务总长安井郁向大会报告了禁止原子弹和氢弹运动发展的情况。他说，截至六日，日本已经有三千五百五十五万六千三百零八人签名赞成禁止原子弹和氢弹。安井说，现在我们的禁止原子弹和氢弹运动进入新的阶段，能够依靠赞成禁止原子弹和氢弹的舆论和力量，使全世界走向禁止核子武器、氢武器和裁减军备的道路。
    广岛和长崎的原子弹受害者代表在大会上控诉了美国原子弹带给他们的灾难和痛苦。他们悲痛地高呼：绝不许广岛和长崎的悲剧重演！他们的每一句话，都被群众雷鸣似的掌声打断。
    大会接到了很多从外国和日本各种团体寄来的贺电和贺信。日本首相鸠山一郎也写信祝贺大会成功。他表示希望把原子能应用到增进世界和平与人类幸福的事业上。
    许多外国代表在大会上致词。中国代表团团长许广平在大会致词说：我们带着全中国人民的和平意志，来参加第二届禁止原子弹和氢弹世界大会，请让我们先在这里表示全中国人民对这次大会的衷心支持，并对遭受原子弹、氢弹灾害的日本人民致以恳切慰问。我们准备在长崎大会献上中国六个人民团体带来的慰问金人民币五万元，作为中国人民对日本原子弹受害者的一点援助。
    许广平呼吁爱好和平的人民携起手来，不许广岛和长崎事件重演！
    苏联代表索弗罗诺夫致词，申述了苏联的普遍禁止原子弹和氢弹的主张。
    美国代表威尔逊谈到原子武器将给人类带来的灾难。他说，很多美国人都在为禁止再次试验核子武器而斗争。
    英国著名和平战士费尔顿夫人说：向广岛和长崎投原子弹是对日本人民所犯的可怕的罪恶。她说：我们要努力不使这种悲剧重演。
    在大会致词的还有：世界和平理事会代表普尔梦夫人、国际新闻工作者协会代表理倍利尼、国际妇女争取和平与自由联盟代表霍耶尔夫人、世界科学工作者协会代表比嘉教授、国际民主妇女联合会代表瓦扬—古久里夫人、世界工会联合会代表克沙迪和波兰、捷克斯洛伐克、罗马尼亚等国代表。代表们在发言中一致呼吁禁止原子武器和氢武器。
    大会在晚间结束后，日本著名的松山树子巴蕾舞团和古月舞蹈团演出了精彩的节目。
    今天上午，各国代表已经从东京乘飞机前往长崎，世界大会将从九日起继续在那个遭受美国原子弹轰炸的第二个日本城市举行。
