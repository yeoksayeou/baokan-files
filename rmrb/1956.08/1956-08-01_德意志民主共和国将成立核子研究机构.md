Title: German Democratic Republic to Establish Nuclear Research Institute
Author:
Date: 1956-08-01
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, Berlin, July 31st. According to a Rostock dispatch from the German News Agency: A nuclear research institute will be established in Rostock, and it will train fifteen to twenty nuclear physicists annually to carry out research in this field.

This was announced to the press on July 30th by Professor Müller, the president of the University of Rostock, along with two other physicists. In making this statement, they emphasized that research in physics, especially nuclear physics, requires international cooperation among scientists from various countries. In this regard, they mentioned that the cultural and scientific cooperation agreement signed between the German Democratic Republic and the Soviet Union in April of this year was a valuable step.

### 德意志民主共和国将成立核子研究机构

1956-08-01
第5版()
专栏：

    德意志民主共和国将成立核子研究机构
    新华社柏林31日电　据德意志通讯社罗斯托克消息：罗斯托克将成立一个核子研究机构，并且将每年训练十五名到二十名核子物理学家来进行这方面的研究工作。
    这是罗斯托克大学校长缪勒教授同另外两位物理学家在七月三十日告诉新闻界的。他们在发表这番谈话的时候强调指出，物理学，特别是核子物理学的研究工作需要各国科学家的国际合作。在这方面，他们提到，民主德国在今年四月间同苏联签订的文化科学合作协定是一个有价值的步骤。
