Title: Eisenhower Says Suez Canal Issue Should Be Handled "With Caution"
Author:
Date: 1956-08-03
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, August 2nd Dispatch from Washington: U.S. President Eisenhower, when discussing the Suez Canal issue at a press conference yesterday, said that the U.S. government is clearly facing a very serious problem. He said that the U.S. aim is to "ensure that all countries can continue to effectively use this canal," which is the current "big issue." He believes that this canal is very important not only to Europe but also to the U.S. economy.

Eisenhower did not specify what actions the U.S. intends to take on the Suez Canal issue. He only said that the issue needs to be "handled with caution, and needs to maintain justice and fairness."

### 艾森豪威尔说要“慎重处理”苏彝士运河问题

1956-08-03
第6版()
专栏：

    艾森豪威尔说要“慎重处理”苏彝士运河问题
    据新华社2日讯　华盛顿消息：美国总统艾森豪威尔昨天在记者招待会上谈到苏彝士运河问题的时候说，美国政府显然面临着一个非常严重的问题。他说，美国目的是要“保证各国可以继续有效地使用这条运河”，这是现在的“大问题”。他认为这条运河不仅对欧洲而且对美国的经济也是非常重要的。
    艾森豪威尔没有说明美国在苏彝士运河问题上打算采取什么行动。他只是说，这个问题需要“慎重处理，需要保持正义和公平”。
