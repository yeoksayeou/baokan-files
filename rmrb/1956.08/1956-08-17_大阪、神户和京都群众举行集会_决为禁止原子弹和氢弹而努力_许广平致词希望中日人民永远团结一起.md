Title: Masses in Osaka, Kobe, and Kyoto Hold Rallies Determined to Work to Ban Atomic and Hydrogen Bombs, Xu Guangping Delivers Speech Hoping Chinese and Japanese People Will Forever Unite
Author: 
Date: 1956-08-17
Page: 5
Xinhua News Agency, 16th, Osaka dispatch: Approximately twenty thousand people from all walks of life in Osaka City, Japan, and representatives from mass organizations in Kobe and Kyoto held a conference in Osaka City last night to ban atomic and hydrogen bombs.

　　The conference unanimously adopted a resolution supporting the declaration and resolutions of the Second World Conference Against Atomic and Hydrogen Bombs. The declaration of the World Conference calls on people all over the world to work to ban atomic and hydrogen bombs. The resolutions of the World Conference include: requiring the United States, Britain, and the Soviet Union to conclude an agreement prohibiting the testing of atomic and hydrogen bombs; the International Court of Justice in The Hague to determine that the use of atomic weapons is illegal; peaceful use of atomic energy, etc.

　　Representatives from various countries attending the Second World Conference Against Atomic and Hydrogen Bombs also participated in this conference, and they delivered speeches at the conference.

　　Xu Guangping, head of the Chinese delegation, said in her speech: "The Chinese people have sent us to your country to tell the Japanese people this: China and Japan are eternally inseparable neighbors. For the sake of opposing war, and for the sake of peace in Asia and the world, the people of China and Japan should be forever united."

　　Representatives of atomic bomb victims from Hiroshima and Nagasaki denounced the disasters brought to them by the American atomic bombs at the meeting.

　　Representatives of Japanese war criminals recently released from China delivered speeches at the meeting, expressing their gratitude for the humanitarian spirit of the Chinese people, and expressing their desire to work for Sino-Japanese friendship and world peace.

　　The conference ended with everyone singing "No Atomic Bombs" and "Don't Forget Hiroshima."

### 大阪、神户和京都群众举行集会  决为禁止原子弹和氢弹而努力  许广平致词希望中日人民永远团结一起

1956-08-17
第5版()
专栏：

    大阪、神户和京都群众举行集会
    决为禁止原子弹和氢弹而努力
    许广平致词希望中日人民永远团结一起
    新华社16日讯　大阪消息：日本大阪市各阶层人民和神户、京都的群众团体代表共约两万人昨天晚间在大阪市举行了禁止原子弹和氢弹大会。
    大会一致通过决议，支持第二届禁止原子弹和氢弹世界大会的宣言和决议。世界大会的宣言呼吁全世界人民为禁止原子弹和氢弹而努力。世界大会的决议包括：要求美、英、苏三国缔结禁止试验原子弹和氢弹的协定；由海牙国际法庭确定使用原子武器是非法的；和平利用原子能等。
    出席第二届禁止原子弹和氢弹世界大会的各国代表也参加了这个大会，他们并且在会上致词。
    中国代表团团长许广平致词说：“中国人民派遣我们到你们的国家来，就是要告诉日本人民这样一句话：中国同日本是永远不可分离的邻邦，为了反对战争，为了亚洲和世界和平，中日两国人民应该永远团结在一起。”
    广岛和长崎的原子弹受害者代表在会上控诉了美国原子弹带给他们的灾害。
    最近从中国释放回来的日本战争犯罪分子代表在会上致词，表示感谢中国人民的人道主义精神，并且表示要为中日友好和世界和平而努力。
    大会在全体合唱“不许使用原子弹”和“不要忘记广岛”的歌声中结束。
