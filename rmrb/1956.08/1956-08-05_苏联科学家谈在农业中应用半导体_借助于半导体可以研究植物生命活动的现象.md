Title: Soviet Scientists Discuss the Application of Semiconductors in Agriculture: Using Semiconductors to Study Plant Life Activities
Author:
Date: 1956-08-05
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, Moscow, August 4th: Soviet Academician Ioffe of the Academy of Sciences of the USSR recently wrote an article in the Soviet "Agricultural Newspaper" discussing semiconductors and their application prospects in agricultural production.

Academician Ioffe said that the electrical characteristic of semiconductors is their resistance to the passage of electric current. External temperature, radioactive radiation, moisture in the air, and pressure on the semiconductor all change the resistance of the semiconductor. Therefore, all the above external conditions can be reflected in the size of the semiconductor's resistance. And resistance measurement is one of the simplest and most accurate methods.

Resistance thermometers can solve problems that mercury thermometers cannot. For example, by placing a small piece of semiconductor, from one-tenth of a millimeter to a few millimeters, on the surface of a leaf, stem, or soil, their temperature can be measured. The temperature at any depth can also be measured in this way. The temperature of any part of the plant and the humidity of every part of the air around it can also be measured by semiconductors. This creates new methods for studying plant life activities under the influence of various temperature, light, and nutritional conditions.

Now, with the help of new instruments, it is possible to discover new phenomena in plant transpiration and photosynthesis.

The Academician said that semiconductors completely changed the methods of automatic devices and production process manipulation. Relying on semiconductors, complex radio circuits can be placed in the smallest volume, and the smallest volume can easily be placed in any device. With this kind of radio instrument, machines working several kilometers away can be manipulated by radio waves.

If semiconductor thermometers are installed in machines, the temperature of the machines can be checked and measured, enabling timely prevention of overheating.

This semiconductor instrument can also be used to maintain a certain temperature in granaries, poultry houses, and livestock sheds.

### 苏联科学家谈在农业中应用半导体  借助于半导体可以研究植物生命活动的现象

1956-08-05
第5版()
专栏：

    苏联科学家谈在农业中应用半导体
    借助于半导体可以研究植物生命活动的现象
    新华社莫斯科4日电　苏联科学院约非院士最近在苏联“农业报”上写了一篇文章，谈半导体和它在农业生产中应用的前景。
    约非院士说，半导体的电气特性就是它对电流通过的阻力。外界温度、放射性幅射、空气中的水分、对半导体的压力，这一切都会改变半导体的电阻。因此，上述的一切外界条件可以从半导体的电阻大小上反映出来。而电阻测定是最简单和精确的方法之一。
    电阻温度计可以解决水银温度计所不能解决的问题。例如，把一小块十分之一公厘到几公厘的半导体放在叶、茎、土壤的表面，就能够测定它们的温度。任何深度的温度也可以这样来测定。植物任何一部分的温度和它的周围空气的每一部分的湿度也可以靠半导体测定出来。这样就创造了研究植物在各种温度、光线、营养条件的影响下的生命活动的新方法。
    现在，借助新仪器已经能够发现植物蒸发作用和光和热作用的新现象。
    这位科学院院士说，半导体完全改变了自动装置和生产过程操纵的方法。依靠半导体，复杂的无线电线路可以放置在最小的容积中，而最小的容积很容易放置在任何装置里面。有了这种无线电仪器就可以用无线电波来操纵在几公里以外工作的机器。
    如果在机器中装上半导体测温器，就可以检查和测定机器的温度，使它能够及时防止过热。
    这种半导体仪器还可以用来保持粮仓、禽舍、畜舍的一定的温度。
