Title: German Scientists Discover Human Fossils From Over 120,000 Years Ago
Author:
Date: 1956-08-28
Page: 5
Xinhua News Agency, Berlin, August 27th. Professor Jacobshagen, an anthropologist at the University of Marburg in West Germany, recently stated that a skull of an ancient human was discovered not long ago in the Hessian village of Renda. It is the skull of a Neanderthal woman dating back more than 120,000 years. He said that this is a scientifically valuable discovery because it is more complete than the skull of a Paleolithic man discovered a hundred years ago (1856) in a limestone cave in the Neanderthal Valley in Germany.

### 德国科学家发现十二万多年以前的人类化石

1956-08-28
第5版()
专栏：

    德国科学家发现十二万多年以前的人类化石
    据新华社柏林27日电　西德马尔堡大学的人类学家亚科布斯哈根教授最近说，不久以前在伦达的黑西安村发现了一具古人类头盖骨，它是十二万多年以前的尼安得特人的妇人头盖骨。他说，这是一个在科学上极有价值的发现，因为它比一百年以前（1856）在德国尼安得特山谷的一个灰石洞中发现的旧石器时代的男人头盖骨还要完整。
