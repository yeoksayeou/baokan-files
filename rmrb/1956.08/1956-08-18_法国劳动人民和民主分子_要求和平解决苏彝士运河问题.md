Title: French Laboring People and Democrats Demand Peaceful Resolution of the Suez Canal Issue
Author:
Date: 1956-08-18
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, August 17th dispatch from TASS, Paris: The laboring people and democrats of France are resolutely struggling to protect Egypt's legitimate rights.

According to the "l'Humanité" newspaper, workers and staff of the Paris Central Railway Factory wrote to Prime Minister Mollet and Foreign Minister Pineau, requesting a peaceful resolution to the Suez Canal issue. Signatures on this letter included members of the Communist Party, the Socialist Party, and the Catholic Workers' Union and other trade union organizations. A group of workers working on the railways in the north also put forward similar demands.

The Montemayor city council of the Ardèche region (most of the councilors are Socialists) unanimously passed a resolution condemning the policy of military threats against Egypt.

### 法国劳动人民和民主分子  要求和平解决苏彝士运河问题

1956-08-18
第6版()
专栏：

    法国劳动人民和民主分子
    要求和平解决苏彝士运河问题
    新华社17日讯　据塔斯社巴黎十七日讯：法国的劳动人民和民主分子都坚决为保护埃及的合法权利而斗争。
    据“人道报”报道，巴黎中央铁路工厂的工人和职员写信给摩勒总理和外交部长比诺，要求用和平方法解决苏彝士运河问题。在这封信上签名的有共产党人、社会党人以及天主教工会和其他工会组织的会员们。在北部的铁路工作的一批工人也提出了类似的要求。
    阿隆省的蒙特米市市议会（大多数议员是社会党人）一致通过了一项决议，谴责对埃及进行军事威胁的政策。
