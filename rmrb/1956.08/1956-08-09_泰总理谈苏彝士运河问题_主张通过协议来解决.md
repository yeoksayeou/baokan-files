Title: Thai Prime Minister Discusses Suez Canal Issue, Advocates Resolution Through Agreement
Author:
Date: 1956-08-09
Page: 6
Xinhua News Agency, August 8th, Bangkok dispatch: Thai Prime Minister Pibul Songgram stated to the press on August 1st that the United Nations would be a powerful institution for resolving the Suez Canal issue, and that the Thai government is currently deeply concerned about the development of this matter.

    Pibul Songgram was unwilling to express a direct opinion on who was right and wrong in this incident. He believes that the Suez Canal issue can be resolved through agreement and will not lead to a world war. If war breaks out, the United Nations should stop it. He said that the United Nations Charter does not allow direct armed action; he believes that the current international situation has sufficient conditions for peace to eliminate the deterioration of international relations.

### 泰总理谈苏彝士运河问题  主张通过协议来解决

1956-08-09
第6版()
专栏：

    泰总理谈苏彝士运河问题
    主张通过协议来解决
    据新华社8日讯　曼谷消息：泰国总理銮披汶在八月一日向报界发表谈话说，联合国将是解决苏彝士运河问题的有力机构，泰国政府目前正深切关注这件事情的发展。
    銮披汶对这个事件中谁是谁非不愿正面表示意见。他认为，苏彝士运河问题是可以通过协议来解决的，不致引起世界战争。如果发生战争，联合国就应当加以制止。他说，联合国宪章不准采取直接武装行动；他相信，当前国际形势具有充分和平的条件，足以消除国际关系恶化的局面。
