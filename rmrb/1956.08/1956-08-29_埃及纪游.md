    An Egyptian Travelogue
    Feng Zhidan
    (XII) The Greco-Roman Museum and the Egyptian Aquarium
    In Alexandria, we visited the Greco-Roman Museum, built in 1891. Here, one can see the vestiges of Greek and Roman culture left in Egypt. Most notable are the beautiful portrait of the Egyptian Queen Cleopatra from around 30 BC and a stone-carved reclining statue of her. Mention of Queen Cleopatra invariably evokes Shakespeare's "Antony and Cleopatra." According to the story, on the eve of the Roman Empire's conquest of Egypt, Queen Cleopatra and Antony, one of the triumvirs of ancient Rome, fell deeply in love. Antony opposed the Roman Empire's conquest of Egypt, thereby becoming an enemy of Caesar. Caesar led a large army to attack Egypt, and Egypt was destroyed by the Romans. When the Romans invaded, Queen Cleopatra killed herself with a poisonous snake in her own palace. The reclining statue of Cleopatra depicts this tragedy. Although the statue is carved from stone, the gauze draped over her body is clearly discernible. Beside her lies a snake biting into her skin.
    Besides this, there is a large glass cabinet containing a collection of over seven thousand coins minted in Alexandria by Greek and Roman rulers...

    Leaving this museum, we entered a world of fish—the Egyptian Aquarium. The aquarium is located close to the seaside, and the strong sea breeze brought a chill. On the ground floor of the aquarium, there is a long row of glass tanks, each holding a different species of fish. A total of more than eighty kinds of colorful and strangely shaped fish freely roam in this "world." Dr. Samra, the director of the aquarium's research institute, accompanied us on the visit. According to him, Egypt's annual aquatic production reaches 50,000 tons. Egyptians are now developing this industry, and they want to catch more fish from the Red Sea, the Mediterranean Sea, and the Nile River.

    In the aquarium's upstairs archive room, Dr. Samra took out an issue from 1951 of a journal from the Department of Aquatic Products at Xiamen University in China. He said, "We are very pleased to keep this journal on aquatic research from a Chinese university. In the future, we hope to exchange more information between aquatic research institutions in Egypt and China." (Attached picture)

### 埃及纪游

1956-08-29
第6版()
专栏：

    埃及纪游
    冯之丹
    （十二）希腊、罗马博物馆和埃及水族馆
    在亚历山大，我们参观了希腊、罗马博物馆。博物馆建于1891年。在这里可以看到希腊和罗马的文化在埃及留下的遗迹。最引人注意的是纪元前三十年左右的埃及女王克里奥帕特拉的美丽的头像和她的一个石刻的卧像。提起克里奥帕特拉女王，谁都会记起莎士比亚的“安东尼与克里奥帕特拉”的故事。据这个故事说，在古罗马帝国征伐埃及的前夕，克里奥帕特拉女王和古罗马的三执政之一安东尼沉入了恋爱。安东尼反对罗马帝国对埃及的征伐，因而与凯撒为敌。凯撒率领大军进攻埃及，埃及被罗马人灭亡。当罗马人入侵时，克里奥帕特拉女王在自己的深宫内用一条毒蛇咬死她自己。克里奥帕特拉的卧像表现了这一幕悲剧。卧像虽然是石刻的，但能清晰地辨认得出她身上披着的罗纱。她的身旁有一条蛇在咬噬着她的肌肤。
    除此以外，还有一个很大的玻璃柜，里面收集了希腊和罗马的统治者在亚历山大铸造的七千多个钱币………。
    走出了这个博物馆，我们又走进了一个鱼的世界——埃及水族馆。水族馆紧靠着海滨，海风猛烈地袭来，给人带来了寒意。在水族馆的底层，有一长列玻璃水池，每一个水池养着一种鱼类，一共有八十多种彩色缤纷、形状奇特的鱼在这个“世界”里自由地游逛。水族馆的研究所长萨姆拉博士陪同我们参观。据他说，埃及每年的水产产量达五万吨。埃及人现正在发展这个事业，他们要从红海、地中海和尼罗河捕捉更多的鱼。
    在水族馆楼上的资料室里，萨姆拉博士拿出一本中国厦门大学水产系1951年的一期刊物。他说：“我们非常高兴地保存着这本中国大学研究水产的刊物，今后我们希望在埃中两国的水产研究机构之间交换更多的资料。”（附图片）
