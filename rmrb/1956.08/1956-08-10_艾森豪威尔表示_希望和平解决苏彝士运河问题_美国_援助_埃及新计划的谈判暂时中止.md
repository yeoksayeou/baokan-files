Title: Eisenhower Expresses Hope for Peaceful Resolution of Suez Canal Issue; Negotiations on New US "Aid" Plan to Egypt Temporarily Suspended
Author:
Date: 1956-08-10
Page: 6

    Xinhua News Agency, August 9th: Washington dispatch: U.S. President Eisenhower said at a press conference on August 8th that he believes there is still "good reason to hope that reason will prevail" in the Suez Canal issue. He hopes that "this very serious and difficult problem can be resolved peacefully."
    He said that in the current situation, "military force cannot be considered a good solution." However, when reporters asked him if this meant he was against the use of military force under any circumstances, he said that this did not mean that some form of force could not be used under certain circumstances, if necessary, to "defend rights."
    Eisenhower also indicated that the United States does not want to bring the Suez Canal issue to the United Nations Security Council for discussion, because there is a veto power there. He believes that "under the current circumstances, it is a better approach to bring together those countries that are most relevant in terms of their maritime activities and their economic nature."
    Xinhua News Agency, August 9th: Washington dispatch: Caldwell, a press officer for the U.S. International Cooperation Administration, spoke on the evening of the 7th about U.S. "aid" to Egypt in connection with Egypt's reclaiming of the Suez Canal. He said: "Before a political decision is made on the current situation, important materials of great value will not be shipped (to Egypt) from ports in the United States and Europe; at the same time, normal materials will still be shipped as usual." Officials from the U.S. State Department and the International Cooperation Administration also said that negotiations between the U.S. and Egypt on a new "foreign aid plan" (potentially totaling $25 million) have been temporarily suspended.
    On the 8th, State Department spokesman Lipp also told reporters: "The new U.S. aid plan (to Egypt) has not been approved, nor has any previously undertaken obligation been revoked."
    Associated Press reporter Hightower said that the United States is temporarily halting "aid" to Egypt in a limited way because such "aid" might be "interpreted as having political significance, as expressing U.S. support for President Nasser. At the same time, the current desire of the U.S. government is to avoid completely severing economic ties with Egypt."

### 艾森豪威尔表示  希望和平解决苏彝士运河问题  美国“援助”埃及新计划的谈判暂时中止

1956-08-10
第6版()
专栏：

    艾森豪威尔表示
    希望和平解决苏彝士运河问题
    美国“援助”埃及新计划的谈判暂时中止
    新华社9日讯　华盛顿消息：美国总统艾森豪威尔八月八日在记者招待会上说，他认为在苏彝士运河问题上现在仍然“有很好的理由可以希望理智将占上风”。他希望“这个非常严重的困难问题能够用和平方法来解决”。
    他说：在人们所了解的目前情况下，“不能认为军事力量是良好的解决办法”。但是，当记者们问他这是不是说他在任何情况下都反对使用军事力量的时候，他说，这并不是说，在必要时为了“维护权利”也不可以在某种情况下使用某种武力。
    艾森豪威尔还表明美国不希望把苏彝士运河问题交到联合国安全理事会去讨论，因为那里有否决权。他认为：“在目前情况下，把那些按照它们的海上活动和按照它们的经济性质来看是最有关的国家集合在一起，是一种较好的办法。”
    新华社9日讯　华盛顿消息：美国国际合作署新闻发布官考德威尔在七日晚上就埃及收回苏彝士运河这件事谈到美国对埃及的“援助”。他说：“在就目前的局势作出政治决定以前，价值很大的重要物资将不从美国和欧洲的港口（向埃及）运出；而同时，通常的物资还是照样运送。”美国国务院和国际合作署的官员同时还说，美国同埃及的关于新的“援外计划”（总数可能为二千五百万美元）的谈判，已经暂时中止。
    八日，美国国务院发言人里普又对记者说：“（对埃及的）新的美援计划既没有通过，以前所承担的任何义务也没有撤销。”
    美联社记者海托华说，美国有限度地暂时停止对埃及进行“援助”，因为这种“援助”可能被人“解释为具有政治意义，被解释为表示美国支持纳赛尔总统。同时，美国政府目前的愿望是避免同埃及完全断绝经济联系。”
