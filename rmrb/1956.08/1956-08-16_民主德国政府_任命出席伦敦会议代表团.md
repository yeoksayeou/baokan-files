Title: Democratic German Government Appoints Delegation to London Conference
Author:
Date: 1956-08-16
Page: 6
    Xinhua News Agency, Berlin, August 14th: The government of the German Democratic Republic today appointed Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Foreign and Domestic Trade, Heinrich Rau, as the head of the delegation to the London Conference to discuss the Suez Canal issue. Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs, Sepp Schwab, and the Director of the Department of Law and Treaties of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, Siegfried Bock, were appointed as members of the delegation. A press release from the Prime Minister's Office of the German Democratic Republic stated that Germany is a signatory to the 1888 treaty; at the same time, the German Democratic Republic has direct relations with Far East shipping. Therefore, it was decided to send a delegation to the conference to represent the views of the German Democratic Republic at the meeting.

### 民主德国政府  任命出席伦敦会议代表团

1956-08-16
第6版()
专栏：

    民主德国政府
    任命出席伦敦会议代表团
    新华社柏林14日电　德意志民主共和国政府今天任命副总理兼对外与国内贸易部部长海因里希·劳为出席讨论苏彝士运河问题的伦敦会议的代表团团长，外交部副部长塞普·施瓦布和外交部法律与条约司司长西格弗里特·博克为代表团团员。德意志民主共和国总理新闻处的公报说，德国是1888年条约签字国；同时，德意志民主共和国和远东航运有直接关系。因此，决定派遣一个代表团出席会议，以便在会议上代表德意志民主共和国的意见。
