    The Treaty of Constantinople of 1888
    The treaty on the neutralization of the Suez Canal, signed in Constantinople, Turkey in 1888, guarantees the permanent free passage of the Suez Canal to all countries. The main provisions of the treaty are as follows:
    Article 1: The Suez Canal shall always be free and open, both in times of war and in times of peace, to all vessels, whether merchant ships or warships, regardless of the flag they fly.
    …In accordance with the above, the contracting parties shall not, either in time of war or in time of peace, commit any act that may impair the free use of the canal.
    The canal shall never be subject to blockade.
    Article 4…No laws of war, no acts of hostility, no acts intended to obstruct the free navigation of the canal, shall be applied at the ports of entry to the canal, nor within a radius of three nautical miles of such ports, even when the Ottoman Empire is one of the belligerents.
    Article 7…No country shall station any warships (including Lake Timsah and the Great Bitter Lake) in the canal.
    Article 14…The obligations stipulated in this treaty shall not be limited by the period of validity of the Suez Canal Company's lease treaty.
    This treaty was subsequently ratified by Egypt, France, Italy, the Netherlands, Spain, Turkey, and Russia. Great Britain did not sign the treaty until sixteen years later, in 1904, when it reached an agreement with France on the division of spheres of influence in Africa.

### 1888年的君士坦丁堡条约

1956-08-04
第6版()
专栏：国际知识

    1888年的君士坦丁堡条约
    1888年在土耳其君士坦丁堡签订的关于苏彝士运河中立化的条约，保证一切国家永久可以自由通过苏彝士运河。条约中的主要条款如下：
    第一条：苏彝士运河对一切船只，无论商船和军舰，无论该船悬挂何国国旗，在战时与平时将永远自由开放。
    ……根据上述，各缔约国无论在战时与平时，不得有任何损害自由利用运河之行为。
    运河将永远不得遭受封锁。
    第四条……任何战争法，任何敌对行动，任何旨在阻止运河自由航行之行为，均不得在运河入口处的各港口施用，也不得在距此等港口三海里之地区内施用，甚至在奥斯曼帝国为交战国之一方时也如此。
    第七条……各国均不得在运河内停泊任何军舰（包括第姆斯湖和苦湖在内）。
    第十四条……本条约所规定之义务，不受苏彝士运河公司租借条约有效时期之限制。
    这个条约当时曾经由埃及、法国、意大利、荷兰、西班牙、土耳其、俄国批准。英国一直到十六年以后，在1904年同法国就划分在非洲的势力范围达成协议的时候才在这个条约上签字。
    （君）
