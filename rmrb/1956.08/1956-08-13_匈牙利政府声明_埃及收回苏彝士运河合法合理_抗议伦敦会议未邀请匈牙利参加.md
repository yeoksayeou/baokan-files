Title: Hungarian Government Statement: Egypt's Reclamation of the Suez Canal is Legal and Reasonable; Protests London Conference for Not Inviting Hungary
Author:
Date: 1956-08-13
Page: 5
Xinhua News Agency, August 12th dispatch from Budapest: The Hungarian Ministry of Foreign Affairs issued a statement on August 11th regarding Egypt's nationalization of the Suez Canal Company, stating that the Republic of Egypt's transformation of the Suez Canal Company into state property is in accordance with Egypt's interests and the principles of international law. The Hungarian government is fully satisfied with the obligations undertaken by the government of the Republic of Egypt, as stipulated in the nationalization law, to guarantee the freedom of navigation on the Suez Canal.

The statement said that the Hungarian government believes that the Western countries' interference in Egypt's sovereignty can only lead to consequences that endanger peace and the peaceful interests of all peoples, and does not help to resolve the issue itself; and believes that the "Twenty-Four Nation Conference" to be held in London does not have the universality necessary to resolve major international issues.

The statement specifically pointed out that the failure to invite Hungary to the London Conference (Hungary participated in the 1888 Convention) is an act of discrimination, and the Hungarian government firmly protests this behavior.

The Hungarian government believes that the United Nations is an institution that should discuss and can use peaceful methods to resolve any problems that may arise regarding the freedom of navigation in the Suez Canal or other international waterways.

### 匈牙利政府声明  埃及收回苏彝士运河合法合理  抗议伦敦会议未邀请匈牙利参加

1956-08-13
第5版()
专栏：

    匈牙利政府声明
    埃及收回苏彝士运河合法合理
    抗议伦敦会议未邀请匈牙利参加
    新华社12日讯　据塔斯社布达佩斯讯：匈牙利外交部十一日发表关于埃及把苏彝士运河公司收归国有的声明说，埃及共和国把苏彝士运河公司变为国家财产，这是符合埃及利益和国际法原则的。匈牙利政府完全满意埃及共和国政府所承担的、在国有化法律中规定的关于保障苏彝士运河通航自由的义务。
    声明说，匈牙利政府认为，西方国家对埃及主权的干涉行为只能带来危害和平和各国人民和平利益的后果，完全无助于问题本身的解决；并且认为将在伦敦召开的“二十四国会议”不具备为解决重大国际问题所必要的普遍性。
    声明特别指出：伦敦会议没有邀请匈牙利参加（匈牙利参加了1888年公约）是一种歧视行为，匈牙利政府坚决抗议这种行为。
    匈牙利政府认为，联合国是一个应当讨论并且可以用和平方法来解决在苏彝士运河或其他国际航道的自由通航方面可能产生的问题的机构。
