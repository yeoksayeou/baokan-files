Title: New York Times Reporter Reston Comments on the Contradictory Attitudes of the US Democratic Party
Author:
Date: 1956-08-22
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, New York, August 21st: "New York Times" reporter James Reston commented on the Democratic Party convention in Chicago, saying, "There is nothing more contradictory than the atmosphere of this convention and Stevenson's attitude towards domestic and foreign affairs."

Reston said that although all of the Democratic Party's foreign relations experts—including former Secretaries of State Acheson, Bowles, Nitze, Finletter, and Cohen—advised the convention to avoid expressing specific attitudes towards future policy on China, the convention did exactly the opposite.

Reston pointed out that the Chicago convention demonstrated that "these politicians are the most interesting, illogical, self-contradictory, argumentative, and quarrelsome people in mankind."

### 纽约时报记者赖斯顿  评美国民主党的矛盾态度

1956-08-22
第6版()
专栏：

    纽约时报记者赖斯顿
    评美国民主党的矛盾态度
    据新华社21日讯　纽约消息：“纽约时报”记者詹姆斯·赖斯顿评论芝加哥的民主党代表大会说：“没有比这次代表大会的气氛和史蒂文森对内政外交问题的态度更矛盾的了。”
    赖斯顿说，虽然民主党所有的外交关系问题专家——包括前国务卿艾奇逊、鲍尔士、尼兹、芬勒特和柯恩——劝告代表大会对于将来对中国的政策避免表示具体的态度，但是大会所做的恰恰相反。
    赖斯顿指出，芝加哥大会表现出“这些政治家是人类中最有趣、不讲逻辑、自相矛盾、好争论、爱吵闹的人们”。
