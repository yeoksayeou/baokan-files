Title: Pakistani Government Refuses to Issue Passports to Football Team Visiting China; "Karachi Times" Hopes Government Will Revise This Decision
Author:
Date: 1956-08-25
Page: 5

    Xinhua News Agency, Karachi, August 24th: The "Karachi Times" today criticized the Pakistani government's passport bureau in an editorial for refusing to issue passports to the Pakistani football team, which intended to visit China at the invitation of the Chinese Football Association.
    The Pakistan Football Federation applied for passports for the football team's eighteen players to travel to Ceylon, Singapore, and China. However, the passport bureau removed China from the list of countries the football team was going to visit.
    The newspaper pointed out: "Sports teams are an important medium for promoting friendship, and restricting their scope of visits will only cause the country to suffer losses from personal contact and enhanced personal experience of athletes."
    "It would only be beneficial for the Pakistani team to visit there. Just like what has been done before in diplomacy and journalism, the necessity of expanding contacts in sports cannot be overemphasized."
    The newspaper concluded by expressing hope that the passport bureau would revise its decision.

### 巴政府不发给足球队访问中国的护照  “喀喇蚩时报”希望政府修改这个决定

1956-08-25
第5版()
专栏：

    巴政府不发给足球队访问中国的护照
    “喀喇蚩时报”希望政府修改这个决定
    新华社喀喇蚩24日电　“喀喇蚩时报”今天在一篇社论中批评巴基斯坦政府的护照局不发给巴基斯坦足球队护照，这个足球队应中国足球协会的邀请打算到中国去访问。
    巴基斯坦足球联合会代表这个足球队的十八名球员申请发给前往锡兰、新加坡和中国的护照。但是护照局从这个足球队将要访问的国家的名单上删去了中国。
    这家报纸指出：“体育代表队是促进友好的一个重要的媒介，要是限制它们的访问范围，就只会使通过个人接触和增进运动员个人的经验而得到的国家的收获遭受损失。”
    “巴基斯坦代表队到那里去访问只会有益处。正像以前在外交和新闻方面所做的那样，在体育方面扩大联系的必要性就是再强调也不会过分的。”
    这家报纸最后表示希望护照局将会修改它的决定。
