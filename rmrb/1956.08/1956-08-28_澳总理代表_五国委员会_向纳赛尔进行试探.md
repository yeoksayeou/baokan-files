Title: Australian Prime Minister Probes Nasser on Behalf of "Five-Nation Committee"
Author: 
Date: 1956-08-28
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, Cairo, August 27th (TASS): It is known here that the Egyptian Foreign Ministry has received a letter from Australian Prime Minister Robert Menzies to the President of the Egyptian Republic, Gamal Abdel Nasser.

According to the Middle East News Agency, the letter raises the question of whether President Nasser agrees to hold talks with the so-called "Five-Nation Committee," composed of representatives from Australia, Sweden, Ethiopia, Iran, and the United States, to discuss the views of some countries participating in the London Conference.

According to press sources, the actions of the "Five-Nation Committee" have nothing to do with the decisions of the London Conference and have not been agreed upon by all attendees of the London Conference. The actions of the "Five-Nation Committee" are a one-sided attempt by Western countries to present Egypt with an ultimatum, using the well-known American plan that stipulates the re-establishment of foreign control over the Suez Canal.

### 澳总理代表“五国委员会”向纳赛尔进行试探

1956-08-28
第6版()
专栏：

    澳总理代表“五国委员会”向纳赛尔进行试探
    新华社27日讯　塔斯社开罗27日讯：这里都已经知道，埃及外交部已经接到了澳大利亚总理孟席斯给埃及共和国总统加麦尔·阿卜杜勒·纳赛尔的信。
    据中东通讯社报道，这封信中提出这样一个问题：纳赛尔总统是否同意同澳大利亚、瑞典、埃塞俄比亚、伊朗和美国代表组成的所谓“五国委员会”进行会谈，以便讨论参加伦敦会议的一些国家的看法。
    据新闻界人士指出，“五国委员会”的行动同伦敦会议的决定没有任何关系，这种行动没有得到伦敦会议全体到会者的协议。“五国委员会”的行动是西方国家企图片面地用最后通牒的方式向埃及提出大家所知道的、规定把苏彝士运河重新置于外国控制下的美国计划。
