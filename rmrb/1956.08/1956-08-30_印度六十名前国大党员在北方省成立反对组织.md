Title: Sixty Former Congress Party Members in India Establish Opposition Organization in Uttar Pradesh
Author:
Date: 1956-08-30
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, New Delhi, August 29th: According to a report in the "Times of India," sixty former Congress Party members in Uttar Pradesh have established a Congress Party opposition organization.

The organization's chairman, Paliwal, a member of the Uttar Pradesh Legislative Assembly, told the press on August 27th that the organization is not a political party and is currently only called the Congress Party Opposition Organization. Its purpose is related to elections, namely to form a united front of opposition parties to defeat the ruling party. It will operate in Uttar Pradesh.

A resolution passed by the organization stated that it supports "a synthesis of Marxism and Gandhism."

### 印度六十名前国大党员在北方省成立反对组织

1956-08-30
第6版()
专栏：

    印度六十名前国大党员在北方省成立反对组织
    新华社新德里29日电　据“印度时报”报道，北方省六十名前国大党党员成立了一个国大党反对组织。
    这个组织的主席、北方省议会议员帕利华尔8月27日对报界说，这个组织并不是一个政党，目前它只称为国大党反对组织。它的目的是关于选举方面的，就是要组成反对党的联合阵线来击败执政党。它将在北方省进行活动。
    这个组织通过的一项决议说，它拥护“马克思主义和甘地主义的综合”。
