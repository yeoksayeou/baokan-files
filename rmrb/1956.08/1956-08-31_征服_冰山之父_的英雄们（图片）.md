Author:
Date: 1956-08-31

Heroes Who Conquered the "Father of Ice Mountains"
    Chinese and Soviet mountaineers advance in the area of glacial crevasses at an altitude of about 6,000 meters.
　Photo by Shi Zhanchun (Xinhua News Agency draft)
    A camel caravan carries supplies towards Muztagh Ata.
                Photo by Shi Zhanchun (Xinhua News Agency draft)
    Chinese and Soviet mountaineers climbed to the summit of Muztagh Ata and solemnly planted the national flags of China and the Soviet Union at the summit.
                                        Photo by Shi Zhanchun (Xinhua News Agency draft)
    Chinese and Soviet mountaineers advance along ice steps with an incline of more than 45 degrees.
                                   Photo by Weng Qingzhang (Xinhua News Agency draft)
    The No. 3 high-altitude camp established by the Chinese-Soviet joint climbing team at an altitude of 6,170 meters.
                                       Photo by Shi Zhanchun (Xinhua News Agency draft)
    After the Chinese-Soviet joint climbing team reached the summit of Muztagh Ata, they returned to the base camp. Here, all team members listened to congratulatory telegrams sent from various places to celebrate their successful climb. 　　Photo by Shi Zhanchun (Xinhua News Agency draft)

### 征服“冰山之父”的英雄们（图片）

1956-08-31
第4版()
专栏：

    征服“冰山之父”的英雄们
    中、苏爬山运动员们在海拔六千公尺左右的冰河裂缝地带进军。
　史占春摄（新华社稿）
    骆驼队驮上物资向慕士塔格山前进。
                史占春摄（新华社稿）
    中、苏爬山运动员攀登到慕士塔格山顶峰，隆重地在顶峰插上了中、苏两国国旗。
                                        史占春摄（新华社稿）
    中、苏爬山运动员踏着冰坡四十五度以上的冰阶前进。
                                   翁庆章摄（新华社稿）
    中、苏混合爬山队在海拔六千一百七十公尺处建立的第三号高山营地。
                                       史占春摄（新华社稿）
    中、苏混合爬山队攀登上了慕士塔格山顶峰后，回到了大本营所在地。在这里，全体队员听读从各地拍发来的庆贺他们爬山胜利的贺电。　　史占春摄（新华社稿）
