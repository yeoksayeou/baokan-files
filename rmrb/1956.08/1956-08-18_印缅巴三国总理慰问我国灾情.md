Title: Prime Ministers of India, Burma, and Pakistan Extend Condolences for Disaster in China

Author: 
Date: 1956-08-18
Page: 1

Xinhua News Agency, 17th: After the typhoon struck the southeastern coastal areas of China, Indian Prime Minister Nehru wrote to Pan Zili, the Chinese Ambassador to India, on August 7th, expressing his sympathy to the Chinese government and people regarding this matter. Burmese Prime Minister U Ba Swe and Pakistani Prime Minister Muhammad Ali also telegraphed Premier Zhou Enlai on August 10th and 13th respectively, expressing their sympathy and condolences to China on behalf of their governments and people for the loss of life and property caused by the typhoon.

Premier Zhou Enlai has replied with telegrams to thank them for their condolences.

### 印缅巴三国总理慰问我国灾情

1956-08-18
第1版()
专栏：

    印缅巴三国总理慰问我国灾情
    新华社17日讯　我国东南沿海地区遭受台风袭击后，印度总理尼赫鲁在八月七日写信给我国驻印度大使潘自力，就此事向我国政府和人民表示同情。缅甸总理吴巴瑞和巴基斯坦总理穆罕默德·阿里也分别在八月十日和十三日致电周恩来总理，对于我国因台风侵袭而造成生命和财产的损失，代表他们的政府和人民向我国表示同情和慰问。
    周恩来总理已分别复电感谢他们的慰问。
