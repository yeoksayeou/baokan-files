Title: Suez Canal Open to All Ships: Egyptian Embassy in Britain Reaffirms Egyptian Government's Determination
Author:
Date: 1956-08-25
Page: 6

　　Xinhua News Agency, August 24th, London dispatch: An Egyptian Embassy spokesman in Britain said on August 23rd that the Egyptian government is "determined to keep the Suez Canal open to all ships forever, regardless of their nationality."

　　The spokesman also said: "The policy of the Egyptian government and the Suez Canal Authority is to keep the canal in the most efficient and navigable condition to accommodate the increasing volume of shipping and trade."

　　That evening, the Egyptian Embassy issued another statement to the press, pointing out that the British Foreign Office spokesman's statement on the 21st, alleging Egyptian "discrimination against British and French ships," was "unfounded because it is completely inconsistent with the Egyptian government's declared policy of maintaining and safeguarding freedom of navigation on the Suez Canal." The statement concluded by emphasizing: "Therefore, the Egyptian Embassy must categorically deny any news of such discrimination."

### 苏彝士运河对一切船只开放  埃及驻英使馆重申埃及政府的决心

1956-08-25
第6版()
专栏：

    苏彝士运河对一切船只开放
    埃及驻英使馆重申埃及政府的决心
    新华社24日讯　伦敦消息：埃及驻英国大使馆发言人八月二十三日说，埃及政府“决心永远使苏彝士运河对一切船只开放，而不问它们的国籍如何”。
    这位发言人还说：“埃及政府和苏彝士运河当局的政策是要使运河保持最有效和适于航行的状态，以适应日益增加的航运和贸易量。”
    在这一天晚上，埃及大使馆又向报界发表声明，指出英国外交部发言人在二十一日所说的所谓埃及“歧视英法两国船只”的说法是“毫无根据的，因为它同埃及政府所宣布的要维持和维护苏彝士运河航运自由的政策是完全不一致的”。声明最后强调说：“因此，埃及大使馆必须断然否认关于这种歧视的任何消息。”
