Title: Asian and African Countries' Political Circles and People Continue to Support Egypt's Just Struggle; Algerian People's Armed Forces Launch Offensive; Strikes and Market Closures Held in Aden, Bahrain, and Other Places

Author: 
Date: 1956-08-18
Page: 6

North Africa
Xinhua News Agency, August 17th dispatch from Algiers: In addition to holding strikes and market closures on the 16th to protest the opening of the London conference, Arabs in French North Africa also intensified their offensives against French colonial troops to express their support for Egypt through actions to fight for independence.

French officials admit that the Algerian people's resistance activities are gaining momentum. News on the 16th said that in Algiers alone, the people's armed forces launched seven fierce attacks in the past 48 hours.

On the morning of the 16th, fighting also took place between the Nibader Dam and Tourenna on the main road to Morocco.

Xinhua News Agency, August 17th dispatch from Rabat: The Moroccan government-controlled radio station broadcast on the 16th, saying, "The Moroccan people have expressed their full sympathy for Egypt and disapprove of the actions of their Western friends."

The "Flag Newspaper" of the Istiqlal Party, Morocco's main ruling party, published an editorial arguing that the proposal to "internationalize" the Suez Canal was unreasonable. It said, "If any country asserts that the navigation of the Thames River is under its jurisdiction, British newspapers would laugh at it."

The newspaper also called the London conference initiated by the three Western countries to discuss the canal issue a "crippled conference."

Xinhua News Agency, August 17th dispatch from Tripoli: Tripoli, Libya's winter capital, held a general strike on the 16th in support of Egypt. All stores, restaurants, and cafes were closed. Almost all of the city's daily life was paralyzed.

Youths gathered in teams to march in the streets, shouting slogans in support of Egypt.

Middle East
Xinhua News Agency, August 17th dispatch from Paris, TASS: According to Agence France-Presse, Cairo, strikes in support of Egypt were held on August 16th in the British protectorates of Aden, Bahrain, Qatar, and Kuwait.

Xinhua News Agency, August 17th dispatch from Beirut, TASS: More than 7,000 people held a rally in Beirut on the 15th in support of Egypt and demanded the nationalization of foreign oil companies in Arab countries. A resolution was passed at the meeting, expressing full support for Egypt's policies.

India
Xinhua News Agency, New Delhi, August 17th: According to a report in the Bengali newspaper "Freedom Daily" on August 15th, a large procession of Calcutta students demonstrated in front of the British Deputy High Commissioner's Office, the French Consulate, and the United States Information Service in Calcutta on the 14th, demanding that imperialism stop interfering in Egypt.

The Kashmir Students Union also passed a resolution on the 14th, guaranteeing that "Kashmir youth support Egyptian youth in their struggle against imperialist forces."

Pakistan
Xinhua News Agency, Karachi, August 16th: A public rally was held in Peshawar, Pakistan, on the afternoon of August 14th under the auspices of the Muslim League in the city. The rally passed a resolution expressing full support for Egypt's nationalization of the Suez Canal Company.

The public rally warned Western countries not to take any military action against Egypt.

Thailand
Xinhua News Agency, August 17th: According to the "Siam Mass News" on August 12th, Nai Pao Sriyanon, Director-General of the Thai Police and Deputy Minister of the Interior, told the newspaper's reporter on August 10th in his capacity as Secretary of the ruling Liberal Manangkasila Party that Egypt's nationalization of the Suez Canal was completely correct. "Nationalizing the Suez Canal Company is Egypt's direct right, because Egypt, like all independent countries, has its sovereignty."

The newspaper reported that Nai Wisit Sibutarat, leader of the Socialist Party of Thailand, also sent a telegram to the Egyptian Minister to Thailand on August 13th, expressing warm support for Egypt's actions in recovering the Suez Canal.

### 亚非国家政界和人民继续声援埃及的正义斗争  阿尔及利亚人民武装发动进攻　亚丁和巴林等地举行罢工罢市

1956-08-18
第6版()
专栏：

    亚非国家政界和人民继续声援埃及的正义斗争
    阿尔及利亚人民武装发动进攻　亚丁和巴林等地举行罢工罢市
    北非
    新华社17日讯阿尔及尔消息：法属北非的阿拉伯人在十六日除了举行罢工、罢市抗议伦敦会议开幕之外，同时还加紧进攻法国殖民军，以争取独立的行动来表示他们对埃及的声援。
    法国官方承认，阿尔及利亚人民的抵抗活动正在得势。十六日的消息说，仅在阿尔及尔一地，人民武装部队在过去四十八小时内就发动了七次猛烈的进攻。
    十六日上午，在尼巴德尔水坝和通往摩洛哥的主要公路上的土伦纳之间也发生了战斗。
    据新华社17日讯　拉巴特消息：摩洛哥政府控制的广播电台在十六日广播说：“摩洛哥人民已经表示，他们完全同情埃及，并且不赞成他们的西方朋友的行动”。
    摩洛哥主要执政党独立党的“旗帜报”发表社论，认为硬要苏彝士运河“国际化”的主张是毫无道理的。它说：“如果有哪个国家断言泰晤士河的航行是属它管辖的，那英国的报纸就会嘲笑它”。
    这家报纸还把西方三国发起的讨论运河问题的伦敦会议叫做“残废了的会议”。
    新华社17日讯　的黎波里消息：利比亚的冬都的黎波里在十六日举行总罢工支持埃及。全市的商店、食堂和咖啡馆都停止营业。城市的日常生活几乎全部陷入瘫痪状态。
    青年们集队在街上游行，高呼支持埃及的口号。
    中东
    新华社17日讯  塔斯社巴黎十七日讯：据法新社开罗消息，英国保护地亚丁、巴林、卡塔尔和科威特在八月十六日都举行了声援埃及的罢工。
    新华社17日讯　据塔斯社贝鲁特十六日讯：七千多人十五日在贝鲁特举行集会支持埃及，并且要求把在阿拉伯国家的外国石油公司国有化。会上通过了一项决议，表示完全支持埃及的政策。
    印度
    据新华社新德里17日电　据孟加拉文日报“自由报”八月十五日报道，加尔各答学生组成的庞大的游行队伍十四日在加尔各答英国副高级专员公署、法国领事馆和美国新闻处前面举行示威，要求帝国主义停止干涉埃及。
    克什米尔学生联合会也在十四日通过决议，保证“克什米尔青年支持埃及青年进行反对帝国主义势力的斗争”。
    巴基斯坦
    据新华社喀喇蚩16日电　巴基斯坦的白沙瓦城八月十四日下午在这个城的穆斯林联盟的主持下举行了一个公众集会。这个集会通过了一项决议，表示完全支持埃及把苏彝士运河公司收归国有。
    公众集会警告西方国家不要对埃及采取任何军事行动。
    泰国
    据新华社17日讯  据“暹罗群众报”八月十二日报道，泰国警察总监兼内政部副部长乃炮·是耶暖在八月十日以执政党自由玛兰卡是拉党秘书名义向这家报纸的记者发表谈话说，埃及把苏彝士运河收归国有的做法是完全正确的。“把苏彝士运河公司收归国有是埃及直接的权利，因为埃及同所有独立国家一样具有它的主权”。
    报纸报道，泰国社会主义党领袖乃威实·诗博他拉也在八月十三日打电报给埃及驻泰国公使，表示热烈支持埃及收回苏彝士运河的行动。
