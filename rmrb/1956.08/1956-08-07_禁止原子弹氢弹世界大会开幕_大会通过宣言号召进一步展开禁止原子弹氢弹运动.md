Title: World Conference Against Atomic and Hydrogen Bombs Opens; Conference Passes Declaration Calling for Further Expansion of the Movement to Ban Atomic and Hydrogen Bombs
Author:
Date: 1956-08-07
Page: 1

Xinhua News Agency, Tokyo, 6th: The Second World Conference Against Atomic and Hydrogen Bombs began at 5:30 pm today at the Tokyo International Stadium.

This world conference was jointly initiated by more than one hundred mass organizations, including Japanese workers, women, and youth. Approximately 30,000 representatives from fourteen countries, including China, the Soviet Union, the United States, the United Kingdom, and France, as well as representatives from various Japanese mass organizations, participated in the conference.

At the meeting, Ikuo Yasui, Secretary-General of the Japan Council Against Atomic and Hydrogen Bombs, gave a report on the development of the movement to ban atomic and hydrogen bombs over the past two years. Atomic bomb victims at the meeting recounted the disasters brought upon them by the atomic bombs.

The conference concluded by passing a declaration calling for further expansion of the movement to ban atomic and hydrogen bombs.

The World Conference will continue in Nagasaki from the 9th to the 11th.

Xinhua News Agency, 6th: The All-China Democratic Women's Federation today sent a telegram to the Japan Council Against Atomic and Hydrogen Bombs, congratulating them on the opening of the Second World Conference Against Atomic and Hydrogen Bombs.

The telegram said: "On the occasion of the opening of the Second World Conference Against Atomic and Hydrogen Bombs, we extend our heartfelt condolences on behalf of Chinese women to the Japanese people who have suffered from atomic and hydrogen bomb disasters; we pay our highest respect to the Japanese people and women who have been tirelessly fighting against atomic weapons. We wish the conference success."

Xinhua News Agency, 6th: The Red Cross Society of China today sent a telegram to the Japan Council Against Atomic and Hydrogen Bombs, congratulating them on the opening of the Second World Conference Against Atomic and Hydrogen Bombs.

The telegram stated: "The Red Cross Society of China extends warm congratulations to the Second World Conference Against Atomic and Hydrogen Bombs and expresses its deep concern and sympathy to the people of your country who have suffered from the atomic bomb attacks and hydrogen bomb testing disasters. We believe that under the leadership of your council and with the support of all the Japanese people and the people of the world, the conference will further make a powerful contribution to banning atomic and hydrogen bombs and safeguarding world peace."

### 禁止原子弹氢弹世界大会开幕  大会通过宣言号召进一步展开禁止原子弹氢弹运动

1956-08-07
第1版()
专栏：

    禁止原子弹氢弹世界大会开幕
    大会通过宣言号召进一步展开禁止原子弹氢弹运动
    新华社6日讯　东京消息：第二届禁止原子弹和氢弹世界大会今天下午五时半开始在东京国际体育场举行。
    这次世界大会是日本工人、妇女、青年等一百多个群众团体联合发起召开的。参加大会的有中国、苏联、美国、英国、法国等十四个国家的代表和日本各种群众团体的代表共约三万人。
    会上，日本禁止原子弹和氢弹协议会事务总长安井郁作了关于两年来禁止原子弹和氢弹运动发展情况的报告。原子弹受害人在会上控诉了原子弹给他们带来的灾难。
    大会最后通过了号召进一步展开禁止原子弹氢弹运动的宣言。
    世界大会将在九日至十一日在长崎继续举行。
    新华社6日讯　中华全国民主妇女联合会今天打电报给日本禁止原子弹和氢弹协议会，庆贺第二届禁止原子弹和氢弹世界大会的召开。
    电报说：“值此第二届禁止原子弹和氢弹世界大会召开之际，谨代表中国妇女向日本遭受原子弹、氢弹灾害的人们致以衷心的慰问；向为反对原子武器而奋斗不懈的日本人民和妇女致以崇高的敬意。祝大会成功。”
    新华社6日讯　中国红十字会今天致电日本禁止原子弹和氢弹协议会，祝贺第二届禁止原子弹和氢弹世界大会的召开。
    电报中说：“中国红十字会对第二届禁止原子弹和氢弹世界大会的召开致以热烈的祝贺，并对贵国遭受原子弹轰炸和氢弹试验灾难的人民表示深切的关怀和慰问。我们相信在贵会的主持以及全日本人民和全世界人民的支援下，大会必将进一步对禁止原子弹、氢弹，维护世界和平作出有力的贡献。”
