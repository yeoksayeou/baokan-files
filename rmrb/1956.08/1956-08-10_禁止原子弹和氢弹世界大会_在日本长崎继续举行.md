Title: World Conference Against Atomic and Hydrogen Bombs Continues in Nagasaki, Japan
Author:
Date: 1956-08-10
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, Nagasaki, 9th August: The Second World Conference Against Atomic and Hydrogen Bombs, which opened on August 6th, continued today in Nagasaki, Japan, the second city to suffer from the American atomic bombing.

Approximately three thousand representatives from various international organizations and fourteen countries, as well as representatives from Japanese groups and various regions, attended the conference.

After the conference began at eight o'clock in the morning, a presidium of fifty people was first elected, including thirteen foreign representatives such as Mrs. Felton, Vice-President of the International Women's Federation.

The Mayor of Nagasaki, Tsutomu Tagawa, delivered a speech at the meeting, welcoming the representatives of various countries attending the conference. When the executive chairman of the conference introduced the foreign representatives, the Japanese representatives warmly applauded, and the atmosphere in the venue was full of friendship.

Ikusaku Yasui, Secretary-General of the Japan Council Against Atomic and Hydrogen Bombs, gave a report on the movement to ban atomic weapons. Tokutaro Kitamura, representative of the Liberal Democratic Party of Japan, Inejiro Asanuma, representative of the Socialist Party, Kenji Nakahara, representative of the Labor-Farmer Party, and Masao Iwama, representative of the Communist Party, delivered speeches successively.

At nearly eleven o'clock, the conference was suspended. All the representatives went to the Peace Park in the atomic bomb explosion center to participate in the Peace Memorial Ceremony commemorating the eleventh anniversary of Nagasaki's suffering from the American atomic bombing.

The conference continued in the afternoon. On August 10, group meetings will be held to discuss various issues related to the prohibition of nuclear weapons, and on August 11, a plenary meeting will be held to adopt resolutions and declarations.

### 禁止原子弹和氢弹世界大会  在日本长崎继续举行

1956-08-10
第5版()
专栏：

    禁止原子弹和氢弹世界大会
    在日本长崎继续举行
    新华社9日讯　长崎消息：八月六日开幕的第二届禁止原子弹和氢弹世界大会今天在日本的遭受美国原子弹轰炸的第二个城市——长崎市继续举行。
    来自各个国际组织和十四个国家的代表以及日本各团体和各地的代表共约三千人出席了大会。
    大会在上午八点开始后，首先选出了五十人的主席团，其中包括国际妇女联合会副主席费尔顿夫人等十三位外国代表。
    长崎市市长田川务在会上致词，表示欢迎出席大会的各国代表。当大会执行主席介绍各位外国代表时，日本代表们热烈鼓掌欢迎，会场里充满着友谊的气氛。
    日本禁止原子弹和氢弹协议会事务总长安井郁作了关于禁止原子武器运动的报告。日本自由民主党代表北村德太郎、社会党代表浅沼稻次郎、劳农党代表中原健次和共产党代表岩间正男相继致词。
    将近十一点的时候，大会暂停。全体代表到原子弹爆炸中心区的和平公园参加了纪念长崎市遭受美国原子弹轰炸十一周年的和平纪念会。
    大会在下午继续举行。八月十日，将分组开会讨论各项有关禁止核子武器的问题，到八月十一日再举行全体会议，通过决议和宣言。
