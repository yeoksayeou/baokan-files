Title: Moon Exploration Failed, Rocket Exploded Mid-Flight
Author:
Date: 1960-12-18
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, December 17th, Cape Canaveral (United States) Dispatch: The United States' attempt to launch a lunar rocket at Cape Canaveral on the 15th ended in failure once again.

    This launch used a three-stage "Atlas-Able" rocket. The United States originally intended this rocket to be the first artificial lunar satellite to orbit the moon, as a grand finale to its rocket activities in 1960. However, shortly after launch, the rocket lost control in flight and exploded.

    The New York Times reported that this event was "new evidence that the wishes of American scientists to conquer the universe still exceed the capabilities of their rockets." "It now appears that (the United States) is too hasty in launching effective payloads to the lunar region in order to repeat the sensational achievements of Soviet lunar rocket launches." "The United States is still far behind in manufacturing reliable and powerful rockets for spaceflight." The newspaper also pointed out that after this failure, "the United States will have to temporarily withdraw from the race to conquer the moon until new, larger rocket combinations and new lunar satellites are made."

### 探月未成身先碎  美月球火箭中途爆炸

1960-12-18
第5版()
专栏：

    探月未成身先碎
    美月球火箭中途爆炸
    新华社17日讯  卡纳维拉尔角（美国）消息：美国15日在卡纳维拉尔角发射月球火箭又一次遭到了失败。
    这次发射使用的是一个三级的“阿特拉斯—埃布尔”火箭。美国原来打算使这支火箭成为围绕月球运行的第一个人造月球卫星，以此作为1960年火箭活动的压轴戏。可是这支火箭在发射后不久就在飞行中失去控制而爆炸了。
    《纽约时报》报道这件事情时说，这是一个“新的证据，证明美国科学家在征服宇宙方面的愿望依旧是超过他们的火箭的可能性的”。“现在看来，在发射有效负荷到月球区域去以便重复苏联发射月球火箭的轰动性成就方面，（美国）是太过于匆忙了”。“在制造宇宙飞行用的可靠而威力强大的火箭方面，美国依然差得很远。”这家报纸还指出，在这次失败以后，“美国将不得不暂时退出征服月球的竞赛，直到制成新的比较大的火箭组合和新的月球卫星时为止”。
