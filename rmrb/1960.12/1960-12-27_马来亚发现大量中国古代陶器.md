Title: A Large Quantity of Ancient Chinese Pottery Discovered in Malaya
Author:
Date: 1960-12-27
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency Report:

    In Malacca, Malaya, more than 7,000 pieces of broken ancient Chinese pottery have recently been excavated. A corner of an ancient, large building was also discovered. The building is believed to have been constructed in 1450 (equivalent to the Ming Dynasty in China), and the pottery is also believed to mainly belong to the same period. According to local archaeologists, some of the 7,000 pieces of pottery belong to many different dynasties.

    This discovery is considered to be of great significance for the study of Malayan history, Southeast Asian history, and the trade relations between ancient China and Southeast Asia.

### 马来亚发现大量中国古代陶器

1960-12-27
第6版()
专栏：

    马来亚发现大量中国古代陶器
    据新华社讯　在马来亚的马六甲，新近发掘出了中国古代破碎陶器七千多件。同时还发现了一个古代巨大建筑物残存的一角。这个建筑物被认为是在1450年（相当于中国明朝时期）建造的，而陶器也被认为主要属于同一时期。据当地考古学家研究，在七千件陶器中还有一些分属于许多不同朝代。
    这一发现被认为对于研究马来亚历史、东南亚历史以及古代中国同东南亚的贸易关系具有重要意义。
