International Briefs

    Tanganyika People Demand Kenyatta's Release
    DAR ES SALAAM—People in Dar es Salaam, the capital of Tanganyika, held a demonstration on the 21st, demanding the immediate release of Jomo Kenyatta, the Kenyan African leader still imprisoned by the British colonial authorities.
    Argentine People Demand Release of Political Prisoners
    BUENOS AIRES—Families of those arrested in Argentina recently held a rally in front of the Presidential Palace, demanding the release of political prisoners and arrested workers and trade union leaders.
    The authorities dispatched police to try to stop the rally, but the families of those arrested continued to demonstrate and distributed leaflets demanding the release of political prisoners before Christmas (December 25).
    In recent days, some opposition congressmen and trade union groups have submitted proposals and demands to the government for the release of political prisoners.
    Arab League Economic Committee Meeting Concludes
    CAIRO—The twelve-day meeting of the Arab League Economic Committee concluded here on the 18th. The meeting passed a resolution calling on Arab countries not to trade with France in order to support the Algerian people's struggle for independence.
    Malayan MP Condemns Government Persecution of Opposition Parties
    KUALA LUMPUR—Some opposition members of the Malayan Federation parliament, during a debate in the Lower House on the Internal Security Ministry's budget for next year on the 20th, condemned the Rahman government for abusing the "Internal Security Act" in November to arrest a group of opposition party members, trade union leaders, teachers, and students.
    In order to continue to suppress the Malayan National Liberation Movement and persecute opposition parties and the people, the authorities of the Malayan Federation formulated the so-called "Internal Security Act." According to this new law, the government can order the detention of anyone for two years without a public trial.
    Saudi Arabian King Forms New Cabinet
    MECCA—According to a broadcast by Mecca Radio on the 21st, Saudi Arabian King Saud has accepted the resignation of Prime Minister Faisal and his cabinet and has formed a new cabinet. The King will personally serve as the Prime Minister of the new cabinet.
    According to foreign news agencies, Prime Minister Faisal had disagreements with the King on state affairs.
    Mali President Visits Guinea
    BAMAKO—Mali Republic President Modibo Keita left here by special plane for Conakry on the 23rd at the invitation of Guinean President Sékou Touré. He was accompanied by Mega, the Vice-President of the Malian National Assembly, and others.
    Ceylon Prime Minister Travels to India
    COLOMBO—Ceylon Prime Minister Sirimavo Bandaranaike left by special plane for India on the 20th for a short vacation and pilgrimage. According to newspapers, during her absence, the Minister of Agriculture, Land, Irrigation and Power, C. P. de Silva, will act as head of the cabinet.

### 国际简讯

1960-12-24
第6版()
专栏：国际简讯

    国际简讯
    坦噶尼喀人民要求释放肯雅塔
    达累斯萨拉姆讯坦噶尼喀首府达累斯萨拉姆的人民二十一日举行示威，要求立即释放仍被英国殖民当局所监禁的怯尼亚非洲人领袖乔莫·肯雅塔。
    阿根廷人民要求释放政治犯
    布宜诺斯艾利斯讯阿根廷被捕者的家属最近在总统府前举行了集会，要求释放政治犯和被捕的工人和工会领袖。
    当局出动了警察企图制止这次集会，但是被捕者的家属仍然进行了示威，并且散发了要求在圣诞节（十二月二十五日）前释放政治犯的传单。
    最近几天，一些反对派众议员和工会团体向政府提出了释放政治犯的提案和要求。
    阿拉伯联盟经委会会议闭幕
    开罗讯　历时十二天的阿拉伯联盟经济委员会会议十八日在这里闭幕。会议通过一项决议，要求阿拉伯国家不要同法国进行贸易，以支持阿尔及利亚人民争取独立的斗争。
    马来亚议员谴责政府迫害反对党
    吉隆坡讯　马来亚联合邦一些反对党议员二十日在下议院辩论内部安全部明年度预算时，谴责拉赫曼政府在十一月份滥用“内部安全法令”逮捕一批反对党党员、工会领袖、教师和学生。
    马来亚联合邦当局为了继续镇压马来亚民族解放运动、迫害反对党和人民，制订了所谓“内部安全法令”，根据这项新法令，政府可以下令拘留任何人两年，而不经公开审讯。
    沙特阿拉伯国王组成新阁
    麦加讯　据麦加电台二十一日广播，沙特阿拉伯国王沙特已经接受了首相费萨尔和他的内阁的辞职，并且组织了新的内阁。国王将亲自担任新内阁的首相。
    据外国通讯社报道，费萨尔首相同国王在国政上有分歧。
    马里总统去几内亚访问
    巴马科讯　马里共和国总统莫迪博·凯塔应几内亚总统塞古·杜尔的邀请二十三日乘专机离开这里前往科纳克里。陪同他前往的有马里国民议会副议长梅加等人。
    锡兰总理前往印度
    科伦坡讯　锡兰总理西丽玛沃·班达拉奈克夫人二十日乘专机前往印度作短期休假和朝圣。据报纸报道，在她出国期间，农业、土地、灌溉和电力部长查·帕·德席尔瓦将代理内阁首长的职务。
