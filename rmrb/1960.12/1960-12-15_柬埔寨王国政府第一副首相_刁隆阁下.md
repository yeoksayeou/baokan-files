Title: First Deputy Prime Minister of the Royal Government of Cambodia, Nhiek Tioulong

Author:

Date: 1960-12-15
Page: 5

Nhiek Tioulong, First Deputy Prime Minister of the Royal Government of Cambodia, was born in Phnom Penh on May 23, 1910. From 1931 to 1959, he successively held the positions of Deputy Provincial Governor, Deputy Secretary-General of the Prime Minister's Office, Provincial Governor, Mayor of Phnom Penh, Minister of Education, Minister of Finance, Royal Delegate of Battambang Province, the first representative of the Royal Government of Cambodia to the French High Commissioner in Saigon, Colonel of the Khmer Royal Army and Private Chief of Staff to the King, Minister of Public Works, Minister of Foreign Affairs, Minister of State for National Defence, Commander of the Khmer Royal Army and Chief of Staff of the Khmer Royal Army, Ambassador to Japan, Ambassador to the Soviet Union concurrently Envoy to Poland and Czechoslovakia, Minister of State for Interior, National Security and Information, and Deputy Prime Minister. During the struggle for the independence of the Kingdom of Cambodia, he accompanied Prince Norodom Sihanouk to talks with France as the Royal Delegate of the Ministry of Information. In 1954, he participated in the Geneva Conference on the issue of Indochina as the head of the Cambodian military delegation. Since April 1960, he has been the First Deputy Prime Minister concurrently Minister of Planning, Production, Public Works and Posts and Telecommunications, and Tourism. Deputy Prime Minister Nhiek Tioulong has made many commendable efforts to enhance the friendship between China and Cambodia.
(Xinhua News Agency)
