Title: Norstad Wants the North Atlantic Group to Accelerate Nuclear Armament; North Atlantic Group Parliamentary Conference Passes Resolution Advocating Further Military Expansion and War Preparations
Author:
Date: 1960-12-06
Page: 6
    Xinhua News Agency, Paris, December 1st. North Atlantic Group Armed Forces Commander, U.S. General Norstad, stated today at the Western European Union Parliamentary Conference that the group is accelerating the establishment of a mobile "strike force" equipped with atomic weapons under U.S. control.

    Norstad revealed that the plan to establish the "strike force" is "actively underway." This force, "composed of reinforced battalions provided by the group's 'four, five, or six Central European countries'," "can be deployed in a timely manner to deal with any emergency."

    He said this force would be coordinated with "battalions equipped with 'Honest John' and other types of nuclear missiles." The force also has air and naval units "to give the force great mobility and significant fire support."

    Norstad advocated that the North Atlantic Group should establish "a powerful deterrent." While emphasizing that the group's army should be equipped with nuclear weapons, he said that conventional weapons must also be expanded.

    Xinhua News Agency, Paris. The North Atlantic Military Group Parliamentary Conference, which closed here on November 26th, passed a resolution supporting the suggestion of the group's armed forces commander, U.S. General Norstad, regarding the establishment of the group's nuclear armed forces.

    The conference, which lasted a week, also passed other resolutions aimed at exacerbating international tensions and strengthening the North Atlantic Group's aggressive activities.

    The conference's resolution on military issues supports Norstad's plan to establish a North Atlantic Group nuclear strike force. It urges the group to accelerate the establishment of a "well-armed, mobile airlift force capable of launching attacks with nuclear or conventional weapons when the situation requires."

    The resolution also recommends realizing the "integration" of the North Atlantic Group's air defense, logistics, etc., and greater cooperation in research, development, and production of weapons.

    Before the conference passed the resolution, the group's Secretary-General, Belgian Spaak, who had just returned from the United States, delivered a closing speech. He advocated preparing for nuclear war and openly opposed "simply prohibiting atomic warfare."

### 诺斯塔德要北大西洋集团加紧核武装  北大西洋集团议员会议通过决议鼓吹进一步扩军备战

1960-12-06
第6版()
专栏：

    诺斯塔德要北大西洋集团加紧核武装
    北大西洋集团议员会议通过决议鼓吹进一步扩军备战
    据新华社巴黎1日电　北大西洋集团武装部队总司令、美国将军诺斯塔德今天在西欧联盟议员会议上表示，这个集团正在加紧建立在美国控制下的用原子武器装备的机动“突击部队”。
    诺斯塔德透露，建立“突击部队”的计划“正在积极进行”，这支由这个集团的“四个、五个或六个中欧国家提供的加强营所组成”的部队，“可以及时调动去对付任何紧急情况”。
    他说，这支部队将有“装备着‘诚实的约翰’式和其他类型的核导弹的营”配合。这支部队还有空军和海军部队，“以便使这支部队具有很大的机动性及重要的火力支持”。
    诺斯塔德鼓吹北大西洋集团要建立“强大的威慑力量”，他在强调这个集团军队要配备核武器的同时，说还必须扩充常规武器。
    据新华社巴黎电　11月26日在这里闭幕的北大西洋军事集团议员会议，通过决议支持这个集团的武装部队总司令、美国将军诺斯塔德关于建立这个集团核武装部队的建议。
    这次历时一周的会议，还通过了其他一些目的在于加剧国际紧张局势和加强北大西洋集团的侵略活动的决议。
    会议关于军事问题的决议支持诺斯塔德关于建立北大西洋集团核打击力量的计划，它敦促这个集团加速建立一支“武装优良的，具有在情况需要时能以核武器或常规武器出击的机动空运部队”。
    决议还建议实现北大西洋集团的空防、后勤等的“一体化”，和在研究、发展、生产武器等方面进行更大的合作。
    在会议通过决议之前，刚从美国回来的这个集团的秘书长比利时人斯巴克，作了闭幕演说，他鼓吹准备核战争，公开反对“单纯地禁止原子战争”。
