Title: World Peace Council Presidium Holds Meeting to Discuss Current International Situation and Work of Peace Forces; Passes Appeal to Peace Movements and Organizations and Statement on Situation in the Congo

Author: 
Date: 1960-12-03
Page: 5
Xinhua News Agency, Bucharest, December 2nd: The Presidium of the World Peace Council held a meeting in Bucharest on November 23rd and 24th. The meeting discussed the current international situation and the development of the work of peace forces around the world.

The press communiqué issued by the Presidium of the World Peace Council meeting stated that in order to help develop and expand the activities of peace movements in various countries and make them more effective, the Presidium of the World Peace Council has formulated a series of recommendations, particularly regarding mobilizing regional initiatives to promote the resolution of specific issues and help unite all peace forces.

The communiqué stated that the Presidium meeting adopted an appeal to all peace movements and organizations and a statement on the situation in the Congo.

The Presidium decided to hold a meeting of the World Peace Council in the first half of March 1961 in order to strengthen the mobilization of all peace forces.

The "Appeal to All Peace Movements and Organizations" adopted at the meeting stated that tension and danger loom over the world today, affecting the lives of all people and threatening their hopes for the future. The expansion of armaments is not only a danger and an obstacle to the resolution of international problems, but also a heavy economic burden on all countries, preventing wealth from being used for underdeveloped regions and the needs of universal social progress.

More and more people in all countries believe that complete disarmament is a matter of urgency. However, the arms race is accelerating. The main obstacle to disarmament is not technical, but the opposition of certain groups who have so far effectively prevented any progress in disarmament.

The appeal stated that our movement has always advocated disarmament, including the abolition of all nuclear weapons and other weapons of mass destruction. Other movements are also wholeheartedly making unremitting efforts for this. The time has indeed come when, no matter what differences we may have on other issues, we should all meet and discuss frankly and sincerely to try to eliminate misunderstandings and strive to work together to help governments reach agreements.

The statement on the situation in the Congo stated that once the Congo gained independence, the parliament established a government headed by Lumumba as Prime Minister.

The United Nations General Assembly's admission of President Kasavubu and the delegation he led, consisting of members of an illegal government established through violence, to occupy the UN seat is a serious violation of the sovereignty of the Congolese people. This decision has fueled a situation in which the Belgian, American, and British governments have been able to restore undisguised colonialism.

The statement said that the mistakes made by the United Nations representatives have created chaos in the Congo, have caused the deaths of thousands of Congolese people, and have endangered peace.

It must be ensured that the legally established government headed by Lumumba can exercise its functions and that the parliament can hold meetings freely.

The United Nations forces must no longer carry out the orders of imperialism. They must prevent Belgian military personnel from returning to the Congo under the guise of technical personnel and stop helping colonial agents to disintegrate the Republic of the Congo.

The Presidium of the World Peace Council decided to send this declaration to the governments of various countries and called on all movements for peace to support the Congolese people's courageous struggle for genuine independence—which is a guarantee of peace in Central Africa.

The Presidium of the World Peace Council also discussed the recommendations made by the National Peace Committees and decided to recommend that the following cultural figures be commemorated in 1961 on the anniversary of their birth or death:

　　Francis Bacon—British philosopher and politician. Commemorating the four hundredth anniversary of his birth (January 22, 1561).
　　Taras Shevchenko—Ukrainian poet. Commemorating the one hundredth anniversary of his death (March 10, 1861).
　　Heraclitus—Greek materialist philosopher. Commemorating the two thousand five hundredth anniversary of his birth.
  Heinrich von Kleist—German poet and playwright. Commemorating the one hundred and fiftieth anniversary of his death (November 21, 1811).
　　Franz Liszt—Hungarian composer. Commemorating the one hundred and fiftieth anniversary of his birth (July 31, 1811).
　　Mikhail Vasilyevich Lomonosov—Russian scientist. Commemorating the two hundred and fiftieth anniversary of his birth (November 20, 1711).
　　Fridtjof Nansen—Norwegian polar scientist. Commemorating the one hundredth anniversary of his birth (October 10, 1861).
　　Park In-ro—Korean poet. Commemorating the four hundredth anniversary of his birth (August 11, 1561).
　　Domingo Faustino Sarmiento—Argentine writer. Commemorating the one hundred and fiftieth anniversary of his birth (February 15, 1811).
　　Rabindranath Tagore—Indian poet and philosopher. Commemorating the one hundredth anniversary of his birth (May 5, 1861).
　　Abu Bakr Ibn Tufail—Arab philosopher, physician, mathematician, and writer. Commemorating the nine hundredth anniversary of his birth.

### 讨论目前国际局势和和平力量的工作  世界和平理事会主席团举行会议  通过给和平运动和组织的呼吁书和关于刚果局势的声明

1960-12-03
第5版()
专栏：

    讨论目前国际局势和和平力量的工作
    世界和平理事会主席团举行会议
    通过给和平运动和组织的呼吁书和关于刚果局势的声明
    新华社布加勒斯特2日电　世界和平理事会主席团11月23日和24日在布加勒斯特举行了会议。会议对目前的国际局势和全世界和平力量的工作的发展进行了讨论。
    世界和平理事会主席团会议发表新闻公报说，为了帮助发展和扩大各国和平运动的活动并使它们更为有效，世界和平理事会主席团已经拟订了一系列的建议，特别是关于调动地区积极性以促进解决具体问题并帮助团结一切和平力量的建议。
    公报说，主席团会议通过了给一切和平运动和组织的呼吁书和关于刚果局势的声明。
    主席团决定在1961年3月上半月举行世界和平理事会会议，以便加强动员一切和平力量。
    会议通过的“给一切和平运动和组织的呼吁书”中说，紧张局势和危险今天笼罩着世界，影响所有人的生活，威胁着他们对将来的希望。扩充军备不仅是一个危险，不仅妨碍国际问题的解决，而且是对一切国家的沉重经济负担，使财富不能用于经济不发达的地区和普遍社会进步的需要。
    世界各国愈来愈多的人民认为全面裁军是当务之急。但是，军备竞赛正在日益加速进行。裁军的主要障碍不是在技术方面，而是某些集团反对裁军，它们迄今在实际上使裁军不能取得任何进展。
    呼吁书说，我们的运动始终拥护裁军，包括废除一切核武器和其他大规模毁灭性武器。其他的运动也一心一意为此作出不懈的努力。这样的时刻的确已经到来：不管我们在其他问题上可能有怎样的不同意见，但是我们大家应该会晤和商谈来坦率和真诚地力求消除误解和设法协力促使各国政府获致协议。
    关于刚果局势的声明中说，刚果一经获得独立，议会就建立了由卢蒙巴担任总理的政府。
    联合国大会接纳卡萨武布总统和他所率领的由通过暴力建立的非法政府的成员组成的代表团占据联合国席位，是对刚果人民的主权的严重侵犯。这个决定助长了这样一种局面：使比利时、美国和英国政府得以恢复不加掩饰的殖民主义。
    声明说，联合国代表们所犯的错误在刚果造成混乱，已经使成千上万的刚果人致死并且危及和平。
    必须保证合法建立的以卢蒙巴为首的政府能行使职能并保证议会能自由举行会议。
    联合国部队必须不再执行帝国主义的命令。他们必须防止比利时军事人员在技术人员的伪装之下回到刚果去，并停止帮助殖民主义代理人瓦解刚果共和国。
    世界和平理事会主席团决定把这项宣言送交各国政府并且号召所有争取和平的运动支持刚果人民争取真正独立的勇敢斗争——这是中非和平的保证。
    世界和平理事会主席团还讨论了各国和平委员会提出的建议，并决定建议在1961年纪念下列文化名人的诞生或逝世：
    弗朗西斯·培根——英国哲学家和政治家。纪念他的诞生（1561年1月22日）四百周年。
    塔拉斯·谢甫琴柯——乌克兰诗人。纪念他的逝世（1861年3月10日）一百周年。
    赫拉克利图——希腊唯物主义哲学家。纪念他的诞生二千五百周年。
  海因里希·克莱斯特——德国诗人和戏剧家。纪念他的逝世（1811年11月21日）一百五十周年。
    弗兰茨·李斯特——匈牙利作曲家。纪念他的诞生（1811年7月31日）一百五十周年。
    米·瓦·罗蒙诺索夫——俄国科学家。纪念他的诞生（1711年11月20日）二百五十周年。
    弗里特约夫·南森——挪威极地科学家。纪念他的诞生（1861年10月10日）一百周年。
    朴仁老——朝鲜诗人。纪念他的诞生（1561年8月11日）四百周年。
    多明戈·福斯蒂诺·萨米恩托——阿根廷作家。纪念他的诞生（1811年2月15日）一百五十周年。
    拉宾德拉纳特·泰戈尔——印度诗人和哲学家。纪念他的诞生（1861年5月5日）一百周年。
    阿布巴克尔·伊本·图费勒——阿拉伯哲学家、医生、数学家和作家。纪念他的诞生九百周年。
