Title: Space Flight Opens Up New Horizons for Science
Author: Bulagolavov
Date: 1960-12-05
Page: 5

    Space Flight Opens Up New Horizons for Science
    Academician of the USSR Academy of Sciences, Bulagolavov
    The problem of conquering space has moved from the stage of theory and fantasy to practical experimentation since January 2, 1959. On this day, the Soviet Union successfully launched a space rocket to the moon. For the first time in human history, a flight device exceeding the second cosmic velocity was manufactured. It is understandable that every Soviet person rejoiced in this, because their country paved a road to the universe for mankind.
    The research work carried out with artificial satellites and space rockets enables us to practically solve the task of relaying radio waves to ultra-long ranges (i.e., hundreds of thousands and millions of kilometers). As the secrets of the universe are revealed, people can gain a deeper insight into climate change and turn meteorology into an increasingly accurate science. Needless to say, the practical significance of this work is understandable. Secondly, sending people into space has put the possibility of utilizing solar energy on the agenda. Furthermore, conquering space opens up vast horizons for the development of astronomical science and for the study of other life forms that do not exist on Earth.
    Conquering space can also open up new horizons for various other sciences. For example, our new and important information about the principles of various natural phenomena will become increasingly rich, the secrets of material changes and transformations will be opened up before us, and we will be able to study more deeply the situation where one type of energy can be transformed into another.
    The use of artificial satellites and rockets can help solve many scientific problems. The Earth's atmosphere is a serious obstacle to the further development of astronomical science because it hinders astronomical observations. The Earth's atmosphere refracts the light emitted to us by the stars and often swallows this light. Therefore, the stellar world we can observe from Earth is a distorted image. The situation is completely different when we observe stars from observatories outside the atmosphere.
    Space solar power stations are also a practical way to utilize the achievements of conquering space. The working efficiency of the solar cells on the Soviet Union's third artificial Earth satellite and the Soviet Union's third cosmic rocket is very good. In future space solar power stations, various tools will be used to convert the heat emitted by the sun into electrical energy. It is possible to find a way to transfer this energy obtained from space to Earth. Establishing meteorological stations in space is also an important task. Long-term climate observation has become the most important practical work of Soviet space meteorology.
    Radio electronics is also a science that has developed rapidly in the process of conquering space. Radio electronic devices are one of the most important methods for studying the universe. These radio devices can control rocket flight, broadcast scientific information, and manage the work of scientific equipment.
    Now, rockets can solve many important scientific tasks. At this stage, one of the important issues facing scientists studying the universe is the study of cosmic rays. The study of cosmic rays depends in many ways on further conquering space. So far, this research has been limited to the study of cosmic rays near the Earth. Therefore, a complete and correct concept of the nature and characteristics of nature and light cannot be produced. Instruments installed on rockets can enable us to study cosmic rays in interstellar space. Another very important task is to determine the concentration of gas in outer space.
    The study of meteor particles is also a major task, which has been solved by the launch of space rockets.
    The satellites and rockets launched by the Soviet Union in the past have made great achievements in this regard. Further research on the space near the sun, research on the planets of the solar system, and research on people flying to other planets are a series of problems that will continue to be solved in the century of conquering space. Judging from the speed of conquering space, the solution of all these problems will not be far away.

### 宇宙飞行开辟了科学的新天地
布拉戈拉沃夫
1960-12-05
第5版()
专栏：

    宇宙飞行开辟了科学的新天地
    苏联科学院院士  布拉戈拉沃夫
    征服宇宙的问题，从1959年1月2日起，就已经从理论和幻想的阶段走上实际实验的阶段。在这一天，苏联成功地发射了飞向月球的宇宙火箭。在人类历史上，第一次制造成超过第二宇宙速度的飞行装置。每一个苏联人为之欢欣鼓舞，是可以理解的。因为他们的国家为人类铺上了一条通向宇宙的道路。
    用人造卫星和宇宙火箭所进行的研究工作，使我们能够切实解决把无线电波转送到超远射程距离（即几十万和几百万公里）的任务。如人所揭示的宇宙秘密，能够使人更深刻地洞察气候的变化，能够把气象学变成一种越来越准确的科学。不用多说，这种工作的实际意义是可以理解的。其次，人飞到宇宙中去，使利用太阳能的可能性提上了议程。再有，征服宇宙，为发展天文学科学，为研究地球上所没有的其他生命，开辟了辽阔无际的天地。
    征服宇宙也能为其他各种科学开辟新的天地。比如说，我们关于各种大自然现象的原理的新的重要情报会越来越丰富，我们的面前将会打开物质变化和变换的秘密，我们能够更深入地研究一种能转变为另一种能的情况。
    利用人造卫星和火箭能够有助于解决许多科学问题。地球上的大气层是进一步发展天文学科学的一个严重障碍。因为它妨碍天文的观察。地球上的大气层折断了星球向我们发射的光线，并且常常吞食这些光线。因此，我们从地球上能够观察到的星球上的世界是一种歪曲了的形象。我们从大气层以外的观测台上来观测星球，情况就完全不同了。
    宇宙日光电力站，也是一种实际利用征服宇宙方面成就的方法。苏联的第三个人造地球卫星和苏联的第三个宇宙火箭上的太阳能电池的工作效能就是很好的。在未来的宇宙日光电力站上，将使用各种工具把太阳所发射的热变成电能。找到一种方法把这种从宇宙中得来的能转送到地球上，这是可能的。在宇宙间建立气象站也是一件重要的工作。气候的长期观测已成为苏联宇宙气象学最重要的实际工作。
    无线电电子学也是一种在征服宇宙进程中发展得异常迅速的科学。无线电电子装置，是研究宇宙最重要方法之一。这种无线电装置可以控制火箭飞行，可以播送科学情报，可以管理科学设备的工作。
    现在，用火箭可以解决许多重要的科学任务。在现阶段，摆在研究宇宙的科学家面前的重要问题之一，就是研究宇宙线。研究宇宙线在许多方面都要依赖于进一步征服宇宙。到目前为止，这种研究还仅限于对地球附近宇宙线的研究。因此，不能产生一种关于大自然和光线特性的完整的和正确的概念。火箭上装置的仪器，可以使我们研究星际空间的宇宙线。另一件非常重要的任务，就是确定宇宙空间瓦斯的浓度。
    对流星粒子的研究，也是一个重大的任务，这个任务由于宇宙火箭的发射已获得了解决。
    苏联过去几次发射的卫星和火箭在这方面已经取得了巨大的成绩。进一步研究太阳附近的空间、研究太阳系的行星、研究人飞上其他行星等一系列问题，将是征服宇宙世纪中继续要解决的问题，根据征服宇宙的速度来判断，所有这些问题的解决将为时不远了。
