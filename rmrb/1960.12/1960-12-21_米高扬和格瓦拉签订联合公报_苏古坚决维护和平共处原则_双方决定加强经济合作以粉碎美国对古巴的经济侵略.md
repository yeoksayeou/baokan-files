Title: Mikoyan and Guevara Sign Joint Communiqué; Soviet Union and Cuba Firmly Uphold the Principle of Peaceful Coexistence; Both Sides Decide to Strengthen Economic Cooperation to Crush U.S. Economic Aggression Against Cuba
Author:
Date: 1960-12-21
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, December 20th: TASS, Moscow, December 19th: The Soviet Union and Cuba declare that they are staunch defenders of peaceful coexistence between countries with different social systems and firmly support Khrushchev's disarmament proposals to the UN member states and proposals to adapt the structure of the United Nations to the new situation in the world. This is according to the Soviet-Cuban joint communiqué signed in Moscow on the 19th by Soviet Minister Council First Vice Chairman Mikoyan and the head of the Cuban government economic delegation, Guevara.

The communiqué points out that both sides have reached a complete agreement on economic issues. A protocol on the exchange of goods for 1961 was signed, stipulating a significant increase in the volume of trade between the two countries. The two sides also signed an agreement on the Soviet Union providing technical assistance to Cuba, as well as an agreement on Soviet assistance to Cuba in cultivating and training experts and a cultural cooperation agreement.

The communiqué points out that the Cuban government economic delegation believes that the most important form of assistance to Cuba is: "Soviet Premier Khrushchev's statement that the Soviet Union is determined to fully support Cuba in defending its independence from aggression."

The Soviet Union and Cuba condemn the imperialist militarist policies, the main bearers of which are the American monopoly groups. Both sides firmly support the anti-colonialist struggle of all peoples.

The communiqué supports the People's Republic of China and the Mongolian People's Republic's entry into the United Nations, and demands a speedy resolution of the Congo issue, requiring the United Nations to recognize the legitimate government of the country headed by Prime Minister Lumumba.

The communiqué points out that the two sides also discussed the problems facing the Cuban economy due to U.S. economic aggression. The Soviet Union agreed to take all measures it can to ensure the provision of items that are urgently important to the Cuban economy and that cannot be bought from other countries, and expressed its readiness to purchase 2.7 million tons of sugar from Cuba if the United States implements its threat not to buy Cuban sugar.

The communiqué emphasizes that the Cuban delegation confirms that the Soviet Union is moving towards communism at a firm pace. The Cuban delegation recognizes the sincere efforts made by the Soviet Union to achieve world peace.

Xinhua News Agency, December 20th: TASS, Moscow, December 20th: The economic delegation of the Cuban Republic government, headed by Guevara, left Moscow today for Prague.

### 米高扬和格瓦拉签订联合公报  苏古坚决维护和平共处原则  双方决定加强经济合作以粉碎美国对古巴的经济侵略

1960-12-21
第5版()
专栏：

    米高扬和格瓦拉签订联合公报
    苏古坚决维护和平共处原则
    双方决定加强经济合作以粉碎美国对古巴的经济侵略
    据新华社20日讯　塔斯社莫斯科19日电：苏联和古巴声明，它们是不同社会制度的国家和平共处的坚决维护者，并且坚决支持赫鲁晓夫向联合国会员国提出的裁军建议和关于使联合国结构适应于世界新形势的建议。苏联部长会议第一副主席米高扬和古巴政府经济代表团团长格瓦拉19日在莫斯科签订的苏联—古巴公报是这样说的。
    公报指出，双方就经济问题取得了完全的协议。签署了1961年换货议定书，规定大大增加两国的贸易额。双方还签署了一项关于苏联给予古巴以技术协助的协定，以及关于苏联援助古巴培养和训练专家的协定和文化合作协定。
    公报指出，古巴政府经济代表团认为，对古巴最重要的一种援助形式是：“苏联总理赫鲁晓夫声明苏联决心完全支持古巴保卫古巴独立不受侵略。”
    苏联和古巴谴责帝国主义的军国主义政策，这个政策的主要承担者是美国垄断集团。双方坚决支持各国人民的反殖民主义的斗争。
    公报支持中华人民共和国和蒙古人民共和国进入联合国，并要求尽速解决刚果问题，要求联合国承认以卢蒙巴总理为首的、这个国家的合法政府。
    公报指出，双方还讨论了由于美国的经济侵略而在古巴经济面前产生的问题。苏联同意采取一切它能做到的措施，来保证提供向其他国家买不到的、对古巴经济来说迫切重要的物品，并且表示准备一旦美国实行不买古巴糖的威胁，就向古巴购买二百七十万吨糖。
    公报着重指出，古巴代表团确认苏联正在以坚定的步伐向共产主义迈进。古巴代表团承认苏联为达到世界和平而进行的真诚的努力。
    据新华社20日讯　据塔斯社莫斯科20日讯：以格瓦拉为首的古巴共和国政府经济代表团今天离莫斯科前往布拉格。
