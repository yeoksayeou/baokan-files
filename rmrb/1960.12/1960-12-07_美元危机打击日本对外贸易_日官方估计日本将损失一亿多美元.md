Title: Dollar Crisis Hits Japan's Foreign Trade; Japanese Officials Estimate Losses of Over 100 Million US Dollars
Author:
Date: 1960-12-07
Page: 6

    Xinhua News Agency, Tokyo, 6th December: The new measures formally announced yesterday by the US government, prohibiting "recipient" countries from using US International Cooperation Administration "aid" funds to purchase goods from nineteen countries and regions, including Japan, have shocked the Japanese government and economic circles.
    A Japanese Ministry of Finance spokesman said today that this US measure will "severely affect Japan's foreign trade." He said that goods procured in Japan using US International Cooperation Administration "aid" funds amounted to over 100 million US dollars in the 1959 fiscal year, and 74 million US dollars in the first half of this year. These include machinery, chemical fertilizers, textiles, and metal products.
    The Japanese Ministry of International Trade and Industry expressed concern that Japan's balance of payments will worsen.
    It is reported that the figure of over 100 million US dollars that Japan will lose due to this new US step represents a quarter of the US International Cooperation Administration's "aid" funds used abroad. US officials also admit that Japan is "the hardest hit country."
    The *Asahi Shimbun* said, "Just as the Diet is holding a special session, the United States has taken this measure, which has confused the Japanese government."

### 美元危机打击日本对外贸易  日官方估计日本将损失一亿多美元

1960-12-07
第6版()
专栏：

    美元危机打击日本对外贸易
    日官方估计日本将损失一亿多美元
    据新华社6日讯　东京消息：美国政府昨天正式宣布禁止受“援”国用美国国际合作署“援助”资金购买包括日本在内的十九个国家和地区的货物的新措施引起了日本政府和经济界的震惊。
    日本大藏省发言人今天说，美国这个措施将“严重地影响日本的对外贸易”。他说，用美国国际合作署“援助”资金在日本采购的货物在1959财政年度达一亿多美元，今年上半年度为七千四百万美元。其中包括机械、化学肥料、纺织品和金属制品。
    日本通商产业省表示担心日本的国际收支将会恶化。
    据报道，日本由于美国这个新步骤而将损失的一亿多美元这个数字，是美国国际合作署用于国外的“援助”资金的四分之一。美国官员也承认日本是遭受“打击最大的一个国家”。
    《朝日新闻》说，“正当国会召开特别会议时，美国采取这种措施，使日本政府感到困惑”。
