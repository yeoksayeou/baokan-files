Title: U.S. Steps Up Development of Spy Satellites; "Newsweek" Says It Will Photograph Soviet Union From Satellites
Author:
Date: 1960-12-08
Page: 7

Xinhua News Agency, New York, 6th: The American magazine *Newsweek* revealed yesterday that the U.S. Air Force is intensifying its efforts to develop new spy satellites to steal Soviet military intelligence.

*Newsweek*, in a report datelined from California on the 5th, said that the U.S. Air Force "is going to photograph Russia from a satellite, and the communists will not know when it is doing it." It also said that "this satellite, included in a highly secret plan, is not the heavily publicized 'Samos' spy satellite already in space."

It is also reported that the U.S. Democratic Party's Vice President-elect, Senator Johnson, recently strongly advocated in the Senate that the U.S. should accelerate the development of so-called "communication satellites," i.e., spy satellites.

### 美国加紧发展间谍卫星  《新闻周刊》说要从卫星上拍摄苏联照片

1960-12-08
第7版()
专栏：

    美国加紧发展间谍卫星
    《新闻周刊》说要从卫星上拍摄苏联照片
    新华社6日讯　纽约消息：美国《新闻周刊》昨天透露，美国空军正加紧努力发展新的间谍卫星以窃取苏联军事情报。
    《新闻周刊》5日在从美国加利福尼亚州发出的一篇报道中说，美国空军“将要从一个人造卫星上拍摄俄国的情况，共产党人将不会了解它在什么时候进行”。它还说，“包括在一项十分秘密的计划中的这个人造卫星不是已大量宣传的已在空间的‘萨姆斯’间谍卫星”。
    另据报道，美国民主党当选副总统、参议员约翰逊最近在参议院里也大力鼓吹要加速发展美国的所谓“通讯卫星”，即间谍卫星。
