International Briefs

    Saudi Arabia's New Cabinet Sworn In
    Mecca Dispatch: According to a broadcast from Mecca Radio on the 25th, Saudi Arabia's new cabinet, headed by King Saud in his capacity as Prime Minister, was sworn in on the 24th and held its first cabinet meeting. King Saud, in a policy statement issued that day, stated that the new government would delegate broad powers to local administrative bodies and announced that in foreign affairs, it would pursue a policy based on neutrality.

    Morocco Approves Draft of Next Year's General Budget
    Rabat Dispatch: The Moroccan cabinet, at a meeting presided over by the King on the 22nd, approved the draft of the 1961 general budget. The total budget expenditure is 1,615,492,303 dirhams, an increase of 8.91 percent compared to 1960.

    Chilean Government Forced to Agree to Increase Workers' Wages
    Santiago Dispatch: Faced with the courageous and persistent struggle of Chilean workers, the Chilean government was forced on the 21st to announce a law stipulating a 15 percent increase in salaries and wages for employees and workers.
    The Unified Workers' Union of Chile pointed out that the 15 percent wage increase promised by the government is still not enough to offset the rise in the cost of living.

    Malaya and West Germany Sign "Investment Guarantee Agreement"
    Kuala Lumpur Dispatch: Representatives of the Federation of Malaya government and the West German government signed an "Investment Guarantee Agreement" in Kuala Lumpur on the 22nd, guaranteeing that West German investments in Malaya will not be threatened by "nationalization," and West German investors are also allowed to transfer or repatriate their investments.

    Price Hikes in Greece
    Athens Dispatch: According to the "Dawn Daily" newspaper, the prices of many foods in Greece have risen by 20 to 30 percent this year. According to official data, the overall price index rose by 5.8 percent in the first ten months of this year alone.

### 国际简讯

1960-12-28
第6版()
专栏：国际简讯

    国际简讯
    沙特阿拉伯新内阁宣誓就职
    麦加讯　据麦加电台二十五日广播，由国王沙特兼任首相的沙特阿拉伯新内阁在二十四日宣誓就职，并举行了第一次内阁会议。国王沙特在当天发表的一项政策声明中表示，新政府将把广泛的权力交给地方行政机构，并且宣布在外交方面将奉行以中立为基础的政策。
    摩洛哥通过明年总预算草案
    拉巴特讯摩洛哥内阁二十二日在国王主持下举行的会议上通过了一九六一年总预算草案。预算开支总额为一，六一五，四九二，三○三迪拉姆。同一九六○年比较，增加了百分之八·九一。
    智利政府被迫答应增加职工工资
    圣地亚哥讯在智利工人坚持英勇斗争的情况下，智利政府被迫在二十一日宣布一项规定为职员和工人增加百分之十五的薪金和工资的法律。
    智利工人统一工会指出，政府答应增加的百分之十五的工资还不能抵消生活费用的上涨。
  马来亚西德签订“投资保障协定”
    吉隆坡讯马来亚联合邦政府和西德政府代表团二十二日在吉隆坡签订了一项“投资保障协定”，保证在马来亚的西德投资将不受“国有化”的威胁，西德投资者还被允许把投资转让或汇回本国。
    希腊物价上涨
    雅典讯据“黎明日报”报道，希腊今年许多食品价格上涨了百分之二十到百分之三十。据官方资料，物价总指数仅在今年前十一个月就上涨了百分之五点八。
