Title: Indian Prime Minister Nehru Arrives in Beijing at the Invitation of the Chinese Government, Receiving a Warm Welcome from Over 200,000 People from All Walks of Life in the Capital
Author:
Date: 1954-10-20
Page: 1

Xinhua News Agency, October 19th—At the invitation of the Government of the People's Republic of China, the Prime Minister of the Republic of India, Nehru, arrived in Beijing by plane at 12:20 PM on the 19th, accompanied by Wang Bingnan, Director of the General Office of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of China, Yuan Zhongxian, the Chinese Ambassador to India, and Raghunath, the Indian Ambassador to China. Accompanying Prime Minister Nehru on his visit to China were his daughter, Mrs. Indira Gandhi, the Secretary-General of the Indian Ministry of External Affairs, N.R. Pillai, and the Deputy Secretary of the Indian Ministry of External Affairs, Bahadur Singh, along with nine others including M.L. Gaitonde, K.F. Rustamji, and N.K. Seshan.

Those who greeted him at the airport included: Premier of the State Council of the People's Republic of China, Zhou Enlai, Vice Chairman of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress, Soong Ching Ling, Vice Premiers of the State Council, Chen Yun, Peng Dehuai, He Long, Ulanhu, Li Fuchun, and Li Xiannian, Vice Chairman of the National Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference, Guo Moruo, Secretary-General of the Central Committee of the Communist Party of China, Deng Xiaoping, Chairman of the Revolutionary Committee of the Chinese Kuomintang, Li Jishen, Vice Chairman of the China Democratic League, Zhang Bojun, Chairman of the All-China Federation of Industry and Commerce, Huang Yanpei, Chairman of the All-China Federation of Industry and Commerce, Chen Shutong, prominent overseas Chinese leader, Chen Jiageng, Mayor of Beijing, Peng Zhen, Commander of the Beijing-Tianjin Garrison, Nie Rongzhen, Vice Minister of Foreign Affairs, Zhang Hanfu and his wife.

Also present at the airport were: Secretary-General of the State Council, Xi Zhongxun, Ministers and Chairmen of various ministries and commissions, Luo Ruiqing, Shi Liang, Bo Yibo, Zhang Naiqi, Ye Jizhuang, Wang Heshou, Huang Jing, Chen Yu, Jiang Guangnai, Jia Tuofu, Zhu Xuefan, Liao Luyan, Liang Xi, Fu Zuoyi, Shen Yanbing, Yang Xiufeng, Zhang Xiruo, and Li Dequan, Vice Ministers of various ministries and Vice Chairmen of various commissions, Lei Renmin, Lü Zhengcao, Wang Shoudao, Zheng Zhenduo, Cai Tingkai, Liu Geping, and Li Renren, as well as Deputy Director of the Foreign Policy Committee of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, Qiao Guanhua, Deputy Director of the General Office, Yan Baohang, Director of the Asian Affairs Department, Chen Jiakang, Director of the Information Department, Gong Peng, and Director of the Protocol Department, Wang Zhuoru.

Also present at the airport were: Deputy Chief of General Staff of the Chinese People's Liberation Army, Su Yu, Deputy Director of the General Political Department, Xiao Hua, Commander of the Navy, Xiao Jingguang, and Commander of the Air Force, Liu Yalou.

Also present at the airport were: Vice Mayors of Beijing, Zhang Youyu and Wu Han, and Vice Chairman of the Beijing Municipal People's Representative Conference Consultative Committee, Liu Ren.

Also present at the airport were: Standing Committee Members of the National Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference, Ma Yinchu, Shao Lizi, and Zhang Zhizhong, Central Standing Committee Members of the Revolutionary Committee of the Chinese Kuomintang, Cheng Qian and Central Committee Member Long Yun, Vice Chairman of the China Democratic League, Luo Longji, Vice Chairman of the All-China Federation of Industry and Commerce, Li Zhuchen, Vice Chairman of the China Association for Promoting Democracy, Wang Shao'ao, Standing Council Member of the Central Committee, Zhou Jianren, Vice Chairman of the Chinese Peasants' and Workers' Democratic Party, Peng Zemin, Chairman of the China Zhi Gong Party, Chen Qiyou, Chairman of the Jiusan Society, Xu Deheng, Vice Chairman of the Taiwan Democratic Self-Government League, Li Chunqing, and Secretary of the Central Secretariat of the Chinese New Democratic Youth League, Hu Yaobang.

Also present at the airport were: President of the Sino-Indian Friendship Association, Ding Xilin, Vice President, Chen Hansheng, Chairman of the All-China Federation of Trade Unions, Lai Ruoyu, Vice Chairman, Liu Ningyi, Vice Chairman of the All-China Democratic Women's Federation, Xu Guangping, Chairman of the All-China Youth Federation, Liao Chengzhi, Chairman of the All-China Students' Federation, Tian Demin, Secretary-General of the China Federation of Literary and Art Circles, Yang Hansheng, Vice President of the Chinese People's Institute of Foreign Affairs, Zhou Gengsheng, Qian Duansheng, President of the Chinese People's Association for Cultural Relations with Foreign Countries, Chu Tunan, Chairman of the Three-Self Patriotic Movement Committee of the Protestant Churches of China, Wu Yaozong, Vice President of the Buddhist Association of China, Zhao Puchu, as well as Tibetan figures in Beijing, Chi-jiang Losang Yexe, Karmapa Living Buddha, Sonam Wangchen Gellek, Ngapoi Ngawang Jigme, Gyitsang Nima, Yao Hsi-Tering Droma, Liu Xia Tuten Taba, Ngawang Jinba, famous figures in Chinese Buddhist circles, Ju Zan, and members of the Sino-Indian Friendship Association delegation who had visited India.

Also present at the airport to welcome him were the Vice Chairman of the All-India Peace Council, Academician of the Indian Academy of Sciences, and Member of the Indian Parliament, Sahib Singh Sokhey, the Mayor of Calcutta, India, Mr. Mukherjee, members of the friendly delegation of the All-India Trade Union Congress, as well as personnel from the Indian Embassy in China and Indian nationals.

The foreign envoys and diplomatic personnel of various countries stationed in China who came to the airport to welcome him were: Ambassador of Burma, U Hla Maung, Ambassador of Pakistan, Raza, Ambassador of Czechoslovakia, Kansala, Ambassador of Poland, Kirylok, Ambassador of the Democratic Republic of Vietnam, Hoang Van Hoan, Ambassador of Mongolia, Ochirbat, Ambassador of Indonesia, Mononutu, Ambassador of the German Democratic Republic, König, Ambassador of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea, Choi Il, Ambassador of the Soviet Union, Yudin, Ambassador of Bulgaria, Dimov, Ambassador of Albania, Nase, Ambassador of Hungary, Siklós, Minister of Finland, Sundström, Minister of Denmark, Graae, Minister of Switzerland, Béguin, Chargé d'Affaires of the United Kingdom, Du Vivier, Interim Chargé d'Affaires of Norway, Gimodim, Interim Chargé d'Affaires of Sweden, Hammarskjöld, Interim Chargé d'Affaires of Romania, Mentianu, Negotiating Representative of the Netherlands, Smits, as well as embassy personnel from various countries.

Also present at the airport to welcome him were more than 10,000 people from all walks of life in the capital.

Indian and Chinese national flags were hung at the airport.

After Prime Minister Nehru disembarked from the plane, he shook hands cordially with Premier Zhou Enlai and other welcomers. The band played the national anthems of India and China. Then, Prime Minister Nehru reviewed the guard of honor and accepted flowers presented by children of the capital.

Prime Minister Nehru delivered a speech at the airport (full text to be published separately), and then walked to the front of the welcoming crowd at the airport and raised his hand to greet them. At this time, the welcoming crowd continuously applauded and shouted slogans such as "Welcome Prime Minister Nehru!" "Long live Sino-Indian friendship!" "Long live Asian peace!" "Long live world peace!", and presented flowers to Prime Minister Nehru.

As Prime Minister Nehru, accompanied by Premier Zhou Enlai, traveled in an open-top car from the airport to the guesthouse, passing through the suburban roads and streets of the city, he was warmly welcomed by over 200,000 workers, citizens, office staff, youth, students, and children of the capital along the way.

Xinhua News Agency, Wuhan, October 19th—The Prime Minister of the Republic of India, Nehru, accompanied by Wang Bingnan, Director of the General Office of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of China, and Yuan Zhongxian, the Chinese Ambassador to India, left Wuhan for Beijing by plane at 8:17 AM on the 19th.

Accompanying Prime Minister Nehru on his departure to Beijing were Prime Minister Nehru's daughter, Mrs. Indira Gandhi, and the Secretary-General of the Indian Ministry of External Affairs, N.R. Pillai, and the Deputy Secretary of the Indian Ministry of External Affairs, Bahadur Singh, and nine others.

The Indian Ambassador to China, Raghunath, also accompanied Prime Minister Nehru on his departure from Wuhan to Beijing.

Those who saw him off at the airport were Liu Zihou, Chairman of the People's Government of Hubei Province, Vice Chairmen, Nie Guoqing, Wang Haishan, Acting Mayor of Wuhan, Wang Renzhong, Vice Mayors, Song Kanfu, Chen Jingshao, Political Commissar of the Wuhan Office of the Central and Southern Military Region of the Chinese People's Liberation Army, Pan Zhenwu, Deputy Chief of Staff of the Hubei Military Region, Xu Jie, as well as responsible persons from various departments of the People's Government of Hubei Province and the People's Government of Wuhan City.

Also present at the airport to see him off were Li Erzong, Chairman of the Wuhan Branch of the Chinese People's Committee for World Peace, Li Da, President of Wuhan University, Zhao Min, Chairman of the Wuhan Federation of Trade Unions, Xin Fu, Secretary of the Wuhan Committee of the Chinese New Democratic Youth League, Xiao Huina, Li Dongqing, Deputy Directors of the Wuhan Democratic Women's Federation, Tao Shuzeng, representative of the scientific and technological community, Yu Heiding, Chairman of the Wuhan Branch of the Chinese Writers Association, as well as representatives of various democratic parties' Hubei Province and Wuhan City organizations, various people's organizations, and representatives from all walks of life.

### 应我国政府邀请来我国访问  印度总理尼赫鲁到北京  受到首都各界二十多万人的热烈欢迎

1954-10-20
第1版()
专栏：

    应我国政府邀请来我国访问
    印度总理尼赫鲁到北京
    受到首都各界二十多万人的热烈欢迎
    【新华社十九日讯】应中华人民共和国政府邀请来我国访问的印度共和国总理尼赫鲁在我国外交部办公厅主任王炳南、我国驻印度大使袁仲贤和印度驻我国大使赖嘉文的陪同下，于十九日十二时二十分乘飞机到达北京。随同尼赫鲁总理访问我国的，有他的女儿英迪拉·甘地夫人、印度外交部秘书长纳·鲁·皮莱和印度外交部副秘书巴哈杜尔·辛格以及墨·勒·盖思德、克·弗·鲁斯滕吉、纳·克·塞善等九人。

    在机场上欢迎的还有首都各界群众一万多人。
    机场上悬挂着印度和中国国旗。
    尼赫鲁总理下机后，和周恩来总理以及其他欢迎人员亲切握手。乐队高奏印度和中国国歌。接着，尼赫鲁总理检阅了仪仗队，并接受了首都儿童的献花。
    尼赫鲁总理在机场上发表了谈话（全文另发），然后，走到在机场上欢迎的群众行列的前面，向欢迎者举手致意。这时欢迎人群不断鼓掌和高呼“欢迎尼赫鲁总理！”“印中友好万岁！”“亚洲和平万岁！”“世界和平万岁！”等口号，并向尼赫鲁总理献花。
    当尼赫鲁总理由周恩来总理陪同乘敞篷汽车由机场前往迎宾馆经过市郊公路和市内街道时，受到沿途二十余万首都工人、市民、机关工作人员、青年学生和儿童们的热烈欢迎。
    【新华社武汉十九日电】印度共和国总理尼赫鲁由我国外交部办公厅主任王炳南和我国驻印度大使袁仲贤陪同，在十九日上午八时十七分乘飞机离武汉赴北京。
    随同尼赫鲁总理启程赴北京的，有尼赫鲁总理的女儿英迪拉·甘地夫人，和印度外交部秘书长纳·鲁·皮莱、印度外交部副秘书巴哈杜尔·辛格等九人。
    印度驻我国大使赖嘉文也陪同尼赫鲁总理由武汉启程赴北京。
    到机场欢送的，有湖北省人民政府主席刘子厚，副主席聂国青、王海山，武汉市人民政府代市长王任重，副市长宋侃夫、陈经畬，中国人民解放军中南军区驻武汉办事处政治委员潘振武，湖北军区副参谋长徐捷，以及湖北省人民政府和武汉市人民政府各部门的负责人。
    到机场欢送的，还有中国人民保卫世界和平委员会武汉市分会主席李尔重，武汉大学校长李达，武汉市工会联合会主席赵敏，中国新民主主义青年团武汉市委员会书记辛甫，武汉市民主妇女联合会副主任萧慧纳、李冬青，科学技术界代表陶述曾，中国作家协会武汉分会主席于黑丁，以及各民主党派湖北省和武汉市组织的代表，各人民团体和各界人民代表。
