Title: The First Soviet Ambassador to the Democratic Republic of Vietnam Arrives in Hanoi
Author:
Date: 1954-10-30
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, Hanoi, October 29th: Alexander Lavrishchev, the first Soviet Ambassador Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary to the Democratic Republic of Vietnam, and embassy staff arrived in Hanoi at approximately 4:00 PM (local time) on the 29th.

At Gia Lam Airport to welcome the Soviet Ambassador were: Pham Van Dong, Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Foreign Affairs of the Democratic Republic of Vietnam; Truong Chinh, General Secretary of the Vietnam Workers' Party; ministers of the Vietnamese government; representatives of the National Assembly, the Vietnam National United Front, and various people's organizations; heads of the Vietnam-Soviet Friendship Association, the Vietnam-China Friendship Association, the Vietnam Committee for the Defense of World Peace, the Hanoi Military and Administrative Committee, and people's organizations; as well as representatives of the citizens of Hanoi and Gia Lam, totaling more than 300 people. Luo Guibo, the Chinese Ambassador to the Democratic Republic of Vietnam, Jerzy Ogródziński, the chief representative of the Polish delegation to the International Supervisory and Control Commission in Vietnam, and the Polish political advisor also went to welcome them.

In his welcoming speech, Pham Van Dong said: The appointment of an ambassador by the Soviet Union to the Democratic Republic of Vietnam is of great significance. It proves that the friendship and brotherly relations between our two peoples will be increasingly consolidated and developed, and will contribute to the restoration and upholding of peace in Vietnam and Indochina, and to the consolidation of peace in Southeast Asia and the world. The Vietnamese people believe that they will always receive the sympathy and support of the great Soviet people in their struggle to consolidate peace, achieve unification, independence, and democracy.

In his reply, Lavrishchev said: I and the staff of the Soviet Embassy are extremely excited to undertake the task of consolidating and developing the friendship between the various nationalities of the Soviet Union and the heroic and hard-working Vietnamese nation. The development of friendship between the Soviet and Vietnamese peoples is in the interests of both nations and will contribute to the consolidation of peace. The Soviet people, as well as all peace-loving nations, deeply sympathize with the Vietnamese people's struggle for their legitimate national interests. We, along with the entire Soviet people, wish the Vietnamese people even greater achievements in the cause of peaceful construction and the struggle for unification, independence, and democracy.

When the Soviet Ambassador to the Democratic Republic of Vietnam, Alexander Lavrishchev, and the embassy staff arrived at Muk Nam Quan and Lang Son on the Sino-Vietnamese border, they were warmly welcomed by the residents along the way and by representatives of the government of the Democratic Republic of Vietnam and representatives of the Vietnam Workers' Party.

### 苏联首任驻越南民主共和国大使到达河内

1954-10-30
第4版()
专栏：

    苏联首任驻越南民主共和国大使到达河内
    【新华社河内二十九日电】苏联驻越南民主共和国首任特命全权大使亚历山大·拉夫里谢夫和使馆人员已在二十九日下午四时许（当地时间）到达河内。
    到嘉林欢迎苏联大使的有：越南民主共和国副总理兼外交部部长范文同，越南劳动党总书记长征，越南政府的部长们，国民大会、越南国民联合战线和各人民团体的代表们，越苏友好协会、越中友好协会、越南保卫世界和平委员会、河内军政委员会及人民团体的负责人以及河内、嘉林市民代表等共三百多人。我国驻越南民主共和国大使罗贵波，越南国际监督和监察委员会的波兰代表团首席代表奥格罗京斯基及波兰政治顾问也前往欢迎。
    范文同致欢迎词说：苏联派大使驻越南民主共和国具有重大的意义，这证明我们两国人民间的友谊和兄弟般的关系将日益巩固和发展，并将对恢复和坚持越南及印度支那的和平、巩固东南亚及世界的和平作出贡献。越南人民相信在自己的巩固和平、实现统一、独立与民主的斗争中将经常得到伟大苏联人民的同情和支持。
    拉夫里谢夫致答词说：我和苏联大使馆人员怀着莫大的兴奋来承担巩固和发展苏联各民族与英勇而勤劳的越南民族之间的友谊的任务。苏联和越南两民族间的友谊的发展符合于两国民族的利益，并将对和平的巩固作出贡献。苏联人民以及一切爱好和平的民族都深深地同情越南人民争取民族正当利益的斗争。我们和全体苏联人民一起敬祝越南人民在和平建设和争取统一、独立、民主的事业中取得更多的成绩。
    当苏联驻越南民主共和国大使亚历山大·拉夫里谢夫和使馆人员一起抵达中越边境的睦南关和谅山时，受到沿途居民和越南民主共和国政府代表、越南劳动党代表的热烈欢迎。
