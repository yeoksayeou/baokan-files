Title: Indian Prime Minister Nehru Leaves Beijing to Visit East China, South China and Other Places Before Returning Home
Author:
Date: 1954-10-28
Page: 1
Xinhua News Agency, October 27th: Indian Prime Minister Jawaharlal Nehru, who was invited by the Government of the People's Republic of China to visit our country, left Beijing by plane at 8:00 a.m. on the 27th and will visit East China, South China, and other places before returning home. Mrs. Indira Gandhi, N.R. Pillai, Bahadur Singh, M.L. Gaind, K.F. Rustamji, and N.K. Seshan, who accompanied Prime Minister Nehru on his visit to China, also left Beijing on the same day. Accompanying Prime Minister Nehru on the plane were Indian Ambassador to China, R.K. Nehru, Director of the General Office of the Chinese Ministry of Foreign Affairs, Wang Bingnan, and Chinese Ambassador to India, Yuan Zhongxian.

Those who saw him off at the airport included: Premier of the State Council of the People's Republic of China, Zhou Enlai; Vice Premiers Chen Yun, Peng Dehuai, Deng Zihui, He Long, Ulanhu, Li Fuchun, and Li Xiannian; Vice Chairpersons of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress, Soong Ching Ling and the Dalai Lama Tenzin Gyatso; Committee Member of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress, Panchen Erdeni Chökyi Gyaltsen; Vice Chairman of the National Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference, Guo Moruo; Secretary-General of the Central Committee of the Communist Party of China, Deng Xiaoping; Chairman of the Revolutionary Committee of the Chinese Kuomintang, Li Jishen; Vice Chairman of the China Democratic League, Luo Longji; Chairman of the All-China Federation of Industry and Commerce, Huang Yanpei; Chairman of the All-China Federation of Industry and Commerce, Chen Shutong; Mayor of Beijing, Peng Zhen; Commander of the Beijing-Tianjin Garrison, Nie Rongzhen; and Vice Minister of Foreign Affairs, Zhang Hanfu and his wife.

Also present at the airport were: Secretary-General of the State Council, Xi Zhongxun; Ministers and Directors of various ministries and commissions Luo Ruiqing, Shi Liang, Bo Yibo, Zhang Naiqi, Ye Jizhuang, Wang Heshou, Huang Jing, Chen Yu, Jiang Guangnai, Jia Tuofu, Teng Daiyuan, Zhu Xuefan, Liao Luyan, Liang Xi, Fu Zuoyi, Shen Yanbing, Yang Xiufeng, Zhang Xiruo; Vice Ministers of various ministries and Vice Directors of various commissions Lei Renmin, Wang Shoudao, Zheng Zhenduo, He Cheng, Liu Geping, Li Renren, as well as Deputy Director of the General Office of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, Yan Baohang, Director of the Asian Department, Chen Jiakang, and Director of the Protocol Department, Wang Zhuoru.

Also present at the airport to see him off were: Members of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress Situ Meitang, Wu Yuzhang, Li Shucheng, Zhou Jianren, Shao Lizi, Xu Teli, Wei Guoqing, Zhang Zhizhong, Zhang Nanxian, Zhang Su, Chen Shaoxian, Cheng Zihua, Huang Shaohong, Liu Ningyi, Liu Lantao, and Long Yun.

Also present at the airport to see him off were: Deputy Chief of the General Staff of the Chinese People's Liberation Army Su Yu, Huang Kecheng, Deputy Director of the General Political Department Xiao Hua, Commander of the Navy Xiao Jingguang, and Commander of the Air Force Liu Yalou.

Also present at the airport to see him off were: Vice Mayors of Beijing Zhang Youyu and Wu Han, Vice Chairman of the Beijing Municipal Consultative Committee Liu Ren, Liang Sicheng, and members of the Beijing Municipal People's Government Committee.

Also present at the airport to see him off were: Standing Committee Member of the National Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference Ma Yinchu, Central Standing Committee Member of the Revolutionary Committee of the Chinese Kuomintang Wang Kunlun, Vice Chairman of the China Democratic League Gao Chongmin, Vice Chairman of the All-China Federation of Industry and Commerce Li Zhuchen, Vice Chairman of the China Association for Promoting Democracy Wang Shao'ao, Vice Chairman of the Chinese Peasants' and Workers' Democratic Party Peng Zemin, Chairman of the China Zhi Gong Party Chen Qiyou, Chairman of the Jiusan Society Xu Deheng, Vice Chairman of the Taiwan Democratic Self-Government League Li Chunqing, and Secretary of the Central Secretariat of the Chinese New Democratic Youth League Hu Yaobang.

Also present at the airport to see him off were: President of the Sino-Indian Friendship Association Ding Xilin, Vice President Chen Hansheng, Chairman of the All-China Federation of Trade Unions Lai Ruoyu, Vice President of the All-China Democratic Women's Federation Xu Guangping, Vice President of the All-China Youth Federation Liu Daosheng, Chairman of the All-China Students' Federation Tian Demin, Secretary-General of the China Federation of Literary and Art Circles Yang Hansheng, Vice President of the Chinese People's Institute of Foreign Affairs Zhou Gengsheng and Qian Duansheng, President of the Chinese People's Association for Cultural Relations with Foreign Countries Chu Tunan, Vice President of the Buddhist Association of China Zhao Puchu, as well as Tibetan figures in Beijing Chijiang Lozang Yexei, Karmapa Living Buddha, Sokang Wangqing Gelek, Ngapoi Ngawang Jigme, Jigme, Yoshei Zeren Drolma, Lyushar Thupten Thabkhe, Ngawang Jinba, prominent figures in China's Buddhist community Jushan, and members of the Sino-Indian Friendship Association delegation who had visited India.

The foreign envoys and diplomatic personnel from various countries to China who came to see him off at the airport were: Burmese Ambassador U Hla Maw, Pakistani Ambassador Raza, Czechoslovak Ambassador Cankar, Polish Ambassador Kirylok, Ambassador of the Democratic Republic of Vietnam Hoang Van Hoan, Mongolian Ambassador Ochirbat, Indonesian Ambassador Mononutu, Ambassador of the German Democratic Republic Koenig, Soviet Ambassador Yudin, Bulgarian Ambassador Dimov, Hungarian Ambassador Siklós, Finnish Minister Sundström, Danish Minister Graeger, Swiss Minister Béguin, British Chargé d'Affaires Tuvilliam, Norwegian Chargé d'Affaires Gimode, Swedish Chargé d'Affaires Hammarskjöld, Romanian Chargé d'Affaires Muntanu, Chargé d'Affaires of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea Li Zhongyi, Albanian Chargé d'Affaires Jasha, Dutch Negotiating Representative Sman, and personnel from various embassies.

Also present at the airport to see him off were personnel from the Indian Embassy in China.

The flags of India and China were hung at the airport. Prime Minister Nehru, accompanied by Premier Zhou Enlai and others, entered the airport, and the band played the national anthems of India and China.

Prime Minister Nehru reviewed the guard of honor and received flowers from the children of Beijing.

Prime Minister Nehru delivered a speech at the airport. He said:

Friends, I take the liberty of calling you that, because in the past week I have made many friends here. So I think I can call you that.

I am about to leave now. I am filled with memories and gratitude for your hospitality and enthusiasm. I will remember it forever, and I believe this is not a personal matter, but a closer relationship between two great countries, and for this reason, I welcome it even more. I believe the people of India are very grateful to you for the honor you have given to me as their representative. I am very grateful to the Chinese government and people, and I would like to express my gratitude to them once again.

### 印度总理尼赫鲁离开北京将在华东、华南等地参观后回国

1954-10-28
第1版()
专栏：

    印度总理尼赫鲁离开北京将在华东、华南等地参观后回国
    【新华社二十七日讯】应中华人民共和国政府邀请来我国访问的印度共和国总理尼赫鲁在二十七日上午八时乘飞机离北京，将在华东、华南等地参观后返国。随同尼赫鲁总理访问我国的英迪拉·甘地夫人、纳·鲁·皮莱、巴哈杜尔·辛格、墨·勒·盖恩德、克·弗·鲁斯滕吉、纳·克·塞善等九人同日飞离北京。同机陪送尼赫鲁总理的有印度驻我国大使赖嘉文和我国外交部办公厅主任王炳南、我国驻印度大使袁仲贤。
