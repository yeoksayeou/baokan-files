Title: Soviet Union Appoints Delegation to European Conference; Polish and German Democratic Republic Delegations Arrive in Moscow
Author:
Date: 1954-11-29
Page: 1

Xinhua News Agency, Moscow, November 28th: TASS Moscow, November 27th: The Soviet Union's Council of Ministers has appointed the Soviet delegation to participate in the European conference discussing the guarantee of peace and security in Europe.

The delegation members are: First Deputy Chairman of the Council of Ministers of the Soviet Union and Minister of Foreign Affairs of the Soviet Union, V.M. Molotov (head of the delegation), First Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of the Soviet Union, A.A. Gromyko, Chairman of the Council of Ministers of the Russian Soviet Federative Socialist Republic, Ya.M. Puzanov, Chairman of the Council of Ministers of the Ukrainian Soviet Socialist Republic, N.T. Kalchenko, Chairman of the Council of Ministers of the Belorussian Soviet Socialist Republic, K.T. Mazurov, Chairman of the Council of Ministers of the Latvian Soviet Socialist Republic, V.D. Latsis, Chairman of the Council of Ministers of the Estonian Soviet Socialist Republic, A.Ya. Müürisepp, Chairman of the Council of Ministers of the Lithuanian Soviet Socialist Republic, M.A. Gedvilas, Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of the Soviet Union, V.A. Zorin.

Xinhua News Agency, November 28th: TASS Moscow: The government delegation of the Polish People's Republic, headed by Chairman of the Council of Ministers of the Polish People's Republic, Józef Cyrankiewicz, arrived in Moscow on the 27th to attend the conference on European peace and security scheduled for the 29th.

Welcoming them at the airport were: First Deputy Chairman of the Council of Ministers of the Soviet Union and Minister of Foreign Affairs of the Soviet Union, V.M. Molotov, First Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of the Soviet Union, A.A. Gromyko, other officials of the Soviet government, and personnel from the Polish Embassy in the Soviet Union.

Cyrankiewicz gave a speech at the airport. He pointed out that it was not accidental that the initiative on this issue, which is of vital importance to the future of Europe, came from Moscow. Thanks to the consistent peace policy of the Soviet government, the great city of Moscow has become a symbol of peace and the freedom of all peoples, and an inspiration and hope for all peace-loving people around the world.

In his statement, Cyrankiewicz, on behalf of the Polish people, paid heartfelt tribute to the residents of Moscow, the capital of the Soviet Union, and to all the Soviet peoples who destroyed Hitler's fascism, liberated Poland, and fought for collective security in Europe and peaceful cooperation among all peoples.

Xinhua News Agency, November 28th: TASS Moscow: The government delegation of the German Democratic Republic, headed by Prime Minister Otto Grotewohl, arrived in Moscow on the 27th to attend the conference on European peace and security scheduled for the 29th.

Welcoming the delegation at the airport were: First Deputy Chairman of the Council of Ministers of the Soviet Union and Minister of Foreign Affairs of the Soviet Union, V.M. Molotov, First Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of the Soviet Union, A.A. Gromyko, other officials of the Soviet government, and personnel from the Embassy of the German Democratic Republic in the Soviet Union.

Grotewohl gave a speech at the airport. He said: Our government accepted the initiative and invitation of the Soviet government with great joy and approval. We believe that the proposals put forward by the Soviet Union on this issue, as well as the unwavering peace policy pursued by the Soviet Union, are a strong guarantee of world peace. The German Democratic Republic especially warmly supports these proposals, because the realization of these proposals can create important preconditions for reunifying the divided Germany, eliminating obstacles to the conclusion of a peace treaty with Germany, and thus eliminating the root cause of unrest in Europe. The realization of the Soviet proposals will help establish lasting peace in Europe.

Grotewohl finally paid tribute to the citizens of Moscow and all the Soviet people.

### 苏联任命出席欧洲会议代表团  波兰和德意志民主共和国代表团到莫斯科

1954-11-29
第1版()
专栏：

    苏联任命出席欧洲会议代表团
    波兰和德意志民主共和国代表团到莫斯科
   【新华社二十八日讯】塔斯社莫斯科二十七日讯：苏联部长会议任命了参加讨论保障欧洲和平和安全的欧洲会议的苏联代表团。
    代表团成员有：苏联部长会议第一副主席、苏联外交部部长维·米·莫洛托夫（代表团团长），苏联外交部第一副部长安·安·葛罗米柯，俄罗斯苏维埃联邦社会主义共和国部长会议主席亚·麦·普扎诺夫，乌克兰苏维埃社会主义共和国部长会议主席尼·特·卡尔钦科，白俄罗斯苏维埃社会主义共和国部长会议主席克·特·马楚罗夫，拉脱维亚苏维埃社会主义共和国部长会议主席维·杰·拉齐斯，爱沙尼亚苏维埃社会主义共和国部长会议主席阿·亚·缪里谢普，立陶宛苏维埃社会主义共和国部长会议主席麦·亚·盖德维拉斯，苏联外交部副部长瓦·亚·佐林。
   【新华社二十八日讯】据塔斯社莫斯科讯：以波兰人民共和国部长会议主席西伦凯维兹为首的波兰人民共和国政府代表团二十七日到达莫斯科，准备出席定于二十九日举行的讨论欧洲和平和安全的会议。
    到机场欢迎的有：苏联部长会议第一副主席兼苏联外交部长维·米·莫洛托夫，苏联外交部第一副部长安·安·葛罗米柯，苏联政府的其他官员以及波兰驻苏联大使馆人员。
    西伦凯维兹在机场上发表了谈话。他指出：在这个对欧洲前途至关紧要的问题上，倡议来自莫斯科，这并不是偶然的。由于苏联政府一贯的和平政策，莫斯科这个了不起的城市已经成为和平和各国人民的自由的象征，成为全世界所有爱好和平人民的鼓舞力量和他们的希望。
    西伦凯维兹在声明中代表波兰人民向苏联首都莫斯科的居民以及摧毁了希特勒法西斯主义和解放了波兰并为欧洲集体安全和各国人民的和平合作而斗争的苏联各族人民致衷心的敬意。
   【新华社二十八日讯】据塔斯社莫斯科讯：以德意志民主共和国总理格罗提渥为首的德意志民主共和国政府代表团二十七日到达莫斯科，准备出席定于二十九日举行的讨论欧洲和平和安全的会议。
    在机场欢迎代表团的有：苏联部长会议第一副主席兼苏联外交部长维·米·莫洛托夫，苏联外交部第一副部长安·安·葛罗米柯，苏联政府的其他官员以及德意志民主共和国驻苏联大使馆人员。
    格罗提渥在机场上发表了谈话。他说：我们的政府以非常高兴和赞许的心情接受了苏联政府的倡议和邀请。我们认为苏联在这个问题上提出的建议以及苏联奉行的坚持不渝的和平政策是全世界和平的坚强保证。德意志民主共和国尤其热烈地拥护这些建议，因为这些建议的实现可为重新统一分裂的德国、消除缔结对德和约的障碍、从而消除欧洲不安的根源创造重要的先决条件。苏联建议的实现将有助于建立欧洲的持久和平。
    格罗提渥最后向莫斯科市民和全体苏联人民致敬。
