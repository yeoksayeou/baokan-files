Title: At the UNESCO General Conference, the Soviet Delegation Proposes a Ban on War Propaganda
Author:
Date: 1954-11-25
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, Montevideo: The Soviet delegation to the UNESCO General Conference has submitted a proposal stating that "the General Conference recommends that all member states take measures to prevent the use of newspapers, radio, and films for war propaganda and any other propaganda that incites hostility and hatred between peoples."
    The Soviet proposal was made after the general debate on the organization's work report concluded at the conference.
    During the general debate, representatives from the Soviet Union, the People's Democratic countries, Mexico, and several other countries spoke about the people's desire for peace and the necessity and possibility of peaceful coexistence among countries with different social and political systems.
    Representatives from the Soviet Union, the People's Democratic countries, Denmark, and others condemned some countries within the organization for preventing representatives of the Chinese people from attending the conference and for rejecting the applications of Romania and Bulgaria to join the organization. The representative from Burma said that a serious shortcoming of this conference was the absence of the voices of the Chinese people, who constitute a quarter of the world's population, and he expressed hope that this shortcoming would be overcome.
    The Japanese representative called for the organization to strive to utilize atomic energy for peaceful purposes. He said, "We Japanese have already experienced the immense damage caused by the use of radioactive elements for destruction."
    The Indian representative suggested that countries focus their efforts on developing public education, science, and culture, and use atomic energy solely for peaceful purposes to improve people's living standards, rather than concentrating on the production of weapons.
    Many national representatives pointed out the need to expand cultural ties between countries and to educate people in the spirit of peace and friendship between nations.
    The U.S. representative avoided addressing the main issues facing the organization in his speech.

### 在联合国教育、科学及文化组织大会上  苏联代表团提出禁止战争宣传的提案

1954-11-25
第4版()
专栏：

    在联合国教育、科学及文化组织大会上
    苏联代表团提出禁止战争宣传的提案
   【新华社二十四日讯】据塔斯社蒙德维的亚讯：苏联代表团在联合国教育、科学及文化组织大会上提出了一个提案，主张“大会建议所有成员国采取措施，以阻止利用报纸、无线电、电影来进行战争宣传和引起各国人民之间的敌视和仇恨的其他任何宣传”。
    苏联这个提案是在大会关于这个组织的工作报告的一般性辩论结束后提出的。
    在一般性辩论过程中，苏联、人民民主国家、墨西哥等若干国家的代表都在发言中谈到各国人民要求和平的愿望，谈到各国不分社会制度和政治制度而和平共处的必要性和可能性。
    苏联、人民民主国家、丹麦等国代表在发言中谴责这个组织中的一些国家阻止中国人民的代表出席大会并拒绝罗马尼亚和保加利亚要求加入这个组织的申请。缅甸代表说，这个组织这次大会的一个严重缺点是在这里没有听到占世界人口四分之一的中国人民的声音，他表示希望这个缺点得到克服。
    日本代表要求这个组织为争取把原子能用于和平目的而努力。他说，我们日本人已领受过把放射性原素用来进行破坏和毁灭所造成的伤害是多么巨大。
    印度代表提出建议，要求各国集中力量发展公共教育、科学和文化，只把原子能用于和平目的从而提高人民的生活水平，而不要集中力量来生产武器。
    许多国家代表指出扩大各国间的文化联系以及用和平和各国人民友好的精神教育人们的必要性。
    美国代表在发言中避而不谈这个组织所面临的主要问题。
