Title: World Peace Council Secretariat Issues Communiqué: Council Meeting Scheduled for the 18th of This Month
Author:
Date: 1954-11-14
Page: 1

Xinhua News Agency, Stockholm, November 13th: The Secretariat of the World Peace Council has issued a communiqué to the press, according to TASS, Stockholm, November 13th.

The communiqué states: The World Peace Council meeting will be held in Stockholm on November 18th, with an expected attendance of approximately 400 people. Half of the meeting participants are invitees. Representatives from sixty countries will be attending, with the largest delegations from: Germany (German Democratic Republic and Federal Republic of Germany), India, Great Britain, Brazil, France, Italy, China, and the Soviet Union.

The first item on the meeting's agenda is the German question and the issue of European security. Professor Patrick Maynard Stuart Blackett, the noted Australian physicist currently working at the University of London, will chair the discussion on disarmament and the prohibition of atomic weapons.

The communiqué states that many prominent social figures have made statements emphasizing the great importance of this meeting. The communiqué cited the remarks of former German Chancellor Joseph Wirth. Wirth said: The proposed Western European Union and the discarded "European Army" plan have the same objective: to piece together a European military bloc. In this bloc, an armed West Germany will occupy a major position. The London and Paris agreements signed by Western governments and the government of the Federal Republic of Germany directly contradict the need to ease tensions in Europe. Therefore, the World Peace Council meeting's inclusion of the issue of European security as the first item on the agenda gives this meeting particular importance.

The communiqué also cited the remarks of Salvador Allende, Vice-President of the Chilean Senate. Allende said: As a representative of South America, I believe that this meeting's inclusion of the issue of studying the situation arising from foreign interference in the political and economic life of our South American countries is of particular significance. Allende said that we should never forget the lessons of the Guatemalan events. He said: The people of South America understand that in order to consolidate universal peace, they should strive to ensure that their independence, sovereignty, and sacred right to control their natural resources are fully respected.

### 世界和平理事会书记处发表公报  理事会会议决在本月十八日举行

1954-11-14
第1版()
专栏：

    世界和平理事会书记处发表公报
    理事会会议决在本月十八日举行
    【新华社十三日讯】据塔斯社斯德哥尔摩十三日讯：世界和平理事会书记处向报界发表公报。
    公报说：世界和平理事会会议将在十一月十八日在斯德哥尔摩举行，预计约有四百人参加。会议的参加者有一半是被邀请的人士。出席会议的将有六十个国家的代表，其中最大的代表团是：德国（德意志民主共和国和德意志联邦共和国）、印度、英国、巴西、法国、意大利、中国和苏联。
    列在会议议程上的第一个问题是德国问题和欧洲安全问题。目前在伦敦大学工作的著名的澳大利亚物理学家柏贺浦教授将主持关于裁减军备和禁止原子武器问题的讨论。
    公报说，许多著名的社会人士都发表了谈话，着重谈到这次会议的极大的重要性。公报举出了前德国总理约瑟夫·维尔特的谈话。维尔特说：建议中的西欧联盟和被摒弃的“欧洲军”计划有着相同的目的：拼凑欧洲军事集团。在这个集团中武装起来的西德将占据主要地位。西方各国政府和德意志联邦共和国政府所签订的伦敦协定和巴黎协定，是同必须缓和欧洲紧张局势直接矛盾的。因此，世界和平理事会会议把欧洲安全问题列入议程的第一项，就使这次会议具有特别重要的意义。
    公报还举出了智利参议院副议长萨尔瓦多·阿伦德的谈话。阿伦德说：作为南美洲的代表，我认为，这次会议把研究由于外国干涉我们南美各国的政治和经济生活而产生的局势问题列入议程，是具有特别意义的。阿伦德说，我们永远不应当忘记危地马拉事件的教训。他说：南美各国人民懂得，为了巩固普遍的和平，他们应该努力奋斗，使自己的独立、主权和控制自己天然资源的神圣权利得到完全的尊重。
