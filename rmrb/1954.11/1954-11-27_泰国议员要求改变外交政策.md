Title: Thai Legislator Calls for Change in Foreign Policy
Author:
Date: 1954-11-27
Page: 4

    Xinhua News Agency, Bangkok: Thai legislator Nai Khlai Narapati distributed a letter to Bangkok newspapers on the 20th addressed to Thai Prime Minister Plaek Phibunsongkhram, requesting a change in Thailand's foreign policy. Narapati advocated following the practices of India, Indonesia, Burma, and Ceylon, to "try to maintain neutrality."

    After discussing the issue of peaceful coexistence, Narapati suggested that Thailand, like some other countries, should also engage in trade with "Communist countries." He said that the Thai economy, like the economies of Britain and France, would benefit from such trade.

### 泰国议员要求改变外交政策

1954-11-27
第4版()
专栏：

    泰国议员要求改变外交政策
   【新华社讯】曼谷消息：泰国议员乃克莱·纳拉帕蒂二十日向曼谷报纸散发一封致泰国总理銮披汶·颂堪的信，要求改变泰国的外交政策。纳拉帕蒂主张效法印度、印度尼西亚、缅甸和锡兰的做法，“设法保守中立”。
    纳拉帕蒂在谈到和平共处的问题后，建议泰国也像一些其他国家一样地同“共产党国家”进行贸易。他说，泰国经济将像英国和法国的经济一样，从这种贸易中得到好处。
