Title: Soke Talks to Hong Kong Press About the Five Principles and Impressions of Visiting China; Mrs. Indira Gandhi Talks About Her Impressions of Traveling in Our Country
Author:
Date: 1954-11-14
Page: 4
Xinhua News Agency, Guangzhou, November 11th: Sahib Singh Soke, Vice-Chairman of the All-India Peace Council, who was invited to visit our country, arrived in Guangzhou on the 9th and left Guangzhou on the morning of the 11th via Hong Kong to return to his country. During his stay in Guangzhou, Soke visited the Botanical Research Institute of Zhongshan University, Guangdong Provincial People's Hospital, railway worker dormitories, and toured the Pearl River.
Xinhua News Agency, Guangzhou, November 13th: According to reports in the Hong Kong "Ta Kung Pao" and "Wen Wei Po" on the 12th: Sahib Singh Soke, Vice-Chairman of the All-India Peace Council, arrived in Hong Kong on the 11th while returning home after visiting China. Soke told the Hong Kong press: "The Sino-Indian relations based on the Five Principles have set a new example for relations between countries in the world. It shows how countries should establish their relations to achieve mutual benefits and even the benefits of all mankind. These Five Principles are not only applicable to China and India, but also to other countries." He pointed out that the two great countries of China and India, joining hands, can not only promote peace in Asia, but also promote world peace.
    When mentioning the issue of striving for peace, Soke said: "I am very optimistic about the future of peace. Mankind has now found the root of war and has realized that they have the ability to stop war."
    Soke expressed his belief that Taiwan will eventually be liberated. He said: "The liberation of Taiwan is not only necessary for defending peace in Asia and the world, but also necessary for the development of trade between Britain and other Western European countries and China. Because trade needs to be developed in an atmosphere of peace and stability."
    Soke talked about his impressions of visiting China for a month, saying: "When I think of China, I think of happy faces. The Chinese people are all so happy because they have been able to use their creativity to achieve the goals they hope to achieve." Soke also praised China's achievements in medicine, education, and architecture. He said: "Judging from the current achievements and progress, I believe that China will very quickly become a socialist industrialized country."
    Xinhua News Agency, New Delhi, November 12th: Mrs. Indira Gandhi, who recently visited China with her father, Prime Minister Nehru, met with reporters in New Delhi on the 10th to talk about her impressions of traveling in the People's Republic of China. She said that the most prominent feature of the lives of the Chinese people today is the enthusiasm of the masses for enhancing the economic strength of their country. She said: The people of the whole country are carrying out active activities for the sole goal of moving towards socialism.
    Mrs. Indira Gandhi said that everywhere you go in China, it seems to be full of healthy and happy children. Mrs. Indira Gandhi was very impressed by the work enthusiasm of Chinese women. Their liberation from the old shackles is "a huge force."
    Mrs. Gandhi said that she returned from the People's Republic of China with a clear impression: an awakened people are working hard to eliminate all traces of backwardness.
    Xinhua News Agency, New Delhi, November 12th: Mukherjee, Speaker of the Legislative Assembly of West Bengal, India, and a member of the delegation of the India-China Friendship Association who recently visited our country, delivered his impressions of visiting China in Calcutta on the 10th. He said: "The Chinese people not only seek friendship with India, but also hope to establish friendly and peaceful relations with all countries in the world." He said that the Chinese people are using extensive social education measures and other methods to encourage the country to implement the principle of establishing peaceful and friendly relations with all countries in the world, especially with India.

### 索克向香港报界谈五项原则和访华观感  英迪拉·甘地夫人谈在我国旅行的印象

1954-11-14
第4版()
专栏：

    索克向香港报界谈五项原则和访华观感
    英迪拉·甘地夫人谈在我国旅行的印象
    【新华社广州十一日电】应邀来我国访问的全印和平理事会副主席萨希布·辛格·索克在九日到达广州，十一日上午离广州取道香港回国。索克在广州期间，曾参观中山大学植物研究所、广东省人民医院、铁路工人宿舍，并游览了珠江。
    【新华社广州十三日电】据香港“大公报”和“文汇报”十二日报道：全印和平理事会副主席萨希布·辛格·索克在访问我国后回国途中于十一日到达香港。索克对香港报界发表谈话说：“以五项原则为基础的中印关系，为世界各国间相处的关系树立了新的榜样，它启示着国家之间应当怎样建立它们的关系，以获致相互间的利益以至全人类的利益。这五项原则不仅适用于中国和印度，而且也适用于其他国家。”他指出，中国和印度这两个伟大的国家携起手来，不但能够促进亚洲和平，并且能够促进世界和平。
    索克在提到争取和平问题时说：“我对和平的前途是很乐观的。人类现在已经找到战争的根源，并且已经明白他们有能力制止战争。”
    索克表示相信，台湾最后一定会得到解放。他说：“解放台湾不但是保卫亚洲和世界和平所必需，也是英国等西欧国家发展同中国的贸易所必需。因为贸易是需要在和平安定的气氛中发展的。”
    索克谈到他访问中国一个月的观感时说：“当我想起中国，就想起了愉快的面孔。中国人民个个都那么愉快，因为他们已经能够为达成他们所希望的目标而发挥他们的创造能力。”索克还赞扬中国在医药、教育和建筑方面的成就。他说：“以目前的成就和进展来看，我相信中国将会很迅速地成为一个社会主义工业化的国家。”
    【新华社新德里十二日电】最近随同她的父亲尼赫鲁总理访问过中国的英迪拉·甘地夫人十日接见新德里的记者，谈她在中华人民共和国旅行的印象。她说，今天中国人民生活中最显著的特点就是：人民群众对增强他们国家的经济力量的热情。她说：全国人民正在为唯一的目标——走向社会主义展开了活跃的活动。
    英迪拉·甘地夫人说，在中国，你到任何地方去，似乎都满是健康和快乐的儿童们。英迪拉·甘地夫人对中国妇女的工作热情印象非常深刻。她们从旧的束缚中获得解放就是“巨大的力量”。
    甘地夫人说，她从中华人民共和国归来时怀着鲜明的印象：觉醒了的人民正在努力地工作着，以消除一切落后的痕迹。
    【新华社新德里十二日电】最近曾到我国访问的印中友好协会访华代表团团员、印度西孟加拉省立法议会议长慕克吉十日在加尔各答发表访问中国的观感。他说：“中国人民不仅寻求印度的友谊，而且也希望和世界一切国家建立友好与和平的关系。”他说，中国人民正在用广泛的社会教育措施等办法，鼓舞全国实行和世界各国尤其是和印度建立和平与友好关系的原则。
