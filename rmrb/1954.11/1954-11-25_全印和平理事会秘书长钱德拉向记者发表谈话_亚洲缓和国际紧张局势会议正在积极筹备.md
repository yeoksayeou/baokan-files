Title: Secretary-General Chandra of the All-India Peace Council Addresses Reporters: Preparations Actively Underway for the Asian Conference on Easing International Tensions
Author:
Date: 1954-11-25
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, Stockholm, November 23rd: This reporter learned from the Indian delegation attending the World Peace Council meeting that the Asian Conference on Easing International Tensions will be held in New Delhi, the capital of India, in February or March 1955. An Indian preparatory committee, including people from all parties, all strata, and all social, economic, cultural, and religious backgrounds, is actively preparing for the conference.

Chandra, Secretary of the Secretariat of the Indian Preparatory Committee and Secretary-General of the All-India Peace Council, told reporters that the news of the Asian Conference on Easing International Tensions has aroused widespread response among the people of Asian countries. Peace advocates in the Soviet Union and China have expressed their support for the conference. In Japan, a preparatory committee has already been formed. In Ceylon, Buddhist circles are very interested in the conference. Indonesian newspapers have published news of the upcoming conference in prominent positions. In Burma, the movement to participate in the conference has penetrated people from all walks of life.

The Asian Conference on Easing International Tensions will exchange views on common issues concerning Asian countries, promote extensive contact and understanding among the people of Asia in all aspects, so as to enhance the atmosphere of peace and expand the area of peace. According to the draft agenda proposed by the Indian Preparatory Committee, the conference will discuss the Five Principles of Peaceful Coexistence in the joint statement of the Prime Ministers of China and India. Political issues in the draft agenda include: colonialism and foreign interference in the internal affairs of Asian countries, the legitimate status of the People's Republic of China in the United Nations, the prohibition and supervision of weapons of mass destruction, the dangers to Asia of military blocs and conventions that divide Asian countries, racial discrimination, discrimination against Asians on issues such as immigration, and equal rights for immigrants.

Cultural issues include: studying the common cultural heritage of Asian countries in order to restore and strengthen cultural ties, preserving national cultures, and developing cultural exchanges among Asian countries.

Economic and social issues include: developing trade relations between Asian countries based on the principles of equality and mutual benefit, economic development of Asian countries and improving living standards, and the status of women and the protection of children.

Representatives of Asian countries attending the World Peace Council meeting here have expressed great concern and warm anticipation for the upcoming Asian Conference on Easing International Tensions.

### 全印和平理事会秘书长钱德拉向记者发表谈话  亚洲缓和国际紧张局势会议正在积极筹备

1954-11-25
第4版()
专栏：

    全印和平理事会秘书长钱德拉向记者发表谈话
    亚洲缓和国际紧张局势会议正在积极筹备
    【新华社斯德哥尔摩二十三日电】本社记者从参加世界和平理事会会议的印度代表团方面获悉，亚洲缓和国际紧张局势会议将于一九五五年二、三月间在印度首都新德里召开。一个包括各党派、各阶层和社会、经济、文化各方面人士和宗教界人士的印度筹备委员会，正在为会议的召开积极进行准备。
    印度筹备委员会书记处书记、全印和平理事会秘书长钱德拉告诉记者说，召开亚洲缓和国际紧张局势会议的消息已在亚洲各国人民中引起了广泛的响应。苏联和中国的和平人士已经对这一会议表示支持。在日本，已经组成了一个筹备委员会。在锡兰，佛教界人士对于这一会议感到很大兴趣。印度尼西亚报纸都用显著的地位刊载会议即将召开的消息。在缅甸，准备参加会议的运动也已深入到各阶层人士中。
    亚洲缓和国际紧张局势会议将就有关亚洲各国的共同问题交换意见，促进亚洲人民各方面的广泛接触和了解，以增进和平气氛，扩大和平区域。根据印度筹备委员会所提出的议程草案，会议将讨论中印两国总理联合声明中关于和平共处的五项原则。在议程草案中关于政治的问题有：殖民主义和外国干涉亚洲国家内政问题、中华人民共和国在联合国中的合法地位问题、禁止和监督大规模毁灭性武器问题、分裂亚洲国家的军事集团和公约对于亚洲的危险问题、种族歧视问题、在移民等问题上对亚洲人的歧视和移居人口的平等权利问题。
    关于文化的问题有：研究亚洲国家共同文化遗产以便恢复和加强文化联系问题、保存民族文化问题及发展亚洲国家的文化交流问题。
    关于经济和社会的问题有：根据平等互利的原则发展亚洲国家间的贸易关系问题、亚洲国家经济发展和提高生活水平问题及妇女地位和保护儿童的工作问题。
    在这里参加世界和平理事会会议的亚洲各国代表，都对即将召开的亚洲缓和国际紧张局势会议表示极大的关心和热烈的期待。
