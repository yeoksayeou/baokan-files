Title: Stalin Replies to Kim Il-sung, Agreeing to Establish Diplomatic Relations Between the Soviet Union and Korea
Author:
Date: 1948-10-19
Page: 3

Xinhua News Agency, Northern Shaanxi, 17th. TASS reports: Soviet Council of Ministers Chairman Stalin sent a letter to Kim Il-sung, Premier of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea, on the 12th, agreeing to establish diplomatic and economic relations between the two countries. The original text of the reply is as follows:
	"Received your letter of October 8th, informing me that the government of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea has begun to perform its duties and suggesting the establishment of diplomatic relations with the Soviet Union, the exchange of ambassadors, and the establishment of appropriate economic relations between the two countries. The Soviet government, which has always supported the rights of the Korean people to establish a unified and independent state, congratulates the establishment of the Korean government and wishes it success in its activities for the national rejuvenation and democratic development of Korea. The Soviet government expresses its willingness to establish diplomatic relations between the Soviet Union and the Democratic People's Republic of Korea, exchange ambassadors, and establish appropriate economic relations between the two countries."
	Kim Il-sung telegraphed Stalin on the 8th, stating that as a result of the national elections, the Supreme People's Assembly of Korea and the government of the Democratic People's Republic had been established, and "had begun to perform duties and activities for the Korean people." He specifically proposed to the Soviet government on behalf of the government the establishment of diplomatic and economic relations. The telegram stated: "It is deeply believed that the establishment of diplomatic relations between the Democratic People's Republic of Korea and the Soviet Union will help enhance the friendship between the peoples of the two countries and promote peace and security in the Far East."
	Xinhua News Agency, Northern Shaanxi, 17th. TASS Tokyo dispatch: Yun Kan (transliteration), chairman of the Korean Residents League in Japan, telegraphed Kim Il-sung, Premier of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea, on the 12th on behalf of 600,000 Korean residents in Japan, expressing ardent love for the government of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea. The telegram emphasized: "Korean residents will do their best to strengthen the Democratic People's Republic of Korea." The telegram declared that Korean residents will be invited to send representatives back to their country on the 17th to celebrate the founding of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea. The League is also preparing to hold a mass rally in Japan to celebrate the founding day of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea. MacArthur Headquarters, which is hostile to Korean national independence, has ordered to prohibit the hanging of the national flag of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea at future mass rallies and to prohibit slogans such as "Withdraw US troops while Soviet troops withdraw from Korea" and "Down with the traitorous Syngman Rhee puppet government."

### 斯大林电复金日成　同意苏联朝鲜建立外交关系

1948-10-19
第3版()
专栏：

　　斯大林电复金日成
  　同意苏联朝鲜建立外交关系
    【新华社陕北十七日电】塔斯社报导：苏联部长会议主席斯大林于十二日函复朝鲜人民民主共和国总理金日成，同意两国间建立外交及经济关系。复函原文如下：
    “接获阁下十月八日来函，得悉朝鲜人民民主共和国政府已开始执行其职务，并建议与苏联建立外交关系，交换大使以及建立两国间适当的经济关系。始终扶持朝鲜民族权利使其建立单一的独立国家的苏联政府，庆贺朝鲜政府的成立，并祝贺它在为朝鲜的民族复兴及民主发展的活动中获得胜利。苏联政府表示愿意建立苏联和朝鲜人民民主共和国间的外交关系，交换大使，同时并建立两国间适当的经济关系。”
    金日成于八日曾致电斯大林，说明由于全国性选举的结果，朝鲜最高人民议会和人民民主共和国政府已告建立，并“已开始为朝鲜民族执行职务与活动”，特代表政府向苏联政府提出建立外交与经济关系的建议，电文中称：“深信朝鲜人民民主共和国和苏联间外交关系的建立，将有利于增强两国人民间友谊和促进远东和平与安全”。
    【新华社陕北十七日电】塔斯社东京讯：日境朝鲜侨民同盟主席云堪（译音），于十二日代表日境六十万朝鲜侨民，致电朝鲜人民民主共和国总理金日成，表示对于朝鲜人民民主共和国政权的热烈爱戴。电文着重声明：“朝鲜侨民将尽力加强朝鲜人民民主共和国”。电文宣称：朝鲜侨民将于十七日应邀选派代表返国，祝贺朝鲜人民民主共和国的成立。该同盟并筹备在日境召开朝鲜人民民主共和国庆祝日群众大会。仇视朝鲜民族独立的麦克阿瑟总部，却下令禁止在未来各群众大会上悬挂朝鲜人民民主共和国国旗，禁止提出“在苏军撤离朝鲜的同时，撤离美军”、“打倒卖国贼李承晚傀儡政府”等口号。
