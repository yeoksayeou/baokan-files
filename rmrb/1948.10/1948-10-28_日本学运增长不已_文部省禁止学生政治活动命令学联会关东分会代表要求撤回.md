Title: Japanese Student Movement Continues to Grow; Ministry of Education's Order Prohibiting Student Political Activities; Representatives of the National Federation of Student Associations' Kanto Branch Demand Withdrawal

Author:
Date: 1948-10-28
Page: 3

Xinhua News Agency, Northern Shaanxi, 24th—Tokyo dispatch: Representatives of the Kanto District (i.e., the district where Tokyo is located) branch of the All-Japan Federation of Student Self-Government Associations recently requested the Director of the Education Bureau to demand that the Ministry of Education immediately withdraw the order prohibiting student movements. The order was issued on the 9th of this month to the presidents of national and private universities, middle schools, and primary schools, stipulating "prohibition of students establishing political party branches in schools, prohibition of nationwide strikes, and prohibition of students participating in labor movements," etc. The purpose of the order is to "restrict the scope of student political activities because the trend of students engaging in political activities is growing incessantly" (TASS).

### 日本学运增长不已　文部省禁止学生政治活动命令学联会关东分会代表要求撤回

1948-10-28
第3版()
专栏：

　　日本学运增长不已
  　文部省禁止学生政治活动命令学联会关东分会代表要求撤回
    【新华社陕北二十四日电】东京讯：日本各校学生自治会联合会关东区（即东京所在区）分会代表，于日前向教育局局长要求文部省立即撤回禁止学生运动的命令。该命令系于本月九日颁发给国立及私立大中小学校长者，规定“禁止学生在学校中成立政党支部，禁止全国性的罢课，和禁止学生参加劳工运动”等。命令的目的在于“限制学生政治活动的范围，因为学生采取政治性活动的趋势增长不已”（塔斯社）。
