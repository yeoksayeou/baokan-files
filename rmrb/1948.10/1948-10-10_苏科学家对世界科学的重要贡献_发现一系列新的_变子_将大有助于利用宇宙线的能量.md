Title: Important Contribution of Soviet Scientists to World Science: Discovery of a Series of New "Variatons" Will Greatly Help Utilize the Energy of Cosmic Rays
Author:
Date: 1948-10-10
Page: 3

Xinhua News Agency, North Shaanxi, 7th: Moscow dispatch: Academician A.I. Alikhanian of the Armenian Academy of Sciences, winner of the Stalin Science Prize, recently reported to the Soviet Academy of Sciences on the significant achievements he and Academician A. Alikhanov of the Soviet Academy of Sciences had made in the study of cosmic rays. As early as 1942, they began observations in a specially designed laboratory on Mount Aragats in Armenia, 3,250 meters above sea level. In the autumn of 1946, they discovered that cosmic rays contain particles of various properties (i.e., microparticles that make up matter), which they called "variations." By 1947, they had discovered a series of new "variations" with masses ranging from 100 to 25,000 times the mass of an "electron." The "variations" exist for an extremely short time, measured in hundredths of a second, during which they are further divided into smaller particles. A series of transformations of variations occur in cosmic rays, which can only be discovered and studied with sophisticated instruments and the profound technology of Soviet physicists. TASS commented: The discovery of "variations" is an important contribution of Soviet scientists to world science. The results of Alikhanian and Alikhanov's research have fundamentally changed the existing concepts of the basic particles that make up matter (i.e., electrons, protons, neutrons, etc. that make up atoms).

Note: Cosmic rays refer to short-wave rays emitted by celestial bodies that reach the Earth, invisible to the naked eye. According to explanation, they are phenomena of atomic ionization, fission, and combination under extremely high temperature, extremely strong electromagnetic action, and extremely large universal gravitation on celestial bodies. It has extremely high energy. It is estimated that the maximum may reach over 100 trillion electron volts, far exceeding the energy that any instrument manufactured by humans to bombard atoms can produce. For example, the latest machine built in the United States last year to bombard atomic nuclei can only produce 300 million electron volts. The discovery of "variations" by Soviet scientists this time will greatly help utilize the energy of cosmic rays.

### 苏科学家对世界科学的重要贡献　发现一系列新的“变子”  将大有助于利用宇宙线的能量

1948-10-10
第3版()
专栏：

　　苏科学家对世界科学的重要贡献
    发现一系列新的“变子”
    将大有助于利用宇宙线的能量
    【新华社陕北七日电】莫斯科讯：荣获斯大林科学奖金的亚美尼亚科学院大学士亚历汉扬，不久前曾向苏联科学院会议报告其与苏联科学院大学士亚历汉诺夫共同进行宇宙线研究的重大成就。远在一九四二年，他们就在拔海三千二百五十公尺的亚美尼亚的阿拉格兹山上，开始在特设的研究室里进行观察。一九四六年秋天，他们发现在宇宙线中含有各种性质不同的质点（即构成物质的微粒子），他们称它为“变子”。到了一九四七年，他们发现一系列新的“变子”，其质量为“电子”质量的一百至二万五千倍。“变子”存在的时间极短，以百分之一秒计算，在此期间，它们又分成质量较小的质点。宇宙线里发生着变子的一系列变形，只有以精良的仪器和苏联物理学家的高深技术才能发现和研究。塔斯社评称：“变子”的发现是苏联科学家对世界科学的重要贡献。亚历汉扬和亚历汉诺夫研究的结果，使关于构成物质的基本质点（即构成原子的电子、质子、中子等）的现存概念起了根本变化。
    按：宇宙线是指宇宙天体放射达于地球的一种肉眼看不见的短波射线，据解释是在天体上极高的温度、极强的电磁作用和极大的万有引力作用下，原子游离、分裂和结合的现象。它具有极高能量，据估计，最高可能达一万万亿电子伏特以上，远超过人们为撞击原子而制造的任何仪器所能产生的能量，例如去年美国造的撞击原子核的最新机器即仅能产生三亿电子伏特。苏联科学家这次对“变子”的发现，将大有助于利用宇宙线的能量。
