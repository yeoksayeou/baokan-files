Title: Telegrams of Congratulations from Indian People's Organizations and Social Celebrities
Author:
Date: 1951-10-09
Page: 4

    [Our Newspaper Dispatch] According to Xinhua News Agency, New Delhi: Leaders of Indian mass organizations, heads of India-China Friendship Associations in various places, and prominent Indian figures have sent telegrams to our embassy in India, congratulating the Central People's Government of China, Chairman Mao Zedong, the leader of the Chinese people, and the Chinese people on the second anniversary of the founding of the People's Republic of China.
    Chairman of the All-India Trade Union Congress, S.A. Dange, said in his telegram: As the chairman of the All-India Trade Union Congress, I can say with great confidence that the working class and other classes and intellectuals are delighted with the amazing victory of the Chinese people in defeating the reactionaries. The long-standing friendship in culture and economy between our two peoples and the new hopes cherished by the people will unite the two peoples, and all malicious attempts by the enemies of peace will never be able to alienate us and separate us into mutually hostile camps. In conclusion, Dange paid tribute to China and the Chinese people on behalf of the All-India Trade Union Congress.
    The India-China Friendship Associations of Bombay, Patna, and Dehradun also sent telegrams of congratulations.
    Prominent figures who sent telegrams include: Dr. Edward, Indian member of the World Peace Council, who recently visited China; Mr. Yalwar, Member of the Indian Parliament and chief writer of the Bombay English news magazine "Forum Weekly"; Mr. Rajinder Singh Bedi, joint secretary of the Bombay Progressive Writers Association and famous Urdu writer; and the chief writer of the Hindi "New Dawn Weekly" in Lucknow, and others.
    In addition, the chairman of the Bhupendra Cement Workers' Union of Surajpur (in the Patiala and East Punjab States Union), the chairman of the Central India Farmers' Union, the chairman of the Indian Seamen's Union, as well as the Madras Progressive Youth League and progressive groups in Nadiad (in Bombay Province), have all sent telegrams congratulating our country's National Day.

### 印度人民团体和社会名流贺电

1951-10-09
第4版()
专栏：

    印度人民团体和社会名流贺电
    【本报讯】据新华社新德里电：印度群众团体负责人、各地印中友好协会负责人以及印度著名人士，纷纷致电我国驻印度大使馆，向我中央人民政府、中国人民领袖毛泽东主席和中国人民祝贺中华人民共和国成立两周年。
    全印总工会主席夏狄亚在贺电中说：作为全印总工会的主席，我可以很有信心地说，工人阶级以及其他阶级和知识分子，对中国人民打败反动派取得惊人的胜利是欢欣鼓舞的。我们两国人民在文化上和经济上的历史悠久的友谊以及人民所怀抱的新的希望，将使两国人民团结起来，和平之敌的一切恶毒企图决无法离间我们、把我们分离到相互敌对的阵营里去。夏狄亚最后代表全印总工会向中国和中国人民致敬。
    孟买、巴特那和台霍拉登的印中友好协会也拍来了贺电。
    拍来贺电的著名人士有：最近访问过中国的世界和平理事会印度理事爱德华博士；印度议会议员、孟买英文新闻杂志“论坛周报”主笔亚尔瓦先生；孟买进步作家协会联合秘书、乌尔都文名作家拉金德·辛·贝地先生和勒克瑙印地文“新黎明周刊”主笔等人。
    此外，苏拉吉普尔（在巴地阿拉及东旁遮普邦联盟）的布本德拉水泥工人工会主席、中央印度邦农民联盟主席、印度海员工会主席，以及马德拉斯进步青年联盟和纳的阿德（在孟买省）的进步团体等，都致电祝贺我国国庆。
