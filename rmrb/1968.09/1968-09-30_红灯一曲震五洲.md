Title: A Red Light Song Shakes the Five Continents
Author: Wáng Yuè, Wú Xuérén
Date: 1968-09-30
Edition: 6

With hearts full of excitement, we watched the color documentary "Piano Accompaniment to 'The Red Lantern.'" The magnificent and melodious piano accompaniment, and the towering and radiant image of the proletariat, have given us profound education.

Chairman Mao teaches us that revolutionary art should "awaken the people, rouse their spirits, and drive the people to unite and struggle, to transform their environment." We love this film because it has achieved such a combatant and inspiring effect. In the film, Li Yuhe and Li Tie-mei's unyielding battle oaths inspire us to move forward; the majestic and heroic tunes strike our hearts. For example, the segment "Brave and Fearless, Full of Courage," the entire melody is powerful and heroic, full of strength, making us feel that Li Yuhe is a true hero. He sacrifices himself for the revolution without flinching, fully demonstrating that "our Chinese people have the spirit to fight to the end with our enemies." We workers, peasants, and soldiers love such heroes and such revolutionary art!

After watching the vivid color documentary "Piano Accompaniment to 'The Red Lantern,'" we couldn't help but think of the Western bourgeois piano art we saw in newsreels. What kind of art is that? It's either shaking their heads and screaming wildly, or stomping on the keys with their feet; either letting monkeys play the piano randomly, or smashing the piano to get the audience's applause. The bourgeois piano art is like their social system, already on the decline, gasping for breath, and soon to be in the museum. Only under the radiant guidance of Mao Zedong Thought, can socialist China's piano art produce such powerful and heroic revolutionary tunes, fully demonstrating the mighty power of Chairman Mao's revolutionary literary line.

In the midst of the victorious songs of the Great Proletarian Cultural Revolution, a new form of proletarian revolutionary art—piano accompaniment to "The Red Lantern"—has been brought to the screen. This is a great joy. The successful filming of the color documentary "Piano Accompaniment to 'The Red Lantern'" has further popularized this new form of proletarian revolutionary art, allowing it to reach more audiences and play a greater role. We eagerly hope to see more revolutionary model operas on the screen.

### 红灯一曲震五洲
王乐　吴学仁
1968-09-30
第6版()
专栏：革命群众来信

    红灯一曲震五洲
    王乐　吴学仁
    怀着十分激动的心情，看了彩色纪录片《钢琴伴唱〈红灯记〉》。壮丽动听的钢琴伴奏，高大光辉的工人阶级的英雄形象，给了我们深刻的教育。
    毛主席教导我们，革命的文艺要“使人民群众惊醒起来，感奋起来，推动人民群众走向团结和斗争，实行改造自己的环境。”我们喜欢这部影片，就在于它起到了这样的战斗作用和鼓舞作用。影片里，李玉和、李铁梅坚贞不屈的战斗誓言，声声都在激励我们前进；雄壮豪迈的曲调，句句都弹到了我们的心坎上。象“浑身是胆雄赳赳”那一段，整个曲调气势磅礴，雄赳赳，气昂昂，充满了力量，叫人一听就感到李玉和是个铁铮铮的英雄汉，他为革命慷慨就义，连眉头都不皱一皱，充分显示了“我们中华民族有同自己的敌人血战到底的气概”。我们工农兵爱的就是这样的英雄人物，喜欢的就是这样的革命艺术！
    看了鲜艳夺目的彩色纪录片《钢琴伴唱〈红灯记〉》，我们不禁想起了从新闻纪录片上看到的西方资产阶级的钢琴艺术。那叫什么艺术？！不是摇头晃脑歇斯底里地乱弹一通，就是用脚在琴键上乱踩一气；不是让猩猩在钢琴上瞎胡闹，就是以砸钢琴来博得观众的喝彩。资产阶级的钢琴艺术象他们的社会制度一样，已经日薄西山，气息奄奄，快进博物馆了。唯独在毛泽东思想的光辉照耀下，社会主义中国的钢琴艺术才能奏出这样雄壮豪迈的革命强音来，这充分显示了毛主席革命文艺路线的强大威力。
    在无产阶级文化大革命的阵阵凯歌声中，无产阶级革命文艺的新品种——钢琴伴唱《红灯记》搬上了银幕，这是一件大喜事。彩色纪录片《钢琴伴唱〈红灯记〉》的拍摄成功，使无产阶级革命文艺的这一新品种得到了进一步的普及，跟更多的观众见了面，发挥了更大的作用。我们热切地盼望能在银幕上见到更多的革命样板戏。
