Title: Sikkim People Hold Anti-Indian Protests
Author:
Date: 1968-09-06
Edition: 5

Sikkim people held an anti-Indian protest. Protesters chanted: We demand freedom, oppose Indian interference in Sikkim.

	New Delhi, September 4 (Xinhua) - According to a report by the Pakistani newspaper "Dawn" on September 3, on August 15, the capital of Sikkim, Gangtok, held an anti-Indian protest demanding the abolition of the 1950 Indo-Sikkim treaty that made Sikkim an "Indian protectorate."

	Protesters chanted slogans such as "We demand freedom," "India cannot rule Sikkim," and "Oppose Indian interference in Sikkim," protesting against the Indian government's interference in Sikkim's political and economic affairs.

	Many protesters were arrested by Indian authorities after the protest.

### 锡金人民举行反印示威  示威者高呼：我们要求自由、反对印度干涉锡金

1968-09-06
第5版()
专栏：

    锡金人民举行反印示威 
    示威者高呼：我们要求自由、反对印度干涉锡金
    新华社拉瓦尔品第四日电　据巴基斯坦《黎明报》三日报道，在八月十五日，锡金首都甘托克举行了一次反印示威，要求废除使锡金成为印度“保护国”的一九五○年印度—锡金条约。
    示威者高呼“我们要求自由”、“印度不能统治锡金”、“反对印度干涉锡金”等口号，抗议印度政府干涉锡金的政治和经济事务。
    许多示威者事后被印度当局逮捕。
