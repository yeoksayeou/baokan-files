Constitution of the Sino-Soviet Friendship Association
(Adopted at the Second National Congress of the Sino-Soviet Friendship Association on December 29, 1954)

Chapter I General Principles

Article 1: This association is named the Sino-Soviet Friendship Association.

Article 2: The purpose of this association is to develop and consolidate the friendly relations between China and the Soviet Union, to promote the connection and cooperation between China and the Soviet Union in culture, economy and other aspects, to introduce and promote the study of the Soviet Union's experience in political, economic and cultural construction and achievements in science and technology, and to strengthen the close unity between the people of China and the Soviet Union in the common cause of striving for lasting world peace.

Article 3: The general office of this association is located in Beijing.

Chapter II Membership

Article 4: This association is formed on the basis of group membership. All democratic parties, people's organizations, and the military, etc., that endorse the constitution of this association may apply to join this association as group members. All members of the group members of this association are members of this association.

Individuals who have not yet joined various democratic parties and people's organizations may also apply to join this association as members if they endorse the constitution of this association.

Article 5: The rights and obligations of members are as follows:

(1) The right to elect and be elected;
(2) The right to make suggestions and criticisms on the association's affairs;
(3) The obligation to abide by the constitution of this association and implement the resolutions of this association;
(4) The obligation to strive to enhance the friendship between the people of China and the Soviet Union, to introduce and promote the study of the Soviet Union's advanced experience and science and technology in various aspects.

Chapter III Organizational Structure

Article 6: The organizational system of this association is as follows:

(1) A general office is established nationwide to lead the association's work nationwide;
(2) Provincial, municipal, and county levels, as well as autonomous regions, autonomous prefectures, and autonomous counties, may establish branches to lead the association's work in their respective localities;
(3) Factories, mines, enterprises, government organs, schools, and cooperatives, etc., may establish branches if conditions permit, to lead the association's work in their respective units.

Article 7: The branches in various localities or units are called the Sino-Soviet Friendship Association of a certain place or unit.

Article 8: The National Congress of this association is held every three years, and may be convened in advance or postponed when necessary, and is convened by the president. Representatives to the congress are elected by various democratic parties, people's organizations, the military, and local branches.

Article 9: The general office of this association, in order to preside over the national association affairs, shall have one president and several vice presidents.

Article 10: The general office of this association, in order to lead the national association affairs, shall establish a National Council under the president and vice presidents, composed of one secretary-general and a number of council members elected by the National Congress.

Article 11: The powers of the National Congress of this association are to amend the constitution of this association, elect the president, vice presidents, secretary-general and council members of the National Council, and listen to and review the work report of the Council.

Article 12: The National Council of this association shall have several deputy secretaries-general to assist the secretary-general in handling daily affairs, and shall establish working bodies as needed, and the organizational methods shall be determined separately.

Article 13: The branches of provinces, cities, counties, autonomous regions, autonomous prefectures, and autonomous counties shall elect a president, vice presidents, and a council as the leading body at the local congress. The organizational structure of local branches shall be determined by the localities according to their local work needs. The branches of factories, mines, enterprises, government organs, schools, and cooperatives, etc., may elect a president and vice presidents to preside over the association's affairs.

Chapter IV Funding

Article 14: The sources of funding for this association are: income from undertakings, donations, and subsidies.

Chapter V Supplementary Provisions

Article 15: This constitution shall be implemented after being adopted by the Second National Congress of this association.

### 中苏友好协会章程

1954-12-30
第2版()
专栏：

    中苏友好协会章程
    （一九五四年十二月二十九日中苏友好协会第二次全国代表会通过）
    第一章　总则
    第一条　本会定名为中苏友好协会。
    第二条　本会宗旨是发展和巩固中苏两国的友好关系，增进中苏两国文化、经济及各方面的联系与合作，介绍和推动学习苏联政治、经济、文化建设的经验与科学技术的成就，加强中苏两国人民在争取世界持久和平的共同事业中的紧密团结。
    第三条　本会总会设于北京。
    第二章　会员
    第四条　本会以团体会员为基础组成。各民主党派、各人民团体和军队等，凡赞成本会章程的，均得申请加入本会为团体会员。凡本会团体会员的成员都是本会的会员。
    个别尚未参加各民主党派、各人民团体的人士，如果赞成本会章程，亦可申请加入本会为会员。
    第五条　会员的权利和义务如下：
    （一）　有选举权和被选举权；
    （二）　有对本会会务提出建议和批评的权利；
    （三）　有遵守本会章程、执行本会决议的义务；
    （四）　有努力增进中苏两国人民的友谊、介绍和推动学习苏联各方面的先进经验及科学技术的义务。
    第三章　组织机构
    第六条　本会组织系统如下：
    （一）　全国设总会，负责领导全国会务；
    （二）　省、市、县和自治区、自治州、自治县均得设分会，负责领导各该地方的会务；
    （三）　工矿、企业、机关、学校及合作社等，如果有条件，得设分会，负责领导各该单位的会务。
    第七条　各地或各单位的分会称为某地或某单位中苏友好协会。
    第八条　本会全国代表会每三年举行一次，必要时得提前或延期召开，由会长召集。代表会代表由各民主党派、各人民团体、军队及各地分会推选。
    第九条　本会总会为主持全国会务，设会长一人，副会长若干人。
    第十条　本会总会为领导全国会务，在正副会长下设全国理事会，由全国代表会选举秘书长一人、理事若干人组成。
    第十一条　本会全国代表会的职权，是修改本会章程，选举总会会长、副会长、全国理事会的秘书长和理事，听取和审查理事会的工作报告。
    第十二条　本会全国理事会设副秘书长若干人，协助秘书长处理日常会务，并根据工作需要设立工作机构，其组织办法另定。
    第十三条　各省、市、县和自治区、自治州、自治县的分会，由当地代表会选举会长、副会长和理事会为领导机构。各地分会的组织机构，由各地根据当地工作需要自行决定。各工矿、企业、机关、学校和合作社等的分会，得推选会长、副会长主持会务。
    第四章　经费
    第十四条　本会经费的来源是：事业的收入、捐助和补助。
    第五章　附则
    第十五条　本章程经本会第二次全国代表会通过后实行。
