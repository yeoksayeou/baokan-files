Title: Burmese Prime Minister U Nu Arrives in Guangzhou, China, at the Invitation of the Chinese Government
Author:
Date: 1954-12-01
Page: 1
Xinhua News Agency, Guangzhou, 30th—At the invitation of the Chinese government, Burmese Prime Minister U Nu arrived in Guangzhou by special plane at 10:40 a.m. on the 30th for a visit to China. Accompanying Prime Minister U Nu on his visit to China were: Mrs. Nu, Burmese Supreme Court Judge U Min Thein, Prime Minister's Secretary and Cabinet Secretary U Win Pe, Prime Minister's Secretary U Than, Army Department Staff Colonel Aung Gyi, Prime Minister's Adjutant Captain Bi So, Prime Minister's Private Secretary Du Qing Su, and five other attendants.

The Burmese Ambassador to China, U Hla Maung, and his wife, and the Chinese Ambassador to Burma, Yao Zhongming, and his wife, accompanied Prime Minister U Nu on the same plane to Guangzhou.

Wang Bingnan, Special Assistant Minister of Foreign Affairs and Director of the General Office, arrived in Guangzhou from Beijing to greet them. U Dong Ting, Third Secretary of the Burmese Embassy in China, also came from Beijing to greet them.

Those who welcomed them at the airport included Tao Zhu, Acting Chairman of the Guangdong Provincial People's Government, Cao Ruoming, Director of the Foreign Affairs Office, Zhu Guang, Wei Jinfei, Qiu Zhe, and Huang Changshui, Vice Mayors of Guangzhou, Chen Zhifang, Secretary-General, Wen Niansheng, Deputy Chief of Staff of the South China Military Region of the Chinese People's Liberation Army, and senior naval and air force generals Fang Zhengping, Zhang Xisan, and Chen Hao.

Also present at the airport to welcome them were responsible persons from the South China Sub-bureau of the Central Committee of the Communist Party of China and the Guangzhou Municipal Committee of the Communist Party of China, representatives of the democratic parties in Guangdong Province and Guangzhou, responsible persons from people's organizations in Guangdong Province and Guangzhou, as well as government officials, representatives from scientific, educational, literary, artistic, journalistic, and religious circles, students, and people from all walks of life.

Soviet Consul in Guangzhou, Kolyagin, also went to the airport to welcome them.

Chen Zhenfu, Burmese Consul in Hong Kong, and his wife also came specially from Hong Kong to Guangzhou to greet them.

A total of 5,000 people welcomed them at the airport.

The Burmese and Chinese national flags were hung at the airport.

After Prime Minister U Nu disembarked from the plane, he shook hands with Acting Chairman Tao Zhu and others.

The band played the Burmese and Chinese national anthems.

Prime Minister U Nu reviewed the guard of honor and accepted flowers from the welcoming crowd.

Then, Prime Minister U Nu walked to the front of the welcoming crowd and waved to them. The welcoming crowd applauded warmly and shouted welcoming slogans.

After leaving the airport, Prime Minister U Nu, accompanied by Tao Zhu, Acting Chairman of the Guangdong Provincial People's Government, took a car to the guesthouse to rest.

In the afternoon of the 30th, Prime Minister U Nu, accompanied by Tao Zhu, Acting Chairman of the Guangdong Provincial People's Government, and Zhu Guang, Vice Mayor of Guangzhou, visited Huanghuagang, Yuexiu Mountain, Sun Yat-sen Memorial Hall, and the Temple of the Six Banyan Trees. Prime Minister U Nu also paid homage to the statue of Sakyamuni Buddha in the Temple of the Six Banyan Trees.

On the evening of the 30th, Prime Minister U Nu watched songs and dances performed by the South China Song and Dance Troupe and Cantonese opera performed by the Pearl River Cantonese Opera Troupe at a welcome banquet in the guesthouse.

Xinhua News Agency, Hanoi, 30th—Burmese Prime Minister U Nu and his wife, who are visiting China at the invitation of the Government of the People's Republic of China, left Hanoi by plane at 6:40 a.m. on the 30th for China. Accompanying them were: Burmese Ambassador to China U Hla Maung and his wife, Burmese Supreme Court Judge and former Burmese Ambassador to China U Min Thein, Burmese Prime Minister's Secretary and Cabinet Secretary U Win Pe, Burmese Prime Minister's Secretary U Than, Burmese Army Department Staff Colonel Aung Gyi, etc.

Yao Zhongming, Ambassador of the People's Republic of China to Burma, and his wife accompanied Prime Minister U Nu and his wife to China.

Those who saw them off at the airport included Pham Van Dong, Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Foreign Affairs of the Democratic Republic of Vietnam, ministers of various ministries of the Democratic Republic of Vietnam, other government officials, and representatives of people's organizations.

Luo Guibo, Ambassador of the People's Republic of China to the Democratic Republic of Vietnam, and his wife, and A. Lavrishchev, Soviet Ambassador to the Democratic Republic of Vietnam, also saw them off at the airport.

### 应我国政府邀请来我国访问  缅甸总理吴努到我国广州

1954-12-01
第1版()
专栏：

    应我国政府邀请来我国访问
    缅甸总理吴努到我国广州
   【新华社广州三十日电】应我国政府邀请前来访问的缅甸联邦总理吴努在三十日上午十时四十分乘专机到达广州。随吴努总理来我国访问的有：努夫人，缅甸联邦最高法院法官吴敏登，总理秘书兼内阁秘书吴温佩，总理秘书吴丹，陆军部参谋昂季上校，总理侍从武官毕梭上尉，总理私人秘书都庆素和其他随员五人。
    缅甸驻我国大使吴拉茂和夫人和我国驻缅甸大使姚仲明和夫人陪同吴努总理同机到达广州。
    我国外交部特派部长助理兼办公厅主任王炳南从北京到广州迎接，缅甸驻华大使馆三等秘书吴栋廷也从北京前来迎接。
    到机场欢迎的，有广东省人民政府代主席陶铸、外事处处长曹若茗，广州市副市长朱光、魏今非、丘哲、黄长水，秘书长陈志方，中国人民解放军中南军区副参谋长文年生，以及海、空军高级将领方正平、张西三、陈浩等。
    到机场欢迎的，还有中共中央华南分局和中共广州市委员会的负责人，各民主党派广东省和广州市组织的代表，广东省和广州市人民团体的负责人，以及政府机关工作人员，科学、教育、文学、艺术、新闻、宗教界的代表，学生和各界群众。
    苏联驻广州领事柯六奇也到机场欢迎。
    缅甸联邦驻香港领事陈振富和夫人也特地由香港来广州迎接。
    到机场欢迎的共有五千人。
    机场上悬挂着缅甸和中国国旗。
    吴努总理下机后，和陶铸代主席等一一握手。
    乐队奏缅甸和中国国歌。
    吴努总理检阅了仪仗队，并接受了欢迎群众的献花。
    然后，吴努总理步行到欢迎群众行列的前面，招手致意。欢迎的群众热烈鼓掌并高呼欢迎口号。
    吴努总理走出机场后，即由广东省人民政府代主席陶铸陪同乘车到迎宾馆休息。
    三十日下午，吴努总理由广东省人民政府代主席陶铸和广州市副市长朱光等陪同游览参观了黄花岗、越秀山、中山纪念堂和六榕寺。吴努总理并在六榕寺内参拜释迦牟尼佛像。
    三十日晚，吴努总理在迎宾馆的欢迎晚会上观看了华南歌舞团演出的歌舞和珠江粤剧团演出的粤剧。
    【新华社河内三十日电】应中华人民共和国政府邀请前往中国访问的缅甸联邦总理吴努和夫人三十日早晨六时四十分乘飞机离开河内前往中国。同行的有：缅甸驻中国大使吴拉茂和夫人，缅甸最高法院法官、前缅甸驻中国大使吴敏登，缅甸总理秘书兼内阁秘书吴温佩，缅甸总理秘书吴丹，缅甸陆军部参谋昂季上校等。
    中华人民共和国驻缅甸大使姚仲明和夫人陪同吴努总理和夫人前往中国。
    到机场送行的有越南民主共和国副总理兼外交部长范文同、越南民主共和国各部部长、其他政府官员和各人民团体的代表。
    中华人民共和国驻越南民主共和国大使罗贵波和夫人，苏联驻越南民主共和国大使亚·拉夫里谢夫也到机场送行。
