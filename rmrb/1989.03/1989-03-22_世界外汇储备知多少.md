Title: How Much Do We Know About the World's Foreign Exchange Reserves
Author:
Date: 1989-03-22
Edition: 7

According to recent statistics from the International Monetary Fund, the foreign exchange reserves (excluding gold) of developed countries and developing countries or regions in 1988 amounted to 733.3 billion U.S. dollars, an increase of 7.2% over 1987.

Japan still holds the most foreign exchange reserves, which increased by 20 billion U.S. dollars in 1988, reaching 97.1 billion U.S. dollars. Taiwan, China, ranks second with 73.7 billion U.S. dollars in foreign exchange reserves.

West Germany's foreign exchange reserves decreased by 16 billion U.S. dollars in 1988, dropping to 58.7 billion U.S. dollars, and thus falling to third place. The United Kingdom ranks fourth with 44.3 billion U.S. dollars, and the United States is fifth.

In 1988, the foreign exchange reserves of Asian developing countries (and regions) increased by over 17 billion U.S. dollars, reaching 149.7 billion U.S. dollars. Among these, South Korea's reserves increased by 12.4 billion U.S. dollars, and China's by 18.6 billion U.S. dollars. The foreign exchange reserves of Latin American countries decreased to 31.7 billion U.S. dollars, those of Middle Eastern countries decreased to 53.7 billion U.S. dollars, and Africa's reserves saw a slight increase, reaching 9.9 billion U.S. dollars.

### 世界外汇储备知多少
华文
1989-03-22
第7版(国际专页（经济）)
专栏：

　　世界外汇储备知多少
    据国际货币基金组织最近的统计数字，一九八八年发达国家和发展中国家或地区的外汇储备（不包括黄金）达七千三百三十亿美元，比一九八七年增加百分之七点二。
    外汇储备最多的依然是日本，去年其外汇储备增加二百亿美元，达九百七十一亿美元。我国台湾位居第二，外汇储备达七百三十七亿美元。
    西德的外汇储备去年减少一百六十亿美元，降到五百八十七亿美元，退居第三。第四位是英国（四百四十三亿美元），美国名列第五。
    去年，亚洲发展中国家（和地区）的外汇储备增加一百七十多亿美元，达一千四百九十七亿美元，其中南朝鲜一百二十四亿美元；中国一百八十六亿美元。拉美国家的外汇储备减到三百一十七亿美元，中东国家的外汇储备减至五百三十七亿美元，非洲的外汇储备略有增加，达九十九亿美元。
    　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　（华文）
