Title: Money Can Make the Mill Turn
Author: Zhū Gēnhuá
Date: 1989-03-23
Edition: 7

Money Can Make the Mill Turn

The title of this article is a Chinese idiom that means "Money can make the mill turn," which implies that money can influence or even control people, especially those in positions of power. This idiom is often used to criticize corruption and the misuse of power.

In recent years, there have been numerous reports of officials abusing their power for personal gain. These incidents have not only damaged the reputation of the government but also undermined the trust of the people. The root of the problem lies in the lack of effective supervision and the absence of a transparent system.

To address this issue, it is crucial to strengthen the supervision mechanism and promote transparency. Officials should be held accountable for their actions, and any form of corruption should be severely punished. The government should also establish a system where the public can report corrupt activities without fear of retaliation.

Moreover, the government should promote a culture of integrity and honesty. Officials should be role models for the society, leading by example. They should be dedicated to serving the people and upholding the law. Only by doing so can we build a clean and efficient government that the people can trust.

In conclusion, the problem of corruption is a serious one that requires immediate attention. By strengthening supervision, promoting transparency, and fostering a culture of integrity, we can effectively combat corruption and build a better society.
