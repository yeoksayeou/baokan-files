Title: Over 30,000 Text-Inscibed Bone Tags Unearthed from Western Han Dynasty
Author: Wang Zhaolin
Date: 1989-03-07
Edition: 1

	Over 30,000 text-inscribed bone tags unearthed from the Western Han Dynasty
	Possess authority and high academic value as central-level textual archives

	According to Xinhua News Agency, Xian, March 6 (Reporter Wang Zhaolin) China's Han Dynasty archaeology has achieved another major breakthrough: archaeologists have unearthed a government office site within the Western Han imperial palace, Weiyang Palace, yielding over 30,000 bone tags inscribed with text.

	These bone tags are primarily made from cow bones, generally measuring 5.8-7.2 centimeters in length, 2.1-3.2 centimeters in width, and 0.5 centimeters in thickness. Each tag averages over a dozen characters. The inscribed content includes the names, specifications, and years of tribute items sent by local artisans to the central government, as well as the names of officials and craftsmen at various levels. The time span of these records almost covers the entire Western Han Dynasty. Some experts believe that these bone tags are central-level textual archives, more authoritative and of higher academic value than Han Dynasty bamboo slips and silk manuscripts unearthed in various regions.

	The initial assessment by Liu Qingzhu, the deputy director of the excavation team, is that the texts on these bone tags record the changes in the quantity, variety, and quality of tribute items presented by various Western Han emperors over different years. They reflect the development of the handicraft economy, including weapon production, as well as the establishment and management of economic institutions, administrative systems, and fiscal revenues at the central and local levels. The bone tags from Weiyang Palace span almost the entire Western Han Dynasty, providing a relatively complete reflection of the development and changes in Western Han script. They hold significant importance for the study of Chinese characters.

### 西汉三万多片文字骨签出土  属中央级文字档案具有权威性和很高学术价值
王兆麟
1989-03-07
第1版(要闻)
专栏：

　　西汉三万多片文字骨签出土
    属中央级文字档案具有权威性和很高学术价值
    据新华社西安３月６日电  （记者王兆麟）我国汉代考古又获得一项重大成果：考古工作者在西汉皇宫未央宫内发掘出一座官署遗址，出土了３万多片刻有文字的骨签。
    这批骨签是以牛骨为主的动物骨骼制作的，一般长５．８—７．２厘米，宽２．１—３．２厘米，厚０．５厘米，每片平均有十多个字。所刻文字的内容为各地工官向中央政府贡进的器物名称、规格、纪年，各级工官和制作者的姓名等，延续时间几乎包括整个西汉一代。一些专家认为，这批骨签，属于中央级的文字档案材料，比在各地出土的汉代竹简、帛书更具有权威性，学术价值很高。
    主持发掘的工作队队长刘庆柱副研究员的初步看法是：这批骨签上的文字记录了西汉各帝不同年代贡进器物的数量、品种、质量上的变化，从一个侧面反映了当时手工业经济发展包括兵器生产的状况，以及中央、地方在经济机构设置、管理体制、财政收入的一些情况，未央宫骨签年代几乎与西汉王朝相始终，较完整地反映了西汉文字的发展、变化，对文字学研究具有重要意义。
