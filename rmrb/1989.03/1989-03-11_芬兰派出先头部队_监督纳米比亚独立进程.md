Title: Finland Sends Advance Team to Monitor Namibia's Independence Process
Author:
Date: 1989-03-11
Edition: 3

A 24-person Finnish unit departed this afternoon from Helsinki Airport for Namibia. They are the advance team of Finland's contingent participating in the United Nations Transition Assistance Group for Namibia.

In February, Finland decided to provide an 886-person monitoring force for the United Nations team overseeing Namibia's independence.

Approximately 4,700 personnel from various countries are participating in the United Nations Transition Assistance Group for Namibia. In addition to Finland, Malaysia and Kenya will also dispatch a battalion of troops. Countries such as the United Kingdom, Canada, Australia, Spain, and Italy will contribute to logistics, communications, engineering, and transportation efforts.

### 芬兰派出先头部队  监督纳米比亚独立进程

1989-03-11
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

　　芬兰派出先头部队
    监督纳米比亚独立进程
    新华社赫尔辛基３月９日电  一支由２４人组成的芬兰部队今天下午从赫尔辛基机场启程前往纳米比亚，他们是参加联合国纳米比亚过渡时期援助团的芬兰先头部队。
    今年２月芬兰决定为联合国监督纳米比亚独立的援助团提供由８８６人组成的监督部队。
    参加联合国纳米比亚过渡时期援助团的各国人员约为４７００余人，除芬兰之外，马来西亚和肯尼亚也将派出一个营的兵力。参加供给、通讯、工兵、运输工作的还有英国、加拿大、澳大利亚、西班牙和意大利等国。
