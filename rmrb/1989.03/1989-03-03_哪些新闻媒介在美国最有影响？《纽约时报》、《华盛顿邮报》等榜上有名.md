Title: Which News Media Are Most Influential in the United States?
Author:
Date: 1989-03-03
Edition: 3

The New York Times, the Washington Post, USA Today, the Wall Street Journal, and the Los Angeles Times were announced today as the most influential news media in the United States for 1989.

This announcement was made during the celebration of the publication of the "Influential News Media Collection." Other media outlets listed as the most influential in the United States include People Magazine, the Associated Press, United Press International, NBC's "Today Show," Cable News Network, Newsweek, and Time Magazine.

The compiler of the collection, renowned public relations lawyer Alan Caruba, said that the selection of "influential news media" primarily depends on their "reach." He added, "A large circulation alone does not necessarily constitute influence."

### 哪些新闻媒介在美国最有影响？《纽约时报》、《华盛顿邮报》等榜上有名

1989-03-03
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    哪些新闻媒介在美国最有影响？
    《纽约时报》、《华盛顿邮报》等榜上有名
    新华社纽约2月28日电　《纽约时报》、《华盛顿邮报》、《今日美国》、《华尔街日报》和《洛杉矶时报》等今天被宣布为1989年美国最有影响的新闻媒介。
    这是在庆祝出版《有影响的新闻媒介选集》时宣布的。同时被列为美国最有影响的新闻媒介的还有《人民杂志》、美联社、合众国际社、全国广播公司的《今日节目》、有线新闻广播网、《新闻周刊》、《时代》杂志等。
    该选集的编撰人、著名公关律师阿兰·卡鲁巴说，“有影响的新闻媒介”的评选主要取决于它们的“接触范围”；他还说，“大的发行量本身并不一定构成影响。”
