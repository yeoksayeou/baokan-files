Title: The United States, West Germany, Japan, Britain, and France Imported $3.2 Billion Worth of Bananas Last Year
Author:
Date: 1989-03-11
Edition: 3

Xinhua News Agency, San Jose, March 9th.

The Banana Exporting Countries Association announced that in 1988, the five major banana-consuming countries in the world imported a total of $3.2 billion worth of bananas, an increase of $440 million compared to 1987.

According to a report published by local newspapers on the 9th, the five major banana-consuming countries are the United States, West Germany, Japan, Britain, and France.

The report states that the United States remains the largest importer of bananas. In 1988, it imported a total of $1.3 billion worth of bananas.

West Germany and Japan rank second and third, respectively.

1988 was one of the best years for banana production in Latin America. The member countries of the Banana Exporting Countries Association collectively exported approximately 221 million boxes of bananas (each box weighing 18 kilograms).

### 美国西德日本英国和法国  去年进口香蕉值３２亿美元

1989-03-11
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

　　美国西德日本英国和法国
    去年进口香蕉值３２亿美元
    新华社圣何塞３月９日电  香蕉出口国联盟宣布，１９８８年，世界５个主要香蕉消费国进口了总值３２亿美元的香蕉，比１９８７年增长了４．４亿美元。
    据此间报纸９日刊登香蕉出口国联盟的一份报告指出，这５大香蕉消费国是美国、联邦德国、日本、英国和法国。
    这份报告说，美国仍继续是边界香蕉头号进口国。１９８８年，它进口的香蕉总值为１３亿美元。
    联邦德国和日本名列第二和第三。
    １９８８年是拉丁美洲香蕉产量最好的年份之一。香蕉出口国联盟的成员国总共出口了约２．２１亿箱香蕉（每箱重１８公斤）。
