Title: 1700-Year-Old Lead Coffin Unearthed in Britain
Author:
Date: 1989-03-08
Edition: 3

British archaeologists have recently unearthed a beautifully decorated lead coffin dating back 1,700 years in St. Albans, Hertfordshire.

The lead coffin was used to bury a wealthy man from the ancient Roman city of Verulamium. It is one of over 600 artifacts excavated from the site. Archaeologists say this is the most significant discovery from the ancient city since the 1950s.

Other artifacts include intricately crafted glass bottles, pottery for storing grain, copper jewelry, and a beautiful relief sculpture depicting a person holding a sheaf of wheat.

### 英国出土１７００年前铅棺

1989-03-08
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

　　英国出土１７００年前铅棺
    英国考古学家最近在赫特福德郡的圣阿尔班斯，挖掘出一口１７００年前的装饰华丽的铅棺。
    这口铅棺是为埋葬罗马古城维鲁拉米乌姆一个富翁用的。它是６００多件出土文物中的一件。考古学家说，这是从五十年代以来挖掘这座古城最重要的发现。
    其他的文物包括做得很精致的玻璃瓶、盛粮食的陶器、铜制珠宝饰物和一个美丽的凹雕，上面雕着一个人手持麦穗。
