Title: Choice
Author: Chāorì
Date: 1989-03-01
Edition: 8

	Choice
	Chao Ri

	When wind encounters a tall mountain
	does it change direction?

	The tragedy of a river is
		marrying the sea

		Sails are opportunistic
		The ship bears the burden of disaster
		The rise and fall of reefs are for
			what?

		The seagulls dare not answer
		There is a route that leads
			to the other shore

	The seabed is lined with sunken ships
	Only letting the anchors rust and peel away layer by layer
	But time is too long,
	Is a catalyst needed?

	Laying a path behind is in vain
	Losing the anchor is a good thing
