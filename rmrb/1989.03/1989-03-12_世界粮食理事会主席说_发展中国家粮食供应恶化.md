Title: Chairman of the World Food Council Says Food Supply in Developing Countries Deteriorating
Author: Zhāng Shèngpíng
Date: 1989-03-12
Edition: 3

Xinhua News Agency, Nicosia, March 10th

The chairman of the United Nations World Food Council, Eduardo Pesquerela, said today here that if effective measures are not taken, the famine in developing countries may worsen.

The council chairman said that international cooperation is necessary to combat famine and malnutrition.

He said that people suffering from famine or malnutrition are mainly distributed in some developing countries in Africa, Southeast Asia, and Central America. He said that every year, about 50 million people, including 15 million children, die from famine and related diseases.

Pesquerela called on all international organizations, including the World Bank, international foundations, the United Nations Food and Agriculture Organization, and other national organizations, to cooperate in solving the famine problem.

Pesquerela made the above remarks at a press conference held during the meeting of the World Food Council Consultative Group here. The meeting is mainly to prepare a specific plan to solve the world famine problem. Representatives from 14 countries, including China, attended the meeting.

### 世界粮食理事会主席说  发展中国家粮食供应恶化
张胜平
1989-03-12
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

　　世界粮食理事会主席说
    发展中国家粮食供应恶化
    新华社尼科西亚３月１０日电  （记者张胜平）联合国世界粮食理事会主席爱德华多·佩斯凯拉今天在这里说，如果不采取有效的措施，发展中国家发生的饥荒将有可能恶化。
    这位理事会主席说，必须进行国际合作以对付饥荒和营养不良问题。
    他说，遭受饥荒或营养不良的人主要分布在非洲、东南亚和中美洲的一些发展中国家。他说，每年大约有包括１５００万儿童在内的５０００万人死于饥荒和有关的疾病。
    佩斯凯拉呼吁包括世界银行、国际基金会、联合国粮农组织和其他一些国家组织在内的一切国际机构在解决饥荒问题上进行合作。
    佩斯凯拉是在出席在这里举行的世界粮食理事会协商小组会议期间举行的记者招待会上说上述话的。
    这次会议主要为制订一项具体解决世界饥荒问题的计划作准备。包括中国在内的１４个国家的代表出席了会议。
