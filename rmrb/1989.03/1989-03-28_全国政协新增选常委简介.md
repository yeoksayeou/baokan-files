Title: Introductions of Newly Elected Standing Committee Members of the National Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference
Author:
Date: 1989-03-28
Edition: 2

Three members of the Communist Party of China and three non-Party members were elected as standing committee members of the Seventh National Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference at the closing meeting of the Second Session of the Seventh National Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference today. They are: Li Kai Xin, former Vice Chairman of the State Planning Commission, Li Gan Le, Vice Chairman of the Central Committee of the China Democratic Revolutionary Party, Yang Di, Vice Secretary of the Shanghai Municipal Committee of the Communist Party of China, He Lu Li, Vice Mayor of Beijing, Qian Zhong Shu, Vice President of the Chinese Academy of Social Sciences, and Jie Feng, former Governor of Hebei Province.

Li Kai Xin, who once served as the Director and Secretary of the Party Group of the State Bureau of Materials, has been engaged in economic work since the founding of the People's Republic of China. Yang Di, who is 64 years old this year, once served as the Director of the Shanghai Post and Telecommunications Management Bureau and the Vice Mayor of Shanghai, concurrently serving as the Director of the Shanghai Municipal Construction Commission. Jie Feng, who is 66 years old this year, once served as a member of the Twelfth Central Committee of the Communist Party of China, Secretary of the Shijiazhuang Municipal Committee, and Vice Secretary of the Hebei Provincial Committee. They are all Communist Party members who joined the revolutionary work around 1938.

Among the newly elected standing committee members, Li Gan Le and He Lu Li are both Vice Chairmen of the Central Committee of the China Democratic Revolutionary Party and both are deputy chief physicians. Li Gan Le once served as a translator at the Beijing Friendship Hospital and Deputy Mayor of Handan City. His father, Li Lie Jun, once served as the Chief of Staff of the Army of the Great Marshal's Headquarters of Sun Yat-sen. He Lu Li, who is 55 years old, once served as the Deputy Mayor of Xicheng District, Beijing. Her father was the Mayor of Beiping 40 years ago, He Si Yuan.

Qian Zhong Shu, who is 79 years old, is the oldest among the six newly elected standing committee members. This non-Party person who once gained fame in the literary world with the novel "Fortress Besieged" graduated from Tsinghua University in 1933 and once served as a professor at Southwest Associated University, Shanghai Aurora Women's College, and Tsinghua University. His main works include "Notes on the Pipe and Awl," "Record of the Art of Writing," and "Seven Pieces."

### 全国政协新增选常委简介
池茂花
1989-03-28
第2版(国内新闻)
专栏：

　　全国政协新增选常委简介
    新华社北京３月２７日电  （记者池茂花）三名中共党员和三名党外人士，在今天闭会的全国政协七届二次会议上，被增选为七届全国政协常委。他们是：原国家计委副主任李开信、民革中央副主席李赣骝、中共上海市委副书记杨堤、北京市副市长何鲁丽、中国社会科学院副院长钱钟书、河北省原省长解峰。
    曾任国家物资总局局长、党组书记的李开信，建国后一直从事经济工作。今年６４岁的杨堤，曾任上海市邮电管理局局长，上海市副市长兼市建委主任。解峰，今年６６岁，曾任中共十二届中央委员、石家庄市委书记、河北省委副书记等职。他们三人都是１９３８年前后参加革命工作的共产党员。
    在新增选的常委中，李赣骝和何鲁丽都是民革中央副主席，都是副主任医师。李赣骝曾任北京友谊医院翻译，邯郸市副市长。他父亲李烈钧，曾经担任过孙中山大元帅府的陆军参谋长。５５岁的何鲁丽曾任北京市西城区副区长，她的父亲是４０年前的北平市市长何思源。
    ７９岁的钱钟书在６位新常委中岁数最大。这位曾以小说《围城》而名噪文坛的无党派人士，１９３３年毕业于清华大学，曾任西南联大、上海震旦女子文理学院、清华大学教授。他的主要著作有《管锥编》、《谈艺录》、《七缀集》等。
