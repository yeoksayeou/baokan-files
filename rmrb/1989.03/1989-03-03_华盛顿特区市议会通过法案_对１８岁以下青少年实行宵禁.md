	Washington D.C. City Council Passes Curfew Law for Minors Under 18

	Washington, February 28 (Xinhua) - To combat the increasing number of violent crimes and drug-related activities, the Washington D.C. City Council today unanimously passed an emergency bill imposing a curfew on minors under 18 in the city.

	The bill was proposed by Council member Frank Smith. It stipulates that from Sunday night 11 PM and from midnight on weekends, anyone under 18 is prohibited from being on the streets. Violators will be detained by D.C. police and released to their parents or guardians. Repeat offenders will face fines ranging from $100 to $500. Those with special circumstances need to carry a signed permit from their parents, guardians, or legal employers.

	According to reports, drug-related crimes are rampant in Washington, with last year's homicide rate topping the nation, earning it the nickname "the city of drugs and murder" in the media.

### 华盛顿特区市议会通过法案  对１８岁以下青少年实行宵禁
仓立德
1989-03-03
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    华盛顿特区市议会通过法案
    对18岁以下青少年实行宵禁
    本报华盛顿2月28日电  记者仓立德报道：为对付日益增多的暴力案件和毒品犯罪活动，华盛顿特区市议会今天一致通过一项紧急法案，对华盛顿市区18岁以下青少年实行宵禁。
    这项法案是市议会成员弗兰克·史密斯提出的。法案规定凡周日晚11时以后及周末午夜以后，任何18岁以下的人不得上街。违法者由特区警察拘留，再让家长或保护人前来认领；重犯者将处以100—500美元罚金；有特殊情况者需持有家长或保护人以及合法雇主签名的证明书。
    据报道，华盛顿毒品犯罪活动十分猖獗，去年凶杀致死人数已跃居全国各城市之冠，被舆论界称为“毒品与凶杀之都”。
