Title: Namibia's Independence Process Imminent
Author:
Date: 1989-03-28
Edition: 4

Namibia's independence process is imminent. The six frontline states will establish a liaison office in Windhoek to supervise the implementation of the UN's plan for Namibia's independence.

According to a report by Xinhua News Agency from Harare on March 26, Zimbabwean Foreign Minister Shamuarira stated to the Pan-African News Agency of Zimbabwe that the six frontline states in southern Africa will set up a liaison office in Windhoek, Namibia. This office will be responsible for overseeing the implementation of the United Nations' plan for Namibia's independence. The work of this liaison office will involve representatives from the six frontline states.

The six frontline states—Zambia, Zimbabwe, Botswana, Mozambique, Angola, and Tanzania—decided at their summit held in Harare on March 8 to send a delegation to Namibia. This delegation aims to assist in ensuring the fairness and transparency of future elections in Namibia.

### 纳米比亚独立进程在即  前线国家将建立联络处  驻纳监督独立计划实施

1989-03-28
第4版(国际新闻)
专栏：

　　纳米比亚独立进程在即        
    前线国家将建立联络处        
    驻纳监督独立计划实施
    新华社哈拉雷３月２６日电  津巴布韦外长沙穆亚里拉今天对津巴布韦全非通讯社发表谈话说，南部非洲６个前线国家将在纳米比亚的温得和克建立一个联络处，以监督联合国纳米比亚独立计划的实施。这个联络处的工作将由６个前线国家的代表参加。
    这６个前线国家——赞比亚、津巴布韦、博茨瓦纳、莫桑比克、安哥拉和坦桑尼亚３月８日在哈博罗内举行的首脑会议上决定，派出一个代表团去纳米比亚，以帮助确保纳米比亚未来的选举自由和公正。
