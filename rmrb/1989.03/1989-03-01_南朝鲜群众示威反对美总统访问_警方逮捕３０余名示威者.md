Title: South Korean Crowds Protest Against U.S. President's Visit
Author:
Date: 1989-02-28
Edition: 3

Xinhua News Agency, Beijing, February 28th.

Mass protests were held in several cities in South Korea on February 26th and 27th against the visit of U.S. President Bush and demanding the withdrawal of U.S. troops from South Korea.

On February 27th, in the capital Seoul, approximately 700 students clashed with police after being prevented from marching to the U.S. Embassy. The police arrested more than 30 dissidents who attempted to deliver a letter protesting Bush's visit to the U.S. Embassy.

On February 26th, thousands of protesters took to the streets in eight cities across South Korea. According to reports, around 150 protesters and police were injured in the clashes, and five government offices were damaged.

### 南朝鲜群众示威反对美总统访问  警方逮捕３０余名示威者

1989-03-01
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

　　南朝鲜群众示威反对美总统访问
    警方逮捕３０余名示威者
    新华社北京２月２８日电  汉城消息：南朝鲜许多城市于２６日和２７日举行群众示威，反对美国总统布什对南朝鲜访问和要求美国从南朝鲜撤军。
    ２７日，在首都汉城，由于警察阻止示威群众前往美国大使馆，约有７００名学生同警察发生了冲突。警方逮捕了３０多名企图向美国大使馆递交一封抗议布什访问的信件的持不同政见者。
    ２６日，南朝鲜８个城市有数以千计的群众举行了反美示威。据报道，约有１５０名示威者和警察在冲突中受伤，政府办公地点有５处遭破坏。
