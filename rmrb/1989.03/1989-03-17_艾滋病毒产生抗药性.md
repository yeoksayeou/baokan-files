Title: HIV Develops Drug Resistance
Author: Cang Lidie
Date: 1989-03-17
Edition: 4

People's Daily, Washington, March 14th.

Some 300,000 AIDS patients in the United States are facing a grim situation: the manufacturer of the anti-AIDS drug AZT has today sent letters to thousands of doctors across the nation, stating that some strains of the AIDS virus have developed resistance to AZT. AZT is currently the only drug approved by the federal government for official use against AIDS.

Some officials and experts have pointed out that despite the discovery of drug-resistant strains of the AIDS virus, patients currently have no reason to stop taking this effective AZT, and doctors have no reason to stop prescribing it.

Samuel Broder, director of the National Cancer Institute, said, "AZT is just an important beginning. In the treatment of any disease, relying on a single drug is a mistake."

### 艾滋病毒产生抗药性
仓立德
1989-03-17
第4版(国际新闻)
专栏：

　　艾滋病毒产生抗药性
    本报华盛顿３月１４日电  记者仓立德报道：美国３０余万名艾滋病患者遇到不祥之兆：生产抗艾滋病药物ＡＺＴ的厂家今天致函全国数千名医生，一些种类的艾滋病病毒已经对ＡＺＴ产生了抗药性。ＡＺＴ是目前唯一经过联邦政府批准正式使用的抗艾滋病药物。
    一些官员和专家指出，尽管发现了艾滋病病毒的抗药性，患者目前还没有理由停止服用这种有效的ＡＺＴ，医生也没有理由停止开处方。
    全国癌症研究所所长塞缪·布罗德说，“ＡＺＴ仅仅是一个重要的开端，在任何疾病治疗中单凭一种药物都是错误的。”
