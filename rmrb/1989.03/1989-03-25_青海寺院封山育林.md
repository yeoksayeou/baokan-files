Title: Green Mountains and Clear Waters: Temples in Qinghai Implement Forestation
Author: Zha Zhenpu
Date: 1989-03-25
Edition: 2

Most Buddhist temples, mosques, and Taoist temples in Qinghai Province have responded to the government's call by implementing forestation and protecting the environment over the past few years.

The renowned Gelugpa temple, Ta'er Monastery, had organized monks to plant more than 200,000 trees by the end of last year. The 15 temples in Huangnan Tibetan Autonomous Prefecture have planted more than 1,600 mu (approximately 106.67 hectares) and 550,000 trees in the past three years. The monks of Wutun Shangxia Village Temple, totaling 444, recently built a nursery covering 44 mu (approximately 2.93 hectares), cultivated more than 6,400 seedlings, and planted more than 7,000 poplar trees.

### 青海寺院封山育林
郅振璞
1989-03-25
第2版(国内新闻)
专栏：四面八方

　　青海寺院封山育林
    本报西宁３月２４日电  记者郅振璞报道：青海省绝大多数佛寺、清真寺、道教宫观响应政府号召，连年来封山育林，维护和美化环境。
    著名黄教寺院塔尔寺，到去年底组织僧众植树已达２０余万株。黄南藏族自治州１５座寺院，３年来也植树１６００多亩、５５万多株。五屯上下庄寺院僧侣４４４人，最近修建苗圃４４亩，育苗６４００多株，栽种白杨树７０００多株。
