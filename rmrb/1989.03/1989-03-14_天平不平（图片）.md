Title: The Unequal Scales
Author: Zhū Gēnhuá
Date: 1989-03-14
Edition: 7

The world is not balanced. The scales of justice are tilted, and the gap between the rich and the poor is widening. This is a stark reality that cannot be ignored.

In many developing countries, people are still struggling with poverty, hunger, and disease. They lack basic necessities such as clean water, adequate food, and proper healthcare. Meanwhile, in the developed world, people enjoy a high standard of living, with access to advanced technology, education, and healthcare.

This disparity is not only a moral issue but also a political one. It fuels resentment and instability, leading to conflicts and wars. It is a threat to global peace and security.

The international community must take action to address this imbalance. We need a fair and just global economic system that ensures equal opportunities for all. We need to promote sustainable development, reduce poverty, and bridge the gap between the rich and the poor.

We must also address the root causes of this imbalance. This includes addressing issues such as unfair trade practices, debt burdens, and the exploitation of resources in developing countries. We need to promote fair trade, cancel debts, and ensure that the benefits of globalization are shared equitably.

In conclusion, the world is not balanced, and the scales of justice are tilted. It is time for the international community to take action to address this imbalance and promote a more just and equitable world.
