Title: The World Has 30 Million Blind People, Possibly Doubling by the Year 2000
Author:
Date: 1989-03-08
Edition: 3

The world currently has 30 million blind people, with 80% of them in the Third World. Most of these blind people in the Third World lose their sight due to eye infections or a lack of vitamin A. By the year 2000, the number of blind people in the world may double.

According to experts who attended the recent meeting of the West German Association for the Prevention of Blindness, there are nine main causes of blindness in West Germany:

1. Age-related macular degeneration leading to blindness;
2. Glaucoma causing blindness;
3. Blindness from childhood;
4. Retinal vascular diseases leading to blindness;
5. High myopia developing into blindness;
6. Congenital retinal pigment epithelium defects causing blindness;
7. Uveitis leading to blindness;
8. Optic nerve atrophy causing blindness;
9. Corneal scarring leading to blindness.

### 世界盲人三千万  到二千年时可能翻一番

1989-03-08
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

　　世界盲人三千万
    到二千年时可能翻一番
    在最近举行的西德防止眼盲协会年会上，西德眼科专家克劳斯教授说，现在世界上共有３０００万盲人，其中８０％是在第三世界。而绝大部分第三世界盲人是由眼睛感染疾病或因缺乏维生素Ａ而导致双目失明的。到２０００年时，世界盲人数字可能再增加一倍。
    据参加会议的专家认为，导致西德一些人双目失明的原因大致有９种：（１）因年龄关系，视网膜中心退化而致盲；（２）青光眼导致失明；（３）幼时致盲；（４）视网膜血管疾病引起失明；（５）近视眼发展成眼盲；（６）视网膜色素层先天障碍引起；（７）血管膜发炎导致失明；（８）视神经衰退导致失明；（９）角膜瘢痕引起。
