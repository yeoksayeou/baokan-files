Date: 1989-03-11
Edition: 2

Vice President of the National Defense University, Qian Diqian, Passes Away

New China News Agency, Beijing, March 10th: Vice President of the National Defense University, General Qian Diqian, passed away due to illness on February 23rd in Beijing, at the age of 70.

The farewell ceremony for Qian Diqian was held today at the Hall of the Revolutionary Martyrs Cemetery in Beijing. Peng Zhen, Yan Mingfu, Yang Dezhi, Yu Quli, Zhang Aiping, Huang Zhen, Wei Guoqing, and Hong Xuezhi, as well as the Central Committee of the Communist Party, the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress, the Central Military Commission, the Central Propaganda Department, the Ministry of National Defense, the General Staff, General Political Department, General Logistics Department, the Military Commission's Discipline Inspection Commission, the National Defense Science and Technology Commission, and the National Defense University, sent wreaths.

Qin Jiwei, Wang Ping, Li Desheng, Xiao Ke, and others attended the farewell ceremony and sent wreaths.

Qian Diqian was from Taicang County, Jiangsu Province. He joined the revolution in 1938 and the Communist Party of China in the same year. He served in various positions, including the Educational and Propaganda Officer of the Anti-Japanese Military and Political University in Yan'an, the Propaganda Department Director of the Brigade, Column, and Field Army, the Editor-in-Chief of the Volunteer Army Newspaper, the Propaganda Department Director, the Deputy Director of the Political Department of the Anhui Military Region, the Deputy Director of the Political Department of the former Kunming Military Region, the Deputy Director of the former Political Academy, the Acting Editor-in-Chief and Party Secretary of the PLA Daily, and others. He was elected as a representative of the Seventh National People's Congress. During the Anti-Japanese War, he contributed to the propaganda of the Party's anti-Japanese and national salvation policies and the training of military cadres. During the War of Liberation, he participated in the counterattack in Henan, crossing the Yellow River, liberating Henan West, advancing into Guangdong, liberating Southwest China, and other battles, assisting the military leadership in a large amount of ideological and political work and propaganda. During the Korean War, he participated in five battles, trench defense battles, and the Battle of Triangle Hill, actively organizing propaganda work, and using patriotism, internationalism, and revolutionary heroism to inspire the troops. During his time at the General Political Department, he adhered to the principle of combining theory with practice, seriously summarized the experience of the military's political work, and correctly propagated Marxism-Leninism, Mao Zedong Thought, and the Party's policies. During the ten years of turmoil, he stood firmly, had clear views, and resolutely fought against the counter-revolutionary cliques of Lin Biao and Jiang Qing. He wholeheartedly supported and resolutely implemented the line, principles, and policies of the Third Plenary Session of the Eleventh Central Committee. During his tenure at the PLA Daily, he actively advocated and implemented news reforms. At the National Defense University, he was in charge of political theory teaching and scientific research. To meet the needs of high-level teaching, he made efforts to reform the teaching content and methods and devoted a lot of energy to compiling and publishing high-quality political theory textbooks and monographs.

Comrade Qian Diqian was a loyal communist warrior, an outstanding Marxist theoretician in our army, and an outstanding warrior on the propaganda and education and news fronts. Throughout his life, he sought truth from facts and insisted on truth; he was consistent in words and deeds; he was diligent and selfless in his work; he was fair and modest; he was honest and selfless; he was close to the people and united with his comrades; he lived a simple life and strictly required his children, always maintaining the noble character and fine traditions of a communist.

### 国防大学副校长钱抵千病逝

1989-03-11
第2版(国内新闻)
专栏：

　　国防大学副校长钱抵千病逝
    新华社北京３月１０日电  国防大学副校长钱抵千中将因病于２月２３日在北京逝世，终年７０岁。
    向钱抵千同志遗体告别的仪式今天在北京八宝山革命公墓礼堂举行。彭真、阎明复、杨得志、余秋里、张爱萍、黄镇、韦国清、洪学智等，中共中央、全国人大常委会、中央军委、中共中央宣传部、国防部、解放军三总部、军委纪委、国防科工委、国防大学等送了花圈。
    秦基伟、王平、李德生、肖克等参加了遗体告别仪式并送了花圈。
    钱抵千同志是江苏省太仓县人，１９３８年参加革命，同年加入中国共产党。历任延安抗大分校教育干事、宣传干事兼校刊编辑，旅、纵队、野战军政治部宣传部长，志愿军报社社长、宣传部部长，总政治部宣传部宣传处长、宣传部副部长，安徽省军区政治部副主任，原昆明军区政治部副主任，原政治学院副院长兼训练部长，解放军报社代社长、党委书记等职。曾当选七届全国人大代表。抗日战争中，他为宣传党的抗日救国方针，培养我军干部作出了贡献。解放战争时期，他参加了豫北反攻、南渡黄河、解放豫西、进军两广、解放大西南等作战，协助部队领导做了大量的思想政治工作和宣传鼓动工作。在抗美援朝战争中，他参加了五次战役、阵地防御作战、上甘岭战役，积极组织开展宣传工作，用爱国主义、国际主义和革命英雄主义精神教育鼓舞部队。他在总政治部工作期间，坚持理论联系实际的方针，认真总结我军政治工作经验，正确地宣传马克思列宁主义、毛泽东思想和党的方针、政策。在十年动乱中，他立场坚定，观点鲜明，同林彪、江青反革命集团进行了坚决斗争。他衷心拥护、坚决贯彻党的十一届三中全会的路线、方针、政策。他在解放军报社任职期间，积极倡导和实行新闻改革。他在国防大学分管政治理论教学和科学研究，为适应高层次教学的需要，对教学内容和教学方法努力进行改革，并为编写、出版高质量的政治理论教材和论著付出了大量心血。
    钱抵千同志是忠诚的共产主义战士，我军优秀的马克思主义理论工作者，宣传教育、新闻战线上的优秀战士。他一生实事求是，坚持真理；言行一致，表里如一；勤奋好学，忘我工作；公道正派，谦虚谨慎；廉洁奉公，不谋私利；联系群众，团结同志；生活俭朴，要求子女严格，始终保持了共产党人的高尚品德和优良传统。
