Title: West German, French, Belgian, and Luxembourg Steel Monopoly Capitalists Sign Secret Steel Sales Agreement to Counter U.S. Competition
Author:
Date: 1953-04-13
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, 9th dispatch: According to a United Press report from Paris: The steel monopoly capitalists of four major steel-producing countries in Western Europe—West Germany, France, Belgium, and Luxembourg—secretly concluded an agreement in mid-March to raise the export price of steel by approximately 10 percent, but still keep it lower than the U.S. price. United Press, quoting local "industry sources," stated that although the agreement did not specifically mention the United States, it was actually "dealing with the United States in two ways." Raising the export price of steel can earn more money from Americans when they order European steel. On the other hand, setting the steel price lower than the U.S. price "is for the convenience of competing with the United States."
    This matter has been kept strictly secret, but a reporter from this U.S. bourgeois news agency ferreted it out. This reporter pointed out: "The main reason for re-establishing this group is to seize the U.S. market."

### 西德、法、比、卢钢铁垄断资本家签订售钢密约对付美国竞争

1953-04-13
第4版()
专栏：

    西德、法、比、卢钢铁垄断资本家签订售钢密约对付美国竞争
    【新华社九日讯】据合众社巴黎消息：西欧大陆四个主要产钢国家西德、法国、比利时和卢森堡的钢铁垄断资本家，三月中旬秘密订了一个协定，把钢的出口价格提高大约百分之十，但是仍然定得比美国的价格低。合众社引当地“工业界人士”的话说，这个协定虽没有特别提到美国，但实际上是“从两方面来对付美国的”。提高钢的出口价格，可以从美国定购欧洲钢方面多赚美国人的钱。另一方面，把钢的价格定得低于美国，“是为了和美国竞争的方便”。
    这件事情一直严守秘密，但是被美国资产阶级的这家通讯社的记者给兜出来了。这个记者指出：“重新建立这个集团的主要原因，是要夺取美国的市场。”
