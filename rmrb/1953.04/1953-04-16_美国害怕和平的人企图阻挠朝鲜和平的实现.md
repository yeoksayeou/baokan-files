Title: U.S. Elements Fearful of Peace Attempt to Obstruct the Realization of Peace in Korea
Author:
Date: 1953-04-16
Page: 1

Xinhua News Agency, April 15th: Recent statements in the American press and news agencies reveal that certain elements in the United States who are fearful of peace are attempting to obstruct the realization of peace in Korea.

    After the Korean-Chinese side put forward new proposals, Ernest Hoberecht, Vice President and Asia Division Manager of the United Press, estimated the future of the negotiations, saying, "Even if things go smoothly without any setbacks, the timetable for peace is not easy to predict." The April 10th issue of "U.S. News & World Report" also said, "Despite the Communist side's recent actions to seek an armistice, true peace in Korea—when all shooting has stopped and U.S. troops can return home—is still a long way off." After the agreement to exchange sick and wounded prisoners of war was reached between the two sides, U.S. elements fearful of peace attempted to set up new obstacles to the next step of resuming negotiations. On April 8, the Associated Press issued a dispatch from Washington, stating that in the next round of negotiations, "there may be some armistice obstacles that are not yet visible." A dispatch from Tokyo by the International News Service on April 11 also indicated that the resumption of armistice negotiations was not optimistic. The dispatch quoted a U.S. military officer as saying:
    "It looks like the honeymoon period is over." United Press International reported from Washington on the same day that some people there believed that "to achieve a glorious armistice, long and arduous negotiations are still needed, not to mention the final political settlement." United Press reporter Merrick made it clearer on April 13 that the negotiations might be delayed, saying, "Even if the Communist side is sincere and cooperative in the armistice negotiations, it will probably take a long process to resolve the armistice issue."

### 美国害怕和平的人企图阻挠朝鲜和平的实现

1953-04-16
第1版()
专栏：

    美国害怕和平的人企图阻挠朝鲜和平的实现
    【新华社十五日讯】最近美国报刊和通讯社的言论透露，美国方面某些害怕和平的人正企图阻挠朝鲜和平的实现。
    在朝中方面的新建议提出以后，合众社的副社长兼亚洲分社社长欧内斯特·霍布雷奇特就估计谈判的前途说，“即使事情不受一点挫折地进行得很顺利，媾和的时间表也不是容易推测的。”四月十日的“美国新闻与世界报道”也说，“尽管共方最近采取了寻求停战的行动，但是朝鲜境内真正的和平——其时所有的射击都已停止，美国军队则能返回家乡——仍然还远不可及。”在交换双方病伤被俘人员的协议达成后，美国害怕和平的人又企图给下一步恢复谈判安放下新的障碍。美联社在四月八日从华盛顿发出一条电讯，表示在下一步谈判中“可能有一些现在还看不出来的停战障碍”。国际新闻社四月十一日从东京发出的电讯也同样表示恢复停战谈判未可乐观。这条电讯引一个美方军官的话说：
    “看起来好像蜜月佳期已经过去了。”合众社同一天从华盛顿报道说，那里的有些人认为，“要达到光荣的停战，还需要经过长期的很艰苦的谈判，更不要说最后的政治解决了”。合众社记者麦里克在四月十三日更明白表示谈判可能拖延，他说，“纵然共方在停战谈判中具有诚意而且实行合作，要想解决停战问题恐怕也还要经过长期的过程。”
