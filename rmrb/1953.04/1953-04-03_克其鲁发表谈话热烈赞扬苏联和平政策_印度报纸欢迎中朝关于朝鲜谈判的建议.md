Title: Kitchlew Delivers Speech Warmly Praising Soviet Peaceful Policy; Indian Newspapers Welcome Sino-Korean Proposal Regarding Korean Negotiations
Author:
Date: 1953-04-03
Page: 1

Xinhua News Agency, New Delhi, April 2nd: Saifuddin Kitchlew, Chairman of the All-India Peace Council and recipient of the Stalin International Prize "For Strengthening Peace Among Nations," held a press conference in New Delhi on March 30th after returning to India from a visit to the Soviet Union. Kitchlew delivered a speech to the reporters, warmly praising Joseph Stalin's immortal contribution to the cause of world peace and the Soviet Union's consistent adherence to a peaceful policy. He also welcomed Marshal Kim Il-sung's and General Peng Dehuai's proposal on exchanging sick and wounded prisoners of war and immediately resuming the Korean armistice negotiations.

Speaking of his meeting with Stalin during his visit to the Soviet Union, Kitchlew said: "First of all, I want to express once again my deepest condolences on the passing of this great and beloved figure. He was one of the great men of all time, a great genius, and a staunch fighter for peace. He devoted his entire life and energy to the cause of maintaining and defending peace, to building his country, and to raising the material and cultural standards of his people." Kitchlew continued: "During the hours I spent talking with him, I was deeply moved by his profound desire for peaceful cooperation among all countries and his love for the Indian people."

Kitchlew said that the news of Marshal Kim Il-sung's and General Peng Dehuai's proposal to exchange sick and wounded prisoners of war would be welcomed by all peace-loving people, and they would sincerely hope that an agreement on this issue would lead to the end of the Korean War.

Kitchlew emphasized the Soviet Union's peaceful policy. He pointed out:

"The deep desire for peace expressed by the representatives of the people of the world in Vienna has been manifested in every activity of the Soviet people." He also said: "When I visited China last October, I saw the same spirit and the same desire for peace there."

Kitchlew stressed: "I am fully convinced that the Soviet Union will continue to pursue its peaceful foreign policy after the death of Marshal Stalin. The leaders of the government headed by Mr. Malenkov have clearly expressed the Soviet Union's fervent desire for peace and their desire to resolve all issues through peaceful negotiations."

Xinhua News Agency, April 2nd: New Delhi reports: Major Indian newspapers have prominently published Premier Zhou Enlai's statement on the Korean armistice negotiations, as well as Marshal Kim Il-sung's and General Peng Dehuai's proposal to exchange sick and wounded prisoners of war and immediately resume the Korean armistice negotiations.

The "Hindustan Times" commented on Premier Zhou's statement on April 1st, saying that the proposal provided extremely convincing evidence to prove China's sincere desire for peace.

The "Free Press" said in its editorial on March 30th that Marshal Kim Il-sung and General Peng Dehuai's proposal gave great comfort to peace-loving people in all countries, and hoped that all countries participating in the Korean War would truly adopt an attitude of eliminating world tensions. The "Bombay Chronicle" said in its editorial on March 30th that the Korean-Chinese proposal opened the way to ending the Korean War and was a contribution to peace.

According to a report from the Indian News Service in New Delhi on March 31st, Premier Zhou's statement received a good response from officials in New Delhi, who hoped that the proposal could lead to the resumption of negotiations to seek a mutually agreed solution.

Mrs. Pandit, head of the Indian delegation to the United Nations, said in New York on March 31st that China's peace proposal on Korea was "very encouraging." She hoped that this peace proposal would be "the beginning of a solution to the Korean problem."

### 克其鲁发表谈话热烈赞扬苏联和平政策  印度报纸欢迎中朝关于朝鲜谈判的建议

1953-04-03
第1版()
专栏：

    克其鲁发表谈话热烈赞扬苏联和平政策
    印度报纸欢迎中朝关于朝鲜谈判的建议
    【新华社新德里二日电】全印和平理事会主席、“加强国际和平”斯大林国际奖金得奖人赛福丁·克其鲁访问苏联回到印度后，三月三十日在新德里举行记者招待会。克其鲁向记者们发表谈话，热烈赞扬约·维·斯大林对世界和平事业的不朽贡献和苏联一贯坚持的和平政策，并表示欢迎金日成元帅、彭德怀将军关于交换病伤战俘并立即恢复朝鲜停战谈判的建议。
    克其鲁在谈到他在访问苏联期间见到了斯大林时说：“我首先要再一次对这位伟大的受人爱戴的人物的逝世表示最深切的哀悼。他是一切时代中的伟人之一，是一位伟大的天才，一位不屈不挠的和平战士。他已把他的全部生命和精力贡献给维护和保卫和平的事业，贡献给建设他的国家和提高他的人民的物质和文化水平的事业。”克其鲁接着说：“在我和他谈话的几个小时中，他对一切国家和平合作的深切愿望，以及他对印度人民的友爱，深深感动了我。”
    克其鲁说：金日成元帅和彭德怀将军关于交换病伤战俘的建议的消息，将受到所有爱好和平的人们的欢迎，他们将衷心希望这个问题的协议会导致朝鲜战争的结束。
    克其鲁着重谈到了苏联的和平政策。他指出：
    “全世界人民的代表们在维也纳表示出的对和平的深切愿望，已在苏联人民的每一种活动中表现出来了。”他并说：“我在去年十月访问中国时，看到那里有着同样的精神和同样的和平愿望。”
    克其鲁强调说：“我完全相信，苏联在斯大林大元帅逝世以后，仍将继续奉行它的和平的外交政策。以马林科夫先生为首的政府的领袖们已经明确地表示了苏联对和平的殷切愿望以及他们希望用和平谈判方式解决一切问题的愿望。”
    【新华社二日讯】新德里消息：印度各大报都以显著地位刊载了我国周恩来总理关于朝鲜停战谈判问题的声明，以及金日成元帅和彭德怀将军关于交换病伤战俘并立即恢复朝鲜停战谈判的建议。
    “印度斯坦时报”四月一日就周总理声明发表评论说：这个建议提供了极其令人信服的证据，足以证明中国要求和平的真诚愿望。
    “自由新闻”在三月三十日的社论中说：金日成元帅及彭德怀将军的建议，给各国爱好和平人民以极大安慰，希望各参与朝鲜战争的国家真正采取消除世界紧张局势的态度。“孟买纪事报”三月三十日社论说：朝中方面建议打开了结束朝鲜战争的道路，是对和平的一个贡献。
    据印度新闻处新德里三月三十一日电报道，周总理声明在新德里官方人士中得到良好的反应，他们希望这个建议可以导向恢复谈判，以谋求一致同意的解决办法。
    印度出席联合国代表团团长潘迪特夫人三月三十一日在纽约说：中国关于朝鲜的和平建议“是非常令人鼓舞的”。她希望这个和平建议成为“解决朝鲜问题的开端”。
