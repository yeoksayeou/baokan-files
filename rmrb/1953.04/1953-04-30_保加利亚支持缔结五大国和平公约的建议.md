Title: Bulgaria Supports the Proposal for a Five-Power Peace Pact
Author:
Date: 1953-04-30
Page: 4
Xinhua News Agency, Sofia, April 28th: The Presidium of the National Assembly of the People's Republic of Bulgaria, the Council of Ministers, and the Standing Committee of the National Committee of the Fatherland Front held a joint meeting in Sofia on the 27th. The meeting issued a statement in response to a telegram from the International Committee of the World People's Peace Congress, which requested the Bulgarian government to support the proposal for a five-power peace pact.

The statement said: The Presidium of the National Assembly, the Council of Ministers, and the National Committee of the Bulgarian Fatherland Front, reflecting the Bulgarian people's desire to consolidate peace and strengthen international cooperation, believe that the maintenance of world peace depends to a very large extent on the five major powers—the United States, the Soviet Union, China, the United Kingdom, and France. Negotiations and the conclusion of a peace pact among the five major powers would eliminate international tension, thereby contributing to a policy of peaceful resolution of all international disputes.

The proposal to conclude a five-power peace pact is fully in line with the policy of the Bulgarian government. To express the Bulgarian people's desire for peaceful and friendly coexistence with all countries, the National Assembly of the People's Republic of Bulgaria and the National Committee of the Fatherland Front issued a special statement on February 6, 1953, expressing support for the resolutions of the World People's Peace Congress, including the call for a five-power peace pact. Six million people in Bulgaria have signed the World Peace Council's declaration. In 1951, the National Assembly passed the Law on the Defense of Peace.

The statement said: Efforts to defend peace are aimed at safeguarding the peaceful labor of the Bulgarian people in their great economic and cultural development work. This necessity determines the Bulgarian government's peace policy and the support of the entire Bulgarian people for the struggle for world peace.

### 保加利亚支持缔结五大国和平公约的建议

1953-04-30
第4版()
专栏：

    保加利亚支持缔结五大国和平公约的建议
    【新华社二十九日讯】据塔斯社索非亚二十八日讯：保加利亚人民共和国国民议会、部长会议和祖国阵线全国委员会常务委员会于二十七日在索非亚举行联席会议。会议发表声明，答复世界人民和平大会国际委员会的来电，这个电文要求保加利亚政府支持五大国缔结和平公约的主张。
    声明说：国民议会主席团、部长会议和保加利亚祖国阵线全国委员会反映保加利亚人民巩固和平、加强国际合作的愿望，认为世界和平能否维护，在非常大的程度上决定于五大国——美国、苏联、中国、英国、法国。五大国举行谈判并缔结和平公约，将消除国际紧张局势，从而有助于采取和平解决国际间所有争执问题的政策。
    缔结五大国和平公约的建议完全符合保加利亚政府的政策，保加利亚人民共和国国民议会、祖国阵线全国委员会为了表示保加利亚人民与各国和平友好相处的愿望，曾在一九五三年二月六日发表特别声明表示支持世界人民和平大会的决议，包括要求缔结五大国和平公约的呼吁在内。保加利亚有六百万人在世界和平理事会的宣言上签了名。一九五一年，国民议会曾通过和平保卫法。
    声明说：努力保卫和平，是为了保卫保加利亚人民在巨大的经济文化发展工作上的和平劳动，这种必要决定了保加利亚政府的和平政策，决定了全体保加利亚人民对于世界和平斗争的支持。
