Title: Pakistan's First Ambassador to China, Roza, Presents Credentials to Chairman Mao
Author:
Date: 1951-11-13
Page: 1

　　Pakistan's First Ambassador to China
    Roza Presents Credentials to Chairman Mao
    [Xinhua News Agency, December 12th] Pakistan's first Ambassador Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary to China, Nawabzada Agha Muhammad Raza, accompanied by Counselor Ahmed Ali and Third Secretary Mahbub Ul Alam, presented his credentials to Chairman Mao Zedong of the Central People's Government of China at 5:00 PM today. Present were Premier and Foreign Minister Zhou Enlai of the Central People's Government, Secretary-General Lin Boqu of the Central People's Government, Acting Chief of General Staff of the Chinese People's Liberation Army Nie Rongzhen, Director of the General Office of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs Wang Bingnan, Director of the Bureau of Ceremonies of the Central People's Government Yu Xinqing, Acting Director of the Asian Department of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs Chen Jiakang, and Chief of the Protocol Division Wang Zhuoru. Ambassador Roza delivered a eulogy to Chairman Mao Zedong when presenting his credentials, and Chairman Mao Zedong delivered a reply.
    Ambassador Roza's eulogy:
    Your Excellency:
    I consider it a great honor to present my credentials to Your Excellency today as the first Ambassador Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary of Pakistan to the People's Republic of China.
    The task entrusted to me by my government is to further consolidate the friendly relations that happily exist between our two countries. I am fully confident that, with Your Excellency's assistance and in cooperation with the officials of the Central People's Government of the People's Republic of China, I will be able to successfully carry out my task.
    For centuries, the people of Pakistan and the great people of China have maintained cultural and trade links. With the establishment of the Pakistani Embassy under my leadership, and the establishment of the Chinese Embassy in Karachi, I am confident that the relationship between the people of Pakistan and the people of China will become closer, and that the people of both countries will have greater and more opportunities to establish pleasant contacts in all aspects.
    In sending me as ambassador to your great country, the Government of Pakistan has also entrusted me with another pleasant task, which is to convey the congratulations of our government and people to Your Excellency on your health and happiness, as well as the congratulations to the people of the People's Republic of China on their happiness and prosperity.
    Chairman Mao's reply:
    Mr. Ambassador:
    I am very pleased to accept the credentials from His Majesty the King of Great Britain, Ireland and the British Overseas Dominions presented by your Ambassador, and thank you for your congratulations.
    There is a long-standing friendship between the peoples of China and Pakistan. Our two peoples share a common desire to promote the development of economic and cultural relations between the two countries and to work for lasting peace in Asia and the world. The establishment of diplomatic relations between China and Pakistan will notonly enhance and consolidate the friendship between the two peoples, but will also contribute to peace and stability in Asia and the world.
    I warmly welcome Your Excellency as the first Ambassador Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary of Pakistan to the People's Republic of China, and I am willing to assist Your Excellency in strengthening the friendly cooperation between the two countries.
    I wish your country prosperity and your people well, and I wish your head of state good health.
    Biography of Ambassador Roza
    [Xinhua News Agency, December 12th] Biography of Nawabzada Agha Muhammad Raza, Ambassador of Pakistan to China: General Roza graduated from the Royal Military Academy Sandhurst in 1927 and was immediately appointed an officer by the King of England. He participated in combat during World War II and was the commander of the 12th Division of the Pakistan Army before coming to China.

### 巴基斯坦首任驻我国大使罗查向毛主席呈递国书

1951-11-13
第1版()
专栏：

　　巴基斯坦首任驻我国大使
    罗查向毛主席呈递国书
    【新华社十二日讯】巴基斯坦首任驻我国特命全权大使那瓦查达·阿格哈·穆罕默德·罗查在今日下午五时偕参赞阿默德·艾礼、三等秘书马哈勃·乌尔·阿兰向我国中央人民政府毛泽东主席呈递国书。在场者有中央人民政府外交部部长周恩来、中央人民政府秘书长林伯渠、中国人民解放军代总参谋长聂荣臻、外交部办公厅主任王炳南、中央人民政府典礼局局长余心清、外交部亚洲司代司长陈家康、交际处处长王倬如。罗查大使在呈递国书时曾向毛泽东主席致颂词，毛泽东主席致答词。
    罗查大使颂词阁下：
    今天我作为巴基斯坦驻中华人民共和国首任特命全权大使向阁下呈递国书，我认为这是极大的荣幸。
    我国政府所付托给我的任务就是要进一步巩固贵我两国之间愉快地存在着的友好关系。我完全相信，在阁下的协助之下，和在中华人民共和国中央人民政府的官员合作之下，我将能够胜利地执行我的任务。
    几世纪以来，巴基斯坦人民和伟大的中国人民之间保持着文化和贸易上的联系。随着我所领导的巴基斯坦使馆的建立，以及中国使馆在喀喇蚩的建立，我深信巴基斯坦人民和中国人民彼此之间的关系将更为密切，并且两国人民将有更大和更多的机会来建立各方面的愉快联系。
    在派遣我出使伟大的贵国同时，巴基斯坦政府也付托给我另一项愉快的任务，就是转达我国政府和人民对阁下健康和幸福的祝贺，以及对中华人民共和国人民幸福和繁荣的祝贺。
    毛主席答词大使先生：
    我很高兴地接受贵大使呈递的大不列颠爱尔兰暨英国海外诸自治领国王的国书，并感谢贵大使的祝贺。
    中巴两国人民间存在着长期的友谊。在促进两国经济、文化关系的发展及争取亚洲及世界的持久和平上，贵我两国人民有着共同的愿望。中巴两国外交关系的建立，不但将使两国人民间的友谊日益增进与巩固，而且必将有助于亚洲及世界的和平与安定。
    我热烈地欢迎阁下出任巴基斯坦驻中华人民共和国首任特命全权大使，并愿对贵大使加强两国友好合作的工作，予以协助。
    谨祝贵国国家繁荣，人民兴旺，并祝贵国元首健康。
    罗查大使简历
    【新华社十二日讯】巴基斯坦驻我国大使那瓦查达·阿格哈·穆罕默德·罗查简历：罗查将军于一九二七年毕业于桑德赫斯特皇家军事学校，当即被英王任命为军官。二次世界大战中彼曾参加作战，来华前任巴基斯坦陆军第十二师师长。
