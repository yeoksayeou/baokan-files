Title: Chinese Cultural Delegation to India and Burma Arrives in Indian Capital, Warmly Welcomed
Author:
Date: 1951-11-01
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, New Delhi, October 31st. The Chinese Cultural Delegation to India and Burma, a group of fifteen people, accompanied by Ms. Laila Ramani Natu, Assistant Secretary of the Indian Ministry of External Affairs, and Mr. Bi Shuowang, First Secretary of the Chinese Embassy in India, arrived in New Delhi by plane from Calcutta on the afternoon of October 29th.

Those who welcomed the delegation at Delhi Airport included: Keskar, Deputy Minister of the Indian Ministry of External Affairs, Menon, Foreign Secretary of the Ministry of External Affairs, Panikkar, Indian Ambassador to China, Chopra, Director of the Protocol Division of the Ministry of External Affairs, Tara Chand, Secretary of the Ministry of Education, Mrs. Indira Gandhi, Dr. Sain, Vice-Chancellor of Delhi University, Professor Majeed, Vice-Chancellor of Delhi Muslim University, representatives of the Communist Party of India and Farooqi, Secretary of the Delhi Committee of the Communist Party of India, as well as representatives from the Delhi Peace Committee, the Delhi State Federation of Trade Unions, the Democratic Women's Federation, the Delhi Students' Union, the Democratic Youth Federation, and the India-China Friendship Association, and other party and mass organization representatives, as well as Barasanov, Counselor of the Soviet Embassy in India, Pokorny, Chargé d'Affaires of the Czechoslovak Embassy in India, and other foreign diplomats stationed in India.

General Yuan Zhongxian, the Chinese Ambassador to India, and representatives of the overseas Chinese community also came to the airport to welcome the delegation.

The Chinese Cultural Delegation stepped off the plane amidst the enthusiastic cheers of the welcomers shouting slogans such as "Long Live Chairman Mao Zedong!" and "Long Live India-China Friendship!" Ding Xilin, the head of the delegation, delivered a speech at the airport. He said: There is a long-standing friendly relationship between the people of India and China. He also said: We believe that the close cooperation and mutual encouragement between cultural workers of India and China will play a significant role in consolidating the friendship between our two peoples and safeguarding world peace.

### 我国赴印缅文化代表团抵印首都受到热烈欢迎

1951-11-01
第4版()
专栏：

    我国赴印缅文化代表团抵印首都受到热烈欢迎
    【新华社新德里三十一日电】中国赴印缅文化代表团一行十五人，由印度外交部助理秘书赖拉玛尼·纳都女士和中国驻印度大使馆一等秘书毕朔望陪同，在十月二十九日下午乘飞机从加尔各答到达新德里。
    到德里机场欢迎的有：印度外交部副部长凯斯卡、外交部外交秘书梅农、印度驻中国大使潘尼迦、外交部交际司司长乔普拉、教育部秘书塔拉·强特、印地拉·甘地夫人、德里大学副校长塞恩博士、德里回教大学副校长马杰伯教授、印度共产党的代表和印度共产党德里委员会书记法鲁基，以及德里和平委员会、德里邦总工会、民主妇女联合会、德里学生联合会、民主青年联合会和印中友好协会等党派和群众组织的代表，以及苏联驻印度大使馆参赞巴拉萨诺夫、捷克斯洛伐克驻印度大使馆代办波克尔尼和其他国家驻印度的外交官员。
    中国驻印度大使袁仲贤将军和华侨代表也到机场欢迎。
    中国文化代表团在欢迎者热烈欢呼“毛泽东主席万岁！”、“印中友好万岁！”的口号声中步下飞机。代表团团长丁西林在机场发表谈话。他说：印、中两国人民之间有着长期的友好关系。他并说：我们相信，印、中两国文化工作者的密切合作和互相鼓励，将在巩固我们两国人民的友谊和保卫世界和平方面起巨大的作用。
