Title: Chinese Cultural and Art Exhibition Grandly Opens in New Delhi
Author:
Date: 1951-11-13
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, New Delhi, 12th: The Chinese Cultural and Art Exhibition grandly opened in New Delhi, the capital of India, on the afternoon of the 4th. General Yuan Zhongxian, the Chinese Ambassador to India, presided over the opening ceremony. Those attending the opening ceremony included Prime Minister Nehru, senior Indian government officials, diplomatic envoys and personnel from various countries stationed in India, as well as Indian workers, students, artists, teachers, and staff. The Chinese Cultural Delegation, which is currently visiting India, also attended the opening ceremony. A huge national flag of the People's Republic of China was hung in front of the venue, adorned with excerpts from Chairman Mao's congratulatory message at the celebration held by the Indian Ambassador to China to commemorate the first anniversary of the Republic of India. The Chinese and Indian national flags were hung in the venue, symbolizing the friendship between the Chinese and Indian people.

In his opening speech, Chinese Ambassador to India Yuan Zhongxian said: We hope to introduce the way of life of the Chinese people and the achievements of the Chinese People's Government to the Indian people, with whom we have had long-standing historical ties, through this exhibition.

Indian Prime Minister Nehru said in his speech that he warmly hoped to see the Chinese paintings and mural reproductions, not only because they were beautiful, but also because they reminded him of the long historical period of contact between India and China. Nehru said: This exhibition will contribute to establishing a more complete mutual understanding between India and China and to establishing cooperation in every possible area.

Ding Xilin, head of the Chinese Cultural Delegation, introduced the exhibits. The exhibits include three parts. The first part is a reproduction of the Dunhuang murals with a history of 1,400 years, the second part is woodcuts and photographs introducing the situation of New China, and the third part is early Chinese handicrafts from the fifth century BC, as well as a small number of paintings by modern Chinese artists.

The Chinese Cultural and Art Exhibition has been praised by the audience. Those exhibits that demonstrate the great achievements of New China in the two years since its founding have particularly aroused the interest of the audience.

### 我国文化艺术展览会在新德里隆重开幕

1951-11-13
第4版()
专栏：

　　我国文化艺术展览会
    在新德里隆重开幕
    【新华社新德里十二日电】中国文化艺术展览会四日下午在印度首都新德里隆重开幕。展览会由我国驻印度大使袁仲贤将军主持。参加开幕典礼的有尼赫鲁总理及印度政府高级官员、各国驻印度的使节及外交人员以及印度工人、学生、艺术家、教师和职员等。正在访问印度的中国文化代表团也参加了开幕典礼。会场门前挂着巨幅的中华人民共和国国旗，并饰着毛主席在印度驻华大使馆为纪念印度共和国一周年举行的庆祝会上的祝词摘录。会场上并悬挂着中国国旗和印度国旗，象征着中印两国人民的友好。
    我国驻印大使袁仲贤在致开幕词中说：我们希望通过这个展览会向和我们有历史悠久的联系的印度人民介绍中国人民的生活方式和中国人民政府的成就。
    印度总理尼赫鲁致词说：他热烈希望看到中国的绘画和壁画复制品，这不仅因为它们是美的东西，而且因为它们使他回想到印度和中国接触的久远的历史时期。尼赫鲁说：这个展览对于建立印、中两国更完满的相互了解和在一切可能的地方建立合作必将有所贡献。
    中国文化代表团丁西林团长对展览品作了介绍。展览品包括三部分。第一部分是有一千四百年历史的敦煌壁画的复制品，第二部分是介绍新中国情况的木刻和摄影，第三部分是公元前五世纪的中国早期的手工艺品，以及少量近代中国艺术家的绘画。
    中国文化艺术展览会受到观众的赞扬。那些表现新中国成立两年以来的巨大成就的展览品，特别引起观众的兴趣。
