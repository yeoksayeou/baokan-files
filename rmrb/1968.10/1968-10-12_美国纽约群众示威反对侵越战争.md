Title: Crowds in New York Protest U.S. Aggression in Vietnam
Author:
Date: 1968-10-12
Edition: 5

About eight hundred people in New York City held a demonstration outside a hotel where U.S. Vice President Hubert Humphrey was conducting activities. The demonstrators held up signs opposing U.S. aggression in Vietnam and waved flags of the National Liberation Front of South Vietnam.

The reactionary ruling clique of the United States dispatched a large number of police to guard the outside of the hotel and sent mounted police to charge at the demonstrators, carrying out brutal suppression and mass arrests. The demonstrators bravely fought back against the mounted police in fierce struggles.

### 美国纽约群众示威反对侵越战争

1968-10-12
第5版()
专栏：

    美国纽约群众示威反对侵越战争
    新华社十日讯　纽约消息：美国纽约市约八百人九日晚高举反对美国侵越战争的标语牌和越南南方民族解放阵线的旗帜，在美国副总统汉弗莱进行活动的旅馆外面举行示威。
    美国反动统治集团派了大批警察守卫在这家旅馆的外面，并出动骑警冲向示威群众，进行残酷的镇压和大规模逮捕。示威群众勇敢地和骑警进行了激烈的搏斗。
