Title: U.S. "Challenge" Weekly Points Out that Infighting Within the U.S. Ruling Clique Reflects U.S. Failure in South Vietnam; Washington Argues Only About the Means of Carrying Out Aggression
Author: 
Date: 1966-03-07
Edition: 4

    U.S. "Challenge" Weekly Points Out that Infighting Within the U.S. Ruling Clique Reflects U.S. Failure in South Vietnam
    Washington Argues Only About the Means of Carrying Out Aggression
    The Argument Must Not Be Used to Divert the People's Struggle Against the War of Aggression Against Vietnam
    Xinhua News Agency, March 6th -- New York Dispatch: The U.S. Progressive Labor Party's organ, the "Challenge" Weekly, published an article on March 2nd stating that the current "increasingly fierce and unprecedented" infighting within the U.S. ruling clique over the policy of aggression against Vietnam is occurring against the backdrop of the continuous failure of the U.S. war of aggression against Vietnam and the bankruptcy of "peace" conspiracies; it is a "direct reflection within the U.S. ruling group of the fact that the United States is sinking deeper and deeper into the quagmire in Vietnam."
    The article wrote, "This is a debate that has broken party lines, causing divisions within both the Democratic and Republican parties. Moreover, this debate has also exposed the personal political ambitions and hopes of the 'opposition' toward the 'ruling party'. Robert Kennedy, as well as many Republicans waiting in the wings, still remember that Eisenhower once reversed the election situation by promising to end the war in Korea and was elected president in 1952."
    However, the article emphasized, "This debate is by no means an argument involving fundamental goals. It is a debate between people who basically belong to the same group. Among those who criticize the government from within the ruling group (including congressmen, retired generals, and famous reporters), no one has suggested that the United States sincerely and unconditionally withdraw from Vietnam. The debate is strictly limited to what is the best long-term global strategy for U.S. imperialism to pursue."
    The article continued, "The Fulbright-Kennedy-Kennan-Gavin faction of dissenters believes that the government's rashly escalating path will endanger the entire global strategy of the United States. Kennan believes that Washington's increasingly deep involvement in Vietnam is very detrimental to America's 'global' policy and is a 'great imbalance in American policy'. General Gavin, the main advocate of the 'hold' isolated strongholds theory, believes that the United States' 'over-involvement' in Vietnam will weaken America's 'entire strategic position' and thus affect America's other global 'obligations'."
    The article emphasized, "In this quarrel that is shaking Washington, only the means and methods are involved, not the goals of the U.S. counter-revolutionary global strategy. Pointing this out is not to diminish the importance of this dispute. These differences must be properly understood and used to seek the true interests of the people of Vietnam and the United States. This difference must not be used to divert the people's struggle against the Vietnam War, leading this struggle away from the only true solution to the Vietnam problem - the immediate withdrawal of the United States from Vietnam!"
    The article said, "The reason why this division has developed is, first of all, because the Vietnamese people are defeating the U.S. aggressors through unparalleled heroic and skillful combat, and also because the American people are launching a great encirclement movement against those who would tarnish their reputation in this most dirty imperialist war."
    The article said, "Expand the cracks in the U.S. imperialist front; stop the war-makers; support the revolutionary war of the Vietnamese people for freedom; save our young people from the disaster of imperialist war; carry out the most enterprising and combative activities to make the United States withdraw from Vietnam, now is the time!"

### 美《挑战》周报指出美统治集团内部争吵反映了美国在南越失败  华盛顿争吵的只是推行侵略的方式

1966-03-07
第4版()
专栏：

    美《挑战》周报指出美统治集团内部争吵反映了美国在南越失败
    华盛顿争吵的只是推行侵略的方式
    决不能让争吵被利用来转移人民反对侵越的斗争
    新华社六日讯　纽约消息：美国进步劳工党机关报《挑战》周报在三月二日一期上刊登一篇文章指出，当前美国统治集团内部围绕侵越政策所爆发的“日益激烈和规模空前的”争吵，是在美国侵越战争不断遭到失败，“和平”阴谋又遭到破产的背景下发生的；它是“美国在越南陷入越来越深的泥沼这一事实在美国统治集团中的直接反映”。
    文章写道，“这是一场打破了党派界线的辩论，在民主党和共和党内部都造成了分裂。而且，通过这场辩论也暴露了‘在野派’对‘在朝派’所怀的个人政治野心和希望。罗伯特·肯尼迪以及不少满怀希望等候在一旁的共和党人都还记得：当年艾森豪威尔曾由于答应去朝鲜结束那场战争而扭转了选举局势，在一九五二年当选为总统。”
    但是，文章强调，“这场辩论决不是一场涉及根本目标的争论。这是在基本上属于同一个班子的人们之间进行的辩论。在那些从统治集团内部批评政府的人（包括议员、退伍将军和名记者等）当中，没有任何人建议美国真心实意地无条件地撤出越南。争论严格限于什么是美帝国主义推行长期性全球战略的上策。”
    文章接着说，“富布赖特—肯尼迪—凯南—加文这一派持反对意见的人认为政府轻率地在逐步升级的道路上走下去，会危及美国整个全球战略。凯南认为华盛顿越来越深地陷入越南对美国的‘世界性’政策非常不利，是‘美国政策大大失去平衡’。‘据守’孤立据点理论的主要鼓吹者加文将军认为，美国‘过度陷入’越南会削弱美国的‘整个战略地位’，并从而将影响美国其他的全球性‘义务’。”
    文章强调说，“在震撼华盛顿的这场争吵中，所涉及的只是方式和方法，而不是美国反革命全球战略的目标。指出这一点，并不是要缩小这次争论的重要意义。对这种分歧必须有恰如其分的了解，并利用它们来谋求越南和美国人民的真正利益。决不能让这种分歧被利用来转移人民反对越南战争的斗争，使这个斗争离开越南问题唯一真正的解决办法——美国立即撤出越南！”
    文章说，“这种分歧之所以发展，首先是由于越南人民通过无比英勇和巧妙的战斗正在打败美国侵略者，而且也由于美国人民正在掀起一个伟大的包围运动，反对那些会在这场最肮脏的帝国主义战争中玷污他们名声的人。”
    文章说，“扩大美帝国主义阵线上的裂缝；制止战争制造者；支持越南人民争取自由的革命战争；把我们的年轻人从帝国主义战争的灾难中拯救出来；开展最富有进取性和战斗性的活动，使美国撤出越南，现在是时候了！”
