Title: Returning Medals and Badges in Protest; American Veterans Demonstrate in Front of Humphrey's Home to Protest U.S. Aggression Against Vietnam; Chicago Youth Demonstrate Demanding an End to the Johnson Administration's Bloody War of Aggression Against Vietnam
Author:
Date: 1966-03-21
Edition: 3

    Returning Medals and Badges in Protest
    American Veterans Demonstrate in Front of Humphrey's Home to Protest U.S. Aggression Against Vietnam
    Chicago Youth Demonstrate Demanding an End to the Johnson Administration's Bloody War of Aggression Against Vietnam
    Xinhua News Agency, 20th – Washington dispatch: A group of American veterans and reservists demonstrated in front of U.S. Vice President Humphrey's residence in suburban Maryland on the 19th, protesting the U.S. government's aggression against Vietnam.
    The demonstrators – the "Vietnam Veterans and Reservists Against the War Organization" – demanded that their medals, ribbons, discharge papers, and retirement certificates be returned to Humphrey, and that Humphrey hand these over to President Johnson to express their protest against the war of aggression against Vietnam. However, Humphrey did not meet with them.
    Members of this organization demonstrated in front of the White House on February 5th and left these badges at the White House, but no one accepted them.
    Because Johnson and Humphrey dared not meet with the demonstrators and accept these badges, a spokesman for the organization said that the organization would burn these badges in Federal Plaza in New York at the end of a vigil demonstration scheduled for March 25.
    According to Xinhua News Agency – Washington dispatch: More than 50 American youths held a demonstration on the 15th when U.S. Vice President Humphrey came to Chicago to attend a conference, shouting slogans: "Get out of Vietnam," "Bring the American soldiers home."
    Humphrey was in Chicago to attend the National Higher Education Conference. The demonstrators gathered outside the venue to hold a demonstration.
    The demonstrators held signs that read: "Stop Johnson's bloody war," "Withdraw immediately," "Democrats and Republicans both want war." (Attached picture)

### 退还奖章证章以抗议  美国侵略越南美退伍军人在汉弗莱家门口示威  芝加哥青年示威要求停止约翰逊政府血腥的侵越战争

1966-03-21
第3版()
专栏：

    退还奖章证章以抗议
    美国侵略越南美退伍军人在汉弗莱家门口示威
    芝加哥青年示威要求停止约翰逊政府血腥的侵越战争
    新华社二十日讯　华盛顿消息：美国一批退伍军人和后备役军人十九日在马里兰市郊美国副总统汉弗莱的住宅前举行示威，抗议美国政府侵略越南。
    示威者——“退伍军人和后备役军人争取结束越南战争组织”——要求把他们的奖章、功勋带、退伍证和退役证退给汉弗莱，并要汉弗莱把这些东西交给约翰逊总统，以表示他们对侵越战争的抗议。但是汉弗莱没有接见他们。
    这个组织的成员曾经在二月五日在白宫前示威，并把这些证章留在白宫，但是没有人接受。
    由于约翰逊和汉弗莱都不敢会见示威者和接受这些证章，这个组织的发言人说，这个组织将在定于三月二十五日举行的守夜示威结束时在纽约联邦广场上烧毁这些证章。
    据新华社讯　华盛顿消息：美国青年五十多人十五日在美国副总统汉弗莱到芝加哥出席一个会议时，举行示威，高呼口号：“退出越南”，“让美国兵回国”。
    汉弗莱是到芝加哥参加全国高等教育会议。示威者聚集在会场外面举行了示威。
    示威者举着标语牌，上面写着：“停止约翰逊的血腥战争”，“马上撤退”、“民主党人和共和党人都是要战争的”。（附图片）
