Title: What is the Essence of the North Atlantic Group's "Integration"?
Author: Jun
Date: 1966-03-16
Edition: 4

    What is the Essence of the North Atlantic Group's "Integration"?
    The North Atlantic Group is a tool for the United States to control its Western European "allies." "Integration" is a means for the United States to establish a dominant position over its "allies" in the military. The main contents of "integration" are:
    1.  Unified organizational structure. For example, there are numerous institutions such as the Council of Ministers, the Council of Permanent Representatives, the Standing Committee of the Council, the International Secretariat, the Military Committee, the Military Representatives Committee, and standing groups. They deliberate on major policy issues related to military, political, and other matters, and handle daily affairs. In name, all resolutions must be passed unanimously, but in reality, they are subject to the will of the United States.
    2.  Unified military command system. For example, there is the Supreme Headquarters Allied Powers Europe (Commander: American General Lemnitzer, under which are four regional Allied commands: Northern Europe, Central Europe, Southern Europe, and the Mediterranean), the Supreme Allied Commander Atlantic (Commander: American Admiral Smith), the Allied Commander-in-Chief Channel (Commander: British Admiral Bingley), and the Canada-United States Regional Planning Group (Commander: American General Partridge). Although a few "allied" generals are used as decorations, the orders are entirely issued by the United States.
    3.  Formulate common strategic principles. In essence, it is to concoct a European strategy according to the American "global strategy." No wonder De Gaulle said that "Europe's strategy within the North Atlantic Organization is America's strategy."
    4.  Each member state must submit troops to the North Atlantic Group. Among them, West Germany and Luxembourg handed over all their forces, and other countries handed over a portion. Since most of the commanders are Americans, the United States has command over these troops.
    5.  Jointly build military bases and logistics facilities. This is conducive to the United States wantonly infringing upon the territorial sovereignty of its "allies," stationing troops, establishing bases, and shifting military burdens.
    6.  Conduct joint military exercises. Every year, the United States drives the troops of the North Atlantic Group to conduct multiple exercises of different natures in different regions, targeting socialist countries as hypothetical enemies, such as nuclear strikes, landings, airborne operations, anti-submarine warfare, and escort, in order to strengthen military expansion and preparations for war, and create tension.
    From the above, it can be seen that "integration" essentially sacrifices the independence of "allies" to serve the American "global strategy." (Jun)

### 北大西洋集团“一体化”的实质是什么

1966-03-16
第4版()
专栏：国际知识

    北大西洋集团“一体化”的实质是什么
    北大西洋集团是美国控制西欧“盟国”的工具。“一体化”则是美国在军事上对“盟国”建立统治地位的手段。“一体化”的主要内容是：
    一、统一组织机构。如设有部长理事会、常任代表理事会、常设的理事会委员会、国际秘书处、军事委员会、军事代表委员会和常设小组等名目繁多的机构。由它们审议有关军事、政治等重大政策性问题和办理日常事务。名义上各项决议必须经全体一致通过，实际上却以美国的意志为转移。
    二、统一军事指挥系统。如设有欧洲盟军最高司令部（司令：美国兰尼兹尔上将，下辖北欧、中欧、南欧和地中海四个地区性盟军司令部）、大西洋盟军最高司令部（司令：美国史密斯海军上将）、英吉利海峡盟军司令部（司令：英国宾莱海军上将）和美国加拿大地区计划委员会（司令：美国帕特里基上将）。虽有少数“盟国”将领作点缀品，但完全由美国发号施令。
    三、制订共同的战略原则。实质上是按美国的“全球战略”来炮制欧洲战略。难怪戴高乐说“欧洲的战略在北大西洋组织内就是美国的战略”。
    四、各成员国必须向北大西洋集团提交军队。其中西德、卢森堡交出了全部兵力，其他国家交出了一部分。由于司令大部分由美国人担任，美国就拥有对这些军队的指挥权。
    五、共同筹建军事基地和后勤设施。这便有利于美国肆意侵犯“盟国”领土主权、派驻军队、建立基地和转嫁军事负担。
    六、共同进行军事演习。每年美国都要驱使北大西洋集团的军队在不同地区举行多次以社会主义国家为假想敌的、不同性质的演习，诸如核突击、登陆、空降、反潜艇、护航等，借以加强扩军备战，制造紧张局势。
    从以上可以看出，“一体化”实质上是牺牲“盟国”独立，为美国“全球战略”服务的。　（君）
