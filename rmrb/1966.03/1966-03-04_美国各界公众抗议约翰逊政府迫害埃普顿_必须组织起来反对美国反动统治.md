Title: Public Figures From All Walks of Life in the U.S. Protest Johnson Government's Persecution of Epton; Must Organize to Oppose the U.S. Reactionary Rule
Author:
Date: 1966-03-04
Edition: 4

    (Our Dispatch) The fascist crimes of the Johnson government in persecuting American Black leader and Vice Chairman of the Progressive Labor Party, Epton, have aroused the anger of the broad masses of the American public. According to reports in the Progressive Labor Party's organ, the *Challenge* weekly, some public figures in the U.S. recently held a rally in New York in support of Epton.

    At the meeting, many people spoke out to condemn the persecution and trial of Epton by the reactionary ruling authorities in the U.S.

    Jeff Gordon, a representative of the May 2nd Movement, one of the organizers of the anti-war movement of the American people, said that the U.S. "ruling class knew what they were doing when they tried Epton. Epton is an openly revolutionary Communist. They don't like him, because if his plan succeeds, they will no longer be able to manage things."

    Felipes De Jesús, an editor of the *Challenge* weekly, vehemently condemned the aggression crimes of U.S. imperialism at the meeting. He said, "The anti-imperialist forces of the world have shattered the myth of U.S. imperialism's omnipotence. U.S. imperialism has been beaten in Vietnam and the Dominican Republic." "American Black and Latin American people striving for a better life are fighting a common enemy – U.S. capitalism, the Latin American oligarchy, and U.S. imperialism. As long as capitalism exists, there will be no equality. Capitalism is the root of all the suffering of the world's people."

    Stanley Aronowitz, a union organizer, said at the meeting, "The lesson that the Epton case gives us is that they want to stop those who demand to organize against the system. Defending Epton is the primary responsibility of all people with progressive ideas. We have a responsibility to ensure that Epton is freed."

    Hugh Schwartz, a representative of the National Coordinating Committee to End the War in Vietnam, said, "Johnson is trying to equate the violence in Watts with the violence of the Ku Klux Klan." "We must begin to organize ourselves against the real enemy. Our real enemy is not the small shopkeepers in Watts, but the Rockefellers who manipulate everything." "Epton is a serious revolutionary, so they opened fire on him."

### 美国各界公众抗议约翰逊政府迫害埃普顿  必须组织起来反对美国反动统治

1966-03-04
第4版()
专栏：

    美国各界公众抗议约翰逊政府迫害埃普顿
    必须组织起来反对美国反动统治
    本报讯　约翰逊政府迫害美国黑人领袖、进步劳工党副主席埃普顿的法西斯罪行，激起了美国广大公众的愤怒抗议。据美国进步劳工党机关刊物《挑战》周报报道，美国一些公众人士不久以前在纽约举行了一次支持埃普顿的集会。
    在会上许多人纷纷发言谴责美国反动统治当局对埃普顿的迫害和审讯。
    美国人民反战运动组织者之一——“五月二日运动”的代表杰夫·戈登说，美国“统治阶级审讯埃普顿的时候，他们是知道自己在干什么的。埃普顿是一个公开主张革命的共产党人。他们不欢喜他，因为如果他的计划成功，他们就不再能够管理事情了”。
    《挑战》周报的一位编辑费利佩斯·德赫苏斯在会上痛斥美帝国主义的侵略罪行。他说，“世界的反帝力量已经粉碎了美帝国主义无所不能的这个神话。美帝国主义在越南和多米尼加挨了打”。“争取较好生活的美国黑人和拉丁美洲人民反对着共同的敌人——美国资本主义、拉丁美洲的寡头政治和美帝国主义。只要资本主义存在就不会有平等。资本主义是世界人民所受的一切苦难的根源”。
    一位工会组织者斯坦利·阿罗诺维茨在会上说，“埃普顿案件所给予我们的教训是：他们要制止那些要求组织起来反对制度的人。保卫埃普顿是一切具有进步思想的人的主要责任。我们有责任确使埃普顿获得自由”。
    “结束越南战争全国协调委员会”的一位代表修·施瓦茨说，“约翰逊企图把瓦茨区的暴力行动与三K党的暴力行动等同起来”。“我们必须开始把自己组织起来反对真正的敌人。我们的真正的敌人不是瓦茨区的小店主，而是操纵一切的洛克菲勒”。“埃普顿是一个严肃的革命者，因此他们向他开火”。
