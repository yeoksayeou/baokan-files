Title: Dominicans Demonstrate, Shouting "Yankees Get Out!" U.S. Magazine Admits America Is in Crisis in Dominican Republic
Author:
Date: 1966-03-01
Edition: 4

    Xinhua News Agency, Santo Domingo, 28th -- News from Santo Domingo: Several thousand residents of Santo Domingo, the capital of the Dominican Republic, held a demonstration in the city center on the 27th, commemorating the 122nd anniversary of the founding of the Dominican Republic and protesting the American military aggression and intervention in the Dominican Republic.
    The angry demonstrators burned an American flag and shouted, "Yankees, get out of the Dominican Republic!" They also distributed leaflets calling for the U.S.-controlled "Inter-American Force" to withdraw from the Dominican Republic.
    Before the demonstration, patriotic masses laid wreaths at Independence Square to honor the heroes who sacrificed their lives in the war of independence.
    U.S. occupation troops and reactionary police tried to disperse the demonstrating masses, but more and more patriotic residents took to the streets to protest U.S. intervention.
    On the 25th, patriotic students in Santo Domingo held a demonstration in the downtown area, demanding that the American aggressor troops get out of the Dominican Republic. The Dominican Republic had long suffered Spanish colonial rule and was once occupied by neighboring Haiti. On February 27, 1844, the Dominican Republic gained independence, but it was soon subjected to constant aggression and armed intervention by U.S. imperialism. Since the U.S. military invaded and interfered in the Dominican Republic last April, the flames of the Dominican people's anti-American patriotic struggle have never ceased.
    This newspaper reports: The February 28th issue of *U.S. News & World Report* exclaimed, "America has fallen into another crisis in the Dominican Republic." This American magazine wrote: "It has been ten months since American troops entered the Dominican Republic to prevent a communist takeover (referring to the Dominican people's patriotic armed uprising last April). It has also been five months since the interim government, backed by 7,000 American troops, took office. Clearly, the crisis here is only temporarily suspended and not over. The leaders of the revolution still hold power and can start new trouble if they wish."
    Referring to the recent situation in the Dominican Republic, the magazine wrote uneasily: "The most recent crisis began on February 9 when a group of students gathered in front of the National Palace. Government troops opened fire on the demonstrating students, killing two. The next day, a general strike began. The strike paralyzed the country for a week. Almost all work—governmental and private—came to a standstill. Shops closed, traffic was interrupted, and crops awaiting harvest rotted in the fields. The capital became a ghost town." It had to admit that this nationwide strike rendered U.S. imperialism and its henchmen, the "Inter-American Force," "powerless."
    The American magazine also worried that due to the instability of the Dominican situation, the large amount of investment made by U.S. monopoly capital in the Dominican Republic since last April "appears to be threatened." (Attached picture)

### 多米尼加人民示威高呼美国佬滚出去  美刊承认美国在多米尼加陷入了危机

1966-03-01
第4版()
专栏：

    多米尼加人民示威高呼美国佬滚出去
    美刊承认美国在多米尼加陷入了危机
    新华社二十八日讯　圣多明各消息：多米尼加首都圣多明各数千居民二十七日在市中心举行示威游行，纪念多米尼加共和国成立一百二十二周年，抗议美国对多米尼加的军事侵略和干涉。
    愤怒的示威群众烧毁了一面美国国旗，并且高呼：“美国佬，滚出多米尼加！”他们还散发传单，要美国控制的“泛美部队”撤出多米尼加。
    在示威以前，爱国群众在独立广场向在独立战争中牺牲的英雄们献了花圈。
    美国占领军和反动警察企图驱散示威的群众，但是越来越多的爱国居民走上街头，抗议美国的干涉。
    在二十五日，圣多明各爱国学生曾经在闹市区举行示威，要美国侵略军滚出多米尼加。多米尼加曾经长期遭受西班牙殖民统治，并且一度被邻国海地侵占。一八四四年二月二十七日，多米尼加取得独立，但不久就受到美帝国主义的不断侵略和武力干涉。从去年四月美国军队入侵干涉多米尼加的局势以后，多米尼加人民反美爱国斗争的烈火一直没有停止。
    本报讯　二月二十八日出版的《美国新闻与世界报道》惊呼：“美国已在多米尼加陷入了另一个危机之中”。这家美国杂志写道：“自从美国军队开入多米尼加防止共产党接管（按指多米尼加人民去年四月的爱国武装起义）以来，已经十个月了。在七千名美国军队支持下的临时政府也就职五个月了。显然，这里的危机，仅仅是暂时停止而没有结束。革命的领袖们仍然握有权力，如果愿意的话，可以开始新的麻烦。”
    这家杂志在谈到多米尼加最近的局势时不安地写道：“最近一次危机是从二月九日一群学生在国民宫前集会开始的。政府军队向示威学生开枪，杀死了两人。第二天大罢工开始了。罢工使全国瘫痪一周之久。几乎所有的工作——政府的和私人的——都停顿了。商店关门，交通中断，等待收割的农作物在地里烂掉。首都成了一个鬼域。”它不得不承认，这次席卷全国的罢工，使美帝国主义和它的帮凶“泛美部队感到无能为力。”
    这家美国杂志还忧心忡忡地表示，由于多米尼加局势的动荡不定，美国垄断资本从去年四月以来在多米尼加的大量投资，“看来都受到了威胁。”（附图片）
