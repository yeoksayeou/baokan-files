    Slip of the Tongue
    Japanese Sato government's roving envoy, Yokoyama, said in Paris:
  "I personally believe that the South Vietnamese government is nothing more than a puppet government of the United States." Yokoyama's statement greatly embarrassed the Sato government. Foreign Minister Shiina immediately issued instructions for him to "pay close attention to his words and actions."
    Yokoyama was originally on a mission to peddle the US's peace talks conspiracy. Unexpectedly, he inadvertently let slip the truth. This not only exposed the sores of the Johnson administration but also, in effect, undermined the Sato government's wholehearted effort to serve the US's policy of aggression against Vietnam. No wonder the Japanese Foreign Ministry felt so uneasy.

### 失言

1966-03-10
第4版()
专栏：三言两语

    失言
    日本佐藤政府的巡回特使横山在巴黎发表谈话说：
  “我个人认为南越政府不过是美国的傀儡政府而已”。横山的这一句话使得佐藤政府大为狼狈。外相椎名马上发出指示，要他“充分注意自己的言行”。
    横山本来肩负着为美国兜售和谈阴谋的使命出外进行活动的。不料他一时不慎说溜了口，讲了实话。这不仅揭了约翰逊政府的疮疤，而且实际上拆了一心要为美国侵越政策效劳的佐藤政府的台。难怪日本外务省如此感到不安了。
