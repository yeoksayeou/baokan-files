Title: American Black Leader Epton Sends Letter from Prison to Comrades Calling for Resolute Arming with Marxism-Leninism to Struggle for the Liberation of Black People and the Realization of Socialism
Author:
Date: 1966-03-16
Edition: 4

    Xinhua News Agency, New York, 14th -- New York News: William Epton, Vice Chairman of the American Progressive Labor Party and Black leader, who was illegally sentenced by the American authorities at the end of January this year, called on his comrades in an open letter from prison to maintain initiative and carry out struggles in Black communities, university campuses, workshops, and factories.
    An excerpt of this open letter was published in a recent issue of the New York bi-weekly, *Challenge*. The letter said, "I am being held in solitary confinement; I suspect this is because I am seen as a 'dangerous element' by this system. Of course, we all know that I am being isolated because my ideas are dangerous to the ruling class."
      "Anyone who directly sees the conditions in prison and the situation of prisoners will increasingly deeply appreciate the viciousness of the American ruling group.
      "Of course, the majority of prisoners are Black and Puerto Rican; for many of them, the only reason they are in prison is because they are poor, and many have directly confronted the government authorities; once the time comes, these people will stand at the forefront of the struggle.
    "We are very fortunate because we have weapons like Marxism-Leninism that enable us to maintain the strength and determination to win victory. I ask all of us to firmly arm ourselves with this science, because in the final analysis, this science will liberate us and enable us to win victory." The letter reminded his comrades not to delay other work in order to rescue him. "We must be careful not to let this happen. The Progressive Labor Party must not turn itself into a 'Defend Epton Committee'."
    "We must maintain the initiative in every possible situation and not fall into the situation of having to struggle under the conditions created by the ruling class. And the initiative exists in Black communities, university campuses, workshops, and factories." The letter concluded, "In the struggle to liberate Black people and the American working class and to realize socialism, we will surely win!"

### 美国黑人领袖埃普顿从狱中寄书战友发出号召  坚决用马克思列宁主义武装起来  为解放黑人实现社会主义而斗争

1966-03-16
第4版()
专栏：

    美国黑人领袖埃普顿从狱中寄书战友发出号召
    坚决用马克思列宁主义武装起来
    为解放黑人实现社会主义而斗争
    新华社十四日讯　纽约消息：今年一月底被美国当局非法判刑的美国进步劳工党副主席、黑人领袖威廉·埃普顿在狱中给他的同志们的一封公开信中，号召他的战友们在黑人区、大学校园、车间和工厂保持主动，开展斗争。
    不久前出版的一期纽约《挑战》双周报刊登了这封公开信的摘要。信中说，“我是被隔离监禁的；据我猜想，这是因为我被这个制度看做是‘危险分子’。当然，我们大家都知道，我之所以被隔离，是因为对于统治阶级来说，我的思想是危险的。
      “谁要是直接看到了监狱中的条件和坐牢人的状况，他就会越来越深刻地体会到美国统治集团的恶毒。
      “当然，大多数囚徒是黑人和波多黎各人；就他们之中的许多人来说，他们所以坐牢的唯一原因是因为他们穷，而且许多人曾经同政府当局直接对抗；一旦时机到来，这些人将站在斗争的最前线。
    “我们十分幸运，因为我们有马克思—列宁主义这样的武器使我们保持赢得胜利的力量和决心。我要求我们大家都来用这门科学把我们自己坚定地武装起来，因为归根到底，这门科学将使我们得到解放和使我们赢得胜利。”信中提醒他的战友们不要因为营救他而把其他工作耽搁下来。“我们千万要注意不要让这种情况出现。进步劳工党切不可使自己变成‘保卫埃普顿委员会’。”
    “我们必须在一切可能的情况下保持主动，不要陷入那种不得不在统治阶级所制造的条件下进行挣扎的局面。而主动权存在于黑人区，大学校园里，车间和工厂里。”这封信最后说，“在解放黑人和美国工人阶级、实现社会主义的斗争中，我们必胜！”
