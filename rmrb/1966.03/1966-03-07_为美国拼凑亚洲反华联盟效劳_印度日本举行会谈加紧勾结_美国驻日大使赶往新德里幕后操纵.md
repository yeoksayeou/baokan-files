Title: Serving the US in Assembling an Anti-China Alliance in Asia: India and Japan Hold Talks to Intensify Collusion; US Ambassador to Japan Rushes to New Delhi for Behind-the-Scenes Manipulation
Author:
Date: 1966-03-07
Edition: 3

    (Xinhua News Agency, New Delhi, March 5th) Following two days of meetings in New Delhi, the official delegations from India and Japan concluded their talks on the 4th.
    The representatives from the two countries participating in this meeting were Indian Foreign Secretary C.S. Jha and Japanese Vice Minister of Foreign Affairs Nobuhiko Ushiba. Indian Foreign Minister Swaran Singh also attended the first meeting on the 3rd.
    The main issue in these talks between India and Japan was a conspiracy to strengthen anti-China activities, in coordination with the US plot to "contain" China. According to reports in the *Indian Express* on the 4th and 5th, during the afternoon talks on the 3rd, "Swaran Singh and Ushiba both emphasized the need for India and Japan to have closer ties in order to formulate a unified plan of action regarding issues in the Asian region when necessary." Jha "explained China's aggressive posture towards Southeast Asia, especially towards India." In the talks on the 4th, Ushiba discussed with Jha the Sato government's hostile "Two Chinas" conspiracy against China. Ushiba said that "Japan is also worried about China's ideological activities," and wants India to publicly "recognize Taiwan as an independent entity of China." It was reported that Jha and Ushiba also discussed the Vietnam issue.
    According to reports, this India-Japan meeting was conducted under the behind-the-scenes manipulation of the United States. According to United Press International on the 3rd, US Ambassador to Japan Reischauer rushed to New Delhi for a "visit" during the India-Japan talks, which "proves that the United States is concerned about the issue of strengthened cooperation between India and Japan." The Japanese newspaper *Asahi Shimbun* reported earlier that in the US's efforts to assemble an anti-China "alliance" in Asia, Japan and India are becoming the "center" of the front that the United States expects.

### 为美国拼凑亚洲反华联盟效劳  印度日本举行会谈加紧勾结  美国驻日大使赶往新德里幕后操纵

1966-03-07
第3版()
专栏：

    为美国拼凑亚洲反华联盟效劳
    印度日本举行会谈加紧勾结
    美国驻日大使赶往新德里幕后操纵
    据新华社五日讯　新德里消息：印度和日本官方代表团在新德里举行了两天会议后，四日已经结束。
    参加这次会议的两国代表是印度外交秘书杰哈和日本外务省次官牛场信彦。印度外交部长斯瓦兰·辛格也参加了三日举行的首次会议。
    印度和日本的这次会谈的主要问题是共谋加强反华活动，以配合美国的“遏制”中国的阴谋。据《印度快报》在四日和五日报道，在三日下午的会谈中，“斯瓦兰·辛格和牛场都强调印度和日本需要更加密切地联系以便必要时就有关亚洲地区的问题制订一致的行动计划”，杰哈“说明了中国对东南亚尤其是对印度的侵略姿态”。在四日的会谈中，牛场同杰哈讨论了佐藤政府所推行的敌视中国的“两个中国”的阴谋计划。牛场说什么“日本对中国的意识形态活动同样感到忧虑”，并且要印度公开“承认台湾为中国的一个独立的实体”。据报道，杰哈和牛场还讨论了越南问题。
    据报道，这次印日会谈是在美国的幕后操纵下进行的。据合众国际社三日报道，美国驻日本大使赖肖尔在日印会谈期间匆匆赶到新德里进行“访问”，这“证明美国关心印日加强合作的问题”。日本《朝日新闻》在早些时候报道，在美国极力拼凑亚洲反华“联盟”中，日本和印度正在成为美国所期待的这个阵线的“中心”。
