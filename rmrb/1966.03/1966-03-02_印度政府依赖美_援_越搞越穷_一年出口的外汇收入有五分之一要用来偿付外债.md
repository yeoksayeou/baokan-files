Title: Indian Government Becomes Poorer and Poorer Relying on US "Aid"; One-Fifth of Annual Export Foreign Exchange Income Used to Repay Foreign Debt
Author:
Date: 1966-03-02
Edition: 4

    According to Xinhua News Agency, New Delhi dispatch: The Indian government relies on foreign "aid," foreign investment, and imports in an attempt to solve economic difficulties, but instead, India's economic difficulties are becoming increasingly serious.
    The Indian government's reliance on "aid" provided by the United States and other countries has saddled India with debts that cannot be repaid. The Indian weekly *People's Democracy* reported on the 20th that, according to estimates by the Indian Ministry of Finance, during the Fourth Five-Year Plan, which began in April 1966, India's debt repayment will reach US$1.865 billion. The principal and interest on foreign debt to be paid in 1965-1966 will account for approximately 20% of the total foreign exchange of India's export income. "The excessive proportion of debt in our expenditures forces us to constantly rely on continued borrowing to repay debts." The newspaper also said that India's position has fallen to the same level as some countries that are perpetually indebted to the United States and must mortgage 14% to 25% of their exports to the United States.
    The British *Times* said on the 23rd, "Debt repayment consumes about one-fifth of all the aid India receives." "India has had to ask the World Bank, Britain, the United States, Japan, West Germany, and France to provide more loans to allow India to repay its debts."
    *People's Democracy* also said: "Seeing that India is unable to repay its debts, foreign monopoly capitalist groups are pressuring India to accept investment, and the Congress Party government has approved this proposal." According to the *Hindustan Standard* on the 23rd, the total amount of foreign investment currently accounts for 41% of India's total investment, the vast majority of which is American capital. Among the 385 joint ventures in India, foreign capital accounts for 207.

### 印度政府依赖美“援”越搞越穷  一年出口的外汇收入有五分之一要用来偿付外债

1966-03-02
第4版()
专栏：

    印度政府依赖美“援”越搞越穷
    一年出口的外汇收入有五分之一要用来偿付外债
    据新华社讯  新德里消息：印度政府依赖外国“援助”、外国投资和进口来企图解决经济困难，反而使印度的经济困难日益严重。
    印度政府依赖美国等国家提供的“援助”，使印度背上了还不清的债务。印度《人民民主报》周刊二十日报道，据印度财政部的估计，在一九六六年四月开始的第四个五年计划期间，印度要偿还的债务将达到十八亿六千五百万美元。一九六五——一九六六年度要偿付的外债本息大约占印度出口收入的外汇总额的百分之二十左右。“债务在我们的开支中占过大的比重，迫使我们老是得靠继续借债来偿付债务。”这家报纸还说，印度的地位已经沦落到同一些国家一样，它们永远欠美国的债，并且要用百分之十四到二十五的出口来给美国作抵押。
    英国《泰晤士报》二十三日说，“偿还债务消耗了印度得到的全部援助的五分之一左右。”“印度已经不得不要求世界银行、英国、美国、日本、西德和法国再给予贷款让印度还债。”
    《人民民主报》还说：“外国垄断资本集团看到印度没有能力还债，正在压印度接受投资，国大党政府已经批准这种建议。”据《印度斯坦旗报》二十三日报道，目前，外国投资总额已占印度的全部投资额的百分之四十一，其中绝大部分是美国资本。在印度的三百八十五项联合企业中，外国资本就占二百零七个。
