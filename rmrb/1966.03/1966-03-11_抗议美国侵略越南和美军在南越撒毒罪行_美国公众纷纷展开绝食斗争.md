Title:  Protesting American Aggression Against Vietnam and Crimes of US Troops Spreading Poison in South Vietnam: American Public Launches Hunger Strike Struggle
Author: 
Date: 1966-03-11
Edition: 3

    Xinhua News Agency, 10th dispatch from New York: A New York City architect is on a hunger strike to protest the Johnson administration's spraying of chemicals in South Vietnam, which is causing widespread destruction of crops.

    This architect, named Robert Nichols, wrote a letter to U.S. President Johnson on February 19, exposing the lies and deception in the U.S.'s "Honolulu Declaration." In his letter, he questioned why Johnson, on one hand, expressed a desire to "help" the South Vietnamese people increase food production, while on the other hand, he sent troops to South Vietnam to spray chemicals to destroy crops. Subsequently, Nichols began a hunger strike on February 28 to protest America's aggressive policies in South Vietnam.

    Nichols' struggle has deepened the U.S. government's unease over the people's widespread opposition to the war of aggression against Vietnam. National Security Council Executive Secretary Bromley Smith wrote a brief reply to Nichols on the 6th, saying that the U.S.'s practice of destroying crops was not "irreconcilable" with the "Honolulu Declaration." However, Nichols found this response unsatisfactory and decided to continue his hunger strike. In this situation, State Department spokesman McCloskey had to issue a lengthy statement on the 9th, sophistically defending America's crimes of antagonizing the South Vietnamese people. But McCloskey actually admitted that the U.S. was attempting to resort to a "starvation policy" to force the South Vietnamese people to submit. He said: "In war, food is as necessary for the strength of a military force as weapons are."

    According to a report in the February 26 issue of the American weekly "National Guardian," people across the United States have recently launched hunger strikes from time to time to protest the Johnson administration's aggression against Vietnam.

    From February 16 to 18, about 150 students at the City College of New York went on a hunger strike. During these students' hunger strike, hundreds of other students held a series of discussions at the same location to oppose the U.S. government's policy of aggression against Vietnam. David Kulak, a lecturer at Brooklyn College, went on a week-long hunger strike from February 11 to 17. In San Francisco, two sailors who were imprisoned by the military for opposing the Vietnam War also recently went on a hunger strike in prison.

### 抗议美国侵略越南和美军在南越撒毒罪行  美国公众纷纷展开绝食斗争

1966-03-11
第3版()
专栏：

    抗议美国侵略越南和美军在南越撒毒罪行
    美国公众纷纷展开绝食斗争
    新华社十日讯  纽约消息：纽约市一名建筑师为了抗议约翰逊政府在南越撒放化学药品大量毁坏庄稼，正在进行绝食斗争。
    这个建筑师名叫罗伯特·尼科尔斯，他曾经在二月十九日写信给美国总统约翰逊，揭穿美国在“檀香山宣言”中扯谎骗人。他在信中质问约翰逊为什么一面表示要“帮助”南越人民增产粮食，另一面却派部队在南越撒放化学药品去毁坏庄稼。接着，尼科尔斯就在二月二十八日开始绝食，对美国在南越的侵略政策表示抗议。
    尼科尔斯的斗争，加深了美国政府对人民群起反对侵越战争而感到的不安。国家安全委员会执行秘书布朗利·史密斯在六日给尼科尔斯写了一封简短的复信，说美国毁坏庄稼的作法同“檀香山宣言”并非“不可调和”。但是，尼科尔斯认为这个答复不能令人满意，决定继续绝食。在这种情况下，国务院发言人麦克洛斯基不得不在九日发表冗长的声明，为美国与南越人民为敌的罪行诡辩。但是麦克洛斯基实际上承认，美国妄图求助于“饥饿政策”来迫使南越人民屈膝。他说：“在战争中，食物对于一个军事部队的实力来说，是必要的，正如武器一样。”
    据二月二十六日一期的美国《国民前卫》周刊报道，美国各地人民最近不时开展绝食斗争，抗议约翰逊政府侵略越南。
    从二月十六日到十八日，约一百五十名学生在纽约市立学院绝食。在这些学生绝食期间，还有数百名学生在同一地点举行了一系列讨论会，反对美国政府侵略越南的政策。布鲁克林学院的讲师戴维·库拉克从二月十一日到十七日绝食一周。在旧金山，因为反对越南战争而被军方囚禁的两名水兵，最近也在狱中进行了绝食斗争。
