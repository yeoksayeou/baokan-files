Title: Wilcox, General Secretary, Sends Telegram to CPSU on Behalf of the Political Committee of the New Communist Party; New Communist Party Refuses to Send Representatives to the 23rd CPSU Congress
Author:
Date: 1966-03-25
Edition: 3

    Xinhua News Agency, Wellington, March 23rd – V.J. Wilcox, General Secretary of the Communist Party of New Zealand, on behalf of the Political Committee of the Communist Party of New Zealand, sent a telegram to the Communist Party of the Soviet Union, rejecting the CPSU's invitation to send representatives to the 23rd Congress of the CPSU. The full text of the telegram is as follows:

    Thank you for inviting the Communist Party of New Zealand to send fraternal representatives to your 23rd Congress.

    After consideration, we must regretfully decline the invitation, because the outstanding ideological differences between our two parties, as well as between the CPSU leadership and several other fraternal parties who adhere to the principles of Marxism-Leninism and reject the insidious ideas of modern revisionism, have not yet been addressed in a Marxist-Leninist manner.

    Therefore, we believe that our participation would not be useful, and in fact, participation would be embarrassing to us, and possibly to you as well.

    We wish to congratulate the members of the CPSU, and hope that your meeting will proceed on the basis of those Marxist principles that Marx, Engels, Lenin, and Stalin elucidated so brilliantly in theory and implemented in practice.

### 威尔科克斯总书记代表新共政治委员会致电苏共  新共拒绝派代表参加苏共二十三大

1966-03-25
第3版()
专栏：

    威尔科克斯总书记代表新共政治委员会致电苏共
    新共拒绝派代表参加苏共二十三大
    新华社惠灵顿二十三日电　新西兰共产党总书记维·乔·威尔科克斯代表新西兰共产党政治委员会，打电报给苏联共产党，拒绝苏共向它提出的派代表参加苏共第二十三次代表大会的邀请，电报全文如下：
    谢谢你们邀请新西兰共产党派兄弟代表参加你们的二十三次代表大会。
    在进行考虑以后，我们必须遗憾地拒绝邀请，因为在我们两党之间以及在苏共领导和坚持马克思列宁主义原则、拒绝现代修正主义的阴险思想的若干其它兄弟党之间悬而未决的意识形态分歧，还没有以马克思列宁主义方式加以处理。
    因此，我们认为，我们参加是不会有用的，事实上，参加会使我们感到为难，可能也使你们感到为难。
    我们愿向苏共党员祝贺，希望你们的会议将在马克思、恩格斯、列宁和斯大林在理论上如此光辉地阐述的、在实践中加以执行的那些马克思主义原则的基础上进行。
