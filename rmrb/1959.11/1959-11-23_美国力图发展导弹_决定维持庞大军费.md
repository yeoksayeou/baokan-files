Title: U.S. Strives to Develop Missiles; Decides to Maintain Huge Military Spending
Author:
Date: 1959-11-23
Page: 6
Xinhua News Agency, Washington, November 19th: U.S. President Eisenhower held a meeting with senior officials of the Department of Defense on the 16th to discuss the U.S. government's military budget for the 1961 fiscal year. According to American news agencies, the meeting "unanimously agreed" that the U.S. government's direct military expenditure for this fiscal year would be approximately $41 billion, and that the focus would be on developing new weapons such as missiles.

After the meeting, Secretary of Defense McElroy clamored that Khrushchev's visit to the United States had "no impact" on the U.S. budget, and that the United States should "maintain its military strength."

### 美国力图发展导弹  决定维持庞大军费

1959-11-23
第6版()
专栏：

    美国力图发展导弹
    决定维持庞大军费
    据新华社19日讯　华盛顿消息：美国总统艾森豪威尔16日已同国防部的高级官员举行了一次会议，讨论美国政府在1961财政年度的军事预算问题。据美国通讯社说，会议“一致同意”这一财政年度美国政府的直接军事开支大约为四百一十亿美元，而且将把重点放在发展导弹等新武器上。
    国防部长麦克耳罗伊在会后叫嚷说，赫鲁晓夫访问美国对美国预算“没有什么影响”，美国要“保持军事力量”。
