Title: Mikoyan Arrives in Mexico and Receives a Warm Welcome to Preside Over the Opening Ceremony of the Soviet Achievements Exhibition
Author:
Date: 1959-11-20
Page: 6
Xinhua News Agency, Mexico City, November 19th. TASS reported from Mexico City on the 18th: First Deputy Chairman of the Soviet Council of Ministers, Mikoyan, and his entourage arrived in the Mexican capital on the 18th to preside over the opening ceremony of the Soviet Science, Technology, and Culture Achievements Exhibition.

　　Among those who went to the airport to welcome Mikoyan were Mexican Foreign Minister Manuel Tello and Minister of National Economy Raúl Salinas Lozano, among others.

　　Many residents of the Mexican capital also came to the airport to welcome the distinguished guests.

　　Amidst the continuous shouts of "Long live the Soviet Union!" and "Welcome to Mexico!", Mikoyan and his entourage proceeded to the airport building.

　　Mikoyan delivered a speech at the airport. He extended his greetings to the Mexican people who fought for independence and freedom.

　　He said: "I have come here according to the decision of my government to attend the opening ceremony of the Soviet exhibition held in Mexico. We believe that this exhibition will help the Mexican people better understand our achievements in science, technology, and culture."

　　He said: "It is gratifying that the exhibition is opening at a time when the international atmosphere is significantly warming up.

　　"Due to the visit of the Chairman of our Council of Ministers, Khrushchev, to the United States, and his talks with President Eisenhower and the American people, there has been a major improvement, beneficial to the people of all countries, not only in the relations between the United States and the Soviet Union but also in the relations between all countries.

　　"Our people and our government are fully engaged in peaceful construction and are using every possibility to rapidly develop the economy and culture. We advocate lasting peace with all the people of all countries and advocate maintaining friendship with everyone who wishes to be friendly with us.

　　"The Soviet people have great goodwill towards the Mexican people. We are very interested in how you live and how you fight for the happy life of the Mexican people.

　　"The Soviet plane has landed on Mexican territory for the first time. We brought by plane from the Soviet Union the warmest greetings and best wishes from our people and our government to the Mexican people and the Mexican government."

　　Subsequently, Mikoyan and his entourage boarded cars. The citizens of the Mexican capital lined the streets to welcome the distinguished guests. They continuously shouted: "Welcome to our Mexico! Long live the Soviet Union!"

　　Xinhua News Agency, November 19th. TASS reported from Mexico City on November 18th: First Deputy Chairman of the Soviet Council of Ministers, Mikoyan, visited Mexican Foreign Minister Tello on the 18th and held talks with him.

　　After the talks, the Mexican Foreign Minister told reporters that Mikoyan had explained to him the Soviet government's policies on issues such as disarmament and colonialism. Tello explained Mexico's traditional policies on various aspects of foreign policy. Speaking of cultural relations between Mexico and the Soviet Union, Tello said: I told Mikoyan about the successful performances of the Soviet Ballet and the Pyatnitsky Choir in Mexico.

　　The Mexican Foreign Minister concluded by saying that he was deeply impressed by Mikoyan, who is a man of great wisdom, erudition, and experience.

　　Xinhua News Agency, November 19th. TASS reported from Mexico on the 18th: Final preparations for the opening of the Soviet Science, Technology, and Culture Achievements Exhibition are being accelerated inside the National Auditorium in the Mexican capital. More than two hundred Mexican workers, under the guidance of Soviet experts, are installing display cases, exhibiting exhibits, and arranging the venue.

　　The Mexican public is very interested in the Soviet exhibition. In the square in front of the National Auditorium, where tractors, combine harvesters, and other agricultural machines are displayed, one can often see groups of Mexicans eager to learn about Soviet agricultural technology. Mexican newspapers publish many articles and pictures introducing the exhibition every day. The newspapers pay close attention to the fact that First Deputy Chairman of the Soviet Council of Ministers, Mikoyan, will come to preside over the opening ceremony of the exhibition.

### 主持苏联成就展览会开幕典礼  米高扬抵墨西哥受到热烈欢迎

1959-11-20
第6版()
专栏：

    主持苏联成就展览会开幕典礼
    米高扬抵墨西哥受到热烈欢迎
    新华社19日讯　据塔斯社墨西哥城18日讯：苏联部长会议第一副主席米高扬和他的随行人员18日抵达墨西哥首都，前往主持苏联科学、技术和文化成就展览会的开幕典礼。
    前往机场欢迎米高扬的有墨西哥外交部长曼努埃尔·泰略、国民经济部长劳尔·萨利纳斯·洛萨诺等人。
    墨西哥首都的许多居民也到机场上来欢迎贵宾。
    米高扬和随行人员在“苏联万岁！”、“欢迎到墨西哥来！”等不断的口号声中前往机场大厦。
    米高扬在机场发表了谈话。他向为独立和自由而斗争的墨西哥人民致意。
    他说：“我是根据本国政府的决定前来参加在墨西哥举办的苏联展览会的开幕典礼的。我们认为，这个展览会将有助于墨西哥人民更好地了解我们在科学、技术和文化方面的成就。”
    他说：“展览会是在国际气氛显著转暖的时期开幕的，这一点令人感到愉快。
    “由于我国部长会议主席赫鲁晓夫访美以及他同艾森豪威尔总统和美国人民的交谈，不仅美苏两国的关系，而且所有国家之间的关系都发生了有利于各国人民的重大好转。
    “我国人民、我国政府正在全力进行和平建设，并且利用一切可能来迅速发展经济和文化。我们主张同所有国家人民有持久和平，主张同一切希望和我们友好的人保持友谊。
    “苏联人民对墨西哥人民抱有很大好感。你们如何生活，如何在为墨西哥人民的幸福生活而斗争，我们对此很感兴趣。
    “苏联的飞机第一次在墨西哥的领土上着陆。我们用飞机从苏联带来了我国人民和我国政府对墨西哥人民和墨西哥政府的最热情的问候和良好的祝愿。”
    随后，米高扬和他的随行人员乘上汽车。墨西哥首都市民夹道欢迎贵宾。他们不断高呼：“欢迎到我们墨西哥来！苏联万岁！”
    新华社19日讯　塔斯社墨西哥城19日讯：苏联部长会议第一副主席米高扬18日拜访了墨西哥外交部长泰略，并同他进行了会谈。
    墨西哥外交部长在会谈后向记者说，米高扬就苏联政府关于裁军和殖民主义等问题的政策向他作了说明。泰略则就外交政策方面的各项问题说明了墨西哥的传统政策。谈到墨西哥和苏联两国之间的文化关系时，泰略说：我把苏联芭蕾舞团和比亚特尼茨基歌唱团在墨西哥演出成功的情况告诉了米高扬。
    墨西哥外长最后说，他对米高扬有深刻的印象，米高扬是智慧过人、学问渊博、经验丰富的人物。
    新华社19日讯　塔斯社墨西哥18日讯：墨西哥首都民族会堂内正在加速进行苏联科学、技术和文化成就展览会开幕的最后准备工作。有两百多名墨西哥工人正在苏联专家的指挥下安装橱窗，陈列展览品和布置会场。
    墨西哥公众对苏联展览会很感兴趣。在民族会堂前的广场上摆着一些拖拉机、联合收获机和其他农业机器，那里常常可看到成群的墨西哥人渴望了解苏联的农业技术。墨西哥报纸每天都有许多介绍展览会的文章和图片。报纸很注意苏联部长会议第一副主席米高扬将来主持展览会开幕典礼这件事。
