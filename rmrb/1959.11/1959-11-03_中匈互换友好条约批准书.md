Title: Exchange of Instruments of Ratification of the Sino-Hungarian Treaty of Friendship

Author:

Date: 1959-11-03
Page: 1

Xinhua News Agency, Budapest, November 2nd: News Communiqué on the Exchange of Instruments of Ratification of the Sino-Hungarian Treaty of Friendship

The instruments of ratification of the Treaty of Friendship and Cooperation between the People's Republic of China and the Hungarian People's Republic, signed in Beijing on May 6, 1959, were solemnly exchanged at noon on November 2, 1959, in the reception room of the Presidium of the Hungarian Parliament. Hao Deqing, Ambassador Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary, signed the certificate of exchange of instruments of ratification on behalf of the People's Republic of China, and Dr. Sik Andrei, Minister of Foreign Affairs, signed on behalf of the Hungarian People's Republic.

Present at the ceremony of exchanging instruments of ratification were: Ferenc Munnich, Prime Minister of the Hungarian Revolutionary Worker-Peasant Government, Károly Kiss, Vice-President of the Presidium of the Hungarian People's Republic, Jenő Incze, Minister of Foreign Trade, János Csergő, Minister of Metallurgy and Machine Industry, István Kristóf, Secretary-General of the Presidium of the Hungarian People's Republic, Károly Sarka, Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs, as well as Xie Feng, Counselor of the Embassy of the People's Republic of China in Hungary, Lin Ye, Cultural Counselor, Zhang Geng, Commercial Counselor, and others.

In their speeches at the exchange of instruments of ratification ceremony, Ambassador Hao Deqing and Foreign Minister Sik Andrei unanimously stated that the signing of the Treaty of Friendship and Cooperation between the People's Republic of China and Hungarian People's Republic is a major event in the history of relations between the peoples of China and Hungary. This treaty codifies in writing the basic principles of the fraternal relationship between the peoples of China and Hungary, and it will help to further strengthen the solidarity of the socialist camp. Ambassador Hao Deqing and Foreign Minister Sik Andrei unanimously expressed their deep conviction that this treaty will promote the friendly and cooperative relations between China and Hungary to a greater stage of development and prosperity, and will permanently serve the interests and happiness of the two fraternal peoples.

### 中匈互换友好条约批准书

1959-11-03
第1版()
专栏：

    中匈互换友好条约批准书
    新华社布达佩斯2日电　关于中匈友好合作条约互换批准书的新闻公报
    1959年11月2日中午在匈牙利国会主席团会客厅隆重地互换了中华人民共和国和匈牙利人民共和国间1959年5月6日在北京签订的友好合作条约的批准书。在互换批准书的证书上签字的，中华人民共和国方面是特命全权大使郝德青，匈牙利人民共和国方面是外交部长西克·安德烈博士。
    出席互换批准书仪式的有：匈牙利工农革命政府总理明尼赫·费伦茨，匈牙利人民共和国主席团副主席基什·卡罗伊，对外贸易部长英切·耶诺，冶金和机械工业部长策尔戈·亚诺什，匈牙利人民共和国主席团秘书长克里斯托夫·伊斯特万，外交部副部长萨尔卡·卡罗利，以及中华人民共和国驻匈牙利大使馆参赞谢丰，文化参赞林耶，商务参赞张耿和等。
    郝德青大使和外交部长西克·安德烈博士在互换批准书仪式上的讲话中一致指出，中华人民共和国和匈牙利人民共和国之间签订的友好合作条约是中匈两国人民关系史上的重大事件。这一条约把中匈两国人民间的兄弟关系的基本原则用文字固定下来，它将有助于进一步加强社会主义阵营的团结。郝德青大使和外交部长西克·安德烈博士一致深信，这一条约将把中匈两国友好合作关系推向更大发展和繁荣的阶段，永久地服务于两国兄弟人民的利益和幸福。
