Title: Precautions for Using Coal Gas Stoves
Author: Hu Xiaomin, Ma Xubin
Date: 1959-11-21
Page: 2

Column: Common Knowledge

    Precautions for Using Coal Gas Stoves
    Producer gas contains over 20% carbon monoxide. According to health standards, the carbon monoxide content in the workplace should not exceed 0.03 mg/liter. When it exceeds 0.3-0.5%, it is potentially fatal. Therefore, coal gas operators must constantly pay attention to the airtightness of gas pipelines, coal gas furnace walls, coal charging hoppers, etc., to prevent gas from escaping.
    When coal gas mixes with air in a certain proportion, it can explode when it encounters a source of ignition, causing damage to equipment and injuries or death to personnel. Therefore, the following should be carefully observed:
    1.  Always maintain the airtightness of coal gas pipelines, coal gas tanks, and various valves, and keep the coal gas pipelines and coal gas tanks under positive pressure to prevent air from seeping in.
    2.  Before igniting the coal gas, first ignite a sufficient source of ignition at the coal gas nozzle, then gradually open the coal gas valve. Do not release the gas first and then ignite it, nor release it too quickly or too much.
    3.  When extinguishing the fire or stopping the blast, the fire source must be cut off first (close the valve connecting the coal gas pipe and the burner), and then the valve that releases the coal gas to the chimney or other kilns must be opened.
    4.  Before coal gas is introduced into a new coal gas passage, the air in the coal gas passage must be purged with flue gas or steam.
    5.  For manually ventilated coal gas furnaces, when opening the coal gas valve, the secondary air must be appropriately opened to avoid a large difference in pressure between the air and coal gas passages, causing the coal gas to run into the air passage and the air pipe to explode during ignition.
    6.  To prevent explosions, a safety valve that can close automatically after an explosion must be installed in the coal gas storage area. The area of the safety valve should generally not be less than 250 square centimeters per cubic meter of coal gas. (Hu Xiaomin, Ma Xubin)

### 使用煤气炉注意事项
胡孝敏  马绪斌
1959-11-21
第2版()
专栏：小常识

    使用煤气炉注意事项
    在发生炉煤气中，含有20％以上的一氧化碳，按照卫生标准，在工作现场内，一氧化碳的含量不许可大于0.03毫克／立升，当超过0.3—0.5％时则有致命的危险。所以煤气操作人员，在操作时必须时刻注意煤气管道、煤气炉壁、加煤斗等的严密，不使煤气冒出。
    煤气与空气成一定比例后，遇到火源即可爆炸，造成设备的破坏及人员的伤亡。因此，应当十分注意：
    1、始终保持煤气管道、煤气箱、各种阀门的严密性，并使煤气管道煤气箱内呈现正压状态。以防空气渗入。
    2、点燃煤气前，先在煤气喷口处点燃起足够的火源，然后逐渐开放煤气。不要先放煤气后点火，也不要放得过猛、过大。
    3、在止火或停止鼓风的时候，必须先切断火源（关闭煤气管与燃烧嘴相通的阀门），然后开放煤气放空烟囱或通往其他窑炉的阀门。
    4、煤气未通入新的煤气通道以前，必须用烟气或蒸汽将煤气通道内的空气排除。
    5、人工通风煤气炉，在开放煤气时，必须适当开放二次空气，以免空气与煤气通道压力相差悬殊，煤气跑入空气通道，使空气管道在点火时候爆炸。
    6、为了防止爆炸，在煤气集存处，必须设立在爆炸后能够自行关闭的安全阀，安全阀的面积，一般一立方米煤气不得小于二百五十平方公分。（胡孝敏、马绪斌）
