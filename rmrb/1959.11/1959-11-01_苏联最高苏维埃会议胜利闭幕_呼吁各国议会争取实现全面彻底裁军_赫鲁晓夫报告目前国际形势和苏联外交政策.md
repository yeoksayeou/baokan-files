Title: The Soviet Supreme Soviet Session Concludes Successfully: Calls on Parliaments of All Countries to Strive for Complete and Thorough Disarmament; Khrushchev Reports on the Current International Situation and Soviet Foreign Policy
Author:
Date: 1959-11-01
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, 31st October: According to TASS, the third session of the Supreme Soviet of the Soviet Union held its final day of meetings on October 31st.

At 10:00 am, representatives of the Union Council and the National Council gathered in the Grand Kremlin Palace. Guests, diplomatic representatives of various countries to the Soviet Union, as well as Soviet and foreign journalists, were seated in the spectator and VIP sections.

Nikita Khrushchev, Chairman of the Council of Ministers of the Soviet Union and First Secretary of the Central Committee of the Communist Party of the Soviet Union, delivered a report on the current international situation and Soviet foreign policy.

His report was met with enthusiastic applause throughout the hall.

Subsequently, the meeting discussed Khrushchev's report. Those who spoke at the meeting included: Bakulev, President of the Soviet Academy of Medical Sciences; Klauson, Deputy Chairman of the Council of Ministers of the Estonian Republic; Brazhenov, a Soviet advanced worker; Gromyko, Minister of Foreign Affairs of the Soviet Union; Zhukov, Chairman of the State Committee for Cultural Relations with Foreign Countries under the Council of Ministers of the Soviet Union; and the writer Kauks Chuk. They all expressed their warm support for the Soviet government's foreign policy.

Based on the proposal of the Foreign Affairs Committees of the Union Council and the National Council of the Supreme Soviet, the meeting passed a resolution fully endorsing the foreign policy of the Soviet government.

Before closing, the meeting adopted a letter addressed to the parliaments of all countries in the world.

The letter to the parliaments of all countries stated that there is currently a way to eliminate the possibility of launching a new war, and that way is complete and thorough disarmament.

The letter to the parliaments of all countries emphasized that the implementation of a complete and thorough disarmament plan would firmly guarantee peaceful coexistence among all countries. It would open a new chapter for the development of underdeveloped countries and pave the way for them to develop their own economies and cultures.

The letter stated that the solution to the issue of complete and thorough disarmament is in the hands of the people of all countries. The Supreme Soviet of the Soviet Union is deeply convinced that this goal is entirely achievable at present.

The letter to the parliaments of all countries pointed out that the international situation has achieved a certain degree of relaxation. The visit of Nikita Khrushchev, Chairman of the Council of Ministers of the Soviet Union, to the United States and his meeting with US President Eisenhower played an outstanding role in this regard.

The letter emphasized that the parliaments, governments, and national leaders of all countries bear a special responsibility at this time. The parliaments of all countries should resolutely seek ways to resolve international disputes, first and foremost the issue of disarmament.

The letter pointed out that the Soviet government's proposal to the United Nations for complete and thorough disarmament points out a realistic way to prevent war. As for the Supreme Soviet of the Soviet Union and the Soviet government, they will make every effort to resolve the issue of complete and thorough disarmament.

The letter to the parliaments of all countries stated that the Supreme Soviet of the Soviet Union expresses its deep conviction that the lofty initiative of the Soviet government will be supported by the parliaments and governments of all countries in the world. The Supreme Soviet of the Soviet Union hopes that the parliaments of all countries will support this initiative, so that the path of mankind toward lasting peace can be guaranteed.

### 苏联最高苏维埃会议胜利闭幕  呼吁各国议会争取实现全面彻底裁军  赫鲁晓夫报告目前国际形势和苏联外交政策

1959-11-01
第5版()
专栏：

    苏联最高苏维埃会议胜利闭幕
    呼吁各国议会争取实现全面彻底裁军
    赫鲁晓夫报告目前国际形势和苏联外交政策
    新华社31日讯　据塔斯社报道：苏联最高苏维埃第三次会议在10月31日举行了最后一天的会议。
    上午十时，联盟院和民族院的代表聚集在大克里姆林宫中。会议旁听席和贵宾席上坐着许多客人、各国驻苏联的外交代表，以及苏联和外国记者。
    苏联部长会议主席、苏共中央第一书记赫鲁晓夫在会上作了报告，他谈到了目前国际形势和苏联的外交政策。
    他的报告不断获得全场热烈的掌声。
    接着，会议讨论了赫鲁晓夫的报告。在会上发言的有：苏联医学科学院院长巴库列夫、爱沙尼亚共和国部长会议副主席克劳森、苏联先进工人布拉任诺夫、苏联外交部长葛罗米柯、苏联部长会议国家对外文化委员会主席茹科夫、作家考?楚克等。他们都表示热烈拥护苏联政府的外交政策。
    会议根据最高苏维埃联盟院和民族院外交委员会的提议，通过了一项决议，完全赞同苏联政府的外交政策。
    会议在闭幕以前通过了告世界各国议会书。
    告世界各国议会书说，目前存在着消除发动新战争的可能的途径，这条途径就是全面彻底裁军。
    告各国议会书强调指出，实现全面彻底裁军计划的结果，将能巩固地保障各国和平共处。将为不发达国家的发展打开新的一页，并为它们打开发展本国经济和文化的道路。
    书中说，全面彻底裁军问题的解决是由各国人民掌握的。苏联最高苏维埃深信，这个目的在目前是完全可以实现的。
    告世界各国议会书指出，在目前，国际局势获得了一定的缓和。苏联部长会议主席赫鲁晓夫的访问美国和他同美国总统艾森豪威尔的会见在这方面起了杰出的作用。
    书中强调指出，当前，各国议会、政府和国家领导人肩负着特别的责任。各国议会应该坚决地寻找解决国际争执问题的途径，首先是解决裁军问题的途径。
    书中指出，苏联政府向联合国提出的全面彻底裁军的建议中指出了防止战争的现实途径。至于苏联最高苏维埃和苏联政府，它们将尽一切努力使全面彻底裁军的问题得到解决。
    告世界各国议会书说，苏联最高苏维埃表示深信，苏联政府的崇高倡议会得到世界各国议会和政府的支持。苏联最高苏维埃希望各国议会支持这个倡议，从而使人类通向持久和平的道路能够得到保障。
