Title: Fraternal Countries Exchange Goods, Promoting Joint Economic Upsurge; Soviet-German Long-Term Trade Agreement Signed, Total Exchange of Goods to Reach 50 Billion Rubles in Six Years; Czechoslovakia, Poland, East Germany, Bulgaria and Other Countries Sign Exchange of Goods Protocols for Next Year, Trade Volume to Increase Significantly
Author:
Date: 1959-11-24
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, Moscow, November 22nd: According to TASS reports, the total exchange of goods between the Soviet Union and the German Democratic Republic will exceed 50 billion rubles between 1960 and 1965.

The trade delegations of the governments of the Soviet Union and the German Democratic Republic recently held negotiations in Moscow on the exchange of goods between the two countries, and on the 21st signed the 1960 Exchange of Goods Protocol and the 1961-1965 Long-Term Trade Agreement.

During the period stipulated in the Protocol and Agreement, the Soviet Union will provide the German Democratic Republic with 32 million tons of coal, 12 million tons of iron ore, 15 million tons of steel, 34,000 cars, and other raw materials and equipment, as well a large amount of grain, butter and vegetable oil, meat and other products. The German Democratic Republic will supply the Soviet Union with machines and equipment worth 16 billion rubles, ships worth over 2 billion rubles, passenger carriages worth 1.67 billion rubles, and machine tools and forging equipment worth 1.5 billion rubles, etc.

Xinhua News Agency, Berlin, November 23rd: "Neues Deutschland" published an interview on the 22nd with the Deputy Prime Minister of the German Democratic Republic, Heinrich Rau, on the Soviet-German 1960 Exchange of Goods Protocol and the 1961-1965 Long-Term Trade Agreement signed in Moscow on the 21st.

He said that the signed agreement stipulates that the total exchange of goods between the Soviet Union and East Germany from 1960 to 1965 will total 50 billion rubles, equivalent to 15 billion US dollars. This is currently the largest trade agreement in the world in terms of value.

Heinrich Rau said that the signing of these two exchange of goods agreements will strengthen the power of the peace camp and more closely unite the Soviet Union and the German Democratic Republic. It shows that the Soviet Union and the German Democratic Republic, as well as the entire socialist camp, are moving towards the realization of their current basic tasks at an increasingly rapid pace.

He said that according to the new exchange of goods agreement, trade with the Soviet Union will, as in the past, account for about 45% of the total foreign trade of the German Democratic Republic. The Soviet Union will supply 95% of the German Democratic Republic's imported oil, 70% of its steel, and all of its pig iron and non-ferrous metals. And 47% of the machinery that the Soviet Union needs to import from abroad will be supplied by the German Democratic Republic.

Xinhua News Agency, Prague: The 1960 Exchange of Goods and Payments Protocol between Czechoslovakia and Poland was signed here on the 19th.

The Protocol stipulates that the quantity of goods exchanged between the two countries in 1960 will increase by approximately 17% compared to 1959.

According to the Protocol, Czechoslovakia will provide Poland with machines and mechanical equipment, especially machine tools, tractors, various trucks, power generation equipment, rubber and leather industry machinery, automobiles, as well as cellulose, rayon, and various raw materials for the chemical industry.

Poland will supply Czechoslovakia with products of the machinery manufacturing industry, especially cranes, excavators, machine tools, mining machinery and equipment, casting equipment, and complete sets of equipment for producing aerated concrete bricks, as well as coal, zinc, salt, etc.

Xinhua News Agency, Berlin: The 1960 Trade Protocol between the German Democratic Republic and Czechoslovakia was signed in Berlin on the 16th. The Protocol stipulates that in 1960, the trade volume between the two countries will increase by 13% compared to this year.

According to the Protocol, the German Democratic Republic will supply Czechoslovakia with steel rolling and smelting casting equipment, machine tools, construction and road construction machinery, tractors, transportation equipment, chemical products, textiles, etc. Czechoslovakia will provide the German Democratic Republic with hard coal, coke, steel, transportation equipment, machinery manufacturing products, textiles, and other daily necessities.

Xinhua News Agency, Berlin: According to the German News Agency, the 1960 Exchange of Goods Agreement between the German Democratic Republic and Bulgaria was signed in Berlin on the 14th. According to the agreement, the exchange of goods volume between the two countries in 1960 will increase by approximately 50% compared to 1959.

The German Democratic Republic will export complete sets of industrial equipment, machinery manufacturing products, electrical machinery, precision instruments, automobiles, chemical products, and daily necessities to Bulgaria. Bulgaria will export non-ferrous metals, selected ores, machinery manufacturing products, and agricultural products to the German Democratic Republic.

Xinhua News Agency, Prague: Czechoslovakia and Bulgaria signed the 1960 Exchange of Goods and Payments Protocol, as well as an agreement on jointly utilizing Bulgaria's "Medet" copper mine here on the 14th.

The Protocol stipulates that the exchange of goods volume between the two countries next year will increase by approximately 45% compared to this year. Czechoslovakia will supply Bulgaria with various machines, power equipment, transportation vehicles, machine tools, and other products. Bulgaria will supply Czechoslovakia with carriages, ocean-going vessels, non-ferrous metals, chemical raw materials, meat, various fruits and vegetables, and other products.

According to the agreement on jointly utilizing the "Medet" copper mine, Czechoslovakia will provide the necessary equipment, machinery and materials for the construction of the open-pit mine, the ore dressing plant, and the expansion of the copper smelter through loans.

Xinhua News Agency, Warsaw: Poland and Bulgaria signed the 1960 Exchange of Goods Protocol in Warsaw on the 21st.

The Protocol stipulates that the exchange of goods volume between the two countries next year will increase by approximately 15% compared to this year.

Bulgaria will export selected zinc ore, lead plates, sprayers, and transformers to Poland. Poland will supply Bulgaria with various machines, industrial equipment, heavy trucks, freight cars, and coke, etc.

### 兄弟国家互通有无　促进经济共同高涨  苏德签订长期贸易协定六年内换货总额达五百亿卢布  捷波德保等国签订明年换货议定书贸易额将显著增长

1959-11-24
第5版()
专栏：

    兄弟国家互通有无　促进经济共同高涨
    苏德签订长期贸易协定六年内换货总额达五百亿卢布
    捷波德保等国签订明年换货议定书贸易额将显著增长
    据新华社莫斯科22日电　据塔斯社报道，在1960到1965年期间，苏联和德意志民主共和国的换货总额将超过五百亿卢布。
    苏联和德意志民主共和国两国政府贸易代表团，最近在莫斯科就两国换货问题举行了谈判，并于21日签订了1960年换货议定书和1961—1965年长期贸易协定。
    在议定书和协定规定的时期内，苏联将向德意志民主共和国提供三千二百万吨煤、一千二百万吨铁矿砂、一千五百万吨钢材、三万四千辆小汽车和其他一些原料及设备，以及大量谷物、奶油和植物油、肉类及其他产品。德意志民主共和国将供给苏联价值一百六十亿卢布的机器和设备，价值二十亿卢布以上的船只，价值十六亿七千万卢布的客车车厢，以及价值十五亿卢布的机床和锻压设备等。
    据新华社柏林23日电　“新德意志报”22日发表了德意志民主共和国副总理海·劳就21日在莫斯科签订的苏德1960年换货议定书和1961—1965年长期贸易协定对这家报纸记者的谈话。
    他说，签订的协定规定，从1960年到1965年苏德两国换货总额共为五百亿卢布，相当于一百五十亿美元。这是目前世界上数额最大的贸易协定。
    海·劳说，这两个换货协定的签订将加强和平阵营的力量，使德苏两国更加紧密地融结在一起。它说明苏联和德意志民主共和国以及整个社会主义阵营正在以越来越迅速的速度走向实现它们当前的基本任务。
    他说，根据新的换货协定，对苏联的贸易仍将和过去一样，占德意志民主共和国对外贸易总额的45％左右。德意志民主共和国进口石油的95％，钢材70％，生铁和有色金属的全部将由苏联供应。而苏联所需从外国进口的机械，将有47％是由德意志民主共和国供应。
    新华社布拉格电　捷克斯洛伐克和波兰两国1960年换货和支付议定书19日在这里签字。
    议定书规定，1960年两国换货数量将比1959年增加大约17％。
    根据议定书，捷克斯洛伐克将向波兰提供机器和机械设备，特别是工作母机、拖拉机、各种卡车、发电设备、橡胶和皮革工业机器、汽车，以及纤维素、人造纤维和各种化学工业用的原料。
    波兰将供给捷克斯洛伐克机械制造业产品，特别是起重机、挖凿机、工作母机、采矿机械设备、铸造设备和生产泡沫混凝土砖用的全套设备，以及煤、锌、食盐等。
    新华社柏林电　德意志民主共和国和捷克斯洛伐克两国1960年贸易议定书16日在柏林签字。议定书规定，1960年，两国贸易额要比今年增长13％。
    根据议定书，德意志民主共和国将供给捷克斯洛伐克轧钢和冶炼铸造设备、工作母机、建筑和筑路机械、拖拉机、运输设备、化工产品、纺织品等。捷克斯洛伐克将向德意志民主共和国提供硬煤、焦炭、钢材、运输设备、机器制造业产品、纺织品和其他日用品。
    新华社柏林电　据德意志通讯社报道，德意志民主共和国和保加利亚1960年换货协定14日在柏林签字。根据协定，两国1960年的换货额将比1959年增加约50％。
    德意志民主共和国将向保加利亚输出全套的工业设备、机器制造业产品、电气机械、精密仪器、汽车、化工产品，以及生活日用品。保加利亚将向德意志民主共和国输出有色金属、精选矿、机器制造业产品和农产品。
    据新华社布拉格电　捷克斯洛伐克和保加利亚14日在这里签订了1960年换货和支付议定书，以及合作利用保加利亚“麦德特”铜矿的协定。
    议定书规定，两国明年的换货额将比今年增加大约45％。捷克斯洛伐克将供给保加利亚各种机器、动力设备、运输工具、工作母机等产品。保加利亚将供给捷克斯洛伐克车厢、远洋轮船、有色金属、化学原料、肉类、各种水果和蔬菜等产品。
    根据合作利用“麦德特”铜矿的协定，捷克斯洛伐克将通过贷款，提供建设露天矿所必需的设备、机器和器材、选矿厂和扩大炼铜厂。
    新华社华沙电　波兰和保加利亚21日在华沙签订了1960年换货议定书。
    议定书规定，明年两国换货额将比今年增加约15％。
    保加利亚将向波兰出口精选锌矿、铅板、喷雾器和变压器等。波兰将供给保加利亚各种机器、工业设备、载重汽车、运货车和焦炭等。
