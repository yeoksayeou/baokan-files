Title: Temporary Supplementary Agreement on the Armistice Agreement
Author: 
Date: 1953-07-28
Page: 2

To meet the needs of handling prisoners of war who are not directly repatriated in accordance with the provisions of the Neutral Nations Repatriation Commission's terms of reference, the Supreme Commander of the Korean People's Army and the Commander of the Chinese People's Volunteers on the one hand, and the Commander-in-Chief of the United Nations Command on the other hand, hereby agree to conclude a temporary supplementary agreement to the Armistice Agreement in accordance with the provisions of Article 5, Paragraph 61 of the Korean Military Armistice Agreement as follows:

I. In accordance with the provisions of Article 2, Paragraphs 4 and 5 of the Neutral Nations Repatriation Commission's terms of reference, the United Nations Command has the right to designate the area between the Military Demarcation Line and the southeastern edge of the Demilitarized Zone, south from the Imjin River and northeast to the road from Ogeum-ri leading due south (excluding the main highway from Panmunjom to the southeast) as the location where the United Nations Command will hand over prisoners of war under its custody who are not directly repatriated to the Neutral Nations Repatriation Commission and the Indian armed forces for takeover. Before the signing of the Armistice Agreement, the United Nations Command shall notify the Korean People's Army and the Chinese People's Volunteers of the approximate number of these prisoners of war under its custody, classified by nationality.

II. If the Korean People's Army and the Chinese People's Volunteers have prisoners of war under their custody who request not to be directly repatriated, they have the right to designate an area adjacent to Panmunjom between the Military Demarcation Line and the northwestern edge of the Demilitarized Zone as the location for handing over such prisoners of war to the Neutral Nations Repatriation Commission and the Indian armed forces for takeover. After the Korean People's Army and the Chinese People's Volunteers are informed that there are prisoners of war under their custody who request not to be directly repatriated, they shall notify the United Nations Command of the approximate number of these prisoners of war, classified by nationality.

III. In accordance with Article 1, Paragraphs 8, 9, and 10 of the Armistice Agreement, the following provisions are specifically stipulated:

	A. After the ceasefire takes effect, unarmed personnel of each side must, with the special permission of the Military Armistice Commission, enter the areas designated by their respective sides above to carry out necessary construction work. After the completion of the construction work, all such personnel shall not remain in the above-mentioned areas.

	B. A certain number of prisoners of war not directly repatriated under the custody of both sides, determined by both sides, must, with the special permission of the Military Armistice Commission, be escorted by a certain number of armed forces from the detaining party into the above-mentioned prisoner of war custody locations designated by both sides for takeover by the Neutral Nations Repatriation Commission and the Indian armed forces; after the takeover, the armed forces of the detaining party shall immediately withdraw from the custody locations and return to their own areas of control.

	C. Personnel of the Neutral Nations Repatriation Commission and its affiliated organizations, the Indian armed forces, the Indian Red Cross, interpretation representatives from both sides, observer representatives from both sides, and the necessary materials and equipment, for the purpose of carrying out the tasks stipulated in the Neutral Nations Repatriation Commission's terms of reference, must be allowed to enter and exit the prisoner of war custody locations designated by both sides with the special permission of the Military Armistice Commission, and shall have complete freedom of movement within those locations.

IV. The provisions of Article 3, Paragraph C of this Agreement shall not be interpreted as weakening the rights that the personnel mentioned in that paragraph should enjoy in accordance with Article 1, Paragraph 11 of the Armistice Agreement.

V. This Agreement shall be terminated upon the completion of the tasks stipulated in the Neutral Nations Repatriation Commission's terms of reference.

Done in Panmunjom, Korea, at 10:00 hours on July 27, 1953, in Korean, Chinese, and English, all texts being equally valid.

### 关于停战协定的临时补充协议

1953-07-28
第2版()
专栏：

    关于停战协定的临时补充协议
    为适应按中立国遣返委员会职权范围的规定以处理未直接遣返的战俘的需要，朝鲜人民军最高司令官及中国人民志愿军司令员一方与联合国军总司令另一方，兹同意根据朝鲜军事停战协定第五条第六十一款的规定，订立对停战协定的临时补充协议如下：
    一、根据中立国遣返委员会职权范围第二条第四款和第五款的规定，联合国军有权指定军事分界线及非军事区东南缘之间，南起临津江，东北至乌金里通往正南的道路之间的地区（由板门店通往东南的主要公路不含）为联合国军将负责看管中未直接遣返的战俘移交给中立国遣返委员会及印度的武装力量接管的地点。在停战协定签字前，联合国军须将在其看管下的此项战俘按国籍分类的概数通知朝鲜人民军与中国人民志愿军方面。
    二、朝鲜人民军与中国人民志愿军，如在其看管下的战俘有要求不直接遣返者，有权指定军事分界线及非军事区西北缘之间邻近板门店地区为将该项战俘交给中立国遣返委员会及印度武装力量接管的地点。朝鲜人民军与中国人民志愿军得知在其看管下的战俘有要求不直接遣返者以后，须将此项战俘按国籍分类的概数通知联合国军方面。
    三、根据停战协定第一条第八款、第九款和第十款，特规定以下各款：
    甲、在停火生效后，各方非武装人员须经由军事停战委员会特许，进入上述各方各自指定的地区进行必要的修建工作。在修建工作完成后，全部此项人员不得再停留于上述地区。
    乙、经双方确定的在双方各自看管下一定数目的未直接遣返的战俘，须经军事停战委员会的特许，由拘留一方一定数量的武装部队各自护送进入上述双方各自指定的看管地点，被移交中立国遣返委员会及印度武装力量接管；接管后拘留一方的武装部队应即撤出看管地点，退回其本方的控制地区。
    丙、为执行中立国遣返委员会职权范围所规定之任务的中立国遣返委员会及其附属机构所属人员、印度武装力量、印度红十字会、双方解释代表、双方观察代表以及所需的物资装备，须经由军事停战委员会特许出入上述双方各自指定的战俘看管地点，并有在该地点移动之完全自由。
    四、本协议第三条丙款的规定不应被解释为削弱该款所述人员依照停战协定第一条第十一款所应享受之权利。
    五、本协议在中立国遣返委员会职权范围所规定的任务完毕后即行废止。
