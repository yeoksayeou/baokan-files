Title: Somali President Siad Barre Expresses Willingness for Government to Dialogue with Opposition Forces
Author:
Date: 1989-07-03
Page: 3

Xinhua News Agency, Mogadishu, July 2nd.

Somali President Siad Barre today, at the closing ceremony of the Somali ruling party—the Revolutionary Socialist Party—proposed that, for national unity, the government is willing to engage in direct dialogue with opposition forces at any time and in any place.

Siad did not specify which opposition organization he was referring to. According to reports, the Somali National Movement has been fighting government troops in the northern region for over a year.

This is the first time Siad Barre has proposed a dialogue since the National Movement launched an attack on government troops in the northern region last May.

### 索马里总统西亚德表示  政府愿同反对力量对话

1989-07-03
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    索马里总统西亚德表示
    政府愿同反对力量对话
    新华社摩加迪沙7月2日电　索马里总统西亚德·巴雷今天在索马里执政党——革命社会主义党会议的闭幕式上提出，为了国家团结，政府愿在任何时间、任何地点同反对力量直接对话。
    西亚德没有指明是哪个反对派组织。据报道，索马里民族运动同政府军在北部地区已打了一年多仗。
    这是民族运动去年5月在北部地区向政府军发动进攻以来，西亚德总统首次提出对话建议。
