Title: U.S. Astronauts Mark 20th Anniversary of Moon Landing
Author: Qǐxīn
Date: 1989-07-17
Edition: 3

U.S. astronauts are marking the 20th anniversary of the moon landing. Twenty years ago on July 20, astronauts Neil Armstrong and Air Force Colonel Edwin Aldrin of the U.S. "Apollo 11" mission became the first humans to set foot on the moon. To commemorate the 20th anniversary of the moon landing, the three astronauts, including Air Force Colonel Michael Collins, began a week-long tour in the United States on July 15.

The three astronauts departed from the Huntsville Space Center in Alaska and arrived at the Kennedy Space Center in Cape Canaveral on July 16. It was from this very location that the "Apollo 11" was launched into space 20 years ago. In Washington, D.C., they will participate in a reception and, on July 20, join President Bush in celebrating the 20th anniversary of the moon landing at the National Air and Space Museum.

However, during the commemoration of past achievements, "Apollo 11" commander Armstrong expressed some regret, saying that looking back, we are disappointed that we have not done more since then. John Logsdon, director of the Space Policy Institute at George Washington University, remarked that as we reflect on the past 20 years with nostalgia, we cannot help but wonder why a nation that once sent men to the moon cannot now use its technology and wealth to solve the problems facing Earth.

### 美宇航员纪念登月20周年  专家慨叹能登月却不能解决地球问题
启昕
1989-07-17
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    美宇航员纪念登月20周年
    专家慨叹能登月却不能解决地球问题
    本报华盛顿7月15日电  20年前的7月20日，美国“阿波罗11号”的宇航员尼尔·阿姆斯特朗和埃德温·奥尔德林空军上校首次登上月球。为纪念登月20周年，他们和另一名“阿波罗11号”的宇航员迈克尔·柯林斯空军中校于7月15日开始在美国作一周旅行。
    三人从阿拉斯加州的亨茨维尔宇航中心出发，16日抵达卡纳维拉尔角的肯尼迪宇航中心。当年这一天，“阿波罗11号”就是从这里发射升空的。在华盛顿的国家宇航馆，他们将参加一个招待会并于20日同布什总统一起出席登月20周年的庆祝仪式。
    但在纪念往日成就之际，“阿波罗11号”机长阿姆斯特朗不无遗憾地说，回首当年，我们对现在不再有所作为而感失望。乔治·华盛顿大学宇宙研究所主任约翰·洛格斯登说，我们以怀旧心情回顾20年间，不禁自问为什么一度能把人送往月球的国家，现在却不能用它的技术与财富解决地球上面临的问题。（启昕）
