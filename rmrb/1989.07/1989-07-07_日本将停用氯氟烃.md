Title: Japan to Phase Out CFCs
Author:
Date: 1989-07-07
Edition: 3

Japan's refrigeration and air conditioning industry association decided on July 3 to completely stop using chlorofluorocarbons (CFCs), which deplete the Earth's ozone layer, by 1998.

From now until 1993, this industry sector will strive to research and develop refrigerants that can replace CFCs and put them into use. In terms of hardware, the first step is to reduce the amount of CFCs leaked by machines, then improve the machines' refrigerant-saving performance, and finally manufacture and sell new-type refrigerants that do not contain CFCs.

Thus, air conditioners used in Japanese buildings and hospitals, car air conditioning systems, refrigerated trucks, refrigerators, cooling machines, and ice machines will be completely updated and replaced by 1996 or 1998, thereby eliminating the use of CFC-containing equipment.

### 日本将停用氯氟烃

1989-07-07
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    日本将停用氯氟烃
    新华社东京7月4日电　日本冷冻空调工业会3日决定，到1998年该业产品完全停止使用破坏地球臭氧层的氯氟烃。
    从现在起至1993年，这个工业部门将努力研究开发能够代替氯氟烃的制冷剂并将其投入应用。在硬件方面，首先设法降低机器泄漏氯氟烃的量，然后提高机器节省制冷剂性能，最后制造和销售采用非氯氟烃的新型制冷剂。
    这样，日本楼房、医院等使用的空调机，日本汽车内空调设备、冷冻车、冰箱、制冷机、制冰机将分别到1996年或1998年全部更新换代，从而废除使用氯氟烃设备。
