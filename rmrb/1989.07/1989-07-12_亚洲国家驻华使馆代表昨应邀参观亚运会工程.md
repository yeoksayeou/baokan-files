Title: Representatives of Asian Countries' Embassies in China Visit Asian Games Construction Sites
Author: Lǐ Hèpǔ
Date: 1989-07-12
Edition: 4

Representatives of Asian Countries' Embassies in China Visit Asian Games Construction Sites

New China News Agency, Beijing, July 12 - (Reporter Li Hepu) Today, more than 30 representatives from the embassies or commercial offices of 23 member countries of the Asian Olympic Council in China were invited by the organizing committee of the 11th Asian Games to visit several sports venues under construction in Beijing.

The guests visited the newly renovated Beijing Workers' Stadium, the newly built Yuetan Gymnasium, the Beijing Institute of Physical Education Gymnasium, and the under-construction Northern Suburbs Sports Center and Athletes' Village Service Center.

Secretary-General Wan Siquan of the Asian Games Organizing Committee briefed the guests on the preparations for the Asian Games. He said that the 27 competition venues required for the 11th Asian Games and other projects, including the Athletes' Village Service Center, will be completed as scheduled. He revealed that in August, the president of the Asian Olympic Council, Fahad, will come to Beijing to inspect the Asian Games venues. In September, China will officially send out invitations to participate in the Asian Games to all member countries and regions of the Asian Olympic Council.

Vice-Chairmen He Zhenliang and Zhang Baifa of the Asian Games Organizing Committee reiterated to the guests that China will do everything possible to ensure the success of the Asian Games. Nepali Charge d'Affaires Dawadi, representing the embassy and commercial office personnel of various countries, thanked the Asian Games Organizing Committee for providing the opportunity to visit. He said, "The construction scale of the Beijing Asian Games is very large, and I believe the Asian Games will definitely be successful."

The representatives from the embassies of Myanmar, Laos, Malaysia, the Philippines, Thailand, Vietnam, Iran, Kuwait, Oman, Syria, the Yemen Arab Republic, the People's Democratic Republic of Yemen, Palestine, the Democratic People's Republic of Korea, Japan, Mongolia, Afghanistan, Bangladesh, India, Nepal, Pakistan, Sri Lanka, and the commercial office of Singapore in China were invited to participate in today's visit.

### 亚洲国家驻华使馆代表昨应邀参观亚运会工程
李贺普
1989-07-12
第4版(综合)
专栏：

    亚洲国家驻华使馆代表昨应邀参观亚运会工程
    新华社北京7月12日电　（记者李贺普）今天，23个亚洲奥林匹克理事会成员国的驻华使馆或商务处的代表30多人，应第11届亚运会组委会的邀请参观了北京亚运会部分体育场馆工程。
    客人们参观了修建一新的北京工人体育场，新建的月坛体育馆、北京体育学院体育馆，以及正在建设中的北郊体育中心和运动员村服务中心。
    亚运会组委会秘书长万嗣铨向客人介绍了亚运会筹备工作情况。他说，第11届亚运会各项比赛所需的27个比赛场馆工程和亚运村服务中心等工程将按计划全部完成。他透露，今年8月，亚奥理事会主席法赫德将来京检查亚运会场馆情况。9月，中国方面将正式向亚奥理事会各成员国家和地区发出参赛邀请书。
    亚运会组委会副主席何振梁、张百发在会见客人时重申，中国方面将尽一切力量把亚运会办好。尼泊尔驻华使馆参赞达瓦迪代表各国使馆、商务处人员感谢亚运会组委会为他们提供这次参观机会。他说：“北京亚运会工程建设规模很大，相信亚运会一定会取得成功。”
    应邀参加今天参观活动的有，缅甸、老挝、马来西亚、菲律宾、泰国、越南、伊朗、科威特、阿曼、叙利亚、阿拉伯也门共和国、也门民主人民共和国、巴勒斯坦、朝鲜民主主义人民共和国、日本、蒙古、阿富汗、孟加拉、印度、尼泊尔、巴基斯坦、斯里兰卡的驻华使馆代表和新加坡驻华商务处人员。
