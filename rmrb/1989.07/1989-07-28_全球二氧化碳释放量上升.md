Title: Global Carbon Dioxide Emissions on the Rise
Author:
Date: 1989-07-28
Edition: 3

    Global carbon dioxide emissions continue to rise, with 1987 marking the fourth consecutive year of increase. The United States is the largest emitter.

    Scientist Greg Marland of the Oak Ridge National Laboratory in the United States said that in 1987, power plants, factories, and motor vehicles worldwide released 5.65 billion tons of carbon dioxide, which contributes to global warming. This is an increase of 1.53% over the 5.57 billion tons released in 1986. Between 1980 and 1987, global emissions increased by 7.3%.

    Marland said that the rate of increase in the United States in 1987 was higher than the global average, reaching 1.92%. It is estimated that in 1988, the increase was 3%. Currently, the United States accounts for 22% of global carbon dioxide emissions, more than any other country.

    Marland said that the regions with the fastest increase in carbon dioxide emissions are South Asia and Southeast Asia.

### 全球二氧化碳释放量上升

1989-07-28
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    全球二氧化碳释放量上升
    新华社华盛顿7月27日电　美国科学家说，全球二氧化碳释放量继续上升，1987年是连续第4年上升，美国是释放量最大的国家。
    美国橡树岭国家实验室的科学家格雷格·马兰说，1987年全球电厂、工厂和机动车辆释放出的导致全球变暖的二氧化碳高达56.5亿吨，比1986年的55.7亿吨高出1.53％。在1980年—1987年间，全球释放量增加了7.3％。
    马兰说，1987年美国的二氧化碳释放量上升的比例比全球高，达到1.92％。1988年估计又增加了3％。目前，美国的二氧化碳释放量占全球总数的22％，比任何一个国家都高。
    马兰说，全球二氧化碳释放量上升最快的是南亚和东南亚地区。
