Title: Harbin Port Opens to the Soviet Union
Author: Zǔ Bóguāng
Date: 1989-07-10
Edition: 2

Today at 10 a.m., the Longhuo 302 cargo ship, bound for Khabarovsk, the largest city in the Soviet Far East, sounded its whistle and slowly set sail, marking the reopening of the China-Soviet water trade route, which had been suspended for 26 years.

Harbin Port currently has a cargo handling capacity of 500,000 tons. This year, it is expected to handle 200,000 to 300,000 tons of cargo for Sino-Soviet trade. After five years of expansion and renovation, the port's capacity will reach over 5 million tons.

In addition to Harbin Port, the State Council has approved the opening of Jiamusi Port and Fujin Port in Heilongjiang Province. The Soviet Union also opened Khabarovsk, Komsomolsk-on-Amur, and Boyarkovo on June 15 and July 1, respectively.

### 哈尔滨港对苏开通
祖伯光
1989-07-10
第2版(国内新闻)
专栏：四面八方

    哈尔滨港对苏开通
    新华社哈尔滨7月10日电　（记者祖伯光）今天上午10时，首航苏联远东最大城市哈巴罗夫斯克的龙货302号货轮汽笛长鸣，缓缓起动，标志着中断26年的中苏水运贸易航线重新开通。
    哈尔滨港现有货物通过能力50万吨，今年可实现中苏贸易过货20万吨至30万吨。今后5年经过扩建改造，通过能力可达500万吨以上。
    这次经国务院批准的开放港口除哈尔滨港以外还有黑龙江省的佳木斯港和富锦港。苏联也于6月15日和7月1日分别开放了哈巴罗夫斯克、共青城和波亚尔科沃3个港口。
