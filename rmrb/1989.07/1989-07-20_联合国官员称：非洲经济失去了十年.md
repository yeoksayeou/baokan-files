Title: UN Official: Africa Has Lost a Decade
Author:
Date: 1989-07-20
Page: 3

Xinhua News Agency, Addis Ababa, July 18th.

The Executive Secretary of the United Nations Economic Commission for Africa, Adebayo Adedeji, stated on July 18th at the 50th regular session of the Council of Ministers of the Organization of African Unity that Africa should firmly grasp its own destiny and mobilize all its people to work together for long-term development over the next 10 years.

Adedeji said that the 1980s were a "lost decade" for Africa, with all economic indicators declining compared to 10 years ago. From 1978 to 1988, the per capita gross domestic product fell from $854 to $565, the investment ratio dropped from 25.2% to 15.8%, while external debt increased from $48.3 billion to $230 billion. The number of least developed countries rose from 17 in 1979 to 28 in 1988.

Adedeji expressed a pessimistic view of Africa's economic situation in 1989. He said that Africa's GDP grew by 2.5% at the end of last year, but only by 2.2% in the first half of this year.

### 联合国官员称：非洲经济失去了十年

1989-07-20
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    联合国官员称：非洲经济失去了十年
    新华社亚的斯亚贝巴7月18日电　联合国非洲经济委员会执行秘书阿德德吉18日在非统组织部长理事会第50次例会上发表讲话说，在未来10年非洲的长期发展中，非洲应牢牢掌握自己的命运，动员全体人民共同努力。
    阿德德吉说，80年代是非洲“失去的10年”，所有的经济指标都比10年前下降了。从1978年到1988年，年人均国内生产总值从854美元降到565美元，投资比重从25.2％下降到15.8％，而外债则由483亿美元增加到2300亿元，最不发达国家的数目则从1979年的17个增加到了1988年的28个。
    阿德德吉对非洲1989年的经济形势表示悲观的看法。他说，去年年底非洲国内生产总值增长2.5％，今年上半年只增长2.2％。
