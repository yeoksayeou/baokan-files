Title: A Poll Indicates Americans More Concerned About Japanese Economic Threat
Author:
Date: 1989-07-30
Edition: 3

A recent poll indicates that the majority of Americans are more concerned about the economic threat posed by Japan than the military threat posed by the Soviet Union.

Among the 1250 adults surveyed, 68% of respondents believed that Japan poses the greatest threat to America's future, while only 22% thought the Soviet Union was the biggest threat.

In responses regarding trade issues between the U.S. and Japan, 68% of respondents believed that Japan's unfair trade barriers are the main problem. 57% thought that the high prices of American products are the main obstacle to their entry into the Japanese market, and 38% believed that the main problem lies in the quality of American products.

The U.S. has a trade deficit of over $50 billion with Japan, which has affected the relationship between the two countries.

In the survey, approximately two-thirds of respondents supported imposing higher tariffs on imports from Japan and restricting the flow of American technology to Japan.

### 一项民意调查表明  美国人更担心日本经济威胁

1989-07-30
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    一项民意调查表明
    美国人更担心日本经济威胁
    新华社华盛顿7月28日电  美国最近进行的一项民意调查表明，大多数美国人更担心日本的经济威胁，而不是苏联的军事威胁。
    在接受调查的1250名成年人中，68％的答复者认为对美国的未来构成最大威胁的是日本，只有22％的人认为苏联是最大的威胁。
    在对有关美日贸易问题的答复中，68％的人认为日本的不公平贸易壁垒是主要问题，57％的人认为美国产品价格高是妨碍其进入日本市场的主要障碍，还有38％的人认为主要问题出在美国的产品质量上。
    美国在对日贸易中有500多亿美元的逆差，这已影响到两国之间的关系。
    在调查中，约2／3的答复者支持对来自日本的进口品征收更高的关税，并限制美国的技术流入日本。
