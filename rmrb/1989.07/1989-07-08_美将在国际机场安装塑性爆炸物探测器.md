Title: The U.S. to Install Plastic Explosive Detectors at International Airports
Author:
Date: 1989-07-08
Page: 3

Xinhua News Agency, Washington, July 7th.

The United States has decided to install high-tech devices capable of detecting plastic explosives at 40 airports in the U.S. and abroad.

According to a draft federal aviation regulation announced yesterday, these devices will be installed at some of the busiest international airports in the U.S. and at major U.S. facilities abroad.

The first device will be installed by the end of this month at New York's Kennedy International Airport. The second device will be installed at London's Gatwick Airport.

Federal aviation officials said it will take two to three years to install the 40 detectors, each costing between $750,000 and $1 million. The government will cover the costs.

The U.S. made this decision following the explosion of a Pan Am aircraft over Scotland on December 21 last year, which resulted in the deaths of 270 people. Investigators believe the explosion was caused by a plastic bomb.

### 美将在国际机场安装塑性爆炸物探测器

1989-07-08
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    美将在国际机场安装塑性爆炸物探测器
    新华社华盛顿7月7日电　美国决定在40个美国和外国机场安装可探测塑性爆炸物的高技术装置。
    根据昨天宣布的联邦航空管理条例草案，该装置将安装在美国一些最繁忙的国际机场和美国在外国使用的一些主要设施。
    第一个装置将于本月底在纽约肯尼迪国际机场安装完毕。第二个装置将安装在伦敦的盖特维克机场。
    据联邦航空官员说，安装这40个探测装置需花费2至3年的时间，每个探测器耗资75万到100万美元。这笔费用将由政府支付。
    美国作出这一决定是因为去年12月21日泛美航空客机在苏格兰爆炸，造成了270人死亡。调查人员认为该飞机爆炸是一枚塑性炸弹引起的。
