Title: Japan Extracts Substance That Inhibits AIDS Virus
Author:
Date: 1989-07-21
Page: 3

Xinhua News Agency, Beijing, July 21st.

Japanese researchers have extracted a chemical substance from pulp waste liquid that can effectively prevent the AIDS virus.

The report states that a joint research team from the pulp company of San'yō Corporation and Tottori University discovered that after removing cellulose from a mixture of wood chips and sulfuric acid, the waste liquid contains lignin sulfonate, which can extremely effectively inhibit the AIDS virus.

Experiments have shown that lignin sulfonate's ability to inhibit the AIDS virus is fully comparable to the current treatment for AIDS, AZT.

### 日提取抑制艾滋病毒物质

1989-07-21
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    日提取抑制艾滋病毒物质
    新华社北京7月21日电　据外电报道，日本研究人员从纸浆废液中提取一种能有效地防止艾滋病毒的化学物质。
    消息说，三洋株式会社纸浆公司和鸟取大学的联合研究小组发现，从木屑和硫酸混合物中除去纤维素后的废液中，提取的木素磺酸能极其有效地抑制艾滋病毒。
    试验表明，木素磺酸抑制艾滋病毒的能力完全可与目前治疗艾滋病的AZT匹敌。
