Title: Ethnic Dispute Leads to Conflict and Casualties; Curfew Imposed in Soviet Autonomous Republic
Author:
Date: 1989-07-19
Edition: 3

The Presidium of the Supreme Soviet of the Abkhaz Autonomous Republic in the Soviet republic of Georgia decided to impose a curfew throughout the autonomous republic starting at 11 PM local time on July 18.

On July 15 and 16, residents of Sukhumi, the capital of the Abkhaz Autonomous Republic, clashed over the issue of whether to establish a branch of the Tbilisi University in the city. The conflict resulted in 11 deaths and 127 injuries.

According to TASS, the situation in Sukhumi continues to deteriorate, with armed groups repeatedly attacking the local police headquarters. By 12 AM on July 19, the death toll had risen to 18.

The report states that most enterprises in the troubled areas have ceased operations, transportation has been disrupted, and there are difficulties in supplying food and fuel.

The police have formed a special task force to investigate illegal activities that disrupt ethnic relations based on public reports.

### 民族纠纷造成冲突和伤亡  苏一自治共和国实行宵禁

1989-07-19
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    民族纠纷造成冲突和伤亡  苏一自治共和国实行宵禁
    新华社莫斯科7月19日电  苏联格鲁吉亚阿布哈兹自治共和国最高苏维埃主席团决定，从当地时间18日23时起，在全自治共和国范围内实行宵禁。
    本月15日和16日，格鲁吉亚阿布哈兹自治共和国首府苏呼米市的阿布哈兹族和格鲁吉亚族居民发生冲突，造成11人死亡、127人受伤。冲突的起因是两族居民在要不要在苏呼米市开设第比利斯大学分校的问题上意见不一致。
    据塔斯社报道，目前苏呼米市的局势仍在继续恶化，一些武装团伙多次袭击内务部机关。到18日24时，死亡人数已增至18人。
    报道说，骚乱地区的大部分企业已经停工，交通中断，食品和燃料供应发生困难。
    内务部机关为了稳定局势已组成专门小组，调查群众检举的破坏民族关系的违法行为。
