Title: Mandela Says Dialogue Can Bring Peace
Author:
Date: 1989-07-14
Edition: 3

    Mandela Says Dialogue Can Bring Peace

    Xinhua News Agency, Gaborone, July 13 — Imprisoned leader of the African National Congress of South Africa, Nelson Mandela, said in a statement on July 10 that dialogue between the South African government and the mass democratic movement, especially the African National Congress of South Africa, is the only way to end violence and bring peace to South Africa.

    The South African prison authorities released Mandela's statement regarding his meeting with South African President P.W. Botha on the evening of July 12.

    Mandela said he issued the statement to respond to media comments about his meeting with Botha. He said he only wanted to contribute to creating an atmosphere conducive to peace in South Africa.

### 曼德拉说对话才能带来和平

1989-07-14
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    曼德拉说对话才能带来和平
    新华社哈博罗内7月13日电　被监禁的南非非洲人国民大会领导人纳尔逊·曼德拉在10日的声明中说，南非政府同群众民主运动，特别是同南非非洲人国民大会之间的对话，是结束暴力和给南非带来和平的唯一途径。
    南非监狱当局12日晚公布了曼德拉有关他同南非总统博塔会晤的声明。
    曼德拉说，他发表声明是为了答复新闻媒介对有关他同博塔会晤发表的评论。他说，他只想为建立促进南非和平的气氛作出贡献。
