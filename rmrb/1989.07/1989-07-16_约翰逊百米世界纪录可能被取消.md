Title: Ben Johnson's 100m World Record May Be Cancelled
Author:
Date: 1989-07-16
Edition: 4

As calls to ban performance-enhancing drugs grow louder in the international sports community, the International Amateur Athletic Federation (IAAF) is considering a series of new measures. If these measures are formally adopted, the 100-meter world record set by Canadian sprinter Ben Johnson two years ago will no longer stand.

The IAAF Council held an informal meeting in Vienna on July 15, where it approved a 12-point proposal. A statement issued after the meeting said, "If an athlete who holds a world, continental, or national record admits to violating the ban on performance-enhancing drugs, that record will no longer be recognized."

These new measures will be discussed at the IAAF Council's full meeting in Barcelona, Spain, on September 4, and will be voted on at the IAAF Congress on September 5 and 6. Once the IAAF adopts these measures, Johnson's world championship title and 9.83-second world record from the 1987 World Athletics Championships in Rome, his bronze medal from the 1984 Olympics, and his gold medal from the 1986 Commonwealth Games will all be declared null and void.

The IAAF also announced that it is considering adopting a measure known as the "challenge system" to combat the use of performance-enhancing drugs. This proposal, put forward by the United States, suggests that any member federation of the IAAF can request that the IAAF conduct drug tests on athletes from another member federation.

### 约翰逊百米世界纪录可能被取消

1989-07-16
第4版(综合)
专栏：

    约翰逊百米世界纪录可能被取消
    新华社北京7月16日电  据外电报道，在国际体坛要求禁用兴奋剂的呼声日益高涨之时，国际业余田径联合会正在考虑采取一系列新的措施。如果这些措施被正式通过采纳，加拿大短跑运动员本·约翰逊两年前创造的男子百米世界纪录就再也“保”不住了。
    国际田联理事会7月15日在维也纳举行的一次非正式的会议上，通过了一项有12点内容的新建议。会后发表的一项声明说，“如果保持某项世界、洲际或全国纪录的运动员承认违反了不得使用兴奋剂的禁令，该项纪录即不再成立”。
    这些新措施将于9月4日在西班牙巴塞罗那举行的国际田联理事会全体会议上进行研究，并在9月5日、6日的田联代表大会上付诸表决。一旦国际田联通过了这些措施，约翰逊在1987年罗马世界田径锦标赛上获得的世界冠军称号和当时创造的9秒83的世界纪录、在1984年奥运会上获得的铜牌和1986年英联邦运动会上获得的金牌，将统统被宣布取消。
    国际田联还宣布，它正在考虑采用一种被称为“挑战制”的用来对付使用兴奋剂行为的措施。由美国提出的这项措施建议，国际田联所属的任何一个联合会有权要求国际田联对另一成员联合会的选手进行兴奋剂抽查。
