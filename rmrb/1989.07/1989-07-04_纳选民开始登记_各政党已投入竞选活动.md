Title: Namibian Voters Begin Registration
Author:
Date: 1989-07-04
Edition: 3

Namibian voters eligible to participate in the elections began registering today for the independence referendum scheduled for November. A South African official in the Namibian administration said that 36 permanent and 35 temporary registration stations have started operations this morning.

Additionally, more than 100 mobile voter registration teams will head to remote areas tomorrow to carry out this work.

The South West Africa People's Organization (SWAPO) issued a campaign manifesto yesterday. The manifesto states that the organization pledges to establish an independent, unified, and democratic state in Namibia, without full nationalization of mining and land.

Other political parties and organizations, including the South Africa-backed Democratic Turnhalle Alliance, have also entered the campaign.

However, the United Nations Secretary-General's Special Representative for Namibia, Martti Ahtisaari, expressed concern about the serious situation in northern Namibia after a three-day inspection of the region.

### 纳选民开始登记  各政党已投入竞选活动

1989-07-04
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    纳选民开始登记
    各政党已投入竞选活动
    新华社温得和克7月3日电　有资格参加选举的纳米比亚选民今天开始为参加11月举行的独立前大选进行注册登记。
    南非驻纳米比亚行政当局一位官方发言人今天在这里说，设在各地的36个永久性和35个临时性登记站今晨已开始工作。
    此外，100多个流动选民登记小组明天将赴边远地区进行这项工作。
    西南非洲人民组织昨天发表竞选宣言。宣言说，该组织保证在纳米比亚建立一个独立、统一和民主的国家，对矿业和土地不实现全面国有化。
    包括南非支持的特恩哈尔民主联盟在内的其他政党和组织也投入竞选活动。
    然而，联合国秘书长负责纳米比亚问题的特别代表马尔蒂·阿赫蒂萨里在对纳米比亚北部地区进行3天视察后，仍对那里的严重局势感到焦虑。
