Title: A South Korean Sailor Defects to the North
Author:
Date: 1989-07-16
Page: 3

Xinhua News Agency, Pyongyang, July 15th.

A sailor from South Korea's Il Dong Commercial Shipping Company, Kim Se-Jong, has defected to North Korea via a third country and arrived in Pyongyang on July 14th.

Kim Se-Jong, 23, was born in Jeollabuk-do, South Korea. At the Pyongyang Airport, he spoke about his reasons for defecting to the North, stating that as a sailor, he had witnessed firsthand the aggressive actions of the United States and was outraged by the South Korean authorities' subservience to the United States. He had been preparing to defect to the North for the past year. This time, he seized the opportunity while his ship was in a third country and resolutely defected to the North.

### 南朝鲜一船员投奔北方

1989-07-16
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    南朝鲜一船员投奔北方
    新华社平壤7月15日电　据朝鲜中央通讯社报道，南朝鲜日东商业海运株式会社船员金世钟通过第三国投奔朝鲜北方，14日抵达平壤。
    金世钟今年23岁，出生于南朝鲜全罗北道。他在平壤机场谈到投奔北方的动机时说，他作为船员在海上直接目睹了美国的侵略行为，对南朝鲜当权者屈从于美国表示义愤。在1年前就开始做投奔北方的准备。这次利用乘船抵达第3国机会，毅然投奔北方。
