Title: Mozambican President Meets with South African Ruling Party Leader
Author:
Date: 1989-07-20
Edition: 3

Mozambican President Chissano met with the leader of the South African ruling party, De Klerk, who is likely to succeed William Botha as the new South African president, in Maputo today.

Both sides discussed ways to promote bilateral relations and exchanged views on the situation in southern Africa and the 14-year-long civil war in Mozambique.

The talks lasted three hours. In a press conference held after the meeting, De Klerk said that the Mozambican opposition group, the National Resistance Movement, should "stop violent struggles and actively participate in Mozambique's peace process." He expressed that South Africa hopes to "play a constructive role without interfering in Mozambique's internal affairs."

### 莫桑比克总统  会晤南非执政党领导人

1989-07-20
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    莫桑比克总统
    会晤南非执政党领导人
    据新华社马普托7月19日电　莫桑比克总统希萨诺今天在这里同有可能接替威廉·博塔成为南非新总统的国民党领导人德克勒克举行了会谈。
    双方讨论了如何促进双边关系的问题，并就南部非洲局势和持续了14年之久的莫桑比克内战问题交换了看法。
    会谈进行了三个小时。德克勒克在会谈之后举行的记者招待会上说，莫桑比克反政府的全国抵抗运动应当“停止暴力斗争，积极投入莫桑比克的和平进程”。他表示，南非希望“在不干涉莫桑比克内政的情况下发挥建设作用”。
