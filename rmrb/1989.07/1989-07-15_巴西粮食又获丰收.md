Title: Brazil Sees Another Bountiful Harvest
Author: Lan Caiji
Date: 1989-07-15
Page: 3

People's Daily, Brasília, July 14th.

Brazil's 1988-1989 agricultural year is coming to an end, with major grain crops such as corn, rice, beans, and soybeans all experiencing a bumper harvest.

According to statistics from the National Production Fund Company, this year's grain and soybean production is expected to reach 70.35 million tons, an increase of 4 million tons over the previous harvest year, setting a new historical record. Among these, corn production is 25.27 million tons, soybeans 23.70 million tons, rice 11.00 million tons, and beans 1.30 million tons.

Since 1986, Brazil has seen three consecutive years of bumper harvests. This not only ensures domestic grain and food supply but also expands exports. Last year, Brazil's exports of soybeans and related products alone brought in nearly $3 billion.

Currently, Brazil has become the world's second-largest agricultural product exporter, second only to the United States.

### 巴西粮食又获丰收
兰才基
1989-07-15
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    巴西粮食又获丰收
    本报巴西利亚7月14日电  记者兰才基报道：巴西1988—1989农业年度收获季节即将结束，主要的粮食作物玉米、稻谷、杂豆以及大豆都获得丰收。
    据全国生产资金公司所作的统计，本年度的粮食和大豆产量可达到7035万吨，比上一个收获年度增产400万吨，创历史最高纪录。其中玉米产量为2527万吨，大豆2370万吨，稻谷1100万吨，杂豆130万吨。
    自1986年以来，巴西农业连续3年获得丰收，这不仅保证了国内粮食和食品供应，而且扩大了出口，去年巴西仅大豆及制成品的出口创汇额就达到近30亿美元。
    目前巴西已跃居为仅次于美国的世界第二大农产品出口国。
