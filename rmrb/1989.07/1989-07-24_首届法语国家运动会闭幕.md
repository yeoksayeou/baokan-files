Title: The First Francophone Games Closing Ceremony
Author: Liǔ Zuòwén
Date: 1989-07-24
Edition: 4

The first Francophone Games concluded on July 22 in Casablanca, Morocco. The Canadian football team won the championship in the final match of the football competition held during the closing ceremony, defeating the host team Morocco 4-1.

The games, which featured 38 participating countries, began on July 8 and included competitions in football, basketball, track and field, as well as artistic events such as painting, sculpture, video, dance, and handicrafts. French athletes achieved the best results, winning a total of 31 gold medals, 23 silver medals, and 19 bronze medals. Canadian athletes won a total of 56 medals, including 11 gold, 18 silver, and 27 bronze. Moroccan athletes ranked third in the number of medals won, with 6 gold, 7 silver, and 6 bronze medals.

The second Francophone Games will be held in France in 1993.

### 首届法语国家运动会闭幕
刘作文
1989-07-24
第4版(综合)
专栏：体育

    首届法语国家运动会闭幕
    新华社卡萨布兰卡七月二十二日电　（记者刘作文）首届法语国家运动会二十二日在摩洛哥的卡萨布兰卡结束。加拿大足球队在闭幕式上进行的本届运动会足球决赛中，以四比一战胜东道主摩洛哥队，赢得冠军。
    有三十八个国家参加的本届法语国家运动会于本月八日开始，进行了足球、篮球、田径等体育项目比赛和绘画、雕刻、录像、舞蹈、手工制品等艺术项目的竞赛。结果，法国选手成绩最佳，共获三十一枚金牌、二十三枚银牌和十九枚铜牌，加拿大选手共获奖牌五十六枚，其中金牌十一枚、银牌十八枚、铜牌二十七枚；摩洛哥选手获得的奖牌数名列第三，其中金牌六枚、银牌七枚、铜牌六枚。
    第二届法语国家运动会将于一九九三年在法国举行。
