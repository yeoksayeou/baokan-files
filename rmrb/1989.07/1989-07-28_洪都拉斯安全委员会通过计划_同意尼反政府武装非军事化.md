Title: Honduras Security Council Approves Plan for Demilitarization of Nicaraguan Anti-Government Forces
Author: Zhū Mǎn Tíng
Date: 1989-07-28
Page: 3

People's Daily, Caracas, July 28th, Reporter Zhu Mǎn Tíng reports: The National Security Council of Honduras approved a plan on the evening of the 26th to allow Nicaraguan anti-government forces to lay down their arms and demilitarize.

President Ascona stated that the plan for the demilitarization of Nicaraguan anti-government forces should be approved by the National Security Council before being submitted to the Central American summit for discussion.

Currently, there are approximately 11,000 Nicaraguan anti-government forces stationed in Honduras. The plan considers the possibility of these forces handing over their weapons to the Organization of American States, but President Ascona noted that this point still needs to be approved by the Central American summit. President Ascona said that the issue of the withdrawal of Nicaraguan anti-government forces from Honduras does not depend on the decisions of these individuals but will be decided by the Central American countries and by Honduras. He said, of course, we must act prudently to avoid conflicts between the Nicaraguan anti-government forces and our armed forces during the demilitarization process. Nicaragua also needs to create a favorable atmosphere for those who wish to leave Nicaragua and go to Honduras to withdraw voluntarily.

### 洪都拉斯安全委员会通过计划  同意尼反政府武装非军事化
朱满庭
1989-07-28
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    洪都拉斯安全委员会通过计划
    同意尼反政府武装非军事化
    本报加拉加斯7月28日电　记者朱满庭报道：洪都拉斯国家安全委员会26日晚通过一项计划，同意尼加拉瓜反政府武装放下武器，实行非军事化。
    阿斯科纳总统说，尼加拉瓜反政府武装非军事化计划在提交中美洲首脑会议讨论之前，应由国家安全委员会先期通过。
    目前，驻扎在洪都拉斯境内的尼加拉瓜反政府武装人员约1.1万人。计划考虑尼反政府组织将武器交给美洲国家组织，但阿斯科纳总统表示，这一点还有待于中美洲首脑会议的最后通过。阿斯科纳总统说，尼反政府人员撤离洪都拉斯的问题不取决于这些人自己的决定，而要由中美洲各国、由洪都拉斯决定。他说，当然，我们要慎重从事，避免在非军事化过程中，尼加拉瓜反政府武装与我们的武装部队发生冲突，尼加拉瓜也有必要创造有利的气氛，使愿意离尼加拉瓜到洪都拉斯的人主动撤离。
