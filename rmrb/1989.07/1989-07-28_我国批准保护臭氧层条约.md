Title: China Approves Treaty to Protect the Ozone Layer
Author:
Date: 1989-07-28
Edition: 3

China Approves Treaty to Protect the Ozone Layer

NAIROBI, July 27 (Xinhua) -- The United Nations Environment Programme today announced that China has approved the Vienna Convention for the Protection of the Ozone Layer.

China's State Environmental Protection Administration informed the executive director of the organization, Mustafa Tolba, that China considers the approval of this convention an important step in its cooperation with the international community to protect the ozone layer.

The Vienna Convention for the Protection of the Ozone Layer was signed in 1985, stipulating that it would come into effect after being ratified by the parliaments of the signatory countries. So far, 48 countries have ratified this convention.

Currently, the ozone layer in the atmosphere is being severely damaged, particularly over the polar regions. The release of chlorofluorocarbons (CFCs), widely used in industries from plastics to electronics, poses the primary threat to the ozone layer.

Following the Vienna Convention, 43 countries signed a protocol in Montreal in 1987, regulating the production and use of CFCs.

A spokesperson for the programme said today that China's cooperation is crucial for the international community's efforts to gradually phase out the use of CFCs.

### 我国批准保护臭氧层条约

1989-07-28
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    我国批准保护臭氧层条约
    新华社内罗毕7月27日电  联合国环境规划署今天在这里宣布，中国批准了维也纳保护臭氧层条约。
    中国国家环境保护局在给该组织秘书处执行主任穆斯塔法·托尔巴的通报中说，中国认为，批准此项条约是中国同国际社会一起为保护臭氧层而迈出的重要步骤。
    维也纳保护臭氧层条约是1985年签订的，当时规定它必须经过各签字国议会批准后方能生效。迄今为止，已有48个国家先后批准了这项条约。
    目前，大气中的臭氧层正在遭到破坏，南北极地区上空尤为严重。释放从塑料工业到电子工业中普遍使用的含氯氟烃气是对臭氧层的主要威胁。
    继维也纳条约之后，43个国家又于1987年在蒙特利尔签订了一项议定书，对制造和使用含氯氟烃气作了规定。
    规划署一位发言人今天说，中国的合作对于国际上为逐步停止使用含氯氟烃气具有至关重要的作用。
