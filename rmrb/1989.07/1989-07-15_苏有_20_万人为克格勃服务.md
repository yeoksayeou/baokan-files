Title: The Soviet Union Has 200,000 People Serving in the KGB
Author:
Date: 1989-07-15
Edition: 3

The Soviet Union has 200,000 people serving in the KGB, according to KGB Chairman Vladimir Kryuchkov, who revealed this information on July 14 during a session of the Supreme Soviet of the USSR.

Kryuchkov stated that the KGB is composed of intelligence agencies, counterintelligence agencies, and border troops. He denied that this organization engages in wiretapping activities, asserting that the Soviet Union does not have an informant system and that the KGB does not maintain files on law-abiding citizens. The tasks of the KGB include combating terrorism, smuggling, and drug trafficking. In the past three years, the Soviet KGB has seized 12 tons of drugs.

### 苏有20万人为克格勃服务

1989-07-15
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    苏有20万人为克格勃服务
    新华社莫斯科7月14日电  苏联有20万人为克格勃服务，这是苏联国家安全委员会（简称克格勃）主席克留奇科夫14日在苏联最高苏维埃会议上透露的。
    克留奇科夫说，克格勃是由情报机关、反情报机关和边防军组成。他否认这个机构从事窃听电话活动，宣称苏联没有告密制度，克格勃也不对守法的人设立档案。克格勃的任务包括反对恐怖活动、反走私和贩毒等内容。最近3年苏联克格勃就没收了12吨毒品。
