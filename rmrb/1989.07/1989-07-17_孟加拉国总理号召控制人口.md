Title: Bangladeshi Prime Minister Calls for Population Control
Author:
Date: 1989-07-17
Page: 3

Xinhua News Agency, Dhaka, July 16th.

Bangladeshi Prime Minister Mujibur Rahman Ahmed today called on the nation to launch a movement to control population growth and raise literacy rates.

He said that if the current rate of population growth is not effectively controlled, Bangladesh's economy will not be able to make significant progress.

Currently, Bangladesh's birth rate is 39 per thousand, and the death rate is 15 per thousand. As a result, the natural population growth rate is 2.4%.

The Bangladeshi government plans to control the population growth rate to below 1.8% by 1990, keeping the national population at around 110 million. Currently, Bangladesh's population is 105 million.

Ahmed urged all government departments to work together to launch a nationwide family planning campaign.

The Bangladeshi government has formulated a plan to achieve universal education and healthcare services by the year 2000.

### 孟加拉国总理号召控制人口

1989-07-17
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    孟加拉国总理号召控制人口
    新华社达卡7月16日电　孟加拉国总理穆杜德·艾哈迈德今天号召全国掀起一个控制人口和提高识字率的运动。
    他说，如果现在不能有效地控制人口增长速度，孟加拉国的经济就不可能有所突破。
    目前，孟加拉国的人口出生率为39‰，死亡率为15‰。结果，人口自然增长率达到2.4％。
    孟加拉国政府计划到1990年将人口增长率控制在1.8％以下，使全国人口保持在1.1亿左右。目前孟加拉国人口为1.05亿。
    艾哈迈德要求政府各部门协调一致，掀起一场全社会的计划生育运动。
    孟加拉国政府已制订出到2000年实现人人受教育和享受保健服务的计划。
