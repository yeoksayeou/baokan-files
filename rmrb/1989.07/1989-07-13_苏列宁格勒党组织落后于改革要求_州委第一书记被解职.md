Title: Leningrad Party Organization Lags Behind Reform Requirements
Author:
Date: 1989-07-13
Page: 3

Xinhua News Agency, Moscow, July 12th.

The first secretary of the Leningrad Oblast Committee of the Communist Party of the Soviet Union (CPSU), Solovyov, was dismissed from his post and announced his retirement on July 12th. The 56-year-old chemist Gidaspov will take over the position.

CPSU General Secretary Gorbachev and the candidate member of the Politburo and Secretary of the CPSU Central Committee responsible for organizational work, Razumovsky, traveled from Moscow to Leningrad to handle this significant personnel change.

In the evening, Gorbachev said in an interview with a television reporter that the Leningrad Party organization lags behind the requirements of reform.

### 苏列宁格勒党组织落后于改革要求  州委第一书记被解职

1989-07-13
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    苏列宁格勒党组织落后于改革要求
    州委第一书记被解职
    据新华社莫斯科7月12日电　苏共列宁格勒州委第一书记索洛维约夫12日被解除职务并宣告退休，这一职务由56岁的化学家吉达斯波夫接任。
    苏共中央总书记戈尔巴乔夫和负责组织工作的苏共中央政治局候补委员、中央书记拉祖莫夫斯基专程从莫斯科前来列宁格勒处理这一重要的人事变动。
    戈尔巴乔夫当晚在接受电视台记者采访时指出，列宁格勒党组织落后于改革的要求。
