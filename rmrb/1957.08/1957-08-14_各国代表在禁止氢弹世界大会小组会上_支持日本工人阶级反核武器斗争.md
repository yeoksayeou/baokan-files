Title: Delegates from Various Countries at the World Conference Against Hydrogen Bombs Support the Anti-Nuclear Weapons Struggle of the Japanese Working Class
Author:
Date: 1957-08-14
Page: 7

Xinhua News Agency, Tokyo, August 13th. The Third World Conference for the Prohibition of Atomic and Hydrogen Bombs and for Disarmament today split into nine groups—workers, women, religious figures, students, youth, atomic bomb victims, farmers and fishermen, representatives of local governments, and business people—to enthusiastically discuss the report submitted to the conference yesterday by the International Preparatory Meeting.

At the workers' conference, attended by more than 1,400 worker representatives and foreign delegates from the World Federation of Trade Unions, China, Australia, and other countries, delegates from various countries supported the Japanese working class's struggle to ban nuclear weapons and defend peace. During the discussions, the delegates resolutely stated that workers should become the central force in the peace movement, and the Japanese working class should unite with their worker brothers and sisters from all over the world to resolutely oppose nuclear weapons.

At the atomic bomb victims' conference, attended by more than one hundred atomic bomb victim representatives and fourteen foreign delegates, Heiichi Imoju, Secretary-General of the Atomic Bomb Victims Groups Council, and atomic bomb victims Tsuru Hiramoto from Hiroshima and Hachiro Kosasa from Nagasaki delivered speeches, expressing their gratitude to the Chinese people for their assistance. Cai Tingkai, head of the Chinese delegation, expressed his cordial greetings to the atomic bomb victims at the meeting. After a lively discussion, the meeting adopted a declaration and resolution, demanding that the Japanese government immediately formulate a law for the protection of the lives of atomic bomb victims and implement comprehensive medical treatment, and expressing the willingness of atomic bomb victims to stand at the forefront of the national movement to ban nuclear weapons and prevent atomic warfare.

At the women's conference, attended by 800 women, the representatives discussed how to break down barriers to strengthen ties between women's groups.

At the farmers' and fishermen's conference, attended by more than one hundred farmer and fisherman representatives, the representative from Okinawa Island, based on personal experience, denounced the United States' crimes of expanding atomic bases on Okinawa Island. Representatives from other regions conducted heated discussions on the impact of radioactivity on agriculture and fisheries. They expressed their determination to fight for the prohibition of nuclear weapons.

Other group meetings enthusiastically discussed the issue of banning nuclear weapons from different standpoints and angles, fully demonstrating the determination of people from all walks of life in Japan to oppose nuclear weapons and defend peace.

At a meeting attended by 600 Japanese student representatives, the discussion was very lively. They emphasized their opposition to the Eisenhower policy, opposition to the use of atomic bombs, opposition to the US-Japan joint communiqué, and opposition to the unfriendly policies adopted by the Kishi Nobusuke cabinet on the issue of Sino-Japanese relations.

The business people's conference discussed how the business community could participate in the anti-atomic bomb movement.

Xinhua News Agency, Tokyo, August 13th. The Chinese delegation attending the Third World Conference for the Prohibition of Atomic and Hydrogen Bombs and for Disarmament was invited to attend a welcome banquet hosted by the Special Committee for the Restoration of Sino-Japanese Diplomatic Relations of the Japan Socialist Party on the evening of the 13th.

Japanese attendees at the banquet included Inejiro Asanuma, Jiichiro Matsumoto, Seiichi Katsumada, Tadataka Sata, Masaru Sone, and Keita Hozumi, leaders of the Socialist Party.

During the banquet, the guests and hosts had a cordial conversation on the issue of Sino-Japanese friendship. (Attached picture)

### 各国代表在禁止氢弹世界大会小组会上  支持日本工人阶级反核武器斗争

1957-08-14
第7版()
专栏：

    各国代表在禁止氢弹世界大会小组会上
    支持日本工人阶级反核武器斗争
    新华社东京13日电　第三届禁止原子弹氢弹和争取裁军世界大会今天分为工人、妇女、宗教家、学生、青年、原子弹受害者、农渔民、地方政权代表、工商业者等九组，热烈讨论了国际预备会议昨天向大会提出的报告。
    在有一千四百多位工人代表和世界工会联合会、中国、澳大利亚等外国代表参加的工人会议上，各国代表都支持日本工人阶级争取禁止核武器、保卫和平的斗争。代表们在讨论中坚决表示，工人要成为和平运动的中心力量，日本工人阶级要同世界各国的工人兄弟团结一致，坚决反对核武器。
    在原子弹受害者代表一百多人和外国代表十四人参加的原子弹受害者会议上，受害者团体协议会事务局长薯居平一和广岛的原子弹受害者平本鹤、长崎的原子弹受害者代表小佐佐八郎先后致词，表示感谢中国人民给予他们的援助。中国代表团团长蔡廷锴在会上向原子弹受害者表示亲切的慰问。会议经过热烈讨论后，通过了宣言和决议，要求日本政府立即制定原子弹受害者生活保护法和实行全面的医疗法，并且表示原子弹受害者愿意站在禁止核武器与阻止原子战争的国民运动的最前列。
    在有八百名妇女参加的妇女会议上，代表们讨论了如何打破障碍以加强妇女团体之间的联系问题。
    在一百多名农民和渔民代表参加的农渔民会议上，冲绳岛代表以亲身的经历，控诉了美国在冲绳岛扩充原子基地的罪行。其他各地的代表就放射能对农业和渔业的影响进行了热烈的讨论。他们表示要坚决为禁止核武器而斗争。
    其他各组会议都从不同的立场和角度，热烈地讨论了禁止核武器问题，充分表现了日本各阶层人民反对核武器和保卫和平的决心。
    在有六百名日本学生代表参加的会议上，讨论进行得非常热烈。他们强调反对艾森豪威尔政策，反对使用原子弹，反对日美会谈联合公报，反对岸信介内阁在中日关系问题上所采取的不友好政策。
    工商业者会议就企业界如何参加禁止原子弹运动进行了讨论。
    新华社东京13日电　出席第三届禁止原子弹氢弹和争取裁军世界大会的中国代表团，13日晚间应邀出席了日本社会党恢复日中邦交特别委员会的欢迎宴会。
    日本方面出席宴会的有浅沼稻次郎、松本治一郎、胜间田清一、佐多忠隆、曾弥益、帆足计等社会党的领导人。
    席间，宾主就日中友好问题进行了亲切交谈。（附图片）
