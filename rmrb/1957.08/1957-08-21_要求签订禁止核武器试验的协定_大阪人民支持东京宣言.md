Title: Osaka People Support Tokyo Declaration, Demanding the Conclusion of an Agreement Prohibiting Nuclear Weapons Testing
Author:
Date: 1957-08-21
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, August 20th: Osaka Dispatch: 15,000 people from all walks of life in Osaka City, Japan, held a grand rally last night to ban atomic and hydrogen bombs. The conference passed a resolution supporting the "Tokyo Declaration" adopted by the Third World Conference Against Atomic and Hydrogen Bombs and For Disarmament.

The "Tokyo Declaration" calls on the governments of relevant countries to conclude an international agreement to immediately and unconditionally prohibit nuclear weapons testing; to prohibit the manufacture, storage, and use of nuclear weapons. The declaration also calls for universal disarmament, the abolition of all military blocs and military bases, and the withdrawal of all foreign troops from the territories of all countries.

Eighteen foreign representatives who attended the World Conference a few days ago also participated in the Osaka conference.

At the conference, representatives from all walks of life spoke one after another, demanding a ban on nuclear weapons; Obata Tadayoshi, representative of the Osaka Peace Defense Association, made a report on the Osaka City movement to ban nuclear weapons.

The conference concluded with all participants singing "No Atomic Bombs Allowed." (Picture attached)

At the Third World Conference Against Atomic and Hydrogen Bombs and For Disarmament, Zhao Puchu, Deputy Head of the Chinese Delegation, embraces an atomic bomb victim from Nagasaki.

### 要求签订禁止核武器试验的协定　大阪人民支持东京宣言

1957-08-21
第6版()
专栏：

    要求签订禁止核武器试验的协定
　　大阪人民支持东京宣言
    新华社20日讯　大阪消息：日本大阪市各界人士一万五千人昨天晚间举行了盛大的禁止原子弹氢弹大会。大会通过决议，支持第三届禁止原子弹氢弹和争取裁军世界大会所通过的“东京宣言”。
    “东京宣言”要求有关各国政府缔结一项立即无条件禁止核武器试验的国际协定；禁止制造、储存和使用核武器。宣言还要求实行普遍裁军，取消一切军事集团和军事基地，并且从所有国家的领土上撤退一切外国军队。
    参加大阪大会的还有前几天出席世界大会的外国代表十八人。
    在大会上，各界代表相继讲话，要求禁止核武器；大阪保卫和平会的代表小畑忠良作了关于大阪市争取禁止核武器运动的报告。
    大会在全体与会者高唱“不许使用原子弹”的歌声中结束。（附图片）
    在第三届禁止原子弹氢弹和争取裁军世界大会上，中国代表团副团长赵朴初和长崎的原子弹受害者拥抱。
