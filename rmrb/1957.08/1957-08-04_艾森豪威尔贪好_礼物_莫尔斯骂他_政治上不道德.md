Title: Eisenhower Enjoys "Gifts"; Morse Accuses Him of Being "Politically Immoral"
Author:
Date: 1957-08-04
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, August 3rd, Washington dispatch: U.S. Senator Morse accused Eisenhower in the Senate on August 2nd of being "politically immoral" and violating the spirit of conflict of interest laws because he accepted "tens of thousands of dollars worth" of gifts.

Morse said: "I think the President of the United States is setting a very bad example. He cannot excuse himself if he wants to use the letter of the law as a pretext for not violating this law."

Morse cited examples of Eisenhower accepting the following gifts: "A $4,000 tractor, a $1,000 bull, and other livestock and equipment for use on his farm."

The senator said that these expensive gifts violated the law prohibiting government officials and employees from accepting money or other compensation, such as gifts, that might affect government work.

### 艾森豪威尔贪好“礼物”  莫尔斯骂他“政治上不道德”

1957-08-04
第6版()
专栏：

    艾森豪威尔贪好“礼物”
    莫尔斯骂他“政治上不道德”
    新华社3日讯　华盛顿消息：美国参议员莫尔斯8月2日在参议院指责艾森豪威尔“政治上不道德”，并且违反了利益冲突法的精神，因为他接受“成千上万美元的”礼物。
    莫尔斯说：“我认为美国总统树立了一个非常不好的榜样。他如果想以没有违反这个法律的文字为借口，那是不能卸过的”。
    莫尔斯举例说，艾森豪威尔接受过下面这样一些礼物：“一辆四千美元的拖拉机，一头一千美元的牡牛和其他牲口以及设备为他的农场使用。”
    这个参议员说，这些昂贵的礼物违反了禁止政府官员和职员接受可能影响政府工作的钱或其他报酬等礼物的法律。
