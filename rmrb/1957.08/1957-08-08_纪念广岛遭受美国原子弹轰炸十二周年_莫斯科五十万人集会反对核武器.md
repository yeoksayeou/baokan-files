Title: Commemorating the Twelfth Anniversary of the US Atomic Bombing of Hiroshima, 500,000 People in Moscow Gather to Oppose Nuclear Weapons
Author:
Date: 1957-08-08
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, Moscow, July 7th. The main activities of the tenth day (August 6th) of the World Youth Festival were rallies against atomic and hydrogen weapons, a travelers' conference, and the unveiling of the results of some art competitions. August 6th was the twelfth anniversary of the US atomic bombing of Hiroshima. On this evening, a mass rally against atomic and hydrogen weapons was held at the Moscow Manege Square (see this newspaper's Moscow dispatch for details).

More than 400 youth representatives from Japan, the Soviet Union, China, India, North Korea, Vietnam, France and other countries also held a special meeting that day to demand a ban on atomic weapons. Many representatives spoke at the meeting. The participants unanimously adopted an appeal calling for a ban on atomic weapons testing. Finally, a Japanese film about the tragic atomic bombing of Hiroshima and Nagasaki was shown.

Twenty-five travelers from countries participating in the festival came by boat to the scenic Lake Seliger that morning to participate in the first International Travelers Conference, which lasted for several days. Upon arrival, they immediately engaged in sketching, photography, fishing, hunting, sports and other activities. The conference opened at twelve noon, with representatives from the Soviet Union and France, and Biro, Secretary of the World Federation of Democratic Youth, speaking at the meeting successively.

The first day of the conference included travel relays, boating, swimming, mountain climbing, rope crossing, tent pitching, and bonfire lighting. A travel film competition was held in the evening.

Participants in the Third International Youth Friendship Games and young Moscow athletes held an Olympic Friendship Evening at Gorky Central Park of Culture and Rest.

On this day, Soviet and Indian youth held a meeting. Youth from the German Democratic Republic held a meeting with youth from the Stalin District of Moscow. Young dockworkers from Denmark, France, the Netherlands, Italy and other countries visited the North Port, the Khimki Wharf and some hydraulic structures.

### 纪念广岛遭受美国原子弹轰炸十二周年  莫斯科五十万人集会反对核武器

1957-08-08
第6版()
专栏：

    纪念广岛遭受美国原子弹轰炸十二周年
    莫斯科五十万人集会反对核武器
    据新华社莫斯科7日电　世界青年联欢节第十天（8月6日）的主要活动是反对原子武器和氢武器的集会、旅行家大会和一些艺术比赛结果的揭晓。8月6日是广岛遭受美国原子弹轰炸十二周年。这天晚上，莫斯科练马场上举行了反对原子武器和氢武器群众大会（详情见本报莫斯科通讯）。
    日、苏、中、印、朝、越、法等国青年代表四百多人这一天还专门举行了会见，要求禁止原子武器。许多代表都在会上讲了话。与会者一致通过呼吁书，要求禁止原子武器试验。最后放映了关于广岛和长崎惨遭原子弹轰炸后的情景的日本电影。
    参加联欢节的二十五国旅行家们这天清晨分别乘船来到风景幽美的谢利格尔湖畔，参加为时数日的第一届国际旅行家大会。他们到达以后立即展开了写生、摄影、钓鱼、打猎、体育等活动。大会在十二时正开幕，苏法两国代表和世界民主青年联盟书记比罗相继在会上讲了话。
    大会的第一天举行了旅行接力、划船、游泳、爬山、攀绳渡河、扎帐幕和燃篝火等活动，晚间举行了旅行影片比赛。
    第三届国际青年友谊运动会参加者同莫斯科青年运动员在高尔基中央文化休息公园举行了奥林匹克友谊晚会。
    这一天苏联和印度青年举行了会见。民主德国青年同莫斯科斯大林区青年举行了会见。丹麦青年访问了弗拉基米尔·伊里奇工厂。波兰、法国、荷兰、意大利等国青年码头工人参观了北港、希姆基码头和一些水力建筑。
