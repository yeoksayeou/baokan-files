Title: Soviet Academy of Sciences Holds Meeting of Leaders of Philosophical Seminars
Author:
Date: 1960-07-22
Page: 7

According to the Soviet journal "Problems of Philosophy" (1960, Issue 5), the Soviet Academy of Sciences held a meeting of leaders, members, and advisors of philosophical seminars from various scientific research institutions to implement the decision of the Presidium of the Soviet Academy of Sciences on August 21, 1959, regarding the inclusion of philosophical seminar work in the work plans of scientific research institutions.

Academician M. Omelyanovsky of the Ukrainian Academy of Sciences delivered a report entitled "The Prospective Plan for Studying the Question of 'Dialectical Materialism and Modern Natural Science' is the Foundation of the Work of Philosophical Seminars." The speaker pointed out that it is necessary to understand the problems occurring in modern physics from a philosophical perspective. The interrelationship between space, time, and matter, and the issues of reality and regularity in physics are becoming increasingly important. Currently, the study of the micro-world and the study of cosmic processes are intertwined. Philosophically expounding on the achievements of modern astronomy will greatly help to explain the unresolved problems of microphysics and macrophysics. The development of cybernetics also raises new philosophical questions, such as the method of analogy and the method of modeling. The development of modern biology and related sciences raises issues that require a deeper study of the origin and nature of life, the interrelationship between forms of matter movement, and the interrelationship between internal and external factors, and between the biological and non-biological.

M. Omelyanovsky pointed out that in 1959, a committee of the Department of Economics, Philosophy, and Law, composed of prominent Soviet physicists, biologists, and philosophers, drafted a prospective plan for scientific research work on the issue of "Dialectical Materialism and Modern Natural Science." This plan stipulates that in the next few years, philosophers and natural scientists will jointly compile a multi-volume, encyclopedic work on the philosophical problems of modern natural science. This work is expected to have the following volumes: Volume I: Part 1 "The Philosophical Problems of Dialectical Materialism and Modern Natural Science"; Part 2 "Dialectical Materialism and Physical and Mathematical Sciences." Volume II: "Dialectical Materialism and Chemical Sciences." Volume III: "Dialectical Materialism and Biological Sciences." Volume IV: "Dialectical Materialism and Physiology." He also pointed out that in exploring the philosophical problems of individual sciences, special attention should be paid to the philosophical problems of physical and mathematical sciences, because these sciences are the foundation of modern natural science.

G. Zhdanov gave a report on the work of the philosophical seminar of the Physics Institute of the Soviet Academy of Sciences. He pointed out that it is necessary to arm scientific cadres with dialectical materialism philosophy. This means not only using these or those principles of dialectical materialism, but also cooperating with philosophical workers to draw new philosophical conclusions from the analysis and generalization of scientific discoveries. The speaker pointed out that the philosophical seminar can play a significant role in realizing this task. The speaker pointed out that the activities of the philosophical seminar of the Physics Institute mainly focus on discussing quantum mechanics and relativity.

G. Deborin gave a report on the work of the philosophical seminar of the Institute of Biochemistry. The leader of the philosophical seminar of this institute is A. Oparin. This year, two issues were discussed at the seminar: 1. The protein problem; 2. The role of biochemistry in understanding the biological form of the movement of matter. G. Deborin pointed out that among philosophers and natural scientists, there are still differences of opinion on how to treat the protein problem from a philosophical methodological standpoint. Regarding the second issue, it is planned to explore the following six topics: 1. The physical form of the movement of matter and its role in understanding biochemical regularities; 2. The conditions for the transformation of the physical form of movement into the chemical form; 3. The chemical form of the movement of matter and its role in understanding biochemical regularities; 4. Biochemical regularities arise from the development of the physical and chemical forms of the movement of matter in organisms; 5. The errors of reducing higher forms of the movement of matter to lower forms and reducing lower forms to higher forms; 6. The biological form of the movement of matter and the path to understanding this form based on the achievements of modern physics, chemistry, and biology.

### 苏联科学院举行哲学讲习会领导人会议

1960-07-22
第7版()
专栏：学术动态

    苏联科学院举行哲学讲习会领导人会议
    据苏联哲学问题杂志1960年第五期报道，为了执行1959年8月21日苏联科学院主席团关于把哲学讲习会的工作列入科学研究机关工作计划的决定，苏联科学院举行了各科学研究机关哲学讲习会的领导人、委员和顾问的会议。
    乌克兰科学院院士莫·奥米里扬诺夫斯基作了题为“研究‘辩证唯物主义和现代自然科学’问题的远景计划是哲学讲习会工作的基础”的报告。报告人指出，必须从哲学上理解现代物理学所发生的问题。空间、时间和物质的相互关系问题，物理学中的实在和规律性问题，越来越具有重要意义。目前，微观世界的研究和宇宙过程的研究交错一起。从哲学上阐述现代天文学的成就，将大大有助于说明微观物理学和宏观物理学的尚未解决的问题。控制论的发展也提出了新的哲学问题，如类比方法和模型化方法的问题。现代生物学和与其相近的科学的发展提出了这样的问题，即必须更加深刻地研究生命的起源和本质问题，物质运动形式的相互关系问题，内部和外部、生物和非生物的相互关系问题等。
    莫·奥米里扬诺夫斯基指出，1959年经济、哲学和法学学部的一个由著名苏联物理学家、生物学家和哲学家组成的委员会，拟定了关于“辩证唯物主义和现代自然科学”问题的科学研究工作远景计划草案。这个计划规定在最近几年内由哲学家和自然科学家共同编出有关现代自然科学哲学问题的多卷本的百科全书式的著作。这部著作预计有下列几卷：第一卷：第一篇“辩证唯物主义和现代自然科学的哲学问题”；第二篇“辩证唯物主义和物理数学科学”。第二卷：“辩证唯物主义和化学科学”。第三卷：“辩证唯物主义和生物科学”。第四卷：“辩证唯物主义和生理学”。他还指出，在探讨个别科学的哲学问题方面，应当特别注意物理数学科学的哲学问题，因为这些科学是现代自然科学的基础。
    格·日丹诺夫作了关于苏联科学院物理研究所哲学讲习会工作的报告。他指出，必须用辩证唯物主义哲学来武装科学干部。这指的是不仅要利用辩证唯物主义的这些或那些原理，而且要同哲学工作者合作来从科学发现的分析和概括中得出新的哲学结论。报告人指出，哲学讲习会能够对实现这一任务起很大作用。报告人指出，在物理研究所哲学讲习会的活动中，基本上是讨论量子力学和相对论问题。
    格·德波林作了关于生物化学研究所哲学讲习会工作的报告。这个所的哲学讲习会的领导人是阿·奥巴林。今年在讲习会上讨论了两个问题：1、蛋白质问题；2、生物化学在认识物质运动的生物学形式方面的作用问题。格·德波林指出，在哲学家和自然科学家当中，关于如何从哲学方法论立场对待蛋白质问题尚有意见分歧。关于第二个问题，准备探讨下列六个专题：1、物质运动的物理学形式及其对于认识生物化学规律性的作用；2、运动的物理学形式向化学形式转化的条件；3、物质运动的化学形式及其对于认识生物化学规律性的作用；4、生物化学规律性由于生物体中物质运动的物理学形式和化学形式的发展而产生；5、把物质运动的高级形式化为低级形式和把低级形式化为高级形式的错误；6、物质运动的生物学形式及在现代物理学、化学和生物学成就的基础上认识这种形式的途径。
