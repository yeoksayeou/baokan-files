Author:
Date: 1960-07-15
Page: 6

　  Resolution on the Question of Kenya
    The situation in Kenya, which has caused and continues to cause unspeakable suffering to the people of Kenya, remains fundamentally unchanged. Kenyan leader Jomo Kenyatta remains unjustly imprisoned; force and repression deprive the people of basic human rights; British imperialism and a handful of white colonists continue to pursue policies of domination and enslavement; the fundamental right of the Kenyan people to independence remains unrealized. The Standing Committee of the World Peace Council fully supports the struggle and just demands of the Kenyan people. It calls on all peace-loving people to put pressure on the British government to immediately release Jomo Kenyatta and meet the demands of all African people for the independence of Kenya and the removal of foreign bases from Kenyan soil.
                              The Standing Committee of the World Peace Council
                                Stockholm, July 11, 1960

### 关于怯尼亚问题的决议

1960-07-15
第6版()
专栏：

　  关于怯尼亚问题的决议
    造成和继续造成怯尼亚人民的无可言状的痛苦的怯尼亚局势基本上仍未改变。怯尼亚领袖乔莫·肯雅塔仍被不公正地监禁着；武力和镇压剥夺了人民的基本人权；英帝国主义和一小撮白人殖民者继续实行着统治和奴役的政策；怯尼亚人民获得独立的这个基本权利仍未实现。世界和平理事会常务委员会完全支持怯尼亚人民的斗争和正义要求，它号召一切爱好和平的人民对英国政府施加压力，要它立即释放乔莫·肯雅塔和满足全体非洲人民关于使怯尼亚独立和从怯尼亚土地上撤除外国基地的要求。
                              世界和平理事会常务委员会
                                斯德哥尔摩1960年7月11日
