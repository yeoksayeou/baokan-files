Title: ANC Opens Office in Washington
Author:
Date: 1989-11-12
Edition: 4

The African National Congress opened an office in Washington on November 9 to strengthen ties with the United States.

The African National Congress is a major anti-apartheid force in South Africa. Since January 1987, when Schultz met with ANC National Executive Committee Chairman Oliver Tambo, the U.S. attitude towards the ANC has gradually changed. ANC International Department Director Thabo Mbeki pointed out that the United States has allowed South African President de Klerk to implement the reforms he promised, without taking new international sanctions and pressures to force South Africa to abolish its apartheid system. He said that the U.S. approach is tantamount to giving South Africa ample time to consolidate its apartheid policy.

### 南非非国大在华盛顿设办事处

1989-11-12
第4版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    南非非国大在华盛顿设办事处
    新华社华盛顿11月10日电　南非非洲人国民大会9日在此开设了一个办事处，以加强同美国的联系。
    非洲人国民大会是南非一支重要的反种族隔离力量。自1987年1月舒尔茨同非国大全国执委会主席奥立弗·坦博会见以来，美国对非国大态度逐渐有所改变。非国大国际部主任塔博·姆贝基指出，美国允许南非总统德克勒克实施他所许诺的改革，而并没有采取新的国际制裁和压力来迫使南非废除它的种族隔离制度。他说，美国的上述做法无异于给南非充裕的时间以巩固它的种族隔离政策。
