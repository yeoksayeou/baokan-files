Title: Three Years of Research Unveils the "El Niño" Phenomenon
Author: Wang Guorui
Date: 1989-11-08
Edition: 3

British scientists have recently discovered that significant changes in ocean salinity trigger the El Niño phenomenon, causing severe global climate anomalies.

El Niño, a term coined by South American fishermen, refers to a natural phenomenon that occurs in the Pacific Ocean every 3-4 years around Christmas. The causes of the El Niño phenomenon have long been one of the greatest mysteries in nature.

190 scientists aboard the 2,000-ton Royal Research Vessel "Charles Darwin" traveled 224,000 kilometers around the world over three years. They recently returned to Britain.

David Webb, a member of the research team and a scientist at the Natural Environment Research Council's Institute of Oceanographic Sciences, said that although this investigation was not specifically aimed at studying the greenhouse effect, understanding the El Niño phenomenon is crucial for estimating future global warming.

Measurement results indicate that in the South Pacific, cold, saline water forms a current that flows towards the equator. As this current moves, it creates massive eddies across the entire Pacific, disrupting the normally stable warm water regions. These warm water regions extend from New Guinea to the center of the Pacific, with surface temperatures reaching around 30 degrees Celsius.

The El Niño phenomenon generates new currents, causing warm seawater to flow towards the eastern Pacific. One result of the interaction between the sea surface and the atmosphere is a change in prevailing winds.

Scientists have found that the El Niño phenomenon originates thousands of miles away on the other side of the Pacific and can cause droughts in countries like China and Australia, floods in Central America, and the destruction of South American fisheries, as well as a reduction in India's monsoon rains.

Their findings may lead to the development of an early warning system that can predict these disasters in advance. It is now possible to detect the rise in surface temperatures in the eastern Pacific a few months before the El Niño phenomenon occurs.

However, Dr. Webb said, "The El Niño phenomenon is much more complex than we originally thought. We have not yet fully solved the El Niño problem."

### 三年研究揭开“圣婴”面纱  海洋含盐量巨变引发厄尔尼诺现象
王国瑞
1989-11-08
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    三年研究揭开“圣婴”面纱
    海洋含盐量巨变引发厄尔尼诺现象
    新华社伦敦11月6日电  （记者王国瑞）英国科学家最近发现，海洋含盐量的巨大变化引起了厄尔尼诺现象，造成世界气候严重反常。
    厄尔尼诺，是南美渔民起的名字，意思是圣婴。它是指一种自然现象，在太平洋每隔3—4年在圣诞节后不久发生一次。造成厄尔尼诺现象的原因一直是自然界最大的神秘之一。
    190名科学家乘坐2000吨的皇家研究船“查理士·达尔文”号，环球航行22.4万公里，进行了为期三年的研究，最近回到英国。
    研究队成员、自然环境研究委员会海洋科学研究所的戴维·韦布说，虽然这次调查并不是专门研究温室效应的，但是，如果不了解厄尔尼诺现象，就不可能对将来的全球变暖作出估计。
    测量结果表明，南太平洋中含盐量大的冷海水汇成一股海流冲向赤道。当这股海流移动时，在整个太平洋的其他地区产生巨大的旋涡，扰乱了通常稳定的热水区。热水区从新几内亚一直延伸到太平洋的中心，海面温度达到30摄氏度左右。
    厄尔尼诺现象造成许多新的海流，使很暖和的海水流向太平洋的东边。海面同大气相互作用的结果之一是，使流行风发生变化。
    科学家发现，厄尔尼诺现象起源于数千英里外太平洋的另一边，会引起远及中国和澳大利亚等国的旱灾以及中美洲的洪水，破坏南美洲的渔业资源，减少印度的季风。
    他们的发现可能会导致发明一种早期警报系统，能够及时预报这些灾难，现在已经可能在厄尔尼诺现象出现几个月前发现东太平洋海面温度上升。
    但是，韦布博士说：“厄尔尼诺现象比我们原先所想像的要复杂得多”，“我们还没有完全解决厄尔尼诺问题”。
