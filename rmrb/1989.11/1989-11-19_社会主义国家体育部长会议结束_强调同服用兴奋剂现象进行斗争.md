Title: Conference of Sports Ministers of Socialist Countries Concludes, Emphasizes Fight Against Doping
Author: Ma Yunliang
Date: 1989-11-19
Edition: 3

The three-day conference of sports ministers from socialist countries concluded today in Warsaw.

According to a report by the Polish Press Agency, a resolution passed at today's meeting emphasized the need to combat the use of performance-enhancing drugs. The resolution stated that athletes from all countries should have equal competitive conditions and be subject to comprehensive supervision. It also mentioned that agreements would be reached to impose sanctions on athletes who violate the rules.

The conference believed that cooperation in mass sports and cultural exchanges among countries should be expanded. The meeting also adopted a proposal to conduct functional tests for different age groups and organize mass sports events as part of the "Sports for All" campaign.

The conference decided that representatives of the Olympic Committees from various countries will meet in Bulgaria in the first half of next year.

In addition to China and Yugoslavia, sports ministers from 11 other socialist countries in Europe, Asia, Latin America, and Africa participated in this conference.

### 社会主义国家体育部长会议结束  强调同服用兴奋剂现象进行斗争
马云亮
1989-11-19
第3版(综合)
专栏：体育

    社会主义国家体育部长会议结束
　  强调同服用兴奋剂现象进行斗争
    新华社华沙11月17日电　（记者马云亮）历时3天的社会主义国家体育部长会议今天在这里闭幕。
    据波通社消息说，在今天会议上通过的一项决议中特别强调要同服用兴奋剂现象作斗争，决议指出，各国运动员应该具有同等的竞争条件，并接受多方面的监督。决议说，将达成一些对破坏规则的运动员进行制裁的协议。
    会议认为应该扩大各国间的群众体育文化合作。会上还通过了一项关于对不同年龄组进行功能测验，并在“体育为人人”运动中组织群众性运动会的建议。
    会议决定，明年上半年各国奥委会代表将在保加利亚举行会晤。
    除中国和南斯拉夫以外，欧亚拉美的11个社会主义国家的体育部长参加了这次会议。
