Title: Situation in El Salvador Returns to Calm
Author:
Date: 1989-11-26
Edition: 4

	The situation in El Salvador has returned to calm after 13 days of fighting between the guerrilla forces and the government army. On November 24, the nationwide situation in El Salvador had stabilized, and the government shortened the curfew in the capital, San Salvador, and some provinces.

	On November 24, a military official in El Salvador said that in the 13 days since the guerrillas launched their offensive on November 11, a total of 1,928 people had been killed in the fighting.

	The military official pointed out that of the deceased, 1,576 were from the guerrilla side, and 352 were from the government army. In addition, approximately 300 to 400 civilians were killed.

	The military expressed that the Salvadoran army had regained control of the national situation.

### 萨尔瓦多局势恢复平静

1989-11-26
第4版(国际新闻)
专栏：

　　萨尔瓦多局势恢复平静
    新华社北京11月25日电　圣萨尔瓦多消息：萨尔瓦多游击队与政府军经过13天交战后，24日，萨尔瓦多全国局势已恢复平静，政府缩短了在首都圣萨尔瓦多和一些省份的宵禁时间。
    萨尔瓦多军方人士24日说，自本月11日游击队发动攻势以来的13天里，交战双方共死1928人。
    军方人士指出，在阵亡者中，游击队方面有1576人，政府军有352人。此外，还有约300至400名平民死亡。
    军方表示，萨军队已控制了全国局势。
