Title: OECD Oil Consumption Rises
Author:
Date: 1989-11-07
Page: 3

Xinhua News Agency, Paris

According to the International Energy Agency's monthly oil report, the 24 member countries of the Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development (OECD) consumed an average of 36.6 million barrels of oil per day in the third quarter of this year, a 1% increase compared to the same period last year.

The OECD member countries' oil production in October increased from an average of 16.1 million barrels per day in September to 16.4 million barrels per day.

The estimated global daily oil supply in October (excluding socialist countries) was 54.3 million barrels, an increase of 1 million barrels compared to September.

### 经合组织石油消费量上升

1989-11-07
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    经合组织石油消费量上升
    据新华社巴黎电  据此间国际能源机构石油月报说，经济合作与发展组织（经合组织）24个成员国今年第3季度每天的石油消费量达3660万桶，与去年同期相比增加了1％。
    经合组织成员国10月份的石油产量由9月份的平均每天1610万桶增加到1640万桶。
    10月份世界石油日均总供应量（不包括社会主义国家）估计为5430万桶，比9月份多100万桶。
