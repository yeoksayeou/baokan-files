Title: Farewell Ceremony for Wu Juenong, Renowned Agronomist and Tea Expert, Held in Beijing
Author:
Date: 1989-11-16
Edition: 2

Farewell ceremony for Wu Juenong, renowned agronomist and tea expert, held in Beijing

Xinhua News Agency, Beijing, November 15 - Wu Juenong, a renowned agronomist and tea expert, patriot, and member of the Standing Committee of the 7th National Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference (CPPCC), Vice Chairman of the 5th Central Consultative Committee of the China Democratic League (CDL), Honorary President of the Chinese Society of Agriculture, and Honorary Chairman of the China Tea Society, passed away in Beijing on October 28, 1989, at the age of 93 due to illness. The farewell ceremony for Wu Juenong was held this afternoon at the Baobao Mountain Revolutionary Cemetery.

Ma Yuelin, Yan Jici, Fei Xiaotong, Sun Qimeng, Wang Renhe, Zhou Peiyuan, Zhao Puchu, Qu Wu, Liu Jingji, Qian Xuesen, Hu Sheng, Sun Xiaocun, Cheng Siyuan, Qian Zhengying, and others went to Baobao Mountain Revolutionary Cemetery to bid farewell to Wu Juenong. Li Xiannian, Deng Yingchao, Tian Jiyun, Ding Guanggen, and Bo Yibo sent wreaths to Wu Juenong.

Wu Juenong was from Shangyu County, Zhejiang Province. In his early years, he joined the China Democratic Revolutionary Alliance (also known as the "Small Democratic Revolutionary Alliance") and did a lot of beneficial work under the leadership of the Communist Party. Throughout his life, he dedicated himself to agricultural research and was a renowned agronomist and tea expert in China. He authored books such as "The Problem of Chinese Tea," "The Revival Plan for Chinese Tea," and "Commentary on the Classic of Tea," enriching the historical literature of the country's tea industry. Over more than half a century, he made significant contributions to the revival of China's tea industry and was hailed as the "Tea Sage of the Modern Era."

Wu Juenong supported the leadership of the Communist Party of China and the socialist path, and he diligently studied Marxism-Leninism and Mao Zedong Thought. After the Third Plenary Session of the 11th Central Committee of the Communist Party of China, Wu Juenong, at an advanced age, actively participated in national political life and patriotic united front activities, contributing many positive suggestions and opinions to the discussion and consultation of major national policies. Wu Juenong was a long-time close friend of the Communist Party of China.

Today's farewell ceremony for Wu Juenong was attended by representatives from the National Committee of the CPPCC, the Central Committee of the CDL, the Central United Front Work Department, the Chinese Society of Agriculture, the China Tea Society, and various sectors, as well as over 500 friends and acquaintances of Wu Juenong. Wang Shudao, Liu Lantao, Xiao Ke, Lu Dingyi, Cheng Zihua, Habib, Zhu Lifan, Rong Yiren, Lei Jieqiong, Fang Yi, Gu Mu, Yang Jingren, Kang Keqing, Pabala Gelengjie, Hu Zihan, Wang Guangying, Deng Zhaoxiang, Ba Jin, Ma Wenrui, Wang Enmao, Qian Weichang, Lu Jiaxi, Su Buqing, Sima Yiming, Hou Jianru, Ding Guangxun, Yang Chengwu, Chen Zaidao, Lü Zhenghao, and Wang Feng, as well as relevant departments, sent wreaths to Wu Juenong.

### 七届政协常委著名农学家茶叶专家  吴觉农遗体告别仪式在京举行

1989-11-16
第2版(国内新闻)
专栏：

    七届政协常委著名农学家茶叶专家
    吴觉农遗体告别仪式在京举行
    新华社北京11月15日电　著名农学家、茶叶专家，爱国民主人士、全国政协第七届常委、民建第五届中央咨议委员会副主任、中国农学会名誉会长、中国茶叶学会名誉理事长吴觉农同志，因病于1989年10月28日在北京逝世，终年93岁。向吴觉农遗体告别仪式今天下午在八宝山革命公墓礼堂举行。
    姚依林、严济慈、费孝通、孙起孟、王任重、周培源、赵朴初、屈武、刘靖基、钱学森、胡绳、孙晓村、程思远、钱正英等前往八宝山革命公墓礼堂向吴觉农遗体告别。李先念、邓颖超、田纪云、丁关根、薄一波等向吴觉农遗体献花圈。
    吴觉农是浙江省上虞县人。他早年参加中国民主革命同盟（即“小民革”），在共产党的领导下，做了不少有益的工作。他一生从事农业研究工作，是我国著名的农业专家和茶叶专家，著有《中国茶叶问题》、《中国茶叶复兴计划》、《茶经述评》等书，丰富了祖国茶业的历史文库。在半个多世纪里，他为振兴祖国茶业事业作出了重要贡献，被誉为“当代茶圣”。
    吴觉农拥护中国共产党的领导和社会主义道路，努力学习马列主义、毛泽东思想。党的十一届三中全会以后，吴觉农以耄耋之年积极参与国家政治生活和爱国统一战线活动，参与国家大政方针的协商和讨论，提出了许多积极的意见和建议。吴觉农是中国共产党的一位长期肝胆相照的亲密朋友。
    参加今天向吴觉农遗体告别仪式的有全国政协、民建中央、中央统战部、中国农学会、中国茶叶学会的各界人士及吴觉农生前友好500多人。王首道、刘澜涛、肖克、陆定一、程子华、习仲勋、朱学范、荣毅仁、雷洁琼、方毅、谷牧、杨静仁、康克清、帕巴拉·格列朗杰、胡子昂、王光英、邓兆祥、巴金、马文瑞、王恩茂、钱伟长、卢嘉锡、苏步青、司马义·艾买提、侯镜如、丁光训、杨成武、陈再道、吕正操、汪锋等，以及有关部门向吴觉农的遗体献了花圈。
