Title: The First Asia-Pacific Economic Cooperation Meeting Held in Australia
Author:
Date: 1989-11-07
Edition: 3

The first Asia-Pacific Economic Cooperation (APEC) meeting opened in Canberra on November 6, discussing the economic challenges faced by the Asia-Pacific region and exploring ways to strengthen economic cooperation in the region in the future.

Twenty-five foreign and trade ministers from 12 countries and regions around the Pacific attended the meeting. These countries and regions include Australia, the United States, Canada, Japan, New Zealand, and six ASEAN countries, as well as South Korea.

The meeting was initiated by Australian Prime Minister Bob Hawke. The agenda includes reviewing the global and regional economic development situation, discussing the progress of global trade liberalization and the role that Asia-Pacific countries should play, determining the scope of regional cooperation in trade, and seeking the best ways to achieve progress in future economic cooperation.

On November 5, Australian Prime Minister Bob Hawke, in a speech at a dinner for representatives attending the meeting, urged the establishment of a new Pacific economic alliance to compete with Europe and North America, and believed that China should be invited to join the organization. He said, "Maintaining a trading environment with an increasingly effective participation of the People's Republic of China is beneficial to all of us." The U.S. representative also expressed support for China's participation in the proposed new Asia-Pacific economic organization.

### 首届亚太经济合作会议在澳举行

1989-11-07
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    首届亚太经济合作会议在澳举行
    新华社堪培拉11月6日电  首届亚太经济合作会议6日在堪培拉开幕，讨论亚太地区所面临的经济挑战和加强该地区今后经济合作的途径。
    来自环太平洋的12个国家和地区的25名外交、贸易部长出席了这次会议。这些国家和地区是澳大利亚、美国、加拿大、日本、新西兰和东盟六国以及南朝鲜。
    这次会议是由澳大利亚总理霍克倡导召开的。会议的议程包括，审议世界性和地区性经济发展情况，探讨全球贸易自由化的进展情况及亚太地区国家对此应起的作用、确定在贸易方面进行区域合作的规模以及寻求将来经济合作取得进展的最佳途径等。
    澳大利亚总理霍克5日在为参加这次会议的代表举行的晚宴上发表讲话，敦促成立一个新的环太平洋经济联盟同欧洲和北美进行竞争，并认为中国应被邀请加入该组织。他说，“保持一个有中华人民共和国日益有效参与的贸易环境对我们大家都有利。”美国代表也表示支持中国加入拟议中的新的亚太经济组织。
