Title: Mubarak Discusses Domestic and Foreign Policies: Seeking Middle East Peace and Improving Economy
Author:
Date: 1989-11-13
Edition: 4

Egyptian President Mubarak today, in a joint session of the People's Assembly and the Consultative Council, elaborated on Egypt's domestic and foreign policies, emphasizing the pursuit of regional peace and stability abroad, and the strengthening of democracy and economic improvement at home.

In discussing foreign policy, Mubarak noted that despite the United States' negative stance in the Middle East peace process, Egypt remains confident in facilitating talks between Israel and the Palestinians. He praised Saudi Arabian King Fahd's efforts in mediating the conflict between the Muslim and Christian factions in Lebanon.

Regarding the Iran-Iraq conflict, Mubarak expressed hope that Iran and Iraq could achieve permanent peace based on a ceasefire, ending the stalemate in the Gulf region.

Mubarak supported the Sudanese government's proposal for a broad national reconciliation with the anti-government forces in the south. Additionally, he endorsed French President Mitterrand's initiative to convene a Euro-Arab conference, agreeing to hold the European and Arab countries' meeting in Paris on December 21 to discuss relations between creditor and debtor nations.

In addressing domestic issues, Mubarak emphasized the principles of peace, pluralism, and freedom of speech, advocating for public participation in governance. He urged the parliament to take measures to rectify the imbalance in trade.

### 穆巴拉克谈内外政策  对外争取中东和平　对内改善经济

1989-11-13
第4版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    穆巴拉克谈内外政策
    对外争取中东和平　对内改善经济
    新华社开罗11月11日电　埃及总统穆巴拉克今天在人民议会和协商会议举行的联席会议上阐述了埃及政府的内外政策，强调对外致力于地区和平与稳定，对内加强民主、改善经济。
    穆巴拉克在谈到对外政策时指出，尽管美国在中东和平进程中采取消极态度，埃及对促成巴以会谈仍抱有信心。他赞扬了沙特阿拉伯国王法赫德为调解黎巴嫩穆斯林和基督教两大派之间的矛盾所作的努力。
    在谈到两伊问题时，穆巴拉克希望伊朗和伊拉克在停火的基础上实现永久的和平，结束海湾地区不战不和的局面。
    穆巴拉克支持苏丹政府提出的同国内南部反政府势力取得广泛民族和解的主张。另外他还支持法国总统密特朗提出的召开欧—阿会议的主张，同意12月21日在巴黎召开欧洲和阿拉伯国家会议，讨论债权国和债务国之间的关系问题。
    在谈到国内问题时，穆巴拉克强调坚持和平、多元化和言论自由的原则，主张人民参政议政。他要求议会采取措施，扭转贸易的不平衡状态。
