Title: OPEC Ministerial Meeting Opens to Discuss Production Quotas for First Half of 1990
Author: Liugang
Date: 1989-11-27
Edition: 4

The 86th ministerial meeting of the Organization of the Petroleum Exporting Countries (OPEC) opened today in Vienna. The main task of this meeting is to determine the oil production quotas for the first half of 1990, aiming to further stabilize oil prices in the international market.

Oil or energy ministers from OPEC's 13 member countries attended the meeting.

Due to the ongoing imbalance of supply exceeding demand in the global oil market, OPEC's oil prices are actually below the official limit of $18 per barrel. According to OPEC Secretary-General Subroto, the global market's demand for OPEC oil in the first half of 1990 is estimated to be 21.5 million barrels per day. Analysts here estimate that despite OPEC's preliminary decision in September to set the production limit for the first half of 1990 at 20.5 million barrels per day, the current production has already exceeded the 19.5 million barrels per day target, reaching 23.5 million barrels per day. Therefore, this meeting is expected to have intense discussions on the production quotas for OPEC oil and the allocation of quotas among member countries for the first half of 1990.

At this meeting, the oil or energy ministers from OPEC's 13 member countries will also coordinate with non-OPEC countries to stabilize the global oil market and discuss OPEC's development strategy for the 1990s. Subroto predicts that non-OPEC countries' oil production in 1990 will increase from the current 29.59 million barrels per day to 30.1 million barrels per day.

Experts here predict that the global daily oil demand in 1990 will increase from 50.92 million barrels in 1989 to 51.8 million barrels.

### 欧佩克部长级会议开幕　商定明年上半年限产指标
刘钢
1989-11-27
第4版(国际新闻)
专栏：

　　欧佩克部长级会议开幕
　　商定明年上半年限产指标
    新华社维也纳11月25日电　（记者刘钢）石油输出国组织欧佩克第86届部长级会议今天在这里开幕。本次会议的主要任务是商定1990年上半年的石油限产指标，以进一步稳定国际市场上的石油价格。
    欧佩克13个成员国的石油或能源部长出席了本届会议。
    由于目前的世界石油市场供大于求的矛盾仍未解决，欧佩克的石油价格实际上低于每桶18美元的官方限价。据欧佩克秘书长苏布罗托估计，1990年上半年，世界市场对欧佩克石油的需求量是每天2150万桶。此间分析家们估计，尽管欧佩克在今年9月的8国部长会议上已初步决定明年上半年的生产限量是2050万桶，但由于目前欧佩克的产量早已大大突破了日产1950万桶的指标，达到2350万桶，因此，本届会议在明年上半年欧佩克石油产量及各成员国的产量限额分配问题上将会有较为激烈的争论。
    在本届会议上，欧佩克13个成员国的石油或能源部长还将就该组织90年代的发展战略同非欧佩克国家协调，共同稳定世界石油市场。苏布罗托推测，1990年非欧佩克国家的石油产量将会从今年的每天2959万桶上升到3010万桶。
    此间的石油问题专家们预计，1990年全世界的石油日需求量将从1989年的5092万桶增加到5180万桶。
