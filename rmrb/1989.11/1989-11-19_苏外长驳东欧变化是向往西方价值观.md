Title: Soviet Foreign Minister Refutes the Idea That Eastern European Changes Are Aspirations for Western Values
Author:
Date: 1989-11-19
Edition: 4

Soviet Foreign Minister Eduard Shevardnadze on November 17 refuted the notion held by some Westerners that the current changes in Eastern European countries are a yearning for Western values.

According to a report by TASS, Shevardnadze, while speaking at the Soviet Parliament's International Affairs Committee about the situation in Eastern Europe, said, "To regard democracy and freedom as Western values is overly arrogant and is a symptom of 'Western chauvinism.'"

Regarding the current Western discussions about the reunification of the two Germanys, he stated, "The security and stability of Europe are based on the existing reality of two Germanys and two major military and political blocs."

He said, "No one has the right to unilaterally change this reality."

### 苏外长驳东欧变化是向往西方价值观

1989-11-19
第4版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    苏外长驳东欧变化是向往西方价值观
    新华社莫斯科11月17日电  苏联外长谢瓦尔德纳泽17日驳斥了西方某些人士关于东欧国家目前发生的变化是向往西方价值观的论调。
    据塔斯社报道，谢瓦尔德纳泽在苏联议会国际事务委员会谈到东欧形势时说，“把民主和自由看作是西方的价值观念，这是过分自负，是犯了‘西方自大狂’症”。
    针对当前西方关于两个德国重新统一的议论，他表示，“欧洲的安全和稳定基于两个德国和两大军事政治集团并存这一既成事实”。
    他说，“谁也无权单方面改变这一现实”。
