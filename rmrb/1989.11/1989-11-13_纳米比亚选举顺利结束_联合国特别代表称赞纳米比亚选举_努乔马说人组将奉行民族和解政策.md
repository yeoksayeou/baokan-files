Title: Namibia Election Concludes Successfully
Author:
Date: 1989-11-13
Edition: 4

The five-day election for the Constituent Assembly of Namibia concluded today in a peaceful and orderly manner under the strict supervision of the United Nations.

Official statistics released this morning indicate that approximately 91.3% of eligible voters participated in the first four days of voting. In the most densely populated Ovambo region, 95.8% of voters turned out.

The results of this Constituent Assembly election will be announced on November 15.

The elected Constituent Assembly will not only draft a constitution for Namibia but also determine the specific date of Namibia's independence. According to United Nations Resolution 435, Namibia's independence must not be later than April 1, 1990.

The United Nations Special Representative for Namibia, Martti Ahtisaari, announced at a press conference held here tonight that the voting process in Namibia's election has been "free and fair, and conducted in accordance with United Nations Resolution 435."

He stated that once all voting is completed, the counting of ballots will commence.

Ahtisaari also praised the quiet, disciplined, and resolute qualities of the Namibian people. He said that this week, "the people of Namibia have given the world a lesson in democracy."

Ahtisaari expressed gratitude to all those involved in the organization, management, and supervision of this election.

According to a report from the Zimbabwean African News Agency in Harare, Sam Nujoma, the chairman of the South West Africa People's Organization (SWAPO), stated today that if SWAPO wins this election, this victory will not only belong to the Namibian people but also to the entire African continent.

Nujoma said that SWAPO's victory in the election will also be an inspiration to blacks still under the rule of South Africa's apartheid regime.

Nujoma accused South Africa of attempting to influence the election results by allowing foreigners and members of Angolan anti-government groups to register to vote.

He said that the primary task of the new Namibian government to be established is to ensure food self-sufficiency. The new government must also provide financial assistance to farmers, those crowded in northern Namibia, and those in need of resettlement.

Nujoma said that SWAPO will pursue a policy of national reconciliation to unite people of all colors and continue to oppose South Africa's attempts to implement the Bantustan policy in Namibia.

### 纳米比亚选举顺利结束  联合国特别代表称赞纳米比亚选举  努乔马说人组将奉行民族和解政策

1989-11-13
第4版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    纳米比亚选举顺利结束
    联合国特别代表称赞纳米比亚选举
    努乔马说人组将奉行民族和解政策
    新华社温得和克11月11日电　为期5天的纳米比亚制宪议会选举，在联合国的严格监督下今天平静而顺利地结束。
    官方今天早晨发表的统计数字表明，在选举的前4天，大约91.3％的选民参加了投票。在人口最密集的奥万博地区，95.8％的选民参加了投票。
    这次制宪议会选举的结果将于15日公布。
    选出的制宪议会不但要为纳米比亚制定一项宪法，而且要决定纳米比亚独立的具体日期。根据联合国435号决议，纳米比亚独立的时间不得迟于1990年4月1日。
    新华社温得和克11月11日电　联合国驻纳米比亚特别代表阿赫蒂萨里今晚在这里举行的记者招待会上宣布，纳米比亚选举的投票过程“一直是自由和公正的，并且是按照联合国435号决议进行的”。
    他说，投票一旦全部结束，将接着进行选票计算工作。
    阿赫蒂萨里还特别赞扬了纳米比亚人民安静、守纪律和有毅力的品质。他说，本周里，“纳米比亚人民给全世界上了一节民主示范课。”
    阿赫蒂萨里对参加本次选举的组织、管理和监督工作的所有人表示感激。
    新华社哈拉雷11月11日电　据津巴布韦非洲通讯社从温得和克报道，西南非洲人民组织主席萨姆·努乔马今天说，如果西南非洲人民组织（人组）在本次选举中获胜，那么这一胜利不仅是纳米比亚人民的胜利，也是整个非洲的胜利。
    努乔马说，人组在选举中的胜利对依然处于南非种族主义政权统治下的黑人亦将是一个鼓舞。
    努乔马指责南非企图通过让外国人和安哥拉反政府组织成员参加选举登记以影响选举结果。
    他说，即将建立的纳米比亚新政府的首要任务是保障本国的粮食自给。新政府还必须向农民、拥挤在纳米比亚北部的人和需要重新安置的人提供财政援助。
    努乔马说，人组将奉行民族和解政策以团结各种肤色的人民并继续反对南非企图在纳米比亚实行班图斯坦政策。
