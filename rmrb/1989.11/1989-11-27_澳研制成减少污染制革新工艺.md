Title: Australia Develops New Tanning Process to Reduce Pollution
Author:
Date: 1989-11-27
Edition: 7

According to the Indian magazine *Science Reporter*, the Commonwealth Scientific and Industrial Research Organization in Melbourne, Australia, has successfully developed a new tanning process called "Xerolime." This process not only quickly removes animal hair and wool but also reduces pollution. Currently, about 90% of leather worldwide is processed using conventional chemical hair removal methods. These methods produce soluble proteins and difficult-to-treat sludge, which easily pollute the environment. The new process can reduce pollution by 80%, and the removed hair can be collected to produce fertilizers and other by-products.

### 澳研制成减少污染制革新工艺

1989-11-27
第7版(国际专页)
专栏：科技简讯

　　澳研制成减少污染制革新工艺
    据印度《科学报告者》杂志报道，澳大利亚联邦科学与工业研究组织在墨尔本的　羊毛技术部门，研制成功一种名为“西罗利梅”的制革新工艺。采用这种工艺，不仅可将兽皮毛快速除掉，而且可减少污染。目前，世界上约百分之九十的皮革是用常规的化学除毛工艺处理的。这种除毛方法形成的可溶性蛋白质以及难处理的沉渣，容易污染环境。用新工艺处理兽皮，可以降低污染百分之八十，退掉的毛还可以收集起来生产化肥等副产品。　（新华社）
