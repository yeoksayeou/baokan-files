Title: Residents of the Philippines' Mindanao Vote on Autonomy Law
Author:
Date: 1989-11-21
Edition: 4

    Residents of Mindanao, a region in the southern Philippines where Muslims are concentrated, began voting on November 19 on whether to implement an autonomy law in the region.

    The Mindanao Autonomy Law grants the region various autonomous powers, excluding defense, foreign affairs, judiciary, postal services, and customs. To ease the long-standing tensions in the region, Philippine President Corazon Aquino signed the law on August 1 of this year, which was approved by the Philippine Supreme Court on November 10.

    There are approximately 3.6 million registered voters in the 13 provinces and 9 cities of Mindanao.

    The official results of the vote will be announced a few days later.

### 菲棉兰老居民投票决定自治法

1989-11-21
第4版(国际新闻)
专栏：

　　菲棉兰老居民投票决定自治法
    菲律宾南部穆斯林聚居的棉兰老地区群众十九日就这个地区是否实行自治法问题开始公民投票。
    棉兰老自治法规定这个地区拥有除防务、外交、司法、邮政和海关以外的各种自治权。为了缓和这一地区多年的紧张局势，菲律宾总统科·阿基诺今年八月一日签署了该自治法，此法在十一月十日得到菲最高法院的批准。
    棉兰老地区十三个省和九个市现有大约三百六十万已登记的投票者。
    正式的投票结果要几天之后才能揭晓。
