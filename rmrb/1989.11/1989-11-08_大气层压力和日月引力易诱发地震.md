Title: Atmospheric Pressure and Lunar-Solar Gravity Easily Trigger Earthquakes
Author:
Date: 1989-11-08
Edition: 3

    Atmospheric pressure changes and the gravitational effects of the sun and moon may have triggered the 7.1 magnitude earthquake that struck California last month, according to some American scientists.

    Investigations by American scientists have shown that when the gravitational pull of the sun and moon on the Earth is at its maximum, and when the air in the atmosphere forms a certain high-pressure system, with related winds pressing down and causing friction on the ground, earthquakes are more likely to occur. A 1983 study indicated that most of the earthquakes of magnitude 6 or above that have occurred in southern California in history happened during times of maximum solar and lunar gravitational pull, specifically around the time of a full moon or new moon, within an hour before sunrise or sunset, and within a year of the 18.6-year peak in the moon's gravitational pull on Earth.

    However, some American seismologists remain skeptical of this seemingly plausible but yet-to-be-verified theory.

### 大气层压力和日月引力易诱发地震

1989-11-08
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    大气层压力和日月引力易诱发地震
    新华社北京11月7日电　据外电报道，一些美国科学家认为，大气层压力的改变以及太阳和月亮的引力作用，可能诱发了上月美国加利福尼亚州的7.1级大地震。
    美国科学家进行的几次调查表明，当太阳和月亮作用于地球的引力最大以及大气层流动着的空气形成某种高压系统，而与此有关的风向下压和摩擦地面时，就往往容易发生地震。1983年一项调查表明，历史上发生在加利福尼亚南部6级以上的一些地震，大部分是在太阳和月亮引力最强的时候发生的，也就是在接近满月或新月时、日出和日落前后一小时之内，以及月亮对地球引力的18.6年周期峰值前后一年内发生的。
    但是一些美国地震科学家对这种似乎有理而尚需证实的理论持怀疑态度。
