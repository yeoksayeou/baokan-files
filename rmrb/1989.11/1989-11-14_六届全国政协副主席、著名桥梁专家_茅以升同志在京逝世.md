Title: Sixth National Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference Vice Chairman and Renowned Bridge Expert Mao Yisheng Passes Away in Beijing
Author:
Date: 1989-11-14
Page: 1

Xinhua News Agency, Beijing, November 13th.

The Vice Chairman of the Sixth National Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference, Honorary Chairman of the Central Committee of the Jiusan Society, Honorary Chairman of the National Committee of the Chinese Association for Science and Technology, Communist Party of China member, and renowned bridge expert Mao Yisheng passed away in Beijing at 15:00 on November 12, 1989, at the age of 94 due to illness.

Mao Yisheng was a native of Zhenjiang, Jiangsu. He studied in the United States and obtained a doctorate in engineering. After returning to China in 1920, he served as the president of Huanhai Institute of Technology and Tianjin North China University. From 1933 to 1937, he oversaw the design and construction of China's first railway bridge—the Qiantang River Bridge. During the Anti-Japanese War and the War of Liberation, he held positions including President of Tangshan Institute of Technology, General Manager of the China Bridge Company, President of the Central Research Institute, and President of the Chinese Society of Engineers. In 1949, he attended the First Plenary Session of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference. After the founding of the People's Republic of China, he served as President of the China Institute of Transportation, Director of the Railway Science Research Institute of the Ministry of Railways, Vice Chairman of the Chinese Association for Science and Technology, Member of the Academic Division of the Chinese Academy of Sciences, President of the Chinese Society of Civil Engineering, and Senior Member of the International Bridge and Structural Engineering Association. In 1982, he was awarded the title of Foreign Member by the U.S. National Academy of Sciences. He joined the Jiusan Society in 1952 and served as Vice Chairman of the Central Committee for a long time. He was a member of the Standing Committee of the First to Sixth National People's Congress and a member of the Second to Sixth National Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference. He joined the Communist Party of China in October 1987.

During Mao Yisheng's hospitalization, leaders such as Li Peng, a member of the Standing Committee of the Political Bureau of the CPC Central Committee and Premier of the State Council, visited the hospital to see him.

### 六届全国政协副主席、著名桥梁专家  茅以升同志在京逝世

1989-11-14
第1版(要闻)
专栏：

    六届全国政协副主席、著名桥梁专家
    茅以升同志在京逝世
    新华社北京11月13日电　中国人民政治协商会议第六届全国委员会副主席、九三学社中央委员会名誉主席、中国科学技术协会全国委员会名誉主席、中国共产党党员、著名桥梁专家茅以升同志，因病于1989年11月12日15时在北京逝世，终年94岁。
    茅以升同志是江苏镇江人，早年留学美国，获工学博士学位。他1920年回国后，曾任河海工科大学、天津北洋大学校长。1933年至1937年，他主持设计建造我国第一座铁路大桥——钱塘江大桥。抗日战争和解放战争时期，他历任唐山工学院院长、中国桥梁公司总经理、中央研究院院长、中国工程师学会会长。他1949年出席中国人民政治协商会议第一届全体会议，中华人民共和国建立后，历任中国交通大学校长、铁道部铁道科学研究院院长、中国科学技术协会副主席、中国科学院学部委员、中国土木工程学会理事长、国际桥梁及结构工程协会高级会员。1982年他被美国国家科学院授予外籍院士称号。他1952年参加九三学社，长期担任九三学社中央副主席，是一至六届全国人大常委会委员、二至六届全国政协委员。1987年10月光荣加入中国共产党。
    茅以升同志生病住院期间，中共中央政治局常委、国务院总理李鹏等领导同志到医院看望。
