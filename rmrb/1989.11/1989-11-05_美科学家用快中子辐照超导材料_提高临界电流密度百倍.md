Title: American Scientists Use Fast Neutron Irradiation to Boost Critical Current Density of Superconductors by 100 Times
Author:
Date: 1989-11-05
Edition: 3

American scientists have used fast neutron irradiation on superconducting materials, resulting in a 100-fold increase in critical current density.

When an electric current passes through a superconductor, it easily generates a magnetic field within the conductor. When the magnetic field strength reaches a certain value, the superconductor loses its superconducting properties. This is a major challenge in the practical application of high-temperature superconducting materials.

Fast neutron irradiation of superconducting materials can create defects within the crystal grains, thereby restricting the movement of small magnetic fields within the material. This reduces the strength of the macroscopic magnetic field.

According to a report in the British journal *Nature*, scientists at the Bell Laboratories of the American Telephone and Telegraph Company used fast neutron irradiation on yttrium-barium-copper-oxygen superconducting materials. In a magnetic field 18,000 times stronger than Earth's magnetic field and at a temperature of -196.1 degrees Celsius, they achieved a current density of 600,000 amperes per square centimeter.

### 美科学家用快中子辐照超导材料  提高临界电流密度百倍

1989-11-05
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    美科学家用快中子辐照超导材料
    提高临界电流密度百倍
    新华社华盛顿11月3日电　美国科学家用快中子辐照超导材料，结果提高临界电流密度100倍。
    电流通过超导体时，易在导体内部产生磁场，而当磁场强度达到一定值时，超导体就失去了超导性，这是高温超导材料实用研究中的一个难题。
    用快中子辐照超导材料，能够造成晶粒内部缺陷，因而得以限制内部各个小磁场的移动，这就降低了宏观磁场的强度。
    据英国《自然》杂志报道，美国电报电话公司贝尔实验室的科学家用快中子辐照钇、钡、铜、氧系超导材料，在1.8万倍于地球磁场强度的磁场中，零下196.1摄氏度时取得了每平方厘米60万安培的电流密度。
