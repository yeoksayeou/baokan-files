Title: Hong Kong's *Wen Wei Po* Publishes a Signed Article Emphasizing That "Internationalizing the Hong Kong Issue" Is Unreasonable and Illegal; For the Prosperity of Hong Kong, It's Best Not to Play the "International Card"

Author:

Date: 1989-11-23

Edition: 3

Hong Kong's *Wen Wei Po* published a signed article by Li Lin on November 21, titled "A Brief Discussion on the Harms of 'Internationalizing the Hong Kong Issue'." The article discusses Hong Kong as an international city and the different meanings of internationalizing the Hong Kong issue, explaining from a legal perspective that internationalizing the Hong Kong issue violates the Sino-British Joint Declaration and relevant international legal principles.

The article argues that China has the sincerity to maintain Hong Kong's status and characteristics as an international city after 1997. "However, 'internationalizing the Hong Kong issue' is to politicize the Hong Kong issue internationally, allowing other countries to interfere at will, or to introduce the influence of other countries into Hong Kong, gradually involving them in Hong Kong's political affairs, and even risking the evolution into a situation similar to international condominium (Condominium)." Its essence "is to internationalize Hong Kong's sovereignty or administrative rights."

The article believes that the practice of internationalizing the Hong Kong issue violates both the Sino-British Joint Declaration and relevant international legal principles. Because:

First, "Internationalizing the Hong Kong issue will inevitably hinder the smooth and complete handover of Hong Kong's sovereignty. According to Article 26 of the Vienna Convention on the Law of Treaties: 'Every treaty in force is binding upon the parties to it and must be performed by them in good faith.' The so-called 'good faith performance' means performing it according to the principles of fairness and justice, not just considering one's own interests. The Joint Declaration's second article states that Britain 'will hand over Hong Kong to the People's Republic of China on July 1, 1997.' According to the requirement of 'good faith performance,' Britain should hand over Hong Kong completely and intact to China at that time, without deducting anything, and certainly not setting obstacles for China to restore its sovereignty over Hong Kong." Britain's attempt to internationalize the Hong Kong issue is "to borrow international forces to exert pressure on China and the future Special Administrative Region government." The article believes that "Britain has enormous economic interests in Hong Kong and hopes to maintain them in the future. This mentality is understandable, but to protect one's own interests at the expense of violating the Joint Declaration and taking 'backdoor' approaches is a fundamental mistake and will not achieve the goal." "Britain can only protect its future interests in Hong Kong through the Joint Declaration and can only achieve this goal by 'performing the Joint Declaration in good faith.' There is no other way."

Second, "Internationalizing the Hong Kong issue will also harm China's restoration of sovereignty over Hong Kong. Indivisibility is an attribute of sovereignty," Britain "attempts to return only the sovereignty to China, but in administrative rights, it engages in 'private transfers,' not allowing China to 'take over,' which itself is a violation of China's sovereignty. Internationalizing the Hong Kong issue will legalize the intervention of international forces in Hong Kong's internal affairs, which completely violates the internationally recognized principles of sovereignty's autonomy and exclusivity. Imagine, after 1997, Britain hands over Hong Kong, but it becomes a politically 'internationalized' city, divided and controlled by foreign forces. How can China truly 'restore the exercise of sovereignty'?"

Third, there is no basis for internationalizing the Hong Kong issue in the Joint Declaration. "The main content of the Sino-British Joint Declaration is the transfer of Hong Kong's sovereignty and China's policies towards Hong Kong after 1997. The former is a matter between China and Britain, while the latter is a matter for China." "China will 'restore the exercise of sovereignty' over its own territory and implement the policy of 'one country, two systems, Hong Kong people governing Hong Kong, and a high degree of autonomy.' This is undoubtedly a purely 'domestic jurisdiction matter.' Any country that acts in accordance with the 'United Nations Charter' should not consider 'internationalizing the Hong Kong issue' reasonable and should not intervene politically."

Fourth, internationalizing the Hong Kong issue hinders Hong Kong's self-governance. "If internationalizing the Hong Kong issue becomes a reality, it will undoubtedly bring great interference to the smooth governance of the Special Administrative Region government. If various international forces directly intervene in the affairs of the Special Administrative Region government and influence the policies of the Special Administrative Region government, 'Hong Kong people governing Hong Kong' will become an empty slogan; once various international forces cannot 'share' the benefits and start fighting, Hong Kong will never have peace." Moreover, internationalizing the Hong Kong issue not only "harms Hong Kong's prosperity and stability but also harms China's sovereignty. How can the Chinese government stand idly by and watch this happen?"

The article concludes by hoping that Britain, "for the stability and prosperity of Hong Kong, for Sino-British friendship, and for Britain's actual interests, it is best not to play the 'international card.'"

### 香港《文汇报》发表署名文章强调　“香港问题国际化”无理又违法　为香港繁荣计，“国际牌”还是不打为好

1989-11-23
第3版(综合)
专栏：

　　香港《文汇报》发表署名文章强调
　　“香港问题国际化”无理又违法
　　为香港繁荣计，“国际牌”还是不打为好
    本报香港11月22日电　香港《文汇报》本月21日发表署名李林的文章，题为《浅议“香港问题国际化”之弊》。文章指出香港作为国际性城市和香港问题国际化的不同含义，从法律上阐述了将香港问题国际化是违反中英联合声明及有关国际法准则的。
    文章认为，中国有诚意维护香港在1997年以后仍然作为国际城市的地位和特色，“而‘香港问题国际化’则是要在政治上将香港问题变成一个国际问题，任由其他国家指手划脚，恣意干预，或者将其他国家的势力引入香港，让其逐步介入香港的政治事务，甚至不惜演变成类似国际共管（Condominium）的局面。”其实质，“就是要将香港的主权或治权国际化。”
    文章认为，香港问题国际化的做法既违反中英联合声明，也违反有关国际法准则。因为：
    一、“香港问题国际化必然妨碍香港主权的顺利、完整交接。根据《维也纳条约法公约》第26条规定：‘凡有效之条约对其各当事国有拘束力，必须由各该国善意履行’。所谓‘善意履行’，即是依照公平、公义原则去履行，而不是只片面顾及己方的利益。联合声明第二条规定，英国‘于1997年7月1日将香港交还给中华人民共和国’。依照‘善意履行’的要求，英国自应届时将香港完完整整地交还给中国，不应从中打折扣，更不应为中国恢复对香港的主权设置障碍”。英国搞香港问题国际化，是“欲借助国际势力向中国和未来的特区政府施加压力”。文章认为，
  “英国在香港有着巨大的经济利益。希望将来仍得到保持，这种心态不足为奇，但是，为了片面维护自己的利益，而不惜违反联合声明，走‘旁门左道’，却是犯了根本的错误，也是不可能达到目的的”。“英国要保护自己未来在港利益，只能通过联合声明，只能在‘善意履行’联合声明的情况下才可以达到目的。舍此别无他途”。
    二、“香港问题国际化还会损及中国对香港恢复行使主权。不可分割是主权的属性之一”，英国“企图只将主权还给中国，而在治权上搞‘私相授受’，不让中国‘过手’，这本身已是对中国主权的破坏。香港问题国际化将使国际势力干预香港内部事务合法化，这完全违反了国际法公认的主权的自主性、排它性原则。试想1997年以后英国交出了香港，但它却变成了一个政治上‘国际化’的城市，被外国势力瓜分和控制，中国又怎能真正地‘恢复行使主权’呢？”
    三、香港问题国际化在联合声明中是找不到任何依据的。“中英联合声明的主要内容是香港主权的移交和中国1997年以后对港政策，前者是中英之间的事，后者则是中方的事”。“中国要对自己的领土‘恢复行使主权’，并实施‘一国两制、港人治港、高度自治’的政策，此无疑是纯粹的‘国内管辖事件’。任何国家只要依照‘联合国宪章’办事，相信都不会认为‘香港问题国际化’合理，不会在政治上作出干预之举”。
    四、香港问题国际化妨碍港人治港。“香港问题国际化如果成为事实，无疑会给特区政府顺利施政带来极大的干扰。如果各种国际势力直接插手特区政府事务，左右特区政府政策，‘港人治港’会变得徒有虚名；一旦各种国际势力为‘利益’不能‘均沾’而争斗，香港更会永无宁日”。此外，香港问题国际化不但
  “损害香港的繁荣稳定，同时也损害了中国的主权。对此，中国政府又怎能袖手旁观呢？”
    文章最后希望英国，“为香港的稳定繁荣计，为中英友好计，为英国的实际利益计，‘国际牌’还是不打为好”。
