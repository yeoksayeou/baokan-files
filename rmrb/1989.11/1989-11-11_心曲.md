Title: Heart's Melody
Author: Yang Wuneng
Date: 1989-11-11
Page: 8

A German folk song goes, “In Heidelberg, I lost my heart…”
In the ancient and beautiful city of Heidelberg,
Did I too
Lose my heart
Lost on
The Philosopher’s Path atop the Haide Mountains
Lost in
The verdant lawns by the Neckar River
Lost in
The ruins of the old palace at sunset
Lost in
The morning light atop the clock tower
Lost in
The soft music of the café
	Lost in
The quiet reading room of the old university
Lost in
The ancient paintings of the museum
Lost in
The bustling crowds of the pedestrian street
Lost in
The shade of the ginkgo trees under Goethe’s love
Lost in
The lingering charm of the romantic poet
Lost it, did I truly lose it? My poor heart
Foreign relics may be magnificent,
Foreign scenery may be enchanting,
But they lack the great rivers and mountains of my homeland,
And they are devoid of the warmth of home.
Nothing is more unbearable than loneliness,
Old trees, crows, and the setting sun,
Who will accompany me, the broken-hearted traveler?
Who will comfort my homesick heart?
If you wish to know my heart’s melody,
If you ask if I have lost my heart,
Listen to the cooing of the swallows by the eaves,
Look at the drifting clouds in the sky…
