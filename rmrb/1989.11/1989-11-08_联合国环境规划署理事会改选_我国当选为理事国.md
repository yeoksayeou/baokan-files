Title: United Nations Environment Programme Governing Council Election
Author: Liú Kāichéng
Date: 1989-11-08
Edition: 3

The 44th session of the United Nations General Assembly today elected new members to the United Nations Environment Programme Governing Council, with China among the 29 countries elected.

China, whose term expires at the end of this year, was re-elected with the highest number of votes, 143, in this partial election of council members. The other 28 countries elected are: Burundi, Gabon, Gambia, Lesotho, Mauritius, Tunisia, Zaire, Zimbabwe, Argentina, Barbados, Brazil, Peru, Venezuela, East Germany, the Soviet Union, Yugoslavia, Austria, France, West Germany, Indonesia, Japan, Kuwait, New Zealand, Norway, the Philippines, Spain, the United States, and Thailand.

The council consists of 58 member countries, each serving a four-year term. Elections are held by secret ballot, and a simple majority is required for election. Members may be re-elected for consecutive terms.

### 联合国环境规划署理事会改选  我国当选为理事国
刘开宸
1989-11-08
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    联合国环境规划署理事会改选
    我国当选为理事国
    本报联合国11月6日电　记者刘开宸报道：第44届联合国大会今天改选联合国环境规划署理事会部分理事国，中国等29国当选。
    中国在今年年底期满，在这次部分理事国改选中以143票的最高票连选连任。其它28国是：布隆迪、加蓬、冈比亚、莱索托、毛里求斯、突尼斯、扎伊尔、津巴布韦、阿根廷、巴巴多斯、巴西、秘鲁、委内瑞拉、民主德国、苏联、南斯拉夫、奥地利、法国、联邦德国、印尼、日本、科威特、新西兰、挪威、菲律宾、西班牙、美国、泰国。
    该理事会共有58个理事国，任期4年。重新选举时，通过无记名投票方式以简单多数当选，可连选连任。
