Title: U.S. Scientists Publish Article Denying the Existence of a Fifth Force in Nature
Author:
Date: 1989-11-13
Edition: 4

U.S. scientists have published an article in the latest issue of *Nature* magazine stating that three widely cited Earth physics experiments do not provide convincing evidence for the existence of a fifth force in nature.

Scientists generally agree that there are four fundamental forces in nature: gravity, electromagnetism, the strong nuclear force, and the weak nuclear force. Gravity is the weakest of these forces, but it has the longest range and can influence entire galaxies.

In a mine 1,000 meters deep in Queensland, Australia, on a television tower 600 meters high near Raleigh, North Carolina, U.S., and in a borehole 2,000 meters deep in the Greenland ice cap, measurements of Earth's gravity have not matched theoretical values. These discrepancies have been cited as evidence for the existence of a fifth force.

Parker and Zumberge of the University of California argue that the discrepancies between measured and theoretical values of gravity are due to slight differences between the actual and theoretical densities of the land, i.e., slight differences between the actual and theoretical values of mass, leading to discrepancies between measured and theoretical values of gravity.

### 美科学家发表文章  否认自然界存在第五种力

1989-11-13
第4版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    美科学家发表文章
    否认自然界存在第五种力
    新华社伦敦电　美国科学家在最近一期《自然》杂志上发表文章说，被广泛引用的3个地球物理实验，没有提出自然界存在第五种力的令人信服的证据。
    科学家认为，自然界存在四种力：引力、电磁力、强核力和弱核力。引力是其中最弱的自然力，但是它的作用距离最远，可影响所有的星系。
    在澳大利亚昆士兰希尔顿矿1000米深的矿井中，在美国北卡罗林纳罗利附近600米高的电视塔上和格林兰冰帽2000米深的钻孔中，地球引力的测量值同理论值不一致，一直被认为是自然界存在第五种力的证据。
    美国加利福尼亚大学的帕克和宗伯格认为，地球引力测量值同理论不一致是因为土地密度的实际值同理论值有细微差别，即质量的实际值同理论值的细微差别造成引力的测量值同理论值的不一致。
