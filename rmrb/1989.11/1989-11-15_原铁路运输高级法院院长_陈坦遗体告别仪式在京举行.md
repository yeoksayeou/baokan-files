Title: Funeral for Former President of the High Court of Railway Transportation, Chen Tan, Held in Beijing
Author:
Date: 1989-11-15
Edition: 2

Chen Tan, an outstanding member of the Communist Party of China, former member of the Central Commission for Discipline Inspection, and former president of the High Court of Railway Transportation, passed away on October 27, 1989, at the age of 78.

Chen Tan was born in Xingning County, Guangdong, in March 1911. He joined the Communist Youth League in August 1927 and soon became a member of the Communist Party. In 1930, he worked underground in Shanghai and was arrested in December of the same year. While in prison, he remained steadfast in his beliefs and maintained the dignity of a Communist Party member. During the War of Resistance Against Japanese Aggression, he worked in Xinjiang and Yan'an. In 1945, he was sent to the Yanbian area of Jilin Province to establish a revolutionary government, serving as the secretary and head of the organization department of the Jidong Provincial Party Committee. In 1948, he transferred to railway work, holding positions such as deputy secretary of the Northeast Railway Bureau Party Committee, director of the political department and deputy director of the bureau, and secretary of the Central Commission for Discipline Inspection of the Northeast Bureau. He began working at the Ministry of Railways in early 1953, first serving as acting director of the Construction Bureau and deputy director of the Engineering Bureau. Later, he became the deputy director of the Political Department of the Ministry of Railways. He was a model for railway political workers in establishing the order of railway ideological and political work and personally promoting ideological construction. After the Third Plenary Session of the Eleventh Central Committee, he was appointed president of the High Court of Railway Transportation, making significant contributions to maintaining railway public order and ensuring transportation and passenger safety. In September 1985, he voluntarily retired. Throughout his life, he firmly believed in communism and always prioritized revolutionary interests. He was upright and incorruptible, earning the love and respect of cadres and the masses.

The funeral for Chen Tan was held yesterday morning at the Babaoshan Revolutionary Cemetery. Wreaths were sent by Li Peng, Chen Yun, Wan Li, Peng Zhen, Xu Xiangqian, Qiao Shi, Wang Zhen, Li Tieying, Ding Guan'gen, Bo Yibo, and others. Jiang Hua, Duan Junyi, Ren Jianxin, Liu Fuzhi, and Li Senmao attended the funeral.

### 原铁路运输高级法院院长  陈坦遗体告别仪式在京举行

1989-11-15
第2版(国内新闻)
专栏：

    原铁路运输高级法院院长
    陈坦遗体告别仪式在京举行
    新华社北京11月14日电  中国共产党优秀党员、原中纪委委员、原铁路运输高级法院院长陈坦同志，于1989年10月27日不幸逝世，终年78岁。
    陈坦同志是广东兴宁县人，1911年3月出生。1927年8月加入共产主义青年团，不久转为共产党员。他曾于1930年在上海从事地下工作，同年12月被捕，在狱中他始终坚持斗争，保持了共产党人的高尚气节。抗日战争时期，他先后在新疆和延安做干部工作。1945年他被派到吉林省延边地区建立革命政权，曾任吉东分省委常委、秘书长兼组织部长。1948年他转到铁路工作，历任东北铁路总局党委副书记、政治部主任兼副总局长、中共中央东北局纪委书记等职。他于1953年初到铁道部工作后，先任部基建总局代局长、工程总局副总局长。后任铁道部政治部常务副主任，在建立铁路思想政治工作秩序，并身体力行地抓思想建设等方面，堪称铁路政工干部的楷模。党的十一届三中全会后，他被任命为铁路运输高级法院院长，对于维护铁路治安秩序、保障运输和旅客安全作出了重要贡献。1985年9月，他主动要求退下来。他毕生坚信共产主义，处处以革命利益为重。他光明磊落，廉洁奉公，受到干部群众的爱戴和尊敬。
    陈坦同志遗体告别仪式于昨天上午在八宝山革命公墓举行。送花圈的有李鹏、陈云、万里、彭真、徐向前、乔石、王震、李铁映、丁关根、薄一波等。江华、段君毅、任建新、刘复之、李森茂等同志参加了遗体告别仪式。
