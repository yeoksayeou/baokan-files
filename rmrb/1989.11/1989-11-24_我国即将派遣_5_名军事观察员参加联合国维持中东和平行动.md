Title: China to Send 5 Military Observers to Participate in UN Peacekeeping Mission in the Middle East
Author:
Date: 1989-11-24
Edition: 4

China will send 5 military observers to the United Nations Truce Supervision Organization to participate in maintaining peace in the Middle East.

The proposal to send 5 military observers from China has been approved by UN Secretary-General Javier Pérez de Cuéllar and members of the Security Council.

The United Nations Truce Supervision Organization was established after the first Arab-Israeli war, with its headquarters in Jerusalem. Currently, it has 298 military personnel from 19 countries stationed in Egypt, Lebanon, Jordan, and other countries.

China was admitted as a member of the UN Peacekeeping Operations Special Committee last year and has already sent 20 civilian officials to the UN Transition Assistance Group in Namibia to monitor the independence process in Namibia.

### 我国即将派遣5名军事观察员参加联合国维持中东和平行动

1989-11-24
第4版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    我国即将派遣5名军事观察员参加联合国维持中东和平行动
    新华社联合国11月22日电　中国将向联合国中东停战监督机构派遣5名军事观察员参加维持中东和平的行动。
    中国派遣5名军事观察员的建议已经得到联合国秘书长佩雷斯·德奎利亚尔和安理会成员国的同意。
    联合国中东停战监督机构是在第一次阿、以战争后成立的，总部设在耶路撒冷，目前有来自19个国家的298名军事人员，他们分别驻扎在埃及、黎巴嫩、约旦等国。
    中国已于去年被接纳为联合国维持和平行动特别委员会的成员，并已派出20名文职官员参加联合国驻纳米比亚过渡时期援助团，监督纳米比亚的独立进程。
