Title: International Conference on Atmospheric Pollution Issues Adopts Declaration
Author:
Date: 1989-11-10
Edition: 4

The two-day international ministerial conference on atmospheric pollution and climate change issues concluded today in Noordwijk, the Netherlands. Ministers and representatives from 68 countries, including China, reached a joint declaration on environmental governance.

The declaration states: "Humanity is altering the Earth at an unprecedented rate, and human society is threatened by the global climate it has changed."

The declaration states: "All countries, especially industrialized countries, should recognize the need to make their socio-economic activities and lifestyles beneficial to the environment."

The declaration holds that while countries have the right to independently manage their own resources, it emphasizes that environmental issues should be addressed through international cooperation.

The declaration points out: "Solving the debt problems of developing countries and establishing reasonable economic and commercial relations between industrialized countries and developing countries will help developing countries protect their environment."

The declaration urges industrialized countries to support international organizations in investigating the feasibility of limiting or reducing carbon dioxide emissions and reducing carbon dioxide emissions by 20% by the year 2005.

The ministers attending the conference agreed to hold a second environmental conference next September.

### 国际大气污染问题会议通过宣言  呼吁各国合作解决环境问题

1989-11-10
第4版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    国际大气污染问题会议通过宣言
    呼吁各国合作解决环境问题
    新华社诺德威克（荷兰）11月7日电　为期2天的国际关于大气污染和气候变化问题部长级会议今天在此结束。来自包括中国在内的68个国家的部长和代表达成了一项治理环境的共同宣言。
    宣言说：“人类正在以前所未有的速度改变着地球，人类社会正受到自己所改变的全球气候的威胁。”
    宣言说：“所有国家，尤其是工业化国家应认识到他们需要使自己的社会经济活动和生活方式有益于环境。”
    宣言认为，各国有权独立管理自己的资源，但它强调应通过国际合作解决环境问题。
    宣言指出：“解决发展中国家外债问题，并建立工业化国家和发展中国家之间合理的经济和商业关系将有助于发展中国家保护其环境。”
    宣言敦促工业化国家支持国际组织调查限制或降低二氧化碳释放量以及到2005年降低20％二氧化碳释放量可行性。
    参加这次会议的部长们同意将在明年9月份召开第二次环境问题会议。
