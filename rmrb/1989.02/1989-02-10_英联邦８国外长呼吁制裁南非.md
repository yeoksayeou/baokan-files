Title: Eight Commonwealth Countries' Foreign Ministers Call for Sanctions Against South Africa
Author:
Date: 1989-02-10
Edition: 6

The foreign ministers of eight Commonwealth countries called for sanctions against South Africa. The eight-nation committee on Southern Africa of the Commonwealth met in Harare and concluded a three-day meeting on February 8, issuing a communiqué calling for strengthened sanctions against the South African apartheid regime.

The communiqué stated that sanctions remain the most effective peaceful method to end the apartheid system in South Africa.

The communiqué urged international financial groups and governments to take measures to restrict trade with South Africa and refuse to provide it with loans.

The communiqué also urged the United Nations to take full responsibility for implementing Security Council Resolution 435 and achieving Namibian independence.

The foreign ministers of Canada, Australia, India, Guyana, Nigeria, Tanzania, Zambia, and Zimbabwe attended the meeting. (Xinhua News Agency)

### 英联邦８国外长呼吁制裁南非

1989-02-10
第6版(国际)
专栏：国际要闻简报

　　英联邦８国外长呼吁制裁南非
    英联邦南部非洲问题８国外长委员会８日在哈拉雷结束了为期３天的会议之后发表一项公报，呼吁强化对南非种族主义政权的制裁。
    公报说，制裁依然是结束南非种族主义制度最有效的和平方法。
    公报呼吁国际财团和各国政府采取措施限制同南非的贸易并拒绝向它提供贷款。
    公报还呼吁联合国对实施安理会４３５号决议和实现纳米比亚独立问题承担起全部责任。
    加拿大、澳大利亚、印度、圭亚那、尼日利亚、坦桑尼亚、赞比亚和津巴布韦的外交部长出席了这次会议。（据新华社）
