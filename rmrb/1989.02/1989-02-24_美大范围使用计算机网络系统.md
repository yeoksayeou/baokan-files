Title: The United States Widely Uses Computer Network Systems
Author:
Date: 1989-02-24
Edition: 3

Residents of Santa Monica, California, can connect with the city hall, check the availability of their favorite books in the library, or inquire about changes to the bus schedule, all through a computer network, as long as they have a personal computer.

According to information from Santa Monica, this public computer network is the latest attempt by local governments across the United States to communicate with citizens via computers.

The system is set up on a computer and is separate from the city's internal data processing system, thus preventing interference from users searching for data or computer "viruses."

The terminals of the system have been installed in all local libraries and are planned to be set up in recreational facilities and the under-construction senior citizen center.

The devices installed in public places are designed to be easy to operate, allowing even those unfamiliar with computers to quickly learn how to use them, and come with simple instructions.

A recent survey found that 33% of Santa Monica residents own a personal computer, and 23% have a modem, a device that allows computers to communicate over telephone lines.

### 美大范围使用计算机网络系统

1989-02-24
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

　　美大范围使用计算机网络系统
    新华社华盛顿２月２２日电  居住在美国加利福尼亚州圣莫尼卡的居民，只要装备了私人计算机，就可通过计算机网络与市政大厅联系，查看图书馆中是否有自己喜爱的图书，或询问原先的公共汽车时间表有无变动。
    据来自圣莫尼卡的消息说，这个公共计算机网络是美国各地政府通过计算机与市民联系的最新尝试。
    这个系统设置在计算机中，与该市的内部数据处理系统相分离，这样可排除用户查找数据或计算机“病毒”造成的干扰。
    系统的终端已设置在地区的所有图书馆中，并计划在娱乐场所和建设中的老年市民中心安装这种终端。
    安装在公共场所的那些设备设计得易于操作，即使不熟悉计算机的人也可很快学会使用，并且备有简单的说明。
    不久前进行的一次调查发现，３３％的圣莫尼卡市民拥有私人计算机，２３％的人拥有调制解调器，这是一种允许计算机通过电话线进行通讯的设备。
