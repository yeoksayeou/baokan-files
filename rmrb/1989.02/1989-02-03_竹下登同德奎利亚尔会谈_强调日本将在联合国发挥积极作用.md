Prime Minister Takeshita Meets with Perez de Cuellar
Emphasizes Japan's Active Role in the United Nations

NEW YORK, Feb. 1 (Xinhua) -- Japanese Prime Minister Takeshita Noboru said today that actively participating in United Nations peacekeeping activities is one of the cornerstones of Japan's foreign policy.

Takeshita held a 55-minute meeting with UN Secretary-General Javier Perez de Cuellar here. A spokesman for Takeshita later briefed reporters on the contents of the meeting.

Takeshita told the UN Secretary-General, "Japan has decided to contribute financially and in other ways to international peace and security." Takeshita arrived in New York yesterday for a one-week visit to the United States.

Prime Minister Takeshita said that Japan not only intends to increase its financial support for UN peacekeeping activities but is also prepared to send dozens of Japanese personnel to the UN Assistance Mission for the Referendum in Namibia.

Takeshita's spokesman said that in addition to providing 11% of the regular budget for the United Nations, the Japanese government also plans to allocate $280 million in special budget funds to the organization over the next two fiscal years.

### 竹下登同德奎利亚尔会谈  强调日本将在联合国发挥积极作用

1989-02-03
第6版(国际)
专栏：

　　竹下登同德奎利亚尔会谈
    强调日本将在联合国发挥积极作用
    新华社联合国２月１日电  日本首相竹下登今天说，积极参加联合国发起的维持和平活动是日本外交政策的基石之一。
    竹下登在这里同联合国秘书长佩雷斯·德奎利亚尔举行了５５分钟会谈。他的一位发言人随后向记者介绍了会谈的内容。
    竹下登对联合国秘书长表示，“日本决定通过提供财政和其他资源对国际和平安全作出积极贡献。”竹下登昨天到达纽约对美国进行为期一周的访问。
    竹下登首相说，日本不仅有意增加对联合国维持和平活动的财政支持，还准备向监督纳米比亚自由选举的联合国援助团派出几十名日本人员。
    竹下登的发言人说，除了向联合国提供１１％的经常性预算经费之外，日本政府还计划在今明两个财政年度中向这个世界组织提供２．８亿美元特别预算拨款。（附图片）
