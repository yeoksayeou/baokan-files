Title: Security Council Debates Situation in Israeli-Occupied Territories
Author:
Date: 1989-02-15
Edition: 6

The Security Council debated the situation in Israeli-occupied territories today. Some countries' representatives urged the Security Council to take "urgent action" to protect the lives of Palestinians in the occupied territories and to promote an international conference on the Middle East issue.

The permanent representatives of Malaysia, Syria, Yemen, Kuwait, Bahrain, Lebanon, and Pakistan spoke at the meeting. They also demanded that Israel recognize the legitimate rights of the Palestinian people and engage in direct talks with the Palestine Liberation Organization.

Pakistan's representative, Shah Nawaz, citing a recent report from the U.S. State Department, pointed out that due to the use of force by Israeli soldiers, many Palestinians have been injured or killed, and the human rights situation in the occupied territories has worsened.

Malaysia's representative, Razali Ismail, urged the Security Council to establish a preparatory committee for convening an international peace conference on the Middle East issue.

The Security Council's debate on the situation in the occupied territories began on the 10th. Representatives from some Arab and African countries condemned Israel's savage suppression of Palestinians in the occupied territories on the first day of the meeting. The debate will continue tomorrow.

According to a report by the Palestine News Agency, the Palestine Liberation Organization (PLO) stated that it will not sacrifice the uprising for the continuation of U.S.-Palestinian dialogue.

The PLO made this statement in response to U.S. Secretary of State James Baker's speech in Iceland on the 11th. Baker claimed that he had instructed the U.S. ambassador to Tunisia to inform the Palestinian ambassador in Tunisia that actions similar to recent attempts to infiltrate Israel from southern Lebanon would create difficulties for the U.S. He warned the PLO not to attack any Israeli military or civilian targets.

### 安理会辩论以色列占领区形势  巴解不会为美巴继续对话而牺牲起义

1989-02-15
第6版(国际)
专栏：

　　安理会辩论以色列占领区形势    
    巴解不会为美巴继续对话而牺牲起义
    新华社联合国２月１３日电  在今天举行的联合国安理会辩论以色列占领区形势的会议上，一些国家的代表敦促安理会采取“紧急行动”，以保护被占领土巴勒斯坦人的生命安全，并促成一次关于中东问题的国际会议。
    马来西亚、叙利亚、也门、科威特、巴林、黎巴嫩和巴基斯坦等国常驻联合国代表在会上发了言。他们还要求以色列承认巴勒斯坦人民的合法权利，并同巴勒斯坦解放组织举行直接会谈。
    巴基斯坦代表沙阿·纳瓦兹援引美国国务院最近的报告指出，由于以色列士兵采取武力镇压手段，许多巴勒斯坦人因此伤亡，以色列占领区的人权情况恶化。
    马来西亚代表拉扎里·伊斯梅尔敦促安理会为召开一次中东问题的国际和平会议成立一个筹备委员会。
    安理会关于以占区形势的辩论是１０日开始的，一些阿拉伯和非洲国家的代表在第一天会议上谴责了以色列对被占区巴勒斯坦人的野蛮镇压。辩论将于明天继续进行。
    新华社突尼斯２月１３日电　据巴勒斯坦通讯社报道，巴解组织表示不会为美巴继续对话而牺牲起义。
    巴解组织是为了答复美国国务卿詹姆斯·贝克１１日晚在冰岛发表的讲话而作出这一表示的。贝克声称他已要求美国驻突尼斯大使通知巴勒斯坦驻突尼斯大使，类似最近从黎巴嫩南部企图向以色列渗透的行动会对美国制造困难。他警告巴解组织不要对以色列的军事或民用目标进行任何袭击。
