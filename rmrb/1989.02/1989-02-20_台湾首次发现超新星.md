Title: Taiwan's First Discovery of a Supernova
Author:
Date: 1989-02-20
Edition: 2

Taiwan's First Discovery of a Supernova

Xinhua News Agency, Beijing, February 19—According to reports from Taiwanese newspapers, Lin Qisheng, a student at Fengjia University, discovered a supernova with a magnitude of 12 on the morning of February 12 at 4 a.m. using a reflecting telescope. The supernova was located in the outer region of the galaxy in the constellation Leo.

The report states that the "Yuan Mountain Observatory" in Taipei has notified the International Astronomical Union, which is based in the United States, about this discovery. After confirmation, it will be the first time that a Taiwanese person has discovered a supernova outside the Milky Way.

### 台湾首次发现超新星

1989-02-20
第2版(国内新闻)
专栏：四面八方

　　台湾首次发现超新星
    新华社北京２月１９日电  据台湾报纸报道，台湾逢甲大学学生林启生２月１２日清晨４时用反射望远镜观测星象，在狮子座外银河部位发现一颗光度１２等级的超新星。
    报道说，台北市“圆山天文台”已将这一情况通知设在美国的世界天文发现联络中心，经确认后，将成为台湾人第一次发现的外银河系超新星。
