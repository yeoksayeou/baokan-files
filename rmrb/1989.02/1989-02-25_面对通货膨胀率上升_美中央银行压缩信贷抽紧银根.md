Title: Facing Rising Inflation Rates, the U.S. Central Bank Tightens Credit
Author: Cang Lide
Date: 1989-02-25
Edition: 3

Facing the threat of rising inflation rates, the U.S. Federal Reserve took significant measures this morning to tighten credit and restrict monetary supply. In the afternoon, two major U.S. banks—Chase Manhattan Bank and Republic National Bank of New York—announced an increase in their prime lending rates by half a percentage point, bringing the rate to 11.5%. This marks the second increase in two weeks, pushing the prime rate to its highest level since the end of 1984, which is expected to slow down the pace of commercial and consumer loans.

The Federal Reserve's move to tighten credit poses a significant threat to the Bush administration's plan to reduce the budget deficit.

Today, Treasury Secretary Nicholas Brady criticized the Federal Reserve for being "overly concerned" about inflation.

### 面对通货膨胀率上升  美中央银行压缩信贷抽紧银根
仓立德
1989-02-25
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

　　面对通货膨胀率上升
    美中央银行压缩信贷抽紧银根
    本报华盛顿２月２３日电  记者仓立德报道：面对通货膨胀率上升的威胁，美国中央银行今晨采取压缩信贷、抽紧银根的重大举措。当天下午，美国两家重要银行——大通曼哈顿银行和纽约共和国民银行宣布将优惠贷款利率提高半个百分点，达到１１．５％，从而使优惠利率在两个星期内上升１个百分点。
    本月１１日，美国主要商业银行的优惠利率曾提高了半个百分点。这次如果其他银行效法大通曼哈顿银行的行动，优惠利率将达１９８４年底以来的最高水平，大批商业及消费贷款将放慢速度。
    中央银行压缩信贷的行动是对布什政府削减预算赤字计划的重大威胁。
    白宫财政部长布雷迪今天指责中央银行对通货膨胀“过分担心”。
