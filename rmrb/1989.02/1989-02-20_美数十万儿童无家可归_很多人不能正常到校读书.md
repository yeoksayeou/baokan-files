Title: Hundreds of Thousands of Children in the U.S. Homeless
Author: Cang Lide
Date: 1989-02-20
Edition: 3

	The U.S. Department of Education released a report yesterday stating that currently, there are approximately 220,000 homeless school-age children in the country, with 30% of them unable to attend school normally. According to the National Coalition for the Homeless, if preschool children are included, the total number of homeless children ranges from 500,000 to 750,000.

	The Department of Education noted that the cities with the highest number of homeless children are: Los Angeles (12,250), New York (10,169), Chicago (10,000), and Minneapolis (5,055).

	According to the McKinney Act, all states in the U.S. should submit detailed plans to improve the education of homeless children by April 30 of this year.

### 美数十万儿童无家可归  很多人不能正常到校读书
仓立德
1989-02-20
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

　　美数十万儿童无家可归    
    很多人不能正常到校读书
    本报华盛顿２月１８日电  记者仓立德报道：美国教育部昨天发表的一项报告说，目前全国大约有２２万名无家可归的学龄儿童，其中３０％不能正常到校读书。据美国全国无家可归者联合会测算，如包括学前儿童，无家可归的儿童总数为５０万—７５万名。
    教育部说，无家可归儿童数量最多的大城市是：洛杉矶（１２２５０名），纽约（１０１６９名），芝加哥（１万名），明尼阿波利斯（５０５５名）。
    根据麦金尼法案规定，美国各州应于今年４月３０日以前为改善无家可归儿童的教育提出详细计划。
