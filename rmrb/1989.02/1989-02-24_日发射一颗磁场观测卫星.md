Title: Japan Launches a Magnetic Field Observation Satellite
Author:
Date: 1989-02-24
Edition: 3

Japan's Ministry of Education launched a magnetic field observation satellite on February 22 to study the mechanisms behind the auroras in the Arctic and Antarctic regions.

According to the *Nihon Keizai Shimbun*, the artificial satellite, named EXOS-D, was launched from the Kagoshima Space Center using an M-3S2 rocket. The satellite has entered an elliptical orbit with an apogee of approximately 10,500 kilometers and a perigee of about 270 kilometers. Observations are scheduled to begin at the end of March.

Auroras occur at altitudes of over 100 kilometers in the Arctic and Antarctic regions when solar plasma particles collide with the atmosphere. Previous research suggests that there is an acceleration region for these particles between 5,000 and 10,000 kilometers above the Earth. The Japanese observation satellite aims to investigate the relationship between the plasma particles and the acceleration region, as well as their impact on auroras.

### 日发射一颗磁场观测卫星

1989-02-24
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

　　日发射一颗磁场观测卫星
    新华社东京２月２３日电  日本文部省为观测北极和南极上空极光的发生机制，２２日发射了一颗磁场观测卫星。
    据《日经产业新闻》报道，这颗称为ＥＸＯＳ—Ｄ的人造卫星是从鹿儿岛宇宙空间观测所用Ｍ—３Ｓ２火箭发射的。卫星已进入远地点高度约１．０５万公里，近地点约２７０公里椭圆形轨道。观测将从３月底开始。
    北极和南极上空１００多公里处的极光，是太阳的等离子粒子冲击大气层时发生的。迄今的研究认为，在５０００至１万公里上空有一等粒子加速区域。日本发射的观测卫星将查明等离子粒子与粒子加速区域的关系及对极光的影响。
