Title: Renowned Writer and Critic Bao Chang Passes Away
Author:
Date: 1989-02-22
Edition: 2

Renowned writer and critic Bao Chang passed away. The New China News Agency reported from Beijing on February 21 that renowned writer and critic Bao Chang succumbed to liver cancer in the evening of February 20 in Beijing, at the age of 59.

Bao Chang joined the Communist Party of China in 1947 and subsequently devoted himself to the party's literary cause. Over the course of more than 40 years, despite numerous hardships, he remained steadfast in his loyalty to the party and to socialism. Throughout his life, he was diligent in his writing, authoring the historical novel "Regeneration of the Dragon," the novel "Blind Flow," and editing the "Dictionary of New Literary and Artistic Terms." He also wrote numerous works on literary theory.

Bao Chang actively participated in literary organizational work. Since 1985, he has served as a member of the Party Group and a standing secretary of the Secretariat of the Chinese Writers Association.

### 著名作家、评论家鲍昌辞世

1989-02-22
第2版(国内新闻)
专栏：

　　著名作家、评论家鲍昌辞世
    新华社北京２月２１日电  著名作家、评论家鲍昌因患肝癌２月２０日晚间在北京辞世，终年５９岁。
    鲍昌１９４７年加入中国共产党，之后投身于党的文学事业。４０余年来，虽历经坎坷，但对党、对社会主义忠贞不渝。他一生勤奋著述，著有长篇历史小说《庚子风云》，长篇小说《盲流》，主编《文学艺术新术语辞典》，并撰写文艺理论著作。
    鲍昌积极从事文学组织工作。１９８５年起担任中国作家协会党组成员、书记处常务书记。
