Date: 1989-02-26

West Germany Welcomes Namibian Independence
Klein Says Government Will Provide Extensive Aid

Bonn, February 24 (Xinhua) - The West German federal government and various parliamentary groups unanimously welcomed Namibian independence in a debate in the Federal Parliament on February 24. They also expressed that the Federal Republic of Germany bears a special responsibility for Namibia's future development.

Federal Minister for Economic Cooperation, Klaus Klein, stated that, out of historical and moral responsibility, the Bonn government will do its utmost to assist in Namibia's peaceful development and will provide extensive aid.

He said that Chancellor Kohl and Foreign Minister Genscher had instructed him to make the necessary preparations for this.

According to Minister Klein, the federal government will contribute 60 million Marks to fund the United Nations observation group. In addition, it will provide civilian election observers, approximately 170 vehicles, and 60 technical personnel.

On February 24, three officials, including a representative from the United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees, arrived in Windhoek, Namibia, to begin preparations for the implementation of United Nations Resolution 435 on the peace and independence of Namibia, which will come into effect on April 1.

### 西德欢迎纳米比亚独立  克莱因说政府将给予广泛援助

1989-02-26
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

　　西德欢迎纳米比亚独立
    克莱因说政府将给予广泛援助
    新华社波恩２月２４日电  西德联邦政府和各议会党团２４日在联邦议院关于纳米比亚的辩论中一致欢迎纳米比亚独立，并表示联邦德国对纳米比亚今后的发展负有特殊的责任。
    联邦经济合作部长克莱因说，出于历史的和道义的责任，波恩政府将尽力协助纳米比亚的和平发展，并将给予广泛的援助。
    他说，联邦总理科尔和外长根舍已责成他为此作好必要的准备。
    据克莱因部长说，联邦政府将为资助联合国观察小组出资６０００万马克，此外还将提供民间选举观察员、大约１７０辆汽车和６０名技术人员。（附图片）
     2月24日，联合国难民事务高级专员公署代表等3名官员飞抵纳米比亚温得和克，开始为执行将于4月1日生效的有关纳米比亚和平与独立的联合国435号决议作准备。
