Title: Former Deputy Commander of the Fuzhou Military Region, Zhu Shaoping, Passes Away
Author:
Date: 1989-02-16
Edition: 4

The farewell ceremony for the late Zhu Shaoping, an outstanding member of the Communist Party of China, an excellent military commander of the People's Liberation Army, and the former Deputy Commander of the Fuzhou Military Region, was held today at the Fujian Provincial Military Region Hall.

Zhu Shaoping passed away on February 3 in Fuzhou due to illness, at the age of 76.

Leaders including Zhao Ziyang, Deng Xiaoping, Yang Shangkun, Li Peng, Xu Xiangqian, Nie Rongzhen, Qiao Shi, Hu Qili, Yao Yilin, Wang Zhen, Qin Jiwei, Li Desheng, Yang Dezhi, Yu Quli, Song Shilun, Zhang Aiping, Lu Dingyi, Ji Pengfei, Peng Chong, Wei Guoqing, Ye Fei, Fang Yi, Hong Xuezhi, and Liu Huaqing sent wreaths.

The Central Committee of the Communist Party, the Central Military Commission, the Central Discipline Inspection Commission, the Ministry of National Defense, the various General Departments of the PLA, the Central Military Commission Discipline Inspection Commission, the Nanjing Military Region, the provinces of Fujian, Shanghai, Jiangsu, Zhejiang, Anhui, Jiangxi, and Hunan, and other party and government military leadership organizations also sent wreaths.

Liao Hansheng and other responsible comrades from the Nanjing Military Region and Fujian Province attended the farewell ceremony and sent wreaths.

Zhu Shaoping was from Huachong County, Hunan Province. He joined the Chinese Workers' and Peasants' Red Army in 1929 and became a member of the Communist Party of China in 1930. He held various positions, including soldier, squad leader, platoon leader, company commander, battalion commander, regiment commander, division commander, military commander, Secretary of the Fujian Provincial Party Committee, and Acting Commander of the Fuzhou Military Region. During the Land Revolution War, he participated in the arduous anti-"encirclement and suppression" battles in the Hunan-Hubei-Western Hubei Soviet Area and the Long March, fighting bravely, fearless of sacrifice, leading by example, and fighting valiantly. During the Anti-Japanese War, he led his troops to advance into the enemy's rear in Huadong and the eastern region of the Tianpu Railway, participating in the Huainan Tianyi Yang battle and the anti-bandit battle in Luxi, actively developing anti-Japanese armed forces, conducting guerrilla warfare, uniting progressive forces, and striking at reactionary forces, making significant contributions to the opening, consolidation, and development of anti-Japanese base areas. During the War of Liberation, he led his troops to participate in the battles of Baita, Chaoyangji, Sizhou, Suzhou, Lu'nan, Laiwu, Menglianggu, Nanma, Yanshan, Huaihai, Dujiang, Shanghai, and Fuzhou, completing combat missions excellently. After the founding of the People's Republic of China, he led his troops to long-term garrison duty in the coastal areas of Fujian, making significant contributions to the construction of the military region's troops and coastal defense in Fujian. During the ten years of turmoil, he firmly opposed the counter-revolutionary cliques of Lin Biao and the "Gang of Four." He firmly supported the line, principles, and policies since the Third Plenary Session of the Eleventh Central Committee, adhered to the Four Cardinal Principles, and maintained high political and ideological consistency with the Central Committee. He was a representative of the Third National People's Congress, a candidate member of the Eleventh Central Committee of the Communist Party, and a member of the Twelfth Central Discipline Inspection Commission. He was awarded the rank of Major General in 1955.

Throughout his more than 60 years of revolutionary career, Zhu Shaoping dedicated his life to the cause of the proletarian revolution. He had a firm communist ideal and belief, was fearless of sacrifice, indomitable, open-hearted, and upright, and was loyal to the revolutionary cause. He had rich leadership experience and outstanding military command abilities. He had a strong sense of duty, worked actively, took his responsibilities seriously, was willing to endure hardships, and completed tasks decisively. He considered the overall situation, had a strong sense of party spirit and organizational discipline, was modest and cautious, united with comrades, connected with the masses, and acted fairly. He was strict with himself, honest and upright, and maintained the political character of hard struggle and the fine traditions of our party and army.

### 原福州军区副司令员朱绍清逝世

1989-02-16
第4版(政治·法律·社会)
专栏：

　　原福州军区副司令员朱绍清逝世
    新华社福州２月１４日电  向中国共产党优秀党员、人民解放军优秀军事指挥员、原福州军区副司令员朱绍清遗体告别的仪式，今天在福建省军区礼堂举行。
    朱绍清同志因病于２月３日在福州逝世，终年７６岁。
    赵紫阳、邓小平、杨尚昆、李鹏、徐向前、聂荣臻、乔石、胡启立、姚依林、王震、秦基伟、李德生、杨得志、余秋里、宋时轮、张爱萍、陆定一、姬鹏飞、彭冲、韦国清、叶飞、方毅、洪学智、刘华清等领导同志，中共中央、中央军委、中央纪委、国防部、解放军各总部、中央军委纪委、南京军区、福建省、上海市、江苏省、浙江省、安徽省、江西省、湖南省等党政军领导机关送了花圈。
    廖汉生以及南京军区和福建省的负责同志参加了向朱绍清遗体告别的仪式并送了花圈。
    朱绍清同志是湖南省华容县人，１９２９年参加中国工农红军，１９３０年转为中国共产党党员，历任战士、班长、排长、连长、营长、团长、师长、军长、中共福建省委书记、原福州军区代理司令员等职。土地革命战争时期，他参加了艰苦卓绝的湘鄂西苏区反“围剿”战斗和二万五千里长征，作战勇敢，不怕牺牲，身先士卒，英勇杀敌。抗日战争时期，他率部挺进皖东敌后和津浦路东地区，参加了淮南天仪扬战役、路西反顽战役，积极发展抗日武装，开展游击战争，团结进步力量，打击顽固势力，为开辟、巩固和发展抗日根据地作出了积极的贡献。解放战争时期，他率部先后参加了白塔、朝阳集、泗县、宿北、鲁南、莱芜、孟良崮、南麻、盐南、淮海、渡江、上海、福州等战役战斗，出色地完成了作战任务。中华人民共和国成立以后，他领导部队长期驻守福建沿海地区，对军区部队和福建沿海的海防建设有重大建树，在十年动乱期间，他对林彪、“四人帮”反革命集团进行了坚决的斗争。他坚决拥护党的十一届三中全会以来的路线、方针、政策，坚持四项基本原则，在政治上思想上自觉与党中央保持高度一致。他是第三次全国人民代表大会代表，党的第十一届中央委员会候补委员，第十二届中央纪律检查委员会委员。他１９５５年被授予少将军衔。
    在６０余年的革命生涯中，朱绍清同志为无产阶级革命事业无私地奉献了毕生的精力。他有坚定的共产主义理想和信念，不怕牺牲，坚韧不拔，胸怀坦荡，光明磊落，对革命事业赤胆忠诚；他具有丰富的领导工作经验和出色的军事指挥才能；他事业心强，工作积极，认真负责，任劳任怨，完成任务坚决；他顾全大局，有坚强的党性和组织纪律观念；他谦虚谨慎，团结同志，联系群众，办事公道；他严于律己，廉洁奉公，保持了艰苦奋斗的政治本色和我党我军的优良传统。
