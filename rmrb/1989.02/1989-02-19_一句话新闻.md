Author:
Date: 1989-02-19
Edition: 6

One-Sentence News

	△ West German police revealed that an Afghan suspected of murdering two Chinese businessmen in Hamburg last year has been extradited from Sweden to West Germany for trial. The suspect is 22 years old and named Ahmad.

	△ South Korean opposition leader Kim Dae-jung arrived in Budapest via Prague on February 12, beginning a four-day visit to Hungary.

	△ The Brazilian government will further push its privatization plan because the state does not have the money to support loss-making state-owned enterprises.

	△ Officials in El Salvador announced that former guerrilla leader Miguel Castaneda was killed by guerrillas in San Salvador on the evening of the 16th.

	△ Muslims in some Pakistani cities continued to rally on the 17th, demanding the extradition of "The Satanic Verses" author Salman Rushdie to Pakistan for trial and execution.

### 一句话新闻

1989-02-19
第6版(国际)
专栏：一句话新闻

　　一句话新闻
    △西德警方透露，涉嫌去年在汉堡谋杀两名中国商人的一名阿富汗人已由瑞典引渡到西德接受审理，此人２２岁，名叫艾哈迈迪。
    △南朝鲜平民党总裁金大中１２日经布拉格抵达布达佩斯，开始对匈牙利为期４天的访问。
    △巴西政府将进一步推行私有化计划，因为国家没有钱来支撑亏空的国营企业。
    △萨尔瓦多官方人士宣布，萨游击队前领导人米格尔·卡斯特利亚诺斯１６日晚在圣萨尔瓦多城被游击队杀害。
    △巴基斯坦一些城市的穆斯林１７日继续集会，要求引渡《撒旦的诗篇》作者萨曼·拉什迪到巴基斯坦，并判处死刑。
