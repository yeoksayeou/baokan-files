Author:
Date: 1989-02-15
Edition: 7

Romania Unearths a Batch of Ancient Coins

	New China News Agency, Bucharest, February 13th — Romanian archaeologists recently unearthed 276 intricately crafted ancient coins in Constanta County.

	According to the Romanian News Agency, the earliest of these coins was minted in 1379, while the latest dates to the late 17th century. The batch includes gold and silver coins from various eras.

	Thanks to the continuous efforts of archaeologists, various ancient artifacts were discovered across Romania last year. Notably, experts from the Arges County Museum found a subterranean water supply system and heating equipment made from ceramic pipes at the ruins of an old castle dating back to the 2nd or 3rd century.

### 罗马尼亚出土一批古币

1989-02-15
第7版(国际)
专栏：

　　罗马尼亚出土一批古币
    新华社布加勒斯特２月１３日电  罗马尼亚考古工作者最近在康斯坦察县发掘出２７６枚制作精美的古币。
    据罗通社报道，这批古币中最早的是１３７９年铸造的，最晚的则在１７世纪末，是一批不同年代的金币和银币。
    由于考古工作者的不断努力，去年罗各地均有古代文物出土。其中有阿尔杰什县博物馆专家在一个二三世纪旧城堡的遗址上发现用陶瓷管制作的地下供水系统和取暖设备。
