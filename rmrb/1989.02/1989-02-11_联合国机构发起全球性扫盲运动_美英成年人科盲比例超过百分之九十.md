Title: United Nations Agencies Launch Global Literacy Campaign
Author:
Date: 1989-02-11
Edition: 7

United Nations Agencies Launch Global Literacy Campaign
	American and British Adults Have Over 90% Science Illiteracy Rate

	NEW YORK, Feb. 11 (Xinhua) -- The heads of four United Nations agencies met in Washington on February 6 to discuss launching a campaign against illiteracy and ignorance in developing countries.

	The global initiative, spearheaded by the United Nations Development Programme, UNESCO, the United Nations Children's Fund, and the World Bank, aims to ensure the quality of primary education for children and provide adults with the basic knowledge necessary to thrive in modern society.

	UNESCO Director-General Federico Mayor stated, "The development of human resources is the core of the entire development process. This meeting, led by the heads of four UN agencies, will determine the focus of this task."

	The meeting agreed to hold a conference in Bangkok, Thailand, from March 5 to 9 next year to formulate a global plan to provide basic primary education to more than 80% of 14-year-olds, equip adults with the basic knowledge and skills needed to improve their quality of life, and significantly reduce illiteracy rates.

	Additionally, the meeting decided to establish a secretariat in New York to prepare for this conference.

	LONDON, Feb. 11 (Xinhua) -- According to a report in the British journal *Nature*, a survey indicates that over 90% of adults in Britain and the United States are scientifically illiterate.

	The survey was presented at the annual meeting of the American Association for the Advancement of Science in San Francisco in January. The questions were straightforward, such as: "Does the Earth orbit the Sun, or does the Sun orbit the Earth?" and "Is it safe to consume milk contaminated by radioactivity after boiling it?"

	The results showed that only 6% of American adults and 7% of British adults possess even a basic level of scientific knowledge.

	Although 80% of Americans and 50% of Britons expressed interest in new discoveries in medicine, 75% of Americans and 70% of Britons mistakenly believed that antibiotics can treat viral infections.

	While over 90% of Americans could correctly identify smoking, lack of exercise, stress, and excessive consumption of animal fats as risk factors for heart disease, 50% to 65% of people mistakenly believed that food additives and vitamin deficiencies are also risk factors for heart disease.

	This survey has drawn attention in both countries, particularly to the need for improved science education in secondary schools.

### 联合国机构发起全球性扫盲运动  美英成年人科盲比例超过百分之九十

1989-02-11
第7版(国际)
专栏：

　　联合国机构发起全球性扫盲运动    
    美英成年人科盲比例超过百分之九十
    新华社华盛顿电  联合国的四个专门机构的负责人二月六日在华盛顿开会，商讨在第三世界国家发起一场向文盲和无知开战的运动。
    这场由联合国开发计划署、联合国教科文组织、联合国儿童基金会和世界银行发起的全球性运动旨在保证儿童所受初等教育的质量和向成人提供立足现代社会所必需的基本知识。
    联合国教科文组织总干事费德里克·马约尔说，“人才资源的开发是整个开发过程的核心，本次由四个联合国机构领导人召开的会议将决定这项任务的重点。”
    会议同意明年三月五日至九日在泰国首都曼谷召开会议，制定一项向百分之八十以上的十四岁儿童提供基本的初等教育，向成人提供改善生活素质所需的基本知识和技能，并且大幅度降低文盲率的全球性计划。
    此外，会议还决定在纽约专门设立了一个秘书处，为召开这一会议进行必要的准备。
    新华社伦敦电　据英国《自然》杂志报道，一项调查说明，在英国和美国的成年人中，科盲比例超过百分之九十。
    这项调查是一月份在旧金山召开的美国科学促进协会年会上发表的。调查的问题很简单，例如：“是地球绕太阳转，还是太阳绕地球转？”“受放射性污染的牛奶经煮沸后食用就安全吗？”
    调查结果表明，只有百分之六的美国成人和百分之七的英国成人具有起码的科学知识。
    虽然百分之八十的美国人和百分之五十的英国人说，他们对医学上的新发现很感兴趣，但是竟有百分之七十五的美国人和百分之七十的英国人误以为抗菌素能治疗病毒感染。
    虽然百分之九十以上的美国人能正确地指出，抽烟、缺少锻炼、紧张和吃动物脂肪太多是得心脏病的危险因素，但是百分之五十至百分之六十五的人误以为食物添加剂和缺乏维生素容易得心脏病。
    这项调查已经引起英美两国重新注意特别是在中学的科学教育。
