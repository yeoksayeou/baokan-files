Title: G7 Finance Ministers Hold Meeting to Coordinate Economic Policies and Seek Solutions to Debt Issues
Author:
Date: 1989-02-04
Edition: 7

The finance ministers and central bank governors of the seven major Western industrialized nations held a meeting in Washington today to discuss how to further coordinate their economic policies and seek solutions to the debt problems of the Third World.

The two-day meeting was chaired by U.S. Treasury Secretary Nicholas Brady, who will elaborate on the economic policies of the Bush administration to the allies.

According to reports, representatives from Japan, West Germany, Britain, France, Canada, and Italy attending the meeting expressed concern over the high U.S. fiscal deficit, as the massive deficit would force the U.S. government to adopt a high-interest rate policy to prevent overheating of the economy, making the dollar even stronger.

On the issue of Third World debt, the attending allies called on the United States to change its so-called "Baker Plan." Under this plan, banks in developed countries would provide new loans to the 15 most indebted countries in the world, but would not reduce their external debt burden.

President Bush has indicated that he will reconsider the U.S. position on Third World external debt. He has ordered the Treasury Department, the State Department, and the National Security Council to study the issue and propose new strategies.

### 西方七国财长举行会议　协调相互之间经济政策　寻求债务问题解决办法

1989-02-04
第7版(国际)
专栏：

　　西方七国财长举行会议
　　协调相互之间经济政策　寻求债务问题解决办法
    新华社华盛顿２月２日电  西方７个主要工业国家的财政部长和中央银行行长今天在这里举行会议，讨论如何进一步协调相互间的经济政策，同时寻求解决第三世界债务问题的途径。
    这次为期两天的会议由美国财政部长尼古拉斯·布雷迪主持，他将向盟国详细阐述布什政府的经济政策。
    据悉，参加会议的日本、西德、英国、法国、加拿大和意大利的代表们对美国居高不下的财政赤字表示关切，因为庞大的赤字将迫使美国政府采取高利率政策来防止经济发展过热，从而使美元更加坚挺。
    在第三世界债务问题上，与会的盟国代表要求美国改变所谓的“贝克计划”，按照这一计划，发达国家的银行将向世界上１５个最大的负债国提供新的贷款，但却不减少这些国家的外债负担。
    布什总统已表示要重新考虑美国在第三世界外债问题上的立场，他已下令财政部、国务院和国家安全委员会研究这一问题，提出新的对策。
