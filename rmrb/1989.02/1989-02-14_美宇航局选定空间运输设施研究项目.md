Title: NASA Selects Space Transportation Facilities Research Project
Author:
Date: 1989-02-14
Edition: 7

	NASA has selected a three-year research project on space transportation facilities. The project will be conducted at NASA's Marshall Space Flight Center.

	The goal of this research is to identify future space systems needed to supplement existing space shuttles and expendable launch vehicles, and to determine the technologies required to manufacture these systems.

	This research will review the interrelationships of NASA's existing space transportation facilities, including the space shuttle, shuttle launch facilities, booster rockets, Centaur rockets, and expendable launch vehicles. According to Alan Lovelace, the director of NASA's Space Systems, the overall thrust of the research will provide a reference for developing concentrated space transportation, which can make the best use of current national space transportation resources. This research will cover all space transportation needs, such as the assembly of the space station to be completed in the 1990s and manned flights to the moon.

### 美宇航局选定空间运输设施研究项目

1989-02-14
第7版(国际)
专栏：

　　美宇航局选定空间运输设施研究项目
    新华社华盛顿２月１２日电  美国宇航局已选定一项为期三年的空间运输设施研究的项目。该项目将在美国宇航局马歇尔空间飞行中心进行。
    这项研究的目标是确认补充现有航天飞机和消耗性发射装置所需要的未来空间系统，并且确认制造这些系统所需的技术。
    在这次研究中将对美国宇航局现有的空间运输装置，包括航天飞机、航天飞机发射装置、助推火箭、半人马座火箭和可消耗性发射装置的相互关系进行审核。据宇航局空间系统总负责人阿兰·洛夫莱斯说，总动力研究将为发展空间集中运输提供参考，空间集中运输可使现在的国家空间运输资源得到最好的应用。这项研究包括全部空间运输需要，如将在９０年代中完成的空间站装配、到月球的载人飞行等。
