Title: Yugoslav Federal Presidency Chairman Dizdarevic Emphasizes That the Non-Aligned Movement Must Reform to Adapt to Changes in International Relations
Author:
Date: 1989-02-15
Edition: 6

Yugoslav Federal Presidency Chairman Dizdarevic today emphasized that the Non-Aligned Movement must undergo reform to adapt to changes in international relations. The ninth summit of non-aligned countries, to be held this September in Yugoslavia, will formulate a new long-term strategy for the Non-Aligned Movement.

Dizdarevic made these remarks at the first meeting of the Yugoslav committee preparing for the ninth summit of non-aligned countries.

He said that the task of preparing for the summit of non-aligned countries is of great significance for Yugoslavia's international status and its interests and prestige in the world. Therefore, he urged the entire country to make active contributions to the successful holding of this summit.

Yugoslav Foreign Minister Loncar made the main speech at today's meeting. He pointed out that the Non-Aligned Movement is an independent global factor that plays a role in promoting the democratization of international relations.

He said that the Non-Aligned Movement should put forward new viewpoints and new ideas on global issues and should show flexibility and effectiveness in solving international problems. Loncar, when discussing the drafting of various documents for the summit, pointed out that the summit documents must be concise and to the point.

According to Federal Government member and Chairman of the summit organizational preparatory committee Mujzinovic, approximately 150 delegations, totaling about 6,000 people, are expected to attend the ninth summit of non-aligned countries. He said that, based on Yugoslavia's current economic capabilities, efforts will be made to economize and seek practical results during the preparation and holding of the summit.

Yugoslavia is one of the founding countries of the Non-Aligned Movement. The first summit of non-aligned countries was held in Belgrade in 1961. Yugoslav leaders hope that the ninth summit of non-aligned countries will become a turning point in the modernization of the Non-Aligned Movement, enabling it to play a greater role in the new international situation.

### 南斯拉夫联邦主席团主席迪兹达雷维奇强调  为适应世界关系变化  不结盟运动必须改革

1989-02-15
第6版(国际)
专栏：

　　南斯拉夫联邦主席团主席迪兹达雷维奇强调    
    为适应世界关系变化    
    不结盟运动必须改革
    新华社贝尔格莱德２月１３日电  南斯拉夫联邦主席团主席迪兹达雷维奇今天强调说，不结盟运动必须进行改革以适应世界关系的变化，将于今年９月在南斯拉夫举行的第９次不结盟国家首脑会议将要制定不结盟运动新的长期战略。
    迪兹达雷维奇是在今天主持南斯拉夫筹备第９次不结盟国家首脑会议委员会第一次会议上说这番话的。
    他说，委托南斯拉夫筹备不结盟国家首脑会议对南斯拉夫的国际地位及其在世界上的利益和威望具有重大意义。因此，他要求南斯拉夫举国上下为努力开好这次会议积极作出贡献。
    南外长隆查尔在今天的会议上作了主要发言。他指出，不结盟运动是对国际关系民主化起促进作用的独立的全球性因素。
    他说，不结盟运动要对世界性问题提出新观点、新看法，在解决国际问题方面要表现出灵活性和有效性。隆查尔在谈到为首脑会议起草各种文件时指出，会议文件必须简明扼要。
    据联邦政府成员、首脑会议组织工作筹备委员会主席穆耶齐诺维奇估计，出席第９次不结盟国家首脑会议的将有１５０个代表团，共约６０００人。他说，根据南斯拉夫目前的经济能力，在筹备和召开首脑会议期间将尽量注意节约，讲求实效。
    南斯拉夫是不结盟运动的创始国之一。１９６１年第一次不结盟国家首脑会议就是在贝尔格莱德举行的。南斯拉夫领导人希望第９次不结盟国家首脑会议成为不结盟运动现代化的转折点，使不结盟运动能在新的国际形势下发挥更大的作用。
