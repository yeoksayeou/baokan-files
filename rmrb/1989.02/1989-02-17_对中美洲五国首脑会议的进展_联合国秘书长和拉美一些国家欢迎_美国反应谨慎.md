Title: Progress in the Summit of Five Central American Countries
Author: Liú Kāichén
Date: 1989-02-17
Edition: 6

Progress in the Summit of Five Central American Countries
United Nations Secretary-General and Some Latin American Countries Welcome
United States Responds Cautiously

BEIJING, Feb. 15 (Xinhua) -- United Nations Secretary-General Javier Perez de Cuellar today praised the "encouraging results" achieved at the recently concluded summit of five Central American countries.

In a statement issued through the UN spokesperson, Perez de Cuellar also said the outcome reflected the sincere efforts of the five Central American countries to bring the peace process back on track.

Perez de Cuellar pointed out that the implementation of the Central American Peace Agreement signed in Guatemala City in August 1987 could be assisted by the United Nations, and he himself was prepared to help promote this peace process.

WASHINGTON, Feb. 15 (Xinhua) -- The United States today responded cautiously to the agreement reached by the presidents of five Central American countries on the withdrawal of anti-government forces from bases in Honduras.

State Department spokesman Richard Boucher said the United States would consult with the five countries on the results of the Central American presidential summit.

Boucher did not rule out the possibility of the United States continuing to support the Nicaraguan anti-government forces, and said President Bush and Secretary of State Baker had clearly expressed this support.

BEIJING, Feb. 16 (Xinhua) -- Leaders of some Latin American countries issued written or verbal statements on Feb. 15, congratulating the positive results achieved at the recently held fourth summit of the five Central American countries on the issue of achieving domestic peace in Nicaragua.

Mexican President Carlos Salinas de Gortari and Ecuadorian President Rodrigo Borja Cevallos sent letters or telegrams to the presidents of the five Central American countries to express their congratulations. Salinas said in his letter that while conflicts in other hotspots around the world are being resolved, the elimination of this conflict point in Central America should be given attention by the international community.

United Nations Secretary-General Perez de Cuellar and the Secretary-General of the Organization of American States, Joao Baena Soares, both expressed their willingness to work with the countries of Central America to achieve peace in the region.

### 对中美洲五国首脑会议的进展  联合国秘书长和拉美一些国家欢迎  美国反应谨慎
刘开宸
1989-02-17
第6版(国际)
专栏：

　　对中美洲五国首脑会议的进展   
    联合国秘书长和拉美一些国家欢迎    
    美国反应谨慎
    本报联合国２月１５日电  记者刘开宸报道：联合国秘书长佩雷斯·德奎利亚尔今天赞扬昨天闭幕的中美洲五国首脑会议取得了“令人鼓舞的成果”。
    德奎利亚尔在这项通过联合国发言人发表的声明中还表示，会议的结果反映了中美洲五国要使和平进程回到轨道上的真诚努力。
    德奎利亚尔指出，１９８７年８月在危地马拉城签署的中美洲和平协议的执行可望得到联合国的帮助，他本人也准备帮助推动这一和平进程。
    新华社华盛顿２月１５日电　美国今天对中美洲五国总统达成的关于撤除尼加拉瓜反政府武装在洪都拉斯的基地的协议反应谨慎。
    国务院发言人雷德曼说，美国将与五国政府就中美洲总统会晤的结果进行磋商。
    雷德曼不愿排除美国继续支持尼反政府武装的可能性，并说布什总统和国务卿贝克已明确表示了这种支持。
    新华社北京２月１６日电　综合本社驻墨西哥、厄瓜多尔和美国记者报道：拉美一些国家领导人１５日纷纷发表书面或口头声明，祝贺最近举行的第４次中美洲５国首脑会议就尼加拉瓜实现国内和平问题取得的积极成果。
    墨西哥总统萨利纳斯和厄瓜多尔总统博尔哈分别写信或打电报给中美洲５国首脑表示祝贺。萨利纳斯在信中说，在世界其它热点地区的冲突趋于解决的时候，消除中美洲这个冲突点应该得到国际社会的重视。
    联合国秘书长德奎利亚尔和美洲国家组织秘书长巴埃纳分别发表谈话表示，愿与中美洲各国合作为实现这一地区的和平而努力。
