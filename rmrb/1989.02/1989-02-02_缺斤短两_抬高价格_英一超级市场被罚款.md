Title: Shortchanging and Price Gouging: A UK Supermarket Fined
Author: Lǐ Zōngyáng
Date: 1989-02-02
Edition: 7

A UK supermarket chain with 185 branches, Sainsbury's, was fined £6,625 (approximately $11,600) today in a London court for overcharging and shortchanging customers, in violation of British laws.

Last summer, local government consumer service officials discovered 102 violations during inspections of three of the supermarket's stores. The most serious offenses included charging £1.99 for a bottle of apple juice that should have cost £0.99, and selling a package of pork liver labeled as 12 ounces that actually weighed only 7 ounces. The prosecutor stated that the supermarket's illegal activities involved a wide range of products, including beverages, mushrooms, chicken, cooking oil, and shampoo.

The supermarket has apologized for the "errors" found in the three stores, attributing them to both human error and failure to strictly enforce existing regulations.

To protect consumer interests, British authorities enacted the "Trade Descriptions Act" in 1968 and the "Weights and Measures Act" in 1985, which strictly regulate the pricing and weight standards of goods.

### 缺斤短两　抬高价格  英一超级市场被罚款
李宗扬
1989-02-02
第7版(国际)
专栏：

　　缺斤短两　抬高价格
    英一超级市场被罚款
    新华社伦敦１月３１日电  （记者李宗扬）英国一家有１８５个分店的超级市场——塞弗维超级市场，因抬高标价和缺斤少两触犯了英国有关法案，今天在伦敦一家地方法院被判罚款６６２５英镑（约合１．１６万美元）。
    去年夏天，地方政府消费者服务官员在访问这家超级市场的三个门市部时发现了１０２项构成触犯法律的行为，其中最严重的行为是：每瓶价值９９便士的苹果汁按１镑９９便士收费，每包１２盎司重的猪肝实重只有７盎司。检察官说，塞弗维超级市场违法行为涉及的商品范围很广，其中包括饮料、蘑菇、鸡、食用油和洗发剂等。
    这家超级市场已经对在三个门市部出现的“错误”表示抱歉，并说明，这里既有人为的错误和失误，也有未能严格执行已有法规制度的原因。
    为维护消费者利益，英国有关当局１９６８年曾颁布执行“行业说明书法案”，１９８５年颁布度量衡法案，对商品的价格构成及重量标准都有严格的规定。
