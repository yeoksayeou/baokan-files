Title: British Prime Minister Condemns the Buying and Selling of Human Organs
Author:
Date: 1989-02-04
Edition: 7

British Prime Minister Margaret Thatcher today said in the House of Commons that the British government is considering enacting new regulations to stop the buying and selling of human organs.

She said, "The buying and selling of kidneys and any other human organs is utterly abhorrent."

A recent investigation ordered by the British Department of Health revealed that two Turkish individuals had sold their kidneys to a hospital in London for 2,000 pounds (approximately $3,058).

Last week, Thatcher had already expressed her indignation in the House of Commons over the buying and selling of human organs, calling it an inhumane act.

### 英首相谴责买卖人体器官

1989-02-04
第7版(国际)
专栏：

　　英首相谴责买卖人体器官
    新华社伦敦２月２日电  英国首相撒切尔夫人今天在下院发表讲话说，英国政府正在考虑制定新的法规，以制止买卖人体器官。 
    她说，“买卖肾脏和其它任何人体器官的行为都是令人深恶痛绝的。”
    英国卫生部最近下令进行的一项调查表明，曾有两名土耳其人向伦敦的一家医院出售了他们的肾脏，索款２０００英镑（约３０５８美元）。
    上星期，撒切尔夫人在议会下院对买卖人体器官的行为已表示愤慨，认为这是不人道的行为。
