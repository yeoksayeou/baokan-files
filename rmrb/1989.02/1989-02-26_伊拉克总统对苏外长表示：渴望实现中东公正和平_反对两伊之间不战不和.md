Title: Iraqi President Tells Soviet Foreign Minister: Eager for Just and Comprehensive Peace in the Middle East, Opposes Neither War Nor Peace Between Iran and Iraq

Author:

Date: 1989-02-26

Edition: 3

Iraqi President Saddam Hussein on February 24 stressed that Iraq is eager to achieve just and comprehensive peace in the Middle East and opposes the current situation of neither war nor peace between Iraq and Iran.

According to the Iraqi News Agency, Saddam made these remarks during a meeting with Soviet Foreign Minister Eduard Shevardnadze, who is visiting Iraq.

During the three-hour meeting, the two sides discussed the stalemated Iran-Iraq talks, the Arab-Israeli conflict, and the Palestinian cause. The Soviet Foreign Minister also handed a letter from Soviet leader Mikhail Gorbachev to Saddam.

Shevardnadze said that the Soviet Union respects Iraq's position and will work to resolve the Iran-Iraq dispute.

The Soviet Foreign Minister arrived here late on the 23rd. Iraq is the fourth stop on his five-nation tour of the Middle East.

### 伊拉克总统对苏外长表示：渴望实现中东公正和平  反对两伊之间不战不和

1989-02-26
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

　　伊拉克总统对苏外长表示：
    渴望实现中东公正和平  反对两伊之间不战不和
    新华社巴格达２月２４日电  伊拉克总统萨达姆·侯赛因２月２４日在这里强调，伊拉克渴望实现中东公正和全面的和平，反对目前伊拉克和伊朗之间不战不和的局面。
    据伊拉克通讯社报道，萨达姆是在会晤正在这里访问的苏联外长谢瓦尔德纳泽时说这番话的。
    双方在３个小时的会晤中讨论了陷于僵局的两伊和谈、阿拉伯—以色列冲突和巴勒斯坦事业。苏外长还向萨达姆转交了苏联领导人戈尔巴乔夫的一封信。
    谢瓦尔德纳泽说，苏联尊重伊拉克的立场，并将为解决两伊争端而努力。
    苏联外长是２３日晚抵达这里的。伊拉克是他中东５国之行的第４站。
