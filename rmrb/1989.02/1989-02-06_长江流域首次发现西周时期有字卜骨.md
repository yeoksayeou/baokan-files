Title: First Discovery of Inscribed Oracle Bones from the Western Zhou Period in the Yangtze River Basin

Author: Shī Yǒngfēng Chén Huìmíng
Date: 1989-02-06
Edition: 1

Archaeologists recently discovered inscribed oracle bones from the Western Zhou period in Zhìwǔshān, Xiāngfán City, Hubei Province. This is the first time such artifacts have been found in the Yangtze River Basin.

The inscribed bone is meticulously crafted from the right scapula of a cow. The bone surface is thin and flat, with two small, connected circular holes drilled on the back. Near the spine edge, there are 13 rows of drilled holes, totaling 79 complete holes, each drilled twice in a rotational manner.

### 长江流域首次发现西周时期有字卜骨
施勇峰　陈惠明
1989-02-06
第1版(要闻)
专栏：

　　长江流域首次发现西周时期有字卜骨
    据新华社武汉２月５日电  （记者施勇峰、通讯员陈惠明）考古工作者最近在湖北省襄樊市直武山发现了西周时期有字卜骨，这是在长江流域首次发现周代有字卜骨。
    这片有文字的卜骨，它用牛的右侧肩胛骨精制而成，整个骨面薄而平，背面有两小圆孔相连排列整齐的钻孔，靠近脊侧缘现存有钻孔１３排，共７９个完整孔，每孔经两次旋钻。
