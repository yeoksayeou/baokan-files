Title: African Countries Stand on the Side of Peace, Demanding Major Powers Stop Nuclear Testing
Author:
Date: 1958-04-24
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, Accra, April 23rd: The Conference of Independent African States passed a resolution on international peace and security on the 22nd, demanding that major powers halt the production and testing of nuclear weapons and devote nuclear energy exclusively to peaceful purposes.

The resolution strongly condemns all atomic tests conducted anywhere in the world, particularly the attempts to conduct such tests in the Sahara.

The resolution also calls for the reduction of conventional armaments; condemns the policy of using arms sales as a means of exerting pressure on other countries' governments and interfering in their internal affairs; demands that the United Nations ensure fair representation for African countries in all international bodies concerned with disarmament; expresses deep concern about the Palestine issue, the Southwest Africa issue, and similar issues that are factors disrupting world peace and security, and calls for just solutions to these problems.

### 非洲国家站在和平一边  要求大国停止核试验

1958-04-24
第6版()
专栏：

    非洲国家站在和平一边
    要求大国停止核试验
    新华社阿克拉23日电　非洲独立国家会议在22日通过的关于国际和平和安全的决议中要求大国停止生产和试验核武器，把原子核能专门用于和平目的。
    决议强烈谴责在世界任何地方进行的一切原子试验，特别是打算在撒哈拉进行这种试验的企图。
    决议还号召裁减常规军备；谴责利用销售武器的办法作为对其他国家政府施加压力和干涉其他国家内政的手段的政策；要求联合国保证非洲国家在有关裁军问题的所有国际机构中有公平的代表权；对于巴勒斯坦问题、西南非洲问题和那些成为扰乱世界和平和安全的因素的类似问题深感不安，并且要求谋求这些问题的公正解决办法。
