Title: American Celebrities Issue Statement Demanding Government End Nuclear Testing
Author:
Date: 1958-04-13
Page: 3

Xinhua News Agency, New York, April 12th: Forty-seven prominent Americans issued a statement in the "New York Times" on the 11th, demanding that the United States halt nuclear testing.

The statement said that the Soviet Union had already announced a halt to nuclear testing, and "if we do not change our position on this issue, it will easily turn this statement into a moral disaster for the United States."

The statement also opposed the United States supplying other countries with atomic weapons, believing that this would "increase the possibility of some military generals launching an atomic war" by pressing the fuse worldwide. The statement said that the bombing of a Tunisian village by French officers using American-made aircraft has already proven this point.

Signatories to the statement included Norman Cousins, chairman of the National Committee for a Sane Nuclear Policy, playwright Oscar Hammerstein II, writers Lewis Mumford, Norman Thomas, James Warburg, New York City Councilman Stanley Isaacs, scientist Linus Pauling, and other figures from the scientific and religious communities.

### 美国名流发表声明要求政府停止核试验

1958-04-13
第3版()
专栏：

    美国名流发表声明要求政府停止核试验
    据新华社12日讯　纽约消息：四十七个著名的美国人士11日在“纽约时报”发表声明，要求美国停止核试验。
    声明说，苏联已经宣布停止核试验，“我们在这个问题上不改变立场，就会很容易地使这个声明变成美国的道义灾难”。
    声明还反对美国供给其他国家原子武器，认为这会在全世界按下导火线，“增加某些军事将领发动原子战争的可能性”。声明说，法国军官用美制飞机轰炸突尼斯村庄一事已经证明了这一点。
    在这个声明上签名的有“争取健全的核政策全国委员会”主席诺曼·卡曾斯、剧作家奥斯卡·哈默斯坦第二、作家刘易斯·穆福特、诺曼·托马斯、詹姆斯·华伯、纽约市议会议员斯坦莱·艾萨克斯、科学家莱纳斯·保林以及其他科学界和宗教界人士。
