Title: Humphrey Criticizes Government Policy, Advocates US Halt to Nuclear Testing
Author:
Date: 1958-04-08
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, 7th Dispatch from Washington: Democratic Senator Kefauver said in a televised interview on the 6th that the United States had "lost most of the world's respect" because the Soviet Union, rather than the United States, was the first to halt nuclear weapons testing, a situation that made him "sad." He said that in the eyes of world public opinion, "the Soviet Union has taken the first major step to prevent the (world) from falling into destruction." Kefauver advocated that the United States announce a halt to nuclear testing after completing a series of scheduled nuclear weapons tests in April.

On the same day, Democratic Senator Humphrey, Chairman of the Senate Disarmament Subcommittee, also delivered a speech expressing unease about the unfavorable position of the United States in world public opinion after the Soviet Union announced a halt to nuclear testing. He criticized the government for "lacking a policy" on the issue of nuclear testing, merely standing "paralyzed" on the sidelines, watching the Soviet Union put forward one new proposal after another. Humphrey advocated that the United States accept the Soviet proposal to halt nuclear testing, provided that international "inspection" is insisted upon.

### 汉弗莱批评政府的政策  主张美国停止核试验

1958-04-08
第5版()
专栏：

    汉弗莱批评政府的政策
    主张美国停止核试验
    新华社7日讯　华盛顿消息：美国民主党参议员克弗维尔6日在电视谈话中说，由于停止核武器试验的首先是苏联而不是美国，“美国已经丧失了世界上大部分的尊敬”，这种情况使他感到“悲哀”。他说，在世界公众看来，“苏联为了阻止（世界）陷入毁灭而采取了第一个重大的步骤”。克弗维尔主张美国在完成4月间预定的一系列核武器试验以后宣布停止核试验。
    同一天，美国民主党参议员、参院裁军小组委员会主席汉弗莱也发表谈话，对苏联宣布停止核试验以后美国在世界舆论前的不利地位表示不安。他批评政府在核试验问题上“缺乏政策”，只是“瘫痪地”站在一边，眼看苏联提出一个又一个新建议。汉弗莱主张美国在坚持要有国际“视察”的条件下接受苏联关于停止核试验的建议。
