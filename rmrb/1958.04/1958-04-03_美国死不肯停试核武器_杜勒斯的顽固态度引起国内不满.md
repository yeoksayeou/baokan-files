Title: The United States Stubbornly Refuses to Stop Nuclear Testing; Dulles' Obstinate Attitude Arouses Domestic Dissatisfaction
Author:
Date: 1958-04-03
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, April 2nd dispatch from Washington: U.S. Secretary of State Dulles stated at a press conference on April 1st that the United States is still determined to resume nuclear weapons testing in the latter half of this month.

Dulles revealed this stubborn attitude of the United States while discussing the Soviet Union's decision to unilaterally cease testing atomic and hydrogen weapons. He revealed that he, Eisenhower, and relevant senior officials had recently held a meeting to discuss whether the United States should "get ahead of the Soviet Union" and announce a "temporary cessation of testing." The conclusion of the discussion was that the United States could not do this "fundamentally inappropriate thing."

In his speech, Dulles tried to downplay the significance and impact of the Soviet Union's decision, insisting that the Soviet Union's decision "essentially contains nothing new." However, Dulles had to admit that the Soviet action "will have considerable influence" and puts the United States in a passive position. He self-deprecatingly said, "We are intentionally accepting this propaganda attack."

Xinhua News Agency, April 2nd dispatch from Washington: U.S. Senate Republican Leader Knowland revealed that President Eisenhower told Republican leaders yesterday that the United States intends to adhere to its nuclear arms race policy.

Knowland told reporters on April 1st that Eisenhower made the above statement at a meeting of Republican leaders to study the Soviet Union's announcement to cease nuclear weapons testing. Knowland also said that he believed the President meant that the United States intends to continue implementing its plan to test hydrogen weapons in the Pacific this summer.

On the same day, Senator Humphrey, Chairman of the Senate Disarmament Subcommittee and a Democrat, condemned the government's rigid attitude on the issue of ceasing nuclear weapons testing in the Senate. He particularly criticized Dulles' remarks at a press conference on April 1st regarding the Soviet Union's decision, calling Dulles' words "disgraceful." He said that the Soviet Union's decision has "exposed the United States to the world." President Eisenhower should now agree to a temporary cessation of nuclear weapons testing.

TASS, New York, April 1st dispatch: According to "New York Times" correspondent in Washington, Kenworthy, people in the U.S. capital widely believe that the U.S. State Department's statement on March 31 regarding the Soviet Union's unilateral cessation of nuclear weapons testing cannot "dilute the impression created by the sensational Soviet statement."

Kenworthy continued: "People here also see that while Moscow is announcing a cessation of testing, the U.S. President is inviting representatives from other countries to observe U.S. tests."

### 美国死不肯停试核武器  杜勒斯的顽固态度引起国内不满

1958-04-03
第5版()
专栏：

    美国死不肯停试核武器
    杜勒斯的顽固态度引起国内不满
    据新华社2日讯　华盛顿消息：美国国务卿杜勒斯4月1日在记者招待会上说，美国仍然决定在本月下半月重新进行核武器试验。
    杜勒斯是在谈到苏联已经决定单方面停止试验原子武器和氢武器时表明美国的这个顽固态度的。他透露，他和艾森豪威尔以及有关的主要高级官员不久以前曾开过一次会，讨论美国是否要“抢在苏联前头”，宣布“停止试验一个时候”。讨论结果是，美国不能做这件“基本上不妥当的事”。
    杜勒斯在谈话中力图冲淡苏联这个决定的重大意义和影响，硬说苏联的决定“实质上没有什么新的东西”。但是，杜勒斯不得不承认，苏联的行动是“会有相当影响的”，它使美国处于被动境地。他自我解嘲说，“我们有意接受这个宣传攻击”。
    新华社2日讯　华盛顿消息：美国参议院共和党领袖诺兰透露，艾森豪威尔总统昨天曾经对共和党的领袖们表示，美国打算坚持它的核军备竞赛政策。
    诺兰在1日告诉记者，艾森豪威尔当天在共和党领袖们集会研究苏联宣布停止试验核武器的声明时，作了上述表示。诺兰还说，他认为总统的意思是说，美国打算继续执行它的计划，在今年夏天在太平洋试验氢武器。
    同一天，参议院裁军小组委员会主席、民主党人汉弗来在参议院谴责政府在停止试验核武器问题上采取僵硬的态度。他特别抨击杜勒斯1日在记者招待会上谈到苏联的决定的那番话，说杜勒斯的话是“可耻的”。他说，苏联的决定已经使美国“暴露在全世界面前”。目前艾森豪威尔总统应该同意暂时停止试验核武器。
    据新华社2日讯　塔斯社纽约1日讯：据“纽约时报”驻华盛顿记者肯华赛报道，美国首都人士广泛认为，美国国务院3月31日就苏联单方面停止试验核武器一事所作的声明不能“冲淡耸人听闻的苏联声明所产生的印象”。
    肯华赛接着说：“这里的人还看到，正当莫斯科宣布停止试验的时候，美国总统却邀请其他国家派代表来观察美国所进行的试验。”
