Title: Two Solar Eclipses and Two Lunar Eclipses in 1958
Author:
Date: 1958-04-10
Page: 7

    1958 will have two solar eclipses, one on April 19th and the other on October 12th to 13th. Both solar eclipses are central eclipses, meaning that the axis of the moon's shadow cone will enter the Earth's surface during these two solar eclipses. The first one occurs immediately after the moon passes the apogee, so the apex of the lunar shadow is still 20,000 kilometers away from the Earth's surface, thus forming an annular eclipse. The second one occurs a few hours before the moon passes the perigee, so the length of the lunar shadow exceeds the distance from the moon to the ground by 10,000 kilometers, thus forming a total eclipse.
    The annular solar eclipse on April 19th will begin in the western Indian Ocean at 9:33 AM Beijing time (the following times are the same). After bypassing southern India, the eclipse path will cross Vietnam, advance along the southern coast of China and Japan, and end in the northern Pacific Ocean at 13:20. In eastern Siberia and the Central Asian republics of the Soviet Union, a partial eclipse will be visible. In the southern part of Hainan Island, northern Taiwan, and parts of the coastal areas of Guangdong and Fujian, the annular eclipse will be visible, and the partial eclipse will be visible throughout the country.
    The second total eclipse will not be visible in China.
    There will also be two lunar eclipses in 1958, the first being a partial lunar eclipse on May 3rd, and the second being a penumbral lunar eclipse on December 27th.
    At 18:10 on May 3rd, the moon will begin to enter the Earth's penumbra. The greatest eclipse will occur at 20:13. The partial lunar eclipse will continue for nearly half an hour. At 22:16, the moon will leave the penumbra.
    The lunar eclipse will be visible in the Pacific Ocean and nearby areas (eastern Asia and the Indian Ocean, the Antarctic region, and the western border of North America). It will be visible throughout China except for the western regions.
    The second lunar eclipse will be a penumbral eclipse, meaning that a partial solar eclipse will occur on the moon. The Earth's penumbra will cover the moon from 21:16 to 1:39 the next morning. During this period, 80% of the moon's apparent diameter will be located in the Earth's penumbra.
    This lunar eclipse will be visible throughout the Soviet Union, and almost throughout Europe (except for the western border), Asia, Australia, the Arctic region, and northwestern North America. All regions of China will be able to see it, but this kind of penumbral eclipse is only theoretical and cannot be seen with the naked eye. (Science)

### 1958年有两次日食两次月食

1958-04-10
第7版()
专栏：

    1958年有两次日食两次月食
    本报讯　1958年将有两次日食，一次在4月19日，另一次在10月12到13日。两次日食都是中心食，即在这两次的日食中月影锥的轴都要进入地球表面。第一次是在月亮通过远地点后立即发生，所以月影的顶点距地球表面还有二万公里，这样就形成环食。第二次是在月亮通过近地点前数小时发生，所以月影长度超过月亮到地面的距离一万公里，这样就形成全食。
    4月19日的日环食将在印度洋西部于北京时间（以下同此）上午九时三十三分开始。食带绕过印度斯坦南部后，将穿过越南，沿中国和日本的南部沿海一带前进，并将在十三时二十分在太平洋北部终了。在苏联西伯利亚东部和中亚细亚各加盟共和国都将能看到这次日食的偏食。在中国海南岛南部、台湾北部和广东、福建沿海的一部分地区，可以看到环食，全国都可以看到偏食。
    第二次的全食，中国看不见。
    1958年还将有两次月食，第一次是5月3日的月偏食，第二次是12月27日的半影食。
    5月3日十八时十分月亮开始进入地球的半阴影中。二十时十三分食甚。月偏食将继续近半小时。二十二时十六分月亮离开半影。
    在太平洋和附近地区（亚洲和印度洋东部、南极地带以及北美洲的西部边境），将能看到这次月食。中国除西部外都能看到。
    第二次月食将为半影食，即在月亮上将发生日偏食。地球半影将从二十一时十六分到第二天早上一时三十九分遮住月亮。在这一段时间内，月亮视直径的80％将位于地球的半阴影中。
    在苏联的全境内都将可以看到这次月食。并且几乎在欧洲全部（西部边境除外）、亚洲、澳洲、北极地方以及北美西北部也将可以看到这次月食。中国全部地区都能看见，不过这种半影食只是理论上的，肉眼不能看见。（科）
