Title: Macmillan Deeply Concerned About the Prospects of the Capitalist World, Worried About the Coming of Economic Depression and Political Turmoil. British Treasury White Paper Shows Military Spending is the Biggest Burden.
Author:
Date: 1958-04-02
Page: 6
　
　　Xinhua News Agency, April 1st dispatch from London: British Prime Minister Macmillan, speaking at a rally in Halifax, England on March 31st, expressed serious unease when discussing the prospects of an economic recession in the capitalist world. He said that if the current economic recession develops and "falls into a world economic depression of the scale we experienced thirty years ago," it would cause "large-scale unemployment, poverty, and suffering." He expressed extreme concern about the consequences of the resulting political turmoil in the capitalist world.

　　When discussing the impact of this economic recession on Britain, Macmillan pointed out that many responsible people in Britain believe that Britain will face the problems of deflation, falling prices, and unemployment this year. He hinted that the British official policy of tightening money supply to deal with inflation may need to be changed. He said: "We must be careful not to worry too much about the inflation problem of the past thirteen years, so that deflation may quietly invade us." He also said: "We must steer a middle course between the dangerous reefs of inflation and the treacherous shoals of deflation."

　　Xinhua News Agency, April 1st dispatch from TASS, London, April 1st: The British government has published a white paper on the national income and expenditure of the United Kingdom from 1952 to 1957. The white paper shows that a large part of British expenditure is related to war.

　　Calculated at current prices, Britain's national income in 1957 was £17.418 billion, an increase of £961 million over 1956. However, according to financial observers here, the 5.7% growth rate this year was mainly due to rising prices. For example, British retail prices rose by 4.6% from December 11, 1956 to December 10, 1957.

　　In 1957, the profits of British business owners further increased. The total profits of all enterprises this year reached £4.483 billion, an increase of £186 million over 1956.

　　As in the past few years, the largest item of national expenditure in 1957 was military expenditure, accounting for 27.7% of the total national budget expenditure. Britain's second largest expenditure was the interest on the national debt. Expenditure in this area in 1957 was £707 million, accounting for 12.8% of the total budget expenditure.

### 麦克米伦对资本主义世界前景胆战心惊  担忧经济萧条和政治动乱来临  英财政白皮书表明军费是最大包袱

1958-04-02
第6版()
专栏：

    麦克米伦对资本主义世界前景胆战心惊
    担忧经济萧条和政治动乱来临
    英财政白皮书表明军费是最大包袱
    据新华社1日讯　伦敦消息：英国首相麦克米伦3月31日在英格兰哈利法克斯的一次集会上发表演说，在谈到资本主义世界经济衰退的前景时，他表示严重的不安。他说，如果目前的经济衰退发展下去而“陷于像我们三十年前经历的那种规模的世界经济萧条的话”，这就会引起“大规模的失业、贫穷和痛苦”。他对于将由此而产生的资本主义世界政治动乱的后果表示极为不安。
    麦克米伦在谈到这次经济衰退对英国的影响时指出，英国许多负责人士认为，英国今年将要碰到通货紧缩、价格下跌和失业的问题。他暗示英国官方一个时期来抽紧银根来对付通货膨胀的做法可能需要作一些改变。他说：“我们必须小心，不要对十三年来的通货膨胀问题过于担心，以致通货紧缩可能悄悄地侵袭我们。”他又说：“我们必须在通货膨胀的危险的礁石和通货收缩的险滩之间把住舵走中间的道路。”
    新华社1日讯　据塔斯社伦敦1日讯：英国政府发表了关于1952年至1957年英国国民收入和支出情况的白皮书。白皮书表明，英国开支中有很大一部分同战争有关。
    按现行价格计算，1957年英国的国民收入是一百七十四亿一千八百万英镑，比1956年多九亿六千一百万英镑。但是，据这里的金融观察家们指出，这一年的5.7％的增长率主要是由于物价上涨造成的。例如，英国的零售价格从1956年12月11日到1957年12月10日就增长了4.6％。
    1957年英国企业主的利润进一步增加。这一年全部企业的利润达到四十四亿八千三百万英镑，比1956年增加了一亿八千六百万英镑。
    同过去几年一样，1957年国家支出的最大项目是军费开支，占国家预算总支出的27.7％。英国第二笔最大的开支是偿还国债的利息。1957年在这方面的支出有七亿零七百万英镑，占预算总支出的12.8％。
