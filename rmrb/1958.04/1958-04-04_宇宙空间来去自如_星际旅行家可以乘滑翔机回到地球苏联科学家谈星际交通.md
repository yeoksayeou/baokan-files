Title: Freely Coming and Going in Outer Space: Interstellar Travelers Can Return to Earth by Glider – Soviet Scientists Discuss Interstellar Transportation
Author:
Date: 1958-04-04
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, Moscow, 3rd -- When spacecraft on artificial satellites or interstellar spaceships return to Earth from outer space, as long as they are piloted correctly, they can land anywhere on Earth, regardless of where they enter the atmosphere. This was stated by Sternfeld, a well-known Soviet expert in interstellar transportation, in an article recently published in "Soviet Russia."

The editors of this newspaper pointed out in an editor's note that Soviet scientists have been able to manufacture spacecraft that can reach outer space, but before interstellar flight can be realized, it is necessary to solve a very important problem - finding a way for spacecraft to return to Earth from space. Sternfeld's article discusses this issue.

The author points out that the entire artificial satellite or interstellar spaceship does not need to land; it is only necessary to bring back the recorded instruments and personnel. Using a specialized landing glider seems to be the best solution. The glider does not need any fuel for landing; it can use the resistance of the atmosphere to achieve deceleration. When the glider is gradually decelerating, the increase in the shell temperature is not significant and it will not burn up like a meteorite. This type of glider should have an ideal streamlined shape. The glider first uses a small jet engine to detach from the satellite, and then travels a long, gently sloped path along a semi-elliptical orbit in the thin atmosphere, gradually reducing speed. The descent process may take several hours. Finally, when the speed reaches about 100 kilometers per hour, it can land like an ordinary glider.

If the circular orbit or the apogee of the elliptical orbit of the satellite or device is far from the ground, then the glider must orbit the Earth several times to reduce its speed.

In addition, in the final stage of landing, a supersonic aircraft can also intercept the glider and "carry" the glider back to the ground.

### 宇宙空间来去自如  星际旅行家可以乘滑翔机回到地球苏联科学家谈星际交通

1958-04-04
第5版()
专栏：

    宇宙空间来去自如
    星际旅行家可以乘滑翔机回到地球苏联科学家谈星际交通
    新华社莫斯科3日电　在人造卫星或星际飞船上的飞行器械从宇宙空间回到地球上来时，只要驾驶正确，不管这个飞行器械在什么地点进入大气层，都是可以在地球上任何地点着陆的。这是苏联著名星际交通专家斯特恩菲尔德最近在“苏维埃俄罗斯报”上发表的一篇文章说的。
    这家报纸的编者在按语中指出，苏联科学家已能够制造可以达到宇宙空间的飞行器械，但是在实现星际飞行之前，必需解决一项很重要的问题——找出飞行器械从宇宙中回到地球上来的方法。斯特恩菲尔德的文章谈的就是这个问题。
    作者指出，整个人造卫星或星际飞船无需着陆，只要让带有纪录的仪器和人员回来就行了。利用专门的着陆滑翔机看来是最好的办法。滑翔机在着陆时，可以不用任何燃料，只要利用大气的阻力就可以达到减速的目的。滑翔机在缓缓减速的时候，外壳温度的增加是不大的，不会像陨星那样烧掉。这种滑翔机应当具有理想的流线型外形。滑翔机首先利用一个小小喷气推进机来脱离卫星，然后在稀薄大气层中沿着半椭圆形的轨道走上一段很长的坡度极小的路，逐步减低速度。降落过程可能要几小时。最后当速度达到每小时一百公里左右的时候，它就可以像普通滑翔机一样着陆了。
    如果卫星或器械的圆形轨道或椭圆形轨道的远地点离地面很远，那么滑翔机必须围绕地球转上若干圈来降低速度。
    此外，在降落的最后阶段，也可以用超高速飞机截获滑翔机，让超高速飞机“驮着”滑翔机回到地面。
