    Vigorous in Old Age vs. Premature Senility
    Yang Xianzhen
    Today I read a memoir about Marx and Engels written by a comrade of Marx and Engels named Kuno, probably shortly after the October Revolution in 1917. It contained these sentences:
    "I am a contemporary of Marx and Engels who is still alive. Although I am eighty-six years and eight months old, I am still healthy, full of energy, and filled with youthful enthusiasm." (Note 1)
    Bebel's "Trip to London" says:
    "Engels... retained extraordinary work ability until the last moment of his life; when he was seventy years old, he was still learning Romanian,... he was always full of vigor and energy..." (Note 2)
    These are models of veteran revolutionaries; they were truly "vigorous in old age."
    In contrast, some of our teaching staff, some of whom are young, or at most middle-aged, should be in their prime, but already have the air of old men, prematurely senile, and some even yearn for Babaoshan. This is not just "lethargy," it is simply "tomb atmosphere."
    We should dig into the root of this "tomb atmosphere" from the depths of our souls.

(Note 1) See: Recollections of Marx and Engels, page 234.
(Note 2) Ibid., page 245.

### 老当益壮与未老先衰
杨献珍
1958-04-05
第8版()
专栏：

    老当益壮与未老先衰
    杨献珍
    今天读了一个马克思和恩格斯的战友叫做库诺的、大概是在1917年十月革命后不久所写的关于马克思和恩格斯的回忆录，其中有这样几句话：
    “我是还活在世上的马克思和恩格斯的同时代人，虽然我已经八十六岁零八个月，但我仍然身体健康，精神饱满，充满青年的热情。”（注一）
    倍倍尔所写的“伦敦之行”一文中说：
    “恩格斯……直到生命的最后一刻，仍有非凡的工作能力；当他七十高龄的时候，他还学习罗马尼亚文，……他总是朝气蓬勃，精神饱满……。”（注二）
    这是前辈老革命家的典范，他们真是“老当益壮”。
    对照我们的教学工作人员中，有青年，有的至多是中年，应当说是还在壮年时代吧，就已有了老头气，未老先衰，有的甚至向往着八宝山，这不仅是“暮气”，简直是有了“墓气”了。
    应当从灵魂深处挖掘一下这种“墓气”的根子。（注一）见：回忆马克思恩格斯第234页。（注二）同上书第245页。
