Title: Nearly 10,000 Workers in Hiroshima, Japan Refuse to Load American Munitions for Chiang Kai-shek Gang

Author:

Date: 1958-10-01
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, Tokyo, 30th – News: More than 9,500 Japanese workers employed by the U.S. military in Hiroshima, Japan, refused to load munitions supplied by the United States to the Chiang Kai-shek clique in Taiwan on the evening of the 29th.

The head of the All-Japan Garrison Workers' Union told reporters on the evening of the 29th, after investigating the situation, that the union supported the workers' action because "Japanese workers are unwilling to be a party to hostile acts in the Taiwan area."

These union leaders also said that the packages containing the munitions were labeled "U.S. - Nationalist China Friendship Package".

### 日本广岛近万工人  拒绝装运美国给蒋帮军火

1958-10-01
第6版()
专栏：

    日本广岛近万工人
    拒绝装运美国给蒋帮军火
    新华社30日讯　东京消息：日本广岛的九千五百多名受雇于驻日美军的日本工人，29日晚上拒绝装运美国供应台湾蒋介石集团军队的军火。
    全日本驻军工会的负责人29日晚上在调查这一情况后对新闻记者说，工会支持工人们的这一行动，因为“日本工人不愿意成为参加台湾地区敌对行为的一方。”
    这些工会领袖还说，那些装着军火的包件上写有“美国——国民党中国友好包”字样。
