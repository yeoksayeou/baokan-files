Title: Electricity Generation Per Capita: East Germany Surpasses All Western European Countries
Author:
Date: 1958-10-19
Page: 4

    Electricity Generation Per Capita
    East Germany Surpasses All Western European Countries

    The German Democratic Republic's per capita electricity generation currently exceeds that of all Western European countries, including West Germany.

    In the past four years, East Germany has built four new power stations and expanded several others. By the end of this year, the country's electricity generation will increase to 34 billion kilowatt-hours, which is more than 30% higher than in 1954.

    The vigorous development of East Germany's industry requires a further increase in electricity generation. According to the plan, by 1965, East Germany's per capita electricity generation will reach 3,500 kilowatt-hours. During the third five-year (1961-1965) plan period, two of the world's largest brown coal power stations will be newly built in the country. These two power stations will produce approximately 15 billion kilowatt-hours of electricity annually, equivalent to half of East Germany's total electricity generation in 1957.

### 按人口平均计算的发电量  民主德国超过所有西欧国家

1958-10-19
第4版()
专栏：

    按人口平均计算的发电量
    民主德国超过所有西欧国家
    目前德意志民主共和国按人口计算的发电量超过了西欧所有国家，包括西德在内。
    在过去四年中，民主德国已经建造了四个新的发电站，还扩大了另外好几个发电站。到今年年底全国的发电量将增加到三百四十亿度，即比1954年增加30％以上。
    民主德国工业的蓬勃发展需要进一步增加发电量。根据计划，到1965年，民主德国按人口计算的发电量将达到三千五百度。在第三个五年（1961——1965年）计划期间，国内将新建两个世界上最大的褐煤发电站。这两个发电站每年将生产大约一百五十亿度的电力，相当于民主德国1957年发电总量的一半。
