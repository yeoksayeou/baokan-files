Title: Central Committee of the Polish United Workers' Party Directs Seven-Year Struggle Goals: Industrial Production Level to Reach Ten Times Pre-Liberation; Per Capita Output of Basic Products: Poland Already Higher Than World Average Production Level
Author:
Date: 1958-10-28
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, Warsaw, October 25th: According to reports in the local newspapers, the Central Committee of the Polish United Workers' Party stipulated in an instruction that by 1965, Poland's overall industrial production level would increase to approximately ten times that of pre-liberation 1938. During the second Five-Year Plan (1961-1965), industrial output will increase by about 50%, and agriculture will increase by 20%.

The 12th Plenary Session of the Central Committee of the Polish United Workers' Party recently adopted instructions on Poland's development during the period of 1959-1965.

The instructions pointed out that, according to estimates, the absolute output increase of major industrial products by 1965 would be as follows: coal 111.5 million tons, electricity 41.5 billion kilowatt-hours, steel 8.8 million tons, machine tools 25,000 units, cotton fabrics 790 million meters, and so on.

When talking about the achievements of national economic construction in Poland since liberation, the instructions pointed out that in the fourteen years of the people's rule, Poland has taken a big step towards reaching the level of the most economically developed countries in Europe. In terms of per capita output of basic products, it is definitely higher than the world average production level.

In 1957, Poland's per capita steel output was 187 kilograms, while the world average output was 105 kilograms; electricity was 748 kilowatt-hours, while the world output was 640 kilowatt-hours; hard coal was 3,428 kilograms, while the world output was 1,404 kilograms; cotton yarn, wool fiber and synthetic fiber were 8.4 kilograms, while the world output was 4.4 kilograms.

The instructions said that the current Five-Year Plan (1956-1960) is, on the whole, being implemented smoothly. From 1956 to 1958, the average annual growth rate of socialist industry reached about 9.5%. The average grain yield per hectare in the three years was 50 kilograms more than the planned output of the Five-Year Plan, reaching 1,450 kilograms.

While estimating the great achievements of People's Poland in the past fourteen years, the instructions also took into account the shortcomings, difficulties and imbalances in the development of the national economy. The instructions pointed out that due to the overcoming of economic difficulties and Poland's domestic political stability, and due to closer cooperation with the Soviet Union and other socialist countries, Poland now possesses the necessary domestic and foreign conditions for further smooth construction of socialism.

Completing the current Five-Year Plan and the plan for developing the national economy from 1961 to 1965 will be a major step forward on this great road.

The instructions defined the basic direction of the 1961-1965 economic plan. It stipulates that in determining the rate of economic development and the proportion of national income creation and distribution, attention must be paid to continuing to implement national socialist industrialization and improve national productivity, so as to improve the living standards of residents and shorten the distance between Poland and the most economically advanced European countries.

In the second Five-Year Plan (1961-1965), industrial output will increase by about 50% (that is, 80% more than in 1958); agricultural output will increase by 20% (that is, about 30% more than in 1958); and industrial labor productivity will increase by about 40%.

The instructions pointed out that during the second Five-Year Plan, the number of employed people in industry will increase by 300,000. The average real wages of workers will increase by 23% to 25% (that is, 33% to 35% higher than in 1958), and the percentage increase in the real income of farmers will be roughly the same.

### 波兰统一工人党中央指示七年奋斗目标  工业生产水平要达到解放前十倍  基本产品产量按人口平均计算  波兰已高于世界产量平均水平

1958-10-28
第5版()
专栏：

    波兰统一工人党中央指示七年奋斗目标
    工业生产水平要达到解放前十倍
    基本产品产量按人口平均计算
    波兰已高于世界产量平均水平
    新华社华沙25日电　据此间报纸报道：波兰统一工人党中央委员会在一项指示中规定，到1965年波兰整个工业生产水平将提高到解放前1938年的十倍左右。在第二个五年计划（1961—1965年）期间，工业产量将增加50％左右，农业将增加20％。
    波兰统一工人党中央委员会最近举行的第十二次全体会议，曾经通过了关于波兰在1959—1965年期间发展的指示。
    指示指出，根据估计，到1965年主要工业产品的绝对产量增加的情况如下：煤一亿一千一百五十万吨，电力四百一十五亿度，钢八百八十万吨，工作母机二万五千台，棉织品七亿九千万公尺，等等。
    在谈到波兰解放以来国民经济建设的成就时，指示指出，在人民执政的十四年中，波兰已经朝着达到欧洲经济最高度发展的国家的水平大大跨了一步，在基本产品产量按人口平均计算方面，已肯定地高于世界产量的平均水平。
    在1957年，波兰钢产量每人平均为一百八十七公斤，而世界平均产量为一百零五公斤；电力为七百四十八度，而世界产量为六百四十度；硬煤为三千四百二十八公斤，而世界产量为一千四百零四公斤；棉纱、羊毛纤维和合成纤维为八点四公斤，而世界产量为四点四公斤。
    指示说，目前的五年计划，（1956—1960年），整个说来，执行得很顺利。在1956年到1958年，社会主义工业每年生产增长率平均达到9.5％左右。三年中的谷物每公顷平均产量比五年计划的计划产量多五十公斤，即达到一千四百五十公斤。
    指示在估计人民波兰十四年来的巨大成就的同时，还考虑到国民经济发展中所产生的缺点、困难和不平衡的现象。指示指出，由于克服了经济困难和波兰国内的政治稳定，由于同苏联和其它社会主义国家的更紧密的合作，目前波兰已具备了进一步顺利地建设社会主义的必要的国内外条件。
    完成当前的五年计划和1961年到1965年的发展国民经济计划，将是在这条大路上向前迈进的一大步。
    指示确定了1961—1965年经济计划的基本方向。它规定，在确定经济发展速度以及国民收入的创造和分配的比例方面，必须注意继续实行国家社会主义工业化和提高国家生产力，以便提高居民的生活水平和缩短波兰同经济上最先进的欧洲国家之间的距离。
    在第二个五年计划（1961—1965年）中，工业产量将增加50％左右（即比1958年增加80％）；农业产量将增加20％（即比1958年增加30％左右）；工业劳动生产率将增加40％左右。
    指示指出，在第二个五年计划中，工业中的就业人数将增加三十万人。工人的平均实际工资将提高23％到25％（即比1958年提高33％到35％），农民的实际收入增加的百分比大体上也是这样。
