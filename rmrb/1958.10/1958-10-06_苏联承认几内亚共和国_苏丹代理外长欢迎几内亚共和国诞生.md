Title: Soviet Union Recognizes the Republic of Guinea; Sudanese Acting Foreign Minister Welcomes the Birth of the Republic of Guinea
Author: 
Date: 1958-10-06
Page: 5

    Soviet Union Recognizes the Republic of Guinea
    Sudanese Acting Foreign Minister Welcomes the Birth of the Republic of Guinea
    Xinhua News Agency, October 5th: TASS, Moscow, October 4th: Chairman of the Presidium of the Supreme Soviet of the USSR, Voroshilov, sent a telegram to the Prime Minister of the Republic of Guinea, Sékou Touré, announcing that the Soviet government officially recognizes the Republic of Guinea and is willing to establish diplomatic relations with it and exchange diplomatic representatives. In his telegram, Voroshilov, on behalf of the Soviet people, wished the people of Guinea success in building their own independent and democratic state.
    Prior to this, Sékou Touré had sent a telegram to Voroshilov, suggesting the establishment of diplomatic relations between the Soviet Union and Guinea.
    Xinhua News Agency, Cairo, October 4th: According to a Middle East News Agency report from Khartoum, Sudanese Acting Foreign Minister Abdalla Abdel Rahman Nugudalla said on October 4th that Sudan welcomes the birth of the Republic of Guinea and wishes it every success.
    He said that Sudan is pleased to see any country achieve independence and freedom, as this will strengthen the power of small countries and multiply the strength of the cause of peace and liberation.
    Nugudalla said that the Sudanese Embassy in Paris has been asked to provide the Ministry of Foreign Affairs with information about this new republic. He said that the matter will be submitted to the Council of Ministers immediately for necessary measures to be taken to grant recognition.
    Xinhua News Agency, Sofia, October 4th: The Bulgarian newspapers "Workers' Cause," "Fatherland Front," and others published articles congratulating Guinea on its declaration of independence.
    An article published in "Workers' Cause" on October 3rd pointed out that Guinea's independence is further proof of the upsurge in the African national liberation movement. Progressive public opinion around the world congratulates the birth of this new independent and sovereign country in Africa and hopes that it will achieve success on the road of freedom and peaceful development. ### 苏联承认几内亚共和国  苏丹代理外长欢迎几内亚共和国诞生

1958-10-06
第5版()
专栏：

    苏联承认几内亚共和国
    苏丹代理外长欢迎几内亚共和国诞生
    新华社5日讯　据塔斯社莫斯科4日讯：苏联最高苏维埃主席团主席伏罗希洛夫打电报给几内亚共和国总理塞古·杜尔，宣布苏联政府正式承认几内亚共和国，愿意同它建立外交关系并互派外交代表。伏罗希洛夫在电报中代表苏联人民祝几内亚人民在建设自己的自主的民主国家的事业中获得成功。
    在这以前，塞古·杜尔曾经打电报给伏罗希洛夫，建议苏联和几内亚两国之间建立外交关系。
    新华社开罗4日电　据中东通讯社喀土穆消息，苏丹代理外交部长阿卜杜拉·阿卜杜勒·拉赫曼·努古达拉4日说，苏丹欢迎几内亚共和国的诞生，并且祝它一切成功。
    他说，苏丹很高兴看到任何国家取得独立和自由，因为这将加强各小国的力量，并且使和平和解放事业的力量倍增。
    努古达拉说，已经要求苏丹驻巴黎大使馆向外交部提供关于这个新的共和国的消息。他说，这个问题将马上提交部长会议，为给予承认采取必要的措施。
    新华社索非亚4日电　保加利亚“工人事业报”、“祖国阵线报”和其他一些报纸发表文章，祝贺几内亚宣布独立。
    “工人事业报”3日发表的一篇文章指出，几内亚的独立是非洲民族解放运动高涨的又一证明。全世界进步舆论都祝贺非洲这个新的独立自主的国家的诞生，并且希望它在自由和和平发展的道路上取得成就。（附图片）
    几内亚于10月2日宣布独立，成立几内亚共和国。左为新总理塞古·杜尔在科纳克里宣布几内亚共和国新政府成立。右为几内亚制宪议会议长阿迪洛·塞福拉耶。　新华社稿
