    Between the Two Congresses of the CPSU
    Since the 20th Congress of the Communist Party of the Soviet Union, the Soviet economy has continued to develop steadily and rapidly along a prosperous path. Industrial production in 1957 increased by 22% compared to 1955. The growth achieved in these two years alone far exceeded the total annual output of pre-revolutionary Russia: steel was about 1.5 times, coal 2.5 times, oil about 4 times, cement more than 4 times, and electricity 20 times. In 1957, the industrial products produced in the Soviet Union every eleven days were equivalent to the entire year's production in 1913.
    This year, Soviet industry has overfulfilled its plan for the first three quarters. In the first half of 1958, coal mined exceeded the total mined in 1949; steel production (including pig iron and steel) exceeded the output of 1950; electricity exceeded the output of 1951; tractors were almost equivalent to the output of 1953; electric locomotives greatly exceeded the output of 1954; oil extraction exceeded the extraction of 1953; and natural gas exceeded the output of 1956.
    While industry in the Soviet Union and other socialist countries is constantly growing, the economy of the capitalist world is in a state of deepening crisis. In the United States, industrial production in 1956 increased by only 3% compared to 1955, industrial production stagnated in 1957, and industrial production fell by 11% in the first quarter of 1958. In Western European capitalist countries, industrial production in 1956 increased by 5% compared to 1955, increased by 5% in 1957 compared to 1956, and increased by 2% in the first quarter of 1958 compared to the same period in 1957. But behind these figures also hide the extremely unbalanced development of some countries. In the United Kingdom, industrial production growth in the first quarter of 1958 was only 1%, in Belgium it fell by 6%, in the Netherlands by 4%, and in Denmark by 2%.
    In the Soviet Union, not only industry is growing rapidly, but also agriculture: from 1954 to 1957, the total agricultural output of the Soviet Union increased by an average of 7.1% per year, while the United States increased by only 1.1%.

### 在苏共两次代表大会之间

1958-10-16
第5版()
专栏：资料

    在苏共两次代表大会之间
    在苏联共产党第二十次代表大会以后，苏联的经济继续稳定地和迅速地走着一条欣欣向荣的道路。1957年的工业生产比1955年增加了22％。仅仅在这两年中所获得的增长额就大大超过了革命前俄国一年的总产量：钢约为一点五倍，煤为二点五倍，石油约为四倍，水泥为四倍以上，电力为二十倍。在1957年，每十一天在苏联生产的工业品相等于1913年全年的生产量。
    今年苏联工业已超额完成了前三季度的计划。1958年上半年，开采的煤超过了1949年全年的开采量；钢的生产量（包括生铁和钢材）超过了1950年的产量；电力超过1951年的产量；拖拉机几乎相等于1953年的产量；电力机车大大超过了1954年的产量；石油开采量超过1953年的开采量；天然瓦斯超过1956年的产量。
    在苏联和其他社会主义国家的工业不断增长的同时，资本主义世界的经济却处于一种危机不断加深的情况之中。在美国，1956年的工业生产比1955年仅只增加了3％，1957年工业生产停滞不前，1958年第一季度，工业生产下降了11％。在西欧资本主义国家，1956年的工业生产比1955年增长5％，1957年比1956年增长5％，1958年第一季度比1957年同期增长了2％。但是这些数字的背后还掩藏着某些国家发展极不平衡的现象。在英国，1958年第一季度的工业生产增长额仅是1％，在比利时下降了6％，在荷兰下降了4％，丹麦下降了2％。
    在苏联，迅速增长的不仅是工业，而且还有农业：在1954年——1957年苏联的农业总产量平均每年增加7.1％，而美国仅只增加1.1％。
