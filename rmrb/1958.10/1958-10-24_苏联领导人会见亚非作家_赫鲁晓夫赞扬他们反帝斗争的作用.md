Title: Soviet Leaders Meet with Asian and African Writers, Khrushchev Praises Their Role in the Anti-Imperialist Struggle
Author:
Date: 1958-10-24
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, Moscow, October 23rd -- TASS reports: Khrushchev and other Soviet leaders met on the evening of the 22nd in the Great Kremlin Palace with a large group of Asian and African writers who came to Moscow for a visit after attending the Asian and African Writers' Conference.

The Soviet government held a grand reception for these writers. Many Soviet writers, artists, actors, and musicians also attended the reception.

Khrushchev, Chairman of the Soviet Council of Ministers, spoke at the meeting. He welcomed the literary figures from Asian and African countries who came to visit the Soviet capital, Moscow, on behalf of the Soviet government.

Khrushchev emphasized that all progressive people in the world know and highly appreciate the positive role that writers play in the upsurge of national awakening of Asian and African people in their struggle to free themselves from the shackles of colonialism and the oppression of imperialism. Khrushchev said that all those who truly sympathize with the fate of your people cannot but feel happy about the strength and broad representation of the Asian and African Writers' Conference held in Tashkent.

The Soviet head of government particularly pointed out that the Tashkent Conference Declaration is a lofty document that will be supported by all those who believe in the victory of human reason over the forces of darkness, who demand mutual understanding, and who hope for world peace.

Khrushchev wished every participant of the Tashkent Conference success in their writing, to enrich world culture, and to make due contributions to the common efforts of people of all countries in striving for world peace.

Chinese writer Mao Dun, Indian writer Anand, Senegalese writer Diop, head of the UAE writers' delegation Ahmed, Sudanese writer Sabir, and head of the Japanese delegation Ito Sei all spoke at the reception. In their speeches, they pointed out the great significance of the Asian and African Writers' Conference. (Attached picture)

### 苏联领导人会见亚非作家  赫鲁晓夫赞扬他们反帝斗争的作用

1958-10-24
第6版()
专栏：

    苏联领导人会见亚非作家
    赫鲁晓夫赞扬他们反帝斗争的作用
    新华社23日讯　据塔斯社莫斯科讯：赫鲁晓夫等苏联领导人22日晚上在大克里姆林宫会见了一大批在参加亚非作家会议后来莫斯科访问的亚非作家。
    苏联政府为这些作家举行了盛大的招待会。苏联许多文学家、美术家、演员、音乐家也参加了招待会。
    苏联部长会议主席赫鲁晓夫在会上讲了话。他代表苏联政府向来苏联首都莫斯科访问的亚非国家文学界人士表示欢迎。
    赫鲁晓夫强调说，世界所有先进人士都知道作家们在亚非人民争取摆脱殖民主义枷锁和帝国主义压迫的民族觉醒高潮中所起的积极作用，并对这种作用给予很高评价。赫鲁晓夫说，凡是真正同情你们人民命运的人，对于在塔什干举行的亚非作家会议的阵容之强大，代表性之广泛，都不能不感到高兴。
    苏联政府首脑特别指出，塔什干会议宣言是一个崇高的文献，凡是相信人类理性战胜黑暗势力、要求互相了解和希望世界和平的人都会拥护这个宣言。
    赫鲁晓夫祝塔什干会议的每个与会者在创作中获得成就，为世界文化增加财富，对争取世界和平的各国人民的共同努力作出应有的贡献。
    中国作家茅盾、印度作家阿南德、塞内加尔文学家迪奥普、阿联作家代表团团长艾哈迈德、苏丹文学家萨比尔、日本代表团团长伊藤整都在招待会上讲了话。他们在讲话中指出亚非作家会议的巨大意义。（附图片）
