Title: Resolution on Cultural Exchange
Author:
Date: 1952-10-14
Page: 2

    Resolution on Cultural Exchange
    We, the representatives attending this Asian and Pacific Regions Peace Conference, come from different countries and have different views, but we are united in our love for peace and culture. We believe that the fear and suspicion that fills many parts of the world today can and must be eliminated through greater understanding. We believe that cultural exchange is one of the best and most noble ways to achieve peaceful coexistence. The cause of peace should be the primary concern of all who love culture.
    The enemies of peace not only restrict the peace movement, prohibit the publication of peace books, and persecute those who work for peace, but they also hinder the free development of culture. They suppress the cultures of various nations and propagate anti-humanitarian ideas. They incite wars and sow seeds of resentment and hatred among nations, races, and religious sects. Science and technology, which should be used to promote human progress, are instead used to produce weapons of mass destruction.
    The rich cultural heritage of the people of the Asian and Pacific regions is not only being threatened by activities preparing for another world war, but is in fact being devastated by wars in Korea, Vietnam, Malaya, and other countries.
    We declare that this aggression against culture should be regarded as serious as war crimes.
    We demand the elimination of all obstacles that prevent the free exchange of healthy and humane culture, and the removal of all prohibitions and restrictions imposed on those who work for the benefit of mankind through art and science.
    We appeal to the people of all countries in the Asian and Pacific regions to rise up, expose and oppose all forms of aggression against culture, and do their utmost to eliminate suspicion and hatred through cultural exchange.
    We propose to strengthen cultural exchange through activities such as: exchanging delegations composed of scientists, technicians, artists, teachers, students, farmers, industrial workers, and craftsmen; celebrating great cultural anniversaries and organizing youth festivals; friendly sports competitions; exchanging works of art, music, drama, folk music and films, children's toys, paintings and reading materials; exchanging book catalogues, literary and scientific books and newspapers and magazines; exchanging scholarships; holding conferences for cultural workers, and translating outstanding works.
    Let us strive for peace on the basis of human dignity, the freedom of all peoples, and the friendship and equality among all nations - qualities we all cherish.
                            October 12, 1952, in Beijing

### 关于文化交流的决议

1952-10-14
第2版()
专栏：

    关于文化交流的决议
    我们参加这次亚洲及太平洋区域和平会议的代表，来自不同的国家，具有不同的见解，但是在对和平与文化的爱好上是团结一致的，我们认为，今天充满在世界上很多地方的恐惧和猜疑，只要彼此间有更多的了解，是可以而且必须被消除的。我们认为文化交流是达到和平共处的一个最好的、最崇高的方法。和平事业应该是一切爱好文化的人士首先关心的事。
    和平的敌人不仅限制和平运动，禁止和平书刊的出版，和迫害为和平工作的人们。他们并且阻挠了文化的自由发展。他们压制各民族的文化并且宣传反人道的思想。他们煽动战争，并在各民族、种族、教派间散播怨毒和仇恨的种子。科学和技术，本来应当是用来推动人类进步的，却被他们用来生产大规模毁灭性的武器。
    亚洲及太平洋区域各国人民的丰富的文化遗产，不但正被准备另一次世界大战的活动所威胁，而且在事实上正被在朝鲜、越南、马来亚以及其他国家的战争所摧残。
    我们宣布：这种对文化的侵略应该被看作是与战争罪行同样的严重。
    我们要求消除一切使健康的、合乎人情的文化不能自由交流的障碍，并且撤除加于那些通过艺术和科学为人类谋福利的人们的一切禁令和限制。
    我们向亚洲及太平洋区域各国人民呼吁：起来揭发并反对一切形式的对文化的侵略，并通过文化交流，尽力来消除猜忌和仇恨。
    我们建议用这些活动来加强文化交流，如：互派由科学家、技术人员、艺术家、教员、学生、农民、产业工人、手工业者等所组成的访问团；庆祝伟大的文化纪念日和组织青年的庆祝节日；体育的友谊比赛；交换艺术品、音乐、戏剧、民族音乐和电影、儿童玩具、绘画和读物；交换图书目录、文学和科学书刊以及报章杂志；交换奖学金；举办文化工作者的会议，翻译优秀作品。
    让我们在人类的尊严、各国人民的自由及各民族间的友爱和平等——这些我们所一致珍视的品质的基础上，争取和平。
                            一九五二年十月十二日于北京
