	International Briefs
	Hungarian Government Delegation Visits Germany at Invitation
	At the invitation of Wilhelm Pieck, President of the German Democratic Republic, and the Republic's government, the Hungarian government delegation arrived in Berlin on October 27. The delegation included Mátyás Rákosi, Chairman of the Council of Ministers of the Hungarian People's Republic (head of the delegation), Ernő Gerő, Minister of State and Chairman of the National Economic Council, and Foreign Minister Károly Kiss. The delegation was accompanied by the head of the diplomatic mission of the German Democratic Republic to Hungary.

	Soviet Chess Players Win Top Five Places in Swedish Competition
	To select five players to participate in next year's international chess competition, a regional chess competition was held in Stockholm, the capital of Sweden, from September 14 to October 21. According to the final results of the competition, Soviet chess players won the top five places with a complete victory. Hungarian and Czechoslovak players won sixth and eleventh places respectively, while American players won thirteenth place and British players won twentieth place.

	Australian Union Representatives Demand Menzies Government Resign
	According to Australian newspaper reports, five hundred representatives from sixteen unions, led by Jim Healy, General Secretary of the Waterside Workers' Federation of Australia, arrived in Canberra on October 15 from Sydney, Newcastle, and other Australian cities. They submitted a petition to Australian Governor-General McKell demanding the resignation of Prime Minister Menzies. The petition was signed by 16,412 people.

	U.S. Assistant Secretary of State Allison Engaged in Activities in Indonesia
	U.S. Assistant Secretary of State for Far Eastern Affairs, John Moore Allison, arrived in Jakarta on October 13. Allison is a member of the U.S. delegation participating in the meeting held in Manila on October 24 to discuss the issue of a "defense system" in the Pacific region. Tanumihardja, Deputy Speaker of the Indonesian Parliament and Chairman of the Christian Party parliamentary group, told an Antara News Agency reporter that if Allison's trip was intended to make Indonesia participate in the so-called Pacific defense system, "his efforts are doomed to failure, because the vast majority of the Indonesian people desire peace."

### 国际简讯

1952-10-30
第4版()
专栏：国际简讯

    国际简讯
    匈牙利政府代表团应邀访德
    匈牙利政府代表团应德意志民主共和国总统皮克和共和国政府的邀请，于十月二十七日抵达柏林。代表团包括匈牙利人民共和国部长会议主席拉科西（代表团团长），国务部长兼国民经济委员会主席格罗，外交部长基斯。代表团由德意志民主共和国驻匈牙利外交使团团长陪同前来。
    苏联象棋手在瑞典比赛荣获前五名
    为选拔五名选手参加明年度国际象棋比赛，区域象棋比赛于九月十四日到十月二十一日在瑞典首都斯德哥尔摩举行。根据比赛的最后结果，苏联象棋手荣获前五名，获得全胜。匈牙利和捷克斯洛伐克选手分别得第六和第十一名，美国选手得第十三名，英国选手得第二十名。
    澳大利亚工会代表要求孟席斯政府辞职
    据澳大利亚报纸消息，以澳大利亚码头工会联合会总书记赫利为首的十六个工会的五百个代表，十月十五日从悉尼、纽卡斯尔和澳大利亚其他城市到达堪培拉。他们向澳大利亚总督麦克尔递送了要求总理孟席斯辞职的请愿书。在请愿书上签名的有一万六千四百一十二人。
    美国助理国务卿艾利逊到印尼活动
    美国负责远东事务的助理国务卿艾利逊于十月十三日抵达雅加达。艾利逊是参加十月二十四日在马尼拉召开的讨论太平洋区域“防御体系”问题的会议的美国代表团团员。印尼国会副议长、基督教党国会议员团主席谭布南对安塔拉通讯社记者说，假如艾利逊此行是为了使印尼参加所谓太平洋防御体系，“他的努力是要遭到失败的，因为绝大多数的印尼人民都希望和平”。
