Title: Preventing the Threat of Another World War, Maintaining Peace, and Strengthening International Security—Vyshinsky's Speech at the Plenary Session of the UN General Assembly on October 18

Author:

Date: 1952-10-21
Page: 1

The Seventh Session of the United Nations General Assembly is commencing its work in a situation where the international agenda is filled with important and complex issues concerning the vital interests of billions of people, entire nations, and countries. At the heart of these issues are peace, peaceful cooperation, and international security. These issues have also held an important place in the work of previous UN General Assemblies, and at each session, the Soviet delegation has consistently and unwaveringly insisted that the Assembly take measures to maintain peace and strengthen cooperation among all peoples.

This session of the UN General Assembly is convened at a time when international relations issues require our special attention, precisely because of the complexity and tension of the current international situation.

In the year since the Sixth Session, it has been proven that the North Atlantic Treaty Organization (NATO) was, and remains, a significant factor in intensifying international tensions and aggravating the international situation. This is because the group harbors aggressive aims, which its organizers and leaders cannot conceal with their rhetoric about so-called defense matters. Within this group, the US ruling circles and prominent figures from the British and French ruling circles who follow US policies, on the one hand, use lies about peace to ingratiate themselves with the people, thereby putting on a facade of peace-loving; on the other hand, they continue to incite a war psychosis, make every effort to maintain a state of tension in their countries, and obstruct the peaceful resolution of important international political issues that remain unresolved—issues that all peace-loving countries demand be resolved as quickly as possible.

In describing the characteristics of the current international situation, we cannot fail to point out the further worsening of the economic and political situation in so-called backward countries. In these countries, despite the abundance of natural resources, hundreds of millions of people are trapped in poverty and starvation.

Even the work report of the UN Secretariat this year cannot conceal this fact. The report points out that the political, economic, and social problems of Asia, the Middle East, North Africa, and Latin America are particularly serious. The report's author is forced to acknowledge that the people in these countries are demanding equal rights and that the national liberation movement to break free from foreign shackles is growing stronger. The report's author does not conceal his hostility towards this movement—a movement that has in fact swept through many countries with a billion people, whose people are rising up to shatter the chains of foreign bondage. He cries out that this movement is a threat to modern civilization and calls on the Assembly to find ways to stop—in the words of the report—terrible riots and upheavals, which, according to the report's author, may be awaiting the United Nations.

US Secretary of State Mr. Acheson mentioned the plight of the people in colonial and dependent countries in his speech the day before yesterday, but he also had to admit that the situation of these people was dire. Mr. Acheson, in talking about these people's situation, said that the tragedy is that more than half the world's population has been living a life of malnutrition, with many living on the brink of starvation, and the world currently has less food to supply each person than before the war.

Mr. Acheson said that half of the world's population is deprived of their livelihood. According to his explanation, the reason for this situation is: Don't you see, there is not enough food to supply everyone, and the rate of food production in many parts of the world cannot keep up with the rate of population increase.

There is no need to explain in detail here that this Malthusian explanation is untenable. Moreover, I believe that Mr. Acheson is fully aware of the real reasons for the tragedy I mentioned. In reality, the current situation of these countries is mainly a direct consequence of the predatory exploitation of these countries by US monopoly capital. US monopoly capital has not only not spared the so-called backward countries of the East, Latin America, and Asia, but also has not spared certain European capitalist countries that are even economically well-developed.

All of this is bound to further intensify international relations, and the wars waged by the colonial powers in the East against the people of Korea, Vietnam, and Malaya, who are fighting for independence and freedom, have particularly exacerbated international relations.

I. Put an End to the Shameful Korean War

The Korean War has been going on for three years. The US military command, in order to defeat the resistance of the Korean people, has not hesitated to use barbaric means of war, destroying Korean cities and slaughtering peaceful residents, past and present, with no sparing of women, children, and the elderly. Nevertheless, it is absolutely clear that the American plan of aggression in Korea has failed. Trampling on all norms of international law and disregarding the basic requirements of human morality, the American troops are resorting to the planned use of poison gas, napalm bombs, and bacteriological weapons.

But the failure of the American intervention has not stopped the American ruling circles from trying to realize their aggressive plans. Mr. Acheson, in his speech the day before yesterday, continued to portray the American intervention in Korea as a struggle by the United Nations against aggression. He repeats the platitudes that have been exposed time and again, saying that the war imposed on the Korean people by American interventionists is defensive and does not pursue aggressive goals—in reality, it pursues aggressive goals.

Mr. Acheson, in answering the question at this General Assembly of how long this (referring to the Korean War) will continue, said again: We must continue to fight until we stop aggression and rebuild peace and security in Korea.

Lie also incites continued intervention in Korea in his report. In the report that the Secretariat distributed to the delegations at this General Assembly, he demanded the continuation of the Korean War to prove his so-called unwavering determination and to take active action.

Indeed, there is a Russian proverb that says: "What the master says, the servant learns!"

Acheson said at the General Assembly that the United States would agree to a ceasefire in Korea when a ceasefire agreement is reached on fair terms. But, Mr. Acheson, why don't you explain what these fair terms are? Maybe you would deign to tell the General Assembly? Until now, all the conditions put forward by the US military command in Korea, with the consent of the US government, cannot be called fair at all. These conditions can only be called a serious violation of the basic concepts of justice, and cannot be called anything else, because it is obviously illegal and contrary to all universally recognized standards of international law and international morality. The American demand for so-called "screening" of prisoners of war is just such a thing, and their so-called "screening" is to use intimidation and even massacres to pick out prisoners of war who the US military command is willing to repatriate and intends to continue detaining. This is the situation in the POW camps set up by the US military for the Chinese and Korean POWs on Koje Island and elsewhere.

In this regard, I cannot but recall some facts about the activities of the Sixth Session of the United Nations General Assembly on the Korean issue. At that time, the US representative also said: The US government will do its best to reach a ceasefire in Korea under conditions acceptable to both sides. The US representative opposed our proposal that the General Assembly discuss the Korean ceasefire issue and assist both sides in achieving positive results, saying: This proposal is too early, it can only hinder the conclusion of a Korean ceasefire agreement. He said: A stable political agreement must be built on a solid and sound foundation that can be laid by the successful conclusion of the ceasefire negotiations.

Our delegation pointed out at the time: This is just an excuse to achieve the purpose of delaying the negotiations. They are delaying the negotiations by intimidating, blackmailing, and oppressing with hostile actions, while at the same time making absurd demands on the Korean People's Army and the Chinese People's Volunteers on the issue of repairing and building airfields and exchanging prisoners of war. At that time, we said: The US military command is trying to use these means to obtain concessions from the other side, to delay the negotiations, and at the same time to replenish its ammunition and reserves in order to prepare for further aggression.

A year has passed since then, don't we have reason to say that this whole year has proven that our concerns were correct? The negotiations were unjustifiably delayed by the US side; at the same time, the US Air Force was bombing the Korean rear, destroying cities, burning villages, and slaughtering Korean civilians—unarmed women, children, and the elderly; the crimes of the US military are one after another.

This is what everything solid and sound that the US representative has been talking about here means. This is what the "glorious" conditions "acceptable" to both sides that the US spokesman boasted to the General Assembly mean.

Now they are talking about what "fair conditions" are. Now they are no longer talking about "glorious" and "acceptable" conditions, no longer talking about a "solid and sound foundation." These words have been replaced by so-called fair conditions aimed at deceiving the easily deceived.

This kind of talk cannot be taken seriously, especially considering the course of the negotiations with the representatives of the US military command over the past year.

Obviously, the US government neither wants to achieve a ceasefire in Korea nor wants to end the Korean War. It still attempts to use the war in Korea to overcome its economic difficulties. It has been striving to delay the ceasefire negotiations in Panmunjom, and a few days ago it completely and indefinitely suspended the negotiations, hoping to put pressure on the other side by this method.

General Kim Il-sung, Supreme Commander of the Korean People's Army, and General Peng Dehuai, Commander of the Chinese People's Volunteers, correctly pointed out in their recent letter to General Clark: If the United States had not deliberately created obstacles on the issue of prisoners of war, then the Korean ceasefire would have been realized long ago, North and South Korea would have returned to peace, and prisoners of war on both sides would have returned home to live a peaceful life long ago. However, this obstruction has had an effect: despite Kim Il-sung and Peng Dehuai's efforts to seek positive results, the negotiations finally broke down a few days ago. In order to seek positive results, the two of them made a proposal on October 8, which only those who would stop the realization of the Korean ceasefire at any cost would refuse.

But what is this proposal after all? The above-mentioned letter to General Clark says: "In accordance with the just demand that all prisoners of war on both sides should be repatriated home to live a peaceful life, we propose: After the ceasefire agreement takes effect, all prisoners of war on both sides may, in accordance with your proposal (that is, in accordance with the proposal of the US military command), be transported to the agreed exchange point in the demilitarized zone and handed over to the other side for reception; after reception, and in accordance with your proposal, in accordance with Article 57 of the draft Korean ceasefire agreement, a joint Red Cross team will conduct visits to explain to the prisoners of war on both sides and guarantee that they will return home to live a peaceful life and no longer participate in the Korean War; then, in accordance with the aforementioned principle of classification by nationality and region proposed by our side, classification will be carried out, and repatriation will be carried out after classification. This work of exchange, visits, classification, and repatriation can be carried out under the observation of a neutral nation inspection team."

What was the response of the American delegation to these truly fair and reasonable conditions? The American delegation completely ignored this proposal; it refused to discuss this proposal and immediately issued its prepared statement, announcing an indefinite recess of the negotiations. It immediately left the meeting tent without even waiting for a reply from the Korean-Chinese command.

Mr. Acheson didn't mention any of these things here, but he was willing to talk about "fair conditions" as a shield, but Kim Il-sung and Peng Dehuai put forward truly fair conditions almost two weeks ago, and they have done their best to strive for the realization of a Korean ceasefire agreement.

The American billionaires have become rich in this war, and of course they don't want this war to stop. They want to continue to use this war as their most important way to get rich, and use this war to avoid the threat of depression and the growing crisis facing capitalist countries, especially the United States. This has long been confirmed by the countless remarks of famous American entrepreneurs, financial and securities tycoons, politicians, soldiers, ministers, and the American president himself.

For example, the prominent American financier James Warburg publicly said that because there is no peace, the United States is rapidly developing legitimate interests. According to Warburg, the war saved the American economy from falling into depression and crisis, and allowed the American military industry to obtain war orders, mainly orders for the continued Korean War.

In the spring of 1952, when talk of a possible ceasefire in Korea spread, the "Wall Street Journal" pointed out that securities market prices had plummeted in the previous period. The newspaper believed that this was caused by the rumors of peace... The newspaper explained that businessmen were very afraid that once the war ended, it might hinder the expansion of armaments, and some large companies with particularly large investments would be unable to adapt to the needs of civilian production and would be in an unbalanced state. British newspapers also held the same view, for example, the famous British weekly "New Statesman and Nation" said that American business people could not welcome peace, because the American economy is maintained by war.

The weekly said that the United States cannot reduce armaments and switch to civilian production as it did in 1945.

The famous American Senator Taft also opposed the realization of a ceasefire in Korea, and he openly advocated delaying the negotiations until America's war production was fully operational. America's current enthusiasm for the frantic expansion of armaments is obviously still not enough to satisfy Taft.

American soldiers, such as General Bradley, Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff, also advocated undermining the Korean ceasefire negotiations. They are proceeding from military interests—they believe that this will allow them to buy time to implement a broader plan of military measures in order to further increase armaments and armed forces. Further construction of new land, sea, and air bases outside the United States, and the renovation of existing land, sea, and air bases. Secretary of the Army Pace also advocated war, he emphasized that, in his opinion, the Korean War gave the United States an opportunity to experiment with and improve its war methods, and improve its weapons and ammunition.

Van Fleet, the commander of the US Eighth Army in Korea, said that the Korean War was gratifying, and he pointed out that such military operations were necessary, whether here (that is, Korea) or elsewhere in the world.

President Truman said in his speech on September 27 this year: He will never adopt the views of those who resolutely advocate the withdrawal of American armed forces from Korea. In his conversation published in today's New York newspapers, he actually repeated the same words: The United States will never allow American troops to withdraw from Korea. This kind of talk by the American president can only be interpreted as the determination of the American ruling circles to continue the shameful war against the Korean people.

In this case, everyone can see very clearly: why did the US military command interrupt the Panmunjom negotiations, despite the Korean side's exhaustion of all efforts to strive for a ceasefire agreement on the basis of the recognized principles of international law; why did the American ruling circles stubbornly refuse every effort to reach an agreement, and create various excuses to make the discussion unable to achieve practical results? Their only purpose is to delay and sabotage the negotiations. They used the issue of exchanging prisoners of war to create excuses, and put forward unprecedented demands that violate the basic principles of recognized international law. Although the US government signed the 1949 Geneva Convention, the US side refused to comply with the provisions of the convention, grossly violated the convention, especially Articles 118 and 119 of the convention. These two articles stipulate that the belligerent parties have the obligation to guarantee the unconditional repatriation of all prisoners of war, except those who are prosecuted for crimes. As we have said before, the US side used all means of pressure and intimidation to carry out illegal screening of prisoners of war, forcing them to declare that they were unwilling to return to their homeland. The protests of Korean and Chinese prisoners of war against these arbitrary and illegal actions were suppressed by the US military command. For example, in the POW camp on Koje Island, the US military command even used massacres to suppress them, and this massacre is still continuing in the American POW camps.

There has never been such a violation of international law in history.

At this session, the General Assembly is again faced with the task of resolving the Korean issue. The peace-loving people of all countries who have formed a great front to defend world peace are increasingly firmly demanding an end to the Korean War, they are demanding that the imperialist aggressors be stopped, and they are increasingly fiercely opposing the American policy of aggression.

The General Assembly has an inescapable responsibility to accomplish this task, and to accomplish this task in accordance with the principles of the United Nations.

The proposal put forward by the Polish delegation is fully in line with the task of the General Assembly in this regard.

We wholeheartedly support the Polish delegation's proposal that the General Assembly recommend that the belligerent parties immediately cease land, sea, and air hostilities.

We wholeheartedly support the proposal to repatriate all prisoners of war in accordance with international norms.

We wholeheartedly support the Polish delegation's proposal that all foreign troops, including the Chinese People's Volunteers, should be withdrawn from Korea within two or three months; and that the Korean issue should be resolved peacefully in the spirit of restoring Korean reunification, and that this work of restoring reunification should be carried out by the Koreans themselves under the supervision of a committee composed of directly related parties and other countries that have not participated in the Korean War.

The Soviet government believes that this is the way to resolve the Korean issue. This is one of the most effective ways to end the current international tensions, maintain peace, and strengthen international security.

II. Stop the US from Using Inhumane Bacteriological Weapons

The Soviet delegation believes that it is necessary to discuss in particular the issue of the use of bacteriological weapons by the US military in Korea.

The official representatives of the United States are trying to deny that the US armed forces used this weapon of inhumane extermination. But the many facts confirmed by the impartial international investigation team—the investigation team of the International Association of Democratic Lawyers and the international scientific committee composed of representatives of six countries (Britain, Brazil, Italy, the Soviet Union, France, and Sweden)—prove that the American aggressors committed this most serious crime.

The committee told the world in its report that it had to agree with the evidence and declare that, despite the unanimous condemnation of the people of the world, these inhumane means were indeed used.

The committee said: The Korean and Chinese people have indeed been the target of bacteriological weapons attacks, and the US military has used these bacteriological weapons in many different ways, some of which appear to be developed from the methods used by the Japanese army during World War II.

Captured American officers O'Neill, Innock, Quinn, and Kneis also confirmed this point, they personally participated in throwing bombs containing bacteria that spread dangerous infectious diseases in order to massively eliminate peaceful residents.

More than five hundred documents, pictures, and other materials proving the use of bacteriological weapons by the US military are displayed in the five halls of the exhibition of evidence of the use of bacteriological weapons by the American army in Beijing. Gentlemen, these are facts, these facts have been proven to be true, and cannot be shaken by any lies, deception, or slander.

On the one hand, the US government groundlessly denies these facts; on the other hand, it refuses to ratify the 1925 Geneva Protocol prohibiting the use of bacteriological weapons, which the United States has signed, apparently in order to be able to freely use bacteriological weapons in the future. American officials, such as General White, former commander of the Chemical Corps of the US Army, have highly praised bacteriological weapons, calling them a practical method of war with great potential. According to White, this weapon cannot be ignored, and the United States should not ignore it even more because this weapon is cheap and easy to use.

It is not accidental that these people attach so much importance to bacteriological weapons; it is not accidental that American universities, such as the University of California, are specifically studying the issue of spreading plague by means of spraying.

In the United States, bacteria have been considered a decisive factor in war.

It is bacteria, not generals, that win wars—this is what General Clise, director of research and development of the Chemical Corps of the US Army, said. This sentence typically illustrates the characteristics of current American policy.

It is also not accidental that the United States has begun to vigorously carry out work to improve methods of bacterial culture in recent years, and many US government agencies and scientific institutions have participated in this work. They are using selective methods to cultivate the most toxic bacteria, and are trying their best to improve the methods of spreading bacteria.

The use of bacteriological weapons in Korea is not only for the heinous purpose of mass slaughter of civilians, but also to test the effectiveness of this weapon in practice, and to train personnel to learn how to use this weapon, and, as US Secretary of the Army Pace said, to determine the value of various methods and means of solving urgent war problems.

In this way, the American aggressors have turned Korea into a testing ground to determine the effect of poisonous bacteria!

This is the limit of moral depravity!

The Soviet delegation believes that it is necessary to particularly emphasize the importance of the Polish delegation's proposal to urge all countries that have not yet participated in or ratified the 1925 Geneva Protocol to participate in and ratify this protocol. This proposal is primarily directed at the United States, which still refuses to ratify the Geneva Protocol.

The Soviet delegation, like the tens of millions of just people in the world, is deeply angered by the use of bacteriological weapons by the US armed forces against the Korean and Chinese people. The Soviet delegation fully supports the Polish delegation's proposal.

III. America's Militarization Policy—A Policy of Impoverishing the People, A Policy of Plundering Other Countries

In the past year, the North Atlantic aggressive bloc has been stepping up its preparations for a new world war. The countries of this bloc have carried out a frantic arms expansion movement.

They are actively building new armies and organizing aggressive military alliances separately in the West and the East. This year, mainly due to the active activities of the United States, Turkey and Greece have joined the North Atlantic aggressive bloc. West Germany and Yugoslavia, two vassals of the United States, actually belong to this bloc.

This year, the Western countries, led by the United States, signed the Bonn Separate Treaty with the Adenauer government, which is actually equivalent to an open military alliance, which legally justifies the revival of German militarism and the establishment of West German mercenaries led by Fascist Hitler generals. Soon after, they signed the so-called "European Defense Group" treaty with West Germany with the same goal.

The US ruling circles have strengthened their pressure on the countries of the Middle East and the Near East, attempting to establish a so-called "Middle East Command" and drag these countries into the North Atlantic bloc, using these countries and other colonial dependencies to establish military bases, and using their populations as human reserves for another world war that the United States is preparing for. When they act in this way, they claim that it is necessary to organize such a "command" to "defend" against the Soviet Union, which they say is threatening the security of these countries. However, this statement is completely untenable. The absurdity of this statement is very obvious, because the Soviet Union, unlike other countries that regard the countries of the Middle East and the Near East as their colonies, has adopted a policy towards these countries that is fully in line with the fundamental national interests of the people of the Near East and the Middle East, and also in line with the interests of all peace-loving people.

In the past year, the US ruling circles have imposed so-called "common security" agreements on some Latin American countries (Brazil, Colombia, and other countries). Even the Latin American bourgeois newspapers have used the following words to illustrate the true meaning of this agreement, saying: "Brazil is being mobilized for war." The US ruling circles, again using the US "defense" as an excuse—an excuse fabricated to deceive people—concluded the Pacific Convention with New Zealand and Australia, saying that these regions are extremely important to the security of the United States, although, as everyone knows, these regions are thousands of kilometers away from the United States.

At the same time, the North Atlantic bloc, led by the United States, held large-scale naval and air force exercises, the nature of which is an open provocation against the Soviet Union and the people's democratic countries, which has been particularly proven by the recent exercise conducted by eight countries of the North Atlantic bloc with the significant name "Grand Repulse".

Everyone knows that the United States plays a leading role in this bloc, and the United States has expanded its arms industry and carried out other large-scale military measures, thereby further strengthening its arms expansion movement.

The US ruling circles are paying particular attention to the work of building new land, sea, and air bases on foreign territories. At present, the United States is building bases in Canada, Greenland, Iceland, Norway, Denmark, the Azores, French Morocco, Tripolitania, Saudi Arabia, Turkey, Outer Jordan, Japan, and many other countries; these bases, as publicly stated, are all aimed at the Soviet Union, the People's Republic of China, and the European people's democratic countries.

It should be pointed out that the United States is building many bases in Western Europe. For example, there are currently twenty-six American air force and other bases in Britain. The whole of Britain has actually been turned into an American air force base. Everyone knows that the Lisbon Conference of the North Atlantic bloc passed a resolution stipulating that a huge network of air force bases should be built in many countries of this bloc, mainly by the United States, and as a result of this resolution, about two hundred airports are to be built.

These are the facts. They prove that the imperialist camp has stepped up its preparations for another war under the cover of the deceptive propaganda about the so-called Soviet threat to peace.

The fact that the members of the North Atlantic bloc, mainly the United States, have further increased military appropriations this year convincingly proves the aggressive plans of this bloc. US military spending in the 1952-1953 fiscal year has increased more than 58 times compared to the 1937-1938 fiscal year. At present, this expenditure accounts for 74 percent of the entire US budget, while in the 1937-1938 fiscal year, military spending only accounted for 14 percent of the entire budget. The huge US budget appropriations are used for the production of atomic weapons, and for the expansion of the atomic industry, which brings huge profits to American monopoly capitalists—in the United States, they call it a "billion-dollar business." It should be pointed out that the appropriations for the production of atomic weapons in 1952 are equivalent to twice that of 1951, and it is planned to further increase this appropriation in the coming years.

These facts are sufficient to expose the hypocrisy of the talks made by US political leaders, and the hypocrisy of the various plans they and their allies have put forward to the United Nations, which are said to be aimed at banning atomic weapons.

Therefore, it is not accidental that the US government wants to further expand the production of atomic weapons. According to the published figures, the expenditure on the production of atomic weapons in 1945-1946 was 418 million yuan, 897 million yuan in 1950-1951, and this expenditure will reach 1.7 billion yuan in the current fiscal year of 1952-1953, and in the next two years, this expenditure is planned to increase to 3 billion yuan.

These figures are sufficient to show that the US ruling circles do not want to ban, or even intend to reduce, the production of atomic weapons at all, and all the statements made by the official representatives of the United States that they are willing to ban atomic weapons are completely hypocritical, and their purpose is only to deceive honest people and simple-minded people.

This year, the scale of militarization of American industry is even greater. The increase in American industrial production is due to the sharp increase in American war production due to the intervention in Korea and the intensified arms expansion movement; the construction of new air bases by the United States around the world, mainly around the Soviet Union and the people's democratic countries; and the increase in the strength of the US armed forces—only these facts can explain why American industrial production has increased.

These materials also confirm that the militarization of the American economy is proceeding on a huge scale. General Eisenhower was forced to admit this in a conversation, saying: The American economy is a war economy, and American prosperity is war prosperity.

The most sober-minded part of the American newspapers, when pointing out the characteristics of the unprecedented rampant militarism in the United States, said: If we compare this universal militarism with the measures taken by the United States in 1941 to prevent war, we can easily see that, as Representative Boffete of Nebraska said, the United States is now much closer to war than it was then.

The same process of industrial militarization can be seen in Britain, where military spending in the 1952-1953 fiscal year accounts for almost 34 percent of the entire budget, more than twice the military spending before World War II.

France is currently spending much more on preparedness than it spent on this aspect in the last year before the war—1938. Today, nearly 40 percent of France's budget is used for military expenditures.

The reasons for the militarization of the economies of the United States, Britain, France, and other capitalist countries can be found in the typical characteristics of monopoly capitalism in our time. The driving force of monopoly capitalism is the greatest profit, as J.V. Stalin pointed out in his recent classic work "Economic Problems of Socialism in the USSR", this greatest profit drives monopoly capitalism "to take such adventurous steps as enslaving and constantly plundering colonies and other backward countries, turning many independent countries into dependencies, organizing new wars that the leaders of modern capitalism believe are the best 'business' for obtaining the greatest profit, and finally attempting to seize the world's economic hegemony."

Everyone knows that the American warmongers have brought huge profits to the American giants. In 1951, this profit almost reached $43 billion, which is 13 times the profit they made in 1938. The same situation exists in Britain and France.

Based on these facts, it is completely understandable why US President Truman constantly demands new appropriations to implement war plans, especially to build military bases; why he opposes reducing appropriations in this area, and why he insists that it would be a terrible disaster if half of the bases were lost with a stroke of the pen.

This explains that the current foreign policy of the US government is completely determined by the interests of the US industrial and financial giants, who are expecting a new world war, hoping to make a fortune in this bloody game, and they have never thought about giving up their war plans and arms expansion movement.

The "New York Post" said that the slightest discussion about a disarmament treaty would make American entrepreneurs, bankers, and politicians tremble with fear.

The militarization of the entire economy of the United States, Britain, France, and some other countries in the North Atlantic bloc, as well as the continuous and alarming increase in military expenditures, inevitably promote the continuous increase in taxes and the tax burden on the people, lower the living standards of millions of people, and reduce appropriations for meeting the basic needs of the people. The direct taxes that the American people need to pay in the 1952-1953 fiscal year have increased more than twelve times compared to the 1937-1938 fiscal year.

In Britain, France, Italy, and other capitalist countries in the North Atlantic bloc, the amount of taxes has increased greatly. Schemington, the former chairman of the Reconstruction Finance Corporation, confirmed that six corners out of every dollar of these countries' taxes go to the Pentagon, which is used for military purposes. The number of unemployed people in these countries is increasing, and even official economic reports and investigations cannot conceal this fact, such as the investigation report for the first half of 1952 written by the US President's Council of Economic Advisers. This investigation report exposed the continuous increase in the army of unemployed and semi-unemployed people in capitalist countries.

The economic and financial situation of Western European countries is constantly deteriorating. Their balance of payments deficits are aggressively increasing. For example, Britain's currency reserves had fallen to less than half of last year's by the end of the first six months of 1952. Therefore, it is not accidental that Mr. Draper, the US representative attending the North Atlantic Council, pointed out in his report that Western Europe is now most distressed by financial problems and payment problems, especially the lack of dollars. The purpose of all these plans and measures is to make the economies of Western European countries subordinate to the military strategic plans of the North Atlantic aggressive bloc, and to—as has long been proven—oppose the Soviet Union, the People's Republic of China, and the European people's democratic countries. The American ruling circles, which issue orders to Western European countries, are currently trying to cover up this plan with the task of so-called strengthening the "free" countries' defense against the Soviet Union. They insist that the Soviet Union is planning to attack the United States, Britain, France, and other countries in this bloc.

However, this so-called "assistance" has put even economically developed countries such as Britain and France in difficulty. Truman had to admit in his message to the US Congress on January 9, 1952 that the "strengthening of defense" work carried out in various "free" countries has caused serious economic problems, and, Truman admitted, has exacerbated European inflation and jeopardized the work of further restoring the strength of the US allies.

Then what should be said about the situation in backward countries? IV. Opposing the Use of the United Nations for Aggression

The aggressive direction of the diplomatic policy pursued by the US ruling circles is reflected in the activities of the representatives of the United States, Britain, France, and some other countries of the North Atlantic bloc in the United Nations.

If we talk about the work of the Disarmament Commission established at the Sixth Session of the United Nations General Assembly, there seems to be no doubt that the majority group in the commission, led by the United States, has done its best to refuse any reduction in armaments, to obstruct the banning of atomic weapons; the purpose of their activities is to legalize the further expansion of armaments, and to reserve the opportunity for the United States to continue accumulating atomic bombs. ### 防止另一次世界大战的威胁、维护和平、加强国际安全——维辛斯基十月十八日在联合国大会全体会议上的演说

1952-10-21
第1版()
专栏：

    防止另一次世界大战的威胁、维护和平、加强国际安全
    ——维辛斯基十月十八日在联合国大会全体会议上的演说
    联合国第七届大会是在这样一种情况下开始进行它的工作的：国际生活的日程上充满了有关亿万人民、整个整个的民族和国家的切身利益的重要而复杂的问题。这些问题的中心就是和平、和平合作与国际安全的问题。这些问题在历届联合国大会的工作中也占有重要地位，在历届会议上，苏联代表团一贯地、坚定不移地坚持大会有必要采取措施来维护和平与加强各族人民间的合作。
    本届联合国大会是在这样一个时期召开的：在这个时期，国际关系问题需要我们特别注意，原因就在于目前国际形势的复杂与紧张。
    在第六届大会以来的一年里，事实证明：北大西洋集团过去是，现在仍然是使国际紧张状态剧烈化和国际形势严重化的重要因素，因为这个集团怀抱着侵略目的，这种侵略目的是它的组织者和领导者用高谈所谓防务问题的手段所掩饰不了的。在这个集团里面，美国统治集团与追随美国政策的英国与法国统治集团的显要人物，一方面用关于和平的谎话来笼络人心，从而给他们自己戴上爱好和平的假面具，另一方面又继续煽动战争心理，千方百计地在他们的国家里保持一种紧张状态，并阻挠和平解决迄今尚未解决的重要国际政治问题——一切爱好和平的国家都要求尽速决定的问题。
    在说明目前的国际形势的特征的时候，我们不能不同时指出像所谓落后国家的经济与政治情况进一步严重化这样的事实。在这些国家里，尽管有大量的天然财富，仍有亿万人民陷于贫穷与饥饿的境地。
    甚至今年联合国秘书处的工作报告也掩饰不了这种事实。该项报告指出：亚洲、中东、北非和拉丁美洲的政治、经济与社会问题特别严重。报告的作者不得不在报告中承认这些国家里的人民要求权利平等与摆脱外国束缚的民族解放运动正在日益壮大。这个报告的作者并不掩饰他对这个运动的敌意——这个运动实际上已经席卷了拥有十亿人口的许多国家，这些国家的人民正在奋起打碎外国束缚的锁链。他叫喊着说：这个运动是对现代文明的威胁，并要求大会设法阻止——用这个报告中的话来说——可怕的暴动和大变乱，不然的话，据报告作者说，这些暴动与变乱就可能在等候着联合国。
    美国国务卿艾奇逊先生在前天的发言中提到了殖民地和附属国人民的处境，但是他也不得不承认这些国家人民的处境悲惨。艾奇逊在这里谈到这些人民的处境时说，悲剧在于，世界上二分之一以上的人民一直过着营养不良的生活，许多人生活在饥饿的边缘上，世界目前可以供给每个人的食物数量比战前少。
    艾奇逊先生说，世界上有二分之一的人民被剥夺了生计。据他解释，产生这种情况的原因是：诸位难道没有看到吗，没有足够的食物可以供给每个人，世界上许多地方的粮食生产速度都赶不上人口增加的速度。
    在这里不需要特别详细说明，这种马尔萨斯式的解释是站不住脚的。不仅如此，我相信艾奇逊先生充分知道我所提到的悲剧的真正原因。实际上，这些国家所处的这种情况，目前主要是美国垄断资本对这些国家的掠夺剥削的直接后果，美国垄断资本不仅没有放过东方、拉丁美洲、亚洲的所谓落后国家，并且也不放过甚至经济上已充分发展的某些欧洲资本主义国家。
    这一切必然会使国际关系愈趋紧张，而殖民国家集团在东方对正在为独立与自由而斗争的朝鲜、越南和马来亚人民所进行的战争，特别促使国际关系紧张化。
    一、制止可耻的朝鲜战争
    朝鲜战争已进行到第三年了。美军司令部为了要击败朝鲜人民的抵抗，过去和现在都不惜使用野蛮的战争手段，毁灭朝鲜城市，屠杀和平居民，妇女、儿童和老年人都不能幸免。尽管如此，绝对明显的是，美国对朝鲜的侵略计划还是遭到了失败。美国军队践踏一切国际法准则，不顾人类道德的起码要求，正在乞灵于有计划地使用毒气、凝固汽油弹和细菌武器。
    但是美国干涉行为的失败并没有使美国统治集团不再企图实现他们的侵略计划。艾奇逊先生在前天的发言里，还是继续把美国对朝鲜的干涉说成是联合国反对侵略的斗争。他重复已被揭发无遗的滥调，说什么美国干涉者硬加在朝鲜人民头上的战争是防御性的，而且不追求侵略目的——实际上，追求的正是侵略目的。
    艾奇逊先生在本届大会上答复这（指朝鲜战争）将继续多久这个问题时又说：我们必须一直作战到制止侵略并在朝鲜重建和平和安全时为止。
    赖伊在他的报告里也煽动继续对朝鲜进行干涉。他在秘书处向本届大会各代表团分发的报告里，要求继续朝鲜战争，以证明他所谓的坚定不移的决心，并积极行动起来。
    俄罗斯的一句俗话的确有道理，这句俗话说：“主人说什么，奴才学什么！”
    艾奇逊在大会上说，在停战协定根据公正的条件达成的时候，美国将在朝鲜停战。但是，艾奇逊先生，你为什么不说明这些公正条件是什么呢？也许你还会屈尊告诉大会吧？迄今为止，在朝鲜的美军司令部在美国政府的同意下提出的所有条件根本就不能称作是公正的。这些条件只能称作是对正义的起码概念的严重破坏，而不能称作任何别的东西，因为它显然是非法的，而且与所有举世公认的国际法和国际道德的标准背道而驰。应该说美国的所谓
    “甄别”战俘的要求就是这样的，他们的所谓“甄别”便是不惜使用恫吓甚至屠杀的手段挑出美军司令部愿意遣返的和打算继续拘留的战俘。美军在巨济岛上和其他地方为中朝战俘所设的战俘营中的情况就是这样。
    在这方面，我不能不追述一下联合国第六届大会在朝鲜问题上所进行的活动的一些事实。那个时候，美国代表也曾经说过：美国政府将尽力在双方都可以接受的条件下达成朝鲜停战。美国代表反对我们所提出的由大会讨论朝鲜停战问题并协助双方获致积极结果的建议，他说：这个建议提得过早，它只能妨害朝鲜停战协定的缔结。他说：一个稳定的政治协定必须建筑在停战谈判圆满结束时所能奠定的坚固而健全的基础上。
    我国代表团当时就曾指出：这只是一个藉口，用来达到拖延谈判的目的。他们一方面以敌对行动来进行恫吓、讹诈和压迫，同时在修复与建筑飞机场和交换战俘问题上向朝鲜人民军和中国人民志愿军提出荒谬的要求，这样来拖延谈判。当时我们说：美军司令部企图以这些手段取得对方的让步，来拖延谈判，同时并补充其弹药与后备兵，以便准备进行进一步的侵略。
    从那个时候到现在已经有一年之久了，难道我们没有理由说，这整整一年的时间已经证明了我们的忧虑是正确的吗？谈判被美方无缘无故地拖延下来；同时，美国空军则在轰炸朝鲜后方，破坏城市，焚毁乡村，屠杀朝鲜平民——赤手空拳的妇女、儿童和老年人；美军的罪行是一个接着一个。
    美国代表在这里口若悬河地谈论的一切坚固而健全的基础，原来就是如此。美国的发言人大吹大擂地向大会所说明的双方都“可以接受的”“光荣”条件，原来就是如此。
    现在他们又谈什么“公正条件”了。现在他们不再谈什么“光荣”而“可以接受的”条件了，不再谈什么“坚固而健全的基础”了。这些话已经被一些旨在迷惑易受欺骗的人的所谓公正条件代替了。
    这一类的话是不能认真对待的，特别是考虑到一年来与美军司令部的代表们所进行的谈判的经过时，更是如此。
    显然，美国政府既不希望在朝鲜达成停战，也不希望结束朝鲜战争。它仍然企图利用在朝鲜的战争来克服它的经济困难。它一直竭力拖延板门店的停战谈判，几天以前它又完全无限期地中断了谈判，希望靠这种方法来对对方加以压力。
    朝鲜人民军最高司令官金日成将军和中国人民志愿军司令员彭德怀将军在最近致克拉克将军的信中十分正确地指出：如果不是美国故意在俘虏问题上横加阻挠，则朝鲜停战必然早已实现，南北朝鲜必然早已恢复和平，双方战俘必然早已回家过和平生活了。可是，这种阻挠已经发生了效果：尽管金日成和彭德怀努力谋求积极的结果，可是几天前谈判终于中断了。他们二人为了谋求积极的结果，曾在十月八日提出了一项建议，这项建议只有那些不惜以任何代价阻止朝鲜停战实现的人们才会加以拒绝。
    但是，这个建议到底是怎样的呢？上面提到的致克拉克将军的信是这样说的：“根据双方战俘应予全部遣返回家过和平生活的正义要求，我方提议：在停战协定生效后，双方全部战俘，可照你方提议
    （即按照美军司令部方面的提议），运往非军事区中双方同意的交换地点，交给对方接收；在接收后，并照你方提议，按朝鲜停战协定草案第五十七款规定，由联合红十字会小组进行访问，向双方战俘解释，保证他们回家过和平生活，不再参加朝鲜战争；然后再根据前述的我方所提的按国籍地区分类原则进行分类，分类后即进行遣返。这种交换、访问、分类和遣返的工作，可在中立国视察小组观察之下进行。”
    对于这些的的确确公平合理的条件，美国代表团方面的反应怎样呢？美国代表团根本无视这个建议；它拒绝讨论这个建议，并马上发表了它事先就准备好的声明，宣告谈判无限期休会。它马上就离开了会议帐篷，甚至没有等待朝中司令部的答复。
    艾奇逊先生在这里对这些事情一点也没有提到，他倒是愿意空谈“公正的条件”，把这拿来当作挡箭牌，然而金日成和彭德怀差不多两个星期以前就提出了真正公正的条件，他们已经尽了最大的力量争取朝鲜停战协定的实现。
    美国亿万富翁们在这个战争里肥了起来，他们当然不愿意让这个战争停止。他们要继续利用这个战争当作他们最重要的一个致富之道，利用这战争来避免资本主义各国、首先是美国面临的萧条和日渐增长的危机的威胁。这一点早就为美国有名的企业家、金融和证券巨头、政客、军人、部长以及美国总统本人的无数言论所证实。
    例如，美国显要金融家杰姆斯·威尔伯就公开地说，因为没有和平，美国正飞速地发展合法的利益。据威尔伯承认，战争挽救了美国经济，使它不致陷于萧条和危机，使美国军火工业得到战争订货，主要是继续进行朝鲜战争的订货。
    一九五二年春天，当朝鲜可能实现停战的谈论传开来的时候，“华尔街日报”指出，在先前的一段时期里，证券市场价格暴跌。该报认为，这是由于和谣盛传所促成的。……该报解释说，商人们很害怕，战争一旦终止，可能就会妨碍扩张军备，投资特别庞大的一些大公司就会不适应民用生产的需要，处于不平衡的状态。英国报纸也都持同样的见解，例如英国有名的“新政治家与民族”周刊就说过，美国企业界人士不可能欢迎和平，因为美国的经济是靠战争来维持的。
    该周刊说，美国不能像一九四五年那样裁减军备，转而进行民用生产。
    美国著名参议员塔夫脱也反对在朝鲜实现停战，他公然主张把谈判拖延下去，一直到美国的战争生产全盘转动起来。美国现在那样热中于疯狂地扩张军备，显然还是不能使塔夫脱满足。
    美国的军人们，例如参谋长联席会议主席布莱德雷，也鼓吹破坏朝鲜停战谈判。他们是从军事上的利害出发的——他们认为这样他们便可以争取时间，实现更加广泛的军事措施计划，以便更进一步增加军备和武装部队。进一步在美国境外建设新的陆、海、空军基地，并改建现有的陆、海、空军基地。陆军部长佩斯也主张战争，他强调指出，在他看来，朝鲜战争使美国有机会试验并改进它的战争方法，改良它的武器和军火。
    在朝鲜的美国第八军军长范佛里特说，朝鲜战争是值得庆幸的，他指出，无论在这里（即朝鲜）也好，在世界的其他地方也好，这样一种军事行动是必要的。
    杜鲁门总统在今年九月二十七日发表的演说里说：他决不采纳那些坚决主张美国武装部队撤出朝鲜的人的意见。在今天纽约各报刊载的他的谈话中，他实际上重复了同样的话：美国决不允许美国军队撤出朝鲜。美国总统的这种谈话只能被解释为美国统治集团决意继续对朝鲜人民进行可耻的战争。
    在这种情况下，每个人都可以看得很清楚：为什么美军司令部中断了板门店谈判，尽管朝鲜方面用尽一切努力争取在符合公认的国际法准则的基础上达成停战协议；为什么美国统治集团顽固地拒绝每个希望达成协议的努力，并制造各种藉口使讨论不能收到实际效果呢？它们唯一的目的是拖延与破坏谈判。他们利用交换战俘问题制造藉口，提出了违反公认的国际法起码准则的史无先例的要求。虽然美国政府曾在一九四九年日内瓦公约上签了字，但是美国方面拒绝遵守公约中的规定，粗暴地违反了该公约，特别是违反了公约的第一百十八条与第一百十九条。这两条规定：交战双方有义务保证除因犯罪而被起诉者外，所有战俘予以无条件遣返。我们在以前已经说过，美国方面利用一切压力与恫吓的手段，对战俘进行非法的甄别，强迫他们声明不愿返回他们的祖国。朝鲜与中国战俘对这些专横与不法行动的抗议，遭到美军司令部的镇压。例如在巨济岛战俘营中，美军司令部甚至用屠杀来进行镇压，这种屠杀目前仍在美国的战俘营中继续进行。
    历史上从来没有过这样违反国际法的事例。
    在本届会议上，大会又面临解决朝鲜问题的任务。已经组成一个保卫世界和平的伟大阵线的爱好和平的各国人民，正在日益坚决地要求结束朝鲜战争，他们要求制止帝国主义侵略者，并且日益激烈地反对美国侵略政策。
    大会有不可推卸的责任完成这个任务，按照联合国的原则完成这个任务。
    波兰代表团所提出的建议是完全符合大会在这一方面的任务的。
    我们衷心支持波兰代表团所提出的要求大会建议交战双方立即停止陆、海、空军敌对行动的建议。
    我们衷心支持按照国际准则遣返所有战俘的建议。
    我们衷心支持波兰代表团的建议，就是：所有外国军队，包括中国人民志愿军在内，在两、三个月之内一律撤出朝鲜；以恢复朝鲜统一的精神和平解决朝鲜问题，这种恢复统一的工作应由朝鲜人自己在直接有关各方和其他没有参加朝鲜战争的国家参加的一个委员会监督之下进行。
    苏联政府认为这是解决朝鲜问题的道路。这是结束目前国际紧张局势、维护和平与加强国际安全的最有效的方法之一。
    二、制止美国使用灭绝人性的细菌武器
    苏联代表团认为有必要特别详细讨论在朝鲜的美国军队使用细菌武器的问题。

    三、美国的军事化政策——使人民贫困的政策、掠夺其他国家的政策
    过去一年里，北大西洋侵略集团在加紧准备新的世界大战。这个集团的各个国家进行了疯狂的扩张军备运动。

    在过去一年内，美国统治集团将所谓“共同安全”协定强加于一些拉丁美洲国家的身上（巴西、哥伦比亚和其他国家）。连拉丁美洲的资产阶级报纸也以下面的话来说明这种协定的真正意义，它们说：“巴西正在被动员起来进行战争。”美国统治集团同样又以美国的“防务”为藉口——这种藉口是捏造出来骗人的——与新西兰和澳大利亚缔结了太平洋公约，说什么这些地区对于美国的安全极其重要，虽然，大家知道，这些地区距离美国有几千公里之远。
    同时，以美国为首的北大西洋集团又举行大规模的海军和空军演习，这种演习的性质就是对苏联和人民民主国家进行公开的挑衅，这一点已经特别由北大西洋集团八个国家最近用“大振奋”这一个饶有意义的名称举行的演习证明了。
    大家知道，美国在这一个集团里起着领导作用，美国已经扩大了它的军火工业，并大规模地进行了其他军事措施，从而进一步加强了它的扩张军备运动。

    北大西洋集团成员国，主要是美国，今年更进一步增加了军事拨款，这一事实令人信服地证明了这个集团的侵略计划。一九五二—一九五三会计年度内的美国军费比一九三七—一九三八会计年度增加了五十八倍以上。目前，这笔费用占美国全部预算的百分之七十四，而一九三七—一九三八会计年度内的军费只占全部预算的百分之十四。庞大的美国预算拨款用在原子武器的生产上，用在扩充给美国垄断资本家带来巨大利润的原子工业——在美国，他们称为“耗资亿万的事业”——上。应该指出：一九五二年生产原子武器的拨款相当于一九五一年的两倍，今后几年中这种拨款并已计划要进一步增加。

    “苏联社会主义经济问题”中所指明的，这种最大利润驱使垄断资本主义“采取这样冒险的步骤：奴役和不断掠夺殖民地和其他落后国家，把许多独立国变为附属国，组织现代资本主义的首领们认为是取得最大利润的最好‘生意’的新战争，最后企图攫取世界的经济霸权。”

    五、反对另一次世界大战的威胁，加强国际和平与友谊
    在一九四九年联合国第四届大会上，苏联遵循它的加强国际和平与合作的政策，希望与各国合作，并考虑到当时侵略力量正在加紧动员的事实，曾表示反对准备另一次战争，维护各国人民的和平与安全。苏联代表团建议大会号召世界各国，用和平的方法解决它们的纠纷和分歧，而不使用武力或以武力相威胁；苏联代表团并且号召它们缔结一项巩固和平的公约。苏联代表团的这种建议反映了全世界各国人民制止另一次战争的威胁和确保和平的不屈不挠的意志和决心，这种意志和决心已经由各国人民争取和平、反对战争贩子的强大运动中表现出来了。
    北大西洋公约不以尊重各方面的主权平等的原则为基础，它要各国听从美国的绝对命令。而五大国和平公约必须是一个以缔约国互相平等的原则为基础的公约，必须是一个以巩固和平并反对侵略——不管这种侵略以什么形式威胁爱好和平的人民——为宗旨的公约。五大国和平公约必须保证五大国施行真正的和平政策，它与北大西洋公约截然不同，北大西洋公约由于内部的基本矛盾而涣散了，而且它是以否认各国主权的错误观念为基础，因此从根本上就是错误的。
