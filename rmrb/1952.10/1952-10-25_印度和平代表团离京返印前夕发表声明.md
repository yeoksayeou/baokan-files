Title: Indian Peace Delegation Issues Statement Before Leaving Beijing to Return to India
Author:
Date: 1952-10-25
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, October 24th: The Indian delegation attending the Asian and Pacific Regions Peace Conference issued the following statement on the eve of their departure from Beijing to return to India. All sixty members of the delegation, including official representatives, observers, guests, and staff, signed the statement.

"We, the members of the Indian delegation attending the just-concluded Asian and Pacific Regions Peace Conference in Beijing, salute the representatives of all other countries and congratulate them on their outstanding contributions to this great gathering representing 1.6 billion people worldwide.

"Every issue was first fully discussed in subcommittees and delegation meetings before being brought to the plenary session for discussion, ultimately becoming resolutions and declarations. Every representative made contributions to the resolutions of the conference.

"All representatives thoughtfully considered every possible viewpoint—no matter how different that viewpoint was from their own, and representatives strived for agreement on every word—all of this proves the true nature of the peace movement.

"Upon our return to India, we will immediately do our utmost to publicize the resolutions of this conference. We believe that these resolutions are in line with the highest interests and common aspirations of the people of the countries in the Asian and Pacific regions.

"We also wish to express our gratitude to the great Chinese people, whose hospitality deeply moved us. We are honored to have witnessed the tremendous progress the Chinese people have made on the road to progress, and their supremely joyful spirit and creativity have deeply impressed us.

"We are determined to use our utmost strength to promote the noble cause of friendship between India and China upon our return."

The following people signed the statement: Saifuddin Kitchlew, Gyan Chand, Romesh Chandra, Abdul Aleem, Banjajee Acharya, Jogindra Kumar Banerjee, Subodh Banerjee, Bijoy Bandopadhya, Rohini Bhad, Keddish Bouse, Monnojh Bouse, Birin Romesh Chandra, Monjusli Chattopadhya, De Silva, Disha, Nandral Dahl, Darla Duda, Trilochan Duda, Faridi, Gatubari, Sarala Gupta, Husen, Uma Shangar Joshi, Kahn, Brijh Rajh Jissor, Kadirga, Krishnaswamy, Ravi Shangar Shivram Vyas, Chatur Narain Malaviya, Balkrishna Mavlenka, Kapilaben Meda, Kumudini Meda, Arvind Meda, Amiya Mukerjee, Joseph Mondesari, Nadeem, Panjwani, Maganlal Balik, Selendra Nas Bauer, Ram Krishna Bati, Patnaik, Padak, Raghavvaihei, Amrit Leia, Reddy, Govind Sahai, Maharal Manilal Shah, Sharma, Shantilia, Huaji Shantistan, Yashwant Shukla, Shastri, Seth, Banerjee, Mrs. Banerjee, Prithvi Singh, Panigrahi, Trilok Singh, Nandi, Wubadaya.

### 印度和平代表团离京返印前夕发表声明

1952-10-25
第4版()
专栏：

    印度和平代表团离京返印前夕发表声明
    【新华社二十四日讯】出席亚洲及太平洋区域和平会议的印度代表团在离开北京返回印度的前夕，发表如下的声明。代表团全体团员六十人，包括正式代表、列席代表、来宾和工作人员，都在声明上签了字。
    “我们，出席刚刚在北京结束的亚洲及太平洋区域和平会议的印度代表团团员，向其他所有国家的代表们致敬，并庆贺他们对代表了世界上十六亿人民的这个伟大集会所作的卓越贡献。
    “每一个问题都是首先在小组委员会和代表团会议上经过充分讨论后，再提到全体会议上讨论，最后成为决议和宣言。每一位代表都对会议的决议作了若干贡献。
    “所有代表们对每一个可能的观点都加以深思熟虑——不论这个观点与自己的观点有多大的差异，代表们并竭力在每一个字句上获得协议——这一切都证明了和平运动的真正性质。
    “在我们回到印度以后，我们将马上尽我们的最大力量，宣传这个会议的决议。我们认为，这些决议符合亚洲及太平洋区域各国人民的最高利益和一致愿望。
    “我们还愿对伟大的中国人民表示感谢，他们的盛情招待使我们十分感动。我们荣幸地看到了中国人民在进步的道路上所作的巨大进展，他们的无上愉快的精神和他们的创造力，使我们得到很深的印象。
    “我们回去后决心用我们最大的力量来促进印度和中国之间的崇高的友好事业。”
    在声明上签名的有：赛福丁·克其鲁，杰安·昌德，罗米西·钱德拉，阿卜杜勒·阿勒姆，班佳紫·阿查里亚，卓金德拉·库玛·班那吉，苏波德·班那吉，比佐伊·班多巴帝亚，萝希妮·巴德，克迪希·鲍斯，蒙诺志·鲍斯，比琳·罗米西·钱德拉，蒙朱斯利·查托巴达雅，德锡尔瓦，迪沙，南德拉尔·达尔，达拉·杜达，特里洛昌·杜达，法里迪，加图巴里，撒拉·古普塔，胡森，乌玛·山加尔·约希，康恩，布立志·拉志·吉索尔，卡迪尔加，克里什纳斯瓦米，拉维·山加尔·史佛兰姆·维亚斯，查图尔·纳拉英·马拉维亚，巴尔克里什纳·马夫兰卡，卡比拉本·梅达，库穆丁妮·梅达，阿尔文德·梅达，阿米亚·穆克吉，约瑟夫·孟德萨里，纳丁姆，潘志瓦尼，马干拉尔·巴立克，赛伦德拉·纳斯·鲍尔，兰姆·克利什纳·巴蒂，帕特奈克，帕达克，拉格哈伐依黑，阿姆利特·莱亚，雷迪，高文德·萨海，马哈拉尔·马尼拉尔·沙阿，沙尔玛，山蒂利亚，华基·山蒂斯坦，雅士万特·舒克拉，沙斯特里，塞士，班纳吉，班纳吉夫人，普理斯维·辛格，潘尼格拉希，特里洛克·辛格，南第，吴巴代亚。
