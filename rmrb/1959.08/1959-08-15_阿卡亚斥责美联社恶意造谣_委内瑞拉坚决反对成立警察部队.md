Title: Acaya Condemns Associated Press for Maliciously Spreading Rumors; Venezuela Firmly Opposes Establishing a Police Force
Author:
Date: 1959-08-15
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, Santiago, August 13th. Venezuelan Foreign Minister Acaya condemned an American news agency at a press conference today for distorting his remarks in a dispatch to Caracas. The dispatch said that Venezuela supported the establishment of a Pan-American police force.

Acaya said that this was a complete fabrication. "On the contrary, I said that our country firmly opposes any police force or Caribbean supervisory committee. Such an organization would give moral support to the Trujillo dictatorship."

Acaya warned, "These news agencies should be careful because people are already accusing them of serving certain interest groups." The reporter from the American news agency Associated Press, who attended the press conference, admitted that he had sent the false news.

### 阿卡亚斥责美联社恶意造谣　委内瑞拉坚决反对成立警察部队

1959-08-15
第5版()
专栏：

　　阿卡亚斥责美联社恶意造谣
　　委内瑞拉坚决反对成立警察部队
    新华社圣地亚哥13日电　委内瑞拉外交部长阿卡亚今天在记者招待会上谴责一家美国通讯社在一则发往加拉加斯的电讯中颠倒黑白，歪曲他的发言。这则消息说，委内瑞拉支持成立泛美警察部队。
    阿卡亚说，这是彻头彻尾的捏造，“正相反，我说的是我国坚决反对（成立）任何警察部队或者加勒比地区监督委员会。这种组织会使特鲁希略独裁政权得到道义上的支持”。
    阿卡亚警告说，“这些通讯社应该当心些，因为人们已经在指责它们为某些利益集团效劳”。出席招待会的美国通讯社美联社的记者承认这条假消息是他发的。
