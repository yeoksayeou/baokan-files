Title: Public Opinion in Various Countries Continues to Welcome Soviet-U.S. Summit Visits, Believing That Improved Soviet-U.S. Relations Are Conducive to Peace
Author:
Date: 1959-08-10
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency dispatch: The decision of the Soviet and U.S. leaders to visit each other has continued to elicit enthusiastic reactions from official and public opinion in various countries.

Havana dispatch: Cuban President Dorticós said at a reception for foreign journalists on the evening of the 7th that the improvement of Soviet-U.S. relations is "what people desire," and this improvement shows the possibility of other countries establishing diplomatic and trade relations with the Soviet Union.

TASS dispatch from Montevideo: The news that Khrushchev and Eisenhower will visit each other remains the focus of attention for the Latin American public and newspapers. Newspapers believe that this event poses to Latin American politicians the need to re-examine their relations with the Soviet Union and other socialist countries. According to a report from Rio de Janeiro, Brazilian officials are actively discussing the issue of restoring diplomatic relations with the Soviet Union. A United Press International reporter reported from Rio de Janeiro that some officials in the Brazilian Foreign Ministry believe that taking this measure is necessary, given the impending exchange of visits between the leaders of the Soviet Union and the United States.

A commentary in Colombia's "El Tiempo" illustrates the mood of the power blocs in Latin America. The newspaper wrote, "The current situation presents a series of noteworthy and analyzable issues to Latin America." The newspaper believes that Latin America should change its attitude towards socialist countries. The newspaper said that Latin America must strive not to be left out of the course of events.

Bangkok dispatch: Thai Deputy Prime Minister Naradhip told the press on the 6th that the decision of Soviet Chairman of the Council of Ministers Khrushchev and U.S. President Eisenhower to visit each other will help ease international tensions and promote world peace.

Kuala Lumpur dispatch: The Government of the Federation of Malaya issued a statement on the 6th, saying, "Undoubtedly, this visit will give Mr. Khrushchev and Mr. Eisenhower an opportunity to exchange views, thereby creating an atmosphere that can promote a deeper understanding between the two major powers. People all over the world place their hopes for peace and security on this mutual understanding."

New Delhi dispatch: The Central Executive Committee of the Communist Party of India expressed warm welcome in a resolution on the 8th to the proposed mutual visits between Khrushchev and Eisenhower, and pointed out that this is "a contribution to the peace efforts made by the Soviet government."

The committee called on all Indians to participate in the worldwide movement to ensure the early convening and success of a summit conference.

Yemen's Sana'a Radio welcomed the upcoming mutual visits between Soviet Chairman of the Council of Ministers Khrushchev and U.S. President Eisenhower. It expressed hope in a commentary on the 4th that they would succeed in their meeting.

Beirut dispatch: "Al-Hawadess" commented on the 9th on the upcoming mutual visits between Khrushchev and Eisenhower, saying that this is a victory for the Soviet Union's peace policy and its consistent efforts to resolve international issues through negotiations. The newspaper also said that the mutual visits are "of great importance to the people of all countries because this is the result of the people's long struggle for peace and the easing of international tensions."

The newspaper pointed out that the growth of the strength of the socialist camp and the world's peace forces is a factor forcing the United States to agree to this important mutual visit.

Paris dispatch: The Executive Bureau of the General Confederation of Labor of France issued a communiqué expressing satisfaction with the upcoming meeting between Eisenhower and Khrushchev. The communiqué said that these meetings are in line with the desire of workers and people around the world for peace; the meetings will help peacefully resolve all international disputes, guarantee universal and simultaneous disarmament under supervision, and develop economic cooperation on an international scale, which will primarily benefit underdeveloped countries.

### 各国公众舆论继续欢迎苏美首脑互访  认为苏美关系改善有利于和平

1959-08-10
第4版()
专栏：

    各国公众舆论继续欢迎苏美首脑互访
    认为苏美关系改善有利于和平
    本报综合报道　据新华社讯：苏美两国领导人决定互相访问一事，继续引起各国官方和公众舆论的热烈反应。
    哈瓦那消息：古巴总统多尔蒂科斯在7日晚上的外国记者招待会上说，苏美关系的改善是“人们所渴望的”，这种改善表明了其他国家同苏联建立外交和贸易关系的可能性。
    据塔斯社蒙得维的亚讯　赫鲁晓夫和艾森豪威尔将相互访问的消息，依然是拉丁美洲公众和报纸注意的中心。报纸认为，这件事向拉丁美洲各国的政治家们提出：必须重新审查自己同苏联和其他社会主义国家的关系。据来自里约热内卢的消息，巴西官方人士正在积极讨论关于恢复同苏联外交关系的问题。合众国际社记者自里约热内卢报道，巴西外交部的一些负责人认为，由于苏美两国领导人即将互相访问，采取这一措施是必要的。
    哥伦比亚“时代报”的评论说明了拉丁美洲势力集团的心情。报纸写道，“当前的形势在拉丁美洲面前提出一系列值得注意和值得分析的问题”。这家报纸认为，拉丁美洲应该改变对社会主义国家的态度。报纸说，拉丁美洲必须努力，不要置身于事态进展之外。
    曼谷消息：泰国副总理那拉底6日向报界发表谈话说，苏联部长会议主席赫鲁晓夫和美国总统艾森豪威尔决定互相访问，将有助于缓和国际紧张局势和促进世界和平。
    吉隆坡消息：马来亚联合邦政府在6日发表声明说，“无可怀疑，这次访问将使赫鲁晓夫先生和艾森豪威尔先生两人有机会交换意见，从而产生可以促进这两个大国之间更深切了解的气氛，全世界人民都把他们对和平和安全的希望寄托在这种互相了解上面”。
    新德里消息：印度共产党中央执行委员会8日在一项决议中表示热烈欢迎赫鲁晓夫和艾森豪威尔的建议中的互相访问，并且指出，这是“苏联政府所作的和平努力的贡献。”
    委员会呼吁全体印度人参加世界性的运动，以确保最高级会议的尽早召开和获得成功。
    也门的萨那电台欢迎苏联部长会议主席赫鲁晓夫和美国总统艾森豪威尔即将举行的互相访问。它在4日发表评论表示希望他们在会晤中获得成功。
    贝鲁特消息：“消息周报”9日就赫鲁晓夫和艾森豪威尔即将互相访问这件事发表评论说，这是苏联和平政策的胜利，也是它始终不渝地为通过谈判来解决国际问题而进行的努力的胜利。报纸还说，互相访问
  “对于各国人民具有很大的重要性，因为这是人民为和平和缓和国际紧张局势长期斗争的结果”。
    报纸指出，社会主义阵营力量和全世界和平力量的壮大是迫使美国同意这个重要的互相访问的因素。
    巴黎消息：法国总工会执行局发表公报，对艾森豪威尔和赫鲁晓夫即将会晤一事表示满意。公报说，这些会晤是符合全世界劳动者和人民对和平的愿望的；会晤将有助于和平解决一切国际争端，保障普遍而同时又是有监督的裁军和发展具有国际规模的经济合作，这种合作将首先对不发达国家有利。
