Title: US Stock Prices Fall Again
Author:
Date: 1953-06-08
Page: 4

    US Stock Prices Fall Again
    [Xinhua News Agency, June 6th, dispatch from New York] Due to rumors that the Korean armistice negotiations are about to reach an agreement, US stock market prices have fallen sharply twice in the past week. According to news agency reports, the two declines on June 1st and 4th caused the average stock price to fall by $2.02. Industrial stocks fell the most, a total of $3.96.
    On the 4th, the stocks of many large companies fell again, ranging from $2 to $3 per share. For example, Chrysler's stock fell $2.25 per share; DuPont fell $1.50; Monsanto Chemical fell $2.50; General Electric fell $1; and American Smelting and Refining Company fell $1.75.
    Associated Press news pointed out that investors are generally waiting to see further developments in the Korean armistice negotiations.

### 美股票价格再度下跌

1953-06-08
第4版()
专栏：

    美股票价格再度下跌
    【新华社六日讯】纽约消息：由于传闻朝鲜停战谈判快要达成协议，美国股票市场一周来价格两次猛跌。据通讯社消息，六月一日和四日的两次跌落使股票价格平均跌落二元零二分。工业股票跌得最多，共跌落三元九角六分。
    四日，许多大公司的股票都再度下跌，从每股二元到三元不等。例如克莱斯勒公司的股票每股下跌二又四分之一元；杜邦公司下跌一·五元；孟山都化学公司下跌二·五元；通用电气公司下跌一元；美国铅公司下跌一元七角五分。
    美联社消息指出，投资者普遍观望朝鲜停战谈判的进一步发展。
