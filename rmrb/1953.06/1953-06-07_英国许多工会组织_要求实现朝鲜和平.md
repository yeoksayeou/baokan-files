Title: Many British Trade Unions Demand Peace in Korea
Author:
Date: 1953-06-07
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, Prague, June 6th: The London "Daily Worker" reported on June 4th that thousands of people in Yorkshire, England, have signed appeals calling for peace in Korea and a Five-Power Conference.

The newspaper continued, saying that many trade unions have passed resolutions making this demand in the past ten days. These trade unions include: the Bradford Trades Council, the National Union of Mineworkers, the Hull Shop Assistants and Delivery Workers Union, the Leeds Shop Assistants and Delivery Workers Union, the Rotherham Fourth Mixed Machine Union, the Sheffield Ninth and Twenty-first Mixed Machine Unions, the Leeds 47th Branch of the National Union of Furniture Trade Operatives, the Leeds Third Branch of the National Union of Tailors and Garment Workers, the Hull Tenth to Forty-fifth Transport and General Workers' Union, and the National Union of Railwaymen.

### 英国许多工会组织  要求实现朝鲜和平

1953-06-07
第4版()
专栏：

    英国许多工会组织
    要求实现朝鲜和平
    【新华社布拉格六日电】伦敦“工人日报”六月四日报道说，英国约克郡已有数千人在要求朝鲜和平和五大国会议的呼吁书上签了名。
    该报继续说，十天来有许多工会通过决议，提出这一要求。这些工会有：布拉德福德工会理事会，全国矿工联盟，赫尔店员与送货工人联合会，里子店员与送货工人联合会，罗塞翰第四混合机器工会，设菲尔德第九和第二十一混合机器工会，全国家具业职工联盟里子第四十七分会，全国成衣工人联合会里子第三分会，赫尔第十—四十五运输与普通工人工会，以及全国铁路工人工会。
