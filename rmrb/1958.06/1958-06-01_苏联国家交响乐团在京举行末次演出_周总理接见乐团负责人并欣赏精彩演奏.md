Title: Soviet National Symphony Orchestra Holds Final Performance in Beijing; Premier Zhou Enlai Receives Orchestra Leaders and Enjoys Splendid Performance
Author: 
Date: 1958-06-01
Page: 2
Xinhua News Agency, May 31st: The Soviet National Symphony Orchestra, together with China's Central Philharmonic Orchestra and the Capital United Orchestra of the Chinese People's Liberation Army, gave a grand joint performance at the Beijing Gymnasium this evening. This performance was also the final performance of the Soviet National Symphony Orchestra in Beijing.
    At the beginning of the concert, Premier Zhou Enlai, along with more than 6,000 audience members in the capital, stood up and applauded for a long time, warmly welcoming the wonderful performance of the Soviet artists. Also present at this evening's concert were Peng Zhen, Vice Chairman of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress and Mayor of Beijing; Ding Xilin and Qu Wu, Deputy Directors of the Commission for Cultural Relations with Foreign Countries; Liu Zhiming and Xia Yan, Vice Ministers of Culture; Chu Tunan, President of the Chinese People's Association for Cultural Relations with Foreign Countries; and Yang Hansheng, Vice President of the same association.
    Bobrovnikov, head of the Moscow City Workers' Soviet Delegation, and Antonov, Chargé d'Affaires ad interim of the Soviet Embassy in China, also attended the concert.
    The grand symphony orchestra, composed of approximately 300 musicians from both the Soviet Union and China, was conducted this evening by Anosov, the head of the Soviet National Symphony Orchestra; Ivanov, the chief conductor of the orchestra and a People's Artist of the Soviet Union; and Zhang Ning and Li Delun, conductors of China's Central Philharmonic Orchestra. A total of ten pieces were performed. The concert began with Beethoven's "Egmont" Overture and concluded with Tchaikovsky's "1812" Overture. Anosov and Ivanov also specially conducted four pieces by Chinese composers Ma Sicong and He Luting.
    The musicians of both countries cooperated and coordinated perfectly during the two-and-a-half-hour performance this evening. Every piece they performed was greeted with thunderous applause from the audience.
    During the intermission of the performance, Premier Zhou Enlai received Anosov, the head of the Soviet National Symphony Orchestra, Ivanov, the chief conductor, and other leaders of the orchestra, and had cordial conversations with them.
    At the end of the entire performance, Zhou Enlai, Peng Zhen, Bobrovnikov, Antonov, Ding Xilin, Qu Wu, Xia Yan, Liu Zhiming, Chu Tunan, Yang Hansheng, and others, walked into the orchestra pit in the gymnasium hall, warmly shook hands with the Soviet artists, and thanked them for their highly skilled artistic performance. The Chinese music community presented the Soviet musicians with a finely crafted violin produced in Beijing. At this moment, enthusiastic applause resounded through the entire hall again.
    The Soviet National Symphony Orchestra performed a total of fourteen performances in Beijing for more than 30,000 audience members, each performance being completely sold out and warmly welcomed by the capital's audience.

### 苏联国家交响乐团在京举行末次演出  周总理接见乐团负责人并欣赏精彩演奏

1958-06-01
第2版()
专栏：

    苏联国家交响乐团在京举行末次演出
    周总理接见乐团负责人并欣赏精彩演奏
    据新华社31日讯　苏联国家交响乐团同我国中央乐团和中国人民解放军首都联合乐团，今晚在北京体育馆举行了一次盛大的联合演出。这次演出也是苏联国家交响乐团在北京的最后一场演出。
    音乐会开始时，周恩来总理同六千多首都听众一道，长时间地起立鼓掌，热烈欢迎苏联艺术家们的精彩演奏。出席今晚音乐会的，还有副委员长、北京市市长彭真，对外文化联络委员会副主任丁西林、屈武，文化部副部长刘芝明、夏衍，中国人民对外文化协会会长楚图南、副会长阳翰笙等人。
    莫斯科市劳动者代表苏维埃代表团团长波布罗夫尼科夫，苏联驻华临时代办安东诺夫也出席了音乐会。
    由苏中两国音乐家约三百人组成的盛大交响乐队，今晚由苏联国家交响乐团团长阿诺索夫，乐团总指挥、苏联人民演员伊万诺夫，我国中央乐团指挥张宁和与李德伦分别担任指挥，总共演出了十个节目。音乐会以贝多芬作曲的“爱格蒙特”序曲开始，最后演出了柴可夫斯基的“一八一二”序曲。阿诺索夫和伊万诺夫还特地指挥演出了中国作曲家马思聪和贺绿汀的四首乐曲。
    两国音乐家在今晚两个半小时的演奏中合作配合得非常完美。他们演出的每一个节目，都博得了全场听众雷鸣般的掌声。
    周恩来总理在演出休息时接见苏联国家交响乐团团长阿诺索夫、总指挥伊万诺夫和乐团的其他负责人，同他们进行亲切的交谈。
    整个演出结束时，周恩来、彭真、波布罗夫尼科夫、安东诺夫、丁西林、屈武、夏衍、刘芝明、楚图南、阳翰笙等人，一道走进体育馆大厅的演奏池内，同苏联艺术家们热烈握手，感谢他们具有高度水平的艺术表演。中国音乐界把北京出产的一把精制小提琴赠送给苏联音乐家。这时，整个大厅再次响起经久不息的热烈掌声。
    苏联国家交响乐团在北京总共为三万多听众演出了十四场，每一场都全部客满，受到首都听众热烈的欢迎。
