Title: Moroccan King Replies to de Gaulle: Satisfying Algerian National Aspirations is Key to Improving Relations Between France and North Africa
Author:
Date: 1958-06-05
Page: 7

Xinhua News Agency, Paris, June 4th: Moroccan King Mohammed V wrote to the new French Prime Minister de Gaulle on June 3rd, stating that “after satisfying the national aspirations of the Algerian people and establishing peace in Algeria,” relations between France and North African countries could be improved.

The Moroccan King's letter was a reply to de Gaulle's letter to him on June 2nd. In his letter, de Gaulle expressed his hope to improve relations between the two countries.

It is reported that Moroccan government officials rarely comment on the prospects of Franco-Moroccan relations. However, a spokesman for the Moroccan Prime Minister's Office in Rabat pointed out on June 3rd that the Moroccan government hopes that de Gaulle will soon be able to address the issues of concern to the Moroccan government, the primary issue being the withdrawal of French troops stationed in Morocco.

### 摩洛哥国王复信戴高乐说  满足阿尔及利亚民族愿望  法国和北非关系才能改善

1958-06-05
第7版()
专栏：

    摩洛哥国王复信戴高乐说
    满足阿尔及利亚民族愿望  法国和北非关系才能改善
    新华社4日讯　巴黎消息：摩洛哥国王穆罕默德五世3日写信给法国新总理戴高乐说：“在满足阿尔及利亚人民的民族愿望和在阿尔及利亚建立和平以后”，法国和北非国家的关系就能够得到改善。
    摩洛哥国王的这封信是答复戴高乐2日写给他的信的。戴高乐在他的信中表示希望改善两国之间的关系。
    据报道，摩洛哥政府人士很少就法—摩关系的前景发表评论。但是摩洛哥首相府发言人3日在拉巴特指出，摩洛哥政府希望戴高乐将能很快地着手处理摩洛哥政府所关心的问题，其中首要问题是法国撤退驻摩洛哥军队的问题。
