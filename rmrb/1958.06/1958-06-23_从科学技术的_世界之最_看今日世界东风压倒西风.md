From Scientific and Technological "World's Best" to See Today's World: The East Wind Prevails Over the West Wind

    The latest issue of the Soviet magazine "Young Technician" published an article entitled "Miracles of Nature and Technology." The article listed some "world's best" materials in science and technology. From this, it can be seen that the Soviet Union is in a leading position in world science and technology, and the East wind prevails over the West wind. The following is a categorized forwarding:

    World's Largest Synchrophasotron: The synchrophasotron located at the Joint Institute for Nuclear Research in Dubna, USSR, has a power of one million electron volts.

    World's Largest Artificial Satellites: The first and second satellites launched by the Soviet Union in 1957 (Xinhua News Agency note: Now the third satellite recently launched by the Soviet Union should be added).

    World's First Civilian Nuclear Ship: The Soviet "Lenin" nuclear icebreaker, launched in 1957.

    World's Largest Cargo Helicopter: The Soviet "Mi-6" helicopter, manufactured in 1957.

    World's Largest Passenger Aircraft: The Soviet "Tu-104" jet aircraft, "Tu-114" and "Il-18" turboprop aircraft manufactured in 1956-1957. Turboprop aircraft are comfortable and convenient, and can carry 200 passengers on intercontinental routes.

    World's Longest Railway: The Siberian Railway in the Soviet Union, from Chelyabinsk to Vladivostok, is 7,416 kilometers long and was built from 1891 to 1916.

    World's Highest Mountain Railway: Built in the Andes Mountains between Chile (Antofagasta) and Argentina (Salta). The railway reaches an altitude of 3,858 meters when passing through the Socompa Pass.

    World's Northernmost Railway: Some Soviet railways, including the railway lines between Dudinka on the Yenisei River and the metallurgical city of Norilsk within the Arctic Circle, and between Murmansk and Pechenga on the Kola Peninsula.

    World's Most Comfortable Underground Tramway: The Moscow Underground Tramway in the Soviet Union, the first line of which opened in 1935.

    World's Highest Mountain Highway: The Chinese Xining-Lhasa and Lhasa-Chengdu highways, both of which pass through the Tibetan Plateau as high as 5,330 meters above sea level.

    World's Longest Bridge: The highway bridge over Lake Pontchartrain in the United States (in Louisiana), is 39 kilometers long and was completed in 1956.

    World's Longest Suspension Bridge: The Golden Gate Bridge in the United States, passing through the strait of the Golden Gate Harbor. The bridge is 1,620 meters long. (According to Xinhua News Agency)

### 从科学技术的“世界之最”看今日世界东风压倒西风

1958-06-23
第5版()
专栏：

    从科学技术的“世界之最”看今日世界东风压倒西风
    苏联最近一期“青年技术”杂志发表了一篇题为“自然和技术奇迹”的文章。文章中列举了关于科学技术方面的一些“世界之最”的材料。从这里可以看出，苏联在世界科学技术方面的领先地位，东风压倒西风。兹分类转发如下：
    世界最大的同步稳相加速器：设于苏联杜布纳的联合原子核研究所的同步稳相加速器，功率为一百万电子伏特。
    世界最大的人造卫星：苏联1957年发射的第一个与第二个卫星（新华社注：现在应当加上苏联新近发射的第三个卫星）。
    世界上第一艘民用原子船：苏联“列宁号”原子破冰船，于1957年下水。
    世界最大的货运直升飞机：苏联1957年制造的“米—6”式直升飞机。
    世界最大的客运飞机：苏联在1956—1957年制造的“图—104”式喷气式飞机、“图—114”式和“伊尔—18”式涡轮喷气螺旋桨飞机。涡轮喷气螺旋桨飞机舒适方便，在洲际航线上可运载二百名旅客。
    世界最长的铁路：苏联西伯利亚大铁道，从车里雅宾斯克起至海参崴，全长七千四百一十六公里，建于1891年到1916年。
    世界最高的高山铁路：建筑在智利（安图法加斯达）和阿根廷（萨尔塔）之间的安达斯山脉中。铁路在经过绍科姆帕山隘时高度达到海拔三千八百五十八公尺。
    世界上最北的铁路：苏联的一些铁路，包括叶尼塞河上的都丁卡和北极圈内的冶金城诺里尔斯克之间以及科拉半岛上的摩尔曼斯克和彼琴加之间的铁路支线。
    世界上最舒适的地下电车道：苏联莫斯科地下电车道，第一条线路于1935年通车。
    世界最高的高山公路：中国西宁—拉萨和拉萨—成都公路，这两条公路都经过高达海拔五千三百三十公尺的西藏高原。
    世界最长的桥：美国庞恰尔特雷恩湖上的公路桥（在路易斯安那州），全长三十九公里，1956年建成。
    世界上跨距最长的桥：美国的金门桥，经过金门港口的海峡。桥长一千六百二十公尺。（据新华社讯）
