Title: US-British Talks Yield No Results; Macmillan Returns Home Empty-Handed
Author:
Date: 1958-06-16
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, London, June 14th: British Prime Minister Macmillan has returned to London empty-handed after a week-long visit to the United States and Canada.

American columnist Walter Lippmann confirmed in an article in today's "New York Herald Tribune" that the US-British summit did not make any decisions. The US government remained noncommittal to the three remedies proposed by Britain to overcome Western economic difficulties, which are: guaranteeing the reserves of the sterling area, expanding the capital reserves of the International Monetary Fund, and raising the price of gold.

The two countries also failed to reach an agreement on exchanging atomic secrets and halting nuclear tests. When reporters asked Macmillan about this aspect upon his return to London, he said that it was not his place to comment on the attitude of the United States.

### 美英会谈没有结果  麦克米伦空手返国

1958-06-16
第6版()
专栏：

    美英会谈没有结果
    麦克米伦空手返国
    据新华社伦敦14日电　英国首相麦克米伦在美国和加拿大访问了一星期后空手回到了伦敦。
    美国专栏作家李普曼今天在“纽约先驱论坛报”上所写的文章中证实，美英首脑会谈没有作出任何决定，美国政府对英国所提出的克服西方经济困难的三种补救办法未置可否，这三种办法就是：保证英镑地区的储备、扩大国际货币基金组织的资本储备和提高黄金价格。
    两国在交换原子秘密和停止核试验方面也没有取得协议。当报界在麦克米伦回到伦敦时问起他这方面的问题时，他说，对美国的态度发表意见不是他的事。
