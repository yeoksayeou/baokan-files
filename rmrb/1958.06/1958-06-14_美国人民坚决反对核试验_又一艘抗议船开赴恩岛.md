Title: The American People Firmly Oppose Nuclear Testing; Another Protest Ship Sails to Eniwetok
Author:
Date: 1958-06-14
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, June 13th, New York dispatch: American Reynold set sail from Honolulu on the evening of the 11th aboard a fifty-foot schooner for the Eniwetok Atoll region in the Pacific to protest the U.S. government's nuclear weapons testing there.

Reynold, formerly a professor of anthropology, was accompanied on this trip to Eniwetok by his wife and children, as well as a Japanese person.

Reynold had previously sent a telegram to U.S. President Eisenhower demanding an end to nuclear weapons testing and protesting the U.S. government's declaration of a large area of the high seas as a danger zone.

### 美国人民坚决反对核试验  又一艘抗议船开赴恩岛

1958-06-14
第5版()
专栏：

    美国人民坚决反对核试验
    又一艘抗议船开赴恩岛
    据新华社13日讯　纽约消息：美国人雷诺兹11日晚上乘坐一只五十英尺长的双桅船从檀香山开往太平洋恩纳威托克地区，抗议美国政府在那里进行核武器试验。
    雷诺兹以前是一个人类学教授，这次同他一起出发去恩纳威托克的还有他的妻子和儿女，另外还有一个日本人。
    雷诺兹曾经在一封给美国总统艾森豪威尔的电报中要求停止核武器试验，并且对美国政府宣布一大片公海地区为危险区提出抗议。
