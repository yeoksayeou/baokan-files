Title: Machines Answer Scientific Questions: The Soviet Union Completes First Step
Author:
Date: 1958-06-20
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, Moscow, June 19th. When solving scientific and engineering problems, is it possible to avoid searching through vast libraries for reference books, and instead check a certain number or physical formula, and find all aspects of materials related to a scientific and technical problem? The two problem-solving machines exhibited at the All-Soviet Industrial Exhibition provide an affirmative answer. They show that Soviet scientists have completed the first step in this area. As long as you make a request to this machine, what you need will appear on its fluorescent screen.

It is a very knowledgeable scientific consultant. It has a special logic and linguistics system, as well as a unique permanent "memory" system. With this system, any book can be recorded in code. The machine uses this system to "read" books. Currently, each machine can only answer eight questions.

### 用机器解答科学问题  苏联完成第一步工作

1958-06-20
第5版()
专栏：

    用机器解答科学问题
    苏联完成第一步工作
    据新华社莫斯科19日电　在解决科学和工程问题时，是否能不到浩如烟海的书库中翻阅参考书，而能查对某个数字或物理公式、找出有关某个科学、技术问题的各方面的材料呢？目前在全苏工业展览会上展出的两部解答问题的机器作出了肯定答复。它们表明，苏联科学家在这方面已经完成了第一步工作。只要你向这种机器提出要求，它的萤光屏上就会出现你所需要的东西。
    它是非常博学的科学顾问。它有一套特殊的逻辑学和语言学系统，还有一套独一无二的永久起作用的“记忆”系统。用这种系统可以把任何书籍以电码形式纪录下来。机器就用这种系统“阅读”书籍。目前每个机器还只能回答八个问题。
