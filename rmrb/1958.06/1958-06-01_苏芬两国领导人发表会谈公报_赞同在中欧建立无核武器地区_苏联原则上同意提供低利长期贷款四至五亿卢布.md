Title: Soviet and Finnish Leaders Issue Joint Communiqué, Support Establishing a Nuclear-Weapon-Free Zone in Central Europe, Soviet Union Principally Agrees to Provide Low-Interest Long-Term Loan of 400-500 Million Rubles
Author:
Date: 1958-06-01
Page: 3

Xinhua News Agency, May 31st dispatch from Moscow: According to TASS, a Soviet-Finnish communiqué was released in Moscow on May 30th, following talks between Finnish President Kekkonen and his entourage with Soviet Supreme Soviet Presidium Chairman Voroshilov, Council of Ministers Chairman Khrushchev, and other leaders.

The communiqué stated that both sides are convinced that reaching an agreement on the immediate and universal cessation of atomic and hydrogen weapon tests would be an important step on the road to solving the disarmament issue and would help reach the necessary agreement on the final and unconditional prohibition of the aforementioned weapons.

The Soviet and Finnish leaders support the establishment of a nuclear weapon-free and rocket weapon-free zone in Central Europe. Both sides believe that the People's Republic of China must obtain its rightful and legitimate seat in the United Nations.

The Finnish side expressed its agreement with the Soviet side's statement that the Soviet Union strives to further strengthen friendly ties and mutual understanding with other Nordic countries. To this end, the Soviet Union believes that continuing to jointly seek mutually acceptable modes of cooperation with them is beneficial. At the same time, the Soviet Union expressed its willingness to study in the spirit of utmost goodwill various proposals that other countries may put forward to strengthen peace in Northern Europe.

The communiqué pointed out that both countries' leaders expressed satisfaction during the talks with the very good and sincere good-neighborly relations existing between the Soviet Union and Finland. Both sides reiterated that they will continue to work to develop this good-neighborly relationship and make every effort to strengthen mutual understanding and trust between the two peoples.

Based on Finland's initiative, the two sides agreed to conclude a new exchange of goods agreement for the period from 1961 to 1965.

The Soviet side principally agreed to provide Finland with a long-term loan at a low interest rate, totaling 400 to 500 million rubles. Finland will use these loans to further develop the productivity of the Finnish economy and ensure employment.

Based on Finland's initiative, the two sides also discussed the issue of the most effective utilization by Finland of the Saimaa Canal, which connects the Saimaa Lake system with the Gulf of Finland, and various possible solutions. The Soviet Union expressed its willingness to reach an agreement on transferring the Soviet portion of the canal to Finland for use for fifty years as a passage on terms favorable to Finland.

Xinhua News Agency, May 31st dispatch from Moscow: According to TASS, more than two thousand working people and representatives from all walks of life in Moscow held a Soviet-Finnish People's Friendship Conference in the Grand Kremlin Palace on May 30th to welcome Finnish President Kekkonen.

President Kekkonen and Soviet leaders Voroshilov, Khrushchev, and others attended the conference.

Voroshilov spoke at the conference. He pointed out that the policy of mutual trust and friendship between the Soviet Union and Finland has a positive impact on relations among the Nordic countries. He said that the efforts of the Scandinavian countries to consolidate peace should be welcomed.

In his speech, Kekkonen pointed out that Finland believes that the good-neighborly relations between Finland and the Soviet Union and the friendship between the two peoples are of great significance. The entire Finnish people firmly hope to maintain friendly and good-neighborly relations with the Soviet Union and make unremitting efforts for peace and mutual understanding among the peoples of the world.

Another report: Finnish President Kekkonen and his entourage left Moscow on the morning of May 31st to return home. President Kekkonen visited the Soviet Union for ten days, visiting Moscow, Leningrad, Sverdlovsk, and Tashkent.

### 苏芬两国领导人发表会谈公报  赞同在中欧建立无核武器地区  苏联原则上同意提供低利长期贷款四至五亿卢布

1958-06-01
第3版()
专栏：

　　苏芬两国领导人发表会谈公报
    赞同在中欧建立无核武器地区
    苏联原则上同意提供低利长期贷款四至五亿卢布
    据新华社31日讯　据塔斯社莫斯科讯：在芬兰总统吉科宁和他的随行人员同苏联最高苏维埃主席团主席伏罗希洛夫、部长会议主席赫鲁晓夫和其他领导人进行会谈后，5月30日在莫斯科发表了苏联—芬兰公报。
    公报说，双方确信，就立即普遍停止原子武器和氢武器试验问题达成协议将是解决裁军问题道路上的一个重要步骤，将有助于达成关于最后和无条件地禁止上述武器的必要协议。
    苏芬两国领导人赞成在中欧建立无核武器和无火箭武器地区。双方认为，必须让中华人民共和国在联合国取得它应有的合法席位。
    芬兰方面表示赞同苏联方面的如下声明：苏联力求同其他北欧国家进一步加强友好联系和互相了解，为此，苏联认为继续同它们共同谋求相互可以接受的合作方式是有益的。同时，苏联表示愿意本着最善意的精神研究其他国家为了加强北欧和平而可能提出的各项建议。
    公报指出，两国领导人在谈话中对苏芬两国间存在的非常良好和真挚的善邻关系感到满意。双方重申，将继续努力发展这种善邻关系，并全力加强两国人民之间的相互谅解和信任。
    根据芬兰的倡议，双方商定缔结一项1961年到1965年的新的换货协定。
    苏联方面原则上同意向芬兰提供利率不大的长期贷款，总额为四亿到五亿卢布。芬兰将利用这些贷款进一步发展芬兰经济的生产率和保证就业。
    根据芬兰的倡议，双方还讨论了关于由芬兰最有效地利用连接塞马湖系统与芬兰湾的塞马运河的问题以及各种可能的解决办法。苏联表示愿意达成关于在有利于芬兰的条件下把运河的苏联部分交给芬兰利用五十年作为通路的协议。
    据新华社31日讯　塔斯社莫斯科讯：为欢迎芬兰总统吉科宁，莫斯科劳动人民和各界代表两千多人5月30日在大克里姆林宫举行了苏芬人民友好大会。
    吉科宁总统和苏联领导人伏罗希洛夫、赫鲁晓夫等出席了大会。
    伏罗希洛夫在会上讲话。他指出，苏芬两国之间相互信任和友好的政策对北欧各国之间的关系有着良好的影响。他说，应当欢迎斯堪的纳维亚各国为巩固和平所作的努力。
    吉科宁在讲话时指出，芬兰认为，芬苏两国的睦邻关系和两国人民间的友好具有非常重大的意义。全体芬兰人民坚定不移地希望同苏联保持友好和睦邻关系，并且不懈地为和平和全世界各国人民的互相谅解而努力。
    另讯　芬兰总统吉科宁和随行人员31日上午离莫斯科回国。吉科宁总统在苏联访问了十天，他访问了莫斯科、列宁格勒、斯维德洛夫斯克和塔什干。
