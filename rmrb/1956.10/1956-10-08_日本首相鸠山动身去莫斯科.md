Title: Japanese Prime Minister Hatoyama Departs for Moscow
Author:
Date: 1956-10-08
Page: 5
Xinhua News Agency, Tokyo, 7th. Japanese Prime Minister Ichiro Hatoyama, Minister of Agriculture and Forestry Ichiro Kono, and others left Tokyo by plane at 9:00 PM tonight for Moscow to conduct negotiations on the normalization of Japanese-Soviet relations.

　　Five thousand people went to Tokyo's Haneda Airport to see off Ichiro Hatoyama. They held up banners saying "Congratulations to the delegation for their success" and shouted cheers and "banzai". Hatoyama, beaming, walked towards the plane and made a brief speech, expressing his confidence that he would return successfully.

　　Hatoyama is scheduled to arrive in Moscow on the 12th.
　　Xinhua News Agency, Tokyo, 7th. The Japan-Soviet Trade Association wrote a letter to Japanese Prime Minister Ichiro Hatoyama on October 5, requesting him to make special efforts to expand trade between Japan and the Soviet Union during his visit to Moscow.

　　The Japan-Soviet Trade Association hopes that the trade volume between Japan and the Soviet Union will reach $76 million each in the first year, and will increase to $160 million each within five years.

### 日本首相鸠山动身去莫斯科

1956-10-08
第5版()
专栏：

    日本首相鸠山动身去莫斯科
    新华社7日讯　东京消息：日本首相鸠山一郎和农林相河野一郎等今天晚上九时乘飞机离开东京，前往莫斯科进行关于日苏关系正常化的谈判。
    有五千人到东京羽田机场欢送鸠山一郎。他们举着“祝谈判代表团成功”的标语，并且发出欢呼声和万岁声。鸠山满面笑容地走向飞机，他发表简短的谈话，表示确信他将成功归来。
    鸠山预订在12日到达莫斯科。
    新华社7日讯　东京消息：日苏贸易协会10月5日写信给日本首相鸠山一郎，要求他在访问莫斯科的时候作出特殊的努力，来扩大日本和苏联之间的贸易。
    日苏贸易协会希望，日苏两国的贸易额在第一年每方各达七千六百万美元，而在五年之内将增加到每方各达一亿六千万美元。
