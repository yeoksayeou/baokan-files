Title: Egypt, Syria, and Other Arab Countries Protest French Atrocities, Demand Immediate Release of Five Algerian National Leaders
Author:
Date: 1956-10-26
Page: 6
　　
    Xinhua News Agency, October 25th, Cairo Dispatch from TASS: Cairo Radio broadcast a statement by the Egyptian government regarding the arrest of five leaders of the Algerian national independence movement.

    The statement said: "The Egyptian government deeply regrets the strong actions taken by the French authorities against the Algerian leaders. Such measures not only violate the principles of morality and etiquette, but are also perfidious means commonly used by imperialism. This action by France violates the basic principles of international law as well as the international aviation conventions it has itself signed."

    The statement said: "The Egyptian government believes that all peoples have the right to live freely and to self-determination, and that the Algerian people have the right to strive for liberation and independence. The Egyptian government declares that it firmly condemns this indecent method of struggle against the wishes of the people."

    Xinhua News Agency, October 25th, Damascus Dispatch: Syrian Prime Minister Sabri al-Asali accused the French authorities of kidnapping five leaders of the "Algerian National Liberation Front" as "a challenge to the entire Arab nation."

    A statement issued by the Prime Minister's Office denounced France's actions as "violating all international law."

    A joint committee of Syrian political parties and religious organizations decided on the 24th to call on all Arab countries to hold a one-hour general strike on the 27th to protest France's kidnapping.

    Xinhua News Agency, October 25th, Baghdad Dispatch: The government of the Kingdom of Iraq protested the French authorities' arrest of five leaders of the "Algerian National Liberation Front" and demanded their immediate release.

    This protest was lodged by Iraqi Foreign Minister Burhanuddin Bashayan when he met with the French Chargé d'Affaires on October 24.

    On the 24th, the Iraqi Foreign Minister also met with the ambassadors of the United States and Britain to inform them of Iraq's protest against France's actions. The Iraqi Foreign Minister said that the governments of the United States and Britain should try to persuade France to release the leaders of the Algerian national independence movement and to cease the hostile actions taken against the Algerians who are fighting for freedom and independence.

    Xinhua News Agency, October 25th, Tripoli Dispatch from TASS: According to the "Tarabulus al-Gharb" newspaper, Libyan Prime Minister Ben Halim protested to the French Minister to Tripoli regarding the arrest of five leaders of the Algerian national liberation movement by the French authorities.

    Ben Halim emphasized that the Libyan government firmly demands the immediate release of those arrested.

### 埃叙等阿拉伯国家抗议法国暴行  要求立即释放五位阿尔及利亚民族领袖

1956-10-26
第6版()
专栏：

    埃叙等阿拉伯国家抗议法国暴行
    要求立即释放五位阿尔及利亚民族领袖
    新华社25日讯　据塔斯社开罗25日讯：开罗电台广播了埃及政府就阿尔及利亚民族独立运动的五名领导人被捕事件所发表的声明。
    声明说：“法国当局对阿尔及利亚领导人所采取的强力行动，使埃及政府深感遗憾。这种措施不仅违背道义和礼仪的原则，而且是帝国主义所惯用的背信弃义的手段。法国的这种行动违反了国际法的基本准则以及它自己也签署的国际航空公约。”
    声明说：“埃及政府认为各国人民有自由生存和自决的权利，认为阿尔及利亚人民有争取解放和独立的权利。埃及政府声明它坚决谴责对人民的愿望采取这种不体面的斗争方式。”
    新华社25日讯　大马士革消息：叙利亚总理萨布里·阿萨利指责法国当局绑架“阿尔及利亚民族解放阵线”五个领袖的行为是“对整个阿拉伯民族的挑战”。
    总理办公厅发表一项声明，抨击法国的行为“违反了全部国际法”。
    叙利亚各政党和宗教团体组织的一个联合委员会24日决定，号召一切阿拉伯国家在27日举行一小时的总罢工，抗议法国的绑架行动。
    新华社25日讯　巴格达消息：伊拉克王国政府抗议法国当局逮捕“阿尔及利亚民族解放阵线”的五个领袖，并且要求立即释放他们。
    这一抗议是由伊拉克外交大臣布哈努丁·巴沙扬在10月24日约见法国临时代办的时候提出的。
    伊拉克外交大臣在24日还约见了美国和英国的大使，把伊拉克对法国行为的抗议通知他们。伊拉克外交大臣说，美国和英国的政府应该设法使法国释放阿尔及利亚的民族独立运动领袖并且停止对为自由和独立而进行斗争的阿尔及利亚人所采取的敌对行为。
    新华社25日讯　塔斯社的黎波里24日讯：据“塔拉勃卢斯加布报”报道，利比亚首相本·哈利姆就法国当局逮捕阿尔及利亚民族解放运动五位领袖一事向法国驻的黎波里公使提出抗议。
    本·哈利姆强调指出，利比亚政府坚决要求立即释放被逮捕的人。
