Title: Soviet Government Accepts Invitation to Send Representatives to Observe US Elections
Author:
Date: 1956-10-23
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, Moscow, 22nd: The Soviet government has accepted the invitation of the US government and decided to send three representatives to the United States to learn about the US election campaign currently underway. These representatives left Moscow for the United States on October 21. They are: Solovyov, Deputy Chairman of the All-Union Central Council of Trade Unions and representative of the Supreme Soviet of the Soviet Union; Kudryavtsev, reporter and member of the editorial board of "Izvestia"; and Rubinstein, Doctor of Economics.

The US government's invitation was made in a note to the Soviet Ministry of Foreign Affairs on September 21 this year. In this note, the US government also hoped that the Soviet government would invite US representatives to visit the Soviet Union during the elections to the Supreme Soviet of the Soviet Union.

### 苏联政府接受邀请派代表参观美国大选

1956-10-23
第6版()
专栏：

    苏联政府接受邀请派代表参观美国大选
    据新华社22日讯　塔斯社莫斯科讯：苏联政府接受美国政府的邀请，决定派三名代表到美国去了解目前正在进行的美国选举运动。这些代表已经在10月21日从莫斯科前往美国，他们是：苏联最高苏维埃代表、全苏工会中央理事会副主席索洛维耶夫，“消息报”记者、编辑委员会委员库德里雅夫采夫和经济学博士鲁宾斯坦。
    美国政府的邀请是在今年9月21日给苏联外交部的一份照会中提出的。在这份照会中，美国政府还希望苏联政府在苏联最高苏维埃举行选举的时候也将邀请美国代表访问苏联。
