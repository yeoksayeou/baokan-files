Title: Dulles Openly Exerts Pressure on Egypt
Author:
Date: 1956-10-08
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, October 6th, New York dispatch: Just as the Security Council began discussing the Suez Canal issue, Dulles openly used pressure on Egypt in a speech today. In a speech at Williams College in Williamstown, Massachusetts, he said, "Is it fair, or is it tolerable," for the Western powers to agree to Egypt's sole control over this international waterway? He also threatened that "war in several parts of the world is an aggressively possible thing today." He said that one way to prevent war is to have the "will and ability" to use force to "punish" so-called "aggressors."

These words of Dulles are considered an attempt to use strong-arm tactics to force Egypt to accept the Western plan for "international management" of the canal in the Security Council.

Dulles described Egypt's legitimate actions to safeguard its sovereignty as "not for the universal good" but to "exert economic pressure on other countries."

### 杜勒斯公开对埃及施压力

1956-10-08
第6版()
专栏：

    杜勒斯公开对埃及施压力
    据新华社6日讯　纽约消息：正当安全理事会开始讨论苏伊士运河问题的时候，杜勒斯今天在一篇演说里，对埃及公然使用压力。他在马萨诸塞州威廉姆敦的威廉学院发表演说里说，让西方大国同意由埃及单独控制这条国际水道，“难道是公平的，或者是能够令人容忍的吗？”他还恫吓说，“今天战争在世界上的好几个地区是一种咄咄逼人的可能出现的东西”。他说，防止战争的方法之一是具备用武力“惩罚”所谓“侵略者”的“意志和能力”。
    杜勒斯的这些话被认为是企图用实力恫吓手段来迫使埃及在安全理事会中接受西方的“国际管理”运河的方案。
    杜勒斯把埃及维护自己主权的正当行动说成“不是为了普遍的利益”而是要“对其它国家施加经济压力”。
