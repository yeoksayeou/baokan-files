Title: Demanding Wage Increases to Improve Living Standards: Japanese Steel and Shipbuilding Workers Strike
Author:
Date: 1956-10-09
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, Tokyo, 8th: Tokyo dispatch: Fifty thousand Japanese shipbuilding workers began a nineteen-hour strike from 1 PM today, demanding a 10% increase in monthly wages.

This strike is led by the All-Japan Shipbuilding Workers' Union. Workers from ten major shipbuilding companies, including Nippon Kokan, Mitsubishi Heavy Industries Japan, Ishikawajima Heavy Industries, and Hitachi Shipbuilding, are participating in the strike.

Union officials say that this strike will delay shipbuilding projects by half a month. According to the union's plan, if the capitalists refuse to reach an agreement with the union, the workers will hold a sixty-four-hour strike starting on October 12.

Xinhua News Agency, Tokyo, 8th: Tokyo dispatch: Twenty-five thousand workers of Yawata Iron & Steel Company, one of Japan's largest steel companies, began a forty-eight-hour strike this morning.

This strike is part of the second major strike by Japanese steelworkers demanding wage increases. The workers held mass rallies in three locations in Yawata City and held demonstrations in the city.

Japanese press sources estimate that this forty-eight-hour strike will cause Yawata Iron & Steel Company to reduce its steel production by 35,000 tons, with losses reaching 1 billion yen.

### 要求增加工资改善生活  日本钢铁和造船工人罢工

1956-10-09
第6版()
专栏：

    要求增加工资改善生活
    日本钢铁和造船工人罢工
    新华社8日讯　东京消息：五万名日本造船工人从今天下午一时开始举行十九小时的罢工，要求提高每月工资10％。
    这次罢工是全日本造船工会领导举行的。参加罢工的包括日本钢管、三菱日本重工业、石川岛重工业、日立造船等十大造船公司的工人。
    工会方面人士说，这次罢工将使造船工程推迟半个月。根据工会的计划，如果资方不肯同工会方面达成协议，工人们将从10月12日开始举行六十四小时的罢工。
    新华社8日讯　东京消息：日本最大的钢铁公司之一——八幡制铁公司的两万五千工人，从今天早晨开始举行四十八小时罢工。
    这次罢工是日本钢铁工人要求提高工资的第二次大罢工的一部分。工人们在八幡市的三个地方举行了群众大会，并且在市内举行了示威游行。
    日本新闻界人士估计，这次四十八小时的罢工将使八幡制铁公司减产钢铁三万五千吨，损失达十亿日元。
