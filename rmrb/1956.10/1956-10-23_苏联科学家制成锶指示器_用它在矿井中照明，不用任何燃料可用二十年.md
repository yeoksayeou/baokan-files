Title: Soviet Scientists Develop Strontium Indicator: Used for Lighting in Mines, No Fuel Needed, Lasts Twenty Years
Author:
Date: 1956-10-23
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, Moscow, 20th: Soviet scientists' research into using atomic energy to manufacture lamps for use in mines has yielded preliminary results.

　　This lamp is called a strontium indicator. It does not require any other fuel and can be used continuously for twenty years.

　　This strontium indicator is made from isotopes of strontium. Originally, strontium isotopes have a remarkable property: they can cause luminescent substances to glow intensely, and the effect of their radiation on the human body can be prevented with just a thin layer of plexiglass.

　　The scientist studying this amazing illuminator is Soviet scientist Kadabu, whose atomic energy soil tester has already been widely used in various construction sites.

### 苏联科学家制成锶指示器  用它在矿井中照明，不用任何燃料可用二十年

1956-10-23
第5版()
专栏：

    苏联科学家制成锶指示器
    用它在矿井中照明，不用任何燃料可用二十年
    新华社莫斯科20日电　苏联科学家研究用原子能来制造矿井中用的灯，已经得到初步成果。
    这种灯叫锶指示器。它不用任何其他燃料，可以不间断地用二十年。
    这种锶指示器是用锶的同位素制成的。原来，锶的同位素具有一种卓越的特性。它能够引起发光的物质强烈地发光，而它的放射线对人体的影响，只要用隔上一层薄薄的有机玻璃就可以预防。
    研究这种奇妙的照明器的是苏联科学家卡达布，他发明的利用原子能的测土器，已经在各建筑工地广泛应用。
