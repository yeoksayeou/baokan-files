Title: International Conference on Peaceful Uses of Atomic Energy Adopts Statute of International Atomic Energy Agency; Soviet Representative Reaffirms International Agency Should Have Representation from Our Country
Author: 
Date: 1956-10-28
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, October 24th dispatch from New York (TASS): The representatives' conference discussing and approving the Statute of the International Atomic Energy Agency held a plenary session on October 23rd, hearing the report of the Consultation Committee on the final draft of the Statute. The conference participants unanimously adopted the Statute of the agency.

After the vote, some representatives spoke.

The head of the Soviet delegation, Mr. Tsarapkin, pointed out in his speech that this conference was successful, and it has laid the foundation for international cooperation in using atomic energy to serve the people of the world.

Tsarapkin particularly pointed out that this agency should be truly universal. The agency should be open to all countries that express their willingness to engage in international cooperation in the peaceful uses of atomic energy.

He said that the Soviet delegation firmly opposes, and will continue to oppose, depriving the representatives of China of the right to participate in the International Atomic Energy Agency. The Soviet delegation hopes that in the near future, the representatives of the People's Republic of China will obtain legitimate status in this agency and in the Management Committee, thereby promoting the more effective work of this agency.

Tsarapkin finally emphasized that the Soviet delegation believes that all activities of this agency must respect the sovereignty of all countries.

The meeting then held the election of the Preparatory Committee of the International Atomic Energy Agency. According to regulations, the representatives of twelve countries are ex officio members of this committee. These twelve countries are Australia, the United Kingdom, Belgium, Brazil, India, Canada, Portugal, the United States, the Soviet Union, France, Czechoslovakia, and the Union of South Africa. In addition, the conference also had to elect representatives from six countries to join the committee by secret ballot. As a result of the vote, Argentina, Egypt, Indonesia, Pakistan, Peru, and Japan were elected as members of the Preparatory Committee.

The committee will begin work on the day the agency's statute is signed, October 26th.

The meeting also passed a resolution selecting Vienna, the capital of Austria, as the headquarters of the agency.

### 和平利用原子能国际会议  通过原子能国际机构的章程  苏联代表重申国际机构应有我国代表参加

1956-10-28
第5版()
专栏：

    和平利用原子能国际会议
    通过原子能国际机构的章程
    苏联代表重申国际机构应有我国代表参加
    新华社24日讯　据塔斯社纽约24日讯：讨论和批准原子能国际机构章程的代表会议10月23日举行全体会议，听取协商委员会关于章程草案定稿的报告。会议参加者一致通过了机构的章程。
    在投票之后，有一些代表发言。
    苏联代表团团长查鲁宾在发言中指出，这个会议是成功的，会议已经为利用原子能为全世界人民服务进行国际合作奠定了基础。
    查鲁宾特别指出，这个机构应当是真正有普遍性的。机构应当为表示愿意在和平利用原子能方面进行国际合作的一切国家敞开大门。
    他说，苏联代表团坚决反对，并且今后将继续反对剥夺中国代表参加原子能国际机构的权利。苏联代表团希望，在最近的将来，中华人民共和国的代表将取得在这个机构和在管理委员会中的合法地位，从而促使这个机构的工作更加有效。
    查鲁宾最后强调指出，苏联代表团认为，这个机构的一切活动必须尊重各国的主权。
    会议接着举行了原子能国际机构筹备委员会的选举。按照规定，有十二个国家的代表是这个委员会的当然会员国，这十二个国家是澳大利亚、英国、比利时、巴西、印度、加拿大、葡萄牙、美国、苏联、法国、捷克斯洛伐克和南非联邦。此外，会议还要用秘密投票的方法选出六个国家的代表加入这个委员会。投票结果，阿根廷、埃及、印度尼西亚、巴基斯坦、秘鲁和日本被选为筹备委员会的会员国。
    委员会将在10月26日机构章程签字那天起开始工作。
    会议还通过一项决议，选定奥地利首都维也纳为这个机构的总部所在地。
