　　Music by the River (and one other poem)
　　Fang Ji
　　Ding-dong, ding-dong, ding-ding-dong-dong,
　　Music rings out by the river,
　　The music of clashing metal and stone.
　　Ding-dong, ding-dong, ding-ding-dong-dong,
　　The simple rhythm of the music
　　Is like the fingers of a five-year-old tapping on piano keys.
　　Why so anxious?
　　Like a passionate heart asking,
　　Anticipating,
　　Rocks, please answer:
　　The river provides accompaniment,
　　Swish, swish, swish—
　　The steam whistle joins in harmony
　　Whoo, whoo, whoo—
　　Countless passionate hearts,
　　Asking,
　　Anticipating;
　　Rocks, please answer:
　　Can a dam be built here?

　　Anticipation
　　On the mountain rocks, sits a group of people:
　　Engineers and geologists;
　　Quickly, silently recording
　　Their survey impressions.
　　The great river surges,
　　White clouds drift,
　　The rocks are silent;
　　The mountains, too, silently seem to anticipate...

### 江边的音乐（外一首）
方纪
1956-10-03
第8版()
专栏：
江边的音乐（外一首）
　方纪叮咚，叮咚，叮叮咚咚，江边上响起音乐，金石撞击的音乐。叮咚，叮咚，叮叮咚咚，节奏单纯的音乐像五岁的手指敲打在琴键上。为什么这样焦急？像一颗热烈的心在询问，在期待，岩石，请你回答：江水在伴奏，哗，哗，哗——汽笛也来和声呜，呜，呜——无数颗热烈的心，在询问，在期待；岩石，请你回答：这里能不能做坝？
    期　待山岩上，坐着一群人：工程师和地质学家；迅速地，无声地纪录下他们查勘的印象。大江激荡，白云飘飘，岩石静默；群山也无声地像有所期待……
