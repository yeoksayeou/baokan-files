Title: Joint Statement by Premier Zhou and Prime Minister Suhrawardy: Determined to Strive to Maintain Peace and Promote Friendly Cooperation Among Asian and African Countries; Develop Commercial and Cultural Relations and Friendly Exchanges Between the Two Countries, and Strengthen Mutual Understanding and Friendship.
Author:
Date: 1956-10-24
Page: 1

Xinhua News Agency, October 23rd: The signing ceremony of the Joint Statement between Zhou Enlai, Premier of the People's Republic of China, and Huseyn Shaheed Suhrawardy, Prime Minister of the Islamic Republic of Pakistan, was held in Beijing on the afternoon of the 23rd.

Participating in the signing ceremony from the Chinese side were Mao Zedong, Chairman of the People's Republic of China, Zhu De, Vice Chairman, Zhou Enlai, Premier of the State Council, Guo Moruo and Huang Yanpei, Vice Chairmen of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress, He Long, Vice Premier of the State Council, Zhang Wentian, Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs, Geng Biao, Chinese Ambassador to Pakistan, Yan Baohang, Chairman of the Treaty Committee of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, and Zhang Wenjin, Director of the Asian Department.

Participating in the signing ceremony from the Pakistani side were Huseyn Shaheed Suhrawardy, Prime Minister of the Islamic Republic of Pakistan, Ahmed, Pakistani Ambassador to China, M. S. A. Baig, Foreign Secretary, S. K. Dehlavi, Joint Secretary of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs and Commonwealth Relations, Aftab Ahmed Khan, Private Secretary to the Prime Minister, Colonel Hamid Nawaz Khan, Director of Protocol of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs and Commonwealth Relations, and A. Ahmed Ali, First Undersecretary of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs and Commonwealth Relations.

At 7:30 PM, Premier Zhou Enlai and Prime Minister Suhrawardy signed the joint statement.

After signing, the two sides shook hands warmly and toasted each other in congratulation.

Prior to the signing of the joint statement, Premier Zhou Enlai held a final meeting with Prime Minister Suhrawardy.

Xinhua News Agency, October 23rd: Joint Statement of Zhou Enlai, Premier of the People's Republic of China, and Huseyn Shaheed Suhrawardy, Prime Minister of the Islamic Republic of Pakistan

At the invitation of Zhou Enlai, Premier of the People's Republic of China, Suhrawardy, Prime Minister of the Islamic Republic of Pakistan, visited the People's Republic of China from October 18 to 29, 1956.

During his stay in Beijing, Prime Minister Suhrawardy held several meetings with Premier Zhou Enlai and Chairman Mao Zedong.

In the meetings, the two sides frankly and freely exchanged views on issues of common concern. These talks, which broadly touched upon many issues, were conducted in a cordial atmosphere and greatly contributed to enhancing the existing friendly relations between the two countries.

The two Prime Ministers expressed their determination to make the greatest efforts to maintain peace and to use all their strength to ease international tensions. They believe that as long as there is goodwill and sincerity, no international dispute cannot be resolved peacefully.

The two Prime Ministers noted the contribution made by the Bandung Conference to easing international tensions and promoting better relations between nations. They reaffirmed their confidence in the Bandung Resolutions and are willing to continue to strive to promote friendly cooperation among Asian and African countries.

Through these meetings, the two Prime Ministers gained a further understanding of each other's problems and are prepared to do their best to promote the resolution of these problems on the basis of peace and justice.

In order to further strengthen mutual understanding and friendship between the two countries, the two Prime Ministers believe it is necessary to develop commercial and cultural relations and friendly exchanges.

Prime Minister Suhrawardy invited Premier Zhou Enlai to visit Pakistan. Premier Zhou Enlai gladly accepted the invitation and will visit Pakistan in the near future.

Beijing, October 23, 1956

　　Zhou Enlai (signed)
      Premier of the State Council of the People's Republic of China
    Suhrawardy (signed)
      Prime Minister of the Islamic Republic of Pakistan

### 周总理和苏拉瓦底总理联合声明  决心努力维护和平和促进亚非各国的友好合作  发展两国商务、文化关系和友好往来，加强互相了解和友谊

1956-10-24
第1版()
专栏：

    周总理和苏拉瓦底总理联合声明
    决心努力维护和平和促进亚非各国的友好合作
    发展两国商务、文化关系和友好往来，加强互相了解和友谊
    新华社23日讯　中华人民共和国周恩来总理和巴基斯坦伊斯兰共和国侯赛恩·沙希德·苏拉瓦底总理联合声明的签字仪式，23日下午在北京举行。
    中国方面参加签字仪式的，有中华人民共和国毛泽东主席、朱德副主席，国务院周恩来总理，全国人民代表大会常务委员会副委员长郭沫若、黄炎培，国务院副总理贺龙，外交部副部长张闻天，中国驻巴基斯坦大使耿飚，外交部条约委员会主任委员阎宝航、亚洲司司长章文晋。
    巴基斯坦方面参加签字仪式的，有巴基斯坦伊斯兰共和国侯赛恩·沙希德·苏拉瓦底总理，巴基斯坦驻中国大使阿哈默德，外交秘书米·西·阿·贝格，外交和联邦关系部联合秘书斯·克·德拉维，总理私人秘书阿夫塔布·艾哈迈德汗，外交和联邦关系部交际司长哈米德·纳瓦兹汗上校，外交和联邦关系部一级副秘书艾阿默德·阿里。
    下午七时三十分，周恩来总理和苏拉瓦底总理分别在联合声明上签了字。
    在签字以后，双方亲切握手，并举杯互相祝贺。
    在联合声明签字以前，周恩来总理同苏拉瓦底总理举行了最后一次会谈。
    新华社23日讯　中华人民共和国周恩来总理和巴基斯坦伊斯兰共和国侯赛恩·沙希德·苏拉瓦底总理联合声明
    巴基斯坦伊斯兰共和国苏拉瓦底总理应中华人民共和国周恩来总理的邀请，从1956年10月18日到29日来中华人民共和国进行访问。
    苏拉瓦底总理在北京停留期间，同周恩来总理并且同毛泽东主席进行了几次会谈。
    在会谈中，双方就共同有关的问题坦率地和无拘束地交换了意见。这些会谈，广泛地涉及到许多问题，是在热诚的气氛中进行的，并且大大有助于增强两国之间已有的友好关系。
    两国总理表示决心尽最大努力来维护和平，并且以一切力量来和缓国际紧张局势。他们相信，只要具有善意和诚意，没有任何国际争端不能用和平方式加以解决。
    两国总理注意到万隆会议对和缓国际紧张局势和增进国与国之间更好的关系所作出的贡献。他们重申对万隆决议的信心，并且愿意为促进亚非各国之间的友好合作而继续努力。
    两国总理通过这些会谈，对于彼此的问题获得了进一步的了解，并且准备在和平和正义的基础上尽力促进这些问题的解决。
    为了进一步加强两国之间的互相了解和友谊，两国总理认为有必要发展商务的、文化的关系、友好往来。
    苏拉瓦底总理邀请周恩来总理访问巴基斯坦。周恩来总理愉快地接受了这个邀请，并且将在不久的将来访问巴基斯坦。
                        1956年10月23日于北京
