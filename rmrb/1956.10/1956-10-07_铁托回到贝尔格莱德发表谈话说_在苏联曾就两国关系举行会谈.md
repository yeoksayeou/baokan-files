Title: Tito Returns to Belgrade and Announces Talks on Bilateral Relations Held in the Soviet Union
Author:
Date: 1956-10-07
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, 6th. Tito returned to Belgrade from the Soviet Union on October 5th and gave a talk to Yugoslav newspapers and radio reporters about his visit to the Soviet Union, according to a report by the Yugoslav News Agency.
    He said: "Comrade Khrushchev invited my wife and I, along with Comrades Rankovic and Pucar, to tour the Crimea in the Soviet Union. This tour was purely private in nature and was for rest. Naturally, we also took advantage of the opportunity to stay there and rest in the Crimea to hold talks with the Soviet Union on issues related to bilateral relations."

### 铁托回到贝尔格莱德发表谈话说  在苏联曾就两国关系举行会谈

1956-10-07
第5版()
专栏：

    铁托回到贝尔格莱德发表谈话说
    在苏联曾就两国关系举行会谈
    新华社6日讯　据南斯拉夫通讯社报道，铁托总统10月5日从苏联返回贝尔格莱德以后，向南斯拉夫报刊和电台记者发表了关于访问苏联的谈话。
    他说：“我和我的夫人、兰科维奇和普察尔同志应赫鲁晓夫同志的邀请游览了苏联的克里米亚。这次游览只是私人性质，是为了休息。自然，我们也利用了在那里停留和在克里米亚休息的机会，同苏联就两国关系问题进行了会谈。”
