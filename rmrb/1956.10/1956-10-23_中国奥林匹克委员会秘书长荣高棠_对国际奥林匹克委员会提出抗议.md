Title: Rong Gaotang, Secretary-General of the All-China Sports Federation, Protests to the International Olympic Committee
Author:
Date: 1956-10-23
Page: 1

Xinhua News Agency, October 22nd: Rong Gaotang, Secretary-General of the All-China Sports Federation, said at a press conference held in Beijing today for Chinese and foreign journalists that the International Olympic Committee should immediately revoke Taiwan's right to send athletes to the 16th Olympic Games and should recognize that in China there is only one All-China Sports Federation in Beijing, which is the only Chinese Olympic Committee that can represent the 600 million Chinese people. Rong Gaotang also lodged a serious protest against the International Olympic Committee's "unreasonable measures to artificially split China and damage China's dignity" by planning to call Taiwan "Formosa China" and arbitrarily changing the great People's Republic of China to "Beijing China" at the 16th Olympic Games.

Rong Gaotang told the more than 50 Chinese and foreign journalists attending the press conference that if this practice by the International Olympic Committee is true, it not only fundamentally violates the Olympic Charter, but also constitutes a serious insult to the Chinese people and Chinese athletes. This is completely intolerable to the entire Chinese people and athletes. Rong Gaotang said that the International Olympic Committee should, based on the principle that only one National Olympic Committee should be recognized in one country and that only one team from one country should participate in the Olympic Games, immediately correct the mistake of recognizing "two National Olympic Committees" in China.

Referring to the fact that the International Olympic Committee recently accepted the so-called protest from the Taiwan Kuomintang group and prepared to discuss at the 52nd International Olympic Committee meeting the All-China Sports Federation's notification to athletes in Taiwan Province to come to Beijing to participate in the Chinese Olympic team selection trials as "engaging in politics," Rong Gaotang said: "We believe that such a practice is extremely absurd for the International Olympic Committee."

Rong Gaotang said that this issue is purely an internal matter of China, and the International Olympic Committee has no right to interfere at all. He also said that notifying Taiwan athletes to participate in the national selection trials is the obligation of the All-China Sports Federation and the right that Taiwan athletes should have.

Rong Gaotang said that Taiwan is a province of China, and in 1948 it sent more than 100 athletes to participate in the 7th National Games at that time. More than 1,400 Chinese athletes from all over the country and residing in Hong Kong and Macao participated in the Chinese Olympic team selection trials recently held in Beijing. Athletes from Taiwan Province should naturally also have the right to participate. We notified them on September 1 to participate in the selection trials. But we feel very sorry that the outstanding athletes in Taiwan who are eager to represent the great motherland in the 16th Olympic Games have not been able to obtain this opportunity because they have been obstructed by the US-Chiang clique.

Rong Gaotang pointed out that those who slander the All-China Sports Federation for "engaging in politics" are precisely the ones who are engaging in politics, because "they are attempting to undermine China's unified Olympic delegation and obstruct our participation in the 16th Olympic Games."

Rong Gaotang announced: "The All-China Sports Federation has decided to send the largest Olympic delegation in Chinese history to participate in the 16th Olympic Games. Our first group of personnel will soon be leaving for Melbourne."

At this press conference, Rong Gaotang distributed to each Chinese and foreign journalist a copy of a letter he wrote to Otto Mayer, Secretary-General of the International Olympic Committee, regarding the above-mentioned protest. This letter has been sent out today. Rong Gaotang wrote in the letter: "The International Olympic Committee wants to discuss the issue of China notifying Taiwan athletes to participate in the national selection trials at the 52nd International Olympic Committee plenary meeting. We believe that this is a serious mistake that is enough to damage the reputation of the International Olympic Committee, because the International Olympic Committee has no right to interfere in the issues between a National Olympic Committee and its own local organizations when the National Olympic Committee has not violated the Olympic Charter." "Since the International Olympic Committee has now accepted the slanderous proposals of those who cannot truly represent the interests of Taiwan athletes, attempting to further interfere in the internal affairs of the All-China Sports Federation (All-China Sports Federation), we firmly oppose this." Rong Gaotang's letter said: "If athletes from Taiwan want to participate in the Olympic Games, they must first participate in the national selection trials in mainland China, and after being selected, participate as members of the unified Chinese Olympic delegation."

Rong Gaotang also answered some questions from Chinese and foreign journalists at this press conference.

Journalists from 31 organizations, including Xinhua News Agency, People's Daily, TASS, Reuters, Agence France-Presse, and Kyodo News, attended today's press conference.

### 中国奥林匹克委员会秘书长荣高棠  对国际奥林匹克委员会提出抗议

1956-10-23
第1版()
专栏：

    中国奥林匹克委员会秘书长荣高棠
    对国际奥林匹克委员会提出抗议
    指出该会邀请我台湾省体育组织参加本届运动会是分裂中国和损害中国尊严的无理措施
    新华社22日讯　中国奥林匹克委员会秘书长荣高棠今天在北京举行的一个中、外记者招待会上说，国际奥林匹克委员会应该立即取消台湾派遣运动员参加第十六届奥林匹克运动会的权利，同时应该承认在中国只有一个在北京的中华全国体育总会是唯一能代表六亿中国人民的中国奥林匹克委员会。荣高棠并且对国际奥林匹克委员会打算在第十六届奥林匹克运动会上把台湾叫做“福摩萨中国”，而把伟大的中华人民共和国擅改为“北京中国”的这种“人为地分裂中国和损害中国尊严的无理措施”提出严重抗议。
    荣高棠对出席招待会的五十多名中、外记者说，国际奥林匹克委员会的这种作法如果属实，它不仅根本违反奥林匹克运动会宪章，而且是对中国人民和中国运动员的严重侮辱。这是全中国人民和运动员所完全不能容忍的。荣高棠说，国际奥林匹克委员会应该根据在一个国家只能承认一个国家奥林匹克委员会和一个国家只应有一个代表队参加奥林匹克运动会的原则，立即纠正在中国承认“两个国家奥林匹克委员会”的错误。
    在谈到国际奥林匹克委员会最近接受台湾国民党集团的所谓抗议，准备在第五十二届国际奥林匹克委员会会议上讨论中国奥林匹克委员会通知台湾省的运动员前来北京参加中国奥林匹克运动队选拔赛为“搞政治”这件事的时候，荣高棠说：“我们认为这样的作法对国际奥林匹克委员会说来是一件极为荒唐的事。”
    荣高棠说，这个问题纯粹是中国内部的问题，国际奥林匹克委员会根本无权过问。他又说，通知台湾运动员参加全国选拔赛是中国奥林匹克委员会的义务，也是台湾运动员应有的权利。
    荣高棠说，台湾是中国的一个省，在1948年它曾经派出了一百多名运动员参加当时的第七届全国运动会。最近在北京举行的中国奥林匹克运动队选拔赛，曾经有全国各地和居住在香港、澳门的中国运动员一千四百多人参加。台湾省的运动员自然也应该有权参加。我们在9月1日已经通知他们来参加选拔赛。但是我们感到很遗憾，渴望着能代表伟大的祖国参加第十六届奥林匹克运动会的台湾的优秀运动员们，却因为受到了美蒋的阻挠而没有获得这个机会。
    荣高棠指出，诬蔑中国奥林匹克委员会“搞政治”的人本身恰恰是在搞政治，因为“他们企图破坏中国的统一的奥林匹克代表团和阻挠我们参加第十六届奥林匹克运动会。”
    荣高棠宣布说：“中国奥林匹克委员会已经决定派遣中国历史上最大的一次奥林匹克代表团参加第十六届奥林匹克运动会。我们的第一批人员不久就要前往墨尔本。”
    在这个记者招待会上，荣高棠把就以上提出的抗议写给国际奥林匹克委员会秘书长奥托·梅耶的一封信的抄本分发给每一个中、外记者。这封信已经在今天寄出。荣高棠在这封信上写道：“国际奥林匹克委员会要在第五十二届国际奥林匹克委员会全体会议上讨论中国通知台湾运动员参加全国选拔赛的问题，我们认为这是足以使国际奥林匹克委员会名誉扫地的严重错误，因为国际奥林匹克委员会没有权利在一个国家奥林匹克委员会并没有违反奥林匹克宪章时，干涉这个国家奥林匹克委员会同它自己所属的地方组织之间的问题。”“由于国际奥林匹克委员会现在竟又接受了那些并不能真正代表台湾运动员利益的分子的诬蔑性提案，企图进一步干涉中国奥林匹克委员会（中华全国体育总会）的内部问题，我们对此坚决反对。”荣高棠的信中说：“台湾的运动员要参加奥林匹克运动会，就必须先参加全中国的选拔赛，在当选以后，再作为统一的中国奥林匹克代表团的成员去参加。”
    荣高棠在这个记者招待会上还解答了中、外记者的一些问题。
    参加今天这个记者招待会的有新华通讯社、人民日报社、塔斯社、路透社、法新社、共同社等三十一个单位的记者。
