    Joint Institute for Nuclear Research in Moscow
    In order to promote cooperation among scientists from various countries in the theoretical and experimental research of nuclear physics, and to expand the possibilities of peaceful use of atomic energy, the Joint Institute for Nuclear Research was established in Moscow in March of this year. To date, the Institute has been joined by twelve countries: the Soviet Union, China, Albania, Bulgaria, Hungary, the German Democratic Republic, the Democratic People's Republic of Korea, Mongolia, Poland, Romania, Czechoslovakia, and the Democratic Republic of Vietnam. The synchrophasotron, the location of the Joint Institute for Nuclear Research, is installed inside this building.
    The central control console in the control room of the synchrophasotron. On duty is engineer Cherkasova.
    A full view of the synchrophasotron, which has recently been installed. This accelerator is a ring-shaped vacuum chamber made of 36,000 tons of magnets. Its diameter is 56 meters. 　　Photo by F. Savostyanov (TASS dispatch)

### 在莫斯科的联合核子研究所（图片）
弗·萨伏斯其亚诺夫
1956-10-13
第5版()
专栏：

    在莫斯科的联合核子研究所
    为了各国科学家在核子物理学的理论研究和实验研究工作方面进行合作，以便扩大和平利用原子能的可能性，今年3月在莫斯科成立了联合核子研究所。到目前为止，参加这个研究所的共有苏联、中国、阿尔巴尼亚、保加利亚、匈牙利、德意志民主共和国、朝鲜民主主义人民共和国、蒙古、波兰、罗马尼亚、捷克斯洛伐克和越南民主共和国等十二个国家。联合核子研究所所在地的全景同步稳相加速器就安装在这座建筑物里面
  同步稳相加速器操纵室的中央操纵台。正在工作的是值班工程师切尔卡索娃。
    最近安装完毕的同步稳相加速器的全貌。这种加速器是用三万六千吨磁铁组成的一座环形真空室。它的直径是五十六公尺。		　弗·萨伏斯其亚诺夫摄（塔斯社稿）
