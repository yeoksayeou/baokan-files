Title: List of Personnel Accompanying Prime Minister Suhrawardy of Pakistan on Visit to China
Author:
Date: 1956-10-19
Page: 1

Xinhua News Agency, 18th – The list of personnel accompanying Prime Minister Suhrawardy of the Islamic Republic of Pakistan on his visit to China is as follows:

    Mrs. Akhtar Suleiman, daughter of Prime Minister Suhrawardy
    Viquarunnisa Noon, wife of Noon, Minister for Foreign Affairs and Commonwealth Relations
    Ahmed, Ambassador of Pakistan to China
    Mrs. Ahmed
    Princess Abida Sultan, Honorary Citizen
    M. S. A. Baig, Foreign Secretary
    Mrs. Baig
    Ghulam Faruque, Chairman of the Pakistan Industrial Development Corporation
    Mrs. Faruque
    S. M. Yusuf, Joint Secretary of the Ministry of Commerce
    S. K. Dehlavi, Joint Secretary of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs and Commonwealth Relations, concurrently Director of the Far East Asia Division and the Middle East Division
    Khan Muhammad Azam Khan, Water Conservancy Expert, Chief Engineer of the Warsak Dam Project
    Aftab Ahmed Khan, Private Secretary to the Prime Minister
    Mrs. Ahmed Khan
    Colonel Hamid Nawaz Khan, Director of Protocol, Ministry of Foreign Affairs and Commonwealth Relations
    Mrs. Nawaz Khan
    Kaiser, Counselor of the Embassy of Pakistan in China
    Mrs. Kaiser
    Aamer Ali, First Undersecretary of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs and Commonwealth Relations
    Colonel Doctor Shahwar, Personal Physician to the Prime Minister
    Noor Khan, Colonel
    Mrs. Rukhsana Iqbal Ahmed
    Abdul Qayyum, Public Relations Officer
    Major Sattar Ahmed Malik, Translator
    Farooqi, Second Secretary of the Embassy of Pakistan in China
    Mrs. Farooqi
    Malik, Attache of the Embassy of Pakistan in China
    Mrs. Malik
    Mohammad Sadiq, Assistant Private Secretary to the Prime Minister
    Merchant, Chief Photographer
    Mohammad Akhtar, Official of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs and Commonwealth Relations
    Zahuruddin Mian, Photographer
    Safdar Quraishi, Representative of the Pakistan United Press
    Ahmed Hassan Shah, Representative of Radio Pakistan
