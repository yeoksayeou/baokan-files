Title: Japanese Steel Workers Continue Strike
Author:
Date: 1956-10-07
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, Tokyo, 5th: Japanese steel workers began another 48-hour strike this morning, continuing their struggle for higher wages.

This strike is another large-scale strike by 100,000 Japanese steel workers, following a 24-hour strike on September 29th.

The unions of four major companies participating in the strike, including Fuji Iron & Steel, Nippon Kokan, Kobe Steel, and Sumitomo Metal, are demanding that the companies increase wages by an average of 2,500 yen per month on top of the original wages, starting from August this year. Unions of small and medium-sized companies are demanding that wages be increased from 1,200 yen to 1,700 yen.

The unions leading this strike are the 25 unions belonging to the Japan Federation of Steel Industry Workers' Unions. In addition, the Shibukawa factory of Kanto Steel Company will hold a 24-hour strike starting tomorrow. The Yawata Iron & Steel Company union is also scheduled to hold a 48-hour strike starting on October 8th.

### 日本钢铁工人继续罢工

1956-10-07
第6版()
专栏：

    日本钢铁工人继续罢工
    新华社5日讯　东京消息：从今天早晨开始，日本钢铁工人再次举行四十八小时罢工，继续进行要求提高工资的斗争。
    这次罢工是日本十万钢铁工人继9月29日二十四小时罢工以后的又一次规模巨大的罢工。
    参加罢工的富士制铁、日本钢管、神户制钢、住友金属等四家大公司的工会，要求公司方面从今年8月以后，在原工资的基础上每月平均增加工资二千五百日元。中小公司的工会要求将工资从一千二百日元提高到一千七百日元。
    领导这次罢工的工会是属于日本钢铁产业工会联合会的二十五个工会。此外，关东制钢公司涩川工厂工会将从明天开始举行二十四小时罢工。八幡制铁公司工会也预定从10月8日起举行四十八小时的罢工。
