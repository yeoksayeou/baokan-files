Title: Joint Communiqué Issued by the Governments of Romania and Yugoslavia
Author:
Date: 1956-10-30
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, October 29th – TASS reported: Belgrade newspapers published on the 28th the joint communiqué on the conclusion of the talks between the government delegations of Romania and Yugoslavia.

The communiqué stated that the two governments expressed satisfaction with the development of economic cooperation between the two countries. The two countries signed a trade and payment agreement and a goods exchange protocol for 1957-1960, as well as a scientific and technological cooperation agreement in Brioni. At the same time, the two governments also signed a cultural cooperation agreement. The two government delegations discussed the results of the work of Yugoslav and Romanian experts on the issue of utilizing the hydropower resources of the Danube River and decided to establish a Romanian-Yugoslav mixed committee to coordinate and lead this work.

The communiqué stated that the negotiations between the two government delegations were conducted in an atmosphere of sincerity, frankness, and mutual understanding.

The two governments noted with satisfaction that the development of international relations in the past few months has confirmed the principles and views contained in the joint statement signed by the two governments in Bucharest in June 1956. The two governments will continue to follow these principles and views in developing mutual relations in the future and will work together to consolidate world peace and develop full international cooperation.

The two governments emphasized with satisfaction that the development of friendly relations between Yugoslavia and Romania so far, based on the principles of sovereignty, territorial integrity, equality, mutual respect, and non-interference in internal affairs, has laid a solid foundation for expanding the good relations between the two socialist countries.

The two governments firmly believe that such principles should also be followed in international relations.

The two governments particularly emphasized that all outstanding international issues can only be resolved through peaceful means, through negotiations, and in accordance with the principles of the United Nations Charter.

The communiqué emphasized that the two governments believe that the upcoming United Nations General Assembly must resolve a number of major international issues, first and foremost the issue of recognizing the legitimate representation of the People's Republic of China in the United Nations, disarmament, and the prohibition of atomic weapons and other weapons of mass destruction.

The communiqué concluded by saying that the two governments agreed to continue constructive cooperation in studying various issues related to further deepening and expanding mutual relations. Both sides also expressed their determination to continue their efforts in the future to further strengthen friendly cooperation between Yugoslavia and Romania for the benefit of the two peoples, peace, and socialism.

### 罗南两国政府发表联合公报

1956-10-30
第5版()
专栏：

    罗南两国政府发表联合公报
    新华社29日讯　据塔斯社报道：贝尔格莱德报纸28日刊载了罗马尼亚和南斯拉夫两国政府代表团会谈结束的联合公报。
    公报说，两国政府对两国经济合作的发展表示满意，两国在布里俄尼签订了1957—1960年的贸易支付协定和换货议定书以及科学技术合作协定。同时，两国政府还签订了一个文化合作协定。两国政府代表团讨论了南斯拉夫和罗马尼亚两国专家在利用多瑙河水力资源问题方面的工作结果，并且决定成立南罗混合委员会来协调和领导这项工作。
    公报说，两国政府代表团的谈判是在诚挚、开诚布公和互相谅解的气氛中进行的。
    两国政府满意地认定，过去几个月中国际关系的发展证实了两国政府1956年6月在布加勒斯特签字的联合声明所包含的原则和观点。两国政府今后将继续遵循这些原则和观点来发展相互关系，并且共同努力以巩固世界和平和发展充分的国际合作。
    两国政府满意地着重指出，南斯拉夫和罗马尼亚两国到目前为止以主权、领土完整、平等、相互尊重和不干涉内政的原则为基础的友好关系的发展，为扩大两个社会主义国家之间的良好关系建立了巩固的基础。
    两国政府坚信，在国际关系中也应当遵循这种原则。
    两国政府特别着重指出，一切悬而未决的国际问题只能通过和平方式、通过谈判和根据联合国宪章的原则来求得解决。
    公报着重指出，两国政府认为即将举行的联合国大会必须解决一些重大的国际问题，首先是解决承认中华人民共和国在联合国中的合法代表权的问题、裁军以及禁止原子武器和其他大规模毁灭性武器的问题。
    公报最后说，两国政府同意在研究进一步加深和扩大相互关系的各项问题方面，继续进行建设性的合作。双方还表示，为了两国人民、和平和社会主义的利益，决心在今后继续努力以求进一步加强南斯拉夫和罗马尼亚之间的友好合作。
