Title: Defend Workers' Democratic Rights and Livelihood: Japanese "Sohyo" Decides to Launch a Series of Struggles
Author:
Date: 1956-10-30
Page: 6

    Defend Workers' Democratic Rights and Livelihood
    Japanese "Sohyo" Decides to Launch a Series of Struggles
    Xinhua News Agency, Tokyo, 28th - The General Council of Trade Unions of Japan (Sohyo) decided on the 26th to launch a series of struggles this year to defend workers' democratic rights and livelihood.
    According to the decision of this nationwide trade union federation with over three million members, Japanese workers will hold local and national mass rallies "to defend livelihood and rights" in various prefectures and Tokyo during a month from early November to early December, and carry out other forms of activities to oppose the extension of the "Strike Regulation Law" that prohibits strikes by coal miners and electrical workers.
    The Japanese government and the ruling party are currently preparing to submit a bill to the extraordinary session of the Diet, scheduled to convene on November 12, to extend the validity of this law. This three-year law, established in August 1953, expired in August of this year.
    While opposing the extension of the "Strike Regulation Law," Japanese workers will also demand higher wages and the issuance of temporary allowances, and will demand the enactment of a law guaranteeing a minimum wage.

### 保卫工人的民主权利和生活  日“总评”决定展开一系列斗争

1956-10-30
第6版()
专栏：

    保卫工人的民主权利和生活
    日“总评”决定展开一系列斗争
    新华社28日讯　东京消息：日本工会总评议会26日决定在今年展开一系列的斗争，来保卫工人的民主权利和生活。
    根据这个拥有三百多万会员的全国性工会联合组织的决定，日本工人将在从11月上旬到12月上旬的一个月里，在全国各县和东京举行地方性和全国性的“保卫生活和权利”的群众大会，并且进行其他方式的活动，来反对延长禁止煤矿工人和电气工人罢工的“罢工规制法”。
    日本政府和执政党目前正准备在计划于11月12日召开的国会临时会议上提出延长这个法律的有效期限的法案。这项在1953年8月成立的为期三年的法律，在今年8月已经满期。
    日本工人在反对延长“罢工规制法”的同时，还将要求提高工资和发给临时津贴，并且要求制定保障最低限度工资的法律。
