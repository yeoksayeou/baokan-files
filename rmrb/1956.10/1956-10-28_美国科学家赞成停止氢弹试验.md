Title: American Scientists Favor Ending Hydrogen Bomb Tests
Author:
Date: 1956-10-28
Page: 6
Xinhua News Agency, Washington, October 26th: The Federation of American Scientists, representing over two thousand one hundred American scientists, issued a statement on October 24th, expressing support for reaching an international agreement on ending hydrogen bomb tests.

The Federation pointed out that nuclear weapon explosions "can be detected from a great distance" and therefore it is not necessary to first establish "adequate" inspection systems before stopping testing.

Nineteen scientists from the University of Rochester also issued a statement on October 25th, accusing Eisenhower's statement refusing to stop hydrogen bomb tests as "misleading." They said that the statement "oversimplified" the issue and "failed to address the important problem of the harm caused by hydrogen bomb tests."

### 美国科学家赞成停止氢弹试验

1956-10-28
第6版()
专栏：

    美国科学家赞成停止氢弹试验
    新华社26日讯　华盛顿消息：代表两千一百多名美国科学家的美国科学家联合会10月24日发表声明，赞成就停止氢弹试验问题达成国际协议。
    这个联合会指出，核子武器的爆炸“可以在很远的距离之外被测知”，因此不一定要先建立“充分的”视察制度然后再停止试验。
    罗契斯特大学的十九位科学家也在10月25日发表声明，指责艾森豪威尔的拒绝停止氢弹试验的声明是“混淆视听的”，他们说，这个声明“太简单化了”，它“没有正视由于氢弹试验所造成的危害这个重要的问题”。
