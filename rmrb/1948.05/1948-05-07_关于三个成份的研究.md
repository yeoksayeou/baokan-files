　　Research on Three Class Statuses

　　Comrade Editor:

　　Regarding a widow in Jianzhang: Before 1916, her family had two people, 25 mu of land, and a yield of 30 shi of grain. Because her husband was a teacher elsewhere, the family hired someone to manage the land. In 1917, her husband died of illness. The family still employed one person, and the amount of land remained unchanged. She has been living this way, running the household in a landlord-like manner, for over 30 years. The land area is more than six times the average for the masses, and the grain yield is more than eight or nine times higher. What class status should this be?

　　Cui Dezhen: Before 1933, his family had seven people, 50 mu of land, a house (14 rooms and two cave dwellings), one ox, and sufficient farm tools. No one in the family participated in labor; a long-term laborer was hired, and short-term laborers were also hired for four or five months at a time. He himself went to Taiyuan to work as an accountant in a grain store. Because he embezzled over 100 yuan, he threw a feast to repay the debt in 1933, leaving 23 mu of land behind. At this time, he used half a laborer while he continued to do business. In 1939, he returned to the village to serve as a cadre but still did not labor, employing half a laborer. After 1951 or 1953, following the burden reduction and rent reduction policies, the land was restored to its original state (over 50 mu), and he hired one laborer, along with several short-term laborers. He himself also participated in some labor. Now he has over 50 mu of land, a family of seven, a house, and one ox. Last year, he only contributed 8 mu of land. Considering that before 1933, his family's land was managed by hired labor, and he was a shop assistant, it should be considered a rich peasant. After 1933, due to debt reduction, only half a laborer was employed, and he was still away, so the exploitation was reduced, and he became a well-to-do middle peasant. But after 1951, he restored the original exploitation, becoming a rich peasant again. However, this household's exploitation exceeded 25% before 1933, then decreased to below 25% in the middle period, and after 1951, the exploitation exceeded 25% again. What class status should this be?

　　Cheng Guilin: In 1936, his family had four people, 25 mu of land, and a grain yield of 28 shi. He himself participated in major labor and lent out 400 yuan. After 1953, the debt interest exploitation stopped. According to the situation before 1953, the family received 28 shi of grain and lent out 400 yuan. At a minimum monthly interest rate of 2%, the annual interest income was 80 yuan (converted to grain at 5 mao per yuan), totaling 16 shi of grain. The total income from both items was 44 shi, with an exploitation income of 16 shi, so the exploitation income accounted for 36.3% of the total income. This should be considered a rich peasant. However, according to the rule that a rich peasant transforms class status after stopping exploitation for three years, this household has stopped exploitation for seven years. But the family's property has not changed. What class status should this household be classified as?

Comrades of the Wuxiang Joint Office:

　　According to the information you provided, the class statuses of the several households you inquired about should be classified as follows: (1) The widow in Jianzhang: Before 1916, if no one in the family participated in major labor, then she should be classified as a managing landlord. Because she fits the description of "possessing more and better land, not engaging in agricultural labor herself," and also fits the description of "relying on hired labor to manage the land as her entire or main source of livelihood." As for whether she is a "new-style managing landlord" or an "old-style managing landlord," it is impossible to determine because the letter does not specify whether her labor conditions are of a feudal-serfdom nature or of a capitalist free-labor nature. (2) Cui Dezhen: The situation before 1933 also fits the above conditions, and his family should be classified as managing landlords (not rich peasants). He himself was a clerk. After 1933, the land was reduced, equivalent to a middle peasant, and he should be reclassified as a middle peasant. However, if the family still relies on support from relatives and friends, past savings, or various improper methods for living, and the living and labor conditions remain the same as in the past, then the class status remains unchanged (the letter does not specify whether this is the case). After 1951 (1940 in the Republican calendar), he returned to the original state before 1933, fitting the conditions of a managing landlord, and has exceeded three years, so he should be classified as a managing landlord. His position as a cadre "not separating from family economic life while holding revolutionary work (village chief, etc.), does not change his class status," that is, the same as his family. (3) Cheng Guilin: The situation before 1953 fits the conditions of an old-style rich peasant (exploitation income exceeds one-quarter of the total income, and his debt claims are feudal usury), and he should be classified as an old-style rich peasant. After 1953, he stopped exploitation and has exceeded three years, so he should be reclassified as a middle peasant. "Those landlords and rich peasants who still retain much feudal property should still hand over their surplus property to be distributed to poor peasants." (See Comrade Ren Bishi: "Several Issues in Land Reform")

### 关于三个成份的研究

1948-05-07
第1版()
专栏：编读往来

　　关于三个成份的研究编辑同志：
    监漳一个寡妇，在民国五年前家中两口人，有土地廿五亩，产量卅石，因他男人在外当教员，家中雇用一个人，经营家中土地，到民国六年，男人得病去世，家中仍是用一个人，土地原份未动，家中只留他一个人，一直到现在过着这种经营地主方式生活卅多年。按土地平均超过群众六倍、产粮超过八、九倍以上，这算什么成份。
    崔德珍，民国廿二年前人七口，土地五十亩，房院一所（十四间房二个窑）牛一个，农具足用，家中没有人参加劳动，雇用长工一个，并时常找短工四、五个月不定，本人去太谷在粮店任会计。这时因揭钱百元以上，到二十二年请客还帐，家中丢下土地二十三亩，这时用成半个人，本人仍做生意。到民国二十八年，回来在村当干部仍不劳动，用人半个。到四○年四二年以后，经负担减租政策，土地又恢复原状（五十多亩），又雇用人一个，还用好些短工，自己也稍微参加劳动。现在有地五十多亩，人七口，房院一所，牛一个，去年只献了八亩田。按二十二年时，家中土地用人经营，自己是店员算劳力，应是个富农，到二十二年以后因债务下降用成半个人，他仍是在外，这时剥削减轻，成为富裕中农，但到四○年以后又恢复剥削原状又成富农；但这户在前剥削超过百分之二十五以上，中间剥削减轻到百分之二十五以下，到四○年以后剥削又超过百分之二十五以上，这算啥成份。
    成贵林，二十五年家中四口人，地二十五亩，产粮二十八石，自己参加主要劳动，放帐四百元，到四二年以后债利剥削停止。这户按四二年前家中收粮二十八石，放帐四百元，每月最低按二分利息，一年收利八十元（五毛钱折粮），共折谷十六石，二项共收入四十四石，剥削收入十六石，故剥削收入占总收入百分之三六·三，这算是富农；但按富农三年停止剥削，即转变成份，这户已停止七年剥削了；但家中财产未动，这户该划啥成份。
    　   　　　　　       　　　　　　　　　　　武乡联合办公室武乡联合办公室同志：
    来信所问几户的成份，按照你所提出的情况，应这样划：（一）监漳的寡妇：民国五年以前，如果家里无人参加主要劳动，则应该是经营地主。因为她适合于“占有较多较好的土地，自己不从事农业劳动，”又适合于“以雇工经营土地为其全部或主要生活来源”。至于她是“新式经营地主”，还是“旧式经营地主”，因来信未说明其雇工条件是封建奴役性质，还是资本主义的自由劳动性质，所以无法决定。（二）崔德珍：民国二十二年前情况，亦适合于上述条件，其家庭应为经营地主（不是富农）。他本人是职员。二十二年后，土地减少，相当于中农，应变为中农。但如果家中仍依靠亲友接济、过去积蓄或各种不正当方法为生，生活状况及劳动状况仍与过去相同，则成份不变（来信未说明是否如此）。一九四○年（民国二十九年）以后又恢复民国二十二年前原状，适合于经营地主条件，且又已超过三年，应为经营地主。他本人“不脱离家庭经济生活而担任革命工作（村长等），其成份不变更，”即是与家庭同。（三）成贵林：一九四二年以前情况适合旧式富农条件（剥削收入超过总收入四分之一，其债权又是封建性高利债权），应是旧式富农成份。一九四二年后停止剥削，至今已超过三年，应变为中农成份。“这些地主富农仍保有许多封建财产者，则仍应交出其多余财产，分给贫苦农民。”（见任弼时同志：“土地改革中几个问题”）
