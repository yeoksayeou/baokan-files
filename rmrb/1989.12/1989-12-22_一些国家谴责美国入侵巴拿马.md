Title: Some Countries Condemn U.S. Invasion of Panama
Author: Guo Weicheng  Zhu Manting  Li Zhimin  Lan Caiji
Date: 1989-12-22
Edition: 4

Some Countries Condemn U.S. Invasion of Panama

	People's Daily, Mexico City, December 20: Correspondent Guo Weicheng reports: Today, the Mexican government, the Senate and House of Representatives, and the Federal District Assembly issued statements or passed resolutions condemning the U.S. invasion of Panama. They demanded that the U.S. immediately withdraw its troops from Panama and pointed out that there is no reason for the U.S. to use military intervention in a sovereign country. Mexican citizens also held demonstrations outside the U.S. embassy.

	People's Daily, Caracas, December 21: Correspondent Zhu Manting reports: The Venezuelan government issued a statement yesterday stating that the U.S. intervention in Panama is "unacceptable to the governments and peoples of Latin American countries." The statement demanded that the U.S. invasion forces withdraw from Panama. President Perez, in his speech that day, condemned and rejected the U.S. military aggression.

	Nicaraguan President Daniel Ortega strongly condemned the U.S. aggression and ordered the military and the entire country to be on high alert. Ortega pointed out that the U.S. actions in Panama "not only violate the sovereignty and territorial integrity of Panama but also threaten the peace and security of the entire Latin America."

	People's Daily, Buenos Aires, December 20: Correspondent Li Zhimin reports: The Argentine government issued a statement today expressing shock at the developments in Panama and demanding that the U.S. quickly withdraw its armed forces from Panama. Tonight, some Argentine political parties organized demonstrations in the capital to protest the U.S. invasion of Panama.

	People's Daily, Brasilia, December 20: Correspondent Lan Caiji reports: Brazilian President Sarney issued a statement today expressing the Brazilian government's opposition to the U.S. armed invasion of Panama. He pointed out that this action is "a setback in U.S. relations with American countries" and expressed deep regret over it.

	The Brazilian Foreign Ministry issued a statement today condemning the U.S. military invasion of Panama. Brazilian Foreign Minister Sodre summoned the U.S. ambassador to Brazil today to express the Brazilian government's opposition to the U.S. government's military intervention in Panama.

	The Brazilian Parliament also issued a statement today strongly condemning the U.S. government's armed invasion of Panama.

	According to Xinhua News Agency, Beijing, December 21: A comprehensive report by our correspondents in the Middle East, Syria, Algeria, and Iran issued official statements and declarations today strongly condemning the U.S. military invasion of Panama.

	The Libyan Foreign Ministry issued a statement tonight saying that the U.S. invasion of Panama is a blatant violation of the United Nations Charter and a violation of international law.

	The Algerian Foreign Ministry spokesperson issued a statement tonight saying that the U.S. armed invasion of Panama is a trampling of the United Nations Charter. The statement demanded that the U.S. immediately stop its invasion of Panama and resolve disputes through peaceful means.

	The Iranian Foreign Ministry issued a statement on the 20th saying that the U.S. military actions are a public violation of Panama's sovereignty, exposing the aggressive nature of the U.S. government.

	Xinhua News Agency, Moscow, December 20: Soviet Foreign Ministry spokesman Perfiliev demanded that the U.S. immediately stop its armed intervention in Panama.

	The spokesman said at a press conference on the 20th that the U.S. military invasion of Panama has caused great concern in the Soviet Union, and these actions violate the United Nations Charter and international norms.

	In response to the question of whether this incident would have a negative impact on Soviet-U.S. relations, Perfiliev said that not every event in the world necessarily affects East-West relations.

	On the same day, the Chairman of the International Affairs Committee of the Supreme Soviet, Zasokhov, also issued a statement condemning the U.S. invasion of Panama.

### 一些国家谴责美国入侵巴拿马
郭伟成  朱满庭  李志明  兰才基
1989-12-22
第4版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    一些国家谴责美国入侵巴拿马
    本报墨西哥城12月20日电　记者郭伟成报道：今天，墨西哥政府、参众两院、联邦区代表大会分别发表声明或通过决议，谴责美国入侵巴拿马，要求美国立即从巴拿马撤兵，并且指出，没有任何理由可以成为美国武力干涉主权国家的借口。墨西哥群众还在美国使馆外举行示威活动。
    本报加拉加斯12月21日电  记者朱满庭报道：委内瑞拉政府昨天发表公报指出，美国对巴拿马干涉是“拉丁美洲各国政府和人民都不能接受的”。公报要求美国入侵部队撤出巴拿马。佩雷斯总统在当天的讲话中谴责并拒绝美国军事侵略的立场。
    尼加拉瓜总统奥尔特加强烈谴责美国的侵略行径并下令军队和全国处于高度戒备。奥尔特加指出，美国在巴拿马的行径“不仅是对巴拿马主权和领土完整的粗暴侵犯，也是对整个拉丁美洲和平与安全的威胁。
    本报布宜诺斯艾利斯12月20日电　记者李志明报道：阿根廷政府今天发表公报，表示对巴拿马局势的发展感到震惊，要求美国尽快从巴拿马撤出武装部队。今晚，阿根廷一些政党在首都组织了示威活动，抗议美国入侵巴拿马。
    本报巴西利亚12月20日电  记者兰才基报道：巴西总统萨尔内今天发表谈话表示，巴西政府反对美国对巴拿马的武装入侵。他指出，这一行动“是美国与美洲国家关系上的一个后退”，并对此“深感遗憾”。
    巴西外交部今天发表公报谴责美国对巴拿马的军事入侵。巴西外长索德今天还召见美国驻巴西大使，表明巴西政府反对美国政府对巴拿马进行军事干涉的立场。
    巴西议会今天也发表公报，对美国政府武装入侵巴拿马的行动表示强烈谴责。
    据新华社北京12月21日电  综合本社驻中东记者报道，叙利亚、阿尔及利亚和伊朗今天发表官方公报和声明，强烈谴责美军入侵巴拿马。
    利比亚对外关系局（外交部）今晚发表公报说，美国入侵巴拿马是明目张胆地破坏了联合国宪章和违反了国际法准则。
    阿尔及利亚外交部发言人今晚发表声明说，美国对巴拿马进行武装入侵是对联合国宪章的践踏。声明要求美国应立即停止对巴拿马的入侵，并通过和平途径解决争端。
    伊朗外交部20日发表声明说，美国的军事行动是对巴拿马主权的公开侵犯，暴露了美国政府的侵略本质。
    新华社莫斯科12月20日电  苏联外交部发言人佩尔菲利耶夫要求美国立即停止对巴拿马的武装干涉。
    这位发言人在20日举行的记者招待会上说，美国军队入侵巴拿马的行动引起苏联的极大关切，这些行动违背联合国宪章和国际关系准则。
    在回答这一事件是否将对苏美关系产生消极影响的问题时，佩尔菲利耶夫说，世界上每一个事件不一定都影响东西方关系。
    同一天，苏联最高苏维埃国际问题委员会主席扎索霍夫也发表讲话，谴责美国入侵巴拿马的行动。
