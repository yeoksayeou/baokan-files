Title: Latin American Film Workers Condemn U.S. Establishment of Anti-Cuba TV Station
Author:
Date: 1989-12-16
Edition: 4

Latin American film workers, reporters, and critics participating in the 11th Havana International Film Festival issued a statement on December 14 condemning the United States for establishing a television station specifically aimed at opposing Cuba.

According to reports, the United States is spending nearly ten million dollars to build the "Marti TV" station in southern Florida for anti-Cuba propaganda.

The statement said that the establishment of an anti-Cuba TV station by the United States is an infringement on Cuba's cultural and political sovereignty, a violation of the United Nations Charter and the regulations of the International Telecommunication Union, and a disregard for the principles of coexistence among nations.

The statement believes that the establishment of an anti-Cuba TV station by the United States will affect regional stability and encourage political and cultural aggression, posing a threat not only to Cuba but also to the peace and security of the entire region.

### 拉美电影工作者谴责美建立反古电视台

1989-12-16
第4版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    拉美电影工作者谴责美建立反古电视台
    新华社哈瓦那12月14日电  正在这里参加第11届哈瓦那国际电影节的拉美电影工作者、记者和评论家14日发表声明，谴责美国设立专门反对古巴的电视台。
    据报道，美国耗资近千万美元正在佛罗里达州南部建设“马蒂电视台”，用来进行反古宣传。
    声明说，美国建立反古电视台是损害古巴的文化和政治主权，违背了联合国宪章和国际通讯联盟的规定，践踏了国家间共处准则。
    声明认为，美国建立反古电视台将会影响地区稳定和起到鼓励政治文化侵略的作用，这不仅威胁古巴，同时也将威胁整个地区的和平和安全。
