Title: Walls Have Ears
Author: Zhū Gēnhuá
Date: 1989-12-21
Edition: 7

    The world is changing rapidly. The Cold War is coming to an end, and the East-West confrontation is easing. The international situation is moving towards a more peaceful and stable direction. However, this does not mean that the world is completely free from turmoil. On the contrary, the contradictions and conflicts in the world are becoming more complex and intertwined. In this context, the issue of national security has become more prominent.

    National security is not just about military defense. It includes political, economic, cultural, and social security. In the era of information, the security of information has become an important part of national security. Information security refers to the protection of information from unauthorized access, use, disclosure, disruption, modification, or destruction. It is a comprehensive concept that includes technical, managerial, and legal aspects.

    In recent years, with the rapid development of information technology, the issue of information security has become increasingly prominent. The widespread use of computers and the Internet has made information more accessible, but it has also increased the risk of information leakage and theft. Hackers, viruses, and other malicious programs have become a major threat to information security. In addition, the globalization of the economy has made the flow of information more frequent, and the risk of information leakage and theft has increased accordingly.

    In this context, it is particularly important to strengthen information security. First, we need to improve our awareness of information security. We should realize that information is a valuable resource, and its security is closely related to national security. We should strengthen our awareness of information security and take effective measures to protect it.

    Second, we need to strengthen our technical capabilities in information security. We should invest more in research and development of information security technologies, and improve our ability to prevent and respond to information security incidents. We should also strengthen international cooperation in information security, and learn from the experiences of other countries.

    Third, we need to strengthen our legal system in information security. We should establish and improve laws and regulations related to information security, and ensure that they are effectively implemented. We should also strengthen the supervision and management of information security, and punish those who violate information security laws and regulations.

    In short, information security is an important part of national security. We should attach great importance to it and take effective measures to protect it. Only in this way can we ensure the security of our country and people, and contribute to world peace and stability.
