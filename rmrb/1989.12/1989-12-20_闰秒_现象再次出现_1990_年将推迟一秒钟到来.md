Title: The Leap Second Phenomenon Occurs Again
Author: 
Date: 1989-12-20
Edition: 4

The leap second phenomenon has occurred again. 1990 will be delayed by one second.

LONDON, Dec. 18 (Xinhua) — Scientists at the Royal Greenwich Observatory in Britain reminded people today that when you are preparing to welcome the first day of the 1990s at midnight on Dec. 31, do not forget to set your clocks back by one second. In other words, 1990 will be delayed by one second.

John Pilkington, a doctor working at this world-famous observatory, said that because the Earth's rotation occasionally slows down irregularly, there is a slight difference between the time shown by highly precise artificial clocks and "solar time."

This phenomenon is similar to the leap year that occurs every four years and can be called a "leap second." Since the Greenwich Observatory began using lasers to measure the Earth's rotation in 1972, this "leap second" phenomenon has occurred 14 times.

### “闰秒”现象再次出现  1990年将推迟一秒钟到来

1989-12-20
第4版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    “闰秒”现象再次出现
    1990年将推迟一秒钟到来
    新华社伦敦12月18日电　英国皇家格林威治天文台的科学家们今天提醒说，当你们在12月31日午夜准备迎接90年代第一天的时候，请不要忘记把钟表向后拨慢一秒钟，也就是说，1990年将推迟一秒钟到来。
    在这座世界驰名的天文台工作的约翰·皮尔金顿博士说，由于地球转动偶尔呈现不规则放慢的状态，因此，超精度人造钟所显示的时间与“太阳时”之间存在着微小的差异。
    这种现象与4年一度的闰年颇为相似，可以称之为“闰秒”。自格林威治天文台从1972年开始使用激光测量地球转动以来，这种“闰秒”现象已经出现过14次。
