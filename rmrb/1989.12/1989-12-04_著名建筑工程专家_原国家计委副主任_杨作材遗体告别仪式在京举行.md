Title: Renowned Architectural Engineer and Former Vice Minister of the State Planning Commission Yang Zuocai's Funeral Held in Beijing
Author:
Date: 1989-12-04
Edition: 2

The funeral of Yang Zuocai, an outstanding member of the Communist Party of China, a loyal communist warrior, a renowned architectural engineer, and former vice minister and advisor of the State Planning Commission, was held in Beijing today. Yang passed away on November 12, 1989, in Beijing due to a sudden heart attack. He was 78 years old.

Yang Zuocai was born in 1912 in Hukou County, Jiujiang, Jiangxi Province, into a family of builders. In his early years, he went to Japan to study architectural engineering. He graduated from the Law Department of Wuhan University in 1936. In May 1936, he joined the revolutionary cause and became a member of the Communist Party of China in August 1938. During the War of Resistance Against Japanese Aggression, he served as a staff member of the Political Department of the Eighth Route Army, director of the General Affairs Office of the Academy of Natural Sciences in Yan'an, and deputy director of the Office of the Central Military Commission. He was mainly in charge of the construction work in Yan'an, the location of the Central Headquarters. During the War of Liberation, he held various positions, including director of the Secretariat of the Political Department of the Jin-Cha-Ji-Liao Military Region, director of the Enemy Work Department of the Hezhongdi Committee, director of the Transportation Bureau of Hebei Province, and director of the Northeast Forestry Bureau. After the liberation of the entire country, he served as deputy director of the Office of the Ministry of Heavy Industry, director of the Design Bureau of the Ministry of Metallurgy, deputy director and member of the Party Group, and secretary of the Party Committee of the State Construction Commission, deputy director and member of the Party Group, deputy secretary of the Party Committee, and deputy director of the Consultative Group of the State Planning Commission.

Yang Zuocai was a member of the Fourth and Fifth National Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference. His life was a revolutionary life, a life dedicated to the cause of the Party and the people. Over more than half a century of revolutionary career, he went through the trials and tests of the War of Resistance Against Japanese Aggression, the War of Liberation, the socialist revolution, and construction. He worked tirelessly, fought tenaciously, and devoted all his efforts to the cause of liberating the Chinese people and building socialist modernization, contributing his entire life to these endeavors.

### 著名建筑工程专家　原国家计委副主任  杨作材遗体告别仪式在京举行

1989-12-04
第2版(国内新闻)
专栏：

    著名建筑工程专家　原国家计委副主任
    杨作材遗体告别仪式在京举行
    新华社北京12月1日电  中国共产党优秀党员，忠诚的共产主义战士，著名建筑工程专家，原国家计委副主任、顾问杨作材同志，因心脏病突发，经抢救无效，于1989年11月12日在北京逝世，终年78岁。
    今天在八宝山革命公墓为杨作材同志举行了遗体告别仪式。姚依林、宋平、薛暮桥、王鹤寿、韩光、吕东、袁宝华、安志文、郭洪涛、柴树藩、戚元靖、柳随年、杨波、曾志、房维中等同志以及数百名干部群众参加了遗体告别仪式。杨尚昆、李鹏、丁关根、薄一波、余秋里、康世恩、谷牧、程子华等送了花圈。
    杨作材同志是江西省九江湖口县人，1912年出生于一个营造世家。早年曾到日本考察建筑工程，1936年毕业于武汉大学法律系。1936年5月参加革命工作，1938年8月加入中国共产党。在抗日战争时期，他曾任八路军总政治部敌工科科员，延安自然科学院总务处处长，中央军委办公厅副主任，主要主持中央所在地延安的建筑工作。解放战争时期，历任冀察热辽军区政治部秘书处处长，热中地委敌工部部长，热河省交通局局长，东北林务总局局长等职。全国解放后，曾任重工业部办公厅副主任，冶金部设计司司长，国家建委副主任、党组成员、党委书记，国家计委副主任、党组成员、党委副书记、咨询小组副组长等职。
    杨作材同志是第四、五届全国政协委员。他的一生，是革命的一生，是为党和人民的事业奋斗的一生。在半个多世纪的革命生涯中，他经历了抗日战争、解放战争、社会主义革命和建设的锻炼和考验，呕心沥血，顽强战斗，鞠躬尽瘁，为中国人民的解放事业和社会主义现代化建设事业献出了毕生的精力。
