Title: Namibian Constitutional Assembly Issues Statement Banning Privatization of Public Facilities
Author:
Date: 1989-12-15
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, Windhoek, December 13th.

The Namibian Constitutional Assembly issued a statement on December 12th, prohibiting the privatization or leasing of any public facilities during the transitional period leading to independence.

The statement explicitly states that starting from 1990, all schools must be open to all children regardless of race, color, or religious affiliation.

The statement is clearly directed at the South African-appointed administrator of Namibia, Pienaar, who attempted to privatize 15 schools.

The statement said that such attempts at racial segregation have deeply hurt the feelings of the vast majority of Namibian citizens and will seriously harm efforts to achieve national reconciliation and peace.

The statement demands that Pienaar cease the implementation of the plan to privatize or lease schools.

SWAPO (South West Africa People's Organization) Chairman Sam Nujoma previously told Namibian church leaders that SWAPO would be "very unwilling" to see anyone in independent Namibia use the preservation of cultural and religious practices as a cover for racial segregation.

### 纳米比亚制宪议会发表声明  禁止将任何公共设施私有化

1989-12-15
第4版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    纳米比亚制宪议会发表声明
    禁止将任何公共设施私有化
    新华社温得和克12月13日电　纳米比亚制宪议会12日发表一项声明，禁止在独立过渡时期将任何公共设施私有化或出租转让。
    声明明确表示，从1990年1月起所有学校都必须不分种族、肤色和教派，对所有儿童开放。
    声明显然是针对南非驻纳米比亚行政长官皮纳尔企图把15所学校私有化而发表的。
    声明说，这种种族隔离的企图伤害了纳米比亚绝大多数公民的感情，也将对实现民族和解与和平的努力造成极大危害。
    声明要求皮纳尔停止实施把学校私有化或出租转让的计划。
    西南非洲人民组织（人组）主席努乔马前些时候对纳米比亚教会领导人说，人组将“非常不愿意”看到任何人在独立后的纳米比亚以保持文化与宗教习惯为名，行种族隔离之实。
