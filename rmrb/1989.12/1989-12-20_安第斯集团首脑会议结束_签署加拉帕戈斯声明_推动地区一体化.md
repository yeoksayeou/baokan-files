Title: Andean Group Summit Concludes
Author: Zhū Mǎn Tíng
Date: 1989-12-20
Edition: 4

The Andean Group Summit concluded yesterday on the Galapagos Islands. The five presidents signed the "Galapagos Declaration on Andean Peace, Security, and Cooperation," expressing their commitment to taking practical steps to promote regional integration.

The five countries of the Andean Group—Bolivia, Colombia, Ecuador, Peru, and Venezuela—declared in the "Galapagos Declaration" that they oppose the use of force in resolving disputes among member states and pledge not to take actions that harm any country's territorial integrity, political independence, or unity.

On the issue of drug trafficking, which is a common concern of the Andean countries, the five presidents agreed that drug trafficking poses a serious threat to the peace, development, and stability of their nations and to regional security. Therefore, they reached a consensus to coordinate policies on combating terrorism and drug trafficking.

The summit also issued a document outlining the strategic goals for economic integration in the region, which includes the eventual establishment of a common currency.

### 安第斯集团首脑会议结束  签署加拉帕戈斯声明　推动地区一体化
朱满庭
1989-12-20
第4版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    安第斯集团首脑会议结束
    签署加拉帕戈斯声明　推动地区一体化
    本报加拉加斯12月19日电  记者朱满庭报道：在厄瓜多尔加拉帕戈斯群岛举行的安第斯集团首脑会议昨天结束，5国总统签署了一项《安第斯和平、安全与合作加拉帕戈斯声明》，总统们表示要采取实际步骤推动地区一体化。
    由玻利维亚、哥伦比亚、厄瓜多尔、秘鲁和委内瑞拉组成的安第斯集团5国首脑在《加拉帕戈斯声明》中表示，反对在处理成员国之间的关系时使用武力，并承担义务不采取于有害任何国家领土完整、政治独立和团结的行动。
    在安第斯国家共同关心的毒品贸易问题上，5国总统认为，贩毒问题正严重威胁着各国的安宁、发展和稳定，威胁着地区安全。因此，在5国首脑达成一致，在反对恐怖主义和贩毒问题上协调政策。
    会议还发表了一份确定该地区经济一体化战略目标的文件，其中包括将来建立一种共同货币。
