Title: Soviet and American Officials Discuss Topics for Summit Meeting
Author: Zhang Qixun
Date: 1989-12-01
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, Moscow, November 29th.

	Soviet and American Officials Discuss Topics for Summit Meeting
	Discussions to Include Soviet-American Relations, Arms Control, and Regional Issues

	Soviet Foreign Ministry Spokesman First Deputy Director Gremitsky said at a press conference in Moscow on November 29th that the upcoming Soviet-American summit meeting in Malta will cover a wide range of issues and will, to varying degrees, touch upon all aspects of arms control.

	Gremitsky also stated, "Of course, this summit meeting does not necessarily have to result in specific agreements on particular issues." He said, "We all have reason to believe that this meeting will greatly promote mutual understanding and provide new impetus to the negotiations on reducing offensive strategic weapons, accelerating the signing of the relevant treaties."

	When asked about naval forces, he said, "The issue of naval forces should be one aspect of arms reduction talks. It is not possible to reduce in one area while ignoring another, as this would lead to unstable consequences."

	He said, "We believe that negotiations on reducing naval forces will eventually begin, naturally, the earlier the better."

	Our correspondent Zhang Qixun reported from Washington on November 29th: U.S. Secretary of State Baker held a press conference at the White House today regarding the U.S.-Soviet summit meeting, saying that President Bush will discuss Soviet-American relations and issues of mutual interest with Chairman Gorbachev.

	Baker said that this meeting is taking place at a very special time in U.S.-Soviet relations, and the United States is willing to work with the Soviet Union to seek mutual benefits. Baker listed five areas that Bush will discuss with Gorbachev: First, recent changes in Eastern Europe. Baker said that the United States will make it clear to the Soviet Union that the United States will not seek unilateral advantages, but believes that any attempt to interfere with or obstruct reforms will be destabilizing and dangerous. Second, regional conflicts. Baker emphasized that Soviet actions in Central America remain the biggest obstacle to comprehensive improvement in bilateral relations. Third, discuss the progress of arms control and how to further advance arms control. Fourth, listen to Gorbachev's views on future Soviet reforms. Fifth, listen to the introduction of the Soviet economic situation. Baker said that the United States is willing to provide technical and economic cooperation in a wide range of areas.

### 苏美官员谈首脑会晤议题  将讨论苏美关系、军备控制和地区问题
张启昕
1989-12-01
第4版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    苏美官员谈首脑会晤议题
    将讨论苏美关系、军备控制和地区问题
    新华社莫斯科11月29日电  苏联外交部新闻局第一副局长格列米茨基赫29日在莫斯科举行的新闻发布会上说，即将举行的苏美首脑马耳他会晤将讨论广泛的问题，并在这样或那样的程度上涉及军备控制的所有问题。
    格列米茨基赫同时表示，“当然，这次首脑会晤不一定就具体问题签署形式上的协议。”他说，“我们都有理由认为，这次会晤将会大大促进相互间的真正了解，并对裁减进攻性战略武器谈判给予新的推动，加快签署相应的条约。”
    他在回答关于海军力量的问题时说，“海军问题应当成为裁军对话的一个方面，不可能在一个方面裁减而放弃另一方面，这种做法会导致不稳定的后果。”
    他说，“我们认为，关于裁减海军力量的谈判迟早会开始，自然，早开始比晚开始要好。”
    本报华盛顿11月29日电  记者张启昕报道：美国国务卿贝克今天在白宫就美苏首脑会晤举行记者招待会时说，布什总统将与戈尔巴乔夫主席讨论美苏关系及与双方利益有关的问题。
    贝克说，这次会晤是在美苏关系非常特殊的时期举行的，美国乐于同苏联一起寻求对双方彼此有利的东西。贝克列举布什将与戈尔巴乔夫讨论的5个方面：一、东欧最近发生的变化。贝克说，美国将让苏联知道，美国不会寻求单方面的好处，但认为任何干预或阻止改革的企图都将是破坏稳定的，危险的。二、地区冲突。贝克强调苏联在中美洲的行径仍是全面改善两国关系的最大障碍。三、讨论军备控制的进展以及如何进一步推进军控。四、听取戈尔巴乔夫对于苏联今后改革的看法。五、听取苏联经济情况的介绍。贝克说，美国愿意在广泛领域提供技术经济合作。
