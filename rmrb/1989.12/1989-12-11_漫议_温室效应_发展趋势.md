Title: A Casual Discussion on the Development Trend of the "Greenhouse Effect"
Author: Zhang Youxin
Date: 1989-12-11
Edition: 7

The problem of atmospheric pollution may evolve into an international political issue.

The U.S. government claims that the U.S. will "defend the air we breathe"; Congressmen even call for the U.S. to "set an example for the rest of the world in significantly reducing carbon dioxide." At first glance, it seems that the U.S. is serious about it. But in reality, it is not.

According to the latest report from the World Resources Institute, the global carbon dioxide emissions in the previous year were 6 billion tons, of which the U.S. accounted for 1.42 billion tons, or 23.7% of the world's total emissions. This means that nearly 1/4 of the world's emissions come from the U.S. Moreover, the growth rate of carbon dioxide emissions in the U.S. is faster than in other regions. The global growth rate was 3.6% last year, while the U.S. reached 4.1%. It is clear that the current severe atmospheric pollution is a responsibility that the U.S. cannot shirk.

The U.S. should take the lead in reducing carbon dioxide emissions, but it faces resistance from the U.S. U.S. government officials have threatened that even if the U.S. emits more than other countries, it cannot unilaterally limit emissions. It must "deal with some developing countries" before taking action, otherwise, the U.S. will "lose the leverage to force developing countries to implement controls (on carbon dioxide)." This is obviously a strategy for the U.S. to exert pressure and shift responsibility. Indeed, the atmosphere has no borders, and preventing air pollution is a global endeavor. However, the U.S. emits so much carbon dioxide, and its growth rate is so fast, that it not only causes concern among U.S. citizens but also arouses dissatisfaction from the international community, especially developing countries. The Washington Post also admits that people are now "more strongly calling for the U.S. to unilaterally limit carbon dioxide."

Humanity is facing the grim reality of air pollution. Among them, carbon dioxide has become the main culprit in global warming. Scientists have calculated that over the past century, the concentration of carbon dioxide in the atmosphere has risen from 274 PPM to 350 PPM. If carbon dioxide rises to 550 PPM, the global average temperature will rise by 3-9 degrees Fahrenheit each year, with serious consequences. Therefore, the attitude of the U.S. is crucial to the development trend of the global "greenhouse effect."

### 漫议“温室效应”发展趋势
张友新
1989-12-11
第7版(国际专页)
专栏：科学漫话

    漫议“温室效应”发展趋势
    张友新
    大气污染问题可能演变为国际政治问题。
    美国政府宣称，美国要
  “保卫我们所呼吸的空气”；国会议员们甚至呼吁，在大量减少二氧化碳方面，美国“要为世界其他国家树立榜样”。乍听，美国似乎要动真格的了。而事实上呢？并非如此。
    据世界资源研究所最新报告，去年全球二氧化碳排放量为60亿吨，其中美国有14.2亿吨，占全世界排放量的23.7％。这就是说，全世界排放量中有近1／4是美国释放的。而且，美国二氧化碳排放量的增长速度比世界其他地区快，去年全世界的增长率为3.6％，而美国达到4.1％。由此可见，当今大气遭到严重污染，美国负有不可推卸的责任。
    美国的责任是率先减少二氧化碳的排放量，但遭到美国的抵制。美国政府官员扬言，即使美国的排放量比其他国家多，也不能单方面限制，要与一些发展中国家“打交道”后才能行动，否则，美国“就会失去逼迫发展中国家实行控制（二氧化碳）的手段”。这显然是美国施加压力、推卸责任的一种策略。诚然，大气是没有国界的，防止空气污染是全人类的事业。但是，美国排放二氧化碳之多，其增长速度之快，不仅造成美国公民的不安，也激起国际社会特别是发展中国家的不满。《华盛顿邮报》也承认，目前人们“更强烈地呼吁美国单方面限制二氧化碳”。
    人类面临着空气遭到污染的严峻事实。其中，二氧化碳已成了全球气温上升的罪魁祸首。据科学家推算，在过去一个世纪里，大气中的二氧化碳含量从274PPM上升至350PPM，若二氧化碳上升至550PPM，全球每年平均气温将会上升3—9华氏度，后果十分严重。所以，美国采取何种态度，对于世界“温室效应”发展趋势关系重大。
