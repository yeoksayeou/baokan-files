Title: Indian President Discusses Domestic and Foreign Policies
Author:
Date: 1989-12-28
Edition: 4

Indian President R. Venkataraman today said in a joint session of parliament that the government will strive to resolve major bilateral issues with neighboring countries.

Venkataraman said India will continue to strengthen mutual understanding and cooperation with China. He hoped that the Sino-Indian border issue could be "justly and reasonably resolved."

He said that while maintaining the existing friendly relations with the Soviet Union, India will develop a constructive cooperative relationship with the United States.

Venkataraman stressed the importance of restoring and strengthening relations with neighboring countries in South Asia. He said India will work to promote regional cooperation and stability.

In discussing domestic issues, Venkataraman said the government "will not yield to separatism nor will it make concessions to extremists." However, he said the government is determined to achieve national harmony and will resolve the problems facing the country through the consensus of the public.

In terms of the economy, Venkataraman said the government will focus on addressing inflation and effectively controlling government expenditure and fiscal deficit.

### 印度总统谈内外政策  表示将加强与中国的理解与合作

1989-12-28
第4版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    印度总统谈内外政策
    表示将加强与中国的理解与合作
    新华社新德里12月20日电  印度总统拉瓦斯瓦米·文卡塔拉曼今天在议会联席会议上说，政府将尽力解决和邻国的主要双边问题。
    文卡塔拉曼总统说，印度将继续加强与中国的相互理解及合作。他希望中印边界问题能“公正、合理地得到解决”。
    他说，在保持和苏联已有的友好关系的同时，印度将发展同美国建设性的合作关系。
    文卡塔拉曼总统强调恢复和加强与南亚地区邻国关系的重要性。他说，印度将致力于促进地区合作与稳定。
    在谈及国内问题时，文卡塔拉曼说，政府“决不向分裂主义妥协，也决不向极端分子让步”。但他说，政府决心实现民族的和谐并将通过公众的一致意见来解决国家面临的问题。
    在经济方面文卡塔拉曼总统说，政府将重点解决通货膨胀问题及有效控制政府开支和财政赤字。
