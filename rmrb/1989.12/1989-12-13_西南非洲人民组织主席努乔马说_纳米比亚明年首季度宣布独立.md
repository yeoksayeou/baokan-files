Title: SWAPO Chairman Nujoma Says Namibia Will Announce Independence in the First Quarter of 1990
Author:
Date: 1989-12-13
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, Windhoek, December 11th.

SWAPO Chairman Nujoma announced that Namibia will declare independence in the first quarter of 1990.

Nujoma said in the afternoon of the 10th at a mass rally in the black township of Katutura on the outskirts of the capital, "The Constituent Assembly has made significant progress in its three weeks of work. I believe that the drafting of the independence constitution will be completed in a relatively short time."

Nujoma also warned in his speech about certain church forces attempting to turn 16 public schools into white private schools. He said, "All schools, including private schools, will be under the leadership of the national education department, and it is absolutely not allowed to use a single cent of the state's money to cultivate apartheid forces."

This rally was held to commemorate the 30th anniversary of the 1959 massacre. On December 10, 1959, the South African colonial authorities suppressed a peaceful demonstration by black residents of Katutura against forced relocation at the old cemetery, resulting in 13 deaths, 40 injuries, and many arrests.

### 西南非洲人民组织主席努乔马说  纳米比亚明年首季度宣布独立

1989-12-13
第4版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    西南非洲人民组织主席努乔马说
    纳米比亚明年首季度宣布独立
    新华社温得和克12月11日电　西南非洲人民组织主席努乔马宣布，纳米比亚将在1990年第一季度宣布独立。
    努乔马10日下午在首都郊区黑人城镇卡图图拉的一次群众集会上发表讲话说，制宪议会在成立3周来，工作取得很大进展。他相信独立宪法的起草将在一个较短时间内完成。
    努乔马在讲话中还警告了某些教会势力把16所公办学校变为白人私立学校的企图。他说，包括私立学校在内的所有学校都将置于国家教育部领导之下，绝不容许把国家的一分钱用于培植种族隔离势力。
    这次集会是为了纪念1959年大屠杀事件30周年而举行的。1959年12月10日，南非殖民当局镇压了卡图图拉黑人居民在旧公墓举行反对强行搬迁的和平示威，13人遭杀害，40人受伤，许多人被逮捕。
