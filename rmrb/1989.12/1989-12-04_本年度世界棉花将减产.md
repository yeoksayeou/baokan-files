Title: World Cotton Production to Decline in the Current Year
Author:
Date: 1989-12-04
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, Mexico City,

The International Cotton Advisory Committee recently announced in Mexico City that world cotton production for the 1989-1990 season will be significantly lower than consumption.

The International Cotton Advisory Committee, citing materials from the U.S. Department of Agriculture, said that this year's world cotton production will be 79.9 million bales (each bale weighing 218 kilograms), while consumption will reach 86 million bales.

The committee reviewed that in the 1988-1989 season, world cotton production was 84.2 million bales, and consumption was 85 million bales.

The committee pointed out that the main reason for the decline in cotton production in 1989-1990 is the reduction in cotton production in the world's major cotton-producing countries, China and the United States.

### 本年度世界棉花将减产

1989-12-04
第4版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    本年度世界棉花将减产
    新华社墨西哥城电　国际棉花咨询委员会最近在这里宣布，1989至1990年度世界棉花产量将大大低于消费量。
    国际棉花咨询委员会引用美国农业部的材料说，本年度世界棉花产量将为7990万包（每包重218公斤），而消费量将达8600万包。
    这个委员会回顾说，1988至1989年度世界棉花产量为8420万包，消费量为8500万包。
    这个委员会指出，世界产棉大国中国和美国棉花产量减少是造成1989至1990年棉花减产的主要原因。
