Title: OAS and Others Condemn U.S. Invasion of Panama
Author: Guo Weicheng
Date: 1989-12-24
Edition: 4

The Organization of American States today passed a resolution condemning the U.S. invasion of Panama with 20 votes in favor, 6 abstentions, and 1 vote (the U.S.) against. This resolution was adopted after two days of debate. It is the first time the organization has passed a resolution against the United States. The Nicaraguan foreign minister said that this is a historic resolution, a victory for Latin American countries.

The Canadian representative said that the Canadian government regrets the use of force by the United States.

The Peruvian representative said that Latin American countries will not accept and firmly protest the United States' rude interference in the internal affairs of other countries.

The Panamanian representative condemned the U.S. savage invasion during the debate and accused the United States of massacring unarmed Panamanian civilians.

Although the United States recognizes Endara as the legitimate representative of the Panamanian government, Venezuela, Ecuador, Colombia, and Peru, members of the Organization of American States, refuse to recognize the Endara government.

Due to its opposition to the U.S. invasion of Panama, the United States decided today to cancel its support for Peru's anti-drug efforts.

According to Xinhua News Agency, Beijing, December 21, some Latin American parliaments, political parties, and mass organizations issued statements on December 20, condemning the U.S. invasion of Panama.

The Venezuelan House of Representatives passed a resolution on December 20, condemning the U.S. armed intervention in Panama and demanding the withdrawal of the invading troops.

Ecuadorian National Assembly President Wilfredo Lucero said that the United States has violated the principle of non-interference in the internal affairs of other countries.

The Chilean Democratic Opposition Party Alliance issued a statement saying: "We condemn the use of force and various forms of intervention to resolve differences between countries."

The Permanent Secretariat of the Latin American Economic System issued a statement expressing serious concern over the U.S. military intervention in Panama.

The Afro-Asian-Latin American Solidarity Organization based in Cuba issued a statement calling on the international community to support Panama.

The Central American Workers' Coordinating Committee, composed of 14 trade unions in Central America, called on the U.S. government to respect the Panamanian government and demanded that the U.S. military immediately stop its invasion of Panama.

The International Chairman of the Christian Democratic Party, Edward Fernandez, believes that the only way to restore normal order in Panama is for U.S. troops to withdraw.

### 美洲国家组织等谴责美军入侵巴拿马  一些拉美国家拒绝承认恩达拉政府
郭伟成
1989-12-24
第4版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    美洲国家组织等谴责美军入侵巴拿马
    一些拉美国家拒绝承认恩达拉政府
    本报墨西哥城12月22日电　记者郭伟成报道：美洲国家组织今天以20票赞成、6票弃权、1票（美国）反对，通过一项决议，谴责美国入侵巴拿马。
    这项决议是美洲国家组织经过两天辩论后通过的。这是该组织第一次通过一项反对美国的决议。尼加拉瓜外长说，这是一个历史性的决议，是拉美国家的胜利。
    加拿大代表说，加拿大政府对美国动用武力表示遗憾。
    秘鲁代表说，拉美国家决不接受并坚决抗议美国粗暴干涉他国的行动。
    巴拿马代表在辩论时谴责了美国的野蛮入侵，控诉美国屠杀手无寸铁的巴拿马平民。
    虽然美国承认恩达拉为巴政府合法代表，但委内瑞拉、厄瓜多尔、哥伦比亚和秘鲁等美洲国家组织成员国拒不承认恩达拉政府。
    秘鲁由于反对美国入侵巴拿马，美国今天决定取消对秘鲁反毒斗争给予支持。
    据新华社北京12月21日电　一些拉美国家的议会、政党和群众组织12月20日纷纷发表声明，谴责美国入侵巴拿马。
    委内瑞拉众议院20日通过一项决议，谴责美国武装干涉巴拿马，并要求入侵的武装部队撤走。
    厄瓜多尔国民议会议长维尔弗里多·卢塞罗说，美国破坏了不干涉别国内部事务的原则。
    智利争取民主反对派政党联盟发表声明说：“我们谴责使用武力和各种干涉来解决国家间的分歧。”
    拉美经济体系常设秘书处发表声明，对美国在巴拿马进行的军事干涉表示严重关切。
    设在古巴的亚、非、拉人民团结组织发表声明，要求国际社会声援巴拿马。
    由中美洲14个工会组织组成的中美洲工人协调委员会呼吁美国政府尊重巴拿马政权，要求美军立即停止对巴拿马的入侵。
    基民党国际主席爱德华·费尔南德斯认为，恢复巴拿马正常秩序的唯一办法就是美国军队撤走。
