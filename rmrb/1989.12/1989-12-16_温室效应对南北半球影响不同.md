Title: The Greenhouse Effect Affects the Southern and Northern Hemispheres Differently
Author:
Date: 1989-12-16
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, London, December 11th.

According to a report in the journal *Nature*, American scientists have discovered that the greenhouse effect has different impacts on the temperatures of the Earth's Southern and Northern Hemispheres.

Scientists at the Geophysical Fluid Dynamics Laboratory of Princeton University used computer simulations of the Earth's climate to analyze the interactions between the Earth's atmosphere and ocean currents.

They compared the future trends of the Earth's climate under two hypothetical conditions. One hypothesis assumes that the concentration of the primary greenhouse gas, carbon dioxide, remains at its current levels. The other hypothesis assumes that the concentration of carbon dioxide in the Earth's atmosphere increases by 1% each year, which is roughly equivalent to the current combined effect of all greenhouse gases causing the Earth's temperature to rise.

The results of the simulations were surprising: the Southern Hemisphere's temperature rises more slowly, while the Northern Hemisphere's temperature rises more quickly and increases with higher latitudes.

### 温室效应对南北半球影响不同

1989-12-16
第4版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    温室效应对南北半球影响不同
    新华社伦敦12月11日电　据《自然》杂志报道，美国科学家发现，温室效应对地球南半球和北半球的气温产生不同的影响。
    美国普林斯顿大学地球物理流体动力学实验室的科学家，用计算机模拟地球气候，以分析地球大气同海洋环流的相互作用。
    他们比较两种假设条件下地球气候的未来趋势。一种假设是，主要的温室气体二氧化碳的浓度保持在现有的值不变；另一种假设是，地球大气中二氧化碳的浓度每年增加1％，这大体上等于现在所有温室气体综合作用造成地球气温升高的增长率。
    模拟计算的结果显然出人意外，南、北半球对二氧化碳浓度增加的反应不同：南半球气温升高慢；北半球气温升高快，而且随纬度的增加而增加。
