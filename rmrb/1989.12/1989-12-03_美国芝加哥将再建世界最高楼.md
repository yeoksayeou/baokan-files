Title: Chicago to Build World's Tallest Tower Again
Author:
Date: 1989-12-03
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency

The 110-story Sears Tower, built in Chicago in 1974, remains the world's tallest building to this day. However, this record may be broken in three and a half years by the 125-story "Chicago Spire."

The Chicago Plan Commission approved the construction of the Chicago Spire near the Sears Tower on November 30, but the plan still needs the final signature from the city government.

The new office building, shaped like a pagoda, will be 1,950 feet high, 496 feet taller than the Sears Tower. It will include a 12-story parking garage, a fitness club, public cultural and entertainment facilities, and several restaurants. Its exterior will feature artistic decorations. Construction of the Chicago Spire is scheduled to begin in the summer of 1990, with completion expected in three years.

### 美国芝加哥将再建世界最高楼

1989-12-03
第4版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    美国芝加哥将再建世界最高楼
    芝加哥市一九七四年建成的一百一十层西尔斯大厦至今仍为世界最高楼，但是这一纪录可能在三年半后被一百二十五层高的“芝加哥尖塔”大厦所打破。
    芝加哥市计划委员会十一月三十日已批准在西尔斯大厦附近建造芝加哥尖塔，但该计划还须经市政府最后签字。
    这幢形似宝塔的新办公大楼的高度为一千九百五十英尺，比西尔斯高四百九十六英尺。楼内设有一个十二层的停车场、健身俱乐部、公共文化娱乐场和数家餐馆。其外表采用美术装潢。芝加哥尖塔拟于一九九○年夏破土，三年建成。
                                        （据新华社）
