Title: Nujoma Says Future Namibian Government Will Not Seize White-Owned Land
Author:
Date: 1989-12-03
Edition: 4

The chairman of the South West Africa People's Organization (SWAPO), Sam Nujoma, has stated that the future government of Namibia will not seize land from white farmers.

At the National Agricultural Union conference held on November 30, Nujoma said that taking land from farmers is not the policy of SWAPO.

Nujoma said that SWAPO's goal is to effectively utilize land to "ensure the achievement of our near-term goal of food self-sufficiency and reduce our dependence on South Africa for food supplies."

He further stated that the future government has the responsibility to ensure that every Namibian citizen who needs land for cultivation can obtain it.

He assured the attending farmers that the future government will redistribute land voluntarily sold by farmers, and that affected farmers will be fully compensated.

In Namibia, there are approximately 5,000 large commercial farms operated by whites.

### 努乔马主席说  纳未来政府不没收白人土地

1989-12-03
第4版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    努乔马主席说
    纳未来政府不没收白人土地
    新华社温得和克12月1日电　西南非洲人民组织（人组）主席努乔马说，未来的纳米比亚政府不会没收白人农场主的土地。
    在11月30日举行的全国农业联盟大会上，努乔马说，从农场主手里夺取土地不是人组的政策。
    努乔马说，人组所希望的是有效地利用土地，以“保障实现我们的粮食自给的近期目标，减少在粮食供给方面对南非的依赖”。
    他又说，未来政府有责任使每个需要耕地的纳米比亚公民获得土地。
    他向与会的农场主们保证，未来政府将把农场主自愿出卖的土地重新分配，受到影响的农场主将得到充分补偿。
    在纳全国，大约有5000个白人经营的大型商业农场。
