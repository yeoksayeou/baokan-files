Title: The Polish United Workers' Party Central Committee Politburo Issues a Statement Denouncing Anti-Soviet Sabotage by Some Individuals
Author:
Date: 1989-12-02
Edition: 4

The Polish United Workers' Party Central Committee Politburo today issued a statement condemning a group of deputies' proposal to abolish the July 22nd Polish Revival Day and denouncing some individuals' anti-Soviet propaganda, the burning of Lenin monuments, and the demolition of monuments to Dzerzhinsky and others.

The statement, released by the Polish News Agency, pointed out that the abolition of this holiday, established 45 years ago, is not a coincidence. It is an attempt to defame the 45 years of the People's Republic of Poland. People have reason to worry that this is preparing the ideological ground for a regression.

The statement listed the achievements made during the People's Republic of Poland in the development of industry, agriculture, culture, education, and national security. It asserted that describing the 45 years as a "wasted period" is a "historical falsehood" that hurts not only the party and its politicians but also several generations of Poles.

The statement pointed out that from persecuting monuments to persecuting opinions, from destroying monuments to destroying people, and from demonstratively changing the names of schools, factories, streets, and squares, "this is a dangerous warning signal to all democratic forces in Poland."

The statement concluded by calling on deputies to resist the aforementioned motions, respect Polish traditions, and "not replace old blank spots with new ones."

### 波党中央政治局发表声明  谴责一些人的反苏破坏行为

1989-12-02
第4版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    波党中央政治局发表声明
    谴责一些人的反苏破坏行为
    新华社华沙11月29日电  波兰统一工人党中央政治局今天发表声明，谴责一批议员提出的取消7月22日波兰复兴节的议案，谴责一些人进行反苏宣传，焚烧列宁纪念碑和拆毁捷尔任斯基等纪念碑的行为。
    由波通社播发的这项声明指出，取消45年前确定的这个节日并非偶然现象，这是妄图给人民波兰的45年抹黑，人们有理由担心，这是在思想上为倒退进程准备条件。
    声明列举了在人民波兰时期在发展工农业、文化教育以及保障国家安全等方面取得的成果，认为把45年说成是“荒废了的年代”，是“历史的虚伪”，它伤害的不仅仅是党和党的政治家，而是几代波兰人。
    声明指出，从迫害纪念碑到迫害观点，从毁灭纪念碑到毁灭人，示威性地改掉学校、工厂、街道和广场的名字，“是对波兰所有民主力量的危险的警告信号”。
    声明最后呼吁议员们抵制上述动议，尊重波兰传统，“不要以新的空白点代替旧的空白点”。
