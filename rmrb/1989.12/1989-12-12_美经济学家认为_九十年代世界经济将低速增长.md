Title: U.S. Economist Predicts Slow Growth for World Economy in the 1990s
Author:
Date: 1989-12-12
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, Beijing, December 9th.

U.S. economist predicts slow growth for world economy in the 1990s

	1980 Nobel Prize in Economics winner, Professor Lawrence R. Klein of the University of Pennsylvania, recently told a special correspondent from China's "Outlook" Weekly that the world economy will experience slow growth in the 1990s.

	Klein said that the global average annual growth rate over the next 10 years might be around 3%. However, if technological development accelerates and is commercialized, if disarmament efforts are successful, and if the economic integration of the Common Market progresses, the world economic growth rate could be between 3% and 4%.

	The first two years of the 1990s will see slow growth, but the pace will quicken after 1992. By the mid-1990s, issues such as energy will have adverse effects, slowing economic growth.

	Klein believes that despite facing various problems, developed industrial countries will have a relatively optimistic outlook over the next 10 years, with a growth rate of 3% to 4%, provided no explosive events occur and no new economic burdens are added.

	The U.S. economy will grow slowly next year, with an annual growth rate of about 1.5% to 2%. By the mid-1990s, the U.S. economy will return to a fast growth track.

	The slowdown in Western Europe's economic growth will lag behind the U.S., and the recovery will also be later than in the U.S.

	The average annual growth rate for the Third World might reach 4%, including the fast-developing "Four Asian Tigers." However, the outlook for the poorest countries in Africa and Latin America is not optimistic, mainly due to their unsolvable external debt and development issues. The heavy debt burden severely impacts the economic development of debtor countries, and this situation is not expected to improve significantly in the next three to five years.

### 美经济学家认为  九十年代世界经济将低速增长

1989-12-12
第4版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    美经济学家认为
    九十年代世界经济将低速增长
    新华社北京12月9日电　1980年诺贝尔经济学奖获得者、美国宾州大学教授劳伦斯·R·克莱因博士，最近在接受我国《瞭望》周刊特约记者访问的时候说，90年代世界经济将低速增长。
    克莱因说，全世界在今后10年里的年平均增长率可能是3％。但是，如果科技得到更快的发展并实现商业化，如果铸剑为犁的裁军愿望得以实现，如果共同市场的经济得到进一步发展，世界经济增长率可能在3％至4％之间。
    90年代前两年将是低速增长，到1992年以后，增长速度将加快，到90年代中期，能源等问题将产生不利影响，会使经济增长速度放慢。
    克莱因认为，发达工业国家尽管面临着种种问题，然而，如果不发生爆炸性的事变，如果发达国家经济不增加新的负担，它们在今后10年里的发展将相当令人乐观，增长率可能达到3％至
  4％。
    明年美国经济将发展缓慢，全年增长率可能在1.5％至2％之间。
    到90年代中期，美国经济将重新走上快速增长的轨道。
    西欧经济增长速度的放慢将晚于美国，回升也会晚于美国。
    第三世界经济的年平均增长率可能达到4％，其中包括发展较快的亚洲“四小龙”。但是，非洲和拉丁美洲最贫穷国家的前景却并不令人乐观，这主要是它们所面临的外债与发展问题难以解决。沉重的债务负担严重影响了债务国的经济发展，估计这种状况在三五年内不会有显著改善。
