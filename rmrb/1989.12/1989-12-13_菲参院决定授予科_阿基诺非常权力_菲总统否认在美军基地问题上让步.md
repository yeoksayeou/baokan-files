Title: Philippine Senate Decides to Grant Corazon Aquino Emergency Powers
Author:
Date: 1989-12-13
Edition: 4

The Philippine Senate decided on the evening of the 11th to expedite the passage of a bill granting Corazon Aquino emergency powers to handle matters related to the military coup and to address issues threatening national security.

The "Emergency Powers Act," once passed, will legitimize Corazon Aquino's declaration of a state of emergency in the Philippines from December 1 to 4 following the failed military coup. According to the bill, Corazon Aquino will have the authority, within six months of the bill's enactment, to order the seizure and control of hoarded food and goods. She will also have the power to temporarily suspend labor laws and regulations that she deems detrimental to production and food distribution. Additionally, she can order the takeover of private public utilities or commercial sectors that affect public interest.

Corazon Aquino stated that the government has already taken over two bus companies in Metro Manila.

The "Emergency Powers Act" faced opposition from opposition senators such as Juan Enrile.

The New China News Agency reported from Manila on the 11th that Philippine President Corazon Aquino denied making any promises regarding U.S. military bases while seeking American aid to quell the coup.

Corazon Aquino told reporters that she did seek American assistance on the morning of December 1, but during her telephone conversation with U.S. Ambassador to the Philippines Nicholas Platt, she did not discuss the issue of U.S. military bases in the Philippines.

Corazon Aquino made this statement in response to opposition criticism that she had promised concessions on U.S. military bases in exchange for American military support during the coup.

In this failed coup, the Philippine government forces, with air cover from U.S. aircraft, destroyed 8 rebel aircraft at Sangley Air Base, thereby turning the tide.

Philippine Senate President Salvador Laurel earlier stated that U.S. military aid had "weakened" the Philippines' position in future negotiations over U.S. military bases.

On December 7, after the remaining coup forces occupying the financial district of Makati in Manila surrendered to the government, Manila residents lined the streets to welcome the government troops entering the area.

### 菲参院决定授予科·阿基诺非常权力  菲总统否认在美军基地问题上让步

1989-12-13
第4版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    菲参院决定授予科·阿基诺非常权力
    菲总统否认在美军基地问题上让步
    新华社马尼拉12月12日电  菲律宾参议院11日晚决定尽快通过一项法案，赋予科·阿基诺总统以“非常权力”，以处理和军事政变有关的事务及解决危及国家安全的问题。
    这项名为“非常权力法”的法案俟获得通过，将认可科·阿基诺总统在12月1日至4日菲发生未遂军事政变以后，宣布全国进入紧急状态的做法。
    按照这项法案，科·阿基诺在法案生效6个月内，将有权下令没收和支配囤积的食物和商品，她也有权暂时终止一些她认为有碍于生产及食品分配的劳动法和条例；她还可以下令接管私营的公共事业部门或关系到公共利益的商业部门。
    科·阿基诺说，政府已接管了大马尼拉的两家公共汽车公司。
    “非常权力法”法案遭反对派议员胡安·恩里莱等人的反对。
    新华社马尼拉12月11日电菲律宾总统科拉松·阿基诺今天在此间否认她为平息政变而谋求美国援助时，曾在驻菲美军基地问题上作任何许诺。
    科·阿基诺对记者说，她在12月1日上午确实向美国寻求援助，但是她与美国驻菲大使普拉特通电话时，没有触及美国在菲军事基地一事。
    科·阿基诺作此声明的原因在于反对派批评她在反政变中，为寻求美军支持而在美军基地问题上作了许诺。
    在这次未遂政变中，菲政府军在美国飞机的空中掩护下击毁了停在桑格雷空军基地的
  8架叛军飞机，从而扭转了局势。
    菲参议院主席萨隆加早些时候也说，美国的军事援助“削弱了”日后菲律宾在菲美军事基地谈判中的地位。（附图片）
    12月7日，占据菲律宾首都马尼拉市马卡蒂金融中心区的政变军残部向政府投降后，马尼拉市民夹道欢迎进入该地区的政府军。　新华社发
