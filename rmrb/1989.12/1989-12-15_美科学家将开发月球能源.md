Title: U.S. Scientists to Develop Lunar Energy
Author:
Date: 1989-12-15
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, Washington, 12th December.

U.S. scientists are working on the design of a system to collect energy from the moon.

According to a report in the U.S. magazine "Popular Mechanics," scientists at the University of Wisconsin are planning to send a transport ship the size of a space shuttle to the moon to bring back lunar soil rich in the helium isotope helium-3.

The scientists plan to use an excavator with a movable bucket to dig a 3-meter deep trench on the lunar surface, screen out the soil rich in helium-3, and then use a solar energy concentrator to heat it to 700 degrees Celsius to melt and concentrate it.

It is estimated that each kilogram of helium-3 isotope can release 100 million kilowatt-hours of electricity through nuclear fusion. The lunar transport ship can bring back 33 kilograms of helium-3 per year, which can generate as much electricity as the entire U.S. consumed in 1985.

### 美科学家将开发月球能源

1989-12-15
第4版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    美科学家将开发月球能源
    新华社华盛顿12月12日电　美国科学家正着手设计一套登月采集能源的系统。
    据美国《大众机械》杂志报道，威斯康星大学的科学家准备让一艘体如航天飞机大小的运输船抵达月球，并运回富含氦同位素氦—3的月球土壤。
    科学家们计划用一台带有活动铲斗的挖掘机在月球表面挖一条3米深的坑，筛选出富含氦—3的土壤，然后用太阳能聚集器将其加热到700摄氏度，使其熔化、浓缩。
    据估计，每公斤氦—3同位素聚变反应后能释放出10兆瓦年的电能。登月运输船一年可运回33公斤的氦—3，可产生相当于美国1985年全年的用电量。
