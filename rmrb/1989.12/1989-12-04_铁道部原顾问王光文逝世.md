Title: Former Advisor to the Ministry of Railways Wang Guangwen Passes Away
Author:
Date: 1989-12-04
Page: 2

Xinhua News Agency, Beijing

    The excellent Communist Party member, loyal communist warrior, and former advisor to the Ministry of Railways, Wang Guangwen, passed away due to illness on November 8, 1989, in Beijing, at the age of 89.

    The farewell ceremony for Wang Guangwen's remains was held on the morning of November 29 at the Babaoshan Revolutionary Cemetery. Attendees included Guan Junyi, Sun Yi, Qiang Xiaochu, and Lu Ping. Wreaths were sent by Chen Yun, Ding Guangen, Song Renqiong, Cheng Zihua, Lü Zhengcao, and Li Senmao.

    Wang Guangwen was born in August 1900 in Teng County, Shandong Province. He joined the revolutionary cause in December 1936 and became a member of the Communist Party of China in 1938. During the Land Revolution, he worked in the Northeast Army. After the outbreak of the War of Resistance Against Japanese Aggression, he participated in the struggle to establish the Suizhong Anti-Japanese Base Area, serving as the deputy chief of staff for the Eighth Route Army Suizhong Military Region, commander of the Independent First Brigade, and commander of the Special Brigade of the Jin-Cha-Ji Military Region. In 1940, he participated in the famous Hundred Regiments Offensive. In the battle, he commanded decisively, bravely, and tenaciously.

    In September 1948, Wang Guangwen was reassigned to work in the railway sector, making significant contributions to the establishment of the people's railway. After liberation, he served as the deputy director of the Qiqihar Railway Bureau, Harbin Railway Bureau, deputy director of the Northeast Railway Bureau, director and party secretary of the Shenyang Railway Bureau, director and party secretary of the Ministry of Railways' Northeast Office, and other positions, working tirelessly to complete railway transportation tasks. After 1975, Wang Guangwen served as an advisor to the Ministry of Railways, a standing committee member of the Liaoning Provincial Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference, a member of the Fifth National Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference, deputy director of the Ministry of Railways' Inspection Office, and other positions. He retired in December 1982.

    Over more than half a century, Wang Guangwen consistently upheld the Party's fine traditions and style, working diligently and selflessly, devoting his entire life to the railway cause.

### 铁道部原顾问王光文逝世

1989-12-04
第2版(国内新闻)
专栏：

    铁道部原顾问王光文逝世
    新华社北京电　中国共产党优秀党员、忠诚的共产主义战士、铁道部原顾问王光文同志因病于1989年11月8日在北京逝世，终年89岁。
    王光文同志遗体告别仪式11月29日上午在八宝山革命公墓举行。段君毅、孙毅、强晓初、陆平等参加了遗体告别仪式。陈云、丁关根、宋任穷、程子华、吕正操、李森茂等送了花圈。
    王光文同志1900年8月生于山东省滕县。1936年12月参加革命工作。1938年加入中国共产党。在土地革命时期，他在东北军工作。抗日战争爆发后，他参加了开辟冀中抗日根据地的斗争，历任八路军冀中军区司令部副官长、独立一旅旅长、晋察冀军区特务团团长。1940年参加了著名的百团大战。在作战中，他指挥果断，勇敢顽强。
    1948年9月，王光文奉调到铁路部门工作，为人民铁路的创建做出了很大贡献。解放后，他历任齐齐哈尔铁路局、哈尔滨铁路局副局长，东北铁路总局副局长，沈阳铁路局局长、党委书记，铁道部驻东北办事处主任、党组书记等职，为完成铁路运输任务忘我地工作。1975年以后，王光文历任铁道部顾问、辽宁省政协常委、第五届全国政协委员、铁道部视察室副主任等职。1982年12月离职休养。
    半个多世纪以来，王光文同志始终保持党的优良传统和作风，兢兢业业，任劳任怨，为铁路事业贡献了毕生的精力。
