Title: U.S. Solar Probe Satellite to Fall to Earth
Author:
Date: 1989-12-02
Edition: 4

The U.S. Air Force Space Command spokesman said that the out-of-control U.S. solar probe satellite will fall to Earth around 10:45 p.m. local time on December 1.

The spokesman said that most of the satellite will burn up in the atmosphere, but a piece weighing about 45 kilograms and more than a dozen fragments weighing one to two kilograms each will pass through the atmosphere and fall to Earth. Due to a six-hour calculation error, the spokesman did not disclose the specific area where the satellite debris will scatter.

The solar probe satellite, valued at $80 million and weighing two tons, was launched into space in 1980. (Xinhua News Agency)

### 美太阳探测卫星将坠落地球

1989-12-02
第4版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    美太阳探测卫星将坠落地球
    美国空军航天指挥中心发言人说，已经失控的美国太阳探测卫星将于十二月一日当地时间下午十点四十五分左右坠落地球。
    这位发言人说，卫星的大部分将在大气中烧毁，但有一块重约四十五公斤的残骸和十多块一至二公斤重的碎片将穿过大气层坠落地球。由于有六小时的计算误差，发言人没有透露卫星残骸的具体散落范围。
    这颗价值八千万美元、重二吨的太阳探测卫星是一九八零年发射上天的。　（据新华社）
