Title: Special Summit of Central American Leaders Concludes
Author: Guo Weicheng
Date: 1989-12-14
Edition: 4

The special summit of Central American leaders concluded at 3:30 AM today in San José, the capital of Costa Rica, near San Isidro de Coronado. The presidents of the five participating countries signed the final documents of the summit, including a joint declaration with 13 points.

Costa Rican President Arias read the declaration. The declaration stated that the special summit of the five presidents discussed the "serious tensions in Central America that affect the peace process" and "condemned the military and terrorist activities carried out by unofficial armed groups in the region." It expressed the support of four presidents for the position of the Salvadoran government to resolve domestic conflicts through peaceful and democratic means; called on the Farabundo Martí National Liberation Front to immediately cease all hostile actions and return to the dialogue track; and demanded that the International Support and Verification Commission take immediate action to disband the Farabundo Martí National Liberation Front.

The declaration expressed support for the position of Nicaraguan President Ortega to disband the Nicaraguan anti-government forces; demanded that the anti-government forces cease all military actions that disrupt the election process and harm civilians; and demanded that the United Nations Observer Group in Central America take measures to stop the supply of weapons to the Salvadoran guerrillas and Nicaraguan anti-government forces from outside.

The Nicaraguan government reiterated that all anti-government forces who accept demobilization and return home by February 5, 1990, can participate in the February 25 elections.

The declaration stated that Guatemala, Honduras, and Costa Rica called on El Salvador and Nicaragua to overcome their differences and restore diplomatic and consular relations. The presidents of Nicaragua and Honduras agreed to suspend Nicaragua's lawsuit against Honduras in the International Court of Justice in The Hague for six months and establish a bilateral representative committee to seek a negotiated solution.

The joint declaration of the five countries also stipulated that the next summit will be held in the first quarter of 1990 in Managua, the capital of Nicaragua.

### 中美洲首脑特别会议结束  5国总统签署联合声明
郭伟成
1989-12-14
第4版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    中美洲首脑特别会议结束
    5国总统签署联合声明
    本报墨西哥城12月12日电  记者郭伟成报道：中美洲5国首脑特别会议于今天凌晨3时30分在哥斯达黎加首都圣何塞附近的圣伊德罗德·科罗纳多结束。与会5国总统签署了会议的最后文件——包括13点内容的联合声明。
    哥斯达黎加总统阿里亚斯宣读了这份声明。声明说，5国总统举行的特别会议，讨论了“严重影响和平进程的中美洲紧张局势”，“谴责本地区非正式武装所进行的军事与恐怖活动”；表示4国总统“支持萨尔瓦多政府通过和平与民主途径解决国内冲突的立场；呼吁法拉本多·马蒂民族解放阵线立即停止一切敌对行动，重新回到对话的轨道上来”；“要求国际支持与核查委员会立即采取行动解散法拉本多·马蒂民族解放阵线。”
    声明表示支持尼加拉瓜总统奥尔特加要求遣散尼加拉瓜反政府武装的立场；要求反政府武装停止一切破坏大选进程及损害平民的军事行动”；“要求联合国驻中美洲观察团采取措施制止外界向萨尔瓦多游击队及尼加拉瓜反政府武装提供武器。”
    尼加拉瓜政府重申，凡在1990年2月5日以前接受遣散回国的反政府武装人员可以参加2月25日大选投票。
    声明说，危地马拉、洪都拉斯、哥斯达黎加3国呼吁萨尔瓦多、尼加拉瓜克服分歧、恢复外交及领事关系。尼加拉瓜和洪都拉斯两国总统同意把尼在海牙国际法庭对洪的指控搁置6个月，成立一个双边代表委员会，谋求协商解决。
    5国联合声明还规定1990年第一季度在马那瓜（尼加拉瓜首都）举行下次首脑会议。
