Title: London Tin Prices Plunge
Author:
Date: 1989-12-04
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, London,

The price of tin on the London Metal Exchange dropped to its lowest point since June 1988 on November 13.

The closing price for the 3-month futures contract that day fell from the opening price of $6,910 per ton to $6,895 per ton. The opening price on November 10 was $7,225 per ton.

The main reason for the drop in tin prices is the increase in inventory at the London Metal Exchange by 70 tons, reaching 8,505 tons. At the same time, production has increased while consumption has been sluggish, leading to a market surplus.

### 伦敦锡价大幅下跌

1989-12-04
第4版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    伦敦锡价大幅下跌
    新华社伦敦电  伦敦五金交易所的锡价11月13日跌到了1988年6月以来的最低点。
    当天3个月期货的下午收盘价由开盘价的每吨6910美元降至6895美元。而11月10日的下午开盘价为每吨7225美元。
    锡价下跌的主要原因是伦敦五金交易所的库存量增加了70吨，达8505吨；与此同时，生产上升而消费却不景气，造成市场过剩。
