Title: Representatives of the Korean National Peace Committee and the Japan-Korea Association Issue Joint Communiqué on Issues Such as Japan-Korea Cultural Exchange
Author:
Date: 1956-03-01
Page: 4
Xinhua News Agency, Pyongyang, 29th. Representatives of the Korean National Peace Committee, Ahn Byung-sam, and representatives of the Japan-Korea Association, Koshizu Kosuke, held talks in Pyongyang on February 27th regarding cultural exchange between the two countries and the urgent issue of Korean residents in Japan, and issued a joint communiqué.

The communiqué stated that both sides will actively strive to realize the exchange of representatives from academic, artistic, cultural, and sports groups between the two countries. In 1956, the Japanese side will invite a Korean dance troupe and sports team to visit Japan, and the Korean side will invite representatives from Japanese academic groups or scholars, as well as artistic groups, to visit Korea; both sides will also exchange film copies, picture materials, and books at any time.

The communiqué stated that both sides believe that the lives of Korean citizens residing in Japan should be stabilized and their legitimate rights must be guaranteed. Both sides expressed regret and sympathy for the current situation of Korean residents detained in reception centers throughout Japan. The Japan-Korea Association will actively assist them in gaining freedom as soon as possible and living a stable life, and will actively assist Korean residents who wish to return to their country in realizing their wishes as soon as possible. The Japan-Korea Association will actively assist representatives of the Korean Red Cross to enter Japan to visit and seek a satisfactory resolution to the issue of Korean residents in Japan.

Xinhua News Agency, Pyongyang, 28th. According to a report by the Korean Central News Agency on the 28th: Talks on promoting trade and exchanging general conditions of goods between Korea and Japan were held in Pyongyang from February 15th to February 26th. The Korean representative was Cha In-deok, a member of the Standing Committee of the Korean International Trade Promotion Committee, and the Japanese representative was Kosuke Miyakoshi, Vice Chairman of the Trade Committee of the Japan-Korea Association and a member of the Japan International Trade Promotion Association.

The talks were conducted in an atmosphere of equality and mutual understanding, and agreements were reached on a series of issues. Cha In-deok and Kosuke Miyakoshi signed the record of the talks on February 26th, and confirmed the consensus of opinion.

The record of the talks stated that the two sides decided to make active efforts to promote the realization of the following measures: mutual dispatch of trade delegations, travel to and from the two countries by personnel engaged in trade, establishment of trade representative offices, holding of commodity exhibitions, and conclusion of payment agreements based on the settlement system between banks in the two countries, in order to realize the trade between the two countries and the future development of economic exchanges between the two countries, as desired by the people of Korea and Japan; both sides will also cooperate to implement the trade agreements concluded by companies in both countries.

The agreement on the general conditions for the exchange of goods makes specific provisions for issues such as barter methods, commodity prices, settlement methods, ports, and transportation.

Xinhua News Agency: A Japanese Red Cross delegation led by Kasai Kashisuke, Vice President of the Japanese Red Cross, arrived in Beijing by plane on the 29th on its way home from Pyongyang.

At the invitation of the Chinese Red Cross, the delegation will pay a short visit to Beijing. The Chinese Red Cross will host a banquet tonight to welcome all members of the delegation. The members of the delegation are: Masutaro Inoue, Director of Foreign Affairs of the Japanese Red Cross Society, Kosuke Miyakoshi, Vice Chairman of the Japan-Korea Association, and interpreter Yasusada Takuwa.

### 朝鲜拥护和平全国民族委员会代表和日朝协会代表  就朝日文化交流等问题发表联合公报

1956-03-01
第4版()
专栏：

    朝鲜拥护和平全国民族委员会代表和日朝协会代表
    就朝日文化交流等问题发表联合公报
    新华社平壤29日电　朝鲜拥护和平全国民族委员会代表安炳三和日朝协会代表宫腰喜助2月27日在平壤就两国间的文化交流和目前亟待解决的在日本的朝鲜侨民问题进行了会谈，并且发表了联合公报。
    公报说，双方将为了使两国互派学术、艺术、文化和体育团体的代表等事得以实现而积极进行努力。1956年，日本方面将邀请朝鲜的舞蹈团和体育团访问日本，朝鲜方面将邀请日本的学术团体代表或学者以及艺术团体访问朝鲜；双方还将随时交换电影拷贝、图片资料和书刊等。
    公报说，双方认为居住在日本的朝鲜公民的生活应该得到安定，他们的合法权利必须得到保证。双方对被扣留在日本各地收容所内的朝鲜侨民的现状表示遗憾和同情。日朝协会将积极帮助他们早日获得自由，过安定的生活，并且积极帮助希望回国的朝鲜侨民尽速实现自己的愿望。日朝协会将积极帮助朝鲜红十字会代表进入日本境内访问日本，以谋求在日本的朝鲜侨民问题得到圆满的解决。
    新华社平壤28日电　据朝鲜中央通讯社28日讯：关于促进朝鲜和日本之间的贸易和交换商品的一般条件的会谈从2月15日到2月26日在平壤举行。朝鲜方面的会谈代表是朝鲜国际贸易促进委员会常务委员会委员车仁德，日本方面的会谈代表是日朝协会贸易委员会副委员长、日本国际贸易促进协会委员宫腰喜助。
    会谈是在平等和互相谅解的气氛中进行的，并且就一系列的问题达成了协议。车仁德同宫腰喜助在2月26日在会谈记录上签了字，并且证实了意见的一致。
    会谈记录表示，会谈双方决定作出积极的努力，促使下列措施实现：双方互派贸易代表团、从事于贸易的人士在两国来往旅行、设立贸易代表处、举行商品展览会、在两国银行之间的结算制度的基础上缔结支付协定，以便早日实现朝日两国人民所期望的两国贸易和在将来发展两国之间的经济交流；双方并且进行合作来执行两国公司所缔结的贸易协定。
    关于交换商品的一般条件的协议，对于换货办法、商品价格、结算办法、港口和运输等问题作出了具体规定。
    新华社讯　以日本红十字会副会长葛西嘉资为首的日本红十字会代表团一行4人，29日在由平壤归国途中，乘飞机到达北京。
    这个代表团应中国红十字会邀请，将在京作短期访问。中国红十字会在今晚设宴欢迎代表团的全体人员。代表团的团员有：日本红十字会外事部长井上益太郎、日朝协会副理事长宫腰喜助和翻译多久安贞。
