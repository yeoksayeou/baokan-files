Title: At the Meeting of the Electric Power Subcommittee of the Economic Commission for Asia and the Far East, the Soviet Representative Expressed Willingness to Provide Assistance to Other Countries in the Peaceful Use of Atomic Energy
Author:
Date: 1956-03-13
Page: 4

　　Xinhua News Agency dispatch from Tass, Delhi, 10th: The "Indian Express" reported that the Electric Power Subcommittee of the United Nations Economic Commission for Asia and the Far East discussed the issue of using atomic energy for power generation at a meeting held in Bangalore on March 8th.
　　Soviet representative Chuplakov pointed out that the Soviet Union has always advocated close cooperation among countries in the peaceful use of atomic energy. He said that the Soviet Union is prepared to share its achievements in this regard with any country on a reciprocal basis. He emphasized that the Soviet Union's proposal to provide assistance to other countries in the peaceful use of atomic energy is not a commercial proposal and does not carry any political, military, or economic conditions.
　　The subcommittee passed a motion to send a group of Asian power experts to visit certain countries in Europe and Africa (including the Soviet Union) as well as the United States.
　　After the meeting, the Soviet delegation screened the documentary "The World's First," about the Soviet atomic power station.

### 在亚洲及远东经济委员会电力小组委员会会议上  苏联代表表示愿在和平利用原子能方面给他国援助

1956-03-13
第4版()
专栏：

    在亚洲及远东经济委员会电力小组委员会会议上
    苏联代表表示愿在和平利用原子能方面给他国援助
    新华社据塔斯社德里10日讯　“印度快报”报道，联合国亚洲及远东经济委员会电力小组委员会3月8日在班加罗尔举行的会议上讨论了利用原子能发电的问题。
    苏联代表楚普拉科夫指出，苏联一向主张各国在和平利用原子能方面进行密切合作。他说，苏联准备在互惠的基础上同任何国家分享它在这方面的成就。他强调指出，苏联关于在和平利用原子能方面给予其他国家援助的建议并不是一种商业上的建议，也不附带任何政治、军事或经济条件。
    小组委员会通过了派遣一批亚洲电力专家到欧洲和非洲的某些国家（包括苏联）以及美国去访问的动议。
    会后，苏联代表团放映了关于苏联原子电力站的纪录片“世界上第一个”。
