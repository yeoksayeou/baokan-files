Title: Tokyo Holds Anti-Atomic and Hydrogen Bomb Test Rally
Author:
Date: 1956-03-02
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, Tokyo, March 1st: To commemorate the second anniversary of Japan's suffering from the American hydrogen bomb test disaster in Bikini Atoll, an anti-atomic and hydrogen bomb test rally was held in Tokyo on March 1st. The rally was organized by the Japan Council Against Atomic and Hydrogen Bombs, with the participation of representatives from mass organizations and 1,300 citizens.

    Yasui Kaoru, Secretary-General of the Japan Council Against Atomic and Hydrogen Bombs, gave a general report at the meeting. Prominent social figures such as Kitamura Tokutaro and Katayama Tetsu also delivered speeches at the meeting. Suzuki, a victim of the Bikini incident, spoke at the meeting, demanding a ban on atomic weapons. He said, "Absolutely do not repeat tragedies like mine."

    Telegrams of congratulations from World Peace Council President Joliot-Curie and peace organizations from the Soviet Union, China, the United States, the United Kingdom, India, and Canada were read out at the meeting. In his telegram, Joliot-Curie pointed out the significant role of the Japanese people's actions against atomic weapons. He said that if all countries in the world took such actions, disarmament and the prohibition of atomic weapons could be achieved.

    The conference concluded with a declaration stating: "We demand that all countries ban atomic and hydrogen bomb tests, and we hope that the Japanese government will take the necessary measures to this end."

    Telegram of congratulations from the Chinese People's Committee for World Peace to the Japan Council Against Atomic and Hydrogen Bombs:
    Having learned that your council is holding the Japanese Anti-Atomic and Hydrogen Bomb Test Rally, the Chinese People's Committee for World Peace sincerely congratulates the success of the rally and would like to take this opportunity to express our deep condolences and sympathy to those who have unfortunately sacrificed and innocently suffered due to atomic bomb explosions and hydrogen bomb tests.

    The convening of this conference will further advance the just struggle of the Japanese people to demand the prohibition of atomic weapons and hydrogen weapons, and to demand the cessation of atomic and hydrogen bomb tests.

    The Chinese people have always firmly advocated the prohibition of atomic weapons and hydrogen weapons, and advocated the use of atomic energy for peaceful purposes. The Chinese people are willing to work together with the Japanese people and all peace-loving people in the world to achieve the prohibition of atomic weapons and hydrogen weapons, universal disarmament, and ensure world peace.
    Chinese People's Committee for World Peace
              March 1, 1956

    Telegram of congratulations from the Chinese Asian Solidarity Committee to the Japan Council Against Atomic and Hydrogen Bombs:

    On the occasion of the convening of the Japanese Anti-Atomic and Hydrogen Bomb Test Rally, we would like to express our highest respect to the Japanese people who are bravely fighting for the prohibition of atomic weapons and hydrogen weapons.

    We are deeply convinced that this conference will make a positive contribution to the cause of defending Asian and world peace. The Japanese people's firm struggle against the use of atomic weapons and hydrogen weapons and demanding the cessation of atomic and hydrogen bomb tests has won the widespread sympathy and support of the people of Asia and the world. The Chinese people are willing to unite closely with the Japanese people, together with the people of Asia and the world, and jointly wage an unremitting struggle against atomic weapons, hydrogen weapons and other weapons of mass destruction.

    We wish the conference a successful victory.
        China Asian Solidarity Committee
              March 1, 1956

    Telegram of congratulations from the Secretariat of the Asian and Pacific Regions Peace Liaison Committee to the Japan Council Against Atomic and Hydrogen Bombs:

    The Secretariat of the Asian and Pacific Regions Peace Liaison Committee extends its heartfelt congratulations to the Japanese Anti-Atomic and Hydrogen Bomb Test Rally.

    The Japanese people's desire to prohibit atomic weapons and hydrogen weapons and to demand the cessation of atomic and hydrogen bomb tests is also the unanimous desire of the people of the Asian and Pacific regions and even the world. The people of all countries deeply sympathize with and support the Japanese people's struggle. Let the people of our Asian and Pacific regions unite with the people of the world to form a huge force to completely eliminate the threat of atomic weapons and hydrogen weapons and strive for lasting peace.

    Secretariat of the Asian and Pacific Regions Peace Liaison Committee
              March 1, 1956

### 东京举行禁止试验原子弹和氢弹大会

1956-03-02
第4版()
专栏：

    东京举行禁止试验原子弹和氢弹大会
    新华社1日讯　东京消息：为了纪念日本遭受美国在比基尼岛试验氢弹的灾害两周年，3月1日在东京举行了禁止试验原子弹和氢弹大会。大会是由日本禁止原子弹和氢弹协议会主办的，参加的有群众团体代表和市民1，300人。
    禁止原子弹和氢弹协议会事务总长安井郁在会上作了一般报告。著名社会人士北村德太郎、片山哲等人也在会上致词。比基尼事件的受害者铃木在会上发言，要求禁止原子武器。他说，“绝对不要重演像我这样的悲剧。”
    会上宣读了世界和平理事会主席约里奥—居里和苏联、中国、美国、英国、印度、加拿大等国和平组织的贺电。约里奥—居里在他的贺电中指出了日本人民反对原子武器的行动的巨大作用。他说，如果世界各国都有这种行动，裁军和禁止原子武器就能够实现。
    大会最后通过宣言说：“我们要求各国禁止原子弹和氢弹试验，并且希望日本政府为此而采取必要的措施。”
    中国人民保卫世界和平委员会的贺电日本禁止原子弹、氢弹协议会：
    获悉贵会召开日本反对原子弹、氢弹试验大会，中国人民保卫世界和平委员会衷心地祝贺大会的成功，并愿借此机会，对由于遭受原子弹轰炸和氢弹试验而不幸牺牲和无辜受难者，表示深切的悼念和慰问。
    这次大会的召开将更进一步推进日本人民要求禁止原子武器、氢武器，要求停止原子弹、氢弹试验的正义斗争。
    中国人民一向坚决主张禁止原子武器、氢武器，和主张把原子能利用于和平目的。中国人民愿与日本人民以及全世界爱好和平的人民一道，为实现禁止原子武器、氢武器和普遍裁军，确保世界和平而共同奋斗。
    中国人民保卫世界和平委员会
              1956年3月1日
    中国亚洲团结委员会的贺电日本禁止原子弹、氢弹协议会：
    值此日本反对原子弹、氢弹试验大会召开之际，谨向为禁止原子武器、氢武器而英勇奋斗的日本人民表示崇高的敬意。
    我们深信，这次大会将对保卫亚洲和世界和平的事业作出积极的贡献。日本人民反对使用原子武器、氢武器，要求停止原子弹、氢弹试验的坚强斗争，获得亚洲和世界人民的广泛同情和支持，中国人民愿与日本人民一道，同亚洲人民和全世界人民紧密地团结，共同为反对原子武器、氢武器及其他大规模毁灭性武器作坚持不懈的斗争。
    谨祝大会的胜利成功。
        中国亚洲团结委员会
              1956年3月1日
    亚洲及太平洋区域和平联络委员会秘书处的贺电日本禁止原子弹、氢弹协议会：
    亚洲及太平洋区域和平联络委员会秘书处谨向日本反对原子弹、氢弹试验大会表示衷心的祝贺。
    日本人民要求禁止原子武器、氢武器，要求停止原子弹、氢弹试验的愿望，也是亚洲及太平洋地区以至全世界人民的一致愿望，各国人民深切同情和支持日本人民的这一斗争。让我们亚洲及太平洋地区人民同全世界人民团结在一起，形成巨大的力量，来彻底消除原子武器、氢武器的威胁，争取持久和平。
    亚洲及太平洋区域和平联络委员
    会秘书处
              1956年3月1日
