Title: Soviet Union and India Oppose US Testing Hydrogen Bombs in Trust Territories; Japanese Public Opposes US Establishing Restricted Zones in International Waters for Atomic Weapon Testing

Author:

Date: 1956-03-05
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency Dispatch: New York News: The Soviet Union and India opposed the United States conducting atomic and hydrogen bomb tests in trust territories at the United Nations Trusteeship Council meeting. The United States government had announced that it would conduct a series of atomic and hydrogen bomb tests in the Pacific trust territories starting April 20th.

    Soviet representative Grubiakov, on February 29th at the meeting, requested the Trusteeship Council to pay attention to the United States' intentions and suggested that the council decide to disallow atomic and hydrogen bomb tests in trust territories. He pointed out that using trust territories as testing grounds for atomic and hydrogen bombs not only threatens the residents at present but may also have serious consequences in the future.

    Indian representative Menon said at the meeting on March 2nd that if the United States conducts the atomic and hydrogen bomb tests it announced before the next Trusteeship Council meeting in the trust territories, the Indian government will request the International Court of Justice to rule on whether the United States has the right to conduct such tests in the trust territories.

    The US representative spoke, insisting that the United States will continue to conduct atomic and hydrogen bomb tests there.

Xinhua News Agency Dispatch: The United States' act of arbitrarily designating a large area of the Pacific Ocean as a testing zone for atomic weapons has aroused strong opposition in Japan.

    In recent days, people across Japan have held commemorative gatherings, demanding a ban on atomic weapon testing. Yasui Kaoru, Secretary-General of the Japan Council Against Atomic and Hydrogen Bombs, said that the Japanese people will hold a nationwide petition on March 20th demanding a ban on atomic weapons. He pointed out that the United States conducting atomic weapons tests in trust territories and international waters is a violation of international law. Japan calls on groups from all countries to protest to the United States.

### 苏印反对美国在托管领土试验氢弹  日本公众反对美国为试验原子武器在公海建立禁区

1956-03-05
第4版()
专栏：

    苏印反对美国在托管领土试验氢弹
    日本公众反对美国为试验原子武器在公海建立禁区
    据新华社讯  纽约消息：苏联和印度在联合国托管理事会会议上反对美国在托管领土进行原子弹和氢弹试验。美国政府曾经宣布，它将从4月20日起在太平洋的托管领土上进行一系列原子弹和氢弹试验。
    苏联代表格鲁比雅科夫2月29日在会议上要求托管理事会注意美国这种打算，并且建议理事会决定不允许在托管领土上进行原子弹和氢弹试验。他指出，以托管领土来作为原子弹和氢弹的试验场不仅在目前威胁着它们的居民，而且可能在将来产生严重的后果。
    印度代表梅农3月2日在会议上说，如果美国在下届托管理事会会议以前在托管领土进行它宣布要举行的原子弹和氢弹试验，印度政府将要求国际法院裁决美国是否有权在托管领土上进行这种试验。
    美国代表发言，坚持美国要继续在那里进行原子弹和氢弹试验。
    据新华社讯  美国擅自把太平洋的大片海面划为进行原子武器试验区域的行为，在日本激起强烈的反对。
    这几天，日本各地人民纷纷举行纪念集会，要求禁止原子武器试验。日本禁止原子弹和氢弹协议会事务总长安井郁发表谈话说，日本人民将在3月20日举行全国规模的要求禁止原子武器的请愿。他指出，美国在托管地区和公海上进行原子武器试验是违反国际法的。日本号召各国的团体向美国提出抗议。
