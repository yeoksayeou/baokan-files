Title: Indian and Pakistani Local Police Authorities Reach Agreement on Resolving Border Disputes
Author: 
Date: 1956-03-29
Page: 4
Xinhua News Agency, New Delhi, March 27th: According to a report in today's "Statesman," the Inspector General of Police of India's East Punjab Province, D.K. Lal, and the Inspector General of Police of West Pakistan, Mian Anwar Ali, held a ninety-minute meeting on March 26th in Kalra, Pakistan, thirty-five miles from Amritsar. After the meeting, they reaffirmed that border disputes should be resolved through consultation.

They unanimously agreed that, according to their past decisions, neither side should open fire in the event of any ground dispute, but should resolve the dispute through mutual consultation at all levels.

### 印巴两国地方警察当局就解决边境争端问题达成协议

1956-03-29
第4版()
专栏：

    印巴两国地方警察当局就解决边境争端问题达成协议
    新华社新德里27日电　据“政治家报”今天报道。印度东旁遮普省警察督察长德·克·拉尔和西巴基斯坦警察督察长米安·安瓦尔·阿里三月二十六日在巴基斯坦境内距离阿姆利则三十五英里的卡尔拉地方举行了九十分钟的会议。会后他们重申，边境争端应该通过协商来解决。
    他们一致认为，按照他们过去的决定，任何一方不得在发生任何地面争端的时候开火，而应该通过各级相互协商来解决争端。
