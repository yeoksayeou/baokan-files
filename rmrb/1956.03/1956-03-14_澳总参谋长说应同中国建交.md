Title: Australian Chief of General Staff Says Diplomatic Relations Should Be Established with China
Author:
Date: 1956-03-14
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, Melbourne, March 13th: According to a report in the Australian weekly "Guardian" on March 8th, the Australian Chief of the General Staff, Henry Wells, stated in a public speech that Australia should establish diplomatic relations with the People's Republic of China.

He said that Australia "will have to recognize China." He said that the Chinese government is a government supported and respected by the people.

He said that China "has almost unlimited manpower, vast land, and at the same time is respected by its neighbors."

### 澳总参谋长说应同中国建交

1956-03-14
第4版()
专栏：

    澳总参谋长说应同中国建交
    新华社13日讯　墨尔本消息：据澳大利亚周报“卫报”3月8日的一期报道，澳大利亚总参谋长亨利·威尔斯在一次公开演说中表示，澳大利亚应同中华人民共和国建立外交关系。
    他说，澳大利亚“将必须承认中国”。他说，中国政府是一个受到人民拥护和尊重的政府。
    他说，中国“有差不多是无限的人力，广大的土地，同时又得到它的邻国的尊重”。
