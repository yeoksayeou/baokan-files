Title: Japan Council Against Atomic and Hydrogen Bombs Holds Conference
Author:
Date: 1956-03-20
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, Tokyo, March 19th: The Japan Council Against Atomic and Hydrogen Bombs held its first national conference in Tokyo on March 18th and 19th.

    The conference decided to hold the Second World Conference Against Atomic and Hydrogen Bombs in Nagasaki, the city in Japan that suffered the second American atomic bomb attack, in August of this year. The conference unanimously agreed that this year's world conference should be jointly prepared by the Japanese government and people.

    The conference also decided that preventing the United States from conducting hydrogen bomb tests in the Pacific region is the main goal of the current movement.

    The conference finally adopted a resolution calling on people at home and abroad to unite to demand a ban on atomic and hydrogen bomb tests and to promote the movement to aid atomic and hydrogen bomb victims.

Xinhua News Agency, Tokyo, March 18th: At the national conference held by the Japan Council Against Atomic and Hydrogen Bombs on March 18th, based on a motion by Secretary-General Ikuo Yasui, it was decided to submit a request to U.S. Secretary of State Dulles, who is visiting Japan, demanding that the United States cease the hydrogen bomb tests scheduled for April in the Pacific region. The request stated that this is the most urgent demand of the Japanese people. The request also stated that the number of people in Japan who have signed petitions calling for a ban on nuclear weapons has exceeded 38.5 million.

### 日本禁止原子弹氢弹协议会举行大会

1956-03-20
第4版()
专栏：

    日本禁止原子弹氢弹协议会举行大会
    新华社19日讯　东京消息：日本禁止原子弹和氢弹协议会在三月十八、十九两日在东京举行了第一次全国大会。
    大会决定今年八月在日本遭受美国第二颗原子弹轰炸的长崎市举行第二次禁止原子弹和氢弹世界大会。大会一致认为：今年的世界大会应当由日本政府和人民共同筹备。
    大会还决定阻止美国在太平洋地区进行氢弹试验是当前运动的主要目标。
    大会最后通过决议，呼吁国内外人民一致起来要求禁止原子弹、氢弹试验和推进救济原子弹、氢弹受害者运动。
    新华社18日讯　东京消息：日本禁止原子弹和氢弹协议会在三月十八日举行的全国大会上，根据事务局长安井郁的动议，决定向前来日本访问的美国国务卿杜勒斯提出一项要求书，要求美国停止将在四月在太平洋地区举行的氢弹试验。要求书说，这是日本全国人民最迫切的要求。要求书并且说，日本签名要求禁止核子武器的人数已经超过了3，850万人。
