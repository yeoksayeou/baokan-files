Title: West German Workers' Conference Decides on Joint Action Against Remilitarization
Author:
Date: 1956-03-29
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, Berlin, March 28th: According to a report by the German News Agency, a workers' conference held in Frankfurt am Main, West Germany, on March 25th discussed taking joint steps to deal with the danger of West German militarism. Participants in the conference included Social Democrats, Communists, Christian workers from 57 Frankfurt factories, and workers from state-owned factories in the German Democratic Republic.

The conference adopted a letter to all working people of Frankfurt am Main, calling on them to establish contact between different factories and different trade unions, as well as between East and West Germany. The letter stated: "Only if the German working class takes unified action can it stop West German remilitarization and pave the way for formulating a policy that promotes mutual understanding among the German people."

### 西德工人会议决定联合行动反对重新军国主义化

1956-03-29
第4版()
专栏：

    西德工人会议决定联合行动反对重新军国主义化
    新华社柏林28日电　据德意志通讯社报道：三月二十五日在西德美因河畔的法兰克福举行的一次工人会议上讨论了采取联合步骤对付西德军国主义的危险的问题。参加这次会议的有社会民主党人、共产党人以及五十七个法兰克福工厂的基督教工人们和来自德意志民主共和国国营工厂的工人们。
    会上通过了告美因河畔的法兰克福全体劳动人民书，号召他们在不同工厂和不同工会之间以及在东德和西德之间建立接触。这个告人民书说：“只要德国工人阶级采取统一行动就能制止西德重新军国主义化并且为制定一个使德国人民互相谅解的政策铺平道路。”
