Title: Negotiations Ended with Friendship and Full Understanding: Sino-Polish Treaty Signed on the Legal Status of Soviet Troops Stationed in Poland
Author:
Date: 1956-12-19
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, Warsaw, December 18th. A treaty between the Soviet and Polish governments regarding the legal status of Soviet troops temporarily stationed in Poland was signed in Warsaw on December 17th.

According to the joint Soviet-Polish government communiqué released here, the Soviet and Polish government delegations conducted friendly negotiations in Warsaw, with full mutual understanding, regarding the legal status of Soviet troops temporarily stationed in Poland, based on the joint Soviet-Polish declaration signed in Moscow on November 18th of this year.

The Soviet side participating in the negotiations included: Soviet Foreign Minister Shepilov (head of the delegation), Soviet Minister of Defense, Marshal of the Soviet Union Zhukov, General Antonov, and others.

The Polish side participating in the negotiations included: Polish Foreign Minister Rapacki (head of the delegation), Minister of Defense Spychalski, Deputy Minister of Defense Zarzycki, and others.

As a result of the negotiations, a treaty between the Soviet and Polish governments regarding the legal status of Soviet troops temporarily stationed in Poland was signed on December 17th.

Soviet Foreign Minister Shepilov and Defense Minister Zhukov signed the treaty on behalf of the Soviet government. Polish Foreign Minister Rapacki and Defense Minister Spychalski signed the treaty on behalf of the Polish government.

Leaders of the Polish United Workers' Party and the government, Gomulka, Cyrankiewicz, Ochab, and others, also attended the signing ceremony.

The communiqué stated that both sides simultaneously determined that the special agreements mentioned in certain clauses of the treaty will be signed within three months from the date the treaty comes into effect.

Xinhua News Agency, Warsaw, December 18th. Soviet Foreign Minister Shepilov and Marshal of the Soviet Union Zhukov, among others, have left Warsaw by plane on the 18th to return to their country after negotiating and signing the treaty between the Soviet and Polish governments regarding the legal status of Soviet troops temporarily stationed in Poland with the Polish government delegation in Warsaw.

### 友好和充分谅解的谈判结束  苏波签订驻波苏军法律地位条约

1956-12-19
第5版()
专栏：

    友好和充分谅解的谈判结束
    苏波签订驻波苏军法律地位条约
    新华社华沙18日电　苏联政府和波兰政府关于暂时驻在波兰的苏联军队的法律地位的条约12月17日在华沙签字。
    据这里发表的苏波两国政府的公报说，苏波两国政府代表团根据今年11月18日在莫斯科签订的苏波联合声明，在华沙就暂时驻在波兰境内的苏军的法律地位进行了友好和充分谅解的谈判。
    苏联方面参加谈判的有：苏联外交部长谢皮洛夫（代表团团长），苏联国防部长、苏联元帅朱可夫，安东诺夫大将等人。
    波兰方面参加谈判的有：波兰外交部长拉帕茨基（代表团团长），国防部长斯彼哈尔斯基、国防部副部长萨热茨基等人。
    谈判的结果，在12月17日签订了苏波两国政府关于暂时驻在波兰的苏联军队的法律地位的条约。
    代表苏联政府在条约上签字的是苏联外交部长谢皮洛夫和国防部长朱可夫。代表波兰政府在条约上签字的是波兰外交部长拉帕茨基和国防部长斯彼哈尔斯基。
    波兰统一工人党和政府的领导人哥穆尔卡、西伦凯维兹、奥哈布等人也出席了签字仪式。
    公报说，双方同时确定了，在条约某些条款中所提到的特别协定，将在这个条约生效之日起三个月内签订。
    据新华社华沙18日电　苏联外交部长谢皮洛夫和苏联元帅朱可夫等，在华沙同波兰政府代表团谈判和签订了波苏两国政府关于暂时驻在波兰的苏联军队的法律地位的条约以后，已经在18日乘飞机离华沙回国。
