Title: Joint Statement by the Prime Ministers of China and Burma: Sino-Burmese Relations Will Continue to Be Based on the Five Principles of Peaceful Coexistence; The Boundary Issue Between the Two Countries Is Closer to Reaching a Mutually Satisfactory Solution
Author: 
Date: 1956-12-21
Page: 1

Xinhua News Agency, Rangoon, December 20th: Chinese Premier Zhou Enlai and Burmese Prime Minister U Ba Swe signed a joint statement this morning at 6:50 AM in the Presidential Palace.

Present at the signing ceremony from the Chinese side were: Vice Premier He Long, Chinese Ambassador to Burma Yao Zhongming, Assistant Minister of Foreign Affairs Qiao Guanhua, Deputy Director of the Premier's Office Zhang Yan, and Director of the Information Department of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs Gong Peng. Present from the Burmese side were: Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Foreign Affairs and Shan State Chief Sao Hkun Hkio, Deputy Prime Minister U Kyaw Nyein, and Burmese Cabinet Ministers.

Xinhua News Agency, Rangoon, December 20th: The following is the joint statement issued by Zhou Enlai, Premier of the State Council of the People's Republic of China, and U Ba Swe, Prime Minister of the Union of Burma, after their talks:

1.  At the invitation of the Government of the Union of Burma, Zhou Enlai, Premier of the State Council of the People's Republic of China, paid a friendly visit to the Union of Burma from December 10th to 20th, 1956. During his stay in the Union of Burma, Premier Zhou Enlai held several talks with the Prime Minister of the Union of Burma, the Honorable U Ba Swe, the President of the Anti-Fascist People's Freedom League, the Honorable U Nu, and other leaders. These talks, held in an extremely harmonious spirit and with full mutual understanding, provided both sides with the opportunity to broadly exchange views on issues of interest to both countries and on certain pressing issues of international importance.

2.  Both Prime Ministers firmly believe that peace in Asia and the world can only be maintained and consolidated on the basis of respecting the Five Principles of Peaceful Coexistence. During the talks, both Prime Ministers reaffirmed that the relations between the two countries are established on these principles of peaceful coexistence and will continue to be based on these principles.

Both Prime Ministers believe that the Five Principles should be applied to relations between all countries. The universal acceptance of these principles will guarantee the peaceful resolution of all international disputes.

3.  During their stay, Premier Zhou Enlai and his entourage also visited Mandalay, Maymyo, Bhamo, Myitkyina, Heho, Nyaungshwe, Taunggyi, and Lashio, where they were warmly welcomed by local leaders and people everywhere. In addition, both Prime Ministers attended a border people's conference held in Mangshi, the capital of the Dehong Dai and Jingpo Autonomous Prefecture in Yunnan. The main feature of this conference, attended by over ten thousand people, was the free exchange between the leaders of the two governments and representatives of the border people on both sides. At the mass rally that formed the climax of the conference, both Prime Ministers and leaders from the border areas on both sides spoke. The cordial and friendly atmosphere of the conference marked the growing strength of goodwill and understanding between the border people of both sides.

4.  In a spirit of sincere mutual understanding, the two Prime Ministers also discussed the settlement of the Sino-Burmese border issue. These discussions further clarified the views of China and Burma and brought the issue closer to reaching a mutually satisfactory solution.

                  Zhou Enlai (Signed)
                  Premier of the State Council of the People's Republic of China
                  U Ba Swe (Signed)
                  Prime Minister of the Union of Burma
                  December 20, 1956, Rangoon

### 中缅两国总理发表联合声明  中缅关系将继续以和平共处五项原则为基础  两国边界问题更接近于达到双方满意的解决

1956-12-21
第1版()
专栏：

    中缅两国总理发表联合声明
    中缅关系将继续以和平共处五项原则为基础
    两国边界问题更接近于达到双方满意的解决
    新华社仰光20日电　中国总理周恩来和缅甸总理吴巴瑞今晨六时五十分在总统府签署了一项联合声明。
    签字时中国方面出席的有：副总理贺龙，中国驻缅甸大使姚仲明，外交部部长助理乔冠华，总理办公室副主任张彦和外交部新闻司司长龚澎。缅甸方面出席的有：副总理兼外交部长和掸邦邦长藻昆卓，副总理吴觉迎以及缅甸内阁的部长们。
    新华社仰光20日电　中华人民共和国国务院总理周恩来和缅甸联邦总理吴巴瑞会谈后发表联合声明如下：
    一、中华人民共和国国务院总理周恩来应缅甸联邦政府的邀请，自1956年12月10日至20日到缅甸联邦作了友好访问。在逗留缅甸联邦期间，周恩来总理同缅甸联邦总理吴巴瑞阁下、反法西斯人民自由同盟主席吴努阁下以及其他领导人进行了几次会谈。这些在极其和谐和具有充分相互了解的精神下举行的会谈，使双方有机会就对两国利益有关的问题和某些具有国际重要性的迫切问题广泛地交换了意见。
    二、两国总理深信，亚洲和世界的和平只能在尊重和平共处五项原则的基础上得到维护和巩固，在会谈中两国总理重申：两国间的关系是在这些和平共处原则的基础上建立的，并将继续以这些原则为基础。
    两国总理认为五项原则应该应用到一切国家间的关系中去。这些原则的普遍接受，将能保证一切国际争端得到和平解决。
    三、在逗留期间，周恩来总理和他的同行者们还访问了曼德勒、眉谬、八莫、密支那、喜河、阳瑞、东枝和腊戍等地，他们到处受到当地领袖和人民的热烈的欢迎。此外，两国总理出席了在云南德宏傣族景颇族自治州首府芒市举行的边民大会。有一万多人参加的这次大会的主要特点是，两国政府的领导人同双方边境人民的代表进行了自由的交谈。在形成大会高潮的群众集会上，两国总理和双方边境地区的领导人都讲了话。大会的亲切友好气氛标志着双方边境人民之间的善意和谅解在成长壮大。
    四、两国总理在热诚的相互谅解的精神下也讨论了解决中缅边界的问题。这些讨论进一步澄清了中缅两国的观点，并使这一问题更接近于达到双方满意的解决。
                  中华人民共和国国务院总理　周恩来（签署）
                   缅甸联邦总理　吴巴瑞（签署）
                          1956年12月20日于仰光
