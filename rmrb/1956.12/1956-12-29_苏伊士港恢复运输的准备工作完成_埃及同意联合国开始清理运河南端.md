Title: Preparations for the Resumption of Transportation in Port Suez Completed; Egypt Agrees to UN Starting Clearance of the Southern End of the Suez Canal
Author:
Date: 1956-12-29
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, Cairo, December 28th: The Middle East News Agency reported today that preparations for restoring normal traffic and transportation in Port Suez have been completed. Cargo ships and other vessels can now enter and exit the port.

The report also stated that 11,000 bales of Egyptian cotton are waiting on the port docks to be shipped to the People's Republic of China.

Xinhua News Agency, December 28th: Cairo News: Wheeler, the United Nations representative assisting Egypt in clearing the Suez Canal, issued a communique on the 27th stating: "The Egyptian government has agreed that the United Nations salvage fleet can immediately begin clearing the canal at Suez, the southern end of the Suez Canal. The clearing work will begin today."

Wheeler also said: "So far, we cannot start salvage operations at the northern end of the (Suez) Canal, because that part has not yet been cleared of dangerous obstacles."

### 苏伊士港恢复运输的准备工作完成  埃及同意联合国开始清理运河南端

1956-12-29
第6版()
专栏：

    苏伊士港恢复运输的准备工作完成
    埃及同意联合国开始清理运河南端
    新华社开罗28日电　中东通讯社今天报道，使苏伊士港的交通运输恢复正常的准备工作已经完成。货船和其他船只目前已经可以在这个港口出入。
    消息还说，在港口码头上有一万一千包埃及棉花正等待着运往中华人民共和国。
    据新华社28日讯　开罗消息：联合国协助埃及清理苏伊士运河的代表惠勒27日发表一项公报说：“埃及政府已经同意，联合国打捞船队可以立即在苏伊士运河的南端苏伊士开始清理运河的工作。清理工作将在今天开始。”
    惠勒还说：“到目前为止，我们还不能在（苏伊士）运河的北端开始打捞工作，因为那一部分还没有把危险的障碍物清理掉。”
