Title: Egyptian Police Scheduled to Enter Port Said Today; Anglo-French Aggressor Troops' Murder and Looting Become Increasingly Rampant
Author:
Date: 1956-12-18
Page: 6

    Xinhua News Agency Report: Cairo News: An Egyptian official spokesman confirmed on the evening of the 16th that Egyptian police would enter Port Said on the morning of the 18th.
    The spokesman said that the United Nations Emergency Force Command had notified the Egyptian authorities through a liaison officer that Egyptian police could enter Port Said at 8:00 AM (local time) on the morning of the 18th.
    Approximately one thousand Egyptian police are gathered in Ismailia awaiting orders to head north. The spokesman said that 350 police officers would be sent to Port Said first, and the rest would follow in succession.
    Xinhua News Agency, December 17th: TASS, Cairo, December 16th: In recent days, the atrocities of the Anglo-French aggressors in Port Said have become increasingly rampant.
    According to the Middle East News Agency, during the city's curfew hours from 5:00 PM to 5:00 AM the next morning, British and French soldiers looted shops, antique stores, and warehouses on the streets of Port Said.
    Reports indicate that in the course of a recent 24-hour period, Egyptian authorities in Port Said learned of fifteen cases of shops and warehouses being robbed and thirty cases of private homes being burglarized.
    Xinhua News Agency, December 17th: Port Said News: In the early hours of the 16th, British aggressor troops in Port Said used tanks and infantry to fire on Egyptian residents in the Arab quarter of Port Said, killing approximately thirty residents and injuring many others.
    According to Western news agencies, British authorities dispatched a large number of troops late on the 15th to surround an Egyptian residential area, claiming they were searching for Egyptian guerrillas. The British troops' provocative actions were met with resistance, and a British major in command was killed. Consequently, British troops launched a large-scale attack and massacre of Egyptian residents using tanks. The intense sounds of machine guns, rifles, and tank cannons shook Port Said.

### 埃及警察定今日进入塞得港  英法侵略军杀人劫掠越来越猖狂

1956-12-18
第6版()
专栏：

    埃及警察定今日进入塞得港
    英法侵略军杀人劫掠越来越猖狂
    据新华社讯　开罗消息：埃及的一位官方发言人在16日晚上证实，埃及警察将在18日上午进入塞得港。
    这位发言人说，联合国紧急部队司令部通过一个联络官通知埃及当局说，埃及警察可以在18日上午八时（当地时间）进入塞得港。
    集结在伊士美利亚待命北上的埃及警察大约有一千人。这个发言人说，将先派三百五十个警察去塞得港，其余的将随后陆续前往。
    据新华社17日讯　据塔斯社开罗16日讯：近几天来，英法侵略者在塞得港的暴行愈来愈猖狂了。
    据中东通讯社报道，从晚上五点到第二天早晨五点的全市戒严时间里，英、法士兵就到塞得港大街上抢劫商店、古玩铺和仓库。
    有消息说，在最近的一个昼夜之间，塞得港的埃及当局就获悉发生抢劫商店和仓库案件十五起，私人住宅失盗三十起。
    据新华社17日讯　塞得港消息：在塞得港的英国侵略军16日凌晨使用坦克和步兵，向塞得港阿拉伯区的埃及居民开火射击，大约有三十个居民被杀害，许多人受伤。
    据西方通讯社报道，英国当局在15日深夜就出动大批军队，包围了一个埃及的居民点，说是要搜捕埃及的游击队。英国军队的挑衅行动遭到反抗，一个带队的英国少校被击毙，于是英国军队就用坦克大举进攻和杀害埃及居民。密集的机关枪声和步枪声以及坦克炮的轰击声震动了塞得港。
