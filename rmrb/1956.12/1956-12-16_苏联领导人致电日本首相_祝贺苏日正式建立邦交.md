Title: Soviet Leaders Send Telegram to Japanese Prime Minister Congratulating on Formal Establishment of Diplomatic Relations Between the Soviet Union and Japan
Author:
Date: 1956-12-16
Page: 5

    Xinhua News Agency, December 15th, Moscow Dispatch: According to TASS, Bulganin, Khrushchev, and Mikoyan sent a joint telegram yesterday to Japanese Prime Minister Ichiro Hatoyama, stating that we are deeply satisfied with the restoration of normal, good-neighborly relations between the Soviet Union and Japan. We believe that the restoration of this relationship will play a positive role in easing international tensions in the Far East and the world.
    The telegram said that we believe that the relationship between our two countries will continuously develop in a spirit of friendship, mutual understanding, and comprehensive cooperation, which will benefit the people of both countries.
    On the same day, Khrushchev and Bulganin also sent telegrams to Ichiro Kono, and Shepilov sent telegrams to Mamoru Shigemitsu and Shunichi Matsumoto, congratulating the formal establishment of diplomatic relations between the Soviet Union and Japan.

### 苏联领导人致电日本首相  祝贺苏日正式建立邦交

1956-12-16
第5版()
专栏：

    苏联领导人致电日本首相
    祝贺苏日正式建立邦交
    新华社15日讯　据塔斯社莫斯科讯：布尔加宁、赫鲁晓夫和米高扬昨天联名打电报给日本首相鸠山一郎说，我们对于苏联和日本两国之间恢复正常善邻关系这件事感到深为满意。我们相信，这种关系的恢复将在缓和远东和全世界的国际紧张局势方面起积极的作用。
    电文说，我们相信，我们两国之间的关系，将本着友好、互相谅解和全面合作的精神不断发展，而利于我们两国人民。
    同一天，赫鲁晓夫和布尔加宁还打电报给河野一郎，谢皮洛夫分别打电报给重光葵和松本俊一，祝贺苏日两国正式建交。
