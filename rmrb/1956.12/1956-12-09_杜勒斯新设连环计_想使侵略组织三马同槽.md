Title: Dulles Devises New Intricate Plot: Attempting to Unite Three Aggressive Organizations
Author:
Date: 1956-12-09
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, December 8th: A United Press dispatch from Washington states that U.S. Secretary of State Dulles assured the ambassadors of the four Middle Eastern countries participating in the Baghdad Pact on the 5th that he would consider their proposal to establish "some kind of connection" between the Baghdad Pact, the North Atlantic Treaty Organization, and the Manila Pact.

Dulles held a meeting with the ambassadors of these four countries – Turkey, Iraq, Iran, and Pakistan – on the morning of the 5th.

The message stated that if such a connection were established, it would help unify the "defense policy" that extends "from the United States and Canada across the Atlantic to Europe, and from there through the Near East to Thailand, Australia, and New Zealand."

### 杜勒斯新设连环计  想使侵略组织三马同槽

1956-12-09
第5版()
专栏：

　　杜勒斯新设连环计
　  想使侵略组织三马同槽
    据新华社8日讯　合众社从华盛顿发出的一条消息说，美国国务卿杜勒斯已经在5日向参加巴格达条约的四个中东国家的大使保证，他将考虑他们所提出的在巴格达条约、北大西洋公约和马尼拉条约之间建立“某种联系”的建议。
    杜勒斯在5日上午同这四个国家——土耳其、伊拉克、伊朗和巴基斯坦——的大使举行了一次会谈。
    消息说，如果建立了这样一种联系，那就有助于把那个“从美国和加拿大越过大西洋到欧洲，并且从那里通过近东到泰国、澳大利亚和新西兰”的“防御政策”统一起来。
