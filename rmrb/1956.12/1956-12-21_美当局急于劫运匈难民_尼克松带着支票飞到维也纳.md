Title: U.S. Authorities Eager to Hijack Hungarian Refugees; Nixon Flies to Vienna with Checks
Author:
Date: 1956-12-21
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, Vienna, 20th - On a gloomy afternoon on the 19th, an American plane delivered the American Vice President to Schwechat Airport in Vienna.

The Vice President, upon disembarking, announced to reporters that he had American dollar checks in his pocket and that he had come to Austria to resolve the "economic" problems of transporting Hungarian refugees to the United States.

He said, "While I am here, I hope to obtain firsthand information from the refugees themselves."

Xinhua News Agency, Washington, 18th - U.S. President Eisenhower announced on the 15th that the United States would allocate another four million dollars to "aid" Hungarian refugees in Austria. This sum will be paid from the President's $100 million emergency fund stipulated by the Mutual Security Act.

### 美当局急于劫运匈难民  尼克松带着支票飞到维也纳

1956-12-21
第5版()
专栏：

    美当局急于劫运匈难民
    尼克松带着支票飞到维也纳
    据新华社20日讯　维也纳消息：19日的一个阴暗的下午，一架美国飞机把美国副总统送到了维也纳的施维哈特机场。
    这个副总统一下飞机就向记者宣布说，他口袋内装的是美金支票，他要来奥地利解决运送匈牙利难民往美国的“经济”问题。
    他说，“当我停留在这里的时候，我希望从难民自己那里获得第一手的情报。”
    据新华社18日讯　华盛顿消息：美国总统艾森豪威尔15日宣布，美国将再拨出四百万美元来“援助”在奥地利的匈牙利难民。这笔款项将从共同安全法所规定的总统一亿美元紧急基金拨付。
