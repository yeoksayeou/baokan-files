Title: Ceylon's Governor-General and Prime Minister Receive Chinese Government Trade Delegation; Bandaranaike Hopes for Early Mutual Visits Between the Prime Ministers of China and Ceylon
Author:
Date: 1956-12-17
Page: 5
Xinhua News Agency, Colombo, 16th: Ceylon's Governor-General, Sir Oliver Goonetilleke, and Prime Minister, S.W.R.D. Bandaranaike, separately received the Chinese Government Trade Delegation led by Minister of Foreign Trade, Ye Ji Zhuang, on the 14th.

　　Governor-General Goonetilleke, in receiving the delegation, emphasized that equal and mutually beneficial trade is beneficial to both Ceylon and China.

　　Prime Minister Bandaranaike received the Chinese Government Trade Delegation at his private residence. He told the delegation that Ceylon and China have had cultural and other connections since ancient times, and he hoped that these connections could be restored and developed. The Ceylonese Prime Minister stated that as an Asian, he was very happy to see China stand up. The founding of the new China has changed the situation, and China is playing an increasingly important role in international affairs. He also expressed his hope that Premier Zhou Enlai would visit Ceylon in the near future, and that he himself hoped to visit China as soon as possible.

　　On the 14th, the Chinese Government Trade Delegation also called on Ceylon's Minister of Commerce and Trade, C.P. de Silva, and subsequently called on the Minister of Agriculture, Food and Cooperative Development, Philip Gunawardena, as well as the Minister of Industries and Fisheries, P.H. William de Silva.

### 锡兰总督和总理接见我政府贸易代表团  班达拉奈克希望锡中两国总理早日互访

1956-12-17
第5版()
专栏：

    锡兰总督和总理接见我政府贸易代表团
    班达拉奈克希望锡中两国总理早日互访
    新华社16日讯　科伦坡消息：锡兰总督古涅狄莱克和总理班达拉奈克在14日分别接见了由对外贸易部长叶季壮率领的中国政府贸易代表团。
    古涅狄莱克总督在接见代表团的时候，强调指出平等互利的贸易对锡中两国都有益处。
    班达拉奈克总理在他的私邸接见了中国政府贸易代表团。他对代表团说，锡中两国自古以来就有着文化和其他方面的联系，希望这些联系能够恢复和发展。这位锡兰总理表示，作为亚洲人，他很高兴看到中国站起来了。新中国的成立改变了局势，中国在国际事务中起着越来越重要的作用。他并且希望周恩来总理以后不久能访问锡兰，他自己也希望以后能够尽早访问中国。
    中国政府贸易代表团在14日还拜会了锡兰商业和贸易部长沈纳那亚克，并且还先后拜会了农业、粮食和合作发展部长古纳瓦地尼以及工业和渔业部长德西尔瓦。
