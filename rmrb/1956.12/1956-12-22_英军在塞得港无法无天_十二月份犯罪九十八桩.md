Title: British Troops Run Amok in Port Said; Ninety-Eight Crimes in December
Author: 
Date: 1956-12-22
Page: 6

    Xinhua News Agency, December 21st - Tass, Cairo, December 21st: According to the Middle East News Agency, Egyptian authorities have notified the United Nations Emergency Force Command in Port Said that British troops have committed ninety-eight crimes since December.
    According to newspaper reports, residents of Port Said returning from British concentration camps recounted the brutal treatment they suffered in these camps, as well as the beatings and torture inflicted by British soldiers.
    According to "Al-Ahram," there are currently at least seven hundred Egyptians in British concentration camps who were arrested during the large-scale searches of the Arab quarter of Port Said by the invaders.

### 英军在塞得港无法无天  十二月份犯罪九十八桩

1956-12-22
第6版()
专栏：

    英军在塞得港无法无天
    十二月份犯罪九十八桩
    新华社21日讯　塔斯社开罗21日讯：据中东通讯社报道，埃及当局通知驻塞得港的联合国紧急部队司令部说，英国军队从12月以来犯了九十八桩罪行。
    据报纸报道，从英国集中营回来的塞得港居民讲述了在这些集中营中他们所受到的残酷虐待，以及英国士兵所施行的毒打和拷问。
    据“金字塔报”报道，在英国的集中营里目前至少还有七百名埃及人，这些埃及人是在侵略者大规模搜查塞得港的阿拉伯区的时候被捕的。
