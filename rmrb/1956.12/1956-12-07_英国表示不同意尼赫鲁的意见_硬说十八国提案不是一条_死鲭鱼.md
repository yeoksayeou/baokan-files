Title: Britain Disagrees with Nehru, Insists the Eighteen-Nation Proposal is Not a "Dead Mackerel"
Author:
Date: 1956-12-07
Page: 6

　　Xinhua News Agency, 6th: London dispatch: The British Foreign Office stated on the 5th that Britain disagrees with Indian Prime Minister Nehru's opinion and insists on "international management" of the Suez Canal. Nehru criticized this attitude of Britain and France when he spoke in the Indian Council of States on the 4th, saying that the so-called eighteen-nation proposal for "international management" of the canal is a "dead mackerel."

　　A spokesman for the British Foreign Office said: "We do not believe that the eighteen-nation plan is dead. We believe that this plan is the most practical plan ever for establishing a system to realize the six principles of canal management passed by the United Nations Security Council."

　　He also stated that Britain wants the United Nations Secretary-General to expedite the resumption of negotiations on the future of the canal.

### 英国表示不同意尼赫鲁的意见  硬说十八国提案不是一条“死鲭鱼”

1956-12-07
第6版()
专栏：

　　英国表示不同意尼赫鲁的意见
　  硬说十八国提案不是一条“死鲭鱼”
    新华社6日讯　伦敦消息：英国外交部在5日表示，英国不同意印度总理尼赫鲁的意见，它坚持要“国际管理”苏伊士运河。尼赫鲁4日在印度联邦院讲话的时候曾经对英法的这种态度提出批评，他说，所谓“国际管理”运河的十八国提案是一条“死鲭鱼”。
    英国外交部的发言人说：“我们不认为十八国计划死亡了。我们认为，这个计划是关于建立一个制度以使联合国安全理事会通过的运河管理六项原则得到实现的、历来最实际的计划。”
    他还表示，英国要联合国秘书长使关于运河的未来的谈判赶快恢复。
