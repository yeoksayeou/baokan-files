Title: Mayor of Bombay Delivers Speech in Tianjin: China and India Uphold the Glorious Tradition of Peaceful Coexistence
Author: 
Date: 1956-12-29
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, Tianjin, December 28th. Mayor Kader of Bombay, India, at the invitation of the Presidium of the First Session of the Second Tianjin Municipal People's Congress, delivered a speech at the congress today.

    He said that there is a long history of exchanges between India and China in culture, religion, and commerce; there has never been a war, which is a glorious tradition. The people of our two countries will do their best to prevent any forces in the world from undermining the close ties of friendship between our brothers.

    Kader believes that a major power still refuses to allow China to enter the United Nations, which is refusing to listen to the demands of the times. He said, "We Indians believe that preventing China from entering the United Nations excludes a peaceful power and dislocates many things. But no matter what, today our two countries are moving forward on the road to peace and prosperity, and nothing in the world can stop it!"

    He continued: "With the development of world politics and the development of our two countries' internal strength, India gained freedom in 1947, and China gained freedom in 1949. We were also companions in the struggle for freedom." Kader added: "Gaining freedom is only the beginning of our struggle. We must also rapidly develop industrial and agricultural production and improve the people's living standards to overcome the backwardness of our two countries. In these areas, we have many things in common. Therefore, we can also see the enormous tasks facing your country and our people. But it will definitely be faced and solved."

    Speaking of foreign policy, Kader said: "India and China have almost identical views on international affairs. Through the great efforts of your Premier Zhou Enlai and our Prime Minister Nehru, the great 'Panchsheel' (Five Principles) was produced, which will become a milestone in world history."

    At the end of his speech, Kader shouted long live to the people of China and India, and long live to "Panchsheel." Thunderous applause rang out throughout the hall.

### 孟买市长在天津发表演说  中印坚持和平共处的光荣传统

1956-12-29
第4版()
专栏：

    孟买市长在天津发表演说
    中印坚持和平共处的光荣传统
    新华社天津28日电　印度孟买市市长卡德尔今天应天津市第二届人民代表大会第一次会议主席团的邀请，在大会上发表演说。
    他说，印中两国在文化、宗教和商业等方面的交流，有着悠久的历史；从来没有过战争，这是一个光荣的传统。我们两国人民将尽力不让世界上的任何力量来破坏我们兄弟之间的密切的友谊联系。
    卡德尔认为，有一个大国到现在仍然拒绝中国进入联合国，这是拒绝听从时代的要求。他说，“我们印度人认为阻止中国进入联合国，就是排除了一个和平的强国，就使许多事情都脱节。但是不管怎么样，今天我们两国正在和平和繁荣的道路上迈进，世界上没有任何东西能够阻挡它！”
    他接着说：“随着世界政治的发展和我们两国内部力量的发展，印度在1947年获得了自由，中国在1949年获得了自由。在争取自由的时候我们两国也是友伴。”卡德尔又说：“获得自由只是我们斗争的开始，我们还须迅速发展工农业生产和提高人民的生活水平，来克服我们两国的落后状态。在这些方面，我们两国有许多共同之处，因此，我们同样可以看到摆在贵国和我国人民面前的是怎样巨大的任务。但是它一定会得到正视和解决。”
    在谈到外交政策时，卡德尔说：“印度和中国对于国际事务有着几乎相同的观点。经过你们的周恩来总理和我们的尼赫鲁总理的巨大努力，产生了伟大的‘潘查希拉’（五项原则），这件事将成为世界历史的里程碑。”
    卡德尔在结束他的演说时为中印两国人民、为“潘查希拉”高呼万岁。全场响起雷鸣般的掌声。
