Title: China and Indonesia Governments Approve China-Indonesia Trade Agreement and Exchange Notes
Author:
Date: 1954-01-18
Page: 1
Xinhua News Agency, 18th: The Trade Agreement between the People's Republic of China and the Republic of Indonesia, signed in Beijing on November 30, 1953, has been approved by the governments of both countries, and notes have been exchanged. The full text of the agreement is as follows:
Trade Agreement between the People's Republic of China and the Republic of Indonesia
The central People's Government of the People's Republic of China and the Government of the of the Republic of Indonesia, in order to promote and develop direct trade relations between the two countries on the basis of equality and mutual benefit, have reached the following agreement:
Article 1: The contracting parties are willing to take all appropriate measures to promote trade between the two countries in every possible way. For this purpose, the contracting parties have exchanged lists of goods normally exported by the two countries, listed in "List A" and "List B" as the scope of trade between the two countries. These two lists are attached to this agreement. This agreement does not intend to restrict trade in commodities not listed in these appendices.
Article 2: In order to implement the principles stipulated in this agreement, the contracting parties agree to further discuss the transactions and arrangements to be carried out.
Article 3: The contracting parties agree that trade between the two countries should be based on the principle of balanced imports and exports.
Article 4: The contracting parties agree that the exchange of goods shall comply with the scope stipulated by the general import and export administration laws and regulations of the two countries in effect at that time.
Article 5: The contracting parties agree to provide convenience for imports and exports from both sides, and issue licenses accordingly.
Article 6: In order to expand trade between the two countries, both parties are willing to take appropriate measures to facilitate trade on a multilateral basis.
Article 7: The contracting parties agree that in the event of problems arising in the implementation of this agreement, both parties may consult each other.
Article 8: This agreement shall come into effect immediately after the governments of the two countries exchange notes, and its validity shall be until December 31, 1954, and may be extended with the consent of both parties before its expiration.
This agreement was signed in Beijing on November 30, 1953, in two copies, each in Chinese and Indonesian, both texts being equally authentic.
(Signed) Lei Renmin, Deputy Minister of Foreign Trade of the Central People's Government of the People's Republic of China, on behalf of the Central People's Government
(Signed) Asmawin, Head of the Economic Delegation of the Government of the Republic of Indonesia, on behalf of the Government of the Republic of Indonesia

### 我国政府和印度尼西亚政府批准中印贸易协定并互致照会

1954-01-18
第1版()
专栏：

    我国政府和印度尼西亚政府批准中印贸易协定并互致照会
    【新华社十八日讯】一九五三年十一月三十日在北京签订的中华人民共和国和印度尼西亚共和国贸易协定已经两国政府批准，并互致照会。该协定全文如下：
    中华人民共和国和印度尼西亚共和国贸易协定
    中华人民共和国中央人民政府和印度尼西亚共和国政府为了在平等互利的基础上促进并发展两国间的直接贸易关系，达成协议如下：
    第一条  缔约双方愿采取一切适宜的措施，就各种可能来促进两国之间的贸易。为此目的，缔约双方交换了两国通常出口的商品货单，分列“甲”“乙”两表作为两国间进行贸易的范围，该两表为本协定的附件。对该附表所未列入之商品的贸易，本协定并无限制之意。
    第二条　为执行本协定所规定之原则，缔约双方同意进一步商讨所欲进行的交易和安排。
    第三条　缔约双方同意：两国间的贸易以进出口平衡为原则。
    第四条　缔约双方同意：商品的交换应遵守两国当时有效的一般进出口管理法令所规定之范围。
    第五条　缔约双方同意：对双方的进出口给予便利，并据此发给许可证。
    第六条　为了扩大两国间的贸易，双方愿采取适宜的措施以利于以多边为基础的贸易。
    第七条　缔约双方同意：在执行本协定中所发生的问题，双方得互相磋商。
    第八条　本协定应在两国政府交换照会后即行生效，其有效期至一九五四年十二月三十一日为止，并得在期满前经双方同意予以延长。
    本协定于一九五三年十一月三十日在北京签订，共两份，每份均以中文及印尼文书就，两种文字的条文均有同等效力。中华人民共和国中央人民政府代表中央人民政府对外贸易部副部长雷任民（签字）印度尼西亚共和国政府代表印度尼西亚共和国政府经济代表团团长阿斯玛温（签字）附表甲：
