Capital Cultural Relics Workers and Archaeological Workers Write to Demand Severe Punishment for Yue Bin, the Unscrupulous Merchant Who Stole and Sold Longmen Grottoes

Editor's Note: The crime of the unscrupulous merchant Yue Bin and others in stealing Longmen Grottoes is extremely serious. The Beijing People's Court arrested Yue Bin and Ni Yushu in August 1952. However, because some judicial cadres responsible for handling this case lacked an understanding of the serious nature of Yue Bin and others colluding with imperialism to steal and sell cultural relics, the sentencing has been delayed until now. The North China Branch of the Supreme People's Court has now urged the Beijing People's Court to promptly conduct a serious handling of this matter.

Comrade Editor:

We were extremely indignant when we saw the serious crimes of the unscrupulous merchant Yue Bin in stealing and selling Longmen Grottoes, as exposed in the letter from sculptor Liu Kaiqu and nine others published in the People's Daily on December 16. The sculptures of Longmen Grottoes are a great artistic masterpiece of our country and one of our precious cultural heritages. Yue Bin and other profiteering merchants actually signed contracts with imperialists and systematically knocked down and stole countless statues and reliefs. We absolutely cannot tolerate such heinous crimes.

Before liberation, imperialist elements colluded with the reactionary government and antique dealers, using various open or covert means to plunder and steal a large number of cultural relics and books with historical and artistic value from our country, causing huge and irreparable losses to our country's precious cultural heritage. Not only we cultural relics workers and archaeologists feel deep hatred, but every Chinese person who loves the motherland and loves culture and art is also filled with indignation. Yue Bin and others recklessly and blatantly stealing the precious cultural relics of the motherland is a typical example of this kind of crime. We demand that the People's Government impose severe legal sanctions on Yue Bin and other running dogs of imperialism.

### 首都文物工作者和考古工作者来信  要求严惩盗卖龙门石刻的奸商岳彬

1954-01-20
第2版()
专栏：

    首都文物工作者和考古工作者来信
    要求严惩盗卖龙门石刻的奸商岳彬
    编者按：奸商岳彬等盗窃龙门石刻的罪行是十分严重的。北京市人民法院曾于一九五二年
    八月将岳彬、倪玉书逮捕。但因负责处理这个案件的某些司法干部对岳彬等勾结帝国主义盗卖
    文物的严重罪恶的性质缺乏认识，以致拖延至今尚未判处。现在最高人民法院华北分院已督促
    北京市人民法院迅速进行严肃处理。编辑同志：
    我们见到十二月十六日人民日报所载雕刻家刘开渠等十人来信所揭发的奸商岳彬盗卖龙门石刻的严重罪行，感到极大的愤慨。龙门石窟的雕刻是我国伟大的艺术杰作，是我国珍贵文化遗产之一。唯利是图的奸商岳彬等，竟与帝国主义订定契约，有计划地将无数的雕像和浮雕敲下盗走。这种丧尽天良的严重罪行是我们绝对不能容忍的。
    解放以前，帝国主义分子与反动政府及古玩奸商狼狈为奸，利用公开的或隐蔽的各种不同方式，巧取豪夺地将我国大量具有历史艺术价值之文物图书盗运出口，使我国珍贵文化遗产遭到了巨大的不可补偿的损失。不仅我们文物工作者和考古工作者感到深切的痛恨，每一个热爱祖国、热爱文化艺术的中国人民也都气愤填膺。岳彬等肆无忌惮、明目张胆地盗劫祖国的珍贵文物，就是这种罪行的典型例证。我们要求人民政府对帝国主义的走狗奸商岳彬等，予以严厉的法律制裁。
