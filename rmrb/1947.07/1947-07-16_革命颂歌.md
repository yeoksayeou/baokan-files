Title: A Song of Revolution
Author: Yisakovskiy
Date: 1947-07-16
Page: 4

    A Song of Revolution
    By Isakovsky
    Translated by Ge Baoquan
    At dawn, in the misty light,
    Along the barren and unlovely fields,
    It is I—a nameless peasant
    Sowing grain and seeds mixed with sorrow.

    It is I, traveling through cities and villages,
    Everywhere seeking your footprints;
    I summon you with unhappy songs,
    I have waited for you for a thousand years.

    It is I, in the cold, long nights,
    Thinking of you with a thousand thoughts.
    It is I, with a gun on my shoulder,
    Fighting for you.

    Through the forests, through the forever newly reclaimed land,
    You heard my sorrow,
    It is you—the first person in a thousand years
    To extend a hand to the peasant.

    Under the un-repaired rooftops,
    You brought a happy life,
    From you, this first person, I heard
    My own true name.

    I feel warm because of your breath,
    I feel the warmth because of your caress,
    I am now the master of land and sea,
    Walking across the entire Soviet land.

    I do not know—what great
    River I must cross,
    What places I must pass through,
    But I know, I can never let you
    Be humiliated by anyone.

Isakovsky is a famous Soviet poet, author of famous poems such as "Katyusha" and "And Who Knows Him," and a recipient of the Stalin Literary Prize. We introduce this poem in the hope of inspiring more authors to sing praises for the victory of our land reform. --Editor

### 革命颂歌
伊萨科夫斯基
1947-07-16
第4版()
专栏：

    革命颂歌
    伊萨科夫斯基作
    戈宝权译
    在黎明，在雾气的曙光中，
    沿着贫瘠而不可爱的田地，
    这是我——一个无名的农夫
    在播着五谷和悲哀参半的种子。
    这是我，走遍了城市和乡村，
    到处在寻觅你的足迹；
    我用不快乐的歌声召唤着你，
    我等你等待了一千年。
    这是我，在寒冷的长夜中，
    千思万思地想念着你。
    这是我，肩上挂着枪
    为了你作战。
    穿过了森林，穿过了永远的新垦地
    你听见了我的哀愁，
    这是你——千年以来第一个人
    向农民伸出了手。
    在没有修过的屋脊下，
    你带来了幸福的生活，
    从你，这第一个人，我听见了
    自己的真正的名字。
    我因为你的呼吸而感到和暖，
    我因为你的爱抚而感到温馨，
    我现在是陆地和海洋的主人，
    正走遍全苏维埃的国土。
    我不知道——我还要越过怎样的大
    河，
    走过怎样的地方，
    但我知道，我永远不能让您，
    受到任何人的凌辱。
    伊萨科夫斯基是苏联名诗人，“卡秋莎”、“而谁又知道他”等名诗的作者，曾得斯大林文艺奖金。我们介绍他的这篇诗，是希望引起更多作者为我们土地改革的胜利而颂歌。          ——编者
