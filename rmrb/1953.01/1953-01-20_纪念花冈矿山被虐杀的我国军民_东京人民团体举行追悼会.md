Title: Tokyo People's Organizations Hold Memorial Service for Chinese Military and Civilians Massacred at Hanaoka Mine
Author:
Date: 1953-01-20
Page: 4

    In Memory of the Chinese Military and Civilians Massacred at Hanaoka Mine
    Tokyo People's Organizations Hold Memorial Service
    [Xinhua News Agency, Tokyo, 18th] News: Japanese militarists massacred more than 400 captured Chinese soldiers and forcibly conscripted Chinese workers at the Hanaoka Mine in Akita Prefecture, Japan, on the eve of their surrender in 1945. To commemorate these murdered Chinese military and civilians, the Tokyo Chinese Chamber of Commerce, the Japan-China Friendship Association, and the Japan Peace Liaison Committee jointly held a memorial service at Zao Temple in Asakusa, Tokyo, on the 17th. Wreaths presented by organizations such as the China-Japan Trade Promotion Association were neatly arranged at the venue. The Japanese delegation preparing to discuss the repatriation of Japanese nationals in China dispatched four people to participate in the memorial service: Tadao Kudo, Yoshitaro Hirano, Masaharu Hatanaka, and Toshio Kashima.

### 纪念花冈矿山被虐杀的我国军民  东京人民团体举行追悼会

1953-01-20
第4版()
专栏：

    纪念花冈矿山被虐杀的我国军民
    东京人民团体举行追悼会
    【新华社十八日讯】东京消息：日本军国主义者在一九四五年投降前夕曾在日本秋田县花冈矿山虐杀被俘的中国士兵和被强征的中国工人共计四百多人。为纪念这些被害的中国军民，东京华商协会、日中友好协会和日本和平联络委员会于十七日在东京浅草区枣寺联合举行追悼会。会场整齐地排列着中日贸易促进会等团体致送的花圈。准备到中国商谈日侨归国问题的日本代表团，推派工藤忠夫、平野义太郎、畑中政春、加岛敏雄等四人参加了追悼会。
