Regulations on Labor Insurance of the People's Republic of China
(Promulgated by the Government Administration Council on February 26, 1951)
(Revised and Promulgated by the Government Administration Council on January 2, 1953)

Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 These Regulations are formulated in accordance with the current economic conditions in order to protect the health of workers and staff and alleviate their difficulties in life.

Article 2 The implementation of these Regulations shall be carried out in a step-by-step manner. The current scope of implementation is tentatively set as follows:

	A. State-owned, joint state-private, private, and cooperative factories, mines, and their affiliated units with more than 100 workers and staff.
	B. Various enterprise units and affiliated units of railways, shipping, and post and telecommunications.
	C. Basic construction units of industrial, mining, and transportation undertakings.
	D. State-owned construction companies.

The Central Labor Department shall submit opinions on the continued promotion of the scope of implementation of these Regulations at any time based on the actual situation, and submit them to the Government Administration Council of the Central People's Government for decision.

Article 3 For enterprises not implementing these Regulations and seasonal enterprises, matters related to labor insurance may be negotiated by the administrative or capitalist side of the enterprise or the administrative side or capitalist and trade union organizations of its affiliated industry or trade, in accordance with the principles of these Regulations and the actual situation of the enterprise, industry, or trade, and stipulated in a collective contract.

Article 4 All workers and staff (including apprentices) working in enterprises implementing labor insurance, regardless of ethnicity, age, gender, or nationality, shall be subject to these Regulations, except for those deprived of political rights.

Article 5 The labor insurance benefits for temporary workers, seasonal workers, and probationary employees working in enterprises implementing labor insurance shall be stipulated separately in the detailed rules for the implementation of these Regulations.

Article 6 Enterprises within the scope of these Regulations that have special economic difficulties and are difficult to maintain, or have not yet formally started business, may temporarily postpone the implementation of these Regulations after consultation and agreement between the enterprise's administrative side or capitalist and the grassroots trade union committee, and with the approval of the local people's government labor administrative organ.

Chapter II Collection and Management of Labor Insurance Funds

Article 7 All expenses for labor insurance stipulated in these Regulations shall be borne by the administrative side or capitalist of the enterprise implementing labor insurance. Part of it shall be paid directly by the administrative side or capitalist of the enterprise, and another part shall be paid as labor insurance funds to the trade union organization for handling.

Article 8 All enterprises implementing labor insurance in accordance with these Regulations shall pay monthly an amount equivalent to 3% of the total wages of all workers and staff in the enterprise as labor insurance funds. This labor insurance fund shall not be deducted from the wages of workers and staff, nor shall it be collected separately from workers and staff.

Article 9 The methods for collecting and managing labor insurance funds are as follows:

	A. The administrative side or capitalist of the enterprise shall calculate according to the total wages of the previous month and pay the monthly labor insurance funds to the state bank designated by the All-China Federation of Trade Unions within the time limit of the first to tenth of each month.
	B. Within the first two months of starting to implement labor insurance, the labor insurance funds paid monthly by the administrative side or capitalist of the enterprise shall be deposited in the account of the All-China Federation of Trade Unions as the general labor insurance fund for the purpose of organizing collective labor insurance undertakings. From the third month of implementation, 30% of the monthly labor insurance funds shall be deposited in the account of the All-China Federation of Trade Unions as the general labor insurance fund; 70% shall be deposited in the account of the grassroots trade union committee of the enterprise as the labor insurance fund for the purpose of paying the funeral expenses, subsidies, and relief funds that workers and staff should receive in accordance with these Regulations.

Article 10 If the administrative side or capitalist of the enterprise fails to pay or underpays the labor insurance funds within the time limit, it shall pay a late fee of 1% of the unpaid amount per day. If it has not been paid after 20 days, for state-owned, local state-owned, joint state-private or cooperative enterprises, the grassroots trade union committee shall notify the local state bank to deduct the payment from its expenses; for private enterprises, the grassroots trade union committee shall report to the local people's government labor administrative organ to hold the capitalist of the enterprise accountable.

Article 11 The management of labor insurance funds shall be entrusted by the All-China Federation of Trade Unions to the People's Bank of China for agency.

Chapter III Provisions on Various Labor Insurance Benefits

Article 12 Provisions on treatment for work-related injuries and disabilities:

	A. Workers and staff who are injured at work shall be treated in the enterprise's clinic, hospital, or contracted hospital. If the enterprise's clinic, hospital, or contracted hospital cannot treat them, the administrative side or capitalist of the enterprise shall transfer them to other hospitals for treatment. All diagnosis and treatment fees, medicine fees, hospitalization fees, food expenses during hospitalization, and travel expenses for medical treatment shall be borne by the administrative side or capitalist of the enterprise. Wages shall be paid as usual during the medical treatment period.
	B. When workers and staff are determined to be disabled due to work-related injuries, the disability benefits or disability allowances due to work-related injuries shall be paid monthly from the labor insurance fund according to the following circumstances:
		1. Those who have completely lost their labor ability and cannot work after retirement, and need assistance with eating and daily life, the amount of their disability benefits due to work-related injuries shall be 75% of their wages, paid until death.
		2. Those who have completely lost their labor ability and cannot work after retirement, and do not need assistance with eating and daily life, the amount of their disability benefits due to work-related injuries shall be 60% of their wages, paid until the recovery of labor ability or death. After the recovery of labor ability, the administrative side or capitalist of the enterprise shall provide appropriate work.
		3. Those who have partially lost their labor ability and can still work shall be assigned appropriate work by the administrative side or capitalist of the enterprise, and the labor insurance fund shall pay disability allowances for work-related injuries according to the degree of loss of labor ability after disability, the amount of which is 10% to 30% of their wages before disability, but the total of the disability allowance and the wages at the time of returning to work after disability shall not exceed the wages before disability. Detailed measures shall be specified in the implementation rules.
	C. The determination and change of the disability status of workers and staff who are disabled due to work-related injuries shall be determined by the Disability Review Committee. Detailed measures shall be specified in the implementation rules.

Article 13 Provisions on treatment for illness, non-work-related injuries, and disabilities:

	A. When workers and staff are ill or injured due to non-work-related reasons, the diagnosis and treatment fees, surgery fees, hospitalization fees, and ordinary medicine fees required for treatment at the enterprise's clinic, hospital, contracted hospital, or contracted Chinese and Western medicine practitioners shall be borne by the administrative side or capitalist of the enterprise; expensive medicine fees, food expenses during hospitalization, and travel expenses for medical treatment shall be borne by the individual. If the individual's economic situation is indeed difficult, a discretionary subsidy may be given from the labor insurance fund. Whether workers and staff who are ill or injured due to non-work-related reasons should be hospitalized or transferred to another hospital for treatment and the time of discharge from the hospital shall be entirely determined by the hospital.
	B. When workers and staff stop working for medical treatment due to illness or non-work-related injuries, if the continuous period of stopping work for medical treatment is within six months, the enterprise's administrative side or capitalist shall pay sick leave wages according to the length of their work experience in the enterprise, the amount of which is 60% to 100% of their wages; if the continuous period of stopping work for medical treatment is more than six months, the labor insurance fund shall pay monthly illness or non-work-related injury relief funds, the amount of which is 40% to 60% of their wages, until they can work or are determined to be disabled or die. Detailed measures shall be specified in the implementation rules.
	C. When workers and staff are determined to be disabled after the end of medical treatment due to illness or non-work-related injuries, and completely lose their labor ability after retirement, the sick leave wages or illness non-work-related injury relief funds shall be stopped, and the non-work-related disability relief funds shall be issued by the labor insurance fund, the amount of which shall be determined according to the following circumstances: those who need assistance with eating and daily life shall receive 50% of their wages, those who do not need assistance with eating and daily life shall receive 40% of their wages, until the recovery of labor ability or death; those who have partially lost their labor ability and can still work shall not be given. The provisions of Article 12, Paragraph C shall apply to the determination and change of disability status.
	D. After workers and staff recover from illness or non-work-related injuries or non-work-related disabilities and restore their labor ability, the administrative side or capitalist of the enterprise shall provide appropriate work after receiving a certificate from the responsible medical institution.
	E. When the direct relatives supported by workers and staff are ill, they may receive free diagnosis and treatment at the enterprise's clinic, hospital, contracted hospital, or contracted Chinese and Western medicine practitioners. The surgery fees and ordinary medicine fees shall be borne by the enterprise's administrative side or capitalist by one-half, and the expensive medicine fees, travel expenses for medical treatment, hospitalization fees, food expenses during hospitalization, and all other expenses shall be borne by the individual.

Article 14 Provisions on treatment for the death of workers and staff and their supported direct relatives:

	A. When workers and staff die due to work-related reasons, the administrative side or capitalist of the enterprise shall pay funeral expenses, the amount of which is three months of the average wages of all workers and staff in the enterprise. In addition, the labor insurance fund shall pay monthly allowances to the supported direct relatives according to the number of supported direct relatives, the amount of which is 25% to 50% of the deceased's wages, until the supported person loses the conditions for being supported. Detailed measures shall be specified in the implementation rules.
	B. When workers and staff die due to illness or non-work-related injuries, the labor insurance fund shall pay funeral subsidies, the amount of which is two months of the average wages of all workers and staff in the enterprise. In addition, the labor insurance fund shall pay relief funds to the supported direct relatives according to the number of supported direct relatives. The amount is six months to twelve months of the deceased's wages. Detailed measures shall be specified in the implementation rules.
	C. When workers and staff are injured at work and become disabled and completely lose their labor ability after retirement, funeral expenses and allowances for supported direct relatives shall be paid in accordance with the provisions of paragraph A of this article. When they die after retirement, or when they die after non-work-related disability and complete loss of labor ability after retirement, funeral subsidies and relief funds for supported direct relatives shall be paid in accordance with the provisions of paragraph B of this article.
	D. When the supported direct relatives of workers and staff die, the labor insurance fund shall pay funeral subsidies to the supported direct relatives: for those who are over ten years old, the amount is one-half of one month's average wage of all workers and staff in the enterprise; for those who are one to ten years old, the amount is one-third of one month's average wage. No payment shall be made for those under one year old.

Article 15 Provisions on old-age treatment:

	A. Male workers and male staff who are 60 years old, have 25 years of general work experience, and have 5 years of work experience in the enterprise may retire for old age. After retirement, the labor insurance fund shall pay monthly retirement subsidies according to the length of their work experience in the enterprise, the amount of which is 50% to 70% of their wages, paid until death. Those who meet the conditions for old-age care but are retained to continue working due to the needs of the enterprise, in addition to paying the original wages, the labor insurance fund shall pay monthly on-the-job old-age subsidies according to the length of their work experience in the enterprise, the amount of which is 10% to 20% of their wages. Detailed measures shall be specified in the implementation rules.
	B. Female workers and female staff who are 50 years old, have 20 years of general work experience, and have 5 years of work experience in the enterprise shall be entitled to the old-age subsidy treatment stipulated in paragraph A of this article.
	C. Underground miners or those who work in low-temperature workplaces below 32 degrees Fahrenheit or high-temperature workplaces above 100 degrees Fahrenheit, male workers and male staff who are 55 years old, and female workers and female staff who are 45 years old, shall be entitled to the old-age subsidy treatment stipulated in paragraph A of this article. When calculating their general work experience and work experience in the enterprise, every year working in such places shall be calculated as one year and three months.
	D. Those who are directly engaged in work harmful to physical health in the industries of refining or manufacturing lead, mercury, arsenic, phosphorus, acid, and other chemical and military industries, male workers and male staff who are 55 years old, and female workers and female staff who are 45 years old, shall be entitled to the old-age subsidy treatment stipulated in paragraph A of this article. When calculating their general work experience and work experience in the enterprise, every year engaged in such work shall be calculated as one year and six months.

Article 16 Provisions on maternity treatment:

	A. Female workers and female staff who give birth shall be given 56 days of leave before and after childbirth, and wages shall be paid as usual during the maternity leave period.
	B. Female workers and female staff who have a miscarriage before seven months of pregnancy shall be given maternity leave of up to 30 days according to the doctor's opinion, and wages shall be paid as usual during the maternity leave period.
	C. Female workers and female staff who have a difficult delivery or give birth to twins shall be given an additional 14 days of leave, and wages shall be paid as usual.
	D. Female workers and female staff who are pregnant and undergo examinations or childbirth at the enterprise's clinic, hospital, or contracted hospital, the examination fees and delivery fees shall be borne by the enterprise's administrative side or capitalist, and other expenses shall be handled in accordance with the provisions of Article 13, paragraph A.
	E. Those who are still unable to work after the expiration of the maternity leave (whether normal delivery or miscarriage) shall be handled in accordance with the provisions of Article 13 regarding illness treatment after being certified by the hospital.
	F. When female workers and female staff or the wives of male workers and male staff give birth, a maternity subsidy of 40,000 yuan shall be issued from the labor insurance fund.

Article 17 Provisions on collective labor insurance undertakings:

	A. All workers and staff working in enterprises implementing labor insurance shall have the right to enjoy collective labor insurance undertakings. Detailed measures shall be formulated by the All-China Federation of Trade Unions.
	B. The grassroots trade union committees of each enterprise may, in accordance with the economic situation of the enterprise and the needs of the workers and staff, jointly run collective labor insurance undertakings such as sanatoriums, after-work sanatoriums, and nurseries with the enterprise's administrative side or capitalist. Detailed measures shall be specified in the implementation rules.
	C. The All-China Federation of Trade Unions may run or entrust local or industrial trade union organizations to run the following collective labor insurance undertakings:
		1. Sanatoriums;
		2. Recuperation centers;
		3. Nursing homes;
		4. Orphanages;
		5. Disability homes;
		6. Others.

Article 18 Workers and staff working in enterprises implementing labor insurance who have not joined the trade union, except for treatment for work-related injuries, disabilities, death, maternity treatment, treatment for illness or non-work-related injuries, and treatment for illnesses of supported direct relatives, shall be entitled to the provisions of these Regulations, other items, such as wages and relief funds during the medical treatment period for illness or non-work-related injuries, relief funds for non-work-related disabilities, relief funds for supported direct relatives, old-age subsidies, and funeral subsidies, can only receive half of the specified amount.

Chapter IV Provisions on Enjoying Superior Labor Insurance Treatment

Article 19 Labor models and combat heroes who have made special contributions to the enterprise, after being proposed by the grassroots trade union committee and approved by the trade union organizations of each province and city or the national committee of the industrial trade union, shall enjoy the following superior labor insurance treatment:

	A. Expensive medicine fees, travel expenses for medical treatment, and food expenses during hospitalization for illness or non-work-related injuries shall be borne by the enterprise's administrative side or capitalist.
	B. Wages shall be paid as usual for the first six months of the medical treatment period for illness or non-work-related injuries. Illness or non-work-related injury relief funds and non-work-related disability relief funds shall be paid at 60% of their wages. Disability benefits for work-related injuries shall be 100% of their wages. Disability allowances for work-related injuries shall be the difference between their wages before disability and their wages at the time of returning to work after disability. Allowances for supported direct relatives in case of death due to work-related injuries shall be 30% to 60% of their wages. Retirement subsidies shall be 60% to 80% of their wages. On-the-job old-age subsidies shall be 20% to 30% of their wages. Detailed measures shall be specified in the implementation rules.
	C. Have priority in enjoying collective labor insurance undertakings.

Article 20 When disabled veterans are transferred to work in the enterprise, wages shall be paid as usual for the first six months of the medical treatment period for stopping work due to illness or non-work-related injuries, regardless of the length of their work experience in the enterprise, and after six months, they shall be handled in accordance with the provisions of Article 13, paragraph B.

Chapter V Allocation of Labor Insurance Funds

Article 21 The allocation of labor insurance funds is as follows:

	A. The general labor insurance fund shall be used by the All-China Federation of Trade Unions to run collective labor insurance undertakings.
	B. The labor insurance fund shall be used by the grassroots trade union committee to pay various disability benefits, subsidies, and relief funds, and subsidies for collective labor insurance undertakings of the enterprise. It shall be settled once a month, and the remaining balance shall be transferred to the account of the provincial and municipal trade union organizations or the national committee of the industrial trade union as labor insurance adjustment funds (hereinafter referred to as adjustment funds).
	C. The adjustment funds shall be used by the provincial and municipal trade union organizations or the national committee of the industrial trade union to subsidize the grassroots trade union committees when the labor insurance funds are insufficient to pay, or to run collective labor insurance undertakings. The national committees of each industrial trade union may authorize their local institutions to manage the allocation of adjustment funds. The All-China Federation of Trade Unions has the right to coordinate the allocation of adjustment funds of the provincial and municipal trade union organizations and the national committees of each industrial trade union, and may use it to run collective labor insurance undertakings. If the adjustment funds of the provincial and municipal trade union organizations or the national committees of each industrial trade union are insufficient to pay, they may apply to the All-China Federation of Trade Unions for the allocation of adjustment funds for subsidies.

Article 22 Labor insurance funds shall not be used for other purposes except for labor insurance undertakings.

Article 23 The accounting departments of each enterprise shall establish independent accounting for labor insurance funds and be responsible for handling the income and expenditure of labor insurance funds. The accounting system for labor insurance funds shall be formulated by the Ministry of Labor of the Central People's Government in conjunction with the All-China Federation of Trade Unions.

Article 24 The income and expenditure of labor insurance adjustment funds shall be handled by the financial departments of trade union organizations at all levels in accordance with the regulations of the All-China Federation of Trade Unions.

Chapter VI Implementation and Supervision of Labor Insurance Undertakings

Article 25 The grassroots trade union committee is the grassroots unit for implementing labor insurance business, and its main tasks are: to supervise the payment of labor insurance funds; to decide on the payment of labor insurance funds; to supervise the expenditure of various expenses directly paid by the enterprise's administrative side or capitalist as stipulated in these Regulations; to promote the improvement of collective labor insurance undertakings and medical and health work of the enterprise; to carry out all practical business related to labor insurance; to compile monthly statements of labor insurance funds, and to compile annual budgets, final accounts, business plans, and business reports to report to the provincial and municipal trade union organizations, the national committees of industrial trade unions, and the local people's government labor administrative organs; and report work to the general membership meeting or the representative assembly of the trade union.

Article 26 The funding review committee of each grassroots trade union committee shall review the income and expenditure accounts of the labor insurance fund and the various expenses directly paid by the enterprise's administrative side or capitalist as stipulated in these Regulations on a monthly basis, and announce them.

Article 27 The provincial and municipal trade union organizations, the national committees of each industrial trade union, or the regional committees shall be responsible for guiding and supervising the labor insurance business of the subordinate grassroots trade union committees, reviewing the monthly income and expenditure statements, budgets, final accounts, and whether the income and expenditure of the labor insurance fund are incorrect, accepting appeals from workers and staff regarding labor insurance incidents, compiling monthly income and expenditure statements of the labor insurance fund and adjustment fund, and compiling annual budgets, final accounts, business plans, and business reports, and reporting according to the following procedures:

	A. The provincial and municipal trade union organizations shall report to the local people's government labor administrative organs and the trade union organizations of the Greater Administrative Region.
	B. The national committees of each industrial trade union shall report to the All-China Federation of Trade Unions and the Ministry of Labor of the Central People's Government.

Article 28 The trade union organizations of the Greater Administrative Region shall be responsible for guiding and supervising the labor insurance work of the subordinate provincial and municipal trade union organizations and the industrial trade union organizations within their regions, reviewing the monthly income and expenditure statements, budgets, final accounts, business plans, and business reports of the labor insurance fund and adjustment fund of the provincial and municipal trade union organizations, and compiling labor insurance fund income and expenditure reports every three months, and compiling annual budgets, final accounts, business plans, and business reports to report to the Labor Department of the People's Government of the Greater Administrative Region, the Ministry of Labor of the Central People's Government, and the All-China Federation of Trade Unions.

Article 29 The All-China Federation of Trade Unions is the highest leading organ of the national labor insurance undertaking, coordinating the implementation of the national labor insurance undertaking, supervising the implementation of relevant labor insurance undertakings by the subordinate local trade union organizations and industrial trade union organizations; reviewing and compiling the income and expenditure reports of the labor insurance fund and the general fund, compiling the annual budgets, final accounts, business plans, and business reports of the labor insurance fund, and submitting them to the Ministry of Labor and the Ministry of Finance of the Central People's Government for record.

Article 30 The labor administrative organs of the people's governments at all levels shall supervise the payment of labor insurance funds, inspect the implementation of labor insurance business, and handle appeals related to labor insurance incidents.

Article 31 The Ministry of Labor of the Central People's Government is the highest supervisory organ of the national labor insurance business, responsible for implementing the implementation of the Labor Insurance Regulations, inspecting the implementation of the national labor insurance business, and its inspection system shall be formulated separately.

Chapter VII Supplementary Provisions

Article 32 These Regulations shall be implemented after being promulgated by the Government Administration Council of the Central People's Government, and the same shall apply when amended.

### 中华人民共和国劳动保险条例

1953-01-10
第2版()
专栏：

    中华人民共和国劳动保险条例
    一九五一年二月二十六日政务院公布
    一九五三年一月二日政务院修正公布
    第一章    总则
    第一条    为了保护工人职员的健康，减轻其生活中的困难，特依据目前经济条件，制定本条例。
    第二条    本条例的实施，采取逐步推广办法，目前的实施范围暂定如下：
    甲、有工人职员一百人以上的国营、公私合营、私营及合作社经营的工厂、矿场及其附属单位。
    乙、铁路、航运、邮电的各企业单位与附属单位。
    丙、工、矿、交通事业的基本建设单位。
    丁、国营建筑公司。关于本条例的实施范围继续推广办法由中央劳动部根据实际情况随时提出意见，报请中央人民政府政务院决定之。
    第三条    不实行本条例的企业及季节性的企业，其有关劳动保险事项，得由各该企业或其所属产业或行业的行政方面或资方与工会组织，根据本条例的原则及本企业、本产业或本行业的实际情况协商，订立集体合同规定之。
    第四条    凡在实行劳动保险的企业内工作的工人与职员（包括学徒），不分民族、年龄、性别、和国籍，均适用本条例，但被剥夺政治权利者除外。
    第五条    凡在实行劳动保险的企业内工作的临时工、季节工与试用人员，其劳动保险待遇在本条例实施细则中另行规定之。
    第六条    本条例适用范围内的企业，因经济特殊困难不易维持，或尚未正式开工营业者，经企业行政方面或资方与工会基层委员会双方协商同意，并报请当地人民政府劳动行政机关批准后，可暂缓实行本条例。
    第二章    劳动保险金的征集与保管
    第七条    本条例所规定之劳动保险的各项费用，全部由实行劳动保险的企业行政方面或资方负担，其中一部分由企业行政方面或资方直接支付，另一部分由企业行政方面或资方缴纳劳动保险金，交工会组织办理。
    第八条    凡根据本条例实行劳动保险的企业，其行政方面或资方须按月缴纳相当于各该企业全部工人与职员工资总额的百分之三，作为劳动保险金。此项劳动保险金，不得在工人与职员工资内扣除，并不得向工人与职员另行征收。
    第九条    劳动保险金的征集与保管方法如下：
    甲、企业行政方面或资方，须按照上月份工资总额计算，于每月一日至十日限期内，一次向中华全国总工会指定代收劳动保险金的国家银行缴纳每月应缴的劳动保险金。
    乙、在开始实行劳动保险的头两个月内，由企业行政方面或资方按月缴纳的劳动保险金，全数存于中华全国总工会户内，作为劳动保险总基金，为举办集体劳动保险事业之用。自开始实行的第三个月起，每月缴纳的劳动保险金，其中百分之三十，存于中华全国总工会户内，作为劳动保险总基金；百分之七十存于各该企业工会基层委员会户内，作为劳动保险基金，为支付工人与职员按照本条例应得的抚恤费、补助费与救济费之用。
    第十条    企业行政方面或资方逾期未缴或欠缴劳动保险金时，须每日增交滞纳金，其数额为未缴部分百分之一。如逾期二十日尚未缴纳，对于国营、地方国营、公私合营或合作社经营的企业，由工会基层委员会通知当地国家银行从其经费中扣缴；对于私营企业，由工会基层委员会报告当地人民政府劳动行政机关，对该企业资方追究责任。
    第十一条    劳动保险金的保管，由中华全国总工会委托中国人民银行代理之。
    第三章    各项劳动保险待遇的规定
    第十二条    因工负伤、残废待遇的规定：
    甲、工人与职员因工负伤，应在该企业医疗所、医院或特约医院医治。如该企业医疗所、医院或特约医院无法治疗时，应由该企业行政方面或资方转送其他医院医治。其全部诊疗费、药费、住院费、住院时的膳费与就医路费，均由企业行政方面或资方负担。在医疗期间，工资照发。
    乙、工人与职员因工负伤确定为残废时，按下列情况，由劳动保险基金项下按月付给因工残废抚恤费或因工残废补助费：
    一、完全丧失劳动力不能工作退职后，饮食起居需人扶助者，其因工残废抚恤费的数额为本人工资百分之七十五，付至死亡时止。
    二、完全丧失劳动力不能工作退职后，饮食起居不需人扶助者，其因工残废抚恤费的数额为本人工资百分之六十，付至恢复劳动力或死亡时止。劳动力恢复后应由企业行政方面或资方给予适当工作。
    三、部分丧失劳动力尚能工作者，应由企业行政方面或资方分配适当工作，并由劳动保险基金项下，按其残废后丧失劳动力的程度，付给因工残废补助费，其数额为残废前本人工资的百分之十至三十，但与残废后复工时的工资合计不得超过残废前本人工资。详细办法在实施细则中规定之。
    丙、工人与职员因工负伤而致残废者，其残废状况的确定与变更，由残废审查委员会审定。详细办法在实施细则中规定之。
    第十三条    疾病、非因工负伤、残废待遇的规定：
    甲、工人与职员疾病或非因工负伤，在该企业医疗所、医院、特约医院或特约中西医师处医治时，其所需诊疗费、手术费、住院费及普通药费均由企业行政方面或资方负担；贵重药费、住院的膳费及就医路费由本人负担，如本人经济状况确有困难，得由劳动保险基金项下酌予补助。患病及非因工负伤的工人职员，应否住院或转院医治及出院时间，应完全由医院决定之。
    乙、工人与职员因病或非因工负伤停止工作医疗时，其停止工作医疗期间连续在六个月以内者，按其本企业工龄的长短，由该企业行政方面或资方发给病伤假期工资，其数额为本人工资百分之六十至百分之一百；停止工作连续医疗期间在六个月以上时，改由劳动保险基金项下按月付给疾病或非因工负伤救济费，其数额为本人工资百分之四十至百分之六十，至能工作或确定为残废或死亡时止。详细办法在实施细则中规定之。
    丙、工人与职员因病或非因工负伤医疗终结确定为残废，完全丧失劳动力退职后，病伤假期工资或疾病非因工负伤救济费停发，改由劳动保险基金项下发给非因工残废救济费，其数额按下列情况规定之：饮食起居需人扶助者为本人工资百分之五十，饮食起居不需人扶助者为本人工资百分之四十，至恢复劳动力或死亡时止；部分丧失劳动力尚能工作者不予发给。关于残废状况的确定与变更，适用第十二条丙款的规定。
    丁、工人与职员疾病或非因工负伤痊愈或非因工残废恢复劳动力后，经负责医疗机关提出证明，该企业行政方面或资方应给予适当工作。
    戊、工人与职员供养的直系亲属患病时，得在该企业医疗所、医院、特约医院或特约中西医师处免费诊治，手术费及普通药费，由企业行政方面或资方负担二分之一，贵重药费、就医路费、住院费、住院时的膳费及其他一切费用，均由本人自理。
    第十四条    工人与职员及其供养的直系亲属死亡时待遇的规定：
    甲、工人与职员因工死亡时，由该企业行政方面或资方发给丧葬费，其数额为该企业全部工人与职员平均工资三个月。另由劳动保险基金项下，按其供养的直系亲属人数，每月付给供养直系亲属抚恤费，其数额为死者本人工资百分之二十五至百分之五十，至受供养者失去受供养的条件时为止。详细办法在实施细则中规定之。
    乙、工人与职员因病或非因工负伤死亡时，由劳动保险基金项下付给丧葬补助费，其数额为该企业全部工人与职员平均工资两个月。另由劳动保险基金项下，按其供养直系亲属人数，付给供养直系亲属救济费。其数额为死者本人工资六个月到十二个月。详细办法在实施细则中规定之。
    丙、工人与职员因工负伤致成残废完全丧失劳动力退职后死亡时，应按本条甲款的规定，付给丧葬费及供养直系亲属抚恤费。退职养老后死亡时或非因工残废完全丧失劳动力退职后死亡时，应按本条乙款的规定，付给丧葬补助费及供养直系亲属救济费。
    丁、工人与职员供养的直系亲属死亡时，由劳动保险基金项下付给供养直系亲属丧葬补助费：死者年龄在十周岁以上者，其数额为该企业全部工人与职员平均工资一个月的二分之一；一周岁至十周岁者，为平均工资一个月的三分之一。不满一周岁者不给。
    第十五条    养老待遇的规定：
    甲、男工人与男职员年满六十岁，一般工龄满二十五年，本企业工龄满五年者，可退职养老。退职后，由劳动保险基金项下，按其本企业工龄的长短，按月付给退职养老补助费，其数额为本人工资的百分之五十至七十，付至死亡时止。合于养老条件，但因该企业工作的需要，留其继续工作者，除发给原有工资外，应由劳动保险基金项下，按其本企业工龄的长短，每月付给在职养老补助费，其数额为本人工资百分之十至二十。详细办法在实施细则中规定之。
    乙、女工人与女职员年满五十岁，一般工龄满二十年，本企业工龄满五年者，得享受本条甲款规定的养老补助费待遇。
    丙、井下矿工或固定在华氏三十二度以下的低温工作场所或华氏一百度以上的高温工作场所工作者，男工人与男职员年满五十五岁，女工人与女职员年满四十五岁，均得享受本条甲款规定的养老补助费待遇。计算其一般工龄及本企业工龄时，每在此种场所工作一年，均作一年零三个月计算。
    丁、在提炼或制造铅、汞、砒、磷、酸的工业中及其他化学、兵工工业中，直接从事有害身体健康工作者，男工人与男职员年满五十五岁，女工人与女职员年满四十五岁，均得享受本条甲款规定的养老补助费待遇。计算其一般工龄及本企业工龄时，每从事此种工作一年，均作一年零六个月计算。
    第十六条    生育待遇的规定：
    甲、女工人与女职员生育，产前产后共给假五十六日，产假期间，工资照发。
    乙、女工人与女职员怀孕不满七个月小产时，得根据医师的意见给予三十日以内的产假，产假期间，工资照发。丙、女工人与女职员难产或双生时，增给假期十四日，工资照发。
    丁、女工人与女职员怀孕，在该企业医疗所、医院或特约医院检查或分娩时，其检查费与接生费由企业行政方面或资方负担，其他费用均按第十三条甲款的规定处理。
    戊、产假期满（不论正产或小产）仍不能工作者，经医院证明后，按第十三条关于疾病待遇的规定处理之。
    己、女工人与女职员或男工人与男职员之妻生育时，由劳动保险基金项下发给生育补助费四万元。
    第十七条    集体劳动保险事业的规定：
    甲、凡在实行劳动保险的企业内工作的工人与职员，均有享受集体劳动保险事业的权利。详细办法由中华全国总工会制定之。
    乙、各企业工会基层委员会得根据各该企业的经济情况及工人与职员的需要，与企业行政方面或资方共同办理疗养所、业余疗养所、托儿所等集体劳动保险事业。详细办法在实施细则中规定之。
    丙、中华全国总工会可举办或委托各地方或各产业工会组织举办下列各项集体劳动保险事业：
    一、疗养所；
    二、休养所；
    三、养老院；
    四、孤儿保育院；
    五、残废院；
    六、其他。
    第十八条    凡在实行劳动保险的企业内工作的工人与职员，未加入工会者，除因工负伤、残废、死亡待遇，生育待遇，因病或非因工负伤治疗待遇，供养直系亲属疾病治疗待遇，均得按本条例的规定享受外，其他各项，如疾病或非因工负伤医疗期间的工资与救济费，非因工残废救济费，供养直系亲属救济费，养老补助费，丧葬补助费，只能领取规定额的半数。
    第四章    享受优异劳动保险待遇的规定
    第十九条    凡对本企业有特殊贡献的劳动模范及转入本企业工作的战斗英雄，经工会基层委员会提出，并经各省、市工会组织或产业工会全国委员会的批准，得享受下列较优异的劳动保险待遇：
    甲、疾病或非因工负伤的贵重药费、就医路费、住院膳费，概由企业行政方面或资方负担。
    乙、疾病或非因工负伤医疗期间前六个月工资照发。疾病或非因工负伤救济费及非因工残废救济费，一律付给本人工资百分之六十。因工残废抚恤费为本人工资百分之一百。因工残废补助费为残废前本人工资与残废后复工时本人工资的差额。因工死亡供养直系亲属抚恤费为本人工资百分之三十至百分之六十。退职养老补助费为本人工资百分之六十至百分之八十。在职养老补助费为本人工资百分之二十至百分之三十。详细办法在实施细则中规定之。
    丙、有享受集体劳动保险事业的优先权。
    第二十条    残废军人转入本企业工作者，疾病或非因工负伤停止工作医疗期间，不计本企业工龄长短，前六个月工资照发，六个月以后，仍按第十三条乙款规定办理。
    第五章    劳动保险金的支配
    第二十一条    劳动保险金的支配办法如下：
    甲、劳动保险总基金由中华全国总工会用以举办集体劳动保险事业。
    乙、劳动保险基金由工会基层委员会用以支付各项抚恤费、补助费与救济费及本企业集体劳动保险事业的补助费。每月结算一次，其余额全部转入省、市工会组织或产业工会全国委员会户内，作为劳动保险调    剂金（以下简称调剂金）。
    丙、调剂金由省、市工会组织或产业工会全国委员会用于对所属各工会基层委员会劳动保险基金不足开支时的补助或举办集体劳动保险事业之用。各产业工会全国委员会得授权其地方机构，掌管调剂金的调用。中华全国总工会对所属各省、市工会组织、各产业工会全国委员会的调剂金，有统筹调用之权，并得用以举办集体劳动保险事业。如省、市工会组织或产业工会全国委员会调剂金不足开支，得申请中华全国总工会调拨调剂金补助之。
    第二十二条    劳动保险金，除用于劳动保险事业外，不得移作其他用途。
    第二十三条    各企业的会计部门，均须设立劳动保险基金的独立会计，负责办理劳动保险基金的收支事宜。劳动保险基金会计制度，由中央人民政府劳动部会同中华全国总工会制定之。
    第二十四条    劳动保险调剂金的收支事宜，由各级工会组织的财务部门根据中华全国总工会的规定办理之。
    第六章    劳动保险事业的执行与监督
    第二十五条    各工会基层委员会，为执行劳动保险业务的基层单位，其主要工作为：督促劳动保险金的缴纳；决定劳动保险基金的支付；监督本条例所规定由企业行政方面或资方直接支付的各项费用的开支；推动该企业改进集体劳动保险事业及医疗卫生工作；执行一切有关劳动保险的实际业务；每月编造劳动保险基金月报表，每年编造预算、决算、业务计划书及业务报告书，报告省、市工会组织，和产业工会全国委员会及当地人民政府劳动行政机关；并向工会全体会员大会或代表大会报告工作。
    第二十六条    各工会基层委员会的经费审查委员会，应按月审核劳动保险基金收支账目及本条例所规定的由企业行政方面或资方直接支付的各项费用，并公布之。
    第二十七条    各省、市工会组织，各产业工会全国委员会或地区委员会对所属各工会基层委员会的劳动保险业务，负指导督促之责，审核劳动保险基金的收支月报表、预算、决算及劳动保险基金的收支有无错误，接受工人与职员有关劳动保险事件的申诉，每月编造劳动保险基金及调剂金的收支月报表，每年编造预算、决算、业务计划书及业务报告书，并依下列程序报告：
    甲、各省、市工会组织向当地人民政府劳动行政机关及大行政区工会组织报告。
    乙、各产业工会全国委员会向中华全国总工会及中央人民政府劳动部报告。
    第二十八条    各大行政区工会组织对所属各省、市工会组织及其区域内产业工会组织的劳动保险工作，负指导督促之责，审核省、市工会组织劳动保险基金及调剂金的收支月报表、预算、决算、业务计划书及业务报告书，并每三个月编造劳动保险基金收支报告、每年编造预算、决算、业务计划书及业务报告书，报告所在地大行政区人民政府劳动部、中央人民政府劳动部及中华全国总工会。
    第二十九条    中华全国总工会为全国劳动保险事业的最高领导机关，统筹全国劳动保险事业的进行，督导所属各地方工会组织、各产业工会组织有关劳动保险事业的执行；审核并汇编劳动保险基金及总基金的收支报告表，每年编造劳动保险金的预算、决算、业务计划书及业务报告书，并送中央人民政府劳动部、财政部备查。
    第三十条    各级人民政府劳动行政机关应监督劳动保险金的缴纳，检查劳动保险业务的执行，并处理有关劳动保险事件的申诉。
    第三十一条    中央人民政府劳动部为全国劳动保险业务的最高监督机关，负责贯彻劳动保险条例的实施，检查全国劳动保险业务的执行，其检查制度另订之。
        第七章    附则
    第三十二条    本条例由中央人民政府政务院通过后公布施行，修改时同。
