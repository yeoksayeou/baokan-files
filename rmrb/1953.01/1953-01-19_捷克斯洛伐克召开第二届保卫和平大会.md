Title: Czechoslovakia Convenes Second Congress for the Defense of Peace
Author:
Date: 1953-01-19
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, Prague: The Second Congress for the Defense of Peace in Czechoslovakia opened in Prague on the 17th. The congress will last two days. 1,600 delegates are attending the congress, including advanced workers and agricultural workers, scientists, representatives from the arts, and housewives. Representatives from the World Peace Council and the peace movements of countries such as China, the Democratic People's Republic of Korea, Bulgaria, and Italy are also attending the congress.

    The opening ceremony of the congress was presided over by Anežka Hodinová-Spurná, Chairman of the Czechoslovak Committee for the Defense of Peace. Viliam Široký, Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Foreign Affairs of Czechoslovakia, delivered a congratulatory message on behalf of the Central Committee of the Communist Party of Czechoslovakia and the Czechoslovak government. Then, a congratulatory telegram from Soviet peace fighters was read at the meeting, and the venue resounded with stormy applause. Oldřich John, Secretary-General of the Central Action Committee of the National Front and Chairman of the National Assembly, then spoke, emphasizing the great contributions of the Soviet Union in the struggle to prevent war and safeguard world peace. Jean Laffargue, Secretary-General of the World Peace Council, after reading the congratulatory message from the World Peace Council, said that he believed that under the guidance of the resolutions of the World People's Congress for Peace in Vienna, Czechoslovak peace defenders would make new contributions to the struggle for world peace with their active efforts. Subsequently, Hodinová-Spurná made a report on the work of Czechoslovak peace fighters and future tasks.
    To celebrate the convening of the congress, hundreds of thousands of working people in cities throughout Czechoslovakia—miners, steelworkers, locomotive drivers, and machine tool makers—participated in special work shifts and exceeded their planned production by many tons of hardware, coal, and oil.
    Furthermore, on January 15th, the Chinese People's Committee for World Peace sent a telegram to the Czechoslovak Committee for the Defense of Peace, congratulating the congress on its complete success.

### 捷克斯洛伐克召开第二届保卫和平大会

1953-01-19
第4版()
专栏：

    捷克斯洛伐克召开第二届保卫和平大会
    【新华社十八日讯】据塔斯社布拉格讯：捷克斯洛伐克第二届保卫和平大会十七日在布拉格开幕。大会将开二天。出席大会的有一千六百名代表，包括先进工人和农业工作者、科学家、艺术界的代表和家庭主妇。出席大会的还有世界和平理事会和中国、朝鲜民主主义人民共和国、保加利亚和意大利等国和平运动的代表。
    大会由捷克斯洛伐克保卫和平委员会主席安涅日卡·荷第诺娃—斯普尔纳主持开幕。捷副总理兼外交部长威廉·西罗基代表捷克斯洛伐克共产党中央委员会和捷克斯洛伐克政府致贺词。接着在会上宣读了苏联和平战士的贺电，会场上响起了暴风雨般的掌声。捷民族阵线中央行动委员会总书记、国民议会主席奥德里赫·约翰继起发言，他强调指出了苏联在防止战争和维护世界和平的斗争中的伟大功绩。世界和平理事会总书记让·拉斐德在宣读了世界和平理事会的贺词后说：他相信捷克斯洛伐克和平保卫者在维也纳世界人民和平大会决议的指导下，会以他们的积极努力，对争取世界和平的斗争作出新的贡献。接着，荷第诺娃—斯普尔纳就捷克斯洛伐克和平战士的工作和今后的任务作了报告。
    为了庆祝大会的召开，捷克斯洛伐克各地城市中的几十万劳动人民——矿工、炼钢工人、机车司机、母机制造工人纷纷参加了特别工作班，并超过计划增产了许多吨的五金、煤炭和石油。
    又讯：一月十五日，我国人民保卫世界和平委员会曾致电捷克斯洛伐克保卫和平委员会，祝贺大会获得圆满成功。
