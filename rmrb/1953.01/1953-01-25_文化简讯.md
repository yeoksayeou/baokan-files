	Cultural Briefs
	Total Lunar Eclipse Visible in China on January 30th
		A total lunar eclipse will be visible in China on January 30th this year. The times of visibility (Beijing Standard Time) are as follows:
	Initial eclipse (lunar eclipse begins): 5:54 AM;
	Totality (total eclipse begins): 7:05 AM;
	Greatest eclipse (the moon is at the center of the Earth's shadow): 7:47 AM;
	Emergence (total eclipse ends): 8:30 AM;
	End of eclipse (lunar eclipse ends): 9:40 AM.

	Solar Eclipse Phenomenon on February 14th
		A solar eclipse will occur on February 14th this year (the first day of the first lunar month of the癸巳 year). Most regions of the country, except for Xinjiang and Tibet, will be able to see it. During this solar eclipse, only the penumbra of the moon will touch the Earth, so only a partial eclipse will be visible, and no location on Earth will be able to see a total eclipse.
		The Nanjing Purple Mountain Observatory has calculated the times (Beijing Standard Time) at which the solar eclipse will be visible in nine major cities in China:
	Beijing: 7:27 AM initial eclipse (when the sun begins to be obscured by the moon), 8:27 AM greatest eclipse (when the largest area of the sun is obscured), 9:31 AM end of eclipse.
	Shenyang: 7:35 AM initial eclipse, 8:37 AM greatest eclipse, 9:43 AM end of eclipse.
	Shanghai: 7:20 AM initial eclipse, 8:17 AM greatest eclipse, 9:19 AM end of eclipse.
	Nanjing: 7:19 AM initial eclipse, 8:16 AM greatest eclipse, 9:17 AM end of eclipse.
	Qingdao: 7:25 AM initial eclipse, 8:24 AM greatest eclipse, 9:25 AM end of eclipse.
	Hankou: 7:17 AM initial eclipse, 8:10 AM greatest eclipse, 9:09 AM end of eclipse.
	Guangzhou: 7:12 AM initial eclipse, 7:59 AM greatest eclipse, 8:49 AM end of eclipse.
	Xi'an: Initial eclipse before sunrise (not visible), 8:13 AM greatest eclipse, 9:11 AM end of eclipse.
	Chongqing: Initial eclipse before sunrise (not visible), 8:04 AM greatest eclipse, 8:58 AM end of eclipse.
		The visibility of the solar eclipse in China varies from place to place and can be roughly divided into three regions:
	(1) The entire Northeast, Inner Mongolia, and East China, as well as most of North and Central-South China, will be able to see the entire solar eclipse process.
	(2) The western parts of North and Central-South China, and parts of the Southwest and Northwest, will only be able to see the period from after the initial eclipse to the greatest eclipse and the end of the eclipse because the initial eclipse occurs before sunrise.
	(3) Most areas of Ningxia, Gansu, western Yunnan, Qinghai, and Xikang will only be able to see the period from after the greatest eclipse to the end of the eclipse because sunrise occurs after the initial and greatest eclipses.

### 文化简讯

1953-01-25

专栏：文化简讯

    文化简讯
    一月三十日我国将可见月全食
    今年一月三十日我国将可见月全食。见食时间
    （北京标准时间）如下：初亏（月食开始）：五时五十四分；食既（全食开始）：七时五分；食甚（月亮走在地球影子的中心时）：七时四十七分；生光
    （全食终了）：八时三十分；复圆（月食终了）：九时四十分。
    二月十四日将有日食现象
    今年二月十四日（农历癸巳年正月初一）将有日食现象。我国除新疆、西藏以外，全国大部分地区都能看到。此次日食，只有月亮的半影和地球接触，所以仅能看到偏食，地球上没有地方可看到全食。
    南京紫金山天文台已推算了我国九大城市看到日食的时间（北京标准时间）：北京七时二十七分初亏（太阳开始被月亮遮住的时候），八时二十七分食甚（太阳被遮住的面积最大的时候），九时三十一分复圆。沈阳七时三十五分初亏，八时三十七分食甚，九时四十三分复圆。上海七时二十分初亏，八时十七分食甚，九时十九分复圆。南京七时十九分初亏，八时十六分食甚，九时十七分复圆。青岛七时二十五分初亏，八时二十四分食甚，九时二十五分复圆。汉口七时十七分初亏，八时十分食甚，九时九分复圆。广州七时十二分初亏，七时五十九分食甚，八时四十九分复圆。西安日出前初亏
    （看不到），八时十三分食甚，九时十一分复圆。重庆日出前初亏（看不到），八时零四分食甚，八时五十八分复圆。
    我国看到日食情况，各地不同，大概可分三个地区：（一）东北、内蒙、华东全部及华北、中南的大部分地区，可见全部的日食过程。（二）华北、中南的西部和西南、西北的一部分地区，因为初亏在日出以前，所以只能见到初亏后至食甚和复圆的一段。（三）宁夏、甘肃、云南的西部和青海、西康的大部分地区，日出在初亏、食甚以后，因此只能看到食甚后至复圆的一段。
