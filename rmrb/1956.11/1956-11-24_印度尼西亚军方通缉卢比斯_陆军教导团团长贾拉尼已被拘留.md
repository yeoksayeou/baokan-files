Title: Indonesian Military Issues Warrant for Lubis; Commander of Army Training Corps Djalani Detained

Author:

Date: 1956-11-24
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, Jakarta, 23rd - Sumitro Kolopaking, a member of the Indonesian Parliament's Defense Subcommittee, said on November 22nd that the Indonesian Army authorities had issued a warrant for the arrest of former Deputy Army Chief of Staff Lubis, whose whereabouts are currently unknown.

According to Antara News Agency, Kolopaking made the announcement after the Defense Subcommittee held a meeting to hear Acting Defense Minister Ali Sastroamidjojo explain the recent situation of the Army and the measures taken by the Army's leadership.

After the meeting, the Army Chief of Staff confirmed that Major Djalani, commander of the Army Training Corps, had been detained. He declined to answer questions about rumors that Colonel Simbolon, commander of the First Military District, had been dismissed. Regarding Major Djuro, commander of the Jakarta Garrison, the Army Chief of Staff said that he had been given "extended leave."

### 印度尼西亚军方通缉卢比斯  陆军教导团团长贾拉尼已被拘留

1956-11-24
第6版()
专栏：

    印度尼西亚军方通缉卢比斯
    陆军教导团团长贾拉尼已被拘留
    据新华社雅加达23日电　印度尼西亚国会国防小组委员会委员苏米特罗·柯洛帕金11月22日说，印度尼西亚陆军当局已经下令通缉至今行踪不明的前陆军副参谋长卢比斯。
    据安塔拉通讯社报道，柯洛帕金是在国防小组委员会举行会议听取代理国防部长阿里·沙斯特罗阿米佐约说明陆军最近情况和陆军领导机构的措施之后，发表这个消息的。
    会后陆军参谋长证实已经拘留了陆军教导团团长贾拉尼少校。关于传闻第一军区司令西姆坡伦上校被免职的问题，他不愿作答。关于雅加达卫戍司令朱罗少校，陆军参谋长说，已经给他“度长假”。
