Title: Egyptian Embassy in Jordan Spokesperson Warns Britain, France, and Israel to Immediately Withdraw Troops; Hammarskjöld Discusses UN Force Mission with Egyptian Foreign Minister
Author:
Date: 1956-11-19
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, 18th - Amman News: The spokesperson for the Egyptian Embassy in Jordan said on November 17th that if British, French, and Israeli forces refuse or delay withdrawing from the Suez Canal Zone, the Sinai Peninsula, and the Gaza Strip, Egypt will rise up to fight again.

Xinhua News Agency, 18th - Cairo News: United Nations Secretary-General Dag Hammarskjöld held another three-hour meeting with Egyptian Foreign Minister Fawzi on November 17th. Sources say the purpose of the meeting was to enable the United Nations police force to take on the work of achieving peace in the Middle East as soon as possible.

The newspaper "Al Gomhuria" said on November 17th: Egypt "insists that the mission of the international force should be limited to supervising the withdrawal of the invading forces."

The weekly "Akher Sa'a" emphasized: The United Nations "international police force has nothing to do with the Suez Canal issue." It said that regarding the Suez Canal issue, the United Nations Security Council has already passed a resolution requiring the parties concerned to consult and resolve the issue in accordance with the six principles adopted by the Security Council. It said that when Egypt believes that the mission of the United Nations police force has been completed, Egypt will immediately ask it to return.

On November 17th, another group of United Nations police forces arrived in Egypt, including forty-four Yugoslav soldiers, and the rest were Danish and Norwegian soldiers. As of November 17th, the number of international police forces arriving in Egypt is nearly four hundred.

### 埃及驻约旦大使馆发言人  警告英法以必须立即撤军  哈马舍尔德同埃外长讨论联合国部队任务问题

1956-11-19
第6版()
专栏：

    埃及驻约旦大使馆发言人
    警告英法以必须立即撤军
    哈马舍尔德同埃外长讨论联合国部队任务问题
    新华社18日讯　安曼消息：埃及驻约旦大使馆发言人在11月17日说，如果英、法和以色列部队拒绝或者拖延从苏伊士运河区、西奈半岛和加沙地带撤退，埃及将重新起来战斗。
    据新华社18日讯　开罗消息：联合国秘书长哈马舍尔德11月17日同埃及外交部长法齐又举行了三小时的会谈。有消息说，会谈的目的在于使联合国警察部队尽快地担当起在中东实现和平的工作。
    “共和国报”在11月17日说：埃及“坚持国际部队的任务应该限于监督侵略部队的撤出”。
    “今日消息”周刊强调指出：联合国“国际警察部队同苏伊士运河问题丝毫没有关系”。它说，关于苏伊士运河问题，联合国安全理事会已经通过决议，要求有关各方根据安全理事会通过的六项原则来协商解决。它说，等到埃及认为联合国警察部队的任务已经完成，埃及将立即请它回去。
    11月17日又有一批联合国警察部队到达埃及，其中有四十四个南斯拉夫士兵，其余的是丹麦和挪威的士兵。到11月17日为止，到达埃及的国际警察部队的人数将近四百人。
