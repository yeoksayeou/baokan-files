Title: Tibetan Sakya Proverbs
Author: Wang Yao
Date: 1956-11-10
Page: 7

    Tibetan Sakya Proverbs
    An upright person, even when faced with life's dangers,
    Will not abandon their inherent nature;
    Natural gold, even after being burned and smelted,
    Will never change its original color.
    Cowardly people, no matter how many gather,
    Cannot accomplish great undertakings;
    Zhejiang grass, no matter how much is bundled together, [Note]
    Cannot serve as the pillars of a mansion.
    Note: Zhejiang grass: name of a grass
    Translated by Wang Yao

### 西藏萨迦格言
王尧选
1956-11-10
第7版()
专栏：

    西藏萨迦格言
    正直的人虽然碰到生命的危难，
    也不会捐弃自己的本性；
    天然黄金虽然经过烧熔冶炼，
    怎么也不会改变它本来的颜色。
    胆小的人不管聚集了多少，
    也不能完成伟大的事业；
    泽江草不管捆了许多，〔注〕
    也不能充当大厦的栋梁。
    注：泽江草：草名
    王尧选译
