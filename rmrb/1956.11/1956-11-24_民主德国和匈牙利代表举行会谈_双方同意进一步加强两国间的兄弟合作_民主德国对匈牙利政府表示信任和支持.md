Title: East German and Hungarian Representatives Hold Talks; Both Sides Agree to Further Strengthen Brotherly Cooperation Between the Two Countries; East Germany Expresses Trust and Support for the Hungarian Government
Author:
Date: 1956-11-24
Page: 5
Xinhua News Agency, Berlin, November 23rd: According to a TASS report from Berlin on November 22nd: The German News Agency reports that a delegation from the German Democratic Republic held talks with representatives of the Hungarian Revolutionary Worker-Peasant Government and the Hungarian Socialist Workers' Party in Budapest on November 20th.

	During the talks, the two sides discussed the situation in Hungary. The participants unanimously agreed that the defeat of the counter-revolution and the failure of the fascist reactionary forces' attempt to seize power was beneficial to the Hungarian people and helped to safeguard world peace.

	The German side expressed confidence in the Hungarian Revolutionary Worker-Peasant Government, which realistically assesses the current situation in Hungary, defends the country's independence and autonomy, safeguards the political and social interests of the Hungarian working people, and strengthens the people's democratic system.

	The German side emphasized that it will fully support the Hungarian Revolutionary Worker-Peasant Government in building socialism in alliance with all socialist countries.

	The participants reached a consensus on the need to further consolidate brotherly cooperation between the two countries in all aspects of political, economic, and cultural life.

	The talks emphasized that the fraternal relationship between the Socialist Unity Party of Germany and the Hungarian Socialist Workers' Party, the two workers' parties, is the foundation for further deepening and expanding the friendly ties between the two countries.

	Participating in the talks on the German side were Hilde Benjamin and Ebert, members of the Political Bureau of the Central Committee of the Socialist Unity Party of Germany, Gelle, First Deputy Chairman of the State Planning Commission, Schwab, Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs, and others. On the Hungarian side were Prime Minister Kadar, President of the Presidential Council of the Republic Dobi, Kish, member of the Political Bureau of the Central Committee of the Hungarian Socialist Workers' Party, Ministers Opro, Marosan, and Ronai, and others.

### 民主德国和匈牙利代表举行会谈  双方同意进一步加强两国间的兄弟合作  民主德国对匈牙利政府表示信任和支持

1956-11-24
第5版()
专栏：

    民主德国和匈牙利代表举行会谈
    双方同意进一步加强两国间的兄弟合作
    民主德国对匈牙利政府表示信任和支持
    新华社23日讯　据塔斯社柏林22日讯：据德意志通讯社报道，德意志民主共和国代表团同匈牙利工农革命政府和匈牙利社会主义工人党的代表11月20日在布达佩斯举行了会谈。
    双方在会谈的过程中讨论了匈牙利局势的问题。会谈参加者一致认为，反革命的被击溃和法西斯反动势力攫夺政权的企图遭到失败是对匈牙利人民有利的，而且有助于保障世界和平。
    德国方面对匈牙利工农革命政府表示信任，这个政府以现实的态度估计匈牙利目前的局势，它保卫着国家的独立和自主，维护着匈牙利劳动人民的政治和社会利益，并且加强着人民民主制度。
    德国方面着重指出，它将全力支持同所有社会主义国家结成联盟的建设社会主义的匈牙利工农革命政府。
    会谈参加者就必须进一步巩固两国在政治、经济和文化生活各方面的兄弟合作的问题取得了一致的意见。
    会谈中着重指出，德国统一社会党和匈牙利社会主义工人党这两个工人政党之间的兄弟关系是进一步加深和扩大两国之间的友好联系的基础。
    参加这次会谈的，德国方面有德国统一社会党中央委员会政治局委员希德万和艾伯特、国家计划委员会第一副主席格累郭尔、外交部副部长施瓦布等人；匈牙利方面有总理卡达尔、共和国主席团主席道比、匈牙利社会主义工人党中央委员会政治局委员基什、各部部长奥普罗、马罗山和罗奈伊等人。
