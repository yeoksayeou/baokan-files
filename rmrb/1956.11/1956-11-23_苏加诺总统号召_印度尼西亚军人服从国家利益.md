Title: President Sukarno Calls on Indonesian Military to Obey National Interests
Author:
Date: 1956-11-23
Page: 6
Xinhua News Agency, Jakarta, November 22nd. Indonesian President Sukarno said on November 21st, "We must live and die with our founding ideology and the spirit of the August 17, 1945, Declaration of Independence."

According to the Indonesian News Agency, President Sukarno told a gathering of officers at the Army Staff School in Bandung: "No other ideas besides this ideology should be allowed in the armed forces." Sukarno said that "new ideas" that were not the ideals of the 1945 Declaration had recently emerged in the armed forces. He said, "This must be suppressed."

He emphasized: "Discipline is a prerequisite for a strong army. The idea that being a soldier or officer exempts one from one's responsibilities as a citizen is wrong. Soldiers and officers, like other citizens, should be patriots."

"Everything must be subordinate to the national interest."

### 苏加诺总统号召  印度尼西亚军人服从国家利益

1956-11-23
第6版()
专栏：

    苏加诺总统号召
    印度尼西亚军人服从国家利益
    新华社雅加达22日电　印度尼西亚总统苏加诺11月21日说，“我们必须同我们的立国思想和1945年8月17日独立宣言的精神共存亡”。
    据印度尼西亚新闻社报道，苏加诺总统在万隆对陆军参谋学校的一次军官集会说：“武装部队中除了这种思想外决不许有任何其他思想。”苏加诺说，最近武装部队中出现了并不是1945年宣言的理想的“新想法”。他说，“这是必须加以压制的”。
    他强调指出：“军纪是一支强大的军队的先决条件。认为当了士兵或军官就可以免除作为一个公民的责任的想法是错误的。士兵和军官同其他公民一样，都应该成为爱国者。”
    “任何事情都必须服从国家利益。”
