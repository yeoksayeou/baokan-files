Title: Hungarian Government Requests Assistance from Socialist Countries; Soviet Union Decides to Donate Large Quantities of Food and Building Materials
Author:
Date: 1956-11-07
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, Budapest, 6th November, dispatch: According to TASS, on November 5th, the Hungarian Revolutionary Worker-Peasant Government issued a letter to the governments of fraternal socialist countries, requesting fraternal assistance to the Hungarian people. The full text is as follows:

To the governments of all fraternal socialist countries, Comrades!

Our motherland and our people have experienced very serious events. We are now launching the final blow against the counter-revolutionary forces attempting to eliminate our people's democratic system and our socialist achievements. Our primary task now is to restore normal peaceful life and heal the wounds. In order to accomplish this task as quickly as possible, we request that you provide us with fraternal assistance. We are currently in particular need of food, fuel, building materials and medicine.

We firmly believe that all fraternal countries and people fighting for a common ideal, for socialism, will certainly fulfill our request.

Kádár János, Prime Minister of the Hungarian Revolutionary Worker-Peasant Government

November 5, 1956

Budapest

Xinhua News Agency, Moscow, 5th November, dispatch: According to TASS, Soviet Chairman of the Council of Ministers, Bulganin, replied to János Kádár, Prime Minister of the Hungarian Revolutionary Worker-Peasant Government, deciding to provide the Hungarian working people with free fraternal assistance. The full text is as follows:

To Comrade János Kádár, esteemed Prime Minister of the Hungarian Revolutionary Worker-Peasant Government:

The Soviet government has considered your request and has decided to provide the Hungarian working people with free fraternal assistance, and to quickly transport the following materials to Hungary:

	Grain and flour: 50,000 tons
	Meat: 6,000 tons
	Butter: 2,000 tons
	Canned milk: 3 million cans
	Sugar: 5,000 tons
	Cement: 10,000 tons
	Timber: 10,000 cubic meters
	Building logs: 5,000 cubic meters
	Window glass: 300,000 square meters
	Roofing sheet iron: 1,000 tons
	Section steel: 3,000 tons

In addition, in order to ensure that Hungarian industry and other national economic sectors do not shut down, the Soviet government has instructed the Ministry of Foreign Trade to provide the People's Republic of Hungary with the materials, raw materials, fuels and other commodities stipulated in the 1956 agreement, as well as the commodities scheduled for the first quarter of 1957 under the 1957 trade agreement, in advance.

At the same time, the Soviet Ministry of Health has been instructed to urgently and freely transport medicines needed by the residents to the People's Republic of Hungary.

With comradely greetings.

	N. Bulganin
	Chairman of the Council of Ministers of the Soviet Union

	November 5, 1956, Moscow
	Kremlin

### 匈政府请求社会主义国家给予援助  苏联决定赠送大批食物和建筑材料

1956-11-07
第5版()
专栏：

    匈政府请求社会主义国家给予援助
    苏联决定赠送大批食物和建筑材料
    新华社6日讯　据塔斯社布达佩斯讯：11月5日，匈牙利工农革命政府发表了给各兄弟社会主义国家政府的信，请求给予匈牙利人民以兄弟援助，全文如下：致各兄弟社会主义国家政府同志们！
    我们的祖国和我们的人民经历了非常严重的事件。现在，我们正在对企图消灭我们人民民主制度和我们的社会主义成果的反革命势力进行最后打击。我们当前的主要任务是恢复正常和平生活和医治创伤。为了最快地完成这项任务，我们请求你们给予我们兄弟援助。现在我们特别需要粮食、燃料、建筑材料和医药。
    我们坚信，为共同理想，为社会主义而斗争的所有兄弟国家和人民一定会满足我们的请求。匈牙利工农革命政府总理卡达尔
            1956年11月5日
                  于布达佩斯
    新华社6日讯　据塔斯社莫斯科5日讯：苏联部长会议主席布尔加宁电复匈牙利工农革命政府总理卡达尔，决定给予匈牙利劳动人民以无偿的兄弟援助。全文如下：
    致匈牙利工农革命政府总理亚诺什·卡达尔敬爱的总理同志：
    苏联政府考虑了您的请求，兹决定给予匈牙利劳动人民以无偿的兄弟援助，并且迅速运往匈牙利下列物资：
    谷物和面粉　五万吨
    肉类　六千吨
    牛油　二千吨
    奶类罐头　三百万厅
    糖　五千吨
    水泥　一万吨
    木材　一万立方公尺
    建筑圆木　五千立方公尺
    窗玻璃　三十万平方公尺
    屋面铁皮　一千吨
    型钢　三千吨
    此外，为了保证匈牙利工业和其他国民经济部门不致停工，苏联政府已经责成对外贸易部，把1956年协定中所规定的物资、原料、燃料和其他商品和1957年贸易协定中所规定的1957年第一季度所应该提供的商品，提前向匈牙利人民共和国提供。
    同时，已经责成苏联卫生部火急无偿地向匈牙利人民共和国运出居民需要的药品。
    此致同志敬礼
            苏联部长会议主席
                  尼·布尔加宁
      1956年11月5日于莫斯科
                    克里姆林宫
