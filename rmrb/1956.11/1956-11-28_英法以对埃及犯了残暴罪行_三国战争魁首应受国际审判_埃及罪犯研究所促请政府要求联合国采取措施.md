Title: Britain, France, and Israel Committed Brutal Crimes Against Egypt; Leaders of the Tripartite Aggression Should Face International Trial; Egyptian Institute of Criminology Urges Government to Request UN Action

Author:
Date: 1956-11-28
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, November 27th, Cairo Dispatch: The Egyptian Institute of Criminology has submitted a memorandum to President Nasser urging Egypt to seek a UN resolution authorizing the trial of "British, French, and Israeli war criminals."

The memorandum, submitted on November 23rd, stated that Britain, France, and Israel had committed crimes of unimaginable brutality against Egypt, and therefore the leaders of Britain, France, and Israel should be tried.

It said that on August 19, 1945, nineteen countries signed an agreement on the establishment of an international tribunal to try war criminals of World War II. This tribunal, later known as the Nuremberg Tribunal, was given the right to convict even in the absence of the accused. On January 19, 1946, a similar tribunal was established in the Far East.

The memorandum said that in 1947, the United Nations General Assembly ordered the establishment of a committee to formulate the principles upon which the Nuremberg Tribunal was based. It is according to these principles that the leaders of Britain, France, and Israel should be tried.

### 英法以对埃及犯了残暴罪行  三国战争魁首应受国际审判  埃及罪犯研究所促请政府要求联合国采取措施

1956-11-28
第6版()
专栏：

    英法以对埃及犯了残暴罪行  三国战争魁首应受国际审判
    埃及罪犯研究所促请政府要求联合国采取措施
    新华社27日讯　开罗消息：埃及罪犯研究所向纳赛尔总统呈交了一项备忘录，敦促埃及设法使联合国通过授权审判“英、法、以色列战争罪犯”的决议。
    这份在11月23日呈交的备忘录说，英、法、以色列对埃及犯下了想像不出更加残暴的罪行，所以应当审判英法和以色列的领袖。
    它说，1945年8月19日，十九个国家曾经签署了关于建立审判第二次世界大战战争罪犯的国际法庭的协定。这个法庭后来被称为纽伦堡法庭，它被给予即使罪犯缺席也可以判罪的权利。1946年1月19日，在远东成立了同样的法庭。
    备忘录说，1947年，联合国大会命令成立一个委员会来拟定纽伦堡审判所曾依据的原则。正是根据这些原则，英法和以色列的领袖应当受审判。
