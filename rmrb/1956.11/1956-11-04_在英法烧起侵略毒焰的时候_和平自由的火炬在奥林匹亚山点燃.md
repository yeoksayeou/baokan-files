Title: While England and France Ignited Flames of Aggression, the Torch of Peace and Freedom Was Lit on Mount Olympia
Author:
Date: 1956-11-04
Page: 5

    While England and France Ignited Flames of Aggression,
    the Torch of Peace and Freedom Was Lit on Mount Olympia
    Xinhua News Agency, November 3rd, Athens Dispatch: The Olympic torch was lit on November 2nd, according to traditional custom, at Mount Olympia in Greece, the birthplace of the Olympic Games.
    This torch will travel over ten thousand miles to Melbourne, Australia, making it the longest Olympic torch relay in history. Due to the war ignited by England and France in Egypt, the plane carrying the torch will have to detour, delaying the arrival of this torch, symbolizing peace and freedom, in Australia by almost a day.
    At the same time, in Melbourne, people are discussing whether the war in Egypt will affect the holding of this year's Olympic Games, but International Olympic Committee President Brundage said that this year's Olympic Games will be held as scheduled.

### 在英法烧起侵略毒焰的时候  和平自由的火炬在奥林匹亚山点燃

1956-11-04
第5版()
专栏：

    在英法烧起侵略毒焰的时候
    和平自由的火炬在奥林匹亚山点燃
    新华社3日讯　雅典消息：奥林匹克火炬已经在11月2日按照传统习惯在奥林匹克运动会发源地的希腊的奥林匹亚山点燃。
    这个火炬将经过一万多英里的路程到达澳大利亚的墨尔本，这是奥林匹克火炬传送历史上路程最长的一次。由于英法在埃及挑起的战火，载运火炬的飞机将不得不绕道而使这个象征着和平和自由的火炬延迟几乎一天到达澳大利亚。
    与此同时，在墨尔本人们议论纷纷，担心埃及的战火会影响今年奥林匹克运动会的举行，但是国际奥林匹克委员会主席布伦戴奇说今年的奥林匹克运动会将会如期举行。
