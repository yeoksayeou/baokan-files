Title: Nagy and Others Leave the Yugoslav Embassy
Author: 
Date: 1956-11-24
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, Budapest, 23rd -- When the Hungarian Worker-Peasant Revolutionary Government, headed by Kádár, was established and, with the assistance of Soviet troops, crushed the counter-revolutionary forces attempting to restore the old order, former Hungarian Chairman of the Council of Ministers, Imre Nagy, and others fled to the Yugoslav Embassy in Hungary on November 4th.

On November 18th, Zoltán Szántó, former member of the Preparatory Committee of the Hungarian Socialist Workers' Party, and György Lukács, Zoltán Vas, and three others from this group left the Yugoslav Embassy first.

After this, on the afternoon of November 22nd, Nagy and others left the Yugoslav Embassy and returned to their homes.

It is reported that Cardinal Mindszenty, who was sentenced to treason by the Hungarian People's Government, fled to the U.S. Legation in Hungary after November 4th. He was released by counter-revolutionaries during the armed riots.

### 纳吉等一批人离开南斯拉夫大使馆

1956-11-24
第5版()
专栏：

    纳吉等一批人离开南斯拉夫大使馆
    新华社布达佩斯23日电　当以卡达尔为首的匈牙利工农革命政府成立，并在苏联军队协助下摧毁企图复辟的反革命势力的时候，前匈牙利部长会议主席伊姆雷·纳吉和其他一些人在11月4日跑到南斯拉夫驻匈牙利大使馆里去。
    11月18日，这一批人中的前匈牙利社会主义工人党筹备委员会委员佐尔坦·桑托以及捷尔吉·卢卡奇、佐尔坦·瓦什等三人先离开了南斯拉夫大使馆。
    在这之后，11月22日下午，纳吉等人，从南斯拉夫大使馆出来，回到他们的家里。
    据悉，曾被匈牙利人民政权判决为叛国罪的红衣主教明曾蒂，在11月4日以后就逃到美国驻匈牙利的公使馆里。他是在武装骚乱期间被反革命分子释放出来的。
