Title: Spanish Anti-Fascists Detained on Corsica Protest French Government's Preparations to Shelter Hungarian Counter-Revolutionaries
Author:
Date: 1956-11-16
Page: 5
Xinhua News Agency, Moscow, November 15th, TASS, Paris, November 14th: A group of Spanish Republicans detained under police surveillance on Corsica collectively wrote a letter to Daniel Mayer, Chairman of the Foreign Affairs Committee of the French National Assembly. The letter stated that Mayer's remarks at the National Assembly meeting on November 6th regarding the French government's preparation to "fraternally" receive Hungarian rioters aroused the indignation of the Spanish anti-fascists. The people who collectively wrote this letter, recalling their participation in the battle for the freedom of France, wrote: "You consider us unwelcome people, persecute us, detain us, and place us under police surveillance, while the Hungarian rioters, welcomed by the bloody executioner Franco, who massacred the Spanish people, are regarded by you as fighters for freedom..." They said, "Should Franco's friends enjoy the right of asylum that the Paris Commune so generously granted?"

### 拘留在科西嘉岛的西班牙反法西斯主义者  抗议法政府准备收容匈反革命分子

1956-11-16
第5版()
专栏：

    拘留在科西嘉岛的西班牙反法西斯主义者
    抗议法政府准备收容匈反革命分子
    新华社15日讯　塔斯社巴黎14日讯：在警察监视下被拘留在科西嘉岛上的一批西班牙共和主义者集体写了一封信给法国国民议会外交委员会主席丹尼埃·麦耶。信中说，麦耶11月6日在国民议会会议上说的关于法国政府准备“兄弟般”接待匈牙利暴乱分子的话引起了西班牙反法西斯主义者的愤慨。集体写这封信的人在回忆起参加争取法国自由的战斗时写道：“你们认为我们是不受欢迎的人，迫害我们，拘留和用警察监视我们，而屠杀西班牙人民的血腥刽子手佛朗哥所欢迎的匈牙利暴乱分子却被你们奉为争取自由的战士……”他们说，“难道应该让佛朗哥的朋友享有巴黎公社国家慷慨给予的避难权吗？”
