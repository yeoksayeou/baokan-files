Title: French Judicial Authorities Fabricate Excuses to Persecute "l'Humanité" Director and Journalist
Author:
Date: 1956-11-17
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, November 15th: According to Agence France-Presse in Paris, the French judicial authorities, on the 12th, under the pretext of so-called defamation of the police, harmed Kilman Bellevier, the director of the French Communist Party's newspaper "l'Humanité," and journalist Roger Vailland. Both were fined 60,000 francs each.

The report said: Vailland published an article in "l'Humanité" on July 6, 1955, stating that the Republican police beating striking workers was an act of "aggression."

### 法司法当局制造借口  迫害“人道报”社长和记者

1956-11-17
第6版()
专栏：

    法司法当局制造借口
    迫害“人道报”社长和记者
    新华社15日讯　据法新社巴黎消息：法国司法当局12日以所谓诽谤警察的罪名加害法国共产党机关报“人道报”社长基尔曼·贝利西埃和记者罗歇·瓦伊扬。两人各被判处罚款六万法郎。
    消息说：瓦伊扬在1955年7月6日的“人道报”上发表过一篇文章，说共和国警察殴打罢工工人是一种“侵犯”行为。
