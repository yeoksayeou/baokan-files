Title: UN General Assembly Resumes General Debate; Polish Foreign Minister Opposes UN Interference in Hungary's Internal Affairs
Author:
Date: 1956-11-29
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, New York, November 28th: The United Nations General Assembly resumed its general debate on the afternoon of the 27th.

The first speaker, Panamanian Foreign Minister Boyd, said that the Suez dispute was of particular concern to Panama. "The Republic of Panama's sovereignty over the Panama Canal is a real thing."

He also said that Egypt enjoys sovereignty over the Suez Canal.

Speaking about the situation in Hungary, Boyd repeated the slander of so-called "genocide" deliberately spread by Western countries, and demanded that the United Nations not be "indifferent" to the situation in Hungary.

Norwegian Foreign Minister Lange believed that restoring the 1949 ceasefire agreement was "not a realistic solution to the Middle East issue." He demanded that Israel and Arab countries "make mutual concessions" to guarantee peace.

When talking about the situation in Hungary, Lange attempted to incite the General Assembly to once again interfere in Hungary's internal affairs.

Polish Deputy Foreign Minister Marian Naszkowski said that the most important task of the United Nations is to satisfy "Egypt's legitimate and just demands." Naszkowski said, "The current international situation, especially the lack of any unanimously agreed solution sufficient to fully guarantee the prevention of the revival of German militarism, is the reason why we believe that a certain number of Soviet troops temporarily stationed in Poland under an agreement signed by our two countries is still appropriate."

He told the General Assembly that the United Nations should not interfere in Hungary's internal affairs in such a way as to make the task of the Hungarian people more difficult.

Cuban representative Emilio Núñez-Portuondo demanded that Secretary-General Hammarskjöld immediately report whether Hungary would allow so-called "United Nations observers" to go to Hungary. He attempted to use this method to stir up new clamor in the United Nations General Assembly against the Hungarian people and the Hungarian Workers' and Peasants' Revolutionary Government.

### 联合国大会恢复一般性辩论  波兰外长反对联合国干涉匈牙利内政

1956-11-29
第6版()
专栏：

    联合国大会恢复一般性辩论
    波兰外长反对联合国干涉匈牙利内政
    据新华社28日讯　纽约消息：联合国大会27日下午恢复一般性辩论。
    巴拿马外交部长波伊德第一个发言说，苏伊士争端是巴拿马所特别关心的。“巴拿马共和国对巴拿马运河的主权是一件真实的事情”。
    他也说，埃及对苏伊士运河是享有主权的。
    在谈到匈牙利局势的时候，波伊德重复西方国家别有用心地散布的所谓“消灭种族”的谰言，他要求联合国不要对匈牙利的局势“无动于衷”。
    挪威外交大臣兰格认为恢复1949年的停火协定“不是中东问题的现实的解决办法”。他要求以色列和阿拉伯国家“互相让步”来保证和平。
    在谈到匈牙利局势时，兰格企图煽动大会再一次干涉匈牙利内政。
    波兰外交部副部长马里安·纳希科夫斯基说，联合国最重要的任务是满足“埃及合法而正当的要求”。纳希科夫斯基说，“目前的国际局势，特别是缺乏足以充分保证防止德国军国主义复活的任何一致同意的解决办法的局面，是我们认为一定数量的苏联军队根据我们两国所签订的一项协定暂时驻在波兰仍然是得当的原因。”
    他对大会说，联合国不应该干涉匈牙利内政，以致使匈牙利人民的任务更加困难。
    古巴代表埃米略·努奈斯—波图翁多要求秘书长哈马舍尔德立即报告匈牙利允不允许所谓“联合国观察员”前去匈牙利，他企图用这种办法来在联合国大会上煽动新的反对匈牙利人民和匈牙利工农革命政府的喧嚣。
