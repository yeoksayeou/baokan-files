Title: Overseas Chinese in Britain Donate to Console Victims of Kowloon Riot

Author: 
Date: 1956-11-16
Page: 4

    Xinhua News Agency, Hong Kong: Ninety-five Chinese workers residing in Liverpool, Britain, recently donated funds to express sympathy to the families of deceased workers and injured workers in the Kowloon riot incident. On November 5th, they remitted 108 pounds and 10 shillings to the Hong Kong Ta Kung Pao, entrusting the newspaper to forward the money to the Hong Kong and Kowloon Federation of Trade Unions.

    In their letter attached to the remittance, they said: "We are deeply shocked and angered by the inhumane Kowloon riot created by special agents, which caused innocent working people to suffer from the frenzy of looting, arson and killing by the mob." The letter also stated: "In addition to expressing our condolences to the deceased fellow workers, we are sending some of our donations as a token of sympathy to the families of the deceased workers and the injured workers.

### 旅英华侨捐款慰问九龙受害职工

1956-11-16
第4版()
专栏：

    旅英华侨捐款慰问九龙受害职工
    据新华社讯　香港消息：旅居英国利物浦的九十五个华侨工人，最近捐款慰问在九龙暴乱事件中的死难工人的家属和受伤的工人。他们在11月5日把募集的一百零八英镑十先令汇给香港大公报，托这家报社转给港九工会联合会。
    他们在汇款中附寄的信上说：“我们对于特务分子制造惨无人道的九龙暴动事件，使一般善良的劳动人民遭遇暴徒的疯狂抢劫烧杀，都深为震惊，也无不感到愤怒和痛恨。”信上又说：“我们除对死难同胞工人致哀悼外，特将我们的一些捐款寄上，作为慰问死难工人家属与受伤工人。
