Title: The Wave of Struggle of the Japanese People Shakes the United States. American Newspapers Worry that America's Aggressive Plans in the Far East Will Be Frustrated
Author: 
Date: 1960-05-31
Page: 4
    Xinhua News Agency, New York, May 30th: The American bourgeois press is alarmed and uneasy about the massive struggle of the Japanese people against the U.S.-Japan military alliance treaty and demanding the resignation of the Shinsuke Kishi traitorous government.

    The "New York Times" said with concern on the 28th, "The fruits of our (American) military diplomacy are showing new signs of a poor harvest in Japan." The newspaper pointed out that the U.S.-backed Syngman Rhee puppet regime in South Korea and the Adnan Menderes dictatorial government in Turkey have already fallen, and in Japan, "it appears that anti-American demonstrations are intensifying."

    "New York Times" commentator Sulzberger worried that the huge wave of popular struggle sweeping South Korea and Turkey "shocking Japan" will further frustrate the U.S.'s plans for aggression and war in the Far East. Sulzberger said, "If it turns out that Kishi Shinsuke cannot withstand the increasing pressure, our (American) position will undoubtedly be damaged." He also worried that in the future the United States "may be forced to remove military facilities on the Japanese islands" and may "face an embarrassing situation" in Okinawa. Sulzberger continued, "If this trend continues to develop, it may extend to Taiwan, thereby overthrowing the Chiang Kai-shek regime and shattering our illusion that it can one day speak for China."

    The "Business Week" on the 28th also wrote, "The United States is facing difficulties in its main ally, Japan." This big business magazine admitted that U.S. President Eisenhower is planning to visit Japan to support the Kishi Shinsuke government. The magazine said, "Washington observers believe that despite the possibility of unfriendly demonstrations, he (Eisenhower) will use this visit to reaffirm the importance of the U.S.-Japan alliance."

### 日本人民的斗争浪潮震动了美国  美报担心美国在远东的侵略计划遭到挫折

1960-05-31
第4版()
专栏：

    日本人民的斗争浪潮震动了美国
    美报担心美国在远东的侵略计划遭到挫折
    新华社30日讯　纽约消息：美国资产阶级报刊对日本人民反对日美军事同盟条约、要求岸信介卖国政府下台的规模浩大的斗争，惊惶不安。
    “纽约时报”28日忧心忡忡地说，“我们（美国）军事外交的果实在日本表现出收成不好的新迹象”。这家报纸指出，美国支持的南朝鲜李承晚傀儡政权和土耳其曼德列斯独裁政府已经倒台了，而在日本，“看来在那里的反美示威正日益高涨”。
    “纽约时报”评论员苏兹贝格担心，席卷南朝鲜和土耳其的人民斗争的巨大浪潮“冲击日本”，将使美国在远东的侵略和战争计划遭到进一步的挫折。苏兹贝格说，“如果事实证明岸信介不能够经得住越来越大的压力的话，我们（美国）的地位无疑将受到损害。”他还忧虑将来美国“可能被迫撤除在日本岛屿上的军事设施”，并且可能在冲绳“面临尴尬的处境”。苏兹贝格接着说，“如果这种倾向继续发展下去的话，可能扩展到台湾，从而推翻蒋介石政权，粉碎我们希望有朝一日它能代表中国讲话的幻想”。
    28日的“商业周刊”也写道，“美国在它的主要盟国日本正面临困难”。这家大企业杂志承认，美国总统艾森豪威尔正打算通过访问日本来为岸信介政府撑腰。杂志说，“华盛顿观察家认为，尽管可能发生不友好的示威，他（艾森豪威尔）将借这次访问再次肯定美日联盟的重要性。”
