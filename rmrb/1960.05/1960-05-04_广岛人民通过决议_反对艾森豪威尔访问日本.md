Title: Hiroshima People Pass Resolution Opposing Eisenhower's Visit to Japan
Author:
Date: 1960-05-04
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, Tokyo, 2nd: At a "May Day" International Labor Day celebration held in Hiroshima City, Japan, on May 1st, 20,000 people passed a resolution expressing their opposition to U.S. President Eisenhower's visit to Japan.

The resolution stated: "We, the Japanese people, feel great anger about the United States dropping the atomic bomb, and so far, the United States has not expressed any apology. If President Eisenhower does not kneel and pay respects before the spirits of the more than 200,000 victims in Hiroshima, apologize for dropping the atomic bomb, and immediately abolish the new Japan-U.S. Security Treaty, we will strongly oppose the plan to visit Japan."

As previously reported, foreign news agencies revealed that Eisenhower's main purpose in visiting Japan was to urge the Kishi Nobusuke government to implement the Japan-U.S. military alliance treaty as soon as possible.

### 广岛人民通过决议  反对艾森豪威尔访问日本

1960-05-04
第5版()
专栏：

    广岛人民通过决议
    反对艾森豪威尔访问日本
    新华社2日讯　东京消息：日本广岛市两万人在1日举行的“五一”国际劳动节庆祝大会上通过决议，表示反对美国总统艾森豪威尔访问日本。
    决议说：“我们日本人民对美国投落原子弹一事感到很大的愤怒，迄今为止，美国丝毫没有表示谢罪。如果艾森豪威尔总统不在广岛二十几万牺牲者的灵前跪拜敬礼，对投落原子弹表示谢罪，并且立即废除新的日美安全条约的话，我们就强烈反对访问日本的计划。”
    前已报道，外国通讯社透露，艾森豪威尔到日本访问的主要目的是催促岸信介政府早日实施日美军事同盟条约。
