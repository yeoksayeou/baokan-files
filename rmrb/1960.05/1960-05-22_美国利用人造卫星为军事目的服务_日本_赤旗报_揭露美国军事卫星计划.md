Title: U.S. Uses Artificial Satellites for Military Purposes: Japanese "Akahata" Exposes U.S. Military Satellite Program
Author:
Date: 1960-05-22
Page: 5

    U.S. Uses Artificial Satellites for Military Purposes
    Japanese "Akahata" Exposes U.S. Military Satellite Program
    (Reported by this newspaper) The Japanese "Akahata" published an article entitled "The U.S. Military Satellite Program" in its Sunday edition on the 15th of this month, exposing that the U.S.'s so-called "space program" has the same robber purpose of engaging in espionage activities and attempting to launch aggressive wars as the U-2 aircraft that was shot down for invading Soviet airspace.
    The "Akahata" article revealed that the U.S. plans to launch nearly one hundred artificial satellites and space rockets from the beginning of this year to the end of next year. Among these nearly one hundred satellites of various types, ninety are for military purposes. The "Tiros," "Transit," "Discoverer," and other satellites launched by the U.S. in succession from February to April are all part of this plan. Moreover, like the espionage activities of the U-2 aircraft, the launching of these artificial satellites is nominally carried out under the guidance of the so-called U.S. National Aeronautics and Space Administration, but in reality, almost all of these artificial satellites are launched by the U.S. Army, Navy, and Air Force.
    According to the design performance of these U.S. artificial satellites, the article pointed out that the U.S.'s "Tiros" satellites, "Discoverer" satellites, and "Samos" satellites are all intended to be used for espionage activities such as reconnaissance and photography of Soviet territory and airspace from outer space.
    The article further pointed out that the U.S. artificial satellite program has been for military aggression purposes from the beginning. In 1946, the second year after the end of World War II, the U.S. planned to build artificial satellites capable of carrying people to engage in espionage work and carrying atomic bombs. Moreover, in 1948, in order to study and manufacture such satellites, the then U.S. Secretary of Defense Forrestal made a special report to Congress. When the Soviet Union launched a space rocket last year, former U.S. Secretary of Defense McElroy said: "The U.S. space program is not in the distant sky... Taking pictures of the back of the moon is of little significance. The U.S. has always been fully engaged in the research of military artificial satellites."

### 美国利用人造卫星为军事目的服务  日本“赤旗报”揭露美国军事卫星计划

1960-05-22
第5版()
专栏：

    美国利用人造卫星为军事目的服务
    日本“赤旗报”揭露美国军事卫星计划
    本报讯　日本“赤旗报”在本月15日的星期日版上刊登了一篇题为“美国的军事卫星计划”的文章，揭露了美国的所谓“空间计划”是同这次侵犯苏联领空被击落的U—2型飞机具有同样从事间谍活动、企图发动侵略战争的强盗目的。
    “赤旗报”的文章揭露说，美国计划从今年年初起到明年年中止，将发射近一百个人造卫星和宇宙火箭。在这将近一百个各种类型的人造卫星中，有九十个是属于军事目的的。从今年2月起到4月，美国接连发射的“太罗斯”、“子午仪”、“发现者”等等卫星都是属于这个计划的一部分。并且，和U—2型飞机的间谍活动一样，发射这些人造卫星，名义上，也是在所谓美国国家航空和宇宙航行局的指导下进行的，而实际上，几乎所有这些人造卫星都是由美国的海、陆、空军所发射。
    文章根据美国这些人造卫星的设计性能等指出，美国的“太罗斯”卫星、“发现者”卫星、“萨摩斯”卫星都是企图用来从宇宙空间对苏联领土领空进行侦察、摄影等间谍活动的。
    文章并且进一步指出，美国的人造卫星计划，从开头起，就是为了军事侵略目的，美国在第二次世界大战结束的第二年——1946年，就计划建造能够搭载人的、用以从事间谍工作，并能携带原子弹的人造卫星。而且在1948年，为了研究制造这种卫星，当时美国的国防部长福莱斯特尔曾在国会上作过特别报告。当去年苏联发射宇宙火箭后，美国前国防部长麦克耳罗伊曾说：“美国的空间计划不在遥远的天空，……拍摄月球背面的照片是没有多大意义的。美国一贯是全力从事军用人造卫星的研究”。
