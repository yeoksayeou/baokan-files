Title: Demanding the Restoration of Panama's Sovereignty over the Canal Zone, Panamanian Youth Hold Anti-American Demonstrations Again; American Publication Admits that the Panamanian People Hate the American Occupation of the Canal Zone
Author:
Date: 1960-05-04
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, 3rd, Panama City: A group of Panamanian youths held an anti-American demonstration in the center of the American-occupied Canal Zone on May 2nd, demanding the restoration of Panama's sovereignty over the Canal Zone. The demonstrators sang the Panamanian national anthem and folk songs, and performed folk dances.

This demonstration was to commemorate the second anniversary of Panamanian patriots planting the national flag in the Canal Zone in May 1958.

Xinhua News Agency, 3rd, New York: The American "Saturday Evening Post" published an article by Harold H. Martin on the Panamanian people's anti-American struggle in its April 23rd issue, entitled: "Why They Hate Us". Although this article tried its best to defend America's banditry in Panama, it had to admit that the fundamental reason for the Panamanian people's anti-Americanism lies in the United States' seizure of sovereignty over the Canal Zone. It said, "When the original treaty (referring to the "Hay-Bunau-Varilla Treaty" of 1903, by which the United States forcibly obtained the right to dig the canal and occupy the Canal Zone) was written fifty-seven years ago, the seeds of the current dispute were sown."

The article said that Panamanians believe that "the United States developed this resource (referring to the narrow strip of land through which the Panama Canal runs) by digging a canal. Thus, came the poverty and hunger of the Panamanian people, because the United States does not give them the deserved portion of the wealth created by the canal." The article also acknowledged that the American occupiers who poured into the Panama Canal Zone also "brought an inherent prejudice against people of another color."

The article admitted that "Panamanians have shown that no matter what concessions we (referring to the United States) may make in the future, they will continue to pursue greater recognition of their sovereignty—not just in terms of cash payments, but also in terms of jurisdiction. This is a challenge that cannot be met with barbed wire and bayonets." The article quoted an "angry Panamanian" as saying: "You (referring to the American occupiers) cannot stay permanently on a small island surrounded by an ocean of hatred."

### 要求恢复巴拿马对运河区的主权  巴拿马青年又举行反美示威  美刊承认巴拿马人民痛恨美国侵占运河区

1960-05-04
第5版()
专栏：

    要求恢复巴拿马对运河区的主权
    巴拿马青年又举行反美示威
    美刊承认巴拿马人民痛恨美国侵占运河区
    新华社3日讯　巴拿马城消息：一批巴拿马青年5月2日在美国霸占的运河区的中心举行了反美示威，要求恢复巴拿马对运河区的主权。示威者高唱巴拿马国歌和民族歌曲，并且跳民族舞蹈。
    这次示威是纪念1958年5月间巴拿马爱国者把国旗插在运河区的两周年。
    新华社3日讯　纽约消息：美国“星期六晚邮报”4月23日出版的一期中，刊载了哈罗德·赫·马丁谈巴拿马人民反美斗争的文章，题目是：“他们为什么仇恨我们”。尽管这篇文章极力为美国在巴拿马的强盗行径进行了辩解，但是，它不得不承认，巴拿马人民反美的根本原因在于美国攫取了运河区的主权。它说，“当五十七年以前写下原条约（指1903年美国强行取得开凿运河和占领运河区权利的“巴美条约”）的时候，就种下了目前正在开花的纠纷的种子”。
    文章说，巴拿马人认为，“美国通过开凿一条运河开发了这个富源（指巴拿马运河流经的狭窄地带）。这样，随着而来的是巴拿马人民的贫困和饥饿，因为美国不给予他们以运河所创造的财富的应得部分。”文章还承认，涌入巴拿马运河区的美国占领者还“带来了天生的反对另一种肤色的人的偏见”。
    文章承认，“巴拿马人已经表明，不管我们（指美国）将来可能作出什么让步，他们要继续追求对他们的主权的更大的承认——不仅仅表现于支付现金，而且表现于管辖。这是一种不能以铁丝网和刺刀来加以对付的挑战”。文章引用一位“愤怒的巴拿马人”的话说：“你们（指美国占领者）不能在一个被仇恨的海洋包围的小岛上永久呆下去”。
