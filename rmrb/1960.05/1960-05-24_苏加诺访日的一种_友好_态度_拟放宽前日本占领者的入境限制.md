Title: Sukarno's "Friendly" Attitude Towards Visit to Japan: Proposed Relaxation of Entry Restrictions for Former Japanese Occupiers
Author:
Date: 1960-05-24
Page: 5

    Sukarno's "Friendly" Attitude Towards Visit to Japan
    Proposed Relaxation of Entry Restrictions for Former Japanese Occupiers
    Xinhua News Agency, San Francisco, 23rd: President Sukarno left Honolulu on May 23 for a visit to Japan after visiting the United States.

    Foreign Minister Subandrio is accompanying Sukarno.

    According to a report by United Press International, the Indonesian government recently revealed that as a "friendly" gesture during President Sukarno's visit to Japan, Indonesia will return the ashes of seventy-six former Japanese soldiers who invaded Indonesia to Japan. This includes the ashes of Lieutenant General Nakajima Tetsuzo, the former Japanese military administrator of Java.

    Prior to this, Indonesian Foreign Ministry officials said that Indonesian authorities are considering relaxing entry restrictions on Japanese people who directly enslaved the Indonesian people during the Japanese occupation of World War II.

### 苏加诺访日的一种“友好”态度  拟放宽前日本占领者的入境限制

1960-05-24
第5版()
专栏：

    苏加诺访日的一种“友好”态度
    拟放宽前日本占领者的入境限制
    新华社23日讯　旧金山消息：苏加诺总统访问了美国以后，于5月23日离开檀香山前往日本访问。
    同苏加诺同行的还有外交部长苏班德里约。
    据合众国际社报道，印度尼西亚政府最近透露，作为苏加诺总统到日本访问时的一种“友好”态度，印度尼西亚将把七十六名侵略印度尼西亚的前日本军人骨灰送回日本。其中包括前日本驻爪哇的军事行政长官中岛铁藏中将的骨灰。
    在这以前，印度尼西亚外交部人士说，印度尼西亚当局正在考虑放宽对第二次世界大战期间日本占领时期曾经直接奴役印度尼西亚人民的日本人的入境限制的问题。
