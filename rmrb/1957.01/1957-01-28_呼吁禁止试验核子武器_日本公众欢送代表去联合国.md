Title: Calling for a Ban on Nuclear Weapons Testing, Japanese Public Sends Representatives to the United Nations
Author:
Date: 1957-01-28
Page: 6

    Calling for a Ban on Nuclear Weapons Testing
    Japanese Public Sends Representatives to the United Nations
    Xinhua News Agency, Tokyo, January 27th: About five hundred Japanese people gathered in Tokyo today to bid farewell to two Japanese representatives who are going to the United Nations General Assembly to call for a ban on nuclear weapons testing. The two representatives are Kubo Yama Suzuko, the wife of Kubo Yama Aikichi, the Japanese fisherman killed by the American hydrogen bomb test, and Iso Fujioko, a lecturer at Japan Women's University. They will appeal to the United States government, women's groups and other civil organizations to ban atomic bombs and hydrogen bombs.

### 呼吁禁止试验核子武器  日本公众欢送代表去联合国

1957-01-28
第6版()
专栏：

    呼吁禁止试验核子武器
    日本公众欢送代表去联合国
    新华社27日讯　东京消息：日本公众约五百人今天在东京集会欢送两位日本代表到联合国大会去呼吁禁止核子武器试验。这两位代表是被美国氢弹试验杀死的日本渔民久保山爱吉的妻子久保山铃和日本女子大学讲师矶野富士子。她们将向美国政府、妇女团体和其他民间组织呼吁禁止原子弹和氢弹。
