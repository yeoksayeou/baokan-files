Title: Sudan Denies Transit to British Officers
Author:
Date: 1957-01-08
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, Cairo, 7th -- According to a Middle East News Agency report from Khartoum, the capital of Sudan, on the 6th, the Sudanese Ministry of Interior refused to issue visas to British Field Marshal Sir Claude Auchinleck and former War Minister Leslie Hore-Belisha. The two men intended to stop briefly in Sudan on their way to East Africa.

The Middle East News Agency said that Auchinleck was the Commander-in-Chief of the British forces in the Middle East from 1941 to 1942. During the recent British attack on Egypt, he served as an advisor to the British War Ministry.

### 苏丹不让英国军官过境

1957-01-08
第6版()
专栏：

    苏丹不让英国军官过境
    新华社开罗7日电　据中东通讯社6日自苏丹首都喀士穆报道，苏丹内政部拒绝发签证给英国的陆军元帅克劳德·奥琴勒克爵士和前陆军部大臣来斯利·侯尔—贝利夏。这两个人打算在前往东非的途中在苏丹稍事停留。
    中东通讯社说，奥琴勒克是1941—1942年英国驻中东军队总司令。在最近英国进攻埃及期间，他担任英国陆军部顾问。
