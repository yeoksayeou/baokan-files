Title: US, Britain, and France Collude, Still Want "International Management" of the Canal
Author:
Date: 1957-01-21
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, January 20th: Some reports in American newspapers reveal that the United States, along with Britain and France, are attempting to force Egypt to accept so-called "international management" of the Suez Canal.

The "New York Times" stated that U.S. officials believe that the economic difficulties brought to Egypt by the Anglo-French aggression will force Egypt to negotiate. However, U.S. officials pointed out with great disappointment that Egypt has adopted an "unyielding" attitude on the issue of Suez Canal negotiations. Therefore, the U.S. government has rejected Egypt's request for short-term aid.

"New York Herald Tribune" reporter Marguerite Higgins said that the United States, Britain, and France have jointly formulated a new plan aimed at handing over the Suez Canal area to "United Nations management."

### 美英法狼狈为奸  仍想“国际管理”运河

1957-01-21
第6版()
专栏：

    美英法狼狈为奸
    仍想“国际管理”运河
    据新华社20日讯　美国报纸的一些报道透露，美国跟英国和法国正在企图强迫埃及接受所谓“国际管理”苏伊士运河。
    “纽约时报”说，美国官方人士认为，英、法的侵略给埃及带来的经济困难，将迫使埃及来进行谈判。可是，美国官方人士非常失望地指出，埃及在苏伊士运河谈判这个问题上采取了“不屈服”的态度，因此，美国政府拒绝了埃及所提出的短期援助的要求。
    “纽约先驱论坛报”女记者希金斯说，美国同英国和法国共同制订了一个新的计划，目的是要把苏伊士运河地区交给“联合国管理”。
