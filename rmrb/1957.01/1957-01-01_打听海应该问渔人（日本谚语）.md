Title: To Inquire About the Sea, Ask the Fisherman (Japanese Proverb)
Author: Zhao Zhiqiang, Wang Sengdao
Date: 1957-01-01
Page: 8

    To Inquire About the Sea, Ask the Fisherman
      (Japanese Proverb) Where there are no weasels, minks put on airs.
  ＊A hungry dog fears no stick.
  ＊A journey of a thousand miles begins with a single step.
  ＊Do not underestimate small enemies, do not fear violent opponents.
  ＊To inquire about the sea, ask the fisherman.
  ＊A branch separated from the tree no longer blooms, a broken mirror no longer reflects a face.
        Translated and Compiled by Zhao Zhiqiang and Wang Sengdao

### 打听海应该问渔人（日本谚语）
赵志强  王僧道
1957-01-01
第8版()
专栏：

    打听海应该问渔人
      （日本谚语）没有黄鼠狼的地方，貂也摆架子。
  ＊饿狗不怕木棍。
  ＊旅程千里，终需开始第一步。
  ＊不轻视微小的敌人，不害怕狂暴的对方。
  ＊打听海应该问渔人。
  ＊离开树的枝子不再盛开鲜花，打碎的镜子不再映出人面。
        赵志强、王僧道 译辑
