Title: Burmese Newspapers Report: Burmese Communist Party Suggests Peace Talks
Author:
Date: 1957-01-19
Page: 6

    Burmese Newspapers Report
    Burmese Communist Party Suggests Peace Talks
    Xinhua News Agency, Yangon, January 18th: According to the English newspaper "The Nation," Burmese Communist Party leader Thakin Than Tun recently wrote two letters to the ruling Anti-Fascist People's Freedom League of Burma, suggesting that peace talks be held to stop the civil war.
    These two letters were delivered by Thakin Ko Daw Hmaing. The letters stated: "When negotiations are underway, let us have fair and impartial honest people as arbiters to promote the negotiations."
    It is reported that the Executive Committee of the Anti-Fascist People's Freedom League met to discuss these two letters. The committee also sent Deputy Prime Minister Thakin Tin, U Myint and Minister Thakin San Myint to deliver the Anti-Fascist People's Freedom League's reply to Thakin Ko Daw Hmaing.

### 缅甸报纸报道  缅共建议举行和谈

1957-01-19
第6版()
专栏：

    缅甸报纸报道
    缅共建议举行和谈
    新华社仰光18日电　据英文报纸“民族报”说，缅甸共产党领袖德钦丹东最近给缅甸执政党反法西斯人民自由同盟写了两封信，建议通过和平谈判来停止内战。
    这两封信是由德钦哥都迈转交的，信中说：“在进行谈判的时候，让我们之间有公正无私的正直人作为公断人以便促进谈判。
    据报道，反法西斯人民自由同盟执行委员会开会讨论了这两封信。委员会并且派副总理德钦丁、吴名和部长德钦山密把反法西斯人民自由同盟的复信转交给德钦哥都迈。
