Title: Hammarskjöld Suggests Six-Nation Conference to Renegotiate Canal Issue
Author:
Date: 1957-01-13
Page: 6
Xinhua News Agency, January 12th — News from the United Nations headquarters in New York says that Hammarskjöld has suggested holding a six-nation round table conference to resume negotiations on the Suez Canal issue. The six countries proposed to participate in the conference are: Egypt, the United Kingdom, France, Norway, Italy, and Ceylon.

The news says that the United Kingdom and France have been discussing this issue with Hammarskjöld, and Hammarskjöld has also been in contact with Egypt on this issue. A spokesman for the British delegation to the United Nations, when talking about this news, stated that the United Kingdom believes that the foundation for resuming negotiations on the canal issue is ready.

### 哈马舍尔德建议举行六国会议重新谈判运河问题

1957-01-13
第6版()
专栏：

    哈马舍尔德建议举行六国会议重新谈判运河问题
    据新华社12日讯　纽约联合国总部传出的消息说，哈马舍尔德已建议举行六国圆桌会议，以恢复解决苏伊士运河问题的谈判。拟议中参加会议的六个国家是：埃及、英国、法国、挪威、意大利和锡兰。
    消息说，英国和法国一直在就这个问题同哈马舍尔德进行讨论，哈马舍尔德也一直在就这个问题同埃及进行接触。英国驻联合国代表团的一个发言人在谈到这个消息时表示，英国认为已经准备好了恢复运河问题谈判的基础。
