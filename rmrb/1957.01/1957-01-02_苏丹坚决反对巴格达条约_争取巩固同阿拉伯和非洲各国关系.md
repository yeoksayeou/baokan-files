Title: Sudan Firmly Opposes the Baghdad Pact, Striving to Consolidate Relations with Arab and African Countries
Author:
Date: 1957-01-02
Page: 6
Xinhua News Agency, January 1st dispatch from Cairo: According to a report from the Middle East News Agency, Sudanese Foreign Minister Muhammad Ahmad Mahjoub stated at a press conference held in Khartoum on December 30th that Sudan is striving to consolidate its mutual relations with the people of Arab countries and the people of African countries.

The Sudanese Foreign Minister pointed out that Sudan firmly opposes the Baghdad Pact and will never participate in it. He said, "We will oppose all attempts to force any Arab country to accept the Baghdad Pact."

Mahjoub said that Sudan intends to conclude a "Good Neighborly Relations Treaty" with Egypt and Ethiopia.

### 苏丹坚决反对巴格达条约  争取巩固同阿拉伯和非洲各国关系

1957-01-02
第6版()
专栏：

    苏丹坚决反对巴格达条约
    争取巩固同阿拉伯和非洲各国关系
    据新华社1日讯　开罗消息：据中东通讯社报道，苏丹外交部长穆罕默德·艾哈迈德·马哈古卜12月30日在喀土穆举行的记者招待会上说，苏丹正在争取巩固同阿拉伯各国的人民和非洲各国人民的相互关系。
    苏丹外交部长指出，苏丹坚决反对巴格达条约，决不参加巴格达条约。他说，我们将要反对强迫任何一个阿拉伯国家接受巴格达条约的一切企图。
    马哈古卜说，苏丹打算同埃及和埃塞俄比亚缔结“睦邻关系条约”。
