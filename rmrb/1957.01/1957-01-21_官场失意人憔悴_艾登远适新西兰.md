Title: Official Career Frustrated, Person Worn Out; Eden Travels Far to New Zealand
Author: 
Date: 1957-01-21
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, 19th – London Dispatch: Former British Prime Minister Eden, who resigned mainly because of the failure of the invasion of Egypt, left London on the 18th and took a boat to New Zealand for an indefinite "vacation."

Eden, appearing in public for the first time since his resignation, looked haggard and dejected. Before the ship sailed, he even cursed Egypt to reporters on the gangway.

### 官场失意人憔悴  艾登远适新西兰

1957-01-21
第6版()
专栏：

    官场失意人憔悴
    艾登远适新西兰
    新华社19日讯　伦敦消息：主要因为侵略埃及失败而下台的前英国首相艾登，在18日离开伦敦，坐船到新西兰去无限期地“休假”。
    下台后第一次在公共场合出现的艾登显得憔悴而沮丧。轮船开行前，他在梯板上还对记者大骂了埃及一顿。
