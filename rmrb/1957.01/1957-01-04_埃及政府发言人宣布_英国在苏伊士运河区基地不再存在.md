Title: Egyptian Government Spokesman Announces: British Bases in the Suez Canal Zone No Longer Exist
Author:
Date: 1957-01-04
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, January 3rd, Cairo Dispatch: An official spokesman for the Egyptian government told reporters from the Middle East News Agency on January 2nd that the British military bases in the Suez Canal Zone mentioned in the 1954 Anglo-Egyptian Treaty no longer exist.

The spokesman said, "This base was immediately abolished after the British aggression against Egypt." He said that Egypt "obtained British military supplies from this Suez base to compensate for the Egyptian materials destroyed by the British attacks on our troops stationed in the Sinai Peninsula."

The Egyptian government spokesman also said: "Repeated fierce bombings of the warehouses in this base by British aircraft have caused considerable losses to the materials stored there."

The statement concluded: "The 1954 (Anglo-Egyptian) Treaty is now considered invalid because the treaty was concluded to defend Egypt from aggression, and Britain does not abide by this provision. Britain is not standing with Egypt against foreign aggression, but is attacking, controlling, and occupying Egypt together with France and Israel."

### 埃及政府发言人宣布  英国在苏伊士运河区基地不再存在

1957-01-04
第6版()
专栏：

    埃及政府发言人宣布
    英国在苏伊士运河区基地不再存在
    新华社3日讯　开罗消息：埃及政府官方发言人1月2日对中东通讯社记者说，1954年英埃条约中所提到的在苏伊士运河区的英国军事基地不再存在了。
    发言人说，“在英国侵略埃及之后，这个基地立即取消了”。他说，埃及“从这个苏伊士基地得到了英国的军需品以补偿由于英国对驻在西奈半岛的我国军队进行的袭击而遭到破坏的埃及物资”。
    埃及政府发言人还说：“英国飞机一再对这个基地的仓库进行的猛烈轰炸，使储藏在那里的物资蒙受相当大的损失。”
    声明最后说：“1954（英埃）条约现在已经被认为无效，因为，缔结这项条约是为了保卫埃及使它免受侵略，而英国并不遵守这个规定。英国并不是同埃及站在一边反对外来的侵略，而是同法国和以色列一起来进攻、统制和侵占埃及。”
