Title: Eisenhower Submits Unprecedentedly Huge War Preparation Budget to Congress, Advocating a Policy of Strength and Greatly Increasing Military Spending and Atomic Weapons Costs
Author:
Date: 1957-01-18
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, Washington, 16th: U.S. President Eisenhower today submitted to the U.S. Congress the budget message for the 1958 fiscal year. The size of this budget breaks the record for peacetime in American history.

The total budget expenditure proposed in the message for the new fiscal year (from July 1, 1957, to June 30, 1958) is 71.8 billion U.S. dollars, an increase of 2.9 billion U.S. dollars compared to the current fiscal year. Among these 2.9 billion U.S. dollars, 2.6 billion U.S. dollars are increases in direct military spending and foreign "aid" with obvious aggressive purposes.

The budgeted expenditures for direct military expenses and foreign "aid" total 45.3 billion U.S. dollars, accounting for 63% of the total budget expenditure. Among them, the expenses for the Army, Navy, and Air Force are 38 billion U.S. dollars, an increase of 2 billion U.S. dollars compared to the current fiscal year. The Air Force portion accounts for 17.4 billion U.S. dollars, an increase of 600 million U.S. dollars compared to the current fiscal year.

In the message, Eisenhower once again advocated a position of strength policy, emphasizing the need to "develop new and large-scale superb weapons" and to continue to maintain a sufficiently powerful "nuclear aerial retaliatory force." The message calls for increasing new and modernized attack aircraft carriers, attack forces with high-speed and high-altitude jet aircraft, atomic submarines, and missile ships.

Eisenhower particularly emphasized the importance of developing nuclear weapons and guided missiles. He said that the United States will continue to increase the number and types of nuclear weapons, and the budget will increase the cost of developing atomic energy (mainly developing atomic weapons) from 1.9 billion U.S. dollars to 2.3 billion U.S. dollars. Guided missile costs increased by 35% compared to the current fiscal year, reaching 2 billion U.S. dollars.

The budget cost for foreign "aid" is 4.4 billion U.S. dollars, of which military "aid" is 2.6 billion U.S. dollars. Economic "aid" increased by 300 million U.S. dollars, to 1.8 billion U.S. dollars. In order to cooperate with the U.S. government's aggressive policy towards the Middle East, the message specifically requests that 200 million U.S. dollars be earmarked in the economic "aid" portion that can be freely disposed of by the president in the Middle East region.

In the entire budget, the non-military expenditure portion increased by only 0.4%. Worth noting in this regard is Eisenhower's request to increase the operating expenses of the United States Information Agency and the Federal Bureau of Investigation.

### 艾森豪威尔向国会提出空前庞大的备战预算  鼓吹实力政策大量增加军费和原子武器费用

1957-01-18
第6版()
专栏：

    艾森豪威尔向国会提出空前庞大的备战预算
    鼓吹实力政策大量增加军费和原子武器费用
    据新华社16日讯　华盛顿消息：美国总统艾森豪威尔今天向美国国会提出1958财政年度的预算咨文，这个预算数字的庞大打破了美国历史上和平时期的纪录。
    咨文提出的新财政年度（从1957年7月1日到1958年6月30日）的总预算开支为七百一十八亿美元，比本财政年度增加二十九亿美元。在这二十九亿美元当中有二十六亿美元都是直接军事费用和带有明显的侵略目的的对外“援助”费用的增加。
    直接军事费用和对外“援助”的预算开支一共是四百五十三亿美元，占总预算开支的63％。其中海陆空军的费用为三百八十亿美元，比本财政年度增加二十亿美元。空军部分占一百七十四亿美元，比本财政年度又增加六亿美元。
    艾森豪威尔在咨文中又一次鼓吹实力地位政策，强调要“发展新的和大量的超级武器”，要继续保持足够强大的“核子空中报复力量”。咨文要求增加新的和现代化的袭击航空母舰，有高速度和高度上升能力的喷气飞机、原子潜水艇和导弹舰只等攻击力量。
    艾森豪威尔特别强调发展核子武器和定向导弹的重要性。他说美国将继续增加核子武器的数目和种类，预算把发展原子能（其中主要是发展原子武器）的费用从十九亿美元增加到二十三亿美元。定向导弹费用比本财政年度增加了35％，达到二十亿美元。
    对外“援助”方面的预算费用是四十四亿美元，其中军事“援助”为二十六亿美元。经济“援助”增加了三亿美元，为十八亿美元。为了配合美国政府对中东的侵略政策，咨文中特别要求在经济“援助”部分划出可以由总统在中东地区自由支配的两亿美元。
    在整个预算中非军事费用部分仅仅增加了0.4％。在这方面值得注意的，是艾森豪威尔要求增加美国新闻署和联邦调查局的活动经费。
