Title: Egypt Abolishes the Anglo-Egyptian Agreement
Author: Yuan
Date: 1957-01-04
Page: 6

    Egyptian Abolition of the Anglo-Egyptian Agreement
    On January 1, 1957, Egyptian President Nasser announced the abolition of the 1954 Anglo-Egyptian Treaty regarding the military base in the Suez Canal Zone. This is an important measure to safeguard Egypt's sovereignty, integrity, and national independence.
    Britain invaded and occupied Egypt in 1882 and unilaterally declared Egypt a British protectorate in 1914. Due to the upsurge of the Egyptian national liberation movement, Britain had to recognize Egypt as an "independent kingdom" in February 1922, but Britain still retained the right to station troops in the Canal Zone. In 1936, Britain and the Egyptian government signed the "Anglo-Egyptian Twenty-Year Alliance Treaty," which stipulated that Britain would continue to occupy several important areas of Egypt, especially the Suez Canal Zone. On October 15, 1951, the Egyptian Parliament announced the abolition of the 1936 Anglo-Egyptian Treaty. After the establishment of the Republic of Egypt in 1953, the Egyptian people continued to oppose the British military presence in the Suez Canal Zone. On July 27, 1954, Britain and Egypt provisionally signed a new agreement on the Suez Canal in Cairo, and formally signed the agreement on October 19 of the same year.
    The 1954 Anglo-Egyptian Agreement stipulated that the British occupation forces should completely withdraw from the Canal base within twenty months after the signing. On June 13, 1956, the last British soldier left Egyptian soil in disgrace. This was a major victory for the Egyptian people's anti-colonialism. However, the 1954 Anglo-Egyptian Agreement also stipulated some unequal clauses for Egypt. The agreement stipulated that after the withdrawal of British troops, the equipment of the British base in the Suez Canal Zone should be maintained by British "civilian contracting companies," which in essence allowed British plainclothes troops to continue to control the Canal base; the treaty was valid for seven years, during which time the British Air Force could fly over Egypt after prior notification, land in the Canal Zone, and use airport facilities; the agreement also stipulated that British troops could return to the Suez Canal area and occupy Egyptian territory on the pretext of so-called "armed aggression by foreign countries" against the Arab League countries and Turkey. This exposed Britain's attempt to rebuild colonial rule in Egypt. The Anglo-French war of aggression against Egypt proved this point. Therefore, it is entirely justified for Egypt to declare the abolition of the unequal treaty of 1954 after driving out the Anglo-French aggressors. As Egypt's "Al-Ahram" pointed out, "Britain is an enemy of Egypt, and there can be no alliance with an enemy."
                                (Yuan)

### 埃及废除的英埃协定

1957-01-04
第6版()
专栏：国际知识

    埃及废除的英埃协定
    1957年1月1日埃及总统纳赛尔宣布废除1954年的英埃关于苏伊士运河区军事基地的条约，这是维护埃及主权完整和民族独立的一个重要措施。
    英国在1882年侵占埃及，在1914年片面宣布埃及为英国的保护国。由于埃及民族解放运动的高涨，英国不得不在1922年2月承认埃及为“独立王国”，但英国仍保留在运河区驻军的权利。1936年，英国与埃及政府签订了“英埃二十年同盟条约”，其中规定，英国继续占领埃及的若干重要地区，尤其是苏伊士运河区。1951年10月15日，埃及议会宣布废除1936年的英埃条约。1953年，埃及共和国建成后，埃及人民继续反对英国在苏伊士运河区驻军。1954年7月27日，英埃在开罗临时签订了关于苏伊士运河的新协定，并于同年10月19日正式签署了该项协定。
    1954年的英埃协定规定英国占领军在签字后二十个月内完全撤出运河基地。1956年6月13日最后一个英国士兵灰溜溜地离开了埃及土地。这是埃及人民反殖民主义的重大胜利。但是，1954年的英埃协定还规定了一些对埃及不平等的条款。协定规定：英军撤出后，苏伊士运河区英国基地的设备要由英国的“民间承包公司”维持，实质上是让英国的便衣军队继续控制运河基地；条约有效期为七年，在这期间，英国空军经过预先通知后可以飞越埃及，在运河区降落，并可使用机场设备；协定还规定，英国军队可以借口所谓阿拉伯联盟国家和土耳其“遭到外国的武装进攻”，而重新回到苏伊士运河地区，占领埃及的领土。这暴露了英国还没有放弃在埃及重建殖民统治的企图。英法对埃及发动的侵略战争，证明了这一点。因而，埃及在把英法侵略者赶走后，宣布废除1954年的不平等条约，是完全正当的。正如埃及“金字塔报”所指出，“英国是埃及的敌人，同敌人是不能成为联盟的。”
                            （元）
