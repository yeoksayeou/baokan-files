Title: After Khrushchev and Johnson's Public Dialogue, Gromyko and Rusk Hold Secret Talks; Johnson Welcomes Khrushchev for "Substantive Discussions"
Author:
Date: 1967-06-22
Edition: 5

	After the heads of the U.S. imperialists, Johnson, and the Soviet revisionist leader, Khrushchev, delivered their respective speeches on the 19th, U.S. Secretary of State Rusk held direct talks with Soviet revisionist Foreign Minister Gromyko in New York at the United Nations on the 20th.

	The White House press secretary, Christian, announced after the Rusk-Gromyko talks: Johnson "welcomes Mr. Khrushchev to come here (Washington) or to go to Camp David or to some other convenient nearby place for a social visit or substantive discussions."

	Johnson and Khrushchev's speeches on the 19th, as well as the Rusk-Gromyko talks, indicate that U.S. imperialism and the Soviet revisionist leadership are intensifying behind-the-scenes negotiations on a series of global issues, including the Middle East and Vietnam. The New York Times on the 20th pointed out, "Khrushchev may be willing to engage in some realistic discussions (with the United States)." The Washington Star Evening News pointed out, "Backdoor diplomacy is still at work, and some realistic discussions are being conducted."

### 柯西金同约翰逊公开对话后  葛罗米柯和腊斯克秘密会谈  约翰逊欢迎柯西金去进行“实质性的讨论”

1967-06-22
第5版()
专栏：

    柯西金同约翰逊公开对话后
    葛罗米柯和腊斯克秘密会谈
    约翰逊欢迎柯西金去进行“实质性的讨论”
    据新华社二十一日讯  纽约消息：在美帝头子约翰逊和苏修头目柯西金十九日公开发表此唱彼和的演说以后，美国国务卿腊斯克同苏修外长葛罗米柯二十日在纽约联合国直接举行会谈。
    美国白宫新闻秘书克里斯琴在腊斯克和葛罗米柯会谈后宣布：约翰逊“欢迎柯西金先生到这里（华盛顿）来，或是去戴维营，或是去附近其他某个方便地点，以进行社交性的访问或是实质性的讨论”。
    约翰逊和柯西金十九日的讲话以及腊斯克和葛罗米柯的会谈表明，美帝国主义和苏修领导集团正在幕后加紧就中东、越南和其他一系列问题进行全球性的“一揽子交易”。《纽约时报》二十日指出，“柯西金可能愿意（同美国）进行某种现实的讨论”。《华盛顿明星晚报》指出，“幕后外交仍在起作用，某种现实的讨论正在进行中”。
