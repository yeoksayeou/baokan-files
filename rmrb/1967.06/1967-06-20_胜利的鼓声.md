Title: The Sound of Victory
Author:
Date: 1967-06-20
Edition: 6

A Burmese friend named Aung Si, upon hearing the joyous news of China's successful hydrogen bomb test, wrote a poem titled "The Sound of Victory Drums Beating." The poem celebrates China's successful hydrogen bomb explosion. The full text of the poem is as follows:

	In the thunder of the rainy season,
	We hear the sound of victory drums beating.
	China has exploded
	Its first hydrogen bomb!
	Let imperialists tremble,
	Let revisionists tremble,
	Let all reactionaries tremble,
	Your nuclear blackmail is finished!
	The people of the world are becoming stronger,
	And more united, rising up,
	To oppose your policies of aggression and war,
	To oppose your policies of oppression and exploitation.
	The bugle calls,
	The people advance,
	A storm is about to come.
	Our supreme commander is with us,
	He shows us the way.
	We fight bravely,
	With confidence and courage.
	Breaking through the dust, thunder, and smoke,
	A rainbow appears on the horizon,
	Victory is in sight.

### 胜利的鼓声

1967-06-20
第6版()
专栏：

    胜利的鼓声
    新华社仰光十八日电　一位名叫昂西的缅甸朋友在听到中国氢弹试验成功的大喜讯之后，作了一首诗，题目是：《胜利的鼓声咚咚响》，欢呼中国氢弹爆炸成功，他作的诗全文如下：
    在雨季的雷声中，
    听得胜利的鼓声咚咚。
    中国爆炸了
    第一颗氢弹！
    让帝国主义者发抖吧，
    让修正主义者发抖吧，
    让一切反动派统统发抖吧，
    你们的核讹诈完蛋了！
    世界人民正在更强大，
    更团结地奋起，
    反对你们的侵略和战争政策，
    反对你们的压迫与剥削政策。
    军号在响，
    人民在前进，
    暴风雨即将来临。
    我们最高的统帅和我们在一起，
    他向我们指明方向。
    我们英勇战斗，
    有信心，有胆量。
    冲破尘埃、雷声和烟雾，
    地平线上出现了彩虹，
    胜利在眼前。
