Title: The Declining Hegemon
Author: Ying Tao
Date: 1967-06-04
Edition: 6

The United States, which once boasted of being the "world policeman" and the "leader of the free world," is now facing a series of setbacks in its international adventures. Its hegemony is crumbling, and its prestige is plummeting. This is a vivid manifestation of the inevitable decline of imperialism and the triumph of people's revolutionary struggles.

The U.S. imperialists, in their attempt to maintain their hegemony, have resorted to all sorts of reactionary means. They have launched aggressive wars, instigated counter-revolutions, and carried out subversive activities. However, their schemes have been foiled one after another. In Vietnam, the U.S. aggressors, despite their superior military strength, have been unable to subdue the heroic Vietnamese people. The war of aggression in Vietnam has become a quagmire for the U.S. imperialists, consuming vast amounts of manpower and resources.

In the Middle East, the U.S. imperialists, in collusion with British imperialism, have been trying to maintain their control over the oil-rich region. However, the Arab people, under the leadership of progressive forces, have been fighting back against imperialist aggression and exploitation. The recent victories of the Arab people in the Six-Day War have dealt a heavy blow to the U.S. and British imperialists, exposing their weakness and vulnerability.

In Latin America, the U.S. imperialists have been attempting to impose their will on the people through military dictatorships and puppet regimes. However, the people of Latin America, inspired by the Cuban Revolution, have been rising up against imperialist oppression and fighting for national liberation and social progress. The recent overthrow of the U.S.-backed dictatorship in the Dominican Republic is a clear indication of the growing strength of the people's revolutionary movement in the region.

The decline of U.S. hegemony is not only evident in its military adventures but also in its economic and political influence. The U.S. dollar, once the dominant currency in the world, is now facing challenges from other currencies. The U.S. economy, plagued by inflation and stagnation, is losing its competitive edge. Politically, the U.S. is increasingly isolated, as more and more countries are asserting their independence and refusing to be dictated to by the U.S. imperialists.

The crumbling of U.S. hegemony is a cause for celebration for the people of the world. It marks the end of an era of imperialist domination and the beginning of a new era of people's liberation and social progress. The people of the world, united in their struggle against imperialism, can look forward to a brighter future, free from the shackles of imperialist oppression.
