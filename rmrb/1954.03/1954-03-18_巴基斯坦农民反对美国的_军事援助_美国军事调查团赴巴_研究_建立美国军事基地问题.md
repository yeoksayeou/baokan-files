Title: Pakistani Farmers Oppose U.S. "Military Aid"; U.S. Military Survey Mission Visits Pakistan to "Study" the Establishment of U.S. Military Bases
Author:
Date: 1954-03-18
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, March 17th dispatch from Karachi: "Today's News" published a statement on the 12th from He B. Jatoi, Chairman of the Sindh Province Farmers' Committee, vehemently criticizing the United States for giving Pakistan military "aid." The statement pointed out that the Turkish-Pakistani agreement, orchestrated by the United States, "constitutes a direct threat to Islamic countries. American policy not only threatens world peace but also aims to deceive Islamic countries."

Jatoi said: "Every action of the imperialists contains their own interests. American aid to Pakistan means American domination over Pakistan." Jatoi condemned the United States for bringing bombs, shells, and tanks to Pakistan. He said that Pakistan "needs peace and industrial development. We should live peacefully and amicably with our neighbors such as India, Afghanistan, Iran, the Soviet Union, and China. Any action that undermines this peaceful and friendly relationship is against our interests and should not be done by us."

Jatoi continued: Turkey is the first country in the Middle East to be ruled by the United States because it accepted American "aid." It sent troops to fight in Korea, most of whom died and were buried there. This is an example of sacrificing the blood of one's own youth to please American and British imperialism.

Xinhua News Agency, March 17th: Regardless of the strong opposition from the broad masses of people in Asian countries, the United States is stepping up its aggressive activities of providing military "aid" to Pakistan. According to a United Press International dispatch from Washington, a U.S. military survey mission departed for Pakistan on the 15th to study "what kind of military aid" the United States should give to Pakistan. The delegation will first hold talks with Iskander Mirza, Secretary-General of the Pakistan Ministry of Defense, when it passes through London.

The United States' activities to "aid" Pakistan are closely related to the United States' conspiracy to transform Pakistan into a U.S. military base. According to an International News Service dispatch from Washington on the 13th, quoting "authoritative sources," the United States is about to negotiate with Pakistan on "the right to establish air force bases in Pakistan," and relevant "exploratory talks" are already underway.

### 巴基斯坦农民反对美国的“军事援助”  美国军事调查团赴巴“研究”建立美国军事基地问题

1954-03-18
第4版()
专栏：

    巴基斯坦农民反对美国的“军事援助”
    美国军事调查团赴巴“研究”建立美国军事基地问题
   【新华社十七日讯】喀喇蚩消息：“今天报”十二日发表了信德省农民委员会主席赫·布·贾托伊的声明，声明中猛烈抨击美国给予巴基斯坦军事“援助”，并指出，美国策划下的土巴协定“构成了对回教国家的直接威胁。美国的政策不但威胁着世界和平，同时也旨在欺骗回教国家”。
    贾托伊说：帝国主义者的“每一个行动都包藏着自己的利益，美国对巴基斯坦的援助，即意味着美国对巴基斯坦的统治”。贾托伊谴责了美国要把炸弹、炮弹和坦克带给巴基斯坦，他说，巴基斯坦“需要和平与工业的发展，我们应当与我们的邻国如印度、阿富汗、伊朗、苏联和中国等和平友好地相处，任何足以破坏这一和平友好关系的行为，都是违反我们的利益的，我们都不应该去做”。
    贾托伊接着说：土耳其是中东国家第一个因接受美“援”而被美国所统治的国家，它派了军队去朝鲜作战，大多数都死在那里，埋在那里，这是一个为了讨好美、英帝国主义而使本国青年流血的实例。
    【新华社十七日讯】不顾亚洲各国广大阶层人民的强烈反对，美国仍加紧对巴基斯坦进行军事“援助”的侵略活动。据合众社华盛顿消息，一个美国军事调查团已于十五日动身赴巴基斯坦，以研究美国给予巴基斯坦“那一类的军事援助”。这个代表团路过伦敦时将首先与巴基斯坦国防部秘书长伊斯坎德尔·米尔扎举行会谈。
    美国“援助”巴基斯坦的活动，是与美国变巴基斯坦为美国的军事基地的阴谋密切相关的。据国际新闻社华盛顿十三日电引“权威人士”的谈话透露：美国即将和巴基斯坦谈判“在巴基斯坦建立空军基地的权利问题”，而且有关的“探讨性会谈”已经在进行。
