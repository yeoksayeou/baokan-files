Title: In the Economic Competition Between Two Social Systems, Socialist Countries Will Win in the Shortest Historical Period · Commentary from the Soviet Journal "Economic Issues" ·
Author:
Date: 1956-09-05
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, Moscow, September 4th. A recent article in the Soviet journal "Economic Issues" pointed out that in the economic competition with world capitalist countries, countries of the world socialist system can achieve victory in the shortest historical period.

The article's authors, Alekseev and Bogomolov, pointed out that socialist camp countries today already possess strong productive forces. In 1955, the total electricity production of the Soviet Union, China, and the European People's Democratic countries had already increased to 263 billion kilowatt-hours, coal to 689 million tons, steel to 63 million tons, oil to 85 million tons, and cement to 44 million tons.

The development speed of industrial production in the Soviet Union and the People's Democratic countries is much faster than that of capitalist countries. In 1955, industrial production in socialist countries almost doubled compared to 1950, with an average annual growth rate of 15%, while in capitalist countries, industrial production only increased by 31% during the same period, with an annual growth rate of less than 6%, and even in the best years of 1951 and 1955, it did not exceed 10%.

Due to the rapid development speed, in 1955, the proportion of industrial production in socialist countries in the world's total industrial output had greatly increased. Among them, coal accounted for about two-fifths, steel about one-quarter, cement one-fifth, electricity more than one-sixth, and oil one-ninth.

The authors pointed out that the degree of production development in various socialist countries is not the same. For countries with particularly developed industrial production such as the Soviet Union, Czechoslovakia, and the German Democratic Republic, striving to solve the task of catching up with and surpassing advanced capitalist countries in terms of industrial output per capita is entirely realistic. For example, Czechoslovakia's electricity and steel production per capita is now close to the level of the United Kingdom, and its and Poland's coal production greatly exceeds the level of the United States; in terms of oil production, the Soviet Union, Poland, Czechoslovakia, Hungary, and Romania all produce much more than the United Kingdom. If compared with the eighteen countries in the so-called "Organization for European Economic Cooperation" such as the United Kingdom, France, Italy, and the Netherlands, then in 1955 the Soviet Union and the European People's Democratic countries had much higher coal and oil production per capita, and the electricity and steel production was very close to the aforementioned countries.

### 在两种社会制度的经济竞赛中  社会主义国家将在最短历史时期内获胜  ·苏联“经济问题”杂志的评论·

1956-09-05
第5版()
专栏：

    在两种社会制度的经济竞赛中
    社会主义国家将在最短历史时期内获胜
    ·苏联“经济问题”杂志的评论·
    新华社莫斯科4日电　最近一期苏联“经济问题”杂志在一篇文章中指出，在同世界资本主义国家展开的经济竞赛中，世界社会主义体系国家可以在最短的历史时期内取得胜利。
    这篇文章的作者阿列克谢耶夫和保戈莫洛夫指出，今天社会主义阵营国家已经拥有强大的生产力。1955年，苏联、中国和欧洲各人民民主国家的电力生产总量已经增加到两千六百三十亿度，煤增加到六亿八千九百万吨，钢增加到六千三百万吨，石油增加到八千五百万吨，水泥增加到四千四百万吨。
    苏联和各人民民主国家的工业生产的发展速度比资本主义国家快得多。1955年，社会主义国家的工业生产几乎比1950年增加了一倍，每年平均增长速度为15％，而在资本主义世界各国，工业生产在同一时期只不过增加了31％，每年增长速度还不到6％，甚至在最好的1951年和1955年也没有超过10％。
    由于发展速度快，1955年，社会主义体系国家的工业生产在全世界工业总产量中所占的比重已经大大提高了，其中，煤约占五分之二，钢约占四分之一，水泥占五分之一，电力占六分之一以上，石油占九分之一。
    作者指出，各社会主义国家生产发展的程度是不相同的。对苏联、捷克斯洛伐克、德意志民主共和国这样一些工业生产特别发达的国家来说，争取解决在工业产量按人口平均计算方面赶上和超过先进资本主义国家的任务是完全现实的。例如，按人口平均计算，捷克斯洛伐克的电力和钢产量现在已经接近英国的水平，它和波兰的煤产量都大大地超过了美国的水平；在石油产量方面，苏联、波兰、捷克斯洛伐克、匈牙利和罗马尼亚等国都比英国多得多。如果和所谓“欧洲经济合作组织”中的英、法、意、荷等十八个国家比较，那么，1955年苏联和欧洲各人民民主国家按人口平均计算的煤和石油产量要多得多，而电力和钢的产量非常接近于上述国家。
