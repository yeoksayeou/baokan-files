Title: Japan Unwilling to Participate in Any Plan Disapproved by Egypt, Not Bound by Resolution on "Users' Association"
Author:
Date: 1956-09-22
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, Tokyo, September 21st: Foreign Minister Mamoru Shigemitsu said today that Japan is unwilling to participate in any plan for resolving the Suez Canal issue that is not approved by Egypt.

Shigemitsu told reporters after a cabinet meeting that although Japan participated in the London conference, it did so only to hear the results of the Five-Nation Committee's negotiations with Egypt, and is unwilling to be bound by the resolution regarding the "Users' Association." Japan should not decide what attitude to take toward the plan until it is convinced that the establishment of the "Users' Association" will help peacefully resolve the Suez Canal issue.

At the same time, many Japanese shipping companies have stated that, from a commercial point of view, they see no reason to participate in the "Users' Association" planned by Western countries. Some company representatives pointed out that they are satisfied with the current situation of ships passing through the canal.

### 日本不愿参加埃及不赞成的任何计划  并不受关于“使用国协会”的决议所约束

1956-09-22
第6版()
专栏：

    日本不愿参加埃及不赞成的任何计划
    并不受关于“使用国协会”的决议所约束
    新华社21日讯　东京消息：日本外务相重光葵今天说，日本不愿意参加任何埃及所不赞成的解决苏伊士运河问题的计划。
    重光在内阁会议以后接见记者的时候说，日本虽然参加了这次伦敦会议，但这只是为了听取五国委员会同埃及谈判的结果，并不愿意受关于“使用国协会”的决议的约束。日本在相信成立“使用国协会”的计划有助于和平解决苏伊士运河问题以前，不应该决定对这个计划采取什么态度。
    同时，日本许多航运公司表示，从商业观点来看，他们看不出有什么理由要参加西方国家策划成立的“使用国协会”。有的公司的代表指出，他们对目前船只在运河上通航的情况感到满意。
