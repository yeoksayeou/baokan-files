Title: World Peace Council Presidium Calls for Peaceful Resolution of the Suez Canal Issue
Author:
Date: 1956-09-26
Page: 1

Xinhua News Agency, September 25th, Vienna dispatch: The Presidium of the World Peace Council issued the following appeal on September 24th:

    World public opinion is deeply concerned about the possible consequences of the dispute arising from the management and use of the Suez Canal.

    The World Peace Council believes that this concern is justified because the development of the dispute may threaten peace.

    The World Peace Council warns all relevant governments, especially those taking measures that may turn this dispute into a conflict. Those who are willing to provoke conflict should know that such a conflict will lead to military action in various parts of the world, and military action may expand and become a real war, threatening the fate of millions of people.

    The World Peace Council declares that only through negotiations can a solution be found that guarantees freedom of navigation on the Suez Canal for ships of all countries without any discrimination, while also guaranteeing Egypt's sovereignty.

    If the negotiations of recent weeks have failed to achieve good results, this cannot be a reason for using force, but in this case it is necessary to ask the United Nations for assistance; in accordance with the spirit of the Charter, the United Nations has the mission of mediating disputes that threaten peace.

    The World Peace Council calls on governments to use all their influence to ensure that this dispute is resolved peacefully.

    Frédéric Joliot-Curie
    (Representing the Presidium of the World Peace Council)
          Paris, September 24, 1956

### 世界和平理事会主席团  呼吁和平解决苏伊士运河问题

1956-09-26
第1版()
专栏：

    世界和平理事会主席团
    呼吁和平解决苏伊士运河问题
    新华社25日讯　塔斯社维也纳讯：世界和平理事会主席团9月24日发表呼吁书如下：
    世界公众对由于管理和使用苏伊士运河问题而引起的纠纷可能产生的后果深感不安。
    世界和平理事会认为这种不安是有根据的，因为纠纷的发展可能使和平遭到威胁。
    世界和平理事会向一切有关政府、特别是向那些正在采取可能使这种纠纷变成冲突的措施的政府提出警告。那些不惜挑起冲突的人应当知道，这种冲突在世界各个地区将会导致军事行动，而军事行动可能扩大和变成真正的战争，使千百万人的命运受到威胁。
    世界和平理事会声明，只有通过谈判的方式才能找出既能保证一切国家的船只在苏伊士运河上的航行自由，不受任何歧视，同时又能保证埃及的主权的解决办法。
    如果说最近几个星期的谈判未能获得良好的结果，这决不能成为使用武力的理由，但是，在这种情况下就有必要请联合国给予帮助；根据宪章的精神，联合国负有调解威胁和平的纠纷的使命。
    世界和平理事会呼吁各国政府利用它们的一切影响，使这种纠纷通过和平的方式获得解决。
    弗雷德里克·约里奥—居里
    （代表世界和平理事会主席团）
          1956年9月24日于巴黎
