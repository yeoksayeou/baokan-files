Title: Italian Communist MPs Demand Government Not Participate in "Users' Association"
Author:
Date: 1956-09-17
Page: 8

Xinhua News Agency, Rome, September 15th: TASS reports that Italian Communist Party MPs Amendola, Ingrao, and Natoli submitted a proposal signed by Communist Party MPs to the Chamber of Deputies, demanding that the Italian government not participate in the "Suez Canal Users' Association," and take the necessary steps to resolve the Suez Canal issue dispute through negotiation. The proposal also requests the government to declare: "Italy condemns any action that attempts to use force to compel the other party to accept a solution unilaterally proposed by a major power."
      "National Evening News" published a statement by the General Secretary of the Communist Party, Togliatti, demanding that the Chamber of Deputies, which is currently in recess, immediately convene a meeting to discuss the proposal of the Communist Party MPs.

### 意共议员要求政府  不参加“使用国协会”

1956-09-17
第8版()
专栏：

    意共议员要求政府
    不参加“使用国协会”
    据新华社16日讯　据塔斯社罗马15日讯：意大利共产党议员阿门多拉、英格拉奥和纳托利向众议院提出一件由共产党议员签名的提案，要求意大利政府不要参加“苏彝士运河使用国协会”，并且采取必要的步骤通过协商解决苏彝士运河问题上的争端。提案还要求政府声明：“意大利谴责任何企图以武力强迫对方接受大国单方面提出的解决办法的行动。”
      “国家晚报”刊载共产党总书记陶里亚蒂的声明，要求正在休会的众议院立即召开会议讨论共产党议员的提案。
