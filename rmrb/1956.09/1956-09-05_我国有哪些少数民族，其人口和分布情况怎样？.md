Title: What are the National Minorities in China, and What is Their Population and Distribution?
Author: 
Date: 1956-09-05
Page: 4

    A reader asked:
    Our country is a unified multi-ethnic nation. In our country, besides the Han people, who make up over 90% of the total population, there are dozens of minority ethnic groups. The total population of all minority ethnic groups is more than 35 million, among which ten ethnic groups have a population of over one million, including Mongol, Hui, Tibetan, Uygur, Miao, Yi, Zhuang, Buyi, Korean, and Manchu; those with a population of less than one million include Kazakh, Dong, Bai, Dai, Kawa, Hani, Yao, Li, Dongxiang, Kyrgyz, Tu, Lisu, Nong, Naxi, Lahu, Shui, Jingpo, Qiang, Gaoshan, Salar, Russian, Xibe, Uzbek, Tajik, Yugur, Tatar, Bonan, Oroqen, Daur, Solon, Maonan, Mulao, Nu, Derung, and Gelao. Among the various ethnic minorities in our country, the Zhuang ethnic group has the largest population, with more than 6.6 million people. Some ethnic minorities have very small populations, such as the Oroqen, with only over 2,000 people. Regardless of population size, all ethnic groups have equal status and enjoy equal rights. They live together like brothers in the big family of our great motherland.
    The areas inhabited by the various ethnic minorities in our country account for 60% of our country's total area, and most of them live in border areas. The Mongolian people are mainly distributed in the Inner Mongolia Autonomous Region, followed by parts of Liaoning, Jilin, Heilongjiang, Xinjiang Uygur Autonomous Region, and Qinghai and Gansu provinces. The Hui people are mainly distributed in Gansu, Henan, Hebei, Shandong, Qinghai, Yunnan and other provinces, and the Xinjiang Uygur Autonomous Region, followed by other parts of the country. The Tibetan people are distributed in Tibet and parts of Sichuan, Qinghai, Gansu, and Yunnan. The Uygur people are distributed in the Xinjiang Uygur Autonomous Region. The Miao people are mainly distributed in Guizhou Province and western Hunan Province, followed by parts of Guangxi, Yunnan, Guangdong and other provinces. The Yi people are mainly distributed in the Greater and Lesser Liangshan areas on the border of Sichuan and Yunnan provinces and most areas of Yunnan Province, followed by parts of Guizhou Province. The Zhuang people are distributed in Guangxi Province. The Buyi people are distributed in Guizhou Province. The Korean people are mainly distributed in eastern Jilin Province. The Manchu people are mainly distributed in Liaoning, Jilin, Heilongjiang, Hebei and other provinces, as well as cities such as the Inner Mongolia Autonomous Region, Beijing, and Tianjin. The Kazakh people are mainly distributed in the northern part of the Xinjiang Uygur Autonomous Region. The Dong people are distributed in parts of Hunan, Guangxi, Guizhou and other provinces. The Bai, Dai, Kawa, Hani, Lisu, Nong, Naxi, Lahu, Nu, and Derung peoples are distributed in Yunnan Province. The Yao people are mainly distributed in Guangxi, followed by parts of Guangdong, Hunan and other provinces. The Li people are distributed in the southern part of Hainan Island, Guangdong Province. The Dongxiang, Bonan, and Yugur peoples are distributed in Gansu Province. The Tu and Salar peoples are distributed in Qinghai Province. The Shui people are distributed in southern Guizhou Province. The Qiang people are distributed in northwestern Sichuan Province. The Russian, Xibe, Tajik, Uzbek, and Tatar peoples are distributed in the Xinjiang Uygur Autonomous Region. The Oroqen people are distributed in the eastern part of the Inner Mongolia Autonomous Region and the northern part of Heilongjiang Province. The Daur people are distributed in parts of the Inner Mongolia Autonomous Region, Heilongjiang Province, and the Xinjiang Uygur Autonomous Region. The Solon people are distributed in the Morin Dawa Banner of the Inner Mongolia Autonomous Region and near the Greater Khingan Mountains. The Maonan and Mulao peoples are distributed in the northern part of Guangxi Province. The Gelao people are distributed in western Guizhou Province. The Gaoshan people are distributed in Taiwan.

### 我国有哪些少数民族，其人口和分布情况怎样？

1956-09-05
第4版()
专栏：答读者问

    我国有哪些少数民族，其人口和分布情况怎样？
    我国是一个统一的多民族的国家。在我们国家里，除了占全国总人口90％以上的汉族外，还有几十个少数民族。各少数民族的总人口有三千五百多万，其中人口在一百万以上的有蒙古、回、藏、维吾尔、苗、彝、僮、布依、朝鲜、满等十个民族；人口在一百万以下的，有哈萨克、侗、白、傣、佧佤、哈尼、瑶、黎、东乡、柯尔克孜、土、傈僳、侬、纳西、拉祜、水家、景颇、羌、高山、撒拉、俄罗斯、锡伯、乌孜别克、塔吉克，裕固、塔塔尔、保安、鄂伦春、达呼尔、索伦、毛难、仫佬、怒、独龙、仡佬等几十个民族。我国各少数民族中人口最多的如僮族，有六百六十多万人，有一些少数民族的人口是很少的，例如鄂伦春族只有两千多人。无论人口多少，各民族都居于平等的地位，享有平等的权利。他们都像兄弟般的共同生活在我们伟大祖国的大家庭里。
    我国各少数民族居住的地区占着我国总面积的60％，而且大多居住在边疆地区。蒙古族主要分布在内蒙古自治区，其次分布在辽宁、吉林、黑龙江、新疆维吾尔自治区和青海、甘肃等省的部分地区，回族主要分布在甘肃、河南、河北、山东、青海、云南等省和新疆维吾尔自治区，其次分布在全国其他各地。藏族分布在西藏和四川、青海、甘肃、云南的部分地区。维吾尔族分布在新疆维吾尔自治区。苗族主要分布在贵州省和湖南省的西部，其次分布在广西、云南、广东等省的部分地区。彝族主要分布在四川省和云南省交界的大小凉山地区和云南省的大部分地区，其次分布在贵州省的部分地区。僮族分布在广西省。布依族分布在贵州省。朝鲜族主要分布在吉林省的东部。满族主要分布在辽宁、吉林、黑龙江、河北等省，内蒙古自治区和北京、天津等城市。哈萨克族主要分布在新疆维吾尔自治区北部。侗族分布在湖南、广西、贵州等省的部分地区。白族、傣族、佧佤族、哈尼族、傈僳族、侬族、纳西族和拉祜族、怒族、独龙族等分布在云南省。瑶族主要分布在广西，其次分布在广东、湖南等省的部分地区。黎族分布在广东省的海南岛南部。东乡族、保安族、裕固族分布在甘肃省。土族、撒拉族分布在青海省。水家族分布在贵州省的南部。羌族分布在四川省的西北部。俄罗斯族、锡伯族、塔吉克族、乌孜别克族和塔塔尔族分布在新疆维吾尔自治区。鄂伦春族分布在内蒙古自治区东部和黑龙江省北部地区。达呼尔族分布在内蒙古自治区、黑龙江省和新疆维吾尔自治区的部分地区。索伦族分布在内蒙古自治区的莫力达瓦旗及大兴安岭附近。毛难族和仫佬族分布在广西省北部。仡佬族分布在贵州省西部。高山族分布在台湾。
