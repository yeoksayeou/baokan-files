Title: Gaitskell Opposes Provocative Actions by British Government. British Newspapers Warn British and French Governments Not to Use Force to Threaten Egypt.
Author:
Date: 1956-09-02
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, 1st September, London dispatch: British Labour Party leader Hugh Gaitskell, in an interview with a reporter from "The Manchester Guardian" on August 31, said that the British government "should avoid taking any provocative actions" in negotiations with Egypt regarding the Suez issue.

Gaitskell said that the London conference made people realize that the idea of forcing Egypt to accept a solution by force "cannot be supported by other countries – not even by our Western European allies."

Gaitskell believes that even if Egypt rejects the Dulles plan for "international control" of the Suez Canal, which the "Five-Nation Committee" will propose in Cairo, there is "no reason to go to war." He said that the West should "rely on economic measures" to force Egypt to accept the West's proposals, "although such measures may work slowly."

Xinhua News Agency, 1st September, London dispatch: Many British newspapers on August 31 warned against the increasing military pressure being applied to Egypt by Britain and France.

The Labour Party's "Daily Herald" said that the concentration of troops by Britain and France in Cyprus demonstrates the seriousness of the situation, and this action "endangers the opportunity for a peaceful resolution of the Suez Canal issue" and undermines people's confidence in the British government's ability to handle the issue responsibly and keep Britain out of the danger of war.

The Liberal Party's "Manchester Guardian" said:

"The government seems prepared to go to war immediately if Menzies' mission fails." "It does not want to spend time reopening the London conference. It may not even intend, as Gaitskell hopes, to wait for a time to consult with Parliament."

"The Manchester Guardian" continued: The consequences of the government's policy will be: damaging the alliance with the United States, splitting the Commonwealth, sacrificing the lives of many British and Egyptian people, the economic isolation of Britain and France, or the end of the United Nations, and the loss of oil..."

Xinhua News Agency, 1st September, London dispatch: The General Council of the British Trades Union Congress passed a special resolution on August 31 stating that if negotiations between the "Five-Nation Committee" and Egypt break down, "force should not be used until the issue is brought before the United Nations and receives the consent of the United Nations."

The resolution said that a solution to the Suez Canal issue should recognize Egypt's sovereign rights and also recognize the extreme importance of the canal in world affairs.

### 盖次克尔反对英政府挑衅行动  英国报纸警告英法两国政府不要用武力威胁埃及

1956-09-02
第6版()
专栏：

    盖次克尔反对英政府挑衅行动
    英国报纸警告英法两国政府不要用武力威胁埃及
    新华社1日讯　伦敦消息：英国工党领袖盖次克尔8月31日接见“曼彻斯特卫报”记者的时候说：英国政府在同埃及的关于苏彝士问题的谈判中“应该避免采取任何方式的挑衅行动”。
    盖次克尔说，伦敦会议使人们认识到，用武力强迫埃及接受一种解决办法的主张“是得不到其他国家支持的——甚至得不到我们的西欧盟国的支持。”
    盖次克尔认为，即使埃及拒绝了“五国委员会”将在开罗提出的关于“国际管制”苏彝士运河的杜勒斯计划，也“没有理由进行战争”。他说，西方应当“依靠经济措施”来迫使埃及接受西方的主张，“尽管这种措施起的作用缓慢”。
    据新华社1日讯　伦敦消息：许多英国报纸8月31日对英法两国加紧对埃及施加军事压力一事提出警告。
    工党的“每日先驱报”说，英法两国在塞浦路斯集结军队说明了形势的严重性，这种行动“危害了和平解决苏彝士运河问题的机会”，并且动摇了人们对英国政府能够负责地处理问题、使英国不冒战争危险的信心。
    自由党的“曼彻斯特卫报”说：
  “政府似乎准备，如果孟席斯的使命失败就立刻开战”。“它并不希望花费时间重开伦敦会议。它可能甚至不打算像盖次克尔所希望的那样，等待一个时候以便同议会磋商。”
      “曼彻斯特卫报”接着说：政府的政策的后果将是：破坏同美国的联盟，分裂英联邦，牺牲许多英国人和埃及人的生命，英法两国的经济孤立或者联合国完蛋，失去石油……”
    新华社1日讯　伦敦消息：英国职工大会全国委员会8月31日通过特别决议说，如果“五国委员会”同埃及的谈判一旦破裂，“在这个问题没有提到联合国并且没有得到联合国的同意之前，不应该使用武力。”
    决议说，解决苏彝士运河问题的办法应该承认埃及的主权权利，也承认运河在世界事务中的极端重要性。
