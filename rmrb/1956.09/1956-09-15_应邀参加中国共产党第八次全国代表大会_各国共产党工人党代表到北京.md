Title: Representatives of Communist and Workers' Parties from Various Countries Arrive in Beijing to Attend the Eighth National Congress of the Communist Party of China
Author:
Date: 1956-09-15
Page: 1
Xinhua News Agency, September 14th. At the invitation of the Central Committee of the Communist Party of China, delegations, representatives, and observers of Communist and Workers' Parties from various countries have arrived in Beijing one after another to attend the Eighth National Congress of the Communist Party of China. A total of fifty-six Communist and Workers' Parties from various countries were invited to attend the Eighth National Congress of the Communist Party of China. In addition, observers from the Communist Party of the United States are attending the congress as non-voting members. As of the 14th, delegations, representatives, and observers from forty-six countries have arrived in Beijing. Representatives from some other Communist and Workers' Parties are on their way to China.

    The head of the delegation of the Communist Party of the Soviet Union is Mikoyan, member of the Presidium of the Central Committee of the CPSU and First Deputy Chairman of the Council of Ministers of the Soviet Union. The members are: Mukhitdinov, alternate member of the Presidium of the Central Committee of the CPSU; Ponomarev, member of the Central Committee; Kapitonov, member of the Central Committee; Yudin, member of the Central Committee and Soviet Ambassador to China; and Satyukov, Deputy Editor-in-Chief of "Pravda." The head of the delegation of the Polish United Workers' Party is Ochab, First Secretary of the Polish United Workers' Party. The members are: Jóźwiak-Witold, member of the Political Bureau of the Polish United Workers' Party; Professor Lange, member of the Central Committee; Marzec, Minister of Light Industry of the Central Committee of the Party; and Kirylok, Polish Ambassador to China.
