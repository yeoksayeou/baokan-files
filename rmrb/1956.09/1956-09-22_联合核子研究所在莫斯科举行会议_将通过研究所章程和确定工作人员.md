Title: Joint Institute for Nuclear Research Holds Meeting in Moscow; To Approve Institute Charter and Determine Staff
Author:
Date: 1956-09-22
Page: 5

    Joint Institute for Nuclear Research Holds Meeting in Moscow
    To Approve Institute Charter and Determine Staff

    (People's Daily, Moscow, 21st) The Joint Institute for Nuclear Research held a meeting of plenipotentiary representatives of member states in Moscow at 11:00 a.m. on September 20th. Participating in the meeting were twelve countries including Albania, Bulgaria, Hungary, the Democratic Republic of Vietnam, the German Democratic Republic, China, North Korea, Mongolia, Poland, Romania, the Soviet Union, and Czechoslovakia. The Joint Institute for Nuclear Research was established in March of this year based on agreements between the relevant countries.

    At the opening session on the 20th, the two items on the agenda for this meeting were adopted: 1. Discussion and adoption of the charter of the Joint Institute for Nuclear Research; 2. Determination of the staff of the Joint Institute for Nuclear Research.

    The opening address at the 20th meeting was given by the Director of the Institute, Sergei Yekov, who said that in the six months since the establishment of the Institute, the Institute has received assets totaling 500,200,000 rubles transferred to it by the Soviet government, including a synchrocyclotron capable of accelerating protons to an energy of 680 million electron volts, as well as laboratories and other equipment. He also said that during this period, the Director's Office drafted the draft charter of the Institute, formulated the long-term plan for scientific research of the Institute, formulated the budget, and submitted the list of staff.

    Based on the proposal of the delegation of the German Democratic Republic, the meeting approved the candidates for the Presidium of the meeting, namely: Wang Ganchang, Deputy Director of the Institute of Physics of the Chinese Academy of Sciences, Slavsky, Chairman of the Soviet Council of Ministers' Atomic Energy Commission, and Ka Reznik, Deputy Secretary-General of the Czechoslovak Academy of Sciences. The secretariat of the meeting was also established at the meeting. The secretariat consisted of representatives from the Soviet Union, China, Czechoslovakia, Romania, and Hungary, with the Soviet representative serving as Secretary-General and the Chinese representative serving as Deputy Secretary-General.

    At the suggestion of Professor Blokhintsev, Director of the Joint Institute, the meeting unanimously approved the Democratic Republic of Vietnam as a formal member of the Joint Institute for Nuclear Research.

    Subsequently, the meeting entered the discussion of the first item on the agenda, and representatives from various countries put forward opinions and suggestions on the draft charter.

### 联合核子研究所在莫斯科举行会议  将通过研究所章程和确定工作人员

1956-09-22
第5版()
专栏：

    联合核子研究所在莫斯科举行会议
    将通过研究所章程和确定工作人员
    本报莫斯科21日电　联合核子研究所9月20日上午十一时在莫斯科举行成员国全权代表会议。参加会议的有阿尔巴尼亚、保加利亚、匈牙利、越南民主共和国、德意志民主共和国、中国、朝鲜、蒙古、波兰、罗马尼亚、苏联和捷克斯洛伐克等十二个国家。联合核子研究所是在今年3月根据各有关国家的协议而成立的。
    在20日的开幕会议上，通过了本届会议的两项议程：一、讨论和通过联合核子研究所的章程；二、确定联合核子研究所的工作人员。
    20日的会议由研究所的执行所长西尔盖也科致开幕词，他说，在研究所成立后的半年来，研究所得到了由苏联政府移交给它的总额达五亿二百万零二千卢布的资财，其中包括能把质子加速到能量达六亿八千万电子伏特的同步回旋加速器以及实验室等等设备。他并说，在这段时期内，所长室草拟了研究所的章程草案，制定了研究所的科学研究远景规划，制定了预算和提出了工作人员名单。
    会议根据德意志民主共和国代表团的提议，通过了会议主席团的人选，他们是：中国科学院物理研究所副所长王淦昌、苏联部长会议利用原子能委员会主席斯拉夫斯基和捷克斯洛伐克科学院副秘书长卡热斯尼克。会上并成立了会议的秘书处。秘书处由苏联、中国、捷克斯洛伐克、罗马尼亚、匈牙利的代表组成，苏联代表任秘书长，中国代表任副秘书长。
    会议在联合研究所所长勃洛欣采夫教授的提议下，一致通过越南民主共和国为联合核子研究所正式会员国。
    接着，会议进入第一项议程的讨论，各国代表对章程草案提出了意见和建议。
