Title: Czechoslovakia Decides to Shorten Workers' Work Hours
Author:
Date: 1956-09-08
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, Prague, 7th: According to the Czechoslovak News Agency, the Czechoslovak government passed a bill on the 6th, deciding to shorten the working hours of employees to 46 hours per week, starting from October 1st this year.

    The bill stipulates that, in principle, employees will work six days a week, with shorter working hours on Saturdays than usual. In mining enterprises and other enterprises that must operate continuously, workers, in addition to their Sunday rest day, will also be entitled to one day of leave after completing twenty-three eight-hour shifts. Workers receiving hourly wages will also benefit from this new system. For young people under the age of sixteen, working hours are stipulated as thirty-six hours per week. This bill will be submitted to the National Assembly for discussion.

### 捷决定缩短工人工作时间

1956-09-08
第5版()
专栏：

    捷决定缩短工人工作时间
    新华社布拉格7日电　据捷克斯洛伐克通讯社消息：捷克斯洛伐克政府6日通过一项法案，决定从今年10月1日起把职工的工作时间缩短为每周四十六小时。
    法案规定，原则上职工每周的工作日是六天，星期六的劳动时间比通常要短些。在采矿业企业和其他必须不间断地进行工作的企业里，工人们除了每星期日的休假外，每做满二十三个八小时一班的班期以后，还可以有一天休假。领计时工资的工人也将从这一新制度中获得好处。对于年龄在十六岁以下的青年人，工作时间规定为每周三十六小时。这一法案将提交国民议会讨论。
