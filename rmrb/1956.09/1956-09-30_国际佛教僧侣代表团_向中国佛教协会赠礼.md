Title: International Buddhist Monks Delegation Presents Gifts to the Buddhist Association of China
Author:
Date: 1956-09-30
Page: 3
Xinhua News Agency, 29th — The International Buddhist Monks Delegation presented gifts to the Buddhist Association of China at the Yonghe Temple in Beijing this morning, and two members gave sermons in a religious ceremony.

    Before the presentation of gifts, the eleven masters from seven Asian countries lit lamps, offered flowers, and recited auspicious sutras in front of the Buddha statue in the main hall.

    Then, in the Hall of the Wheel of Dharma, brightly lit with candles and hung with colorful banners, the foreign masters presented gifts, including a Bodhi tree sapling taken from the Bodhi field where Sakyamuni attained enlightenment, Buddha statues, publications of the Indian Maha Bodhi Society, and books from Vietnam.

    The delegation's deputy head, Indian Venerable Niruwe Jinaratana, Thai Phra Maha Obons Obhaso, and Nepalese Asaghosha delivered speeches during the presentation, mentioning the traditional friendship between Buddhists of various countries and wishing for the development of this friendship, the prosperity of the People's Republic of China, and lasting world peace.

    Master Xiraojiacuo, president of the Buddhist Association of China, said that these gifts represent the sincere friendship of the vast number of Buddhists from various countries towards Chinese Buddhists. He presented gifts to the masters of the delegation on behalf of the Buddhist Association of China.

    The delegation's head, Badanda Ananda Kausalya Yana, said when accepting the gifts from the Buddhist Association of China that these gifts were "truly given in the spirit of almsgiving, and the masters of the delegation will use them in the most beneficial way for the public."

    After the lamas recited Tibetan scriptures, Thai Phra Maha Manas Jettatamo preached to more than 700 Han, Mongolian, and Tibetan monks, nuns, lamas, and believers. Jagdish Kashyap, Honorary Director of the Indian Nalandan Pali Institute, cited two stories from the Agama Sutra in his sermon, explaining that only under a good government and a good social environment can Buddhists live a pure life in accordance with the Dharma.

### 国际佛教僧侣代表团  向中国佛教协会赠礼

1956-09-30
第3版()
专栏：

    国际佛教僧侣代表团
    向中国佛教协会赠礼
    新华社29日讯　国际佛教僧侣代表团今天上午在北京雍和宫向中国佛教协会赠礼，两位团员并且在宗教仪式上说法。
    赠礼开始前，这十一位来自七个亚洲国家的法师在大殿内佛像前点灯、献花，并诵吉祥经。
    接着外国法师们在灯烛辉煌、彩幡高悬的法轮殿里赠送礼品，其中有采自释迦牟尼悟道所在地的菩提场的菩提树苗，佛像，印度摩诃菩提会出版的刊物和越南的书籍。
    代表团副团长印度尼鲁威·吉那拉塔那法师，泰国拔罗·摩诃·奥邦斯·奥巴梭法师和尼泊尔阿萨哥夏法师在赠礼时致词，他们提到各国佛教徒之间的传统友谊，并祝愿这种友谊发展，中华人民共和国繁荣昌盛，世界获得持久和平。
    中国佛教协会会长喜饶嘉错大师说，这些礼物代表着各国广大佛教徒对中国佛教徒的真挚友谊。他代表中国佛教协会向代表团各位法师赠送了礼物。
    代表团团长巴丹达·阿难陀·柯萨尔雅雅那法师在接受中国佛教协会的礼物时说，这些礼物是“真正本着布施的精神赠与的，代表团的法师们将把它们用在最有益于大众的方面去。”
    在喇嘛们诵藏文经后，泰国拔罗·摩诃·摩那斯·捷达塔摩法师向七百多汉、蒙古、藏族的比丘、比丘尼、喇嘛和信众说法。印度那灿陀巴利文研究院名誉董事贾格蒂西·迦什波在说法中引证了阿含经中的两段故事，说明只有在良好的政府和良好的社会情况下，佛教徒才能如法地过清净的生活。
