Title: Report on the Suggestions for the Second Five-Year Plan for National Economic Development
Author: Zhou Enlai
Date: 1956-09-19
Page: 1

The first five-year plan for the development of our national economy will be completed in about a year. To ensure that we can immediately and smoothly begin the construction of the second five-year plan after the completion of the first five-year plan, the Central Committee of the Party believes that it is necessary to start the work of formulating the second five-year plan as early as possible. Now, the Central Committee of the Party has submitted the suggestions for the development of the national economy in the second five-year plan to the Eighth National Congress of the Party for review. After this congress discusses and adopts these suggestions, they will be submitted to the State Council for discussion.

Regarding the basic principles and policies for the development of our national economy in the second five-year plan, these have already been explained in the political report delivered by Comrade Liu Shaoqi on behalf of the Central Committee of the Party. Now, I, on behalf of the Central Committee of the Party, will report to the congress on the suggestions for the second five-year plan.

I. The Situation of the Implementation of the First Five-Year Plan

Before I explain the suggestions for the second five-year plan, I would like to say a few words about the basic situation of the implementation of the first five-year plan.

In the process of implementing the first five-year plan, due to the efforts of the entire nation, especially the working people, our socialist construction and socialist transformation have achieved greater and faster victories than expected. Our achievements are enormous, but there have also been some shortcomings and errors in our work that we must strive to overcome.

In the field of basic construction, by the end of 1957, the total investment in basic construction nationwide is expected to exceed the original plan by more than 10%. Most of the construction units above the planned limit will be able to complete or even complete ahead of schedule. Additionally, several new construction units have been added each year. By the end of 1957, approximately 500 new and renovated industrial enterprises above the planned limit will be completed, thus increasing our country's industrial production capacity, establishing some new industrial sectors, and to a certain extent, renovating some existing sectors. This will begin to change the extremely backward face of our country's industry. After the construction of the first five-year plan, the northeastern industrial base centered around the Anshan Iron and Steel Combined Enterprise will be significantly strengthened, and many new industrial cities will begin to emerge in Inner Mongolia, Northwest, North China, and other regions. Five thousand five hundred kilometers of new and restored railways will be built within five years, with important trunk lines such as Jincheng to Erlian, Baoji to Chengdu already completed, and the railway from Yingtan to Xiamen will also be completed. The important highways such as the Kangding Line and the Qinghai-Tibet Line have also been fully opened to traffic. The completion of these railways and highways has strengthened the connection between the vast regions of Northwest and Southwest China and other regions of the country. Regarding water conservancy projects, we are continuing to control the Huai River, beginning the construction of the Sanmenxia Water Conservancy and Hydropower Hub on the Yellow River, and carrying out other large and many medium and small water conservancy projects. Many completed water conservancy projects have already begun to play a role in flood control and irrigation. Over the past few years, geological work has also achieved significant results, ensuring the needs of our country's basic construction. As mentioned above, the basic construction of our country's first five-year plan, in terms of investment and the progress of most important construction units, will be able to meet our predetermined requirements. However, it should also be noted that some sectors may not be able to complete the original investment plan, and some important construction units may not be able to complete the original progress plan. These sectors and construction units should strengthen their work in the next year or so to strive to complete the original plan as much as possible. It should also be noted that some construction units only focus on speed, neglecting quality and safety, resulting in poor engineering quality, many accidents, and waste. This is a lesson to be learned.

In the field of industrial production, the total industrial output value (including modern industry and handicraft industry output value, calculated at constant 1952 prices, the same below) has exceeded the annual plan every year. In 1956, it will reach the level of 1957 as planned in the five-year plan; by 1957, it is expected to exceed the original plan by about 15%. In terms of the output of major industrial products, by 1957, most products will exceed the original planned targets. For example, steel will reach 5.5 million tons, electricity generation will reach 18 billion kilowatt-hours, coal will reach 120 million tons, metal-cutting machine tools will reach 30,000 units, electric power equipment will reach 340,000 kilowatts, logs will reach 24 million cubic meters, cotton yarn will reach 560 million pounds, refined sugar will reach 800,000 tons, and paper will reach 800,000 tons. In terms of important new industrial products, some power generation equipment, metallurgical equipment, mining equipment, and new models of metal-cutting machine tools that our country could not produce before are now being produced; cars and jet aircraft that our country could not produce before are now being produced; large steel and alloy high-quality steel that our country could not produce before are now being partially produced. However, there are a few products, such as oil, sulfur, edible vegetable oil, cigarettes, and matches, due to insufficient raw materials, poor sales, or technical reasons, that may not be able to complete the original production plan.

In the field of agricultural production, in 1953 and 1954, many regions in our country suffered relatively severe natural disasters, and the agricultural production plans for these two years were not fully completed. However, the grain output still increased compared to the bumper year of 1952. In 1955, our country had a bumper harvest of agricultural products, with grain production (excluding soybeans, the same below) reaching 349.6 billion jin, cotton production reaching 30.36 million dan, and other agricultural products also increasing. In 1956, many regions suffered severe disasters such as floods, waterlogging, typhoons, and droughts, and some crops, especially cotton, suffered some losses. However, due to the high tide of cooperation in rural areas nationwide, regions that did not suffer disasters will increase production, and the total grain output for the whole country in 1956 is still expected to reach the level of 1957 as planned in the first five-year plan. As long as there are no major natural disasters or unexpected accidents in the next year or so, the output of major grain crops and some economic crops in 1957 will be able to exceed the original plan. However, the output of soybeans, peanuts, rapeseed, hemp, and some livestock numbers may not be able to complete the original plan, and we must take effective measures to strengthen these weak links.

In the fields of transportation and postal services, with the development of industrial and agricultural production and the expansion of the scale of basic construction, the volume of transportation and postal services has increased every year. It is expected that by 1957, the planned turnover of goods in major transportation sectors will be able to exceed the plan. However, due to the incomplete technical renovation of some existing lines and equipment, some lines and transportation hubs are currently experiencing transportation bottlenecks and even congestion, and efforts are being made to improve this situation.

In the field of commerce, with the continuous development of socialist commerce, a planned and organized market has been formed domestically, and its leading position has been increasingly consolidated. In 1956, compared to 1952, the total value of domestic social commodities will increase by 66.3%, and the total value of imports and exports will increase by 65%. It is expected that by 1957, the planned total retail value of domestic social commodities and the sales volume of most commodities, as well as the planned imports and exports, will be able to be completed, with some even exceeding the plan. Over the past few years, based on the development of production, the turnover of domestic commodities has been expanded, and for the most important few types of daily necessities, the policy of unified purchase and unified sales has been implemented, ensuring the supply of daily necessities for the people, thus basically maintaining price stability and promoting the development of industrial and agricultural production and the improvement of people's living standards. The main shortcomings in commercial work at present are the poor organization of commodity supply and poor management, resulting in the phenomenon of commodities sometimes being overstocked and sometimes out of stock.

In the fields of culture, education, scientific research, and health, these undertakings have all developed considerably over the past few years. It is expected that by 1957, except for a few, higher education, secondary education, primary education, scientific research, news, publishing, broadcasting, literature, arts, film, sports, and medical care will all be able to exceed the original planned targets. For example, by 1957, the number of students in higher education institutions will reach approximately 470,000, exceeding the original target by about 9%; the number of research institutions of the Chinese Academy of Sciences will reach 68, which is 17 more than the original target.

In the field of agricultural and handicraft cooperativization, by the end of June 1956, 992,000 agricultural production cooperatives have been organized nationwide, accounting for 91.7% of the total number of rural households nationwide, of which 62.6% have joined senior cooperatives. The number of organized handicraft workers accounts for about 90% of the total number of handicraft workers. We estimate that after another year or so of work, by the end of 1957, except for a few border regions, agricultural and handicraft cooperativization can be basically achieved nationwide.

In the field of socialist transformation of private industry and commerce, by the end of June 1956, in the capitalist industry, 99% of the output value and 98% of the workforce in enterprises have realized joint state-private ownership; in the private commercial and catering industries, 68% of the total number of establishments and 74% of the total number of employees have been converted into joint state-private stores, cooperative stores, and cooperative groups. The implementation of joint state-private ownership and the fixed interest system in the capitalist industry and commerce has laid the foundation for the nationalization of capitalist means of production.

Here, I would like to briefly describe the improvement of the people's material living standards.

Over the past few years, from an overall perspective, the growth rate of workers' wages has basically been in line with the growth rate of labor productivity. However, there was a period when the growth rate of wages was significantly lower than the growth rate of labor productivity. For example, in 1955 compared to 1954, the labor productivity in the industrial sector (excluding private industry) increased by about 10%, while the average wage of workers only increased by 0.6%. Other sectors had similar situations. This error in work was discovered by us at the end of 1955, and we immediately began to correct it. Starting from April 1956, we implemented a wage system reform nationwide and determined that the average wage of workers in 1956 would be increased by about 13% compared to 1955. Thus, the average wage of workers in 1956 was increased by 33.5% compared to 1952, exceeding the target of a 33% increase in wages over five years as planned in the five-year plan. The growth of labor productivity will also exceed the original plan. For example, in the state-owned industrial sector, labor productivity in 1956 will be increased by 70.4% compared to 1952, exceeding the target of a 64% increase in labor productivity over five years as planned in the five-year plan.

Over the past few years, we have stabilized the agricultural tax burden on farmers and appropriately increased the purchase price of grain, thereby gradually improving farmers' living standards based on the development of production. However, we have also made mistakes. In 1954, due to our failure to fully understand the national grain production situation, we purchased more grain from farmers, causing dissatisfaction among some farmers. In 1955, we implemented the grain policy of fixed production, fixed purchase, and fixed sales, thereby stabilizing farmers' emotions and increasing their production enthusiasm. It is now estimated that farmers' total income will be able to increase by about 30% over five years.

From the above, it can be seen that our country's first five-year plan for national economic development will definitely be successfully completed. As long as we work harder and there are no major natural disasters or unexpected accidents in the future, most of the targets can be exceeded. The implementation and over-fulfillment of the first five-year plan have already and will continue to bring about profound changes in our country's national economy. This is mainly reflected in the significant improvement in the production levels of industry and agriculture. It is expected that by 1957, the total industrial and agricultural output value (including modern industry, handicraft industry, and agricultural output value) will increase by more than 60% compared to 1952. In the total industrial and agricultural output value, the proportion of industrial output value (including handicraft industry output value) will reach about 50%, and in the industrial output value, the proportion of industrial output value for means of production will reach more than 40%, thus strengthening the leading role of industry in the national economy. Due to the decisive victory of the socialist transformation, whether in industry, agriculture, transportation, or commerce, the socialist economic component has already taken an absolute dominant position. With the above changes, all sectors of the national economy have shown a prosperous scene, and the undertakings of culture, education, and scientific research are also entering a period of prosperity, thereby creating conditions for the continuous improvement of people's living standards.

It should be pointed out that the unity and solidarity of all ethnic groups, all democratic parties, and all patriotic individuals under the leadership of the Communist Party of China in socialist transformation and socialist construction have shown great enthusiasm and initiative, which is the foundation and guarantee for our great achievements.

It should also be pointed out that during the implementation of our country's first five-year plan, the great Soviet Union and other people's democratic countries have provided us with tremendous assistance. During this period, the Soviet Union provided our country with preferential loans, helped design 205 industrial enterprises and supplied most of the equipment, sent a large number of outstanding experts, and provided many other technical assistance. Other people's democratic countries also provided us with significant assistance in equipment, materials, and technical strength. The Soviet and other people's democratic countries' experts working in our country have made outstanding contributions to our country's socialist construction. We would like to take this opportunity to express our deep gratitude for the sincere and brotherly assistance of the Soviet Union and other people's democratic countries.

In the process of implementing the first five-year plan, we have gained a lot of experience and lessons. Learning from these experiences and lessons will enable us to do a better job in socialist construction. Now, I will only mention a few relatively prominent issues that we have felt in leading economic work in recent years and offer some opinions.

First, we should reasonably determine the speed of national economic development based on needs and possibilities, and place the plan on a foundation that is both active and stable and reliable, to ensure the relatively balanced development of the national economy. Since it is difficult to fully anticipate all possible situations and problems that may arise during the implementation of a long-term plan, the indicators of a long-term plan should be set as reliably as possible, and adjustments should be made through annual plans. The indicators of various items in our country's first five-year plan are basically correct. The arrangements for the past four annual plans are generally in line with the specific situation at that time, thus ensuring the over-fulfillment of our country's first five-year plan. However, it should be noted that the plans for 1955 and 1956 partially had the shortcomings of being too low or too high, causing some difficulties in work.

When we formulated the 1955 plan, due to the poor harvest in the previous two years, we set the scale of basic construction relatively small, and during the savings movement that year, we also inappropriately reduced the investment in some non-production basic construction. At the same time, the basic construction plan changed frequently and was issued late, resulting in poor plan completion. As a result, not only did the financial surplus appear excessive, but in important construction materials such as steel, cement, and timber, a temporary false surplus phenomenon also appeared. If we had prepared some reserve projects earlier, expanded the construction scale in time, or planned to increase the reserves of certain materials, the problem of material accumulation could have been solved. However, we underestimated the subsequent development situation, treating the temporary material surplus as a relatively long-term trend, and thus used the method of export to solve the temporary difficulties of surplus steel and cement. Obviously, this was inappropriate.

When we formulated the 1956 plan, due to the bumper harvest of agriculture in the previous year and the great victory of socialist transformation, it was necessary and possible to set the speed of national economic development relatively high. However, we did not properly balance the scale of basic construction and the supply capacity of materials, thus setting the scale of basic construction relatively large. At the same time, some sectors of the national economy also showed the tendency of simultaneous development and impatience for quick results. As a result, not only did the financial situation become relatively tense, but it also caused a serious shortage of materials such as steel, cement, and timber, thus overusing the state's material reserves and creating a relatively tense situation in all aspects of the national economy.

Experience has shown that when we formulate a long-term plan, we should, based on our fundamental requirement to achieve socialist industrialization and the possible conditions of the country's material, financial, and human resources, realistically determine the indicators of various items. At the same time, we should also reserve a certain amount of backup strength to make the plan more reliable. When we formulate an annual plan, we should, based on the possible development conditions of the current year and subsequent years, actively tap potential to ensure the completion and over-fulfillment of the long-term plan. Experience has also shown that when we formulate an annual plan, in favorable conditions, we must pay attention to the existence of some unfavorable factors in the current and subsequent years and not be impatient and rash; conversely, in unfavorable conditions, we must also pay attention to the existence of many favorable factors in the current and subsequent years and not be hesitant. In other words, we should make a comprehensive analysis of the objective situation and, as far as possible, make a unified arrangement of the main indicators of the current year and the next year to ensure that each year can be mutually connected and develop relatively balanced.

Second, we should combine key construction with overall planning to ensure that various sectors of the national economy can develop proportionally. In the past few years, while we prioritized the development of heavy industry, we adopted the policy of accelerating agricultural cooperativization to promote agricultural production, and correspondingly developed the production of light industry, thus avoiding the danger of mutual disconnection in the development of the major sectors of the national economy.

However, in handling the relationship between key construction and overall planning, we have also made some mistakes. For example, in 1953, some sectors and some places, in the construction work, had the tendency of spreading everywhere, reviving everything, not considering conditions, and blindly rushing forward, which affected the state's key construction and caused financial difficulties and waste of manpower and materials. In early 1956, after the "Outline for the Development of Agriculture in China for 1956-1967" (draft) was published, this tendency occurred again. Some sectors and some places were impatient for quick results, attempting to complete in three, five, or even one or two years what could only be completed in seven or twelve years. These tendencies were all discovered and corrected in time by the Central Committee of the Party.

During this period, another tendency also appeared, that is, some important work was overly prominent, causing it to be disconnected with related aspects. For example, in early 1956, in order to accelerate the development of agriculture, we overestimated the annual demand for double-wheel double-shovel plows and tractor-drawn seeders, and formulated an overly high production plan. Although the plans for these two products were repeatedly revised and production was reduced, the plans were still too high, resulting in the consumption of too much steel, causing the steel supply to be even tighter in 1956, and causing some machinery factories to experience a situation of rushing production and increasing output at one time and idling and reducing output at another time. Again, for example, in our construction, some industrial enterprises, due to advancing too fast, could not fully supply the required raw materials, thus encountering difficulties in fully utilizing production capacity. Of course, in the early stage of our industrial construction, such situations are difficult to completely avoid, but it is not impossible to better arrange in anticipation of such difficulties.

In recent years, in our construction, we have also made appropriate arrangements for the relationship between the central and local governments, coastal regions and inland areas, but we still have shortcomings in this regard. For a period of time, we emphasized the development of central construction, while paying less attention to the development of local construction; we emphasized the development of inland construction, while paying less attention to the development of coastal construction. In the future, we should pay constant attention to adjusting the above relationships and avoiding one-sidedness.

The above explanations show that we emphasize key construction, not to say that we can develop the key independently without overall planning; we advocate overall planning, not to say that we can develop everything simultaneously without ensuring key construction. When we formulate plans and arrange work, we must combine the key and overall well.

Third, we should increase backup strength and improve the material reserve system. In the development of the national economy, imbalances are bound to occur, so it is necessary to maintain necessary backup strength in materials, finance, mineral resources, and production capacity, especially to increase the state's material reserves, to ensure the balanced development of the national economy and the smooth implementation of annual plans, and to cope with possible unexpected difficulties. In the next few years, the impact of natural disasters on our country's agricultural production will still be significant, so to cope with poor harvests, it is necessary to have reserves of grain and major economic crops; to meet the needs of our country's construction and production scale, which is expanding day by day, it is necessary to have reserves of materials and raw materials. In addition, our planning work still lacks experience, and plans often have the shortcomings of being incomplete and inaccurate. Even if the plan is relatively accurate at the time of formulation, it may still produce new imbalances due to unforeseen factors. For example, in 1956, due to the adoption of new technologies, the utilization efficiency of open-hearth furnaces and blast furnaces was improved, resulting in a shortage of ore and coke. To eliminate or reduce the various imbalances that may occur in the implementation of the plan, it is also necessary to reserve necessary backup strength.

Over the past few years, although the state's reserved materials are not many, they have played a certain role in ensuring the needs of production and basic construction, and have also played a certain role in easing the material supply tension in 1956. However, it should be pointed out that we have not fully recognized the importance of material reserves in the past. Just as we mentioned earlier, when some materials were slightly surplus in 1955, we inappropriately exported some of them; in 1956, due to the expansion of the scale of basic construction, we felt a serious shortage of these materials.

It must be recognized that in a country like ours, which is economically backward and has a large population, the shortage of various materials is a common phenomenon for a relatively long period, while the surplus of materials is a temporary phenomenon. Therefore, we need to pay more attention to increasing backup strength, establishing a material reserve system, and reserving necessary materials for the state, especially relatively scarce and important materials. At the same time, each state-owned enterprise should also have appropriate reserves. Of course, whether it is the state's or state-owned enterprises' material reserves, we should strengthen planning, set reasonable quotas, and gradually increase them, not requiring a sudden increase in a large amount, so as not to hinder current production and construction. We should also oppose treating the accumulation of products caused by blind production as the state's material reserves, because this practice will inevitably cause the accumulation and waste of state funds and is not beneficial to production and construction.

Fourth, we should correctly handle the relationship between the economy and finance. Long-term experience has shown that our financial revenue must be based on economic development, and our financial expenditure must first ensure economic development. Therefore, we should first consider the economic development plan, especially the industrial and agricultural production development plan, and then formulate the financial plan based on it to ensure the smooth implementation of the economic plan. Not based on the economic development situation to open up financial sources, to calculate the financial revenue too little, or only from the perspective of saving financial expenditure, to retain too much backup strength, will all limit the full development of economic construction, which is wrong.

When we formulate the financial revenue plan, we must consider the possibility of economic development and the correct proportion between accumulation and consumption to avoid setting the revenue too tightly. When we formulate the financial expenditure plan, in addition to correctly allocating based on the requirements of ensuring key construction and the proportional development of the national economy, we must also consider the balance between the scale of construction and the supply of materials, consider the unexpected needs and leave a certain amount of reserve funds to avoid setting the expenditure too tightly. If we only consider the requirements of construction and do not consider the financial possibilities, do not consider whether equipment, materials, and technical strength can be supplied, and propose overly high and large funding and investment plans, it is obviously wrong.

Comrades often like to discuss whether there should be a "financial framework." Our opinion is that without considering the needs of economic development, subjectively setting a "financial framework" to limit economic development is wrong and should be opposed. However, if the financial plan is in line with the actual situation of economic development, reflecting the correct relationship between accumulation and consumption, the correct relationship between key construction and overall planning, then such a financial plan should undoubtedly be strictly implemented and should not be blindly opposed as a "financial framework."

Here, it should also be pointed out that some shortcomings and errors in our work are inseparable from subjectivism and bureaucratism in leadership. Some leading comrades are aloof, do not approach the masses, do not understand the actual situation, and subjectively handle problems and arrange work, so the decisions made are difficult to be correct and may even be wrong. The bureaucratism of higher-level leaders also fosters the commandism of lower-level leaders.

Now, the organizational structure of the State Council departments is large and has many levels, resulting in many documents, telegrams, and forms, causing lower-level agencies to be busy running around. Some department leaders do not even know what instructions and regulations their department has issued. Such bureaucratic phenomena must be quickly corrected.

In our government's work, although we have achieved great results, we must not be the slightest bit complacent. We should see that our country's national economy is developing rapidly, the situation is changing quickly and frequently, and new problems are constantly emerging, and many problems are interrelated in a complex manner. Therefore, we must constantly approach the masses, get down to the ground, strengthen investigation and research work, grasp the changes in the situation, make specific analyses of favorable and unfavorable factors, and have sufficient estimates of both smooth and difficult aspects, so as to make timely decisions, adjust the activities of various sectors and aspects of the national economy, and avoid the occurrence of mutual disconnection or mutual conflict. In such a vast and complex country, where the economy is undergoing drastic changes, any negligence will inevitably lead to major errors and cause major losses. Therefore, overcoming subjectivism and bureaucratism is of special importance to us.

II. The Basic Tasks of the Second Five-Year Plan

The Central Committee of the Party believes that the formulation of our country's second five-year plan for national economic development should take the achievements that the first five-year plan can possibly achieve as the starting point, link it to the basic requirement that our country should complete the general tasks of the transitional period by approximately the end of the third five-year plan, and realistically estimate the various conditions at home and abroad during the second five-year plan period, and carry out comprehensive planning. Only in this way can the plan be both active and stable and reliable.

The Central Committee of the Party proposes that the basic tasks of our country's second five-year plan for national economic development should be: (1) to continue to carry out industrial construction with heavy industry as the center, promote the technological transformation of the national economy, and establish a solid foundation for the socialist industrialization of our country; (2) to continue to complete the socialist transformation, consolidate and expand collective ownership and public ownership; (3) on the basis of developing basic construction and continuing to complete the socialist transformation, to further develop the production of industry, agriculture, and handicrafts, and correspondingly develop transportation and commerce; (4) to strive to cultivate construction talents, strengthen scientific research work, and adapt to the needs of socialist economic and cultural development; (5) on the basis of the development of industrial and agricultural production, to strengthen national defense, and improve the material and cultural living standards of the people.

The main requirement of our country's socialist industrialization is to basically build a complete industrial system within approximately three five-year plan periods. Such an industrial system can produce various major machinery and equipment and raw materials, basically meeting the needs of our country's expanded reproduction and technological transformation of the national economy. At the same time, it can also produce various consumer goods to appropriately meet the needs of the people's living standards that are constantly improving.

Someone may ask: since the economies of socialist countries led by the Soviet Union are developing and there is the possibility of extensive economic and technological cooperation among socialist countries, does our country still need to build a complete industrial system? We believe that although the current situation of our country is very different from the situation of economic isolation and lack of assistance at the beginning of the Soviet Union's construction, the existence and development of the Soviet Union and other people's democratic countries are extremely favorable conditions for our country's socialist construction. However, for a country like ours, which has a large population, rich resources, and great needs, it is still necessary to build our own complete industrial system. This is because, from the domestic requirements, we must quickly change the long-term backward state of the national economy; from the international requirements, the establishment of a powerful industry in our country can promote the common prosperity of the economies of socialist countries and can also strengthen the forces to defend world peace. Therefore, the idea of relying on international assistance without the need to build our own complete industrial system is wrong.

Another idea of building behind closed doors is also wrong. It goes without saying that in the long term, our country still needs the assistance of the Soviet Union and other people's democratic countries to build a complete industrial system, and we also need to develop and expand economic, technological, and cultural exchanges with other countries. Even if we build a socialist industrial country in the future, we cannot imagine that we can close the door and not ask anyone for anything. The facts show that not only will the scope of economic and technological cooperation among socialist countries continue to expand, but also due to the strengthening of the forces of various countries' people striving for peace, democracy, and national independence, the international situation will continue to ease, and our country's economic, technological, and cultural ties with the world will inevitably develop day by day. Therefore, the idea of isolation in building the socialist cause is also wrong.

To establish a solid foundation for the socialist industrialization of our country, during the second five-year plan period, we must continue to expand the construction of the metallurgical industry, vigorously promote the construction of the machinery manufacturing industry, strengthen the construction of the power industry, coal industry, and building materials industry, and actively carry out the construction of backward sectors in industry such as the petroleum industry, chemical industry, and radio industry. At the same time, we must also promote the technological transformation of the national economy, first of all, carry out the technological transformation of industry, to improve the technological level of our country's industry.

Experience has shown that industrial construction with heavy industry as the center cannot and should not be carried out in isolation, it must be coordinated with various aspects, especially with agriculture. Agriculture is an indispensable condition for the development of industry and the entire national economy. Delaying the development of agriculture will not only directly affect the development of light industry and the improvement of people's living standards, but will also greatly affect the development of heavy industry and the entire national economy, and the consolidation of the worker-peasant alliance. Therefore, during the second five-year plan period, we should continue to strive to develop agriculture and seek the coordinated development of agriculture and industry. To ensure that various sectors and aspects of the national economy develop proportionally and harmoniously, we should also properly arrange the relationship between heavy industry and light industry, industrial and agricultural production and transportation, commodity circulation, economic construction and cultural construction, national construction and people's living standards; at the same time, we should also properly arrange the relationship between the central and local governments, coastal regions and inland areas, and various ethnic groups, to organize all positive factors and useful forces into the great cause of building socialism.

During the second five-year plan period, our country's basic construction and industrial and agricultural production will be able to continue to maintain a relatively fast development speed. The Central Committee of the Party believes that during the second five-year plan period, on the basis of the increase in financial revenue, the proportion of basic construction investment in the total financial revenue will increase from about 35% in the first five-year plan to about 40%, so the total investment in basic construction in the second five-year plan will be about double that of the first five-year plan. In the field of industrial and agricultural production, preliminary calculations show that by 1962, compared to 1957, the total industrial output value will increase by about one fold, among which, the output value of means of production and consumer goods will both increase significantly, but the growth rate of means of production will be faster; the total agricultural output value will increase by about 35%. By 1962, the total industrial and agricultural output value of our country will be about 75% higher than the figure for 1957 as planned in the first five-year plan.

Here, it should be explained that the percentage increases listed in the suggestions for the second five-year plan are all compared based on the planned figures of the first five-year plan, without considering the possible over-fulfillment factors, so the percentage increases appear higher; after the first five-year plan is completed, if the actual figures for 1957 are used as the base, then the percentage increases in the current suggestions may relatively decrease. For example: in the suggestions, it is proposed that the steel production of our country will reach 10.5 million to 12 million tons in 1962, which, compared to the 4.12 million tons planned for 1957 in the first five-year plan, will increase by 1.5 to 1.9 times, but if compared to the current estimated 5.5 million tons for 1957, then the increase will be nearly one fold to 1.2 times.

The above-mentioned scale of basic construction and the speed of industrial and agricultural production development are considered appropriate and both active and stable and reliable. We should believe that as long as we can rely on the masses, tap the masses' enthusiasm and creativity, we will be able to gain great strength, overcome all difficulties on the road ahead, and like the implementation of the first five-year plan, successfully complete the tasks of the second five-year plan.

III. Some Major Issues in the Suggestions for the Second Five-Year Plan

Above, I have explained the basic tasks of the second five-year plan. Regarding the specific principles and indicators of the second five-year plan, these have all been mentioned in the suggestions proposed by the Central Committee of the Party. Here, I will only briefly explain some major issues in the suggestions for the second five-year plan.

(1) Reasonably Accumulating and Allocating Funds

The scale of national construction is mainly determined by how much funds we can accumulate and how to allocate them. If we accumulate more funds and allocate them appropriately, the speed of social expanded reproduction will be faster, and various sectors of the national economy will be able to develop proportionally. Therefore, it is very important to reasonably solve the problems of fund accumulation and fund allocation.

National income is the new material wealth created by the working people of the whole country in the process of production. In a socialist country, all national income belongs to the working people themselves. The working people use part of the national income to maintain and improve their own lives, and use another part for social expanded reproduction, that is, for accumulation. When distributing and redistributing national income, it is necessary to maintain an appropriate proportion between the consumption part and the accumulation part. If the proportion of the consumption part is too small, it will hinder the improvement of people's living standards; if the proportion of the accumulation part is too small, it will reduce the speed of social expanded reproduction. Both situations are not beneficial to the people.

During the second five-year plan period, national income will be able to increase by about 50% compared to the first five-year plan. Since our country's national economy is still backward, agriculture still accounts for a relatively large proportion, and the living standards of the people are still relatively low, therefore, the proportion of the accumulation part in national income cannot and should not have too much and too fast growth, but it can be slightly higher than the level already reached in the first five-year plan. Thus, during the second five-year plan period, the total amount of accumulation will still increase significantly with the growth of national income.

After solving the problem of fund accumulation, we must also solve the problem of fund allocation. Considering the current domestic and international conditions, the Central Committee of the Party believes that during the second five-year plan period, we have the necessity and possibility to appropriately reduce the proportion of national defense and administrative expenses in the state budget, and increase the proportion of economic and cultural education expenditures. The national defense and administrative expenses in the first five-year plan accounted for about 32% of the financial expenditure, and in the second five-year plan, we should strive to reduce it to about 20%. Thus, the proportion of economic and cultural education expenditures will be able to increase from about 56% in the first five-year plan to 60% to 70%, thereby ensuring the rapid development of our country's economic and cultural education undertakings.

When allocating national basic construction investment, we should ensure that industry and agriculture can develop at a relatively high speed. In the total investment, the proportion of industrial investment can be increased from 58.2% in the first five-year plan to about 60%; the proportion of investment in agriculture, water conservancy, and forestry can be increased from 7.6% in the first five-year plan to about 10%. In addition, we should also pay attention to the allocation of investment in transportation and postal services, culture, education, science, and health departments, urban construction departments, and commercial departments, to maintain appropriate proportions.

When allocating industrial investment, we should also properly arrange the relationship between light and heavy industry. In the first five-year plan, due to the great potential of our country's light industry, the plan stipulated that the investment in light industry accounted for 11.2% of the industrial investment, and in the process of implementation, it slightly increased, and such a proportion is appropriate. Considering the gradual improvement of people's consumption levels in the second five-year plan, the production capacity of some light industrial products will be insufficient, we believe that it is necessary to appropriately increase the proportion of investment in light industry. However, due to the fact that the production potential of some light industrial enterprises has not been fully tapped, especially a large number of joint state-private enterprises, after reorganization and arrangement, it is possible to further increase production, and handicrafts, after the realization of cooperativization, will also further increase the production of living consumer goods, therefore, when arranging investment in light industry, we should also consider these factors.

(2) Correctly Arranging the Basic Construction Plan

Regarding basic construction, in addition to the need to reasonably allocate investment as mentioned above, we should also pay attention to the following issues.

First, regarding the strengthening of the construction of the machinery manufacturing industry and metallurgical industry

In the industrial construction with heavy industry as the center, we should pay special attention to the construction of the machinery manufacturing industry and metallurgical industry.

The development of the machinery manufacturing industry is one of the key links in establishing our country's complete industrial system. During the first five-year plan period of our country, many large and precise machines and complete sets of equipment still could not be manufactured, and this situation made about 40% of the machines and equipment needed for our country's construction rely on imports to solve. Therefore, we must strive to develop the machinery manufacturing industry, especially to develop the manufacturing of various large equipment, heavy machinery, precision machinery, and instruments that we need but are lacking, which is a key point for future industrial construction. We should strive to, through the construction of the second five-year plan, increase the self-sufficiency rate of machinery and equipment to about 70%.

The metallurgical industry is the foundation of heavy industry, and without a powerful metallurgical industry, the development of the machinery manufacturing industry is also difficult. During the first five-year plan period, the steel produced in our country only accounted for about 80% of the domestic demand, and many special types of steel were entirely or almost entirely dependent on imports to solve. Therefore, we must strive to develop the metallurgical industry, which is another key point for future industrial construction. We should strive to, through the construction of the second five-year plan, make the quantity and variety of steel and major non-ferrous metals basically meet the needs of various sectors of the national economy, especially the machinery manufacturing industry.

In the various sectors of heavy industry, in addition to the machinery manufacturing industry and metallurgical industry, many weak links need to be strengthened, and many blanks need to be filled, such as the mining and refining of rare metals, the establishment and development of organic synthetic chemical industry, the peaceful use of atomic energy, etc., all should be regarded as important aspects of our construction and given sufficient attention.

To develop heavy industry, we must continue to strengthen geological work and correctly combine geological surveys with key prospecting work, strive to discover more new mineral areas and mineral types, and determine more mineral reserves to meet the current and long-term needs of industrial construction.

Second, regarding the distribution of productive forces

To reasonably allocate our country's productive forces, promote the economic development of various regions, and make the layout of our country's industry adapt to the conditions of resources and national defense, it is necessary to build new industrial bases in the interior in a planned manner. This is an unshakable policy that we must adhere to. Strengthening the new construction of industry in the interior will also promote the economic and cultural development of ethnic minority areas. During the second five-year plan period, we must continue to carry out the construction of industrial bases centered on the steel industry in the Central and Inner Mongolia regions, actively carry out the construction of new industrial bases centered on the steel industry and large hydropower stations in the Southwest, Northwest, and the surrounding areas of Sanmenxia, continue to carry out the construction of the petroleum industry and non-ferrous metal industry in the Xinjiang region, and strengthen geological work in the Tibet region to prepare conditions for the development of Tibet's industry.

At the same time, we must make full use of the existing industrial base in the coastal areas. The original industrial base in the coastal cities is needed for the industrial construction we are carrying out in the interior, and it is also needed for the supply of raw materials and equipment, technical personnel, and funds. It can be said that the original industrial base in the coastal areas is the starting point of our country's industrialization. We make full use of and strengthen the industrial base in the coastal areas, not only to meet the needs of the country and the people that are increasing day by day, but also precisely to establish a stronger industrial base in the interior. During the second five-year plan period, we should continue to strengthen the industrial base in the Northeast, make full use of and appropriately strengthen the industry in the coastal cities of East China, North China, and South China, to give full play to their role in national construction.

Of course, when we make full use of the original industrial base in the coastal cities, we should pay attention to rationality and avoid blindness. This rationality is: to renovate those enterprises that have the necessity and possibility to be renovated, not to renovate all existing enterprises; in cities where industrial enterprises are already relatively numerous, generally fewer new enterprises should be built; new and renovated enterprises must pay attention to the source of raw materials, the sales of products, production technology, and transportation conditions, and pay attention to the reasonable division of labor with other regions.

In the problem of the distribution of industrial locations, whether it is the industry in the interior or the industry in the coastal areas, our policy is to be appropriately dispersed and mutually coordinated, opposing both excessive concentration and mutual disconnection.

With the reasonable distribution of industrial productive forces, we will build many new cities and expand many existing cities, for this, we should strengthen the planning and construction of cities to coordinate with industrial construction.

Third, regarding the combination of large enterprises and medium and small enterprises

Many large-scale industrial enterprises that we have started construction during the first five-year plan period and will start construction during the second five-year plan period are the backbone of our country's complete industrial system. However, while constructing large enterprises, we must also construct many medium and small enterprises to be able to produce more industrial products in a shorter time, to meet the needs of national construction and people's lives.

Some people think that constructing large enterprises is more reasonable in terms of economy and technology, so we should build more large enterprises and fewer medium and small enterprises. Others think that constructing medium and small enterprises requires less time and has a faster effect on investment, so we should build more medium and small enterprises and fewer large enterprises. We believe that it is not possible to generalize, in some industrial sectors and under certain conditions, constructing large enterprises is reasonable, while in other industrial sectors and under other conditions, constructing medium and small enterprises is more reasonable. In each type of enterprise, generally speaking, there should be some large enterprises as the backbone, and there should also be many medium and small enterprises to coordinate.

To make the construction of enterprises more reasonable, for large enterprises that have the necessity and possibility to be constructed in stages, we can consider constructing in stages; for medium and small enterprises that have surplus resources and other conditions, we can carry out overall planning and make preparations for future development. At the same time, when planning medium and small enterprises to coordinate with large enterprises, we should first make use of existing state-owned and joint state-private medium and small enterprises and handicrafts to tap their production potential.

(3) Developing Industrial Production

Our country's first five-year plan stipulates that by 1957, compared to 1952, the total industrial output value (including handicraft industry output value) will increase by 90.3%; the Central Committee of the Party proposes that by 1962, compared to the original plan for 1957, the total industrial output value should increase by about one fold. During the second five-year plan period, the total industrial output value can maintain a relatively high growth rate because: the newly built and renovated enterprises that have been put into production will increase, most existing enterprises will adopt production-increasing technical measures or carry out technological transformation, joint state-private enterprises will complete economic reorganization and basically realize nationalization, handicrafts, except for a few, will complete cooperativization, and at the same time, agricultural development will also be able to maintain a relatively high speed.

Regarding the development of industrial production, I will only talk about the following few issues here.

First, regarding the issue of tapping the production potential of industrial enterprises

In our country's total industrial output value, according to rough calculations, by 1957, the output of newly built and renovated enterprises will account for about 15%; while by 1962, the output of newly built and renovated enterprises completed in the first and second five-year plans will account for about 50%. Therefore, strengthening organizational work and fully tapping the role of this type of enterprise is of great significance for the development of industrial production.

The new and reconstructed enterprises, especially the heavy industrial enterprises, require a certain period from the start of production to reach the designed production capacity, during which time technicians and workers need to master the performance of machines and equipment and familiarize themselves with the technological processes. However, if we can fully mobilize the enthusiasm and wisdom of technicians, workers, and staff, this period can be shortened, and in some cases, the designed production capacity can even be exceeded. According to statistics from April 1956, out of 141 industrial construction projects that started production between 1953 and 1955, 30 had already reached and exceeded their designed capacity ahead of schedule, 33 could reach their designed capacity ahead of schedule, 71 could reach their designed capacity on schedule, and only 7 could not reach their designed capacity on schedule. This means that nearly half of the enterprises can shorten the time to reach their designed capacity. Examples include the reconstructed Shenyang Wind Tools Factory, which was originally estimated to take four years to reach its designed capacity but actually achieved it in the second year of operation, with the production volume in 1957 expected to exceed the designed capacity by more than onefold. The newly built Fushun Aluminum Plant, which started production in early 1955, already reached approximately 110% of its designed capacity in that year. This shows that new and reconstructed enterprises have significant production potential. To fully tap this potential, the primary task is to strengthen production preparation, especially in personnel training, technical preparation, organizational coordination, and raw material supply. Relevant departments should study, summarize, and promote specific experiences in this regard.

However, we must not neglect the production of existing enterprises just because we have new and reconstructed enterprises. During the second five-year plan period, the output value of existing enterprises will still account for a significant proportion of the total industrial output. Moreover, many new and reconstructed enterprises still need the cooperation and support of existing enterprises. We should adopt different measures based on specific conditions, reconstruct or technologically upgrade some enterprises, adjust and add certain equipment to others, and continue to improve the management of the remaining enterprises to further tap the production potential of existing enterprises.

Second, regarding the promotion of industrial production specialization and cooperation:

Promoting the specialization and cooperation of industrial production, especially heavy industrial production, can improve labor productivity, reduce production costs, and promote technological development. However, industrial production specialization and cooperation are relatively complex issues that need to be gradually and separately resolved over a considerable period, in line with the improvement of China's industrial level and in combination with China's specific conditions and possibilities. We cannot proceed blindly or forcibly. During the first five-year plan period, on the one hand, we fully utilized the role of existing comprehensive factories to make their production adapt to the multifaceted needs of national construction and people's lives. On the other hand, in the machinery manufacturing industry, we began to establish some specialized factories according to product categories and adjusted some machine factories with overly complex product categories, enabling these enterprises to initially move towards specialization. This was entirely necessary. During the second five-year plan period, in addition to building some specialized factories, we should reasonably arrange the product plans for various new and reconstructed enterprises, avoiding the drawbacks of overly complex product categories and inappropriate pursuit of specialization. For existing enterprises, on the one hand, we should appropriately adjust the product plans of some enterprises to make production more reasonable. On the other hand, we should still retain some comprehensive factories. For most joint state-private enterprises, we should allow them to continue with their original product categories to meet the diverse needs of society and the cooperation needs of state-owned enterprises. Within an industrial area or city, we can organize the specialized production of certain forgings, castings, and standard parts based on needs and possibilities. In the process of promoting industrial production specialization, we should prevent the tendency of reducing product categories.

As industrial production gradually moves towards specialization, the task of cooperation becomes increasingly heavy and complex. Therefore, we must further correct the tendency of wanting to operate independently without cooperating. Enterprises that must and can cooperate should have specific cooperation tasks and contracts stipulated in their annual plans.

Third, regarding the improvement of product quality and the increase of product varieties:

The quality of many industrial products, especially certain light industrial products, is not high, and the varieties are not many, which has become a prominent issue in industrial development and has had a negative impact on national construction and people's lives. There is no doubt that the quality of many of our heavy industrial products and light industrial products has been continuously improving, and the varieties have been continuously increasing. However, not all industrial products are like this, and even some products have seen a decline in quality and a reduction in varieties. This phenomenon must be vigorously corrected.

The low quality and few varieties of industrial products are indeed largely due to China's low technological level and backward equipment. However, this does not mean that we have no way to improve the quality of China's industrial products and increase the varieties of industrial products, nor can we use this as an excuse for the decline in quality and the reduction in varieties. Some industrial departments have not paid due attention to product quality and product varieties, lacking long-term planning and effective measures; when checking the implementation of plans, they often focus on checking the completion of production volume plans while neglecting the completion of quality plans and new product variety production plans; there are rewards for exceeding production volume plans, but there are no rewards for improving quality and increasing varieties. All these are important reasons for the current low quality and few varieties of China's industrial products. In addition, in the case of light industrial products, factories' finished products were previously purchased and sold by the commercial department; good quality and poor quality, new products and old products were all priced the same or with very little difference. These systems and methods have also encouraged enterprises to neglect product quality and product varieties. Therefore, during the current period and the second five-year plan period, each industrial department should formulate long-term development plans for industrial technology, actively organize product design forces, and strengthen the leadership of new product design and trial production work. Strengthen the technical management work of enterprises, improve the supply of raw materials, implement the product quality reward system; especially, we should mobilize the broad masses of workers to strive for improving product quality and increasing product varieties. At the same time, the commercial department should gradually implement the selective purchase system and the price differentiation system based on quality.

Fourth, regarding the development of agricultural production:

The Central Committee of the Party proposed in the draft of the second five-year plan that the total grain production over five years should reach approximately 220 billion jin, with 500 billion jin in 1962; the total cotton production over five years should reach approximately 2.1 billion dan, with 4.8 billion dan in 1962. The total agricultural output value in 1962 should increase by approximately 35% compared to 1957. These targets are proposed considering the following two aspects: on the one hand, except for a few areas, agricultural cooperativization will be completed, allowing us to further implement the measures and experiences for increasing production as stipulated in the "Outline for the Development of Agriculture in China (1956-1967) (Draft)," and to expand irrigation areas and cultivated land, increase the supply of chemical fertilizers, and improve production tools and cultivation techniques, all of which will promote the further development of agricultural production. On the other hand, various natural disasters are still unavoidable, many seriously harmful rivers have not been treated, large-scale land reclamation work has not been fully launched, and the conditions for agricultural mechanization have not been met, all of which make us unable to demand a higher speed for agricultural production development. Of course, we should fully utilize the aforementioned favorable conditions to strive for greater development of agricultural production during the second five-year plan period.

Regarding the development of agricultural production, we should pay special attention to the following two issues.

First, regarding the improvement of agricultural crop yield per unit area:

The main approach to agricultural increase during the second five-year plan period in China is to, based on cooperativization, rely on the enthusiasm of farmers to gradually improve agricultural production technology, develop water conservancy, increase fertilizers, promote advanced experiences, etc., to improve the yield per unit area. When implementing various increase measures, we must adopt the principle of combining national work with cooperative work.

Regarding water conservancy development, on the one hand, the state and local governments should undertake several large and medium-sized water conservancy projects, such as the comprehensive treatment of the Yellow River, Huai River, Hai River, and local flood control and waterlogging prevention projects. On the other hand, cooperatives should undertake a large number of small water conservancy projects, improve existing water conservancy facilities, and strengthen water and soil conservation work. In low-lying and waterlogged areas, we should study and implement various waterlogging and drainage measures, change cultivation systems to reduce the harm of internal waterlogging.

Regarding the increase of fertilizers, on the one hand, the state should actively develop the fertilizer industry and strive to import more chemical fertilizers to increase the supply of fertilizers. On the other hand, and more importantly, cooperatives and individual members should widely engage in pig raising and manure accumulation, and in some places, sheep raising and manure accumulation, and manufacture green manure and accumulate other natural fertilizers.

Regarding the promotion of various technological measures and advanced increase experiences, we should actively carry out technical guidance work, on the one hand, absorb and promote advanced increase experiences from outside, and based on scientific experiments and research results, implement them in a way that suits local conditions. On the other hand, we should also actively summarize and promote local advanced increase experiences.

Here, we must especially point out that the promotion of technological improvement measures and the promotion of advanced experiences must be carried out in an active yet cautious manner. In recent years, significant achievements have been made in this work, but in some places, there have been issues of mechanical transplantation and even forced promotion, resulting in negative outcomes. In the future, any increase measures and advanced experiences must first be proven effective through experiments before being gradually promoted, and when promoting them, appropriate implementation steps must be regulated based on the specific conditions at that time and place. At the same time, when promoting these measures and experiences, we should also seek the opinions of local farmers, especially experienced elderly farmers, and should not forcefully promote them. We should not arbitrarily negate local cultivation habits.

Second, regarding the development of diversified agricultural economy:

Grain is the foundation for ensuring people's lives and the development of the entire agricultural economy, and we must pay sufficient attention to it. In recent years, various places have emphasized the increase of grain and cotton production, which is entirely necessary. However, some places have neglected other agricultural economiesvarious economic crops other than cotton, animal husbandry, forestry, aquaculture, sericulture, and various sideline occupations of farmersdue to this emphasis. Coupled with the fact that some agricultural products and local specialties have been affected by low purchase prices, the result is that the agricultural economy cannot develop fully and adequately, thereby affecting the development of the entire national economy and the income of farmers. Therefore, each place, and even every agricultural production cooperative, should, based on the historical situation and current situation, natural conditions, economic and technical conditions, farmers' production habits, and living habits, etc., plan the development of agriculture comprehensively to avoid the tendency of single-mindedness and one-sidedness. In pastoral areas, forest areas, and aquaculture areas, we should plan respectively with animal husbandry, forestry, or aquaculture as the center, and based on possible conditions, develop agriculture and other sideline occupations.

To promote the comprehensive development of the agricultural economy, we must take some specific measures. All production that farmers have the habit of operating and that is needed by society should be continued and developed. All products that are urgently needed by society, especially those with significant economic value, such as subtropical and tropical crops, various agricultural products and by-products needed for export, should, in addition to being operated by the state or local governments, also be encouraged to be operated by cooperatives, with the state providing technical guidance. All sideline occupations that are not necessary for cooperatives to operate uniformly should be encouraged for individual members to operate. The commercial department should reasonably regulate the purchase prices of agricultural products and sideline products and improve the purchase system. At the same time, relevant departments should help cooperatives appropriately restore some agricultural product processing industries in rural areas.

Fifth, regarding the development of transportation and postal services:

During the second five-year plan period, with the development of industrial and agricultural production and the expansion of the scale of basic construction, the opening up and construction of inland and remote areas will require a significant increase in transportation and communication capacity. This requires us to, with railways as the key, correspondingly carry out the construction of the national transportation and communication networks. This puts forward enormous demands on the transportation and postal departments: on the one hand, we should carry out necessary reconstruction and technological transformation of existing lines and equipment. On the other hand, we should continue the construction of new lines, mainly the construction of railways and highways in the northwestern and southwestern regions, and the construction of ports along the coast and the Yangtze River. At the same time, we should also increase the necessary transportation and communication equipment. The transportation and postal departments should, based on the aforementioned two aspects of demands, plan comprehensively according to the priority and urgency, to ensure the completion of various tasks proposed in the draft of the second five-year plan regarding transportation and postal services.

Currently, some lines have already shown signs of tension in transportation and communication, which is mainly due to insufficient equipment capacity. However, we should also see that some transportation and communication lines and equipment still have certain potential capacities that have not been tapped. Therefore, the transportation and postal departments should also actively take effective technical measures and strengthen the organization of transportation and communication work.

Due to the insufficient modern transportation tools and uneven distribution of transportation lines in China, and the large number and wide distribution of traditional transportation tools such as wooden sailboats and animal-drawn carts in China, these traditional transportation tools will still be important auxiliary transportation forces for a considerable period, and in some areas, they are still the main transportation forces. Therefore, we should fully utilize and appropriately develop traditional transportation tools and gradually carry out technical improvements on them. Under possible conditions, we should also combine modern transportation tools with traditional transportation tools to meet the growing needs of transportation.

Sixth, regarding the strengthening of commercial work:

Improving people's lives requires not only increasing people's monetary income but also ensuring that they can buy a certain quantity of and suitable commodities. According to rough calculations, the total retail sales of various daily necessities and some means of production for urban and rural people in 1962 will increase by approximately 50% compared to the original plan for 1957. This is a heavy task for the commercial department. The commercial department must continue to strengthen procurement and sales work, continue to implement the policy of unified purchase and sale of major daily necessities, reasonably set up commercial networks, and organize a part of the free market under state leadership in a planned manner to meet the growing needs of the people. Regarding foreign trade, we should organize the export of relevant materials in a planned manner to ensure the import of equipment and materials necessary for national construction.

Commercial work, as a link between production and consumption, industrial production and agricultural production, not only undertakes the task of meeting the living needs of residents and a part of production needs and the task of accumulating funds for the state, but also undertakes the task of promoting the development of industrial and agricultural production. Since the law of value still plays a certain role in China's economic life and plays an important role in some aspects, correctly using the law of value and correctly grasping the price policy can promote the development of industrial and agricultural production.

During the first five-year plan period, the prices in China's market were generally stable, and the price ratio between industrial products and agricultural products was generally appropriate, which shows that our price policy was correct. It promoted the development of industrial and agricultural production and national construction and ensured the smooth progress of socialist transformation. However, there were still some shortcomings and errors in the implementation of the price policy, mainly: some agricultural products and local specialties had purchase prices set too low, or sometimes high and sometimes low, affecting the increase of these products, and even causing a reduction in some products; the price difference between some light industrial products in terms of quality and variety was too small, affecting the improvement of the quality and increase of the varieties of these products. These shortcomings have been discovered and are being gradually corrected, but they have not been completely overcome and need further study and adjustment.

Correctly grasping the price policy is a very complex matter. In a country with a large population and complex economic conditions like ours, the adjustment of prices must be done with great caution and cannot be done lightly. For example, inappropriate increases in the purchase prices of agricultural products will have adverse effects on industrial production and workers' lives, and on maintaining the correct development ratio between various agricultural products; inappropriate reductions in the sales prices of industrial products may cause shortages of commodities. Therefore, such inappropriate price increases or reductions will not be conducive to the development of industrial and agricultural production and the improvement of people's lives. During the second five-year plan period, we will continue to adopt a stable policy in terms of prices, while also making necessary and appropriate adjustments to some unreasonable prices.

Due to the victory of the socialist transformation, socialist economy has occupied an absolute dominant position in China, which makes it possible for us to better use the law of value within an appropriate scope to influence the production of those industrial and agricultural products that are not necessary for the state to purchase and sell uniformly, have small output value, and have many varieties, to meet the diverse needs of people's lives. To adapt to the aforementioned situation and prevent the occurrence of excessive and rigid unification that affects the reduction of product quality and varieties, now and during the second five-year plan period, we will take many important measures in the commercial sector. For example, under the leadership of the state unified market, we will organize a part of the free market in a planned manner; within a certain scope, we will implement the self-production and self-sale of products; for some daily-use industrial products, we will promote the selective purchase and price differentiation system based on quality, etc. Taking these measures will not only not damage the state unified market but will also play a beneficial supplementary role for the state unified market.

Seventh, regarding the reorganization of enterprises and personnel arrangements during the socialist transformation process:

In the work of socialist transformation, I will only talk about the following two issues.

First, regarding the reorganization of medium and small joint state-private enterprises and handicraft and small commercial cooperative organizations:

Large joint state-private enterprises, because they have been in operation for a relatively long time, have gradually brought their production and operation into the track of national planning, and their management systems have generally undergone initial transformation. However, a large number of scattered medium and small new joint state-private enterprises still need appropriate reorganization and arrangement. Many individual handicraftsmen and small traders, after achieving cooperativization, also need necessary reorganization and arrangement. Only in this way can they operate and manage production under relatively reasonable conditions and gradually adapt to the requirements of national planning management. However, when reorganizing them, we must pay attention to preventing and correcting the tendency of over-concentration.

Regarding the industrial sector, small factories indeed have their drawbacks, but they are more flexible and adaptable in production and operation, making it easier to meet diverse and constantly changing needs. Therefore, all small factories that are reasonably operated and can meet social needs should be retained and not hastily merged or eliminated. Handicraft cooperative organizations generally should not be overly concentrated and should, based on the principles of developing production, meeting social needs, and increasing members' income, allow large cooperatives, small cooperatives, and small groups to coexist. Certain manufacturing industries, especially many repair and service industries, should be allowed to continue their scattered activities and original operational characteristics to directly serve residents and to absorb family auxiliary labor to participate in production. Some handicrafts can continue to produce independently under the leadership of handicraft cooperative organizations, or they can completely self-produce and self-sell and do not need to be forcibly organized.

Regarding the commercial sector, the distribution of commercial institutions should maximize the convenience for residents. Therefore, they should not be overly concentrated but should be appropriately dispersed and adopt diverse operational methods to serve residents. Our commercial leadership departments have often considered their own management convenience more than how to convenience residents, resulting in the tendency of over-concentration, and inappropriate merging and elimination of some small shops and small traders. This tendency should be quickly corrected. In the future, whether in urban residential areas or vast rural areas, we should maintain a certain number of small shops and small traders, adopting cooperative stores, cooperative groups, agency sales, and even completely self-purchase and self-sale, etc., to better serve residents.

Second, regarding the arrangement and transformation of industrial and commercial capitalists and small business owners:

After the realization of joint state-private operation in industrial and commercial capitalism, we should cultivate and promote outstanding workers to participate in enterprise leadership and at the same time absorb the original industrial and commercial capitalists and small business owners to undertake a part of the operational and leadership work. After the implementation of joint state-private operation and the fixed interest system, capitalist elements in enterprises have the dual identity of capitalists and employees. Therefore, state capital representatives should cooperate well with these capitalists, utilize their strengths and enthusiasm, and in the process of work, strengthen their education and transformation, overcome their capitalist ideology and style, help them achieve results in work, and gradually transform them into genuine laborers, rather than adopting a discriminatory attitude towards them. Doing so is beneficial to enterprises, the state, and the working class. To do this work well, we should make the workers in enterprises understand this principle and regard the unity, education, and transformation of capitalists as an important task.

In tens of thousands of capitalists in joint state-private enterprises, the vast majority have some production technology or operational management experience, and some have relatively high production technology and relatively rich operational management experience. We must fully utilize their production technology and useful operational management experience. State capital representatives should learn well from them in this regard.

Eighth, regarding the improvement of the state administrative system and the mobilization of local initiatives:

During the second five-year plan period, many national construction projects will be more undertaken by local governments or will require the efforts of local governments to complete. Therefore, mobilizing local initiatives is an important condition for the completion of socialist construction projects.

Now, the socialist transformation in China has achieved decisive victories, and people's democratic dictatorship has been further consolidated, which makes it necessary and possible for us to, based on the principle of unified leadership, hierarchical management, and suitability to local conditions and specific circumstances, further delineate the administrative powers of the central and local governments and improve the state administrative system to fully mobilize local initiatives. The State Council held a national system conference from May to August this year, which examined the current phenomenon of excessive centralization of power and discussed the improvement of the state administrative system, and proposed a draft resolution on the improvement of the state administrative system, which is now being widely solicited for opinions from all parties.

When delineating the administrative powers of the central and local governments, the following principles should be followed: (1) clearly stipulate that provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the central government have certain scopes of planning, financial, enterprise, institutional, material, and personnel management powers; (2) enterprises and institutions that are related to the entire national economy and have overall, key, and centralized characteristics should be managed by the central government; other enterprises and institutions should be handed over to local governments as much as possible; when enterprises and institutions are handed over, the related planning, financial management, and personnel management should generally be handed over together; (3) the management of enterprises and institutions should seriously improve and promote the dual leadership management method of central leadership with local assistance or local leadership with central assistance, and strengthen the leadership of enterprises and institutions; (4) the main planning and financial indicators managed by the central government should be uniformly issued by the State Council, changing the previous practice of many main indicators being issued by various departments; (5) certain main planning indicators and personnel quotas should leave a certain adjustment margin and flexibility for local governments; (6) the autonomous rights of ethnic autonomous areas should be specifically implemented, and attention should be paid to helping the political, economic, and cultural development of ethnic minority areas; (7) the improvement of the system should be gradually implemented, and major changes should be carried out in steps, such as preparation this year, trial implementation next year, and full implementation during the second five-year plan period, to proceed steadily.

To effectively implement the aforementioned principles, we believe that the key issue is to appropriately expand the powers of local governments under the unified leadership of the central government. Because local governments are closer to the basic units of enterprises and institutions and closer to the masses, they are more familiar with the actual situation. Appropriately expanding the powers of local governments can better mobilize all forces and positive factors in local areas to participate in socialist construction projects.

To further mobilize local initiatives and strengthen the unity of all ethnic groups in the country, now and during the second five-year plan period, we should pay more attention to ethnic minority work. All areas where ethnic minorities reside should establish autonomous regions that have not yet been established according to the Constitution and actively help them establish autonomous regions. The various autonomous rights of autonomous regions should be strictly respected. A large number of ethnic minority cadres should be cultivated and promoted, and their political awareness and management capabilities in various matters should be continuously improved, so that they can truly take charge and have the right to do so in work. Whether in ethnic minority concentrated, mixed, or scattered areas, their national equality rights, religious freedom, customs, and languages should be respected. For those ethnic minorities that do not have their own script or have incomplete scripts, we should actively help them create and reform their own ethnic scripts.

Ninth, regarding the cultivation of construction talents and the strengthening of scientific research work:

During the second five-year plan period, to establish a solid foundation for socialist industrialization and to carry out national construction and technological transformation of the national economy, we must strive to cultivate construction talents and strengthen scientific research work.

First, regarding the cultivation and allocation of construction talents:

Cultivating various construction talents for the country, first and foremost industrial and technical talents and scientific research talents, is the primary task of education work. In recent years, China's work in cultivating construction talents has made significant progress, but from the perspective of national construction needs, the number of talents cultivated in colleges and universities and secondary specialized schools, especially in quality and categories, still cannot meet the needs. Therefore, during the second five-year plan period, we should further develop higher education and secondary specialized education and, based on the principle of "focusing on key areas and considering others" and combining needs with possibilities, carry out comprehensive planning.

To do a good job in cultivating construction talents, we must correctly handle the relationship between quantity and quality. In recent years, we have had the tendency to emphasize quantity and neglect quality, which must be corrected. From the perspective of the education department, we should actively mobilize forces to increase the number of students under the condition of ensuring a certain quality. From the perspective of the personnel department, we should consider the actual needs and possibilities and not propose overly high requirements to avoid reducing the quality of students due to blindly increasing the number.

Higher education and secondary specialized education should realistically and not subjectively adjust the departments and set up specialties, and practically improve the teaching plans, syllabi, textbooks, and teaching methods to make the cultivated talents better adapt to the specific needs of various departments of the national economy. Currently, the biggest difficulty in developing and improving higher education and secondary specialized education is the lack of teachers and the low quality of students. Therefore, we must select an appropriate number of outstanding graduates from colleges and universities to cultivate more postgraduates and send graduates and teachers of colleges and universities abroad to study the subjects we lack, to increase the number of teachers. At the same time, we must correspondingly develop and run well senior high schools and junior high schools to improve the quality of students. The existing books and instruments in colleges and universities are generally still insufficient and should be gradually supplemented; the necessary buildings for these schools in their development should also be solved.

Cultivating construction talents also requires the development of adult education to absorb those with conditions for further study from workers to study in evening schools or correspondence schools and gradually cultivate them into senior and intermediate specialized talents. Learning must be truly voluntary and should be carried out in batches and phases; various units should guarantee the necessary study time for learners, and the study time should not be too long, and the study should not be too tight, to avoid affecting production and the health of workers.

In the context of insufficient scientific and technical talents, the rational allocation of construction talents is even more important. When allocating construction talents, the needs of production, construction, and scientific research, and the increase of teachers should be guaranteed first, while considering other aspects; and we should continue to correct the phenomenon of improper allocation of scientific and technical talents, using them for non-specialized purposes, and wasting talents.

Second, regarding the strengthening of scientific research work:

Recently, under the direct leadership of the Central Committee of the Party and the State Council, hundreds of outstanding scientists from across the country have been gathered to draft the twelve-year national scientific and technological development plan and the philosophy and social sciences development plan, respectively proposing the most important research tasks in natural sciences and social sciences. This is an extremely important measure to improve scientific research work and ensure that many important aspects of science and technology in China can approach the advanced level of the world in the next twelve years. This should be completed under the leadership of the Central Committee of the Party and the State Council as soon as possible, and the national scientific research forces should be organized to implement the tasks proposed in these two plans in a phased manner. Due to the heavy tasks of science and technology that need to be solved, the insufficient number of existing scientific research talents, and the rapid development of modern science and technology, and because the current focus of China's scientific research is mostly our weak links in work, and even gaps, we should concentrate our efforts to first solve the problems in important aspects and prevent the tendency of starting everything at once and evenly using forces.

To strengthen scientific research work, we must gradually establish and improve the scientific research institutions of the Chinese Academy of Sciences and various business departments, strengthen the scientific research work of colleges and universities, and achieve division of labor and cooperation among various aspects and close integration. The establishment of scientific research institutions should seek a reasonable distribution in regions. Scientific research work should be closely integrated with the various construction projects of the state, especially economic construction projects. In scientific research, the policy of "letting a hundred flowers bloom and a hundred schools of thought contend" should be implemented, and academic freedom should be encouraged to fully mobilize the enthusiasm and creativity of scientific research personnel.

To develop China's scientific research work, we should also timely solve the problems of necessary books, materials, instruments, and experimental sites, actively improve the working conditions of scientific research personnel, and further strengthen international scientific exchanges and cooperation, and collect and exchange scientific and technological information at home and abroad.

Tenth, regarding the further improvement of people's lives:

During the second five-year plan period, based on the development of production and the increase of national income, we have the possibility to further improve people's lives.

Fundamentally, all the construction projects carried out by our country are for the welfare of the people. However, in the process of construction, it is often difficult to arrange the long-term interests of the people and the current interests, and the collective interests and individual interests. Therefore, we must properly arrange the ratio of accumulation and consumption in national income, while ensuring the gradual expansion of the scale of national construction, to gradually improve people's lives.

Here, I would like to emphasize the following issues:

First, regarding the improvement of workers' material lives:

During the second five-year plan period, the average wage of workers will increase by 25% to 30%. Such a growth rate is in line with China's economic development level and the growth rate of labor productivity. Based on the aforementioned experience, we must pay attention to maintaining the appropriate ratio between wage growth and labor productivity growth in the annual plan to make the wage level of workers rise relatively evenly with the improvement of labor productivity. And when formulating the annual wage growth plan for workers, we should also consider the production and supply possibilities of daily necessities to avoid the phenomenon of wage growth and material supply becoming decoupled.

To improve the material lives of workers, in addition to further raising the wage level of workers, now and during the second five-year plan period, we should also, based on possible conditions, take practical measures to gradually improve the conditions of workers' housing, safety, medical and health, and appropriately increase other welfare facilities. In this regard, the State Council has formulated several specific measures and will soon announce their implementation.

We should continue to oppose the bureaucratic attitude of indifference to workers' lives. In fact, many facilities to increase workers' welfare have not been implemented, not entirely because of the lack of financial and material conditions, but mainly because some leading officials of relevant departments have adopted a bureaucratic attitude towards the improvement of workers' lives. It should be pointed out that some welfare undertakings do not require increasing the national budget expenditure and can be implemented. As long as we can overcome the bureaucratic style, pay more attention to the lives of the masses, and seriously implement the state's plans and various regulations, we will definitely be able to do a better job in improving the material lives of workers.

Second, regarding the improvement of farmers' material lives:

To improve the material lives of farmers, on the one hand, we should pay attention to adjusting the ratio of national accumulation and cooperative income and correctly solving the problem of farmers' burdens; on the other hand, we should also pay attention to adjusting the ratio of public accumulation and individual income within agricultural production cooperatives and correctly solving the problem of cooperative income distribution. During the second five-year plan period, agricultural taxes should be maintained at an appropriate ratio, and the regular and additional agricultural taxes will be unified for collection to simplify the tax system; at the same time, we require all cooperatives to implement the regulations of the "Model Constitution for Advanced Agricultural Production Cooperatives" to generally not exceed the proportion quotas stipulated in the constitution for public accumulation funds, public welfare funds, and management expenses. In this way, if the agricultural increase plan can be completed during the second five-year plan period, it will be possible to increase farmers' total income by 25% to 30% in five years.

During the second five-year plan period, the state will continue to help agricultural production cooperatives develop production from various aspects, and the investment in water conservancy construction and agriculture will be significantly increased compared to the first five-year plan, and agricultural loans will also be increased; at the same time, the state will still allocate a portion of special funds for disaster relief in rural areas. For many mountainous areas and old revolutionary base areas with poor natural conditions, the state should pay special attention to helping local people develop production and improve their lives in the future.

Third, regarding the improvement of the cultural lives of the people:

During the second five-year plan period, with the development of economic construction and the increase of people's demand for culture, we should, according to the proposal, continue to strive to eliminate illiteracy, develop primary education, carry out adult education for workers and peasants, and gradually promote the reform of the script. At the same time, we should further carry out cultural work for the masses, develop news, publishing, broadcasting, literature, art, film, etc. When carrying out these undertakings, we should especially pay attention to improving quality.

In carrying out cultural and educational work among the broad masses of people, we must adopt active yet steady steps. In recent years, we have had either conservative or rash tendencies in the work of eliminating illiteracy, primary education, social culture, and publishing, causing unnecessary losses to the work. In the future, we must learn from this lesson and carry out cultural and educational work based on needs and possibilities.

In carrying out cultural and educational work for the masses, we must fully rely on the strength of the masses and adopt the mass line work method. In the past, cultural and educational work often had the tendency of doing everything for the masses, and we must correct this. We must adhere to the principle of voluntarism in work and consult with the masses on everything. For those undertakings that are truly needed by the masses, have the conditions, and are voluntarily carried out by the masses, such as people's schools, literacy classes, clubs, amateur drama troupes, etc., we should support and help them and strengthen leadership. Of course, when using the strength of the masses, we must always pay attention to cherishing manpower and time and not arbitrarily increase the burden on the people.

Fourth, regarding the improvement of people's health:

During the second five-year plan period, we must continue to develop the health and medical industry, further carry out sports, and appropriately promote family planning.

In recent years, the Patriotic Health Campaign has played a significant role in improving environmental hygiene and reducing diseases. However, in recent years, we have relaxed the leadership of the Patriotic Health Campaign, and in the future, we should continue to vigorously carry out this campaign and make it more in-depth and regular to further improve the environmental hygiene of cities and villages and reduce the incidence of various infectious diseases and occupational diseases. At the same time, we should actively promote the experience of treating schistosomiasis and carry out the planned and phased elimination of serious local diseases. The primary medical organizations in cities and villages across the country have played a significant role in preventing and treating diseases, and in the future, the health department should strengthen their leadership.

In the health and medical industry, there are still many shortcomings, such as: due to poor management in hospitals, high fees, making the limited number of beds not fully utilized, and making some people unable to see a doctor or stay in a hospital; sanatorium beds lack unified management, causing great waste; in addition, there are also inappropriate places in the public medical care system and medical work system. To eliminate these unreasonable phenomena, the health department should seriously investigate and study and propose feasible improvement measures.

We should further carry out sports among the broad masses to effectively enhance the physical fitness of the people and improve the level of sports in China. When carrying out sports, we must proceed based on the specific situation of people's production, work, study, and physical conditions, and in a differentiated and phased manner, avoiding the tendency of setting too high and urgent requirements and generalizing.

To protect women and children, to raise the next generation well, and to benefit the health and prosperity of the nation, we support appropriate control in the aspect of childbirth. The health department should cooperate with relevant parties to appropriately publicize the issue of family planning and take effective measures.

Eleventh, regarding the continued promotion of thrift:

Thrift is a fine tradition of the Chinese people. All state organs, state-owned enterprises, institutions, and cooperatives should promote thrift to make full use of manpower, material resources, and financial resources to accelerate the construction of Chinese socialism.

A year ago, the Central Committee of the Party and the State Council called on all state organ workers and the people of the whole country to promote thrift, oppose waste, and overcome the phenomena of excessive non-productive construction, high costs of productive construction, poor quality of projects and products, large material losses, and bloated organizations and idle personnel. After more than a year of efforts, significant achievements have been made. However, it should be pointed out that the policy of thrift has not been well implemented in all departments, and the phenomenon of waste still exists; at the same time, in the process of promoting thrift and opposing rightist conservative thoughts, due to one-sided thrift views or the tendency of pursuing quantity and speed while neglecting quality and economy, the quality of projects and products has also encountered many problems, some of which require rework and repair, some of which will reduce effectiveness, and even become waste, thus not only failing to achieve the goal of thrift but also causing waste.

During the second five-year plan period, due to the expansion of the scale of national construction, we will encounter many difficulties in material supply, funding sources, and technical forces, and promoting thrift and rationally using manpower, material resources, and financial resources is an important way to overcome these difficulties. It should be admitted that the good or bad of a plan has a significant impact on thrift or waste. The thrift caused by a plan is the greatest thrift, and the waste caused by a plan is the greatest waste. Therefore, all levels of state organs and enterprise departments should first do a good job in planning. All enterprise units should strengthen quota management, promote various reasonable advanced quotas; strengthen technical management, improve the quality of products and projects, reduce defective products, substandard products, and project quality accidents; implement the responsibility system, overcome the phenomenon of no one being responsible, to prevent waste and tap the potential of thrift. All institutional units should reduce unnecessary institutional expenditures and personnel quotas, strengthen financial management and auditing work, to reduce the proportion of institutional funds in the budget. All cooperatives should continue to implement the policy of thrift in management.

In state administrative organs, we should continue to overcome the phenomena of overlapping organizations and idle personnel. Currently, in various levels of state administrative organs, generally speaking, the organizations are still too large, and the personnel are still too many, and this phenomenon is more serious in higher-level organs and larger organs than in lower-level organs and smaller organs. We must take effective measures to continue to adjust the organizations and personnel of various levels, streamline administrative organs, appropriately transfer organ personnel to production units, and transfer non-production personnel to production units. This is an effective way to implement the policy of thrift in state organs at present.

Twelfth, regarding the strengthening of unity and cooperation with the Soviet Union and other people's democratic countries, and the expansion of international economic, technological, and cultural cooperation and exchanges:

To complete our socialist construction, in addition to mobilizing all positive factors at home, we must also unite all possible forces internationally and strive for all favorable conditions internationally. We have always striven to strengthen unity with the great Soviet Union and other people's democratic countries and carry out all-round cooperation and mutual assistance with them. We have also striven to develop economic cooperation, trade exchanges, and cultural and technological exchanges with countries of different social systems, especially with Asian and African countries.

The mutual assistance and cooperation relationship between China and the Soviet Union and other people's democratic countries is based on an unbreakable brotherly friendship and aims to promote the common prosperity of the socialist countries' economies and continuously improve the material welfare and cultural level of the people of socialist countries.

As mentioned earlier, whether in the period of restoring the national economy or in the period of implementing the first five-year plan for developing the national economy, China has received tremendous and sincere assistance from the Soviet Union in all aspects, and has also received significant assistance from various brotherly countries. This assistance has helped us overcome many difficulties and enabled China's socialist construction to develop at a relatively high speed. During the second five-year plan period, the Soviet Union and other people's democratic countries will continue to provide us with large-scale brotherly assistance, especially those large enterprises designed and equipped by the Soviet Union and many people's democratic countries will further lay the foundation for China's socialist industrialization. In the past, we have benefited a lot from learning the advanced construction experience and scientific and technological achievements of the Soviet Union and other people's democratic countries, and in the future, we must continue to learn from them humbly.

As a member of the socialist camp, China has its own responsibility, and we must do a good job in this regard. We have the obligation to supply various brotherly countries with many agricultural products, animal products, mineral raw materials, and some machinery and equipment, and industrial products needed in their construction. We must strive to increase production or appropriately save domestic consumption to ensure the supply of these products. To carry out equal and mutually beneficial, complementary trade with other countries, we also need to properly plan our domestic production and consumption to ensure necessary exports.

In recent years, China's economic, technological, and cultural cooperation and exchanges with many Asian and African countries have become increasingly close. Especially since the Bandung Conference, this cooperation and exchange has developed more broadly. Most Asian and African countries urgently need to eliminate the economic and cultural backwardness caused by long-term colonial rule, and deeply feel the necessity of economic and cultural cooperation with each other. China has always advocated carrying out such cooperation with Asian and African countries on the basis of equality, mutual benefit, and no conditions attached, promoting their independent development in the economy and technology, and developing their own strengths and learning from each other in culture. This cooperation helps to safeguard the national independence of Asian and African countries and expand the peaceful regions, and is therefore beneficial to China's peaceful construction. Although the scope of this cooperation is not very large at present, it is important that Asian and African countries have begun to cooperate and are expanding their ties with Latin American countries, and such cooperation and exchanges undoubtedly have a broad prospect for development.

We are also willing to develop economic, technological, and cultural ties with other countries in the world based on the principle of equality and mutual benefit. We have always been working to expand trade with Western countries and are willing to absorb useful things from their scientific and technological and management methods for our construction. Although the United States has imposed an embargo on us and forced many countries to adopt the same policy, this policy that completely violates the interests of the peoples of all countries has already encountered increasingly strong opposition from all sides. This unreasonable man-made obstacle will eventually be eliminated.

We advocate the expansion of international economic, technological, and cultural cooperation and exchanges, not only to accelerate the completion of our socialist construction but also because this will lay a reliable foundation for peaceful coexistence among countries. Therefore, this is completely in line with the interests of the people of the world and completely in line with the interests of the peace cause.

Comrades, the day of the successful completion of the first five-year plan and the start of the second five-year plan is not far away. In the next year or so, all comrades of the Party should, under the leadership of the Central Committee of the Party and Comrade Mao Zedong, redouble their efforts, together with the workers, peasants, and intellectuals of the whole country, and together with all ethnic groups, parties, and patriotic individuals of the whole country, strive to overfulfill the first five-year plan and actively prepare for the second five-year plan. As long as we are modest and prudent, not arrogant or impatient, and correct the subjective and bureaucratic tendencies in our work, we will be able to mobilize all forces, overcome all difficulties, and advance victoriously in the struggle to build China into a great socialist industrial power!
