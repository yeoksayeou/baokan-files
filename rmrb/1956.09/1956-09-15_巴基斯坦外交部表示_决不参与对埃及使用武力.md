Title: Pakistan Foreign Ministry States It Will Never Participate in the Use of Force Against Egypt
Author:
Date: 1956-09-15
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, Karachi, September 14th: A Foreign Ministry spokesman said on September 13th that Pakistan has always advocated resolving the Suez Canal issue peacefully through negotiations with Egypt. He said that Pakistan will not participate in the use of force under any circumstances.

Prior to the release of this brief statement, Foreign Secretary Baig and Prime Minister Suhrawardy held emergency consultations. Before this, the British Deputy High Commissioner had conveyed the views of the three major Western powers to the Pakistani Foreign Ministry. According to newspaper reports, officials here are concerned about recent developments regarding the Suez Canal issue. The general opinion here seems to favor referring the dispute to the United Nations for resolution, and it is believed that Pakistan is unlikely to endorse the proposal of the three major powers to establish a Canal Users' Association to manage the Suez Canal.

### 巴基斯坦外交部表示  决不参与对埃及使用武力

1956-09-15
第5版()
专栏：

    巴基斯坦外交部表示
    决不参与对埃及使用武力
    据新华社喀喇蚩14日电　外交部发言人9月13日说，巴基斯坦一直主张通过同埃及谈判的方法和平地解决苏彝士运河问题。他说，巴基斯坦无论在什么情况下都不参与使用武力。
    在发表这项简短声明之前，外交部秘书巴伊格和总理苏拉瓦底举行了紧急会商。在这以前，英国副高级专员曾经将西方三大国的看法转达给巴基斯坦外交部。据报纸消息，这里的官方人士对苏彝士运河问题最近的发展感到关切。这里一般意见似乎赞成提到联合国去解决这个争端，并且认为巴基斯坦不大可能赞同三大国关于成立运河使用国协会来管理苏彝士运河的建议。
