Title: British Labour Party Decides to Impeach the Eden Government
Author:
Date: 1956-09-14
Page: 5
Xinhua News Agency, London, September 13th. British Prime Minister Eden's speech in the House of Commons on September 12th has placed the Conservative and Labour parties in serious division on how to deal with the Egyptian question. After listening to Eden's speech, the Labour Party decided on the evening of the 12th to propose a motion of impeachment against the government's policy. This motion of impeachment demands that the House of Commons express regret over the Conservative government's refusal to request the United Nations to handle the Suez dispute, and demands that the government immediately submit the dispute to the United Nations, declare that the government will not use force except in accordance with the obligations stipulated in the United Nations Charter, and will not take any form of provocative action.

    On the other hand, the Conservative government has also decided to put forward a motion of confidence, demanding support for the government's policy.

    These two proposals will be submitted when the House of Lords and the House of Commons reconvene on the 13th.

### 英工党决定弹劾艾登政府

1956-09-14
第5版()
专栏：

    英工党决定弹劾艾登政府
    新华社伦敦13日电　英国首相艾登9月12日在下院的发言使保守党和工党在如何对付埃及的问题上处于严重的分裂状态。工党在听取艾登的发言后，已经在12日晚上决定对政府的政策提出弹劾案。这个弹劾案要求下院对保守党政府拒绝请求联合国处理苏彝士争论表示遗憾，并且要求政府把这个争端立即提交联合国，宣布政府除了按照联合国宪章所规定的义务以外将不使用武力，同时不采取任何形式的挑衅行动。
    另一方面，保守党政府也已经决定提出信任案，要求支持政府的政策。
    这两个提案将在上院和下院13日复会的时候提出。
