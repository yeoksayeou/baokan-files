Title: Respecting Egyptian National Sovereignty and Promoting Peaceful Resolution of the Canal Issue: Soviet Union and Other Countries Support New Proposals from the Egyptian Government
Author:
Date: 1956-09-16
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, September 15th, Cairo Dispatch: TASS reported from Cairo on September 15th: The Soviet Embassy in Cairo delivered a note from the Soviet government to the Egyptian government on September 14th. This note was a reply to the Egyptian government's note to various governments on September 10th regarding the Suez Canal issue. In its note, the Egyptian government expressed its willingness to convene a conference with the governments of other countries that signed the 1888 Convention of Constantinople, in order to review this convention and discuss the issue of signing an agreement to ensure freedom of navigation in the Suez Canal. The Egyptian government also suggested establishing a negotiating body that adequately represents the differing opinions of the countries using the Suez Canal as a direct step.

The full text of the Soviet government's reply note to the Egyptian government is as follows:

The Embassy of the Union of Soviet Socialist Republics in Egypt presents its compliments to the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Egypt and has the honor to express the following opinion in response to the Egyptian government's note of September 10, 1956:

The Soviet government notes with satisfaction the Egyptian government's statement that it is willing to resolve the Suez issue peacefully, strictly in accordance with the principles of the United Nations Charter, while respecting Egyptian sovereignty and the interests of the Suez Canal user states.

The Soviet government hopes to promote a peaceful settlement of the Suez issue and is prepared to participate in an international conference to review the 1888 Convention and discuss the conclusion of an agreement ensuring freedom of navigation in the Suez Canal. It also notes that the countries participating in this conference will include the signatories of the 1888 Convention, including the legal successors of the countries that signed the aforementioned convention, Arab countries whose territories are close to the Suez Canal and are closely concerned with a just solution to this issue, and other countries using the Suez Canal.

In view of the above, and for the sake of a peaceful solution to the Suez issue, the Soviet government expresses its willingness to participate in the negotiating body proposed by the Egyptian government, which represents the different opinions of the countries using the Suez Canal, in order to seek an acceptable basis for resolving the Suez Canal issue and convening the aforementioned conference.

Xinhua News Agency, September 15th, Cairo Dispatch: The Saudi Arabian government has notified the Egyptian government that the Saudi Arabian government unreservedly agrees with Egypt's proposal to organize a negotiating body to peacefully resolve the Suez Canal issue.

Xinhua News Agency, September 15th, Karachi Dispatch: According to a report by the Pakistan Press International on September 14th, Pakistan has expressed its willingness to participate in the conference suggested by Egypt, with broad national participation, to discuss the Suez Canal issue.

Xinhua News Agency, September 15th, Prague Dispatch: According to a report by the Czechoslovak News Agency on the evening of September 14th, Czechoslovak Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs, Mrs. Sekaninova-Čakrtová, received Egyptian Ambassador Hanna on the 13th and handed him the Czechoslovak government's reply to Egypt's note of September 11th.

The reply note expresses the Czechoslovak government's agreement with the content of the Egyptian government's note, especially the agreement to establish the proposed negotiating body; and states that it is willing to participate in discussions regarding the composition, location, and date of the meeting of this body. The Czechoslovak government believes that issues such as freedom and safety of navigation in the canal, expansion of the canal, and determination of tolls can be resolved through peaceful negotiations without prejudice to Egypt's sovereignty and dignity.

Xinhua News Agency, September 15th, Berlin Dispatch: According to a message from the Press Office of the Prime Minister of the German Democratic Republic: Prime Minister of the German Democratic Republic, Grotewohl, issued a statement on September 14th regarding the Suez Canal issue, welcoming the Egyptian government's proposal to convene an international conference. At this conference, all issues of concern to the users of the Suez Canal and the issue of ensuring free navigation of the canal will be discussed with full respect for Egyptian sovereignty.

### 尊重埃及国家主权并促进和平解决运河问题  苏联等国支持埃及政府的新建议

1956-09-16
第6版()
专栏：

    尊重埃及国家主权并促进和平解决运河问题
    苏联等国支持埃及政府的新建议
    新华社15日讯　据塔斯社开罗15日讯：苏联驻开罗大使馆在9月14日把苏联政府的照会一件交给埃及政府。这个照会是答复埃及政府9月10日就苏彝士运河问题给各国政府的照会的。埃及政府在照会中表示准备同签订1888年君士坦丁堡公约的其他国家政府一起召开一次会议，以便重新审查这一公约和讨论关于签订确保苏彝士运河通航自由的协定的问题，埃及政府还建议成立一个足以代表使用苏彝士运河各国的不同意见的谈判机构作为直接的步骤。
    苏联政府给埃及政府的复照全文如下：
    苏维埃社会主义共和国联盟驻埃及大使馆向埃及外交部表示敬意，并且荣幸地表示下列意见作为对埃及政府1956年9月10日的照会的答复：
    苏联政府满意地接受了埃及政府关于它愿意严格根据联合国宪章的原则、在尊重埃及的主权和苏彝士运河使用国的利益的条件下，以和平的方式来解决苏彝士问题的声明。
    苏联政府希望促进和平解决苏彝士问题，它准备参加关于重新审查1888年公约和讨论缔结一项确保苏彝士运河通航自由的协定问题的国际会议。同时注意到，参加这个会议的国家将有1888年公约签订国，其中包括签订上述公约国家的合法继承国、领土靠近苏彝士运河并且密切关心公正解决这一问题的阿拉伯国家以及使用苏彝士运河的其他国家。
    苏联政府鉴于上述情况，并且从和平解决苏彝士问题的利益出发，表示愿意参加埃及政府提出的代表使用苏彝士运河的各国的不同意见的谈判机构，以便为解决苏彝士运河问题和召开上述会议寻求可接受的基础。
    新华社15日讯　开罗消息：沙特阿拉伯政府已经通知埃及政府，沙特阿拉伯政府无保留地赞同埃及提出的组织谈判机构来和平解决苏彝士运河问题的建议。
    据新华社喀喇蚩15日电　据巴基斯坦联合通讯社9月14日报道，巴基斯坦已经表示愿意参加埃及建议召开的有广泛国家参加的讨论苏彝士运河问题的会议。
    据新华社布拉格15日电　据捷克斯洛伐克通讯社14日晚间报道，捷克斯洛伐克外交部副部长塞卡尼诺娃—查克尔托娃13日接见了埃及大使汉纳，把捷克斯洛伐克政府对埃及在9月11日提出的照会的复照交给了他。
    复照表示捷克斯洛伐克政府同意埃及政府的照会的内容，特别是同意建立拟议中的谈判机构；并且表示它愿意参加关于这个机构的组成、开会的地点和日期的讨论。捷克斯洛伐克政府认为，像运河的航行自由和航行安全、扩建运河和确定通行费这样一些问题是可以在不损害埃及的主权和尊严的情况下通过和平谈判获得解决的。
    新华社柏林15日电　据德意志民主共和国总理新闻处消息：德意志民主共和国总理格罗提渥9月14日就苏彝士运河问题发表声明表示欢迎埃及政府提出的召开一次国际会议的建议。在这次会议上，将在完全尊重埃及主权的条件下讨论苏彝士运河使用国所关心的一切问题以及保障运河的自由通航问题。
