Title: Celebrating the Liberation of Pyongyang · Demonstrating to the Aggressors: Beijing Literary and Art Circles Hold Parade Rally, Determined to Use All Literary and Art Weapons to Further Strike at American Imperialism
Author:
Date: 1950-12-11
Page: 1
Xinhua News Agency, December 10th: Beijing's literary and art circles and representatives from the National Opera Work Conference, totaling over three thousand people, held a parade rally today to celebrate the liberation of Pyongyang.

The ranks of various literary and art workers participating in the demonstration gathered at Tiananmen Square at 1 p.m. The participating units included: All-China Federation of Literary and Art Circles, representatives of the National Opera Work Conference, Central Institute of Literature, Central Academy of Drama, Central Academy of Fine Arts, China Youth Art Theatre, Beijing Opera Research Institute, Experimental Opera School, Railway Cultural Troupe, Beijing Film Studio, Beijing Federation of Literary and Art Workers, Beijing Laboring People's Cultural Palace, Beijing Workers' Broadcasting Cultural Troupe, Beijing People's Art Theatre, New Opera Research Association, Beijing Peking Opera Guild, Beijing Ping Opera Guild, Beijing Quyi Guild, People's Fine Arts Workshop, the Communist Youth League Beijing Municipal Committee's Department of Literature and Art, student waist drum teams, children's art troupes, etc.

The rally was led by Zhou Yang, Vice Chairman of the All-China Federation of Literary and Art Circles, as the overall leader, and Li Bozhao, Vice Chairman of the Beijing Federation of Literary and Art Circles, as the overall commander.

The participating parade showcased the characteristics of the literary and art circles in various artistic forms. Beneath thousands of red flags were waist drum teams, yangko dance teams, music teams, broadcasting singing teams, cartoon teams, and costumed tableau teams.

The rally began at 2 p.m. First, Zhou Yang, Ding Ling, Li Bozhao, and others reviewed the assembled ranks. After the review, Zhou Yang addressed the rally, saying: The liberation of Pyongyang is not only a victory for the Korean people but also a victory for the Chinese people and all peace-loving people around the world. This victory proves that American imperialism is a paper tiger that can be defeated. Our literary and art circles must use all literary and art weapons to further strike at the aggressors to defend world peace. The rally unanimously passed a telegram of respect to Chairman Mao and General Kim Il-sung. The parade demonstration then began, with the mighty literary and art contingent departing from Tiananmen Square towards Xinhua Gate.

The parade was led by a military band and a red flag honor guard, followed by huge portraits of Chairman Mao, Marshal Stalin, and General Kim Il-sung, as well as portraits of people's leaders from various countries. Next were the parade contingents of various literary and art workers led by Zhou Yang, Ding Ling, Zhou Jianren, Tian Han, A Ying, Ouyang Yuqian, Shakov, Huang Yaomian, Cao Yu, Li Bozhao, Yuan Muzhi, Feng Zhi, Bian Zhilin, Yang Hui, Zhou Xinfang, Yi Bing, Ma Jianling, Li Lun, Qin Youmei, Gao Baisui, Zhang Decheng, Gai Jiaotian, Ma Decheng, Shang Heyu, Xiao Changhua, Liu Xikui, Bao Jixiang, Li Shaochun, Yuan Shihai, and Ye Shengzhang. Various parade contingents held high red flags and slogans such as "Defend World Lasting Peace," "Down with American Imperialism," "Resist America, Aid Korea, Defend Our Homes and Country," and "Celebrate the Liberation of Pyongyang." As the parade moved forward, it also performed. Beijing Quyi workers and the Workers' Broadcasting Cultural Troupe used broadcast vehicles to sing works such as "The Angry Tide Surges on the Yalu River," "Blood Debt Must Be Paid in Blood," "Accuse the Crimes of American Imperialism," and "The Peace Camp is Strong." The parade was welcomed by the broad masses of citizens, and tens of thousands of citizens spontaneously followed the parade and shouted slogans such as "Long Live the Solidarity of the Chinese and Korean People" and "Long Live the Great Unity of the People of the World." Many schools and shops set up tea stalls at their doors to welcome the parade.

As the procession approached the Soviet Embassy in Dongjiaomin Lane, playwright Cao Yu excitedly led the crowd in shouting "Long Live the Solidarity of the Chinese and Soviet, Chinese and Korean People!", the shouts reverberating through the clouds.

The parade passed through Xinhua Gate, Liubukou, Hepingmen, Hufangqiao, Zhushikou, Qianmen, Dongjiaomin Lane, Taijichang, Wangfujing, and finally to Dong'an Market. The parade concluded at 5 p.m. amidst the cheers of the masses. (Attached image)

### 庆祝平壤解放·向侵略者示威  京文艺界举行游行大会  决心运用一切文艺武器进一步打击美帝

1950-12-11
第1版()
专栏：

    庆祝平壤解放·向侵略者示威
    京文艺界举行游行大会
    决心运用一切文艺武器进一步打击美帝
    【新华社十日讯】北京文艺界和全国戏曲工作会议的代表共三千多人，今日举行庆祝平壤解放游行大会。
    参加游行示威的各种文艺工作者的队伍，于下午一时齐集天安门广场。参加的单位有：中华全国文学艺术界联合会、全国戏曲工作会议的代表、中央文学研究所、中央戏剧学院、中央美术学院、中国青年艺术剧院、京剧研究院、戏曲实验学校、铁道文工团、北京电影制片厂、北京市文学艺术工作者联合会、北京劳动人民文化宫、北京工人广播文工团、北京人民艺术剧院、新戏曲研究会、北京京剧公会、北京评戏公会、北京曲艺公会、人民美术工作社、新民主主义青年团北京市委会文艺部、学生腰鼓队、儿童文工团等。
    大会由中华全国文学艺术界联合会副主席周扬任总领队，北京市文联副主席李伯钊任总指挥。
    参加游行的队伍以各种各样的艺术形式表现了文艺界的特色，在千百面红旗之下，有腰鼓队、秧歌队、音乐队、广播演唱队、漫画队、化装活报队等。
    大会于下午二时开始，首先由周扬、丁玲、李伯钊等检阅了到会的队伍。检阅后周扬向大会发表讲话，他说：平壤解放，不仅是朝鲜人民的胜利，而且是中国人民和全世界爱好和平的人民的胜利。这一胜利证明了美国帝国主义是可以打垮的一只纸老虎。我们文艺界要运用一切文艺武器更进一步地打击侵略者，以保卫世界和平。大会一致通过通电向毛主席及金日成将军致敬。接着开始游行示威，浩浩荡荡的文艺队伍由天安门广场向新华门出发。
    游行队以军乐队和红旗的仪仗队为前导，接着是毛主席、斯大林大元帅、金日成将军的巨幅画像和各国人民领袖的画像，其次是由周扬、丁玲、周建人、田汉、阿英、欧阳予倩、沙可夫、黄药眠、曹禺、李伯钊、袁牧之、冯至、卞之琳、杨晦、周信芳、伊兵、马健翎、李纶、秦友梅、高百岁、张德成、盖叫天、马德成、尚和玉、萧长华、刘喜奎、鲍吉祥、李少春、袁世海、叶盛章所领导的各种文艺工作者的游行队伍。各种游行队伍都高举着红旗和“保卫世界持久和平”、“打倒美国帝国主义”、“抗美援朝保家卫国”“庆祝平壤解放”等标语。游行队伍一面行进，一面表演，北京曲艺工作者和工人广播文工团利用广播车向群众演唱了“鸭绿江边怒潮涌”、“血债血还”、“控诉美帝暴行”、“和平阵营力量大”等作品。游行队伍得到广大市民的欢迎，成千成万的市民都自动跟着游行队伍一道行进，并一道高呼“中朝人民团结万岁”、“世界人民大团结万岁”等口号。许多学校和商店都在门口摆设茶水，招待游行队伍。
    队伍行近东交民巷苏联大使馆时，剧作家曹禺兴奋地领头高呼“中苏、中朝人民团结万岁！”，口号声震云霄。
    游行队伍经过了新华门、六部口、和平门、虎坊桥、珠市口、前门、东交民巷、台基厂、王府井，最后到东安市场。游行于下午五时在群众的欢呼声中结束。（附图片）
