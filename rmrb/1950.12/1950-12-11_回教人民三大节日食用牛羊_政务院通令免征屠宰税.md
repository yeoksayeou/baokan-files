Title: Government Administration Council Issues Circular Exempting Slaughter Tax on Cattle and Sheep for the Three Major Festivals of Muslim People

Author: 
Date: 1950-12-11
Page: 2

    Muslim People's Three Major Festivals' Consumption of Cattle and Sheep
    Government Administration Council Issues Circular Exempting Slaughter Tax
    [Xinhua News Agency, 8th] The Central People's Government Administration Council issued a circular on exempting slaughter tax on cattle and sheep consumed during the three major festivals of Islamic faith for all ethnic groups. The circular states: "In order to accommodate ethnic customs, during the three major festivals of Islam (Muslim), namely Eid al-Adha (referred to as Rozi Festival in Xinjiang), Qurban (i.e., Eid al-Adha), and Mawlid (referred to as Maulud Festival in Xinjiang), slaughter tax should be exempted for cattle and sheep consumed by people of all ethnic groups who believe in Islam for their own consumption. For those with restrictions on cattle slaughter, the provincial (municipal) tax authorities should consult with the provincial (municipal) people's governments to formulate specific measures for relaxing inspection standards. Please comply accordingly."

### 回教人民三大节日食用牛羊  政务院通令免征屠宰税

1950-12-11
第2版()
专栏：

    回教人民三大节日食用牛羊
    政务院通令免征屠宰税
    【新华社八日讯】中央人民政府政务院发布关于信仰伊斯兰教各族人民三大节日食用牛羊免征屠宰税的通令。通令称：“为照顾民族习惯，遇伊斯兰教（回教）之尔代（新疆称肉孜节）、古尔邦（即宰牲节）、圣祭（新疆称冒路德节）三大节日，对信仰伊斯兰教各民族人民自己食用的牛羊，应予免征屠宰税；其有限制宰牛的规定者，并应由各省（市）税务机关商承省（市）人民政府定出放宽检验标准的具体办法。望即遵照办理。”
