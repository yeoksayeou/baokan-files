General Regulations on the Organization of District People's Governments and District Offices
(Passed at the 62nd Political Affairs Council Meeting of the Government Administration Council on December 8, 1950)

Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1: Any district that needs to be established as a level of political power may have a District People's Government established with the approval of the Provincial People's Government upon request from the County People's Government, and the provisions of Chapter II of these regulations shall apply.

Article 2: Any district that does not need to be established as a level of political power shall establish a District Office, which shall be a dispatched agency of the County People's Government. The provisions of Chapter III of these regulations shall apply.

Chapter II District People's Government

Article 3: The organs through which the people of the district exercise political power are the District People's Congress (or the District People's Representative Conference of All Circles exercising its functions and powers on its behalf, the same in the following articles) and the District People's Government. During the adjournment of the District People's Congress, the District People's Government shall be the organ through which the district exercises political power.

The District People's Government Committee is the local political power organ at the district level and is under the leadership of the County People's Government.

Article 4: The District People's Government Committee shall be composed of one District Head, Deputy District Heads, and a number of committee members elected by the District People's Congress, and shall be appointed by the Provincial People's Government upon reporting by the County People's Government.

Article 5: The term of office for the District Head, Deputy District Heads, and committee members shall be one year, and they may be re-elected.

Article 6: The District People's Government Committee shall exercise the following functions and powers under the leadership of the County People's Government:

	(1) Implement the resolutions and orders of the higher-level People's Government;
	(2) Implement the resolutions passed by the District People's Congress and approved by the higher-level People's Government;
	(3) Lead and supervise the work of the various departments of the District People's Government and the various townships under its jurisdiction;
	(4) Propose to the County People's Government the appointment and removal of the Township Head, Deputy Township Head, and committee members of the various Township People's Governments under its jurisdiction;
	(5) Abolish or revise, or propose to the County People's Government to abolish or revise, resolutions of the People's Congress or Representative Conference of All Circles of the various townships under its jurisdiction that contradict the policies and laws of the higher-level People's Government.

Article 7: The District Head shall preside over the meetings of the District People's Government Committee and administrative meetings, and lead the work of the District People's Government; the Deputy District Heads shall assist him.

District People's Government committee members may, according to regional division of labor, be responsible for guiding and supervising the work of the various Township People's Governments.

Article 8: The District People's Government shall have one secretary and a number of assistants to handle various tasks.

Article 9: The District People's Government may establish various standing and temporary committees as needed for its work.

Article 10: Meetings of the District People's Government Committee shall be held once a month, convened by the District Head. They may be convened earlier or postponed if necessary. A committee meeting may be held only if more than half of the committee members are present, and a resolution may be passed only if more than half of the committee members present agree.

The chairman and vice-chairman of the District People's Representative Conference of All Circles, the secretary and assistants of the District People's Government, etc., may attend the meetings of the District People's Government Committee as nonvoting delegates.

Article 11: The administrative meeting of the District People's Government shall be convened by the District Head; the Deputy District Heads, secretary, assistants, etc., of the District People's Government shall all attend.

Chapter III District Office

Article 12: The District Office shall have one District Head, Deputy District Heads, a secretary, and a number of assistants, who shall be appointed by the County People's Government.

Article 13: The District Office shall carry out the tasks assigned by the County People's Government and, under the orders of the County People's Government, guide, supervise, and assist the work of the Township People's Governments under its jurisdiction.

Article 14: The District Office may establish various standing and temporary committees as needed for its work.

Article 15: The administrative meeting of the District Office shall be convened by the District Head; the Deputy District Heads, secretary, and assistants, etc., shall all attend.

Article 16: In order to facilitate the transmission of policies, contact with the masses, and promote work, the District Office may convene an enlarged meeting of cadres, attended by some or all of the following personnel:

	(1) The chairman and vice-chairman of the Township People's Congress or Township People's Representative Conference, the Township Head and Deputy Township Head of the Township People's Government.
	(2) The persons in charge of district and township people's organizations.
	(3) Other relevant personnel.

Chapter IV Supplementary Provisions

Article 17: These regulations shall be implemented after being passed by the Government Administration Council of the Central People's Government; the same applies to their amendment.

### 区人民政府及区公所组织通则  （一九五零年十二月八日政务院第六十二次政务会议通过）

1950-12-31
第3版()
专栏：

    区人民政府及区公所组织通则
    （一九五零年十二月八日政务院第六十二次政务会议通过）
    第一章总则
    第一条  凡需作为一级政权的区，得由县人民政府呈请省人民政府批准设区人民政府，适用本通则第二章的规定。第二条  凡不需作为一级政权的区，设区公所，为县人民政府的派出机关。适用本通则第三章的规定。
    第二章  区人民政府
    第三条  区人民行使政权的机关为区人民代表大会（或代行其职权的区各界人民代表会议，以下各条款同）和区人民政府。在区人民代表大会闭会期间，区人民政府即为区的行使政权的机关。
    区人民政府委员会为区一级的地方政权机关，受县人民政府领导。
    第四条  区人民政府委员会由区人民代表大会选举区长一人、副区长及委员各若干人组成之，并由县人民政府报请省人民政府批准任命。
    第五条  区长、副区长及委员的任期为一年，连选得连任。
    第六条  区人民政府委员会在县人民政府领导下行使下列职权：
    （一）执行上级人民政府的决议和命令；
    （二）执行区人民代表大会通过并经上级人民政府批准的决议事项；
    （三）领导、检查区人民政府各部门及所辖各乡的工作；
    （四）提请县人民政府任免所辖各乡人民政府的乡长、副乡长及委员；
    （五）废除、修改或提请县人民政府废除、修改所辖各乡人民代表大会或代表会议与上级人民政府政策法令相抵触的决议。
    第七条  区长主持区人民政府委员会会议及行政会议，并领导区人民政府的工作，副区长协助之。
    区人民政府委员得依地区分工，负责指导、检查各乡人民政府的工作。
    第八条  区人民政府设秘书一人及助理员若干人，分工办理各项工作。
    第九条  区人民政府因工作需要，得设各种经常的及临时的委员会。
    第十条  区人民政府委员会会议每月举行一次，由区长召集之。必要时得提前或延期召开。委员会会议须有委员过半数的出席始得开会，须有出席委员过半数的同意始得通过决议。
    区各界人民代表会议的主席、副主席、区人民政府的秘书、助理员等均得列席区人民政府委员会会议。
    第十一条  区人民政府的行政会议，由区长召集之，区人民政府的副区长、秘书、助理员等均出席。
    第三章  区公所
    第十二条  区公所设区长一人，副区长、秘书及助理员若干人，由县人民政府委派之。
    第十三条  区公所执行县人民政府交办事项，并承县人民政府之命，指导、监督与协助所辖乡人民政府的工作。
    第十四条  区公所因工作需要得设各种经常的及临时的委员会。
    第十五条  区公所的行政会议，由区长召集之；副区长、秘书及助理员等均出席。
    第十六条  区公所为便于传达政策、联系群众、推动工作起见，得召开干部扩大会议，由下列人员一部或全部参加：
    （一）乡人民代表大会或乡人民代表会议主席、副主席，乡人民政府乡长、副乡长。
    （二）区和乡人民团体的负责人。
    （三）其他有关人员。
    第四章  附则
    第十七条  本通则经中央人民政府政务院政务会议通过后施行，其修改同。
