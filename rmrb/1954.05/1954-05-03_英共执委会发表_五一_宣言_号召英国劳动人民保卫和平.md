Title: British Communist Executive Committee Issues "May Day" Manifesto, Calling on British Working People to Defend Peace
Author:
Date: 1954-05-03
Page: 2

    British Communist Executive Committee Issues "May Day" Manifesto
    Calling on British Working People to Defend Peace
    TASS, London: The Executive Committee of the British Communist Party has issued a "May Day" manifesto. The manifesto states that the primary task of working people today is "to fight for peace." Most importantly: The British people should firmly refuse to participate in any military intervention organized by the United States against any country in the world.
    The manifesto puts forward the following demands: "Britain should not participate in the intervention in Indochina organized by the United States. Demand that the Americans withdraw from Britain. End the wars in Kenya, Malaya, and Indochina, and let the people manage their own affairs. Establish friendly and trade relations with the people of all countries. Reduce military spending and use the money for pensions and all social welfare and services."
                              (Xinhua News Agency)

### 英共执委会发表“五一”宣言  号召英国劳动人民保卫和平

1954-05-03
第2版()
专栏：

    英共执委会发表“五一”宣言
    号召英国劳动人民保卫和平
    据塔斯社伦敦讯：英国共产党执行委员会发表了一篇“五一”宣言。宣言说，劳动人民当前的主要任务是“为和平而斗争”。最重要的是：英国人民应当坚决不参加美国所组织的对世界上任何国家的任何军事干涉。
    宣言提出下列要求：“英国不参加美国所组织的对印度支那的干涉。要求美国人撤出英国。终止怯尼亚、马来亚和印度支那的战争，并让人民管理他们自己的事务。和所有国家的人民建立友好和贸易关系。裁减军费，把这些钱作为养老金和一切社会福利与服务的费用。”
                              （新华社）
