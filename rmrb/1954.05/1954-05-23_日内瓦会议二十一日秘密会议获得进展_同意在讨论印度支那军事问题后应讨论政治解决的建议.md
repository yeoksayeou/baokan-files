Title: Geneva Conference Twenty-first Secret Meeting Makes Progress; Agreement Reached to Discuss Proposals for a Political Solution After Discussing Indochina Military Issues
Author:
Date: 1954-05-23
Page: 4

    Xinhua News Agency, Geneva, May 21st: The delegations of the nine countries participating in the Geneva Conference on the Indochina issue issued the following communique after a restricted meeting held on the afternoon of the 21st:
    "At the restricted meeting on May 21st, the nine delegations continued to discuss the issue of restoring peace in Indochina. The next meeting on this issue will be held on May 24th."
    Xinhua News Agency, May 22nd: TASS, Geneva, May 22nd: According to information obtained here, the foreign ministers continued to discuss the issue of restoring peace in Indochina at the secret meeting on May 21st. The news indicates that, as a result of the exchange of views, a certain degree of progress has been made in the further discussion of the Indochina issue. The participants in the meeting agreed to first discuss the main issues included in the proposals of the Delegation of the Democratic Republic of Vietnam and the proposals of the French Delegation.
    Among these issues are: the simultaneous ceasefire throughout Indochina; the determination of concentration areas for the troops of the belligerent parties; the cessation of the transport of new troops and weapons into Indochina; the supervision by a neutral nations commission, concurrently with the supervision by a mixed commission composed of representatives of the belligerent parties, of the implementation of the terms of the armistice agreement; and the provision of guarantees by the participants in the Geneva Conference.
    According to available information, an agreement has been reached at the meeting that after the discussion of military issues, proposals related to the political settlement of Indochina should also be discussed.
    The participants in the meeting reached an agreement that, in addition to the points unanimously agreed to be discussed above, issues raised by the participants in the meeting could also be discussed. The meeting is also prepared to discuss the proposals put forward by other delegations. It is reported that the next meeting to discuss the Indochina issue will be held on Monday, May 24th.
    After the meeting on May 21st, Geneva observers hope that the procedure for discussing the Indochina issue, adopted at the initiative of the Soviet delegation, will help to discuss this issue more effectively.

### 日内瓦会议二十一日秘密会议获得进展  同意在讨论印度支那军事问题后应讨论政治解决的建议

1954-05-23
第4版()
专栏：

    日内瓦会议二十一日秘密会议获得进展
    同意在讨论印度支那军事问题后应讨论政治解决的建议
    【新华社日内瓦二十一日电】参加日内瓦会议关于印度支那问题讨论的九个国家的代表团二十一日下午举行了限制性的会议后发表公报如下：
    “在五月二十一日的限制性会议中，九国代表团继续讨论恢复印度支那和平问题。关于此问题的下一次会议将在五月二十四日举行。”
    【新华社二十二日讯】塔斯社日内瓦二十二日讯：根据此间得到的消息，外长们在五月二十一日的秘密会议上继续讨论恢复印度支那和平的问题。消息指出，交换意见的结果，在印度支那问题的进一步讨论方面已经获得了一定的进展。会议的参加者同意首先讨论越南民主共和国代表团的建议和法国代表团的建议中所包括的主要问题。
    这些问题中有：印度支那全境同时停火的问题；确定交战双方部队的集中地区的问题；停止把新的军队和武器运入印度支那的问题；与由交战双方代表所组成的混合委员会监督停战协定条款的执行的同时，由中立国委员会加以监督的问题；日内瓦会议的参加者提供保证的问题。
    根据现有的消息，在会上已获致协议：在讨论了军事问题以后，也应该讨论有关印度支那的政治解决的建议。
    会议参加者获致协议，除以上一致同意加以讨论的各点以外，还可以讨论会议参加者所提出的问题。会议也准备讨论其他代表团所提出的建议。据悉，下一次讨论印度支那问题的会议将于五月二十四日（星期一）举行。
    在五月二十一日的会议以后，日内瓦观察家希望，在苏联代表团倡议下通过的讨论印度支那问题的程序，将有助于更有效地讨论这个问题。
