Unearthed Cultural Relics from National Capital Construction Projects

    With the progress of the nation's socialist industrialization and the large-scale capital construction projects in various regions, ancient cultural sites and ancient tombs buried underground have been successively excavated, and scientifically sorted and preserved. These discovered ancient relics are not only valuable materials for the history of material culture of China through the ages, but also concrete materials for conducting patriotic education for the broad masses of the people. Now the Ministry of Culture of the Central People's Government has concentrated a part of the ancient cultural relics unearthed in the capital construction projects of various regions in the Capital Museum of History for exhibition. With the increasing development of capital construction work, there will inevitably be more discoveries of ancient cultural relics. Doing a good job in protecting cultural relics in capital construction projects is the responsibility of all the people of our country.
   ·Image Description·
    One
    The skull fossil of Ziyang Man in Sichuan Province was unearthed in the late Pleistocene stratum of the Quaternary Period, approximately contemporaneous with the "Cro-Magnon Man" in Europe or the "Upper Cave Man" in Zhoukoudian, China, providing valuable information and clues for the study of physical evolution of ancient humans in China.
    Two
  　Glazed Pottery Zun (Shang Dynasty, approximately 1562-1066 BC) was unearthed from a Shang Dynasty tomb in Erligang, Zhengzhou, and is the earliest glazed pottery.
    Three
    Flat Bronze He (Zhou Dynasty, approximately 1066-771 BC) unearthed in Luoyang, Henan, its shape is outstanding among ancient bronzes.
    Four
  　Bronze Ding, Bronze Lei, Bronze Yi (Spring and Autumn Period, 770-403 BC) were unearthed in Jia County, Henan Province. This is an important discovery for the study of Chinese Spring and Autumn history and art materials.
    Five
    Iron Molds (Warring States Period, 403-221 BC), including more than 70 pieces of hoe molds, sickle molds, and axe molds, this is a part of them. This batch of production tool molds was unearthed in Rehe. This is not only new material to help us understand the form, effectiveness, and productivity of agricultural and handicraft production tools at that time, but also a sign of the level of the iron casting industry at that time.
    Six
  　Iron Plowshare and Hoe (Han Dynasty, 206 BC - 220 AD) unearthed from several construction sites in Chang'an County and Lintong County, Shaanxi Province, providing new materials for the study of agricultural productivity two thousand years ago.
    Seven
  　Pottery Horse (Han Dynasty, 206 BC - 220 AD) unearthed in Yangzishan, Chengdu. The horse is accurately shaped, with a vigorous and lively posture, full of vitality. This proves that two thousand years ago, the working people of our country had a high degree of achievement in sculpture art.
    Eight
  　Bronze Chariot (Eastern Han Dynasty, 25-220 AD) unearthed in Nanyang County, Henan Province.
    Nine
  　Murals (Eastern Han Dynasty, 25-220 AD) were found in Han tombs in Wangdu County, Hebei Province, and are the most important remains of Han murals other than Liaoyang.
    Ten
  　Singing Female Figurines (Northern Wei Dynasty, 386-534 AD) unearthed at the Caichangpo construction site in the suburbs of Xi'an.
    Eleven
  　Murals (Tang Dynasty, 618-907 AD) unearthed in Xianyang County, Shaanxi Province.
    Twelve
  　Glazed Figurines (Song Dynasty, 960-1279 AD) unearthed in Guanghan, Sichuan.
    Thirteen
  　Female Figurines (Yuan Dynasty, 1260-1368 AD) unearthed at the construction site of the Huguang Yiyuan School in the suburbs of Xi'an.
    Fourteen
  　Pottery Figurines (Yuan Dynasty, 1260-1368 AD) unearthed at the construction site in the eastern suburbs of Jinan. The figurine has a high nose, deep eyes, long beard, and a silk turban, and is presumed to be a "Semu person" at that time. 1234567891011121314 Second 78 Issues

### 全国基本建设工程中的出土文物（图片）

1954-05-24
第5版()
专栏：

    全国基本建设工程中的出土文物
    随着国家社会主义工业化的进展和各地区大规模基本建设工程的开展，埋藏地下的古文化遗址、古墓葬，陆续被发掘出来，并得到科学的整理与保存。这些被发现的古代文物，不仅是我国历代物质文化史的宝贵资料，而且是对于广大人民进行爱国主义教育的具体材料。现在中央人民政府文化部把各地区在基本建设工程中出土的古代文物的一部分，集中在首都历史博物馆展出。随着基本建设工作的日益开展，古代文物必将会有更多的发现。在基本建设工程中做好保护文物工作乃是我国全体人民的责任。
   ·图片说明·
    一
    四川资阳人头骨化在第四纪更新统晚期的地层中出土，与欧洲的 “克鲁马努人”或我国周口店的“山顶洞人”约略同时，为研究我国古代人类体质进化上提供了珍贵的资料和线索。
    二
  　釉陶尊（商·约公元前一五六二——一○六六）出土于郑州二里岗商墓，是最早期的带釉陶器。
    三
    扁铜盉（周·约公元前一○六六——七七一）出土于河南洛阳，其形制是古代铜器中的杰出者。
    四
  　铜鼎、铜罍、铜匜（春秋·公元前七七○——四○三）出土于河南省郏县。对研究中国春秋历史艺术材料上是一个重要的发现。
    五
    铁范（战国·公元前四○三——二二一）其中包括锄范、镰范、斧范等七十多件，这是一部分。这批生产工具铸范在热河出土。这不仅是帮助我们了解当时农业、手工业生产工具的形式、效能和生产力的新的材料，而且是当时铸铁工业水平的标志。
    六
  　铁质铧、锄（汉·公元前二○六——公元二二○）出土于陕西省长安县和临潼县几个工地，为研究两千年前农业生产力提供了新的材料。
    七
  　陶马（汉·公元前二○六——公元二二○）出土于成都杨子山。马的造形准确，姿态雄健而活泼，富有生命力。这证明了两千年前，我国劳动人民在雕塑艺术上有了高度的成就。
    八
  　铜马车（东汉·公元二五——二二○）出土于河南省南阳县。
    九
  　壁画（东汉·公元二五——二二○）在河北省望都县汉墓中发现，是辽阳以外，汉壁画的最重要的遗存物。
    十
  　唱歌女俑（北魏·公元三八六——五三四）出土于西安市郊草场坡工地。
    十一
  　壁画（唐·公元六一八——九○七）出土于陕西省咸阳县。
    十二
  　釉俑（宋·公元九六○——一二七九）出土于四川广汉。
    十三
  　女俑（元·公元一二六○——一三六八）出土于西安市郊湖广义园建校工地。
    十四
  　陶俑（元·公元一二六○——一三六八）出土于济南东郊工地。俑的形象是高鼻、深目，长须，盘着绡头，被推断为当时的“色目人”。一二三四五六七八九十十一十二十三十四第二七八期
