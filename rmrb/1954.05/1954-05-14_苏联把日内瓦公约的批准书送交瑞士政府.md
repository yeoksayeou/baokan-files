Title: The Soviet Union Submits Ratification of the Geneva Conventions to the Swiss Government
Author:
Date: 1954-05-14
Page: 4

　　Xinhua News Agency, Bern, 12th: Soviet Minister to Switzerland, Molochkov, submitted the Soviet Union's ratification of the four Geneva Conventions on the protection of war victims, ratified in April, to the Swiss Federal Government for archiving. These conventions are: the Geneva Convention for the Amelioration of the Condition of the Wounded and Sick in Armed Forces in the Field, the Geneva Convention for the Amelioration of the Condition of Wounded, Sick and Shipwrecked Members of Armed Forces at Sea, the Geneva Convention relative to the Treatment of Prisoners of War, and the Geneva Convention relative to the Protection of Civilian Persons in Time of War.

　　Note: The above-mentioned conventions were drafted at the 1949 Geneva Conference, and representatives from sixty-one countries signed them. As of now, thirty-six countries have ratified these conventions, and countries that have not yet ratified them include the United States and the United Kingdom.

### 苏联把日内瓦公约的批准书送交瑞士政府

1954-05-14
第4版()
专栏：

    苏联把日内瓦公约的批准书送交瑞士政府
    【新华社十三日讯】据塔斯社伯尔尼十二日讯：苏联驻瑞士公使莫洛奇科夫，把苏联四月间批准的关于保护战争受难者的四项日内瓦公约的批准书送交瑞士联邦政府存档。这些公约就是：改善战地武装部队伤者病者境遇之日内瓦公约，改善海上武装部队伤者病者及遇船难者境遇之日内瓦公约，关于战俘待遇之日内瓦公约，关于战时保护平民之日内瓦公约。
    按：上述这些公约是一九四九年日内瓦会议拟订的，在上面签字的共有六十一个国家的代表。截至现在为止，已有三十六个国家批准了这些公约，尚未批准的国家包括美国和英国在内。
