Title: President Ayub Points Out the Danger of the Soviet Union Providing Weapons to India; Soviet Weapons to India are Against Pakistan; Pakistani Newspaper Says Soviet Arms Supply to India is Following the "Same Policy" as the United States

Author:
Date: 1966-06-02
Edition: 5

    Xinhua News Agency, Rawalpindi, 1st – According to a Pakistan Press International report on May 31, Pakistan President Ayub Khan said on the 31st that Pakistan has explained to the Soviet government the danger of providing arms aid to India.
    President Ayub said while talking to reporters in Quetta, West Pakistan, that the weapons recently provided by the Soviet Union to India are the type of weapons that can be used on the plains, and "are clearly intended to be used against Pakistan."
    Xinhua News Agency, Rawalpindi, 31st – Pakistani public opinion strongly condemns the Soviet Union’s recent provision of large-scale military aid to India. The *Mountainous Area Daily* published an editorial on the 30th stating that the Soviet Union is "following the same policy" as the United States.
    The editorial exposed the Soviet Union's double-dealing towards Pakistan by revealing that five Soviet ships were unloading war materials at the port of Calcutta. It said: "While continuing to provide India with military aid, the Soviet Union is still assuring Pakistan that it is sincerely concerned about the peaceful resolution of all disputes between the two countries."
    The editorial said: The Soviet Union "has not cooled down India's war frenzy and forced it to abandon its unreasonable attitude on the Kashmir issue, but instead has given it batches of war materials, thereby strengthening its aggressive ambitions."
    The *Oriental Post* of Lahore said in an editorial on the 31st that this step taken by the Soviet Union will not only not ease the tension between Pakistan and India, but will also prompt India to adopt a more stubborn attitude and prompt it to take any extreme measures in its anti-Pakistan fervor.
    According to a report in the *Pakistan Times* on the 31st, Inamullah Khan, Secretary-General of the World Muslim Congress, wrote an open letter on the 30th to the Soviet delegation led by First Deputy Chairman of the Council of Ministers of the Soviet Union, Mazurov, which is currently visiting Pakistan. The letter stated that Soviet arms aid to India "is certainly not a way to promote peace." The open letter pointed out that Pakistan and the people of the world were "very shocked by the Soviet government's recent dispatch of ships to India carrying military equipment."
    At a meeting held in Lahore on the 26th, the Pakistan Muslim League passed a resolution expressing great anger at the Soviet Union's provision of military equipment to India, and demanded that the Soviet Union immediately stop transporting weapons to India.

### 阿尤布总统指出苏联向印度提供武器的危险性  苏联给印度的武器是对付巴基斯坦的  巴报说苏联给印度提供军火是同美国实行“同样方针”

1966-06-02
第5版()
专栏：

    阿尤布总统指出苏联向印度提供武器的危险性
    苏联给印度的武器是对付巴基斯坦的
    巴报说苏联给印度提供军火是同美国实行“同样方针”
    新华社拉瓦尔品第一日电　据巴基斯坦联合通讯社五月三十一日报道，巴基斯坦总统阿尤布·汗三十一日说，巴基斯坦已向苏联政府说明向印度提供武器援助的危险。
    阿尤布总统在西巴基斯坦的基达同记者们谈话时说，苏联最近向印度提供的武器是可以用于平原的那类武器，“显然是用来对付巴基斯坦的”。
    据新华社拉瓦尔品第三十一日电巴基斯坦舆论强烈谴责苏联最近向印度提供大量军火援助。《山岳报》三十日发表社论说，苏联同美国“实行同样的方针”。
    社论就五艘苏联船在加尔各答码头上卸下战争物资揭露了苏联对巴基斯坦采取的两面手法。它说：“苏联一方面继续给印度军火援助，另一方面仍在向巴基斯坦保证它是真诚地关心这两国间一切争端的和平解决。”
    社论说：苏联“不是使印度的战争狂冷却下来，迫使它放弃对克什米尔问题的不合理的态度，而是给它一批又一批的战争物资，从而加强了它的侵略野心”。
    拉合尔《东方报》三十一日的社论说，苏联采取的这个步骤，不仅不能缓和巴基斯坦和印度之间的紧张局势，而且促使印度采取更加顽固的态度，并促使它在反巴基斯坦的热狂中不惜采取任何极端措施。
    据《巴基斯坦时报》三十一日报道，世界穆斯林大会秘书长伊纳穆拉汗三十日给由苏联部长会议第一副主席马祖罗夫率领的、现在正在巴基斯坦访问的苏联最高苏维埃代表团写了一封公开信，这封信说，苏联向印度提供军火援助“肯定不是促进和平的办法”。公开信指出，巴基斯坦和全世界人民对苏联“政府最近派船只往印度运送军备感到非常震惊”。
    巴基斯坦穆斯林联盟二十六日在拉合尔举行的会议上通过了一项决议，对苏联向印度提供军备表示极大的愤怒，要求苏联立即停止运送武器给印度。
