Title: Pakistani Foreign Minister Bhutto Takes Leave for Medical Treatment; President Ayub Temporarily Oversees Foreign Ministry Affairs
Author:
Date: 1966-06-22
Edition: 5

    Xinhua News Agency, Rawalpindi, 18th – According to a report by the Pakistan Press International, Pakistani President Ayub Khan announced in a statement on the 18th: "Foreign Minister Bhutto will be taking leave for medical treatment. I have agreed that I will temporarily oversee the work of the Foreign Ministry.”
    President Ayub Khan's statement said: "Some people have said that Bhutto's departure will lead to changes in Pakistan's foreign policy. I would like to state that there is no need to worry about this.”
    He said, "A country's foreign policy is based on national interests, and Pakistan's foreign policy is formulated on this basis, and it will continue to pursue this policy.”
    He said, "Bhutto has served his country well as a minister for the past seven years, and I wish him health and a safe journey.”

### 巴基斯坦外长布托请假治病  阿尤布总统暂时照管外交部工作

1966-06-22
第5版()
专栏：

    巴基斯坦外长布托请假治病
    阿尤布总统暂时照管外交部工作
    新华社拉瓦尔品第十八日电　据巴基斯坦联合通讯社报道，巴基斯坦总统阿尤布·汗十八日在一项声明中宣布：“（外交部长）布托将请假治病。我已经同意，我暂时将照管外交部的工作。”
    阿尤布·汗总统的声明说：“有人说布托的离开将导致巴基斯坦外交政策的改变。我愿意表明，不必有这种担心。”
    他说，“一个国家的外交政策是以国家利益为基础的，巴基斯坦的外交政策正是在这种基础上制定的，它将继续奉行这种政策。”
    他说，“布托在过去七年里作为一个部长很好地为他的国家服务，我祝他健康和一路平安。”
