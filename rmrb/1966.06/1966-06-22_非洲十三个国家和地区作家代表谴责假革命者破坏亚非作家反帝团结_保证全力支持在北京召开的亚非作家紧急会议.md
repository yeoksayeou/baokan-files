Title: African Writers Representatives from Thirteen Countries and Regions Condemn False Revolutionaries for Sabotaging Anti-Imperialist Unity of Asian-African Writers, Guarantee Full Support for Emergency Meeting of Asian-African Writers in Beijing
Author:
Date: 1966-06-22
Edition: 5

    Xinhua News Agency, June 21st - African writers' representatives from thirteen countries and regions, who have already arrived in Beijing to attend the scheduled Emergency Meeting of Asian-African Writers, issued a joint statement today, guaranteeing their unreserved and complete support for the scheduled meeting and the proposed agenda. The joint statement condemned the attempts by false revolutionaries and their agents to sabotage the anti-imperialist unity of Asian-African writers and to disrupt the upcoming emergency meeting. The full text of the statement is as follows:

    At the invitation of Mr. Sena nanayake, Secretary-General of the Permanent Bureau of Asian-African Writers, the African writers' delegations currently in Beijing to attend the Emergency Meeting of Asian-African Writers scheduled to begin here on June 27th, express our unreserved and complete support for the upcoming scheduled emergency meeting.

    We unanimously accept the Secretary-General's gracious invitation because we are fully convinced that the upcoming writers' meeting will contribute to the lofty anti-imperialist cause of the Asian-African people and further promote this cause. This meeting is being held at a very important moment in the principled struggle of the African people against imperialism led by the United States, and against neo-colonialism and old colonialism.

    We unanimously support the proposed agenda for the upcoming emergency meeting:

    1.  Support the struggle of the Vietnamese people against U.S. imperialist aggression, for the liberation of the South, the defense of the North, and the reunification of the country.

    2.  The tasks of writers in the struggle of the Asian-African people against imperialism led by the United States, and for the attainment and defense of national independence.

    3.  Oppose cultural aggression by imperialism and neo-colonialism and old colonialism, and develop the national cultures of the Asian-African peoples.

    We are watching with great concern and strongly condemn all attempts to sabotage this emergency meeting. Such sabotage and divisive activities, planned and instigated by false revolutionaries, are a malicious attempt to undermine the great cause of the anti-imperialist revolutionary unity of Asian-African writers, a great betrayal of the interests of the struggle of the Asian-African people, which only benefits imperialism and reactionaries and pleases them. We fully support the correct measures taken by the Secretary-General to counter such divisive and destructive activities.

    At a time when the Vietnamese people are waging a heroic struggle against U.S. imperialist aggression; when imperialists and their local lackeys are attempting to erase the achievements of national democratic revolutions and suppress political independence through various means of subversion, interference, and aggression, as evidenced by the counter-revolutionary coups d'état orchestrated by imperialists in Ghana and Indonesia; when our brothers in Congo (Leopoldville), Zimbabwe, Angola, Mozambique, "Portuguese" Guinea, Basutoland, Swaziland, Bechuanaland, Azania (South Africa), and the people of other regions of Asia, Africa, Latin America, and the world are struggling for national liberation and independence, we guarantee and call upon all Asian-African writers to unreservedly support the upcoming Asian-African Writers' Meeting convened by Mr. Sena nanayake, Secretary-General of the Permanent Bureau of Asian-African Writers, at this significant and urgent moment, and to use their pens as powerful weapons to fight alongside the Asian-African people against and overthrow our common enemy – imperialism led by belligerent America – and its running dogs and apologists.

### 非洲十三个国家和地区作家代表谴责假革命者破坏亚非作家反帝团结  保证全力支持在北京召开的亚非作家紧急会议

1966-06-22
第5版()
专栏：

    非洲十三个国家和地区作家代表谴责假革命者破坏亚非作家反帝团结
    保证全力支持在北京召开的亚非作家紧急会议
    新华社二十一日讯　已经来到北京参加预定的亚非作家紧急会议的来自十三个国家和地区的非洲作家代表今天发表联合声明，保证毫无保留地完全支持预定的会议和提出的日程。联合声明谴责假革命者及其代理人最近破坏亚非作家的反帝团结，破坏即将举行的紧急会议的企图。声明全文如下：
    应亚非作家常设局秘书长森纳那亚克的邀请，目前已经来到北京参加从六月二十七日起在这里举行的亚非作家紧急会议的非洲作家代表团表示，我们毫无保留地完全支持即将举行的预定的紧急会议。
    我们一致接受秘书长的盛情邀请，因为我们完全相信，即将举行的作家会议将有助于亚非人民的崇高的反帝事业，并起进一步促进这一事业的作用。这次会议是在非洲人民同以美国为首的帝国主义和新老殖民主义进行有原则的斗争的非常重要的时刻举行的。
    我们一致支持即将召开的这次紧急会议的已经提出的议程：
    一、支持越南人民反对美帝国主义侵略，解放南方，保卫北方和统一全国的斗争。
    二、作家在亚非人民反对以美国为首的帝国主义以及争取和保卫民族独立的斗争中的任务。
    三、反对帝国主义和新老殖民主义的文化侵略，发展亚非各国人民的民族文化。
    我们怀着极大的关切注视着，并强烈谴责破坏这次紧急会议的一切企图。假革命者所策划和指使的这种破坏和分裂活动，是破坏亚非作家反帝革命团结的伟大事业的恶毒企图，是对亚非人民斗争利益的大出卖，只有利于帝国主义和反动派，并使它们感到高兴。我们完全支持秘书长为反击这种分裂和破坏活动而采取的正确措施。
    越南人民正在为反对美帝国主义的侵略而进行英勇的斗争；帝国主义者及其在当地的走狗企图通过颠覆、干涉和侵略等种种手段，勾销民族民主革命的成果，镇压政治独立，就象帝国主义者在加纳和印度尼西亚策动的反革命政变所证明的那样；我们在刚果（利）、津巴布韦、安哥拉、莫三鼻给、“葡属”几内亚、巴苏陀兰、斯威士兰、贝专纳、阿扎尼亚（南非）的弟兄们，以及亚非拉其他地区和全世界人民，正在为民族解放和独立而斗争。在这样的时候，我们保证并号召所有亚非作家在这个意义重大和紧急的时刻，无保留地支持由亚非作家常设局秘书长森纳那亚克召集的即将举行的亚非作家会议，把自己的笔杆作为强大的武器，为反对和打倒共同的故人——以好战的美国为首的帝国主义——及其走狗和辩护士，同亚非人民并肩进行斗争。
