Title: Soviet Leaders Lavishly Court Washington, Presenting a Series of Lunar Photographs to Promote Soviet-U.S. Dealings
Author:
Date: 1966-06-11
Edition: 5

    Xinhua News Agency, Washington, 10th—U.S. State Department Spokesman McCloskey announced on the 9th that in the past few weeks the Soviet government has provided the United States with a series of lunar photographs taken by Soviet lunar satellites.
    McCloskey said that when Soviet Ambassador to the United States Dobrynin provided the U.S. government with these photographs, he also delivered a letter from Soviet Supreme Soviet Presidium Chairman Podgorny to the U.S. President. This situation shows that the Soviet Union and the United States have taken another step forward in the so-called transaction of "free exploration and use" of the moon and other celestial bodies.
    McCloskey did not reveal the contents of the letter, and even evasively said that the Soviet Union's letter and those lunar photographs had "no special political motives." But he praised, "Of course they (referring to the letter and photographs) are welcome." He also said, "We have been interacting with the Soviet Union on scientific issues for the past few years."
    McCloskey seized the opportunity to promote that the U.S. and the Soviet Union should conclude an international treaty for the use of the moon for "peaceful" purposes as soon as possible, without having to wait for the next session of the United Nations General Assembly.
    U.S. President Johnson suggested on May 7 "concluding a treaty for the peaceful exploration of the moon and other celestial bodies." The Soviet government responded promptly, submitting to the United Nations on May 31 a proposal to conclude an "international treaty" with the United States on this issue. It also said that if an agreement were reached in this regard, it would be an "important step" towards achieving "complete and thorough disarmament" and "strengthening friendly relations between countries" globally.

### 苏联领导向华盛顿大献殷勤  赠送一系列月球照片促进苏美交易

1966-06-11
第5版()
专栏：

    苏联领导向华盛顿大献殷勤
    赠送一系列月球照片促进苏美交易
    新华社十日讯　华盛顿消息：美国国务院发言人麦克洛斯基九日宣布，在过去几个星期中苏联政府向美国提供了苏联月球卫星所拍摄的一系列月球照片。
    麦克洛斯基说，苏联驻美国大使多勃雷宁在向美国政府提供这些照片时，还递交了苏联最高苏维埃主席团主席波德戈尔内给美国总统的信件。这种情况表明：苏联和美国在所谓“自由探索和使用”月球及其他天体的交易中，又向前跨进了一步。
    麦克洛斯基没有透露这封信的内容，而且还遮遮掩掩地说什么苏联给美国这封信和那些月球照片并没有“任何特别的政治动机”。但是他夸奖说：“当然它们（指信件和照片）是受欢迎的。”他还说：“过去几年来，我们一直同苏联在科学问题上进行交往。”
    麦克洛斯基乘机鼓吹美苏应该及早缔结为“和平”目的使用月球的国际条约，而不必等到下一届联合国大会开会。
    美国总统约翰逊在五月七日曾建议“缔结一项和平探索月球和其他天体的条约”，苏联政府连忙响应，在五月三十一日向联合国提出要就这一问题同美国缔结一个“国际条约”。它还说，如果在这方面达成协议，将是朝着在全球实现“全面彻底裁军”和“加强各国之间的友好关系”的方向跨出的一个“重要步骤”。
