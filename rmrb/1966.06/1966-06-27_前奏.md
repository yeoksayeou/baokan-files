    Prelude
    The New York Times revealed that the Johnson administration, while hastening to concoct a "peace talk" charade, is simultaneously preparing to escalate bombing of North Vietnam. This newspaper wrote: "Whenever the U.S. government takes new military actions, it always tries to present new evidence to prove that North Vietnam refuses to engage in negotiations or to agree to reciprocal actions to reduce combat."

    The New York Times thus exposes the secret of the Johnson administration's counter-revolutionary two-pronged strategy: every time it stages a "peace talk" charade, it is merely a prelude to further escalating the war.

### 前奏

1966-06-27
第5版()
专栏：三言两语

    前奏
    《纽约时报》透露，约翰逊政府在加紧搞“和谈”骗局的同时，正准备扩大对越南北方的轰炸。这家报纸写道：“每当美国政府采取新的军事行动时，它总是设法提出新的证据来证明北越拒绝进行谈判或同意采取减弱战斗的互惠行动的”。
    《纽约时报》这就一语道破了约翰逊政府反革命两手策略的奥秘：每一次大搞“和谈”骗局，都不过是进一步扩大战争的前奏。
