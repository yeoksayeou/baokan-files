Title: Invoking the Spirit of the 1930s Arts
Author:
Date: 1966-12-27
Edition: 6

In 1962, Yang Hansheng, in a speech to drama actors, insidiously said, "We are very anxious about the art of drama, especially those of us who have been involved for a long time."

What is the matter? Is Yang Hansheng genuinely concerned about our socialist drama? No, he is using this as an opportunity to publicly oppose Chairman Mao's literary line of serving the workers, peasants, and soldiers, and to promote Zhou Yang's revisionist literary line. In this speech, Yang Hansheng falsely claimed, "Chinese drama has a revolutionary and combative tradition, a red thread running through it for over fifty years." He also boasted that in the 1930s, drama activities had "penetrated into the workers and a part of the peasants." This is a blatant contradiction of Chairman Mao's "Talks at the Yan'an Forum on Literature and Art," a brazen attack on the Party.

Yang Hansheng is an old hand at opposing the Party. As early as the 1930s, he collaborated with Zhou Yang, Xia Yan, and Tian Han to attack Lu Xun. Using the pseudonym Hua Han, he publicly published articles slandering Lu Xun as having "no vision of the rising class," as a "spokesman for the petty bourgeoisie who cannot leap over the historical gate," and attacking Lu Xun as "negative" and "pessimistic," capable only of depicting and expressing "miserable lives." After liberation, he continued to hold his reactionary stance, openly opposing Mao Zedong Thought. In 1957, he, along with Tian Han and Xia Yan, raised the black banner of commemorating the fiftieth anniversary of the drama movement. Using his position as a leader of the Federation of Literary and Art Circles, he gave speeches, wrote articles, held symposiums, and organized exhibitions, all in a conspiracy to revive the artistic traditions of the 1930s. In the plan he drafted for publishing "Left League" materials, he even included the names of traitors, counter-revolutionaries, and rightists such as Chen Duxiu, Yang Chunren, Hu Feng, Feng Xuefeng, and Ding Ling.

In February 1961, at a meeting held at the New Overseas Hotel to commemorate the "Left League," he and Lin Mo Han distorted history, reversed black and white, and openly declared that in the 1930s they had "criticized Lu Xun for good reason," plotting to rehabilitate Zhou Yang and his group. In June of the same year, Tian Han published the poisonous weed "Summarizing the Experience of Drama in the Democratic Revolutionary Period" in the "Drama Newspaper," and Yang Hansheng closely collaborated, publishing a poisonous weed in the same period's publication, advocating for "national defense drama." In 1962, he called for local troupes to perform more plays written by Tian Han and Xia Yan in the 1930s and to seriously study and summarize their experiences. He also praised his own 1933 film "Iron Plate Red Tears," which promotes class reconciliation, as the first work to reflect rural class struggle. Additionally, he provided materials to the editors of the poisonous weed "History of Chinese Film Development," where he erected a monument to himself, praising his own films as having "strong combative power" and being "filled with revolutionary heroism." To further usurp the proletarian literary and artistic front, he personally went to Shanghai to arrange forces, preparing to bring his works praising traitors, "The Death of Li Xiucheng," and praising the reactionary "Brotherhood" of Sichuan, "Grassland Heroes," to the screen.

Yang Hansheng's true intention behind his "anxiety" for drama is to revive the arts of the 1930s according to his blueprint and contend for the stage with socialist arts. He openly expressed his regret that there were not enough performances of dramas from the "1930s," urging everyone to follow his so-called fifty-year "red thread," selecting works from "Five-Four" playwrights, thus "enriching," "making a fortune," and "becoming fat," and performing them for a lifetime!

We must not allow Yang Hansheng to revive the bourgeois arts of the 1930s. We must thoroughly uproot this black line and let the red flag of Mao Zedong's literary and artistic thought fly high on our literary and artistic front!

### 为“三十年代”文艺招魂

1966-12-27
第6版()
专栏：

    为“三十年代”文艺招魂
    一九六二年，阳翰笙在一次报告会上，别有用心地对话剧演员们说：“对话剧艺术我们很焦虑，特别是我搞得久的人。”
    怎么回事情？难道阳翰笙是真的关心我们社会主义的话剧吗？不，他是借机公开反对毛主席制定的为工农兵服务的文艺路线，推行周扬的修正主义文艺路线。就在这个讲话中，阳翰笙胡说：“中国话剧有个革命的战斗的传统，五十多年一根红线下来的。”他还吹嘘三十年代的话剧活动，已经“深入到工人里面和一部分农民里面。”这是明目张胆地跟毛主席的《在延安文艺座谈会上的讲话》唱反调，猖狂地向党进攻。
    阳翰笙是一个反党老手，早在三十年代就与周扬、夏衍、田汉合伙围攻鲁迅。他化名华汉，公开发表文章诬蔑鲁迅“没有新兴阶级的眼光”，是“不能跳过历史的大门”的“小资产阶级的代言人”，攻击鲁迅“消极”、“悲观”，只会描写和表现“悲惨的人生”。解放后，他一直坚持反动立场，明目张胆地反对毛泽东思想。一九五七年，他同田汉、夏衍等一起打出了纪念话剧运动五十年的黑旗。他利用文联领导人的职权，到处作报告，写文章，召开座谈会，组织展览会，大搞复活“三十年代”文艺传统的阴谋活动。在由他主持制定的出版“左联”资料计划中，竟把叛徒、反革命分子、右派分子如陈独秀、杨邨人、胡风、冯雪峰、丁玲等人的名单列入计划。
    一九六一年二月，他在新侨饭店召开的回忆“左联”的会上，同林默涵等人歪曲历史，颠倒黑白，公然叫嚣：在三十年代他们“批判鲁迅也有道理”，阴谋为周扬他们一伙翻案。同年六月，田汉在《戏剧报》上发出了《要总结一下民主革命阶段的戏剧经验》大毒草，阳翰笙紧密地配合，在同期刊物上也抛出了一株毒草，鼓吹“国防戏剧”。一九六二年，他号召各地剧团要多演田汉、夏衍等人在三十年代写的戏，要认真研究总结他们的经验。他还在报刊上吹捧自己在一九三三年创作的宣扬阶级调和论的电影《铁板红泪录》，把它说成是第一部反映农村阶级斗争的作品。此外，他又提供材料给大毒草《中国电影发展史》的编者，在书中为自己树碑立传，吹捧自己编写的电影“有强烈的战斗性”，“充满了革命英雄主义精神”。为了进一步篡夺无产阶级文艺阵地，他亲赴上海布置力量，准备把他创作的歌颂叛徒的《李秀成之死》和歌颂四川反动青红帮“袍哥”的毒草《草莽英雄》搬上银幕。
    阳翰笙对话剧“焦虑”的真正用心，是要按照他的蓝图来复活“三十年代文艺”，同社会主义文艺争夺阵地。他对剧团上演的
  “三十年代”的剧本还不够多而公开表示：“我觉得很歉然”，要大家沿着他所谓的五十多年的一根“红线”往下排，把“五四”以来的“剧作家”的作品都选出来，这样就“丰富了”，“发财了，就肥了”，而且“要演它一辈子！”
    我们决不能让阳翰笙宣扬的三十年代的资产阶级文艺重新复活，一定要彻底铲掉这条黑线，让毛泽东文艺思想的红旗在我们的文艺阵地上高高飘扬！
