    What is the True Purpose of U.S. Foreign "Aid"?
    See What U.S. Officials Say
    "(1) No military, economic, or technical assistance shall be furnished to any country... if the recipient country does not agree to fulfill its military obligations under multilateral and bilateral treaties or agreements to which the United States is a party. (2) No economic and technical assistance shall be furnished to any country... if such assistance does not strengthen the security of the United States."
    -- From Section 511 of the U.S. "Mutual Security Act"
    "The total amount of military aid allocated by the United States since the foreign aid program began in 1950 is more than $20.5 billion... Allies have contributed $130 billion, so the ratio is that for every dollar the United States puts in, they contribute five dollars themselves."
    -- From U.S. President Eisenhower's 1959 annual "Foreign Aid" Report
    "I don't care whether we make friends. In many of these situations, I don't care whether they are our friends. We do these things because it is going to be good for the interests of the United States."
    -- From the late U.S. Secretary of State Dulles's remarks to the U.S. Senate Appropriations Subcommittee on August 10, 1957
    "The purposes of the program (the U.S. "Mutual Security Program") are simple, straightforward, and clear... Our security is not only related to the situation in other countries, it also depends on the support of our allies. We need joint military forces, bases, strategic resources, and production capacity; this need is real and unavoidable."
    -- From U.S. Under Secretary of State Dillon's remarks to the U.S. House Foreign Affairs Committee on March 17, 1959
    "First, we should select those countries with anti-communist governments that are friendly to us, and that are already linked to us through military agreements. In this regard, government subsidies and aid can take the form of primarily military appropriations. Because fish that are already hooked do not need bait."
    -- From a secret letter from U.S. oil tycoon and current New York State Governor Rockefeller to the U.S. President in 1956

### 美国对外“援助”的真正目的何在？  请看美国官方的言论

1960-02-19
第6版()
专栏：备忘录

    美国对外“援助”的真正目的何在？
    请看美国官方的言论
    “（一）任何军事、经济或技术援助将不给予任何一个国家……如果受援国不同意履行它们在有美国参加的多边与双边条约或协定中所承担的军事义务。（二）任何经济与技术援助将不给予任何一个国家……如果这种援助不能加强美国的安全。”
    ——摘自美国“共同安全法”第511条
    “美国自从1950年援外计划开始以来所拨出的军事援助总数在二百零五亿美元以上……盟国拿出了一千三百亿美元，因此比例是，美国每拿出一个美元，他们自己拿出五个美元。”
    ——摘自美国总统艾森豪威尔1959年度“援外”报告
    “我们是不是交了朋友，我是不在乎的。在许多这样的情况中，我不在乎他们是不是我们的朋友。我们做这些事情是因为将对美国的利益有好处。”
    ——摘自美国已故国务卿杜勒斯1957年8月10日对美国参议院拨款小组委员会的讲话
    “这项计划（美国“共同安全计划”）的各项目的是简单的、直截了当的和清楚的。……我们的安全不仅仅同其他国家的局势有关，它还依靠各盟国的支援。我们需要联合的军事力量、基地、战略资源和生产能力，这种需要是实在的和不可避免的。”
    ——摘自美国副国务卿狄龙1959年3月17日在美国众议院外交委员会的讲话
    “首先，我们应该挑选那些反共的、但对我们友好的政府的国家，它们已经通过军事协定同我们连系在一起。在这方面，政府的补助和援助可以采取主要是军事拨款的方式。因为已经上钩的鱼儿不需要香饵。”
    ——摘自美国石油大王、现任纽约州州长洛克菲勒1956年给美国总统的秘密信
