Title: Soviet-Nepalese Joint Communiqué
Author:
Date: 1960-02-07
Page: 3
Xinhua News Agency, Kathmandu, February 5th: A Soviet-Nepalese joint communiqué signed in Kathmandu states that during the visit of Voroshilov, Chairman of the Presidium of the Supreme Soviet of the USSR, Kozlov, First Deputy Chairman of the Council of Ministers of the USSR, and Furtseva, representative of the Supreme Soviet of the USSR, to the Kingdom of Nepal, they held talks with King Mahendra of Nepal, Prime Minister and Foreign Minister Koirala, and other government officials. The talks and meetings were conducted in an atmosphere of sincerity, based on complete mutual understanding and respect. Opinions were exchanged on issues concerning the existing relations between the two countries.

The communiqué stated that both sides emphasized that they believe further strengthening the friendly relations between the Soviet Union and Nepal is of great significance. They reaffirmed their conviction that the principles of peaceful coexistence as the basis for relations between countries will help establish world peace.

Both sides expressed their belief that complete and thorough disarmament is the only way to permanently ban war.

His Majesty the King and the Government of Nepal pointed out that Nepal is a peace-loving country, and it greatly values the Soviet Union's tremendous efforts to ease international tensions and strengthen universal peace by achieving complete and thorough disarmament and a complete ban on the testing and use of nuclear weapons. His Majesty the King and the Government of Nepal believe that the Soviet Union's recent unilateral reduction of its armed forces is a major step on the road to achieving common goals and complete and thorough disarmament.

The Soviet side noted with satisfaction Nepal's positive attitude on issues such as the need to make progress in disarmament, mutual visits by activists from Eastern and Western countries, and meetings between heads of state.

The communiqué concluded by saying that both sides expressed satisfaction with the commencement of the Soviet-Nepalese agreement on Soviet economic and technical assistance to Nepal. This agreement is an important step in establishing better economic ties between the two countries.

Both sides agreed that the visit of Soviet state activists to Nepal and the visit of His Majesty King Mahendra of Nepal to the Soviet Union have strengthened and will further strengthen the existing mutual understanding and friendly ties between the Soviet Union and Nepal, and will benefit both countries and the great cause of strengthening universal peace and friendship.

### 苏联尼泊尔发表联合公报

1960-02-07
第3版()
专栏：

    苏联尼泊尔发表联合公报
    据新华社6日讯　据塔斯社加德满都5日讯：在加德满都签署的苏联—尼泊尔联合公报说，苏联最高苏维埃主席团主席伏罗希洛夫、苏联部长会议第一副主席科兹洛夫和苏联最高苏维埃代表福尔采娃在访问尼泊尔王国期间，同尼泊尔国王马亨德拉、首相兼外交大臣柯伊拉腊以及政府的其他官员进行了会谈。会谈和会晤都是本着完全相互了解和尊重的精神，在真诚的气氛中进行的。就两国之间现存关系的问题交换了意见。
    公报说，双方强调指出，他们认为进一步加强苏联和尼泊尔的友好关系具有重大的意义。他们重申，深信作为国与国之间关系中的政策基础的和平共处原则将有助于建立世界和平。
    双方表示相信，全面彻底裁军是永久禁止战争的唯一的方法。
    尼泊尔国王陛下和政府指出，尼泊尔是一个爱好和平的国家，它十分珍视苏联为通过实现全面彻底裁军以及完全禁止试验和使用核武器来缓和国际紧张局势和加强普遍和平而作出的巨大努力。尼泊尔国王陛下和政府认为，苏联不久前单方面裁减武装部队是在达到共同目标和实现全面彻底裁军道路上迈出的一大步。
    苏联方面满意地注意到，尼泊尔在必须在裁军方面取得进展、东西方国家活动家相互访问和各国首脑会晤等问题上采取积极的态度。
    公报最后说，双方对苏联—尼泊尔关于苏联给予尼泊尔经济技术援助的协定的开始实施表示满意。这个协定是两国之间建立更好的经济联系的重要步骤。
    双方一致认为，苏联国家活动家在尼泊尔的访问以及尼泊尔国王马亨德拉陛下对苏联的访问加强了，并将进一步加强苏尼两国现存的相互谅解和友好联系，并有利于两国，有利于加强普遍和平和友谊的伟大事业。
