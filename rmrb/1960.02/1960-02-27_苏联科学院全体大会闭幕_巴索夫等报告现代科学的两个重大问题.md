Title: Closing of the General Assembly of the Soviet Academy of Sciences: Basov and Others Report on Two Major Issues in Modern Science
Author:
Date: 1960-02-27
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, Moscow, February 26th: According to TASS reports, the General Assembly of the Soviet Academy of Sciences closed on the 25th. Prominent Soviet scientists such as Basov and Mintz delivered reports at the meeting.

The title of the report given by Soviet Doctor of Physics and Mathematics Basov was: Development Prospects of a New Branch of Science—Quantum Radio Physics. He said that it is now entirely possible to use molecular generators under "Earth conditions" to verify Einstein's general theory of relativity. A decisive step in the development of quantum radio physics is the construction of molecular generators (Basov and another Soviet scientist, Prokhorov, were awarded the Lenin Prize last year for their contributions in this area). Using the high frequency stability of molecular generators, the effect of gravity on time can be tested (general relativity believes that if two clocks are placed in gravitational fields of different magnitudes, they will run at different speeds).

Basov suggested using two molecular generators for the experiment, one placed on the Earth's surface and the other placed on an artificial satellite. When the satellite is running outside the atmosphere, the effect of Earth's gravity will be greatly reduced. According to Basov's calculations, the frequency emitted by the molecular generator placed on the artificial satellite should be a few Hertz (a few cycles) slower. This is easy to measure.

Basov said that molecular generators can also be used in astronomical research. It will create new possibilities for studying the motion of celestial bodies. The work of scientists comparing astronomical clocks with atomic clocks and molecular clocks has already begun, and this work is of great significance. The speeds of astronomical clocks and molecular clocks may not match.

Basov also said that applying molecular amplifiers in radio astronomy can greatly expand the range of the universe that can be detected with radio telescopes. And increasing the sensitivity of radio telescopes can measure the smallest amount of compounds existing in the universe. Using molecular amplifiers can also establish extremely accurate radio navigation equipment for interplanetary flight.

Academician Mintz of the Soviet Academy of Sciences said at the meeting that researching and applying the fourth state of matter—plasma—is the most important direction for the development of modern science, especially radio electronics. This issue is of particular significance in conquering the universe.

### 苏联科学院全体大会闭幕  巴索夫等报告现代科学的两个重大问题

1960-02-27
第6版()
专栏：

    苏联科学院全体大会闭幕
    巴索夫等报告现代科学的两个重大问题
    新华社莫斯科26日电　据塔斯社报道：苏联科学院全体大会25日闭幕，苏联著名科学家巴索夫、明次等在会上作了报告。
    苏联物理数学博士巴索夫报告的题目是：科学的新部门——量子射电物理学发展前景。他说，目前已经完全有可能在“地球条件”下，利用分子发生器来证验爱因斯坦的广义相对论。量子射电物理学发展的决定性的一步，是建造分子发生器（巴索夫和苏联另一个科学家普罗霍罗夫曾经因在这一方面的贡献而获得了去年的列宁奖金）。利用分子发生器所发出频率的高度稳定性，可以检验重力对于时间的影响（广义相对论认为：如果把两只表放在大小不同的重力场内，它们走的快慢是不一样的）。
    巴索夫建议用两个分子发生器来进行试验，一个放在地球的表面；另一个放在人造卫星上，当它在大气层以外运行时，地球重力的影响将大大减少。根据巴索夫的计算，放在人造卫星上的分子发生器发出的频率应该慢几赫（几周）。这很容易测量出来。
    巴索夫说，分子发生器还可以在天文研究中应用。它将为研究天体的运动创造新的可能性。科学家把天文钟同原子钟、分子钟互相比较的工作已经开始，这个工作具有很大的意义。天文钟和分子钟的快慢可能是不符的。
    巴索夫还说，在射电天文学中应用分子放大器，可以大大地扩大用射电望远镜探测宇宙的范围。而提高了射电望远镜的灵敏度，就可以测出宇宙中存在的最少量的化合物。应用分子放大器，还可以建立行星际飞行的极其精确的无线电导航设备。
    苏联科学院院士明次在会上说，研究和应用物质的第四状态—等离子体，是发展现代科学，首先是无线电电子学的最重要的方向。这个问题在征服宇宙方面具有特别重大的意义。
