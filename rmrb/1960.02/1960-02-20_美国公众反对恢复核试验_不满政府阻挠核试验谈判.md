Title: American Public Opposes Resumption of Nuclear Testing; Dissatisfaction with Government Obstruction of Nuclear Test Ban Negotiations
Author:
Date: 1960-02-20
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, New York, February 17th: The U.S. government's plot to resume nuclear weapons testing in order to intensify preparations for nuclear war has caused great unease and increasing opposition within the United States. The American public is widely demanding that the U.S. government promptly sign a treaty with the Soviet Union banning all nuclear weapons tests, including underground tests.

Thirty-eight prominent American social activists issued an open letter on February 15th, urging U.S. President Eisenhower to "promptly sign an agreement on banning all nuclear tests" and to extend the U.S.'s temporary moratorium on all nuclear testing.

The open letter stated that a nuclear test ban agreement could reduce the possibility of nuclear war. Such an agreement would serve as an example, allowing negotiations on broader disarmament to draw many lessons.

The chairman of the Federation of American Scientists, the renowned physicist Inglis, speaking on behalf of the 2,200 American scientists participating in the federation in Chicago, reiterated his firm demand that the U.S. government sign an agreement banning nuclear weapons testing. Inglis emphasized that France's recent blatant explosion of a nuclear device in the Sahara, disregarding world opinion, makes a nuclear test ban even more necessary than before.

Magazine editor Cousins of the "Saturday Review" and science editor Lawrence of "The New York Times" also gave television speeches on NBC, demanding that the United States sign a complete ban on nuclear weapons testing, including a ban on underground testing. The U.S. government's proposal for a so-called phased cessation of nuclear testing, an attempt to undermine the continuing nuclear test ban negotiations in Geneva, has also been condemned by the American press.

"The New York Times" published a statement on February 16th from seven non-English newspapers in New York. The statement demanded that U.S. President Eisenhower do everything possible to promptly conclude an agreement banning all nuclear weapons testing and declare that the purpose of U.S. policy is to ensure comprehensive, complete, and supervised disarmament.

The statement pointed out that the ongoing negotiations in Geneva on the cessation of nuclear weapons testing "are threatened by the U.S. refusal to extend the moratorium on nuclear weapons testing." The statement also expressed serious concern about the U.S. government's attempt to hand over nuclear weapons to its allies.

### 美国公众反对恢复核试验  不满政府阻挠核试验谈判

1960-02-20
第6版()
专栏：

    美国公众反对恢复核试验
    不满政府阻挠核试验谈判
    据新华社17日讯　纽约消息：美国政府准备恢复核武器试验以加紧准备核战争的阴谋在美国国内引起了巨大不安和越来越多的反对。美国公众纷纷要求美国政府迅速同苏联签订禁止包括地下试验在内的一切核武器试验的条约。
    美国三十八个著名社会活动家15日发表了一封公开信，要求美国总统艾森豪威尔“迅速签订关于禁止一切核试验的协定”，并且延长美国暂时停止一切核试验的期限。
    这封公开信说，禁止核试验协定能使爆发核战争的可能性减小。这样的协定将成为一个范例，使更广泛裁军的谈判吸取许多教益。
    美国科学家联合会主席、著名物理学家英格利斯代表参加这个联合会的二千二百个美国科学家在芝加哥讲话，再次坚决要求美国政府签订禁止核武器试验的协定。英格利斯着重指出，法国最近不顾世界舆论的谴责，悍然在撒哈拉爆炸核装置，这就使得禁止核试验比以前更加必要了。
    美国“星期六评论”杂志编辑卡津斯和“纽约时报”科学部编辑劳伦斯也在美国全国广播公司发表电视演说，要求美国签订包括禁止地下试验的完全禁止核武器试验的协定。美国政府提出所谓分阶段停止核试验的建议企图破坏日内瓦不继续核试验谈判的阴谋也受到美国报刊的谴责。
    “纽约时报”16日发表了纽约七家非英文报纸的声明。声明要求美国总统艾森豪威尔尽一切可能尽速缔结禁止一切核武器试验的协定，并宣布美国政策的目的是要保证全面、彻底和有监督的裁军。
    声明指出，目前在日内瓦进行的不继续核武器试验的谈判“由于美国拒绝延长停止核武器试验期而遭到了威胁”。声明还对美国政府企图把核武器交给美国的盟国的作法表示严重不安。
