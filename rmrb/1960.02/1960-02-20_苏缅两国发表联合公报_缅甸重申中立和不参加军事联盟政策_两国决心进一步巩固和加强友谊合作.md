Title: Joint Communiqué Issued by the Soviet Union and Burma: Burma Reaffirms Policy of Neutrality and Non-Participation in Military Alliances; Both Countries Determined to Further Consolidate and Strengthen Friendship and Cooperation
Author:
Date: 1960-02-20
Page: 5

    Xinhua News Agency, Rangoon, February 19th: According to TASS, a joint Soviet-Burmese communiqué was issued in Rangoon by the Chairman of the Council of Ministers of the Soviet Union, Khrushchev, and the Prime Minister of Burma, Ne Win. The communiqué stated that the Chairman of the Council of Ministers of the Soviet Union, Khrushchev, and the Prime Minister of the Union of Burma, General Ne Win, took advantage of the opportunity provided by this visit to exchange opinions freely and frankly on issues of common concern to both countries, as well as on certain pressing international issues.

    The communiqué said: "The leaders of the two countries noted with satisfaction that the relations between the Soviet Union and the Union of Burma continue to develop on the basis of the principles of peaceful coexistence and friendly cooperation. They believe that this will not only help to develop cooperation between the two countries on the basis of equality, mutual respect for the independence of each country, and mutual non-interference in internal affairs, but will also contribute to the cause of universal peace and international cooperation."

    The communiqué said: "An agreement has been reached that it is appropriate to sign a cultural agreement in the near future. The two sides also agreed to formulate a plan for cultural and scientific exchanges in 1960 that is acceptable to both sides."

    "The Prime Minister of Burma reaffirmed that his country adheres to a policy of neutrality and non-participation in military alliances. The Chairman of the Council of Ministers of the Soviet Union stated that the Soviet Union respects this political orientation of the Union of Burma and considers it a positive contribution to the cause of maintaining universal peace."

    The communiqué pointed out that complete disarmament is "the most important issue facing the world today." "The proposal for complete and thorough disarmament put forward by the Chairman of the Council of Ministers of the Soviet Union, Khrushchev, not long ago, should be discussed most carefully in the upcoming disarmament negotiations. The Union of Burma welcomes the Soviet Union's recent action to reduce its own armed forces."

    The leaders of the two countries called on all major powers possessing nuclear weapons, as well as other countries, to voluntarily abandon nuclear weapons testing. The leaders of the two countries expressed the hope that the upcoming summit conference will achieve positive results. The communiqué positively pointed out the contribution made by the visit of the Chairman of the Council of Ministers of the Soviet Union, Khrushchev, to strengthening the friendship between the Soviet Union and Burma and strengthening international cooperation.

    The communiqué concluded by saying, "The heads of government of the two countries reiterated their determination to consolidate and further strengthen the existing friendship and cooperation between the two countries."

### 苏缅两国发表联合公报  缅甸重申中立和不参加军事联盟政策  两国决心进一步巩固和加强友谊合作

1960-02-20
第5版()
专栏：

    苏缅两国发表联合公报
    缅甸重申中立和不参加军事联盟政策
    两国决心进一步巩固和加强友谊合作
    新华社19日讯　据塔斯社仰光19日讯：苏联部长会议主席赫鲁晓夫和缅甸总理奈温在仰光发表了苏缅联合公报。公报说，苏联部长会议主席赫鲁晓夫和缅甸联邦总理奈温将军利用这次访问提供的机会，就两国共同关心的问题，以及某些迫切的国际问题自由和坦率地交换了意见。
    公报说：“两国领导人高兴地指出，苏联和缅甸联邦的关系在和平共处和友好合作的原则的基础上继续得到了发展。他们相信，这不仅有助于发展两国之间在平等、互相尊重每一个国家的独立和互不干涉内政基础上的合作，而且也将有助于普遍和平和国际合作的事业。”
    公报说：“已经达成协议，认为希望在最近的将来签订文化协定是适宜的。双方还同意制订一项双方都能接受的1960年文化科学交流计划。”
    “缅甸总理重申，他的国家信守中立和不参加军事联盟的政策。苏联部长会议主席表示，苏联尊重缅甸联邦的这一政治方针，认为这是对维持普遍和平事业的积极贡献。”
    公报指出，全面裁军是“世界今天所面临的最重要的问题”。“苏联部长会议主席赫鲁晓夫不久前提出的全面彻底裁军建议，应当在即将举行的裁军谈判中加以最仔细的讨论。缅甸联邦欢迎苏联不久前裁减自己武装部队的行动。”
    两国领导人呼吁拥有核武器的所有大国以及其他国家自愿放弃核武器试验。两国领导人表示希望，即将举行的最高级会议将会取得积极的结果。公报肯定指出了苏联部长会议主席赫鲁晓夫的访问对加强苏缅两国友谊和加强国际合作所作的贡献。
    公报最后说，“两国政府首脑重申他们决心巩固，并且进一步加强两国之间现存的友谊和合作。”
