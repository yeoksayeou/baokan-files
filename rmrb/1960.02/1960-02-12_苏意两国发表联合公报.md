Title: Soviet-Italian Joint Communiqué Released
Author:
Date: 1960-02-12
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, Moscow, February 10th. TASS, Moscow, February 9th: A Soviet-Italian joint communiqué was released in Moscow on February 9th regarding the official visit of Italian President Gronchi to the Soviet Union.

The communiqué stated that during Italian President Gronchi’s visit to the Soviet Union, Chairman of the Presidium of the Supreme Soviet of the Soviet Union, Voroshilov, Chairman of the Council of Ministers of the Soviet Union, Khrushchev, and Gronchi, with the participation of the Foreign Ministers of both countries, Gromyko and Pella, extensively exchanged opinions. Both sides studied the international situation, discussed some extremely important issues, and compared their respective viewpoints.

The communiqué pointed out that both sides believed it was necessary to ensure peace under the conditions of the security, economic progress, and social progress of all peoples. Both sides comprehensively discussed the proposition of peaceful coexistence based on the principle of excluding war as a means of resolving international disputes and the principle of non-interference in the internal affairs of other countries.

The communiqué said that both sides paid particular attention to the study of disarmament and European security issues. Both sides firmly believed that complete and thorough disarmament with appropriate supervision is the most reliable means of maintaining and consolidating peace. Achieving concrete results in disarmament will promote the strengthening of trust between countries and greatly reduce the burden of military expenditure.

After noting the smooth development of trade relations between the Soviet Union and Italy, both sides decided to study ways to further expand trade between the two countries. Both sides agreed on new methods for resolving visa issues to promote the exchange of goods and travel between the two countries.

Gronchi already left Moscow on the 11th to return to his country.

Xinhua News Agency, February 10th. TASS, Moscow: The Soviet Union and Italy signed a cultural cooperation agreement in Moscow on the 9th. The agreement defined the principles of cooperation between the two countries in the fields of culture, science, technology, and sports.

The agreement stipulated that the Soviet Union and Italy would send scientists, university students, writers, actors, journalists, etc., to visit each other's countries. The Academies of Sciences and other scientific institutions of the two countries will exchange scientific and technological documents and information.

The agreement also stipulated promoting the development of the tourism industry between the two countries.

### 苏意两国发表联合公报

1960-02-12
第5版()
专栏：

    苏意两国发表联合公报
    据新华社10日讯　据塔斯社莫斯科9日讯：莫斯科9日就意大利总统格隆基正式访问苏联一事发表了苏联—意大利联合公报。
    公报说，在意大利总统格隆基访问苏联期间，苏联最高苏维埃主席团主席伏罗希洛夫、苏联部长会议主席赫鲁晓夫和格隆基，在两国外交部长葛罗米柯和贝拉的参加下，广泛地交换了意见。双方研究了国际局势，谈到了一些极重要的问题，比较了彼此的观点。
    公报指出，双方认为有必要在各国人民安全、经济进步和社会进步的条件下保证和平。双方全面讨论了以排除战争作为解决国际争端的手段的原则和互不干涉他国内政的原则为基础的和平共处的主张。
    公报说，双方特别注意研究了裁军和欧洲安全问题。双方坚信，有适当监督的全面彻底裁军是维护和巩固和平的最可靠的手段，在裁军方面取得具体结果，将促进各国间信任的加强以及大大减轻军事费用的负担。
    双方在指出苏意两国间贸易关系的顺利发展之后，决定研究进一步扩大两国贸易的途径。双方商定了解决签证问题的新方法，以促进两国间的商品交换和旅行。
    格隆基已经在11日离开莫斯科返国。
    新华社10日讯　据塔斯社莫斯科讯：苏联和意大利9日在莫斯科签订了文化合作协定。协定确定了两国在文化、科学、技术和体育方面合作的原则。
    协定规定，苏意两国将互派科学家、大学生、作家、演员、记者等到对方国家访问，两国科学院和其他科学机关之间将交换科学技术文件和情报。
    协定还规定促进两国之间旅行事业的发展。
