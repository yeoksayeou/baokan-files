Title: Japanese Representative to Asian-African Conference Hopes Conference Will Promote Peace, Yugoslav Spokesman Says Yugoslavia Attaches Importance to Asian-African Conference
Author:
Date: 1955-04-11
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, April 10th – According to a United Press International dispatch from Tokyo: Kazuo Toshikazu, a member of the Japanese delegation to the Asian-African Conference, told a United Press International reporter on the 9th that the Asian-African Conference, attended by leaders from twenty-nine countries, "will inevitably become an epoch-making event in world history."

He said that Japan hopes to contribute to the success of this important conference so that it can be a step towards promoting world peace.

He also said that Japan would actively cooperate with other countries and take all possible measures to stabilize the situation in the Asia-Africa region.

Xinhua News Agency, April 10th – According to a report from the Yugoslav News Agency, Belgrade, April 8th: The Director of the Yugoslav Information Bureau, Branko Draskovic, said at a press conference on the 8th that Yugoslavia attaches great importance to the Asian-African Conference held in Bandung. He said that the conference will discuss important issues concerning Asia and Africa based on a policy of peaceful coexistence and international cooperation.

### 日本出席亚非会议代表希望会议促进和平  南斯拉夫发言人表示南斯拉夫重视亚非会议

1955-04-11
第4版()
专栏：

    日本出席亚非会议代表希望会议促进和平
    南斯拉夫发言人表示南斯拉夫重视亚非会议
    新华社十日讯　据合众社东京消息：日本出席亚非会议代表团团员加濑俊一，九日接见合众社记者说，由二十九个国家的领袖们参加的亚非会议，“必然将成为世界史上划时代的事件”。
    他说，日本希望能对这个重要会议的成就作出贡献，以便使它成为促进世界和平的一个步骤。
    他又说，日本要和其他国家积极合作，采取一切方法来稳定亚非地区的局势。
    新华社十日讯　据南斯拉夫通讯社贝尔格莱德八日讯：南斯拉夫新闻局局长布朗科·德拉斯科维奇八日在记者招待会上说，南斯拉夫非常重视在万隆召开的亚非会议。他说，这次会议将根据一种共处和国际合作的政策来讨论有关亚洲和非洲这两个大洲的重要问题。
