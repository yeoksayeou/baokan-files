Title: Japanese "Sohyo" Leader Meets with Prime Minister Hatoyama, Demanding the Restoration of Diplomatic Relations Between Japan and China and the Soviet Union
Author:
Date: 1955-04-07
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, April 6th. According to Kyodo News Agency, Fujita Tohtaro, chairman of the General Council of Trade Unions of Japan (Sohyo), Japan's largest national trade union organization, and Secretary-General Takano Minoru, met with Japanese Prime Minister Ichiro Hatoyama on the 5th, demanding the restoration of diplomatic relations between Japan and China and the Soviet Union and rejecting the storage of atomic weapons in Japan. They also expressed opposition to the policy of freezing wages.

Ichiro Hatoyama replied that he was willing to do his best to adjust Japan's diplomatic relations with China and the Soviet Union, and strive to lift trade restrictions.

On the same day, representatives of the right-wing trade union organization, the All-Japan Trade Union Congress, also met with Ichiro Hatoyama, and put forward their opinions requesting the government to fulfill its promise of tax reduction and improve the social insurance system.

### 日“总评”负责人会见鸠山首相  要求恢复日本同中苏的邦交

1955-04-07
第4版()
专栏：

    日“总评”负责人会见鸠山首相
    要求恢复日本同中苏的邦交
    新华社六日讯　据共同社报道，日本最大的全国性工会组织日本工会总评议会主席藤田藤太郎、事务局长高野实等人，五日会见日本首相鸠山一郎，要求恢复日本同中、苏两国的邦交、拒绝在日本国内储存原子武器。他们并且表示反对冻结工资的政策。
    鸠山一郎答复说：他愿尽力调整日本同中、苏两国的邦交，并且力求解除贸易限制。
    在同一天，右翼的工会组织全日本工会会议的代表也会晤了鸠山一郎，提出了要求政府实现减税诺言和改善社会保险制度的意见。
