Title: Vietnamese, Indian and Other Countries' Delegations Depart for Asian-African Conference
Author:
Date: 1955-04-16
Page: 4
Xinhua News Agency, Hanoi, April 15th: The Vietnam News Agency reported on the 15th that Pham Van Dong, Vice-Premier and Minister of Foreign Affairs of the Democratic Republic of Vietnam, will lead the delegation of the Democratic Republic of Vietnam to attend the Asian-African Conference scheduled to open in Bandung on April 18th. The delegation list is as follows: Head of Delegation: Pham Van Dong, Vice-Premier and Minister of Foreign Affairs. Deputy Head of Delegation: Hoang Minh Giam, Minister of Propaganda. Members: Dr. Pham Ngoc Thach, Deputy Minister of Health; Hoang Van Duc, Member of the Economic Committee of the Government of the Democratic Republic of Vietnam; and Giao Van Chinh, Director of the Southeast Asia Department of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs.

Xinhua News Agency, Hanoi, April 15th: Pham Van Dong, head of the delegation of the Democratic Republic of Vietnam to the Asian-African Conference, deputy head Hoang Minh Giam, and members Pham Ngoc Thach, Hoang Van Duc, and Giao Van Chinh left Hanoi today and flew to Rangoon en route to Bandung. General Commander of the Vietnam People's Army Vo Nguyen Giap and heads of government departments, people's organizations, and parties, as well as diplomatic envoys from China, the Soviet Union, and other countries, and representatives of the International Supervisory and Control Commission, all saw them off at the airport.

Xinhua News Agency, New Delhi, April 15th: Indian Prime Minister Nehru, Egyptian Prime Minister Nasser, and Afghan Deputy Prime Minister and Foreign Minister Naim Khan left New Delhi together by plane on the 15th, via Rangoon, to attend the Asian-African Conference in Bandung. They were accompanied by Nehru's daughter, Mrs. Indira Gandhi, as well as members of the Indian delegation to the Asian-African Conference, Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs Syed Mahmud, and Indian Representative to the United Nations V.K. Krishna Menon, among others.

Before his departure, Nehru told reporters at the airport that he was going to Indonesia "with complete hope because the Bandung Conference represents the new spirit of Asia, and the new spirit of Asia itself represents the spirit of the times. Despite all the difficulties, anything that is in harmony with the course of history is bound to succeed. We are marching in step with history, so we are bound to succeed."

Nehru said that this victory was not against any country. "It means Asia asserting its right to prove itself. It means a healthy atmosphere of peace and cooperation in Asia and the world."

Xinhua News Agency, Rangoon, April 15th: Indian Prime Minister Nehru, Egyptian Prime Minister Nasser, and Afghan Deputy Prime Minister and Foreign Minister Naim Khan arrived in Rangoon by the same plane at 2:30 PM on the 15th on their way to Indonesia to attend the Asian-African Conference.

Burmese Prime Minister U Nu and Chinese Premier Zhou Enlai, who is currently in Rangoon, welcomed them at the airport.

Also present at the airport to welcome them were Burmese Foreign Minister Sao Khun Cho and senior government officials, as well as the envoys to Burma from India, Indonesia, China, the Soviet Union, the United Kingdom, and other countries.

Xinhua News Agency, Bandung, April 15th: The Laotian delegation to the Asian-African Conference, led by Laotian Prime Minister Katay Don Sasorith, and the Jordanian delegation to the Asian-African Conference, led by Jordanian Foreign Minister Salah, arrived in Bandung on the 14th.

Upon arriving in Bandung, Salah told reporters: The Asian-African Conference will have important consequences. The people of Africa and Asia must do everything possible to make it a great success.

Xinhua News Agency, Jakarta, April 14th: The Iraqi delegation to the Asian-African Conference, led by the head of the delegation, former Prime Minister Jamali, arrived in Bandung on the 12th.

Xinhua News Agency, April 15th: Singapore News: Major General Thapa, head of the Nepalese delegation to the Asian-African Conference, who arrived in Singapore on the 14th en route to Bandung, told reporters: The Nepalese delegation will support the Five Principles of Peaceful Coexistence announced by the Prime Ministers of India and China. He said that he believed that there was no contradiction between these principles and the principles of the United Nations Charter supported by Nepal.

Thapa said that the Nepalese delegation would support any action taken by the Asian-African Conference to resolve world problems through the principles of coexistence.

### 越南、印度等国出席亚非会议代表团启程

1955-04-16
第4版()
专栏：

    越南、印度等国出席亚非会议代表团启程
    新华社河内十五日电　据越南通讯社十五日讯：越南民主共和国副总理兼外交部长范文同将率领越南民主共和国代表团出席定于四月十八日在万隆开幕的亚非会议。代表团名单如下：团长：副总理兼外交部长范文同。副团长：宣传部长黄明鉴。团员：卫生部副部长范玉石博士，越南民主共和国政府经济委员会委员黄文德和外交部东南亚司司长葭文清。
    新华社河内十五日电　前往参加亚非会议的越南民主共和国代表团团长范文同、副团长黄明鉴和团员范玉石、黄文德、葭文清等在今日离开河内，乘飞机前往仰光转赴万隆。越南人民军总司令武元甲和政府各部门、各人民团体、各党派负责人以及中国、苏联和其他国家的外交使节和国际监督和监察委员会的代表，都到机场送行。
    新华社新德里十五日电　印度总理尼赫鲁、埃及总理纳赛尔和阿富汗副首相兼外交大臣纳伊姆汗十五日一同乘飞机离新德里，取道仰光前往万隆出席亚非会议。同行的还有尼赫鲁的女儿英迪拉·甘地夫人及出席亚非会议的印度代表团团员、外交部副部长赛·马茂德和印度驻联合国代表维·克·克里希纳·梅农等人。
    尼赫鲁启程前在机场对记者们发表谈话说，他到印度尼西亚去“是完全抱有希望的，因为万隆会议代表着亚洲的新精神，而亚洲的新精神本身代表着时代的精神。尽管有种种困难，任何东西只要和历史的进程协调，是一定会胜利的。我们正在和历史齐步前进，所以我们一定会取得胜利。”
    尼赫鲁说，这种胜利并不是反对任何国家的。“它意味着亚洲自我证明它自己的权利。它意味着亚洲和世界和平和合作的健康气氛。”
    新华社仰光十五日电　印度总理尼赫鲁、埃及总理纳赛尔、阿富汗副首相兼外交大臣纳伊姆汗在赴印度尼西亚出席亚非会议途中，在十五日下午二时三十分同机到达仰光。
    缅甸联邦政府总理吴努和现在仰光的中华人民共和国国务院总理周恩来到机场欢迎。
    到机场欢迎的还有缅甸外交部长藻昆卓及政府高级官员，以及印度、印度尼西亚、中国、苏联、英国等国驻缅甸使节。
    新华社万隆十五日电　由老挝首相克特率领的老挝出席亚非会议代表团以及由约旦外交大臣萨拉率领的约旦出席亚非会议代表团，已经在十四日到达万隆。
    萨拉在到达万隆后对记者发表谈话说：亚非会议将有重要的后果。非洲和亚洲的人民必须尽一切努力使它获得巨大的成功。
    新华社雅加达十四日电　出席亚非会议的伊拉克代表团在团长、前首相贾马利率领下，已经在十二日到达万隆。
    新华社十五日讯　新加坡消息：在前往万隆途中于十四日抵达新加坡的尼泊尔出席亚非会议代表团团长塔帕少将对记者说：尼泊尔代表团将支持印中两国总理所宣布的关于和平共处的五项原则。他说，他认为，这些原则同尼泊尔支持的联合国宪章的原则之间是没有矛盾的。
    塔帕表示，尼泊尔代表团将支持亚非会议所采取的通过共处原则来解决世界问题的任何行动。
