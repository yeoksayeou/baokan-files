Title: Government Officials and Envoys to China from Various Countries Express Condolences for the Death of Staff and Journalists of the Chinese Delegation to the Asian-African Conference
Author:
Date: 1955-04-18
Page: 4
Xinhua News Agency, April 17th: Regarding the staff and journalists of the Chinese delegation to the Asian-African Conference who were murdered by American-Chiang Kai-shek special agents, the Chinese government and embassies abroad continue to receive written or oral condolences from many foreign government officials and envoys to China.
    Academician Yudin, the Soviet Ambassador, was the first to express concern to the Chinese Ministry of Foreign Affairs on the evening of the plane crash involving members of the Chinese delegation. On the 16th, he made a special visit to Vice Minister Zhang Wentian to express condolences on behalf of the Soviet Ministry of Foreign Affairs and the Soviet Embassy in China.
    The Prime Minister of the Union of Burma, U Nu, and the Prime Minister of the Republic of Indonesia, Sastroamidjojo, both expressed their concern over the incident when receiving the Chinese Ambassadors to Burma and Indonesia, respectively. The Vice President of the Republic of India, Radhakrishnan, sent a letter to the Chinese Embassy in India, expressing condolences for the plane crash involving members of the Chinese delegation. The Prime Minister and Foreign Minister of the State of Israel, Moshe Sharett, sent a telegram to Premier Zhou Enlai expressing condolences. The Minister of Foreign Affairs of the People's Republic of Albania, Shytulla, received the Chinese Ambassador to Albania and expressed condolences for the incident.
    The Finnish Ambassador to China, Sundström, the Swiss Minister to China, Béguin, the Chargé d'Affaires ad interim of the Indonesian Embassy in China, Wiyogo Wirjodojo, the Chargé d'Affaires ad interim of the Pakistani Embassy in China, Sultan Muhammad Khan, and all the staff of the Romanian Embassy in China sent notes or letters of condolence and sympathy to the Chinese Ministry of Foreign Affairs. The Secretary-General of the Indian Ministry of External Affairs, Pillai, the French Ambassador to India, S. Ostrorog, and the Swiss Minister to India, R. Ressoniko, all sent letters to the Chinese Embassy in India expressing condolences.

### 对于我国参加亚非会议代表团工作人员和记者的遇难事件  各国政府官员和驻我国使节向我表示悼唁

1955-04-18
第4版()
专栏：

    对于我国参加亚非会议代表团工作人员和记者的遇难事件
    各国政府官员和驻我国使节向我表示悼唁
    新华社十七日讯　对于被美蒋特务谋杀的我国参加亚非会议代表团工作人员和记者，我国政府和驻外使馆继续收到许多外国政府官员和驻华使节的书面或口头悼唁。
    苏联大使尤金院士在我国代表团人员座机失事的当晚，首先向我国外交部表示关怀，十六日特访问张闻天副部长代表苏联外交部和苏联驻华大使馆表示吊唁。
    缅甸联邦总理吴努和印度尼西亚共和国总理沙斯特罗阿米佐约在接见我国驻缅甸大使和驻印度尼西亚大使时，都分别对这一事件表示关切。印度共和国副总统拉德哈克里希南致函我国驻印度大使馆，对我国代表团人员座机失事事件表示慰问。以色列共和国总理兼外交部长摩西·夏里特致电周恩来总理表示慰问。阿尔巴尼亚人民共和国外交部长什图拉特地接见我驻阿尔巴尼亚大使，对此次事件表示慰问。
    向我国外交部致送悼唁和慰问的照会或函件的，还有芬兰共和国驻华大使孙士敦、瑞士联邦驻华公使贝努义、印度尼西亚共和国驻华大使馆临时代办维约维尔多约、巴基斯坦驻华大使馆临时代办苏尔唐·穆罕默德·汗、罗马尼亚人民共和国驻华大使馆全体馆员。印度外交部秘书长皮莱、法国驻印度大使斯·奥斯特罗洛格、瑞士驻印度公使雷索尼柯都致函我国驻印度大使馆表示慰问。
