Title: Premier Zhou Enlai's Statement Universally Praised by Representatives from Various Countries
Author:
Date: 1955-04-24
Page: 1
Xinhua News Agency, April 23rd - Premier Zhou Enlai of the People's Republic of China delivered a statement at the meeting of the heads of delegations of the eight countries participating in the Asian-African Conference on the afternoon of April 23rd, which was universally praised by representatives from various countries.

According to a Reuters report, Burmese Prime Minister U Nu said that the statement "took a big step towards easing world tensions." "Moreover, this opinion is also the opinion of the other four Colombo countries."

Pakistani Prime Minister Ali believed that Premier Zhou Enlai's statement would "have an immediate impact on easing world tensions." He added, "Pakistan welcomes China's statement."

Indonesian Prime Minister Sastroamidjojo said that Premier Zhou Enlai's statement gave "us hope for the future and for easing world tensions."

A spokesman for the Egyptian delegation said that the statement "fully accords with the purpose of the Asian-African Conference."

Sri Lankan Prime Minister Kotelawala also said that it was "a very good statement." At the same time, he said that he did not intend to make further comments at this stage.

### 周恩来总理的声明受到各国代表普遍赞扬

1955-04-24
第1版()
专栏：

    周恩来总理的声明受到各国代表普遍赞扬
    新华社二十三日讯　中华人民共和国周恩来总理二十三日下午在参加亚非会议的八国代表团团长会议上发表的声明，受到各国代表的普遍赞扬。
    据路透社报道，缅甸总理吴努说，这个声明“向缓和世界紧张局势走了一大步。”“而且这个意见也是其他四个科伦坡国家的意见”。
    巴基斯坦总理阿里认为，周恩来总理的声明将会“在缓和世界紧张局势方面立即发生影响”。他又说，“巴基斯坦欢迎中国的声明”。
    印度尼西亚总理沙斯特罗阿米佐约说，周恩来总理的声明使“我们有了对于将来和对于缓和世界紧张局势的希望”。
    埃及代表团的一位发言人说，这个声明“完全符合亚非会议的目的”。
    锡兰总理科特拉瓦拉也说，这是“一篇非常好的声明”。同时，他说，在现阶段他不打算发表进一步的意见。
