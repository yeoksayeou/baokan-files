Title: Soviet-Japanese Talks to be Held in London on June 1st
Author:
Date: 1955-04-28
Page: 4
Xinhua News Agency, Moscow, April 26th: TASS reported on the 26th that the Soviet representative to the United Nations, Sobolev, had given the Japanese observer to the United Nations, Ambassador Sawada, a note stating that the Soviet government was willing to agree to hold Soviet-Japanese talks in Geneva or London.
    On April 23rd, Sawada gave Sobolev a note stating that the Japanese government had chosen London as the location for the aforementioned talks. At the same time, the ambassador said that the Japanese side expressed the hope that the talks would begin on June 1, 1955.
    On April 25th, Sobolev gave Ambassador Sawada a reply note. The reply note stated that the Soviet government agreed to hold Soviet-Japanese talks in London. The reply note also stated that the Soviet side considered it possible to start negotiations on June 1, 1955.

### 苏联和日本的谈判定六月一日在伦敦举行

1955-04-28
第4版()
专栏：

    苏联和日本的谈判定六月一日在伦敦举行
    新华社二十七日讯　塔斯社莫斯科二十六日讯：前已报道，苏联驻联合国代表索波列夫曾交给日本驻联合国观察员泽田大使一项照会，这项照会说，苏联政府愿意同意在日内瓦或伦敦举行苏日谈判。
    四月二十三日，泽田交给索波列夫一项照会，这项照会说，日本政府已选择伦敦作为上述谈判的地点。同时，大使说，日本方面表示希望在一九五五年六月一日开始举行谈判。
    四月二十五日，索波列夫交给泽田大使复照一件。复照说，苏联政府同意在伦敦举行苏日谈判。复照又说，苏联方面认为，在一九五五年六月一日开始谈判是可能的。
