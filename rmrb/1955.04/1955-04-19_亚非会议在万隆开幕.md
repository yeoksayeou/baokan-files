Title: Asian-African Conference Opens in Bandung
Author:
Date: 1955-04-19
Page: 1

Xinhua News Agency, Bandung, April 18th: The Asian-African Conference opened at 9:30 AM (local time) on the 18th in the Independence Building in Bandung. Approximately 340 delegates representing the governments of twenty-nine Asian and African nations, representing more than half of humanity, attended the conference. The twenty-nine countries attending the conference are: Afghanistan, Burma, Cambodia, Ceylon, China, Egypt, Ethiopia, Gold Coast, India, Indonesia, Iran, Iraq, Japan, Jordan, Laos, Lebanon, Liberia, Libya, Nepal, Pakistan, Philippines, Saudi Arabia, Sudan, Syria, Thailand, Turkey, Democratic Republic of Vietnam, South Vietnam, and Yemen. Among those attending today's opening meeting were Indonesian President Sukarno, Vice President Hatta, the heads and members of the delegations of the twenty-nine countries, ministers of various ministries of the Indonesian government, the speaker of the parliament, diplomatic envoys from various countries stationed in Jakarta, staff members of the joint secretariat of the conference, officials of the Bandung local government, as well as journalists and representatives of various news agencies from around the world.

On the rostrum were the President and Vice President of Indonesia, as well as the Prime Ministers of the five sponsoring countries. The national flags of the twenty-nine countries attending the conference were arranged in front of the huge crimson curtain on the rostrum.

Outside the conference hall, thousands of people cheered for the delegates, and they remained there throughout the conference.

Indonesian Prime Minister Sastroamidjojo invited Indonesian President Sukarno to deliver the opening speech. After President Sukarno finished his speech, the plenary session adjourned at 10:20 AM.

Xinhua News Agency, Bandung, April 18th: The plenary session of the Asian-African Conference on the morning of the 18th continued at 11:45 AM after a short break following President Sukarno's speech.

Egyptian Prime Minister Nasser suggested electing Indonesian Prime Minister Ali Sastroamidjojo as the Chairman of the conference. Premier of the People's Republic of China Zhou Enlai seconded the motion. Representatives from Jordan and the Philippines also expressed their support. The motion was unanimously passed by the conference. Prime Minister Ali Sastroamidjojo took the chair amidst warm applause.

Indian Prime Minister Nehru suggested that the conference express its gratitude to President Sukarno. This motion was seconded by Ceylon Prime Minister Kotelawala, Burma Prime Minister U Nu, and Pakistan Prime Minister Muhammad Ali, and was unanimously adopted.

Ali Sastroamidjojo then delivered a speech.

After Prime Minister Sastroamidjojo delivered his speech, a closed-door meeting began. The closed-door meeting adjourned at 1:30 PM in the afternoon.

When the delegates walked out of the conference hall, the tens of thousands of people standing outside cheered enthusiastically for them.

### 亚非会议在万隆开幕

1955-04-19
第1版()
专栏：

    亚非会议在万隆开幕
    据新华社万隆十八日电　亚非会议十八日上午九时三十分（当地时间）在万隆独立厅开幕。代表人类一半以上的二十九个亚洲和非洲国家的政府的约三百四十名代表出席了会议。出席会议的二十九个国家是：阿富汗、缅甸、柬埔寨、锡兰、中国、埃及、埃塞俄比亚、黄金海岸、印度、印度尼西亚、伊朗、伊拉克、日本、约旦、老挝、黎巴嫩、利比里亚、利比亚、尼泊尔、巴基斯坦、菲律宾、沙特阿拉伯、苏丹、叙利亚、泰国、土耳其、越南民主共和国、南越和也门。参加今天开幕会议的有印度尼西亚共和国总统苏加诺、副总统哈达、二十九国代表团团长和团员、印度尼西亚政府各部部长、国会议长、各国驻雅加达外交使节、会议联合秘书处工作人员、万隆地方政府官员以及世界各地来的记者和各通讯社代表。
    在主席台上的是印度尼西亚总统和副总统以及五个发起国家的总理。在主席台上巨大的深红色的帷幕前排列着出席会议的二十九个国家的国旗。
    在会议厅外面的街上有上千的人向代表们欢呼，他们在会议进行时一直留在那里。
    印度尼西亚总理沙斯特罗阿米佐约请印度尼西亚总统苏加诺致开幕词。在苏加诺总统演说完毕后，全体会议在上午十点二十分休会。
    新华社万隆十八日电　亚非会议十八日上午全体会议在苏加诺总统发表演说后，经短时间休息，在上午十一时四十五分继续开会。
    埃及总理纳赛尔建议选举印度尼西亚总理阿里·沙斯特罗阿米佐约为会议主席。中华人民共和国总理周恩来附议。约旦和菲律宾的代表也表示附议。会议一致通过了这个动议。阿里·沙斯特罗阿米佐约总理在热烈的掌声中就主席席位。
    印度总理尼赫鲁建议会议对苏加诺总统表示谢意。这个动议得到锡兰总理科特拉瓦拉、缅甸总理吴努、巴基斯坦总理穆罕默德·阿里的附议，并获得一致通过。
    阿里·沙斯特罗阿米佐约接着发表了演说。
    在沙斯特罗阿米佐约总理发表演说后，开始举行秘密会议。秘密会议在下午一时三十分休会。
    当代表们从会议厅走出来时，站在外面的成千上万的人们向他们热烈欢呼。
