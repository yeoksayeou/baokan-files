Title: Diagram of the Sino-Indian Border Issue
Author:
Date: 1959-09-16
Page: 6

To prove the illegality of the so-called McMahon Line, we now publish the following six maps:

　　Next Map: This is the part of the map "India and its Neighboring Countries" drawn by the Indian Survey Bureau from 1919 to 1932, showing the junction of northeastern India and the Kangzang region of China. On the map, the Sino-Indian border line extending due east from the southeast corner of Bhutan, although represented by the symbol of a tribal boundary line, its position and form are consistent with the Sino-Indian border line on our current maps.

　　Next Second Map: This is a part of the "India" full map on pages 60-61 of the "Oxford Advanced Atlas" (Seventh Edition) published by Oxford University in 1942. On this map, the so-called McMahon Line is drawn in red. However, the original Sino-Indian border line still exists.

　　Right First Map: This is a part of the "India" full map on pages 60-61 of the "Oxford Advanced Atlas" (Sixth Edition) published by Oxford University in 1940. The Sino-Indian eastern section of the border line shown on the map is still consistent with our maps, and there is still no so-called McMahon Line.

　　Right Second Map: This is a part of the full map of India on pages 50-51 of Volume 24 of the American edition of the Encyclopedia Britannica in 1947. There is no so-called McMahon Line on the map. The Sino-Indian border line is consistent with what is drawn on our current maps.

　　Right Third Map: This is a part of the full map of China on pages 54-55 of Volume 24 of the American edition of the Encyclopedia Britannica in 1947. Although printed in the same book as the previous map, the dotted line of the "McMahon Line" appears on the part where the Kangzang region of China and northeastern India meet, and the original Sino-Indian border line is gone.

　　Right Fourth Map: This is a part of the full map of India on page 67 of the "Philips' Atlas" (Fourteenth Edition) published in Britain in 1950. On this map, the so-called McMahon Line is even drawn as a solid line. (Xinhua News Agency Draft)

### 中印边界问题示意图（图片）

1959-09-16
第6版()
专栏：

    中印边界问题示意图
    为了证明所谓麦克马洪线是非法的，现在再发表以下六份地图：
    下一图　这是1919年至1932年印度测量局绘制的“印度及其邻国”图上印度东北部和中国康藏地区接壤的部分。在图上，由不丹东南角向正东延伸的中印界线，虽然是以部落界线的符号表示的，但其位置和形势是和我国现行地图上的中印界线相符的。
    下二图　这是1942年英国牛津大学出版的“牛津高级地图”（第七版）第60—61页所载“印度”全图的一部分。在这张图上把所谓麦克马洪线用红线画上了。但原来的中印边界线还依然存在。
    右一图　这是1940年英国牛津大学出版的“牛津高级地图”（第六版）第60—61页所载的“印度”全图的一部分，图上所示中印东段边界线仍同我国地图相符，仍没有所谓麦克马洪线。
    右二图　这是1947年美国版大英百科全书第24卷第50—51页上的印度全图的一部分，图上并没有所谓麦克马洪线。中印边界线和我国现行地图上所画的相符。
    右三图　这是1947年美国版大英百科全书第24卷第54—55页上的中国全图的一部分。虽然和前图同印在一本书中，但中国康藏地区和印度东北部接壤的部分却出现了“麦克马洪线”的虚线，原来的中印边界线没有了。
    右四图　这是1950年英国出版的“菲利浦图”（第十四版）67页上印度全图的一部分，在这图上竟把所谓麦克马洪线画成实线了。　（新华社稿）
