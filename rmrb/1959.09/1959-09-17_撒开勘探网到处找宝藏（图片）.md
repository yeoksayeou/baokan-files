Author:
Date: 1959-09-17
Page: 4

Spreading the Prospecting Net to Find Treasures Everywhere
    Geological personnel are prospecting for a large oilfield in western Gansu. Xinhua News Agency reporter Li Feng photo.
    Prospecting workers tread through thousands of miles of ice and snow in the Kunlun Mountains, searching for treasures for the motherland on mountain ridges at an altitude of 5,150 meters.
            Xinhua News Agency reporters Xu Bihua and Yang Liben photo.
    Geological exploration personnel in the Gobi Desert of Xinjiang are listening to news of construction from all over the country.
                    Xinhua News Agency reporter Li Yifang photo.
    Exploration personnel are conducting a general survey using radioactive methods to find raw materials for the atomic industry.
　　　　Xinhua News Agency draft
    In the primeval forests of western Yunnan, the survey teams are conducting surveys in the year-round snow-covered mountains.
                    Xinhua News Agency reporter Qi Guanshan photo.
    The Qilian Mountains, known as the "Chinese Urals," are beautiful and rich. Geological vanguards have already found abundant mineral deposits of copper, iron, lead, nickel, gold, silver, etc. here.
                    Xinhua News Agency reporter Zhao Zhunqing photo.

### 撒开勘探网到处找宝藏（图片）

1959-09-17
第4版()
专栏：

    撒开勘探网到处找宝藏
    地质人员正在甘肃西部勘探一个大油田。　新华社记者　黎枫摄
    勘探工人踏破昆仑千里冰雪，他们在海拔五千一百五十米的山岭上为祖国探宝。
            新华社记者　许必华、杨立本摄
    在新疆戈壁滩上的地质勘探人员，正在收听祖国各地建设的消息。
                    新华社记者　李义芳摄
    勘探人员用放射性方法进行普查，为原子工业寻找原料。　　　　
　新华社稿
    在终年积雪的云南滇西原始森林里，勘察队员们正在进行勘察。
                    新华社记者　齐观山摄
    号称“中国乌拉尔”的祁连山，美丽、富饶。地质尖兵已经在这里找到了铜、铁、铅、镍、金、银等丰富的矿藏。
                    新华社记者　赵准青摄
