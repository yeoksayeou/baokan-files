Title: Soviet Union Launches Second Cosmic Rocket - Expected to Reach Moon Tomorrow
Author: 
Date: 1959-09-13
Page: 1

Xinhua News Agency, Moscow, September 12th, TASS dispatch: TASS communique regarding the Soviet Union's launch of its second cosmic rocket towards the moon.

    In accordance with the plan for studying outer space and preparing for interplanetary flight, the Soviet Union successfully launched a second cosmic rocket on September 12, 1959.

    The purpose of launching the rocket is to study cosmic space on the way to the moon.

    The launch was carried out using a multi-stage rocket.

    The speed of the final stage of the rocket exceeded the second cosmic velocity (11.2 kilometers per second), and it is heading towards the moon.

    At 15:00 Moscow time on September 12, the Soviet cosmic rocket had left the ground by 78,500 kilometers, at which time it was located over an area north of New Guinea Island.

    The final stage of the cosmic rocket is a controllable rocket, weighing 1,511 kilograms (excluding fuel). It carries a container equipped with scientific instruments and radio equipment. This spherical container is sealed, filled with gas, and has an automatic temperature regulation system.

    After the container with scientific measuring instruments entered its orbit, it separated from the final stage rocket.

    The Soviet Union's second cosmic rocket will conduct the following research and investigations:

    To study the Earth's magnetic field and the Moon's magnetic field;

    To study the radiation belts around the Earth;

    To study the intensity and intensity variations of cosmic radiation;

    To study the heavy nuclei in cosmic radiation;

    To study the gaseous composition of interplanetary matter;

    To study meteor particles.

    The total weight of the scientific instruments and measuring instruments, together with the power source and container, is 390.2 kilograms.

    In order to send all scientific information back to Earth and to measure the rocket's operating parameters and control its flight, the rocket is equipped with:

    A radio transmitter operating at two frequencies – 20.003 MHz and 19.997 MHz.

    The radio transmitter sends telegraph signals with a length of 0.8 seconds to 1.5 seconds. The radio transmitter operates as follows: during the interval when the first frequency 20.003 MHz is not transmitting, pulses are emitted at the second frequency 19.997 MHz;

    A radio transmitter operating at frequencies of 19.993 MHz and 39.986 MHz.

    The radio transmitter's signals are alternating pulses with a length ranging from 0.2 to 0.8 seconds. The pulse repetition frequency is 1± (i.e., plus or minus) 0.15 Hz;

    A radio transmitter operating at a frequency of 183.6 MHz.

    The cosmic rocket has markings bearing the national emblem of the Union of Soviet Socialist Republics, and the inscription "September 1959".

    To facilitate visual observation of the cosmic rocket, the rocket is equipped with a special instrument for creating a sodium cloud - an artificial comet. The artificial comet will form at 21:39:42 Moscow time on September 12 and can be observed within the constellation Aquarius, its position approximately on the line connecting the stars Altair in the constellation Aquila and Fomalhaut in the constellation Piscis Austrinus.

    The comet's equatorial coordinates will be: Right Ascension - 20 hours 41 minutes, Declination - negative 7.2 degrees.

    Central Asia, the Caucasus, Ukraine, Belarus, the central part of the European Soviet Union, as well as Europe, Africa, Near Eastern countries, India, and western regions of China can observe and photograph the artificial comet using optical instruments equipped with filters that can resolve sodium spectral lines.

    All transmitters installed on the cosmic rocket are working normally. Ground-based radio engineering stations are receiving scientific information sent from the rocket.

    Special automated measuring instrument stations are located throughout the Soviet Union, continuously measuring the rocket's motion parameters through these sets of instruments. Processing of the measurement results and determination of various orbital elements are carried out by high-speed electronic computers.

    All Soviet radio stations will broadcast information about the cosmic rocket's operation.

    According to preliminary data, the rocket is operating along a trajectory close to the calculated orbit. It is expected that the cosmic rocket will reach the moon at 00:05 Moscow time on September 14.

    The successful launch of the Soviet Union's second cosmic rocket is a new and important stage in mankind's research and conquest of the universe. This expands the prospects for international cooperation in conquering outer space, which will help to further ease international tensions and consolidate the cause of peace.

### 苏联发射第二个宇宙火箭  预计将在明日到达月球

1959-09-13
第1版()
专栏：

    苏联发射第二个宇宙火箭
    预计将在明日到达月球
    新华社12日讯　塔斯社莫斯科12日讯：塔斯社关于苏联向月球发射第二个宇宙火箭的公报
    依照研究宇宙空间和准备行星际飞行的计划，苏联于1959年9月12日第二次成功地发射了一个宇宙火箭。
    发射火箭的目的是，研究飞向月球道路上的宇宙空间。
    发射是用多级火箭进行的。
    最后一级火箭的速度超过了第二宇宙速度（每秒钟十一点二公里），它正在向月球前进。
    9月12日莫斯科时间十五时，苏联宇宙火箭已离开地面七万八千五百公里，这时它已处于新几内亚岛以北区域的上空。
    宇宙火箭的最后一级是一个可操纵火箭，它的重量为一千五百一十一公斤（不包括燃料）。它带有一个装着科学仪器和无线电仪器的容器。这个球形的容器是密封的，内中装满气体，并有自动调节温度的系统。
    装有科学测量仪器的容器进入轨道以后，就脱离了最后一级火箭。
    苏联的第二个宇宙火箭将进行下列的研究考察：
    研究地球的磁场和月球的磁场；
    研究地球周围的辐射带；
    研究宇宙辐射的强度和强度变化；
    研究宇宙辐射中的重核；
    研究行星际物质的气体成分；
    研究流星粒子。
    科学仪器和测量仪器连同能源和容器的总重量是三百九十点二公斤。
    为了把全部科学情报发回地球和测量火箭运行参数和控制火箭的飞行，在火箭上设置有：
    一台用两种频率——二○·○○三兆周和一九·九九七兆周工作的无线电发射机。
    无线电发射机发出长度为○·八秒到一·五秒的电报信号。无线电发射机的工作情况如下：在第一个频率二○·○○三兆周发射间歇时，用第二个频率一九·九九七兆周发射出脉冲；
    一台用频率为一九·九九三兆周和三九·九八六兆周工作的无线电发射机。
    无线电发射机的信号是交替长度从○·二到○·八秒的脉冲。脉冲重复频率为1±（即正负号）0.15赫；
    一台用频率为一八三·六兆周工作的无线电发射机。
    在宇宙火箭上，有带有苏维埃社会主义共和国联盟国徽的标记，并写有1959年9月的字样。
    为了便于肉眼观察宇宙火箭，在火箭上装有一种制造钠云——人造彗星的专门仪器。人造彗星将于莫斯科时间9月12日二十一时三十九分四十二秒形成，并能在宝瓶星座内观测到人造彗星，位置约在联接天鹰星座星宿一和南鱼星座星宿一的线上。
    彗星的赤道座标将等于：赤经——二十时四十一分，赤纬——负七·二度。
    中亚细亚、高加索、乌克兰、白俄罗斯、苏联欧洲部分中部以及欧洲、非洲、近东国家、印度和中国西部地区可以用装有能析出钠谱线的滤光器的光学仪器来观测人造彗星和拍摄照片。
    宇宙火箭上安装的所有发射机都正常地工作着。地面上的无线电技术站正在接受火箭上发来的科学情报。
    苏联各地设有专门自动化测量仪器站，通过这些成套仪器不间断地测量火箭运动参数。整理测量的结果和确定轨道的各个要素，是由快速电子计算机进行的。
    苏联所有的电台都将播发关于宇宙火箭运行的情报。
    根据初步数据，火箭沿着接近于计算的轨道运行。预计宇宙火箭将在9月14日莫斯科时间零时零五分到达月球。
    苏联第二个宇宙火箭发射成功是人类研究和征服宇宙的新的重要阶段。这扩大了在征服宇宙空间方面国际合作的前景，这将有助于进一步缓和国际紧张局势和巩固和平事业。
