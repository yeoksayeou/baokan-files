Title: Indian Fair-Minded Public Opinion Calls for Maintaining Sino-Indian Friendship; Advises Supporters of Tibetan Reactionaries to Calm Down; Points Out That Those Who Shout for "Tibetan Independence" Are India's Greatest Enemies
Author:
Date: 1959-04-25
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, New Delhi, April 23rd: The Bengali newspaper "Yugantar" published an editorial on the 22nd refuting the fallacy that Tibet is an "independent country" and safeguarding the friendship between China and India.

The newspaper pointed out: "Tibet is not an independent sovereign state. If we rupture our relations with China over the exiled Dalai Lama or the Tibetan rebellion, the consequences will be terrible."

The editorial welcomed Premier Zhou Enlai's report to the National People's Congress. It praised Premier Zhou Enlai's patience and composure and commended his belief that Sino-Indian friendship would continue to be maintained. The editorial pointed out that "in view of the current atmosphere in India," it was necessary to specifically discuss Premier Zhou Enlai's report.

The editorial said, "After the Chinese Premier expressed this clear viewpoint and policy, analyzed the situation, and appealed to India, will those in our country who support the Tibetan reactionaries calm down a bit? Will they stop the shouting and jumping that began when the Dalai Lama arrived?"

Referring to the British editor Kingsley Martin's statement that those who ask Nehru to abandon his neutral policy are "fools," the editorial said, "But Kingsley Martin will regret to know that India has no shortage of such fools."

Xinhua News Agency, New Delhi, April 23rd: The Bengali newspaper "Swadhinata" published an editorial today saying, "Those who raise the slogan of 'Tibetan independence' and endanger Sino-Indian relations are not only enemies of China, they are also India's greatest enemies."

The editorial said, "Tibet is a part of great China. In the struggle to liberate Tibet, China's 650 million people cooperated actively and sincerely. The imperialist elements' and Indian reactionary forces' conspiracies against the liberation struggle are doomed to fail. It is the patriotic responsibility of the Indian people to oppose such conspiracies carried out by imperialism in India."

The editorial strongly condemned the activities of the leaders of the Praja Socialist Party, condemned the resolution passed by the National Executive Committee of the Praja Socialist Party, the speeches of Asoka Mehta and Kripalani, and their attempt to hold a so-called "Tibet Conference." The editorial said that they are "plotting to wage a cold war against China and push our country into the imperialist war bloc."

The newspaper mentioned Nehru's statement in the Council of States on April 20th, in which Nehru stated that political activities against another country cannot be carried out from one country.

The newspaper questioned who sympathizes with the upper-class Tibetan traitors. It pointed out that those who express sympathy for these people are "the number one enemies of peace, independence, and Panchsheel, the imperialists."

Xinhua News Agency, New Delhi, April 23rd: The latest issue of the Indian weekly "New Age" published an editorial signed by Joshi, entitled "The Fourth Anniversary of the Bandung Conference."

The editorial said that just as the epoch-making fourth anniversary of the Bandung Conference is approaching, some Indian newspapers are making a big fuss about the recent Tibetan rebellion, and they are trying to relegate the threat to India from the US-Pakistan bilateral military agreement and other imperialist aggressive conspiracies to a secondary position. Those elements who hope for the failure of the Bandung Conference are the very elements who are using the Tibetan rebellion to achieve their own goals.

The editorial said that on the fourth anniversary of the Bandung Conference, our common enemies are using the Tibetan rebellion to create discord between India and China.

The editorial said that it is not difficult to see that weakening Sino-Indian unity is a blow to Afro-Asian unity, undermining national liberation movements, and playing into the hands of imperialism.

The editorial pointed out that the imperialist bloc headed by the United States is constantly striving to conclude military agreements with puppets in Asian countries, spread discord, and conspire to undermine the Geneva Accords on the Indochina issue, thereby dividing and destroying the international friendship of Bandung. The editorial said that the Tibetan rebellion is part of this conspiracy.

### 印度公正舆论呼吁维护印中友好  劝告支持西藏反动派的人冷静些  指出叫嚷“西藏独立”的人正是印度最大的敌人

1959-04-25
第5版()
专栏：

    印度公正舆论呼吁维护印中友好
    劝告支持西藏反动派的人冷静些
    指出叫嚷“西藏独立”的人正是印度最大的敌人
    新华社新德里23日电　孟加拉文日报“划时代报”22日发表社论驳斥所谓西藏是“独立国家”的谬论，维护中印两国友好。
    这家报纸指出：“西藏并不是独立的主权国家。如果我们在流亡的达赖喇嘛或西藏叛乱问题上使我们同中国的关系破裂，其后果将是可怕的。”
    社论欢迎周恩来总理在全国人民代表大会上所作的报告，它赞扬周恩来总理的耐心和沉着，并且称赞他认为中印友谊将继续保持下去的信念。社论指出，“鉴于印度目前的气氛”，所以要特别谈一谈周恩来总理的这个报告。
    社论说，“在中国总理表示了这种明确的观点和政策，分析了形势，并且向印度提出呼吁以后，我们国家内那些支持西藏反动派的人士是否会稍许冷静一点？他们是否会停止在达赖喇嘛到达时开始的叫嚣和蹦跳？”
    社论在谈到一位英国编辑金斯莱·马丁认为要求尼赫鲁放弃中立政策的人是“蠢货”的这种说法时说，“但是金斯莱·马丁将会遗憾地知道，印度是不乏这种蠢货的。”
    新华社新德里23日电　孟加拉文日报“自由报”今天发表社论说，“那些提出‘西藏独立’口号而危害印中关系的人不仅是中国的敌人，他们也是印度最大的敌人。”
    社论说，“西藏是伟大中国的一部分，在解放西藏的斗争中，中国六亿五千万人民积极和真诚地合作。帝国主义分子和印度反动势力反对解放斗争的阴谋是注定要失败的。反对帝国主义在印度进行的这种阴谋是印度人民的爱国责任。”
    社论强烈谴责人民社会党领导人的活动，谴责人民社会党全国执行委员会所通过的决议、阿索卡·梅达和克里帕拉尼等的讲话，以及他们想举行所谓“西藏大会”的企图。社论说，他们“阴谋进行反对中国的冷战，而把我国推进帝国主义的战争集团”。
    报纸提到尼赫鲁4月20日在联邦院发表的声明，尼赫鲁曾经表示，不能从一个国家进行反对另一个国家的政治活动。
    报纸质问什么人同情西藏的上层卖国贼？它指出，表示同情这些人的是“和平、独立和潘查希拉的头号敌人帝国主义”。
    新华社新德里23日电　印度最近一期的“新世纪”周报发表了署名乔希的一篇社论，题目是“万隆会议四周年”。
    社论说，正当划时代的万隆会议四周年纪念到来的时候，一部分印度报纸正在最近发生的西藏叛乱事件上大做文章，他们企图把美国—巴基斯坦双边军事协定和其它帝国主义侵略阴谋对印度的威胁置于次要地位。那些希望万隆会议失败的分子，就是正在利用西藏叛乱事件来达到自己目的的分子。
    社论说，在万隆会议四周年纪念的时候，我们的共同敌人利用西藏叛乱事件，制造印度和中国的不和。
    社论说，完全不难看到，削弱印中的团结就是对亚非团结的打击，就是破坏民族解放运动和玩帝国主义的把戏。
    社论指出，以美国为首的帝国主义集团正在不断地努力，同亚洲国家中的傀儡缔结军事协定，散布不和以及阴谋破坏关于印度支那问题的日内瓦协议，从而分裂和破坏万隆的国际友谊。社论说，西藏叛乱事件就是这种阴谋的一部分。
