　　International Briefs
    Kishi Government Illegally Arrests Takakura Teru
    Tokyo dispatch: Takakura Teru, former member of the Central Committee of the Japanese Communist Party and writer, was illegally arrested by the police authorities of the Kishi Nobusuke government at the airport on the evening of the 15th when he returned to Tokyo from abroad.
    Nixon to Visit Soviet Union in July
    New York dispatch: U.S. President Eisenhower announced that U.S. Vice President Nixon will visit the Soviet Union in July this year to preside over the opening ceremony of the American Industrial Exhibition to be held in Moscow at that time. Eisenhower said that Nixon's trip to the Soviet Union is only a private and informal visit.
    Pakistani President Announces Ban on Political Party Activities
    Karachi dispatch: Pakistani President Ayub Khan announced in Burari, West Pakistan, on the 16th that political parties will not be allowed to resume activities in Pakistan before the constitution is formulated.

### 国际简讯

1959-04-18
第6版()
专栏：国际简讯

　　国际简讯
    岸政府非法逮捕高仓辉
    东京讯：前日本共产党中央委员会委员、作家高仓辉15日晚上从国外回到东京时，在机场被岸信介政府的警察当局非法逮捕。
    尼克松将于七月访苏
    纽约讯：美国总统艾森豪威尔宣布，美国副总统尼克松将于今年7月访问苏联，主持届时在莫斯科举行的美国工业展览会的开幕典礼。艾森豪威尔说，尼克松这次苏联之行只是私人性质的非正式访问。
    巴总统宣布禁止政党活动
    卡拉奇讯：巴基斯坦总统阿尤布汗16日在西巴基斯坦的布拉里宣布，巴基斯坦在宪法制定之前，不准政党恢复活动。
