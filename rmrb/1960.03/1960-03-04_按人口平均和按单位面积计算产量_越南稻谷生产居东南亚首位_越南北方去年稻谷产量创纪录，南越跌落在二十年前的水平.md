Title: Rice Production in Vietnam Leads Southeast Asia in Terms of Per Capita and Per Unit Area Output.
Author:
Date: 1960-03-04
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, Hanoi, 2nd. Compared with South Vietnam, North Vietnam has left it far behind in rice production, whether in terms of planting area, total output, yield per unit area, or per capita output. In terms of rice yield per unit area or per capita output, North Vietnam has also surpassed any other country in Southeast Asia.

This was pointed out in statistical data on the significant achievements made by North Vietnam in rice production in recent years after the liberation, which was newly published by the Vietnam News Agency yesterday.

The data pointed out that North Vietnam's rice production and yield per unit area in 1959 reached unprecedented levels. In that year, North Vietnam's total rice output was 5,194,000 tons, with an average yield per unit area of 2,285 kilograms per hectare, and a per capita average of 352 kilograms.

The data stated that South Vietnam's yield per unit area, planting area, or total output are declining or remaining at the production levels of fifteen or twenty years ago. The average yield per hectare in the North in 1959 was about 1,100 kilograms more than in the South (almost double). Compared with before the August Revolution in 1945, North Vietnam's rice production in 1959 increased by 2,787,000 tons. However, the South's production decreased by 1,360,000 tons compared to the pre-war period. During the French rule, the annual rice production in the North was 2,000,000 tons less than in the South, but by 1959, the total output in the North was 2,254,000 tons more than in the South, and this excess alone was equivalent to 76% of the total output of the South in 1959.

The data pointed out that North Vietnam's rice production yield per unit area and per capita output have also surpassed Southeast Asian countries such as Thailand, Malaya, and the Philippines. According to statistics from the Food and Agriculture Organization of the United Nations, Thailand's average yield per hectare in recent years was 1,330 kilograms, with a per capita average of 330 kilograms. Malaya's average yield per hectare was 2,260 kilograms, with a per capita average of 151 kilograms. The Philippines' average yield per hectare was 1,260 kilograms, with a per capita average of 123 kilograms.

The data pointed out that the above figures powerfully prove that only under the socialist system, only after the feudal relations of production are eliminated and the peasants embark on the road of collective production, can the productivity of agriculture be developed at a high speed.

### 按人口平均和按单位面积计算产量  越南稻谷生产居东南亚首位  越南北方去年稻谷产量创纪录，南越跌落在二十年前的水平

1960-03-04
第5版()
专栏：

    按人口平均和按单位面积计算产量
    越南稻谷生产居东南亚首位
    越南北方去年稻谷产量创纪录，南越跌落在二十年前的水平
    新华社河内2日电　越南北方稻谷生产同南越比较，无论在种植面积、总产量、单位面积产量或者按人口平均计算产量，都已远远地把它抛在后面。在稻谷单位面积产量或按人口平均计算产量，越南北方也已经超过东南亚任何一个国家。
    这是越南通讯社昨天新发表的关于北越解放后几年来在稻谷生产方面取得的重大成就的统计资料所指出的。
    这篇资料指出，越南北方1959年稻谷产量和单位面积产量达到空前未有的水平。这一年，越南北方稻谷总产量是五百十九万四千吨，平均单位面积产量达到每公顷二千二百八十五公斤，按人口平均计算为每人三百五十二公斤。
    资料说，但是南越无论是单位面积产量、种植面积或者总产量都在下降或者停留在十五、二十年前的生产水平上。北方1959年平均每公顷产量比南方多一千一百公斤左右（即将近一倍）。与1945年八月革命以前比较，北方1959年的稻谷产量增加了二百七十八万七千吨。而南方却比战前减产了一百三十六万吨。在法国统治时期，北方每年的稻谷产量都比南方少二百万吨，但到1959年，北方的总产量已比南方多二百二十五万四千吨，仅这超过的数量就相当于南方1959年总产量的76％。
    资料指出，越南北方稻谷生产单位面积产量和按人口平均计算产量也已经超过泰国、马来亚、菲律宾等东南亚国家。据联合国粮食和农业组织的统计，泰国近年平均每公顷产量为一千三百三十公斤，按人口平均计算为每人三百三十公斤。马来亚平均每公顷产量为二千二百六十公斤，按人口平均为每人一百五十一公斤。菲律宾平均每公顷产量一千二百六十公斤，按人口平均为每人一百二十三公斤。
    资料指出，上述数字有力地证明，只有在社会主义制度下，只有在封建的生产关系被消灭和农民走上了集体生产的道路之后，农业的生产力才有可能获得高速度发展。
