Title: To Provide Full-Day Care for Children, Soviets Will Establish Extended-Day Schools
Author:
Date: 1960-03-14
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, Moscow, March 12th: TASS reports that the Central Committee of the Communist Party of the Soviet Union and the Council of Ministers of the Soviet Union have decided that, in addition to developing a network of boarding schools, they will also establish extended-day schools for children and adolescents in urban and rural areas.

    In these schools, children can receive full-day care from teachers and experienced instructors. These schools are generally established for students in grades one through eight and will be located in existing or newly built schools.

    Students in these schools can receive two meals at their own expense, free of charge, or at a reduced rate.

### 让儿童整天受到照顾  苏将建立长日制学校

1960-03-14
第5版()
专栏：

    让儿童整天受到照顾
    苏将建立长日制学校
    据新华社13日讯　塔斯社莫斯科12日讯：苏联共产党中央委员会和苏联部长会议决定：除了发展寄宿学校网以外，还要为城乡儿童和少年建立长日制学校。
    在这些学校里孩子们可以整天受到教师和有经验的教导员的照顾。这些学校一般是为一到八年级的学生建立的，将设在已有的或新建的学校中。
    这些学校的学生可以自费、免费、或减费获得两顿饭食。
