Title: A Handful of Gold Scattered Across the Sky (and Two Other Poems)
Author: Lǐ Chén
Date: 1960-03-10
Page: 8

    A Handful of Gold Scattered Across the Sky (and Two Other Poems)
    Lǐ Chén
    Skilled hands build rice paddies,
    Fields as smooth as mirrors reflecting the blue sky;
    Sowing girls are like fairies,
    A handful of gold scattered across the sky.

    Planting Out Spring Light Everywhere
    Planting millions of acres of green seedlings,
    Planting out spring light everywhere;
    Now green seas ripple gently,
    Autumn's harvest fills the eye with golden waves;
    Last year, stepping on mountains of grain to gather cloud flowers,
    This autumn, stepping on mountains of grain to pluck the moon.

    Spring Rain No Longer as Precious as Oil
    Water fills the ponds, water fills the ditches,
    Perennial clear water, green and leisurely;
    Not waiting for Grain Rain to thunder,
    Spring water already flows everywhere.

### 一把金子撒满天（外二首）
李晨
1960-03-10
第8版()
专栏：

    一把金子撒满天（外二首）
    李晨
    能工巧手作秧田，
    田平如镜映蓝天；
    播种姑娘赛仙女，
    一把金子撒满天。
    栽出遍地春光
    插下万顷绿秧，
    栽出遍地春光；
    如今绿海泛微波，
    秋熟满眼翻金浪；
    去年踏着粮山采云花，
    今秋踏着粮山摘月亮。
    春雨不再贵如油
    水满塘，水满沟，
    长年清水绿悠悠；
    不等谷雨响春雷，
    春水早已遍地流。
