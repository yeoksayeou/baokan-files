Title: Afghan Prime Minister Hosts Khrushchev; Daoud Expresses Gratitude for Soviet Aid to Afghanistan; Khrushchev Points Out That the Traditional Friendly Relations Between the Two Countries Cannot Be Shaken
Author:
Date: 1960-03-04
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, Kabul, March 3rd: According to TASS, Soviet Chairman of the Council of Ministers Khrushchev, who is currently on a friendly visit to Afghanistan, paid a call on Afghan Prime Minister Daoud on the morning of March 3rd.

Khrushchev and Daoud exchanged views warmly.

On March 3rd, Khrushchev also visited the Kabul airport project, which the Soviet Union is helping Afghanistan to build. The airport is scheduled to be completed in 1962. It will be Afghanistan's largest and most modern airport, capable of handling jet aircraft takeoffs and landings.

On March 2nd, Khrushchev formally called on Afghan King Mohammed Zahir Shah. They had a long and friendly conversation. Khrushchev also presented the King with a replica of the emblem commemorating the Soviet cosmic rocket sending a lunar probe to the moon as a memento.

On the same day, Khrushchev laid a wreath at the tomb of the late Afghan King Mohammed Nadir Shah.

Afghan Prime Minister Daoud hosted a banquet on March 2nd in honor of Khrushchev. In his speech at the banquet, Daoud emphasized the friendly relations between Afghanistan and the Soviet Union and expressed his gratitude for the Soviet Union's very benevolent and unconditional assistance to Afghanistan.

Daoud said that the Afghan government pursues a policy of neutrality, the basic content of which is to safeguard peace through the establishment of friendship and cooperation with the people of all countries. He also pointed out that the Afghan government supports Khrushchev's proposal for complete disarmament.

In his speech at the banquet, Khrushchev pointed out that the foundation of the friendly relations between the Soviet Union and Afghanistan is that the people and governments of both countries are committed to maintaining and strengthening peace, and to building their relations on the principles of mutual respect and non-interference in each other's internal affairs. He said that if the Soviet Union and Afghanistan continue to cherish and strengthen this friendship in the future, then enemies will absolutely not be able to shake the traditional friendly relations between the two countries.

### 阿富汗首相宴请赫鲁晓夫  达乌德表示感谢苏联向阿富汗提供的援助  赫鲁晓夫指出两国传统友好关系动摇不了

1960-03-04
第5版()
专栏：

    阿富汗首相宴请赫鲁晓夫
    达乌德表示感谢苏联向阿富汗提供的援助
    赫鲁晓夫指出两国传统友好关系动摇不了
    新华社3日讯　据塔斯社喀布尔消息：正在阿富汗进行友好访问的苏联部长会议主席赫鲁晓夫3日上午拜会了阿富汗首相达乌德。
    赫鲁晓夫和达乌德热情地交换了意见。
    赫鲁晓夫3日还参观了苏联帮助阿富汗修建的喀布尔飞机场工程。这个飞机场计划在1962年完工。它将是阿富汗最大和最现代化的机场，可以供喷气式客机起飞和降落。
    赫鲁晓夫在2日正式拜会了阿富汗国王穆罕默德·查希尔·沙阿。他们进行了长时间的友好谈话。赫鲁晓夫还把苏联宇宙火箭送上月球的标志的复制品送给国王作为纪念。
    同一天，赫鲁晓夫到已故阿富汗国王穆罕默德·纳迪尔·沙阿的陵墓前献了花圈。
    阿富汗首相达乌德在2日举行了宴会招待赫鲁晓夫。达乌德在宴会上致词时，强调阿富汗和苏联两国的友好关系，并且表示感谢苏联向阿富汗提供的十分善意的和不附带任何条件的援助。
    达乌德说，阿富汗政府奉行着中立政策，这一政策的基本内容是通过同各国人民建立友谊和合作来保障和平。他还指出，阿富汗政府支持赫鲁晓夫提出的全面裁军建议。
    赫鲁晓夫在宴会上指出，苏联和阿富汗两国的友好关系的基础是：两国人民和政府都致力于维护和加强和平，致力于把两国的关系建立在互相尊重和互不干涉内政的原则上。他说，如果苏联和阿富汗两国今后都珍视和加强两国的这种友谊，那么敌人就绝对动摇不了两国的传统的友好关系。
