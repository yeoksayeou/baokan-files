　　International Briefs
　　Indian Deputy Prime Minister Admits
  　Decision to Expand Armaments
    TASS, Delhi: Indian Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Home Affairs, Patel, declared at a mass rally in Dehradun that the Indian government has decided to establish a "huge and mobile armed force." Patel said the Indian government will spend 1.6 billion rupees annually for military expenses, while before the partition of India, the annual military expenditure was 1 billion rupees.
    Furthermore, according to a report from the Indian Politician newspaper: India has already supplied arms and ammunition to the reactionary government of Ba Swe in Burma.
    Hungarian Council of Ministers
　　Resigns According to Law
    Budapest Dispatch: In view of the recent parliamentary elections in Hungary, the Hungarian Council of Ministers held a meeting on the 20th and decided to resign according to law. They notified the President that they agreed to continue to perform all government affairs until the new parliament convenes and a new government is formed.

### 国际简讯

1949-05-27
第3版()
专栏：

　　国际简讯
　　印副总理招认
  　决扩充军备
    塔斯社德里讯：印度副总理兼内政部部长巴特尔在德拉登群众大会上声称，印度政府决定建立“庞大和机动的武装部队”。巴特尔说印政府每年将支出十六亿卢比供军费之需，而在印度分治之前，每年军费为十亿卢比。
    又据印度政治家报消息指出：印度已经把军火和弹药供给缅甸达钦努反动政府。
    匈部长会议
　　依法辞职
    布达佩斯讯：鉴于匈牙利最近举行的议会选举，匈牙利部长会议于二十日上午举行会议，决定依法辞职，并通知总统，他们同意继续履行一切政务，直到新的议会召开与新政府成立为止。
