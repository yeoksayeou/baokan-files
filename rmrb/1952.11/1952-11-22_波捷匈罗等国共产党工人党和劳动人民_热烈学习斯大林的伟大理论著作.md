Title: Communist Parties, Workers' Parties, and Working People in Poland, Czechoslovakia, Hungary, Romania, and other countries are enthusiastically studying Stalin's great theoretical works

Author:
Date: 1952-11-22
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, November 21st: The Communist Parties, Workers' Parties, and working people of Poland, Czechoslovakia, Hungary, and Romania are actively studying Stalin's great work "Economic Problems of Socialism in the USSR" and the various documents of the 19th Congress of the Communist Party of the Soviet Union.

    The Polish United Workers' Party convened a conference of party activists in Warsaw on November 4th. Bolesław Bierut, Chairman of the Central Committee of the Polish United Workers' Party, who participated in the CPSU Congress, personally delivered a report at the meeting, elaborating in detail the historical significance of Stalin's work and the CPSU Congress. He pointed out that the primary task of Polish United Workers' Party activists is to creatively apply the experiences and lessons of the CPSU Congress to the specific tasks of building socialism in Poland. The meeting read out a resolution of the Secretariat of the Central Committee of the Polish United Workers' Party, instructing party organizations at all levels to immediately launch the publicity and discussion of Stalin's work and the documents of the CPSU Congress in November and December. The resolution also stipulated that the documents of the 19th Congress of the CPSU should be used as teaching materials for party organizations at all levels, that systematic discussions on Stalin's work and the documents of the CPSU Congress should be carried out in newspapers and periodicals, that experiences in research and discussion should be exchanged, that exhibitions on the CPSU Congress should be organized, and that a series of lectures on the CPSU Congress should be organized in radio programs.

    The Communist Party of Czechoslovakia held a cadre conference in Prague in late October, attended by 10,000 people. Klement Gottwald, Chairman of the Central Committee of the CPCz, delivered a report at the meeting and instructed on improving the work of the Party in the spirit of the CPSU Congress. He said that Stalin's great work "Economic Problems of Socialism in the USSR" is of great significance to the working people of Czechoslovakia who are embarking on the construction of socialism. This work and the achievements of the CPSU Congress are an inexhaustible source of experience in our daily work. The Political Secretariat of the Central Committee of the CPCz recently adopted a resolution deciding to hold a "Party Education Year" in 1952-1953, requiring party organizations at all levels to focus on studying the documents of the CPSU Congress and Stalin's new work. The Czechoslovak-Soviet Friendship Association also convened a meeting on October 21st to study in detail the theories revealed in Stalin's brilliant work and how to apply these theories to Czechoslovakia's socialist economic construction.

    The Central Committee of the Hungarian Working People's Party held a meeting in Budapest in late October. Mátyás Rákosi, General Secretary of the Working People's Party, gave a report at the meeting on behalf of the delegation of the Hungarian Working People's Party that attended the CPSU Congress. The meeting adopted a resolution calling on party organizations and members at all levels to study Stalin's "Economic Problems of Socialism in the USSR" and the documents of the 19th Congress of the CPSU. The resolution also decided to formulate study methods and organize a committee with Rákosi's participation to lead this study.

    The Central Committee of the Romanian Workers' Party, together with the Bucharest Municipal Committee of the Workers' Party, held a meeting in Bucharest on October 21st. The meeting was attended by secretaries of the Workers' Party district committees, as well as more than 4,000 Workers' Party members and activists. Gheorghe Gheorghiu-Dej, General Secretary of the Central Committee of the Romanian Workers' Party, who participated in the CPSU Congress, reported on the world-historical significance and achievements of the CPSU Congress. Currently, factories and institutions throughout Romania are enthusiastically launching discussions on Stalin's works and the documents of the 19th Congress of the CPSU.

### 波捷匈罗等国共产党工人党和劳动人民  热烈学习斯大林的伟大理论著作

1952-11-22
第4版()
专栏：

    波捷匈罗等国共产党工人党和劳动人民
    热烈学习斯大林的伟大理论著作
    【新华社二十一日讯】波兰、捷克斯洛伐克、匈牙利和罗马尼亚四国的共产党、工人党和劳动人民，正在积极学习斯大林的伟大著作“苏联社会主义经济问题”和苏联共产党第十九次代表大会的各项文件。
    波兰统一工人党十一月四日在华沙召集了党的活动分子大会。参加联共代表大会的波兰统一工人党中央委员会主席贝鲁特亲自在会上作了报告，详尽地阐述了斯大林的著作和联共代表大会的历史意义。他指出：波兰统一工人党活动分子的首要任务，是将联共代表大会的经验教训创造性地应用到波兰建设社会主义的具体任务上。会上宣读了波兰统一工人党中央委员会书记处的一项决议，责成各级党组织立即在十一月和十二月内展开对斯大林的著作和联共代表大会各项文件的宣传和讨论。决议并确定：联共第十九次代表大会的文件应作为各级党组织的教材，应在报刊上有系统地展开对斯大林著作和联共代表大会文件的讨论，交流研讨的经验，组织关于联共代表大会的展览会，在广播节目中组织一连串的关于联共代表大会的讲演。
    捷克斯洛伐克共产党十月下旬在布拉格举行了干部大会，到会的有一万人。捷共中央委员会主席哥特瓦尔德在会上作了报告，并根据联共代表大会的精神来指示改进党的工作。他说：斯大林的伟大著作“苏联社会主义经济问题”对于着手建设社会主义的捷克斯洛伐克劳动人民，同样具有伟大的意义。这部著作和联共代表大会的成果，是我们的日常工作中取之不尽的经验的源泉。捷共中央委员会政治书记处最近已通过了一项决议，决定在一九五二—一九五三年举行“党内教育年”，要各级党组织着重研究联共代表大会的文件和斯大林的新著作。捷苏友好协会也在十月二十一日召开一次会议，详细研究了斯大林天才著作中所揭示的理论和如何将这些理论应用到捷克斯洛伐克社会主义经济建设上。
    匈牙利劳动人民党中央委员会十月下旬在布达佩斯举行了会议。劳动人民党总书记拉科西代表出席联共代表大会的匈牙利劳动人民党代表团在会上作了报告。会上通过一项决议，号召各级党组织和党员学习斯大林的“苏联社会主义经济问题”和联共第十九次代表大会的文件。决议并决定制订学习办法，组织有拉科西参加的委员会来领导这个学习。
    罗马尼亚工人党中央委员会十月二十一日会同工人党布加勒斯特市委员会在布加勒斯特举行了会议。参加会议的有工人党各区委员会书记，以及工人党党员和活动分子四千多人。参加联共代表大会的罗马尼亚工人党中央委员会总书记乔治乌—德治在会上报告了联共代表大会的世界性历史意义和成就。现在，罗马尼亚全国各地的工厂、机关正在热烈展开关于斯大林著作和联共第十九次代表大会文件的讨论。
