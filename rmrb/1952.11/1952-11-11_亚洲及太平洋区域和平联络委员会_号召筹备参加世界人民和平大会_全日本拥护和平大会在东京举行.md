Title: Asian and Pacific Regions Peace Liaison Committee Calls for Preparations to Participate in the World People's Peace Congress; All-Japan Peace Protection Congress Held in Tokyo
Author: 
Date: 1952-11-11
Page: 4

    Asian and Pacific Regions Peace Liaison Committee
    Calls for Preparations to Participate in the World People's Peace Congress
    All-Japan Peace Protection Congress Held in Tokyo
    [Xinhua News Agency, 10th] The Asian and Pacific Regions Peace Liaison Committee has sent a telegram to the National Peace Committees of the Asian and Pacific Regions, expressing the hope that the people of all countries will intensify their preparations to participate in the World People's Peace Congress. The telegram is as follows: To the Peace Committees of all countries in the Asian and Pacific Regions:
    The World People's Peace Congress of great significance will be held in Vienna on December 12. At the Asian and Pacific Regions Peace Conference on October 12, our representatives unanimously passed a resolution supporting the convening of the World People's Peace Congress. We firmly believe that the convening of this congress, through free and broad discussions, will contribute to the most concerned issue of easing international tensions and seeking peaceful and reasonable solutions.
    Now that the date of the Vienna Peace Congress is approaching, in order to welcome this meeting, we earnestly hope that people from all walks of life, national peace committees, and political, social, and religious organizations in Asian, Australian, and Pacific coastal countries will unite and intensify various preparatory works. We earnestly request you to: (1) Convey the significance of convening this meeting to the men and women of your country and widely launch discussions; (2) Actively form delegations with the broadest possible representation, including people who have not participated in the peace movement, to attend this congress together; (3) Promptly inform the World Peace Council and the Asian and Pacific Regions Peace Liaison Committee of your preparations and the composition of your delegation. We hope that we will jointly strive for the successful convening of the World People's Peace Congress.
    Asian and Pacific Regions Peace Liaison Committee
    Secretary-General Liu Ningyi
    November 10th
    [Xinhua News Agency] TASS, Tokyo, 6th: The All-Japan Peace Protection Congress was held in Tokyo on October 31 and November 1. The congress was opened by the famous trade unionist, Toshio Tokunaga. Elected to the presidium of the congress were: Ikuo Oyama, Yoshitaro Hirano, Tomi Kora, Shoji Ogata and many other well-known peace activists. Approximately five hundred delegates participated in the congress, including Hoashi Kei of the left-wing Socialist Party, Hisao Ishino, one of the leaders of the Labor-Farmer Party, Jiaoliu Hosokawa, representative of the Communist Party, and representatives of various trade unions, social groups, and democratic organizations.
    Masaharu Hatanaka delivered a report on the Japanese people's struggle for peace. Then Kei Hoashi, Tomi Kora, and representatives of Chinese and Korean residents living in Japan delivered congratulatory speeches. The participants listened to a report on the resolutions of the Asian and Pacific Regions Peace Conference. Reports were given by Ikuo Oyama, Kenjiro Yanagida, Hisao Ishino, Tansoku Saburo, and Yukizo Abe, among others. The congress delegates unanimously supported these reports. Koichi Ideta, a trade unionist, gave a report on organizational issues, suggesting that the Japan Preparatory Committee for the Asian and Pacific Regions Peace Conference be reorganized into the Preparatory Committee for the Japan Peace Liaison Committee. Then Yoshitaro Hirano reported, describing the significance of the Asian and Pacific Regions Peace Conference and the domestic situation in Japan. Michi Kobayashi (transliteration) reported on the issue of implementing the resolutions of the Asian and Pacific Regions Peace Conference. The participants engaged in heated discussions on these reports, and after the discussions, the congress unanimously supported these reports. The congress passed a letter to the Japanese people, a letter to the people of the Asian and Pacific Regions, a letter to the United Nations, a letter to the heads of the five major powers, a letter of protest to the Japanese government, and some other resolutions. The congress proceeded with the active efforts of the delegates. A total of one hundred and twenty delegates made reports and speeches at the meeting.

### 亚洲及太平洋区域和平联络委员会  号召筹备参加世界人民和平大会  全日本拥护和平大会在东京举行

1952-11-11
第4版()
专栏：

    亚洲及太平洋区域和平联络委员会
    号召筹备参加世界人民和平大会
    全日本拥护和平大会在东京举行
    【新华社十日讯】亚洲及太平洋区域和平联络委员会致电亚洲及太平洋区域各国和平委员会，期望各国人民加紧进行筹备参加世界人民和平大会的工作。电文如下：亚洲及太平洋区域各国和平委员会：
    具有重要意义的世界人民和平大会将于十二月十二日在维也纳举行。在十月十二日亚洲及太平洋区域和平会议上，我们全体代表曾一致通过了一项拥护召开世界人民和平大会的决议。我们深信这个大会的召开，在自由和广泛的讨论中，将对世界人民所最关心的缓和国际紧张局势，求得和平合理解决的办法有所贡献。
    现在维也纳和平大会的日期即将到来，为了迎接这个会议，我们热切地期望亚洲、澳洲及太平洋沿岸各国的各界人民，各国和平委员会以及各国的政治、社会、宗教团体一致行动起来，加紧进行各项筹备工作。我们并热切地向你们要求：（一）向本国的男男女女传达召开这个会议的意义并广泛地展开讨论；（二）积极地组成尽可能具有最广泛代表性的代表团，把没有参加和平运动的人士也包括进来，一道去出席这次大会；（三）尽速地把你们的筹备工作情况和代表团组成情况告诉给世界和平理事会和亚洲及太平洋区域和平联络委员会。我们希望我们共同为胜利召开世界人民和平大会而努力。
    亚洲及太平洋区域和平联络委员会
    秘书长  刘宁一
    十一月十日
    【新华社讯】塔斯社东京六日讯：十月三十一日、十一月一日两天，在东京举行了全日本拥护和平大会。大会由著名的工会工作者德永利雄主持开幕。被选入大会主席团的有：大山郁夫、平野义太郎、高良富、尾形昭二和其他著名的和平人士多人。参加大会的约有五百名代表，其中包括左派社会党人帆足计、劳农党领袖之一石野久男、共产党代表细川嘉六以及各工会、社会团体和民主组织的代表。
    畑中政春在会上做了关于日本人民为和平而斗争的报告。然后帆足计、高良富、侨居日本的中国和朝鲜居民的代表等都致了贺词。与会者听取了关于亚洲及太平洋区域和平会议决议的报告。做报告的有大山郁夫、柳田谦十郎、石野久男、淡德三郎、阿部行藏等人。大会代表一致拥护这些报告。工会工作者出田孝一作了有关组织问题的报告，他建议把亚洲及太平洋区域和平会议日本筹备委员会改组为日本和平联络委员会的筹备委员会。接着由平野义太郎报告，他叙述了亚洲及太平洋区域和平会议的意义和日本的国内形势。小林通（译音）就执行亚洲及太平洋区域和平会议的决议的问题作了报告。与会者对这些报告展开了热烈的讨论，讨论后大会一致拥护这些报告。大会通过了告日本人民书、告亚洲及太平洋区域人民书、致联合国书、致五大国元首的信、对日本政府的抗议书和其他一些决议。大会是在代表们积极努力下进行的。在会上作报告和发言的代表共有一百二十人。
