    4. In the past few years, the Party has nurtured me. In 1949, the organization wanted me to serve as the deputy director of the boiler factory. The director was the Soviet expert Comrade Darasov. At that time, I was very timid, thinking that I was just a gas welder, how could I manage a factory! The leading comrade said to me: "It doesn't matter if you don't know how to manage a factory, as long as you learn with humility, there are no difficulties that cannot be overcome."
    5. On the same day, the leading comrade introduced me to Comrade Darasov, the director of the boiler factory. I shook his hand tightly, and Comrade Darasov smiled kindly at me. From then on, we began to work together.
    6. After I arrived at the boiler factory, I first encountered many technical problems that I did not understand. ### 我们怀念着苏联同志（图片）
苗地  赵志方
1952-11-30
第3版()
专栏：

    我们怀念着苏联同志
    苗地  赵志方作
    四、几年来党培养了我。一九四九年组织上要我担任锅炉厂的副厂长，正厂长是苏联专家达拉索夫同志，当时自己很胆怯，认为自己是个瓦斯焊工，怎样能够管理工厂！领导同志对我说：“不会管理工厂不要紧，只要虚心学习，没有克服不了的困难。”
    五、当天领导同志引我见了锅炉厂厂长达拉索夫同志。我紧握着他的手，达拉索夫同志亲切地对我笑着。从此，我们就开始在一起工作了。
    六、我到锅炉厂后，首先在技术上遇到许多不懂的问题，工人和技术员来问我，我都不敢决定。在联合国大会政治委员会十一月二十六日会议上
