Title: People's Democratic Countries Celebrate the October Revolution Day; Soviet-Polish Friendship Month and German-Soviet Friendship Month Inaugurated Respectively
Author:
Date: 1952-11-10
Page: 4
Xinhua News Agency, November 9th: According to TASS, the people of the Mongolian People's Republic, the European People's Democratic countries, and the German Democratic Republic are enthusiastically celebrating the 35th anniversary of the Great October Socialist Revolution.

In Mongolia, the working people celebrate the 35th anniversary of the Great October Socialist Revolution as their greatest holiday. On the 7th, the capital, Ulan Bator, was filled with a festive atmosphere, with flags, slogans, and portraits of the founders of the Soviet state, Lenin and Stalin, as well as the Mongolian people's leaders Sukhe-Bator, Choibalsan, Tsedenbal, and the leaders of the People's Democratic countries, displayed everywhere. Representatives of workers, intellectuals, and herdsmen gathered in Sukhe-Bator Square. The ranks of the Mongolian People's Revolutionary Army were lined up in the square. Attending the rally were Tsedenbal, General Secretary of the Mongolian People's Revolutionary Party and Prime Minister of the Mongolian People's Republic, members of the Politburo of the Central Committee of the Mongolian People's Revolutionary Party, government members, and the ambassadors of the Soviet Union, the People's Republic of China, and the Democratic People's Republic of Korea. Damba, Secretary of the Central Committee of the Mongolian People's Revolutionary Party, delivered a speech to the officers and soldiers of the Mongolian People's Revolutionary Army and the working people. At the end of his speech, he shouted: Long live the great Soviet state! Long live the unbreakable friendship between the Mongolian and Soviet peoples! Long live Comrade Stalin, the leader and teacher of the world's working people! After the meeting, a military parade and a procession of the working people were held. Tens of thousands of people participated in the parade.

The Polish people are celebrating the October Revolution Day with new achievements on the production front. Thousands of celebrations were held across the country on the 7th. In the capital, Warsaw, representatives from all walks of life, led by the President of the Polish People's Republic, Bolesław Bierut, laid wreaths at the cemetery of Soviet soldiers who sacrificed their lives to liberate the city. The Soviet-Polish Friendship Month was solemnly inaugurated on the 8th at the Warsaw Sports Hall. An editorial in the People's Tribune on the 7th stated: The celebration of the 35th anniversary of the Great October Socialist Revolution once again powerfully demonstrates the strength and solidarity of the world's peace, democracy, and socialism, and the Soviet Union.

In Prague, the capital of Czechoslovakia, tens of thousands of working people held a celebration rally on Staroměstské Square on the 7th. Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Foreign Affairs, Viliam Široký, delivered a speech at the rally. He said: The 35th anniversary of the Great October Socialist Revolution is also a festival for the working people of Czechoslovakia. We cannot help but deeply thank the Soviet people, thank them for the victory of the revolution, and thank the Communist Party of the Soviet Union and the great leaders Lenin and Stalin. The meeting adopted a letter from the Czechoslovak people to the Soviet people. This letter will be delivered by the "Messengers of Friendship and Peace" relay team through the states of Czechoslovakia to the Czech-Soviet border and handed over to the representatives of the Soviet Union.

On the 7th, the city of Budapest, the capital of Hungary, was dressed in festive attire, and portraits of revolutionary leaders Lenin and Stalin could be seen everywhere. The working people of the capital flocked to the cemetery of Soviet martyrs to pay their respects and lay wreaths. On this great holiday, the Presidium of the Hungarian People's Republic commended many outstanding workers, engineers, and scientists. Many exhibitions introducing the Soviet Union and the Great October Revolution were held throughout the country.

In Romania, masses of people in cities and industrial centers across the country held parades to celebrate this great holiday. The working people of the capital, Bucharest, held a celebration rally on Stalin Square on the 7th, followed by a grand parade. They were all filled with high political enthusiasm. The parade lasted for more than four hours.

In Bulgaria, the working people of the capital, Sofia, also celebrated the holiday with a grand parade on the 7th. The parade procession passed through September 9th Square and lasted for three hours. The leading group in the parade was the young men and women of the Bulgarian capital. They carried brightly colored banners: "Long live the Great October Socialist Revolution!" and huge silk portraits of the revolutionary leaders. Then, a group of two thousand people singing "The Internationale" walked across the square. The parade contingents from various enterprises carried banners showing their labor achievements. The parade shouted slogans: "Strengthen the fraternal friendship between Bulgaria and the Soviet Union!", "Strive for peace, strive for the victorious completion of the Dimitrov Five-Year Plan and the socialist construction of Bulgaria!" Celebrations were held in Plovdiv, Stalin City, and some other cities in Bulgaria.

In the German Democratic Republic, the working people of Berlin held a grand meeting at the State Opera House on the 7th to celebrate the 35th anniversary of the Great October Socialist Revolution and the inauguration of the German-Soviet Friendship Month. Present at the meeting were: Wilhelm Pieck, President of the German Democratic Republic, and other leaders, I. Semichastnov, Acting Chairman of the Soviet Control Commission in Germany, heads of diplomatic missions from various People's Democratic countries, and members of the Soviet cultural delegation. Prime Minister of the German Democratic Republic, Otto Grotewohl, delivered a lengthy speech at the meeting.

### 各人民民主国家庆祝十月革命节  波苏友好月和德苏友好月分别揭幕

1952-11-10
第4版()
专栏：

    各人民民主国家庆祝十月革命节
    波苏友好月和德苏友好月分别揭幕
    【新华社九日讯】据塔斯社消息：蒙古人民共和国、欧洲人民民主国家和德意志民主共和国的人民热烈庆祝伟大的十月社会主义革命三十五周年。
    在蒙古，劳动人民像自己最大的节日一样庆祝伟大的十月社会主义革命三十五周年。七日，首都乌兰巴托充满节日气象，到处悬挂着旗帜、标语和苏维埃国家的创立者列宁、斯大林的画像和蒙古人民领袖苏赫—巴托尔、乔巴山、泽登巴尔以及人民民主国家领袖的画像。工人、知识分子和牧人的代表都集合在苏赫—巴托尔广场上。广场上排列着蒙古人民革命军的队伍。参加集会的有蒙古人民革命党总书记、蒙古人民共和国总理泽登巴尔和蒙古人民革命党中央委员会政治局委员，政府委员以及苏联、中华人民共和国和朝鲜民主主义人民共和国的大使等。蒙古人民革命党中央委员会书记达姆巴向蒙古人民革命军官兵和劳动人民发表了演说。他在演说的最后高呼：伟大的苏维埃国家万岁！蒙苏两国人民牢不可破的友谊万岁！世界劳动人民的领袖和导师斯大林同志万岁！会后举行阅兵式和劳动人民的游行。参加游行的有数万人。
    波兰人民以生产战线上的新成就庆祝十月革命节。全国各地七日举行了成千的庆祝会。在首都华沙，以波兰人民共和国总统贝鲁特为首的各界代表向解放该城而牺牲的苏军烈士墓献了花圈。波苏友好月八日在华沙体育馆隆重揭幕。人民论坛报七日的社论说：伟大的十月社会主义革命三十五周年的庆祝又一次有力地显示了世界和平、民主及社会主义的力量和苏联的团结一致。
    在捷克斯洛伐克的首都布拉格，数万劳动人民七日在斯大洛梅斯特广场举行了庆祝大会。副总理兼外交部长西罗基在大会上发表了演说。他说：伟大的十月社会主义革命三十五周年纪念日也就是捷克斯洛伐克劳动人民的节日。我们不由得要深深地感谢苏联人民、感谢他们革命的胜利，感谢苏联共产党及伟大的领袖列宁和斯大林。大会通过了捷克斯洛伐克人民给苏联人民的信。这封信将由“友谊与和平的使者”接力跑队经过捷克斯洛伐克各州，把信传到捷苏边境转交给苏联的代表。
    匈牙利首都布达佩斯城七日穿上了节日的盛装，到处都可以看到革命领袖列宁和斯大林的画像。首都的劳动人民纷纷拥往苏军烈士墓致敬并献花圈。在这伟大的节日，匈牙利人民共和国主席团褒奖了许多优秀的工人、工程师和科学家。全国各地举行了许多介绍苏联和伟大的十月革命的展览会。
    在罗马尼亚，全国各城市和工业中心的人民群众为庆祝这个大节日举行了游行。首都布加勒斯特的劳动人民七日在斯大林广场上举行了庆祝大会，会后开始盛大的游行。他们都充满了高涨的政治热情。游行历时四小时余。
    在保加利亚，首都索非亚的劳动人民七日也以盛大的游行庆祝这个节日。游行队伍通过九九广场，历时三小时之久。游行行列的领先队伍是保加利亚首都的青年男女。他们抬着鲜艳的标语牌：“伟大的十月社会主义革命万岁！”和巨幅绸质的革命领袖们的画像。然后是合唱着“国际歌”的两千人队伍走过广场。各企业的游行队伍打着表示自己劳动成就的标语牌。游行队伍喊着口号：“加强保加利亚与苏联之间的兄弟般的友谊！”，“争取和平，争取胜利完成季米特洛夫五年计划和保加利亚的社会主义建设！”保加利亚的普罗夫迪夫、斯大林城和一些其他城市都举行了庆祝会。
    在民主德国，柏林劳动人民七日在国家歌剧院举行盛会，庆祝伟大的十月社会主义革命三十五周年和德苏友好月的揭幕。到会的有：德意志民主共和国总统威廉·皮克及其他首长，苏联对德管制委员会代理主席伊·塞米查斯诺夫等、各人民民主国家外交使团团长和苏联文化代表团人员。德意志民主共和国总理格罗提渥在会上作了长篇演说。
