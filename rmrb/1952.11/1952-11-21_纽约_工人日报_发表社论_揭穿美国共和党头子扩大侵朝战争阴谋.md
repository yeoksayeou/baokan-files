Title: New York "Daily Worker" Publishes Editorial Exposing U.S. Republican Leaders' Conspiracy to Expand the War of Aggression Against Korea
Author:
Date: 1952-11-21
Page: 4
Xinhua News Agency, Prague, November 20th: The New York "Daily Worker" editorial on November 14th stated: "Just a few days after General Eisenhower was elected president on the strength of deceptive 'peace' promises, fear at home and abroad began to increase, believing that the real purpose of his trip to Korea was to formulate plans to expand the war and increase casualties. This fear is based on the open admission of Eisenhower's most loyal supporters."

The editorial cited an article by Bridges, a senior Republican strategist in the U.S. Congress and a senator from New Hampshire. Bridges said in the article: "We are likely to be at war for many years. The president we have elected will be a war president; our economies will continue to be war economies. Our taxes will remain high; our children will be drafted; our responsibilities will be very heavy. To my knowledge, no responsible Republican or Democrat, naval or army general has any plan to ‘end’ this war."

Bridges said: "We cannot end this war unless we win throughout the world." He said: "There is no line of longitude or latitude or national border that allows us to 'sign a truce' and then 'go home'."

The "Daily Worker" editorial then cited remarks made by Martin, a congressman from Massachusetts and another spokesman for the Eisenhower group. In a speech on November 13, Martin demanded that Eisenhower "consult with General MacArthur and use his knowledge."

The editorial then cited a report by Allen in the "New York Post" column on November 12. Allen reported that General Clark at the Tokyo headquarters will ask Eisenhower to agree to use Chiang Kai-shek's Kuomintang troops in Korea. Allen also pointed out that the Joint Chiefs of Staff "have authorized General Clark to submit this plan to Eisenhower."

The "Daily Worker" editorial used the words of the British "Manchester Guardian" as an example to illustrate the fear of Eisenhower's plan among most of the pro-Labor Party newspapers in Britain, and even the Liberal Party newspapers. The "Manchester Guardian" said: "There is currently a very dangerous possibility: The new government will try to win the war by expanding the Korean War beyond the borders of Korea."

The "Daily Worker" editorial said: "It is clear that Eisenhower and the villains around him do not want to give the people what they hoped for when they voted—peace. However, if the people do not passively sit by and watch the plans to expand the war be put into effect, they can now force a decision for peace."

### 纽约“工人日报”发表社论  揭穿美国共和党头子扩大侵朝战争阴谋

1952-11-21
第4版()
专栏：

    纽约“工人日报”发表社论
    揭穿美国共和党头子扩大侵朝战争阴谋
    【新华社布拉格二十日电】纽约“工人日报”十一月十四日社论说：“艾森豪威尔将军靠了骗人的‘和平’诺言当选总统以后没有几天，国内外的恐惧心理就开始加大了，认为他到朝鲜去的真正目的是要制订扩大战争与增加伤亡率的计划。产生这种恐惧心理的根据是艾森豪威尔最忠诚的支持者对这一点的公开承认。”
    社论引证了美国国会共和党高级策士之一、新罕布什尔州参议员勃里奇发表的一篇文章。勃里奇在那篇文章里说：“我们很可能进行许多年战争。我们选出的总统将是一位战争总统；我们的经济将继续是战争经济。我们的赋税仍将很高；我们的孩子们将被征入伍；我们的责任将非常沉重。据我了解，没有一个负责的共和党人或民主党人、海军或陆军将领有任何‘结束’这场战争的计划。”
    勃里奇说：“如果我们不在全世界胜利，我们决不能结束这个战争。”他说：“没有一条经纬线或国界能使我们就此‘签订停战’，然后‘回家’。”
    “工人日报”社论又引证了艾森豪威尔集团的另一发言人、马萨诸塞州众议员马丁的话。马丁在十一月十三日发表的谈话里要求艾森豪威尔“与麦克阿瑟将军磋商，并利用他的知识。”
    社论接着引证了十一月十二日“纽约邮报”专栏中艾伦的报道。艾伦报道说：东京总部的克拉克将军将要求艾森豪威尔答应在朝鲜利用蒋介石的国民党军队。艾伦又指出：三军参谋长“已授权克拉克将军把这个计划提交艾森豪威尔”。
    “工人日报”社论以英国“曼彻斯特卫报”的话为例子，说明英国大部分亲工党报纸，甚至自由党报纸，对艾森豪威尔计划的恐惧。“曼彻斯特卫报”说：“目前有一个非常危险的可能性，那就是：新政府将试图用扩大朝鲜战争到朝鲜境外的办法来打赢战争。”
    “工人日报”社论说：“很清楚，艾森豪威尔和他周围的坏人并不想给人民以他们在投票时所希望的东西——和平。但是，如果人民并不消极地坐视扩大战争的计划付诸实施，他们现在就能强迫作出和平的决定。”
