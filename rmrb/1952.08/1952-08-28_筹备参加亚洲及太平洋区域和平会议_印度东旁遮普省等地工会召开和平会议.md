Title: Preparing to Participate in the Asian and Pacific Regions Peace Conference: Trade Unions in East Punjab Province and Other Regions of India Hold Peace Meetings
Author:
Date: 1952-08-28
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, New Delhi, August 27th: The Indian working class, under the leadership of trade union organizations in various regions, is enthusiastically supporting and preparing to participate in the Asian and Pacific Regions Peace Conference to be held in Beijing.

The East Punjab Province General Trade Union convened the East Punjab Province, Patiala and East Punjab States Union, and Himachal Pradesh Trade Union Peace Conference on August 17th in Ambala, East Punjab Province. Approximately 125 representatives from 17 trade unions belonging to various central trade union organizations participated in this peace conference.

These trade unions have decided to present gifts to the Asian and Pacific Regions Peace Conference to be held in Beijing. Knitwear workers in Ludhiana announced that they would send clothing. Construction workers in Chandigarh announced that they would donate cement-made peace doves. Other trade unions are also preparing to present gifts.

In a resolution, the conference called on workers to unite and strive for world peace. The conference also passed resolutions demanding an immediate end to wars being waged on Asian territories, condemning bacteriological warfare, demanding that the Indian government stop recruiting Gurkha soldiers in Indian territory, expressing support for the liberation struggles of the people of Malaya, Vietnam, and Korea, and demanding the conclusion of a Five-Power Peace Pact to end international tensions.

The conference also decided to elect a delegation of 400 workers to represent the workers of these provinces and attend the All-India Peace Conference scheduled to be held in Jullundur in September.

The well-known trade union leader Chopra said at the meeting that this conference was a manifestation of the rising peace movement of the working class in East Punjab Province, Patiala and East Punjab States Union, and Himachal Pradesh. Kishori Lal, Chairman of the East Punjab Province General Trade Union, and Longba, General Secretary, also delivered speeches at the meeting.

On August 18th, several thousand Hyderabad workers meeting in Mamos to commemorate the sixth anniversary of the founding of the All-Hyderabad General Trade Union passed a congratulatory telegram to the Asian and Pacific Regions Peace Conference. They pledged that Hyderabad workers would join the world working class in fighting for world peace. Moshudin, Chairman of the All-Hyderabad General Trade Union, and General Secretary Gore (who is also a member of the Council of States) both spoke at the meeting.

The West Bengal Province General Trade Union has issued a notice scheduling a meeting of the Standing Committee and trade union representatives on August 23rd to elect representatives to attend the Asian and Pacific Regions Peace Conference.

Trade unions in Bombay are raising funds for the travel expenses of the representatives of these trade unions to attend the Asian and Pacific Regions Peace Conference. The Bombay Printing Workers' Union has decided to send representatives to the Peace Conference.

Xinhua News Agency, New Delhi, August 24th: The Third All-India Peace Conference will be held in Jullundur, East Punjab Province, from September 12th to 14th. Prominent figures dedicated to the cause of peace in India are undertaking extensive visits throughout the country to carry out preparatory work.

Dr. Kitchlew (formerly translated as Saifuddin), Chairman of the All-India Peace Council, after visiting Bihar Province and Uttar Pradesh Province, will visit East Punjab Province on August 30th, where he will stay until September 5th. Accompanying him are Singh, Vice-Chairman of the All-India Peace Council, and Malaviya, a member of the Indian National Congress party and former Prime Minister of Bhopal State. Dr. Kumarappa, a prominent Gandhian economist, will travel to Madhya Bharat and Central Province, and Kalanji, a member of the All-India Peace Council, will travel to Madras in the last week of August. Sundarlal, head of the Indian delegation that visited China last year, has made extensive visits to various regions of Bihar Province. Chandra, Secretary of the Secretariat of the All-India Peace Council, has visited East Punjab Province, Bihar Province, and Uttar Pradesh Province.

### 筹备参加亚洲及太平洋区域和平会议  印度东旁遮普省等地工会召开和平会议

1952-08-28
第4版()
专栏：

    筹备参加亚洲及太平洋区域和平会议
    印度东旁遮普省等地工会召开和平会议
    【新华社新德里二十七日电】印度工人阶级正在各地的工会组织领导下，以极大的热情，积极支持并筹备参加即将在北京召开的亚洲及太平洋区域和平会议。
    东旁遮普省总工会八月十七日在东旁遮普省的翁巴拉，召开了东旁遮普省、巴地阿拉与东旁遮普土邦联盟及喜马偕尔邦工会和平会议。参加这次和平会议的有属于各中央工会组织的十七个工会的代表约一百二十五人。
    这些工会已决定向即将在北京召开的亚洲及太平洋区域和平会议献赠礼物。卢德希那地方的针织工人宣布他们要送衣物。昌的迦地方的建筑工人宣布他们要赠送水泥做的和平鸽。其他工会也正在筹备赠送礼物。
    会议在决议中号召工人团结起来争取世界和平。会议并通过决议，要求立即停止在亚洲领土上进行的战争，谴责细菌战，要求印度政府制止在印度领土上招募廓尔喀兵，声援马来亚、越南与朝鲜人民的解放斗争，并要求缔结五大国和平公约，以结束国际紧张局势。
    会议还决定选出四百名工人组成代表团，代表这些省区的工人，出席定于九月间在朱龙多城举行的全印和平大会。
    著名的工会领袖乔普拉在会议上说，这次会议是东旁遮普省，巴地阿拉与东旁遮普土邦联盟、喜马偕尔邦工人阶级的和平运动高涨的表现。东旁遮普省总工会主席基索里拉尔和总书记龙巴也在会议上发表了演说。
    八月十八日，为纪念全海得拉巴总工会成立六周年在马摩斯开会的数千名海得拉巴工人，通过了给亚洲及太平洋区域和平会议的贺电。他们保证，海得拉巴工人将和世界工人阶级一道为争取世界和平而斗争。全海得拉巴总工会主席莫休丁和总书记（他也是联邦院议员）哥尔德都曾在会上发言。
    西孟加拉省总工会已发出通知定于八月二十三日召开常务委员会和工会代表会议，以推选出席亚洲及太平洋区域和平会议的代表。
    孟买各工会正在筹募这些工会的代表出席亚洲及太平洋区域和平会议的路费。孟买印刷工人工会已决定派遣代表出席和平会议。
    【新华社新德里二十四日电】第三届全印和平大会将于九月十二日至十四日在东旁遮普省朱龙多城举行，印度努力和平事业的著名人士正在全国各地从事广泛访问，进行筹备工作。
    全印和平理事会主席克其鲁（前译赛福丁）博士在访问过比哈尔省和北方省以后，将于八月三十日访问东旁遮普省，在那里停留到九月五日。同行的有全印和平理事会副主席辛格和印度国会国大党议员、前波保尔邦总理马拉维亚。信奉甘地主义的著名经济学家库马拉巴博士将前往中印度邦和中央省，全印和平理事会理事卡朗吉将在八月的最末一周前往马德拉斯。曾于去年访问中国的印度代表团团长森德拉尔曾到比哈尔省各个地区广泛进行访问。全印和平理事会秘书处秘书钱德拉曾访问过东旁遮普省、比哈尔省和北方省。
