Title: Japanese Seamen's Strike Ends in Victory
Author:
Date: 1952-08-23
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, Tokyo, August 22nd: The Japanese seamen's strike, which began on August 7th, has forced the employers to accept a portion of the workers' demands for increased retirement pay. Therefore, the strike was declared victoriously ended on the 20th. According to the agreement reached between labor and management, seamen with more than thirty years of service will receive retirement pay equivalent to thirty-seven months plus twelve days of wages, and retirement pay for seamen with more than twenty-one years of service will also be increased.

From the 7th to the 19th, the seamen held two 48-hour strikes and one 120-hour strike, and had decided to strike for another three days starting on the 20th if the employers still refused to concede. During the strike, more than 400 ships were paralyzed and unable to sail.

### 日本海员工人罢工胜利结束

1952-08-23
第4版()
专栏：

    日本海员工人罢工胜利结束
    【新华社二十二日讯】东京消息：从八月七日开始的日本海员工人罢工，已经迫使资方接受了工人关于增加退职金的要求的一部分。因此，罢工已在二十日宣布胜利结束。根据劳资双方所达成的协议，凡工龄在三十年以上的海员工人将获得相当于三十七个月另十二天的工资的退职金，工龄在二十一年以上的海员工人的退职金也将增加。
    海员工人从七日到十九日，曾经先后举行两次四十八小时的罢工和一次一百二十小时的罢工，并曾决定如果资方仍然拒不让步，将从二十日起再罢工三天。在罢工期间，曾有四百多艘船舶陷于瘫痪，不能开驶。
