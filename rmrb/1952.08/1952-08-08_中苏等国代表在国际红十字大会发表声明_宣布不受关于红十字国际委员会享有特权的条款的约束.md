Title: Representatives of China, the Soviet Union, and Other Countries Issue Statement at International Red Cross Conference Declaring Non-binding to Clauses Granting Privileges to the International Committee of the Red Cross
Author: 
Date: 1952-08-08
Page: 1

Xinhua News Agency, Toronto, July 7th: The International Red Cross Conference continued its sessions on the morning and afternoon of August 5th. At the plenary session of the International Red Cross Conference on the afternoon of August 5th, delegations from the Soviet Union, China, and other People's Democratic countries reiterated their positions on the so-called International Committee of the Red Cross and voted against all clauses granting privileges to the International Committee of the Red Cross when the new charter, rules, and other resolutions were put to a vote. The delegations from the Soviet Union, China, and Romania announced that they were not bound by those clauses and requested that their statements be included in the conference record. The work of the plenary session on the morning of August 5th was to approve the proposals of the conference's youth committee.

### 中苏等国代表在国际红十字大会发表声明  宣布不受关于红十字国际委员会享有特权的条款的约束

1952-08-08
第1版()
专栏：

    中苏等国代表在国际红十字大会发表声明
    宣布不受关于红十字国际委员会享有特权的条款的约束
    【新华社多伦多七日电】国际红十字大会在五日上午和下午继续举行会议。在五日下午的国际红十字大会全体会议上，苏联、中国和其他人民民主国家的代表团重申它们对所谓红十字国际委员会的立场，并在新章程、细则与其他决议提付表决时投票反对其中关于红十字国际委员会享有特权的全部条款。苏联、中国和罗马尼亚代表团宣布它们不受那些条款的约束，并要求把它们的声明列入大会记录。五日上午的全体会议的工作就是通过大会青少年委员会的提案。
