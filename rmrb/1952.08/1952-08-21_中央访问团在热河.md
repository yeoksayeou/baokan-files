    "title": "Central Visiting Delegation in Rehe",
    "author": "Si Rege, Wang Juzheng",
    "date": "1952-08-21",
    "page": "1",
    "translation": "The first sub-delegation of the Central Visiting Delegation to visit ethnic minorities in Northeast China arrived in Rehe on July 27th and spent sixteen days visiting people of all ethnicities in the province. Wherever the delegation went, the Mongolian, Han, Hui, and Korean people, as if celebrating a festival, wore their bright ethnic costumes and played their own ethnic musical instruments, lining the streets to welcome them, regardless of age or gender. They all wanted to see the people sent by Chairman Mao, listen to the words brought by Chairman Mao, and tell Chairman Mao what was in their hearts. To thank Chairman Mao for his concern, Lama representatives presented Hada to the delegation, and Hui compatriots presented white prayer caps. When the delegation arrived on the Ongniud Banner grassland, Jin Yu, a Rehe province learning model, and others who had come to welcome them on tall horses, dismounted and sang and danced, offering their beloved pouches to the delegation. All of this expressed the love of the people of all ethnic groups for Chairman Mao. Listen to a song sung by a young Mongolian singer on the Ongniud Banner grassland:\n\n\tChairman Mao is the spring sun,\n\tHis radiance shines red across the sky!\n\tYou have brought hope to all our ethnic groups.\n\tDay and night, the grasslands echo with your name—\n\tSavior, Chairman Mao.\n\tJoyful, jubilant, our hearts are pounding,\n\tDear comrades,\n\tWe come to welcome you.\n\tGood, Chairman Mao!\n\tGood, Beijing!\n\tAcross mountains and rivers, you come to see us!\n\tWe wish you good health!\n\tI sing a song to welcome you.\n\nDing Zhende, a representative of the Hui people in Chengde City, said, \"Seeing the delegation, I thought of the cruel oppression of the Hui people by the Chiang bandits. I was arrested and imprisoned twice. The People's Liberation Army rescued us. Now I am the master of the new China. I was elected as a member of the Provincial People's Government and vice chairman of the Chengde Municipal People's Representative Conference Consultative Committee.\" His experience is also the experience of the people of all ethnic groups in the province. Today, the Mongolian, Hui, Korean, and other ethnic minorities, who account for about 6% of the province's population, have 9.6% of the representative seats in the People's Congresses of the province, cities, banners, and counties. They are united with the people of the whole province and have truly turned over and become masters of their own affairs.\n\nThe people of all ethnic groups held the hands of the delegation comrades and repeatedly urged them to tell Chairman Mao about their good lives after their liberation. When the herdsmen of Ongniud Banner talked about their current lives, expressions of happiness and pride appeared on their faces. Now the banner's livestock exceeds the highest level under the puppet Manchukuo regime by 29.7%. Among them, cattle, the main source of livelihood, have exceeded the highest level of the puppet Manchukuo period by 50%. On average, each person in the banner has almost eleven head of livestock. Baiyin Menghe, a labor model, now has 327 head of livestock, an average of 36 head per person in his family. Butter and milk curd, which they could not eat in the past, have now become everyday food for the herdsmen. Chairman Mao's trade policy has brought a new look to the lives of the herdsmen. A two-year-old bull, which could only be exchanged for two bricks of tea in the old society, can now be exchanged for seventeen bricks of tea; a medium-sized horse, which could only be exchanged for more than thirty feet of cloth in the past, can now be exchanged for more than five hundred feet of cloth. From August 7th to 11th, at the horse racing and livestock comparison conference held on the Ongniud Banner grassland, flour, satin, woolen cloth, harmonicas, and flashlights, etc., became the best-selling goods. Bu Hejiregala's family in the Fifth Gacha (village) of the Second Nuutuk (district) bought goods worth 2.5 million yuan.\n\nChairman Mao's ethnic policy has illuminated the hearts of the people of all ethnic groups. The disunity among the people of all ethnic groups caused by the instigation of imperialism and the reactionaries in the past has melted away like accumulated snow in spring, leaving no trace.\n\nIn Balibao Village, west of Lingyuan City, live 213 Han households, 78 Hui households, and 23 Mongolian households. In the past, this village had a \"Mountain King\" Wang Zuozhou and a \"Land King\" Zhang Guofu, as well as a Hui landlord Li Jinfu. From the puppet Manchukuo period to the Kuomintang rule, they had been in power, doing whatever they pleased, and deliberately provoking ethnic relations in order to facilitate their rule and oppression of the people. At that time, the Han, Mongolian, and Hui people were like enemies, and they hardly spoke when they met, and even the wells for drinking water were separated. After liberation, Chairman Mao's ethnic policy illuminated Balibao, and the people of all ethnic groups raised their awareness, and the knots were untied. At the request of the masses, the government executed the heinous \"Mountain King\" and \"Land King\" and placed Li Jinfu under control. The masses elected Wang Shulan (a Mongolian) as the village head because she was fair in her work, saying that she \"stood firm and walked straight\"; they elected Ding Shouchang (a Hui), a labor model, as the deputy village head. Since then, the three ethnic groups have been managing the affairs of the village very harmoniously together. This time, when the village elected Ding Shouchang as a representative to meet the delegation, Li Zhenzong, a Hui military dependent, excitedly grabbed Ding Shouchang's hand and said, \"Because of Chairman Mao, I have food and clothing; my eldest son graduated from middle school and became a teacher. Everyone in the village is living a happy life, like a family. This is truly a good world that has never existed before. Please have the delegation send a message to Chairman Mao: I will definitely act according to his words.\""

### 中央访问团在热河
斯热歌  王居正
1952-08-21
第1版()
专栏：承德通讯

    中央访问团在热河
    斯热歌  王居正
    赴东北等地访问各少数民族的中央访问团的第一分团七月二十七日到达热河，历时十六天，访问了省内各族人民。访问团每到一处，蒙、汉、回、朝鲜各族人民像过年节一样，都穿上鲜艳的民族服装，吹奏着本民族的乐器，不分男女老少，排着队夹道欢迎。他们都要看一看毛主席派来的人，听听毛主席捎来的话，还要亲口把自己心里的话告诉给毛主席。为了感谢毛主席的关怀，喇嘛代表向访问团敬献哈达，回族同胞向访问团献白色的礼拜帽。访问团来到翁牛特旗草原上时，骑着高头大马来欢迎的热河省学习模范金玉等，下马就唱歌，跳舞，把心爱的荷包献给访问团。这一些都表示了各族人民对毛主席的热爱。请听翁牛特旗草原上蒙古青年歌手唱的一首歌吧：
    春天的太阳毛泽东，
    光辉四射满天红！
    您给我们各族人民带来了希望。
    草原上昼夜传诵您——
    救星毛泽东。
    愉快、欢腾，心儿在跳动，
    亲爱的同志们，
    我们来欢迎。
    好呀，毛主席
    好呀，北京城
    千山万水来看我们！
    祝您身体健康！
    我唱着歌儿来欢迎。
    承德市回民代表丁振德说：“见到访问团，我想起了蒋匪对回民的残酷压迫。我曾被捕入狱两次。人民解放军救出了我们。现在我是新中国的主人了。我被推选为省人民政府委员、市各界人民代表会议协商委员会副主席。”他的经历，也正是全省各族人民的经历。今天，约占全省人口百分之六的蒙、回、朝鲜等各少数民族人民，在省、市旗县的人民代表中，有了百分之九点六的代表名额。他们和全省人民团结起来，真正翻身作了主人。
    各族人民拉着访问团同志的手，千叮咛万嘱咐，要他们把自己翻身后的好光景说给毛主席。翁牛特旗的牧民提起现在的生活，脸上就露出幸福和骄傲的表情。现在全旗的牲畜已超过伪满最高水平的百分之二十九点七。其中主要生活资料来源的牛，已超过伪满时期的最高水平百分之五十。全旗平均每人差不多有十一头牲畜。劳动模范白音孟和现在已有三百二十七头牲畜，全家每人平均三十六头。过去吃不到的黄油、奶豆腐，现在成了牧民们的家常便饭。毛主席的贸易政策，给牧民生活带来了新的面貌。在旧社会只能换两块砖茶的一头二岁牛，现在能换十七块砖茶；过去只能换三十多尺布的一匹中等马，现在能换五百多尺布。从八月七日到十一日，在翁牛特旗草原举行的赛马比畜大会上，面粉、缎子、大绒、口琴和手电筒等等，都成了最畅销的货物。第二努图克（区）五嘎查（村）布和吉热嘎拉一家，就买了二百五十万元的货物。
    毛主席的民族政策照亮了各族人民的心。过去由于帝国主义和反动派的挑拨离间给各族人民造成的不团结，像春天里的积雪一样，已经溶解得不留痕迹了。
    凌源城西八里堡村，住着二百一十三户汉民、七十八户回民和二十三户蒙民。过去，这村有个“坐山王”王作州和一个“寸土王”张国富，还有个回族地主李进福。从伪满到国民党统治时期，他们一直掌握大权，作威作福，专门挑拨民族关系，以便于他们统治和压迫人民。那时候，汉、蒙、回三族人民就像冤家，走碰头都不大说话，连吃水的井都分家。解放后，毛主席的民族政策照亮了八里堡，各族人民提高了觉悟，疙疸解开了。政府根据群众要求，枪毙了罪大恶极的“坐山王”和“寸土王”，管制了李进福。群众选举了办事公正的王淑兰（蒙民）当村长，说她“脚跟站得稳，脚步走得正”；选举了劳动模范丁守昌（回民）当副村长。从此，三族人民非常融洽地共同管理着村里的大事。这次村里选丁守昌当代表去见访问团，回民军属李振宗兴奋地抓住丁守昌的手说：“有了毛主席，我才有了吃，有了穿；大儿子在中学毕了业，当了教员。全村人日子都过得顺心，像一家人一样。这真是亘古以来没有过的好世道。你让访问团给毛主席捎个话：我一定要照着他老人家的话办事。”
