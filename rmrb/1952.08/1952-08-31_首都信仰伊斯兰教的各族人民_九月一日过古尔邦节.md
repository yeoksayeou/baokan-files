Title: People of All Ethnic Groups in the Capital Who Believe in Islam Celebrate the Corban Festival on September 1st
Author:
Date: 1952-08-31
Page: 1

    People of All Ethnic Groups in the Capital Who Believe in Islam
    Celebrate the Corban Festival on September 1st
    [Dispatch from this newspaper] September 1st (Lunar July 13th) is the Corban Festival, or Feast of Sacrifice, for people of all ethnic groups who believe in Islam. On this day, people of all ethnic groups who believe in Islam will slaughter cattle and sheep to celebrate the festival. The Beijing Municipal People's Government, in accordance with the Government Administration Council's two circulars—one on exempting slaughter taxes on cattle and sheep consumed during the three major festivals of ethnic groups who believe in Islam, and the other on unified national holidays and commemorative days—has decided to exempt this slaughter tax. It also stipulates that all ethnic compatriots who believe in Islam in Beijing's government agencies, organizations, schools, public security forces, public and private enterprises, etc., will have a day off on September 1st to celebrate the Corban Festival.
    Note: The Corban Festival, Eid al-Fitr, and the Mawlid al-Nabi are the three major festivals for people of all ethnic groups who believe in Islam.

### 首都信仰伊斯兰教的各族人民  九月一日过古尔邦节

1952-08-31
第1版()
专栏：

    首都信仰伊斯兰教的各族人民
    九月一日过古尔邦节
    【本报讯】九月一日（夏历七月十三日）为信仰伊斯兰教各族人民的古尔邦节，即宰牲节。在这一天，信仰伊斯兰教的各族人民将屠宰牛羊过节。北京市人民政府遵照政务院关于信仰伊斯兰教各族人民三大节日食用牛羊免征屠宰税和关于统一全国年节及纪念日放假办法两项通令的规定，决定免征此项屠宰税，并规定北京市各机关、团体、学校、公安部队、公私营企业等信仰伊斯兰教的各族同胞在九月一日均休假一天，以便欢度古尔邦节。
    按：古尔邦节、尔代节和圣祭节是信仰伊斯兰教各族人民一年中的三大节日。
