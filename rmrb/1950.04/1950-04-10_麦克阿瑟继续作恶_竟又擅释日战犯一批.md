Title: MacArthur Continues Evil Deeds, Arbitrarily Releases Another Group of Japanese War Criminals
Author:
Date: 1950-04-10
Page: 4

　　MacArthur Continues Evil Deeds
    Arbitrarily Releases Another Group of Japanese War Criminals
    [Xinhua News Agency, April 9th] According to a United Press report from Tokyo: Another twenty-four Japanese war criminals, who had been sentenced by Dutch military tribunals in Indonesia and transferred to Sugamo Prison in Japan in January of this year, were arbitrarily released by MacArthur on March 31st. As a result of MacArthur's arbitrary and successive releases of a large number of Japanese war criminals sentenced by the Dutch Military Commission, only one remains imprisoned in Sugamo Prison. According to an announcement by MacArthur's headquarters on February 16th, a total of 864 Japanese war criminals were transferred from Indonesia to Japan in January. In mid-February, MacArthur had released approximately 100 of these war criminals at one time.

### 麦克阿瑟继续作恶  竟又擅释日战犯一批

1950-04-10
第4版()
专栏：

　　麦克阿瑟继续作恶
    竟又擅释日战犯一批
    【新华社九日讯】据合众社东京讯：被荷兰军法机关在印尼判决而于今年一月移禁日本巢鸭监狱的日本战犯，又有二十四名于三月三十一日被麦克阿瑟擅自释放。为荷兰军事委员会判决的大批日本战犯，经麦克阿瑟擅自陆续释放的结果，现在尚囚在巢鸭监狱的仅剩一名。按据麦克阿瑟总部二月十六日宣布：一月间自印尼移禁于日本的日本战犯共有八百六十四名。二月中旬，麦克阿瑟曾一次释放该项战犯约一百名之多。
