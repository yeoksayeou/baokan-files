Title: Pakistan Holds Peace Conference, Passes Resolution Demanding Ban on Atomic Weapons and Severance of Relations with Anglo-American Bloc
Author:
Date: 1950-04-20
Page: 4
Xinhua News Agency, Karachi, 18th (TASS dispatch): The "Pakistan Times" reports: The first All-Pakistan Peace Conference was held in Okara, West Punjab. The conference was organized by progressive writers in Pakistan and was attended by representatives of workers, peasants, and student groups from Lahore, Karachi, Lyallpur, Gujrat, and other cities. The conference unanimously passed a resolution demanding a ban on atomic weapons and demanding that Pakistan sever relations with the Anglo-American bloc; withdraw from the British Commonwealth; dismiss British personnel from the Pakistan Army; recall all Pakistani personnel in the Malayan Army hired and paid for by the British, and greatly reduce Pakistan's military budget.

The conference also elected an organizing committee composed of Faiz, the editor of the "Pakistan Times," Afzal, the General Secretary of the Pakistan Trade Union Federation, and local writer Chati. The purpose of the committee is to mobilize the Pakistani people to fight for peace. The "Pakistan Times" further pointed out: Landlords, agents of feudal forces, and local authorities in Okara attempted to disrupt the conference. They beat and robbed conference delegates and attempted to rob the bookstore belonging to the Progressive Writers Association. The newspaper strongly stated: "All attempts by mobs, instigated by government authorities, to disrupt the conference have failed."

### 巴基斯坦举行和平大会  通过决议要求禁用原子武器并要求与英美集团断绝关系

1950-04-20
第4版()
专栏：

　　巴基斯坦举行和平大会
    通过决议要求禁用原子武器并要求与英美集团断绝关系
    【新华社十九日讯】塔斯社喀喇蚩十八日讯：《巴基斯坦时报》消息：全巴基斯坦首次和平大会在西旁遮普省的奥卡拉举行。大会系由巴基斯坦的进步作家所组织，到有拉合尔、喀喇蚩、莱里浦尔、格查拉特、以及其他城市的工人、农民、学生团体的代表。大会一致通过要求禁止原子武器的决议，并要求巴基斯坦和英美集团断绝关系；退出不列颠联邦；辞退巴基斯坦陆军中的英国人；召回所有马来亚陆军中由英国出钱雇用的巴基斯坦人以及大大减少巴基斯坦的军事预算等。
    大会并选出组织委员会，由《巴基斯坦时报》编辑费兹、巴基斯坦工会联合会总书记阿夫萨尔与当地作家蔡迪组成。委员会的目的在于动员巴基斯坦人民为争取和平而斗争。《巴基斯坦时报》进一步指出：奥卡拉的地主、封建势力的代理人以及地方当局企图破坏大会。他们殴伤和抢劫与会代表，并企图抢劫属于进步作家协会的书店。该报力称：“受政府当局怂恿破坏大会的暴徒们的一切企图，都已失败”。
