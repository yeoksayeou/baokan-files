Title: Pakistani People Demand Freedom from British Imperial Shackles
Author:
Date: 1950-04-09
Page: 4

　　Xinhua News Agency, Karachi, 7th. The Pakistan Times reports that on "Petition Day," many worker, peasant, and student organizations in Rawalpindi held numerous meetings and mass rallies. Workers, students, and writers demanded that the Pakistani government break away from the British Empire and sever relations with the Anglo-American bloc. They also demanded recognition of the government of the Democratic Republic of Vietnam, an end to financial and military aid to Burma, the abolition of Pakistan's reactionary laws, increased wages, the implementation of compulsory education, the revision of land tenancy systems, and the nationalization of major industries. The nationwide "Petition Day" was initiated by the Pakistan Trade Union Federation. The Federation called on all oppressed people of Pakistan to establish a broad democratic front to fight for occupational, economic, and democratic demands.

### 巴基斯坦人民要求脱离英帝束缚

1950-04-09
第4版()
专栏：

　　巴基斯坦人民要求脱离英帝束缚
    【新华社八日讯】塔斯社喀喇蚩七日讯：巴基斯坦时报消息，在“请愿日”这天，拉瓦尔品梯的许多工人、农民和学生组织举行许多次会议和群众大会。工人、学生和作家要求巴基斯坦政府脱离不列颠帝国，并和英美集团断绝关系。他们还要求承认越南民主共和国政府，停止对缅甸的财政和军事的援助，废止巴基斯坦的反动法律，增加工资，实行义务教育，修改土地租赁制度和将主要工业收归国有。按全国“请愿日”是巴基斯坦总工会发起的。总工会号召巴基斯坦的所有被压迫人民，建立广泛的民主战线，为职业、经济和民主的要求而斗争。
