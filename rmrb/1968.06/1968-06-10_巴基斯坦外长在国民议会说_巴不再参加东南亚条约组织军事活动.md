Title: Pakistani Foreign Minister Says Pakistan Will No Longer Participate in SEATO Military Activities
Author:
Date: 1968-06-10
Edition: 6

Pakistani Foreign Minister Ashad Hussein said here on the 6th that Pakistan will no longer participate in the military activities of the Southeast Asia Treaty Organization and the Central Treaty Organization, and is gradually withdrawing from these military treaty organizations.

Ashad Hussein said in the National Assembly while answering questions, "With the passage of time and changes in the world situation, these treaty organizations are no longer necessary. It is for this reason that Pakistan is gradually withdrawing from them. Pakistan no longer participates in their military activities, and in fact, attends ministerial-level meetings as an observer."

Someone asked whether Pakistan intends to continue to maintain the Pakistan-U.S. Mutual Defense Treaty. Ashad Hussein replied, "Given that the United States announced a new policy of providing weapons to the Indian subcontinent in April 1967, the 1954 Pakistan-U.S. Mutual Defense Treaty has no meaning."

When talking about Sino-Pakistani relations, he said, "Sino-Pakistani relations are very good and will continue to be so."

### 巴基斯坦外长在国民议会说  巴不再参加东南亚条约组织军事活动

1968-06-10
第6版()
专栏：

    巴基斯坦外长在国民议会说
    巴不再参加东南亚条约组织军事活动
    新华社拉瓦尔品第七日电　巴基斯坦外长阿沙德·侯赛因六日在这里发表讲话说，巴基斯坦不再参加东南亚条约组织和中央条约组织的军事活动，并且在渐渐地脱离这些军事条约组织。
    阿沙德·侯赛因在国民议会回答问题时说，“随着时间的推移和世界政局的变化，这些条约组织大可不必存在。正是由于这个原因，巴基斯坦在渐渐地脱离它们。巴基斯坦不再参加它们的军事活动，而且实际上是以观察员的身份出席部长级会议。”
    有人问，巴基斯坦是否打算继续保持巴美共同防御条约。阿沙德·侯赛因回答说，“鉴于美国在一九六七年四月宣布了对印巴次大陆奉行新的（提供）武器的政策，巴美一九五四年共同防御条约就没有什么意义了。”
    他在谈到中巴关系时说，“中巴关系非常好，并将继续保持下去。”
