Title: Reactions to the American Small Satellite
Author:
Date: 1958-02-04
Page: 5

　　Reactions to the American Small Satellite
　　The American satellite is just a small pill compared to the Soviet Union's second satellite. But now that they've accomplished something, the Americans have no reason to delay the highest-level talks.
　　　　　　——British "Daily Mail"
　　The successful launch of an American satellite does not indicate a change in the balance of political power. If the United States tries to use this as a political means of attacking its opponents and continues to escalate international tensions, it is destined to fail.
　　　　　　——Indonesian "Eastern Star"
　　The American success has not become a sensational event, because the road to world space has been opened by the Russians. The weight of each Russian satellite is fundamentally incomparable to the weight of the satellites launched by their American counterparts.
　　　　　　——French "Le Monde"
　　Despite the launch of an American satellite, the United States is still far behind the Soviet Union in this regard. There is no doubt that it will take several years for the Americans – if they are very focused on this goal – to fully catch up with the Russian level.
　　　　　　——West German rocket expert Eugen Sänger
　　Compared to the Soviet Union's second satellite, America's first satellite is too small and may look like a toy.
　　　　　　——Japanese "Asahi Shimbun"

### 对美国小卫星的反应

1958-02-04
第5版()
专栏：

　　对美国小卫星的反应
    美国的人造卫星同苏联第二颗人造卫星比起来只是一颗小药丸。但是既然已经做出一点名堂来了，美国人现在就没有理由拖延最高级会谈了。
　　        　　——英国“每日邮报”
    美国发射一颗人造卫星成功，并不显示政治力量对比的变化。如果美国试图利用这个来作为攻击它的对手的政治手段，并且继续加剧国际紧张局势的话，它是注定要失败的。
　　　　    　　——印度尼西亚“东星报”
    美国人的这次成功并没有成为轰动一时事件，因为通向世界空间的道路已由俄国人开辟了。俄国人的每一颗卫星的重量是他们的美国对手发射的卫星的重量所根本不能相比的。
                ——法国“世界报”
    尽管美国发射了一个人造卫星，美国在这方面仍旧大大落后于苏联。毫无疑问，需要若干年，美国人——假如他们非常专心于这个目标的话——才能完全赶上俄国人的水平。
    　　　　　　  ——西德火箭专家欧伊根·戈格
    和苏联第二个人造卫星相比，美国的第一个人造卫星太小，看来可能像个玩具。
             　　 ——日本“朝日新闻”
