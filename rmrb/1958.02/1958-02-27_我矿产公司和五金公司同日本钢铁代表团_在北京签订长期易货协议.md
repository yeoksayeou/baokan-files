Title: My Minerals Company and Metals Company Sign Long-Term Barter Agreement with Japanese Steel Delegation in Beijing
Author:
Date: 1958-02-27
Page: 4
Xinhua News Agency, 26th.

    My Minerals Company and China National Metals Import Corporation signed a "Long-Term Barter Agreement" and a "1958 Barter Protocol" with the Japanese Steel Delegation in Beijing this morning. The agreement and protocol stipulate that, based on the principle of equality, mutual benefit, and balanced barter, both parties agree that China's export commodities will be mainly mineral products such as iron sand and coal, and Japan's export commodities will be various types of steel. The total export value for each party will be £100 million over the five years from 1958 to 1962, with £10 million for 1958.
    Shang Guangwen, Manager of China National Minerals Corporation, Li Zhuozhi, Manager of China National Metals Import Corporation, and Yoshihiro Inayama, Head of the Japanese Steel Delegation, signed the agreement and protocol on behalf of their respective parties.
    In his speech after the signing ceremony, Shang Guangwen said that the signing of the agreement and protocol is a major event in the development of Sino-Japanese trade relations. It is not only conducive to the economic development and prosperity of the two countries, but also creates a good start for establishing a long-term and stable mutual supply relationship between the two countries, thereby further expanding trade between the two countries.
    Yoshihiro Inayama also delivered a speech at the signing ceremony, saying that the signing of the agreement and protocol is an important milestone in Sino-Japanese trade relations, the purpose of which is for the industrial development and peace of the two countries. He also stated in his speech that after returning to his country, the delegation would make its best efforts to ensure the complete realization of the agreement and protocol.
    The Chinese side participating in the signing ceremony included Nan Hanchen, Chairman of the China Council for the Promotion of International Trade, Vice-Chairmen Lei Renmin and Li Zhuchen, as well as Li Dequan, Liao Chengzhi, Hou Debang, and many others.
    In addition to all members of the Japanese Steel Delegation, the Japanese side participating in the signing ceremony included Kiichi Katsumada and Kanichi Kawakami, standing directors of the Diet Members' League for Promotion of Japan-China Trade, Kumaichi Yamamoto, Chairman of the Japan International Trade Promotion Association, Saburo Nango, Chairman of the Japan-China Export-Import Association, representatives of the Japanese Trade Mission, Kazutsura Kawase, Deputy Chairman of the Japan-China Export-Import Association and Representative Director of Toko Bussan Co., Ltd., Kazuo Suzuki, Managing Director of the Japan-China Trade Association, and Koichi Saionji, a Japanese peace activist in Beijing, and many others.
    After the signing ceremony, Yoshihiro Inayama, head of the Japanese Steel Delegation, deputy heads Masayasu Nakajima and Yoshio Shimizu, and some members of the delegation left Beijing by plane this morning to return to their country. Some members of the delegation will remain in China for visits.

### 我矿产公司和五金公司同日本钢铁代表团  在北京签订长期易货协议

1958-02-27
第4版()
专栏：

    我矿产公司和五金公司同日本钢铁代表团
    在北京签订长期易货协议
    新华社26日讯　中国矿产公司和中国五金进口公司，今天早晨同日本钢铁代表团在北京签订了1958年到1962年的“长期易货协议”和“1958年易货议定书”。协议和议定书规定，根据平等互利易货平衡的原则，双方同意中国出口商品是以铁砂和煤为主的矿产品，日本出口商品是各种钢材，在1958年到1962年的五年内，每方出口金额共为一亿英镑，1958年为一千万英镑。
    中国矿产公司经理商广文、中国五金进口公司经理李琢之和日本钢铁代表团团长稻山嘉宽，分别代表双方在协议和议定书上签字。
    商广文在签字后的讲话中说，协议和议定书的签订是中日贸易关系发展中的一件大事，它不仅有利于两国经济的发展和繁荣，并且为两国间建立长期而稳定的相互供应关系，从而进一步扩大两国贸易，创造了一个良好的开端。
    稻山嘉宽也在签字仪式上发表讲话说，协议和议定书的签订，是日中贸易关系上的一个重要的里程碑，其目的是为了两国的工业发展与和平。他在讲话中并且表示，代表团回国后要尽最大的努力保证协议和议定书的完满实现。
    中国方面参加签字仪式的，有中国国际贸易促进委员会主席南汉宸，副主席雷任民和李烛尘，还有李德全、廖承志和侯德榜等多人。
    日本方面参加签字仪式的除日本钢铁代表团的全体人员外，还有日本国会议员促进日中贸易联盟常任理事胜间田清一、川上贯一，日本国际贸易促进协会会长山本熊一，日中输出入组合理事长南乡三郎等日本通商使节团的代表，日中输出入组合副理事长、东工物产公司代表董事川濑一贯，日本中日贸易会专务理事铃木一雄和在北京的日本和平人士西园寺公一等多人。
    签字仪式结束后，日本钢铁代表团团长稻山嘉宽，副团长中岛正保、清水芳夫和部分团员已在今天早晨乘飞机离开北京回国。代表团部分人员还将留在中国进行参观访问。
