Title: We will definitely catch up with England!
Author:
Date: 1958-02-01
Page: 3

Editor's note: Can we catch up with and surpass England in the output of major industrial products in the next fifteen years? This is a question of great concern to everyone. Here we publish some statistics on seven major industrial products for your reference when studying this issue. (In these statistics, the development speed of China and the United Kingdom after 1958 is entirely based on the statistics of previous years. Of course, China's future industrial development speed will definitely be more than this, and the United Kingdom may not be able to maintain this speed for every product.) (Attached table)
　　
　　Steel
　　China  United Kingdom
　　1952: 1.35 million tons
　　1957 (estimated): 5.24 million tons
　　1941: 12.51 million tons
　　1956: 20.99 million tons
　　Average annual growth rate from 1952 to 1957: 31.2%
　　Average annual growth rate from 1941 to 1956: 3.5%
　　Annual average growth rate required for China to catch up with the UK's 1972 production level: 13.8% (assuming the UK can guarantee an annual growth rate of 3.5%)
