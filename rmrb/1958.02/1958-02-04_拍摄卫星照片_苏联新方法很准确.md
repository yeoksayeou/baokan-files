Title: Taking Satellite Photos - New Soviet Method is Very Accurate
Author:
Date: 1958-02-04
Page: 6

　　Taking Satellite Photos
    Soviet New Method is Very Accurate
    Xinhua News Agency, Alamaty, 3rd dispatch: According to TASS Alamaty news: The Astrophysical Institute of the Kazakh Academy of Sciences is using a new method to photograph artificial earth satellites.
    A high-mountain observatory of this institute, located near Alamaty, is successfully using this new method. Scientists at the observatory have installed a swinging plate on a giant Maksutov telescope, which blocks the satellite's trace on the photographic plate at regular intervals. These time intervals are recorded with high accuracy by an oscilloscope. Using this device, scientists at the observatory took three clear photos at the end of January of the second artificial earth satellite passing over Alamaty. Based on these photos, it is possible to determine the observed position of the satellite with high accuracy (error of less than one-thousandth of a degree), as well as the exact time of the satellite's passage (accuracy of a few thousandths of a second).

### 拍摄卫星照片  苏联新方法很准确

1958-02-04
第6版()
专栏：

　　拍摄卫星照片
    苏联新方法很准确
    新华社3日讯　据塔斯社阿拉木图讯：哈萨克科学院天体物理学研究所使用了一种新的方法来拍摄人造地球卫星的照片。
    这个研究所的一座位于阿拉木图附近的高山天文台正成功地运用着这种新方法。天文台的科学家们在一架马克苏托夫式的巨大天文望远镜上装上一块摆动的板，它每隔一定时间就遮断卫星在感光板上留下的形迹。这些时间间隔就会照样地被有高度准确性的示波器纪录下来。利用这一装置，天文台的科学家在1月底拍摄了三张第二颗人造地球卫星经过阿拉木图上空时的清晰照片。根据这些照片，可能高度准确地测定所看见的卫星位置（误差不到千分之一度），以及卫星经过的准确时间（精确度达千分之几秒）。
