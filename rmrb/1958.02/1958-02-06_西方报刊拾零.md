　　Western Press Clippings

　　　Tiptoeing Around
　　If an American plane carrying a hydrogen bomb crashes in Britain, people and animals within several square miles of the area must be evacuated until the radioactive contamination on the ground is cleared. Medical workers are happy to answer questions about precautions to take in the event of a plane crash.
　　　　　　——“British Medical Weekly”

　　The Consequences of “Prosperity”
　　Today in the United States, seventeen million people live in dilapidated, dirty, rat-infested, uninhabitable houses, lacking adequate light, heating, and running water.
　　Economic prosperity is the main reason.
　　　　　　——American “Fortune” Magazine

　　Hard to Deal With
　　In New York, we may not need to deal directly with the Communists, but we must note that the number of people opposing those who oppose the Communists is constantly increasing.
　　　　　　——“American Political Herald”

### 西方报刊拾零

1958-02-06
第6版()
专栏：西方报刊拾零

　　西方报刊拾零
    提心吊胆
    如果携带氢弹的美国飞机在英国坠毁，在地面所受的放射性污染清除之前，这个地区数平方英里以内的人畜必须撤离。医务工作者乐于解答有关一旦发生飞机坠毁事件时采取预防措施的问题。
              ——“英国医学周刊”
    “繁荣”的后果
    今天美国有一千七百万人住在破烂、肮脏和老鼠成群、不堪居住的房屋里，光线不足，缺乏保暖和自来水设备。
    经济繁荣是最主要的原因。
              ——美国“幸福”杂志
    不好对付
    在纽约，我们可能不须要直接对付共产党人，但是我们必须注意到，反对反对共产党的人数正在不断增加。
            ——“美国先驱政治家报”
