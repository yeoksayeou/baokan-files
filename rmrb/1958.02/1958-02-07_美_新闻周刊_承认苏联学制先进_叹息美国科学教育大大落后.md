Title: U.S. "Newsweek" Admits Soviet Education System is Advanced, Laments U.S. Science Education is Far Behind
Author:
Date: 1958-02-07
Page: 6

　　Xinhua News Agency, New York, February 6th: A recent article in the American magazine "Newsweek" admitted that the United States is far behind the Soviet Union in science education.

　　"Newsweek," discussing the superiority of Soviet schools, said: "Nearly 50% of the textbooks in the Soviet ten-year school system are physics, chemistry, biology, astronomy, and mathematics, while the proportion of these subjects in American primary and secondary school textbooks is less than 25%. All Russian middle school students study algebra and geometry, while only 67% of American middle school students study algebra, let alone geometry."

　　According to the magazine, "The Soviet Union trains 150,000 scientists and engineers every year, while the United States trains only 70,000." The magazine said that in a recent NBC television program, a high school girl from Texas said: A scientist is "a funny little guy who spends all day in a dark room. He is a bachelor because no girl wants to marry someone who spends his life on things that make people itch or stink."

### 美“新闻周刊”承认苏联学制先进  叹息美国科学教育大大落后

1958-02-07
第6版()
专栏：

　　美“新闻周刊”承认苏联学制先进
    叹息美国科学教育大大落后
    据新华社6日讯　纽约消息：美国“新闻周刊”最近刊载的一篇文章承认，美国在科学教育方面大大落在苏联后面。
    “新闻周刊”谈到苏联学校的优越性说：“苏联十年制学校教材中几乎有50％是物理、化学、生物学、天文学和数学，美国中小学教材在这方面的比例不到25％。俄国的所有中学生都学习代数和几何，美国只有67％的中学生学习代数，几何更不用说了。”
    据这家杂志说：“苏联每年训练十五万名科学家和工程师，美国每年只训练七万名。”杂志说，在全国广播公司最近一次电视节目中，得克萨斯州的一个中学女生说：科学家是“一个滑稽的小家伙，整天关在黑屋里。他是一个单身汉，因为没有女孩子愿意嫁给一个把自己的一生花在使人发痒的东西或者有臭味的东西上的人”。
