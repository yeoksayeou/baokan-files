Title: Traveling in the Caucasus Mountains
Author: Cheng Guangrui
Date: 1958-02-22
Page: 8

    Traveling in the Caucasus Mountains
    Cheng Guangrui
    The morning mist has dissipated, the rosy clouds have risen,
    Beneath the blue sky, the Caucasus peaks shimmer,
    We advance in the valleys shaded by green trees,
    Like sailing on a vast, undulating sea.
    The sound of springs never ceases, wave after wave,
    The green mountains never tire the eye, range upon range,
    I am intoxicated, not because I tasted your fine grape wine,
    Georgia, your green hills and clear waters have drawn me into a dream.
    Night has come, the mountains are hidden,
    The roadside bushes become clusters of black shadows,
    Caucasus night, how tranquil,
    Only the moon accompanies us on our journey.
    The moonlight illuminates the road like a shining little river,
    We are rushing along the final voyage on this river,
    The lights in the farmhouses ahead are still not extinguished,
    As if waiting for guests who visit late at night.
            En route to Maharadze

### 高加索山中行
程光锐
1958-02-22
第8版()
专栏：

    高加索山中行
    程光锐
    晨雾消散了，朝霞升起了，
    蓝天下闪跃着高加索的群峰，
    我们在绿树掩映的山谷中前进，
    像在碧波起伏的大海上航行。
    听不完的泉声一阵又一阵，
    看不厌的青山一重又一重，
    我沉醉了，不是因为尝了你的葡萄美酒，
    格鲁吉亚，你的青山绿水把我引入梦中。
    黑夜来临了，群山隐没了，
    路旁的树丛变成一簇簇黑影，
    高加索之夜啊，多么静谧，
    只有月亮伴随着我们前行。
    月光把公路照耀得像发亮的小河，
    我们在这河上赶着最后的航程，
    前面农庄里灯光依然未熄，
    仿佛在等待着深夜来访的客人。
            马哈拉泽途中
