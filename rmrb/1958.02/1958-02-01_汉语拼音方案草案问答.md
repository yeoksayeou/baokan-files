　　Questions and Answers on the Draft of the Chinese Phonetic Alphabet Scheme
　　Ye Laishi
    7. How are the names of the letters determined?
    The names of the twenty-six Chinese phonetic alphabet letters are determined based on the following three principles: first, their pronunciation is used as the basis for their names; second, letters with similar pronunciations are made as easy to distinguish as possible in terms of name; and finally, international conventions are appropriately considered when determining the names of the letters.
    The five vowel letters a, e, i, o, u, use their pronunciations as their names because the pronunciation of vowel letters is generally sonorous. Most consonant letters add a vowel after their pronunciation to serve as their name: b, c, d, g, k, n, p, t, v, z are followed by ê (i.e., 注音字母ㄝ); h, w, y are followed by a; j is followed by ie; q is followed by iu; x is followed by i. There are also five consonant letters that add a vowel before their pronunciation to serve as their name: f, l, m, s are preceded by ê; r is preceded by a. The name of j is “街” (jie); the name of q is “丘” (qiu): these are close to the traditional names of these two letters in China and also consistent with their pronunciation. The pronunciations of y and i, w and u are the same, but they are clearly distinguished in terms of name: y is called "呀" (ya), i is called "衣" (yi); w is called "娃" (wa), u is called "乌" (wu). When determining the names of f, l, m, r, s, the customary names of these letters internationally were considered, and their current names are not unfamiliar to us. We have handled l and n differently: the name of l adds a vowel before the pronunciation, and the name of n adds a vowel after the pronunciation; because these two sounds are easily confused in some Chinese dialects, it is necessary to have a clear distinction in the name.
    Of course, in some special occasions, certain letters may have flexible pronunciations, not subject to the pronunciations and names prescribed by the phonetic scheme. For example, X in "X-ray" and x, y in mathematical symbols can still be read as "爱克斯" (ài kè sī) and "娃衣" (wá yī). There are similar situations in Russian. For example, in Russian, X is pronounced h (喝), and Y is pronounced u (乌), but in mathematics, they are read as iks and ipsilon.
    8. How should the alphabet be recited?
    For the convenience of recitation, the twenty-six letters are listed in four rows: the first and second rows each have seven letters, and the third and fourth rows each have six letters. When we recite the alphabet, we should divide it into four sentences, that is, two sentences of seven characters and two sentences of six characters. After reciting ABCD in the first sentence, pause slightly, and then recite EFG; after reciting HIJK in the second sentence, pause slightly, and then recite LMN; after reciting OPQ in the third sentence, pause slightly, and then recite RST; after reciting UVW in the fourth sentence, pause slightly, and then recite XYZ. That is, it should be recited in a pattern of four-three, four-three, three-three, three-three, so that each sentence rhymes, making it extremely easy to remember.
    If the names of these twenty-six letters are recited according to the following Chinese characters, it may be more convenient for northerners:
    啊 (A), 拜 (B), 猜 (C), 歹 (D), 厄 (E), 哀夫 (F), 该 (G);
    哈 (H), 衣 (I), 街 (J), 开 (K), 哀而 (L), 哀姆 (M), 乃 (N);
    喔 (O), 配 (P), 丘 (Q), 啊而 (R), 哀思 (S), 态 (T);
    乌 (U), 维 (V), 娃 (W), 希 (X), 呀 (Y), 再 (Z).
    If people from Shanghai recite this alphabet, they can recite it according to the following Chinese characters:
    啊 (A), 碑 (B), 猜 (C), 歹 (D), 厄 (E), 哀夫 (F), 该 (G);

　　叶籁士
    7、字母的名称是怎样规定的？
    二十六个汉语拼音字母的名称，是根据以下三个原则规定的：首先以它的读音作为名称的基础；其次读音相近的字母尽可能做到在名称上容易区别；最后规定字母名称的时候适当考虑国际习惯。
    五个母音字母a，e，i，o，u，是用它们的读音作为它们的名称：因为母音字母的读音一般都是响亮的。大多数子音字母，在它们的读音后面加上母音，作为它们的名称：b，c，d，g，k，n，p，t，v，z后面加ê（即注音字母ㄝ）；h，w，y后面加a；j后面加ie；q后面加iu；x后面加i。另有五个子音字母，在它们的读音前面加上母音，作为它们的名称；f，l，m，s前面加ê；r前面加a。j的名称作“街”（jie）；q的名称作“丘”（qiu）：跟这两个字母在我国的传统名称接近，同时又符合它们的读音。y和i，w和u读音相同，但在名称上分得清楚：y叫“呀”，i叫“衣”；w叫“娃”，u叫“乌”。f，l，m，r，s的名称，在规定时考虑了这些字母在国际上的习惯称呼，它们现在的名称对我们并不生疏。对于l和n我们作了不同的处理：l的名称是在读音前面加母音，n的名称是在读音后面加母音；因为这两个音在我国好些方言区容易混淆，因此在名称上有明确区分的必要。
    当然，在一些特殊的场合，某些字母可以有变通的读法，不受拼音方案规定的读音和名称的约束。例如“X光”中的X，数学符号中的x，y，可以仍旧读作“爱克斯”和“娃衣”。俄语中也有类似情况，例如在俄语中X念h（喝），Y念u（乌），但在数学中却读作iks和ipsilon。
    8、字母表应该怎样念？
    为了念起来方便，二十六个字母分列四行：第一行和第二行各有七个字母，第三行和第四行各有六个字母。我们念字母表时，要分成四句来念，即两句七言，两句六言。第一句念完ABCD，稍顿，再念EFG；第二句念完HIJK，稍顿，再念LMN；第三句念完OPQ，稍顿，再念RST；第四句念完UVW，稍顿，再念XYZ。就是要念成四——三，四——三，三——三，三——三的格局，这样句句押韵，极便记忆。
    这二十六个字母的名称，如果按照下面的汉字来念，对于北方人也许更方便：
    啊（A）、拜（B）、猜（C）、歹（D），厄（E）、哀夫（F）、该（G）；
    哈（H）、衣（I）、街（J）、开（K），哀而（L）、哀姆（M）、乃（N）；
    喔（O）、配（P）、丘（Q），啊而（R）、哀思（S）、态（T）；
    乌（U）、维（V）、娃（W），希（X）、呀（Y）、再（Z）。
    如果上海人来念这个字母表的时候，可以按照下面的汉字来念：
    啊（A）、碑（B）、猜（C）、歹（D），厄（E）、哀夫（F）、该（G）；
