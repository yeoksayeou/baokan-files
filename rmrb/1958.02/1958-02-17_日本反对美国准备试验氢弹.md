Title: Japan Opposes U.S. Preparation for Hydrogen Bomb Test
Author:
Date: 1958-02-17
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, Tokyo, 16th. Japanese Foreign Ministry spokesman expressed regret on the 15th over the United States' preparations to conduct hydrogen bomb tests on Eniwetok Atoll in the Pacific.

The spokesman said, in reference to the test plan announced by the U.S. Atomic Energy Commission, that the Japanese government would once again lodge a protest with the U.S. government, demanding that the hydrogen bomb tests be stopped, and would reserve the right to demand compensation for any losses suffered by Japanese people as a result of such tests.

The U.S. Atomic Energy Commission announced on the 14th that the United States would conduct hydrogen bomb tests on Eniwetok Atoll from April to August, and would designate 390,000 square nautical miles of international waters surrounding Eniwetok Atoll as a "danger zone."

The Japan Council Against Atomic and Hydrogen Bombs has announced March 1st as a "Joint Action Day" to oppose U.S. hydrogen bomb tests, calling for joint action by Asian and African countries.

### 日本反对美国准备试验氢弹

1958-02-17
第5版()
专栏：

    日本反对美国准备试验氢弹
    新华社16日讯　东京消息：日本外务省发言人15日对美国准备在太平洋恩纳威托克岛进行氢弹试验表示遗憾。
    发言人在谈到美国原子能委员会宣布的试验计划时说，日本政府将再一次向美国政府提出抗议，要求停止氢弹试验，并且保留就日本人可能因为这种试验而遭受到的损失要求赔偿的权利。
    美国原子能委员会14日宣布，从4月到8月期间，美国将在恩纳威托克岛进行氢弹试验，并且划定恩纳威托克岛周围三十九万平方海里的公海为“危险地区”。
    日本禁止原子弹和氢弹协议会已经宣布3月1日为反对美国氢弹试验的“共同行动日”，要求亚非国家采取联合行动。
