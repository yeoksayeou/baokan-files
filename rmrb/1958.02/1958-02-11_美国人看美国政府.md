Americans Look at the American Government

On January 20, the American "Washington Post and Times Herald" published an article by Pearson comparing Eisenhower's 1952 campaign promises with the current state of the United States:

	Budget Deficit
	"We will stop wasting your money and prevent the recurrence of national deficits by making every effort to curb inflation." (October 17, 1952, Wilmington, Delaware) Inflation is getting worse and worse, and the value of the currency is falling more and more.
	Agricultural Product Prices
	"The Republican Party guarantees to maintain 90% farm product price supports, or even more price supports to give farmers 100% parity." (October 4, 1952, Brookings, South Dakota)
	Recently, Mr. Eisenhower gave Congress a message requesting that farm product price supports be reduced to 60%.
	Unemployment
	"Never again allow the evil of mass unemployment to befall our people." (October 20, 1952, Worcester, Massachusetts)
	The number of unemployed is now close to four million.
	Education
	"On the serious problem of adequate education, we will set about helping states short of money to build schools." (October 9, 1952, Los Angeles, California)
	No school construction bill has been passed in five years. Last summer, the White House didn't even bother to call Congress to ask them to pass the bill.
	Housing
	"We must have better housing for those Americans who are now forced to live in slums or uninhabitable houses and unsanitary environments." (October 27, 1952, Pittsburgh)
	Last year, Ike criticized Congress for passing a $177 million urban slum clearance housing plan.
	Tax Cuts
	"Taxpayers will understand our direction. They will know that we will implement a balanced budget, reduce the national debt, and reduce taxes." (September 20, 1952, St. Louis)
	Taxes have not been lowered, and the President has indicated that the budget cannot be balanced, and the national debt will increase.
	Land Reclamation
	"In developing reservoirs to reclaim land in the West, I will recommend to Congress that public funds be fully used to construct necessary projects anywhere." (November 2, 1952)
	America's largest reservoir, "Hell's Canyon," has been rejected by the White House, and the government has exerted enormous pressure on Congress to prevent the implementation of this plan.

### 美国人看美国政府

1958-02-11
第7版()
专栏：

    美国人看美国政府
    1月20日，美国“华盛顿邮报和时代先驱报”刊载了皮尔逊一篇文章，将艾森豪威尔在1952年的竞选诺言和今天美国的现状作了比较：
    预算赤字
    “我们要停止乱花你们的钱，并且以竭力压缩通货的办法来防止国家赤字再出现。”（1952年10月17日在特拉华州威尔明顿）通货膨胀日益严重，币值越来越下跌。
    农业品价格
    “共和党保证维持90％的农产品价格补贴，甚至更多的价格补贴来使农民得到百分之百的平价。”（1952年10月4日南达科他州布洛金斯）
    最近艾森豪威尔先生给国会一项咨文，要求减少农产品补贴价格为60％。
    失业
    “永远不许大规模失业的罪恶重新降临我国人民的身上。”（1952年10月20日在马萨诸塞州禾尔奢斯特）
    失业人数现在接近四百万。
    教育
    “在适当的教育的这个严重问题上，我们将着手帮助缺钱的各州修建学校”。（1952年10月9日在加利福尼亚州洛杉矶）
    五年中从来没有通过一件修建学校的法案。去年夏天，白宫甚至连给国会通一次电话要求通过法案也不干。
    住宅
    “我们必须要有更好的住宅，供给那些现在被迫居住在贫民窟或不宜居住的房屋以及环境卫生恶劣的美国人。”（1952年10月27日在匹兹堡）
    去年，艾克批评国会通过一亿七千七百万美元的清理城市贫民窟的住宅计划。
    减税
    “纳税人会明白我们的方向。他们会知道我们将要实行平衡预算、减少国债和减轻捐税。”（1952年9月20日在圣路易斯）
    税并没有降低，总统已表明不能平衡预算，而国债将有所增加。
    垦荒
    “在发展水库开垦西部土地方面，我将向国会建议充分使用公款建筑任何地方必需的工程”。（1952年11月2日）
    美国最大的水库“地狱峡谷”已被白宫否定，同时政府还对国会施加巨大压力来阻止实行这项计划。
