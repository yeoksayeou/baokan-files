Title: Embroidered Phoenix
Author: Fang Qing
Date: 1958-02-11
Page: 8

    Embroidered Phoenix
    Fang Qing
    The breeze sways your long braids,
    The evening glow reflects on your round face,
    You sit on the haystack, head bowed;
    Girl, are you perhaps thinking of your hometown?
    No—your eyes reveal excitement,
    A sweet mood hangs on the corner of your mouth,
    Your nimble hands thread the needle;
    Girl, are you sewing clothes for your beloved?
    Neither thinking of hometown,
    Nor sewing clothes;
    Taking advantage of today being Sunday,
    Embroidering a colorful phoenix on a snow-white pillowcase.
    Going to the fields under the stars, returning stepping on dewdrops,
    Bean-sized sweat drops fall on the plow handle,
    You don't even bother to prick the blisters on your feet;
    Girl, where do you find the leisure to embroider a phoenix?
    Building a wormwood grass shed is your house,
    Spreading a layer of bulrush grass is your bed,
    Green grass grows on the damp ground;
    Where will you put your beautiful pillow?
    Not afraid of blisters on your feet,
    Not minding the wormwood shed as a home;
    A fiery life is beckoning us,
    Don't you have a beautiful ideal?

### 绣凤凰
方青
1958-02-11
第8版()
专栏：

    绣凤凰
    方青
    微风摇晃着你长长的发辫，
    晚霞映红了你圆圆的脸庞，
    你坐在草堆上低着头儿；
    姑娘呀，莫非想起了家乡？
    不是——你的眼睛流露着兴奋，
    甜??的心情挂在嘴角上，
    你灵巧的双手在穿针引线；
    姑娘呀，难道是给心爱的人缝衣裳？
    一不是想家乡，
    二不是缝衣裳；
    趁着今天是星期日，
    雪白的枕头套上绣个五彩的凤凰。
    顶着星星下地，踩着露水回来，
    豆大的汗珠儿掉在犁柄上，
    脚上的血泡你都顾不得挑破呦；
    姑娘，你哪有闲情绣凤凰？
    搭个蒿草窝棚就是你的房，
    铺一层三棱子草当你的床，
    潮湿的地上长着青草；
    漂亮的枕头你往哪里放？
    不怕脚上起血泡，
    不嫌窝棚当住房；
    火炭儿般的日子正向我们招手，
    难道你就没有个美好的理想？
