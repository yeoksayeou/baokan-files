Title: Czechoslovak Government Issues Statement Before August 21
Author:
Date: 1989-08-18
Edition: 3

The Czechoslovak government issued a statement on August 17, declaring its determination to stop anti-socialist groups from exploiting the anniversary of August 21, 1968, to create tension and violence. The statement emphasized the government's commitment to maintaining law and order.

The statement said that actions aimed at creating tension and violence "objectively oppose reform, democratization, and socialism."

August 21, 1968, marks the day when Soviet troops invaded Czechoslovakia.

The Czechoslovak Federal Ministry of the Interior also announced on August 17 that authorities in Bratislava, the capital of Slovakia, are pursuing criminal charges against four citizens for incitement and disrupting the republic.

### “八·二一”之前捷克斯洛伐克政府发表声明  决心制止利用纪念日制造紧张

1989-08-18
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    “八·二一”之前捷克斯洛伐克政府发表声明
    决心制止利用纪念日制造紧张
    新华社布拉格8月17日电　捷克斯洛伐克政府17日发表一项声明，宣布“决心制止反社会主义集团利用1968年8月21日周年纪念日制造紧张和行施暴力”，“确保法制、安宁和秩序”。
    这项声明说，这种制造紧张和行施暴力的行动，“在客观上是反对改革、民主化和社会主义的”。
    1968年8月21日是苏联出兵捷克斯洛伐克的日子。
    捷联邦内务部17日还宣布，在斯洛伐克首府布拉迪斯拉发的公安部门正以煽动罪和破坏共和国罪对4名公民进行刑事追究。
