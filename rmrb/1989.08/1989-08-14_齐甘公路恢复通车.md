Title: The Qi-Gan Highway Resumes Traffic
Author: Wén Dá
Date: 1989-08-14
Edition: 2

The Qi-Gan Highway, which was washed out by the Nenjiang River flood, resumed traffic at 8:00 AM on August 13.

This highway is a major transportation route from western Heilongjiang Province to Inner Mongolia and the Soviet Union, with a daily traffic volume of 15,000 vehicles.

On July 31, the Qi-Gan Highway was severed in three places by a major flood of the Nenjiang River, totaling 283 meters in length and depths exceeding 2 meters. The daily direct economic loss due to the disruption of traffic on this highway amounted to over 2 million yuan. Starting from 14:00 on August 5, the Qiqihaer Municipal Party Committee and Municipal Government organized 500 soldiers and officers of the People's Liberation Army, 1,260 militia members, and 303 motor vehicles to repair the highway day and night.

### 齐甘公路恢复通车
闻达
1989-08-14
第2版(国内新闻)
专栏：四面八方

    齐甘公路恢复通车
    新华社哈尔滨8月14日电　（记者闻达）被嫩江洪水冲断的齐甘公路，8月13日上午8时正式恢复通车。
    这条公路是黑龙江省西部地区通往内蒙古、苏联的交通干道，日通车量达1.5万辆。
    今年7月31日，齐甘公路被嫩江特大洪水冲断3个口子总长283米，断口深度在2米以上。每天因这条公路中断交通而造成的直接经济损失达200多万元。从8月5日14时开始，齐齐哈尔市委、市政府组织500名解放军指战员、1260名民兵以及303辆机动车，日夜抢修公路。
