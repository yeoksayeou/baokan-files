Title: Humans First Observe Neptune's Mysteries via Probe
Author: Cang Lide
Date: 1989-08-25
Edition: 3

    Humans First Observe Neptune's Mysteries via Probe
    Voyager 2 Completes Close Encounter with Neptune

    BEIJING, Aug. 25 (Xinhua) -- Reporter Cang Lide reports from Washington: The U.S. Voyager 2 space probe, after a 12-year journey covering approximately 7.2 billion kilometers, reached its closest point to the planet Neptune, about 4,800 kilometers from the sun, at 11:56 a.m. (Eastern Standard Time) on Aug. 24. Voyager 2 transmitted the latest images and data of Neptune to Earth, discovering two additional moons of Neptune. As Voyager 2 flew through Neptune's cloudy atmosphere at a speed of 60,000 kilometers per hour, ground stations received radio signals with a "sand-like" sound caused by friction with dust particles. About four hours later, Voyager 2, using Neptune's gravitational pull, flew past its moon Triton and captured close-up images of its volcanic craters.

    This marks the first time in scientific history that humans have observed Neptune's mysteries at such a close distance using a probe. Scientists will need several years to complete the analysis of the new data.

    Due to the 8-hour round-trip communication delay between Earth and Voyager 2, operators at the control center in Pasadena, California, sent the final set of computer commands in the afternoon of Aug. 24 to ensure the probe's rendezvous with Neptune. The live footage seen by American viewers early this morning was actually transmitted by Voyager 2 from approximately 4.5 billion kilometers away, four hours and six minutes earlier.

    After successfully flying past Neptune, Voyager 2's primary mission has been completed.

    (Accompanying photograph)

    The Jet Propulsion Laboratory in Pasadena, California, released a photograph of Neptune taken by the Voyager 2 space probe on Aug. 24. The bright spot in the middle right of the photograph is a cloud cluster. (Xinhua photo via fax)

### 人类首次通过探测器观察海王星奥秘  “旅行者二”号完成对海王星近距探测
仓立德
1989-08-25
第3版(国际新闻·体育)
专栏：

    人类首次通过探测器观察海王星奥秘
    “旅行者二”号完成对海王星近距探测
    本报华盛顿8月25日电　记者仓立德报道：美国“旅行者2”号太空探测器在飞行12载、行程约72亿公里，于24日（美国东部标准时间）11时56分按期抵达距太阳系行星海王星约4800多公里的最近点。“旅行者2”号向地球传输了海王星的最新影像及其他资料，新发现了海王星的另外两颗卫星。当“旅行者2”号以每小时6万公里的速度穿过海王星周围的云雾时，地面接收到的无线电信号发出“沙沙”的与尘埃颗粒摩擦的声音。约4小时后，“旅行者2”号靠海王星引力掠过其卫星“海神”，并拍摄了其表面的火山口等近距图像。
    这是科技史上人类首次通过探测器在这样近的距离观察海王星的奥秘。科学家将用数年时间才能完成对新资料的研究。
    由于地面与“旅行者2”号之间的联系往返一次约需8小时，加利福尼亚州帕萨迪纳控制中心的操纵人员早在昨日下午便发出最后一组电脑指令，使这个探测器与海王星会合。美国观众今日凌晨看到的这一历史性实况镜头，实际是“旅行者2”号4小时零6分前用电视摄像机从距地球约45亿公里处传送回来的。
    “旅行者2”号成功飞越海王星后，主要使命已告结束。
