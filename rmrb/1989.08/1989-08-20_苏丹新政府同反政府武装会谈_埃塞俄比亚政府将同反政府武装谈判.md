Title: Sudan's New Government Holds Talks with Rebel Forces
Author: Chen Jinjun
Date: 1989-08-20
Edition: 3

	Sudan's New Government Holds Talks with Rebel Forces
	Ethiopian Government to Negotiate with Rebel Forces

	Xinhua News Agency, Addis Ababa, August 19 — (Reporter Chen Jinjun) The new government of Sudan and the rebel Sudan People's Liberation Army held their first contact here this afternoon to discuss peace in the country.

	Mohamed Amin Khalifa, head of the Sudanese government delegation, said before the secret talks began that Khartoum considers the war in southern Sudan to be the country's primary problem. The ultimate resolution of the civil war requires the establishment of a stable situation.

	He hopes that both sides will extend the ceasefire period and carry out relief operations in southern Sudan, stop hostile propaganda, and create an atmosphere of mutual trust and serious dialogue.

	Riam Akol, head of the Sudan People's Liberation Army delegation, said that the Sudan People's Liberation Army is participating in the negotiations with an open mind to listen to the government's plans for saving the country.

	He assured that the Sudan People's Liberation Army's commitment to peace is clear and important. He said that the Sudan People's Liberation Army will do everything possible to seek a just peace to end the suffering of the Sudanese people.

	Xinhua News Agency, Addis Ababa, August 18 — The Ethiopian government has accepted the invitation of former U.S. President Jimmy Carter to send representatives to Atlanta, Georgia, in the United States on September 7 to hold preliminary talks with representatives of the Ethiopian rebel forces.

	Ethiopian Foreign Minister Berhanu Bayeh announced the government's decision today, saying that next month's talks will focus on procedural issues related to substantive peace negotiations in Ethiopia, such as the selection of observers, the location of the talks, and other preparatory work.

	Carter made this proposal during his visit to Ethiopia at the end of July. Previously, the Ethiopian government had proposed a "6-point peace initiative" in early July to end the 28-year-long domestic conflict, calling for unconditional talks with any rebel representatives under the participation of observers.

	Berhanu said that the government side has done a lot of preparatory work for the preliminary talks with the rebel representatives.

### 苏丹新政府同反政府武装会谈  埃塞俄比亚政府将同反政府武装谈判
陈进军
1989-08-20
第3版(国际新闻·体育)
专栏：

    苏丹新政府同反政府武装会谈
    埃塞俄比亚政府将同反政府武装谈判
    新华社亚的斯亚贝巴8月19日电　（记者陈进军）苏丹新政府同反政府的苏丹人民解放军今天下午在这里首次进行接触，就这个国家的和平问题进行谈判。
    苏丹政府代表团团长穆罕默德·阿明·哈利法在举行秘密会谈前说，喀土穆认为，苏丹南部的战争是这个国家面临的首要问题。最终解决内战需要以建立稳定的局势为基础。
    他希望双方将延长停火期限和在苏丹南部的救济活动，停止敌对宣传，以建立促进彼此信任和认真对话的气氛。
    苏丹人民解放军代表团团长拉姆·阿科尔说，苏丹人民解放军是以不抱成见的听取政府关于解救这个国家的和平计划的态度参加谈判的。
    他作出保证，苏丹人民解放军对和平的承诺是毫不含糊的和重要的。他说，苏丹人民解放军将尽一切努力，寻求公正的和平，以结束苏丹人民蒙受的痛苦。
    新华社亚的斯亚贝巴8月18日电　埃塞俄比亚政府已接受美国前总统吉米·卡特的邀请，将于今年9月7日派代表前往美国亚特兰大市同埃反政府武装代表进行预备性会谈。
    埃塞俄比亚外交部长伯哈努·巴耶赫今天在宣布政府的这项决定时说，下个月的会谈将集中讨论有关埃塞俄比亚实质性和平谈判的程序问题，如观察员的选择、谈判地点及其他准备工作的问题。
    卡特是在7月底访问埃塞俄比亚时提出这一建议的。在此以前，埃塞俄比亚政府在7月初曾提出了一个关于结束持续了28年之久的国内冲突的“6点和平建议”，要求在观察员的参与下同任何反政府武装代表举行无条件的和谈。
    伯哈努说，政府方面已经为同反政府武装代表进行预备会谈做了大量的准备工作。
