Title: The Prospects of Ocean Development Are Enticing
Author: Lu Jichuan
Date: 1989-08-13
Edition: 7

Today, the vast ocean, with its rolling waves, is no longer an untouched virgin land. It is gradually being conquered to benefit humanity.

The July issue of the American magazine "Discover" reports that by the mid-1990s, a power plant measuring 390 feet in length and weighing 2.5 million tons will float near the coast of Indonesia, stably supplying 100 megawatts of electricity to the shore. The energy source for this power plant will not be mineral fuels or nuclear fusion heat, but rather the temperature difference in seawater. The inventor, 80-year-old engineer Hilbert Anderson, has agreed to design four offshore solar power plants for Indonesia. For 26 years, he has been dedicated to designing the utilization of seawater thermal energy for power generation, and his success has garnered widespread attention.

This signifies that ocean energy has the potential to be further utilized and developed, and it may even become a crucial energy source in the next century.

It is known that the total area of the world's oceans accounts for more than 70% of the global surface, making it an immense and inexhaustible energy reservoir. People can harness the waves on the sea surface, the temperature difference in seawater (ocean thermal energy), the rise and fall of tides (tidal energy), and other phenomena to generate enormous amounts of energy. Anderson's design for Indonesia's offshore power plant utilizes the principle of seawater temperature difference for power generation. According to research data, if the water temperature in the global tropical oceans drops by one degree Celsius, it can release 1200 billion kilowatts of energy. This is roughly equivalent to twice the total electricity generated worldwide in the late 1970s. Countries like Japan and China are investing in the research and utilization of this thermal energy. The United States has decided to develop a 10-100 megawatt seawater temperature difference power generation device by the year 2000. To date, there are several tidal power stations around the world with annual power generation capacities of several hundred billion kilowatt-hours. Developed countries such as the Soviet Union, Japan, and Western Europe place great importance on the utilization of tidal energy. By the end of 1986, the Soviet Union's 400-kilowatt tidal power station in Kislaya Kuba had been operating continuously, and a 15-million-kilowatt industrial tidal power station was under construction on the Kola Peninsula. Additionally, the Soviet Union is building three more tidal power stations with a combined capacity of over 100 million kilowatts along the coast of the Sea of Okhotsk.

It is clear that ocean energy holds immense economic significance. Moreover, given the global climate trend, in the coming years, the rise in global temperatures due to the greenhouse effect will likely compel countries worldwide to limit the use of mineral fuels to reduce or eliminate carbon dioxide emissions (a major cause of the greenhouse effect). The effective and pollution-free ocean energy is undoubtedly highly attractive.

### 开发海洋前景诱人
卢继传
1989-08-13
第7版(国际专页)
专栏：科学漫话

    开发海洋前景诱人
    卢继传
    今天，波浪涛涛的海洋已不再是未开垦的处女地，它正在逐步被征服，以造福于人类。
    美国《发现》月刊今年7月号报道，到90年代中期，将有一座长390英尺、重2.5万吨的发电厂漂浮在印度尼西亚海岸附近，稳定地向岸上输送100兆瓦的电力。这座发电厂所用的能源将不是矿物燃料，也不是核聚变热能，而是海水的温差。这位发明人希尔伯特·安德森是一位80岁的工程师。他答应为印度尼西亚设计4座海上太阳能发电厂。26年来，他一直在进行利用海水热能发电的苦心设计，所赢得的成功受到人们的普遍关注。
    这意味着海洋能源有可能进一步得到利用、开发，甚至可能成为下个世纪的重要能源。
    人们知道，世界海洋总面积占全球面积的70％以上，是一个无比浩大的、用之不尽的能源库，人们可以利用海面上的波浪、海水温差（海洋热能）、海水涨落（潮汐）等生产巨大能源。安德森为印度尼西亚设计的海上发电厂就是利用海水温差原理来发电的。据研究资料，全球热带海洋水温只要下降摄氏1度，即可释放出1200亿千瓦的能量。这大约相当于70年代末全世界新生产电能的2倍。日本和中国等国家都在为这项热能的利用和研究进行投资。美国已决定在2000年前研制10——100万千瓦的海水温差发电装置。迄今，世界已有几座年发电量几百亿度的潮汐电站。苏联、日本和西欧等发达国家十分重视利用潮汐能源。1986年末，苏联在克斯拉伊·库巴的400千瓦潮汐发电站一直在运行，还在科拉半岛上建造一座装机容量为1500万千瓦的工业潮汐发电站。苏联又正在鄂霍次克海沿岸再建造装机容量为1亿多千瓦的3座潮汐发电站。
    不言而喻，海洋能源具有巨大的经济意义，同时，根据世界气候发展趋势，在今后几年里，由于温室效应造成的全球气温上升，不难预料这将迫使世界各国限制矿物燃料的使用，以减少或消除二氧化碳的排放（造成温室效应的最大原因之一），而这种有效的无污染的海洋能源显然具有强大的吸引力。
