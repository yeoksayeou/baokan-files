Title: UN Secretary-General's Special Representative Expresses Satisfaction with Namibia's Independence Process
Author:
Date: 1989-08-05
Page: 3

Xinhua News Agency, Windhoek, August 4th.

The United Nations Secretary-General's Special Representative for Namibia, Martti Ahtisaari, today expressed satisfaction with the progress of Namibia's independence process, despite some unresolved issues.

He told the local press that the voter registration process is proceeding smoothly, with 70% of voters already registered. However, the registration deadline, originally set for mid-November, may be extended.

He said that in the coming weeks, he will discuss with the South African administrator in Namibia two draft declarations regarding elections and the establishment of a constituent assembly. He said that according to the plan, during the transitional period, the responsibility for maintaining law and order in Namibia will primarily fall to the existing South West Africa Police. The South African administrator in Namibia will ensure that the performance of this police force meets United Nations requirements.

Ahtisaari also mentioned that he will work to resolve the issue of the South African riot police, which has been disbanded and integrated into the South West Africa Police.

### 联合国秘书长特别代表说  对纳独立进程表示满意

1989-08-05
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    联合国秘书长特别代表说
    对纳独立进程表示满意
    新华社温得和克8月4日电　联合国秘书长负责纳米比亚问题的特别代表马尔蒂·阿赫蒂萨里今天表示，尽管还有一些悬而未决的问题，但他对纳米比亚独立进程感到满意。
    他向这里的新闻界说，选民登记工作进展顺利，到目前已有70％的选民登了记。但是原定于11月中旬结束的登记限期可能延长。
    他说，在未来的几周，他将同南非驻纳米比亚行政长官讨论有关选举和组成立宪议会的两个宣言草案。他说，根据计划，在过渡时期，维护纳米比亚法律和秩序的责任主要由现有的西南非洲警察承担。南非驻纳米比亚行政长官将保证该警察部队的表现符合联合国的要求。
    阿赫蒂萨里还说，他将努力寻求解决南非防暴警察部队的问题。这支部队已被解散，编入西南非洲警察部队。
