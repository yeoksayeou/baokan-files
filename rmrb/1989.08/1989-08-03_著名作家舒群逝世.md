Title: Renowned Writer Shuqun Passes Away
Author:
Date: 1989-08-03
Edition: 2

Renowned writer, consultant to the Chinese Writers Association, member of the Sixth and Seventh National Committees of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference, and outstanding member of the Communist Party of China, Shuqun, passed away in Beijing on August 2, 1989, due to illness. He was 76 years old.

Shuqun joined the Party in 1932 and began literary creation activities as a member of the "Left League" in 1935. He went to Yan'an in 1937. He served as the director of the Literature Department of the Lu Xun Academy of Arts, director of the Northeast Film Studio, deputy secretary-general of the Chinese Federation of Literary and Art Circles, secretary-general of the Chinese Writers Association, and deputy secretary of the Anshan Iron and Steel Company Party Committee, among other positions.

Shuqun's major works include the short story "Children Without a Homeland" written in 1936, the novel "This Generation" from the 1950s depicting the lives of steelworkers, and the "Shuqun Collected Works in Four Volumes," among others.

### 著名作家舒群逝世

1989-08-03
第2版(国内新闻)
专栏：

    著名作家舒群逝世
    新华社北京8月3日电　著名作家、中国作家协会顾问、全国政协第六、第七届委员会委员、中国共产党优秀党员舒群同志，因病医治无效，于1989年8月2日在北京逝世，终年76岁。
    舒群同志1932年入党，1935年参加“左联”并从事文学创作活动，1937年到延安。曾任鲁艺文学系主任、东北电影制片厂厂长、中国文联副秘书长、中国作协秘书长、鞍钢党委副书记等职。
    舒群主要作品有写于1936年的短篇小说《没有祖国的孩子》、50年代描绘钢铁工人生活的长篇小说《这一代人》以及《舒群文集四卷》等。
