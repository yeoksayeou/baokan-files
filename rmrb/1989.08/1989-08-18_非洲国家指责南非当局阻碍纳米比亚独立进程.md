Title: African Countries Accuse South African Authorities of Hindering Namibia's Independence Process
Author: Liu Kai Chen
Date: 1989-08-18
Edition: 3

The United Nations Security Council held a meeting today to discuss the "situation in Namibia."

During the meeting, representatives from African countries accused South Africa of not adhering to UN Resolution 435, thereby obstructing Namibia's independence process.

This meeting was convened at the request of the African Group and the Non-Aligned Movement.

The African representatives mainly criticized South Africa for plotting to sabotage free and fair elections in Namibia.

Egyptian representative Abdel-Badeel pointed out that if the obstacles created by South Africa for free and fair elections in Namibia are not removed, the future independence of Namibia will be "unreliable," and the consequences will be unpredictable.

Zambian representative Peter Juse said that South Africa's obstruction of free and fair elections in Namibia is an attempt to select some individuals to form a government in Namibia, thereby maintaining South Africa's control over Namibia.

### 非洲国家指责南非当局阻碍纳米比亚独立进程
刘开宸
1989-08-18
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    非洲国家指责南非当局阻碍纳米比亚独立进程
    本报联合国8月16日电  记者刘开宸报道：联合国安理会今天开会，审议“纳米比亚形势”问题。
  会上，非洲国家代表指责南非没有遵照联合国435号决议行事，阻碍了纳米比亚独立进程。
    这次会议是应非洲国家集团和不结盟国家的要求而召开的。
    非洲国家代表们的发言，主要是指责南非阴谋破坏在纳米比亚进行自由和公正选举。
    埃及代表阿卜杜勒·巴达维指出，如果南非为纳米比亚进行自由公正的选举制造的障碍不消除，将来的纳米比亚独立是“靠不住的”，其后果难测。
    赞比亚代表彼得·朱泽说，南非阻碍纳米比亚进行自由和公正的选举，是打算由它挑选一些人在纳米比亚组成一个政府，以保持南非对纳米比亚的控制。
