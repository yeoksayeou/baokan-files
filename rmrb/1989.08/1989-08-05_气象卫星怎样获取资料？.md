Title: How Do Meteorological Satellites Obtain Data?
Author: Zhū Huìmíng
Date: 1989-08-05
Page: 8

Over the past 20 years, more than 100 meteorological satellites launched by various countries have sent back a wealth of valuable data about the Earth's weather and non-weather phenomena. Many people wonder, how do meteorological satellites obtain data about the Earth?

It turns out that meteorological satellites orbit the Earth at a distance of several hundred kilometers, making it impossible to use cameras to capture detailed images of the Earth's surface. However, any object above absolute zero (-273.16°C) can emit, reflect, and absorb electromagnetic waves. Visible light, infrared rays, and sound waves are all types of electromagnetic waves with specific wavelengths. Different objects (such as clouds, vegetation, seawater, etc.) emit electromagnetic waves of different wavelengths, with varying directionality and penetrability. By leveraging this characteristic, scientists have installed instruments on satellites that can remotely sense the electromagnetic waves emitted by various objects on the Earth's surface (including the atmosphere). These instruments convert the electromagnetic waves into electrical signals, which are then digitized and transmitted to ground stations, similar to television signals (but with higher frequencies). Computers then process and identify these signals using established mathematical and physical models, and with the help of computer image processing, optics, color science, and other specialized fields, they compile the data into various required images. Therefore, the information from meteorological satellites can be used not only for cloud analysis, disaster weather analysis, and forecasting but also for applications such as forest fire monitoring, mapmaking, vegetation distribution analysis, crop yield forecasting, fishery forecasting, and marine data investigation and research.

(July 20, 1989, *Southern Daily*, article by Zhū Huìmíng, excerpted by Lín Qíchéng)

### 气象卫星怎样获取资料？
朱惠明
1989-08-05
第8版(每周文摘)
专栏：

    气象卫星怎样获取资料？
    近20年来，世界各国发射的100多颗气象卫星，发回许多有关地球气象及非气象的宝贵资料。许多人不禁要问，气象卫星是怎样获取地球资料的呢？
    原来，气象卫星绕地轨道半径有数百公里之遥，无法利用摄像系统大面积地拍摄地球上较细致的资料。然而，在绝对零度（－273.16℃）以上的物体，都能辐射、反射和吸收电磁波。可见光、红外线和声波一样，都是具有特定波长的电磁波。而不同的物体（如云、植物、海水等等）产生的电磁波的波长各不相同，方向性、穿透性各异。利用这一特点，人们在卫星上安装能遥感检测地表（含大气层）各种物体的电磁波的仪器。这些仪器把电磁波转变为电信号并数字化，类似电视信号（但频率更高）传到地面站，然后由计算机根据已建立的数学物理模型进行处理识别，并运用计算机图像处理、光学、色度学、特定应用学科等多领域的配合，拼造成各种所需的图像。因此，气象卫星信息除了用作云分析、灾害天气分析预报以外，还可以应用到林火监测、编制地图、植被分布分析、作物产量预测、渔场预报、海洋资料调查研究等各个方面。
        （7月20日《南方日报》朱惠明文　林其成摘）
