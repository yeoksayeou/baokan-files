Title: 33 Latin American Countries Pass a Document Condemning U.S. Unequal Trade Laws
Author:
Date: 1989-08-04
Edition: 3

The 15th Ministerial Council meeting of the Latin American Economic System concluded late on the evening of August 2 in the coastal city of Cartagena, Colombia. The 33 Latin American countries participating in the meeting passed a final document condemning the unequal trade laws enacted by the United States.

During the two-day meeting, the foreign ministers and representatives of the 33 Latin American and Caribbean countries discussed issues such as the region's external debt, drug trafficking, the situation in Panama, and U.S. trade laws.

Regarding the trade law enacted by the United States in October of the previous year, the final document states that this law "has caused confusion in the trade sector of Latin America and the Caribbean, seriously damaging our economies."

In discussing the external debt issue in Latin America, the document states that the debt problem has become the most serious economic issue in the region. The document advocates that the Latin American debt problem should be resolved based on the debt situation of each country through separate negotiations.

The meeting decided to hold another meeting in the first quarter of next year to specifically discuss how to address the region's total external debt of $443 billion.

### 拉美33国通过一项文件  谴责美国不平等贸易法

1989-08-04
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    拉美33国通过一项文件
    谴责美国不平等贸易法
    新华社波哥大8月3日电　拉美经济体系第15届外长级理事会会议2日晚在哥伦比亚沿海城市卡塔赫纳闭幕。参加会议的33个拉美国家在会议结束时通过了一项最后文件中谴责美国颁布的贸易条件不平等的贸易法。
    33个拉美和加勒比国家的外长和代表在这次为期2天的会议上讨论了本地区外债、毒品走私、巴拿马局势和美国贸易法等问题。
    在谈到美国去年10月颁布的贸易法时，最后文件说，这项贸易法“在拉美和加勒比贸易领域中引起了混乱，给我们的经济造成严重损害”。
    在谈到拉美地区的外债问题时，文件认为，外债问题已成为本地区最严重的一个经济问题。文件主张拉美的外债问题应根据各国的负债情况，通过分别谈判的方式来解决。
    会议决定，在明年第一季度将再次举行会议，专门讨论如何解决拉美总额高达4430亿美元的外债问题。
