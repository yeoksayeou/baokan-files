Title: Lullaby
Author: Long Bide
Date: 1989-08-06
Edition: 8

Lullaby
Long Bide

A cradle made of birch bark
Life dwells in the trees
(No wonder the birch is called the tree of life)
The wind sways the trees, the trees sway the person
Sparkling waves rise like silk ribbons
The scent of birch permeates the swaddling clothes
The birch rings turn into years
The ticking sound is not from a small alarm clock
But from the colorful beads hanging down
Tiger teeth, pig bones, lynx claws, little bear noses
(What unique toys)
Colliding with the parents' experiences and hopes
Eaglets tear apart fresh prey
Puppies attack massive leopards
Which dream is not thrilling
Which day forgets the sacred duty
“La la, la la…”
The trees sway the person, the person sways the sky
A lullaby of deep affection and longing
Calling for the rise of a new generation of mountains
