Title: China and West Germany Jointly Conduct Expedition to the Glaciers of the Qinghai-Tibet Plateau

Author: Zhang Shusheng
Date: 1989-08-01
Edition: 2

The Chinese Academy of Sciences' first Sino-foreign joint expedition team, the China-West Germany Qinghai-Tibet Plateau Joint Expedition Team, set off for Ando in Tibet on July 27 as scheduled and established a base camp there.

This is the fourth joint expedition to the Qinghai-Tibet Plateau by China and West Germany since 1981. The joint expedition team consists of 40 members from China, West Germany, and Poland.

The joint expedition includes modern glaciers and their development conditions, Quaternary deposits and ancient glaciers, modern geomorphological processes and high-altitude disasters, as well as vegetation and ecological environments. The main expedition areas are the Yuladong Glacier and its southern lake basin in the source region of the Yangtze River, the Kangtang Glacier and the Gangma Tsangpo Valley on the eastern slope of Mount Everest, the glaciers of Namcha Barwa Peak and the Yarlung Zangbo River Valley, and the extensive oceanic glacier distribution area to the east.

### 中国西德联合组队考察青藏高原冰川
张述圣
1989-08-01
第2版(国内新闻)
专栏：四面八方

    中国西德联合组队考察青藏高原冰川
    本报兰州7月31日电  记者张述圣报道：由中国科学院兰州冰川冻土研究所组织的中国科学院第一支中外联合考察队——中国、联邦德国青藏高原联合考察队，7月27日如期开赴西藏安多，并在那里建立大本营。
    这是1981年以来中国与联邦德国第四次联合考察青藏高原。这支联合考察队由中、德、波兰40名人员组成。
    联合考察的内容包括现代冰川及其发育条件，第四纪沉积与古冰川，现代地貌作用与高山灾害以及植被与生态环境等。主要考察地区为长江源头各拉丹东冰川及其南侧湖盆地区、珠穆朗玛峰东坡的康滩冰川与甘玛藏布河谷、南迦巴瓦峰冰川及雅鲁藏布江河谷及其以东的广大海洋性冰川分布区。
