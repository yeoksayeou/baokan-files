Title: Human Remains Found in the Taklamakan Desert
Author: Shēn Zūn Jìng
Date: 1989-08-09
Page: 1

Xinhua News Agency, Urumqi, August 9th — (Reporter: Shēn Zūn Jìng) A large number of ancient artifacts and some ruins indicate that humans have been developing and utilizing the water system of the Keriya River in the southern part of the Taklamakan Desert for nearly 10,000 years. Recently, Wang Binghua, the director of the Xinjiang Archaeological Institute, announced these findings.

The Xinjiang Archaeological Institute recently sent a team to the southern and eastern parts of the desert for in-depth investigations. Archaeologists discovered the Bashikangsuolek Microlithic Site, dating back 10,000 years, in a river valley terrace upstream of the Keriya River. In the desert interior, 230 kilometers north of the city of Yutian, archaeologists found red-brown flat-bottomed pottery pots, pottery shards, slag, arrowheads, and some wooden artifacts. At the ruins of the ancient city of Milan (a Tang Dynasty fort), they discovered Han Dynasty coins and pottery. This city is likely to be the site of the Han Dynasty's Yichun agricultural settlement.

### 塔克拉玛干沙漠有人类遗迹
申尊敬
1989-08-09
第1版(要闻)
专栏：

    塔克拉玛干沙漠有人类遗迹
    据新华社乌鲁木齐8月9日电　（记者申尊敬）大量古文物和一些遗址表明，人类在近1万年时间内，一直在开发利用塔克拉玛干沙漠南部的克里雅河水系。新疆考古所所长王炳华最近宣布了这一考察结果。
    新疆考古所前不久派队前往沙漠南部和东部深入考察，考古工作者在克里雅河上游一处河谷台地，首次发现了距今1万年前的巴什康苏勒克细石器遗址。在距于田县城北230公里的沙漠腹地，考古工作者发现了红褐色的平底陶锅、陶片、炼渣、箭头和一些木器。在沙漠东部的米兰古堡（唐代戍堡）废墟底部，发现汉代钱币、陶器等文物，此城可能是汉代伊循屯田遗址。
