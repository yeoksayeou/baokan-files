Title: Mount Lao
Author: Chai Yin
Date: 1989-08-07
Edition: 8

Each rock is
A profound and open riddle
Stacked upon stacked
Layer upon layer
Cicadas buzz
Flitting past my mind
As if pointing out the fleeting doubts and mysteries
In the glance back
Green rain rises in the visible valley
Wafting up
I have no intention of withering or blooming in the mountains
A blade of green grass, a cluster of ants
All smile and ask me
If I have first understood
The eternally strange self that comes with birth

### 崂山
柴吟
1989-08-07
第8版(副刊)
专栏：

   崂山
   柴吟
   每一块岩石都是
   一片深邃且坦荡的谜语
   一摞一摞　叠叠层层
   鸣蝉　从脑际飞掠而过
   似是指点瞬间的疑云幽雾
   回眸处　翠雨在触目的低谷
   袅袅升起
   我无意在山中凋零或是开花
   一粒碧草一丛蚂蚁
   都在含笑询问我
   是否首先读懂了
   与生俱来的永远陌生的自己
