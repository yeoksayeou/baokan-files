Title: Latin America Holds Over a Thousand Billion Barrels of Oil
Author:
Date: 1989-08-03
Edition: 3

The general manager of Ecuador's state-owned oil company, Luis Ramon, revealed on August 2 that Latin America's proven oil reserves amount to 114.5 billion barrels. This quantity can meet the region's consumption needs for the next 45 years. In 1980, Latin America's oil production accounted for 5% of the world's total, and this figure rose to 8% by 1987. Currently, Mexico and Venezuela have the largest oil reserves in Latin America. The countries with the highest oil exports are Mexico, Venezuela, and Trinidad and Tobago.

### 拉美储油一千多亿桶

1989-08-03
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    拉美储油一千多亿桶
    新华社基多8月2日电　厄瓜多尔国家石油公司总经理路易斯·拉蒙2日透露，拉丁美洲已探明的石油储量为1145亿桶，可以满足该地区未来45年的消费需要，产量1980年占世界总产量的5％，1987年上升到8％。目前，拉丁美洲石油储量最多的国家是墨西哥和委内瑞拉，出口石油最多的国家为墨西哥、委内瑞拉、特立尼达和多巴哥。
