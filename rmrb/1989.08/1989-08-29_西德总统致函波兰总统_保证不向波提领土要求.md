Title: West German President Writes to Polish President, Guarantees No Territorial Claims
Author:
Date: 1989-08-29
Page: 3

Xinhua News Agency, Bonn, August 28th.

West German President Richard von Weizsäcker wrote to Polish President Wojciech Jaruzelski on the 50th anniversary of Nazi Germany's invasion of Poland, guaranteeing that the Federal Republic of Germany will never make any territorial claims against Poland in the future.

In his letter, Weizsäcker also expressed his hope for strengthened cooperation between the two countries in political, economic, and cultural areas. He emphasized that there are greater opportunities for European cooperation today than at any time since the end of World War II, and these opportunities should not be missed.

Reflecting on the historical events of 50 years ago, Weizsäcker stated that the people living in Poland and the Federal Republic of Germany today seek peace and need peace. To find a path from "guilt and misfortune" to a future filled with trust, it is necessary to acknowledge reality and practice mutual understanding.

### 西德总统致函波兰总统  保证不向波提领土要求

1989-08-29
第3版(国际新闻·体育)
专栏：

    西德总统致函波兰总统
    保证不向波提领土要求
    据新华社波恩8月28日电  西德总统魏茨泽克在纳粹德国入侵波兰50周年之际致信波兰总统雅鲁泽尔斯基，保证联邦德国即使在将来也不向波兰提出任何领土要求。
    魏茨泽克在信中同时表示，希望加强两国在政治、经济和文化方面的合作。他强调，对欧洲合作来说，今天有着比二次大战结束以来的任何时候都大的机会，不能随便放过。
    魏茨泽克在回顾了50年前纳粹德国入侵波兰的历史后说，今天生活在波兰和联邦德国的人们在寻找和平，他们需要和平。为了从“罪过和不幸”中找到一条通向充满信任的未来的道路，一种承认现实并实行相互谅解的意愿是必要的。
