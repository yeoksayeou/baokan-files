Title: Six Nations' Heroes Set Out to Traverse the Antarctic Continent
Author: Zhang Jimin
Date: 1989-07-31
Page: 2

Xinhua News Agency, Beijing, July 31st.

Six nations' heroes set out to traverse the Antarctic continent. According to a telephone call from China's Great Wall Station in the Antarctic, on July 28th, an international expedition team consisting of six people from China, Japan, the United States, France, the Soviet Union, and England set out from the tip of the Antarctic Peninsula, embarking on this globally significant expedition.

Driving dog-sleds and trekking over 6,000 kilometers on foot, the team plans to traverse the Antarctic from east to west over seven months, aiming to reach the Soviet Union's Mirny Station by early March next year. This expedition is the longest and most perilous in the history of polar exploration.

### 横穿南极大陆  六国勇士出征
张继民
1989-07-31
第2版(国内新闻)
专栏：

    横穿南极大陆  六国勇士出征
    据新华社北京7月31日电  （记者张继民）记者从来自中国南极长城站的电话中获悉：7月28日，由中、日、美、法、苏、英6国6人组成的国际横穿南极大陆考察队，已经从南极半岛的端部出发，踏上了这一举世瞩目的考察征程。
    赶着狗拉雪橇，徒步6000多公里，7个月横贯南极东西，明年3月初到达苏联和平站，这是考察队的基本行动计划。纵观南、北极地考察史，这次穿越的距离最长，也很艰险。
