Title: Leaders of the Polish and Soviet Parties Agree: Without the Involvement of the Polish United Workers' Party, the Polish Question Cannot Be Resolved
Author:
Date: 1989-08-23
Edition: 3

Leaders of the Polish and Soviet parties have agreed that without the involvement of the Polish United Workers' Party, the Polish question cannot be resolved.

	Xinhua News Agency, Warsaw, August 22 - The first secretary of the Polish United Workers' Party Central Committee, Jaruzelski, and the general secretary of the CPSU Central Committee, Gorbachev, exchanged views on the current political situation in Poland over the telephone on August 22.

	The two leaders agreed that "without the involvement of the Polish United Workers' Party, the Polish question cannot be resolved."

	The spokesperson for the Polish United Workers' Party Central Committee, Bysztyga, introduced the content of this telephone conversation between the two party leaders at a press conference this evening.

	The spokesperson pointed out that Gorbachev expressed concern about the situation in Poland and hoped that the Polish United Workers' Party would "smoothly resolve the social, political, and economic problems it faces in a manner that is in the interests of the Polish people and socialism."

### 波苏两党领导人一致认为  没有波统一工人党参与  波兰问题就得不到解决

1989-08-23
第3版(国际新闻·体育)
专栏：

    波苏两党领导人一致认为
    没有波统一工人党参与  波兰问题就得不到解决
    新华社华沙8月22日电  波兰统一工人党中央第一书记拉科夫斯基和苏共中央总书记戈尔巴乔夫22日在电话中就当前波兰政治形势交换了意见。
    两位领导人一致认为，“没有波兰统一工人党的参与，波兰问题是得不到解决的。”
    波兰统一工人党中央委员会发言人比什蒂加在今晚举行的新闻发布会上介绍了两党领导人的这次电话谈话内容。
    这位发言人指出，戈尔巴乔夫对波兰形势表示关切，希望波兰统一工人党“在符合波兰人民和社会主义的利益的情况下”顺利地解决面临的社会、政治和经济问题。
