Title: Prime Minister Kaifu Decides Not to Visit Yasukuni Shrine
Author: Yu Qing
Date: 1989-08-10
Edition: 3

Prime Minister Kaifu has decided not to visit Yasukuni Shrine, considering the sentiments of neighboring Asian countries such as China.

August 15, the day marking the end of World War II, is approaching, and whether the new cabinet members will visit Yasukuni Shrine has become a focal point of public attention. Last night, when new cabinet members met with reporters, Prime Minister Kaifu stated that he would not go, but out of 20 cabinet ministers, 13 expressed their intention to visit. The cabinet members' consultation meeting advised those who plan to visit to avoid forms of Shinto worship that conflict with the spirit of the constitution.

### 海部决定不参拜靖国神社  新内阁中13人表示将前往参拜
于青
1989-08-10
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    海部决定不参拜靖国神社
    新内阁中13人表示将前往参拜
    本报东京8月10日电　记者于青报道：据日本政府人士透露，海部首相表示，考虑到中国等亚洲邻国的感情，决定不去参拜靖国神社。
    8月15日停战纪念日即将来临，新内阁成员是否参拜靖国神社，成为此间舆论界注目的问题。昨天晚上新内阁成员分别会见记者时，在被问到是否参拜靖国神社时，海部首相说不去，但20名内阁大臣中有13人表示将前去参拜。内阁成员恳谈会对将去参拜的大臣提出注意不要采取与宪法精神抵触的神道参拜形式。
