Title: Chinese Foreign Minister Meets with U.S. Secretary of State; Both Sides Emphasize Developing Sino-American Relations

Author: Zhang Qihua, Lü Wangda

Date: 1989-08-01

Page: 3

Xinhua News Agency, Paris, August 1st.

Yesterday afternoon, Foreign Minister Qian Qichen met with U.S. Secretary of State James Baker in Paris. Both sides exchanged views on resolving the Cambodian issue and expressed satisfaction with the cooperation between the two countries' delegations at the Paris International Conference.

Foreign Minister Qian Qichen briefed Secretary Baker on the domestic situation in China. He emphasized that China's independent and peaceful foreign policy and its policy of reform and opening up will not change. Baker stated that President Bush highly values the relationship between the two countries that has been cultivated over the past 12 years and hopes that both sides will work together to maintain and develop this relationship. Foreign Minister Qian emphasized that although the social systems, values, and cultural backgrounds of China and the United States are different, there are common interests in politics and economics. As long as both sides respect each other and do not interfere in each other's internal affairs, Sino-American relations can be developed.

### 钱外长会晤贝克国务卿  双方强调发展中美关系
张启华  吕网大
1989-08-01
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    钱外长会晤贝克国务卿  双方强调发展中美关系
    本报巴黎8月1日电　记者张启华、吕网大报道：昨天下午，钱其琛外长在巴黎会见了美国国务卿贝克，双方就解决柬埔寨问题交换了意见，并对两国代表团在巴黎国际会议上的合作表示满意。
    钱其琛外长向贝克国务卿介绍了我国国内形势，他强调指出，中国独立自主的和平外交政策和改革开放政策不会改变。贝克表示，布什总统十分珍视12年来双方培育起来的中美关系，希望共同努力，使这种关系得到维持和发展。钱外长强调，中美两国的社会制度、价值观念和文化背景不同，但在政治上和经济上有共同利益，只要相互尊重，互不干涉内政，中美关系是能够得到发展的。
