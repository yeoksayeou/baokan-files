Title: Cars Bought with Road Repair Funds
Author: Xu Pengfei
Date: 1989-08-12
Edition: 5

    Cars Bought with Road Repair Funds
    Xu Pengfei

    Originally published in "Satire and Humor"

    In a small town, the local government decided to repair a major road. The funds allocated for this project were substantial, but the town's officials had other plans. Instead of using the money to fix the potholes and improve the road's surface, they decided to purchase several luxury cars for their personal use.

    The news spread quickly among the townspeople, who were outraged. They had been hoping for better roads to ease their daily commutes, but their expectations were dashed when they saw the shiny new cars parked in front of the government offices. The officials, however, seemed unfazed by the public's reaction. They justified their actions by claiming that the cars were necessary for "official business."

    The town's residents were not convinced. They organized protests and wrote letters to higher authorities, demanding an explanation and justice. The local media picked up the story, and soon it became a national scandal. The officials, facing intense pressure, were eventually removed from their positions, and the funds were redirected to the road repair project.

    This incident highlighted the widespread corruption and misuse of public funds that plagued many local governments at the time. It served as a wake-up call for the authorities to tighten oversight and ensure that public funds were used for their intended purposes. The town's roads were finally repaired, and the residents could once again hope for a smoother journey.
