Title: A Soviet Woman Donates Blood for 36 Years, Providing 67 Liters
Author:
Date: 1989-08-17
Page: 3

Xinhua News Agency, Moscow, August 16th.

A female worker at the Minsk Building Ceramics Factory in the Soviet Union has continuously donated blood for 36 years, providing a total of 67 liters of blood without any compensation.

The Soviet newspaper *Pravda* published an interview with this woman on August 16th. The woman, named Leonida Apanasenko, stated that regularly donating 400 to 500 milliliters of blood is not only harmless but also beneficial to the body. The lost blood can be fully replenished within 15 to 20 days.

Apanasenko has donated blood a total of 184 times. The total blood volume in a person's body is about 4 to 5 liters. Over the past 36 years, the amount of blood she has donated is approximately 15 times her total blood volume.

### 苏一妇女无偿献血  36年提供鲜血67升

1989-08-17
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    苏一妇女无偿献血
    36年提供鲜血67升
    新华社莫斯科8月16日电　苏联明斯克建筑陶瓷厂的一位女工连续献血36年，无偿提供鲜血67升。
    苏联《真理报》8月16日刊登了这位妇女对该报记者的谈话。这位名叫列昂妮达·阿帕纳谢诺克的妇女说，定期献400至500毫升的血对人体不但无害，反而有益。失去的血液在15至20天内就能完全弥补过来。
    阿帕纳谢诺克一共献血184次。一个人体内的总血量为4至5升。36年来，她所献出的血液约等于她全身血液量的15倍。
