Title: School-run Microenterprises Flourish in Britain
Author: Xǔ Rúyuán
Date: 1989-08-27
Edition: 7

In recent years, Britain has seen a surge in school-run microenterprises aimed at developing the intellectual capabilities of young students. Learning through these school-run enterprises has become a compulsory course for students aged 9 to 16. The accompanying image shows a group of enthusiastic young students using computers to learn how to edit the daily edition of the *Brookwood Reporter*.
