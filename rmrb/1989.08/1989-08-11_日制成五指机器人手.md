Title: Japan Develops Five-Fingered Robot Hand
Author:
Date: 1989-08-11
Edition: 3

Japan's Industrial Technology Institute's Mechanical Technology Research Institute has successfully developed a robotic hand with five fingers that closely mimics human hand functions. This robotic hand can grasp objects of complex shapes, is easy to control, and is expected to be useful in environments such as nuclear reactors, underwater, and space where humans cannot easily enter.

According to the *Nihon Keizai Shimbun*, the entire robotic arm weighs about 5 kilograms and, like a human wrist, has an upper arm, forearm, palm, and fingers. The robotic hand is powered by 20 motors, each driving fine steel wires to move the fingers and wrist. The elbow's movements are controlled by gears.

### 日制成五指机器人手

1989-08-11
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    日制成五指机器人手
    新华社东京8月11日电　日本工业技术院机械技术研究所，试制成功具有近似于人手功能的5个指头的机器人手。该机器人手能抓住复杂形状的东西，易控制，可望在原子反应堆、海底和宇宙等人类难以进去的环境中发挥作用。
    据《日本工业新闻》报道，机器人的整个手臂重约5公斤，和人的手腕一样，有上腕、前腕、手掌和手指。
    该机器人手的动力源是20个马达，利用各自的马达牵动细钢丝，驱动手指和手腕只，有肘的动作是利用齿轮。
