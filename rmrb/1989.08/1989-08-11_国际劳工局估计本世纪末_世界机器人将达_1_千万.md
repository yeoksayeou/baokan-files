Title: International Labour Office Estimates 10 Million Robots Worldwide by End of Century
Author:
Date: 1989-08-11
Page: 3

Xinhua News Agency, Geneva, August 10th.

The International Labour Office issued a statement today predicting that by the end of the century, the total number of robots in the world could reach 10 million.

The statement said that the number of robots in the world is increasing at a rate of doubling every two years. According to the figures provided in the statement, Japan currently has the most robots, with approximately 141,000 units.

The International Labour Office pointed out that robots have numerous advantages; they can work continuously for 24 hours without being affected by adverse external factors and can free workers from dangerous and harmful labor.

### 国际劳工局估计本世纪末  世界机器人将达1千万

1989-08-11
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    国际劳工局估计本世纪末
    世界机器人将达1千万
    新华社日内瓦8月10日电  国际劳工局10日发表的一份公报预计，到本世纪末，全世界的机器人总数可达1000万台。
    公报说，世界上机器人的数目正以每两年翻一番的速度增加。据公报提供的数字，目前拥有机器人最多的国家是日本，数量约为14.1万台。
    国际劳工局指出，机器人优点众多，它可24小时连续工作，不受外界不良因素的影响，能把工人从危险和有损健康等劳动中解放出来。
