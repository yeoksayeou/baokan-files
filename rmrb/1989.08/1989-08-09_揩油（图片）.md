Title: Squeezing Oil
Author: Wang Chengxi
Date: 1989-08-09
Page: 5

Originally published in "Satire and Humor"

The term "squeezing oil" is a euphemism used to describe the practice of taking advantage of others, often in a sneaky or underhanded way. This phrase has become increasingly popular in recent years, reflecting a growing awareness of the need to address issues of fairness and honesty in society.

In the workplace, "squeezing oil" can manifest in various forms, such as overcharging for services, providing substandard goods, or exploiting workers. These practices not only harm the interests of individuals but also undermine the trust and cohesion of society as a whole.

To combat "squeezing oil," it is essential to promote a culture of transparency and accountability. This includes strengthening legal frameworks, enhancing public awareness, and encouraging whistleblowing. By fostering an environment where honesty and integrity are valued, we can work towards eliminating this detrimental practice and building a more just and equitable society.

In everyday life, individuals can also play a role in preventing "squeezing oil." By being vigilant and reporting instances of unfair practices, we can help to create a more honest and trustworthy community. Additionally, supporting businesses that prioritize ethical conduct can send a strong message about the importance of integrity in all aspects of life.

In conclusion, "squeezing oil" is a pervasive issue that requires collective effort to address. By promoting transparency, accountability, and ethical behavior, we can work towards a society where fairness and honesty prevail.
