Title: Mongolian People's Revolutionary Army Song and Dance Troupe Leaves Hangzhou for Guangzhou
Author:
Date: 1955-08-01
Page: 1

Xinhua News Agency, Hangzhou, July 29th. The Mongolian People's Revolutionary Army Song and Dance Troupe, led by Major General Damdin Süren, left Hangzhou by special train on the afternoon of the 28th.

Those who came to the station to see them off included Lin Weixian, Commander of the Zhejiang Military Region of the Chinese People's Liberation Army, Zhou Guanwu, Deputy Political Commissar, Li Fengping, Vice Governor of Zhejiang Province, Liu Wenxue, Director of the Political Department of the Zhejiang Military Region of the Chinese People's Liberation Army, Hong Dazhong, Minister of the Cultural Department of the Political Department, Huang Yuan, Director of the Zhejiang Provincial Culture Bureau, Gu Chunlin, Deputy Mayor of Hangzhou, and representatives of literary and art workers from Zhejiang Province and Hangzhou City. At the station, Major General Damdin Süren and Colonel Chimiddorgee accepted flowers from those who saw them off. Major General Damdin Süren repeatedly shook hands with the well-wishers.

The Mongolian People's Revolutionary Army Song and Dance Troupe performed two shows for the people of Hangzhou, and their wonderful performances were warmly welcomed by the audience.

During their stay in Hangzhou, the distinguished guests also visited the scenic spots and historical sites of West Lake and the Naval Sanatorium of the Chinese People's Liberation Army. During the visit, Major General Damdin Süren kindly inquired about the health of the naval soldiers who were recuperating. Namjildorj, the deputy head of the Song and Dance Troupe, on behalf of all the members, presented many books and photos to the recuperating personnel and staff of the Naval Sanatorium.

Xinhua News Agency, Guangzhou, July 30th. The Mongolian People's Revolutionary Army Song and Dance Troupe, led by Major General Dunru Bu Damdin Süren, arrived in Guangzhou by special train from Hangzhou on the afternoon of the 30th.

As the train carrying the distinguished guests slowly entered the station, people who had been waiting on the platform waved bouquets to warmly welcome them. The band played "Victory March" and "The Hearts of People All Over the World are One." After getting off the train, Major General Damdin Süren shook hands cordially with Wen Niansheng, Liu Xingyuan, Chen Renqi, and Gao Tigan, responsible persons of the leadership organs of the Chinese People's Liberation Army Guangzhou Garrison, He Ximing, Vice Governor of Guangdong Province, and Chen Zhifang, Deputy Mayor of Guangzhou, and accepted flowers from those who welcomed them. All the members of the Song and Dance Troupe also accepted flowers from the welcomers. At this time, songs, cheers and applause resounded throughout the station.

In the square of the station, a welcome ceremony was held with the participation of more than 900 people, including the Guangzhou Garrison of the Chinese People's Liberation Army, literary and art workers from Guangzhou, and people from all walks of life. Wang Yanshi, representative of the Guangzhou Garrison of the Chinese People's Liberation Army, and Ding Bo, Director of the Guangzhou Municipal Bureau of Culture, delivered welcome speeches on behalf of the troops stationed in Guangzhou and the people of Guangzhou.

Lieutenant Colonel Namjildorj, Deputy Head of the Mongolian People's Revolutionary Army Song and Dance Troupe, delivered a reply in the warm applause of the welcoming crowd. He said: Since our visit to China, we have introduced our art to the Chinese people, and at the same time we have personally seen that the hardworking and brave Chinese people, under the leadership of the great Chinese Communist Party and Chairman Mao, are building their motherland and have achieved brilliant achievements. The Mongolian people are pleased and admire this. Finally, he congratulated the unbreakable and eternal friendship between the Chinese and Mongolian peoples for becoming even more consolidated.

In the evening, all members of the Mongolian People's Revolutionary Army Song and Dance Troupe, headed by Major General Damdin Süren, attended a welcome banquet hosted by Wen Niansheng, head of the Guangzhou Garrison of the Chinese People's Liberation Army, He Ximing, Vice Governor of Guangdong Province, and Chen Zhifang, Deputy Mayor of Guangzhou.

### 蒙古人民革命军歌舞团离杭州到广州

1955-08-01
第1版()
专栏：

    蒙古人民革命军歌舞团离杭州到广州
    新华社杭州二十九日电　由达姆丁呼少将率领的蒙古人民革命军歌舞团，在二十八日下午乘专车离开杭州。
    到车站欢送的有中国人民解放军浙江军区司令员林维先，副政治委员周贯五，浙江省副省长李丰平，中国人民解放军浙江军区政治部主任刘文学，政治部文化部部长洪大中，浙江省文化局局长黄源，杭州市副市长顾春林，以及浙江省、杭州市文艺工作者代表。在车站上，达姆丁呼少将和钦迪上校等接受了欢送者的献花，达姆丁呼少将一再亲切地和欢送者握手道别。
    蒙古人民革命军歌舞团在杭州曾为市人民演出二场，他们精采的表演受到观众们的热烈欢迎。
    贵宾们在杭州期间还游览了西湖名胜古迹，参观了中国人民解放军海军疗养院。达姆丁呼少将在参观时亲切地询问正在疗养的海军战士们的健康情况。歌舞团的政治副团长那木吉拉道尔吉代表全体团员把许多书籍和照片送给海军疗养院的休养人员和工作人员。
    新华社广州三十日电　三十日下午，由敦儒布·达姆丁呼少将率领的蒙古人民革命军歌舞团乘专车从杭州到达广州。
    当载着贵宾的列车徐徐进站时，早在月台上迎候的人们挥舞着花束热烈欢迎他们。乐队奏起“胜利进行曲”和“全世界人民心一条”的歌曲。达姆丁呼少将下车后和中国人民解放军广州驻军领导机关负责人文年生、刘兴元、陈仁麒、高体乾，广东省副省长贺希明，广州市副市长陈志方等一一亲切握手，并接受了欢迎者的献花；歌舞团全体人员也接受了欢迎者的献花。这时，车站上的歌声、欢呼声和鼓掌声响成一片。
    在车站的广场上，举行了有中国人民解放军广州驻军和广州文艺工作者及各界人士共九百多人参加的欢迎仪式。中国人民解放军广州驻军代表王燕士和广州市文化局局长丁波代表驻广州部队和广州市人民致欢迎词。
    蒙古人民革命军歌舞团副团长那木吉拉道尔吉中校在欢迎群众的热烈鼓掌声中致答词。他说：我们在中国访问以来，向中国人民介绍了自己的艺术，同时也亲眼看到了勤劳勇敢的中国人民在伟大的中国共产党和毛主席的领导下，建设着自己的祖国，并获得了辉煌的成就，蒙古人民对此感到愉快和钦佩。最后，他祝贺中蒙两国人民牢不可破的、永恒的友谊更加巩固。
    晚上，以达姆丁呼少将为首的蒙古人民革命军歌舞团全体人员出席了中国人民解放军广州驻军负责人文年生、广东省副省长贺希明和广州市副市长陈志方的欢迎宴会。
