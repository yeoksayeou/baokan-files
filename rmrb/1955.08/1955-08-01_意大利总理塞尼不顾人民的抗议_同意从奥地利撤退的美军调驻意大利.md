Title: Italian Prime Minister Segni, Ignoring Popular Protests, Agrees to Transfer U.S. Troops Withdrawing from Austria to Italy
Author:
Date: 1955-08-01
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, Rome: Despite strong protests from the Italian public, newly-appointed Italian Prime Minister Segni stated in a speech to the Senate on the 22nd that he agreed to transfer the American occupation forces withdrawing from Austria to be stationed in Italy. He stated that the Italian government would agree as long as the North Atlantic Treaty Organization made such a decision.

According to a United Press International dispatch from Washington on the 23rd, the North Atlantic Council has decided to transfer U.S. troops to Italy upon completion of the withdrawal of occupation forces from Austria.

Reports say that the U.S. decision to transfer troops stationed in Austria to Italy is not only to strengthen the North Atlantic Group's forces in the Eastern Alps, but also to strengthen control over the turbulent Italy.

### 意大利总理塞尼不顾人民的抗议  同意从奥地利撤退的美军调驻意大利

1955-08-01
第4版()
专栏：

    意大利总理塞尼不顾人民的抗议
    同意从奥地利撤退的美军调驻意大利
    新华社讯　罗马消息：不顾意大利广大公众的强烈抗议，新上任的意大利塞尼总理二十二日在上议院发表的演说中表示同意把从奥地利撤退的美国占领军调到意大利境内驻扎。他表示，只要北大西洋公约组织作出这样的决定，意大利政府将予以同意。
    据合众社华盛顿二十三日消息透露，北大西洋集团理事会已决定，在完成从奥地利撤退各国占领军的工作时，把美国部队调到意大利去。
    有消息说，美国所以决定把驻扎奥地利的军队调驻意大利，除了为加强北大西洋集团在东阿尔卑斯山地区的力量外，也为了要加强对动荡中的意大利的控制。
