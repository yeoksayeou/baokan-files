Title: China's Decision to Release American Spies Early Generates Wide Response
Author:
Date: 1955-08-03
Page: 4
Xinhua News Agency, August 2nd: Washington dispatch: China's Supreme People's Court's decision to release eleven American spies early has generated a wide response in the United States.

    U.S. President Dwight D. Eisenhower issued a statement on the 1st, saying: "The nation will feel relieved and welcome with joy the news that the eleven American airmen who have been detained in Communist China since 1953 will finally be released."

    U.S. Secretary of State John Foster Dulles also issued a statement on the 1st, saying that he was "very pleased" with the release of these personnel.

    According to Reuters, the announcement from China has had a great impact in the U.S. Congress. Members of both the House and Senate stated that the decision to release the airmen at the time when the two countries' ambassadors are beginning their discussions in Geneva is satisfactory.

    Some senators said it was "good news."

    Senator George, Chairman of the Senate Foreign Relations Committee and a Democrat, said he was "very pleased with this news" and that "it proves the Geneva talks are worth holding."

    Senator Wiley, a Republican member of the Senate Foreign Relations Committee, said it was a "welcome statement."

    Republican Senator Brick said, "I think this is encouraging, and I hope it means a turning point toward a more reasonable international relationship."

    Xinhua News Agency, August 2nd: According to Reuters London dispatch, British officials warmly welcomed the decision of the Supreme People's Court of China to release eleven American spies early. The British Foreign Office said: "We are very, very happy to hear the news of their release." London generally believes that this decision is a good omen for the talks between the Chinese and American ambassadors in Geneva.

    According to the Associated Press, people in London believe that China's release of eleven American airmen is an important step in easing tensions in the Far East.

  V.K. Krishna Menon, Indian Representative to the United Nations
  Welcomes China's Early Release of American Spies

    Xinhua News Agency, Geneva, August 2nd: V.K. Krishna Menon, the Indian representative to the United Nations, told reporters on the 1st regarding the decision of the Supreme People's Court of China to release eleven American spies early: "I am very happy to hear this news." He said: "I believe the announcement of this matter will greatly contribute to the success of the talks that Ambassador Johnson and Ambassador Wang Bingnan are conducting here."

    Menon arrived in Geneva on the evening of July 31st from London.

### 我国提前释放美间谍的决定引起广泛反响

1955-08-03
第4版()
专栏：

    我国提前释放美间谍的决定引起广泛反响
    新华社二日讯　华盛顿消息：中国最高人民法院决定提前释放十一名美国间谍的消息，在美国引起了广泛的反响。
    美国总统艾森豪威尔一日发表声明说：“对于自从一九五三年以来被拘在共产党中国的十一名美国飞行人员终将获释的消息，全国将会感到宽慰，并以欢欣之情予以欢迎。”
    美国国务卿杜勒斯一日也发表声明说，对这些人员获释“感到非常满意”。
    据路透社消息说，中国的这一宣布在美国国会中产生了很大影响。参众两院议员表示，在两国大使在日内瓦开始他们的讨论时候作出释放空军人员的决定是令人满意的。
    一些参议员说，这是一个“好消息”。
    参议院外交委员会主席、民主党人乔治说，他“对这消息感到很愉快”，“这证明日内瓦会谈是值得举行的”。
    参议院外交委员会委员、共和党人维利说，这是一个“值得欢迎的声明”。
    共和党参议员布里克说，“我认为这是令人鼓舞的，我希望这意味着是国际关系比较合理的一个转折点。”
    新华社二日讯　据路透社伦敦消息，英国官方人士热烈欢迎中国最高人民法院决定提前释放十一名美国间谍。英国外交部说：“我们非常，非常高兴听到释放他们的消息。”伦敦一般认为，这个决定是中美两国大使在日内瓦会谈的吉兆。
    据美联社报道，伦敦的人士认为中国释放十一名美国空军人员一事是缓和远东紧张局势方面的一个重要步骤。印度驻联合国代表梅农
  欢迎我国提前释放美间谍
    新华社日内瓦二日电　印度驻联合国代表梅农一日就中国最高人民法院决定提前释放十一名美国间谍一事对记者发表谈话说：“我非常高兴听到这个消息。”他说：“我相信这件事的宣布将大大有助于约翰逊大使和王炳南大使正在这里进行的会谈的成功。”
    梅农是在七月三十一日晚从伦敦到达日内瓦的。
