Title: Pakistan's New Cabinet Takes Office
Author: 
Date: 1955-08-13
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, New Delhi, August 12th: Karachi dispatch: The new Pakistani cabinet, with Chaudhry Muhammad Ali, leader of the Muslim League, as Prime Minister, was sworn in on the evening of the 11th.

Of the eleven cabinet members in the new cabinet, five belong to the Muslim League, five belong to the United Front, and one is a non-partisan person. Fazlul Huq, leader of the United Front, joined the cabinet.

In a radio address to the nation after taking office, Chaudhry Muhammad Ali said that the Pakistani government faces three primary tasks: resolving the Kashmir issue, formulating a constitution, and solving economic problems.

He said that the Kashmir issue must be resolved in accordance with the "greater interests of world peace" and the "special interests of friendship between India and Pakistan."

Chaudhry Muhammad Ali also said that he hopes the constitution can be formulated as soon as possible, followed by new general elections.

### 巴基斯坦新内阁就职

1955-08-13
第4版()
专栏：

    巴基斯坦新内阁就职
    新华社新德里十二日电　喀喇蚩消息：以回教联盟领袖乔德里·穆罕默德·阿里任总理的巴基斯坦新内阁已经在十一日晚上宣誓就职。
    新内阁的十一名阁员当中，五名属于回教联盟，五名属于联合阵线，一名是无党派人士。联合阵线领袖法兹鲁尔·胡克参加了内阁。
    乔德里·穆罕默德·阿里在就职后向全国发表的广播演说中说，巴基斯坦政府面临着解决克什米尔问题，制定宪法以及解决经济问题三个首要任务。
    他说，克什米尔问题必须在符合“世界和平的更大利益”和“印度与巴基斯坦友谊的特殊利益”的情况下加以解决。
    乔德里·穆罕默德·阿里还说，他希望宪法能在尽快的时间内制定好，随后就举行新的大选。
