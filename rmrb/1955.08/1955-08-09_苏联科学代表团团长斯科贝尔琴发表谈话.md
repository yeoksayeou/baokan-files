Title: Soviet Scientific Delegation Leader Skobeltsyn Issues Statement
Author:
Date: 1955-08-09
Page: 1
Xinhua News Agency, Moscow, August 8th: "Pravda" published an interview with Academician Skobeltsyn, head of the Soviet scientific delegation attending the International Conference on the Peaceful Uses of Atomic Energy in Geneva, titled: "Atomic Energy for the Benefit of Mankind."

    Skobeltsyn said: Soviet scientists and engineers have submitted 102 reports to the Geneva Conference. These reports summarize the work of Soviet researchers in applying atomic energy in physics, biology, engineering, chemistry, technology, medicine, and agriculture. These documents also include several new proposals and initiatives that Soviet scientists hope to discuss with their foreign colleagues. They especially hope to have a broad discussion with the Soviet Union and other countries, mainly the United States, Britain, France, and Canada, on the work carried out in the development of atomic power equipment, especially atomic power plants, and the prospects for using atomic energy for these purposes.

    Skobeltsyn said that the Soviet atomic power plant has been in production for more than a year, and the experience of establishing and managing this power plant will be the subject of a report. This report draws the important conclusion that, under certain conditions, the establishment of atomic power plants with enormous capacity can now be economically beneficial.

    Skobeltsyn said that we also hope that the participants in the conference will be interested in the Soviet documents on the establishment and manipulation of various experimental nuclear reactors, the technology of nuclear fuels, the materials for building reactors, and the exploitation of uranium and thorium deposits.

    Skobeltsyn pointed out that among the reports submitted by Soviet scientists to the conference are included studies on the use of tracer atoms in the study of metals, metallurgy, and other engineering problems, some research on the application of radioactive isotopes in various aspects of medicine in the Soviet Union, and a relatively comprehensive account of the application of radioactive isotopes in biology and agriculture.

    Skobeltsyn concluded by saying that Soviet scientists hope that the Soviet Union's contribution to the work of this conference and the peaceful use of atomic energy will strengthen peace and cooperation between countries. Soviet scientists are convinced that the strengthening of ties and personal contacts between scientists from different countries, and the mutual exchange of research results, are important conditions for the success of the peaceful use of atomic energy.

### 苏联科学代表团团长斯科贝尔琴发表谈话

1955-08-09
第1版()
专栏：

    苏联科学代表团团长斯科贝尔琴发表谈话
    新华社八日讯　据塔斯社莫斯科八日讯：“真理报”刊登了一篇出席和平利用原子能日内瓦国际会议的苏联科学代表团团长、科学院院士斯科贝尔琴的访问记，标题是：“为人类造福的原子能”。
    斯科贝尔琴说：苏联科学家和工程师们已经向日内瓦会议提出了一百零二份报告。这些报告总结了苏联研究人员在把原子能应用在物理学、生物学、工程学、化学、工艺学、医学和农业方面的工作。这些文件也包括若干新的建议和主张，苏联科学家们希望同外国同事们讨论这些新的建议和主张。他们希望特别广泛地讨论苏联和其他国家，主要是美国、英国、法国和加拿大在发展原子动力设备、特别是原子能发电站方面所进行的工作以及把原子能用于这些用途的前景的问题。
    斯科贝尔琴说，苏联原子能发电站投入生产已经一年多，建立和管理这个发电厂的经验将是一个报告的主题，这个报告得出重要的结论：在一定的条件下，建立具有巨大能力的原子能发电站现在已经可以在经济上获得利益。
    斯科贝尔琴说，我们还希望参加会议的人将会对苏联关于建立和操纵各种实验核子反应堆问题、核子燃料的工艺学问题、建立反应堆的材料问题、铀矿和钍矿矿藏的开采问题的各种文件发生兴趣。
    斯科贝尔琴指出，在苏联科学家提交会议的报告中，包括关于示踪原子用于研究金属、冶金和其他工程方面问题、苏联在医药的各个方面应用放射性同位素的一些研究和在生物学和农业中应用放射性同位素的比较全面的情况。
    斯科贝尔琴最后说，苏联科学家们希望苏联对这次会议的工作和在和平使用原子能方面所作的贡献将能加强和平和各国之间的合作。苏联科学家们确信，不同国家的科学家之间的联系和个人接触的加强以及研究结果的相互交流是使和平利用原子能获得成功的重要条件。
