Title: Indian Government Taking Measures to Ensure Early Liberation of Goa; Indian Volunteers Entering Goa Beaten and Arrested by Portuguese Authorities

Author:

Date: 1955-08-22
Page: 1

Xinhua News Agency, New Delhi, August 21st: According to the Indian Press Trust, Indian Prime Minister Nehru stated at a meeting of the Uttar Pradesh Congress Committee Executive Committee held in Sitapur on the 20th that the Indian government is taking measures to ensure the early liberation of Goa and other Portuguese territories in India. Nehru also said that the Goa liberation movement is an issue involving international relations.

Xinhua News Agency, New Delhi, August 21st: According to the Indian Information Service on the 21st, another 226 Indian volunteers entered Goa in three batches on August 19th.

Of the first batch of 82 who entered Goa on the evening of the 19th, 71 have been driven out. Most of them had injuries from being beaten, and six of them have been sent to hospitals for treatment due to serious injuries.

The second batch of 104 entered Goa that same evening. It is said that they have all been arrested by the Portuguese authorities and taken to the interior of Goa.

On the same evening, a third batch of 40 people entered Goa, and there has been no news about them so far.

### 印度政府正采取措施以保证早日解放果阿　印度志愿人员进入果阿又遭到葡萄牙当局的毒打和逮捕

1955-08-22
第1版()
专栏：

　　印度政府正采取措施以保证早日解放果阿
　　印度志愿人员进入果阿又遭到葡萄牙当局的毒打和逮捕
    新华社新德里二十一日电　据印度报业托辣斯报道，印度总理尼赫鲁二十日在西塔普举行的国大党北方省委员会执行委员会的会议上说，印度政府正在采取措施来保证早日解放果阿和葡萄牙在印度的其他属地。尼赫鲁又说果阿解放运动是一个牵涉到国际关系的问题。
    新华社新德里二十一日电　据印度新闻处二十一日报道，八月十九日，又有二百二十六名印度志愿人员分三批进入果阿。
    第一批八十二名在十九日傍晚进入果阿后，已有七十一名被赶出来。多数人身上都有被毒打的伤痕，其中六名因负重伤已被送进医院治疗。
    第二批一百零四人在当天晚上进入了果阿。据说他们已全部被葡萄牙当局逮捕，并且被带到果阿内地去了。
    同一天晚上，又有第三批四十人进入了果阿，到现在还没有关于他们的消息。
