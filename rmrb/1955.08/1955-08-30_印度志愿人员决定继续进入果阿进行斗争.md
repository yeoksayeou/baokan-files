Title: Indian Volunteers Decide to Continue Entering Goa to Fight
Author:
Date: 1955-08-30
Page: 4
　　
Xinhua News Agency, New Delhi, August 29th: The Goa Liberation Aid Committee of All Parties of India, which held a meeting in Pune, Bombay Province, announced after the meeting on the 28th that despite the "wanton violence" used by the Portuguese, the Pune Goa Liberation Aid Committee of All Parties will continue peaceful resistance in collective and individual forms until final victory is achieved.

　　The committee called on the people to continue to support the Goa liberation struggle in order to sweep away the last traces of colonialism on Indian soil.

　　The committee announced that in the next week, ninety-five volunteers from various provinces of India will enter Goa in groups. On October 2nd, Gandhi's birthday, five hundred volunteers will go to Goa.

　　The committee also decided to raise funds to provide relief to the families of the martyrs who sacrificed themselves for the liberation of Goa.

### 印度志愿人员决定继续进入果阿进行斗争

1955-08-30
第4版()
专栏：

　　印度志愿人员决定继续进入果阿进行斗争
    新华社新德里二十九日电　在孟买省浦那举行会议的印度各党派支援解放果阿委员会二十八日在会议结束后宣布：尽管葡萄牙人使用了“放肆的暴力”，浦那各党派支援解放果阿委员会将继续以集体的和个别的形式进行和平抵抗，一直到取得最后胜利为止。
    委员会号召人民继续支持果阿的解放斗争，以扫除印度土地上的殖民主义的最后痕迹。
    委员会宣布：在最近一个星期内，将有九十五名来自印度各省的志愿人员分成小组进入果阿。在十月二日甘地的生日，将有五百名志愿人员前往果阿。
    委员会还决定募款救济为争取果阿解放而牺牲的烈士家属。
