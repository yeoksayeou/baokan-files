Title: Tu Changwang, Bernal Talk About the International Conference on Peaceful Uses of Atomic Energy. Experts from Six Countries Including the Soviet Union, the United States, Britain, and Czechoslovakia Discuss Technical Issues of the Peaceful Use of Atomic Energy
Author:
Date: 1955-08-25
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, August 23rd, Vienna dispatch: The Secretariat of the World Peace Council reported that Professor Bernal of the University of London and Tu Changwang, an observer from the World Federation of Scientific Workers, made statements on the closing of the International Conference on Peaceful Uses of Atomic Energy.

Professor Bernal said that after this conference, malicious people can no longer create barriers of distrust among scientists around the world. Scientists have met and become acquainted with each other. Scientists have found that they share common opinions. This conference is a symbol of the spirit that guarantees human progress, a friendly and peaceful competition for the great benefits that peaceful use of atomic energy can bring. The conference itself greatly contributes to creating the necessary conditions for eliminating the fear of war.

Tu Changwang pointed out that this conference has been a great achievement from a scientific point of view. The history of science proves that close international cooperation among scientists is the basis for scientific progress. He expressed his hope that this conference could open the way for closer international cooperation among scientists.

Tu Changwang said, "As a Chinese scientific worker, I regret that scientists from the People's Republic of China were not invited to attend this conference, and the issue of prohibiting atomic weapons, which is demanded by millions of ordinary people, was not included on the conference agenda."

Xinhua News Agency, August 23rd, Geneva dispatch: The European Information Bureau of the United Nations Secretariat issued a communique stating that after the conclusion of the International Conference on Peaceful Uses of Atomic Energy, experts from Canada, Czechoslovakia, France, the Soviet Union, the United Kingdom, and the United States will hold several closed-door meetings in Geneva to discuss technical aspects of ensuring the peaceful use of atomic energy.

The Soviet side's expert team included Academician D.V. Skobeltsyn, Corresponding Member of the Soviet Academy of Sciences V.S. Yemelyanov, Academician A.P. Vinogradov, and others.

### 涂长望、柏贺浦谈和平利用原子能国际会议　苏美英捷等六国专家讨论和平利用原子能的技术问题

1955-08-25
第4版()
专栏：

　　涂长望、柏贺浦谈和平利用原子能国际会议
　　苏美英捷等六国专家讨论和平利用原子能的技术问题
    新华社二十三日讯　据塔斯社维也纳二十三日讯：世界和平理事会书记处报道，伦敦大学教授柏贺浦和世界科学工作者协会观察员涂长望就和平利用原子能国际会议闭幕发表谈话。
    柏贺浦教授说，这次会议之后，恶意的人不能再在全世界科学家之间制造不信任的障碍了。科学家已经互相会面和熟识起来。科学家们发现，他们有着共同的意见。这次会议是保证人类进步的那种精神的象征，这种精神就是为争取和平利用原子能可以带来的巨大福利而进行友好和平的竞赛。这次会议本身就大大有助于为消除对战争的恐惧创造必要的条件。
    涂长望指出，这次会议从科学的观点来看是有巨大的成就的。科学史证明，科学家之间进行密切的国际合作是科学进步的基础。他表示希望这次会议能够打开科学家进行更加密切的国际合作的道路。
    涂长望说，“作为一个中国科学工作者，我感到遗憾的是，中华人民共和国的科学家没有被邀请出席这次会议，在会议议程上也没有列入千百万普通人所要求的禁止原子武器的问题。”
    新华社二十三日讯　据塔斯社日内瓦二十二日讯：联合国秘书处欧洲新闻局发表公报说：在和平利用原子能国际会议结束以后，加拿大、捷克斯洛伐克、法国、苏联、英国和美国的专家将在日内瓦举行几天不公开的会议，讨论保证和平利用原子能的技术方面的问题。
    苏联方面参加专家小组工作的包括德·维·斯科贝尔琴院士，苏联科学院通讯院士维·斯·叶麦利雅诺夫，亚·普·维诺格拉多夫院士等人。
