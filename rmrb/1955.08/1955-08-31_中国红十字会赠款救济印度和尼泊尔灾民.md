Title: China Red Cross Grants Aid to Disaster Victims in India and Nepal
Author:
Date: 1955-08-31
Page: 1

Xinhua News Agency, August 30th: India's Uttar Pradesh, Bihar, West Bengal, and Assam provinces have recently experienced successive floods, affecting approximately 19 million residents. To aid the affected residents, the Red Cross Society of China, on August 29th, donated RMB 100,000 (equivalent to 191,570 Indian Rupees) to the Indian Prime Minister's National Relief Fund through Yuan Zhongxian, the Chinese Ambassador to India. Simultaneously, Li Dequan, President of the Red Cross Society of China, also sent separate telegrams to the Indian Prime Minister's National Relief Fund and Mrs. Rajkumari Amrit Kaur, President of the Indian Red Cross Society, expressing condolences to the disaster victims.

Furthermore, several provinces in Nepal have suffered from drought, with 3 million residents facing famine. The Red Cross Society of China also donated RMB 50,000 to the Nepalese government on August 29th as funds for disaster relief.

### 中国红十字会赠款救济印度和尼泊尔灾民

1955-08-31
第1版()
专栏：

　　中国红十字会赠款救济印度和尼泊尔灾民
　　新华社三十日讯　印度北方省、比哈尔、西孟加拉和阿萨姆等省最近连续发生水灾，受灾居民约一千九百万人。为了援助受灾居民，中国红十字会在八月二十九日通过中国驻印度大使袁仲贤捐赠人民币十万元（折合十九万一千五百七十印度卢比）给印度总理全国救济基金会。同时，中国红十字会会长李德全并且分别致电印度总理全国救济基金会和印度红十字会主席拉志库马里·阿姆里特·考尔夫人对灾民表示慰问。
　　又讯尼泊尔若干省份遭受旱灾，有居民三百万人面临饥荒。中国红十字会也在八月二十九日捐赠人民币五万元给尼泊尔政府作为救济灾民的款项。
