Title: Prime Minister Nehru Reiterates Determination to Oppose Portuguese Colonial Rule
Author:
Date: 1955-08-24
Page: 4

　　Xinhua News Agency, New Delhi, August 23rd: Indian Prime Minister Nehru, speaking at a political conference in Sitapur, Uttar Pradesh, on the 21st, said on the Goa issue: "Since we are opposed to colonialism everywhere, we cannot tolerate the continued existence of colonial rule in a small part of India's territory. We do not covet Goa. This small piece of territory is nothing to this great nation. But land under foreign colonial rule, even if it is small, makes the situation different and is a constant irritation to India's self-respect and national interests. Especially if a hostile and reactionary country like Portugal continues to control this territory, it could become a source of danger."
    Nehru also accused the Portuguese of establishing a police state in Goa, where even slight opposition to their views would be punished with long prison sentences.

### 尼赫鲁总理重申决心反对葡萄牙殖民统治

1955-08-24
第4版()
专栏：

　　尼赫鲁总理重申决心反对葡萄牙殖民统治
    新华社新德里二十三日电　印度总理尼赫鲁二十一日在北方省西塔普举行的一个政治会议上，在谈到果阿问题时说：“由于我们是反对任何地方的殖民主义的，我们不可能容忍在印度的一小部分领土上继续存在着殖民统治。我们并不垂涎果阿。这一小块领土对于这个大国来说算不了什么。可是在外国殖民统治之下的土地，即是很小的，就使情况有所不同，并且对于印度的自尊心和国家的利益来说经常是一种刺激。特别是如果像葡萄牙这样敌对的和反动的国家继续控制这块领土的话，它可能成为危险的泉源。”
    尼赫鲁还指责葡萄牙人在果阿建立了警察国家，甚至略微表示反对他们的意见就要被判处长期徒刑。
