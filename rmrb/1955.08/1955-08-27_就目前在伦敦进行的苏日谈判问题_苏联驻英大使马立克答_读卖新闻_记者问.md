Title: On the Current Soviet-Japanese Negotiations in London: Soviet Ambassador to England Malik Answers Questions from "Yomiuri Shimbun" Reporter
Author:
Date: 1955-08-27
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, London, August 25th: TASS London, August 25th - "Yomiuri Shimbun" correspondent in London, Toshio Shimizu, raised many questions with the Soviet Ambassador to England, Malik, regarding the current Soviet-Japanese negotiations in London. Malik gave the following answers to Toshio Shimizu:

　　"I have received your letter and regret to inform you that I am too busy with work to address all the questions you are interested in detail one by one.

　　As for the current Soviet-Japanese negotiations in London, their smooth progress and the signing of a peace treaty will establish relations between the Soviet Union and Japan on a lasting foundation of peace, which fully conforms to the interests of both countries and the requirements for consolidating universal peace and easing international tensions.

　　The restoration of normal relations between the two countries will promote the establishment of an atmosphere of mutual trust between the Soviet Union and Japan and the development of international economic cooperation between the two countries in the Far East.

　　The Soviet side's attitude in the current Soviet-Japanese negotiations is to promote a complete outcome of these negotiations for the benefit of the people of both countries."

### 就目前在伦敦进行的苏日谈判问题　苏联驻英大使马立克答“读卖新闻”记者问

1955-08-27
第4版()
专栏：

　　就目前在伦敦进行的苏日谈判问题
　　苏联驻英大使马立克答“读卖新闻”记者问
    新华社二十六日讯　塔斯社伦敦二十五日讯：日本“读卖新闻”驻伦敦记者清水俊雄就目前在伦敦进行的苏日谈判向苏联驻英大使马立克提出许多问题。马立克向清水俊雄作了如下的答复：
    “我已经接到你的来信，非常遗憾地通知你，我因为工作很忙不能详尽地一一谈到你所感兴趣的问题。
    至于目前在伦敦进行的苏日谈判，它的顺利进行和和约的签订将会使苏联和日本的关系建立在持久的和平的基础上，这完全符合两国的利益和巩固普遍和平以及和缓国际紧张局势的要求。
    两国正常关系的恢复将会促进苏联和日本建立相互信任的气氛和两国在远东的国际经济合作的发展。
    苏联方面在现在的苏日谈判中所采取的态度是：为两国人民的利益促使这次谈判取得完满的结果。”
