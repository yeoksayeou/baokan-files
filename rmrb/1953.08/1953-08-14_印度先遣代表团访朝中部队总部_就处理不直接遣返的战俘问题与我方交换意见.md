Title: Indian Advance Delegation Visits Headquarters of Chinese People's Volunteers in Korea; Exchanges Views with Our Side on the Issue of Handling POWs Not Directly Repatriated
Author:
Date: 1953-08-14
Page: 1
Xinhua News Agency, Pyongyang, August 13th: The Indian advance delegation, consisting of twelve members, led by Mr. R.K. Nehru, Secretary of External Affairs of the Indian Ministry of Foreign Affairs, and Major General Thorat, Chief of Staff of the Indian Army, visited the headquarters of the Korean People's Army and the Chinese People's Volunteers in Pyongyang on the morning of the 12th at 10:30 AM, to make necessary preparations for India's participation in the Neutral Nations Repatriation Commission. Vice Marshal Choi Yong-kun, Deputy Commander-in-Chief of the Korean People's Army Supreme Headquarters, and General Li Zhimin, Director of the Political Department of the Chinese People's Volunteers, received all members of the Indian advance delegation. During the reception, Vice Marshal Choi Yong-kun delivered a speech on behalf of the headquarters, warmly welcoming and thanking Mr. R.K. Nehru and the Indian advance delegation for their visit. Mr. R.K. Nehru then delivered a speech, expressing his gratitude for the meeting. He said: According to the provisions of the Korean Armistice Agreement, India has assumed certain responsibilities. These responsibilities are heavy. But India is willing to assume these responsibilities, full of hope, and believes that doing so will not only lead to peace, unification, and freedom in Korea, but also to peace for all mankind.

During the reception, the two sides also discussed issues related to the Neutral Nations Repatriation Commission's handling of POWs not directly repatriated. The reception lasted approximately one hour.

From 12:30 PM to 3:20 PM, the responsible persons designated by the headquarters of the Korean People's Army and the Chinese People's Volunteers and Mr. R.K. Nehru and Major General Thorat exchanged views on specific issues related to the handling of POWs not directly repatriated. Subsequently, they divided into three groups to exchange views on logistics, press coverage, and medical and Red Cross services related to the handling of POWs not directly repatriated.

The Minister of Foreign Affairs of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea, Nam Il, and Vice Minister of Foreign Affairs, Li Dong-geon, received Mr. R.K. Nehru at approximately 12:00 PM. In the afternoon, the Indian advance delegation also met with the press in Pyongyang and held talks. Mr. R.K. Nehru and Major General Thorat also took a car tour of Pyongyang.

In the evening, the headquarters of the Korean People's Army and the Chinese People's Volunteers hosted the Indian advance delegation at the Moranbong Underground Theater in Pyongyang to watch performances by the Korean National Art Theater and the National Theater, and hosted a banquet for the Indian advance delegation. At the banquet, Vice Marshal Choi Yong-kun and General Li Zhimin delivered speeches respectively, welcoming and expressing sympathy to the Indian advance delegation, and congratulating the advance delegation on the successful completion of their work in Pyongyang. Mr. R.K. Nehru delivered a speech, thanking the headquarters of the Korean People's Army and the Chinese People's Volunteers for their hospitality.

After the banquet, the Indian advance delegation traveled by car to Pyongyang Railway Station, preparing to go to Panmunjom via Kaesong, and then return to India via Tokyo. Representatives from the headquarters of the Korean People's Army and the Chinese People's Volunteers went to Pyongyang Railway Station to see them off. The train attendants, in order to express their sympathy to the advance delegation, decorated the carriages with a large number of flowers.

Xinhua News Agency, Kaesong, August 13th: The Indian advance delegation, making necessary preparations for India's participation in the Neutral Nations Repatriation Commission, returned to Kaesong at 3:45 PM on August 13th after visiting the headquarters of the Korean People's Army and the Chinese People's Volunteers.

Lieutenant General Li Sang-cho, Chief Commissioner of the Korean-Chinese side of the Korean Military Armistice Commission in Kaesong, and Commissioners Major General Park Il-young, Major General Choi Yong-han, General Ding Guoyu, and General Chai Chengwen hosted a farewell banquet for the Indian advance delegation, and introduced the Polish and Czechoslovak representatives of the Neutral Nations Supervisory Commission to meet with the Indian advance delegation.

The Indian advance delegation left Kaesong at 5:05 PM on the 13th. Our side, Lieutenant General Li Sang-cho and General Ding Guoyu, accompanied them all the way to Panmunjom.

### 印度先遣代表团访朝中部队总部  就处理不直接遣返的战俘问题与我方交换意见

1953-08-14
第1版()
专栏：

    印度先遣代表团访朝中部队总部
    就处理不直接遣返的战俘问题与我方交换意见
    【新华社平壤十三日电】为印度参加中立国遣返委员会作必要准备工作的印度先遣代表团一行十二人，在印度外交部外事秘书鲁·克·尼赫鲁先生、印度陆军参谋长托拉特少将率领下，十二日上午十时半在平壤访问朝鲜人民军和中国人民志愿军总部。朝鲜人民军最高司令部副司令官崔庸健次帅和中国人民志愿军政治部主任李志民将军，接见了印度先遣代表团全体人员。接见时，崔庸健次帅代表总部致词，热烈欢迎和感谢鲁·克·尼赫鲁先生及印度先遣代表团一行的访问。鲁·克·尼赫鲁先生接着致词，对这次会见表示感谢。他说：根据朝鲜停战协定的条款，印度担负了某些责任。这些责任是很重的。但是印度很愿意担负这些责任，对此满怀着希望，并相信这样做不但会导致朝鲜的和平、统一与自由，而且会导致全人类的和平。
    接见时，双方并交谈了有关中立国遣返委员会处理不直接遣返的战俘的问题。接见历时约一小时。
    十二时三十分到下午三时二十分，朝鲜人民军和中国人民志愿军总部指定的总部负责人和鲁·克·尼赫鲁先生、托拉特少将，就有关处理不直接遣返的战俘的具体问题交换了意见。此后，分三个小组，对有关处理不直接遣返的战俘事宜的后方勤务、新闻报道以及医药与红十字会服务的问题，分别交换了意见。
    朝鲜民主主义人民共和国外务相南日、外务省副相李东建，在十二时许接见了鲁·克·尼赫鲁先生。下午，印度先遣代表团并和平壤的新闻界会见，进行了谈话。鲁·克·尼赫鲁先生、托拉特少将并乘车参观平壤市区。
    晚间，朝鲜人民军和中国人民志愿军总部在平壤牡丹峰地下剧场招待印度先遣代表团观看朝鲜国立艺术剧场和国立剧场的演出，并设宴招待印度先遣代表团。在宴会上，崔庸健次帅、李志民将军分别致词，对印度先遣代表团表示欢迎与慰问，并祝贺先遣代表团在平壤工作的顺利完成。鲁·克·尼赫鲁先生致词，对朝鲜人民军和中国人民志愿军总部的招待表示感谢。
    宴会毕，印度先遣代表团一行乘车赴平壤火车站，准备经开城前往板门店，然后再经东京返回印度。前往平壤火车站送行的有朝鲜人民军和中国人民志愿军总部的代表等。先遣代表团所乘的列车的乘务员们，为了对先遣代表团表示慰问，在车厢中布置了大量的鲜花。
    【新华社开城十三日电】为印度参加中立国遣返委员会作必要准备的印度先遣代表团一行，在访问朝鲜人民军与中国人民志愿军总部后于八月十三日下午三时四十五分返抵开城。
    在开城朝鲜军事停战委员会朝中方面首席委员李相朝中将及委员朴一英少将、崔龙汉少将、丁国钰将军、柴成文将军设宴欢饯印度先遣代表团，并介绍中立国监察委员会波兰和捷克斯洛伐克代表与印度先遣代表团见面。
    印度先遣代表团于十三日下午五时零五分离开开城，我方李相朝中将与丁国钰将军一路陪送至板门店。
