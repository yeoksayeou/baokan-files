    Arab League Pact
      The "Arab League Pact," officially the "Pact of the League of Arab States," is the pact signed by Arab countries when they formed the "Arab League" in March 1945. The pact stipulates that the purpose of the "Arab League" is "to make the relations between member states closer, and to coordinate their political activities in order to achieve close cooperation between member states; to defend their independence and sovereignty; and to comprehensively consider the affairs and interests of Arab countries." The "Arab League Pact" stipulates that every independent Arab country has the right to join the League. The countries participating in this league are Egypt, Saudi Arabia, Jordan, Syria, Lebanon, Iraq, Yemen, Libya, and Sudan. The pact also stipulates that the headquarters of the "Arab League" is located in Cairo, and regular meetings are held every March and October. (True)

1958-07-22
第4版()

    阿拉伯联盟公约
      “阿拉伯联盟公约”全称“阿拉伯国家联盟公约”，是阿拉伯国家在1945年3月组成“阿拉伯联盟”时所签订的公约。公约规定，“阿拉伯联盟”的宗旨是“使会员国间的关系更加密切，并为了实现会员国之间的紧密合作而协调他们的政治活动；保卫他们的独立和主权；以及全面考虑阿拉伯国家的事务和利益”。“阿拉伯联盟公约”规定每一个独立的阿拉伯国家都有权加入联盟。参加这个联盟的国家有埃及、沙特阿拉伯、约旦、叙利亚、黎巴嫩、伊拉克、也门、利比亚和苏丹九个国家。公约还规定“阿拉伯联盟”的总部设在开罗，每年3月和10月各举行例会一次。  （真）
