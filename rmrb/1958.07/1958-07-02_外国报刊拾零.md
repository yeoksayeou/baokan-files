Foreign Press Clippings

    Sleeping in the Same Bed, Dreaming Different Dreams

    The United States and Britain do not agree on how to deal with France. Washington is willing to give de Gaulle all possible assistance in his arduous task. Britain opposes Washington giving de Gaulle any assistance unless it is first consulted with London.

    The main reason is that Britain wants to maintain its special status with the United States and is not eager to see France restore its position as one of the (Western) Big Three.

        ——American "Newsweek"
            (June 16, 1958)

    Skillful in Extortion

    Ordinary voters in the United States will soon be asked to bear higher political expenses. A campaign is currently underway to persuade every American who is not affiliated with a party to become a party donor. The slogan of this campaign is: "Don't pass the buck, cough up a buck!"
      ——"U.S. News & World
      Report" (May 30, 1958) (with illustration)

### 外国报刊拾零

1958-07-02
第6版()
专栏：外国报刊拾零

    外国报刊拾零
    同床异梦
    美国和英国在对待法国问题上意见并不一致。华盛顿愿意对戴高乐的艰巨任务给予一切援助。英国反对华盛顿给予戴高乐任何援助，除非事先同伦敦协商。
    主要的原因是：英国希望维持她同美国的特殊地位，并不急于看到法国恢复（西方）三强的地位。
        ——美国“新闻周刊”
            （1958年6月16日）
    搜刮有术
    美国的普通选民，不久将被要求负担更高的政治费用。目前正在开展一项运动，劝告每一个没有参加党派的美国人都必须成为政党的捐款人。这项运动的口号是：“不要把责任推给别人，请拿出一块钱来！”
      ——“美国新闻与世界
      报道”（1958年5月30日）（附图片）
