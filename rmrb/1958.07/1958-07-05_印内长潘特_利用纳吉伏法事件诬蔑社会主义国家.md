Title: Indian Home Minister Pant Uses the Nagy Execution to Smear Socialist Countries
Author: 
Date: 1958-07-05
Page: 6
Xinhua News Agency, New Delhi, July 2nd—According to a report by the Indian Press Trust, Indian Home Minister Pant, speaking at a mass rally in Ferozepore, Punjab on July 1st, attacked socialist countries and the Communist Party regarding the Hungarian government's execution of Imre Nagy, organizer of the counter-revolutionary rebellion. Pant said, "Stalinism has made a comeback within communist countries. Countries that once gave people freedom are now taking it back. The urgent demands of Hungary and Poland for independence have been thwarted." Pant also said something about "the slogan of 'letting a hundred flowers bloom' is now dead." He further said that the Indian communists had recently guaranteed to adopt peaceful methods. "But what we are seeing shows that they (communists worldwide) have deviated from this path. A recent example is the killing of Nagy."

### 印内长潘特  利用纳吉伏法事件诬蔑社会主义国家

1958-07-05
第6版()
专栏：

    印内长潘特
    利用纳吉伏法事件诬蔑社会主义国家
    新华社新德里2日电　据印度报业托辣斯报道，印度内政部长潘特1日在旁遮普邦费罗吉波尔地方举行的群众大会上发表讲话时，就匈牙利政府处决反革命暴乱组织者纳吉一事，攻击社会主义国家，攻击共产党。潘特说：“在共产党国家内部，斯大林主义已经卷土重来。曾经给予人民以自由的国家目前正在收回自由。匈牙利和波兰希望得到独立的迫切要求受到了阻挠。”潘特还说什么“‘百花齐放’的口号现在已经死亡了。”他又说，印度的共产党人最近保证采取和平方法。“但是我们所看到的事情表明，他们（全世界的共产党人）已经离开了这条道路。最近的一个例子就是纳吉被杀害。”
