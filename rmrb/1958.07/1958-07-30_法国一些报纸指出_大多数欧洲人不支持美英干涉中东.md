Title: Some French Newspapers Point Out That Most Europeans Do Not Support U.S. and British Intervention in the Middle East
Author:
Date: 1958-07-30
Page: 3
Xinhua News Agency, Paris, 29th. The "Dauphiné Libéré," published in Grenoble, believes that De Gaulle will "act as the mouthpiece of Europe" at the summit conference. The newspaper wrote: "Everyone knows that most Europeans do not support Anglo-Saxon military initiatives (referring to the armed intervention in Lebanon and Jordan)... Some Mediterranean countries believe that a new policy more in line with the actual situation should be implemented in the Middle East."

Another local newspaper, "Libre Poitou," commentator Henri Viaud pointed out the divisions among Western countries. He said, "The West has not reached a consensus on action, and France is blameless." The commentator wrote: "Washington and London still cannot adapt to the new style of our national policy. For many years, our allies have been accustomed to first consulting internally and then presenting the prepared resolutions to the French government. Now everything has changed."

### 法国一些报纸指出  大多数欧洲人不支持美英干涉中东

1958-07-30
第3版()
专栏：

    法国一些报纸指出
    大多数欧洲人不支持美英干涉中东
    新华社29日讯巴黎消息：在格累诺贝尔出版的“多菲奈自由报”认为，在政府首脑会议上戴高乐会“起欧洲传声筒的作用”。报纸写道：“大家知道，大多数欧洲人都不支持盎格鲁—萨克逊的军事倡议（指对黎巴嫩和约旦的武装干涉）……一些地中海国家认为，在中东应当执行一种更符合实际情况的新政策”。
    另一家地方报纸“自由的波瓦图报”评论员安里维奥指出了西方国家的分歧。他说，“西方没有达成一致行动，法国是无过的”。评论员写道：“华盛顿和伦敦还不能适应于我国民族政策的新风格。我们的盟国很多年以来习惯于首先在内部进行协商，然后再向法国政府提出已准备好了的决议。现在一切都改变了。”
