Title: Most North Atlantic Group Countries Unwilling to Follow the United States into War
Author:
Date: 1958-07-18
Page: 4
Xinhua News Agency, July 17th—According to a dispatch from London on the 16th by United Press International reporter Sunderland, the reaction throughout Western Europe to the United States sending troops to invade Lebanon is "generally in favor but with worries." He said that in the capitals of the Atlantic Group countries, "officials are taking a hands-off approach to the whole thing." Most countries believe this is a bad thing and are unwilling to get involved.

According to an Associated Press report from Paris, the Permanent Representatives Council of the North Atlantic Group discussed the events in Lebanon and Iraq on the 16th, and people generally adopted a "wait and see attitude" towards the development of the situation.

### 北大西洋集团多数国家  不愿跟随美国卷进战争

1958-07-18
第4版()
专栏：

    北大西洋集团多数国家
    不愿跟随美国卷进战争
    新华社17日讯　据合众国际社记者桑德兰16日自伦敦发出的一条消息说，整个西欧对美国派兵侵略黎巴嫩一事的反应是“一般赞成但是又担心”。他说，在大西洋集团国家首都，“官员们对这整个事情采取不插手的态度”。大多数国家认为这是一件坏事，不愿意卷进去。
    另据美联社自巴黎报道说，北大西洋集团常任代表理事会16日讨论了黎巴嫩和伊拉克事件，人们对于局势的发展普遍采取一种“等着瞧的态度”。
