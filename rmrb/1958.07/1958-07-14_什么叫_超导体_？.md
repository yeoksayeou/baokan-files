Title: What is a "Superconductor"?
Author: Wu Yuan
Date: 1958-07-14
Page: 7

What is a "Superconductor"?

　　When an electric current passes through a metal, it encounters a kind of resistance. Different metals have different resistances. For example, copper has relatively low resistance, so it is commonly used to make electrical wires. Resistance is also related to temperature. At ordinary temperatures, when the temperature decreases, the resistance also gradually decreases. Many metals (such as pure lead, etc.), when the temperature drops to minus 260-odd degrees Celsius, their resistance will sharply decrease to near zero. We call metals that lose resistance at low temperatures "superconductors."

　　If "cryotrons" made of superconductors are used in electronic computers, the calculation speed can be greatly increased, estimated to be increased to more than 10 million times per second. With further development, superconductors may become important materials in power systems and radio electronics.

　　　　　　　　　　　　　　 (Wu Yuan)

### 什么叫“超导体”？
吴元
1958-07-14
第7版()
专栏：科学知识

    什么叫“超导体”？
    电流在通过金属体时，要受到一种阻滞作用。不同金属的阻滞作用不同，如铜的阻滞作用就比较小，所以通常都用铜来做电线。阻滞作用和温度也有关系。在普通温度下，当温度下降时，阻滞作用也缓缓下降。很多金属（如纯铅等）当温度下降到摄氏零下二百六十几度时，它的阻滞作用会急剧地下降到接近于零。这种在低温下失去阻滞作用的金属，我们称它为“超导体”。
    电子计算机中如果使用了超导体做的“冷子管”，就可以大大提高计算速度，据估计可以提高到每秒一千万次以上。超导体进一步发展以后，将可能成为电力系统中和无线电电子学中的重要材料。
                      （吴元）
