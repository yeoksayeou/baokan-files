Title: Indian Newspapers Warn US-British Aggressors: Armed Suppression Cannot Stifle Awakened People
Author:
Date: 1958-07-24
Page: 3
Xinhua News Agency Report: Indian newspapers continue to severely condemn the aggressive actions of US and British troops in Lebanon and Jordan. The "Bombay Chronicle" pointed out in an editorial on the 19th that the US and British actions in the Middle East are "clearly a division of labor agreed upon." The Hindi daily "Hindustan Daily" warned that Britain had not learned any lessons from its failed aggression against Egypt in 1956. The "Deccan Herald" said on the 18th that President Eisenhower's and US Representative to the United Nations Lodge's justifications for US aggression in the Middle East are "full of hypocrisy and an insult to the United Nations and world opinion."

    The "National Herald" accused "the British cabinet of attempting to re-enact in West Asia what it did on the Suez Canal issue.
    The newspapers also praised the strong support given by socialist countries to Arab nations. The Hindi daily "Today's News" pointed out that the new republic quickly gained recognition from socialist and nationalist countries, while "other countries are systematically stifling the newborn infant." The newspaper emphasized that the Republic of Iraq "has provided great strength for the awakening of the people of West Asia. It will never be suppressed." The Bengali daily "Epoch-Making Times" warned Western countries not to ignore the Soviet Union's warnings.
    This newspaper reports, according to Calcutta news: The American-run "Statesman" said on the 18th that "It would be a dangerous mistake to link the recent developments in Iraq with the ten-week civil war in Lebanon. The American military intervention in Lebanon has neither a political nor a moral basis. The Soviet government's statement correctly pointed out that 'no one is threatening the territorial integrity and independence of Lebanon.'"
    The "Indian Nation," one of the two major English dailies in Patna, Bihar, called the Western intervention in the Middle East a "wrong act" and an "indefensible action."

### 印度报纸向美英侵略者提出警告  武装镇压不能扼杀觉醒了的人民

1958-07-24
第3版()
专栏：

    印度报纸向美英侵略者提出警告
    武装镇压不能扼杀觉醒了的人民
    据新华社讯　印度各报继续严厉谴责美英两国军队在黎巴嫩和约报”19日在社论中指出，美英两国在中东的侵略行动“显然是经过同意的分工”。印地文日报“印度斯坦日报”警告说，英国并没有从它在1956年侵略埃及的失败中取得任何教训。“德康先驱报”18日说，美国总统艾森豪威尔和美国驻联合国代表洛奇对美国在中东的侵略行为所进行的辩解“充满了虚伪的气味，并且是对联合国和世界舆论的污辱”。
      “国民先驱报”指责“英国内阁企图在西亚续演它曾经在苏伊士运河问题上所作的事情。
    各报还赞扬社会主义国家给予阿拉伯国家的有力支持。印地文日报“今天报”指出，这个新共和国一方面迅速得到了社会主义国家和民族主义国家的承认，而“另一方面的国家则在有计划地扼杀这个新生的婴儿”。这家报纸强调说，伊拉克共和国“已经为西亚人民的觉醒提供了巨大力量。它是决不会被镇压下去的”。孟加拉文日报“划时代报”警告西方国家不要忽视苏联的警告。
    本报讯　据加尔各答消息：美国人所办的“政治家报”18日说，“如果把伊拉克最近的发展同黎巴嫩已经有十周时间的内战连系起来，那将是一个危险的错误。美国对黎巴嫩的军事干涉既没有政治根据，也没有道义根据。苏联政府的声明正确地指出，‘谁也没有威胁黎巴嫩的领土完整和独立。’”
    比哈尔邦巴特那地方两家主要英文日报之一“印度民族日报”把西方对中东的干涉称为“错误行为”和“无法辩解的行动”。
