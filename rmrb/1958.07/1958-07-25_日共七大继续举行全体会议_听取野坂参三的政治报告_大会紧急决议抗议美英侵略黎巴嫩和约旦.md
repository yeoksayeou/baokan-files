Title: Japanese Communist Party's Seventh Congress Continues Plenary Session, Hears Political Report by Nosaka Sanzo; Congress Passes Emergency Resolution Protesting U.S. and British Aggression Against Lebanon and Jordan
Author:
Date: 1958-07-25
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, July 24th, Tokyo dispatch: "Akahata" reported today that the Japanese Communist Party's Seventh Congress, at its plenary session on the afternoon of the 23rd, heard the political report by Nosaka Sanzo, First Secretary of the Japanese Communist Party, and the supplementary report by Shiga Yoshio, member of the Standing Committee of the JCP Central Committee, regarding the two declarations of the Moscow meeting.

    The meeting passed an emergency resolution protesting the armed aggression of Lebanon and Jordan by the U.S. and British imperialists.

    At the meeting held on the morning of the 23rd, Nosaka Sanzo read out congratulatory telegrams and letters from fraternal parties in various countries, including the Communist Party of China, the Workers' Party of Korea, the French Communist Party, and the American Communist Party, to which all participants responded with warm applause.

    Subsequently, congratulatory letters from the Japan Socialist Party and the Okinawa People's Party to the Congress were read out at the meeting.

    Iwai Akira, Secretary-General of the General Council of Trade Unions of Japan (Sohyo), Yaoita Tadashi, representative of the All-Japan Farmers' Association Federation, Yasui Kaoru, Director of the Japan Council Against Atomic and Hydrogen Bombs, Yanagida Kenjuro, Permanent Director of the Japan Peace Committee, Tanaka Orinosuke, Secretary-General of the National Federation for Buraku Liberation, Namba Hideo, Vice Chairman of the Japan National Relief Association, Kono Sadzaburo, Chairman of the National Federation of Commerce and Industry Organizations, and representatives of the Japan Democratic Youth League, who were invited to participate in the Seventh Congress of the Japanese Communist Party, successively delivered speeches at the meeting to congratulate the convening of the Congress.

    In his congratulatory speech, Iwai Akira said that he believed that in the future struggle for Japan's independence and peace, the Japanese Communist Party would always stand at the forefront of the people. Iwai pointed out that the direction of the struggle of "Sohyo" and the JCP is consistent. Iwai expressed that "Sohyo" is willing to move forward in a direction that seeks consistency with the Japanese Communist Party.

    Xinhua News Agency, July 23rd dispatch: According to TASS Tokyo, July 23rd: The delegation of the Communist Party of the Soviet Union, composed of Mitin, member of the Central Committee of the CPSU, and Satiukov, editor-in-chief of "Pravda," arrived in Tokyo today at the invitation of the Central Committee of the Japanese Communist Party.

    The CPSU delegation, as guests of the Japanese Communist Party, is attending the Seventh Congress of the Japanese Communist Party, which officially opened today. (Attached picture)

### 日共七大继续举行全体会议  听取野坂参三的政治报告  大会紧急决议抗议美英侵略黎巴嫩和约旦

1958-07-25
第4版()
专栏：

    日共七大继续举行全体会议
    听取野坂参三的政治报告
    大会紧急决议抗议美英侵略黎巴嫩和约旦
    新华社24日讯　东京消息：“赤旗报”今天报道，日本共产党第七次代表大会在23日下午的全体会议上，听取了日本共产党第一书记野坂参三的政治报告和日共中央常任干部会委员志贺义雄的补充报告——关于莫斯科会议两个宣言的报告。
    会上通过了一项紧急决议，抗议美、英帝国主义者武装侵略黎巴嫩和约旦。
    在23日上午举行的会议上，野坂参三宣读了中国共产党、朝鲜劳动党、法国共产党和美国共产党等各国兄弟党的贺电和贺信，全体与会者报以热烈的掌声。
    接着，会上宣读了日本社会党和冲绳人民党致大会的贺信。
    应邀参加日本共产党第七次代表大会的日本工会总评议会事务局长岩井章、全日本农民协会联合会代表八百板正、日本禁止原子弹和氢弹协议会理事长安井郁、日本和平委员会常任理事柳田谦十郎、全国部落解放同盟书记长田中织之进、日本国民救援会副会长难波英夫、全国工商团体联合会会长河野贞三郎和日本民主青年团的代表等相继在会上致词，祝贺大会的召开。
    岩井章致贺词说，他相信在今后的争取日本独立与和平的斗争中，日本共产党将始终站在人民的最前列。岩井指出，“总评”和日共的斗争方向是一致的。岩井表示，“总评”今后愿意向着寻求同日本共产党一致的方向前进。
    新华社23日讯　据塔斯社东京23日讯：以苏联共产党中央委员会委员米丁和“真理报”总编辑萨丘科夫组成的苏联共产党代表团应日本共产党中央委员会的邀请今天到达东京。
    苏共代表团作为日本共产党的来宾，出席今天正式开会的日本共产党第七次代表大会。（附图片）
