Title: Tunisian "Action" Weekly Points Out that North African People Will Abandon Their Last Illusions About the U.S. and Britain
Author:
Date: 1958-07-23
Page: 3
    Xinhua News Agency, Tunis: The Tunisian ruling party Neo-Destour's organ "Action" weekly condemned the U.S. and British armed intervention in the Middle East on the 20th. It said that the U.S. and British intervention is actually aimed at Iraq; but "neither the U.S. Marines nor the British paratroopers can rebuild a regime of the Baghdad Pact type in Iraq."
    The weekly pointed out that the people who are struggling to win or complete national independence, especially the people of North African countries, will "without difficulty" "draw their own lessons" from the U.S. and British armed intervention in the Arab nationalist movement and will abandon their "last illusions" about the U.S. and Britain.

### 突尼斯“行动”周刊指出  北非人民将抛弃对美英的最后幻想

1958-07-23
第3版()
专栏：

    突尼斯“行动”周刊指出
    北非人民将抛弃对美英的最后幻想
    据新华社讯  突尼斯消息：突尼斯执政党新宪政党的机关刊“行动”周刊20日谴责美英对中东进行武装干涉。它说，美英的干涉实际上是针对伊拉克的；但是“不论是美国的海军陆战队，还是英国的伞兵，都绝不能再在伊拉克重建起一个属于巴格达条约类型的政权”。
    这家周刊指出，正在为争取或是完成民族的独立而斗争的人民，特别是北非各国人民，将“毫不困难”地从美英武装干涉阿拉伯民族主义运动的事件中“得出自己的教训”，并且将抛弃他们对美英所抱的“最后的幻想”。
