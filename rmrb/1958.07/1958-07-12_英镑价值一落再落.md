Title: The Value of the Pound Continues to Fall
Author:
Date: 1958-07-12
Page: 6

    Xinhua News Agency, London, July 10th. British Financial Secretary Reginald Maudling explained the devaluation of the pound in a written reply to Conservative Party members of parliament today. He said: "If the domestic purchasing power of the pound in 1925 is taken as twenty shillings, the corresponding figures for different periods thereafter are as follows: 22 shillings and 7 pence in 1938, 13 shillings and 9 pence in 1945, 8 shillings and 10 pence in 1955, and 8 shillings in May 1958.”

### 英镑价值一落再落

1958-07-12
第6版()
专栏：

    英镑价值一落再落
    新华社伦敦10日电　英国会计大臣雷金纳德·莫德林今天在对保守党议员的书面答复中说明了英镑贬值的情况。他说：“如果以1925年英镑的国内购买力作为二十个先令，在那以后不同时期的相应的数字如下：1938年二十二先令七便士，1945年十三先令九便士，1955年八先令十便士，1958年5月则为八先令。”
