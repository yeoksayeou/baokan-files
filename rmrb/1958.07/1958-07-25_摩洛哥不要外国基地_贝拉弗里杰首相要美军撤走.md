Title: Morocco Does Not Want Foreign Bases: Prime Minister Ben Barka Wants US Troops to Leave

Author:

Date: 1958-07-25
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, New York, July 24th: Tass reports: Moroccan Prime Minister Ben Barka, in an interview with the "New York Times" correspondent in Rabat, said that Morocco does not approve of "the United States and Britain interfering in the internal affairs of Lebanon and Jordan."

Regarding the issue of American military bases in Morocco, Ben Barka said that the removal of all foreign bases in Morocco (including American bases) has become a matter of urgency. Ben Barka said, "If the French should abandon their bases, then the American guests should follow their example."

Ben Barka continued, the negotiations on the temporary legal status of the American bases "have been delayed for too long, to the point where they have lost their value. I do not know if we should still conduct negotiations regarding the temporary legal status, since the American side has not paid attention to it (referring to the United States not responding to Morocco's note on the legal status of the base personnel)."

### 摩洛哥不要外国基地  贝拉弗里杰首相要美军撤走

1958-07-25
第5版()
专栏：

    摩洛哥不要外国基地
    贝拉弗里杰首相要美军撤走
    据新华社24日讯　据塔斯社纽约讯：摩洛哥首相贝拉弗里杰同“纽约时报”驻拉巴特记者谈话时说，摩洛哥不赞成“美国和英国干涉黎巴嫩和约旦的内政”。
    贝拉弗里杰在谈到美国在摩洛哥的军事基地问题时说，撤除在摩洛哥的所有外国基地（包括美国基地在内）的问题已经成为刻不容缓的事了。贝拉弗里杰说，“如果法国人应该放弃基地，那么美国客人也应该仿效他们的榜样”。
    贝拉弗里杰继续说，关于美国基地临时法律地位的谈判“由于拖延得太久，以至失去了它的价值。我不知道，既然美国方面没有予以注意（指美国没有答复摩洛哥关于基地人员法律地位问题的照会），我们是否仍然要进行关于临时法律地位的谈判”。
