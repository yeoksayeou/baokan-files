Title: The Soviet Union Cautiously Welcomes Israel's Election Plan
Author: Lin Jiaoming
Date: 1989-05-31
Page: 3

	Xinhua News Agency, Cairo, May 31st.

	The Soviet Union's foreign minister, Eduard Shevardnadze, has expressed "cautious welcome" for Israel's plan to hold elections in the occupied territories.

	The statement by the Soviet foreign minister was made in response to a broadcast by Israeli radio.

	Shevardnadze said that the plan to hold elections in the West Bank and Gaza Strip, followed by a five-year transitional period of Palestinian self-rule, is "worthy of attention and study."

	Observers here noted that this is the first time the Soviet Union has publicly expressed a cautious welcome for Israeli Prime Minister Yitzhak Shamir's election plan. However, it does not mean that the Soviet Union believes the Shamir plan can be implemented quickly.

### 苏谨慎欢迎以选举计划
林皎明
1989-05-31
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

　　苏谨慎欢迎以选举计划
    本报开罗５月３１日电  记者林皎明报道：据此间电台消息，苏联外长谢瓦尔德纳泽对以色列关于在被占领土举行选举的计划表示“谨慎的欢迎”。
    苏联外长的这一表态是对以色列电台发表的。
    谢瓦尔德纳泽说，在约旦河西岸和加沙地带进行选举，随后导致被占领土巴勒斯坦人为期５年的自治过渡期，这个计划“值得重视和研究”。
    此间人士注意到，这是苏联官方第一次公开对以色列沙米尔选举计划表示谨慎的欢迎立场，但它并不意味着苏联认为沙米尔计划可以很快实施。
