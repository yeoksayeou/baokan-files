Title: Changes in Global Oil Demand
Author:
Date: 1989-06-19
Edition: 3

	World oil demand has decreased from an average of 65.4 million barrels per day in 1979 to 63 million barrels per day in 1987.

	According to a report released by the International Energy Agency on June 18, industrialized countries account for the majority of global oil demand. In 1971, these countries consumed an average of 35.19 million barrels per day, while the global total was 49.6 million barrels per day.

	In 1987, the oil demand of these industrialized countries averaged 35.98 million barrels per day.

	The oil demand of developing countries and centrally planned economies increased from 14.4 million barrels per day in 1971 to 27 million barrels per day in 1987.

	In 1987, the Soviet Union's demand was 9.1 million barrels per day, accounting for 33.8% of the total oil demand of developing countries and centrally planned economies. China ranked second with a demand of 2.1 million barrels per day. Following were Mexico with 1.5 million barrels per day, Brazil with 1.3 million barrels per day, and India with 0.968 million barrels per day.

### 世界对石油需求量发生变化

1989-06-19
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

　　世界对石油需求量发生变化
    新华社巴黎６月１９日电  世界石油需求量由１９７９年的平均每天６５４０万桶降至１９８７年的平均每天６３００万桶。
    据国际能源机构６月１８日发表的一份报告说，工业化国家占世界石油总需求量的大部分，１９７１年为每天３５１９万桶，该年世界总需求量为每天４９６０万桶。
    １９８７年，这些国家的石油需求量平均每天为３５９８万桶。
    发展中国家和中央计划经济国家的石油需求量由１９７１年的每天１４４０万桶增至１９８７年的每天２７００万桶。
    １９８７年，苏联的需求量为每天９１０万桶，占发展中国家和中央计划经济国家的石油总需求量的３３．８％。中国占第二位，需求量每天为２１０万桶。以下依次为：墨西哥，每天为１５０万桶；巴西，每天为１３０万桶；印度，每天为９６．８万桶。
