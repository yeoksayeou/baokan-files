Title: Kenyan President Moi Hopes to See a Stable China; Ghana Condemns Western Intervention in China's Internal Affairs
Author:
Date: 1989-06-24
Edition: 3

Kenyan President Moi hopes to see a stable China. Ghana condemns Western intervention in China's internal affairs.

	NAIROBI, June 22 (Xinhua) -- Kenyan President Daniel arap Moi said today that Kenya hopes to see a stable China so that the Chinese government can concentrate on developing its national economy.

	Moi made the remarks when he met with the outgoing Chinese Ambassador to Kenya, Xue Mouhong.

	Moi also expressed his support for the Chinese government and hoped that China would continue to implement its policy of reform and opening up to the outside world.

	ACCRA, June 23 (Xinhua) -- Ghana Radio commented on June 22 on the recent suppression of the counter-revolutionary rebellion in China, saying that the strong reaction from the Western world is "clear evidence of a long-planned conspiracy by counter-revolutionary and foreign forces to destabilize China."

	The commentary pointed out that the Western world's interference cannot force a legitimate government to yield to an unimaginable chaotic situation.

### 莫伊总统希望看到中国稳定  加纳谴责西方干涉中国内政

1989-06-24
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

　　莫伊总统希望看到中国稳定  加纳谴责西方干涉中国内政
    新华社内罗毕６月２２日电  肯尼亚总统莫伊今天在这里说，肯尼亚希望看到中国稳定，以使中国政府能够集中力量发展国民经济。
    莫伊总统是在接见即将离任的中国驻肯尼亚大使薛谋洪时说这番话的。
    莫伊总统还说，他支持中国政府，并希望中国继续执行改革和对外部世界开放的政策。
    新华社阿克拉６月２３日电　加纳电台２２日就中国最近平息反革命暴乱事件发表评论说，西方世界对此事件作出的强烈反应“是反革命和外国力量企图使中国不稳定的蓄谋已久的计划的明证”。
    这一篇评论指出，西方世界的干涉无法迫使一个合法政府向不可思议的混乱局势屈服。
