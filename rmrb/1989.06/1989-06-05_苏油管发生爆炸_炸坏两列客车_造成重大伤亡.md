Title: Pipeline Explosion in the Soviet Union Damages Two Passenger Trains and Causes Major Casualties
Author:
Date: 1989-06-05
Edition: 3

A pipeline explosion occurred in the central Ural region of the Soviet Union on June 3, damaging two passenger trains and resulting in major casualties. Gorbachev and Ryzhkov, along with other Soviet leaders, flew to the scene early on June 4 to handle the aftermath.

According to Tass, at 23:14 Moscow time on June 3, a gas leak in a pipeline near the Chelyabinsk-Ufa railway line caused a powerful explosion and fire, which destroyed two passing passenger trains.

Also on the plane to the scene were the Chairman of the Council of Ministers of the Russian Federation, Vlasov, the Soviet Minister of Defense, Yazov, and the Minister of Health, Chazov.

The cause of the accident is under investigation.

### 苏油管发生爆炸  炸坏两列客车　造成重大伤亡

1989-06-05
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

　　苏油管发生爆炸
    炸坏两列客车　造成重大伤亡
    新华社莫斯科６月４日电  苏联中乌拉尔地区３日发生油管爆炸事件，炸坏两列客车，造成重大伤亡。戈尔巴乔夫和雷日科夫等苏联领导人４日晨飞往现场处理善后。
    据塔斯社报道，莫斯科时间３日２３时１４分，车里亚宾斯克—乌法铁路段旁的管道瓦斯溢漏，发生强烈爆炸并起火，此时经过的两列客车被炸坏。
    同机飞往现场的还有俄罗斯联邦部长会议主席弗拉索夫、苏联国际部长亚佐夫和卫生部长恰佐夫。
    事故原因在调查中。
