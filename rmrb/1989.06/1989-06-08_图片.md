Title: China's First "Hundred Dragons Inkstone" Completed
Author: Li Yongqing
Date: 1989-06-08
Edition: 4

    China's first "Hundred Dragons Inkstone of Famous Chinese Scenic Spots" was successfully carved by young craftsmen, including Liu Jihua, at the New Life Ceramics Factory in Baoding, Hebei Province. This giant inkstone, weighing 265 kilograms, is made from Yishi inkstone and features a map of the People's Republic of China. It is adorned with famous scenic spots from across the country, and 100 dragons are depicted playing among the clouds and waves.

    (Photograph by Li Yongqing, Xinhua News Agency)
