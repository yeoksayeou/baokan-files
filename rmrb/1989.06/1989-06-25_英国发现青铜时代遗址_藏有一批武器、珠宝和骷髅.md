Title: Bronze Age Site Discovered in Britain
Author:
Date: 1989-06-25
Edition: 3

British archaeologists announced on June 24 that they had discovered a Bronze Age site dating back 3,000 years, claiming it to be the largest prehistoric sacrificial site unearthed in Europe this century.

Archaeologists found a highly valuable underground storage pit near a newly constructed power station in Peterborough in eastern Britain. The pit contained nearly intact weapons, jewelry, and skeletons.

Most of the 30 sets of remains are well-preserved because they have been "pickled in peat" for thousands of years. Most of the items in the pit date from the late Bronze Age, with a small number from the early Iron Age.

Francis Pryor, who led the excavation, said, "This is the most important and exciting discovery. What we have found so far is just the tip of the iceberg."

More than 90% of the site remains to be excavated. The entire excavation process is expected to take about 30 years.

### 英国发现青铜时代遗址  藏有一批武器、珠宝和骷髅

1989-06-25
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

　　英国发现青铜时代遗址    
    藏有一批武器、珠宝和骷髅
    新华社伦敦６月２４日电  英国考古学家２４日宣称，发现了一个３０００年前青铜时代遗址，并称这是本世纪中在欧洲发掘出的最大的史前祭祀遗址。
    考古学家们在英国东部彼得伯勒的一座新建发电站附近发现了一个极富考古价值的地窖，里面藏有几乎完整无损的武器、珠宝和骷髅。
    ３０具遗体中的大部分目前保存完好，因为这些遗体在数千年中一直“腌渍于泥炭之中”。窖中物品所属年代大部分为青铜时代晚期，只有小部分属于铁器时代早期。
    领导这次发掘工作的考古学家弗朗西斯·普赖尔说，“这是最为重要的和激动人心的发现。我们到目前为止所发现的还只是冰山的山尖。”
    这个遗址９０％以上的部分仍有待于进一步发掘。全部发掘工作大约需要３０年时间。
