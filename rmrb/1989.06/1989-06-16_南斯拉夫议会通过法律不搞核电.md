Title: Yugoslav Parliament Passes Law Against Nuclear Power
Author:
Date: 1989-06-16
Edition: 3

Yugoslav Parliament on June 15 passed a law prohibiting the establishment of nuclear power plants.

The law stipulates that within the territory of Yugoslavia, it is forbidden to build any power plants using atomic energy and facilities for the production and processing of nuclear fuel waste. It also prohibits investment aimed at building nuclear power plants and the preparation of relevant materials.

Following the Chernobyl nuclear power plant accident in the Soviet Union, the Yugoslav Youth League and the Human Environment Protection Committee proposed the initiative to prohibit the construction of nuclear power plants in Yugoslavia. This proposal received support from some members of the Yugoslav Parliament, who then proposed drafting a law to prohibit the construction of nuclear power plants. Although the Yugoslav government repeatedly insisted that the law should be valid until the year 2000, this was rejected by the Parliament.

### 南斯拉夫议会通过法律不搞核电

1989-06-16
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

　　南斯拉夫议会通过法律不搞核电
    新华社贝尔格莱德６月１５日电  南斯拉夫议会１５日通过一项禁止建立核电站的法律。
    这项法律规定，在南斯拉夫境内禁止建立任何以原子能为动力的发电站和用于生产和加工核燃料废料的设施，禁止旨在建立核电站的投资和制订有关资料。
    在苏联切尔诺贝利核电站发生事故之后，南青年联盟和人类环境保护委员会提出了禁止在南建立核电站的倡议。这一倡议得到了南议会一些议员的赞同，他们提出制订有关禁止建立核电站的法律。虽然南政府一再坚持这项法律可规定到２０００年以前，但被议会否定。
