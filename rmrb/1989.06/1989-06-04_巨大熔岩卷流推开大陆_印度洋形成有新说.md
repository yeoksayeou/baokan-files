Title: Enormous Magma Plume Pushes Apart Continents, New Theory on the Formation of the Indian Ocean

Author:

Date: 1989-06-04

Page: 3

Xinhua News Agency, Washington

British and Canadian scientists have proposed a new theory about the formation of the Indian Ocean, suggesting that a massive magma "plume" rising from deep within the Earth pushed apart India, Antarctica, Australia, and Africa, thereby creating the Indian Ocean.

According to a report in The New York Times, scientists from four British universities and the University of Waterloo in Australia used computers to simulate how the Indian Ocean was formed. They discovered that India and Africa had already separated from Antarctica 110 million years ago.

By 60 million years ago, when Antarctica and Australia were separating, India was almost colliding with Asia.

Their hypothesis is the latest application of the "plume" theory proposed in 1971. This theory posits that the movement of the Earth's surface is partly caused by the upward flow of semi-molten rock, or "plumes," within the Earth's mantle.

### 巨大熔岩卷流推开大陆  印度洋形成有新说

1989-06-04
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

　　巨大熔岩卷流推开大陆
    印度洋形成有新说
    新华社华盛顿电  英国和加拿大科学家对印度洋的形成提出新说，认为是地球深处升起的一股巨大熔岩“卷流”，将印度、南极洲、澳大利亚和非洲推开，从而形成印度洋。
    据《纽约时报》报道，英国４所大学和澳大利亚滑铁卢大学的科学家，在用计算机再现印度洋是怎样形成时，发现印度和非洲在１．１亿年前就与南极洲分开了。
    在６０００万年前南极洲与澳大利亚分开时，印度已经快要与亚洲相遇了。
    他们的推想是１９７１年提出的“卷流”理论的最新应用。这一理论认为，地球表面的运动，部分是由地壳内部半熔岩上升的“卷流”造成的。
