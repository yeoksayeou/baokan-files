Title: What Does the Appearance of the "Goddess of Democracy" Statue in Tiananmen Square Mean?
Author: Wú Yè
Date: 1989-05-31
Edition: 1

The appearance of the "Goddess of Democracy" statue in the solemn Tiananmen Square has sparked widespread discussion. Placing memorials in Tiananmen Square must follow government regulations; this is a common practice. The square's gate tower, flagpole, memorials, halls, museums, and Great Hall of the People are all arranged in an orderly and solemn manner. This is where grand celebrations, important diplomatic events, and domestic and international visitors come to pay their respects and tour. It is the heart of the People's Republic of China and attracts global attention.

Every citizen should cherish Tiananmen Square. This is not only a show of love for the country and the nation but also a show of love for oneself. The square is sacred, and no one has the right to arbitrarily add or remove any permanent memorials. China does not allow such incidents, and similarly, other countries, including some Western nations, do not permit such actions in comparable places.

The broad masses of young students who advocate for the promotion of democracy are completely understandable. Advancing socialist democratic politics is also a demand of the entire nation and a goal that the Party and the government are striving to achieve. The active demands of the broad masses of young students have played a role in promoting this goal. However, some individuals erecting a "Goddess of Democracy" statue in Tiananmen Square is not an active pursuit of freedom and democracy but rather a serious misunderstanding of freedom and democracy. It is also a disregard for the freedom and democratic rights of others.

Freedom must be exercised within the bounds of the law. No citizen, when exercising freedom and rights, may infringe upon the interests of the state, society, the collective, or the lawful freedoms and rights of other citizens. Acting arbitrarily is not acceptable.

### 天安门广场出现“民主之神”像意味着什么？
吴叶
1989-05-31
第1版(要闻)
专栏：

　　天安门广场出现“民主之神”像意味着什么？
    吴叶
    在庄严的天安门广场上，一些人擅自放置一座“民主之神”像，引起人们议论纷纷。在天安门广场设置纪念物，必须按照政府有关法令，才能进行，这是常理常规。广场上的城楼、旗杆、纪念碑、纪念堂、博物馆、大会堂，错落有致，整齐肃穆，这是举行隆重庆祝典礼、重要外事活动以及国内外人民瞻仰游览的地方，是中华人民共和国的心脏，举世注目。
    对天安门广场，每一位公民都要爱护，这既是对国家、民族的爱护，也是对自己的爱护。广场是神圣的，谁也没有权利在这里随便增添或减少点什么永久性纪念物。中国不允许发生这种事情，外国包括一些西方国家同样也不允许在类似的地方发生类似的事情。
    广大青年学生要求促进民主建设的心情是完全可以理解的，推动社会主义民主政治的建设，这也是全国人民的要求，是党和政府努力实现的目标。广大青年学生的积极要求对实现这个目标起到了推动作用。但是，一些人在天安门广场建起一座“民主之神”像，并不是追求自由、民主的积极行动，而是对自由、民主的严重误解，也是对其他人自由、民主权利的不尊重。
    自由要在法律允许的范围内实行，任何公民在行使自由和权利的时候，不得损害国家的、社会的、集体的利益和其他公民的合法的自由和权利，为所欲为是不行的。
