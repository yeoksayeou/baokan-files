Title: European Council Issues Statement Making Unfounded Accusations Against China; Foreign Ministry Spokesman Expresses Deep Regret
Author:
Date: 1989-06-29
Edition: 1

The European Council issued a statement on June 27, making unfounded accusations against China without fully understanding the facts of China's suppression of the counter-revolutionary riots. The Foreign Ministry spokesman today expressed deep regret over this incident.

The spokesman said, "The Chinese government, in accordance with the constitution and laws, suppressed the recent counter-revolutionary riots in Beijing aimed at overthrowing the People's Republic of China. This is entirely an internal affair within China's sovereignty."

The spokesman said, "The Chinese government will unswervingly implement the policy of reform and opening up and an independent foreign policy of peace. Any foreign government or organization attempting to exert pressure on the Chinese government in any form, or interfere in China's internal affairs, is unwise and futile, and will ultimately harm their own interests."

The spokesman said, "We believe that far-sighted individuals in European countries, after understanding the relevant facts, will cherish the friendly relations we have established through long-term efforts and stop the aforementioned erroneous actions."

### 欧理会发表声明对我妄加指责  外交部发言人对此事深表遗憾

1989-06-29
第1版(要闻)
专栏：

　　欧理会发表声明对我妄加指责  外交部发言人对此事深表遗憾
    新华社北京６月２９日电  外交部发言人今天说，欧洲理事会在没有弄清中国平息反革命暴乱的事实真相之前，竟然轻率地于６月２７日发表声明，对中国妄加指责，并单方面作出损害双边关系的决定。对欧洲理事会的这一无理举动，中国政府不能不深为遗憾。
    发言人说，中国政府根据宪法和法律，平息最近在北京发生的、旨在颠覆中华人民共和国的反革命暴乱，这完全是中国主权范围内的事情。
    发言人说，中国政府将坚定不移地执行改革开放政策和独立自主的和平外交政策。任何外国政府或组织以这种或那种方式向中国政府施加压力、干涉中国内政的企图都是不明智的、徒劳的，到头来只会给它们自己带来损害。
    发言人说，“我们相信，欧洲各国有远见的人士在了解了有关事实真相之后，是会珍惜我们之间经过长期努力而建立起来的友好关系，并制止上述错误做法的。”
