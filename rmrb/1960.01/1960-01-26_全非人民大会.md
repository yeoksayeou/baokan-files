All-African People's Conference

    The Second All-African People's Conference began in Tunis on the 25th. The issues to be discussed at the conference include the development of the situation on the African continent since the Accra Conference, the organizational paths and means to achieve African independence, how to get rid of colonialism, African economic and social development, and African unity.

    The First All-African People's Conference, marking the great unity of the African people, was held in Accra, the capital of Ghana, from December 5 to 13, 1958. The conference was initiated in the name of thirty-six African nationalist parties and mass organizations. More than 300 representatives from 62 organizations from 28 countries attended the conference.

    The meeting discussed various issues related to African national independence and adopted important declarations and resolutions on colonialism and imperialism, demanding that colonial countries withdraw from African land. This was the first gathering in Africa with the broadest representation of people from various countries on the African continent. It strengthened the unity and strength of the African people in the struggle against colonialism and powerfully promoted the development of the African national independence movement.

    In order to guide the national liberation struggle of the African people and accelerate the liberation of the African people from the colonial rule of imperialism, the conference decided to establish a permanent secretariat in Accra. On December 14, 1958, the conference also established the General Affairs Committee as a permanent body of the conference. The committee consists of one chairman and fourteen members.

    The Permanent Secretariat of the All-African People's Conference has repeatedly issued statements condemning the heinous acts of colonialists in suppressing the African nationalist movement.

    The General Affairs Committee held meetings in Conakry, Tunis, and Accra last year to discuss issues related to the struggle of the African people for freedom and independence. (Xinhua News Agency)

### 全非人民大会

1960-01-26
第5版()
专栏：资料

    全非人民大会
    第二届全非人民大会25日在突尼斯开始举行。大会将讨论的问题包括阿克拉会议以来非洲大陆形势的发展、达到非洲独立的组织途径和手段、摆脱殖民主义、非洲经济和社会发展以及非洲团结等问题。
    标志着非洲人民大团结的第一届全非人民大会，于1958年12月5日到13日在加纳首都阿克拉召开，大会是以三十六个非洲民族主义政党和群众团体名义发起的。参加大会的有二十八个国家的六十二个组织的三百多名代表。
    会议讨论了有关非洲民族独立的各项问题，通过了关于殖民主义和帝国主义、要求殖民国家从非洲土地上撤走等重要宣言和决议。这是第一次在非洲举行的非洲大陆各国人民代表性最广泛的集会。它加强了非洲人民在反对殖民主义斗争中的团结和力量，并有力地推动了非洲民族独立运动的发展。
    为指导非洲人民民族解放斗争，加速非洲人民从帝国主义的殖民统治下面获得解放，大会决定在阿克拉建立常设秘书处。1958年12月14日大会又成立了作为大会常设机构的总务委员会。委员会由主席一人和委员十四人组成。
    全非人民大会常设秘书处曾经屡次发表声明，谴责殖民主义者镇压非洲民族主义运动的罪恶行为。
    总务委员会去年曾在科纳克里、突尼斯和阿克拉等地举行会议，讨论了有关非洲人民争取自由和独立的斗争的问题。　（新华社）
