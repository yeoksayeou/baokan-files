Title: Nepalese Prime Minister Visits India; Believes There is No Need to Conclude a Defense Treaty with India
Author:
Date: 1960-01-20
Page: 5

    Xinhua News Agency, New Delhi, January 19th: According to a report in the "Indian Express", Nepalese Prime Minister Koirala said yesterday morning at the Patna airport when leaving that there is no need for India and Nepal to conclude any joint defense treaty.
    Nepalese Prime Minister Koirala began a twelve-day visit to India on January 17th at the invitation of Indian Prime Minister Nehru. He arrived in Patna, Bihar, India on the 17th and left Patna for Jamshedpur on the 18th.
    At the Patna airport, when reporters asked Koirala about the Nepalese opposition party Gorkha Dal's claim that China has already conducted large-scale infiltration in certain areas of Nepal, Koirala replied, "I have not received such news."
    When a reporter asked whether Nepal intends to mediate between the two countries in the Sino-Indian border dispute, Koirala said that it should be resolved by both sides.

### 尼泊尔首相到印度访问  他认为没有必要同印度缔结防御条约

1960-01-20
第5版()
专栏：

    尼泊尔首相到印度访问
    他认为没有必要同印度缔结防御条约
    新华社新德里19日电　据“印度快报”报道，尼泊尔首相柯伊拉腊昨天上午在离开巴特那机场时说，印度和尼泊尔没有必要缔结任何联合防御条约。
    尼泊尔首相柯伊拉腊应印度总理尼赫鲁的邀请在1月17日开始到印度作为期十二天的访问，他在17日到达印度比哈尔邦的巴特那，18日离巴特那去詹谢普尔。
    在巴特那机场上，有记者向柯伊拉腊提出关于尼泊尔反对党廓尔喀党所谓中国已经在尼泊尔的某些地区进行大规模的渗入的问题，柯伊拉腊回答说，“我没有得到这样的消息”。
    有记者问到尼泊尔是否打算在印中边界争端上为两国进行调解，柯伊拉腊说，应当由两国双方来解决。
