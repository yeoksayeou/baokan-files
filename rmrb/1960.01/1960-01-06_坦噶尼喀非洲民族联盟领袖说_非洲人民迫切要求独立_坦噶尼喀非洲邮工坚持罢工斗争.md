Title: Tanganyika African National Union Leader Says African People Urgently Demand Independence, Tanganyika African Postal Workers Persist in Strike Struggle
Author:
Date: 1960-01-06
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, 4th - New York dispatch: Julius Nyerere, leader of the Tanganyika African National Union, said in an interview with NBC television reporters on the 3rd that the African people are eager to gain independence. He pointed out that this is the right of the African people. Nyerere said: "The people of the African colonies suffer all kinds of humiliation, and the greatest humiliation is being ruled by another country."

Xinhua News Agency, 4th - Dar es Salaam dispatch: Twelve hundred African postal workers in Tanganyika are persisting in their strike struggle, which has now entered its second week. The Tanganyika African postal workers began this strike on December 24, 1959, to protest the British colonial postal and telecommunications authorities' long delay in meeting their demands for wage increases and improved working conditions.

### 坦噶尼喀非洲民族联盟领袖说  非洲人民迫切要求独立  坦噶尼喀非洲邮工坚持罢工斗争

1960-01-06
第6版()
专栏：

    坦噶尼喀非洲民族联盟领袖说
    非洲人民迫切要求独立
    坦噶尼喀非洲邮工坚持罢工斗争
    据新华社4日讯　纽约消息：坦噶尼喀非洲民族联盟领袖朱利叶斯·尼雷尔3日对美国全国广播公司电视网记者发表谈话说，非洲人民急于要获得独立。他指出，这是非洲人民的权利。尼雷尔说：“非洲殖民地人民遭受种种耻辱，而最大的耻辱就是被另一个国家统治。”
  据新华社4日讯　达累斯萨拉姆消息：坦噶尼喀一千二百名非洲邮政工人坚持已进入第二周的罢工斗争。坦噶尼喀非洲邮政工人从1959年12月24日开始举行这次罢工斗争，是为了抗议英国殖民者的邮电管理当局迟迟不满足他们提出的关于增加工资、改善劳动条件等要求。
