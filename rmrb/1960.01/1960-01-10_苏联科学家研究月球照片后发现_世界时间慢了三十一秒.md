Title: Soviet Scientists Discover World Time is Thirty-One Seconds Slow After Studying Lunar Photographs
Author:
Date: 1960-01-10
Page: 3

Xinhua News Agency, Moscow, 9th -- According to the Soviet newspaper "Culture and Life," Soviet scientific personnel discovered that world time is thirty-one seconds slow. This discrepancy has been accumulating since the first decade of this century.

Staff members at the Pulkovo Observatory of the Soviet Academy of Sciences, near Leningrad, reached this conclusion after studying nearly a hundred photographs concerning the moon's movement and the situation of the planets around the moon.

From 1960 onwards, scientists from all over the world will be able to use the calibrated time to conduct scientific work such as studying celestial body movements, determining the orbits of artificial cosmic bodies, radio physics, and cosmic structure.

World time is usually determined by the Earth's rotation. However, this rotation has uneven phenomena, which affects the accuracy of timekeeping.

### 苏联科学家研究月球照片后发现  世界时间慢了三十一秒

1960-01-10
第3版()
专栏：

    苏联科学家研究月球照片后发现
    世界时间慢了三十一秒
    据新华社莫斯科9日电　据苏联“文化与生活”报报道：苏联科学工作人员发现了世界时间慢了三十一秒，这一差误是从这一世纪的最初十年就开始累积起来的。
    列宁格勒附近的苏联科学院普尔科沃天文台的工作人员研究了近百张有关月球运动和月球周围星球情况的照片后，得出了这一结论。
    从1960年起，世界各国的科学家就可以按照校准了的时间，来从事研究天体运动、决定人造宇宙天体的运行轨道和无线电物理、宇宙构造等科学工作。
    世界时间通常是按照地球自转来决定的。但是这种运转有不均匀现象，这使计时的准确性受到影响。
