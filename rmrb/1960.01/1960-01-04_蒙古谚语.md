Mongolian Proverbs

Following the sun, one will not suffer from the cold; following the Party, one will not suffer losses.
＊　＊　＊
One person's wisdom is not enough; two people's wisdom is inexhaustible.
＊　＊　＊
Truth fears no evil; real gold fears no fire.
＊　＊　＊
A long journey begins with a short step; great things start with small things.
＊　＊　＊
Rather than lament one's fate, it is better to believe in one's own strength.
＊　＊　＊
A withered tree does not grow leaves; empty talk is useless.
＊　＊　＊
Gold comes from labor; sparks can fly even from cow dung.
＊　＊　＊
The felt bag has a lot of dust; the person with shallow knowledge has great arrogance.

Compiled and translated by Han Su

### 蒙古谚语
韩速
1960-01-04
第8版()
专栏：

    蒙古谚语跟随太阳不受寒冷，跟着党走不受亏损。
    ＊　＊　＊一人的智慧不够，两人的智慧用不穷。
    ＊　＊　＊是真理不畏邪恶，是真金不怕火炼。
    ＊　＊　＊远行从近处开始，大事从小事做起。
    ＊　＊　＊与其悲叹自己的命运，莫如相信自己的力量。
    ＊　＊　＊枯树不长叶，空话没有用。
    ＊　＊　＊工作里面出黄金，牛粪也能冒火星。
    ＊　＊　＊毡袋子的灰尘多，学问浅薄者的傲气大。
    韩速辑译
