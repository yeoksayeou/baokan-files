Title: Washington Issues the Most Unpleasant New Year's Greetings; "New York Post" Considers the US Decision Regrettable
Author:
Date: 1960-01-03
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, New York, January 2nd. A commentary in the "New York Post" believes that the US government's announced decision that it is free to resume nuclear weapons testing at any time is "the most unpleasant New Year's greetings from Washington to the world."

The "New York Post" emphasized that "this decision carries the disheartening spirit of Dulles' past brinkmanship." The newspaper believes it is regrettable that the United States gives the impression that, just when all of humanity's hopes are pinned on diplomatic negotiations, it is "raising excuses for resuming the nuclear arms race." The newspaper called the US government's action "most inappropriate and out of step with the times."

The newspaper worried that activities in the US's so-called "sound nuclear policy" are "unlikely to give people reason to treat Washington's resumption of the old policy of brinkmanship with trust."

### 华盛顿发表的最不愉快的新年祝词  “纽约邮报”认为美国这一决定令人遗憾

1960-01-03
第5版()
专栏：

    华盛顿发表的最不愉快的新年祝词
    “纽约邮报”认为美国这一决定令人遗憾
    新华社2日讯　据塔斯社纽约1日讯：“纽约邮报”的一篇评论认为美国政府所宣布的它有自由随时恢复试验核武器的决定是“华盛顿向全世界发表的最不愉快的新年祝词”。
    “纽约邮报”强调指出，“这个决定带有过去杜勒斯的令人沮丧的保持在战争边缘上的精神”。这家报纸认为令人遗憾的是美国给人这样一种印象：正当人类的一切希望都寄托在外交谈判的时候，它却“为恢复核军备竞赛提出借端”。报纸把美国政府的这种行动称为“最不恰当和不符合时代潮流的。”
    这家报纸担心美国的所谓“健全的核政策”方面的活动也“未必使人有理由以信任的态度对待华盛顿这种恢复保持战争边缘老政策的做法”。
