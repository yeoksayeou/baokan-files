Title: Three Million Japanese Workers Hold Nationwide General Strike Demanding Higher Average Wages and the Establishment of a Minimum Wage System; Asian-Australasian Liaison Bureau of the World Federation of Trade Unions and the All-China Federation of Trade Unions Send Messages of Support
Author:
Date: 1957-03-12
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, 11th dispatch from Tokyo: Three million Japanese workers began their third wave of forceful struggle this morning, taking various actions to demand an increase of 2,000 yen in the monthly average wage and the establishment of a minimum wage system.

A total of 47 national public and private enterprises and public education worker unions affiliated with the General Council of Trade Unions of Japan (Sohyo) are participating in this struggle. Among them, 160,000 union members of the Japan Coal Miners' Union, belonging to 14 large companies, launched a 72-hour full strike in 52 mines across the country.

Approximately 60,000 union members from 912 stations and 89 passenger and freight car depots on the main lines of the National Railway Workers' Union held half-day mass meetings this morning. Police authorities dispatched 25,300 police officers to Tokyo and various stations nationwide to maintain order.

The Federation of Private Railway Workers' Unions of Japan, affiliated with 12 large companies, also held a strike. This ended in the afternoon after the union and management reached an agreement on wage increases.

Public corporations and government agencies staff, deprived of the right to strike, took leave and held mass meetings as forms of struggle. Teachers across the country dismissed classes early this afternoon.

Xinhua News Agency, 11th dispatch: The Asian-Australasian Liaison Bureau of the World Federation of Trade Unions sent a telegram today to Yukitaka Haraguchi, Chairman of the General Council of Trade Unions of Japan, expressing its enthusiastic support for the Japanese working class's struggle to establish a minimum wage system.

Currently, approximately three million Japanese workers, under the leadership of the General Council of Trade Unions of Japan, are engaged in a large-scale nationwide spring struggle, demanding wage increases and the establishment of a minimum wage system.

The Asian-Australasian Liaison Bureau of the World Federation of Trade Unions also expressed in the telegram its sincere hope that the Japanese working class would achieve a brilliant victory in this just struggle, and expressed its firm belief that the united action of Japanese workers would achieve good development in this struggle. The telegram said that the unity of all Japanese trade unions would provide a reliable guarantee for the victory of this struggle.

Xinhua News Agency, 11th dispatch: The All-China Federation of Trade Unions today sent a telegram to Yukitaka Haraguchi, Chairman of the General Council of Trade Unions of Japan, representing the Chinese working class in support of the ongoing struggle by Japanese workers to establish a minimum wage system, and wishing them great success in this struggle.

The telegram said that establishing a minimum wage system and guaranteeing the basic livelihood of workers are the legitimate demands of the broad masses of Japanese worker brothers. This will help improve the situation of Japanese workers and further promote the development of unified action by Japanese workers.

### 要求提高平均工资和确定最低工资制度  日本三百万工人举行全国性大罢工  世界工联亚澳联络局和我全国总工会分别致电支持

1957-03-12
第6版()
专栏：

    要求提高平均工资和确定最低工资制度
    日本三百万工人举行全国性大罢工
    世界工联亚澳联络局和我全国总工会分别致电支持
    新华社11日讯　东京消息：三百万日本工人从今天早晨开始，采取各种方式展开了第三次波浪式实力斗争，要求把每月平均工资提高二千日元，并且确定最低工资制度。
    参加日本工会总评议会发动的这次斗争的共有四十七个全国性的公私企业和公教人员工会。其中，日本煤矿工会所属十四家大公司在全国各地五十二个矿山的工会会员十六万人采取了举行七十二小时的全面罢工的行动。
    国营铁道工会在全国主要干线中的九百十二个车站和八十九个客车段和货车段的工会会员约六万人在今天上午举行半天的群众大会。警察当局出动了二万五千三百名警察到东京及全国各车站进行戒备。
    日本私营铁道工会总联合会所属十二家大公司工会也一度举行罢工。到了今天下午，工会同资方就提高工资问题达成协议，因此结束了罢工。
    被剥夺了罢工权的公营企业和政府机关工作人员采取了请假和举行群众大会的斗争方式。全国各地的教职员今天下午一齐提前下课。
    新华社11日讯　世界工联亚澳联络局今天打电报给日本工会总评议会议长原口幸隆，表示热烈支持日本工人阶级要求确立最低工资制的斗争。
    目前，约三百万日本工人正在日本工会总评议会的领导下，进行声势浩大的全国性春季斗争，要求提高工资和确立最低工资制。
    世界工联亚澳联络局在电报中还表示衷心希望日本工人阶级在这一正义斗争中取得光辉的胜利，并且表示深信日本工人的统一行动将在这一斗争中获得良好的发展。电文说，日本各工会的团结一致将为这一斗争的胜利提供可靠的保证。
    新华社11日讯　中华全国总工会今天打电报给日本工会总评议会议长原口幸隆，代表中国工人阶级表示支持日本工人正在进行的争取确立最低工资制的斗争，并且预祝日本工人在这一斗争中获得巨大的成就。
    电报说，确立最低工资制、保障工人的最低生活是日本广大工人兄弟的正当要求。这将有助于改善日本工人的处境，并且将进一步促进日本工人的统一行动的发展。
