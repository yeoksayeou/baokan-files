Title: US Senate Passes Amendment to Military Portion of Plan to Invade Middle East; Kefauver Reveals Plan Stemmed From Needs of Oil Companies
Author:
Date: 1957-03-03
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, Washington, March 2nd—The U.S. Senate passed on February 28 the amendment to the military portion of the Eisenhower Middle East Plan. This amendment states: "The United States is prepared, when requested, to use armed force to assist any nation or group of nations requesting assistance against armed aggression from any country controlled by international communism." The condition is: "Such use of force shall be consistent with U.S. treaty obligations and the U.S. Constitution."

After passing the military portion, Senate Democratic Leader Johnson introduced a motion attempting to limit the discussion time for the economic portion of the Middle East Plan. However, this motion was opposed by members and failed to pass.

Senate Armed Services Committee Chairman Russell has already proposed an amendment requiring the deletion of the entire economic portion, and requiring "simply a statement that the United States intends to use armed forces to deal with armed aggression in the Middle East when necessary." Johnson admitted that Russell's amendment would receive "a lot of support."

In the Senate debate on March 1, Democratic Senator Kefauver criticized the government's Middle East Plan, saying: "There is ample evidence that this plan was mainly drafted according to the needs of international oil companies." He said: "The oil issue is now not just a factor considered when formulating foreign policy, but has become the dominant influence."

At the same time, many members supported the government's plan and expressed their intention to defeat Russell's proposal. Government officials also attacked Russell's suggestion. Assistant Secretary of State Robert Hill believed that if economic aid were cancelled, it "would have a very unfortunate impact on America's efforts to win" the "trust" of the Middle East.

### 美参院通过侵略中东计划的军事部分修正案  克弗维尔揭露这个计划出于石油公司需要

1957-03-03
第6版()
专栏：

    美参院通过侵略中东计划的军事部分修正案
    克弗维尔揭露这个计划出于石油公司需要
    新华社2日讯  华盛顿消息：美国参议院2月28日通过了艾森豪威尔中东计划关于军事部分的修正案，这项修正案说：“美国准备在必要时使用武力来帮助”所谓“要求帮助反对国际共产主义控制下的任何国家的武装侵略的任何国家或国家集团”。条件是：“这种使用武力应当符合美国的条约义务和美国的宪法”。
    参议院在通过了军事部分之后，参议院民主党领袖约翰逊又提出一项动议，企图限制关于中东计划的经济部分的讨论时间。但是这个动议遭到议员们的反对而没有通过。
    参议院军事委员会主席鲁塞尔已经提出一项修正案，要求删去整个经济部分，和要求“简单地声明美国打算在必要时使用武装部队来对付中东的武装侵略”就行了。约翰逊承认，鲁塞尔的修正案将得到“很多的支持”。
    在参议院3月1日举行的辩论中，民主党参议员克弗维尔指责了政府的中东计划，他说：“有够多的证据证明这个计划主要是按照国际石油公司的需要来拟订的。”他说：“石油问题现在不只是作为制定外交政策时考虑的一个因素，而且已经成为占统治地位的影响。”
    同时，也有许多议员支持政府的计划，并且表示要击败鲁塞尔的提议。政府官员也对鲁塞尔的建议进行了攻击，助理国务卿罗伯特·希尔认为，如果取消经济援助，那“将对美国要赢得”中东的“信任的努力”具有“非常不幸的影响”。
