Title: Nixon's Maneuvering in Morocco to Undermine France Angers French Senator
Author:
Date: 1957-03-07
Page: 6
Xinhua News Agency, March 6th, Paris Dispatch: During an oral inquiry at the Council of the Republic on March 5th, French Senator Michel Debré stated that U.S. Vice President Nixon’s recent visit to Morocco was “unfriendly” to France. He demanded that the French government immediately protest to Washington regarding this matter, so as to prevent Nixon from “repeating the same mistake” during his visit to Tunisia.

Debré said that in Nixon's recent speech in Rabat, he "did not say a single word expressing friendship towards France and the French people living in Morocco." Furthermore, he even discussed the future of Algeria with the Sultan of Morocco. Debré believes this is "contempt" for France's position in Algeria.

### 尼克松在摩洛哥挖法国墙根  引起法参议员不满

1957-03-07
第6版()
专栏：

    尼克松在摩洛哥挖法国墙根
    引起法参议员不满
    新华社6日讯　巴黎消息：法国参议员密歇尔·德勃雷5日在共和国参议院的口头质询中提出，美国副总统尼克松最近对摩洛哥的访问具有对法国“不友好”的性质。他要求法国政府就这件事迅速向华盛顿提出抗议，使尼克松在访问突尼斯时不至于“再发生同样的错误”。
    德勃雷说，尼克松最近在拉巴特的演说中，“没有说过一句对法国和住在摩洛哥的法国人表示友好的话”。不但如此，他还曾经同摩洛哥苏丹讨论过阿尔及利亚的前途问题。德勃雷认为，这是对法国在阿尔及利亚地位的“藐视”。
