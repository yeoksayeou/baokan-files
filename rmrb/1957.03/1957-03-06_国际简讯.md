	International Briefs
	One Engineer for Every 60 Industrial Workers in Poland
	In Poland, there is one engineer for every sixty industrial workers. This ratio is almost the same as in the United States. This was stated in an article recently published in "Warsaw Life" by Polish engineer Mukha.
	India Sends Metallurgical Engineers to Study in the Soviet Union
	According to a statement by the Indian Information Bureau on March 3rd, eleven Indian metallurgical engineers have departed for the Soviet Union to study metallurgical technology. After their return, they will work at the Bhilai steel plant, which the Soviet Union is helping India build. Currently, eighty Indian engineers are studying in the Soviet Union. India has also sent sixty-seven engineers to West German steel factories to study, and plans to send seventy-five Indian engineers to the United Kingdom for training this year.
	Yugoslav Parliamentary Delegation Departs for Britain
	A Yugoslav parliamentary delegation headed by Pijade, Chairman of the Federal National Assembly of Yugoslavia, left Belgrade on the evening of March 3rd for Britain. The delegation is visiting Britain at the invitation of both houses of the British Parliament. (Xinhua News Agency)

### 国际简讯

1957-03-06
第5版()
专栏：国际简讯

    国际简讯
    波60个工业工作者中有一个工程师
    波兰每六十个工业工作人员中就有一个工程师。这个比例和美国的比例差不多。这是波兰工程师穆哈最近发表在“华沙生活报”上的一篇文章说的。
    印度派冶金工程师赴苏学习
    据印度新闻局3日宣布，十一位印度冶金工程师已经动身前往苏联，去学习冶金技术。他们回来以后将在苏联帮助印度建立的比莱炼钢厂工作。目前已经有八十位印度工程师在苏联学习。印度还派了六十七名工程师到西德钢铁工厂去学习，并且准备在今年派七十五名印度工程师去英国受训练。
    南议会代表团启程访英
    以南斯拉夫联邦国民议会主席皮雅杰为首的南斯拉夫议会代表团3日晚间离开贝尔格莱德前往英国。代表团是应英国议会两院的邀请到英国访问的。（新华社）
