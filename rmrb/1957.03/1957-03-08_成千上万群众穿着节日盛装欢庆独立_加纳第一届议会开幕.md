Title: Thousands of People in Festive Attire Celebrate Independence; Ghana's First Parliament Opens
Author:
Date: 1957-03-08
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, Accra, 7th: Ghana's first parliament held its opening ceremony on the 6th.

When Ghana's Prime Minister, Kwame Nkrumah, Chairman of the Convention People's Party, and the leader of the opposition party—the National Liberation Movement, Kofi Abrefa Busia, entered parliament, members of both the ruling and opposition parties cheered enthusiastically.

Delegations from all parts of Ghana attended the ceremony, wearing their traditional national costumes.

At the meeting, the Duchess of Kent of Britain delivered an opening speech on behalf of Queen Elizabeth of Britain and handed over the constitutional documents to the Ghanaian government.

Charles Arden-Clarke was sworn in as Ghana's first Governor-General at the meeting.

Many foreign delegations also attended the opening ceremony.

Outside the parliament, thousands of Ghanaian citizens in festive attire shouted slogans to celebrate independence.

### 成千上万群众穿着节日盛装欢庆独立  加纳第一届议会开幕

1957-03-08
第6版()
专栏：

    成千上万群众穿着节日盛装欢庆独立
    加纳第一届议会开幕
    新华社7日讯　阿克拉消息：加纳第一届议会6日举行了开幕式。
    当加纳总理、人民大会党主席恩克鲁玛和反对党——民族解放运动党领袖布西厄走进议会的时候，执政党和反对党议员一道热烈欢呼。
    从加纳各地来的代表团都穿着他们传统的民族服装参加这次仪式。
    在这次会议上，英国的肯特公爵夫人代表英国女王伊丽莎白向会议致了开幕词，同时把立宪文件交给了加纳政府。
    阿登—克拉克在这次会议上宣誓就任加纳第一任总督。
    许多外国代表团也参加了这次开幕式。
    在议会外面，成千上万穿着节日服装的加纳公民高呼口号庆祝独立。
