Title: Mongolia's Expanding Cultural Ties with Foreign Countries
Author: 
Date: 1957-03-05
Page: 5

    Mongolia's Expanding Cultural Ties with Foreign Countries

    (Reported by this newspaper) The Mongolian People's Republic's cultural exchanges with foreign countries saw significant development in 1956. During that year, over 700 actors, painters, journalists, and athletes visited foreign countries, while more than 600 foreign representatives visited the Mongolian People's Republic.

    Exhibitions such as "The Mongolian People's Republic Advancing on the Socialist Road" were held in Moscow, Beijing, Berlin, Warsaw, Budapest, Tirana, Hanoi, Bucharest, Sofia, and Pyongyang in the past year. Paintings from the Soviet Union and Poland, Chinese artworks, and works by famous Japanese painters were also exhibited in Ulan Bator.

    In April 1956, Chinese weightlifters visited the Mongolian People's Republic for performances. The Mongolian People's Republic also sent a volleyball team to China. In June 1956, the first chess competition in Asia was held in Ulan Bator, with participation from famous chess experts from the Soviet Union, China, Czechoslovakia, Hungary, Bulgaria, Romania, and the German Democratic Republic. Mongolian chess players also participated in the Seventh International Chess Competition.

    Famous works by foreign writers will be published extensively in the Mongolian People's Republic. Works by Lu Xun, Ma Feng, Zhao Jingchen, and Gao Yubao from China, a collection of works by Romanian writer Caragiale, poems by Bulgarian writer Vaptsarov, and works by Korean writer Han Xueye are soon to be translated and published in Mongolian. Works by Mongolian writers will be published or are being prepared for publication in the Soviet Union, China, the Democratic People's Republic of Korea, Romania, and other people's democratic countries.

    In 1957, the Mongolian People's Republic will further strengthen its cultural ties with foreign countries, preparing to exchange delegations in science, art, and other fields with some countries, and to exchange experiences in science, culture, art, health, and other aspects.

### 蒙古对外文化联系日益扩大

1957-03-05
第5版()
专栏：

    蒙古对外文化联系日益扩大
    本报讯  蒙古人民共和国的对外文化联系在1956年有了很大的发展。在这一年内，有七百多名演员、画家、记者和运动员曾经到国外去访问，外国的代表到蒙古人民共和国去进行访问的有六百多名。
    在莫斯科、北京、柏林、华沙、布达佩斯、地拉那、河内、布加勒斯特、索非亚和平壤等城市，曾在过去一年内分别举办过“蒙古人民共和国沿着社会主义道路前进”的展览会；苏联和波兰的绘画、中国的艺术品和日本著名画家的作品也在乌兰巴托展出过。
    1956年4月，中国的举重运动员曾经到蒙古人民共和国去访问表演；蒙古人民共和国也派遣了排球代表队到中国来。1956年6月，在乌兰巴托举行了在亚洲首次的象棋比赛会，参加这次比赛的有苏联、中国、捷克斯洛伐克、匈牙利、保加利亚、罗马尼亚和德意志民主共和国著名的象棋专家。蒙古人民共和国的象棋手也参加了第七届国际象棋比赛。
    外国作家著名的作品将要大量地在蒙古人民共和国出版。中国的鲁迅、马烽、赵景琛、高玉宝的作品，罗马尼亚作家卡拉迦列的选集、保加利亚作家瓦普查洛夫的诗和朝鲜作家韩雪野的作品即将译成蒙文出版。在苏联、中国、朝鲜民主主义人民共和国、罗马尼亚和其它人民民主国家将要出版或者正在准备出版蒙古作家的作品。
    在1957年内，蒙古人民共和国将要更进一步加强同外国的文化联系，准备和一些国家互相交换科学、艺术等代表团，并且互相交流科学、文化艺术、卫生等各方面的经验。
