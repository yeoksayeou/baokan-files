Title: Japanese Government Responds to U.S. Opposition to Strengthening Embargo
Author:
Date: 1957-03-18
Page: 7

Xinhua News Agency, 16th Dispatch: According to Kyodo News, the Japanese government recently formally notified the U.S. government through the Japanese Embassy in the United States that Japan opposes strengthening export controls and believes that the embargo against China should be relaxed to the same level as the embargo against the Soviet Union and Eastern European People's Democracies.

The U.S. government conducted "explorations" with the other fourteen countries participating in the Coordinating Committee for Multilateral Export Controls (COCOM) at the beginning of this year, requesting them to strengthen embargo measures. After research, the Japanese government formally gave a negative response to the U.S. government.

### 日本政府答复美国反对加强禁运

1957-03-18
第7版()
专栏：

    日本政府答复美国反对加强禁运
    据新华社16日讯　据共同社报道，日本政府最近通过日本驻美国大使馆正式通知美国政府说，日本反对加强出口管制，并且认为应该把对中国的禁运放宽到同对苏联和东欧人民民主国家的禁运同样的程度。
    美国政府在今年年初曾经向参加巴黎统筹委员会的其他十四个国家进行“试探”，要它们加强禁运措施。日本政府经过研究以后，正式向美国政府作了否定的答复。
