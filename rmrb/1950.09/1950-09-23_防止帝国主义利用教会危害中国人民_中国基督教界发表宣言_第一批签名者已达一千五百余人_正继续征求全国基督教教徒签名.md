Title: Preventing Imperialism from Using the Church to Harm the Chinese People
Author: 
Date: 1950-09-23
Page: 1

Xinhua News Agency, September 22nd

Chinese Christian leaders, to prevent imperialism from using the church to harm the interests of the Chinese people, issued a declaration titled "The Path of Effort for Chinese Christianity in New China's Construction" at the end of July. They also solicited signatures from Christians nationwide who agreed with this declaration. By the end of August, the first batch of signatories had reached 1,527 people. The signatories include leaders and members of various local churches; the work of soliciting signatures is still ongoing. The initiators of the declaration are: Brutus Y.C. Wu (Director of the Publication Department, National Association of YMCA of China), Deng Yuzhi (General Secretary, National Association of YWCA of China), Zhao Zichen (Professor and Dean of the Institute of Religion, Yenching University, Beijing), Liu Liangmo (Director of the Work Department, National Association of YMCA of China), Yu Yiqing (General Secretary, National Association of YMCA of China), Cui Xianxiang (General Secretary, National Association of the Christian Church in China), Miao Qisheng (Secretary, National Committee of the China Christian Council), Shaojing San (General Secretary, National Association of the Christian Church in China), Li Zhaohuan (Chairman, Shanghai Christian Fellowship), Jiang Wenhan (Deputy General Secretary, National Association of YMCA of China), Wang Jimin (Former Secretary of the Medical Committee, National Association of the Christian Church in China), Lu Zhiwei (President, Yenching University), Wang Zizhong (General Secretary, North China Christian Council), Jiang Changchuan (Superintendent, Methodist Church), Chen Wenrun (General Secretary, North China Christian Council), Zhao Fusan, Ding Xiansheng, Wang Shijing, Fang Shuxuan, Ai Nian San, Wang Gaozhi, Lin Yongyu, Zhao Guanhai, Hu Yiyun, Wei Zhaomin, Gao Fengshan, Sun Wanguixiu, Ling Yuxiu, Qi Qingcai, Chen Zhimei, Chen Chonggui, Yang Xiaopeng, Xiong Xiapei, Liu Weicheng, Zheng Jianye, Bao Zheqing, Tan Renmei, Pang Zhixian, Xiao Guogui, and forty others.

The full text of the declaration is as follows:

Christianity has been in China for over 140 years, and during this period, it has made significant contributions to Chinese society. Unfortunately, soon after Christianity was introduced to China, imperialism began its activities here. Since the main people who brought Christianity to China were from these imperialist countries, Christianity and imperialism became connected, intentionally or unintentionally, visibly or invisibly. Now that China's revolution has succeeded, imperialism will not accept this historical fact. They will use every means possible to try to undermine this established fact; they will also use Christianity to carry out their schemes of incitement and to try to create reactionary forces in China. To heighten our vigilance against imperialism, to express Christianity's clear political stance in New China, to promote a Chinese church led by Chinese people, and to point out the responsibilities that Christians nationwide should undertake in the construction of New China, we are issuing this document. We wish to call on all Christians in the country to strive for the realization of the principles provided in this document.

**Overall Task**

The Chinese Christian Church and organizations must fully support the Common Program, under the leadership of the government, to oppose imperialism, feudalism, and bureaucratic capitalism, and to struggle for the construction of an independent, democratic, peaceful, unified, and prosperous New China.

**Basic Principles**

(1) The Chinese Christian Church and organizations must make the greatest efforts and use effective methods to make the church members clearly understand the evils that imperialism has caused in China, recognize the historical fact that imperialism has used Christianity, eliminate the influence of imperialism within Christianity, be vigilant against the schemes of imperialism, especially American imperialism, to use religion to cultivate reactionary forces, and at the same time, call on them to participate in the anti-war, pro-peace movement, and educate them to thoroughly understand and support the government's land reform policy.

(2) The Chinese Christian Church and organizations must use effective means to cultivate the patriotic and democratic spirit and self-respecting and confident psychology of ordinary believers. The self-governance, self-support, and self-propagation movement advocated by Chinese Christianity in the past has achieved considerable results. In the shortest possible time, this task should be completed; at the same time, self-criticism should be advocated, and in various works, self-examination and reorganization should be carried out, and economy and simplicity should be achieved to reach the goal of Christian reform.

**Specific Measures**

(1) The Chinese Christian Church and organizations that still rely on foreign talents and economic assistance should formulate specific plans to achieve self-reliance in the shortest possible time.

(2) In the future, the Chinese Christian Church and organizations should pay attention to the deep understanding of the essence of Christianity, the unity among denominations, the cultivation of leadership talents, and the improvement of church institutions in religious work; in general work, they should pay attention to anti-imperialism, anti-feudalism, anti-bureaucratic capitalism education, and labor production, understanding the times, cultural activities, literacy education, medical and health care, and child care services for the people.

The first batch of signatories of the declaration issued by Chinese Christians, titled "The Path of Effort for Chinese Christianity in New China's Construction," totaled 1,527 people. Among them are: four representatives of the religious community in the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference, 22 national Christian organizations, 66 regional Christian organizations, 380 churches (including 30 from the Methodist Church, 27 from the Anglican Church, 32 from the Lutheran Church, 8 from the Christian Church, 79 from the National Association of the Christian Church in China, 17 from the Baptist Church, 12 from the Congregational Church, 3 from the Presbyterian Church, 17 from the Brethren Church, 16 from the China Inland Mission, 3 from the Salvation Army, 7 from the Independent Church, 10 from the Pentecostal Church, 6 from the Presbyterian Church, and 113 from unspecified denominations), 381 members of the Jesus Family, 109 members of the YMCA, 197 members of the YWCA, 43 university students, 25 theology students, 66 middle school students, 20 child education workers, 123 hospital workers, 22 book and magazine editors, and 69 other organizations and individuals.

The signatories by region are as follows: Shanghai 190, Jiangsu 107, Zhejiang 76, Anhui 51, Fujian 19, Guangdong 148, Guangxi 4, Jiangxi 38, Pingyuan 14, Shandong 407, Beijing 51, Hebei 51, Henan 40, Hubei 43, Hunan 74, Sichuan 114, Shaanxi 34, Shanxi 22, Gansu 1, Northeast 20, Yunnan 2, Guizhou 1, and 20 from unspecified regions.

    宣言全文如下：
    基督教传到中国，已经有一百四十多年的历史，在这一百多年当中，它对中国的社会，曾经有过相当的贡献。但是，不幸得很，基督教传到中国不久以后，帝国主义便在中国开始活动，又因为把基督教传到中国来的人们，主要地都是从这些帝国主义国家来的，基督教同帝国主义，便在有意无意、有形无形之中发生了关系。现在中国的革命胜利了。帝国主义对中国历史这一个空前的事实，是不会甘心的。它们一定要用尽千方百计，企图破坏这个既成的事实；它们也会利用基督教，去进行它们挑拨离间，企图在中国制造反动力量的阴谋。为要提高我们对帝国主义的警惕，为要表示基督教在新中国中鲜明的政治立场，为要促成一个为中国人自己所主持的中国教会，为要指出全国的基督徒对新中国建设所应当负起的责任，我们发表了下面这个文件。我们愿意号召全国的基督徒，为实现这个文件所提供的原则而努力。
  总的任务
    中国基督教教会及团体彻底拥护共同纲领，在政府的领导下，反对帝国主义、封建主义及官僚资本主义，为建设一个独立、民主、和平、统一和富强的新中国而奋斗。
  基本方针
    （一）中国基督教教会及团体应以最大的努力，及有效的方法，使教会群众清楚地认识帝国主义在中国所造成的罪恶，认识过去帝国主义利用基督教的事实，肃清基督教内部的帝国主义影响，警惕帝国主义、尤其是美帝国主义利用宗教以培养反动力量的阴谋，同时号召他们参加反对战争、拥护和平的运动，并教育他们彻底了解及拥护政府的土地改革政策。
    （二）中国基督教教会及团体应用有效的办法，培养一般信徒爱国民主的精神，和自尊自信的心理。中国基督教过去所倡导的自治、自养、自传的运动，已有相当成就，今后应在最短期内，完成此项任务；同时提倡自我批评，在各种工作上，实行检讨整理，精简节约，以达到基督教革新的目标。
  具体办法
    （一）中国基督教教会及团体，凡仍仰赖外国人才与经济之协助者，应拟定具体计划，在最短期内，实现自力更生的目标。
    （二）今后基督教教会及团体，在宗教工作方面，应注重基督本质的深刻认识，宗派间的团结、领导人才的培养、和教会制度的改进；在一般工作方面，应注重反帝、反封建、反官僚资本主义的教育，及劳动生产、认识时代、文娱活动、识字教育、医药卫生、儿童保育等为人民服务的工作。
