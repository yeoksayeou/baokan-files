Title: Student Representatives from Various Places in Japan Gather in Tokyo to Oppose Yoshida's "Purge" of Progressive Professors
Author:
Date: 1950-09-21
Page: 4
　　
　　Student Representatives from Various Places in Japan Gather in Tokyo
    Oppose Yoshida's "Purge" of Progressive Professors
    [Xinhua News Agency, 19th] The Yoshida reactionary government's conspiracy to "purge" progressive professors has been met with opposition from Japanese students. According to a Reuters dispatch from Tokyo: Under the auspices of the All-Japan Student Self-Government Association Federation (Zengakuren), 70 student representatives from 43 universities across Japan held a meeting in Tokyo on the 17th and 18th. The meeting discussed how to launch a movement to oppose the reactionary measures of the Yoshida government to "purge" progressive professors. Zengakuren also issued a statement stating: Zengakuren will resist any measures to "purge" progressive professors.
    According to Kyodo News Agency, Tokyo, 18th, the Yoshida government has decided to "purge" progressive faculty members. The Ministry of Education plans to begin reviewing faculty members before mid-October, and the ministry is currently busy formulating review standards and carrying out other preparatory work.

### 日各地学生代表在东京集会  反对吉田“整肃”进步教授

1950-09-21
第4版()
专栏：

　　日各地学生代表在东京集会
    反对吉田“整肃”进步教授
    【新华社十九日讯】日本吉田反动政府企图“整肃”进步教授的阴谋计划，已遭到日本学生的反对。据路透社东京讯：在全日学生自治会联合会（全日学联）的主持下，来自日本各地四十三所大学的七十名学生代表，于十七、十八两日在东京举行会议。会议讨论了如何展开运动以反对吉田政府即将实行的“整肃”进步教授的反动措施。全日学联并发表声明称：全日学联将抵抗任何“整肃”进步教授的措施。
    另据共同社东京十八日讯，吉田政府已决定“整肃”进步教职员。文部省计划在十月中旬以前开始审查教职员，该部现正忙于制订审查标准及进行其他准备工作。
