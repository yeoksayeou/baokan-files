Title: Capital Hui People Celebrate Eid al-Adha Festival; Government Exempts Cattle and Sheep Slaughter Tax
Author: Feng Wancai
Date: 1950-09-24
Page: 3

　　Capital Hui People Celebrate Eid al-Adha Festival
    Government Exempts Cattle and Sheep Slaughter Tax
    [Beijing Municipal News Agency] September 24th (August 13th of the lunar calendar) is the Eid al-Adha (Sacrifice Festival) for the Hui people. According to Hui customs, cattle and sheep are slaughtered on this day to express the meaning of sacrifice. The slaughtered cattle and sheep are not only for their own consumption, but the remainder is given to relatives and friends. In order to take care of the customs of ethnic minorities, the government has decided to exempt the cattle and sheep slaughter tax for the Eid al-Adha celebration. These commemorative ceremonies will be held in various mosques throughout the city.
        (Feng Wancai)

### 首都回民纪念古尔巴尼节  政府免征此项牛羊屠宰税
冯万才
1950-09-24
第3版()
专栏：

　　首都回民纪念古尔巴尼节
    政府免征此项牛羊屠宰税
    【京市新闻处讯】九月二十四日（旧历八月十三日）为回民古尔巴尼节（宰牲节），按回民风俗习惯，是日宰牛羊牲畜表示牺牲之意，其所宰之牛羊除自己食用之外，并将其余部分丑赠亲友。政府为照顾少数民族风俗习惯，决定免征纪念古尔巴尼节用的牛羊屠宰税。此项纪念仪式将在全市各清真寺分别举行。
        （冯万才）
