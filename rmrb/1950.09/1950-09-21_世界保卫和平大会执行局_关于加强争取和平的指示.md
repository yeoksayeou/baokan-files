Title: Executive Bureau of the World Peace Congress: Instructions on Strengthening the Struggle for Peace
Author: 
Date: 1950-09-21
Page: 6

　　The Secretariat of the Chinese Committee for the Defense of World Peace has received a letter dated August 29th from the Secretariat of the World Peace Congress Committee in Paris. The original letter is as follows:

Dear Friends:

　　The Executive Bureau of the World Peace Congress Committee concluded its 19th meeting held in Prague on August 16th and 17th.

　　The Executive Bureau adopted a notice convening the Second World Peace Congress and also adopted a recommendation to the United Nations Security Council on the peaceful resolution of the Korean conflict. These documents were sent to you on August 28th.

　　The Prague meeting of the Executive Bureau demonstrated with righteous pride the tremendous growth of our movement and particularly emphasized the worldwide response to the Stockholm Declaration banning atomic weapons.

　　At a time when the worsening international situation has brought new concerns to the people of the world, the Prague resolutions clearly explain the principles and objectives of our movement, thus providing us with the necessary instructions to continue to advance, expand, and strengthen our struggle for peace. On this basis, the next step is to prepare to send delegations to the Second World Peace Congress to be held in the United Kingdom from November 13th to 19th.

　　The preparatory work for the Second Congress should therefore become a large-scale movement, intensifying action against atomic weapons, while also beginning to include other aspects of the peace issue.

　　We believe that each national congress committee should immediately study the above-mentioned documents and inform us of the relevant policies and practical decisions regarding the preparatory work for the Second Congress.

　　We suggest that you use newspapers, radio, leaflets, posters, rallies, press conferences, etc., to publicize the Prague resolutions as widely as possible. We suggest that, based on the Prague resolutions, you request the full support of all institutions and organizations that have already participated in the peace movement, and, in accordance with the declaration issued in Prague, strive for the support of other groups and peace-loving organizations.

　　In accordance with the Prague resolutions, we suggest that each national congress committee convey the recommendations on the peaceful resolution of the Korean conflict to the government by appropriate means.

　　With kind regards,

　　Secretary-General of the World Peace Congress Committee,  La Fayette

　　Paris, August 29, 1950

Furthermore:

　　Given that in some cases passport visas may be refused, it is essential that you send us the list of delegates as soon as possible.

　　We recommend applying for visas as early as possible to avoid rejection on the pretext of late application.

　　Attached is an editorial from our publication analyzing the Prague resolutions.

　　Editor's Note: According to the resolutions of the Prague meeting of the Executive Bureau of the World Peace Congress Committee, the Chinese Committee for the Defense of World Peace has established a preparatory office for the Second World Peace Congress at the Academy of Sciences, actively preparing for the procedures for the Chinese delegation to participate in the Second World Peace Congress to go abroad, and widely collecting information and documents on the Chinese people's defense of world peace and opposition to American imperialist aggression, for distribution and reference by the delegation when attending the congress. In addition, a congress fund of US$20,000 has been raised. The letter from the World Peace Congress Committee points out that the preparatory work for the Second Congress should become a large-scale movement, intensifying action against atomic weapons and carrying out struggles in other aspects of defending world peace. We hope that all national institutions and organizations that have already participated in the movement to defend world peace will fully support the Prague resolutions in accordance with this instruction.

### 世界保卫和平大会执行局  关于加强争取和平的指示

1950-09-21
第6版()
专栏：

　　世界保卫和平大会执行局  关于加强争取和平的指示
    中国保卫世界和平大会委员会秘书处顷接巴黎世界保卫和平大会委员会秘书处八月二十九日来信，原信如下：亲爱的朋友们：
    世界保卫和平大会委员会执行局，于八月十六、十七两日在布拉格举行的第十九次会议，顷已结束。
    执行局通过了召开第二次世界保卫和平大会的通告，又通过了致联合国安全理事会关于和平解决朝鲜冲突的建议。这些文件已在八月二十八日寄给你们。
    执行局在布拉格的会议以正义的骄傲表明了我们运动的巨大增长，并特别强调指出了全世界对于斯德哥尔摩宣言禁用原子武器的反应。
    当国际形势恶化，给全世界人民带来了新的忧虑的时候，布拉格的决议，清楚的说明了我们这一运动的原则和目标，因此就给我们一个必要的指示，使我们继续前进，扩大并加强我们为和平的斗争。在这个基础上，次一步骤就在于准备派遣代表团参加十一月十三日至十九日在英国召开的第二次世界保卫和平大会。
    第二次和大的筹备工作，因此要成为一个大规模的运动，加紧行动反对原子武器，同时使这一运动开始包括和平问题的其他方面。
    我们觉得每一国家的和大委员会，应该立即研究上述文件，把有关第二次和大筹备工作的政策和实际决定通知我们。
    我们建议请你们用报纸、广播、传单、招贴、集会、记者招待会等等方式，尽可能的广泛宣传布拉格决议。我们建议请你们根据布拉格决议，要求已经参加和平运动的各机关团体全力支持，并按照在布拉格发出的宣言，争取其他团体和爱好和平组织的支持。
    依照布拉格决议，我们建议每一国家的和大委员会，把关于和平解决朝鲜冲突的建议，用适当的方法传达给政府。此致敬礼
        世界保卫和平大会委员会秘书长  拉斐德
          一九五○年八月二十九日于巴黎再者：
    鉴于在有些情况下，护照签证要被拒绝，必须请你们把代表名单赶快寄来。
    我们建议尽早申请签证，以免借口申请过迟而遭拒绝。
    附送我们刊物上所登载的分析布拉格决议的社论一篇。
    编者按：中国保卫世界和平大会委员会根据了世界保卫和平大会委员会执行局布拉格会议的决议，业已在科学院设立第二次保卫世界和平大会筹备处，积极筹备中国参加第二次保卫世界和平大会代表团的出国手续，并广泛征集关于中国人民保卫世界和平反对美帝侵略的各项资料及文件，以备代表团出席和大时的分送和参考。此外，并已募集了和大基金美金二万元。世界保卫和平大会委员会这一信上指出：第二次和大的筹备工作要成为一个大规模的运动，加紧行动，反对原子武器，并在保卫世界和平问题的其他方面进行斗争。我们希望业已参加保卫世界和平运动的全国各机关团体，按照这一指示，全力支持布拉格决议。
