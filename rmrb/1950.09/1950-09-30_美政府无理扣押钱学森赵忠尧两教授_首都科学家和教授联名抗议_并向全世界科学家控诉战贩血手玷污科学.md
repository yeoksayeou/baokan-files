　　US Government Unjustifiably Detains Professors Qian Xuesen and Zhao Zhongyao
    Scientists and Professors in the Capital Jointly Protest
    And Accuse War Profiteers of Tainting Science to Scientists Worldwide
    [Reported by our newspaper] Scientists and university professors in Beijing, including Zeng Zhaolun, Ye Qisun, Lu Zhiwei, Ding Xielin, Rao Yutai, Xiang Da, Liu Xianzhou, Tang Peisong, Qian Chongshu, Yan Jici, Qian Sanqiang, and 189 others, have issued the following protest against the unreasonable detention of Dr. Qian Xuesen and Professor Zhao Zhongyao by US imperialism:
    We protest with utmost anger against the US government's illegal detention of Dr. Qian Xuesen, who was returning from the United States, and the unreasonable detention by the US occupation forces in Japan of Professor Zhao Zhongyao and students Luo Shijun and Shen Shanjiong, who were en route to return to China. We believe that this series of atrocities violating personal freedom is yet another manifestation of the US imperialists' determination to be enemies of the Chinese people by all possible means, which is both despicable and insane.
    Facts have proven that the Chinese people's love for their great motherland is the same whether they are abroad or at home. Despite the fact that US imperialism has spent a lot of money trying to entice overseas students not to return to China, the so-called "American way of life" has no allure for the Chinese people. Chinese students are still returning to the embrace of the motherland batch after batch, filled with hatred for American imperialism. Therefore, when enticement failed, it was followed by threats, and when threats failed, it was followed by persecution. If the US imperialists think that this is enough to scare the Chinese from returning, that is completely delusional. The Chinese people's strong and unyielding will will be fully demonstrated in every corner.
    We would like to solemnly tell the US imperialists that the Chinese people's love for their great motherland will never be intimidated by the imperialists' various persecutions. We believe that Chinese professors and students in the United States will continue to break through all difficulties to return to the motherland, and will continue to expose the truth about the so-called "American way of life" and the "democracy" and "freedom" manufactured by the United States to peace-loving people all over the world.
    We want to accuse scientists all over the world of how the dignity of "science" is being tainted by the bloody hands of war profiteers. For the sake of our cause, for the sake of our next generation, and for the sake of peace and happiness for all mankind, scientists must unite and resolutely fight the war profiteers to the end. Facts teach us that only by completely eliminating the war profiteers can we have the freedom to conduct scientific research in the future!
    Finally, we resolutely demand that all relevant US authorities immediately release Dr. Qian Xuesen, Professor Zhao Zhongyao, and other detainees, allow them to return to their motherland, and guarantee that similar incidents will not occur in the future.

### 美政府无理扣押钱学森赵忠尧两教授  首都科学家和教授联名抗议  并向全世界科学家控诉战贩血手玷污科学

1950-09-30
第4版()
专栏：

　　美政府无理扣押钱学森赵忠尧两教授
    首都科学家和教授联名抗议
    并向全世界科学家控诉战贩血手玷污科学
    【本报讯】北京市科学家、大学教授曾昭抡、叶企孙、陆志韦、丁燮林、饶毓泰、向达、刘仙洲、汤佩松、钱崇澍、严济慈、钱三强等一百八十九人为美帝无理扣留钱学森博士赵忠尧教授特发表抗议如下：
    我们以无比的愤怒抗议美国政府非法地扣押将自美返国的钱学森博士，在日本的美占领军当局无理地扣留了返国途中的赵忠尧教授和罗时钧、沈善炯两位同学。我们认为这一连串的侵犯人身自由的暴行是美国帝国主义者想尽种种方法决心与中国人民为敌的又一次表现，既卑劣又疯狂。
    事实证明中国人民对自己伟大祖国的热爱，无论身在国外或国内都是一样的。尽管美帝国主义花了不少钱想诱骗留学生不要回国，可是事实上所谓“美国生活方式”对中国人民并无任何诱惑的力量，中国留学生还是一批一批地满怀着憎恨美帝的心情返回到祖国的怀抱。于是利诱既不成，继之以威胁，威胁不成，继之以迫害，如果美帝国主义者，认为这样便足以使中国人吓得不敢回来，那完全是梦想。中国人民坚强不屈的意志，在任何一个角落里都会充分表现出来的。
    我们愿在此正告美国帝国主义者，中国人民对于伟大的祖国的热爱决不是帝国主义者各种各样的迫害所能吓倒。我们相信，留美的中国教授学生们将继续不断的冲破一切的困难回到祖国来，也将在全世界爱好和平的人们面前继续不断地揭露美帝的所谓“美国生活方式”和美国制造的“民主”与“自由”的真相。
    我们要向全世界的科学工作者控诉“科学”的尊严正如何遭受着战争贩子血手的玷污。为了我们的事业，为了我们的下一代，为了全人类的和平和幸福，科学工作者必须团结起来和战争贩子坚决斗争到底。事实教训我们，只有把战争贩子彻底消灭，以后我们才能有科学研究的自由！
    最后，我们坚决地要美国的一切有关当局立即释放钱学森博士，赵忠尧教授和其他被扣留的人，让他们返回祖国并保证以后不得发生类似事件。
