Title: Opposition to the Vietnam War and Racial Discrimination
Author:
Date: 1968-05-03
Edition: 5

New York, May 2 (Xinhua) - On April 30, thousands of faculty and students at Columbia University in New York City clashed with over a thousand fascist police and mounted police in fierce struggles. The university decided to suspend classes, bringing the students' struggle against the Vietnam War and racial discrimination, which began on April 23, to a new high.

To oppose racial discrimination and demand the withdrawal of the Defense Department's "scientific research institution" serving the Vietnam War from Columbia University, many white and black students at the university began a sit-in and protest on April 23. By April 24, the protesting students had occupied five buildings, searched and taken over the president's office, and detained the academic dean and two assistants for more than 20 hours, forcing Columbia University to suspend classes starting April 26.

The struggle at Columbia University received strong support from the black community in the neighboring Harlem district. On April 25, many black residents from Harlem came to support the students' struggle. On April 26, a large number of high school students, mostly black, rushed to Columbia University to express their support. The panicked reactionary authorities dispatched a large number of police to blockade Columbia University in an attempt to cut off communication between the students and the outside world.

In the early hours of April 30, the reactionary authorities sent more than a thousand fully armed police to storm Columbia University and launch a savage fascist suppression of the students. The protesting students stood firm in the five occupied buildings. The fascist police used crowbars and saws to break into the buildings, beating and arresting the students for over an hour. About 600 students were arrested on the spot, but the students, undeterred by the violence, sang struggle songs even while in police custody.

The reactionary authorities' savage suppression enraged thousands of faculty and students at Columbia University, who rushed from all directions to support the protesting students and engaged in fierce struggles with the police wielding batons and mounted police charging through the crowd. In the afternoon of April 30, the students held a mass meeting on campus, deciding to suspend classes to protest the police brutality and demanding the immediate resignation of the university president and vice president. At the same time, about 250 faculty members issued a statement and went on strike to support the protesting students.

The struggle at Columbia University has won the sympathy and support of people from all walks of life in New York City. It is reported that many schools in New York City have begun sympathetic strikes. Some students at the State University of New York have occupied an administrative building of the university on May 1.

### 反对侵越战争　反对种族歧视　美国纽约哥伦比亚大学师生同军警激烈搏斗　正义斗争得到黑人群众大力支持，许多学校举行同情罢课

1968-05-03
第5版()
专栏：

　　反对侵越战争　反对种族歧视
　　美国纽约哥伦比亚大学师生同军警激烈搏斗
　　正义斗争得到黑人群众大力支持，许多学校举行同情罢课
    新华社二日讯　纽约消息：美国纽约市哥伦比亚大学的广大师生四月三十日同千余名法西斯警察和骑警进行了激烈的搏斗，并决定全校罢课，从而使这个学校学生在四月二十三日掀起的反对侵越战争和反对种族歧视的斗争达到新的高潮。
    为了反对种族歧视，要求为侵越战争服务的国防部“科学研究机构”撤离哥伦比亚大学，这个学校的许多白人和黑人学生从四月二十三日起就开始举行静坐示威。到二十四日，示威学生占领了五座大楼，搜查并接管了校长办公室，把教务长和两名助理扣留了二十多小时，迫使哥伦比亚大学不得不从四月二十六日起开始停课。
    哥伦比亚大学学生的斗争特别得到了这个学校邻区哈莱姆区黑人群众的大力支持。二十五日就有不少哈莱姆区的黑人群众前往支援哥伦比亚大学学生的斗争。二十六日，又有大批中学生，其中大部分是黑人中学生，赶到哥伦比亚大学表示支持这个学校学生的斗争。惊慌失措的反动当局派出大批警察封锁了哥伦比亚大学，妄图割断学生和外界的联系。
    四月三十日凌晨，反动当局竟然出动一千余名全副武装的警察，冲进哥伦比亚大学对学生进行疯狂的法西斯镇压。示威学生固守在占领的五座大楼之内毫不动摇，法西斯警察用撬棍和钢锯破坏了建筑物，冲进大楼，对示威学生进行了一个多小时的野蛮殴打和疯狂逮捕，约有六百余名学生当场被捕，但学生们不畏强暴，英勇斗争，被捕的学生在警察署中还高唱斗争歌曲。
    反动当局的野蛮镇压激起了哥伦比亚大学数千名师生的极大愤怒，他们从四面八方赶来，声援示威学生，同乱挥棍棒的警察和在人群中横冲直撞的骑警进行了激烈的搏斗。学生们在四月三十日下午在校内举行群众大会，决定全校罢课，抗议警察的暴行，并要求哥伦比亚大学校长和副校长立即辞职。与此同时，约有二百五十名教员也发表声明，举行罢教，支持示威学生的斗争。
    哥伦比亚大学学生的斗争，赢得了纽约市各阶层人民的同情和支持。据报道，纽约市许多学校已开始举行同情罢课。纽约州立大学的一些学生已于五月一日占领了这个大学的一所行政大楼。
