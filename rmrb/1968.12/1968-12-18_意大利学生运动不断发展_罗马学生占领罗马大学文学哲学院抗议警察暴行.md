Title: Italian Student Movement Continues to Develop
Author:
Date: 1968-12-18
Edition: 5

The wave of student struggles against the decadent capitalist educational system sweeping across Italy continues to develop. On the 15th, just 24 hours after the new government in Rome took office, 300 students occupied the Faculty of Literature and Philosophy at the University of Rome to protest police repression and to firmly demand educational reform.

In Sanremo, thousands of students, with the support of workers, demonstrated outside a hall where an international boxing match was taking place. The demonstrators waved flags and held up signs reading, "We want fewer competitions, more schools," protesting the municipal government's allocation of funds to competitions rather than increasing educational expenditures for schools.

Student demonstrations also took place in Naples and Sardinia. The National Association of University Professors, Rome University Branch, also publicly demanded an increase in university funding.

### 意大利学生运动不断发展  罗马学生占领罗马大学文学哲学院抗议警察暴行

1968-12-18
第5版()
专栏：

    意大利学生运动不断发展
    罗马学生占领罗马大学文学哲学院抗议警察暴行
    本报讯　席卷意大利全国的反对腐朽资本主义教育制度的学生斗争浪潮在继续发展。十五日，在鲁莫尔新政府宣誓就职刚刚过了二十四小时，罗马的三百名学生占领了罗马大学文学哲学院，以抗议警察镇压和坚决要求教育改革。
    在圣雷莫，几千名学生在工人的支持下，在一个正在举行国际拳击比赛的大厅外面挥动旗帜，举行示威。示威群众举着“我们要少进行比赛，多开办学校”的标语牌，抗议市政当局把钱用在比赛上，而不愿增加学校的教育经费。
    在那不勒斯和撒丁也发生了学生示威。全国教授协会罗马大学分会也公开提出增加大学经费的要求。
