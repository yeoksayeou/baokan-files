　　　　Between Master and Servant
　　Indian Prime Minister Indira Gandhi said in Washington: “India is as important to the United States as the United States is to India.” It's truly embarrassing that she could utter such nauseating words.
　　However, what the Indian Prime Minister said reflects the real situation. The Indian reactionaries cannot do without the support of U.S. imperialism, and U.S. imperialism also needs the help of the Indian reactionaries. The two sides are like Robinson Crusoe and "Friday" in the writings of the English writer Daniel Defoe. Neither can do without the other, and it is difficult to say who is more important to whom.

### 主仆之间

1966-04-03
第5版()
专栏：三言两语

    主仆之间
    印度总理英迪拉·甘地在华盛顿说：“印度对美国来说同美国对印度来说一样重要”。这样肉麻的话，真亏她讲得出口。
    但是，印度总理说的倒是实在情况。印度反动派不能没有美帝国主义撑腰，美帝国主义也需要印度反动派帮忙。双方有如英国文学家达尼尔·笛福笔下的鲁滨逊和“礼拜五”，谁也离不开谁，很难说谁对谁更重要。
