Title: U.S. Imperialist Plunder of the Dominican Republic; U.S. Monopoly Capital's Investment in the Dominican Republic Reaches Over $200 Million, Squeezing Out $25 Million in Profits Annually

Author:
Date: 1966-04-30
Edition: 5

    U.S. imperialism is the largest landowner in the Dominican Republic. According to the Dominican newspaper *June Fourteenth* (*El 14 de Junio*), "The large plantation system is the primary tool of penetration used by U.S. imperialism to exploit our country, because the largest plantations belong to U.S. imperialist companies or their local servants who unconditionally obey them." The rural population of the Dominican Republic accounts for about 70 percent of the total population, and landless poor peasants account for more than 80 percent of the rural population, while the vast majority of the land is concentrated in the hands of domestic and American plantation owners.

    U.S. monopoly capital controls the Dominican Republic's finance and banking through Citibank and Chase Manhattan Bank. According to the Dominican reactionary authorities, U.S. investment in the Dominican Republic was $250 million in 1961. U.S. monopoly groups admit that they receive an average of $25 million in huge profits from the Dominican Republic each year. U.S. monopoly capital controls most of the Dominican Republic's foreign trade. The United States is the main buyer of the Dominican Republic's four most important export commodities—sugar (80 percent), coffee (89 percent), cocoa (99 percent), and bauxite (all).

    U.S. monopoly capital has turned the Dominican Republic into a country that produces only raw materials. Sugar is its main product, and sugar exports account for 60 percent of the country's total exports. Sixteen of the nineteen sugar mills in the Dominican Republic belong to U.S. monopoly capital.

    The United Fruit Company, called the "Green Devil," has a subsidiary in the Dominican Republic called the Grenada Company. It occupies more than 1,700 hectares of banana plantations and about 3,000 hectares of fertile land in the Dominican Republic. This company earns more than $8 million in profits each year.

    Alcoa Exploration Company, a branch of Alcoa, mines rich bauxite deposits in southwestern Dominican Republic. Its investment is less than $17 million, but it earns $8 million in profits each year.

    U.S. monopoly capital also controls the Dominican Republic's economic lifelines such as electricity, fuel, telegraph, telephone, sea and air transport, and port facilities.

    The United States uses the Dominican Republic's cheap labor to extract super-profits. It is reported that sugar workers in the Dominican Republic work up to twelve hours a day, but their income is meager, earning only $1.30 a day. Agricultural workers earn even less, only $0.30 to $0.40 a day.

    Since the U.S. carried out armed intervention and military occupation of the Dominican Republic last April, the Dominican economy has been further severely damaged, prices have soared, industrial and commercial enterprises have closed down one after another, and the number of unemployed has surged to 400,000, more than one-tenth of the country's population.

　　　　　　　
　　　　(Fan)

### 美帝国主义对多米尼加的掠夺  美国垄断资本在多米尼加的投资已达两亿多美元，每年榨取的利润达两千五百万美元

1966-04-30
第5版()
专栏：国际知识

    美帝国主义对多米尼加的掠夺
    美国垄断资本在多米尼加的投资已达两亿多美元，每年榨取的利润达两千五百万美元
    美帝国主义是多米尼加最大的地主。据多米尼加《六月十四日报》揭露：“大庄园制是美帝国主义为了剥削我国而使用的主要渗透工具，因为最大的庄园是属于美帝国主义的公司或无条件服从它的本地仆从的”。多米尼加农村人口约占总人口的百分之七十，无地的贫雇农占农村人口的百分之八十以上，而绝大部分土地集中在本国和美国庄园主手中。
    美国垄断资本通过花旗银行、曼哈顿—大通银行对多米尼加财政金融进行控制。据多米尼加反动当局公布，美国在多米尼加的投资一九六一年为两亿五千万美元。美国垄断集团承认，它们平均每年从多米尼加得到两千五百万美元的巨额利润。美国垄断资本控制了多米尼加的大部分对外贸易。美国是多米尼加最重要的四项出口商品——糖（百分之八十）、咖啡（百分之八十九）、可可（百分之九十九）、铁矾土（全部）的主要购买者。
    美国垄断资本把多米尼加变成单一生产原料的国家。糖是它的主要产品，食糖出口占国家全部出口总值的百分之六十。多米尼加全国十九家制糖厂中，有十六家是属于美国垄断资本的。
    被叫做“绿色魔鬼”的美国联合果品公司，在多米尼加有一个名叫格雷纳达公司的附属公司。它霸占了多米尼加一千七百多公顷的香蕉种植园和约三千公顷的肥沃土地。这家公司每年获得的利润在八百万美元以上。
    美国铝公司的一个分支机构阿尔考开采公司，在多米尼加西南部开采丰富的铁矾土。它的投资不足一千七百万美元，但是它每年获得的利润达八百万美元。
    美国垄断资本还控制着多米尼加的电力、燃料、电报、电话、海空运输、港口设备等国家经济命脉。
    美国利用多米尼加的廉价劳动力榨取超额利润。据报道，多米尼加的糖业工人每天工作长达十二小时，但是收入微薄，每天只得工资一点三美元。而农业工人的工资更少，每天只有零点三至零点四美元。
    自从去年四月美国对多米尼加实行武装干涉和军事占领以来，多米尼加经济进一步遭到严重破坏，物价飞涨，工商企业纷纷倒闭，失业人数激增到四十万人，超过全国人口的十分之一。　　　　　　　
　　　　（凡）
