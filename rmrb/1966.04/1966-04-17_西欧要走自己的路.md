　　《美国新闻与世界报道》四月十一日刊登一篇报道，摘译如下：
　　戴高乐已告诉美国在一年内把它的军队撤出法国。法国将在七月前结束同北大西洋公约组织盟国的一切军事合作。
　　所发生的事情是，旧式的民族主义正在重新兴起，打击了一度看来是会永久存在下去的联盟。
　　对美国来说，其结果显然将是更多而不是更少的麻烦。
　　欧洲的前景在一段时期内（大概是一段长时期）将出现自然而然的危险倾向。
　　戴高乐已表明自己能够摧毁象北大西洋公约组织和共同市场这样一些国际组织。然而看来奇怪的是，其它欧洲人并未为戴高乐的破坏行动所动。
　　为什么？本刊驻巴黎记者的答案如下：
　　“戴高乐正竭力促进法国的民族利益。他在取得成功。现在其它欧洲人想要强调他们自己的民族利益，有他们自己的对外政策。同美国的密切合作看来已不那么重要了。”
　　来自巴黎的这个报道最后说：
　　“北大西洋公约组织没有法国也能存在下去，代价是美国付出也许是十亿到二十亿美元。但是，美国离开北大西洋公约组织是什么？对同美国结成大西洋共同体不再感兴趣的欧洲人正在越来越走他们自己的道路。联盟正在迅速变成一条单行线。”

　　　　　　

Summary of Foreign Commentary
Western Europe to Take Its Own Path
*U.S. News & World Report* published a report on April 11th, excerpted and translated as follows:

De Gaulle has told the United States to withdraw its troops from France within a year. France will end all military cooperation with its NATO allies by July.

What is happening is that old-fashioned nationalism is re-emerging, striking a blow against alliances that once seemed would last forever.

For the United States, the result will obviously be more, not fewer, troubles.

The prospects for Europe will, for a period of time (probably a long period), show naturally dangerous tendencies.

De Gaulle has shown himself capable of destroying international organizations such as NATO and the Common Market. Yet, strangely enough, other Europeans have not been moved by De Gaulle's destructive actions.

Why? The answer from this magazine's Paris correspondent is as follows:

"De Gaulle is striving to promote the national interests of France. He is succeeding. Now other Europeans want to emphasize their own national interests and have their own foreign policies. Close cooperation with the United States seems less important."

This report from Paris concludes:

"NATO can survive without France, at the cost of perhaps one to two billion dollars for the United States. But what is the United States leaving NATO for? Europeans who are no longer interested in forming an Atlantic community with the United States are increasingly going their own way. The alliance is rapidly becoming a one-way street."
