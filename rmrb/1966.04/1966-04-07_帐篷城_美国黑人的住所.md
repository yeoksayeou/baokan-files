Author: 
Date: 1966-04-07
Edition: 5

    "Tent Cities" –
    Homes of Black Americans

    In the southern United States, in states like Alabama and in the "Triangle Area" along the Mississippi River from Memphis to Vicksburg, some "tent cities" have emerged. The residents of these "tent cities" are displaced black people.

    According to the *New York Times*, in Alabama, some black people and their families were evicted by landlords for participating in voter registration, leaving them with no place to live but temporarily erected "tent cities."

    In the "Triangle Area," 12,000 black agricultural workers and tenant farmers have been displaced due to the mechanization of farming, forced out of their plantations. Many of them are also living in tents, exposed to the harsh winds.

    The American weekly *The Nation* said that U.S. Representative Resnick (of New York) took a trip to some of the "tent cities" in the American South. Upon returning to Washington, he submitted a report to the U.S. government stating that the situation in the "tent cities" and the sentiments of black people in Mississippi could lead to "a comprehensive guerrilla war that will shame us all." He said that unless the Johnson administration takes immediate action, "the events in Watts will be but a tea party compared to what will happen in Mississippi."

### “帐篷城”——美国黑人的住所

1966-04-07
第5版()
专栏：资料

    “帐篷城”——
    美国黑人的住所
    在美国南部亚拉巴马州和沿密西西比河从孟菲斯到维克斯堡的“三角地带”等地，出现了一些“帐篷城”。“帐篷城”的居民是流离失所的黑人。
    据《纽约时报》报道，在亚拉巴马州，一些黑人因为参加了选举登记，他们和他们的家庭就被房主驱逐，无处安身，只得住在临时搭成的“帐篷城”里。
    在“三角地带”，有一万二千名黑人农业工人和佃农，由于耕作机械化而遭受排挤，被逐出庄园。他们中间也有不少的人，在帐篷里栖身，餐风露宿在凛冽的寒风之中。
    美国《民族》周刊说，美国众议员雷斯尼克（纽约州）到美国南部出现的一些“帐篷城”去跑了一趟，回到华盛顿以后，向美国政府提出报告说，“帐篷城”的情况和密西西比黑人的情绪，可能造成“使我们大家丢脸的全面的游击战争”。他说，除非约翰逊政府立即采取行动，否则“在瓦茨区发生的事件与将要在密西西比发生的事件比较起来，只不过是一次茶话会而已”。
