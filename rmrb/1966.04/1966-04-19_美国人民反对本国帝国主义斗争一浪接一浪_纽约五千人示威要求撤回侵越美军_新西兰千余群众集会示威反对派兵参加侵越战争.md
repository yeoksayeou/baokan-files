Title: The American People's Struggle Against Their Own Imperialism Rises Wave Upon Wave. 5,000 People Demonstrate in New York Demanding the Withdrawal of U.S. Troops from the Invasion of Vietnam. More Than a Thousand People in New Zealand Rally and Demonstrate Against Sending Troops to Participate in the War of Aggression Against Vietnam.
Author: 
Date: 1966-04-19
Edition: 3

    (Xinhua News Agency, April 17th dispatch) New York News: In New York, the largest city in the United States, nearly 5,000 people demonstrated in the city center on the 15th to protest the Johnson administration's war of aggression against Vietnam. The demonstrators gathered near the New York Public Library, formed neat ranks, and marched into Times Square, led by veterans and reservists who opposed the American war of aggression against Vietnam, and paraded around the square. Many people in the demonstration held signs demanding the withdrawal of American troops from the invasion of Vietnam and opposing the government's policies in Vietnam.
    The authorities dispatched several hundred police officers, including many mounted police, to closely monitor the demonstration along the way. A small group of reactionaries gathered in Times Square to hurl abuse at the demonstrators in an attempt to disrupt the march. However, the demonstrators ignored all of this and continued to advance according to the original plan. The demonstration lasted for about two hours and ended after the procession reached Broadway.
    On the same day, about forty women held their weekly demonstration in Times Square to protest the war of aggression against Vietnam.
    (Xinhua News Agency, Wellington, April 17th) Hundreds of New Zealanders held two rallies on the evenings of the 16th and 17th in Christchurch to oppose the American war of aggression against Vietnam and the New Zealand government's sending of troops to participate in this war of aggression. From the evening of the 15th, Christchurch launched activities to oppose the war of aggression against Vietnam, including protest rallies and demonstrations. More than a thousand New Zealanders participated in these activities. (Attached photo)
    On the evening of April 12th, approximately two thousand Americans held a rally in Berkeley, near San Francisco, to protest American aggression against Vietnam. Fax photo (Xinhua News Agency)

### 美国人民反对本国帝国主义斗争一浪接一浪  纽约五千人示威要求撤回侵越美军  新西兰千余群众集会示威反对派兵参加侵越战争

1966-04-19
第3版()
专栏：

    美国人民反对本国帝国主义斗争一浪接一浪
    纽约五千人示威要求撤回侵越美军
    新西兰千余群众集会示威反对派兵参加侵越战争
    据新华社十七日讯　纽约消息：在美国第一大城市纽约，十五日又有将近五千人在市中心示威，反对约翰逊政府侵略越南的战争。示威者在纽约公共图书馆附近集合，排成整齐的队伍，由反对美国侵略越南战争的退伍军人和后备役军人为前导，浩浩荡荡进入时报广场，并且绕着广场游行。示威队伍当中许多人举着标语牌，要求撤回侵越美军，并且反对政府在越南的政策。
    当局出动了好几百名警察，其中还有不少骑警，沿途严密监视示威游行的队伍。还有一小撮反动分子聚集在时报广场上辱骂示威者，企图扰乱游行。但是示威队伍毫不理会这一切，仍然按照原定计划前进。示威持续了大约两个小时，在队伍到达百老汇大街后，才宣告结束。
    同一天，时报广场上还有大约四十个妇女举行每周一次的示威，反对侵越战争。
    据新华社惠灵顿十七日电  数百名新西兰公众十六日晚上和十七日在克赖斯特彻奇市举行两次集会，反对美国侵越战争和反对新西兰政府派兵参加这个侵略战争。从十五日晚上开始，克赖斯特彻奇市展开了反对侵越战争的活动，包括举行抗议集会和示威游行等。参加这些活动的新西兰公众达一千多人。（附图片）
    四月十二日晚上，大约两千名美国人在旧金山附近的伯克利举行抗议美国侵略越南大会。传真照片（新华社发）
