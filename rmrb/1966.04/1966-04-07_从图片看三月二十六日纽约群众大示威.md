    Viewing the Mass Demonstration in New York on March 26th Through Pictures

    On March 26th, a massive demonstration of 100,000 people in New York protested the Johnson administration's war of aggression in Vietnam. This was the largest demonstration in American history of the people opposing their own country’s imperialism.

    At 1:15 PM, the mighty demonstration procession started from 94th Street, passed through Fifth Avenue, the most bustling street in New York, and converged on Central Park in New York, where a protest rally was finally held.

    Participating in the demonstration were many students, youth, veterans, Black people, workers, writers, artists, teachers, and people from other walks of life. During the march, the New York puppet theater also performed puppet shows on the street with the theme of opposing American aggression in Vietnam. Although a small number of secret agents, hooligans, and fascists sabotaged the march, the demonstrators resolutely marched towards their goal.

    The three photos on the left show some scenes from the marching procession.

    The top picture shows the 600 veterans and 200 women and children at the forefront of the march. The middle picture shows the Black contingent from Harlem, the Black residential area of New York. They held a huge banner that read, "Don't fight for American racists, call our Black soldiers back, Black people oppose the Vietnam War..." The bottom picture shows the student contingent participating in the demonstration. Their signs read: "Stop the Vietnam War immediately."

### 从图片看三月二十六日纽约群众大示威

1966-04-07
第5版()
专栏：

    从图片看三月二十六日纽约群众大示威
    三月二十六日，纽约十万公众举行了声势浩大的抗议约翰逊政府侵越战争的运动，这是美国有史以来规模最大的一次人民反对本国帝国主义的示威行动。
    下午一时十五分，浩浩荡荡的示威队伍从第九十四号街出发，穿过纽约最繁华的五马路，向纽约的中央公园集中，最后就在那里举行抗议集会。
    参加示威行列的，有许多学生、青年、退伍军人、黑人、工人、作家、艺术家、教师和其他各阶层人士。在游行过程中，纽约的木偶剧团还在街头表演了以反对美国侵越政策为题材的傀儡戏。尽管一小撮特务、流氓和法西斯分子对游行进行捣乱破坏，但是示威群众仍然坚决地向目标行进。
    左面三幅照片是游行队伍中的几个情景。
    上图是走在游行队伍最前列的六百名退伍军人和两百名妇女、儿童。中图是从纽约哈莱姆黑人聚居区前来的黑人队伍。他们举着“不要为美国种族主义者作战，把我们的黑人士兵召回来，黑人反对越南战争……”的巨幅标语。下图是参加示威行列的学生队伍，他们的标语上写着：“立即停止越南战争”。
