Title: The Fourth Plenary Meeting of the Secretariat of the Asian-African Journalists Association Issues Communiqué: Asian-African Journalists Strengthen Militant Unity and Firmly Support the Anti-Imperialist Struggle of the Asian-African People
Author: Liu Changzhong
Date: 1966-04-25
Edition: 3

    The Fourth Plenary Meeting of the Secretariat of the Asian-African Journalists Association Issues Communiqué
    Asian-African Journalists Strengthen Militant Unity and Firmly Support the Anti-Imperialist Struggle of the Asian-African People
    Xinhua News Agency, April 24th -- The communiqué of the Fourth Plenary Meeting (Enlarged) of the Secretariat of the Asian-African Journalists Association is as follows:
    The Secretariat of the Asian-African Journalists Association held its Fourth Plenary Meeting (Enlarged) in Beijing from April 20 to 24, 1966. Delegations from nineteen countries and regions attended this meeting, namely: Algeria, Azania (South Africa), Cambodia, Ceylon, China, Congo (Leopoldville), Guinea, Indonesia, Japan, Democratic People's Republic of Korea, Mali, Mozambique, Palestine, Syria, Tanzania, United Arab Republic, Democratic Republic of Vietnam, South Vietnam (National Liberation Front), and Zimbabwe. Delegations from Pakistan and Zambia were unable to attend but sent telegrams supporting the meeting.
    The Secretariat received a letter of support for this meeting from Cambodian Head of State Prince Norodom Sihanouk, expressing understanding for the Secretariat's withdrawal from Jakarta, and sympathy for the current meeting; it also received congratulatory telegrams from journalists' organizations and national liberation movements in many countries. Mr. Wan Li, Vice Mayor of Beijing, attended the opening ceremony and delivered a speech at the meeting. He spoke on behalf of the host country, extending a warm welcome to the delegates and congratulating the meeting.
    The meeting heard the report by Secretary-General Djawoto. Subsequently, the meeting conducted full and enthusiastic discussions.
    The meeting adopted a series of political resolutions, expressing unequivocal and firm support for the just struggles of the people of Vietnam, Congo (Leopoldville), Palestine, and other Asian-African countries against imperialism led by the United States and old and new colonialism. The meeting also strongly condemned the persecution of progressive journalists by imperialists and reactionaries in various countries.
    After hearing the Secretary-General's report on the temporary withdrawal of the Secretariat of the Asian-African Journalists Association from Jakarta, the meeting unanimously adopted a resolution supporting the decision made by the Resident Secretaries in December 1965 to temporarily withdraw from Jakarta, and expressed gratitude for the outstanding efforts made by the Resident Secretaries to continue their work in accordance with the guiding principles of the Jakarta Declaration and the Constitution of the Asian-African Journalists Association.
    The meeting unanimously decided that the Second Asian-African Journalists Conference will be held in Algiers within one year.
    The meeting unanimously decided that the working location of the Secretariat of the Asian-African Journalists Association should be in Beijing until the Second Asian-African Journalists Conference is convened.
    The meeting accepted the membership applications of journalists from Congo (Leopoldville), Korean residents in Japan, Laos, Mozambique, Palestine, and the United Arab Republic. Their representatives have accepted the Jakarta Declaration adopted by the First Asian-African Journalists Conference in 1963.
    The meeting was filled with a spirit of friendship and cooperation from beginning to end, which fully demonstrated the militant solidarity of Asian-African journalists.
    On April 24th, more than 1,500 Chinese and Asian-African journalists gathered solemnly to celebrate "Asian-African Journalists Day" and congratulate the great success of the Fourth Plenary Meeting (Enlarged) of the Secretariat of the Asian-African Journalists Association. ### 亚非记协书记处第四次全体会议发表公报  亚非新闻工作者加强战斗团结  坚决支持亚非人民的反帝斗争
刘长忠
1966-04-25
第3版()
专栏：

    亚非记协书记处第四次全体会议发表公报
    亚非新闻工作者加强战斗团结  坚决支持亚非人民的反帝斗争
    新华社二十四日讯　亚非新闻工作者协会书记处第四次全体会议（扩大）公报，全文如下：
    亚非新闻工作者协会书记处于一九六六年四月二十日到二十四日在北京举行了第四次全体会议（扩大）。出席这次会议的有十九个国家和地区的代表团，即：阿尔及利亚、阿扎尼亚（南非）、柬埔寨、锡兰、中国、刚果（利奥波德维尔）、几内亚、印度尼西亚、日本、朝鲜民主主义人民共和国、马里、莫三鼻给、巴勒斯坦、叙利亚、坦桑尼亚、阿拉伯联合共和国、越南民主共和国、越南南方（民族解放阵线）和津巴布韦。巴基斯坦和赞比亚的代表团不能出席，发来了支持会议的电报。
    书记处收到了柬埔寨国家元首西哈努克亲王一封支持这次会议的信件，对书记处的撤离雅加达表示谅解，并且对目前这次会议表示同情；还收到了许多国家的新闻工作者组织和民族解放运动发来的贺电。北京市副市长万里先生出席了开幕式，并在会上致词。他代表东道国讲了话，向代表们表示热烈欢迎，并且向会议表示祝贺。
    会议听取了总书记查禾多先生的报告。随后会议进行了充分和热烈的讨论。
    会议通过了一系列政治决议，表示毫不含糊和坚决地支持越南人民、刚果（利）、巴勒斯坦和其他亚非国家反对以美国为首的帝国主义和新老殖民主义的正义斗争。会议还强烈谴责了帝国主义者和各国反动派对进步新闻工作者的迫害。
    会议在听取了总书记关于亚非新闻工作者协会书记处暂时撤离雅加达的报告后，一致通过了一项决议，支持书记处常驻书记一九六五年十二月作出的关于暂时撤离雅加达的决定，并且对常驻书记们为了按照雅加达宣言和亚非新闻工作者协会章程的指导原则继续他们的工作所作的杰出努力表示感谢。
    会议一致决定，第二次亚非新闻工作者会议于一年内在阿尔及尔召开。
    会议一致决定，亚非新闻工作者协会书记处的工作地点应在北京，直到第二次亚非新闻工作者会议召开时为止。
    会议接受了刚果（利）、旅日朝鲜侨民、老挝、莫三鼻给、巴勒斯坦和阿联的新闻工作者的入会申请。他们的代表已接受了第一次亚非新闻工作者会议于一九六三年通过的雅加达宣言。　
    会议自始至终洋溢着友好合作的精神，这充分显示了亚非新闻工作者的战斗的团结。
    四月二十四日，一千五百多名中国和亚非国家的新闻工作者隆重集会，热烈庆祝“亚非新闻工作者日”，祝贺亚非新闻工作者协会书记处第四次全体会议（扩大）获得巨大成功。图为亚非新闻工作者协会总书记查禾多在会上讲话。　　　　　　　新华社记者　刘长忠摄
