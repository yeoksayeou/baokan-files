Title: Afro-Asian Journalists' Association Secretariat Meeting Holds Plenary Session; Secretary-General Djawoto Delivers General Report, Representatives from Various Countries Begin to Speak

Author: 
Date: 1966-04-22
Edition: 4

    New China News Agency, April 21st -- The Fourth Plenary Meeting (Expanded) of the Secretariat of the Afro-Asian Journalists' Association (AAJA) held a plenary session today. AAJA Secretary-General Djawoto delivered a general report at the morning session. The general report was divided into two parts: the first part was on the current international political situation, and the second part was on the AAJA’s activities over the past year and more, and future work tasks.
    At the afternoon session, Djawoto reported on the AAJA Secretariat's temporary relocation from Jakarta. Subsequently, representatives from various countries began to speak.
    At the meeting, telegrams from the Pakistan Federation of Journalists and the United National Independence Party of Zambia were read. In their telegrams, they stated that although they were unable to send representatives to the meeting due to various reasons, they supported the convening of the meeting and congratulated it on its great success. The telegram from the Pakistan Federation of Journalists stated: "The Pakistan Federation of Journalists believes that this meeting will overcome all obstacles placed in your path by new and old colonialists. May the Bandung spirit guide you in uniting the Afro-Asian world into an unbreakable bond of friendship."
    The meeting also read congratulatory telegrams and statements from Mali, Cambodia, Azania (South Africa), Swaziland, Albania, and Basutoland. The congratulatory telegram from the National Union of Journalists of Mali stated: "The journalists of Mali firmly believe that the Afro-Asian journalists have always stood at the forefront of the struggle of the Third World people to liberate themselves from the clutches of imperialism and new and old colonialism." The congratulatory telegram from the Cambodian Journalists Association stated: "We extend our warm congratulations to you and sincerely hope that this expanded meeting of the Secretariat of the Afro-Asian Journalists' Association and your mission will achieve brilliant success." The statement from the Pan-Africanist Congress of Azania (South Africa)'s Department of Publicity and Information stated: "All progressive journalists of Asia and Africa must continue to uphold the Bandung spirit, cooperate with the people who are struggling in Vietnam, South Africa, and many other places in Asia and Africa, until the imperialists are completely defeated." The statement from the Swaziland Progressive Party stated: "The Swaziland Progressive Party supports the Afro-Asian Journalists' Association's relocation from Jakarta and condemns the current leadership of the Indonesian Journalists Association for creating difficulties for the Afro-Asian Journalists' Association to carry out its activities. The people of Swaziland have been educated by your example, and it will remain vigilant against any form of imperialism as always. We stand with you and continue to adopt a principled approach under the banner of the Bandung spirit." The congratulatory telegram from the Albanian Journalists' Association stated: "We wish our Afro-Asian counterparts victory in the common struggle against imperialism, new and old colonialism, and reactionaries." The congratulatory telegram from the Basutoland Congress Party stated: "We wish you great success in the upcoming meeting of the Secretariat of the Afro-Asian Journalists' Association and once again express our appreciation for the role played by the Afro-Asian Journalists' Association in the human struggle against imperialism, colonialism, and neo-colonialism."
    Beladjoug and Benazouz from Algeria, Sebastien Ramazani, Joseph Kissouby, and Leon Loukongo from Congo (Brazzaville), Bakary Traoré, Lassina Kané, and Modibo Alfred Keita from Mali, Valentim سیسول and Alfredo Chembene from Mozambique, who arrived in Beijing on the evening of the 20th, together with the representatives from various countries who had already arrived in Beijing, attended today's meeting.

### 亚非记协书记处会议举行全体会议  查禾多在会上作了总报告，各国代表开始发言

1966-04-22
第4版()
专栏：

    亚非记协书记处会议举行全体会议
    查禾多在会上作了总报告，各国代表开始发言
    新华社二十一日讯　亚非新闻工作者协会书记处第四次全体会议（扩大），今天举行全体会议。亚非新闻工作者协会总书记查禾多在上午的会议上作了总报告。总报告共分两部分，第一部分是关于目前的国际政治形势，第二部分是关于亚非新闻工作者协会一年多来的活动和今后的工作任务。
    在下午的会议上，查禾多就亚非新闻工作者协会书记处暂时撤离雅加达作了报告。接着，各国代表开始发言。
    会上宣读了巴基斯坦新闻工作者联合会和赞比亚联合民族独立党的来电。他们在电文中表示，虽然他们因故不能派出代表出席这次会议，但他们支持这次会议的召开，并祝贺会议获得巨大成功。巴基斯坦新闻工作者联合会的电文中说：“巴基斯坦新闻工作者联合会相信，这次会议将克服新老殖民主义者摆在你们道路上的一切障碍。愿万隆精神引导你们把亚非世界团结成牢不可破的友谊纽带。”
    会上还宣读了来自马里、柬埔寨、阿扎尼亚（南非）、斯威士兰、阿尔巴尼亚和巴苏陀兰的贺电和声明。马里全国新闻工作者协会的贺电说：“马里的新闻工作者坚信，亚非新闻工作者始终站在第三世界人民为了从帝国主义和新老殖民主义的触角下解放出来而进行的斗争的最前列。”柬埔寨新闻工作者协会的贺电说：“我们向你们表示热烈的祝贺，并衷心地希望亚非新闻工作者协会书记处这次扩大会议和你们的使命获得辉煌的成就。”阿扎尼亚（南非）泛非主义者大会宣传和新闻部的声明说：“亚非所有的进步新闻工作者必须继续支持万隆精神，同越南、南非和亚洲、非洲许多其他地方的进行斗争的人民合作，直到帝国主义者遭到彻底失败为止。”斯威士兰进步党的声明说：“斯威士兰进步党支持亚非新闻工作者协会撤离雅加达，并谴责印度尼西亚新闻工作者协会现领导为亚非新闻工作者协会进行活动制造困难。斯威士兰人民从你们的榜样中受到了教育，它将一如既往地保持警惕，反对任何形式的帝国主义。我们同你们站在一起，在万隆精神的旗帜下继续采取有原则的方针。”阿尔巴尼亚新闻工作者协会的贺电说：“祝亚非同行们在反对帝国主义、新老殖民主义和反动派的共同斗争中取得胜利。”巴苏陀兰大会党的贺电说：“祝你们在即将举行的亚非新闻工作者协会书记处会议中取得巨大的成就，并再一次对亚非新闻工作者协会在人类反对帝国主义、殖民主义和新殖民主义的斗争中所起的作用表示赞赏。”
    二十日晚到达北京的阿尔及利亚的贝拉朱格、贝纳佐兹，刚果（利）的塞巴斯蒂安·拉马扎尼、约瑟夫·基苏比、列昂·卢孔戈，马里的巴卡里·特拉奥雷、拉西纳·卡内、莫迪博·艾尔弗雷德·凯塔，莫三鼻给的瓦朗蒂姆·西索尔、阿尔弗雷多·切姆贝尼，今天同前已到达北京的各国代表一起，出席了今天的大会。
