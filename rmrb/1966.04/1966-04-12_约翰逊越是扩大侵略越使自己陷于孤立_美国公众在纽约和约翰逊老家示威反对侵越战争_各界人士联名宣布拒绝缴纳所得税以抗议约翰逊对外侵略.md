Title: The More Johnson Expands Aggression, the More Isolated He Becomes
Author:
Date: 1966-04-12
Edition: 4

    Johnson's Expansion of Aggression Only Leads to Greater Isolation
    American Public Demonstrates in New York and Johnson's Hometown Against the War of Aggression Against Vietnam
    People From All Walks of Life Jointly Announce Refusal to Pay Income Tax to Protest Johnson's Foreign Aggression
    (Xinhua News Agency, Washington, 11th) A group of American people held a three-hour demonstration near President Johnson's ranch in Texas on the 10th to protest the war of aggression against Vietnam.
    The demonstrators handed a protest letter to Johnson's security personnel.
    The protest letter pointed out: The recent demonstrations in South Vietnam provide irrefutable evidence that the lives of American youth and the nation's resources "have been used to support a military dictatorship that violates the will of the people."
    (Xinhua News Agency, New York, 11th) More than one hundred American citizens held a demonstration in New York on the 10th to oppose the American aggression against Vietnam.
    The demonstrators carried flags and wore black armbands with the word "Vietnam" during an "Easter" march. They distributed leaflets along the way condemning the American aggression against Vietnam. These leaflets pointed out that the American war in Vietnam is "immoral and illegal."
    (Xinhua News Agency, New York, 10th) Hundreds of people from all walks of life in the United States recently jointly issued a statement announcing their refusal to pay all or part of their 1965 federal income tax to protest the Johnson administration's "illegal use" of force in Vietnam and the Dominican Republic.
    In their statement, they pointed out that the U.S. government's military actions in Vietnam and its intervention in the Dominican Republic "are clearly in violation of the U.S. Constitution, international law, and the UN Charter"; and that the U.S. military actions in Vietnam can be compared to "Italy's unforgivable atrocities in Ethiopia" in the past.
    According to reports, U.S. federal tax law stipulates that anyone who intentionally refuses to pay federal income tax will be sentenced to a maximum of one year in prison and fined ten thousand U.S. dollars.
    The statement was signed by biochemist Szent-Gyorgyi, folk singer Baez, poet Ferlinghetti, and Catholic Worker editor Day, among others. (Attached picture)

### 约翰逊越是扩大侵略越使自己陷于孤立  美国公众在纽约和约翰逊老家示威反对侵越战争  各界人士联名宣布拒绝缴纳所得税以抗议约翰逊对外侵略

1966-04-12
第4版()
专栏：

    约翰逊越是扩大侵略越使自己陷于孤立
    美国公众在纽约和约翰逊老家示威反对侵越战争
    各界人士联名宣布拒绝缴纳所得税以抗议约翰逊对外侵略
    据新华社十一日讯　华盛顿消息：一批美国群众十日在美国总统约翰逊的老家得克萨斯州牧场附近举行三小时的示威，抗议侵越战争。
    示威者向约翰逊的保卫人员递交一封抗议信。
    抗议信指出：最近南越爆发的示威游行提供了无可争辩证据：美国青年人的生命和国家的资源“已经被用来支撑一个违反人民大众意愿的军事独裁政权”。
    据新华社十一日讯　纽约消息：一百多名美国公民十日在纽约举行示威游行，反对美国侵略越南。
    示威者在举行“复活节”游行时打着旗帜，并且带着写有“越南”字样的黑色臂章。他们沿途散发了谴责美国侵略越南的传单。这些传单指出：美国在越南的战争是“不道德的，非法的”。
    新华社十日讯　纽约消息：美国各界人士数百人最近联名发表声明，宣布拒绝缴纳一九六五年联邦所得税的全部或一部分，以抗议约翰逊政府在越南和多米尼加等地“非法使用”武力。
    他们在声明中指出：美国政府在越南的军事行动以及它对多米尼加的干涉，“显然都是违反美国宪法、国际法和联合国宪章的”；而美国在越南的军事行动则可以和当年“意大利在埃塞俄比亚的不可饶恕的暴行”相比拟。
    据报道，美国的联邦税法规定，凡是有意拒缴联邦所得税的人，最高要判刑一年和罚款一万美元。
    在声明上签名的有生物化学家申特—乔尔基、民歌手巴兹、诗人弗林盖蒂以及《天主教工人报》主编戴伊等。（附图片）
