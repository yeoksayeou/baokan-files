Title: Bush Administration to Improve Relations with Africa
Author:
Date: 1989-01-17
Edition: 6

    The Bush administration will improve relations with Africa.

    According to a report by the Nigerian News Agency, U.S. Senator Paul Simon said on the 15th in Lagos that during the presidency of President-elect Bush, relations between the United States and Africa will improve.

    Simon made these remarks at the end of his two-day visit to Nigeria. He said that George Bush understands African issues. There is "great hope" for improving U.S.-African relations during his presidency.

    Regarding racist South Africa, he said he supports imposing compulsory sanctions on the South African regime. He hopes that the United States will end its support for the National Union for the Total Independence of Angola.

    He said that improving relations with African countries will accelerate Namibia's independence from racist South African rule.

### 布什政府将改善同非洲关系

1989-01-17
第6版(国际)
专栏：

　　布什政府将改善同非洲关系
    新华社拉各斯１月１５日电  据尼日利亚通讯社报道，美国参议院非洲事务小组委员会保罗·西蒙１５日在这里说，在当选总统布什执政期间，美国同非洲之间的关系将得到改善。
    西蒙是在结束对尼日利亚为期两天的访问时讲这番话的。他说，乔治·布什理解非洲问题。他执政期间，改善美国同非洲的关系有“很大希望”。
    他在谈到关于种族主义南非时说，他支持对南非政权实行强制性制裁。他希望美国将结束对争取安哥拉彻底独立全国联盟的支持。
    他说，改善同非洲国家的关系将加快纳米比亚从种族主义南非统治下获得独立。
