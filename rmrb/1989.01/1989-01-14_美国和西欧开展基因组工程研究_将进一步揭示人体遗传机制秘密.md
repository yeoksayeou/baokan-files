Title: The United States and Western Europe Launch Genomics Research
Author: Hu Ansheng
Date: 1989-01-14
Edition: 7

A major project to map and decode every gene in the human body is underway in the medical and biological fields of the United States and Western Europe. This genomics project will further reveal the secrets of human genetics, aid in the diagnosis and treatment of genetic diseases, and help in researching the causes of various intractable diseases such as cancer, as well as developing new drugs and therapies.

The cost of this genomics project is estimated to be $100 million. The U.S. federal government has allocated $29 million for this project in its budget from October 1, 1988, to September 30, 1989. On January 9, it proposed a budget for the period from October 1, 1989, to September 30, 1990. Western European countries have also proposed a budget of $20 million for the next two years for genomics research.

Biologists estimate that the human body is composed of approximately 30 trillion cells. Each cell contains 23 pairs of chromosomes. Each chromosome contains a strand of deoxyribonucleic acid (DNA) encoding various genes. If all the DNA in a cell is connected, it would be about 0.9 meters long. In the entire set of human chromosomes, there are approximately 50,000 to 100,000 genes encoding signals that control human growth and determine characteristics such as hair color and physical appearance. These genes make up the human genome.

Genomics involves mapping the location of each gene on the chromosomes and then determining the sequence of each gene's code. Biologists use four-letter codes to represent the genetic information of each gene. Thus, the entire genetic code consists of 3.5 billion characters. Scientists may discover various regular or irregular gene locations and codes, thereby identifying genetic genes and developing genetic engineering products and drugs.

### 美国和西欧开展基因组工程研究  将进一步揭示人体遗传机制秘密
胡安生
1989-01-14
第7版(国际)
专栏：

　　美国和西欧开展基因组工程研究
    将进一步揭示人体遗传机制秘密
    新华社华盛顿一月十一日电  （记者胡安生）一项重大的绘制和译解人体每一个基因的基因组工程，正在美国和西欧的医学、生物学领域中展开。它将进一步揭示人体的秘密——遗传机制，有助于诊断和治疗有关遗传性疾病，以及研究各种顽症如癌症等致病的原因，研制新的药物和疗法等。
    这项基因组工程的费用将达一亿美元。美国联邦政府在去年十月一日开始至今年九月三十日止的预算中，已拨款二千九百万美元用于这项工程。一月九日又提出今年十月一日开始至明年九月三十日止的预算。西欧国家对基因组工程的研究，也提出了明后两年总数为二千万美元的预算。
    生物学家估计，人体大约由三十万亿个细胞组成。每个细胞含有二十三对染色体。每个染色体含有一串脱氧核糖核酸编码的各种基因。如果把一个细胞中的全部脱氧核糖核酸连接起来约有零点九米。在人体的全部染色体中大约有五万至十万个基因编码信号，从而控制人的生长，决定包括人的头发颜色、外表特征等。这些基因就是人的基因组。
    基因组工程就是绘制染色体的每一个基因位置，然后确定每个基因的代码排列顺序。生物学家用四个字母代码来表示每个基因的遗传信息。这样，整个遗传代码有三十五亿个字符。科学家有可能发现各种有规则或不规则的基因位置和代码，从而找出遗传性的基因，研制遗传工程制品和药物。
