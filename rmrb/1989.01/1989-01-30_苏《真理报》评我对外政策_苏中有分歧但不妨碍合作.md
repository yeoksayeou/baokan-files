Title: Pravda Evaluates China's Foreign Policy
Author:
Date: 1989-01-30
Edition: 6

The Soviet newspaper Pravda's political observer Ovchinnikov believes that China's foreign policy has undergone two major changes in the past decade: pragmatism and openness. The differences between the Soviet Union and China on certain international issues do not hinder the development of cooperation.

He wrote in today's Pravda an article titled "In Accordance with the Spirit of the Times—The Evolution of China's Foreign Policy." He said that China is increasingly integrating into the world and exerting a more significant positive influence on international developments and the resolution of global issues. The article notes that the reform goals of the Soviet Union and China are similar, and both sides hope to exchange specific experiences in renewing socialism. Of course, it is unrealistic to expect complete agreement on all issues, but this does not hinder the development of cooperation. Moreover, there is still great potential for cooperation in solving global problems.

### 苏《真理报》评我对外政策  苏中有分歧但不妨碍合作

1989-01-30
第6版(国际)
专栏：

　　苏《真理报》评我对外政策    
    苏中有分歧但不妨碍合作
    新华社莫斯科１月２８日电《真理报》政治观察家奥夫钦尼科夫认为，中国的对外政策近１０年来发生了务实和开放的两大变化，苏中在某些国际问题上的分歧并不妨碍发展合作。
    他今天在《真理报》发表题为《符合时代精神——中国对外政策的演变》的文章。他说，中国进一步走向世界，对国际事态的发展、对全球问题的解决施加越来越大的积极影响。文章认为，中苏两国改革目标相近，彼此都希望交流在革新社会主义方面的具体经验。当然，指望在所有问题上观点完全吻合是不切实的，但这并不妨碍合作的发展，而且在解决全球问题方面的合作尚有很大潜力。
