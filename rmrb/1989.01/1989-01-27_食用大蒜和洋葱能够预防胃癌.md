Title: Consuming Garlic and Onions Can Prevent Stomach Cancer
Author:
Date: 1989-01-27
Edition: 7

    A joint study by the U.S. National Cancer Institute and Chinese researchers in Linqu County, Shandong Province, has shown that garlic and onions, which have strong flavors, can help prevent stomach cancer.

    The results of the study were published in a journal of the U.S. National Cancer Institute. During the investigation, researchers compared the diets of 685 stomach cancer patients and 1,131 healthy individuals in Linqu County, one of the regions in China with the highest stomach cancer mortality rates. The study found that in the control group of individuals without stomach cancer, each person consumed at least 11 to 22.5 kilograms of Allium vegetables per year, including onions, green onions, chives, and scallions. In contrast, stomach cancer patients generally consumed less than 11 kilograms of these vegetables annually.

### 食用大蒜和洋葱能够预防胃癌

1989-01-27
第7版(国际)
专栏：

　　食用大蒜和洋葱能够预防胃癌
    由美国国家癌症研究所和中国的研究人员合作，在我国山东临朐县进行的调查表明，大蒜和洋葱等味道浓烈的蔬菜能够防止胃癌生长。
    这项调查结果发表在美国国家癌症研究所的刊物上。在调查中，研究人员对临朐县６８５名胃癌患者和１１３１名健康人的饮食进行了比较。临朐县是中国胃癌死亡率最高的地区之一。调查发现，在没有患胃癌的对照组中，每人每年至少消耗１１公斤—２２．５公斤葱属蔬菜，包括洋葱、韭葱、细香葱和大葱等。而胃癌病人普遍食用这些蔬菜少于１１公斤。
