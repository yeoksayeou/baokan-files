Title: Japan to Build World's Largest Particle Accelerator
Author:
Date: 1989-01-07
Edition: 7

	Japan to Build World's Largest Particle Accelerator
	Contributions to Multiple Disciplines Expected

	BEIJING, Jan. 5 (Xinhua) — According to Japanese newspapers, the Institute for Nuclear Study at the University of Tokyo plans to construct the world's largest particle accelerator.

	The project is scheduled to begin in 1991 and is expected to be completed by 1995.

	The particle beam intensity will be the highest in the world. The accelerator will be 400 meters long and will be equipped with three circular accelerators, each with a diameter of 54 meters.

	The particle accelerator will be built in Tsukuba Science City. It will be capable of conducting experiments on high-temperature superconductors, biological macromolecules, and the analysis of microscopic structures, as well as simulating the synthesis of elements that occurred shortly after the universe's birth. This will significantly contribute to research in atomic physics, particle physics, nuclear fusion, material physics, chemistry, and life sciences, among other fields.

### 日将建设世界最大粒子加速器  将对多学科的研究作出贡献

1989-01-07
第7版(国际)
专栏：

　　日将建设世界最大粒子加速器
    将对多学科的研究作出贡献
    新华社东京１月５日电  据日本报纸报道，东京大学原子核研究所计划建设世界最大规模的粒子加速器。
    这项计划将于１９９１年实施，１９９５年完成。
    它的粒子束强度将是世界最大的，加速器全长４００米，并配有３个直径为５４米的环形加速器。
    这座粒子加速器将建设在筑波科学城内，它能进行高温超导体、生物高分子等微观结构分析和再现宇宙诞生后元素合成等试验，对原子物理、粒子、核聚变、物性物理、化学、生命科学等许多学科的研究将作出贡献。
