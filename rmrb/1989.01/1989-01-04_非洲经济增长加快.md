Date: 1989-01-04
Edition: 7

    Africa's Economic Growth Accelerates

    Xinhua News Agency, Addis Ababa, January 2nd - In 1988, the economies of African countries grew by 2.5 percent, compared to a growth rate of only 1.3 percent in 1987.

    This was announced by Adedeji Adebayo, the Secretary of the United Nations Economic Commission for Africa, at a press conference held today in Addis Ababa.

    Adebayo said that food production is given top priority in Africa, and in 1988 there was a significant increase, with a growth rate of 3.8 percent. Moreover, the growth rate of food production exceeded the population growth rate in Africa that year (3.1 percent).

    It is estimated that in 1988, Africa's grain harvest reached 71.8 million tons.

### 非洲经济增长加快

1989-01-04
第7版(国际)
专栏：

　　非洲经济增长加快
    新华社亚的斯亚贝巴一月二日电  一九八八年，非洲国家的经济增长了百分之二点五，而在一九八七年只增长了百分之一点三。
    这是联合国非洲经济委员会秘书阿德巴约·阿德德吉今天在这里举行的记者招待会上宣布的。
    阿德德吉说，粮食生产在非洲占有最优先的地位，在一九八八年有了明显的增长，增长率为百分之三点八，而且粮食增长率超过了当年非洲人口的增长率（百分之三点一）。
    据推算，一九八八年，非洲的谷物收成达到七千一百八十万吨。
