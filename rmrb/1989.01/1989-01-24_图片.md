Title: Bamako, the Capital of Mali
Author: Fang Yugui
Date: 1989-01-24
Edition: 7

Bamako, the capital of Mali, is one of the major cities in West Africa. The Niger River flows through the entire city, bringing it vibrant life.

In the Bambara language, Bamako means "the river of crocodiles." At the end of the 19th century, it was just an ordinary small village. After the construction of the railway in 1904, it rapidly developed. Today, Bamako has become a modern city with a population of 600,000. The picture shows the bustling market of Bamako.

	New China News Agency reporter Fang Yugui
