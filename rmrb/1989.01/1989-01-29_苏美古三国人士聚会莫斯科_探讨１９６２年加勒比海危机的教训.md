Title: Officials and Scholars from the Soviet Union, the United States, and Cuba Gather in Moscow to Discuss the Lessons of the 1962 Caribbean Crisis
Author: Sheng Shiliang
Date: 1989-01-29
Edition: 6

Soviet, American, and Cuban officials and scholars gathered in Moscow today to begin a three-day roundtable discussion on the lessons to be learned from the 1962 Caribbean Crisis, which brought the world to the brink of nuclear disaster.

From the footage broadcast on Soviet television, it was evident that the former Soviet Ambassador to the United States and former Chairman of the Presidium of the Supreme Soviet, Andrei Gromyko, who retired last September 30, was seated upright and alert. The U.S. Ambassador to the Soviet Union, Jack Matlock, smiled as he read a congratulatory message from President Bush. The message stated, "A clearer and more specific understanding of the 'Caribbean Missile Crisis' will help to strengthen world security."

The head of the International Department of the CPSU Central Committee, Valentin Falin, read a congratulatory message from Mikhail Gorbachev. The message emphasized that studying the causes of this crisis and the ways to resolve it remains relevant today. The interests of all humanity should be and can be placed first.

### 苏美古三国人士聚会莫斯科  探讨１９６２年加勒比海危机的教训
盛世良
1989-01-29
第6版(国际)
专栏：

　　苏美古三国人士聚会莫斯科    
    探讨１９６２年加勒比海危机的教训
    新华社莫斯科１月２７日电（记者盛世良）１９６２年加勒比海危机当事国——苏联、美国和古巴的知名政治家、学者和政治观察家，今天在莫斯科开始为期３天的圆桌讨论会，探讨应该如何从这场使人类面临核灾难的危机中吸取教训。
    从苏联电视播出的镜头看，当时苏联驻美大使、去年９月３０日辞职退休的前苏联最高苏维埃主席团主席葛罗米柯，精神矍铄地正襟危坐，美国驻苏大使马特洛克笑眯眯地宣读了布什总统给讨论会的贺信。信中说，“更清楚而具体地理解‘加勒比海导弹危机’的实质，有助于巩固世界的安全”。
    苏共中央国际部长法林宣读的戈尔巴乔夫的贺辞谈到，研究这一危机的形成原因和调解途径，今天仍不失其现实意义，全人类利益可以而且应当置于首位。
