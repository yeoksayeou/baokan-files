Title: West Germany, Switzerland, and Other Western European Countries Raise Interest Rates

Author:

Date: 1989-01-21

Edition: 7

West Germany, Switzerland, France, and Austria, four Western European countries, raised their interest rates on January 19 to ease inflationary pressures.

The West German Federal Bank announced on the afternoon of January 19 that starting from January 20, the discount rate would be raised from 3.5% to 4%, and the mortgage loan rate from 5.5% to 6%. Subsequently, the Swiss Central Bank also announced that it would raise the discount rate from 3.5% to 4%, and the mortgage loan interest rate from 5.5% to 6%. The Austrian Central Bank announced that its discount rate would be raised from 4% to 4.5%, and the mortgage loan interest rate from 5.5% to 6%. The French Central Bank then announced that it would raise the money market intervention rate from 7.75% to 8.25%, and the repurchase rate for commercial bills of 5 to 10 days from 8.5% to 9%.

### 西德瑞士等西欧四国提高利率

1989-01-21
第7版(国际)
专栏：

　　西德瑞士等西欧四国提高利率
    新华社北京一月二十日电  综合本社记者报道：西德、瑞士、法国和奥地利四国中央银行十九日相继提高利率，以缓解通货膨胀的压力。
    西德联邦银行十九日下午宣布，从二十日起将贴现率由百分之三点五提到百分之四，将抵押贷款率由百分之五点五提到百分之六。之后，瑞士中央银行也宣布将贴现率从百分之三点五提到百分之四，将抵押贷款利率由百分之五点五提到百分之六。奥地利中央银行宣布，其贴现率也将由百分之四升到百分之四点五，抵押贷款利率由百分之五点五升到百分之六。法国中央银行随后宣布将货币市场干预利率从百分之七点七五提到百分之八点二五，将五至十天的商业票据回购利率由百分之八点五提高到百分之九。
