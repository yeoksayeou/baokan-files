Title: Former Director of the Ministry of Communications Salvage Bureau, Comrade Wu Yingcheng, Passes Away
Author:
Date: 1989-01-20
Edition: 4

The funeral service for Comrade Wu Yingcheng, former Director and Advisor of the Ministry of Communications Salvage Bureau, was held today at the Revolutionary Martyrs' Cemetery in Beijing. Wu Yingcheng passed away in Beijing on November 24, 1988, at the age of 72.

Wu Yingcheng was born in Guanwangmiao Township, Banzhuyuan District, Jinzhai County, Anhui Province. In August 1930, during a low point in the Great Revolution, he joined the revolutionary cause. He served in various positions, including soldier, platoon leader, company commander, political instructor, political department head, deputy political commissar, and political commissar of the Shangcheng Independent Regiment. He joined the Communist Youth League in 1932 and became a member of the Communist Party of China in May 1933. After the New Fourth Army Incident, he was responsible for the security of Comrade Liu Shaoqi in the East China region and participated in various campaigns against encirclement and suppression. He took part in the Liaoshen, Pingjin, Yangtze River Crossing, and campaigns to liberate South China. After the liberation of the country, he served in various positions, including Political Commissar of the Logistics Department of the South Sea Fleet, Political Commissar of the Logistics Department of the North Sea Fleet, and Political Commissar of the Naval Armament Equipment Department, making significant contributions to the ideological and political work, logistics support, and modernization of equipment for the Navy. In 1964, Comrade Wu Yingcheng was transferred to the First Highway Engineering Bureau of the Ministry of Communications to serve as the Party Committee Secretary. In 1977, he was appointed Director of the Ministry of Communications Maritime Salvage Bureau, making significant contributions to the salvage industry.

Throughout his more than half-century of revolutionary work, Comrade Wu Yingcheng remained loyal to the Party, the people, and the cause of communism, dedicating his life to the revolution and the cause of transportation for the Chinese people.

Comrade Yu Qiuyi attended the funeral service. Zhang Jiefu, Ye Fei, Chen Xitong, Deng Zhaoxiang, Qian Yongchang, and others sent wreaths. Representatives from the Ministry of Communications, Beijing Municipality, and over 200 friends of Comrade Wu Yingcheng attended the funeral service.

### 原交通部救助打捞局局长  吴英诚同志逝世

1989-01-20
第4版(政治·法律·社会)
专栏：

　　原交通部救助打捞局局长
    吴英诚同志逝世
    新华社北京１月１９日电  中国共产党党员，原交通部救助打捞局局长、顾问吴英诚同志遗体告别仪式今日在北京八宝山革命公墓礼堂举行。吴英诚１９８８年１１月２４日在北京病逝，终年７２岁。
    吴英诚同志生于安徽省金寨县斑竹园区关王庙乡。１９３０年８月在大革命处于低潮时期投身革命，历任商城独立团红军战士、班长、连长、教导员、政治处主任、团副政委、政委等职。他１９３２年加入共产主义青年团，１９３３年５月转为中国共产党党员。皖南事变后，曾担任过刘少奇同志在华东地区的安全警卫工作，参加过各次反围剿斗争。他先后参加过辽沈、平津、渡江战役和解放华中华南的战役。全国解放后，他历任海军南海舰队后勤部政委、北海舰队后勤部政委、海军军械装备部政委，为海军的思想政治工作和后勤保障工作以及装备现代化做出了很大的贡献。１９６４年，吴英诚同志转业到交通部第一公路工程局任党委书记。１９７７年他调任交通部海难救助打捞局任局长，为救捞事业作出了很大贡献。
    吴英诚同志参加革命工作半个多世纪以来，忠于党、忠于人民、忠于共产主义事业，为中国人民的革命和交通事业奋斗了一生。
    余秋里同志参加了遗体告别仪式。张劲夫、叶飞、陈希同、邓兆祥、钱永昌等送了花圈。交通部、北京市等单位以及吴英诚的生前友好２００多人参加了遗体告别。
