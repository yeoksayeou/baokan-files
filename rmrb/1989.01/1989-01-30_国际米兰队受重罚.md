Title: Inter Milan Faces Heavy Penalty
Author:
Date: 1989-01-30
Edition: 3

The Union of European Football Associations recently penalized several teams. The Italian team Inter Milan was fined the largest amount in its history.

A message from Bern, Switzerland, stated that Inter Milan would be fined 225,000 Swiss francs, approximately $143,000.

In December of last year, Inter Milan faced Bayern Munich in a European Cup match. Fans of Inter Milan set a fire on the stands. For this incident, UEFA fined Inter Milan 150,000 Swiss francs. Additionally, 50,000 Swiss francs were fined for Inter Milan's illegal insertion of commercial advertisements on the large screen in the stadium. The remaining 25,000 Swiss francs were fined for Inter Milan's unsportsmanlike conduct during the match.

In 1985, Inter Milan had previously set a record with a fine of 200,000 Swiss francs.

### 国际米兰队受重罚

1989-01-30
第3版(科教·文化·体育)
专栏：体育

　　国际米兰队受重罚
    欧洲足球联合会最近处罚了一批球队。意大利的国际米兰队被处以有史以来最大数目的罚款。
    来自瑞士伯尔尼的消息说，国际米兰队将被课以２２５０００瑞士法郎的罚款，约折合１４３０００美元。
    去年１２月，国际米兰队与拜尔恩·慕尼黑队在欧洲杯足球赛中相遇。国际米兰队的球迷在看台上放了一把火。为此一项，欧洲足联判罚国际米兰队１５００００瑞士法郎。另有５００００瑞士法郎，罚的是国际米兰队在比赛中，非法在赛场的巨型荧光屏中，插入商业广告。最后的２５０００瑞士法郎则是对国际米兰队在比赛中表现出的“非体育精神”行为的罚金。
    国际米兰队１９８５年就曾以２０００００瑞士法郎的金额，创下受罚数额的最高纪录。　　　　　　　
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　（新华社）
