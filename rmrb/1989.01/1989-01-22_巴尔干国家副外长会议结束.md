Title: The Second Meeting of Deputy Foreign Ministers of Balkan Countries Concludes
Author:
Date: 1989-01-22
Edition: 6

The second meeting of high-level representatives from the foreign ministries of Balkan countries concluded at noon on the 20th in Tirana. The meeting, which began on the 18th, was attended by deputy foreign ministers from Albania, Bulgaria, Greece, Turkey, Romania, and Yugoslavia.

The representatives from the six countries positively evaluated the progress of understanding and cooperation since the first meeting of Balkan foreign ministers in Belgrade in February of last year. They believed that "there exists the possibility of expanding cooperation in various fields on the basis of common interests." The representatives emphasized that the Balkan countries will promote and strengthen Balkan cooperation on the basis of complete equality and mutual respect, which will be beneficial for understanding, good neighborliness, and trust in the region, and for the peace and security of the Balkans and Europe.

### 巴尔干国家副外长会议结束

1989-01-22
第6版(国际)
专栏：国际新闻简报

　　巴尔干国家副外长会议结束
    十八日开始的第二次巴尔干国家外交部高级代表会议二十日中午在地拉那结束。
    与会的阿尔巴尼亚、保加利亚、希腊、土耳其、罗马尼亚和南斯拉夫六国副外长对去年二月贝尔格莱德首次巴尔干外长会议的谅解与合作进程予以积极评价，并认为“存在着在共同利益的基础上扩大各领域合作的可能”。六国代表强调，巴尔干各国将在完全平等、相互尊重的基础上推进和加强巴尔干合作的积极进程，以利于本地区的谅解、睦邻和信任，以利于巴尔干以及欧洲的和平与安全。
