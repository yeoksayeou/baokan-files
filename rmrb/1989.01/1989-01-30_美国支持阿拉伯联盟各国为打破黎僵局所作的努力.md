Title: The United States Supports the Efforts of Arab League Countries to Break the Lebanese Stalemate
Author:
Date: 1989-01-30
Edition: 6

The United States supports the efforts of Arab League countries to break the Lebanese stalemate, said U.S. State Department spokesman Richard Boucher today. The goal is to establish a unified central government in Lebanon.

Since former President Amin Gemayel stepped down in September last year, Lebanon has been without a president but has two prime ministers, one from the Christian faction and the other from the Muslim faction.

A committee composed of foreign ministers from Arab League countries will meet in Tunis on January 29 to seek a solution to the Lebanese crisis.

According to Agence France-Presse, Lebanon's two current prime ministers and former Speaker Hussein may attend the meeting.

Boucher said the U.S. government welcomes the efforts of the Arab League countries, believing it to be an "important opportunity to rebuild the Lebanese central government."

### 美国支持阿拉伯联盟各国为打破黎僵局所作的努力

1989-01-30
第6版(国际)
专栏：

　　美国支持阿拉伯联盟各国为打破黎僵局所作的努力
    新华社华盛顿１月２７日电  美国国务院发言人雷德曼今天表示，美国支持阿拉伯联盟各国为打破黎巴嫩僵局所做的努力，以便在黎巴嫩成立一个单一的中央政府。
    自从前总统阿明·杰马耶勒去年９月离任以来，黎巴嫩一直没有总统，但却有两位分属基督教派和穆斯林派的总理。
    由阿拉伯联盟各国外长组成的一个委员会本月２９日将在突尼斯会晤，以求解决黎巴嫩的这一危机。
    据法新社报道，黎巴嫩的两位现任总理以及前议长侯赛因可能参加这次会议。
    雷德曼说，美国政府对阿联各国的努力表示欢迎，认为这是“重建黎巴嫩中央政府的重要机会”。
