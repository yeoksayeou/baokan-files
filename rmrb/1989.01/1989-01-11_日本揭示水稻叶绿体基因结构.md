Title: Japan Reveals the Gene Structure of Rice Chloroplasts
Author:
Date: 1989-01-11
Edition: 7

Japanese researchers have successfully deciphered the gene structure of rice chloroplasts after two years of effort.

Researchers Hirazuka Junzo from the Mitsui Plant Technology Research Institute and Professor Sugihara Masahiro from Nagoya University isolated the genetic material from the leaves of a high-quality Japanese rice variety called "Nihon-shari." They used enzymes to break down the genes and then employed molecular biology techniques and microcomputers to decode their structure. The results showed that the gene structure of rice chloroplasts is a circular double-stranded deoxyribonucleic acid (DNA) composed of 134,525 base pairs, containing approximately 80 genetic codes for protein synthesis.

This research achievement is the first of its kind in the world and will play a significant role in understanding the photosynthetic mechanism of plants and in breeding new varieties of rice.

Prior to this, Japanese scientists had already decoded the entire chloroplast genes of tobacco leaves.

### 日本揭示水稻叶绿体基因结构

1989-01-11
第7版(国际)
专栏：

　　日本揭示水稻叶绿体基因结构
    新华社东京１月６日电  三井植物技术研究所研究员平塚纯造和名古屋大学教授杉浦昌弘，用了两年的时间成功地揭示出水稻叶绿体的基因结构。
    这个研究小组从日本的优质稻种“日本晴”的叶子中分离出水稻叶绿体的遗传基因，用酶将基因切碎后再用分子生物学技术和微型电脑破译了它的结构。结果发现，水稻叶绿体的基因结构是环状双股脱氧核糖核酸，由１３４５２５个碱基连接而成，其中包括大约８０个制造蛋白质的遗传信息。
    这一科研成果在世界上属首创，它对于揭示植物的光合机制及培育水稻新品种将发挥很大作用。
    在此之前，日本科学家已经破译了烟叶的全部叶绿体的遗传基因。
