International News Brief

	United Nations Envoy to Visit Iran and Iraq for Talks
	A spokesman for the United Nations announced on the 9th that UN Secretary-General Javier Pérez de Cuéllar will dispatch special envoy Jan Eliasson to Iran and Iraq in the last week of this month to continue discussions on resolving the Iran-Iraq conflict.
	The spokesman said the talks will focus on the implementation of UN Security Council Resolution 598 on resolving the Iran-Iraq conflict.
	This decision was made after Pérez de Cuéllar met with Iraqi Vice President and Foreign Minister Tariq Aziz and Iranian Foreign Minister Ali Akbar Velayati in Paris on the 7th.

	New President of Rwanda Takes Oath of Office
	Rwanda's new president, Juvénal Habyarimana, took the oath of office in Kigali on the 8th.
	Habyarimana, 51, was re-elected with 99.98% of the vote in December 1988, beginning his third five-year term.

	Colombian Government and Guerrillas Hold First Direct Dialogue
	The Colombian government and the country's most active guerrilla group, the "April 19 Movement," held their first direct dialogue.
	This direct dialogue took place on January 8 in a camp in the Ortega region of Tolima province. The main government representative was Virgilio Barco's advisor, Pablo Rueda; the guerrilla representative was Carlos Pizarro, the highest leader of the "April 19 Movement."
	According to reports, both sides evaluated in detail the peace initiative proposed by the Barco government on September 1, 1988, and indicated that this dialogue would help prevent further bloodshed in the country.

	El Salvador Proposes New Dates for Summit
	The Salvadoran government proposed on the 9th that the Central American summit be held on January 29 and 30 or February 20.
	El Salvador, Nicaragua, Guatemala, Honduras, and Costa Rica had originally planned to hold the Central American summit on January 15 and 16 to promote regional peace. However, at the request of Costa Rican President Oscar Arias, the summit was postponed. The main reason given was that the new U.S. administration had not yet finalized its policy towards Central America.

	Nicaragua Accuses Honduras of New Attack
	The Nicaraguan Ministry of Defense condemned on the 9th the Honduran military's attack on a border outpost called "La Ceiba" in the Somotillo area of northern Nicaragua's Jinotega province.
	The Nicaraguan military stated that the attack occurred on January 7, with the Honduran military using rifles, grenades, and grenade launchers against Nicaraguan forces.
	Since August of last year, the border area between Nicaragua and Honduras has been in a state of military tension.

### 国际要闻简报

1989-01-11
第6版(国际)
专栏：国际要闻简报

    国际要闻简报
　　联合国特使将赴两伊会谈
    联合国的一位发言人９日宣布，联合国秘书长佩雷斯·德奎利亚尔将于本月最后一周内派遣特使让·埃里阿松前往伊朗和伊拉克继续就解决两伊争端问题进行会谈。
    这位发言人说，会谈将集中讨论安理会关于解决两伊冲突的５９８号决议的执行情况。
    这一决定是在佩雷斯·德奎利亚尔同伊拉克副总理兼外长阿齐兹和伊朗外长韦拉亚提于７日在巴黎会晤后作出的。
　　卢旺达新任总统宣誓就职
    卢旺达新任总统哈比亚利马纳８日在基加利正式宣誓就职。
    哈比亚利马纳现年５１岁，去年１２月１９日以９９．９８％的选票再次蝉联总统，开始了他为期５年的第三个任期。
　　哥政府与游击队首次对话
    哥伦比亚政府与国内最活跃的一支游击队组织——“四·一九”运动举行第一次直接对话。
    这次直接对话是１月８日在托利马省奥尔特加山区一个营地中举行的。参加对话的政府方面主要代表是比尔希略·巴尔科总统的顾问帕尔多·鲁埃达；游击队方面的代表是“四·一九”运动最高领导人卡洛斯·皮萨罗。
    据报道，双方在对话中详细评价了巴尔科政府去年９月１日提出的和平倡议，并指出这次对话将有助于在今后制止国内发生的流血冲突。
　　萨提出首脑会议的新日期
    萨尔瓦多政府９日建议１月２９日和３０日或２月２０日举行中美洲首脑会议。
    萨尔瓦多、尼加拉瓜、危地马拉、洪都拉斯和哥斯达黎加原先计划在１月１５日和１月１６日举行中美洲首脑会议，以推动地区和平进程。但由于哥斯达黎加总统奥斯卡·阿里亚斯的请求，这次会议推迟召开，其主要理由是美国新政府还未最终制定好其对中美洲的政策。
　　尼指责洪军队发动新进攻
    尼加拉瓜国防部９日谴责洪都拉斯军队进攻位于尼加拉瓜北部奇南德加省索莫蒂略的一个名叫“拉塞瓦”的边境哨所。
    尼军方指出，这次进攻发生在１月７日，洪军队使用步枪、手榴弹和枪榴弹发射器袭击尼军。
    从去年８月以来，尼、洪接壤的边境地区军事形势一直处于紧张状态之中。
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　（据新华社）
