Title: Encourage Investment to Promote Private Economic Development
Author:
Date: 1989-01-27
Edition: 7

The World Bank is willing to continue assisting African countries, especially those in East and Southern Africa, in economic reform and structural adjustment.

This was announced by Calisto Madavo, the director of the World Bank's regional office, at a press conference held here today.

He said that East and Southern African countries are facing some difficult problems, and the World Bank regards these countries as priority recipients of aid, helping them achieve sustained growth through economic adjustment.

The current focus of the World Bank's assistance is on encouraging investment, promoting the development of the private economy, helping to train personnel, and finding solutions to food and debt problems.

When discussing the economic adjustment currently underway in Africa, he said, "The economic adjustment work in countries like Ghana, Malawi, Mauritius, Senegal, and Cameroon is proceeding smoothly... These countries are now showing signs of economic improvement."

### 鼓励投资促进私有经济发展  世界银行愿帮助非洲国家调整经济

1989-01-27
第7版(国际)
专栏：

　　鼓励投资促进私有经济发展     
    世界银行愿帮助非洲国家调整经济
    新华社内罗毕１月２５日电  世界银行愿意继续援助非洲国家，特别是东部和南部非洲国家进行经济改革和结构调整。
    这是世界银行地区办事处主任卡利斯托·马达沃今天在这里举行的记者招待会上宣布的。
    他说，东部和南部非洲国家正面临着一些难以解决的问题，世界银行将这些国家视作优先援助的对象，帮助它们通过经济调整，取得持续增长。
    世界银行目前的援助重点是，鼓励投资、促进私有经济发展、帮助培训人员、寻找解决粮食和债务问题的办法。
    在谈到目前非洲正在进行的经济调整问题时，他说，“加纳、马拉维、毛里求斯、塞内加尔和喀麦隆等非洲国家经济调整工作进行得很顺利……这些国家现在已经出现了经济好转的迹象。”
