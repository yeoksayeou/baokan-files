Title: The UK to Establish Europe's Largest Marine Research Center
Author:
Date: 1989-01-11
Edition: 7

The UK is set to establish Europe's largest marine research center in Southampton, according to a recent plan drafted by British scientists.

It is expected that Kenneth Baker, the Secretary of State for Education and Science, will approve this plan later this month.

Recently, the United Nations allocated approximately 40% of the world's ocean floor to various countries for development. Countries like the US and France are heavily investing in the development of these regions, as they are rich in mineral and biological resources.

John Woods of the UK's Natural Environment Research Council stated, "We must do the same."

One of the goals of ocean development is to collect mineral nodules. These fist-sized mineral blocks are rich in cobalt, copper, and zinc.

These mineral blocks are scattered on the ocean floor, much like tennis balls scattered on a tennis court.

Additionally, there are abundant deposits of phosphate and sulfur minerals near the ocean floor volcanoes that can be mined.

Under a contract between the UK and US governments, British scientists are using highly precise sonar equipment to map the ocean floor around the US.

The marine research center to be established in Southampton will strengthen ties with enterprises and promptly transfer research and development results to them.

### 英将建立欧洲最大海洋研究中心

1989-01-11
第7版(国际)
专栏：

　　英将建立欧洲最大海洋研究中心
    新华社伦敦１月９日电  据《观察家报》报道，根据英国科学家最近制订的计划：英国将在南安普敦建立欧洲最大的海洋研究中心。
    预料教育和科学大臣肯尼思·贝克将在这个月晚些时候批准这项计划。
    最近，联合国把大约４０％的世界洋底分给各国开发。美、法等国正在大量投资开发这些区域，因为这些区域富含矿物和生物资源。
    英国自然环境研究委员会约翰·伍兹说：“我们也必须这样做。”
    开发海洋的目的之一是采集矿物结核，这些像拳头大小的矿石块，富含钴、铜和锌。
    这些矿石块覆盖在洋底，就像散落在网球场上的网球。
    此外，在海底火山附近还有丰富的磷矿和硫矿可供开采。
    根据英美两国政府的一项合同，英国科学家用高度精密的声纳装置，正在美国周围绘制海底地图。
    英国在南安普敦建立的海洋研究中心将加强同企业的联系，并把研究开发成果及时移交给企业。
