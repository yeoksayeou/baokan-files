U.S. Commemorates Black Civil Rights Leader Martin Luther King Jr.

Xinhua News Agency, Washington, January 15 — January 16 is the memorial day for Martin Luther King Jr., the leader of the U.S. black civil rights movement. During commemorative activities held across the country, many civil rights activists criticized the continued existence of racial segregation in the United States.

King's widow, Coretta Scott King, spoke in Atlanta, saying that although the "white man's world" of the 1950s and 1960s has passed, racial segregation still pervades American life.

Statistics show that 13% of black Americans are unemployed, while the unemployment rate for whites is only 5.3%. Additionally, 31% of blacks live below the poverty line, which is 20 percentage points higher than the rate for whites.

### 美纪念黑人民权运动领袖马丁·路德·金

1989-01-17
第6版(国际)
专栏：

　　美纪念黑人民权运动领袖马丁·路德·金
    新华社华盛顿１月１５日电  １月１６日是美国黑人民权运动领袖马丁·路德·金的纪念日。在各地举行的纪念活动中，许多民权活动家批评美国目前仍存在种族隔离现象。
    金牧师的遗孀斯科特在亚特兰大发表讲话时说，尽管五、六十年代“白人的世界”已经过去，但种族隔离现象依然普遍存在于美国的生活之中。
    据统计，美国黑人中有１３％的失业者，而白人的失业率仅为５．３％；生活在贫困线以下的黑人占黑人人口总数的３１％，比白人的贫困人口高出２０个百分点。
