Title: Japan, the US and others implement an international deep-sea drilling plan
Author:
Date: 1989-01-08
Edition: 6

The United States, Japan, West Germany, France, and Canada will begin implementing the "International Deep-Sea Drilling Plan" in April near the Japanese archipelago to reveal the formation history of the Japanese archipelago.

According to this plan, they will use a deep-sea drilling ship to drill twelve sites in the seabed south of the Ogasawara Islands, south of Shikoku Island in the South China Sea, and the Sea of Japan, reaching depths of up to one kilometer. They will extract seabed sediments and rocks to analyze past climate and environmental changes as well as the history of crustal movements. This will verify that the Japanese archipelago was originally connected to the Asian continent but later separated due to the subsidence of the Japan Trench, thus forming the archipelago and the Sea of Japan.

### 日美等实施国际深海钻探计划

1989-01-08
第6版(国际)
专栏：国际新闻简报

　　日美等实施国际深海钻探计划
    美国、日本、西德、法国、加拿大将从四月起在日本列岛近海实施“国际深海钻探计划”，以揭示日本列岛的形成历史。
    根据这一计划，他们将使用深海钻探船在小笠原群岛南方、四国岛南面的南海板块和日本海等海底钻探十二处，深达千米，取出海底沉积物和岩石，分析过去的气候、环境的变化及地壳变动的历史，以证实日本列岛原与亚洲大陆相连，后因受到日本海沟下陷的影响，离开大陆，遂形成列岛和日本海的历史。
    　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　（据新华社）
