Title: 29 Illegal Economic Organizations Shut Down for Using Forged Documents in Fraudulent Activities
Author: Pān Gǎng
Date: 1989-01-19
Edition: 1

The People’s Daily reported from Beijing on January 18: Reporter Pan Gang reports: "China Elderly Welfare Issues Research Association" and 28 other unapproved and unregistered organizations illegally engaging in economic activities, such as "companies" and "groups," were officially shut down today by the State Administration for Industry and Commerce.

Relevant officials introduced the situation, indicating that these illegal entities used forged documents, letters of introduction, notices, guarantees, promises, checks, drafts, and proof of funds to lure investments and introduce foreign capital. They engaged in fraud, speculation, and disrupted social and economic order, causing some units to suffer losses. According to reports, Li Shuxiang, an unemployed person from Jiamusi City, Heilongjiang Province, illegally established the "China Elderly Welfare Issues Research Association" in July 1987. In just one and a half years, this association forged State Council seals, State Administration for Industry and Commerce enterprise standard registration notices, and various domestic and international credit certificates. They established nearly 20 branch organizations, including the "China Urban-Rural Economic Construction General Company," "China International Zhongshan Financial Group," and "China Overseas Chinese Development Group," and engaged in fraudulent and speculative activities.

The "China Urban-Rural Economic Construction General Company," established earlier last year, used the pretext of providing 10,000 tons of castings to sign a contract with a certain agricultural machinery casting factory in a county in Northeast China, defrauding a commission of 160,000 yuan. In April of the same year, this "General Company" again used the bait of providing electrolytic copper to defraud a certain cable factory in Shenyang of 800,000 yuan. The "China Overseas Chinese Development Group" forged Ministry of Foreign Economic Relations and Trade documents to establish the "China Overseas Chinese Zhuhai Overseas Chinese Business Group" in Hainan Province. After registration, they partnered with a certain trading company in Hong Kong to establish the "China Overseas Chinese Hainan International Investment Group."

An official from the State Administration for Industry and Commerce's Enterprise Registration Bureau pointed out that these 29 illegal economic organizations were discovered by various localities over the past year. The State Administration for Industry and Commerce has notified local industry and commerce administrative departments to strictly investigate and resolutely shut down these organizations. Currently, there are more than 20 other organizations under investigation, and their cases will be publicly announced and handled after verification.

### 采用伪造证件等手段进行诈骗活动  ２９家非法经济组织被取缔
潘岗
1989-01-19
第1版(要闻)
专栏：

　　采用伪造证件等手段进行诈骗活动
    ２９家非法经济组织被取缔
    本报北京１月１８日讯  记者潘岗报道：“中国老龄福利问题研究会”等２９家未经批准和登记注册、非法从事经济活动的“公司”、“集团”，今天被国家工商局通告取缔。
    有关负责人介绍的情况表明，这些非法单位采用伪造各种批件、介绍信、通知书、担保书、承诺书、支票、汇票、资金证明等手段，以投资、引进外资等为诱饵，招摇撞骗，投机倒把，使一些单位大上其当，遭受损失，严重扰乱了社会经济秩序。据介绍，黑龙江省佳木斯市来京无业人员李树香于１９８７年７月非法成立的“中国老龄福利问题研究会”，短短一年半间，就伪造国务院公章、国家工商局企业标准登记通知书、国内外各种资信证明，先后设立了“中国城乡经济建设总公司”、“中国国际中山金融集团”、“中国侨联开发集团”等近２０个分支机构，猖狂从事招摇撞骗、投机倒把活动。
    去年早些时候成立的“中国城乡经济建设总公司”以提供铸件一万吨为名，与东北某县农机铸造厂签订协议，骗取“提成”１．６万元；去年４月，这家“总公司”又以提供电解铜为诱饵，骗取沈阳某电缆厂８０万元。“中国侨联开发集团”伪造经贸部批件，在海南省成立“中国侨联珠海侨联实业集团”，骗取注册后，又与香港某贸易公司合营，成立了“中国侨联海南国际投资集团”。
    国家工商局企业登记司负责人指出，这２９家非法经济组织是去年以来各地陆续发现的，国家工商局已通知各地工商管理机关严加查处和坚决取缔。目前正在核查的还有２０多个，准备查清后再行公布处理。
