Title: Estrogen Therapy Can Prevent Bone Fractures in Elderly Women
Author:
Date: 1989-01-12
Edition: 7

The Royal College of Physicians in Britain recently released a report stating that postmenopausal elderly women should take estrogen to prevent bone fractures.

After menopause, the secretion of estrogen decreases, making bones prone to calcium loss and becoming brittle. Hip fractures are most common among elderly women. Approximately 60 percent of hip fractures occur in women over the age of 75.

In the past 20 years, the number of elderly women with hip fractures in Britain has doubled. In England and Wales, more than 46,000 people suffer hip fractures each year, and about one-quarter of them die as a result. Dr. John Kanis, a lecturer in human metabolism at the University of Sheffield, said that estrogen helps prevent calcium loss in bones. After menopause, obese women still secrete some estrogen. However, thin women secrete very little estrogen and should therefore receive hormone replacement therapy.

Raymond Hoffenberg, president of the Royal College of Physicians, said that hormone replacement therapy is suitable for all women over the age of 50. However, women with a family history of breast cancer should be excluded.

### 老年妇女服雌激素可防骨折

1989-01-12
第7版(国际)
专栏：

　　老年妇女服雌激素可防骨折
    新华社伦敦电  英国皇家医生学院近日发表一份报告说，绝经后的老年妇女应服雌激素以防骨折。
    绝经后，雌激素的分泌减少，骨头容易失钙而变脆。髋骨骨折在老年妇女中最常见。大约百分之六十的髋骨骨折发生在年过七十五岁的妇女中。
    英国在过去的二十年内，老年妇女髋骨骨折的人数增加了一倍。在英格兰和威尔士，每年髋骨骨折人数超过四点六万人，约四分之一的人因此死亡。设菲尔德大学人体新陈代谢讲师约翰·卡尼斯博士说，雌激素帮助防止骨头失钙，绝经后，肥胖妇女仍然分泌一些雌激素。但是体瘦的妇女分泌的雌激素非常少，因此她们应该接受激素补充疗法。
    皇家医生学院院长雷蒙德·霍芬伯格说，所有年过五十的妇女接受激素补充疗法都是适宜的。但是近亲得过乳腺癌的妇女应例外。
