Title: West Germany Decides to Further Strengthen Export Controls
Author:
Date: 1989-01-13
Edition: 7

West Germany has decided to strengthen legal and administrative measures to strictly supervise and control the export of nuclear technology, chemical equipment, and other materials with military strategic significance.

These measures include: first, improving and strengthening cooperation among relevant departments and improving the work of supervisory agencies; second, amending and formulating new laws to increase penalties for enterprises and individuals violating export regulations; third, strengthening the obligation to declare and approval regulations.

Officials here noted that West Germany's decision to further strengthen export controls is directly related to the recent dissatisfaction of the United States regarding the chemical plant issue in Libya. The United States accused a chemical plant in Libya of producing chemical weapons, while West Germany participated in the construction of the plant. The West German side denied this. The dispute between the two countries on this issue has cast a shadow over West German-American relations.

### 西德决定进一步加强出口控制

1989-01-13
第7版(国际)
专栏：

　　西德决定进一步加强出口控制
    新华社波恩一月十日电  西德政府今天决定强化法律和行政措施，严格监督和控制核技术、化工设备以及其他具有军事战略意义的物资出口。
    这些措施是：第一，改善和加强有关部门之间的合作，改进监督机构的工作；第二，修改和制定新的法律，对违反出口规定的企业和个人加重处罚；第三，强化申报义务和批准规定。
    这里的人士注意到，西德政府决定进一步加强出口控制，同美国最近在利比亚化工厂问题上对西德的不满有着直接关系。美国指责利比亚一家化工厂是生产化学武器的工厂，而西德参与了工厂的建造工作。西德方面予以否认。两国在这一问题上的争论给西德—美国关系罩上了一层阴影。
