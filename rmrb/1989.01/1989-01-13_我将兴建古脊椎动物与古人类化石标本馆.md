Title: I Will Build a Fossil Museum of Ancient Vertebrates and Ancient Humans
Author: Lǐ Xīguāng
Date: 1989-01-13
Edition: 3

A museum boasting the most abundant collection of ancient vertebrate and ancient human fossils in Asia will be constructed in Beijing.

Currently, the Institute of Vertebrate Paleontology and Paleoanthropology under the Chinese Academy of Sciences possesses 120,000 fossils of vertebrates, ancient humans, and ancient apes, ranking first in Asia. To protect these precious treasures, with the care and support of relevant departments of the central government and the Beijing municipal government, the Chinese Academy of Sciences has decided to commence construction of the fossil museum this year, marking the 60th anniversary of the discovery of Peking Man. The museum is scheduled to be completed and put into use by 1991.

The museum will be located in the western suburbs of Beijing, near the Beijing Planetarium, Xiyuan Hotel, and Beijing Zoo, with a total area of over 10,000 square meters.

### 我将兴建古脊椎动物与古人类化石标本馆
李希光
1989-01-13
第3版(科教·文化·体育)
专栏：

　　我将兴建古脊椎动物与古人类化石标本馆
    据新华社北京１月１２日电  （记者李希光）一座拥有亚洲最丰富的古脊椎动物与古人类化石的标本馆将在京兴建。
    目前中国科学院古脊椎动物与古人类研究所拥有的脊椎动物、古人类和古猿化石数量达１２万件，居亚洲首位。为了保护这些宝贵的财富，在中央、北京市有关部门的关怀支持下，中国科学院决定在今年北京猿人发现６０周年之际破土动工兴建化石标本馆，并拟于１９９１年竣工使用。
    这座标本馆地处北京西郊，与北京天文馆、西苑饭店、北京动物园相近，总面积达１万多平方米。
