Title: The Soviets Create the World's Smallest Microplane
Author:
Date: 1989-01-06
Edition: 6

The Soviets have created the world's smallest microplane. According to a report from Tass, this microplane, including its wings, has a total area of approximately 1.5 square meters, weighs 47 kilograms, is about 3 meters long, and 1 meter high. Its minimum flying speed is 42 kilometers per hour, and its maximum speed is 130 kilometers per hour. It consumes about 3 liters of fuel per hour. The microplane is easy to operate and can take off and land within a 30-meter runway on asphalt roads, hard ground, or grasslands.

The designer of this plane is Viktor Dmitriev.

### 苏制成世界上最小的微型飞机

1989-01-06
第6版(国际)
专栏：国际新闻简报

　　苏制成世界上最小的微型飞机
    据塔斯社报道，苏制成世界上最小的微型飞机。这架微型飞机加上机翼的总面积约一点五平方米，重四十七公斤，长约三米，高一米，最低飞行时速为四十二公里，最快为一百三十公里。飞行每小时耗油量约三升。微型飞机操作简单，起降跑道可以在三十米以内，在柏油马路、硬土地或草地上均可起降。
　　这架飞机的设计师是维克托·德米特里耶夫。　　（据新华社）
