Date: 1989-01-20

	Soviet Public Opinion Survey on Bush's Policies: Majority Optimistic About Future of Soviet-American Relations

	New survey reveals Soviet views on U.S. President Bush's policies

	MOSCOW, Jan. 17 (Xinhua) - According to a report by TASS today, the Soviet Academy of Sciences' Institute of Sociology conducted a public opinion survey in the Russian Federation before the end of the year to gauge Soviet citizens' views on the policies of the new U.S. President, George Bush.

	The survey included 800 respondents. The majority expressed the hope that Bush would pursue a peaceful policy and develop economic, scientific, and cultural ties with the Soviet Union. 85% of the respondents believe that the development of Soviet-American relations is crucial for easing global tensions. However, a significant number of people are dissatisfied with the current state of Soviet-American relations, with only 15% believing that the relations are good. The majority are optimistic about the future of Soviet-American relations.

	One-third of the respondents think that Bush will continue Reagan's policies in his dealings with the Soviet Union. On the issue of holding regular high-level Soviet-American meetings, opinions were most unified, with 93% of people believing that such meetings are appropriate.

### 苏对布什政策作民意测验  多数人对苏美关系前景乐观

1989-01-20
第6版(国际)
专栏：

　　苏对布什政策作民意测验
    多数人对苏美关系前景乐观
    新华社莫斯科１月１７日电  据塔斯社今天报道，苏联科学院社会学研究所年前在俄罗斯联邦进行了一次民意测验，调查苏联人对美国新总统布什所持政策的看法。
    接受询问的共８００人，其中多数希望布什奉行和平政策，同苏联发展经济、科学和文化联系。８５％的人认为，苏美关系的发展对缓和世界紧张局势十分重要。但相当多的人对苏美关系现状表示不满，认为苏美关系良好的人仅占１５％。多数人对苏美关系的前景持乐观态度。
    有１／３的被询问者认为，在对苏关系方面，布什将会继续执行里根的政策。在定期举行苏美最高级会晤问题上，人们意见最为一致，９３％的人认为这种会晤是适宜的。
