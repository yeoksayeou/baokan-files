Title: Ministry of Posts and Telecommunications to Issue "Year of the Snake" Stamps
Author:
Date: 1989-01-04
Edition: 3

The Ministry of Posts and Telecommunications will issue a set of special stamps titled "Year of the Snake" on January 5, 1989. The set consists of one stamp.

1989 is the Year of the Snake according to the Chinese lunar calendar. The "Year of the Snake" special stamps are the tenth set in the series of twelve zodiac animal stamps issued by the Ministry of Posts and Telecommunications. The design features a coiled snake, inspired by the Chinese folk legend of snakes protecting all things. The snake is adorned with flowers of the four seasons and holds a lingzhi mushroom in its mouth, symbolizing peace and good fortune throughout the year. The stamp was designed by Lü Shengzhong from the Central Academy of Fine Arts.

New China News Agency

### 邮电部明发行《己巳年》邮票

1989-01-04
第3版(科教·文化·体育)
专栏：

　　邮电部明发行《己巳年》邮票
    邮电部定于１９８９年１月５日发行《己巳年》特种邮票一套，共一枚。
    １９８９年是农历己巳年，即蛇年。《己巳年》特种邮票是邮电部发行的十二生肖系列邮票的第十套，图案为一条盘蛇，取自我国民间盘蛇保护万物的传说。蛇身饰四季花卉，口含灵芝，像征四季平安，美好吉祥。这套邮票由中央美术学院吕胜中设计。
