Title: Non-Aligned Countries in the Security Council Hold Secret Meeting to Discuss the Issue of the UN Peacekeeping Force in Namibia
Author:
Date: 1989-01-15
Edition: 6

The non-aligned countries' representatives in the Security Council held a secret meeting today, attempting to break the deadlock in the Security Council's consultations on the size and expenses of the UN peacekeeping force in Namibia.

The spokesman did not disclose the details of the meeting.

The Security Council's consultations on the UN peacekeeping force in Namibia began last December. The five permanent members proposed reducing the number of troops to save $200 million in expenses. However, some non-permanent members opposed reducing the number of troops from 7,500 to 3,000, in order to ensure that South Africa would not interfere with or manipulate Namibia's elections.

### 安理会不结盟国家举行秘密会议  讨论纳米比亚维持和平部队问题

1989-01-15
第6版(国际)
专栏：

　　安理会不结盟国家举行秘密会议
    讨论纳米比亚维持和平部队问题
    新华社联合国１月１３日电  据联合国一位发言人透露，安理会中的不结盟国家代表今天举行了一次秘密会议，试图打破安理会就联合国磋商纳米比亚维持和平部队的规模和开支等问题出现的僵局。
    这位发言人没有透露会议的详细内容。
    安理会关于纳米比亚维持和平部队的磋商是从去年１２月开始的。安理会５个常任理事国在谈判中提出减少这支部队的人数以节省２亿美元开支。然而，一些非常任理事国反对将维持和平部队的人数从７５００名削减到３０００名，以保证南非不对纳米比亚大选进行干涉和操纵。
