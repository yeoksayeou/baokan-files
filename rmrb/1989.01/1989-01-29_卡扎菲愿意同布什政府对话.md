Title: Gaddafi Willing to Engage in Dialogue with the Bush Administration
Author:
Date: 1989-01-29
Edition: 6

Libyan leader Gaddafi once again expressed his willingness to engage in direct dialogue with the Bush administration in a televised interview broadcast on the evening of the 27th.

He said that Mr. Bush is a practical person, "completely different from Reagan." Earlier this month, the United States, citing Libya's support for terrorist activities, rejected Gaddafi's call for direct dialogue between the two countries to resolve disputes.

When mentioning the controversial chemical plant issue, Gaddafi reiterated that the chemical plant under construction only produces chemical drugs and is not, as Washington alleges, used to produce chemical weapons. He also stated that he would invite diplomats and journalists to attend the plant's opening ceremony.

### 卡扎菲愿意同布什政府对话

1989-01-29
第6版(国际)
专栏：国际要闻简报

　　卡扎菲愿意同布什政府对话
    利比亚领导人卡扎菲在二十七日晚间播放的一次电视采访节目中再次表示，他愿意同布什总统的政府进行直接对话。
    他说，布什先生是一位讲求实际的人，“与里根截然不同”。本月初，美国以利比亚支持恐怖活动为由拒绝了卡扎菲关于两国直接对话以解决争端的呼吁。
    在提到有争议的化工厂问题时，卡扎菲重申，这家正在建设中的化工厂只生产化学药品，而不像华盛顿所指责的那样用来生产化学武器。他还表示，将邀请外交官和记者参加该厂的开工典礼。
