Title: UK Uses Genetic Engineering to Produce Anticoagulants
Author:
Date: 1989-01-25
Edition: 7

Doctors at London's Guy's Hospital have used genetically engineered leech saliva anticoagulants to treat heart disease and strokes.

Biologists have known for decades that leech saliva contains an anticoagulant. A single leech has only one billionth of a gram of this substance.

Now, scientists at a pharmaceutical company have used genetic engineering to insert the gene that produces the anticoagulant into yeast cells, cultivating them like brewing yeast, to mass-produce this substance.

### 英用遗传工程技术制造抗凝血剂

1989-01-25
第7版(国际)
专栏：

　　英用遗传工程技术制造抗凝血剂
    新华社伦敦１月２３日电  据《每日电讯报》报道，伦敦盖斯医院的医生用遗传工程技术制造的水蛭唾液抗凝血剂，治疗心脏病和中风。
    生物学家几十年前就知道，水蛭的唾液中含有一种抗凝血剂。一条水蛭只有十亿分之一克这种物质。
    现在，一家制药公司的科学家用遗传工程技术把制造抗凝血剂的基因插入酵母细胞，像酿酒酵母一样培养，让它大量生产这种物质。
