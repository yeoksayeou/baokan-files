Title: Bush's Inauguration Ceremony Kicks Off
Author: Huang Yong
Date: 1989-01-20
Edition: 6

This afternoon, 50,000 people gathered in front of the Lincoln Memorial in Washington, D.C., forming a vast sea of humanity. Amidst music and fireworks, the grand inauguration ceremony for the 41st President of the United States, George H.W. Bush, began.

After performances by some of America's top music groups, five parachutists descended from the sky. Twenty-one jet fighters soared through the sky, paying tribute to the new Bush administration.

U.S. President-elect George H.W. Bush and Vice President-elect Dan Quayle, along with their families, attended the grand ceremony.

Among the 50,000 attendees, 20,000 received a special gift—a miniature flashlight. This symbolized Bush's campaign promise that countless Americans would contribute their light and warmth to the cause they were dedicated to.

In the evening, Bush declared, "My ambition during my term as president is to make these countless lights shine brighter than ever before." He lit a torch, and millions of flashlights illuminated the dark night sky. At the same time, the sky was filled with a dazzling display of fireworks.

The ceremony will last for five days, including the swearing-in, banquets, dances, and outdoor performances, totaling 70 events. The entire ceremony will cost $30 million, setting a new record for the most expensive presidential inauguration in U.S. history. Most of the funds will come from ticket sales and the sale of commemorative items.

Most of the activities will charge participants. Each person attending tonight's two banquets must pay $1,500, and on Thursday evening, for Bush's inauguration ceremony, a box seat will cost $20,000.

The high cost of this ceremony has been criticized by some organizations that aid the poor and homeless. Outside the building where the inauguration banquet is being held, a charity organization dramatically arranged a "soup banquet" for the homeless tonight.

### 布什就职盛典揭开序幕
黄泳
1989-01-20
第6版(国际)
专栏：特写

　　布什就职盛典揭开序幕
    新华社记者  黄泳
    今天下午，５万人在美国首都华盛顿的林肯纪念堂前汇成了一个浩瀚的海洋，在音乐和焰火的交响中，盛况空前的美国第４１任总统的就职庆典在这里拉开了序幕。
    在美国一些第一流的音乐团体精彩的表演之后，５名跳伞队员从空中飘然落下，只见２１架喷气式战斗机翱翔于苍穹，向布什新政府致以敬礼。
    美国当选总统布什和副总统当选人丹·奎尔及其家人参加了这个盛大的庆典。
    参加庆典的５万人中，有２万名观众每人收到了一件特殊的礼物——一只微型手电筒，这象征着布什在竞选总统时所强调的思想，那就是无数的美国人在为之献身的事业中所发出的星星点点的光和热。
    傍晚时分，布什宣布，“使这千万点光比以往照得更亮，是我总统任期内的雄心。”他点燃火炬，于是，千万只手电筒的光芒射向漆黑的夜空，此时，空中开满了缤纷的礼花。
    庆典将进行５天，包括宣誓就职、晚宴、舞会及露天表演等７０个项目。整个庆典将耗费３０００万美元，这个数字创下了美国历届总统就职庆典费用的最高纪录，资金大部分将来源于各类活动的票房和出售纪念品的收入。
    大部分的活动向参加者收费。参加今晚的两场宴会每人必须付１５００美元，星期四晚上布什的就职仪式，参加者订一个包厢需付２万美元。
    这次庆典昂贵的开支受到了一些救济穷人和无家可归者组织的批评。在举行就职晚宴的大楼外面，一个慈善机构颇具戏剧性地为今晚的无家可归者安排了一个“汤宴”。　　　　　　　　　　　　　　（新华社华盛顿１月１８日电）
