Title: U.S. Scientists Propose Building a "Data Highway"
Author:
Date: 1989-01-04
Edition: 7

According to a report by the New York Times, American computer experts and government officials strongly advocate for the construction of a nationwide "data highway." They believe this will have a significant economic impact.

This "data highway" refers to a high-speed fiber-optic network that connects numerous supercomputers in national laboratories, enabling tens of thousands of researchers in colleges and industries across the country to use them for scientific research.

The new network will cost $400 million and is expected to be completed by the mid-1990s. Existing high-speed networks can only transmit 1.5 million bits per second, whereas the new network will be capable of transmitting 3 billion bits per second.

Officials from the National Science Foundation predict that through computer-based "collaboration," scientists will be able to directly observe and control the operation of equipment such as particle accelerators, wind tunnels, telescopes, nuclear reactors, and complex machinery, even if they are far away from the actual devices.

### 美科学家建议建造“数据高速公路”  将会产生巨大经济影响

1989-01-04
第7版(国际)
专栏：

　　美科学家建议建造“数据高速公路”
    将会产生巨大经济影响
    新华社华盛顿１９８８年１２月３０日  电据《纽约时报》报道，美国计算机专家和政府官员极力主张建造一条全国范围的“数据高速公路”，认为这将会产生巨大的经济影响。
    这条“数据高速公路”就是一个高速纤维光学网络，连接国家实验室的许许多多超大计算机，并使全国成千上万的学院和工业研究人员能用它们进行科学研究。
    新网络将耗资４亿美元，可望在９０年代中期造成。现有的许多高速网络每秒钟只能传送１５０万个比特。而新的网络每秒钟可传送３０亿个比特。
    国家科学基金会的官员预言，通过计算机化的“协作”，即使科学家们距实际设备非常遥远，他们用计算机工作站就可直接观察和控制像粒子加速器、风洞、望远镜、核反应堆和复杂设备的运转情况。
