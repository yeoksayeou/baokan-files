    Educational, Scientific, and Cultural News

    ● The giant panda "Meimei" raised at the Chengdu Zoo has given birth to 7 litters totaling 9 cubs, with 6 cubs successfully raised. Among artificially raised giant pandas, "Meimei" has the highest reproductive rate and the highest survival rate for offspring, earning the title of "best mother animal." (According to Xinhua News Agency)

    ● The book "New Ideas, New Explorations, New Patterns—An Overview of the Reforms at Shenzhen University," which reflects the educational model of Shenzhen University, has been published by Beijing Language Institute Press.

    ● On January 22, the Hong Kong government officially issued a set of colored postcards titled "Wild Plants First Discovered in Hong Kong." This set of 10 postcards introduces 10 wild plants that were first discovered in Hong Kong between 1840 and 1955. Among them, Ge Lianghong tea was first discovered by a police officer in 1955 and has not been found in any other regions of the world. (According to Xinhua News Agency)

### 教科文零讯

1989-01-23
第3版(科教·文化·体育)
专栏：教科文零讯

　　教科文零讯
    ●成都动物园饲养的大熊猫“美美”，至今已产７胎９仔，育成６仔。在人工饲养的大熊猫中繁殖力最强，育幼成活率高，被誉为“最佳母兽”。（据新华社）
    ●反映深圳大学办学模式的《新思路　新探索　新格局——深圳大学改革面面观》一书，已由北京语言学院出版社出版。　　　　　　　
　　　　（陈兴）
    ●香港政府１月２２日正式发行一套名为“首次在香港发现的野生植物”的彩色明信片。这套明信片共１０张，所介绍的１０种野生植物是从１８４０年至１９５５年间在香港被人们首次发现的。其中葛量洪茶是一位警察在１９５５年首次发现的，目前在世界其它地区还未有发现。　　　　　　　　（据新华社）
