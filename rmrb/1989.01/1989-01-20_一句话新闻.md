Title: One-Sentence News
Author:
Date: 1989-01-20
Edition: 6

One-Sentence News

    △ Libyan leader Muammar Gaddafi issued a serious warning to the United States on the 17th, stating that Libya will respond to violence with violence, and any attack on Libya's Rabta factory will be a repeat of the Hiroshima tragedy.

    △ U.S. Department of Defense spokesman Pete Williams said on the 17th that over the past few years, the United States has been purchasing old Soviet fighter jets and weapons from the market to test their performance and use them to train U.S. troops.

    △ The Polisario Front issued a statement on the 17th saying that Western Sahara's Foreign Minister Mohammed Sidati called on the United States to support international efforts to negotiate a resolution to the Western Sahara conflict during a meeting held in Lusaka last week to discuss Africa-U.S. relations.

    △ The Libyan News Agency issued a statement on the 18th announcing that the General Council of the Arab Trade Union International Federation decided to halt all services for U.S. transportation vehicles at Arab ports and airports for one week starting from February 1.

### 一句话新闻

1989-01-20
第6版(国际)
专栏：一句话新闻

　　一句话新闻
    △利比亚领导人卡扎菲１７日向美国发出严重警告说，利比亚将以暴力来对付暴力，对利比亚拉布塔工厂的任何袭击将是广岛悲剧的重演。
    △美国国防部发言人博丹伊１７日说，在过去的几年里，美国一直在市场上购买苏联旧式战斗机和武器，以检查其性能，并用其训练美国军队。
    △西撒人阵１７日发表一项公报说，西撒外长穆罕默德·西达提上周在卢萨卡举行的一次讨论非洲和美国关系的会议上，呼吁美国支持国际社会为谈判解决西撒冲突所作的努力。
    △利比亚国家通讯社１８日发表一项公报宣布，阿拉伯工会国际联合会中央理事会决定从２月１日起的一周内在阿拉伯的港口和机场停止为美国的交通运输工具提供的任何服务。　　　　　　　　　　　　　
　　　　　　（本报讯）
