Title: Resisting America, Aiding Korea, Firmly Opposing the United States Arming Japan, Workers, Women, and Others in Various Locations Hold Parades and Demonstrations
Author:
Date: 1951-02-20
Page: 1
　　Resisting America, Aiding Korea, Firmly Opposing the United States Arming Japan
    Workers, Women, and Others in Various Locations Hold Parades and Demonstrations
    [Report from this newspaper] People in Nanchang, Harbin, Xi'an, Fuzhou, and other places have successively held demonstration parades and rallies to deeply expand the Resist America, Aid Korea movement and resolutely oppose the United States arming Japan.
    More than 30,000 workers in Nanchang held a demonstration parade on the evening of the 16th. At the rally held before the parade, the workers unanimously expressed their intention to deeply launch a patriotic production competition and the Resist America, Aid Korea movement in order to smash American aggression.
    More than 20,000 women from various agencies, factories, schools, neighborhoods, and ethnic minorities in Harbin held a demonstration parade rally in Zhaolin Park on the 11th. At the parade rally, Guo Jiyun, director of the Harbin Municipal Democratic Women's Federation, model production team worker Chi Guizhen, and army representative Shi Jianfang spoke successively, and then passed six patriotic pledges and telegrams to Chairman Mao and others.
    More than 4,000 literary and art workers in Xi'an, at a demonstration parade rally held on the 15th, Ke Zhongping, director of the Northwest Federation of Literary and Art Circles, called on the literary and art circles of Xi'an to continue to use various forms of literature and art to deeply publicize the Resist America, Aid Korea movement and expose the conspiracy of American imperialism to arm Japan. The conference passed four patriotic pledges for literary and art workers in Xi'an to expand and deepen the Resist America, Aid Korea propaganda and not to perform dramas and movies containing toxins.
    More than 2,000 Christian men and women in Fuzhou also held a demonstration parade on the 17th to oppose the United States arming Japan. Participants included Christians from 42 units, including the Fuzhou Chinese Christian Sheng Kung Hui, the Chinese Christian Minzhong Association, the Fuzhou Chinese Christian Methodist Episcopal Church, Fukien Christian University, Hwa Nan Women's College, and the Union Hospital. The parade was led by pastors of various churches, principals of church schools, and directors of church hospitals. The Christian men and women held up huge portraits of Chairman Mao, Commander-in-Chief Zhu, and General Kim Il-sung, chanting slogans such as "Resolutely oppose the United States rearming Japan" and "Unite under the banner of Chairman Mao and resolutely carry out the Three-Self Patriotic Movement." At the rally before the parade, they unanimously passed a congratulatory telegram to Chairman Mao.
    The demonstration parades and rallies in Harbin, Xi'an, Fuzhou, and other places all passed congratulatory telegrams to the Chinese and Korean People's Army or telegrams to the Japanese people.
    In addition, returned overseas Chinese in Fuzhou, Xiamen, Quanzhou, Zhangzhou, and other places in Fujian Province are actively preparing to hold a demonstration parade rally on February 21 (Anti-Colonial System Struggle Day) to resist America, aid Korea, and oppose the United States arming Japan, in response to the call of the All-China Federation of Returned Overseas Chinese in the capital. The Fuzhou Municipal Federation of Returned Overseas Chinese held an expanded council meeting on the 13th to form a preparatory committee to be responsible for the propaganda, organization, and other work of preparing for the demonstration parade rally. The meeting also discussed launching returned overseas Chinese to sign patriotic pledges, raise funds to comfort the Korean People's Army and the Chinese People's Volunteers, and provide relief to Korean refugees. More than 40 returned overseas Chinese enthusiastically donated on the spot, donating a total of more than 12 million yuan in consolation money.

### 抗美援朝坚决反对美国武装日本  各地工人妇女等举行游行示威

1951-02-20
第1版()
专栏：

　　抗美援朝坚决反对美国武装日本        
    各地工人妇女等举行游行示威
    【本报讯】南昌、哈尔滨、西安、福州等地人民纷纷举行示威游行大会，深入扩大抗美援朝运动，坚决反对美国武装日本。
    南昌市三万余工人十六日晚举行示威游行。在游行前举行的集会上，工人们一致表示将深入展开爱国主义的生产竞赛和抗美援朝运动，以粉碎美国的侵略。
    哈尔滨市各机关、工厂、学校、街道以及少数民族的妇女两万余人，于十一日在兆麟公园举行了示威游行大会。在游行大会上，哈尔滨市民主妇女联合会主任郭霁云、模范生产小组工人迟桂珍、部队代表石建芳相继讲话后，通过了六项爱国公约和致毛主席等的电文。
    西安市文艺工作者四千余人于十五日举行的游行示威大会上，西北文学艺术界联合会主任柯仲平号召西安文艺界继续以各种文艺形式，深入宣传抗美援朝和揭发美帝国主义武装日本的阴谋。大会通过了西安市文艺工作者扩大和深入抗美援朝宣传与不演含有毒素的戏曲电影等四项爱国公约。
    福州市二千余男女基督教徒于十七日也举行了反对美国武装日本示威游行。参加游行的包括福州中华基督教圣公会、中华基督教闽中协会、福州中华基督教卫理公会、协和大学、华南女子文理学院、协和医院等四十二个单位的基督徒。游行队伍以各教堂牧师和教会学校校长、教会医院院长为先导。男女基督教徒们高举着毛主席、朱总司令、金日成将军的巨幅画像，沿途高呼“坚决反对美国重新武装日本”、“团结在毛主席旗帜下，坚决做好三自革新运动”等口号。在游行前的集会上，他们一致通过了向毛主席的致敬电。
    以上哈尔滨、西安、福州等地的示威游行大会，都通过了向中朝人民部队的致敬电文，或致日本人民的电文。
    此外福建福州、厦门、泉州、漳州等地的归国侨胞，纷纷响应首都归国华侨联谊会的号召，正积极筹备于二月二十一日（反对殖民制度斗争日）举行抗美援朝、反对美国武装日本的示威游行大会。福州市归国华侨联谊会已于十三日召开了扩大理事会议，组成筹备委员会，负责筹备示威游行大会的宣传、组织等工作。会议并讨论了发动归国华侨订立爱国公约、募款慰劳朝鲜人民军与中国人民志愿军及救济朝鲜难民等问题。当场即有四十多位归国华侨踊跃捐输，共捐献了慰劳金一千二百多万元。
