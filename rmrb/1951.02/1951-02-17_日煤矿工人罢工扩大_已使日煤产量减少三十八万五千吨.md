Title: Japanese Coal Miners' Strike Expands, Resulting in a Reduction of 385,000 Tons in Japanese Coal Production
Author:
Date: 1951-02-17
Page: 4

　　Japanese Coal Miners' Strike Expands
　　Has already caused a reduction of 385,000 tons in Japanese Coal Production
    [Xinhua News Agency, Tokyo, 15th] News: The Japanese coal miners' strike is continuing to expand. The workers of the Mitsui, Mitsubishi, Iwahara, and Hokkaido four major coal mining companies have been persisting in their strike since the 7th until the 13th. Simultaneously, about 40,000 members of another six coal mine unions in Kyushu also began a strike on the 13th after negotiations with the management broke down. According to Reuters, as of the 13th, the strike had already reduced Japanese coal production by 385,000 tons.

### 日煤矿工人罢工扩大  已使日煤产量减少三十八万五千吨

1951-02-17
第4版()
专栏：

　　日煤矿工人罢工扩大
　  已使日煤产量减少三十八万五千吨
    【新华社十五日讯】东京消息：日本煤矿工人的罢工正在继续扩大中。三井、三菱、井华、北海道四大煤矿公司的工人，自七日开始罢工以来，迄十三日为止尚在坚持中。同时，九州另外六个煤矿工会约四万名会员，也在与资方谈判破裂后于十三日起开始罢工。据路透社消息，截至十三日为止，罢工已使日本煤产减少三十八万五千吨。
