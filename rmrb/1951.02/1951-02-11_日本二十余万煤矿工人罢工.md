Title: Over Two Hundred Thousand Japanese Coal Miners Strike

Author: 
Date: 1951-02-11
Page: 4

　　Xinhua News Agency, February 10th dispatch from Tokyo: TASS reported on the 8th that workers belonging to the unions of four coal companies, Mitsui, Mitsubishi, Iihua, and Kitatan, announced a general strike on the 7th. The decision to strike was taken when negotiations between representatives of the unions of the above-mentioned four companies and the company authorities regarding changes to the established minimum wage broke down on February 1st.
    According to a dispatch from the Electric Communication Agency in Tokyo, as of the 9th, more than 200,000 Japanese coal miners were on strike demanding wage increases. The national daily coal production has been reduced by 55,000 tons, a decrease of 40 percent.

### 日本二十余万煤矿工人罢工

1951-02-11
第4版()
专栏：

　　日本二十余万煤矿工人罢工
    【新华社十日讯】塔斯社八日电引东京消息：三井、三菱、井华和北炭四煤炭公司工会所属的工人，已于七日宣布总罢工。罢工的决定是在上述四公司工会代表和公司当局关于改变既定的最低工资的谈判于二月一日宣告失败时采取的。
    又据电通社东京消息：迄至九日为止，已有二十多万日本煤矿工人因要求增加工资而进行罢工。全国每日煤产量已减少五万五千吨，即减少了百分之四十。
