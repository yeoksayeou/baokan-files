Title: Japan Peace Supporters Committee Calls for Opposition to the Rearmament of Japan
Author:
Date: 1951-02-05
Page: 4

　　Xinhua News Agency, Tokyo, 3rd. TASS Shanghai, 2nd dispatch: The Japan Peace Supporters Committee sent a letter to local Japanese peace movement organizations and democratic mass organizations stating: "The main task of the current peace movement is to publicize and implement the decisions of the Second World Peace Congress." The Peace Supporters Committee believes that it is necessary to "immediately begin activities to cooperate with the worldwide movement to defend peace."
    The letter pointed out: The struggle against the rearmament of Japan and for the conclusion of an overall peace treaty is the specific task of Japan's peace movement.
    The organ of the Japan Peace Supporters Committee, "Strive for Peace," stated: As of January 10, 6,269,856 people in Japan had signed the Stockholm Appeal.

### 日本拥护和平委员会号召反对重新武装日本

1951-02-05
第4版()
专栏：

　　日本拥护和平委员会号召反对重新武装日本
    【新华社三日讯】塔斯社上海二日电引东京消息：日本拥护和平委员会致函日本各地方和平运动组织和民主群众团体称：“目前和平运动的主要任务，是向民众宣传和实施第二届世界保卫和平大会的决定。”拥护和平委员会认为必须“立即开始活动，以配合全世界保卫和平的运动”。
    函中指出：反对重新武装日本、争取缔结全面和约的斗争，是日本的和平运动的具体任务。
    日本拥护和平委员会机关报《争取和平》称：到一月十日为止，日本已有六百二十六万九千八百五十六人在斯德哥尔摩宣言上签了名。
