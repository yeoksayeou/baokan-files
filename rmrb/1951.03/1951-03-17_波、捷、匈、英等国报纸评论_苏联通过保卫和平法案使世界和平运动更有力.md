Title: Polish, Czech, Hungarian, British and other countries' newspapers comment that the Soviet Union's passage of the Law on Safeguarding Peace strengthens the world peace movement
Author: 
Date: 1951-03-17
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, 16th. TASS dispatch: Newspapers in Poland, Czechoslovakia, Hungary, Bulgaria, Britain, Italy, and other countries published news on the 13th and 14th regarding the Soviet Supreme Soviet's passage of the Law on Safeguarding Peace, and wrote articles commenting on it. The Polish United Workers' Party organ newspaper, *People's Tribune*, stated: "The promulgation of this law is a valuable response from the Soviet people to the call of the Second World Peace Congress and a new manifestation of the Soviet Union's unwavering determination to fight for the defense of peace." Czechoslovakia's *Red Right*, *Labor News*, *People's Defense*, and *Agricultural News* all published the news in detail under large headlines. All newspapers emphasized that the Law on Safeguarding Peace passed by the Soviet Supreme Soviet has great international significance. Hungarian newspapers all published the full text of the Soviet Law on Safeguarding Peace. *Hungarian National News* emphasized: "The Soviet Union's passage of the Law on Safeguarding Peace has strengthened the struggle of progressive humanity around the world to defend world peace." Bulgarian newspapers published many comments from workers, scientists, actors, and artists. Bulgarian Academy of Sciences Academician and World Peace Council member Popov wrote in *Worker's Cause*: "This decision by the Soviet people will give more strength to the world peace movement and the resolutions of the World Peace Council." Ninova, Secretary-General of the Bulgarian National Committee for the Defense of Peace, said: "The Soviet Supreme Soviet's passage of this law is a fatal moral blow to the US-British aggressive bloc within the United Nations."

The British *Daily Worker*, in commenting on the Soviet Law on Safeguarding Peace, compared the situation in the Soviet Union and the people's democratic countries with the war propaganda being conducted by the ruling classes of capitalist countries such as the United States and Britain against their people. The *Daily Worker* emphasized: "Only those countries that prohibit war propaganda and support the conclusion of a peace pact among the five major powers are true advocates of peace." Democratic newspapers in Rome, the capital of Italy, also published the news of the Soviet Union's passage of the Law on Safeguarding Peace with great prominence.

### 波、捷、匈、英等国报纸评论  苏联通过保卫和平法案使世界和平运动更有力
新华社
1951-03-17
第4版()
专栏：

　　波、捷、匈、英等国报纸评论
    苏联通过保卫和平法案使世界和平运动更有力
    【新华社十六日讯】塔斯社消息：波、捷、匈、保和英国、意大利等国报纸，于十三、十四日刊载了苏联最高苏维埃通过保卫和平法案的消息，并纷纷著文加以评论。波兰统一工人党机关报《人民论坛》报称：“这个法案的颁布是苏联人民对于第二届世界保卫和平大会的呼吁的极有价值的回答，而且是苏联始终不渝地为保卫和平而斗争的坚定意志的新表现。”捷克斯洛伐克的《红色权利报》、《劳动报》、《人民防线报》和《农业报》等均在大字标题下详细登载了这一消息。各报均着重指出：苏联最高苏维埃通过的保卫和平法案具有巨大的国际意义。匈牙利各报均全文刊载了苏联的保卫和平法案。《匈牙利民族报》强调指出：“苏联通过保卫和平法案已加强了全世界进步人类为保卫世界和平而进行的斗争。”保加利亚各报发表了许多工人、科学家、演员、艺术家的评论。保加利亚科学院院士、世界和平理事会理事波波夫在《工人事业报》撰文称：“苏联人民的这一决定将使世界保卫和平运动及世界和平理事会的决议更有力量。”保加利亚全国保卫和平委员会秘书长尼诺瓦说：“苏联最高苏维埃通过这一法案，就是对联合国内美英侵略集团的一个致命的道义上的打击。”
    英国工人日报在评论苏联保卫和平法案时，将苏联及人民民主国家的情况和美、英等资本主义国家的统治阶级向人民进行战争宣传的情况作了对比。工人日报强调称：“只有那些禁止战争宣传和支持五大国缔结和平公约的国家才是真正的和平拥护者。”意大利首都罗马的民主报纸也以巨大的篇幅刊载了苏联通过保卫和平法案的消息。
