Title: My Ambassador to India Holds Reception to Introduce Chinese Sports Delegation and Host Athletes from Asian Games

Author:

Date: 1951-03-21
Page: 1

　　My Ambassador to India Holds Reception to Introduce Chinese Sports Delegation and Host Athletes from Asian Games
    Xinhua News Agency, New Delhi, 20th—News from New Delhi: Our Ambassador to India, General Yuan Zhongxian, held a cocktail party on the evening of the 11th to introduce the Chinese sports delegation visiting the Asian Games and to host athletes from various countries participating in the Asian Games. Among those invited were Indian Prime Minister Nehru, Soviet Ambassador to India Novikov, representatives from diplomatic missions of several countries to India, and athletes from India, Burma, Indonesia, Nepal, and other countries, totaling 400 people.
    The banquet was held on the lawn of the embassy garden. The venue was decorated with a huge banner composed of lights: "Asian youth unite to defend peace in Asia and the world!" Young people from various Asian countries joyfully raised their glasses in celebration. Indian athletes were particularly fond of commemorative badges of Chairman Mao Zedong, the leader of the Chinese people. Indonesian athletes requested members of the Chinese sports delegation to sign their autograph books. Nepalese athletes expressed their admiration and envy for Beijing, the capital of the Chinese people, and hoped to have the opportunity to visit.
    The final program of the banquet was the screening of the Chinese film "Liberated China." When Chairman Mao appeared on the screen, the entire audience burst into warm applause.
    Note: The first Asian Games were held in New Delhi, India, from March 4th to 11th. Participants included India, Burma, Indonesia, Pakistan, Ceylon, Afghanistan, Nepal, Singapore, Japan, the Philippines, and Thailand. The Preparatory Committee of the All-China Sports Federation received an invitation to the Asian Games and sent a sports delegation to visit. The sports delegation consisted of eight people, led by Wu Xueqian, and arrived in New Delhi on the 7th, where they were warmly welcomed by all parties involved in the Games.

### 我驻印度大使举行酒会  介绍我体育观光团并招待亚洲运动会各国选手

1951-03-21
第1版()
专栏：

　　我驻印度大使举行酒会
    介绍我体育观光团并招待亚洲运动会各国选手
    【新华社新德里二十日电】新德里消息：我国驻印度大使袁仲贤将军十一日晚举行鸡尾酒会，介绍我国参观亚洲运动会的体育观光团，并招待参加亚洲运动会的各国选手。应邀出席的，有印度总理尼赫鲁、苏联驻印大使诺维科夫、若干国家驻印外交使团代表及印度、缅甸、印尼、尼泊尔等国的体育选手共四百人。
    宴会在大使馆花园草地上举行。会场上饰有灯光构成的巨幅标语：“亚洲青年团结起来，保卫亚洲和世界的和平！”来自亚洲的各国青年们尽情地举杯联欢。印度的选手们特别喜欢中国人民领袖毛泽东主席的纪念章。印尼选手要求我国体育观光团团员在纪念册上签名。尼泊尔的选手对中国人民首都北京表示景仰和羡慕，并希望有机会去游览。
    宴会的最后节目是放演中国影片《解放了的中国》。当毛主席在银幕上出现时，全场响起热烈的掌声。
    按：第一届亚洲运动会已于三月四日至十一日在印度新德里举行。参加的有印度、缅甸、印尼、巴基斯坦、锡兰、阿富汗、尼泊尔、新加坡、日本、菲律宾和泰国等。中华全国体育总会筹备会曾接到亚洲运动会的邀请，并派遣了体育观光团前往参观。体育观光团一行八人，由团长吴学谦率领，于七日到达新德里，受到大会各方面的热烈欢迎。
