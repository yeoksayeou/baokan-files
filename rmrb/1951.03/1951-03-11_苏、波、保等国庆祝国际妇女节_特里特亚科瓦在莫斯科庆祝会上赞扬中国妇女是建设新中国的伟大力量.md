Title: Soviet Union, Poland, Bulgaria and Other Countries Celebrate International Women's Day; Tret'yakova Praises Chinese Women at Moscow Celebration as a Great Force in Building the New China
Author:
Date: 1951-03-11
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, March 9th: According to TASS, Moscow: International Women's Day on March 8th was enthusiastically celebrated throughout the Soviet Union. Celebrations were held on the 8th in Moscow, Leningrad, Kiev, Minsk, Tbilisi, and other cities in the Soviet Union.

　　The Moscow celebration was held at the Bolshoi Theatre in Moscow, attended by Stakhanovite workers, engineers, scientists, teachers, doctors, artists from Moscow enterprises, and advanced collective farm women from various districts of the Moscow region. Tret'yakova, Secretary of the Moscow Regional Committee of the Communist Party of the Soviet Union, delivered a report at the meeting, outlining the remarkable achievements of Soviet women in all aspects of political, economic, and cultural life. When talking about women in people's democratic countries, Tret'yakova emphasized that the heroism shown by Chinese women in the liberation struggle against the Kuomintang regime and the shackles of foreign capital is well-known throughout the world, and now they are a great force in the construction of the new China. The patriotism and bravery of Korean women have won the admiration of all progressive mankind. Tret'yakova continued to discuss the role of women in the powerful movement for peace and democracy on a world scale, and emphasized that women in all countries cannot and do not want to stand outside the common cause of all progressive mankind. They increasingly understand that without peace, their families and their children will not have happiness. Tret'yakova concluded by pointing out that Soviet women are at the forefront of the international democratic women's movement for peace, and cheered for the great Soviet Union, for the leader of all working people, the great standard-bearer of peace, Stalin.

　　Xinhua News Agency, March 9th: According to TASS, Poland, Bulgaria, and Romania enthusiastically celebrated International Women's Day on March 8th. The Central Committee of the Polish United Workers' Party issued a proclamation on the 8th congratulating women, stating: "Millions of women of different countries, races, and beliefs are inspired by the common desire to defend peace and thwart the plans of warmongers, and remember in their hearts Stalin's famous saying: 'If the people of all countries take on the cause of maintaining peace and defend this cause to the end, peace can be maintained and consolidated.'" Polish women are the masters of their motherland and active builders of a new life. There are now 13,305 women representatives on local People's Committees, and 300 women serve as judges and prosecutors. Many women hold important positions in state organs, parliament, and the government. The proclamation concluded by urging all Polish women to actively participate in the work of Poland's Six-Year Plan, because this plan is a great contribution of the Polish people to the defense of peace.

　　The Central Committee of the Bulgarian Communist Party issued a proclamation on the 8th stating: "Bulgaria now has 37 women representatives in the National Assembly, more than 5,000 women are jurors and members of local People's Committees, and 42 women have received the Dimitrov Prize. Bulgarian women should stand at the forefront of the work of defending peace and building socialism, carry out large-scale work to publicize the resolutions of the first meeting of the World Peace Council, and sign the declaration requesting the five major powers to conclude a peace pact."

　　Women in various parts of Romania held celebrations on the 8th. The Romanian Democratic Women's Union, which has united more than 2 million working women, organized many report meetings and lectures. Romania now has 100,000 women who are members of local Peace Defense Committees. In the past three years, 1.5 million illiterate women have learned to read and write.

### 苏、波、保等国庆祝国际妇女节  特里特亚科瓦在莫斯科庆祝会上赞扬中国妇女是建设新中国的伟大力量

1951-03-11
第4版()
专栏：

　　苏、波、保等国庆祝国际妇女节
    特里特亚科瓦在莫斯科庆祝会上赞扬中国妇女是建设新中国的伟大力量
    【新华社九日讯】据塔斯社莫斯科讯：苏联各地热烈庆祝三八国际妇女节。莫斯科、列宁格勒、基辅、明斯克、第比利斯及苏联其他各城市都在八日举行了庆祝会。
    莫斯科的庆祝会在莫斯科大剧院举行，出席者有莫斯科各企业中的斯塔哈诺夫式工作者、工程师、科学家、教师、医生、艺术工作者和莫斯科州各区先进的集体农妇。苏联共产党莫斯科州委会书记特里特亚科瓦在会上作了报告，陈述了苏联妇女在政治、经济和文化生活各方面的显著成就。特里特亚科瓦在谈到人民民主国家的妇女时，强调指出：中国妇女在反对国民党政权和外国资本桎梏的解放斗争中所表现的英雄主义是举世皆知的，现在她们是新中国建设中的伟大力量。朝鲜妇女的爱国主义和勇敢已赢得全体进步人类的敬佩。特里特亚科瓦继论述妇女在世界规模的争取和平与民主的强大运动中所起的作用，并着重指出一切国家的妇女都不能也不希望站在全体进步人类的共同事业之外。她们愈益了解到没有和平，她们的家庭和她们的孩子就不会有幸福。特里特亚科瓦结语指出苏联妇女走在国际民主妇女争取和平的运动的最前列，并为伟大的苏联、为一切劳动人民的领袖、伟大的和平旗手斯大林欢呼。
    【新华社九日讯】据塔斯社消息：波、保、罗三国热烈庆祝三八国际妇女节。波兰统一工人党中央委员会于八日发表文告向妇女祝贺称：不同国家、不同种族、不同信仰的千万妇女被保卫和平、挫败战争贩子计划的一致愿望鼓舞着，心里铭记着斯大林的名言：“如果各国人民将维护和平的事业担当起来，并且把这一事业保卫到底，和平就能够保持和巩固。”波兰妇女是祖国的主人和新生活的积极建设者，现在已有一万三千三百零五名妇女是各地人民委员会的代表，有三百名妇女担任法官和检察官，许多妇女在国家机关、议会和政府中担任重要职务。文告最后敦促波兰全体妇女踊跃参加波兰六年计划的工作，因为这个计划是波兰人民对保卫和平的一大贡献。
    保加利亚共产党中央委员会八日发表文告称：保加利亚现在有三十七名妇女是国民议会的代表，五千余名妇女是陪审员和各地人民委员会的委员，有四十二名妇女得到季米特洛夫奖金。保加利亚妇女应站在保卫和平与建设社会主义工作的最前列，进行大规模工作宣传世界和平理事会第一次会议的决议，在要求五大国缔结和平公约的宣言上签名。
    罗马尼亚各地妇女在八日纷纷举行了庆祝会。已团结了二百多万劳动妇女的罗马尼亚民主妇女联合会组织了许多报告会和演讲会。罗马尼亚现在已有十万名妇女是各地保卫和平委员会的委员。过去三年中有一百五十万文盲的妇女学会了读和写。
