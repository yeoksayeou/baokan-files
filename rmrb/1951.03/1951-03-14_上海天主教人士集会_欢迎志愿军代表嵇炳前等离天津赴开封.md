Title: Shanghai Catholics Gather to Welcome Volunteer Army Representative Ji Bingqian and Others Leaving Tianjin for Kaifeng
Author:
Date: 1951-03-14
Page: 1
　　
　　Xinhua News Agency, Shanghai, 12th. Shanghai's Catholic community held a grand gathering on the 11th to oppose the United States rearming Japan and welcome representatives of the returning Chinese People's Volunteers. Over 1,500 Catholics attended. Zhu Kongjia, Chairman of the Shanghai Catholic Action Committee, addressed the meeting, expressing respect on behalf of the city's Catholics to the Chinese People's Volunteers who bravely fought against the American aggressors. He said, "Shanghai's Catholics have stood together with the vast number of people nationwide and launched an anti-American patriotic movement." Chai Chuanruo, a representative of the returning Chinese People's Volunteers, reported to the assembly on the heroic deeds and great victories of the Chinese and Korean soldiers, which was warmly welcomed by the attending Catholics. Catholics from twenty-two units, including Aurora University and Guangci Hospital, presented banners and flowers to the Volunteer Army representatives.
    The assembly listened to a report by Lan Xuzhang, Director of Guangci Hospital, on the resolutions of the "Shanghai People from All Walks of Life Opposing the United States Arming Japan Conference," and passed the "Ten Patriotic Pledges for Shanghai Catholics." The assembly concluded by passing a telegram of respect to Chairman Mao and a telegram of respect to the Chinese People's Volunteers and the Korean People's Army.

　　Xinhua News Agency, Tianjin, 13th. Chinese People's Volunteers returning representatives Ji Bingqian, Li Weiying, and Zhang Fu left Tianjin for Kaifeng on the evening of the 11th and will proceed to Xi'an, Lanzhou, Chengdu, Kunming, and other places to report on the heroic battles of the Volunteer Army in Korea against the American aggressors.
    Ji Bingqian and others stayed in Tianjin for nine days, giving more than twenty reports to people from all walks of life, with an audience of over 100,400 people. To express their respect for the Chinese People's Volunteers, the people of Tianjin presented gifts to Ji Bingqian and others. They received a total of twenty-eight banners, over three hundred letters of sympathy, and over twenty silk autograph books signed by the city's silk workers. When Ji Bingqian gave a report on behalf of the Tianjin State-owned Cotton Textile Factory No. 2, over a hundred workers gave him their model medals won in the patriotic production competition. Zhang Dexin, Deputy Engineer of the Tianjin Civil Aviation Bureau, gave his beloved radio to the Volunteer Army returning representatives, writing on it the seven characters "The Voice of the Motherland's People." He hoped that the Volunteer Army soldiers at the front could often hear news of the Chinese people's resistance against America and aid to Korea.

### 上海天主教人士集会  欢迎志愿军代表嵇炳前等离天津赴开封

1951-03-14
第1版()
专栏：

　　上海天主教人士集会
    欢迎志愿军代表嵇炳前等离天津赴开封
    【新华社上海十二日电】上海市天主教人士于十一日举行反对美国重新武装日本、欢迎中国人民志愿军回国代表的盛大集会，到会天主教徒有一千五百多人。大会主席天主教公教进行会理事长朱孔嘉在致词中代表全市教友向英勇打击美国侵略者的中国人民志愿军致敬。他说：“上海市天主教人士已与全国广大人民站在一起，展开了反美爱国运动”。中国人民志愿军回国代表柴川若，向大会报告了中朝战士的英勇事迹和伟大胜利，受到了与会教友的热烈欢迎。震旦大学、广慈医院等二十二个单位的教友，都向志愿军代表献了锦旗和鲜花。
    大会听取了广慈医院院长蓝绪彰关于“上海各界人民反对美国武装日本会议”的决议的传达报告，并通过了“上海天主教人士十项爱国公约”。大会最后通过了向毛主席致敬电、向中国人民志愿军和朝鲜人民军致敬电。
    【新华社天津十三日电】我国人民志愿军归国代表嵇炳前、李维英、张甫三人于十一日晚离天津赴开封，并将转赴西安、兰州、成都、昆明等地报告志愿军在朝鲜打击美国侵略军的英勇战迹。
    嵇炳前等在天津市逗留九日，共对各界人民作了二十多次报告，听众达十万零四千余人。天津市人民为表示对我国人民志愿军的崇敬，纷纷向嵇炳前等献送礼物。他们共收到天津市各界人民所送的锦旗二十八面、慰问信三百余封和全市纺绸工人亲笔签名的绸制签名书二十余本。嵇炳前代表在天津国营棉纺二厂作报告时，当场就有百余工人，把自己在爱国主义生产竞赛中获得的模范奖章赠给他。津市民航局副工程师张德馨将自己心爱的收音机送给志愿军归国代表，并在上面写着“祖国人民的声音”七个字。他希望志愿军战士们在前线能够经常听到祖国人民抗美援朝的消息。
