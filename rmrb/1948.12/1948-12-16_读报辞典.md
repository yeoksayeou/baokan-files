　　Reading the Newspaper Dictionary
    Soviet Republic
    ——Member states that voluntarily join the Union of Soviet Socialist Republics, unanimously follow the instructions of the Supreme Soviet externally, and are equal among the republics, each with its own state power organs. The Soviet Union has sixteen member republics: the Russian Soviet Federative Socialist Republic, the Ukrainian Soviet Socialist Republic, the Byelorussian Soviet Socialist Republic, the Azerbaijan Soviet Socialist Republic, the Georgian Soviet Socialist Republic, the Armenian Soviet Socialist Republic, the Turkmen Soviet Socialist Republic, the Uzbek Soviet Socialist Republic, the Tajik Soviet Socialist Republic, the Kazakh Soviet Socialist Republic, the Kyrgyz Soviet Socialist Republic, the Karelo-Finnish Soviet Socialist Republic, the Moldavian Soviet Socialist Republic, the Lithuanian Soviet Socialist Republic, the Estonian Soviet Socialist Republic, and the Latvian Soviet Socialist Republic.
    Siberia
    ——Located in the eastern and central parts of the Asian portion of the Soviet Union, it is a vast plain. It covers an area of approximately 12.5 million square kilometers with a population of about 10 million. It is part of the Russian Soviet Socialist Republic. Its southern border adjoins our Northeast provinces and the Mongolian People's Republic. During the Tsarist era, its culture was extremely backward, but since the October Revolution, under the leadership of the All-Union Communist Party (Bolsheviks), vigorous construction has been carried out, and industry, agriculture, culture, and education have all flourished. The barren and infertile land of the past has now become prosperous.
    Monopoly Capital
    ——A product of the development of capitalism to the stage of imperialism. The concentration of capital in a country in the hands of a few large capitalists means combining the capital of many capitalists and investing it in the same enterprise. This makes it impossible for small and medium capitalists to compete with them, and they are annexed by various methods. As a result, domestic capital becomes even more concentrated in the hands of these large capitalists. At this time, they further manipulate the market, monopolize prices, and control the entire country's economy and people's lives. Such large enterprises are the organizational form of monopoly capital. Examples include the Standard Oil Company of America and the automobile magnates.
    Financial Oligarchy
    ——Finance is the general term for the circulation, exchange, remittance, savings, and lending of money. Banks and money houses operate this business. In capitalist countries, the vast majority of industries are controlled by a few bankers and financial groups. The state's revenue and expenditure must be subject to their control, so that all measures of the state's politics, military affairs, and diplomacy must also follow their opinions. These few people are the financial oligarchy. Examples include the four families of Chiang, Soong, Kung, and Chen in China, Morgan in the United States, and Mitsui and Mitsubishi in Japan.

专栏：读报辞典

　　读报辞典
    苏联加盟共和国
    ——按自愿联合原则参加苏维埃社会主义共和国联盟的盟员国，对外一致遵循最高苏维埃的指示，各加盟共和国间是平权的，各有其自己的国家政权机关。苏联共有十六个加盟共和国：俄罗斯苏维埃联邦社会主义共和国、乌克兰苏维埃社会主义共和国、白俄罗斯苏维埃社会主义共和国、阿塞拜疆苏维埃社会主义共和国、佐治亚苏维埃社会主义共和国、亚美尼亚苏维埃社会主义共和国、土库曼苏维埃社会主义共和国、乌兹别克苏维埃社会主义共和国、达吉克苏维埃社会主义共和国、哈萨克苏维埃社会主义共和国、吉尔吉斯苏维埃社会主义共和国、卡罗利亚芬兰苏维埃社会主义共和国、摩尔达维亚苏维埃社会主义共和国、立陶宛苏维埃社会主义共和国、爱莎尼亚苏维埃社会主义共和国及拉脱维亚苏维埃社会主义共和国。
    西伯利亚
    ——在苏联亚洲部分的东部及中部，为一广阔大平原。面积约一千二百五十万方公里，人口约一千万。是俄罗斯苏维埃社会主义共和国的一部分。其南面与我东北各省及外蒙古共和国接壤。帝俄时代这里文化极落后，但自十月革命后，在联共（布）领导下，大力进行建设，工农业及文化教育事业，无不蒸蒸日上，过去荒芜不毛之地，今已变成繁荣。
    独占资本
    ——是资本主义发展到帝国主义时期的产物。一个国家内的资本集中在少数大资本家手里，即是，把许多资本家的资本结合起来，投入同一企业，这样使中小资本竞争不过他，并用各种方法将他们吞并，于是国内的资本就更集中到这些大资本家手里了。这时他们就更操纵了市场，垄断价格，掌握整个国家的经济和人民生活。这样的大企业就是独占资本的组织形式。如美国的美孚煤油公司、汽车大王等都是。
    财政金融寡头
    ——金融是钱的流通、交换、汇兑、储蓄、借贷等关系的总称。银行、钱庄就是经营这种事业的。资本主义国家内绝大部分产业操纵在少数银行家、财团的手里，国家的收支要听他们的支配，以致国家的政治、军事、外交等一切措施，也得遵循他们的意见。这少数人就是财政金融寡头。如中国的蒋宋孔陈四大家族，美国的摩尔根，日本的三井、三菱等财团。
