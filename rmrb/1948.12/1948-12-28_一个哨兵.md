Title: A Sentry
Author: Xing Xing
Date: 1948-12-28
Page: 4

　　A Sentry

　　Gusty winds sweep the fallen leaves,
　　The bright moon shines on East Mountain;
　　The towering concrete pillboxes,
　　Their necks shortened,
　　Tremble in the artillery fire.
　　The lights on the city walls of Taiyuan,
　　Cast a pathetic, scornful glare!
  　×　　　×　　　×
　　The lonely sentry before the pillbox,
　　Thinking of the past,
　　Looking at the present,
　　This mute who can speak,
　　Is longing for his lovely home.
　　——Suddenly,
　　A shout from the opposite hillside:
　　"Come over, we won't kill you, and you'll be treated well,
　　The policies of the People's Liberation Army are generous and broad!"
　　He—the mute who can speak,
　　Steeled himself and looked around,
　　Like the wind, he bypassed the outer moat,
　　And escaped the danger zone;
　　Carrying a 7.9 rifle,
　　And more than thirty rounds of American-made bullets.
　　He shrugged his shoulders,
　　And lightly wiped the sweat from his face.
  　×　　　×　　　×
　　Ten minutes later,
　　A quarter of an hour earlier,
　　Warm, cold,
　　Heaven and hell, completely different.
　　He smiled,
　　Then couldn't stop wiping away the tears;
　　——"My name is Tian Heshun,
　　My hometown is in Meng County,
　　I joined the militia to overthrow the landlords,
　　The poor turned over and my heart was filled with joy;
　　I never thought the Yan bandits would come on the very first day,
　　And kidnap me—
　　To guard the pillboxes in the People's Guard.
　　The pillbox is like sitting in jail,
　　One day feels like ten years!"
　　×　　×　　×
　　——Endless grievances,
　　Countless injustices:
　　Three liang of coarse millet per meal,
　　Even the elm leaves in the garrison area are all eaten.
　　The officers eat rice and noodles,
　　The soldiers eat see-through gruel.
　　The officers call it "camouflage,"
　　And beat you half to death—
　　Not being buried alive is a great favor!
　　………
　　Hoping day and night on the pillbox,
　　Finally, this day has come!
　　Gritting his teeth, he brandished the 7.9 rifle,
　　I swear not to return home without avenging this hatred!
　　×　　　×　　　×
　　The gusty wind continues to sweep the fallen leaves,
　　The bright moon still shines on East Mountain,
　　The towering concrete pillboxes,
　　Shrink smaller and smaller,
　　Collapsing in the storm of explosives;
　　The ancient Taiyuan night market,
　　Reveals half of its scorched face!
　　　　　　　　　　　(Sent from Shandong, Taiyuan)

### 一个哨兵
行型
1948-12-28
第4版()
专栏：

　　一个哨兵
    行型
    劲风扫着落叶，
    明月照着东山；
    林立的洋灰碉，
    缩短了脖子，
    在炮火中震战。
    太原城头的灯光，
    凄凉的贬着白眼！
  　×　　　×　　　×
    碉堡前孤独的哨兵，
    想想过去，
    看看眼前，
    这个会说话的哑巴，
    心里正想念着他可爱的家。
    ——忽然，
    对面山坡一阵喊：
    “过来吧，不杀还优待，
    解放军的政策宽又宽！”
    他——会说话的哑巴，
    硬起头皮四下看，
    风似的绕过外壕，
    逃出了危险圈；
    托着一支七九枪，
    还有三十余发美造弹。
    他耸耸肩膀，
    轻快地擦着脸上的汗。
  　×　　　×　　　×
    十分钟后，
    一刻钟前，
    温暖、冷酷，
    天堂地狱不一般。
    他笑了一笑，
    随即眼泪擦不干；
    ——“我叫田和顺，
    老家在孟县，
    参加民兵闹翻身，
    穷人翻身喜心田；
    不想阎匪来到第一天，
    把我绑架去——
    民卫军里把碉看。
    碉堡象坐监牢狱，
    一天顶十年！”
    ×　　×　　×
    ——倒不完苦水，
    诉不尽的冤：
    每顿三两粗小米，
    驻地榆叶都吃完。
    当官的吃的米和面，
    士兵们吃的照影饭。
    当官的说声是“伪装”，
    打你个死去又活来——
    不被活埋是高看！
    ………
    碉堡上日夜盼，
    果然还能有今天！
    咬牙晃晃七九枪，
    不报此仇誓不回家园！
    ×　　　×　　　×
    劲风继续扫着落叶，
    明月仍然照着东山，
    林立的洋灰碉，
    越缩越小，
    在炸药的风暴里崩散；
    古太原的夜市，
    露着半边焦黄脸！
  　　　　　　　　　　　（寄自太原山东）
