Title: Sino-Indian Trade to Double in the Next Three Years
Author:
Date: 1960-10-28
Page: 5
Xinhua News Agency, New Delhi, 25th -- TASS reported from New Delhi: The Soviet Union and India signed a communique in New Delhi on the 24th on developing trade relations between the two countries in the next three years.

The communique stated that trade between the Soviet Union and India has greatly expanded, and the volume of goods mutually supplied between the two countries will double in the next three years.

During this period, the Soviet Union will provide India with the equipment, petroleum products, non-ferrous metals, and many other commodities necessary for developing India's heavy industry during the Third Five-Year Plan.

India will export coffee, spices, shellac, leather and leather shoes,麻制品（hemp products), cotton textiles, and wool textiles to the Soviet Union, as well as other commodities traditionally exported by India.

### 苏印贸易今后三年中将增一倍

1960-10-28
第5版()
专栏：

    苏印贸易今后三年中将增一倍
    新华社25日讯　据塔斯社新德里讯：苏联和印度24日在新德里签订了一项在今后三年中发展两国贸易关系的公报。
    公报说，苏印之间的贸易已经大大扩大，在今后三年中，两国之间互相提供的商品额将增加一倍。
    在这个期间内，苏联将向印度提供在第三个五年计划期间发展印度重工业所必需的设备、石油产品、有色金属和许多其他商品。
    印度将向苏联出口咖啡、调味品、干漆、皮革和皮鞋、麻制品、棉织品和毛织品，以及印度传统出口的其他商品。
