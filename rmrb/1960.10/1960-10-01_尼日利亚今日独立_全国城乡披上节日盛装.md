Title: Nigeria Gains Independence Today; National Urban and Rural Areas Adorn Festive Attire
Author:
Date: 1960-10-01
Page: 7

Xinhua News Agency, Lagos, 29th Dispatch: According to TASS reports from Lagos, Nigeria, Britain's largest remaining colony, located in West Africa, will declare its independence on October 1st. This is the result of the heroic struggle of the people of this country.

Nigeria's capital, Lagos, as well as other cities and villages, have been adorned with festive decorations. A triumphal arch has been built in Lagos, inscribed with "1960, Nigerian Independence" and "Freedom and Independence."

The city streets have been decorated with national flags and lanterns. Many people are strolling in the squares.

Local newspapers and radio stations are publishing articles and broadcasting special programs for the independence holiday every day. More than sixty foreign delegations and over two hundred foreign journalists have arrived in Lagos from all corners of the world.

Various Nigerian political parties and organizations have successively issued statements warmly congratulating the Nigerian people on their great festival and calling on the people to continue the struggle to consolidate national unity and independence.

### 尼日利亚今日独立  全国城乡披上节日盛装

1960-10-01
第7版()
专栏：

    尼日利亚今日独立
    全国城乡披上节日盛装
    新华社29日讯　据塔斯社拉各斯消息，英国目前最大殖民地，位于非洲西部的尼日利亚将在10月1日宣布独立。这是这个国家的人民英勇斗争的结果。
    尼日利亚首都拉各斯以及其他城市和乡村都已披上了节日的盛装。在拉各斯建成了一座凯旋门，上面写着“一九六零年，尼日利亚独立”、“自由和独立”。
    城市的街道已用国旗、花灯装饰起来。很多人在广场上漫步。
    当地报纸和电台每天都为独立节日发表文章和播送特别节目。已经有六十多个外国代表团和二百多名外国记者从世界各个角落来到拉各斯。
    尼日利亚各政党、团体先后发表声明，热烈祝贺尼日利亚人民的伟大节日，并号召人民继续斗争，以巩固国家的统一和独立。
