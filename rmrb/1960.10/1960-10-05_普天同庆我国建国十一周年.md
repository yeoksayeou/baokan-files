Title: Celebrating the 11th Anniversary of the Founding of Our Great Nation
Author:
Date: 1960-10-05
Page: 6

Celebrating the 11th Anniversary of the Founding of Our Great Nation

    Soviet Union
    A celebration was held at Moscow University on the evening of October 1st. The celebration was presided over by Academician Petrovsky, President of Moscow University. At the beginning of the meeting, he said that all faculty, students, and staff of Moscow University celebrate the 11th anniversary of the founding of the People's Republic of China with the Chinese people. He extended festive greetings to the Chinese students on behalf of all faculty, students, and staff of Moscow University, and through them, to the Chinese people.
    Zhang Xiongfei, Counselor of the Chinese Embassy in the Soviet Union, attended the evening and spoke at the meeting. Liang Zijun, a representative of Chinese students, also spoke at the meeting.
    According to TASS, a celebration was also held in a club in Dubna, a town near Moscow where the Joint Institute for Nuclear Research is located.
    The working people of Belarus also widely celebrated the 11th anniversary of the founding of the People's Republic of China. Many enterprises, construction sites, agencies, and schools in Belarus held rallies and evening parties, or organized lectures and report meetings to celebrate this festival.
    A celebration was also held in Tbilisi, the capital of the Georgian Republic, on the evening of October 1st.
      Albania
    To celebrate the 11th anniversary of the founding of the People's Republic of China, the Albanian-Chinese Friendship Association held various celebrations in Tirana, Vlora, and Elbasan.
    Under the auspices of the Albanian-Chinese Friendship Association, the Tirana Light Industry United Factory held a celebration on September 29th. Minai Shutriqi, a member of the journalist delegation that had just visited China, gave a report on his impressions of China, introducing China's continuous Great Leap Forward, water conservancy, and the policy of "walking on two legs."
    German Democratic Republic
    On the occasion of the 11th anniversary of the founding of the People's Republic of China, the Chinese Embassy in the German Democratic Republic and other stationed units received warm congratulations from the people of the German Democratic Republic.
    The lobby of the embassy was filled with congratulatory flowers from all walks of life. Many congratulatory letters and telegrams used enthusiastic words to congratulate our country on its rapid development in socialist construction and expressed the hope that the friendly unity between the two brotherly countries of China and Germany would be further consolidated and developed. The secretary of the district committee of the Socialist Unity Party of Germany in Lichtenberg District, where the Chinese Embassy is located, personally came to the embassy to congratulate the festival.
      Poland
    To celebrate the 11th anniversary of the founding of the People's Republic of China, the Polish-Chinese Friendship Association Club held an evening meeting on the evening of October 2nd for activists of the Polish-Chinese Friendship Association to meet with staff of the Chinese Embassy in Poland and Chinese students studying in Poland.
    Burkin, Secretary-General of the Presidium of the Polish-Chinese Friendship Association, Golanski, member of the Presidium of the Polish-Chinese Friendship Association and Minister of Higher Education, and Wang Bingnan, Chinese Ambassador to Poland, attended the meeting.
      Hungary
    The Pushkin Cinema in Budapest, the capital of Hungary, held a film reception on the evening of October 1st, showing the Chinese film "Thousands of Rivers and Mountains." This film reception was jointly organized by the Hungarian Ministry of Culture, the Bureau of Foreign Cultural Relations, the Presidium of the Hungarian-Chinese Friendship Association, and the Executive Committee of the Budapest City Council.
      Bulgaria
    The four cinemas in Sofia, the capital of Bulgaria, "Liberation," "Student House," "Teacher's Cinema" and "Ha. Dimitar" respectively screened Chinese feature films "The Storm," "Song of Youth" and "The Story of an Old Soldier," to celebrate our National Day.
      Japan
    According to a survey by the Japanese National Council for the Restoration of Japan-China Diplomatic Relations, the Japanese people held 150 rallies across the country to celebrate the 11th anniversary of the founding of the People's Republic of China. These rallies strongly demanded the restoration of Japan-China diplomatic relations.
    In Tokyo, representatives from all walks of life, about 700 people, held a central meeting to strive for the restoration of Japan-China diplomatic relations at the Kudan Kaikan on the evening of October 3rd to celebrate China's National Day. The meeting was held under the auspices of the National Council for the Restoration of Japan-China Diplomatic Relations.
    Kazami Akira, Chairman of the Board of Directors of the National Council for the Restoration of Japan-China Diplomatic Relations, Toki Tsuyoshi, Alternate Member of the Central Committee of the Communist Party, Tanaka Toshio, Member of the Central Executive Committee of the Socialist Party, Kaneda Tomitaro, Vice Chairman of the General Council of Trade Unions of Japan, Mishima Kazuo, Vice Chairman of the Japan-China Friendship Association, Otani Eijun, Chairman of the Committee for the Consolation of Chinese Martyrs in Japan, Kawazaki Natsu, Chairman of the Japan Mothers' Congress Liaison Committee, and Yadoriya Eiichi, Vice Chairman of the Japan International Trade Promotion Association, all spoke at the meeting.
    More than 1,000 Chinese residing in Tokyo, Japan, held a conference at the Toyoshima Public Hall on October 1st to warmly celebrate the 11th anniversary of the founding of the People's Republic of China. The conference was held by the Tokyo Overseas Chinese Association.
    The Tokyo Overseas Chinese Association also held a grand celebration reception on the evening of September 30th to celebrate the 11th anniversary of the founding of the People's Republic of China.
      Ceylon
    People from all walks of life in Ceylon flocked to the Chinese Embassy in Ceylon on October 1st to congratulate Ambassador Zhang Canming and his wife on the 11th anniversary of the founding of the People's Republic of China and expressed their best wishes. S.A. Wickremasinghe, Chairman of the Ceylon Communist Party, members of the Ceylon-China Friendship Association, representatives of trade unions and youth organizations, and other people had come to the Chinese Embassy to congratulate. Diplomats from socialist countries' embassies in Ceylon and overseas Chinese in Ceylon also came to the embassy to congratulate.
      Afghanistan
    The Chinese painting exhibition held by the Chinese Embassy to celebrate the 11th anniversary of the founding of the People's Republic of China closed on October 1st. Since the opening of the exhibition on September 26, a large number of intellectuals and other people in the Afghan capital have come to visit every day.
      Iraq
    The Iraqi-Chinese Friendship Association held a celebration on September 30 to celebrate the 11th anniversary of the founding of the People's Republic of China.
    Zhang Weilie, Chinese Ambassador to Iraq, and embassy staff were invited to attend the celebration.
    Abud Zarzara, Chairman of the Iraqi-Chinese Friendship Association, emphasized in his speech at the celebration that the liberation of China eleven years ago was a major event. Ambassador Zhang Weilie said in his speech that in the past two years, the traditional friendship between the people of China and Iraq has achieved new development on the basis of the Five Principles of Peaceful Coexistence and the Bandung Spirit. He emphasized that the Chinese people and the Iraqi people, who are struggling to safeguard national independence, oppose imperialism and build the country, should support each other.
      Cuba
    More than 3,000 people from all walks of life in Cuba and overseas Chinese held a rally in Havana's Chinatown on October 2 to celebrate the 11th anniversary of the founding of the People's Republic of China.
    After the military band played the national anthems of the two countries, Lv Gezi, Chairman of the Chinese New Democratic League, announced the opening of the conference.
    Zou Siyi, Deputy Head of the Chinese Trade Delegation, spoke at the meeting.
    Carlos Luis Fallas, a famous Costa Rican writer, spoke at the meeting on behalf of the Costa Rican people and the Costa Rican People's Vanguard Party to express his congratulations to the Chinese people. He said that the glorious example of the Chinese people inspires the struggle of the Costa Rican people.
    According to the Cuban "Maestra Mountain Daily," people from all walks of life in Santiago, Cuba, held a grand rally on October 1 to celebrate the 11th anniversary of the founding of the People's Republic of China.
      Chile
    A public gathering to celebrate the 11th anniversary of the founding of the People's Republic of China was held in the auditorium of the University of Chile on the afternoon of October 1. This gathering brought the series of activities held by the Chilean-Chinese Cultural Association to celebrate China's National Day to a climax.
    The meeting began with the national anthems of Chile and China. Juan Campos, Chairman of the Chilean-Chinese Cultural Association and Radical Party Congressman, Olga Poblete, Secretary-General of the Chilean National Peace Committee, a famous university professor, and Juan GÃ³mez Millas, President of the University of Chile, all spoke at the meeting. Wu Shoulian, a Chinese female student studying at the Faculty of Education of the University of Chile, also spoke at the meeting.
    On the evening of September 30, a seminar was held at the office of the Chilean-Chinese Cultural Association. Genaro Medina Vera, First Vice President of the Chilean Journalists Association and President of "See" magazine, spoke about his impressions of his visit to China last year.
      Peru
    The celebration week of the 11th anniversary of the founding of the People's Republic of China, organized by the Peruvian-Chinese Friendship Association, closed at the Jose Carlos Mariategui College. During this event, entitled "Homage to China Week," some prestigious cultural figures held report meetings on the Chinese revolution and socialist construction.
    Activities celebrating China's National Day were also held at the University of Cusco, the University of Trujillo, and the University of Arequipa.
      Uruguay
    The Chinese photo exhibition held by the Uruguayan-Chinese Cultural Association to celebrate the 11th anniversary of the founding of the People's Republic of China opened in Montevideo on the evening of October 1. The exhibition featured photos reflecting China's industry, urban and rural life, art and other aspects.
    Felipe Novoa, a member of the leadership committee of the Uruguayan-Chinese Cultural Association and a writer, presided over the opening ceremony.
      Finland
    The Finnish-Chinese Association held a Chinese film reception on September 30 to celebrate the 11th anniversary of the founding of the People's Republic of China. Among the 500 guests attending the reception was Eetio, First Deputy Speaker of the Finnish Parliament. Trani, Chairman of the Finnish-Chinese Association, spoke at the meeting.
    The two Chinese films screened, "The Story in the Mural" and "Spring and Autumn on the Water," were warmly welcomed by the audience.
      Sweden
    The Swedish-Chinese Association held an evening party on the evening of September 30 to celebrate the 11th anniversary of the founding of the People's Republic of China. Stan Schuberg, Chairman of the Swedish Postal and Telecommunications Workers' Union, who had led a delegation of Swedish workers to visit China in May this year, spoke at the meeting, praising China's construction achievements. The famous poet Blomberg also enthusiastically recited classical Chinese poetry at the meeting.
      France
    More than 3,000 people from all walks of life in Paris held a grand evening party at the Chaillot Palace to celebrate the 11th anniversary of the founding of the People's Republic of China. The evening party was hosted by the French-Chinese Friendship Association.
    Professor DelÃ©age, President of the French-Chinese Friendship Association, delivered a speech at the meeting.
    Two telegrams were also passed at the meeting: one was a congratulatory telegram congratulating the 11th anniversary of the founding of the People's Republic of China, and the other was a telegram to the President of France requesting recognition of the People's Republic of China.
    Prominent figures from all walks of life in France attended the evening party, including Waldeck Rochet, Member of the Political Bureau and Secretary of the Secretariat of the French Communist Party. Diplomatic envoys from various countries stationed in France also attended the evening party. (Xinhua News Agency)
