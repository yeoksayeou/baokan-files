Title: Tsedenbal Returns Home After Visiting Afghanistan, Ceylon, and India; Polish and Ceylonese Issue Joint Communiqué on Bilateral Relations and International Issues
Author:
Date: 1960-10-06
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency dispatch: Polish Chairman of the Council of Ministers, Józef Cyrankiewicz, returned to Warsaw on the night of the 4th, after visiting Afghanistan, India, and Ceylon.

Cyrankiewicz left Ceylon on the afternoon of October 1st, after a five-day visit that began on September 27th.

A joint communiqué issued in Colombo on October 1st by Ceylonese Prime Minister Mrs. Bandaranaike and Cyrankiewicz stated: They unanimously agreed that the visit of the Polish Chairman of the Council of Ministers and the talks they held would help to further strengthen the friendly relations and cooperation between the two countries, thereby benefiting the peoples of both countries and world peace.

In exploring international issues, the two Prime Ministers emphasized that relations between countries should be based on the principles of peaceful coexistence.

The two Prime Ministers pointed out that decisions on world issues cannot be made without considering the voices of the peoples of Asian and African countries. They expressed their firm belief that the People's Republic of China will eventually regain its legitimate seat in the United Nations.

The communiqué stated, "The two Prime Ministers fully support the people of all countries struggling to liberate themselves from colonial oppression, and they welcome the emergence of many independent countries in Africa."

### 西伦凯维兹访问阿、锡、印后回国  波锡就两国关系和国际问题发表联合公报

1960-10-06
第5版()
专栏：

    西伦凯维兹访问阿、锡、印后回国
    波锡就两国关系和国际问题发表联合公报
    据新华社讯  波兰部长会议主席西伦凯维兹在访问了阿富汗、印度和锡兰以后，于4日夜间回到华沙。
    西伦凯维兹从9月27日开始对锡兰进行了五天的访问以后，在1日下午离开锡兰。
    锡兰总理班达拉奈克夫人和西伦凯维兹1日在科伦坡发表的一项联合公报说：他们一致认为，波兰部长会议主席的这次访问及他们所举行的会谈，将有助于进一步加强两国之间的友好关系和合作，从而有利于两国人民和世界和平。
    两国总理在探讨国际问题时强调各国的关系应建立在和平共处的原则上。
    两国总理指出，不考虑亚非各国人民的呼声，就不能就世界问题作出决定。他们表示深信，中华人民共和国终将恢复自己在联合国的合法席位。
    公报说，“两国总理完全支持为争取从殖民主义压迫下获得解放而斗争的各国人民，他们欢迎在非洲出现许多独立国家。”
