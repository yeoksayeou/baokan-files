Title: UN General Assembly Passes Twenty-Eight Nation Proposal Calling on Member States Not to Aggravate International Tensions
Author:
Date: 1960-10-20
Page: 5

    United Nations, October 17th—At the final meeting of the United Nations General Assembly's general debate on the evening of the 17th, a proposal put forward by twenty-eight countries, including India, calling for "cooperation among member states of the United Nations" was adopted by a vote of ninety-one to zero, with eight abstentions. The full text of the proposal is as follows:
    “The General Assembly,
    “Deeply concerned by the aggravation of world tensions,
    “Considering that the deterioration of international relations constitutes a serious danger to world peace and cooperation,
    “Recognizing the need, both in the General Assembly and throughout the world, to halt this trend in international relations and to contribute to the promotion of harmony among nations, regardless of their political and economic systems,
    “1. Requests all States, in accordance with the Charter of the United Nations, to refrain from actions which might aggravate international tensions;
    “2. Reaffirms the belief that the strength of the United Nations lies in the cooperation of its Members, which should be fully realized in order to make the Organization a more effective instrument for safeguarding peace and promoting economic and social progress for all mankind;
    “3. Further requests that immediate and constructive steps be taken on urgent problems concerning world peace and human progress;
    “4. Calls upon all Member States to make the utmost effort to achieve these ends.”
    The twenty-eight countries participating in this proposal are: Austria, Bolivia, Burma, Ecuador, Finland, Ghana, India, Indonesia, Mexico, Morocco, Panama, Tunisia, the United Arab Republic, Venezuela, Yugoslavia, Cambodia, Ceylon, Ethiopia, Nepal, Nigeria, Sudan, Libya, Lebanon, Saudi Arabia, Yemen, Iraq, Guinea, and Somalia.

### 联大通过二十八国提案  呼吁会员国不要加剧国际紧张局势

1960-10-20
第5版()
专栏：

    联大通过二十八国提案
    呼吁会员国不要加剧国际紧张局势
    本报讯　17日晚上，在联合国大会一般性辩论的最后一次会议上，以九十一票对零票，八票缺席，通过了印度等二十八国提出的呼吁“联合国会员国合作”的提案。提案全文如下：
    “大会，
    “对世界紧张局势的加剧感到深切的不安，
    “考虑到国际关系的恶化将构成对世界和平和合作的严重危险，
    “意识到在大会中以及在全世界都有必要在国际关系方面制止这种趋势，并对增进各国之间的和谐作出贡献，不管这些国家的政治和经济制度有什么不同。
    “1、要求所有国家，根据联合国宪章，不采取可能加剧国际紧张局势的行动；
    “2、重申联合国的力量在于其会员国的合作的信念，这种合作应当充分实现，以便使这个机构成为保障和平和促进全人类经济和社会进步的更加有效的工具；
    “3、进一步要求就有关世界和平和人类进步的迫切问题立即采取建设性的步骤；
    “4、呼吁所有会员国尽最大努力来达到这些目的。”
    参加这个提案的二十八国是：奥地利、玻利维亚、缅甸、厄瓜多尔、芬兰、加纳、印度、印度尼西亚、墨西哥、摩洛哥、巴拿马、突尼斯、阿联、委内瑞拉、南斯拉夫、柬埔寨、锡兰、埃塞俄比亚、尼泊尔、尼日利亚、苏丹、利比亚、黎巴嫩、沙特阿拉伯、也门、伊拉克、几内亚和索马里亚。
