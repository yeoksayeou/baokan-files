Title: President of the Republic of Mali Delivers Speech Reaffirming Mali as an Independent Sovereign State
Author: 
Date: 1960-10-26
Page: 7

Xinhua News Agency, Bamako, October 24th. President Modibo Keita of the Republic of Mali, in a speech delivered in Segou, a major city in Mali, on October 22nd, reaffirmed that Mali is an independent sovereign state. He said that Mali has no political or legal connection with France and enjoys complete sovereignty at home and abroad.

President Keita visited Segou accompanied by the Minister of Interior, Defense, and Security of Mali, Madeira Keita, and delivered a speech at a mass rally there.

President Keita said: Mali is willing to cooperate with all countries in the world without exception, demonstrating its complete independence to France and other countries. He said that today there are guests from Africa, the Americas, Europe, and Asia here. He said, we hope that people will first respect our personality and guarantee our independence, sovereignty, and territorial integrity.

He said that no matter what difficulties people create for us or what bad things they say about us, we will continue to strive to achieve our goals of seeking economic, social, and cultural progress.

### 马里共和国总统发表演说  重申马里是独立的主权国家

1960-10-26
第7版()
专栏：

    马里共和国总统发表演说
    重申马里是独立的主权国家
    新华社巴马科24日电　马里共和国总统莫迪博·凯塔10月22日在马里大城市塞古发表的演说中重申马里是一个独立的主权国家。他说，马里在政治上和法律上同法国没有任何联系，它在国内外享有完全的主权。
    凯塔总统在马里内政兼国防和安全部长马德拉·凯塔的陪同下访问了塞古，并在那里的群众集会上发表了演说。
    凯塔总统说：马里愿意无例外地同世界上的所有国家合作，向法国和其他国家表明它的完全独立。他说，今天在这里有来自非洲、美洲、欧洲、和亚洲的客人。他说，我们希望人们首先尊重我们的人格并保障我们的独立、主权和领土完整。
    他说，不管人们给我们造成怎样的困难，说我们怎样的坏话，我们将继续争取实现我们谋求经济、社会和文化进步的目的。
