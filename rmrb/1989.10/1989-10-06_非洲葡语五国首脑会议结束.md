Title: Summit of Five Portuguese-Speaking African Countries Concludes
Author:
Date: 1989-10-06
Edition: 3

The summit of the five Portuguese-speaking African countries concluded after approximately seven hours on Thursday in Luanda.

According to the Angolan National News Agency, the heads of state from Angola, Mozambique, Cape Verde, Guinea-Bissau, and São Tomé and Príncipe participated in this meeting. The primary purpose of the meeting was to analyze the situation in Southern Africa, cooperation among the five countries, and changes in the international situation.

The joint communiqué issued after the meeting stated that the heads of state emphasized that the peace-loving group should be more active in its work to achieve peace and stability on the African continent, particularly in Angola and Mozambique.

### 非洲葡语五国首脑会议结束

1989-10-06
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    非洲葡语五国首脑会议结束
    非洲葡语五国首脑特别会议持续了大约七个小时以后，四日晚在罗安达结束。
    据安哥拉国家通讯社报道，由安哥拉、莫桑比克、佛得角、几内亚比绍、圣多美和普林西比国家元首参加的这次会议，主要目的是分析南部非洲的形势、五国之间的合作及国际形势的变化。
    会后发表的联合公报说，五国首脑强调争取和平集团应该更积极地开展工作，以便尽快实现非洲大陆，特别是安哥拉和莫桑比克的和平与稳定。
                                    （据新华社）
