Title: Global Steel Consumption to Reach 800 Million Tons by 1995
Author: Liudi
Date: 1989-10-18
Edition: 7

The International Iron and Steel Institute recently held its 23rd annual meeting in West Berlin, where it released a forecast for global steel demand from 1990 to 1995. The forecast suggests that steel consumption in developing countries will increase, with global crude steel consumption expected to reach 784 million tons in 1990 and 800 million tons in 1995.

According to the institute's estimates, steel consumption in developed countries and regions such as the United States, the European Community, and Japan will decrease by 2% to 5% in 1990 compared to 1989. The primary reason for this is that the global economic boom in 1989 has already peaked steel consumption in the European Community and Japan, which is expected to gradually decline. However, steel demand in Latin America and Asia will remain strong. Globally, steel demand in 1990 is expected to decrease by only 0.8% compared to 1989.

The forecast indicates that from 1985 to 1995, the annual growth rate of steel consumption in developed countries will be 0.4%, while in developing countries it will be 4.3%. Specifically, Asia's consumption in 1995 is expected to reach 90 million tons, approximately double the amount in 1985.

### 1995年世界钢铁消费量将达8亿吨
刘迪
1989-10-18
第7版(国际专页)
专栏：

    1995年世界钢铁消费量将达8亿吨
    国际钢铁协会最近在西柏林召开的第23届年会上发表了1990年至1995年世界钢铁需求的预测，认为发展中国家钢铁消费量将增加，1990年世界粗钢消费量将达7.84亿吨，1995年将达8亿吨。
    据该协会估计，1990年，美国、欧共体、日本等发达国家和地区的钢铁消费量将比1989年下降2％至5％。
    其主要原因是1989年世界性的繁荣使欧共体、日本的钢铁消费量已经到顶，其后将徐徐下降。但是，拉美和亚洲的钢铁需求将持续坚挺，从世界范围来看，1990年钢铁需求将仅比1989年下降0.8％。
    据预测，1985年至1995年，发达国家的钢铁消费年增长率为0.4％，而发展中国家为4.3％，特别是亚洲1995年的消费量将达9000万吨，约为1985年的两倍。
