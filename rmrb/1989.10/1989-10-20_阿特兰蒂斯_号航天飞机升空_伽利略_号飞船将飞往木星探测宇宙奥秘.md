Title: Atlantis Space Shuttle Launches, Galileo Spacecraft to Explore Jupiter's Mysteries
Author: Cang Lide
Date: 1989-10-20
Edition: 3

The U.S. space shuttle Atlantis, which had been delayed twice, finally launched at 12:53 PM today from Cape Canaveral in Florida. The primary mission of Atlantis' space journey is to place the Jupiter-bound Galileo spacecraft into Earth's orbit.

At 7:13 PM tonight, Atlantis successfully released the Galileo spacecraft and its booster. With this, the Galileo spacecraft has embarked on a 6-year journey covering approximately 3.8 billion kilometers to Jupiter. Before reaching Jupiter in December 1995, an automatic probe will separate from the spacecraft and enter Jupiter's atmosphere. This will be the first time a human-made aircraft has entered the atmosphere of an outer planet in the solar system. Local media have commented that the successful launch of Galileo signifies that the U.S. has entered another "golden age" of space exploration.

The Atlantis, originally scheduled to launch last Thursday, was postponed due to a computer malfunction and then delayed again yesterday due to thunderstorms.

The Atlantis is expected to return to Earth next Monday. Among the five astronauts carrying out this mission is Asian-American physicist Zhang Fulin.

According to the plan, the Galileo spacecraft will fly by Venus in February 1990, and will fly past Earth twice in December 1990 and December 1992. It will then use Earth's gravity to reach Jupiter in December 1995.

The Galileo spacecraft, costing nearly $1 billion, is the most precise interstellar probe the U.S. has ever built. The entire launch plan cost approximately $1.5 billion. The spacecraft weighs 2,550 kilograms and is equipped with a nuclear power device containing 22.7 kilograms of radioactive uranium-238. The spacecraft is equipped with 17 scientific instruments, including cameras, near-infrared spectrometers, magnetometers, cloud detectors, and atmospheric structure detectors, for surveying and researching Jupiter's atmospheric composition, cloud structure, temperature, magnetic field, and more.

This Galileo Jupiter exploration mission is a joint effort between the U.S. and West Germany.

### “阿特兰蒂斯”号航天飞机升空  “伽利略”号飞船将飞往木星探测宇宙奥秘
仓立德
1989-10-20
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    “阿特兰蒂斯”号航天飞机升空
    “伽利略”号飞船将飞往木星探测宇宙奥秘
    本报华盛顿10月18日电  记者仓立德报道：两度推迟发射的美国“阿特兰蒂斯”号航天飞机，终于在今天午间12时53分在佛罗里达州卡纳维拉尔角呼啸着冲上天空。“阿特兰蒂斯”号此次太空之行的主要任务是将探索木星的“伽利略”号宇宙飞船送入地球轨道。
    今晚7时13分，“阿特兰蒂斯”号已顺利地释放出“伽利略”号飞船及其推进器。至此，“伽利略”号飞船已踏上为时6年、行程约38亿公里的漫长征途飞往木星。在1995年12月飞临木星前，一具自动探测器将脱离飞船，落入木星大气层。这将是人类制造的飞行器首次深入外层空间太阳系行星的大气层。此间舆论认为，“伽利略”号发射成功意味着美国进入探索太空的又一个“黄金时代”。
    原定于上周四发射的“阿特兰蒂斯”号航天飞机因电脑故障而推迟至昨日，但又遇雷雨天气再度推迟。
    “阿特兰蒂斯”号预计下星期一返回地面。执行此次飞行任务的5名宇航员中包括华裔物理学家张福林。
    按照计划，“伽利略”号飞船将于1990年2月掠过金星，1990年12月和1992年12月两次飞越地球，然后借助地球引力于1995年12月抵达木星。
    “伽利略”号飞船造价近10亿美元，是美国迄今最精密的星际飞行器，整个发射计划耗资约15亿美元。飞船总重2550公斤，有一具核动力装置，内装22.7公斤放射性铀—238。飞船配备着摄像机、近红外勘测分光仪、磁强仪、测云仪、大气结构仪等17种科学仪器，用于木星大气层构成、云层结构、温度、磁场等方面的勘测和研究。
    此次“伽利略”号木星探测计划是由美国和联邦德国联合进行的。（附图片）
