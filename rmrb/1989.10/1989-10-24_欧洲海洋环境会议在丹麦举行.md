Title: European Marine Environment Conference Held in Denmark
Author: Gù Yàomíng
Date: 1989-10-24
Page: 3

A three-day European Marine Environment Conference concluded today in Copenhagen, the capital of Denmark.

The conference's communiqué emphasized the importance of the ocean to human survival and called on all countries to take immediate and effective measures to protect the ocean from pollution.

The conference was organized by the Nordic Council, with representatives from 16 countries in Eastern and Western Europe in attendance. The delegates expressed grave concern over the increasing pollution of the world's oceans, noting that this poses a significant threat to human survival.

The 16 nations agreed to strive to reduce pollutants entering the ocean by 50% by 1995. Eastern and Western European countries will collaborate to establish a scientific advisory body and develop action plans to clean up the marine environment.

### 欧洲海洋环境会议在丹麦举行
顾耀铭
1989-10-24
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

　　欧洲海洋环境会议在丹麦举行
    本报斯德哥尔摩10月19日电　记者顾耀铭报道：为期三天的欧洲海洋环境会议今天在丹麦首都哥本哈根闭幕。
    会议发表的公报强调了海洋对人类生存环境的重要意义，呼吁各国立即采取有效措施，使海洋免受污染。
    这次会议由北欧理事会主持，东西欧16个国家的代表出席。代表们对世界海洋受到的污染日益增多表示严重的担忧，认为这对人类生存的环境已形成了威胁。
    16国代表一致赞成争取在1995年前将流入海洋的污染物减少50％。东西欧将合作建立科学咨询机构，制定行动计划，以清理海洋环境。
