Title: Ten Years Before and After
Author: Song Yingli
Date: 1989-10-11
Page: 5

The cartoon "Ten Years Before and After" by Song Yingli, originally published in "Satire and Humor," reflects on the significant changes that have occurred in China over the past decade. The work captures the essence of societal transformations, economic developments, and cultural shifts that have taken place since the late 1970s.

The cartoon depicts various scenes from daily life, contrasting the conditions of a decade ago with the present. It highlights the improvements in living standards, technological advancements, and the opening up of China to the world. The artwork is a commentary on the rapid modernization and reform that have reshaped the country, providing a visual narrative of progress and change.

Song Yingli's work is known for its sharp wit and satirical edge, often addressing social issues with a humorous yet critical perspective. This particular piece is a testament to the artist's ability to capture the zeitgeist of the era, offering a glimpse into the collective consciousness of the Chinese people during a time of profound transition.
