Title: A Glimpse of Qingdao
Author: Yīng Nǎi'ěr
Date: 1989-10-21
Page: 8

At 2:50 PM on August 11, 1989, at the seaside of Lu Xun Park in Qingdao, a child fell into the sea. A young man dove into the water and swam towards the child, grabbing him tightly...

    The child's laughter, like silver bells, must not be interrupted.
    The child's agility, like a swift swallow, must not be lost.
    From the jaws of the fierce waves,
    From the sharp rocks below,
    From the embrace of the god of death,
    We must seize the child,
    Our lively world,
    A clear and bright tomorrow—

    And, lift high above the vast blue sea,
    The child's smile, like a blooming flower,
    Already blossoming between the waves and troughs.
    So, I raised my camera
    And captured this moment—
    An instant of struggle against the surging waves,
    A path of light and waves,
    A grand scene of life,
    A magnificent and beautiful youth,
    A small glimpse of Qingdao.

### 青岛一瞥
应乃尔
1989-10-21
第8版(副刊)
专栏：

　　青岛一瞥
　　应乃尔
    1989年8月11日下午2时50分，青岛鲁迅公园海边，一小孩落海，一青年奋身跳下海向孩子游去，一把将他抱住……
    不能中断
    那孩子银铃般快活的笑声
    不能失去
    那孩子海燕般奋飞的轻灵
    从凶恶的浪咀里
    从尖利的礁牙下从死亡之神的怀抱把孩子把我们活泼泼的世界把一个清凌凌的明天夺过来——
    并且，高高地托出浩渺的蓝色海面
    那鲜花般孩子的微笑
    已经绽开在潮头浪谷之间
    于是，我举起了我的相机
    摄下了此刻——与惊涛骇浪搏击的一瞬
    一条光摇波动的大海之路
    一幕雄视旷古的人生
    一个壮丽美妙的青春
    一个小小的青岛缩影
