Title: A Major Theft Case Occurs in Yugoslavia
Author:
Date: 1989-10-26
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency

A major theft case has recently occurred in Skopje, Yugoslavia. The safe of a casino manager was stolen, resulting in a loss of one million U.S. dollars.

According to a bulletin issued by the Skopje City Internal Affairs Department on the 23rd, the metal safe in the home of Ivanovic, the manager of the "Mrdaj" casino, was stolen on the evening of October 14th. The safe contained 412,000 West German Marks, 340,000 U.S. dollars, 170,000 British pounds, 16 gold watches, and over 100 pieces of valuable jewelry, totaling 40 billion dinars, approximately one million U.S. dollars.

Yugoslavian relevant departments are currently making all-out efforts to track down the criminals.

### 南斯拉夫发生一特大盗窃案

1989-10-26
第4版(国际新闻)
专栏：

　　南斯拉夫发生一特大盗窃案
    南斯拉夫斯科普里市最近发生一起特大盗窃案，赌场经理的保险柜被窃，损失达一百万美元。
    据斯科普里市内务部二十三日发表的公报说，十月十四日傍晚，“姆尔达伊”赌场经理伊瓦诺维奇家里的金属保险柜被窃，内装现金四十一点二万西德马克、三十四万美元、一点七万英镑、十六块金表和一百多件贵重首饰等，总价为四百亿第纳尔，约合一百万美元。
    南斯拉夫有关部门正全力侦缉罪犯。
                                      （据新华社）
