Photography's Contributions

– Selected Works from the Exhibition Commemorating the 150th Anniversary of the Announcement of Photography

August 19, 1839, was a day of great significance in human history. On this day, the French Academy of Sciences announced to the public the invention of the daguerreotype by the French physicist and painter Louis Daguerre. From then on, humanity's dream of permanently capturing and preserving the true images of people and the world became a reality. Photography, upon its appearance, "conquered the world at lightning speed." 150 years later, it has developed into an omnipresent, indispensable part of photographic culture, possessing unique value and important functions as a social productive force.

Photography's "greatest contribution" to humanity is its ability to accurately and vividly record reliable images, leaving behind historical image archives.

	Excerpted from the foreword to "Photography's Contributions – Selected Works from the Exhibition Commemorating the 150th Anniversary of the Announcement of Photography"

专栏：

　　摄影的贡献
    ——纪念摄影术公布150年回顾展作品选
    1839年8月19日，是人类历史上一个具有划时代意义的日子。
    这一天，法国科学院向公众宣布了法国物理学家、画家达盖尔发明的银版摄影术。从此，人类把人和世间的真实形象永久固定和保存起来的梦想变成了现实。摄影术一出现，就以“闪电般的速度征服了世界”。150年后的今天，它已发展成无所不在、无处不用的摄影文化，具有独特价值和重要作用的社会生产力。
    摄影术对人类“最大的贡献”，是能真实纪录准确、生动的可信图像，留下历史形象档案。
        摘自《摄影的贡献——纪念摄影术公布150年回顾展》前言
