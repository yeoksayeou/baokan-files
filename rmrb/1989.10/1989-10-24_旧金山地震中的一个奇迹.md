Title: A Miracle in the San Francisco Earthquake
Author:
Date: 1989-10-24
Edition: 3

On the fifth day after the San Francisco Bay earthquake, a man who had been trapped in the ruins of the 880 Freeway overpass for nearly 90 hours miraculously survived and was rescued at noon on the 21st. The man's name is Buck Helm, aged 57, and he is a worker at the Port of Oakland.

At 6 AM on the 21st, an engineer from the California Department of Transportation was inspecting the collapsed section of the overpass when he suddenly noticed someone signaling from a crushed car. It was Helm. After 5 hours of effort, rescue workers finally pulled Helm out.

### 旧金山地震中的一个奇迹

1989-10-24
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

　　旧金山地震中的一个奇迹
    在旧金山湾大地震的第5天，被困在880号公路引桥废墟中近90个小时的一名男子奇迹般地死里逃生，于21日中午被救脱险。这名男子名叫巴克·赫尔姆斯，今年57岁，是奥克兰港口的工人。
    21日清晨6时，加州交通局一位工程师正在坍塌的引桥路段检查，突然发现在一辆压坏的汽车中有人向他打手势，他就是赫尔姆斯。抢救工人经过5小时的努力，终于把赫尔姆斯救出。
    （本报华盛顿10月21日电）
