Title: Electronic Immigrants
Author:
Date: 1989-10-20
Edition: 7

According to the latest issue of *Futurist* magazine, with the increasing application of computers and the continuous improvement of information processing technology, scientists may be able to use international networking technology to develop a type of remote-controlled robot that can work in foreign countries.

Electronic communications expert Joseph Pelton calls this new type of labor force "electronic immigrants." He predicts that in the next century, there will be an increasing number of "electronic immigrants" entering Third World countries to work for technologically advanced nations. Some technologically advanced countries will compete to hire electronic professionals from Third World countries at low wages, which will be a prominent issue in international trade in the 21st century. These individuals, without leaving their home countries, can provide services such as computer programming and word processing for foreign employers.

Pelton also warns that if this economic "immigration" trend becomes widespread, it will cause employment crises in Third World countries, potentially leading to political problems.

### “电子移民”

1989-10-20
第7版(国际专页)
专栏：

    “电子移民”
    据最新一期《未来学家》杂志报道，随着计算机日益广泛的应用和信息处理技术的不断提高，科学家们有可能利用国际联网技术研制出一种能够在异国他乡工作的遥控机器人。
    电子通讯专家约瑟夫·佩尔顿把这种新型劳动力称为“电子移民”。他预言，下个世纪将会有越来越多的“电子移民”涌入第三世界国家，为技术大国工作。一些技术大国争相从第三世界国家以低薪招聘电子专业方面的人才，将会是二十一世纪国际贸易中的突出问题。这些人不出国，就可以为国外雇主做一些服务性的工作，如计算机程序处理、文字加工等。
    佩尔顿同时警告说，若这种经济上的“移民”一旦呈现潮流，就会在第三世界国家中引起就业危机，从而带来政治上的麻烦。
              （新华社）
