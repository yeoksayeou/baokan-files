Title: Funeral Ceremony for Two Hong Kong National Political Consultative Conference Members Held in Beijing
Author:
Date: 1989-10-19
Edition: 2

The funeral ceremony for Chen Pei-shi, a member of the 7th National Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference (CPPCC) and a prominent Hong Kong barrister, and Mu Run-sun, a member of the 7th National Committee of the CPPCC and a former professor at the Chinese University of Hong Kong, was held in Beijing on October 16. Their ashes were escorted to Beijing by their family members and relevant personnel from the New China News Agency's Hong Kong branch.

Officials including Wu Lianyuan, Zhang Qia, and Liu Xiaoping, as well as family members and friends of Chen Pei-shi and Mu Run-sun, including Qi Gong and Bai Shouyi, attended the ceremony at the Babaoshan Revolutionary Cemetery.

Chen Pei-shi, a native of Zhongshan, Guangdong, was born in 1901. He previously served in the Foreign Affairs Department and the Executive Yuan of the National Government. In 1947, he became a barrister at the High Court of Hong Kong. He participated in the founding of the "Chinese Reform Association" and served as its chairman from 1962. In 1980, he became the honorary chairman. He was a member of the 6th National Committee of the CPPCC. His father, Chen Youren, was a well-known leftist in the Kuomintang and served as an advisor, secretary, and Foreign Minister to Dr. Sun Yat-sen. Chen Youren had close ties with Song Qingling, He Xiangning, Deng Yanda, and others. Chen Pei-shi passed away in Hong Kong on February 20, 1989.

Mu Run-sun, a native of Fushan, Shandong, was born in 1909. He previously taught at Fu Jen Catholic University, Henan University, Tongji University, and National Taiwan University. In 1955, he moved to Hong Kong and served as the head and professor of the Department of History and Literature at New Asia College, a professor at the Chinese University of Hong Kong, and a researcher at the Chinese Cultural Research Institute. He was a member of the 6th National Committee of the CPPCC. Mu Run-sun passed away in Hong Kong on November 19, 1988.

Chen Pei-shi and Mu Run-sun, during their lifetimes, loved the socialist motherland and made beneficial contributions to the prosperity and unity of the country. Before their passing, they both left instructions to have their ashes placed in the capital of the great socialist motherland.

### 香港两位全国政协委员  陈丕士牟润孙骨灰安放仪式在京举行

1989-10-19
第2版(国内新闻)
专栏：

    香港两位全国政协委员
    陈丕士牟润孙骨灰安放仪式在京举行
    新华社北京10月17日电  第七届全国政协委员、香港大律师陈丕士和第七届全国政协委员、原香港中文大学教授牟润孙的骨灰10月16日由家属和新华社香港分社有关人员从香港护送到北京，在八宝山革命公墓举行了安放仪式。
    有关方面负责人武连元、张洽、刘小萍，陈丕士、牟润孙两位先生的家属和在京亲友启功、白寿彝等，参加了骨灰安放仪式。
    陈丕士先生，广东中山人，1901年生。曾在国民政府外交部、行政院任职。1947年任香港高等法院律师。参与创办“华人革新协会”，1962年起任这个协会主席，1980年改任名誉会长。他是第六届全国政协委员。其父陈友仁是著名的国民党左派，曾任孙中山先生的外事顾问、秘书、国民政府外交部长，与宋庆龄、何香凝、邓演达等交情甚笃。陈丕士先生是1989年2月20日在香港病逝的。
    牟润孙先生，山东福山人，1909年生。曾在辅仁大学、河南大学、同济大学、台湾大学任教。1955年去香港，先后任新亚书院文史系主任、教授，中文大学教授，中国文化研究所研究员。他是第六届全国政协委员。牟润孙先生是1988年11月19日在香港病逝的。
    陈丕士、牟润孙两位先生，生前热爱社会主义祖国，为国家的繁荣和统一做了有益的工作。
    他们逝世前都留下遗言，要求将骨灰安放在伟大社会主义祖国的首都。
