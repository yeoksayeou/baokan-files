Title: The U.S. Bans Financial Transactions with Noriega
Author:
Date: 1989-10-21
Page: 3

The Bush administration recently decided to impose new economic sanctions on Panama, prohibiting all U.S. citizens from conducting any financial transactions with Noriega, his wife, and 32 companies in which they hold shares. Violators will face fines of up to $500,000 and imprisonment of up to 15 years for each transaction. It is reported that this is a further measure by the U.S. government to pressure Noriega following the failed coup against him in Panama on October 3. (Special Report)

### 美禁止同诺列加进行金融交易

1989-10-21
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

　　美禁止同诺列加进行金融交易
    布什政府近日决定对巴拿马采取新的经济制裁措施，禁止所有美国人同诺列加及夫人以及他们拥有股票的三十二家公司进行任何金融交易。如有违者，从事每笔交易将被课以多至五十万美元的罚款和多至十五年的监禁。据称，这是巴拿马十月三日反诺列加兵变失败后，美国政府对诺列加进一步施加压力的手段。　（本报专电）
