Title: U.S. Space Shuttle Returns to Earth, "Galileo" Headed for Venus
Author: Cang Lidie
Date: 1989-10-25
Edition: 3

    U.S. Space Shuttle Returns to Earth
    "Galileo" Headed for Venus

    Our reporter Cang Lidie reports from Washington: Due to poor weather conditions on the ground, the 97-ton "Atlantis" space shuttle today pierced through the fog and strong winds to safely land at Edwards Air Force Base in California at noon, more than three hours ahead of schedule.

    The main mission of the "Atlantis" during its five-day flight was to launch the "Galileo" spacecraft into orbit to explore Jupiter. Currently, the "Galileo" is flying towards Venus at a speed of over 14,000 kilometers per hour and is expected to reach Jupiter in December 1995.

    In addition, the five astronauts on the space shuttle collected data on the Earth's protective ozone layer, studied the effects of weightlessness on the human body, and conducted a series of scientific experiments including polymer processing, corn cultivation, and crystal growth.

### 美国航天飞机返回地面  “伽利略”号正飞向金星
仓立德
1989-10-25
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

　　美国航天飞机返回地面
　　“伽利略”号正飞向金星
    本报华盛顿10月23日电　记者仓立德报道：由于地面气候恶劣，97吨重的“阿特兰蒂斯”号航天飞机今天穿过雾气和大风，于中午安全降落在加利福尼亚州爱德华兹空军基地，比预定时间提前了3个多小时。
    “阿特兰蒂斯”号为期5天飞行的主要使命是将探索木星的“伽利略”号宇宙飞船送入轨道。目前“伽利略”号正以1.4万多公里的时速飞向金星，预计1995年12月抵达木星。
    此外，航天飞机上的5名宇航员收集了对地球起保护作用的臭氧层的数据，研究了失重对人体的影响，进行了聚合物的加工、培植玉米和使水晶体生长等一系列科学试验。
