Title: Indian President Meets Vice Premier Wu Xueqian
Author: Tán Rénxiá
Date: 1989-10-14
Edition: 3

Indian President Meets Vice Premier Wu Xueqian
Wu Xueqian and Indian Foreign Minister Express Willingness to Continue Developing Bilateral Relations

NEW DELHI, Oct. 12 (Xinhua) -- Indian President Venkataraman met with Chinese Vice Premier Wu Xueqian here today and said that India and China have broad prospects for cooperation in various fields.

Wu Xueqian and his delegation arrived here late last night for a one-week friendly visit to India.

The Indian president said that the friendly relations between India and China can be traced back to two thousand years ago. He expressed the hope that the visit by the Chinese vice premier would promote broader and more powerful development of Indo-China relations.

Wu Xueqian briefed the president on the achievements of the People's Republic of China over the past 40 years. He reaffirmed that China's independent and peaceful foreign policy remains unchanged, and that the policy of reform and opening up remains unchanged.

NEW DELHI, Oct. 12 (Xinhua) -- (Reporter Tan Renxia) Chinese Vice Premier Wu Xueqian and Indian Foreign Minister Rao held talks here today and expressed their willingness to work together to establish a new international political and economic order.

The two sides exchanged views "sincerely and friendly" on bilateral relations and international issues during the one-and-a-half-hour talk.

Wu Xueqian said that the Five Principles of Peaceful Coexistence jointly advocated by the two countries have strong vitality. He hoped that relevant countries would jointly work to establish a new international political order based on the Five Principles, and a new international economic order based on equality and mutual benefit.

He said that China is willing to improve and develop relations with India on the basis of the Five Principles, and also hopes that South Asian countries will develop good-neighborly and friendly relations on the basis of the Five Principles.

Rao emphasized that India and China have similar and consistent views on many issues. India is willing to work with China to strive for a new international political and economic order.

He said that the Five Principles advocated by the leaders of the two countries are not only applicable to bilateral relations but also to international relations.

Both sides expressed satisfaction with the improvement and development of bilateral relations in recent years and hoped that this momentum would continue.

Rao said that bilateral relations are at a very promising stage of development. The 40th anniversary of the establishment of diplomatic relations between India and China in 1990 is a historic opportunity to further promote bilateral relations.

### 印度总统会见吴学谦副总理  吴学谦和印外长表示将继续发展两国关系
谭仁侠
1989-10-14
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    印度总统会见吴学谦副总理
    吴学谦和印外长表示将继续发展两国关系
    新华社新德里10月12日电　印度总统文卡塔拉曼12日在新德里会见吴学谦副总理时说，印度和中国之间存在着在各个领域进行合作的广阔余地。
    吴学谦副总理一行是11日晚到达这里对印度进行为期一周的友好访问的。
    印度总统说，印中两国的友好关系可以追溯到两千年以前。他表示希望中国副总理的访问将使印中关系得到更广、更有力的发展。
    吴学谦向总统介绍了中华人民共和国成立40年以来取得的成就。他重申中国独立自主的和平外交政策不变，改革开放不变。
    新华社新德里10月12日电　（记者谭仁侠）中国副总理吴学谦和印度外长拉奥今天在这里举行会谈时表示，两国愿为建立新的国际政治和经济秩序共同努力。
    据悉，双方在一个半小时的会谈中，就双边关系和国际问题“诚挚友好地”交换了意见。
    吴学谦说，两国共同倡导的和平共处五项原则有强大的生命力。他希望有关国家共同努力在五项原则的基础上建立国际政治新秩序，在平等互利的基础上建立国际经济新秩序。
    他说，中国愿在五项原则的基础上改善和发展同印度的关系，也希望南亚国家在五项原则的基础上发展睦邻友好关系。
    拉奥强调印度和中国在许多问题上观点接近和一致。印度愿同中国一起争取建立新的国际政治和经济秩序。
    他说，两国领导人倡导的五项原则不仅适用于两国关系，也适用于国际关系。
    双方对近几年两国关系的改善和发展表示满意，并希望这种发展势头继续保持下去。
    拉奥说，两国关系正面临十分有希望的发展阶段。1990年印中建交40周年，是进一步推动两国关系的历史性机会。
