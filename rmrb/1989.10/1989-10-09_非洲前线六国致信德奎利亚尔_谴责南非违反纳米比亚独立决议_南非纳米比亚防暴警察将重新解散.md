Title: Six Frontline States and SWAPO Condemn South Africa for Violating Namibia Independence Resolution
Author: Yáng Yuèhuá
Date: 1989-10-09
Page: 3

Xinhua News Agency, United Nations, October 5th.

Six frontline African states, along with Nigeria and the South West Africa People's Organization (SWAPO), today condemned the South African regime for ignoring the United Nations' provisions on Namibia's independence plan, obstructing free and fair elections in the country.

This condemnation was made in a letter dated September 29th, addressed to the United Nations Secretary-General, which was released today. The letter stated that the South African authorities continue to deploy riot police in Namibia to suppress the Namibian people.

The letter also said that including the Namibia issue in the agenda of this session of the General Assembly is a "constructive" move.

Tanzania's Foreign Minister Benjamin Mkapa, speaking at the General Assembly today, urgently called on the international community to pressure Pretoria until the apartheid system in South Africa is finally eliminated. Tanzania is one of the six frontline African states.

Xinhua News Agency, Windhoek, October 4th.

The South African administration in Namibia has accepted the United Nations Transitional Assistance Group's (UNTAG) demand to re-dissolve the Namibian riot police force.

The agreement was reached on the afternoon of the 3rd. Previously, UNTAG had expressed dissatisfaction with the dissolution activities held at the riot police base in Oshakati, northern Namibia, on the 30th of last month.

According to United Nations Resolution 640, this 3,000-strong riot police force, used to suppress SWAPO, should have been dissolved long ago. However, part of this force has been integrated into the regular police force. Since the end of last month, only about 1,200 personnel remained at their base in Oshakati.

It is understood that the UNTAG police commissioner today discussed the specifics of re-dissolving this riot police force with the South West African police commissioner.

### 非洲前线六国致信德奎利亚尔  谴责南非违反纳米比亚独立决议  南非纳米比亚防暴警察将重新解散
杨跃华
1989-10-09
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

    非洲前线六国致信德奎利亚尔
    谴责南非违反纳米比亚独立决议
    南非纳米比亚防暴警察将重新解散
    新华社联合国10月5日电　（记者杨跃华）6个非洲前线国家以及尼日利亚和西南非洲人民组织今天谴责南非政权无视联合国关于纳米比亚独立计划的条款，阻碍纳米比亚进行自由和公正的选举。
    这一谴责是在9月29日交给联合国秘书长的一封信中提出的。这封信于今天在这里发表。这封信说，南非当局继续在纳米比亚部署防暴警察部队，镇压纳米比亚人民。
    信件说，把纳米比亚问题列入本届联大议程是“建设性”的举动。
    坦桑尼亚外长本杰明·姆卡帕今天在联大发言中还紧急呼吁国际社会对比勒陀利亚施加压力，直至最终消除南非种族隔离制度。坦桑尼亚是6个非洲前线国家之一。
    新华社温得和克10月4日电　南非纳米比亚当局已接受联合国纳米比亚过渡时期援助团提出重新解散纳米比亚防暴警察部队的要求。
    双方是在3日下午达成这一协议的。在此之前，联合国纳米比亚过渡时期援助团曾表示不满意上月30日在纳米比亚北部奥沙卡蒂的防暴警察部队基地举行的解散活动。
    根据联合国640号决议，这支拥有3000人、用来对付西南非洲人民组织的防暴警察部队早就应该解散。然而，这支部队中的部分人员已并入了正规的警察部队。自上月底以来，在其奥沙卡蒂的基地上只剩下了大约1200人。
    据悉，联合国纳米比亚过渡时期援助团警察专员今天同西南非洲警察专员讨论了有关重新解散这支防暴警察部队的具体事宜。
