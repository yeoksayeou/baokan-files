Title: A Calligraphic Work by Xu Dehong
Author:
Date: 1989-10-17
Edition: 8

    The calligraphic work titled "先天下之忧而忧，后天下之乐而乐" (Worry before the world worries, and enjoy after the world enjoys) by Xu Dehong is a profound expression of his deep concern for the nation and his people. This piece, written in a style that reflects both tradition and personal innovation, showcases Xu's mastery of calligraphy and his deep philosophical insights.

    Xu Dehong, a renowned calligrapher and statesman, has always been known for his commitment to the betterment of society. His calligraphic works often carry a sense of responsibility and duty, reflecting his belief in serving the people and the nation. This particular piece, with its powerful and elegant strokes, embodies the spirit of selflessness and dedication that Xu has always advocated.

    The phrase "先天下之忧而忧，后天下之乐而乐" is a famous line from the essay "Yuan Dao" by Fan Zhongyan, a statesman and writer of the Song Dynasty. It encapsulates the idea of prioritizing the welfare of the nation and its people above personal interests. Xu Dehong's rendition of this phrase not only pays homage to the classical literature but also serves as a reminder of the timeless values that continue to guide us today.

    This calligraphic work is a testament to Xu Dehong's artistic prowess and his unwavering commitment to the principles of service and dedication. It is a piece that inspires reflection and encourages us to strive for a better future for all.
