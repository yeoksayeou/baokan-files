Title: Bronze Artifacts from the Shang and Zhou Dynasties Unearthed in Jiangxi
Author: Lu Jing
Date: 1989-10-20
Edition: 4

Jiangxi recently unearthed over 100 bronze artifacts. Experts, based on the shapes and patterns, preliminarily identified these as bronze artifacts from the late Shang Dynasty to the early Western Zhou Dynasty.

It is reported that this batch of unearthed bronze artifacts is numerous and diverse, setting a record in the south. Among them, a square ding with tiger-shaped ears stands at 97 centimeters high, the first of its kind discovered in the south. The earliest known metal musical instrument, the nao, was also found among these artifacts.

### 江西出土商周青铜器
卢晶
1989-10-20
第4版(综合)
专栏：

    江西出土商周青铜器
    据新华社南昌电　（记者卢晶）江西省最近出土了100多件青铜器。专家们根据造型、花纹等特征，初步认定其属于商代晚期到西周早期的青铜器。
    据介绍，这次出土的青铜器数量多、种类齐全，创江南之最。其中一个虎耳方鼎高达97厘米，在南方属首次发现。我国最早的金属乐器铙也在其中。
