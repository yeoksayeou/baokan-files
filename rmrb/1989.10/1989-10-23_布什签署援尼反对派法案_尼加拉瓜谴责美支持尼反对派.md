Title: Bush Signs Bill to Aid Nicaraguan Opposition
Author:
Date: 1989-10-23
Page: 3

Xinhua News Agency, Washington, 21st October.

President Bush signed a bill on the 21st providing $9 million in aid to the Nicaraguan opposition. The bill, submitted by Congress, aims to help the Nicaraguan opposition win the elections scheduled for February 25th next year.

White House spokesman Fitzwater stated in a declaration that the use of these funds might help the elections achieve genuine "freedom" and "fairness."

He said that the United States has also provided substantial funds to support democratic movements in the elections of Chile, Paraguay, Poland, and other countries.

Xinhua News Agency, Managua, 19th October.

The Nicaraguan National Assembly condemned on the 18th the U.S. Congress' decision to provide $9 million in campaign funds to the Nicaraguan opposition alliance. In a resolution passed on the 18th, the National Assembly demanded that the United States not interfere in Nicaragua's election process, respect the agreement reached at the summit of the five Central American heads of state, and suggested that the United States transfer the $9 million to the International Support and Verification Commission to promote peace and democracy in the region.

### 布什签署援尼反对派法案  尼加拉瓜谴责美支持尼反对派

1989-10-23
第3版(国际新闻)
专栏：

　　布什签署援尼反对派法案
　　尼加拉瓜谴责美支持尼反对派
    新华社华盛顿10月21日电　美国总统布什21日签署了由国会提交的向尼加拉瓜反对派提供900万美元的法案。这项法案旨在帮助尼加拉瓜反对派在明年2月25日举行的大选中获胜。
    白宫发言人菲茨沃特在一项声明中说，这笔资金的运用也许能使选举达到真正的“自由”和“公正”。
    他说，美国为支持民主运动也曾将大笔资金用于援助智利、巴拉圭、波兰等国的选举。
    新华社马那瓜10月19日电　尼加拉瓜国民议会18日谴责美国国会决定向尼加拉瓜反对派联盟提供900万美元竞选费用的做法。尼国民议会在18日通过的一份决议中要求美国不要干涉尼选举进程，尊重中美洲5国首脑会议达成的协议，并建议美国将这900万美元交给国际支持与核查委员会，以促进中美洲地区的和平与民主。
