Title: Romania Warmly Celebrates the Seventh Anniversary of Liberation; Grand Military Parade and Procession Held in Bucharest; Stalin Sends Congratulatory Telegram to Chairman of Romanian Council of Ministers
Author:
Date: 1951-08-25
Page: 4

　　Romania Warmly Celebrates the Seventh Anniversary of Liberation
    Grand Military Parade and Procession Held in Bucharest
    Stalin Sends Congratulatory Telegram to Chairman of Romanian Council of Ministers
    [Xinhua News Agency, August 24th] According to TASS, Bucharest: The working people of Bucharest held a grand meeting on August 22nd to celebrate the seventh anniversary of the Soviet Army's liberation of Romania. Attending the meeting were Gheorghe Gheorghiu-Dej, General Secretary of the Romanian Workers' Party, and other leaders of the government and the Party. Also present at the meeting were the Soviet government delegation headed by Voroshilov and government delegations from various people's democratic countries. They were all warmly welcomed by the audience.
    Romanian Deputy Chairman of the Council of Ministers, Luca, delivered a report. Voroshilov then delivered a speech, which was repeatedly interrupted by enthusiastic cheers. Representatives from other countries also delivered congratulatory speeches at the meeting. At the end of the meeting, a telegram of respect was sent to Stalin, and the audience stood up unanimously, shouting "Long live the Soviet Union" and "Long live Stalin" for a long time.
    [Xinhua News Agency, August 24th] According to the Romanian News Agency, Bucharest, August 23rd: Stalin, Chairman of the Council of Ministers of the Soviet Union, sent a telegram to Groza, Chairman of the Council of Ministers of the Romanian People's Republic, congratulating him on the anniversary of Romania's liberation. The text of the telegram is as follows:
    "On the occasion of the anniversary of the liberation of Romania, please accept, Comrade Chairman, and the government of the Romanian People's Republic, the congratulations of the Union of Soviet Socialist Republics and myself, and wish the friendly Romanian people new victories."
    [Xinhua News Agency, August 24th] According to TASS, Bucharest, August 24th: The working people of Romania warmly celebrated the seventh anniversary of the Soviet Army's liberation of Romania. On the 23rd, a military parade and a demonstration by 500,000 working people were held in Bucharest.
    The military parade was held in Stalin Square. At 8:30 a.m., Parhon, Chairman of the Presidium of the National Assembly of Romania, Groza, Chairman of the Council of Ministers, Gheorghe Gheorghiu-Dej, General Secretary of the Romanian Workers' Party, and government and Workers' Party leaders Pauker, Luca, Georgescu, Chisinevschi, and Apostol, stepped onto the rostrum amidst thunderous cheers. Also participating in the celebration were the Soviet government delegation headed by Voroshilov, and the diplomatic representatives of the People's Republic of China, the German Democratic Republic, and various people's democratic countries.
    General Bodnaras, Minister of the Armed Forces of Romania, reviewed the majestic troops and congratulated the reviewed troops on the festival.
    Then, the working people of Bucharest began the grand festival procession. Leading the way were armed workers' teams, followed by Young Pioneer and primary school student teams, carrying tens of thousands of bouquets of flowers. The team of working people from the "August 23rd" district, who had achieved excellent results in the socialist emulation before the festival, passed in front of the rostrum and were cheered by all the people in the stands. At the head of this team, a row of people held up huge golden characters: "Glory to Stalin." Countless red flags and charts illustrating production achievements fluttered in the procession, and the marchers held up portraits of Stalin, portraits of members of the Political Bureau of the Central Committee of the Communist Party of the Soviet Union, portraits of Romanian government and Romanian Workers' Party leaders, and portraits of Mao Zedong, Kim Il-sung, Rakosi, Thorez, Togliatti, and Ho Chi Minh. People enthusiastically cheered Stalin and his comrades-in-arms, Marshal Voroshilov. After the working people's teams, athletes in neat formations passed through the square. A large group of athletes stopped in the square and formed the words "Glory to Stalin." Other groups formed a huge five-pointed star with the number "23" in the center. The athletes also performed acrobatics, forming the abbreviation of the Romanian Workers' Party. These performances all won thunderous applause. The demonstration lasted four hours.
    Many cities and villages in Romania held gatherings and demonstrations to celebrate the festival.

### 罗马尼亚热烈庆祝解放七周年  布加勒斯特举行盛大阅兵和游行  斯大林致电罗部长会议主席祝贺

1951-08-25
第4版()
专栏：

　　罗马尼亚热烈庆祝解放七周年
    布加勒斯特举行盛大阅兵和游行
    斯大林致电罗部长会议主席祝贺
    【新华社二十四日讯】据塔斯社布加勒斯特讯：布加勒斯特劳动人民于二十二日举行庆祝苏军解放罗马尼亚七周年纪念大会。出席大会的有罗马尼亚部长会议主席格罗查、罗马尼亚工人党总书记乔治乌—德治，以及政府和党的其他领袖。出席大会的还有以伏罗希洛夫为首的苏联政府代表团和各人民民主国家的政府代表团。他们都受到全场热烈的欢迎。
    罗马尼亚部长会议副主席卢加作了报告。伏罗希洛夫接着发表演说，他的演说屡次被热烈的欢呼声打断。其他国家的代表也分别在会上致祝辞。大会最后通过向斯大林致敬电，全场一致起立，“苏联万岁”、“斯大林万岁”的欢呼声经久不息。
    【新华社二十四日讯】罗马尼亚通讯社布加勒斯特二十三日讯：苏联部长会议主席斯大林致电罗马尼亚人民共和国部长会议主席格罗查，祝贺罗马尼亚解放纪念日。电文如下：
    “际此罗马尼亚解放纪念日，请你——主席同志，和罗马尼亚人民共和国政府接受苏维埃社会主义共和国联盟和我个人的庆贺，并祝友好的罗马尼亚人民获得新的胜利。”
    【新华社二十四日讯】据塔斯社布加勒斯特二十四日讯：罗马尼亚劳动人民热烈地庆祝了苏军解放罗马尼亚七周年纪念日。二十三日布加勒斯特举行了阅兵和五十万劳动人民的示威大游行。
    阅兵在斯大林广场举行。上午八时三十分，罗马尼亚国民议会主席团主席巴洪，部长会议主席格罗查，罗马尼亚工人党总书记乔治乌——德治及政府和工人党领袖波克、卢加、乔治斯库、契西尼夫斯奇和阿波斯托尔等人在雷动的欢呼声中，步上主席台。参加庆典的还有以伏罗希洛夫为首的苏联政府代表团，中华人民共和国、德意志民主共和国和各人民民主国家的外交代表。
    罗马尼亚武装部队部部长波德纳拉西上将检阅了雄壮的军队，并向受检阅的部队祝贺节日。
    接着布加勒斯特劳动人民开始了节日大游行。走在前面的是武装的工人队伍，后面是少年先锋队和小学生的队伍，他们拿着成千上万的花束。在节日前的社会主义竞赛中获得优良成绩的“八·二三”区劳动人民的队伍通过主席台前，受到看台上全体人员的欢呼。这个队伍为首的一排人高举着巨大的金字：“光荣归于斯大林”。游行队伍里飘扬着无数红旗，和说明生产成就的图表，游行者高举着斯大林的画像，苏联共产党中央委员会政治局委员的画像，罗马尼亚政府和罗马尼亚工人党领袖的画像，和毛泽东、金日成、拉科西、多列士、托里亚蒂、胡志明的画像。人们热烈地为斯大林和他的战友、伏罗希洛夫元帅欢呼。在劳动人民的队伍之后，运动员排成整齐的队伍通过广场。有一大队运动员在广场上停下来，组成了“光荣归于斯大林”的字样。其他小组排成了一个巨大的五角星，星的中央有“二十三”的字样。运动员们还表演了叠罗汉。组成罗马尼亚工人党缩写的字母。这些表演都博得了雷鸣般的掌声。示威游行历时四小时。
    罗马尼亚的许多城市和乡村，都举行了庆祝节日的集会和示威游行。
