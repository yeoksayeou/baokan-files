Title: Notice from the Military Court of the Beijing Military Control Commission of the People's Liberation Army
Author:
Date: 1951-08-25
Page: 5

Beijing Municipal Public Security Bureau, after thorough and meticulous investigation, recently uncovered 418 cases of counter-revolutionary crimes with conclusive evidence. The Bureau drafted a handling opinion along with relevant case files, which were submitted to the Beijing Municipal People's Government Committee and the Joint Conference of the Beijing Municipal People's Congress and the Beijing Municipal People's Political Consultative Conference on August 11. After the conference reviewed each case in groups and conducted detailed discussions, the Public Security Bureau transferred all cases to this court and initiated public prosecutions against the criminals. After the court's trial, based on the "Regulations on Punishing Counter-Revolutionaries" of the People's Republic of China and the sentencing principles announced by this court on May 23, the 418 counter-revolutionary criminals were sentenced according to the severity of their crimes. The breakdown is as follows:

1. For those with blood debts or other extremely serious crimes that cannot be appeased without execution, and those who have caused the most severe harm to the state, a total of 237 were sentenced to death and executed immediately. This includes notorious counter-revolutionary spy leaders such as "National Defense Department Second Bureau" staff officer Wen Tianhe, "Military Stat Major Communications Officer" Guan Yifu, traitor "North China Pacification Army Commander" Du Sijun, "One Path Society" representative Yu Deyu, "Nine Palace Society Five Great Assembly" leader Li Cai, bandits Zhao Zhen Dong and Zhao Fu Chen, and notorious bandits Wei Dechen and Liu Shu Yuan, etc.

2. For those without blood debts, with lesser public outrage, and although they have caused serious harm to the state but not to the most severe extent, and whose crimes warrant the death penalty, a total of 25 were sentenced to death with a two-year reprieve, forced labor, and observation of their behavior. This includes 14 counter-revolutionary spies, 10 notorious bandits, and 1 leader of a counter-revolutionary sect.

3. A total of 25 counter-revolutionary criminals were sentenced to life imprisonment.

4. A total of 128 counter-revolutionary criminals were sentenced to fixed-term imprisonment.

5. For those whose crimes are relatively minor and have shown signs of repentance, a total of 3 were sentenced to release on bail.

The above judgments will be announced and executed immediately.

Issued by the Court
