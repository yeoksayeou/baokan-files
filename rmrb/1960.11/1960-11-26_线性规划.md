Linear Programming

    Linear programming is a branch of operations research. It is a scientific method for studying how to accomplish the maximum task with the least amount of manpower and material resources.

    The specific methods of linear programming, currently the most widely used, include the graphical method and the tabular method. For example, when formulating a material allocation plan or a transportation tool scheduling plan, first draw a simple route map or a table, and then calculate using mathematical methods. After adjustments, an excellent plan can be obtained.

    Linear programming can be applied to many sectors of the national economy. For example, in agricultural production, it can be used to rationally organize the labor force, rationally arrange crop layouts, and plan irrigation channels; in industrial production, it can be used for the rational layout of factories and warehouses, rational material cutting, and rational internal and external transportation. Many things in daily life also conform to the principles of linear programming, such as walking the bowstring instead of the back of the bow, and the clever tailoring of clothes.

### 线性规划

1960-11-26
第3版()
专栏：小常识

    线性规划
    线性规划是运筹学的一个分支。它是研究如何用最少的人力、物力来完成最大限度的任务的一种科学方法。
    线性规划的具体做法，目前应用最广泛的有图上作业法、表上作业法等。如在制订一个物资调运方案或者运输工具调度方案时，先画出一个简单的线路图或者一张表，然后用数学方法计算，经过调整，便可以得出一个优良的方案。
    线性规划可以运用于国民经济的许多部门。例如，在农业生产方面，可以用于合理组织劳动力，合理安排作物布局、灌溉渠道规划等；在工业生产方面，可以用于工厂、仓库的合理布局，合理下料，厂内外的合理运输等。在日常生活中也有不少事情符合线性规划的原理，如走路时要走弓弦不走弓背，做衣服的巧裁缝等。
