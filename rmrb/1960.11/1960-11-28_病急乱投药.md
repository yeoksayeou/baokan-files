Resorting to Any Remedy in Desperation
Editor's Note: "U.S. News & World Report" published an article in its November 28th issue (published in advance) entitled "Steps to End Gold Outflow—What May Happen Next." The article discussed the actions that the U.S. authorities have taken and may take in an attempt to stop the outflow of gold and the huge deficit in the balance of international payments. This article demonstrates the desperate and chaotic state of the U.S. ruling group as the dollar nears its death. The full text is translated below:

According to the new orders from the White House, the U.S. will take these actions:

	——Withdraw a large number of military dependents stationed overseas back to the country, reducing the number of dependents overseas from 484,000 to 200,000.

	——Stop overseas military stores from purchasing foreign goods.

	——Reduce the amount of foreign products purchased with U.S. aid.

	——Reduce military purchases of foreign goods.

	——Insist that allies increase foreign aid expenditures.

	——Promote exports, step up sales overseas, and demand that foreign countries relax tariffs and restrictions on U.S. goods.

	——Ensure that the disposal of surplus agricultural products does not hinder normal sales of U.S. agricultural products.

	——Reduce the number of staff in U.S. overseas agencies.

	——Demand that world organizations provide loans in currencies other than the U.S. dollar.

Later, if far-reaching measures must be taken to curb the outflow of gold, the U.S. government may:

	——Restrict Americans from traveling to foreign countries.

	——Lower the amount of gold backing for the dollar required by current law.

	——Limit the spending of American travelers overseas.

	——Raise U.S. tariff barriers and take other steps to reduce U.S. imports.

	——Reduce U.S. foreign aid expenditures.

	——Restrict U.S. companies and private individuals from investing overseas.

	——Stop the issuance of foreign securities in the United States.

	——Lower the value of the dollar against gold.

	——Seek to hold a world conference to readjust currency values.

	——Raise interest rates to attract foreign capital into the United States.

	——Withdraw some American troops currently stationed overseas back to the country.

	——Make arrangements to pay U.S. troops stationed overseas with foreign currency.

### 病急乱投药

1960-11-28
第6版()
专栏：

    病急乱投药
    编者按：《美国新闻与世界报道》在11月28日（提前出版）的一期中发表了一篇文章，题目是：《结束黄金外流的步骤——下一步可能发生些什么》。文章谈到了美国当局企图制止黄金外流和国际收支的巨额逆差所已经采取和可能采取的行动。这篇文章表现了在美元病入膏肓之际，美国统治集团病急乱投药的一副狼狈相。现将全文译载如下：
    根据白宫的新命令的美国将采取这些行动：
    ——把大批海外军人家属撤回国内，把在海外家属的人数由484，000减少到200，000人。
    ——使海外军中商店停止购买外国商品。
    ——减少以美援购买外国产品的情况。
    ——减少军方对外国商品的购买。
    ——坚持盟国增加援外费用。
    ——促进输出，加紧对海外的销售，要求外国放宽对美国货物的关税和限制。
    ——使剩余农产品的处理不妨碍美国农产品的正常销售。
    ——减少美国海外机构的工作人员。
    ——要求世界机构以美元以外的其他国家货币进行贷款。
    以后，如果必须采取影响深远的措施来遏止黄金外流的话，美国政府可能：
    ——限制美国人到外国去旅行。
    ——降低现在法律所需要的对美元的黄金支持量。
    ——限制海外的美国旅行者的花费。
    ——提高美国的关税壁垒，采取另外的步骤以减少美国的进口。
    ——减少美国对海外的援助费用。
    ——限制美国的公司和私人在海外的投资。
    ——制止在美国发行外国证券。
    ——降低美元对黄金的价值。
    ——设法举行一次世界性会议，来重新调整货币价值。
    ——提高利率以吸引外国资本投入美国。
    ——将现在驻在海外的某些美国军队撤回国。
    ——作出安排向美国海外军队发给外国货币的军饷。
