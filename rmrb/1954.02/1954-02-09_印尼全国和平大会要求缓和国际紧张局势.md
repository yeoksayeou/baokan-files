Title: Indonesian National Peace Conference Calls for Easing International Tensions
Author:
Date: 1954-02-09
Page: 4

    Xinhua News Agency, Jakarta, 8th: The Indonesian National Peace Conference, which began in Jakarta on January 24th, concluded on January 28th. The conference expressed the desire for peace of all sectors of Indonesian society with its resolutions and circular telegrams based on the principle of negotiated settlement of international disputes.

    "Resolving international disputes through negotiation" and "Indonesia's attitude towards peace and war issues" were the two main agenda items of the conference. Professor Priyono and Professor Poerbodiningrat presented reports on these two issues respectively.

    Three resolutions of the conference were read out at the closing meeting on the evening of January 28th, indicating that the conference supported proposals aimed at easing international tensions. The conference's resolutions called on all countries to develop peaceful socio-economies, promote normal trade relations between countries, develop cultural and educational undertakings, and called on religious figures to engage in peace work.

    In a telegram to the United Nations on January 28th, the conference expressed its demand for the prompt convening of a Korean Political Conference to realize the peace desired by all the people of Korea. The conference also demanded that the United Nations must have the participation of the People's Republic of China, representing a quarter of the world's population, respect Vietnam's right to national self-determination, and grant genuine independence to Malaya in accordance with the wishes of the Malayan people and the UN Charter. Prior to this, on the evening of January 26th, the conference resolved to send a telegram to the Four-Power Foreign Ministers Conference being held in Berlin, advocating the holding of a Five-Power Conference with the participation of the People's Republic of China to better resolve the issue of peaceful coexistence among nations.

    The more than 100 Indonesian representatives attending the conference will bring the conference's determination and spirit to strive for world peace back to all regions of Indonesia.

    Xinhua News Agency, Jakarta, 8th: The President of the Republic of Indonesia, Sukarno, received representatives attending the Indonesian National Peace Conference held in Jakarta and Montague, the representative of the World Peace Council who was invited to attend the conference, on January 29th.

    President Sukarno said during the meeting: "The resolutions of the conference are very valuable and very important, and I will give them deep consideration."

### 印尼全国和平大会要求缓和国际紧张局势

1954-02-09
第4版()
专栏：

    印尼全国和平大会要求缓和国际紧张局势
    【新华社八日讯】雅加达消息：从一月二十四日起在雅加达举行的印尼全国和平大会，已于一月二十八日闭幕。大会以它的基于协商解决国际纠纷这一原则的决议和通电，表达了印尼各界对和平的愿望。
    “通过协商途径解决国际纠纷”和“印尼对于和平与战争问题的态度”是大会的两个主要议程。普里约诺教授和普波迪宁格拉教授分别就这两个问题作了报告。
    在一月二十八日晚闭幕会议上宣读了大会的三项决议案，表明大会拥护目的在于谋求缓和国际紧张局势的主张。大会的决议案呼吁各国发展导向和平的社会经济，促进国际间实现正常贸易关系，发展文化教育事业，并呼吁宗教界人士从事和平工作。
    大会在一月二十八日决议打电报给联合国，表示要求迅速举行朝鲜政治会议以实现朝鲜全体人民所希望的和平。大会并要求：联合国必须有代表全世界四分之一人口的中华人民共和国参加，尊重越南的民族自决权利，按照联合国宪章给予符合马来亚人民所愿望的真正独立。在此以前，大会于一月二十六日晚决议打电报给正在柏林举行的四国外长会议，主张举行有中华人民共和国参加的五大国会议，更好地解决各国间和平共处的问题。
    出席大会的印尼代表一百多人将把大会争取世界和平的决心和精神带回到印尼各个地区去。
    【新华社八日讯】雅加达消息：印度尼西亚共和国总统苏加诺在一月二十九日接见了参加在雅加达举行的印尼全国和平大会的代表，以及应邀参加这次大会的世界和平理事会代表蒙塔古。
    苏加诺总统在接见时说：“大会的决议是很有价值的和非常重要的，我将予以深刻的考虑。”
