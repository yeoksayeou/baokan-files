Title: Finnish Peace Congress Advocates for Resolving International Issues Through Consultation
Author:
Date: 1954-02-26
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, Helsinki, 24th: The Finnish National Peace Congress was held in Helsinki on February 21st. Approximately 1,700 delegates from all walks of life across the country attended the congress. Guests included representatives from the Soviet Peace Committee, as well as peace advocates from Hungary and Sweden.

    Felix Iversen, a member of the World Peace Council, reported on the results of discussions held in Finland regarding the easing of international tensions. He pointed out that the demand to resolve international issues through consultation has received widespread support from all levels of Finnish society. The discussions showed that the vast majority of people believe that the cause of maintaining peace is closely related to them, and they are willing to do everything possible to prevent war. Many gatherings emphasized the importance of strengthening Finnish-Soviet friendship and international friendship. Many gatherings called for the reunification of Germany on a peaceful and democratic basis, the prohibition of weapons of mass destruction, and disarmament. These gatherings pointed out that it would be beneficial to the cause of peace if the People's Republic of China participated in the United Nations. Professor Iversen said that these discussions have expanded the Finnish peace movement.

    Professor Bonsdorff, a member of the Standing Committee of the "Finnish Peace League," gave a report on "International Politics and Peace Issues at the Present Stage." He analyzed the current international situation in detail and emphasized that the call for peace is gaining more and more popular support. He spoke in particular detail about the German question, and expressed opposition to the re-militarization of West Germany.

    During the congress discussions, more than 30 delegates spoke, all of whom warmly supported the demand to resolve international issues through consultation, and expressed their strong desire to do everything in their power to prevent war and promote the establishment of lasting world peace.

    The congress passed a resolution emphasizing that consultation is the only correct way to adjust relations between the peoples of all countries.

### 芬兰和平大会主张通过协商解决国际问题

1954-02-26
第4版()
专栏：

    芬兰和平大会主张通过协商解决国际问题
    【新华社二十四日讯】据塔斯社赫尔辛基讯：芬兰全国和平大会于二月二十一日在赫尔辛基举行。参加大会的有来自全国各地的各界代表约一千七百人。到会的来宾中有苏联拥护和平委员会的代表以及匈牙利和瑞典的和平拥护者代表。
    世界和平理事会理事费利克斯·伊维尔逊在会上报告了芬兰国内就有关缓和国际紧张局势的问题展开的讨论的结果。他指出，要以协商方式解决国际问题的要求得到了芬兰各阶层人民的普遍支持。讨论情况说明，绝大多数人民认为，维护和平的事业与他们有切身的关系，他们愿意尽一切力量防止战争。许多集会强调了加强芬苏友谊与国际友谊的重要性。许多集会要求在和平民主的基础上统一德国，禁止大规模毁灭性的武器，并要求裁减军备。这些集会指出如果让中华人民共和国参加联合国，对和平事业是有好处的。伊维尔逊教授说，这些讨论使芬兰和平运动扩大了。
    “芬兰和平联盟”常务委员会委员邦斯多夫教授就“现阶段的国际政治与和平问题”作了报告。他详细地分析了目前的国际局势，并强调指出，和平的主张愈来愈得人心。他特别详细地谈到了德国问题，并表示反对西德重新军事化。
    在大会讨论过程中有三十多位代表发言，他们都热烈支持通过协商办法解决国际问题的要求，并表示了要用全力防止战争并促进建立世界持久和平的强烈愿望。
    大会通过决议，强调指出协商是调整各国人民关系的唯一正确途径。
