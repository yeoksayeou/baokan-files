Title: People from All Walks of Life in Delhi Hold Grand Meeting to Welcome Premier Zhou
Author:
Date: 1954-06-29
Page: 1
Xinhua News Agency, New Delhi, June 28th: People from all walks of life in Delhi held a grand welcome meeting at 7 p.m. on the 27th to welcome Zhou Enlai, Premier of the State Council of the Central People's Government of the People's Republic of China.

    More than 2,000 people from all walks of life in Delhi, including various professions and religions, participated in the welcome meeting.

    Senior Indian government officials and diplomatic envoys from various countries to India also attended the welcome meeting.

    Yuan Zhongxian, Ambassador of the People's Republic of China to India, also attended the welcome meeting.

    The welcome meeting was held on the lawn outside the Hall of Private Audience in the "Red Fort," the former palace of India. The national flags of the People's Republic of China and the Republic of India were raised on the lawn in front of the hall, and the surrounding trees were decorated with colored electric lights.

    At 6:45 p.m., Prime Minister Nehru went to the Presidential Palace to greet Premier Zhou Enlai and accompany him to the meeting at the "Red Fort." As the two premiers drove in an open car towards the "Red Fort," thousands of Delhi citizens stood along the avenue spanning New Delhi and Delhi, cheering and saluting the two premiers.

    At 7 p.m. sharp, Premier Zhou Enlai, accompanied by Prime Minister Nehru, ascended the Hall of Private Audience. At this moment, all attendees stood up and applauded to welcome them, and the band played the national anthems of the People's Republic of China and the Republic of India. Abdul Karim, Vice Chairman of the Delhi Municipal Committee, then put a garland on Premier Zhou Enlai amidst enthusiastic applause from the audience and presented a gift to Premier Zhou Enlai.

    After the band played Indian folk music, I. Shyam Nath, Chairman of the Delhi Municipal Committee, delivered a speech on behalf of the citizens of Delhi. He pointed out that Premier Zhou Enlai's visit to India has far-reaching significance for Asia and the world. He emphasized the friendship between the people of China and India and congratulated Premier Zhou Enlai on his achievements in the work of striving for peace.

    Premier Zhou Enlai then delivered a reply (full text to be published separately). His speech continuously evoked warm applause from the audience.

    The welcome meeting proceeded in a warm and friendly atmosphere and ended at about 8 p.m.

### 德里各界人民盛会欢迎周总理

1954-06-29
第1版()
专栏：

    德里各界人民盛会欢迎周总理
    【新华社新德里二十八日电】德里各界人民在二十七日下午七时举行盛大的欢迎会，欢迎中华人民共和国中央人民政府政务院总理周恩来。
    参加欢迎会的有：德里各界人民二千多人，包括了各种不同的职业和宗教。
    参加欢迎会的还有印度政府高级官员和各国驻印度使节。
    中华人民共和国驻印度大使袁仲贤也出席了欢迎会。
    欢迎会在印度故宫“红堡”的枢密殿外的草坪上举行。大殿前的草坪上升起了中华人民共和国和印度共和国的国旗，周围的树上都装满了彩色的电灯。
    六时四十五分，尼赫鲁总理到总统府迎接周恩来总理赴会。当两位总理坐着敞篷汽车向“红堡”进发时，横贯新德里与德里的大道上站着成千上万的德里市民，他们向两位总理欢呼致敬。
    七时正，周恩来总理在尼赫鲁总理陪同下登上枢密殿。此时，全体到会者起立鼓掌欢迎，乐队奏起了中华人民共和国国歌和印度共和国国歌。德里市政委员会副主席阿卜杜勒·卡里姆接着在全场热情的掌声中为周恩来总理带上花环并向周恩来总理献礼。
    在乐队奏印度民族音乐后，德里市政委员会主席伊·夏姆·纳特代表德里市民致辞。他指出，周恩来总理的访问印度对亚洲和世界具有深远的意义。他强调中印两国人民的友谊，并祝贺周恩来总理在争取和平的工作中获得成就。
    周恩来总理接着致答辞（全文另发）。他的讲话不断引起全场热烈的鼓掌。
    欢迎会在热烈友好的气氛中进行，到八时余结束。
