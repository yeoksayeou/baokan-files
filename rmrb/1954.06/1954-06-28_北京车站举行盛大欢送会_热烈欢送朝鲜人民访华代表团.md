Title: Grand Farewell Rally Held at Beijing Railway Station: Warm Send-off to the Korean People's Delegation to China
Author:
Date: 1954-06-28
Page: 1

Xinhua News Agency, June 27th: The Korean People's Delegation to China, headed by Kim Ung-gi, and its accompanying art troupe, left Beijing by special train on the morning of June 27th to return home after a three-month visit to China. Ding Xilin, Liu Zhiming, Yi Lirong, and Wu Maosun accompanied the delegation to Andong.

Those who went to the station to see them off included: Chen Shutong, Vice Chairman of the National Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference (CPPCC) and Vice Chairman of the Chinese People's Resist America, Aid Korea General Association; Peng Zhen, Vice Chairman of the Chinese People's Resist America, Aid Korea General Association; standing committee members of the CPPCC National Committee and the Chinese People's Resist America, Aid Korea General Association in Beijing; and responsible persons and representatives of the North China Resist America, Aid Korea General Sub-Association and the Beijing Resist America, Aid Korea Sub-Association.

Also present at the station were: Dong Biwu, member of the Political Bureau of the Central Committee of the Communist Party of China; responsible persons and representatives of various democratic parties and people's organizations across the country.

Those who went to the station to see them off also included: Xi Zhongxun, Secretary-General of the State Council of the Central People's Government; Zhang Hanfu, Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs; heads of various commissions, ministries, bureaus, and academies of the State Council; Huang Kecheng, Deputy Chief of the General Staff of the People's Revolutionary Military Commission; Xiao Hua and Gan Siqi, Deputy Directors of the General Political Department, and heads of various departments; commanders and deputy commanders of various services and arms of the Chinese People's Liberation Army; responsible persons and representatives of the North China Administrative Committee and the Beijing Municipal People's Government; responsible persons of previous Chinese People's Comfort Missions to Korea; representatives of various ethnic groups; prominent figures from cultural, scientific, educational, artistic, journalistic, and religious circles; combat heroes, model workers in industry and agriculture, family members of the Chinese People's Volunteers, and cadres, workers, and students from government organs.

Choi Il, Ambassador of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea to China, and embassy staff also went to the station to see them off.

More than 3,000 people went to the station to see them off.

Beijing Railway Station was filled with a warm and friendly atmosphere. The national flags of China and Korea fluttered in the breeze on the platform. Red gauze lanterns and huge banners decorated with red silk ribbons hung in the corridors. The banners were inscribed in both Korean and Chinese: "Salute to the heroic Korean people!" "Long live the unbreakable friendship between the Chinese and Korean peoples!"

Amidst warm applause and solemn music, the Korean People's Delegation to China, headed by Kim Ung-gi, and all members of the accompanying art troupe entered the station. Young Pioneers of China, dressed in new and beautiful clothes, presented them with flowers. The entire venue immediately erupted again in enthusiastic applause and singing, chanting for a long time: "Long live Marshal Kim Il-sung!" "Long live Chairman Mao Zedong!" "Long live the peace and democracy camp headed by the Soviet Union!"

A grand farewell rally was held at the station.

After the band played the national anthems of China and Korea, Chen Shutong delivered a farewell speech. On behalf of the National Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference (CPPCC) and the Chinese People's Resist America, Aid Korea General Association, as well as the entire Chinese people, he warmly bid farewell to the delegation and expressed heartfelt gratitude for the encouragement the delegation's visit to China had given to the Chinese people. He also asked Kim Ung-gi and all members of the delegation to convey to the Korean people that the Chinese people will continue to struggle alongside the Korean people, the peace and democracy camp headed by the Soviet Union, and all peace-loving people in the world to strive for a peaceful resolution of the Korean issue.

Kim Ung-gi delivered a reply speech, expressing gratitude for the warm welcome the delegation received in China, and stated that after returning home, the delegation would definitely introduce to the Korean people all the great and solemn facts seen and heard in China, in order to further strengthen the friendly unity between the Chinese and Korean peoples, and to inspire the will of the Korean people to devote themselves to the restoration of post-war economic construction and the struggle for the peaceful reunification of the fatherland.

At 9:30, a grand and enthusiastic get-together began on the platform. The people who came to see them off crowded around and embraced the distinguished guests tightly. Numerous dance circles immediately formed on the platform. Guests and hosts held hands and sang and danced enthusiastically together. Many combat heroes and model workers in industry and agriculture from the delegation were lifted high by the people, and many distinguished guests were adorned with red scarves by the Young Pioneers of China. Some Chinese and Korean young girls wiped away tears of farewell for each other. Guests and hosts, with reluctance and attachment, took group photos in small groups, exchanged souvenirs, and signed notebooks as mementos.

At 9:50, to the strains of the "Victory March," amidst a flurry of flowers waving from the train, the train carrying the distinguished guests slowly started to move, and thunderous applause and cheers immediately erupted from the platform. At this moment, Kim Ung-gi and all the deputy heads of the delegation stood on the last carriage of the train and waved goodbye to the send-off party.

### 北京车站举行盛大欢送会  热烈欢送朝鲜人民访华代表团

1954-06-28
第1版()
专栏：

    北京车站举行盛大欢送会
    热烈欢送朝鲜人民访华代表团
    【新华社二十七日讯】以金应基团长为首的朝鲜人民访华代表团和随行艺术团在中国进行了为期三个多月的访问后，已在二十七日上午乘专车离开北京返国。陪送代表团到安东的有丁西林、刘芝明、易礼容、吴茂荪。
    前往车站欢送的有：中国人民政治协商会议全国委员会副主席、中国人民抗美援朝总会副主席陈叔通，中国人民抗美援朝总会副主席彭真，中国人民政治协商会议全国委员会和中国人民抗美援朝总会在京常务委员以及华北抗美援朝总分会、北京市抗美援朝分会负责人和代表。
    到车站欢送的还有：中国共产党中央委员会政治局委员董必武，全国各民主党派、各人民团体负责人和代表。
    到车站欢送的还有：中央人民政府政务院秘书长习仲勋，外交部副部长章汉夫，政务院各委、部、会、院首长，人民革命军事委员会副总参谋长黄克诚，总政治部副主任萧华、甘泗淇以及各部首长，中国人民解放军各兵种司令员、副司令员，华北行政委员会和北京市人民政府负责人和代表，中国人民历届赴朝慰问团负责人，各兄弟民族代表，文化、科学、教育、艺术、新闻、宗教界著名人士，战斗英雄、工农业劳动模范，中国人民志愿军家属和机关干部、工人、学生等。
    朝鲜民主主义人民共和国驻我国大使崔一和使馆人员也到车站欢送。
    到车站欢送的共有三千多人。
    北京车站充满了热烈友好的气氛；朝中两国国旗在月台上迎风招展，走廊上悬挂着红色纱灯和为红色彩绸装饰着的巨幅标语，标语上用朝中两国文字写着：“向英雄的朝鲜人民致敬！”“中朝两国人民牢不可破的友谊万岁！”
    在热烈的掌声和庄严的乐声中，以金应基团长为首的朝鲜人民访华代表团和随行艺术团全体人员进入车站。穿着簇新、美丽服装的中国少年先锋队队员们向他们献花。全场立刻再一次响起了热烈的掌声和歌唱声，长久地高呼：“金日成元帅万岁！”“毛泽东主席万岁！”“以苏联为首的和平民主阵营万岁！”
    在车站上举行了盛大的欢送会。
    在乐队高奏朝中两国国歌后，陈叔通首先致欢送词。他代表中国人民政治协商会议全国委员会和中国人民抗美援朝总会以及全体中国人民热烈欢送代表团，并对代表团来华访问所给予中国人民的鼓舞表示衷心感谢。他并请金应基团长和代表团全体人员转告朝鲜人民，中国人民将和朝鲜人民一起，和以苏联为首的和平民主阵营一起，和全世界爱好和平的人民一起，为争取朝鲜问题的和平解决而继续进行斗争。
    金应基团长致答词，对代表团在中国受到热烈欢迎表示感谢，并表示代表团回国后，一定把在中国所见所闻的一切伟大的庄严的事实介绍给朝鲜人民，以进一步加强中朝两国人民的友好团结，并鼓舞投入恢复战后经济建设和为祖国的和平统一而奋斗的朝鲜人民的意志。
    九时半，在月台上开始了盛大的热烈的联欢。欢送的人们都拥上去和贵宾们紧紧地拥抱着；月台上马上围成了无数舞圈；宾主们手拉着手在一起热情地唱歌、跳舞。代表团中很多战斗英雄、工农业劳动模范被人们高高地抬了起来，很多贵宾的项上都被中国少年先锋队队员们套上了红领巾。有些中国的和朝鲜的年轻姑娘互相替对方擦着惜别的眼泪。宾主们都怀着依恋不舍的心情，三五成群地在一起合影，在互相赠送纪念品，在笔记本上签字留念。
    九时五十分，在“胜利进行曲”的乐声中，在列车上的一片鲜花的挥动中，载着贵宾们的列车徐徐开动了，月台上立即又发出了雷动的掌声和欢呼声。这时，金应基团长和各位副团长都站在列车的最后一节车厢上向欢送者频频挥手告别。
