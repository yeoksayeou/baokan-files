Title: "Pravda" Correspondent in Delhi Reports on Meeting Between Chinese and Indian Prime Ministers
Author:
Date: 1954-06-29
Page: 4
Xinhua News Agency, June 28th Dispatch from Moscow, June 27th, TASS: "Pravda" correspondent in Delhi, Orestov, reports on Premier Zhou Enlai's visit to India.

The correspondent writes: Premier Zhou Enlai received a very warm and cordial welcome from the Indian public. Thousands of residents of the Indian capital went to Delhi Airport early in the morning to welcome Zhou Enlai. This welcome vividly demonstrated the friendship between India and China.

The correspondent said that Indian newspapers pointed out that never before had a foreign political figure received such a warm reception in Delhi.

Referring to the talks between Zhou Enlai and Indian Prime Minister Nehru, the correspondent said: Well-informed journalists pointed out that the talks were "friendly, cordial, and sincere."

Although no information about the content of the talks has been released, Indian newspapers widely commented on and discussed the meeting between the two Prime Ministers. Newspapers deeply hoped that the talks would help solve the most important problems in Asia and ensure universal peace. The newspapers pointed out that at the same time as the talks in Delhi, Eisenhower and Churchill were also holding talks in Washington. The newspapers emphasized the essential differences between these two international events.

The correspondent pointed out that the basis of the Delhi talks was the sincere hope of the peoples of India and China to further consolidate existing friendly relations and seek ways to solve pressing problems in Asia.

The correspondent also emphasized that the talks between Nehru and Zhou Enlai had attracted great attention from other Asian countries.

The correspondent concluded by writing that Indian newspapers particularly pointed out that the talks between Nehru and Zhou Enlai would undoubtedly promote the consolidation of friendship between India and China.

Xinhua News Agency Moscow, June 28th: Moscow newspapers have been carrying news of Premier Zhou Enlai's visit to India for days. On the 26th, newspapers carried news of Premier Zhou Enlai's arrival in New Delhi. On the 27th, newspapers published a TASS report on Premier Zhou Enlai's visit to the Indian President and Vice President, and his talks with Prime Minister Nehru. "Pravda" also published excerpts from the "People's Daily" editorial of the 26th from Beijing. "Pravda" also published a dispatch from its correspondent in New Delhi, describing the situation after Premier Zhou Enlai's arrival in New Delhi.

### “真理报”驻德里记者报道中印两国总理会谈

1954-06-29
第4版()
专栏：

    “真理报”驻德里记者报道中印两国总理会谈
    【新华社二十八日讯】据塔斯社莫斯科二十七日讯：“真理报”驻德里记者奥列斯托夫报道周恩来总理访问印度。
    记者写道：周恩来总理受到印度公众十分热烈和亲切的欢迎。印度首都的数以千计的居民一清早就到德里机场去欢迎周恩来。这次欢迎生动地显示了印中两国之间的友谊。
    记者说，印度报纸指出，从来没有一个外国政治人物在德里受到这样热烈的接待。
    记者在谈到周恩来和印度总理尼赫鲁的会谈时说：消息灵通的记者指出，会谈是“友好的，亲切的，真诚的”。
    虽然没有公布过关于会谈内容的任何消息，但是印度报纸广泛地评论和讨论两位总理的会谈。各报深切地希望，这次会谈将有助于解决亚洲的最重要的问题和保障普遍和平。报纸指出，在和德里举行会谈同时，艾森豪威尔和邱吉尔在华盛顿也在举行会谈。报纸着重指出了这两桩国际事件的本质上的区别。
    记者指出，德里会谈的基础是：印中两国人民真诚希望进一步巩固现有的友好关系，并谋求解决亚洲迫切问题的途径。
    记者还着重指出，尼赫鲁和周恩来的会谈引起了亚洲其他国家的很大的注意。
    记者最后写道，印度报纸特别指出，尼赫鲁和周恩来的会谈无疑地会促进印中两国的友谊的巩固。
    【新华社莫斯科二十八日电】莫斯科各报连日来都刊载了周恩来总理访问印度的消息，二十六日各报刊载了周恩来总理到达新德里的消息。二十七日各报刊登了塔斯社报道周恩来总理拜会印度总统、副总统，并和尼赫鲁总理会谈的消息。“真理报”并刊登了北京“人民日报”二十六日社论的摘要。“真理报”还刊登了它的记者发自新德里的通讯，叙述周恩来总理到达新德里后的情况。
