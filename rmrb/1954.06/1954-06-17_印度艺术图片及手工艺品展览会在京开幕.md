Title: Indian Art Picture and Handicraft Exhibition Opens in Beijing
Author:
Date: 1954-06-17
Page: 3
Xinhua News Agency

    Indian Art Picture and Handicraft Exhibition Opens in Beijing

    The "Indian Art Picture and Handicraft Exhibition," sponsored by the Sino-Indian Friendship Association, opened on the morning of the 15th at the waterside pavilion in Zhongshan Park.

    The exhibition hall was decorated solemnly and beautifully. On the prologue on the front of the exhibition hall hung portraits of Chairman Mao Zedong and President Rajendra Prasad, with the national flags of China and India suspended on either side. The room was filled with flowers.

    Among those attending the opening ceremony of the exhibition were Wu Yuzhang, President of the Renmin University of China, Chen Hansheng, Vice President of the Sino-Indian Friendship Association, Hong Shen, Vice President of the Chinese People's Association for Cultural Relations with Foreign Countries, Chen Zhongjing, Secretary-General, Lao She, Vice Chairman of the Chinese Writers Association, Liu Kaiqu, Vice Chairman of the Chinese Artists Association, Wu Zuoren, and well-known figures from the cultural, educational, and artistic circles.

    Also attending the opening ceremony of the exhibition were Zhang Hanfu, Vice Minister of Foreign Affairs of the Central People's Government, Zheng Zhenduo, Vice Minister of Culture, He Ying, Deputy Director of the Asian Department of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, Wu Han, Deputy Mayor of the Beijing Municipal People's Government, and others.

    Indian Ambassador to China, Mr. Raghunath and embassy officials also attended the opening ceremony. Other foreign guests attending the opening ceremony included: heads of diplomatic missions in China or embassy officials from various countries, and personnel from the British and Dutch Negotiation Delegation Offices.

    The opening ceremony was presided over by Chen Hansheng. He gave a brief introduction to the exhibition, saying that from this exhibition, the scope of contact between Chinese and Indian art is even greater.

    Wu Yuzhang delivered the opening speech. He said that this exhibition is another good opportunity for Chinese audiences to appreciate Indian art since the "Indian Art Exhibition" was exhibited in China in 1952 and the visit of the Indian Art Delegation to China in 1953. He pointed out that with the development of cultural exchanges between the two countries, the friendship between the Chinese and Indian peoples will further develop and consolidate. He also said that from this exhibition, we can see the excellent artistic traditions of the Indian people and the outstanding achievements of Indian artists; at the same time, we can also see that there is a deep and long-standing relationship between ancient Indian art and ancient Chinese art; this shows that there is a long-standing close connection between the two great nations of China and India, and also shows that Sino-Indian cultural exchange plays a significant role in the history of Eastern and world culture. He said that this is inseparable from the long-term peaceful coexistence between the two peoples; this historical fact provides glorious evidence that cultural exchanges between nations are conducive to enhancing mutual understanding and common peaceful interests among the people of all countries. Finally, he wished the exhibition a successful run in Beijing.

    Ambassador Raghunath said in his speech: I should express my thanks and heartfelt praise to the Sino-Indian Friendship Association for being able to participate in the opening of this exhibition. He said that such exhibitions not only contribute to our mutual understanding, but also promote mutual understanding. Finally, he congratulated the Sino-Indian Friendship Association on the success of its work.

    Ambassador Raghunath then cut the ribbon for the exhibition.

    After the ceremony, the guests visited the exhibits.

    Nearly three hundred exhibits were on display at the exhibition, including outstanding pictures of ancient Indian art, exquisite handicrafts, and beautiful art publications. In addition, many of the exhibits were gifts from the Government of the Republic of India, various political parties, and mass organizations to the delegation of the Sino-Indian Friendship Association that previously visited India, vividly illustrating the friendship between the people of the two countries.

### 印度艺术图片及手工艺品展览会在京开幕

1954-06-17
第3版()
专栏：

    印度艺术图片及手工艺品展览会在京开幕
    中印友好协会主办的“印度艺术图片及手工艺品展览会”十五日上午在中山公园水榭开幕。
    展览室布置得庄严、美丽。在展览室里的正面的序幕上挂着毛泽东主席和拉金德拉·普拉沙德总统的画像，中国和印度的国旗分悬在两边。室内布满了鲜花。
    参加展览会开幕仪式的有：中国人民大学校长吴玉章，中印友好协会副会长陈翰笙，中国人民对外文化协会副会长洪深、秘书长陈忠经，中国作家协会副主席老舍，中国美术家协会副主席刘开渠，吴作人以及文化、教育、艺术界著名人士。
    参加展览会开幕仪式的还有：中央人民政府外交部副部长章汉夫，文化部副部长郑振铎，外交部亚洲司副司长何英，北京市人民政府副市长吴晗等。
    印度共和国驻我国大使赖嘉文和使馆官员也参加了开幕仪式。参加开幕仪式的外宾还有：各国驻华使节或使馆官员，英国、荷兰谈判代表办公处人员等。
    开幕仪式由陈翰笙主持。他对展览会作了简要介绍，他说，从这次展览会来看，中国和印度艺术间的接触范围更大了。
    吴玉章致开幕词。他说，这次展览会是继一九五二年的“印度艺术展览会”在中国的展出和一九五三年的印度艺术代表团在中国的访问以来，中国观众欣赏印度艺术的又一个良好的机会。他指出，随着两国文化交流的发展，中印两国人民之间的友谊也将进一步的发展和巩固起来。他又说，从这个展览会里我们可以看到印度人民优秀的艺术传统和印度艺术家们的杰出的成就；同时，还可以看到印度古代艺术与中国古代艺术之间有着深长的渊源关系；这显示着中印两大民族之间存在着悠久的密切联系，也表现着中印文化交流在东方文化与世界文化史上起着重大的作用。他说，这是和两国人民长时期内和平共处的关系分不开的；这个历史事实提供了辉煌的证据：各民族间的文化交流是有助于增进各国人民的相互了解和共同的和平利益的。最后，他谨祝展览会在北京的展出获得成功。
    赖嘉文大使致词说：我能参加这次展览会的开幕，应对中印友好协会致以谢意和衷心的赞扬。他说，这样的展览会不仅在使我们相互认识上，同时在促进彼此之间的谅解上都有一定的贡献。最后，他并祝贺中印友好协会的工作成功。
    展览会继由赖嘉文大使剪彩。
    仪式进行毕，来宾们参观了展览品。
    展览会上展出的展览品近三百件，包括卓越的印度古代艺术图片、精致的手工艺品和优美的艺术出版物。另外，在展览品中也有很多是印度共和国政府、各党派及各人民群众团体赠给前访问印度的中印友好协会代表团的礼品，它生动地说明了两国人民的友谊。　（新华社）
