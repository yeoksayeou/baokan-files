Date: 1960-04-29

Memorandum

    Li Seung-man in the Eyes of the U.S. Ruling Clique

    Editor's Note: Here are excerpts of statements by Eisenhower and others regarding South Korea and Li Seung-man. U.S. imperialism collects the world's most rotten and filthy garbage, such as Li Seung-man, Ngo Dinh Diem, Chiang Kai-shek, Franco, Menderes, and Karamanlis, and treats them as "treasures," supporting and backing them as tools and pillars for implementing its aggressive policies. Li Seung-man is a traitor whom the Korean people hate to the bone, yet Eisenhower calls him a "great patriot." Li Seung-man's rule is the most brutal fascist rule, yet Dulles calls it a "beautiful and attractive place." Now, the South Korean people, having reached the end of their rope, have risen up in resistance. In the flames of the people's anger, Li Seung-man's small court has collapsed. Comparing the actions of the South Korean people with the words of Eisenhower and others, one can only draw the conclusion that U.S. imperialism is the head of all evil, the most vicious gendarme in the capitalist world, and the sworn enemy of all peoples striving for independence, democracy, and peace!

    Eisenhower, in a speech to reporters in Seoul on December 5, 1952, said that Li Seung-man "demonstrated the various talents of a great leader." In a press conference on April 27, 1960, he also said: "Li Seung-man is not only a great man in his country at present and in the past, but also has been a great patriot. I think he is one of those who deserve the title of 'Father of the Nation.'"

    Dulles, speaking at the U.S. State Department on August 1, 1953, at a meeting of ambassadors from fifteen countries participating in the Korean War, said that the U.S. goal was to make South Korea a "most beautiful and attractive place," which would make South Korea "a powerful magnet attracting North Korea."

    In a statement upon arriving in South Korea on March 18, 1956, he said that Li Seung-man was "one of the outstanding figures in the free world."

    Hul, in a statement after visiting South Korea in Seoul on September 18, 1957, said that Li Seung-man was one of the "great anti-communist leaders" in the world.

    Robertson, during a visit to South Korea in July 1953, said: "President Li Seung-man is a... inspiring example to the world of determination, courage and fortitude."

    Wiley (former chairman of the U.S. Senate Foreign Relations Committee) said on July 28, 1954, in praising Li Seung-man's warmongering rhetoric: Li Seung-man is "a great patriot" who is "filled with American ideals."

    Judd (U.S. Republican Congressman) told reporters on April 27, 1960, that Li Seung-man "is one of the bravest freedom fighters in the world."

    Carlson (U.S. Senator) said on the eve of Li Seung-man's "resignation" that Li Seung-man was "a great symbol of freedom and a leader of a democratic form of government."

    Hickenlooper (U.S. Representative to the United Nations General Assembly) said on November 5, 1958, at a meeting of the Political Committee of the UN General Assembly discussing the Korean issue: "The United Nations Commission for the Unification and Rehabilitation of Korea has repeatedly demonstrated that the democratic system of the Republic of Korea (South Korea) continues to grow and develop." "In the Republic of Korea, there is freedom of expression."

    Greenbaum (U.S. Representative to the United Nations General Assembly) said on January 4, 1957, at a meeting of the Political Committee of the UN General Assembly discussing the Korean issue: In South Korea, "work has been underway to deepen the foundations of democracy." In South Korea, "there is good indication of free elections."

### 美国统治集团眼里的李承晚

1960-04-29
第5版()
专栏：备忘录

    美国统治集团眼里的李承晚
    编者按：这里摘录了艾森豪威尔等关于南朝鲜和李承晚的一些言论。美帝国主义把世界上最臭、最肮脏的垃圾，如李承晚、吴庭艳、蒋介石、佛朗哥、曼德列斯、卡拉曼利斯之流，一律搜集起来，当作“宝贝”，加以扶植、支持，作为它推行侵略政策的工具和支柱。李承晚是朝鲜人民痛恨入骨的卖国贼，艾森豪威尔却称他为“伟大的爱国者”；李承晚的统治是最残暴的法西斯统治，杜勒斯却称他为“美丽的诱人的地方”。现在，忍无可忍的南朝鲜人民已奋起反抗，在人民的怒火中，李承晚小朝廷土崩瓦解。把南朝鲜人民的行动，同艾森豪威尔等的言论对照一下，人们只能得出这样的结论：美帝国主义是万恶之首，是资本主义世界中最凶恶的宪兵，是争取独立、民主和和平的各国人民的死敌！
    艾森豪威尔1952年12月5日在汉城对记者发表谈话说：李承晚“表现了一个伟大领袖的各种才能”。在1960年4月27日记者招待会上他又说：“李承晚不仅在目前和过去是他的国家里的一个伟人，而且还曾经是一个伟大的爱国者。我认为，他是当得起‘国父’称号的人之一。”
    杜勒斯1953年8月1日在美国国务院向参加侵朝战争的十五国大使会谈时说：美国的目标是使南朝鲜成为一个“最美丽诱人的地方”，这将使南朝鲜成为“吸引北朝鲜的一个强有力的磁极”。
    他在1956年3月18日到达南朝鲜时发表声明说，李承晚是“自由世界杰出人物之一”。
    赫脱1957年9月18日在汉城发表访问南朝鲜的声明中说：李承晚是世界上“伟大的反共领袖”之一。
    罗伯逊1953年7月访问南朝鲜时说：“李承晚总统是一个……向世界提供了决心、勇敢和刚毅的令人鼓舞的榜样。”
    维利（曾任美国参议院外交委员会主席）1954年7月28日在赞扬李承晚好战叫嚣时说：李承晚是“一位伟大的爱国者”，他“充满了美国人的理想”。
    周以德（美国共和党众议员）1960年4月27日对记者说，李承晚“是世界上最勇敢的自由战士之一”。
    卡尔逊（美国参议员）在李承晚“辞职”前夕说，李承晚是“一个伟大的自由的象征，并且是一个民主形式的政府的领袖”。
    希肯鲁普（美国出席联合国大会的代表）1958年11月5日在联大政委会会议上讨论朝鲜问题时说：“联合国朝鲜统一复兴委员会一再证明，大韩民国（南朝鲜）的民主制度在继续成长和发展。”“在大韩民国有发表意见的自由。”
    格林博姆（美国出席联合国大会的代表）1957年1月4日在联大政委会上讨论朝鲜问题时说：在南朝鲜，“加深民主基础的工作一直在进行”。在南朝鲜“有自由选举的良好表示”。
