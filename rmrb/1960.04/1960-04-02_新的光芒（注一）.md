Title: A New Radiance (Note 1)
Author: Alek Çaçi
Date: 1960-04-02
Page: 8

    A New Radiance (Note 1)
    〔Albania〕Alek Çaçi
    Clouds drift in the sky.
    From the small window of the hospital,
    I see the world
    will become so beautiful!
    Not far from here on the streets,
    In those ordinary houses
    fluttering red flags,
    People are building communism.
    The era of "Thousands of homes deserted, ghosts sing" (Note 2)
    will never return.
    In China,
    even winter becomes like spring.
    People no longer say: "I"
    but say: "We".
    Not far from here on the streets,
    In those ordinary houses,
    I see
    a new radiance is shining.

    (Note 1) This poem was written by the author after learning about how Chinese cities organize people's economic life and after visiting street work.
    (Note 2) Quoted from Chairman Mao's poem "Farewell to the God of Plague."
  (Translated by Li Ninglai, Proofread by Ge Baoquan)

### 新的光芒（注一）
阿列克斯·恰奇
1960-04-02
第8版()
专栏：

    新的光芒（注一）
    〔阿尔巴尼亚〕阿列克斯·恰奇
    白云在天空中飘荡。
    从医院的小窗，
    我看到世界
    将会变得多么美丽漂亮！
    在这儿不远的街道上，
    在那些飘拂着红旗的
    平凡的房子里，
    人们正在建设共产主义。
    “万户萧疏鬼唱歌”（注二）的时代，
    从此一去永不复返。
    在中国，
    冬天也变得像春天一样。
    人们不再说：“我”
    而是说：“我们”。
    在离这儿不远的街道上，
    在那些平凡的房子里，
    我看到
    正闪耀着新的光芒。
    （注一）这首诗是作者在了解了中国城市组织人民经济生活和访问了街道工作之后写成的。
    （注二）引自毛主席的“送瘟神”一诗。
  （李宁来译　戈宝权校）
