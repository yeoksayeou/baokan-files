Title: A Flower on the Atlantic: Recited at the "Poetry and Art Gathering in Support of the Cuban People"
Author: Yuan Shuipai
Date: 1960-04-23
Page: 8

    A Flower on the Atlantic
    Recited at the "Poetry and Art Gathering in Support of the Cuban People"
    Yuan Shuipai
    A storm rises in the Caribbean Sea,
    A heroic island stands amidst the white waves,
    Looking down on the paper tiger with its head held high,
    The Cuban people have soaring aspirations!
    With fists they strike down the traitors,
    And kick away the Yankee imperialists,
    Speaking out for the people of Latin America,
    And pointing out the right path for all colonies.
    Cuba! Cuba!
    A flower on the Atlantic!
    The revolutionary flower blooms for thousands of miles,
    The enemy grinds his teeth, loved ones praise!
    Though the road is long, our hearts are connected,
    Beijing—Havana!
    The flames in the sugarcane fields,
    Are like burning in our own home!
    May the words of the poets be forged into swords,
    To help you shoot down the iron crows in the sky.
    Viva glorious revolution!
    Viva heroic Cuba!
      [Note] Viva means "long live" in Spanish.

### 大西洋上一枝花  在“支援古巴人民诗歌文艺会”上朗诵
袁水拍
1960-04-23
第8版()
专栏：支援古巴人民

    大西洋上一枝花
    在“支援古巴人民诗歌文艺会”上朗诵
    袁水拍
    加勒比海起风暴，
    白浪里站起了英雄岛，
    昂首小看纸老虎，
    古巴人民志气高！
    挥拳打倒卖国贼，
    一脚踢开花旗佬，
    为拉丁美洲人民吐气，
    为一切殖民地指出了阳关道。
    古巴！古巴！
    大西洋上一枝花！
    革命花开香万里，
    敌人咬牙亲人夸！
    路虽远，心相通，
    北京——哈瓦那！
    甘蔗田里的火焰，
    就像烧在我们自己的家！
    愿诗人们的语言锻成剑，
    支援你们击落天空的铁老鸦。
    Viva　光荣的革命！
    Viva　英雄的古巴！
      〔注〕Viva　西班牙语“万岁”之意。
