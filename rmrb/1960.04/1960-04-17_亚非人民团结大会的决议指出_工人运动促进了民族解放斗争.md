Title: Afro-Asian People's Solidarity Conference Resolution Points Out that the Workers' Movement Promoted National Liberation Struggles
Author:
Date: 1960-04-17
Page: 5
　
Xinhua News Agency, Conakry, April 15th: The resolution on social issues adopted by the Second Afro-Asian People's Solidarity Conference pointed out that the upsurge of the national liberation movement and the development of the Afro-Asian solidarity movement have had a tremendous impact on the social development of the Afro-Asian countries.

The resolution, in discussing the development of Afro-Asian social (welfare) undertakings since the First Afro-Asian People's Solidarity Conference, stated that there has been vigorous development in youth movements and in activities for women and children. The broad masses of Afro-Asian workers have been playing an increasingly prominent role in the struggle to defend peace and oppose imperialism; particularly encouraging is the development of the Afro-Asian workers' movement, which has greatly promoted the national liberation movement. In addressing the issues of women and children, the resolution called for the realization of equal rights for men and women and the granting of political rights to women. In mentioning youth welfare issues, the resolution decided to establish Afro-Asian youth camps and encourage mutual visits between Afro-Asian youth, hold Afro-Asian youth conferences, and support the African Youth Festival to be held in Conakry in 1961.

The resolution, in mentioning workers' issues, suggested that governments of all countries establish the basic, democratic rights of labor on a legal basis. The resolution supports the establishment of the All-African Trade Union Federation. The resolution called for the distribution of land to landless agricultural workers, the full utilization of resources to eliminate unemployment, and the improvement of the living standards of the working class. The resolution also called on the governments of Afro-Asian countries to guarantee the right of workers to establish their own trade unions, implement equal pay for equal work, and refrain from wage discrimination based on race or religion.

The resolution on cultural relations adopted by the conference called on Afro-Asian countries to take measures to develop and promote cultural undertakings, and called on the people of Afro-Asian countries to condemn colonialist destructive activities carried out in the name of education.

### 亚非人民团结大会的决议指出  工人运动促进了民族解放斗争

1960-04-17
第5版()
专栏：

    亚非人民团结大会的决议指出
    工人运动促进了民族解放斗争
    据新华社科纳克里15日电　第二届亚非人民团结大会通过的关于社会问题的决议指出，民族解放运动的高涨和亚非团结运动的发展，对亚非国家人民的社会发展产生了巨大的影响。
    决议在谈到自从第一届亚非人民团结大会以来的亚非社会（福利事业）的发展时说，在青年运动以及妇女和儿童活动方面已经出现了蓬勃的发展。广大的亚非工人群众在保卫和平、反对帝国主义的斗争中一直起着越来越显著的作用；特别令人鼓舞的是亚非工人运动的发展，大大地促进了民族解放运动。决议在谈到妇女和儿童问题时，要求实现男女平等权利和给妇女以政治权利。决议在提到青年福利问题时决定建立亚非青年营和鼓励亚非青年相互访问，举行亚非青年会议，支持将于1961年在科纳克里举行的非洲青年节。
    决议在提到工人问题时，建议各国政府在法律的基础上确定劳工的基本的、民主的权利。决议支持成立全非工会联合会。决议要求把土地分配给无地的农业工人，充分利用资源以便消灭失业和提高工人阶级的生活水平。决议还号召亚非各国政府保证工人建立自己的工会的权利，实行同工同酬，不得由于种族或宗教原因在工资方面实行歧视。
    大会通过的关于文化关系的决议，号召亚非国家采取措施来发展和推广文化事业，并且号召亚非各国人民谴责以教育为名而进行殖民主义的破坏活动。
