Title: Plains (with two other poems)
Author: Bao Minglu
Date: 1957-12-19
Page: 8

　　Plains (with two other poems)
　　Bao Minglu
　　The plains are as boundless as the sea!
　　The hills and mounds are its undulating waves;
　　The scattered villages,
　　Are like specks of water splashed from its embrace.
　　The sun here is perfectly round and golden,
　　Hanging on the tops of the towering poplars,
　　Like a declaration of war,
　　Arousing our passion for every workday.
　　The moon here is exquisite and crystal clear,
　　A few white clouds like sails,
　　Carrying it, and also carrying our dreams,
　　Sailing into the distance every night;
　　And the piles of grain stacks here,
　　Head to tail, tail to head,
　　Like a fleet of ships,
　　Protecting the prosperity and strength of the motherland.
　　Ah! Plains, plains,
　　When we leave our narrow homes,
　　When we emerge from ink bottles and piles of books,
　　When the hands accustomed to holding pens take up hoes,
　　Your complete richness and magnificence of color,
　　Are then presented to us without reservation.

　　Shepherdess
　　She takes off her embroidered shirt and puts on a blue cloth jacket,
　　Setting up a home in a small mountain village;
　　The shepherd's whip is her usual chalk,
　　The blue sky is just the right transparent blackboard.
　　This group of new students is so docile and kind,
　　After eating their fill of grass, they cuddle at her feet;
　　And the girl is also a new student!
　　She is entering the classroom of labor for the first time.
　　Raising her whip, facing the cooking smoke,
　　Her songs are imprinted on the winding paths;
　　Life has woven countless songs for her,
　　Her heart has become a warehouse full of songs.

　　Work Point Ledger
　　There is no music more lively than this,
　　When the sickle cuts off the wheat stalks with a "嚓嚓" sound;
　　There is no moment more joyous than this,
　　When the work point ledger records a day's harvest.
　　A number, a handful of sweat,
　　Behind the number hides our pride;
　　Work point ledger, this is a red passport,
　　Holding it, we can travel the world without shame.

### 平原（外二首）
鲍明路
1957-12-19
第8版()
专栏：来自农村的诗

　　平原（外二首）
　　鲍明路
    平原如大海样无边无涯！
    山坡土岗是它起伏的波浪；
    星罗棋布的村庄，
    是从它怀里激起的点点水花。
    这里的太阳浑圆金黄，
    挂在挺立的白杨树梢，
    像是一封战书，
    挑起我们对每个劳动日的激情
    这里的月亮玲珑晶莹，
    几片白云如帆，
    载着它，也载着我们的梦，
    夜夜驶向远方；
    而这里的堆堆谷垛，
    头衔尾，尾衔头，
    仿佛一列列舰队，
    保卫着祖国的繁荣富强。
    呵！平原，平原，
    当我们离开了窄小的家，
    当我们钻出了墨水瓶和书堆，
    当我们握惯笔杆的手握起锄把，
    你全部色彩的丰富瑰丽，
    才无遗地呈献在我们面前。
    牧羊姑娘
    脱去绣花衫，穿上蓝布褂，
    小小山村里安下个家；
    牧鞭是她往日常用的粉笔，
    蓝天恰好是透明的黑板。
    这一群新学生多么驯服善良，
    饱食青草后依偎在她的脚旁；
    而姑娘也是个新学生呵！
    她第一次走进劳动的课堂。
    举起牧鞭，迎着炊烟，
    歌声刻印在羊肠小路上；
    生活呵为她编织了无数歌曲，
    她的心成了满装歌曲的库房。
    工分簿
    再没有比这更轻快的音乐，
    当镰刀嚓嚓地割断麦杆；
    再没有比这更喜悦的时辰，
    当工分簿记上一天的收获。
    一个数字，一掬汗珠，
    数字后面隐藏着我们的自豪；
    工分簿，这是红色的护照，
    拿着它走遍天涯再不用害臊。
