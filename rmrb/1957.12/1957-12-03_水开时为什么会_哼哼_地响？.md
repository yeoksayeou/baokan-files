Author: Dong Yi
Date: 1957-12-03
Page: 8

Why Does Water "Hum" When It Boils?

　　When water boils, a large number of vapor bubbles rise from the bottom of the kettle. When a bubble rises, it inevitably squeezes the surrounding water, and the water will inevitably hit the kettle wall. Although this is a very small impact, there are many bubbles, so the force of hitting the kettle wall is not small. When the kettle is subjected to this impact, it will vibrate, so the air around the kettle also vibrates. This vibration, transmitted to the human eardrum, becomes a "humming" sound. (Dong Yi)

### 水开时为什么会“哼哼”地响？
董鷾
1957-12-03
第8版()
专栏：为什么

　　水开时为什么会“哼哼”地响？
    当水开的时候，茶壶底里有大量的汽泡往上升。一个汽泡升上来时，必定要挤一下四周的水，而水又必然会撞一下壶壁。虽然这是很小的撞击，但是汽泡很多，撞击壶壁的力量也就不小了。茶壶受到这种撞击后，就要振动，因此壶周围的空气也振动。这种振动，传到人的耳膜里就成为“哼哼”的声音。　（董鷾）
