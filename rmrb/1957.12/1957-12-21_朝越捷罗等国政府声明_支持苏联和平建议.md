Title: North Korean, Vietnamese, Czechoslovakian, and Romanian Governments Declare Support for Soviet Peace Proposals
Author:
Date: 1957-12-21
Page: 5
Xinhua News Agency dispatches: The peace initiatives proposed by Soviet Chairman of the Council of Ministers Bulganin in his letters and notes to Western countries and member states of the United Nations, regarding ending the Cold War, halting the arms race, and ceasing nuclear weapons testing starting next year, have been welcomed and supported by the governments of North Korea, Vietnam, Czechoslovakia, Romania, and other countries.

    The Government of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea, in a statement issued on the 16th, pointed out that the Soviet government's proposals are in line with the interests of world peace and contribute to maintaining peace in Korea and peacefully resolving the Korean issue. The Government of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea fully supports and agrees with these proposals of the Soviet government. If the United States and Britain accept the Soviet government's proposals, expressing their sincere attitude towards peace, the most acute international problems can be resolved.
    The Government of the Democratic Republic of Vietnam issued a statement stating that the practical and reasonable measures proposed by the Soviet government are a strong guarantee for world peace and security. These measures will lead to mutual trust between countries, halt the "Cold War," and become the basis for resolving the disarmament issue. The statement said that the Soviet government's proposals fully meet the urgent demands of peace-loving people around the world.
    The Czechoslovak government issued a statement on the 14th, stating that the Czechoslovak people fully accept and actively support the Soviet proposals. The statement said that if the major powers stop storing nuclear weapons on the territory of the Federal Republic of Germany and the German Democratic Republic, and if the two German states reach an agreement on not producing nuclear weapons and not storing such weapons in Germany, then Czechoslovakia will voluntarily undertake the same obligations.
    The Romanian government issued a statement on the 18th regarding the Soviet government's note of December 12 to all member states of the United Nations, stating that the Romanian government believes that, in order to prevent war and to promote mutual understanding and the development of friendly relations between countries, urgent measures must be taken on the proposals put forward in the Soviet government's note. The statement expressed full support for the Soviet government's proposals for preventing a new war and maintaining peace, and will work towards its realization.

### 朝越捷罗等国政府声明  支持苏联和平建议

1957-12-21
第5版()
专栏：

    朝越捷罗等国政府声明
    支持苏联和平建议
    本报综合报道  据新华社讯：苏联部长会议主席布尔加宁给西方国家和联合国各会员国的信件和照会中所提出的关于结束冷战、停止军备竞赛以及从明年起停止试验核武器等和平倡议，受到朝、越、捷、罗等国政府的欢迎和支持。
    朝鲜民主主义人民共和国政府在16日发表声明指出，苏联政府的这些建议符合于世界和平的利益，并且有助于维护在朝鲜的和平局面以及和平解决朝鲜问题。朝鲜民主主义人民共和国政府完全支持和赞同苏联政府的这些建议。如果美国和英国接受苏联政府的建议，表示它们对和平的真诚态度，目前最尖锐的国际问题是可以得到解决的。
    越南民主共和国政府发表声明指出，苏联政府提出的切实、合理的措施，是对世界和平和安全的有力保证。这些措施将导致各国之间的互相信任，制止“冷战”，并且成为解决裁军问题的基础。声明说，苏联政府的建议完全符合世界爱好和平人民的迫切要求。
    捷克斯洛伐克政府14日发表声明表示，捷克斯洛伐克人民完全接受而且积极支持苏联的这些建议。声明说，如果大国停止在德意志联邦共和国和德意志民主共和国的领土上储存核武器，如果两个德国就不生产核武器和不在德国储存这些武器问题达成协议，那么，捷克斯洛伐克自愿承担同样的义务。
    罗马尼亚政府18日就苏联政府12月12日给联合国全体会员国的照会发表声明说，罗马尼亚政府认为，为了防止战争，为了使各国互相谅解和发展友好关系，必须就苏联政府照会提出的建议采取紧急措施。声明表示完全支持苏联政府为防止新战争和维护和平而提出的建议，并且将为它的实现而努力。
