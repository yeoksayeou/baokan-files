Title: Syria, Lebanon, Jordan and other countries warn the Paris Conference not to interfere in the Palestinian issue

Author:

Date: 1957-12-17
Page: 5

(Comprehensive report by this newspaper) Xinhua News Agency, December 16th: French sources revealed that the NATO government leaders' meeting will not only discuss issues within the group, but also issues related to North Africa and Asia, including the situation in Indonesia and the possibility of a "small war breaking out" in the Middle East.

The NATO Paris Conference's plot to interfere in the Middle East situation, especially the Palestinian issue, has aroused protests from Arab countries.

Syrian Foreign Minister Bitar stated on the 15th that the Palestinian issue is a matter for the Palestinians and all Arabs, and "the Arabs will not agree to let any foreign group discuss this issue, or allow such a group to use this issue in international disputes to achieve its own goals."

The statement also said that the attempts by some Arab figures, together with Western countries, to plan a solution to the Palestinian issue for NATO will inevitably fail.

According to Beirut newspapers, Lebanese President Chamoun recently wrote to US President Eisenhower, requesting that the NATO meeting not make any decisions on the Palestinian issue without prior consultation with Arab countries.

It is pointed out that he made this request because of rumors that Britain intends to propose modifying Israel's borders at the NATO meeting.

A Jordanian Foreign Ministry spokesperson said on the 15th that Arab countries will not consider any resolution on Arab issues passed by the NATO summit in Paris without consultation with Arab countries to be binding.

The spokesperson said that the Jordanian government is particularly concerned about the discussion of Middle East issues, especially Arab issues, at the NATO meeting. Jordanian King Hussein recently exchanged letters with Lebanese President Chamoun, Saudi Arabian King Saud, and Iraqi King Faisal on this issue.

### 叙黎约等国警告巴黎会议  不许干涉巴勒斯坦问题

1957-12-17
第5版()
专栏：

    叙黎约等国警告巴黎会议
    不许干涉巴勒斯坦问题
    本报综合报道　据新华社16日讯：法国人士透露，北大西洋集团政府首脑会议将不仅讨论集团范围内的问题，而且还要讨论有关北非及亚洲的问题，其中包括印度尼西亚局势和中东“爆发小型战争”的问题。
    北大西洋集团巴黎会议企图干涉中东局势特别是巴勒斯坦问题的阴谋引起了阿拉伯国家的抗议。
    叙利亚外交部长比塔尔15日发表声明说，巴勒斯坦问题是巴勒斯坦人和全体阿拉伯人的事情，“阿拉伯人不会同意由任何外国集团来讨论这个问题，或者让这种集团在国际争端中利用这个问题来达到它的目的”。
    声明还说，某些阿拉伯人士同西方国家一起企图为北大西洋集团策划巴勒斯坦问题的解决办法，这种企图一定会遭到失败。
    据贝鲁特报纸报道，黎巴嫩总统夏蒙最近曾经写信给美国总统艾森豪威尔，要求北大西洋公约组织会议不要在没有事先同阿拉伯国家磋商就对巴勒斯坦问题作出任何决定。
    据指出，他所以提出这个要求，是由于传闻英国打算在北大西洋公约组织会议上建议修改以色列边界。
    约旦外交部发言人15日说，在巴黎举行的北大西洋公约组织最高级会议在没有同阿拉伯国家磋商的情况下所通过的关于阿拉伯问题的任何决议，阿拉伯国家将不认为具有约束力。
    发言人说，约旦政府对于北大西洋公约组织会议上所要进行的关于中东问题、特别是阿拉伯问题的讨论格外关切。约旦国王侯赛因跟黎巴嫩总统夏蒙、沙特阿拉伯国王沙特和伊拉克国王费萨尔最近曾经就这一问题交换过信件。
