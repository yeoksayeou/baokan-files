Title: Students Seek Degrees, Professors Don't Teach
Author:
Date: 1957-12-08
Page: 6

An American named Theodore Klibenger said: American society respects university students' degrees rather than their knowledge, and professors have reached the point of "not teaching."

Klibenger pointed out in an article in "The Messenger Magazine" entitled "University Professors Don't Teach": "Since the end of World War II, the focus of American universities has shifted from university education to university degrees. For an increasing number of job opportunities, a bachelor's, master's, or doctoral degree is equivalent to an honorary trade union membership card. With a degree, one is qualified for a job, otherwise there is no hope at all."

The author said: "College students recognize this, they know that the important thing is a degree, they are simply biding their time until graduation." He believes that "our colleges and universities are no longer worthy of the trust of parents," "because as a whole, university professors are not teaching."

(Xinhua News Agency)

### 学生图学位　教授不教书

1957-12-08
第6版()
专栏：美国黑幕

　　学生图学位　教授不教书
    一个名叫西奥多·克利文格的美国人说：美国社会上尊重大学生的学位而不是学问，教授们也已经到了“不教书”的地步。
    克利文格在“信使杂志”的一篇题为“大学教授不教书”的文章中指出：“自从第二次世界大战结束以来，美国大学的重点已经从大学教育转到大学学位。对于越来越多的工作机会说来，学士、硕士或博士学位就等于是一种荣誉工会会员证。有了学位，就有工作资格，否则就根本没有希望。”
    作者说：“大学生们认识到这一点，他们知道要紧的是学位，他们简直就是在挨时间等毕业。”他认为“我们的学院和大学已经不配获得做父母的人的信任”，“因为作为一个整体来说，大学教授没有教书”。
                        （新华社）
