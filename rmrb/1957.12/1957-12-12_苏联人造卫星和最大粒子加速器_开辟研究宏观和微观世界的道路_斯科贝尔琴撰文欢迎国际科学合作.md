Title: Soviet Artificial Satellites and Largest Particle Accelerator: Opening Paths to the Study of the Macroscopic and Microscopic Worlds; Skobeltsyn Writes to Welcome International Scientific Cooperation
Author:
Date: 1957-12-12
Page: 5
Xinhua News Agency, Moscow, December 11th: Academician Skobeltsyn, Director of the Institute of Physics of the Soviet Academy of Sciences, recently stated in an article written for the United Press International that the successful launch of the world's first and second artificial satellites made by the Soviet Union, and the commissioning of the Soviet-made largest particle accelerator – the synchrophasotron – have opened up paths for studying two "extreme" worlds: one is the unimaginably small world of space and time – the microcosm; the other is the inconceivably vast universe. No matter how far apart these two poles are, they are truly important parts of a whole.

Skobeltsyn pointed out that the commissioning of the synchrophasotron in Dubna, near Moscow, is a huge milestone in the history of science. This accelerator is the only one of its kind in the world, and its scale and energy surpass those of two similar pieces of equipment used in the United States.

The particles accelerated by this accelerator are, in effect, artificial cosmic rays. Cosmic rays are a stream of particles originating from somewhere deep in cosmic space, accelerated to astonishing speeds. Physicists have long been using these few natural high-energy particles to study the phenomena of the microscopic world. Now, with this giant accelerator as a source of artificial cosmic rays on Earth, it provides the possibility of meticulously studying various phenomena of the microscopic world.

Analyzing the role of artificial satellites in studying the universe, Skobeltsyn pointed out that cosmic rays can be said to be the precursors or vanguards of processes occurring in the distant parts of cosmic space. In-depth study of these original cosmic rays can bring science the most valuable information about the origins of these rays; cosmic rays can tell us what happens during their long journey through the universe, which takes hundreds of millions of years.

Observations made with instruments in the atmosphere are very incomplete, because even the thin layer of the atmosphere between those instruments and cosmic space distorts the entire picture in many ways. Satellites, by traversing the atmosphere and entering cosmic space, open up entirely different new frontiers for conducting cosmic research. Preliminary observational materials of cosmic rays obtained with instruments in the second satellite have already been received and will soon be decoded for study.

In addition to these problems, obtaining information about changes occurring on the sun and phenomena related to these processes will be very interesting. This material can be obtained from the study of cosmic rays, solar ultraviolet rays, and soft X-rays, and observations of these things can only be carried out by artificial satellites outside the Earth's atmosphere.

Skobeltsyn said that past and future observations of cosmic rays and other rays in cosmic space are only the beginning and merely a part of a broad scientific research program.

In the future, perhaps in the near future, we will be able to conduct experiments on some physical problems of fundamental importance, such as Einstein's principle of general relativity.

Skobeltsyn finally expressed the hope that artificial satellites will also open a new era in the development of international cooperation among scientists.

### 苏联人造卫星和最大粒子加速器  开辟研究宏观和微观世界的道路  斯科贝尔琴撰文欢迎国际科学合作

1957-12-12
第5版()
专栏：

    苏联人造卫星和最大粒子加速器
    开辟研究宏观和微观世界的道路
    斯科贝尔琴撰文欢迎国际科学合作
    新华社莫斯科11日电　苏联科学院物理研究所所长斯科贝尔琴院士最近在为合众社写的一篇文章中说，苏联制成的世界上第一个和第二个人造卫星的发射成功，苏联制造的世界上最大的粒子加速器——同步稳相加速器的启用，为研究两个“极端”的世界开辟了道路，一个是小得不可想像的空间和时间的世界——微观世界，一个是大得不可思议的宇宙。不管这两个极相距多远，它们却是一个整体的真正重要的两个部分。
    斯科贝尔琴指出，设在莫斯科近郊杜布诺的同步稳相加速器的启用是科学史上一个巨大的里程碑，这个加速器是全世界唯一的一座，这台新的巨大的加速器的规模和能量都超过了美国使用的两台类似装备。
    这座加速器所加速的粒子实际上就是人造宇宙线。宇宙线是一种来自宇宙空间深处某些地方被加速到具有惊人速度的粒子流。物理学家很久以来一直是用这种为数不多的自然的高能量粒子来研究微观世界的现象的。现在有了这种巨大加速器作为地球上人造宇宙线源泉，就提供了细致研究微观世界各种现象的可能性。
    斯科贝尔琴在分析人造卫星对研究宇宙的作用时指出，宇宙射线可以说是在宇宙空间的遥远部分发生的过程的先驱或前哨。深入地研究这种原始宇宙线，能够给科学带来有关这些射线起源的最宝贵的情报，宇宙线能告诉我们，在通过宇宙的需时几万万年的长途旅行中，有些什么情况。
    用大气中的仪器来进行的观察，是很不完整的，因为那怕是那些仪器和宇宙空间之间隔着较稀薄的大气层，也会在很多方面歪曲了整个景象。卫星由于穿过大气进入宇宙空间，因而打开了进行宇宙研究的完全不同的新境界。用第二个卫星中的仪器对宇宙线进行的初步观察材料已经收到了，并且不久将译解出来进行研究。
    除了这些问题之外，获得太阳上发生的变化以及和这些过程有关的现象的情报将是很有意思的。可以从对宇宙线、太阳紫外线和软X射线的研究获得这种材料，而对于这些东西的观察只能靠人造卫星在地球大气层以外进行。
    斯科贝尔琴说，过去和将来对于宇宙线和宇宙空间中的其他射线的观察，只不过是一个广泛的科学研究计划的一个开端，并且仅仅是一个部分。
    将来，可能是不久的将来，我们就能够对一些具有根本性重要意义的物理问题，如爱因斯坦的广义相对论原理，进行试验。
    斯科贝尔琴最后表示，希望人造卫星也会在科学家们国际合作的发展中打开新的纪元。
