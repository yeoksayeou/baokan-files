Title: To a Burmese Friend
Author: Chen Yi
Date: 1957-12-17
Page: 8

　　To a Burmese Friend
　　Chen Yi
　　I live at the river's head,
　　You live at the river's tail,
　　Our affection is boundless,
　　Together we drink the river's water.
　　I draw from the upper reaches,
　　You drink from the lower reaches,
　　The river flows unceasingly,
　　Together we share its sweetness.
　　We are close neighbors,
　　Friendship grows with time,
　　Evergreen like the mountains,
　　Unending like the flowing stream.
　　Our lands are connected,
　　Leaning on mountains, relying on water,
　　Liberated from imperialism,
　　On the same track of peace.
　　We are like relatives,
　　With much language in common,
　　United and mutually helpful,
　　Our peaceful power is great.
　　Gazing at the vast water,
　　Singing of the towering rocks,
　　The mountains all seem to face north,
　　Every stream flows south.
　　　　　　December 14, 1957

### 赠缅甸友人
陈毅
1957-12-17
第8版()
专栏：

　　赠缅甸友人
　　陈毅
    我住江之头，
    你住江之尾，
    彼此情无限，
    共饮一江水。
    我吸川上流，
    你喝川下水，
    川流去不息，
    彼此共甘美。
    彼此为近邻，
    友谊长积累，
    不老如青山，
    不断似流水。
    彼此地相联，
    依山复靠水，
    反帝获自由，
    和平同一轨。
    彼此是亲戚，
    语言多同轨，
    团结而互助，
    和平力量伟。
    临水叹浩淼，
    登山歌石磊，
    山山似北向，
    条条南流水。
            1957年12月14日
