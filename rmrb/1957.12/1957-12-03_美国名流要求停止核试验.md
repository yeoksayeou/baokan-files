Title: American Celebrities Demand a Halt to Nuclear Testing
Author:
Date: 1957-12-03
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, New York, December 2nd -- A number of prominent American social figures recently published a full-page advertisement in the "New York Times" announcing the establishment of a "National Committee for a Sane Nuclear Policy."

They pointed out that the US government has not resolved "the serious problem regarding nuclear testing -- especially concerning the poisoning of air, drinking water, and food, and endangering human health," and they demanded that "all countries immediately halt nuclear testing."

Those participating in this committee include: the President of the American Federation of Scientists, Paul Doty; Mrs. Roosevelt; the Executive Secretary of the American Friends Service Committee, Robert Kilmore; the National Chairman of the Americans for Democratic Action, Robert Nathan; the President of the National Farmers Union, James Patton; Professor Stanley Livingston of the Massachusetts Institute of Technology; Professor Paul Tillich of Harvard University; and the leader of the American Socialist Party, Thomas, etc.

### 美国名流要求停止核试验

1957-12-03
第5版()
专栏：

　  美国名流要求停止核试验
    据新华社2日讯　纽约消息：美国一些著名的社会人士最近在“纽约时报”上刊登了整页广告，宣布成立一个“争取头脑清醒的核政策全国委员会”。
    他们指出，美国政府还没有解决“关于核试验的严重问题——特别是关于毒化空气、饮水和食物以及危害人身的问题”，他们要求“一切国家立即停止核试验”。
    参加这个委员会的包括：美国科学工作者协会主席保罗·多蒂、罗斯福夫人、美国公谊会服务委员会执行秘书罗伯特·基尔摩、美国人民主行动协会全国主席罗伯特·纳桑、全国农民联盟主席杰姆斯·巴顿、马萨诸塞理工学院教授斯坦莱·利文斯顿、哈佛大学教授保罗·蒂利奇、美国社会党领袖托马斯等。
