Title: At the Atlantic Group Permanent Representative Council Meeting, US-Prepared Documents Met with Cold Reception
Author:
Date: 1957-12-11
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, Paris, 10th dispatch: The North Atlantic Group Permanent Representative Council began discussing on the 9th the "working document" prepared by the United States for the heads of government meeting of this group which is to open on the 16th. According to the Associated Press, this document did not arouse "enthusiasm" among the allies of the United States. The secret meetings of the Permanent Representative Council will continue until the end of this week in order to prepare for the heads of government meeting.

It is said that the main part of the US document is to guarantee that the United States will endeavor to have Congress amend the McMahon Act, which prohibits the supply of atomic energy information to foreign countries, so that "friendly countries" can share nuclear secrets. However, there is no guarantee that nuclear weapons will be handed over to the members of the North Atlantic Group.

### 在大西洋集团常任代表理事会议上  美国准备的文件遭到冷视

1957-12-11
第5版()
专栏：

    在大西洋集团常任代表理事会议上
    美国准备的文件遭到冷视
    据新华社10日讯　巴黎消息：北大西洋集团常任代表理事会9日开始讨论美国为16日开幕的这个集团的政府首脑会议所准备的“工作文件”。据美联社说：这个文件引不起美国盟国的“热忱”。常任代表理事会的秘密会议将继续举行到本周末，以便为政府首脑会议作准备。
    据说，美国的文件的主要部分是保证美国将努力使国会修改禁止供给外国以原子能情报的麦克马洪法，以便“友好国家”能够分享原子核秘密。但是，并没有保证要把原子核武器交给北大西洋集团各成员国。
