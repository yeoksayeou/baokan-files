Title: Joint Statement by Peace Representatives from Four Nordic Countries Calls on People to Intensify the Defense of Peace
Author:
Date: 1957-12-23
Page: 6
　
Xinhua News Agency, Berlin, 22nd - According to a Deutsche Presse-Agentur (DPA) dispatch on the 21st: Representatives of the peace movements from Denmark, Norway, Sweden, and Finland, who are currently visiting the German Democratic Republic, issued a joint statement in Rostock, calling on the people of Scandinavian countries to intensify the struggle to defend peace following the Paris meeting of the North Atlantic Treaty Organization (NATO).

The joint statement pointed out that the only way out of the current serious situation is to hold negotiations on limiting armaments, first and foremost negotiations on banning nuclear weapons. The statement expressed satisfaction with the opposition of the Norwegian and Danish representatives at the NATO Paris meeting to the deployment of atomic weapons and rockets in these two countries.

The joint statement called on the people of Scandinavian countries to intensify the struggle for understanding, disarmament, and the maintenance of peace, to promote economic and cultural ties between the Baltic Sea countries, and to strive for the establishment of a region including both Germanys that does not store nuclear weapons.

### 北欧四国和平代表发表联合声明  号召人民加紧保卫和平

1957-12-23
第6版()
专栏：

    北欧四国和平代表发表联合声明
    号召人民加紧保卫和平
    新华社柏林22日电  据德意志通讯社21日讯：正在访问民主德国的丹麦、挪威、瑞典和芬兰的和平运动代表在罗斯托克发表了联合声明，号召斯堪的纳维亚国家的人民在北大西洋集团巴黎会议举行后加紧进行保卫和平的斗争。
    联合声明指出：在目前严重的局势下的唯一出路就是举行关于限制军备的谈判，首先是关于禁止核武器的谈判。声明对于挪威和丹麦两国的代表在北大西洋集团巴黎会议上反对在这两国设置原子武器和火箭，表示欣幸。
    联合声明号召斯堪的纳维亚国家的人民加紧进行争取谅解、裁军和维护和平的斗争，增进波罗的海沿岸国家的经济和文化联系，争取建立一个包括两个德国在内的不存放核武器的地区。
