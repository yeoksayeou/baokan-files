Title: Regulations of the People's Republic of China on Frontier Health and Quarantine – Adopted at the 88th Meeting of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress on December 23, 1957
Author:
Date: 1957-12-24
Page: 4

These regulations were passed by the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress.

Article 1 These regulations are formulated to prevent infectious diseases such as plague, cholera, yellow fever, smallpox, typhus, and relapsing fever from being introduced into or transmitted out of the People's Republic of China, and to implement health and quarantine inspections at the national borders.

Article 2 Frontier health and quarantine authorities shall be established at international seaports and airports open to international traffic in the People's Republic of China, as well as at land border crossings and ports of entry and exit on border rivers and waterways.

Article 3 Frontier health and quarantine authorities shall be responsible for conducting medical examinations, health inspections, and necessary sanitary treatments of persons, means of transport, luggage, and goods entering or leaving the country.

Article 4 The Ministry of Health of the People's Republic of China, in conjunction with relevant departments, shall handle the notification of epidemics of quarantine infectious diseases between the People's Republic of China and foreign countries.

Article 5 In the event of a major epidemic of a quarantine infectious disease at home or abroad, the State Council of the People's Republic of China may order the closure of relevant areas of the national border.

Article 6 The implementing rules of these regulations shall be formulated by the Ministry of Health of the People's Republic of China, and shall be implemented after being submitted to and approved by the State Council of the People's Republic of China.

Article 7 Any person who violates these regulations and the implementing rules thereof may be given a warning or fined up to 1,000 yuan by the frontier health and quarantine authority, depending on the seriousness of the circumstances; if the violation of these regulations and the implementing rules thereof causes the spread of quarantine infectious diseases, or there is a serious risk of causing the spread of quarantine infectious diseases, the people's court may, depending on the seriousness of the circumstances, impose a sentence of imprisonment of not more than two years or criminal detention, and may also impose a fine of not less than 1,000 yuan and not more than 5,000 yuan, or impose such a fine separately.
　　If a person subjected to a penalty is dissatisfied with the penalty, he may, within ten days after receiving the penalty notice or judgment, apply to the original penalty authority or its superior authority for reconsideration or appeal, or file an appeal with the people's court of second instance in accordance with the law.

Article 8 These regulations shall come into force on the date of promulgation.

### 中华人民共和国国境卫生检疫条例——一九五七年十二月二十三日全国人民代表大会常务委员会第八十八次会议通过

1957-12-24
第4版()
专栏：

    中华人民共和国国境卫生检疫条例
    ——一九五七年十二月二十三日全国人民代表大会常务委员会第八十八次会议通过
    第一条　为了防止鼠疫、霍乱、黄热病、天花、斑疹伤寒和回归热等传染病由国外传入和由国内传出，在中华人民共和国国境实施卫生检疫。
    第二条　在中华人民共和国的国际通航的海港和机场所在地，以及陆地边境和国界江河的进出口岸，设立国境卫生检疫机关。
    第三条　国境卫生检疫机关负责对进出国境的人员和交通工具、行李、货物实施医学检查、卫生检查和必要的卫生处理。
    第四条　中华人民共和国和外国之间有关检疫传染病的疫情通报，由中华人民共和国卫生部会同有关部门办理。
    第五条　在国内或者国外检疫传染病大流行的时候，中华人民共和国国务院可以下令封锁国境的有关区域。
    第六条　本条例的实施规则，由中华人民共和国卫生部制定，报经中华人民共和国国务院批准后发布施行。
    第七条　对违反本条例和本条例实施规则的人，国境卫生检疫机关可以根据情节轻重，给予警告或者处以一千元以下罚金；如果因违反本条例和本条例实施规则而引起检疫传染病的传播，或者有引起检疫传染病传播的严重危险，人民法院可以根据情节轻重依法判处二年以下有期徒刑或者拘役，并处或者单处一千元以上五千元以下罚金。
    受处分人对所受的处分如果不服，可以在接到处分通知或者判决书后十日内，向原处分机关或者它的上级机关声请复议或者申诉，或者依法向上诉审人民法院提起上诉。第八条　本条例自公布日施行。
