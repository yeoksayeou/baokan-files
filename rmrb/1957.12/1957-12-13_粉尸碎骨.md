Title: Shattered Remains
Author: Zang Kejia
Date: 1957-12-13
Page: 8

　　Shattered Remains
　　Zang Kejia
　　The American "satellite,"
　　Wholeheartedly wanted to ascend to the sky,
　　A crowd of reporters came from thousands of miles away,
　　A "success" report only waited for the opportune moment.

　　It wasn't far from the ground,
　　Suddenly, there was a "crackling" sound,
　　"Success" abruptly turned into "failure,"
　　Neither crying nor laughing could happen.

　　A satellite ascending to the sky is not simple,
　　Relying on boasting will not succeed,
　　The fantasy of flying is quite high,
　　Falling down loses all "prestige."

　　This "satellite," though shattered,
　　Couldn't be loyal to its master,
　　Its lifespan was only two seconds,
　　It lives forever in people's laughter.

### 粉尸碎骨
臧克家
1957-12-13
第8版()
专栏：

　　粉尸碎骨
　　臧克家
　　美国人手里的“卫星”，
　　一心一意想上天空，
　　成群的记者千里而来，
　　“成功”的报道只待东风。
　　它离地面没有多远，
　　忽然“噼啪”的响了一声，
　　“成功”突变成“失败”，
　　啼不是来笑不成。
　　卫星上天不简单，
　　凭着吹嘘不成功，
　　幻想飞得倒挺高，
　　跌下来丢尽“威风”。
　　这“卫星”虽然粉尸碎骨，
　　没法为它的主子尽忠，
　　它的寿命只有两秒钟，
　　在人们的笑谈里它永生。
