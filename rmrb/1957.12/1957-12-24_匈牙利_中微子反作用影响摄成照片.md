Title: Hungary: Neutrino Recoil Effects Photographed
Author:
Date: 1957-12-24
Page: 6

    Hungary
    Neutrino Recoil Effects Photographed
    At the annual meeting of the Hungarian Academy of Sciences, Szalay Sándor, corresponding academician of the Hungarian Academy of Sciences and director of the Debrecen Institute of Nuclear Physics, announced that he and his assistant, Associate Doctor Csikai Gyula, successfully photographed the recoil effects of neutrinos using a Wilson cloud chamber. It is said that this is the first successful photograph of this kind in the world.
    In the experiment, they used the radioactive isotope helium-6. When helium-6 atoms decay in the Wilson cloud chamber, they release a high-speed electron. The nucleus of the helium-6 atom is then subject to recoil effects, like a cannon recoiling when fired. The direction of the nuclear recoil is not exactly opposite to the emitted electron. Therefore, the photograph proves that a neutrino is also emitted when the nucleus emits an electron. The direction of the neutrino's flight is different from that of the electron. Based on the photograph, the angles of the electron's and neutrino's trajectories at the time of emission can be determined. This is of great importance for understanding the nature of the atomic nucleus.

### 匈牙利  中微子反作用影响摄成照片

1957-12-24
第6版()
专栏：科学消息

    匈牙利
    中微子反作用影响摄成照片
    匈牙利科学院通讯院士、得布勒森原子核物理研究所所长萨拉伊·山道尔在科学院年会会议上宣布，他和他的助手奇卡伊·久洛副博士应用威尔逊云雾室成功地把中微子的反作用影响拍成了照片。据说，这是世界上第一次拍摄成功的这种照片。
    在试验中，他们应用了氦的放射性同位素氦6。氦6原子在威尔逊云雾室中衰变时，放出一个高速度的电子。氦6原子的核则受其反作用影响而向后退，就如炮在发射时后坐一样。原子核后退的方向不是恰恰与放出的电子相反。因此照片证明在原子核放出电子同时，还放出一个中微子。这个中微子的飞行方向与电子不同。根据照片可以确定电子及中微子放出时轨道的角度。而这对了解原子核内部的性质是有很大作用的。
