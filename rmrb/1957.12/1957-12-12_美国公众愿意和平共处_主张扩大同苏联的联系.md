Title: American Public Willing to Coexist Peacefully, Advocates Expanding Contacts with the Soviet Union
Author:
Date: 1957-12-12
Page: 6

    Xinhua News Agency, December 11th: According to a TASS dispatch from New York on December 10th: The "New York Herald Tribune" published an article by Gallup, head of the American Institute of Public Opinion, stating that recent opinion polls show that the American public continues to advocate expanding contacts with the Soviet Union.

    Gallup acknowledged: The U.S. government's intensified calls for further military expansion in response to the Soviet Union's tremendous achievements in science and technology "have not fundamentally changed the widespread desire of people to coexist peacefully with the Russians." Most residents support proposals for broader engagement with the Soviet Union. They advocate that the Soviet Union and the United States conclude agreements on expanding trade and increasing exchanges of students and travelers.

### 美国公众愿意和平共处  主张扩大同苏联的联系

1957-12-12
第6版()
专栏：

    美国公众愿意和平共处
    主张扩大同苏联的联系
    新华社11日讯　据塔斯社纽约10日讯：“纽约先驱论坛报”刊载了美国民意测验所负责人盖洛普的一篇文章说，最近举行的民意测验表明，美国公众继续主张扩大同苏联的联系。
    盖洛普承认：美国政府看到苏联在科学和技术方面的巨大成就而加紧叫喊进一步扩充军备“并没有使人们普遍要求同俄国人和平共处的愿望发生根本变化。”大多数居民都支持更广泛地同苏联交往的建议。他们主张苏美两国缔结关于扩大贸易的协定，主张扩大学生和旅行者的往来。
