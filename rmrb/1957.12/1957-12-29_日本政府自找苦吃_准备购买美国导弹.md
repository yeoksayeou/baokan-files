Title: The Japanese Government Is Asking For Trouble: Preparing to Purchase American Missiles
Author:
Date: 1957-12-29
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, December 28th: According to a report by Jiji Press today, the Japanese "Defense Agency" plans to purchase fifty to sixty "Sidewinder" air-to-air missiles from the United States in the 1958 and 1959 fiscal years to arm the Japanese military.

The report said that the Japanese "Defense Agency" has already begun negotiations with the U.S. Military Assistance Advisory Group stationed in Japan on accepting these American missiles. According to the "Sankei Jiji" newspaper on the 27th, the Japanese "Defense Agency" has reached an agreement with the U.S. Military Assistance Advisory Group on Japan's acceptance of fifty-four "Sidewinder" air-to-air missiles. Currently, Japan is awaiting the U.S. government's final decision on the quantity of missiles it will supply.

In order to purchase twenty "Sidewinder" air-to-air missiles from the United States in the next year, the "Defense Agency" is planning to request an additional 50 million yen in funding from the Japanese Ministry of Finance.

### 日本政府自找苦吃  准备购买美国导弹

1957-12-29
第6版()
专栏：

    日本政府自找苦吃
    准备购买美国导弹
    新华社28日讯　据时事通讯社今天报道，日本“防卫厅”计划在1958和1959财政年度内从美国购买五十个到六十个“响尾蛇”式空空导弹，来武装日本的军队。
    消息说，日本“防卫厅”已经开始同驻在日本的美国军事援助顾问团谈判接受美国这种导弹的问题。据“产经时事”报27日透露，日本“防卫厅”已经同美国军事援助顾问团达成了关于日本接受五十四颗“响尾蛇”式空空导弹的协议。目前，日本正在等待美国政府最后决定供给它这种导弹的数量。
    为了在明年度内向美国购买二十个“响尾蛇”式空空导弹，“防卫厅”正在计划要求日本大藏省补拨五千万日元的经费。
