Title: Baghdad Pact Muslim Countries Advocate Alignment with Atlantic Bloc
Author:
Date: 1957-12-14
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, Ankara, December 13th: The Baghdad Pact's four Muslim member countries unanimously agreed at a meeting on December 11th that establishing ties between the Baghdad Pact and the North Atlantic Treaty Organization is essential. Simultaneously, they requested increased military and economic aid.

Prior to this, Western news agencies reported that the United States was attempting to link several military blocs it had established and supported. According to Alam, a key official of the Iranian Foreign Ministry who attended the Ankara meeting, in a recent interview with the Iranian newspaper "Jahan," measures are being taken to foster closer cooperation between the Baghdad Pact and the North Atlantic Treaty Organization. "Jahan" stated that Baghdad Pact member states would also participate in the Southeast Asia Treaty Organization in the future.

### 巴格达条约穆斯林国家主张同大西洋集团挂钩

1957-12-14
第6版()
专栏：

　　巴格达条约穆斯林国家主张同大西洋集团挂钩
    据新华社13日讯　安卡拉消息：巴格达条约的四个穆斯林国家会议在12月11日一致认为，巴格达条约和北大西洋公约组织之间建立联系是十分必要的，同时他们要求得到更多的军事和经济援助。
    在这之前，西方通讯社曾报道，美国企图把它所建立的和支持的几个军事集团联成一片。据参加安卡拉会议的伊朗外交部重要官员阿拉姆最近对伊朗“世界报”记者说，现在正在采取措施来使巴格达条约和北大西洋公约组织更紧密地合作。“世界报”说，以后巴格达条约成员国还将参加东南亚条约组织。
