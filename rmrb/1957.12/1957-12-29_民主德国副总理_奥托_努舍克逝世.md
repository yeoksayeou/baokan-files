Title: Deputy Prime Minister of the German Democratic Republic, Otto Nuschke, Passes Away
Author:
Date: 1957-12-29
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, Berlin, December 28th. Otto Nuschke, Deputy Prime Minister of the German Democratic Republic and Chairman of the Christian Democratic Union of Germany, passed away in Berlin on December 27th due to heart failure at the age of seventy-four.

The government of the German Democratic Republic issued a communique stating: "The death of Otto Nuschke deprives the Council of Ministers of the German Democratic Republic of a respected and admired member."

The communique stated: "Nuschke's integrity and impartiality made him one of the most active fighters for cooperation and unity among all good people, for the defense of peace, for the peaceful restoration of the motherland's unity, and for the increasing happiness of the German people's lives."

Biography of Otto Nuschke

On the same day, the Presidium of the Central Committee of the Christian Democratic Union of the German Democratic Republic issued an obituary regarding the death of Otto Nuschke. The obituary pointed out that Nuschke was a man who always faithfully served the interests of the German people. He was committed to unifying Germany through peaceful and democratic methods.

Nuschke was born in Frohburg, Saxony, on February 23, 1883. In 1919, Nuschke was elected to the National Assembly as a representative of the "German Democratic Party." In 1933, Nuschke was one of the few members of the National Party Central Committee who opposed the Fascist Enabling Act. He later continued his anti-fascist struggle in secret resistance groups and was arrested several times.

After the Soviet Union defeated Fascism, Nuschke actively participated in the new democratic construction, and he became one of the founders of the Christian Democratic Union. From 1948, he served as the chairman of this party. He actively participated in the world peace movement and accompanied the delegation of the German Democratic Republic government to several important diplomatic negotiations and international conferences.

### 民主德国副总理  奥托·努舍克逝世

1957-12-29
第6版()
专栏：

    民主德国副总理
    奥托·努舍克逝世
    据新华社柏林28日电　德意志民主共和国副总理、德国基督教民主联盟主席奥托·努舍克12月27日因心脏瘫痪症在柏林去世，享年七十四岁。
    德意志民主共和国政府为此发表公报说：“奥托·努舍克的逝世使德意志民主共和国部长会议失去了一位令人尊敬和景仰的成员。”
    公报说：“努舍克为人公正不阿，这使他成为争取一切善良的人们合作和团结、争取维护和平、争取和平恢复祖国统一和争取使德国人民生活日益幸福的最积极的奋斗者之一。”
    奥托·努舍克生平
    同一天，民主德国基督教民主联盟中央委员会主席团发表了关于奥·努舍克逝世的讣告。讣告指出，努舍克是一个永远为德国人民的利益忠诚服务的人。他致力于通过和平和民主的方法统一德国。
    努舍克1883年2月23日出生在萨克森的弗罗堡市。1919年，努舍克作为“德国民主党”的代表被选入国民议会。1933年，努舍克是国家党中央委员会中反对法西斯的授权法案的少数党员之一。他后来在秘密抵抗团体中继续进行了反法西斯的斗争，屡次遭到逮捕。
    在苏联战胜法西斯主义以后，努舍克积极参加了新的民主建设，他成为基督教民主联盟的奠基者之一。从1948年起，他一直担任这个党的主席。他积极参加了世界和平运动，并且随同德意志民主共和国政府代表团参加过几次重要的外交谈判和国际会议。
