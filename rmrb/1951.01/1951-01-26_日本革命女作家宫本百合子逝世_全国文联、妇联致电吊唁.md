Title: Japanese Revolutionary Woman Writer Miyamoto Yuriko Passes Away; All-China Federation of Literary and Art Circles, All-China Women's Federation Send Telegrams of Condolence
Author:
Date: 1951-01-26
Page: 4

　　Japanese Revolutionary Woman Writer Miyamoto Yuriko Passes Away
    All-China Federation of Literary and Art Circles, All-China Women's Federation Send Telegrams of Condolence
    [Xinhua News Agency, Tokyo, 24th] News: The famous Japanese revolutionary woman writer, Miyamoto Yuriko, passed away in Tokyo on the 23rd at the age of 52. Miyamoto Yuriko (original name Nakajo Yuriko, changed to Miyamoto after marrying Miyamoto Kenji) was one of the oldest fighters on the Japanese proletarian revolutionary literary front and was arrested and imprisoned many times. After Japan's surrender in 1945, in addition to continuing her literary activities, she became a leader of the Japanese democratic women's movement, leading the activities of the Japanese Democratic Women's Club and the Democratic Women's Association. She was a director of the Japan-China Friendship Association and the Japan-Soviet Union Friendship Association, and strongly advocated developing friendly relations between the peoples of China and Japan and the peoples of Japan and the Soviet Union.
    [Xinhua News Agency, 24th] The All-China Federation of Literary and Art Circles sent a telegram to the Japan-China Friendship Association, expressing condolences on the death of its director, Miyamoto Yuriko. The telegram reads as follows: To the Japan-China Friendship Association:
    We are shocked to learn of the death of the revolutionary writer and director of the Japan-China Friendship Association, Comrade Miyamoto Yuriko. We express our deep condolences.
              All-China Federation of Literary and Art Circles
                January 24, 1951
    [Xinhua News Agency, 24th] The All-China Democratic Women's Federation sent a telegram to the Japan-China Friendship Association, expressing condolences on the death of its director, Miyamoto Yuriko. The telegram reads as follows: To the Japan-China Friendship Association:
    We express our condolences on the death of Comrade Miyamoto Yuriko, leader of the Japanese democratic women's movement and director of the Japan-China Friendship Association.
              All-China Democratic Women's Federation
              January 24, 1951

### 日本革命女作家宫本百合子逝世  全国文联、妇联致电吊唁

1951-01-26
第4版()
专栏：

　　日本革命女作家宫本百合子逝世
    全国文联、妇联致电吊唁
    【新华社二十四日讯】东京消息：著名的日本革命女作家宫本百合子，已于二十三日在东京病逝，年五十二岁。宫本百合子（本名中条百合子，与宫本显治结婚后改姓宫本）是日本无产阶级革命文学战线最老的战士之一，曾多次被捕入狱。一九四五年日本投降后，她除了继续从事文学活动之外，并成为日本民主妇女运动的领导者，领导日本民主妇女俱乐部和民主主义妇女协会的活动。她是日中友好协会和日苏亲善协会的理事，亟力主张发展中日人民和日苏人民之间的友好关系。
    【新华社二十四日讯】中华全国文学艺术界联合会致电日中友好协会，吊唁该会理事宫本百合子的逝世，电文如下：日中友好协会：
    惊悉革命作家，日中友好协会理事宫本百合子同志逝世，谨致深切的哀悼。
              中华全国文学艺术界联合会
                一九五一年一月二十四日
    【新华社二十四日讯】中华全国民主妇女联合会致电日中友好协会，吊唁该会理事宫本百合子的逝世。电文如下：日中友好协会：
    对于日本民主妇女运动领导者，日中友好协会理事宫本百合子同志的逝世，谨致哀悼。
              中华全国民主妇女联合会
              一九五一年一月二十四日
