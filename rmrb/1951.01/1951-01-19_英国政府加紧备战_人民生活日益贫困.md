Title: The British Government Intensifies War Preparations; People's Lives Grow Increasingly Impoverished
Author:
Date: 1951-01-19
Page: 4

　　【本报讯】据新华社讯：英国工党政府的备战政策，已为英国经济和英国人民的生活招致了巨大的灾难。在物价高涨、物资缺乏的情况下，英国人民面临着日益增加的负担和困难。连路透社也不能不承认：在一九五○年岁末的各报上出现了一片黯淡的年终消息，“预告英国人民将要感受愈来愈多的窘迫与匮乏”。
　　巨额军费负担直接压在英国人民身上。泰晤士报承认，为实现北大西洋侵略集团加紧备战的决定，重整军备的费用“将大大超过数月前制定的三年计划中的三十六亿英镑”。要弥补这个巨大开支，据《雷诺新闻报》称，“更高的税额将是不可避免的”。
　　由于备战经济而引起的物价上涨的压力，正在不断增强。据塔斯社消息，英国报纸上每天都在报道新的物价高涨。英商务部已宣布毛织品价格将自本年二月起增加百分之三十到四十（这是近年来最大的一次涨价），现成服装价格已在扶摇直上。据十二月上旬出版的英国商务部杂志发表的官方数字，去年十一月份英国批发物价比一九四九年九月英镑贬值时高出百分之二十五，其中工业原料与制成品上涨最剧。批发物价的猛涨，意味着已经不断上升的生活费用将跟随跃进。
　　和高物价同时俱来的，是粮食和消费物品的短缺和配给制度的变本加厉。去年十月粮食大臣韦伯曾经公开宣告：“现在我们将不能像我们曾经希望的那样放弃现行的配给制度了”，因为“英国的粮食情况不如一年以前”。肉类配给量则已削减到每周一百五十公分的空前微小数量。保守党的《每日邮报》透露，英国肉类存量比战时还少，该报预告“到一月底以前，英国可能要实行强迫性的素食了”。
　　为供应军事生产的需要，配给制度正逐渐扩大到工业原料方面。供应大臣斯特劳斯去年年底宣布自今年二月一日起，禁止在制造一百七十二种和平时期用品中使用锌、黄铜和铜。分配给和平工业的锌的全部供应量将削减百分之四十。这一决定将使许多种家庭设备、日常用具及其他物品停止生产。甚至工党的每日先驱报也不得不惋惜地说：“在新年中，水壶、钢笔、碗、钮扣、熨斗……将变得很稀少了。”每日邮报更透露：成千家公司因此而面临着转业或停闭的关头。斯特劳斯自己也承认：这一新的行动“一定会引起”某些地区失业的增加。
　　另外，煤荒也非常严重。由于煤荒，英国正面临该国有史以来国内最大的危机之一。“除非这个危机被克服了，成百万的人就要失业，工厂就要停止，家庭就要遭受饥寒交迫的灾难。我们所面临的燃料危机可能甚至比一九四七年的可怕的冬天还要严重些”。

Xinhua News Agency report: The British Labour government's war preparations policy has brought enormous disasters to the British economy and the lives of the British people. Amidst soaring prices and shortages of goods, the British people face increasing burdens and difficulties. Even Reuters cannot but admit: At the end of 1950, the newspapers were filled with gloomy year-end news, "predicting that the British people will experience more and more embarrassment and deprivation."

　　The heavy burden of military spending falls directly on the British people. The Times admitted that in order to implement the North Atlantic aggressive bloc's decision to intensify war preparations, the cost of rearmament "will greatly exceed the 3.6 billion pounds in the three-year plan formulated a few months ago." To make up for this huge expenditure, according to the Reynolds News, "higher taxes will be inevitable."

　　The pressure of rising prices caused by the war economy is constantly increasing. According to TASS, British newspapers are reporting new price increases every day. The British Board of Trade has announced that the price of wool fabrics will increase by 30 to 40 percent from February of this year (the largest price increase in recent years), and the prices of ready-made clothing are skyrocketing. According to official figures published in the British Board of Trade Journal in early December, in November of last year, British wholesale prices were 25 percent higher than when the pound was devalued in September 1949, with the most dramatic increases in industrial raw materials and manufactured goods. The sharp rise in wholesale prices means that the already rising cost of living will follow suit.

　　Along with high prices come shortages of food and consumer goods and the intensification of the rationing system. Last October, Minister of Food Webb publicly declared: "Now we will not be able to abandon the current rationing system as we had hoped," because "the food situation in Britain is not as good as it was a year ago." The meat ration has been reduced to an unprecedentedly small amount of 150 grams per week. The Conservative Party's Daily Mail revealed that Britain's meat stocks are less than during the war, and the newspaper predicted that "by the end of January, Britain may have to implement compulsory vegetarianism."

　　In order to supply the needs of military production, the rationing system is gradually being extended to industrial raw materials. Minister of Supply Strauss announced at the end of last year that from February 1st of this year, the use of zinc, brass, and copper will be prohibited in the manufacture of 172 peacetime products. The total supply of zinc allocated to peaceful industries will be reduced by 40 percent. This decision will stop the production of many kinds of household equipment, daily utensils, and other items. Even the Labour Party's Daily Herald had to lament: "In the New Year, kettles, pens, bowls, buttons, irons... will become very scarce." The Daily Mail further revealed that thousands of companies are therefore facing the crossroads of changing their business or closing down. Strauss himself admitted that this new action "will definitely cause" an increase in unemployment in some areas.

　　In addition, the coal shortage is also very serious. Due to the coal shortage, Britain is facing one of the biggest domestic crises in its history. "Unless this crisis is overcome, millions of people will lose their jobs, factories will stop, and families will suffer from hunger and cold. The fuel crisis we face may be even more serious than the terrible winter of 1947."
