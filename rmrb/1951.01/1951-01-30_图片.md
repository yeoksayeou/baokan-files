The All-Japan Automobile Industry Workers' Union originally planned to hold the "General Mobilization Rally to Break Through the Industrial Crisis" in Hibiya Park, Tokyo, on June 3, 1950, and had already obtained permission to do so. However, on that day, the reactionary Japanese government mobilized a large number of police to disperse around the park and prevent the rally from taking place. The American occupation forces deliberately conducted drills in the park at this time.
