    November 26th, when the Korean People's Army and the Chinese People's Volunteer Army shattered MacArthur's so-called "general offensive" and turned to a large-scale counter-offensive, the entire 148 men of Company C of the 24th Infantry Regiment, a black infantry unit of the 25th Infantry Division of the U.S. invading army, led by Company Commander Stanley and Deputy Company Commander A. A. C. Doruff, surrendered to the Chinese People's Volunteer Army in the area south of Unsan on the northwestern Korean front.
    Under MacArthur's command, this black company, over ninety percent of whom were black, along with scattered soldiers from Company B of the 35th Regiment and other engineers and attached artillery units, attempted to invade towards the Sino-Korean border. However, on the third day of MacArthur's so-called "general offensive," they were surrounded by the heroic Chinese People's Volunteer Army between a small stream and a high mountain south of Unsan.
    After the black company was surrounded, some died and some were wounded. The soldiers were unwilling to fight to the death, so the black company commander raised a white flag and surrendered to the Volunteer Army.
    These black soldiers, who suffered oppression and bullying in the U.S. invading army, were deeply moved by the lenient treatment of prisoners by the Chinese People's Volunteer Army. Deputy Company Commander A. A. C. Doruff said, "Your leniency towards us is beyond words. The facts have convinced me that you are right." Soldier Ferguson said, "We saw the soldiers of the 1st Cavalry Division you released. They said: The Chinese don't kill prisoners, they treat the wounded, they provide cotton clothes to those with torn clothes, and they give food. After hearing this, we didn't want to fight anymore."
    Another soldier expressed their feelings at the time: "The company commander ordered us to surrender, and everyone was extremely happy."
    Under the lenient treatment of the Chinese People's Volunteer Army, the soldiers of this black company, recalling the discrimination and oppression they suffered in the U.S. invading army, told the Volunteer Army about the tragic treatment of blacks in the U.S. invading army. Sergeant Christy said, "In the army, there is a clear line between blacks and whites. Whites despise us everywhere. When there are supply difficulties, it is the blacks who go hungry, and everything is given to the whites first. There are many places where whites can go, but blacks cannot." Corporal James said sadly, "Whites insult blacks, and all I can do is endure it. Blacks cannot walk equally with whites, or they will run into trouble. If a black man argues with a white man, he will never win the case." Black soldier Siddilic said, "I personally witnessed the genuine leniency of the Chinese People's Volunteer Army towards prisoners. The wounded are treated, food is given, and there is no abuse. The Chinese People's Volunteer Army treats us very well."

### 一个被解放的黑人连队
雪立
1951-01-16
第1版()
专栏：朝鲜通讯

　　一个被解放的黑人连队
    雪立
    十一月二十六日，在朝鲜人民军和中国人民志愿军粉碎了麦克阿瑟的所谓“总攻势”并转入大反攻时，美国侵略军步兵第二十五师黑人步兵第二十四团Ｃ连全部一百四十八人，在连长斯坦莱、副连长阿爱考乌斯·杜尔夫的率领下，在朝鲜西北战线云山以南地区，向中国人民志愿军投降了。
    在麦克阿瑟的驱使下，这个百分之九十以上都是黑人的黑人连队，还有第三十五团Ｂ连的散兵和另外一些工兵与附属炮兵，曾企图向着中朝边境进犯。可是，就在麦克阿瑟发动所谓“总攻势”的第三天，他们便在云山以南的一条小溪与一座高山之间，被英勇的中国人民志愿军包围住了。
    黑人连被包围后，死的死，伤的伤，士兵们都不肯卖命，于是黑人连的连长就竖起白旗向志愿军投降了。
    这些在美国侵略军中饱受压迫和欺凌的黑人士兵，对于中国人民志愿军的宽待俘虏，甚为感动。副连长阿爱考乌斯·杜尔夫说：“你们对我们的宽待，非一般言语所能表达出来。事实使我相信你们是对的。”士兵福开森说：“我们看到了你们放回去的骑兵第一师的士兵。他们说：中国人不杀俘虏，受伤给医治，衣裳破的给发棉衣，给饭吃。我们听了以后，就不想打仗了。”
    另一个士兵说出了他们当时的心情：“连长命令我们投降，大家心里高兴极了。”
    在中国人民志愿军的宽待下，这个黑人连队的士兵们想起他们在美国侵略军中所受的歧视和压迫，纷纷向志愿军诉说黑人在美国侵略军中所受的悲惨的待遇。军曹克里斯梯说：“在军队里，黑人与白人有着明显的界线。白人处处讨厌我们。遇到补给困难时，挨饿的是黑人，什么都先发给白人。许多白人能去的地方，黑人不能去。”伍长詹姆士沉痛地说：“白人侮辱黑人，而我所能够做的就只有忍耐。黑人不能与白人平等的走路，不然就会碰到钉子。黑人与白人争吵，官司是永远打不赢的。”黑人士兵息底利克说：“我亲眼看到了中国人民志愿军真正的宽待俘虏。受伤的给医治，给饭吃，不虐待。中国人民志愿军对待我们真好。”
                                                  【新华社】
