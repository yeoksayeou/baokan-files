Title: Gallery of Portraits of Warmongers
Author: Kukryniksy, Marshak, Zhang Menghui
Date: 1951-01-19
Page: 4

　　Gallery of Portraits of Warmongers
　　Painting by Kukryniksy
　　Poetry translated by Zhang Menghui
　　Poetry by Marshak
　　The artist Kukryniksy, winner of the Stalin Prize, exhibited a collection of satirical paintings entitled "Warmongers" at the 1950 All-Soviet Art Exhibition.
　　With sharp and severe strokes, the brilliant artist exposed the sins of the new disciples of that madman (Hitler). Now, all honest people around the world call out the names of these people with contempt.
　　Seven warmongers,
　　Appear grimly on the wall.
　　Look, this priest in formal attire,
　　Though smelling faintly of the lamp of God,
　　Holds an olive branch in his hand,
　　But it has a bizarre handle.
　　You don't see wings on him,
　　But bombs are exposed behind him.
　　His hypocritical speech,
　　Has never deceived anyone.
　　In meetings he swears,
　　To do everything to defend lasting peace,
　　But a series of fighting insults,
　　Sometimes suddenly come to intimidate people.
　　What he brings is not peace,
　　But war,
　　When he launched the Korean War,
　　He told his own country,
　　To turn to the police district.
　　The famous advisor,
　　Stares at you, like an owl.
　　He uses his hat to cover
　　The eye wounded on the left.
　　He came to the United Nations wearing a cloak,
　　So you can't guess,
　　That this is the armed-to-the-teeth
　　Heartless and lungless Mr. Dulles.
　　Marshall makes plans, builds horse collars,
　　To make the "little horses sweat all over,"
　　Although those guys are listless,
　　Once they put on the harness, they have to work obediently.
　　Now, the lovely Mr. Attlee
　　Has come to Washington.
　　--Any instructions?
　　He asks bowing down.
　　This Churchill gives a speech,
　　As if a volcano is smoking.
　　He wants to burn the whole world--
　　The South Pole, the North Pole, plus the equator.
　　He raises his fist like a jazz band
　　--The conductor of a gang of bandits,
　　Whenever he waves his hand,
　　Then comes the cannibalistic propaganda offensive.
　　When he climbs onto the stage,
　　He dreams of Hitler's tricks...
　　But to comb the Führer's hairstyle,
　　Where can you find that lock of hair combed diagonally?
　　From under the American hat,
　　(This is a gift from Wall Street)
　　White special agent Tito,
　　Stares with white eyes.
　　This is a portrait of the general.
　　He is taller than anyone else.
　　He blew a tremendous boast,
　　But lost the Korean war completely!
　　The villages and towns of Korea,
　　He ruthlessly robbed and burned,
　　But when retreating,
　　He doesn't cherish his long legs.
　　He doesn't have the habit of thinking twice,
　　He kills without cutting -- simply,
　　A heavy bayonet in his head,
　　Replaces his brain.
　　Even if he is strict with his subordinates,
　　And looks very dignified,
　　But the only one who fawns on him
　　Is Yoshida!…
　　On the walls of the portrait gallery,
　　Those who set fire in Korea,
　　The warmongers, grim and unsightly.
　　(Originally published in the Soviet Komsomolskaya Pravda, January 1, 1951)

### 战贩画像陈列室（图片）
库克雷尼克赛  马尔夏克  张孟恢
1951-01-19
第4版()
专栏：

　　战贩画像陈列室
    库克雷尼克赛作画
    张孟恢译诗
    马尔夏克作诗
    荣膺斯大林奖金的艺术家库克雷尼克赛，在一九五○年全苏艺术展览会上，展览了讽刺画集《战争贩子》。
    天才的艺术家以锋利而严厉的笔，揭露了那位疯狂元首（希特勒）新的徒子徒孙们的罪恶，现在全世界一切正直的人们，都以鄙视的态度来叫着这些人的名字。
    七名战争贩子，
    阴森难看地在墙上出现。
  　瞧，这位穿礼服的牧师，
    虽然有点儿神灯的油香味，
    但手中握着的橄榄枝，
    却有个离奇古怪的刀把子。
  　没看见他有翅膀，
    背后却露出炸弹。
    他那伪善的演说，
    并未曾有人受过骗。
  　在集会上他发誓许愿，
    要全力保卫持久和平，
    但一连串要打架的骂声，
    有时又突然拿来唬人。
  　他带来的不是和平，
    而是战争，
    当他发动朝鲜战争时，
    又叫自己的国家，
    向警管区转身。
  　大名鼎鼎的顾问，
    盯着你，像只猫头鹰。
    他用帽子遮着
    那左边给打伤的眼睛。
　  他身披大氅来到联合国，
    要你们猜不出，
    这就是武装到了牙齿的
    狼心狗肺的杜勒斯先生。 　马歇尔订计划，造马颈圈，
    要叫“小马儿满身大汗”，
    那些家伙们虽然没精打采，
    套上马具，就得乖乖儿干。
  　现在，可爱的艾德礼先生
    莅临了华盛顿。
    ——有没有吩咐？
    他躬身下问。
  　这位邱吉尔发表讲演，
    好像火山口在冒烟。
    他想燃烧全世界——
    南极北极，再加赤道线。
  　他高举拳头，像爵士乐队
    ——一群匪徒的指挥，
    每当他的手一挥起，
    于是来了吃人的宣传攻势。
   当他爬上舞台时，
    就梦想要希特勒的把戏……
    但要梳元首的发式，
    到哪去找那一绺斜梳的头发呢？
  　从美式大帽子下面，
   （这是华尔街的礼品）
    白色特务铁托，
    瞪着白色的眼睛。
  　这是大将军的肖像。
    他比谁都长得长。
    他吹下了弥天大牛，
    朝鲜一仗却输得精光！
  　朝鲜的村镇，
    他无情地抢劫烧毁，
    但在败退的时候，
    却不爱惜自己的长腿。
  　他没有三思而行的习惯，
    不砍就杀——简简单单，
    头中一把沉重的刺刀，
    代替了他的大脑。
  　哪怕他对部下严厉，
    而且样子也很威严，
    但对他低声下气的
    却只有一个吉田！……
  　在画像陈列室的墙上，
    那些在朝鲜放火的
    战争贩子，一个个阴森难看。
　   （原文载一九五一年一月一日苏联共青团真理报）
