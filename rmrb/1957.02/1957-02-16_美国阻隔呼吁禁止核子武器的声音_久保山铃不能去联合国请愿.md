Title: The U.S. Blocks Voices Calling for a Ban on Nuclear Weapons: Kuboyama Suzuki Unable to Petition the United Nations
Author:
Date: 1957-02-16
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, 15th: Tokyo Dispatch: Kuboyama Suzuki has been forced to cancel her plan to go to the United Nations to call for a ban on nuclear weapons. This Japanese woman representative is the wife of Kuboyama Aikichi, the Japanese fisherman who was the first victim of American hydrogen bomb tests.

The Japan Mothers' Association announced today that the plan was cancelled because Kuboyama Suzuki was having difficulty obtaining a U.S. visa. The U.S. Embassy has been delaying issuing the visa.

The Japan Mothers' Association originally planned to send Kuboyama Suzuki and Iso no Fujiko, a lecturer at Japan Women's University, to New York to petition the United Nations General Assembly for a ban on nuclear weapons testing.

### 美国阻隔呼吁禁止核子武器的声音  久保山铃不能去联合国请愿

1957-02-16
第6版()
专栏：

    美国阻隔呼吁禁止核子武器的声音
    久保山铃不能去联合国请愿
    新华社15日讯　东京消息：久保山铃已经被迫取消了到联合国去呼吁禁止核子武器的计划。这位日本妇女代表是美国氢弹试验的第一个牺牲者日本渔民久保山爱吉的妻子。
    日本母亲会今天宣布，这个计划所以取消，是因为久保山铃要取得美国的签证有困难。美国大使馆一直在拖延不发签证。
    日本母亲会原来准备派遣久保山铃和日本女子大学讲师矶野富士子到纽约去，向联合国大会请愿禁止核子武器试验。
