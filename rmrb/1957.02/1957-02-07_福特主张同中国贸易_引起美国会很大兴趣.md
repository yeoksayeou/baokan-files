Title: Ford Advocates Trade with China, Arousing Great Interest in the US Congress
Author:
Date: 1957-02-07
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, New York, 6th: According to the "New York Times," Henry Ford II, General Manager of Ford Motor Company, said at the National Automobile Dealers Association annual meeting in San Francisco on January 29th: "Trading with Communist China may be in the best interests of this country (the United States)."

Ford asked the United States to make realistic decisions on its trade and "aid" policies toward communist areas.

Ford's speech aroused great attention in the US Congress. "Newsweek" said, "In recent years, no foreign policy statement has aroused more interest in Congress than Ford's suggestion." It said that Washington "every member of Congress received a copy (of Ford's speech). Within hours, the speech became the topic of conversation in the Democratic and Republican congressional lounges."

The "Wall Street Journal" and the "Washington Evening Star" published editorials expressing their approval of Ford's proposal.

### 福特主张同中国贸易  引起美国会很大兴趣

1957-02-07
第6版()
专栏：

    福特主张同中国贸易
    引起美国会很大兴趣
    据新华社6日讯　纽约消息：据“纽约时报”报道，美国福特汽车公司总经理亨利·福特第二1月29日在旧金山全国汽车零售商协会年会上说：“和共产党中国贸易可能符合这个国家（美国）的最大利益”。
    福特要求美国在对共产主义地区的贸易和“援助”政策上作出现实的决定。
    福特的讲话在美国国会中引起很大的注意。美国“新闻周刊”说，“在最近几年中，没有哪一篇外交政策声明比福特的建议更引起国会的兴趣”。它说，华盛顿“每一个议员都得到了（福特讲话的）一份副本。在几小时以内，这篇演说就成为民主党和共和党议会休息室里的谈话主题”。
    “华尔街日报”和“华盛顿明星晚报”发表社论表示赞同福特的主张。
