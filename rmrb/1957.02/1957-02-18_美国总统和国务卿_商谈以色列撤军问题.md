Title: U.S. President and Secretary of State Discuss Israeli Withdrawal
Author: 
Date: 1957-02-18
Page: 6

    Xinhua News Agency, February 17th, Washington dispatch: U.S. President Eisenhower discussed Israel's refusal to comply with UN resolutions to withdraw its troops from Egypt on February 16th in Thomasville, Georgia, with Secretary of State Dulles and U.S. Representative to the United Nations Lodge.
    Dulles issued a statement after the meeting, acknowledging that "so far, Israel has not responded to the repeated demands of the United Nations General Assembly to end its occupation of non-Israeli territory and return to the agreed armistice line." The statement also said that the United States hopes that Israel will "further clarify" its attitude.
    In the statement, Dulles also stated that the United States "has not yet made a definite decision on further actions it might take to support the United Nations." The Asian and African countries of the United Nations have already put forward demands for sanctions against Israel in this regard.

### 美国总统和国务卿  商谈以色列撤军问题

1957-02-18
第6版()
专栏：

    美国总统和国务卿
    商谈以色列撤军问题
    新华社17日讯　华盛顿消息：美国总统艾森豪威尔16日在佐治亚州托马斯维尔同国务卿杜勒斯和美国驻联合国代表洛奇讨论了以色列拒绝遵照联合国决议从埃及撤军的问题。
    杜勒斯在会谈后发表声明，承认“到现在为止以色列一直没有响应联合国大会的一再要求：结束对非以色列领土的占领和退到协议的停战线以后去”。声明还说，美国希望以色列“进一步澄清”它的态度。
    杜勒斯在声明中同时表示，美国到现在“还没有就它可能采取的支持联合国的进一步行动作出确定的决定”。联合国的亚非国家已经在这方面提出了制裁以色列的要求。
