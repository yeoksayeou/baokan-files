Title: Soviet Delegation Writes to UN General Assembly President Requesting Inclusion of the US Aggression Issue on the Agenda, Indian Representative Supports Algerian Autonomy in UN General Assembly Political Committee
Author:
Date: 1957-02-14
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, 13th. According to TASS, New York: The Soviet delegation wrote to the President of the United Nations General Assembly on the 12th, requesting that the issue of "Aggressive Actions by the United States Threatening Peace and Security" be included on the agenda of the 11th United Nations General Assembly.

The UN Secretariat announced that the General Committee of the United Nations General Assembly is scheduled to meet on the afternoon of the 13th to review this request from the Soviet delegation.

The General Committee meeting will also review the Secretary-General's recommendations regarding the closing date of this session.

Xinhua News Agency, 13th. New York Dispatch: Indian representative Menon spoke in the Political Committee of the United Nations General Assembly on the 12th, supporting the proposal put forward by eighteen Asian and African countries requesting France to satisfy the Algerian people's desire for autonomy.

Menon said that the fact of the Algerian issue is that today there is an Algerian nation, and its desire to become part of the family of nations cannot be ignored. Establishing national freedom is a matter of international concern. Menon said that the eighteen-nation proposal is a practical plan.

Japanese representative Kase Shunichi stated that Japan is unwilling to participate in the proposal of the eighteen Asian and African countries because Japan opposes the aims of these countries. He, representing Japan, the Philippines, and Thailand, put forward a proposal expressing the hope that France and the Algerians would find a way to end the bloodshed in Algeria and peacefully resolve the current difficulties through appropriate negotiations. The proposal did not mention the issue of self-determination.

Argentina, Brazil, Cuba, Italy, and Peru also put forward a proposal that merely expresses the hope that the Algerian issue can find a peaceful and democratic solution. This proposal was supported by the United States, some Latin American countries, and Western European countries.

### 苏联代表团写信给联大主席  要求把美国侵略问题列入议程  印度代表在联大政委会上支持阿尔及利亚实现自治

1957-02-14
第6版()
专栏：

    苏联代表团写信给联大主席
    要求把美国侵略问题列入议程
    印度代表在联大政委会上支持阿尔及利亚实现自治
    新华社13日讯　据塔斯社纽约消息：苏联代表团12日写信给联合国大会主席，要求把“关于美国威胁和平与安全的侵略行动”问题列入第十一届联合国大会议程。
    联合国秘书处宣布，联合国大会总务委员会定13日下午召开会议，审查苏联代表团的这个要求。
    总务委员会会议还要审查联合国秘书长提出的关于本届会议闭幕期限的建议。
    据新华社13日讯　纽约消息：印度代表梅农12日在联合国大会政治委员会上发言支持十八个亚非国家提出的要求法国满足阿尔及利亚人民的自治愿望的提案。
    梅农说，阿尔及利亚问题的事实是，今天有一个阿尔及利亚国家，而它希望成为各国大家庭的一分子的愿望是不能忽视的。确立民族自由是国际所关怀的一件事。梅农说，十八国提案是一个切实可行的方案。
    日本代表加濑俊一表示日本不愿参加十八个亚非国家的提案，因为日本反对这些国家的目的。他代表日本、菲律宾和泰国提出一项提案，这个提案表示希望法国和阿尔及利亚人设法通过适当的谈判来结束阿尔及利亚的流血和和平解决目前的困难。提案没有提到自决权的问题。
    阿根廷、巴西、古巴、意大利和秘鲁也提出一个提案，这个提案仅仅表示希望阿尔及利亚问题能够找到一个和平的和民主的解决办法。这个提案得到了美国、一些拉丁美洲国家和西欧国家的支持。
