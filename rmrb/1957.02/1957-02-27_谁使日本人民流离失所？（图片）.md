Who Displaced the Japanese People?

    (Left) In Ashiya City, Japan, 800 culverts were prepared to repair the river, but for some unknown reason, construction has not started for several years. As a result, these culverts have become the "homes" of homeless Japanese people. Recently, the Ashiya City authorities asked these people to move, but where can they move to?
    (Below) Okinawan residents, who were brutally driven out of their homes by the U.S. military due to the expansion of U.S. military bases on Okinawa Island, have no choice but to live a hard life in tents in the wilderness. (Xinhua News Agency draft)

### 谁使日本人民流离失所？（图片）

1957-02-27
第6版()
专栏：

    谁使日本人民流离失所？
    （左）日本芦屋市准备了八百个涵管要修理河川，但几年来不知为什么原因没有动工，于是这些涵管变成了无家可归的日本人民的“住宅”。最近芦屋市当局要这些人搬走，但是，搬到哪里去呢？
    （下）由于美国在冲绳岛上扩大军事基地而被美军暴横地赶出家园的冲绳居民，只好在荒野的帐篷里过苦日子。　（新华社稿）
