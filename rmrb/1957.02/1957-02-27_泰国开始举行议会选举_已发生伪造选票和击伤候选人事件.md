Title: Thailand Begins Parliamentary Elections; Ballot Forgery and Shooting of Candidate Reported
Author:
Date: 1957-02-27
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, Bangkok, February 26th -- Thailand's parliamentary elections began today throughout the country. This election will select 160 Category One members of the Thai parliament.

    As voters went to vote at more than 11,000 polling stations across the country, all Thai police and reserve soldiers were on standby in barracks. According to Thai newspapers, an opposition party candidate was shot by a machine gun on election day.

    At the same time, Nai Khuang Aphaiwong, leader of the Thai opposition Democratic Party, made accusations of discovering 50,000 forged ballots.

    The Thai government admits that the four parties forming the Socialist United Front may obtain more votes in various provinces of northeastern Thailand. However, it believes that the ruling party will have the advantage overall.

### 泰国开始举行议会选举  已发生伪造选票和击伤候选人事件

1957-02-27
第6版()
专栏：

    泰国开始举行议会选举
    已发生伪造选票和击伤候选人事件
    新华社26日讯　曼谷消息：泰国议会选举今天开始在全国各地举行。这次选举将要选出泰国议会中的一百六十名第一类议员。
    当选民们前往设在全国各地的一万一千多个投票站去投票的时候，泰国的全体警察和后备兵在兵营中待命出动。据泰国报纸报道，在选举日，已经有一个反对党的候选人被机关枪击伤。
    与此同时，泰国反对党民主党领袖乃宽·亚派旺提出了发现五万张伪造的选票的指责。
    泰国政府承认，在泰国东北部各个府内，组成社会主义联合阵线的四个党派可能获得较多的选票。但是，它相信整个说来执政党将占优势。
