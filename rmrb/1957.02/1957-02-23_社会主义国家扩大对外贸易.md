Title: Socialist Countries Expand Foreign Trade
Author:
Date: 1957-02-23
Page: 5

Socialist Countries Expand Foreign Trade
    朝鲜对外贸易有很大发展
朝鲜对外贸易在战后时期有了很大发展。去年对外贸易总额比1953年增加了109.5％，比1955年增加了36％。
    去年朝鲜同包括一些资本主义国家在内的十多个国家进行了贸易，今年还要同更多的国家进行贸易。1954年时朝鲜只同苏联和中国进行了贸易。
    朝鲜战后由于工业的迅速恢复，增加了工业产品的出口，其中主要有：工具钢、铁砂、水泥、电石、无烟煤、云母等。朝鲜城津炼钢厂出产的工具钢可以制造各种高速度金属切削车刀等工具，质量优良。
    朝鲜的土特产近年的出口量也有显著增加。国际闻名的高丽人参受到各国的普遍欢迎，目前在国际市场上供不应求，朝鲜正在大力增产人参。
    民主德国和苏联签订贸易协定
    德意志民主共和国和苏联的1957年度贸易协定20日在柏林签字。根据两国政府联合声明中所确定的原则，两国1957年的贸易额将比1956年增加35％以上。
    民主德国将输出机器、光学和精密器械、轧钢设备、起重和运输设备、锻冶设备、母机、铁路车辆、船只、木材加工和化工设备等。苏联将输出硬煤、铁砂、锰砂、压延品、石油、木材、谷类、肉类、蔬菜、动物脂肪、羊毛、棉花等。
    苏联挪威增加今年贸易额
    苏联和挪威2月5日在奥斯陆签订了1957年增加换货议定书，这个议定书增加了苏挪两国1955年11月15日签订的三年为期的换货协定所规定的今年贸易额。
    苏联和瑞典签订换货议定书
    苏联和瑞典的贸易谈判已于2月5日在莫斯科结束。谈判是在友好的气氛中进行的，谈判结果，商定了苏联和瑞典在1957年的换货单，并且签订了换货议定书。
    社会主义国家同西德贸易额增加
    1956年西德同苏联、捷克斯洛伐克、波兰、匈牙利、罗马尼亚和保加利亚的贸易额是十八亿一千八百万马克，比1955年增加了70％以上。1955年的贸易额是十亿四千九百万马克。
    同1954年相比较，1956年西德对上述国家的贸易额几乎增加了两倍。
    苏捷本季度买埃及棉花最多
    埃及财政部18日发表的统计数字表明，在目前这一季度里，苏联和捷克斯洛伐克购买埃及棉花的数量最大。
    苏联在今年已经购买了埃及棉花二十二万九千八百十四坎塔尔（一坎塔尔等于大约九十九磅重），而在去年同时期是购买了十一万八千七百八十九坎塔尔。捷克斯洛伐克购买了十八万六千八百九十五坎塔尔。
　（据新华社讯）

    Korean Foreign Trade Has Greatly Developed
    Korean foreign trade has greatly developed in the post-war period. Last year, the total value of foreign trade increased by 109.5% compared with 1953 and 36% compared with 1955.
    Last year, Korea traded with more than ten countries, including some capitalist countries, and will trade with more countries this year. In 1954, Korea only traded with the Soviet Union and China.
    Due to the rapid recovery of industry after the war, Korea has increased the export of industrial products, mainly including: tool steel, iron sand, cement, calcium carbide, anthracite, mica, etc. The tool steel produced by the Chengjin Steel Plant in Korea can manufacture various high-speed metal cutting tools and other tools, with excellent quality.
    The export volume of Korea's local specialties has also increased significantly in recent years. The internationally renowned Goryeo ginseng is generally welcomed by all countries and is currently in short supply in the international market. Korea is vigorously increasing the production of ginseng.
    East Germany and the Soviet Union sign a trade agreement
    The 1957 trade agreement between the German Democratic Republic and the Soviet Union was signed in Berlin on the 20th. According to the principles established in the joint declaration of the two governments, the trade volume between the two countries in 1957 will increase by more than 35% compared with 1956.
    East Germany will export machinery, optical and precision instruments, rolling mill equipment, hoisting and transportation equipment, forging equipment, machine tools, railway vehicles, ships, woodworking and chemical equipment, etc. The Soviet Union will export hard coal, iron sand, manganese sand, rolled products, petroleum, wood, grains, meat, vegetables, animal fats, wool, cotton, etc.
    Soviet Union and Norway increase this year's trade volume
    The Soviet Union and Norway signed a protocol on increasing trade volume for 1957 in Oslo on February 5th. This protocol increased the trade volume for this year stipulated in the three-year trade agreement signed by the Soviet Union and Norway on November 15, 1955.
    Soviet Union and Sweden sign a barter protocol
    Trade negotiations between the Soviet Union and Sweden ended in Moscow on February 5. The negotiations were conducted in a friendly atmosphere. As a result of the negotiations, the exchange list for the Soviet Union and Sweden in 1957 was agreed upon, and a barter protocol was signed.
    Socialist countries increase trade with West Germany
    In 1956, West Germany's trade volume with the Soviet Union, Czechoslovakia, Poland, Hungary, Romania, and Bulgaria was 1.818 billion marks, an increase of more than 70% compared with 1955. The trade volume in 1955 was 1.049 billion marks.
    Compared with 1954, West Germany's trade volume with the above-mentioned countries almost doubled in 1956.
    Soviet Union and Czechoslovakia buy the most Egyptian cotton this quarter
    Statistics released by the Egyptian Ministry of Finance on the 18th show that the Soviet Union and Czechoslovakia purchased the largest amount of Egyptian cotton in the current quarter.
    The Soviet Union has purchased 229,814 cantars of Egyptian cotton this year (one cantar equals approximately 99 pounds), while in the same period last year, it purchased 118,789 cantars. Czechoslovakia purchased 186,895 cantars.
(Xinhua News Agency)
