Title: Europe's Largest Uranium Mine is Located in East Germany
Author:
Date: 1957-02-18
Page: 5

　　Xinhua News Agency, Berlin, February 17th: Deputy Prime Minister of the German Democratic Republic, Selbmann, said on the 14th that the uranium mine mined by the German-Soviet joint venture "Wismut" mining company in the German Democratic Republic is the largest in Europe.

　　In an interview with the "Sunday" weekly, Selbmann also said that the Soviet Union had given great help to the development of atomic science and technology in the German Democratic Republic. For example, the German Democratic Republic participated in the Dubna Joint Institute for Nuclear Research, and the Soviet Union supplied technical drawings and materials needed for the German Democratic Republic to build a nuclear physics center and the first atomic power station. He also said that the German Democratic Republic had applied to join the International Atomic Energy Agency last fall.

### 欧洲最大的铀矿在民主德国境内

1957-02-18
第5版()
专栏：

    欧洲最大的铀矿在民主德国境内
    新华社柏林17日电　德意志民主共和国副总理赛尔布曼14日说：民主德国境内德苏合营的“维斯穆特”采矿公司所开采的铀矿是全欧洲最大的。
    赛尔布曼在对“星期日”周报记者谈话时还说，苏联对民主德国原子科学技术的发展给了很大帮助，譬如民主德国参加了杜布纳联合核子研究所，苏联对民主德国供应技术图纸和供应民主德国建设核子物理中心和第一个原子发电站所需的材料。他还说，民主德国已在去年秋季申请加入国际原子能机构。
