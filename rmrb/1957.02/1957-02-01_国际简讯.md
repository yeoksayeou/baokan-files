International Briefs

    Soviet Union Asks Two U.S. Embassy Officials to Leave the Country
    TASS, Moscow: It is learned here that Soviet Deputy Foreign Minister Patolichev received U.S. Ambassador Bohlen on January 30 and handed him a note. The note requested that U.S. Assistant Military Attaché Major Tanzer and Captain Stoker of the U.S. Embassy in Moscow immediately leave the Soviet Union because they were engaged in activities incompatible with their diplomatic status.
    Poland Receives More Grain from the Soviet Union
    On January 28, 3,211 tons of wheat shipped from the Soviet Union to Poland were unloaded at the port of Gdynia. Last December, 3,170 tons of barley were unloaded at Gdynia, and approximately 3,200 tons of rye were unloaded at Gdańsk. The grain unloaded at these two ports is part of the grain supplied by the Soviet Union to Poland on credit, according to the agreement between the two countries. (Xinhua News Agency)

### 国际简讯

1957-02-01
第5版()
专栏：国际简讯

    国际简讯
    苏联要求美使馆两官员离境
    据塔斯社莫斯科讯：此间获悉，苏联外交部副部长帕托利切夫1月30日接见了美国大使波伦，并且交给他一份照会。照会要求美国驻莫斯科大使馆助理陆军武官坦塞少校和斯托克尔上尉立即离开苏联国境，因为他们从事于同外交工作人员的身份不相称的活动。
    波兰又接到苏联的谷物
    1月28日，在琴尼亚港卸下了三千二百十一吨从苏联运到波兰的小麦。去年12月，有三千一百七十吨大麦在琴尼亚港卸下，约三千二百吨黑麦在革但斯克港卸下。在这两个港口卸下的谷物都是根据波苏两国的协定由苏联以赊卖方式供应波兰的谷物的一部分。　（新华社）
