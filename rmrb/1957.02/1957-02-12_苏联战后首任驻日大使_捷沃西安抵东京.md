Title: Soviet Union's First Post-War Ambassador to Japan, Tevosyan, Arrives in Tokyo
Author:
Date: 1957-02-12
Page: 5

    Xinhua News Agency, Tokyo, 11th: According to a TASS dispatch from Tokyo: Tevosyan, the Soviet Union's first ambassador to Japan since the restoration of diplomatic relations between the Soviet Union and Japan, and his entourage of 10 people arrived in Tokyo on the 10th.

    Kuhara Fusanosuke, Chairman of the National Council for the Restoration of Japan-China-Soviet Diplomatic Relations, delivered a welcoming speech at the airport. He emphasized the great significance of the restoration of diplomatic relations between Japan and the Soviet Union and called for further development of Japanese-Soviet relations.

    In his reply, Tevosyan said that the Soviet people have deep respect for their neighbor, the hardworking Japanese people. The Soviet Union sincerely hopes to develop and strengthen friendly relations with Japan.

### 苏联战后首任驻日大使  捷沃西安抵东京

1957-02-12
第5版()
专栏：

    苏联战后首任驻日大使
    捷沃西安抵东京
    据新华社11日讯　据塔斯社东京讯：苏日邦交恢复后，苏联第一任驻日本大使捷沃西安和他的随员10日抵达东京。
    日本恢复日中日苏邦交国民会议议长久原房之助在机场致欢迎词。他强调日苏恢复邦交的重大意义，并且要求进一步发展日苏关系。
    捷沃西安致答词说，苏联人民对自己的邻居，勤劳的日本人民怀着深深的尊重。苏联真诚地希望发展和巩固同日本的友好关系。
