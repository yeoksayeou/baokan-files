Title: Poland and Japan Sign Agreement to End State of War and Establish Diplomatic Relations; Japanese Socialist Party Hopes to Promote the Restoration of Diplomatic Relations with Other People's Democratic Countries
Author:
Date: 1957-02-10
Page: 5
Xinhua News Agency, February 9th -- According to a report by the Polish Press Agency: Government representatives of Poland and Japan signed an agreement in New York on the 8th regarding the end of the state of war and the establishment of diplomatic relations between the two countries.

The agreement stated that the state of war between Poland and Japan is declared terminated from the date this agreement comes into effect. Diplomatic relations should be restored between Poland and Japan, and the two countries should exchange ambassadors without delay. The two countries declared in the agreement that they would abide by the principles of the United Nations Charter. The two countries also assured each other that, whether for economic, political, or ideological reasons, neither country would directly or indirectly interfere in the other's internal affairs.

The agreement also stipulated that Poland and Japan mutually waive all claims arising from the war between the two countries between the groups and citizens of the two countries, and agree that the two countries will conduct negotiations as soon as possible to conclude various treaties and agreements to establish commercial and shipping relations between the two countries on a stable and friendly basis.

The agreement finally stipulated that the ratification of the agreement and the exchange of instruments of ratification should be carried out in Warsaw as soon as possible. This agreement shall take effect from the date of the exchange of instruments of ratification.

Signing the agreement on behalf of the Polish side was the Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Poland, the head of the Polish delegation to the Eleventh United Nations General Assembly, Mr. Winiewicz; signing on behalf of the Japanese side was the Japanese Ambassador to the United States, the head of the Japanese delegation to the Eleventh United Nations General Assembly, Mr. Shunichi Kase.

In a joint communiqué issued by Poland and Japan regarding the signing of this agreement, it was stated that the two countries will henceforth restore their traditional close and friendly relations. This will not only benefit the well-being of the people of both countries, but also contribute to the promotion of world peace by easing international tensions.

Xinhua News Agency, Warsaw, February 9th -- The Polish Press Agency announced letters from Polish Foreign Minister Rapacki and Japanese Foreign Minister Nobusuke Kishi to the people of the other country, mutually congratulating the signing of the agreement between Poland and Japan to end the state of war and establish diplomatic relations.

Xinhua News Agency, February 9th -- Tokyo News: The Japanese Socialist Party issued a statement today, welcoming the restoration of diplomatic relations between Japan and Poland, and hoping to further promote the restoration of diplomatic relations with other Eastern European People's Democratic countries.

The statement of the Socialist Party believes that following the restoration of diplomatic relations between Japan and the Soviet Union, this time Japan has restored diplomatic relations with Poland, and will soon restore diplomatic relations with Czechoslovakia, which will help ease international tensions.

The Socialist Party's statement reiterated that an important condition for striving for peace in Asia lies in normalizing relations between Japan and China. The Socialist Party requests the Japanese government to take active actions to restore diplomatic relations between Japan and China, and that the Japanese government remember that the normalization of relations between Japan and China is the path to striving for Japan's true independence and peace in Asia.

Japanese Foreign Minister Nobusuke Kishi also issued a statement today, saying that the significance of the restoration of diplomatic relations between Japan and Poland is far-reaching. This is a new development in Japan's restoration of diplomatic relations with Eastern European countries, making Japan's diplomacy take a step forward.

### 波日两国签订结束战争状态和建交的协定  日本社会党希望促进同其他人民民主国家恢复邦交

1957-02-10
第5版()
专栏：

    波日两国签订结束战争状态和建交的协定
    日本社会党希望促进同其他人民民主国家恢复邦交
    新华社9日讯　据波兰通讯社报道：波兰和日本两国政府代表8日在纽约签订了一项关于两国之间结束战争状态和建立外交关系的协定。
    协定说，波兰和日本之间的战争状态自本协定生效之日起宣告终止。波兰和日本之间应该恢复外交关系，两国之间应毫不延迟地互派大使级外交使节。两国在协定中申明，它们将遵行联合国宪章的原则。两国并且相互保证，不论是为了经济上、政治上或观念形态上的原因，两国都不得直接地或间接地干涉对方的内政。
    协定还规定，波兰和日本相互放弃由于两国之间的战争而产生的两国团体与国民之间的一切要求，并且同意两国尽速进行谈判，以便缔结各种条约和协定，把两国之间的商业与海运关系建立在稳定和友好的基础上。
    协定最后规定，协定的批准及批准书的交换应该尽速在华沙进行。本协定于批准书交换之日起生效。
    代表波兰方面在协定上签字的是波兰外交部副部长、波兰出席第十一届联合国大会代表团团长维尼维奇；代表日本方面签字的是日本驻美国大使、日本出席第十一届联合国大会代表团团长加濑俊一。
    波日两方为签订这项协定发表联合公报说，两国将从此恢复传统的亲密和友好的关系。这不但有利于两国人民的福利，而且有利于通过缓和国际紧张局势来促进世界和平。
    据新华社华沙9日电　波兰通讯社公布了波兰外交部长拉帕茨基和日本外务相岸信介分别写给对方国家的人民的信，这两封信互相祝贺波日结束战争状态和建立外交关系协定的签订。
    新华社9日讯　东京消息：日本社会党今天发表声明，欢迎日本同波兰恢复邦交，并且希望进一步促进同其它东欧人民民主国家恢复邦交。
    社会党的声明认为，继日苏恢复邦交之后，这次日本又同波兰恢复邦交，并且不久还将同捷克斯洛伐克恢复邦交，这将有助于缓和国际紧张局势。
    社会党的声明再次强调，争取亚洲和平的重要条件在于使日中两国关系正常化。社会党要求日本政府为恢复日中邦交而采取积极的行动，并且要日本政府记住：日中两国关系的正常化是争取日本的真正独立和亚洲和平的道路。
    日本外务相岸信介今天也发表声明说，日本同波兰恢复邦交的意义是深远的，这是日本同东欧国家恢复邦交的一个新发展，使日本的外交前进了一步。
