Czechoslovakia Expands Foreign Trade

Egyptian-Czech Trade Continues to Increase Over Three Years

An Egyptian Ministry of Finance spokesman said that trade between Egypt and Czechoslovakia has increased considerably in the past three years. He said that in 1956, Egypt imported a total of 3,820,167 Egyptian pounds of goods from Czechoslovakia, compared to 3,733,025 Egyptian pounds in 1955, and 2,734,359 Egyptian pounds in 1954.

In 1956, Egypt exported a total of 20,793,689 Egyptian pounds of goods to Czechoslovakia, compared to 8,803,597 Egyptian pounds in 1955 (of which 8,578,863 Egyptian pounds of goods were cotton), and 6,034,869 Egyptian pounds in 1954 (of which 5,712,898 Egyptian pounds of goods were cotton).

Signed 1957 Trade Exchange Agreement with Korea

The Czechoslovak Republic and the Democratic People's Republic of Korea signed a trade exchange and payment agreement for 1957 in Prague on February 13. This year, the trade volume between the two countries will increase by 12% compared to last year.

According to the agreement, Czechoslovakia will import non-ferrous metal ores, emery, various oilseeds, tobacco, and other items from the Democratic People's Republic of Korea.

Czechoslovakia will supply the Democratic People's Republic of Korea with various machines and tools, conductive electrical materials, medicines, chemical products, textiles, etc.

Signed New Trade Protocol with West Germany

A new trade protocol between the Federal Republic of Germany and Czechoslovakia for 1957 was signed in Bonn on February 4.

Signed Trade Agreement with the Netherlands

Representatives of the Netherlands and Czechoslovakia signed a one-year trade agreement in Prague on February 12.

According to the agreement, Czechoslovakia will import certain agricultural products, fish, and various edible oils and fats, including butter and lard, as well as various industrial products and raw materials from the Netherlands. Czechoslovakia will export agricultural and industrial products to the Netherlands.

Signed 1957 Trade Exchange Protocol with Finland

After negotiations between the Czechoslovak delegation and the Finnish delegation on trade relations between the two countries, the Czechoslovak-Finnish 1957 trade exchange protocol was signed in Prague on February 2.

The protocol stipulates that the two countries will maintain roughly the same level of trade exchange as last year.

Extended Trade Exchange Agreement with Switzerland

Czechoslovak representatives and Swiss representatives exchanged notes in Prague on February 4, deciding to extend the trade exchange and payment agreement between Czechoslovakia and Switzerland to 1957.

Signed 1957 Trade Exchange Agreement with Austria

Czechoslovakia and Austria signed the 1957 trade exchange agreement in Vienna on February 13. This year, the trade volume between the two countries will increase by 30% compared to last year. (Xinhua News Agency)

### 捷克斯洛伐克对外贸易扩大

1957-02-18
第5版()
专栏：

    捷克斯洛伐克对外贸易扩大
    埃捷贸易三年来不断增加
    埃及财政部的一位发言人说，在过去三年中，埃及和捷克斯洛伐克之间的贸易有了相当大的增加。他说，1956年埃及从捷克斯洛伐克输入的货物共达三，八二○，一六七埃镑，1955年是三，七三三，○二五埃镑，1954年是二，七三四，三五九埃镑。
    1956年埃及向捷克斯洛伐克输出的货物共达二○，七九三，六八九埃镑，1955年是八，八○三，五九七埃镑（其中八，五七八，八六三埃镑的货物是棉花），1954年是六，○三四，八六九埃镑（其中五，七一二，八九八埃镑的货物是棉花）。
    同朝鲜签订1957年换货协定
    捷克斯洛伐克共和国和朝鲜民主主义人民共和国2月13日在布拉格签订了1957年换货和支付协定。今年两国间的贸易额将比去年增加12％。
    根据协定，捷克斯洛伐克将从朝鲜民主主义人民共和国进口有色金属矿石、金刚砂、各种油籽、烟草和其他物品。
    捷克斯洛伐克将供应朝鲜民主主义人民共和国各种机器和工具、导电器材、药品、化学制品、纺织品等。
    同西德签订新的贸易议定书
    2月4日在波恩签订了1957年德意志联邦共和国和捷克斯洛伐克的新的贸易议定书。
    同荷兰签订贸易协定
    荷兰和捷克斯洛伐克的代表12日在布拉格签订了一项为期一年的贸易协定。
    根据协定，捷克斯洛伐克将从荷兰进口若干农产品、鱼类以及包括黄油和猪油的各种食用油脂，各种工业产品和原料。捷克斯洛伐克将向荷兰出口农产品和工业品。
    同芬兰签订1957年换货议定书
    捷克斯洛伐克代表团和芬兰代表团就两国之间的贸易关系问题举行了谈判以后，2月2日在布拉格签订了捷芬1957年换货议定书。
    议定书规定两国将大体上保持同去年一样的换货水平。
    延长同瑞士的换货协定
    捷克斯洛伐克代表和瑞士代表2月4日在布拉格互换了照会，决定把捷克斯洛伐克和瑞士之间的换货和支付协定延长到1957年。
    同奥地利签订1957年换货协定
    捷克斯洛伐克和奥地利13日在维也纳签订了1957年换货协定。今年两国间的贸易额比去年将增加30％。　　　　　　（据新华社讯）
