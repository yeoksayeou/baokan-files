Title: Indonesian Government Prepares to Unfreeze Profits of Foreign Enterprises
Author:
Date: 1957-02-15
Page: 6

    Indonesian Government
    Prepares to Unfreeze Profits of Foreign Enterprises
    Xinhua News Agency, Jakarta, February 11th. Antara News Agency reports that the Indonesian government is preparing to unfreeze the profits of foreign enterprises frozen in the Bank of Indonesia and allow them to be used for national enterprises that benefit the Indonesian national economy.
    Atmo Diningrat, secretary of the Monetary Board, said today when announcing the news that relevant foreign enterprises and national enterprises can negotiate on the use of the funds.
    In order to reduce foreign exchange expenditures, the Indonesian government once stipulated that 40% of the profits of foreign enterprises should be frozen, stipulating that this part of the profits can only be used to expand these foreign enterprises or increase investment, and must not be remitted or used for other purposes. The foreign enterprises concerned have requested to amend this regulation, so the government has made the above decision.

### 印度尼西亚政府  准备将外国企业利润解冻

1957-02-15
第6版()
专栏：

    印度尼西亚政府
    准备将外国企业利润解冻
    新华社雅加达11日电　据安塔拉通讯社报道，印度尼西亚政府准备把冻结在印度尼西亚银行中的外国企业的利润解冻，允许使用到有利于印度尼西亚国民经济的民族企业方面去。
    金融委员会的秘书阿特莫·迪宁格拉特今天在宣布这个消息时说，可以由有关的外国企业和民族企业就使用这笔款项的事宜进行谈判。
    印度尼西亚政府为了减少外汇支出，曾经规定把外国企业利润的40％加以冻结，规定这部分利润只能用来扩充这些外国企业或者增加投资而不得汇出或作其他用途。有关的外国企业曾经要求修改这项规定，因此政府作出了上述的决定。
