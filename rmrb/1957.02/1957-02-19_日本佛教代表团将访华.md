Title: Japanese Buddhist Delegation to Visit China
Author:
Date: 1957-02-19
Page: 1

Xinhua News Agency, Tokyo, 18th—The All-Japan Buddhist Federation, a nationwide Buddhist organization in Japan, decided at its standing directorate meeting held in Tokyo today that, in order to promote Buddhist exchanges between Japan and China, it would send a Japanese Buddhist delegation to visit China in late April. The Japanese Buddhist delegation will be composed of fifteen leaders from various Buddhist sects in Japan, including Takakai Ryusen of the Soto sect, Masuda Nichien of the Nichiren sect, Watanabe Shinkai of the Jodo sect, Yamada Mumon, President of Hanazono University, Nakano Gisho, President of Koyasan University, and Tsukamoto Zenryu, Professor of Kyoto University.

### 日本佛教代表团将访华

1957-02-19
第1版()
专栏：

    日本佛教代表团将访华
    新华社18日讯　东京消息：日本全国性的佛教组织全日本佛教会今天在东京举行的常任理事会会议决定，为了促进日中两国佛教的交流，决定派遣日本佛教代表团在4月下旬到中国访问。这个日本佛教代表团将由日本佛教界各宗派的十五位负责人组成，其中包括曹洞宗的高阶珑仙、日莲宗的增田日远、净土宗的渡边真海、花园大学校长山田无文、高野山大学校长中野义照和京都大学教授冡本善隆等。
