Title: Dalai Lama Lays Foundation Stone for Sikkim Tibetan Language and Culture Institute
Author:
Date: 1957-02-12
Page: 1

Xinhua News Agency, New Delhi, 11th – The Dalai Lama laid the foundation stone for the Tibetan Language and Culture Institute on a small hill in Gangtok on the afternoon of February 10th. This institute will be built by the Sikkim government with the aim of studying Tibetan language and culture and promoting Buddhism. The institute's buildings will be constructed in the Tibetan style.

Nearly one hundred high-ranking officials and social celebrities attended the foundation stone laying ceremony. The Dalai Lama addressed them, saying that Sikkim "on the one hand, has long been an important trade route between India and Tibet, and on the other hand, has played a significant role in promoting Buddhism due to its geographical position." He hoped that the institute would invite scholars to hold conferences to study, discuss, and exchange views on Mahayana Buddhism.

The Dalai Lama donated a collection of scriptures to the library affiliated with the institute, saying that he would provide further assistance and donate more scriptures.

Sikkim's Prince Palden Thondup Namgyal spoke on behalf of the Sikkim government, recalling the close religious and cultural ties between Sikkim, Tibet, and India, and expressing hope that through the establishment of the institute, these close ties would be further developed and enrich the lives of their people.

The Indian government official stationed in Sikkim, Pant, donated 100,000 rupees to the institute on behalf of the Indian government.

### 达赖喇嘛为锡金藏族语言文化研究所奠基

1957-02-12
第1版()
专栏：

    达赖喇嘛为锡金藏族语言文化研究所奠基
    新华社新德里11日电　达赖喇嘛2月10日下午在刚渡的一个小山上奠定了藏族语言文化研究所的基石。这个研究所将要由锡金政府兴建，它的目的是研究藏族语言和文化，并且宣扬佛教。研究所的房屋将按照西藏的式样建造。
    参加奠基典礼的有高级官员和社会名流将近一百人。达赖喇嘛向他们讲了话。他说：锡金“一方面是长期以来印度和西藏之间的重要商道，另一方面，又由它所处的地位，在宣扬佛法方面起了很大的作用”。他希望这个研究所邀请学者举行会议，就大乘佛教进行研究、讨论和交换意见。
    达赖喇嘛向这个研究所附设的图书馆捐赠了一批经书，他说还要进一步提供援助，赠给更多的经书。
    锡金亲王帕尔登·顿杜普·纳姆加尔代表锡金政府讲话，他追述了锡金、西藏和印度之间在宗教和文化方面的密切关系；并且希望通过这个研究所的成立，这种密切的关系进一步获得发展，而丰富它们的人民的生活。
    印度驻锡金政府官员潘特代表印度政府向这个研究所捐赠了十万卢比。
