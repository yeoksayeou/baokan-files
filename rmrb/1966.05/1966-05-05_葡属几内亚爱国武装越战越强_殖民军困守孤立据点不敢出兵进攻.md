Title: Patriotic Armed Forces of Portuguese Guinea Grow Stronger in Fighting; Colonial Troops Confined to Isolated Strongholds, Dare Not Launch Offensive
Author:
Date: 1966-05-05
Edition: 6

    Portuguese Guinea Patriotic Armed Forces Grow Stronger in Fighting
    Colonial Troops Confined to Isolated Strongholds, Dare Not Launch Offensive

    (Reported by this newspaper) An article about "Portuguese" Guinea published in the American *New York Herald Tribune* on April 14 stated that the people of "Portuguese" Guinea are waging a large-scale guerrilla war against the Portuguese colonialists. The Portuguese colonial troops, trapped in isolated strongholds, are frequently attacked and are in a desperate situation.

    The reporter for this article accompanied a team of guerrilla fighters from Senegal into territory nominally belonging to Portugal. He wrote: "Throughout the patrol, there was no sign of Portuguese military activity, and the rebels (referring to the patriotic armed forces) took no precautions to prevent Portuguese attacks. The patrol leader said, 'About two miles ahead, in Santo Domingo, there are about two hundred Portuguese soldiers holding out, and they are surrounded. They dare not attack us because they know that in the jungle, we have the advantage.'"

    According to the guerrilla leaders, "All roads leading out of the city have been mined and are under our control. The Portuguese can only rely on airlifts for supplies."

### 葡属几内亚爱国武装越战越强  殖民军困守孤立据点不敢出兵进攻

1966-05-05
第6版()
专栏：

    葡属几内亚爱国武装越战越强
    殖民军困守孤立据点不敢出兵进攻
    本报讯　四月十四日美国《纽约先驱论坛报》刊登一篇关于“葡属”几内亚的报道说，“葡属”几内亚人民正在进行大规模的游击战争，对抗葡萄牙殖民者。困守在孤立的据点中的葡萄牙殖民军，经常遭受打击，狼狈不堪。
    这篇报道的记者伴随一队游击战士，从塞内加尔进入了在名义上属于葡萄牙的领土。他写道：“在整个巡逻中，既没有葡萄牙人军事活动的迹象，而叛乱者（指爱国武装）也没有采取任何警戒措施来防止葡萄牙人的袭击。巡逻队的首领说，‘就在前面两哩的圣多明戈地方，大约有两百名葡萄牙人在据守，他们被包围了。他们不敢向我们进攻，因为他们知道，在丛林里，我们占有优势’。”
    据游击队的领导人称，“通往这座城市以外去的所有的道路，都被我们布了雷，并且受我们控制。葡萄牙人只能依靠空投接济。”
