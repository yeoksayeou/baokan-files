    Brief Comments
    Self-Evident

    A reporter asked the American invading-Vietnam military leader Westmoreland, "Do you have enough manpower to win the war?" This American aggressor commander said something very interesting: "Currently, we are not stuck in political issues, but stuck in the mud."

    "Stuck in the mud" is indeed a depiction of the true situation of the American aggressors in South Vietnam today. Although Westmoreland did not give a clear answer to the reporter's question, the answer is actually very clear.

    Rare Merit

    McNamara said that the U.S.'s implementation of a "limited goals policy" in the Vietnam War is "a policy that American citizens can be proud of... a merit of President Johnson."

    The so-called "limited goals policy" is just a lie of the Johnson government to appease people and cover up its expansion of the war of aggression against Vietnam. Even the American press believes that this policy has caused "increasing annoyance and unease" among the American people. What is there to be proud of! As for attributing any merit to Johnson, there is one. Isn't his Vietnam policy mobilizing an increasingly broad segment of the American people to rise up against their own imperialist government? Such merit is still rare in American history.

### 三言两语

1966-05-17
第6版()
专栏：三言两语

    三言两语
    不言自明
    有记者问美国侵越军头目威斯特摩兰，他“是否有足够的人力打赢战争？”这个美国侵略军司令说了句很有意思的话：“目前，我们不是陷在政治问题中间，而是陷在烂泥中间。”
    “陷在烂泥中间”，这的确是今天美国侵略者在南越的真实处境的写照。威斯特摩兰虽然没有对记者的问题作出明确的答复，其实它的答案是很清楚的。
    少有的功绩
    麦克纳马拉说，美国在侵越战争中执行“有限目标的政策”是“美国公民可以引为自豪的一项政策……是约翰逊总统的功绩。”
    所谓“有限目标的政策”，不过是约翰逊政府为了安抚人心，掩盖它扩大侵越战争的鬼话。这个政策连美国报刊也认为引起美国人民“日益烦恼和不安”，那里有什么自豪！至于给约翰逊按上什么功绩，那倒是有的。他的侵越政策不正在动员日益广泛的美国人民起来反对本国的帝国主义政府吗？这样的功绩在美国历史上还是少有的哩。
