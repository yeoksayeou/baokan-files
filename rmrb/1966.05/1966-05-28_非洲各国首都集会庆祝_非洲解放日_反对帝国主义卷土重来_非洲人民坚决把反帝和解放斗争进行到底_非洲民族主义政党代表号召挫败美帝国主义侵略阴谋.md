Title: African Capitals Rally to Celebrate "African Liberation Day" Against the Resurgence of Imperialism; African People Firmly Carry the Anti-Imperialist and Liberation Struggle Through to the End; Representatives of African Nationalist Parties Call for Frustrating the Aggressive Plots of U.S. Imperialism
Author:
Date: 1966-05-28
Edition: 6

    Xinhua News Agency, 26th dispatch: Mass rallies were held in African capitals on the 25th to celebrate "African Liberation Day."

    At the mass rally held in Cairo, the capital of the United Arab Republic, slogans such as "Down with imperialism!", "Down with the United States!", and "Africa for Africans!" resounded. Approximately one thousand people attended the rally.

    Ali Sabri, Vice President of the United Arab Republic and Secretary-General of the Arab Socialist Union, said at the meeting that imperialism is employing various plots in an attempt to prevent Africa's march towards freedom and to restore its lost positions. However, the African people will surely defeat imperialism and achieve victory.

    Mohammed Khan, the representative of the African nationalist parties stationed in Cairo, condemned the U.S. imperialist conspiracies against Africa at the meeting. He called on all African people to unite and frustrate the plots of imperialism headed by the United States.

    Youssef el-Sebai, Secretary-General of the Afro-Asian People's Solidarity Organization, said at the meeting: Colonialism will not casually abandon its attempt to continue to extract the wealth of our people and suppress our people's rights. They must be completely eliminated.

    In Dar es Salaam, the capital of Tanzania, parades and mass rallies were held. At the rally, all speakers called on the African people to unite more closely against imperialism and old and new colonialism, and to be highly vigilant against their conspiracies.

    Tanzanian First Vice President Karume said at the meeting that Tanzania actively supports the struggle for African liberation until the entire Africa is liberated. Zambian Vice President Kamanga, who is visiting Tanzania, strongly condemned the Smith colonial regime in Southern Rhodesia and the British government's policy towards Southern Rhodesia. He expressed support for the liberation struggles of the people of Zimbabwe, Mozambique, Angola, South Africa, and other places in Africa.

    The chairman of the Pan-Africanist Congress of Azania (South Africa), the chairman of the South West Africa People's Organisation, and the representative of the Zimbabwe African National Union in Dar es Salaam also spoke at the meeting, condemning imperialist aggression and subversive activities in Africa, and expressing their determination to carry the struggle against imperialism and for liberation through to the end.

    At a public rally held in Brazzaville, the capital of the Congo (Brazzaville), Julien, a member of the Political Bureau of the National Revolutionary Movement of the Congo (Brazzaville), said in his speech that only the vigilance of the people can defend the independence already achieved. He also condemned the heinous crimes committed by U.S. imperialism in Vietnam and other regions, and condemned the subversive plots of neo-colonialists against the Congo (Brazzaville) and other African countries.

    In Mogadishu, the capital of Somalia, a mass rally attended by thousands of people was held. Somali Foreign Minister Duale strongly condemned the British policy of condoning the Smith colonial regime in Southern Rhodesia. He stated that Somalia will make its own contribution to the liberation of Southern Rhodesia, South Africa, South West Africa, Angola, Mozambique and other African countries that are not yet independent.

    In Addis Ababa, the capital of Ethiopia, celebrations were also held to commemorate "African Liberation Day." Ethiopian Emperor Haile Selassie I reiterated in a broadcast address to the nation that Ethiopia will continue to support the African people under colonial oppression.

    In a statement issued on the "African Liberation Day" on the 24th, the Executive Secretariat of the Algerian National Liberation Front said that "the historical trend of the struggle for liberation by the people of African countries cannot be stopped." The statement said: "Experience shows that in order to achieve freedom and independence, it is necessary to take up arms and fight."

### 非洲各国首都集会庆祝“非洲解放日”反对帝国主义卷土重来  非洲人民坚决把反帝和解放斗争进行到底  非洲民族主义政党代表号召挫败美帝国主义侵略阴谋

1966-05-28
第6版()
专栏：

    非洲各国首都集会庆祝“非洲解放日”反对帝国主义卷土重来
    非洲人民坚决把反帝和解放斗争进行到底
    非洲民族主义政党代表号召挫败美帝国主义侵略阴谋
    新华社二十六日讯　非洲各国首都二十五日举行群众集会，庆祝“非洲解放日”。
    在阿联首都开罗举行的群众集会上，响彻了“打倒帝国主义！”“打倒美国！”和“非洲是非洲人的！”等口号。约有一千多人出席了这次集会。
    阿联副总统、阿拉伯社会主义联盟总书记阿里·萨布里在会上讲话说，帝国主义正在施展种种阴谋，企图阻止非洲走向自由的进军，恢复它失去的阵地。但是，非洲人民必将打败帝国主义，取得胜利。
    非洲各民族主义政党驻开罗办事处的代表穆罕默德·汗在会上谴责美帝国主义对非洲的阴谋活动。他要求全体非洲人民团结起来，挫败以美国为首的帝国主义的阴谋。
    亚非人民团结组织总书记西巴伊在大会上说：殖民主义不会随便放弃它继续榨取我们人民的财富和压制我们人民的权利的企图。必须把他们完全消灭掉。
    在坦桑尼亚首都达累斯萨拉姆，举行了游行和群众集会。在集会上，所有讲话的人都呼吁非洲人民更紧密地团结起来，反对帝国主义和新老殖民主义，并高度警惕他们的阴谋活动。
    坦桑尼亚第一副总统卡鲁姆在会上说，坦桑尼亚积极支持非洲解放的斗争，直到整个非洲得到解放。正在坦桑尼亚访问的赞比亚副总统卡曼加在会上强烈谴责南罗得西亚的史密斯殖民政权和英国政府对南罗得西亚的政策。他表示支持津巴布韦、莫三鼻给、安哥拉、南非和非洲其他地方人民的解放斗争。
    阿扎尼亚（南非）泛非主义者大会主席，西南非洲人民组织主席，以及津巴布韦非洲民族联盟驻达累斯萨拉姆的代表也在会上讲了话，谴责帝国主义在非洲的侵略和颠覆活动，并表示要把反对帝国主义、争取解放的斗争进行到底。
    在刚果（布）首都布拉柴维尔举行的公众集会上，刚果（布）全国革命运动政治局委员儒利昂在讲话中说，只有人民的警惕，才能保卫已取得的独立。他还谴责美帝国主义在越南和其他地区犯下的滔天罪行。谴责新殖民主义者对刚果（布）和非洲其他国家的颠覆阴谋。
    在索马里首都摩加迪沙，举行了有数千人参加的群众集会。索马里外交部长杜阿莱在会上强烈谴责英国纵容南罗得西亚史密斯殖民政权的政策。他表示索马里将为南罗得西亚、南非、西南非洲、安哥拉、莫三鼻给和其他尚未独立的非洲国家的解放，作出自己的贡献。
    在埃塞俄比亚首都亚的斯亚贝巴，也举行了纪念“非洲解放日”的庆祝活动。埃塞俄比亚皇帝海尔·塞拉西一世在向全国人民发表广播演说中重申埃塞俄比亚将继续支持在殖民统治压迫下的非洲人民。
    阿尔及利亚民族解放阵线执行书记处二十四日就“非洲解放日”发表声明说，“非洲各国人民争取解放的斗争这一历史潮流是阻挡不了的。”声明说：“经验表明，为了取得自由和独立，必须拿起武器进行斗争。”
