    A Few Words

    Overwhelmed with Emotion
    UN Secretary-General U Thant said with overwhelming emotion: The position he holds, "is not only the loneliest job in the world, I think it is sometimes the most thankless job in the world."
    The plight of U.S. imperialism is extremely isolated. U Thant plays a role of carrying out Washington's will, how can he not have a sense of "loneliness"? And he has to serve the flustered American boss, so it is inevitable that he will "work hard." As for being "thankless," that depends on who you ask. The Washington rulers are obviously satisfied with U Thant's active service.

    Equivocation
    U.S. Senator Foreign Relations Committee Chairman Fulbright, when talking about the war of aggression against Vietnam, said: "The United States is exhibiting some signs of that fatal recklessness, that excessive use of force, and that excessive expansion of mission. That approach caused the downfall of ancient Athenians, Napoleonic France, and Nazi Germany. This process has hardly even begun, but the war we are now waging will only accelerate this process."
    If the "process" really hasn't even begun, then how can one talk about "accelerating this process"? The reality is that U.S. imperialism is heading towards destruction, and the war of aggression against Vietnam is accelerating this process. Fulbright's equivocations and stammering, what's the point!

### 三言两语

1966-05-03
第5版()
专栏：三言两语

    三言两语
    不胜感慨
    联合国秘书长吴丹不胜感慨地说：他所担任的职务，“不仅是世界上最寂寞的工作，我认为有时候这是世界上最吃力不讨好的工作。”
    美帝国主义的处境极端孤立。吴丹扮演奉行华盛顿意旨的角色，怎能不有“寂寞”之感呢？而且他要为焦头烂额的美国老板效劳，“吃力”也是必然。至于说到“不讨好”，那就要看是对谁了。华盛顿统治者对于吴丹的积极服务显然是满意的。闪铄其词
    美国参院外交委员会主席富布赖特在谈到侵越战争时说：“美国正表现出那种致命的冒失、那种过度使用力量和过度扩大使命的某些迹象。那种做法曾使古代的雅典人、使拿破仑的法国和使纳粹德国遭到覆亡。这个过程还差不多没有开始，但是我们现在在进行的战争只会加速这个过程”。
    如果“过程”真的还没有开始，那末哪里谈得到“加速这个过程”呢？实际情况是，美帝国主义是在走向灭亡，而侵越战争加速了它的这个过程。富布赖特闪铄其词，吞吞吐吐，又何苦来哉！
