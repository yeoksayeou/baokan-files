Title: The United States Intensifies its "Bridge-Building" to Promote the Restoration of Capitalism in the Soviet Union; Rusk Asks the U.S. Congress to Approve Granting the Soviet Union and Other Countries "Most Favored Nation" Trade Status
Author:
Date: 1966-05-13
Edition: 6

    Xinhua News Agency, Washington, May 12th - U.S. Secretary of State Dean Rusk submitted a bill to the U.S. Congress on the 11th proposing to grant "most favored nation" trade status to the Soviet Union and some Eastern European countries, requesting Congressional approval. He stated that the purpose of this bill is to accelerate the "bridge-building" between the United States and the Soviet Union and to encourage and promote the peaceful evolution of the Soviet Union and Eastern European countries.

    In a letter to Congress, Rusk brazenly stated that in the Soviet Union and some Eastern European countries, "great changes have recently taken place," and that "these new developments provide opportunities for the United States." The United States should "respond to their growing desire to expand relations with the West." He also bluntly stated that the U.S.'s so-called "expanding trade" aims to encourage the Soviet Union and some Eastern European countries to restore capitalism. He said that by increasing trade with the Soviet Union and some Eastern European countries, the United States "has the potential to influence them, bit by bit, towards greater domestic freedom and peaceful relations with the free world." He said that if Congress does not pass this bill, the United States "will continue to lose significant opportunities to influence the course of events in Eastern Europe."

    Rusk said that current U.S. laws do not allow the United States to fully utilize these opportunities and that passing this new bill is "in the interests of the United States."

    Rusk emphasized that this bill only applies to the Soviet Union and some Eastern European countries and will not apply to countries such as China, the Democratic Republic of Vietnam, and the Democratic People's Republic of Korea. He said that this bill also does not apply to the German Democratic Republic because the United States does not recognize the German Democratic Republic.

    Rusk was instructed by Johnson to submit this bill to the U.S. Congress. In a speech on May 3rd regarding U.S. policy towards the Soviet Union and Eastern Europe, Johnson stated that "close peaceful trade—over a period of time—can influence the societies of Eastern Europe," causing them to develop along paths that are "favorable" to American expectations.

### 美国加紧“搭桥”促进苏联复辟资本主义  腊斯克要求美国会批准给予苏联等国以贸易“最惠国待遇”

1966-05-13
第6版()
专栏：

    美国加紧“搭桥”促进苏联复辟资本主义
    腊斯克要求美国会批准给予苏联等国以贸易“最惠国待遇”
    新华社十二日讯　华盛顿消息：美国国务卿腊斯克十一日向美国国会提出了一个在贸易上给苏联和一些东欧国家以“最惠国待遇”的法案，要求国会批准。他表明，这个法案的目的是要加速美苏之间的“搭桥”，鼓励和促进苏联以及东欧国家的和平演变。
    腊斯克在给国会的一封信中明目张胆地说，在苏联和东欧的一些国家里，最近“发生了很大的变化”，“这种新变化给美国提供了机会”，美国要“响应它们扩大同西方的关系的日益增长的愿望”。他还露骨地表明，美国所谓的“扩大贸易”，目的在于鼓励苏联和一些东欧国家复辟资本主义。他说，通过增加同苏联和一些东欧国家的贸易，就使得美国“有可能一点又一点地影响它们走向更大的国内自由和同自由世界建立和平关系”。他说，如果国会不通过这个法案，美国“就会继续丧失影响东欧的事态进展的重大机会”。
    腊斯克说，美国目前的法律不允许美国充分利用这些机会。而通过这个新法案是“符合美国的利益”的。
    腊斯克强调，这个法案只适用于苏联和一些东欧国家，它将不适用于中国、越南民主共和国、朝鲜民主主义人民共和国等国。他说，这个法案也不适用于对德意志民主共和国，因为美国不承认德意志民主共和国。
    腊斯克是奉约翰逊之命向美国国会提出这项法案的。约翰逊在本月三日关于美国对苏联和东欧政策的一次讲话中曾讲到，“密切地进行和平贸易——在一段时期后，能够影响东欧各国社会”，使其沿着“有利于”美国期望的道路发展。
