Title: Indonesian Right-Wing Military Regime Repeatedly Expresses Willingness to Stop Confrontation with "Malaysia"; Britain Announces It Will Award the Indonesian Right-Wing Regime One Million Pounds

Author: 
Date: 1966-05-03
Edition: 5

    Xinhua News Agency dispatch from London: After the Indonesian right-wing military regime repeatedly and publicly expressed its willingness to cease confrontation with "Malaysia" and hold negotiations with Britain, British Foreign Secretary Stewart announced in the House of Commons on the 29th that Britain would grant the Indonesian right-wing military regime one million pounds in the name of "emergency aid" to help it cope with its current severe economic difficulties.
    When Stewart spoke in the House of Commons on the 25th, he had already expressed his appreciation for the Indonesian right-wing military regime's repeated statements of willingness to cease confrontation with "Malaysia." He said that Britain "certainly hopes to improve relations with Indonesia, and we are engaging with the Indonesian government for this purpose."
    At the same time, Indonesian right-wing military regime Foreign Minister Adam Malik told Brody, a reporter for the British Daily Express, that "we are not currently taking any military action" and "we want to resolve the (Malaysia) issue peacefully." Malik also obsequiously begged, "We are sincere, and we believe that the British will help us solve this problem." According to a report in the Daily Mail on the 29th, British Chargé d'Affaires in Indonesia, James Murray, held talks with Malik on the 28th and proposed that Britain provide loans. "Indonesian Foreign Minister Malik immediately accepted."
    After Stewart spoke, some members of parliament blatantly demanded that the Indonesian right-wing military regime take further action to end the confrontation, and advocated that Britain continue to exert pressure on Indonesia. According to a report by United Press International, opposition foreign affairs spokesman Barnier said, "We hope that this generous gesture of helping the Indonesian economy will enable them to abandon their past attitude of confrontation."
    Liberal Party spokesman Lubbock demanded that "the Foreign Secretary not relax his efforts to get Indonesia to end the confrontation."

### 印尼右派军人政权一再表示愿停止对抗“马来西亚”  英国宣布将赏给印尼右派一百万英镑

1966-05-03
第5版()
专栏：

    印尼右派军人政权一再表示愿停止对抗“马来西亚”
    英国宣布将赏给印尼右派一百万英镑
    据新华社讯　伦敦消息：在印度尼西亚右派军人政权一再公开表示愿意停止对抗“马来西亚”并且同英国举行谈判后，英国外交大臣斯图尔特二十九日在下院宣布，英国将以“紧急援助”的名义给予印度尼西亚右派军人政权一百万英镑，帮助它应付目前的严重经济困难。
    斯图尔特二十五日在下院发表讲话时，就曾经对印度尼西亚右派军人政权一再声明愿意停止对抗“马来西亚”的态度表示赞赏。他说，英国“当然希望改善同印度尼西亚的关系，我们正在为这一目的同印度尼西亚政府接触。”
    与此同时，印度尼西亚右派军人政权的外交部长阿达姆·马利克向英国《每日快报》记者布罗迪发表谈话说，“我们目前没有采取任何军事行动”，“我们要和平地解决（马来西亚）这个问题”。马利克还奴颜婢膝地乞求说：“我们是有诚意的，我们相信英国人帮助我们解决这个问题。”据《每日邮报》二十九日报道，英国驻印度尼西亚代办詹姆斯·默里在二十八日同马利克举行了会谈，并且提出英国给予贷款的建议。“印度尼西亚外长马利克立刻表示接受。”
    在斯图尔特讲话之后，一些议员露骨地要印度尼西亚右派军人政权在结束对抗方面采取进一步的行动，主张英国要继续对印度尼西亚施加压力。据合众国际社报道，反对党外交事务发言人巴尼尔说，“我们希望帮助印度尼西亚经济的这种慷慨的姿态，使他们能够放弃过去在对抗方面所持的态度。”
    自由党发言人卢伯克要求“外交大臣将不要放松他使印度尼西亚结束对抗的努力”。
