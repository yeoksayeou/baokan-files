Title: Mao Zedong
Author:
Date: 1966-05-07
Edition: 4

    Mao Zedong
    What we mean by literature and art being subordinate to politics is that this politics refers to the politics of classes, the politics of the masses, not the so-called politics of a few politicians. Politics, whether revolutionary or counter-revolutionary, is the struggle of class against class, not the behavior of a few individuals. Revolutionary ideological struggle and artistic struggle must be subordinate to political struggle, because only through politics can the needs of classes and the masses be concentratedly expressed.

### 毛泽东

1966-05-07
第4版()
专栏：

    毛泽东
    我们所说的文艺服从于政治，这政治是指阶级的政治、群众的政治，不是所谓少数政治家的政治。政治，不论革命的和反革命的，都是阶级对阶级的斗争，不是少数个人的行为。革命的思想斗争和艺术斗争，必须服从于政治的斗争，因为只有经过政治，阶级和群众的需要才能集中地表现出来。
