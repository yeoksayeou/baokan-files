Title: Famous Soviet Writer Pavlenko Dies; China Federation of Literary and Art Circles Sends Condolences

Author:
Date: 1951-06-19
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, Moscow: According to TASS, the famous Soviet writer and Deputy to the Supreme Soviet of the USSR, Pyotr Andreyevich Pavlenko, passed away on June 16th. Soviet writers Fadeyev and Surkov, among others, issued an obituary stating: Pavlenko's death is a serious loss to Soviet literature. Pavlenko was a new type of writer actively participating in the communist struggle and an active peace fighter. His screenplays "Alexander Nevsky," "The Vow," and "The Fall of Berlin," as well as his novel "Happiness," have successively won Stalin Prizes. After visiting the United States in 1949, he wrote "American Impressions," denouncing the American warmongers. Recently, he was also writing the third part of his epic work "Stalin and the People, Stalin and Humanity." His works are an important milestone in the development of Soviet literature and film art. Due to his outstanding contributions to the motherland, he was awarded the Order of Lenin, the Order of the Red Banner, the Order of the Red Star, and other medals. All his talent and wisdom were devoted to the motherland and the cause of communism.

Xinhua News Agency: Upon hearing the news of the passing of the famous Soviet writer Mr. Pavlenko on June 16th, the China Federation of Literary and Art Circles sent a telegram of condolence on June 18th. The telegram reads as follows:

	We are deeply saddened to hear of the passing of the famous Soviet writer Mr. Pavlenko. This outstanding writer, throughout his life, unyieldingly fought for socialist construction and the defense of world peace; his world-renowned works, such as "The Vow," "The Fall of Berlin," and "Happiness," have given us great inspiration and edification. His diligent work in literature and art has left a brilliant, fighting example for literary and art workers around the world.

					China Federation of Literary and Art Circles

### 苏联名作家巴甫连科逝世  我全国文联致电表示哀悼

1951-06-19
第4版()
专栏：

　　苏联名作家巴甫连科逝世        
    我全国文联致电表示哀悼
    【新华社十八日讯】据塔斯社莫斯科讯：苏联著名作家、苏联最高苏维埃代表彼得·安德列维奇·巴甫连科于六月十六日逝世。苏联作家法捷耶夫，苏尔科夫等人发表讣告说：巴甫连科的逝世是苏联文学界的严重损失。巴甫连科是积极参加共产主义斗争的新型作家，也是积极的和平战士。他的电影剧本《亚历山大·聂夫斯基》、《宣誓》、《攻克柏林》和长篇小说《幸福》曾先后荣膺斯大林奖金。他在一九四九年访问美国后写了《美国印象记》，痛斥美国的战争贩子。最近他还在写作他的史诗式的作品《斯大林与人民、斯大林与人类》的第三部。他的作品是苏联文学和电影艺术发展的重要里程碑。由于他对祖国的卓越贡献，他曾荣获列宁勋章、红旗勋章、红星勋章和其他奖章。他的全部天才和智慧都贡献给祖国和共产主义事业。
    【新华社讯】苏联名作家巴甫连科先生于六月十六日逝世，中华全国文学艺术界联合会闻讯后于六月十八日特电致唁，电文如下：
    惊闻苏联名作家巴甫连科先生逝世，我们表示沉痛的哀悼。这位杰出的作家，一生不屈不挠，始终为社会主义建设与保卫世界和平而战斗；他的全世界闻名的著作：如《宣誓》、《攻克柏林》、《幸福》等曾经给我们以极大的鼓舞和教益。他在文艺上的辛勤劳作，为全世界文学艺术工作者留下一个光辉的、战斗的范例。
                                      中华全国文学艺术界联合会
