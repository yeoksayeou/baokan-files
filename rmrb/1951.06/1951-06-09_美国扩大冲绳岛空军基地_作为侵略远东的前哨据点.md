Title: U.S. Expands Okinawa Air Force Base as an Outpost for Aggression in the Far East
Author:
Date: 1951-06-09
Page: 4

　　Xinhua News Agency, June 8th—American aggressors are expanding the air force base on Okinawa, which they are attempting to permanently occupy, as an outpost for American aggression in the Far East. According to a Tokyo dispatch from Pan-Asia News Agency on the 5th, the United States is expanding the Kadena and Naha Bay air force bases on Okinawa, making Okinawa one of the largest "air force wing bases" in the United States. According to the American plan, Kadena will become "America's foremost bombing base in the Pacific," while Naha Bay will become America's "fighter base." The U.S. government has allocated more than $109 million to implement the plan to militarize Okinawa.

　　A large number of Okinawa residents are being forced to engage in military base construction work. Pan-Asia News Agency revealed that the U.S. "Army and Air Force's two construction projects have used nearly 50,000 Okinawan indigenous laborers. Among the skilled workers are Filipinos and Japanese."

### 美国扩大冲绳岛空军基地  作为侵略远东的前哨据点

1951-06-09
第4版()
专栏：

　　美国扩大冲绳岛空军基地
    作为侵略远东的前哨据点
    【新华社八日讯】美国侵略者正在扩大它企图永久霸占的冲绳岛的空军基地，作为美国侵略远东的前哨据点。据泛亚社东京五日电透露，美国正在扩充冲绳岛上的嘉手纳与那霸湾两地的空军基地，使冲绳岛成为美国最大的“空军联队基地”之一。根据美国的计划，嘉手纳将成为“美国在太平洋最前面的轰炸基地”，而那霸湾将成为美国的“战斗机基地”。美国政府已拨付一亿零九百余万美元来实现使冲绳岛军事基地化的计划。
    大批冲绳岛居民被驱使从事军事基地建筑工作。泛亚社透露，美国“陆军与空军的两种建筑工程几乎用了五万名冲绳土著劳工。在熟练工人中有菲律宾人和日本人。”
