Title: Indian "Crossroads" Weekly Publishes Statement, Calling on Indian People to Launch Aid-Korea Movement
Author:
Date: 1951-05-06
Page: 4
　
    Xinhua News Agency, New Delhi, 3rd. The Indian weekly "Crossroads" in Bombay, responding to the call of the Women's International Democratic Federation, is launching a "Aid Korea" movement in India. The publication issued a statement on April 20, calling on the Indian people to collect medicines, clothes, and various gifts for the Korean people to support them.

    "Crossroads" Weekly calls on all its readers, individually or through specially established "Support Korea" committees, as well as their trade unions, clubs, factories, and institutions, to carry out fundraising campaigns to support Korea.

    "Crossroads" Weekly asks its readers to solicit medicines from doctors and chemists, and clothes from every household. It calls on women's organizations to form sewing groups, youth and students to conduct fundraising, and artists and writers to hold exhibitions, concerts, poetry recitals, and stage dramas to aid Korea.

### 印度《十字路》周报发表文告  号召印人民展开援朝运动

1951-05-06
第4版()
专栏：

　　印度《十字路》周报发表文告
    号召印人民展开援朝运动
    【新华社新德里三日电】印度孟买的《十字路》周报响应国际民主妇联的号召，在印度发起“援助朝鲜”运动。该刊四月二十日发表文告，号召印度人民为朝鲜人民征募医药品、衣服和各种礼物，以支援朝鲜人民。
    《十字路》周报号召它的全体读者分别地或者通过特设的“支援朝鲜”委员会以及他们的工会、俱乐部、工厂和机关开展支援朝鲜的募捐运动。
    《十字路》周报请它的读者们向医生和化学师征募医药品，向每一住户征募衣服。它号召妇女组织缝衣小组，青年和学生进行募捐，艺术家与作家为援助朝鲜而举办展览会、音乐会、诗歌朗诵会，并上演戏剧。
