Title: List of Members of the Standing Committee of the World Congress of Defenders of Peace
Author:
Date: 1949-04-30
Page: 3

Xinhua News Agency, Beiping, 29th. TASS, Paris: The list of the Standing Committee of the World Congress of Defenders of Peace, adopted at the final meeting of the World Congress of Defenders of Peace, is as follows:

    France – Professor Frédéric Joliot-Curie (senior expert on atomic bombs), Irène Joliot-Curie (Nobel Prize winner in Physics), Louis Aragon (writer), Eugénie Cotton (President of the Women's International Democratic Federation), Guy de Boysson (President of the World Federation of Democratic Youth), Pablo Picasso (artist), Louis Saillant (General Secretary of the World Federation of Trade Unions), Abbé Jean Boulier, Laurent Casanova (Member of the National Assembly), de Chambroum (Member of Parliament), Billoux (Member of Parliament), Yves Farge (former Minister), Jean Laffargue (writer), Alain Le Leap (General Secretary of the General Confederation of Labour), Marie-Claude Vaillant-Couturier (General Secretary of the Women's International Democratic Federation), Emmanuel d'Astier de la Vigerie (Member of Parliament), Françoise Leclerc (Secretary of the French Women's Union), Alain (miner), Fernand Clavier (farmer).

    United States – John Rogge (lawyer), W.E.B. Du Bois (historian), Albert Kahn (journalist), Moulton (Bishop), Paul Robeson (black singer), Howard Fast (writer), Donald Henderson (union leader), Joel W. Seidman (Professor at Columbia University).

    United Kingdom – Professor Bernal (Member of Parliament), Pritt (lawyer), Crowther (writer), Hewlett Johnson (Dean of Canterbury), Bill Carr (worker), Marie Pritt, Zilliacus (Member of Parliament), Nora Wooster (physicist), Mrs. Allen, John Wood (miner), Platts-Mills (Member of Parliament).

    Soviet Union – Alexander Fadeyev (writer), Alexander Korneichuk (writer), Wanda Wasilewska (female writer), Ilya Ehrenburg (writer), Kinada Gagaleva (member of the Soviet Women's Anti-Fascist Committee), Lyubov Kosmodemyanskaya (school teacher), Alexei Maresyev (pilot), Pavel Shirakhin (Secretary of the Central Council of the Miners' Union), Gunila (Archbishop).

    China – Professor Guo Moruo (historian), Professor Ma Yinchu (economist), Liu Ningyi (Vice-Chairman of the All-China Federation of Trade Unions), Xiao San (poet).

    Italy – Pietro Nenni (Member of Parliament), Luchino Visconti (writer), Ambrogio Donini (professor), Renato Guttuso (artist), Tito Ruffo (actor), Giuseppe Di Vittorio (Secretary of the Italian General Confederation of Labour), Giletti (President of the Italian Cooperative League), Maria Maddalena Rossi (President of the Italian Women's Union), Emilio Sereni (Senator), Guido Cigognani (Secretary of the Land Constituent Assembly), Adalindo Sandrini (Representative of the Christian Peace Movement), Dr. Amedeo (Secretary of the Southern Democratic Front), Pisano (peasant woman).

    Africa – Gabriel D'Arboussier (General Secretary of the African General Confederation of Labour), Guy Abass (General Secretary of the Dakar Trade Union Federation).

    Albania – Manor Konomi (President of the Academy of Sciences).

    Algeria – Abed Hamman Bougama (sportsman).

    Germany – Otto Nuschke (Chairman of the Presidium of the German People's Council), Alexander Abusch (writer), Fritz Heckert (Member of the Saarland Parliament), Heinrich Funk (dock worker), Anna Seghers (female writer).

    Argentina – Dr. Horacio Bellof, and Rada Skolamiri.

    Australia – Noel Counihan (artist).

    Belgium – Brenford (Secretary of the Belgian Women's League for the Defence of Peace).

    Brazil – Jorge Amado (writer), Helen Prado, Professor Paulo Fonseca.

    Bulgaria – Ludmil Stoyanov.

    Canada – James Endicott (writer) (known as Wen Youzhang in China).

    Chile – Pablo Neruda (poet).

    Korea – Ri Ki-yong (writer), Park Ting-eun, etc., both translated names.

    Cuba – Juan Marinello (writer).

    Denmark – Martin Andersen Nexø (writer), Edvard Thomsen (sportsman), Agnes Olsen.

    Spain – José Giral (former Chairman of the Council of Ministers), Manuel Sánchez Arcas (sportsman).

    Finland – Väinö Meltti (Governor).

    Greece – Marfo Axioti (female writer).

    Guatemala – José Manuel Fortuny (journalist).

    Hungary – György Lukács (writer).

    Netherlands – Odinot, Marcus Bakker (Chairman of the National Youth League of the Netherlands).

    India – Jammibai Patel.

    Indonesia – Dr. Prayoto (journalist).

    Iraq – Dr. Ussif Musani.

    Iran – Eskandari (former Minister).

    Ireland – Hilda Walling.

    Lebanon – Antoine Tlélès (sportsman), Mustafa Alis (President of the Lebanese Trade Union Federation).

    Luxembourg – Professor Edmond Rutter.

    Madagascar – Racha Karivony (Member of the Economic Committee).

    Mexico – General Cárdenas, Lombardo Toledano (General Secretary of the Latin American Labor Confederation).

    Mongolian People's Republic – Zagal Sarhan (Member of the Scientific Committee of the Mongolian People's Republic).

    Norway – Mimi Sverdrup, Professor Lundin, Peter Wistrand (trade union worker).

    Poland – Jerzy Borejsza (General Secretary of the Polish National Peace Committee), Tadeusz Szelachowski (General Secretary of the Central Council of Trade Unions), Irene Stachurska (President of the Polish Women's League), Professor Mumbrowski.

    Romania – Mihail Sadoveanu (writer), Professor Florica Mezincescu, Constantin Lehadatu (railway worker).

    Sweden – Professor Nils Sieverskold.

    Switzerland – Professor André Bonnard.

    Czechoslovakia – Jan Mukařovský (University Provost), Koldinova Spurna (Vice-Chairman of the National Assembly), Rosicki (miner).

    Transvaal (in South Africa) – Desmond Buckle (trade union worker).

    Trieste – Otto Braun (engineer).

    Tunisia – Nouri Boudali (trade union worker).

    Uruguay – Horacio Arigaro (Senator).

    Venezuela – Miguel Otero Silva (journalist).

    Vietnam – Ba Yi Ding (poet).

    Yugoslavia – Professor Josip Vilfan (Chairman of the National Council of the People's Assembly).

    International Student Union – Grohmann.

    International Association of Journalists – Gronik.
