Title: Cuban Dockworkers Refuse to Unload American Goods
Author:
Date: 1949-04-17
Page: 3

　　Xinhua News Agency, Peiping, 15th: Tass, New York dispatch: The New York Times' Havana (Cuban capital) dispatch states that Cuban dockworkers are continuing to refuse to unload textiles imported from the United States, in support of the textile workers who were laid off due to the closure of Cuban textile factories caused by the import of American textiles. According to news from Buenos Aires (Argentine capital), 100,000 textile workers demanded wage increases and held a strike. The 40,000 textile workers in Buenos Aires and Rosario won wage increases. News from Montevideo (Uruguayan capital) states that the strike involving 10,000 workers from three meatpacking companies has ended after the workers' demand for wage increases was resolved.

### 古巴码头工人拒卸美货

1949-04-17
第3版()
专栏：

　　古巴码头工人拒卸美货
    【新华社北平十五日电】塔斯社纽约讯：纽约时报哈瓦那（古巴京城）消息称，古巴码头工人继续拒卸自美国输入的纺织品，以支持因美国纺织品输入古巴使古巴纺织工厂歇闭而遭开除的纺织工人。据来自布宜诺斯艾利斯（阿根廷京城）的消息，十万纺织工人要求增加工资，曾举行罢工，布宜诺斯艾利斯及罗萨利俄四万纺织工人赢得了增加工资的胜利。来自蒙得维的亚（乌拉圭京城）的消息称，有三家肉类包装公司一万工人参加的罢工，已于工人增加工资要求获得解决后结束。
