    Peaceful Use of Atomic Energy in the Soviet Union and People's Democratic Countries

    Soviet Union Applies Radioactive Isotopes to Treat Diseases

    The achievements of modern nuclear physics have shown Soviet doctors the unlimited potential for safeguarding human health.

    The "Molotov" Scientific Research Institute for the Study of X-ray Rays and Radiology is widely using various radioactive isotopes to diagnose and treat a variety of diseases.

    With the help of tracer atoms, it is now possible to determine the speed of blood flow, which is very important for the treatment of cardiovascular diseases. Some important issues in metabolism can also be resolved by using tracer atoms.

    The work carried out by the Institute to improve tomographic imaging (a photographic method that can see individual sections of an organ) is of great significance. Obtaining this kind of photograph is very important for the detailed diagnosis of lung, bone, and heart diseases.

    The Institute is continuing to deepen its research methods on the heart and large blood vessels, and is studying methods of injecting a special contrast agent, "Cardiotrast," into the blood. This method allows doctors to see various lesions of the heart and large blood vessels more clearly in photographs, and can identify early-stage cancer and tuberculosis, various heart and vascular diseases, especially congenital heart defects.

    The Institute has designed and adopted a rotary X-ray therapy device, which allows only the part of the organ that needs to be treated to be irradiated, while the patient's skin and healthy organs are less affected by the harmful effects of the rays.

    Romania Studies the Application of Atomic Energy in Medicine

    Romania has begun to use radioactive isotopes in medical research. For example, radioactive phosphorus is used to treat some blood diseases, and radioactive colloidal gold is used to treat disseminated diseases.

    So far, the "Con. I. Parhon" Institute of Endocrinology of the Romanian Academy of Sciences has conducted many experiments on the thyroid gland and used "Iodine-131" to determine it. Due to the practical importance of thyroid pathology, special attention is paid to research using "Iodine-131." The Institute of Endocrinology has also been studying some thyroid goiters and hyperthyroidism, using "Iodine-131" in the research process.

    Romania's research on the medical use of radioactive isotopes is only a beginning. In the coming years, Soviet technical assistance will enable Romania to produce a considerable number of various isotopes, thereby providing the necessary material basis for the development of the peaceful use of atomic energy in medicine.

    Poland's Plan for the Peaceful Use of Nuclear Energy

    When the President of the Polish Academy of Sciences, Mr. Dembowski, recently made a report at the plenary session of the Academy of Sciences held in Warsaw, he said that the issue of the peaceful use of nuclear energy occupies the most important position among the many issues to be studied by the Polish Academy of Sciences in the near future. In the next ten to fifteen years, the application of nuclear energy will completely change Polish industry. The use of nuclear energy will play an equally large role in medicine, agriculture, biology and other sciences. The Institute of Nuclear Research of the Polish Academy of Sciences, established this year, will become the largest institute of the Academy of Sciences in the near future.

    Dembowski said that this institute will carry out large-scale research work in physics and reactor technology. And it is necessary to manufacture radioactive isotopes on a large scale. Studying methods of applying isotopes in other sciences is also one of its tasks.

    In his report, Professor Dembowski emphasized that it is necessary to train scientists in the field of nuclear energy on a large scale in the near future.

    Hungary and Bulgaria Jointly Establish Radiation Research Institute

    In order to promote international cooperation on the peaceful use of atomic energy, Hungary and Bulgaria plan to jointly establish a Radiation Research Institute. This institute will be located on the famous Stalin Peak in Bulgaria. The instruments required by the institute will be supplied by Hungary. Bulgaria has currently sent physicists to Hungary to jointly study the method of using the radiation equipment in the institute.

### 和平利用原子能在苏联和人民民主国家

1955-11-03
第4版()
专栏：

    和平利用原子能在苏联和人民民主国家
    苏联应用放射性同位素治病
    近代核子物理学的成就为苏联的医生们展示了保卫人的健康的无限潜能。
    研究爱克斯光射线学和放射学的“莫洛托夫”科学研究所正在广泛利用各种放射性同位素来诊断和治疗多种疾病。
    依靠示踪原子的帮助，现在已能够确定血液流动的速度，这对于心脏血管病的治疗有着非常重要的意义。新陈代谢作用中的一些重要问题也可依靠利用示踪原子来解决。
    研究所为改进断层摄影映像（能看到某一器官的个别断面的照相方法）所进行的工作，有着重大的意义。这种照片的取得，对于肺、骨和心脏疾病的详细诊断是非常重要的。
    研究所正在继续加深对心脏和大血管的研究方法、正在研究向血液中注入一种特别的显影物质“卡尔基奥特拉斯特”的方法。这种方法使医生可以在照片上更清楚地看到心脏和大血管的各种病变，并且能够辨认出初期的癌和肺结核，各种心脏和血管疾患，特别是先天的心脏缺陷。
    研究所已设计并采用了一种回转式爱克斯射线治疗设备，这种设备使得只有那部分需要治疗的器官受到照射，而病人的皮肤和没有病的器官则很少受到光线的有害影响。
    罗研究在医学方面应用原子能
    罗马尼亚已经在医学方面开始使用放射性同位素来进行研究工作。例如，使用放射性磷来治疗一些血液病，使用放射性胶体金来治疗散发病。
    到目前为止，罗马尼亚科学院的“康·伊·巴洪”内分泌学研究所已进行过许多关于甲状腺的实验，并且利用“碘一三一”来予以确定。由于甲状腺病理学问题所具有的实际重要性，对于用“碘一三一”来进行的研究给以特别的重视。内分泌学研究所并已从事研究一些甲状腺肿疡和甲状腺机能亢进的病症，在研究过程中使用了“碘一三一”。
    罗马尼亚在医学上使用放射性同位素的研究还只是一个开始。在未来的几年中，苏联的技术帮助将使罗马尼亚能够生产相当数量的各种同位素，从而为发展在医学方面和平利用原子能提供必需的物质基础。
    波兰和平利用核子能的计划
    波兰科学院院长邓博夫斯基最近在华沙举行的科学院全体大会上作报告时说，和平利用核子能问题在波兰科学院最近将来所要研究的许多问题中占最主要的地位。在今后十年到十五年中，核子能的应用将使波兰工业完全改观。在医学、农业、生物学和其他科学方面，核子能的使用将发挥同样大的作用。今年建立的波兰科学院原子核研究所在最近将来将成为科学院的最大的研究所。
    邓博夫斯基说，这个研究所将大规模地进行物理学和反应堆技术方面的研究工作。并且要大规模制造放射性同位素。研究在其他科学方面应用同位素的方法也是它的任务之一。
    邓博夫斯基教授在报告中强调指出，必须在最近将来大规模训练核子能方面的科学工作者。
    匈保共同设立放射线研究所
    为了促进和平利用原子能问题上的国际合作，匈牙利和保加利亚计划将共同设立放射线研究所。这个研究所将设立在保加利亚著名的斯大林峰上。研究所里所需用的仪器将由匈牙利方面供给。目前保加利亚已派遣了物理学家到匈牙利共同研究研究所里放射线装备的利用方法。
