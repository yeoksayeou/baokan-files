Title: Premier Zhou Receives the Japanese National Federation for the Protection of the Constitution Delegation; The Delegation Conducts Various Visit Activities in Beijing for Several Days
Author:
Date: 1955-11-16
Page: 1

Xinhua News Agency, November 15th. Premier Zhou Enlai of the State Council of the People's Republic of China received the entire delegation of the Japanese "National Federation for the Protection of the Constitution," led by Tetsu Katayama and Tohtaro Fujita, on the afternoon of November 15th.

Present at the reception were Vice Premier of the State Council Chen Yi, President of the Chinese People's Institute of Foreign Affairs Zhang Xiruo, Vice Presidents Chen Hansheng, Qian Duansheng, Qiao Guanhua, Luo Longji, Council Members Chen Jiakang, Feng Naichao, Zhao Anbo, Secretary-General Wu Maosun, Chairman of the All-China Federation of Trade Unions Lai Ruoyu, Vice Chairman Liu Ningyi, Secretaries of the Secretariat Li Jiebo, Director of the International Liaison Department Ma Chungu, Chairman of the China Peace Committee Guo Moruo, Vice Chairman Liao Chengzhi, Chairman of the China Council for the Promotion of International Trade Nan Hanchen, Vice Chairmen Lei Renmin, Li Zhucheng, Vice President of the Chinese People's Association for Cultural Relations with Foreign Countries Zhao Yimin, and Yang Hansheng.

After the reception, the delegation presented gifts to Premier Zhou Enlai.

Xinhua News Agency, November 15th. The Japanese "National Federation for the Protection of the Constitution" delegation, led by Tetsu Katayama, has been engaging in various discussions and visits in Beijing for the past several days.

On the morning of November 15th, the head of the delegation, Tetsu Katayama, and the Secretary-General, Kouichi Ohno, paid a visit to Guo Moruo, Chairman of the China Peace Committee. At noon, Guo Moruo invited Katayama and Ohno to lunch.

On the afternoon of the 15th, the delegation presented a banner to the China Peace Committee.

On the morning of November 12th, thirteen trade union workers from the delegation, led by Tohtaro Fujita, held a discussion with Zhu Xuefan, Vice Chairman of the All-China Federation of Trade Unions, Dong Xin, Secretary of the Secretariat, and heads of various departments. The Japanese guests gained detailed understanding of the general situation of Chinese trade union work, as well as wages, labor protection, and worker welfare.

On the 12th and 14th, the delegation held discussions on economic construction and agricultural issues with Xue Muqiao, Vice Chairman of the State Planning Commission, Liao Luyan, Minister of Agriculture, and others. On the afternoon of the 13th, delegation members Taminemura Kurazo and Yokote Masao visited the National Committee of the Railway Workers' Union of China and the National Committee of the Second Machine Building Workers' Union of China, respectively. Another part of the delegation visited the Chinese People's Association for Cultural Relations with Foreign Countries and people in Chinese literary and artistic circles, and held talks with Chinese architects, people in the political and legal fields, and responsible persons of the Workers' Daily, the Railway Workers' Union of China, and the Second Machine Building Workers' Union of China on issues such as architecture, politics and law, and trade unions. Female delegation members Ayako Ishigaki and Chikako Yoshii were also invited to attend a luncheon hosted by the All-China Women's Federation for them.

The entire delegation visited and toured the Central Institute for Nationalities on the 10th. Some members of the delegation also visited Tsinghua University, Peking University, and the former residence of Lu Xun. They were warmly received by Lu Xun's widow, Xu Guangping, at Lu Xun's former residence.

When the head of the delegation, Tetsu Katayama, the deputy head, Tohtaro Fujita, and a group of twenty-three people visited the Yellow River harnessing exhibition, they expressed their admiration for the abundance of Chinese resources, the completeness of the Yellow River harnessing plan, and the methods used in the exhibition. Members of the delegation also visited the exhibition of the natural environment and mineral resources of the motherland, the Beijing No. 2 State-owned Cotton Textile Factory, and toured the Summer Palace, Biyun Temple, the Forbidden City, the Temple of Heaven, and the Great Wall.

On the morning of the 11th, delegation members Kenji Ishihara, Ayako Ishigaki, and Koreya Senda paid their respects at the grave of Agnes Smedley. Ayako Ishigaki presented a Japanese translation of Smedley's book "The Great Road: The Life and Times of Chu Teh" at the grave. (Attached picture)

### 周总理接见日本拥护宪法国民联合会访华团　访华团连日在北京进行各种访问活动

1955-11-16
第1版()
专栏：

　　周总理接见日本拥护宪法国民联合会访华团
　　访华团连日在北京进行各种访问活动
    新华社十五日讯　中华人民共和国国务院总理周恩来在十五日下午接见了以片山哲和藤田藤太郎为首的日本“拥护宪法国民联合会”访华团全体人员。
    接见时在座的有，国务院副总理陈毅，中国人民外交学会会长张奚若、副会长陈翰笙、钱端升、乔冠华、罗隆基，理事陈家康、冯乃超、赵安博，秘书长吴茂荪，中华全国总工会主席赖若愚、副主席刘宁一、书记处书记李颉伯、国际联络部部长马纯古，中国人民保卫世界和平委员会主席郭沫若、副主席廖承志，中国国际贸易促进委员会主席南汉宸、副主席雷任民、李烛尘，中国人民对外文化协会副会长赵毅敏、阳翰笙。
    接见后，访华团还向周恩来总理赠礼。
    新华社十五日讯　以片山哲为首的日本“拥护宪法国民联合会”访华团连日来在京进行各种座谈和访问活动。
    十五日上午，访华团团长片山哲和秘书长大野幸一拜访了中国人民保卫世界和平委员会主席郭沫若。中午，郭沫若邀请片山哲和大野幸一共进午餐。
    访华团在十五日下午，向中国人民保卫世界和平委员会献了锦旗。
    十二日上午，以藤田藤太郎为首的访华团中的工会工作者十三人，同中华全国总工会副主席朱学范、书记处书记董昕和各部门负责人举行座谈会。日本客人们对中国工会工作概况和工资、劳动保护、工人福利等方面情况都作了详细了解。
    访华团在十二日和十四日，曾先后同中华人民共和国国家计划委员会副主任薛暮桥、农业部部长廖鲁言等举行了关于经济建设问题和农业问题座谈会。十三日下午，访华团团员田村民藏和横手真夫分别访问了中国铁路工会全国委员会和中国第二机械工会全国委员会。访华团另一部分人员还访问了中国人民对外文化协会和中国文艺界人士，曾分别同中国的建筑学家、政法界人士及工人日报、中国铁路工会、中国第二机械工会负责人就建筑学、政法和工会方面等问题进行交谈。访华团中的女团员石垣绫子和吉井千贺子还曾应邀出席中华全国民主妇女联合会招待她们的便餐。
    访华团全体人员在十日访问和参观了中央民族学院。访华团部分人员并访问了清华大学、北京大学和鲁迅故居。在鲁迅故居受到了鲁迅夫人许广平的热情接待。
    团长片山哲和副团长藤田藤太郎等一行二十三人在参观治理黄河展览会时，对中国资源之丰富、治黄计划之完整以及这个展览的方式方法都表示赞许。访华团人员还分别参观了祖国自然环境与矿产资源展览、国营北京第二棉纺织厂，游览了颐和园、碧云寺、故宫、天坛、长城等处。
    十一日上午，访华团团员石原宪治、石垣绫子、千田是也并曾谒史沫特莱墓。石垣绫子在墓前献了一部史沫特莱的著作“伟大的道路（朱德生涯和时代）”日译本。（附图片）
