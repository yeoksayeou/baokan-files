Title: Communiqué Issued After Soviet-Norwegian Talks
Author:
Date: 1955-11-17
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, Moscow: The signing ceremony for various documents between the Soviet Union and Norway took place on November 15th in the Grand Kremlin Palace in Moscow.

Soviet Council of Ministers Chairman N.A. Bulganin and Norwegian Prime Minister Einar Gerhardsen signed the text of the Soviet-Norwegian communiqué.

Soviet Minister of Foreign Trade N.S. Patolichev and Norwegian Minister of Commerce Arne Skaug signed the 1956-1958 Soviet-Norwegian Trade Exchange Protocol and the 1956 Supplementary Soviet-Norwegian Trade Exchange Protocol.

The Soviet-Norwegian communiqué stated:

During his visit to Moscow, Norwegian Prime Minister Einar Gerhardsen and Minister of Commerce A. Skaug, accompanying him, met with the leaders of the Soviet Union and held talks with Soviet Council of Ministers Chairman N.A. Bulganin, Soviet Supreme Soviet Presidium Member N.S. Khrushchev, and Soviet Council of Ministers First Deputy Chairman A.I. Mikoyan.

During the talks, the two sides frankly exchanged views on issues related to Soviet-Norwegian relations and certain general international issues of concern to both countries.

Both sides expressed their unwavering determination to promote the development of friendly and good-neighborly relations between the Soviet Union and Norway.

Both sides comprehensively discussed issues related to economic ties between the two countries. In this regard, both sides expressed their willingness to further develop Soviet-Norwegian trade and other economic ties on a basis of full reciprocity.

In the course of the exchange of views, it was deemed appropriate to establish scientific and technological ties between the Soviet Union and Norway, and it was stipulated that negotiations should continue through diplomatic channels to discuss specific steps in this regard.

At the suggestion of the Norwegian side, the two governments have reached an agreement on the establishment of a Soviet-Norwegian expert committee, whose task is to make recommendations on the most rational allocation of the rights to use the water resources of the Paatsjoki River (Pasvikelva) on the Soviet-Norwegian border.

At the suggestion of the Soviet side, an agreement was also reached that the two governments should begin negotiations in Oslo in the near future on the conclusion of an agreement on lifesaving services in the Barents Sea.

Both sides discussed cultural cooperation, which the two governments attach great importance to. It was unanimously affirmed that mutual contact in this area has greatly expanded in recent times, which is welcome.

Both sides unanimously expressed their willingness to take further measures to promote mutual understanding between the peoples of the Soviet Union and Norway through the exchange of delegations and other methods.

Cooperation between important youth organizations in the Soviet Union and Norway is a good example of such direct contact between representative organizations of the two countries, and they plan to exchange young representatives who are undergoing internships and theoretical studies. Both sides agreed to support such initiatives taken by Soviet and Norwegian organizations and institutions in the fields of sports, art, culture, science, and education.

In the exchange of views on the international situation, both sides expressed their willingness to promote the further relaxation of the international situation and to promote peaceful cooperation among the peoples of all countries on the basis of mutual respect for independence and sovereignty and non-interference in internal affairs.

The exchange of views on international issues provided both sides with a clearer understanding of each other's attitudes, which will undoubtedly help improve Soviet-Norwegian relations. It has been observed that the two governments share similar views on many issues discussed by the various bodies of the United Nations.

Prime Minister E. Gerhardsen assured the Soviet Union on a question raised by the Soviet side that the Norwegian government will not encourage policies that pursue aggressive aims and that, as long as Norway is not attacked or threatened with attack, it will not provide bases on Norwegian territory to foreign armed forces.

The talks between Prime Minister E. Gerhardsen and Minister of Commerce A. Skaug and the leaders of the Soviet government took place in an atmosphere of sincerity and friendly mutual understanding, and both sides are convinced that these talks will promote the development of friendly and good-neighborly relations between the Soviet Union and Norway.

### 苏联和挪威会谈后发表公报

1955-11-17
第4版()
专栏：

    苏联和挪威会谈后发表公报
    据新华社讯　塔斯社莫斯科讯：苏联和挪威之间的各项文件的签字仪式，十一月十五日在莫斯科大克里姆林宫举行。
    苏联部长会议主席布尔加宁和挪威首相基哈德森在苏联—挪威公报的文本上签了字。
    苏联对外贸易部长卡巴诺夫和挪威商务大臣斯科格在苏联和挪威一九五六—一九五八年换货议定书以及苏联和挪威一九五六年补充换货议定书上签了字。
    苏联—挪威公报说：
    挪威首相埃纳尔·基哈德森先生和随同他访问的商务大臣阿·斯科格在访问莫斯科期间同苏联的国家领导人员进行了会晤，并且同苏联部长会议主席尼·亚·布尔加宁、苏联最高苏维埃主席团委员尼·谢·赫鲁晓夫和苏联部长会议第一副主席阿·伊·米高扬进行了会谈。
    在会谈中，双方就苏挪两国的关系问题以及有关两国利益的某些一般的国际问题坦率地交换了意见。
    双方表示了坚定不移的决心，要促进苏联和挪威两国之间友好善邻关系的发展。
    双方全面地讨论了两国之间的经济联系问题。在这方面，双方表示愿意在完全互惠的基础上进一步发展苏挪贸易和其他的经济联系。
    在交换意见的过程中，认为建立苏联和挪威两国之间的科学技术联系是适宜的，并且规定继续通过外交途径举行谈判，来讨论这方面的具体步骤。
    根据挪威方面的建议，双方政府已经就成立一个苏挪专家委员会的问题达成协议，这个委员会的任务是就最合理地分配苏联和挪威边界的帕齐·约基河（帕斯维阿凯尔夫河）的水力资源的使用权问题提出建议。
    根据苏联方面的建议，还达成了一项协议：双方政府最近应该在奥斯陆就缔结巴伦支海救生服务协定开始进行谈判。
    双方讨论了两国政府都非常重视的文化合作问题。一致肯定，最近时期在这方面的相互联系大大地扩大了，这是值得欢迎的。
    双方一致表示愿意采取进一步的措施，通过交换代表团和其他方法来促进苏联和挪威两国人民之间的相互了解。
    苏联和挪威的重要的青年组织之间的合作是两国有代表性的组织实行这类直接联系的良好的范例，它们计划互相交换正在进行实习和理论学习的青年代表。双方同意支持苏联和挪威的组织和机构在体育、艺术、文化、科学、教育等方面所采取的这类倡议。
    在就国际局势交换意见的过程中，双方表示愿意促进国际局势的进一步缓和，促进各国人民在互相尊重独立和主权、不干涉内政的基础上发展和平合作。
    关于国际问题的交谈使双方对彼此的态度有了更明确的了解，这无疑地将有助于改进苏挪两国的关系。已经看出，两国政府对联合国各个机构所讨论的许多问题的观点是一致的。
    埃·基哈德森首相对苏联提出的一个问题作了保证：挪威政府将不鼓励追求侵略目的的政策，而且，只要挪威不遭到攻击或者遭到攻击的威胁，将不在挪威领土上向外国武装力量提供基地。
    埃·基哈德森首相和阿·斯科格商务大臣同苏联政府领导人员的会谈是在诚挚和友好地相互谅解的气氛中进行的，双方深信，这次会谈将促进苏挪两国友好善邻关系的发展。
