Title: Dulles Meets with President Tito

Author: 
Date: 1955-11-08
Page: 4

　　Xinhua News Agency, Prague, 7th. According to a report from the Yugoslav News Agency, Belgrade: U.S. Secretary of State Dulles and his entourage arrived in Yugoslavia by plane from Geneva on the 6th. In the afternoon of the same day, he held talks with President Tito of the Federal People's Republic of Yugoslavia on Brioni Island.

　　On the Yugoslav side, Vice-President of the Federal Executive Council Kardelj and Secretary-General of Foreign Affairs Popovic also participated in the talks; on the American side, U.S. Ambassador to Yugoslavia Liddleberg and State Department Counselor MacArthur II also participated.

　　A communique issued after the talks stated that both sides reached a consensus on many issues, particularly on further efforts to improve international relations and the possibility and necessity of expanding friendly cooperation between the two countries. The communique also stated that the talks once again proved that personal contact is useful for mutual understanding of viewpoints and for promoting the cause of peace and international cooperation in the spirit of the United Nations Charter. The communique emphasized that the two sides had exchanged views on the international situation and issues of common concern, and Secretary of State Dulles spoke about these issues at a press conference he held subsequently.

　　Dulles already left Yugoslavia for Geneva on the evening of the same day.

　　Xinhua News Agency, Prague, 7th. According to a report by the Yugoslav News Agency: U.S. Secretary of State Dulles held a press conference in Brioni on the 6th after meeting with President Tito of the Federal People's Republic of Yugoslavia. Dulles said at the conference that President Tito and he had studied the most important current international issues during the talks. The theme of the talks was Mr. Dulles providing President Tito with information on the course of the Geneva Conference, particularly on the discussion of European security issues and the German question. Dulles also said that he hoped to hear President Tito's opinions on the possibility of resolving these issues. The situation in the Near East was also discussed, especially considering the fact that President Tito will be visiting Egypt. Dulles continued, saying that the independence of Eastern European countries was also discussed, and he added, "We reached a common belief that these countries have the right to achieve their independence, free from any external interference, and have the right to decide for themselves what they want to do."

### 杜勒斯同铁托总统会谈

1955-11-08
第4版()
专栏：

    杜勒斯同铁托总统会谈
    新华社布拉格七日电　据南斯拉夫通讯社贝尔格莱德讯：美国国务卿杜勒斯和随行人员六日从日内瓦乘飞机到达南斯拉夫。当日下午他在布莱俄尼岛和南斯拉夫联邦人民共和国总统铁托举行了会谈。
    参加这次会谈的南斯拉夫方面还有联邦执行委员会副主席卡德尔和外交秘书长波波维奇；美国方面还有美国驻南斯拉夫大使立德尔贝格和国务院参事麦克阿瑟第二。
    会谈后发表的公报说，双方在许多问题上、特别是在进一步努力改善国际关系和扩大两国之间的友好合作的可能性和必要性的问题上，取得了一致意见。公报又说，会谈再一次证明，个人接触对于互相了解看法和按照联合国宪章的精神促进和平和国际合作的事业是有用处的。公报着重指出，双方已经就国际形势和共同有关的问题交换了意见，杜勒斯国务卿在他随后举行的记者招待会上谈到了这些问题。
    杜勒斯已经在当日晚上离南斯拉夫前往日内瓦。
    新华社布拉格七日电　据南斯拉夫通讯社报道：美国国务卿杜勒斯六日在和南斯拉夫联邦人民共和国总统铁托会谈后在布莱俄尼举行了记者招待会。杜勒斯在会上说，铁托总统和他本人在会谈时曾经研究了当前最重要的国际问题。会谈中的主题是杜勒斯先生向铁托总统提供关于日内瓦会议的经过情况、特别是关于讨论欧洲安全问题和德国问题的情况。杜勒斯又说，他希望听取铁托总统对解决这些问题的可能性的意见。同时还讨论了近东局势问题，特别是考虑到这个事实：铁托总统将到埃及进行访问。杜勒斯继续说，还讨论了东欧国家的独立问题，他又说，我们取得了共同的信念：这些国家有权利取得它们的独立，不受任何外来的干涉，而且有权利自己决定他们希望做的事情。
