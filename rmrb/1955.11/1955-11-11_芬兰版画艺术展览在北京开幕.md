Finnish Print Art Exhibition Opens in Beijing

　　The "Finnish Print Art Exhibition," jointly organized by the Chinese People's Association for Cultural Relations with Foreign Countries and the Chinese Artists Association, opened on the morning of the 10th at the Waterside Pavilion in Zhongshan Park, Beijing.

　　Finnish prints experienced significant development in the late 19th and early 20th centuries. This exhibition features the works of forty-two Finnish printmakers, totaling more than one hundred and thirty prints, including woodcuts, lithographs, and etchings. These prints depict various subjects such as people's lives, landscapes, and still lifes, showcasing a unique style in portraying the character of figures and expressing natural scenery.

　　Yang Hansheng, Vice President of the Chinese People's Association for Cultural Relations with Foreign Countries, delivered a speech at the opening ceremony, congratulating the opening of the Finnish Print Art Exhibition. He said that not long ago, an exhibition of Chinese woodblock watermarks was held in Finland, and the Chinese Classical Opera and Dance Theater also visited Finland for performances. These cultural exchange activities will greatly enhance the friendship between the Chinese and Finnish people.

　　Finnish Ambassador to China, Mr. Sundström, then delivered a speech, pointing out that the opening of the exhibition is a major event in the cultural exchange between China and Finland, which will promote mutual understanding and the growth of friendship between the two peoples.

　　Professor Tuhka, the exhibition director and Finnish printmaker, also spoke at the opening ceremony.

　　Among those who attended today's opening ceremony were Zheng Zhenduo and Xia Yan, Vice Ministers of the Ministry of Culture of the People's Republic of China, Liu Aifeng, Vice Minister of the Ministry of Higher Education, Ye Shengtao, Vice Minister of the Ministry of Education, Lei Renmin, Vice Minister of the Ministry of Foreign Trade, Huang Hua, Director of the Department of West European and African Affairs of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, Jiang Feng, Liu Kaiqu, and Wu Zuoren, Vice Chairmen of the Chinese Artists Association, and renowned figures from the art world such as Gu Yuan, Li Hua, Wang Chaowen, Yu Feian, and Chen Banding, as well as diplomatic officials from various countries stationed in China. (Xinhua News Agency)

### 芬兰版画艺术展览在北京开幕

1955-11-11
第3版()
专栏：

　　芬兰版画艺术展览在北京开幕
    中国人民对外文化协会、中国美术家协会共同举办的“芬兰版画艺术展览”，十日上午在北京中山公园水榭开幕。
    芬兰的版画在十九世纪末和二十世纪初已有很大发展。这次展览会展出了四十二位芬兰版画家的作品，共有木刻、石版、腐蚀版等版画一百三十多幅。这些版画描绘了人民生活、风景和静物等不同题材，在刻划人物的性格和表现自然景色上都有独特的风格。
    中国人民对外文化协会副会长阳翰笙在开幕式上致词，祝贺芬兰版画艺术展览的开幕。他说，不久前，在芬兰举行了中国木版水印画的展览，中国古典歌舞剧团也到芬兰进行访问演出。这些文化交流活动，将使中芬两国人民的友谊大为增进。
    芬兰驻华大使孙士敦接着致词，他指出展览会的开幕是中芬两国文化交流中的一件大事，它将促进两国人民的相互了解和友谊的增长。
    展览主任、芬兰版画家图赫卡教授也在开幕式上讲了话。
    参加今天开幕式的，有中华人民共和国文化部副部长郑振铎、夏衍，高等教育部副部长刘皑风，教育部副部长叶圣陶，对外贸易部副部长雷任民，外交部西欧非洲司司长黄华，中国美术家协会副主席江丰、刘开渠、吴作人和美术界著名人士古元、李桦、王朝闻、于非暗、陈半丁等，以及各国驻华外交官员。　（新华社）
