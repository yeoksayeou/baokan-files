Title: Ceylon Prime Minister States at Bangkok Press Conference: Unwilling to Participate in Southeast Asia Treaty Organization
Author:
Date: 1955-11-23
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, Bangkok dispatch: Ceylon Prime Minister Sir John Kotelawala, who is currently visiting Thailand, reiterated at a press conference held in Bangkok on the 21st that Ceylon is unwilling to participate in the Southeast Asia Treaty Organization. He said, "We have many things to do in terms of domestic construction, and we don't understand who we are supposed to fight." He also said that after the Bandung Conference, major changes have taken place in Southeast Asia, and there is more mutual trust.

When Kotelawala spoke about Ceylon's trade with China, he said that this is a very natural thing.

In response to a question from a reporter regarding the visit of Bulganin and Khrushchev to India, Kotelawala said that visits of this nature will help to build peace. He expressed his support for mutual visits between leaders of various countries.

### 锡兰总理在曼谷记者招待会上表示  不愿意参加东南亚条约组织

1955-11-23
第4版()
专栏：

　　锡兰总理在曼谷记者招待会上表示
　  不愿意参加东南亚条约组织
    据新华社讯曼谷消息：现正访问泰国的锡兰总理科特拉瓦拉二十一日在曼谷举行的记者招待会上再次表明锡兰不愿意参加东南亚条约组织。他说：“我们国内建设方面有许多事情要做，而且我们不明白要跟谁打仗。”他还说，在万隆会议以后，东南亚起了重大的变化，互相信任的人更加多了。
    科特拉瓦拉在谈到锡兰同中国进行贸易一事时说，这是很自然的事情。
    科特拉瓦拉在回答新闻记者提出的关于布尔加宁和赫鲁晓夫访问印度的问题时说，这种性质的访问将有助于缔造和平。他表示赞成各国领袖之间的相互访问。
