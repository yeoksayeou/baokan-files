Title: Soviet Proposals on Arms Reduction and Banning of Atomic Weapons
Author: 
Date: 1955-11-12
Page: 4

　　Soviet Proposals on Arms Reduction and Banning of Atomic Weapons
　　(I)
　　In order to ease tensions in international relations, strengthen mutual trust between countries, and eliminate the threat of a new war, the governments of the Soviet Union, the United States, the United Kingdom, and France believe it is necessary to strive for the early conclusion of an international convention on arms reduction and the prohibition of atomic weapons.
　　After exchanging views on the issue of arms reduction and the prohibition of atomic weapons, they reached a consensus on the following points:
　　1. The levels of armed forces for the United States, the Soviet Union, and China should be set at 1 million to 1.5 million each; the levels of armed forces for the United Kingdom and France should be set at 650,000 each; the level set for China and other issues related to the Chinese armed forces should be considered with the participation of the Government of the People's Republic of China.
　　The levels of armed forces for all other countries should not exceed 150,000 to 200,000, and should be agreed upon at an appropriate international conference.
　　2. Measures to completely prohibit atomic and hydrogen weapons should take effect after conventional armaments and armed forces have been reduced by 75 percent according to the agreed reduction amounts. The work of eliminating and destroying these weapons from the armaments of all countries should be completed during the process of reducing the last 25 percent of the armaments by the agreed reduction amounts. After this, all atomic materials should only be used for peaceful purposes.
　　3. While starting to take measures to reduce armaments and armed forces, and before the agreement on the complete prohibition of atomic and hydrogen weapons takes effect, the four countries should solemnly guarantee not to use nuclear weapons, considering nuclear weapons to be prohibited for them. Exceptions to the provisions of this article may be allowed if the Security Council makes corresponding resolutions for the purpose of defending against aggression.
　　4. As one of the preliminary measures to implement the plan for arms reduction and the prohibition of atomic weapons, countries possessing atomic and hydrogen weapons should guarantee to stop conducting tests of such weapons.
　　5. Effective international supervision should be established over the implementation of measures for arms reduction and the prohibition of atomic weapons.
　　(II)
　　The Foreign Ministers of the four countries believe it is necessary to strive to reach the necessary agreements on the issues of the aforementioned convention that will be considered in the United Nations Organization and on which a consensus has not yet been reached.
　　In the spirit of easing international tensions, strengthening trust between countries, and stopping the arms race, the Foreign Ministers of the four countries agreed that it is first necessary to review the principles included in the following proposals:
　　1. The Soviet Union's proposal of May 10 this year on the issue of arms reduction, the prohibition of atomic weapons, and the elimination of the threat of a new war.
　　2. The US President's proposal of July 21 on aerial photography and the exchange of military information.
　　3. The British Government's proposals of July 21 and August 29 on disarmament issues.
　　4. The French government's proposal on implementing financial supervision over disarmament and using the resources saved from disarmament for peaceful purposes.
　　(III)
　　At the same time, the governments of the Soviet Union, the United States, the United Kingdom, and France, determined not to allow the use of weapons of mass destruction of people—atomic weapons and hydrogen weapons—and determined to save the people of all countries from the threat of devastating atomic war, solemnly declare:
　　Before the conclusion of an international convention on arms reduction and the prohibition of atomic weapons, the Soviet Union, the United States, the United Kingdom, and France undertake the obligation not to be the first to use atomic weapons and hydrogen weapons against any country, and call on all other countries to participate in this declaration. (Xinhua News Agency)

### 苏联关于裁减军备和禁止原子武器的建议

1955-11-12
第4版()
专栏：

　　苏联关于裁减军备和禁止原子武器的建议
　　（一）
    为了缓和国际关系中的紧张局势、加强国与国之间的相互信任和消除新战争的威胁，苏联、美国、英国和法国四国政府认为有必要为了早日缔结一项关于裁减军备和禁止原子武器的国际公约而努力。
    它们就裁减军备和禁止原子武器的问题交换意见后，对下列各项取得了一致的意见：
    一、美国、苏联和中国的武装部队的水平应规定各为一百万到一百五十万人；英国和法国的武装部队的水平应规定各为六十五万人；为中国规定的水平以及与中国武装部队问题有关的其他问题应该在中华人民共和国政府参加之下加以考虑。
    所有其他国家的武装部队的水平不得超过十五万到二十万人，并且应该在一次适当的国际会议上予以商定。
    二、在常规军备和武装部队按照一致商定的裁减数额减少了百分之七十五以后，完全禁止原子武器和氢武器的措施就应该生效。从各国军备中取缔这些武器并加以销毁的工作应在裁减一致商定的裁减数额的最后百分之二十五的军备的过程中完成。在此以后，所有的原子物质应只能用于和平目的。
    三、在开始采取裁减军备和武装部队的措施的同时，在关于完全禁止原子武器和氢武器的协议生效以前，四国应庄严保证不使用核子武器，认为核子武器对他们来说是被禁止的。如果为了对侵略进行防御，当安全理事会作出相应的决议的时候，可以允许对本条规定有例外。
    四、作为执行裁减军备和禁止原子武器的计划的初步措施之一，拥有原子武器和氢武器的国家应保证停止进行这类武器的试验。
    五、应对裁减军备和禁止原子武器的措施的实行建立有效的国际监督。
　　（二）
    四国外交部长认为必须努力对将在联合国组织中考虑的上述公约的还没有取得一致意见的问题达成必要的协议。
    本着缓和国际紧张局势、加强国与国之间的信任和停止扩充军备的愿望，四国外交部长同意：在这方面首先必须审查以下的各项建议中包括的那些原则：
    一、苏联在今年五月十日提出的关于裁减军备、禁止原子武器和消除新战争威胁问题的建议。
    二、美国总统在七月二十一日提出的关于空中摄影和交换军事情报的建议。
    三、英国政府在七月二十一日和八月二十九日提出的关于裁军问题的建议。
    四、法国政府提出的关于对裁军实行财政监督和把因裁军而节省出来的资源用于和平目的的建议。
　　（三）
    同时，苏联、美国、英国和法国四国政府决心不准使用大规模屠杀人的武器——原子武器和氢武器，决心使各国人民免遭毁灭性的原子战争的威胁，特庄严地宣布：
    在关于裁减军备和禁止原子武器的国际公约缔结以前，苏联、美国、英国和法国承担不首先对任何国家使用原子武器和氢武器的义务，并且呼吁其余一切国家也参加这一宣言。（新华社）
