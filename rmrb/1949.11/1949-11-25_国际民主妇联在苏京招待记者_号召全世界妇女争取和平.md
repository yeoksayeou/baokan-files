Title: International Democratic Women's Federation Hosts Reception for Journalists in Soviet Capital; Calls on Women Worldwide to Fight for Peace
Author:
Date: 1949-11-25
Page: 1

The International Democratic Women's Federation Hosts Reception for Journalists in Soviet Capital
Calls on Women Worldwide to Fight for Peace

    The International Democratic Women’s Federation recently held a reception in Moscow for journalists from various countries.
    The Federation’s Secretary-General, Madame Vaillant-Couturier, delivered a speech at the reception, pointing out that the most important task of women worldwide today is to safeguard peace. She called on women of all countries to actively participate in the movement to defend peace, and to oppose the instigators of new wars. She said: The International Democratic Women's Federation must unite even more closely with women of the world, launch extensive mass movements under the leadership of the Standing Committee, expand and strengthen the struggle for peace, fight to end the wars in Greece, Vietnam, Indonesia, and Malaya, and strive for the immediate reduction of armaments, the prohibition of atomic bombs, and the signing of peace pacts by the great powers.
