Title: Greeting the Asian-Australian Trade Union Conference: Northeast Federation of Trade Unions Issues Notice
Author:
Date: 1949-11-07
Page: 1

　　Greeting the Asian-Australian Trade Union Conference
  　Northeast Federation of Trade Unions Issues Notice
    Xinhua News Agency, Shenyang, November 6th. In order to welcome the Asian-Australian Trade Union Conference to be held soon in Beijing, the Northeast Federation of Trade Unions issued a notice on the 5th, calling on trade unions in various parts of Northeast China to immediately combine the current movement to create new production records with publicity about the great significance of the Asian-Australian Trade Union Conference being held in New China, and to introduce the struggles of Asian and Australian countries for national liberation, democracy, and freedom, so that the worker comrades understand the great responsibility of the Chinese working class in this conference, and strive to bring into play the internationalism and patriotism of the working class. The notice pointed out: After the conference, delegates from various countries will come to visit various parts of Northeast China. In addition to warmly welcoming and receiving them at that time, this opportunity should also be used to carry out internationalist education among the workers.
    Xinhua News Agency, Shanghai, November 4th. The delegation from our country attending the World Federation of Trade Unions Congress left Shanghai for Beijing on the evening of the 3rd. After arriving in Shanghai on October 22nd, the delegation began to convey the resolutions of the World Federation of Trade Unions and report on their experiences abroad to the workers and staff of Shanghai. During the eleven days in Shanghai, the delegation successively held 134 conferences, giving reports to trade unions in various industries in the city, as well as the People's Liberation Army, the Garrison Force, and teachers, with a total audience of more than 210,000 people. On October 31st, seven members of the delegation were sent to Wuxi to give reports to factories, organs, and schools, holding a total of 19 conferences, with a total audience of more than 11,000 people. These reports greatly encouraged the workers and people from all walks of life in Shanghai and Wuxi. In order to quickly complete the task so as to rush back to Beijing to participate in the preparatory work for the Asian-Australian Trade Union Conference, the delegation worked extremely intensively.

### 迎接亚澳工会会议　东北总工会发出通知

1949-11-07
第1版()
专栏：

　　迎接亚澳工会会议
  　东北总工会发出通知
    【新华社沈阳六日电】东北总工会为迎接即将在北京召开的亚澳工会会议，特于五日发出通知，号召东北各地工会立即结合当前的创造生产新纪录运动，宣传亚澳工会会议在新中国召开的巨大意义，介绍亚澳各国争取民族解放、民主自由的斗争情况，使工人兄弟们认识中国工人阶级在这次会议中的重大责任，努力发挥工人阶级国际主义和爱国主义的精神。通知指出：会议之后各国代表将来东北各地参观，届时除进行热烈欢迎和招待外，并应利用这一机会在工人中展开国际主义的教育工作。
    【新华社上海四日电】我国出席世界工联大会的代表团于三日晚离沪返京。该团自十月二十二日抵沪后，即开始向沪市职工传达世界工联决议及报告出国经过。该团在上海的十一天中，曾先后召开了一百三十四次大会，向本市各业工会和人民解放军、警备部队、教职员等作报告，听众共达二十一万多人。十月三十一日，该团曾派出七位团员前往无锡向各工厂、机关、学校作报告，共举行大会十九次，听众共达一万一千多人。他们的这些报告给上海、无锡的工人及各界市民以很大的鼓舞，这次代表团为了迅速完成任务以便赶回北京参加筹备亚澳工会会议的工作，工作极为紧张。
