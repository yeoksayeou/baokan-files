Title: World Peace Council Standing Committee Passes Resolution Opposing Foreign Interference in Indonesia's Internal Affairs, Advocates for Swift Negotiations to Restore Peace in Algeria
Author: 
Date: 1958-03-27
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, New Delhi, March 26th: The World Peace Council Standing Committee today passed the following resolution regarding the situation in Indonesia:

　　The peace forces of the world are deeply concerned about the grave situation of the recent developments in Indonesia.

　　Foreign interference threatens the unity of the Indonesian people, the integrity of its territory, its independence, and its sovereignty. It creates international chaos and endangers world peace.

　　In order to maintain peace in this region, the principle of non-interference in the internal affairs of other countries, as declared in Bandung, must be adopted as it is elsewhere.

　　The Standing Committee of the World Peace Council calls upon public opinion to be vigilant in ensuring that the whole world abides by this principle, which is one of the foundations of peace.

Xinhua News Agency, New Delhi, March 26th: The World Peace Council Standing Committee issued the following statement on the Algerian question:

　　Recent events in Tunisia demonstrate that the Algerian war carries the risk of spreading to the whole of North Africa at any moment, thereby threatening world peace.

　　We must, without delay, halt such a war, which is becoming increasingly brutal for combatants and civilians alike. This war is becoming increasingly inhumane, as the French government refuses to recognize belligerent rights, thus violating the humanitarian principles stipulated in international conventions. The people of all countries must remind their governments that they are obliged to comply with the unanimously adopted resolutions of the United Nations General Assembly, which stipulate that a peaceful, democratic, and just solution to the Algerian question must be achieved through negotiations, in accordance with the principles and purposes of the United Nations Charter.

　　The Mediterranean Pact that is being planned is not a solution to the Algerian question, because it seeks to keep Algeria in a subordinate position and will deepen the division of the world into two military blocs.

　　Only by recognizing the independence and national sovereignty of the Algerian people will it be possible to end hostilities and establish a new and voluntary relationship between France and Algeria.

　　The actions of world public opinion, and the growing peace trends in France and other countries, working together against the continued pursuit of a policy of strength, can and must take measures without delay to enable the restoration of peace in Algeria through negotiations.

### 世界和平理事会常委会通过决议  反对外国干涉印度尼西亚内政  主张迅速谈判恢复阿尔及利亚和平

1958-03-27
第5版()
专栏：

    世界和平理事会常委会通过决议
    反对外国干涉印度尼西亚内政
    主张迅速谈判恢复阿尔及利亚和平
    新华社新德里26日电　世界和平理事会常务委员会今天上午通过了关于印度尼西亚问题的决议如下：
    全世界的和平力量对于印度尼西亚最近事件发展的严重局势深为忧虑。
    外国的干涉威胁着印度尼西亚人民的团结、领土的完整、独立和主权。它造成了国际间的混乱并且危及了世界和平。
    为了维护这个地区的和平，必须像在其他地方一样，采用在万隆宣布的互不干涉他国内政的原则。
    世界和平理事会常务委员会要求舆论警惕，以保证全世界都遵守这个作为和平基础之一的原则。
    新华社新德里26日电　世界和平理事会常务委员会就阿尔及利亚问题发表声明如下：
    突尼斯最近的事态表明，阿尔及利亚战争随时随刻都有蔓延到整个北非从而威胁世界和平的危险。
    我们必须毫不延迟地制止这样一个对于战斗人员和平民越来越残酷的战争。这个战争越来越变得惨无人道了，法国政府拒绝承认交战权利，因此就违反国际公约所规定的人道原则。所有国家的人民必须提醒他们的政府，它们有义务遵守一致通过的联合国大会的决议，这些决议规定遵照联合国宪章的原则和目的，通过谈判取得阿尔及利亚问题的和平、民主和公正的解决。
    正在策划中的地中海公约并不是解决阿尔及利亚问题的办法，因为它要使阿尔及利亚继续处于附属地位，并且会加深世界分裂为两个军事集团的状态。
    只有承认阿尔及利亚人民的独立和民族主权，才有可能结束敌对行为，并且在法国和阿尔及利亚之间建立起一种新的和自愿的关系。
    世界舆论的行动以及法国和其他国家中日益强大的和平趋势，协力一致反对继续奉行实力政策，能够而且必须毫不延迟地采取措施，使能通过谈判来恢复阿尔及利亚的和平。
