Title: Pakistani President Suggests Major Powers Sign Non-Aggression Treaty
Author: 
Date: 1958-03-17
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, Karachi, March 15th. According to Karachi newspapers today, Pakistani President Mirza has suggested that major powers sign a non-aggression treaty and hold a summit meeting.

President Mirza made this suggestion in an interview with the Karachi monthly "Diplomat." He suggested that signing a non-aggression treaty and holding a summit meeting should be the first steps. He also said that the foreign ministers of the countries concerned should first lay the groundwork for such a meeting.

President Mirza also said that Pakistan supports the anti-colonial struggle in Africa.

In the interview, Mirza also defended the Baghdad Pact and the Southeast Asia Treaty Organization, as well as Pakistan's participation in these military treaties.

### 巴基斯坦总统建议  各大国签订互不侵犯条约

1958-03-17
第5版()
专栏：

    巴基斯坦总统建议
    各大国签订互不侵犯条约
    新华社卡拉奇15日电　据今天卡拉奇报纸报道，巴基斯坦总统米尔扎建议各大国签订互不侵犯条约和举行最高级会议。
    米尔扎总统是在对卡拉奇“外交家”月刊发表谈话时这样说的。他建议签订互不侵犯条约和举行最高级会议作为第一步，他又说，有关国家的外交部长应当事先为这种会议准备基础。
    米尔扎总统还说，巴基斯坦支持非洲的反殖民主义斗争。
    米尔扎在谈话中还为巴格达条约和东南亚条约组织以及巴基斯坦参加这些军事条约的行动辩解。
