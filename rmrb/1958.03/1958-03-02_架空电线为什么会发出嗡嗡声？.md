Title: Why do overhead power lines hum?
Author: Guilin
Date: 1958-03-02
Page: 4

Why do overhead power lines hum?
　　When we walk on the road, we often hear the overhead power lines humming. Why is this?

　　It turns out that when the weather is cold, the length of the erected power lines shortens due to the effects of thermal expansion and contraction. At this time, it is like a tightened erhu string. The wind keeps blowing, and it vibrates like an erhu string being plucked. If the number of vibrations reaches 20 to 20,000 times per second, it is just within the range of human hearing, and we hear the humming sound.

### 架空电线为什么会发出嗡嗡声？
桂林
1958-03-02
第4版()
专栏：为什么？

    架空电线为什么会发出嗡嗡声？
  当我们走在公路上，常听到路旁架设的电线发出嗡嗡的声音。这是为什么呢？
    原来，架设的电线，当天冷时，由于冷缩热胀的作用，它的长度缩短了。这时就好像被拉紧的胡琴弦子，风不断吹击着，它就和胡琴弦子被弹着时一样地发生振动。振动的次数，如果每秒达二十至二万次，就刚好在人们的听觉范围内，我们就听到了嗡嗡的声音。
                          （桂林）
