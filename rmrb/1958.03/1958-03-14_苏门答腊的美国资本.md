    American Capital in Sumatra

    According to a report in the American "Newsweek" on March 10th, American monopoly capital has huge interests in Sumatra, Indonesia. The main American companies here are as follows:

    (1) Texaco: Employs 4,000 Indonesian workers and 150 American personnel, owns huge oil fields in Central Sumatra, and produces 50 million barrels of oil (crude oil) annually (approximately half of Indonesia's oil production). The company's original capital was approximately $75 million. Recently, it has increased its investment by $50 million.

    (2) Mobil Oil: Employs 13,000 workers (96% Indonesian), and owns oil fields in Central and South Sumatra. It has a refinery in Palembang, producing more than 20 million barrels of oil annually. The company's total assets amount to $135 million to $140 million.

    (3) Goodyear Tire & Rubber Company: Owns 55,000 acres of rubber plantations in Sumatra, with capital of $12 million to $15 million.

    (4) U.S. Rubber Company: Has 60,000 acres of rubber plantations in Sumatra, employing 11,500 workers, and producing 36 million pounds of rubber annually.

### 苏门答腊的美国资本

1958-03-14
第5版()
专栏：小资料

    苏门答腊的美国资本
    据3月10日美国“新闻周刊”报道，美国垄断资本在印度尼西亚的苏门答腊拥有巨大的利益。在这里，主要的美国公司的情况如下：
    （一）德士古石油公司：雇有印度尼西亚职工四千人，美国人员一百五十人，拥有中苏门答腊的巨大油田，每年生产石油（原油）五千万桶（约等于印度尼西亚油产量的一半）。公司原有资本约七千五百万美元。最近又增加投资五千万美元。
    （二）美孚油公司：雇有职工一万三千人（96％为印度尼西亚人），在中苏门答腊及南苏门答腊均拥有油田。在巨港有一炼油厂，每年产油二千万桶以上。公司财产总值达一亿三千五百万至一亿四千万美元。
    （三）固特异轮胎及橡胶公司：在苏门答腊拥有五万五千英亩橡胶园，资本额为一千二百万至一千五百万美元。
    （四）美国橡胶公司：在苏门答腊有六万英亩橡胶园，雇用职工一万一千五百人，每年产橡胶三千六百万磅。
