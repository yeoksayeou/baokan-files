Title: U.S. Economic Recession Severe, U.S. President and Treasury Secretary Urgently Discuss Countermeasures
Author:
Date: 1958-03-14
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, Washington, March 13th: U.S. President Eisenhower, after summoning Republican congressional leaders on the 11th to discuss countermeasures to deal with the surge in unemployment, summoned Treasury Secretary Anderson on the 12th to discuss the "economic recession problem."

Afterwards, Anderson read a reassuring statement to reporters, saying that the President had taken steps to help "restore the economy." When talking about using tax cuts to stop the economic deterioration, Anderson indicated that the U.S. government intends to wait and see if its current "anti-recession" measures will work before making a decision on tax cuts.

Shortly after Anderson issued the statement, White House spokesman Hagerty announced that on the same day, Eisenhower had submitted a request to Congress for approval of an additional $85 million in expenses, and would submit another request to Congress for $125 million this weekend to accelerate public works projects to support U.S. economic activity.

### 美国经济衰退严重  美总统同财长急商对策

1958-03-14
第6版()
专栏：

    美国经济衰退严重
    美总统同财长急商对策
    新华社13日讯  华盛顿消息：美国总统艾森豪威尔继11日召集国会共和党领袖商讨应付失业激增的对策以后，在12日又召见财政部长安德逊商谈“经济衰退问题”。
    事后，安德逊对记者们宣读了一篇安定人心的声明，他说，总统已经采取了步骤，来帮助使“经济得到恢复”。安德逊在谈到用减税办法来制止经济恶化的时候表示，美国政府打算等到弄明白它目前的一些“反衰退”措施是否能起作用之后，再来就减税问题作出决定。
    在安德逊发表声明之后不久，白宫发言人哈格蒂宣布，在同一天，艾森豪威尔已经向国会提出请求批准一笔八千五百万美元的追加费用，本周末还要再向国会请求拨款一亿二千五百万美元，以便加速进行公共工程来支持美国的经济活动。
