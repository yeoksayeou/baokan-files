Title: Soviet Artificial Satellite to Pass Over Beijing Today and Tomorrow

Author:

Date: 1958-03-22
Page: 7

Xinhua News Agency, March 21st: According to a telegram received by relevant departments in Beijing from the Soviet Astronomical Committee, the Soviet Union's second artificial satellite can be seen over Beijing on the evening of the 22nd and the morning of the 23rd.

On the 22nd, at 18:29 Beijing time, the satellite will appear at an azimuth angle of 0 degrees (due south) and a height of 28 degrees. At 18:31, the satellite will advance to an azimuth angle of 300 degrees (southeast by east) and a height of 52 degrees. On the 23rd, at 4:18, the satellite's position will be at an azimuth angle of 67 degrees (southwest by west) and a height of 38 degrees.

### 苏联人造卫星今明日过北京

1958-03-22
第7版()
专栏：

    苏联人造卫星
    今明日过北京
    新华社21日讯　据北京有关方面接获苏联天文学委员会电报，北京22日晚和23日晨都可以看到苏联第二个人造卫星。
    22日北京时间十八点二十九分，卫星出现在方位角零度（正南方）、高度二十八度的位置，十八点三十一分，卫星前进到方位角三百度（东南偏东）、高度五十二度的地方。23日四点十八分，卫星的位置是方位角六十七度（西南偏西），高度三十八度。
