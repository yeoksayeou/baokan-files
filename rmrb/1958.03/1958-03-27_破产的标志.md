Title: The Sign of Bankruptcy
Author:
Date: 1958-03-27
Page: 6

　　The Sign of Bankruptcy

　　According to Western news agency reports, America’s five major automobile companies are planning to launch a promotional campaign to deal with the increasingly severe economic recession. At the same time, stores in Paterson, New Jersey, are planning special auctions to stimulate purchasing power, and are calling this special auction campaign "Believe in America" Week.

　　This shows how worthless those boasts about the basic health of the American economy are.

　　In particular, using special auctions as a campaign to salvage "faith" in America all the more highlights the sad mood of the American capitalists. Because, holding special auctions can only be a sign of bankruptcy, not a means of restoring "faith" in America.

### 破产的标志

1958-03-27
第6版()
专栏：札记

    破产的标志
    据西方通讯社报道，美国的五大汽车公司正计划发动一个推销运动来对付日益严重的经济衰退，同时新泽西州的巴特逊市的商店也计划进行特价拍卖来促进购买力，并且把这种特价拍卖的运动叫做“相信美国”周。
    这说明那种关于美国经济基本健康的吹嘘是如何的不值钱。
    特别是把特价拍卖当作一种挽救对美国的“信仰”的运动，就更突出地表明了美国的资本家们的悲哀心情了。因为，实行特价拍卖只能是一种破产的标志，而不是什么恢复对美国的“信仰”的手段。
