Title: Soviet Industrial Production to Make a Great Leap Forward; Various Republics and Economic Regions are Formulating Long-Term Plans

Author:
Date: 1958-03-12
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, Moscow, March 11th: The Soviet Union's various republics and economic regions are currently formulating long-term plans for developing the national economy from 1959 to 1965. From reports in the Soviet newspapers, it is already possible to see that Soviet industrial production will experience a great leap forward in these seven years.

The Soviet Seven-Year Plan is being formulated after the restructuring of industrial and construction management and the decentralization of leadership. Therefore, when formulating plans, various localities can fully consider the conditions and production potential of their regions, so as to make more rational use of the region's natural resources. The Rostov Economic Region of the Russian Federation has large coal reserves. Therefore, the Seven-Year Plan stipulates that the output of coking coal and anthracite should be increased by 100% and 50% respectively. Lviv, a major industrial center of the Ukrainian Republic, has a solid industrial base. In the next seven years, its total industrial output will increase threefold. The Belorussian Republic has already established a strong machine-building industry, but considering the need for comprehensive economic development and existing conditions, Belorussia has decided to rapidly develop the machine-building industry, increasing output threefold compared to 1956 by 1965.

The resource-rich eastern region will be developed to a greater extent during the Seven-Year Plan. During this period, the Kazakh Republic will develop the coal mining, chemical, power, and metallurgical industries at a very fast pace. Kazakhstan's machine-building industry is not yet very developed, but by 1965, almost all of the republic's nine economic regions will have established new machine-building industrial enterprises. Among them, the output of the machine-building industry in the Karaganda Economic Region will increase ninefold. By 1965, the Uzbek Republic will develop into the most important heavy industry region in the Soviet Central Asian region. In 1965, its steel output will increase tenfold compared to 1958, coal mining will almost triple last year's output, oil and electricity production will double last year's output, and fluorite production will rank first in the Soviet Union.

The prospects for Siberia and the Soviet Far East are also encouraging. Eight years later, Siberia will develop into a more important base for Soviet electricity, steel, coal mining, and other industrial production. The total power generation capacity of the power stations in the Krasnoyarsk Economic Region alone will increase more than tenfold compared to last year by 1965.

### 苏联工业生产将大跃进  各加盟共和国和经济区正制订远景计划

1958-03-12
第6版()
专栏：

    苏联工业生产将大跃进
    各加盟共和国和经济区正制订远景计划
    新华社莫斯科11日电　苏联各加盟共和国和各经济区现在正在制订1959—1965年发展国民经济长期计划。从苏联报纸的报道中已经可以初步看出，苏联工业生产在这七年中将获得飞跃的发展。
    苏联七年远景计划是在改组了工业和建筑业的管理工作、把领导权力下放以后制订的。因此，各地在制订计划时能够充分考虑本地区条件和生产潜力，以便做到更合理地利用本地区的自然资源。俄罗斯联邦的罗斯托夫经济区的煤藏量很大。因此，七年计划规定要把炼焦用煤和无烟煤产量分别增加一倍和半倍。乌克兰共和国的大工业中心里沃夫有着雄厚的工业基础，在未来的七年中它的工业总产量将增加两倍。白俄罗斯共和国已经建立了强大的机器制造工业，但是，考虑到经济综合发展的需要和现有条件，白俄罗斯仍然决定要迅速发展机器制造工业，到1965年把产量增加为1956年的三倍。
    资源丰富的东部地区在七年计划期间将得到更大的开发。在这期间，哈萨克共和国将以非常快的速度发展采煤、化学、电力和冶金工业。哈萨克的机器制造工业目前还不很发达，但是在1965年以前全共和国的九个经济区差不多都将建立起新的机器制造工业企业。其中，卡拉干达经济区的机器制造工业产量就将增加九倍。乌兹别克共和国到1965年将发展成苏联中亚细亚地区最主要的重工业区。1965年它的钢铁产量将增加为1958年的十倍，采煤量差不多将增加为去年的三倍，石油和电力产量将增加为去年的两倍，萤石产量将跃居苏联第一位。
    西伯利亚和苏联远东地区的前景也是鼓舞人心的。八年后，西伯利亚将发展成为苏联电力、钢铁、采煤等工业生产的更加重要的基地。单是克拉斯诺雅尔斯克经济区的发电站1965年的总发电能力就将增加为去年的十倍以上。
