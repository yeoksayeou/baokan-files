    True Colors
    The "New York Times" editorial on March 21st put on the face of a promoter of a summit meeting, accusing the Soviet Union's recent letter to British Prime Minister Macmillan of "not only not promoting a summit meeting, but rather hindering it."
    The "New York Times"' accusation is based on: "In a letter to Prime Minister Macmillan, the Soviet Union accused the West of deliberately delaying another head of government meeting while intensifying 'war preparations.' At the same time, it actually demanded a worldwide military standstill to promote the convening of this meeting and prevent the deterioration of the international situation..."
    The Soviet Union's demand for a worldwide military standstill and prevention of the deterioration of the international situation would actually hinder a summit meeting. This logic can only be familiar to those who are fanatical about arms expansion and war preparations. The "New York Times" is proficient in this logic, which is precisely its true color as a loyal mouthpiece of the American monopoly groups who yearn to obtain excessive profits from arms expansion and war preparations.

### 本色

1958-03-24
第5版()
专栏：札记

    本色
    “纽约时报”3月21日的社论装出一副最高级会议的促进者的面孔，指责苏联最近给英国首相麦克米伦的信“不仅没有促进最高级会议，反而促退了它。”
    “纽约时报”这种指责的根据是：“苏联在给麦克米伦首相的一封信中，指责西方一方面故意推迟另一次政府首脑会议，一面加紧‘准备战争’，同时它实际上是要求世界范围的军事停顿，来促进这次会议的召开，防止国际局势恶化……”
    苏联要求世界范围的军事停顿和防止国际局势恶化，竟会促退了最高级会议，这只能是那些扩军备战的狂热病患者所熟悉的逻辑。“纽约时报”精通于这种逻辑，这正是它作为那些渴望从扩军备战中获得超额利润的美国垄断集团的忠实喉舌的本色。
