U.S. Corporate Investment Plummets

U.S. corporate investment is plummeting. According to a report in the March 17th issue of U.S. "Newsweek," investment appropriations by the thousand largest U.S. manufacturing companies in the fourth quarter of 1957 fell by 33% compared to the same period in 1956. "Newsweek" acknowledged that the reason for the decline in investment is due to the increasingly apparent trend of U.S. industrial production capacity exceeding consumer purchasing power. In other words, the U.S. is facing a serious crisis of overproduction.

The sharp decline in corporate investment is a major factor in the U.S. economic recession. The reduction in investment in the fourth quarter of 1957 foreshadows a continued decline in industrial production in 1958. (See attached chart)

### 美国企业投资急剧下降

1958-03-22
第6版()
专栏：小统计

    美国企业投资急剧下降
    美国的企业投资正在急剧下降。据3月17日美国“新闻周刊”报道，美国一千家最大的制造公司在1957年第四季度的投资拨款比1956年同期下降了33％。“新闻周刊”承认，投资下降的原因，是由于美国工业的生产能力超过消费者购买力这种趋势越来越明显了。换句话说，美国正面临着严重的生产过剩危机。
    企业投资的急剧下降，是美国经济衰退的一个主要因素。1957年第四季度投资的减少，预示1958年工业生产将要继续下降。（附图表）
