Title: The Moon Palace Knows Hot and Cold: Intolerably Hot During the Day, Unbearably Cold at Midnight

Author: 
Date: 1958-03-05
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, March 4th: TASS reports that Soviet Doctor of Physics and Mathematics, Markov, said that during the lunar day, equivalent to fourteen and a half Earth days, the temperature on the moon's surface is 120 degrees Celsius when the sun is directly overhead, and -150 degrees Celsius at midnight.

Markov made these remarks at a meeting of the Academic Committee of the Pulkovo Observatory in Leningrad. The task of the meeting was to discuss the preliminary scientific results of measuring the temperature of the moon's surface.

The Pulkovo Observatory measures the moon's temperature using telescopes with thermocouples and huge radio telescopes. Observations show that the moon's crust, especially its outer layer, has very low thermal conductivity.

The Pulkovo Observatory will soon install a special telescope that can measure the temperature of the outermost layer of the moon's crust, which is only about one centimeter thick, using wavelengths of about one millimeter.

### 月宫知冷暖  白昼热难耐  午夜不胜寒

1958-03-05
第6版()
专栏：

    月宫知冷暖
    白昼热难耐
    午夜不胜寒
    新华社4日讯  据塔斯社报道，苏联物理数学博士马尔科夫说，在相当于地球上十四个半昼夜的月球白天里，月球表面在太阳当顶时的温度是摄氏零上一百二十度，在午夜时的温度是零下一百五十度。
    马尔科夫这番话是在列宁格勒普尔科沃总天文台学术委员会会议上宣布的，这次会议的任务是讨论测量月球表面温度的初步科学工作成果。
    普尔科沃天文台测量月球温度用的是带温差电偶的天体望远镜和巨大的无线电天体望远镜。观测结果表明，月球外壳，特别是它的外层，导热性非常小。
    普尔科沃天文台很快还将设置一架专门的天体望远镜，这架望远镜能用一毫米左右的波长来测量月球外壳薄到只有一公分左右的最外层的温度。
