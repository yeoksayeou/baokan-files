Western Press Clippings

    Eisenhower's Contradiction
    Mr. Eisenhower is still using a great deal of rhetoric to elect a Republican Congress in the November elections, but what he needs most now is to get more Republican votes in Congress to support his plans.
    ——U.S. "St. Louis Post-Dispatch"

    Millionaire's Cold Comfort
    Gulbenkian has always been a millionaire. He earns £8,000 a day for life from oil deals alone.
    Gulbenkian's favorite motto is: Life is tough - we all have to work.
   ——British "Daily Mirror"

      Campaign Capital
    What special capital does Mr. Bishop (the promising Conservative parliamentary candidate for the Edmonton district of London) have that makes him stand out among many Conservative candidates?
    I will cite one item: he has a yacht. He often lends it to those who are willing to accommodate him for weekend cruises at sea.
        ——British "Sunday Express"

### 西方报刊拾零

1958-03-05
第5版()
专栏：西方报刊拾零

    西方报刊拾零
    艾森豪威尔的矛盾
    艾森豪威尔先生仍然在为了要在11月的选举中选出一个共和党的国会而大费唇舌，但是现在他最需要的，却是在国会里取得更多共和党人的票数来支持他的计划。
    ——美国“圣路易邮电报”
    百万富翁的风凉话
    古尔本基安一向是一位百万富翁。他单靠了石油一项买卖就终生每天赚钱八千英镑。
    古尔本基安最喜欢的一句格言是：生活是很艰苦的——我们所有的人都必需工作。
   ——英国“每日镜报”
      竞选资本
    比索普先生（伦敦艾德蒙顿区有前途的保守党国会候选人）有什么特殊的资本使他能在许多保守党候选人中突出自己呢？
    我举出一项：他有一只游艇。他经常借给愿意成全他的人周末在海上游玩。
        ——英国“星期快报”
