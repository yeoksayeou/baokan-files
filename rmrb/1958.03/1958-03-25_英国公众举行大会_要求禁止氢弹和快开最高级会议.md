Title: British Public Hold Meetings Demanding a Ban on Hydrogen Bombs and an Early Summit Conference
Author:
Date: 1958-03-25
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, London, March 23rd: Frank Cousins, General Secretary of the Transport and General Workers' Union, which has 1.25 million members, spoke at a gathering of 2,000 people in Bristol on the 22nd, calling for a ban on hydrogen bombs and an early summit conference.

    Cousins ​​criticized British Prime Minister Macmillan's defense of establishing rocket bases in Britain and Foreign Secretary Lloyd's remarks that Britain would be the first to use nuclear weapons in certain circumstances. He said: "If we use this weapon against others, then we will not get the next day's newspaper to read reports about our heroic attitude. For some people, there will be no next day. We cannot let politicians continue to indulge in such fantasies."

    In Manchester, representatives representing one million members of the Labour Party held a meeting and unanimously demanded the launch of a campaign against hydrogen bombs and against American planes carrying hydrogen bombs flying in British airspace.

### 英国公众举行大会  要求禁止氢弹和快开最高级会议

1958-03-25
第5版()
专栏：

    英国公众举行大会
    要求禁止氢弹和快开最高级会议
    据新华社伦敦23日电　拥有会员一百二十五万人的运输与普通工人工会的总书记弗兰克·卡曾斯22日在布利斯托尔举行的两千人的集会上讲话时，要求禁止氢弹和赶快举行最高级会议。
    卡曾斯抨击了英国首相麦克米伦关于在英国建立火箭基地问题的辩解和外交大臣劳埃德关于英国将在某些情况下首先使用核武器的谈话。他说：“如果我们对别人使用这个武器，那么我们就不会拿到第二天的报纸从报上读到有关我们的英勇态度的报道。对于某些人来说，也不会有什么第二天。我们不能让政治家一味作这样的幻想。”
    在曼彻斯特，代表工党一百万党员的代表们举行了会议，一致要求开展反对氢弹和反对载有氢弹的美国飞机在英国领空飞行的运动。
