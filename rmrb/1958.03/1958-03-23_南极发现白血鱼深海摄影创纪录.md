Title: White-blooded Fish Discovered in Antarctica, Deep-Sea Photography Sets Record
Author:
Date: 1958-03-23
Page: 3

    Antarctica Discovery of White-blooded Fish, Deep-Sea Photography Sets Record
    Biologists from the Soviet Second Marine Expedition have discovered a fish in Antarctica that is unknown to the scientific community. This fish has white blood, and its blood lacks hemoglobin.

    Scientists caught eighteen of these white-blooded fish. These unique vertebrates live in the ocean along the Antarctic coast and near the islands adjacent to Antarctica.

    The expedition's zoologists, Barsukov, Korotkevich, and Gusev, had previously studied the biology of the Antarctic Ocean aboard the "Ob" diesel-electric ship from 1956 to 1957.

### 南极发现白血鱼深海摄影创纪录

1958-03-23
第3版()
专栏：

    南极发现白血鱼深海摄影创纪录
    苏联第二海洋考察队的生物学家在南极又发现了一种科学界还不知道的鱼，这种鱼的血液是白色的，血液里没有血色素。
    科学家捕到了十八尾这样的白血鱼，这种独特的脊椎动物生活在南极沿岸和南极附近的岛屿旁的海洋中。
    这个考察队的动物学家巴尔苏科夫、科罗特凯维奇和古谢夫三人曾于1956年到1957年期间在“鄂毕号”柴油电动船上考察过南极海洋中的生物。
