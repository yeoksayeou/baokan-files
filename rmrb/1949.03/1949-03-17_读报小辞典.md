　　Reading Newspaper Little Dictionary
　　Women's International Democratic Federation
　　The Women's International Democratic Federation was founded in Paris, France, on October 26, 1945. At that time, 850 representatives of democratic women from 42 countries participated. Resolutions were passed on striving for peace, anti-fascism, improving the economic, legal, and social status of women, protecting children, and improving education. From December 1st to 6th, 1948, the Women's International Democratic Federation held its Second Congress in Budapest, the capital of Hungary, with the participation of democratic women representatives from more than 50 countries, and the membership increased to 80 million. The new leadership elected by the Second Congress included: President Eugénie Cotton (France); Vice-Presidents Nina Popova (Soviet Union), Dolores Ibárruri (Republican Spain), Cai Chang (China Liberated Area), Jane Watt Vischer (United States); Secretary-General Marie-Claude Vaillant-Couturier (French garment worker).
　　World Congress of Intellectuals in Defense of Peace
　　The World Congress of Intellectuals in Defense of Peace was established in Wrocław, western Poland, from August 25th to 28th, 1948. Representatives of cultural workers from 46 countries attended the congress. At this conference, progressive cultural workers seriously criticized and attacked the reactionary remarks of figures like Huxley and Taylor from Britain, adopted the "Declaration of Cultural Workers in Defense of Peace," and decided to establish a liaison committee. The following were elected as liaison committee members: Fadeyev (famous writer) and Fedoseyev from the Soviet Union, Robeson (black singer) from the United States, Finkelstein (editor of the American "Mainstream" magazine), Neruda (famous poet) from Chile, Aragon (famous poet) and Curie (famous physicist) from France, and Giral (former Prime Minister and political commentator) from Republican Spain, Dąbrowski from Poland, and others.

　　读报小辞典
    国际民主妇联
    国际民主妇联，是一九四五年十月二十六日在法国巴黎成立的。当时有四十二个国家的民主妇女代表八百五十人参加。通过关于争取和平、反法西斯、改善妇女经济、法律、社会地位、保护儿童、改善教育等决议。一九四八年十二月一日至六日，国际民主妇联在匈牙利京城布达佩斯举行第二届代表大会时，有五十多个国家的民主妇女代表参加，会员增至八千万人。第二届代表大会所选出的新的领导机构，包括：主席尤金尼·戈登夫人（法国）；副主席尼娜·波波娃（苏联）、多洛列斯·伊巴露丽（共和西班牙）、蔡畅（中国解放区）、杰因·维特费施（美国）；总书记玛丽·克劳德·瓦伦特（法国成衣女工）。
    世界文化工作者保卫和平大会
    世界文化工作者保卫和平大会，是一九四八年八月二十五日至二十八日在波兰西部罗克劳城成立的。出席大会的有四十六个国家的文化工作者的代表。这次大会上进步的文化工作者们严肃地批评与打击了如英国代表赫胥黎及泰乐之流的反动言论，通过了“文化工作者保卫和平宣言”并决定成立联络委员会，苏联的法捷耶夫（名作家）、菲多塞夫、美国的罗伯逊（黑人歌王）、费斯特（美国“主流”杂志编辑）、智利的尼鲁达（名诗人）、法国的阿拉贡（名诗人）、居里女士（名物理学家）、共和西班牙的吉拉尔（前总理、政论家）、波兰的董布罗夫斯基等当选为联络委员。
