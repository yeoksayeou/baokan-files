Title: Soviet Delegation Stops in Berlin En Route to Four-Power Foreign Ministers Conference; Pieck Receives Molotov to Exchange Views on Four-Power Foreign Ministers Conference Issues
Author:
Date: 1955-10-27
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, October 26th dispatch from Berlin: TASS reports: The Soviet delegation, led by Soviet First Deputy Chairman of the Council of Ministers and Minister of Foreign Affairs Molotov, en route to the Four-Power Foreign Ministers Conference, arrived in Berlin by plane on the 25th.

Welcoming the Soviet delegation at the airport were Chairman of the Presidium of the People's Chamber of the German Democratic Republic, Dieckmann, Premier of the German Democratic Republic, Grotewohl, First Secretary of the Central Committee of the Socialist Unity Party of Germany and Deputy Premier Ulbricht, and other government personnel. Also present to welcome the delegation were Soviet Ambassador to the German Democratic Republic Pushkin, Commander-in-Chief of the Group of Soviet Forces in Germany and Soviet Marshal Grechko, and diplomatic envoys from various countries.

Premier Grotewohl delivered a welcome speech to the Soviet delegation. He emphasized the great significance of the upcoming Geneva Four-Power Foreign Ministers Conference, saying that peace-loving men and women placed great hopes on it.

Molotov then delivered a short speech. Molotov said: The Soviet Union and the German Democratic Republic have established solid, friendly relations. The recently effective treaty on relations between our two countries is the foundation for the further development of the fraternal friendship and cooperation between our two peoples and will promote the peaceful resolution of the German question. The normalization of relations between the Soviet Union and the Federal Republic of Germany is also in line with this goal.

Molotov said: Ensuring peace and security is the primary task of the peoples of all countries in Europe. The German people will realize the reunification of Germany along this path, in accordance with peaceful and democratic principles.

Molotov expressed his sincere hope that the Geneva Foreign Ministers Conference would promote the further relaxation of international tensions and live up to the hopes of all countries yearning for peace and security.

On the same day, Grotewohl held a banquet for the Soviet delegation.

Xinhua News Agency, October 26th dispatch from Berlin: TASS Berlin, October 26th: President Pieck of the German Democratic Republic received First Deputy Chairman of the Council of Ministers and Minister of Foreign Affairs of the Soviet Union Molotov on the 25th.

Accompanying Molotov were members of the Soviet delegation to the Four-Power Foreign Ministers Conference: Soviet Marshal Sokolovsky, First Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of the Soviet Union Gromyko, and Soviet Ambassador to the German Democratic Republic Pushkin.

Present on the German Democratic Republic side at the reception were Premier Grotewohl, Deputy Premier Ulbricht, and Deputy Premier and Minister of Foreign Affairs Bolz.

During the reception, the two sides exchanged views on issues related to the upcoming Four-Power Foreign Ministers Conference in Geneva.

### 苏联代表团出席四国外长会议途中到柏林  皮克接见莫洛托夫就四外长会议问题交换意见

1955-10-27
第4版()
专栏：

    苏联代表团出席四国外长会议途中到柏林
    皮克接见莫洛托夫就四外长会议问题交换意见
    新华社二十六日讯　据塔斯社柏林讯：由苏联部长会议第一副主席兼外交部长莫洛托夫率领的苏联出席四国外长会议代表团在赴日内瓦途中在二十五日乘飞机到达柏林。
    在机场欢迎苏联代表团的有德意志民主共和国人民议院主席团主席狄克曼，德意志民主共和国总理格罗提渥，德国统一社会党中央委员会第一书记、副总理乌布利希和其他政府人员。前往欢迎的还有苏联驻德意志民主共和国大使普希金，驻德苏军部队总司令、苏联元帅格列奇科以及各国的外交使节。
    格罗提渥总理向苏联代表团致欢迎词。他着重指出即将举行的四国外长日内瓦会议的重大意义，他说，爱好和平的男女对它抱着很大希望。
    莫洛托夫然后发表了简短的讲话。莫洛托夫说：苏联同德意志民主共和国已经建立了巩固的、友好的关系。最近生效的关于我们两国的关系的条约是我们两国人民的兄弟友谊和合作的进一步发展的基础，并且将会促进德国问题的和平解决。苏联同德意志联邦共和国的关系的正常化也是符合这种目的的。
    莫洛托夫说：保障和平和安全是欧洲一切国家人民的首要任务。德国人民将沿着这条道路，按照和平和民主的方针实现德国的重新统一。
    莫洛托夫表示真诚希望日内瓦外长会议将促进国际紧张局势的进一步缓和和不负渴望和平和安全的各国人民的希望。
    当天，格罗提渥为苏联代表团举行了宴会。
    新华社二十六日讯　塔斯社柏林二十六日讯：德意志民主共和国总统皮克二十五日接见了苏联部长会议第一副主席兼外交部长莫洛托夫。
    随同莫洛托夫前往的有苏联参加四国外长会议的代表团团员——苏联元帅索科洛夫斯基，苏联外交部第一副部长葛罗米柯和苏联驻德意志民主共和国大使普希金。
    接见时德意志民主共和国方面在座的有总理格罗提渥，副总理乌布利希和副总理兼外交部长博尔茨。
    接见时，双方就即将在日内瓦举行的四国外长会议有关的问题交换了意见。
