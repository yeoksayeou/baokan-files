Title: Lieutenant General Ne Win, Head of the Burmese Military Friendship Delegation, Returns Home; Burmese Buddhist Delegation Holds Farewell Banquet
Author:
Date: 1955-10-07
Page: 1
Xinhua News Agency, October 6th—Lieutenant General Ne Win, head of the Burmese Military Friendship Delegation and Commander-in-Chief of the Burmese Armed Forces, left Beijing by plane this morning, October 6th, to return home.

    The flags of the Union of Burma and the People's Republic of China were flying at the airport, and the apron was filled with people seeing him off. As Lieutenant General Ne Win stepped onto the airport, the military band played the national anthems of the Union of Burma and the People's Republic of China. Marshal Peng Dehuai, Minister of National Defense of the People's Republic of China, accompanied Lieutenant General Ne Win to review the guard of honor. After the review, Lieutenant General Ne Win shook hands with those seeing him off.

    Those who came to the airport to see him off included Cheng Qian, Fu Zuoyi, and Long Yun, Vice-Chairmen of the National Defense Council; General Huang Kecheng, Vice Minister of National Defense; General Tan Zheng, General Wang Shusheng, General Li Da, Senior Generals of the Chinese People's Liberation Army Su Yu, Chen Geng, Xu Guangda, Li Kenong, Zhang Zongxun, Xiao Hua, Gan Siqi, Liu Yalou (Air Force General), Wang Hongkun (Navy General), and Yang Chengwu (General, Commander of the Beijing-Tianjin Garrison); generals from various ministries, military branches, and services; Zhang Hanfu, Vice Minister of Foreign Affairs; Feng Jiping, Vice Mayor of Beijing; and Xu Yimin, Deputy Military Attaché of the Chinese Embassy in Burma.

    U Hla Maw, Ambassador of the Union of Burma to China, military attachés of various countries to China, Brigadier General Te Khlift, Acting Head of the Burmese Military Friendship Delegation and Commander of the Burmese Air Force, and all members of the delegation also came to the airport to see him off.

    Members of the Burmese Overseas Chinese Returning Home Sightseeing Group also went to the airport to see them off.

    Before the plane took off, Lieutenant General Ne Win waved goodbye to the people seeing him off, and warm applause rang out at the airport.

    Brigadier General Kyaw Win, Director of the Supreme Headquarters Military Affairs Department, and Captain Tin Kyi, aide-de-camp, also accompanied Lieutenant General Ne Win on his return.

    Major General An Dong, representative of the Ministry of National Defense, also accompanied Lieutenant General Ne Win to leave Beijing.

    Xinhua News Agency, October 6th—The Burmese Buddhist Delegation, headed by U Tun Tin Maung, will soon leave Beijing to return home. A farewell banquet was held at noon on the 6th.

    Those invited to attend the banquet included: Zhou Enlai, Premier of the State Council of the People's Republic of China; Vice Premiers Chen Yi and Ulanhu; Li Jishen and Chen Shutong, Vice-Chairmen of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress; Zhang Bojun, Vice-Chairman of the National Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference; Xi Zhongxun, Secretary-General of the State Council; Zhang Hanfu, Vice Minister of Foreign Affairs; He Chengxiang, Director of the Religious Affairs Bureau of the State Council; Zheng Zhenduo, President of the Sino-Burmese Friendship Association; Zhao Puchu, Vice-President of the Buddhist Association of China; and responsible persons of relevant government departments, democratic parties, and people's organizations.

    Also attending the banquet were: Wu Han and Wang Kunlun, Vice Mayors of Beijing, and figures from Beijing's religious and cultural circles.

    The Burmese side was represented by: U Hla Maw, Ambassador of the Union of Burma to China, and embassy staff, as well as U Than Sein, Deputy Head of the Burmese Cultural Delegation.

    At the banquet, U Tun Tin Maung, head of the Burmese Buddhist Delegation, delivered a speech. He said that the Burmese Buddhist Delegation's visit to China to welcome the Buddha's tooth relic demonstrated the unprecedented development of Sino-Burmese friendship. He expressed gratitude for the warm reception received by the delegation in China and the assistance provided by the Chinese People's Government.

    Then Zhao Puchu, Vice-President of the Buddhist Association of China, delivered a speech saying: The common wish of the Chinese and Burmese people is peace. Since Premier Zhou Enlai and Prime Minister U Nu jointly issued a statement, the bells of peace have rung loudly. The peaceful aspirations of the Chinese and Burmese people will surely be fulfilled, and the voice of peace will fill the whole world.

    During the banquet, the hosts and guests repeatedly stood up to toast Sino-Burmese friendship, cultural exchange, and cooperation.

### 缅甸军事友好代表团团长奈温中将回国  缅甸佛教代表团举行辞行宴会

1955-10-07
第1版()
专栏：

    缅甸军事友好代表团团长奈温中将回国
    缅甸佛教代表团举行辞行宴会
    新华社六日讯缅甸军事友好代表团团长、缅甸武装部队总司令奈温中将，在六日清晨乘飞机离北京返国。
    飞机场上悬挂着缅甸联邦和中华人民共和国两国的国旗，停机场前站满了欢送的人群。当奈温中将步入机场，军乐队奏缅甸联邦和中华人民共和国两国国歌后，中华人民共和国国防部长彭德怀元帅陪同奈温中将检阅了仪仗队。检阅后，奈温中将同送行者一一握手告别。
    到机场欢送的，有国防委员会副主席程潜、傅作义、龙云，国防部副部长黄克诚大将、谭政大将、王树声大将、李达上将，中国人民解放军高级将领粟裕大将、陈赓大将、许光达大将、李克农上将、张宗逊上将、萧华上将、甘泗淇上将、刘亚楼空军上将、王宏坤海军上将和京津卫戍司令员杨成武上将，各部、各军种、兵种的将军们，外交部副部长章汉夫，北京市副市长冯基平，中国驻缅甸大使馆副武官徐毅民。
    缅甸联邦驻华大使吴拉茂和各国驻华武官，缅甸军事友好代表团代理团长、缅甸空军司令特·克利夫特准将和全体团员，也到机场送行。
    前往机场送行的还有缅甸华侨归国观光团的团员们。
    在飞机起飞前，奈温中将向欢送的人们挥手告别，机场上响起了热烈的掌声。
    随同奈温中将回国的还有最高统帅部军务署署长觉温准将、侍卫官丁季上尉。
    国防部代表安东少将，也同机陪送奈温中将离京。
    新华社六日讯以吴登貌为首的缅甸联邦佛教代表团即将离京返国，六日中午举行辞行宴会。
    应邀出席宴会的有：中华人民共和国国务院总理周恩来，副总理陈毅、乌兰夫，全国人民代表大会常务委员会副委员长李济深、陈叔通，中国人民政治协商会议全国委员会副主席章伯钧，国务院秘书长习仲勋，外交部副部长章汉夫，国务院宗教事务局局长何成湘，中缅友好协会会长郑振铎，中国佛教协会副会长赵朴初以及政府有关部门负责人，各民主党派、各人民团体的负责人。
    参加宴会的还有：北京市副市长吴晗、王崑崙和北京宗教、文化界人士。
    缅甸方面出席的有：缅甸联邦驻华大使吴拉茂和使馆人员及缅甸联邦文化代表团副团长吴丹盛等。
    在宴会上，缅甸联邦佛教代表团团长吴登貌致词。他说此次缅甸联邦佛教代表团能前来中国奉迎佛牙，说明中缅友谊获得了空前的发展。他对代表团在中国受到的热烈款待和中国人民政府给予的协助表示感激。
    接着中国佛教协会副会长赵朴初致词说：中缅人民共同的愿望就是和平。自从周恩来总理和吴努总理联合发表声明以来，和平的钟声大大地响起来了，中缅人民和平的愿望一定会得到满足，和平的声音将充满全世界。
    宴会上，宾主不断起立为中缅友谊，文化交流和合作干杯。
