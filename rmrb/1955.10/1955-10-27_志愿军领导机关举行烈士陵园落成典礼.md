Title: Volunteer Army Leading Organ Holds Dedication Ceremony for Martyrs' Cemetery
Author:
Date: 1955-10-27
Page: 4

    Xinhua News Agency, Pyongyang, October 25th: The seven central martyrs' cemeteries and memorial pavilions built by the Chinese People's Volunteer Army for the martyrs who sacrificed themselves in the War to Resist U.S. Aggression and Aid Korea have been completed successively recently. The leading organ of the Volunteer Army held a dedication ceremony for the martyrs' cemetery on the 25th.
    More than 700 Volunteer Army leaders, officers, and soldiers from various units attended the dedication ceremony. Vice Premier of the Cabinet and Minister of National Defense of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea, Choi Yong-kun, the head of the Korean people's delegation congratulating the 5th anniversary of the Chinese People's Volunteer Army's participation in the War to Resist U.S. Aggression and Aid Korea, and all members of the delegation also attended the ceremony.
    Pan Zili, Ambassador of the People's Republic of China to the Democratic People's Republic of Korea, as well as more than 200 representatives of the Korean government, people, and overseas Chinese in the location of the organ, also attended the dedication ceremony.
    The magnificent martyrs' cemetery was full of blooming chrysanthemums, and each tomb was filled with wreaths.
    At about 1:00 p.m., the dedication ceremony of the martyrs' cemetery began. The military band played the national anthem of the People's Republic of China and the battle song of the Chinese People's Volunteer Army.
    Amidst the music, Li Zhimin, Political Commissar of the Volunteer Army, and Zhang Nansheng, Deputy Director of the Political Department, unveiled the monument to the martyrs. Then, the inscription was read and wreaths were presented to the martyrs. Li Zhimin, Political Commissar of the Volunteer Army, presented a wreath on behalf of Peng Dehuai, the former Commander of the Volunteer Army. The Korean People's Delegation presented a wreath on behalf of Marshal Kim Il-sung.
    Zhang Nansheng, Deputy Director of the Political Department of the Chinese People's Volunteer Army, spoke first at the dedication ceremony. In his speech, he first expressed his highest respect and greatest honor to the martyrs who gloriously sacrificed themselves in the great War to Resist U.S. Aggression and Aid Korea. Zhang Nansheng said: Now we swear to the martyrs: We must take the noble character of the martyrs as an example and complete the unfinished work of the martyrs. We must work better with the heroic Korean people to maintain the Korean Armistice Agreement and strive for a peaceful solution to the Korean issue.
    Vice Premier of the Cabinet and Minister of National Defense of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea, Choi Yong-kun, then spoke. He first represented the respected leader of Korea, Marshal Kim Il-sung, and the Korean people in extending the whole nation's respect to the martyrs of the Chinese People's Volunteer Army who participated in the just Fatherland Liberation War of the Korean people and made immortal contributions. He said: The martyrs demonstrated the great spirit of proletarian internationalist fighters on the Korean battlefield and made outstanding contributions. Their glorious achievements will be passed down forever by our descendants, and their heroic names will coexist with the prosperity of our motherland. He concluded by saying: We must further strengthen the friendship and unity between the people of the two countries, China and Korea, better cherish and support the Chinese People's Volunteer Army, and strive for greater victories in the struggle to restore and develop the people's economy after the war in order to consolidate our democratic base.
    Pan Zili, Chinese Ambassador to Korea, also spoke at the dedication ceremony. He expressed his deep condolences to the martyrs who gloriously sacrificed themselves for resisting U.S. aggression and aiding Korea, defending their homes and country, and defending peace in the Far East and the world.
    After the ceremony, Volunteer Army leaders, all members of the Korean People's Delegation, and more than 3,000 attendees visited the cemetery.

### 志愿军领导机关举行烈士陵园落成典礼

1955-10-27
第4版()
专栏：

    志愿军领导机关举行烈士陵园落成典礼
    新华社平壤二十五日电　中国人民志愿军部队为在抗美援朝战争中牺牲的烈士修建的七个中心烈士陵园和纪念亭在最近先后完工。志愿军领导机关在二十五日举行了烈士陵园的落成典礼。
    参加落成典礼大会的有志愿军首长、各部队军官和士兵七百多人。朝鲜人民祝贺中国人民志愿军抗美援朝五周年代表团团长、朝鲜民主主义人民共和国内阁副首相兼民族保卫相崔庸健次帅和全体团员也参加了大会。
    参加落成典礼大会的还有中华人民共和国驻朝鲜民主主义人民共和国大使潘自力，以及机关驻地的朝鲜政府、人民和华侨代表二百多人。
    壮丽的烈士陵园菊花盛开，每个墓地上都摆满了花圈。
    下午一时许，烈士陵园的落成典礼开始。军乐队奏起中华人民共和国国歌和中国人民志愿军战歌。
    在乐声中，志愿军政治委员李志民和政治部副主任张南生为烈士纪念碑揭碑。接着读碑文和向烈士献花圈。志愿军政治委员李志民代表志愿军前任司令员彭德怀献花圈。朝鲜人民代表团代表金日成元帅献花圈。
    在落成典礼大会上首先讲话的是中国人民志愿军政治部副主任张南生。他在讲话中首先对在伟大的抗美援朝战争中光荣牺牲的烈士们，表示最崇高的敬意和给予最大的荣誉。张南生说：现在我们向烈士宣誓：一定要以烈士们高贵的品德为榜样，完成烈士们未竟的事业。我们要更好地和英雄的朝鲜人民在一起为维护朝鲜停战协定，为和平解决朝鲜问题而奋斗。
    朝鲜民主主义人民共和国内阁副首相兼民族保卫相崔庸健次帅接着讲话。他首先代表朝鲜敬爱的领袖金日成元帅和朝鲜人民向参加朝鲜人民正义的祖国解放战争，建立了不朽功勋的中国人民志愿军牺牲的烈士们致以全民性的敬意。他说：烈士们在朝鲜战场上表现了无产阶级国际主义战士的伟大精神，建立了卓越的功勋。他们光辉的业绩将为我们子孙后代永远传颂，他们英雄的名字将和我们祖国繁荣昌盛一起共存。他最后说：要进一步加强朝中两国人民之间的友谊团结，更好地爱护和支援中国人民志愿军，为了巩固我们的民主基地，在恢复和发展战后人民经济的斗争中争取更大的胜利。
    在落成典礼大会上讲话的还有中国驻朝鲜大使潘自力。他对为抗美援朝、保家卫国，为保卫远东和世界和平而光荣牺牲的烈士们表示深切哀悼。
    礼成后，志愿军首长和朝鲜人民代表团全体团员以及到会者三千多人集体参观了陵园。
