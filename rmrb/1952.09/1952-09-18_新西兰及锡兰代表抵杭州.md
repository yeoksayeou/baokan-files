Title: New Zealand and Ceylon Representatives Arrive in Hangzhou
Author:
Date: 1952-09-18
Page: 1
Xinhua News Agency, Hangzhou, September 17th. The head of the New Zealand delegation to the Asian and Pacific Regional Peace Conference, Montagu, and ten others, and the Ceylon delegation representative, Bandaranaike, and eleven others, arrived in Hangzhou on the morning of the 17th. Chen Shui, Secretary-General of the Zhejiang Provincial Branch of the Chinese People's Committee for World Peace, writer Song Yunbin, and hundreds of representatives of educators, workers, students, and women from Hangzhou City went to the station to welcome them. After the representatives got off the train, they accepted flowers from the welcomers. The New Zealand and Ceylon delegation representatives sang "Workers, Peasants, Sailors, Unite!" and "New Zealand Peace Fighters" at the station to express their gratitude to the welcomers.

### 新西兰及锡兰代表抵杭州

1952-09-18
第1版()
专栏：

    新西兰及锡兰代表抵杭州
    【新华社杭州十七日电】出席亚洲及太平洋区域和平会议的新西兰代表团团长蒙太施等十一人及锡兰代表团代表般达拉纳克一行共十二人于十七日上午到达杭州。前往车站欢迎的有中国人民保卫世界和平委员会浙江省分会秘书长陈水、著作家宋云彬以及杭州市教育工作者、工人、学生、妇女代表等数百人。代表们下车后，接受了欢迎者的献花。新西兰和锡兰代表团代表们在车站上合唱了“工人农民海员们团结起来”和“新西兰和平战士”两首歌，表示他们对欢迎者的谢意。
