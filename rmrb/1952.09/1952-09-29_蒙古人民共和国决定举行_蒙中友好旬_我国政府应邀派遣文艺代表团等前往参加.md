Title: Mongolian People's Republic Decides to Hold "Mongolia-China Friendship Week"; Our Government Sends Art Troupe and Others to Participate at Invitation
Author:
Date: 1952-09-29
Page: 1
Xinhua News Agency, Ulan Bator: Friendship between the Mongolian People's Republic and its great neighbor, the People's Republic of China, is growing stronger. The Mongolian people have sent delegations to visit China many times. The Chinese people are also constantly visiting Mongolia. The two countries are exchanging pictures, films, and literary works that illustrate the achievements of the two brotherly republics. The economic relations between the two countries are strengthening day by day.

In order to further strengthen cooperation between the two countries, the Mongolian People's Republic has decided to hold a "Mongolia-China Friendship Week." The Friendship Week is scheduled to open on the third anniversary of the founding of the People's Republic of China. During the Friendship Week, which will run from October 1st to 10th, various report meetings will be held throughout Mongolia to explain the significance of economic, political, and cultural cooperation between the two countries and to introduce the achievements of the People's Republic of China. A grand exhibition about China will be held in Ulan Bator, and Chinese films will be screened. Theaters and clubs across the country will hold Chinese song and dance evenings or literary evenings about the works of famous Chinese writers.

Many of Chairman Mao Zedong's classic works have been translated into Mongolian. "The Youth of Comrade Mao Zedong," compiled by poet Xiao San, has also been translated into Mongolian. A collection of short stories depicting the selfless labor of the Chinese working class and the lives of Chinese women is also being prepared for publication.

Xinhua News Agency, September 27th: The Art Troupe of the People’s Republic of China, dispatched by the Central People's Government of our country at the invitation of the Government of the Mongolian People's Republic, left Beijing by plane on the 27th to participate in the "Mongolia-China Friendship Week" hosted by the Mongolian government and to pay a friendly visit. The delegation is headed by Zhou Libo, member of the National Committee of the All-China Federation of Literary and Art Circles, writer, and Stalin Prize winner. Members of the delegation include: Ding Yi, Dean of the Central and Southern Theater, playwright, and Stalin Prize winner; Chao Wangwen, professor of the Central Academy of Fine Arts, artist and literary critic; Bu He, literary and art worker from the Inner Mongolia Autonomous Region; Zhang Yunxi, deputy head and famous actor of the Classical Dance Troupe of the Chinese Academy of Traditional Opera. In addition, four staff members are accompanying the delegation.

Those who saw them off at the airport included Ding Xilin, Vice Minister of the Ministry of Culture of the Central People's Government, Xu Maijin, Director of the General Office of the Government Administration Council's Cultural and Educational Committee, Hong Shen, Director of the Bureau of Foreign Cultural Relations, Yan Baohang, Deputy Director of the General Office of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of the Central People's Government, He Ying, Deputy Director of the Asian Department, Ai Qing, member of the National Committee of the All-China Federation of Literary and Art Circles, and others.

Mongolian Ambassador to China, Jalkhasaikhan, and embassy staff also saw them off at the airport.

In order to strengthen and promote friendship and cultural exchange between China and Mongolia, the delegation also led the Chinese Song and Dance Troupe to participate in the activities of the "Mongolia-China Friendship Week," and the Song and Dance Troupe will perform a friendly performance in Mongolia. Ding Yi, a member of the delegation, also serves as the head of the Chinese Song and Dance Troupe, and Bu He and Zhang Yunxi serve as the deputy heads. The Song and Dance Troupe has a total of ninety-four members. The troupe left Beijing for Ulan Bator, the capital of Mongolia, in batches by plane from the 25th to the 27th.

Prior to this, a working group of twenty-two people from our country to participate in the Industrial Exhibition of the "Mongolia-China Friendship Week" of the Mongolian People's Republic has left the country in batches. The exhibits of the exhibition totaled more than 2,700 kinds and were shipped out in batches in early September.

### 蒙古人民共和国决定举行“蒙中友好旬”  我国政府应邀派遣文艺代表团等前往参加

1952-09-29
第1版()
专栏：

    蒙古人民共和国决定举行“蒙中友好旬”
    我国政府应邀派遣文艺代表团等前往参加
    【新华社二十七日讯】据塔斯社乌兰巴托讯：蒙古人民共和国与它的伟大邻邦中华人民共和国之间的友谊日益巩固。蒙古人民曾多次派出代表团访问中国。中国人民也不断访问蒙古。两国交换着说明两个兄弟共和国成就的图片、电影和文学著作。两国的经济关系一天天加强。
    为了进一步加强两国的合作，蒙古人民共和国决定举行“蒙中友好旬”。友好旬拟定于中华人民共和国建国三周年开幕。在从十月一日到十日的友好旬期间，蒙古全国各地将举行各种报告会，说明两国经济、政治和文化合作的意义和介绍中华人民共和国的成就。在乌兰巴托将举行盛大的关于中国的展览会，并放映中国电影。全国各剧院和俱乐部将举行中国歌舞晚会或关于中国名作家作品的文艺晚会。
    毛泽东主席的经典著作，有许多已译成蒙文。诗人萧三编述的“毛泽东同志的青少年时代”也已经译成蒙文。现在准备出版的还有描写中国工人阶级忘我劳动和中国妇女生活的小说集。
    【新华社二十七日讯】我国中央人民政府应蒙古人民共和国政府的邀请而派遣的中华人民共和国文艺代表团，已于二十七日乘飞机离京，前往蒙古人民共和国参加蒙古政府主办的“蒙中友好旬”，并进行友谊的访问。代表团由中华全国文学艺术界联合会全国委员会委员、作家、斯大林奖金获得者周立波任团长。团员有：中南剧院院长、剧作家、斯大林奖金获得者丁毅，中央美术学院教授、美术家兼文艺批评家王朝闻，内蒙古自治区文艺工作者布赫，中国戏曲研究院古典舞工作团副团长、名演员张云溪。此外，尚有工作人员四人随行。
    到机场欢送的有中央人民政府文化部副部长丁西林，政务院文化教育委员会办公厅主任徐迈进，对外文化联络事务局局长洪深，中央人民政府外交部办公厅副主任阎宝航，亚洲司副司长何英，中华全国文学艺术界联合会全国委员会委员艾青等人。
    蒙古驻华大使贾尔卡赛汗及使馆人员亦到机场欢送。
    为加强与促进中蒙两国间的友谊和文化交流，代表团并率领中国歌舞团前往参加“蒙中友好旬”的活动，歌舞团将在蒙古作友谊的演出。中国歌舞团由代表团团员丁毅兼任团长，布赫、张云溪兼任副团长。歌舞团团员共九十四人。该团自二十五至二十七日分批乘飞机离京赴蒙古首都乌兰巴托。
    在此之前，我国参加蒙古人民共和国“蒙中友好旬”工业展览会工作团共二十二人，已分批出国。展览会的各项展品计有二千七百余种，在九月上旬即已分批运出。
