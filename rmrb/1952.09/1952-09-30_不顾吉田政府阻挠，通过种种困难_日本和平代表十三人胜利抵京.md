Title: Defying Obstruction by the Yoshida Government, Overcoming Numerous Difficulties: Thirteen Japanese Peace Delegates Triumphantly Arrive in Beijing
Author:
Date: 1952-09-30
Page: 1

Xinhua News Agency, September 29th: Thriteen Japanese peace delegates participating in the Asian and Pacific Regions Peace Conference, defying all kinds of obstruction by the Japanese Yoshida government and overcoming numerous difficulties, have triumphantly arrived in Beijing and were warmly welcomed by the Preparatory Committee of the Asian and Pacific Regions Peace Conference. The Japanese peace delegates who arrived in Beijing are: Kan'emon Nakamura, Hiroshi Minami, Kenta Kaneko, Togo Kameda, Saburo Ueki, Fengjiu Huang, Kunio Toda, Kiyoshi Ito, Taro Hada, Shunpei Fukui, Kinkichi Ogura, Hiroki Kojima, and Kiyoshi Ozawa. Among them, Kan'emon Nakamura is the representative of the head of the Japanese Peace Delegation, Jiichiro Matsumoto, and Togo Kameda is the Deputy Secretary-General of the Preparatory Committee of the Asian and Pacific Regions Peace Conference.

Before the meeting of the Preparatory Committee of the Asian and Pacific Regions Peace Conference to hear the report on the preparations, Secretary-General Liu Ningyi announced the triumphant arrival of the Japanese peace delegates. When the Japanese peace delegates entered the venue, all the representatives stood up and applauded warmly. In his welcoming speech, Liu Ningyi pointed out that the Japanese Yoshida government's refusal to issue passports to the Japanese peace delegates and its violent attacks exposed the Yoshida government's fear of peace and sabotage of peace. However, the peace aspirations of the Japanese people and the people of Asian and Pacific countries cannot be stopped and will surely be realized. We warmly welcome the Japanese peace delegates.

Kan'emon Nakamura, the representative of the head of the Japanese Peace Delegation, Jiichiro Matsumoto, then rose to deliver a statement on behalf of the Japanese delegation (the full text of the statement is in a separate article). The Japanese Peace Delegation's statement was met with enthusiastic applause from all participants.

Kan'emon Nakamura, Togo Kameda, Hiroshi Minami, and Kenta Kaneko participated in the Preparatory Committee meeting.

The majority of the Japanese Peace Delegation, headed by Jiichiro Matsumoto, have been unable to leave Japan due to the Japanese Yoshida government's refusal to issue passports and its strict surveillance. ### 不顾吉田政府阻挠，通过种种困难  日本和平代表十三人胜利抵京

1952-09-30
第1版()
专栏：

    不顾吉田政府阻挠，通过种种困难
    日本和平代表十三人胜利抵京
    【新华社二十九日讯】参加亚洲及太平洋区域和平会议的日本和平代表十三人不顾日本吉田政府的种种阻挠，通过种种困难，已经胜利地抵达北京，并受到亚洲及太平洋区域和平会议筹备委员会的热烈欢迎。抵达北京的日本和平代表是：中村玩右卫门、南博、金子健太、龟田东伍、植木三郎、黄凤九、户田国夫、伊藤清、羽田太郎、福井骏平、小仓金吉、儿岛博基、小泽清。其中中村玩右卫门是日本和平代表团团长松本治一郎的代表，龟田东伍是亚洲及太平洋区域和平会议筹备委员会的副秘书长。
    在亚洲及太平洋区域和平会议筹备委员会会议听取关于筹备情况的报告之前，刘宁一秘书长宣布了日本和平代表的胜利到达。当日本和平代表进入会场时，全体代表一致起立，热烈鼓掌欢迎。刘宁一致欢迎词时指出：日本吉田政府对日本和平代表拒发护照并横施殴打，暴露了吉田政府畏惧和平、破坏和平的面目。但是，日本人民和亚洲及太平洋各国人民的和平愿望是无法阻止的，它是一定能够实现的。我们热烈欢迎日本和平代表。
    日本和平代表团团长松本治一郎的代表中村玩右卫门继起代表日本代表团发表一项声明（声明全文见另文）。日本和平代表团的声明，获得了全体与会者的热烈掌声。
    中村玩右卫门、龟田东伍、南博及金子健太等四人参加了筹备委员会会议。
    以松本治一郎为首的日本和平代表团由于日本吉田政府拒发护照，并严加监视，大多数不能离开日本。但是，中村玩右卫门等代表终于通过种种困难，由日本抵达北京。日本代表团代表名单如下：
