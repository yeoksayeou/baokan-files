Title: Catalogue of the Report and Appendices of the International Scientific Commission

Author:
Date: 1952-09-15
Page: 

Xinhua News Agency, dated 15th: The Report and Appendices of the International Scientific Commission investigating the facts of bacteriological warfare in Korea and China totals approximately 450,000 words, printed in French, English, Russian, and Chinese. The table of contents of the report and the table of contents of the appendices are as follows:

The report is divided into 19 chapters. The table of contents is as follows: Preface, Organization and Work of the Committee, Documents, Relevant Materials on the Japanese Army's Germ Warfare during World War II, Methods of Analysis of Events by the Committee, Insectological Data from the Prague Documents, Medical Notes on Dispersed Insects, Information on Plant Pathology, Events in Korea (Plague), Gannan Incident, Kuandian Incident, Liaodong and Liaoxi Incidents (Respiratory Anthrax), Datong Incident (Cholera), Types of Containers or Bombs, Confessions of Captured Special Agents, Confessions of American Air Force Prisoners of War, Public Health in New China, General Discussion, Conclusion.

There are 46 appendices in total. The table of contents is as follows:

    I. Summary of the Committee's Work Schedule;
    II. Memorandum on Insect Vector Issues;
    III. Memorandum on Quantitative Research on Insect Vectors;
    IV. Memorandum on the Mechanical Transmission of Bacteria by Normal Flies in China, Research and Analysis of Specimens Collected on the Spot;
    V. Investigation Report on Whether Flies Naturally Growing in Shenyang Carry Enteric Pathogens or Anthrax Bacillus;
    VI. Investigation Report on Whether Local Chicken Feathers in Shenyang and Kuandian Carry Anthrax Bacillus;
    VII. General Table of Incidents in Northeast China and Korea;
    VIII. Information on the Dispersal of Insects by U.S. Military Aircraft;
    IX. Questions Submitted to the Korean Minister of Public Health and the Answers Received;
    X. (A) Report on the Throwing of Germ-Carrying Plants by U.S. Military Aircraft in North Korea and Northeast China, (B) Report on the Scattering of Leaves of Two Types of Trees Native to Southern Korea by U.S. Military Aircraft in North Korea and Northeast China;
    XI. "Report on the Plague in Changde, Hunan" (December 12, 1941);
    XII. Memorandum on Certain Facts of Japanese Germ Warfare;
    XIII. Report on the Airdropping of Field Mice Carrying Plague Bacilli by U.S. Military Aircraft in Gannan County, Heilongjiang Province;
    XIV. Records of Hearings on the Gannan Incident, Statements of Witnesses, On-site Inspections by the Committee, etc.;
    XV. Comparison of Gannan Field Mice with Microtus gregalis (pallas);
    XVI. Comments on the Identification of Field Mice;
    XVII. Excerpt from the Article "Bacterial Warfare" by Lieutenant Colonel Ryohei Sakaki of the Former Japanese Kwantung Army Epidemic Prevention Unit;
    XVIII. Report on the Case of Plague in Park Yeon-ho, Gangseo County, South Pyongan Province, Korea;
    XIX. Records of Hearings on the Gangseo County Incident (Plague);
    XX. Report on the Scattering of Human Fleas Infected with Plague Bacilli by U.S. Military Aircraft;
    XXI. Records of Hearings on the Huaiyang County Incident (Plague), Statements of Witnesses, etc.;
    XXII. Report on the Chalky Bacterial Bombs Dropped by U.S. Military Aircraft in Kuandian County, Liaodong Province;
    XXIII. Notes on the Case of the "Eggshell" Container Discovered in Kuandian;
    XXIV. Brief Record of the June 6th Incident;
    XXV. Report on the Throwing of Four-Compartment Bombs by U.S. Military Aircraft in Changbai County, Liaodong Province;
    XXVI. Records of U.S. Military Germ Warfare Containers Exhibited at the Pyongyang Central Laboratory;
    XXVII. Report on the Respiratory Anthrax Occurring After U.S. Military Aircraft Invaded Northeast China and Scattered Insects and Other Objects Carrying Anthrax Bacilli;
    XXVIII. Records of Hearings on the Incident of U.S. Military Aircraft Scattering Anthrax Bacilli and Causing Deaths in Liaodong and Liaoxi Provinces;
    XXIX. Report on the Case of Cholera in Zhao Wanfu, Datong County;
    XXX. Records of Hearings on the Datong County Incident (Cholera), Statements of Scientists, and Testimonies of Witnesses;
    XXXI. Memorandum on the Carrying of Cholera Vibrio by Clams (Meretrix meretrtx);
    XXXII. Memorandum on a New Disease – Acute Encephalitis – Caused After U.S. Military Bacteria-Spreading Aircraft Invaded Shenyang and its Vicinity;
    XXXIII. Records of Hearings on the Discovery of New Encephalitis in Shenyang;
    XXXIV. Information on Human Encephalitis Transmitted by Arthropods;
    XXXV. Notes on the Shenyang Incident (Encephalitis);
    XXXVI. Excerpts from Cases of South Korean Agents Infiltrating North Korea to Collect and Transmit Epidemic Information to U.S. Military Headquarters;
    XXXVII. Confession of U.S. Air Force Prisoner of War Lieutenant Inak Participating in the Bacteriological Warfare Conducted by the U.S. Military in Korea;
    XXXVIII. Confession of U.S. Air Force Prisoner of War Lieutenant Quinn Participating in the Bacteriological Warfare Conducted by the U.S. Military in Korea;
    XXXIX. Confession of U.S. Air Force Prisoner of War Lieutenant O'Neill Participating in the Bacteriological Warfare Conducted by the U.S. Military in Korea;
    XL. Confession of U.S. Air Force Prisoner of War Lieutenant Knis Participating in the Bacteriological Warfare Conducted by the U.S. Military in Korea;
    XLI. Notes on Talks with Four U.S. Air Force Prisoners of War;
    XLII. Memorandum on Public Health Undertakings and Health Campaigns in New China;

    XLIV. Statement of Dr. Frank Grauchi;

    XLVI. Brief Biographies of Chinese and Korean Scientists and Medical Workers.
