Title: Japan Peace Conference Calls for Easing International Tensions
Author:
Date: 1954-04-22
Page: 4

    Xinhua News Agency, Tokyo, 21st: A peace conference initiated by Ikuo Oyama, Chairman of the Japan Peace Committee, and prominent figures from all walks of life in Japan, was held in Tokyo from the 18th to the 20th. The conference passed five resolutions concerning the easing of international tensions. These five resolutions are: (1) To achieve a ceasefire in Indochina; (2) To peacefully resolve the Korean issue; (3) To restore the legitimate status of the People's Republic of China in the United Nations; (4) To establish an organization to guarantee the security of all Asia and to promote the establishment of normal relations between Japan and countries with which it has not established diplomatic relations; (5) To ban the use of atomic weapons.
    The conference also decided to submit these resolutions to the Geneva Conference to express the Japanese people's desire for peace.

### 日本和平会议要求缓和国际紧张局势

1954-04-22
第4版()
专栏：

    日本和平会议要求缓和国际紧张局势
    【新华社二十一日讯】东京消息：由日本拥护和平委员会主席大山郁夫以及日本各界知名人士发起的和平会议，自十八日至二十日在东京举行，会议通过五项有关缓和国际紧张局势的决议。这五项决议是：（一）实现印度支那的停战；（二）和平解决朝鲜问题；（三）恢复中华人民共和国在联合国中的合法地位；（四）建立保障全亚洲安全的机构，促使日本与未建立邦交的各国建立正常关系；（五）禁止使用原子武器。
    会议并决定向日内瓦会议提出这些决议，以表达日本人民爱好和平的意志。
