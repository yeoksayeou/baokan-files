Title: Soviet Science Holds World Leading Position

Author:

Date: 1954-04-19
Page: 4

    Soviet Science Holds World Leading Position
    New Achievements in Cultivating Wheat Varieties
    Professor Alexander Krylov, Chairman of the State Commission for Testing New Varieties of Agricultural Crops, told TASS reporters: There are currently 296 varieties of wheat planted in the Soviet Union, each of which is "regionalized," meaning they are planted in the most suitable areas. For example, drought-resistant varieties that can withstand winters without snow are planted in the steppe regions of the Soviet Union. In the central states, varieties that are resistant to rain and wind are planted, yielding very good results. Special varieties have been cultivated to suit the conditions of irrigated areas.

    In the non-black soil zone, hybrid wheat cultivated from wheat and perennial grasses under the guidance of Academician Nikolai Tsitsin is widely used. In the Moscow region, this variety yields up to 6,000 kilograms of grain per hectare.

    Alexander Krylov said that Soviet breeders have recently cultivated fourteen varieties of winter and spring wheat.

    Special varieties of spring wheat have been selected for the virgin lands now being reclaimed in eastern Soviet Union. Most of these new lands will be planted with durum spring wheat, which produces high-quality flour with a high protein content. These varieties have been tested with high yields in Siberia, Kazakhstan, the Altai Territory, and the Volga region.

    Discovery of New Minerals and New Deposits

    Geological research and mineral exploration are being carried out throughout the Soviet Union. Of the 427 minerals discovered worldwide between 1935 and 1950, the Soviet Union discovered 145. The Soviet Union has discovered thousands of new deposits of metallic and non-metallic minerals. The Soviet Union has made considerable progress in the study of platinum, chromite, nickel, and manganese ores. Currently, vector analysis, X-ray measurement analysis, and many other precise and time-saving research methods are being used on a large scale to study minerals. These methods help to discover some unknown properties of minerals, which are of great importance to the practical application of minerals.

    Confirming the Historical Age of the Earth

    Soviet geochemists have conducted many new studies using isotope analysis of natural compounds. This research involves using special instruments to measure the quantitative relationship of isotopes of a substance. Through this research, it is possible to determine the age and history of the formation of a substance. For example, after studying the relationship of lead isotopes in lead compounds, Soviet scientists confirmed their previous conclusion that the Earth is at most about five billion years old. This research helps to solve many previously controversial geological problems and is of great help to the exploration and research of valuable minerals.

      Artificial Corundum and Rubies

    Soviet crystallographers completed more than sixty scientific research projects in the past year. A research work conducted by Dr. G. Lemlein, a doctor of geology and mineralogy, is to carefully study the small pores on crystals with slow-motion photography. These small pores contain small amounts of the original liquid before the mineral crystallized. Such pores exist on quartz, rubies, calcite, and other minerals. This research helps to understand the basic process of crystal formation, and thus is of great importance to the exploration, mining, and artificial manufacture of crystals of various minerals. At present, the Soviet Union can mass-produce various artificial corundum, rubies, etc. These artificial gemstones can be used in the watchmaking industry and can be used to manufacture small ball bearings for precision instruments.

                                (Xinhua News Agency)

### 占居世界首位的苏维埃科学

1954-04-19
第4版()
专栏：

    占居世界首位的苏维埃科学
    培育小麦品种的新成就
    国家试验农作物新品种委员会主席亚历山大·克里洛夫教授告诉塔斯社记者说：目前在苏联种植的小麦品种有二百九十六种，这些品种每一种都“区域化”了，这就是说种植在最适合的地区了。譬如在苏联的草原地区种植了能够经受没有雪的冬季的抗旱品种。在中部诸州种植了不怕雨和风的品种，得到了非常好的结果。特别的品种已经培育出来，以适合灌溉的地区的条件。
    在非黑土地带，广泛使用了在尼古拉·齐津院士指导下培养起来的小麦和速生草培育出来的杂种小麦。在莫斯科州，这种品种每公顷出产多达六千公斤的谷物。
    亚历山大·克里洛夫说，苏联育种学家最近培育了十四种冬小麦和春小麦品种。
    为现在苏联东部开垦的生荒地选择了春小麦的特别品种。这些新的土地大多数将种植硬粒春小麦，这些小麦制成的面粉质量很高，含有很丰富的蛋白质。这些品种已经在西伯利亚、哈萨克、阿尔泰边疆区和伏尔加河地区试种，收成很高。
    发现新矿物和新矿床
    苏联全国各地都在进行地质研究和矿物探测的工作。在一九三五年到一九五○年间全世界所发现的四百二十七种矿物中，苏联发现的占一百四十五种。苏联已发现了几千个金属和非金属矿物的新矿床。苏联在研究白金、铬铁矿、镍矿和锰矿矿砂方面已有相当大的进展。目前正在大规模地使用向量分析、伦琴测量分析等许多精确而又节省时间的研究方法来研究矿物。这些方法有助于发现矿物的一些未知特性。而这些特性对矿物的实际应用具有重要的作用。
    证实地球的历史年代
    苏联地球化学家以天然化合物的同位元素分析法进行了许多新的研究工作。这种研究工作就是用特殊仪器来测知某种物质的同位元素的量的相互关系。通过这种研究可以决定物质的生成年代和经过。例如在研究了铅化合物中的铅的同位元素的相互关系后，苏联科学家证实了他们以前所作的地球最多约有五十亿年历史的结论。这种研究工作帮助解决了许多以前引起争论的地质问题，对有价值的矿物的勘察和研究工作有很大帮助。
      人造的刚玉和红宝石
    苏联结晶学家在去年一年内完成了六十多种科学研究工作。地质学和矿物学博士格·列姆林所进行的一种研究工作就是用慢动作的摄影仔细研究结晶体上的小孔，这些小孔中包含着小量的矿物结晶前原有的液体。石英、红宝石、方解石和其他矿物上都有这样的小孔。这种研究工作有助于了解结晶体形成的基本过程，因此对矿物的勘测、开采和人工制造各种矿物的结晶体有重要作用。目前苏联已能大量生产各种人造刚玉、红宝石等。这些人造宝石可以用于制表工业，并可用来制造精密仪器的小滚珠轴承。
                                （新华社）
