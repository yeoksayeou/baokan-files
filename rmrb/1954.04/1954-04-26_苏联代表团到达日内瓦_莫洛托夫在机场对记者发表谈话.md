Title: Soviet Delegation Arrives in Geneva; Molotov Makes Statement to Reporters at Airport
Author:
Date: 1954-04-26
Page: 1

Xinhua News Agency, Geneva, April 25th: TASS Geneva, April 24th: Vyacheslav Mikhailovich Molotov, First Deputy Chairman of the Council of Ministers of the USSR and Minister of Foreign Affairs, Andrei Andreyevich Gromyko, Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of the USSR, and advisors and experts of the Soviet delegation arrived in Geneva on April 24th to attend the Foreign Ministers' Conference.

Molotov was welcomed at Geneva Airport by Mr. Zhender, Director of Political Affairs of the Swiss Political Department (i.e., Ministry of Foreign Affairs), Mr. Maurice, Head of the Protocol Department of the Swiss Political Department, Mr. Dubalet, Chairman of the Government of the Canton of Geneva, representatives of the Geneva authorities, F.F. Molochkov, Soviet Minister to Switzerland, Zhou Enlai, Premier of the State Administration Council of the Central People's Government of the People's Republic of China and Minister of Foreign Affairs, Nam Il, Minister of Foreign Affairs of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea, advisors and experts of the delegations of the People's Republic of China and the Democratic People's Republic of Korea to the Geneva Conference, Mr. Pierrete, Director of the European Office of the United Nations, and diplomatic envoys of various People's Democratic countries to Switzerland.

Reporters from Soviet and foreign newspapers were at the airport to interview him. Molotov made the following statement to the reporters:

The Geneva Foreign Ministers' Conference will open on April 26th. The conference will discuss the Korean question and the restoration of peace in Indochina.

It must be pointed out that this is an important fact: that representatives of all the major powers - France, Great Britain, the United States, the People's Republic of China, and the Soviet Union - are participating together in an international conference for the first time in recent years.

The Soviet delegation will do its utmost at the conference to promote the peaceful establishment of a unified, independent, and democratic Korea, in accordance with the interests of consolidating peace in the Far East and the world.

At the same time, the Soviet delegation believes that the quickest possible restoration of peace in Indochina and the guarantee of the freedom and national rights of the people of Indochina is the most important task of the Geneva Conference.

The Geneva Conference should strive to ease international tensions and consolidate universal peace. In this way, the conference can achieve positive results.

Today, please allow me, on behalf of the Soviet delegation, to extend special greetings to the residents of Geneva and the citizens of Switzerland.

### 苏联代表团到达日内瓦  莫洛托夫在机场对记者发表谈话

1954-04-26
第1版()
专栏：

    苏联代表团到达日内瓦
    莫洛托夫在机场对记者发表谈话
    【新华社二十五日讯】塔斯社日内瓦二十四日讯：苏联部长会议第一副主席、外交部部长维·米·莫洛托夫，苏联外交部副部长安·阿·葛罗米柯，以及苏联代表团的顾问和专家于四月二十四日抵达日内瓦参加外长会议。
    在日内瓦机场欢迎莫洛托夫的有瑞士政治部
  （即外交部）政务司司长琴德尔、瑞士政治部礼宾处处长莫里斯、日内瓦州政府主席杜博莱、日内瓦当局的代表、苏联驻瑞士公使费·弗·莫洛奇科夫、中华人民共和国中央人民政府政务院总理兼外交部部长周恩来、朝鲜民主主义人民共和国外务相南日、中华人民共和国和朝鲜民主主义人民共和国出席日内瓦会议代表团的顾问和专家、联合国欧洲办事处主任皮尔特、各人民民主国家驻瑞士使节。
    到机场采访的有苏联和外国报纸的记者。莫洛托夫向记者们发表谈话如下：
    日内瓦外长会议将于四月二十六日开幕。会上将讨论朝鲜问题和恢复印度支那和平问题。
    不能不指出这一重要事实，即：所有的大国——法国、英国、美国、中华人民共和国和苏联的代表最近几年来首次共同参加一个国际会议。
    苏联代表团在会上将竭力促成用和平的方式建立一个统一、独立、民主的朝鲜，以符合巩固远东和世界和平的利益。
    同时，苏联代表团认为，尽速恢复印度支那和平，保障印度支那人民的自由和民族权利是日内瓦会议最重要的任务。
    日内瓦会议应当致力于缓和国际紧张局势和巩固普遍和平。这样，会议就能获得积极的结果。
    今天请让我代表苏联代表团特别向日内瓦居民和瑞士公民致意。
