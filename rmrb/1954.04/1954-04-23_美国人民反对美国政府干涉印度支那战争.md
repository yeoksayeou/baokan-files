Title: American People Oppose U.S. Government Interference in Indochina War
Author:
Date: 1954-04-23
Page: 4

    Xinhua News Agency, Berlin, April 22nd: The peace-loving people of the United States hope for the success of the Geneva Conference. They oppose the U.S. government's interference in the Indochina War and the U.S. government's stirring up of international tensions on the eve of the Geneva Conference.

    According to the "People's World" report: The Standing Committee of the American Peace Crusade demanded that the United States not be involved in the Indochina War, and demanded that the U.S. government seek a rapid ceasefire in Indochina and a peaceful settlement of the Indochina War at the Geneva Great Powers Conference. The Standing Committee also called on the American people to do their utmost to demand a halt to hydrogen bomb tests in various ways.

    The Northern California Peace Committee in the United States also called on the people to demand a halt to all hydrogen bomb tests. Committee Secretary Mrs. Winter said: "The best guarantee to prevent this terrible weapon from being used is to seek an end to the cruel war in Indochina at the upcoming Geneva Conference."

    The New York "Daily Worker" reprinted many readers' letters published in American newspapers on the 15th and 16th. A reader named H.D. Woodruff wrote to the "Plain Dealer" published in Cleveland, saying, "The American people oppose giving any military aid to Indochina or any interference in Indochina affairs. They want nothing to do with this dirty colonial war."

    Another reader, Myron A. Ellis, wrote to the "New York Times," condemning Dulles's actions, saying: "Instead of working to ease tensions before an important world conference, Secretary of State Dulles is trying to limit any achievements such a conference might achieve in advance, and even threatening to invade mainland China." Reader Edward S. Barclay wrote to the New York "Herald Tribune" saying, "If Dulles means to take the same kind of joint action in Indochina that we have taken in Korea, then he is not taking into account the opinions of the American people. Sending any American military forces to Indochina will arouse fierce opposition from the American people."

### 美国人民反对美国政府干涉印度支那战争

1954-04-23
第4版()
专栏：

    美国人民反对美国政府干涉印度支那战争
    【新华社柏林二十二日电】美国爱好和平人民希望日内瓦会议成功。他们反对美国政府干涉印度支那战争，反对美国政府在日内瓦会议前夕煽动国际紧张空气。
    据“人民世界报”报道：美国和平十字军常设委员会要求美国不要卷入印度支那战争，并要求美国政府在日内瓦的大国会议上设法求得迅速在印度支那停火并获致印度支那战争的和平解决。常设委员会并号召美国人民尽最大可能以各种方式要求停止氢弹试验。
    美国北加利福尼亚和平委员会也号召人民要求停止一切氢弹试验。委员会书记温特夫人说：“使得这个可怕的武器不致使用的最好保证，是在即将举行的日内瓦会议上求得结束印度支那的残酷战争。”
    纽约“工人日报”十五日与十六日转载了美国报纸上刊载的许多读者来信。一个名叫赫·德·伍德拉夫的读者写信给在克利夫兰出版的“实话报”说，“美国人民反对给予印度支那任何军事援助，或是对印度支那事务进行任何干涉。他们不要与这个肮脏的殖民战争发生任何关系。”
    另外一个读者迈伦·阿·艾利斯写信给“纽约时报”，谴责杜勒斯的做法，他说：“国务卿杜勒斯在一个重要的世界会议之前非但不致力于缓和紧张局势，反而在事先就设法缩小这样一个会议可能获得的任何成就，甚至还威胁说可能要侵略中国大陆。”读者爱德华·斯·巴克莱写信给纽约“先驱论坛报”说，“假如杜勒斯意思是说要在印度支那采取像我们在朝鲜所曾采取过的那样的联合行动的话，那他是没有考虑到美国人民的意见。派遣任何美国军事部队到印度支那去都将引起美国人民的激烈反对。”
