Title: New Life in the Mountains and Fields (Picture)
Author: 
Date: 1954-04-18
Page: 5

The Kirgiz people, who live in the northwest frontier of our motherland, in Tekes County, Xinjiang Province, have lived a nomadic life in the valleys for many years. After liberation, with the help of the People's Government, they have changed their way of life.

Photography: Our reporter Zhu Bo
牧民大会讨论生产工作。The herders' meeting discusses production work.
人民政府一年有两季派兽医为柯族同胞的牲畜进行防疫注射和医疗工作。The People's Government sends veterinarians twice a year to provide vaccinations and medical care for the livestock of the Kirgiz people.
柯族的布比送儿子上学。Bubi of the Kirgiz people sends his son to school.
人民政府派来医生为柯族同胞进行防疫注射，这里的传染性疾病，逐渐减少了。The People's Government sent doctors to provide vaccinations for the Kirgiz people, and infectious diseases here have gradually decreased.
人民政府为柯族妇女组织了识字班，她们在专心地学习着本民族的文字。The People's Government organized literacy classes for Kirgiz women, and they are concentrating on learning their own national language.
骑着马，带着帐篷，那儿草好就到那儿去。Riding horses and carrying tents, they go wherever the grass is good.
柯族七十多岁的老牧民那麻孜别克高兴地对他儿媳妇说：“我们真是人畜两旺啊。”Namazbek, an elderly Kirgiz herder in his seventies, happily said to his daughter-in-law: "We are truly prosperous in both people and livestock!"
羊群一年比一年增加。The flocks of sheep increase year by year.
牧民们在庆祝日益富裕的新生活的大会上，举行“赛马”。The herders hold a "horse race" at the conference celebrating their increasingly prosperous new life.
贸易小组给柯族牧民带来好货物。The trade team brings good goods to the Kirgiz herders.
