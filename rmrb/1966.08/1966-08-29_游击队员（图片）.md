    Guerrilla Fighter   [Albania] Andre Mani

    Andre Mani, a guerrilla fighter from Albania, was born in 1924 in the village of Lushnje. He joined the Communist Party of Albania in 1943 and participated in the anti-fascist struggle. During the war, he served as a political commissar in the People's Liberation Army and played an active role in organizing and leading the guerrilla forces.

    After the liberation of Albania, Mani continued to serve in the military and held various positions, including commander of a regiment and deputy commander of a division. He was also a member of the Central Committee of the Party of Labor of Albania and a deputy to the People's Assembly.

    Mani was known for his bravery and leadership during the war. He was awarded the title of "Hero of the People" in 1945 for his outstanding contributions to the liberation of Albania. His life and deeds have become a symbol of the Albanian people's struggle for independence and socialism.

    In his later years, Mani continued to work for the Party and the people, serving in various capacities and contributing to the development of Albania. He passed away in 1998, leaving behind a legacy of courage and dedication to the cause of socialism.

    The story of Andre Mani is a testament to the spirit of the Albanian people, who have fought tirelessly for their freedom and independence. His life serves as an inspiration to all those who strive for a better world, free from oppression and exploitation.
