Title: Diplomatic Envoys from People's Democratic Countries Stationed in the Soviet Union Visit Lenin's Mausoleum and Present Wreaths; Broad Masses of Soviet People Go to Red Square to Pay Respects to Lenin's Remains
Author: 
Date: 1950-01-24
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, Beijing, January 23rd. TASS, Moscow, January 22nd: On the 21st, Polish Ambassador Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary Naszkowski, Czechoslovak Republic Ambassador Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary Lastovicka, People’s Republic of China Ambassador Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary Wang Jiaxiang, Democratic People’s Republic of Korea Ambassador Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary Zhu Ninghe, Bulgarian People’s Republic Ambassador Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary Mrs. Blagoeva, Hungarian People’s Republic Ambassador Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary Szobek, Mongolian People’s Republic Minister Plenipotentiary Idamjab, Albanian People’s Republic Minister Plenipotentiary Natanaeli, Head of the Diplomatic Mission of the German Democratic Republic Abel, Romanian People’s Republic Chargé d'Affaires ad interim Bradisteanu, and the embassy staff of these countries, all visited Lenin's Mausoleum and presented wreaths on behalf of their respective embassies.

In addition, the Bulgarian Embassy presented a wreath on behalf of the Central Committee of the Bulgarian Communist Party, the Romanian Embassy presented a wreath on behalf of the Romanian Workers' Party and the Romanian government, and the Albanian Legation presented a wreath on behalf of the Albanian Workers' Party.

Xinhua News Agency, Beijing, January 23rd. TASS, Moscow, January 22nd: In recent days, thousands upon thousands of working men and women from all over the Soviet Union have flocked to Red Square and to Lenin's Mausoleum to pay their respects to the greatest genius of mankind, the organizer and leader of the Bolshevik Party, and the founder of the world's first socialist state.

Before the Lenin Mausoleum was opened, a long line of people had already gathered in front of the mausoleum's doors to pay homage to the great Lenin. Among them were workers, staff, scientists, and famous artists from Moscow; miners from the Donbas; steelworkers from the Urals; cotton farmers from Uzbekistan; collective farmers from Ukraine; Stakhanovite workers from the tea gardens of Georgia; soldiers and officers of the Soviet Army; students; and representatives of the working people of foreign countries.

When the mausoleum doors opened, people entered the final resting place of Lenin with solemn hearts, slowly walking past the sarcophagus where Lenin's remains have lain for twenty-six years.

Since that sorrowful day of January 21, 1924, approximately 27 million people have visited Lenin's Mausoleum. On the 21st and 22nd alone, approximately 6,000 people came to pay their respects.

### 驻苏人民民主国家外交使节往谒列宁墓呈献花圈  广大苏联人民赴红场向列宁遗体致敬

1950-01-24
第4版()
专栏：

    驻苏人民民主国家外交使节往谒列宁墓呈献花圈
    广大苏联人民赴红场向列宁遗体致敬
    【新华社北京二十三日电】塔斯社莫斯科二十二日讯：二十一日，波兰特命全权大使纳兹科夫斯基、捷克斯洛伐克共和国特命全权大使拉斯托维契卡、中华人民共和国特命全权大使王稼祥、朝鲜民主主义人民共和国特命全权大使朱宁河、保加利亚人民共和国特命全权大使布拉果耶娃夫人、匈牙利人民共和国特命全权大使索贝克、蒙古人民共和国特命全权公使伊丹甲布、阿尔巴尼亚人民共和国特命全权公使纳塔奈利、德意志民主共和国外交使团团长阿贝尔、罗马尼亚人民共和国临时代办勃拉第斯古和这些国家的使馆职员，均往谒列宁墓，并代表各该使馆呈献花圈。
    此外，保加利亚大使馆代表保加利亚共产党中央委员会、罗马尼亚大使馆代表罗马尼亚工人党和罗马尼亚政府、阿尔巴尼亚公使馆代表阿尔巴尼亚工人党呈献了花圈。
    【新华社北京二十三日电】塔斯社莫斯科二十二日讯：这几天来自苏联各地数以千计的劳动男女，成群结队地往红场，往列宁墓，向人类最伟大的天才，布尔什维克党的组织者和领袖，和世界上第一个社会主义国家的缔造者致敬。
    在列宁墓开放之前，就有一长列前来向伟大的列宁致敬的人民群众，站在列宁墓门前。这里有莫斯科的工人、职员、科学家、著名艺术家，顿巴斯的矿工、乌拉尔的钢铁工人、乌兹贝克的植棉农，乌克兰的集体农民、乔治亚茶园的斯塔哈诺夫式工作者、苏军将士、学生和外国劳动人民的代表。
    当墓门启放时，人们怀着沉重的心情步入列宁最后安息的地方。慢步地从那二十六年来躺着列宁遗体的石棺旁走过。
    自从一九二四年一月二十一日沉痛的日子以来，约有二千七百万人已进谒过列宁墓。在二十一和二十二日两天中，前来谒陵的群众约六千人。
