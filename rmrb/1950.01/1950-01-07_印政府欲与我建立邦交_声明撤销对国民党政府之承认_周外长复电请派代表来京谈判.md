Title: Indian Government Intends to Establish Diplomatic Relations with Us Declares Withdrawal of Recognition of the Kuomintang Government Zhou Enlai Replies, Requesting Representatives to Come to Beijing for Negotiations
Author: 
Date: 1950-01-07
Page: 1

Xinhua News Agency, Beijing, 5th— Foreign Minister Zhou Enlai of the People's Republic of China, upon receiving a note from Indian Foreign Minister Jawaharlal Nehru regarding the Indian government's desire to establish diplomatic relations with our government, replied on the 4th of this month, requesting the Indian government to send representatives to Beijing to negotiate this matter. Indian Foreign Minister Nehru sent a note to Foreign Minister Zhou Enlai on the 30th of last month stating: After receiving Foreign Minister Zhou Enlai's letter of October 1, 1949, regarding the Central People's Government of the People's Republic of China's willingness to establish diplomatic relations with various governments, the Indian government now expresses its desire to enter into diplomatic relations with the People's Republic of China. Subsequently, Yan Yesen, the secretary of the former Indian Embassy to the Kuomintang government in Nanjing, delivered an official letter to Huang Hua, director of the Foreign Affairs Office of the Nanjing Military Control Commission, on the 31st of last month, stating: The Indian government has declared the withdrawal of its recognition of the Kuomintang government and has notified the Kuomintang government's ambassador to Delhi to cease all diplomatic relations thereafter. In view of this situation, Foreign Minister Zhou Enlai replied to Indian Foreign Minister Jawaharlal Nehru on the 4th of this month, stating in brief: "The People's Republic of China is willing to establish diplomatic relations between the People's Republic of China and the Indian government on the basis of equality, mutual benefit, and mutual respect for territorial sovereignty, and hopes that the Indian government will send representatives to Beijing to negotiate this matter." Hoping the Indian government will send representatives to Beijing to negotiate this matter."

### 印政府欲与我建立邦交  声明撤销对国民党政府之承认  周外长复电请派代表来京谈判

1950-01-07
第1版()
专栏：

    印政府欲与我建立邦交
    声明撤销对国民党政府之承认         
    周外长复电请派代表来京谈判
    【新华社北京五日电】中华人民共和国外交部周恩来部长在收到印度外交部长加瓦哈尔拉·尼赫鲁关于印度政府欲与我国政府建立外交关系之愿望的照会后，已于本月四日予以答复，请印度政府派遣代表来北京就此项问题进行谈判。印度外交部长尼赫鲁于上月三十日致周恩来部长照会称：印度政府在收到周恩来部长在一九四九年十月一日关于通知中华人民共和国中央人民政府愿与各国政府建立外交关系的公函后，现在表示欲与中华人民共和国进入外交关系之愿望。随后，印度前驻南京国民党政府大使馆秘书雁谒森于上月三十一日向我南京市军事管制委员会外事处处长黄华送致公函一件，内称：印度政府业已声明撤销其对国民党政府之承认，并已通知国民党政府驻德里大使此后停止一切外交关系。鉴于这种情况，我外交部周恩来部长乃于本月四日电复印度外交部部长加瓦哈尔拉·尼赫鲁，略称：“中华人民共和国愿在平等、互利及互相尊重领土主权的基础上与印度政府建立中华人民共和国与印度政府之间的外交关系，并望印度政府派遣代表前来北京，就此项问题进行谈判。”望印度政府派遣代表前来北京，就此项问题进行谈判。”
