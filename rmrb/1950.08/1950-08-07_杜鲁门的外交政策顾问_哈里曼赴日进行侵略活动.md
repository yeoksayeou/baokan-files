Title: Truman's Foreign Policy Advisor Harriman Goes to Japan to Conduct Aggressive Activities
Author:
Date: 1950-08-07
Page: 4

　　Truman's Foreign Policy Advisor
    Harriman Goes to Japan to Conduct Aggressive Activities
    [Xinhua News Agency, 6th] Following the recent visits to Japan by American warmongers Dulles, Johnson, Collins, and others, Truman's Special Advisor on Foreign Policy, Harriman, carrying a "special mission" given to him by Truman, flew from Washington to Tokyo on the morning of the 4th. According to Truman's announcement at a press conference on the 3rd, Harriman's mission is to "discuss the political situation in the Far East with MacArthur." Accompanying Harriman were U.S. Air Force Vice Chief of Staff Lieutenant General Norstad and former U.S. Joint Chiefs of Staff military attaché Major General Lowell. Sources from Washington reveal that Harriman's talks with MacArthur will involve strengthening the United States' aggressive actions in Korea, further supporting Japanese militarist forces, and implementing aggressive policies towards our country's Taiwan, and even the entire Far East.

### 杜鲁门的外交政策顾问  哈里曼赴日进行侵略活动

1950-08-07
第4版()
专栏：

　　杜鲁门的外交政策顾问
    哈里曼赴日进行侵略活动
    【新华社六日讯】继美国战争贩子杜勒斯、詹逊、柯林斯等人最近访日之后，杜鲁门外交政策特别顾问的哈里曼，负着杜鲁门给他的“特殊使命”，于四日晨从华盛顿飞往东京。据杜鲁门于三日在招待记者会上宣布：哈里曼的使命是“与麦克阿瑟讨论远东的政治局势”。与哈里曼同行者有美国空军副参谋长诺斯塔德中将及前美国参谋总部武官洛威少将。据来自华盛顿的消息透露，哈里曼与麦克阿瑟的会谈，将涉及加强美国在朝鲜的侵略行动，进一步扶植日本军国主义势力和侵略我国台湾以至对整个远东的侵略政策等问题。
