　　1950 National Spring Water Conservancy Project

　　In 1950, the national spring water conservancy project completed a total of 365,000,000 cubic meters of earthwork. If this earthwork were used to build a levee with a height and thickness of one meter each, connecting them together, this levee could circle the Earth 9.1 times. The most important of these were flood control projects. The planned and completed earthwork quantities in various regions of the country are as follows: (Unit: 10,000 cubic meters)
Region  Planned Earthwork     Completed Earthwork     Percentage Completed
   National          34,192   31,082     90.9
   East China          15,776   14,995     95.0
   Central and South China           11,742   11,185    95.2
   North China             4,115     2,950    71.7
   Northeast China             2,559     1,952    76.3
