Title: Provisional Regulations for Institutions of Higher Education Promulgated by the Ministry of Education of the Central People's Government
Author:
Date: 1950-08-19
Page: 3

Xinhua News Agency

The Ministry of Education of the Central People's Government Promulgates
Provisional Regulations for Institutions of Higher Education

【Xinhua News Agency News】The Ministry of Education of the Central People's Government promulgated the "Provisional Regulations for Institutions of Higher Education" on the 14th. The full text is as follows:

Chapter I General Principles

Article 1 The purpose of institutions of higher education in the People's Republic of China is, in accordance with the provisions of Chapter V of the Common Program of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference, to cultivate senior construction talents with advanced cultural levels, mastery of modern science and technology achievements, and wholeheartedly serving the people, using educational methods that combine theory with practice.

Article 2 The specific tasks of institutions of higher education are as follows:

	(1) To conduct revolutionary political and ideological education in accordance with the Common Program of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference, eliminate feudal, comprador, and fascist ideology, establish correct viewpoints and methods, and promote the idea of serving the people;
	(2) To adapt to the needs of national construction, carry out teaching work, and cultivate specialized talents who are proficient in basic theories and can apply them in practice: such as engineers, teachers, doctors, agricultural technicians, financial and economic cadres, language and art workers;
	(3) To use correct viewpoints and methods to study natural sciences, social sciences, philosophy, literature, and art, in order to achieve inventions, writings, and other achievements that meet practical needs;
	(4) To popularize scientific and technological knowledge and disseminate literary and artistic achievements.

Article 3 Institutions of higher education include two categories: universities and specialized colleges. In order to meet the urgent needs of national construction, specialized schools may be established, and their regulations will be determined separately.

Article 4 The establishment and closure of universities and specialized colleges shall be decided by the Ministry of Education of the Central People's Government (hereinafter referred to as the Central Ministry of Education) after reporting to the State Council of the Central People's Government (hereinafter referred to as the State Council).

Article 5 Universities and specialized colleges shall establish several departments, and their establishment or change shall be decided by the Central Ministry of Education.

Article 6 If necessary, universities may establish colleges and set up several departments within the colleges; the establishment or change of colleges and departments shall be decided by the Central Ministry of Education.

Article 7 The length of study in universities and specialized colleges shall be determined according to the complexity of the curriculum of each department, with three to five years as the principle.

Article 8 In order to cultivate and improve teachers and strengthen research work, universities and specialized colleges may, with the approval of the Central Ministry of Education, establish research departments or institutes, and their regulations will be determined separately.

Article 9 In order to meet the urgent needs of national construction, universities and specialized colleges may, with the approval of the Central Ministry of Education, establish special training courses and training classes.

Chapter II Admission

Article 10 Any person who is at least seventeen years old, in good health, and graduated from a senior high school or equivalent school, or has equivalent academic ability, and passes the entrance examination, may be admitted regardless of gender, ethnicity, or religious belief.

Article 11 Universities and specialized colleges shall provide special care for the admission and study of the following students who have the equivalent of a high school diploma: (1) revolutionary cadres with considerable work experience; (2) young workers and peasants; (3) students from minority nationalities; (4) overseas Chinese students. The specific measures will be determined separately.

Chapter III Curriculum, Examinations, and Graduation

Article 12 The curriculum of each department in universities and specialized colleges shall be formulated according to the needs of national construction and the principle of combining theory with practice.

Curriculum standards will be determined separately.

Article 13 Universities and specialized colleges shall submit the teaching plans and teaching outlines of each subject to the Central Ministry of Education for record.

Article 14 Students of universities and specialized colleges must determine a topic in the final academic year, which shall be approved by the department head, and guided by the director of the teaching research guidance group or a teacher designated by him, to write a graduation thesis or special report. Under special circumstances, the graduation thesis may be replaced by other work achievements.

Article 15 Examinations in universities and specialized colleges are divided into entrance examinations, regular examinations, semester examinations, and graduation examinations.

Article 16 Students of universities and specialized colleges who have completed the prescribed courses for the prescribed period and have passed the examinations shall be issued graduation certificates by the school after reporting to the Central Ministry of Education for approval.

Chapter IV Teaching Organization

Article 17 Teachers in universities and specialized colleges are divided into four levels: professors, associate professors, lecturers, and teaching assistants, all of whom are appointed by the president (dean) and reported to the Central Ministry of Education for record.

Article 18 The teaching research guidance group (hereinafter referred to as the teaching research group) is the basic teaching organization, composed of all teachers of one subject or several subjects of similar nature; each teaching research group shall have one director, who shall be appointed by the president (dean) from among the professors and reported to the Central Ministry of Education for record. Its responsibilities are as follows:

	(1) To lead all teachers in the group, discuss and formulate the teaching plan and teaching outline for the group's courses;
	(2) To lead and inspect the teaching work and research work of the group;
	(3) To lead and organize students' self-study, experiments, and internships.

Chapter V Administrative Organization

Article 19 Universities and specialized colleges adopt the president (dean) responsibility system; universities shall have one president, and specialized colleges shall have one dean, whose responsibilities are as follows:

	(1) To represent the school;
	(2) To lead all teaching, research, and administrative matters of the school (college);
	(3) To lead the political study of all teachers, students, staff, workers, and security personnel of the school (college);
	(4) To appoint and dismiss teachers, staff, workers, and security personnel;
	(5) To approve the resolutions of the school (college) affairs committee.

Article 20 Universities and specialized colleges may have one or two vice-presidents (vice-deans) to assist the president (dean) in handling school (college) affairs; the vice-president (vice-dean) may concurrently serve as the provost.

Article 21 Universities and specialized colleges shall have one provost, and may have a vice-provost if necessary, who shall be responsible to the president (dean) and selected by the president (dean) from among the professors and submitted to the Central Ministry of Education for appointment. His/her responsibilities are as follows:

	(1) To plan, organize, supervise, and inspect the teaching work of all departments and teaching research groups in the school (college);
	(2) To plan, organize, supervise, and inspect the scientific research work of the school (college);
	(3) To act on behalf of the president (dean) and vice-president (vice-dean) when both are absent.

Article 22 Universities and specialized colleges shall have one director of general affairs, who shall be responsible to the president (dean) and preside over the administrative affairs of the school (college). He/She shall be nominated by the president (dean) and appointed by the Central Ministry of Education.

Article 23 The library of universities and specialized colleges shall have one librarian or director, who shall be responsible to the provost and preside over all matters of the library. He/She shall be appointed by the president (dean) and reported to the Central Ministry of Education for record.

Article 24 The department of universities and specialized colleges is the basic unit of teaching administration. Each department shall have one director, who shall be under the leadership of the provost (in universities with colleges, under the dual leadership of the provost and the dean); he/she shall be appointed by the president (dean) from among the professors and reported to the Central Ministry of Education for record. His/Her responsibilities are as follows:

	(1) To plan and preside over the teaching administrative work of the department;
	(2) To supervise the implementation of the department's teaching plan;
	(3) To lead and inspect students' self-study, experiments, and internships in the department;
	(4) To assess students' performance in the department;
	(5) To summarize the teaching experience of the department;
	(6) To propose suggestions on the appointment and removal of faculty and staff in the department.

Article 25 Each college in a university shall have one dean, who shall be appointed by the president from among the professors and reported to the Central Ministry of Education for record. His/Her responsibilities are as follows:

	(1) To plan and preside over the teaching administrative work of the college;
	(2) To supervise the implementation of the teaching plans of each department in the college;
	(3) To propose suggestions on the candidates for the heads of each department in the college.

Article 26 Universities and specialized colleges shall establish a school (college) affairs committee under the leadership of the president (dean), which shall be composed of the president (dean), vice-president (vice-dean), provost, vice-provost, director of general affairs, librarian (director), deans of each college (in universities), heads of each department, four to six representatives of the labor union, and two representatives of the student union. The president (dean) shall be the ex officio chairman. The powers of the school (college) affairs committee are as follows:

	(1) To review the teaching plans, research plans, and work reports of each department and each teaching research group;
	(2) To approve the budget and final accounts;
	(3) To approve various important systems and regulations;
	(4) To deliberate on major awards and punishments related to students;
	(5) To deliberate on major reforms and innovations in the school (college).

The school (college) affairs committee may establish a standing committee and various specialized committees.

Article 27 Universities and specialized colleges shall hold teaching affairs meetings under the leadership of the provost, joint meetings of several department heads, and joint meetings of several teaching research group directors; general affairs meetings shall be held under the leadership of the director of general affairs; and department affairs meetings shall be held under the leadership of each department head. Universities with colleges shall hold college affairs meetings under the leadership of the dean, replacing the joint meeting of department heads.

Chapter VI Organizations

Article 28 Labor unions, student unions, and other organizations in universities and specialized colleges shall unite all staff and students of the school (college), assist the school in completing teaching and administrative plans, promote the political, professional, and cultural learning of all staff and students of the school (college), and improve the welfare of staff and students.

Article 29 Universities and specialized colleges may establish various academic organizations to promote the improvement and popularization of science and culture.

Chapter VII Supplementary Provisions

Article 30 Existing universities or specialized colleges that have practical difficulties in fully implementing the provisions of these Regulations regarding administrative organization may report to the education department (cultural and educational department) of the large administrative region for review and then report to the Central Ministry of Education for approval to implement them with modifications.

Article 31 Private universities and specialized colleges shall, in addition to complying with these Regulations, also comply with the "Provisional Measures for the Management of Private Institutions of Higher Education."

Article 32 These Regulations shall be promulgated and implemented after being approved by the State Council after being reported by the Central Ministry of Education, and the same shall apply to their modification.

### 中央人民政府教育部颁布  高等学校暂行规程
新华社
1950-08-19
第3版()
专栏：

　　中央人民政府教育部颁布
    高等学校暂行规程
    【新华社讯】中央人民政府教育部于十四日颁布《高等学校暂行规程》。全文如下：
    第一章  总纲
    第一条  中华人民共和国高等学校的宗旨为根据中国人民政治协商会议共同纲领第五章的规定，以理论与实际一致的教育方法，培养具有高级文化水平，掌握现代科学和技术的成就，全心全意为人民服务的高级建设人才。
    第二条  高等学校的具体任务如下：
    （一）根据中国人民政治协商会议共同纲领，进行革命的政治及思想教育，肃清封建的、买办的、法西斯主义的思想，树立正确的观点和方法，发扬为人民服务的思想；
    （二）适应国家建设的需要，进行教学工作，培养通晓基本理论并能实际运用的专门人才：如工程师、教师、医师、农业技师、财政经济干部、语文和艺术工作者；
    （三）运用正确的观点和方法，研究自然科学、社会科学、哲学、文学、艺术，以期有切合实际需要的发明、著作等成就；
    （四）普及科学和技术的知识，传播文学和艺术的成果。
    第三条  高等学校包括大学及专门学院两类。为适应国家建设的急需得设立专科学校，其规程另定之。
    第四条  大学及专门学院的设立与停办，由中央人民政府教育部（以下简称中央教育部）报请中央人民政府政务院（以下简称政务院）决定之。
    第五条  大学及专门学院设若干学系，其设立或变更由中央教育部决定之。
    第六条  大学如有必要，得设学院，并在学院内设若干学系；学院及学系的设立或变更，由中央教育部决定之。
    第七条  大学及专门学院修业年限，依各该系课程的繁简分别规定以三年至五年为原则。
    第八条  大学及专门学院为培养及提高师资，加强研究工作，经中央教育部批准，得设研究部或研究所，其规程另定之。
    第九条  大学及专门学院为适应国家建设的急需，经中央教育部批准，得附设专修科及训练班。
  第二章  入学
    第十条  凡年满十七岁、身体健康、在高级中学或同等学校毕业或有同等学力，经入学考试及格者，不分性别、民族、宗教信仰，均得入学。
    第十一条  大学及专门学院对于具有相当于高中毕业程度的下列学生：（一）具有相当工作历史的革命干部；（二）工农青年；（三）少数民族学生；（四）华侨学生；应予以入学及学习的特别照顾。其办法另定之。
    第三章  课程、考试、毕业
    第十二条  大学及专门学院各系课程，应根据国家建设的需要及理论与实际一致的原则制定。
    课程标准另定之。
    第十三条  大学及专门学院应将各课目的教学计划及教学大纲，报请中央教育部备案。
    第十四条  大学及专门学院学生须于最后一学年确定专题经系主任核准，由教学研究指导组主任或其指定的教师指导，撰写毕业论文或专题报告。在特殊情形下毕业论文得以他种工作成绩代替之。
    第十五条  大学及专门学院考试分为入学考试、平时考试、学期考试及毕业考试。
    第十六条  大学及专门学院学生依照规定课程修业期满，成绩及格者，由学校报请中央教育部核准发给毕业证书。
    第四章  教学组织
    第十七条  大学及专门学院教师，分为教授、副教授、讲师、助教四级，均由校（院）长聘任，报请中央教育部备案。
    第十八条  教学研究指导组（以下简称教研组）为教学的基本组织，由一种课目或性质相近的几种课目之全体教师组成之；各教研组设主任一人，由校（院）长就教授中聘任，报请中央教育部备案。其职责如下：
    （一）领导本组全体教师，讨论及制定本组课目的教学计划与教学大纲；
    （二）领导及检查本组的教学工作和研究工作；
    （三）领导与组织本组学生的自习、实验及实习。
    第五章  行政组织
    第十九条  大学及专门学院采校（院）长负责制；大学设校长一人，专门学院设院长一人，其职责如下：
    （一）代表学校；
    （二）领导全校（院）一切教学、研究及行政事宜；
    （三）领导全校（院）教师、学生、职员、工警的政治学习；
    （四）任免教师、职员、工警；
    （五）批准校（院）务委员会的决议。
    第二十条  大学及专门学院得设副校（院）长一人或二人，协助校（院）长处理校（院）务，校（院）长缺席时代行其职务；副校（院）长得兼教务长。
    第二十一条  大学及专门学院，设教务长一人，必要时得设副教务长，对校（院）长负责，由校（院）长就教授中遴选提请中央教育部任命之。其职责如左：
    （一）计划、组织、督导、检查全校（院）各系及各教研组的教学工作；
    （二）计划、组织、督导、检查全校（院）的科学研究工作；
    （三）校（院）长及副校（院）长均缺席时代行其职务。
    第二十二条  大学及专门学院设总务长一人，对校（院）长负责，主持全校（院）的行政事务工作。由校（院）长提请中央教育部任命之。
    第二十三条  大学及专门学院图书馆，设馆长或主任一人，对教务长负责，主持图书馆一切事宜，由校（院）长聘任，报请中央教育部备案。
    第二十四条  大学及专门学院的系，为教学行政的基层组织，各设主任一人，受教务长领导（在设有学院之大学，则受教务长与院双重领导）；由校（院）长就教授中聘任，报请中央教育部备案。其职责如下：
    （一）计划并主持本系的教学行政工作；
    （二）督导执行本系教学计划；
    （三）领导并检查本系学生的自习、实验及实习；
    （四）考核本系学生成绩；
    （五）总结本系教学经验；
    （六）提出有关本系教职员任免的建议。
    第二十五条  大学设有学院者各院设院长一人，由校长就教授中聘任，报请中央教育部备案。其职责如下：
    （一）计划并主持本院教学行政工作；
    （二）督导本院各系执行教学计划；
    （三）提出本院各系主任人选的建议。
    第二十六条  大学及专门学院在校（院）长领导下设校（院）务委员会，由校（院）长、副校（院）长、教务长、副教务长、总务长、图书馆长（主任）、各院（大学中的学院）院长、各系主任、工会代表四人至六人及学生会代表二人组成之，校（院）长为当然主席。校（院）务委员会的职权如下：
    （一）审查各系及各教研组的教学计划、研究计划及工作报告；
    （二）通过预算和决算；
    （三）通过各种重要制度及规章；
    （四）议决有关学生重大奖惩事项；
    （五）议决全校（院）重大兴革事项。
    校（院）务委员会得设常务委员会及各种专门委员会。
    第二十七条  大学及专门学院在教务长领导下举行教务会议，若干系主任的联席会议及若干教研组主任的联席会议；在总务长领导下举行总务会议；在各系主任领导下举行系务会议。大学设有学院者，在院长领导下举行院务会议，代替系主任联席会议。
    第六章  社团
    第二十八条  大学及专门学院的工会、学生会等社团应团结全校（院）员工、学生，协助学校完成教学及行政计划，推动全校（院）员工、学生的政治、业务与文化学习，并增进员工、学生的生活福利。
    第二十九条  大学及专门学院得成立各种学术团体以促进科学、文化的提高与普及。
    第七章  附则
    第三十条  现有大学或专门学院因实际困难，不能完全实施本规程中关于行政组织的规定者，得报经大行政区教育部（文教部）审核后，转报中央教育部批准，变通执行。
    第三十一条  私立大学及专门学院除遵守本规程外，并须遵守《私立高等学校管理暂行办法》。
    第三十二条  本规程由中央教育部报经政务院批准后颁布施行，其修改同。
