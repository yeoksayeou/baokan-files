Title: European People Protest US Imperialist Aggression Against Korea; Polish and Romanian People's Organizations Voice Support for Korean People; 300,000 Berlin Workers Rally and Demonstrate in Protest
Author:
Date: 1950-07-08
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, July 7th—Comprehensive reports from the Polish News Agency and Romanian News Agency: The people of Poland and Romania are angrily protesting the US imperialist act of aggression in Korea. They unanimously sympathize with and support the just struggle of the Korean people for independence and unification, and demand that the US imperialist troops withdraw from Korea. Meetings have been held continuously throughout Poland. At the meetings, working people put forward production pledges, determined to strengthen the forces of the peace camp to support the just struggle of the Korean people.

The Polish National Committee for the Defense of Peace, the Central Council of Trade Unions of Poland, and women's and student organizations sent telegrams to the Korean people, expressing their firm belief in the victory of the Korean people. The Romanian General Confederation of Labor and the Romanian Working Youth Union both sent telegrams to the Korean people expressing their support. The Romanian Working Youth Union stated: Romanian working youth will unite with Korean youth to smash the imperialists' conspiracy to incite a new war and to fight for peace, freedom, and the victory of socialism.

Xinhua News Agency, Prague, July 7th—News of protests by the people of Western Europe against the US imperialist armed aggression against Korea is constantly coming from various places. Various people's organizations in France have submitted protests to the US Embassy in France or issued declarations condemning the US imperialist acts of aggression. These organizations include the Paris Southeast Railway Workers' Union, the Metal, Timber, and Construction Workers' Union affiliated with the General Confederation of Labor, the Paris France-Soviet Friendship Association, members of the French Republican Youth League, and many other social organizations. The National Committee of the Progressive Christians League also issued a statement supporting the Korean people against American aggression. On July 3, many delegations from workers and mass organizations from various enterprises across the country went to the American Embassy in Paris to demand an end to armed intervention in Korea.

In Italy, according to the British "Daily Worker," hundreds of factories held protest strikes, and there were mass demonstrations in front of US consulates in various places.

"L'Humanité" reported that more than 300,000 Berlin workers rallied in Lustgarten to protest against US imperialism's aggression against Korea. The Free German Trade Union Federation issued a protest statement. Workers in all enterprises in Democratic Germany voted in favor of condemning the US imperialist aggression in Korea. In Hamburg, West Germany, masses organized by the Democratic Women's League held a protest demonstration in front of the US Consulate.

According to a report in the British "Daily Worker" on the evening of June 29, more than 2,000 people gathered at Marble Arch in London to protest the aggressive war launched by US imperialism in Korea. In Merseyside (a port exiting the Irish Sea) and Tilbury (a port at the mouth of the Thames east of London), dockworkers, porters, and housewives held protest rallies. The broad masses of the people in Manchester participated in mass rallies held by the Communist Party of Great Britain in front of major factories. Trade union organizations and people's organizations across the country have continuously sent telegrams to Prime Minister Attlee and the US Embassy, protesting against interference in Korea's internal affairs and firmly stating that the British people will never participate in any war of aggression. These include railway workers, clothing workers, veterans, the Amalgamated Engineering Union, the Valleyfield Branch of the National Union of Mineworkers, and workers at the Johnstones factory in Earlestown. Mass rallies were held in Langshaw, Liverpool, and Stafford to protest American aggression against Korea and demand the immediate withdrawal of American troops from Korea.

### 欧洲人民抗议美帝侵略朝鲜  波、罗各人民团体声援朝鲜人民  柏林卅万工人集会举行抗议示威

1950-07-08
第4版()
专栏：

　　欧洲人民抗议美帝侵略朝鲜
    波、罗各人民团体声援朝鲜人民
    柏林卅万工人集会举行抗议示威
    【新华社七日讯】综合波兰通讯社罗马尼亚通讯社消息：波兰和罗马尼亚两国人民愤怒地抗议美帝国主义在朝鲜的侵略行为，一致同情和声援朝鲜人民争取独立和统一的正义斗争，并要求美帝国主义的军队退出朝鲜。波兰全国各地连续地举行了集会，劳动人民在会上提出了生产保证，决以加强和平阵营的力量来支援朝鲜人民的正义斗争。
    波兰全国拥护和平委员会、波兰工会中央理事会以及妇女和学生团体致电朝鲜人民，表示了对朝鲜人民的胜利的坚定信念。罗马尼亚总工会和罗马尼亚劳动青年联盟均致电朝鲜人民表示声援。罗马尼亚劳动青年联盟表示：罗国劳动青年将与朝鲜青年团结一致，为粉碎帝国主义者煽动新战争的阴谋，为争取和平、自由与社会主义的胜利而斗争。
    【新华社布拉格七日电】西欧人民抗议美帝国主义武装侵略朝鲜的消息不断从各地传来。法国各人民团体纷纷向美国驻法大使馆提出抗议，或发表斥责美帝国主义侵略行为的宣言。这些团体中包括巴黎东南铁路工会、法总工会所属五金木材建筑工会、巴黎法苏友好协会、法兰西共和青年联盟盟员及其他许多社会团体。进步基督徒联盟全国委员会也发表公告，支持朝鲜人民反对美国的侵略。七月三日有许多来自各地的各企业工人和民众团体的代表团，到巴黎美国大使馆，要求停止对朝鲜的武装干涉。
    在意大利，据英工人日报消息，成百的工厂举行抗议罢工，各地美领馆门前均有群众示威。
    人道报报道：三十余万柏林工人曾在鲁斯特公园集会，抗议美帝侵略朝鲜。自由德国工会联合会发表了抗议声明。民主德国所有企业中的工人都投票赞成斥责美帝在朝鲜的侵略行为。在西德汉堡，民主妇女联盟所组织的群众，在美领事馆前举行了抗议示威。
    另据英工人日报消息：六月二十九日晚，两千余人在伦敦“大理石拱门”集会，抗议美帝在朝鲜发动侵略性的战争。在默锡赛德（出爱尔兰海的港口）和提尔伯利（伦敦以东泰晤士河出口处港口），均有码头工人、搬运工人与家庭妇女举行抗议大会。曼彻斯特广大人民参加了英国共产党在各大工厂门前召开的群众大会。全国各工会组织和人民团体不断致电首相艾德礼和美使馆，抗议干涉朝鲜内政，坚决表示英国人民决不参加任何侵略战争。其中有铁路工人、服装工人、退伍军人、混合机器工会、全国矿工联盟谷田分会以及厄尔郡约翰斯顿的约翰兰斯工厂工人等。在朗邵尔、利物浦、士塔佛德都举行了群众性集会，抗议美国侵略朝鲜，要求美军立即自朝鲜撤退。
