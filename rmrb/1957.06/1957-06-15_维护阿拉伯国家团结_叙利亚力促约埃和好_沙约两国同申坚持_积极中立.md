Title: Maintaining Arab Unity: Syria Urges Reconciliation Between Jordan and Egypt; Saudi Arabia and Jordan Declare Adherence to "Positive Neutrality"
Author:
Date: 1957-06-15
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, June 14th: Damascus dispatch: Syrian Prime Minister al-Assali, in a conversation published in "Al-文明报" on the 13th, denied the so-called news that the Syrian government "intends to sever relations with Jordan."

　　Al-Assali said: "On the contrary, the Syrian government is continuing to engage with other Arab leaders to enable Jordan and Egypt to restore reconciliation."

　　Al-Assali said that Syria is determined to "maintain the unity between brotherly Arab countries in order to deal with the dangers that threaten them."

　　Xinhua News Agency, June 14th: Amman dispatch: Jordanian King Hussein and Saudi Arabian King Saud issued a joint communique on June 13th, stating that the two countries adhere to a policy of "positive neutrality" and will not participate in any foreign conventions or alliances of any nature.

　　This communique concluded Saudi Arabia's one-week visit to Jordan. Saud left Amman on the morning of the 14th to return home. Before leaving, he delivered a speech at the airport, guaranteeing full support for Jordan.

　　The communique stated that the defense of the Arab world should be the responsibility of the Arab countries themselves. The two countries decided to support the military cooperation between Jordan, Saudi Arabia, Syria, and Egypt, in accordance with the agreements already concluded, in order to deal with their common enemies.

　　The communique stated that they abide by the Charter of the Arab League, the Charter of the United Nations, the Arab Collective Security Treaty, the Bandung Conference resolutions, the resolutions of the Cairo meeting of the four Arab heads of state in February of this year, and the Arab Solidarity Agreement.

　　The communique stated that the two countries insist that the Gulf of Aqaba is Arab territorial waters and oppose the opinion that this gulf is an international gulf or that it implies that Israel or any other country has any rights in this gulf.

### 维护阿拉伯国家团结  叙利亚力促约埃和好  沙约两国同申坚持“积极中立”

1957-06-15
第6版()
专栏：

　　维护阿拉伯国家团结
　　叙利亚力促约埃和好
　　沙约两国同申坚持“积极中立”
    新华社14日讯　大马士革消息：叙利亚总理阿萨利13日在“文明报”发表的一篇谈话中否认了所谓叙利亚政府“打算同约旦绝交”的消息。
    阿萨利说：“相反的，叙利亚政府正在继续同其他阿拉伯国家领袖进行接触，以使约旦和埃及之间能恢复和好。”
    阿萨利说，叙利亚决意“维护兄弟般的阿拉伯国家之间的团结，以对付威胁着它们的危险。”
    据新华社14日讯　安曼消息：约旦国王侯赛因和沙特阿拉伯国王沙特6月13日发表会谈联合公报说，两国坚持“积极中立”政策，并且不参加任何性质的外国公约和联盟。
    这项公报结束了沙特在约旦的一星期访问，沙特已经在14日上午离开安曼回国。他临走时在机场发表演说，保证全力支持约旦。
    公报说，阿拉伯世界的防务应该是阿拉伯国家自己的责任。两国决定根据已经缔结的协定支持约旦、沙特阿拉伯、叙利亚和埃及之间的军事合作，以对付它们的共同敌人。
    公报表示他们遵守阿拉伯联盟盟章，联合国宪章，阿拉伯集体安全条约，万隆会议决议，今年2月阿拉伯四国首脑开罗会议决议，以及阿拉伯团结协定。
    公报说，两国坚持认为，亚喀巴湾是阿拉伯领水，反对说这个海湾是国际海湾或者暗示以色列或任何其它国家在这个海湾中有任何权利的意见。
