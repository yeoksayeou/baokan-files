Title: Key Event in Soviet-Finnish Relations: Soviet Leaders Visit Finland; Finnish Prime Minister Expresses Effort to Maintain Soviet-Finnish Understanding
Author:
Date: 1957-06-08
Page: 5

    Key Event in Soviet-Finnish Relations
    Soviet Leaders Visit Finland
    Finnish Prime Minister Expresses Effort to Maintain Soviet-Finnish Understanding
    (Comprehensive report by our newspaper) Xinhua News Agency reported: Chairman of the Council of Ministers of the Soviet Union, Bulganin, and member of the Presidium of the Supreme Soviet of the Soviet Union, Khrushchev, arrived in Helsinki, the capital of Finland, on the 6th.
    The Speaker of the Finnish Parliament, Fagerholm, and Prime Minister Sukselainen, among others, greeted the Soviet dignitaries at the station, which was decorated with the national flags of both the Soviet Union and Finland. Finnish Prime Minister Sukselainen delivered a speech on behalf of the Republic of Finland, welcoming Bulganin and Khrushchev. He said, "We regard this visit, which is now beginning, as a key event in the history of our two countries' relations." In his reply, Bulganin expressed his gratitude for the invitation and the warm welcome. He said that the Soviet people greatly cherish the development of friendly relations between the Soviet Union and Finland.
    On the same day, Bulganin and Khrushchev separately paid visits to Finnish President Kekkonen and Prime Minister Sukselainen.
    Sukselainen also hosted a banquet on the 6th in honor of the Soviet dignitaries. At the banquet, Sukselainen announced that Finland would continue to adhere to a peaceful and neutral foreign policy and would make every effort to maintain the atmosphere of mutual understanding, mutual respect, and trust between it and the Soviet Union forever. He also pointed out that the development of Finnish-Soviet relations in recent years has been gratifying. Bulganin also spoke at the banquet. He pointed out that direct contact between national leaders plays an important role in the development of good-neighborly relations between the two countries. He hoped that this visit would become a new contribution to the development and consolidation of Soviet-Finnish relations.
    On the morning of the 7th, the delegations of the two governments began talks.

### 苏芬关系中的首要事件  苏联领袖到芬兰访问  芬兰总理表示尽力保持芬苏谅解

1957-06-08
第5版()
专栏：

    苏芬关系中的首要事件
    苏联领袖到芬兰访问
    芬兰总理表示尽力保持芬苏谅解
    本报综合报道　据新华社消息：苏联部长会议主席布尔加宁和苏联最高苏维埃主席团委员赫鲁晓夫，6日到达芬兰首都赫尔辛基。
    芬兰议会议长法格霍姆和总理苏克西莱宁等人在悬有芬苏两国国旗的车站上迎接苏联贵宾。芬兰总理苏克西莱宁代表芬兰共和国致词欢迎布尔加宁和赫鲁晓夫。他说，“我们把现在开始的这次访问看成我们两国关系史中的首要事件”。布尔加宁在答词中对这次的邀请和热烈的欢迎表示感谢。他说，苏联人民非常珍视苏芬两国友好关系的发展。
    当天，布尔加宁和赫鲁晓夫分别拜会了芬兰总统吉科宁和总理苏克西莱宁。
    苏克西莱宁并于6日设宴招待了苏联贵宾。在宴会上，苏克西莱宁宣布，芬兰要继续坚持和平中立的外交政策，并且要尽力使它和苏联之间的互相谅解、互相尊重和信任的气氛永远保持下去。他还指出，近年来芬苏关系的发展是令人欣慰的。布尔加宁也在宴会上讲了话。他指出，国家领导人的直接接触对发展两国间的睦邻关系起着重要的作用。希望这次访问将成为发展和巩固苏芬关系方面的新贡献。
    7日上午，两国政府代表团开始会谈。
