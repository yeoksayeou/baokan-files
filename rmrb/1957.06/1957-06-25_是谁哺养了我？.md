Title: Who Nourished Me?……
Author: Gu Gong
Date: 1957-06-25
Page: 8

　　Who Nourished Me?……
　　Gu Gong
　　I often use grateful words,
　　To ask myself a series of quiet questions:
　　Who nourished me?
　　Who nourished me?……
　　It is my mother's pure milk!
　　It is the rolling river flowing east,
　　It is a large expanse of black land,
　　It is the spring rain, it is the summer breeze,
　　It is the old man watching the forest, it is the hoe-carrying farmer,
　　It is the swaying wheat, it is the fruit on the treetops,
　　It is the golden days of August,
　　It is the season of cheers everywhere in October….
　　Without all of this, how could I have
　　A handsome face, nimble fingers,
　　How could I have thick black hair
　　And fresh, warm blood?……
　　Who nourished me?
　　Who nourished me?……
　　It is many songs sung by the cradle,
　　It is the stories told by my grandmother while sunning herself,
　　It is the bustling streets, it is the school threshold,
　　It is "Dream of the Red Chamber" hidden in my father's bookcase,
　　It is a tattered volume of Tang Dynasty poems,
　　It is the simple yet mysterious elements in nature,
　　It is the many glass tubes in the laboratory,
　　It is Darwin's discovery, it is the blackboard covered with chalk,
　　It is the friendship of classmates, it is Pushkin's dream,
　　It is "Anna Karenina" that made me cry again and again,
　　It is Lu Xun's cry of "Call to Arms"….
　　Without all of this, how could I have
　　Intelligent eyes, deep thoughts,
　　How could I have the excitement of love,
　　And the inspiration that bursts like a fountain?……
　　Who nourished me?
　　Who nourished me?……
　　It is the blacksmith who lives next door to my house,
　　It is the thick smoke that rises from the tall chimney every day,
　　It is the angry and indignant drama,
　　It is the sincere conversation of an underground party member,
　　It is the red flag fluttering in the guerrilla隊,
　　It is the warm hands of the political commissar,
　　It is the village burned into ruins by the enemy,
　　It is a rifle whose rifling is almost worn out,
　　It is the starry sky that illuminates our night march,
　　It is the names of Mao Zedong and Lenin,
　　It is the figure rushing forward in the bursting flames….
　　Without all of this, how could I have
　　The courage of a soldier, the proud stride,
　　How could I have resounding language,
　　And a tomorrow that is bluer and clearer than the last?……
　　How should I answer my inner voice,
　　It asks so broadly, and so deeply:
　　Who nourished me?
　　Who nourished me?……

### 是谁哺养了我？……
顾工
1957-06-25
第8版()
专栏：

　　是谁哺养了我？……
　　顾工
　　我常常用感激的心声，
　　向自己提出一串低问：
　　是谁哺养了我啊？
　　是谁哺养了我？……
　　是母亲纯净的乳液啊！
　　是滔滔不断向东流去的河水，
　　是一大片一大片黑色的土地，
　　是春天的细雨，是夏天的熏风，
　　是看林的老人，是背锄头的农妇，
　　是摇摆的麦穗，是树梢上的果实，
　　是八月金色的日子，
　　是十月到处都在欢呼的季节……。
　　没有这一切，我怎么会有
　　俊美的脸庞，灵活的手指，
　　怎么会有乌黑的密发
　　和新鲜的温热的血液？……
　　是谁哺养了我啊？
　　是谁哺养了我？……
　　是许多在摇篮边唱起的歌子，
　　是外祖母在晒太阳时讲的故事，
　　是喧嚣的街道，是学校的门槛，
　　是藏在父亲书橱里的“红楼梦”，
　　是一卷读得很破的唐代的诗篇，
　　是大自然间单纯而又神秘的原素，
　　是实验室里许许多多的玻璃管，
　　是达尔文的发现，是那块写满粉笔字的黑板，
　　是同学的友情，是普希金的梦幻，
　　是让我一次又一次流泪的“安娜·卡列尼娜”，
　　是鲁迅发出的“呐喊”……。
　　如果没有这一切，我怎么会有
　　聪慧的眼睛，深深的思索，
　　怎么会有爱的激动，
　　和那像喷泉一样迸发的灵感？……
　　是谁哺养了我啊？
　　是谁哺养了我？……
　　是住在我家隔壁的那位铁匠，
　　是那每天从高大烟囱里冒出的浓烟，
　　是愤怒不平的戏剧，
　　是一位地下党员诚挚的谈话，
　　是在游击队里飘展的一面红旗，
　　是政治委员一双热情的手，
　　是被敌人烧成一片废墟的村庄，
　　是一支几乎磨光了来复线的枪，
　　是照耀我们夜行军的星空，
　　是毛泽东和列宁的名字，
　　是在爆烈的火光中扑向前去的身影……。
　　如果没有这一切，我怎么会有
　　士兵的勇敢，自豪的阔步，
　　我怎么会有响亮的语言，
　　和一个比一个更蓝、更清朗的明天？……
　　我应该怎么回答我的心声，
　　它问得这样的广阔，又这样的深沉：
　　是谁哺养了我啊？
　　是谁哺养了我？……
