Title: Studying Upper Atmosphere Conditions: Soviet Union to Launch Over One Hundred High-Altitude Rockets
Author:
Date: 1957-06-12
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, 11th June. TASS reports: Soviet newspapers and magazines have recently introduced the situation regarding the Soviet Union's launch of artificial satellites and high-altitude rockets during the International Geophysical Year.

Soviet scientist Fedorov wrote in "Literaturnaya Gazeta" that the Soviet Union will launch over one hundred high-altitude rockets during the International Geophysical Year to study the conditions of the upper atmosphere. These rockets are planned to be launched from the following three locations: Franz Josef Land in the Arctic region, the mid-latitude region of the European part of the Soviet Union, and the "Mirny Station" in Antarctica. They will ascend to an altitude of two hundred kilometers. The rockets contain a special device, which automatically launches the instrument container when it reaches a certain altitude. When this container descends to a height of ten to twelve thousand meters above the ground, the parachute on the container will automatically open to ensure the safe landing of the instruments.

During the flight, the instruments on the rocket will continuously transmit various conditions of the upper atmosphere to the ground receiving station via radio.

However, rockets can only collect limited materials in a short period of time. The artificial satellites manufactured by the Soviet Union will also be launched soon.

The "Ogoniok" magazine published an article on the 9th, predicting the launch of artificial satellites, saying that a three-stage rocket will be vertically placed on the reinforced concrete rocket launch site. A small metal ball is installed at the head of the rocket, which is the artificial satellite. Scientific researchers will turn on the switch in the concrete underground shelter. Then, with a deafening explosion, the rocket will rush into the dim sky before dawn.

In the first stage, the rocket's speed is 10,000 kilometers per hour. When the fuel of the first-stage rocket is exhausted and automatically detached, the second-stage rocket will begin to fly at a speed of 21,000 kilometers per hour. When the engine of the third-stage rocket starts to run, its speed has reached 29,000 kilometers per hour. When it rises to a predetermined altitude, the artificial satellite separates from the rocket head and begins to revolve around the earth independently. It takes about one and a half hours to orbit the earth once, and it can orbit sixteen times per day and night.

When the artificial satellite equipped with various instruments revolves around the earth, its radio station will regularly send out the records of each instrument. Scientists can continuously observe the rotation of the artificial satellite and receive the signals it emits through radar stations and optical instrument stations.

After a certain period of time, the artificial satellite will begin to reduce its speed, descend into the atmosphere, and be burned up by friction with the air.

### 研究大气上层情况  苏联将放射一百多个高空火箭

1957-06-12
第5版()
专栏：

　　研究大气上层情况
　　苏联将放射一百多个高空火箭
    新华社11日讯  据塔斯社报道：苏联报纸和杂志最近介绍了苏联在国际地球物理年期间将发射人造卫星和高空火箭的情形。
    苏联科学家费多罗夫在“文学报”上撰文说：苏联在国际地球物理年期间，将发射一百多个高空火箭，以研究大气上层的情况。这些火箭准备在以下这三个地点发射：北极地区的佛兰兹约瑟夫地、苏联欧洲部分的中纬度地区和南极“和平村”。它们将上升到二百公里的高空。火箭中有一种特殊的装置，当它升到一定高度的时候，安装仪器的容器便会被自动发射出去。当这个容器下降到离地面一万到一万二千公尺的时候，容器上的降落伞便自动打开，保证仪器安全着陆。
    在飞行期间，火箭上的仪器便不断把大气上层的各种情况通过无线电自动地发播到地面接收站。
    但是火箭只能在很短的时间内收到有限的材料，苏联制造的人造卫星不久也要发射了。
    “火星”杂志9日载文预测人造卫星发射的情况说，在钢筋混凝土的火箭放射场上，将垂直地安放一个三段火箭。在火箭的头部装着一个不大的金属球，这就是人造卫星，科学研究人员将在混凝土的地下掩蔽部中接通开关。于是，火箭将在一声震耳欲聋的爆炸中，冲向黎明前昏暗的天空。
    在第一个阶段，火箭速度是每小时一万公里。当第一段火箭的燃料耗尽，并且自动脱落之后，第二段火箭将开始以每小时两万一千公里的速度继续飞行。到第三段火箭的发动机开始转动的时候，它的速度已经达到每小时两万九千公里。当它上升到预定高度的时候，人造卫星就脱离火箭头部，开始独立地围绕地球回转。它绕地球一周约需一个半小时，每昼夜可绕十六周。
    当装备各种仪器的人造卫星绕地球旋转时，它的无线电台就定时把各仪器的记录发出。科学家通过雷达站和光学仪器站，就可以不断地观察人造卫星的旋转情况，和接受它发出的信号。
    经过一定时期以后，人造卫星便开始减低速度，下降到大气层中和空气磨擦而焚毁。
