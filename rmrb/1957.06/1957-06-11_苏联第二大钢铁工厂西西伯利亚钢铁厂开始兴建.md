Title: Construction Begins on the West Siberian Iron and Steel Plant, the Soviet Union's Second Largest Steel Factory
Author:
Date: 1957-06-11
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, Moscow Telegram The construction of one of the world's largest metallurgical plants, the West Siberian Iron and Steel Plant, has begun in the Kuzbass region of Siberia. This factory's production capacity is second only to the Magnitogorsk Iron and Steel Works in the Urals, ranking second in the Soviet Union.

The West Siberian Iron and Steel Plant will be built seventeen kilometers from the Siberian metallurgical city of Stalinsk. This giant enterprise is part of the Soviet Union's third iron and steel industrial base, decided upon by the 20th Congress of the Communist Party of the Soviet Union to be built in Siberia. This base will produce 15 to 20 million tons of pig iron annually. Its other two steel plants will be built in the Krasnoyarsk Krai and Irkutsk Oblast in East Siberia.

The West Siberian Iron and Steel Plant will be equipped with some extremely powerful production equipment. In terms of production capacity, the equipment is larger than any currently owned by steel industrial enterprises worldwide. For example, the factory will build blast furnaces with a volume of 2,286 cubic meters, each capable of producing 4,400 tons of pig iron per day and night. The factory also plans to build open-hearth furnaces with a hearth area of 500 square meters, a coking oven consisting of 77 furnaces, and other huge workshops and auxiliary factories.

In addition to the iron and steel plant, a metal products factory and a pipe manufacturing factory are also planned. Construction of these two factories will begin after 1960.

According to the provisions of the new Five-Year Plan, the West Siberian Iron and Steel Plant can begin partial production in 1960. The iron ore it needs will be supplied by mines throughout Siberia. Therefore, simultaneously with the construction of the factory, several large iron mines will also be built, and the existing Demir, Tashtagol, and Sheregesh iron mines of the Kuznetsk Iron and Steel Combine will be rebuilt and expanded.

### 苏联第二大钢铁工厂西西伯利亚钢铁厂开始兴建

1957-06-11
第5版()
专栏：

　　苏联第二大钢铁工厂西西伯利亚钢铁厂开始兴建
    新华社莫斯科电  在西伯利亚库兹巴斯地区，已经开始建设世界最大冶金工厂之一——西西伯利亚钢铁工厂。这个工厂的生产能力仅次于乌拉尔的马格尼托哥尔斯克钢铁联合工厂，而居苏联第二位。
    西西伯利亚钢铁工厂将建设在西伯利亚的冶金工业城斯大林斯克附近十七公里的地方。这个巨型企业是苏联共产党第二十次代表大会决定在西伯利亚建设的苏联第三个钢铁工业基地的一部分，这个基地每年将生产一千五百万到两千万吨生铁，它的其他两个钢铁工厂将在东西伯利亚的克拉斯诺雅尔斯克边疆区和伊尔库次克州兴建。
    西西伯利亚钢铁工厂将装备一些极为强大的生产设备，就生产能力来说，目前全世界钢铁工业企业所拥有的设备中还没有比它们更大的。譬如，这个工厂将建设一些容积为两千二百八十六立方公尺的高炉，每个这样的高炉一昼夜将可以生产四千四百吨生铁。工厂还计划建设一些炉底面积达五百平方公尺的平炉，由七十七个炉组成的炼焦炉，以及其他巨大的车间和附属工厂。
    除钢铁工厂以外，还准备建设一个金属制品工厂和一个制管工厂。这两个工厂的建设工程将在1960年以后动工。
    根据新五年计划的规定，西西伯利亚钢铁工厂在1960年就可以开始部分投入生产。它所需要的铁矿砂将由西伯利亚各地的矿山供应。因此，和建设工厂同时，还将建设几个大铁矿，并且改建和扩大库兹涅茨克钢铁联合工厂现有的杰米尔、塔什塔哥尔和谢列盖什斯克等铁矿。
