Title: Britain Refuses to Grant Nigeria Independence in 1959; Nigerian Authorities State They Reserve the Right to Demand Complete Independence
Author:
Date: 1957-06-28
Page: 6

Xinhua News Agency, London, June 27th. The demand made by the Nigerian people for final independence in 1959 was rejected by the British Colonial Office after a thirty-three-day discussion at the Nigeria Independence Steps Conference, which concluded here on the 26th.

While refusing to acknowledge this right to independence for the Nigerian people, Colonial Secretary Lennox-Boyd said at the conference that the British government would sympathetically consider Nigeria's independence within the Commonwealth in 1960.

The premiers of Nigeria's three major regions and the government leader of Southern Cameroons expressed "disappointment" in their statements regarding this matter and stated that they reserved the right to demand complete independence before April 2, 1960.

Without a resolution of the fundamental issue, the conference did reach an agreement on granting autonomy to the Western and Eastern Regions of Nigeria currently, and granting autonomy to the Northern Region in 1959.

The proceedings of this conference will be published as a White Paper within ten days, and further meetings will be held later.

### 英国不让尼日利亚后年独立  尼当局表示保留要求完全独立的权利

1957-06-28
第6版()
专栏：

　　英国不让尼日利亚后年独立
    尼当局表示保留要求完全独立的权利
    新华社伦敦27日电  尼日利亚人民提出的在1959年最后独立的要求，经26日在这里结束的尼日利亚独立步骤问题会议讨论了三十三天以后，被英国殖民地部拒绝了。
    在拒绝承认尼日利亚人民这种独立权利的同时，殖民地大臣伦诺克斯—波伊德在会议上说，英国政府将在1960年同情地考虑尼日利亚在英联邦内的独立。
    尼日利亚的三个主要地区的总理和南喀麦隆的政府领袖在他们所发表的声明中，对这件事表示“失望”，并且表示保留要求在1960年4月2日以前完全独立的权利。
    在根本问题没有解决的情况下，会议在目前给予尼日利亚西部和东部两地方以自治权以及在1959年给予北部地方以自治权的问题上取得了协议。
    这次会议的情况将在十天内作为白皮书发表，以后会议还要举行。
