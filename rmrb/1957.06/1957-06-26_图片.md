Title: Picture

Author:

Date: 1957-06-26
Page: 6

The workers of the Omi Silk Textile Company of Japan, who had persisted in a 106-day strike in 1954 and won the abolition of feudal shackles and wage increases, held a scheduled strike again on June 10 to oppose the return of Mr. Kakuji Natsukawa, the former head of the company, and demanded the issuance of summer allowances. The picture shows the workshop meeting at the Omi Silk Textile Company's Fujinomiya factory on the day of the strike.
