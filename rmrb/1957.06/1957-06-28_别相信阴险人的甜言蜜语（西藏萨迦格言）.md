Title: Don't Trust the Sweet Words of Insidious People (Sakya Proverbs of Tibet)
Author:
Date: 1957-06-28
Page: 8

　　Don't Trust the Sweet Words of Insidious People
　　(Sakya Proverbs of Tibet)
　　Insidious people will pretend to be sweet-talking and of upright conduct,
　　Don't trust them until you understand them;
　　The peacock has a beautiful figure and a clear voice,
　　Who knows that what it eats is poisonous snakes.
　　When evil people are frustrated,
　　Their minds may still be gentle;
　　When poisonous thorns are not yet ripe,
　　They will not prick people!
　　Cunning people say nice things,
　　It is for their own sake, not to respect you;
　　The owl makes a pleasant laugh,
　　It is to spread bad omens, not to like you.
　　          (Selected and translated by Wang Yao)

### 别相信阴险人的甜言蜜语（西藏萨迦格言）

1957-06-28
第8版()
专栏：

　　别相信阴险人的甜言蜜语
　　（西藏萨迦格言）
　　阴险的人会装成甜言蜜语行止端庄，
　　在没了解他以前，可别相信；
　　孔雀的身材美丽、歌喉嘹亮，
　　谁知他吃的却是一条条毒蛇。
　　恶人在不得志的时候，
　　他的心术还可能是温顺的；
　　有毒的荆棘在没成熟的时候，
　　他还不会去刺人哩！
　　狡猾的人说了些好听的话，
　　是为他自己打算，不是恭敬你；
　　猫头鹰发出悦耳的笑声，
　　是为了散布不祥，不是喜欢你。
　　          （王尧选译）
