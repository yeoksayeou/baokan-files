Title: Two Poems on Harbin
Author: Yuan Shuipai
Date: 1957-06-27
Page: 7

　　Two Poems on Harbin
　　Yuan Shuipai
　　Sun Island
　　Sun Island—what a peculiar name,
　　Perhaps from Mayakovsky's poem,
　　Did the sun ever visit here?
　　Or, does the sun live here?
　　Light yellow sands border the tender green island,
　　Workers' sanatoriums connect one after another in the thick shade,
　　The yachts turning over in the courtyard are being freshly painted,
　　The ones that are painted are already in the water, red bottoms and powder blue sides.
　　Ice and snow are gone, the Northern Country has passed the severe winter,
　　The broad Songhua River has gently rippling waves,
　　Sun Island, you quietly await the smiles of the holidays,
　　But the hearts of the people on the opposite bank have already flown into your embrace.
　　                        June 9th

　　Young Female Worker
　　The automatic lathe has a hundred hands,
　　Meticulously polishing a piece of stubborn iron,
　　The clumsy machine has become truly clever,
　　Just like its owner, eighteen or nineteen years old.
　　Brushing back her hair, she answers the visitor:
　　It was the beloved Party that let me learn about machines,
　　Before graduation, I thought about becoming a primary school teacher,
　　But, I love the line I'm in!
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　June 10th

专栏：

　　哈尔滨二首
　　袁水拍
　　太阳岛
　　太阳岛——多么奇特的名字，
　　也许出自马雅柯夫斯基的诗，
　　太阳曾到这儿作客？
　　或者，太阳就在这儿住。
　　浅黄的沙滩给嫩绿的岛镶边，
　　浓荫下工人休养所挨个儿相连，
　　院子里翻转的游艇正在新漆，
　　漆好的已下水，红舱底粉蓝舷。
　　冰雪无踪，北国过了严冬，
　　宽阔的松花江微波溶溶，
　　太阳岛，你静候着假日的笑容，
　　可是对岸人们的心早已飞到你怀中。
　　                        6月9日
　　青年女工
　　自动车床有一百只手，
　　把一块顽铁的溜溜精磨细镂，
　　笨重的机器变得真聪明，
　　就像它的主人，年纪十八九。
　　掠一下额发，答复来访：
　　让我学机器的是亲爱的党，
　　毕业前曾想当小学教员，
　　但是，做一行爱一行！
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　6月10日
