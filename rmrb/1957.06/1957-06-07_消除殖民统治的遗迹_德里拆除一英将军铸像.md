Title: Eliminating Vestiges of Colonial Rule: Statue of British General Removed in Delhi
Author:
Date: 1957-06-07
Page: 6

    Eliminating Vestiges of Colonial Rule
    Statue of British General Removed in Delhi

    Xinhua News Agency, New Delhi, June 6th. On June 5th, Delhi police removed the statue of British General Taylor from Mori Gate in Old Delhi. Taylor was a British general who suppressed the Indian anti-British uprising of 1857.

    This event is seen as the prelude to the removal of other statues of British generals in Delhi.

    Following the recent celebrations in India commemorating the centenary of the anti-British uprising, several cities in Uttar Pradesh have also strongly demanded the removal of these British statues symbolizing colonial rule. The state government has assured that it will take immediate measures to remove statues of British figures in public places.

### 消除殖民统治的遗迹  德里拆除一英将军铸像

1957-06-07
第6版()
专栏：

    消除殖民统治的遗迹
    德里拆除一英将军铸像
    新华社新德里6日电　德里的警察6月5日把英国将军泰勒的铸像从旧德里城莫里门拆除了。泰勒是镇压1857年印度反英起义的一个英国将军。
    这件事被认为是拆除在德里的其他英国将军铸像的先声。
    在印度最近举行了纪念反英起义一百周年的活动以后，北方邦的好几个城市也都强烈地要求拆除这些象征殖民统治的英国铸像。邦政府已经提出保证，将立即采取措施拆除公共场所的英国人的铸像。
