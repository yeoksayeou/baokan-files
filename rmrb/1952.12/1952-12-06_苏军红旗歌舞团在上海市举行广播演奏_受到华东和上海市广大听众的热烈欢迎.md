Title: Soviet Army Red Flag Song and Dance Ensemble Holds Radio Performance in Shanghai, Receives Warm Welcome from Broad Audience in East China and Shanghai
Author: 
Date: 1952-12-06
Page: 1
Xinhua News Agency, Shanghai, December 5th. The Soviet Army Red Flag Song and Dance Ensemble participated in a broadcast rally organized by the China-Soviet Friendship Association of East China and Shanghai, as well as the People's Broadcasting Station, etc., at 6:30 PM on the 4th. They performed for the people of East China and Shanghai via radio broadcast, receiving a warm welcome from the millions of listeners in East China and Shanghai.

Shanghai listeners from all walks of life gathered at listening stations set up throughout the city before 6:00 PM. Many neighborhoods and shops installed amplifiers or placed radios in appropriate locations so that every citizen could share the joy and happiness brought by their Soviet friends. Before the radio performance, a continuous stream of people, carrying beautiful bouquets and precious gifts and letters, lined up tightly around the People's Broadcasting Station building, waiting to present them to the honored guests. When B.A. Alexandrov and the members of the Soviet Army Red Flag Song and Dance Ensemble arrived at the People's Broadcasting Station, the crowd waved flowers and colorful ribbons, forming a sea of ​​flowers, and shouts of "Mao Zedong - Stalin" and applause rose one after another. A grand performance ceremony was held before the Soviet Army Red Flag Song and Dance Ensemble's broadcast performance.

At 6:30 PM, the broadcast performance rally began with the music of "Moscow-Beijing." Xia Yan, Vice President of the Shanghai Sino-Soviet Friendship Association, delivered a welcome speech, followed by a speech by B.A. Alexandrov, the head of the Soviet Army Red Flag Song and Dance Ensemble. At this time, from the broadcast performance rally venue in the broadcasting hall of the radio station to every corner of Shanghai and East China, there was prolonged and enthusiastic applause and cheers. The Soviet Army Red Flag Song and Dance Ensemble successively performed nineteen wonderful programs, including the chorus "Glory to the Great Stalin" and "The East is Red." When the scheduled end time of the concert arrived, all the lines in the three telephone exchanges of the People's Broadcasting Station were occupied by calls from listeners requesting additional performances. The listeners sincerely pleaded with their Soviet friends to extend the performance time. Many listeners repeatedly requested encores of songs such as "The East is Red," "People of the World are of One Mind," "Song of the Communist Youth League," and "Along the Peter Street." At the enthusiastic request of the audience, the artists of the Soviet Army Red Flag Song and Dance Ensemble extended the performance by another twenty minutes. During the broadcast, the rally received tens of thousands of letters and calls filled with gratitude. According to statistics, within three hours, more than 10,000 letters representing more than three million listeners from all walks of life in the city, more than 2,000 phone calls, thousands of bouquets of flowers, and hundreds of gifts were received. In addition, 42 long-distance calls and telegrams were sent from various parts of East China to pay tribute to the Soviet Army Red Flag Song and Dance Ensemble. During the extended performance, the enthusiasm of the audience was even higher, and there were even more letters and calls paying tribute to the Soviet friends and reporting on the production work during the "Sino-Soviet Friendship Month." The Soviet Army Red Flag Song and Dance Ensemble sent members to the telephone to directly talk with the listeners through translation. After the entire staff of the East China Health Hospital and more than 300 sick and wounded people listening on the hospital beds received the Soviet friends' telephone wishes for a speedy recovery, they immediately called the Soviet friends again to express their determination to take good care of themselves so that they could better serve the people and strive for Sino-Soviet friendship and cooperation and the cause of lasting world peace. Many factory workers also sent many gifts to the Soviet friends. The flowers sent by the masses filled a whole truck. During the radio performance, the Soviet Army Red Flag Song and Dance Ensemble also sent members in ten groups to more than thirty listening stations in factories, schools, troops, government offices, suburban market towns, and other places in Shanghai to meet with more than 50,000 listeners, receiving an even warmer welcome from the masses. Every place they went, they were greeted with enthusiastic cheers, applause, and flowers.

### 苏军红旗歌舞团在上海市举行广播演奏  受到华东和上海市广大听众的热烈欢迎

1952-12-06
第1版()
专栏：

    苏军红旗歌舞团在上海市举行广播演奏
    受到华东和上海市广大听众的热烈欢迎
    【新华社上海五日电】苏军红旗歌舞团四日晚六时半参加了华东和上海市的中苏友好协会及人民广播电台等单位主办的广播大会，向华东和上海人民进行广播演奏，受到了华东和上海收听广播的千千万万人民的热烈欢迎。
    上海市的各界听众，在下午六时前就汇集到设在全市各处的收听站。许多里弄、店铺都装置了扩大器，或将收音机安放在适当的地方，好让每个市民都能分享到苏联友人带来的欢乐和幸福。在广播演奏之前，络绎不绝的群众，携带着美丽的花束和珍贵的礼品、信件，水泄不通地挨次排列在人民广播电台大楼的周围，等候着向贵宾献赠。当鲍·亚·亚历山大罗夫和苏军红旗歌舞团的团员们到达人民广播电台时，人群挥舞起鲜花和彩绢，汇成了一片花团锦簇的海洋，“毛泽东—斯大林”的欢呼和鼓掌声，此伏彼起。在苏军红旗歌舞团进行广播演奏之前，并举行了隆重的演出仪式。
    六时半，广播演奏大会在“莫斯科—北京”的乐声中开始。上海市中苏友好协会副会长夏衍致欢迎词后，苏军红旗歌舞团团长鲍·亚·亚历山大罗夫接着讲话。这时，从电台播音大厅的广播演奏大会会场直到全上海、全华东每一个角落，都响起了持久热烈的鼓掌欢呼声。苏军红旗歌舞团先后演唱了合唱“光荣属于伟大的斯大林”、“东方红”等十九个精彩节目。到了演奏会预定结束的时间，人民广播电台的三间电话交换室的线路全部为听众打来的要求增加演奏的电话所占满，听众们纷纷诚挚地恳请苏联友人延续演奏时间，许多听众一再要求重唱“东方红”、“全世界人民心一条”、“青年团员之歌”和“沿着彼得大街”等歌曲。苏军红旗歌舞团的艺术家们应听众的热烈要求，又延长了二十分钟。广播进行期间，大会收到成千成万洋溢着感激心情的信件和电话，据统计：在三小时内共收到代表本市各界三百多万听众的一万多封信件、二千多次电话、数千束鲜花和数百件礼品；此外，华东各地并发来长途电话与电报四十二次，向苏军红旗歌舞团致敬。在延长演奏时，听众的热情更高，向苏联友人致敬和报告“中苏友好月”中生产工作情况的信件和电话也更多了。苏军红旗歌舞团派了团员在电话机旁经过翻译直接与听众交谈，华东保健医院全体职工和在病床上收听的伤病员三百余人，得到苏联友人在电话中预祝伤病员早日恢复健康的回话后，马上又打电话给苏联友人，表示要好好养病，以便更好地为人民服务，为中苏友好合作及世界持久和平事业而奋斗。许多工厂的工人并送来了许多礼品，献给苏联友人。群众送来的鲜花载了满满一卡车。在广播演奏时，苏军红旗歌舞团还派出团员分十路到上海市的工厂、学校、部队、机关、郊区集镇等三十余处收听站和五万多听众见面，更受到了群众的热烈欢迎，每到一处都获得群众的热烈欢呼、鼓掌和献花。
