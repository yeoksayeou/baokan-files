Title: China Red Cross replies to Japanese Red Cross Society and other three groups, agreeing to send a delegation to Beijing to discuss the issue of Japanese nationals returning to their country

Author:
Date: 1952-12-23
Page: 1

Xinhua News Agency, December 22nd. Following the publication of the relevant departments of the Central People's Government's reply to the Xinhua News Agency reporter on various issues concerning Japanese nationals in China on December 1st, the Chinese Red Cross Society has successively received telegrams from the Japanese Red Cross Society, the Japanese Peace Liaison Committee, and the Japan-China Friendship Association, proposing to send representatives to China to negotiate specific issues related to the repatriation of Japanese nationals. On December 22nd, the Chinese Red Cross Society replied to the three Japanese groups separately, inviting them to consult with each other and jointly form a delegation to come to Beijing to discuss with the Chinese Red Cross Society the procedures for incoming ships and specific issues related to the repatriation of Japanese nationals. The delegation may consist of five representatives and two staff members. The Chinese Red Cross Society also requested them to promptly submit a list of representatives and staff members to the Chinese Red Cross Society in order to determine the date of the delegation's arrival in Beijing.

### 中国红十字会电复日本红十字会等三团体  同意他们派代表团来京商讨日侨归国问题

1952-12-23
第1版()
专栏：

    中国红十字会电复日本红十字会等三团体
    同意他们派代表团来京商讨日侨归国问题
    【新华社二十二日讯】十二月一日本社发表了我中央人民政府有关方面答复本社记者关于在中国的日本侨民的各项问题后，中国红十字会曾先后收到日本红十字会、日本和平联络委员会及日中友好协会来电，提出愿派代表前来中国协商解决日侨归国各项具体问题。中国红十字会已于二十二日分别复电该三个日本团体，请它们进行协商，共同组成代表团来北京与中国红十字会商讨来船手续以及日侨归国的各项具体问题，代表团的组成可包括代表五人，工作人员二人。中国红十字会并请它们迅速提出代表及工作人员名单，通知中国红十字会，以便商定代表团前来北京的日期。
