Title: Three British Soldiers Stationed in West Berlin Request Asylum in the German Democratic Republic
Author:
Date: 1952-12-08
Page: 4

    Three British Soldiers Stationed in West Berlin
    Request Asylum in the German Democratic Republic
    According to a Deutsche Nachrichten-Agentur report: Three British soldiers, John Smith, Francis Remf, and David James, wrote to the government of the German Democratic Republic, requesting that they be granted political asylum within the Republic. The letter stated: "To the esteemed Government of the German Democratic Republic: We are soldiers of the 1st Battalion of the Welsh Guards, stationed in West Berlin with the British Rhine Army, and have now decided to request that the Government of the German Democratic Republic permit us to live and work in the German Democratic Republic. Although we are all patriots of Great Britain and our people, we are willing to remain within the German Democratic Republic. The reason is that Great Britain has become an aircraft carrier for American capitalists and a provoker of another war. But we soldiers, like all the British people, do not need war. We only need peace. John Smith, Francis Remf, David James (signed)."

### 驻柏林西区英国士兵三名  要求在民主德国避难

1952-12-08
第4版()
专栏：

    驻柏林西区英国士兵三名
    要求在民主德国避难
    据德意志通讯社报道：英国士兵约翰·史密斯、法兰西斯·伦姆夫和大卫·詹姆士三人写信给德意志民主共和国政府，请求给予他们在共和国境内的政治避难权。信中写道：“德意志民主共和国政府公鉴：我们是随英国莱因军在柏林西区值勤的威尔斯近卫军第一营的士兵，现已决定请求德意志民主共和国政府准许我们在德意志民主共和国居住与工作。我们虽然都是大不列颠和我国人民的爱国者，但我们愿意留在德意志民主共和国境内。理由是大不列颠已经变成美国资本家的航空母舰和另一次战争的挑拨者。但我们士兵却和全体英国人民一样，都不需要战争。我们只需要和平。约翰·史密斯、法兰西斯·伦姆夫、大卫·詹姆士（签字）。”
