Title: Foreign Ministers of Sweden, Norway, Denmark, and Iceland Issue Communiqué, Unanimously Advocating for the Restoration of China's Seat in the United Nations
Author:
Date: 1954-09-06
Page: 4
    
    Xinhua News Agency, Reykjavik, 2nd: A communiqué issued on August 31st by the foreign ministers of Sweden, Norway, Denmark, and Iceland, following their meeting in Reykjavik, the capital of Iceland, unanimously advocated that China's legitimate seat in the United Nations should be restored in the near future.
    The communiqué also stated that the main purpose of the meeting was to discuss the policies the four countries would adopt at the upcoming Ninth Session of the United Nations General Assembly.
    Xinhua News Agency, Jakarta, 5th: Manuaba, an advisor to the Indonesian delegation to the United Nations General Assembly, told reporters on the 4th that Indonesia would support the People's Republic of China's entry into the United Nations in accordance with Indonesia's independent foreign policy. He said that without a doubt, the People's Republic of China, a major Asian country with such a large population, could not be ignored. Indonesia believes that the People's Republic of China's entry into the United Nations will make a tremendous contribution to world peace, especially to peace in Asia.
    Manuaba said that the Indonesian delegation will work to help achieve world peace and the national independence of colonial and semi-colonial countries. Indonesia will also propose that the West Irian issue be included on the agenda. He believes that this request from Indonesia will receive the support of United Nations member states, especially Asian and African countries.
    Xinhua News Agency, 31st: Rotnok dispatch: At a meeting of the Liberal International Committee in Rotnok, the leader of the Norwegian Liberal Party, Christian Oftedahl, spoke in favor of China's participation in the United Nations. He said, "The foundation of a world organization must be universal membership." He said that the People's Republic of China must have a place in the United Nations.

### 瑞典、挪威、丹麦、冰岛四国外长会议发表公报  一致主张恢复我国在联合国的席位

1954-09-06
第4版()
专栏：

    瑞典、挪威、丹麦、冰岛四国外长会议发表公报
    一致主张恢复我国在联合国的席位
    【新华社二日讯】雷克雅未克消息：在冰岛首都雷克雅未克举行的瑞典、挪威、丹麦、冰岛四国外长会议于八月三十一日发表公报，一致主张应在不久的将来恢复中国在联合国的合法席位。
    公报又说，这次会议的主要目的是讨论四国在即将召开的联合国第九届大会上所要采取的政策。
    【新华社雅加达五日电】印度尼西亚出席联合国大会代表团的顾问马努巴四日告诉新闻记者说，印度尼西亚将依照印度尼西亚的独立外交政策，支持中华人民共和国进入联合国。他说，毫无疑问，对于中华人民共和国这个拥有那么多人口的亚洲大国是不能加以忽视的。印度尼西亚相信，中华人民共和国进入联合国对世界和平，特别是对亚洲的和平，将会有巨大的贡献。
    马努巴说，印度尼西亚代表团将致力于帮助实现世界和平以及殖民地和半殖民地国家的民族独立。印度尼西亚并将提出把西伊里安问题列入议程。他相信印度尼西亚这一要求将会得到联合国会员国，特别是亚洲和非洲国家的支持。
    【新华社三十一日讯】若特诺克消息：挪威自由主义领袖克里斯蒂安·奥夫台戴尔在若特诺克举行的自由国际委员会会议上发言主张中国参加联合国。他说：“一个世界性组织的基础必须是普遍性的成员制。”他说，联合国中必须有中华人民共和国的地位。
