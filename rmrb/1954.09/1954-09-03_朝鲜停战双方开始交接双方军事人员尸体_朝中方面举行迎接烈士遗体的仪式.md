Title: Korean Armistice: Both Sides Begin to Exchange the Remains of Military Personnel; Chinese and Korean Sides Hold Ceremony to Welcome Remains of Martyrs
Author:
Date: 1954-09-03
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, Kaesong, September 1st: The exchange of military personnel remains by both sides of the Korean Armistice began on September 1st.

On the 1st, the Korean and Chinese side handed over 200 sets of remains of American military personnel from our area, including 193 Americans and 7 whose nationalities could not be identified. At 10:00 AM, the Korean and Chinese side transported the remains of the American military personnel to the American receiving point according to the agreement. Each set of remains of American military personnel was sealed in a special cloth bag made of rainproof cloth. Included with the remains were dog tags, military IDs, and other identifiable items that could be collected, as well as the deceased's belongings.

The American side handed over 600 sets of remains of our military personnel from the American side's area, sealed in paper bags, at 1:00 PM, including 500 Koreans and 100 Chinese.

Xinhua News Agency, Kaesong, September 1st: The Korean People's Army and the Chinese People's Volunteers held a welcoming ceremony in Dongchang-ri on September 1st to welcome the remains of the martyrs of the Korean People's Army and the Chinese People's Volunteers handed over by the American side. At 1:00 PM, members of the Korean and Chinese side of the Military Armistice Commission, members of the Korean and Chinese side of the Cemetery Registration Committee, representatives of the Korean People's Army, and representatives of the Chinese People's Volunteers stood solemnly in the receiving area of Dongchang-ri to welcome the remains of the martyrs.

The welcoming ceremony was held at 3:00 PM in front of a golden archway engraved with "Eternal Glory to the Heroes" in Korean and Chinese. Amidst mournful music, the entire audience paid their respects to the remains of the martyrs, followed by a moment of silence. General Li Sang-cho, Chief Member of the Korean and Chinese side of the Military Armistice Commission, and General Ding Guoyu, Member of the Commission, presented wreaths successively. Representatives of the Korean People's Army and representatives of the Chinese People's Volunteers also presented wreaths respectively.

The remains of the martyrs of the Korean People's Army and the martyrs of the Chinese People's Volunteers will be buried separately in the Korean People's Army Martyrs' Cemetery and the Chinese People's Volunteers Martyrs' Cemetery in the suburbs of Kaesong. Construction of both cemeteries has already begun. After the cemeteries are completed, permanent memorial towers for the martyrs will also be built.

### 朝鲜停战双方开始交接双方军事人员尸体  朝中方面举行迎接烈士遗体的仪式

1954-09-03
第4版()
专栏：

    朝鲜停战双方开始交接双方军事人员尸体
    朝中方面举行迎接烈士遗体的仪式
     【新华社开城一日电】朝鲜停战双方交接军事人员尸体的工作已在九月一日开始。
    朝中方面在一日送交了在我方地区的美方军事人员尸体二百具，其中一百九十三具是美国人，无法识别国籍的七具。上午十时，朝中方面即按照协议将美方军事人员尸体运到美方接收地点。每一具美方军事人员尸体均用防雨布特制的布袋封装。随同送交的有军号牌、军人证及其他可能搜集到的识别物，以及死者的各项遗物。
    美方在下午一时交来以纸袋封装的在美方地区的我方军事人员尸体六百具，其中五百具是朝鲜人，一百具是中国人。
   【新华社开城一日电】朝鲜人民军与中国人民志愿军于九月一日在东场里举行迎灵式，迎接美方交来的朝鲜人民军与中国人民志愿军烈士遗体。下午一时，军事停战委员会朝、中方面委员、墓地注册委员会朝、中方面委员、朝鲜人民军代表和中国人民志愿军代表肃立在东场里接收地区，迎接烈士遗体。
    迎灵式于下午三时在镌有朝、中文的“英灵千古”的金字牌坊前举行。在哀乐声中，全场向烈士遗体致敬，接着肃立默哀。军事停战委员会朝中方面首席委员李相朝将军、委员丁国钰将军相继敬献花圈，朝鲜人民军代表和中国人民志愿军代表也分别敬献花圈。
    朝鲜人民军烈士和中国人民志愿军烈士的遗体，将分别安葬在开城近郊的朝鲜人民军烈士陵园和中国人民志愿军烈士陵园。这两个陵园都已兴工修建。在陵园落成后，并将修建永久性的烈士纪念塔。
