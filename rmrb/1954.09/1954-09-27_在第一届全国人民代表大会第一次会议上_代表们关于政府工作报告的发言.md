Xinhua News Agency, Beijing, September 27th.

**Speech by He Xiangning:**

Chairman, fellow representatives:

I fully agree with and support Premier Zhou Enlai's government work report.

The first Constitution of the People's Republic of China has been solemnly adopted. This Constitution, of great historical significance, reflects the common will and aspirations of the Chinese people, and also reflects the long-standing wishes of overseas Chinese. It embodies the deep concern and care of the motherland for overseas Chinese. It will undoubtedly inspire the Chinese people to redouble their efforts to build a prosperous and happy socialist society, and it will also promote closer unity among overseas Chinese around the great motherland.

The 12 million overseas Chinese love their motherland. Over the past 50 years, in the Chinese people's revolution to overthrow the feudal imperial system, in the First and Second Chinese Civil Wars, the War of Resistance Against Japan, and the Chinese People's Liberation War to thoroughly overthrow imperialism, feudalism, and bureaucratic capitalism, many patriotic overseas Chinese participated. The bond between overseas Chinese and the motherland is unbreakable, as it was in the past and will be in the future.

Overseas Chinese have made significant contributions to the economic development and prosperity of their host countries over the years. They do not wish to participate in local political conflicts and do not want to interfere in local politics. What they ask for is to live in harmony with the local people and hope that the governments of their host countries will establish friendly relations with their motherland.

However, the situation of overseas Chinese is very complex. On one hand, the motherland is becoming stronger, and the international status and relations of the motherland are improving and developing. The imperialist war bloc is becoming increasingly isolated, and the international situation is beginning to ease, all of which are beneficial to the improvement of the situation of overseas Chinese. However, the imperialist war bloc, which is hostile to the People's Republic of China, is even more hostile to the Chinese. Therefore, the difficulties faced by overseas Chinese will increase. On the other hand, the motherland is steadily transitioning to a socialist society, but the vast majority of overseas Chinese live in completely different environments. Furthermore, overseas Chinese work abroad, and most of their production resources and property are abroad. However, most of their relatives are in China, and the living expenses of their relatives in China largely depend on the earnings of overseas relatives. This is a complex situation and is a long-term existence. Therefore, in the past five years, the state has considered the special situation of overseas Chinese in protecting the legitimate rights and interests of overseas Chinese, in settling and guiding returned overseas Chinese, and in the policy towards the relatives of overseas Chinese in China. In other words, when the government implements and carries out the general policies and laws of the state, it must consider and formulate various policies related to overseas Chinese as an inseparable part of the state's overall policy.

In recent years, the government's overseas Chinese policy has mainly assisted and guided overseas Chinese in mutual aid and mutual assistance, established cultural and welfare undertakings, promoted friendship and unity among overseas Chinese, and fostered patriotism. At the same time, efforts have been made to promote friendly relations between overseas Chinese and the people of the host countries. In China, appropriate arrangements and guidance have been provided for returned overseas Chinese and difficult overseas Chinese, facilitated remittances, established supplementary schools for returned overseas Chinese students, guided returned overseas Chinese to participate in industrial and agricultural production, and guided the relatives of overseas Chinese in China to participate in various labor and production on a voluntary basis. Assistance has also been provided to relevant departments in formulating regulations on the entry and exit of overseas Chinese to facilitate their travel. At the same time, in important social reform movements, consideration has been given to the actual situation of overseas Chinese and their relatives in China to provide appropriate care. For example, the Central People's Government's State Council formulated the "Regulations on the Handling of Overseas Chinese Land and Property in Land Reform" in 1950; and in the propaganda and education movement for the implementation of the Marriage Law, the Central People's Government's State Council issued the "Regulations on the Handling of Disputes over Marriage Issues of Overseas Chinese."

Fellow representatives, from the above situation, it is not difficult to see that the scope of overseas Chinese affairs is very wide, and many works are partly or entirely related to the business of other government departments. In the past, various levels of overseas Chinese affairs organizations have implemented policies related to overseas Chinese in cooperation and assistance from various government departments to achieve results. Therefore, if there have been some achievements in overseas Chinese work, it is absolutely not just the achievement of the overseas Chinese affairs department, but the result of the joint efforts of various government departments.

However, in the past, we have had shortcomings in overseas Chinese work. Some of our specific policies for overseas Chinese were not formulated in a timely manner, or there were policies but these policies did not meet the broad masses, or they met too late. In the land reform movement, some areas in South China had a tendency to ignore overseas Chinese issues, and our steps to correct these biases were too slow. As a result, some overseas Chinese and their relatives who should have been taken care of did not receive the care they deserved. These biases were basically corrected in the land reform re-examination in 1953. For example, the protection of remittances, the Central Committee for Overseas Chinese Affairs and the People's Bank of China had already formulated specific policies, but until recently, it was found that these policies had not been publicly explained to township and village cadres and relevant groups, and the policies had not been fully disseminated and had not been well communicated to the masses. If there are still individual places where there are phenomena of violating remittance policies, then the main reason is that the policies have not been disseminated and explained, and therefore have not been implemented.

On the other hand, it must be pointed out that some local cadres, and even leaders, have not been willing to face the special nature of overseas Chinese issues, fearing trouble and seeking convenience, and have not been willing to distinguish between complex special issues and general issues, study them, and handle them. This shortcoming and error should also be corrected in future work.

The upcoming National People's Congress will soon produce new state organs. I believe that the new overseas Chinese affairs organizations at various levels established according to the Constitution and the organizational laws of local people's congresses and local people's committees at various levels should strive to promote the unity of overseas Chinese, carry forward the traditional patriotism of overseas Chinese; for returned overseas Chinese in China, appropriate arrangements should be made as much as possible, and they should be guided and organized to accept socialist education and reform, and appropriate and necessary care should be given according to their different situations. Overseas Chinese affairs organizations must actively serve overseas Chinese, returned overseas Chinese, and relatives of overseas Chinese in China, overcome bureaucratism and indifference to the vital interests of overseas Chinese.

Currently, the major issues that need to be addressed in overseas Chinese work include the issue of overseas Chinese nationality, which Premier Zhou has already mentioned, and I will not repeat it here. I only add one sentence to confirm the principle pointed out by Premier Zhou in the government work report regarding the issue of overseas Chinese nationality, that is, overseas Chinese are also very concerned about the proper resolution of the issue of overseas Chinese nationality. Next, regarding the issue of remittances, it is suggested that the government take effective measures to order relevant regions and departments to ensure that the remittance policy is implemented in earnest. Third, in addition to continuing to guide overseas Chinese to return to China to participate in industrial production, it is welcome for overseas Chinese to develop mountains, wastelands, and forests in various places in China, plant agricultural gardens, establish ranches, and build a large number of houses and cultural and educational undertakings. Doing so is beneficial to overseas Chinese and helps the socialist construction of the state.

After the Kuomintang bandits fled the mainland, they, under the protection of American imperialism, have been stationed in Taiwan Province and have been carrying out sinister activities to harm overseas Chinese, deceiving overseas Chinese of their money, and forcing overseas Chinese to be cannon fodder. They are doing all kinds of evil. Overseas Chinese, in order to reduce the harm they suffer from the Kuomintang bandits, all hope that the motherland will liberate Taiwan as soon as possible and eliminate the Kuomintang traitorous clique.

The situation of overseas Chinese abroad is difficult. In places where the Kuomintang bandits openly carry out activities to harm overseas Chinese, the situation of overseas Chinese is even more difficult. But the hearts of overseas Chinese are still patriotic. The people and government of the motherland are deeply concerned about the situation of overseas Chinese in these places and understand their difficulties. The Kuomintang bandits who fled to overseas countries are driven by American imperialism and the Kuomintang bandits to do evil and have done many bad things. We must advise them to immediately stop all activities to harm overseas Chinese. Do not follow the enemy of the Chinese people, Chiang Kai-shek, any longer. Today, under the great call of the people of the motherland, as long as they can repent and turn to good, stop their evil deeds in overseas Chinese, the people of the motherland and the broad overseas Chinese will allow them to turn to the light and return to their homeland to reunite with their families.

Fellow representatives can see from here that our overseas Chinese work is closely related to the sacred cause of the Chinese people to liberate Taiwan and eliminate the Kuomintang traitorous clique; and also see that the domestic overseas Chinese work is closely related to the overseas Chinese work. Domestic overseas Chinese work is closely related to the patriotic unity of overseas Chinese. Every measure of domestic overseas Chinese work will directly affect overseas Chinese work; either promoting the patriotic unity of overseas Chinese, blocking the loopholes for the Kuomintang traitorous clique to carry out activities to disrupt the unity of overseas Chinese; or it will affect the unity of overseas Chinese, giving the Kuomintang traitorous clique an opportunity to disrupt the unity of overseas Chinese.

All this shows the importance of the task of state overseas Chinese work, and we overseas Chinese workers and relevant departments must attach sufficient importance to overseas Chinese work, work even more diligently, and redouble our efforts to do a good job in domestic and overseas overseas Chinese work.

Finally, I hope everyone will pay more attention to overseas Chinese work and give guidance.

In the future, we will more closely unite around the Communist Party of China, the Central People's Government, and Chairman Mao, be humble and prudent, and follow the direction indicated by the Constitution of the People's Republic of China to strive for the continued consolidation of the unity of our great motherland, the strengthening of national unity, the vigorous support for the liberation of Taiwan, and the building of our country into a prosperous and happy socialist society.

**Speech by Zhang Zhirou:**

Chairman, fellow representatives:

I fully agree with Premier Zhou Enlai's government work report. I would like to add a few words about general education as a supplement.

Under the correct leadership of the Communist Party of China and the Central People's Government, with the support of the people of the whole country and the efforts of educational workers of the whole country, China's general education has achieved unprecedented development over the past five years.

According to statistics from 1953, there were 51.5 million primary school students nationwide, 2.959 million ordinary middle school and agricultural and industrial accelerated middle school students, 36.9 million normal school students, and 3.9 million higher normal school students. These figures have far exceeded the highest figures in the history of the old China.

Minority education has also developed significantly. According to statistics from 1953, the number of primary schools for national minorities nationwide has developed to 26,436, with 2,546,600 students; 275 middle schools, with 163,300 students. In various national minority schools, the language of the respective nationality is used for teaching. Some national minorities do not yet have a written language, and the people's government is helping them to create one.

All levels of schools have implemented the policy of education serving workers and peasants, and all levels of schools have tried to provide convenient conditions for the children of workers and peasants to enter school. Now, the children of workers and peasants have become the majority or a large part of the students in primary schools, middle schools, and normal schools. To quickly cultivate talents of workers and peasant origin, we have also established agricultural and industrial accelerated middle schools, and now a large number of cadres and industrial workers of workers and peasant origin have entered these schools to study.

Over the past five years, we have carried out a series of reforms in the education system, education content, and teaching methods, and carried out revolutionary political and ideological education among students at all levels of schools, and carried out ideological reform among teachers. These have achieved great results, and the quality of education has been continuously improving.

However, our shortcomings are still many, mainly the low quality of education. First of all, political and ideological education at all levels of schools is still very weak, and the phenomenon of neglecting labor education is particularly serious. Many middle school and primary school students, after graduation, only want to continue their studies and do not want to engage in labor production. Some schools pay insufficient attention to discipline education, resulting in the phenomenon of lax discipline among students. Secondly, the cultural and scientific knowledge level of students is still not high, and many senior high school and junior high school graduates who took the college entrance examination and middle school entrance examination in recent years still have many who failed. In addition, the health condition of teachers and students is not good enough. Although there have been improvements in this regard over the past few years, the poor health of teachers and students is still a relatively serious problem.

Of course, these shortcomings have certain historical roots. The educational foundation left by the old China is very weak, and the ideological influence left by the old society's education is also very deep; but the main reason is that our work is not done well. The Ministry of Education did not pay enough attention to political and ideological education, especially did not conduct a systematic and profound criticism of the ideology of despising manual labor and despising manual laborers, which is a serious mistake. In addition, we lack specific and planned leadership in teaching work, textbook compilation, and teacher learning. In a period of time, we also had the deviation of blindly rushing forward in education development. These are what the Ministry of Education should continue to deeply reflect on and work hard to correct.

In the future, general education must adapt to the needs of national construction and actively and steadily develop on the basis of production development. First of all, it is necessary to develop senior high schools and complete middle schools to meet the needs of higher education to cultivate talents for national construction; secondly, it is necessary to develop junior high schools and primary schools in a planned and focused manner, especially in large cities and industrial areas, to appropriately meet the cultural needs of the people. However, the key to the overall development of education is the teacher problem, so the development of normal education, especially higher normal education, is our most important task.

However, the country is currently concentrating its efforts on industrial construction and cannot allocate more financial, material, and human resources to develop middle schools and primary schools on a larger scale; therefore, in the next relatively long period of time, only a small part of middle school and primary school graduates will be able to continue their studies, and the majority will have to engage in industrial and agricultural production after graduation. This is absolutely necessary for the development of industry and agriculture in the current stage.

In the entire work of general education and normal education, we must pay special attention to improving the quality of education. This requires us to seriously implement the policy of comprehensive development. First of all, we must strengthen political and ideological education to educate young people and children with socialist ideology, so that they can have a socialist political direction, a dialectical materialist worldview, and communist moral qualities. Currently, we should focus on patriotic education and collective education, especially to correctly strengthen labor education and discipline education. We must make students establish a socialist attitude towards labor and labor, fully understand that "labor is the honorable thing for all labor-capable citizens of the People's Republic of China," and establish the idea of loving labor and loving the laboring people. We must also make students deeply understand that the Constitution, laws, and various disciplines of New China are extremely important weapons for the people's government to consolidate the people's democratic dictatorship, guarantee citizens' rights and safety, and ensure the development of production and various construction undertakings. Every patriotic citizen "must abide by the Constitution and laws, abide by labor discipline, abide by public order, and respect social public morality." Every student must abide by the rules and disciplines of the school, cultivate the habit of law-abiding. However, to implement the above-mentioned policies, in addition to school education, it is also necessary for student parents and social figures to work together with the school to educate the new generation with socialist ideology.

The primary task of improving teaching at present is to use socialist ideology and advanced scientific knowledge to formulate and revise the teaching outlines and textbooks of various levels of schools, correspondingly improve teaching methods, and improve the political level and professional level of in-service teachers. We must also effectively overcome the heavy workload of teachers and students and the current chaotic situation in school work.

In addition, we must take effective measures to further improve the health of teachers and students, continue to implement Chairman Mao's instructions of "good health, good study, good work" and the State Council's "Decision on Improving the Health Condition of Students at Various Levels of Schools."

Over the past few years, the hard work of educational workers across the country has achieved great results. In the future, with the continuous development of the national economy, the development of China's general education will be very magnificent, and our work tasks will be even more arduous. We believe that under the correct leadership of the Communist Party of China and Chairman Mao, with the support and assistance of the people of the whole country, all of us educational workers will continue to strengthen unity, overcome difficulties, and give full play to our enthusiasm and creativity to complete the glorious and great task of cultivating socialist new people entrusted to us by the motherland and the people.

**Speech by Zeng Xisheng:**

Chairman, fellow representatives:

I fully agree with and support Premier Zhou Enlai's government work report.

Over the past five years, there have been great achievements in all aspects and regions of the country, and Anhui is no exception. However, I do not intend to elaborate on the overall work, I only want to say a few words about the production and disaster relief work that the people of Anhui are most grateful for, that the people of the whole country are most concerned about, and that is still a relatively arduous task at present.

Anhui is located between the Yangtze River and the Huai River and is a province prone to disasters. According to historical records, from 246 BC to 1948, approximately two thousand years, there were 979 major and minor floods. According to investigations by the people over the past few years, in the past hundred years, there has been a small disaster every five years, a major disaster every ten years, and a continuous disaster every thirty years. During the period of Kuomintang rule, due to the neglect of water conservancy, the digging of the Yellow River into the Huai River, the situation of "small rain, small disaster, big rain, big disaster, no rain, drought disaster" became a serious phenomenon of annual disasters. Everyone still remembers: in 1949, the Yangtze and Huai rivers both flooded, five floods in five years, eight million people suffered from disasters; in 1950, the Huai River suffered a severe flood, nearly ten million people suffered from disasters; in 1951, it was the best year, but there were still two to three hundred thousand acres of waterlogging and drought disasters; in 1952, although there was no flood, waterlogging reached more than 15 million acres, and 5.7 million people suffered from disasters; in 1953, although waterlogging and drought were relatively light, an unexpected frost disaster suddenly struck; this year's flood is unprecedented in a hundred years, but the disaster situation in the Huai River area has been greatly reduced due to the effect of flood control, but the disaster situation in the Yangtze River area is very serious, many high-yield crop areas have been completely lost due to deep water or dyke breaches. This is the general situation of the serious disaster in Anhui.

Although this is the case, we have overcome this serious disaster. The reason we can overcome it is, first of all, because the Central People's Government and the people of the whole country have given us tremendous material support. From 1949 to 1953, the Central Government allocated a total of 150 billion yuan of relief grain and various materials to Anhui. Especially this year, the Central Government has supported us with greater strength, first by investing a large amount of materials in flood control and rescue, and then by providing a large amount of grain and seeds, tents, and other materials to relieve the disaster-stricken people. When transportation by car is not enough, it is followed by air transportation, so not only do the disaster-stricken people receive relief, but they are also helped to solve many production difficulties. Secondly, because the Central Government formulated the policy of "production self-reliance," so over the past few years, although we have suffered serious disasters year after year, agricultural production has still increased year by year. In 1949, the total output of the province was only 9.1 billion jin, in 1950 it increased to 10.1 billion jin, in 1951 it increased to 17.3 billion jin; in 1952, although the disaster situation was more serious than in 1951, the output still increased to 17.6 billion jin; in 1953, although the disaster situation was more serious than in 1952, the output increased again to 17.9 billion jin. This year, although the flood is unprecedented in a hundred years, due to the strengthening of field management in non-disaster areas and the adoption of measures such as draining water, replanting, eliminating fallow land, cultivating regenerated rice, regenerated sorghum, and mixed planting, the output can still reach about 15 billion jin, which is 12.8% higher than the highest output before liberation. At the same time, due to the development of various sideline productions and the expansion of vegetable planting areas, income from various aspects has been increased for the disaster-stricken people, so we have overcome the serious disasters over the past few years and accumulated production experience in disaster-prone areas. Thirdly, because the Central Government made the decision to "definitely improve the Huai River," large-scale projects have been built along the Huai River, especially in the middle reaches, which not only eliminated small disasters caused by small rains and reduced large disasters caused by large rains, but also increased the conditions for increased production. For example, the Su County area in northern Anhui, which has always been a disaster-prone area in Anhui, still has the possibility of increased production in the unprecedented flood situation this year, which is a clear test.

The people of Anhui say: "In the past few years, without clothes, we were given clothes, without grain, we were given grain, without straw, we were given coal, when we were sick, we were given medicine, and we were also helped with production tools and fertilizers, guided in production technology, and at the same time, the Huai River was treated well to ensure production. The people's government is truly warmer than the winter sun and closer than parents." They also say: "The suffering of the reactionaries is endless; the kindness of Chairman Mao is unforgettable for eight generations." Here, let me, on behalf of the people of Anhui, express our heartfelt thanks to the people of the whole country, and express our highest respect to the Central People's Government and the great leader of the Chinese people, Chairman Mao!

Because of the correct leadership and support of the Communist Party of China led by Chairman Mao and the Central People's Government, and because of the encouragement and assistance of the people of the whole country, we have completely overcome the various disasters from 1949 to 1953 and have achieved the requirement of disaster relief without starvation, which is something that no other dynasty in history could achieve. From this, we can also be sure that we will be able to overcome the disasters of this year even better. However, we will not be complacent. We deeply know that not only is the elimination of disasters a long-term and arduous task, but in the past few years of production and disaster relief work, there are still many serious shortcomings and errors. For example, in the masses and grassroots cadres, because of the continuous disasters over the years, there has been a sense of unease, and because of the continuous disaster relief, there has been a sense of dependence, which has not been completely overcome; because the disaster situation is not grasped in reality, there have been phenomena of disproportionate relief in the distribution of relief grain and funds, and even the phenomenon of embezzlement or over-receipt of relief grain and funds has not been completely eliminated; because of the development of sideline production, there has been a tendency to increase the spontaneous capitalist forces, which has not been prevented in advance. All these phenomena are inseparable from the existence of certain bureaucratic work styles in our leadership, such as insufficient education, insufficient inspection, and unclear instructions. I deeply feel that to do a good job in various work, especially in disaster situations, we must review Chairman Mao's works, learn Chairman Mao's work methods, and especially remember Chairman Mao's instructions in the opening speech of this conference: "Be honest, diligent, and hardworking, encourage and help each other, and strictly avoid any boasting and arrogance." I believe that as long as we can act according to the Constitution and act according to Chairman Mao's instructions, we will definitely be able to complete the task of socialist construction and socialist transformation, and definitely be able to complete the task of overcoming disasters and conquering nature.

Of course, all these are only the beginning of the work, and in the future, it will be a very arduous historical task to make various different nationalities in the province, in such a vast area where each county includes several nationalities, transition to socialism in a stable and orderly manner according to the provisions of the Constitution and in full accordance with the actual situation of each nationality. If we do not seriously overcome the shortcomings in our work, especially in the work of national unity, and do not constantly struggle against various erroneous ideas, the realization of this task is not guaranteed. There are many shortcomings in our work, for example, the work of training and promoting a large number of minority cadres is still not done enough, and there is still a lack of more planned training and education for the more than 11,000 minority cadres who are already in place; in the implementation of policies, sometimes we deviate from the original work basis, mechanically copy the methods of Han areas, and are not good at using the ways that various nationalities like to see and hear to carry out work, thus showing impatience and impetuousness; or neglect the growth of progressive factors in minority nationalities, do not see the influence and changes brought about by the achievements of work in recent years, and thus passively deal with some necessary and possible work; some Han cadres do not respect the customs and habits of minority nationalities, and cannot patiently and enthusiastically help and rely on minority masses, and also show incorrect attitudes in uniting national public leaders. These great nationalism thoughts and local nationalism thoughts in some minority cadres are all obstacles to the realization of the above tasks, and must be corrected through education, criticism, and serious struggle.

The convening of the First National People's Congress marks a new stage in the people's democratic system. I am honored to be among them and must guarantee to the Congress: to study Liu Shaoqi's report on the draft Constitution of the People's Republic of China and Premier Zhou Enlai's government work report, and to guarantee to implement the spirit of these two reports: to strictly abide by the Constitution and all laws, to be loyal to the people's democratic system, to serve the people wholeheartedly, to be a sincere and honest servant of the people. At the same time, under the cultivation of the Communist Party of China and Chairman Mao, to carry out thorough ideological transformation, to overcome the errors and shortcomings in my work; to guarantee to contribute all my strength to the socialist construction of the motherland, to the struggle for the liberation of Taiwan, and to the cause of world peace.

Title: Speeches at the First Session of the First National People's Congress
Author:
Date: 1954-09-21
Page: 1

Li Renren, a representative from Yunnan Province, spoke at the First Session of the First National People's Congress. He acknowledged that Yunnan, being one of the later regions to be liberated and with a relatively weak foundation for work, faces significant challenges. Despite these obstacles, he emphasized the need for active and cautious efforts to achieve the great task of socialist industrialization and the socialist transformation of agriculture, handicrafts, and capitalist industry and commerce. He stressed the importance of following the centralized and unified leadership of the central government, overcoming shortcomings in the current construction work, and ensuring that the tasks assigned by the state are successfully executed.

Li Renren highlighted several issues in Yunnan's work, including the lack of clear understanding among some cadres about relying on the working class and completing national plans comprehensively and evenly. He noted problems such as waste, rework, and violations of construction procedures in building projects, as well as inconsistent production performance in some manufacturing units in Kunming. In agricultural transformation, while mutual aid and cooperation have shown results, these efforts have not been widely implemented, and capitalist spontaneous forces in rural areas have not been adequately curbed.

To address these issues, Li Renren called for strengthening unity among all ethnic groups in Yunnan, based on the provisions of the Constitution and in conjunction with local conditions. He urged all state organ workers, especially leading cadres, to conduct thorough self-criticism and criticism, promptly rectify errors and shortcomings in their work, and remember Chairman Mao's instructions to "be humble and prudent, guard against arrogance and impetuosity." He emphasized the need to rely closely on the masses and work diligently to accomplish various tasks.

Li Renren expressed his excitement and support for the government work report delivered by Premier Zhou Enlai. He praised the significant achievements made in various fields since the founding of the People's Republic of China and the progress in building a socialist new China. He reiterated his support for the correct policies and guidelines of the Central People's Government and the Chinese Communist Party.

Li Renren also discussed specific issues in Yunnan, such as the need for the government to plan and construct based on regional characteristics, develop livestock and forestry, and utilize the rich marine resources. He suggested improving transportation and allocation of materials to ensure timely delivery of products and supply of necessary materials.

In conclusion, Li Renren proposed that the conference adopt the government work report and that he would strive to disseminate and implement it widely.
