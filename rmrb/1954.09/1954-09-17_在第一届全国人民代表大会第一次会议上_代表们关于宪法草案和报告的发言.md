Title: Speeches by Representatives on the Draft Constitution and Reports at the First Session of the First National People's Congress
Author:
Date: 1954-09-17

At the first session of the First National People's Congress, representatives discussed the draft constitution and reports. Below are the speeches from several representatives:

**Lin Boqu's Speech**

Comrades and Representatives:

The First National People's Congress of the People's Republic of China is about to solemnly adopt the Constitution of the People's Republic of China. This is a great event for our country, a great event for the entire nation, and a great event for all our friends around the world.

This Constitution is a summary of the victories of the Chinese people's revolution against imperialism, feudalism, and bureaucratic capitalism over the past century, especially the past thirty years. It is a summary of the experience of nation-building since the founding of the People's Republic of China. It is a guarantee that our country will build a socialist society through a peaceful path.

In the process of drafting this Constitution, people of all ethnic groups in China have enthusiastically participated in the discussion. It is a combination of the experiences of the leaders and the broad masses of the people, and a combination of the experiences of China and the Soviet Union and other people's democratic countries.

The draft Constitution stipulates that all power in the People's Republic of China belongs to the people, and that the organs through which the people exercise their power are the National People's Congress and local people's congresses at various levels. It stipulates that the National People's Congress is the highest organ of state power and the sole organ exercising legislative power. It stipulates that the National People's Congress, local people's congresses at various levels, and other state organs all practice the system of democratic centralism. It stipulates that people's representatives are supervised by their original electoral units and voters. It stipulates that all personnel of state organs must strive to serve the people. It stipulates that the state's armed forces belong to the people, and their task is to defend the fruits of the people's revolution and national construction, to defend the sovereignty, territorial integrity, and security of the state.

The various parts of the draft Constitution are all imbued with the spirit of equality, friendship, mutual assistance, and cooperation among the various nationalities within the country. All these regulations concerning political and state systems are summaries of the actual experiences of China's political life, now fixed in legal form.

The draft Constitution points out that our country is in a historical period of gradually transitioning to a socialist society, reflecting the fundamental requirements of our country in the transitional period and the common aspirations of the people of the whole country to build a socialist society. It stipulates the general tasks of the state in the transitional period, points out the internal and external conditions for achieving the general tasks, and stipulates the path for the Chinese people to strive for these goals. These stipulations are all based on the current social situation of China. Due to the leadership of the Chinese working class and the efforts of the broad laboring masses, the socialist factors in the political and economic aspects of the country have already become the decisive leading force, which determines that the gradual transition to a socialist society is an inevitable trend of our country's existence and development; at the same time, due to the large existence of non-socialist economic components and the fact that the development of the socialist economy cannot keep up with the needs of the broad masses, the transformation of the various existing non-socialist ownership systems must be carried out gradually and differently. Only by guaranteeing the priority development of state-owned economy, that is, the socialist economy of full public ownership, can the leading force in the national economy be strengthened and the material foundation for the realization of socialist transformation be expanded. Only by relying on state organs to take specific administrative measures and combining them with the socialist consciousness and active actions of the broad masses can the socialist transformation be gradually realized, gradually eliminate the system of exploitation, and build a socialist society. Over the past few years, we have been following these principles to proceed. This is a summary of the actual experiences of the economic life of the Chinese people, now also fixed in legal form.

Since the founding of the People's Republic of China, the great achievements of various measures have already brought profound and fundamental changes to the entire Chinese society in all aspects. The material and cultural living standards of the people have significantly improved compared to before liberation, and the various freedoms and rights of the people have begun to be guaranteed materially. However, we are still a new country that has just been established from the ruins of the old, impoverished, and backward China, and our socialist construction and socialist transformation are just beginning to be carried out in a planned manner. The accumulation of national wealth is still not sufficient. Therefore, it can be said that at present, our country's material guarantees for the various basic rights of citizens are still insufficient. It should be pointed out that developing economic construction is the prerequisite for improving people's lives, and the view that ignores the interests of economic construction and demands the improvement of people's lives too high and too urgently is incorrect; it should also be pointed out that among the personnel of our state organs, there are still some people who adopt a bureaucratic and indifferent attitude towards improving people's lives under possible conditions, and even violate laws and discipline, infringing upon the basic rights of the people, which is also wrong and must be resolutely opposed.

People of all ethnic groups across the country, whether in factories, in rural areas, in schools, in government agencies, or in other positions, are enthusiastically welcoming the birth of the Constitution of the People's Republic of China with actual actions. We firmly believe that under the leadership of Comrade Mao Zedong and the Chinese Communist Party, after the people have their own Constitution, the enthusiasm and creativity of the masses will be further unleashed. We must unite with the people of the whole country to continue to strengthen unity and strive to thoroughly accomplish the general tasks of the state in the transitional period, and build our country into a great socialist state!

**Han En's Speech**

Chairman, Comrades and Representatives:

The convening of the National People's Congress to adopt the Constitution of the People's Republic of China is a great event for our people. I am very happy to be able to participate in such an important conference as a representative. Farmers participating in the management of state affairs and the formulation of the state constitution is something that has never happened before. Only under the leadership of the Communist Party and Chairman Mao, can we farmers have such a good day today.

I am the director of the Ba'an Village Agricultural Production Cooperative in Jilin Province. Last winter, when we farmers learned about the general tasks of the state in the transitional period, our production enthusiasm was even higher. The light of socialism has illuminated everyone's hearts. We have seen with our own eyes that many large factories are under construction, new agricultural tools and chemical fertilizers are increasingly being delivered to the countryside, and we have heard that tractor factories are also being built. In the future, farming with machines will be like what we have seen in many state farms, deep plowing, good growth, and more grain will be harvested.

In June this year, after the draft Constitution was published, we farmers discussed this draft Constitution, and our production enthusiasm was even greater. The Constitution consolidates the fruits of our country's revolutionary victories and land reform, and the Constitution also guarantees that our country will gradually transition to socialism. The Constitution stipulates that developing cooperatives is the main path to transform individual agriculture, which fully represents the wishes of the broad masses of farmers. To make everyone prosperous, we must take the path of cooperation, and this point we farmers are increasingly understanding. Our Ba'an Village was once famous for being a "poor stick village." The Communist Party came, the farmers divided the land, and organized mutual aid teams. By 1952, agricultural production cooperatives were established. Although we suffered a disaster that year, the cooperative still ensured a good harvest; so more farmers wanted to join, and by the end of the year, it developed to 47 households. Now our cooperative has developed to 107 households, with 160 mu of water and dry fields, equivalent to 2,400 mu in the interior, and this year we can harvest 12 million jin of grain. We calculate: after paying 130,000 jin of public grain, leaving seeds for next year and the grain for the members to eat, we can still sell 700,000 jin of grain to the state. This year we have already sold 20,000 eggs and 12,000 jin of pork to the state at fair and reasonable prices, and after the autumn harvest, we will sell another 7,000 jin of pork and 5,000 jin of beef to the state.

In the two years since the establishment of our cooperative, we have planted more than 4,000 fruit trees and more than 100,000 ordinary trees, built a one-mu fish pond, and released 20,000 fish fry. Because production has developed, the income of the members has increased year by year, and life has improved year by year; just the houses that the members live in, 40 new ones have been built in the past two years, and the public granary and livestock shed of the cooperative are not included. The cultural level of the members has also improved, and generally, members can recognize 300-500 characters and are still continuing to study. Like our agricultural production cooperative, there are many in Jilin Province, and there are even more across the country. However, our cooperative still has many shortcomings. We have not fully utilized the functions of new agricultural tools; although the amount of fertilizer is large, the quality is poor; some old members are not enthusiastic about absorbing poor households to join the cooperative when expanding the cooperative. We still need to learn from other cooperatives to make our cooperative better and further develop production.

These benefits are all thanks to the Communist Party and Chairman Mao leading us to drive out imperialism, defeat the Kuomintang reactionaries, distribute land, and organize mutual aid and cooperation. They are also inseparable from the help of the worker big brothers and the development of industry. Therefore, we farmers enthusiastically support this constitution that guarantees the construction of socialism; after the conference adopts this constitution, we will definitely use our labor and production to guarantee the realization of the goals set by this constitution as soon as possible.

We must learn from the Soviet Union in river development, formulate a long-term power development plan as the basis for national economic development; establish a unified organization for national topographic surveys, geological surveys, hydrological observations, and various aspects; compile a national water resource development outline and a ten-to-twenty-year water power generation construction plan, so that the water conservancy construction of the country's rivers can be implemented step by step, in an orderly manner, and with a focus under the guidance of a unified national plan. At present, our country is planning the comprehensive development plan of the entire Yellow River and the design and construction of the Guanting and other water power stations in many places. We will be able to and definitely will develop the water power industry on a large scale in the future, making the rivers of the entire country effectively serve the national economy. As a water conservancy and power generation worker, I am grateful to the state for the encouragement and help given to scientific research and creative work. Let us accelerate the progress of the water conservancy industry across the country and contribute all our strength to the construction of the socialist industrialization of the country.

Wang Yun Sheng's Speech:

I am now speaking about the draft constitution of the People's Republic of China, and I feel extremely excited and greatly honored.

Since March this year, I have participated in the discussion of the initial draft of the draft constitution. In the past half year, I have seen the draft constitution change four times, extensively absorbing everyone's opinions and making meticulous revisions. Now it is almost perfect and will soon become the most practical, ideal, and perfect constitution for China's transitional period.

The constitution guarantees every citizen's various basic rights and requires every citizen to use their rights well in their work, life, and thoughts. Combining my personal profession, I have two thoughts:

First, Article 87 of the draft constitution stipulates that "citizens of the People's Republic of China have the freedom of speech, publication..." Our country urgently needs to carry out a healthy and vigorous criticism and self-criticism. However, the situation in the past five years of building the country is that our speech work often lags behind reality and lacks a vigorous and powerful critical atmosphere. This situation, we speech workers should first criticize ourselves, that we have not been conscientious and responsible, resulting in our speech work being weak and powerless, and newspapers being dull and lifeless. Another reason is that there is still some bureaucracy within us. Bureaucracy lacks the attitude of accepting good advice. Our country has made great progress and achieved a lot, which is recognized by the world; at the same time, some people have become arrogant and complacent, not only do they not encourage speech criticism but even resist criticism. Of course, this situation is not universal and is not very serious, but it must be noted and corrected. Speech workers should, under the protection of the constitution, grasp the right of free speech, be conscientious and responsible, and carry out a healthy and vigorous criticism and self-criticism for the country.

Second, Article 95 of the draft constitution stipulates that "the People's Republic of China guarantees the freedom of citizens to engage in scientific research, literary and artistic creation, and other cultural activities." The situation in the past five years is that politics has made great progress, the economy is catching up, and culture is even more catching up. In the cultural sector, there is a particular lack of creative work. Novels and scripts are rare; general articles of discussion are mostly turned over and over, clichs and clichs; newspaper news writing is filled with a fixed format and a fixed tune. This style of writing is a serious eight-part, formulaic, and monotonous. It should be pointed out that after liberation, the style of writing is careful in words and correct in reasoning, which is good; but generally, people like to write long sentences that are full of repetitions and quotations, writing dry and emotionless articles, and a lack of eight-part formulas, with few people daring to make creative attempts. First of all, it is me myself, who has not written anything lively and powerful, and there are few good articles in the Daguo Daily. Such a style of writing, compared with the rapid development of our country's situation, is extremely unmatched. The draft constitution wants "the state to encourage and assist the creative work of citizens engaged in scientific, educational, literary, artistic, and other cultural undertakings." We cultural workers should not disappoint the state's encouragement and assistance and create a new style of writing for New China.

I propose that the conference solemnly adopt this constitution.
