Title: Pakistan Notifies Foreign Minister Zhou of Willingness to Establish Diplomatic Relations with China; Vice Foreign Minister Li Kenong Notifies Pakistani Government, Welcoming Da Zhuding to Come to Beijing for Negotiations
Author:
Date: 1950-02-05
Page: 1

Xinhua News Agency, Beijing, February 4th: The Pakistani government, through its ambassador to the Soviet Union, Mr. Karesi, notified our Foreign Minister Zhou Enlai on January 5th that the Pakistani government is willing to establish diplomatic relations with our country. Our Vice Foreign Minister Li Kenong, through Mr. Karesi, notified the Pakistani government on February 4th, expressing that our Central People's Government agrees to establish diplomatic relations with the Pakistani government on the basis of equality, mutual benefit, and mutual respect for territorial sovereignty. The Central People's Government welcomes Mr. Da Zhuding, currently in Nanjing, as a special envoy of the Pakistani government to come to Beijing to negotiate on preliminary procedural matters related to the establishment of diplomatic relations between China and Pakistan.

### 巴基斯坦通知周外长  愿与我建立外交关系  李克农副外长通知巴政府欢迎达朱丁前来北京谈判

1950-02-05
第1版()
专栏：

　　巴基斯坦通知周外长
    愿与我建立外交关系
    李克农副外长通知巴政府欢迎达朱丁前来北京谈判
    【新华社北京四日电】巴基斯坦政府于一月五日经过其驻苏大使喀莱西先生通知我外交部周恩来部长，谓巴基斯坦政府愿与我国建立外交关系。我外交部李克农副部长于二月四日经过喀莱西先生通知巴基斯坦政府，表示我中央人民政府同意在平等互利及互相尊重领土主权的基础上，与巴基斯坦政府建立外交关系，并欢迎现在南京之达朱丁先生作为巴基斯坦政府的特派员前来北京，就有关建立中巴两国外交关系的初步的程序的事宜进行谈判。
