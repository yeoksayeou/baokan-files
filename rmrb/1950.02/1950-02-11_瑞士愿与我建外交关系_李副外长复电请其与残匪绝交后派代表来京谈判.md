Title: Switzerland Willing to Establish Diplomatic Relations with Us; Vice Foreign Minister Li Replies, Requesting Severance of Ties with Remaining Bandits Before Sending Representatives to Beijing for Negotiations
Author:
Date: 1950-02-11
Page: 1
Xinhua News Agency, Beijing, 10th—Max Petitpierre, President and Foreign Minister of the Swiss Confederation, sent a telegram to Chairman Mao on January 17th, expressing the Swiss Federal Government's willingness to establish diplomatic relations with our Central People's Government. On February 6th, Petitpierre sent another telegram to our Minister of Foreign Affairs, Zhou Enlai, wishing to appoint Mr. Sven Stinner, currently in Hong Kong, as Chargé d'affaires ad interim to come to Beijing to contact us. Li Kenong, Vice Minister of Foreign Affairs of our Central People's Government, replied to Petitpierre on February 10th, stating that after the Swiss government severs relations with the remnants of the Kuomintang reactionaries, our Central People's Government is willing to establish diplomatic relations with the Swiss Federal Government on the basis of equality, mutual benefit, and mutual respect for territorial sovereignty, and is prepared to accept Mr. Sven Stinner as a representative to come to Beijing to negotiate the establishment of diplomatic relations between the two countries.

### 瑞士愿与我建外交关系  李副外长复电请其与残匪绝交后派代表来京谈判

1950-02-11
第1版()
专栏：

　　瑞士愿与我建外交关系
    李副外长复电请其与残匪绝交后派代表来京谈判
    【新华社北京十日电】瑞士联邦政府主席兼外交部长马克思·波蒂彼爱，于一月十七日致电毛主席，表示瑞士联邦政府愿与我中央人民政府建立外交关系。二月六日波氏又电我外交部周恩来部长，欲任命现在香港的司文·司丁纳先生为临时代办前来北京，与我取得联系。我中央人民政府外交部李克农副部长，于二月十日电复波氏，谓在瑞士政府与国民党反动派残余断绝关系之后，我中央人民政府愿在平等互惠及互相尊重领土主权的基础上，与瑞士联邦政府建立外交关系，并准备接受司文·司丁纳先生为代表前来北京，就建立两国外交关系问题进行谈判。
