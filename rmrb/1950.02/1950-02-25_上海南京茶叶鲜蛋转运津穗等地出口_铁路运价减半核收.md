Title: Shanghai, Nanjing Tea and Fresh Eggs Transferred to Tianjin, Guangzhou and Other Places for Export: Railway Freight Rates Reduced by Half

Author: 
Date: 1950-02-25
Page: 2

　　Shanghai, Nanjing Tea and Fresh Eggs Transferred to Tianjin, Guangzhou and Other Places for Export
    Railway Freight Rates Reduced by Half
    [Xinhua News Agency, Beijing, February 24th] In order to further assist Shanghai and other places in solving the sales difficulties caused by the blockade of seaports by the American imperialists and the Kuomintang bandits, the Ministry of Railways of the Central People's Government has recently decided that tea and tea bricks shipped from Shanghai to Tianjin, Qingdao, and Guangzhou for export, and fresh eggs shipped from stations between Shanghai and Nanjing to Tianjin for export, shall be charged half the freight rate for whole-car shipments, provided that they hold certificates from the China National Foreign Trade Corporation. The Ministry of Railways issued an order on the 16th, stipulating that this measure shall be implemented from the date of the order. Note: The Ministry of Railways previously stipulated that the freight rates for hog bristles, tea, tung oil, and raw silk transported from Jiangnan to Tianjin for export would be reduced by half.

### 上海南京茶叶鲜蛋转运津穗等地出口  铁路运价减半核收

1950-02-25
第2版()
专栏：

　　上海南京茶叶鲜蛋转运津穗等地出口
    铁路运价减半核收
    【新华社北京二十四日电】中央人民政府铁道部为进一步协助上海等地解决由于美帝国主义和国民党匪帮封锁海口所造成的销路困难，顷决定：自上海运至天津、青岛、广州出口的茶叶、茶砖，和自上海、南京间各站起运至天津出口的鲜蛋，凡持有国外贸易总公司证明者，整车运输，均减半核收运价。铁道部已于十六日发出命令，规定此项办法于令到之日起实行。按：铁道部前已规定自江南运至天津出口的猪鬃、茶叶、桐油、生丝等项，减半核收运价。
