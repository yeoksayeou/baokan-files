Title: Burmese People's Liberation Army Liberates Most of Central Burma
Author:
Date: 1950-02-08
Page: 4

　　Xinhua News Agency, Beijing, 7th: TASS, London, 6th: Mr. Errol, former member of the British Parliament, commented on the situation in Burma in the Sunday Times, saying: Burma's trade is declining day by day, and its annual income is decreasing day by day. The Burmese People's Liberation Army "occupies large areas of central Burma, cutting off the main transportation lines from north to south." He emphasized: The Burma Corporation's lead and silver mines have lost railway transportation, and the lead mine in Tavoy, southern Burma, was occupied by guerrillas in January.
　　It is reported that Errol was a member of the British parliamentary delegation that visited Burma not long ago.

### 缅人民解放军解放中缅大部

1950-02-08
第4版()
专栏：

　　缅人民解放军解放中缅大部
    【新华社北京七日电】塔斯社伦敦六日讯：英国前议会议员伊罗尔顷在星期泰晤士报评论缅甸形势称：缅甸的贸易日渐衰退，岁收日益减少。缅甸人民解放军“占据着中缅甸的大片地区，截断了从北到南的主要交通线”。他强调说：缅甸公司的铅矿和银矿都失去了铁路运输，缅甸南部塔伐的铅矿，已在一月间被游击队占领。
    按伊罗尔是不久以前访问缅甸的英国议会代表团团员之一。
