Title: Iceland Firmly Defends Twelve-Nautical-Mile Territorial Waters
Author:
Date: 1958-09-10
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, Reykjavik, September 8th: According to TASS, Reykjavik, September 8th, Icelandic Prime Minister Emil Jónsson announced at a press conference on the 8th that Iceland "will never compromise on the decision regarding the twelve-nautical-mile fishing zone."

He pointed out that fishing is the sole source of livelihood for the Icelandic people, and therefore the overfishing by foreign vessels near the Icelandic coast poses a serious threat to the survival of the Icelandic people. He said, "To deny us the protection of our fishing zone is, in the final analysis, to deny our country the right to live."

Jónsson emphasized that "the twelve-nautical-mile principle does not violate any international legal norms." He said: "Our decision has been met with resistance based on strength (referring to the armed interference of British vessels near the Icelandic coast - editor's note). We strongly protested against this use of force. At the same time, we believe that we will be able to prove that fishing under the protection of warships is impossible. The measures taken against us are unrealistic and will not succeed."

### 冰岛坚决保卫十二浬领水

1958-09-10
第5版()
专栏：

　　冰岛坚决保卫十二浬领水
    新华社9日讯　据塔斯社雷克雅未克8日讯：冰岛总理荣纳逊8日在记者招待会上宣布，冰岛“永远不会在十二浬的捕鱼区这个决定上让步。”
    他指出，捕鱼是冰岛人民生活的唯一来源，因此外国船只在冰岛海岸附近滥捕鱼产对冰岛人民的生存是一个严重的威胁。他说，“不准我们保护我们的捕鱼区，归根结底就是不让我国活下去。”
    荣纳逊强调指出，“十二浬的原则不违背任何国际法准则。”他说：“我们的决定遭到了实力的抵制（指英国船只在冰岛海岸附近的武装干涉活动——编者注）。我们坚决抗议了这种采用实力的做法。同时我们相信，我们将能证明在军舰的保护下捕鱼是不可能的。针对我们而采取的措施是不现实的，是不会得到成功的。”
