Title: Seething Anger – Against America’s Military Provocations
Author: Sha Ou (沙鸥)
Date: 1958-09-08
Page: 8

　　Seething Anger
　　——Against America’s Military Provocations
　　Sha Ou
　　Ten thousand red flags roar in the wind,
　　A torrent gathers before Tiananmen,
　　Six hundred million people are enraged,
　　Clenched tightly into a fist.
　　A copper wall, ten thousand fathoms thick,
　　Fears not the fierce wind and angry waves,
　　If the American imperialism dares to crash against it,
　　It will dig its own grave and bury itself.
　　We exercise our sovereignty,
　　America calls it “expansion”;
　　We handle our internal affairs,
　　America calls it “aggression”;
　　Our sacred inland sea,
　　America calls it its “defense line”;
　　America holds in one hand,
　　The dictionary of imperialism,
　　And in the other,
　　Waves knives and brandishes guns at us.
　　The seething anger surges,
　　The waves beat the sky,
　　The entire people are mobilized,
　　The mountains and rivers make way!
　　Who can be frightened by the threat of war,
　　Taiwan must be liberated!

### 怒潮澎湃——反对美国的军事挑衅
沙鸥
1958-09-08
第8版()
专栏：

　　怒潮澎湃
　　——反对美国的军事挑衅
　　沙鸥
　　红旗万杆迎风吼，
　　天安门前汇洪流，
　　六亿人民被激怒，
　　紧紧捏成一拳头。
　　一道铜墙厚千丈，
　　哪怕狂风和恶浪，
　　美帝胆敢用头撞，
　　自掘坟墓把身葬。
　　我们行使主权，
　　美国说是“扩张”；
　　我们处理内政，
　　美国说是“侵略”；
　　我国神圣的内海，
　　美国说是它的“边防”；
　　美国一手举着
　　帝国主义的字典，
　　一手向我们
　　挥刀弄枪。
　　怒潮滔滔，
　　云天拍浪，
　　全民动员起来，
　　三山五岳把路让！
　　战争威胁能吓谁，
　　台湾一定要解放！
