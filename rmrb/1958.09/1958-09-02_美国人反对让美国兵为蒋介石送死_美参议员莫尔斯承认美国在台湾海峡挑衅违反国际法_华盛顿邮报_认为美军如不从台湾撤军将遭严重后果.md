Title: Americans Oppose Sending American Soldiers to Die for Chiang Kai-shek; U.S. Senator Morse Admits U.S. Provocation in the Taiwan Strait Violates International Law; "Washington Post" Believes U.S. Military Will Suffer Severe Consequences If It Does Not Withdraw from Taiwan
Author:
Date: 1958-09-02
Page: 5

Xinhua News Agency, 1st dispatch from Oregon: U.S. Senator Wayne Morse, a member of the Senate Foreign Relations Committee, admitted that the U.S. provocation in the Taiwan Strait is a violation of international law.

Morse said yesterday at a picnic of Democrats in Bend, Oregon, that the United States has no legitimate right to make demands on Jinmen or Mazu, and that the actions currently taken by the U.S. government are "illegal" under international law. He opposed "sending American boys to die on these islands" to maintain the Chiang Kai-shek puppet clique.

Morse also mocked the Eisenhower administration's approach to foreign policy, saying, "We have gone so far down the road of secret politics that neither I, a member of the Senate Foreign Relations Committee, nor any other member of this committee, could pass an elementary school test on American foreign policy."

Xinhua News Agency, 1st dispatch from Washington: An article published in "The Washington Post" on August 28 admitted that the U.S. provocation on the Chinese coastal islands has put the United States in a dilemma. The article believes that the United States is neither willing to stop its provocation against China, nor can it expand this provocation "without risking a full-scale war."

The article said: "We cannot retreat without losing face," because the issue of face is "extremely important" to the United States, "but if we stay on those islands," "we will be pulverized by artillery and air force bombardment."

The article said that U.S. military leaders are dissatisfied with this predicament of the United States, and "some military leaders privately grumble" that Dulles should have made the United States and Chiang Kai-shek's troops "gracefully withdraw from those islands" long ago.

### 美国人反对让美国兵为蒋介石送死  美参议员莫尔斯承认美国在台湾海峡挑衅违反国际法  “华盛顿邮报”认为美军如不从台湾撤军将遭严重后果

1958-09-02
第5版()
专栏：

    美国人反对让美国兵为蒋介石送死
    美参议员莫尔斯承认美国在台湾海峡挑衅违反国际法
    “华盛顿邮报”认为美军如不从台湾撤军将遭严重后果
    新华社1日讯　俄勒冈消息：美国参议院外交委员会委员魏恩·莫尔斯承认美国在台湾海峡进行挑衅是违反国际法的行为。
    莫尔斯昨天在班得尔敦民主党人的一次野餐会上说，美国不论对金门或马祖都没有合法的提出要求的权利，美国政府目前所采取的行动按国际公法来说是“不合法”的。他反对为了维持蒋介石傀儡集团“要美国男儿们去死在这些岛屿上”。
    莫尔斯还嘲弄美国艾森豪威尔政府在外交政策方面的做法，他说“我们在秘密政治道路上已经走得这样远，以致我这个参议院外交委员会委员或这个委员会的任何其他委员都无法在小学里的美国外交政策考试中及格。”
    新华社1日讯　华盛顿消息：“华盛顿邮报”8月28日刊载的一篇文章承认，美国在中国沿海岛屿的挑衅已经使美国处于进退两难的地位。文章认为，美国既不愿停止对中国的挑衅，也不能扩大这种挑衅而“不冒全面战争的危险”。
    这篇文章说：“我们不能够后退而不顾面子”，因为面子问题对美国来说是“极为重要的”，“但是，如果我们留在那些岛屿上”，“便会被大炮、空军轰炸得粉碎。”
    文章说，美国军事领袖对美国这种骑虎难下的情况感到不满，还有“某些军事领袖私下发牢骚”，他们认为杜勒斯早应该使美国和蒋介石的军队“漂亮地从那些岛屿撤退”。
