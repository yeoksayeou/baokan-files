Title: Iraq Tries Traitor Jamali, Exposing a Pile of Evidence of US and British Interference in the Middle East
Author: 
Date: 1958-09-27
Page: 5

　　 本报综合报道 据新华社讯：伊拉克特别法庭在20日开庭审讯前努里·赛义德卖国政权的外交大臣贾马利。总检察长以贾马利积极参与已死的努里·赛义德和王储伊拉的叛国罪行，要求特别军事法庭把他判处死刑。
　　伊拉克特别军事法庭24日晚上在审讯贾马利的时候所提出的文件证据，证实了被告的叛国罪行，揭露了美英帝国主义干涉中东的经过。
　　为了证明被告图谋颠复和吞并叙利亚的罪行，法庭宣读了被告在1954年给伊拉克前首相奥马里的电报，这封电报说：“叙利亚局势中的发展正在形成中。我会见了叙利亚民族主义者的领袖们，保证必须同他们达成协议以掌握局势和谋求结成联邦。”被告在给奥马里的另一封电报中说：“局势越来越严重，请派人来协助我”。
　　奥马里为此派去了艾哈迈德·穆赫塔尔·巴班。他们举行了会议。被告在电报中报告说，“叙利亚真正的麻烦在于军队里的混乱情况，军队属于五个政党。保障利益的最好办法是由我们的朋友发动陆军叛乱，为伊拉克军队的干涉作好准备，随后由我方占领。”电报又说，“这个行动必须同美国和英国加以研究，以便取得它们的同意，保证以色列不采取敌对行动。”
　　法庭宣读了被告在华盛顿同杜勒斯、朗特里和科耳曼进行会谈的记录。被告当时说：“我们在中东的目的是同美国的目的一致的。最重要的是改变叙利亚的政权。然后使其他阿拉伯国家站到西方一边。”
　　被告在1954年发电报给首相，要求他对约旦国王侯赛因施加压力，来请伊拉克部队镇压约旦的解放运动。
　　其次一个问题是黎巴嫩危机。贾马利在1958年得到被他叫作叙利亚“民族主义领袖”的国家社会党头子阿萨德·阿什卡尔一封电报。这封电报说：“我们期待黎巴嫩发生的事情已经发生了。政府不稳固。在黎巴嫩的叙利亚民族主义分子和难民应当得到武器同人民作战。这样就需要钱，装甲车和武器。战事应当马上转移到叙利亚”。
　　被告复电说：“我们同这里的英国大使进行了接触，他对我说，他们驻贝鲁特的大使奉命给予协助。”被告给伊拉克驻贝鲁特大使馆的电报说：“去见黎巴嫩外交部长，告诉他，我们在继续就黎巴嫩情况同这里的美国和英国大使馆接触。将给予一切协助”。
　　在这以后，贾马利还发了另一封电报给这个大使馆。这封电报说：“请夏蒙向叙利亚民族主义分子供应武器，来抗击黎巴嫩的革命。万一他不能这样办，我们将把武器运给他们。”
　　被告给黎巴嫩前总统夏蒙的电报说：“黎巴嫩需要外国援助，最好是阿拉伯国家的援助。最好的办法是，要么黎巴嫩参加哈希姆的联邦，要么同它缔结条约，然后请伊拉克帮助它击败革命。”
　　被告在联合国进行活动以后从纽约发回电报，要求巴格达条约成员国家坚定地对付革命，并且要求派遣国际警察部队。
　　夏蒙谈到了伊拉克军队干涉黎巴嫩革命的可能性。他说，人们知道伊拉克军人不开枪打阿拉伯兄弟，他们对夏蒙政权没有用处。被告致电伊拉克驻贝鲁特大使馆，催促他们对夏蒙保证，伊拉克将制止革命。
　　被告从纽约发出的电报说：“会见了杜勒斯，商定了加强哈希姆联邦，办法是从美国取得二千五百万美元，从英国取得四百万美元。还同意利用联合国宪章第五十一条使黎巴嫩危机国际化，并且要第六舰队干涉。”
　　法庭庭长多次强调指出，同西方帝国主义者结成联盟和对抗社会主义国家的事实，是极大的叛国行动和罪恶。
　　法庭还提出了证据，证明在伊拉克旧政权时期的选举的欺骗性。被告有一次打电报给阿卜杜勒·加尼·达利，问他是愿意当议员呢，还是愿意被任命为大使。法庭到此宣告休庭。
Xinhua News Agency report: The Iraqi Special Court opened on the 20th to try Jamali, the foreign minister of the former Nuri al-Said traitorous regime. The Prosecutor General, on the grounds that Jamali actively participated in the treasonous crimes of the deceased Nuri al-Said and Crown Prince Ira, requested the Special Military Court to sentence him to death.

The documentary evidence presented by the Iraqi Special Military Court during the trial of Jamali on the evening of the 24th confirmed the defendant's treasonous crimes and exposed the interference of US and British imperialism in the Middle East.

In order to prove the defendant's crimes of attempting to subvert and annex Syria, the court read out a telegram sent by the defendant to former Iraqi Prime Minister Omari in 1954. The telegram stated: "Developments in the Syrian situation are taking shape. I have met with the leaders of Syrian nationalists and assured them that an agreement must be reached with them to control the situation and seek federation." In another telegram to Omari, the defendant said: "The situation is becoming increasingly serious, please send someone to assist me."

Omari then sent Ahmed Mukhtar Baban. They held a meeting. The defendant reported in a telegram, "The real trouble in Syria lies in the chaos in the army, which belongs to five political parties. The best way to safeguard interests is for our friends to launch an army rebellion to prepare for the intervention of the Iraqi army, followed by our occupation." The telegram also stated, "This action must be studied with the United States and Britain in order to obtain their consent and ensure that Israel does not take hostile action."

The court read out the records of the defendant's meetings with Dulles, Rountree, and Coleman in Washington. The defendant said at the time: "Our objectives in the Middle East are consistent with those of the United States. The most important thing is to change the regime in Syria. Then bring other Arab countries to the side of the West."

In 1954, the defendant sent a telegram to the Prime Minister, requesting him to put pressure on King Hussein of Jordan to ask Iraqi forces to suppress the liberation movement in Jordan.

The next issue was the Lebanese crisis. In 1958, Jamali received a telegram from Assad Ashkar, the head of the National Socialist Party, whom he called a Syrian "nationalist leader." The telegram stated: "What we expect to happen in Lebanon has already happened. The government is unstable. Syrian nationalists and refugees in Lebanon should be armed to fight the people. This requires money, armored vehicles, and weapons. The war should be immediately transferred to Syria."

The defendant replied by telegram: "We have been in contact with the British Ambassador here, who told me that their ambassador in Beirut is instructed to provide assistance." The defendant's telegram to the Iraqi Embassy in Beirut stated: "Go and see the Lebanese Foreign Minister and tell him that we are continuing to contact the American and British embassies here regarding the situation in Lebanon. All assistance will be given."

After this, Jamali sent another telegram to the embassy. The telegram stated: "Please have Chamoun supply Syrian nationalist elements with weapons to resist the Lebanese revolution. If he cannot do so, we will transport weapons to them."

The defendant's telegram to former Lebanese President Chamoun stated: "Lebanon needs foreign aid, preferably aid from Arab countries. The best way is either for Lebanon to join the Hashemite Federation or to conclude a treaty with it, and then ask Iraq to help it defeat the revolution."

After conducting activities at the United Nations, the defendant sent a telegram back from New York, demanding that the member states of the Baghdad Pact resolutely deal with the revolution and demanding the deployment of an international police force.

Chamoun spoke of the possibility of Iraqi forces intervening in the Lebanese revolution. He said that people knew that Iraqi soldiers would not shoot Arab brothers, and they were of no use to the Chamoun regime. The defendant telegraphed the Iraqi Embassy in Beirut, urging them to assure Chamoun that Iraq would stop the revolution.

The defendant's telegram from New York stated: "Met with Dulles and agreed to strengthen the Hashemite Federation by obtaining 25 million US dollars from the United States and 4 million US dollars from Britain. It was also agreed to use Article 51 of the United Nations Charter to internationalize the Lebanese crisis and to have the Sixth Fleet intervene."

The presiding judge of the court repeatedly emphasized that the fact of forming an alliance with Western imperialists and confronting socialist countries was an extremely treasonous act and crime.

The court also presented evidence proving the deceptive nature of elections during the old regime in Iraq. The defendant once telegraphed Abdul Ghani Dali, asking whether he would prefer to be a member of parliament or be appointed as an ambassador. The court then adjourned.
