Title: Poem on a Portrait of Dulles
Author: Chun Cheng
Date: 1958-09-09
Page: 8

　　Poem on a Portrait of Dulles
　　Chun Cheng
　　A tiger skin pasted on bamboo frame,
　　Face swollen, legs broken, arm missing from the frame,
　　A crippled veteran of the Korean battlefield,
　　His inflated ball has long lost its yield.
　　Like a moth flying blindly to its doom,
　　Brandishing atomic bombs in the room.
　　Seeing the East Wind blowing mightily,
　　He can only furrow his brow and bitterly cry.
　　American imperialist rats, sparrows, mosquitoes, and flies,
　　Are all pests harmful to humanity,
　　To recognize the arch-criminal,
　　A portrait is displayed for all to see.

### 题杜勒斯像
春城
1958-09-09
第8版()
专栏：诗画配
 　 题杜勒斯像
　　春城
　　竹壳纸糊虎皮，
　　脸肿腿断缺臂，
　　朝鲜战场残卒，
　　皮球早已泄气。
　　飞蛾至死不悟，
　　原子炸弹挥舞。
　　眼看东风猛吹，
　　只得皱眉叫苦。
　　美帝鼠雀蚊蝇，
　　都是人类害虫，
　　为了认清元凶，
　　画影图形示众。
