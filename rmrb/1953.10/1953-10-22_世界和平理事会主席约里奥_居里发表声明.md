Title: World Peace Council President Joliot-Curie Issues Statement
Author:
Date: 1953-10-22
Page: 1

Xinhua News Agency, Prague, October 21st. Frédéric Joliot-Curie, President of the World Peace Council, issued a statement in Paris on October 20, 1953, regarding the upcoming meeting of the World Peace Council. The full text of the statement is as follows:

    The World Peace Council will meet in Vienna from November 23rd to 28th, 1953.

    Based on the current state of the peace movement, the World Peace Council suggests considering actions that all peace-loving people—regardless of past or present disagreements—should take to urge governments to resolve international disputes and conflicts through mutually acceptable agreements, giving due consideration to the independence and security of each nation. In particular, consideration should be given to:

    Prohibiting and controlling hydrogen bombs and all weapons of mass destruction, while reducing armaments;

    Resolving the main threats currently facing world peace—the Korean question and the German question.

### 世界和平理事会主席约里奥—居里发表声明

1953-10-22
第1版()
专栏：

    世界和平理事会主席约里奥—居里发表声明
    理事会下届会议定下月二十三日在维也纳举行
    【新华社布拉格二十一日电】世界和平理事会主席弗雷德里克·约里奥—居里一九五三年十月二十日在巴黎发表关于召开世界和平理事会下届会议的声明。声明全文如下：
    世界和平理事会将于一九五三年十一月二十三日至二十八日在维也纳开会。
    根据目前和平运动的情况，世界和平理事会建议考虑一切和平爱好者——不论他们过去或现在有无分歧——为了促使各国政府通过为各方面可以接受的协议——适当顾及每一个国家的独立和安全——解决各国间的争端和冲突所应采取的行动；特别要考虑的是：
    禁止及管制氢弹及一切大规模杀人武器，同时裁减军备；
    解决目前世界和平面临的主要威胁——朝鲜问题和德国问题。
