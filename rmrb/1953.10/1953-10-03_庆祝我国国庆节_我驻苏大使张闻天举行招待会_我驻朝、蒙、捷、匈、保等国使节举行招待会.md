Xinhua News Agency, Pyongyang, 2nd. Gan Yetao, Chargé d'Affaires of the Chinese Embassy in the Democratic People's Republic of Korea, held a celebration banquet in Pyongyang on the evening of the 1st to celebrate our National Day.

Invited to attend the banquet were: Vice Premiers of the Cabinet of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea Choi Chang-ik, Choi Yong-kun, Chung Il-yong, Park Ui-wan, Vice Chairmen of the Central Committee of the Korean Workers' Party Park Jung-ae, Kim Il, Vice Chairman of the Standing Committee of the Supreme People's Assembly Hong Ki-ju, Vice Chairmen of the Supreme People's Assembly Kim Tal-hyun, Lee Young, Ministers of the Cabinet, representatives of various democratic parties and people's organizations, Chief of General Staff of the Korean People's Army Kim Kwang-hyup and senior generals and combat heroes of the Korean People's Army, labor heroes of the Pyongyang area, and celebrities from all walks of life, totaling more than 300 people.

Also invited to attend the banquet were foreign envoys and diplomatic officials stationed in Korea.

A delegation of 15 members of the Chinese People's Volunteers, led by Chief Representative Zu Yaorong, also attended the banquet.

During the banquet, the hosts and guests frequently toasted the unbreakable friendship and solidarity of Korea, the Soviet Union, and China, for peace in the Far East and the world, and for the health of Chairman Mao Zedong, Marshal Kim Il-sung, and Chairman Malenkov.

The banquet proceeded throughout in an atmosphere filled with friendship, solidarity, and internationalist spirit.

Xinhua News Agency, Ulaanbaatar, 2nd. Chen Ying, Chargé d'Affaires ad interim of our Embassy in the People's Republic of Mongolia, held a reception on the evening of the 1st to celebrate the fourth anniversary of the founding of the People's Republic of China. Among those attending the reception were Deputy Premiers of the People's Republic of Mongolia Shilin Dibu and Luvsan, Deputy Chairman of the Presidium of the Great People's Hural Ze Debu, and more than 250 ministers of various Mongolian government departments. Damba, Lamsuren, and Dugsurun, secretaries of the Central Committee of the Mongolian People's Revolutionary Party, also attended the reception.

The Soviet Chargé d'Affaires ad interim in Mongolia and the Korean Ambassador to Mongolia and embassy staff were also invited to attend.

At the reception, hosts and guests frequently toasted the great achievements of the new China in all aspects over the past four years, the fraternal friendship between the Chinese and Mongolian peoples, and the solidarity and strength of the peace and democracy camp headed by the Soviet Union, as well as the health of Chairman Mao Zedong, the leader of the Chinese people, Prime Minister Tsedenbal, the leader of the Mongolian people, and the leaders of the Soviet government headed by Comrade Malenkov.

Xinhua News Agency, Prague, 2nd. Tan Xilin, China's Ambassador to the Czechoslovak Republic, held a reception on the evening of the 1st to celebrate the fourth anniversary of the founding of the People's Republic of China. Invited to the reception were: Premier of the Czechoslovak Republic Siroky, First Deputy Premiers Duransky and Cepicka, Deputy Premier Uher, First Secretary of the Communist Party of Czechoslovakia Novotny, as well as heads of government ministries, leaders and representatives of people's organizations, combat heroes, and labor models, totaling approximately 700 people. Also attending the reception were: Soviet Ambassador to Czechoslovakia Bogomolov, diplomatic envoys of People's Democratic countries and other countries stationed in Czechoslovakia, as well as secretaries of the World Peace Council and representatives of other progressive international organizations.

The reception proceeded in a friendly atmosphere. The Chinese film "National Day 1952" was screened at the reception, and a dance party was held.

Xinhua News Agency, Prague, 2nd. Budapest Dispatch: Huang Zhen, China's Ambassador to the Hungarian People's Republic, held a reception in Budapest on the evening of the 1st to celebrate the fourth anniversary of the founding of our country. Among those invited were: First Secretary of the Hungarian Working People's Party Rakosi, Vice Chairmen of the Presidential Council of the Hungarian People's Republic Rivai and Dániel Nagy, Chairman of the Council of Ministers I. Nagy, First Deputy Chairman and Minister of Interior Gerő, First Deputy Chairman and Minister of Agriculture Hegedüs, First Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs Béres, as well as heads of government ministries, heads or representatives of mass organizations, Stakhanovite workers, and outstanding writers and artists, totaling more than 800 people. Among the guests invited were also: Soviet and other foreign envoys and diplomatic officials stationed in Hungary, as well as international friends such as representatives of the World Federation of Democratic Youth.

The reception was filled with a warm and friendly atmosphere throughout. Hosts and guests frequently toasted the leaders of the Soviet Communist Party and government headed by Malenkov, the health of Comrade Rakosi and Chairman Mao Zedong, and the fraternal friendship between the Chinese and Hungarian peoples.

A dance party was also held after the reception.

Xinhua News Agency, Prague, 2nd. Sofia Dispatch: Cao Xiangren, China's Ambassador to the People's Republic of Bulgaria, held a reception at the Bulgaria Hotel in Sofia on the evening of the 1st to celebrate our National Day. A total of more than 600 people were invited to attend the reception. Among them were: Chairman of the Council of Ministers of Bulgaria Chervenkov, Deputy Chairman of the Council of Ministers G. Chankov, R. Damyanov, Minister of Foreign Affairs M. Neichev, Deputy Chairman of the Council of Ministers A. Yugov, Members of the Political Bureau of the Central Committee of the Bulgarian Communist Party T. Zhivkov, Alternate Member of the Political Bureau G. Gimov, Secretary of the Central Committee of the Bulgarian Communist Party E. Staikov, Minister of Defense Panchevski, as well as other heads of government ministries, figures from the scientific and cultural circles, heads of people's organizations, labor heroes, and model soldiers. Also attending the reception were Soviet Ambassador to Bulgaria Petrov and personnel from the diplomatic missions of People's Democratic countries and other countries stationed in Bulgaria. The reception proceeded throughout in a friendly atmosphere.
