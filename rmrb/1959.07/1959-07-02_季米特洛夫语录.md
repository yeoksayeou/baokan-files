Date: 1959-07-02

    Dimitrov's Quotations
    To fight his enemy well, one must learn to know the enemy.
    We must even unhesitatingly learn from the enemy, if doing so can help us more quickly and effectively wring the enemy's neck.
    Learn, master, and apply.
    Heroism alone is not enough. Heroism must be combined with constant work among the masses and concrete anti-fascist struggle to achieve the most certain results in this area.
    If we do not learn from our own experience and the experience of the masses, then we are not true revolutionaries.
  When writing or speaking, always keep in mind the working masses who must understand your words, believe in your calls, and willingly follow you! You must think about for whom you are writing and to whom you are speaking.
    All rigid formulas are our mortal enemies. We must always and everywhere take into account the specific circumstances, and not act according to a fixed and rigid form in any place and at all places, and not forget that the position of the Communist Party will not be the same in the ever-changing environment.
    Whoever truly wishes to free our work from rigid and fixed formulas, from poisonous pedantry, should work with the masses and lead them in practical and active struggles, while constantly striving to master the great, rich, and all-powerful teachings of Marx, Engels, Lenin, and Stalin, to completely eliminate those formulas.
    Life is more complex than any formula.
    Obstacles should be removed, not succumbed to.
    Victory never comes by itself, but must be fought for.
    Since we resolutely oppose any dependence on spontaneity, we should not stand in a passive position as bystanders when considering the revolutionary development process, but should stand in an active position as participants in this process.
    There must be no indolence or complacency. We must not be content with our achievements, but must learn to work tirelessly.
    It is harmful for theory to be separated from practice. There must be coordination between the practical work we do and our theoretical doctrines.

### 季米特洛夫语录

1959-07-02
第8版()
专栏：

    季米特洛夫语录
    一个人要想很好地与他的敌人作战，就应该学习认识敌人。
    我们甚至必须毫不踌躇地向敌人学习，倘若那样做就能帮助我们更迅速而有效地扭断敌人的脖子的话。
    要学习，要精通，还要应用。
    单靠英雄气概是不够的。英雄气概必须和群众中的经常工作相结合，和反法西斯的具体斗争相结合，才可以在这一范围内得到最确实的成绩。
    如果我们不从我们自己的经验和群众的经验中取得教训，那末，我们就不是真正的革命家。
  写作或谈话的时候，要念念不忘那些必须了解你的话、必须相信你的号召而甘愿追随着你的工人大众！你必须想到你为谁写作，对谁讲话。
    一切呆板的公式都是我们的死敌。我们要随时随地顾到具体情况，而不要在任何地方和一切地方都按照一种固定的刻板的形式来活动，不要忘记在千变万化的环境里共产党的地位是不会一样的。
    谁要真愿意使我们的工作免于僵化的刻板公式，免于有毒的繁琐哲学，谁就应该与群众一起并领导群众参加实际的积极斗争，同时不断地力求精通马克思、恩格斯、列宁、斯大林的伟大的丰富的全能的教言，来彻底消灭那些公式。
    生活比任何公式都复杂。
    障碍应当加以铲除，而不是在障碍面前屈服。
    在任何时候，胜利都不会自己到来的，而必须去争取它。
    我们既然坚决反对对于自发性的任何依赖，我们在考虑革命发展过程时，就不应该站在旁观者的被动地位，而应该站在这一过程的参加者的主动地位。
    必不可有任何怠惰与自满。我们必不可安于我们的成就，而必须学会不疲倦地工作。
    理论与实践脱离是有害的。在我们所做的实际工作与我们的理论学说之间，必须协调。
