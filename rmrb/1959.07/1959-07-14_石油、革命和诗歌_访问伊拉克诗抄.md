Title: Oil, Revolution, and Poetry – A Visit to Iraqi Poetry
Author: Wang Yafan
Date: 1959-07-14
Page: 8

Oil, Revolution and Poetry
——A Visit to Iraqi Poetry

　　Wang Yafan

　　At dusk, we sat by the Shatt al-Arab River, listening to the rhythmic creaking of the oars. I still vividly remember that extremely friendly conversation, like the shimmering afterglow on the river surface.

　　About Oil

　　We are both poor and rich,
　　We have so much oil,
　　Several pipelines leading to foreign countries,
　　Like arteries, our blood flows…
　　Our blood flows continuously,
　　But we only have two rough hands,
　　They live among the Taurus stars,
　　But those who manage us go hungry every day.
　　Putting on an overbearing manner,
　　As if they were all our superiors,
　　Establishing military bases here,
　　Yet claiming it's the best reward for us.
　　We can produce light and heat,
　　We also have a fiery character,
　　Struggle, fail, struggle again,
　　To live a human life.

　　About Revolution

　　That day, the sun showed its smile,
　　We welcomed the victorious revolution,
　　All the windows were opened,
　　What a refreshing breeze.
　　Shattering the shackles to pieces,
　　The bells rang the joyous dawn,
　　Awakened people went to the streets,
　　Burying treaties and tyrants in the tomb.
　　Imperialism sent warships and cannons,
　　Hesitating, not daring to invade our country,
　　Who gave the republic such strength,
　　The horn of peace scattered the clouds in the sky.
　　They can no longer stand firm in their sinful rule,
　　A paper package can't contain the sparks of revolution,
　　Launching an assault on the forces of darkness,
　　We know who will weep bitterly.

　　About Poetry

　　On the desert journey,
　　Ancient poets rode on camels,
　　The camel bells jingled, jingled, jingled,
　　That is the rhythm of our ancient poetry.
　　In today's struggle,
　　Our poets participate in the ranks of the masses,
　　Peace, friendship, struggle,
　　We create such new poetry.
　　Look at the countless shining eyes,
　　How can poets not be inspired with passion,
　　Singing the hearts of the people,
　　Calling for the people's struggle.

　　(Attached picture)

　　Breaking Free from the Shackles of Imperialism (Written for the first anniversary of the founding of the Republic of Iraq)
　　Fu Tianqiu, Liu Xiaocen, Wang Hongwen Collaboration

### 石油、革命和诗歌——访问伊拉克诗抄
王亚凡
1959-07-14
第8版()
专栏：

    石油、革命和诗歌
    ——访问伊拉克诗抄
    王亚凡黄昏，我们坐在阿拉伯河边，听荡船的桨声咯咯咯，那次极为友好的谈话，像河面闪动的霞光我还牢记着。
  关于石油我们很穷困也很富有，我们有很多很多石油，好几条输油管通向外国，像大动脉我们的血在流……我们的血在流不断地流，可我们只有两支粗手，他们生活在金牛星之间，那管我们的人天天饿肚。摆出一副盛气凌人的样子，仿佛他们都是我们的上司，在这儿建立起军事基地，却说是给我们最好的赏赐。我们能够生产光和热，我们也有火样的性格，斗争，失败，再斗争，为了要过人样的生活。
  关于革命那天，太阳露出笑容，我们迎接胜利的革命，所有的窗户都打开，多么爽人的清风。把镣铐打得落花流水，钟声敲响欢乐的黎明，觉醒的人都到大街上去，把条约和暴君埋进墓坑。帝国主义派来军舰和大炮，徘徊着不敢闯进我们国门，是谁给共和国这样力量，和平的号角吹散满天乌云。他们再也站不稳罪恶的统治，草纸包不住革命的火种，向黑暗势力发动冲击，我们知道谁会痛哭失声。
  关于诗歌在沙漠的旅途中，古代的诗人骑在骆驼背上，驼铃叮当叮当叮当，这就是我们古诗的节奏。在今天的斗争中，我们的诗人参加群众的行列，和平，友谊，斗争我们创作这样新的诗歌。看那无数发亮的眼睛，诗人怎不振奋激情，歌唱人们的心灵，呼唤人们的斗争。（附图片）
