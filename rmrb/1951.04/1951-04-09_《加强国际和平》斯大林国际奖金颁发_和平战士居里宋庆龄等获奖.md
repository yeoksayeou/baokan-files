Title: Stalin International Peace Prize Awarded: Peace Fighters Joliot-Curie, Soong Ching Ling, and Others Win

Author:

Date: 1951-04-09
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, Eighth Dispatch: According to TASS, the Stalin International Peace Prize Committee held meetings in Moscow on the 2nd, 5th, and 6th of April. The meetings were presided over by Committee Chairman, Academician Skobeltsyn of the Soviet Academy of Sciences. Those attending the meetings included Committee Vice-Chairmen: Guo Moruo, President of the Chinese Academy of Sciences, and French writer Aragon, Committee Members: Professor Bernal of the University of London in England, Chilean poet Neruda, Professor Debski of the University of Lodz in Poland, German writer Kellermann, Academician Sadoveanu of the Romanian Academy of Sciences, Soviet writers Fadeyev and Ehrenburg, and others.

　　After studying various suggestions regarding the awarding of the 1950 Stalin International Peace Prizes, the meeting decided to award the Stalin International Peace Prize "For Strengthening Peace Among Nations" to the following representatives of democratic forces from various countries around the world who have made outstanding contributions in the struggle to safeguard and consolidate peace: (1) Joliot-Curie, Professor at the Collège de France, Member of the French Academy of Sciences; (2) Soong Ching Ling, Chairman of the China Welfare Institute; (3) Johnson, Deputy Dean of Canterbury, England; (4) Madame Eugénie Cotton, Honorary Director of the École Normale Supérieure de Sèvres, France; (5) Arthur Moulton, former Protestant Bishop of the United States; (6) Pak Chong-ae, Chairman of the Korean Democratic Women's Union; (7) Heriberto Jara, former Minister of the Navy of Mexico.

　　Note: The Stalin International Peace Prize "For Strengthening Peace Among Nations" was established according to the decree issued by the Presidium of the Supreme Soviet of the Soviet Union on December 20, 1949, in celebration of Stalin's 70th birthday. This prize is awarded annually by the Stalin International Peace Prize Committee "For Strengthening Peace Among Nations," with five to ten recipients. Citizens of any country in the world, regardless of their political views, religious beliefs, or ethnicity, who have made outstanding contributions in the struggle to safeguard and consolidate peace, are eligible for this prize. Stalin International Peace Prize winners will each receive a certificate, a gold medal engraved with Stalin's image, and a prize of 100,000 rubles. Guo Moruo, President of the Chinese Academy of Sciences, was appointed Vice-Chairman of the Stalin International Peace Prize Committee, and he left China on March 27 to attend this selection meeting in the Soviet Union.

### 《加强国际和平》斯大林国际奖金颁发  和平战士居里宋庆龄等获奖

1951-04-09
第4版()
专栏：

　　《加强国际和平》斯大林国际奖金颁发
　  和平战士居里宋庆龄等获奖
    【新华社八日讯】据塔斯社讯：《加强国际和平》斯大林国际奖金委员会于二日、五日及六日在莫斯科举行会议。会议由委员会主席、苏联科学院院士斯科贝尔琴主持，出席会议的有委员会副主席：中国科学院院长郭沫若、法国作家阿拉贡，委员：英国伦敦大学教授贝尔纳、智利诗人尼鲁达、波兰罗兹大学教授德布斯基、德国作家凯勒曼、罗马尼亚科学院院士沙杜维亚努、苏联作家法捷耶夫及爱伦堡等人。
    会议在研究了关于颁发一九五○年斯大林国际奖金的各项建议后，决定以《加强国际和平》斯大林国际奖金授予下列在维护与巩固和平的斗争中有卓越贡献的世界各国民主力量的代表：（一）约里奥—居里，法兰西学院教授，法国科学院委员；（二）宋庆龄，中国人民救济总会主席；（三）约翰逊，英国坎特伯雷副主教；（四）尤金尼·戈登夫人，法国塞夫勒省高等师范学校名誉董事；（五）阿瑟·墨尔顿，美国前新教主教；（六）朴正爱，朝鲜民主妇女同盟委员长；（七）赫里伯托·雅拉，墨西哥前海军部长。
    按：《加强国际和平》斯大林国际奖金，是根据苏联最高苏维埃主席团于一九四九年十二月二十日为庆祝斯大林七十寿辰而颁发的命令设立的。此项奖金每年由《加强国际和平》斯大林国际奖金委员会评选颁发一次，名额为五名至十名。全世界任何国家的公民，凡在维护与巩固和平的斗争中贡献卓著者，不论其政治见解、宗教信仰和种族如何，均可获得此项奖金。斯大林国际奖金得奖人将各领受奖状一纸，刻有斯大林像的金质奖章一枚及奖金十万卢布。我国科学院郭沫若院长被聘为斯大林国际奖金委员会副主席，他是在三月二十七日离国赴苏出席此次评选会议的。
