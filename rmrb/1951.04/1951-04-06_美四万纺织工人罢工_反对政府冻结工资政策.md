Title: US 40,000 Textile Workers Strike Against Government Wage Freeze Policy
Author:
Date: 1951-04-06
Page: 4

　　US 40,000 Textile Workers Strike
　　Against Government Wage Freeze Policy
    Xinhua News Agency, New York, 5th: American workers are intensifying their struggle against the government's wage freeze policy while the cost of living continues to rise.
    According to a report from Charlotte, North Carolina: 40,000 workers employed by 47 textile industry enterprises in six southern states went on strike on the 2nd, demanding increased wages, allowances and other relief payments.
    The first to strike were the workers of the Gold Textile Company in Rock Hill, North Carolina, followed by workers in other textile enterprises in the South. In Greensboro, North Carolina, a central organization has been established by the union to guide the strike.
    The president of the Textile Workers Union of the CIO of America, Mr. Rieve, stated that the union has firmly decided to adhere to the demands put forward.

### 美四万纺织工人罢工  反对政府冻结工资政策

1951-04-06
第4版()
专栏：

　　美四万纺织工人罢工
　  反对政府冻结工资政策
    【新华社五日讯】塔斯社纽约讯：美国工人们正加紧斗争，反对政府实行冻结工资政策而生活费用却不断增长。
    据报载北卡罗来纳州沙罗特城消息：南方六个州中，纺织工业的四十七个企业所雇用的四万工人，已于二日罢工，要求增加工资、津贴和其他救济费。
    首先罢工的是北卡罗来纳州罗克希尔的果尔德织品公司的工人们，随后，南方其他各纺织企业的工人们也跟着罢工。在北卡罗来纳州的格林斯巴罗，已成立了工会指导罢工的中心机关。
    美国产联所属的纺织工人工会主席利夫声明：工会已坚决地决定坚持所提出的要求。
