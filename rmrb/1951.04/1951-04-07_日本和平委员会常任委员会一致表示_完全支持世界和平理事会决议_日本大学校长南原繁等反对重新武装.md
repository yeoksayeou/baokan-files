Title: Japan Peace Committee Standing Committee Unanimously Expresses Full Support for World Peace Council Resolution; Japan University President Shigeru Nanbara and Others Oppose Rearmament
Author:
Date: 1951-04-07
Page: 4

Xinhua News Agency, 6th: According to a United Press dispatch: The Japan Peace Committee convened a meeting of its Standing Committee on March 14th. The meeting discussed the World Peace Council's declaration calling for the five major powers to conclude a peace pact, a resolution on the United Nations, and a resolution on resolving the Japanese issue peacefully. The meeting unanimously expressed support for the World Peace Council's resolutions and decided to launch a signature campaign calling for the conclusion of a peace pact.

The meeting confirmed that the signature campaign calling for an overall peace settlement and the "Resolution on Resolving the Japanese Issue Peacefully" are fundamentally consistent. Therefore, this movement should be promoted more actively.

All those present at the meeting immediately signed the declaration calling for the conclusion of a peace pact.

Xinhua News Agency, 6th, Tokyo Dispatch: Some prominent presidents of Japanese public universities oppose rearmament. Shigeru Nanbara, President of National Tokyo University, said to the graduating class of his university on March 28th: Japan should adhere to its ideal of permanently renouncing war after its surrender. Nanbara claimed: Rearmament would inevitably revive militarism or totalitarianism.

Risaburo Torikai, President of National Kyoto University, said on February 15th, in response to questions raised by Kimura and others, chair of the university's student union: I hope for an overall peace settlement, and I have not changed my position on this in the slightest. Rearmament is not possible under the current constitution. I will resolutely defend the spirit of the constitution to the end.

Hisashi Tsuneto, President of Municipal Osaka University of Commerce, gave a lecture on February 13th at the university entitled "The Issue of Peace Settlement from a Legal Perspective." Tsuneto pointed out: Rearmament violates the Japanese constitution. The Diet cannot recognize treaties that violate the constitution.

### 日本和平委员会常任委员会一致表示  完全支持世界和平理事会决议  日本大学校长南原繁等反对重新武装

1951-04-07
第4版()
专栏：

　　日本和平委员会常任委员会一致表示
　  完全支持世界和平理事会决议
　  日本大学校长南原繁等反对重新武装
    【新华社六日讯】据联合通讯社消息：日本拥护和平委员会曾于三月十四日召开常任委员会。会议讨论了世界和平理事会所通过的要求五大国缔结和平公约的宣言、关于联合国的决议、关于以和平方式解决日本问题的决议。会议一致表示拥护世界和平理事会的各项决议，并决定发起要求缔结和平公约的签名运动。
    会议确认要求全面媾和的签名运动与“关于以和平方式解决日本问题的决议”在基本上是一致的，因此，要更加积极地推进这个运动。
    全体出席会议者当场都在要求缔结和平公约的宣言上签了名。
    【新华社六日讯】东京消息：日本一部分著名公立大学的校长反对重新武装。国立东京大学校长南原繁曾于三月二十八日对该校本年毕业班的学生说：日本应该遵守它在投降之后所抱的永远废弃战争的理想。南原声称：重新武装势将使军国主义或集权主义复活。
    国立京都大学校长鸟养利三郎于二月十五日在答复该校学生会主席木村等所提的问题时说：我希望全面媾和，对于这一点我丝毫没有改变。根据现行的宪法是不能讲重新武装的。我要坚决地保卫宪法的精神到底。
    市立大阪商科大学校长恒藤曾于二月十三日在该校举行题为“从法理上看媾和问题”的演讲。恒藤指出：重新武装是违反日本宪法的。国会不能承认违反宪法的条约。
