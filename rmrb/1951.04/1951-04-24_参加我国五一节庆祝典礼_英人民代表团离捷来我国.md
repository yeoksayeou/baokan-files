Title: British People's Delegation Leaves Czechoslovakia for China to Attend Our May Day Celebrations
Author:
Date: 1951-04-24
Page: 4

　　British People's Delegation Leaves Czechoslovakia for China to Attend Our May Day Celebrations
    Xinhua News Agency, Prague, 22nd — The British People's Delegation, which will participate in our May Day celebrations, left here this morning for Beijing via Moscow. The delegation consists of nine male and two female representatives, led by Yorkshire miner from Doncaster, York Caine. They arrived in Prague from London on the 20th, and our ambassador to Czechoslovakia, Tan Xilin, went to the airport to welcome them. According to a report in the London Workers' Daily, Caine said before his departure: "Our mission is a mission of peace. We are going to China to express to the Chinese people the friendly wishes of the British people and to learn about the great achievements made by the People's Republic of China in the year and a half since its founding."

### 参加我国五一节庆祝典礼  英人民代表团离捷来我国

1951-04-24
第4版()
专栏：

　　参加我国五一节庆祝典礼
　  英人民代表团离捷来我国
    【新华社布拉格二十二日电】参加我国五一节庆祝典礼的英国人民代表团今晨离此取道莫斯科前往北京。代表团系由九位男代表与两位女代表组成，由敦卡斯特矿工约克·凯恩任团长，于二十日由伦敦飞抵布拉格，我驻捷大使谭希林曾前往机场欢迎。据伦敦工人日报报道：凯恩在启程前曾发表谈话说：“我们的使团是和平的使团。我们要前往中国向中国人民表示英国人民与他们友好的愿望，并学习中华人民共和国成立一年半以来所取得的伟大成就。”
